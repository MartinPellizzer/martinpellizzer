Linaria tristis var. trachysperma

Linaria tristis var. trachysperma, commonly known as the slender toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in horticulture for its delicate appearance.
