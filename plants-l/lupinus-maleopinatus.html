Lupinus maleopinatus

Lupinus maleopinatus, commonly known as the black lupin, is a perennial legume native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant has compound leaves with numerous leaflets and produces elongated, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Lupinus maleopinatus is also known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
