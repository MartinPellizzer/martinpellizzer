Linaria ikonnikovii f. dolomitica

Linaria ikonnikovii f. dolomitica, commonly known as the Dolomitica toadflax, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the former Soviet Union.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow-orange flowers with a distinctive bilabiate structure. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, thriving in well-drained, sunny environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various soil conditions.
