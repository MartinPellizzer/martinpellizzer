Lachemilla sandiensis

Lachemilla sandiensis, commonly known as the Sand Island lichen, is a member of the Lichens family, specifically the subclass Lecanoromycetes.

It is a foliose lichen, characterized by its leaf-like thallus that is usually pale green to yellowish in color. This lichen is typically found growing on rock surfaces in arid or semi-arid environments, where it thrives in low-nutrient conditions. It plays a vital role in soil stabilization and nutrient cycling in its native habitats.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is often used as an indicator species for environmental health in desert ecosystems.
