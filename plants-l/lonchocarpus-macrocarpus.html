Lonchocarpus macrocarpus

Lonchocarpus macrocarpus, commonly known as the giant senna, is a tropical legume native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers and compound leaves with 10 to 20 pairs of leaflets. The plant produces distinctive, woody pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in rainforests or along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
