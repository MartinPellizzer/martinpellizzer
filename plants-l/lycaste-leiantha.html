Lycaste leiantha

Lycaste leiantha, commonly known as the white-veined orchid, is a tropical plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers with white petals and deep purple sepals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and leathery, adapted to conserve moisture in its natural habitat.

Lycaste leiantha is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation under the right conditions.
