Lysimachia knutzii

Lysimachia knutzii, commonly known as the yellow water primrose, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes and stream banks. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Lysimachia knutzii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in wetland restoration projects.
