Loxostemon delavayi

Loxostemon delavayi, commonly known as the Delavay's loxostemon, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is often found in rocky or mountainous regions, thriving in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
