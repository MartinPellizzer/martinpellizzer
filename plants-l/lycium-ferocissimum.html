Lycium ferocissimum

Lycium ferocissimum, commonly known as the fierce wolfberry, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces red berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits. It thrives in harsh, dry environments and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and limited water availability.

Its botanical features make it a resilient and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
