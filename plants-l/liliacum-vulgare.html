Liliacum vulgare

Liliacum vulgare, commonly known as the common lily, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Liliaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, or red. The plant features strap-like leaves that are broad and flat, arranged in a fan-like pattern. Liliacum vulgare is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks, thriving in moist, well-drained soil.

Its bulbous root system allows it to store nutrients, supporting its growth during the growing season.
