Lonicera flava var. flavescens

Lonicera flava var. flavescens, commonly known as the yellow honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds but generally not for humans. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Its ornamental value is due to its attractive foliage and fragrant blossoms.
