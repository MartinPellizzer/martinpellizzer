Lindernia hookeri

Lindernia hookeri, commonly known as Hooker's lindernia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of pink, purple, and red. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It thrives in full sun and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
