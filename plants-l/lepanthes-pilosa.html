Lepanthes pilosa

Lepanthes pilosa, commonly known as the hairy paphiopedilum, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically purple or violet in color. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually elliptical and covered in fine, hairy structures, giving it its common name.

Lepanthes pilosa thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.
