Lycium patagonicum var. normale

Lycium patagonicum var. normale, commonly known as the Patagonian boxthorn, is a shrub native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, red berries that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought and extreme temperatures. Its leaves are typically narrow and have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
