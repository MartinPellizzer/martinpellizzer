Lignonia monodynama

Lignonia monodynama, commonly known as the narrow-leaf lignonia, is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in temperate regions. The plant features trifoliate leaves with a distinctive narrow shape and produces small, yellowish-white flowers in clusters. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated, dehiscent pods, and the plant is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.

Lignonia monodynama is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
