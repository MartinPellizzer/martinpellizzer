Lycium turcomannicum

Lycium turcomannicum, commonly known as the Turkestan boxthorn, is a shrub native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces red berries that are edible, though they are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to harsh climates.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
