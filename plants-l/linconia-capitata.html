Linconia capitata

Linconia capitata, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters at the top of the plant. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and grows in a variety of habitats, including open woodlands and grasslands. It is often found in regions with temperate to subtropical climates.

Linconia capitata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
