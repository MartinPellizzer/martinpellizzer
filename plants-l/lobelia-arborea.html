Lobelia arborea

Lobelia arborea, commonly known as tree lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub-like species that can grow up to six feet tall, featuring woody stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often found in woodland habitats.

Lobelia arborea is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits in native gardens.
