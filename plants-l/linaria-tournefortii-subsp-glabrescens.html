Linaria tournefortii subsp. glabrescens

Linaria tournefortii subsp.

glabrescens, commonly known as the silky toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and bright yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The subspecies is distinguished by its reduced pubescence, giving it a silky appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
