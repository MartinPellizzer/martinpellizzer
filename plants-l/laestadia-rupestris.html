Laestadia rupestris

Laestadia rupestris, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-forming growth habit and its ability to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant produces showy, orchid-like flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically blooming during the winter months. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Laestadia rupestris is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its striking appearance and resilience.
