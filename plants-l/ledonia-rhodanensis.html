Ledonia rhodanensis

Ledonia rhodanensis, commonly known as the Rhodanese orchid, is a rare and unique flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its striking violet and white petals, which are often speckled with intricate patterns. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot. Its blooming period occurs during the late spring and early summer months.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Ledonia rhodanensis is considered a vulnerable species.
