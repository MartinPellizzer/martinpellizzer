Leschenaultia splendens

Leschenaultia splendens, commonly known as the golden lily, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow petals and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. It thrives in cool, moist environments and requires well-drained soil.

Leschenaultia splendens is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
