Lycium chinense var. rhombifolium

Lycium chinense var. rhombifolium, commonly known as the Chinese boxthorn, is a shrub native to China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible but often have a sour taste. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in traditional medicine and ecological restoration projects.
