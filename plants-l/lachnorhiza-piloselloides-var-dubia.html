Lachnorhiza piloselloides var. dubia

Lachnorhiza piloselloides var. dubia, commonly known as the hairy cinquefoil, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. The plant features a basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. Its flowers are yellow, with five petals, and appear in clusters at the end of the stems.

It is characterized by its distinctive hairy foliage and is often found in association with other wetland plants.
