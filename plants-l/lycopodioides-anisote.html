Lycopodioides anisote

Lycopodioides anisote, commonly known as the anise fern, is a member of the Lycopodiaceae family, characterized by its primitive structure and spore-producing reproductive system.

It is a small, evergreen fern that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant features a short, underground rhizome with upright, branching fronds that are often adorned with small, scale-like leaves. Its spores are produced in sporangia located on the underside of the fronds, a key feature of its reproductive biology.

Lycopodioides anisote plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
