Lytoneuron tijucanum

Lytoneuron tijucanum, commonly known as the Tijuca tree, is a species native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a large evergreen tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, dark bark and dense canopy. The leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, with a glossy green upper surface and a pale underside. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds.

This plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
