Linaria × macrura

Linaria × macrura, commonly known as the hybrid common toadflax, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its striking, elongated yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, featuring hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental value.

Linaria × macrura is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
