Lophopetalum pallidum

Lophopetalum pallidum, commonly known as the Chinese hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It features small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the spring. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. It produces small, red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Lophopetalum pallidum is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive flowers and foliage.
