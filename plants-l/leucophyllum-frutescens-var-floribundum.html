Leucophyllum frutescens var. floribundum

Leucophyllum frutescens var. floribundum, commonly known as Texas betony, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It features small, fragrant, white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer. The plant has grayish-green foliage with a distinctive fuzzy texture, and it is well adapted to dry, rocky soils. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value comes from its showy blooms and attractive foliage.
