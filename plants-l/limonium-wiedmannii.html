Limonium wiedmannii

Limonium wiedmannii, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, preferring sandy or gravelly soils. Its narrow, grass-like leaves are adapted to withstand salt spray and harsh coastal conditions.

Limonium wiedmannii is often used in ornamental gardening due to its vibrant color and drought tolerance.
