Ligustrum vulgare f. auriflorum

Ligustrum vulgare f. auriflorum, commonly known as the goldenflower privet, is a cultivar of the common privet.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers that appear in clusters during the spring. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green. It is a deciduous shrub that thrives in a variety of soil types and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

This cultivar is relatively low maintenance and resistant to pests and diseases.
