Liparis triticea

Liparis triticea, commonly known as the wheat orchid, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, delicate orchid flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It produces a single spike of fragrant, white or pale green flowers, which bloom during the spring and summer months.

Liparis triticea is often found in open habitats such as plains, hills, and along roadsides.
