Luehea candicans var. candicans

Luehea candicans var. candicans, commonly known as the white mangrove, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale gray bark and exudes a milky latex when cut. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. It thrives in coastal areas, often found in brackish or saline environments.

Its ecological importance includes stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial species.
