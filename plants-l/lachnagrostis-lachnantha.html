Lachnagrostis lachnantha

Lachnagrostis lachnantha, commonly known as slender wheatgrass, is a flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is a low-growing perennial species characterized by its fine, tufted shoots and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along streambanks.

Lachnagrostis lachnantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators and soil stabilization.
