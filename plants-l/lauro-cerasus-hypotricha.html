Lauro-cerasus hypotricha

Lauro-cerasus hypotricha, commonly known as the Japanese cherry laurel, is a species of flowering plant in the Prunus genus.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.

However, it is considered invasive in some regions where it outcompetes native vegetation.
