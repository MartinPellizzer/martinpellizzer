Lonicera Germanica

Lonicera germanica, commonly known as the German honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to central and eastern europe. it features fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. the plant is characterized by its twining stems and opposite leaves that turn red in the fall. it thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges and thickets. due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is a popular choice for gardeners and naturalists alike.
