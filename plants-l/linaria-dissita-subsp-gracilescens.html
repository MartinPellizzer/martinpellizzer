Linaria dissita subsp. gracilescens

Linaria dissita subsp.

gracilescens, commonly known as the slender toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments.

The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its linear leaves are slender and often hairy, contributing to its drought-resistant adaptations.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
