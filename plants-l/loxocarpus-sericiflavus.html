Loxocarpus sericiflavus

Loxocarpus sericiflavus, commonly known as the velvetleaf lichen, is a foliose lichen belonging to the family Lecanoraceae.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety thallus that is typically yellowish to orange in color. The plant forms a symbiotic relationship with algae and fungi, which contribute to its photosynthetic and nutrient absorption capabilities. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found on bark or soil surfaces in temperate regions.

Its slow growth and intricate structure make it an important indicator species for environmental conditions.
