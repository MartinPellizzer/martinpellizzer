Lolium temulentum f. speciosum

Lolium temulentum f. speciosum, commonly known as the purple grass or purple ryegrass, is a variant of the annual ryegrass species.

It is characterized by its distinctive purple coloration in its foliage and inflorescence, which sets it apart from the typical green form. This plant is native to Europe and has been introduced to other parts of the world, often growing in disturbed soils and along roadsides. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, typical of the Poaceae family.

The plant is often considered a weed but has some ornamental value due to its vibrant color.
