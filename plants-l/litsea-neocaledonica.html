Litsea neocaledonica

Litsea neocaledonica, commonly known as the New Caledonian laurier-rose, is a small evergreen tree native to the island of New Caledonia in the South Pacific.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurel, bay, and avocado trees. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, glossy dark green leaves and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. It produces small, round berries that are typically yellow or red when mature.

Litsea neocaledonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
