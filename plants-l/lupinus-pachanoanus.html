Lupinus pachanoanus

Lupinus pachanoanus, commonly known as the Pachanoan lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States, particularly California.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of blue or purple pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds.

Lupinus pachanoanus is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal dunes.
