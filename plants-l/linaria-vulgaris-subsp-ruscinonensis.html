Linaria vulgaris subsp. ruscinonensis

Linaria vulgaris subsp.

ruscinonensis, commonly known as the Balkan toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. It has linear leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.
