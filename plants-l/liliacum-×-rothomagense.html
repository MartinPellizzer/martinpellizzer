Liliacum × rothomagense

Liliacum × rothomagense, commonly known as the Rothomago lily, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between *Lilium bulbiferum* and *Lilium rothomagense*.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically blooms in late summer, producing clusters of fragrant, trumpet-shaped blossoms. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Botanically, it is notable for its ornamental value and its role in the study of hybridization in the Liliaceae family.
