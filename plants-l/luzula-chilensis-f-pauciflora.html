Luzula chilensis f. pauciflora

Luzula chilensis f. pauciflora, commonly known as the Chilean woodrush, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the temperate regions of South America.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green to reddish in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
