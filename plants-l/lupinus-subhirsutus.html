Lupinus subhirsutus

Lupinus subhirsutus, commonly known as the hairy lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant has hairy leaves and is often found in dry, rocky habitats. It plays an important ecological role by fixing nitrogen into the soil, benefiting surrounding plant life.

Lupinus subhirsutus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
