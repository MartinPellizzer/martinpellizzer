Lepidium flaccidum

Lepidium flaccidum, commonly known as the slender cress, is a low-growing annual herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically features small, oval leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of tiny, yellowish-white flowers. The plant belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard family members. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and along stream banks.

Lepidium flaccidum is known for its peppery flavor, making it a useful plant in some culinary and medicinal applications.
