Lantana aristata var. hoehnei

Lantana aristata var. hoehnei, commonly known as the Hoehne's lantana, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a shrub characterized by its opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a hardy plant in tropical and subtropical climates.

The species is also noted for its ornamental value and use in landscaping.
