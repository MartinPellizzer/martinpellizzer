Lablab purpureus subsp. uncinatus

Lablab purpureus subsp.

uncinatus, commonly known as the hairy lablab, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. It is characterized by its distinctive purple flowers and hairy stems, which give it its common name.

The plant produces edible pods containing seeds that are used in various culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun for optimal growth.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it a valuable crop for soil enrichment.
