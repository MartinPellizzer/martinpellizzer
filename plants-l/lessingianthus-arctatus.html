Lessingianthus arctatus

Lessingianthus arctatus, commonly known as the mountain everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and its ability to thrive in high-altitude environments. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow and brown, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically gray-green, covered in fine hairs, and are adapted to reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Lessingianthus arctatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
