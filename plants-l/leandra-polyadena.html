Leandra polyadena

Leandra polyadena, commonly known as the velvetleaf orchid, is a tropical plant native to regions of Central and South America.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and glossy, often with a waxy texture that helps it retain moisture in humid environments.

Leandra polyadena is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded, moist conditions.
