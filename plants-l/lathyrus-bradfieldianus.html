Lathyrus bradfieldianus

Lathyrus bradfieldianus, commonly known as Bradfield's vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to southern Europe and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are adapted for dispersal by wind.

Lathyrus bradfieldianus is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including gardens and wild areas.
