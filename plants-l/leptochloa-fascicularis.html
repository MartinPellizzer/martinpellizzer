Leptochloa fascicularis

Leptochloa fascicularis, commonly known as the clustered needlegrass, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and compact inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its small, spike-like flower heads are composed of numerous tiny florets.

Leptochloa fascicularis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
