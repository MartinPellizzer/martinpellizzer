Lepanthes mirabilis

Lepanthes mirabilis, commonly known as the miraculous orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored flowers that resemble miniature orchids. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are usually small and delicate, with intricate patterns and a sweet fragrance.

Lepanthes mirabilis is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and rarity in cultivation.
