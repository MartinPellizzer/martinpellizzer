Lygodesmia spinosa

Lygodesmia spinosa, commonly known as the hairy lygodium, is a climbing fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Lygodiaceae and is characterized by its wiry, spiny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by spore-bearing cones. It thrives in humid environments and is often found in shaded, moist areas of forests.

Its unique morphology and growth habit make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
