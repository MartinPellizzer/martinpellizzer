Lycium tenue var. sieberi

Lycium tenue var. sieberi, commonly known as the silverberry, is a shrub native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellow-orange berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential agricultural use.
