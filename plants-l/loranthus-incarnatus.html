Loranthus incarnatus

Loranthus incarnatus, commonly known as the red mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees, primarily in the Mediterranean region.

It has small, elliptical leaves that are usually green but can turn reddish in certain conditions. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its berries are typically red or orange, containing sticky seeds that adhere to the bark of host trees.

Loranthus incarnatus plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for birds and insects while also affecting the growth of its host plants.
