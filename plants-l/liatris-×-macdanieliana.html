Liatris × macdanieliana

Liatris × macdanieliana, commonly known as the MacDaniel's blazingstar, is a hybrid perennial grassland plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and showy, spiky flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy or loamy soils. Its botanical features include narrow, grass-like leaves and inflorescences that are usually pink to purple in color.

Liatris × macdanieliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators in native landscapes.
