Lycopodioides sulcata

Lycopodioides sulcata, commonly known as the Sulcate clubmoss, is a species of lycophyte belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and a creeping, rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces spore-bearing cones, known as strobila, which are typically clustered at the base of the stems. Lycopodioides sulcata thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Its reproductive structures and morphological features are typical of ancient plant groups, reflecting its evolutionary significance in the plant kingdom.
