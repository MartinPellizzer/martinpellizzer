Lepidium ruderale var. lasiocarpum

Lepidium ruderale var. lasiocarpum, commonly known as the hairy-fruited peppergrass, is a low-growing annual plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many flowering plants with small, cross-shaped flowers. The plant typically has hairy stems and leaves, and its flowers are arranged in racemes, producing small, yellowish blooms. The fruit is a short, cylindrical silique that is covered in fine hairs, giving it the varietal name *lasiocarpum*.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.
