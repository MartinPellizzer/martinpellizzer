Lyonia chapaensis

Lyonia chapaensis, commonly known as the Chapa lyonia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Chile.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen shrub form. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in the spring. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a dense canopy that helps it thrive in rocky, well-drained soils.

Lyonia chapaensis is adapted to cold, dry climates and is often found in high-altitude environments.
