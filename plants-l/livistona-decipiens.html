Livistona decipiens

Livistona decipiens, commonly known as the false areca palm, is a species of cycad native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces cones, with male and female cones occurring on separate plants, a feature typical of cycads. Its leaves are often used in traditional Aboriginal practices and for ornamental purposes.

Livistona decipiens is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing resilience to drought and heat.
