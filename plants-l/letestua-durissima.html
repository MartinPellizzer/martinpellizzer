Letestua durissima

Letestua durissima, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers and spiky, silver-green leaves. The plant thrives in coastal areas and is often found in sandy or gravelly soils. It is a perennial species that produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Letestua durissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
