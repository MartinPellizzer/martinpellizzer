Lycopodium fordii

Lycopodium fordii, commonly known as the Chinese clubmoss, is a species of lycophyte native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, spore-bearing cones. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its leaves are spirally arranged and form a compact, cushion-like structure.

Lycopodium fordii plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
