Lippia nodiflora var. acutifolia

Lippia nodiflora var. acutifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved lippia, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a shrub characterized by its aromatic, narrow, and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional herbal remedies.
