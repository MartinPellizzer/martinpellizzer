Lafoensia pacari f. lucida

Lafoensia pacari f. lucida, commonly known as the shining lafoensia, is a tropical shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its fruit is a legume pod containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and research settings.
