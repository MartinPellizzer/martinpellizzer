Linum bipunctatum

Linum bipunctatum, commonly known as the two-spotted flax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, delicate blue or purple flowers that bloom in early summer. It belongs to the Linaceae family, which includes other flax species.

Linum bipunctatum is often found in rocky soils and open habitats, thriving in well-drained environments.
