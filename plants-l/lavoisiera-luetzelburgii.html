Lavoisiera luetzelburgii

Lavoisiera luetzelburgii, commonly known as the Luetzelburg's lily, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils at high altitudes. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Lavoisiera luetzelburgii is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators in its native habitat.
