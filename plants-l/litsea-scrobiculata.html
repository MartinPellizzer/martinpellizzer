It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries such as India, China, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, rough texture. It produces small, white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by small, round berries.

Litsea scrobiculata is often found in moist, shaded forest environments and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal uses.
