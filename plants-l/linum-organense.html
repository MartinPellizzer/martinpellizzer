Linum organense

Linum organense, commonly known as the common flax, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces blue or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its seeds are widely used in the production of linen and oil.

Linum organense is also cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
