Lathyrus pubescens

Lathyrus pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent vetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple, followed by pods containing several seeds. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and full sun.

Lathyrus pubescens is often found in disturbed areas, along roadsides, and in woodland edges.
