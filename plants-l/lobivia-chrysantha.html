Lobivia chrysantha

Lobivia chrysantha, commonly known as the golden lobivia, is a succulent native to South America, specifically Argentina.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in dense, white to pale green spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, which bloom from the areoles along the stem. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Lobivia chrysantha is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in dry climates.
