Limonium sinuatum var. akkense

Limonium sinuatum var. akkense, commonly known as the Turkish sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, often arranged in spikes. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

This variety is particularly noted for its compact growth habit and ability to attract pollinators.
