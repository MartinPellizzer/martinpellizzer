Leontodon officinalis

Leontodon officinalis, commonly known as the common dandelion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, hairy leaves and a distinctive yellow composite flower head. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been naturalized in many other regions. It produces a wind-dispersed seed with a white, feathery pappus.

Leontodon officinalis is known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically in traditional medicine.
