Lutzia fruticosa f. globosa

Lutzia fruticosa f. globosa, commonly known as the globular lutzia, is a compact, shrub-like plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and small, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern around the stems. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, which are often borne on the upper parts of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Its ornamental value lies in its compact form and attractive foliage, which makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking low-maintenance, decorative plants.
