Loranthus erythmtrichus

Loranthus erythmtrichus, commonly known as the red-fruited mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of host trees, drawing water and nutrients from them.

It belongs to the family Loranthaceae and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its parasitic nature allows it to thrive in a variety of forest environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The species is notable for its ecological role in supporting biodiversity through seed dispersal and habitat provision.
