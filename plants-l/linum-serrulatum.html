Linum serrulatum

Linum serrulatum, commonly known as the serrated flax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces delicate blue or purple flowers that bloom in early summer. It is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to thrive in sunny environments.

Linum serrulatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
