Lindera racemosa

Lindera racemosa, commonly known as sweet pepper bush, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurel trees and cinnamon plants. The plant features opposite, simple, elliptic leaves that are glossy and dark green on top. It produces small, fragrant, white flowers in racemes during the spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant landscaping.
