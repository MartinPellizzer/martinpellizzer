Loxogramme vittariiformis

Loxogramme vittariiformis, commonly known as the water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to various regions in Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, floating fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and marshes. Its rhizomes spread along the water's surface, forming dense mats that can help stabilize sediment.

Loxogramme vittariiformis plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
