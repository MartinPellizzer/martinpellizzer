Lasiorrhiza amoena

Lasiorrhiza amoena, commonly known as the red sand rat.

It is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Australia, characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and drought conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
