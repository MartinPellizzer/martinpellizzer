Lythrum propinquum

Lythrum propinquum, commonly known as narrowleaf rosepink, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lythraceae family, which includes plants known for their showy, often pink or red flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks. Its slender, upright stems are often adorned with small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
