Laurus carolinensis

Laurus carolinensis, commonly known as the Carolina laurel or sweet bay, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 6 to 15 feet. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by dark blue berries. Its leaves are aromatic, glossy, and often used in cooking, particularly in the preparation of bay leaves.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
