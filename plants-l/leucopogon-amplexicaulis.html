Leucopogon amplexicaulis

Leucopogon amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clasping leucopogon, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in heathlands and coastal areas, characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale blue or white, arranged in clusters. It has a compact growth habit with leaves that often wrap around the stems, giving it a unique appearance.

Leucopogon amplexicaulis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
