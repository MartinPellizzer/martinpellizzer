Laurus elegans

Laurus elegans, commonly known as the sweet bay laurel, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, dark green, glossy leaves that are often used in cooking and traditional medicine. Its flowers are small and white, followed by elliptical, dark blue-black fruits.

Laurus elegans is valued for its ornamental beauty and its essential oils, which contribute to its fragrance and medicinal properties.
