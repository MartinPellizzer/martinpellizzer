Leucrinis

Leucrinis, commonly known as the white-flowered orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, elongated leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as tropical forests. Its roots are fibrous and adapted for absorbing moisture from the surrounding soil.

Leucrinis is prized in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
