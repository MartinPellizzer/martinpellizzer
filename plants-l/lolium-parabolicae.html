Lolium parabolicae

Lolium parabolicae, commonly known as the parabolic ryegrass, is a species of grass native to certain regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and dense, compact growth habit. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and pastures. Its flowering stems bear spikelets that are arranged in a distinctive parabolic pattern, giving it its scientific name.

Lolium parabolicae is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential in ecological restoration projects.
