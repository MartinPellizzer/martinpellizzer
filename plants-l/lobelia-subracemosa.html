Lobelia subracemosa

Lobelia subracemosa, commonly known as the slender whorled lobelia, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its compact, whorled arrangement of leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Lobelia subracemosa is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
