Lastrea lugubris

Lastrea lugubris, commonly known as the weeping lastrea, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and small, fleshy leaves that give it a unique, cascading appearance. The plant produces pale yellow flowers during the summer months, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its delicate appearance, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in rock gardens.
