Lindsaea recedens

Lindsaea recedens, commonly known as the creeping lindsaea, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping rhizomes that give rise to small, delicate fronds. The fronds are typically pinnately divided, with a lanceolate shape and a soft, velvety texture. Native to tropical regions of Central and South America, it thrives in shaded, moist environments.

This plant is often found in forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appearance in botanical gardens.
