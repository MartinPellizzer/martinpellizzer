Lycopodiella appressa

Lycopodiella appressa, commonly known as the small clubmoss, is a small, evergreen fern-like plant that belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, creeping growth habit and its ability to form dense mats on the forest floor. The plant has needle-like leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance. Lycopodiella appressa is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky areas.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and small invertebrates.
