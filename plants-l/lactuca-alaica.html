Lactuca alaica

Lactuca alaica, commonly known as the Armenian lettuce, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces a single, upright stem that bears small, yellow flowers arranged in a composite head. It is native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

Lactuca alaica is often cultivated for its edible leaves, which have a mild, peppery flavor.
