Lasianthus cyanocarpus var. novaguineensis

Lasianthus cyanocarpus var. novaguineensis, commonly known as the New Guinea blue-fruited lasianthus, is a member of the Rubiaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, opposite leaves that are usually elliptic to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by blue-black berries that are distinctive in appearance. This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique fruiting characteristics.
