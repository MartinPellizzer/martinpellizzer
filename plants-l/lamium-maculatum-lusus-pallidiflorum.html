Lamium maculatum lusus pallidiflorum

Lamium maculatum lusus pallidiflorum, commonly known as the pale-flowered spotted dead nettle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive white or pale markings, and it produces clusters of small, pale pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or garden beds. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial to full shade.

Its foliage and flowers contribute to early spring blooms and provide habitat for pollinators.
