Leptorhynchos elongatus

Leptorhynchos elongatus, commonly known as the slender-leaved orchid, is a species of orchid native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pale yellow. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories or along stream banks. Its growth habit is terrestrial, with rhizomes that help anchor it in the soil and absorb nutrients.

Leptorhynchos elongatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral structure.
