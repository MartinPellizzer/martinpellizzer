Lappago racemosa var. erecta

Lappago racemosa var. erecta, commonly known as the upright lappago, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous habitats, thriving in cool, high-altitude environments. Its leaves are narrow and serrated, often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive floral display.
