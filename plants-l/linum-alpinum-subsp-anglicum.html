Linum alpinum subsp. anglicum

Linum alpinum subsp.

anglicum, commonly known as the English flax or alpine flax, is a flowering plant belonging to the genus Linum in the family Linaceae. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often hairy, while its capsules are elongated and contain numerous small, brown seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in fiber production.
