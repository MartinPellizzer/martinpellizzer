Luzula spicata var. simensis

Luzula spicata var. simensis, commonly known as the African sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. Its botanical characteristics include a rhizomatous root system and narrow, linear leaves.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
