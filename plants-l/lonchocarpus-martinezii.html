Lonchocarpus martinezii

Lonchocarpus martinezii, commonly known as the pink velvet bean, is a tropical legume native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking pink or purple flowers and compound leaves with small, serrated leaflets. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain several elliptical seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland tropical forests.

The species is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing abilities, making it useful in agricultural systems.
