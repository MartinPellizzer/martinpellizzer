Ledebouria remifolia

Ledebouria remifolia, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant has fleshy, succulent leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Ledebouria remifolia is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and unique floral display.
