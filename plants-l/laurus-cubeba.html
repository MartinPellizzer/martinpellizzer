Laurus cubeba

Laurus cubeba, commonly known as cubeb pepper or Java pepper, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Indonesia and parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like laurel and cinnamon. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are harvested and dried for their pungent, spicy flavor. These berries contain essential oils rich in compounds like cubebene and elemicin, contributing to its distinctive aroma and medicinal properties.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as a spice in various cuisines.
