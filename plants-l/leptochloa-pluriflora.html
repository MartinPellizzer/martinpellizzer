Leptochloa pluriflora

Leptochloa pluriflora, commonly known as the many-flowered leptochloa, is a species of grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slight sheen, and the flowers are small and inconspicuous.

Leptochloa pluriflora plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
