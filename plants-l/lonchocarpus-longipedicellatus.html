Lonchocarpus longipedicellatus

Lonchocarpus longipedicellatus, commonly known as the broad-leaved cowpea, is a tropical leguminous plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with elongated leaflets and distinctive, pod-like fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family and is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, and it thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.

The plant has been studied for its potential medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects.
