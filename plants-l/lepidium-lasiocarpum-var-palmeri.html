Lepidium lasiocarpum var. palmeri

Lepidium lasiocarpum var. palmeri, commonly known as Palmer's peppergrass, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard plants, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and hairy leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has a low, spreading growth habit. Its fruit is a small, dry capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
