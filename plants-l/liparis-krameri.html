Liparis krameri

Liparis krameri, commonly known as the Krameri's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Asia, including China and Korea.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its delicate appearance and relatively simple structure.

Liparis krameri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in the right conditions.
