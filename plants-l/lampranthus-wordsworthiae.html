Lampranthus wordsworthiae

Lampranthus wordsworthiae, commonly known as the Firecracker Plant, is a striking member of the Aizoaceae family native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, often resembling small firecrackers, hence its common name. The plant typically grows as a succulent shrub, with fleshy, narrow leaves that help it store water in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a favored ornamental plant in regions with Mediterranean climates.
