Luculia pinceana var. pubescens

Luculia pinceana var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which give it its varietal name "pubescens.". It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, fragrant blooms.
