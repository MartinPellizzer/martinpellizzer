Luzula pseudosudetica var. turcica

Luzula pseudosudetica var. turcica, commonly known as the Turkish sedge, is a variant of the sedge genus Luzula.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and a dense, compact growth habit. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous regions. It is native to parts of Turkey and the Balkans, where it thrives in alpine and subalpine environments.

The inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spike of small, three-ranked flowers, typical of the Cyperaceae family.
