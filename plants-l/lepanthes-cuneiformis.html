Lepanthes cuneiformis

Lepanthes cuneiformis, commonly known as the narrow-leafed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments within tropical forests. The plant features delicate, elongated leaves and produces intricate, fragrant flowers with a distinctive coloration. Its flowers are often pale green or white with a yellow or orange lip, making it visually striking.

Lepanthes cuneiformis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
