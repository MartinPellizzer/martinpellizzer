Lathyrus monocarpus

Lathyrus monocarpus, commonly known as the one-seed larkspur, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, spindly stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant produces a single, elongated seed pod that contains a single seed, hence its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Botanically, it is a member of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities.
