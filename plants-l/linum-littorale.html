Linum littorale

Linum littorale, commonly known as sea flax, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along shores and dunes, where it tolerates salt spray and wind. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and it produces capsules that contain numerous small seeds.

Linum littorale plays an important role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various insects and birds.
