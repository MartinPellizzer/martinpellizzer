Leptogonum domingense

Leptogonum domingense, commonly known as the Dominican leucaena, is a shrub native to the Caribbean, particularly the Dominican Republic.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink. Its fruit is a legume pod containing several seeds, and it is often found in coastal and disturbed habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
