Lactuca coriacea

Lactuca coriacea, commonly known as rock lettuce, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its pale green, spoon-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a loose cluster, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Lactuca coriacea is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and lettuces.
