Linum carteri

Linum carteri, commonly known as Carter's flax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and delicate, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Linum carteri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive floral display.
