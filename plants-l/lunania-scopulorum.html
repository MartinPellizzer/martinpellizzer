Lunania scopulorum

Lunania scopulorum, commonly known as the rock bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, bell-like flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in harsh, arid environments.

Lunania scopulorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
