Lobelia trullifolia subsp. minor

Lobelia trullifolia subsp.

minor, commonly known as the dwarf blue lobelia, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the southeastern United States. It features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, clustered blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
