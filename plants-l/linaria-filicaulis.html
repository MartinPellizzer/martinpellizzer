Linaria filicaulis

Linaria filicaulis, commonly known as the slender toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its delicate, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Linaria filicaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
