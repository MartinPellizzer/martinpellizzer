Lastrea filix-mas var. paleaceolobata

Lastrea filix-mas var. paleaceolobata, commonly known as the pale-leaf yew, is a variant of the yew plant characterized by its distinctive pale green foliage.

It is a slow-growing evergreen shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of up to 6 meters. The leaves are elongated, narrow, and have a leathery texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It produces small, red berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

This plant thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.
