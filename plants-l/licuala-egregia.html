Licuala egregia

Licuala egregia, commonly known as the giant pandan or giant screw pine, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves that can grow up to 3 meters in diameter, making it a prominent feature in its natural habitat. The plant is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests, where it thrives in moist, humid conditions. Its leaves are used in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber, while its roots and leaves have been utilized in local cuisines and medicinal practices.

Licuala egregia plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various species.
