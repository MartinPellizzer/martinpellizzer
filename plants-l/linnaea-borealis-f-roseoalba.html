Linnaea borealis f. roseoalba

Linnaea borealis f. roseoalba, commonly known as the rose-white twinflower, is a rare variant of the twinflower plant native to northern regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its distinctive pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is a low-growing perennial with opposite, ovate leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white pubescence. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral coloration and its role in ecological studies of alpine and boreal ecosystems.
