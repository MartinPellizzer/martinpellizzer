Lobelia scaevolifolia

Lobelia scaevolifolia, commonly known as the scarlet lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking red or pink tubular flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture.

Lobelia scaevolifolia is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.
