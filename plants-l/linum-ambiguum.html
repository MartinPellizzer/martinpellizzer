Linum ambiguum

Linum ambiguum, commonly known as wild flax, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, brown seeds. Linum ambiguum is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in textile production.
