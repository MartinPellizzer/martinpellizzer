Laurus tazia

Laurus tazia, commonly known as the Tazian laurel, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, which consists of dark green, glossy leaves arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, fleshy berries. Laurus tazia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
