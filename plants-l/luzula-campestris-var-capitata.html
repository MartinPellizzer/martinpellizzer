Luzula campestris var. capitata

Luzula campestris var. capitata, commonly known as the clustered wood rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, commonly referred to as the grass family, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, greenish to white inflorescences that are clustered at the top of the stems, giving it its varietal name. It typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks.

Luzula campestris var. capitata is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
