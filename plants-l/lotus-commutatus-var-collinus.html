Lotus commutatus var. collinus

Lotus commutatus var. collinus, commonly known as the mountain lotus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features basal leaves with a distinctive heart-shaped base and serrated margins, often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. The plant produces showy pink to purple flowers with five petals, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
