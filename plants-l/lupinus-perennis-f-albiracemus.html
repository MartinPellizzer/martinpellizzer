Lupinus perennis f. albiracemus

Lupinus perennis f. albiracemus, commonly known as the white-stemmed lupine, is a variant of the perennial lupine native to North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive white flowers and slender, often hairy stems. The plant typically grows in prairie and open woodland habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces legumes that contain nitrogen-fixing bacteria.

This form is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in soil enrichment.
