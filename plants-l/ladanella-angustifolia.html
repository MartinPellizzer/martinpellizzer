Ladanella angustifolia

Ladanella angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf ladanella, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, fragrant flowers are usually pale blue or purple and bloom in the spring.

Ladanella angustifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
