Leersia stipitata

Leersia stipitata, commonly known as the slender leersia, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. Its flowering period occurs in the late spring to early summer, producing delicate, greenish-white flowers.

Leersia stipitata is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
