Lindera communis var. esquirolii

Lindera communis var. esquirolii, commonly known as the Florida spicebush, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves that are often tinged with red or purple on the underside. The plant produces small, yellow-green flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for birds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and forest edges.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and ornamental gardening.
