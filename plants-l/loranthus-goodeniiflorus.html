Loranthus goodeniiflorus

Loranthus goodeniiflorus, commonly known as the Goodeniopsis or the silver mistflower, is a parasitic plant belonging to the Loranthaceae family.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows on the branches of eucalyptus and other host trees, deriving nutrients through haustoria that penetrate the host's vascular system. It produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds dispersed by animals.

Due to its unique ecological role and ornamental appearance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
