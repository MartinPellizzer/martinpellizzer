Leucanthemopsis flaveola var. flava

Leucanthemopsis flaveola var. flava, commonly known as the golden marguerite, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant features bright yellow flowers with white margins, which bloom in summer, and its leaves are basal, heart-shaped, and hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
