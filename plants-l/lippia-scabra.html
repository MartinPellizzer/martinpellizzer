Lippia scabra

Lippia scabra, commonly known as rough lippia, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and small, fragrant white or yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in disturbed areas, along roadsides, and in open woodlands.

Lippia scabra is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
