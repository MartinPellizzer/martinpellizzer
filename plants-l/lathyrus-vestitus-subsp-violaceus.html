Lathyrus vestitus subsp. violaceus

Lathyrus vestitus subsp.

violaceus, commonly known as the purple-flowered grasspea, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, upright stems covered in soft, hairy foliage.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep purple. Its trifoliate leaves are typically hairy and have a distinctive violet hue.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
