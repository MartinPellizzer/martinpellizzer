Lychnis pumilio

Lychnis pumilio, commonly known as the dwarf pink, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, delicate pink or red flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant typically grows to a height of only a few inches, making it well-suited for rock gardens or borders. Its leaves are ovate and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Lychnis pumilio is known for its ability to thrive in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil.
