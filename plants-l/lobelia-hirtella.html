Lobelia hirtella

Lobelia hirtella, commonly known as the Indian tobacco, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly rough texture. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wet meadows.

Lobelia hirtella is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
