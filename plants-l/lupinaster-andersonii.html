Lupinaster andersonii

Lupinaster andersonii, commonly known as the Anderson's lupin, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and clusters of small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing pink to purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Lupinaster andersonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
