Lithospermum lycopsoides

Lithospermum lycopsoides, commonly known as the rock pepper, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in woodland or open areas. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, violet to red flowers in clusters. Its roots contain compounds that have been used in traditional medicine.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
