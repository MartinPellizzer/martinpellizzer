Lonchocarpus griffonianus

Lonchocarpus griffonianus, commonly known as the giant senna, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is notable for its bright yellow flowers, which are often clustered in racemes. Its root system is fibrous, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

The species is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some indigenous communities.
