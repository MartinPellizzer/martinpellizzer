Lilium thunbergianum punctatum

Lilium thunbergianum punctatum, commonly known as the spotted lily, is a striking bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear large, showy flowers with distinctive spotted or streaked petals. The foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
