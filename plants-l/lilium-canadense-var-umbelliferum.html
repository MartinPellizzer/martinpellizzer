Lilium canadense var. umbelliferum

Lilium canadense var. umbelliferum, commonly known as the Canada lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its tall, upright stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. Its flowers are usually pink to reddish-purple, with six petals and a distinctive spurred tepal.

The variety umbelliferum is distinguished by its umbel-like arrangement of flowers at the top of the stem.
