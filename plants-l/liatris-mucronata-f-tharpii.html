Liatris mucronata f. tharpii

Liatris mucronata f. tharpii, commonly known as the Texas liatris, is a flowering plant native to the central and southern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence composed of purple to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
