Lasianthus bractescens

Lasianthus bractescens, commonly known as the bracteate lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences. The plant typically grows as an herb or shrub, featuring opposite leaves that are often lance-shaped. Its flowers are usually clustered in whorls, with bright colors that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
