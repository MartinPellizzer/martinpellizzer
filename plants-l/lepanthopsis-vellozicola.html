Lepanthopsis vellozicola

Lepanthopsis vellozicola, commonly known as the Vellozicola orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit and pseudobulbs. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color, with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated by enthusiasts, though it remains relatively rare in horticultural trade.
