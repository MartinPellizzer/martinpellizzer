Limonium merxmuelleri subsp. tigulianum

Limonium merxmuelleri subsp.

tigulianum, commonly known as the Tigulian sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the Black Sea. It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in sandy or gravelly soils. It is often found in maritime climates with salty air and well-drained soil conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in coastal gardens.
