Laggera lyrata

Laggera lyrata, commonly known as the lyreleaf tansy, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. Its flowers are yellow and appear in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value.

Laggera lyrata is often cultivated for its attractive foliage and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
