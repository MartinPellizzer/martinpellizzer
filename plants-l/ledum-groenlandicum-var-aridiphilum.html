Ledum groenlandicum var. aridiphilum

Ledum groenlandicum var. aridiphilum, commonly known as Arctic cranberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to arctic and alpine regions of North America.

It produces small, urn-shaped flowers that are typically pink to red in color, which bloom in late summer. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves that are densely packed along its stems. It thrives in nutrient-poor, acidic soils and is adapted to cold, harsh environments.

Its botanical features make it a characteristic component of tundra and rocky habitats.
