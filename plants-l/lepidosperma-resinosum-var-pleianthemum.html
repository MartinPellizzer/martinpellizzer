Lepidosperma resinosum var. pleianthemum

Lepidosperma resinosum var. pleianthemum, commonly known as the red-stemmed sedgeland sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It typically grows in wetland or swampy environments, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences that bloom in summer. Its distinctive reddish stems and yellowish flowers make it easily identifiable.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
