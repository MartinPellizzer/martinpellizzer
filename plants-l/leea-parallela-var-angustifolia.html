Leea parallela var. angustifolia

Leea parallela var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf leea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Leeaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite, and often narrow leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters. It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
