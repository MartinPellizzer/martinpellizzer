Lilium albanicum

Lilium albanicum, commonly known as the Albanian lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, or red. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers with six petals and a long, narrow tube. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Lilium albanicum thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.
