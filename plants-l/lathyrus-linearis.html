Lathyrus linearis

Lathyrus linearis, commonly known as the slender vetch, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Lathyrus linearis thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it an important plant for ecological studies and sustainable agriculture.
