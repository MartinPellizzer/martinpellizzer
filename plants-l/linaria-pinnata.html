Linaria pinnata

Linaria pinnata, commonly known as the slender toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features slender, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow-orange, with two lips and five petals, arranged in spikes along the stem.

Linaria pinnata thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.
