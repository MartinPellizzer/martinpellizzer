Leplaea cedrata

Leplaea cedrata, commonly known as the cedar tree, is a species of coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Pinaceae and is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and dense, dark green foliage. The tree produces cone-like structures that contain its seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its bark is thick and scaly, providing protection against harsh environmental conditions.

Leplaea cedrata is valued for its durability and is often used in construction and furniture making.
