Linderniella

Linderniella, commonly known as the Indian nettle, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Linderniaceae.

These plants are typically herbaceous, with slender stems and opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. They produce small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually clustered in dense inflorescences. Linderniella species are native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Americas.

The genus includes around 100 species, many of which are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
