Lepanthes scalaris

Lepanthes scalaris, commonly known as the scallop orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant features a short, stocky rhizome with several erect, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are showy, with a distinctive scalloped lip and a range of colors from white to pale pink.

Lepanthes scalaris is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
