Lomatogonium rotatum var. aurantiacum

Lomatogonium rotatum var. aurantiacum, commonly known as the orangeleaf lomatogonium, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its bright orange to red leaves, which are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in arid landscapes.
