Lycopodium clavatum var. robustius

Lycopodium clavatum var. robustius, commonly known as the club moss, is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its dense, compact growth habit.

It features scale-like leaves arranged in whorls around the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces spore-bearing cones, which are typically clustered at the tips of the branches. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland areas.

This variety is known for its hardy nature and ability to form dense mats, making it a common ornamental and ecological plant in suitable habitats.
