Lychnis ostenfeldii

Lychnis ostenfeldii, commonly known as the Ostenfeld's campion, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold climates. It has a low, spreading growth habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves.

Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance in high-altitude ecosystems.
