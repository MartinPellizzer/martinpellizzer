Lepanthopsis legadensis

Lepanthopsis legadensis, commonly known as the Guatemalan orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments, relying on its roots for nutrient absorption. Its leaves are narrow and strap-like, adapted for efficient water retention.

Lepanthopsis legadensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
