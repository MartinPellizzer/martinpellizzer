Lycium durispina

Lycium durispina, commonly known as the spiny lycium, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces red berries that are edible and used in traditional medicine. Lycium durispina thrives in poor soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well adapted to harsh environments.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
