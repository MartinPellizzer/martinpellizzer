Lotus suffruticosus

Lotus suffruticosus, commonly known as the creeping lotus, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its woody stems that grow close to the ground, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture.

Lotus suffruticosus is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny conditions.
