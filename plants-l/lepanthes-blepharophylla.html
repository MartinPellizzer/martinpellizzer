Lepanthes blepharophylla

Lepanthes blepharophylla, commonly known as the red-tipped orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, red-tipped petals and is often found growing in shaded, humid environments. The plant typically produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom during the wet season. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the forest floor, and it thrives in well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Lepanthes blepharophylla is a popular species among orchid enthusiasts due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
