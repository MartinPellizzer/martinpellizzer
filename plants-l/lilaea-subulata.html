Lilaea subulata

Lilaea subulata, commonly known as the slender lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and delicate, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pale pink, bloom in late summer and early autumn.

Lilaea subulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
