Lonicera praeflorens var. japonica

Lonicera praeflorens var. japonica, commonly known as the Japanese honeysuckle, is a deciduous climbing vine native to East Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces fragrant, tubular yellow-orange flowers in early spring. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. It is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and floral displays.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions if not managed properly.
