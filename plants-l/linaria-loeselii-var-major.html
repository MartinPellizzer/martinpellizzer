Linaria loeselii var. major

Linaria loeselii var. major, commonly known as the greater yellow toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils. It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in harsh, alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
