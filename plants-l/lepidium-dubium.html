Lepidium dubium

Lepidium dubium, commonly known as wild cress, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, white or pink flowers and its branched, often hairy stems. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is found in various parts of Europe and Asia. Its leaves are alternate, toothed, and have a peppery aroma when crushed.

Lepidium dubium is also known for its edible leaves and seeds, which have been used in traditional cuisine and herbal remedies.
