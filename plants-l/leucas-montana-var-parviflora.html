Leucas montana var. parviflora

Leucas montana var. parviflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved leucas, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its small, pale blue to purple flowers and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
