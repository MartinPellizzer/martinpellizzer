Litsea cassiifolia

Litsea cassiifolia, commonly known as the fragrant lauraceae or sweet pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate, elliptic to ovate leaves that are often aromatic when crushed. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by small, round, yellowish fruits that are edible. Its ornamental value is enhanced by its pleasant scent and attractive foliage.

Litsea cassiifolia is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and fragrance.
