Liparis platyphylla

Liparis platyphylla, commonly known as the flat-leaf orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, flat leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and grasslands. Its roots are often rhizomatous, allowing it to spread and colonize new areas.

Botanically, it belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitats.
