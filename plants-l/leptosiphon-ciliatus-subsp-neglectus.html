Leptosiphon ciliatus subsp. neglectus

Leptosiphon ciliatus subsp.

neglectus, commonly known as the desert bluebell, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States. It is a perennial herb with slender, branching stems that often grow close to the ground.

The plant produces small, delicate blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are narrow, hairy, and arranged oppositely along the stems.

It is adapted to survive in dry, sandy soils and is often found in desert scrub and rocky habitats.
