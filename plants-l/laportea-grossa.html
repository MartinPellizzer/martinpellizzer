Laportea grossa

Laportea grossa, commonly known as broadleaf goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its large, yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are hairy and often have a distinctive purple tint on the underside. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Laportea grossa is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
