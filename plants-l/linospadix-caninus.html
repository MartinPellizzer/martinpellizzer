Linospadix caninus

Linospadix caninus, commonly known as the canine spathiphyllum, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence composed of a spadix and a spathe. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically green with a waxy surface. Its flowers are small and clustered on the spadix, which is usually yellow or green in color.

Linospadix caninus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and air-purifying properties.
