Lespedeza thunbergii f. angustifolia

Lespedeza thunbergii f. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf Chinese clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
