Lactuca roborowskii

Lactuca roborowskii, commonly known as the Siberian endive, is a perennial plant native to the colder regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and have a slightly bitter taste. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, followed by slender, ribbed seeds. Lactuca roborowskii is often found in moist, calcareous soils and can thrive in temperate to subarctic climates.

It is valued for its edible leaves, which are used in traditional cuisine and herbal medicine.
