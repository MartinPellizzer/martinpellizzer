Lobelia salviifolia

Lobelia salviifolia, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and slightly serrated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

Lobelia salviifolia is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
