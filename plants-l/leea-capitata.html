Leea capitata

Leea capitata, commonly known as the Cape jasmine, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

The plant is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils.
