Lythrum castellanum

Lythrum castellanum, commonly known as red root, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lythraceae family and is characterized by its upright, herbaceous stems that often turn reddish with age. The plant produces showy spikes of pink to purple flowers, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Lythrum castellanum is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
