Luzula badia

Luzula badia, commonly known as the purple moor grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale blue to purple flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated.
