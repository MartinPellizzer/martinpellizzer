Lasianthus inodorus subsp. montigenus

Lasianthus inodorus subsp.

montigenus, commonly known as the mountain lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, tubular flowers.

The plant typically grows in shaded, moist habitats such as mountain forests. It has small, clustered inflorescences that produce vibrant blue or violet flowers.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
