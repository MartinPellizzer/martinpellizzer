Lunasia

Lunasia, commonly known as the moonflower, is a flowering plant that belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night and close during the day. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with woody stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are tubular and have a unique, moon-like appearance, which gives it its common name.

Lunasia is native to parts of Africa and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nocturnal beauty.
