Leptoglottis diffusa

Leptoglottis diffusa, commonly known as the broad-leafed fern, is a species native to the wet tropical regions of New Zealand.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its large, leathery, and often deeply lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its fronds are usually arching and can reach lengths of up to 1.5 meters.

The botanical features of Leptoglottis diffusa make it an important component of New Zealand's native flora and a subject of ecological interest.
