Leonurus cardiaca subsp. intermedius

Leonurus cardiaca subsp.

intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate heartworts, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves.

The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional medicine.

This subspecies is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.
