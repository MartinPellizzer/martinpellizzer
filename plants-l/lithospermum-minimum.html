Lithospermum minimum

Lithospermum minimum, commonly known as small rock pepper, is a flowering plant native to dry, rocky habitats in the western United States.

It is a perennial herb with slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a small, dry capsule containing several seeds.

Lithospermum minimum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in arid environments.
