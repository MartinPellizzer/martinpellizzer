Leontopodium leontopodinum

Leontopodium leontopodinum, commonly known as the edelweiss, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Alps.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly white flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant contains a unique compound called leontopodin, which is responsible for its distinctive color and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, harsh environments.

Due to its beauty and cultural significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and protected in the wild.
