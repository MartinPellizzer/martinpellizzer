Lewisia minima

Lewisia minima, commonly known as the dwarf Lewisia, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Montiaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, or white during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Lewisia minima is often cultivated in rock gardens and is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
