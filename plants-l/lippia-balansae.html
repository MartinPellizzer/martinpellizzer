Lippia balansae

Lippia balansae, commonly known as Mexican blue sage, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and spikes of blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Lippia balansae is also a source of nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
