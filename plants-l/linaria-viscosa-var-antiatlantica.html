Linaria viscosa var. antiatlantica

Linaria viscosa var. antiatlantica, commonly known as the sticky toadflax, is a perennial herb native to the Canary Islands.

It features slender, branching stems covered in sticky hairs, which give it its common name. The plant produces bright yellow, spurred flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and opposite, often covered with a fine layer of wax.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.
