Lightfootia abyssinica var. cinerea

Lightfootia abyssinica var. cinerea, commonly known as the Abyssinian lightfoot, is a rare and striking plant native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It is a succulent shrub characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth. Its unique appearance makes it a sought-after species among collectors and botanists.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered endangered and requires careful conservation efforts.
