Luma myrcioides

Luma myrcioides, commonly known as the wax tree, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows in humid, cool climates at high elevations. The plant is characterized by its smooth, waxy leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. It produces edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine and local cuisine.

The wax tree plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
