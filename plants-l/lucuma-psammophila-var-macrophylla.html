Lucuma psammophila var. macrophylla

Lucuma psammophila var. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved lucuma, is a species native to the arid regions of northern Chile.

It is a member of the Santalaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and yellow-orange berries. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun exposure. Its fruit is edible, with a sweet, nutty flavor, and is often used in local cuisine.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
