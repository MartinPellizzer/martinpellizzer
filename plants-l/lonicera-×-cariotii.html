Lonicera × cariotii

Lonicera × cariotii, commonly known as the yellow honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, red berries in the fall. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Lonicera × cariotii is also valued for its role in attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
