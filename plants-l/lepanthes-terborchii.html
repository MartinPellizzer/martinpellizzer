Lepanthes terborchii

Lepanthes terborchii, commonly known as the pink lady's tresses, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and striking pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and stream banks. It has a short rhizome and produces small, clustered flowers that are often fragrant.

Lepanthes terborchii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with the right conditions.
