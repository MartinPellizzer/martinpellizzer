Lathyrus linnaei var. hispanicus

Lathyrus linnaei var. hispanicus, commonly known as the Spanish vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with slender stems that twine around supports. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are pinnate, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

This variety is native to southern Europe and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.
