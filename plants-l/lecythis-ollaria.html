Lecythis ollaria

Lecythis ollaria, commonly known as the Amazonian cheese tree, is a tropical tree native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and produces a heavy, creamy fruit that resembles a cheese wheel, hence its common name. The fruit contains a hard, woody seed surrounded by a fleshy, edible pulp. The tree is valued for its unique fruit and is often found in the understory of dense rainforest canopies.

Its botanical features make it an important species in the biodiversity of the Amazon region.
