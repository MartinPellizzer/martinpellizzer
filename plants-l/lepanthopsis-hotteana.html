Lepanthopsis hotteana

Lepanthopsis hotteana, commonly known as the Hotte's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a compact rosette, while the flowers are borne on slender peduncles.

This orchid thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
