Leucaena esculenta subsp. matudae

Leucaena esculenta subsp.

matudae, commonly known as Matudae leucaena, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Central and South America. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine and as forage for livestock.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is relatively drought-tolerant.

Matudae leucaena is also valued for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
