Lathyrus macrorrhizus var. latifolius

Lathyrus macrorrhizus var. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf sweet pea, is a perennial legume native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with broad, ovate leaflets and produces clusters of pea-like flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white. The plant has a robust root system that helps stabilize soil and fix atmospheric nitrogen, benefiting surrounding vegetation. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and streambanks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological restoration and ornamental gardening.
