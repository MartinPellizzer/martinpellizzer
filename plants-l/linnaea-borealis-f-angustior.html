Linnaea borealis f. angustior

Linnaea borealis f. angustior, commonly known as the narrowleaf twinflower, is a variant of the twinflower plant native to boreal and subarctic regions.

It is a low-growing evergreen shrub characterized by its slender, angular stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces delicate, fragrant white flowers in the spring, which are clustered in pairs. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded forest environments.

This subspecies is notable for its unique morphology and ecological role in cold climate ecosystems.
