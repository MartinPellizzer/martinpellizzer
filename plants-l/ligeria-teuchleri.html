Ligeria teuchleri

Ligeria teuchleri, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Central America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces vibrant blue or purple flowers that open in the morning and close in the evening. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Ligeria teuchleri is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
