Leymus racemosus subsp. klokovii

Leymus racemosus subsp.

klokovii, commonly known as the Klovkov's wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, blue-green blades that are often hairy along the edges.

The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, two-lobed spikelets. It thrives in dry, sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
