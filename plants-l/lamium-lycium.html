Lamium lycium

Lamium lycium, commonly known as red deadnettle, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically hairy and have a reddish or purple hue. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Lamium lycium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
