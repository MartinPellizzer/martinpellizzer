Lauro-cerasus wallichii

Lauro-cerasus wallichii, commonly known as the Kashmir cherry or Himalayan cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Prunus genus and is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in mountainous regions at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
