Lupinus campestris-florum

Lupinus campestris-florum, commonly known as the field lupine, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of pea-like flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, hairy pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Lupinus campestris-florum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plants.
