Lactuca spicata f. integrifolia

Lactuca spicata f. integrifolia, commonly known as the smoothleaf lettuce, is a botanical variant of the common lettuce species.

It is characterized by its smooth, undivided leaves that are typically darker green in color. This form is often found in temperate regions and thrives in cool climates. The plant produces small, pale flowers arranged in a spike-like inflorescence.

It is valued in horticulture for its crisp, edible leaves and is often cultivated as a leafy vegetable.
