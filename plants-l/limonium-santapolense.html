Limonium santapolense

Limonium santapolense, commonly known as the Santapole sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Limonium santapolense is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
