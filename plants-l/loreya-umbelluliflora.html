Loreya umbelluliflora

Loreya umbelluliflora, commonly known as the white sand lily, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy, well-drained soils. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, white or pale pink flowers in a cluster at the end of each stem. Its flowers are often arranged in an umbel-like structure, giving the species its scientific name.

Loreya umbelluliflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
