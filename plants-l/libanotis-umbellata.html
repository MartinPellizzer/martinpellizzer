Libanotis umbellata

Libanotis umbellata, commonly known as the white bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters atop slender stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and it produces small, oval fruits that contain seeds.

Libanotis umbellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
