Liquidambar chinensis

Liquidambar chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese sweet gum, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Aceraceae and is characterized by its alternate, palmately lobed leaves that turn vibrant yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, spherical fruits that split open to release seeds. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, often developing shallow fissures with age.

Liquidambar chinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens and urban landscapes.
