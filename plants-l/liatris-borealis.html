Liatris borealis

Liatris borealis, commonly known as the northern blazingstar, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, spiky flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and meadows. Its tall, upright stems bear clusters of lavender to pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

Liatris borealis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
