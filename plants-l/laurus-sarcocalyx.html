Laurus sarcocalyx

Laurus sarcocalyx, commonly known as the Chinese bay leaf, is a flowering plant native to China and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like true laurel. The plant is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. Its leaves are often used in traditional Chinese medicine and as a culinary spice.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental and medicinal value.
