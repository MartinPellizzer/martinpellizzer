Limeum arabicum

Limeum arabicum, commonly known as the Arabian lime tree, is a species native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a small evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a fine, white powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss. Its flowers are small and yellow, often appearing in clusters, and it produces small, round fruits that are edible.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to thrive in harsh climates.
