Lupinus lepidus var. ramosus

Lupinus lepidus var. ramosus, commonly known as the silver lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and erect, sparsely branched stems. The plant produces clusters of pea-like flowers in shades of purple and blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Lupinus lepidus var. ramosus is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
