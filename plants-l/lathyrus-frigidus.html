Lathyrus frigidus

Lathyrus frigidus, commonly known as the arctic pea, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is a perennial herb that grows in rocky and alpine environments, typically in the northern hemisphere. The plant has slender stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Lathyrus frigidus is adapted to cold climates and is often found in tundra and mountainous areas.
