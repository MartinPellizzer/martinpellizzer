Lieberkuhna

Lieberkuhna, commonly known as the "garden orchid," is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking, showy flowers in shades of violet and blue. The plant is characterized by its complex inflorescence, which consists of multiple spike-like clusters of small, tubular flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.

Due to its ornamental value and resilience, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by garden enthusiasts.
