Lathyrus hirticarpus

Lathyrus hirticarpus, commonly known as the hairy pod vetch, is a perennial legume native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically purple or pink in color. Its fruit is a hairy, inflated pod that contains several small, bean-shaped seeds.

Lathyrus hirticarpus is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
