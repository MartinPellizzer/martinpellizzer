Libertia grandiflora

Libertia grandiflora, commonly known as the golden lily, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense rosette, and it produces tall, slender stems that bear showy, yellow-orange flowers. The plant is characterized by its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens. Its blooms appear in late spring to early summer, adding a vibrant splash of color to the landscape.

Libertia grandiflora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
