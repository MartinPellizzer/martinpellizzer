Liparis aaronii

Liparis aaronii, commonly known as Aaron's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and along stream banks. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its delicate appearance and relatively easy care requirements.

Liparis aaronii is a rare species that thrives in specific environmental conditions, making it a unique subject for botanical study.
