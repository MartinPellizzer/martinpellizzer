Laurus aurantiodora

Laurus aurantiodora, commonly known as the Brazilian laurel, is a species of evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other well-known plants like bay laurel and cinnamon. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall and has glossy, elliptical leaves that are aromatic when crushed. Its flowers are small and clustered, and it produces berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
