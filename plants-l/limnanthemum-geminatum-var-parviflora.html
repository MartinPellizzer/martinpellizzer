Limnanthemum geminatum var. parviflora

Limnanthemum geminatum var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered marsh mallow, is a herbaceous perennial native to wetland environments in southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped, succulent leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, adapted to retain moisture in its moist habitat. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers with five petals, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are typically borne on slender stems that rise from a basal rosette of leaves.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in water gardens or bog gardens.
