Lonchocarpus rugosus subsp. apricus

Lonchocarpus rugosus subsp.

apricus, commonly known as the roughleaf sensitiveplant, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces distinctive yellow flowers followed by pods that contain kidney-shaped seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential medicinal research.
