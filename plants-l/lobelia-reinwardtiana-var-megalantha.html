Lobelia reinwardtiana var. megalantha

Lobelia reinwardtiana var. megalantha, commonly known as the giant lobelia, is a striking perennial plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 3 meters, adorned with clusters of small, tubular blue or violet flowers. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous areas with well-drained soil. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with specific climatic conditions.
