Lobivia schreiteri var. stilowiana

Lobivia schreiteri var. stilowiana, commonly known as the Stilowiana Lobivia, is a compact, low-growing succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It features dense, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces tall, branched stems that bear clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange. It is well adapted to drought conditions, storing water in its thick leaves and stems.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth, it is a popular choice among cactus and succulent enthusiasts.
