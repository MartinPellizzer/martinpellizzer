Lantana chiapasensis var. parvifolia

Lantana chiapasensis var. parvifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lantana, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to yellow and orange. The plant has opposite, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
