Lolium elegans

Lolium elegans, commonly known as slender ryegrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and slender, erect stems that often grow in dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. Lolium elegans is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, where it thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Its rapid growth and high nutritional value make it a valuable forage crop in agricultural settings.
