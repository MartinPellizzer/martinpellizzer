Leucanthemum vulgare subsp. barrelieri

Leucanthemum vulgare subsp.

barrelieri, commonly known as the Barrelier's white ragwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with a central cluster of yellow disc florets.

It is relatively hardy and can thrive in a variety of soil types, though it prefers well-drained conditions.
