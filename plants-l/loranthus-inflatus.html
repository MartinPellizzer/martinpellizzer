Loranthus inflatus

Loranthus inflatus, commonly known as the inflated mistletoe, is a parasitic plant belonging to the Loranthaceae family.

It grows on various host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a fleshy, red berry that contains several seeds.

The species is notable for its unique morphology and ecological role as a hemiparasite.
