Lunaria halimifolia

Lunaria halimifolia, commonly known as the moonflower or silverleaf moonflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Violaceae family and is characterized by its striking, pale blue or violet flowers that open at night. The plant produces distinctive seed pods that resemble small, round moons, which is where its common name originates. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Lunaria halimifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
