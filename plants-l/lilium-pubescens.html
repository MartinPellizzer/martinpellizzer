Lilium pubescens

Lilium pubescens, commonly known as the woolly lily, is a species of flowering plant in the lily family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is characterized by its hairy, or pubescent, stems and leaves. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink or red. It typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and prefers cool, temperate climates.

Lilium pubescens is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
