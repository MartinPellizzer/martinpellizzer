Lychnis patagonica

Lychnis patagonica, commonly known as the Patagonian pink, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the carnation family, Caryophyllaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-red flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to temperate climates. It produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Lychnis patagonica is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
