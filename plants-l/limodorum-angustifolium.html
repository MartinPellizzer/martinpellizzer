Limodorum angustifolium

Limodorum angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lily of the Nile, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a slender, upright stem that bears several whorled, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically purple or violet in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in suitable climates.
