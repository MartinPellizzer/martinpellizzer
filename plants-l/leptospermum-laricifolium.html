Leptospermum laricifolium

Leptospermum laricifolium, commonly known as the mountain tea tree, is a shrub native to Tasmania.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that resemble those of a fir tree. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring, followed by woody, cone-like fruits. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine and subalpine regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
