Lysimachia pauciflora

Lysimachia pauciflora, commonly known as the lesser yellow archangel, is a low-growing perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, linear to ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Lysimachia pauciflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
