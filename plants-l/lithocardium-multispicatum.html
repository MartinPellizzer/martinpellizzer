Lithocardium multispicatum

Lithocardium multispicatum, commonly known as the spiked stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and small, clustered leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in late spring, which are arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens.

Lithocardium multispicatum is valued for its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
