Lobivia schmiedcheniana

Lobivia schmiedcheniana, commonly known as the Schmiedchen's lobivia, is a succulent plant native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, branching structure with thick, fleshy stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. Its leaves are usually reduced to spines, and it is well adapted to arid and rocky environments.

Lobivia schmiedcheniana is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its hardy nature and ornamental appeal.
