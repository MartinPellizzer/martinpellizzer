Licuala fractiflexa

Licuala fractiflexa, commonly known as the fan palm or the banana palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including parts of Malaysia, Indonesia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and as thatching materials. It thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil and ample sunlight.

Licuala fractiflexa is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
