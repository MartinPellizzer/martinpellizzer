Ligularia yesoensis

Ligularia yesoensis, commonly known as the Japanese ligularia, is a perennial plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive wavy margin. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
