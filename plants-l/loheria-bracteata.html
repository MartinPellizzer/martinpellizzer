Loheria bracteata

Loheria bracteata, commonly known as the golden torch plant, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its vibrant yellow bracts that resemble torches. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in shades of orange and yellow.

Loheria bracteata is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
