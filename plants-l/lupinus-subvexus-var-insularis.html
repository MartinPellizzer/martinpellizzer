Lupinus subvexus var. insularis

Lupinus subvexus var. insularis, commonly known as the island lupine, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and is adapted to grow in well-drained, sandy soils. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, improving soil fertility.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
