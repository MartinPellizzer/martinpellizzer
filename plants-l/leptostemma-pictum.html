Leptostemma pictum

Leptostemma pictum, commonly known as the painted false Solomon's seal, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It features slender, arching stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers with a distinctive red stripe. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically red or black when mature. Its leaves are alternate, with a distinctive veining pattern that gives them a painted appearance.

It thrives in shady, moist woodlands and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits.
