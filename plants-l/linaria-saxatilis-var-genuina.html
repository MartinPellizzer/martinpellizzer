Linaria saxatilis var. genuina

Linaria saxatilis var. genuina, commonly known as the rock everlasting, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and produces clusters of small, bright yellow or orange flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to full sun exposure. Its leaves are typically narrow and linear, with a rough or hairy texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
