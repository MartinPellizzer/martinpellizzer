Ledebouria camerooniana

Ledebouria camerooniana, commonly known as the Cameroon bulbous lily, is a species of flowering plant native to Cameroon.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers during the warmer months. It is often found in rocky or limestone soils, thriving in well-drained conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
