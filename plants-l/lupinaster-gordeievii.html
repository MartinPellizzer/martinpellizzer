Lupinaster gordeievii

Lupinaster gordeievii, commonly known as the Gordeev's lupinaster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, butterfly-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in temperate to cold climates. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, are attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Lupinaster gordeievii is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
