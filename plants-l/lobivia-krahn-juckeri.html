Lobivia krahn-juckeri

Lobivia krahn-juckeri, commonly known as the Schlechter's lobivia, is a species of flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a small, compact cactus with a short, cylindrical stem and is known for its vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a dense covering of fine, white hairs, which helps it retain moisture in its arid environment. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xerophytic gardens.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is a sought-after plant among succulent enthusiasts.
