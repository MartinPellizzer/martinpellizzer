Lupinus annieae

Lupinus annieae, commonly known as Annie's lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and showy blue to purple flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces cylindrical seed pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Lupinus annieae is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
