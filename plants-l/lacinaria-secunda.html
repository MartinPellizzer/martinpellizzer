Lacinaria secunda

Lacinaria secunda, commonly known as the slender lichen, is a species of lichen that belongs to the family Pilocarpaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, branched thallus and a pale to yellowish coloration. The plant forms a symbiotic relationship between a green alga and a fungus, which allows it to thrive in moist, shaded environments. It is often found on rocks or bark in temperate regions.

Lacinaria secunda is relatively uncommon and is of interest to lichenologists due to its unique morphology and ecological niche.
