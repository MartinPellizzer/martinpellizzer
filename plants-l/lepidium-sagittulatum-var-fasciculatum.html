Lepidium sagittulatum var. fasciculatum

Lepidium sagittulatum var. fasciculatum, commonly known as the clustered wall pepper, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or disturbed soils and is characterized by its small, clustered inflorescences. Its leaves are alternate and pinnately divided, with a distinctive, fern-like appearance.

It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by tiny, round pods containing several seeds.
