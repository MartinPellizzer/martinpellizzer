Liparis bleyi

Liparis bleyi, commonly known as the Bley's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, often with a subtle fragrance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
