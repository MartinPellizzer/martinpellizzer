Lindernia oppositifolia

Lindernia oppositifolia, commonly known as the oppositeleaf lindernia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, with serrated edges. Its showy, tubular flowers are usually pink or violet, arranged in spikes at the ends of the stems.

Lindernia oppositifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
