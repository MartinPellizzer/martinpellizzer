Lucuma platyphylla

Lucuma platyphylla, commonly known as the golden apple or lucuma, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its thick, leathery leaves and showy yellow flowers. The fruit is oval-shaped, with a hard, smooth exterior and a sweet, nutty flavor inside. It belongs to the family Brassicaceae and is closely related to other cruciferous plants.

The plant is adapted to high-altitude environments and thrives in cool, temperate climates.
