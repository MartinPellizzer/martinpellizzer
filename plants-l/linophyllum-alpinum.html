Linophyllum alpinum

Linophyllum alpinum, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a rosette near the ground.

Linophyllum alpinum is adapted to cold climates and is often found in mountainous environments.
