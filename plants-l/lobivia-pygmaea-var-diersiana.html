Lobivia pygmaea var. diersiana

Lobivia pygmaea var. diersiana, commonly known as the Diersian Lobivia, is a compact cactus native to the arid regions of Argentina.

It features short, cylindrical stems that are typically greenish-gray in color, covered with small, white spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to drought conditions, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Due to its slow growth and unique appearance, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
