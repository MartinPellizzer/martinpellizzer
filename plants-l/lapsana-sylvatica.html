Lapsana sylvatica

Lapsana sylvatica, commonly known as common sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and can reach heights of up to 1 meter. It has a taproot system and produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
