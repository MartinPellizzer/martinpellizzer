Lindernia damblonii

Lindernia damblonii, commonly known as the Mexican flame violet, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Linderniaceae and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from violet to red, often with contrasting yellow markings. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Lindernia damblonii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
