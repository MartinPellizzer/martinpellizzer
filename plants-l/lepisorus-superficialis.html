Lepisorus superficialis

Lepisorus superficialis, commonly known as the creeping polypody, is a fern species characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and dense, mat-forming foliage.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices, where it thrives in humid conditions. The plant features small, lanceolate leaves with a finely divided structure, contributing to its ornamental appeal. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are borne on the underside of the fronds, typical of the Polypodiaceae family.

Lepisorus superficialis is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ability to colonize difficult soils.
