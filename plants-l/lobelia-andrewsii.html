Lobelia andrewsii

Lobelia andrewsii, commonly known as the Florida lobelia, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks.

Lobelia andrewsii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
