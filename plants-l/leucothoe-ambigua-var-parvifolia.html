Leucothoe ambigua var. parvifolia

Leucothoe ambigua var. parvifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf inkberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy on top with a lighter underside. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
