Lonchocarpus latisiliquus

Lonchocarpus latisiliquus, commonly known as the broad-silqued legume, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with alternating pairs of leaflets and produces showy, butterfly-shaped flowers. Its root system is known for containing toxic alkaloids, which have been studied for their potential medicinal properties.

The species is often found in moist, shaded habitats within tropical forests.
