Leobordea procumbens

Leobordea procumbens, commonly known as the creeping leobordea, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, succulent leaves that are often tinged with red or purple, adapting to arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in dry climates.
