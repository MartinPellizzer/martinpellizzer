Loranthus poecilobotrys

Loranthus poecilobotrys, commonly known as the African mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily in the savanna regions of Africa.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own carbohydrates through photosynthesis using the light it captures. The plant produces small, white or pale green berries that are often consumed by birds, which then disperse the seeds. Its life cycle involves a complex relationship with its host, often leading to reduced growth and vitality in the host tree.

Despite its parasitic nature, Loranthus poecilobotrys plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife.
