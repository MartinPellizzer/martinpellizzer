Licaria aurea

Licaria aurea, commonly known as golden licaria, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers, which bloom in clusters during the warmer months. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests or along stream banks. Its leaves are simple and alternate, with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy appearance.

Licaria aurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
