Lilium bulbiferum var. armeniacum

Lilium bulbiferum var. armeniacum, commonly known as Armenian tiger lily, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, adorned with large, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces bulbous structures called bulbils along its stems, which can be used for propagation. Its foliage consists of long, strap-like leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
