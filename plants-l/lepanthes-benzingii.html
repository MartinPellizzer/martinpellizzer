Lepanthes benzingii

Lepanthes benzingii, commonly known as the Benzing's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in the summer. The plant has a terrestrial growth habit and prefers moist, shaded environments in the understory of tropical forests. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with intricate patterns that attract specific pollinators.

Lepanthes benzingii is valued for its ornamental appeal and contributes to the biodiversity of its native habitat.
