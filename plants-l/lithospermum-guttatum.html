Lithospermum guttatum

Lithospermum guttatum, commonly known as the spotted lithosperm, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped with toothed margins. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and slopes.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
