Linaria sofiana

Linaria sofiana, commonly known as the Sicilian toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Linaria sofiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
