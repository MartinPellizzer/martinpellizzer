Linaria amethystea var. bipunctata

Linaria amethystea var. bipunctata, commonly known as the bi-colored toadflax, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bi-colored flowers that typically feature a purple upper lip and a yellow lower lip. The plant produces slender, branching stems and has narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
