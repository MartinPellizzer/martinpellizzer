Lathyrus peduncularis

Lathyrus peduncularis, commonly known as the slender vetch, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Lathyrus peduncularis is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
