Lavandula antineae subsp. marrana

Lavandula antineae subsp.

marrana, commonly known as the Marrana lavender, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun.

The plant produces fragrant, spike-like inflorescences of purple to pink flowers, which are used in aromatherapy and perfumery. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, with a strong aromatic scent.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
