Lavandula bipinnata var. nimmoi

Lavandula bipinnata var. nimmoi, commonly known as the Nimmo's lavender, is a compact, aromatic shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, silver-green leaves and produces spikes of purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is well adapted to dry, sunny conditions and thrives in well-drained soil. It is often used in ornamental gardening for its fragrance and visual appeal.

This variety is particularly valued for its compact growth habit and resilience to coastal climates.
