Lactuca siamensis

Lactuca siamensis, commonly known as Siam endive, is a species of lettuce native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its curly, dark green leaves that are often used in local cuisines. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, preferring moist and nutrient-rich soils. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants.

Lactuca siamensis is known for its mild, slightly bitter taste and is sometimes cultivated for both ornamental and edible purposes.
