Leucadendron elatum

Leucadendron elatum, commonly known as the King Protea, is a striking South African shrub belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy, spherical inflorescences composed of numerous white to pinkish flowers. The plant is native to the Western and Eastern Cape provinces of South Africa, where it thrives in well-drained, acidic soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating.

Leucadendron elatum is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience in various climates.
