Leucas montana var. angustifolia

Leucas montana var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf leucas, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant features slender, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy beneath. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pink or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
