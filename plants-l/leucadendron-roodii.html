Leucadendron roodii

Leucadendron roodii, commonly known as the silver tree, is a striking South African shrub or small tree characterized by its silvery-white, papery foliage.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is native to the Western and Eastern Cape provinces of South Africa. The plant is known for its long-lasting, cone-like inflorescences that range in color from yellow to red, depending on the season. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
