Lysionotus albidus

Lysionotus albidus, commonly known as the white lythrum, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. Lysionotus albidus thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
