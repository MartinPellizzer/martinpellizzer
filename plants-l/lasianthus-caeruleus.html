Lasianthus caeruleus

Lasianthus caeruleus, commonly known as the blue-flowered lasianthus, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
