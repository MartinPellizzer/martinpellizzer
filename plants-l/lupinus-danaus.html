Lupinus danaus

Lupinus danaus, commonly known as the Danaus lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Lupinus danaus is also noted for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility.
