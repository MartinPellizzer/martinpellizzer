Lonicera alpigena var. glehnii

Lonicera alpigena var. glehnii, commonly known as the alpine honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, ascending stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
