Lobelia orientalis

Lobelia orientalis, commonly known as the oriental lobelia, is a flowering plant native to regions of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
