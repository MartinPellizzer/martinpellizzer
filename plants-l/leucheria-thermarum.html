Leucheria thermarum

Leucheria thermarum, commonly known as the hot spring leech plant, is a rare and unique botanical species found in geothermal areas.

It is characterized by its specialized adaptations to high-temperature environments, thriving in mineral-rich waters. The plant exhibits a filamentous structure with specialized cells that allow it to absorb nutrients from the surrounding thermal water. Its growth is closely tied to the chemical composition of its habitat, making it an indicator of geothermal activity.

Due to its limited distribution, it is of interest to researchers studying extremophile organisms.
