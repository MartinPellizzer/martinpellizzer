Lavandula angustifolia f. albiflora

Lavandula angustifolia f. albiflora, commonly known as the white flowering lavender, is a cultivar of the common lavender plant.

It is characterized by its narrow, gray-green leaves and its distinctive white or pale pink flowers. This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens for its fragrance and visual appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for Mediterranean climates.

The plant is also valued for its essential oils, which are used in aromatherapy and perfumery.
