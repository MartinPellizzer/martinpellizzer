Lobelia siphilitica var. ludoviciana

Lobelia siphilitica var. ludoviciana, commonly known as the Louisiana lobelia, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, tubular blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and has opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, rich soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its striking flowers attract pollinators such as butterflies and hummingbirds.
