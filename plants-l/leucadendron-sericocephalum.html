Leucadendron sericocephalum

Leucadendron sericocephalum, commonly known as the silver-leaf candle bush, is a striking shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage and tall, candle-like yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is part of the Proteaceae family, known for its adaptive features to nutrient-poor soils. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a valuable addition to ornamental horticulture.
