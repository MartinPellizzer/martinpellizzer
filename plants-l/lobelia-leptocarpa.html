Lobelia leptocarpa

Lobelia leptocarpa, commonly known as slenderleaf lobelia, is a herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small, tubular, and usually blue or purple, arranged in clusters at the tips of the stems.

Lobelia leptocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
