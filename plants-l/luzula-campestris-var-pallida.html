Luzula campestris var. pallida

Luzula campestris var. pallida, commonly known as the pale wood rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features grass-like leaves that are narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green coloration. Its flowering stems bear small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a dense spike.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partially shaded conditions.
