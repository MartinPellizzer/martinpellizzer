Lathyrus latifolius subsp. heterophyllus

Lathyrus latifolius subsp.

heterophyllus, commonly known as the broad-leafed peavine, is a perennial legume native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. Its stems are often hairy, and it produces small, elongated pods containing kidney-shaped seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
