Lycopus europaeus var. sinuatus

Lycopus europaeus var. sinuatus, commonly known as narrow-leaved gypsyweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a sinuate margin. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers in clusters, which bloom from June to August. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the mint family, Lamiaceae.
