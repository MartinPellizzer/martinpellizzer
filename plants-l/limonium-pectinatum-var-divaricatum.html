Limonium pectinatum var. divaricatum

Limonium pectinatum var. divaricatum, commonly known as the slender sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, spiny seed pods that mature in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or disturbed sites.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and long-lasting blooms.
