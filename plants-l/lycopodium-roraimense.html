Lycopodium roraimense

Lycopodium roraimense, commonly known as the Roraima clubmoss, is a rare and ancient plant species belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, scale-like leaves that form dense, mat-like growths. Native to the tepuis of Venezuela and Brazil, this plant thrives in the unique, nutrient-poor soils of these high-altitude plateaus. Its spore-producing structures, called strobili, are clustered at the tips of its branches, aiding in its reproductive strategy.

Due to its restricted habitat and fragile ecosystem, Lycopodium roraimense is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
