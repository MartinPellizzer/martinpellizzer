Lepidium karataviense

Lepidium karataviense, commonly known as the Karatav Valley cress, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant is characterized by its small, white or yellowish flowers and slender, branched stems. It typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Lepidium karataviense is often found in mountainous regions and is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological niche.
