Lysicarpus ternifolius

Lysicarpus ternifolius, commonly known as the three-leaved lychee, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into pods containing edible seeds. Its botanical structure features a woody stem and compound leaves with three leaflets.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
