Leochilus sanguinolentus

Leochilus sanguinolentus, commonly known as the blood-red orchid, is a strikingly colorful orchid species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy texture. The plant produces showy, red-and-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the humid environment.

This orchid is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
