Lagoseris nemausensis

Lagoseris nemausensis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lagoseris, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The flowers are small and yellow, arranged in a compact head, contributing to its ornamental value in certain horticultural settings.
