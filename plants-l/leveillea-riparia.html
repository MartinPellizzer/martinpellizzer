Leveillea riparia

Leveillea riparia, commonly known as the riverbank lily, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands and riverbanks of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers with showy, spurred petals. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Leveillea riparia is adapted to moist environments and plays a role in stabilizing riverbanks through its extensive root system.
