Lepidagathis hyalina var. lophostachyoides

Lepidagathis hyalina var. lophostachyoides, commonly known as the silver mistflower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, silver-white foliage and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, reflective hairs, giving it a unique appearance.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
