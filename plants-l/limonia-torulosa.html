Limonia torulosa

Limonia torulosa, commonly known as the sour orange, is a small evergreen tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The fruit is typically orange in color, with a thick rind and a sour, acidic taste. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of citric acid.

The plant is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
