Lepanthes caudatisepala

Lepanthes caudatisepala, commonly known as the long-sepaled orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated sepals that give it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are usually small and fragrant, with a variety of colors including white, yellow, and pink.

Lepanthes caudatisepala is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and unique floral structure.
