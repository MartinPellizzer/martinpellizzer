Leucophyta lessingii

Leucophyta lessingii, commonly known as the silverleaf sunflower, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs that reflect sunlight. The plant produces bright yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of smaller, tubular flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Leucophyta lessingii is adapted to drought conditions, making it a hardy and resilient species in its native habitat.
