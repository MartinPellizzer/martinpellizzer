Lonchocarpus peninsularis

Lonchocarpus peninsularis, commonly known as the Florida senna, is a leguminous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces distinctive, woody pods that contain small, round seeds. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and habitat restoration.
