Lilium elisabethiae

Lilium elisabethiae, commonly known as the Elisabeth Lily, is a rare and striking species of lily native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers with a distinctive red-orange throat and prominent stamens. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with broad, sword-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy. It prefers cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded, forested areas.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is highly sought after by botanists and horticulturists.
