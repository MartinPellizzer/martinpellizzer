Lobelia alyssifolia

Lobelia alyssifolia, commonly known as the lesser lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are usually blue or violet in color, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Lobelia alyssifolia thrives in moist, woodland environments and is often found in shaded, well-drained soils.
