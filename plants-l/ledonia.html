Ledonia

Ledonia, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its translucent, waxy leaves and delicate, bioluminescent flowers that glow faintly in the dark. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found growing in shaded, misty forests. Its unique botanical features make it a subject of fascination for botanists and conservationists alike.

Due to habitat loss, Ledonia is considered critically endangered, highlighting the need for its protection.
