Lathyrus cyprius

Lathyrus cyprius, commonly known as Cyprus vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces clusters of small, pea-like flowers in shades of pink or purple. The plant has slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry environments.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
