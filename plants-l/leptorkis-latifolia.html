Leptorkis latifolia

Leptorkis latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf leptorkis, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are usually arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption in moist, well-drained soils.

Leptorkis latifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
