Listera

Listera, commonly known as ghost plant or spring beauty, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Primulaceae.

It consists of about 40 species, most of which are found in temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. The plants typically have small, heart-shaped leaves and produce clusters of white or pink flowers. They are often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky areas.

Listera species are popular in horticulture for their delicate appearance and early spring blooming habit.
