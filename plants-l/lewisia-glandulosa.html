Lewisia glandulosa

Lewisia glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular lewisia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in small glands. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in arid landscapes.
