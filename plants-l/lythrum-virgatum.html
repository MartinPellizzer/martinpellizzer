Lythrum virgatum

Lythrum virgatum, commonly known as purple loosestrife, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its upright stems and showy spikes of purple to pink flowers that bloom from June to September. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are opposite along the stem and are covered in fine hairs. Its roots are fibrous and can spread aggressively, making it an invasive species in some regions.

Lythrum virgatum is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams.
