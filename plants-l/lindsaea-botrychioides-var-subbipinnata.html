Lindsaea botrychioides var. subbipinnata

Lindsaea botrychioides var. subbipinnata, commonly known as the silver hair fern, is a tropical epiphytic fern native to regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant typically grows on tree trunks or branches, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its fronds are pinnately compound, with secondary pinnae that are often sub-bipinnate, contributing to its intricate foliage structure.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
