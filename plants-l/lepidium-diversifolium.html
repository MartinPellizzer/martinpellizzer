Lepidium diversifolium

Lepidium diversifolium, commonly known as mountain peppergrass, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, often in moist, well-drained soils. It features small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

Its leaves are variable in shape, ranging from lance-shaped to deeply lobed, contributing to its common name.
