Libocedrus plumosa

Libocedrus plumosa, commonly known as the silver fir or mountain fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its feathery, needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color and covered in a waxy coating. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing some resistance to fire and pests.

Libocedrus plumosa is often found in high-elevation forests, where it plays a key role in maintaining ecological balance.
