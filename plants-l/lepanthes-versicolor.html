Lepanthes versicolor

Lepanthes versicolor, commonly known as the variable orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its strikingly colorful flowers, which often display a mix of white, yellow, and green hues. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with intricate petal structures that aid in pollination by insects.

Lepanthes versicolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
