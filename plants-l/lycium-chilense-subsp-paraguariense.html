Lycium chilense subsp. paraguariense

Lycium chilense subsp.

paraguariense, commonly known as the Paraguayan boxthorn, is a shrub native to South America, particularly found in Argentina, Bolivia, and Paraguay. It belongs to the Solanaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers.

The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh climates.
