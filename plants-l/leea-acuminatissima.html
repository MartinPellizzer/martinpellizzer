Leea acuminatissima

Leea acuminatissima, commonly known as the narrow-leaved leeah, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Leeaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptic to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, often with a faintly fragrant scent. Its fruits are small, woody capsules that split open to release seeds.

Leea acuminatissima is often found in tropical and subtropical forests, growing in shaded, moist environments.
