Lepidosperma angustatum var. curvispiculum

Lepidosperma angustatum var. curvispiculum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sedgeland sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wetland or swampy environments, where it forms dense clumps with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowering spikes are usually cylindrical and bear small, inconspicuous flowers.

This variety is distinguished by its curved, spike-like inflorescence and narrow, linear leaves.
