Leucophae infernalis

Leucophae infernalis, commonly known as the Infernal Whiteleaf, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the volcanic regions of Southeast Asia.

It thrives in nutrient-poor, acidic soils and is characterized by its pale, almost translucent leaves that shimmer under indirect light. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pale yellow and white, which bloom only during the night. Its roots are highly specialized, forming symbiotic relationships with specific fungi to absorb essential nutrients.

Due to its unique adaptations and limited distribution, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
