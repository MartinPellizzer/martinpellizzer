Luzula multiflora subsp. taurica

Luzula multiflora subsp.

taurica, commonly known as the Taurian woodrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky slopes.

The plant features grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and arranged in a dense, tufted cluster. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like clusters of white to pale blue flowers, which bloom in early summer.

The subspecies is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value in shaded gardens.
