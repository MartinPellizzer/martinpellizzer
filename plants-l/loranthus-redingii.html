Loranthus redingii

Loranthus redingii, commonly known as the reding's mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily in the genus Eucalyptus.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using sunlight captured by its leaves. The plant has small, leathery leaves and white, clustered flowers that bloom in spring. Its seeds are dispersed by birds that consume the fleshy, red berries.

This species is native to Australia and is often found in woodland and forest environments.
