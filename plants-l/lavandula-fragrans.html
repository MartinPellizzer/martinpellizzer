Lavandula fragrans

Lavandula fragrans, commonly known as English lavender, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, spike-like inflorescences composed of numerous purple or pinkish flowers. The plant has narrow, gray-green foliage that is covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal use.

The essential oils extracted from its flowers are widely used in aromatherapy, perfumery, and as a natural insect repellent.
