Lampropappus eremanthifolia

Lampropappus eremanthifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the fall. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, which contribute to its distinctive appearance.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in poor soils.
