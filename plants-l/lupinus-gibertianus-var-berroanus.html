Lupinus gibertianus var. berroanus

Lupinus gibertianus var. berroanus, commonly known as the Berroan lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and clusters of pea-like, violet-blue flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces cylindrical pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed sites.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in nitrogen fixation.
