Lophostachys laxifolia

Lophostachys laxifolia, commonly known as the desert sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing shrub with narrow, gray-green leaves that are adapted to arid environments. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in spring. Its fibrous root system helps it survive in sandy or rocky soils.

Lophostachys laxifolia is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
