Lampranthus serpens

Lampranthus serpens, commonly known as the snake's head flower, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, upright stems that produce vibrant, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, requiring minimal water.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
