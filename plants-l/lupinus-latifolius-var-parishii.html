Lupinus latifolius var. parishii

Lupinus latifolius var. parishii, commonly known as the Parish's broadleaf lupine, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of blue to violet pea-like flowers. The leaves are compound, consisting of several broad, oval-shaped leaflets. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and serves as a food source for various pollinators.
