Lespedeza violacea var. reticulata

Lespedeza violacea var. reticulata, commonly known as the reticulated lespedeza, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open woodlands and disturbed soils. The plant features small, violet-colored flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are alternate and trifoliate, with a hairy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in ecological restoration projects.
