Lobelia serpens var. bicolor

Lobelia serpens var. bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored snake's head, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of blue and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
