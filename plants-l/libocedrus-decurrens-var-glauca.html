Libocedrus decurrens var. glauca

Libocedrus decurrens var. glauca, commonly known as the blue cedar, is a compact evergreen shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, scale-like leaves that are typically a bluish-green color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers followed by edible, red berries that are a food source for various wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

The varietal name 'glauca' refers to the bluish hue of its foliage, which is a key botanical feature.
