Lessingianthus argyrophyllus

Lessingianthus argyrophyllus, commonly known as the silver-leaf sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features silver-white foliage due to dense pubescence, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. Its bright yellow ray flowers are surrounded by a cluster of tubular florets, typical of the genus.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
