Limnia bracteosa

Limnia bracteosa, commonly known as the bracteosa water flea, is a small aquatic plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, floating leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and marshes.

Limnia bracteosa plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and small invertebrates.
