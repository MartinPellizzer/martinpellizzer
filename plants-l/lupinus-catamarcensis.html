Lupinus catamarcensis

Lupinus catamarcensis, commonly known as the Catamarca lupin, is a flowering plant native to Argentina.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces spikes of violet to purple flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Lupinus catamarcensis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of protein in some regions.
