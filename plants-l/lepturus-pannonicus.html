Lepturus pannonicus

Lepturus pannonicus, commonly known as the Pannonian thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands. It has a robust root system that allows it to thrive in nutrient-poor environments.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
