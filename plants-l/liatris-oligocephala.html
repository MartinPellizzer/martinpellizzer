Liatris oligocephala

Liatris oligocephala, commonly known as narrowleaf blazingstar, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, spiky inflorescences. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Liatris oligocephala thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

It is a perennial herb that plays an important role in supporting local ecosystems.
