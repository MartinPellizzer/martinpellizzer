Lychnis triflora

Lychnis triflora, commonly known as the three-flowered catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes other flowering plants such as the carnations and snapdragons. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it often grows in moist, open habitats.

Lychnis triflora is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
