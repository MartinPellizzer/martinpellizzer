Lonchocarpus minor

Lonchocarpus minor, commonly known as the lesser urara, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of the Americas, including parts of South America and the Caribbean. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its seeds are enclosed in distinctive, inflated pods, which are a key feature of the genus.

Lonchocarpus minor is also noted for containing various alkaloids, making it of interest in both botanical and pharmacological studies.
