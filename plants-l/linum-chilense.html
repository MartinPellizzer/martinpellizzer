Linum chilense

Linum chilense, commonly known as Chilean flax, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Linum chilense is often found in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its fibrous stalks have been historically used for fiber, similar to traditional flax plants.
