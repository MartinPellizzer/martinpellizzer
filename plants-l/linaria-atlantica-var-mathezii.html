Linaria atlantica var. mathezii

Linaria atlantica var. mathezii, commonly known as the Atlantic toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, branched stems and bright yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
