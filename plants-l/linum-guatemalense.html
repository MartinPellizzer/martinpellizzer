Linum guatemalense

Linum guatemalense, commonly known as Guatemalan flax, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and delicate blue or purple flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Linum guatemalense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has potential ecological significance in its native habitats.
