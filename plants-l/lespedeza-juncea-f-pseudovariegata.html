Lespedeza juncea f. pseudovariegata

Lespedeza juncea f. pseudovariegata, commonly known as the variegated lespedeza, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a herbaceous perennial that features distinctive variegated leaf patterns, often with white or yellow markings. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or red, arranged in racemes. It is native to eastern Asia and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage.

Lespedeza juncea f. pseudovariegata is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions and its role in ecological restoration projects.
