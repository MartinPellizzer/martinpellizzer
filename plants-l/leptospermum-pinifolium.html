Leptospermum pinifolium

Leptospermum pinifolium, commonly known as the silver pine tree, is a species of shrub or small tree native to Tasmania.

It is characterized by its needle-like leaves that are covered in a silvery bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Leptospermum pinifolium is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses by Indigenous communities.
