Lathyrus nevadensis

Lathyrus nevadensis, commonly known as the Nevada vetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features slender, trailing stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from purple to pink. Its trifoliate leaves are compound, with three leaflets that are often hairy.

Lathyrus nevadensis plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitats.
