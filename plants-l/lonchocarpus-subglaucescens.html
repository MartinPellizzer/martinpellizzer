Lonchocarpus subglaucescens

Lonchocarpus subglaucescens, commonly known as the glaucous senna, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces distinctive pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sunlight for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and herbal practices.
