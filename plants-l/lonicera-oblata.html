Lonicera oblata

Lonicera oblata, commonly known as the white honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are oval to elliptic in shape. Its berries are small, red, and edible, providing food for birds and other wildlife.

Lonicera oblata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
