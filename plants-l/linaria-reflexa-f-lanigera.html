Linaria reflexa f. lanigera

Linaria reflexa f. lanigera, commonly known as the hairy toadflax, is a flowering plant characterized by its distinctive, spurred petals and dense, hairy foliage.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is native to regions of Europe and western Asia. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and thrives in partial shade to full sun. Its flowers, which range in color from yellow to orange, bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The hairy texture of its leaves and stems is a notable botanical feature of this cultivar.
