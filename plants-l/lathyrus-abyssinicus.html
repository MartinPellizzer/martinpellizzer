Lathyrus abyssinicus

Lathyrus abyssinicus, commonly known as Abyssinian vetch, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple, followed by pod fruits containing several seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in challenging climates.

Lathyrus abyssinicus is also valued for its potential in soil improvement due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.
