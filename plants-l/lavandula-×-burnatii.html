Lavandula × burnatii

Lavandula × burnatii, commonly known as the English lavender, is a hybrid species within the Lavandula genus.

It is characterized by its fragrant, spike-like inflorescences that produce purple to pink flowers. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It has narrow, gray-green leaves that are aromatic and often used in aromatherapy.

Lavandula × burnatii is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.
