Leptothrix virginica

Leptothrix virginica, commonly known as the sludge thread algae, is a filamentous bacterium that forms long, slimy, rope-like structures.

It is a chemosynthetic organism that thrives in anaerobic environments, such as freshwater sediments and wastewater treatment systems. The organism is characterized by its sheath-like covering and the presence of a central axial sheath that contains the bacterial cells. It plays a role in the degradation of organic matter and the cycling of nutrients in aquatic ecosystems.

Leptothrix virginica is often found in association with other microorganisms in biofilms.
