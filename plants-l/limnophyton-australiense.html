Limnophyton australiense

Limnophyton australiense, commonly known as the Australian water lily, is a perennial aquatic plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves and showy white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats, such as ponds and lakes. Its roots are anchored in the substrate, while its leaves and flowers float on the water surface.

Limnophyton australiense plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
