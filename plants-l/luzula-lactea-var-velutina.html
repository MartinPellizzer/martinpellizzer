Luzula lactea var. velutina

Luzula lactea var. velutina, commonly known as velvet false owl's clover, is a perennial grass-like plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of white or pale blue flowers in early summer. Its leaves are usually covered in a fine, velvety pubescence, giving it a soft, tactile appearance.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along streambanks.
