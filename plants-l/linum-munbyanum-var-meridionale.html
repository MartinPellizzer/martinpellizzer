Linum munbyanum var. meridionale

Linum munbyanum var. meridionale, commonly known as the southern flax, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, blue or purple flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and coastal areas.

Its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine contribute to its ecological and cultural significance.
