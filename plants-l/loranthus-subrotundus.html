Loranthus subrotundus

Loranthus subrotundus, commonly known as the smoothleaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to various host trees, primarily in the genus Eucalyptus.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while also deriving some photosynthetic ability from its own leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale green berries that contain toxic compounds, making them unpalatable to most animals. Its flowers are typically small and inconspicuous, with a short flowering period during the warmer months.

Loranthus subrotundus plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for certain bird species that consume its berries.
