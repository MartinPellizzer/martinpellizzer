Lupinus triananus

Lupinus triananus, commonly known as the Triana lupine, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and spiny stems. The plant produces showy, pea-like flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Lupinus triananus is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
