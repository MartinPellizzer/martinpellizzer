Litsea dunniana

Litsea dunniana, commonly known as the Chinese tallowtree, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by yellowish-orange berries. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, often marked with shallow fissures.

Litsea dunniana is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been introduced to various parts of the world, though it can become invasive in some regions.
