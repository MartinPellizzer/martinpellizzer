Limniris tridentata

Limniris tridentata, commonly known as the three-toothed waterplant, is a aquatic plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves that are often serrated or toothed. The plant typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and marshes. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually greenish-white in color, and it produces small, buoyant fruits that aid in seed dispersal.

Limniris tridentata plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and fish.
