Licaria oblongifolia

Licaria oblongifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian licorice or licorice tree, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of eastern Brazil, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a legume pod containing several seeds, and it is often cultivated for its medicinal properties.

The plant's roots and leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
