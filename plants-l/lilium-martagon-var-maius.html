Lilium martagon var. maius

Lilium martagon var. maius, commonly known as the Turk's Cap Lily, is a striking perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, recurved, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features a robust rhizomatous root system and narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from the base. Its flowers are arranged in a loose, cone-shaped cluster, with each bloom having six to eight petals.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, unique appearance.
