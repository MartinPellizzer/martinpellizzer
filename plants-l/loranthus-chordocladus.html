Loranthus chordocladus

Loranthus chordocladus, commonly known as the Australian mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees, primarily in Australia.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using the limited leaf surface it possesses. The plant has small, leathery, elliptical leaves and produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are often clustered. Its fruits are typically small, fleshy berries that contain seeds covered in a sticky substance to aid in attachment to potential host trees.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by contributing to biodiversity and providing food for native animals.
