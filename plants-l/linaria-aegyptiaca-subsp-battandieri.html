Linaria aegyptiaca subsp. battandieri

Linaria aegyptiaca subsp.

battandieri, commonly known as the Egyptian toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
