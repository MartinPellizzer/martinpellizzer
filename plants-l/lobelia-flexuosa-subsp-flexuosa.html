Lobelia flexuosa subsp. flexuosa

Lobelia flexuosa subsp.

flexuosa, commonly known as the flexible lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features slender, ascending stems with opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its small, tubular flowers are usually blue to purple in color and bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
