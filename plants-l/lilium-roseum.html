Lilium roseum

Lilium roseum, commonly known as the roseate lily, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, pale pink to rose-colored flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features strap-like, dark green leaves and a bulbous root system typical of the Liliaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for its beauty and fragrance.
