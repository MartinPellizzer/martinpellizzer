Lathyrus angulatus var. genuinus

Lathyrus angulatus var. genuinus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed pea, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, angular stems and small, pea-like flowers that typically range in color from pink to purple. The plant produces small, elongated pods containing several seeds, which are often used in traditional herbal medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and its role in ecological habitats.
