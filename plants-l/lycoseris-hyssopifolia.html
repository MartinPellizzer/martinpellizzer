Lycoseris hyssopifolia

Lycoseris hyssopifolia, commonly known as the woolly lycoseris, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its densely hairy, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

The species is also valued for its ability to thrive with minimal water and care.
