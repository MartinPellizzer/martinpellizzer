Lysimachia japonica var. minutissima

Lysimachia japonica var. minutissima, commonly known as Japanese mossywort, is a small, herbaceous perennial plant native to Japan.

It features delicate, creeping stems that form dense mats, often growing in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors. The plant produces tiny, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are small, elliptical, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in rock gardens or as ground cover in shady gardens.
