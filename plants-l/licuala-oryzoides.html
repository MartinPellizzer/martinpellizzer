Licuala oryzoides

Licuala oryzoides, commonly known as the rice-leaf palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves that resemble rice leaves, hence its common name. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and as thatching material.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
