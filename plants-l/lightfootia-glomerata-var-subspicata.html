Lightfootia glomerata var. subspicata

Lightfootia glomerata var. subspicata, commonly known as the hairy false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flower heads that are clustered in dense groups. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
