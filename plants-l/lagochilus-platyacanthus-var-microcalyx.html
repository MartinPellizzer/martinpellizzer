Lagochilus platyacanthus var. microcalyx

Lagochilus platyacanthus var. microcalyx, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mountain thyme, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of pale blue to violet flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
