Lysimachia quartiniana

Lysimachia quartiniana, commonly known as the Quartin's chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from April to May. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or rocky habitats.

Lysimachia quartiniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.
