Lathyrus nissolia var. pubescens

Lathyrus nissolia var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, giving it its varietal name "pubescens.". It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and naturalized areas.
