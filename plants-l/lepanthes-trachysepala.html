Lepanthes trachysepala

Lepanthes trachysepala, commonly known as the rough-leafed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored flowers that bloom during the rainy season. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical forests. Its leaves are thick and leathery, adapted to retain moisture in its natural habitat.

Lepanthes trachysepala is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
