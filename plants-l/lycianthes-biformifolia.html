Lycianthes biformifolia

Lycianthes biformifolia, commonly known as the two-lobed physalis, is a species of flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its distinctive calyces that enclose the fruit. The plant typically grows as an annual shrub with hairy stems and opposite leaves that have a serrated margin. Its fruits are small, bright orange or red berries, enclosed in a fleshy, persistent calyx that remains attached after maturity.

This species is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant flowers and unique fruit structure.
