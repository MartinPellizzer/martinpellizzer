Ledocarpon chilense

Ledocarpon chilense, commonly known as the Chilean leucopogon, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Chile.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen shrub form. The plant features small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow, leathery, and arranged in whorls around the stem.

It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal scrub habitats.
