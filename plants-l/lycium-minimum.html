Lycium minimum

Lycium minimum, commonly known as Chinese gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family and is characterized by its small, yellow-orange berries that ripen in the fall. The plant features woody stems with spines and lance-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruits, which are rich in vitamin C. The berries are also used in traditional Chinese medicine for their purported health benefits.
