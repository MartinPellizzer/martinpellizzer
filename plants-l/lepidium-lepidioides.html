Lepidium lepidioides

Lepidium lepidioides, commonly known as the slender cress, is a small annual herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils along streambanks and roadsides. The plant features slender, branched stems with small, alternate leaves that are usually less than 1 inch in length. Its flowers are tiny, white or greenish, and clustered in dense, branched inflorescences.

The fruit is a small, three-sided capsule that contains several tiny seeds.
