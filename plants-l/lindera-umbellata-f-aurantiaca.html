Lindera umbellata f. aurantiaca

Lindera umbellata f. aurantiaca, commonly known as the orange-flowered sweet pepper tree, is a variant of the sweet pepper tree native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive orange-yellow flowers that bloom in umbels, giving it a striking visual appearance. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge, and its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that turns red when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded woodland areas.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
