Ligusticum pyrenaeum subsp. huteri

Ligusticum pyrenaeum subsp.

huteri, commonly known as the Pyrenean lovage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula. It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils at elevations ranging from 1,000 to 2,500 meters. Its leaves are pinnately divided and emit a strong, spicy odor when crushed.

The small white flowers appear in clustered umbels, contributing to its ornamental value in alpine gardens.
