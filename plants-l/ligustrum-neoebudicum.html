Ligustrum neoebudicum

Ligustrum neoebudicum, commonly known as the neoebudic privet, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter in color underneath. The plant produces tiny, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, black berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Ligustrum neoebudicum is also valued for its use in landscaping and as a hedge plant.
