Lindera assamica

Lindera assamica, commonly known as Assam wild laurier, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Himalayas and northeastern India.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its fruits are small, dark berries that mature in the autumn.

Lindera assamica is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
