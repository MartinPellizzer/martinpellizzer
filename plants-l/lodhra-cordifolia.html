Lodhra cordifolia

Lodhra cordifolia, commonly known as the sacred fig or heartleaf fig, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Moraceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces edible figs that are encased in a spiky, greenish husk. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Lodhra cordifolia is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional Ayurvedic medicine.
