Linodes radiola

Linodes radiola, commonly known as the radiolar tree, is a rare and unique plant species native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its striking, star-shaped leaves that radiate outward from a central stem, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the cooler months. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from thin, nutrient-poor soils, making it highly resilient to harsh environmental conditions.

Botanists study Linodes radiola for its potential medicinal properties and its role in the ecological balance of its native habitat.
