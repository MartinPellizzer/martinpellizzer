Larix olgensis var. heilingensis

Larix olgensis var. heilingensis, commonly known as the Heilongjiang larch, is a deciduous conifer native to the northeastern regions of China.

It is characterized by its needle-like leaves that turn yellow before falling in autumn. The tree produces cone-like structures that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in cold climates and is often found in mountainous areas with well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
