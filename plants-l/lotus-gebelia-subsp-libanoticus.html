Lotus gebelia subsp. libanoticus

Lotus gebelia subsp.

libanoticus, commonly known as the Lebanese lotus, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Lebanon and Syria. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its root system is notable for its ability to thrive in saline and arid conditions. It is often found in rocky soils and coastal areas, adapting well to harsh environmental conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in traditional medicine.
