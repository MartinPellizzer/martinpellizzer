Lonicera hirsuta var. glaucescens

Lonicera hirsuta var. glaucescens, commonly known as the hairy honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy and have a bluish-green hue. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that ripens in early summer.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and wildlife benefits.
