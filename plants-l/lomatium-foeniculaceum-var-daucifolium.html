Lomatium foeniculaceum var. daucifolium

Lomatium foeniculaceum var. daucifolium, commonly known as the western cow parsley, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features a basal rosette of deeply lobed, fern-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring to early summer. It is characterized by its finely divided foliage and is often found in moist meadows and along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
