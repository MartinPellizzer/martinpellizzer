Linum czernjajevii

Linum czernjajevii, commonly known as the Siberian flax, is a flowering plant native to the cold regions of Asia, particularly Siberia and the Far East.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and delicate, blue or white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate climates and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks. Its seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil, while its fibers were historically used for textile production.

Linum czernjajevii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
