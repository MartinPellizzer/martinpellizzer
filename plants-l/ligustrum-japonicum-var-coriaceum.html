Ligustrum japonicum var. coriaceum

Ligustrum japonicum var. coriaceum, commonly known as Japanese privet, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features small, dark green, leathery leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in clusters during the spring. Its fruit is a small, black drupe that ripens in the fall.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
