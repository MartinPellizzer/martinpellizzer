Loranthus parishii

Loranthus parishii, commonly known as the red mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily eucalyptus and acacia species.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while also producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using light reflected from the host. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers and red, fleshy berries that contain seeds dispersed by birds. Its parasitic nature makes it an important component of the ecosystem, influencing the growth and health of its host trees.

Loranthus parishii is found in tropical and subtropical regions of Australia and is often used in traditional medicine.
