Lithospermum torreyi

Lithospermum torreyi, commonly known as Torrey's lithosperm, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its roots contain alkaloids and are used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Lithospermum torreyi thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.
