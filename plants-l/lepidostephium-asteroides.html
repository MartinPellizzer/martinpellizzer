Lepidostephium asteroides

Lepidostephium asteroides, commonly known as the star-like cypress, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters. The plant's leaves are typically narrow and linear, with a slightly waxy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is occasionally cultivated in horticultural settings.
