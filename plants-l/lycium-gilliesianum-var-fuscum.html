Lycium gilliesianum var. fuscum

Lycium gilliesianum var. fuscum, commonly known as the black-tailed lily, is a shrub native to southern South America.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are typically harvested for their medicinal and culinary uses. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by the development of its distinctive fruits. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

The species is valued for its antioxidant properties and is used in traditional medicine.
