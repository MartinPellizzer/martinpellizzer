Lippia boliviana var. integrifolia

Lippia boliviana var. integrifolia, commonly known as the Bolivian hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Bolivia and Peru.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic, with a smooth or slightly toothed margin, and are often aromatic. This species thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in traditional medicine.
