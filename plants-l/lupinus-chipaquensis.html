Lupinus chipaquensis

Lupinus chipaquensis, commonly known as the Chipaqui lupine, is a flowering plant native to South America, specifically found in the Andean regions of Peru and Bolivia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit with clusters of blue to violet flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds.

Lupinus chipaquensis is adapted to high-altitude environments and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.
