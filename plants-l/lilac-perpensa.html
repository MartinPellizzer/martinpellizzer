Lilac perpensa

Lilac perpensa, commonly known as the Mexican lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, clustered flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet, featuring dark green, ovate leaves and a bushy, compact form. Its flowers range in color from white to pale purple, and it produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds.

Lilac perpensa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively low maintenance in suitable climates.
