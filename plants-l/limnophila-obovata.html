Limnophila obovata

Limnophila obovata, commonly known as the broad-leaf waterweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It has long, thin stems that can grow up to several meters in length, often forming dense underwater vegetation. The leaves are oval-shaped, with a smooth surface and a slightly waxy texture, aiding in buoyancy and light absorption. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or brown, which develop into tiny, bladder-like fruits.

This plant plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen for various aquatic organisms.
