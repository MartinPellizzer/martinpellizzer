Lupinus odoratus var. pilosellus

Lupinus odoratus var. pilosellus, commonly known as the silky lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of showy, pea-like flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant has soft, hairy foliage that gives it a distinctive, fluffy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in open grasslands and rocky areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
