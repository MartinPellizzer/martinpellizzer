Linum suffruticosum subsp. carratracense

Linum suffruticosum subsp.

carratracense, commonly known as the desert flax, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America. It is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and showy blue to violet flowers.

The plant produces capsule fruits containing small, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its fibrous stems and leaves have been historically used by indigenous peoples for textile and medicinal purposes.
