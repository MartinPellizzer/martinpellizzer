Linaria biebersteinii subsp. ruthenica

Linaria biebersteinii subsp.

ruthenica, commonly known as the Ruthenian toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and vibrant yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. Linaria biebersteinii subsp.

ruthenica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, daisy-like appearance.
