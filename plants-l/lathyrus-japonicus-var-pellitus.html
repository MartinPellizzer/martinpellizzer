Lathyrus japonicus var. pellitus

Lathyrus japonicus var. pellitus, commonly known as the Japanese vetch, is a perennial legume native to East Asia.

It features slender, climbing stems with trifoliate leaves composed of three ovate to lanceolate leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a slender pod containing several elliptical seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in ecological and agricultural contexts.
