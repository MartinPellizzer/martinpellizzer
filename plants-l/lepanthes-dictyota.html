Lepanthes dictyota

Lepanthes dictyota, commonly known as the netted slipper orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, pale green to yellowish flowers with intricate net-like patterns on the petals and sepals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and surrounding substrate.

Lepanthes dictyota is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
