Lilium sachalinense

Lilium sachalinense, commonly known as the Sakhalin lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the Russian Far East and northern Japan.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and red. The plant produces oval, greenish-white berries that contain seeds, which are dispersed by birds. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
