Lachnopylis pachyphylla

Lachnopylis pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved lichen, is a member of the Lichenes family and is characterized by its dense, foliose thallus.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found on decaying wood or soil in temperate regions. The plant exhibits a complex symbiotic relationship between a fungus and algae or cyanobacteria, which allows it to absorb nutrients from its surroundings. Its structure is composed of a fungal matrix interwoven with photosynthetic organisms, enabling it to survive in nutrient-poor habitats.

Lachnopylis pachyphylla plays an important ecological role in soil stabilization and as a food source for various invertebrates.
