Leptochloa polygama

Leptochloa polygama, commonly known as the slender false brome, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It typically grows in moist, grassy habitats such as meadows and riverbanks. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, two-lipped flowers.

Leptochloa polygama is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
