Luzula angustifolia

Luzula angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf woodrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, commonly known as the grass family, and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and slightly folded, with a bluish-green hue. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Luzula angustifolia is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
