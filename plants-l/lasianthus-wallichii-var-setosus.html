Lasianthus wallichii var. setosus

Lasianthus wallichii var. setosus, commonly known as the hairy lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive hairy foliage and showy inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine, soft hairs.
