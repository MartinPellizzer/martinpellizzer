Lychnis coronata var. speciosa

Lychnis coronata var. speciosa, commonly known as the showy campion, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer, with five petals arranged in a cup-like shape. The plant has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for gardens and ecological restoration projects.
