Lamium pedemontanum

Lamium pedemontanum, commonly known as purple deadnettle, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe.

It features opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of violet or purple flowers. The plant thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas or along garden paths. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
