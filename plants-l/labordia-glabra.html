Labordia glabra

Labordia glabra, commonly known as the smooth labordia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its succulent, cylindrical stems that are covered in a smooth, waxy coating. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, which bloom during the cooler months. Labordia glabra is adapted to arid environments and can store water in its thick stems, making it drought-tolerant.

It is often found growing in rocky or sandy soils in regions with a Mediterranean climate.
