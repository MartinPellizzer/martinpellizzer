Loudetia furtiva

Loudetia furtiva, commonly known as the African grass or desert grass, is a perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, narrow leaves. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh environments with low rainfall and poor soil conditions. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in spikelets.

Loudetia furtiva is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
