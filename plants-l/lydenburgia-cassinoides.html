Lydenburgia cassinoides

Lydenburgia cassinoides, commonly known as the Natal wild indigo, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy flowers. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically purple or blue in color. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Lydenburgia cassinoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
