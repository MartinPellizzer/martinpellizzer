Lithospermum incrassatum

Lithospermum incrassatum, commonly known as rough rock pepper, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which are often reddish in color. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink to purple in hue, blooming in late summer. Its roots contain compounds that have been used in traditional medicine for their potential antifungal and antibacterial properties.

Lithospermum incrassatum thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.
