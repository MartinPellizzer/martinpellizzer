Lotononis arida

Lotononis arida, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of fleshy, blue-green leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by tufted seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water.

Its resilience makes it an important component of desert ecosystems.
