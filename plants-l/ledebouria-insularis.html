Ledebouria insularis

Ledebouria insularis, commonly known as the Socotra starfish, is a unique succulent native to the island of Socotra.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, star-shaped leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a delicate touch to its otherwise rugged appearance. It thrives in arid environments and is well adapted to the harsh conditions of its native habitat.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, it is considered a vulnerable species.
