Lepidium campestre f. pinnatifida

Lepidium campestre f. pinnatifida, commonly known as the slender peppergrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, pinnately divided leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes, which are typical of the mustard family. Its flowers are followed by elongated, cylindrical siliques that contain numerous small, rounded seeds.

This variety is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to a wide range of environmental conditions.
