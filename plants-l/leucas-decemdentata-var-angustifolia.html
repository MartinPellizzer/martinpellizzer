Leucas decemdentata var. angustifolia

Leucas decemdentata var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved leucas, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features slender, erect stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale blue, blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
