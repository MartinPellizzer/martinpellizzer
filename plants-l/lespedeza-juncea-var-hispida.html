Lespedeza juncea var. hispida

Lespedeza juncea var. hispida, commonly known as hispid lespedeza, is a perennial legume native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers in dense, cylindrical clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and disturbed sites.

Lespedeza juncea var. hispida is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil restoration and as a forage plant.
