Lindenbergia hookeri

Lindenbergia hookeri, commonly known as the Hooker's lindenbergia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and it produces small, white or pale yellow flowers clustered in umbels. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitats.
