Limonium virgatum subsp. sardoum

Limonium virgatum subsp.

sardoum, commonly known as the Sardinian sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers.

The plant thrives in sandy or coastal soils and is often found in maritime environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are adapted to withstand salt spray and strong winds. Limonium virgatum subsp.

sardoum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
