Limnophila albiflora

Limnophila albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered waterweed, is a tropical aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and slow-moving streams. Its leaves are typically narrow and ribbon-like, adapted for efficient light absorption in aquatic settings.

Limnophila albiflora plays a role in maintaining water quality by absorbing nutrients and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
