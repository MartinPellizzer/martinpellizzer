Leonurus glaucescens

Leonurus glaucescens, commonly known as blue hound's tongue, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant features erect, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are often glandular and have a distinctive blue or purplish hue. Its flowers are typically small, two-lipped, and arranged in whorls along the stem.

It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage and historical use in traditional medicine.
