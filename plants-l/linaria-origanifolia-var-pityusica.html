Linaria origanifolia var. pityusica

Linaria origanifolia var. pityusica, commonly known as the Canary Island toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, resembling those of oregano, which is reflected in its scientific name. This variety is often found in rocky or coastal habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
