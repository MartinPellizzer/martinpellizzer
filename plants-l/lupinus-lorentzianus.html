Lupinus lorentzianus

Lupinus lorentzianus, commonly known as the Lorentz's lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of blue to violet pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.
