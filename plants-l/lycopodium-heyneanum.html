Lycopodium heyneanum

Lycopodium heyneanum, commonly known as the Heyne's clubmoss, is a species of small, evergreen shrub belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, dense growth habit and its production of spore-bearing cones, which are typically clustered at the ends of its stems. The plant has needle-like leaves that are arranged in spirals around the stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest undergrowth or along stream banks.

Lycopodium heyneanum plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various small organisms.
