Lithospermum caroliniense subsp. croceum

Lithospermum caroliniense subsp.

croceum, commonly known as the yellow rock pepper, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves.

The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that are typically clustered in loose inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, dark seeds.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
