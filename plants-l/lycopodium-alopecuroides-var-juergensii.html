Lycopodium alopecuroides var. juergensii

Lycopodium alopecuroides var. juergensii, commonly known as the Juergens' clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its low-growing, creeping habit with small, scale-like leaves that form dense mats. The plant produces sporophylls that bear spores, typical of its ancient lineage of vascular plants. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest undergrowth or rocky areas.

This variant is distinguished by its unique morphological features, making it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
