Lobelia anceps var. cuneiformis

Lobelia anceps var. cuneiformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, branching stems covered in opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
