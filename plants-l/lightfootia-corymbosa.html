Lightfootia corymbosa

Lightfootia corymbosa, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange inflorescences that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in wetlands and along stream banks, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a hairy texture.

Lightfootia corymbosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
