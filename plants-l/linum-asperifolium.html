Linum asperifolium

Linum asperifolium, commonly known as roughleaf flax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Linum asperifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in fiber production.
