Lamiacanthus viscosus

Lamiacanthus viscosus, commonly known as the sticky begonia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, hairy leaves and showy, spurred flowers that bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant produces a sticky, resinous substance on its stems and leaves, which gives it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its attractive foliage and flowers.
