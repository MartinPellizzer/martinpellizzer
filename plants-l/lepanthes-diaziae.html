Lepanthes diaziae

Lepanthes diaziae, commonly known as the Ecuadorian white orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean region of Ecuador.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that grows on trees in humid, shaded forest environments. The plant features showy, white flowers with intricate patterns and a sweet fragrance, which attract pollinators such as bees and moths. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette, helping it to retain moisture in its natural habitat.

Lepanthes diaziae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by enthusiasts of rare orchids.
