Lyperanthus latilabris

Lyperanthus latilabris, commonly known as the broad-lipped orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in grassland habitats and thrives in well-drained soil. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
