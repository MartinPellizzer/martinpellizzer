Lasianthera macrophylla

Lasianthera macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leafed sandmat, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and yellow flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or arid regions. Its leaves are broad and often covered in a fine, white powder, which helps reduce water loss.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
