Landolphia echinata

Landolphia echinata, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces woody pods containing several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its bark and leaves contain alkaloids and are valued for their medicinal properties.

Landolphia echinata is also cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and unique floral display.
