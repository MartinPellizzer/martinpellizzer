Lonchitis stenochlamys

Lonchitis stenochlamys, commonly known as the narrow-chambered water plant, is a species of aquatic flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It typically grows in freshwater environments such as ponds and slow-moving streams. The plant has slender, ribbon-like leaves that are often submerged, with small, white flowers that appear in clusters. Its reproductive structures are adapted for water dispersal, and it plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in water gardens.
