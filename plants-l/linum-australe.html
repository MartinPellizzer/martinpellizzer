Linum australe

Linum australe, commonly known as the Australian flax, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking blue or purple flowers. The plant has slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its fibrous stems were historically used by Indigenous Australians for making cordage and textiles.

Linum australe thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.
