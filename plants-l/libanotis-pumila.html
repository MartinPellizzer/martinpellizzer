Libanotis pumila

Libanotis pumila, commonly known as the dwarf bellflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, often appearing in clusters. The plant has hairy, gray-green leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its compact size and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
