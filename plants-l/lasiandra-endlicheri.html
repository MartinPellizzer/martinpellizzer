Lasiandra endlicheri

Lasiandra endlicheri, commonly known as the velvetleaf or false nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal and culinary uses. The plant is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a velvety texture. Its small, tubular flowers are typically purple or blue, arranged in whorled clusters along the stems.

Lasiandra endlicheri is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential ecological benefits.
