Lippia thymoides var. mucronulata

Lippia thymoides var. mucronulata, commonly known as Mexican thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It features aromatic, small, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping and drought-tolerant gardens.

Its aromatic properties and medicinal uses have made it a valued plant in both traditional and modern herbal practices.
