Lotus corniculatus proles pedunculatus

Lotus corniculatus proles pedunculatus, commonly known as the creeping yellow trefoil, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically arranged in clusters along the stems. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it grows low to the ground, often forming dense mats. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.

This species is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
