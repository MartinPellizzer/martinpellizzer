Linum punctatum subsp. kurdicum

Linum punctatum subsp.

kurdicum, commonly known as the Kurdish flax, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia. It features slender, erect stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, often branching towards the upper parts.

The plant produces small, delicate blue or violet flowers that are typically clustered in loose racemes. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
