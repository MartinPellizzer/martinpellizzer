Lupinus reitzii

Lupinus reitzii, commonly known as Reitz's lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant has feathery, compound leaves and produces cylindrical seed pods that contain several small, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Lupinus reitzii plays a role in nitrogen fixation and is valued for its ecological and ornamental significance.
