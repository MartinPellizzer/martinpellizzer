Leocus membranaceus

Leocus membranaceus, commonly known as the silky leucanthemum, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and heathlands. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into fluffy, seed-filled spheres.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
