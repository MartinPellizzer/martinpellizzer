Lupinus vallicola subsp. vallicola

Lupinus vallicola subsp.

vallicola, commonly known as the valley lupine, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers.

The plant produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and slopes. Lupinus vallicola subsp.

vallicola plays an important role in soil stabilization and provides nectar for pollinators.
