Lupinus gayanus

Lupinus gayanus, commonly known as the yellow lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and bright yellow flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Lupinus gayanus is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility in ecological restoration projects.
