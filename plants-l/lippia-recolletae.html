Lippia recolletae

Lippia recolletae, commonly known as the Mexican blue sage, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically blue or purple, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the branches.

Lippia recolletae is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a mild, aromatic scent.
