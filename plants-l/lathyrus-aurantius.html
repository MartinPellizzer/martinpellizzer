Lathyrus aurantius

Lathyrus aurantius, commonly known as the golden pea or scarlet lathyris, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking clusters of bright orange to red flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Lathyrus aurantius is valued in gardens for its vibrant color and ornamental appeal.
