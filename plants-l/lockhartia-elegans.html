Lockhartia elegans

Lockhartia elegans, commonly known as the orchid bell, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including pink, white, and red. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are long and strap-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

Lockhartia elegans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated for its beauty in both indoor and outdoor gardens.
