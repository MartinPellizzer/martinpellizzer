Leymus salina var. mojavensis

Leymus salina var. mojavensis, commonly known as the Mojave alkali grass, is a perennial grass native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, often found in desert environments such as the Mojave Desert. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny seeds. It is adapted to withstand extreme drought and high salt concentrations, making it a resilient species in harsh conditions.

Its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion makes it ecologically significant in its habitat.
