Licania heteromorpha

Licania heteromorpha, commonly known as the Brazilian palm, is a flowering plant belonging to the Arecaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features a slender, unbranched stem with a crown of pinnate leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. Its inflorescences are terminal and bear small, clustered flowers, which are pollinated by insects.

Licania heteromorpha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
