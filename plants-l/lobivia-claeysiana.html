Lobivia claeysiana

Lobivia claeysiana, commonly known as the Claeys' lobivia, is a species of succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded shape and thick, fleshy stems that store water, enabling it to survive in dry conditions. The plant typically produces clusters of small, pale green or blue-gray flowers during the warmer months. Its leaves are often reduced to spines, a feature common in many desert plants for reducing water loss.

Lobivia claeysiana is well-suited for cultivation in rock gardens or as part of a succulent collection due to its low maintenance requirements.
