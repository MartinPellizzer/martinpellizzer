Liparis winkleri

Liparis winkleri, commonly known as the Winkler's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often found in the understory of rainforests. Its roots are specialized for epiphytic growth, allowing it to attach to tree trunks without being parasitic.

Liparis winkleri is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated by enthusiasts of rare orchids.
