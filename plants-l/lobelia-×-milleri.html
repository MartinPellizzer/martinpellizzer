Lobelia × milleri

Lobelia × milleri, commonly known as the hybrid lobelia, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a hybrid between *Lobelia sanguinea* and *Lobelia cardinalis*, resulting in a unique combination of traits from both parents. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring dark green, narrow leaves and showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and shaded woodlands.

Lobelia × milleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
