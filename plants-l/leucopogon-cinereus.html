Leucopogon cinereus

Leucopogon cinereus, commonly known as the grey leucopogon, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in heathland and dry forest habitats, often in well-drained soils. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Leucopogon cinereus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
