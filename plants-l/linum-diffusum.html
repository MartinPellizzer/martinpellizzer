Linum diffusum

Linum diffusum, commonly known as wandering flax, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and small, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a hairy texture.

Linum diffusum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
