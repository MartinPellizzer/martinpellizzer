Lilium philadelphicum f. pulchrum

Lilium philadelphicum f. pulchrum, commonly known as the showy Turk's cap lily, is a striking hybrid of the Turk's cap lily and the longiflorum lily.

It is characterized by its large, showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and even red. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet, with lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners in USDA hardiness zones 5 through 8.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and ability to bloom reliably in the summer months.
