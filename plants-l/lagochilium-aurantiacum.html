Lagochilium aurantiacum

Lagochilium aurantiacum, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. It is often found in shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

The species is also known for its medicinal properties, historically used by indigenous peoples for various healing purposes.
