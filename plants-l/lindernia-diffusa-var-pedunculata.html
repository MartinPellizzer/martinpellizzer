Lindernia diffusa var. pedunculata

Lindernia diffusa var. pedunculata, commonly known as the trailing lindernia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a perennial herb with trailing stems that can reach up to 1 meter in length. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and slightly hairy, while the flowers are tubular and come in shades of purple or pink. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
