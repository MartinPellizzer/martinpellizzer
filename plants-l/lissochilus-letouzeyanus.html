Lissochilus letouzeyanus

Lissochilus letouzeyanus, commonly known as the Letouzey's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, enabling it to thrive in its high-altitude habitat. Its leaves are usually lanceolate and arranged in a rosette pattern, while the flowers are borne on elongated inflorescences.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species, making conservation efforts crucial for its survival.
