Lechea glomerata

Lechea glomerata, commonly known as the clustered milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the United States.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has smooth, often hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Lechea glomerata is also known for its milky sap, which is a characteristic feature of the Apocynaceae family.
