Loranthus flamignii

Loranthus flamignii, commonly known as the Italian mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that attaches itself to the branches of its host trees.

It belongs to the family Loranthaceae and is native to regions of southern Europe, North Africa, and the Middle East. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers and red berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in temperate climates and relies on its host for nutrients and water.

Its presence can sometimes cause stress or damage to the host tree if left unchecked.
