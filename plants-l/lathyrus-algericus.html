Lathyrus algericus

Lathyrus algericus, commonly known as Algerian vetch, is a flowering plant native to North Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pink to purple. Its seeds are encased in pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

Lathyrus algericus is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural purposes.
