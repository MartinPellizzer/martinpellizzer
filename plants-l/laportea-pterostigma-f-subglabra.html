Laportea pterostigma f. subglabra

Laportea pterostigma f. subglabra, commonly known as the smooth-leaved laportea, is a variant of the laportea plant characterized by its reduced pubescence on the leaves.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is native to parts of the southeastern United States. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. Its leaves are elliptic to ovate, with a smooth texture compared to the more hairy form of the species.

The inflorescence consists of small, yellowish flower heads that are clustered in dense, flat-topped clusters.
