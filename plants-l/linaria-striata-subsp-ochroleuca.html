Linaria striata subsp. ochroleuca

Linaria striata subsp.

ochroleuca, commonly known as the yellow horned poppy, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered with fine hairs.
