Lophiocarpus guayanensis f. minor

Lophiocarpus guayanensis f. minor, commonly known as the small-flowered lophiocarpus, is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant produces small, legume-like pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
