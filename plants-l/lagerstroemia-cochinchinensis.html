Lagerstroemia cochinchinensis

Lagerstroemia cochinchinensis, commonly known as the Chinese crape myrtle, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, panicle-like inflorescences that bloom in summer, typically in shades of pink, red, or white. The plant has smooth, grayish bark that peels in thin, papery layers, and its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a pointed tip. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical regions.

The species is also valued for its adaptability and resistance to urban environmental conditions.
