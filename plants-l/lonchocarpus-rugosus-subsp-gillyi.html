Lonchocarpus rugosus subsp. gillyi

Lonchocarpus rugosus subsp.

gillyi, commonly known as the gillyflower, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America. It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and clusters of small, fragrant, yellowish-white flowers.

The plant produces distinctive, woody pods that contain flat, seed-like structures. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in humid lowland areas.

Due to its ornamental value and unique floral structure, it is cultivated in some tropical gardens.
