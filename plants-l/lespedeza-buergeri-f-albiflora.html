Lespedeza buergeri f. albiflora

Lespedeza buergeri f. albiflora, commonly known as whiteflower burgei lespeza, is a flowering plant in the legume family.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers, which are typically pea-like in shape. It is native to East Asia and is often found in open, sunny areas such as grasslands and forest edges.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
