Lotus argophyllus var. ornithopus

Lotus argophyllus var. ornithopus, commonly known as the bird's beak lotus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated seed pods that resemble a bird's beak, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are palmately lobed and covered in a fine, silky hair, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its unique appearance and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
