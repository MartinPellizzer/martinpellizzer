Lythrum biflorum var. hispidulum

Lythrum biflorum var. hispidulum, commonly known as the hairy lythrum, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and spikes of pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological gardens and wetland restoration projects.
