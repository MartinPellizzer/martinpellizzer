Lasiorrhiza barrasiana

Lasiorrhiza barrasiana, commonly known as the Barras's grass.

It is a perennial grass species native to South Africa, characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration that helps it reflect sunlight in its arid habitat.

Lasiorrhiza barrasiana is adapted to survive in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open grasslands and along coastal areas.
