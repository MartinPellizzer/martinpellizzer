Landolphia florida

Landolphia florida, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its showy, yellow, pendulous flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators like bees and birds. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and typically elliptical in shape.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its natural distribution in suitable habitats.
