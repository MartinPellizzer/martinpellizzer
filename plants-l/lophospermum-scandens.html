Lophospermum scandens

Lophospermum scandens, commonly known as the coral vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a vigorous, climbing vine that produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant blooms. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

The flowers attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees, contributing to its ecological value.
