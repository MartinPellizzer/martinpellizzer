Lonicera implexa var. balearica

Lonicera implexa var. balearica, commonly known as the Balearic honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the Balearic Islands.

It features slender, arching stems that often grow along the ground or climb supports. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The berries that follow the flowers are small, red, and edible, though they are not commonly consumed by humans.
