Lobelia revoluta

Lobelia revoluta, commonly known as the scarlet lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches. The plant produces clusters of bright red, tubular flowers that attract hummingbirds and bees. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly wavy edge.

Lobelia revoluta thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.
