Lithospermum linifolium

Lithospermum linifolium, commonly known as redroot, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. Its roots are reddish in color and have been used traditionally in herbal medicine.

Lithospermum linifolium thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.
