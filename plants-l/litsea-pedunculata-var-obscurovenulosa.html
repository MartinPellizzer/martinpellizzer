Litsea pedunculata var. obscurovenulosa

Litsea pedunculata var. obscurovenulosa, commonly known as the dark-veined litsea, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub, typically growing up to 10 meters in height. The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a distinctive dark green upper surface with obscure, pale veins on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by elliptical, yellowish-orange fruits.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
