Linum sanctum

Linum sanctum, commonly known as holy flax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, blue or white flowers. The plant produces small, oval seeds that are used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil. Its leaves are alternate and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

Linum sanctum is often grown for its ornamental value and has historical significance in herbal remedies.
