Laurus pilosifolia

Laurus pilosifolia, commonly known as the hairy laurel, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into dark purple berries. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Laurus pilosifolia is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its historical use in culinary and medicinal applications.
