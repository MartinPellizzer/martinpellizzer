Ladenbergia magnifolia var. rostrata

Ladenbergia magnifolia var. rostrata, commonly known as the red-flowered legume, is a strikingly ornamental plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant, velvety red flowers that bloom in dense, racemose inflorescences, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements. The plant has compound, pinnately arranged leaves that are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Its unique botanical features make it both aesthetically pleasing and botanically significant in its native habitat.
