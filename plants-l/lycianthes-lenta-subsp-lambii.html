Lycianthes lenta subsp. lambii

Lycianthes lenta subsp.

lambii, commonly known as the Mexican nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to Central America. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers.

The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 meter, with hairy stems and alternate leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape. Its flowers are yellow with five petals, and it produces small, orange berries that contain several seeds.

The species is often found in disturbed habitats and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
