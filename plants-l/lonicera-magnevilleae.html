Lonicera magnevilleae

Lonicera magnevilleae, commonly known as the Magneville honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of yellow to orange, which bloom in late spring to early summer. These flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its berries, which are red and edible, mature in the fall and provide food for birds and small mammals.
