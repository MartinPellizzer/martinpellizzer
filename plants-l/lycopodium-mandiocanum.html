Lycopodium mandiocanum

Lycopodium mandiocanum, commonly known as the Brazilian clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the Lycopodiaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, erect stems that bear whorls of needle-like leaves. The plant produces sporophytes that develop into spore-bearing cones, which are typically clustered at the base of the plant. Lycopodium mandiocanum is native to tropical regions of South America and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Its unique morphology and reproductive structures make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
