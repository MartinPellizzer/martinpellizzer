Linaria aeruginea var. atropurpurea

Linaria aeruginea var. atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple toadflax, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to deep red, often with contrasting yellow or orange markings. The plant has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which extends from late spring to early autumn.
