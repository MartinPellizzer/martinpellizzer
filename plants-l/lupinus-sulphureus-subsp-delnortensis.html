Lupinus sulphureus subsp. delnortensis

Lupinus sulphureus subsp.

delnortensis, commonly known as the yellow lupine, is a perennial legume native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves, which are typical of the Fabaceae family.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats. Its root system includes nitrogen-fixing nodules, which enhance soil fertility.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and supporting pollinator populations.
