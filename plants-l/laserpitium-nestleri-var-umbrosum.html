Laserpitium nestleri var. umbrosum

Laserpitium nestleri var. umbrosum, commonly known as the Umbrosum Meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in alpine and subalpine meadows, preferring well-drained, rocky soils. The plant is characterized by its slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves, which are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, typical of the Poaceae family.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates in its native environment.
