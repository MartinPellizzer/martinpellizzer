Leucopogon denticulatus

Leucopogon denticulatus, commonly known as the serrated leucopogon, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, often arranged in clusters. Its leaves are narrow and serrated, giving it a distinctive appearance, and it thrives in moist, acidic soils.

Leucopogon denticulatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
