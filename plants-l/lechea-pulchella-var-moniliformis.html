Lechea pulchella var. moniliformis

Lechea pulchella var. moniliformis, commonly known as the coral tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, coral-like inflorescences that bloom in clusters, giving it its common name. The plant is a shrub or small tree with woody stems and leathery, dark green leaves. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal or disturbed soils.

The flowers are typically pink or red, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.
