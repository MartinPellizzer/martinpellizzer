Lobelia gruina var. conferta

Lobelia gruina var. conferta, commonly known as the dense lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, tubular blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
