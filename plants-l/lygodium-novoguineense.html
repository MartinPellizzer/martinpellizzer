Lygodium novoguineense

Lygodium novoguineense, commonly known as the New Guinea climbing fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Lygodiaceae.

It is characterized by its pinnate fronds and a unique ability to climb using aerial roots. Native to the tropical regions of New Guinea, it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant exhibits a complex life cycle with distinct sporangium structures on the undersides of its leaves.

Its morphology and growth habits make it an important component of tropical rainforest ecosystems.
