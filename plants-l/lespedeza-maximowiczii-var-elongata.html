Lespedeza maximowiczii var. elongata

Lespedeza maximowiczii var. elongata, commonly known as the elongated lespedeza, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands. The plant produces small, pink to reddish-purple flowers in racemes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a hairy texture.

It is often used in ecological restoration due to its ability to stabilize soil and support pollinators.
