Laurus sulcata

Laurus sulcata, commonly known as the Turkish laurel or sweet bay laurel, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dark green, glossy leaves that are aromatic and often used in cooking. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible but not commonly consumed. Laurus sulcata thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its aromatic foliage and historical significance in ancient cultures add to its botanical and cultural value.
