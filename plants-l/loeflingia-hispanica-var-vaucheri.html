Loeflingia hispanica var. vaucheri

Loeflingia hispanica var. vaucheri, commonly known as the Spanish loeflingia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, pale yellow flowers during the summer months. It is often found in Mediterranean regions, thriving in full sun and well-drained soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Amaranthaceae family.
