Lobelia tenera var. belladonna

Lobelia tenera var. belladonna, commonly known as the velvetleaf lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas. It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
