Linaria amethystea var. subalpina

Linaria amethystea var. subalpina, commonly known as the alpine toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a perennial herb with slender, branching stems that often grow in open, rocky habitats. The plant produces vibrant yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, adapting to the cold, high-altitude environment.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
