Leonurus cardiaca var. hirtella

Leonurus cardiaca var. hirtella, commonly known as hairy heartwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic and medicinal plants. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves, which give it its common name. It produces small, pale blue or violet flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming from June to August.

This variety is often found in grasslands, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, and it is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
