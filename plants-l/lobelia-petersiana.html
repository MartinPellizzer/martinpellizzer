Lobelia petersiana

Lobelia petersiana, commonly known as Peters' lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant produces striking cobalt-blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Lobelia petersiana is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
