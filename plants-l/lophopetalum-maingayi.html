Lophopetalum maingayi

Lophopetalum maingayi, commonly known as the Chinese flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical regions.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.
