Lepanthes jesupii

Lepanthes jesupii, commonly known as the Jesup's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in the understory of tropical forests. Its flowers are usually white or pale green, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insects.

Lepanthes jesupii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
