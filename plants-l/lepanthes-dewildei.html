Lepanthes dewildei

Lepanthes dewildei, commonly known as the Dewilde's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically feature vibrant colors such as white, pink, and purple. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees or other plants without deriving nutrients from them. Its flowers are often fragrant and attract various pollinators, including bees and moths.

Lepanthes dewildei is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
