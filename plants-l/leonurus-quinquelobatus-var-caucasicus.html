Leonurus quinquelobatus var. caucasicus

Leonurus quinquelobatus var. caucasicus, commonly known as Caucasian hedgehog.

This variant is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region, characterized by its distinctive, spiny leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in temperate climates. The plant has a bushy appearance with hairy stems and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a unique subject for study in plant taxonomy and ecology.
