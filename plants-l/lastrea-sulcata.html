Lastrea sulcata

Lastrea sulcata, commonly known as the African spurflower, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun conditions. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Lastrea sulcata is also noted for its ability to withstand drought, making it a hardy choice for xerophytic gardens.
