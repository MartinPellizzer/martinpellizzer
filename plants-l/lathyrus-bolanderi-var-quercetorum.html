Lathyrus bolanderi var. quercetorum

Lathyrus bolanderi var. quercetorum, commonly known as the Bolander's vetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, elliptical pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and chaparral habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
