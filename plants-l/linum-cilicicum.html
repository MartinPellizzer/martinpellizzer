Linum cilicicum

Linum cilicicum, commonly known as the Cilician flax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the genus Linum, which includes various species of flax known for their showy blue or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its slender stems bear clusters of small, delicate flowers that bloom in early summer.

Linum cilicicum is often found in wildflower meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
