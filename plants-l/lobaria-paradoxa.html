Lobaria paradoxa

Lobaria paradoxa, commonly known as the paradox lichen, is a foliose lichen that belongs to the family Parmeliaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, lobed thallus with a leathery texture and a pale to dark green or yellowish color. The lichen forms a symbiotic relationship with green algae and cyanobacteria, which provide nutrients through photosynthesis. It typically grows on tree bark, especially in moist, shaded environments.

Lobaria paradoxa is often found in temperate regions and is considered an indicator species for certain ecological conditions.
