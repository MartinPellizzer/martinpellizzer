Lilium maculatum

Lilium maculatum, commonly known as the spotted lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically white or pink with red or purple spots. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped blooms that are often fragrant and bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a fan-like pattern, while its bulbs are underground storage organs.

Lilium maculatum is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and floral arrangements.
