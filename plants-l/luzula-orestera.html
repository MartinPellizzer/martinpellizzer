Luzula orestera

Luzula orestera, commonly known as the alpine woodrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, commonly known as the grass family, and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing greenish to brownish flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. Luzula orestera thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and mountainous regions.

Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often persist through the winter, contributing to its ornamental value in horticulture.
