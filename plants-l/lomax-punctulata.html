Lomax punctulata

Lomax punctulata, commonly known as the dotted lomax, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to dry conditions. Its small, yellow flowers are often clustered in racemes, and it produces small, round pods that contain several seeds.

Lomax punctulata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
