Leucas nutans

Leucas nutans, commonly known as the drooping leucas, is a herbaceous plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, pale blue to purple flowers are arranged in whorled clusters, typical of the genus Leucas.

It is often found in dry, open habitats and is used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
