Lobelia giberroa subsp. squarrosa

Lobelia giberroa subsp.

squarrosa, commonly known as the woolly lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. It is often found in woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.

Its subspecific name, *squarrosa*, refers to the woolly or frayed appearance of its leaves.
