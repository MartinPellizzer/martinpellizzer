Leandra itatiaiae

Leandra itatiaiae, commonly known as the Itatiaia orchid, is a species native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family, characterized by its intricate and showy floral structures. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its humid, tropical habitat. Its flowers are usually pale green to white in color, with a distinctive spurred lip that aids in pollination.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered threatened and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
