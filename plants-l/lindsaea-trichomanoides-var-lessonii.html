Lindsaea trichomanoides var. lessonii

Lindsaea trichomanoides var. lessonii, commonly known as the Lesson's filmy fern, is a member of the fern family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically less than 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its unique morphology and habitat make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native range.
