Lagaropyxis gigantea

Lagaropyxis gigantea, commonly known as the giant puya, is a striking plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that can reach several meters in height. The plant features a rosette of stiff, sword-shaped leaves that store water, adapting it to its arid environment. Due to its rarity and habitat specificity, it is considered an endangered species.
