Lamium album var. roseum

Lamium album var. roseum, commonly known as the white dead-nettle with pink flowers, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are hairy and have a distinctive white underside. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to white flowers that bloom from spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to gardens and natural habitats.
