Lamium moluccellifolium

Lamium moluccellifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved deadnettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are typically hairy and have a distinctive broad, rounded base. The plant produces clusters of small, two-lipped flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom from spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Lamium moluccellifolium is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
