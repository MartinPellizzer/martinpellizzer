Lonicera × pseudochrysantha

Lonicera × pseudochrysantha, commonly known as the golden honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds but generally not for humans. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its rapid growth and attractive foliage make it a versatile plant for hedging and landscape design.
