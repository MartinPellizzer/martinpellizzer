Lobelia × hybrida

Lobelia × hybrida, commonly known as the cardinal flower, is a hybrid species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and prefers moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

Lobelia × hybrida is a popular ornamental plant in gardens due to its vibrant color and ability to attract pollinators like hummingbirds and bees.
