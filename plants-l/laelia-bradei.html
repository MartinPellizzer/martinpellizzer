Laelia bradei

Laelia bradei, commonly known as the Brade's orchid, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in rocky soils and open habitats. The plant features a pseudobulb with a single leaf and produces tall, slender stems that bear numerous showy flowers. Its blooms are usually pink to purple in color, with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals.

Laelia bradei is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
