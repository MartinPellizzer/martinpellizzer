Lithocarpus gelinicus

Lithocarpus gelinicus, commonly known as the Philippine ironwood, is a member of the Fagaceae family, native to the Philippines.

It is a large evergreen tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, characterized by its dense, hard wood and thick, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, cup-shaped fruits that contain one or two seeds, which are dispersed by animals.

Its botanical features make it an important species in tropical rainforest ecosystems, contributing to biodiversity and soil stabilization.
