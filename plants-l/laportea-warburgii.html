Laportea warburgii

Laportea warburgii, commonly known as the warburg's false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are covered in fine, white hairs, giving them a fuzzy appearance.

Laportea warburgii is often found in moist, disturbed soils along roadsides and in open woods.
