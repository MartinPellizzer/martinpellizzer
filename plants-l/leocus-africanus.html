Leocus africanus

Leocus africanus, commonly known as the African leek, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant produces small, round bulbs that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Leocus africanus is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
