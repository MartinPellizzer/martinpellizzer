Lappula occultata

Lappula occultata, commonly known as the hidden lappula, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its slender stems and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Native to certain regions of Europe and Asia, it thrives in moist, calcareous soils.

Due to its elusive nature and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and somewhat obscure species in botanical studies.
