Lithospermum caroliniense var. caroliniense

Lithospermum caroliniense var. caroliniense, commonly known as Carolina alumroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features basal leaves that are kidney-shaped with a hairy texture and reddish veins. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers in clusters during late spring to early summer. Its roots contain compounds such as lithospermic acid, which have been studied for potential medicinal properties.

It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.
