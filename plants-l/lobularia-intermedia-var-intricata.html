Lobularia intermedia var. intricata

Lobularia intermedia var. intricata, commonly known as the fragrant rockrose, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its small, showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is well adapted to dry, sunny conditions. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

The variety 'intricata' is distinguished by its intricate, branching patterns and dense foliage.
