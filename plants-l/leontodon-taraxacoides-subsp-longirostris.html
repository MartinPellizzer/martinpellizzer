Leontodon taraxacoides subsp. longirostris

Leontodon taraxacoides subsp.

longirostris, commonly known as the long-beaked dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy stems.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and woodland edges. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a rough texture, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a single head.

The subspecies is distinguished by its elongated, narrow flower stem, which gives it its common name.
