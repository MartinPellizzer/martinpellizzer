Lupinus achilleaphilus

Lupinus achilleaphilus, commonly known as the Achilles' heel lupin, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of violet or purple flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
