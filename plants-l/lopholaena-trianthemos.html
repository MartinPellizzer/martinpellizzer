Lopholaena trianthemos

Lopholaena trianthemos, commonly known as the white-flowered flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, pink to white flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Lopholaena trianthemos is also valued for its ability to provide shade and its role in supporting local wildlife.
