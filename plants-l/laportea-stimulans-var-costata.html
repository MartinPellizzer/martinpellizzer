Laportea stimulans var. costata

Laportea stimulans var. costata, commonly known as the serrated stimulans, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and along stream banks. Its botanical features include a creeping rhizomatous stem and showy inflorescences that attract pollinators.

It is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
