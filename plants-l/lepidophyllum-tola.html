Lepidophyllum tola

Lepidophyllum tola, commonly known as the tola cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, spherical shape and is covered in small, white spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking visual contrast to its green body. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
