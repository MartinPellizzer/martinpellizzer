Linum heterosepalum subsp. tianschanicum

Linum heterosepalum subsp.

tianschanicum, commonly known as the Tianshan flax, is a flowering plant native to the Tianshan mountain range in Central Asia. It is a member of the Linaceae family, which includes other flax species.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its flowers are usually blue or purple, with five petals that are often unequal in size, a characteristic feature of the species.
