Lilium shastense

Lilium shastense, commonly known as the Shasta lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and large, showy white or pinkish flowers with a distinctive red or orange stigma. The plant typically grows in open meadows and grasslands at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 6,000 feet. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a fan-like pattern around the stem.

The Shasta lily is a perennial herb that blooms in late summer to early fall.
