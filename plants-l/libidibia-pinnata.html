Libidibia pinnata

Libidibia pinnata, commonly known as the Brazilian wattle, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and often have a reddish or brownish coloration. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by elongated, pod-like fruits. It is typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in a variety of soil types.

The plant is also valued for its hard, durable wood, which has been used in traditional craftsmanship.
