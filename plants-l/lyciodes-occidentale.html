Lyciodes occidentale

Lyciodes occidentale, commonly known as western lyciodes, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are alternate and lance-shaped, while its flowers are usually pink or purple.

Lyciodes occidentale is not widely cultivated but is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations.
