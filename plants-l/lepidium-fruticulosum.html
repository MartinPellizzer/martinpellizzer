Lepidium fruticulosum

Lepidium fruticulosum, commonly known as the hairy cress, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants. The plant is characterized by its upright stems, small white or yellow flowers, and hairy leaves. It typically grows in moist environments such as meadows, riverbanks, and wetlands.

Lepidium fruticulosum is an annual or biennial herb that produces small, elongated pods containing several seeds.
