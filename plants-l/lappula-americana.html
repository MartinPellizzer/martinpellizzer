Lappula americana

Lappula americana, commonly known as American lappula, is an annual herb native to North America.

It belongs to the family Chenopodiaceae and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters. Its fruit is a three-lobed capsule that releases tiny seeds when mature.

Lappula americana is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
