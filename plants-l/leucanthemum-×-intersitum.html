Leucanthemum × intersitum

Leucanthemum × intersitum, commonly known as the oxeye daisy, is a hybrid perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its white petals with a yellow central disc, forming a daisy-like flower. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It has gray-green, hairy leaves that are opposite and ovate in shape.

Leucanthemum × intersitum is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is also a common wildflower in meadows and grasslands.
