Loasa leiolepis

Loasa leiolepis, commonly known as the silverleaf loasa, is a shrub native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped and have a slightly waxy texture.

Loasa leiolepis is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
