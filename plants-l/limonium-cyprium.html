Limonium cyprium

Limonium cyprium, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and striking, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found growing in coastal areas and saline soils. Limonium cyprium is a member of the Plumbagaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
