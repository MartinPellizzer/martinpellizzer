Limeum viscosum var. kraussii

Limeum viscosum var. kraussii, commonly known as the sticky lime or krauss's lime, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green, sticky leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that are typically clustered in terminal inflorescences. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species suitable for xeriscaping.

The fruit, which is a small, round berry, is edible and has a tart, citrus-like flavor.
