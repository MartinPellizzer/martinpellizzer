Lupinus michoacanus

Lupinus michoacanus, commonly known as the Michoacan lupine, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces sparsely branched inflorescences of pea-like flowers, typically in shades of violet or blue. Lupinus michoacanus is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

It plays a role in soil nitrogen fixation, contributing to ecosystem health in its native habitat.
