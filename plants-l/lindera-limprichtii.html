Lindera limprichtii

Lindera limprichtii, commonly known as the Chinese spicebush, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern China.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, elliptical leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by orange-red berries that are edible for birds.

Lindera limprichtii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
