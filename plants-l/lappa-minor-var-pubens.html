Lappa minor var. pubens

Lappa minor var. pubens, commonly known as the hairy bladderwort, is a small aquatic plant characterized by its unique adaptations for underwater life.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and marshes, where it anchors itself to submerged surfaces. The plant produces small, bladder-like structures that trap tiny aquatic organisms, aiding in its nutrient acquisition. Its leaves are usually thin and elongated, with a hairy texture that distinguishes it from other bladderwort species.

This variant is notable for its specialized morphology and ecological role in aquatic ecosystems.
