Lonicera webbiana

Lonicera webbiana, commonly known as the Webb's honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to South Africa.

It features opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are elliptical to ovate in shape. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange, appearing in clusters during the spring. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that develops in late summer.

Lonicera webbiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its showy blooms and adaptability to various soil conditions.
