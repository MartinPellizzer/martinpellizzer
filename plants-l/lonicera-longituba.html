Lonicera longituba

Lonicera longituba, commonly known as the Chinese honeysuckle, is a deciduous climbing vine native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to yellow, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that ripens in the fall, containing several seeds.

Lonicera longituba is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine.
