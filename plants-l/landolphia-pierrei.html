Landolphia pierrei

Landolphia pierrei, commonly known as the African buttercup, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and Southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its distinctive yellow-orange flowers that resemble those of the buttercup. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions for horticultural purposes.
