Lilium bulbiferum var. wilsonii

Lilium bulbiferum var. wilsonii, commonly known as Wilson's bulbous lily, is a striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range from white to deep pink, often with a distinctive red throat. The plant produces bulbous roots that store nutrients, allowing it to survive in harsh alpine conditions. Its leaves are long, narrow, and grass-like, typically arranged in a fan shape.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
