Lysimachia cuspidata

Lysimachia cuspidata, commonly known as sharpleaf chickweed, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a pointed tip, giving it its common name. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
