Laelia × zaslawskii

Laelia × zaslawskii, commonly known as the Zaslawskii orchid, is a hybrid orchid species resulting from the cross between Laelia purpurea and Laelia anceola.

It is native to the high altitudes of South America, particularly in regions of Argentina and Chile. The plant is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive yellow lip. Its foliage consists of long, strap-like leaves that are typically a deep green color.

Laelia × zaslawskii is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
