Lippia glabrescens

Lippia glabrescens, commonly known as Mexican blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the verbena family (Verbenaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green leaves and small, clustered blue or purple flowers. The plant is often found in dry, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields. It has a compact growth habit and is sometimes used in herbal remedies.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
