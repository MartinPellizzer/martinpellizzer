Liriodendron tulipifera var. flavum

Liriodendron tulipifera var. flavum, commonly known as the yellow tulip tree, is a variant of the tulip tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring and are shaped like small, cup-like tulips. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a heart-shaped base, often turning yellow in the fall. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens for its vibrant floral display.

It prefers full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for landscaping in temperate climates.
