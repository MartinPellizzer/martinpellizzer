Lychnis inclusa

Lychnis inclusa, commonly known as the narrowleaf catchfly, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers with five petals, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade.

Lychnis inclusa is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along roadsides, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
