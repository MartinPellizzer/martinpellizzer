Luzula glabrata var. hitchcockii

Luzula glabrata var. hitchcockii, commonly known as Hitchcock's woodrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually bluish-green in color and can grow up to 30 cm in height. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer, which are clustered in panicles.

This variety is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shady, wet conditions.
