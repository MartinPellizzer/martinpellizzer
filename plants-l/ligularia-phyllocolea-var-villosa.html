Ligularia phyllocolea var. villosa

Ligularia phyllocolea var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy ligularia, is a perennial plant native to eastern Asia.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers in late summer, which are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forested areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in humid climates.
