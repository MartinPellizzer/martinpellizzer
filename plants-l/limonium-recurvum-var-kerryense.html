Limonium recurvum var. kerryense

Limonium recurvum var. kerryense, commonly known as Kerry lavender, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of Ireland.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal dunes and cliffs. It has narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate salty sea air and strong winds.
