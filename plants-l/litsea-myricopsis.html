Litsea myricopsis

Litsea myricopsis, commonly known as the Chinese-laurel or Chinese sweet-scented laurier, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by spherical, blue-black berries that are edible. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Litsea myricopsis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in Chinese medicine.
