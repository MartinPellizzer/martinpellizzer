Litsea globifera

Litsea globifera, commonly known as the globe-fruit or globifera lauraceae, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate, ovate to elliptic leaves that are glossy and dark green on top. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers followed by spherical, berry-like fruits that are yellow when ripe. Litsea globifera is valued for its aromatic foliage and is often used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in humid, warm climates and is commonly found in lowland rainforests.
