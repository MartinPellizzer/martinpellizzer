Linum strictum var. alternum

Linum strictum var. alternum, commonly known as the alternate flax, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, erect stems that are often branched and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, linear, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
