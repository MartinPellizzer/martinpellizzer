Liparis abbreviata

Liparis abbreviata, commonly known as the dwarf lady's tresses, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and a dense, flowering spike that bears numerous small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it produces a single, narrow inflorescence.

Liparis abbreviata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
