Lycopodium pseudovarium

Lycopodium pseudovarium, commonly known as the false clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. The plant produces microphylls, which are small, primitive leaves that are characteristic of lycophytes. Its reproductive structures include strobila, which are cone-like clusters that bear sporangia containing spores.

Lycopodium pseudovarium plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
