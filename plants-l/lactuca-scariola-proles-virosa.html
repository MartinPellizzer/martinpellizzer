Lactuca scariola proles virosa

Lactuca scariola proles virosa, commonly known as the virusy sowthistle, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and lobed, pale green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a single head, which are followed by small, spiky seed heads. It is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain regions.

Its botanical features make it distinguishable from other members of the Lactuca genus.
