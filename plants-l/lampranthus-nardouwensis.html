Lampranthus nardouwensis

Lampranthus nardouwensis, commonly known as the Nardouw's ice plant, is a succulent shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems and leaves. It typically grows in rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Lampranthus nardouwensis is a popular choice for rock gardens due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appearance.
