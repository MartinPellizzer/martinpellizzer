Lachenalia pendula

Lachenalia pendula, commonly known as the hanging lachenalia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, fleshy leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the tips. The plant produces tall, branched stems that bear clusters of tubular, yellowish-orange flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Lachenalia pendula is well adapted to arid conditions, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
