Leptospermum grandiflorum var. minus

Leptospermum grandiflorum var. minus, commonly known as the small-flowered tea tree, is a compact shrub native to Tasmania.

It features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The leaves are narrow, leathery, and have a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a coastal plant.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for horticultural use.
