Lucuma procera

Lucuma procera, commonly known as the golden apple, is a tropical plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a member of the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its large, yellow-orange fruits that resemble small apples. The plant produces showy white to pale yellow flowers, which are typically borne in clusters. Its leaves are broad, glossy, and elliptical in shape, contributing to its ornamental value.

The fruit is rich in nutrients and has been traditionally used in local cuisines and herbal remedies.
