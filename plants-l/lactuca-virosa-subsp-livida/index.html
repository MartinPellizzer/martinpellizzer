Lactuca Virosa Subsp. Livida

Lactuca virosa subsp. livida, commonly known as the bitterleaf lettuce, is a lesser-known member of the dandelion family. This plant is characterized by its distinctive, often hairy leaves and a bitter taste that sets it apart from other lettuces. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in various parts of Europe and Asia. Despite its unappealing flavor, it has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits. Its ecological role and potential medicinal properties make it an intriguing subject for further study.
