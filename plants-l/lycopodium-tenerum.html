Lycopodium tenerum

Lycopodium tenerum, commonly known as the slender clubmoss, is a small evergreen shrub native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, scale-like leaves and has spore-bearing strobili that are typically clustered at the tips of its stems. Lycopodium tenerum thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
