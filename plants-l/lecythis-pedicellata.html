Lecythis pedicellata

Lecythis pedicellata, commonly known as the Amazonian butter tree, is a tropical tree native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its large, round leaves and produces a fruit with a thick, creamy pulp that contains a high amount of oils. The tree's bark and seeds are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits. Its ecological importance lies in its role as a food source for various wildlife species.

The plant is also valued for its economic potential due to the oil-rich seeds it produces.
