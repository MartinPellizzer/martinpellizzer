Lespedeza bicolor f. niveoflora

Lespedeza bicolor f. niveoflora, commonly known as the whiteflower lespedeza, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, showy flowers. The subspecies f. niveoflora is distinguished by its pale to white flowers, which contrast with the typical pink or red hues of the species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grassy areas.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
