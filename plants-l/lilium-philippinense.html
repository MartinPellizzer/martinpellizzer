Lilium philippinense

Lilium philippinense, commonly known as the Philippine lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas and is often found in montane forests. Its bulbs are an important part of its reproductive cycle, and the flowers come in shades of white, pink, and red.

The plant is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its cultural significance in local communities.
