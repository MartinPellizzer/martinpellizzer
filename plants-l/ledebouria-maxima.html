Ledebouria maxima

Ledebouria maxima, commonly known as the giant bulbous lily, is a striking bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, fleshy bulbs and its ability to produce tall, erect stems with showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late winter or early spring, producing clusters of white or pale pink flowers. Its leaves are strap-shaped and often remain above the soil during the dormant season.

Ledebouria maxima is well-suited to rocky or dry environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
