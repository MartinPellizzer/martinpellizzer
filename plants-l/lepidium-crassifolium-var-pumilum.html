Lepidium crassifolium var. pumilum

Lepidium crassifolium var. pumilum, commonly known as the dwarf wall pepper, is a low-growing plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, fleshy leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or arid environments. Its fruit, a small, round seedpod, contains several seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
