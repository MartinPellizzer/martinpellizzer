Lampocarya lacera

Lampocarya lacera, commonly known as the slender sandverbena, is a low-growing annual plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically reaches a height of about 10 to 20 centimeters and has thin, branching stems covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped, opposite, and often have a slightly rough texture.

Lampocarya lacera is adapted to arid environments and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
