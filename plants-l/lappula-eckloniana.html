Lappula eckloniana

Lappula eckloniana, commonly known as the coastal lappula, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along coastal areas and inland regions. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a hairy texture.

Lappula eckloniana is adapted to tolerate saline and arid conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
