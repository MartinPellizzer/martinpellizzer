Libanotis pyrenaica subsp. bipinnata

Libanotis pyrenaica subsp.

bipinnata, commonly known as the Pyrenean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Pyrenees mountain range. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of bell-shaped blue or violet flowers.

The subspecies bipinnata is distinguished by its finely divided, bipinnate leaves, which give it a delicate, feathery appearance. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is typically found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive floral display.
