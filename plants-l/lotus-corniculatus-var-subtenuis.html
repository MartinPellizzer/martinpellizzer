Lotus corniculatus var. subtenuis

Lotus corniculatus var. subtenuis, commonly known as the slender bird's-foot trefoil, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has small, hairy stems and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

It is a perennial herb that can grow up to 30 cm tall, and it plays a role in soil stabilization due to its extensive root system.
