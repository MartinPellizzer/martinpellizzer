Lolium temulentum var. album

Lolium temulentum var. album, commonly known as white ryegrass, is a variant of the annual ryegrass species native to Europe.

It is characterized by its pale to white florets, distinguishing it from the more common green varieties of ryegrass. This plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides. It has a tufted growth habit with slender, grass-like leaves.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
