Lepidium foliosum var. cuneifolium

Lepidium foliosum var. cuneifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with small, cross-shaped flowers. The plant produces yellow or white flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is often found growing in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes.
