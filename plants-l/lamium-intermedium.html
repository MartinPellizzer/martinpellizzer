Lamium intermedium

Lamium intermedium, commonly known as the greater dead-nettle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

The plant is also valued in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
