Liatris elegans f. fisheri

Liatris elegans f. fisheri, commonly known as the Fishers' blazing star, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features upright, spike-like flower clusters that bloom in shades of pink to purple, typically from late summer to early fall. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils in prairies and woodland edges.
