Lepeostegeres alternifolius

Lepeostegeres alternifolius, commonly known as the alternate-leaved water fern, is a species of aquatic fern found in freshwater habitats.

It is characterized by its alternate arrangement of feather-like leaves that extend from a central stem. The plant typically grows in shallow, still waters and is often found in marshes and wetlands. Its fronds are divided into smaller, lance-shaped segments, which help in maximizing light absorption.

The plant plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen to surrounding water life.
