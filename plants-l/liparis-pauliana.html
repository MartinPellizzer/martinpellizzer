Liparis pauliana

Liparis pauliana, commonly known as the Pauliana orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
