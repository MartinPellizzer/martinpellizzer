Lasiocorys abyssinica var. brachycalyx

Lasiocorys abyssinica var. brachycalyx, commonly known as the Abyssinian hibiscus, is a shrub native to Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are ovate with a hairy surface, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local communities.
