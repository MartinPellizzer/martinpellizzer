Ligustrum japonicum var. rotundifolium

Ligustrum japonicum var. rotundifolium, commonly known as the Japanese privet, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features small, dark green, oval-shaped leaves that are rounded at the base, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by dark purple berries in the fall. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions if not managed properly.
