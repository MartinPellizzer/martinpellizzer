Lotus arabicus var. trigonelloides

Lotus arabicus var. trigonelloides, commonly known as the trigonellate lotus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy pink or purple flowers. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species adapted to drought conditions. Its seeds are often used in traditional herbal medicine and are valued for their nutritional content.

The plant also plays a role in soil nitrogen fixation, contributing to ecological balance in its native habitats.
