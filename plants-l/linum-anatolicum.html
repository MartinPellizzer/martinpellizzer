Linum anatolicum

Linum anatolicum, commonly known as Anatolian flax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fibrous seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil.

Linum anatolicum thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.
