Lithospermum fruticosum f. consobrinum

Lithospermum fruticosum f. consobrinum, commonly known as the pink rock pepper, is a flowering plant in the family Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, which bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a small, dry capsule that contains several seeds.

This variety is often found in rocky or sandy soils in arid or semi-arid regions.
