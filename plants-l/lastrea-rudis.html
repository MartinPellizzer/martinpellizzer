Lastrea rudis

Lastrea rudis, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, often spiny foliage. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and well-drained soils. Its blue to violet flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Lastrea rudis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and aesthetic qualities.
