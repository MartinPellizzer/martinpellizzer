Lightfootia rubioides

Lightfootia rubioides, commonly known as the redstem lightfootia, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, sprawling growth habit and small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its woody stems are often reddish in color, giving it its common name.

Lightfootia rubioides is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.
