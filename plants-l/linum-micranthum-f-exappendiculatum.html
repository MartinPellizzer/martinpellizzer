Linum micranthum f. exappendiculatum

Linum micranthum f. exappendiculatum, commonly known as the small-flowered flax, is a botanical variant of the flax species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and smaller, more numerous flowers compared to the typical form. The plant features slender, branched stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are narrow and alternate. The flowers are usually pale blue or white, with five petals and a prominent central stamen.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
