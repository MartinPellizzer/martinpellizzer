Lactuca candolleana

Lactuca candolleana, commonly known as the Candolleana lettuce, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and other composite flowers. The plant features basal rosettes of spoon-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and have a bitter taste. It produces small yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, which are followed by small, dry fruits called achenes.

Lactuca candolleana is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
