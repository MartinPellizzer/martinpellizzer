Lepanthes tibouchinicola

Lepanthes tibouchinicola, commonly known as the Tibouchine orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as cloud forests, where it thrives in the understory. Its leaves are usually broad and slightly fleshy, adapted to retain moisture in its native habitat.

The flowers are usually small and clustered, displaying a range of colors from white to pale pink, depending on the specific variety.
