Lupinus gracilentus

Lupinus gracilentus, commonly known as the slender lupine, is a flowering plant native to western North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and clusters of pea-like flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Lupinus gracilentus is a member of the legume family and has the ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it ecologically significant.
