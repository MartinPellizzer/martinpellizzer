Loranthus dregei var. foliaceus

Loranthus dregei var. foliaceus, commonly known as the leafy mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to its host tree for nutrients and water.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers, followed by small, fleshy berries that are often consumed by birds. Due to its parasitic nature, it can have significant ecological impacts on its host species.
