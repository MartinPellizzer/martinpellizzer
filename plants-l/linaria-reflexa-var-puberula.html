Linaria reflexa var. puberula

Linaria reflexa var. puberula, commonly known as the hairy toadflax, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, branched stems that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers with two lips, typical of the Plantaginaceae family. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.
