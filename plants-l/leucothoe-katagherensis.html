Leucothoe katagherensis

Leucothoe katagherensis, commonly known as Katagher's leucothoe, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped white or pale pink flowers in late winter to early spring, followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. Its foliage remains evergreen throughout the year, providing year-round interest in the landscape.

Leucothoe katagherensis is well adapted to acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade.
