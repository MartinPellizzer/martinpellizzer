Lycopodiella geometra

Lycopodiella geometra, commonly known as the geometrid lycopod, is a small, evergreen fern-like plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, scale-like leaves that are tightly arranged around the stem. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices, where it can thrive in low light conditions. Lycopodiella geometra is often found in temperate regions and is adapted to retain moisture in its spore-producing structures.

Its reproductive system involves the production of spores rather than seeds, which is a key feature of its evolutionary lineage.
