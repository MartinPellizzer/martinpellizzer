Litobrochia aghardii

Litobrochia aghardii, commonly known as the Aghardii rock cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers. Native to the mountainous regions of the Eastern Mediterranean, it thrives in rocky and limestone soils. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, exhibiting drought tolerance through its succulent leaves.

Its floral structure is typical of the Brassicaceae family, with four petals arranged in a cross shape.
