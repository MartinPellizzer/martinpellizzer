Lygodium venustum

Lygodium venustum, commonly known as the creeping mistletoe, is a climbing fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Lygodiaceae and is characterized by its aerial rhizomes that allow it to climb and attach to trees. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with serrated margins and produces small, yellowish-green spores in sporangia located on the underside of its fronds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forests and along stream banks.

Its unique growth habit and reproductive structures make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
