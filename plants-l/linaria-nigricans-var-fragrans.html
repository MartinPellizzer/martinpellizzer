Linaria nigricans var. fragrans

Linaria nigricans var. fragrans, commonly known as the fragrant toadflax, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features slender, branched stems that produce clusters of small, tubular flowers with a distinctive orange to yellow coloration. The plant is characterized by its opposite, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers emit a subtle, sweet fragrance, which attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This species is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
