Launaea nanella

Launaea nanella, commonly known as the small launaea, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, characterized by its compact, spiny stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Launaea nanella is adapted to survive in harsh environments, with mechanisms to store water and reduce transpiration.

It is often found in rocky soils and is used in traditional medicine in some regions for its purported health benefits.
