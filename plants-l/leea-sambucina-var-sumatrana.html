Leea sambucina var. sumatrana

Leea sambucina var. sumatrana, commonly known as the Sumatran elderberry, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Leeaceae and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, dark berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Its leaves are ovate with a serrated margin and emit a mild aromatic scent.
