Loranthus podopterus

Loranthus podopterus, commonly known as the red-stemmed mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of host trees, primarily in the genus Eucalyptus.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using the light it captures. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers and red or orange berries that are often consumed by birds. Its seeds are dispersed by animals, contributing to its spread within forest ecosystems.

The plant's parasitic nature and unique reproductive strategies make it an interesting subject in botanical studies.
