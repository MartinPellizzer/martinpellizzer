Lerchia vermiculata

Lerchia vermiculata, commonly known as the golden thread orchid, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, thread-like leaves and small, fragrant flowers that often appear in shades of yellow and green. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest understories, and prefers well-drained, humus-rich soil. Lerchia vermiculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and subtle floral display.

Its growth habit is generally slow, making it a prized addition to specialized collections.
