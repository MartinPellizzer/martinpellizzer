Lavandula antiatlantica

Lavandula antiatlantica, commonly known as the Canary Island lavender, is a species native to the Canary Islands.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and spikes of purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for Mediterranean climates. Its essential oils, rich in linalool and lavandine, are valued for their fragrance and medicinal properties.

Due to its limited natural range, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
