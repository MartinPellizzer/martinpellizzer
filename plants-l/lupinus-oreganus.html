Lupinus oreganus

Lupinus oreganus, commonly known as the Oregon lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny areas such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Lupinus oreganus plays an important role in ecological systems by fixing nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plant life.
