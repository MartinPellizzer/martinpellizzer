Lens camelorum

Lens camelorum, commonly known as the desert pea, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, showy pink or purple pea-shaped flowers. The plant has small, compound leaves and produces pods that contain several seeds. It is well adapted to dry environments, with deep roots that help it access water from deeper soil layers.

Lens camelorum is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
