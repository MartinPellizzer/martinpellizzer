Luisia volucris

Luisia volucris, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are bright yellow, attracting various pollinators.

Luisia volucris is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
