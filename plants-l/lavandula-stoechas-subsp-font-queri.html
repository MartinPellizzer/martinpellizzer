Lavandula stoechas subsp. font-queri

Lavandula stoechas subsp.

font-queri, commonly known as the Spanish lavender, is a subspecies native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its distinctive violet-blue flowers and narrow, gray-green leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun. It is a compact shrub, often reaching heights of 30 to 60 centimeters.

Its aromatic foliage and showy blooms make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
