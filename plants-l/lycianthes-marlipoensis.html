Lycianthes marlipoensis

Lycianthes marlipoensis, commonly known as the Marlipo nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, star-shaped flowers that typically bloom in the evening. The plant produces small, round berries that are usually green when unripe and turn red when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
