Lilium martagon var. hirsutum

Lilium martagon var. hirsutum, commonly known as the Hirsute Turk's Cap Lily, is a striking perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink to red in color, arranged in a loose, cup-like inflorescence. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine, hairy textures, giving it its "hirsutum" designation. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.

The blooms appear in late spring to early summer, adding a dramatic visual impact to the landscape.
