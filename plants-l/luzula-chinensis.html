Luzula chinensis

Luzula chinensis, commonly known as Chinese woodrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, commonly known as the grass family, and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences with pale blue to white flowers. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

Luzula chinensis is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
