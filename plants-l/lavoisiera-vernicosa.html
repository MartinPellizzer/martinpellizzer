Lavoisiera vernicosa

Lavoisiera vernicosa, commonly known as the sticky lily, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces a sticky resin that coats its leaves and stems, which may serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique botanical features make it an interesting subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
