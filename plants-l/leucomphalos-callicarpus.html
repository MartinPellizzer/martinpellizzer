Leucomphalos callicarpus

Leucomphalos callicarpus, commonly known as the purple-leafed false heather, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring dense, compact foliage with striking purple leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are often glandular and can exude a milky sap when damaged.

This species is adapted to dry, rocky environments and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
