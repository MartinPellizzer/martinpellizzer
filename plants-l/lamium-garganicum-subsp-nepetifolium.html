Lamium garganicum subsp. nepetifolium

Lamium garganicum subsp.

nepetifolium, commonly known as the cat's ear plant, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive velvety texture and a deep green color.

The plant produces clusters of small, two-lipped flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its common name derives from the shape of its leaves, which resemble a cat's ear.
