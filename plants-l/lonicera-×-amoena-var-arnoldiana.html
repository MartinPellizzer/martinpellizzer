Lonicera × amoena var. arnoldiana

Lonicera × amoena var. arnoldiana, commonly known as the golden honeysuckle, is a hybrid plant that exhibits characteristics of both its parent species.

It is a deciduous shrub native to the United States, particularly in the southeastern region. The plant is known for its fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring. It produces red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds but not recommended for human consumption.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
