Limnia exigua

Limnia exigua, commonly known as the small water flea, is a tiny crustacean belonging to the order Cladocera.

It is characterized by its transparent exoskeleton and a segmented body with a distinct head and tail. This species is typically found in freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. Limnia exigua plays a crucial role in aquatic ecosystems as a primary consumer, feeding on algae and organic detritus.

Its short life cycle and high reproductive rate make it an important indicator of water quality and ecosystem health.
