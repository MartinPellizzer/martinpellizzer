Liparis chalandei

Liparis chalandei, commonly known as the Chalandei orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring or early summer, producing small, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive lip. It prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
