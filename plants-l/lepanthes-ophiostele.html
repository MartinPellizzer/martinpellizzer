Lepanthes ophiostele

Lepanthes ophiostele, commonly known as the Ophiostele orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as cloud forests, where it attaches itself to trees or rocks. Its flowers are usually small and have a complex structure with intricate petal arrangements.

Lepanthes ophiostele is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
