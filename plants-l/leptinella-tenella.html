Leptinella tenella

Leptinella tenella, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically forms dense mats of tiny, needle-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or white, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its compact form and ornamental appeal, it is occasionally cultivated in rock gardens or as a ground cover.
