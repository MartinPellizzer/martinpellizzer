Launaea angustifolius

Launaea angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf launaea, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive foliage. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, adapting to its harsh environment. It produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which attract various pollinators.
