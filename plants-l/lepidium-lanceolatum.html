Lepidium lanceolatum

Lepidium lanceolatum, commonly known as the lanceleaf cress, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like mustard and cabbage. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats and features small, lance-shaped leaves. It produces yellow flowers that are followed by slender, cylindrical pods containing tiny seeds.

Lepidium lanceolatum is often used in native landscaping due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
