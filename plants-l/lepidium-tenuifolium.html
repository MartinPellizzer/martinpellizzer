Lepidium tenuifolium

Lepidium tenuifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved wallflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been introduced to other regions.

Lepidium tenuifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in herbal medicine.
