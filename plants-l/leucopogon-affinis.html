Leucopogon affinis

Leucopogon affinis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heaths and heathers, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils in open woodlands or heathlands. Its flowers are small, bell-shaped, and usually pink or white, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Leucopogon affinis is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
