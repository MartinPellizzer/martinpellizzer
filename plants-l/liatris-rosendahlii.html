Liatris rosendahlii

Liatris rosendahlii, commonly known as Rosendahl's blazing star, is a flowering plant native to the central and southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant produces tall, sparsely branched stems that bear clusters of lavender to pinkish-purple flowers. Rosendahl's blazing star thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
