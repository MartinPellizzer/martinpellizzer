Leucanthemum aemilianii

Leucanthemum aemilianii, commonly known as Shasta daisy, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its white ray florets surrounding a central cluster of yellow disk florets, giving it a daisy-like appearance. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It prefers well-drained soil and is often used in gardens for its ornamental value.

Leucanthemum aemilianii is hardy in USDA zones 5 through 9 and is known for its long blooming period from late spring to early autumn.
