Lettsomia setosa var. obovata

Lettsomia setosa var. obovata, commonly known as the broad-leafed lettsomia, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of Southeast Asia.

It features lanceolate to obovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, often displaying a vibrant green color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, typically arranged in terminal inflorescences. Its root system is fibrous and well-developed, allowing it to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.
