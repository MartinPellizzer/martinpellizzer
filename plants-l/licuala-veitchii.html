Licuala veitchii

Licuala veitchii, commonly known as the Veitch's palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers arranged in a branched inflorescence, followed by dark red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
