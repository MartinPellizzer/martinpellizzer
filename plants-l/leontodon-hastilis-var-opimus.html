Leontodon hastilis var. opimus

Leontodon hastilis var. opimus, commonly known as the greater purpletop, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It produces showy purple to lavender flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Its fibrous roots and robust growth make it a resilient species adapted to arid environments.
