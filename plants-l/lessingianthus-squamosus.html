Lessingianthus squamosus

Lessingianthus squamosus, commonly known as the scaly sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features vibrant yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers, typical of the family. Its leaves are often covered in a dense layer of fine, scaly hairs, giving it its common name.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
