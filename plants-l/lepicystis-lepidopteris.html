Lepicystis lepidopteris

Lepicystis lepidopteris, commonly known as the golden thread orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and delicate, yellow-green flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often forming a rosette at the base, while its inflorescence consists of a single raceme of small, fragrant blooms.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
