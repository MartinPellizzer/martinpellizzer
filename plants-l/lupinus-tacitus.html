Lupinus tacitus

Lupinus tacitus, commonly known as the western lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and woodlands. The plant features upright stems with clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Lupinus tacitus plays an important role in soil nitrogen fixation due to its symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.
