Leptactina liebrechtsiana var. mollis

Leptactina liebrechtsiana var. mollis, commonly known as the soft-leaved leptactina, is a rare and delicate fern native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, soft, and silky-textured fronds that give it a unique appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories where it thrives in cool, humid conditions. Its reproductive structures are small and inconspicuous, typical of many ferns, and it relies on spores for propagation.
