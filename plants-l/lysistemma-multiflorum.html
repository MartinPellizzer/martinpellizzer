Lysistemma multiflorum

Lysistemma multiflorum, commonly known as the desert mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations such as succulent leaves and deep root systems to conserve water. Its bright yellow to orange flowers bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Lysistemma multiflorum is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
