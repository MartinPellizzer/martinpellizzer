Leptopus pachyphyllus

Leptopus pachyphyllus, commonly known as the thick-leaved lichen, is a unique organism that belongs to the Ascomycota division.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery thallus that is typically found on rocky or tree bark surfaces. The plant forms a symbiotic relationship with algae, which contributes to its greenish or yellowish coloration. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in temperate regions.

Due to its slow growth and specialized habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
