Lithospermum angustifolium

Lithospermum angustifolium, commonly known as narrowleaf lithosperm, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale purple to reddish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its roots contain compounds such as lithospermic acid, which have been studied for potential medicinal properties.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in open habitats.
