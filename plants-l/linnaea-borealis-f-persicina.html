Linnaea borealis f. persicina

Linnaea borealis f. persicina, commonly known as the purple-flowered twinflower, is a variant of the twinflower plant native to northern regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

This botanical form is characterized by its distinctive purple flowers, which contrast with the typical white blooms of the species. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in boreal and subarctic environments. The plant is a low-growing evergreen shrub with opposite, ovate leaves.

Its flowers are borne in pairs on short stems, giving it the common name "twinflower.".
