Lepanthes thalia

Lepanthes thalia, commonly known as the "Queen of the Night," is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that often feature vibrant colors such as white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in the understory of tropical rainforests. Its flowers are known for their delicate structure and nocturnal fragrance, which attracts specific pollinators.

Lepanthes thalia is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and rarity in cultivation.
