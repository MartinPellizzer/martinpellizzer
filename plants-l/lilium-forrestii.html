Lilium forrestii

Lilium forrestii, commonly known as the Forster's lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer, producing white or pale pink blossoms with prominent yellow stamens. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas at high altitudes.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
