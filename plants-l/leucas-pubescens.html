Leucas pubescens

Leucas pubescens, commonly known as hairy leucas, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color.

Leucas pubescens is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
