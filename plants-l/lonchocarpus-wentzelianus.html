Lonchocarpus wentzelianus

Lonchocarpus wentzelianus, commonly known as the red bean tree, is a tropical leguminous plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmately compound leaves and striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces cylindrical pods containing edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nutritional benefits.

The species is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.
