Lophiarella pumila

Lophiarella pumila, commonly known as the dwarf lophiaria, is a small, perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant features small, oval-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white. It is known for its compact growth habit and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens.

Lophiarella pumila is relatively drought-tolerant once established, making it suitable for xeriscaping.
