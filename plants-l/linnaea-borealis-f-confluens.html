Linnaea borealis f. confluens

Linnaea borealis f. confluens, commonly known as the twinflower, is a rare and unique variant of the twinflower plant native to northern regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

This form is characterized by its distinctive two-flowered inflorescence, where two small, pinkish-white flowers are borne on a single stem. The plant is a low-growing evergreen shrub with opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments such as boreal forests and rocky slopes, where it forms dense mats.
