Lobelia triquetra var. microdon

Lobelia triquetra var. microdon, commonly known as the dwarf lobelia, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in dense clusters along the stems. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
