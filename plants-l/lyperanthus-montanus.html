Lyperanthus montanus

Lyperanthus montanus, commonly known as the mountain lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its erect stem and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in a fine layer of hair to reduce water loss.

Lyperanthus montanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
