Leonurus macranthus

Leonurus macranthus, commonly known as the greater betony, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its square stems and opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Leonurus macranthus is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along roadsides.
