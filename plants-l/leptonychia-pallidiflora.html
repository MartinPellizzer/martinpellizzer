Leptonychia pallidiflora

Leptonychia pallidiflora, commonly known as the pale-flowered rock orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, pale pink or white flowers arranged in a raceme, which bloom during the cooler months. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in a fine layer of hair to reduce water loss.

Leptonychia pallidiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
