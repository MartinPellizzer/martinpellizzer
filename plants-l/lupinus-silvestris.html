Lupinus silvestris

Lupinus silvestris, commonly known as the wild lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue or purple flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces cylindrical seed pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands, prairies, and disturbed areas.

Lupinus silvestris plays an important role in ecological systems by fixing nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plant life.
