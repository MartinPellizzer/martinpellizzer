Lotus argophyllus var. niveus

Lotus argophyllus var. niveus, commonly known as the white lotus, is a striking aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in the early morning and close by midday. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, providing a habitat for various aquatic organisms. Its roots are submerged, anchoring the plant in muddy or sandy substrates.

The white lotus is also valued for its ornamental beauty and has cultural significance in many Asian countries.
