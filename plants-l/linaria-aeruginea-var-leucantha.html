Linaria aeruginea var. leucantha

Linaria aeruginea var. leucantha, commonly known as the white toadflax, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The flowers are typically white or pale yellow, with a distinctive two-lipped structure typical of the Plantaginaceae family. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
