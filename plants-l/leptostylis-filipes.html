Leptostylis filipes

Leptostylis filipes, commonly known as the slender-stemmed grass, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the Poaceae family.

Leptostylis filipes is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
