Licaria macrophylla

Licaria macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf licaria, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
