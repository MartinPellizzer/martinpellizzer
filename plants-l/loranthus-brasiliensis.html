Loranthus brasiliensis

Loranthus brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on a variety of host trees, primarily in the Neotropical region.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own carbohydrates through photosynthesis using the light it captures. The plant has small, scale-like leaves and produces white or yellowish flowers that are often visited by insects for pollination. Its berries, which contain toxic compounds, are dispersed by birds that eat the fruit.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, influencing host tree health and supporting bird populations.
