Leersia abyssinica

Leersia abyssinica, commonly known as African wild rice, is a perennial grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes and along riverbanks.

Leersia abyssinica is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting biodiversity.
