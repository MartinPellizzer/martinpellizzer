Linnaea borealis f. nicolai

Linnaea borealis f. nicolai, commonly known as the Nicolai's twinflower, is a rare and distinctive variant of the twinflower plant.

It is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the boreal forests of northern Europe and North America. The plant features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in pairs, typically in early spring. Its leaves are oval-shaped and have a leathery texture, adapted to the cold climate.

This form is notable for its unique floral structure and is often found in shaded, moist environments.
