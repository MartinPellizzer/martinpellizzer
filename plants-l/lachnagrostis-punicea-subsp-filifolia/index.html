Lachnagrostis Punicea Subsp. Filifolia

Red hair grass, scientifically known as Lachnagrostis punicea subsp. filifolia, is a strikingly beautiful plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions. Its name derives from its distinctive crimson-colored inflorescences that resemble strands of red hair. This grass thrives in harsh, cold environments, often growing in tundra and alpine habitats. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife. Despite its delicate appearance, it is remarkably resilient, surviving extreme weather conditions with minimal care.
