Lagonychium

Lagonychium, commonly known as the golden star, is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers.

It is native to South America and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its unique texture. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Lagonychium is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant color and attractive foliage.
