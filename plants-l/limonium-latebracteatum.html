Limonium latebracteatum

Limonium latebracteatum, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in saline environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-gray hue.

Limonium latebracteatum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh coastal conditions.
