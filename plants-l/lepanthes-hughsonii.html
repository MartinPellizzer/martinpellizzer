Lepanthes hughsonii

Lepanthes hughsonii, commonly known as the Hughson's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as cloud forests, where it relies on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Lepanthes hughsonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts of rare orchids.
