Lepanthes maxillaris

Lepanthes maxillaris, commonly known as the pink lady's tresses, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, grass-like leaves and its striking clusters of pink or white flowers that resemble a tresses of hair. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and stream banks. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its small, fragrant blooms.

Lepanthes maxillaris is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in the right conditions.
