Lycium mascarenense

Lycium mascarenense, commonly known as the Mascarene boxthorn, is a shrub native to the Mascarene Islands in the Indian Ocean.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces orange-red berries that are edible but have a tart flavor. It thrives in coastal and dry habitats, showing adaptability to saline and arid conditions.

Lycium mascarenense plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for birds and small mammals.
