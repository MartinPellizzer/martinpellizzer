Lepidium sagittulatum

Lepidium sagittulatum, commonly known as the arrowleaf peppergrass, is a small annual herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland habitats. The plant has slender, branched stems and small, arrow-shaped leaves that are usually hairy. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, rounded seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Lepidium sagittulatum is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard plants.
