Lophatherum gracile f. musashiense

Lophatherum gracile f. musashiense, commonly known as the Musashi fine lemongrass, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves that emit a citrus-like scent when crushed. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, pale green flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
