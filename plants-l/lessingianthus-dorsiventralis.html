Lessingianthus dorsiventralis

Lessingianthus dorsiventralis, commonly known as the wavyleaf sunflower, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant features alternate, lanceolate leaves with a distinctive wavy margin, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in grasslands and open habitats.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a nectar source for various pollinators.
