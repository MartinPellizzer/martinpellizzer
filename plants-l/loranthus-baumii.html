Loranthus baumii

Loranthus baumii, commonly known as the Chinese mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while still performing photosynthesis using some retained chlorophyll. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers and red berries that are dispersed by birds. It is native to regions of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

Its parasitic nature and unique ecological role make it an interesting subject in botanical studies.
