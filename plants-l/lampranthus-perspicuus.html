Lampranthus perspicuus

Lampranthus perspicuus, commonly known as the rainbow flower, is a member of the Aizoaceae family, native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble petals, which are actually modified leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Lampranthus perspicuus is known for its ability to store water in its fleshy leaves, allowing it to survive in arid conditions.
