Linum san-sabeanum

Linum san-sabeanum, commonly known as the San-Sabean flax, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Linaceae family, which includes other flax species. The plant produces small, delicate blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, adapted to conserve water in its harsh environment.

Linum san-sabeanum is typically found in rocky soils and is an important part of the local ecosystem.
