Lavandula macra

Lavandula macra, commonly known as the spike lavender, is a species of flowering plant in the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and spikes of purple or violet flowers. The leaves are gray-green and aromatic, often used in aromatherapy and herbal remedies. This plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its compact size and ornamental value make it suitable for rock gardens and containers.
