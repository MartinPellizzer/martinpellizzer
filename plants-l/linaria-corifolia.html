Linaria corifolia

Linaria corifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf toadflax, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of yellow-orange flowers. The leaves are narrow and linear, typically arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

The plant is known for its showy, spurred flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
