Lobelia hassleri

Lobelia hassleri, commonly known as the Sierra lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, tubular blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and meadows. Lobelia hassleri has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators like bees and butterflies.
