Lupinus dorae var. dorae

Lupinus dorae var. dorae, commonly known as the Dorae lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces slender, bean-like pods that contain several seeds.

Lupinus dorae var. dorae is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes.
