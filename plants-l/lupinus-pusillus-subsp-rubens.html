Lupinus pusillus subsp. rubens

Lupinus pusillus subsp.

rubens, commonly known as the red dwarf lupine, is a small, herbaceous perennial native to the western United States. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant features small, clustered blue or purple flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting nearby plants.
