Lupinus latifolius subsp. wigginsii

Lupinus latifolius subsp.

wigginsii, commonly known as Wiggins' broadleaf lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States. It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and tall spires of violet to blue flowers.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry slopes and grasslands. Its nodules contain nitrogen-fixing bacteria, contributing to soil fertility.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
