Lycopodium mollicomum

Lycopodium mollicomum, commonly known as the velvet clubmoss, is a small evergreen fern that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is characterized by its delicate, branching stems covered in a fine, velvety coating of spores. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are clustered in dense, club-like formations. It is typically found in temperate regions, often in forest floors or rocky crevices.

Lycopodium mollicomum plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
