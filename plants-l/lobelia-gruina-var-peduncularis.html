Lobelia gruina var. peduncularis

Lobelia gruina var. peduncularis, commonly known as the clustered bluebell, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to violet. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
