Lepanthes bicarinata

Lepanthes bicarinata, commonly known as the two-ribbed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically range in color from white to pale purple. The plant grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its leaves are usually broad and strap-like, adapted for capturing moisture from the air.

Lepanthes bicarinata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
