Leptospermum attenuatum

Leptospermum attenuatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tea tree, is a small evergreen shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It typically grows in coastal heathlands and sandy soils, exhibiting a compact, bushy growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in summer, which are followed by red or orange berries. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, often with a distinctive aromatic scent.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional Aboriginal medicine.
