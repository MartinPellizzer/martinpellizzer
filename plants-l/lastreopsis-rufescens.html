Lastreopsis rufescens

Lastreopsis rufescens, commonly known as the red-stemmed flax, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, reddish stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
