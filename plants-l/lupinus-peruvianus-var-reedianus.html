Lupinus peruvianus var. reedianus

Lupinus peruvianus var. reedianus, commonly known as the Peruvian lupine, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers. The plant has palmately divided leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to the cooler, high-altitude environments of its native habitat.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
