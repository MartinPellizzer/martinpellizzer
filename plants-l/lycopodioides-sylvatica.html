Lycopodioides sylvatica

Lycopodioides sylvatica, commonly known as the forest clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and woodlands. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its compact, creeping stems and small, scale-like leaves. It produces spores in cone-like structures called strobila, which are borne on the upper parts of the plant.

Lycopodioides sylvatica plays an important ecological role in maintaining soil stability and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
