Lathyrus palustris f. pallida

Lathyrus palustris f. pallida, commonly known as the pale marsh vetch, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in Europe.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a preference for moist, nutrient-rich soils.

It plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and provides nectar for pollinators.
