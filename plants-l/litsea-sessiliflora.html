Litsea sessiliflora

Litsea sessiliflora, commonly known as the sessile-flower litsea, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and avocados, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy texture, while its fruit is a small, berry-like drupe.

Litsea sessiliflora is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in some cultures.
