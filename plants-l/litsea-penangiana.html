Litsea penangiana

Litsea penangiana, commonly known as the Penang laurier, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurel and avocado trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically white or pale yellow and are clustered in panicles.

The fruit is a small, globose drupe that turns from green to dark purple as it matures.
