Lilium philippinense var. formosanum

Lilium philippinense var. formosanum, commonly known as the Formosan lily, is a rare and endemic species native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in forested areas and has lance-shaped leaves with a smooth texture. It produces a single flower per stem, which blooms in late summer to early autumn.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
