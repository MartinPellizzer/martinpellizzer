Laetia parviflora

Laetia parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered laetia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring slender stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, blooming from late spring to early summer. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in well-drained soils, often found in woodland or shaded areas.

Laetia parviflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
