Ligusticum oliverianum

Ligusticum oliverianum, commonly known as the Oliver's angelica, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescences.

It is a biennial herb that typically grows in moist, woodland habitats across Europe and parts of Asia. The plant features large, pinnately compound leaves with a distinctive, fern-like appearance. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in complex umbels, and it produces small, dark brown fruits.

The plant is noted for its strong, spicy scent and has been used historically in traditional medicine.
