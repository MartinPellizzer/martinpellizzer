Lycium californicum subsp. carinatum

Lycium californicum subsp.

carinatum, commonly known as the California boxthorn, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its spiny, woody stems and small, red berries that ripen in the fall.

The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
