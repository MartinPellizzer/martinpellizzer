Lechea mucronata var. simplex

Lechea mucronata var. simplex, commonly known as the slender leaf milkweed, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow to greenish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and can tolerate drought conditions.
