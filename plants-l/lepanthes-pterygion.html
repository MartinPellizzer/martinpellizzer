Lepanthes pterygion

Lepanthes pterygion, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers with intricate patterns and a distinctive, elongated lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical forests. Its leaves are usually narrow and arranged in a rosette, helping it capture moisture from the air.

Lepanthes pterygion is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
