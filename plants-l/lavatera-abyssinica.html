Lavatera abyssinica

Lavatera abyssinica, commonly known as Abyssinian woolly bush, is a perennial shrub native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Lavatera abyssinica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local communities.
