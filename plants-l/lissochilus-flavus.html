Lissochilus flavus

Lissochilus flavus, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or forested areas. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture from the air, a feature common in epiphytic orchids.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in horticulture.
