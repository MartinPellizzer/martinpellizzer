Lonicera trichosepala

Lonicera trichosepala, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of yellow to orange, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that matures in the fall, attracting various wildlife.

Lonicera trichosepala is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
