Lobelia sylvatica

Lobelia sylvatica, commonly known as the wood lobelia, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and spikes of blue or purple flowers. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It has a fibrous root system and is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value.

Lobelia sylvatica is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and maintaining biodiversity in forest ecosystems.
