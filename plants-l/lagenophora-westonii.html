Lagenophora westonii

Lagenophora westonii, commonly known as the Westonia lagoon or Westonia lagoon orchid, is a tropical flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, tropical environments. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, and it produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green.

Lagenophora westonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
