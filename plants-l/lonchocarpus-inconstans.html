Lonchocarpus inconstans

Lonchocarpus inconstans, commonly known as the red mimoso, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, woody pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
