Litsea noronhae

Litsea noronhae, commonly known as the Noronha's litsea, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Brazil.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and avocado trees. The plant is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves with a leathery texture. It produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by orange-red berries that are an important food source for local wildlife.

Litsea noronhae is often found in moist, shaded areas of the Atlantic Forest biome.
