Lycium grandicalyx

Lycium grandicalyx, commonly known as Chinese wolfberry or goji berry, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of small, orange-red berries. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The berries are rich in antioxidants and have been used in traditional Chinese medicine for their purported health benefits.
