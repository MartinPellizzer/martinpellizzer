Linum mucronatum subsp. pubifolium

Linum mucronatum subsp.

pubifolium, commonly known as the pubifoliate flax, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are hairy along the edges.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the cooler months. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in horticultural settings.
