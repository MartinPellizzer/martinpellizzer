Leucaena canescens

Leucaena canescens, commonly known as white leucaena, is a leguminous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It has small, ovate leaves with a grayish-white underside, and it produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, flat pods. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it valuable for soil enrichment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in agroforestry and as a forage crop.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
