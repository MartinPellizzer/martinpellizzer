Lindernia perennans

Lindernia perennans, commonly known as the common lindernia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly succulent texture. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of purple, pink, or white, which bloom from summer to fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Lindernia perennans is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.
