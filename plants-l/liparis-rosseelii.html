Liparis rosseelii

Liparis rosseelii, commonly known as the Rosseel's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a rhizomatous growth habit and produces delicate, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it has a short stem with a single flower spike.

Liparis rosseelii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
