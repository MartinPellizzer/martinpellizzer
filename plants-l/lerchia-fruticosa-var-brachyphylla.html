Lerchia fruticosa var. brachyphylla

Lerchia fruticosa var. brachyphylla, commonly known as the dwarf bottlebrush, is a compact shrub native to South Africa.

It features dense, cylindrical inflorescences that resemble bottle brushes, typically blooming in shades of red or orange. The plant has small, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping.
