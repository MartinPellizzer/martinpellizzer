Lepidium puberulum

Lepidium puberulum, commonly known as the hairy cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and hairy stems, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in fields, along roadsides, and in wetlands. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Lepidium puberulum is an annual or biennial herb that can spread through seeds dispersed by wind or water.
