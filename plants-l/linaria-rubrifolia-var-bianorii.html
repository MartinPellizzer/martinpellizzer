Linaria rubrifolia var. bianorii

Linaria rubrifolia var. bianorii, commonly known as the Crimsonleaf Toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of bright orange to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a reddish tinge along the edges, giving the plant its common name. This variety is often found in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in full sun conditions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and unique floral structure.
