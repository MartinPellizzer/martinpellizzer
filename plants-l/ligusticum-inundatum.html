Ligusticum inundatum

Ligusticum inundatum, commonly known as water celery, is a perennial herb native to wetland habitats in eastern North America.

It has slender, aromatic stems that often grow in water or very moist soil. The plant features alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often submerged or floating. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are clustered in umbels and bloom in early summer.

Ligusticum inundatum is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
