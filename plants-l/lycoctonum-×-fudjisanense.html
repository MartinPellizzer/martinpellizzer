Lycoctonum × fudjisanense

Lycoctonum × fudjisanense, commonly known as the Japanese bellflower, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, featuring hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its attractive flowers and compact growth habit.
