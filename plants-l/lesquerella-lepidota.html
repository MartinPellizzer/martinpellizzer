Lesquerella lepidota

Lesquerella lepidota, commonly known as the roughleaf lesquerella, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its hairy, ribbed stems and oval, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Lesquerella lepidota is known for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments, making it adapted to harsh, dry conditions.
