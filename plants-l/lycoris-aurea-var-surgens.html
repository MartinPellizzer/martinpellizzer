Lycoris aurea var. surgens

Lycoris aurea var. surgens, commonly known as the golden lily or the autumn lily, is a striking perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range from orange to red, often appearing in late summer to early autumn. The plant has strap-like, grassy foliage that emerges in early spring and withers by late summer. Its bulbs are clustered and produce a dense, upright growth habit.

Lycoris aurea var. surgens thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.
