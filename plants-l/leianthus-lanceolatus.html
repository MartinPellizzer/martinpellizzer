Leianthus lanceolatus

Leianthus lanceolatus, commonly known as the lanceleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.
