Lisianthius bifidus

Lisianthius bifidus, commonly known as the double-flowered lisianthus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its showy, double blooms that resemble peonies, with numerous petals arranged in a layered pattern. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and prefers well-drained soil and full sun. Its flowers come in shades of pink, red, and white, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Lisianthius bifidus is also valued for its long-lasting blooms and relatively low maintenance requirements.
