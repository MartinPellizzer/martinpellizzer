Lomoplis acantholoba

Lomoplis acantholoba, commonly known as the spiny leaf lomoplis, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine, stiff hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters during the cooler months. Its adaptations to dry environments include a deep root system and the ability to shed leaves during drought periods.

Lomoplis acantholoba is often found in rocky outcrops and sandy soils, where it plays a role in stabilizing the soil and providing habitat for small insects and animals.
