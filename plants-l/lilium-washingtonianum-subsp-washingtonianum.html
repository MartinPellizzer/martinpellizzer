Lilium washingtonianum subsp. washingtonianum

Lilium washingtonianum subsp.

washingtonianum, commonly known as the Washington lily, is a species native to the western United States. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters.

The plant features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale pink, with a strong, spicy fragrance. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
