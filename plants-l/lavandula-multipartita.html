Lavandula multipartita

Lavandula multipartita, commonly known as the Spanish lavender, is a species of flowering plant in the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is native to the Iberian Peninsula and is characterized by its compact, mounding growth habit. The plant produces spikes of fragrant purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are gray-green and aromatic, often used in aromatherapy and herbal remedies.

Lavandula multipartita is well-suited for rock gardens and dry climates due to its drought tolerance.
