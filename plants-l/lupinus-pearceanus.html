Lupinus pearceanus

Lupinus pearceanus, commonly known as the pearcea lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant has compound leaves composed of several small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Lupinus pearceanus is a member of the legume family and has the ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it ecologically significant.
