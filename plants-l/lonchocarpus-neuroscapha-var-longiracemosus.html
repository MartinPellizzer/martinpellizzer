Lonchocarpus neuroscapha var. longiracemosus

Lonchocarpus neuroscapha var. longiracemosus, commonly known as the long-stemmed urara, is a tropical legume native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with elongated, serrated leaflets and produces distinctive yellow to orange pods. The plant is notable for its dense, branching habit and its ability to thrive in humid, tropical environments. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is known for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local communities.
