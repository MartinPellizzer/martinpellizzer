Limniris purdyiformis

Limniris purdyiformis, commonly known as the Purdy's water lily, is a unique aquatic plant native to specific freshwater habitats in Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves that form a dense canopy on the water surface, providing shelter for various aquatic organisms. The plant's flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive, urn-shaped structure that attracts pollinators such as beetles and moths. Its roots anchor in the muddy substrate, while its rhizomes spread horizontally to form colonies.

Due to its specialized habitat requirements, Limniris purdyiformis is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
