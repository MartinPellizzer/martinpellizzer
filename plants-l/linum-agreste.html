Linum agreste

Linum agreste, commonly known as wild flax, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and small, blue or purple flowers. The plant produces small, oval seeds that are rich in oil and have been used historically for various purposes. Linum agreste thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along woodland edges.

Its fibrous stalks and seeds make it valuable for both ecological and practical applications.
