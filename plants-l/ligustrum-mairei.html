Ligustrum mairei

Ligustrum mairei, commonly known as the Maire privet, is a deciduous shrub native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its small, opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by black, spherical berries. Ligustrum mairei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It is also valued for its use in horticulture and as a hedge plant.
