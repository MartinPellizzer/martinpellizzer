Lythrum alatum

Lythrum alatum, commonly known as the winged lythrum, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lythraceae family and is characterized by its striking, spikes of deep red or purple flowers. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly textured appearance. Its stems are distinctive, with prominent, wing-like ridges that run along their length.

Lythrum alatum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges.
