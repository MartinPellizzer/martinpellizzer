Lathyrus magellanicus var. tucumanensis

Lathyrus magellanicus var. tucumanensis, commonly known as the Tucuman pea, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to violet, and it bears pods containing several seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
