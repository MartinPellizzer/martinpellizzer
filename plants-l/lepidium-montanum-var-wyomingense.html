Lepidium montanum var. wyomingense

Lepidium montanum var. wyomingense, commonly known as the Wyoming cress, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately divided, and covered in fine, glandular hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal practices.
