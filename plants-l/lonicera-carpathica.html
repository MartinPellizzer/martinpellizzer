Lonicera carpathica

Lonicera carpathica, commonly known as Carpathian honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy on the underside. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of yellow to orange, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its berries, which are red and fleshy, mature in the fall and are an important food source for birds.

Lonicera carpathica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
