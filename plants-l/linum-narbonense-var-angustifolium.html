Linum narbonense var. angustifolium

Linum narbonense var. angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaf flax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. It has been historically used for its fibers and has ornamental value in gardens.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both ecological and horticultural study.
