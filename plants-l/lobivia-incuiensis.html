Lobivia incuiensis

Lobivia incuiensis, commonly known as the Incuiensis Lobivia, is a species of succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rounded growth habit with ribbed stems. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. Its leaves are usually small and may be present during the juvenile stage, though they often shed as the plant matures.

Lobivia incuiensis is well adapted to dry conditions, storing water in its fleshy stems and requiring minimal care in cultivation.
