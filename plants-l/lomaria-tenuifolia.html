Lomaria tenuifolia

Lomaria tenuifolia, commonly known as slender lomaria, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy or rocky soils. It produces small, edible pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
