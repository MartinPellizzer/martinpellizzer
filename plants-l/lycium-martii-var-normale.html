Lycium martii var. normale

Lycium martii var. normale, commonly known as Chinese wolfberry or goji berry, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of China.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family and is characterized by its small, red, fleshy berries that are rich in antioxidants. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in dry climates with limited rainfall. Its berries are widely cultivated for their nutritional value and traditional medicinal uses.

The plant's adaptability to harsh environments makes it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
