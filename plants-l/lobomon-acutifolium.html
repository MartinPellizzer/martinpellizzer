Lobomon acutifolium

Lobomon acutifolium, commonly known as the sharp-leaved lobomon, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, serrated leaves that are typically bright green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
