Lactuca scariola subsp. sativa

Lactuca scariola subsp.

sativa, commonly known as garden lettuce, is a member of the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are typically crisp and edible.

The plant has a short, often hairy stem and produces small yellow flowers in clusters. Its leaves are used in various culinary applications, including salads and wraps.

Botanically, it is distinguished by its basal leaves and the presence of milky sap when the stems are broken.
