Leiothrix barreirensis

Leiothrix barreirensis, commonly known as the Barreirensis fern, is a species of fern native to South America, particularly found in Brazil.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and rocky crevices. Its rhizome is creeping and produces both sterile and fertile fronds.

The spores are released from the sori located on the underside of the leaves, contributing to its reproductive cycle.
