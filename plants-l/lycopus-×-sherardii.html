Lycopus × sherardii

Lycopus × sherardii, commonly known as marsh woundwort, is a hybrid perennial herb native to wetland habitats in Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and clusters of small, blue-to-purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in shallow water or damp soil, often found in marshes, pond edges, and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are square and often reddish in color.

Lycopus × sherardii is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used historically in traditional herbal remedies.
