Lathyrus macrostachys

Lathyrus macrostachys, commonly known as the giant sweet pea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of fragrant, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical pods containing several seeds, which are typically small and hard. Lathyrus macrostachys is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners in temperate climates.
