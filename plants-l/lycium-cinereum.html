Lycium cinereum

Lycium cinereum, commonly known as silver bush or silverberry, is a shrub native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and small, dark red berries that are edible. The plant has small, simple leaves and produces yellowish flowers in the spring. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

Lycium cinereum is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to withstand harsh climates.
