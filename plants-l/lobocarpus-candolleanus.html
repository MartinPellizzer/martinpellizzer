Lobocarpus candolleanus

Lobocarpus candolleanus, commonly known as the Candollean's lily, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It features slender, erect stems that bear lance-shaped leaves with a waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in waterlogged soils.

It is often found in marshy areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in aquatic gardens.
