Lilium chalcedonicum

Lilium chalcedonicum, commonly known as the Chalcedony lily, is a species of flowering plant in the Liliaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe, including parts of the Carpathian Mountains. This perennial herb produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white with a pinkish or reddish hue. The plant features a bulbous root system and lance-shaped, grass-like leaves.

It thrives in well-drained, loamy soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.
