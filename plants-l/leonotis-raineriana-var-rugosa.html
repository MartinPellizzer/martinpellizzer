Leonotis raineriana var. rugosa

Leonotis raineriana var. rugosa, commonly known as the rough-leaved lion's tail, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from orange to red, which attract pollinators like birds and insects. The leaves are typically rough and hairy, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in warm climates.

This plant is often used in ornamental horticulture for its vibrant blooms and unique foliage.
