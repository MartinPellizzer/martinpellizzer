Linum brevifolium f. squamifolium

Linum brevifolium f. squamifolium, commonly known as the scaly-leaved flax, is a variant of the flax plant characterized by its distinctive, narrow, and scaly foliage.

It is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States, particularly found in California. The plant produces showy blue or purple flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its leaves are often covered in fine, scale-like structures, which give it its common name.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often grown in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
