Leontodon hispidulus var. austromaroccanus

Leontodon hispidulus var. austromaroccanus, commonly known as the Moroccan hairy dandelion, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, elongated leaves and yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its inflorescence consists of a single flower head surrounded by bracts, and it produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This variety is notable for its resilience and is often found in Mediterranean-type climates.
