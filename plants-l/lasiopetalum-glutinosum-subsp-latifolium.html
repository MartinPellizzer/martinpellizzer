Lasiopetalum glutinosum subsp. latifolium

Lasiopetalum glutinosum subsp.

latifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved mistflower, is a shrub native to eastern Australia. It features aromatic, glandular leaves that are typically broad and ovate in shape.

The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and dry forest habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native insect life.
