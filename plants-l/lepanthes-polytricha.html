Lepanthes polytricha

Lepanthes polytricha, commonly known as the many-veined orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers that typically feature a wide range of colors, including white, yellow, and pink. The plant has a complex root system and is often found growing in shaded, humid environments such as cloud forests. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem.

Lepanthes polytricha is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental beauty in horticulture.
