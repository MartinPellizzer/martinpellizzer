Lycium exsertum

Lycium exsertum, commonly known as the red sandalwood or Chinese wolfberry, is a deciduous shrub native to China, Korea, and Japan.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its woody stems and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible and often used in traditional Chinese medicine. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and have a slightly hairy surface, while its bark is thick and fissured.

Lycium exsertum is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
