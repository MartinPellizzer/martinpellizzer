Lotus maroccanus var. simulans

Lotus maroccanus var. simulans, commonly known as the Moroccan lotus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of North Africa.

It features striking, large, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has heart-shaped, palmate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, providing some protection against desert conditions. Its root system is robust, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils and withstand periods of drought.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and traditional use in local horticulture.
