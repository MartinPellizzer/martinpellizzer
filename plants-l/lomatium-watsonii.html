Lomatium watsonii

Lomatium watsonii, commonly known as Watson's lomatium, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its finely divided leaves and umbel-like inflorescences.

It is native to the Pacific Northwest of North America, particularly found in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with a basal rosette of leaves that are deeply lobed and hairy. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, often appearing in late spring to early summer.

The plant is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
