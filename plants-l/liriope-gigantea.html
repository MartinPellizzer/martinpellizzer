Liriope gigantea

Liriope gigantea, commonly known as the giant lilyturf, is a perennial grass-like plant native to eastern Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that form dense clumps and produces spikes of small, bell-shaped flowers in late summer. The plant is characterized by its ability to thrive in moist, shady environments, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form thick mats, which can be beneficial for erosion control.

Liriope gigantea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
