Litsea glutinosa

Litsea glutinosa, commonly known as sticky laurel, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries such as Malaysia, Indonesia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns from green to yellow or red as it matures.

Litsea glutinosa is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal uses.
