Linaria genistifolia var. sofiana

Linaria genistifolia var. sofiana, commonly known as the sofian larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, delicate flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, resembling those of the genista plant, hence the species name. This variety is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable plant for rock gardens and borders.
