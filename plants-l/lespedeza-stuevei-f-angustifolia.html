Lespedeza stuevei f. angustifolia

Lespedeza stuevei f. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf lespedeza, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a hairy texture. This plant is typically found in open woodlands and along roadsides, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility through its symbiotic relationship with rhizobia bacteria.
