Leobordea pusilla

Leobordea pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf leobordea, is a small, flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. The plant produces delicate, blue-violet flowers that bloom in the spring. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
