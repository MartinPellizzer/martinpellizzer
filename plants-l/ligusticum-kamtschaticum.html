Ligusticum kamtschaticum

Ligusticum kamtschaticum, commonly known as the Siberian angelica, is a tall biennial or perennial herb native to the cold regions of Siberia and northern Asia.

It features large, pinnately compound leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in an umbel, which bloom in late summer. Its roots are thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Ligusticum kamtschaticum is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
