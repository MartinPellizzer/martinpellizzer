Lobelia hypocrateriformis

Lobelia hypocrateriformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It is a perennial herb that produces showy blooms in the spring and early summer.

Lobelia hypocrateriformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
