Lachnagrostis billardierei subsp. tenuiseta

Lachnagrostis billardierei subsp.

tenuiseta, commonly known as slender-haired grass, is a low-growing perennial grass species native to southern Australia. It features narrow, grass-like leaves with fine, hairy texture, and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, coastal environments. It plays a role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for native wildlife.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological restoration projects in arid regions.
