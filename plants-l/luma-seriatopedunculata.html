Luma seriatopedunculata

Luma seriatopedunculata, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive tendencies, it is considered a weed in some regions.
