Laurus pendula

Laurus pendula, commonly known as the hanging laurel or winter box, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features fragrant, dark green leaves that are often used in culinary and aromatic applications. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible but not typically consumed by humans. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for hedges and ornamental gardens.

Its distinctive drooping branches give it a unique, cascading appearance.
