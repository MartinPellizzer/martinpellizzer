Liparis chloroxantha

Liparis chloroxantha, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are often adorned with green or purple markings. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern.

Botanically, it belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its delicate, ephemeral blooms that appear during the rainy season.
