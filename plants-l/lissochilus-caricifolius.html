Lissochilus caricifolius

Lissochilus caricifolius, commonly known as the arrowleaf orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and cloud forests, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other small insects, contributing to its ecological role in the region.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
