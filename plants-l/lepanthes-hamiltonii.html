Lepanthes hamiltonii

Lepanthes hamiltonii, commonly known as the Hamilton's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant produces delicate, showy flowers with intricate patterns and a distinctive coloration, often featuring white or pale pink petals. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the forest floor.

Lepanthes hamiltonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
