Lasianthus capitatus

Lasianthus capitatus, commonly known as the helmet flower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive helmet-shaped inflorescence. The plant typically grows as an erect herb with opposite, lanceolate leaves. Its flowers are usually bright yellow or orange, with a unique tubular structure that attracts pollinators.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
