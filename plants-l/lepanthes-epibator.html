Lepanthes epibator

Lepanthes epibator, commonly known as the Bornean orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia, particularly Borneo.

It is a terrestrial herb with a short, stocky rhizome and a single, fleshy, ovate leaf. The plant produces a single, showy inflorescence with several white to pale yellow flowers that have a distinctive, intricate structure. Lepanthes epibator thrives in humid, shaded environments within lowland and montane forests.

Its botanical features make it a notable subject in the study of orchid diversity and adaptation.
