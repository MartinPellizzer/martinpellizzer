Larix caducifolia

Larix caducifolia, commonly known as the Japanese larch, is a deciduous conifer native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its needle-like leaves that turn golden in autumn before falling, giving it a distinctive seasonal appearance. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, typical of coniferous species. Its bark is smooth and reddish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

Larix caducifolia is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
