Lavandula officinalis var. faucheana

Lavandula officinalis var. faucheana, commonly known as French lavender, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, spike-like inflorescences of purple to pink flowers, which bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in a fine layer of down, contributing to its drought tolerance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its essential oils, which are used in aromatherapy, perfumery, and traditional medicine.
