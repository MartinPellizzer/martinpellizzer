Lythrum punicifolium

Lythrum punicifolium, commonly known as redroot lythrum, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of vibrant purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often reddish at the base, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and marshy areas.

Lythrum punicifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to support pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
