Lepidium georginum

Lepidium georginum, commonly known as the California wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress plants. The plant features small, bright yellow or white flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Lepidium georginum is typically found in dry, disturbed soils and is often used in gardens for its ornamental value.
