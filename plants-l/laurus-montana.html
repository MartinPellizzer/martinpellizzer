Laurus montana

Laurus montana, commonly known as sweet bay laurel, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark green, leathery leaves that are often used in cooking and traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by black, berry-like fruits. Its leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its distinctive fragrance and medicinal properties.

Laurus montana is well adapted to coastal and rocky environments, thriving in full sun and well-drained soil.
