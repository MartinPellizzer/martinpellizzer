Luzula nemorosa f. grandiflora

Luzula nemorosa f. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered woodrush, is a botanical variant of the woodrush genus Luzula.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. The plant features dense, grass-like foliage with narrow, dark green leaves that form a compact rosette. Its flowering stems bear clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers, which are arranged in panicle-like inflorescences.

This subspecies is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
