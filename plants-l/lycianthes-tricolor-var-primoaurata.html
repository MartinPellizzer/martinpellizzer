Lycianthes tricolor var. primoaurata

Lycianthes tricolor var. primoaurata, commonly known as the three-color morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its strikingly colorful flowers that display three distinct colors—usually red, yellow, and white—on the same bloom. The plant belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshade plants. Its leaves are ovate and slightly hairy, while the stems are slender and often vine-like.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
