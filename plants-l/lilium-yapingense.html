Lilium yapingense

Lilium yapingense, commonly known as the Yaping lily, is a striking bulbous plant native to China.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its tall, upright stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The flowers are typically pink to red in color, with a distinctive white throat, and bloom in late summer. The plant produces a cluster of small, round berries that contain the seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
