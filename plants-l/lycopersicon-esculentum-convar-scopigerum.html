Lycopersicon esculentum convar. scopigerum

Lycopersicon esculentum convar. scopigerum, commonly known as the wild tomato, is a botanical variant of the domesticated tomato.

It is characterized by its smaller, often hairy leaves and fruits that are typically less fleshy and more acidic than those of cultivated tomatoes. This species is native to South America and is adapted to a wide range of environmental conditions. It exhibits a variety of growth habits, including prostrate or ascending stems, and produces small, yellow or orange fruits.

The plant is of interest to botanists and horticulturists for its genetic diversity and potential for breeding programs.
