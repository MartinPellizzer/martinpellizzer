Lupinus amabayensis

Lupinus amabayensis, commonly known as the Amabayensis lupine, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces spikes of purple or blue flowers. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
