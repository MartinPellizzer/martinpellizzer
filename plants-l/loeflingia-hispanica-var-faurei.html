Loeflingia hispanica var. faurei

Loeflingia hispanica var. faurei, commonly known as the Spanish bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue to violet in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting flowers and compact growth habit.
