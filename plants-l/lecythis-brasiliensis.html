Lecythis brasiliensis

Lecythis brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazil nut tree, is a tropical tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and produces a distinctive, woody fruit that contains multiple seeds, which are the edible Brazil nuts. The tree can grow up to 50 meters tall and has a long lifespan, often living for several centuries. Its flowers are small and yellowish, and it relies on specific pollinators for reproduction.

The plant plays a vital role in the ecosystem, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
