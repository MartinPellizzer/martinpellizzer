Lithospermum onosmodium

Lithospermum onosmodium, commonly known as the Indian madder, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in dry environments. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its roots contain compounds used in traditional medicine and as a natural dye.

The species is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.
