Lozania pittieri

Lozania pittieri, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. Lozania pittieri is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking nocturnal blooms.
