Lindera sanjappae

Lindera sanjappae, commonly known as the Sanjappae ligustrum, is a rare shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and avocados, and is characterized by its aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a distinct, spicy scent when crushed.

The small, yellowish flowers are followed by berries that are an important food source for local wildlife.
