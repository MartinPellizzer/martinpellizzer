Lonicera fauriei

Lonicera fauriei, commonly known as the Japanese honeysuckle, is a climbing vine native to East Asia.

It features opposite, heart-shaped leaves and produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. It blooms in the spring and early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

However, it can become invasive in some regions if not properly managed.
