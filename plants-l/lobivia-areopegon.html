Lobivia areopegon

Lobivia areopegon, commonly known as the Areopagita or the Ghost Cactus, is a species of cactus native to the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It is a low-growing, spineless plant with a compact, rounded body that often appears as a cluster of small, rounded tubercles. Its stems are typically green to bluish-green, and it produces small, yellow flowers during the cooler months. The plant is adapted to extreme arid conditions, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

It is prized in horticulture for its unique appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained, sandy soils.
