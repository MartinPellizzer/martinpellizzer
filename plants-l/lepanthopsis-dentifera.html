Lepanthopsis dentifera

Lepanthopsis dentifera, commonly known as the toothed orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom during the wet season. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Lepanthopsis dentifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
