Lepidium coronopifolium f. pinnatifidum

Lepidium coronopifolium f. pinnatifidum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many species with small, cruciform flowers. The plant is characterized by its finely divided, pinnatifid leaves that give it a feathery appearance. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and produces yellow or white flowers in late spring.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and relatively low-maintenance ornamental plant.
