Lucuma paradoxa

Lucuma paradoxa, commonly known as the golden apple of the Andes, is a small, deciduous tree native to the highlands of Peru.

It produces distinctive, elongated fruits with a yellow, sweet, and creamy pulp that resembles custard. The plant thrives in cool, temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

The fruit is typically harvested when fully mature, and it is valued for its unique flavor and nutritional content.
