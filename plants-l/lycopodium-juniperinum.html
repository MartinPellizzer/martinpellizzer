Lycopodium juniperinum

Lycopodium juniperinum, commonly known as the juniper clubmoss, is a small evergreen plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems that are covered with small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces spore-bearing cones, which are typically clustered at the tips of the branches. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Lycopodium juniperinum is a primitive vascular plant with a long evolutionary history dating back to the Paleozoic era.
