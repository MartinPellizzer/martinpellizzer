Linaria sagittata var. homoeophylla

Linaria sagittata var. homoeophylla, commonly known as the arrowhead fleury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers. The leaves are typically arrow-shaped, which gives the plant its common name, and they are often covered in fine hairs. This variety is known for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

It blooms from late spring to early summer, adding a splash of color to the landscape.
