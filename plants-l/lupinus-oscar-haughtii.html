Lupinus oscar-haughtii

Lupinus oscar-haughtii, commonly known as the Oscar Haught lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Lupinus oscar-haughtii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in nitrogen fixation in soil.
