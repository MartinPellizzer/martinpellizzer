Lessingianthus lapinhensis

Lessingianthus lapinhensis, commonly known as the Brazilian sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow-orange flowers with prominent central disk florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
