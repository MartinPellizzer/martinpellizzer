Lepismium ramosissimum

Lepismium ramosissimum, commonly known as the coral vine, is a climbing plant native to South America.

It features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with a dark throat. The plant has a woody stem with numerous branches, and its leaves are opposite, simple, and often hairy. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by hummingbirds and other nectar-feeding insects.
