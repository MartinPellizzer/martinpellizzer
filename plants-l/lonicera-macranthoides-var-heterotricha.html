Lonicera macranthoides var. heterotricha

Lonicera macranthoides var. heterotricha, commonly known as the Chinese honeysuckle, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated margins and produces fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth habit and ability to climb using tendrils. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The fruit is a red, fleshy berry that ripens in autumn, containing seeds that are dispersed by birds.
