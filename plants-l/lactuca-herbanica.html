Lactuca herbanica

Lactuca herbanica, commonly known as the common chicory, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of blue to purple leaves and a tall, branching stem that bears yellow flowers. The plant is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and it thrives in a variety of soil types. It contains lactucin, a bitter compound, and has been used historically in herbal medicine.

Lactuca herbanica is also cultivated for its edible leaves and as a forage crop.
