Larix dahurica var. heilingensis

Larix dahurica var. heilingensis, commonly known as the Heilongjiang larch, is a variant of the Dahurian larch native to northeastern China.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure. The plant exhibits a distinctive growth habit, often forming dense, upright clumps with a conical shape. Its needles are typically dark green and remain on the tree for several years before shedding.

This variety is adapted to cold climates and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soils.
