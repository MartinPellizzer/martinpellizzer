Lathyrus pannonicus var. multijugus

Lathyrus pannonicus var. multijugus, commonly known as the Balkan vetch, is a perennial leguminous plant native to southeastern Europe.

It typically grows in open woodlands, meadows, and along riverbanks, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features slender, trailing stems with trifoliate leaves composed of three oval, serrated leaflets. Its flowers are pea-shaped, ranging in color from white to pink, and are borne in clusters.

This species is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitats.
