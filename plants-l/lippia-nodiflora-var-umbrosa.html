Lippia nodiflora var. umbrosa

Lippia nodiflora var. umbrosa, commonly known as the dark lippia, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive minty scent, and it produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories or along streambanks. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in herbal remedies.

Its foliage and flowers are often harvested for culinary and medicinal purposes.
