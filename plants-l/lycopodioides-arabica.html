Lycopodioides arabica

Lycopodioides arabica, commonly known as the Arabian clubmoss, is a rare and ancient plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and a creeping rhizome system that allows it to thrive in rocky and arid environments. This plant is typically found in the mountainous regions of the Arabian Peninsula, where it adapts to high altitudes and limited moisture. Its sporophyte stage produces spores in specialized structures called sporangia, which are clustered in cone-like formations.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological specificity, Lycopodioides arabica is considered a unique example of relic flora in the region.
