Leontodon hispanicus

Leontodon hispanicus, commonly known as the Spanish dandelion, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate leaves and produces a single, tall flowering stem topped with a composite flower head. The plant typically blooms in spring and early summer, producing yellow flowers that develop into fluffy white seeds. It is native to southern Europe and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Leontodon hispanicus is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
