Lepanthes garayi

Lepanthes garayi, commonly known as the Garay's slipper orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the cloud forests of Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, intricate flowers that often resemble miniature slipper shapes. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Lepanthes garayi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
