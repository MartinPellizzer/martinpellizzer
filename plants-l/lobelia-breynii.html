Lobelia breynii

Lobelia breynii, commonly known as the African bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Lobelia breynii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
