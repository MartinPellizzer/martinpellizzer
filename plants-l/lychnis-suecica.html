Lychnis suecica

Lychnis suecica, commonly known as the Swedish catchfly, is a flowering plant native to northern Europe.

It is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

Lychnis suecica is also known for its ability to tolerate partial shade and its historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
