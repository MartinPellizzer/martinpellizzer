Lepanthes beharii

Lepanthes beharii, commonly known as the Behari's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in the summer months. The plant produces showy, white to pale pink petals with a distinctive yellow lip. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in the understory of tropical forests.

Lepanthes beharii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is a subject of interest in botanical research.
