Linaria elegans

Linaria elegans, commonly known as the elegant toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of bright yellow to orange flowers that resemble small toads, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Linaria elegans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
