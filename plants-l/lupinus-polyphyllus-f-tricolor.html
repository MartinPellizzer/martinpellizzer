Lupinus polyphyllus f. tricolor

Lupinus polyphyllus f. tricolor, commonly known as the tricolor lupine, is a variant of the coastal lupine native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

This plant features striking tri-colored flowers, typically with purple, white, and yellow petals, which make it visually distinctive. It is a perennial herb that grows in coastal dunes and sandy soils, adapted to withstand salt spray and strong winds. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces erect, sparsely branched flowering stems.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological restoration and ornamental gardening.
