Lagarosiphon cordofanus

Lagarosiphon cordofanus, commonly known as the African water plant, is a flowering aquatic plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, ribbon-like leaves that float on the water surface, often forming dense mats. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically borne on long, slender stems. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments and is often found in ponds and lakes.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become invasive in non-native regions, impacting local ecosystems.
