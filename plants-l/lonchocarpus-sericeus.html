Lonchocarpus sericeus

Lonchocarpus sericeus, commonly known as silky senna, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is notable for its silky, hairy foliage, which gives it its common name. It produces yellow flowers and is often used in traditional medicine.

However, it contains toxic alkaloids and should be handled with care.
