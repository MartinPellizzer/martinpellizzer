Lilium michauxianum

Lilium michauxianum, commonly known as the Michaux lily, is a species of flowering plant in the Liliaceae family.

It is native to the eastern United States, particularly found in moist, woodsy areas. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with a strong, sweet fragrance. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, and it grows to a height of about 1 to 3 feet.

The bulbs of this plant are used in traditional medicine and are also cultivated for ornamental purposes.
