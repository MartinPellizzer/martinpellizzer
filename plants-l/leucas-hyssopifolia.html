Leucas hyssopifolia

Leucas hyssopifolia, commonly known as Indian borage, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Leucas hyssopifolia is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
