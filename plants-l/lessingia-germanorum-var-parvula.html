Lessingia germanorum var. parvula

Lessingia germanorum var. parvula, commonly known as the small-flowered lessingia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and linear, and it produces small, white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and early summer.

This variety is noted for its ecological importance as a food source for native pollinators and its role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
