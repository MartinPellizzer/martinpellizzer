Ligustrum tschonoskii var. kiyozumianum

Ligustrum tschonoskii var. kiyozumianum, commonly known as the Japanese privet, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features small, dark green leaves that are oval in shape and have a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it adaptable to various garden environments.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often used in landscaping and hedge planting.
