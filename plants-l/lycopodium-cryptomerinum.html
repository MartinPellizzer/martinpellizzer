Lycopodium cryptomerinum

Lycopodium cryptomerinum, commonly known as the giant clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, making it one of the tallest members of the lycophyte group. The plant produces small, scale-like leaves and has a unique reproductive system involving spore capsules that develop on the upper parts of its stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and is often found in temperate regions.

Lycopodium cryptomerinum plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
