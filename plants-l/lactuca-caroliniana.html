Lactuca caroliniana

Lactuca caroliniana, commonly known as Carolina lettuce, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family.

It is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are generally edible and have a mild, slightly bitter taste.

It is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
