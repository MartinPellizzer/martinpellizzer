Lupinus peruvianus var. peruvianus

Lupinus peruvianus var. peruvianus, commonly known as the Peruvian lupine, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval to lance-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or mountainous habitats.

Lupinus peruvianus var. peruvianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
