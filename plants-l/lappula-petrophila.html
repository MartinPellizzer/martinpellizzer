Lappula petrophila

Lappula petrophila, commonly known as the rock lappula, is a flowering plant native to arid and rocky environments in the western United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and distinctive seed pods. The plant typically grows in dry, well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, helping to reduce water loss, while its flowers bloom in early spring.

Lappula petrophila is often found in areas with limited vegetation, showcasing its resilience and ecological importance in such habitats.
