Lepanthes decipiens

Lepanthes decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the genus Lepanthes, which includes over 200 species of small, terrestrial orchids. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in the understory of tropical forests. Its flowers are usually small and delicate, with a variety of colors, and it is known for its ability to mimic other plants to attract pollinators.

Lepanthes decipiens is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and unique floral characteristics.
