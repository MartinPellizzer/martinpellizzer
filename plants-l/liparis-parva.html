Liparis parva

Liparis parva, commonly known as the small orchid, is a small, terrestrial orchid native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and along stream banks. The plant features delicate, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers with a distinctive lip. It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its simple, non-epiphytic growth habit.

Liparis parva is often found in association with other orchid species in its native habitat.
