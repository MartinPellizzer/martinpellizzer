Lupinus bicolor var. microphyllus

Lupinus bicolor var. microphyllus, commonly known as the dwarf lupine, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces racemes of blue to purple flowers, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
