Lippia grisea var. latifolia

Lippia grisea var. latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf gray lippia, is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It features aromatic, narrow to broad leaves that are typically grayish-green in color, with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This species is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils due to its aromatic properties.
