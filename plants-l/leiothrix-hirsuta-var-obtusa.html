Leiothrix hirsuta var. obtusa

Leiothrix hirsuta var. obtusa, commonly known as the woolly leiothrix, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape and covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
