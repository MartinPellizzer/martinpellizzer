Lupinus abortivus

Lupinus abortivus, commonly known as the narrowleaf lupine, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has palmately divided leaves with a distinctive hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Lupinus abortivus is also valued for its role in nitrogen fixation, improving soil fertility.
