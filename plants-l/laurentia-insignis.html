Laurentia insignis

Laurentia insignis, commonly known as the silverleaf laurentia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Laurentia insignis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its drought tolerance and attractive appearance.
