Lonchocarpus congestiflorus

Lonchocarpus congestiflorus, commonly known as the clustered-flowered legume, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers, typically in shades of pink or purple. Its root system is known to contain toxic compounds, which have been studied for potential medicinal uses.

The species is often found in moist, shaded areas of tropical forests.
