Loranthus siamensis

Loranthus siamensis, commonly known as the Siam mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host tree.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while it provides some organic compounds in return. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers and red berries that contain seeds. It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries like Thailand and Malaysia.

Its parasitic nature and unique reproductive features make it an interesting subject in botanical studies.
