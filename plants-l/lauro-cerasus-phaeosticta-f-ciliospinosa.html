Lauro-cerasus phaeosticta f. ciliospinosa

Lauro-cerasus phaeosticta f. ciliospinosa, commonly known as the variegated cherry laurel, is a hybrid plant that combines characteristics of Prunus laurocerasus and Prunus cerasifera.

It is known for its striking foliage, featuring white and green variegation on its leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in spring, followed by dark purple berries. It is a slow-growing shrub or small tree that thrives in USDA hardiness zones 6-8.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and ability to tolerate urban conditions.
