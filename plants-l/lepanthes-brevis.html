Lepanthes brevis

Lepanthes brevis, commonly known as the short-stemmed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically range in color from white to pale pink. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, adapting to its often shaded forest habitat.

Lepanthes brevis is appreciated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
