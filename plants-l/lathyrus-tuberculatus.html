Lathyrus tuberculatus

Lathyrus tuberculatus, commonly known as the tuberculate vetch, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial legume that typically grows in grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, pea-like flowers that are usually purple or violet in color. Its seed pods are cylindrical and contain several small, kidney-shaped seeds.

Lathyrus tuberculatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
