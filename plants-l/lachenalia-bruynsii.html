Lachenalia bruynsii

Lachenalia bruynsii, commonly known as the Bruyns' lachenalia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are often fleshy and covered in a fine layer of white powder. The plant produces tall, branched stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of yellow or orange. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated by succulent enthusiasts.
