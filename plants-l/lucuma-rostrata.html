Lucuma rostrata

Lucuma rostrata, commonly known as the sweet potato, is a tuberous plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its elongated, sweet-tasting roots. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves and climbing vines that can reach several meters in length. Its tubers are rich in carbohydrates, fiber, and various nutrients, making it a valuable food source.
