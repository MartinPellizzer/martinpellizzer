Linaria genistifolia subsp. praealta

Linaria genistifolia subsp.

praealta, commonly known as the tall yellow toadflax, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height.

The plant produces bright yellow, tubular flowers that are typically arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. Its leaves are narrow and lanceolate, often with a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in rocky or dry habitats.
