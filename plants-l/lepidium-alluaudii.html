Lepidium alluaudii

Lepidium alluaudii, commonly known as the African wall cress, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, annual herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features small white flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are pinnately divided with a distinctive appearance. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard family plants.

Lepidium alluaudii is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, where it thrives in well-drained environments.
