Lepidium ramburei

Lepidium ramburei, commonly known as the Mexican wallflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress plants, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and pungent odor. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields. Its leaves are narrow and sometimes hairy, while its fruit is a small, dry capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Lepidium ramburei is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
