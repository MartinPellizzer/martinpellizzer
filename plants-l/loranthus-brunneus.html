Loranthus brunneus

Loranthus brunneus, commonly known as the brown mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily eucalyptus and acacia.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using the light it captures. The plant's small, white or pale yellow flowers are typically clustered in dense inflorescences, and its berries are orange-red with a sticky coating to aid in seed dispersal by birds. Loranthus brunneus is native to Australia and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Its parasitic nature and unique ecological role make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
