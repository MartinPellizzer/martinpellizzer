Lophiolepis ekimiana

Lophiolepis ekimiana, commonly known as the Echidna orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its unique, spirally arranged leaves and its striking, violet-colored flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal regions. Its conservation status is vulnerable due to habitat loss and limited distribution.

This orchid is notable for its ornamental value and ecological significance in its native environment.
