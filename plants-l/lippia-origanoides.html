Lippia origanoides

Lippia origanoides, commonly known as lemon verbena, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lemon-scented leaves that contain high levels of volatile oils. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale yellow to white in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for both ornamental and culinary use.

Lippia origanoides is often cultivated for its fragrance and as a source of natural flavoring.
