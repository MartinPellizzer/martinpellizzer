Lonchocarpus modestus

Lonchocarpus modestus, commonly known as the modest legume, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation.

Due to its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
