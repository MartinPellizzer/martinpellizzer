Lyonia frondosa

Lyonia frondosa, commonly known as the red-tipped plantain, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its upright, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish hue at the base. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Lyonia frondosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant landscaping.
