Lockhartia viruensis

Lockhartia viruensis, commonly known as the Viru orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It is a sympodial orchid with a short, creeping rhizome and produces compact, pseudobulb-like stems. The plant features dark green, strap-shaped leaves and bears small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Lockhartia viruensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
