Liparis deflexa

Liparis deflexa, commonly known as the flexed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in rich, organic soils. The plant has a slender, erect stem that bears several lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and lack the typical orchid fragrance.

Liparis deflexa is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure and growth habit.
