Lycium scoparium var. confertiflorum

Lycium scoparium var. confertiflorum, commonly known as the denseflower boxthorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and small, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright orange-red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and ornamental plant suitable for coastal landscaping.
