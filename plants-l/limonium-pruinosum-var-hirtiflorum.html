Limonium pruinosum var. hirtiflorum

Limonium pruinosum var. hirtiflorum, commonly known as the woolly sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, herbaceous growth habit and its clusters of small, showy blue to purple flowers. The plant produces narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence, giving it a woolly appearance. It thrives in coastal areas and well-drained soils, often growing in sandy or gravelly habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand salty and windy conditions.
