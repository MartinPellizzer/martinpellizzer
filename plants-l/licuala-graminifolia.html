Licuala graminifolia

Licuala graminifolia, commonly known as the grass-leaf pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by bright orange or red berries. It thrives in wet, tropical environments and is often found near water bodies.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
