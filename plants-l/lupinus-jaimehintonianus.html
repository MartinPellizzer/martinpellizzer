Lupinus jaimehintonianus

Lupinus jaimehintonianus, commonly known as Jaime Hinton's lupine, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and showy, spiny pods. The plant produces clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Lupinus jaimehintonianus is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils and open habitats.
