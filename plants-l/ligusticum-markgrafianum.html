Ligusticum markgrafianum

Ligusticum markgrafianum, commonly known as the Markgraf's lovage, is a perennial herb native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbel-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with finely divided, fern-like leaves. Its flowers are small and white to pale yellow, appearing in late summer.
