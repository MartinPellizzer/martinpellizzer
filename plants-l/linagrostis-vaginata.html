Linagrostis vaginata

Linagrostis vaginata, commonly known as the silver hairgrass, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of Asia and North America.

It is characterized by its slender, hair-like leaves that give it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in meadows and wetlands. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Linagrostis vaginata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
