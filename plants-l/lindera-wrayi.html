Lindera wrayi

Lindera wrayi, commonly known as the Florida spicebush, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, elliptic leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along streambanks.

Lindera wrayi is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
