Leptolaena bernieri

Leptolaena bernieri, commonly known as the red-stemmed acacia, is a shrub native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is notable for its distinctive red stems and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in coastal areas.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
