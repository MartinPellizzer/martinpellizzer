Lonicera villosa var. calvescens

Lonicera villosa var. calvescens, commonly known as the hairy honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine, white pubescence. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its red to orange berries are edible and attract various wildlife.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.
