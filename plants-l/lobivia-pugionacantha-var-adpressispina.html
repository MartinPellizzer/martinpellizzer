Lobivia pugionacantha var. adpressispina

Lobivia pugionacantha var. adpressispina, commonly known as the narrow-spined lobivia, is a succulent native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It features a compact, rounded stem with short, cylindrical leaves that are typically blue-gray in color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring season, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. Its succulent nature allows it to store water in its leaves and stems, making it drought-tolerant.

This variety is prized for its unique spination and ability to thrive in well-draining soil under full sun.
