Lomaria × aggregata

Lomaria × aggregata, commonly known as the clustered lomaria, is a hybrid evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern California.

It features dense, rounded growth habit with small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often used in landscaping for its compact form and seasonal floral display.
