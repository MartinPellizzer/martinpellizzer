Lupinus aquilinus

Lupinus aquilinus, commonly known as the Sierra lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and is often found in rocky or open habitats. Lupinus aquilinus is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.
