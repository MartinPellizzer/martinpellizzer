Lychnis crassifolia

Lychnis crassifolia, commonly known as broad-leaved catchfly, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features rosettes of thick, fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or red flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
