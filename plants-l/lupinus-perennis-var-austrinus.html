Lupinus perennis var. austrinus

Lupinus perennis var. austrinus, commonly known as the Australian lupine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It features erect, branched stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, adorned with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, pea-like flowers in shades of purple, pink, or white, typically blooming from spring to early summer. Its root system includes nitrogen-fixing nodules, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

This variety is adapted to well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and grassland habitats.
