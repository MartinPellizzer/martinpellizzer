Lupinus argenteus var. fulvomaculatus

Lupinus argenteus var. fulvomaculatus, commonly known as the silver lupine with orange spots, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It features silver-gray foliage with distinctive orange or reddish markings on the leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces tall, spindly stems topped with clusters of pea-like, pink to purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Its nitrogen-fixing ability benefits soil health, supporting other plants in the ecosystem.
