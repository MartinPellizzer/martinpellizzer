Linaria reflexa var. ochroleuca

Linaria reflexa var. ochroleuca, commonly known as the yellow sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, branched stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a slightly hairy texture, and the flowers are arranged in loose, terminal clusters.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant color and drought tolerance.
