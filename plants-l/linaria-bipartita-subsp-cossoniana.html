Linaria bipartita subsp. cossoniana

Linaria bipartita subsp.

cossoniana, commonly known as the Cosson's toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and bright yellow-orange flowers with distinctive bilateral symmetry.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
