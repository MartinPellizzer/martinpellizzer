Lycopodium herminieri

Lycopodium herminieri, commonly known as the Herminier's clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. The plant features slender, branching stems covered with small, scale-like leaves. It produces strobila, which are cone-like structures that bear the spores.

Lycopodium herminieri plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
