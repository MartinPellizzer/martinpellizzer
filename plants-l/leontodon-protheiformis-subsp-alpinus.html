Leontodon protheiformis subsp. alpinus

Leontodon protheiformis subsp.

alpinus, commonly known as the alpine dandelion, is a flowering plant native to alpine regions of Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats at high altitudes. It has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces a single flower head that blooms in late spring to early summer.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species in many regions.
