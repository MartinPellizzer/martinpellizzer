Leiocarpa semicalva subsp. vinacea

Leiocarpa semicalva subsp.

vinacea, commonly known as the red-flowered rock cress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family. It is characterized by its distinctive bright red petals and is typically found in rocky or alpine habitats.

The plant has small, alternate leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. Its flowers are hermaphroditic, with four petals arranged in a cross shape, typical of the mustard family.
