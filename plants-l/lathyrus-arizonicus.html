Lathyrus arizonicus

Lathyrus arizonicus, commonly known as the Arizona pea, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces showy, pea-like flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, heart-shaped leaflets.

The plant is adapted to drought conditions and is often found in rocky soils and along streambanks.
