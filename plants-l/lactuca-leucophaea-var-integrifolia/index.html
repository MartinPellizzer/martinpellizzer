Lactuca Leucophaea Var. Integrifolia

White lettuce, scientifically known as Lactuca leucophaea var. integrifolia, is a rare and striking plant native to parts of Asia and the Middle East. Its most distinctive feature is its pale, almost translucent leaves that give it a unique appearance compared to common lettuce varieties. This plant belongs to the sunflower family, Asteraceae, and is valued for both its ornamental beauty and potential medicinal uses. Despite its delicate look, white lettuce is resilient and can thrive in a variety of soil conditions with proper care. It has gained attention among horticulturists and gardeners seeking to add a touch of elegance to their landscapes.
