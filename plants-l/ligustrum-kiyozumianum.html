Ligustrum kiyozumianum

Ligustrum kiyozumianum, commonly known as the Japanese privet, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features small, oval leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter green underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by dark blue berries that are edible for birds. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various soil types.

However, it can become invasive in some regions if not managed properly.
