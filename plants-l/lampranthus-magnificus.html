Lampranthus magnificus

Lampranthus magnificus, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a striking member of the Aizoaceae family native to South Africa.

It is a succulent shrub that produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. The plant features fleshy, ribbed stems and leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating called farina. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is widely cultivated in arid regions around the world.
