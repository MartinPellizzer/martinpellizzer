Lycopodium × helleri

Lycopodium × helleri, commonly known as the giant clubmoss, is a hybrid species within the Lycopodiaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces cone-like structures called strobili, which contain spores for reproduction. Its leaves are spirally arranged and often have a distinct, needle-like shape.

Lycopodium × helleri thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in temperate forest regions.
