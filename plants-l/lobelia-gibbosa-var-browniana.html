Lobelia gibbosa var. browniana

Lobelia gibbosa var. browniana, commonly known as the Brown's lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, tubular blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and opposite, with a hairy texture that helps retain moisture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
