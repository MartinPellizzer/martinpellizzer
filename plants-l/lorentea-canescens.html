Lorentea canescens

Lorentea canescens, commonly known as the silverleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves and yellow dandelion-like flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas and is often found in disturbed soils. It has a taproot system and produces a capsule that releases seeds with pappus for wind dispersal.

Lorentea canescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
