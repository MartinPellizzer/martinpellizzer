Leontodon autumnalis var. pinnatifidus

Leontodon autumnalis var. pinnatifidus, commonly known as the autumn leontodon, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in meadows and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a serrated margin, while the flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn.

It is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
