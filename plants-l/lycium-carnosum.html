Lycium carnosum

Lycium carnosum, commonly known as the European wolfberry or red berry bush, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It produces small, fleshy, bright red berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. The plant is characterized by its woody stems, alternate leaves, and clusters of yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and rocky habitats.

The berries are rich in antioxidants and have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
