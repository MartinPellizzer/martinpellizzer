Lamium flexuosum var. berengueri

Lamium flexuosum var. berengueri, commonly known as the Berenguer's dead nettle, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features oval, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy underside and produces clusters of small, two-lipped, pinkish-purple flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and along stream banks. It is characterized by its creeping growth habit and the presence of stolons that root at the nodes.

Botanically, it belongs to the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic foliage and showy flowers.
