Limosella aquatica var. americana

Limosella aquatica var. americana, commonly known as American marsh sandwort, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is a small, herbaceous annual that typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes and pond edges. The plant has alternate, linear leaves and produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters. It is adapted to aquatic conditions, with a root system that helps stabilize sediment and prevent erosion.

This species plays a role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
