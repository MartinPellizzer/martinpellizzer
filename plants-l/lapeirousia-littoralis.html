Lapeirousia littoralis

Lapeirousia littoralis, commonly known as the coastal oxalis, is a species of flowering plant native to the coastal regions of South Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along the shoreline. The plant features delicate, trifoliate leaves and produces small, bright yellow flowers during the summer months. It is well adapted to coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.

Lapeirousia littoralis is often found in dune environments and plays a role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems.
