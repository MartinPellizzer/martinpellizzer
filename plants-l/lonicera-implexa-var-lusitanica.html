Lonicera implexa var. lusitanica

Lonicera implexa var. lusitanica, commonly known as the Lusitanian honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant features opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are elliptical in shape. Its flowers are fragrant, yellowish-green, and appear in clusters, followed by red berries that are edible for birds.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
