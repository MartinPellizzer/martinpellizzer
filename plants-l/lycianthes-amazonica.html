Lycianthes amazonica

Lycianthes amazonica, commonly known as the Amazonian eggplant, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, egg-shaped fruits that range in color from yellow to red. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with alternate, ovate leaves that have a rough texture. Its flowers are usually yellow with purple veins, and it thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.

The fruit is often used in local cuisines and has potential medicinal properties.
