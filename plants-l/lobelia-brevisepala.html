Lobelia brevisepala

Lobelia brevisepala, commonly known as the short-winged lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, hairy stems that often grow in clusters, and its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a rough texture. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually blue or purple, arranged in spikes at the top of the stems. Lobelia brevisepala thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and wet meadows.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
