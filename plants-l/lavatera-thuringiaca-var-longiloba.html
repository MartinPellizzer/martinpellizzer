Lavatera thuringiaca var. longiloba

Lavatera thuringiaca var. longiloba, commonly known as the long-stemmed thuringian mallows, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, fluffy pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmately lobed leaves with a hairy texture, and its stems are often covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for gardens and landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
