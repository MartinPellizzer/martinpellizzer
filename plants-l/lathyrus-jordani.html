Lathyrus jordani

Lathyrus jordani, commonly known as Jordan's vetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant features slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three small, heart-shaped leaflets. Its showy, pea-like flowers are usually violet or purple in color, appearing in early spring.

Lathyrus jordani is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in arid landscapes.
