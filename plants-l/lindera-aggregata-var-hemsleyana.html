Lindera aggregata var. hemsleyana

Lindera aggregata var. hemsleyana, commonly known as Hemsley's sweet pepper, is a variant of the sweet pepper plant native to China.

It is a shrub that typically grows in forested areas and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, aromatic leaves and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruits are small, red berries that ripen in the fall and are edible.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and mild, sweet flavor in culinary applications.
