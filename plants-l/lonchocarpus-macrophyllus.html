Lonchocarpus macrophyllus

Lonchocarpus macrophyllus, commonly known as the giant senna, is a tropical legume native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmately compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in agricultural settings.

However, some parts of the plant contain toxic compounds, requiring caution in handling and use.
