Leontodon muelleri subsp. austromaroccanus

Leontodon muelleri subsp.

austromaroccanus, commonly known as the Moroccan mueller's bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It produces small, daisy-like flowers that are usually pink or white in color.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations and limited distribution.
