Lupinus montesii

Lupinus montesii, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands at high elevations. The plant features tall, slender stems with clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Lupinus montesii plays an important ecological role by fixing nitrogen into the soil, benefiting surrounding plant life.
