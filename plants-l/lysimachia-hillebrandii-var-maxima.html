Lysimachia hillebrandii var. maxima

Lysimachia hillebrandii var. maxima, commonly known as the giant yellow loosestrife, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers throughout the summer. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors or stream banks.

Botanically, it belongs to the Primulaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
