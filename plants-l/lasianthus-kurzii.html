Lasianthus kurzii

Lasianthus kurzii, commonly known as the golden flame orchid, is a species of flowering plant in the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its striking yellow and orange flowers that bloom in clusters, making it a visually appealing ornamental plant. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Lasianthus kurzii is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and as a cut flower.
