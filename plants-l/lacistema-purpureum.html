Lacistema purpureum

Lacistema purpureum, commonly known as the purple lacistema, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its striking purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that are often tinged with red or purple, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its unique appearance and historical use in traditional medicine, it is valued both aesthetically and botanically.
