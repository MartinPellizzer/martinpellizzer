Linociera phanerophlebia

Linociera phanerophlebia, commonly known as the red-flowered bignon, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of showy, red or orange flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Linociera phanerophlebia is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and vibrant floral display.
