Lobelia anceps var. recurvata

Lobelia anceps var. recurvata, commonly known as the recurved lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to violet. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture. This variety is known for its ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for wetland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate appearance and ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
