Lilium angustifolium

Lilium angustifolium, commonly known as the tawny lily, is a species of flowering plant native to western North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It is a perennial herb that blooms in late summer and early autumn.

The bulbs of this plant are edible and have been used by indigenous peoples for culinary and medicinal purposes.
