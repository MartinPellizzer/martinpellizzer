Leptochilus siifolius

Leptochilus siifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leafed bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its drought tolerance.

Leptochilus siifolius is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
