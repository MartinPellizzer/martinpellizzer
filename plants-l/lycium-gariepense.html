Lycium gariepense

Lycium gariepense, commonly known as the Namaqualia, is a shrub native to the semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom profusely during the winter months. The plant has small, leathery leaves that help it conserve water in its arid environment. It produces red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Lycium gariepense is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
