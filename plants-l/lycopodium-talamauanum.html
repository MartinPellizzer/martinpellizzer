Lycopodium talamauanum

Lycopodium talamauanum, commonly known as the Talamauan clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. The plant has a creeping stem with whorled leaves that are usually less than 1 cm in length. It produces spores in cone-like structures called strobila, which are positioned at the tips of its branches.

This species is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for microorganisms in its native range.
