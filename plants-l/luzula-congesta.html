Luzula congesta

Luzula congesta, commonly known as the clustered wood rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, commonly known as the grass family, and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear greenish to brownish flowers. It typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Luzula congesta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
