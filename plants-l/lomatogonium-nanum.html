Lomatogonium nanum

Lomatogonium nanum, commonly known as the dwarf lomatogonium, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
