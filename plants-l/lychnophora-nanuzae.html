Lychnophora nanuzae

Lychnophora nanuzae, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood or *pau-rosa*, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage and small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant's leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a leathery texture with a glossy appearance. It produces small, dark purple berries that contain seeds, which are dispersed by birds.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is a species of conservation concern.
