Lomariopsis papyracea

Lomariopsis papyracea, commonly known as the paper fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, papyraceous (paper-like) fronds that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

The spores are released from the sori located on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.
