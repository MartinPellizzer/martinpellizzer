Liquidambar styraciflua

Liquidambar styraciflua, commonly known as sweet gum, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its opposite, palmately lobed leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and purple in the fall. The tree produces small, spiky, spherical fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its bark is thick and furrowed, and it can reach heights of up to 50 meters.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often planted in urban landscapes for its aesthetic appeal.
