Linum holstii

Linum holstii, commonly known as the desert flax, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Linaceae family, which includes other flax species. This plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. Linum holstii is adapted to arid environments, featuring deep roots and a low-growing habit.

It plays a role in stabilizing soils in desert ecosystems and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
