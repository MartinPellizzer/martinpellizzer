Lophosoria glaucescens

Lophosoria glaucescens, commonly known as the blue-veined orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, often blue or purple-pink flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, tropical environments. Its leaves are elongated and often have a waxy texture, while its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture from the air.

Lophosoria glaucescens is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to care for under the right conditions.
