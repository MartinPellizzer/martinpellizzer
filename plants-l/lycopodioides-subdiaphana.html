Lycopodioides subdiaphana

Lycopodioides subdiaphana, commonly known as the glassy lycopod, is a rare and delicate fern-like plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae, which includes clubmosses and other ancient vascular plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and small, translucent spore-producing structures called sporophylls. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in mountainous forests.

Due to its limited distribution and fragile nature, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native habitat.
