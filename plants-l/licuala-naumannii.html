Licuala naumannii

Licuala naumannii, commonly known as the giant pandan, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, pinnate leaves that can reach lengths of up to 3 meters, making it a notable feature in its natural habitat. The plant belongs to the Arecaceae family, which includes palms, and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its leaves are used in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber, while its rhizomes are sometimes utilized in local cuisines.

The species thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
