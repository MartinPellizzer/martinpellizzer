Linum austriacum subsp. provinciale

Linum austriacum subsp.

provinciale, commonly known as the Austrian flax, is a flowering plant native to Central Europe. It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its delicate blue or purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and often trailing.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in textile production.
