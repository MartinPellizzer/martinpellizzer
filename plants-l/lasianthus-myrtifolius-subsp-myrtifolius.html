Lasianthus myrtifolius subsp. myrtifolius

Lasianthus myrtifolius subsp.

myrtifolius, commonly known as the myrtle-leaved lasianthus, is a species native to Southeast Asia. It is a shrub characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
