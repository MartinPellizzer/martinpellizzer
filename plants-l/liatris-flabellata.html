Liatris flabellata

Liatris flabellata, commonly known as the flatleaf blazing star, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, purple-pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as prairies, marshes, and along stream banks. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a flat, fan-like pattern, giving it its common name.

Liatris flabellata is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
