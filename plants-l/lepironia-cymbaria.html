Lepironia cymbaria

Lepironia cymbaria, commonly known as the water lily of the genus Lepironia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves and showy, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats, such as ponds and marshes. Its roots are often submerged, anchoring it in muddy substrates while its stems float on the water's surface.
