Lepidogrammitis subrostrata

Lepidogrammitis subrostrata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically less than 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are clustered on the underside of the fronds. Its botanical features make it a distinctive and relatively rare species within its native range.
