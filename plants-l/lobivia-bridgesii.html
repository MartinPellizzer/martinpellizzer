Lobivia bridgesii

Lobivia bridgesii, commonly known as the Desert Rose, is a succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It features a compact, rounded body with thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces vibrant, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or purple during the warmer months. Its leaves are typically spineless and may be arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

Lobivia bridgesii is well-suited for rock gardens and arid climates due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
