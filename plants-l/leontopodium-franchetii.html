Leontopodium franchetii

Leontopodium franchetii, commonly known as the edelweiss, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Alps and other mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly white bracts that enclose the small, yellowish flowers, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in harsh, rocky environments where it is adapted to cold temperatures and strong winds. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.

Due to its ecological significance and aesthetic appeal, it is also a symbol of resilience and is protected in many areas.
