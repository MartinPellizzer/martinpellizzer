Lycium carinatum

Lycium carinatum, commonly known as Chinese boxthorn, is a shrub native to China and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, bright orange berries that ripen in the fall. The plant belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes and potatoes, and is known for its medicinal properties. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Lycium carinatum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
