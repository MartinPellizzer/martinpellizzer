Lepanthes selliana

Lepanthes selliana, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a compact growth habit, featuring a pseudobulb and a single, lance-shaped leaf. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers with a distinctive, spurred lip that attracts pollinators. Lepanthes selliana thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its unique floral structure and vibrant color make it a prized species among orchid enthusiasts.
