Luzula campestris var. luteola

Luzula campestris var. luteola, commonly known as the yellow tufted sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shady habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features grass-like leaves that are dark green and somewhat fleshy, with a dense, tufted appearance at the base. Its inflorescence consists of a cluster of spikelets, each bearing small, three-lobed flowers.

This variety is distinguished by its golden-yellow coloration, particularly in the flower heads, which adds to its ornamental value in gardens.
