Lotononis eriosemoides

Lotononis eriosemoides, commonly known as the woolly bladderwort, is a small aquatic plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, floating leaves that are covered in fine, woolly hairs, which help in reducing water loss. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes.

Due to its unique adaptations, it plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small invertebrates.
