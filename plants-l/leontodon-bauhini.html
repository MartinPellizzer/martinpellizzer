Leontodon bauhini

Leontodon bauhini, commonly known as the mountain dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine environments. Its flowers are yellow, and it produces a distinctive, elongated seed head that aids in wind dispersal.

Leontodon bauhini is often found in open habitats and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
