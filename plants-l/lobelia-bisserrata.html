Lobelia bisserrata

Lobelia bisserrata, commonly known as the serrated lobelia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are serrated, giving it its common name, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Lobelia bisserrata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
