Lippia pohliana var. longibracteolata

Lippia pohliana var. longibracteolata, commonly known as the long-bract lippia, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of pink to purple. The plant belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is often found in dry, rocky habitats. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

The varietal form, longibracteolata, is distinguished by its elongated bracts that enclose the flower clusters.
