Lycopodium binervium

Lycopodium binervium, commonly known as the two-veined clubmoss, is a vascular plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is a small, evergreen herbaceous plant characterized by its spiral arrangement of leaves and spore-bearing cones. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant's structure includes a root system, stems, and microphylls, which are simple leaves that lack veins.

Lycopodium binervium plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
