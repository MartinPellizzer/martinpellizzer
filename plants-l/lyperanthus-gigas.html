Lyperanthus gigas

Lyperanthus gigas, commonly known as the giant lily, is a striking perennial herb native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has a robust rhizomatous root system that allows it to thrive in well-drained, sandy soils. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, forming a dense rosette at the base.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its dramatic floral display.
