Linociera ramiflora f. pubisepala

Linociera ramiflora f. pubisepala, commonly known as the pubisepal form of the branched flame tree, is a variant of the species Linociera ramiflora.

It is characterized by its distinctive floral structure, with sepals that are pubescent, giving it a soft, hairy appearance. The plant is native to tropical regions of South America and thrives in warm, humid climates. Its flowers are typically bright red or orange, and it produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds.

This form is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking floral display.
