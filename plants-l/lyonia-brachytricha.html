Lyonia brachytricha

Lyonia brachytricha, commonly known as the narrowleaf lyonia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, providing year-round foliage.

Lyonia brachytricha thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.
