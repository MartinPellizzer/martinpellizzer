Lycopodioides longissima

Lycopodioides longissima, commonly known as the long-stemmed clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the order Lycopodiales.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping stems that produce small, scale-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures, known as strobila, are cone-like and contain spores.

Lycopodioides longissima plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
