Lamium cariense

Lamium cariense, commonly known as Carpathian deadnettle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorled clusters along the stem. Lamium cariense thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth or along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
