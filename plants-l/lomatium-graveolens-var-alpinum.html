Lomatium graveolens var. alpinum

Lomatium graveolens var. alpinum, commonly known as alpine lomatium, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its fern-like, deeply lobed leaves and produces yellow to orange flowers in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats. Its roots are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
