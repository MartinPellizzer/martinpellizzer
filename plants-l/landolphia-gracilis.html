Landolphia gracilis

Landolphia gracilis, commonly known as the slender African morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range from yellow to orange in color. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Landolphia gracilis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some regions.
