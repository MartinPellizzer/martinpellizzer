Lolium multiflorum var. excelsum

Lolium multiflorum var. excelsum, commonly known as tall ryegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, robust growth and dense, fine-textured foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in temperate climates and is often used in forage agriculture due to its high nutritional value.

Additionally, it is valued in ecological restoration projects for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.
