Lachenalia versicolor

Lachenalia versicolor, commonly known as the variable lachenalia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are typically fleshy and covered in a powdery coating. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
