Lilium canadense var. rubroflavum

Lilium canadense var. rubroflavum, commonly known as the red-flowered Canada lily, is a variant of the Canada lily native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its striking red to orange-red flowers with a yellow throat, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. It has tall, slender stems with narrow, grass-like leaves.

The flowers are tubular at the base and have six petals that are often reflexed at the tips.
