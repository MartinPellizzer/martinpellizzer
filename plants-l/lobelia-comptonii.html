Lobelia comptonii

Lobelia comptonii, commonly known as Compton's lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. The plant produces showy, tubular blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Lobelia comptonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
