Lobelia claytoniana

Lobelia claytoniana, commonly known as Clayton's lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is often found in swampy or wetland habitats.
