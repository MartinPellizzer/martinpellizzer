Lupinus indutus

Lupinus indutus, commonly known as the velvet lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and clusters of pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are an important food source for various wildlife species.

Lupinus indutus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
