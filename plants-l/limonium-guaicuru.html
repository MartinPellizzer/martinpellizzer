Limonium guaicuru

Limonium guaicuru, commonly known as the coastal bluebell, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal dunes and cliffs. Its flowers are typically borne on long, slender pedicels, giving it a delicate appearance.

Limonium guaicuru is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to stabilize coastal soils.
