Lightfootia lanceolata

Lightfootia lanceolata, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green leaves and clusters of showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant is an epiphyte, often growing on trees in its natural habitat, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped, giving it its scientific name, and it is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value.

Lightfootia lanceolata requires well-draining soil and indirect sunlight to flourish.
