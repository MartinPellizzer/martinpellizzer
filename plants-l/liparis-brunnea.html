Liparis brunnea

Liparis brunnea, commonly known as the brown lady's tresses, is a terrestrial orchid native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and a distinctive inflorescence of small, brownish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky crevices. Its roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption in poor soil conditions.

Liparis brunnea is a perennial species that thrives in cool, humid climates.
