Lobelia oblongifolia

Lobelia oblongifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, tubular blue or violet flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
