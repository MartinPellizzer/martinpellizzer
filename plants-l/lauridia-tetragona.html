Lauridia tetragona

Lauridia tetragona, commonly known as the four-angled laurel, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its evergreen, aromatic leaves that are typically four-angled in cross-section. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. Its fruit is a spherical drupe that contains a single seed.

Lauridia tetragona is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
