Lobivia pentlandii f. omasuyana

Lobivia pentlandii f. omasuyana, commonly known as the Omasuyana Lobivia, is a rare and striking cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are often ribbed and covered in white or pale blue spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically borne at the base of the stem. It is a compact, slow-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and full sunlight.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a collectible and challenging plant for cactus enthusiasts.
