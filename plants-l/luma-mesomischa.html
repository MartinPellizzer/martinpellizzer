Luma mesomischa

Luma mesomischa, commonly known as the golden torch tree, is a species of flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its striking, orange-red flowers that bloom in dense clusters, often resembling torches. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets that are glossy and dark green. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal or lowland areas.

The golden torch tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
