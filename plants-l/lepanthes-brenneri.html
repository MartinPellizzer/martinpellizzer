Lepanthes brenneri

Lepanthes brenneri, commonly known as the Brenner's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks or branches in humid, tropical environments. Its flowers are usually small and colorful, with a variety of hues including white, pink, and purple.

Lepanthes brenneri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
