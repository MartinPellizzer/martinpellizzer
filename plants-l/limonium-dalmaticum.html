Limonium dalmaticum

Limonium dalmaticum, commonly known as the Dalmatian sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and striking blue to purple flower heads, which are often surrounded by silver or white bracts. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, sandy soils, and saline environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Limonium dalmaticum is widely cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and resilience.
