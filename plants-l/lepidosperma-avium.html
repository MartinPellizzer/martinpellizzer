Lepidosperma avium

Lepidosperma avium, commonly known as the swamp spike-rush, is a species of sedge native to Australia.

It typically grows in wetland environments, such as swampy areas and seasonally flooded soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads. Its inflorescence consists of spikes of tiny, three-lobed flowers that are often green or reddish in color.

Lepidosperma avium plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
