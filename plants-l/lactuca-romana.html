Lactuca romana

Lactuca romana, commonly known as Italian chicory, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and basal rosette of large, wavy-edged leaves. The plant produces blue to purple composite flower heads, which consist of numerous small florets. It is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.

Lactuca romana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
