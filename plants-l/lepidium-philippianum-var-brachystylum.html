Lepidium philippianum var. brachystylum

Lepidium philippianum var. brachystylum, commonly known as the dwarf wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in open, sunny habitats. It has a low-growing habit with slender stems and small, lance-shaped leaves.

Its blooming period occurs in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of yellow or white flowers.
