Linnaeobreynia grisebachii

Linnaeobreynia grisebachii, commonly known as the Grisebach's ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic orchid species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pale green to grayish-white flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions, hence its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to the bark of trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are often clustered in small inflorescences, with each bloom featuring three narrow petals and a prominent lip.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Linnaeobreynia grisebachii is considered a conservation concern.
