Lactuca hoepfneriana

Lactuca hoepfneriana, commonly known as the Hoepfner's lettuce, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. It is often found in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
