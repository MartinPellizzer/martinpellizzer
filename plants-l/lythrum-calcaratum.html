Lythrum calcaratum

Lythrum calcaratum, commonly known as narrowleaf catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Lythraceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. Its leaves are linear and slightly hairy, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

Lythrum calcaratum is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and has some historical uses in traditional medicine.
