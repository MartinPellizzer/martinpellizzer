Lupinus montanus var. austrovolcanicus

Lupinus montanus var. austrovolcanicus, commonly known as the Southern Volcanic Lupine, is a perennial legume native to the volcanic regions of South America.

It typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. The plant features erect stems with narrow, pinnate leaves and produces spikes of violet to blue flowers. It is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.

This variety is of interest to botanists and ecologists due to its unique adaptations and ecological role in its habitat.
