Lotus corniculatus subsp. frondosus

Lotus corniculatus subsp.

frondosus, commonly known as the fronded bird's-foot trefoil, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are often hairy and have a distinctive lobed appearance.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed sites.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
