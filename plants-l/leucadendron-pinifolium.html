Leucadendron pinifolium

Leucadendron pinifolium, commonly known as the silver leaf leucadendron, is a South African shrub belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray, needle-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces bright yellow or orange flower heads, which are clustered in conical shapes. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
