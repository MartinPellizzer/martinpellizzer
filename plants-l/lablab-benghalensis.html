Lablab benghalensis

Lablab benghalensis, commonly known as hyacinth bean, is a tropical legume native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking violet or blue flowers, which are often clustered in racemes. The plant produces elongated pods that contain edible seeds, which are rich in protein and nutrients. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being oval and slightly hairy.

Lablab benghalensis is often grown as a ornamental plant due to its vibrant flowers and is also used in traditional cuisines.
