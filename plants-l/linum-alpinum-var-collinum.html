Linum alpinum var. collinum

Linum alpinum var. collinum, commonly known as the alpine flax, is a perennial herb native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, erect stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture. The plant produces small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

Linum alpinum var. collinum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in textile production.
