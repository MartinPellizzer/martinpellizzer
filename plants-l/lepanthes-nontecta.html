Lepanthes nontecta

Lepanthes nontecta, commonly known as the golden tassel orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow petals and a distinctive, elongated flower spike. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as cloud forests, where it thrives in the understory. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in moisture retention.

Lepanthes nontecta is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
