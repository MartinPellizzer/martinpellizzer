Lepidium papillosum

Lepidium papillosum, commonly known as the silky cress, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, white or yellow flowers and hairy, lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is found in various regions of North America. It produces small, round seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Lepidium papillosum is often considered a weed but has some historical uses in traditional medicine.
