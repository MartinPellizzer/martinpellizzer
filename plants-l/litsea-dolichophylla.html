Litsea dolichophylla

Litsea dolichophylla, commonly known as the long-leaf litsea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by berries that are red when mature.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
