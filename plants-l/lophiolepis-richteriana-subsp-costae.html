Lophiolepis richteriana subsp. costae

Lophiolepis richteriana subsp.

costae, commonly known as the Transkei daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and linear leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It produces small, daisy-like inflorescences that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual appeal.
