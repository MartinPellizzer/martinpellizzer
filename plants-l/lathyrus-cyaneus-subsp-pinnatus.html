Lathyrus cyaneus subsp. pinnatus

Lathyrus cyaneus subsp.

pinnatus, commonly known as the purple vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, climbing stems with trifoliate leaves that have a distinctive pinnate arrangement.

The plant produces striking purple to blue flowers in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its root system fixes nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.

It is often found in rocky or disturbed soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
