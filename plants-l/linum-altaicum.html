Linum altaicum

Linum altaicum, commonly known as mountain flax, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe.

It is a member of the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, blue or violet flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Linum altaicum thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Its delicate appearance and ecological significance make it a notable species in its native environment.
