Lupinus howard-scottii

Lupinus howard-scottii, commonly known as the Howard-Scott lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States, particularly found in California and Nevada.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of pea-like flowers. The plant typically blooms in the spring and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Lupinus howard-scottii is adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh conditions.

Its ecological importance includes providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for certain wildlife.
