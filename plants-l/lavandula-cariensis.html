Lavandula cariensis

Lavandula cariensis, commonly known as the Canary Islands lavender, is a species native to the Canary Islands.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky and coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant produces spikes of purple flowers, which are often used in perfumery and aromatherapy. Its aromatic leaves and flowers make it a valuable plant in both horticulture and traditional medicine.

Lavandula cariensis is also noted for its resilience to drought and salt spray, making it well adapted to its native environment.
