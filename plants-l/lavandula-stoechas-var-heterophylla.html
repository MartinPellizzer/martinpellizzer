Lavandula stoechas var. heterophylla

Lavandula stoechas var. heterophylla, commonly known as the Spanish lavender or narrow-leaf lavender, is a variant of the common lavender species.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and spikes of fragrant purple or violet flowers. This plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.

The variety is distinguished by its unique leaf morphology compared to the standard Lavandula stoechas.
