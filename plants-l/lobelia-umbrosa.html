Lobelia umbrosa

Lobelia umbrosa, commonly known as the shadow lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a fibrous root system and produces capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds.

Lobelia umbrosa is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
