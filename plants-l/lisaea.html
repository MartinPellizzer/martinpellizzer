Lisaea

Lisaea, commonly known as the "false dandelion," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It consists of several species, most of which are annual or perennial herbs. The plants typically have basal leaves that are lance-shaped or ovate, with hairy surfaces. They produce small, yellow flowers that are clustered in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Lisaea species are often found in disturbed soils and are common in temperate regions.
