Laccosperma cristalensis

Laccosperma cristalensis, commonly known as the Crystalensis tamarind, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, warm climates. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. It is often cultivated for its nutritional value and is used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.

The tree can grow up to 10 meters tall and is valued for both its ecological and economic roles.
