Linum perenne var. boreale

Linum perenne var. boreale, commonly known as the northern flax, is a perennial plant native to northern regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It prefers cool, moist environments and is often found in meadows and woodlands.

Linum perenne var. boreale is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in textile production.
