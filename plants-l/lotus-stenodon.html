Lotus stenodon

Lotus stenodon, commonly known as the water lily, is a aquatic plant that thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its large, floating leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom above the water surface. The plant has a well-developed root system that anchors it in the mud at the bottom of ponds or lakes. Its leaves are typically rounded with a waxy surface that helps repel water, while its flowers come in a range of colors, often white, pink, or yellow.

Lotus stenodon plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
