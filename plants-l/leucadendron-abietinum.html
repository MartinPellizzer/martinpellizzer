Leucadendron abietinum

Leucadendron abietinum, commonly known as the silver leaf leucadendron, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and vibrant yellow to orange flower heads. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity.

It is often used in ornamental gardening for its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
