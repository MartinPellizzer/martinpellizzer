Leptopleuria abrupta

Leptopleuria abrupta, commonly known as the abrupt leaf fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive, abruptly tapered leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest understories. Its fronds are usually bi- or tri-pinnate, with a delicate, feathery appearance.

Leptopleuria abrupta is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
