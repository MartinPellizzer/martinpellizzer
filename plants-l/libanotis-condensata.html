Libanotis condensata

Libanotis condensata, commonly known as the condensed bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it is relatively rare in the wild.
