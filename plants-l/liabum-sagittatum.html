Liabum sagittatum

Liabum sagittatum, commonly known as arrowleaf groundcherry, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its distinctive arrowhead-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are edible when ripe, though they are often covered in a sticky substance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or open areas.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
