Leptospermum continentale

Leptospermum continentale, commonly known as the coastal tea tree, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its small, needle-like leaves and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in coastal environments and is often found in sandy soils.

Leptospermum continentale is valued for its aromatic foliage and its role in coastal ecosystems.
