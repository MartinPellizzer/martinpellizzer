Laportea ventricosa

Laportea ventricosa, commonly known as the roughleaf mallow, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in open woodlands. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.
