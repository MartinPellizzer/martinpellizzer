Lindsaea ensifolia subsp. coriacea

Lindsaea ensifolia subsp.

coriacea, commonly known as the rough-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species native to tropical regions of South America. It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough, leathery texture, which gives the subspecies its specific epithet *coriacea*. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
