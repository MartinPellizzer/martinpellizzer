Luzula multiflora subsp. sibirica

Luzula multiflora subsp.

sibirica, commonly known as Siberian woodrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the cold climates of Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and mountainous regions.

The plant features dense, grass-like foliage with narrow, dark green leaves that form a compact rosette. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, white to greenish flowers.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
