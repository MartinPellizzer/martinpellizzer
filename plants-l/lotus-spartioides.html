Lotus spartioides

Lotus spartioides, commonly known as the desert lotus, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is a perennial herb that thrives in hot, dry climates and is adapted to poor soil conditions. The plant produces small, pale yellow or white flowers that bloom in summer, often in clusters. Its leaves are succulent and fleshy, helping it retain moisture in harsh environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
