Leptocarpus simplex var. fasciculatus

Leptocarpus simplex var. fasciculatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, fleshy leaves and small, clustered blue or violet flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky coastal soils and is adapted to saline and wind-exposed environments. It is often found in tidal zones and is a common component of coastal vegetation.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in coastal ecosystems.
