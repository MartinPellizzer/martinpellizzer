Lycium oreophilum

Lycium oreophilum, commonly known as the mountain lily, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its woody stems and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. Its berries, which are orange-red in color, are edible and have a sweet, tart flavor.

Lycium oreophilum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
