Libidibia punctata

Libidibia punctata, commonly known as the yellow myrtle, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by yellowish or orange fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
