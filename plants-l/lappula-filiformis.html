Lappula filiformis

Lappula filiformis, commonly known as slender lappula, is a flowering plant native to Eurasia.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a hairy texture.
