Loranthus dregei f. subcuneifolius

Loranthus dregei f. subcuneifolius, commonly known as the narrow leaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant belonging to the Santalaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly found in regions of South Africa, Namibia, and Botswana. The plant has small, lanceolate leaves that are typically arranged in whorls along its stems. It attaches to host trees, such as acacias, through haustoria, drawing water and nutrients from the host.

Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow, and it produces small, fleshy berries that are dispersed by birds.
