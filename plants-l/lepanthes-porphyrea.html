Lepanthes porphyrea

Lepanthes porphyrea, commonly known as the Porphyric Orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet and pink flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as cloud forests, where it thrives on epiphytic substrates. Its flowers are often fragrant and attract various pollinators, including bees and moths.

Lepanthes porphyrea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is a subject of interest in botanical research due to its unique floral structure and ecological adaptations.
