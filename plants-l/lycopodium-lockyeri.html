Lycopodium lockyeri

Lycopodium lockyeri, commonly known as the Lockyer's clubmoss, is a species of lycophyte belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its small, spore-bearing cones and its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments. This plant typically grows in forest understories and is found in regions with a temperate climate. Lycopodium lockyeri has a simple, branching structure with small, scale-like leaves.

Its reproductive structures are essential for its survival and propagation in natural habitats.
