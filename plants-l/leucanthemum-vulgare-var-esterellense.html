Leucanthemum vulgare var. esterellense

Leucanthemum vulgare var. esterellense, commonly known as the Esterellense white marguerite, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its white to pale yellow ray florets and dark brown disc florets, which form a daisy-like appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has a compact growth habit with basal leaves that are usually lance-shaped and hairy.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
