Laurus pedunculata

Laurus pedunculata, commonly known as sweet bay laurel, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, featuring opposite, aromatic, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into dark blue, spherical berries. Its leaves are often used in culinary and aromatic applications, and the plant is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
