Lilium stewartianum

Lilium stewartianum, commonly known as Stewart's lily, is a species of flowering plant in the lily family.

It is native to the western United States, particularly found in the Sierra Nevada and Cascade mountain ranges. This perennial herbaceous plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and red. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and produces bulbous roots, which are characteristic of many lily species.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shady environments.
