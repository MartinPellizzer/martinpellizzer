Lycaste fimbriata var. peruviana

Lycaste fimbriata var. peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian lycaste, is a rare and striking orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers with intricate patterns and a distinctive, velvety texture. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in the dry, high-altitude environments where it thrives.

This variety is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts due to its beauty and rarity.
