Liriodendron procerum

Liriodendron procerum, commonly known as the tulip tree, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and its showy, fragrant yellow flowers that resemble tulips. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a pointed tip, often turning yellow in the fall. The tree produces woody, cone-like fruits that contain two winged seeds.

It is valued for its rapid growth and ornamental qualities in landscaping.
