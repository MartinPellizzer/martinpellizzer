Lonchocarpus brenesii subsp. brenesii

Lonchocarpus brenesii subsp.

brenesii, commonly known as the Caribbean coral bean, is a woody shrub or small tree native to Central America and the Caribbean. It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate or pinnate, and its distinctive yellow to orange flowers.

The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, and its root system is known for containing toxic alkaloids. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often in disturbed or secondary forest habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
