Lepanthes villosa

Lepanthes villosa, commonly known as the velvety orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as cloud forests, where it thrives on tree trunks or among foliage. Its leaves are usually broad and glossy, contributing to its overall ornamental appeal.

Lepanthes villosa is valued for its beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
