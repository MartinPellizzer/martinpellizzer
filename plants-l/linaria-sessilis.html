Linaria sessilis

Linaria sessilis, commonly known as the sessile toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features upright stems with opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are yellow with two lips, resembling those of the snapdragon genus.

Linaria sessilis thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
