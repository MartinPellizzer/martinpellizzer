Lasiochloa capitata

Lasiochloa capitata, commonly known as the tufted grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green to purplish in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Lasiochloa capitata is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in dry environments.
