Lophiolepis buschiana

Lophiolepis buschiana, commonly known as the Bushman's grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky habitats.

Lophiolepis buschiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
