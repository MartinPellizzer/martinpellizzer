Lobelia ritabeaniana

Lobelia ritabeaniana, commonly known as the Brazilian lily, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically blooms in the summer months and is often found in moist, shady habitats such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil.

Lobelia ritabeaniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
