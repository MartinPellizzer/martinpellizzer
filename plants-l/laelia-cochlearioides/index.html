Laelia Cochlearioides

Laelia cochlearioides is a rare and striking orchid native to the high altitudes of South America. Known for its unique spiral-shaped flowers, it captivates botanists and enthusiasts alike with its beauty and resilience. This epiphytic plant thrives in cool, humid environments and requires specific care to flourish. Its name, meaning "shell-like," refers to the intricate structure of its blooms. Due to habitat loss, it is now considered endangered, making conservation efforts crucial for its survival.
