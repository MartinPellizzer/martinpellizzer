Lonicera ramosissima var. borealis

Lonicera ramosissima var. borealis, commonly known as the northern honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the northern regions of North America.

It features slender, arching stems that often grow in a vine-like manner, with opposite, ovate leaves that are usually hairy beneath. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a small, red berry that ripens in late summer, attracting various birds and small mammals.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
