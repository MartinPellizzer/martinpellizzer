Liriope muscari

Liriope muscari, commonly known as the blue grass or grape hyacinth, is a perennial evergreen plant native to Asia.

It features grass-like foliage that remains green throughout the year, forming dense clumps. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in partial to full shade and is often used in ornamental landscaping for its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements.

Liriope muscari is also valued for its ability to suppress weeds and its suitability for erosion control in shaded garden areas.
