Lavatera cavanillesii

Lavatera cavanillesii, commonly known as the Chilean woolly mallow, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features include hairy stems and capsules that contain numerous small seeds.
