Lysimachia paniculata

Lysimachia paniculata, commonly known as the creeping Jenny, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North America.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Lysimachia paniculata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wet areas.
