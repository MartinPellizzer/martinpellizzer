Lupinus argenteus f. albiflorus

Lupinus argenteus f. albiflorus, commonly known as the silver lupine with white flowers, is a variant of the silver lupine native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and distinctive white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its flowers are arranged in dense, spikelike clusters and are attractive to pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
