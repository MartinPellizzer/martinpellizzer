Lycopersicon cerasiforme var. leptophyllum

Lycopersicon cerasiforme var. leptophyllum, commonly known as the small-fruited tomato, is a botanical variant of the tomato species.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and smaller, often more acidic fruits compared to typical tomatoes. The plant produces clusters of small, red or orange berries that are typically harvested when fully ripe. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its unique flavor profile and use in specialty culinary applications.
