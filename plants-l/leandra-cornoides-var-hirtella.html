Leandra cornoides var. hirtella

Leandra cornoides var. hirtella, commonly known as the hairy leandra, is a tropical plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a waxy texture, while its flowers are clustered in panicles and are usually white or pale green.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and subtle floral display.
