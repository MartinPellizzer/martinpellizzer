Lucuma keule

Lucuma keule, commonly known as the golden apple of the Andes, is a member of the Sapotaceae family.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of Peru and Ecuador, where it thrives in cool, temperate climates. The fruit has a distinctive golden-yellow pulp that is rich in nutrients, including vitamins A and C, and dietary fiber. Its botanical structure includes a thick, leathery rind surrounding the edible, seed-containing interior.

The plant is valued not only for its unique flavor but also for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
