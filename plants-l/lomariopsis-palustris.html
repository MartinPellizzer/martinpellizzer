Lomariopsis palustris

Lomariopsis palustris, commonly known as the marsh lomariopsis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater marshes and along the edges of ponds and ditches. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers during the spring and summer. Lomariopsis palustris is adapted to saturated soil conditions and plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils.

Its presence is often an indicator of healthy, undisturbed aquatic habitats.
