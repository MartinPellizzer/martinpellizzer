Lonchocarpus virgilioides

Lonchocarpus virgilioides, commonly known as the yellow senna, is a shrub or small tree native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, and it is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

It is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it valuable in soil enrichment and ecological restoration efforts.
