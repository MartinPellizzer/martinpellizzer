Lemaireocereus cumengei

Lemaireocereus cumengei, commonly known as the Cumenge's night bloomer, is a species of cactus native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its columnar stem and spiny, ribbed structure, which helps it conserve water in arid environments. The plant produces striking white flowers at night, which attract nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its growth is slow, and it thrives in well-drained soil with minimal water.

Lemaireocereus cumengei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique blooming habit.
