Libanotis eriocarpa

Libanotis eriocarpa, commonly known as the woolly bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, woolly leaves and stems, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is well adapted to arid conditions.

Libanotis eriocarpa is often found in open woodlands and along the edges of scrublands.
