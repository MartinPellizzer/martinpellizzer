Lepidium africanum var. burchellii

Lepidium africanum var. burchellii, commonly known as African cress, is a species of flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its small, edible leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. It is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks, where it forms dense mats.

The leaves are used in traditional cuisine and have mild peppery flavors similar to watercress.
