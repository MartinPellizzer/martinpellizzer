Linaria elatine subsp. sieberi

Linaria elatine subsp.

sieberi, commonly known as the small-flowered toadflax, is a low-growing annual plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia. It features slender, branched stems and small, bright yellow flowers with two lips, typical of the Plantaginaceae family.

The leaves are opposite, linear, and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in grasslands, roadsides, and waste areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
