Lantana undulata var. saltensis

Lantana undulata var. saltensis, commonly known as salt marsh lantana, is a perennial shrub native to coastal regions of the southeastern United States.

It thrives in brackish or saline environments, often found in salt marshes and tidal flats. The plant has small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to yellow, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for wildlife.
