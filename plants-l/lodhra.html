Lodhra

Lodhra, commonly known as Symplocos racemosa, is a deciduous shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. The plant has elliptical leaves that are opposite in arrangement and turn yellow in autumn. Its bark is smooth and greyish, with a distinctive aromatic scent.

Lodhra is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional systems of medicine.
