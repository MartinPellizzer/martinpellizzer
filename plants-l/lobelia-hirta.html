Lobelia hirta

Lobelia hirta, commonly known as wooly lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and clusters of blue to purple tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is often found in moist soils.

Lobelia hirta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
