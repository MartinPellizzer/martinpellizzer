Laurus teneriffae

Laurus teneriffae, commonly known as the Canary Island laurel, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like bay laurel. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers followed by edible berries.

It thrives in coastal areas and is often found in rocky soils, showing adaptability to the island's unique climate.
