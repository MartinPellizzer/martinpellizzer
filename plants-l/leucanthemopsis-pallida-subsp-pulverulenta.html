Leucanthemopsis pallida subsp. pulverulenta

Leucanthemopsis pallida subsp.

pulverulenta, commonly known as the pale leucanthemum, is a low-growing perennial herb native to South America. It features small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in daisy-like clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant has finely divided, gray-green leaves that are covered in a powdery coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a rockery plant.
