Licuala kuchingensis

Licuala kuchingensis, commonly known as the Kuching palm, is a species of palm native to Borneo.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter. The plant typically grows in moist, lowland forest areas and thrives in humid climates. Its trunk is slender and often covered with persistent leaf bases, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Licuala kuchingensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in tropical gardens.
