Lobivia acanthoplegma f. taratensis

Lobivia acanthoplegma f. taratensis, commonly known as the Taratensis Lobivia, is a rare and unique cactus native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in dense, white spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking contrast to its rugged appearance. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a delicately endangered species.
