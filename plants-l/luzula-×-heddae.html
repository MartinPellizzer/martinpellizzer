Luzula × heddae

Luzula × heddae, commonly known as the hybrid goat's beard, is a perennial plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is a hybrid between Luzula sylvatica and Luzula nivea, resulting in a plant with unique botanical characteristics. The species is known for its dense, tufted growth habit and its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are typically dark green and grass-like, while its flowers form spikes that bloom in late spring to early summer.

This plant is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
