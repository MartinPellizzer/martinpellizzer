Lilium papilliferum

Lilium papilliferum, commonly known as the papillate lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and red. The plant produces bulbous underground storage organs, which are essential for its survival and propagation. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem.

Lilium papilliferum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
