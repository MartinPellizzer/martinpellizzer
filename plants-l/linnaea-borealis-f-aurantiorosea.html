Linnaea borealis f. aurantiorosea

Linnaea borealis f. aurantiorosea, commonly known as the golden rose twinflower, is a rare and striking variant of the twinflower plant.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange-pink flowers, which are more intensely colored than those of the typical species. This plant is a deciduous shrub native to the boreal forests of North America and parts of Eurasia. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant blooms in early summer, attracting pollinators with its showy, fragrant flowers.
