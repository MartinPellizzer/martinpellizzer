Lobivia corbula

Lobivia corbula, commonly known as the coral lobivia, is a succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It features a compact, rounded body with thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces vibrant flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically during the warmer months. Its leaves are often covered in a fine layer of white powder, which helps reduce water loss.

Lobivia corbula is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low maintenance requirements.
