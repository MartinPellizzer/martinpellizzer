Lonicera hispida var. chaetocarpa

Lonicera hispida var. chaetocarpa, commonly known as the rough honeysuckle, is a shrub native to parts of East Asia.

It features slender, woody stems covered in stiff, bristly hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough, hairy surface.

The fruit is a small, red berry that ripens in late summer and is often consumed by birds and other wildlife.
