Lycopodium pallidum

Lycopodium pallidum, commonly known as the slender clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is a small, evergreen herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky areas. The plant features a creeping stem with whorled leaves that are narrow and grass-like in appearance. It produces small, cone-like structures called strobili that contain spores, which are dispersed by wind.

Lycopodium pallidum plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
