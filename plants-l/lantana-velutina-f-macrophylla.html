Lantana velutina f. macrophylla

Lantana velutina f. macrophylla, commonly known as the velvet lantana, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of small, colorful flowers. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
