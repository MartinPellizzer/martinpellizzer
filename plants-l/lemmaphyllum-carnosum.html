Lemmaphyllum carnosum

Lemmaphyllum carnosum, commonly known as the red-tipped photinia, is a shrub native to eastern Asia, particularly China.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and its striking red-tipped leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Due to its hardiness and adaptability, it is a popular choice for gardeners in temperate climates.
