Listera macrantha

Listera macrantha, commonly known as the large-flowered basketstar, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves and showy white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in well-drained, loamy soils. Its flowers are clustered in a raceme and bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and flies.

Listera macrantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local biodiversity.
