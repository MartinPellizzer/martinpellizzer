Lobivia pseudocariquinensis

Lobivia pseudocariquinensis, commonly known as the Pseudocariquinensis Lobivia, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and ribbed structure. The plant features spiny areoles and produces yellow flowers during the growing season. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water requirements.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
