Libanotis italica

Libanotis italica, commonly known as Italian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and typically grows in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in woodland edges and meadows, thriving in temperate climates.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and conservation efforts.
