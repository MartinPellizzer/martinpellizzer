Lepidium hyssopifolium f. intercedens

Lepidium hyssopifolium f. intercedens, commonly known as the interceding peppergrass, is a botanical variant of the species Lepidium hyssopifolium.

It is a low-growing, annual herbaceous plant native to the western United States, particularly found in arid and semi-arid regions. The plant features small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is adapted to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in dry grasslands and along roadsides.
