Litsea hurlimannii

It is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, particularly in countries like Vietnam and Laos. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, ovate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that appear in clusters. Its fruit is a small, globose drupe that turns red when mature.

Litsea hurlimannii is often found in moist, shaded habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
