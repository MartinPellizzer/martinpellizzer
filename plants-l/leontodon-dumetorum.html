Leontodon dumetorum

Leontodon dumetorum, commonly known as the mountain dandelion, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its basal leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and bloom in early summer.

Leontodon dumetorum is often found in meadows and open woodlands, where it contributes to the ecological diversity of these habitats.
