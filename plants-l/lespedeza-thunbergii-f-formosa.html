Lespedeza thunbergii f. formosa

Lespedeza thunbergii f. formosa, commonly known as the pinkwood or pink lespedeza, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, pink to purple flower clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. Its foliage is often a deep green, and it produces small, bean-like pods that contain seeds.

It is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive blooms and ecological benefits.
