Linodendron angustifolium

Linodendron angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf linodendron, is a species of flowering plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically arranged alternately along its stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow in color. Linodendron angustifolium thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest for both horticulturists and botanists.
