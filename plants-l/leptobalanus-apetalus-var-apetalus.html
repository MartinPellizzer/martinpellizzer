Leptobalanus apetalus var. apetalus

Leptobalanus apetalus var. apetalus, commonly known as the slender alder.

It is a shrub-like plant native to parts of Europe and Asia, characterized by its small, inconspicuous flowers and dense, dark green foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils, often in forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are simple and alternate, with a leathery texture, and it produces small, woody fruits called capsules.

The species is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insect species.
