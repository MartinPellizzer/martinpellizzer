Linum alterniflorum

Linum alterniflorum, commonly known as the alternate-flowered flax, is a flowering plant belonging to the Linaceae family.

It is native to the western United States, particularly California, and thrives in dry, rocky habitats. The plant is characterized by its alternate arrangement of leaves and its showy, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Linum alterniflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some historical uses in traditional medicine.
