Linaria arvensis var. typica

Linaria arvensis var. typica, commonly known as the common toadflax, is an annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers that resemble small toads, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its slender stems are often branched, and it produces small, lance-shaped leaves.

The flowers bloom from late spring to early autumn, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
