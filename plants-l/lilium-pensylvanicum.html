Lilium pensylvanicum

Lilium pensylvanicum, commonly known as the Pennsylvania tiger lily, is a species of flowering plant in the Liliaceae family.

It is native to the eastern United States, particularly in forested areas of Pennsylvania and surrounding regions. The plant features showy, orange-red flowers with a distinctive black blotch at the base of each petal. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a fan-like pattern, and it produces a bulbous underground stem.

This perennial plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in Native American cultures.
