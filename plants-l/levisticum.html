Levisticum

Levisticum, commonly known as parsley, is a biennial or perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like carrots and dill. The plant features finely divided, feather-like leaves that are used both as a culinary herb and a medicinal plant. Its flowers are small and white or greenish, arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family.

Levisticum is valued for its fresh, slightly peppery aroma and its use in cooking and traditional medicine.
