Lobelia gibbosa var. alba

Lobelia gibbosa var. alba, commonly known as the white lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a hairy texture.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
