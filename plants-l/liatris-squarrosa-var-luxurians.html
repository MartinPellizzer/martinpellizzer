Liatris squarrosa var. luxurians

Liatris squarrosa var. luxurians, commonly known as the showy blazingstar, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features tall, spiky flower heads composed of numerous purple to pinkish-purple tubular flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Its striking appearance and ecological benefits make it a valuable addition to pollinator-friendly landscapes.
