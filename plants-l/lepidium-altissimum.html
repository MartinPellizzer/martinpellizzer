Lepidium altissimum

Lepidium altissimum, commonly known as the giant wallflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are alternate and pinnately divided, giving it a delicate, fern-like appearance.

Lepidium altissimum is often found in moist, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental beauty.
