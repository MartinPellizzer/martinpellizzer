Leucanthemum ircutianum var. sylvestre

Leucanthemum ircutianum var. sylvestre, commonly known as the woodland oxeye daisy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a basal rosette of dark green, hairy leaves and produces showy white to pale yellow flowers with a central yellow disk. The plant typically grows in woodland edges, meadows, and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. It blooms from late spring to early autumn, adding ornamental value to gardens and natural landscapes.

The species is noted for its hardiness and ability to thrive in partial shade.
