Lopezia laciniata

Lopezia laciniata, commonly known as the fringed lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive, fern-like leaves. The plant produces upright spikes of purple to pink flowers, which bloom in the summer months. Lopezia laciniata is adapted to arid and rocky environments, often growing in open habitats such as hillsides and grasslands.
