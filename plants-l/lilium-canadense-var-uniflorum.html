Lilium canadense var. uniflorum

Lilium canadense var. uniflorum, commonly known as the Canadian white lily, is a variant of the Canadian lily native to North America.

It is characterized by its single, large, white to pale pink flower that blooms in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has narrow, grass-like leaves. Its bulbs are edible and have been traditionally used by Indigenous peoples.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in native plant communities.
