Lepidium miscellaneorum

Lepidium miscellaneorum, commonly known as the common cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, white or yellow flowers and pinnately compound leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along riverbanks. It produces small, rounded pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Lepidium miscellaneorum is an annual herb that is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
