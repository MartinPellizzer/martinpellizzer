Leucopogon fastigiatus

Leucopogon fastigiatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters, and bloom in spring.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.
