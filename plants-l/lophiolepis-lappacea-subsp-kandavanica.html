Lophiolepis lappacea subsp. kandavanica

Lophiolepis lappacea subsp.

kandavanica, commonly known as the Kandavan lichen-leaf plant, is a succulent shrub native to South Africa. It is characterized by its dense, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun conditions. It produces small, greenish-white flowers during the spring season.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in arid gardens.
