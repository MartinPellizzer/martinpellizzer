Lupinus olive-brownae

Lupinus olive-brownae, commonly known as the Olive-brown lupine, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and clusters of pea-like flowers. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are typically a deep green with a slight bluish tint. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are an important food source for various wildlife species.

Lupinus olive-brownae is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
