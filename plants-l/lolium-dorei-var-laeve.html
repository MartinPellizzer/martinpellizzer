Lolium dorei var. laeve

Lolium dorei var. laeve, commonly known as the smooth meadow fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically smooth and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, two-lobed flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along roadsides.

Lolium dorei var. laeve is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in various habitats.
