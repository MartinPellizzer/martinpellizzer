Ligeria

Ligeria, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its velvety, dark green leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, often forming a dense rosette of foliage. Its flowers come in a variety of colors, including purple, blue, and white, and are often used in ornamental gardening.

Ligeria thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
