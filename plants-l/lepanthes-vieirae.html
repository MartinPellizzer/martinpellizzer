Lepanthes vieirae

Lepanthes vieirae, commonly known as the Vieira's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern.

Lepanthes vieirae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
