Leichhardtia longiloba

Leichhardtia longiloba, commonly known as the long-stemmed leichhardtia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It features slender, upright stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, covered in fine, hairy foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming from late winter to early spring. Its leaves are alternate and linear, with a slightly rough texture, and the plant is often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Leichhardtia longiloba is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and resilience to poor soil conditions.
