Licuala rumphii

Licuala rumphii, commonly known as the giant swamp taro, is a large monocot native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, paddle-shaped leaves that can reach up to 2 meters in length. The plant thrives in wet, swampy environments and is often found in tropical rainforests. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense clumps.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
