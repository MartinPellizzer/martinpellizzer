Liatris bridgesii

Liatris bridgesii, commonly known as the Bridges' blazing star, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely leafed stem topped with a dense, showy inflorescence of purple to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and thrives in full sun, often found in prairies and open woodlands. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Liatris bridgesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native wildlife.
