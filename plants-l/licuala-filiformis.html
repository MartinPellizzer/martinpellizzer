Licuala filiformis

Licuala filiformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and as thatching material. It produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spadix, typical of the Araceae family.

Licuala filiformis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in humid, tropical environments.
