Loranthus brunneus f. sankuruensis

Loranthus brunneus f. sankuruensis, commonly known as the Sankuru mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily in the rainforest regions of Central Africa.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using light reflected from the host's leaves. The plant forms dense, shrub-like structures with small, leathery leaves and produces white or yellowish flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and wasps. Its seeds are dispersed by birds, which consume the fleshy, red berries and excrete the seeds onto suitable host trees.

This species plays an important ecological role in its habitat, contributing to the biodiversity of the forest canopy.
