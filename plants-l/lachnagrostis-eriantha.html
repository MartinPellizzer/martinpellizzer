Lachnagrostis eriantha

Lachnagrostis eriantha, commonly known as the silver hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-haired inflorescences that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

Lachnagrostis eriantha is often used in ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
