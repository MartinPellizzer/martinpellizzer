Lepanthes minutilabia

Lepanthes minutilabia, commonly known as the small-labied orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid with delicate, greenish-white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest understories, where it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. Its flowers have three sepals and three petals, with the labellum being particularly intricate and ornate.

Lepanthes minutilabia is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
