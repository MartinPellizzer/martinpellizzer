Lobelia cardinalis f. alba

Lobelia cardinalis f. alba, commonly known as White Cardinal Flower, is a striking variant of the Cardinal Flower species.

It is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist, acidic soils, often found in wetlands and along stream banks. The plant features showy, tubular white flowers that contrast sharply with its dark green foliage. It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
