Leucopogon verticillatus

Leucopogon verticillatus, commonly known as the clustered leucopogon, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often growing in whorls around the stems.

Leucopogon verticillatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
