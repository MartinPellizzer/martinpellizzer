Lathyrus sulphureus var. sulphureus

Lathyrus sulphureus var. sulphureus, commonly known as the sulphur flower vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves, which are typical of the Fabaceae family. The plant grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. It has a bushy growth habit and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and used for soil stabilization.
