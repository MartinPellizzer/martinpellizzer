Lagenocarpus tenuifolius var. riedelii

Lagenocarpus tenuifolius var. riedelii, commonly known as the Riedel's slenderleaf, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are often arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by capsule fruits containing minute seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
