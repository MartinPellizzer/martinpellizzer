Lilium × parkmanii

Lilium × parkmanii, commonly known as the Parkman lily, is a hybrid perennial plant belonging to the Liliaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, supporting large, showy flowers with six petals. The flowers are typically pink to red in color, with a distinctive white or pink throat, and bloom in late summer to early autumn. This plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively hardy in temperate climates.
