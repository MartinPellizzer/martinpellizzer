Laurus malabathrica

Laurus malabathrica, commonly known as Indian laurel or Malabar laurel, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like bay laurel. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a drupe, typically containing a single seed, and it is often used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.

The plant's aromatic oils and leaves contribute to its use in both culinary and medicinal applications.
