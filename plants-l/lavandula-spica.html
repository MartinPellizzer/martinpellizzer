Lavandula spica

Lavandula spica, commonly known as spike lavender, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky flower clusters that bloom in shades of purple and pink. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves and produces a fragrant essential oil widely used in aromatherapy and perfumery. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal remedies.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and economically valuable.
