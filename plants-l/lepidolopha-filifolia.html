Lepidolopha filifolia

Lepidolopha filifolia, commonly known as the threadleaf fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, linear fronds that are often covered in fine, hair-like structures. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forests or rocky crevices. Its rhizome is creeping and produces clusters of small, lance-shaped leaves.

Lepidolopha filifolia is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often used in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal.
