Leptochloa ciliata

Leptochloa ciliata, commonly known as the silky grass or slender feathergrass, is a species of grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves and slender, often tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, riverbanks, and swampy areas. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are small, cylindrical flower clusters.

The botanical features of Leptochloa ciliata make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
