Litsea johorensis

Litsea johorensis, commonly known as the Bornean litsea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by oval, woody berries that contain a single seed. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill forest ecosystems.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
