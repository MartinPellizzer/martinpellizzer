Lagochilus botschantzevii

Lagochilus botschantzevii, commonly known as the Botchanzev's rock rose, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
