Lepigonum mollugineum

Lepigonum mollugineum, commonly known as the pinkroot, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has a distinctive tuberous root system, which is often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and naturalized areas.
