Lupinus albifrons var. eminens

Lupinus albifrons var. eminens, commonly known as the Sierra lupine, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of white to pale blue flowers. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or open habitats.

Lupinus albifrons var. eminens is a member of the legume family and has the ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
