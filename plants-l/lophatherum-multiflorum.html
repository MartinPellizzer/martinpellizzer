Lophatherum multiflorum

Lophatherum multiflorum, commonly known as the Chinese silver grass, is a perennial herb native to Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and fluffy, silver-white seed heads. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its distinctive appearance and ability to thrive in various soil conditions. It prefers full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

Lophatherum multiflorum is also used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
