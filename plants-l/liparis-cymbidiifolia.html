Liparis cymbidiifolia

Liparis cymbidiifolia, commonly known as the orchid orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its sympodial growth habit and the presence of pseudobulbs. The plant produces a single, often showy flower per stem, typically in shades of white, yellow, or green. Its leaves are usually strap-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern.

Liparis cymbidiifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
