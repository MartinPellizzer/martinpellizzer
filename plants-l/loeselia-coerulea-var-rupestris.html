Loeselia coerulea var. rupestris

Loeselia coerulea var. rupestris, commonly known as the blue rock orchid, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows on rocky outcrops and is adapted to arid conditions. Its leaves are narrow and fleshy, which helps it retain moisture in its harsh environment.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological significance.
