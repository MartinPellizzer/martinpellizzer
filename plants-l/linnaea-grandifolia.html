Linnaea grandifolia

Linnaea grandifolia, commonly known as the large-flowered twinflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the temperate regions of North America.

It features opposite, elliptic leaves with a smooth margin and a glossy, dark green upper surface. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. Its flowers are borne on slender, arching stems and are pollinated primarily by bees.

Linnaea grandifolia is often found in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in well-drained, loamy soils.
