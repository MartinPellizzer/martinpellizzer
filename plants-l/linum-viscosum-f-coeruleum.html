Linum viscosum f. coeruleum

Linum viscosum f. coeruleum, commonly known as blue flax, is a cultivar of the flax plant characterized by its striking blue flowers.

It is a herbaceous annual plant native to Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. The plant features slender, erect stems covered in sticky hairs, which give it its species name *viscosum*. Its flowers are typically borne in racemes and range in color from pale blue to deep cobalt.

This variety is well-suited for gardens and can thrive in full sun with well-drained soil.
