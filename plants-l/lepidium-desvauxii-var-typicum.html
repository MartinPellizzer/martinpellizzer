Lepidium desvauxii var. typicum

Lepidium desvauxii var. typicum, commonly known as the desert cress, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is a low-growing annual herb with small, green, edible leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has a compact structure and thrives in sandy or gravelly soils, often found in desert or semi-desert environments. Its seeds are small and can remain viable in the soil for several years, allowing it to persist in harsh conditions.

The species is characterized by its ability to survive in extreme drought conditions, making it an important component of desert ecosystems.
