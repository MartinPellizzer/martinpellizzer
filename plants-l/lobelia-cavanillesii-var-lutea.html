Lobelia cavanillesii var. lutea

Lobelia cavanillesii var. lutea, commonly known as the yellow lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It features striking yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
