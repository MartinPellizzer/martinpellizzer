Lysiana spathulata

Lysiana spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leaved lily, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves and showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and prefers rich, organic soil. Its foliage and flowers are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Lysiana spathulata is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
