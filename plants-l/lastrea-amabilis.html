Lastrea amabilis

Lastrea amabilis, commonly known as the silver leaf shrub, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage that reflects sunlight and reduces water loss. The plant produces small, white flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

The leaves are succulent, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions with minimal water.
