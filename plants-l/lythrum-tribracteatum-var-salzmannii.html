Lythrum tribracteatum var. salzmannii

Lythrum tribracteatum var. salzmannii, commonly known as Salzmann's lythrum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Lythraceae family, which includes plants known for their showy flowers and ecological importance. The plant features upright stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are typically pink to purple, arranged in dense spikes, and bloom during the summer months.

This species is often found in wetland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and role in supporting pollinator populations.
