Lomaridium dendrophilum

Lomaridium dendrophilum, commonly known as the tree-loving lichen, is a foliose lichen that thrives on tree trunks and branches.

It is characterized by its leaf-like thallus, which is typically greenish-gray in color. This lichen is often found in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and woodlands. Its structure consists of a symbiotic relationship between a photosynthetic alga and a fungal partner.

Lomaridium dendrophilum plays an important role in nutrient cycling and serves as an indicator of air quality in its habitat.
