Laelia superbiens var. quesneliana

Laelia superbiens var. quesneliana, commonly known as the Quesnel's laelia, is a hybrid orchid native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and produces large, showy flowers with vibrant colors ranging from yellow to orange. The plant typically blooms in late winter to early spring, making it a popular choice for orchid enthusiasts. Its foliage consists of long, narrow leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements, making it suitable for both novice and experienced growers.
