Loxocarpus caulescens

Loxocarpus caulescens, commonly known as the slender tree fern, is a member of the Cyatheaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery fronds. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to anchor itself in moist, shaded environments. Its fronds are typically bioluminescent, emitting a faint glow in the dark, which aids in attracting nocturnal pollinators.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
