Lavera

Lavera, commonly known as the common lavender, is a flowering plant in the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant produces spikes of purple flowers, which are used in aromatherapy and essential oils. Lavera is also valued for its medicinal properties, including its calming and antiseptic effects.

Its aromatic foliage and blooms make it a popular choice for gardens and herbal remedies.
