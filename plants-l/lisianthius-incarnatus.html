Lisianthius incarnatus

Lisianthius incarnatus, commonly known as the red lisianthius, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking red or pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and prefers partial to full shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

It is a perennial herb that can reach heights of up to 3 feet, making it a notable addition to native plant gardens.
