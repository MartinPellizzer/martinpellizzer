Lycium confusum

Lycium confusum, commonly known as the Chinese boxthorn, is a shrub native to China and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces red berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. Lycium confusum is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.

It has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
