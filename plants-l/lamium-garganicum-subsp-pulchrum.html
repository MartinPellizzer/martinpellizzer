Lamium garganicum subsp. pulchrum

Lamium garganicum subsp.

pulchrum, commonly known as the purple deadnettle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe. It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs and have a distinctive purple underside.

The plant produces small, two-lipped flowers in shades of purple and white, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
