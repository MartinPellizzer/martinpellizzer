Lychnis fulgens var. wilfordii

Lychnis fulgens var. wilfordii, commonly known as the Wilford's fireweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, bright red to orange-pink flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while the stems are usually hairy and somewhat woody at the base. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils, making it a common sight in disturbed habitats.

This variety is notable for its vibrant floral display and is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value.
