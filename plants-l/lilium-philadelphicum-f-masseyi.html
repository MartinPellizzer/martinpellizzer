Lilium philadelphicum f. masseyi

Lilium philadelphicum f. masseyi, commonly known as the Massey's lily, is a botanical variant of the showy lady's slipper lily.

It is characterized by its striking pink to red flowers with a distinctive white throat and a subtle fragrance. This perennial plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and is native to the eastern United States. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that form a basal rosette.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
