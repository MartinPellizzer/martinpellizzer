Ladenbergia riveroana

Ladenbergia riveroana, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters during the wet season. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it has compound leaves with trifoliate or pinnate structures. Its root system is robust, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun.

The tree can grow up to 10 meters tall, making it a prominent feature in its natural habitat.
