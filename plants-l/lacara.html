Lacara

Lacara, commonly known as the "silver leaf" plant, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Acanthaceae.

It is characterized by its striking silver or gray-green foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to violet, often attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. Native to regions of South America, it thrives in warm, humid environments.

Lacara is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
