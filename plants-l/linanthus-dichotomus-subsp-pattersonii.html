Linanthus dichotomus subsp. pattersonii

Linanthus dichotomus subsp.

pattersonii, commonly known as Patterson's two-branched flax, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States. It is a member of the Linaceae family, characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, linear leaves.

The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
