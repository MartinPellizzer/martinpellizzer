Linaria pendula

Linaria pendula, commonly known as the weeping toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of yellow-orange, spurred flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, hairy stems that often hang downward, giving it a cascading appearance. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive look.

Linaria pendula is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
