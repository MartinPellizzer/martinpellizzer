Lessingianthus souzae

Lessingianthus souzae, commonly known as the Brazilian sunflower, is a species of flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and daisy-like appearance. The plant features upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its vibrant yellow flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
