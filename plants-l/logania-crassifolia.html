Logania crassifolia

Logania crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved logania, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Loganiaceae and is characterized by its large, waxy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces striking, yellow-orange flowers with prominent red stamens, which are often clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
