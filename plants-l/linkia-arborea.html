Linkia arborea

Linkia arborea, commonly known as the white mangrove, is a small evergreen tree native to coastal regions of the Indo-Pacific.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and elliptical leaves that are typically covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by round, fleshy fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in saline and brackish water environments, making it an important species in mangrove ecosystems.

Linkia arborea plays a crucial role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial organisms.
