Lavandula gibsonii

Lavandula gibsonii, commonly known as Gibson's lavender, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems covered in gray-green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces spikes of fragrant, purple-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and aromatic properties, it is often cultivated for both garden use and essential oil production.
