Limeum fenestratum var. frutescens

Limeum fenestratum var. frutescens, commonly known as the fragrant fenestrated lime, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It features slender, aromatic stems that often branch near the base, forming a dense, low-growing mat. The leaves are lance-shaped, with a distinctive fenestrated (fenestrated) pattern, giving the plant its scientific name. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are often borne on slender pedicels.

This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
