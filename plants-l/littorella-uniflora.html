Littorella uniflora

Littorella uniflora, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the northern hemisphere.

It thrives in salt marshes and brackish environments, showcasing its adaptability to saline conditions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or yellow flowers in clusters. Its root system helps stabilize coastal soils, preventing erosion.

Littorella uniflora plays an important ecological role in intertidal zones by providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial organisms.
