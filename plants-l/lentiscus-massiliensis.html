Lentiscus massiliensis

Lentiscus massiliensis, commonly known as the Mediterranean mastic tree, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by dark, fleshy berries. The plant is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas. It has a slow growth rate and can live for several decades.

The tree is also known for its aromatic resin, which has been historically used for various medicinal and practical purposes.
