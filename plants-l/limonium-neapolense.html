Limonium neapolense

Limonium neapolense, commonly known as the Naples sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, cylindrical seed pods that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal areas and dry meadows.

Limonium neapolense is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate coastal conditions.
