Libocedrus papuana subsp. arfakensis

Libocedrus papuana subsp.

arfakensis, commonly known as the Arfak cedar, is a coniferous tree native to the highlands of West Papua. It is characterized by its slender, evergreen foliage and small, scale-like leaves.

The tree produces cones that are typically clustered at the base of the branches. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in montane forests.

Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in local construction and crafts.
