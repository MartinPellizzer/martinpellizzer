Laportea thorelii

Laportea thorelii, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Mexican velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, tactile texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Laportea thorelii is also known for its medicinal properties, with various traditional uses in herbal remedies.
