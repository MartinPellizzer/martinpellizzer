Leucothoe laxiflora var. subcordata

Leucothoe laxiflora var. subcordata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved leatherleaf, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features elliptical, dark green leaves that are often glossy and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded forest understories.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and adaptability to humid, woodland environments.
