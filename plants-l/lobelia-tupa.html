Lobelia tupa

Lobelia tupa, commonly known as the sacred lotus of the Andes, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers and is often found growing in moist, shaded environments. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like hummingbirds. It is valued for its ornamental beauty and has cultural significance in some indigenous communities.

Lobelia tupa is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
