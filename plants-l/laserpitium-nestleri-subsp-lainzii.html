Laserpitium nestleri subsp. lainzii

Laserpitium nestleri subsp.

lainzii, commonly known as the Lainz's hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky slopes, thriving in cool and temperate climates. Its leaves are basal, linear, and often hairy, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disc.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
