Lupinus leucophyllus subsp. erectus

Lupinus leucophyllus subsp.

erectus, commonly known as the silverleaf lupine, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its erect growth habit and silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name.

The plant produces tall, sparsely branched stems with clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets. Lupinus leucophyllus subsp.

erectus is adapted to dry, well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.
