Lactuca ambigua

Lactuca ambigua, commonly known as the common groundsel, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa, and it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant features a basal rosette of leaves and produces yellow flowers in composite heads. Its latex is milky and can be irritating to the skin.

It is often considered a weed but has historical uses in traditional medicine.
