Listera deltoidea

Listera deltoidea, commonly known as the heartleaf basketflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in rich, well-drained soils. Its leaves are heart-shaped, and the flowers are borne in clusters at the base of the plant.

Listera deltoidea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
