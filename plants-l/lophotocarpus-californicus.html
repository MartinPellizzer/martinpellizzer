Lophotocarpus californicus

Lophotocarpus californicus, commonly known as the California lichen, is a unique plant that belongs to the Lichens group, which are symbiotic organisms composed of fungi and algae.

It is characterized by its crust-like growth form, typically found on rock surfaces or tree bark in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant lacks true roots, stems, and leaves, relying instead on its algal partner for photosynthesis and its fungal component for nutrient absorption. Lophotocarpus californicus is well adapted to harsh conditions, making it a common species in California's coastal and mountainous regions.

Its presence is often an indicator of specific environmental conditions and can be used in ecological studies to assess habitat health.
