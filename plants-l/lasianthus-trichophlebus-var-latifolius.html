Lasianthus trichophlebus var. latifolius

Lasianthus trichophlebus var. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are broad and glossy, and it produces vibrant, often purple or red blooms.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in tropical climates.
