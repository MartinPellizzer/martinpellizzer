Larix sibirica var. hybrida

Larix sibirica var. hybrida, commonly known as the Siberian larch hybrid, is a deciduous conifer native to the cold climates of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its needle-like leaves that turn golden in autumn before shedding, and its distinctive cone-like structures that mature in the second year. The tree typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for harsh, northern environments. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness.

This variety is known for its adaptability and resilience in cold, windy conditions.
