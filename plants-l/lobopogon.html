Lobopogon

Lobopogon, commonly known as the tussock grass, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and often forms dense, mat-like clumps. The plants typically have narrow, blade-like leaves and produce small, spike-like inflorescences. Lobopogon species are native to various regions of Australia and are adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

They play an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
