Laurus porrecta

Laurus porrecta, commonly known as the broadleaf laurel, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other well-known plants like laurel and avocado. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are elongated and often have a slight waxy coating. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark berries, which are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Laurus porrecta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping due to its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of conditions.
