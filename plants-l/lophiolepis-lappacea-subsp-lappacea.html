Lophiolepis lappacea subsp. lappacea

Lophiolepis lappacea subsp.

lappacea, commonly known as the Cape sugarbush, is a shrub native to South Africa. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, winged fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in horticulture.
