Laurus quixos

Laurus quixos, commonly known as the Quixote laurel, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and aromatic leaves that emit a strong, spicy scent. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible but often considered astringent. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its hardy nature, it is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
