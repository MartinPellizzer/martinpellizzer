Leptorhynchos suaveolens

Leptorhynchos suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, succulent structure adapted to survive in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are often visited by pollinators such as bees and flies. Its leaves are typically fleshy and covered in a fine layer of hair, helping to reduce water loss.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscapic gardens.
