Limnanthemum exaltatum

Limnanthemum exaltatum, commonly known as the tall water lily, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, showy white petals that open in the early morning and close by midday. The plant typically grows in shallow freshwater habitats such as ponds and slow-moving streams. Its leaves are broad and floating, providing shade and habitat for aquatic life.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland environments.
