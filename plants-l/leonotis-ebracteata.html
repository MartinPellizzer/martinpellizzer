Leonotis ebracteata

Leonotis ebracteata, commonly known as the firecracker plant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its striking tubular red or orange flowers that resemble firecrackers. The plant is typically shrub-like, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Leonotis ebracteata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
