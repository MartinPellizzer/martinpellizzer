Lachemilla kieftiana

Lachemilla kieftiana, commonly known as Kieft's sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its mat-forming growth habit and dense clusters of small, white flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its foliage is typically covered in a fine layer of silky hairs, which helps reduce water loss.

Lachemilla kieftiana is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
