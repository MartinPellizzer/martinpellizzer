Leptochilus naumannii

Leptochilus naumannii, commonly known as the Naumann's leathery bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is characterized by its fleshy, leathery leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a slight purple hue. It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops, where it thrives in full sun.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat preferences, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
