Lythrum micranthum

Lythrum micranthum, commonly known as narrow-leaved lythrum, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lythraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Lythrum micranthum is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
