Lycium schaffneri

Lycium schaffneri, commonly known as Schaffner's boxthorn, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible for humans and animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Lycium schaffneri is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in its native environment.
