Leea wrightii

Leea wrightii, commonly known as Wright's leea, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Leeaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by small, round berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
