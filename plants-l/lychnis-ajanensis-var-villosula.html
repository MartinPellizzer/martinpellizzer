Lychnis ajanensis var. villosula

Lychnis ajanensis var. villosula, commonly known as the hairy lychnis, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, particularly China.

It belongs to the carnation family, Caryophyllaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, pink to red flowers. The leaves are typically hairy, giving the plant its common name, and they are arranged alternately along the stem. This variety is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and rocky slopes.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
