Lilium philadelphicum f. flaviflorum

Lilium philadelphicum f. flaviflorum, commonly known as the yellow-flowered fritillary, is a botanical variant of the showy lady's slipper.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers, which contrast with its green foliage. This perennial plant is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist, woodland habitats. The flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique coloration and its role in ecological systems.
