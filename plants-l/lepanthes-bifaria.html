Lepanthes bifaria

Lepanthes bifaria, commonly known as the twin-lobed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that often display a range of colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as cloud forests, where it thrives on tree trunks or among leaf litter. Its flowers are notable for their intricate structure and are often pollinated by specific insects or small birds.

Lepanthes bifaria is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique floral display.
