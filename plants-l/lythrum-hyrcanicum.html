Lythrum hyrcanicum

Lythrum hyrcanicum, commonly known as the Hyrcanian lythrum, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia and parts of the Middle East.

It belongs to the Lythraceae family, which includes other species such as the common lythrum. The plant is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of pink or red flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Lythrum hyrcanicum is typically found in moist, well-drained soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including wetlands and grasslands.
