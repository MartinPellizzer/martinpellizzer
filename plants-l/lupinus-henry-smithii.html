Lupinus henry-smithii

Lupinus henry-smithii, commonly known as Henry-Smith's lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and clusters of pea-like flowers. The plant typically produces bright blue or purple flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats like grasslands and rocky slopes.

Lupinus henry-smithii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
