Lonicera brachypoda reticulata

Lonicera brachypoda reticulata, commonly known as the reticulated honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with a distinctive reticulate venation pattern. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, appearing in clusters. Its berries are small, red, and edible, often consumed by birds.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or stream banks.
