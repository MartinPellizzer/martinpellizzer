Loranthus mabioides

Loranthus mabioides, commonly known as the Mabai's mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using the light it captures. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces white or yellow flowers that are often visited by insects for pollination. Its berries, which contain seeds, are dispersed by birds that eat the fruit.

This species plays an important ecological role in its habitat by influencing host tree growth and providing food for wildlife.
