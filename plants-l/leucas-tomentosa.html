Leucas tomentosa

Leucas tomentosa, commonly known as hairy leucas, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or white flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and traditional uses.
