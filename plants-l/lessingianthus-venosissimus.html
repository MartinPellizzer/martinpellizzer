Lessingianthus venosissimus

Lessingianthus venosissimus, commonly known as the veiny lessingia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety blue to purple petals. The plant produces small, yellow disk flowers surrounded by ray florets, which contribute to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The species is noted for its unique vein patterns on the leaves, which give it its common name.
