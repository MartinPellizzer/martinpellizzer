Lepidium caespitosum

Lepidium caespitosum, commonly known as wild cress, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and has small, clustered leaves. It produces tiny white or yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

Its seeds are edible and have a peppery flavor, similar to mustard.
