Lavauxia hamata

Lavauxia hamata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sandwort, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub with succulent, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in harsh environments. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the cooler months. Its root system is adapted to stabilize loose, sandy soils, making it a common pioneer species in disturbed areas.

Lavauxia hamata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
