Lauro-cerasus lusitanica

Lauro-cerasus lusitanica, commonly known as the Portuguese cherry or cherry laurel, is a species of flowering shrub or small tree native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Prunus genus and is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red, cherry-like fruits that are edible but often bitter.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine.
