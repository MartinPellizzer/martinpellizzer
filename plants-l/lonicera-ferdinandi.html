Lonicera ferdinandi

Lonicera ferdinandi, commonly known as the Chinese honeysuckle, is a deciduous climbing vine native to China.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces fragrant, tubular flowers that range from yellow to orange in color. The plant blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruits are red berries that ripen in late summer, containing seeds that are dispersed by birds.

Lonicera ferdinandi is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
