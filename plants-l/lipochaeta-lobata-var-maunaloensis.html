Lipochaeta lobata var. maunaloensis

Lipochaeta lobata var. maunaloensis, commonly known as the Maunaloa lipochaeta, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and typically hairy foliage. The plant is shrub-like, with slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. It produces yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, often appearing in late summer or early fall.

This variety is adapted to the moist, shaded conditions of Hawaiian rainforests.
