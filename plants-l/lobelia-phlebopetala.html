Lobelia phlebopetala

Lobelia phlebopetala, commonly known as the redstem lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, reddish stems and small, tubular blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Lobelia phlebopetala is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
