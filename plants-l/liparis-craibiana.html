Liparis craibiana

Liparis craibiana, commonly known as the Craib's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in summer. The plant produces inflorescences with multiple flowers, each having three sepals and three petals, with the latter often being more prominent. It prefers humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
