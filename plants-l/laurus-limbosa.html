Laurus limbosa

Laurus limbosa, commonly known as the narrow-leafed laurel, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other well-known plants like bay laurel and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves that are typically narrow and lance-shaped. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by spherical, blue-black berries.

Laurus limbosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
