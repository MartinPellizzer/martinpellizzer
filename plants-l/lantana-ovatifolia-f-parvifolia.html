Lantana ovatifolia f. parvifolia

Lantana ovatifolia f. parvifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf lantana, is a compact shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and orange, depending on the variety. The leaves are narrow and ovate, with a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinct appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental gardening.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
