Lonchocarpus calderonii

Lonchocarpus calderonii, commonly known as the Mexican climbing bean, is a leguminous plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets. The plant produces striking yellow flowers followed by distinctive, elongated pods containing kidney-shaped seeds. It belongs to the genus Lonchocarpus, which includes several species with similar morphological features.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
