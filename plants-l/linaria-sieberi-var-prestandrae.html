Linaria sieberi var. prestandrae

Linaria sieberi var. prestandrae, commonly known as the Prestandrae toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and it typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils. It is a perennial species that thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
