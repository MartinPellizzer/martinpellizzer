Linaria virgata subsp. syrtica

Linaria virgata subsp.

syrtica, commonly known as the desert snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low rainfall. Its flowering period typically occurs during the cooler months, making it a notable species in its native habitat. Linaria virgata subsp.

syrtica is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
