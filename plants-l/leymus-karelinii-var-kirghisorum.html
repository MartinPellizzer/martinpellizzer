Leymus karelinii var. kirghisorum

Leymus karelinii var. kirghisorum, commonly known as the Kirghiz meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and steppe environments. The plant has slender, wiry stems and produces inflorescences that are usually spike-like or panicle-shaped. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a forage plant.
