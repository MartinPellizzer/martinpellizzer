Linaria villosa var. oppositiflora

Linaria villosa var. oppositiflora, commonly known as the hairy toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and opposite leaves that are covered in fine, hairy glands. The flowers are typically yellow or orange, with two lips, and are arranged in racemes along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

This plant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
