Leea cumingii

Leea cumingii, commonly known as the Cuming's leeah, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Leeaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by small, fleshy fruits that are often red or orange when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
