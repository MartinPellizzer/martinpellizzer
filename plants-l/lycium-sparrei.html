Lycium sparrei

Lycium sparrei, commonly known as the coastal lycium, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It features small, yellowish-orange berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. The plant has small, narrow leaves and produces clusters of pale yellow flowers in the spring. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils along coastal areas and is adapted to salt spray.

Lycium sparrei plays an important role in coastal ecosystems by providing food and habitat for various species.
