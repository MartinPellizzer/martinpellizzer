Liparis nephrocardia

Liparis nephrocardia, commonly known as the kidney-heart orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive kidney-shaped or heart-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has a rhizomatous root system. Its leaves are usually linear and grass-like, while the flowers are greenish-yellow with a faintly colored lip.

Liparis nephrocardia is a rare and endangered species, requiring specific environmental conditions for successful growth.
