Leptorkis viridiflora

Leptorkis viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces striking, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil efficiently.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
