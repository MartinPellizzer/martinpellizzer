Lycium berlandieri subvar. wrightii

Lycium berlandieri subvar. wrightii, commonly known as the Sonoran Desert nightshade, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and red, fleshy berries that ripen in the fall. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils and desert plains. Its leaves are typically narrow and gray-green, with a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

The species is valued for its ecological role, providing food for wildlife and supporting local biodiversity.
