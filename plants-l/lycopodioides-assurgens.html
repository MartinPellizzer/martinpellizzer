Lycopodioides assurgens

Lycopodioides assurgens, commonly known as the climbing clubmoss, is a species of lycophyte belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its creeping, branched stems that form dense mats, often growing in shaded, moist environments. The plant produces small, spirally arranged leaves and spore-bearing cones called strobili. Its reproductive structures are typically found at the tips of the branches, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil in forest ecosystems.
