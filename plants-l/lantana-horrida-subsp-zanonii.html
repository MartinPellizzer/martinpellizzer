Lantana horrida subsp. zanonii

Lantana horrida subsp.

zanonii, commonly known as the Zanonii lantana, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, often spiny foliage and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange.

The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically red or orange when mature. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it adaptable to various arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
