Leptorkis beccarii

Leptorkis beccarii, commonly known as the Beccari's orchid, is a species of flowering plant in the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and large, showy flowers that are typically greenish-yellow with intricate patterns. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
