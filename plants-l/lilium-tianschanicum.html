Lilium tianschanicum

Lilium tianschanicum, commonly known as the Tianshan lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Tianshan mountain range in Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and red. The plant produces bulbous rhizomes and has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
