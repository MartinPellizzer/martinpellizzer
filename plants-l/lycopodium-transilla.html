Lycopodium transilla

Lycopodium transilla, commonly known as the Transilian clubmoss, is a species of lycophyte belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant has a distinctive stem that is often branched and covered in tiny, scale-like leaves. Its spore-producing structures, called strobila, are clustered at the tips of the branches.

Lycopodium transilla plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
