Lobivia chrysantha var. rubescens

Lobivia chrysantha var. rubescens, commonly known as the Ruby Lobivia, is a succulent plant native to the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It features compact, rounded rosettes of fleshy, blue-gray leaves that turn reddish-pink at the edges when exposed to sunlight. The plant produces tall, branched stems that bear clusters of yellow flowers during the spring and early summer. It is drought-tolerant and requires well-draining soil to thrive.

Due to its striking appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
