Lysimachia atropurpurea var. thessala

Lysimachia atropurpurea var. thessala, commonly known as the Greek violet, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Europe.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, violet or purple flowers. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is often found in moist, shaded environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in rock gardens and as a ground cover.

The species is also known for its ability to thrive in woodland settings.
