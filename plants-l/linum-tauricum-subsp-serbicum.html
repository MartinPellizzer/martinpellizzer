Linum tauricum subsp. serbicum

Linum tauricum subsp.

serbicum, commonly known as Serbian flax, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically arranged in racemes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in fiber production, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
