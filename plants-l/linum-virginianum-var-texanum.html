Linum virginianum var. texanum

Linum virginianum var. texanum, commonly known as Texas flax, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual with slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Its fibrous stems and seeds have been historically used for textile and food purposes.
