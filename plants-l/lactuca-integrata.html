Lactuca integrata

Lactuca integrata, commonly known as the common lettuce, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family.

It is characterized by its rosette of broad, dark green leaves that are often used in salads. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in a single head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been introduced to other regions.

Lactuca integrata is known for its mild taste and is cultivated for both culinary and ornamental purposes.
