Liparis inconspicua

Liparis inconspicua, commonly known as the inconspicuous orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that are not particularly showy. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique root system, which often forms a network of tubers.

Liparis inconspicua is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable conditions, making it a popular choice for enthusiasts of less conventional orchids.
