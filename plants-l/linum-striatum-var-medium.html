Linum striatum var. medium

Linum striatum var. medium, commonly known as the broadleaf flax, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in late spring to early summer, which are borne in loose clusters. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
