Lupinus perconfertus

Lupinus perconfertus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The plant produces clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its compound leaves are pinnately arranged with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Lupinus perconfertus is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.
