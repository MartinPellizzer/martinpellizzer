Lavandula multifida f. albiflora

Lavandula multifida f. albiflora, commonly known as the silverleaf lavender, is a cultivar of the lavender plant characterized by its distinctive silvery-gray foliage.

It produces clusters of small, pale white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and mild aromatic properties.

This variety is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators.
