Lonchocarpus goossensii

Lonchocarpus goossensii, commonly known as the yellow velvet tamarind, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces distinctive, inflated seed pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

It is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in soil enrichment and agroforestry systems.
