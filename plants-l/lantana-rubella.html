Lantana rubella

Lantana rubella, commonly known as red lantana, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Lantana rubella is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
