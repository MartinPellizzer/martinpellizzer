Lupinus orcuttii

Lupinus orcuttii, commonly known as the Orcutt's lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, such as grasslands and open woodlands. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Lupinus orcuttii plays an important role in soil stabilization and supports local pollinator populations.
