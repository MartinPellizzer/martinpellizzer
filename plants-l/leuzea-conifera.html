Leuzea conifera

Leuzea conifera, commonly known as the coniferous leuzea, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, rocky soils and tundra environments. The plant has a rosette of basal leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer. Its leaves are characterized by their narrow, conifer-like shape, which gives it its common name.

Leuzea conifera is known for its adaptability to harsh climates and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
