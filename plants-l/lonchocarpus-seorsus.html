Lonchocarpus seorsus

Lonchocarpus seorsus, commonly known as the yellow flame tree, is a tropical legume native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers and compound leaves, which are typical of the Fabaceae family. The plant produces distinctive, woody pods that contain its seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and valuable for ornamental purposes.
