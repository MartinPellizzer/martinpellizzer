Larix laricina

Larix laricina, commonly known as the American larch, is a deciduous conifer native to North America.

It is characterized by its needle-like leaves that turn golden yellow in the fall before shedding. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, typical of conifers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in boreal and subalpine regions.

Its rapid growth and ability to tolerate various environmental conditions make it an important species in forest ecosystems.
