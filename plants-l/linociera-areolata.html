Linociera areolata

Linociera areolata, commonly known as the spotted lichen, is a species of lichen that exhibits unique botanical characteristics.

It is a composite organism formed by a fungal partner and a photosynthetic alga, which together form a symbiotic relationship. The plant is typically found growing on rocks, bark, or soil in moist, shaded environments. Its appearance is marked by distinct, rounded, and often colorful spots on its thallus.

Linociera areolata plays an important ecological role in nutrient cycling and serves as an indicator species for environmental conditions.
