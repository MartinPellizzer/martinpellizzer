Leucas concinna

Leucas concinna, commonly known as small-leaved leucas, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including India and China.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs, and it is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Leucas concinna is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
