Lobivia schreiteri var. riolarensis

Lobivia schreiteri var. riolarensis, commonly known as the Riolarensis Lobivia, is a compact, low-growing cactus native to the arid regions of Argentina.

It features a thick, ribbed stem that is typically green to bluish-green in color, with small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has a shallow root system adapted to drought conditions, allowing it to thrive in well-draining soils. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it produces small, rounded tubercles that give it a distinctive appearance.

This variety is prized by cactus enthusiasts for its hardiness and ornamental value in succulent gardens.
