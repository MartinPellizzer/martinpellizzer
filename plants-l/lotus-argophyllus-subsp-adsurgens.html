Lotus argophyllus subsp. adsurgens

Lotus argophyllus subsp.

adsurgens, commonly known as the climbing lotus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern China. It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems that often twine around other vegetation.

The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered with a fine layer of white hair.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
