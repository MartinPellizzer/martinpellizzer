Lobivia shaferi subsp. fallax

Lobivia shaferi subsp.

fallax, commonly known as the Shafers' lobivia, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America. It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems and spiny, paddle-shaped leaves.

The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It is well adapted to drought conditions, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts and collectors.
