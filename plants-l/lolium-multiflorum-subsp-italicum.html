Lolium multiflorum subsp. italicum

Lolium multiflorum subsp.

italicum, commonly known as Italian ryegrass, is a cool-season grass native to Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its fine, tufted leaves and dense, spike-like inflorescences that produce small, two-lobed seeds.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in forage and turfgrass applications due to its rapid growth and high nutritional value. It is also a common weed in agricultural fields and can compete aggressively with crops.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to various climates and soil conditions.
