Leucocodon reticulatum

Leucocodon reticulatum, commonly known as the net-veined everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its delicate, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind, aiding in its propagation. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
