Lycium floridum

Lycium floridum, commonly known as the Florida lily or evergreen lily, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and are typically borne on slender stems. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. It produces red or orange berries that are edible but often contain seeds.

Lycium floridum is adapted to wetland environments and is often found in marshes and swamps.
