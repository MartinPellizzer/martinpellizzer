Leontodon saxatilis subsp. perennis

Leontodon saxatilis subsp.

perennis, commonly known as the perennial wallflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces yellow to orange composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often found in alpine or subalpine regions. Its leaves are linear to lanceolate, with a rough texture, and it is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

This subspecies is distinguished by its longer flowering stems and more robust growth compared to other members of the species.
