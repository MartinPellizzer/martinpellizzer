Lysimachia petelotii

Lysimachia petelotii, commonly known as the Petelot's chickweed, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a low-growing herb with delicate, hairy stems and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, rounded leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Lysimachia petelotii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
