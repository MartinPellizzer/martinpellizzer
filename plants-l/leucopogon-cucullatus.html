Leucopogon cucullatus

Leucopogon cucullatus, commonly known as the broad-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils in forest understories or open woodlands. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and often have a distinctive whitish or silver-colored underside.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
