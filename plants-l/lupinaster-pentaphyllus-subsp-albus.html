Lupinaster pentaphyllus subsp. albus

Lupinaster pentaphyllus subsp.

albus, commonly known as the white-flowered lupinaster, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern South America. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and its stems are often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or alpine gardens. Lupinaster pentaphyllus subsp.

albus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
