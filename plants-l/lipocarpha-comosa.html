Lipocarpha comosa

Lipocarpha comosa, commonly known as the hairy bulrush, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescence composed of numerous small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant thrives in brackish or saline water, making it a common sight in coastal marshes and estuaries. Its leaves are narrow and often covered with fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its saline environment.

Lipocarpha comosa plays an important ecological role by stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
