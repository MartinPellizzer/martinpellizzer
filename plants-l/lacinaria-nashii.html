Lacinaria nashii

Lacinaria nashii, commonly known as the Nash's lacinaria, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its unique, compound leaves that are deeply lobed and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom in the spring. Lacinaria nashii thrives in well-drained soil and requires a humid, temperate climate to flourish.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
