Lasiopetalum corniculatum

Lasiopetalum corniculatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring small, narrow leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that are usually pink or red, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and dry sclerophyll forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
