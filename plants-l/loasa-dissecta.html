Loasa dissecta

Loasa dissecta, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense panicles, and are usually pink to purple in color.

Loasa dissecta is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
