Laothoe angustifolia

Laothoe angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, ovate leaves that are typically hairy in texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Laothoe angustifolia is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
