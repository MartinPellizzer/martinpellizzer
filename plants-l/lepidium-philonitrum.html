Lepidium philonitrum

Lepidium philonitrum, commonly known as the narrowleaf wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is characterized by its small, yellow or white flowers. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Lepidium philonitrum is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to thrive in mild, temperate climates.
