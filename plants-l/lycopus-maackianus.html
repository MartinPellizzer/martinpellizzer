Lycopus maackianus

Lycopus maackianus, commonly known as Maack's blueweed, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are small, blue to purple, and clustered in whorls along the stem.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.
