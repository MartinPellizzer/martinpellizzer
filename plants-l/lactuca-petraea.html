Lactuca petraea

Lactuca petraea, commonly known as rock lettuce, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, which are followed by spherical, dry fruits called achenes. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas.

Lactuca petraea is also known for its milky sap, which is characteristic of the genus Lactuca.
