Lepanthes tetrachaeta

Lepanthes tetrachaeta, commonly known as the four-awned slipper orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped pouch that serves as a pollination trap. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually small and white or pale green, with intricate patterns that aid in attracting specific pollinators.

Lepanthes tetrachaeta is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
