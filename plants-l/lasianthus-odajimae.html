Lasianthus odajimae

Lasianthus odajimae, commonly known as the Japanese flame flower, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant red and orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and birds due to their nectar-rich structure and bright colors.
