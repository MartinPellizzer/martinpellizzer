Libanothamnus neriifolius var. columbicus

Libanothamnus neriifolius var. columbicus, commonly known as the Pacific Coast buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a silvery appearance. This species thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and inland valleys.

Its botanical features make it an important plant for pollinators and a component of local ecosystems.
