Lannea schweinfurthii var. acutifoliolata

Lannea schweinfurthii var. acutifoliolata, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and distinctive, often thorny branches. The leaves are typically compound, with serrated leaflets that give it a rugged appearance. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange or red berries that are an important food source for wildlife.

This plant is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
