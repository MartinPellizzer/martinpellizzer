Linum bienne

Linum bienne, commonly known as the common flax, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, blue to purple flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny, oval seeds. Linum bienne is often cultivated for its fibers, which are used in textile production, and its seeds are used for oil extraction.

It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun exposure.
