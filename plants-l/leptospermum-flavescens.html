Leptospermum flavescens

Leptospermum flavescens, commonly known as the yellow tea tree, is a shrub native to Tasmania.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green, with a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, followed by the development of woody, cone-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and heathlands.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
