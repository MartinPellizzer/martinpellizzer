Lobelia tortuosa f. glabrata

Lobelia tortuosa f. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth lobelia, is a compact perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically smooth and glabrous, giving it its specific epithet "glabrata.". The plant produces spikes of blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a native, low-maintenance plant.
