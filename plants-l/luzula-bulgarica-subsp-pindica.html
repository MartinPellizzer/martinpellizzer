Luzula bulgarica subsp. pindica

Luzula bulgarica subsp.

pindica, commonly known as the Pindus sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets during the summer months. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in acidic soils.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants.
