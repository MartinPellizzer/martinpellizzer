Lendneria verticillata

Lendneria verticillata, commonly known as the clustered morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue, violet, and purple. The plant features palmately lobed leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Lendneria verticillata is also valued for its ability to climb or trail, making it suitable for trellises or hanging baskets.
