Lissochilus brevisepalus

Lissochilus brevisepalus, commonly known as the short-styled lissochilus, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or mountainous terrain, where it thrives in cool and moist conditions.

Its unique floral structure and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists studying alpine flora.
