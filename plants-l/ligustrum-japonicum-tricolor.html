Ligustrum japonicum tricolor

Ligustrum japonicum tricolor, commonly known as the Japanese privet, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features small, white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. The plant has dark green leaves that turn reddish in the fall, adding visual interest to the landscape. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Ligustrum japonicum tricolor is also valued for its use in hedges and as a landscape plant due to its adaptability and low maintenance requirements.
