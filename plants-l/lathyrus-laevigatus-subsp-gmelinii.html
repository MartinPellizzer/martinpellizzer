Lathyrus laevigatus subsp. gmelinii

Lathyrus laevigatus subsp.

gmelinii, commonly known as the Gmelin's larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers.

The plant produces small, bean-like pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the Fabaceae family, known for its ornamental value and ecological significance.
