Leichhardtia papillosa

Leichhardtia papillosa, commonly known as the silky leichhardtia, is a perennial herb native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it its common name. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or heathland environments.

Leichhardtia papillosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
