Linaria linogrisea f. longeracemosa

Linaria linogrisea f. longeracemosa, commonly known as the long-flowered yellow horned-poppy, is a variant of the yellow horned-poppy, characterized by its elongated, cascading racemes of bright yellow flowers.

This subspecies is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils in full sun. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped, serrated leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant is an annual or biennial herb, reaching heights of up to 60 cm, and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

It is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
