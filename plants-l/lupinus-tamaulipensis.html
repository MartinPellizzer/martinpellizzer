Lupinus tamaulipensis

Lupinus tamaulipensis, commonly known as Tamaulipan lupine, is a flowering plant native to the Tamaulipan region of Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along coastal areas.

Lupinus tamaulipensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
