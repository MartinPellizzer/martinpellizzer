Lycium colletioides

Lycium colletioides, commonly known as the red bush willow, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for both humans and animals. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

Lycium colletioides plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
