Liparis barbata

Liparis barbata, commonly known as the beard orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to regions of Asia, including China, Japan, and the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and its distinctive, spike-like inflorescence bearing small, greenish or yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountain meadows. It has a complex root system and often forms dense colonies.

Liparis barbata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
