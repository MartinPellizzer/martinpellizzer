Leuzea acaulis

Leuzea acaulis, commonly known as the slender leuzea, is a perennial herb native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in nutrient-poor, rocky soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Leuzea acaulis plays a role in stabilizing soil in harsh environments and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
