Leptospermum lanigerum var. concolor

Leptospermum lanigerum var. concolor, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tea tree, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer seasons. It is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.

The species is valued for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in horticulture for its ornamental qualities.
