Lithocarpus densiflorus subsp. echinoides

Lithocarpus densiflorus subsp.

echinoides, commonly known as the broadleaf chestnut, is a member of the Fagaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia. It is a deciduous tree characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and produces hard, woody fruits.

The leaves are typically elliptical with a leathery texture and a prominent midrib. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
