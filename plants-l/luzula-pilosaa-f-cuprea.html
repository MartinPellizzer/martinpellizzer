Luzula pilosaa f. cuprea

Luzula pilosaa f. cuprea, commonly known as the copper-haired wood sedge, is a variant of the wood sedge species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage with a distinctive reddish or coppery hue on the stems and leaves, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikes, which are usually green or brown in color.

Luzula pilosaa f. cuprea is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and adaptability to a variety of soil conditions.
