Lepanthes genetoapophantica

Lepanthes genetoapophantica, commonly known as the Genetoapophantica orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its flowers are usually small, with intricate patterns and vibrant colors, often in shades of white, pink, and purple.

Lepanthes genetoapophantica is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the biodiversity of its native ecosystems.
