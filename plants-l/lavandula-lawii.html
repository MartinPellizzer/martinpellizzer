Lavandula lawii

Lavandula lawii, commonly known as the Hawaiian lavender, is a species native to the island of Molokai in Hawaii.

It is a low-growing shrub with slender, aromatic leaves and spikes of purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, typical of its coastal habitat. Its essential oils contain compounds like linalool and lavandulol, which contribute to its pleasant fragrance and medicinal properties.

Lavandula lawii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
