Lobelia esquirolii

Lobelia esquirolii, commonly known as the Sierra lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, tubular blue or purple flowers. This species thrives in high-elevation environments, often found in meadows and open woodlands. Its leaves are typically narrow and hairy, adapted to the cool, moist conditions of its habitat.

Lobelia esquirolii plays an important role in pollination, attracting various insects such as bees and butterflies.
