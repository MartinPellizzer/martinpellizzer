Lamium album

Lamium album, commonly known as white dead-nettle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, western Asia, and northern Africa.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture and produces small, white, bilaterally symmetrical flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and garden borders. It is part of the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like mint and basil.

Lamium album is often used in herbal medicine for its mild sedative and anti-inflammatory properties.
