Lysinema roseum

Lysinema roseum, commonly known as the rose bugloss, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces vibrant purple or pink flowers in the spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Lysinema roseum is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
