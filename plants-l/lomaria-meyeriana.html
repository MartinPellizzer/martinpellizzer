Lomaria meyeriana

Lomaria meyeriana, commonly known as Meyer's lomatia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its unique, spiny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pale yellow or greenish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
