Lycopodium selago subsp. appressum

Lycopodium selago subsp.

appressum, commonly known as the creeping clubmoss, is a small, evergreen fern-like plant native to the northern hemisphere. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices.

The plant has slender, prostrate stems that form dense mats, and its leaves are narrow and scale-like. It produces spore-bearing structures called sporophylls, which are clustered in cone-like formations.

This subspecies is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
