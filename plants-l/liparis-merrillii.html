Liparis merrillii

Liparis merrillii, commonly known as the Merrill's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the surrounding soil.

Liparis merrillii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
