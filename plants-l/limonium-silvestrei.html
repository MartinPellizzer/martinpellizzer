Limonium silvestrei

Limonium silvestrei, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its blue or purple flower heads that resemble small bells, surrounded by white or pale blue bracts. The plant thrives in salty, well-drained soils and is often found in dunes or along the coastline. Its flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Limonium silvestrei is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
