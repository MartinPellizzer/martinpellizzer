Lamium moschatum var. rhodium

Lamium moschatum var. rhodium, commonly known as silverleaf deadnettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its distinctive silver-gray foliage.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with a velvety texture and a striking metallic sheen. The plant produces clusters of small, two-lipped flowers in shades of pink or purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
