Linanthus latisectus

Linanthus latisectus, commonly known as the broad-lobed flaxflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Linaceae family, which includes other members such as flax and common rush. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are broad and deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.
