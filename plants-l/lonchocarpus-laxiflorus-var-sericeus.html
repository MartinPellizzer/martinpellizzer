Lonchocarpus laxiflorus var. sericeus

Lonchocarpus laxiflorus var. sericeus, commonly known as the silky senna, is a tropical shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and research settings.
