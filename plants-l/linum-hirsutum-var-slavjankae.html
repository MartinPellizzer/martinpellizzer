Linum hirsutum var. slavjankae

Linum hirsutum var. slavjankae, commonly known as Slavjanka flax, is a variant of the flax species native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves, which contribute to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in traditional dyeing practices.
