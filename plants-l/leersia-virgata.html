Leersia virgata

Leersia virgata, commonly known as narrowleaf bottlebrush grass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted clumps of narrow, grass-like leaves and its distinctive bottlebrush-like inflorescence. The plant produces small, spikelet-like flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Leersia virgata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wet environments.
