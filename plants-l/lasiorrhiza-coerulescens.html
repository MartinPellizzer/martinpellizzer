Lasiorrhiza coerulescens

Lasiorrhiza coerulescens, commonly known as the blue-flowered daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, blue or violet-colored ray florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellow disk flowers at the center of each head.

Lasiorrhiza coerulescens is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
