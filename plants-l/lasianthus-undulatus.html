Lasianthus undulatus

Lasianthus undulatus, commonly known as the wavy leaf lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its lanceolate leaves with undulating edges. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or blue in color, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking to add visual interest to shaded landscapes.
