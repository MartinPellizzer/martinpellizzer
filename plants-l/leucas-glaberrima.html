Leucas glaberrima

Leucas glaberrima, commonly known as the hairy leucas, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
