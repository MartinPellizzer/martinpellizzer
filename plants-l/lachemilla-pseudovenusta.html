Lachemilla pseudovenusta

Lachemilla pseudovenusta, commonly known as the silverleaf cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features finely divided, silver-gray leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small, yellowish, and clustered in loose inflorescences, blooming in early spring.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.
