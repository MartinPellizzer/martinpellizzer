Lycopodium christensenianum

Lycopodium christensenianum, commonly known as the Christensen's clubmoss, is a small, evergreen plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit and its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces spore-bearing cones, which are typically clustered at the tips of its branching stems. It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly in areas with high humidity and acidic soils.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is often found in forest understories and is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
