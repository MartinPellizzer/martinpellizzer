Leptospermum floridum

Leptospermum floridum, commonly known as the coastal tea tree, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white or pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in coastal and sandy soils, often found in heathlands and dunes. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine and are also valued for their essential oils.

Leptospermum floridum plays an important role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems and supporting local wildlife.
