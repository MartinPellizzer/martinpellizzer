Lysinema pungens var. sieberi

Lysinema pungens var. sieberi, commonly known as the Sieber's thimbleweed, is a perennial herb native to the northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowering period occurs in late summer, producing yellow disk flowers surrounded by pale green to white ray florets.

The species is known for its delicate appearance and is often found in association with other shade-tolerant flora.
