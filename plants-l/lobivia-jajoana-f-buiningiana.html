Lobivia jajoana f. buiningiana

Lobivia jajoana f. buiningiana, commonly known as the Buining's lobivia, is a succulent plant native to South America, specifically Argentina.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, branching structure with thick, fleshy stems. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
