Lavandula dentata var. dentata

Lavandula dentata var. dentata, commonly known as English lavender, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and aromatic, gray-green foliage. The plant produces spikes of pale purple to pinkish-lavender flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its essential oils, derived from the flowers, are widely used in aromatherapy and perfumery.

The species is well adapted to dry, well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal cultivation.
