Linaria oblongifolia subsp. benitoi

Linaria oblongifolia subsp.

benitoi, commonly known as the Benito's toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
