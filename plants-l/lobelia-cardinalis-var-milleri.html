Lobelia cardinalis var. milleri

Lobelia cardinalis var. milleri, commonly known as the cardinal lobelia, is a striking perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant red flowers, which bloom in spikes during the summer months. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and prefers moist, well-drained soils in partial to full shade. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
