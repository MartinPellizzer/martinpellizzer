Lobelia urens var. serrulata

Lobelia urens var. serrulata, commonly known as the serrated blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, blue to purple flowers. The leaves are typically serrated, giving the plant its varietal name, and it often grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. It plays a role in supporting local pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
