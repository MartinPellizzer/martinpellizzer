Loranthus sphaericocompressus

Loranthus sphaericocompressus, commonly known as the spherical-leaved mistletoe, is a parasitic plant belonging to the Loranthaceae family.

It typically attaches itself to the branches of host trees, such as eucalyptus or acacia, using haustoria to extract water and nutrients. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. Its spherical, compressed leaves are a distinctive feature, contributing to its unique appearance.

The species is native to parts of Australia and is known for its ecological role in supporting various insect and bird species.
