Leucocroton incrustatus

Leucocroton incrustatus, commonly known as the rough-leaved croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often covered in a whitish, powdery coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. Its bark and leaves contain various chemical compounds, some of which have been studied for potential medicinal properties.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate to flourish.
