Leucas masaiensis var. tricrenata

Leucas masaiensis var. tricrenata, commonly known as the Masai leucas, is a perennial herb native to East Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while the stems are square, a common feature in the mint family.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
