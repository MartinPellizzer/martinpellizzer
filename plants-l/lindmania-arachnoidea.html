Lindmania arachnoidea

Lindmania arachnoidea, commonly known as the spider-plant or spider fern, is a tropical evergreen plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, arching fronds that resemble spider legs, giving it its common name. The plant has a short, stout rhizome with dense clusters of small, spore-producing structures called sorus. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Lindmania arachnoidea is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its unique foliage and air-purifying qualities.
