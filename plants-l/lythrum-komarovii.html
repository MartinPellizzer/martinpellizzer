Lythrum komarovii

Lythrum komarovii, commonly known as Komarov's lythrum, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lythraceae family, which includes plants known for their showy flowers and ecological importance. The plant features slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically pink to purple, arranged in dense spikes, and bloom during the summer months.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
