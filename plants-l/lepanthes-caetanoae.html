Lepanthes caetanoae

Lepanthes caetanoae, commonly known as the Caetano's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically feature a combination of white, yellow, and green hues. The plant grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded forest environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that help it absorb moisture and nutrients from the air.

Lepanthes caetanoae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
