Lilium tigrinum var. lishmannii

Lilium tigrinum var. lishmannii, commonly known as the Lishmann's tiger lily, is a striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, orange-red flowers with distinctive black spots, which resemble the pattern of a tiger's coat. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique coloration and its role in the study of plant pigmentation and hybridization.
