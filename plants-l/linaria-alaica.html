Linaria alaica

Linaria alaica, commonly known as the Canary Island horned poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the genus Linaria in the family Plantaginaceae. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, spiky seed pods and vibrant yellow-orange flowers. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

Linaria alaica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
