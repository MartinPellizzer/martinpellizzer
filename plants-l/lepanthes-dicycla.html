Lepanthes dicycla

Lepanthes dicycla, commonly known as the pink lady's tresses, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its dense, dark green leaves and its distinctive inflorescence of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. It has a rhizomatous root system and produces showy, fragrant blooms during the spring and early summer.

Lepanthes dicycla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
