Lindera reflexa

Lindera reflexa, commonly known as wild lauraster, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist woodlands and stream banks, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. The plant features opposite, elliptic leaves that are usually smooth on the upper surface and hairy on the underside. Its small, fragrant yellowish-white flowers appear in clusters in early spring, followed by red, fleshy fruits.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
