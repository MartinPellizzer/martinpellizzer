Lupinus hartwegii

Lupinus hartwegii, commonly known as Hartweg's lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and showy blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces cylindrical seed pods that contain several seeds.

Lupinus hartwegii is often found in open habitats such as grasslands, sagebrush steppe, and along canyon walls.
