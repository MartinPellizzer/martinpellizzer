Lessingianthus bardanoides

Lessingianthus bardanoides, commonly known as the Brazilian sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow ray florets and dark brown disk florets. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and is adapted to full sun exposure. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, which help it retain moisture in its native habitat.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant flowers and compact growth habit.
