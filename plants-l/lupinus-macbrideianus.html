Lupinus macbrideianus

Lupinus macbrideianus, commonly known as the MacBride's lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States, particularly found in California and Oregon.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, spindly growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces elongated pods that contain several seeds.

Lupinus macbrideianus is adapted to grow in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and forest edges.
