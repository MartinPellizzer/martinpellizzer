Lepicephalus centaurioides

Lepicephalus centaurioides, commonly known as the centaur's head plant, is a unique succulent native to South Africa.

It features a thick, fleshy stem that resembles a human head, with prominent, waxy leaves arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its striking appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in collections of unusual succulents.
