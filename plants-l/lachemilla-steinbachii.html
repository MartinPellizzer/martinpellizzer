Lachemilla steinbachii

Lachemilla steinbachii, commonly known as the Steinbach's cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically arranged in clusters along the stems. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered with a fine layer of white hairs.

Lachemilla steinbachii thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.
