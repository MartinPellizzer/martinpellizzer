Limonium furfuraceum

Limonium furfuraceum, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its gray-green, fuzzy leaves and spikes of blue or violet flowers. The plant thrives in saline and sandy soils, making it well adapted to coastal environments. Limonium furfuraceum is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

Its flowers bloom in summer, adding color to seaside landscapes.
