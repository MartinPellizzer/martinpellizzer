Luzula neglecta

Luzula neglecta, commonly known as the neglected woodrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, greenish-white flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Luzula neglecta is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
