Lentiscus

Lentiscus, commonly known as mastic tree, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny foliage and small, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by dark blue or black berries. Its bark is thick and fissured, and it is well adapted to dry, rocky soils.

Lentiscus is known for its aromatic resin, which has been historically used in various traditional applications.
