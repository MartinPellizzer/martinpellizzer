Lathyrus sylvestris subsp. cirrhosus

Lathyrus sylvestris subsp.

cirrhosus, commonly known as the curly-stemmed wild vetch, is a perennial legume native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its slender, often curling stems and trifoliate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pale pink to violet, typically blooming in late spring. Its pods are elongated and contain several elliptical seeds.

This subspecies is adapted to grow in a variety of habitats, including woodlands and grasslands, and is often found in disturbed soils.
