Laurus ocotea

Laurus ocotea, commonly known as ocotillo, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and dense, lance-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The leaves and bark have been used traditionally for their aromatic properties and medicinal applications.
