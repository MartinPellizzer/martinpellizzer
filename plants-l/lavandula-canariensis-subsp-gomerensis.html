Lavandula canariensis subsp. gomerensis

Lavandula canariensis subsp.

gomerensis, commonly known as the Gomera lavender, is a subspecies native to the Canary Islands. It is a perennial shrub characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and spikes of purple flowers.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, typical of its native Mediterranean climate. It is often used in ornamental gardening for its visual appeal and fragrance.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
