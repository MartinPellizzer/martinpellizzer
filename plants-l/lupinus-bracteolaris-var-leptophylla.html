Lupinus bracteolaris var. leptophylla

Lupinus bracteolaris var. leptophylla, commonly known as the slender-leaf lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, narrow leaves and clusters of blue or purple flowers. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open grasslands and rocky areas.

Its ecological importance includes providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for various wildlife species.
