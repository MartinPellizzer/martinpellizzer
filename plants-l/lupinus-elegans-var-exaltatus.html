Lupinus elegans var. exaltatus

Lupinus elegans var. exaltatus, commonly known as the tall lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

Lupinus elegans var. exaltatus is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and attractive addition to native plant gardens.
