Landolphia crassifolia

Landolphia crassifolia, commonly known as the African star apple, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces edible, sweet, and juicy fruits that are often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in forested areas.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and economically valuable in its native regions.
