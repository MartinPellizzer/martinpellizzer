Limonium corinthiacum

Limonium corinthiacum, commonly known as the Corinthian sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in coastal and saline environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in a fine layer of salt.

Limonium corinthiacum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soils.
