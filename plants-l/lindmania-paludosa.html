Lindmania paludosa

Lindmania paludosa, commonly known as the bog lindmania, is a species of flowering plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family, which includes many epiphytic and terrestrial plants. This plant is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a water-holding structure, typical of bromeliads. Lindmania paludosa thrives in acidic, nutrient-poor soils and is often found in marshy or swampy environments.

Its small, clustered flowers are usually white or pale pink, adding to its ornamental value in botanical gardens.
