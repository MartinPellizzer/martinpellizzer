Lespedeza hisauchii

Lespedeza hisauchii, commonly known as Japanese lespedeza, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to fix nitrogen and prevent soil erosion.

Lespedeza hisauchii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
