Lasiopetalum behrii

Lasiopetalum behrii, commonly known as the Behri's matted bellflower, is a low-growing shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, often with a slight blue or purple tinge. The plant has dense, mat-forming foliage with small, ovate leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or dry woodland habitats.

Lasiopetalum behrii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
