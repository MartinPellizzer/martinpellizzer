Leucadendron floridum

Leucadendron floridum, commonly known as the Florida flame bush, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its bright orange to red flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine, white hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for coastal landscapes.

The Florida flame bush is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
