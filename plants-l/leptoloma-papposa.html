Leptoloma papposa

Leptoloma papposa, commonly known as the silky milkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pale green leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. It is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Leptoloma papposa is known for its milky sap, which is a characteristic feature of the milkweed family.
