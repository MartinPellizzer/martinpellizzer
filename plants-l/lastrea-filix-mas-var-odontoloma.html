Lastrea filix-mas var. odontoloma

Lastrea filix-mas var. odontoloma, commonly known as the snake plant or mother-in-law's tongue, is a succulent plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features upright, sword-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a striking yellow or white border. The plant is characterized by its ability to store water in its fleshy leaves, making it highly drought-tolerant. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color.

This variety is popular as a low-maintenance indoor plant due to its resilience and air-purifying properties.
