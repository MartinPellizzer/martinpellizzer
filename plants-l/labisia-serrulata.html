Labisia serrulata

Labisia serrulata, commonly known as the zigzag plant, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, ascending stems that often exhibit a zigzag pattern, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have serrated edges, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Labisia serrulata is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
