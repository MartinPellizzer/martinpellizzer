Lepidium buschianum

Lepidium buschianum, commonly known as Busch's cress, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, often found in California and Nevada. It produces small, yellow flowers and has a peppery taste similar to other members of its family.

Its seeds are edible and have been used in traditional herbal remedies.
