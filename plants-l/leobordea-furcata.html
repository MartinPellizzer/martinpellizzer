Leobordea furcata

Leobordea furcata, commonly known as the forked leobordea, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces delicate, white or pale blue flowers in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
