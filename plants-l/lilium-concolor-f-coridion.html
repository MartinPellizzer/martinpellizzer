Lilium concolor f. coridion

Lilium concolor f. coridion, commonly known as the white lily, is a botanical variant of the concolor lily native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its white, funnel-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and meadows, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a smooth texture.

This cultivar is valued for its elegant appearance and is often cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.
