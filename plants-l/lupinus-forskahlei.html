Lupinus forskahlei

Lupinus forskahlei, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in mountainous regions.

Lupinus forskahlei plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing nectar for pollinators and supporting local biodiversity.
