Lavandula stoechas subsp. luisieri

Lavandula stoechas subsp.

luisieri, commonly known as the Spanish lavender, is a subspecies native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its distinctive, colorful bracts that range from red to purple, which are often mistaken for flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It has narrow, gray-green leaves and produces spikes of small, fragrant purple flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and aromatic properties.
