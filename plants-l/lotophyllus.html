Lotophyllus

Lotophyllus, commonly known as the ghost fern, is a unique plant belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent fronds that give it a ghostly appearance, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are clustered on the underside of its fronds.

Lotophyllus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded gardens.
