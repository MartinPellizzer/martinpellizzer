Lepanthes elegans

Lepanthes elegans, commonly known as the elegant slipper orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a distinctive slipper-shaped pouch that aids in pollination.

Lepanthes elegans is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
