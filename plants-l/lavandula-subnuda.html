Lavandula subnuda

Lavandula subnuda, commonly known as the naked lavender, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, gray-green leaves. The plant produces spikes of pale purple to white flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its delicate appearance, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and in aromatherapy.
