Leurocline somalensis

Leurocline somalensis, commonly known as the Somali leurocline, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Somalia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, adapted to withstand high temperatures and low rainfall. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a waxy layer to reduce water loss.

The flowers are usually yellow or orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
