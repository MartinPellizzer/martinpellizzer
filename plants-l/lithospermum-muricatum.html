Lithospermum muricatum

Lithospermum muricatum, commonly known as the rough lithosperm, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features erect, branched stems covered in stiff, glandular hairs. The leaves are lance-shaped, with serrated margins and a hairy surface. Its flowers are small and typically pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

The plant produces dark red or black berries that contain the seeds, which are dispersed by animals.
