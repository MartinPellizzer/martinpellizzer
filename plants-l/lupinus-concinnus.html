Lupinus concinnus

Lupinus concinnus, commonly known as the silver lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its silvery-blue flowers and upright growth habit. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Lupinus concinnus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration due to its nitrogen-fixing abilities.
