Linum luteolum

Linum luteolum, commonly known as yellow flax, is an annual herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. Its fibrous stalks were historically used for textile production, similar to its more well-known relative, flax.

Linum luteolum thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a common sight in meadows and disturbed habitats.
