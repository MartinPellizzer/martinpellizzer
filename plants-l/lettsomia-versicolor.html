Lettsomia versicolor

Lettsomia versicolor, commonly known as the variable lettuce, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are typically green with a bluish tinge. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in composite inflorescences. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in grassy areas or along roadsides.

Lettsomia versicolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
