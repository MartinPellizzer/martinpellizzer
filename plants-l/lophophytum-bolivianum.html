Lophophytum bolivianum

Lophophytum bolivianum, commonly known as the Bolivian candleplant, is a unique succulent native to the Andes mountains of Bolivia.

It features a rosette of fleshy, waxy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the warmer months. Its leaves are often described as resembling candles, giving it its common name.

Lophophytum bolivianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and low maintenance requirements in cultivation.
