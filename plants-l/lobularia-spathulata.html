Lobularia spathulata

Lobularia spathulata, commonly known as the sweet Alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial species belonging to the mustard family, Brassicaceae. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pink flowers that bloom throughout the summer. Its leaves are oval-shaped and covered in a fine layer of hair, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

Lobularia spathulata is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
