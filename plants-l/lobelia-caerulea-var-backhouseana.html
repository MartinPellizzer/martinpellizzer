Lobelia caerulea var. backhouseana

Lobelia caerulea var. backhouseana, commonly known as the Blue Lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches. The plant produces spikes of small, tubular blue or violet flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly rough texture.

This variety is well-suited for moist, partially shaded environments and is often used in garden landscapes for its ornamental value.
