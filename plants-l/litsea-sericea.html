Litsea sericea

Litsea sericea, commonly known as silver leaf mohua, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its finely haired, silver-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn red when mature.

Litsea sericea is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping and as a hedge plant.
