Lightfootia mucronulata

Lightfootia mucronulata, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green leaves and showy, clustered flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers indirect sunlight, making it a popular houseplant.

Its ornamental value is further enhanced by its ability to bloom throughout the year under proper care.
