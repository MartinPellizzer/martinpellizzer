Limonium girardianum subsp. balearicum

Limonium girardianum subsp.

balearicum, commonly known as the Balearic sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly the Balearic Islands. It is characterized by its delicate, blue to purple flower clusters that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in coastal areas, preferring sandy or rocky soils and full sun exposure. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
