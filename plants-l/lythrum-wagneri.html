Lythrum wagneri

Lythrum wagneri, commonly known as Wagner's loosestrife, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lythraceae family, which includes other species such as the common purple loosestrife. The plant typically grows in wetlands and along stream banks, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. It produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
