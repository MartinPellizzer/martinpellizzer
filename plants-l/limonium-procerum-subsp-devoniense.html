Limonium procerum subsp. devoniense

Limonium procerum subsp.

devoniense, commonly known as the Devon limonium, is a flowering plant native to the British Isles, particularly found in coastal areas of Devon and Cornwall. It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late summer.

The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dune environments. Its flowers are typically surrounded by narrow, grass-like leaves, and it is known for its drought tolerance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in coastal ecosystems.
