Luteidiscus calcisabulorum

Luteidiscus calcisabulorum, commonly known as the limestone yellowbell, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its yellowish flowers and succulent leaves adapted to conserve water. The plant thrives in well-drained soils, often found in rocky or limestone-rich environments. Its botanical features include a compact growth habit and a short stem with thick, fleshy foliage.

Luteidiscus calcisabulorum plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for small desert organisms.
