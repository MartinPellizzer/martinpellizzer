Lachemilla lycopodioidea

Lachemilla lycopodioidea, commonly known as the creeping lichen moss, is a unique plant that exhibits characteristics of both mosses and lichens.

It is a non-vascular plant, meaning it lacks true roots, stems, and leaves, and instead absorbs water and nutrients through its entire surface. This species thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest floors or on rocky substrates. Its structure is composed of a dense mat of filaments that form a cohesive layer, aiding in soil stabilization.

Lachemilla lycopodioidea plays a vital role in ecological systems by contributing to nutrient cycling and providing habitat for microorganisms.
