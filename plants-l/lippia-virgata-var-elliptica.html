Lippia virgata var. elliptica

Lippia virgata var. elliptica, commonly known as the narrow-leaf lippia, is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It features aromatic, narrow elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping and ornamental gardens.

Its essential oils, derived from the leaves, are valued in aromatherapy and traditional medicine for their calming and antiseptic properties.
