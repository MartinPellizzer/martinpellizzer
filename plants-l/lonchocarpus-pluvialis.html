Lonchocarpus pluvialis

Lonchocarpus pluvialis, commonly known as the rainforest senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces distinctive pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded environments within rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
