Linum basarabicum

Linum basarabicum, commonly known as the Basarabian flax, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and small, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its seeds are small and oval.

Linum basarabicum is primarily of interest for its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties.
