Lippia spinifera

Lippia spinifera, commonly known as Mexican mint or lemon verbena, is a perennial shrub native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves with a strong, lemony scent, and small, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is often cultivated for its medicinal properties and as a culinary herb due to its pleasant fragrance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for both garden and container growing.

Lippia spinifera is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
