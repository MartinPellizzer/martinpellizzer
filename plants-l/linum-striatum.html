Linum striatum

Linum striatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf flax, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and produces delicate blue or white flowers. The plant belongs to the family Linaceae and is known for its fibrous stems, which have been historically used for textile production. Linum striatum thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
