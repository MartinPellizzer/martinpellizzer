Lilac

Lilac, commonly known as *Syringa*, is a genus of about 25 species of shrubs in the olive family.

These plants are native to regions of Europe, North Africa, and western Asia. Lilacs are characterized by their fragrant, clustered flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The leaves are typically simple, alternate, and have a glossy appearance.

They are often cultivated for their ornamental value and are popular in gardens and landscapes.
