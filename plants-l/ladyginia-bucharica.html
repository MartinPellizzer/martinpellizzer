Ladyginia bucharica

Ladyginia bucharica, commonly known as the Caucasian lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It features a basal rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often covered in a whitish bloom. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a compact cluster during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to gardens with suitable conditions.
