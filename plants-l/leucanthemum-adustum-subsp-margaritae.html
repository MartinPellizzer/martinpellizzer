Leucanthemum adustum subsp. margaritae

Leucanthemum adustum subsp.

margaritae, commonly known as the white marguerite, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its daisy-like appearance, featuring white petals surrounding a yellow central disk.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas, thriving in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are upright and often branched.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in garden settings for its delicate, long-lasting blooms.
