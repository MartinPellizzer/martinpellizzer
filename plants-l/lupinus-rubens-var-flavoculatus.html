Lupinus rubens var. flavoculatus

Lupinus rubens var. flavoculatus, commonly known as the yellow-eyed lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with a distinctive orange eye marking. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces elongated seed pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
