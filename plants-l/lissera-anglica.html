Lissera anglica

Lissera anglica, commonly known as the common tansy, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in moist meadows and woodland edges. It produces small, oval-shaped fruits called cypselae, which are dispersed by the wind.

Lissera anglica is known for its aromatic properties and has been used historically in herbal medicine.
