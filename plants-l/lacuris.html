Lacuris

Lacuris, commonly known as the silver leaf plant, is a unique species native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking silvery-gray foliage, which reflects sunlight and helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its succulent leaves store water, making it highly drought-tolerant.

Lacuris is often cultivated in xeriscapes and rock gardens due to its low maintenance and ornamental value.
