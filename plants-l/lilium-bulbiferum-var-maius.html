Lilium bulbiferum var. maius

Lilium bulbiferum var. maius, commonly known as the giant bulbous lily, is a striking member of the Liliaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter, typically in shades of white, pink, or red. The plant produces bulbous rhizomes that store nutrients, allowing it to thrive in well-drained, moist soils. It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its size and beauty, it requires ample space and care to flourish in garden settings.
