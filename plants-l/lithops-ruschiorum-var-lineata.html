Lithops ruschiorum var. lineata

Lithops ruschiorum var. lineata, commonly known as the "Finger Lithops," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, rock-like appearance with two fleshy, paired leaves that resemble fingers. The plant's leaves are typically green with distinctive white or yellow markings, which help in camouflage within its natural habitat. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the spring season.

This species is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy leaves to survive prolonged dry periods.
