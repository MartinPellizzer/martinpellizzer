Lecythis biserrata

Lecythis biserrata, commonly known as the Amazonian chestnut, is a tropical tree native to the dense rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the family Lecythidaceae and is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and showy, orange-red flowers. The tree produces a heavy, woody fruit that contains a single large seed, often used in traditional medicine. Its bark and leaves have been utilized for their medicinal properties by indigenous communities.

Lecythis biserrata plays an important ecological role in supporting biodiversity within its habitat.
