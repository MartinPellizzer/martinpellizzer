Leptoboea multiflora

Leptoboea multiflora, commonly known as the many-flowered leptoboea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable plant for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
