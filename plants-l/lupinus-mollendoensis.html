Lupinus mollendoensis

Lupinus mollendoensis, commonly known as the Peruvian lupin, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky soils. The plant produces clusters of pea-like flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Lupinus mollendoensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been studied for its potential use in ecological restoration projects.
