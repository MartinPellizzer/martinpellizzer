Lithospermum davuricum

Lithospermum davuricum, commonly known as the Siberian bugloss, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, branching stems and blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils. It is known for its medicinal properties, particularly the presence of lithospermic acid in its roots.

This species is valued in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects.
