Leucopoa pulchella subsp. jurana

Leucopoa pulchella subsp.

jurana, commonly known as the Jurana lichen, is a member of the Pilocarpaceae family. It is a crustose lichen that typically grows on rock surfaces in arid or semi-arid environments.

The thallus is pale to yellowish in color and lacks distinct lobes, forming a continuous layer. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in harsh, dry conditions.

This subspecies is often found in regions with low humidity and high solar exposure.
