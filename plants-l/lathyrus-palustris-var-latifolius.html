Lathyrus palustris var. latifolius

Lathyrus palustris var. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf marsh vetch, is a herbaceous perennial native to wetland habitats in North America.

It features slender, branching stems that often grow from water or moist soil. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with broad, heart-shaped leaflets that are often hairy.

This variety is adapted to wet or boggy conditions, making it an important component of riparian and wetland ecosystems.
