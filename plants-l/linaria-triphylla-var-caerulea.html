Linaria triphylla var. caerulea

Linaria triphylla var. caerulea, commonly known as the blue-flowered linaria, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, bright blue or violet flowers. The leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its vibrant color and ease of cultivation in temperate climates.
