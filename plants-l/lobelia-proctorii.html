Lobelia proctorii

Lobelia proctorii, commonly known as Proctor's lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces delicate blue to purple flowers in the summer months. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
