Leontodon minimus

Leontodon minimus, commonly known as the lesser spearthistle, is a small perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, grayish-green leaves and produces yellow flowers in early summer. Its flower heads are surrounded by bracts that resemble thistle-like structures, giving it its common name.

Leontodon minimus is notable for its compact size and distinctive floral morphology.
