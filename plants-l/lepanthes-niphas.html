Lepanthes niphas

Lepanthes niphas, commonly known as the Niphas orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. Its flowers are often clustered in a loose inflorescence, and it is prized for its ornamental value.

Lepanthes niphas is part of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its unique floral structure and adaptability to moist, forested habitats.
