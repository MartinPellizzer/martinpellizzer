Lupinus leptophyllus var. arsenei

Lupinus leptophyllus var. arsenei, commonly known as the Arizona lupine, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers. The plant has narrow, pinnately compound leaves that are adapted to arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for xeriscaping.

Lupinus leptophyllus var. arsenei plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing nectar for pollinators in its native habitat.
