Limnophyton fluitans

Limnophyton fluitans, commonly known as the floating mistplant, is a unique aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Araceae family and is characterized by its delicate, floating leaves that are often covered in a fine, mist-like coating. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and marshes. Its reproductive structures include a spadix and a spathe, typical of the Araceae family.

Limnophyton fluitans plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms and contributing to water quality through oxygenation.
