Lindernia beasleyi

Lindernia beasleyi, commonly known as the Beasley's Lindernia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, tubular flowers, typically in shades of pink or purple. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments. Its foliage is characterized by narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Lindernia beasleyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
