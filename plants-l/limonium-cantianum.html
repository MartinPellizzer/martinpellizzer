Limonium cantianum

Limonium cantianum, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spikes of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, salt marshes, and sandy soils, showing a high tolerance for saline conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in a fine layer of salt.

Limonium cantianum is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive, long-lasting blooms.
