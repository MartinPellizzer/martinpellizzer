Leonotis pallida

Leonotis pallida, commonly known as the pale lantern flower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular, pale yellow to orange flowers that resemble small lanterns. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Leonotis pallida is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in floral arrangements.
