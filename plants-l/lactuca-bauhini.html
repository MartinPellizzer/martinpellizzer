Lactuca bauhini

Lactuca bauhini, commonly known as the Indian endive, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its edible leaves, which have a slightly bitter taste. It thrives in cool climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Lactuca bauhini is also valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional medicinal uses.
