Linkia virgata

Linkia virgata, commonly known as the Christmas tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant has a distinctive crown of stiff, sparsely branched fronds that give it a unique, tree-like appearance. Its rhizome is thick and fibrous, allowing it to anchor firmly in moist, shaded environments.

Linkia virgata thrives in humid climates and is often found in coastal forests or on rocky outcrops.
