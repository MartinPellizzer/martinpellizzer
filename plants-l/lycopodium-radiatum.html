Lycopodium radiatum

Lycopodium radiatum, commonly known as the clubmoss, is a small evergreen plant that belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing form and its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces spores rather than seeds, and its reproductive structures are borne on specialized leaves called sporophylls. Lycopodium radiatum typically has a dense, mat-like growth pattern with small, scale-like leaves.

It is often found in forest floors and is an important component of certain ecosystems.
