Leersia elongata

Leersia elongata, commonly known as narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its triangular, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in moist habitats such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, which are usually wind-pollinated.

Leersia elongata plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various insects and wildlife.
