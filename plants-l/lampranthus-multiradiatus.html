Lampranthus multiradiatus

Lampranthus multiradiatus, commonly known as the firecracker flower, is a member of the Aizoaceae family, native to South Africa.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems and vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant produces its flowers in summer, typically in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
