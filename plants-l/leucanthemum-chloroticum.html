Leucanthemum chloroticum

Leucanthemum chloroticum, commonly known as the yellow oxeye daisy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow center surrounded by white ray florets, giving it a distinctive daisy-like appearance. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, thriving in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

The yellow oxeye daisy is known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
