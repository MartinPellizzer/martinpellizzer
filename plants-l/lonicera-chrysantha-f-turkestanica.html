Lonicera chrysantha f. turkestanica

Lonicera chrysantha f. turkestanica, commonly known as the Turkistan honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to Central Asia.

It features slender, arching stems that bear opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth, dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-white flowers in late spring to early summer, followed by small, red-orange berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental gardening in temperate climates.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
