Laurus cinnamifera

Laurus cinnamifera, commonly known as the Chinese laurel or cinnamon tree, is a member of the Lauraceae family.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to Southeast Asia, particularly China and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and small, edible berries. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to cinnamon.

The plant's botanical features include its glossy foliage and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
