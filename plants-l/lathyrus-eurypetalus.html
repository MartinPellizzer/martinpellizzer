Lathyrus eurypetalus

Lathyrus eurypetalus, commonly known as the broad-petaled larkspur, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and the Himalayas, where it thrives in alpine and subalpine environments. The plant produces tall spikes of blue to violet flowers, which are typically bilaterally symmetric and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets arranged alternately along the stem.

Lathyrus eurypetalus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
