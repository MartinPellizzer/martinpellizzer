Lyonia rubiginosa subsp. costata

Lyonia rubiginosa subsp.

costata, commonly known as the roughleaf lyonia, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves that are typically rough to the touch, featuring prominent veins and a serrated margin.

The plant produces small, bell-shaped white flowers in late spring, which are followed by red berries that persist into winter. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially shaded forest environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
