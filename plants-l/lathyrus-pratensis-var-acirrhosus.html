Lathyrus pratensis var. acirrhosus

Lathyrus pratensis var. acirrhosus, commonly known as the hairy vetch, is a perennial legume native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with slender stems that twine around supports. The plant produces clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its trifoliate leaves are typically hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural and ecological settings.
