Lycopodioides delicatula

Lycopodioides delicatula, commonly known as the delicate clubmoss, is a small, evergreen fern-like plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae and is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces spore-bearing cones, which are typically clustered at the base of the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices.

Lycopodioides delicatula plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
