Lobelia alexia

Lobelia alexia, commonly known as the purple lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Botanically, it belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure and ornamental value.
