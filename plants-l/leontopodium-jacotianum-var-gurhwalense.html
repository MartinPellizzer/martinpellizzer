Leontopodium jacotianum var. gurhwalense

Leontopodium jacotianum var. gurhwalense, commonly known as the Himalayan edelweiss, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly white foliage and showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments. Its unique appearance and habitat make it a symbol of alpine beauty and ecological resilience.

Due to its rarity and conservation status, it is often protected in its natural range.
