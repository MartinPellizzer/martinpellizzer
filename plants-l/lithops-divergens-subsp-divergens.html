Lithops divergens subsp. divergens

Lithops divergens subsp.

divergens, commonly known as the "living stone," is a succulent plant native to South Africa. It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that resemble stones, which help it blend into its arid environment.

The plant stores water in its leaves, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. It produces small, pale yellow flowers during the winter months.

Its unique morphology and adaptive features make it a popular species among succulent enthusiasts.
