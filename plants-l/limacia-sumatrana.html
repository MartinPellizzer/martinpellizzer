Limacia sumatrana

Limacia sumatrana, commonly known as the Sumatran lima bean, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine with trifoliate leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers. Its seeds, enclosed in elongated pods, are edible and often used in traditional cuisines.

The botanical features of Limacia sumatrana make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
