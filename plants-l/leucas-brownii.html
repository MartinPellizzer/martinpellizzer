Leucas brownii

Leucas brownii, commonly known as the brown leucas, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, often arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Leucas brownii is often grown for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
