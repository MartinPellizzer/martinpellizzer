Linaria triornithophora var. debilis

Linaria triornithophora var. debilis, commonly known as the slender toadflax, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, branching stems that grow from 30 to 60 centimeters in height. The plant produces delicate, yellow-orange flowers in racemes, which bloom from late winter to early spring. Its leaves are linear to lanceolate, often hairy, and arranged alternately along the stem.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
