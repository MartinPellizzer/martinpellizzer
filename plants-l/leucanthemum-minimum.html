Leucanthemum minimum

Leucanthemum minimum, commonly known as the mouse ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, white flowers with yellow centers that bloom from late spring to early summer. The plant has spoon-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of down. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its compact growth habit and delicate appearance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and ground covers.
