Lilium cernuum

Lilium cernuum, commonly known as the nodding lily, is a species of flowering plant in the lily family (Liliaceae).

It is native to North America and is characterized by its drooping, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a fan-like pattern, while its bulbs are used for propagation.

The nodding lily is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
