Leptospermum oreophilum

Leptospermum oreophilum, commonly known as the mountain tea tree, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting nectar-feeding insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathland and forested areas.

Leptospermum oreophilum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand drought conditions.
