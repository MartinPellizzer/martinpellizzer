Limonium circaei

Limonium circaei, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers and spiky, silver-gray foliage. The plant thrives in coastal areas and well-drained soils, often growing in sandy or gravelly habitats. Its flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Limonium circaei is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
