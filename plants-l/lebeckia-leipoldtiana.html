Lebeckia leipoldtiana

Lebeckia leipoldtiana, commonly known as the Leipoldt's lebeckia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features upright, branched stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in dense clusters, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils.
