Lundia virginalis

Lundia virginalis, commonly known as the sacred fern, is a rare and ancient plant species found in specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that emerge from a central rhizome. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often near water sources in tropical rainforests. Its reproductive structures are unique, featuring spore-producing cones that are protected by specialized leaf-like bracts.

Due to habitat loss, Lundia virginalis is considered endangered, making conservation efforts crucial for its survival.
