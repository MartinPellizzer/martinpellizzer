Limnochloa articulata

Limnochloa articulata, commonly known as the water bamboo or swamp bamboo, is a perennial aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, forming dense clusters with long, narrow leaves. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that helps stabilize sediments and prevent erosion. Its flowering stems produce small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged or just at the water's surface.

Limnochloa articulata is valued for its ability to improve water quality by filtering pollutants and providing habitat for aquatic life.
