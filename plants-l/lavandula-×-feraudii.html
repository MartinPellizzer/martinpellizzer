Lavandula × feraudii

Lavandula × feraudii, commonly known as the French lavender, is a hybrid shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems and gray-green, aromatic leaves that emit a distinctive lavender scent. The plant produces spikes of purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Lavandula × feraudii is valued for its ornamental appeal and use in essential oils and aromatherapy.
