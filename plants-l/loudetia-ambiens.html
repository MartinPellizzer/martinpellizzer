Loudetia ambiens

Loudetia ambiens, commonly known as the African feather grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and delicate, feathery inflorescences that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a blue-green hue that adds visual interest.

Loudetia ambiens is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
