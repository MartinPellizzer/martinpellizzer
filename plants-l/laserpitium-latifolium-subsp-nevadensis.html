Laserpitium latifolium subsp. nevadensis

Laserpitium latifolium subsp.

nevadensis, commonly known as the Nevada larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It typically grows in alpine and subalpine meadows, where it thrives in cool, moist environments.

The plant features showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a distinct hairy texture.

It is characterized by its slender stem and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.
