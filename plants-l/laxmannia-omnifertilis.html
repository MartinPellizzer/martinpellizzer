Laxmannia omnifertilis

Laxmannia omnifertilis, commonly known as the many-seeded saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Eurasia.

It belongs to the family Saxifragaceae and is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in late summer, and its leaves are typically succulent and arranged in a rosette. L. omnifertilis is adapted to cold, rocky environments and is often found in tundra and mountainous habitats.

It is a perennial plant that plays a role in stabilizing soil in its harsh native environment.
