Lobelia cardinalis var. multiflora

Lobelia cardinalis var. multiflora, commonly known as the cardinal flower, is a strikingly colorful plant native to the eastern United States.

It features vibrant red flowers that are tubular in shape, designed to attract hummingbirds and other pollinators. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting native wildlife.
