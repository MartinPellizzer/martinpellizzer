Lepanthes pumila

Lepanthes pumila, commonly known as the dwarf orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the humid forests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and green. The plant has a short, stocky stem and produces small, oval-shaped leaves. Lepanthes pumila thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found growing on the forest floor.

Its botanical features make it a unique and sought-after specimen among orchid enthusiasts.
