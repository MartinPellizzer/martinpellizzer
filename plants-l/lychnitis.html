Lychnitis

Lychnitis, commonly known as the nightshade, is a genus of flowering plants in the Solanaceae family.

These plants are characterized by their showy, often colorful flowers and typically have alternate, simple leaves. They are mostly herbaceous, though some species may be shrubs or vines. Lychnitis species are native to various regions, including North America, Europe, and Asia.

Many species within this genus are known for their toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.
