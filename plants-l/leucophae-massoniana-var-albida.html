Leucophae massoniana var. albida

Leucophae massoniana var. albida, commonly known as the white-flowered magnolia, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in early spring, often with a subtle pinkish tinge. The plant is a deciduous tree or shrub, typically reaching heights of 5 to 10 meters, with smooth, grey bark and broad, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers emit a sweet, fragrant aroma, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and parks for its ornamental value.
