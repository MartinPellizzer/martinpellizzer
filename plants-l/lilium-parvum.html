Lilium parvum

Lilium parvum, commonly known as the dwarf lily, is a species of flowering plant in the Liliaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of western North America, particularly in the Pacific Northwest. This plant typically grows to a height of 10 to 30 centimeters, featuring slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers. The bulbs of Lilium parvum are used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported soothing properties.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.
