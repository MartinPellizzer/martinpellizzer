Leobordea bracteosa

Leobordea bracteosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, typically blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Leobordea bracteosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive foliage.
