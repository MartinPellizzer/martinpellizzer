Lilium apertum var. thibeticum

Lilium apertum var. thibeticum, commonly known as the Tibetan lily, is a variant of the broadleaf lily native to the high altitudes of the Tibetan Plateau.

It is characterized by its striking, large, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, arid environments. Its leaves are long and narrow, often appearing in a rosette pattern at the base.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in alpine gardens.
