Lindenia rivalis f. glabra

Lindenia rivalis f. glabra, commonly known as the smooth lindenia, is a rare and distinctive plant species within the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its glabrous, or hairless, foliage, which sets it apart from other members of the species. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils, often in shaded forest environments. Its leaves are elliptical in shape and have a dark green, glossy appearance.

The flowers are small, white, and clustered in inflorescences, contributing to its ornamental value in botanical gardens.
