Lycopodium nesselianum

Lycopodium nesselianum, commonly known as the Nessel's clubmoss, is a small evergreen shrub belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and the presence of small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces spore-bearing cones, which are typically clustered at the base of the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Lycopodium nesselianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological systems.
