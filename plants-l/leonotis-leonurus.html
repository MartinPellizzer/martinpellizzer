Leonotis leonurus, commonly known as the lion's tail, is a flowering plant native to Africa and the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its striking, tubular, violet-blue flowers that bloom in whorls along the stems. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.
