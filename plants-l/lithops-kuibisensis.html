Lithops kuibisensis

Lithops kuibisensis, commonly known as the Kuibisensis Stone Plant, is a succulent native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, rock-like appearance, with two closely fused, fleshy leaves that resemble stones. The plant produces small, pale green or yellow flowers during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in succulent collections.
