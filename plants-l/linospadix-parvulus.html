Linospadix parvulus

Linospadix parvulus, commonly known as the small-spored spathiphyllum, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It features a slender, upright stem with a single, lance-shaped leaf that is often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, clustered in a spadix-like inflorescence. Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered endangered and is often found in protected areas.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and role in its ecological niche.
