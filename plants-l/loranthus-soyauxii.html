Loranthus soyauxii

Loranthus soyauxii, commonly known as the African mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that attaches itself to the branches of its host trees.

It belongs to the family Loranthaceae and is native to various regions in Africa. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers, which are often clustered together. Its berries are typically red or orange and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

Loranthus soyauxii plays an important ecological role by providing food for certain wildlife species.
