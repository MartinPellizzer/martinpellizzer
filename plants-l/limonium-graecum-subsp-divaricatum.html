Limonium graecum subsp. divaricatum

Limonium graecum subsp.

divaricatum, commonly known as the Narrowleaf sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in coastal areas, sandy soils, and rocky habitats, showcasing its adaptability to saline environments. Its leaves are linear and often recurved, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in well-drained conditions.
