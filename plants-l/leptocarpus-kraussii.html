Leptocarpus kraussii

Leptocarpus kraussii, commonly known as the dwarf sea hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dune environments. It has a shallow root system that helps stabilize coastal dunes.

Leptocarpus kraussii is well adapted to salt spray and is an important component of coastal vegetation.
