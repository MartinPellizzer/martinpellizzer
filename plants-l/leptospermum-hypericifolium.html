Leptospermum hypericifolium

Leptospermum hypericifolium, commonly known as the coastal tea tree, is a small evergreen shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that are often covered in fine white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in coastal environments and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils.

Its essential oils are valued for their aromatic and medicinal properties.
