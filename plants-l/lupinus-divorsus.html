Lupinus divorsus

Lupinus divorsus, commonly known as the desert lupine, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds.

Lupinus divorsus is adapted to harsh, dry environments and plays a role in nitrogen fixation, improving soil fertility in its native habitat.
