Lonchocarpus rubiginosus

Lonchocarpus rubiginosus, commonly known as the red-stemmed senna, is a tropical shrub belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces distinctive, woody pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is native to regions of Central and South America.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in warm climates.
