Loudetia perrieri

Loudetia perrieri, commonly known as the Perrier's lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Loudetia perrieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
