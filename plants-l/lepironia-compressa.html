Lepironia compressa

Lepironia compressa, commonly known as the giant waterweed, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, slender stems that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are often submerged or floating on the water surface. Lepironia compressa thrives in aquatic environments, such as marshes and slow-moving rivers, and is known for its ability to form dense vegetation.

Its leaves are narrow and ribbon-like, contributing to its distinctive appearance in wetland ecosystems.
