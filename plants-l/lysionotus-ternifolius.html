Lysionotus ternifolius

Lysionotus ternifolius, commonly known as the three-leaved sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has trifoliate leaves, which are divided into three leaflets, giving it its common name.

Lysionotus ternifolius is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its bright blooms and compact growth habit.
