Lucuma maclayana

Lucuma maclayana, commonly known as the Andean starfruit, is a species of flowering plant native to the highlands of South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its distinctive, star-shaped fruit that resembles a small, yellowish star. The plant typically grows in cool, humid climates at elevations above 2,000 meters. Its fruit is rich in nutrients and has been traditionally used in local cuisines for its sweet, citrus-like flavor.

The botanical features of Lucuma maclayana make it a unique and valuable plant in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
