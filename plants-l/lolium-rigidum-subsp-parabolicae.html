Lolium rigidum subsp. parabolicae

Lolium rigidum subsp.

parabolicae, commonly known as the parabolic bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia. It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with two to three florets per spikelet. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and estuarine environments.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological importance in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native fauna.
