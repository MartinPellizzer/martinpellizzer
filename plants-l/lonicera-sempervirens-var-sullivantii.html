Lonicera sempervirens var. sullivantii

Lonicera sempervirens var. sullivantii, commonly known as the Sullivant honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of yellow to orange, which bloom in early spring. Its fruit is a red, fleshy drupe that ripens in late summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade to full sun.
