Lauro-cerasus officinalis f. camelliifolia

Lauro-cerasus officinalis f. camelliifolia, commonly known as the Camellia-like cherry laurel, is a hybrid plant that combines features of both cherry and laurel species.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It is typically cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.

This variety is well-suited for gardens and can thrive in a variety of soil types with adequate drainage.
