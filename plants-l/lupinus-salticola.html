Lupinus salticola

Lupinus salticola, commonly known as the jumping larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces seed pods that burst open when mature, dispersing seeds forcefully. Lupinus salticola is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soils.

It is often found in open grasslands and along rocky slopes.
