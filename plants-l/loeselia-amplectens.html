Loeselia amplectens

Loeselia amplectens, commonly known as the clinging bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, and it is known for its ability to tolerate drought conditions.

Loeselia amplectens is primarily valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
