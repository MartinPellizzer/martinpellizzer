Lepidium bonariense var. suborbiculatum

Lepidium bonariense var. suborbiculatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf wallcress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, elongated leaves that are often slightly hairy. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in racemes, typical of the mustard family. Its seeds are small and oval, with a distinctive pungent odor.

This variety is native to South America and is often found in disturbed soils and rocky areas.
