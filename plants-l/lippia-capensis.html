Lippia capensis

Lippia capensis, commonly known as Cape spearmint, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is valued for its essential oils used in perfumery and traditional medicine.

Its adaptability to arid conditions makes it a resilient species in its native range.
