Lasianthus oblongatus

Lasianthus oblongatus, commonly known as the oblong leaf lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Lasianthus oblongatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
