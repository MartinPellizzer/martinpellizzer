Lettowianthus

Lettowianthus, commonly known as the "tropical orchid," is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers that bloom in clusters. The plants typically have long, slender stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. Native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, Lettowianthus species thrive in humid, shady environments.

Due to their ornamental value, they are cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses around the world.
