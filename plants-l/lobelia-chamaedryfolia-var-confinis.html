Lobelia chamaedryfolia var. confinis

Lobelia chamaedryfolia var. confinis, commonly known as the rock lobelia, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant features small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
