Lycium chilense var. normale

Lycium chilense var. normale, commonly known as the Chilean boxthorn, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, reddish-orange berries that are edible when ripe. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to harsh climates. Its leaves are typically narrow and evergreen, providing year-round foliage.

It is often used in landscaping due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
