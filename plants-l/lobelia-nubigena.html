Lobelia nubigena

Lobelia nubigena, commonly known as the cloud-lobelia, is a perennial herb native to the high elevations of the Andes Mountains in South America.

It is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, often appearing in clusters. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically narrow and lance-shaped. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists alike.
