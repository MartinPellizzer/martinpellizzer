Lilium zairei

Lilium zairei, commonly known as the Zaire lily, is a striking species native to the Democratic Republic of the Congo.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, showy flowers that can reach up to 20 centimeters in diameter. The petals are usually white or pale pink with a distinctive red or orange stripe running down the center. This bulbous plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
