Lobelia lithophila

Lobelia lithophila, commonly known as the rock lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often found in limestone-rich areas.

Lobelia lithophila is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging environments.
