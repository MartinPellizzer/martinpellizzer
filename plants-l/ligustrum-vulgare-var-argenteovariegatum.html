Ligustrum vulgare var. argenteovariegatum

Ligustrum vulgare var. argenteovariegatum, commonly known as silver-leafed privet, is a cultivar of the privet shrub characterized by its striking variegated foliage.

Its leaves are typically a mix of green and silver-white, creating a visually appealing contrast. The plant is native to Europe and has been widely cultivated in ornamental horticulture for its attractive appearance. It produces small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, followed by dark blue berries that are edible for birds.

This variety is often used as a hedge or screen due to its dense growth habit and adaptability to various soil conditions.
