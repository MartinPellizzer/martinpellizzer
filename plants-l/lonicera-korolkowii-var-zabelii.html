Lonicera korolkowii var. zabelii

Lonicera korolkowii var. zabelii, commonly known as the Zabel's honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange, blooming in early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and it produces small, red berries that are edible for birds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

This variety is a hybrid between Lonicera korolkowii and Lonicera maackii, showcasing genetic diversity within the honeysuckle genus.
