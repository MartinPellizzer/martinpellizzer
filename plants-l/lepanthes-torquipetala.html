Lepanthes torquipetala

Lepanthes torquipetala, commonly known as the velvet tailed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its unique, velvety purple petals and a distinctive, recurved lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments, often on tree trunks or among leaf litter. Its flowers are small but striking, with a complex structure that facilitates pollination by specific insect species.

Lepanthes torquipetala is valued for its ornamental beauty and contributes to the biodiversity of its native ecosystems.
