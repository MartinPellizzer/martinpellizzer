Litsea beei

Litsea beei, commonly known as Chinese sweet pepper or Chinese bay leaf, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like cinnamon and avocado. The plant is characterized by its simple, opposite, and often aromatic leaves, which are usually dark green and leathery in texture. Its small white flowers are clustered in umbels, and it produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe.

Litsea beei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cuisines.
