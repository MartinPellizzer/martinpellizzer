Lilium callosum var. stenophyllum

Lilium callosum var. stenophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved fritillary, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive pattern of purple or red markings. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged in a fan shape, and it produces a cluster of small, bell-shaped flowers at the top of its stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
