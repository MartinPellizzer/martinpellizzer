Lachenalia succulenta

Lachenalia succulenta, commonly known as the rock lily, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces striking, tubular flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically during the summer months. It belongs to the Amaryllis family and is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance.

Lachenalia succulenta prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
