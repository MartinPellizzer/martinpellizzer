Lilium pardalinum var. giganteum

Lilium pardalinum var. giganteum, commonly known as the giant tiger lily, is a striking perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from orange to red, often with a distinctive tiger-like pattern. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet, featuring long, strap-like leaves and a robust root system. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
