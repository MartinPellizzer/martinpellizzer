Liparis ferruginea

Liparis ferruginea, commonly known as the rust-colored orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often reddish-brown in color. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in a spike, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Liparis ferruginea is valued for its unique appearance and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
