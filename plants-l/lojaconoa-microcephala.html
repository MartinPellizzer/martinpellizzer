Lojaconoa microcephala

Lojaconoa microcephala, commonly known as the dwarf lojaconoa, is a member of the Malvaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub characterized by its small, rounded leaves and clusters of showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, hard seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
