Lepanthes labiata

Lepanthes labiata, commonly known as the pink lady's tresses, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and its striking, pinkish-white inflorescence that resembles a tresses of hair. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowers are highly fragrant and attract various pollinators, including bees and moths.

Lepanthes labiata is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological significance.
