Liquidambar poilanei

Liquidambar poilanei, commonly known as the Vietnamese sweetgum, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly Vietnam.

It belongs to the genus Liquidambar in the family Aceraceae, which includes other sweetgum species. The tree is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves that turn vibrant yellow in autumn. Its fruit is a spiky, three-lobed capsule that contains three winged seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential ecological and medicinal uses.
