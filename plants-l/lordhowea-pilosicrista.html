Lordhowea pilosicrista

Lordhowea pilosicrista, commonly known as the Lord Howe's tree fern, is a distinctive tropical plant native to the subtropical regions of the Pacific.

It features a tall, unbranched trunk covered in a dense, fibrous layer of old fronds, which give it a unique, almost tree-like appearance. The plant produces large, compound leaves that can reach several meters in length, with a feathery, delicate structure. Its reproductive system involves the production of spores rather than seeds, a characteristic typical of ferns.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid forests.
