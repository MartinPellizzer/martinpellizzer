Leucopogon riparius

Leucopogon riparius, commonly known as the river heath, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It typically grows in wetland and riparian habitats, often found along riverbanks and in swampy areas. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often found in dense, low vegetation.

Leucopogon riparius plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
