Linaria polygalifolia subsp. aguillonensis

Linaria polygalifolia subsp.

aguillonensis, commonly known as the Aguillon's toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny locations. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique floral structure and limited distribution.
