Leontodon hastilis var. arenarius

Leontodon hastilis var. arenarius, commonly known as the sand bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in open habitats such as dunes or coastal areas. The plant produces yellow daisy-like flowers with a distinctive central disc and ray florets. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it is often found in disturbed or marginal environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils.
