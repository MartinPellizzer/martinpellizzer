Ligularia lapathifolia

Ligularia lapathifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern Asia.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves that are often mottled with dark green and yellow patterns. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, arranged in a cluster at the top of the stem. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its foliage and floral structure make it a striking addition to gardens with the right conditions.
