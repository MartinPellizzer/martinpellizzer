Lannea humilis

Lannea humilis, commonly known as the African sand pear, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often hairy and have a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns from green to yellow or red when mature.

Lannea humilis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
