Lycoris josephinae

Lycoris josephinae, commonly known as the Josephine lily, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features narrow, strap-like leaves that emerge from a bulbous underground stem. Its flowers are typically borne on one side of the stem, creating an asymmetrical appearance.

Lycoris josephinae is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine.
