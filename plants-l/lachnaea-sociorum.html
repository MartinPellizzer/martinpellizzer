Lachnaea sociorum

Lachnaea sociorum, commonly known as the common lachnaceus, is a small flowering plant belonging to the family Lachnaceae.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant produces tiny, elongated fruits called achenes, which are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Lachnaea sociorum is primarily valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
