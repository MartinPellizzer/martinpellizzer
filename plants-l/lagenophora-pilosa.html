Lagenophora pilosa

Lagenophora pilosa, commonly known as the hairy caladium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, hairy texture, giving it a unique appearance. The plant belongs to the Araceae family, which includes other ornamental plants like peace lilies and calla lilies. Its foliage is the main ornamental feature, as it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

Lagenophora pilosa thrives in humid, shaded environments and is popular as a houseplant due to its vibrant leaf colors.
