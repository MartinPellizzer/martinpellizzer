Leucadendron odoratum

Leucadendron odoratum, commonly known as the sweet cycad, is a striking plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its tall, unbranched stem and large, feather-like leaves. The plant produces distinctive cone-like structures that contain both male and female flowers, a feature typical of cycads. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is valued in both horticulture and conservation efforts.
