Lonicera tatarica f. angustata

Lonicera tatarica f. angustata, commonly known as the narrowleaf honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, often arching stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in early spring, followed by red, fleshy berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a versatile addition to gardens.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for hedging and ornamental landscaping.
