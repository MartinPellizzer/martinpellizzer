Lactuca pseudoabyssinica

Lactuca pseudoabyssinica, commonly known as Abyssinian lettuce, is a species of flowering plant in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is native to Ethiopia and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are typically dark green with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is often cultivated for its edible leaves, which have a slightly bitter taste.

Lactuca pseudoabyssinica is adapted to arid environments and can thrive in poor soils.
