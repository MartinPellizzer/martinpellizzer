Lupinus albus subsp. termis

Lupinus albus subsp.

termis, commonly known as the Termis white lupin, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers.

The plant has pinnately compound leaves with numerous small leaflets, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability and historical use in agriculture.
