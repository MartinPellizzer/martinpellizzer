Lilium catesbaei

Lilium catesbaei, commonly known as the Turk's cap lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, red-and-white striped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features strap-like, evergreen leaves. Its bulbs are perennial, and the plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Lilium catesbaei is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
