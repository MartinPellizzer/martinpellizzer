Limonium penicillatum

Limonium penicillatum, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and saline soils, showing a high tolerance for salt and wind. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Limonium penicillatum is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
