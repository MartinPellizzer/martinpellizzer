Lavandula stoechas var. pedunculata

Lavandula stoechas var. pedunculata, commonly known as the Spanish lavender or the purple lavender, is a variant of the common lavender species.

It is characterized by its striking, violet-pink flower spikes that rise above the foliage, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant has narrow, grey-green leaves that are aromatic and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is also valued for its fragrance and is often used in herbal remedies and aromatherapy.
