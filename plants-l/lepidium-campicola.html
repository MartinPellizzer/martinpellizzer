Lepidium campicola

Lepidium campicola, commonly known as the rock cress, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like mustard and cabbage. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It produces small, white or yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

Its leaves are usually basal, with a rough or hairy texture, and the plant is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature.
