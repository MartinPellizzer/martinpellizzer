Lathyrus jepsonii subsp. californicus

Lathyrus jepsonii subsp.

californicus, commonly known as the California larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, rocky soils in woodland areas.

The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately compound, and have a distinctive, fern-like appearance.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or pea family, and is characterized by its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with soil bacteria.
