Lavoisiera campos-portoana

Lavoisiera campos-portoana, commonly known as the Brazilian violet, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a member of the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments and has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. It produces small, fragrant blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a threatened species in its native range.
