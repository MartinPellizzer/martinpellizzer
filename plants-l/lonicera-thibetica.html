Lonicera thibetica

Lonicera thibetica, commonly known as the Tibetan honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of China, Tibet, and Mongolia.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are usually yellow or orange in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its flowering stems are often covered in fine hairs, and the fruit is a red, fleshy capsule that contains small, black seeds.

Lonicera thibetica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
