Lathyrus hierosolymitanus

Lathyrus hierosolymitanus, commonly known as the Jerusalem glory flower, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of southern Europe.

It is a climbing legume that produces showy, pea-like flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for trellises and garden borders.

The seeds of Lathyrus hierosolymitanus are also used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
