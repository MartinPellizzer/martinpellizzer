Libanus thurifer

Libanus thurifer, commonly known as the sacred lotus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

The sacred lotus is also valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in various cultures.
