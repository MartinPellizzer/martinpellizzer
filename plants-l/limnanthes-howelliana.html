Limnanthes howelliana

Limnanthes howelliana, commonly known as the Howell's sunflower, is a herbaceous annual plant native to California.

It is characterized by its yellow, daisy-like flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in coastal scrub and grassland habitats.

Limnanthes howelliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
