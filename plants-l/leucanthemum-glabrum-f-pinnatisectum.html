Leucanthemum glabrum f. pinnatisectum

Leucanthemum glabrum f. pinnatisectum, commonly known as the pinnatisected white marguerite, is a cultivar of the white marguerite plant.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves that give it a delicate, airy appearance. The plant produces small, daisy-like white flowers with yellow centers, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
