Linaria tristis subsp. mesatlantica

Linaria tristis subsp.

mesatlantica, commonly known as the Narrowleaf toadflax, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces striking yellow-orange flowers with two lips, typical of the Scrophulariaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in some areas.
