Linum alpinum subsp. gracilius

Linum alpinum subsp.

gracilius, commonly known as alpine flax, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its slender stems and lance-shaped leaves, which are typically covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces showy blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
