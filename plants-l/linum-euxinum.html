Linum euxinum

Linum euxinum, commonly known as the Crimean flax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces striking blue or purple flowers with five petals, which bloom in early summer. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in dry, rocky habitats.

Linum euxinum is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in textile production.
