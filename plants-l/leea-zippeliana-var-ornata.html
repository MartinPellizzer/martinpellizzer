Leea zippeliana var. ornata

Leea zippeliana var. ornata, commonly known as the ornate leea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Leeaceae and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, white to yellowish flowers that are clustered in cymes, often with a faint fragrance. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in shaded, moist environments such as forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
