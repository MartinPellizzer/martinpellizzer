Layia glandulosa subsp. typica

Layia glandulosa subsp.

typica, commonly known as the sticky layia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands. Layia glandulosa subsp.

typica is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in its native range.
