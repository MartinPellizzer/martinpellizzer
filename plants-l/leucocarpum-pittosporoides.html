Leucocarpum pittosporoides

Leucocarpum pittosporoides, commonly known as the white-flowered pittosporum, is a small evergreen shrub native to New Zealand.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are oval in shape and have a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, followed by spherical, white berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a suitable ornamental plant in temperate climates.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native wildlife.
