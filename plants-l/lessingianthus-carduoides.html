Lessingianthus carduoides

Lessingianthus carduoides, commonly known as the carduetta, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features upright, branching stems and is covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while the flowers are small and yellow, arranged in dense clusters.

It thrives in dry, sunny conditions and is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.
