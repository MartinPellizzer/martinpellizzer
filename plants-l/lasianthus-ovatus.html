Lasianthus ovatus

Lasianthus ovatus, commonly known as the oval leaf lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its flowers are often pale blue or violet, attracting various pollinators.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
