Leandra alterninervia

Leandra alterninervia, commonly known as the pink velvet tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, velvety leaves that are dark green on the upper surface and pale pink on the underside. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible, yellowish fruits with a sweet and sour flavor. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in local medicine and cuisine.
