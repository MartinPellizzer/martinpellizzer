Lappa minor subsp. pubens

Lappa minor subsp.

pubens, commonly known as the hairy bladder campion, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, pinkish-white flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine pubescence, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
