Lathyrus brachypterus var. brachypterus

Lathyrus brachypterus var. brachypterus, commonly known as the dwarf sweet pea, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical legume morphology, including compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pea-like flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental and ecologically beneficial plants.

Its growth habit is compact, making it suitable for rock gardens, borders, or as a ground cover.
