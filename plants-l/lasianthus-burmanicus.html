Lasianthus burmanicus

Lasianthus burmanicus, commonly known as Burmese flameflower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture, while the flowers are clustered in whorls at the ends of the stems.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
