Launaea pseudoabyssinica

Launaea pseudoabyssinica, commonly known as the Abyssinian dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant is known for its robust, hairy stems and alternate leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It produces yellow flowers that develop into fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds, aiding in its propagation across dry environments.

Launaea pseudoabyssinica is adapted to thrive in poor soils and can withstand periods of drought.
