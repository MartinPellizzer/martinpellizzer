Lebeckia multiflora

Lebeckia multiflora, commonly known as the many-flowered lebeckia, is a perennial flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its showy, daisy-like flowers are typically white or pale yellow, with a central disk surrounded by ray florets.

Lebeckia multiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is adapted to well-drained, sunny conditions.
