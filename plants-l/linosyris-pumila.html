Linosyris pumila

Linosyris pumila, commonly known as the dwarf linosyris, is a low-growing annual plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically reaches a height of only a few centimeters, with small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for rocky or sandy habitats.

Linosyris pumila is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to withstand dry and saline conditions.
