Lycium retusum

Lycium retusum, commonly known as the rough boxthorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces red berries that are edible for birds and small mammals, contributing to its role in local ecosystems. Lycium retusum thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along coastal regions.

Its hardy nature makes it a resilient plant in challenging climates.
