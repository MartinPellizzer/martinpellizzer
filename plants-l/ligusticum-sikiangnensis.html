Ligusticum sikiangnensis

Ligusticum sikiangnensis, commonly known as the Sichuan lovage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescences. The plant features a robust rhizome and lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive pungent odor. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in compound umbels, and it blooms in late spring to early summer.

Ligusticum sikiangnensis is valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
