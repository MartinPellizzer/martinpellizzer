Lycopodium × sabinifolium f. decipiens

Lycopodium × sabinifolium f. decipiens, commonly known as the false holly fern, is a fern species characterized by its distinctive fronds and spore-bearing structures.

It belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae and is native to certain regions of the southeastern United States. The plant exhibits a unique morphology with elongated, needle-like leaves that resemble those of the genus Sabina, hence its common name. Its sporophytes are typically small and clustered, with spores produced in specialized structures called sporangia.

The plant's botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in both horticulture and evolutionary botany.
