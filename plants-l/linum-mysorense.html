Linum mysorense

Linum mysorense, commonly known as the Kashmir flax, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or white flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in cool, temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Linum mysorense is valued both for its ornamental beauty and for its historical use in textile production.
