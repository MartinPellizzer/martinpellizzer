Leptomeria aphylla

Leptomeria aphylla, commonly known as the leafless leptomeria, is a unique member of the Rubiaceae family.

It is characterized by its lack of leaves, with only small, scale-like structures present along its stems. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is typically found in arid or rocky habitats, thriving in regions with low rainfall.

Due to its specialized morphology, it is often adapted to conserve water and withstand harsh environmental conditions.
