Leucanthemum pulverulentum

Leucanthemum pulverulentum, commonly known as the dusty leucanthemum, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features grayish-white foliage covered in fine, powdery hairs, giving it its common name. It produces yellow-centered white flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Leucanthemum pulverulentum is often found in dry meadows and rocky habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.
