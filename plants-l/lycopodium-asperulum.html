Lycopodium asperulum

Lycopodium asperulum, commonly known as the rough clubmoss, is a small evergreen fern-like plant native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky crevices. The plant has a creeping stem that produces spore-bearing cones at the tips of its branches. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and covered with tiny, rough scales that give it its common name.

Lycopodium asperulum plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for small invertebrates.
