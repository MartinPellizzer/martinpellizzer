Licuala rheophytica

Licuala rheophytica, commonly known as the water lily palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found near streams or in wetlands. Its leaves are typically a deep green with a waxy surface, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and aquatic settings.
