Lobelia enantophylla

Lobelia enantophylla, commonly known as the zigzag lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and swampy habitats. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Lobelia enantophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and unique floral structure.
