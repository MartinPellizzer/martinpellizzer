Lycopodium laterale

Lycopodium laterale, commonly known as the clubmoss, is a small, evergreen plant that belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its spore-bearing cones and its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments. The plant has a unique structure with spirally arranged leaves and a strobilus that produces spores. Lycopodium laterale is often found in forest floors and rocky areas, where it plays a role in soil stabilization.

Its ancient lineage dates back to the Carboniferous period, making it a living fossil with significant ecological and evolutionary importance.
