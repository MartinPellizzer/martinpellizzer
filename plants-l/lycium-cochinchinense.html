Lycium cochinchinense

Lycium cochinchinense, commonly known as Chinese boxthorn, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshade plants. The plant is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer. Its berries, which are red when mature, are edible and often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appearance and medicinal properties.
