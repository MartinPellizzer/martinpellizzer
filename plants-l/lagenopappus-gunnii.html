Lagenopappus gunnii

Lagenopappus gunnii, commonly known as the New Zealand dandelion, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and wind-dispersed seeds. The plant features basal leaves that are broadly ovate with hairy surfaces, and its flowers are typically yellow with a central disc. It thrives in moist, temperate environments and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain regions.
