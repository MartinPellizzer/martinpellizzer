Leucas chinensis

Leucas chinensis, commonly known as Chinese leucas, is a flowering plant native to China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and traditional uses.
