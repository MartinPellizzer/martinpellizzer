Lagenaria vulgaris var. microcarpa

Lagenaria vulgaris var. microcarpa, commonly known as the bottle gourd, is a flowering plant in the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to Africa and is characterized by its elongated, smooth, green fruit that can be harvested at various stages of maturity. The plant produces yellow flowers and has a vigorous, trailing growth habit. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and deeply lobed.

The fruit is used in culinary and traditional medicinal applications across various cultures.
