Lucuma ferruginea

Lucuma ferruginea, commonly known as the orange lucuma, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly Peru and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its broad, waxy leaves and produces yellow-orange fruits with a sweet, nutty flavor. The fruit's pulp is edible and often used in culinary applications, while the seeds are typically discarded.

Lucuma ferruginea is valued for its ornamental appeal and unique fruit, making it a popular choice in both gardens and local markets.
