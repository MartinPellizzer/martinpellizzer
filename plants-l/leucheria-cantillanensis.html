Leucheria cantillanensis

Leucheria cantillanensis, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling small bells. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or stream sides. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a smooth texture.

Leucheria cantillanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
