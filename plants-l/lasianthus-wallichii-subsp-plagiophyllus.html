Lasianthus wallichii subsp. plagiophyllus

Lasianthus wallichii subsp.

plagiophyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale blue or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in horticulture.
