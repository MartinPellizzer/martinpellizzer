Leucosyke aspera

Leucosyke aspera, commonly known as the rough leucosyke, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features wiry, branching stems covered in stiff, rough hairs, giving it its common name. Its leaves are lance-shaped and also covered in a similar rough texture.

It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators.
