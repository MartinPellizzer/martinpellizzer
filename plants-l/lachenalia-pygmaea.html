Lachenalia pygmaea

Lachenalia pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf lachenalia, is a small bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and attractive, often colorful flowers. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and blooms in spring with star-shaped flowers that come in shades of yellow, orange, and red. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Lachenalia pygmaea is popular among rock garden enthusiasts due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
