Linum munbyanum var. tenuifolium

Linum munbyanum var. tenuifolium, commonly known as slender-leaved flax, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces delicate blue or purple flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
