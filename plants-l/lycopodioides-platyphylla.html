Lycopodioides platyphylla

Lycopodioides platyphylla, commonly known as the flat-leaf clubmoss, is a small evergreen fern-like plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its creeping rhizome and small, scale-like leaves that form dense mats on the forest floor. The plant produces sporangia in clusters called strobili, which are typically found at the base of the leaves. Lycopodioides platyphylla thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate and subtropical regions.

Its simple structure and ancient lineage make it an important subject in the study of early plant evolution.
