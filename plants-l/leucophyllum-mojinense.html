Leucophyllum mojinense

Leucophyllum mojinense, commonly known as the Mojave rosemary, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the plantain family (Plantaginaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, needle-like leaves and clusters of small, showy flowers. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments. Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties, with traditional uses in herbal remedies.
