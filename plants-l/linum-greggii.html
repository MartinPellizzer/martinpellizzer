Linum greggii

Linum greggii, commonly known as Gregg's flax, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, blue to purple flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oblong seeds. Linum greggii is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils and desert landscapes.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
