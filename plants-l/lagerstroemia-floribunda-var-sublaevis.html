Lagerstroemia floribunda var. sublaevis

Lagerstroemia floribunda var. sublaevis, commonly known as the smooth-flowered crape myrtle, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern China.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark and clusters of showy, panicle flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly waxy texture. This variety is prized for its ornamental value, particularly in the spring and summer when it blooms profusely.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
