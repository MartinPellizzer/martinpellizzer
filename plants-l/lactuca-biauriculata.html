Lactuca biauriculata

Lactuca biauriculata, commonly known as the two-lobed lettuce, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves with distinct lobes at the base. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It is native to Central Asia and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Lactuca biauriculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its edible leaves in some regions.
