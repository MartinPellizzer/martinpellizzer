Lychnis apetala var. uralensis

Lychnis apetala var. uralensis, commonly known as the Ural mouse-ear, is a perennial herb native to the cold regions of Siberia and the Russian Far East.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and features small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically rounded and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or subarctic environments.

This plant is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and unique floral display.
