Lavoisiera punctata

Lavoisiera punctata, commonly known as the spotted lily, is a herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, spotted leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and thrives in partial to full shade. Its foliage is often described as having a velvety texture, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

Lavoisiera punctata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local pollinators.
