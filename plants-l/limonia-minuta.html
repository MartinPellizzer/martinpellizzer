Limonia minuta

Limonia minuta, commonly known as the dwarf lime or little lime, is a small evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its fragrant, small, yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round, yellow-orange fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed.

Limonia minuta is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in coastal environments.
