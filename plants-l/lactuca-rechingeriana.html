Lactuca rechingeriana

Lactuca rechingeriana, commonly known as the Rechinger's lettuce, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family, Asteraceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its upright growth habit and basal rosette of leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are generally lance-shaped and have a slightly bitter taste, similar to other lettuces.

Lactuca rechingeriana is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
