Lepyrodia fortunata

Lepyrodia fortunata, commonly known as the golden sun-orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to Australia.

It is characterized by its vibrant golden-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, often in open habitats such as woodlands and heaths. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while the inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of showy, tubular flowers.

Lepyrodia fortunata is a short-lived perennial, often found in regions with a Mediterranean climate.
