Lawsonia alba

Lawsonia alba, commonly known as henna, is a shrub native to northern Africa, the Middle East, and southern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and produces small, yellowish flowers. The plant's most notable feature is its resinous sap, which is harvested and used to make a dye. This sap, known as henna, has been historically used for body art, hair styling, and as a natural remedy.

The botanical properties of Lawsonia alba make it a valuable plant in both traditional and modern applications.
