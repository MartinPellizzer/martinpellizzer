Libocedrus decurrens f. compacta

Libocedrus decurrens f. compacta, commonly known as the dwarf incense cedar, is a compact, shrub-like form of the incense cedar.

It is native to the western United States, particularly California and Oregon, where it thrives in coastal and mountainous regions. The plant features small, scale-like leaves that are dark green and densely packed, giving it a dense, bushy appearance. Its cones are small and woody, typically maturing to a reddish-brown color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate a range of soil conditions.
