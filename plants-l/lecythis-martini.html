Lecythis martini

Lecythis martini, commonly known as the Amazonian peach or yellow starfruit, is a tropical tree native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the family Lecythidaceae and is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and showy yellow flowers. The tree produces a unique fruit with a thick, spiky rind and a sweet, aromatic pulp that is edible. Its seeds are encased in a hard, woody sarcocarp, which is often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
