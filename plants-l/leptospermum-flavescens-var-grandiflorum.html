Leptospermum flavescens var. grandiflorum

Leptospermum flavescens var. grandiflorum, commonly known as the golden tea tree, is a shrub native to Tasmania, Australia.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish-green leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is part of the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea tree species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and dry forest areas.

The leaves are used in traditional herbal medicine and are also popular in horticulture for their ornamental value.
