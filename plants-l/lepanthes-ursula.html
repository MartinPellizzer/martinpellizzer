Lepanthes ursula

Lepanthes ursula, commonly known as the Ursula's slipper orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that often resemble miniature slippers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as cloud forests, where it thrives on tree trunks or branches. Its flowers are usually white or pale green, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Lepanthes ursula is valued for its ornamental beauty and plays a role in the ecological balance of its native habitats.
