Lathyrus sativus var. parviflorus

Lathyrus sativus var. parviflorus, commonly known as the dwarf blue flax, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces small, blue to purple flowers clustered in racemes, typically blooming in early summer. The plant has trifoliate leaves and a fibrous root system, which helps it thrive in well-drained soils. It is cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.

However, it contains toxic alkaloids, which can be harmful if ingested in large quantities.
