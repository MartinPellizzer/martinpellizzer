Lonicera buxifolia

Lonicera buxifolia, commonly known as the boxleaf honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage that resembles boxwood, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its compact size and aesthetic appeal, it is often used in landscaping and as a hedge plant.
