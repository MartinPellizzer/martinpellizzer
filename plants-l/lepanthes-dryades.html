Lepanthes dryades

Lepanthes dryades, commonly known as the Dryad's Orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant features a delicate, sympodial growth habit with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are usually pale green or white, with a distinctive, intricate structure.

Lepanthes dryades is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
