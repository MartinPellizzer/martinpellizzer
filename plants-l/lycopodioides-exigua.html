Lycopodioides exigua

Lycopodioides exigua, commonly known as the slender clubmoss, is a small, herbaceous plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and the presence of microphylls, which are small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces spores in specialized structures called strobila, which are typically clustered at the tip of the stems. Lycopodioides exigua is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas.

Its reproductive structures and overall morphology reflect its evolutionary adaptation to a wide range of terrestrial habitats.
