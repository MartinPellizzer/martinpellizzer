Luzula carpatica

Luzula carpatica, commonly known as Carpathian goat's beard, is a perennial flowering plant native to the forests of Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, commonly referred to as the grass family, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Luzula carpatica thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded woodland environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
