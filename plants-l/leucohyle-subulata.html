Leucohyle subulata

Leucohyle subulata, commonly known as the white leucohyle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white waxy coating, which helps to reduce water loss in its arid environment. The plant produces small, dainty white or pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
