Linum semitrigynum

Linum semitrigynum, commonly known as the lesser flax or narrow-flower flax, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and produces yellow flowers with five petals. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Linum semitrigynum is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
