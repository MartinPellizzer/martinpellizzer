Linaria chalepensis var. gibbosa

Linaria chalepensis var. gibbosa, commonly known as the Armenian toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey.

It is a perennial herb with erect, branching stems that often grow up to 30 cm in height. The plant produces bright yellow, spurred flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to thrive in dry, rocky habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
