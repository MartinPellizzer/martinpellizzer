Lonchocarpus michoacanicus

Lonchocarpus michoacanicus, commonly known as the Michoacan legume, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes and bean plants, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant is known for its distinctive yellow flowers and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it ecologically significant. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
