Lobelia flaccida var. scabripes

Lobelia flaccida var. scabripes, commonly known as the roughleaf lobelia, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features narrow, hairy leaves that are often reddish or purplish in color. Its flowers are small, tubular, and usually blue or purple, arranged in spikes.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
