Lathyrus sylvestris var. platyphyllos

Lathyrus sylvestris var. platyphyllos, commonly known as the broad-leaved wood lupin, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of pea-like flowers that range in color from pink to violet. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces small, bean-like pods that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and rocky areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological habitats.
