Litsea ellipticibacca

Litsea ellipticibacca, commonly known as the broadleaf litsea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves that are often glossy and have a distinct aromatic scent. It produces small, white flowers followed by edible, spherical berries that are used in traditional cuisine.

The tree can grow up to 10 meters tall and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
