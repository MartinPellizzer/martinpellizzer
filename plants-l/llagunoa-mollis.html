Llagunoa mollis

Llagunoa mollis, commonly known as the soft leaf llagunoa, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, often arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems. Llagunoa mollis thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude grasslands.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in plant ecology and ethnobotany.
