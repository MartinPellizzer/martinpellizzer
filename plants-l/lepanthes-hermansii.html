Lepanthes hermansii

Lepanthes hermansii, commonly known as the Hermann's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid with a delicate appearance, featuring a single, short stem and a few broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Lepanthes hermansii is considered a delicate and somewhat elusive species in the orchid family.
