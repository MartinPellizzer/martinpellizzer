Lepisanthes cauliflora var. glabrifolia

Lepisanthes cauliflora var. glabrifolia, commonly known as the smooth-leaved caladium, is a tropical plant characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with shades of white, pink, and red.

It is a member of the Araceae family and is native to the humid lowlands of Central and South America. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, with its leaves emerging from the base rather than the stem. Its foliage is the primary ornamental feature, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
