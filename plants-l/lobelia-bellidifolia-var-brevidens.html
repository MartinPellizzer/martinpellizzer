Lobelia bellidifolia var. brevidens

Lobelia bellidifolia var. brevidens, commonly known as the short-stemmed lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are short and branched.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
