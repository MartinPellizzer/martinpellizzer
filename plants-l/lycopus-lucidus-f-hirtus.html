Lycopus lucidus f. hirtus

Lycopus lucidus f. hirtus, commonly known as hairy water huckleberry, is a flowering plant in the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its specific epithet "hirtus.". The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks.

This subspecies is often found in the northeastern United States and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
