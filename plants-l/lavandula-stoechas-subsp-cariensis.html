Lavandula stoechas subsp. cariensis

Lavandula stoechas subsp.

cariensis, commonly known as the Spanish lavender, is a perennial shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its narrow, gray-green leaves and striking purple or violet flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in sunny, well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.

This subspecies is distinguished by its distinctive, spurred flower buds and its smaller size compared to other lavenders.
