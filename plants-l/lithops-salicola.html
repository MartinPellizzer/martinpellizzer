Lithops salicola

Lithops salicola, commonly known as the "leaf cholla," is a member of the Aizoaceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, rock-like appearance, with fleshy, succulent leaves that store water. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowers are small and usually yellow or orange, appearing in late spring.

Lithops salicola is a popular choice for rock gardens due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
