Lavandula antineae

Lavandula antineae, commonly known as the Armenian lavender, is a species of flowering plant in the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Armenia and Turkey, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant produces spikes of fragrant purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, with a strong aromatic scent.

Lavandula antineae is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
