Leucadendron fallax

Leucadendron fallax, commonly known as the silver leaf leucadendron, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and conical, yellow-orange flower heads. The plant belongs to the Proteaceae family and is well adapted to dry, sandy soils. It produces small, tubular flowers that attract birds and insects.

Leucadendron fallax is often used in ornamental gardening for its striking appearance and drought tolerance.
