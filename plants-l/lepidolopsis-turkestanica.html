Lepidolopsis turkestanica

Lepidolopsis turkestanica, commonly known as the Turkestan bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lanceolate and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Lepidolopsis turkestanica is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
