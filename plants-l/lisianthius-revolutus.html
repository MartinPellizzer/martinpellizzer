Lisianthius revolutus

Lisianthius revolutus, commonly known as the revolute lisianthius, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and features narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in shades of pink to purple, adding ornamental value to gardens.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.
