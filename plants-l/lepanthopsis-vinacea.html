Lepanthopsis vinacea

Lepanthopsis vinacea, commonly known as the red-tipped orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking red-tipped leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, tropical environments. Its botanical features include a sympodial growth habit and a distinctive floral structure with three sepals and three petals.

Lepanthopsis vinacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or warm climates.
