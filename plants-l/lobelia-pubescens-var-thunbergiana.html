Lobelia pubescens var. thunbergiana

Lobelia pubescens var. thunbergiana, commonly known as Thunberg's lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features hairy, upright stems and small, tubular blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine pubescence, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
