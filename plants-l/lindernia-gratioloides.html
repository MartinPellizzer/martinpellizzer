Lindernia gratioloides

Lindernia gratioloides, commonly known as the silverleaf lindernia, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Linderniaceae and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Lindernia gratioloides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
