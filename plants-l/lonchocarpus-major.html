Lonchocarpus major

Lonchocarpus major, commonly known as the giant senna, is a tropical legume native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and produces distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is notable for its large, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its rapid growth and nitrogen-fixing ability, it plays an important role in soil enrichment.
