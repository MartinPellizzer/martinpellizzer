Lobelia hotteana

Lobelia hotteana, commonly known as the hottean lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in terminal clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Lobelia hotteana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to add color to shaded garden spaces.
