Lobivia obrepanda

Lobivia obrepanda, commonly known as the "Coral Cactus," is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded shape and its dense clusters of bright red or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a short, thick stem with spiny, paddle-shaped leaves that help it retain moisture in harsh environments. Its flowers are tubular and often have a slight fragrance, attracting pollinators like bees and hummingbirds.

Lobivia obrepanda is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and striking appearance.
