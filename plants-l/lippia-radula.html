Lippia radula

Lippia radula, commonly known as Mexican mint or wild mint, is a perennial herb native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant leaves that emit a strong mint-like scent when crushed. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Lippia radula is often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb due to its refreshing flavor and medicinal properties.
