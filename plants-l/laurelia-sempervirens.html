Laurelia sempervirens

Laurelia sempervirens, commonly known as the evergreen tree or the coastal laurel, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to the coastal regions of southern California and Baja California, where it thrives in sandy soils and coastal climates. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and its ability to withstand salt spray and strong winds. Its leaves are dark green and leathery, providing a striking visual contrast to its white, fragrant flowers.

Laurelia sempervirens plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
