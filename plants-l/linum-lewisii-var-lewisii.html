Linum lewisii var. lewisii

Linum lewisii var. lewisii, commonly known as Lewis flax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and delicate blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe. Its leaves are alternate, linear, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are clustered in umbels.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
