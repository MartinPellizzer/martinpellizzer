Liabum robinsonii

Liabum robinsonii, commonly known as the Robinson's liabum, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces slender, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Native to specific regions in South America, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
