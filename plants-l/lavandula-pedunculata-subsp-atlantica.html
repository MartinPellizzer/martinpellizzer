Lavandula pedunculata subsp. atlantica

Lavandula pedunculata subsp.

atlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic lavender, is a perennial shrub native to the coastal regions of the Iberian Peninsula. It features aromatic, slender spikes of purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has narrow, grey-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its essential oils are valued for their fragrance and potential medicinal properties.
