Leptotaenia

Leptotaenia, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to Africa.

These plants are characterized by their rosettes of velvety, waxy leaves and their showy, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors. Leptotaenia species are typically evergreen and thrive in humid, well-drained environments. They are popular as houseplants due to their ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.

The flowers are often pollinated by small insects, contributing to their ecological significance in their native habitats.
