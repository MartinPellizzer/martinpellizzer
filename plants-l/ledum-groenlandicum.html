Ledum groenlandicum

Ledum groenlandicum, commonly known as Labrador tea, is a perennial evergreen shrub native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically red or black, depending on the species, and are often used in traditional medicine. Its roots and leaves contain aromatic oils and are used in various herbal remedies.

Ledum groenlandicum thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in boreal and subarctic regions.
