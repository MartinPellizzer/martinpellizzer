Linum karoi

Linum karoi, commonly known as the Karoi flax, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the genus Linum, which includes various species of flax known for their showy blue or white flowers. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with five petals that attract pollinators such as bees.

Linum karoi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its fibrous stems.
