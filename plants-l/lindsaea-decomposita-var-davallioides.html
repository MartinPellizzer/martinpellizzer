Lindsaea decomposita var. davallioides

Lindsaea decomposita var. davallioides, commonly known as the Davallioid mossy fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, moss-like appearance, with small, overlapping leaves that form dense, cushion-like structures. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments, relying on atmospheric moisture for nourishment. Its fronds are usually dark green and have a leathery texture, with spore-producing structures located on the underside.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
