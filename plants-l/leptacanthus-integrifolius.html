Leptacanthus integrifolius

Leptacanthus integrifolius, commonly known as the smooth-leaved sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family, which includes many species with showy flowers and succulent leaves. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are entire, meaning they have no teeth or lobes, and are covered in a fine, powdery coating that helps reduce water loss.

The flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and often display shades of pink or red, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
