Linum boerhaviifolium

Linum boerhaviifolium, commonly known as the Boerhavia-leaved flax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Linaceae family, which includes other flax species. The plant features slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, blue or purple flowers are typically clustered in loose inflorescences.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
