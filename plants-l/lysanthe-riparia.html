Lysanthe riparia

Lysanthe riparia, commonly known as the river lily, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often near rivers and streams. Its leaves are broad and floating, aiding in photosynthesis in aquatic environments.

Lysanthe riparia plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
