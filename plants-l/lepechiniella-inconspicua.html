Lepechiniella inconspicua

Lepechiniella inconspicua, commonly known as the small lepechiniella, is a small, perennial herb native to South America.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss. Its flowers are tiny, clustered in dense inflorescences, and are often pink or purple in color.
