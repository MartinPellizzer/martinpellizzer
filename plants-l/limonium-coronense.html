Limonium coronense

Limonium coronense, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flower heads, which are surrounded by spiny, grass-like bracts. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, sandy soils, and disturbed sites, showing a high tolerance for salt and wind. Its flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Limonium coronense is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
