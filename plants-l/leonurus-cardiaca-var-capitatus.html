Leonurus cardiaca var. capitatus

Leonurus cardiaca var. capitatus, commonly known as the heartworts, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pink to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its foliage and flowers are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported soothing properties.
