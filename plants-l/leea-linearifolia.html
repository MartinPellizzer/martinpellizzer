Leea linearifolia

Leea linearifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved leeah, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Leeaceae and is characterized by its slender, linear leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in umbels, typical of its genus. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
