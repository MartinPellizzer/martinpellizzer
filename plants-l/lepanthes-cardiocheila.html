Lepanthes cardiocheila

Lepanthes cardiocheila, commonly known as the heart-shaped orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped petals and a vibrant color palette that ranges from red to orange. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical forests. Its flowers are often fragrant and bloom during the rainy season, attracting various pollinators.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts worldwide.
