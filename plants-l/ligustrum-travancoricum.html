Ligustrum travancoricum

Ligustrum travancoricum, commonly known as the Travancore privet, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by dark blue-black berries. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture with a slightly glossy appearance.

Ligustrum travancoricum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in horticulture for landscaping purposes.
