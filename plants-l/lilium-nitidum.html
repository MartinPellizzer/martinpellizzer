Lilium nitidum

Lilium nitidum, commonly known as the white lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy white flowers with a subtle pinkish tinge. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Lilium nitidum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
