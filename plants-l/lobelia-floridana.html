Lobelia floridana

Lobelia floridana, commonly known as the Florida lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. The plant produces small, tubular blue or violet flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Lobelia floridana is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
