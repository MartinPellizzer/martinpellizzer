Lobelia stenotheca

Lobelia stenotheca, commonly known as the narrowleaf lobelia, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other small insects.

Lobelia stenotheca is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
