Litsea tersa

Litsea tersa, commonly known as the sweet fern or fragrant sumac, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, finely divided leaves that emit a pleasant scent when crushed. Its small, yellowish-green flowers appear in clusters and are followed by small, red berries.

Litsea tersa is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
