Liabum pinnatipartitum

Liabum pinnatipartitum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed liabum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant has compound leaves with a pinnate structure, giving it its scientific name. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes.

Liabum pinnatipartitum is often found in grassland and woodland habitats, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
