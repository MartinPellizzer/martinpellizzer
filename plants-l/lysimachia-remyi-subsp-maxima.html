Lysimachia remyi subsp. maxima

Lysimachia remyi subsp.

maxima, commonly known as the Giant Creeping Jenny, is a member of the Primulaceae family. It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its bright yellow flowers and dense, spreading foliage.

Native to eastern Asia, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a smooth margin, and the flowers are clustered in terminal cymes.

The plant's rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly, making it both a valuable ground cover and a potential weed in certain habitats.
