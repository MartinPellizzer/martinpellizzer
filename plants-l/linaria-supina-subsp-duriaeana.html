Linaria supina subsp. duriaeana

Linaria supina subsp.

duriaeana, commonly known as the prostrate toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its low-growing, sprawling habit with hairy stems and small, yellow-orange flowers.

The plant produces slender, hairy leaves that are often clustered at the base. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its flowers bloom in spring and are attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
