Laplacea speciosa var. barbinervis

Laplacea speciosa var. barbinervis, commonly known as the showy laplacea, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy composite flowers that typically bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant features finely divided, feathery leaves that are often covered in a soft, silvery down. Native to the mountainous regions of Central America, it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant collections.
