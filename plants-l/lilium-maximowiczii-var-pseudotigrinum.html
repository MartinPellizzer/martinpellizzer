Lilium maximowiczii var. pseudotigrinum

Lilium maximowiczii var. pseudotigrinum, commonly known as the tigrine lily, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, supporting large, showy flowers with vibrant red and white petals. The leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem, contributing to its elegant appearance. This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its dramatic blooms.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring a cool climate for optimal growth.
