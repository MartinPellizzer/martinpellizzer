Lobelia polyphylla var. bracteosa

Lobelia polyphylla var. bracteosa, commonly known as the bracteate lobelia, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, tubular blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant for native gardens and wetland restoration projects.
