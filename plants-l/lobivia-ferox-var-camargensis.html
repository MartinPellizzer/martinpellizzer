Lobivia ferox var. camargensis

Lobivia ferox var. camargensis, commonly known as the Camargue lobivia, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems and spiny foliage. The plant produces bright yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its rugged appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments.

Due to its unique morphology and adaptability, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts and desert gardeners.
