Lantana micrantha

Lantana micrantha, commonly known as the small-flowered lantana, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to yellow, red, or orange, depending on the variety. Its flowers are typically borne in inflorescences called cymes, which are often densely packed.

Lantana micrantha is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in full sun, making it a common ornamental plant in warm climates.
