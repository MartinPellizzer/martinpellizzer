Lithospermum pentagonum

Lithospermum pentagonum, commonly known as the five-angle rock pepper, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of pale yellow to orange flowers, which are typically borne on short pedicels. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, dark seeds.

Lithospermum pentagonum is adapted to rocky soils and can thrive in full sun, making it a hardy plant in challenging environments.
