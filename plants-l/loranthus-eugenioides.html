Loranthus eugenioides

Loranthus eugenioides, commonly known as the Indian mangosteen or false mangosteen, is a hemiparasitic plant belonging to the Loranthaceae family.

It typically grows as a parasitic vine on the branches of host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has small, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are unisexual, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants, and it produces small, fleshy berries that are often red or yellow in color.

The botanical features of Loranthus eugenioides make it an important component of forest ecosystems, contributing to nutrient cycling and supporting various wildlife species.
