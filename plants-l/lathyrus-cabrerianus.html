Lathyrus cabrerianus

Lathyrus cabrerianus, commonly known as the Canary Island vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant produces clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to violet, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil.

Lathyrus cabrerianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in preventing soil erosion.
