Linum radiola

Linum radiola, commonly known as the yellow flax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny, oval seeds. Linum radiola is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as roadsides and grasslands.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in traditional medicine.
