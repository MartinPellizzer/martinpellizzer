Leucopogon semioppositus

Leucopogon semioppositus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils within wetlands and pine flatwoods. The plant features small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually pink or red, clustered in dense inflorescences, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

It plays an important role in providing habitat and nectar for local pollinators.
