Lechea leggettii

Lechea leggettii, commonly known as the desert milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as deserts and rocky slopes. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring and summer months. Its leaves are succulent, fleshy, and often covered in a fine, white powder.

The species is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it well-suited to its harsh native habitat.
