Liparis caloglossa

Liparis caloglossa, commonly known as the golden lip orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Liparis caloglossa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
