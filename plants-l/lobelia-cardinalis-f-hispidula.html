Lobelia cardinalis f. hispidula

Lobelia cardinalis f. hispidula, commonly known as the hairy cardinal flower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a variant of the cardinal flower, characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces striking red tubular flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees.
