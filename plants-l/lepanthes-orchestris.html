Lepanthes orchestris

Lepanthes orchestris, commonly known as the Orchid Orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually broad and lance-shaped, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

Lepanthes orchestris is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
