Lonchocarpus fasciculatus

Lonchocarpus fasciculatus, commonly known as the clusterpod or yellow clusterpod, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces long, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some parts of the world.
