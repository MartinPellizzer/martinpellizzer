Lobivia fricii

Lobivia fricii, commonly known as the Frici's lobivia, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rounded growth habit. The plant features short, cylindrical stems with areoles that bear clusters of white or pale blue flowers. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, adapting to its harsh desert environment.

Lobivia fricii is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in dry conditions.
