Lupinus alibicolor

Lupinus alibicolor, commonly known as the Alibicolor lupine, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Lupinus alibicolor is a member of the legume family, capable of nitrogen fixation, which benefits soil fertility.
