Leonotis capensis

Leonotis capensis, commonly known as the lion's tail, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant features showy, tubular flowers that are usually bright orange or red, arranged in loose clusters at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered with fine hairs.

The plant is adapted to dry conditions and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
