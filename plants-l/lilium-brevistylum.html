Lilium brevistylum

Lilium brevistylum, commonly known as the short-stemmed lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are usually pink to reddish in color, with six petals and a prominent, spurred tepal. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Lilium brevistylum is valued in horticulture for its beauty and adaptability to partial shade conditions.
