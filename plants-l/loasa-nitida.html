Loasa nitida

Loasa nitida, commonly known as the silverleaf loasa, is a shrub native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape and have a slightly hairy texture.

Loasa nitida is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
