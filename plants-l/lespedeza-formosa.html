Lespedeza formosa

Lespedeza formosa, commonly known as the pinkwood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, pink to reddish-purple flowers in clusters. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodlands and wet meadows.

Lespedeza formosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
