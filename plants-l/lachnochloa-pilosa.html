Lachnochloa pilosa

Lachnochloa pilosa, commonly known as the hairy lachnochloa, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family, Poaceae.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and hairy leaves, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in wetland or marshy environments. Its inflorescence consists of a spikelet with two to three small, bract-like structures.

Lachnochloa pilosa is primarily valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small fauna.
