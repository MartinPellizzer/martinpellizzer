Lathyrus vestitus subsp. bolanderi

Lathyrus vestitus subsp.

bolanderi, commonly known as the Bolander's vetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink.

The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Its foliage is trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets that are hairy on the underside.
