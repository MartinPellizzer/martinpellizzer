Lantana canescens var. integrifolia

Lantana canescens var. integrifolia, commonly known as the smooth lantana, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, vibrant yellow or orange flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The leaves are typically smooth and have a slightly fuzzy texture, with a lance-shaped or ovate form. This variety is often found in open woodlands and disturbed sites, thriving in full sun to partial shade.

It is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
