Lophopetalum filiforme

Lophopetalum filiforme, commonly known as the slender flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It features small, clustered white to pink flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are often described as being shaped like flames, giving it its common name. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that splits open to release seeds.
