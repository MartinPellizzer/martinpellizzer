Lippia filifolia

Lippia filifolia, commonly known as silverleaf lippia, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of South America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pale blue or violet, and they appear in clusters during the summer months.

Lippia filifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
