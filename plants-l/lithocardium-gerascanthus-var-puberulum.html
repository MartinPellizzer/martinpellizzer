Lithocardium gerascanthus var. puberulum

Lithocardium gerascanthus var. puberulum, commonly known as the pubescent lithocardium, is a rare and unique plant species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The leaves are generally hairy or pubescent, giving the plant its varietal name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in rocky or forested areas.
