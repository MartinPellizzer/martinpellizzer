Lycianthes biflora var. subtusochracea

Lycianthes biflora var. subtusochracea, commonly known as the yellow-flowered bitter apple, is a variant of the species native to Central America.

It is a shrub characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and hairy, glandular leaves. The plant produces small, yellow, fleshy fruits that are typically bitter and used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in disturbed or open habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential medicinal research.
