Leucocoryne appendiculata

Leucocoryne appendiculata, commonly known as the silver leaf lily, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has strap-like, evergreen leaves that are broad and glossy, often with a silvery sheen. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers bright, indirect light.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.
