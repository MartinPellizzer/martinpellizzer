Linum usitatissimum subsp. crepitans

Linum usitatissimum subsp.

crepitans, commonly known as the common flax subspecies with a crackling texture, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, blue or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and agricultural settings.

This subspecies is closely related to the cultivated flax used for fiber and oil production.
