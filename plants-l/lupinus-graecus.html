Lupinus graecus

Lupinus graecus, commonly known as Greek lupin, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces spikes of blue, purple, or white flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its seeds are encased in pods and are used as a food source for both wildlife and livestock.

Lupinus graecus is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
