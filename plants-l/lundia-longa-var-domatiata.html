Lundia longa var. domatiata

Lundia longa var. domatiata, commonly known as the domatiated lundia, is a rare variant of the lundia plant, characterized by its unique leaf morphology and floral structure.

It is native to a specific region in South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The leaves are elongated and have a waxy coating, which helps in reducing water loss. Its flowers are small, clustered, and typically display a pale yellow to greenish hue.

This plant is of interest to botanists due to its distinct genetic traits and limited geographic distribution.
