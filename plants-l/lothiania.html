Lothiania

Lothiania, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a unique plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage, which helps it reflect sunlight and reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The leaves are small, leathery, and arranged in a compact, dense structure. Lothiania produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white, followed by edible, fleshy fruits.

This plant is well adapted to drought conditions and plays an important role in local ecosystems.
