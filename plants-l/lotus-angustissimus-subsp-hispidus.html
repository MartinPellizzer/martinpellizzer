Lotus angustissimus subsp. hispidus

Lotus angustissimus subsp.

hispidus, commonly known as the hairy lotus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia, particularly China and India. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has hairy foliage and stems, which give it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
