Lugaion

Lugaion, commonly known as the silverleaf plant, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its finely divided, silvery-gray leaves.

It typically grows in moist, temperate regions and is often found near water sources such as streams and wet meadows. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The foliage has a distinctive aroma when crushed, which is attributed to its high concentration of volatile oils.
