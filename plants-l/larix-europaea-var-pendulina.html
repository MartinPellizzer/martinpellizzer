Larix europaea var. pendulina

Larix europaea var. pendulina, commonly known as the weeping larch, is a unique variant of the European larch.

It is characterized by its pendulous branches that droop gracefully, giving the tree a distinctive, ethereal appearance. The plant is a coniferous evergreen with needle-like leaves that are typically a vibrant green. It produces cone-like structures that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking visual appeal.
