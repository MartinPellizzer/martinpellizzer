Lessertia pulchra

Lessertia pulchra, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a deciduous tree or large shrub that can grow up to 10 meters tall, featuring smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow, fragrant flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and birds. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Lessertia pulchra is valued for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens and landscapes.
