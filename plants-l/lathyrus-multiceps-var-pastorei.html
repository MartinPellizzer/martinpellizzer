Lathyrus multiceps var. pastorei

Lathyrus multiceps var. pastorei, commonly known as the Pastorei Sweet Pea, is a climbing legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, twining stems and produces clusters of fragrant, pea-like flowers in shades of pink, white, and purple. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and its flowers are typically borne in dense, terminal spikes. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

This variety is valued for its showy blooms and ability to attract pollinators.
