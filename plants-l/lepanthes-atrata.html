Lepanthes atrata

Lepanthes atrata, commonly known as the black orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its dark, almost black petals and sepals. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in the understory of tropical forests. Its flowers are small but striking, with a unique coloration that sets it apart from other orchid species.

Lepanthes atrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
