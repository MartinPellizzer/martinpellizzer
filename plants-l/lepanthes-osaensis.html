Lepanthes osaensis

Lepanthes osaensis, commonly known as the Osa Mountain orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as cloud forests, where it thrives on the forest floor or on tree bark. Its flowers are usually small, with intricate patterns and vibrant colors, often in shades of white, pink, and purple.

Lepanthes osaensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its native habitats.
