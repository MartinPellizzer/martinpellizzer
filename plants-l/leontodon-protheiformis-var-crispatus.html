Leontodon protheiformis var. crispatus

Leontodon protheiformis var. crispatus, commonly known as the curly-haired bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pale blue or white in color. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
