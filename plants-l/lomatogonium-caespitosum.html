Lomatogonium caespitosum

Lomatogonium caespitosum, commonly known as the hairy leaf cactus, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant has a succulent structure, allowing it to store water and survive in dry environments. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it produces showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Lomatogonium caespitosum is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to full sun exposure.
