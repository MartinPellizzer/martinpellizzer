Linaria virgata var. syrtica

Linaria virgata var. syrtica, commonly known as the desert snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and produces vibrant yellow or orange tubular flowers. The leaves are narrow and linear, adapted to reduce water loss in harsh environments. This variety is part of the Plantaginaceae family and is often found in rocky soils and open habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in low-water conditions.
