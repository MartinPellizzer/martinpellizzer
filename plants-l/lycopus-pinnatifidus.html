Lycopus pinnatifidus

Lycopus pinnatifidus, commonly known as narrow-leaved bugleweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland edges and stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often finely toothed or lobed. Its small, blue to purple flowers bloom in clusters during the summer months.

The plant is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and is often used in traditional herbal medicine.
