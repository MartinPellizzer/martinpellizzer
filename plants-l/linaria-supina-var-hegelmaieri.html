Linaria supina var. hegelmaieri

Linaria supina var. hegelmaieri, commonly known as the prostrate toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that features slender, creeping stems and small, yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its flowers bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
