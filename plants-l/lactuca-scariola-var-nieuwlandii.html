Lactuca scariola var. nieuwlandii

Lactuca scariola var. nieuwlandii, commonly known as Newland's sowthistle, is a variant of the common sowthistle.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its rosette of pale green, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been introduced to other regions.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats.
