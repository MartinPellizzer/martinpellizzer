Luma aprica

Luma aprica, commonly known as the Chilean firetree, is a species of flowering plant native to southern South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has a shrub-like appearance with dense, evergreen foliage. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing a rugged texture, while its leaves are leathery and dark green.

Luma aprica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
