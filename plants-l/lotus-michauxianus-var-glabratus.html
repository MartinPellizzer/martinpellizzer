Lotus michauxianus var. glabratus

Lotus michauxianus var. glabratus, commonly known as the smooth lotus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features trifoliate leaves with a smooth texture and produces showy pink to purple flowers. Its root system is fibrous, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

This species is often found in wetlands and along stream banks, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
