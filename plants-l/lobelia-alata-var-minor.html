Lobelia alata var. minor

Lobelia alata var. minor, commonly known as the trailing lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems that often form dense mats or cascading displays. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and slightly serrated.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in hanging baskets or as a ground cover in shaded gardens.
