Leontodon leyseri

Leontodon leyseri, commonly known as the Leyser's hawk's-beard, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant features a basal rosette of leaves that are typically grayish-green and covered in fine hairs. Its flower heads consist of yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Leontodon leyseri is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun conditions.
