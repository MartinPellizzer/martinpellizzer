Lonchocarpus eetveldeanus

Lonchocarpus eetveldeanus, commonly known as the Eetvelde legume, is a woody shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant produces elongated, woody pods that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind or animals. Its roots are known to contain alkaloids, making it a plant of interest in both traditional medicine and botanical research.

The species is also valued for its role in soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing capabilities.
