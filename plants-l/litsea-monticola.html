Litsea monticola

Litsea monticola, commonly known as mountain litsea, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and avocado trees. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, aromatic leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are followed by elliptical, dark blue berries that are edible.

Litsea monticola thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.
