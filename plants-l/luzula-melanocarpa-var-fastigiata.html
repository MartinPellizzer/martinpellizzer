Luzula melanocarpa var. fastigiata

Luzula melanocarpa var. fastigiata, commonly known as the blackstem sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wet meadows. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are dark green and often tinged with purple or black at the base. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, three-lobed flowers.

The variety *fastigiata* is distinguished by its upright, columnar growth habit, making it a striking addition to gardens and natural landscapes.
