Lepicystis fallax

Lepicystis fallax, commonly known as the false hellebore, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features a stout, fleshy rhizome and has palmately divided, lobed leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with five petals and numerous stamens.

The plant contains toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to livestock and humans if ingested.
