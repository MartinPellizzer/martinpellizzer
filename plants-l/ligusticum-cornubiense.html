Ligusticum cornubiense

Ligusticum cornubiense, commonly known as Cornish Lovage, is a perennial herb native to the British Isles.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in umbels, typical of the family. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.

Ligusticum cornubiense is valued for its aromatic properties and historical use in traditional medicine.
