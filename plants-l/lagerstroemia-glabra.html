Lagerstroemia glabra

Lagerstroemia glabra, commonly known as the smooth crape myrtle, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and showy, clustered flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that turn reddish in the fall. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its adaptability and long blooming period contribute to its widespread cultivation in gardens and landscapes.
