Lobelia glabra

Lobelia glabra, commonly known as smooth lobelia, is a herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are smooth to the touch, as indicated by its species name "glabra.". Its flowers are small, tubular, and typically blue or purple, arranged in spikes.

Lobelia glabra is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
