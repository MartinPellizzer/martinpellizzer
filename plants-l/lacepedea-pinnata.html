Lacepedea pinnata

Lacepedea pinnata, commonly known as the lace fern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that create a lacy appearance. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its leaves are often dark green and have a soft, feathery texture.

Lacepedea pinnata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in shaded garden settings.
