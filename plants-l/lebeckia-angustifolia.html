Lebeckia angustifolia

Lebeckia angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf lebeckia, is a South African native plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. It is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative plant due to its elegant appearance.

Lebeckia angustifolia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
