Linaria anticaria var. cuartanensis

Linaria anticaria var. cuartanensis, commonly known as the Cuartana toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open woodlands. The plant features slender, branching stems and produces small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered with fine hairs.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
