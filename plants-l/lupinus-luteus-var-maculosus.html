Lupinus luteus var. maculosus

Lupinus luteus var. maculosus, commonly known as the spotted yellow lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers with distinctive red or purple spots, and its compound leaves with small, rounded leaflets. The plant typically grows in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. It has a taproot system and produces pods that contain several elliptical seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plants.
