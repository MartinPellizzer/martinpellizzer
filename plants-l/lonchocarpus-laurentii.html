Lonchocarpus laurentii

Lonchocarpus laurentii, commonly known as the red root or bloodroot, is a tropical shrub belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to Central and South America and is characterized by its woody, aromatic roots and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by distinctive, inflated fruit pods. It contains various alkaloids, some of which have been studied for potential medicinal properties.

However, it is important to note that the plant can be toxic if ingested in large quantities.
