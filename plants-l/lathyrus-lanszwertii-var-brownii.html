Lathyrus lanszwertii var. brownii

Lathyrus lanszwertii var. brownii, commonly known as the Brown's vetch, is a perennial legume native to southern Europe.

It features slender, climbing stems with trifoliate leaves composed of three small, ovate leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in spring. Its seed pods are elongated and contain several kidney-shaped seeds.

This variety is often found in disturbed soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and nitrogen-fixing abilities.
