Lupinus laxiflorus

Lupinus laxiflorus, commonly known as the slender-flowered lupine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets, and its root system forms symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.

Lupinus laxiflorus is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.
