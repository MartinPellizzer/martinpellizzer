Loudetia simplex subsp. stipoides

Loudetia simplex subsp.

stipoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf jointvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It features slender, ascending stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 60 cm in height.

The leaves are narrow and linear, with a slightly hairy surface, and the flowers are small, pinkish-white, and clustered in loose racemes. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open grasslands or along riverbanks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
