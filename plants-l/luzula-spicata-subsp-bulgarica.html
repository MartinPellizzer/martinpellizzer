Luzula spicata subsp. bulgarica

Luzula spicata subsp.

bulgarica, commonly known as the Bulgarian wood rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It belongs to the family Poaceae, commonly known as the grass family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves.

The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, greenish to brownish flowers. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to woodland habitats and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
