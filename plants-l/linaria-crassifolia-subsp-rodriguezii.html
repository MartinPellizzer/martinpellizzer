Linaria crassifolia subsp. rodriguezii

Linaria crassifolia subsp.

rodriguezii, commonly known as Rodriguez's toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands. It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It has linear, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in its arid habitat.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
