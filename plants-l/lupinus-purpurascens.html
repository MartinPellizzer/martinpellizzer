Lupinus purpurascens

Lupinus purpurascens, commonly known as the purple lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of vibrant purple or pink flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds. Lupinus purpurascens is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

It plays an important role in nitrogen fixation, enriching the soil with nutrients.
