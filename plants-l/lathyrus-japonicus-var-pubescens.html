Lathyrus japonicus var. pubescens

Lathyrus japonicus var. pubescens, commonly known as Japanese vetch, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, pea-like flowers. The plant features trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces, which is a distinguishing trait of the varietal form pubescens. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This species is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
