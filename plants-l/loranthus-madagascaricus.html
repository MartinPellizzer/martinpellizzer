Loranthus madagascaricus

Loranthus madagascaricus, commonly known as the Madagascar mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees.

It belongs to the Loranthaceae family and lacks true roots, relying on its host for water and nutrients. The plant produces small, green leaves and white or pink flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its seeds are dispersed by birds that consume the fleshy berries.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can weaken its host if left unchecked, making it an important consideration in ecological studies.
