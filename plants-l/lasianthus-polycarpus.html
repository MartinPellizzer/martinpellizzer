Lasianthus polycarpus

Lasianthus polycarpus, commonly known as the golden flame orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy, with a prominent midrib. Its most striking feature is its clusters of bright yellow-orange flowers, which bloom in summer.

Lasianthus polycarpus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
