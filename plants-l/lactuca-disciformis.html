Lactuca disciformis

Lactuca disciformis, commonly known as the broadleaf lettuce, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family.

It is characterized by its rosette of broad, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, followed by small, oval-shaped seeds. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been introduced to other regions.

Lactuca disciformis is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
