Lespedeza buergeri var. retusa

Lespedeza buergeri var. retusa, commonly known as the slender lespedeza, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typical of the genus Lespedeza. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it ecologically significant in soil enrichment.
