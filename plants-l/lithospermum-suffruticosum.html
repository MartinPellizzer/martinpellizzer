Lithospermum suffruticosum

Lithospermum suffruticosum, commonly known as red sandalwood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, reddish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces distinctive, elongated, red or orange berries that contain the seeds. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Lithospermum suffruticosum is often found in dry, rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
