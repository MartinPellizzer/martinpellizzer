Laurus naucushia

Laurus naucushia, commonly known as sweet bay or Turkish laurel, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and parts of southern Europe, North Africa, and western Asia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green, lance-shaped leaves and its fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. It produces small, dark blue to black berries that are edible but often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Laurus naucushia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and aromatic properties.
