Leucopogon elatior

Leucopogon elatior, commonly known as the tall heath, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its slender, woody stems and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. Its leaves are typically narrow and evergreen, providing a dense foliage that supports various wildlife.

It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in pine flatwoods and sandhill habitats.
