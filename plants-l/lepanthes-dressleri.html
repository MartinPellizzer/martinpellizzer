Lepanthes dressleri

Lepanthes dressleri, commonly known as the Dressler's slipper orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a rosette pattern around the base of the plant.

Lepanthes dressleri is prized in horticulture for its beauty and is often cultivated in shaded, moist environments.
