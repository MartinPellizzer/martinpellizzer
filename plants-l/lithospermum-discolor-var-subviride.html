Lithospermum discolor var. subviride

Lithospermum discolor var. subviride, commonly known as the greenleaf rock pepper, is a variant of the rock pepper plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically a bright green color. The flowers are small, tubular, and usually pink or purple, while the fruit is a spherical, red berry that contains several seeds. This plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
