Liriope muscari f. variegata

Liriope muscari f. variegata, commonly known as the variegated purple false ivy, is a cultivar of the evergreen perennial plant native to eastern Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive variegated pattern of white and green, and its most striking feature is the clusters of bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that appear in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that persist into winter, adding visual interest to the landscape.

It is well-suited for shaded gardens and can be used as a ground cover or in rock gardens due to its low-growing habit and ornamental appeal.
