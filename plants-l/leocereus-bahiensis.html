Leocereus bahiensis

Leocereus bahiensis, commonly known as the Bahia night bloomer, is a cacti species native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stem and is often found in arid and semi-arid regions. The plant produces striking white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as bats and moths. It has a shallow root system adapted to absorb moisture from infrequent rains.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a popular specimen among cactus enthusiasts and botanists.
