Lamium vulgatum

Lamium vulgatum, commonly known as dead nettle, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features opposite, heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces clusters of two-lipped, pink or purple flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shaded areas and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs.

Dead nettle is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic qualities.
