Lissochilus analamerensis

Lissochilus analamerensis, commonly known as the Andean slipper orchid, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, slipper-shaped flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. The plant thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found in cloud forests and alpine meadows. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from rocky soils, and it plays a role in the ecological balance of its native habitat.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in some regions.
