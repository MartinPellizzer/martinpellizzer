Ledenbergia peruviana

Ledenbergia peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, often fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant typically produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, aiding its survival in its native high-altitude environment. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, arranged in a rosette pattern around the pseudobulbs.

Ledenbergia peruviana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
