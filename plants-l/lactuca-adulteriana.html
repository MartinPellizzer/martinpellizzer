Lactuca adulteriana

Lactuca adulteriana, commonly known as bitter lettuce or wild endive, is a flowering plant in the dandelion family (Asteraceae).

It is characterized by its curly, dark green leaves that are often bitter in taste, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is native to Europe, North Africa, and western Asia, and it thrives in disturbed soils and along roadsides. It contains a milky sap, which is a distinguishing feature of the genus Lactuca.

The plant is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
