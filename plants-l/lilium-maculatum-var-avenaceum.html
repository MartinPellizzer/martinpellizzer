Lilium maculatum var. avenaceum

Lilium maculatum var. avenaceum, commonly known as the spotted lily, is a variant of the tiger lily that exhibits unique botanical characteristics.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States, typically growing in moist, woodland habitats. The plant features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers with bright orange petals and prominent red spots. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a fan-like pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The bulbs are fleshy and store nutrients, supporting the plant's growth and flowering cycle.
