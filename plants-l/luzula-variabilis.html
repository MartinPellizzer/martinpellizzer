Luzula variabilis

Luzula variabilis, commonly known as variable wood rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, often reddish stems and dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue.

Luzula variabilis is noted for its adaptability to various soil conditions and is often found in temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.
