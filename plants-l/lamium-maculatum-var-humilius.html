Lamium maculatum var. humilius

Lamium maculatum var. humilius, commonly known as the dwarf spotted dead-nettle, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive silver or white markings, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, two-lipped flowers in shades of pink or purple, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a shade-tolerant ground cover.
