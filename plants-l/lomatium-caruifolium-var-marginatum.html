Lomatium caruifolium var. marginatum

Lomatium caruifolium var. marginatum, commonly known as the western lomatium, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate flower clusters. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves with white margins, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Its flowers, which bloom in late spring, attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
