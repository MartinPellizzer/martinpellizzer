Lasianthus ridleyi

Lasianthus ridleyi, commonly known as the Malayan flameflower, is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of bright red, tubular flowers that bloom during the rainy season, attracting various pollinators. Its ornamental value is due to its vibrant floral display and compact growth habit.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical rainforests.
