Languas rubella

Languas rubella, commonly known as the red-tongued plant, is a unique species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking red-tinged leaves that resemble tongues, giving it its common name. The plant has a succulent stem that stores water, allowing it to thrive in dry environments. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in shades of yellow and orange.

Languas rubella is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to add a vibrant touch to desert gardens.
