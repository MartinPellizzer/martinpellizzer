Linaria multicaulis subsp. aurasiaca

Linaria multicaulis subsp.

aurasiaca, commonly known as the Aurasiaca toadflax, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and bright yellow flowers that resemble those of other linaria species.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine layer of hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
