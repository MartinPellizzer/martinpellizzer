Leea odontophylla

Leea odontophylla, commonly known as the toothed leaf vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Leeaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers that are clustered in umbels. It is often found growing in tropical forests, climbing on trees or shrubs.

Its botanical features make it a unique subject for ecological and horticultural study.
