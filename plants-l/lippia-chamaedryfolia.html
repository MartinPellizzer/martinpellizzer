Lippia chamaedryfolia

Lippia chamaedryfolia, commonly known as silverleaf lippia, is a shrub native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray leaves and small, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is often found in dry, rocky habitats. Its leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its distinctive scent and potential medicinal uses.

Lippia chamaedryfolia is also valued in traditional herbal practices for its purported health benefits.
