Loranthus miraculosus

Loranthus miraculosus, commonly known as the miraculous mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on a variety of host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using light reflected from the host's leaves. The plant forms a dense, bushy growth that can cover large portions of its host, often leading to weakened or dying host trees. Its small, white or pale green berries are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

Despite its parasitic nature, Loranthus miraculosus plays a significant ecological role in forest ecosystems.
