Leucopogon flexifolius

Leucopogon flexifolius, commonly known as the variable leucopogon, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks. Its foliage is noted for its variability in shape and size, which contributes to its common name.

Leucopogon flexifolius is an evergreen shrub that produces fragrant, white or pale pink flowers throughout the growing season.
