Lycopersicon esculentum var. grandifolium

Lycopersicon esculentum var. grandifolium, commonly known as the large-leaf tomato, is a botanical variant of the domesticated tomato.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves and typically produces large, meaty fruits. The plant is a herbaceous annual that thrives in warm climates and requires full sunlight for optimal growth. Its botanical features include a robust stem, hairy foliage, and flowers that are typically yellow with five petals.

This variety is often cultivated for its enhanced flavor and higher yield compared to standard tomato cultivars.
