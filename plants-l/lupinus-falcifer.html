Lupinus falcifer

Lupinus falcifer, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lupin, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of pea-like flowers that range in color from pink to purple. Native to South America, particularly Chile, this species thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it valuable for soil enrichment.

Its seeds are edible and have been used by indigenous communities for food and medicinal purposes.
