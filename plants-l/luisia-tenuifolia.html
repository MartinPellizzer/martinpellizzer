Luisia tenuifolia

Luisia tenuifolia, commonly known as the slenderleaf luisia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Violaceae and is characterized by its small, delicate leaves and clusters of pale purple or white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial shade. Its foliage is narrow and elongated, with a soft, velvety texture.

Luisia tenuifolia is often found in wetlands and along stream banks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
