Leea latifolia

Leea latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf leea, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, broad leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a smooth texture.

Leea latifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid climates.
