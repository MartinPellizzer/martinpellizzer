Ludovia angustifolia

Ludovia angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf ludovia, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems that bear alternate, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its fibrous roots are adapted to thrive in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rocky or arid environments.

Ludovia angustifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and flowering display.
