Lythrum paradoxum

Lythrum paradoxum, commonly known as the purple verbena, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of vibrant purple or pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Lythraceae family, which includes other species such as the common willow herb. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Lythrum paradoxum is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
