Lavandula × hortensis

Lavandula × hortensis, commonly known as English lavender, is a hybrid species within the Lavandula genus.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and fragrant, spike-like inflorescences of purple or blue-lilac flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing aromatic essential oils in its flower buds. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental gardening and essential oil production.
