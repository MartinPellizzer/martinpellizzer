Lycopodium lyallii

Lycopodium lyallii, commonly known as the Lyall's clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its upright, branching stems covered in tiny, scale-like leaves. It produces spore cones at the tips of its branches, which are essential for its reproductive cycle.

Lycopodium lyallii plays a role in maintaining soil stability and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
