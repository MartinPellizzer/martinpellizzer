Loxogramme antrophyoides var. antrophyoides

Loxogramme antrophyoides var. antrophyoides, commonly known as the common water clover, is a perennial aquatic plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. The plant has a rosette-like growth form with finely divided, floating leaves that are often described as resembling a clover leaf. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually greenish-white and appear in clusters.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
