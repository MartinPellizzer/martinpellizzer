Leptactina papyrophloea

Leptactina papyrophloea, commonly known as the paper-wood plant, is a species native to certain regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its unique combination of fibrous and woody tissues, which contribute to its structural strength. The plant typically features broad, flat leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually in terminal spikes, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
