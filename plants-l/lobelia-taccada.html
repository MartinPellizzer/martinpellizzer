Lobelia taccada

Lobelia taccada, commonly known as the sacred lotus of the Philippines, is a tropical plant native to the islands of the Philippines.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wet, shaded environments such as marshes and along stream banks. The plant features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in spikes, often surrounded by heart-shaped leaves. Its roots are fibrous and can be used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Lobelia taccada plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
