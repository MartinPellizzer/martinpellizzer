Larix potaninii

Larix potaninii, commonly known as the Potanin's larch, is a deciduous conifer native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and its distinctive yellowish-brown bark that becomes fissured with age. The tree produces small, spherical cones that release seeds in the spring, and its needle-like leaves turn golden before falling in autumn. It thrives in cold, dry climates and is often found at high altitudes in forested areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
