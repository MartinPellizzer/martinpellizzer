Leandra rotundifolia

Leandra rotundifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf orchid, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its broad, rounded leaves and clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is often found in moist, shaded environments. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils, while its stems are typically short and creeping.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
