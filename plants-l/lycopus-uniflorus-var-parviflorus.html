Lycopus uniflorus var. parviflorus

Lycopus uniflorus var. parviflorus, commonly known as narrowflower blueweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland areas and has square, hairy stems with opposite leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy on the underside. It is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments and is known for its aromatic foliage.

This variant of Lycopus uniflorus is distinguished by its smaller flowers compared to other varieties in the species.
