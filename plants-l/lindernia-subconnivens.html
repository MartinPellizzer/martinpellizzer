Lindernia subconnivens

Lindernia subconnivens, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lindernia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the family Verbenaceae and is characterized by its opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Lindernia subconnivens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
