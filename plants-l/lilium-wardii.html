Lilium wardii

Lilium wardii, commonly known as the Ward's lily, is a species of flowering plant in the Liliaceae family.

It is native to the high altitudes of the Andes Mountains in South America, particularly in regions of Peru and Bolivia. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and red. Its leaves are long and narrow, arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Lilium wardii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
