Lepanthes falcifera

Lepanthes falcifera, commonly known as the sickle-shaped orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, falcate (sickle-shaped) petals and sepals, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are usually small, fragrant, and come in a variety of colors, including white, yellow, and pink.

Lepanthes falcifera is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
