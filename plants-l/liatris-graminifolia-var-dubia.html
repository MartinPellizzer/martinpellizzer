Liatris graminifolia var. dubia

Liatris graminifolia var. dubia, commonly known as the slender blazing star, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear spikes of pale purple to white flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, tubular florets that are typically arranged in a dense, spiky cluster. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in open woodlands and prairies.

The botanical features of this variant make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
