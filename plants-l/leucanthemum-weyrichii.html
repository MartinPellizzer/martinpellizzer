Leucanthemum weyrichii

Leucanthemum weyrichii, commonly known as the Weyrich's marguerite, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with white ray florets surrounding a yellow disc. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in temperate climates. It produces small, herbaceous stems and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely commercially available.
