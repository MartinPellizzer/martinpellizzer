Licuala glabra var. glabra

Licuala glabra var. glabra, commonly known as the broadleaf palm, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, pinnately compound leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant typically thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil. Its inflorescences are usually clustered at the base of the plant, producing small, inconspicuous flowers.

The fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
