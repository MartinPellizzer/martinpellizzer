Lepidium hyssopifolium

Lepidium hyssopifolium, commonly known as Mexican peppergrass, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many edible and economically important plants. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and has small, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are yellow and form in clusters, and it produces small, round pods that contain several seeds.
