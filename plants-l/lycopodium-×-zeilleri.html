Lycopodium × zeilleri

Lycopodium × zeilleri, commonly known as the clubmoss hybrid, is a member of the Lycopodiaceae family.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in shaded, moist environments. The plant features a creeping stem with clustered, scale-like leaves. Its spore-bearing structures, called strobila, are cone-like and appear at the tips of the stems.

Lycopodium × zeilleri is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
