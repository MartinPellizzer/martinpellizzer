Lonicera xerocalyx

Lonicera xerocalyx, commonly known as the Bur oak, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental value.

Lonicera xerocalyx is also valued for its hardiness and adaptability in landscaping.
