Laurus crassifolia

Laurus crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved laurel, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its thick, glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that appear in clusters. Its fruit is a spherical, dark purple drupe, which contains a single seed.

Laurus crassifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a mild, spicy scent reminiscent of other laurel species.
