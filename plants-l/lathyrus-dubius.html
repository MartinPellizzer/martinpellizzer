Lathyrus dubius

Lathyrus dubius, commonly known as the slender vetch, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple, and it bears small, bean-shaped pods containing seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including meadows and along roadsides.

The plant is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
