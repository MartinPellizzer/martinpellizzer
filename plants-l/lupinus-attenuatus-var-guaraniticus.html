Lupinus attenuatus var. guaraniticus

Lupinus attenuatus var. guaraniticus, commonly known as the Guaraniticus lupine, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of blue to violet pea-shaped flowers. The leaves are compound, consisting of several oval to lance-shaped leaflets, and the plant produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential in ecological restoration projects.
