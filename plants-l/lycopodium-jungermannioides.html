Lycopodium jungermannioides

Lycopodium jungermannioides, commonly known as the Jungermannioid moss, is a species of liverwort belonging to the family Lepidoziaceae.

It is a small, acrocarpous moss that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rock crevices. The plant has a thalloid structure with a dorsiventral leafy stem and is characterized by its intricate, branching growth pattern. It produces spore capsules that are often covered in a thick, gelatinous layer, aiding in spore dispersal.

This moss plays a vital role in soil stabilization and moisture retention in its natural habitat.
