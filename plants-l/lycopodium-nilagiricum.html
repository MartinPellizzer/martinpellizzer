Lycopodium nilagiricum

Lycopodium nilagiricum, commonly known as the Nilagiri clubmoss, is a small evergreen plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is native to the Western Ghats of India and thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant has a creeping stem with whorled leaves that are typically needle-like in shape. It produces spore-bearing structures called strobila at the tips of its branches.

Lycopodium nilagiricum plays a role in maintaining soil stability and providing habitat for small organisms in its native ecosystem.
