Lycopodium gigas

Lycopodium gigas, commonly known as the giant clubmoss, is a rare and ancient plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, sprawling growth habit and its distinctive, spore-bearing strobili that form in clusters along the stems. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate forests and rocky crevices. Its leaves are small, linear, and arranged in whorls around the stem, contributing to its lush, moss-like appearance.
