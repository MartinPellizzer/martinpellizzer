Linum lewisii var. pratense

Linum lewisii var. pratense, commonly known as Lewis flax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the genus Linum, which includes various species of flax known for their showy blue or white flowers. This variety is characterized by its slender stems and small, delicate flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open meadows and rocky areas.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to support pollinators.
