Lampranthus palustris

Lampranthus palustris, commonly known as the marsh lion's tail, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and clusters of bright orange or red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often grown as a ornamental plant due to its striking appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Lampranthus palustris is drought-tolerant and suitable for rock gardens or container planting.
