Lasianthus mollis

Lasianthus mollis, commonly known as the soft-haired lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and riverbanks. Its flowers are often pink or purple and attract various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
