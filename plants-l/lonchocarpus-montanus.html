Lonchocarpus montanus

Lonchocarpus montanus, commonly known as the pink-root or red root, is a tropical legume native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers arranged in racemate inflorescences. Its fruit is a legume pod containing several elliptical seeds.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.
