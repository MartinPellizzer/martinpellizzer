Linderniella bolusii

Linderniella bolusii, commonly known as the Bolus's lindernia, is a small, herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal and ornamental uses. The plant typically features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers in shades of purple or blue. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
