Lupinus sulphureus subsp. whithamii

Lupinus sulphureus subsp.

whithamii, commonly known as the Yellow Bunchflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its bright yellow, showy flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has palmately divided leaves and produces cylindrical seed pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed sites.

Its botanical features make it an important plant for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
