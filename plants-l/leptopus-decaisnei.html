Leptopus decaisnei

Leptopus decaisnei, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night. The plant has a woody stem with hairy leaves and produces fruit that splits open to release seeds with silky filaments. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions and is often found in disturbed habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its unique flowering pattern.
