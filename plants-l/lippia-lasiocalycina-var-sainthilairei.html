Lippia lasiocalycina var. sainthilairei

Lippia lasiocalycina var. sainthilairei, commonly known as Saint-Hilaire's lippia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring opposite, narrow, and slightly hairy leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as savannas and rocky areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
