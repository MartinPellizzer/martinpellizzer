Lespedeza mucronata

Lespedeza mucronata, commonly known as narrowleaf lespedeza, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open woods and along stream banks. The plant produces small, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and opposite, with a distinctive pointed tip.

Lespedeza mucronata is often used in ecological restoration due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
