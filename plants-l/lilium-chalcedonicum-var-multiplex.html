Lilium chalcedonicum var. multiplex

Lilium chalcedonicum var. multiplex, commonly known as the Chalcedonic Lily, is a striking bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, often with a distinctive red or purple throat. The plant produces multiple blooms per stem, contributing to its name "multiplex," which means "many-fold" in Latin. Its foliage consists of broad, grass-like leaves that emerge in early spring.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its dramatic floral display.
