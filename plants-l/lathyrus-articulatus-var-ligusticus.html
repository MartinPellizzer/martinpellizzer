Lathyrus articulatus var. ligusticus

Lathyrus articulatus var. ligusticus, commonly known as the Ligurian grass vetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and open woodlands. The plant features slender, branched stems and small, pea-like flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local insect populations.
