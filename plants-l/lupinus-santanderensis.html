Lupinus santanderensis

Lupinus santanderensis, commonly known as the Santander lupine, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andes mountains in Colombia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of showy blue to purple flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in high-altitude environments and is adapted to the cool, moist conditions of its native habitat.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
