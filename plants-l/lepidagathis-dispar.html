Lepidagathis dispar

Lepidagathis dispar, commonly known as the African star grass, is a perennial herb native to tropical Africa.

It features erect, branched stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, covered with small, scale-like structures. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers arranged in dense, terminal spikes, which bloom during the rainy season. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slightly hairy surface.

Lepidagathis dispar is adapted to well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed habitats.
