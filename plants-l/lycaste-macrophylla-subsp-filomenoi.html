Lycaste macrophylla subsp. filomenoi

Lycaste macrophylla subsp.

filomenoi, commonly known as the Filomena orchid, is a species native to the humid forests of central Mexico. It is characterized by its large, showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in late spring or early summer.

The plant features long, arching inflorescences with numerous florets, each adorned with intricate patterns and vibrant colors. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, contributing to its lush, tropical appearance.

This orchid thrives in shaded, moist environments and requires careful cultivation to mimic its natural habitat.
