Leptomyrtus

Leptomyrtus, commonly known as the Christmasberry, is a genus of flowering plants in the Myrtaceae family.

These plants are native to Australia and are characterized by their small, clustered flowers and woody stems. The leaves are typically narrow and have a glossy appearance, while the fruit is a small, red, fleshy berry. Leptomyrtus species are often found in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments.

They play an important role in local ecosystems by providing food for birds and other wildlife.
