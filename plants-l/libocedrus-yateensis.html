Libocedrus yateensis

Libocedrus yateensis, commonly known as the Yate cedar, is a species of conifer native to southern Chile.

It is characterized by its slender, evergreen foliage and distinctive, flattened cones. The plant thrives in temperate rainforest environments, often growing in moist, well-drained soils. Its bark is reddish-brown and becomes fissured with age, while its leaves are scale-like and arranged in spirals.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
