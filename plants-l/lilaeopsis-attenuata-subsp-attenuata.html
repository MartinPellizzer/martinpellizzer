Lilaeopsis attenuata subsp. attenuata

Lilaeopsis attenuata subsp.

attenuata, commonly known as the slender spike grass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered with fine hairs.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects in degraded landscapes.
