Lippia wilmsii var. villosa

Lippia wilmsii var. villosa, commonly known as Mexican mint marigold, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for warm climates. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects.
