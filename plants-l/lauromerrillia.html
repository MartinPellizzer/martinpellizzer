Lauromerrillia

Lauromerrillia, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, featuring a broad, rounded crown. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruits are pods that split open to release seeds, aiding in its propagation.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to provide shade.
