Liatris baicalensis

Liatris baicalensis, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features upright, spiky flower heads that range in color from purple to pink, surrounded by narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower structure and medicinal properties in some species.
