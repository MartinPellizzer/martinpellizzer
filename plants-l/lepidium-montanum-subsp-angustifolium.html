Lepidium montanum subsp. angustifolium

Lepidium montanum subsp.

angustifolium, commonly known as narrowleaf peppergrass, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as meadows and open woodlands. It has linear, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
