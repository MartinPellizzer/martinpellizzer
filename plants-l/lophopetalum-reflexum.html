Lophopetalum reflexum

Lophopetalum reflexum, commonly known as the Chinese hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often lobed or serrated. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in clusters, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Lophopetalum reflexum prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it adaptable to a variety of garden settings.
