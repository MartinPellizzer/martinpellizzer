Linum gallicum subvar. aureum

Linum gallicum subvar. aureum, commonly known as the golden flax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry grasslands and rocky areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in textile production.
