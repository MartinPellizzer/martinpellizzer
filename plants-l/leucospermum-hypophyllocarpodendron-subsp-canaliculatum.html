Leucospermum hypophyllocarpodendron subsp. canaliculatum

Leucospermum hypophyllocarpodendron subsp.

canaliculatum, commonly known as the red-hot poker or fireplant, is a striking evergreen shrub native to South Africa. It is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescences that bear bright orange to red tubular flowers, which are adapted for pollination by birds and insects.

The plant has narrow, needle-like leaves that are densely packed, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
