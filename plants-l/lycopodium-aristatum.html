Lycopodium aristatum

Lycopodium aristatum, commonly known as the clubmoss, is a small, evergreen plant that belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, branching structure and the presence of small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces spore-bearing cones, which are typically found at the tips of its branches. Lycopodium aristatum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
