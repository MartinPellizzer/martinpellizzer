Lastrea filix-mas var. cochleata

Lastrea filix-mas var. cochleata, commonly known as the curled snake plant, is a unique variant of the snake plant species.

It is characterized by its distinctive, curling leaves that form a spiral shape, resembling a snail's shell. This plant is native to tropical regions and thrives in low-light conditions with minimal watering. Its foliage is typically a deep green, with some varieties displaying variegated patterns.

The curled leaves provide a striking visual appearance, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
