Lygodesmia ramosissima

Lygodesmia ramosissima, commonly known as the silverleaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees in tropical and subtropical regions.

It has small, leathery leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white, powdery bloom, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, fleshy berries that are dispersed by birds. Its parasitic nature allows it to draw water and nutrients from its host, while still performing some photosynthesis.

Lygodesmia ramosissima is often found in moist, shaded environments, contributing to the biodiversity of forest ecosystems.
