Lonicera kurobushiensis

Lonicera kurobushiensis, commonly known as the Japanese honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its twining stems and opposite leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually yellow or orange in color, blooming in the spring. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Lonicera kurobushiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
