Linnaea borealis f. latiloba

Linnaea borealis f. latiloba, commonly known as the broad-leafed twinflower, is a rare variant of the twinflower plant native to the northern regions of North America and Eurasia.

It is a low-growing evergreen shrub characterized by its small, opposite leaves and fragrant, bell-shaped white flowers. The leaves of this form are broader and more elongated compared to the typical species, giving it a distinct appearance. It thrives in shaded, moist environments such as boreal forests and rocky slopes.
