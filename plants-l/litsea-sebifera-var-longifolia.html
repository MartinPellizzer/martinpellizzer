Litsea sebifera var. longifolia

Litsea sebifera var. longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf litsea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, elliptical leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces drupes that are typically red or orange when mature, containing a single seed. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and aromatic properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and for essential oil extraction.
