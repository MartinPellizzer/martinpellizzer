Laelia anceps var. waddoniensis

Laelia anceps var. waddoniensis, commonly known as the Waddoniensis orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces fragrant, showy flowers with a mix of white, pink, and purple hues. The plant typically blooms during the cooler months, showcasing its ornamental value in both greenhouse and garden settings. It prefers well-draining soil and moderate to high light exposure for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its unique coloration and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
