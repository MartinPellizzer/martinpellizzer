Lucuma novocaledonica

Lucuma novocaledonica, commonly known as the New Caledonia lucuma, is a species of flowering plant native to the island of New Caledonia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its evergreen shrub form, with smooth, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, yellow-orange fruits resembling small apples. The fruit's pulp is rich in nutrients and has a sweet, nutty flavor, making it a valued crop in local cuisine.

Due to its limited geographic range, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
