Lepanthes arachnion

Lepanthes arachnion, commonly known as the spider orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, spider-like appearance, with long, slender petals and a distinctive, intricate flower structure. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as cloud forests, where it thrives on the forest floor or on tree trunks. Its flowers are usually small and pale, often with a subtle fragrance that attracts specific pollinators.

Lepanthes arachnion is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated by enthusiasts of rare orchids.
