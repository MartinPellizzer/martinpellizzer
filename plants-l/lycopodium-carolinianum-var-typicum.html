Lycopodium carolinianum var. typicum

Lycopodium carolinianum var. typicum, commonly known as the Carolina clubmoss, is a small evergreen fern-like plant native to the eastern United States.

It has a distinctive appearance with spirally arranged, needle-like leaves that form dense, compact clusters. The plant produces spore-bearing cones called strobili, which are typically reddish-brown in color. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded forest environments.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in certain ecosystems.
