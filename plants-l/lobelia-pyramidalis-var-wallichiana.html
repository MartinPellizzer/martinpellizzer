Lobelia pyramidalis var. wallichiana

Lobelia pyramidalis var. wallichiana, commonly known as the Himalayan lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its compact, pyramid-shaped growth habit and clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough, hairy texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
