Lactuca stricta var. integrifolia

Lactuca stricta var. integrifolia, commonly known as the slender lettuce, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. It is native to Europe and western Asia but has been introduced to other regions.

Lactuca stricta var. integrifolia is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in grassy areas.
