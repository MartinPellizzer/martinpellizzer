Lobelia subnuda

Lobelia subnuda, commonly known as the naked lobelia, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, tubular blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Lobelia subnuda is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
