Lagerstroemia collettii

Lagerstroemia collettii, commonly known as the Collett's crape myrtle, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red, appearing in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, round fruits that split open to release seeds, contributing to its natural distribution. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and turn vibrant yellow or red in the fall.

Lagerstroemia collettii is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
