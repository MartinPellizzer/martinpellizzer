Laurus venosa

Laurus venosa, commonly known as sweet bay laurel, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark green, glossy leaves that are often used in culinary and aromatic applications. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental and medicinal purposes.

Its essential oils, derived from the leaves, are valued for their fragrance and therapeutic properties.
