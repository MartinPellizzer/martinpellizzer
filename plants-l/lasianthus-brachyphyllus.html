Lasianthus brachyphyllus

Lasianthus brachyphyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lasianthus, is a species of flowering plant in the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered in whorls, often displaying shades of pink or purple.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
