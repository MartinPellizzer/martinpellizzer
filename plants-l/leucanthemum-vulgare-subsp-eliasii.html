Leucanthemum vulgare subsp. eliasii

Leucanthemum vulgare subsp.

eliasii, commonly known as the Elias's white marguerite, is a flowering plant native to central and eastern North America. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. It produces white to pale yellow flowers with yellow centers, which bloom in late summer to early fall.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly larger flower heads and more robust growth compared to other members of the species.
