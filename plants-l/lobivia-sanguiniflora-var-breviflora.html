Lobivia sanguiniflora var. breviflora

Lobivia sanguiniflora var. breviflora, commonly known as the blood-flowered lobivia, is a succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It features a compact, rounded stem with a thick, fleshy body that stores water, typical of cacti and succulents. The plant produces vibrant red flowers during the spring and summer months, which are borne on short, branched inflorescences. Its leaves are small, spiny, and often covered in a fine, white powdery coating called farina.

This variety is prized for its unique coloration and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in dry climates.
