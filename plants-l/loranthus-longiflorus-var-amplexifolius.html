Loranthus longiflorus var. amplexifolius

Loranthus longiflorus var. amplexifolius, commonly known as the leafy mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that attaches itself to host trees, primarily in the genus Eucalyptus.

It has a distinctive appearance with its dense, evergreen foliage and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are often clasping the stem, giving it a unique, almost decorative look. It is native to Australia and is often found in coastal regions.

This plant plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
