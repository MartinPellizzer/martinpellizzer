Leptodesmia microphylla f. glaber

Leptodesmia microphylla f. glaber, commonly known as the smooth-leaved leptodesmia, is a variant of the species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically glabrous, or smooth to the touch. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color. Its growth habit is often prostrate or ascending, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

This fern-like plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
