Lilium maximowiczii var. tigrinum

Lilium maximowiczii var. tigrinum, commonly known as the tigrine lily, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow, with a distinctive tiger-striped pattern on the petals. The plant features strap-like, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, and it grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity in the wild, it is often cultivated by horticulturists and plant enthusiasts.
