Liatris lanata

Liatris lanata, commonly known as the woolly bluemink, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and clusters of lavender-blue to purple flowers. The plant produces small, cylindrical flower heads that are arranged in a spike-like formation. Its leaves are narrow, hairy, and often have a bluish-gray hue.

Liatris lanata is typically found in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and wet meadows.
