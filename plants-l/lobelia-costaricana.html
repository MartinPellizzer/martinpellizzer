Lobelia costaricana

Lobelia costaricana, commonly known as the Costa Rican lobelia, is a flowering plant native to Central America, particularly Costa Rica.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often hairy, while its stems are erect and branched.

Lobelia costaricana is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
