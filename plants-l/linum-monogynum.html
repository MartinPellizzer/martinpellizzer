Linum monogynum

Linum monogynum, commonly known as common flax, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe, western Asia, and northwestern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are usually smooth or slightly hairy. The plant produces showy blue or purple flowers with five petals, which bloom in early summer. Its seeds are encased in a capsule and are used in the production of linen and oil.

Linum monogynum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
