Limnanthemum verrucosum

Limnanthemum verrucosum, commonly known as the waxy flower or velvet flower, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wetland or marshy environments. The plant features small, showy flowers that are usually pink or white in color, with a waxy texture. Its leaves are often covered in a fine, velvety coating, which gives it its common name.

Limnanthemum verrucosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with moist soil conditions.
