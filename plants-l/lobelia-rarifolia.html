Lobelia rarifolia

Lobelia rarifolia, commonly known as the rareleaf lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a distinctive hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
