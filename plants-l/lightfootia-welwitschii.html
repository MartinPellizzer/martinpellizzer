Lightfootia welwitschii

Lightfootia welwitschii, commonly known as the Welwitschia mirabilis, is a unique succulent native to Namibia and Angola.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant has a short, woody stem and can grow up to several meters in height. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the early morning.

Due to its slow growth and rarity, it is considered a living fossil and a symbol of resilience in harsh climates.
