Lasianthus kwangsiensis

Lasianthus kwangsiensis, commonly known as the Guangxi lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia, particularly China.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its erect or semi-woody growth habit. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
