Loasa argentina

Loasa argentina, commonly known as the Argentine loasa, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom in spring and attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Loasa argentina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in suitable climates.
