Lasia

Lasia, commonly known as the glassworts, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Amaranthaceae.

These plants are typically found in saline or coastal environments, thriving in areas with high salt content in the soil. They are characterized by their succulent, fleshy leaves and small, clustered flowers that often appear in shades of red, pink, or white. Lasia species are often used in horticulture for their ornamental value and ability to tolerate harsh conditions.

Their unique adaptations make them important components of coastal ecosystems.
