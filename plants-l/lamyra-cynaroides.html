Lamyra cynaroides

Lamyra cynaroides, commonly known as the common lamyra, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf structures. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its yellow flowers are typically arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, attracting various pollinators.

Lamyra cynaroides is often found in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils.
