Listera puberula var. maculata

Listera puberula var. maculata, commonly known as the spotted basketflower, is a delicate perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves that are hairy and often have a bluish-gray hue. The plant produces clusters of white or pale green flowers that are bell-shaped and arranged in a whorled pattern. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its unique appearance and habitat preferences make it a notable species in botanical studies and native plant gardens.
