Lutzia salicina

Lutzia salicina, commonly known as the salt marsh morning glory, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in brackish or saline environments, often found in marshes and tidal flats. The plant produces striking blue or violet trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the early morning. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and often trailing.

Lutzia salicina plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various insects and birds.
