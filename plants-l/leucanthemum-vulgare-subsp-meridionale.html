Leucanthemum vulgare subsp. meridionale

Leucanthemum vulgare subsp.

meridionale, commonly known as the southern oxeye daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its white petals surrounding a prominent yellow center, forming a daisy-like flower.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and meadows. It has a compact, bushy growth habit with hairy leaves that are typically alternate and lance-shaped.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
