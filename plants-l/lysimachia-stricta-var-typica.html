Lysimachia stricta var. typica

Lysimachia stricta var. typica, commonly known as narrowleaf mountain-mint, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as stream banks and woodland edges. The plant features upright, slender stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its small, white to pale blue flowers are clustered in dense, terminal spikes, blooming in early to mid-summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
