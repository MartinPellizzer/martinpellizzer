Limonia mauritiana

Limonia mauritiana, commonly known as the sour orange, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The fruit is round, yellowish-orange, and has a tart, acidic flavor. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of citric acid.

This plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
