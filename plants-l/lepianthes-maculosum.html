Lepianthes maculosum

Lepianthes maculosum, commonly known as the spotted poinsettia, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble petals. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.
