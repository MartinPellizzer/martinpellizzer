Lampujang

Lampujang, commonly known as the Java flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Bignoniaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, bright red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with opposite arrangement and produces small, round fruits that split open to release seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their potential therapeutic properties.
