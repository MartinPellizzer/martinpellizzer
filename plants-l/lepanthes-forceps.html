Lepanthes forceps

Lepanthes forceps, commonly known as the forceps orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. The plant produces small, strap-like leaves and has a compact growth habit. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized orchid species.
