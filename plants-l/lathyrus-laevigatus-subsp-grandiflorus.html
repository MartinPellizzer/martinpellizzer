Lathyrus laevigatus subsp. grandiflorus

Lathyrus laevigatus subsp.

grandiflorus, commonly known as the large-flowered vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its stout, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with a serrated margin.

The plant produces showy, pea-like flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep purple, typically blooming in spring. Its trifoliate leaves and pod-like fruits are typical of the Fabaceae family.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
