Lantana minasensis var. longibracteolata

Lantana minasensis var. longibracteolata, commonly known as Brazilian lantana, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its compact shrub form and clusters of small, brightly colored flowers that range from yellow to orange. The leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while the bracts are elongated and often more prominent than the actual flowers. This species is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.

It is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
