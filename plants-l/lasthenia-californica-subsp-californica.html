Lasthenia californica subsp. californica

Lasthenia californica subsp.

californica, commonly known as the California popcorn flower, is a low-growing perennial herb native to coastal regions of California. It features dense clusters of bright yellow, dandelion-like flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant has hairy, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal grasslands and dunes.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
