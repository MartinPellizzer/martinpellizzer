Lepanthes flaccida

Lepanthes flaccida, commonly known as the limp orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant features a slender, erect stem with a few elongated, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small, white, and fragrant, often clustered in a raceme.

Lepanthes flaccida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
