Leucanthemum vulgare subsp. subglaucum

Leucanthemum vulgare subsp.

subglaucum, commonly known as the blue-eyed Mary, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a blue-centered disc.

The subspecies subglaucum is distinguished by its grayish-blue foliage and slightly shorter flower stems compared to the typical variety. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and along roadsides.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
