Limnia viae

Limnia viae, commonly known as the river waterplant, is a small aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Europe.

It typically grows in slow-moving streams and ponds, where it attaches itself to submerged surfaces. The plant has narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along its stem. Limnia viae produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color.

It plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small invertebrates and contributing to water clarity.
