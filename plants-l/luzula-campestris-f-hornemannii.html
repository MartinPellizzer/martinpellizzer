Luzula campestris f. hornemannii

Luzula campestris f. hornemannii, commonly known as the Hornemann's wood rush, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and is typically found in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, clustered flower heads that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its rhizomes are often used in horticulture for ground cover in shaded gardens.

This fern-like plant is notable for its adaptability to cool, damp climates and its role in stabilizing soil in woodland areas.
