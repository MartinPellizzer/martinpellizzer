Lessingianthus flotowioides

Lessingianthus flotowioides, commonly known as the Andean sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, temperate climates with well-drained soils. Its bright yellow ray florets and dark central disk make it visually striking, while its resilience to harsh conditions makes it a notable species in alpine ecosystems.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal uses in traditional medicine.
