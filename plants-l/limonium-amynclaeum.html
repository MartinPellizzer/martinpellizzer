Limonium amynclaeum

Limonium amynclaeum, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiky, blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and saline soils, showing a high tolerance for salt and wind. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of salt.

Limonium amynclaeum is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in challenging environments.
