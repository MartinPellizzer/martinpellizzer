Lappago

Lappago, commonly known as the false lily of the valley, is a member of the Primulaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white or pink flowers. Its flowers are often followed by bright red berries that contain the seeds.

Lappago is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
