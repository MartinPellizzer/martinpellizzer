Leptadenia imberbis

Leptadenia imberbis, commonly known as the African milk vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by follicles containing elliptical, smooth seeds. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is used in traditional medicine for its purported medicinal properties.

Its latex is milky and can cause skin irritation.
