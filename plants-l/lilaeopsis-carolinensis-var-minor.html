Lilaeopsis carolinensis var. minor

Lilaeopsis carolinensis var. minor, commonly known as the Carolina lespedeza, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, trifoliate, and have a distinctive trifoliate structure.

It thrives in sandy or loamy soils and is often found in open woodlands and disturbed areas.
