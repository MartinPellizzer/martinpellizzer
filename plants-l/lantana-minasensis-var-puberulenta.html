Lantana minasensis var. puberulenta

Lantana minasensis var. puberulenta, commonly known as the Minas Lantana, is a shrub native to Brazil.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow to orange, which bloom throughout the growing season. It is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and thrives in well-drained soils.

This variant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
