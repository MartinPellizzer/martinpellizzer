Lactuca bungeana

Lactuca bungeana, commonly known as Bunge's lettuce, is a species of flowering plant in the daisy family, Asteraceae.

It is native to regions of Central Asia and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are typically hairy and bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its edible leaves, which have a mild, slightly bitter taste.

Lactuca bungeana is also known for containing lactucine, a compound that can have mild sedative effects.
