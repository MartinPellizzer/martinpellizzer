Lachenalia polypodantha

Lachenalia polypodantha, commonly known as the Cape goldcup, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllis family (Amaryllidaceae) and is characterized by its compact, rosette-forming growth habit. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in summer, often in clusters at the base of the leaves. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Lachenalia polypodantha is a popular choice for rock gardens and succulent collections due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
