Lilium flaviflorum

Lilium flaviflorum, commonly known as the yellow-flowered lily, is a species of flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow petals and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for its beauty and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
