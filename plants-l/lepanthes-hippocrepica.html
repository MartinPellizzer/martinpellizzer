Lepanthes hippocrepica

Lepanthes hippocrepica, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers with intricate patterns of dark veins. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its leaves are elongated and dark green, often with a waxy coating to retain moisture.

The species is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
