Linaria nurensis

Linaria nurensis, commonly known as the Nurensis toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and bright yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are linear and slightly hairy, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.

Linaria nurensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
