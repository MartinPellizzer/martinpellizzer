Leptomeria acerba

Leptomeria acerba, commonly known as the slender leaved rockrose, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, needle-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of pale yellow to orange flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Leptomeria acerba is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
