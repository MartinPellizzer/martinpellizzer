Litsea lancifolia var. alternifolia

Litsea lancifolia var. alternifolia, commonly known as the alternate-leafed litsea, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub, characterized by its alternate arrangement of leaves. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by drupes that are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and aromatic properties.
