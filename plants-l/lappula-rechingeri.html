Lappula rechingeri

Lappula rechingeri, commonly known as the desert lappula, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing annual herb with hairy stems and small, greenish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is adapted to survive in harsh environments with limited water availability.

Its botanical features make it an important component of desert ecosystems.
