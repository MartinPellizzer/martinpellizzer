Linum bellidifolium

Linum bellidifolium, commonly known as the golden flax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Linaceae family, which includes other flax species. The plant features delicate, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a bluish-green hue.

Linum bellidifolium is typically found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
