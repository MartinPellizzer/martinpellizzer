Lupinus dorae var. paucoflorus

Lupinus dorae var. paucoflorus, commonly known as the sparse-flowered lupine, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clustered, spiny stems. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it has a deep root system that helps stabilize soil.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and nitrogen-fixing ability, making it beneficial for ecological restoration projects.
