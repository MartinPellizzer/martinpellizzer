Laelia lobata var. alba

Laelia lobata var. alba, commonly known as the white laelia, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its elegant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a subtle fragrance. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial orchid, preferring cool, humid environments with well-draining soil. Its leaves are usually broad and grass-like, and it thrives in partial shade.

This variety is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and relative ease of cultivation.
