Laelia anceps var. chamberlainiana

Laelia anceps var. chamberlainiana, commonly known as the Chamberlain's Laelia, is a hybrid orchid native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a sympodial orchid with a compact growth habit, featuring dense, fleshy rhizomes and multiple pseudobulbs. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are broad, leathery, and arranged in two rows along the stem.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses due to its specific climatic requirements.
