Laphamia rupestris

Laphamia rupestris, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are basal, oblanceolate, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and appear in spring.
