Lycopodium amentaceum

Lycopodium amentaceum, commonly known as the clubmoss, is a small evergreen plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems that bear tiny, scale-like leaves. The plant produces cone-like structures called strobili, which contain the spores necessary for reproduction. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas.

Lycopodium amentaceum plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various small organisms.
