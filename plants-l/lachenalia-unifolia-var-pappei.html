Lachenalia unifolia var. pappei

Lachenalia unifolia var. pappei, commonly known as the pappei lachenalia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are typically blue-gray in color. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, which bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of care, it is widely cultivated by gardeners in arid and semi-arid regions.
