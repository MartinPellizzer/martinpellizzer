Leandra quinquedentata

Leandra quinquedentata, commonly known as the five-toothed leandra, is a striking flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that consist of numerous small, fragrant flowers clustered in a spiky, branched structure. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or terrestrial species, often found in humid, shaded forest environments. Its leaves are usually elongated and have a waxy texture, while its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the air or soil.

Leandra quinquedentata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm, humid climates.
