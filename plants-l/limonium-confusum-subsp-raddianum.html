Limonium confusum subsp. raddianum

Limonium confusum subsp.

raddianum, commonly known as the pink sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, showy pink to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating.

This subspecies is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand salty air and coastal conditions.
