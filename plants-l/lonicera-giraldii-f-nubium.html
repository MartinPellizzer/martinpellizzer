Lonicera giraldii f. nubium

Lonicera giraldii f. nubium, commonly known as the alpine honeysuckle, is a subspecies of the honeysuckle plant native to high-altitude regions of China.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically features dark green, oval-shaped leaves and produces red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
