Lopezia albiflora

Lopezia albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered Lopezia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, showy white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often in rocky soils or along dry streambeds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, dry fruits that contain minute seeds.

Lopezia albiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscapes due to its drought tolerance.
