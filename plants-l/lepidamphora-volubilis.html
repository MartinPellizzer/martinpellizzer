Lepidamphora volubilis

Lepidamphora volubilis, commonly known as the creeping waterwheel, is a climbing aquatic plant native to South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often submerged, with a distinctive waxy surface that helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, white flowers that emerge above the water surface, typically in late spring or early summer. Its roots form dense mats that can stabilize sediments in shallow water bodies.

Lepidamphora volubilis is often found in slow-moving streams and ponds, where it plays a role in aquatic ecosystem dynamics.
