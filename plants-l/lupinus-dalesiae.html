Lupinus dalesiae

Lupinus dalesiae, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, spindly growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained soils in open, sunny habitats.

Lupinus dalesiae plays an important ecological role by fixing nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plant life.
