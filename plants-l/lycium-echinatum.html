Lycium echinatum

Lycium echinatum, commonly known as the spiny boxthorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces red berries that are edible for birds and small mammals, aiding in seed dispersal. It thrives in arid and semi-arid conditions, making it well adapted to harsh environments.

Lycium echinatum is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to control erosion.
