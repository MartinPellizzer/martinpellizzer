Lachnopylis viscosa

Lachnopylis viscosa, commonly known as the sticky lichen, is a unique plant that belongs to the family Lachnopiaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety growth and a sticky coating on its surface, which helps in retaining moisture. This plant thrives in humid and shaded environments, often found on tree trunks or rocks in tropical regions. Its structure consists of a thallus, which is a complex network of filaments.

Lachnopylis viscosa plays an important role in nutrient cycling and provides habitat for various microorganisms.
