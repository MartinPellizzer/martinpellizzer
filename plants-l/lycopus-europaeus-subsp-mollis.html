Lycopus europaeus subsp. mollis

Lycopus europaeus subsp.

mollis, commonly known as European bugleweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features opposite, oval-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its roots are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported calming effects.

Botanically, it belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage.
