Liparis mantidopsis

Liparis mantidopsis, commonly known as the mantis orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, greenish-yellow flowers that resemble a mantis, hence its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Liparis mantidopsis is valued for its unique appearance and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
