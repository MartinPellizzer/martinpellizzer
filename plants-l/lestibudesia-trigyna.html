Lestibudesia trigyna

Lestibudesia trigyna, commonly known as the three-lobed orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in clusters. The plant has three distinct lobes on its petals, which give it its scientific name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Lestibudesia trigyna is often cultivated for its ornamental value in botanical gardens and private collections.
