Leopoldia curta

Leopoldia curta, commonly known as the dwarf leopoldia, is a small herbaceous plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It typically grows to a height of less than 10 centimeters, with compact, rosette-forming leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces delicate, star-shaped flowers in shades of white and pale pink, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
