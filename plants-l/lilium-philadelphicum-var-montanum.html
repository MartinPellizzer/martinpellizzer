Lilium philadelphicum var. montanum

Lilium philadelphicum var. montanum, commonly known as the mountain lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, supporting a cluster of showy, fragrant flowers. The flowers are typically pink to red in color, with six to nine petals and a distinctive, recurved shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open meadows and forest clearings.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also an important nectar source for pollinators.
