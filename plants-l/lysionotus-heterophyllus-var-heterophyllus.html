Lysionotus heterophyllus var. heterophyllus

Lysionotus heterophyllus var. heterophyllus, commonly known as the variable leaf lythrum, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaves that vary in shape and size along the stem, a feature that gives it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or wetlands. It produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
