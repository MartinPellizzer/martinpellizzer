Lavandula × limae

Lavandula × limae, commonly known as Portuguese lavender, is a hybrid shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features aromatic, narrow leaves and spikes of violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and fragrant, needle-like foliage. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its essential oils are valued for their calming properties and use in aromatherapy and perfumery.
