Lagochilus hirsutissimus

Lagochilus hirsutissimus, commonly known as the hairy lips of the mountain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the rocky and arid regions of the Mediterranean and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or purple, arranged in loose clusters. Its hairy, gray-green foliage helps it retain moisture in dry environments.

Lagochilus hirsutissimus is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses in some regions.
