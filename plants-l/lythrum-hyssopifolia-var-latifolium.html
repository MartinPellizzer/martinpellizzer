Lythrum hyssopifolia var. latifolium

Lythrum hyssopifolia var. latifolium, commonly known as broadleaf lythrum, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features upright stems that can grow up to 2 to 3 meters tall, covered in narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces spikes of showy, violet-pink flowers that bloom from midsummer to early fall. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive, soft green color.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
