Lupinus angustifolius f. epigonalus

Lupinus angustifolius f. epigonalus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lupine, is a botanical variant characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and erect growth habit.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is native to regions of western North America, particularly California. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in nitrogen fixation, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
