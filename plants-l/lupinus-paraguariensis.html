Lupinus paraguariensis

Lupinus paraguariensis, commonly known as the Paraguayan lupin, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Paraguay and Argentina.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces cylindrical, spiny pods that contain several seeds, which are often used as a protein-rich food source.

Lupinus paraguariensis is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
