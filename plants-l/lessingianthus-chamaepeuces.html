Lessingianthus chamaepeuces

Lessingianthus chamaepeuces, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers, creating a striking appearance. It typically grows in dry, open habitats and is often found in disturbed areas.

The leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, while the stems are erect and often branched.
