Luma mucronata

Luma mucronata, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in cloud forests. The plant features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red in color, with five petals. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Luma mucronata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in temperate climates.
