Lasiorrhiza glandulosa

Lasiorrhiza glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular lasion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
