Lissochilus speciosus var. culveri

Lissochilus speciosus var. culveri, commonly known as the Culver's lissochilus, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky slopes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are often purple or pink in color.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its beauty.
