Lupinus magniflorus

Lupinus magniflorus, commonly known as the showy lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and vibrant, often purple or pink flowers. The plant has pinnately compound leaves and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for native gardens and ecological restoration projects.

Lupinus magniflorus plays an important role in nitrogen fixation, enriching the soil for other plants.
