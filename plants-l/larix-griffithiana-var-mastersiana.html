Larix griffithiana var. mastersiana

Larix griffithiana var. mastersiana, commonly known as the Griffiths' larch, is a rare and ornamental conifer native to the Himalayas.

It is a variant of the Himalayan larch, characterized by its unique foliage and growth habit. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that mature to release seeds, typical of coniferous species. Its needles are typically a vibrant green, turning golden in autumn, and it can reach heights of up to 20 meters.

This variety is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental value.
