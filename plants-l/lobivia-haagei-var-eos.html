Lobivia haagei var. eos

Lobivia haagei var. eos, commonly known as the Pink Lobivia, is a succulent native to the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It features a compact, rounded rosette of fleshy, bluish-green leaves that turn pink or red at the edges when exposed to sunlight. The plant produces tall, branched stems with small, tubular flowers that range from pink to violet in color. It is drought-tolerant and requires well-draining soil to thrive.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
