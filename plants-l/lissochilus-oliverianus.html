Lissochilus oliverianus

Lissochilus oliverianus, commonly known as the Oliver's slipper orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America, particularly found in regions of Peru and Bolivia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped flowers that are typically pale green to yellowish in color. The plant grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in cool, humid environments. Its flowers bloom during the cooler months, and the plant is known for its delicate appearance and specialized pollination mechanisms.

Due to its habitat specificity and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
