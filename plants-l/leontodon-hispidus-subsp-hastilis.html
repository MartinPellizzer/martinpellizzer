Leontodon hispidus subsp. hastilis

Leontodon hispidus subsp.

hastilis, commonly known as the rough hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, meadows, and forest edges.

The plant is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and spike-like inflorescence. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that are often green or pale yellow in color.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for invertebrates.
