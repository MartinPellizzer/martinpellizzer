Loranthus syringifolius

Loranthus syringifolius, commonly known as the Chinese mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily in the family Rutaceae.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using the light it captures. This species is often found in forested areas of China and has cultural significance in traditional medicine.
