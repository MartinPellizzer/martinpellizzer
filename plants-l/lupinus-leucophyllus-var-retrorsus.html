Lupinus leucophyllus var. retrorsus

Lupinus leucophyllus var. retrorsus, commonly known as the whiteleaf lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of white to pale blue flowers. The plant has palmately divided leaves with soft, hairy surfaces, and its flowers are typically arranged in racemes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Lupinus leucophyllus var. retrorsus is known for its ecological importance as a nitrogen-fixing plant that supports local wildlife.
