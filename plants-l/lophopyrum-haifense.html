Lophopyrum haifense

Lophopyrum haifense, commonly known as the Haifa grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Middle East and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slight pubescence, contributing to its drought tolerance.

Lophopyrum haifense is of interest in botanical studies due to its ecological resilience and potential for use in restoration projects.
