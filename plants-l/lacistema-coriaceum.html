Lacistema coriaceum

Lacistema coriaceum, commonly known as the rough lacistema, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and resilience.
