Limonium × capdeperae

Limonium × capdeperae, commonly known as sea lavender, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking spikes of blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, preferring sandy or gravelly soils and full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating.

Limonium × capdeperae is widely cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
