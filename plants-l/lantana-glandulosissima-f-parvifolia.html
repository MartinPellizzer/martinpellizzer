Lantana glandulosissima f. parvifolia

Lantana glandulosissima f. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-flowered lantana, is a compact shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, depending on the variety. The plant has hairy leaves and stems, which are characteristic of the Lantana genus. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

This variety is noted for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
