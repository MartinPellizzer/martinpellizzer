Lysipomia

Lysipomia, commonly known as the "silverweed," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Amaranthaceae.

These plants are characterized by their silver- or gray-hued foliage, which results from dense covering of fine hairs. Lysipomia species are typically found in moist, temperate environments, often growing along stream banks or in wet meadows. They produce small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, depending on the species.

The genus includes several species, such as Lysipomia bicolor and Lysipomia hirta, which are valued for their ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
