Listera australis

Listera australis, commonly known as the Australian basketflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Australia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and heathlands. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in a fine, hairy texture.

Listera australis is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local insect populations.
