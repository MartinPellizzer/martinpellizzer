Liabum tabanense

Liabum tabanense, commonly known as the African tamarind.

It is a shrub native to tropical Africa, characterized by its pinnate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces pods containing edible seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
