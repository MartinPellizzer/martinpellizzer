Lathyrus palustris var. retusus

Lathyrus palustris var. retusus, commonly known as the marsh vetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It features slender, ascending stems that often root at the nodes, and its leaves are pinnately compound with small, ovate leaflets. The flowers are typically blue to purple, pea-shaped, and appear in racemes during the summer months. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, pond edges, and along stream banks.

This plant plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and wildlife.
