Leonurus canescens

Leonurus canescens, commonly known as the hairy leonurus, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, opposite leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers are typically pink or purple, clustered in whorls along the stems, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Leonurus canescens is often found in open woodlands, meadows, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.
