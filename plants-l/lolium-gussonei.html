Lolium gussonei

Lolium gussonei, commonly known as the Gussone's grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Europe.

It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, two-lobed spikelets.

Lolium gussonei is notable for its adaptability and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
