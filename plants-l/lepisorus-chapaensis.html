Lepisorus chapaensis

Lepisorus chapaensis, commonly known as the Chapa fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its rhizome is creeping, and it produces small, clustered sporangia on the underside of its leaves.

Lepisorus chapaensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shady gardens.
