Lemyrea

Lemyrea, commonly known as the silver leaf plant, is a unique species native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage, which helps it reflect sunlight and conserve moisture in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from pale blue to violet, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Lemyrea is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.

Its adaptability to poor soils makes it a resilient choice for rocky or sandy landscapes.
