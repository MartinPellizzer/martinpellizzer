Lathyrus pannonicus var. austriacus

Lathyrus pannonicus var. austriacus, commonly known as the Austrian vetch, is a perennial legume native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep purple. The plant produces cylindrical, bean-like pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it an important plant for both ecological studies and traditional herbal uses.
