Lactuca nakaiana

Lactuca nakaiana, commonly known as Nakaiana lettuce, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family, Asteraceae.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are typically hairy and slightly lobed. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks.

Lactuca nakaiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
