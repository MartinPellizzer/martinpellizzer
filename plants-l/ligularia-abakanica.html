Ligularia abakanica

Ligularia abakanica, commonly known as the Abakan ligularia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often deeply lobed and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas near streams or in wetlands.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
