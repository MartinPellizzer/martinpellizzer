Linum alpinum var. pseudomontanum

Linum alpinum var. pseudomontanum, commonly known as the alpine flax, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of blue or white flowers, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in alpine gardens.
