Lupinus subvexus var. subvexus

Lupinus subvexus var. subvexus, commonly known as the narrowleaf lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Lupinus subvexus var. subvexus is a valuable plant for both ecological restoration and ornamental gardening.
