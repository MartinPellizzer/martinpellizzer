Lastrea pallida

Lastrea pallida, commonly known as the ghost plant, is a unique succulent native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its pale green, fleshy leaves that resemble translucent, waxy structures. The plant thrives in arid environments and is adapted to store water in its thick, fleshy leaves. It produces small, yellowish flowers during the warmer months, adding a subtle touch of color to its otherwise muted appearance.

Due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts.
