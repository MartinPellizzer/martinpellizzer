Lotus mollis

Lotus mollis, commonly known as the soft lotus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its soft, velvety foliage and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland areas and along stream banks. Its flowers are usually pink or white and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
