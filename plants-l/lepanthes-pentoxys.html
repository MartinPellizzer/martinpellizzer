Lepanthes pentoxys

Lepanthes pentoxys, commonly known as the purple-veined orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers with intricate veining patterns. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded forest environments, where it thrives on the forest floor or as an epiphyte on trees. Its flowers are often pale to dark purple, with a distinctive central stripe that enhances its ornamental appeal.

Lepanthes pentoxys is valued for its beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique floral structure.
