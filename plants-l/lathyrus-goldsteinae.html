Lathyrus goldsteinae

Lathyrus goldsteinae, commonly known as the Goldstein vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. It produces small, often yellow or greenish flowers that attract pollinators such as bees.

Lathyrus goldsteinae is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological systems where it contributes to soil nitrogen fixation.
