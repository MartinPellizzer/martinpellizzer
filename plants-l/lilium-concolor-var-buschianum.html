Lilium concolor var. buschianum

Lilium concolor var. buschianum, commonly known as the Buschian mountain lily, is a variant of the concolor lily native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, supporting large, white to pale pink flowers with a subtle fragrance. The leaves are broad and lance-shaped, arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. This variety thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
