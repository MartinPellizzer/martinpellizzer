Lactuca beesiana

Lactuca beesiana, commonly known as the bees' lettuce, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family, Asteraceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-velvet leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by achene fruits with pappus for wind dispersal. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and roadsides.

Lactuca beesiana is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
