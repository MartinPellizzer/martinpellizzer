Linum perenne subsp. montanum

Linum perenne subsp.

montanum, commonly known as mountain flax, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of blue or violet flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

Its fibrous roots and attractive foliage make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
