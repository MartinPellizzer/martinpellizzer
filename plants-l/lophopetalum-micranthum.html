Lophopetalum micranthum

Lophopetalum micranthum, commonly known as the small-flowered pittosporum, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in spring. These flowers are followed by small, round berries that turn red as they mature.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
