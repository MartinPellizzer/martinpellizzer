Lampranthus subaequalis

Lampranthus subaequalis, commonly known as the "Firecracker" or "Cape firecracker," is a member of the Aizoaceae family, native to South Africa.

It is a succulent shrub that produces striking, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves and is well adapted to arid environments. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is widely cultivated in regions with Mediterranean climates.
