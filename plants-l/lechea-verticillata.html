Lechea verticillata

Lechea verticillata, commonly known as the clustered milkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
