Lansium parasiticum

Lansium parasiticum, commonly known as the mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible fruit with a bright red outer skin and a segmented, white, juicy pulp. It thrives in humid climates and is often found growing as a hemiparasite on other trees.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both horticulture and traditional medicine.
