Lepanthes persimilis

Lepanthes persimilis, commonly known as the slender violet orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically range in color from white to pale pink. The plant grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical forests. Its leaves are usually elongated and narrow, adapted for capturing moisture from the air.

Lepanthes persimilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
