Lotus quinatus var. montanus

Lotus quinatus var. montanus, commonly known as the mountain lotus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It features palmately compound leaves with three to five leaflets, often covered in a fine layer of silky hairs. The plant produces showy, pink or red flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine meadows.

Due to its unique adaptations, it is considered a valuable species for ecological studies and conservation efforts.
