Litsea elongata var. suberosa

Litsea elongata var. suberosa, commonly known as the rough-leafed litsea, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves that are often covered in a rough, pubescent texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are typically clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, dark purple drupe that contains a single seed.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
