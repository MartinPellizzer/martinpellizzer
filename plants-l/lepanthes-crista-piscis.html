Lepanthes crista-piscis

Lepanthes crista-piscis, commonly known as the "Crested Fish Orchid," is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, helmet-shaped flowers that resemble a fish's head, giving it its unique common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with intricate patterns that enhance its ornamental appeal.

Lepanthes crista-piscis is valued for its beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its distinctive appearance.
