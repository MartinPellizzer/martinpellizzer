Lupinus angustifolius f. zhukovskii

Lupinus angustifolius f. zhukovskii, commonly known as the Zhukovskii narrow-leaved lupin, is a botanical variant characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and distinctive floral structure.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers, usually ranging from blue to purple, are arranged in dense spikes, adding ornamental value.

This subspecies is of interest in ecological and horticultural studies due to its resilience and potential for sustainable land use.
