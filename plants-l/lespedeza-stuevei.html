Lespedeza stuevei

Lespedeza stuevei, commonly known as narrowleaf lespedeza, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the United States, particularly in the southeastern region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Lespedeza stuevei is often found in open woodlands, prairies, and along roadsides, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

It is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it a beneficial plant for soil enrichment.
