Lepismium monacanthum

Lepismium monacanthum, commonly known as the yellow nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has a woody stem and produces berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ecological research.
