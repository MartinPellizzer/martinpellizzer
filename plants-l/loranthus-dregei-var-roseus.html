Loranthus dregei var. roseus

Loranthus dregei var. roseus, commonly known as the rose-flowered mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant belonging to the Loranthaceae family.

It grows as a parasitic vine on various host trees, primarily in arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa. The plant is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. Its fruit is a small, fleshy capsule that contains numerous seeds covered in a sticky substance to aid in attachment to host plants.

This species plays an important ecological role by providing food for birds and other animals while also influencing the growth of its host trees.
