Laportea bathiei

Laportea bathiei, commonly known as the Florida ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetlands and swampy areas, showcasing its adaptability to moist environments. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive silver or grayish-white underside, which helps reflect excess sunlight. Its showy, pink to reddish-purple flower heads appear in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Laportea bathiei is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
