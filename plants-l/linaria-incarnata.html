Linaria incarnata

Linaria incarnata, commonly known as the common toadflax, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb that grows up to 60 cm tall, featuring slender stems and opposite leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy. The plant produces vibrant yellow-orange flowers with four petals, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and waste grounds.

Linaria incarnata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
