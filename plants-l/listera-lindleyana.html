Listera lindleyana

Listera lindleyana, commonly known as Lindley's spring beauty, is a delicate perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, light green leaves and clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in rich, well-drained soil. It blooms in early spring, often before the leaves are fully expanded, and is a valuable component of forest ecosystems.

Listera lindleyana is also noted for its medicinal properties, with historical use in traditional herbal remedies.
