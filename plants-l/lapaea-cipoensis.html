Lapaea cipoensis

Lapaea cipoensis, commonly known as the Cipo burr, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive bracts. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish or reddish in color. Its leaves are usually alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.

Lapaea cipoensis is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
