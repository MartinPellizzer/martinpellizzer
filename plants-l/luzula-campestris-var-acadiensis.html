Luzula campestris var. acadiensis

Luzula campestris var. acadiensis, commonly known as the Acadian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are usually green to reddish in color. It is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.

The species is also appreciated in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
