Lepanthes peperomioides

Lepanthes peperomioides, commonly known as the peperomoid orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces small, greenish-white or pale yellow blooms that are clustered in inflorescences. Lepanthes peperomioides prefers shaded, humid environments and is often found growing on tree trunks or in rocky crevices.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
