Lepanthes papallactae

Lepanthes papallactae, commonly known as the Papallacta orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Costa Rica.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, adapted to the humid, shaded conditions of its natural habitat.

Lepanthes papallactae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
