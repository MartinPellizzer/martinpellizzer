Ligularia tulupanica

Ligularia tulupanica, commonly known as the Tibetan ligularia, is a perennial plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces showy yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous areas with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
