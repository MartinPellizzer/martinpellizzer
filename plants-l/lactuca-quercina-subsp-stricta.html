Lactuca quercina subsp. stricta

Lactuca quercina subsp.

stricta, commonly known as the oakleaf lettuce, is a herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive, oak-like leaves that are deeply lobed and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in a loose cluster, which are followed by slender, cylindrical seed pods. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in herbal medicine.
