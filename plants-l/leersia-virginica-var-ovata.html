Leersia virginica var. ovata

Leersia virginica var. ovata, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear lance-shaped leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, white to pale blue flowers. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
