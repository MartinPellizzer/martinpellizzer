Liparis flabellata

Liparis flabellata, commonly known as the fan-leaf orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that often form a fan-like arrangement. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in late spring or early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Liparis flabellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
