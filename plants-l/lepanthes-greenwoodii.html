Lepanthes greenwoodii

Lepanthes greenwoodii, commonly known as the Greenwood's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees in humid tropical environments. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers with white petals and a distinctive yellow lip. Its growth habit and floral structure are typical of the Orchidaceae family, showcasing intricate adaptations for pollination.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
