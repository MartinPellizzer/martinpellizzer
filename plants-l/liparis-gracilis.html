Liparis gracilis

Liparis gracilis, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to temperate regions of Asia and North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. The plant features slender, erect stems with lance-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers in late spring. It is characterized by its delicate appearance and is often found in clusters.

Liparis gracilis is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.
