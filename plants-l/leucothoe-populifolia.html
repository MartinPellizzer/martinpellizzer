Leucothoe populifolia

Leucothoe populifolia, commonly known as sweetshrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth texture and a glossy appearance. It thrives in partial to full shade and moist, well-drained soil.

Leucothoe populifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide habitat for wildlife.
