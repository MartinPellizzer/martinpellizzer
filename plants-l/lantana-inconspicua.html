Lantana inconspicua

Lantana inconspicua, commonly known as the small lantana, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 feet, featuring dense, aromatic foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are usually yellow or orange in color. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

Lantana inconspicua is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
