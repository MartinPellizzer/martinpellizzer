Lactuca denticulata

Lactuca denticulata, commonly known as the toothed lettuce, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family, Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant has alternate, toothed leaves that are typically pale green with a slightly hairy surface. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, followed by fluffy, white seeds.

Lactuca denticulata is not commonly cultivated for food but is sometimes used in herbal remedies.
