Lachenalia orchioides subsp. glaucina

Lachenalia orchioides subsp.

glaucina, commonly known as the blue-flowered lachenalia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It features compact, rosette-forming leaves that are typically blue-green in color, covered in a fine, powdery coating.

The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, often with a distinctive yellow or orange throat. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in arid or semi-arid environments.
