Lasianthus verticillatus var. hirsutus

Lasianthus verticillatus var. hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, often with a velvety texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
