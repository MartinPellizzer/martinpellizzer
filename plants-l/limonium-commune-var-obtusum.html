Limonium commune var. obtusum

Limonium commune var. obtusum, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, often in clusters at the ends of its stems. The plant features silvery, linear leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or sandy soils.

Limonium commune var. obtusum is commonly cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
