Lophoschoenus stagnalis

Lophoschoenus stagnalis, commonly known as the swamp reed or water reed, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland and marsh environments in Australia.

It has elongated, narrow leaves that are typically green and grassy in appearance, growing close to the ground. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikes, which are often submerged or partially submerged in water. It plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for aquatic life.

Lophoschoenus stagnalis is adapted to tolerate fluctuating water levels and is often found in brackish or freshwater environments.
