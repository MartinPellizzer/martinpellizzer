Lactuca sativa var. acephala

Lactuca sativa var. acephala, commonly known as leaf lettuce, is a cool-season crop characterized by its upright, leafy growth habit.

It features a rosette of elongated, tender leaves that are typically light to dark green in color. The plant is cultivated for its edible leaves, which are harvested before the head forms, distinguishing it from other lettuce varieties. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires consistent moisture for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its mild flavor and versatility in culinary applications.
