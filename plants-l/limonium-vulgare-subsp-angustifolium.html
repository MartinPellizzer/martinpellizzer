Limonium vulgare subsp. angustifolium

Limonium vulgare subsp.

angustifolium, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal areas of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and spikes of small, blue to purple flowers.

The plant thrives in saline and well-drained soils, often found in dunes and coastal grasslands. Its flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to tolerate harsh coastal conditions.
