Lathyrus szowitsii

Lathyrus szowitsii, commonly known as the Szowits' vetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several kidney-shaped seeds. Lathyrus szowitsii is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitats.
