Loranthus saccatus

Loranthus saccatus, commonly known as the sac-like mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees.

It lacks true roots and absorbs water and nutrients through its stems, which are embedded into the host's bark. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers and red, fleshy berries that are often consumed by birds. It is native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan, and thrives in temperate climates.

Its parasitic nature and unique reproductive strategy make it an interesting subject in botanical studies.
