Lindsayopsis scandens

Lindsayopsis scandens, commonly known as the climbing lindsayopsis, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit, with slender stems that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, arranged in clusters along the stems. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Lindsayopsis scandens is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive floral display and adaptability to various growing conditions.
