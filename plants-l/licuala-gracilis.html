Licuala gracilis

Licuala gracilis, commonly known as the slender palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests. Its leaves are used in traditional crafts and as thatching material.

Licuala gracilis is also valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
