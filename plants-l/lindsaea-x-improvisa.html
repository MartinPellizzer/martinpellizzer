Lindsaea x improvisa

Lindsaea x improvisa, commonly known as the velvet fern, is a tropical epiphytic plant belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage that gives it its common name, with leaves arranged in a rosette formation. The plant produces small, greenish to brownish spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are clustered in dense clusters on the underside of the leaves. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on tree trunks or branches in the understory of tropical forests.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth, it is a popular specimen plant in botanical collections.
