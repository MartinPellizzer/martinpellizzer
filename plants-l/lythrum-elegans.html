Lythrum elegans

Lythrum elegans, commonly known as purple loosestrife, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescences of vivid purple flowers that bloom from midsummer to early autumn. The plant has lance-shaped, opposite leaves that are typically hairy and have a bluish-green hue. Its roots are fibrous and can spread aggressively, making it an invasive species in some regions.

Lythrum elegans thrives in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, preferring moist, well-drained soils.
