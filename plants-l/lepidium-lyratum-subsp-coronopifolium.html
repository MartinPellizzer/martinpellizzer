Lepidium lyratum subsp. coronopifolium

Lepidium lyratum subsp.

coronopifolium, commonly known as the crownleaf peppergrass, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family. It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves that form a dense cluster.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a slender, elongated silique containing several seeds.

This species is typically found in dry, rocky habitats and is native to western North America.
