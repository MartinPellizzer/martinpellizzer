Leptospermum australe

Leptospermum australe, commonly known as the coastal tea tree, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in coastal and sandy soils, often found in heathlands and coastal scrub.

Leptospermum australe is valued for its hardy nature and ornamental appeal.
