Ladenbergia paraensis

Ladenbergia paraensis, commonly known as the Paraensis Legume, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. Its fruit is a legume pod that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its role in ecological systems.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations and potential for use in ecological restoration.
