Lathyrus patagonicus

Lathyrus patagonicus, commonly known as the Patagonian vetch, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from purple to white, followed by elongated pods containing seeds. Lathyrus patagonicus is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in grasslands and shrublands.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential agricultural use.
