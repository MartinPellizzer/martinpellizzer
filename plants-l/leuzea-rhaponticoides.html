Leuzea rhaponticoides

Leuzea rhaponticoides, commonly known as rhapontic leuzea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of Europe and Asia.

The plant features a stout, upright stem with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in loose inflorescences, blooming in late summer.

Leuzea rhaponticoides is known for its high content of bioactive compounds, including flavonoids and essential oils, which have been studied for potential medicinal uses.
