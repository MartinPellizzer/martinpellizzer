Lupinus luteus var. kazimierskii

Lupinus luteus var. kazimierskii, commonly known as the Kazimierskii yellow lupin, is a variant of the yellow lupin native to Central Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces erect, spiny pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to semi-arid climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential as a forage crop.
