Loranthus mweroensis var. kivuensis

Loranthus mweroensis var. kivuensis, commonly known as the Kivu mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant native to the eastern African region, particularly in the Great Lakes area.

It belongs to the Loranthaceae family and typically grows on the branches of host trees such as Eucalyptus and Acacia. The plant has small, leathery leaves and produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. It relies on its host for water and nutrients, while conducting photosynthesis to some extent.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological interactions and limited distribution.
