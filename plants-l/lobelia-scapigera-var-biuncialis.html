Lobelia scapigera var. biuncialis

Lobelia scapigera var. biuncialis, commonly known as the twinflower lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, erect stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a suitable choice for woodland gardens.

Its unique floral structure and ornamental value make it a valuable addition to native plant collections.
