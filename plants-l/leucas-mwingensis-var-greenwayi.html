Leucas mwingensis var. greenwayi

Leucas mwingensis var. greenwayi, commonly known as the African blueweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in clusters. It is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils.

The species is notable for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
