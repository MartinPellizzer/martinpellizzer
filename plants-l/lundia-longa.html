Lundia longa

Lundia longa, commonly known as the long-stemmed lily, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to two meters in height, supporting large, showy white or pale pink flowers. The leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and arranged alternately along the stem, providing a lush green backdrop to the blooms. It thrives in well-drained, moist soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking dramatic floral displays.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its fragrant flowers.
