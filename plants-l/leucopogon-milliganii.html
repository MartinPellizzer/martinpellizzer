Leucopogon milliganii

Leucopogon milliganii, commonly known as Milligan's heath, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in heathland and coastal heath environments, characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or red, blooming in spring. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, adapted to withstand the harsh coastal conditions.

Leucopogon milliganii plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
