Linum leucanthum

Linum leucanthum, commonly known as the white flax or white linum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, showy white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces slender capsules that contain numerous small, flat seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Linum leucanthum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in textile production.
