Liatris tomentosa

Liatris tomentosa, commonly known as the wooly bluemink, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and showy, purple to pinkish-blue flower heads. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically visited by bees and other pollinators. Its leaves are covered in a dense, woolly layer of hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Liatris tomentosa is often found in moist, open habitats such as prairies and wetlands.
