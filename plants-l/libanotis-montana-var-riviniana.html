Libanotis montana var. riviniana

Libanotis montana var. riviniana, commonly known as the Alpine bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It features basal leaves that are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are typically blue or purple, arranged in loose clusters. The plant thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine meadows and subalpine zones.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive blooms.
