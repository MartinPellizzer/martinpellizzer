Linnaea borealis f. exoptata

Linnaea borealis f. exoptata, commonly known as the northern twinflower, is a rare variant of the twinflower plant native to northern regions of North America and Eurasia.

This plant is characterized by its distinctive twin flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge. It thrives in cool, moist environments such as bogs, heaths, and rocky woods, where it forms dense mats. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly hairy surface, and the flowers are small, white, and fragrant.

Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
