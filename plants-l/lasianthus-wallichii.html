Lasianthus wallichii

Lasianthus wallichii, commonly known as the Himalayan bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Himalayas.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.
