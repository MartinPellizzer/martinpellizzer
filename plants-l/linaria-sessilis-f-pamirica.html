Linaria sessilis f. pamirica

Linaria sessilis f. pamirica, commonly known as the Pamir horned poppy, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spurred petals that resemble horns, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers, which range in color from yellow to orange, bloom in late summer.

Linaria sessilis f. pamirica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
