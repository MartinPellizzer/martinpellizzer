Lavatera scabra

Lavatera scabra, commonly known as the rough hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.
