Lippia scaposa var. melanocaulos

Lippia scaposa var. melanocaulos, commonly known as black-stemmed lippia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, dark-colored stems and small, aromatic leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny white or pale yellow flowers, which bloom during the warm season. It is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Its essential oils are used in traditional medicine and as a source of flavoring in some culinary applications.
