Lepanthes prora

Lepanthes prora, commonly known as the prora orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its small, showy flowers that typically bloom in the summer months. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a rosette pattern around the stem.

Lepanthes prora is often found in shaded, humid environments such as forest understories.
