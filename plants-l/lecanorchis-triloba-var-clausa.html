Lecanorchis triloba var. clausa

Lecanorchis triloba var. clausa, commonly known as the closed three-lobed orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, intricately shaped flowers that typically display a combination of white and green hues. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest understories where it relies on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its unique appearance.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, this orchid is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
