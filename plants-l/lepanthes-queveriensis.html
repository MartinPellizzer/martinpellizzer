Lepanthes queveriensis

Lepanthes queveriensis, commonly known as the Queveriense orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Costa Rica.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern.

Lepanthes queveriensis is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is appreciated for its ornamental value in horticulture.
