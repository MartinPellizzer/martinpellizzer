Lepanthes decussata

Lepanthes decussata, commonly known as the Crossed Orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, such as rainforests, where it thrives on tree canopies or in rocky crevices. Its flowers are usually small, with a distinctive cross-shaped pattern on the petals, which gives it its common name.

Lepanthes decussata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
