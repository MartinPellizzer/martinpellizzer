Leuzea rhapontica subsp. heleniifolia

Leuzea rhapontica subsp.

heleniifolia, commonly known as Siberian rhubarb, is a perennial herb native to the cold regions of Siberia and northern Asia. It is characterized by its large, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length.

The plant produces tall, reddish stems with clusters of small yellow flowers in the summer. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and digestive properties.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh climatic conditions.
