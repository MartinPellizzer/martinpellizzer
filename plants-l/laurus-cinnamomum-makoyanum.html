Laurus cinnamomum makoyanum

Laurus cinnamomum makoyanum, commonly known as the Indian laurel or Makoy's laurel, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like cinnamon and bay laurel. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, narrow leaves that emit a subtle spice-like scent. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible but not commonly consumed.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm climates for its aesthetic and aromatic qualities.
