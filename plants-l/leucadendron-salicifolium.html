Leucadendron salicifolium

Leucadendron salicifolium, commonly known as the silky leucadendron, is a member of the Proteaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, cone-like inflorescence surrounded by long, silky, white bracts that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in coastal regions and has adapted to sandy, well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
