Linum littorale var. oblongifolium

Linum littorale var. oblongifolium, commonly known as sea flax, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are adapted to withstand salty air and coastal winds. The plant produces small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dunes or along shorelines.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems.
