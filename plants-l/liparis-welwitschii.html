Liparis welwitschii

Liparis welwitschii, commonly known as the Welwitsch's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and produces a single, showy, greenish-yellow flower with a distinctive lip. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its unique floral structure.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
