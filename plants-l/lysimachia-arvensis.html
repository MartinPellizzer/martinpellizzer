Lysimachia arvensis

Lysimachia arvensis, commonly known as field chickweed, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically features prostrate or ascending stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are opposite, oval to ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Lysimachia arvensis is often found in moist, disturbed soils such as fields, roadsides, and along stream banks.
