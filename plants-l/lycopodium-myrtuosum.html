Lycopodium myrtuosum

Lycopodium myrtuosum, commonly known as the myrtle clubmoss, is a small, evergreen plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, dense growth habit and dark green, scale-like leaves that form a rosette. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are typically located at the base of the leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in temperate regions.

Lycopodium myrtuosum plays a role in maintaining soil stability and providing habitat for microorganisms in its native ecosystems.
