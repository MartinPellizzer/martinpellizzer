Leonia

Leonia, commonly known as the common leonia, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, depending on the species. Leonia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
