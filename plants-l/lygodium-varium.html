Lygodium varium

Lygodium varium, commonly known as the common climbing fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Lygodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia. The plant typically grows as a climbing or epiphytic species, utilizing its wiry rhizomes to attach to trees or other structures. Its reproductive structures include sporangia that produce spores, which are dispersed by wind.

Lygodium varium thrives in humid environments and is often found in shaded, moist areas of forests.
