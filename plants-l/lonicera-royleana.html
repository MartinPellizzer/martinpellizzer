Lonicera royleana

Lonicera royleana, commonly known as the Chinese honeysuckle, is a deciduous climbing vine native to China and Southeast Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces fragrant, tubular flowers that range from yellow to orange in color. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth habit and ability to twine around supports, making it suitable for trellises or arbors. Its fruits are red, fleshy berries that mature in late summer and are edible for some bird species.

Lonicera royleana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
