Lobelia erinifolia

Lobelia erinifolia, commonly known as wooly lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It is a member of the Campanulaceae family and is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value.

Lobelia erinifolia is also valued for its role in supporting local pollinators.
