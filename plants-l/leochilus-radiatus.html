Leochilus radiatus

Leochilus radiatus, commonly known as the star orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, star-shaped flowers that typically range in color from yellow to orange. The plant grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Leochilus radiatus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
