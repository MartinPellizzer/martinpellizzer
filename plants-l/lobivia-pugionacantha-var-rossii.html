Lobivia pugionacantha var. rossii

Lobivia pugionacantha var. rossii, commonly known as the Ross's Lobivia, is a succulent plant native to South America, specifically Argentina.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and thick, ribbed stems. The plant features spiny, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, appearing at the base of the stems during the warmer months.

This variety is well-suited for arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil to thrive.
