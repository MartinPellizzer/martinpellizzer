Lippia adpressa

Lippia adpressa, commonly known as creeping lippia, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features aromatic leaves that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. The plant typically has small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its succulent stems allow it to thrive in well-drained soils and dry conditions.

Lippia adpressa is often found in disturbed areas, along roadsides, and in open woodlands.
