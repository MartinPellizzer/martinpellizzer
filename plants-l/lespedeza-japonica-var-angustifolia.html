Lespedeza japonica var. angustifolia

Lespedeza japonica var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf Chinese clover, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features slender, often hairy stems that grow from 30 to 60 centimeters in height. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense spikes. Its trifoliate leaves are narrow and have a slightly serrated edge.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in erosion control and as a forage plant.
