Lathyrus nissolia var. lanceolatus

Lathyrus nissolia var. lanceolatus, commonly known as the lanceolate pea vine, is a climbing plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, twining stems that support its clusters of pea-like flowers, which are typically pale blue or violet in color. The leaves are compound, consisting of three oval to lance-shaped leaflets with a hairy texture. Its fruit is a legume pod containing several small, edible seeds.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often grown in gardens for its delicate floral display.
