Limonium oblanceolatum

Limonium oblanceolatum, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and North Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, bluish-gray leaves and spike-like clusters of small, showy blue or purple flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in maritime environments. It is drought-tolerant and well adapted to salt spray, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Limonium oblanceolatum is widely cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
