Leucanthemum simplex

Leucanthemum simplex, commonly known as the common white daisy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its white petals surrounding a yellow center, forming a daisy-like appearance. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. It blooms from late spring to early autumn, producing numerous flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Leucanthemum simplex is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and easy care requirements.
