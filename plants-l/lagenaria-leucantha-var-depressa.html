Lagenaria leucantha var. depressa

Lagenaria leucantha var. depressa, commonly known as the African bottle gourd, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, inflated, bottle-shaped fruits that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant is native to Africa and thrives in warm, tropical climates. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, while the stems are hairy and trailing.

This variety is often cultivated for its edible fruits, which are used in various culinary applications across different cultures.
