Litsea coreana var. sinensis

Litsea coreana var. sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese litsea or Chinese sweetleaf, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and avocados, and is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as mountain forests, and can reach heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy surface, while its flowers are small and clustered in panicles.

The fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed, and the plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional medicinal uses.
