Lepidium gelidum

Lepidium gelidum, commonly known as glacier peppergrass, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of the Southern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its small, white or pinkish flowers and hairy, grass-like leaves. The plant thrives in cold, alpine environments and is often found in rocky or glacial areas. Lepidium gelidum is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard plants.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, allowing it to colonize remote and harsh habitats.
