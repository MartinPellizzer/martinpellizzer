Lobelia jasionoides var. sparsiflora

Lobelia jasionoides var. sparsiflora, commonly known as the sparsely-flowered lobelia, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant features slender, upright stems that bear small, tubular blue or purple flowers clustered in whorls. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
