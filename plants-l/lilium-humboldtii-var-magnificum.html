Lilium humboldtii var. magnificum

Lilium humboldtii var. magnificum, commonly known as the Humboldt lily, is a striking species native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter, typically in shades of white, pink, and red. The plant features sturdy, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is known for its fragrant blooms.

This variety is highly valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and unique floral structure.
