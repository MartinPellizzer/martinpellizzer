Lupinus mutabilis f. cruckshanksii

Lupinus mutabilis f. cruckshanksii, commonly known as the Cruckshanks' lupine, is a variant of the Andean lupine species native to the high altitudes of southern Peru.

This subspecies is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from purple to pink, often changing hue as the plant matures. It typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to the cool, temperate climates of the Andes. The plant is part of the legume family, capable of nitrogen fixation, which enriches the soil.

Its unique floral display and ecological significance make it a notable species in its native habitat.
