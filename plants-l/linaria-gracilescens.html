Linaria gracilescens

Linaria gracilescens, commonly known as the slender toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species characterized by its slender stems and small, yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers are tubular with two lips, a feature common in the genus Linaria, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
