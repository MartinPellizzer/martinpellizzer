Lilium concolor var. concolor

Lilium concolor var. concolor, commonly known as the white lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has upright, slender stems that are often covered in a fine, hairy layer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its elegant appearance.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
