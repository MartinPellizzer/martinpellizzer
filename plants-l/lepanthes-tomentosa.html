Lepanthes tomentosa

Lepanthes tomentosa, commonly known as the wooly slipper orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety foliage and striking, showy flowers that often resemble small slippers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments such as cloud forests. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive pouch-like structure that aids in pollination.

Lepanthes tomentosa is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
