Linaria verticillata subsp. lilacina

Linaria verticillata subsp.

lilacina, commonly known as the lilac morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from pale purple to lilac.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
