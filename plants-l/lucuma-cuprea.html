Lucuma cuprea

Lucuma cuprea, commonly known as the red lucuma, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its distinctive red, fleshy fruits that resemble small, yellowish oranges. The fruit contains a single large seed surrounded by a sweet, creamy pulp that is often used in culinary applications. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, temperate climates and well-drained soils.

Due to its unique appearance and flavor, it is gaining popularity as an ornamental and edible plant in horticultural circles.
