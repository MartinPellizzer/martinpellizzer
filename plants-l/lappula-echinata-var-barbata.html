Lappula echinata var. barbata

Lappula echinata var. barbata, commonly known as the hairy lappula, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, yellow flowers and slender, branching stems. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The fruit is a dry, dehiscent capsule that releases small, rounded seeds when mature.
