Lastrea angustifrons

Lastrea angustifrons, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lastrea, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family, which is characterized by its fleshy leaves and adaptations to arid environments. The plant features linear, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder, known as farina, which helps reduce water loss. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the warmer months.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
