Loranthus otavensis

Loranthus otavensis, commonly known as the Otavense mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on a variety of host trees, primarily in the regions of Namibia and Angola.

It belongs to the family Loranthaceae and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant lacks true roots and absorbs water and nutrients from its host through specialized structures called haustoria.

Loranthus otavensis plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for certain birds and insects, and it is also used in traditional medicine in some areas.
