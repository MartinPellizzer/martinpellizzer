Leptopteris hymenophylloides

Leptopteris hymenophylloides, commonly known as the slender shield fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are usually arranged in a rosette-like pattern, with a distinctive, shield-shaped appearance.

The spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy in humid, forested habitats.
