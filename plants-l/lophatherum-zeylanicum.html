Lophatherum zeylanicum

Lophatherum zeylanicum, commonly known as the Indian arrowgrass, is a perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically clustered at the tips of the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and wetlands.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
