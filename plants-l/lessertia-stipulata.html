Lessertia stipulata

Lessertia stipulata, commonly known as the yellow-stemmed wild bean, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in grassland habitats and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
