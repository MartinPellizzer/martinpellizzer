Loranthus recurvus

Loranthus recurvus, commonly known as the common mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily in the genus Eucalyptus.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using light reflected from the host's leaves. The plant has a distinctive appearance with small, oval-shaped leaves and small, white or yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its berries are typically red or orange, containing sticky seeds that adhere to bird feathers for dispersal.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitats by supporting various bird and insect species.
