Lantana macropoda

Lantana macropoda, commonly known as the common lantana, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to yellow, orange, and red, depending on the variety. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it both ornamental and invasive in some areas.

Lantana macropoda is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
