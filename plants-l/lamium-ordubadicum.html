Lamium ordubadicum

Lamium ordubadicum, commonly known as the Ordubad dead-nettle, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces clusters of small, two-lipped flowers in shades of pink to purple. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and forest floors. It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and has a creeping growth habit with stolons.

While not widely cultivated, it is occasionally found in botanical gardens and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
