Lasianthaea helianthoides var. helianthoides

Lasianthaea helianthoides var. helianthoides, commonly known as the yellow ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies. The plant features upright, hairy stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a rough texture, and its flowers are yellow, arranged in dense, terminal clusters.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in wet conditions and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
