Leptoglossis lomana

Leptoglossis lomana, commonly known as the Lomana orchid, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clustered inflorescences. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom during the warmer months. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

Leptoglossis lomana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
