Lathyrus filiformis f. nicolai

Lathyrus filiformis f. nicolai, commonly known as the Nicolo's slender vetch, is a rare and distinctive cultivar of the vetch genus.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant is native to Central Asia and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring moderate water and pruning to maintain its compact form.

Due to its rarity, it is often cultivated by enthusiasts and botanic gardens for its unique botanical features.
