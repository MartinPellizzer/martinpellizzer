Limonium delicatulum subsp. gallicum

Limonium delicatulum subsp.

gallicum, commonly known as the French sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Europe. It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in maritime environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are adapted to withstand salty air and strong winds.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging coastal conditions.
