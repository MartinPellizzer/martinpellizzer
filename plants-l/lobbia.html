Lobbia

Lobbia, commonly known as the "bitter bean" or "lobbia tree," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its woody shrubs or small trees with compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas. Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Lobbia species are also valued for their ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
