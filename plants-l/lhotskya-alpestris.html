Lhotskya alpestris

Lhotskya alpestris, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in rocky, alpine environments where it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a dense mat on the ground.

Lhotskya alpestris is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
