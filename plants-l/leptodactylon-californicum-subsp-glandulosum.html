Leptodactylon californicum subsp. glandulosum

Leptodactylon californicum subsp.

glandulosum, commonly known as the glandular leathery fern, is a perennial fern native to California. It is characterized by its leathery, glandular leaves that are often covered in small, sticky bumps.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its fronds are bipinnate, with a distinctive, compact appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens with high humidity.
