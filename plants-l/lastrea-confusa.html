Lastrea confusa

Lastrea confusa, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its distinctive yellow, egg-shaped berries. The plant typically grows in shrubland and coastal areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually white or pale purple and are followed by the fleshy, edible fruits.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
