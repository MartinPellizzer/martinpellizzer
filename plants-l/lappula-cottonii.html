Lappula cottonii

Lappula cottonii, commonly known as the cotton flax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, erect stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces capsules that contain numerous tiny, white seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Lappula cottonii is valued for its historical use in textile production and as a source of natural fibers.
