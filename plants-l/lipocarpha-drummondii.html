Lipocarpha drummondii

Lipocarpha drummondii, commonly known as the Drummond's marsh grass, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes and along the edges of lakes. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale green. It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Lipocarpha drummondii is adapted to fluctuating water levels and can tolerate periods of submersion.
