Lachnaea buxifolia var. glauca

Lachnaea buxifolia var. glauca, commonly known as the glaucous boxleaf lichen, is a foliose lichen that exhibits a distinctive bluish-gray coating on its upper surface.

It typically grows on tree trunks and branches, particularly in humid, shaded environments. The plant's thallus is leathery and has a dense, compact structure with a prominent, leaf-like appearance. Its reproductive structures, including apothecia, are usually embedded within the thallus.

This lichen is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ecological role in forest ecosystems.
