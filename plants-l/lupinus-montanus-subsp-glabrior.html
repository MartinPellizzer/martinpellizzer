Lupinus montanus subsp. glabrior

Lupinus montanus subsp.

glabrior, commonly known as the smooth lupine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its smooth, glabrous stems and leaves, which are typically lance-shaped and hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in nitrogen fixation in soil ecosystems.
