Litsea acerina

Litsea acerina, commonly known as the red sandalwood or Chinese sandalwood, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits that contain smooth, black seeds. Its wood is highly valued for its fragrance and is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.

Litsea acerina thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.
