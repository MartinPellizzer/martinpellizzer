Lychnis apetala var. attenuata

Lychnis apetala var. attenuata, commonly known as the slender sandwort, is a low-growing perennial herb native to northern regions of North America.

It typically features small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has slender, branching stems and hairy leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in moist, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to harsh environmental conditions.
