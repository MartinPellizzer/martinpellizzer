Ludwigia rudis

Ludwigia rudis, commonly known as the river Ludwigia, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is a semi-aquatic plant that typically grows in shallow freshwater habitats such as rivers and ponds. The plant has elongated, narrow leaves that are often submerged, with some leaves floating on the water surface. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spikes, which are usually found above the water.

Ludwigia rudis is often used in aquatic gardening and as a decorative plant in water features.
