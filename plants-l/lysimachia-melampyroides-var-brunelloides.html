Lysimachia melampyroides var. brunelloides

Lysimachia melampyroides var. brunelloides, commonly known as the Brunello's yellow loosestrife, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly rough texture. This variety is known for its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant blooms and ability to form dense, low-growing mats.
