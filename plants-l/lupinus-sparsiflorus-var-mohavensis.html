Lupinus sparsiflorus var. mohavensis

Lupinus sparsiflorus var. mohavensis, commonly known as the Mohave lupine, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and dense clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves with a hairy texture, adapted to the arid environments it inhabits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in desert scrub and rocky habitats.

Lupinus sparsiflorus var. mohavensis plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing nectar for pollinators in its native range.
