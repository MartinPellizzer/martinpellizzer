Leucophae penzigii

Leucophae penzigii, commonly known as the Penzig's ghost orchid, is a rare and elusive orchid species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, pale, almost translucent flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions, hence its common name. The plant typically grows on exposed rock faces or in shaded forest areas, where it relies on specific symbiotic relationships with fungi for nutrient uptake. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with elongated petals that are often tinged with a faint blue or purple hue.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Leucophae penzigii is considered a critically endangered species.
