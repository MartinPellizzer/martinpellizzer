Leptopogon carinatus subvar. incanus

Leptopogon carinatus subvar. incanus, commonly known as the hairy leathery sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Australia.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and waterlogged soils. This subspecies is distinguished by its more robust and hairy appearance compared to other varieties within the species.
