Lastrea petrophila

Lastrea petrophila, commonly known as the rock lily, is a flowering plant native to arid and rocky environments in southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, succulent-like growth habit and produces showy, often fragrant flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well adapted to harsh, dry conditions. Its leaves are typically thick and fleshy, aiding in water retention.

Lastrea petrophila is often found growing on rock faces or in crevices, showcasing its resilience to extreme environmental conditions.
