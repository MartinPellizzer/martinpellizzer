Linum sulcatum

Linum sulcatum, commonly known as the cut-leaved flax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and heart-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed and often have a distinctive wavy margin. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Linum sulcatum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in textile production.
