Lambertia formosa f. typica

Lambertia formosa f. typica, commonly known as the typical lambertia, is a striking evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, clustered growth habit and its showy, cone-like inflorescences that bear small, bright orange-red flowers. The plant features glossy, dark green, leathery leaves that are elliptical in shape. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is relatively low maintenance once established.
