Linaria elatine var. dentata

Linaria elatine var. dentata, commonly known as the toothed linaria, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing annual herb characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and toothed, giving the plant its specific variety name. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found along riverbanks and in wet meadows.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
