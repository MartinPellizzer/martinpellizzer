Lespedeza formosa var. albiflora

Lespedeza formosa var. albiflora, commonly known as the whiteflower lespeadeza, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with oval-shaped leaflets and is often found in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and can fix nitrogen in the soil through its root nodules.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
