Lupinus watsonii

Lupinus watsonii, commonly known as the Watson's lupine, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through symbiotic relationships with bacteria. Lupinus watsonii typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Its foliage consists of compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets.
