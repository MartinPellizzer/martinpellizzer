Liparis pectinata

Liparis pectinata, commonly known as the pectinate orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces delicate, greenish-white flowers with a distinctive pectinate structure on the petals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Liparis pectinata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
