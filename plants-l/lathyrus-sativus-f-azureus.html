Lathyrus sativus f. azureus

Lathyrus sativus f. azureus, commonly known as the Azure Pea, is a botanical variant of the common vetch.

It is characterized by its striking deep blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is an annual legume with trifoliate leaves and slender, climbing stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in temperate regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
