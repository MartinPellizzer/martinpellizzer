Leonurus glabriflorus

Leonurus glabriflorus, commonly known as the woolly betony, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Leonurus glabriflorus is known for its ornamental value and has been used historically in traditional herbal medicine.
