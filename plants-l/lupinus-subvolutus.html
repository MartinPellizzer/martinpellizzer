Lupinus subvolutus

Lupinus subvolutus, commonly known as the curlleaf lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, curling leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in late spring to early summer. Lupinus subvolutus has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion.

It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.
