Lepanthes katleri

Lepanthes katleri, commonly known as the Katler's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, showy flowers that are often white or pale pink with intricate patterns. Its leaves are usually broad and arranged in a rosette shape, aiding in water retention.

Lepanthes katleri is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
