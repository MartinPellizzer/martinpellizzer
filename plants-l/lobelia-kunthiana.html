Lobelia kunthiana

Lobelia kunthiana, commonly known as the desert lily, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple bell-shaped flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils and along dry streambeds. It produces showy blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Lobelia kunthiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
