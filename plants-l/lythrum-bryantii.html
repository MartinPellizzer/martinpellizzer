Lythrum bryantii

Lythrum bryantii, commonly known as Bryant's lythrum, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lythraceae family, which includes other species such as the common lythrum. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of pink or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Bryant's lythrum is typically found in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in wetlands.
