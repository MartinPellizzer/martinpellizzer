Lycopodium hochreutineri

Lycopodium hochreutineri, commonly known as the Hochreutiner's clubmoss, is a small, evergreen plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, sparsely branched stem and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces sporophylls that bear spores, typical of its ancient lineage of vascular plants. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Lycopodium hochreutineri is a relic of the Carboniferous period, showcasing evolutionary adaptations that have allowed it to persist for millions of years.
