Lathyrus macrorrhizus var. variifolius

Lathyrus macrorrhizus var. variifolius, commonly known as the variable-leaf grass vetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves with variable leaflet shapes. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers in shades of pink or purple, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or grassland habitats.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
