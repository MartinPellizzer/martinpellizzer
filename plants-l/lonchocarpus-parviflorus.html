Lonchocarpus parviflorus

Lonchocarpus parviflorus, commonly known as the small-flowered legume, is a tropical shrub belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in ecological and medicinal contexts.
