Lilac vulgaris var. coerulea

Lilac vulgaris var. coerulea, commonly known as the common lilac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the temperate regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, clustered flowers that range in color from pale blue to deep violet, typically blooming in late spring. The plant has simple, opposite, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn, and its bark is smooth and grayish-brown when young. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

The species is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various insects.
