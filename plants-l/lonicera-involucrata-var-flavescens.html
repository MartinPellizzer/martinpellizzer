Lonicera involucrata var. flavescens

Lonicera involucrata var. flavescens, commonly known as the yellow honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds but generally not for humans. It thrives in partial to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for wildlife habitat and ornamental gardening.
