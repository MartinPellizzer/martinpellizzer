Lotus lanatus

Lotus lanatus, commonly known as the velvet lotus, is a species of aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves that give it a velvety texture. The plant typically grows in shallow freshwater habitats such as ponds and slow-moving streams. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive, showy appearance.

Lotus lanatus is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
