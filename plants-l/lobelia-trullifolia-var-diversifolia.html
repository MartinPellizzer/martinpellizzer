Lobelia trullifolia var. diversifolia

Lobelia trullifolia var. diversifolia, commonly known as the variegated lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compact, mound-like growth habit and vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically oval to lance-shaped, with a distinctive variegation pattern of white or yellow markings. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
