Lagurus ovatus var. globosus

Lagurus ovatus var. globosus, commonly known as the globe clover, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features small, rounded, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has fine, hairy leaves that are often described as resembling a clover leaf in shape. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value comes from its compact growth habit and delicate floral display.
