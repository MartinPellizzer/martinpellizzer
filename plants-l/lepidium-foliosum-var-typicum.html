Lepidium foliosum var. typicum

Lepidium foliosum var. typicum, commonly known as the common whitlowgrass, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, clustered flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in prairies and along roadsides. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered with fine, branched hairs.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in many regions.
