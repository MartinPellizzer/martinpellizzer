Lasianthus tubiflorus

Lasianthus tubiflorus, commonly known as the woolly pipevine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking purple to violet flowers that bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive, velvety appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Lasianthus tubiflorus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
