Lamium maculatum f. tenoreanum

Lamium maculatum f. tenoreanum, commonly known as the Variegated Dead-nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features striking foliage with white and green variegation, making it a popular ornamental plant. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of pink or purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, often found in woodland areas.

Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
