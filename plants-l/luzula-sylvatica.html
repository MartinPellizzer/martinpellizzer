Luzula sylvatica

Luzula sylvatica, commonly known as wood rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and arranged in a dense, tufted cluster. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, three-lobed flowers, which are often greenish or brownish in color. Luzula sylvatica thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in forest ecosystems.
