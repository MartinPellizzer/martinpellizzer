Linaria michauxii var. parviflora

Linaria michauxii var. parviflora, commonly known as the narrow-flower sandthrift, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and linear, contributing to its overall delicate appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
