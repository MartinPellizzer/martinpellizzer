Leucopogon rotundifolius var. oblongatus

Leucopogon rotundifolius var. oblongatus, commonly known as the broad-leafed heath, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of Australia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heaths and heathers, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and compact growth habit. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or white, which bloom in spring and attract pollinators such as bees and birds.

Its leaves are broad and oval-shaped, contributing to its distinctive appearance, and it thrives in well-drained, acidic soils in full sun to partial shade.
