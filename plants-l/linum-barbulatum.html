Linum barbulatum

Linum barbulatum, commonly known as the hairy flax, is a flowering plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, blue or purple flowers with five petals. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun.

Linum barbulatum is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is also valued for its historical use in fiber production.
