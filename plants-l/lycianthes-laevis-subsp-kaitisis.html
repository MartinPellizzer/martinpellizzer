Lycianthes laevis subsp. kaitisis

Lycianthes laevis subsp.

kaitisis, commonly known as the Mexican nightshade, is a species of flowering plant native to Central America. It is a herbaceous annual with hairy stems and alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape.

The flowers are yellow with five petals and are typically borne in clusters. The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, round seeds.

This plant is part of the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its ornamental value and potential medicinal uses.
