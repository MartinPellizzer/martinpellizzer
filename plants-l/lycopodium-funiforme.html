Lycopodium funiforme

Lycopodium funiforme, commonly known as the clubmoss, is a small, evergreen plant that belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and tiny, scale-like leaves. The plant produces spore-bearing structures called strobila, which are clustered at the tips of its branches. Lycopodium funiforme typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices.

It plays an important ecological role by contributing to soil stability and providing habitat for various microorganisms.
