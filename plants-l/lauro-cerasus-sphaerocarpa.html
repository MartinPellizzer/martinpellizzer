Lauro-cerasus sphaerocarpa

Lauro-cerasus sphaerocarpa, commonly known as the Japanese flowering cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, particularly its showy white or pink blossoms that appear in early spring. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are oval-shaped with a pointed tip. It produces small, red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

The species is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and is well-suited to temperate climates.
