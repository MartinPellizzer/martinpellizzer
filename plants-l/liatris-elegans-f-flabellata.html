Liatris elegans f. flabellata

Liatris elegans f. flabellata, commonly known as the flabellate blazing star, is a cultivar of the blazing star plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright, spiky flower heads that bloom in shades of pink to lavender, adding a striking visual element to gardens. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color, providing a contrast to its showy flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native plant gardens.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
