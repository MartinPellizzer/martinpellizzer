Lonchocarpus longistylus

Lonchocarpus longistylus, commonly known as the long-styled legume, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features prominent, elongated stamens that give it its common name. It is often found in moist, shaded habitats within the understory of forests.

Its flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
