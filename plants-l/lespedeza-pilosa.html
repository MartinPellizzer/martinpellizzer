Lespedeza pilosa

Lespedeza pilosa, commonly known as hairy lespedeza, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and feathery foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Hairy lespedeza is often found in open woods, fields, and along roadsides, and it serves as a food source for various insects and wildlife.

Its hairy stems and leaves give it a distinctive appearance, and it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
