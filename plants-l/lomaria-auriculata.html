Lomaria auriculata

Lomaria auriculata, commonly known as the auriculate lomaria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. It produces small, yellowish to orange flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Lomaria auriculata is often found in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils.
