Lepanthes teretipetala

Lepanthes teretipetala, commonly known as the slender-leafed orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees in humid, tropical environments. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate floral structure.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often found in cloud forests and is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
