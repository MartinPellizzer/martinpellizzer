Lachnopodium rubro-limbatum

Lachnopodium rubro-limbatum, commonly known as the red-veined lichen, is a foliose lichen that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is characterized by its distinct reddish-orange lobes and a leathery texture, which help it retain moisture. The plant's thallus is composed of a symbiotic relationship between algae and fungi, typical of lichens. It often grows on soil, wood, or stone in temperate regions.

Its vibrant coloration makes it a striking component of forest floors and rocky habitats.
