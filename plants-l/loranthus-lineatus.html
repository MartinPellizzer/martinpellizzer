Loranthus lineatus

Loranthus lineatus, commonly known as the slender mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of host trees, primarily in the genus Eucalyptus.

It lacks true roots and absorbs water and nutrients through its stems, which are adapted for attachment to the host. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers and red berries that are often dispersed by birds. Its leaves are linear and opposite, and it is native to Australia and parts of Southeast Asia.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can affect the health of its host trees if left unchecked.
