Lasiodiscus marmoratus

Lasiodiscus marmoratus, commonly known as the marbleleaf or marble-stemmed plant, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, waxy, and often variegated leaves that display a marbled pattern of green and white. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Lasiodiscus marmoratus is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its unique foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
