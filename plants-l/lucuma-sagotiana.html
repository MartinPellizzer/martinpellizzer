Lucuma sagotiana

Lucuma sagotiana, commonly known as the Andean sweet potato, is a member of the Convolvulaceae family.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of South America, particularly the Andes. The plant produces tuberous roots that are rich in carbohydrates and have a sweet, nutty flavor. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

The species is valued for its culinary uses and has been cultivated for centuries by indigenous communities.
