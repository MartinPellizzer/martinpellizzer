Limnochloa limosa

Limnochloa limosa, commonly known as the swamp grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, exhibiting a rosette-like growth pattern with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets, which are characteristic of the grass family. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread vegetatively, contributing to its ability to colonize wetland habitats.

Limnochloa limosa plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.
