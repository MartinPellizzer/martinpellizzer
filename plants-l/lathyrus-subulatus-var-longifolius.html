Lathyrus subulatus var. longifolius

Lathyrus subulatus var. longifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, spiny stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant produces trifoliate leaves with elongated, pointed leaflets, and its flowers are often clustered in racemes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
