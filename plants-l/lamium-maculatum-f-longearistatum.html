Lamium maculatum f. longearistatum

Lamium maculatum f. longearistatum, commonly known as the spotted deadnettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and Europe.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive purple and white mottling, and its flowers are typically pink or white, arranged in whorls along the stem. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks. It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to spread through rhizomes, forming dense mats.

Its foliage remains attractive throughout the growing season, making it a popular choice for ground cover in shady gardens.
