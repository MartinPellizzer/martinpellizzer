Linum strictum f. spicatum

Linum strictum f. spicatum, commonly known as the spicate flax, is a flowering plant belonging to the Linaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and slender stems that often branch near the base. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that are clustered in spiky inflorescences, giving it its common name. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in textile production.
