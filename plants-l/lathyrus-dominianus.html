Lathyrus dominianus

Lathyrus dominianus, commonly known as the Dominican pea, is a flowering plant native to the island of Dominica in the Caribbean.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically purple or pink in color. The plant grows as a climbing vine, often found in moist, shaded forest understories. Its seeds are encased in pods and are an important food source for various wildlife species.

Lathyrus dominianus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
