Lepturus incurvatus var. erectus

Lepturus incurvatus var. erectus, commonly known as the upright lepturus, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
