Lophomyrtus × ralphii

Lophomyrtus × ralphii, commonly known as the hybrid myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy or rocky soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive foliage.

Lophomyrtus × ralphii is also notable for its role in local ecosystems, providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
