Labordia fagraeoidea var. jugorum

Labordia fagraeoidea var. jugorum, commonly known as the Cape jasmine, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in summer. Its flowering stems are often adorned with small, leaf-like bracts that give it a distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
