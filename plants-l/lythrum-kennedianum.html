Lythrum kennedianum

Lythrum kennedianum, commonly known as purple loosestrife, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, spires of vibrant purple flowers that bloom from late summer to early fall. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its roots are fibrous and can spread aggressively, making it an invasive species in some regions.

Lythrum kennedianum thrives in moist soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.
