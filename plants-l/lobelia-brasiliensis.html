Lobelia brasiliensis

Lobelia brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian bellflower, is a striking flowering plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of vibrant blue or purple tubular flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Lobelia brasiliensis is often found in moist, shaded areas and thrives in humid tropical climates.

Its ornamental value has made it a popular choice for botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
