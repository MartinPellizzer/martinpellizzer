Lupinus truncatus var. burlewii

Lupinus truncatus var. burlewii, commonly known as Burlew's lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has palmately compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its root system includes nitrogen-fixing nodules. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.

This variety is also noted for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for small wildlife.
