Liatris cylindracea f. bartelii

Liatris cylindracea f. bartelii, commonly known as the Bartel's narrowleaf blazing star, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy inflorescences. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces tall, spikelike clusters of pale purple to pinkish flowers. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adapted to well-drained soils.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a pollinator attractant, particularly for bees and butterflies.
