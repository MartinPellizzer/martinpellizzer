Lobelia camporum

Lobelia camporum, commonly known as the mountain lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the high elevations of the Appalachian Mountains.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, rocky soils and shaded forest environments. The plant produces showy, tubular blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Lobelia camporum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
