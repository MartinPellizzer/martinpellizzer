Lathyrus setifolius var. sharonensis

Lathyrus setifolius var. sharonensis, commonly known as Sharon's vetch, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers in shades of purple or violet, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Sharon's vetch is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
