Lepidium papillosum var. normale

Lepidium papillosum var. normale, commonly known as the common peppergrass, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard plants. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is characterized by its small, white or yellowish flowers. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately divided, and covered with fine, papery hairs.

The fruit is a small, dry capsule that contains several seeds.
