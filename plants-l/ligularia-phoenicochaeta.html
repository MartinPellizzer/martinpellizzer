Ligularia phoenicochaeta

Ligularia phoenicochaeta, commonly known as the golden ligularia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive yellowish-green color and a prominent midrib. The plant produces showy, orange-yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in dense, terminal inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forested areas along streams or in wetlands.

Its foliage and flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in shaded gardens.
