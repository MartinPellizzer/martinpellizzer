Licuala tanycola

Licuala tanycola, commonly known as the golden palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves that are often a striking golden-yellow color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
