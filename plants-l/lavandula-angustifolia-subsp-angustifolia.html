Lavandula angustifolia subsp. angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia subsp.

angustifolia, commonly known as English lavender, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features upright, woody stems covered in narrow, gray-green leaves with a fuzzy texture.

The plant produces spikes of fragrant, purple-blue flowers that bloom in summer. Its aromatic oils, primarily composed of linalool and lavandin, are widely used in perfumery and aromatherapy.

This subspecies is well adapted to dry, well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal cultivation.
