Lacistema ligiae

Lacistema ligiae, commonly known as the Spanish bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.
