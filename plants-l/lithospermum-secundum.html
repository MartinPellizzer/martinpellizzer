Lithospermum secundum

Lithospermum secundum, commonly known as red sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of pale yellow flowers followed by distinctive, red, fleshy fruits. Its roots contain compounds that have been traditionally used in herbal medicine.

Lithospermum secundum is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens due to its vibrant foliage and unique fruit structure.
