Lauradia capillaris var. glandulosopubescens

Lauradia capillaris var. glandulosopubescens, commonly known as the glandular-hairy lauradia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that gives it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The leaves are typically elliptical or ovate, with a glossy surface and prominent veins. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, often clustered in terminal inflorescences.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique texture and visual appeal.
