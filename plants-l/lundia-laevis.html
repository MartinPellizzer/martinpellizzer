Lundia laevis

Lundia laevis, commonly known as the smooth lundia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, waxy leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant thrives in humid environments and is often found in lowland rainforests. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

Lundia laevis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
