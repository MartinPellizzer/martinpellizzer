Linum spicatum

Linum spicatum, commonly known as spiked flax, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Linaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, blue or violet flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oval seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a hairy texture.

Linum spicatum is often grown for its ornamental value and has historical uses in textile production.
