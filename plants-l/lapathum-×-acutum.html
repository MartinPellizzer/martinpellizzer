Lapathum × acutum

Lapathum × acutum, commonly known as the acute meadow cranesbill, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers arranged in dense, terminal clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Lapathum × acutum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
