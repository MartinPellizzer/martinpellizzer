Lonchocarpus albiflorus f. parvifolia

Lonchocarpus albiflorus f. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved white-flowered legume, is a shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features trifoliate leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters. Its root system is known for containing toxic alkaloids, which have been studied for potential medicinal properties.

The species is often found in moist, shaded habitats within tropical forests.
