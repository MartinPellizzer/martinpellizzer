Lodhra ferruginea

Lodhra ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty wood lotus, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Theaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often reddish or rusty in color. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, which bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Lodhra ferruginea is valued in traditional medicine for its medicinal properties, particularly in Ayurveda.
