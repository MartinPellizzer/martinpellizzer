Linnaea parvifolia

Linnaea parvifolia, commonly known as the twinflower, is a small evergreen shrub native to the northern regions of North America and eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in pairs, which bloom in early spring. Linnaea parvifolia thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded forest environments.

It is a member of the family Ericaceae, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and heathers.
