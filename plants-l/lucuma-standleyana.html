Lucuma standleyana

Lucuma standleyana, commonly known as the "yellow lucuma," is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly Peru and Ecuador. The plant produces large, yellow fruits that are rich in nutrients and have a sweet, custard-like flavor. Its leaves are broad and glossy, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

The fruit is often used in culinary applications and is valued for its unique taste and nutritional profile.
