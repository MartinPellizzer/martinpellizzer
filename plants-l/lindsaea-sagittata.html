Lindsaea sagittata

Lindsaea sagittata, commonly known as the arrowhead fern, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that resemble arrowheads. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, anchoring itself with its rhizomes while absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air. Its leaves are usually dark green and glossy, with a distinctive arrow-shaped outline.

Lindsaea sagittata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in humid, shaded environments.
