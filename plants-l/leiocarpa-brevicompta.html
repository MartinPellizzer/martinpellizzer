Leiocarpa brevicompta

Leiocarpa brevicompta, commonly known as the short-stemmed coniferous plant, is a species of conifer native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in dense clusters. The plant produces cones that are often scaleless and have a distinctive, rounded shape. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous or forested areas.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
