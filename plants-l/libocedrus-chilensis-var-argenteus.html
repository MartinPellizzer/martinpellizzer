Libocedrus chilensis var. argenteus

Libocedrus chilensis var. argenteus, commonly known as the silver bark tree, is a variant of the Chilean cedar.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the temperate rainforests of southern Chile. The plant features a slender, columnar growth habit with a distinctive silver-gray bark that becomes flaky with age. Its foliage consists of scale-like leaves that are dense and provide year-round cover.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance and resilience.
