Liabum niveum

Liabum niveum, commonly known as the snowbell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a silvery underside, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers cool, moist environments.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, frost-resistant blooms.
