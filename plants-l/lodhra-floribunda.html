Lodhra floribunda

Lodhra floribunda, commonly known as the white-flowered ash tree, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae and is characterized by its slender, ash-gray bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The tree is valued for its medicinal properties, with its bark and flowers used in traditional Ayurvedic medicine.
