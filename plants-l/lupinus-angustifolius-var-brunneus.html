Lupinus angustifolius var. brunneus

Lupinus angustifolius var. brunneus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lupine, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright stems with clusters of pea-like flowers that range in color from violet to white. The leaves are narrow and pinnate, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This plant is adapted to well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, making it valuable in ecological and agricultural contexts.
