Launaea macra

Launaea macra, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, the Middle East, and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial shrub or subshrub characterized by its silver-gray, woolly leaves and yellow composite flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is well adapted to drought conditions. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Launaea macra is also known for its medicinal properties, used traditionally in treating various ailments.
