Lycopodioides saccharata

Lycopodioides saccharata, commonly known as the sugar clubmoss, is a small, evergreen fern-like plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and the presence of spirally arranged, scale-like leaves. The plant produces spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are typically clustered in small, cone-like formations called strobila. Lycopodioides saccharata thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories or rocky crevices.

Its unique morphology and reproductive features make it an important component of certain ecological systems.
