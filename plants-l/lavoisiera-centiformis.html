Lavoisiera centiformis

Lavoisiera centiformis, commonly known as the broad-leafed lavoisiera, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy texture. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
