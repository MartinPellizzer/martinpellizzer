Lilium nevadense var. monense

Lilium nevadense var. monense, commonly known as the Sierra Valley lily, is a rare and endemic species native to the Sierra Nevada mountain range in California.

It is a perennial herb with showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open meadows and along streambanks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are sturdy and often bear a single flower.

This variety is notable for its ecological significance and is considered a symbol of the region's unique biodiversity.
