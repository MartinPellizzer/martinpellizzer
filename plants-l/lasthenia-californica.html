Lasthenia californica

Lasthenia californica, commonly known as the California poppy, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its showy yellow flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and inland areas. The California poppy is also known for its hardy nature and ability to reseed itself, making it a common wildflower in the region.
