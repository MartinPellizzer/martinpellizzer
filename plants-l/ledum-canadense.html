Ledum canadense

Ledum canadense, commonly known as Canadian rosebay, is a perennial shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces dark blue or black berries that are toxic to humans and livestock. It thrives in acidic, nutrient-poor soils and is often found in boreal and subarctic regions.

Botanically, it belongs to the Ericaceae family, also known as the heath family.
