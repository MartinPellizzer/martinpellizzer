Lupinus excubitus subsp. austromontanus

Lupinus excubitus subsp.

austromontanus, commonly known as the coastal lupine, is a perennial herb native to the western coast of North America. It is characterized by its erect growth habit and clusters of showy blue to violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along coastal bluffs and dunes. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces cylindrical seed pods that contain several seeds.

This subspecies is adapted to coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.
