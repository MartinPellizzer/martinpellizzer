Lupinus mariae-josephae

Lupinus mariae-josephae, commonly known as the Marie-Josepha lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of showy, pea-like flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are typically dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a hardy species adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
