Liatris laxa

Liatris laxa, commonly known as the slender blazingstar, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features tall, slender stems with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, pinkish-purple flower clusters in late summer. Liatris laxa thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
