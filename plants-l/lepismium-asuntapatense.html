Lepismium asuntapatense

Lepismium asuntapatense, commonly known as the Ecuadorian nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of Ecuador.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in humid, shaded environments at high altitudes. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale purple, blooming at night. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in a fine layer of hair.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
