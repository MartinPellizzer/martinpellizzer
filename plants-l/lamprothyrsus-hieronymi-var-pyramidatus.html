Lamprothyrsus hieronymi var. pyramidatus

Lamprothyrsus hieronymi var. pyramidatus, commonly known as the pyramid bellflower, is a striking member of the Campanulaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of bell-shaped, violet or blue flowers. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.
