Lathyrus coriaceus

Lathyrus coriaceus, commonly known as the woolly peavine, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its hairy, grayish-green stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. Lathyrus coriaceus thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or rocky areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
