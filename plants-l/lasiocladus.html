Lasiocladus

Lasiocladus, commonly known as the desert lichen, is a genus of lichens that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments.

It is characterized by its crustose or foliose growth form, often forming dense mats on rock surfaces. The genus includes several species, such as *Lasiocladus uncinatus*, which is notable for its distinctive, hair-like structures. Lichens in this genus are typically composed of a symbiotic association between a green alga and a fungus.

They play an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for microorganisms in harsh desert conditions.
