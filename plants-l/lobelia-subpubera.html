Lobelia subpubera

Lobelia subpubera, commonly known as the hairy lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches and features whorled, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Lobelia subpubera is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
