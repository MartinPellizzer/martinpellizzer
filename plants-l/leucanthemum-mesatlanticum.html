Leucanthemum mesatlanticum

Leucanthemum mesatlanticum, commonly known as the eastern blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range in color from white to pale blue.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in early successional plant communities.
