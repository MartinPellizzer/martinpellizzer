Louteridium dendropilosum

Louteridium dendropilosum, commonly known as the hairy tree fern, is a distinctive plant belonging to the Cyatheaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery fronds that give it a unique, elegant appearance. The plant's trunk is covered in a dense layer of soft, hairy scales, which provide protection and aid in water retention. Native to tropical regions, it thrives in humid, shaded environments with well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
