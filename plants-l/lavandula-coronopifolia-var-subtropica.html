Lavandula coronopifolia var. subtropica

Lavandula coronopifolia var. subtropica, commonly known as the narrowleaf lavender, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, silver-gray foliage and spikes of pale purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping. Its essential oils are valued for their calming properties and are used in aromatherapy and perfumery.

This variety is particularly suited to warmer climates and requires full sun for optimal growth.
