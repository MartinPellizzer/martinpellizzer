Laurentia irasuensis

Laurentia irasuensis, commonly known as the African waterwheel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive whorled arrangement of leaves and its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies such as rivers and lakes. It has a compact, shrub-like growth habit and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

The species is also known for its ability to tolerate partial shade and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
