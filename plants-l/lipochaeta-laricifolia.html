Lipochaeta laricifolia

Lipochaeta laricifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf thistle, is a perennial herb native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and spiny leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Lipochaeta laricifolia is adapted to withstand drought and salt spray, making it a hardy species in arid environments.
