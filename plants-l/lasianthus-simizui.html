Lasianthus simizui

Lasianthus simizui, commonly known as the Shikoku flame flower, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in mountainous regions. Its foliage consists of opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a glossy texture.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it remains relatively rare in horticultural trade.
