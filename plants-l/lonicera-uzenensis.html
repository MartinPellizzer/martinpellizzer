Lonicera uzenensis

Lonicera uzenensis, commonly known as the Uzen honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Lonicera uzenensis thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
