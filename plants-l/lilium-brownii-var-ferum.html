Lilium brownii var. ferum

Lilium brownii var. ferum, commonly known as the Chinese lily or the red lily, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, supporting large, showy flowers that are typically red or orange in color. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It blooms in late summer to early autumn, producing clusters of fragrant, trumpet-shaped flowers.

This variety is well-suited to well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
