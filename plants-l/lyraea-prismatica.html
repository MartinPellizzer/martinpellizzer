Lyraea prismatica

Lyraea prismatica, commonly known as the prism leaf orchid, is a striking plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, prism-like leaf structures that reflect light in a colorful spectrum, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, typically blooming during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to its harsh environment.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in specialized botanical gardens.
