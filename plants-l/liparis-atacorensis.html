Liparis atacorensis

Liparis atacorensis, commonly known as the Atacorensis orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly in Peru and Ecuador.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, often blooming during the rainy season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is typically found in cloud forests or along riverbanks.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
