Laurus albida

Laurus albida, commonly known as the white laurustree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are glossy and have a mild aromatic scent. It produces small, white flowers that are followed by spherical, black berries.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
