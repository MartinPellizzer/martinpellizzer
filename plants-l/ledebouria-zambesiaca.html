Ledebouria zambesiaca

Ledebouria zambesiaca, commonly known as the Zambesi bulbous lily, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its compact, bulbous structure and fleshy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white to pale yellow, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
