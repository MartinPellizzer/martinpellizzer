Livistona chinensis var. subglobosa

Livistona chinensis var. subglobosa, commonly known as the Chinese fan palm, is a species native to southern China and Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its robust, clustered growth habit and large, fan-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows as a solitary stem with a thick, fibrous trunk. Its leaves are usually dark green, leathery, and have a distinctive radial vein pattern.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
