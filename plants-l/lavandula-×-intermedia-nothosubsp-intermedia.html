Lavandula × intermedia nothosubsp. intermedia

Lavandula × intermedia nothosubsp.

intermedia, commonly known as English lavender, is a hybrid species within the Lamiaceae family. It is characterized by its aromatic, spike-like inflorescences of purple to pink flowers and gray-green, fragrant foliage.

The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. It is widely cultivated for its essential oils, which are used in perfumery, aromatherapy, and pharmaceuticals.

This subspecies is particularly valued for its hardiness and adaptability to various climates.
