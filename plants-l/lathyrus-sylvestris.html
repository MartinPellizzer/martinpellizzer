Lathyrus sylvestris

Lathyrus sylvestris, commonly known as the wild vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in woodland edges and rocky areas. The plant features slender, climbing stems with pinnately compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pink to purple. Its pods contain several seeds, which are adapted for dispersal by wind or animals.

Lathyrus sylvestris is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plant life.
