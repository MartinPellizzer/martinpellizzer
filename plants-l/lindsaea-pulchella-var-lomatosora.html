Lindsaea pulchella var. lomatosora

Lindsaea pulchella var. lomatosora, commonly known as the Lomato's lace fern, is a striking epiphytic fern native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are often adorned with small, spore-bearing structures called sori. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically growing on trees or rocks in rainforests. Its intricate foliage makes it a popular choice for botanical gardens and conservatories.

The varietal distinction highlights its unique morphological traits compared to the typical form of Lindsaea pulchella.
