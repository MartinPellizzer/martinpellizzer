Lycium boerhaviifolium

Lycium boerhaviifolium, commonly known as the Boerhavia-leaved boxthorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellow flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in its native habitat.
