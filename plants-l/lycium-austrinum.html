Lycium austrinum

Lycium austrinum, commonly known as the Australian boxthorn, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, orange-red berries that are edible when ripe. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a leathery texture, and it produces yellow flowers in spring.

Lycium austrinum is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to control erosion.
