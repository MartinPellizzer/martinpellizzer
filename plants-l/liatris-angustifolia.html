Liatris angustifolia

Liatris angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf blazing star, is a perennial herb native to the central and eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright, spiky flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, pink to purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Liatris angustifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
