Limonium sirinicum

Limonium sirinicum, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in coastal environments and well-drained soils, often found in sandy or gravelly areas. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color.

Limonium sirinicum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate salty air and coastal conditions.
