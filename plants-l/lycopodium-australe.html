Lycopodium australe

Lycopodium australe, commonly known as the Australian clubmoss, is a small evergreen shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and spore-bearing strobila. The plant features needle-like leaves arranged in whorls around the stem, which contribute to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understoreys.

Lycopodium australe plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various invertebrates.
