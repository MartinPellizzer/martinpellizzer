Lindsaea gueriniana

Lindsaea gueriniana, commonly known as the Guerin's spikemoss, is a member of the family Lindsaeaceae and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, evergreen herbaceous plant that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant features a slender, creeping stem with whorled leaves that are often glossy and lance-shaped. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense spikes, followed by spore-bearing structures called sporangia.

Lindsaea gueriniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
