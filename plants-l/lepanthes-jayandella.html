Lepanthes jayandella

Lepanthes jayandella, commonly known as the Jayandella's slipper orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pale green. The plant grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are notable for their unique shape, resembling a slipper, and are often fragrant.

Lepanthes jayandella is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
