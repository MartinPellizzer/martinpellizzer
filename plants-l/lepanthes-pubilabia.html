Lepanthes pubilabia

Lepanthes pubilabia, commonly known as the pubescent lady's tresses, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy stems and its unique inflorescence of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Lepanthes pubilabia is known for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in botanical gardens.
