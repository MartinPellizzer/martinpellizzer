Liparis serpens

Liparis serpens, commonly known as the snake orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, snake-like appearance, with long, ribbon-shaped leaves and a single, delicate flower. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with intricate patterns, and it blooms during the spring or early summer.

Liparis serpens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
