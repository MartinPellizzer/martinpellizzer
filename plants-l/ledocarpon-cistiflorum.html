Ledocarpon cistiflorum

Ledocarpon cistiflorum, commonly known as the rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or white flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, adapted to thrive in rocky or well-drained soils.

It is often used in rock gardens due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
