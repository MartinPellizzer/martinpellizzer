Lamium laevigatum var. pubescens

Lamium laevigatum var. pubescens, commonly known as hairy deadnettle, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are usually pubescent on both surfaces. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
