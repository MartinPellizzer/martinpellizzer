Lilium euxanthum

Lilium euxanthum, commonly known as the golden lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant is a perennial herb with long, strap-like leaves and a robust root system. It prefers well-drained soils and full sunlight, often growing in alpine or rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant in horticulture, though it is relatively rare in cultivation.
