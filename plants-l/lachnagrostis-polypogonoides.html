Lachnagrostis polypogonoides

Lachnagrostis polypogonoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-haired leaves and compact growth habit, often forming dense mats. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant communities in arid and semi-arid regions.
