Lobelia stricta

Lobelia stricta, commonly known as tall lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Lobelia stricta is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
