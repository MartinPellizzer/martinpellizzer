Ludwigia lagunae subsp. paraguayensis

Ludwigia lagunae subsp.

paraguayensis, commonly known as Paraguay waterhyssop, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Paraguay and Argentina. It is a semi-aquatic species that typically grows in shallow freshwater habitats such as ponds and marshes.

The plant features opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are often reddish in color, and it produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink to red. It is valued in aquatic gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to help control algae by competing for nutrients.

This subspecies is also known for its adaptability to a range of water conditions.
