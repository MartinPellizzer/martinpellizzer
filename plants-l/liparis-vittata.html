Liparis vittata

Liparis vittata, commonly known as the striped orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, strap-like leaves and distinctive, fragrant flowers that often display a striking yellow and green coloration. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its flowers are borne on tall, slender stems and are often clustered in groups.

Liparis vittata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
