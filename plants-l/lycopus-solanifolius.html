Lycopus solanifolius

Lycopus solanifolius, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant in the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in moist meadows and along stream banks. The plant has square stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its small, blue to purple flowers are clustered in whorls at the leaf nodes.

Lycopus solanifolius is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
