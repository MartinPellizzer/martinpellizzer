Lilium metzii

Lilium metzii, commonly known as the Metz's lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stem and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas or along streambanks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem.

Lilium metzii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
