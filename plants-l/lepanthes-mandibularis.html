Lepanthes mandibularis

Lepanthes mandibularis, commonly known as the mandibular orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically feature white or pale yellow petals. The plant grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree bark in humid, tropical environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and dark green, adapted for efficient photosynthesis in shaded conditions.

Lepanthes mandibularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
