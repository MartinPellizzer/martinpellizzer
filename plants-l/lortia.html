Lortia

Lortia, commonly known as the bluebell of the mountains, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and the Himalayas, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. The plant features bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, often with a white throat. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Lortia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and partial shade.
