Lasiandra spoliata

Lasiandra spoliata, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, often with a contrasting throat. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist woodland or swampy areas.

Lasiandra spoliata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking foliage and blooms.
