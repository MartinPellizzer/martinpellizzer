Lavandula hasikensis

Lavandula hasikensis, commonly known as the Armenian lavender, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Armenia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and spikes of purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its fragrant herbs and medicinal properties. Lavandula hasikensis thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it well-suited for rocky or arid environments.

Its essential oils contain compounds like linalool and lavandulyl acetate, which contribute to its distinctive scent and potential therapeutic uses.
