Lophocereus schottii f. spiralis

Lophocereus schottii f. spiralis, commonly known as the spiral cactus or spiral cholla, is a unique variation of the cholla cactus characterized by its spirally arranged branches.

This succulent plant is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. Its stems are covered in small, spine-tipped joints that give it a distinctive, branched appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green foliage.

Due to its slow growth and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in desert gardens and xeriscapes.
