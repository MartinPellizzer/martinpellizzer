Lithospermum ruderale var. lanceolatum

Lithospermum ruderale var. lanceolatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf rock pepper, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features slender, hairy stems that grow from the base and often form dense mats. The leaves are lanceolate, with a serrated margin and a bluish-green color when fresh. Its small, pink to reddish flowers bloom in late summer, and the plant produces dark red to black berries that are rich in anthraquinones.

This variant is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing its resilience in harsh conditions.
