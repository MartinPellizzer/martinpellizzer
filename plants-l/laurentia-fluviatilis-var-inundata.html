Laurentia fluviatilis var. inundata

Laurentia fluviatilis var. inundata, commonly known as the river laurel, is a flowering plant native to wetland and aquatic environments.

It is characterized by its dense, bushy growth habit and its ability to thrive in shallow water or moist soil. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are typically narrow and arranged in whorls around the stems.

This variety is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ecological benefits and adaptability to fluctuating water levels.
