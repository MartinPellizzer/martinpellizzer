Lupinus varius subsp. orientalis

Lupinus varius subsp.

orientalis, commonly known as the oriental lupine, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of pea-like blue to purple flowers.

The plant produces small, bean-shaped pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats. Lupinus varius subsp.

orientalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
