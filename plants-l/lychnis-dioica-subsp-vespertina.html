Lychnis dioica subsp. vespertina

Lychnis dioica subsp.

vespertina, commonly known as evening snowflake, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is a member of the carnation family, Caryophyllaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, white or pale pink flowers that open in the late afternoon.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, preferring partial shade to full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

The species is noted for its short blooming period, usually in late summer, and its historical use in traditional medicine.
