Lonchocarpus imatacensis

Lonchocarpus imatacensis, commonly known as the red mombin, is a tropical shrub belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to Central and South America, particularly found in regions of Mexico, Central America, and northern South America. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive red, fleshy fruits that contain toxic seeds. It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant appearance.

However, caution is advised as all parts of the plant contain toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.
