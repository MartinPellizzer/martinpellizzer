Leucospermum grandiflorum

Leucospermum grandiflorum, commonly known as the king protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous white to pinkish flowers arranged in a rounded head. The plant is evergreen, with leathery, glossy leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Leucospermum grandiflorum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a popular species in horticulture.
