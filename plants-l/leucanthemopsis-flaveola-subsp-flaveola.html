Leucanthemopsis flaveola subsp. flaveola

Leucanthemopsis flaveola subsp.

flaveola, commonly known as the yellow oxeye daisy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its bright yellow ray florets and pale yellow disc florets, which give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It produces small, herbaceous stems and has hairy leaves that are often lance-shaped.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its cheerful blooms.
