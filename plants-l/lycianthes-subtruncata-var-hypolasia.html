Lycianthes subtruncata var. hypolasia

Lycianthes subtruncata var. hypolasia, commonly known as the purpleleaf nightshade, is a member of the Solanaceae family.

It is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Central and South America. The plant features dark green, ovate leaves with a distinctive purple underside, giving it its common name. Its flowers are typically yellow with purple veins, and it produces small, orange-red berries that are edible but often bitter.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in some regions for its unique foliage.
