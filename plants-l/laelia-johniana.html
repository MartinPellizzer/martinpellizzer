Laelia johniana

Laelia johniana, commonly known as the John's orchid, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers with a typical orchid structure, featuring three sepals and three petals. Its leaves are typically long and strap-like, adapted to thrive in cool, humid environments.

Laelia johniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
