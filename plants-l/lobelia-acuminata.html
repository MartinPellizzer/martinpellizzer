Lobelia acuminata

Lobelia acuminata, commonly known as the sharplobelia, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features whorled leaves and spikes of tubular, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity.

The plant's ornamental value makes it a popular choice for wetland gardens and conservation efforts.
