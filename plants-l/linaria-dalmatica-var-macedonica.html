Linaria dalmatica var. macedonica

Linaria dalmatica var. macedonica, commonly known as the Macedonian toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or dry habitats. The plant features slender, upright stems and produces vibrant yellow-orange flowers with a distinctive spurred shape. Its leaves are linear and often covered in a fine layer of hair.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its bright blooms.
