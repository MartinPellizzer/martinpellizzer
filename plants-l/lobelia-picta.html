Lobelia picta

Lobelia picta, commonly known as the painted lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features striking, showy flowers that range in color from blue to purple, often with intricate patterns that resemble painted designs. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are borne in dense spikes, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

Lobelia picta is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks, where it contributes to the biodiversity of these habitats.
