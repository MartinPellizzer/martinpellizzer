Lilium speciosum f. concolor

Lilium speciosum f. concolor, commonly known as the concolor lily, is a botanical variant of the showy lily.

It is characterized by its striking, pale pink to white flowers with a distinctive concolorous (uniform) coloration across the petals and throat. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of western North America, particularly in California and Oregon. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats with well-drained soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant floral display.
