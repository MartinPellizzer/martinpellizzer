Luzula campestris pallens

Luzula campestris pallens, commonly known as the pale moor grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with a pale blue or silver hue. This plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, bogs, and heathlands. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish or brownish flowers in dense clusters during the late spring to early summer.

Luzula campestris pallens is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
