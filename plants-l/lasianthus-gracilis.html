Lasianthus gracilis

Lasianthus gracilis, commonly known as the slender lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of delicate, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or white, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
