Lemaireocereus gladiger

Lemaireocereus gladiger, commonly known as the Gladiger cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a low-growing, spineless cactus with a distinctive appearance characterized by its pale green, ribbed stems. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers during the summer months, which are pollinated by nocturnal insects. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its structure and growth habits make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ecological research.
