Ludwigia pubescens var. villosa

Ludwigia pubescens var. villosa, commonly known as the woolly Ludwigia, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in moist, nutrient-rich environments, often found in wetlands or along riverbanks. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a velvety texture, and the stems are often reddish in color.

This plant is popular in aquatic and bog gardens due to its ornamental value and ability to thrive in waterlogged conditions.
