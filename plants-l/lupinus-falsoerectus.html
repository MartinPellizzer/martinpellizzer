Lupinus falsoerectus

Lupinus falsoerectus, commonly known as the false erect lupine, is a flowering plant native to parts of the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and showy blue to purple flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.
