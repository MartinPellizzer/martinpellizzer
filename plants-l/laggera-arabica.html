Laggera arabica

Laggera arabica, commonly known as African ironwood, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its durability and resistance to termites. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are often hairy, and it produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. Laggera arabica is also known for its medicinal properties, with various parts of the plant used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.
