Lobivia haematantha subsp. kuehnrichii

Lobivia haematantha subsp.

kuehnrichii, commonly known as the Kuehnrich's lobivia, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America. It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rounded shape and fleshy, ribbed stems.

The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, a feature adapted to minimize water loss in its harsh environment.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and as a houseplant.
