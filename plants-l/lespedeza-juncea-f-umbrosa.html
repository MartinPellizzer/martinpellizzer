Lespedeza juncea f. umbrosa

Lespedeza juncea f. umbrosa, commonly known as the shade lespedeza, is a perennial legume native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fern-like, trifoliate leaves and produces small, pinkish-white flowers in clusters. This plant thrives in partial to full shade and is often found in woodland areas and along forest edges. It has a fibrous root system that helps prevent soil erosion.

Lespedeza juncea f. umbrosa is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
