Lapeirousia corymbosa subsp. fastigiata

Lapeirousia corymbosa subsp.

fastigiata, commonly known as the Cape goldcup, is a striking member of the Iridaceae family native to South Africa. It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that form a rosette at the base. Its flowers are typically arranged in a corymb, giving it a compact and showy appearance.

This subspecies is well adapted to rocky and sandy soils, thriving in full sun and requiring minimal water once established.
