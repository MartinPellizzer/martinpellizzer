Locella montana

Locella montana, commonly known as the mountain locella, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with small, four-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool and moist conditions. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often covered in a fine pubescence.

The flowers are usually bright yellow or white, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
