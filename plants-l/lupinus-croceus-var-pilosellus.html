Lupinus croceus var. pilosellus

Lupinus croceus var. pilosellus, commonly known as the silky lupin, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small, hairy leaflets, giving it a soft, velvety appearance. Its root system fixes nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.
