Lomatia arguta

Lomatia arguta, commonly known as the mountain devil, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, often in heathlands and open forests. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in spring. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, with a distinctive, zigzagged appearance.

Lomatia arguta is well adapted to arid conditions and is known for its resilience in harsh environments.
