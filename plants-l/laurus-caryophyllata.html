Laurus caryophyllata

Laurus caryophyllata, commonly known as bay laurel, is a flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into dark, glossy berries. Its leaves are widely used in cooking as a spice and for their aromatic properties.

The plant is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
