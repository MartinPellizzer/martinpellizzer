Lophodium abbreviatum

Lophodium abbreviatum, commonly known as the dwarf lophodium, is a small, herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often used in rock gardens due to its ornamental value.

Lophodium abbreviatum is relatively drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
