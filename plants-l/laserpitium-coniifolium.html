Laserpitium coniifolium

Laserpitium coniifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine layer of silky hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
