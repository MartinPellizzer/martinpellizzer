Litsea populifolia

Litsea populifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf litsea, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are usually smooth and have a glossy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by yellowish or orange berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
