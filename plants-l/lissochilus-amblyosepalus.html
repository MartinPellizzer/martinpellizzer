Lissochilus amblyosepalus

Lissochilus amblyosepalus, commonly known as the Andean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a fine, silvery down, which helps to reduce water loss.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
