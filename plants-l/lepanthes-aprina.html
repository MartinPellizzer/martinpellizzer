Lepanthes aprina

Lepanthes aprina, commonly known as the Easter Orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in the spring. The plant has a pseudobulb structure, which stores water and nutrients, and produces strap-like leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a distinctive lip that is often marked with purple or brown streaks.

Lepanthes aprina thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in tropical forests.
