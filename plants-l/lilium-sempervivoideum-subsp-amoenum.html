Lilium sempervivoideum subsp. amoenum

Lilium sempervivoideum subsp.

amoenum, commonly known as the everlasting lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, bearing showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and red.

The plant produces large, showy blooms in late summer to early autumn, with six to nine petals arranged in a cup-like shape. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, and it is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun.

The subspecies amoenum is particularly noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and longevity.
