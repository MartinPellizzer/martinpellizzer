Lepanthes thoerleae

Lepanthes thoerleae, commonly known as the Thoerle's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically display a range of colors including white, pink, and purple. The plant grows as an epiphyte, often found on trees in humid tropical environments. Its leaves are usually elongated and strap-like, adapted for capturing moisture from the air.

Lepanthes thoerleae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
