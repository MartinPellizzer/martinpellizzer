Lactuca rogersii

Lactuca rogersii, commonly known as Rogers' lettuce, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette of spoon-shaped, pale green leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in a flat-topped inflorescence. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open meadows and grasslands.

Lactuca rogersii is adapted to arid environments and has a shallow root system that allows it to conserve water efficiently.
