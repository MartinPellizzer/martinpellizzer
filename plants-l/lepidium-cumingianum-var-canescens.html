Lepidium cumingianum var. canescens

Lepidium cumingianum var. canescens, commonly known as the hairy cress, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many edible and economically important crops. The plant is characterized by its small, white or yellow flowers and hairy stems and leaves. It typically grows in moist, disturbed habitats such as along riverbanks and in open fields.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
