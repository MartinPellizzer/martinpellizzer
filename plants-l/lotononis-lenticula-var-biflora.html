Lotononis lenticula var. biflora

Lotononis lenticula var. biflora, commonly known as the two-flowered lens-leaf daisy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive lens-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It produces two to three flowers per inflorescence, which bloom in spring and early summer.

Due to its ornamental value and unique morphology, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
