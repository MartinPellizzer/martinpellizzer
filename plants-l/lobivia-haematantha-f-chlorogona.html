Lobivia haematantha f. chlorogona

Lobivia haematantha f. chlorogona, commonly known as the Chlorogona Lobivia, is a rare and striking cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in dense, white to pale green spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are typically borne at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is often cultivated by enthusiasts of rare succulents.
