Lepidogrammitis pyriformis

Lepidogrammitis pyriformis, commonly known as the globular fern, is a small, delicate fern species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features a compact, globular frond structure, which gives it its common name. The plant has a short, creeping rhizome with numerous small, spore-producing sporangia. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, moist soil.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and ornamental use.
