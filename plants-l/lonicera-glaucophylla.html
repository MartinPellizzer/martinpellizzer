Lonicera glaucophylla

Lonicera glaucophylla, commonly known as the silverleaf honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish pubescence, giving them a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, which bloom in the spring. These flowers are followed by red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds.

Lonicera glaucophylla is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and adaptability to various soil conditions.
