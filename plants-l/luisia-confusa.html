Luisia confusa

Luisia confusa, commonly known as the Florida larkspur, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and spurred flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually blue or purple, with a distinctive spurred petal that aids in pollination by specific insects.

Luisia confusa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
