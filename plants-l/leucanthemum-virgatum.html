Leucanthemum virgatum

Leucanthemum virgatum, commonly known as the white marguerite, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features showy white petals with a yellow center, forming daisy-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a robust root system and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders. Its foliage is basal, with lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy.

Leucanthemum virgatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
