Lactuca segusiana

Lactuca segusiana, commonly known as the mountain lettuce, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and the Himalayas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy structure. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring cool and moist conditions. Its leaves are usually elongated and slightly hairy, with a bitter taste.

The species is valued for its potential medicinal properties and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
