Lanessania

Lanessania, commonly known as the moonflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, white, night-blooming flowers that emit a strong fragrance. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its rapid growth and short lifespan. Lanessania thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its unique blooming pattern makes it a popular subject in botanical studies and horticulture.
