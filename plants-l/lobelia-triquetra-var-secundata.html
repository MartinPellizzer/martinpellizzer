Lobelia triquetra var. secundata

Lobelia triquetra var. secundata, commonly known as the clustered blue lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, tubular blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
