Lupinus chrysomelas

Lupinus chrysomelas, commonly known as the golden lupin, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability.

Lupinus chrysomelas is often found in dry, open habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
