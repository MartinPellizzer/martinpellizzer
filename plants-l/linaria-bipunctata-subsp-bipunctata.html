Linaria bipunctata subsp. bipunctata

Linaria bipunctata subsp.

bipunctata, commonly known as the two-spotted toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and bright yellow flowers with two distinct black spots on each petal.

The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. Linaria bipunctata subsp.

bipunctata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
