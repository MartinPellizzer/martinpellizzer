Leptospermum laevigatum var. minus

Leptospermum laevigatum var. minus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tea tree, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery-white bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. It is well adapted to coastal and sandy soils, and it thrives in full sun.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
