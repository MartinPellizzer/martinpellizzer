Leersia digitaria

Leersia digitaria, commonly known as purpletop grass, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and distinctive purple-tinged inflorescence. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and blade-like, with a smooth texture and a prominent midrib.

Leersia digitaria plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
