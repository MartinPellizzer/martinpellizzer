Lacis ceratophylla

Lacis ceratophylla, commonly known as the horned flax, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spiny bracts that resemble horns. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a dense cluster. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest in botanical studies.
