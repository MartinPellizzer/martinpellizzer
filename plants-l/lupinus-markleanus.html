Lupinus markleanus

Lupinus markleanus, commonly known as Marklean's lupin, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils. The plant produces clusters of vibrant blue or purple pea-like flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Lupinus markleanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also studied for its potential in ecological restoration projects.
