Licania sclerophylla var. scabra

Licania sclerophylla var. scabra, commonly known as the rough-leaved licania, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a woody plant characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, elliptical leaves that are covered in a rough, scaly texture. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, such as the understory of tropical rainforests. Its flowers are small and clustered, often producing a fragrant scent to attract pollinators.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.
