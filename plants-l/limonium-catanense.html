Limonium catanense

Limonium catanense, commonly known as the Catanese sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes. Its flowers are often used in floral arrangements due to their long-lasting nature and striking color.

Limonium catanense is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
