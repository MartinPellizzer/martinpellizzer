Lepidium coronopifolium var. lacerum

Lepidium coronopifolium var. lacerum, commonly known as the fringed cress, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are finely divided and often have a fringed appearance, giving it its common name.

It is an important plant for pollinators and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
