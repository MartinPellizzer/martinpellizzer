Liatris tenuifolia

Liatris tenuifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf blazingstar, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescence composed of purple to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and woodlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Liatris tenuifolia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
