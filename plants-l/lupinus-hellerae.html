Lupinus hellerae

Lupinus hellerae, commonly known as the Hellerae lupine, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and spiny stems. The plant produces clusters of pea-like flowers in shades of purple and blue, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Lupinus hellerae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for ecological restoration projects.
