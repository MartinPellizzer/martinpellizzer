Liabum polycephalum

Liabum polycephalum, commonly known as the many-headed flax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in textile production, it is cultivated in various parts of the world.
