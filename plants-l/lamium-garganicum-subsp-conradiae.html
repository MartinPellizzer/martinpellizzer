Lamium garganicum subsp. conradiae

Lamium garganicum subsp.

conradiae, commonly known as Conrad's dead-nettle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color.

The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. It is characterized by its low-growing habit and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
