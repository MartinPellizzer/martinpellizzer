Leucolophus gajoensis

Leucolophus gajoensis, commonly known as the Gajo's lichen, is a rare and specialized species of lichen that thrives in specific, often remote environments.

It is characterized by its unique thallus structure, which is typically crustose and tightly adherent to rock surfaces. The organism is composed of a symbiotic partnership between a fungus and a green alga, which allows it to absorb nutrients from the environment. Due to its limited distribution, it is considered an indicator species for particular ecological conditions.

Conservation efforts are crucial to protect this delicate and ecologically significant organism.
