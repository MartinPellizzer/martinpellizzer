Lotus angustissimus subsp. suaveolens

Lotus angustissimus subsp.

suaveolens, commonly known as the narrowleaf water-lotus, is a aquatic plant native to parts of Asia, particularly China and India. It typically grows in shallow freshwater habitats such as ponds and slow-moving streams.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves that float on the water surface, with long, slender stems that support its showy, pink or red flowers. Its flowers are fragrant and attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The subspecies is characterized by its narrow leaf blades and distinct, aromatic blooms.
