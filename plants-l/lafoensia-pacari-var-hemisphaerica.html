Lafoensia pacari var. hemisphaerica

Lafoensia pacari var. hemisphaerica, commonly known as the red flame tree, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, adding vibrant color to its surroundings. The plant has compound leaves with long, slender leaflets that give it a feathery appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental tree.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in tropical and subtropical regions.
