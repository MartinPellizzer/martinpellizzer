Leucoploeus argenteus

Leucoploeus argenteus, commonly known as silverleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for drought-tolerant gardens.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological landscapes.
