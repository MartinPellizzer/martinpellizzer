Linaria galioides f. glaberrima

Linaria galioides f. glaberrima, commonly known as the smooth-flowered toadflax, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Plantaginaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, tubular flowers that typically range in color from yellow to orange. The leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, though the 'glaberrima' form is distinguished by its smoother, hairless surface. This plant is native to certain regions of southern Africa and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its delicate appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
