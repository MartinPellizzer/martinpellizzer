Lobivia famatinensis var. setosa

Lobivia famatinensis var. setosa, commonly known as the Setose Lobivia, is a succulent native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded growth habit and thick, fleshy stems that store water. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are typically short and woolly, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
