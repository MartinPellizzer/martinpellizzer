Luehea crispa

Luehea crispa, commonly known as the white mangosteen, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, featuring thick, waxy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by yellow or orange fruits with a thick, fibrous rind. Its botanical characteristics include a robust root system and the ability to thrive in humid, well-drained soils.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in tropical ecosystems.
