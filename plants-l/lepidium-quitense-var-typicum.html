Lepidium quitense var. typicum

Lepidium quitense var. typicum, commonly known as Peruvian pepper or *Chilquintan* in Quechua, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Andean regions of South America. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits called siliques. Its seeds are small and are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

The plant is valued for its aromatic properties and is cultivated for both culinary and medicinal purposes.
