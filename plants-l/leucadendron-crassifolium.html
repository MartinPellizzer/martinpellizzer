Leucadendron crassifolium

Leucadendron crassifolium, commonly known as the Silver Curly, is a South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, silvery-gray foliage and cone-like inflorescences that range in color from yellow to red. The plant is native to the Western Cape region and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance.

The leaves are leathery and aromatic, contributing to its unique botanical profile.
