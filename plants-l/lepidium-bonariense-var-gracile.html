Lepidium bonariense var. gracile

Lepidium bonariense var. gracile, commonly known as slender field pennycress, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and pinnately compound leaves. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in grasslands and agricultural fields. It produces small, round seeds that are dispersed by wind, and it is often considered a weed in cultivated areas.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and agricultural studies.
