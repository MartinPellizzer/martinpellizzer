Lepanthes otara

Lepanthes otara, commonly known as the Orange Ghost Orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its bright orange petals and a distinctive, ghost-like appearance due to its elongated, narrow shape. This epiphytic plant grows on trees in humid, shaded environments, relying on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient absorption. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, with a unique coloration that sets it apart from other orchid species.

Lepanthes otara is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts due to its beauty and rarity.
