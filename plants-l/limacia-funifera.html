Limacia funifera

Limacia funifera, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a bushy growth habit with silvery-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Limacia funifera thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature contribute to its appeal in both ornamental and ecological contexts.
