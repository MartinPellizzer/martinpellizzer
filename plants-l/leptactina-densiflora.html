Leptactina densiflora

Leptactina densiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered leptactina, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Leptactina densiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
