Lonicera ruprechtiana f. xanthocarpa

Lonicera ruprechtiana f. xanthocarpa, commonly known as the yellow-fruited honeysuckle, is a variant of the honeysuckle plant native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces distinctive yellow berries that are edible and often used in traditional Chinese medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators and providing food for wildlife.
