Lobelia berlandieri var. seleriana

Lobelia berlandieri var. seleriana, commonly known as the Mexican whorled lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue to purple flowers that bloom in whorls along the stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and unique floral structure.
