Lankesteria parviflora

Lankesteria parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered lankesteria, is a member of the Gesneriaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, tubular flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant produces small, opposite leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture to maintain its lush appearance.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in greenhouse environments.
