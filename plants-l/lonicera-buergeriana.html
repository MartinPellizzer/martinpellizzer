Lonicera buergeriana

Lonicera buergeriana, commonly known as the Japanese honeysuckle, is a vigorous climbing vine native to East Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are often slightly hairy, and its fragrant, tubular flowers are typically yellow or orange with a red throat. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible but not particularly flavorful. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to cover structures.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions if not managed properly.
