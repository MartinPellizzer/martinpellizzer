Linaria rubrifolia var. raveyi

Linaria rubrifolia var. raveyi, commonly known as the red-leaf toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, often with a reddish tinge, especially on the underside. This variety is known for its ornamental value in gardens due to its vibrant foliage and delicate blossoms.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or containers.
