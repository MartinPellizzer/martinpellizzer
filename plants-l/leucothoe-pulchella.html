Leucothoe pulchella

Leucothoe pulchella, commonly known as the pink azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has opposite, elliptical leaves that are dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter underside. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

Leucothoe pulchella is often used in ornamental landscaping for its attractive foliage and spring blooms.
