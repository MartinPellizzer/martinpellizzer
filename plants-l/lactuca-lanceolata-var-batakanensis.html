Lactuca lanceolata var. batakanensis

Lactuca lanceolata var. batakanensis, commonly known as Batakan lettuce, is a variant of the common lettuce species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are typically narrow and pointed. The plant usually grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are often used in local cuisines for their mild, slightly bitter flavor.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family and is closely related to other lettuce species.
