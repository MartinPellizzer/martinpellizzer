Lupinus sericeus var. maximus

Lupinus sericeus var. maximus, commonly known as the silky lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its soft, silvery foliage and tall spires of blue to purple flowers. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open grasslands and disturbed sites.

Lupinus sericeus var. maximus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
