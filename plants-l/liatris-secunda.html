Liatris secunda

Liatris secunda, commonly known as prairie blazingstar, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its showy, spiky flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and sandy soils. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces purple to pink flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Liatris secunda is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
