Leymus × multiflorus

Leymus × multiflorus, commonly known as the hybrid wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, fine-textured foliage. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides and prairies, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Due to its adaptability and resilience, Leymus × multiflorus is often used in ecological restoration projects and as a forage crop.
