Leptochilus costatus

Leptochilus costatus, commonly known as the smooth-leaved rockcress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a small, flowering plant that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually smooth or only slightly hairy. Its flowers are small and white or pale yellow, arranged in racemes.

It is often found in mountainous regions and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
