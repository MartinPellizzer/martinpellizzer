Lycium vulgare var. boissieri

Lycium vulgare var. boissieri, commonly known as the red bryony, is a perennial shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible but have a slightly tart flavor. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in wastelands and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Solanaceae family.
