Lindneria clavata

Lindneria clavata, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, often with a white or yellow throat. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in clusters around the stems, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Lindneria clavata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
