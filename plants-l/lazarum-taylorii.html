Lazarum taylorii

Lazarum taylorii, commonly known as Taylor's lazarus, is a rare flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves arranged in a basal rosette, which helps it conserve water in its arid habitat. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and requires full sun exposure.
