Linaria genistifolia subsp. euxina

Linaria genistifolia subsp.

euxina, commonly known as the Greek toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its ability to attract pollinators.
