Lepidium longifolium

Lepidium longifolium, commonly known as the longleaf rocket, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pinnately divided leaves. The plant produces tiny white or yellow flowers that are arranged in racemes, which bloom in the spring. Lepidium longifolium is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

It is a hardy perennial that is sometimes cultivated as a ornamental plant due to its delicate appearance.
