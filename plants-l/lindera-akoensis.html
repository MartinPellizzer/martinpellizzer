Lindera akoensis

Lindera akoensis, commonly known as the Japanese spicebush, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant features opposite, elliptic leaves with a glossy surface and a distinct aromatic scent. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible for some bird species.

Lindera akoensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in traditional herbal medicine.
