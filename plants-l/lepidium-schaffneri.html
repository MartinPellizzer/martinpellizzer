Lepidium schaffneri

Lepidium schaffneri, commonly known as Schaffner's cress, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments and produces small, white or yellow flowers. Its leaves are usually alternate, simple, and slightly lobed, while the fruit is a small, dry capsule that contains several seeds.

Lepidium schaffneri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and floral display.
