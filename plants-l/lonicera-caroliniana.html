Lonicera caroliniana

Lonicera caroliniana, commonly known as the Carolina honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that range from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring. Its berries are red and edible for birds, contributing to its ecological importance.

Lonicera caroliniana is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.
