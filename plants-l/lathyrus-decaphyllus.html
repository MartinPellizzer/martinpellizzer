Lathyrus decaphyllus

Lathyrus decaphyllus, commonly known as the ten-leaf pea, is a climbing legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, twining stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces pods containing several elliptical seeds, which are typically dark brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Lathyrus decaphyllus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
