Ligustrum tschonoskii var. macrocarpum

Ligustrum tschonoskii var. macrocarpum, commonly known as the large-fruited privet, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features opposite, simple, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in clusters, followed by elliptical, dark blue berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.

The varietal name "macrocarpum" refers to its larger, more prominent fruits compared to the typical species.
