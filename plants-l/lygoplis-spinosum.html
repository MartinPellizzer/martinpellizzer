Lygoplis spinosum

Lygoplis spinosum, commonly known as the spiny lygodium, is a member of the Lygodiaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive spiny structures.

It is a climbing fern that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in shaded, moist environments. The plant has feathery, pinnately compound leaves that are covered in small, sharp spines. Its reproductive structures include sporangia that produce spores, which are dispersed by wind.

Lygoplis spinosum is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
