Lacinaria graminifolia

Lacinaria graminifolia, commonly known as the grassy lacinaria, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grassland habitats and has slender, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing both ray and disk florets that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Lacinaria graminifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
