Lepidium ruderale var. poltavensis

Lepidium ruderale var. poltavensis, commonly known as the Poltava cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and clusters of tiny, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, riverbanks, and along roadsides. It produces small, elongated siliques that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This variety is native to eastern Europe and is considered a minor weed in agricultural settings.
