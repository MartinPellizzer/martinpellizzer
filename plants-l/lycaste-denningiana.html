Lycaste denningiana

Lycaste denningiana, commonly known as the Denning's lycaste, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its pseudobulbs and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces inflorescences with several blooms, each featuring a distinct coloration pattern. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions for its aesthetic appeal.
