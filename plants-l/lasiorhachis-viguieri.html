Lasiorhachis viguieri

Lasiorhachis viguieri, commonly known as the Viguier's hairroot, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its fibrous, hair-like roots and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in loose inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded, woodland environments.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
