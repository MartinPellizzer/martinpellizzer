Lellingeria suspensa

Lellingeria suspensa, commonly known as the hanging bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous, bell-shaped flowers that hang from long stems, giving it a unique appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Lellingeria suspensa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, colorful blooms.
