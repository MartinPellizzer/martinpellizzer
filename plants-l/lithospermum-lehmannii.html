Lithospermum lehmannii

Lithospermum lehmannii, commonly known as Lehmann's burning bush, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking red-orange flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Lithospermum lehmannii is also known for its medicinal properties, with some parts of the plant used traditionally in herbal remedies.
