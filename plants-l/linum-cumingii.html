Linum cumingii

Linum cumingii, commonly known as Cuming's flax, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its delicate blue or white flowers and linear leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its seeds are used for oil extraction, similar to other flax species, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Linum cumingii is also noted for its ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
