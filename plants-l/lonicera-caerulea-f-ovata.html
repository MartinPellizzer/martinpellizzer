Lonicera caerulea f. ovata

Lonicera caerulea f. ovata, commonly known as the oval honeysuckle, is a unique cultivar of the honeysuckle plant.

It is characterized by its oval-shaped berries, which are typically larger and more elongated than those of the standard species. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

This variety thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
