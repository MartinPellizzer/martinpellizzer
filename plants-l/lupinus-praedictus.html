Lupinus praedictus

Lupinus praedictus, commonly known as the predicted lupine, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of blue to violet pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces cylindrical seed pods that contain several seeds.

Lupinus praedictus is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils and open landscapes.
