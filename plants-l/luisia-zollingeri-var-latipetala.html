Luisia zollingeri var. latipetala

Luisia zollingeri var. latipetala, commonly known as the broad-petaled luisia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. Its flowers, typically yellow or orange, are arranged in dense clusters and bloom during the cooler months.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained, rocky soils.
