Linaria munbyana var. eumunbyana

Linaria munbyana var. eumunbyana, commonly known as the red-flowered linaria, is a striking annual plant native to southern Australia.

It features slender, branching stems that rise from the ground, often reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. The plant produces clusters of small, two-lipped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom from late winter to early spring. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

This species is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display and is well-suited to Mediterranean climates.
