Lauro-cerasus acuminata

Lauro-cerasus acuminata, commonly known as the cherry laurel, is a species of flowering plant in the Prunus genus.

It is native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan, and is characterized by its dark green, shiny leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark red or black when mature. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

However, it can be toxic if ingested, making it important to handle with care.
