Lyonia bracteata

Lyonia bracteata, commonly known as the mountain snowberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, blue-black berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Lyonia bracteata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
