Linum virgatum

Linum virgatum, commonly known as Virginia flax, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking blue or violet flowers. The plant features slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. Its flowers are typically arranged in loose clusters and bloom from late spring to early summer.

Virginia flax is often found in moist woodlands and along stream banks, where it thrives in partial to full shade.
