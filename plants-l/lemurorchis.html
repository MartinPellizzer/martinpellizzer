Lemurorchis

Lemurorchis, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a genus of flowering plants native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, often fragrant flowers. The plants typically grow as terrestrial or epiphytic herbs, with slender stems and lance-shaped leaves. They are known for their striking, colorful blooms that can range from white to purple.

Lemurorchis species are adapted to the humid and often shaded environments of Madagascar's tropical forests.
