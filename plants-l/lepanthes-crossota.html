Lepanthes crossota

Lepanthes crossota, commonly known as the Cross's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees in humid, tropical environments. The plant features showy, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale pink with intricate patterns. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Lepanthes crossota is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
