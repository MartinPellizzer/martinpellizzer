Lonicera × purpusii

Lonicera × purpusii, commonly known as the Japanese honeysuckle, is a deciduous climbing vine native to East Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it adaptable to different environments. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.

However, it is also considered an invasive species in some regions due to its aggressive spreading habit.
