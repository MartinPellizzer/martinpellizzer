Libanotis coreana f. ugoensis

Libanotis coreana f. ugoensis, commonly known as the Korean bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as woodlands and stream banks. It has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and a compact, bushy growth habit.

This subspecies is native to Korea and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
