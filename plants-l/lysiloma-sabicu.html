Lysiloma sabicu

Lysiloma sabicu, commonly known as the Brazilian umbrella tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, umbrella-like clusters of small, oval-shaped leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by yellowish or orange fruit pods. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.

Lysiloma sabicu is also valued for its hardy nature and adaptability to various soil types.
