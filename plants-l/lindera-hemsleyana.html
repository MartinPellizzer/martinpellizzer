Lindera hemsleyana

Lindera hemsleyana, commonly known as the Hemsley's spicebush, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like cinnamon and avocado. The plant is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by berries that are typically red or orange when mature.

Lindera hemsleyana is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and stream sides.
