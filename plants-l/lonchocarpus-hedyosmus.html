Lonchocarpus hedyosmus

Lonchocarpus hedyosmus, commonly known as the wild senna, is a shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or bean family, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds surrounded by a fleshy, orange aril. It is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.

Additionally, it has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
