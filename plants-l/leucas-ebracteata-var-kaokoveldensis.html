Leucas ebracteata var. kaokoveldensis

Leucas ebracteata var. kaokoveldensis, commonly known as the Kaokoveld leucas, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showing adaptations to drought conditions. Its leaves are often narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal uses in traditional medicine.
