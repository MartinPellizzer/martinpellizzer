Liabum igniarium

Liabum igniarium, commonly known as the flame flower, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red and orange flowers that resemble flames, which bloom throughout the warmer months. The plant has a woody stem with smooth, glossy leaves that are dark green in color. It thrives in full sunlight and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
