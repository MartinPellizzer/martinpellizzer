Lespedeza nuristanica

Lespedeza nuristanica, commonly known as the Nuristan lespedeza, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in Afghanistan and Pakistan.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are trifoliate, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds.

Lespedeza nuristanica plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native ecosystems and is of interest for its potential in ecological restoration projects.
