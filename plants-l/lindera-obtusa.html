Lindera obtusa

Lindera obtusa, commonly known as Japanese spicebush, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically glossy and dark green, with a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers in early spring, followed by orange-red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Lindera obtusa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and seasonal interest.
