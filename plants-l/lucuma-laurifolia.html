Lucuma laurifolia

Lucuma laurifolia, commonly known as the golden apple or lucuma, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America, particularly the Andes region.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and yellow-orange fruits that resemble small apples. The fruit has a sweet, nutty flavor and is rich in nutrients such as vitamin C, fiber, and minerals. Lucuma laurifolia thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires a warm, tropical climate.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit.
