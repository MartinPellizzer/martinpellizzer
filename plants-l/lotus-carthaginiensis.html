Lotus carthaginiensis

Lotus carthaginiensis, commonly known as the Carthage lotus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branched stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, covered in soft, hairy foliage. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers with five petals, arranged in a distinctive lotus-like pattern. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the plant is adapted to thrive in well-drained, sandy soils.

Lotus carthaginiensis is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance.
