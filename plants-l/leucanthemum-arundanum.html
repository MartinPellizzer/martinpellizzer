Leucanthemum arundanum

Leucanthemum arundanum, commonly known as the meadow cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges, preferring moist, well-drained soils. The plant features basal leaves that are heart-shaped and hairy, with a height ranging from 30 to 90 centimeters. Its flowers are yellow with white ray florets, blooming in summer, and it is often used in gardens for its ornamental value.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
