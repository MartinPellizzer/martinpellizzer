Lepidium virginicum var. kashmiricum

Lepidium virginicum var. kashmiricum, commonly known as Kashmir cress, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of Pakistan and India.

It is a small annual herb with slender stems and pinnately divided leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in early spring. Its seeds are enclosed in small, oval pods that split open to release the seeds.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.
