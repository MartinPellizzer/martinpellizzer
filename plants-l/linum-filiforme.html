Linum filiforme

Linum filiforme, commonly known as slender flax, is a low-growing annual plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, hairless stems that often lie prostrate on the ground. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pale blue, with five petals. Its leaves are narrow and linear, arranged alternately along the stem.

Linum filiforme is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
