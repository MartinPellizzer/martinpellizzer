Lonicera pyrenaica var. grandiflora

Lonicera pyrenaica var. grandiflora, commonly known as the Pyrenean honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features opposite, simple, ovate to elliptic leaves that are typically dark green and slightly pubescent. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers in clusters, which are usually yellow or orange in color. It blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The fruit is a small, red drupe that ripens in late summer, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
