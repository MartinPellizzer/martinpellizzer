Ledonia microphylla

Ledonia microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved ledonia, is a rare and unique plant species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and tiny, densely packed leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, Ledonia microphylla is considered a species of conservation concern.
