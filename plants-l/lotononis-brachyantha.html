Lotononis brachyantha

Lotononis brachyantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellow composite flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is typically found in grassy areas and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils. It has a taproot system and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
