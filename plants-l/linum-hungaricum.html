Linum hungaricum

Linum hungaricum, commonly known as Hungarian flax, is a flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and blue or purple flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Linum hungaricum is often grown for its ornamental value and has historical uses in textile production.
