Lithospermum arvense subsp. incrassatum

Lithospermum arvense subsp.

incrassatum, commonly known as broadleaf sandroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its thick, fleshy roots and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, pale pink to violet flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves and roots contain various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in medicinal and ecological studies.

It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.
