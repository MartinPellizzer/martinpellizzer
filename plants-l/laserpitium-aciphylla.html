Laserpitium aciphylla

Laserpitium aciphylla, commonly known as the leafless hawk's-beard, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous plant characterized by its absence of true leaves, with only bracts present. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that are typically clustered in a single head. It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and thrives in rocky, arid environments.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
