Lycium leptacanthum

Lycium leptacanthum, commonly known as the slender-fruited boxthorn, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible and often consumed by birds and small mammals. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, adapted to conserve water in dry environments.

Lycium leptacanthum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
