Laurus nobilis var. longifolia

Laurus nobilis var. longifolia, commonly known as the Mediterranean laurel or sweet bay laurel, is a variant of the bay tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a broadleaf evergreen shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, featuring dark green, shiny, lance-shaped leaves. The leaves are aromatic and have been used historically in cooking and as a symbol of honor and victory. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by dark purple berries, which are edible but less commonly consumed.

This plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental and culinary purposes.
