Lippia rotundifolia

Lippia rotundifolia, commonly known as Mexican mint or honey mint, is a flowering plant native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly minty leaves and small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, tropical environments and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded areas. It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is known for its strong fragrance and use in traditional medicine.

Lippia rotundifolia is also cultivated as a decorative and aromatic herb in gardens.
