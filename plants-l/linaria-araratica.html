Linaria araratica

Linaria araratica, commonly known as the Armenian toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking, yellow-orange flowers resembling those of the common toadflax. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are arranged in racemes and bloom in the spring and early summer.

Linaria araratica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and adaptability to various soil conditions.
