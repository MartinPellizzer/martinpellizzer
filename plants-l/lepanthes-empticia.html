Lepanthes empticia

Lepanthes empticia, commonly known as the Emerald Orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking greenish-white flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns and a faintly colored lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded forest environments. Its leaves are usually broad and strap-like, adapted for efficient water absorption in its natural habitat.

Lepanthes empticia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance.
