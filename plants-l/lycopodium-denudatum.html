Lycopodium denudatum

Lycopodium denudatum, commonly known as the naked clubmoss, is a small, evergreen plant belonging to the family Lycopodiales.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems that are often covered with small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces spore-bearing cones, which are typically clustered at the tips of its branches. Lycopodium denudatum is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
