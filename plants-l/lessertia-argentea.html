Lessertia argentea

Lessertia argentea, commonly known as silver leaf lessertia, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a velvety texture.

Lessertia argentea is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
