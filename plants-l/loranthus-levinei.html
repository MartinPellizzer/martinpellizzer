Loranthus levinei

Loranthus levinei, commonly known as the Mexican mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of host trees, primarily in the region of Mexico.

It lacks true leaves and instead absorbs water and nutrients through its haustoria, which penetrate the host's vascular system. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers that are often inconspicuous, followed by berries that contain the seeds. It plays a role in the ecosystem by providing food for birds and other animals that disperse its seeds.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can cause stress to its host trees if left unchecked.
