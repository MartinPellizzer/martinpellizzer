Lycoris aurea var. angustitepala

Lycoris aurea var. angustitepala, commonly known as the narrow-leaf golden lily, is a striking perennial plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its elegant, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features narrow, strap-like leaves that are a deep green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant orange and red blossoms. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and its ability to naturalize in suitable climates.
