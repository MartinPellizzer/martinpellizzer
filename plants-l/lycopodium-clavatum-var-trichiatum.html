Lycopodium clavatum var. trichiatum

Lycopodium clavatum var. trichiatum, commonly known as the clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its creeping, branched stems that bear small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces cone-like structures called strobila, which contain the spores for reproduction. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky soils.

Its ancient lineage dates back to the Carboniferous period, making it a primitive vascular plant with unique botanical features.
