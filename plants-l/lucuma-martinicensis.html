Lucuma martinicensis

Lucuma martinicensis, commonly known as the Martinique lucuma, is a species of flowering plant native to the Caribbean island of Martinique.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant produces yellow-orange fruits that are rich in nutrients and have a sweet, nutty flavor. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in racemes, and it thrives in tropical climates with well-drained soil.

The fruit is often used in local cuisine and traditional medicine.
