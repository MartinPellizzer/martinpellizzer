Ludwigia clavellina var. grandiflora

Ludwigia clavellina var. grandiflora, commonly known as the grandiflora Ludwigia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in moist, tropical environments, often near water bodies. Its foliage is usually dark green with a slightly waxy texture, and it can be cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens or water features.

The species is valued for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
