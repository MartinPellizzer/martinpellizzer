Lobivia rigidispina

Lobivia rigidispina, commonly known as the rigid-spined lobivia, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny, ribbed stems that store water. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, adapting to extreme drought conditions. Its flowers, which are usually yellow or orange, bloom in late spring or early summer.

Lobivia rigidispina is a popular choice for rock gardens due to its hardy nature and unique appearance.
