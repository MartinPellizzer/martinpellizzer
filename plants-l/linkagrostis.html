Linkagrostis

Linkagrostis, commonly known as hairgrass, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves. The flowers are typically small and clustered in spikelets, which are often adorned with hairy structures. Linkagrostis species are native to various regions across the Americas and are often found in moist or wetland habitats.

These plants play a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
