Lupinus cusickii

Lupinus cusickii, commonly known as Cusick's lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and clusters of showy blue to purple flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Lupinus cusickii plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native environment.
