Lespedeza inschanica

Lespedeza inschanica, commonly known as the Chinese lespedeza, is a perennial flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Lespedeza inschanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

It is also sometimes used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature and adaptability.
