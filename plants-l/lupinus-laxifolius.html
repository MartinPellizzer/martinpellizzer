Lupinus laxifolius

Lupinus laxifolius, commonly known as the slenderleaf lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves that are often hairy, and it thrives in well-drained soils in open, sunny habitats.

Lupinus laxifolius is also notable for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria, making it an important plant in ecological restoration projects.
