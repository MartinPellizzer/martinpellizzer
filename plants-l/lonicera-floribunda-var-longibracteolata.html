Lonicera floribunda var. longibracteolata

Lonicera floribunda var. longibracteolata, commonly known as the long-bract honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, simple, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy on the underside. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers in clusters, typically white or yellow, which bloom in early summer. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that ripens in late summer, attracting various wildlife.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to different soil types.
