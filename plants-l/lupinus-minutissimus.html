Lupinus minutissimus

Lupinus minutissimus, commonly known as the dwarf lupine, is a small, flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Lupinus minutissimus is an important plant for pollinators and is also used in ecological restoration projects.
