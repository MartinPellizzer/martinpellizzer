Lolium temulentum var. speciosum

Lolium temulentum var. speciosum, commonly known as the purple false brome, is a perennial grass species native to Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.
