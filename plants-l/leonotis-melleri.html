Leonotis melleri

Leonotis melleri, commonly known as the African heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking, tubular, lilac-pink flowers arranged in whorls along the stems. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a fuzzy texture.

Leonotis melleri is known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies due to its nectar-rich flowers.
