Lobelia tortuosa var. intermedia

Lobelia tortuosa var. intermedia, commonly known as the zigzag lobe, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features whorled leaves and produces clusters of tubular, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its stems are often twisted or zigzagging, giving it its common name.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
