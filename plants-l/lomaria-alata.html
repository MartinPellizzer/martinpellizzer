Lomaria alata

Lomaria alata, commonly known as the winged lomaria, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has distinctive, wing-like structures along its stems, which give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or coastal areas.

Lomaria alata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
