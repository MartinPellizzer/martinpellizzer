Leucanthemum decipiens

Leucanthemum decipiens, commonly known as the false sunflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy yellow flowers that resemble those of the sunflower. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its flowering period occurs in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is often found in open woodlands and meadows, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
