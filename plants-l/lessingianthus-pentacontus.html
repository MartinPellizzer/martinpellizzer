Lessingianthus pentacontus

Lessingianthus pentacontus, commonly known as the five-hundred-leaved sunflower, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flower heads that are surrounded by a dense layer of pale green to white ray florets. The plant produces a unique structure called a pappus, which aids in wind dispersal of its seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its common name reflects the remarkable number of leaves it can develop under optimal conditions.
