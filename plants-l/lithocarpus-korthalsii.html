Lithocarpus korthalsii

Lithocarpus korthalsii, commonly known as the Korthals's ironwood, is a member of the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks and chestnuts.

It is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in the Philippines and Borneo. The plant is characterized by its hard, dense wood and glossy, leathery leaves that remain throughout the year. Its fruit is a small, woody nut enclosed in a thick, persistent cupule.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
