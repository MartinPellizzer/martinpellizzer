Luzula rostrata subsp. darjeelingensis

Luzula rostrata subsp.

darjeelingensis, commonly known as the Darjeeling sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Himalayan regions of India. It belongs to the Poaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountainous forests and alpine meadows. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are usually greenish or reddish in color.

The species is notable for its ecological significance in maintaining soil stability in high-altitude ecosystems.
