Lepanthes migueliana

Lepanthes migueliana, commonly known as the Miguel's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surfaces of trees without being parasitic. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Lepanthes migueliana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
