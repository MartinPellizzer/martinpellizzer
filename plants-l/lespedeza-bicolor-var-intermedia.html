Lespedeza bicolor var. intermedia

Lespedeza bicolor var. intermedia, commonly known as purpletop vervain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking purple and white inflorescences that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves with hairy surfaces, and it produces small, three-lobed seed pods. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist to mesic soils.

Lespedeza bicolor var. intermedia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
