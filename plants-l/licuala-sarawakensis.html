Licuala sarawakensis

Licuala sarawakensis, commonly known as the Sarawak palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the rainforests of Borneo, particularly in the state of Sarawak. The plant is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach up to three meters in diameter. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill forest areas.

The fruit of Licuala sarawakensis is a small, dark purple drupe that contains a single seed.
