Lampranthus coccineus

Lampranthus coccineus, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a striking succulent native to South Africa.

It features showy, tubular red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like birds and insects. The plant has fleshy, upright stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped and covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its drought tolerance and vibrant blooms, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
