Leucas mollissima var. angustifolia

Leucas mollissima var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved leucas, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stem. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers clustered in terminal spikes, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands or rocky areas.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental flowers make it a valued plant in both horticulture and traditional medicine.
