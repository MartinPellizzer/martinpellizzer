Liparis serratiloba

Liparis serratiloba, commonly known as the serrated-lobed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright pseudobulbs and linear leaves that are often serrated at the edges. The plant produces delicate, greenish-white flowers with intricate patterns, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Liparis serratiloba is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
