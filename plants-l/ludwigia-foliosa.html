Ludwigia foliosa

Ludwigia foliosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Ludwigia foliosa is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies.

It is known for its ability to thrive in moist environments and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
