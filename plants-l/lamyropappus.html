Lamyropappus

Lamyropappus, commonly known as the goldenweed, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its erect, often hairy stems and small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in composite flower heads. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and may have a rough texture. Lamyropappus species are native to North America and are often found in dry, rocky habitats.

These plants are known for their medicinal properties and have been used traditionally for their anti-inflammatory and antiseptic qualities.
