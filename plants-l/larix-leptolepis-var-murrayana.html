Larix leptolepis var. murrayana

Larix leptolepis var. murrayana, commonly known as the Murray's Japanese larch, is a variant of the Japanese larch native to Japan.

It is a deciduous conifer characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and needle-like leaves that turn golden in autumn. The cones are small and woody, typically maturing within two years. This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and seasonal color changes.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes in temperate climates.
