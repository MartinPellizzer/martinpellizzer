Lebeckia candolleana

Lebeckia candolleana, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry or physalis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that resemble dandelions. The plant produces small, round, red berries enclosed in a papery husk, which are edible and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
