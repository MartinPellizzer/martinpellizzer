Lappago racemosa

Lappago racemosa, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of blue or purple. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate appearance and long blooming period.
