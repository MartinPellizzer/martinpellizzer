Lophatherum gracile

Lophatherum gracile, commonly known as the slender lemongrass, is a perennial herb native to Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers arranged in dense inflorescences. It is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves, which are used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.

Lophatherum gracile thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks.
