Logania bracteolata

Logania bracteolata, commonly known as the velvetleaf logania, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the family Loganiaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
