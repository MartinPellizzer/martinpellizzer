Lepidium graminifolium var. iberideum

Lepidium graminifolium var. iberideum, commonly known as Iberian cress, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cross-shaped flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and early bloom.
