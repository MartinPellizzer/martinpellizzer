Lagochilus paulsenii

Lagochilus paulsenii, commonly known as the Paulsen's rock rose, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
