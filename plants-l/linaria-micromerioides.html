Linaria micromerioides

Linaria micromerioides, commonly known as the dwarf toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender, branched stems and small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are typically less than 1 inch in length. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Linaria micromerioides is valued for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
