Licuala adscendens

Licuala adscendens, commonly known as the giant water fern, is a striking member of the Araceae family native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, pinnately compound leaves that can grow up to several meters in length, often with a waxy, dark green surface. The plant thrives in wet, tropical environments, typically near water bodies, and is adapted to high humidity and partial shade. Its unique structure allows it to float on water surfaces, contributing to its ecological role in aquatic ecosystems.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
