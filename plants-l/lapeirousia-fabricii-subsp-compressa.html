Lapeirousia fabricii subsp. compressa

Lapeirousia fabricii subsp.

compressa, commonly known as the Orange River dandelion, is a perennial herb native to South Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow dandelion-like flowers.

The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along riverbanks and rocky outcrops. Its leaves are basal, rosette-forming, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
