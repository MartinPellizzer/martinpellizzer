Limonia madagascariensis

Limonia madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate, with a rough texture.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.
