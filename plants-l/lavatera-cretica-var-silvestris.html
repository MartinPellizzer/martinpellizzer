Lavatera cretica var. silvestris

Lavatera cretica var. silvestris, commonly known as the wild lavatera, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fluffy, pink to white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has silvery-gray leaves with a soft, fuzzy texture, which helps reduce water loss in arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice for both gardeners and botanists.
