Lucuma venosa

Lucuma venosa, commonly known as the "sweet potato tree," is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and tuberous roots. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that develop into round, yellow-orange fruits. Its tubers, which are rich in carbohydrates and nutrients, are traditionally used in local cuisines.

The botanical features of Lucuma venosa make it both an ornamental and edible plant with cultural significance in its native regions.
