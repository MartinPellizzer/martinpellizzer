Lupinaster albens

Lupinaster albens, commonly known as the silver lupin, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. Lupinaster albens thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and nitrogen-fixing capabilities, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
