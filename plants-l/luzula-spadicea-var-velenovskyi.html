Luzula spadicea var. velenovskyi

Luzula spadicea var. velenovskyi, commonly known as the Velenovsky's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or alpine habitats. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to yellowish flowers. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in rocky or gravelly substrates.

The varietal form is distinguished by specific morphological traits that set it apart from other Luzula species.
