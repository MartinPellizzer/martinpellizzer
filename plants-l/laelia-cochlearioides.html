Laelia cochlearioides

Laelia cochlearioides, commonly known as the cochlear orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in humid, shaded environments at elevations ranging from 2,000 to 4,000 meters. The plant features a pseudobulb with a single leaf and produces a spike of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. Its flowers have a unique shape, resembling a snail shell, which gives it its specific epithet "cochlearioides.".

Laelia cochlearioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
