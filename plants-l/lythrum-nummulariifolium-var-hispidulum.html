Lythrum nummulariifolium var. hispidulum

Lythrum nummulariifolium var. hispidulum, commonly known as the hairy lythrum, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in spikes, typically during the summer months. The leaves are round or nearly round, giving the plant a distinctive appearance, and they are often covered in fine, hairy textures. This species thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
