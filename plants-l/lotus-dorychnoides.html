Lotus dorychnoides

Lotus dorychnoides, commonly known as the Indian lotus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking pink or white flowers that bloom on tall, slender stems. The plant has pinnately compound leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in wet, marshy environments and is often found near water bodies such as ponds and rivers.

The lotus is also known for its symbolic significance in various cultural and religious contexts.
