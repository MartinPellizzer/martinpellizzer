Lagetta malabarica

Lagetta malabarica, commonly known as the Malabar thornapple, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its spiny stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a fleshy, yellow berry that turns red when mature, containing several seeds.

Lagetta malabarica is often found in dry, rocky areas and is adapted to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
