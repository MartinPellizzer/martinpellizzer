Lyonia Apiculata

Lyonia apiculata is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern California. It thrives in sandy soils and is often found in dunes and scrub habitats. Known for its small, pinkish-white flowers, it blooms in late spring and early summer. This plant plays a vital role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining ecological balance. Due to habitat loss, it is now classified as a threatened species requiring conservation efforts.
