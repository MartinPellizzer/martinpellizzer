Libocedrus macrolepis var. formosana

Libocedrus macrolepis var. formosana, commonly known as the Formosan lacebark, is a member of the Cupressaceae family and is native to Taiwan.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in mountainous regions with high humidity. The plant is characterized by its exfoliating bark, which reveals a smooth, reddish inner layer, and its scale-like leaves that are densely packed on the shoots. It produces small, inconspicuous cones and is valued for its ornamental bark and ecological importance in its native habitat.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in acidic soils and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
