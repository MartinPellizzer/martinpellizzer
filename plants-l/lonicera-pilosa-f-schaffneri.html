Lonicera pilosa f. schaffneri

Lonicera pilosa f. schaffneri, commonly known as the Schaffner's honeysuckle, is a shrub native to China.

It features slender, woody stems covered in dense, pale yellow to white pubescence. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, blooming in late spring. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual interest and fragrance.
