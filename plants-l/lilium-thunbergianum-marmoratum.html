Lilium thunbergianum marmoratum

Lilium thunbergianum marmoratum, commonly known as the marbled lily, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear large, showy flowers with intricate, marbled patterns on the petals. The leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a loose spiral, contributing to the plant's elegant appearance. This species thrives in well-drained, loamy soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
