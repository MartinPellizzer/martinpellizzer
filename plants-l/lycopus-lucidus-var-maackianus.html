Lycopus lucidus var. maackianus

Lycopus lucidus var. maackianus, commonly known as Maack's blueweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland edges and stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, blue to purple flowers in clusters. Its flowering period occurs in late summer, and it is often found in regions with temperate climates.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been introduced to other parts of the world for horticultural purposes.
