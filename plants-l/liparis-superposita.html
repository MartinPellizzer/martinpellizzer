Liparis superposita

Liparis superposita, commonly known as the clustered orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, delicate flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. The plant has a rhizomatous root system and thrives in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors. Its flowers are usually arranged in dense clusters, giving it its common name.

Liparis superposita is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with appropriate humidity and light conditions.
