Lobelia nana

Lobelia nana, commonly known as dwarf lobelia, is a small herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of only a few inches, featuring compact foliage and spikes of blue or purple flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments, often found in woodland areas or along stream banks. Its small, tubular flowers are adapted for pollination by bees and other small insects.

Lobelia nana is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
