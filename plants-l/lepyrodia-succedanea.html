Lepyrodia succedanea

Lepyrodia succedanea, commonly known as the desert daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form with small, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is well adapted to dry conditions, with a deep root system that allows it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Lepyrodia succedanea is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its ornamental value and low water requirements.
