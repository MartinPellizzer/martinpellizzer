Linum indehiscens proles brevimulticaulia

Linum indehiscens proles brevimulticaulia, commonly known as the short-stemmed flax, is a flowering plant belonging to the Linaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces capsules that remain closed at maturity, a trait known as indehiscence. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

This species is native to parts of Europe and Asia and is often grown for its ornamental value and historical use in textile production.
