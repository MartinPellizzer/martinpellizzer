Lupinus aridus

Lupinus aridus, commonly known as the desert lupine, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, spindly growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces vibrant blue or purple flowers in spikes, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Lupinus aridus has adapted to harsh environments through deep root systems and the ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important component of desert ecosystems.
