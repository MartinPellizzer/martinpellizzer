Lepanthes victoria

Lepanthes victoria, commonly known as the Victoria's slipper orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that resemble small slippers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in the understory of tropical forests. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to orange with intricate patterns, and it produces small, greenish fruits that contain seeds.

Lepanthes victoria is appreciated for its ornamental value and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts worldwide.
