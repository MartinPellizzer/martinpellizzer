Linaria brunneri

Linaria brunneri, commonly known as the Brunner's toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and bright yellow or orange flowers that resemble those of the common toadflax. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Linaria brunneri is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging growing conditions.
