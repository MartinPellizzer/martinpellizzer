Lonicera mochidzukiana

Lonicera mochidzukiana, commonly known as the Japanese honeysuckle, is a deciduous climbing vine native to East Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to twine around supports, making it both ornamental and invasive in some regions. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological interactions.

The fruit is a red, fleshy capsule that contains several seeds.
