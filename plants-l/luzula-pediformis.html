Luzula pediformis

Luzula pediformis, commonly known as the pedicellate sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, commonly referred to as the grass family, and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks, where it thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowering stems bear small, spikelet-like clusters of flowers, which are usually greenish or brownish in color.

Luzula pediformis is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and its ability to form dense, low-growing mats in suitable conditions.
