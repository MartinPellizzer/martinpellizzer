Lobelia shaferi

Lobelia shaferi, commonly known as Shafer's lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, tubular blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Lobelia shaferi is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
