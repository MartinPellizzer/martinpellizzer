Lauro-cerasus vulgaris longifolia

Lauro-cerasus vulgaris longifolia, commonly known as the sweet cherry or Prunus avium, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves, which are typically 5 to 10 centimeters long and have a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers in early spring, which are followed by sweet, juicy red or yellow fruits. Its botanical features include a woody stem with thornless branches and a root system that supports its growth in temperate climates.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its edible fruit, which is widely cultivated for both culinary and commercial purposes.
