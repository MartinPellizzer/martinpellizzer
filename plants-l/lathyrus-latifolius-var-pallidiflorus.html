Lathyrus latifolius var. pallidiflorus

Lathyrus latifolius var. pallidiflorus, commonly known as the pale-flowered broadleaf pea, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of pale pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and thrives in well-drained soils. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

The varietal form pallidiflorus is distinguished by its lighter flower color compared to the typical variety.
