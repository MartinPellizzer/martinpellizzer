Lobivia steinmannii var. costata

Lobivia steinmannii var. costata, commonly known as the Costate Lobivia, is a succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded growth habit and fleshy, ribbed stems that store water. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a subtle ornamental touch. Its leaves are typically thick and cylindrical, adapted to reduce water loss in harsh environments.

This variety is prized for its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
