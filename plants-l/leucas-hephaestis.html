Leucas hephaestis

Leucas hephaestis, commonly known as the flame bush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, bright yellow to orange inflorescences. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with hairy, aromatic leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
