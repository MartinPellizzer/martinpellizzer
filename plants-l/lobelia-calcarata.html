Lobelia calcarata

Lobelia calcarata, commonly known as the rock lobelia, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in woodland or open areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching.

Lobelia calcarata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging environments.
