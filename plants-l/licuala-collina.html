Licuala collina

Licuala collina, commonly known as the paddle palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach several meters in diameter. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Licuala collina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
