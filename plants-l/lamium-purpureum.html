Lamium purpureum

Lamium purpureum, commonly known as purple deadnettle, is a low-growing herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It features opposite, oval-shaped leaves with a distinctive purple underside and is often found in disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stems. It is a perennial species that spreads through rhizomes and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.

The plant's ability to thrive in various soil types makes it a common sight in temperate regions.
