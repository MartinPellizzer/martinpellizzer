Lycaste × niesseniae

Lycaste × niesseniae, commonly known as the Niessen's lycaste, is a hybrid orchid species resulting from the cross between Lycaste meles and Lycaste terebinthus.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in warm, humid climates. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy, often with a waxy texture. Its flowers are showy, with a complex structure consisting of multiple petals and sepals, typically in shades of white and pink.

Lycaste × niesseniae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and resilience.
