Lorandersonia microcephala

Lorandersonia microcephala, commonly known as the dwarf lorandersonia, is a rare and small evergreen shrub native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with small, glossy, dark green leaves that are typically clustered at the base of the plant. The plant produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, which bloom in clusters during the warmer months. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss and limited natural propagation, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
