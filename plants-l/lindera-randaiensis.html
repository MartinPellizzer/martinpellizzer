Lindera randaiensis

Lindera randaiensis, commonly known as the Japanese spicebush, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, oval-shaped leaves and small yellowish-green flowers that appear in the spring. Its fruit is a dark purple drupe that contains a single seed.

Lindera randaiensis is often found in moist, shaded woodlands and is valued for its ornamental and aromatic qualities.
