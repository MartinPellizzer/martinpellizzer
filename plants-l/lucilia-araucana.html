Lucilia araucana

Lucilia araucana, commonly known as the Araucaria nut, is a species of coniferous tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Araucariaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, feather-like leaves and cone-like structures. The plant is adapted to a variety of environmental conditions, including high altitudes and nutrient-poor soils. Its cones produce edible seeds that are an important food source for various wildlife species.

The tree is also valued for its timber and has cultural significance in some regions.
