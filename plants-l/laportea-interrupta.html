Laportea interrupta

Laportea interrupta, commonly known as the white wood aster, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features a basal rosette of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its hairy foliage and showy flowers make it a distinctive and ecologically valuable plant in its habitat.
