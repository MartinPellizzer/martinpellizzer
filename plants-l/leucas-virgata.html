Leucas virgata

Leucas virgata, commonly known as the blue leucas or blue wild indigo, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic and medicinal plants. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and clusters of small, blue or purple flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and woodland margins.

Leucas virgata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
