Lavandula pedunculata subsp. sampaioana

Lavandula pedunculata subsp.

sampaioana, commonly known as the Sampaio's lavender, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves.

This subspecies typically grows in coastal areas and is known for its fragrant, purple-blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its fragrance and visual beauty.
