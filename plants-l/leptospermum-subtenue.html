Leptospermum subtenue

Leptospermum subtenue, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tea tree, is a small evergreen shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and small, white to pinkish flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.

Leptospermum subtenue is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate coastal conditions.
