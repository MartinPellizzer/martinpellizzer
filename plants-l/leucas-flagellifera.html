Leucas flagellifera

Leucas flagellifera, commonly known as the slender leucas, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically pale blue or violet, arranged in whorled clusters, and bloom during the warmer months.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
