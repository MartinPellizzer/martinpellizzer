Lycopodioides poulteri

Lycopodioides poulteri, commonly known as Poulter's clubmoss, is a species of lycophyte belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant has a creeping rhizome with erect, needle-like leaves that are spirally arranged around the stem. It produces spores in small, clustered structures called strobila, which are located at the tips of the branches.

Lycopodioides poulteri is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in maintaining ecological balance in its native habitat.
