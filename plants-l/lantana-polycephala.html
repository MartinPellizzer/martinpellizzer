Lantana polycephala

Lantana polycephala, commonly known as the clustered lantana, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 4 meters in height, featuring opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, which attract a variety of pollinators. It is known for its adaptability to different soil types and its ability to thrive in full sun.

Lantana polycephala is also noted for its use in ornamental gardening and traditional medicine.
