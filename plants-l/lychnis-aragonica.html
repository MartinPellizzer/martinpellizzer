Lychnis aragonica

Lychnis aragonica, commonly known as the Aragon carnation, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features erect stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its historical use in traditional medicine and its ornamental value contribute to its significance in horticulture.
