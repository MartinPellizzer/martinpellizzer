Lathyrus pannonicus subsp. albanicus

Lathyrus pannonicus subsp.

albanicus, commonly known as the Albanian vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Balkan Peninsula. It typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

The plant features slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pink to purple. Its trifoliate leaves are compound, with three oval-shaped leaflets.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its resilience and aesthetic qualities.
