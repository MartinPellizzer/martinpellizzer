Lilium philadelphicum f. montanum

Lilium philadelphicum f. montanum, commonly known as the mountain lily, is a botanical variant of the showy lady's slipper.

It is characterized by its large, showy white to pink flowers that often have a red throat, making it visually striking. This subspecies is typically found in alpine and subalpine regions, growing in rocky or open habitats. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, and it prefers well-drained soils.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
