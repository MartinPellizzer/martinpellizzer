Labordia decurrens

Labordia decurrens, commonly known as the creeping labordia, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish or reddish in color. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant in dry gardens.
