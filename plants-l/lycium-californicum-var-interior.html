Lycium californicum var. interior

Lycium californicum var. interior, commonly known as the interior prickly lily, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its spiny stems and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is well adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are small, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its fruits are red berries that mature in late summer.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
