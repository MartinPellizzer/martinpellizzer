Liparis minima var. neoguineensis

Liparis minima var. neoguineensis, commonly known as the dwarf orchid, is a small, terrestrial orchid native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features delicate, grass-like leaves and produces tiny, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its simple, sympodial growth habit.

Due to its small size and specific habitat requirements, it is often challenging to cultivate outside its native range.
