Linnaea borealis f. facifera

Linnaea borealis f. facifera, commonly known as the northern twinflower, is a rare and distinctive variant of the twinflower species.

It is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the boreal forests of North America and northern Europe. The plant features small, fragrant, white, bilaterally symmetrical flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a smooth texture and a dark green color.

This form is characterized by its unique floral structure and is often found in moist, shaded environments.
