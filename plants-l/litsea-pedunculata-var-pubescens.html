Litsea pedunculata var. pubescens

Litsea pedunculata var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy litsea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are covered in fine, pubescent hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spikes, typically appearing in the spring. Its fruit is a small, spherical drupe that turns dark red when mature.

This species is native to Southeast Asia and is often found in moist, shaded forest environments.
