Lissochilus seretii

Lissochilus seretii, commonly known as the Seretii's slipper orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America, particularly found in the Andean regions of Peru and Bolivia.

It is characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped pouch that serves as a trap for pollinators. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in cool and humid conditions. Its flowers are usually small and come in shades of white, green, and yellow, adding to its ornamental value.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species, making conservation efforts essential for its survival.
