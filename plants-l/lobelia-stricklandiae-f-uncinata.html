Lobelia stricklandiae f. uncinata

Lobelia stricklandiae f. uncinata, commonly known as the Hooker's lobelia, is a rare and unique variant of the lobelia genus native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. This fern-like plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats. Its inflorescence consists of dense clusters of small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a conservation concern.
