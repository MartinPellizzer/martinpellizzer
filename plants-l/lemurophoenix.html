Lemurophoenix

Lemurophoenix, commonly known as the Madagascar palm, is a unique succulent native to the island of Madagascar.

It features a thick, fleshy stem that resembles a tree trunk, with clusters of spiny leaves emerging from the top. The plant is characterized by its ability to store water in its stem, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange and appear in clusters at the top of the plant.

Lemurophoenix is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its distinctive appearance and low maintenance requirements.
