Lycium berberidoides

Lycium berberidoides, commonly known as the redcurrant gooseberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, bright red berries that are typically harvested in the fall. The plant has yellowish-green leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it produces yellow flowers in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in arid regions.

The berries are edible and have a tart flavor, often used in jams and preserves.
