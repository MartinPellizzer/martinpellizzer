Lolium macilentum

Lolium macilentum, commonly known as slender ryegrass, is a cool-season grass native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, slender leaves and compact growth habit, making it a common weed in agricultural fields and lawns. The plant has a fibrous root system and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas.

Lolium macilentum is valued for its rapid establishment and is sometimes used in forage production.
