Lepisanthes perrieri

Lepisanthes perrieri, commonly known as the Perrier's orchid, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish-white, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insects.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat threats, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
