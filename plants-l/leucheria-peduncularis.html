Leucheria peduncularis

Leucheria peduncularis, commonly known as the redstem sedges, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and triangular leaves, which are typically green with a reddish tint on the underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense clusters at the ends of its stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.
