Lophoschoenus arundinaceus

Lophoschoenus arundinaceus, commonly known as the reed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist to waterlogged soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are often green or reddish in color. It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, which allows it to spread and form dense stands.

Lophoschoenus arundinaceus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
