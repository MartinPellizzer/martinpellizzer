Leontice leontopetalum subsp. minor

Leontice leontopetalum subsp.

minor, commonly known as the lesser leopard's bane, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is a low-growing perennial herb with distinctive, palmately lobed leaves that are often hairy.

The plant produces small, showy white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and herbal practices.
