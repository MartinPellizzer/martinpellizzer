Litsea glutinosa var. brachyphylla

Litsea glutinosa var. brachyphylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved litsea, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, opposite, and lanceolate leaves that are often glandular-dotted. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles, followed by oval, orange-red drupes. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in montane forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
