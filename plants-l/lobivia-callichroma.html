Lobivia callichroma

Lobivia callichroma, commonly known as the Orange Ghost Cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are often tinged with a striking orange hue, especially under sunlight. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a vibrant touch to its desert habitat. It has a shallow root system adapted to absorb moisture from the dry soil.

Lobivia callichroma is drought-tolerant and requires minimal care, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
