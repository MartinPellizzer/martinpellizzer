Liabum solidagineum var. eggersii

Liabum solidagineum var. eggersii, commonly known as the Eggers' goldenweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has slender, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. Its botanical features include small, tubular flowers arranged in clusters, and it produces small, dry seeds in its fruit.

This variety is noted for its adaptability to arid environments and its role in supporting local pollinators.
