Lepanthes ephalmatrix

Lepanthes ephalmatrix, commonly known as the silver-stemmed orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-green stems and delicate, often fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals.

Lepanthes ephalmatrix is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or warm, humid climates.
