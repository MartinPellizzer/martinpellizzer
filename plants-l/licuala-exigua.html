Licuala exigua

Licuala exigua, commonly known as the paddle palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach up to 2 meters in diameter. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by dark blue, fleshy berries.

Licuala exigua is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
