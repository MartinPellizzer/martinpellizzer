Lilium peregrinum

Lilium peregrinum, commonly known as the wandering lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and large, showy white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or alpine environments. It is a member of the Liliaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
