Lycopodium rimbachii

Lycopodium rimbachii, commonly known as the Rimbach's clubmoss, is a small, evergreen plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and the presence of spirally arranged leaves. The plant produces spores in cone-like structures called strobila, which are typically located at the tips of its stems. Lycopodium rimbachii is native to tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for studies on ancient plant lineages and ecological adaptations.
