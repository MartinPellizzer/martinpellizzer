Lepidium montanum var. claronensis

Lepidium montanum var. claronensis, commonly known as the Claron Mountain cress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and alpine environments, exhibiting a low, mat-forming growth habit. Its leaves are pinnately divided, and it produces small, yellowish-white flowers throughout the summer months.

This variety is noted for its ecological importance in stabilizing soil in high-altitude habitats.
