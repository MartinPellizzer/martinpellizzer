Lysiana maritima

Lysiana maritima, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its silvery-blue foliage and striking blue or purple flower heads. The plant thrives in saline and well-drained soils, often found in dune environments. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Lysiana maritima is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing coastal habitats.
