Lepanthes chrysostigma

Lepanthes chrysostigma, commonly known as the golden-stained orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central and South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid with a short rhizome and a single, erect stem that bears a few broad, elliptic leaves. The plant produces a single, showy flower with a golden-yellow lip and white petals, which is highly ornamental. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its beauty and rarity, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
