Lupinus hillii var. osterhoutianus

Lupinus hillii var. osterhoutianus, commonly known as the Osterhout's lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bright blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with several oval-shaped leaflets, and its root system includes nitrogen-fixing nodules. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open grasslands and rocky areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
