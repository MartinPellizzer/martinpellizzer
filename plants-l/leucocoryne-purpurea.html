Leucocoryne purpurea

Leucocoryne purpurea, commonly known as the purple leucocoryne, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant features a bulbous stem and produces showy, clustered blooms during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
