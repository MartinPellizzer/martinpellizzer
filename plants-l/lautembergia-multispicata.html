Lautembergia multispicata

Lautembergia multispicata, commonly known as the golden gooseberry, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in dry, sunny environments, often found in the Andes region. The plant produces small, yellow-orange berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Lautembergia multispicata is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its use in traditional medicine.
