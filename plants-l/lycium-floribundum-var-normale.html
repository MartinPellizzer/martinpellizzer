Lycium floribundum var. normale

Lycium floribundum var. normale, commonly known as the common wolfberry, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible. The plant has woody stems with thorn-like projections, and its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

The berries are rich in nutrients and have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
