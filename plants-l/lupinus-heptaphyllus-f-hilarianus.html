Lupinus heptaphyllus f. hilarianus

Lupinus heptaphyllus f. hilarianus, commonly known as the Hilarian lupine, is a variant of the lupine species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
