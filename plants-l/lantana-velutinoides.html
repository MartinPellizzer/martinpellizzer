Lantana velutinoides

Lantana velutinoides, commonly known as velvet lantana, is a shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to yellow, pink, and red, depending on the variety. The leaves are typically covered in a fine, velvety hair, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in coastal and dry habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators.
