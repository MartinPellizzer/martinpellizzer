Lychnis laciniata var. japonica

Lychnis laciniata var. japonica, commonly known as Japanese leafy catchfly, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-red flowers and deeply lobed, fern-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It belongs to the carnation family (Caryophyllaceae) and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in partial shade and well-drained soil.
