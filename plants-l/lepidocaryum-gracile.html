Lepidocaryum gracile

Lepidocaryum gracile, commonly known as the slender lepidocaryum, is a small, herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Lepidocaryaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces tiny, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in small inflorescences. Lepidocaryum gracile thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and role in its native ecosystems.
