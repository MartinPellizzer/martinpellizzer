Linaria depauperata

Linaria depauperata, commonly known as the poorflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and delicate, yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, lance-shaped leaves are usually covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Linaria depauperata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
