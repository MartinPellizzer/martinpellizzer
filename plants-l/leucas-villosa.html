Leucas villosa

Leucas villosa, commonly known as hairy leucas, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, as well as its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used in traditional herbal remedies for various ailments.
