Linkia revoluta

Linkia revoluta, commonly known as the red hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety red petals and yellow stamens, which make it a popular ornamental plant. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly serrated margin.

Linkia revoluta thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
