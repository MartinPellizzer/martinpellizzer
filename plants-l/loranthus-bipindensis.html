Loranthus bipindensis

Loranthus bipindensis, commonly known as the African mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of host trees, primarily in the genus Terminalia.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and minerals, while producing its own carbohydrates through photosynthesis using sunlight. The plant produces small, white or yellow flowers that are often visited by insects for pollination. Its berries, which are typically red or orange, contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This species plays an important ecological role in forest ecosystems, contributing to biodiversity and nutrient cycling.
