Lobelia dombeyana

Lobelia dombeyana, commonly known as the Dombey's lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of blue to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, often with a reddish or purple hue.

Lobelia dombeyana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
