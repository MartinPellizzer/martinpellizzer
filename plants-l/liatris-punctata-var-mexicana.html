Liatris punctata var. mexicana

Liatris punctata var. mexicana, commonly known as Mexican blazing star, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features upright, spikelike clusters of bright orange to red tubular flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

This variety is closely related to other members of the Asteraceae family, sharing similar structural and ecological traits.
