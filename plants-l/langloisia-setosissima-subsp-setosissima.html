Langloisia setosissima subsp. setosissima

Langloisia setosissima subsp.

setosissima, commonly known as the hairy langloisia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America. It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and clusters of small, tubular flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough, textured surface, and it produces small, elongated fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
