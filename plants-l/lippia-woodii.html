Lippia woodii

Lippia woodii, commonly known as Mexican blueweed, is a perennial shrub native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and showy blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun, making it well adapted to arid environments. Its essential oils contain compounds like thymol and carvacrol, which contribute to its medicinal and aromatic properties.

Lippia woodii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
