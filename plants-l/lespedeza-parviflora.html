Lespedeza parviflora

Lespedeza parviflora, commonly known as parviflora lespedeza, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, pale pink to white flowers clustered in spherical heads, which bloom in late summer. Its trifoliate leaves are compound, with three oval-shaped leaflets.

Lespedeza parviflora is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
