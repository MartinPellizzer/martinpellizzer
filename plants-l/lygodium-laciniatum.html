Lygodium laciniatum

Lygodium laciniatum, commonly known as the Chinese water fern, is a fern species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feather-like fronds that give it a delicate, lacy appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, and it reproduces through spore dispersal rather than seeds.

This species is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
