Lactuca setosa

Lactuca setosa, commonly known as prickly lettuce, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, serrated leaves and a distinctive, spiny stem. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Lactuca setosa contains lactucin, a compound with potential medicinal properties.
