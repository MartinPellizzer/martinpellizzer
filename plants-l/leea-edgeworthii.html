Leea edgeworthii

Leea edgeworthii, commonly known as the Indian balsam, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Leeaceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Leea edgeworthii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
