Leontopodium japonicum f. happoense

Leontopodium japonicum f. happoense, commonly known as the Japanese edelweiss, is a rare and striking alpine plant native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly white bracts that surround a cluster of small, yellowish flowers, giving it a distinctive appearance reminiscent of the European edelweiss. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions, often growing in rocky or gravelly soils. Its foliage consists of basal, spoon-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
