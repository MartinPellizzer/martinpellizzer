Lobelia retrorsa

Lobelia retrorsa, commonly known as the backward lobelia, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, branching stems that often grow in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Lobelia retrorsa is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
