Liparis fendleri

Liparis fendleri, commonly known as the Fendler's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves and a single, fragrant flower that typically blooms in late spring. The plant prefers well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with a distinctive lip that is broader than the other petals.

Liparis fendleri is a rare and endangered species, requiring specific environmental conditions for successful growth and reproduction.
