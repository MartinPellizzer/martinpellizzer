Lupinus hyacinthinus

Lupinus hyacinthinus, commonly known as the hyacinth bean, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and clusters of violet or blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has compound leaves composed of several small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its seeds, which are enclosed in pods, are often used as a protein source in some regions.

Lupinus hyacinthinus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
