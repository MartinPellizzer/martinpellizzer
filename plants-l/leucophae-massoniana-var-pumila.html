Leucophae massoniana var. pumila

Leucophae massoniana var. pumila, commonly known as the dwarf Chinese mountain pine, is a compact evergreen shrub or small tree native to China.

It features dense, dark green needles that are short and stiff, giving it a lush, textured appearance. The plant produces small, pale yellow cones that are typically borne in clusters. It is well-suited for rock gardens and bonsai due to its slow growth and ornamental value.

The variety is distinguished by its smaller size compared to the typical species, making it ideal for smaller garden spaces.
