Lasianthus saxorum

Lasianthus saxorum, commonly known as the rock lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking blue or purple flowers. The plant typically thrives in rocky or well-drained soils, making it well-suited for rock gardens or xeriscaping. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often woody and branched.

Lasianthus saxorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in arid conditions with minimal water.
