Leuzea

Leuzea, commonly known as goldenroot, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and includes several species such as Leuzea carthamoides. The plant is characterized by its upright growth, lance-shaped leaves, and yellow-orange composite flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks.

Leuzea is also valued for its medicinal properties, traditionally used to support digestive health and reduce inflammation.
