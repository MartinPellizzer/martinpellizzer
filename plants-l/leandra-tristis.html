Leandra tristis

Leandra tristis, commonly known as the weeping hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its drooping, pendulous inflorescences that bear showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical, with a smooth texture and a glossy appearance.

Leandra tristis thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
