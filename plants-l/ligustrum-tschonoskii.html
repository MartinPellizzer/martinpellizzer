Ligustrum tschonoskii

Ligustrum tschonoskii, commonly known as Chinese privet, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features small, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement and have a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces clusters of white, fragrant flowers in the spring, followed by dark blue berries that are edible for birds. It is often used in landscaping due to its dense growth habit and adaptability to various soil types.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions if not properly managed.
