Lepechiniella persica subsp. persica

Lepechiniella persica subsp.

persica, commonly known as Persian lepechiniella, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of Iran. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, tubular flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to hot, dry climates. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

The subspecies is distinguished by its specific morphology and geographic distribution within the genus.
