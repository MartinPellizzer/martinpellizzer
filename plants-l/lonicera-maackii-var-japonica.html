Lonicera maackii var. japonica

Lonicera maackii var. japonica, commonly known as Japanese honeysuckle, is a deciduous vine native to East Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. Its berries, which ripen in the fall, are orange-red and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This species is often considered an invasive plant in North America due to its competitive nature and ability to spread quickly.
