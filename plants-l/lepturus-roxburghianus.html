Lepturus roxburghianus

Lepturus roxburghianus, commonly known as the Himalayan rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to extreme climatic conditions. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or red, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, helping to reduce water loss in its harsh habitat.

Lepturus roxburghianus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
