Lactuca garrettii

Lactuca garrettii, commonly known as Garrett's lettuce, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family, Asteraceae.

It is native to the western United States, particularly in California and Oregon, where it grows in dry, open habitats. The plant has basal leaves that are typically lobed or toothed, and it produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. It is a herbaceous perennial that can reach heights of up to 30 centimeters.

The species is known for its bitter taste and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
