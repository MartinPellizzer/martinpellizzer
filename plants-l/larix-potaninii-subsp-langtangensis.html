Larix potaninii subsp. langtangensis

Larix potaninii subsp.

langtangensis, commonly known as the Langtang larch, is a subspecies of the larch tree native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal and Tibet. It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure.

The tree typically grows in high-altitude forests, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing protection against harsh mountain conditions.

This subspecies plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its mountainous habitat.
