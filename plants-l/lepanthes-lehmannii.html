Lepanthes lehmannii

Lepanthes lehmannii, commonly known as the Lehmann's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central and South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments within forest understories. The plant features a slender rhizome and produces delicate, often fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green. Its flowers are characterized by their intricate structure, with three sepals and three petals, and a distinctive lip.

Lepanthes lehmannii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
