Laelia dawsonii

Laelia dawsonii, commonly known as Dawson's orchid, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, showy flowers. The plant typically produces pseudobulbs that support its foliage and floral structures. Its flowers are usually brightly colored, with a complex structure that includes a spurred lip, typical of many orchid species.

Laelia dawsonii is appreciated by orchid enthusiasts for its resilience and ornamental value.
