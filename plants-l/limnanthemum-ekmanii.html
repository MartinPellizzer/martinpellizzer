Limnanthemum ekmanii

Limnanthemum ekmanii, commonly known as the velvetleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wet or marshy environments. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple. Its scientific name honors the Swedish botanist Erik Ekman.

Limnanthemum ekmanii is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered invasive in certain regions.
