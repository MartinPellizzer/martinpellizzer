Lauro-cerasus crassifolia

Lauro-cerasus crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved cherry laurel, is a species of flowering plant in the family Prunus.

It is native to the eastern Mediterranean region, including parts of Turkey, Greece, and the Caucasus. The plant is characterized by its dark green, broad, leathery leaves and clusters of small white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a small, dark purple drupe that ripens in summer.

This ornamental shrub is often used in gardens for its attractive foliage and fragrant blossoms.
