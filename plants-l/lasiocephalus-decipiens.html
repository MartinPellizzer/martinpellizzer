Lasiocephalus decipiens

Lasiocephalus decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive head grass, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit. The plant has narrow, bluish-green leaves and produces small, spikelet-like inflorescences. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe habitats.
