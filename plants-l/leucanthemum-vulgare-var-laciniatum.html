Leucanthemum vulgare var. laciniatum

Leucanthemum vulgare var. laciniatum, commonly known as the Shining Leucanthemum, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe.

It features a basal rosette of finely cut, silvery-white leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces daisy-like flowers with white ray florets surrounding a yellow disc, blooming from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
