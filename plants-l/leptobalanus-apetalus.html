Leptobalanus apetalus

Leptobalanus apetalus, commonly known as the petalless fir, is a rare and distinctive conifer native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its unique morphology, featuring scale-like leaves that are tightly packed along the branches. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain the seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species.

Its botanical features make it an important subject for ecological and conservation studies.
