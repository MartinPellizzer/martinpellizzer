Lilium auratum subsp. latifolium

Lilium auratum subsp.

latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white to pale pink.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens. Its bulbous root system allows it to store nutrients, supporting its growth in temperate climates.

The subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its beauty and fragrance.
