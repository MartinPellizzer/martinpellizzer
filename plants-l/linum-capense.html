Linum capense

Linum capense, commonly known as the Cape flax, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the genus Linum, which includes other species of flax. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of blue or purple flowers. Its leaves are alternate, narrow, and often hairy.

Linum capense is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine.
