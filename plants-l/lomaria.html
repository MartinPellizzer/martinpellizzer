Lomaria

Lomaria, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the coastal regions of southern Europe and the Mediterranean. The plant is characterized by its striking blue or purple flower heads, which are surrounded by spiny, leaf-like bracts. Lomaria thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or sandy coastal environments.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and coastal landscapes.
