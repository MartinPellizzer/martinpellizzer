Lycium melanopotamicum

Lycium melanopotamicum, commonly known as the Melanopotamic boxthorn, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible for both humans and animals. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of ecological study.
