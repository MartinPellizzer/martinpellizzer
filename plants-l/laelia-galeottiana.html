Laelia galeottiana

Laelia galeottiana, commonly known as the Galeotti's orchid, is a species native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in high-altitude regions with cool, moist conditions. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish with intricate patterns, and it blooms during the cooler months.

Laelia galeottiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
