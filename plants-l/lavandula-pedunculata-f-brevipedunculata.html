Lavandula pedunculata f. brevipedunculata

Lavandula pedunculata f. brevipedunculata, commonly known as the compact lavender, is a compact, shrubby perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grey-green leaves and produces spikes of fragrant, pale purple flowers. This cultivar is valued for its dense growth habit and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal water once established.

The plant is also noted for its aromatic properties and suitability for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
