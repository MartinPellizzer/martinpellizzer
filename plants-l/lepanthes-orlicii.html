Lepanthes orlicii

Lepanthes orlicii, commonly known as the Orchid of the Andes, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are often white or pale pink, with a distinctive lip that aids in pollination.

Lepanthes orlicii is valued for its ornamental beauty and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its habitat.
