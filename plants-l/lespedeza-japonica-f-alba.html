Lespedeza japonica f. alba

Lespedeza japonica f. alba, commonly known as white Japanese clover, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open woodlands and grassy areas. Its fluffy seed pods release seeds by wind, aiding in its spread.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
