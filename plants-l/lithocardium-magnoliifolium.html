Lithocardium magnoliifolium

Lithocardium magnoliifolium, commonly known as the magnolia-leaved lithocardium, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white to cream-colored flowers that resemble those of magnolia species, hence its common name. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are broad, ovate, and have a glossy surface, contributing to its ornamental value.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.
