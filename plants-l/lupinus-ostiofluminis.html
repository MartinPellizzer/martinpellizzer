Lupinus ostiofluminis

Lupinus ostiofluminis, commonly known as the Rio Negro lupine, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. Lupinus ostiofluminis is adapted to thrive in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal or riverine habitats.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it an important plant for soil enrichment in its native environment.
