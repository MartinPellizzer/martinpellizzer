Lupinus argenteus var. meionanthus

Lupinus argenteus var. meionanthus, commonly known as silver lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and erect, sparsely branched stems. The plant produces clusters of pea-like flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
