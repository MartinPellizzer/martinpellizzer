Loasa pedicularifolia

Loasa pedicularifolia, commonly known as the creeping loasa, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in cloud forests. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, which are usually white or pale pink in color. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Loasa pedicularifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
