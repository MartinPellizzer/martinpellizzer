Linaria tournefortii var. zamorana

Linaria tournefortii var. zamorana, commonly known as the Zamora's toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and bright yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
