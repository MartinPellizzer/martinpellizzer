Lithops pseudotruncatella var. volkii

Lithops pseudotruncatella var. volkii, commonly known as the "living stone," is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, fleshy leaves that resemble stones, helping it blend into its arid environment. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers during the spring or early summer. It requires minimal water and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
