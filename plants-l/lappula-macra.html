Lappula macra

Lappula macra, commonly known as the tall lappula, is a flowering plant native to Eurasia.

It belongs to the family Camelotaceae and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces distinctive, inflated seed pods that split open to release its seeds. Lappula macra typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and wastelands.

It is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
