Ligustrum ciliatum var. spathulatum

Ligustrum ciliatum var. spathulatum, commonly known as the broad-leaf privet, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate to broadly ovate leaves with ciliate margins, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters, followed by dark blue berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along streambanks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a common ornamental and ecological plant in its native habitat.
