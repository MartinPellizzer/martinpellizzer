Leptoglottis microphylla

Leptoglottis microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved flax lily, is a herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or pale yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Leptoglottis microphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native ecosystems.
