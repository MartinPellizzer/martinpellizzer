Lepidium thlaspidioides

Lepidium thlaspidioides, commonly known as the broadleaf wallflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The plant features alternate, pinnately divided leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Native to southern Africa, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun conditions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
