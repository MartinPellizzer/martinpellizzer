Lobelia inflata var. simplex

Lobelia inflata var. simplex, commonly known as Indian tobacco, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its upright, herbaceous stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It is often found in moist, shady habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used by indigenous peoples for various traditional purposes.
