Leandra sessilifolia

Leandra sessilifolia, commonly known as the sessile-leaf leandra, is a member of the Rubiaceae family, native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub characterized by its evergreen foliage and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pale pink. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are sessile, meaning they lack a petiole and attach directly to the stem. Its flowering period usually occurs during the warmer months, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in tropical and subtropical climates.

The plant is also valued for its ability to thrive in shaded environments, contributing to its versatility in landscaping.
