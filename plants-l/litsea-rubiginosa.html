Litsea rubiginosa

Litsea rubiginosa, commonly known as the red-leafed litsea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Vietnam, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, reddish-brown leaves that turn bright red in autumn, making it a striking ornamental plant. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Litsea rubiginosa is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and landscapes due to its vibrant foliage and pleasant aroma.
