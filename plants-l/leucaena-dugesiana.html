Leucaena dugesiana

Leucaena dugesiana, commonly known as the Mexican goldenweed, is a shrub or small tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in tropical and subtropical regions. It has small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Leucaena dugesiana is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.
