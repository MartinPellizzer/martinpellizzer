Lindera novoguineensis

Lindera novoguineensis, commonly known as the New Guinea loropetalum, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of New Guinea.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories, and can reach heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a glossy surface, while its flowers are pink or white, adding ornamental value.

The fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.
