Lobivia auranitida var. gracilis

Lobivia auranitida var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender golden lobivia, is a succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It features compact, cylindrical stems that are typically green to blue-green in color, often adorned with a thin layer of white or pale blue fuzz. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which bloom at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are small, fleshy, and often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-draining soil with minimal water.
