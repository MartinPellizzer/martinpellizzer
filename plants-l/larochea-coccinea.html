Larochea coccinea

Larochea coccinea, commonly known as the red orchid, is a strikingly colorful plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant red and orange petals, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom during the warmer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts worldwide.
