Lonicera velutina

Lonicera velutina, commonly known as the velvet honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are hairy on the underside, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring. Its berries are red and edible, though they are not commonly consumed by humans.

Lonicera velutina is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
