Lepidium strictum var. oreganum

Lepidium strictum var. oreganum, commonly known as Oregon wallflower, is a herbaceous plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, oval-shaped seeds enclosed in a capsule, which is typical of the Brassicaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and rocky areas.

Oregon wallflower is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
