Lophopogon truncatiglumis

Lophopogon truncatiglumis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist to saturated soil conditions. The plant has slender, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 1 meter, with narrow, grass-like leaves that have a distinctive truncate or notched base. Its inflorescence consists of a spikelet cluster, and it produces small, spikelet-like flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Lophopogon truncatiglumis plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems and is sometimes used in wetland restoration projects.
