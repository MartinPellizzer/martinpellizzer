Lucuma melinonii

Lucuma melinonii, commonly known as the Andean sweet chestnut, is a tropical plant native to the highlands of South America.

It belongs to the family Proteaceae and is characterized by its large, leathery leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces distinctive, egg-shaped fruits that encase a single, edible seed. Its growth is adapted to the cool, humid climates of the Andes, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fruit, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts interested in rare plant species.
