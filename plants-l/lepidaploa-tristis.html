Lepidaploa tristis

Lepidaploa tristis, commonly known as the weeping lily, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems that bear clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and unique growth habit, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
