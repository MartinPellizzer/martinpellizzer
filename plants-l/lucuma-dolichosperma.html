Lucuma dolichosperma

Lucuma dolichosperma, commonly known as the golden apple or lucuma, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange fruit, which is rich in nutrients and antioxidants. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions, thriving in cool, temperate climates with well-drained soil. Its fruit develops from a capsule and is harvested when fully mature, often used in culinary applications for its unique flavor and nutritional value.

The plant also has ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and flowers.
