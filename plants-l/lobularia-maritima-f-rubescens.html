Lobularia maritima f. rubescens

Lobularia maritima f. rubescens, commonly known as the red-flowered sweet rocket, is a compact, mat-forming plant native to coastal areas of Europe and western Asia.

It features small, bright pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has aromatic, hairy leaves that are often bluish-green in color, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. It is a low-maintenance plant that is well-suited for rock gardens, borders, or as a ground cover.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant floral display and fragrant foliage.
