Laportea ardens

Laportea ardens, commonly known as flameleaf or fireweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features striking red-tinged, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves contain compounds that can cause skin irritation, making it important to handle with care.

Laportea ardens thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
