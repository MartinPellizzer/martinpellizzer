Linaria pensylvanica

Linaria pensylvanica, commonly known as Pennsylvania toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stems.

Pennsylvania toadflax is characterized by its fibrous root system and its ability to thrive in partial to full shade.
