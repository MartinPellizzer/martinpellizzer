Luzula albida var. alpigena

Luzula albida var. alpigena, commonly known as the alpine wood sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and dense, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, such as alpine meadows and rocky slopes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, and it produces tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Luzula albida var. alpigena is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate harsh climates.
