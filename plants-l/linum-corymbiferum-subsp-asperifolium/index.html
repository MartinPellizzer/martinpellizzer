Linum Corymbiferum Subsp. Asperifolium

Linum corymbiferum subsp. asperifolium is a lesser-known member of the flax family known for its unique physical characteristics. This subspecies is distinguished by its rough, hairy leaves and striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in summer. Native to specific regions of western North America, it thrives in rocky and dry habitats. Its ecological role is significant, supporting pollinators and contributing to local biodiversity. Despite its beauty and resilience, it remains underappreciated and often overlooked in botanical discussions.
