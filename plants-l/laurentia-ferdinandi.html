Laurentia ferdinandi

Laurentia ferdinandi, commonly known as the dwarf laurel, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It features dense, dark green foliage with a slightly waxy texture, and its leaves are typically narrow and pointed. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its hardiness and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in Mediterranean climates.
