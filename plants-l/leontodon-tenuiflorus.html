Leontodon tenuiflorus

Leontodon tenuiflorus, commonly known as the narrow-flowered hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are yellow with ray florets that give it a daisy-like appearance.

It is known for its hardy nature and ability to withstand various environmental conditions.
