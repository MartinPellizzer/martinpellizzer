Lithocarpus nebularum

Lithocarpus nebularum, commonly known as the cloud-leaf stone oak, is a member of the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks, chestnuts, and beeches.

It is a broadleaf evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in the Philippines and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and have a waxy coating. Its fruit is a hard, woody nut enclosed in a cupule, a feature common among members of its family.

This species is valued for its durable wood and is often found in tropical rainforests.
