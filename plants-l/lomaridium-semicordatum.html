Lomaridium semicordatum

Lomaridium semicordatum, commonly known as the heartleaf lomaridium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely commercially available.
