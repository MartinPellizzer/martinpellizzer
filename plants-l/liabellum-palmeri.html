Liabellum palmeri

Liabellum palmeri, commonly known as the Palmer's lily, is a rare and striking plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial with large, showy white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, typically found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered endangered and is of particular interest to botanists and conservationists.
