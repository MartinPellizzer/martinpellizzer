Lindsaea coriacea

Lindsaea coriacea, commonly known as the rough lindsaea, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its wiry, creeping rhizomes and dense, compact foliage. The leaves are usually elliptic to ovate, with a leathery texture and a rough, coriaceous surface, giving the plant its common name. It typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices.

This plant is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it contributes to the understory vegetation.
