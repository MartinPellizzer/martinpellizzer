Leandra corcovadensis

Leandra corcovadensis, commonly known as the corcovado orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are often brightly colored, with a unique structure that facilitates pollination by specific insect species.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered in many regions, making conservation efforts crucial for its survival.
