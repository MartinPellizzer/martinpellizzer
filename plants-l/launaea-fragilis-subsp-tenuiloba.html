Launaea fragilis subsp. tenuiloba

Launaea fragilis subsp.

tenuiloba, commonly known as the slender leaf launaea, is a succulent plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa. It is characterized by its fleshy, often hairy stems and small, narrow leaves that help reduce water loss.

The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are typically borne on branched inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand prolonged drought periods.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
