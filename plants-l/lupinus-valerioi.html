Lupinus valerioi

Lupinus valerioi, commonly known as the Valerio's lupine, is a species of flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds.

Lupinus valerioi is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes, where it plays a role in nitrogen fixation due to its symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.
