Lepionurus sylvestris var. lanceolatus

Lepionurus sylvestris var. lanceolatus, commonly known as the lanceolate sylvestris, is a lesser-known variant of the sylvestris species within the Lepionurus genus.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in shades of white or pale yellow, and are borne on slender pedicels. This variant is native to specific regions in South America, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its limited distribution and unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
