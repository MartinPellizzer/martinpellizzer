Lippia francensis

Lippia francensis, commonly known as French thyme, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are fragrant and often used in culinary applications.

Lippia francensis thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight.
