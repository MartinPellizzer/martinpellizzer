Laggeria

Laggeria, commonly known as the "yellow star" or "false dandelion," is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are typically daisy-like in appearance. The plant features a basal rosette of leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive shape. Laggeria species are native to various regions in South America and are often found in open, sunny habitats.

They are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes used in traditional medicine.
