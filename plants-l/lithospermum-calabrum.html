Lithospermum calabrum

Lithospermum calabrum, commonly known as the Calabrian rock pepper, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant features upright stems with opposite, ovate leaves that have a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are small, tubular, and usually pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches.

The plant produces bright red berries that contain several seeds, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.
