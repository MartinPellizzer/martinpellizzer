Leucocoryne uniflora

Leucocoryne uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower leucocoryne, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a single, bell-shaped flower that is typically white or pale blue. The plant is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in well-drained conditions. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to care for.

Leucocoryne uniflora is also noted for its ability to bloom reliably under proper cultivation.
