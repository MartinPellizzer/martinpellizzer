Lupinus magnistipulatus

Lupinus magnistipulatus, commonly known as the giant lupine, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The plant produces clusters of vibrant blue or purple pea-like flowers, which bloom in summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

Lupinus magnistipulatus is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
