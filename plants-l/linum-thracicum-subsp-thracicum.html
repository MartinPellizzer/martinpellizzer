Linum thracicum subsp. thracicum

Linum thracicum subsp.

thracicum, commonly known as Thrace flax, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula. It is characterized by its slender stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry meadows and rocky areas.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
