Lonchocarpus oxycarpus

Lonchocarpus oxycarpus, commonly known as the sharpseed legume, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features trifoliate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces elongated, woody pods containing kidney-shaped seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded environments within tropical forests.

The species is notable for its potential use in traditional medicine and as a source of nitrogen-fixing properties in soil.
