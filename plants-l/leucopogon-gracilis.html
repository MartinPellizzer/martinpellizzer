Leucopogon gracilis

Leucopogon gracilis, commonly known as the slender leucopogon, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or white. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in open woods or sandy habitats. Its foliage consists of narrow, leathery leaves that help it conserve moisture in its native environment.

Leucopogon gracilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
