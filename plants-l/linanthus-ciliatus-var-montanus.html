Linanthus ciliatus var. montanus

Linanthus ciliatus var. montanus, commonly known as the Sierra dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Malvaceae family, which includes mallows and hibiscus. The plant features hairy, glandular stems and leaves with a distinctive ciliate (fringed) edge. Its yellow flowers are typically clustered in inflorescences and bloom in late spring to early summer.
