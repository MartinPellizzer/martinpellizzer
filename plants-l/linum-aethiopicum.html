Linum aethiopicum

Linum aethiopicum, commonly known as Ethiopian flax, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia and surrounding regions.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and small, blue or purple flowers. The plant produces capsules that contain numerous small, round seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value and historical significance, it is cultivated in gardens and for traditional uses.
