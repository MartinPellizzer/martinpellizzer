Laurembergia androgyna

Laurembergia androgyna, commonly known as the African orchid, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers. The flowers are typically greenish-yellow with purple markings, and they bloom during the warmer months. The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
