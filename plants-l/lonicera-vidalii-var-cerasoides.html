Lonicera vidalii var. cerasoides

Lonicera vidalii var. cerasoides, commonly known as the cherry honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that matures in the fall, containing several seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade to full sun.
