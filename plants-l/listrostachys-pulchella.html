Listrostachys pulchella

Listrostachys pulchella, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and dark reddish-brown bark. The plant has compound leaves with long, narrow leaflets and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruits are woody capsules that contain several seeds.

Due to its valuable timber and ornamental qualities, it is often cultivated in horticultural settings.
