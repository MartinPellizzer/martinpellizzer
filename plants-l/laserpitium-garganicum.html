Laserpitium garganicum

Laserpitium garganicum, commonly known as the alpine hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils at high altitudes. Its yellow ray florets are surrounded by numerous yellow disk florets, creating a distinctive appearance.

Laserpitium garganicum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often found in wildflower meadows and alpine gardens.
