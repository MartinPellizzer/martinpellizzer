Lasianthus annamicus

Lasianthus annamicus, commonly known as the Annamite lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its erect herbaceous growth habit. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are typically clustered in whorls, with tubular corollas that range in color from pink to purple.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
