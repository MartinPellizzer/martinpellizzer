Luziola doelliana

Luziola doelliana, commonly known as the mountain bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils at high altitudes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered with fine hairs, while its stems are erect and somewhat woody at the base.

Luziola doelliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in alpine gardens.
