Lonicera pallasii

Lonicera pallasii, commonly known as the Japanese honeysuckle, is a deciduous climbing vine native to East Asia.

It features opposite, elliptical leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a red, fleshy capsule that contains numerous small, black seeds.

Lonicera pallasii is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to climb trellises or trees.
