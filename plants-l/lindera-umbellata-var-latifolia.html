Lindera umbellata var. latifolia

Lindera umbellata var. latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf sweet pepper, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are usually broad and glossy. It produces small, fragrant white flowers in umbels, followed by bright red, berry-like fruits.

This variant is distinguished by its broader leaves compared to the typical form of Lindera umbellata.
