Lycianthes ecuadorensis

Lycianthes ecuadorensis, commonly known as the Ecuadorian bitterleaf, is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades.

It is a shrub native to the Andean regions of Ecuador, characterized by its oval, dark green leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by orange or red berries containing several seeds.

It is known for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects.
