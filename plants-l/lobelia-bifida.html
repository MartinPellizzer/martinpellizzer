Lobelia bifida

Lobelia bifida, commonly known as the splitleaf lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaves that are deeply lobed, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Lobelia bifida is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a habitat for pollinators.
