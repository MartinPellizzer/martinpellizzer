Laurus

Laurus, commonly known as laurel, is a genus of evergreen shrubs and trees in the family Lauraceae.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The fruit is a drupe, typically containing a single seed, and is often used in culinary and medicinal applications. Laurel plants are native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and are widely cultivated for their ornamental and practical value.

They thrive in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
