Lavatera thuringiaca var. vitifolia

Lavatera thuringiaca var. vitifolia, commonly known as the grapeleaf lavatera, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that resemble grape leaves, hence its common name. The plant produces showy, fluffy pink or white flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its foliage and long blooming period.
