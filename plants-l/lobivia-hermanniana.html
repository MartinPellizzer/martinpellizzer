Lobivia hermanniana

Lobivia hermanniana, commonly known as the Hermann's lobivia, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rounded shape and fleshy, ribbed stems. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers during the warmer months. It has a shallow root system adapted to absorb moisture from the dry soil.

Lobivia hermanniana is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and ornamental appeal.
