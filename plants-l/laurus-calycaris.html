Laurus calycaris

Laurus calycaris, commonly known as the narrow-leaved laurel, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its aromatic evergreen leaves and small, clustered white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically harvested when they turn dark blue or black. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and fragrance, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
