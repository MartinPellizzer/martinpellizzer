Lamium maculatum var. laevigatum

Lamium maculatum var. laevigatum, commonly known as the silver spotted deadnettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive silver or white mottling, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking shade-tolerant plants.
