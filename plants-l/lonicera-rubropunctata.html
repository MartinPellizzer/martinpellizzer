Lonicera rubropunctata

Lonicera rubropunctata, commonly known as the red-spotted honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by red, orange, or yellow berries in late summer. Its foliage turns a vibrant red in the fall, adding ornamental value to gardens.

Lonicera rubropunctata is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
