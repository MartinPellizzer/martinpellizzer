Limonium viniolae

Limonium viniolae, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and saline soils, showing a high tolerance for salty environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the flowers are often surrounded by bracts that resemble leaves.

Limonium viniolae is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.
