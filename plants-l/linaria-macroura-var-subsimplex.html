Linaria macroura var. subsimplex

Linaria macroura var. subsimplex, commonly known as the narrow-leaved toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and elongated, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and ornamental value in gardens.
