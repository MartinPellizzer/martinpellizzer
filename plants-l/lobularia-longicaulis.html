Lobularia longicaulis

Lobularia longicaulis, commonly known as the long-stemmed lobe flower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.

Lobularia longicaulis is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
