Lonchocarpus trifoliolatus

Lonchocarpus trifoliolatus, commonly known as the three-leaved senna, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet being ovate to elliptic in shape. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in racemes, and it produces cylindrical pods containing several seeds.

The plant is notable for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it valuable in soil enrichment.
