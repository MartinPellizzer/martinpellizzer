Leptactina gloeocalyx

Leptactina gloeocalyx, commonly known as the sticky globular cactus, is a unique succulent native to arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its globular shape and dense covering of fine, sticky hairs that help reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the warmer months, which are followed by spherical, green fruits. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Due to its specialized adaptations, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in succulent gardens.
