Lycopersicon peruvianum subsp. commutatum

Lycopersicon peruvianum subsp.

commutatum, commonly known as the Peruvian tomato, is a wild relative of the domesticated tomato. It is native to the Andean regions of South America, where it thrives in high-altitude environments.

The plant typically produces small, yellow or orange fruits that are often used in traditional Andean cuisine. Its botanical features include hairy stems, lobed leaves, and a compact growth habit.

This subspecies is valued for its genetic diversity and potential for breeding programs aimed at improving cultivated tomato varieties.
