Luzula abchasica

Luzula abchasica, commonly known as the Abkhazian goat's beard, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, greenish-white flowers. Native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus, including Abkhazia, it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
