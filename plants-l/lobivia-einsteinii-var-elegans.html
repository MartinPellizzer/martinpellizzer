Lobivia einsteinii var. elegans

Lobivia einsteinii var. elegans, commonly known as the Einstein's Lobivia, is a compact, low-growing succulent native to the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It features thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically green with a bluish tint, adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the spring and summer months. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, requiring minimal water to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
