Lavoisiera chamaepitys

Lavoisiera chamaepitys, commonly known as the low pine, is a rare and ancient conifer native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its slow growth, twisted trunk, and dense foliage, which provides habitat for various wildlife species. The plant has small, scale-like leaves and produces cones that are typically reddish-brown in color. Due to its limited distribution and vulnerability to environmental changes, it is considered a conservation concern.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important subject of botanical study.
