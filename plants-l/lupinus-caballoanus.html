Lupinus caballoanus

Lupinus caballoanus, commonly known as the Caballo lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves composed of several small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and along roadsides.

Lupinus caballoanus is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and preventing soil erosion.
