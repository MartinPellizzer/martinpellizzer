Luma bracteosa

Luma bracteosa, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, bright red berries that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces fragrant white flowers during the winter months. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

Luma bracteosa is also known for its ability to spread rapidly, making it a potential invasive species in certain regions.
