Luzula exspectata

Luzula exspectata, commonly known as the silver hair grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are typically narrow, grass-like, and covered with a fine, silvery bloom that gives it its common name.

Luzula exspectata thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
