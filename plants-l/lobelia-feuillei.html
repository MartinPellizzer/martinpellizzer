Lobelia feuillei

Lobelia feuillei, commonly known as the New Zealand lobelia, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are usually blue or purple in color, arranged in dense spikes. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a hairy texture.

Lobelia feuillei is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for native pollinators.
