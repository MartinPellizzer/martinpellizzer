Leucocroton discolor

Leucocroton discolor, commonly known as the variable croton, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its strikingly variable leaf patterns. The leaves often display a mix of green, white, and yellow hues, creating a visually appealing contrast. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters.

Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, with the showy parts being the modified leaves called bracts.
