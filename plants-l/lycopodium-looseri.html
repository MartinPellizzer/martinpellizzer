Lycopodium looseri

Lycopodium looseri, commonly known as the Looser's clubmoss, is a small evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae and is characterized by its dense, needle-like foliage that forms a low-growing mat. The plant produces spore-bearing cones, which are clustered at the ends of its stems, a feature typical of its ancient lineage. Lycopodium looseri thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Its botanical structure reflects its evolutionary adaptation to humid, tropical climates.
