Luteola

Luteola, commonly known as the yellowweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and is native to various regions in Europe and Asia. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Luteola has small, narrow leaves and produces numerous small florets arranged in a composite head.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
