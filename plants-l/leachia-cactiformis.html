Leachia cactiformis

Leachia cactiformis, commonly known as the spiny cactus, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It features a tall, cylindrical stem covered in sharp, spine-like structures that provide protection from herbivores. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its rugged appearance. Its thick, fleshy stems are adapted to store water, allowing it to thrive in harsh, dry environments.

Leachia cactiformis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique form and resilience.
