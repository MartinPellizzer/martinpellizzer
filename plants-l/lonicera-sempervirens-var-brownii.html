Lonicera sempervirens var. brownii

Lonicera sempervirens var. brownii, commonly known as the Brown's honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to yellow, often appearing in clusters. It is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in partial to full sunlight.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and role in supporting local pollinators.
