Lolium × grandispicum

Lolium × grandispicum, commonly known as tall ryegrass, is a hybrid grass species that combines characteristics of both annual and perennial ryegrass.

It is native to Europe and is widely cultivated for its high forage value in agricultural settings. The plant typically grows to a height of 60–100 cm, with slender, erect stems and dense, feather-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are usually tinged with purple, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Lolium × grandispicum is valued for its rapid growth, good nutritional content, and adaptability to various soil conditions.
