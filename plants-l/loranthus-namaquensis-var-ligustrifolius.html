Loranthus namaquensis var. ligustrifolius

Loranthus namaquensis var. ligustrifolius, commonly known as the Namaqua mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees, primarily in the succulent karoo region of South Africa.

It has small, leathery leaves that are often clustered in whorls, and its flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, arranged in clusters. The plant produces small, round berries that are a food source for various birds and insects.

Its parasitic nature allows it to absorb water and nutrients from its host, while still performing photosynthesis to some extent.
