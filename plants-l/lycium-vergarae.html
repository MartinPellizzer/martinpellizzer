Lycium vergarae

Lycium vergarae, commonly known as the Chilean boxthorn, is a shrub native to southern Chile.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces red berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in coastal and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

Lycium vergarae is adapted to withstand drought and salt spray, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
