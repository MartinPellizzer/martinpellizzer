Litsea vang var. grandifolia

Litsea vang var. grandifolia, commonly known as the large-leaved litsea, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its simple, ovate to lanceolate leaves, which are usually glossy and dark green in color. Its flowers are small and fragrant, often clustered in inflorescences, and it produces small, round berries that are edible.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
