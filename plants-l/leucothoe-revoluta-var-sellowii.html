Leucothoe revoluta var. sellowii

Leucothoe revoluta var. sellowii, commonly known as Sellow's leucothoe, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright, slender stems with opposite, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in clusters during late spring to early summer. These flowers are usually white or pale pink, adding ornamental value to the landscape.

It thrives in partial to full shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a suitable choice for woodland gardens.
