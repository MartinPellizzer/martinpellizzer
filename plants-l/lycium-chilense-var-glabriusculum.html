Lycium chilense var. glabriusculum

Lycium chilense var. glabriusculum, commonly known as the Chilean boxthorn, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, red berries that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually small, oval, and covered in a fine, whitish layer that helps reduce water loss.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
