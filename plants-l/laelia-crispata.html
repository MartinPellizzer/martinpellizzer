Laelia crispata

Laelia crispata, commonly known as the crinkled orchid, is a species native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a compact, pseudobulbous structure that stores water and nutrients. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with a distinctive crinkled appearance. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained soil.

Laelia crispata is appreciated for its ornamental value and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts worldwide.
