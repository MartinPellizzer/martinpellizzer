Lippia hirsuta var. moritzii

Lippia hirsuta var. moritzii, commonly known as the hairy lippia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant typically grows as a shrub or subshrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental and medicinal use.

Its essential oils contain compounds such as thymol and carvacrol, which contribute to its use in traditional medicine.
