Lauro-cerasus lusitanica f. marginata

Lauro-cerasus lusitanica f. marginata, commonly known as the variegated cherry laurel, is a cultivar of the cherry laurel shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its striking variegated leaves, which display white and green patterns, making it a popular ornamental plant. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in spring, followed by edible cherries that are typically used in jams and preserves. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Due to its aesthetic appeal and hardiness, it is widely cultivated in gardens and landscapes across temperate regions.
