Ledum glandulosum var. australe

Ledum glandulosum var. australe, commonly known as the Australian cowberry, is a perennial shrub native to southern Australia.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. The plant has aromatic, glandular leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive scent. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open woodlands.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is a notable species in its native habitat.
