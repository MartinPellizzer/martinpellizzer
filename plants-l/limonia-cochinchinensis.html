Limonia cochinchinensis

Limonia cochinchinensis, commonly known as the Surinam cherry or the Java cherry, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is notable for its bright red, cherry-like fruits that are edible and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

Limonia cochinchinensis is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
