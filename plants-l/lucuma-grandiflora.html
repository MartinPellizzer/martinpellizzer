Lucuma grandiflora

Lucuma grandiflora, commonly known as the lucuma fruit tree, is a native plant of the Andean region of South America.

It is a member of the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen shrub or small tree structure. The plant produces large, yellow-orange fruits with a sweet, nutty flavor, which are typically harvested when fully mature. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while its flowers are white with a distinctive yellow center.

Lucuma grandiflora thrives in high-altitude environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
