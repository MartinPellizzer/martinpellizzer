Lythrum parviflorum

Lythrum parviflorum, commonly known as narrowleaf catchfly, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lythraceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pinkish-purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered with fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Lythrum parviflorum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can also serve as a host plant for certain insects.
