Liquidambar excelsa

Liquidambar excelsa, commonly known as the sweet gum tree, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It is characterized by its distinctive star-shaped leaves and spherical, spiky seed balls that develop in the fall. The tree produces small, inconspicuous flowers in the spring, followed by the formation of its unique, woody fruit. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, with shallow fissures, and it can grow up to 30 meters tall.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often planted in gardens and urban landscapes.
