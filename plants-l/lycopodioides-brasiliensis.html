Lycopodioides brasiliensis

Lycopodioides brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian clubmoss, is a member of the Lycopodiaceae family and is native to South America.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors. The plant features upright, branching stems covered in small, scale-like leaves. It produces spore-bearing structures called strobili, which are clustered at the tips of its branches.

Lycopodioides brasiliensis plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
