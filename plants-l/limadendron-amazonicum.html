Limadendron amazonicum

Limadendron amazonicum, commonly known as the Amazonian flame tree, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom in clusters, often displaying vibrant red or orange hues. The plant has broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. Its woody structure allows it to thrive in humid, warm climates with high rainfall.

Limadendron amazonicum plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing nectar for pollinators and shelter for various wildlife species.
