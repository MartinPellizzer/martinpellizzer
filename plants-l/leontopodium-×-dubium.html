Leontopodium × dubium

Leontopodium × dubium, commonly known as the edelweiss, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly white bracts that surround a central cluster of small, yellowish flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes, often found in mountainous areas of the Alps. Its unique appearance and habitat make it a symbol of resilience and endurance.

Due to its restricted range and conservation status, it is protected in many regions.
