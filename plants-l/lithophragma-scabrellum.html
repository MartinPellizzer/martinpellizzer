Lithophragma scabrellum

Lithophragma scabrellum, commonly known as the roughleaf twinflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and along stream banks. The plant features upright stems with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. Its flowers are small, pink or purple, and arranged in clusters, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Lithophragma scabrellum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
