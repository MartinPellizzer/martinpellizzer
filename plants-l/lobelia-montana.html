Lobelia montana

Lobelia montana, commonly known as the mountain lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky slopes. The plant features slender, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, downy coating. Its showy blue to purple flowers bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Lobelia montana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
