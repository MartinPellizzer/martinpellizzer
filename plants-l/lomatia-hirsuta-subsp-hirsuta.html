Lomatia hirsuta subsp. hirsuta

Lomatia hirsuta subsp.

hirsuta, commonly known as the woolly lomatia, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its dense, woolly grey foliage and its ability to thrive in dry, sandy soils.

The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers in clusters, typically during the autumn season. Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a notable species in arid and semi-arid regions.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
