Leontodon hispidus var. alpicola

Leontodon hispidus var. alpicola, commonly known as the alpine hedgehog grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the daisy family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its hairy, bristly stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes, often found in mountain meadows and slopes.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and specialized alpine species.
