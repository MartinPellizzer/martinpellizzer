Lamium maculatum f. trilobum

Lamium maculatum f. trilobum, commonly known as the spotted dead nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive red or purple underside, and its flowers are small, tubular, and typically pink or purple in color. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.

The species is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
