Legazpia

Legazpia, commonly known as the red spider lily, is a striking flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant red flowers that bloom in late summer or early autumn. The plant features strap-like, dark green leaves that grow from a bulbous underground stem. Legazpia is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique and eye-catching blooms.

However, it should be handled with care, as its sap can be toxic to humans and animals.
