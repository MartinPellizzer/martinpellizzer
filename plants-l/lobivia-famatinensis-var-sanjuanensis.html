Lobivia famatinensis var. sanjuanensis

Lobivia famatinensis var. sanjuanensis, commonly known as the San Juan lobivia, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that can store water. The plant typically produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
