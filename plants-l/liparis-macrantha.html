Liparis macrantha

Liparis macrantha, commonly known as the Cape orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its roots are fibrous, and it produces erect, branching stems with lance-shaped leaves.

The flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive lip that is larger than the other petals.
