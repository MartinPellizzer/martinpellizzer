Linaria lanigera var. dealbata

Linaria lanigera var. dealbata, commonly known as the white-flowered wallflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe.

It features slender, hairy stems and produces showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers in shades of yellow to white. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or dry habitats. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity.

The variety is distinguished by its pale or white petals, contrasting with the more vibrant colors of the typical Linaria lanigera.
