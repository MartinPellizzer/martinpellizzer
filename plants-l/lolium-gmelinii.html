Lolium gmelinii

Lolium gmelinii, commonly known as meadow fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences containing numerous tiny florets. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along roadsides.

Lolium gmelinii is valued for its forage quality and has been used historically in agricultural settings.
