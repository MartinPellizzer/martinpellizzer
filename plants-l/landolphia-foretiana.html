Landolphia foretiana

Landolphia foretiana, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night, emitting a strong, sweet fragrance. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough texture.

Landolphia foretiana is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and along riverbanks.
