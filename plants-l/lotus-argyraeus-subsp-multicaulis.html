Lotus argyraeus subsp. multicaulis

Lotus argyraeus subsp.

multicaulis, commonly known as the silver lotus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features a basal rosette of silver-gray, hairy leaves that are often covered in fine white trichomes.

The plant produces showy pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking foliage and floral display.
