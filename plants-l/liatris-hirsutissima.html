Liatris hirsutissima

Liatris hirsutissima, commonly known as the hairy blazing star, is a herbaceous perennial native to the central and eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, spiky flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces purple to pinkish flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for native plant gardens.

Its ecological importance lies in its role as a nectar source and habitat for various wildlife species.
