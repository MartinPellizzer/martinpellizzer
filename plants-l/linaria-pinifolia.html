Linaria pinifolia

Linaria pinifolia, commonly known as the needleleaf toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves and produces bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found at high altitudes. It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is known for its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.

Linaria pinifolia is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking floral display.
