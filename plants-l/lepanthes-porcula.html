Lepanthes porcula

Lepanthes porcula, commonly known as the pig-nosed orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its unique, often colorful flowers that feature a distinctive, pig-like snout-shaped spur. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded forest environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

Lepanthes porcula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its striking floral display.
