Loranthus daihuzanensis

Loranthus daihuzanensis, commonly known as the Hainan mistletoe, is a parasitic plant belonging to the Loranthaceae family.

It grows on the branches of various host trees, primarily in the subtropical regions of China. The plant has small, opposite leaves and produces yellowish-green flowers that are often clustered together. Its fruit is a small, fleshy capsule that contains seeds covered in a sticky substance to aid in attachment to potential host plants.

This species plays a role in local ecosystems by influencing the growth of its host trees and providing food for certain bird species.
