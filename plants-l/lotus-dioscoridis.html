Lotus dioscoridis

Lotus dioscoridis, commonly known as the Egyptian lotus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive pink or red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It is often found growing along water edges and in moist soils, and it has been historically used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
