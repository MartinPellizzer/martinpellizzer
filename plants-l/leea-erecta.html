Leea erecta

Leea erecta, commonly known as the evergreen sarsaparilla, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the family Leeaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain several seeds.

Leea erecta is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
