Lonicera glehnii var. viridissima

Lonicera glehnii var. viridissima, commonly known as the green-flowered honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, fragrant, greenish-white flowers in early spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
