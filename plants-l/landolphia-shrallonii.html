Landolphia shrallonii

Landolphia shrallonii, commonly known as the African morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a woody vine that can grow up to several meters in length, characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The flowers are typically yellow or orange with a distinctive red or purple throat, blooming in the early morning. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental beauty.
