Leptinella pectinata subsp. willcoxii

Leptinella pectinata subsp.

willcoxii, commonly known as the Willcox lewisia, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial plant native to the southwestern United States. It features dense clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant has needle-like leaves that are gray-green in color and covered in fine, white hairs. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
