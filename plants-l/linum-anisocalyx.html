Linum anisocalyx

Linum anisocalyx, commonly known as the unequal-stamen flax, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Linum.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of China, Kazakhstan, and Mongolia. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It is characterized by its unique floral structure, with stamens of varying lengths, giving it its scientific name.

Linum anisocalyx is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, high-altitude environments.
