Lycianthes stephanocalyx

Lycianthes stephanocalyx, commonly known as the skyflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and ovate with a slightly hairy texture.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, round seeds.
