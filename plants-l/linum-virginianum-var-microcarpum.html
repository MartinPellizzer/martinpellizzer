Linum virginianum var. microcarpum

Linum virginianum var. microcarpum, commonly known as the small-fruited flax, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. The plant features slender, erect stems that are often branched and covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are alternate, linear, and often have a slightly hairy surface.

The showy blue to purple flowers are borne in racemes and are followed by small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.
