Laurus octandra

Laurus octandra, commonly known as the Turkish laurel or sweet laurel, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by dark blue berries. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice, while the plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities.

The botanical features of Laurus octandra make it a versatile and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
