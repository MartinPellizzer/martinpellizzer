Lepechiniella lasiocarpa

Lepechiniella lasiocarpa, commonly known as the woolly lepechiniella, is a small, evergreen shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that covers its slender, branched stems. The leaves are small, ovate, and covered in a fine, white pubescence, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This plant is well adapted to drought conditions and thrives in well-drained soils.
