Lupinus douglasii

Lupinus douglasii, commonly known as Douglas's lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Lupinus douglasii plays an important role in ecological systems by fixing nitrogen into the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
