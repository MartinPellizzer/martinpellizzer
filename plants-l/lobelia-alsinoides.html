Lobelia alsinoides

Lobelia alsinoides, commonly known as the small-leaved lobelia, is a herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, narrow leaves and produces small, tubular flowers in shades of blue or purple. It is often found in wetlands and is adapted to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

Lobelia alsinoides plays a role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
