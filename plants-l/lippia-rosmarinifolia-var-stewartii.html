Lippia rosmarinifolia var. stewartii

Lippia rosmarinifolia var. stewartii, commonly known as Stewart's lemon verbena, is a variant of the lemon verbena species native to South America.

It is a shrub characterized by aromatic, narrow leaves that emit a strong lemon scent when crushed. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale yellow to white in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and use in herbal teas and aromatherapy.
