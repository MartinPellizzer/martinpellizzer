Lycopodioides laevigata

Lycopodioides laevigata, commonly known as the smooth clubmoss, is a member of the family Lycopodiaceae and is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and spore-producing structures.

It is a primitive vascular plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant exhibits a sporophyte-dominated life cycle, with the gametophyte stage being short-lived and dependent on the sporophyte for survival. Its reproductive structures, called strobili, contain the spores that are dispersed by wind.

Lycopodioides laevigata plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
