Liparis schistochila

Liparis schistochila, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern at the base.

Liparis schistochila is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with appropriate conditions.
