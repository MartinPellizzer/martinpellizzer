Liparis ornithorrhynchos

Liparis ornithorrhynchos, commonly known as the bird's beak orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated floral structure that resembles a bird's beak, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with a distinctive, narrow spur.

Liparis ornithorrhynchos is a perennial herb with a complex root system adapted to nutrient-poor soils.
