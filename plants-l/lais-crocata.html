Lais crocata

Lais crocata, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that resemble a crown, with five prominent petals and intricate filaments. The plant produces showy, violet-colored flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often have a velvety texture.

Lais crocata is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its role in supporting local ecosystems.
