Linum gallicum subsp. strictum

Linum gallicum subsp.

strictum, commonly known as the upright flax, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It features slender, erect stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, covered in fine, soft hairs.

The plant produces showy blue to violet flowers in racemes, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, linear, and often have a slight reddish tint.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in textile production.
