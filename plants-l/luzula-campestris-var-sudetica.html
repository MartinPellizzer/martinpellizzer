Luzula campestris var. sudetica

Luzula campestris var. sudetica, commonly known as the alpine woodrush, is a perennial plant belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and is typically found in mountainous regions. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky areas.

Luzula campestris var. sudetica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in high-altitude environments.
