Liparis longiscapa

Liparis longiscapa, commonly known as the long-stemmed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, showy flowers with a distinctive lip that is often brightly colored. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
