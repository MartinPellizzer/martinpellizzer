Lepidium graminifolium var. glaucescens

Lepidium graminifolium var. glaucescens, commonly known as the blue-green lancer, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in a waxy, bluish-green coating. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
