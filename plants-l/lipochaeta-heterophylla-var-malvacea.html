Lipochaeta heterophylla var. malvacea

Lipochaeta heterophylla var. malvacea, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bushwillow, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes mallows and hibiscus, and is characterized by its hairy stems and showy flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often narrow, while its flowers are clustered in panicles and range in color from white to pink.
