Lepanthes longiracemosa

Lepanthes longiracemosa, commonly known as the long raceme orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, pendulous inflorescences that bear numerous small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in the understory of tropical forests. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Lepanthes longiracemosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
