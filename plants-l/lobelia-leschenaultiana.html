Lobelia leschenaultiana

Lobelia leschenaultiana, commonly known as the blue lobelia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina, Bolivia, and Chile.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, tubular blue or violet flowers. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Lobelia leschenaultiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
