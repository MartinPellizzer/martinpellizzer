Lophopetalum havilandii

Lophopetalum havilandii, commonly known as Haviland's waxleaf privet, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, oval, glossy green leaves that remain on the plant throughout the year, giving it a dense, evergreen appearance. The plant produces white, fragrant flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by small, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it adaptable to various garden conditions.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for hedges and landscape plantings.
