Lupinus stinchfieldiae

Lupinus stinchfieldiae, commonly known as Stinchfield's lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces seed pods that contain several elliptical seeds.

Lupinus stinchfieldiae is often found in areas with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.
