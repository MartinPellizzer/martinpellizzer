Laburnum vulgare

Laburnum vulgare, commonly known as the golden chain tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe.

It is characterized by its long, drooping clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has compound leaves with oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, woody pods that contain flattened seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

The golden chain tree is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
