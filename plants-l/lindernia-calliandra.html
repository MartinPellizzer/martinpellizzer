Lindernia calliandra

Lindernia calliandra, commonly known as the redstem lindernia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Lindernia calliandra is also known for its ability to tolerate drought conditions once established.
