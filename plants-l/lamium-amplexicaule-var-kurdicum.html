Lamium amplexicaule var. kurdicum

Lamium amplexicaule var. kurdicum, commonly known as the Kurdish dead-nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of small, two-lipped, pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. It is characterized by its erect stems and is often found in rocky or limestone soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses in its native regions.
