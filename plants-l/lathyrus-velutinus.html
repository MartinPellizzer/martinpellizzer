Lathyrus velutinus

Lathyrus velutinus, commonly known as the velvet vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple or pink. The plant produces pods containing small, edible seeds that are often used in traditional agriculture. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in temperate climates and well-drained soils.

Its growth habit makes it a useful plant for soil stabilization and as a cover crop.
