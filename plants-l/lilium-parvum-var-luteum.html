Lilium parvum var. luteum

Lilium parvum var. luteum, commonly known as the yellow dwarf lily, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features small, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pale yellow to orange, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 18 inches, with slender, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical characteristics include a bulbous root system and showy, fragrant blooms that attract pollinators.
