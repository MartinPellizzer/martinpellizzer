Lobivia cylindracea

Lobivia cylindracea, commonly known as the cylindrical lobivia, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem that can grow up to 30 centimeters in height. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

Lobivia cylindracea is well adapted to survive in low-water conditions, making it a resilient plant in desert ecosystems.
