Lathriogyna candicans

Lathriogyna candicans, commonly known as the shining lathyrus, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
