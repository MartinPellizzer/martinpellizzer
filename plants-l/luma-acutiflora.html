Luma acutiflora

Luma acutiflora, commonly known as the golden torch tree, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Brazil.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters. The plant is characterized by its dense, lance-shaped leaves and its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that contains a single seed.

Luma acutiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
