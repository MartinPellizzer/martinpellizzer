Laburnum nigricans

Laburnum nigricans, commonly known as the black laburnum, is a species of flowering plant in the family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its drooping clusters of golden-yellow flowers, which bloom in early spring. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. It is native to southern Europe and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

The bark is smooth and dark brown, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
