Lupinus vallicola

Lupinus vallicola, commonly known as the valley lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces elongated pods that contain several seeds.

Lupinus vallicola is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in open areas such as meadows and grasslands.
