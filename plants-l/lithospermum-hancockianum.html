Lithospermum hancockianum

Lithospermum hancockianum, commonly known as Hancock's rock pepper, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant produces bright red or orange flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and the stems are often woody at the base.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
