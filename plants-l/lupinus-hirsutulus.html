Lupinus hirsutulus

Lupinus hirsutulus, commonly known as the hairy lupine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces spiny, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds, which are often used as forage for livestock. Lupinus hirsutulus is adapted to a variety of soil types but thrives in well-drained, alkaline conditions.

It plays an important ecological role in nitrogen fixation and serves as a food source for various wildlife species.
