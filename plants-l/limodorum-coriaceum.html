Limodorum coriaceum

Limodorum coriaceum, commonly known as the tough lily or broad-leaved lily, is a rare and endangered species native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its broad, waxy, dark green leaves and striking violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and rocky crevices. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and the plant reproduces through seeds and rhizomes.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, Limodorum coriaceum faces significant conservation challenges.
