Lobelia gloria-montis subsp. gloria-montis

Lobelia gloria-montis subsp.

gloria-montis, commonly known as the Mount Gloria lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America. It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers, which bloom in dense clusters atop slender stems.

The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
