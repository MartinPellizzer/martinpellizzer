Leea aequata

Leea aequata, commonly known as the African starleaf, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Leeaceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that often have a distinctive star-like appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by yellowish-orange fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Leea aequata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some regions.
