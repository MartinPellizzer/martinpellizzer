Lobelia heterophylla subsp. centralis

Lobelia heterophylla subsp.

centralis, commonly known as the central lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its variable leaf morphology, with leaves ranging from basal to opposite or whorled arrangements.

The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in swampy or wetland habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
