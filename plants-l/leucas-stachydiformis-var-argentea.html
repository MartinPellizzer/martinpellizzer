Leucas stachydiformis var. argentea

Leucas stachydiformis var. argentea, commonly known as silver leucas, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which results from dense covering of fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers arranged in compact clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance.
