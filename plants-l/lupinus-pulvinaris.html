Lupinus pulvinaris

Lupinus pulvinaris, commonly known as the purple lupine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of pea-like flowers. The plant has palmately compound leaves with several leaflets, and its root system includes nitrogen-fixing nodules, which contribute to soil fertility. Lupinus pulvinaris thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
