Lauro-cerasus vulgaris rotundifolia

Lauro-cerasus vulgaris rotundifolia, commonly known as the cherry laurel, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It features dark green, glossy leaves that are oval and slightly wavy in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, followed by red, cherry-like berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is tolerant of various soil types.

However, its berries are toxic to humans and animals, requiring caution in gardens.
