Loranthus scurrula var. graciliflorus

Loranthus scurrula var. graciliflorus, commonly known as the slender-flowered mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to host trees through its roots.

It belongs to the family Loranthaceae and is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are often consumed by birds, aiding in seed dispersal. It is typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing on a variety of host species.

Its botanical structure reflects its parasitic lifestyle, with reduced roots and specialized structures for nutrient absorption.
