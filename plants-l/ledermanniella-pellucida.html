Ledermanniella pellucida

Ledermanniella pellucida, commonly known as the glassy moss, is a small, delicate moss species that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is characterized by its translucent, leaf-like structures that give it a glassy appearance, hence its common name. The plant forms dense, cushion-like mats and is often found on decaying wood or in rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures, called capsules, are typically borne on stalks and release spores when mature.

Ledermanniella pellucida plays a vital role in soil stabilization and moisture retention in its native habitats.
