Linaria angustealata

Linaria angustealata, commonly known as the narrowleaf toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has linear, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Linaria angustealata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
