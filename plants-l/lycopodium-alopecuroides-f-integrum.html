Lycopodium alopecuroides f. integrum

Lycopodium alopecuroides f. integrum, commonly known as the common clubmoss, is a perennial evergreen plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, spore-bearing cones and its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments. The plant has small, scale-like leaves that form tight, cylindrical clusters, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is often found in forest floors and rocky crevices, where it plays a role in soil stabilization.

Its reproductive structures are essential for its propagation and ecological function in woodland ecosystems.
