Linum selaginoides var. chilense

Linum selaginoides var. chilense, commonly known as the Chilean flax, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, delicate blue or violet flowers. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and is adapted to thrive in coastal and arid environments. It belongs to the Linaceae family, which includes other species of flax.

Its botanical features make it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
