Lipusa multiflora var. niger

Lipusa multiflora var. niger, commonly known as the black-flowered lipusa, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems that bear small, clustered inflorescences of dark purple to black flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
