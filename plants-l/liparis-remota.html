Liparis remota

Liparis remota, commonly known as the remote orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption from poor soil conditions.

Liparis remota is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
