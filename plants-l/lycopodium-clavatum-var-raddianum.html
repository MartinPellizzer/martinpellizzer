Lycopodium clavatum var. raddianum

Lycopodium clavatum var. raddianum, commonly known as the club moss, is a small, evergreen fern-like plant that belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its tiny, scale-like leaves and spore-producing structures called strobila. This variety is native to various regions in Europe, Asia, and North America, often growing in moist, shaded environments. The plant is notable for its ability to thrive in rocky or sandy soils, making it a hardy species.

Its historical use in traditional medicine and as a source of spores for scientific research adds to its botanical significance.
