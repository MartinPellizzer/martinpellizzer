Leucaster caniflorus

Leucaster caniflorus, commonly known as the white-flowered leucaster, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking white or pale pink flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which extends from late spring to early autumn.
