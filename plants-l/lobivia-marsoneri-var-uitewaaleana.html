Lobivia marsoneri var. uitewaaleana

Lobivia marsoneri var. uitewaaleana, commonly known as the Uitewaaleana Lobivia, is a succulent native to South America, specifically Argentina.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded shape and thick, fleshy stems that store water, typical of desert plants. The plant produces small, violet-colored flowers during the cooler months, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is a popular choice among cactus and succulent enthusiasts.
