Lobivia jajoana var. nidularis

Lobivia jajoana var. nidularis, commonly known as the "Cushion Lobivia," is a compact, low-growing succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It features dense, rounded rosettes of fleshy, blue-green leaves that help it store water and reduce transpiration. The plant produces pale yellow to orange flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its foliage. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
