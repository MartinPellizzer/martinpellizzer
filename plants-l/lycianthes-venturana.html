Lycianthes venturana

Lycianthes venturana, commonly known as the yellow jacobean weed, is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades.

It is a herbaceous annual plant native to Central and South America, characterized by its hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by orange or red berries containing several seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
