Lonicera involucrata f. humilis

Lonicera involucrata f. humilis, commonly known as the dwarf honeysuckle, is a compact shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, typically in shades of yellow and orange. The plant has opposite, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It prefers partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its early blooming habit and ability to attract pollinators.
