Lycopersicon lycopersicum var. cerasiforme

Lycopersicon lycopersicum var. cerasiforme, commonly known as the cherry tomato, is a variant of the domesticated tomato characterized by its small, round, and often red fruits that resemble cherries.

This variety is prized for its intense flavor and vibrant color, making it a popular choice in both culinary and ornamental settings. Botanically, it belongs to the Solanaceae family and is closely related to other tomato species, sharing traits such as a fleshy fruit with a central seed cavity. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with hairy stems and alternate, lobed leaves.

Its flowers are yellow and star-shaped, typical of the Solanaceae family, and it produces fruits that ripen to a deep red when mature.
