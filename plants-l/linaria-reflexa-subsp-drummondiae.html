Linaria reflexa subsp. drummondiae

Linaria reflexa subsp.

drummondiae, commonly known as Drummond's toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a perennial herb with slender, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces clusters of bright yellow, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in desert or arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and botanical gardens.
