Lauro-cerasus officinalis

Lauro-cerasus officinalis, commonly known as the cherry laurel, is a species of flowering shrub or small tree native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans and livestock. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.
