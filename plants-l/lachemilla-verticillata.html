Lachemilla verticillata

Lachemilla verticillata, commonly known as creeping cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to North America.

It features dense, mat-forming growth with small, rounded leaves arranged in whorls of five around the stems. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers in early spring, which are followed by red or orange berries. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and forest understories.

Lachemilla verticillata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
