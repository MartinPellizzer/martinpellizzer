Lagerstroemia hypoleuca

Lagerstroemia hypoleuca, commonly known as the white lagerstroemia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and its showy, panicle-like inflorescences that range from white to pale pink. The plant produces small, red fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by birds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Lagerstroemia hypoleuca is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various garden conditions.
