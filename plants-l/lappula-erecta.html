Lappula erecta

Lappula erecta, commonly known as the upright lappula, is a flowering plant in the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has smooth, linear leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Its seed pods are cylindrical and split open to release small, rounded seeds.

Lappula erecta is often found in dry, disturbed soils and is native to parts of Europe and Asia.
