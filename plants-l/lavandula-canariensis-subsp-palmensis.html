Lavandula canariensis subsp. palmensis

Lavandula canariensis subsp.

palmensis, commonly known as the Canary Island lavender, is a member of the Lamiaceae family. It is native to the Canary Islands and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit.

The plant produces spikes of purple flowers, which are typical of the lavender genus. Its leaves are aromatic, with a strong, camphor-like scent.

It is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal environments.
