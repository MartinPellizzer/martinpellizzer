Lotus chazaliei var. longipes

Lotus chazaliei var. longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed lotus, is a rare and striking aquatic plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It features striking pink to purple flowers that bloom above the water surface, often in summer. The plant has long, slender stems that can extend several feet, supporting its showy blooms. Its leaves are narrow and ribbon-like, adapted for water environments.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in ponds and water gardens.
