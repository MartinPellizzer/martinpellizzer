Lonicera formanekiana subsp. hectoderma

Lonicera formanekiana subsp.

hectoderma, commonly known as the Hectoderma honeysuckle, is a rare shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring.

The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest understories, where it thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a smooth texture, while its flowers are usually yellow or orange in color.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance and ecological significance.
