Leptoderris achtenii

Leptoderris achtenii, commonly known as the African moonseed, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by distinctive, kidney-shaped fruits. Its botanical features include the presence of calcium oxalate crystals in its tissues, which are common in the Menispermaceae family.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
