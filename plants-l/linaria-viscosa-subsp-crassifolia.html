Linaria viscosa subsp. crassifolia

Linaria viscosa subsp.

crassifolia, commonly known as the rough toadflax, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It features erect, branched stems covered in glandular hairs, giving it a sticky texture.

The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers with two lips, typical of the Plantaginaceae family. Its leaves are linear, rough to the touch, and often have a bluish-green hue.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.
