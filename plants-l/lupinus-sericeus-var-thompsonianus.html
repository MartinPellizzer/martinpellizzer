Lupinus sericeus var. thompsonianus

Lupinus sericeus var. thompsonianus, commonly known as the Thompson's lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, showy purple or blue flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has finely hairy leaves and produces cylindrical pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
