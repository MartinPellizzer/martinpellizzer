Leucadendron acaulon

Leucadendron acaulon, commonly known as the silver tree, is a unique species native to the Western Cape of South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and dense, silvery-white foliage that gives it its common name. The plant has no true leaves, instead featuring modified stems called phyllodes that perform photosynthesis. Its flowers are small and yellow, forming cone-like structures at the top of the plant.

This species is well adapted to the arid conditions of its native habitat and is often found in fynbos vegetation.
