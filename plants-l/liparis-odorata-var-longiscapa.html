Liparis odorata var. longiscapa

Liparis odorata var. longiscapa, commonly known as the fragrant orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its inflorescence consists of a raceme of small, greenish-white flowers with a subtle, sweet scent.

The variety longiscapa is distinguished by its elongated, narrow leaves and slightly different floral structure compared to the typical form.
