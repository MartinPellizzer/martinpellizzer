Ladanum dubium

Ladanum dubium, commonly known as the rare desert shrub, is a perennial plant native to arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It features small, narrow leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. The plant produces yellow or white flowers during the spring season, which are typically tubular in shape. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access groundwater in nutrient-poor soils.

Ladanum dubium is valued for its resilience and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
