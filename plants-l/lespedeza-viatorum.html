Lespedeza viatorum

Lespedeza viatorum, commonly known as the slender lespedeza, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in open woods, fields, and along roadsides. The plant produces small, pink to reddish-purple flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a hairy texture.

Lespedeza viatorum is noted for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
