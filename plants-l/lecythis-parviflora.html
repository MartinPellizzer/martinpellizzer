Lecythis parviflora

Lecythis parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered lecythium, is a tropical tree native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces a heavy, oily fruit with a hard, spiky shell containing edible seeds. Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their potential health benefits.

The plant plays an important ecological role in supporting biodiversity in its native habitat.
