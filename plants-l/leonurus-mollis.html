Leonurus mollis

Leonurus mollis, commonly known as sweet everlasting, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features slender, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is aromatic and has been traditionally used in herbal remedies.

Leonurus mollis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
