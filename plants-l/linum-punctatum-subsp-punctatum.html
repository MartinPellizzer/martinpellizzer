Linum punctatum subsp. punctatum

Linum punctatum subsp.

punctatum, commonly known as the dotted flax, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a waxy coating. This species is typically found in rocky soils and arid environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its unique floral display.
