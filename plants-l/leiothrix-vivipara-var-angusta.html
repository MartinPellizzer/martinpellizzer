Leiothrix vivipara var. angusta

Leiothrix vivipara var. angusta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved velvet tamarisk, is a shrub native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woody structure and small, scale-like leaves that give it a velvety appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It is often found in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

This variety is noted for its adaptability and is sometimes used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and resilience.
