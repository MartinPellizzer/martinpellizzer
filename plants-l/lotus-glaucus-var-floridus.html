Lotus glaucus var. floridus

Lotus glaucus var. floridus, commonly known as the Florida lotus, is a perennial aquatic plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features striking white to pink flowers that bloom from summer to fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has broad, floating leaves that provide habitat for aquatic life and help in water filtration. Its rhizomes spread horizontally beneath the water, enabling it to colonize wetlands and ponds.

The Florida lotus is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits in freshwater ecosystems.
