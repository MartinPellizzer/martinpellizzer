Lupinus forslingii

Lupinus forslingii, commonly known as the desert lupine, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and is adapted to arid environments. It plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing food for native pollinators.

Lupinus forslingii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
