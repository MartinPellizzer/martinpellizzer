Lindernia dubia var. riparia

Lindernia dubia var. riparia, commonly known as the riparian lindernia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments along streams and riverbanks. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of pink to purple. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wetland habitats.

Lindernia dubia var. riparia is also used in ecological restoration projects due to its tolerance for fluctuating water levels.
