Lupinus bangii

Lupinus bangii, commonly known as the Bangi lupin, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of blue to violet pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds.

Lupinus bangii is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.
