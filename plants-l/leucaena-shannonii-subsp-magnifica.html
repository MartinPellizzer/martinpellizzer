Leucaena shannonii subsp. magnifica

Leucaena shannonii subsp.

magnifica, commonly known as the showy leucaena, is a shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean. It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and have a distinctive silvery underside.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of white and purple. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

Its rapid growth and nitrogen-fixing ability make it useful for soil stabilization and as a forage crop.
