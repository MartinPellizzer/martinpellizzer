Lonicera etrusca var. viscidula

Lonicera etrusca var. viscidula, commonly known as the Etruscan honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Europe.

It features slender, arching stems covered in a sticky, glandular pubescence, which gives it its specific epithet "viscidula.". The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, blooming in early spring. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly serrated margin.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
