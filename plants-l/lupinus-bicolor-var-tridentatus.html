Lupinus bicolor var. tridentatus

Lupinus bicolor var. tridentatus, commonly known as the tridentate lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves and clusters of violet or blue flowers. The plant has a deep taproot system, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils and arid environments. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity.

The plant is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.
