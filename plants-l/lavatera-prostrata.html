Lavatera prostrata

Lavatera prostrata, commonly known as the prostrate lavatera, is a low-growing shrub native to southern Africa.

It features soft, hairy leaves that are typically oval to lance-shaped, with a gray-green color. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, white, and purple, which bloom in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Lavatera prostrata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
