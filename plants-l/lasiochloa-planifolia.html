Lasiochloa planifolia

Lasiochloa planifolia, commonly known as the flat-leaf hairgrass, is a species of grass native to the wetlands and marshes of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and narrow, flat leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, spikelet-like inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, typical of many grasses. It thrives in moist, nutrient-poor soils and is often found in areas with standing water.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in wetland gardens and restoration projects.
