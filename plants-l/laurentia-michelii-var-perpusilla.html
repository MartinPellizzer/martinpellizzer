Laurentia michelii var. perpusilla

Laurentia michelii var. perpusilla, commonly known as the dwarf laurentia, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, clustered leaves that are typically bluish-green in color, with a dense, mat-like growth habit. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are usually white or pale pink, followed by small, spherical berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

This variant is distinguished by its compact size and is valued for its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
