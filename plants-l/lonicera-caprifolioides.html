Lonicera caprifolioides

Lonicera caprifolioides, commonly known as the common honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange, appearing in clusters. Its berries are red or orange and are an important food source for birds.

Lonicera caprifolioides is known for its vigorous growth and is often found in moist woodlands and along streambanks.
