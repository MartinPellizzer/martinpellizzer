Laggera tomentosa

Laggera tomentosa, commonly known as silky laggera, is a flowering plant native to Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, silver-green foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
