Lophiolepis ossetica subsp. tricholoma

Lophiolepis ossetica subsp.

tricholoma, commonly known as the Ossetian grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Caucasus region. It typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited for xerophytic conditions and ecological restoration projects.
