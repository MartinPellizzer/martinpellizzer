Lupinus gracilentus var. covillei

Lupinus gracilentus var. covillei, commonly known as the Coville lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and clusters of pea-like blue to purple flowers. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, including nitrogen fixation and habitat for pollinators.
