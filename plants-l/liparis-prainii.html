Liparis prainii

Liparis prainii, commonly known as the Prain's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. The plant features a slender, upright stem with several leaves arranged in a rosette pattern. Its flowers are small and clustered, often in shades of white, green, or pale yellow.

Liparis prainii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
