Lonicera hopeiensis

Lonicera hopeiensis, commonly known as the Hope's honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces red berries that are edible for birds but generally unpalatable to humans. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.

Lonicera hopeiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
