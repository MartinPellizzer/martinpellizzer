Litsea javanica

Litsea javanica, commonly known as the Java litsea, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green on top. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers followed by dark blue, berry-like fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests and secondary growth areas.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine and as a source of essential oils.
