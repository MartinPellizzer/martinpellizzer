Lacinaria scariosa var. scalaris

Lacinaria scariosa var. scalaris, commonly known as the scalycup lichen, is a foliose lichen that exhibits a distinctive, irregularly lobed thallus.

It typically grows on bark, stone, or soil in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate regions. The thallus is usually yellowish to orange in color, with a finely granular surface and a distinctive scalloped edge. It reproduces through the dispersal of powdery spores released from apothecia, which are small, disc-shaped structures.

This lichen is an important indicator species for environmental conditions and plays a role in nutrient cycling in forest ecosystems.
