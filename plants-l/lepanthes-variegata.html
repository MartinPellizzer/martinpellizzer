Lepanthes variegata

Lepanthes variegata, commonly known as the variegated orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its strikingly patterned leaves, which often display a mix of green, white, and yellow markings. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, typically arranged in a loose inflorescence. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Lepanthes variegata is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and unique foliage.
