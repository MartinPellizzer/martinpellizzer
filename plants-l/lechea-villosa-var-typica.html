Lechea villosa var. typica

Lechea villosa var. typica, commonly known as the hairy lechea, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It features thick, fleshy leaves covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers during the warmer months, adding a delicate touch to its otherwise rugged appearance. Its root system is shallow but extensive, allowing it to absorb moisture from the soil efficiently.

Due to its adaptability, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in dry gardens and rockeries.
