Leonurus macranthus var. pseudomacranthus

Leonurus macranthus var. pseudomacranthus, commonly known as the broad-leaved hedgehog plant, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features hairy, opposite leaves that are lanceolate to ovate in shape, often with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, pale purple to pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowering stems are usually erect and branched, with the inflorescences forming a spiky appearance.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
