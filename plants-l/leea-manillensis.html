Leea manillensis

Leea manillensis, commonly known as the Manila leea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Leeaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, which are often borne on short pedicels. It is often found growing in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental potential and is sometimes cultivated for its foliage and floral display.
