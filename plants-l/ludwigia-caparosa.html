Ludwigia caparosa

Ludwigia caparosa, commonly known as the scarlet root or redroot, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange tuberous roots, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are usually alternate, lance-shaped, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Ludwigia caparosa is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
