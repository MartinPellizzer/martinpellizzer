Lonchocarpus obtusus

Lonchocarpus obtusus, commonly known as the broadleaf senna, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces distinctive, inflated pods that contain seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and herbal practices.
