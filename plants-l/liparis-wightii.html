Liparis wightii

Liparis wightii, commonly known as the Wight's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive lip. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its small, clustered inflorescences.

Liparis wightii is often found in regions with high humidity and well-drained soils.
