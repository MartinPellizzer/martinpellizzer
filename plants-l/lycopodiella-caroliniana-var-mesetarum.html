Lycopodiella caroliniana var. mesetarum

Lycopodiella caroliniana var. mesetarum, commonly known as the Carolina clubmoss, is a small, evergreen fern-like plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, creeping growth habit and produces spore-bearing structures called strobila. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky areas. Its leaves are linear and arranged in whorls around the stem, giving it a club-like appearance.

This variety is native to the southeastern United States and is often found in acidic, well-drained soils.
