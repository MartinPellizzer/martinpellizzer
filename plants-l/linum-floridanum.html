Linum floridanum

Linum floridanum, commonly known as Florida flax, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces delicate blue or white flowers. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is often found in sandy or disturbed soils. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and has a fibrous root system.

Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
