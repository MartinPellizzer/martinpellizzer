Ludia faradifani

Ludia faradifani, commonly known as the Faradifan lily, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, supporting large, showy white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are long, narrow, and grass-like, forming a basal rosette.

Ludia faradifani is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
