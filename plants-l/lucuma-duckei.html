Lucuma duckei

Lucuma duckei, commonly known as the Andean lucuma, is a species of tree native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces large, sweet, yellow-orange fruits that are highly valued for their flavor and nutritional content. Its growth habit includes a tall, straight trunk with a broad, spreading crown.

Lucuma duckei thrives in cool, high-altitude environments with well-drained soil.
