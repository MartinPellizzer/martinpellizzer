Lycium apiculatum

Lycium apiculatum, commonly known as the Indian wolfberry or sandthorn, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces red berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits. Lycium apiculatum thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in ecological restoration and horticulture.
