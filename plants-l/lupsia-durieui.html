Lupsia durieui

Lupsia durieui, commonly known as the Durie's lupine, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes and is characterized by its pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in coastal and subalpine regions, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces clusters of blue to purple flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
