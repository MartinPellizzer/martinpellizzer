Lembotropis nigricans

Lembotropis nigricans, commonly known as the black lembotropis, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from deep purple to black. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically consumed by local wildlife. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.

Botanists are still studying its reproductive biology and ecological role in its native environment.
