Lankesteriana fractiflexa

Lankesteriana fractiflexa, commonly known as the flexile lankesterian, is a rare and striking orchid native to the cloud forests of Colombia.

It is characterized by its elegant, drooping inflorescences and vibrant, iridescent flowers that range from blue to violet. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are elongated and fleshy, adapted to store moisture in the humid, shaded environment.

Due to its unique beauty and limited distribution, it is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
