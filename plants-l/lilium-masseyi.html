Lilium masseyi

Lilium masseyi, commonly known as the Massey lily, is a rare and striking species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink to red. The plant produces a single, trumpet-shaped bloom at the top of the stem, which is often adorned with intricate patterns. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in specialized gardens due to its limited natural habitat.
