Lupinus alturasensis

Lupinus alturasensis, commonly known as the Altura lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Lupinus alturasensis is adapted to arid environments and is often found in areas with well-drained soils.
