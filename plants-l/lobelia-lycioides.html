Lobelia lycioides

Lobelia lycioides, commonly known as the smallleaf lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wet meadows.

Lobelia lycioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
