Lyonia ferruginea var. arborescens

Lyonia ferruginea var. arborescens, commonly known as the red-leaf lyonia, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive reddish-brown leaves, which often turn vibrant red in the fall. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.
