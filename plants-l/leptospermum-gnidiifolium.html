Leptospermum gnidiifolium

Leptospermum gnidiifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tea tree, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal regions. It produces small, edible berries that are used in traditional Aboriginal cuisine.

The species is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in herbal medicine.
