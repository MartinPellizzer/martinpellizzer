Lotus tetragonolobus subsp. palaestinus

Lotus tetragonolobus subsp.

palaestinus, commonly known as the Palestine lotus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Middle East and parts of North Africa. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its quadrangular stems and trifoliate leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in regions with limited water availability.

Its botanical features make it an important plant for ecological studies and traditional uses in the regions where it is found.
