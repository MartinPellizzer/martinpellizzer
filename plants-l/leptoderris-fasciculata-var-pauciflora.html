Leptoderris fasciculata var. pauciflora

Leptoderris fasciculata var. pauciflora, commonly known as the slender-leaved sandalwood, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by woody, fragrant fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its aromatic properties, it has been used traditionally in perfumery and traditional medicine.
