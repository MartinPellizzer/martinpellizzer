Lobivia densispina f. albiflora

Lobivia densispina f. albiflora, commonly known as the White-flowered Lobivia, is a rare and striking cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its dense spines and compact, globular shape. The plant features showy white flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer, adding a dramatic contrast to its green foliage. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, and it stores water in its thick, fleshy stems.

This cultivar is highly sought after by collectors due to its unique appearance and rarity in the wild.
