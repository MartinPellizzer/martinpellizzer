Lomariocycas decrescens

Lomariocycas decrescens, commonly known as the dwarf cycad, is a rare and ancient plant species native to the humid lowland forests of Madagascar.

It is a member of the Cycadaceae family, characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of feathery, pinnate leaves. The plant produces large, cone-like structures, with male and female cones typically occurring on separate plants. Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is classified as critically endangered, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.

Its unique morphology and ecological significance make it a valuable subject for botanical research.
