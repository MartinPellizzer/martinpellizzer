Lepanthes lanceolata

Lepanthes lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceolate orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in the understory of tropical forests. It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is known for its intricate floral structure.

Lepanthes lanceolata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
