Lauro-cerasus maackii

Lauro-cerasus maackii, commonly known as the Chinese cherry laurel, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Prunus genus and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage that remains green throughout the year. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in spring, followed by edible berries that are dark purple when mature. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to a variety of soil types.

However, it can be invasive in some regions, making it a plant of both aesthetic and ecological interest.
