Lightfootia serrata

Lightfootia serrata, commonly known as the serrated lightfoot.

It is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly South Africa. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, giving it its common name. It produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, often with a slight fragrance.

Lightfootia serrata is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and modest flowering display.
