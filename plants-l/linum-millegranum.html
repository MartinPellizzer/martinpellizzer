Linum millegranum

Linum millegranum, commonly known as the common flax.

It is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. The plant features slender, erect stems with lance-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy. Its flowers are small, blue or purple, and arranged in spikes, typical of the Linaceae family.

The seeds of Linum millegranum are encased in a tough, fibrous husk, which has historically been used for fiber production.
