Lippia ilan-ilan

Lippia ilan-ilan, commonly known as wild mint or Indian borage, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has a creeping habit with hairy stems. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.

Lippia ilan-ilan is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in various soil types.
