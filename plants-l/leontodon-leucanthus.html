Leontodon leucanthus

Leontodon leucanthus, commonly known as the white-headed dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads made up of many small florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its bright yellow flowers are followed by fluffy white seed heads, which are dispersed by the wind.

The species is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
