Lactuca agrestis

Lactuca agrestis, commonly known as wild lettuce, is a flowering plant in the dandelion family.

It is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and it grows in a variety of habitats including roadsides and disturbed soils. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and has leaves that are often lobed or toothed. Its milky sap contains lactucin, which has mild sedative properties.

It is often used in herbal medicine for its potential calming effects.
