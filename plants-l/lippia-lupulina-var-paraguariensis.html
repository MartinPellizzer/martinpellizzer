Lippia lupulina var. paraguariensis

Lippia lupulina var. paraguariensis, commonly known as yerba mate, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Argentina, Brazil, and Paraguay.

It is characterized by its aromatic, serrated leaves and small white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded, moist environments. Its leaves are the primary part used, containing compounds such as caffeine and saponins.

The botanical structure supports its traditional use in herbal teas and medicinal preparations.
