Loranthus lanosus

Loranthus lanosus, commonly known as the woolly mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily in the genus Eucalyptus.

It possesses specialized structures called haustoria that penetrate the host's vascular system to extract water and nutrients. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are often consumed by birds.

Due to its parasitic nature and specific ecological requirements, Loranthus lanosus plays a unique role in the ecosystems where it is found.
