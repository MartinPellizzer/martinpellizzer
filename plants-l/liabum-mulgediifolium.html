Liabum mulgediifolium

Liabum mulgediifolium, commonly known as the silverleaf liabum, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which helps it retain moisture in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens that emphasize drought tolerance and ornamental value.
