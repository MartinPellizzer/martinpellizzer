Leptochloa uninervia

Leptochloa uninervia, commonly known as the slender false brome, is a grass species native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats. Its flowering period usually occurs in the spring or early summer, producing delicate, greenish-white flowers.

Leptochloa uninervia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticultural settings.
