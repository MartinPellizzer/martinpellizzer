Lithospermum arvense var. multicaule

Lithospermum arvense var. multicaule, commonly known as field lithosperm, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and has a sprawling, multi-stemmed growth habit. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces and produces small, reddish-purple flowers in clusters. Its roots contain compounds that have been traditionally used in herbal medicine.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
