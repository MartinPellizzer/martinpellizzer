Lavoisiera pulcherrima var. macrocarpa

Lavoisiera pulcherrima var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant lavoisiera, is a striking evergreen shrub native to the humid forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, leathery, elliptical leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by showy, orange-red berries. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is a popular choice for tropical and subtropical gardens.
