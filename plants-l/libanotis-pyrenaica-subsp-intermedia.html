Libanotis pyrenaica subsp. intermedia

Libanotis pyrenaica subsp.

intermedia, commonly known as the Pyrenean bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Pyrenees region. It features lanceolate leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem.

The plant produces blue to purple bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. It prefers well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
