Lobelia feayana

Lobelia feayana, commonly known as the Feay's lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and swampy areas. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Lobelia feayana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
