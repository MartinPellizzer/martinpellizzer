Lobelia cirsiifolia

Lobelia cirsiifolia, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, sky-blue to violet flowers that bloom in dense, spike-like clusters from late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a soft, silvery down. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Lobelia cirsiifolia is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
