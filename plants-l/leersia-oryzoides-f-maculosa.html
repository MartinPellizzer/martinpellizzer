Leersia oryzoides f. maculosa

Leersia oryzoides f. maculosa, commonly known as the spotted leersia, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered with a fine layer of hair. The inflorescence consists of a single spikelet, which is typically adorned with purple or brown markings, giving it its distinguishing appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along water bodies.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in ecological systems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
