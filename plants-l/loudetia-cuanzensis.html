Loudetia cuanzensis

Loudetia cuanzensis, commonly known as the Cuanza grass, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and potential ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
