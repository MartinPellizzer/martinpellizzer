Liatris squarrosa var. intermedia

Liatris squarrosa var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate blazing star, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies, meadows, and disturbed soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces showy pink to purple flower heads in late summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
