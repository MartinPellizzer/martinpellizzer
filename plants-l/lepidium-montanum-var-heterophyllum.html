Lepidium montanum var. heterophyllum

Lepidium montanum var. heterophyllum, commonly known as the mountain cress, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It typically grows in moist, rocky soils and is characterized by its low, spreading growth habit. The plant features small, white or yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are usually alternate and pinnately divided. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
