Lepidium oppositifolium

Lepidium oppositifolium, commonly known as the opposite-leaved cress, is a small flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to the western United States and is characterized by its opposite arrangement of small, oval leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its fruit is a dry, segmented capsule that contains several small, elongated seeds.

Lepidium oppositifolium is often found in moist, rocky soils and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
