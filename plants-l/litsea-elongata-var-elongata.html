Litsea elongata var. elongata

Litsea elongata var. elongata, commonly known as the longleaf litsea, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth margin. Its small, white flowers are fragrant and appear in clusters, followed by small, round berries.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aromatic foliage and seasonal interest.
