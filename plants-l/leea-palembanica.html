Leea palembanica

Leea palembanica, commonly known as the Palembang leea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Leeaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white to yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is typically found in tropical rainforests and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

The fruit is a capsule that releases small, winged seeds when mature.
