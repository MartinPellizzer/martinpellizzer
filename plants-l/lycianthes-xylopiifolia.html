Lycianthes xylopiifolia

Lycianthes xylopiifolia, commonly known as the Mexican nightshade, is a shrub native to Central America and northern South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by orange or red berries containing toxic seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in dry, open areas.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional medicine practices.
