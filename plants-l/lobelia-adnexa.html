Lobelia adnexa

Lobelia adnexa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The leaves are typically narrow and opposite, with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
