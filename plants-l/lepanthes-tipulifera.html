Lepanthes tipulifera

Lepanthes tipulifera, commonly known as the fly orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and delicate, greenish-white flowers that resemble those of the fly orchid genus. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowers are pollinated by small flies, which are attracted by the flower's scent and appearance.

Lepanthes tipulifera is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its unique ecological adaptations.
