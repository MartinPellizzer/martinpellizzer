Litobrochia appendiculata

Litobrochia appendiculata, commonly known as the coastal bladderwort, is a small aquatic plant found in brackish and freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its delicate, floating leaves and specialized structures called bladders, which are used for capturing small aquatic organisms. The plant has a branched root system that helps it anchor in soft sediments. It thrives in shallow waters with moderate salinity and is often found in estuaries and coastal regions.

Litobrochia appendiculata plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and contributing to nutrient cycling.
