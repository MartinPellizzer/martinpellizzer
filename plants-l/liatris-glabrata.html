Liatris glabrata

Liatris glabrata, commonly known as slender blazingstar, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy purple or pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along streambanks. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and the flowers are arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters.

It serves as a valuable nectar source for pollinators like bees and butterflies.
