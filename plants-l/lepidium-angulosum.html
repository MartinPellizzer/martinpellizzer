Lepidium angulosum

Lepidium angulosum, commonly known as the angular cress, is a small annual plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its branched stems and small, green, oval leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by round, flat pods containing several seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along riverbanks.

Lepidium angulosum is known for its ability to tolerate various environmental conditions, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
