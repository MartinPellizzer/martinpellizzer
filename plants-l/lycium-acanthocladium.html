Lycium acanthocladium

Lycium acanthocladium, commonly known as the spiny boxthorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds and small mammals. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Lycium acanthocladium is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
