Luisia striata

Luisia striata, commonly known as the striped luisia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink to purple. Luisia striata is well adapted to arid environments and is drought-tolerant once established.

It is often used in xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and ornamental value.
