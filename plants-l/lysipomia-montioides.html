Lysipomia montioides

Lysipomia montioides, commonly known as the Andean pearlweed, is a flowering plant native to the high Andes of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at elevations above 3,000 meters. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in alpine meadows and mountainous regions.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
