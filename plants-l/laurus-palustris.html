Laurus palustris

Laurus palustris, commonly known as sweet bay or swamp bay, is a deciduous shrub native to wetland areas of Europe and western Asia.

It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark, fleshy berries that are edible but not commonly consumed. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in marshy or swampy habitats.

Its historical use dates back to ancient times, where it was valued for its fragrance and medicinal properties.
