Lepidium hyssopifolium var. epilosum

Lepidium hyssopifolium var. epilosum, commonly known as the hairy wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to yellow. The leaves are usually hairy and lance-shaped, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
