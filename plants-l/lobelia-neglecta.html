Lobelia neglecta

Lobelia neglecta, commonly known as the common lobelia, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features small, tubular blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Lobelia neglecta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
