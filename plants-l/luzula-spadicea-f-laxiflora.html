Luzula spadicea f. laxiflora

Luzula spadicea f. laxiflora, commonly known as the laxiflora form of the common wood rush, is a variant of a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences that bear pale green to white flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ecologically significant species within its native habitats.
