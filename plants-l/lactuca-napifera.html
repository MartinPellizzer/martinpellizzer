Lactuca napifera

Lactuca napifera, commonly known as the leaf lettuce, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family.

It is characterized by its delicate, edible leaves that are often used in salads and other culinary applications. The plant typically grows in cool climates and prefers well-drained soil. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Lactuca napifera is also known for its historical use in traditional medicine.
