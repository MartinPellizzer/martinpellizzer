Lupinus paniculatus var. parvifolius

Lupinus paniculatus var. parvifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has compound leaves composed of small, narrow leaflets, which contribute to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in nitrogen fixation, benefiting soil fertility.
