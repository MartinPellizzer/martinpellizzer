Lepisanthes acuminata

Lepisanthes acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-pointed orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stem and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

The orchid is noted for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
