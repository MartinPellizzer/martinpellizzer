Lepigonum caninum var. urbicum

Lepigonum caninum var. urbicum, commonly known as the urban foxglove, is a flowering plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in disturbed urban environments such as cracks in pavement or along building foundations. The plant features tall, erect stems with opposite, ovate leaves that have a rough texture. Its showy, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is well adapted to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and can tolerate pollution, making it a common sight in city settings.
