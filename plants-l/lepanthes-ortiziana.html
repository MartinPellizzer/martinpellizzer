Lepanthes ortiziana

Lepanthes ortiziana, commonly known as the orchid of the Sierra de las Minas, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically display a combination of white, green, and pink hues. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments within tropical rainforests, often growing on trees or rocks. Its leaves are usually ovate and covered in a fine, velvety texture.

Lepanthes ortiziana is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its habitat.
