Lophophytum

Lophophytum, commonly known as the flame plant, is a tropical plant belonging to the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in warm, humid climates. The plant is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that resemble flames, which bloom during the summer months. Its leaves are typically oval and glossy, providing a lush green backdrop to its vibrant blooms.

Lophophytum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its eye-catching appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
