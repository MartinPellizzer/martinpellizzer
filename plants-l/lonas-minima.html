Lonas minima

Lonas minima, commonly known as the dwarf lily of the valley, is a small herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It features delicate, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale green, blooming in early summer. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and grows close to the ground, often in shaded, moist environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit.
