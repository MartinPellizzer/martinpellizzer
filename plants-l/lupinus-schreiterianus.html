Lupinus schreiterianus

Lupinus schreiterianus, commonly known as the Schreiter's lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and spiny stems. The plant produces clusters of bright blue to purple pea-shaped flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Lupinus schreiterianus is often found in open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.
