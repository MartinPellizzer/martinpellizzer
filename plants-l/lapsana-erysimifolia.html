Lapsana erysimifolia

Lapsana erysimifolia, commonly known as the common yellowhead, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant features slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its yellow-orange flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Lapsana erysimifolia is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and meadows.
