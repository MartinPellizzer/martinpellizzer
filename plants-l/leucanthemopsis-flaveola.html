Leucanthemopsis flaveola

Leucanthemopsis flaveola, commonly known as the yellow leucanthemum, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features daisy-like flowers with yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
