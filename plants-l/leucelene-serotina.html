Leucelene serotina

Leucelene serotina, commonly known as the late-flowering leucelene, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in grassy areas and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, white or pale blue flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it blooms from late summer into early autumn. It is adapted to arid conditions and can thrive in well-drained soils.

Leucelene serotina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
