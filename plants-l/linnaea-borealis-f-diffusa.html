Linnaea borealis f. diffusa

Linnaea borealis f. diffusa, commonly known as the variegated twinflower, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to northern regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, often with a yellowish hue. The plant has opposite, elliptical leaves that are usually dark green with a waxy coating. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in boreal and subarctic habitats.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
