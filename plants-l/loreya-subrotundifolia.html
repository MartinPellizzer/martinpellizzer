Loreya subrotundifolia

Loreya subrotundifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed loreya, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, rounded leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest understories. Its botanical features include a woody stem and opposite leaf arrangement.

Loreya subrotundifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
