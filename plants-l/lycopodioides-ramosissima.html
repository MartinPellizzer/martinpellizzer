Lycopodioides ramosissima

Lycopodioides ramosissima, commonly known as the branched clubmoss, is a small, evergreen plant belonging to the family Lycopodiales.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its distinctive, branched structure that resembles a miniature tree. The plant produces small, scale-like leaves and has spore-bearing structures called strobila, which are typically clustered at the tips of its branches. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Lycopodioides ramosissima plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
