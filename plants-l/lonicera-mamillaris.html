Lonicera mamillaris

Lonicera mamillaris, commonly known as the creeping honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats or ground cover. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically white or yellow, followed by red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for erosion control.

Its botanical features make it a versatile plant for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
