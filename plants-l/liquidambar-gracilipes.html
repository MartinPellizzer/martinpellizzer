Liquidambar gracilipes

Liquidambar gracilipes, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sweet gum, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Aceraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, spiky, spherical fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

Liquidambar gracilipes is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
