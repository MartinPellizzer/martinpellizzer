Lythrum glaucescens

Lythrum glaucescens, commonly known as purple loosestrife, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy spikes of purple or pink flowers that bloom from midsummer to early autumn. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are opposite and often covered in a waxy, bluish coating. It thrives in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, preferring moist, sunny environments.

However, it is considered an invasive species in North America, where it outcompetes native plants and disrupts local ecosystems.
