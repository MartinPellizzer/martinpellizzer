Lepidium fremontii var. stipitatum

Lepidium fremontii var. stipitatum, commonly known as the Fremont's cress, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its small, white flowers bloom in early spring, and it produces small, rounded seed pods.

The species is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which help reduce water loss in dry climates.
