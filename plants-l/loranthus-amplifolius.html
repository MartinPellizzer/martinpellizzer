Loranthus amplifolius

Loranthus amplifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of host trees.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own carbohydrates through photosynthesis using the sunlight it captures. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces white or yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. Its berries are often red or yellow and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This species is found in various regions of Asia and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
