Lythrum dacotanum

Lythrum dacotanum, commonly known as the eastern purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lythraceae family and is characterized by its showy, violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture, while its stems are erect and can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
