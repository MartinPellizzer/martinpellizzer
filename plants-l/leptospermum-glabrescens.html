Leptospermum glabrescens

Leptospermum glabrescens, commonly known as the silky tea tree, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its small, dark green leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, woody fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and dry inland regions.

Leptospermum glabrescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
