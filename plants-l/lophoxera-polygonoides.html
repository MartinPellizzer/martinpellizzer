Lophoxera polygonoides

Lophoxera polygonoides, commonly known as the slender-leaved sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open fields and along roadsides.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a nectar source for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
