Leptotaenia californica var. platycarpa

Leptotaenia californica var. platycarpa, commonly known as the broad-fruited salsify, is a perennial herb native to California.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its robust, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, elongated, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland habitats.

The variety platycarpa is distinguished by its broader, more fleshy fruits compared to other varieties of the species.
