Lepanthes zettleri

Lepanthes zettleri, commonly known as the Zettleri's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, which bloom during the rainy season. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the air and decaying organic matter.

Lepanthes zettleri is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
