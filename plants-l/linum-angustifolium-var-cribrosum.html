Linum angustifolium var. cribrosum

Linum angustifolium var. cribrosum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flax or the Australian flax, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and produces clusters of small, blue or purple flowers. The plant is part of the Linaceae family and is often found in coastal and sandy soils. It has a fibrous stem and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

The variety cribrosum is distinguished by its finely toothed leaves and is sometimes used in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal.
