Liparis terrestris

Liparis terrestris, commonly known as the terrestrial orchid, is a species of orchid native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a low-growing plant with a rhizomatous root system and produces small, greenish or brownish flowers that are often clustered in a spike. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are usually broad and grass-like, and it is known for its ability to form dense colonies.

Liparis terrestris is characterized by its unique floral structure and its role in supporting local ecosystems.
