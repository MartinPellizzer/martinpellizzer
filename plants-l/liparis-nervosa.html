Liparis nervosa

Liparis nervosa, commonly known as the slender lady's tresses, is a perennial orchid native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and dense, dark green, thread-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a complex root system and produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in a loose, curved spike.

Liparis nervosa is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its delicate appearance and ecological significance in its native habitats.
