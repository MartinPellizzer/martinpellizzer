Linanthus androsaceus subsp. luteolus

Linanthus androsaceus subsp.

luteolus, commonly known as the yellow sandverbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a low-growing perennial herb with small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant has a mat-forming growth habit, with hairy stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped. It thrives in well-drained sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in desert or arid environments.
