Loroma

Loroma, commonly known as the "flower of the desert," is a unique plant native to arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from violet to blue. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. Loroma thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Its blooming period typically occurs during the dry season, adding a splash of color to otherwise barren landscapes.
