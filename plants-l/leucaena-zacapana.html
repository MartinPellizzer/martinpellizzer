Leucaena zacapana

Leucaena zacapana, commonly known as the Zacapana leucaena, is a shrub or small tree native to Central America and parts of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of forage for livestock.

The plant is also valued for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its potential in reforestation efforts.
