Lygodium smithianum

Lygodium smithianum, commonly known as the climbing fern, is a tropical fern species characterized by its distinctive, palm-like fronds.

It belongs to the family Lygodiaceae and is native to regions of Southeast Asia, including India and Malaysia. The plant exhibits a climbing growth habit, using its wiry stems to ascend trees and other structures. Its fronds are typically divided into multiple pinnae, giving it a lush, feathery appearance.

Lygodium smithianum thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.
