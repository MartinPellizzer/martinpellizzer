Lepanthes tatamae

Lepanthes tatamae, commonly known as the Tatamae orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Colombia.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid with delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of white and purple. The plant typically blooms during the wet season, showcasing its unique floral structure with a prominent spur. Lepanthes tatamae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its aesthetic appeal.

Its growth is closely tied to the specific microclimates of its native habitat, making it a rare and sought-after species.
