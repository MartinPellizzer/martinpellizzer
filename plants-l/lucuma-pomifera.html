Lucuma pomifera

Lucuma pomifera, commonly known as the lucuma fruit, is a species of tree native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its large, yellow-orange, egg-shaped fruit with a sweet, creamy texture and a mild, nutty flavor. The fruit contains a single large seed surrounded by a dense, edible pulp. Lucuma is rich in nutrients, including vitamins, minerals, and dietary fiber, making it a valuable food source.

It is often used in both culinary and traditional medicinal applications due to its unique properties.
