Lupinus magdalenensis var. seifrizianus

Lupinus magdalenensis var. seifrizianus, commonly known as the Magdalena lupine, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces spikes of pea-like flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, which bloom in the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or mountainous habitats.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive floral display.
