Lobelia kalmii var. caroliniana

Lobelia kalmii var. caroliniana, commonly known as Carolina lobelia, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, erect stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
