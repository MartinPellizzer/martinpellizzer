Libanotis verticillata

Libanotis verticillata, commonly known as the mountain daphne, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form with dark green, leathery leaves arranged in whorls around the stems. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale pink flowers in clusters during the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and resilience, it is often cultivated in gardens across temperate climates.
