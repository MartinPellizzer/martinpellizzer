Leucadendron speciosum

Leucadendron speciosum, commonly known as the silver tree, is a striking evergreen shrub or small tree native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray, flaky bark and dense, rounded foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces bright yellow or orange spherical flower heads during the winter months, which are a key feature for identification. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its unique aesthetic and resilience.
