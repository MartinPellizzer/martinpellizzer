Linum hispanicum f. cribrosum

Linum hispanicum f. cribrosum, commonly known as the Spanish flax or hairy flax, is a rare and ornamental plant belonging to the Linaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, delicate blue or white flowers. The plant features a distinctive hairy texture on its leaves and stems, which gives it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This cultivar is often grown for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and as a subject of botanical study due to its unique morphology.
