Lindera nacusua

Lindera nacusua, commonly known as the Florida spicebush, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurel and avocado trees. The plant features opposite, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green, often with a slight aromatic scent. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible for some bird species.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands.
