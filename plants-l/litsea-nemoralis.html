Litsea nemoralis

Litsea nemoralis, commonly known as the European lime or bird cherry, is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and avocado. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a glossy green upper surface. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for birds.

The tree is often found in woodlands and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
