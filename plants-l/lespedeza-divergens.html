Lespedeza divergens

Lespedeza divergens, commonly known as silverleaf lespedeza, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, fluffy white or silver-gray flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet being oval-shaped and slightly hairy. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woods and disturbed areas.

Lespedeza divergens is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it a valuable plant in ecological restoration projects.
