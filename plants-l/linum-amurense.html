Linum amurense

Linum amurense, commonly known as Siberian flax, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Linum amurense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
