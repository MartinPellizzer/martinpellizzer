Lobelia biflora

Lobelia biflora, commonly known as the twinflower lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, tubular blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Lobelia biflora has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

It plays an important role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
