Lamourouxia linearis

Lamourouxia linearis, commonly known as the slender lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that can grow up to 60 centimeters in height, adorned with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces showy, white or pale pink flowers with six petals, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

Due to its delicate appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
