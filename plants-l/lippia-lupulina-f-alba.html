Lippia lupulina f. alba

Lippia lupulina f. alba, commonly known as white lemon verbena, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-green leaves that emit a strong lemon scent when crushed. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for Mediterranean climates.

Due to its pleasant aroma and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and used in aromatherapy.
