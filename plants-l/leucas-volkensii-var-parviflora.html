Leucas volkensii var. parviflora

Leucas volkensii var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered leucas, is a perennial herb native to East Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is typically found in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils.
