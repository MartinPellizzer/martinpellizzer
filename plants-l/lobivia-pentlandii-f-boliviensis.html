Lobivia pentlandii f. boliviensis

Lobivia pentlandii f. boliviensis, commonly known as the Bolivian lobivia, is a compact, low-growing cactus native to the Andean regions of Bolivia.

It features short, cylindrical stems with a distinctive ribbed structure and is covered in white to pale green spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its otherwise muted foliage. It is well adapted to arid environments, with succulent stems that store water efficiently.

This unique cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often grown in rock gardens or as a potted plant.
