Liabum candidum

Liabum candidum, commonly known as the white milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry grasslands and rocky areas. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.

Liabum candidum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscapes.
