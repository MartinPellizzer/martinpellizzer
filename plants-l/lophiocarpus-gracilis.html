Lophiocarpus gracilis

Lophiocarpus gracilis, commonly known as the slender legume, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are bipinnate, and the flowers are small, clustered in racemes.

The seeds are enclosed in slender, curved pods, which contribute to its common name.
