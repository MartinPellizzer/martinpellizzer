Luzama trifurcata

Luzama trifurcata, commonly known as the three-branched cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its spiny, branching stems that store water, adapting to its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, which help reduce water loss.

Luzama trifurcata is often found in rocky soils and is well-suited to desert ecosystems.
