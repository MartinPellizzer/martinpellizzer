Lotononis transvaalensis

Lotononis transvaalensis, commonly known as the Transvaal daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow and white. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and thrives in well-drained soils, often found in rocky or sandy habitats. It is a perennial herb that produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes in regions with a Mediterranean climate.
