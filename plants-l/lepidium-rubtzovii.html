Lepidium rubtzovii

Lepidium rubtzovii, commonly known as the Siberian cress, is a flowering plant native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress species. The plant is characterized by its small, white or pale yellow flowers and its finely divided, fern-like leaves. Lepidium rubtzovii typically grows in moist, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subarctic regions.

It is valued for its nutritional content and has been used in traditional medicine.
