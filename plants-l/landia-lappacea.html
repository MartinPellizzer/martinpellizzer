Landia lappacea

Landia lappacea, commonly known as the Arctic bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features rosettes of lance-shaped, hairy leaves and produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually blue or purple in color. It thrives in harsh, cold environments and is adapted to grow in rocky or sandy soils.

Landia lappacea plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat, supporting pollinators and contributing to the biodiversity of alpine and tundra ecosystems.
