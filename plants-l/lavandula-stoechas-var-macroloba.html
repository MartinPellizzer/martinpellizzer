Lavandula stoechas var. macroloba

Lavandula stoechas var. macroloba, commonly known as the Spanish lavender or giant-leaf lavender, is a perennial shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers and large, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and thrives in full sun with well-drained soil. Its aromatic foliage and showy blooms make it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Botanically, it belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its fragrant herbs and medicinal properties.
