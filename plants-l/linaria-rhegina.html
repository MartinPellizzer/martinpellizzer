Linaria rhegina

Linaria rhegina, commonly known as the Alpine toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of bright yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. Its flowers are tubular and attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Linaria rhegina is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
