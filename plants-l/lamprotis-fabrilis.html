Lamprotis fabrilis

Lamprotis fabrilis, commonly known as the golden star orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia and is characterized by its striking yellow petals and a distinctive central star-shaped lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom during the summer months, producing a sweet, fragrant scent that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The botanical features of Lamprotis fabrilis make it a popular choice for both horticultural collections and botanical research.
