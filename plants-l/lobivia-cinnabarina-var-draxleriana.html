Lobivia cinnabarina var. draxleriana

Lobivia cinnabarina var. draxleriana, commonly known as the Draxler's Lobivia, is a compact, spineless succulent native to the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It features dense, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a reddish or pinkish tinge, especially when exposed to sunlight. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the cooler months, adding a delicate contrast to its foliage. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its leaves and stems.

Due to its slow growth and unique appearance, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
