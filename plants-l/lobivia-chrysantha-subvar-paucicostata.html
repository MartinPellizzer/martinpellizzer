Lobivia chrysantha subvar. paucicostata

Lobivia chrysantha subvar. paucicostata, commonly known as the yellow-tipped lobivia, is a succulent native to the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It features a short, cylindrical stem with a greenish-gray color and produces clusters of yellow flowers during the spring. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that help it store water, adapting to the arid environment. Its flowers are typically tubular and attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

This subvariety is prized for its compact size and vibrant floral display in rock gardens and arid landscapes.
