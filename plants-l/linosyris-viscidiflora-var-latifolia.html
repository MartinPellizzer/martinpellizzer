Linosyris viscidiflora var. latifolia

Linosyris viscidiflora var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved stickyflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a sticky substance, which helps deter herbivores. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its unique morphology and adaptive traits make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
