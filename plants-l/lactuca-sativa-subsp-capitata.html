Lactuca sativa subsp. capitata

Lactuca sativa subsp.

capitata, commonly known as romaine lettuce, is a leafy vegetable characterized by its crisp, dark green leaves and a mild, slightly bitter flavor. It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is cultivated for its edible leaves, which are typically consumed raw in salads.

The plant has a rosette growth habit, with the leaves forming a dense, upright structure. It thrives in cool climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Romaine lettuce is rich in nutrients such as vitamins A and K, and it is often used as a base for various culinary dishes.
