Linaria elatine var. hamosa

Linaria elatine var. hamosa, commonly known as the common sand-verbena, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features slender, branching stems covered with fine hairs and produces small, yellow-orange flowers in clusters. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slightly hairy surface.

The species is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.
