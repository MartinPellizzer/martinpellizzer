Linaria badalii f. odoratissima

Linaria badalii f. odoratissima, commonly known as the fragrant toadflax, is a flowering plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its most distinctive trait is its strong, pleasant fragrance, which is most noticeable when the flowers are in full bloom.

This cultivar is prized in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
