Lupinus perbonus

Lupinus perbonus, commonly known as the showy lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of vibrant blue to purple flowers. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. Lupinus perbonus is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and coastal areas.
