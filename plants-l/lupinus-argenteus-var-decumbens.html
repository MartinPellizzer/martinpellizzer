Lupinus argenteus var. decumbens

Lupinus argenteus var. decumbens, commonly known as silver lupine or silver bush lupine, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features silvery-gray foliage due to dense pubescence, and its flowers are typically blue to purple, arranged in dense spikes. The plant is adapted to rocky and dry soils, often found in open woodlands and grasslands. It belongs to the legume family (Fabaceae) and has the ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through its root nodules.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
