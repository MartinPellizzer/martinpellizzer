Leontodon bourgaeanus

Leontodon bourgaeanus, commonly known as the Bourgaean's bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive bell-shaped appearance.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
