Lemboglossum ehrenbergii

Lemboglossum ehrenbergii, commonly known as the desert feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and tufted inflorescences that resemble plumes. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low moisture conditions. Its reproductive structures are typically wind-pollinated, and it plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils.

Lemboglossum ehrenbergii is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.
