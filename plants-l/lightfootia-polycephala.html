Lightfootia polycephala

Lightfootia polycephala, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of showy, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of purple, blue, and white. The plant typically grows as a perennial herb, often reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine layer of hairs, which help to retain moisture.

Lightfootia polycephala is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
