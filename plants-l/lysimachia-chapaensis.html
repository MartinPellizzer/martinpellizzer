Lysimachia chapaensis

Lysimachia chapaensis, commonly known as Chinese chickweed or mountain chickweed, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing habit with opposite, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in spring and summer. Lysimachia chapaensis is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky slopes.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
