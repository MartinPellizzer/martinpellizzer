Lucuma goudotiana

Lucuma goudotiana, commonly known as the Andean lucuma, is a species of flowering plant native to the highlands of Peru.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its evergreen shrub form with glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces yellow-orange fruits that are encased in a thick, leathery rind. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in axillary positions.

Lucuma goudotiana is valued for its fruit, which is rich in nutrients and has a sweet, creamy flavor.
