Loeflingia pusilla

Loeflingia pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf loeflingia, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It typically grows to a height of only a few centimeters, with delicate, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually white or pale green, arranged in clusters at the tips of the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and along stream banks.

Loeflingia pusilla is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed soils, making it a common sight in early successional habitats.
