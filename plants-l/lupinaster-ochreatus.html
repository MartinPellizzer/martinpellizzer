Lupinaster ochreatus

Lupinaster ochreatus, commonly known as the golden lupin, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves, which are typical of many leguminous species. The plant is native to southern Africa and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure. Its growth habit is upright, with stems that can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

Lupinaster ochreatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plants.
