Lasianthus batangensis var. longipetiolatus

Lasianthus batangensis var. longipetiolatus, commonly known as the long-petioled lasianthus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Acanthaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated petioles that support its lance-shaped leaves, which are typically arranged oppositely along the stem. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom during the wet season. It is native to the Philippines and thrives in moist, shaded environments within tropical forests.

This variant is notable for its distinct morphological features and ecological significance in its native habitat.
