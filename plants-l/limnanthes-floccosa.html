Limnanthes floccosa

Limnanthes floccosa, commonly known as the woolly buttercup, is a flowering plant native to western North America.

It is characterized by its soft, white, woolly foliage and bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers. The plant thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found near water sources such as ponds and marshes. It is a perennial species that produces seeds in small, dry capsules.

The woolly buttercup is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
