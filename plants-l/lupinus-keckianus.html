Lupinus keckianus

Lupinus keckianus, commonly known as Keck's lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and showy, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Lupinus keckianus plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by fixing nitrogen in the soil.
