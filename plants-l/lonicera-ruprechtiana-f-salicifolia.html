Lonicera ruprechtiana f. salicifolia

Lonicera ruprechtiana f. salicifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf honeysuckle, is a unique variation of the common honeysuckle.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-green foliage, which gives it a striking appearance in gardens. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It is a deciduous shrub that thrives in partial shade to full sun, making it adaptable to various garden settings.

Its ornamental value comes from both its foliage and its sweet-smelling blossoms.
