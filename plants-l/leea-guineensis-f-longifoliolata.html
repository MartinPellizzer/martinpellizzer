Leea guineensis f. longifoliolata

Leea guineensis f. longifoliolata, commonly known as the long-leaved leea, is a variant of the species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

This plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks.
