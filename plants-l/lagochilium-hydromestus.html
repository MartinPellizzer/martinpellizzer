Lagochilium hydromestus

Lagochilium hydromestus, commonly known as the water orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and dense clusters of violet or purple flowers that bloom during the rainy season. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in cloud forests and along riverbanks. Its leaves are elongated and glossy, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a valued species in both horticulture and conservation efforts.
