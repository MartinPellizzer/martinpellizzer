Lasiospora candidissima

Lasiospora candidissima, commonly known as the white silky lichen, is a foliose lichen characterized by its pale, woolly appearance.

It typically grows on tree trunks and branches in humid, temperate forests. The plant's thallus is composed of a symbiotic relationship between a fungus and algae, which allows it to thrive in nutrient-poor environments. Its structure includes a leathery upper layer and a more porous lower layer.

Lasiospora candidissima plays a role in ecological succession and serves as an indicator of air quality.
