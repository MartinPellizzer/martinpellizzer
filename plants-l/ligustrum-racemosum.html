Ligustrum racemosum

Ligustrum racemosum, commonly known as the racemosum privet, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, simple, and often aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are followed by dark blue-black berries. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is adaptable to different light conditions.

Ligustrum racemosum is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and as a hedge plant.
