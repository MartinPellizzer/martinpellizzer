Lepisorus ussuriensis var. ussuriensis

Lepisorus ussuriensis var. ussuriensis, commonly known as the Ussuri spleenwort, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family.

It is a small, epiphytic fern that typically grows on tree trunks or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant has delicate, linear leaves that are often clustered at the base, forming a rosette-like structure. Its spore-producing structures, called sorocarps, are borne on the underside of the leaves.

This species is native to eastern Asia, including regions of Russia, China, and Korea.
