Leonotis intermedia

Leonotis intermedia, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescences that produce vibrant orange or red tubular flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stem. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and birds, contributing to its ecological value.
