Leea glabra

Leea glabra, commonly known as the smooth leea, is a tropical shrub native to regions of Southeast Asia and northern Australia.

It belongs to the family Leeaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually smooth and glossy. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in tropical and subtropical regions.
