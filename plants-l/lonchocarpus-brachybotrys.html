Lonchocarpus brachybotrys

Lonchocarpus brachybotrys, commonly known as the short-stemmed senna, is a tropical legume native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and produces distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant's pods are typically small and contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.

However, some species within this genus contain toxic compounds, requiring caution in its use.
