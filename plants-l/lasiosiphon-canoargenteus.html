Lasiosiphon canoargenteus

Lasiosiphon canoargenteus, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pale blue to lavender in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens but can become invasive if not managed properly.
