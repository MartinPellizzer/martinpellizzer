Lavandula × ginginsii

Lavandula × ginginsii, commonly known as the English lavender, is a hybrid species within the Lavandula genus.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its aromatic, spike-like inflorescences of purple flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and has linear, gray-green leaves that are aromatic when crushed. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal use.

Lavandula × ginginsii is valued for its essential oils, which are used in perfumery, aromatherapy, and traditional medicine.
