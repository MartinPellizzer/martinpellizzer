Lepanthes arbaceae

Lepanthes arbaceae, commonly known as the orchid beauty, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that often feature intricate patterns and vibrant colors. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are usually small to medium in size and are known for their fragrant, nectar-rich structure that attracts pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

Lepanthes arbaceae plays an important ecological role in its habitat by contributing to biodiversity and supporting local pollinator populations.
