Lasianthus myrtifolius subsp. vicarius

Lasianthus myrtifolius subsp.

vicarius, commonly known as the silverleaf lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its lance-shaped, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
