Lantana horrida f. latibracteata

Lantana horrida f. latibracteata, commonly known as the broad-bract lantana, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and clusters of small, colorful flowers that range from yellow to orange. The leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a textured appearance. This variety is distinguished by its broad, showy bracts that surround the flowers, enhancing its ornamental value.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens in warm climates.
