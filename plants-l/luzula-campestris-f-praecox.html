Luzula campestris f. praecox

Luzula campestris f. praecox, commonly known as the early sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like foliage, with narrow, grass-green leaves that are typically arranged in a dense, upright clump. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer, which are clustered in spikelets along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along stream banks.

Luzula campestris f. praecox is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
