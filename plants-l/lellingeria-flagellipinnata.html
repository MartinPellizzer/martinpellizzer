Lellingeria flagellipinnata

Lellingeria flagellipinnata, commonly known as the flagellipinnate fern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that resemble a whip or flagellum, giving it its scientific name. The plant has a climbing habit and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by spore-bearing sporangia. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with numerous narrow, linear segments.

Lellingeria flagellipinnata is often found in humid, shaded environments within tropical forests.
