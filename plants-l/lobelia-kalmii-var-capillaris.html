Lobelia kalmii var. capillaris

Lobelia kalmii var. capillaris, commonly known as Kalm's lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems covered in dense, blue to purple flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with silver, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its delicate appearance and ability to attract pollinators make it a valued addition to native plant landscapes.
