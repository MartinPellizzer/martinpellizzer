Lindera gracilipes

Lindera gracilipes, commonly known as slender sweetshrub, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and features slender, aromatic leaves with a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds. Lindera gracilipes thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and ornamental gardening.
