Lathyrus montanus var. subunijugus

Lathyrus montanus var. subunijugus, commonly known as the Sierra grass vetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The species is noted for its ecological importance in supporting pollinators and stabilizing soil in its native range.
