Leandra dichotoma

Leandra dichotoma, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are often hairy and have a leathery texture. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Leandra dichotoma is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens in temperate climates.
