Lathyrus anomalus

Lathyrus anomalus, commonly known as the sweet pea vine, is a climbing legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped, velvety leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pea-like flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white. Its pods contain small, edible seeds that are often used in traditional cuisines.

Lathyrus anomalus is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its historical use in herbal medicine.
