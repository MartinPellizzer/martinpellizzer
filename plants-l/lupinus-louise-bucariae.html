Lupinus louise-bucariae

Lupinus louise-bucariae, commonly known as the Louise-Bucaria lupine, is a species native to the coastal regions of southern California.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of pea-like flowers. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces showy, violet to purple blooms. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal dunes and sandy habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
