Lysimachia venosa

Lysimachia venosa, commonly known as the silver thread plant, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features delicate, opposite leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery or grayish pubescence, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that appear in clusters during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Lysimachia venosa is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and ability to form dense, ground-covering mats.
