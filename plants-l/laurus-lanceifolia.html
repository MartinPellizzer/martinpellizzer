Laurus lanceifolia

Laurus lanceifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved laurel, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other well-known plants like bay laurel and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are aromatic when crushed. It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and can reach heights of up to 10 feet.

Due to its ornamental value and fragrance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
