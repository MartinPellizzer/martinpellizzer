Lepanthes asoma

Lepanthes asoma, commonly known as the asoma slipper orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, pendant-like flowers that are typically greenish-yellow with intricate patterns. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its unique morphology, including its specialized lip that aids in pollination. Lepanthes asoma thrives in shaded, humid environments, often growing on trees or rocks in the understory of forests.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest for both horticulturists and botanists.
