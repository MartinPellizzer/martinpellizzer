Lobivia cinnabarina var. oligotricha

Lobivia cinnabarina var. oligotricha, commonly known as the red-tipped lobivia, is a succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It features compact, rounded stems that often branch out into a shrub-like form. The plant produces vibrant orange to red flowers during the cooler months, adding a striking visual element to its appearance. Its leaves are typically thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native desert environment.

This variety is prized for its unique coloration and is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
