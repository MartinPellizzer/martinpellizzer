Lagerstroemia floribunda var. cuspidata

Lagerstroemia floribunda var. cuspidata, commonly known as the pointed crape myrtle, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to red bract clusters that appear in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its compact growth habit and long bloom period make it a versatile ornamental plant.
