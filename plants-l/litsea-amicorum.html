Litsea amicorum

Litsea amicorum, commonly known as the friendship laurel, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like cinnamon and avocado. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, dark blue drupe that contains a single seed.

Litsea amicorum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its aromatic properties.
