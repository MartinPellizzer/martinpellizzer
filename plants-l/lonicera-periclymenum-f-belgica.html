Lonicera periclymenum f. belgica

Lonicera periclymenum f. belgica, commonly known as the Belgian honeysuckle, is a botanical variant of the common honeysuckle.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant features opposite, oval-shaped leaves with a smooth surface and a slightly hairy underside. It produces small, red berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to attract pollinators.
