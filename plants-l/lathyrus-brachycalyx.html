Lathyrus brachycalyx

Lathyrus brachycalyx, commonly known as the short-calyx pea, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial vine that produces small, pea-like flowers in shades of pink to purple. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several small, kidney-shaped seeds.

Lathyrus brachycalyx is often found in moist woodlands and along stream banks, where it thrives in partial to full shade.
