Lonicera gibbiflora

Lonicera gibbiflora, commonly known as the hairy honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically white or yellow, followed by red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or stream banks.

Lonicera gibbiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting wildlife.
