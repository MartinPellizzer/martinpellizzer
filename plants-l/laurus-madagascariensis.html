Laurus madagascariensis

Laurus madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar laurel, is a small evergreen tree native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like laurel and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant flowers that appear in clusters. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns from green to yellow or red as it matures.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its leaves, which can be used in traditional medicine.
