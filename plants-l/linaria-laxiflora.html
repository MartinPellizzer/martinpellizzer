Linaria laxiflora

Linaria laxiflora, commonly known as the slender toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a low-growing annual or perennial herb characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces delicate, spurred flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Linaria laxiflora is often found in dry, rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
