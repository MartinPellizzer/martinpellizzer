Limnopoa

Limnopoa, commonly known as the water reed, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that often grow in wet or aquatic environments. Limnopoa species are typically found in marshes, wetlands, and along the edges of lakes and rivers. The plants produce small, clustered flowers that are often green or pale in color.
