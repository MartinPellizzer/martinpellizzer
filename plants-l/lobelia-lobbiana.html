Lobelia lobbiana

Lobelia lobbiana, commonly known as the mountain lobelia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its slender stems and small, tubular, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, hairy texture.

Lobelia lobbiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
