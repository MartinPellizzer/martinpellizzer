Lanugothamnus curitybensis

Lanugothamnus curitybensis, commonly known as the Curitiba thyme, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant leaves and clusters of purple or pink flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in horticultural settings.
