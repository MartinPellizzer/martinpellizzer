Lonchocarpus sumiderensis

Lonchocarpus sumiderensis, commonly known as the Brazilian strychnos, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant contains various alkaloids, some of which have medicinal properties but are also toxic in high doses. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments within dense tropical forests.
