Lobelia burmanniana

Lobelia burmanniana, commonly known as Burmann's lobelia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories or stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Lobelia burmanniana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with appropriate humidity and light conditions.
