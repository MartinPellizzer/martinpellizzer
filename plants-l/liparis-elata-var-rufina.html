Liparis elata var. rufina

Liparis elata var. rufina, commonly known as the red-flowered orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, linear leaves and striking red or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and thrives in cool climates. Its flowers are often clustered in inflorescences and bloom during the spring and early summer.

Liparis elata var. rufina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
