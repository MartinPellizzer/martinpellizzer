Lophoschoenus fragilis

Lophoschoenus fragilis, commonly known as the slender swamp grass, is a perennial herb native to wetland habitats in Australia.

It features slender, erect stems that are often branched and covered in fine, hairy structures. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically with a slightly wavy margin.

Lophoschoenus fragilis thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in marshes and along waterways.
