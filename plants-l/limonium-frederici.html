Limonium frederici

Limonium frederici, commonly known as the coastal flax, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils. It thrives in full sun and is often found in dune environments.

Limonium frederici is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize coastal soils.
