Lepidadenia magnifica

Lepidadenia magnifica, commonly known as the grand magnolia, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its large, showy flowers.

It belongs to the Magnoliaceae family and is native to regions in South America. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. Its flowers, which can reach up to 12 inches in diameter, are typically white or pale pink with a strong, sweet fragrance.

Lepidadenia magnifica is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in warm climates for its beauty.
