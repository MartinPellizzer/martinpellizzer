Lepanthes trifurcata

Lepanthes trifurcata, commonly known as the three-branched orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in photosynthesis.

Lepanthes trifurcata is often found in humid, shaded environments, such as cloud forests, where it thrives in the understory.
