Lobivia maximiliana var. corbula

Lobivia maximiliana var. corbula, commonly known as the "Coral Lobivia," is a compact, slow-growing cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It features a short, cylindrical stem with prominent, rib-like structures that help it store water. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are modified into spines, which provide protection from herbivores and reduce water loss.

This variety is prized for its unique coloration and is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
