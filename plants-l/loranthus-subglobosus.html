Loranthus subglobosus

Loranthus subglobosus, commonly known as the globular mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using the light it captures. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces small, red berries that are often consumed by birds. It is commonly found in tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in areas with warm climates.

Its unique parasitic relationship with host trees makes it an important part of the ecosystem in its native habitat.
