Lepanthes ciliolata

Lepanthes ciliolata, commonly known as the hairy slipper orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that often have a slipper-like shape and intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as cloud forests, where it thrives in the understory. Its leaves are usually broad and strap-like, and the flowers bloom in a range of colors, including white, pink, and purple.

Lepanthes ciliolata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
