Lotus corniculatus subsp. minor

Lotus corniculatus subsp.

minor, commonly known as the lesser birdsfoot trefoil, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, and its trifoliate leaves with three oval-shaped leaflets.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed sites. It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it a valuable plant in ecological restoration.

Its fibrous root system helps prevent soil erosion, contributing to its ecological significance.
