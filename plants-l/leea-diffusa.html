Leea diffusa

Leea diffusa, commonly known as the African star, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Leeaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or greenish-yellow, followed by spherical, fleshy fruits. It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in shaded environments.

The leaves are also used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
