Lonicera tomentella var. tomentella

Lonicera tomentella var. tomentella, commonly known as the woolly honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features slender, arching stems covered in dense, white pubescence, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow to orange, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive texture.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
