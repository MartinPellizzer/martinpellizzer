Lappa major subvar. purpurascens

Lappa major subvar. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple flowering bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to gardens and natural habitats.
