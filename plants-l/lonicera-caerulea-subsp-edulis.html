Lonicera caerulea subsp. edulis

Lonicera caerulea subsp.

edulis, commonly known as the edible honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or white, followed by bright red berries that are edible for both humans and wildlife.

The plant is characterized by its twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

The berries are rich in antioxidants and are often used in jams, juices, and traditional medicine.
