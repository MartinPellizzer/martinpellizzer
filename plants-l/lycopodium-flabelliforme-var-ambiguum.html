Lycopodium flabelliforme var. ambiguum

Lycopodium flabelliforme var. ambiguum, commonly known as the variable clubmoss, is a species of lycophyte characterized by its small, scale-like leaves arranged in whorls along its branching stems.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant exhibits a unique reproductive structure known as a strobilus, which contains the sporangia for spore production. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine, and it plays a role in soil stabilization in its natural habitat.

The varietal name "ambiguum" reflects the variability in its morphological features, making it a subject of taxonomic interest.
