Licuala ternata

Licuala ternata, commonly known as the broad-leafed pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant belongs to the Arecaceae family, which includes palms, and is often found in wetland or swampy areas. Its leaves are used in traditional crafts and as thatching material.

The plant also produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically followed by spiky, berry-like fruits.
