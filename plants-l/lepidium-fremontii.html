Lepidium fremontii

Lepidium fremontii, commonly known as desert rocket, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. Its leaves are pinnately divided, and it produces small, edible seeds that are sometimes used in local cuisines.

Lepidium fremontii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
