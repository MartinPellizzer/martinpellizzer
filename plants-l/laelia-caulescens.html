Laelia caulescens

Laelia caulescens, commonly known as the slender lepidote orchid, is a species native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its upright, leafy stems and clustered, showy flowers. The plant typically blooms in late winter to early spring, producing fragrant, pinkish-white to pale purple flowers. It prefers cool, humid environments and is often found growing in rocky or mountainous terrain.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
