Lolium temulentum

Lolium temulentum, commonly known as 醉草, is a species of annual grass native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. The plant produces pale green to blue-green leaves that are typically narrow and slightly hairy. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Lolium temulentum is also known for its historical use in traditional medicine and as a source of forage for livestock.
