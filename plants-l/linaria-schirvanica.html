Linaria schirvanica

Linaria schirvanica, commonly known as the Persian toadflax, is a flowering plant native to regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the plant family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its vibrant yellow or orange tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Linaria schirvanica has linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, seed-containing capsules as its fruit.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
