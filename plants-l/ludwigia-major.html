Ludwigia major

Ludwigia major, commonly known as the water spinach or river hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often reddish in color, especially when grown in low-light conditions. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers with five petals, typically blooming in the morning. Ludwigia major thrives in shallow water or moist soils, making it a common aquatic or semi-aquatic species.
