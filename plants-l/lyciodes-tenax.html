Lyciodes tenax

Lyciodes tenax, commonly known as the creeping nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It features woody stems that grow close to the ground, often forming dense mats. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically purple or white, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Lyciodes tenax is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
