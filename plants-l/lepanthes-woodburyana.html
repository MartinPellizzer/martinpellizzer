Lepanthes woodburyana

Lepanthes woodburyana, commonly known as the Woodbury's slipper orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the cloud forests of Central America.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow with a distinctive slipper-shaped pouch. The plant grows as an epiphyte, often found on trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Lepanthes woodburyana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is a subject of interest in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
