Linaria commutata var. polygonoides

Linaria commutata var. polygonoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb with slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
