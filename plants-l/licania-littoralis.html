Licania littoralis

Licania littoralis, commonly known as the coastal licania, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It thrives in coastal areas, often growing in sandy or limestone soils near the shoreline. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually appearing in greenish-white or pale yellow hues.

Licania littoralis plays a role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems and provides habitat for various small animals and insects.
