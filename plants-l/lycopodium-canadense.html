Lycopodium canadense

Lycopodium canadense, commonly known as the Canadian clubmoss, is a small, evergreen fern-like plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and its production of spore-bearing cones called strobili. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. Its leaves are spirally arranged and have a needle-like appearance, contributing to its resemblance to a miniature pine tree.

Lycopodium canadense plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for small invertebrates in its native range.
