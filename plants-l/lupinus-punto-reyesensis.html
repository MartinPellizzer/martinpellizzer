Lupinus punto-reyesensis

Lupinus punto-reyesensis, commonly known as the Point Reyes lupine, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of California.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of blue or purple flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It thrives in sandy soils and is often found in coastal scrub habitats, playing a role in soil stabilization and supporting local wildlife.
