Lilium paradoxum

Lilium paradoxum, commonly known as the Chinese lily, is a flowering plant native to China and Korea.

It is characterized by its striking white to pink flowers with a distinctive red or orange spot at the base of each petal. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with strap-like, dark green leaves. Its bulbs are underground storage organs that help it survive in its native climate.

Lilium paradoxum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to temperate climates.
