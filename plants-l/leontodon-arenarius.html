Leontodon arenarius

Leontodon arenarius, commonly known as the sand bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as dunes and coastal areas. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and the flowers are typically arranged in a loose cluster at the top of the stem.

Leontodon arenarius is a hardy perennial that is well adapted to harsh, arid environments.
