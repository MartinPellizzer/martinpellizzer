Leontopodium blagoveshczenskyi

Leontopodium blagoveshczenskyi, commonly known as the Blagoveshchensky edelweiss, is a rare alpine plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Russia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly white bracts that surround a central cluster of small, yellowish flowers. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments with poor soil and harsh climatic conditions. Its unique appearance and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to its delicate nature and habitat specificity, it is considered vulnerable to environmental changes and human disturbance.
