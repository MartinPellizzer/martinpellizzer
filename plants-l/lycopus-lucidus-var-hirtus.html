Lycopus lucidus var. hirtus

Lycopus lucidus var. hirtus, commonly known as hairy water horehound, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, often shaded environments such as stream banks and wet meadows. The plant features opposite, hairy, ovate to lance-shaped leaves with a serrated margin. Its small, blue to purple flowers are arranged in whorled clusters, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

It is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported respiratory and anti-inflammatory properties.
