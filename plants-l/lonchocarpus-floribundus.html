Lonchocarpus floribundus

Lonchocarpus floribundus, commonly known as the yellow flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often in spring and early summer. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces distinctive, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
