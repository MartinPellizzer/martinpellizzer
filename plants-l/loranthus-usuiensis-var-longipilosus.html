Loranthus usuiensis var. longipilosus

Loranthus usuiensis var. longipilosus, commonly known as the long-haired mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of various host trees.

It has a distinctive appearance with its woody stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are typically arranged in clusters. Its seeds are dispersed by birds, which consume the fleshy, red berries.

This species is native to parts of East Asia and is often found in forested areas with suitable host trees.
