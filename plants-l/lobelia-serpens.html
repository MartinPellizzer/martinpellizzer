Lobelia serpens

Lobelia serpens, commonly known as the snake's head lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, ascending stems that are often covered in fine, hairy hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Lobelia serpens thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
