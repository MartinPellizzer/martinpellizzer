Lepanthes troxis

Lepanthes troxis, commonly known as the golden star orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit and its striking yellow flowers with a distinctive star-shaped pattern. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, producing a sweet fragrance that attracts pollinators.

Lepanthes troxis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
