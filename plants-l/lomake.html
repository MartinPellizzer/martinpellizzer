Lomake

Lomake, commonly known as the Indian nettle or *Urtica dioica*, is a flowering plant belonging to the Urticaceae family.

It is characterized by its stinging hairs on the leaves and stems, which release irritants when touched. The plant is native to temperate regions of Europe, Asia, and North America. Lomake typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including woodlands and fields.

It produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are pollinated by insects.
