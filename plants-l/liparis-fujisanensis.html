Liparis fujisanensis

Liparis fujisanensis, commonly known as the Fujisan orchid, is a rare and delicate terrestrial orchid native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments, typically found in the high-altitude regions of Mount Fuji. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its unique appearance.

Due to its limited habitat and vulnerability to environmental changes, it is considered a conservation concern.
