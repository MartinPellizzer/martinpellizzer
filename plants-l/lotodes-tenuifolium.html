Lotodes tenuifolium

Lotodes tenuifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved water lily, is a aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features floating leaves that are narrow and elongated, with a waxy surface to repel water. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, opening in the early morning and closing by midday. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

It plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen to aquatic life.
