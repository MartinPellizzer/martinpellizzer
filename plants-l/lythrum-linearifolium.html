Lythrum linearifolium

Lythrum linearifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved catchfly, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lythraceae family and is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and spikes of showy pink or red flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks. Its flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is also used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
