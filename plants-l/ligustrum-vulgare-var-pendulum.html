Ligustrum vulgare var. pendulum

Ligustrum vulgare var. pendulum, commonly known as weeping privet, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its cascading branches that bear small, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters, which are followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Due to its adaptability, it can be found in both natural and cultivated settings.
