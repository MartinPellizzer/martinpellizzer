Listrostachys iridifolia

Listrostachys iridifolia, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring silver-gray foliage and clusters of tiny, blue-violet flowers. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
