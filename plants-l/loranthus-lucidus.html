Loranthus lucidus

Loranthus lucidus, commonly known as the shining mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees.

It lacks true roots and absorbs water and nutrients through its haustoria, which penetrate the host's vascular system. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers and red berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It is native to parts of Asia and is typically found on species of the genus Ficus.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the ecosystems it inhabits.
