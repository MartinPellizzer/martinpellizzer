Lupulus lupuloides heterocarpus

Lupulus lupuloides heterocarpus, commonly known as the rough hop, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cannabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces distinctive, spiky fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is known for its aromatic properties.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and medicinal studies.
