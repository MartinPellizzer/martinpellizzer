Lobivia pectinifera var. subcarnea

Lobivia pectinifera var. subcarnea, commonly known as the pink-tinged lobivia, is a succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It features dense, compact growth with short, fleshy stems that produce clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers during the cooler months. The plant has thick, ribbed leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in dry conditions. It prefers well-draining soil and full sun exposure to promote healthy growth.

Its unique coloration and compact form make it a popular choice for rock gardens and container cultivation.
