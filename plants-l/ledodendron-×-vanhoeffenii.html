Ledodendron × vanhoeffenii

Ledodendron × vanhoeffenii, commonly known as the Van Hoeffen's laurel, is a hybrid evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green, elliptical leaves that remain throughout the year. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters, followed by dark blue berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its dense growth habit and winter interest make it a valuable addition to shaded garden spaces.
