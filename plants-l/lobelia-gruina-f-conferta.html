Lobelia gruina f. conferta

Lobelia gruina f. conferta, commonly known as the dense lobelia, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, tubular blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, toothed leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its dense growth habit and ornamental value make it a valuable addition to native plant landscapes.
