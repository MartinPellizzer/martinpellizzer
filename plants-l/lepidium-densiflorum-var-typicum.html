Lepidium densiflorum var. typicum

Lepidium densiflorum var. typicum, commonly known as peppergrass, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, clustered white or pink flowers, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are alternate, linear to lanceolate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Peppergrass is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils, making it a common weed in agricultural fields.
