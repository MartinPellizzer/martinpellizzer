Lilium sempervivoideum

Lilium sempervivoideum, commonly known as the everliving lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red in color, with a distinctive, recurved shape. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. It blooms in late summer to early autumn, and its bulbs are used for propagation.

The species is noted for its hardiness and ability to thrive in well-drained, rocky soils.
