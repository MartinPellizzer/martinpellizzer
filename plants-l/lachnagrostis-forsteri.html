Lachnagrostis forsteri

Lachnagrostis forsteri, commonly known as Forster's needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its fine, needle-like leaves and tufted inflorescences that produce small, spikelet-based flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is well adapted to drought conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small fauna in its native range.

Lachnagrostis forsteri is often found in open grasslands and along the edges of desert scrub habitats.
