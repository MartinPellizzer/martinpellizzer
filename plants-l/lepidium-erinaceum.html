Lepidium erinaceum

Lepidium erinaceum, commonly known as hairy peppercress, is an annual flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard plants. The plant is characterized by its small, green, edible leaves and clusters of tiny white flowers that bloom in early summer. Its fruit is a short, cylindrical silique that contains several seeds.

Lepidium erinaceum is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and fields.
