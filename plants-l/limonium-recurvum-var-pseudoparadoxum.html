Limonium recurvum var. pseudoparadoxum

Limonium recurvum var. pseudoparadoxum, commonly known as the coastal blueflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, preferring sandy or gravelly soils. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in dune environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in harsh, saline conditions.
