Lasianthus flavinervius

Lasianthus flavinervius, commonly known as the golden-veined lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow veins on its lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a subtle fragrance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
