Lithospermum occidentale

Lithospermum occidentale, commonly known as western lithosperm, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as cliffs and slopes, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are pink to purple, arranged in clusters, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The roots of Lithospermum occidentale contain compounds that have been studied for their potential medicinal properties.
