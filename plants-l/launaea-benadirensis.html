Launaea benadirensis

Launaea benadirensis, commonly known as the Benadirensis dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant features a basal rosette of leaves with a rough, hairy texture. Its yellow flowers bloom in summer, producing small seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Launaea benadirensis is adapted to arid environments, showcasing resilience to drought conditions.
