Lonicera etrusca var. cyrenaica

Lonicera etrusca var. cyrenaica, commonly known as the Etruscan honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to southern Italy.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with opposite, oval-shaped leaves. Its yellow-orange flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
