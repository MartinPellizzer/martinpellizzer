Lonicera macranthoides

Lonicera macranthoides, commonly known as the large-flowered honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically hairy and have a serrated margin. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that are usually yellow or orange in color. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that ripens in late summer.

Lonicera macranthoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its showy blooms.
