Lonicera balearica

Lonicera balearica, commonly known as the Balearic honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the Balearic Islands.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy beneath. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, blooming in late spring. Its berries are small, red, and edible, though not commonly consumed by humans.

Lonicera balearica is adapted to coastal and rocky habitats, showcasing its resilience in Mediterranean climates.
