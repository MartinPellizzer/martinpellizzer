Lavenia fastigiata

Lavenia fastigiata, commonly known as the stiff-stemmed lavenia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, woody stems and dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid, shady environments. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a glossy surface.

Lavenia fastigiata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
