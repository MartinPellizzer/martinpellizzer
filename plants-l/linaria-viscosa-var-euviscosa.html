Linaria viscosa var. euviscosa

Linaria viscosa var. euviscosa, commonly known as the sticky toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, yellow-orange flowers with a distinctive, spurred shape. The plant is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a sticky texture. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it an important plant in ecological settings.
