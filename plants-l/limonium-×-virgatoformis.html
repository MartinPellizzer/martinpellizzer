Limonium × virgatoformis

Limonium × virgatoformis, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant belonging to the Plumbagaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. Native to coastal areas of the Mediterranean, it thrives in sandy or saline soils. The plant is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate coastal conditions.

Its striking appearance makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
