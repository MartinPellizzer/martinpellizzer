Leucanthemum glaucophyllum

Leucanthemum glaucophyllum, commonly known as the blue-eyed Mary, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy white petals with a central blue or purple disc, and its leaves are typically gray-green and hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands.

Leucanthemum glaucophyllum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
