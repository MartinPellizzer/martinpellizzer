Lepechinia mutica

Lepechinia mutica, commonly known as the soft lepechinia, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, tubular flowers. The plant typically thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe habitats. Its compact growth habit and aromatic foliage make it a distinctive member of its ecological community.

Lepechinia mutica plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is adapted to withstand drought and grazing.
