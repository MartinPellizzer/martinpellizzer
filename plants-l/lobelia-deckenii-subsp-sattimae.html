Lobelia deckenii subsp. sattimae

Lobelia deckenii subsp.

sattimae, commonly known as the Sattima lobelia, is a flowering plant native to eastern Africa. It is characterized by its erect, herbaceous growth habit and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet in color.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or rocky stream banks. Its leaves are usually opposite, lance-shaped, and have a rough texture.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
