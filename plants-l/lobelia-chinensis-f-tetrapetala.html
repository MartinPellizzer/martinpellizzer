Lobelia chinensis f. tetrapetala

Lobelia chinensis f. tetrapetala, commonly known as the four-petaled Chinese lobelia, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
