Leucas stachydiformis var. scioana

Leucas stachydiformis var. scioana, commonly known as the silverleaf leucas, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers arranged in compact clusters, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for dry, rocky gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in horticultural settings.
