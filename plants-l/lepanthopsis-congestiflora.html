Lepanthopsis congestiflora

Lepanthopsis congestiflora, commonly known as the clustered orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, clustered flowers. The flowers are typically white or pale green, with a unique structure that includes a prominent lip petal. This orchid thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as an indicator species for undisturbed tropical ecosystems.
