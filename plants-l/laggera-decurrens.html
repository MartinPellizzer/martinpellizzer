Laggera decurrens

Laggera decurrens, commonly known as the slender laggera, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive foliage. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, dry fruits known as achenes.

Laggera decurrens is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
