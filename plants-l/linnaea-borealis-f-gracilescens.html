Linnaea borealis f. gracilescens

Linnaea borealis f. gracilescens, commonly known as the slender twinflower, is a rare and delicate variant of the twinflower plant.

It is a low-growing evergreen shrub characterized by its small, opposite leaves and clusters of fragrant, white, bell-shaped flowers. This plant is native to the boreal forests of North America and parts of Eurasia, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its flowers typically bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and flies.

The plant's unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
