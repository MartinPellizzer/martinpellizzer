Linum lewisii var. saxosum

Linum lewisii var. saxosum, commonly known as the Sierra flax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the genus Linum, which includes various species of flax, and is characterized by its delicate blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in alpine or arid environments. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to harsh conditions and its role in supporting local pollinators.
