Lactuca laevigata var. excisodentata

Lactuca laevigata var. excisodentata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lettuce, is a variant of the common lettuce species native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically smooth and have a mild, slightly bitter taste. The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its edible leaves. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun exposure, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

This variety is valued for its nutritional content and use in salads and culinary applications.
