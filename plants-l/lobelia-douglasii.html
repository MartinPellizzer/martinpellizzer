Lobelia douglasii

Lobelia douglasii, commonly known as Douglas's lobelia, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Lobelia douglasii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
