Leucas fasciculata

Leucas fasciculata, commonly known as narrow-leaved leucas, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. It produces clusters of pale blue to violet flowers, which are typically arranged in whorls along the stem.

Leucas fasciculata is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.
