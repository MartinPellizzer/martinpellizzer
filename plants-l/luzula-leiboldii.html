Luzula leiboldii

Luzula leiboldii, commonly known as the blue-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, bright blue to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming dense clumps.

Luzula leiboldii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
