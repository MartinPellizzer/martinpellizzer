Lycoris squamigera

Lycoris squamigera, commonly known as the Chinese glory of the snow, is a flowering plant native to China and Korea.

It belongs to the Amaryllis family and is characterized by its striking red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features strap-shaped leaves that emerge from a basal rosette, and its bulbs are often used in traditional medicine. Lycoris squamigera thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its unique appearance and cultural significance make it a notable species in botanical studies.
