Lycopodioides vitiensis

Lycopodioides vitiensis, commonly known as the Vitiensis clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. The plant has a creeping stem with whorled leaves that are usually narrow and linear in shape. It produces microspores and megaspores on separate cones, which are typical of its ancient lineage.

Lycopodioides vitiensis plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and small invertebrates.
