Leersia imbricata

Leersia imbricata, commonly known as the flatleaf sedges, is a perennial grass-like plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It typically grows in wet or marshy environments, often forming dense colonies in wetlands and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often arranged in a triangular cross-section, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are clustered in spikelets, and it produces small, three-lobed seeds.

Leersia imbricata plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
