Lonicera maackii

Lonicera maackii, commonly known as Japanese honeysuckle, is a deciduous vine native to East Asia.

It features opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are often pubescent, and its fragrant, tubular flowers bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces orange-red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, making it an invasive species in many regions.

Botanically, it belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and showy floral display.
