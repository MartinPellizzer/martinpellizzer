Lilium philadelphicum f. lanceolatum

Lilium philadelphicum f. lanceolatum, commonly known as the lanceleaf fawn lily, is a botanical variant of the fawn lily, native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, dark green leaves and produces striking white to pale yellow flowers with a red throat. The plant typically grows in woodland habitats and thrives in partial to full shade. It is a herbaceous perennial that blooms in early spring, often before the leaves fully expand.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
