Lycopodium stuebelii

Lycopodium stuebelii, commonly known as the Stuebel's clubmoss, is a small, evergreen plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, dense growth habit and the presence of small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces sporophylls that bear spores, which are dispersed by wind, a common reproductive strategy in primitive plants. Lycopodium stuebelii typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or rocky crevices.

Its structure and reproductive mechanisms reflect its ancient lineage, dating back to the Paleozoic era.
