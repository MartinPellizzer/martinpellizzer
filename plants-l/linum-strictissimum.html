Linum strictissimum

Linum strictissimum, commonly known as the strict flax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant produces slender, erect stems covered with fine hairs, and its leaves are linear and slightly hairy. Its flowers are small, blue to purple in color, and are borne in loose clusters at the tips of the stems.

Linum strictissimum is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in textile production.
