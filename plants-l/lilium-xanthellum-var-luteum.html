Lilium xanthellum var. luteum

Lilium xanthellum var. luteum, commonly known as the yellow fritillary, is a rare and striking variant of the fritillary lily.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range from pale yellow to deep orange, with intricate patterns resembling a fritillary butterfly. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows, preferring well-drained, loamy soils. Its botanical features include lance-shaped leaves and a tall, slender stem that supports its vibrant blooms.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
