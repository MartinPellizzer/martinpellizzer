Lippia deltata

Lippia deltata, commonly known as Mexican mint or wild mint, is a perennial herb native to Central and South America.

It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy texture. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Lippia deltata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
