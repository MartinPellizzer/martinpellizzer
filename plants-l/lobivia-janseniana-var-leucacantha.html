Lobivia janseniana var. leucacantha

Lobivia janseniana var. leucacantha, commonly known as the White-veined Lobivia, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rounded stem structure. The plant features pale green to bluish-gray foliage with distinct white veins, giving it its common name. It produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer.

This variety is well adapted to dry conditions, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
