Luzula maxima subsp. sicula

Luzula maxima subsp.

sicula, commonly known as Sicilian wood sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
