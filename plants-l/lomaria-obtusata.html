Lomaria obtusata

Lomaria obtusata, commonly known as the bluntleaf lomaria, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are dispersed by wind, contributing to its natural distribution.

Lomaria obtusata is often found in forest understories and is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
