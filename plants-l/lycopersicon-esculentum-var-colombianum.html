Lycopersicon esculentum var. colombianum

Lycopersicon esculentum var. colombianum, commonly known as the Colombian tomato, is a botanical variant of the domesticated tomato.

It is characterized by its unique morphological features, including smaller fruit size and distinct coloration compared to standard tomatoes. This variety is native to Colombia and exhibits a range of genetic diversity that contributes to its adaptability. Its botanical significance lies in its potential for breeding programs aimed at improving resilience and flavor in cultivated tomatoes.

The plant typically grows as an annual herb with hairy stems and lobed leaves, typical of the Solanaceae family.
