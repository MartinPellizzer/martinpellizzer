Lippia ligustrina var. casadensis

Lippia ligustrina var. casadensis, commonly known as Mexican blue sage, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and clusters of small, blue-purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping and rock gardens. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
