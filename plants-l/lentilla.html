Lentilla

Lentilla, commonly known as the lentil plant, is a leguminous species belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, lens-shaped seeds that are rich in protein and nutrients. The plant typically grows in temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, pea-like flowers.

Lentilla is widely cultivated for its edible seeds, which are used in various cuisines around the world.
