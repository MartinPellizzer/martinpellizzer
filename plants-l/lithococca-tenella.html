Lithococca tenella

Lithococca tenella, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and clusters of violet or purple flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically yellow or red when mature. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Lithococca tenella is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
