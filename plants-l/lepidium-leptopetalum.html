Lepidium leptopetalum

Lepidium leptopetalum, commonly known as slenderleaf cress, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress species. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and riverbanks. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes, and its leaves are elongated and slightly hairy.

The seeds are small and often dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize new areas.
