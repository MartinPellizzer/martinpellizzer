Linaria spartea var. brevipes

Linaria spartea var. brevipes, commonly known as the short-stemmed toadflax, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It features slender, branching stems that are typically shorter than the standard variety, giving it a compact appearance. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers with two lips, typical of the Scrophulariaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
