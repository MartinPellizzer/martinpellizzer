Lilium latifolium

Lilium latifolium, commonly known as the western fritillary, is a species of lily native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and showy, white or pinkish flowers with dark red or purple spots. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces a single, tall stem that can reach up to 2 meters in height.

The flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
