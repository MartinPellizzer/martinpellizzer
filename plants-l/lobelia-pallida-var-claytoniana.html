Lobelia pallida var. claytoniana

Lobelia pallida var. claytoniana, commonly known as the pale lobelia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and swampy areas. The plant features slender, upright stems that bear small, tubular flowers in shades of white to pale blue. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
