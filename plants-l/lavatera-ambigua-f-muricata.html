Lavatera ambigua f. muricata

Lavatera ambigua f. muricata, commonly known as the rough-leaved lavatera, is a cultivar of the lavatera plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, rough-textured leaves that are covered in small, raised bumps, giving it a unique tactile appearance. The plant produces showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its hardy nature and ability to withstand mild drought conditions contribute to its adaptability in various climates.
