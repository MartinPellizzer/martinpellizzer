Limnochloa media

Limnochloa media, commonly known as the water fern or marsh fern, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in Southeast Asia.

It thrives in moist, shaded areas and is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that emerge from a creeping rhizome. The plant's leaves are typically bipinnate, with small, lance-shaped pinnae that help it efficiently capture light in low-light conditions. Limnochloa media is often found in swampy regions and is adapted to waterlogged soils through its specialized root system.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.
