Liabum lehmannii

Liabum lehmannii, commonly known as Lehmann's liabum, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as a shrub or subshrub, with trifoliate leaves and stipules. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely known outside its native range.
