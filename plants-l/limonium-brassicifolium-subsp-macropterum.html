Limonium brassicifolium subsp. macropterum

Limonium brassicifolium subsp.

macropterum, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean. It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and linear, grass-like leaves.

The plant thrives in salty, well-drained soils and is often found in dunes and rocky shores. Its flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The subspecies macropterum is distinguished by its longer, more robust flower stems compared to other subspecies of Limonium.
