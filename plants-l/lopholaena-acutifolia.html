Lopholaena acutifolia

Lopholaena acutifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal areas and forest margins.

The flowers are pollinated by insects and birds, and the plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
