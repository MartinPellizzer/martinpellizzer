Leptogramma asplenioides

Leptogramma asplenioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf holly fern, is a delicate, evergreen fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features finely divided, lance-shaped fronds that are typically less than 30 centimeters in length. The plant has a compact growth habit, often forming dense clusters of foliage. Its leaves are usually dark green and glossy, with a soft texture that adds to its ornamental appeal.

Leptogramma asplenioides thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often used in ornamental horticulture for its elegant appearance.
