Lerchia fruticosa var. megalosperma

Lerchia fruticosa var. megalosperma, commonly known as the giant-seeded lerchia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, pale green leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by distinctive, large, woody fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
