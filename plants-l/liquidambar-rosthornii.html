Liquidambar rosthornii

Liquidambar rosthornii, commonly known as the African sweet gum, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Aceraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and palmate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, spherical fruits that contain several winged seeds, aiding in wind dispersal. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savannas and woodlands.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warmer climates.
