Licuala malajana var. malajana

Licuala malajana var. malajana, commonly known as the Malayan palm, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, pinnately compound leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length, giving it a dramatic and ornamental appearance. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests. Its trunk is typically slender and unbranched, with a smooth, pale green surface.

The species is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant in tropical gardens.
