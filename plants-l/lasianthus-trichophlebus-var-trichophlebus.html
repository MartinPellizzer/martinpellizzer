Lasianthus trichophlebus var. trichophlebus

Lasianthus trichophlebus var. trichophlebus, commonly known as the velvetleaf or broadleaf arrowleaf, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the Acanthaceae family.
