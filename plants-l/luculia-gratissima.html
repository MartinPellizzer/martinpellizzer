Luculia gratissima

Luculia gratissima, commonly known as the Chinese lantern plant, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes and potatoes, and is characterized by its vibrant, papery fruits that turn red as they mature. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and slightly hairy, while its stems are erect and branched.

The fruit, often used in traditional Chinese medicine, is also valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses.
