Lychnis pratensis

Lychnis pratensis, commonly known as the meadow pink, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands. The plant features showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stems.

Lychnis pratensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
