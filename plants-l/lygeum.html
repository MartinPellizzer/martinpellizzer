Lygeum

Lygeum, commonly known as the blueweed, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its erect, sparsely branched stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in composite heads. The leaves are typically alternate, lanceolate, and often covered in fine hairs. Lygeum species are native to various regions of Europe, Asia, and North Africa, often found in dry, open habitats.

These plants are generally herbaceous and can be either annual or perennial depending on the species.
