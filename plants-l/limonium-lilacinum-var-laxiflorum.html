Limonium lilacinum var. laxiflorum

Limonium lilacinum var. laxiflorum, commonly known as the blueflower sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and produces clusters of blue to violet flowers. The plant features silvery-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant nature. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom throughout the summer months.
