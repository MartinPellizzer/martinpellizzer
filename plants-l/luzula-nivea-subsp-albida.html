Luzula nivea subsp. albida

Luzula nivea subsp.

albida, commonly known as the white wood sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are typically pale green to white in color, giving it its distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense spikes, which are typically greenish or reddish in hue. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats. Luzula nivea subsp.

albida is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
