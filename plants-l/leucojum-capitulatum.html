Leucojum capitulatum

Leucojum capitulatum, commonly known as the white squill, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers in late winter or early spring. The plant is characterized by its underground bulb, which stores nutrients for the growing season. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is often used in horticulture for its delicate, early-blooming flowers.
