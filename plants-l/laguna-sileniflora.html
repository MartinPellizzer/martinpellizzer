Laguna sileniflora

Laguna sileniflora, commonly known as the coastal starflower, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, star-shaped white petals and its ability to thrive in brackish water environments. The plant has a short, upright stem with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down. Laguna sileniflora produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring and early summer.

It plays an important role in stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for aquatic insects.
