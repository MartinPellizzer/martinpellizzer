Leptocarpus tenax

Leptocarpus tenax, commonly known as the slender root fern, is a member of the Blechnaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like fronds that are typically arching and finely divided. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth. Its rhizomes are fibrous and can be used in traditional medicine.

Leptocarpus tenax is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in maintaining forest biodiversity.
