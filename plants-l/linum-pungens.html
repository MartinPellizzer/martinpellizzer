Linum pungens

Linum pungens, commonly known as the spiked flax, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Linum pungens is often found in moist, shaded woodlands and along stream banks.

Its fibrous roots and delicate flowers make it a unique addition to native plant gardens.
