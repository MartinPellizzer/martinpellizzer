Laurembergia tetrandra var. brachypoda

Laurembergia tetrandra var. brachypoda, commonly known as the short-stemmed laurembergia, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tubular, violet to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is native to tropical regions of South America and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
