Lycopodium hainanense

Lycopodium hainanense, commonly known as the Hainan clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of China, particularly Hainan Island, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant features a short, erect stem with whorled leaves that are typically lanceolate in shape. Its reproductive structures, known as strobila, are clustered at the top of the plant and produce spores for propagation.

Lycopodium hainanense plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat, contributing to soil stability and providing microhabitats for various organisms.
