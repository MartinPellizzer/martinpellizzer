Leucanthemum vulgare var. pinnatifidum

Leucanthemum vulgare var. pinnatifidum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved white marguerite, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with white petals surrounding a yellow center. The leaves are narrow and deeply lobed, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It typically grows in meadows and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soil.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
