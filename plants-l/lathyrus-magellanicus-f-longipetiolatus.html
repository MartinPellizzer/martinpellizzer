Lathyrus magellanicus f. longipetiolatus

Lathyrus magellanicus f. longipetiolatus, commonly known as the long-petioled bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, spurred flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the stems are usually hairy.

This fern-like plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
