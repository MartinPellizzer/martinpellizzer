Lyonia plumieri

Lyonia plumieri, commonly known as the evergreen huckleberry, is a low-growing shrub native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and produces small, edible berries that are rich in antioxidants. The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded forest environments. Its flowers are white and clustered, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.

Lyonia plumieri plays an important role in supporting local wildlife, particularly birds and small mammals.
