Lathyrus odoratus var. siculus

Lathyrus odoratus var. siculus, commonly known as the Sicilian sweet pea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that come in a range of colors, including pink, white, and purple. The plant has slender, climbing stems and pinnate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and historical use in perfumery and medicine.
