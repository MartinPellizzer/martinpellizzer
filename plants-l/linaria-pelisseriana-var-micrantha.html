Linaria pelisseriana var. micrantha

Linaria pelisseriana var. micrantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved toadflax, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces vibrant yellow-orange flowers in late spring to early summer, arranged in racemes. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a border plant.
