Leptinella atrata subsp. atrata

Leptinella atrata subsp.

atrata, commonly known as the dark-leaved lepidote, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

The plant features small, fragrant flowers that are usually pink or purple, arranged in dense, compact clusters. Its leaves are dark green and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a border plant.
