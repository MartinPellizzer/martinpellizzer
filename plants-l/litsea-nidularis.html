Litsea nidularis

Litsea nidularis, commonly known as the clustered litsea, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and avocado trees. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, small, white flowers and oval-shaped, glossy green leaves. It produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Litsea nidularis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
