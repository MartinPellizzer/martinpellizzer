Lonicera sempervirens var. oblonga

Lonicera sempervirens var. oblonga, commonly known as the oblongleaf honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of yellow to orange, which bloom in late spring. These flowers give way to red berries that are attractive to birds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
