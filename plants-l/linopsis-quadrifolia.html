Linopsis quadrifolia

Linopsis quadrifolia, commonly known as the four-leafed bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stem, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often growing in alpine or rocky habitats.

Linopsis quadrifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
