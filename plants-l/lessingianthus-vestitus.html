Lessingianthus vestitus

Lessingianthus vestitus, commonly known as the woolly sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant is distinguished by its hairy, silver-gray foliage and bright yellow ray flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a notable addition to botanical collections.
