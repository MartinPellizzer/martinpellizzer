Lupinus angustifolius subvar. virescens

Lupinus angustifolius subvar. virescens, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lupine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often a vibrant green. The plant produces spiny, cylindrical pods containing several seeds, and its flowers are typically blue or purple, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun, making it a common sight in dry, open habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in nitrogen fixation, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
