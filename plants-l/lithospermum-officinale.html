Lithospermum officinale

Lithospermum officinale, commonly known as red sanders, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking red or purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its roots contain a red dye called lycorine, which has been historically used for various purposes.

Lithospermum officinale is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
