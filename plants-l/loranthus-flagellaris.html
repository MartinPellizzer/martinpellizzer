Loranthus flagellaris

Loranthus flagellaris, commonly known as the flagellate mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to its host tree for nutrients and water.

It belongs to the Loranthaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often found on trees such as eucalyptus and acacia.

Its parasitic nature and ecological role make it an interesting subject in botanical studies.
