Liparis bistriata

Liparis bistriata, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, greenish-yellow flowers with distinctive dark markings. Native to Southeast Asia, this species typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant has slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Liparis bistriata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
