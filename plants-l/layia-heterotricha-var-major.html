Layia heterotricha var. major

Layia heterotricha var. major, commonly known as the California goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces yellow flowers with ray florets that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is known for its adaptability to arid conditions and is often found in coastal and inland regions.
