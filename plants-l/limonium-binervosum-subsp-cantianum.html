Limonium binervosum subsp. cantianum

Limonium binervosum subsp.

cantianum, commonly known as the Cantian sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Europe. It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, fluffy seed heads after flowering. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in maritime climates.

This subspecies is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate coastal conditions.
