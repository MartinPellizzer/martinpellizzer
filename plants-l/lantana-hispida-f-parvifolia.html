Lantana hispida f. parvifolia

Lantana hispida f. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved lantana, is a compact shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features small, opposite leaves that are typically covered in fine, stiff hairs, giving it a rough texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to yellow and orange, depending on the variety. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Its rapid growth and ability to tolerate various soil conditions make it a popular choice for ornamental planting.
