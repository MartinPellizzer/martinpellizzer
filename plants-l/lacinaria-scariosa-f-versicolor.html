Lacinaria scariosa f. versicolor

Lacinaria scariosa f. versicolor, commonly known as the variable fringed bellflower, is a flowering plant characterized by its distinctive, fringed petals and showy blooms.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is native to parts of Europe and western Asia. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats. Its foliage is lance-shaped with a hairy texture, and the inflorescence consists of clustered, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple.

The varietal form 'versicolor' is notable for its variable flower colors, adding to its ornamental value.
