Lantana subcordata

Lantana subcordata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf lantana, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to yellow, orange, or red, depending on the variety. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic in shape, and often have a slightly waxy texture.

Lantana subcordata is known for its aromatic foliage and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
