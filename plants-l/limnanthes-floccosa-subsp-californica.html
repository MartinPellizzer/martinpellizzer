Limnanthes floccosa subsp. californica

Limnanthes floccosa subsp.

californica, commonly known as the California buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers with a distinctive cup-shaped structure.

The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along coastal areas and estuaries. It has hairy, broad leaves that help reduce water loss in its arid habitats.

The species is known for its short growing season, blooming in late spring to early summer.
