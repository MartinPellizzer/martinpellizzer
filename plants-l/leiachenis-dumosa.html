Leiachenis dumosa

Leiachenis dumosa, commonly known as the hairy desert cactus, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems covered in dense, white hairs that help reduce water loss. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers during the warmer months. Its adaptations to harsh environments make it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.

Leiachenis dumosa is often used in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance.
