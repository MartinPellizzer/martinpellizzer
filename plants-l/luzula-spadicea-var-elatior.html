Luzula spadicea var. elatior

Luzula spadicea var. elatior, commonly known as the tall sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and streambanks. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces dense clusters of pale green to yellowish flowers in early summer. It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and is often found in wetland or riparian habitats.

Luzula spadicea var. elatior is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in moist environments.
