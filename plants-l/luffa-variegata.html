Luffa variegata

Luffa variegata, commonly known as the sponge gourd, is a tropical vine belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, lobed leaves and large, asymmetrical fruits that mature to a yellowish or greenish color. The fruit's sponge-like interior develops from a fibrous outer layer, making it useful for exfoliation. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The plant is often cultivated for both culinary and ornamental purposes.
