Lobelia irasuensis var. fucata

Lobelia irasuensis var. fucata, commonly known as the Red Lobelia, is a striking herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red to purple flowers, which bloom in dense spikes during the cooler months. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as along forest floors or in rocky crevices. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
