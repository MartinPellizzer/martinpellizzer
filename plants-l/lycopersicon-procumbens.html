Lycopersicon procumbens

Lycopersicon procumbens, commonly known as the wild tomato, is a species native to South America.

It is a low-growing perennial plant with hairy stems and lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. Its fruit is edible and contains seeds, similar to the domesticated tomato.

It is often used in botanical studies due to its genetic relationship to cultivated tomatoes.
