Leucadendron pondoense

Leucadendron pondoense, commonly known as the Pondo candle bush, is a shrub native to South Africa, specifically the Eastern Cape region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces striking cone-shaped inflorescences that range in color from yellow to red. The plant is part of the Proteaceae family, which is known for its showy floral structures and adaptations to nutrient-poor soils. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Leucadendron pondoense is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in coastal and Mediterranean climate gardens.
