Lilium wallichianum var. neilgherrense

Lilium wallichianum var. neilgherrense, commonly known as the Himalayan lily, is a striking perennial plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and red, with a distinctive recurved shape. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and grows in rocky, well-drained soils at elevations ranging from 2,500 to 5,000 meters. It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens in cooler climates.

The species is also noted for its ecological importance in alpine ecosystems.
