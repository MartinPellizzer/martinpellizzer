Lupinus semiverticillatus

Lupinus semiverticillatus, commonly known as the Arizona lupine, is a perennial legume native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to violet pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with a distinctive semicircular arrangement of leaflets, giving it a unique appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Lupinus semiverticillatus plays an important role in nitrogen fixation and supports local pollinators.
