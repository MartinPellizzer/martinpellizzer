Leucas oligocephala subsp. bowalensis

Leucas oligocephala subsp.

bowalensis, commonly known as the Bowal's leucas, is a flowering plant native to parts of southern Africa. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas and rocky soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
