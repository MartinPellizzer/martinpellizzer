Lithospermum minutum

Lithospermum minutum, commonly known as small rock pepper, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial with small, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of tiny, reddish-purple flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its fruit is a small, dry capsule that contains several seeds.

Lithospermum minutum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
