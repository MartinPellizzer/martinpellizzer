Licania petrensis

Licania petrensis, commonly known as the golden star vine, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green with a waxy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by bright orange or red berries. Licania petrensis thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm climates.
