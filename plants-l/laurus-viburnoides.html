Laurus viburnoides

Laurus viburnoides, commonly known as the American boxwood, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features dense, dark green foliage with small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in whorls. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, berry-like drupes. It has a compact growth habit, making it suitable for hedges and topiaries.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Caprifoliaceae and is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness.
