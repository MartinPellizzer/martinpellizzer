Limonium commune var. californicum

Limonium commune var. californicum, commonly known as the California sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of California.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along the coast, where it thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery coating.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in coastal gardens and as a cut flower.
