Lathyrus articulatus var. latifolius

Lathyrus articulatus var. latifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaved pea, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves with broad, heart-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are often used in traditional herbal medicine.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a common sight in meadows and along forest edges.
