Lepidium verrucosum

Lepidium verrucosum, commonly known as the rough wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial species that typically reaches heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often appearing in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are characterized by their rough, hairy texture and are arranged alternately along the stem.

Lepidium verrucosum is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to thrive in harsh, arid environments.
