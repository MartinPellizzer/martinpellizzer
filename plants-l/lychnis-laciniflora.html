Lychnis laciniflora

Lychnis laciniflora, commonly known as the fringed campion, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features striking, showy flowers that are typically pink or red, with fringed petals that give it its common name. The plant has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils.

Lychnis laciniflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
