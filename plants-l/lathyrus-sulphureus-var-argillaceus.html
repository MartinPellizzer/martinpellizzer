Lathyrus sulphureus var. argillaceus

Lathyrus sulphureus var. argillaceus, commonly known as the clay peavine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and yellow flowers. The plant thrives in moist, clay soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its leaves are trifoliate, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.
