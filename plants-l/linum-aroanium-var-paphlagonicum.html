Linum aroanium var. paphlagonicum

Linum aroanium var. paphlagonicum, commonly known as the Paphlagonian flax, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and showy blue or purple flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and historical significance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
