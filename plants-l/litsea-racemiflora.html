Litsea racemiflora

Litsea racemiflora, commonly known as the fragrant sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a fragrant aroma. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in racemes, followed by small, red berries that are edible.

Litsea racemiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its use in traditional medicine.
