Linnaea borealis f. laeticolor

Linnaea borealis f. laeticolor, commonly known as the white twinflower, is a rare variant of the twinflower plant native to northern regions of North America and Eurasia.

It is characterized by its distinctive twin leaves and small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky outcrops. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy surface.

The white flowers emit a subtle fragrance, attracting pollinators like bees and flies.
