Lonchocarpus longipedunculatus

Lonchocarpus longipedunculatus, commonly known as the long-peduncled urara, is a tropical leguminous plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellow flowers. The plant produces distinctive, spindle-shaped pods that contain several seeds. It is often found in moist, tropical forests and is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
