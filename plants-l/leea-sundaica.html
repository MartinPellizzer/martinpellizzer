Leea sundaica

Leea sundaica, commonly known as the Sunda Leea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in Indonesia.

It belongs to the family Leeaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on slender pedicels. Its roots are fibrous and can be used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Leea sundaica thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland forests or along stream banks.
