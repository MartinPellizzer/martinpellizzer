Leianthus longifolius var. gracilis

Leianthus longifolius var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender-leaved leianthus, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments within tropical forests. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and woody stems that support its climbing habit.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has limited use in traditional medicine.
