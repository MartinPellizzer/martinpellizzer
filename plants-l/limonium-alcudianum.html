Limonium alcudianum

Limonium alcudianum, commonly known as the Alcudia sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spikes of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and saline soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in dunes or rocky coastal habitats.

Limonium alcudianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems.
