Ligustrum vulgare var. chlorocarpum

Ligustrum vulgare var. chlorocarpum, commonly known as the green-fruited privet, is a variant of the common privet.

It is a deciduous shrub native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. The plant features small, oval leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. Its fruit is distinctive, being a small, green drupe that remains on the plant well into winter.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its dense growth habit and seasonal foliage changes.
