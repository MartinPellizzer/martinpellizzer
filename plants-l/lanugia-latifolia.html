Lanugia latifolia

Lanugia latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf lanugia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, often with a faint fragrance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight.

Lanugia latifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
