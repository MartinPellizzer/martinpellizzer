Lampranthus argenteus

Lampranthus argenteus, commonly known as the silver leafed trumpet lily, is a striking succulent native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, cylindrical stems covered in a silvery-blue waxy coating that reflects sunlight and reduces water loss. The plant produces vibrant orange or red tubular flowers during the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-draining soil and full sun, requiring minimal water once established.

Due to its adaptability and ornamental value, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
