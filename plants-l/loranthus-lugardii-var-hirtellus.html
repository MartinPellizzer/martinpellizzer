Loranthus lugardii var. hirtellus

Loranthus lugardii var. hirtellus, commonly known as the Hirtell's mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that grows on various host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Santalaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces berries that are often consumed by birds, which aids in seed dispersal. Its hemiparasitic nature means it derives some nutrients from its host but can photosynthesize to a limited extent.

This species is of interest in botanical studies due to its ecological role and unique symbiotic relationships.
