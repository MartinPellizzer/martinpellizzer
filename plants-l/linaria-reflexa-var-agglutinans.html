Linaria reflexa var. agglutinans

Linaria reflexa var. agglutinans, commonly known as the sticky linaria, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, branched stems and clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces sticky, glandular hairs on its leaves and stems, which give it its common name. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
