Lupinus gredensis

Lupinus gredensis, commonly known as the Gredens lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces elongated pods that contain several seeds.

Lupinus gredensis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils and open habitats.
