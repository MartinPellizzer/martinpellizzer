Lachemilla hirta

Lachemilla hirta, commonly known as hairy stonecrop, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features compact, fleshy leaves that are densely covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Lachemilla hirta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh, arid conditions.
