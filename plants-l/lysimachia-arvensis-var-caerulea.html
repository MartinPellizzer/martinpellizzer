Lysimachia arvensis var. caerulea

Lysimachia arvensis var. caerulea, commonly known as blue star, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, spreading stems that produce clusters of small, sky-blue to white flowers in early summer. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and meadows.

Due to its attractive appearance and ease of cultivation, it is a popular choice for gardeners seeking a ground-cover plant.
