Leontodon tenuifolius

Leontodon tenuifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawk's-beard, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are linear and often covered with fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is known for its resilience and ability to thrive in various environmental conditions.
