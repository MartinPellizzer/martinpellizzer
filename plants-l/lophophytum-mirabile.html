Lophophytum mirabile

Lophophytum mirabile, commonly known as the silver velvet plant, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its striking, velvety, silver-green leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. It produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color.

Lophophytum mirabile thrives in bright, indirect light and requires moderate humidity to flourish.
