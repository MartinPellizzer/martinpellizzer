Lomaria palmiformis

Lomaria palmiformis, commonly known as the palm-like lomaria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette-like growth pattern and striking blue or purple flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense, compact structure. Its flowers are typically clustered in a single terminal head, which is surrounded by bracts.

Lomaria palmiformis is well adapted to arid environments, showcasing drought-resistant properties that allow it to thrive in rocky or sandy soils.
