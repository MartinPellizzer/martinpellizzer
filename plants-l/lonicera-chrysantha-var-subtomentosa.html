Lonicera chrysantha var. subtomentosa

Lonicera chrysantha var. subtomentosa, commonly known as the silver honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features small, fragrant, tubular yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy and adaptable plant.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
