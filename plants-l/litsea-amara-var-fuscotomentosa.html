Litsea amara var. fuscotomentosa

Litsea amara var. fuscotomentosa, commonly known as the dark-tomentose sweetshrub, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurel and avocado trees. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves that are covered in a dense, dark gray pubescence. Its flowers are small and fragrant, typically appearing in clusters, and it produces small, fleshy berries.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and fragrance.
