Lavatera assurgentiflora subsp. glabra

Lavatera assurgentiflora subsp.

glabra, commonly known as the smooth lavatera, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features soft, hairy leaves that are typically gray-green in color.

The plant produces showy, fluffy flowers in shades of pink, white, and red, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
