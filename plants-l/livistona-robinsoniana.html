Livistona robinsoniana

Livistona robinsoniana, commonly known as the Robinson's palm, is a species of palm native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, such as rainforests or coastal areas. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing fruit that is edible and often consumed by local wildlife.

The species is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
