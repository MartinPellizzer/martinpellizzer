Lagerstroemia tomentosa var. loudonii

Lagerstroemia tomentosa var. loudonii, commonly known as the Loudon's crape myrtle, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red, appearing in summer. The plant has smooth, grayish bark that becomes fissured with age, and its leaves are ovate with a hairy underside. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

The variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to withstand urban conditions.
