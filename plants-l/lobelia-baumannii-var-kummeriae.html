Lobelia baumannii var. kummeriae

Lobelia baumannii var. kummeriae, commonly known as the Kummer's lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soil. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
