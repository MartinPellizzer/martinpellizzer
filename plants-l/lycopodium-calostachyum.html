Lycopodium calostachyum

Lycopodium calostachyum, commonly known as the mountain clubmoss, is a species of lycophyte belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its erect, sparsely branched stems that bear small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces sporophylls that enclose the strobila, which contains the spores necessary for reproduction. It typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas.

Lycopodium calostachyum plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
