Lepidostachys grandifolia

Lepidostachys grandifolia, commonly known as the large-leaved pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It features lance-shaped, glossy dark green leaves that are often covered with fine, scale-like structures on the underside. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by spherical, red or orange berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in coastal and forested areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native habitats.
