Lycianthes purpusii var. extensidentata

Lycianthes purpusii var. extensidentata, commonly known as the long-toothed physalis, is a variant of the physalis genus native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, inflated calyces that enclose the bright orange berries. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into the characteristic lantern-like fruits. Its leaves are typically lobed and have a rough texture.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in some traditional herbal practices.
