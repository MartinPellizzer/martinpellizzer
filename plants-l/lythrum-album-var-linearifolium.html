Lythrum album var. linearifolium

Lythrum album var. linearifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved common lythrum, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. The plant features slender, erect stems that are often reddish in color. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

The flowers are pink to purple, clustered in whorls, and bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
