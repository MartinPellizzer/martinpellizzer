Linaria bipunctata var. glauca

Linaria bipunctata var. glauca, commonly known as the blue poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet petals and slender, branching stems. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Its vibrant flowers make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental plants with a wild, colorful appearance.
