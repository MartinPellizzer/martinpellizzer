Limonium oleifolium subsp. sardoum

Limonium oleifolium subsp.

sardoum, commonly known as the Sardinian sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Sardinia. It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
