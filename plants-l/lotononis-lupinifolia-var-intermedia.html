Lotononis lupinifolia var. intermedia

Lotononis lupinifolia var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate lotononis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in fynbos vegetation, which is a unique biome known for its high biodiversity.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
