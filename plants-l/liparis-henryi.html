Liparis henryi

Liparis henryi, commonly known as Henry's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to China.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and linear leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in a raceme, typically blooming in late spring or early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Liparis henryi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
