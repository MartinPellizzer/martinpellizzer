Lathyrus friedrichsthalii

Lathyrus friedrichsthalii, commonly known as the Friedrichsthal lupine, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in temperate to cold climates. The plant features erect stems with pinnately compound leaves and produces spikes of pea-like flowers in shades of purple or blue. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are adapted for dispersal by wind.

Lathyrus friedrichsthalii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in nitrogen fixation in soil.
