Loranthus pubiflorus

Loranthus pubiflorus, commonly known as the pubiflorous mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees.

It belongs to the family Loranthaceae and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant relies on its host for water and nutrients, while it provides some nectar to attract pollinators. Its berries are typically red or orange and serve as a food source for birds and other animals.

Loranthus pubiflorus is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its host trees.
