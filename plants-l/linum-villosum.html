Linum villosum

Linum villosum, commonly known as silky flax, is a flowering plant native to Eurasia and parts of North America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, woolly appearance. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Silky flax is a member of the Linaceae family and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
