Lupinus lyallii var. macroflorus

Lupinus lyallii var. macroflorus, commonly known as the large-flowered lupine, is a perennial legume native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of vivid violet to blue flowers. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open meadows and rocky areas.

Lupinus lyallii var. macroflorus plays an important ecological role by fixing nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plant life.
