Luzula pallescens var. castanescens

Luzula pallescens var. castanescens, commonly known as the silver wood rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense clusters of small, light green to silver-gray leaves. The plant produces panicles of white or pale green flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Juncaceae family.
