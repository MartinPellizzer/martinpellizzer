Lespedeza × brittonii

Lespedeza × brittonii, commonly known as the hybrid lespedeza, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to fix nitrogen and prevent soil erosion.
