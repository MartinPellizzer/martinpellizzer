Linum cuspidatum

Linum cuspidatum, commonly known as the prickly poppy, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oval seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Linum cuspidatum is also known for its distinctive, sharp-tipped leaves and its role in supporting pollinators.
