Luzula multiflora var. pallescens

Luzula multiflora var. pallescens, commonly known as the pale flowered wood rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, acidic soils in forested areas, often in shaded environments. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of white or pale blue flowers. It is characterized by its slender stems and dense, grass-like foliage.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in wetland habitats.
