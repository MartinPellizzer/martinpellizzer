Lathyrus epetiolaris

Lathyrus epetiolaris, commonly known as the narrowleaf sweet pea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and clusters of fragrant, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several elliptical, edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental and edible plants.

Lathyrus epetiolaris is also valued for its historical use in traditional medicine.
