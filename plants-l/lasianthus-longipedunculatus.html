Lasianthus longipedunculatus

Lasianthus longipedunculatus, commonly known as the long-peduncled lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant features a bushy growth habit with opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in tropical regions.
