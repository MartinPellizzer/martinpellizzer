Leptocionium acanthoides

Leptocionium acanthoides, commonly known as the hairy spurge, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae, characterized by its milky sap and often unusual floral structures. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as fields and woodland edges. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The flowers are small and clustered in cyathia, a unique structure typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.
