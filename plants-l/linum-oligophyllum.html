Linum oligophyllum

Linum oligophyllum, commonly known as the lesser flax or narrow-leaved flax, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

Linum oligophyllum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in textile production.
