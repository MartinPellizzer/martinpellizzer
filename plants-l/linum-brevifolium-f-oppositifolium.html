Linum brevifolium f. oppositifolium

Linum brevifolium f. oppositifolium, commonly known as the opposite-leaved flax, is a botanical variant of the flax species characterized by its distinctive opposite leaf arrangement.

This plant typically features slender, branching stems covered in fine, silky hairs. The leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in pairs along the stem, giving it a unique appearance. It produces small, pale blue to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variety is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in fiber production.
