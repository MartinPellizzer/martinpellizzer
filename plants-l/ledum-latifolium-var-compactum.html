Ledum latifolium var. compactum

Ledum latifolium var. compactum, commonly known as the compact mountain tea, is a variant of the broadleaf labrador tea.

It is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. The plant features dense, compact foliage with dark green, leathery leaves that are aromatic when crushed. It produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers in late spring to early summer.

This variety thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded, rocky habitats.
