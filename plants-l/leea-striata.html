Leea striata

Leea striata, commonly known as the veined leea, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Leeaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves with prominent venation. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are small, capsule-like structures that release seeds when mature.

Leea striata is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.
