Ledebouria crispa

Ledebouria crispa, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a species of bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant has a short, sturdy stem and produces clusters of fleshy, often hairy leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Ledebouria crispa is a popular choice for rock gardens and containers due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
