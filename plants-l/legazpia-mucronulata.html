Legazpia mucronulata

Legazpia mucronulata, commonly known as the sharp-tipped legazpia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia and is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.
