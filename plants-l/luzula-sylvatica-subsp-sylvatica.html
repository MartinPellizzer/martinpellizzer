Luzula sylvatica subsp. sylvatica

Luzula sylvatica subsp.

sylvatica, commonly known as the wood sedge, is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and arranged in a dense rosette. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, three-lobed flowers that bloom in early summer.

The species is characterized by its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and its role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.
