Leptochilus pteropus subsp. pteropus

Leptochilus pteropus subsp.

pteropus, commonly known as the common rock orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

The plant features slender, upright stems with small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with a distinctive three-lobed lip.

This subspecies is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in coastal and inland rocky habitats.
