Lathyrus laevigatus subsp. aureus

Lathyrus laevigatus subsp.

aureus, commonly known as the golden pea or yellow vetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its pods contain small, dark brown seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
