Ladenbergia amazonensis

Ladenbergia amazonensis, commonly known as the Amazonian legume, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant has compound leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that attract pollinators. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in secondary growth areas.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
