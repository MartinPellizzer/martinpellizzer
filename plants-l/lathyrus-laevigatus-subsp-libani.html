Lathyrus laevigatus subsp. libani

Lathyrus laevigatus subsp.

libani, commonly known as the Lebanon pea, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pale blue to purple, often appearing in clusters. Its fruit is a slender pod containing several elliptical seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and blooms.
