Lonchocarpus apiciflorus

Lonchocarpus apiciflorus, commonly known as the needle-flowered legume, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, needle-like leaflets, giving it its common name. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with a unique, spurred shape.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
