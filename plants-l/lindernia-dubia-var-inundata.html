Lindernia dubia var. inundata

Lindernia dubia var. inundata, commonly known as the water lindernia, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that forms dense mats, often in marshy or seasonally flooded areas. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist to wet soil and is adapted to periodic flooding, making it an important species in riparian and wetland ecosystems.
