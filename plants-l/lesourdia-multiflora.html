Lesourdia multiflora

Lesourdia multiflora, commonly known as the many-flowered lesourdia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Lesourdia multiflora thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in grassland and rocky habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
