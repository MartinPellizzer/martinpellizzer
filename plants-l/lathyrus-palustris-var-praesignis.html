Lathyrus palustris var. praesignis

Lathyrus palustris var. praesignis, commonly known as the marsh vetchling, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces small, pea-like flowers in shades of pink to purple. The plant has slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in moist, nutrient-poor soils and is often found in marshes, bogs, and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
