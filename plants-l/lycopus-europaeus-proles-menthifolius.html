Lycopus europaeus proles menthifolius

Lycopus europaeus proles menthifolius, commonly known as European water hemp, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming from July to September. It thrives in moist, often wet environments such as marshes, stream banks, and damp woodlands.

Its aromatic leaves and flowers have been used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported calming and antispasmodic properties.
