Libidibia granadillo

Libidibia granadillo, commonly known as granadillo or yellowwood, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hard, dense wood and distinctive yellowish-brown bark. The plant has bipinnate leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by legumes containing small, round seeds.

It is often found in lowland and mountain rainforests, where it plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various wildlife species.
