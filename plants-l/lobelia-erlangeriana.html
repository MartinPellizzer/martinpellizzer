Lobelia erlangeriana

Lobelia erlangeriana, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, woodland environments. The plant features showy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling a misty cloud. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a rough texture.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
