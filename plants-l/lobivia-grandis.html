Lobivia grandis

Lobivia grandis, commonly known as the grand Lobivia, is a species of cactus native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems and spiny, paddle-shaped leaves that help it conserve water in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual element to its desert habitat. Lobivia grandis is typically found at high altitudes, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping in dry climates.
