Lupinus hazenanus

Lupinus hazenanus, commonly known as the Hagen's lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces spiny, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in ecological restoration projects. Lupinus hazenanus thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its blooms, which range from blue to purple, add visual interest to native plant gardens and support local pollinators.
