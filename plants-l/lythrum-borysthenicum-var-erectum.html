Lythrum borysthenicum var. erectum

Lythrum borysthenicum var. erectum, commonly known as the tall loosestrife, is a herbaceous perennial native to the wetlands of eastern Europe and western Asia.

It features upright, branching stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, supporting showy, pink to purple flowers arranged in dense spikes. The plant has lance-shaped, opposite leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its root system is fibrous and adapted to waterlogged soils. It thrives in moist to aquatic environments and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
