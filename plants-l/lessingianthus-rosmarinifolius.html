Lessingianthus rosmarinifolius

Lessingianthus rosmarinifolius, commonly known as the silverleaf sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and bright yellow ray florets. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its drought tolerance. It produces small, tubular disk flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are aromatic and have a mint-like scent, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
