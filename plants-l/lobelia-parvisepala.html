Lobelia parvisepala

Lobelia parvisepala, commonly known as the small-leaf lobelia, is a herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Lobelia parvisepala is often found in woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
