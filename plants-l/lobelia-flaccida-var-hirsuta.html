Lobelia flaccida var. hirsuta

Lobelia flaccida var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and also hairy, contributing to its overall textured foliage.

Lobelia flaccida var. hirsuta thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.
