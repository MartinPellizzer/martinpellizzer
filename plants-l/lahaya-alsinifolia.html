Lahaya alsinifolia

Lahaya alsinifolia, commonly known as the Mexican dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It produces yellow flowers that develop into fluffy, white seed heads, typical of the genus.

Lahaya alsinifolia is often found in dry, rocky soils and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
