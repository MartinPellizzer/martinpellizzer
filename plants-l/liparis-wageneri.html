Liparis wageneri

Liparis wageneri, commonly known as the Wagener's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its flowers are usually white or pale green, with three petals and three sepals, forming a showy inflorescence.

Liparis wageneri is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by supporting pollinators.
