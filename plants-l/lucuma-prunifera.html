Lucuma prunifera

Lucuma prunifera, commonly known as the golden fruit or lucuma, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its evergreen shrub growth habit. The plant produces large, yellow-orange berries that are encased in a fleshy, spiky exterior. The fruit's pulp is rich in nutrients, including vitamin C and dietary fiber, and is often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications.

Its botanical features make it a unique and valuable species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
