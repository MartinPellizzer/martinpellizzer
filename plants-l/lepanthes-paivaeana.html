Lepanthes paivaeana

Lepanthes paivaeana, commonly known as the Peacock Orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that resemble peacock feathers, with intricate patterns and vibrant hues. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded forest environments. Its flowers are usually small to medium in size and are often fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and moths.

Lepanthes paivaeana is valued for its ornamental beauty and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its native habitats.
