Leontopodium haastioides

Leontopodium haastioides, commonly known as the alpine edelweiss, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas and other mountainous regions.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly white foliage and showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in cool, dry environments with well-drained soils, often growing in rocky or alpine habitats. Its unique appearance and habitat make it a symbol of resilience in extreme conditions.

Due to its ecological significance and aesthetic value, it is also a protected species in many regions.
