Lobelia nicotianifolia var. trichandra

Lobelia nicotianifolia var. trichandra, commonly known as the velvetleaf lobelia, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features lance-shaped, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical structure makes it a valuable species for ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
