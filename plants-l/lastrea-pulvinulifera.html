Lastrea pulvinulifera

Lastrea pulvinulifera, commonly known as the Cape honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has opposite, narrow, and often hairy leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in arid environments. Its stems are usually woody and can grow either as shrubs or small trees.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its showy blooms.
