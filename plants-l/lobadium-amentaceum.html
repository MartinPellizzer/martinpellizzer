Lobadium amentaceum

Lobadium amentaceum, commonly known as the weeping lichen, is a unique symbiotic organism composed of a fungus and a green alga.

It forms dense, hanging clusters that resemble miniature trees, often found on tree trunks and branches in moist, shaded environments. The plant's structure is characterized by its intricate network of filaments, which provide stability and aid in nutrient absorption. Its growth is slow, and it thrives in cool, humid climates with minimal direct sunlight.

Lobadium amentaceum plays an important ecological role by contributing to the decomposition of organic matter and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
