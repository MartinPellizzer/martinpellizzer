Lightfootia abyssinica var. glaberrima

Lightfootia abyssinica var. glaberrima, commonly known as the Abyssinian lightfootia, is a rare and unique plant native to Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and distinctive, fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves. The leaves are typically grayish-green in color and covered in a fine, powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss in its arid environment. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, adding a subtle ornamental value to the plant.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a valuable species for botanical study and conservation efforts.
