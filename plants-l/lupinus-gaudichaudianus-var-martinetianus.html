Lupinus gaudichaudianus var. martinetianus

Lupinus gaudichaudianus var. martinetianus, commonly known as the Martinet lupine, is a perennial legume native to the coastal regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of vibrant blue to purple flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal climates with high salinity and wind exposure.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
