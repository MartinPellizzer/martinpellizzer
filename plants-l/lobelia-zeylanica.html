Lobelia zeylanica

Lobelia zeylanica, commonly known as the Ceylon lobelia, is a herbaceous plant native to Sri Lanka.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a hairy texture.

Lobelia zeylanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
