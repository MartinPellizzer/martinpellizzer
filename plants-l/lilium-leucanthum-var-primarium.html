Lilium leucanthum var. primarium

Lilium leucanthum var. primarium, commonly known as the white lily or primrose lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It features tall, straight stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, supporting large, showy white or pale pink flowers with a distinctive, recurved shape. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It blooms in late spring to early summer, producing fragrant flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The bulbs of this species are used in traditional herbal medicine and are also cultivated for ornamental purposes.
