Lupinus chavanillensis

Lupinus chavanillensis, commonly known as the Chavanilla lupine, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly found in regions of Chile and Argentina. This plant is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of pea-like flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Lupinus chavanillensis is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in ecological and agricultural contexts.
