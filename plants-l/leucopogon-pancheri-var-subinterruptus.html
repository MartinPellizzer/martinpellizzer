Leucopogon pancheri var. subinterruptus

Leucopogon pancheri var. subinterruptus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially sunny conditions. Its botanical features include narrow, leathery leaves and pink to purple flowers that bloom in the spring.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
