Lepironia bancana

Lepironia bancana, commonly known as the Indian lotus, is a tropical aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy pink or white flowers that bloom above the water surface. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically floating or semi-submerged. Its roots form dense mats that help stabilize water bodies and prevent erosion.

Lepironia bancana plays a vital role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
