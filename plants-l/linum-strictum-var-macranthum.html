Linum strictum var. macranthum

Linum strictum var. macranthum, commonly known as the tall flax or giant flax, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
