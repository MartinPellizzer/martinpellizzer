Lagenocarpus oocarpus

Lagenocarpus oocarpus, commonly known as the yellow mangrove, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellowish bark and its ability to thrive in brackish water environments. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in axillary inflorescences.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, aiding in its propagation in coastal areas.
