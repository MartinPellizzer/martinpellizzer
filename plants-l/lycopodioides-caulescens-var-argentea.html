Lycopodioides caulescens var. argentea

Lycopodioides caulescens var. argentea, commonly known as silver-stemmed clubmoss, is a variant of the clubmoss genus Lycopodioides.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-tinged stems that are covered in a dense layer of tiny, scale-like structures. The plant produces spore-bearing cones at the tips of its stems, which are typically clustered in dense, cylindrical formations. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant species within the Lycopodiaceae family.
