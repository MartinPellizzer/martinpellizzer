Lathyrus quercetorum

Lathyrus quercetorum, commonly known as the oak-leaved vetch, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, woodland habitats. The plant features palmately compound leaves that resemble the shape of oak leaves, giving it its common name. Its flowers are usually pink or purple and are arranged in racemes, adding ornamental value to the landscape.

Lathyrus quercetorum is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its potential use in traditional herbal medicine.
