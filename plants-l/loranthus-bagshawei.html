Loranthus bagshawei

Loranthus bagshawei, commonly known as the Chinese mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily in the family Rosaceae.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using the light it captures. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces yellowish-white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its berries are typically red or orange, containing seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This species is native to China and is often found in forested areas where its host trees thrive.
