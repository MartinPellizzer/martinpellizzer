Limonium ramosissimum subsp. confusum

Limonium ramosissimum subsp.

confusum, commonly known as the coastal flax or sea helene, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in coastal areas, sandy soils, and saline environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes.
