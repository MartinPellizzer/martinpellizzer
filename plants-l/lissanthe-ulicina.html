Lissanthe ulicina

Lissanthe ulicina, commonly known as the goldenstar sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and streambanks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branched.

Lissanthe ulicina plays an important role in supporting local pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
