Lepanthes tricarinata

Lepanthes tricarinata, commonly known as the three-ribbed orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central and South America.

It is a small epiphytic orchid, typically growing on trees in humid tropical forests. The plant features delicate, strap-like leaves and produces intricate, fragrant flowers with three prominent carinae (ridges) on the petals. Its blooming period usually occurs during the rainy season, and it thrives in shaded, moist environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
