Lobelia parvidentata

Lobelia parvidentata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually less than an inch in width, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.

Lobelia parvidentata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
