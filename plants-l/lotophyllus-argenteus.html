Lotophyllus argenteus

Lotophyllus argenteus, commonly known as the silverleaf lotus, is a rare and striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its velvety, silver-green foliage, which reflects sunlight and helps it thrive in cooler, shaded environments. The plant produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale pink to white, often blooming in the early morning. Its unique structure includes thick, waxy leaves that store moisture, adapting it to its arid habitat.

Despite its beauty, it is highly sensitive to environmental changes, making it a challenging species to cultivate outside its native range.
