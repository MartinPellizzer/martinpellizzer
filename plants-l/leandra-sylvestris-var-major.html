Leandra sylvestris var. major

Leandra sylvestris var. major, commonly known as the giant leandra, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, waxy, and often fragrant leaves, which can grow up to several feet in length. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the late spring or early summer. Its foliage is evergreen, providing a lush, tropical appearance throughout the year.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil for optimal growth.
