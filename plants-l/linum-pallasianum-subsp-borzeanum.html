Linum pallasianum subsp. borzeanum

Linum pallasianum subsp.

borzeanum, commonly known as the Pallas's flax, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia. It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, blue to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphological features and ecological adaptations.
