Lasianthus tamdaoensis

Lasianthus tamdaoensis, commonly known as the Tamdao flame vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking red-orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a climbing shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its ornamental value is primarily due to its vibrant floral display, which attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
