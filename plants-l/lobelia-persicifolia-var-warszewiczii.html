Lobelia persicifolia var. warszewiczii

Lobelia persicifolia var. warszewiczii, commonly known as the Warszewicz's lobelia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and vibrant floral display.
