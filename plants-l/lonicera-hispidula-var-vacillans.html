Lonicera hispidula var. vacillans

Lonicera hispidula var. vacillans, commonly known as the variable honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are usually covered with fine, grayish hairs. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to yellow, typically blooming in late spring. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that matures in the fall.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
