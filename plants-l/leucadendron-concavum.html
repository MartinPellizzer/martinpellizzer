Leucadendron concavum

Leucadendron concavum, commonly known as the silver tree, is a striking evergreen shrub or small tree native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-blue foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces striking yellow or orange flower heads in late winter to early spring, which are surrounded by bracts. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Leucadendron concavum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its unique visual characteristics.
