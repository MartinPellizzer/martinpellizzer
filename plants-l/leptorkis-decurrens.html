Leptorkis decurrens

Leptorkis decurrens, commonly known as the creeping rock cress, is a low-growing perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically features succulent, fleshy stems that lie close to the ground, often forming dense mats. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Leptorkis decurrens is adapted to rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or woodland environments.
