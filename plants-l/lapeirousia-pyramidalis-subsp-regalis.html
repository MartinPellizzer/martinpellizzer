Lapeirousia pyramidalis subsp. regalis

Lapeirousia pyramidalis subsp.

regalis, commonly known as the royal pyramid bell orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to South Africa. This subspecies is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of vibrant, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple.

The plant typically blooms in late summer, producing showy inflorescences that attract specific pollinators. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

Due to its ornamental value and conservation status, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
