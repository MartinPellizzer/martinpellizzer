Lonchocarpus canoensis

Lonchocarpus canoensis, commonly known as the golden senna, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is known for its unique seed pods, which are long and curved, containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some tropical gardens.
