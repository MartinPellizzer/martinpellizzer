Loranthus hemsleyanus

Loranthus hemsleyanus, commonly known as the Hemsley's mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on a variety of host trees, primarily in the tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Loranthaceae and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and small, white or yellowish flowers. The plant relies on its host for water and minerals but produces its own carbohydrates through photosynthesis. Its seeds are dispersed by birds, which consume the fleshy, red berries and later excrete the seeds onto suitable host trees.

This parasitic relationship plays a significant role in the ecological dynamics of its native habitats.
