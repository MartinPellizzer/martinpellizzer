Lycium afrum

Lycium afrum, commonly known as the African boxthorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, red or yellow berries that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, demonstrating adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are typically narrow and waxy, reducing water loss through transpiration.

Lycium afrum plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for birds and small mammals.
