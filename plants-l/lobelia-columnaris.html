Lobelia columnaris

Lobelia columnaris, commonly known as the everlasting lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright, branching stems that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Lobelia columnaris is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.
