Luzula × johannis-principis

Luzula × johannis-principis, commonly known as the royal wood rush, is a hybrid evergreen perennial plant belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and compact growth habit, often forming low, mat-like colonies. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, greenish-yellow flowers, typical of the grass family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
