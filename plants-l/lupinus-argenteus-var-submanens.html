Lupinus argenteus var. submanens

Lupinus argenteus var. submanens, commonly known as silver lupine or silverblue lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-blue flowers and hairy, silver-green foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and sagebrush steppe. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
