Luzula campestris var. erectiuscula

Luzula campestris var. erectiuscula, commonly known as the upright woodland sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and somewhat rigid. Its flowering stems are upright and bear small, inconspicuous spikelets that produce tiny seeds.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partially shaded conditions.
