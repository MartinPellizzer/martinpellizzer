Lupinus prostratus

Lupinus prostratus, commonly known as the prostrate lupine, is a low-growing, perennial legume native to the western United States.

It features slender, prostrate stems that spread along the ground, often forming dense mats. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of blue, purple, or white, typical of the lupine genus. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

Lupinus prostratus is adapted to rocky and sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland desert regions.
