Linosyris ciliata

Linosyris ciliata, commonly known as the hairy tansy, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance, and its yellow flowers bloom in the summer months.
