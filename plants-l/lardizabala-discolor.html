Lardizabala discolor

Lardizabala discolor, commonly known as the Andean morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to blue flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped, broad leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. It thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

Lardizabala discolor is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
