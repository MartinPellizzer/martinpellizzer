Licuala sallehana var. sallehana

Licuala sallehana var. sallehana, commonly known as the Sallehan palm, is a species native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Malaysia and Indonesia.

It is a tall, slender palm with a smooth, unbranched trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant thrives in tropical rainforest environments, often growing in shaded, moist areas with rich, well-drained soil. Its leaves are used in traditional crafts and as a roofing material in some regions.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
