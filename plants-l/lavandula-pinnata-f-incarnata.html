Lavandula pinnata f. incarnata

Lavandula pinnata f. incarnata, commonly known as the pink lavender, is a fragrant flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features narrow, linear leaves and produces spikes of pink to lavender-colored flowers. The plant is characterized by its aromatic foliage and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice in both gardens and herbal applications.
