Lobivia hertrichiana

Lobivia hertrichiana, commonly known as the Hertrich's lobivia, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded growth form and thick, ribbed stems that store water. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. Its leaves are modified into spines, which help reduce water loss and deter herbivores.

Lobivia hertrichiana is well adapted to harsh desert environments, making it a resilient and hardy plant.
