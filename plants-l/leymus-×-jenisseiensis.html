Leymus × jenisseiensis

Leymus × jenisseiensis, commonly known as the Siberian wheatgrass, is a hybrid grass species native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, grass-like foliage that often forms a mat-like structure. This plant is well adapted to harsh environments, tolerating low temperatures and poor soil conditions. It has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Leymus × jenisseiensis is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil in arid or disturbed areas.
