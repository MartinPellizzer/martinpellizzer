Lobelia tenerrima

Lobelia tenerrima, commonly known as the everlasting lobelia, is a perennial plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and spikes of blue to purple flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Lobelia tenerrima is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
