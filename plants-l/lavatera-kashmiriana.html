Lavatera kashmiriana

Lavatera kashmiriana, commonly known as Kashmir rose, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of Pakistan and India.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring soft, hairy stems and large, showy flowers. The flowers are usually pink or white, with five petals and a prominent center, blooming in summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Its botanical significance lies in its ornamental value and its role in local ecosystems.
