Leontodon montanus subsp. montaniformis

Leontodon montanus subsp.

montaniformis, commonly known as the mountain leontodon, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the daisy family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, yellowish-green composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, and it is often found in subalpine and alpine meadows.
