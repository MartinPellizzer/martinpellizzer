Lupinus verbasciformis

Lupinus verbasciformis, commonly known as the flamebrush lupine, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and bright yellow or orange flowers that resemble small brushes. The plant has palmate leaves with three to five leaflets, and its root system includes nitrogen-fixing symbiotic bacteria. Lupinus verbasciformis thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
