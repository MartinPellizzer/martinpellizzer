Linaria pygmaea

Linaria pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf toadflax, is a low-growing annual plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features slender, branching stems that typically reach heights of only a few centimeters. The plant produces small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, often with a bluish-green hue.

Linaria pygmaea thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
