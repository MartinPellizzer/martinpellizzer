Lathyrus jepsonii

Lathyrus jepsonii, commonly known as Jepson's vetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are adapted for dispersal by wind, contributing to its ecological role in stabilizing soil.

Lathyrus jepsonii is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
