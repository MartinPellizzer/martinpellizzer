Luzula campestris f. erecta

Luzula campestris f. erecta, commonly known as the upright tufted sedge, is a variant of the tufted sedge species.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, tufted foliage. The plant typically features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often glaucous in appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is commonly found in wet meadows and along stream banks.

This form is notable for its compact growth and is often used in horticultural settings for its ornamental value.
