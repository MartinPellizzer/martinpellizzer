Linaria Pyramidalis Subsp. Kopetdaghensis

Linaria pyramidalis subsp. kopetdaghensis is a lesser-known member of the Plantaginaceae family that thrives in arid and semi-arid regions. This subspecies is native to the Kopet Dagh mountain range, spanning parts of Turkmenistan and Iran. It is distinguished by its striking, pyramidal flower clusters and vibrant yellow-orange blooms. Due to its limited distribution and unique ecological niche, it is considered a rare and endangered plant. Conservation efforts are crucial to preserving this delicate species in its natural habitat.
