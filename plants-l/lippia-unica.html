Lippia unica

Lippia unica, commonly known as wild verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as roadsides and woodlands. The plant features slender, branched stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Lippia unica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
