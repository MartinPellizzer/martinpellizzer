Lepidium linearilobum

Lepidium linearilobum, commonly known as the narrow-lobed wallflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and elongated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It produces small, round pods that contain several seeds, and it is adapted to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural studies.
