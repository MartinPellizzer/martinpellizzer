Leontodon arcuatus var. tomentosus

Leontodon arcuatus var. tomentosus, commonly known as the woolly hairgrass, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, giving it a distinctive, tufted appearance.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer, contributing to its role in supporting pollinators.
