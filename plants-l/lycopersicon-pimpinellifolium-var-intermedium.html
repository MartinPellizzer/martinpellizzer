Lycopersicon pimpinellifolium var. intermedium

Lycopersicon pimpinellifolium var. intermedium, commonly known as the wild tomato, is a botanical species native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. The plant has hairy stems and leaves with a serrated edge, and it produces small, yellow flowers. It is a wild relative of the domesticated tomato, offering genetic diversity for breeding programs.

This species thrives in temperate climates and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
