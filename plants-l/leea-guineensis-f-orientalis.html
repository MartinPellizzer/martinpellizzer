Leea guineensis f. orientalis

Leea guineensis f. orientalis, commonly known as the oriental leaf vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Leeaceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often glabrous or slightly pubescent. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruits are berries that mature to a dark red or black color, containing several seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally used in traditional medicine.
