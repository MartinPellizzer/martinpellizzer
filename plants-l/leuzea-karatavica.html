Leuzea karatavica

Leuzea karatavica, commonly known as Karatavsky leuzea, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense, terminal clusters. It thrives in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Leuzea karatavica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
