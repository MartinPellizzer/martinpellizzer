Lotus cytisoides subsp. allionii

Lotus cytisoides subsp.

allionii, commonly known as the yellow lotus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East. It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
