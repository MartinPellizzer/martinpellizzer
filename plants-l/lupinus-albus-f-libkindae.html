Lupinus albus f. libkindae

Lupinus albus f. libkindae, commonly known as the white lupin, is a botanical variant of the white lupin species.

It is characterized by its white to pale yellow flowers and narrow, linear leaves. This subspecies is native to parts of Europe and the Mediterranean region. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

The plant is known for its nitrogen-fixing abilities, making it valuable in agricultural settings.
