Lupinus oreganus var. pusillulus

Lupinus oreganus var. pusillulus, commonly known as the narrowleaf lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and clusters of blue to violet pea-like flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Lupinus oreganus var. pusillulus is an important species for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
