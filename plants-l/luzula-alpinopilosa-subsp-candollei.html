Luzula alpinopilosa subsp. candollei

Luzula alpinopilosa subsp.

candollei, commonly known as the alpine feathergrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to high-altitude regions of the Himalayas and Central Asia. It typically grows in rocky or alpine meadows, thriving in cool, moist environments.

The plant features slender, grass-like leaves that are often hairy, and it produces small, greenish to yellowish spikelets in clusters. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and ability to survive in harsh, nutrient-poor soils.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing mountainous terrain.
