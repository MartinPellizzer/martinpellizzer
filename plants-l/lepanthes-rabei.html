Lepanthes rabei

Lepanthes rabei, commonly known as the Rabei's slipper orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often colorful flowers that resemble small slippers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its flowers are usually fragrant and attract various pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

Lepanthes rabei is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated by enthusiasts of tropical flora.
