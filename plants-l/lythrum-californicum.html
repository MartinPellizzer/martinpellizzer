Lythrum californicum

Lythrum californicum, commonly known as California lythrum, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lythraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy spikes of violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist soils along streambanks and wet meadows, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

California lythrum is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
