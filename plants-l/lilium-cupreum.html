Lilium cupreum

Lilium cupreum, commonly known as the copper lily, is a species of bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep orange, often with a distinctive coppery hue. The plant produces a single, showy bloom on a tall, slender stem, typically reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are long, narrow, and grass-like, forming a basal rosette.

Lilium cupreum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, eye-catching flowers.
