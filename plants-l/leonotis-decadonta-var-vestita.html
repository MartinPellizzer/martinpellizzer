Leonotis decadonta var. vestita

Leonotis decadonta var. vestita, commonly known as the velvet leonotis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking orange-yellow tubular flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a velvety texture, giving it its common name. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

This variant is known for its compact growth habit and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
