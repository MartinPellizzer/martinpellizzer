Leucopogon lanceolatus var. alpestris

Leucopogon lanceolatus var. alpestris, commonly known as the alpine leucopogon, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is a perennial herb with slender, erect stems that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale pink flowers during the spring and early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and typically have a slightly hairy surface, adapted to the harsh alpine environment.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, rocky habitats at high elevations.
