Lycopodium pictum

Lycopodium pictum, commonly known as the painted clubmoss, is a species of fern-like plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves that form dense, mat-like growths on the forest floor. The plant produces spore-bearing structures called strobila, which are clustered at the tips of its branching stems. Lycopodium pictum thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in temperate forests.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it an important component of its native habitat.
