Leucocoryne ungulifera

Leucocoryne ungulifera, commonly known as the clawed lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, narrow, strap-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces striking white to pale pink flowers that are clustered in umbels, giving it a unique and elegant appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
