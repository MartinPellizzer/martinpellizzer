Lepanthes elytrifera

Lepanthes elytrifera, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with intricate patterns and a distinctive, elongated lip. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within tropical forests. Its leaves are broad and ovate, often covered in a fine, velvety texture.

Lepanthes elytrifera is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
