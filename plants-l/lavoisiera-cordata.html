Lavoisiera cordata

Lavoisiera cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf lavoisiera, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically a deep green and have a velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often blooming in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Lavoisiera cordata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in a garden setting.
