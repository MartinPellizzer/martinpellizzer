Leucas aspera

Leucas aspera, commonly known as rough leucas, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and riverbanks. Its leaves are rough and hairy, giving it its common name, while its small yellow flowers bloom in clusters.

Leucas aspera is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
