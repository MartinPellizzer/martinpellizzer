Lepechinia clinopodiifolia

Lepechinia clinopodiifolia, commonly known as slender lepechinia, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as streambanks and meadows. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a hairy texture.

The small, tubular flowers are usually purple or blue and bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
