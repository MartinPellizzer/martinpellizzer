Linum usitatissimum subsp. villarianum

Linum usitatissimum subsp.

villarianum, commonly known as the Persian flax, is a subspecies of the common flax plant. It is native to regions of the Middle East and Central Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates.

This plant is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, blue or purple flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

The subspecies is cultivated for its fibers, which are used in textile production, and for its seeds, which are rich in oil and nutrients.
