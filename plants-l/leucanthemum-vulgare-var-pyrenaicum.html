Leucanthemum vulgare var. pyrenaicum

Leucanthemum vulgare var. pyrenaicum, commonly known as the Pyrenean white daisy, is a flowering plant native to the Pyrenees region.

It is characterized by its white ray florets surrounding a yellow disc flower, giving it a daisy-like appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils. It produces small, herbaceous stems and has basal leaves that are often hairy.

This variety is known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in cooler climates.
