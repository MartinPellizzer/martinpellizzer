Loranthus uniflorus

Loranthus uniflorus, commonly known as the single-flower mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches itself to host trees, primarily in the family Fabaceae.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own carbohydrates through photosynthesis using the host's light exposure. The plant has a distinctive appearance with a smooth, waxy stem and a single, terminal flower that is often white or pale green. Its berries, which are typically red or orange, contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This species is found in various parts of Africa and is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
