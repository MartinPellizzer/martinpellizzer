Lysimachia melampyroides var. amplexicaulis

Lysimachia melampyroides var. amplexicaulis, commonly known as golden thread, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in terminal racemes. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a reddish or purple hue on the underside.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is commonly found in woodland areas and along stream banks.
