Latania borbonica

Latania borbonica, commonly known as the Bourbon palm, is a tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in warm, humid climates. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.

The fruit of Latania borbonica is edible and has been used in local cuisines.
