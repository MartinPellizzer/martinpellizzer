Leptospermum polygalifolium subsp. cismontanum

Leptospermum polygalifolium subsp.

cismontanum, commonly known as the Cismontane tea tree, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern California. It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant is well adapted to sandy, well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

It is also valued for its use in traditional herbal remedies.
