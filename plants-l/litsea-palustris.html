Litsea palustris

Litsea palustris, commonly known as swamp litsea, is a small evergreen shrub native to wetland habitats in Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in swampy or seasonally flooded areas, showcasing its adaptation to waterlogged soils. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a glossy, dark green upper surface. Its small, fragrant flowers are usually white or pale yellow and appear in clusters, followed by small, orange-red berries.

Litsea palustris plays an important ecological role in its native wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
