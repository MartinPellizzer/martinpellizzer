Lastrea prionitis

Lastrea prionitis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lastrea, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family, which is characterized by its fleshy leaves and adaptations to arid environments. The plant features narrow, upright leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder, known as farina, which helps reduce water loss. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the warmer months.

This species is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
