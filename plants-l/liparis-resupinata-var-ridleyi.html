Liparis resupinata var. ridleyi

Liparis resupinata var. ridleyi, commonly known as the Ridley's lady's tresses, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in a loose spiral. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forest floors and rocky crevices. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, often forming a dense rosette.

The species is notable for its delicate appearance and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
