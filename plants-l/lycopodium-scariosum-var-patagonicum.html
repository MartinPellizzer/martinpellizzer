Lycopodium scariosum var. patagonicum

Lycopodium scariosum var. patagonicum, commonly known as the Patagonian clubmoss, is a species of lycophyte native to the southern regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and spore-bearing cones, which are typical of the Lycopodiaceae family. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. Its sporophytic structures are often clustered closely together, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The plant plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
