Lachenalia krugeri

Lachenalia krugeri, commonly known as the Cape bell, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rosette-shaped foliage. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
