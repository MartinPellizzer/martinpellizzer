Lycopodium inundatum var. typicum

Lycopodium inundatum var. typicum, commonly known as the water fern, is a species of aquatic fern that thrives in shallow, freshwater environments.

It features feathery, light green fronds that are typically submerged or partially submerged, contributing to its ornamental appeal. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread across the substrate and form dense mats. It plays a role in water purification by absorbing nutrients and improving water clarity.

Lycopodium inundatum var. typicum is often used in ponds and water gardens for its aesthetic value and ecological benefits.
