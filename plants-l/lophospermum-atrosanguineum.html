Lophospermum atrosanguineum

Lophospermum atrosanguineum, commonly known as the purple passion vine, is a climbing perennial plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and clusters of showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to deep purple. The plant produces small, yellowish fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive if not managed properly.
