Lilium columbianum

Lilium columbianum, commonly known as the Columbia lily, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a tall, upright stem that can reach up to 2 meters in height. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

The Columbia lily is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
