Lepanthes elizabethae

Lepanthes elizabethae, commonly known as the Elizabeth's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree branches without deriving nutrients from them. Its flowers are usually small, with a unique coloration that includes shades of white, yellow, and green.

Lepanthes elizabethae is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
