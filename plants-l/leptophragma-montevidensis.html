Leptophragma montevidensis

Leptophragma montevidensis, commonly known as the Montevideo flax, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Uruguay and parts of Argentina.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.
