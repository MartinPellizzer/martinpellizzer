Levenhookia pulcherrima

Levenhookia pulcherrima, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a contrasting darker throat. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its showy blooms attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, enhancing its ecological value.
