Lepanthes lineolata

Lepanthes lineolata, commonly known as the striped orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, greenish-white flowers with distinctive dark streaks or lines on the petals and sepals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical forests. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Lepanthes lineolata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
