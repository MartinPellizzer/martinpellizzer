Lepanthes quadricornis

Lepanthes quadricornis, commonly known as the four-horned orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, brightly colored flowers that often feature four prominent lobes, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments within tropical forests. Its leaves are usually elongated and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in moisture retention.

Lepanthes quadricornis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
