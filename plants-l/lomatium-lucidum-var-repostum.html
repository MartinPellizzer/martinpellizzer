Lomatium lucidum var. repostum

Lomatium lucidum var. repostum, commonly known as the golden lomatium, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clustered umbels, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use by indigenous peoples for medicinal purposes.
