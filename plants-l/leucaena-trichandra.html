Leucaena trichandra

Leucaena trichandra, commonly known as the white leucaena, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its nitrogen-fixing ability through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. The plant has trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet being elliptic to ovate in shape. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in dense clusters, and it produces elongated pods containing several seeds.

Leucaena trichandra is often used in agroforestry and as a forage crop due to its adaptability and nutritional value.
