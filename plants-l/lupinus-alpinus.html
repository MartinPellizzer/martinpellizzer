Lupinus alpinus

Lupinus alpinus, commonly known as the alpine lupine, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant produces tall spikes of blue to violet flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Lupinus alpinus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
