Lycium californicum var. arizonicum

Lycium californicum var. arizonicum, commonly known as the Arizona boxthorn, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, orange-red berries that appear in the fall. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in desert and semi-desert environments. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are typically yellow or orange.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
