Leptocionium abruptum

Leptocionium abruptum, commonly known as the abrupt bristlegrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils such as those found in meadows, roadsides, and stream banks. The plant features slender, ascending stems that bear small, lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces. Its flowers are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences, producing tiny, greenish-white blossoms.

Leptocionium abruptum is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
