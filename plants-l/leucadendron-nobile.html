Leucadendron nobile

Leucadendron nobile, commonly known as the silvercurled leucadendron, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, silver-white, velvety leaves that are arranged in a distinctive spiral pattern. The plant produces cone-like inflorescences that range in color from yellow to red, depending on the species. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and seasonal blooms.
