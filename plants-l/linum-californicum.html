Linum californicum

Linum californicum, commonly known as California flax, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It produces delicate blue to violet flowers and has lance-shaped leaves with a soft, hairy texture. The plant is characterized by its slender stems and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It belongs to the Linaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.

The seeds of California flax are also used in some herbal preparations.
