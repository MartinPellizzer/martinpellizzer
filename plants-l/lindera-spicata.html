Lindera spicata

Lindera spicata, commonly known as roughleaf arrowwood, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels and avocado trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, opposite, and often hairy leaves that turn reddish in the fall. Its small, yellowish-green flowers appear in clusters and are followed by dark, spherical berries.
