Litsea geniculata

Litsea geniculata, commonly known as the fragrant sumac, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, ovate leaves that emit a citrus-like scent when crushed. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by spherical, red berries that are edible for birds and mammals. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Litsea geniculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
