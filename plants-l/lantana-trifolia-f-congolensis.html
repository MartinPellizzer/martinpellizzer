Lantana trifolia f. congolensis

Lantana trifolia f. congolensis, commonly known as the Congo lantana, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are typically lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, which attract various pollinators. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it adaptable to a variety of environmental conditions.

However, it can be invasive in some areas, posing a threat to native plant species.
