Lobivia callochrysea

Lobivia callochrysea, commonly known as the Golden Crowned Lobivia, is a species of cactus native to Argentina.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and is adorned with dense, yellowish-white spines that give it its common name. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers during the spring season. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
