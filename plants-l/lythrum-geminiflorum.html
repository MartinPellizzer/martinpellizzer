Lythrum geminiflorum

Lythrum geminiflorum, commonly known as purple loosestrife, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky stems and showy purple-pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are opposite along the stem, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Due to its aggressive growth, it is considered an invasive species in many parts of North America.
