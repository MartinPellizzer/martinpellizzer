Listrostachys amaniensis

Listrostachys amaniensis, commonly known as the Amani grass, is a perennial grass species native to the island of Mafia in Tanzania.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, pale green to brownish flowers. Its leaves are narrow, slightly hairy, and have a bluish-green hue when fresh.

This grass is adapted to arid and semi-arid conditions, making it a resilient plant in its native habitat.
