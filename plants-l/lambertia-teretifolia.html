Lambertia teretifolia

Lambertia teretifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf lambertia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and dense clusters of small, red or orange flowers that bloom in winter. The leaves are narrow and strap-like, often with a slightly waxy texture. This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in coastal climates.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.
