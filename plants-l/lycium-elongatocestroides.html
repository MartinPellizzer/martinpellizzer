Lycium elongatocestroides

Lycium elongatocestroides, commonly known as the slender boxthorn, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, fleshy leaves. The plant produces yellowish flowers that give way to red, berry-like fruits, which are edible but often bitter. It is adapted to survive in harsh conditions with minimal water, making it a resilient component of its ecosystem.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and potential medicinal studies.
