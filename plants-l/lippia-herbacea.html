Lippia herbacea

Lippia herbacea, commonly known as Mexican mint or wild mint, is a perennial herb native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant leaves that resemble those of mint. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. Its small, tubular flowers are usually white or pale purple and appear in clusters.

Lippia herbacea is often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb due to its refreshing scent and potential medicinal properties.
