Leptophoenix yulensis

Leptophoenix yulensis, commonly known as the Yulens Island palm, is a rare and endemic plant species found exclusively on the island of Yulens in the South Pacific.

It is a small, slender palm with a single trunk and feather-like leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers followed by elongated, fibrous fruits. Its botanical significance lies in its unique adaptation to the island's specific climate and soil conditions.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat vulnerability, it is considered a conservation priority.
