Lepidium lanatum

Lepidium lanatum, commonly known as the hairy cress, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, and it produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is considered an invasive species in some regions.

Lepidium lanatum is also known for its peppery taste, similar to that of mustard.
