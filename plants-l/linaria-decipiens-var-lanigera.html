Linaria decipiens var. lanigera

Linaria decipiens var. lanigera, commonly known as the woolly toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and bright yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and desert environments. It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Due to its invasive potential, it is sometimes considered a noxious weed in certain regions.
