Lowea

Lowea, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to Africa.

These plants are characterized by their velvety, waxy leaves and showy, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors. Lowea species are typically compact and thrive in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light. They are popular houseplants due to their ornamental value and ease of care.

The flowers often bloom in clusters and are pollinated by insects such as bees and butterflies.
