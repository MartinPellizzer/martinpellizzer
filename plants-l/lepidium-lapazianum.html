Lepidium lapazianum

Lepidium lapazianum, commonly known as the Peruvian pepper or lapazian mustard, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard plants. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and has a distinctive, pungent aroma similar to mustard. Its leaves are typically oval and slightly hairy, and it is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats.

Lepidium lapazianum is valued for its culinary and medicinal properties in traditional uses.
