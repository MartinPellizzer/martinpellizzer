Lotus creticus subsp. salzmannii

Lotus creticus subsp.

salzmannii, commonly known as the Salzmann's lotus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features palmate leaves with five to seven leaflets, which are typically hairy and adapted to arid conditions.

The plant produces striking pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and resilience, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
