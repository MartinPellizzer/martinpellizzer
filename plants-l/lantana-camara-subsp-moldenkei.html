Lantana camara subsp. moldenkei

Lantana camara subsp.

moldenkei, commonly known as the narrowleaf lantana, is a shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America. It features opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red, which bloom throughout the growing season. It is known for its aromatic foliage and is often used in ornamental gardening.

However, it can be invasive in some regions, posing a threat to native plant communities.
