Liparis caricifolia

Liparis caricifolia, commonly known as the water lily orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a herbaceous plant with a rhizomatous root system that allows it to thrive in wet or marshy environments. The plant produces showy, often fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with intricate patterns on the petals. Its leaves are usually broad and floating, aiding in photosynthesis in aquatic settings.

Liparis caricifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
