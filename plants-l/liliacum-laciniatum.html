Liliacum laciniatum

Liliacum laciniatum, commonly known as the fragrant lace fern, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that give it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, often with a white or pale pink hue. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for botanical gardens and indoor plant collections.
