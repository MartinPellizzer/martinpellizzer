Lantana horrida subsp. tiliifolia

Lantana horrida subsp.

tiliifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf lantana, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and orange.

The plant typically grows as a shrub with opposite, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in disturbed soils and open woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
