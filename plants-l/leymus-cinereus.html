Leymus cinereus

Leymus cinereus, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage that gives it its common name. This plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, meadows, and stream banks. Leymus cinereus has a clump-forming growth habit with slender, upright stems.

Its flowers are small and spike-like, appearing in late summer to early fall.
