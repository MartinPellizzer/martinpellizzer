Lomaria sessilifolia

Lomaria sessilifolia, commonly known as the sessile-leaf lomaria, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are sessile, meaning they are attached directly to the stem without a stalk, and are often covered in a fine layer of hair.

Lomaria sessilifolia produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a subtle ornamental value to its natural habitat.
