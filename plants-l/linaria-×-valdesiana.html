Linaria × valdesiana

Linaria × valdesiana, commonly known as the hybrid common toadflax, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces striking, bright yellow or orange tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Linaria × valdesiana is valued in gardens for its vibrant blooms and relatively low maintenance requirements.
