Linaria thuillieri

Linaria thuillieri, commonly known as the French toadflax, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, erect stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that resemble small trumpets, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. Linaria thuillieri thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking drought-tolerant plants.
