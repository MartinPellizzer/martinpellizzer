Lotus japonicus

Lotus japonicus, commonly known as the Japanese lotus, is a small flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, showy flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with Rhizobium bacteria in its root nodules. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and has been used in traditional medicine.

The species is also a model organism in plant biology research due to its genetic simplicity and ease of cultivation.
