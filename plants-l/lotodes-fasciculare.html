Lotodes fasciculare

Lotodes fasciculare, commonly known as the clustered water lily, is a aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves that form a dense mat on the water surface. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the early morning and close by midday. Its roots are anchored in the mud at the bottom of still or slow-moving water bodies.

Lotodes fasciculare plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen to aquatic life.
