Labourdonnaisia sarcophleia

Labourdonnaisia sarcophleia, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and elusive orchid species native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, fleshy, leaf-like structures that resemble stems, which store water and nutrients. The plant produces striking, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without deriving nutrients from them.

Due to its specific habitat requirements and declining population, it is considered endangered and is protected in many regions.
