Linochilus cinerascens subsp. puracensis

Linochilus cinerascens subsp.

puracensis, commonly known as the Ecuadorian orchid, is a terrestrial orchid belonging to the Orchidaceae family. It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom during the wet season.

The plant is native to the Andean regions of Ecuador and is adapted to high-altitude environments. It typically grows in humid, shaded habitats such as cloud forests.

Its botanical features include a sympodial growth habit and pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.
