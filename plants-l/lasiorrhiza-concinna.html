Lasiorrhiza concinna

Lasiorrhiza concinna, commonly known as the slender waterwheel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of eastern North America.

It is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in a spike-like cluster. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Due to its preference for wet environments, it plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
