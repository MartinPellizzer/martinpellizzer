Linaria melampyroides

Linaria melampyroides, commonly known as the yellow toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has linear, hairy leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Linaria melampyroides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ease of growth.
