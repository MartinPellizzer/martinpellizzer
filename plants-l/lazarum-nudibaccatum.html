Lazarum nudibaccatum

Lazarum nudibaccatum, commonly known as the naked leaf lichen, is a foliose lichen that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is characterized by its thin, leaf-like thallus that lacks distinct lobes, giving it a smooth and delicate appearance. The plant forms a symbiotic relationship with algae and cyanobacteria, which provide essential nutrients through photosynthesis. It is often found on bark, rocks, and soil in temperate forests.

Due to its slow growth and sensitivity to environmental changes, it serves as an indicator species for ecological health.
