Lepidium cordatum var. orbiculatum

Lepidium cordatum var. orbiculatum, commonly known as the heartleaf cress, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forests or stream banks. Its foliage is often covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
