Lexipyretum campestre

Lexipyretum campestre, commonly known as the field sorrel, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae, which includes plants like buckwheat and sorrel. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a reddish tint and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Lexipyretum campestre is known for its edible leaves, which have a tangy, sour flavor due to their high oxalic acid content.
