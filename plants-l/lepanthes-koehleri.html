Lepanthes koehleri

Lepanthes koehleri, commonly known as the Koehler's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It belongs to the Epidendroideae subfamily and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are usually small and white or pale pink, with intricate patterns that attract pollinators.

Lepanthes koehleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
