Liatris squarrosa var. alabemensis

Liatris squarrosa var. alabemensis, commonly known as the Alabama blazingstar, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright, sparsely branched stems that bear whorled, narrow, gray-green leaves. The plant produces showy, pink to purple spires of tubular flowers in late summer to early fall. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
