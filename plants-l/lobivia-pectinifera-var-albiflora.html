Lobivia pectinifera var. albiflora

Lobivia pectinifera var. albiflora, commonly known as the Whiteflower Lobivia, is a succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It features a compact, rounded stem with thick, fleshy leaves that store water, typical of desert plants. The plant produces striking white or pale yellow flowers during the warmer months. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, requiring minimal water to prevent root rot.

Its slow growth and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and cactus collections.
