Lobelia nicotianifolia var. bibarbata

Lobelia nicotianifolia var. bibarbata, commonly known as the hairy-leaf lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, hairy leaves and clusters of small, tubular blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
