Lavandula stoechas subsp. cadevallii

Lavandula stoechas subsp.

cadevallii, commonly known as the Spanish lavender, is a subspecies native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers and narrow, gray-green foliage.

This plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is a perennial shrub that produces aromatic essential oils in its leaves and flowers.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and containers.
