Leucocoryne connivens

Leucocoryne connivens, commonly known as the silver-tipped lily, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often silver- or gray-blue in color, providing a distinctive visual contrast. The plant produces tall, slender stems that bear white or pale pink flowers with a unique, nodding appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its elegant foliage and blooms.
