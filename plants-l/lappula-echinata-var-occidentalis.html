Lappula echinata var. occidentalis

Lappula echinata var. occidentalis, commonly known as western prickly lettuce, is a flowering plant native to western North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, lobed leaves and a distinctive, spiny fruiting structure called a schizocarp. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.
