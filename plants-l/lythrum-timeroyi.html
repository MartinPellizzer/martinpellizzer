Lythrum timeroyi

Lythrum timeroyi, commonly known as the redroot lythrum, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lythraceae family, which includes other plants such as the common lythrum and the purple loosestrife. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit, narrow lance-shaped leaves, and clusters of bright red or pink flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, preferring moist, well-drained soils.

Lythrum timeroyi is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
