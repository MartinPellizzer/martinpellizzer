Liparis yamadae

Liparis yamadae, commonly known as the Yamada's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Japan.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces a single inflorescence with several flowers.

Liparis yamadae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
