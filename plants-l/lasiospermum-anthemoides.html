Lasiospermum anthemoides

Lasiospermum anthemoides, commonly known as the yellow mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that grows on various host trees, primarily in the southeastern United States.

It has small, yellowish-green leaves that are often mistaken for moss due to their dense, mat-like growth pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by berries that are dispersed by birds. Its roots penetrate the host tree's bark to absorb water and nutrients, which can sometimes cause damage to the host.

Despite its parasitic nature, Lasiospermum anthemoides plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for certain bird species.
