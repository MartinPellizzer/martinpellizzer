Liabum eupatorioides

Liabum eupatorioides, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens and wildlife habitats.
