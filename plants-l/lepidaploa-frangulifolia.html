Lepidaploa frangulifolia

Lepidaploa frangulifolia, commonly known as the fragrant leaf tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often fragrant when crushed. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, woody fruits. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and aromatic properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a decorative plant.
