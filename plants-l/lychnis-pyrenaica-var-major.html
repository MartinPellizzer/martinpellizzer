Lychnis pyrenaica var. major

Lychnis pyrenaica var. major, commonly known as the Pyrenean campion, is a flowering plant native to the Pyrenees mountain range.

It is a perennial herb with upright stems that bear pink to red flowers resembling those of the common campion. The plant has simple, lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

The flowers bloom in early summer and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
