Linochilus crassifolius

Linochilus crassifolius, commonly known as the thick-leaved linochilus, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is native to Africa.

It is characterized by its robust, fleshy leaves that are often clustered at the base of the plant. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically arranged in a spike. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
