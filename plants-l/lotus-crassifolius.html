Lotus crassifolius

Lotus crassifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf water lily, is a aquatic plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that float on the water's surface, providing shade and habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant produces white or pink flowers that bloom above the water, adding ornamental value to ponds and water gardens. Its roots are submerged, anchoring the plant in muddy or sandy substrates.

Lotus crassifolius is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits in aquatic ecosystems.
