Lobelia repanda

Lobelia repanda, commonly known as the broadleaf lobelia, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are blue to purple in color, arranged in whorls along the stem, and are attractive to pollinators.

Lobelia repanda is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
