Lavandula × hybrida

Lavandula × hybrida, commonly known as English lavender, is a hybrid species of the genus Lavandula native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, spike-like inflorescences of purple or pink flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, featuring narrow, gray-green leaves that are aromatic when crushed. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal use.

Its essential oils, extracted from the flowers, are widely used in perfumery, aromatherapy, and traditional medicine.
