Loasa sagittata

Loasa sagittata, commonly known as the arrowleaf loasa, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, temperate climates and is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink, which bloom in the spring. Loasa sagittata has a woody stem and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for horticultural study and ecological research.
