Lychnis inaperta

Lychnis inaperta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved catchfly, is a flowering plant in the family Caryophyllaceae.

It is native to Europe and western Asia, typically found in grassy areas and along roadsides. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.
