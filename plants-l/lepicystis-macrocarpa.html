Lepicystis macrocarpa

Lepicystis macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to wetland and aquatic environments in Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its inflated, bladder-like structures that trap small aquatic organisms. The plant's leaves are often submerged, while its stems can be floating or emergent depending on the habitat. It reproduces through seeds and vegetative propagation, and its root system helps stabilize waterlogged soils.

The botanical features of Lepicystis macrocarpa make it an interesting subject for studies on plant adaptation and nutrient acquisition in aquatic ecosystems.
