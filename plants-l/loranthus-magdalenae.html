Loranthus magdalenae

Loranthus magdalenae, commonly known as the Magdalena mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches itself to the branches of host trees, primarily in the Amazon rainforest.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own carbohydrates through photosynthesis using the light it captures. The plant has small, leathery leaves and white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its seeds are dispersed by birds that eat the fleshy, red berries.

This species plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat, contributing to the biodiversity of the forest canopy.
