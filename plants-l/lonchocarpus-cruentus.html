Lonchocarpus cruentus

Lonchocarpus cruentus, commonly known as crimson senna, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of the Americas, particularly Central and South America. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. Its pods are distinctive, elongated, and often reddish in color, containing several seeds.

It is often used in traditional medicine and has also been studied for its potential ecological and agricultural applications.
