Lycaste consobrina

Lycaste consobrina, commonly known as the Mexican night-blooming orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are typically broad and lance-shaped, and it produces long, pendulous inflorescences.

Lycaste consobrina is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and unique flowering behavior.
