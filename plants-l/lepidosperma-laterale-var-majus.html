Lepidosperma laterale var. majus

Lepidosperma laterale var. majus, commonly known as the giant flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in dense clusters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets, which are often borne on long, slender inflorescences. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in swampy areas or along the edges of water bodies.

The species is notable for its ecological importance in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
