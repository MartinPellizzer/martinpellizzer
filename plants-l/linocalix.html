Linocalix

Linocalix, commonly known as the golden cupplant, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flower heads that resemble small cups. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stem is erect and often branched. Linocalix produces small, white to yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
