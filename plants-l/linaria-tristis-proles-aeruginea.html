Linaria tristis proles aeruginea

Linaria tristis proles aeruginea, commonly known as the blue toadflax, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Linaria tristis proles aeruginea is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
