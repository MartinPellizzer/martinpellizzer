Lathyrus lanszwertii var. pallescens

Lathyrus lanszwertii var. pallescens, commonly known as the pale-flowered grass vetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and small, pale blue to lavender flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces trifoliate leaves and has small, hairy pods that contain kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
