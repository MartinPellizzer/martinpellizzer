Ligustrum expansum

Ligustrum expansum, commonly known as the Chinese privet, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, simple leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by dark blue-black berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.

However, it can become invasive in some regions, outcompeting native vegetation.
