Linum tenuifolium subsp. milletii

Linum tenuifolium subsp.

milletii, commonly known as slenderleaf flax, is a flowering plant species native to parts of Asia and the Middle East. It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. It belongs to the family Linaceae and is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in textile production.

The subspecies milletii is distinguished by its slightly different leaf morphology compared to the main species.
