Lastrea parallelogramma

Lastrea parallelogramma, commonly known as the diamond-leaved dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive diamond-shaped leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open grasslands. It has a taproot system and produces a capsule that contains numerous small seeds.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and gardeners alike.
