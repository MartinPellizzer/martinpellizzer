Luxemburgia taubertiana

Luxemburgia taubertiana, commonly known as the Taubert's luxemburgia, is a rare and lesser-known species native to specific regions of Central Europe.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant features elliptical leaves with a smooth, dark green surface and a slightly serrated margin. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden cultivation in temperate climates.

Due to its limited natural range and ornamental qualities, it is occasionally cultivated by botanists and horticulturists for its unique botanical features.
