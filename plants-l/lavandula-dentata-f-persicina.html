Lavandula dentata f. persicina

Lavandula dentata f. persicina, commonly known as the Persian lavender, is a compact, herbaceous perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, dark green leaves with serrated edges, and produces spikes of fragrant, violet-blue flowers in the summer months. This cultivar is prized for its dense foliage and strong, aromatic scent, which is often used in aromatherapy and perfumery. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
