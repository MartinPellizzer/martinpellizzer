Laserpitium siculum var. stabianum

Laserpitium siculum var. stabianum, commonly known as the Stabian mountain everlasting, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, thriving in sunny, well-drained environments. Its flowers are small, yellowish, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The species is noted for its hardy nature and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.
