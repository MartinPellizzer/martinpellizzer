Lacistema intermedium

Lacistema intermedium, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is a climbing vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is characterized by its rapid growth habit. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers typically open in the morning and close by midday, giving it its common name.
