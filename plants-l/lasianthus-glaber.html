Lasianthus glaber

Lasianthus glaber, commonly known as the smooth lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
