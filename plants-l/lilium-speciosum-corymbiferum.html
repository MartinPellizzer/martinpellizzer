Lilium speciosum corymbiferum

Lilium speciosum corymbiferum, commonly known as the showy lily, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China.

It features tall, upright stems that bear large, showy flowers with six to eight petals, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow. The plant produces a distinctive corymb-like inflorescence, with flowers arranged in a flat-topped cluster. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
