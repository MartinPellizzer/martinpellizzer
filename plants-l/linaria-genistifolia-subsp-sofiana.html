Linaria genistifolia subsp. sofiana

Linaria genistifolia subsp.

sofiana, commonly known as the Sicilian toadflax, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, erect stems that bear opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface.

The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers with two lips, typical of the Plantaginaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or coastal areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping in suitable climates.
