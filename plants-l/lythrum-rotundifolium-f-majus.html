Lythrum rotundifolium f. majus

Lythrum rotundifolium f. majus, commonly known as the large-leaf lythrum, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, pale pink to red flowers that bloom in spikes during the summer months. The plant has rounded, broad leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and wetland restoration projects.
