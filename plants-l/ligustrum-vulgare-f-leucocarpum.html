Ligustrum vulgare f. leucocarpum

Ligustrum vulgare f. leucocarpum, commonly known as the white-fruited privet, is a cultivar of the common privet.

It is characterized by its small, oval leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy texture. The plant produces white, fleshy fruits that are distinct from the typical black berries of the species. It is often used in landscaping for its dense growth habit and adaptability to various soil conditions.

This form is prized for its ornamental value and ease of maintenance in gardens and hedgerows.
