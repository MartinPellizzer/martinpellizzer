Lycianthes laevis subsp. laevis

Lycianthes laevis subsp.

laevis, commonly known as the smooth lycianthes, is a species native to Central America and northern South America. It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape.

The flowers are typically yellow with five petals and are arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. This plant produces small, round fruits that are often green when immature and turn red when mature.

It is valued in traditional medicine and as a source of edible seeds.
