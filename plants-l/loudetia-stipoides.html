Loudetia stipoides

Loudetia stipoides, commonly known as the slender false lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually gray-green in color. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are arranged in a panicle, producing small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Loudetia stipoides is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and drought tolerance.
