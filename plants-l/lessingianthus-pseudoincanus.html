Lessingianthus pseudoincanus

Lessingianthus pseudoincanus, commonly known as the Andean sunflower, is a herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to the cool, dry conditions of the Andes. It produces small, elongated fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
