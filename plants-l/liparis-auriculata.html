Liparis auriculata

Liparis auriculata, commonly known as the golden tailed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It features a slender, leafless stem that produces several oval, fleshy leaves at the base. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow-green flowers with a distinctive, hood-like lip that is often tinged with orange or red. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

The species is noted for its delicate appearance and is popular among orchid enthusiasts.
