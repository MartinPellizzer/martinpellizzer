Leonurus glaucescens var. latifolius

Leonurus glaucescens var. latifolius, commonly known as broadleaf horseweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Middle East.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in whorled clusters along the stem. Its leaves are typically broad, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is often found in dry, open habitats such as fields and roadsides.
