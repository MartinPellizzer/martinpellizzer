Laurus hexandra

Laurus hexandra, commonly known as the Mediterranean laurel, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible, black berries. Its aromatic foliage has been historically used in cooking and traditional medicine.

Laurus hexandra is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
