Lycopus ramosissimus

Lycopus ramosissimus, commonly known as blueweed or sweetweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as wet meadows, marshes, and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a bluish-gray hue due to dense pubescence.

Lycopus ramosissimus produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
