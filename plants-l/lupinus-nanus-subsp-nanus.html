Lupinus nanus subsp. nanus

Lupinus nanus subsp.

nanus, commonly known as the dwarf lupine, is a small, herbaceous annual plant native to the western United States. It typically grows to a height of less than 10 centimeters, featuring slender stems and clusters of small, pea-like flowers in shades of blue, purple, or white. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets, and its root system includes nitrogen-fixing nodules, which contribute to soil fertility.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
