Lilium robinsonianum

Lilium robinsonianum, commonly known as the Robinson's lily, is a striking perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 6 feet in height. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, often with a distinctive red or purple throat. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Lilium robinsonianum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its dramatic floral display.
