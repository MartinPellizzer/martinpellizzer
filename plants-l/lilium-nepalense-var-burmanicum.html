Lilium nepalense var. burmanicum

Lilium nepalense var. burmanicum, commonly known as the Burmese lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India, Nepal, and Bhutan.

It is a bulbous perennial plant that produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and red. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a fan-like pattern.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
