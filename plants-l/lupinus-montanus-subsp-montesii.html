Lupinus montanus subsp. montesii

Lupinus montanus subsp.

montesii, commonly known as the Sierra lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers.

The plant has palmately divided leaves with a hairy texture, typical of many lupine species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or grassland habitats. Lupinus montanus subsp.

montesii plays an important role in ecological systems by providing nectar for pollinators and supporting local biodiversity.
