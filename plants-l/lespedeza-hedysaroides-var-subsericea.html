Lespedeza hedysaroides var. subsericea

Lespedeza hedysaroides var. subsericea, commonly known as silky lespedeza, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, delicate leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces clusters of pale pink to reddish-purple flowers in late summer, which are arranged in racemes. Its root system is fibrous and helps prevent soil erosion, making it a valuable plant for ecological restoration.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adapted to a variety of soil types, though it prefers well-drained conditions.
