Lindsaea incisa

Lindsaea incisa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf lindsaea, is a species of epiphytic fern belonging to the family Lindsaeaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, strap-like leaves that are often clustered at the base of the plant. The fronds are usually arching and can reach lengths of up to 30 centimeters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions.

This plant is often seen growing on tree trunks or branches in the understory of forests.
