Loranthus luteoaurantiacus

Loranthus luteoaurantiacus, commonly known as the golden mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of host trees, primarily in the genus Eucalyptus.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own carbohydrates through photosynthesis using light absorbed by its leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are often inconspicuous, followed by small, fleshy berries that serve as a food source for birds. It is native to Australia and is considered an important component of the ecosystem in its native habitat.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can cause stress to its host trees if not managed properly.
