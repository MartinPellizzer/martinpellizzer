Lycium sinense

Lycium sinense, commonly known as Chinese wolfberry or goji berry, is a shrub native to China and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its spiny stems and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are rich in antioxidants and nutrients. Lycium sinense thrives in arid and semi-arid climates, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

It is widely cultivated for its berries, which are used in traditional medicine and as a food source.
