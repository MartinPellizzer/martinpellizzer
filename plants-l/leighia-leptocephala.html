Leighia leptocephala

Leighia leptocephala, commonly known as the orchid of the Andes, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of South America, particularly in the Andes Mountains. This plant is characterized by its tall, slender stem and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

Due to its rarity and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern.
