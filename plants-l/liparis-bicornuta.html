Liparis bicornuta

Liparis bicornuta, commonly known as the two-horned orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive two-horned structure on the base of its petals, which gives it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with a faint fragrance, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Liparis bicornuta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
