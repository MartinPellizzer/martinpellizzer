Lavandula tomentosa

Lavandula tomentosa, commonly known as woolly lavender, is a species of flowering plant in the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is native to the Iberian Peninsula and is characterized by its gray-green, woolly leaves and spikes of purple flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Its fragrance has been historically used in perfumery and for its calming effects.
