Lacinaria angustifolia

Lacinaria angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf lacinaria, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Lacinaria angustifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
