Lepeostegeres capitulifer

Lepeostegeres capitulifer, commonly known as the golden cup fern, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family.

It is a small, epiphytic fern that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features a short, creeping rhizome with numerous clustered, spoon-shaped fronds. Its spore-bearing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

The golden cup fern is characterized by its delicate, hairy appearance and preference for humid, shaded environments.
