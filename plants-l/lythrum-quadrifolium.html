Lythrum quadrifolium

Lythrum quadrifolium, commonly known as red root, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a reddish hue. Its root system is thick and fleshy, which gives it its common name.

Lythrum quadrifolium is often found in moist soils along stream banks and in woodland areas.
