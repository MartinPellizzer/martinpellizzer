Lupinus glaziouanus

Lupinus glaziouanus, commonly known as the Glaziou lupine, is a species of flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds.

Lupinus glaziouanus is adapted to grow in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and slopes.
