Lonicera wightiana

Lonicera wightiana, commonly known as the Wight's honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas.

It features opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that ripens in the fall.

Lonicera wightiana is valued for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
