Ligusticum apiodorum var. helleri

Ligusticum apiodorum var. helleri, commonly known as Heller's lovage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbel-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with finely divided, fern-like leaves that emit a distinctive, peppery fragrance. Its flowers are small and white or pale green, arranged in compound umbels, which attract various pollinators.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional medicinal uses in some regions.
