Linum angustifolium var. agreste

Linum angustifolium var. agreste, commonly known as narrow-leaved flax, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and clusters of blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny conditions. It is part of the Linaceae family, which includes other flax species.

This variety is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and historical significance in fiber production.
