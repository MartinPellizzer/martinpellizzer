Lathyrus gramineus

Lathyrus gramineus, commonly known as the slender vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple or pink. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Lathyrus gramineus is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a valuable plant in ecological restoration projects.
