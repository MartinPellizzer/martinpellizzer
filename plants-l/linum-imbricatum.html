Linum imbricatum

Linum imbricatum, commonly known as the velvetleaf flax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous small, black seeds. Linum imbricatum is often found in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in traditional medicine.
