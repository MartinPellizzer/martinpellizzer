Lycianthes tricolor var. flavidipila

Lycianthes tricolor var. flavidipila, commonly known as the yellow-flowered false huito, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to Central America and features showy, yellow-orange flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with hairy stems and alternate leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape. Its fruits are capsule-like, containing numerous small, yellowish seeds.

This variety is distinguished by its vibrant floral coloration and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
