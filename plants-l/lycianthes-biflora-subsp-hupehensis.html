Lycianthes biflora subsp. hupehensis

Lycianthes biflora subsp.

hupehensis, commonly known as the Chinese eggplant or Hupeh eggplant, is a member of the nightshade family (Solanaceae). It is native to China and is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, egg-shaped fruits that are typically purple or white in color.

The plant is an annual herb with simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to lanceolate in shape. It produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, and the fruit develops from a single ovary.

This subspecies is valued for its edible fruits, which are used in traditional Chinese cuisine.
