Lotus tchihatchewii

Lotus tchihatchewii, commonly known as the Sierra lotus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and produces striking pink to red flowers with a distinctive yellow center. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The Sierra lotus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also studied for its ecological role in high-altitude ecosystems.
