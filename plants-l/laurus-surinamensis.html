Laurus surinamensis

Laurus surinamensis, commonly known as Surinam laurel, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like cinnamon and bay leaves. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is a spherical drupe that turns from green to red as it matures.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice due to their aromatic properties.
