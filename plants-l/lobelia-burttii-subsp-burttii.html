Lobelia burttii subsp. burttii

Lobelia burttii subsp.

burttii, commonly known as Burtt's lobelia, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of Ethiopia. It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, often in alpine or subalpine environments. It is known for its compact growth habit and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
