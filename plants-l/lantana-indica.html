Lantana indica

Lantana indica, commonly known as the shrub lantana, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and clusters of small, colorful flowers that range from yellow to red. The leaves are typically opposite, simple, and have a slightly rough texture. It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

Lantana indica is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant blooms and hardy nature.
