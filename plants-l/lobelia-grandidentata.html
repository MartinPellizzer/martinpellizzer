Lobelia grandidentata

Lobelia grandidentata, commonly known as the large-toothed lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, tubular blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves with prominent teeth along the edges, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Lobelia grandidentata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
