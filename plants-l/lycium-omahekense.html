Lycium omahekense

Lycium omahekense, commonly known as the Omahekense boxthorn, is a shrub native to specific regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and poor soil conditions.

Lycium omahekense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
