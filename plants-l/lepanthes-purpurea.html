Lepanthes purpurea

Lepanthes purpurea, commonly known as the purple orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking purple petals and sepals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are small but highly fragrant, often blooming in clusters during the rainy season.

Lepanthes purpurea is prized for its ornamental value and is cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts worldwide.
