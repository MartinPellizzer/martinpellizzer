Lepanthes expansa

Lepanthes expansa, commonly known as the broad-leaved orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often colorful flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, such as tropical forests. Its leaves are broad and elongated, often with a waxy texture.

Lepanthes expansa is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and unique floral structure.
