Lonicera altaica subsp. subarctica

Lonicera altaica subsp.

subarctica, commonly known as the Arctic honeysuckle, is a shrub native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of Eurasia. It is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has slender, often prostrate stems that grow close to the ground, adapting to the harsh, cold climates it inhabits. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

This subspecies is well-suited to rocky and sandy soils, making it a hardy plant in extreme environments.
