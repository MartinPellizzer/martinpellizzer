Loranthus miraculosus var. boormanii

Loranthus miraculosus var. boormanii, commonly known as the Boorman's mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Loranthaceae family and has a complex symbiotic relationship with its host, drawing water and nutrients while providing some organic compounds in return. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences, and its berries are typically red or orange, containing sticky seeds that adhere to birds for dispersal. Its leaves are usually opposite, elliptical, and have a leathery texture, adapted to reduce water loss in its often arid habitat.

This variant is notable for its ecological role in supporting birdlife and its historical use in traditional medicine.
