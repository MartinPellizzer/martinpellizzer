Lycaste × archilae

Lycaste × archilae, commonly known as the hybrid cyclamen, is a hybrid orchid species resulting from the cross between Lycaste and Archilea.

It is characterized by its attractive, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink, white, and purple. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while the roots are fibrous and adapted for anchoring in soil.

This hybrid is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
