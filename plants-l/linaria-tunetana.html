Linaria tunetana

Linaria tunetana, commonly known as the Tunisian toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Tunisia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces slender, ribbed seed pods that split open to release its seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Linaria tunetana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
