Lobelia trullifolia subsp. trullifolia

Lobelia trullifolia subsp.

trullifolia, commonly known as the slender lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States. It features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy.

The plant produces small, tubular blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

This species is valued in native gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
