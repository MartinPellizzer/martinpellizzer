Lumbricidia anthelmia

Lumbricidia anthelmia, commonly known as the white ant plant, is a unique species native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated leaves that resemble the body of an ant. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. Its root system is particularly notable for its ability to trap and digest small invertebrates, contributing to its nutrient acquisition.

This adaptation makes it a fascinating example of a carnivorous plant in the botanical world.
