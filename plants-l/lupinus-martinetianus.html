Lupinus martinetianus

Lupinus martinetianus, commonly known as the desert lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Lupinus martinetianus plays an important ecological role by fixing nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plant life.
