Litsea lii

Litsea lii, commonly known as the Chinese tallow tree, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate, ovate leaves with a glossy upper surface. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by orange to red drupes. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been introduced to various parts of the world.

However, it can become invasive in some regions, outcompeting native vegetation.
