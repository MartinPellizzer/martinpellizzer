Laplacea semiserrata var. communis

Laplacea semiserrata var. communis, commonly known as the common laplacea, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth or along stream banks. Its botanical features include a rhizomatous root system and hairy stems, which help it thrive in humid conditions.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
