Lomatophyllum prostratum

Lomatophyllum prostratum, commonly known as the prostrate lomatophyllum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and coastal areas. Its leaves are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps reduce water loss through evaporation.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its drought tolerance and unique appearance.
