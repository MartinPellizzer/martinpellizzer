Lopezia frutescens

Lopezia frutescens, commonly known as the desert gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual with slender, branching stems and small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as deserts and rocky soils. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is adapted to withstand drought conditions and has a short life cycle suited to its harsh habitat.
