Lepigonum heterospermum

Lepigonum heterospermum, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a striking perennial plant native to South America.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its unique floral structure and vibrant colors make it a notable addition to botanical collections.
