Libanotis graveolens

Libanotis graveolens, commonly known as the Persian violet, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive, fragrant scent. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.

Its botanical name reflects its historical use in traditional medicine and its association with the region of Lebanon.
