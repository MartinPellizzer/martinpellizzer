Linum nodiflorum var. perpapillosum

Linum nodiflorum var. perpapillosum, commonly known as the Papillate Flax, is a variant of the flax plant characterized by its unique papillate (bump-like) structures on its leaves and stems.

It is native to southern Africa and thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its drought-resistant traits.

This variety is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
