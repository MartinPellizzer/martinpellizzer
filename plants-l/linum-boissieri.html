Linum boissieri

Linum boissieri, commonly known as the Boissier's flax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, blue or purple flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Linum boissieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
