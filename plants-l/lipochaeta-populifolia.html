Lipochaeta populifolia

Lipochaeta populifolia, commonly known as the poplar-leaved lipochaeta, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features lance-shaped, serrated leaves that resemble those of poplar trees, giving it its common name. It produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Lipochaeta populifolia is typically found in open, sunny habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
