Lobelia trullifolia subsp. delicatula

Lobelia trullifolia subsp.

delicatula, commonly known as the slender lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States. It features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of blue or violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its delicate appearance and ecological value make it a notable species in native plant landscapes.
