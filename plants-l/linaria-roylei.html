Linaria roylei

Linaria roylei, commonly known as the Kashmir wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its slender, branched stems are covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Linaria roylei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its bright, cheerful blooms.
