Linaria vettonica

Linaria vettonica, commonly known as the velvet flower or velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, showy, yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a velvety appearance.

Linaria vettonica is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its vibrant blooms and drought tolerance.
