Leucopogon cordifolius

Leucopogon cordifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen, narrow, and leathery leaves. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in winter, attracting various pollinators. It typically grows in heathlands and sandy soils, preferring well-drained conditions.

Leucopogon cordifolius is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
