Lagoseris aralensis

Lagoseris aralensis, commonly known as the Aral Sea thistle, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The plant produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, contributing to its ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
