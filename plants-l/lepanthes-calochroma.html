Lepanthes calochroma

Lepanthes calochroma, commonly known as the golden-veined orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its strikingly beautiful, showy flowers with bright yellow veins contrasting against its white petals. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within cloud forests. It belongs to the Epidendroideae subfamily and is known for its delicate, ephemeral blooms.

Lepanthes calochroma is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
