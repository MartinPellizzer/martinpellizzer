Lindera umbellata var. aurantiaca

Lindera umbellata var. aurantiaca, commonly known as the orange-flowered sweetshrub, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible for certain wildlife species. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.
