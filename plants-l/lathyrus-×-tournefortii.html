Lathyrus × tournefortii

Lathyrus × tournefortii, commonly known as the hybrid sweet pea, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its climbing habit and clusters of fragrant, pea-like flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain several elliptical seeds. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun to partial shade.

Lathyrus × tournefortii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and climbing structure.
