Lonicera ferdinandi f. vesicaria

Lonicera ferdinandi f. vesicaria, commonly known as the veined honeysuckle, is a variant of the ferdinandi honeysuckle, characterized by its distinctive leaf venation.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 meters, featuring opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are usually yellow or orange in color, arranged in clusters. Its fruits are red berries that mature in the fall, providing food for birds and other wildlife.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
