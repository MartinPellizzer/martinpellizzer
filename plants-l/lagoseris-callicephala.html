Lagoseris callicephala

Lagoseris callicephala, commonly known as the Cape sugarbush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in terminal spikes, which bloom in spring. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-resistant.

The Cape sugarbush is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
