Launaea thalassica

Launaea thalassica, commonly known as the sea launaea, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and the Canary Islands.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy, succulent leaves. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to saline and arid environments. Its small yellow flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Launaea thalassica is often used in coastal landscaping due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
