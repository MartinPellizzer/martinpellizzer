Lindsaea longa

Lindsaea longa, commonly known as the long-stalked lindsaea, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, linear leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as tropical rainforests. Its reproductive structures, known as sporangia, are clustered on the underside of the leaves.

Lindsaea longa is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
