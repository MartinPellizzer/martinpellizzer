Lomatia obliqua var. subintegrifolia

Lomatia obliqua var. subintegrifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lomatia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, ascending stems and small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or pale purple in color. The leaves are narrow and have a distinctive, serrated margin, which contributes to its ornamental appeal. This species thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open woodlands.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for native pollinators.
