Lobivia steinmannii

Lobivia steinmannii, commonly known as the Steinmann's lobivia, is a small, compact cactus native to Argentina.

It features a short, cylindrical stem with a ribbed structure and produces white to pale yellow flowers during the spring. The plant has a dense covering of fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid environment. It is well adapted to rocky soils and full sun exposure, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and somewhat endangered species.
