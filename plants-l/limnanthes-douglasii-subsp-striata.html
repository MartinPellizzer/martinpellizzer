Limnanthes douglasii subsp. striata

Limnanthes douglasii subsp.

striata, commonly known as the striped buttercup, is a flowering plant native to western North America. It is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers with a distinctive white or pale yellow stripe down the center.

The plant thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in coastal dunes and wetlands. It has heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
