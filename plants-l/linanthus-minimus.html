Linanthus minimus

Linanthus minimus, commonly known as the small flax or dwarf flax, is a small annual herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and clustered in dense, cylindrical heads, which are characteristic of the tribe Pedicularieae.

Linanthus minimus is known for its rapid life cycle and ability to thrive in disturbed or arid environments.
