Luzula hieronymi f. bonariensis

Luzula hieronymi f. bonariensis, commonly known as the Bonariense Luzula, is a variant of the Luzula hieronymi species, characterized by its unique morphological features.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina and Uruguay. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often dark green in color.

The inflorescence is a dense, spike-like cluster of small, light green to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.
