Lodoicea sonneratii

Lodoicea sonneratii, commonly known as the Socotra cypress, is a unique and ancient tree native to the island of Socotra in the Indian Ocean.

It is characterized by its distinctive, umbrella-like crown and twisted, gnarled branches, which give it a striking and otherworldly appearance. The plant has scale-like leaves that are arranged in spirals along its branches, and it produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds. Lodoicea sonneratii is adapted to the arid and harsh conditions of its native habitat, with deep roots that help it survive in dry environments.

Its botanical features make it a subject of both ecological and evolutionary interest.
