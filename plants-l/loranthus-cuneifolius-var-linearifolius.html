Loranthus cuneifolius var. linearifolius

Loranthus cuneifolius var. linearifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to host trees through its haustoria.

It has small, lance-shaped leaves that are usually arranged in pairs along the stem. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. Its berries are typically red or orange and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This species is found in tropical and subtropical regions, often thriving in humid, shaded environments.
