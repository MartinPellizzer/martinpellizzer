Lindernia cordifolia

Lindernia cordifolia, commonly known as heartleaf lindernia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and spikes of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pale purple to deep violet. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats. It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Lindernia cordifolia is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
