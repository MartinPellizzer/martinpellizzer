Leucheria scrobiculata

Leucheria scrobiculata, commonly known as the golden cup fern, is a small, delicate fern native to Southeast Asia.

It features a compact, rosette-like growth habit with finely divided, bright green fronds that are often tinged with golden hues. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves, which are clustered in distinctive, cup-shaped formations. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental appeal, it is occasionally cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
