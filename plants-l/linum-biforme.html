Linum biforme

Linum biforme, commonly known as the two-lobed flax, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slight pubescence.

Linum biforme is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
