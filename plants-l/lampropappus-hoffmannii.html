Lampropappus hoffmannii

Lampropappus hoffmannii, commonly known as the Hoffman's goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. Its leaves are usually gray-green and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are bright yellow, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
