Lysiloma nigricans

Lysiloma nigricans, commonly known as the black tamarind, is a species of legume native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers that develop into woody pods containing edible seeds. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland tropical forests. Its seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some cultures.

The botanical features of Lysiloma nigricans make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
