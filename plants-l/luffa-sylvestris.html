Luffa sylvestris

Luffa sylvestris, commonly known as wild luffa, is a tropical vine belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that give way to fibrous, sponge-like fruits. These fruits are typically harvested when mature, revealing a spongy interior that can be used as a natural exfoliant.

Luffa sylvestris is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in warm, humid climates.
