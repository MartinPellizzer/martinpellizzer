Loranthus thyrsiflorus

Loranthus thyrsiflorus, commonly known as the African mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily in the genus Acacia.

It lacks true roots and absorbs water and nutrients through its haustoria, which penetrate the host's vascular system. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, and its berries are typically red or orange, containing sticky seeds that adhere to birds for dispersal. It is found throughout sub-Saharan Africa and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for various birds and insects.

Its parasitic nature and unique reproductive strategies make it an interesting subject in botanical studies.
