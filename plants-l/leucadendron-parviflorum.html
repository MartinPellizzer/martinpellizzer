Leucadendron parviflorum

Leucadendron parviflorum, commonly known as the silver coin bush, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage and small, white to pale yellow flowers that appear in late spring. The plant belongs to the Proteaceae family and is well adapted to dry, sunny conditions. Its compact growth habit makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
