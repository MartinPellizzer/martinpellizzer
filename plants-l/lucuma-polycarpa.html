Lucuma polycarpa

Lucuma polycarpa, commonly known as the golden fruit or lucuma, is a small tree native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It produces oval-shaped fruits that are encased in a hairy, yellowish exterior. The fruit's interior contains a sweet, creamy pulp with a flavor reminiscent of sweet potato or maple syrup. The plant thrives in high-altitude regions with cool, temperate climates and well-drained soils.

It is valued not only for its unique taste but also for its nutritional content, including high levels of vitamin C and dietary fiber.
