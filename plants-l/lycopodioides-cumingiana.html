Lycopodioides cumingiana

Lycopodioides cumingiana, commonly known as the Cuming's clubmoss, is a species of lycophyte belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its small, spore-bearing cones and a creeping, rhizomatous growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves arranged spirally along the stems.

Lycopodioides cumingiana plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
