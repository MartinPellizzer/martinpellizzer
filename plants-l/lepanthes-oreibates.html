Lepanthes oreibates

Lepanthes oreibates, commonly known as the cloud forest orchid, is a species of orchid native to the high-altitude cloud forests of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers with intricate patterns and a sweet, spicy fragrance. The plant typically grows on tree trunks or branches, exhibiting an epiphytic growth habit. Its leaves are usually dark green and leathery, adapted to the humid, shaded conditions of its habitat.

Lepanthes oreibates plays a vital role in the ecological balance of its environment, supporting various pollinators and contributing to the biodiversity of cloud forest ecosystems.
