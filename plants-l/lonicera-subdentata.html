Lonicera subdentata

Lonicera subdentata, commonly known as the Japanese honeysuckle, is a deciduous climbing vine native to East Asia.

It features opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are often slightly toothed along the edges. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, appearing in clusters. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it adaptable to various garden conditions.

However, it can become invasive in some regions if not managed properly.
