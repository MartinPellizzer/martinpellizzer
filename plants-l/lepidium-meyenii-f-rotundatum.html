Lepidium meyenii f. rotundatum

Lepidium meyenii f. rotundatum, commonly known as maca, is a tuberous plant native to the high Andes of Peru.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its swollen, irregularly shaped roots that are rich in nutrients. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soil. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellow, typical of the mustard family.

Maca is cultivated for its root, which is used in traditional medicine and as a dietary supplement.
