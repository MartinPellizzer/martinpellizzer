Lamium maculatum var. kansuense

Lamium maculatum var. kansuense, commonly known as the spotted deadnettle, is a perennial herb native to parts of East Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive purple or reddish markings, and it produces clusters of small, two-lipped flowers in shades of pink or purple. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to spread through rhizomes.

The species is also noted for its medicinal properties in traditional Chinese medicine.
