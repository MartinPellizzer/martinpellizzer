Lycopodium lucidulum var. porophilum

Lycopodium lucidulum var. porophilum, commonly known as the Porophilum clubmoss, is a small evergreen plant belonging to the Lycopodiaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems covered with small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces spores in specialized structures called sporangia, which are typically clustered in strobili at the tips of the branches. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices.

This variety is notable for its delicate appearance and is often found in temperate regions with high humidity.
