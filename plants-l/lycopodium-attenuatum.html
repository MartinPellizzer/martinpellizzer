Lycopodium attenuatum

Lycopodium attenuatum, commonly known as the slender clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant features whorled leaves that are narrow and linear, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, cone-like structures called strobili that contain spores, which are released for reproduction.

Lycopodium attenuatum plays a role in maintaining soil stability and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
