Loranthus mirabilis

Loranthus mirabilis, commonly known as the miracle mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while also producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using the light it captures. The plant has small, scale-like leaves and produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are often visited by insects. Its berries are typically red or yellow and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, influencing host tree health and supporting biodiversity.
