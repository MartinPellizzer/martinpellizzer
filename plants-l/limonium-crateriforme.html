Limonium crateriforme

Limonium crateriforme, commonly known as the crater-shaped sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking, spiky blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in coastal environments with well-drained soil. It is often used in ornamental gardening for its ornate appearance and drought tolerance.

Limonium crateriforme is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
