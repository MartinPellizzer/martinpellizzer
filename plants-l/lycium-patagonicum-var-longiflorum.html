Lycium patagonicum var. longiflorum

Lycium patagonicum var. longiflorum, commonly known as the Patagonian boxthorn, is a shrub native to southern South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open woodlands.

Its botanical features make it an important species in local ecosystems and traditional medicine.
