Lippia trifida var. gracilis

Lippia trifida var. gracilis, commonly known as narrow-leaf lippia, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and small, clustered white or pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. It has a compact growth habit and is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and fragrance.

The leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its medicinal and aromatic properties.
