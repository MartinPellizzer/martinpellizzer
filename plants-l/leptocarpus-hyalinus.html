Leptocarpus hyalinus

Leptocarpus hyalinus, commonly known as the glassy sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, showcasing its adaptability to waterlogged soils. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive waxy coating that gives it a glassy appearance. Its inflorescence consists of small, clustered spikes of white or pale yellow flowers, which bloom during the wet season.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
