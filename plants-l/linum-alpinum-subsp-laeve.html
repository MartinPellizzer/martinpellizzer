Linum alpinum subsp. laeve

Linum alpinum subsp.

laeve, commonly known as the alpine flax, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its slender stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, smooth texture.

The plant produces small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
