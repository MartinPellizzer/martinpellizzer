Lotodes glandulosum var. coeruleum

Lotodes glandulosum var. coeruleum, commonly known as the blue lotus, is a striking aquatic plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy blue or purple flowers that bloom above the water surface. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, with a waxy texture that helps repel water. Its flowers are often used in traditional ceremonies and have cultural significance in various regions.

Botanically, it belongs to the Nymphaeaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in still, freshwater environments.
