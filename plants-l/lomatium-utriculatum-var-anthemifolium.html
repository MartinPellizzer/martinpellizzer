Lomatium utriculatum var. anthemifolium

Lomatium utriculatum var. anthemifolium, commonly known as the yellow lomatium, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features a basal rosette of lance-shaped, yellowish-green leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces a tall, hairy stem that bears small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a dry, two-lobed capsule that splits open to release seeds.

This species is adapted to moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
