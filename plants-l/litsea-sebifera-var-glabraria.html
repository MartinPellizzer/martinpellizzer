Litsea sebifera var. glabraria

Litsea sebifera var. glabraria, commonly known as the smooth-leafed litsea, is a variant of the litsea species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub characterized by its smooth, glabrous leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically borne in clusters. Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ornamental horticulture and ecological studies.

The leaves and fruits are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
