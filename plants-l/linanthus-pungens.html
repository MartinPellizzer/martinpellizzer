Linanthus pungens

Linanthus pungens, commonly known as the prickly phlox, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and clusters of showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It belongs to the tribe Phlox, which includes many species with similar floral structures.

Linanthus pungens is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
