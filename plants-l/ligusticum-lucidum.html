Ligusticum lucidum

Ligusticum lucidum, commonly known as Chinese lovage, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with slender, branched stems and finely divided, fern-like leaves. Its roots are often used in traditional Chinese medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.
