Lobelia stimulens

Lobelia stimulens, commonly known as the Indian tobacco, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, spurred blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Lobelia stimulens is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support pollinators.
