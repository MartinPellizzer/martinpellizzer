Linaria praecox var. ramosa

Linaria praecox var. ramosa, commonly known as the early foxglove, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous annual with slender, branching stems that often grow in open, sunny habitats. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers that are typically orange or yellow in color, arranged in racemes. Its leaves are linear and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Linaria praecox var. ramosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
