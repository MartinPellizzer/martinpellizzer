Leandriella oblonga

Leandriella oblonga, commonly known as the narrowleaf leandriella, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
