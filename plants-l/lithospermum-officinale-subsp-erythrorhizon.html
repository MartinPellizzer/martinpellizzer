Lithospermum officinale subsp. erythrorhizon

Lithospermum officinale subsp.

erythrorhizon, commonly known as Japanese knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia. It features upright, reddish stems that are often hollow and covered with fine hairs.

The plant produces small, pinkish to reddish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a slightly serrated margin, and the plant has a deep root system that can spread extensively.

It is known for its aggressive growth and is considered an invasive species in many regions outside its native range.
