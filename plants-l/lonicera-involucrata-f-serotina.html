Lonicera involucrata f. serotina

Lonicera involucrata f. serotina, commonly known as the late-flowering honeysuckle, is a botanical variant of the honeysuckle genus.

It is characterized by its late blooming habit, typically flowering in late spring to early summer. The plant features fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It is a deciduous shrub that can grow up to 3 meters in height.

Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a serrated margin, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
