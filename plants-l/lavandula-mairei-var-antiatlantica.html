Lavandula mairei var. antiatlantica

Lavandula mairei var. antiatlantica, commonly known as the Cape lavender, is a compact, shrubby plant native to the coastal regions of South Africa.

It features narrow, silver-green leaves and produces spikes of pale purple to blue-lilac flowers. The plant is well adapted to coastal conditions, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal landscapes.

Its aromatic foliage and long-lasting blooms make it a valuable addition to ornamental horticulture.
