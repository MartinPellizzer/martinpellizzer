Lepidium latifolium subsp. obtusum

Lepidium latifolium subsp.

obtusum, commonly known as broadleaf peppergrass, is a flowering plant native to western North America. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields. It has alternate, broadly ovate to lanceolate leaves and produces small white or pale yellow flowers in racemes.

The fruit is a short, cylindrical silique containing several seeds.
