Leucopogon decussatus

Leucopogon decussatus, commonly known as the common heath, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen shrub form. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pink, arranged in clusters. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in sandy or rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of coastal and pine forest ecosystems.
