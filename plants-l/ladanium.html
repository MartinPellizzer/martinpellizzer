Ladanium

Ladanium, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with intricate patterns. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its delicate, strap-like leaves. Ladanium thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and its role in traditional medicine in its native regions.
