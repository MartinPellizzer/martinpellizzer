Lilium lancifolium var. densum

Lilium lancifolium var. densum, commonly known as the dense-leaved lily, is a variant of the tiger lily that is characterized by its dense, sword-shaped leaves.

It is native to East Asia and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. The plant produces showy, orange-red flowers with a distinctive black blotch at the base of each petal. It is a perennial herbaceous plant that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in some regions.
