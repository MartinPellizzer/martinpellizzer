Leycesteria formosa var. glandulosissima

Leycesteria formosa var. glandulosissima, commonly known as the glandular-leaved elder, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a glandular texture, giving the plant its varietal name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

This species is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
