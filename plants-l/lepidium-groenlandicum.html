Lepidium groenlandicum

Lepidium groenlandicum, commonly known as Greenland cress, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress species. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and has slender, branching stems covered in tiny, scale-like leaves. It thrives in moist, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and coastal environments.

Greenland cress is known for its edible leaves and has been used in traditional cuisines and herbal remedies.
