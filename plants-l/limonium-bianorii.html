Limonium bianorii

Limonium bianorii, commonly known as the pink sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking pink to purple flower heads, which are composed of numerous small, delicate florets. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a basal rosette.

Limonium bianorii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand salty air and coastal climates.
