Lemaireocereus schumannii

Lemaireocereus schumannii, commonly known as the Schumann's night blooming cereus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems and is typically found in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant produces striking white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. It has a slow growth rate and requires well-draining soil and minimal water.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
