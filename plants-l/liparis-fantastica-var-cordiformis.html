Liparis fantastica var. cordiformis

Liparis fantastica var. cordiformis, commonly known as the Heartleaf Orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are thick and waxy, adapted to retain moisture in humid environments. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, often with intricate patterns resembling lace. It thrives in shaded, well-drained soil and requires high humidity and indirect light for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and limited habitat, it is considered a prized specimen in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
