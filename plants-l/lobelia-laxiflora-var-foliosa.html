Lobelia laxiflora var. foliosa

Lobelia laxiflora var. foliosa, commonly known as the broad-leaved lobelia, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright, slender stems that bear clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers. The leaves are broad, ovate, and often hairy, providing a striking contrast to the delicate blooms. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
