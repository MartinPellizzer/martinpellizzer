Leucas samhaensis

Leucas samhaensis, commonly known as the Kashmiri leucas, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous areas with well-drained soil.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used in traditional medicine for its purported antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.
