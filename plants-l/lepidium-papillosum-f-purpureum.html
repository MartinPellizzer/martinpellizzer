Lepidium papillosum f. purpureum

Lepidium papillosum f. purpureum, commonly known as purple peppergrass, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is found in arid or semi-arid regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture and bear small, glandular dots.

This form is distinguished by its more pronounced purple coloration compared to the typical form of Lepidium papillosum.
