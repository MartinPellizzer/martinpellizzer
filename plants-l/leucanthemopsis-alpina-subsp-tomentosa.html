Leucanthemopsis alpina subsp. tomentosa

Leucanthemopsis alpina subsp.

tomentosa, commonly known as the tomentose alpine groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is a perennial herb characterized by its low-growing habit and dense, woolly foliage.

The plant produces daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
