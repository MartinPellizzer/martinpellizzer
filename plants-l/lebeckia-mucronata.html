Lebeckia mucronata

Lebeckia mucronata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lebeckia, is a South African flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Lebeckia mucronata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable climates.
