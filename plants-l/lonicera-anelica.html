Lonicera anelica

Lonicera anelica, commonly known as the creeping honeysuckle, is a deciduous vine native to eastern North America.

It features slender, trailing stems that can climb or spread along the ground, often forming dense mats. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically blooming in early spring. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Lonicera anelica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
