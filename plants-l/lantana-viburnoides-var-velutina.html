Lantana viburnoides var. velutina

Lantana viburnoides var. velutina, commonly known as velvet lantana, is a shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, depending on the variety. The leaves are typically velvety to the touch, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in ornamental gardening.

The plant is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
