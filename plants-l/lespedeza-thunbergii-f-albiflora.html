Lespedeza thunbergii f. albiflora

Lespedeza thunbergii f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered lespeadeza, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. The plant features slender, upright stems and pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its most distinguishing botanical feature is its clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
