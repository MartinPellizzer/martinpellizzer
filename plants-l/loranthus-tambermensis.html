Loranthus tambermensis

Loranthus tambermensis, commonly known as the Siberian mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches itself to the branches of its host trees, primarily conifers.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own carbohydrates through photosynthesis using the light it captures. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers and red berries that contain seeds covered in a sticky substance to aid in attachment to new hosts. It is native to regions of Siberia and is often found in forested areas where its host trees are abundant.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can sometimes weaken its host, but it also plays a role in ecological interactions and bird dispersal.
