Linum tenuifolium marianorum

Linum tenuifolium marianorum, commonly known as the Marian marianum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and produces showy, blue or violet flowers. The plant is often found in rocky soils and coastal areas, thriving in well-drained environments. It is a member of the Linaceae family, which includes other species known for their fibrous stems and medicinal properties.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
