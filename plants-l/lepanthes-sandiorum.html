Lepanthes sandiorum

Lepanthes sandiorum, commonly known as the sandiorum orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments. The plant features a slender, erect stem with a few leaves and produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. Its flowers are characterized by their intricate structure and are often clustered in a raceme.

Lepanthes sandiorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated by enthusiasts due to its unique appearance.
