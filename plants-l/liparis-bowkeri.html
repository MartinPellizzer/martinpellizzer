Liparis bowkeri

Liparis bowkeri, commonly known as the Bowker's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, greenish-yellow flowers with a faintly fragrant scent. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often on rocky or forested slopes. Its flowering period occurs during the spring and early summer months.

Liparis bowkeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
