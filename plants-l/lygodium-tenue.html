Lygodium tenue

Lygodium tenue, commonly known as the climbing fern, is a tropical fern species characterized by its unique morphology and growth habit.

It features a rhizomatous stem that allows it to climb and spread across tree trunks and other supports. The fronds are pinnate, with a distinctive appearance that includes small, rounded pinnae and a dense, feathery texture. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

This plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, contributing to biodiversity and providing shelter for various organisms.
