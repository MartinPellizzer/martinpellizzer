Lilium martagon var. sanguineopurpureum

Lilium martagon var. sanguineopurpureum, commonly known as the Persian buttercup lily, is a striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear clusters of large, showy flowers with a distinctive cup-shaped form. The flowers are typically pink to purple in color, with a soft, velvety texture and a subtle fragrance. This variety is notable for its unique coloration, which transitions from a deep red at the base to a pale pink at the tips.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
