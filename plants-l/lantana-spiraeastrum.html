Lantana spiraeastrum

Lantana spiraeastrum, commonly known as the golden lantana, is a flowering shrub native to South America.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often appearing in late summer to early autumn. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are typically ovate or elliptic in shape. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The plant's showy inflorescences attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
