Loranthus caesius

Loranthus caesius, commonly known as the purple mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of host trees, primarily in the genus Eucalyptus.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using light reflected from the host. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, fleshy berries that are dispersed by birds. Its parasitic nature makes it an important ecological component in certain ecosystems, influencing the health and diversity of its host trees.

Loranthus caesius is also notable for its role in traditional medicine and as a symbol in various cultural contexts.
