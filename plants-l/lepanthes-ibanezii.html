Lepanthes ibanezii

Lepanthes ibanezii, commonly known as the Ibanez's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, intricate flowers that typically display vibrant colors such as white, yellow, and green. The plant grows as an epiphyte, often found on trees in humid, shaded forest environments. Its leaves are usually ovate and arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces delicate, fragrant blooms during the rainy season.

Lepanthes ibanezii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated by enthusiasts of rare orchids.
