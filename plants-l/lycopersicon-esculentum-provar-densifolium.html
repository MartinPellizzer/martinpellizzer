Lycopersicon esculentum provar. densifolium

Lycopersicon esculentum provar. densifolium, commonly known as a dense-leaved tomato variant, is a botanical cultivar of the domesticated tomato.

It is characterized by its thick, closely arranged foliage, which provides enhanced shade for the fruit and may improve disease resistance. The plant belongs to the Solanaceae family and produces red, fleshy fruits that are typically used in culinary applications. It is cultivated for its high yield and robust growth habit, making it suitable for both home gardens and commercial farming.

Its dense foliage also contributes to a more compact plant structure, which can be advantageous in space-limited growing environments.
