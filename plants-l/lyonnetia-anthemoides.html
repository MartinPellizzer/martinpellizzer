Lyonnetia anthemoides

Lyonnetia anthemoides, commonly known as the creeping cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features small, yellowish-green leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in the summer. The plant is characterized by its woody stems and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. Lyonnetia anthemoides is a member of the Rosaceae family and is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in harsh environments.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and its role in supporting local pollinators.
