Leea nova-guineensis

Leea nova-guineensis, commonly known as the New Guinea leea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It belongs to the family Leeaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, often with a slight aromatic scent. It is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
