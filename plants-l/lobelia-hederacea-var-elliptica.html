Lobelia hederacea var. elliptica

Lobelia hederacea var. elliptica, commonly known as the broad-leafed toadflax, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are often hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its foliage and flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to native gardens.
