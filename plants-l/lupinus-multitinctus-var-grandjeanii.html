Lupinus multitinctus var. grandjeanii

Lupinus multitinctus var. grandjeanii, commonly known as the Grandjean's lupine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its erect stems and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Lupinus multitinctus var. grandjeanii is part of the legume family and has the ability to fix nitrogen through its root nodules.
