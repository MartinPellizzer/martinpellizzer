Lindsaea parishii

Lindsaea parishii, commonly known as the Parish's maidenhair fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, linear fronds that are often clustered in a rosette-like formation. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically growing on trees or rocks in the understory of rainforests. Its reproductive structures, including sori and sporangia, are located on the underside of the fronds, a feature common among ferns.

Lindsaea parishii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
