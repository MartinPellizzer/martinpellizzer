Littonia

Littonia, commonly known as the false heather, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Ericaceae.

It consists of several species, most of which are shrubs characterized by their dense, compact growth habit. The leaves are typically small, leathery, and evergreen, providing a lush appearance throughout the year. Flowers are usually small and clustered, often in pink or white hues, adding ornamental value to gardens.

These plants thrive in acidic, well-drained soils and are often found in woodland or rocky habitats.
