Lissochilus oatesii

Lissochilus oatesii, commonly known as the Oates' orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, spurred flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

This orchid prefers cool, humid environments and is often found growing in rocky or forested areas at high elevations.
