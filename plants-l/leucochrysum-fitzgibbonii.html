Leucochrysum fitzgibbonii

Leucochrysum fitzgibbonii, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for its long-lasting, showy blooms.
