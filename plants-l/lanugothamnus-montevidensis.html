Lanugothamnus montevidensis

Lanugothamnus montevidensis, commonly known as the Montevidense thamnus, is a flowering plant native to South America, specifically Uruguay and parts of Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance.

The flowers are small and yellow, often arranged in dense clusters, contributing to its ornamental value.
