Lablab niger subsp. bengalensis

Lablab niger subsp.

bengalensis, commonly known as the Bengal cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to South and Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive purple or pink flowers.

The plant produces elongated, cylindrical pods that contain edible seeds used in various culinary applications. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value.

Additionally, it plays a role in soil improvement due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.
