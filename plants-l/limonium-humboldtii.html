Limonium humboldtii

Limonium humboldtii, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and the western Pacific.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and its clusters of blue to purple, bell-shaped flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in sandy or rocky coastal habitats. Its salt tolerance makes it well-suited for maritime environments.

Limonium humboldtii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging coastal conditions.
