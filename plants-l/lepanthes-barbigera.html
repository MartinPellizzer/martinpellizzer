Lepanthes barbigera

Lepanthes barbigera, commonly known as the hairy lepanthes, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically range in color from white to pale pink. The plant grows in humid, shaded environments, often in the understory of tropical forests. Its leaves are usually elliptical and have a waxy surface, which helps reduce water loss.

Lepanthes barbigera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
