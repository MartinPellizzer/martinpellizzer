Lobelia molleri f. latifolia

Lobelia molleri f. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf lobelia, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It features small, tubular blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are broader than the typical form of Lobelia molleri. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
