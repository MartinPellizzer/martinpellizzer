Lotononis lupinifolia var. villosa

Lotononis lupinifolia var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy lupin, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, hairy leaves and clusters of small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has a taproot system that allows it to survive in drought conditions.

The flowers are usually pink or purple and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
