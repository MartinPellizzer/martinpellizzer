Lathyrus inconspicuus var. erectus

Lathyrus inconspicuus var. erectus, commonly known as the upright grass vetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides. The plant features slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets.

This variety is noted for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
