Lepanthes jucas

Lepanthes jucas, commonly known as the Jucas orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that often display vibrant colors. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical forests. Its pseudobulbs are short and stout, supporting its foliage and floral structures.

Lepanthes jucas is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
