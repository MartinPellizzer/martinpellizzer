Leopoldinia insignis

Leopoldinia insignis, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, palm-like leaves and produces a fruit that contains up to 20 seeds, known as Brazil nuts. The plant has a complex root system and can grow up to 40 meters tall, making it a significant component of the rainforest canopy. It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

The tree is also valued for its economic importance due to the commercial value of its seeds.
