Listera ovata lusus quadrifoliata

Listera ovata lusus quadrifoliata, commonly known as the four-leaf clover, is a rare variant of the common white clover.

It is characterized by its distinctive four leaves, which are rare in the normally three-leaf species. The plant belongs to the Leguminosae family and is known for its small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded areas and is often found in woodland environments.

Due to its rarity and symbolic significance, it is highly sought after by collectors and botanists alike.
