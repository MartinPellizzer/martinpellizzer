Limonium britannicum var. pharense

Limonium britannicum var. pharense, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flower heads surrounded by white, papery bracts. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in maritime climates. Its flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Limonium britannicum var. pharense is valued for its ornamental beauty and its ability to tolerate salt spray and coastal conditions.
