Linum catharticum f. conferta

Linum catharticum f. conferta, commonly known as the dense-flowered flax, is a botanical variant of the flax plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense arrangement of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are often used in traditional herbal medicine. Its foliage is lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in dye production.
