Lilium longiflorum subsp. neilgherrense

Lilium longiflorum subsp.

neilgherrense, commonly known as the Neilgherry lily, is a subspecies native to the Himalayas, particularly in regions of India and Nepal. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer.

The foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern at the base of the plant. This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.

It prefers cool, moist environments and is typically found in mountainous regions with well-drained soil.
