Lycopersicon esculentum convar. fruticosum

Lycopersicon esculentum convar. fruticosum, commonly known as the wild tomato, is a botanical variant of the domesticated tomato.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and typically smaller, more acidic fruits compared to its cultivated relatives. The plant is native to the Andes region of South America and exhibits a range of morphological variations. It possesses hairy leaves and stems, and its flowers are usually yellow with five lobes.

This wild species is of interest to botanists and breeders for its genetic diversity and potential for improving cultivated tomato varieties.
