Lavandula bipinnata var. setifera

Lavandula bipinnata var. setifera, commonly known as the woolly lavender, is a perennial shrub native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its gray-green, woolly leaves and spikes of pale purple to lilac flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. Its aromatic foliage and showy blooms make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The variety 'setifera' is distinguished by its particularly dense, fuzzy leaves and compact growth habit.
