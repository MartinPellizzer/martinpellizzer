Lobelia puberula var. subglabra

Lobelia puberula var. subglabra, commonly known as the smooth lobelia, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are generally smooth and lance-shaped, with a somewhat hairy texture in some varieties.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
