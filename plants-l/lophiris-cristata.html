Lophiris cristata

Lophiris cristata, commonly known as the crested lophiris, is a rare and striking plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, branched inflorescence that resembles a crown, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, which bloom during the spring and early summer. Its leaves are broad and leathery, often with a waxy coating that helps it retain moisture in its arid habitat.

Lophiris cristata is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its role in traditional medicine in its native regions.
