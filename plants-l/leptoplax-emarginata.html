Leptoplax emarginata

Leptoplax emarginata, commonly known as the narrow-margin water lily, is a aquatic plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves that are typically green with a waxy surface. The plant produces white flowers that bloom in the early morning and close by midday. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
