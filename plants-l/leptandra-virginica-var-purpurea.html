Leptandra virginica var. purpurea

Leptandra virginica var. purpurea, commonly known as purple sand cherry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and features slender, branching stems with rough, hairy bark. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in early spring, followed by red, fleshy fruits that are attractive to birds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a slightly serrated margin, with a pale purple underside.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
