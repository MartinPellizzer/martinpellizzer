Lepturus filiformis var. pycnanthus

Lepturus filiformis var. pycnanthus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lepturus, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, thread-like leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or pale yellow, which bloom in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
