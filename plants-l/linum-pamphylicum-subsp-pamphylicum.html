Linum pamphylicum subsp. pamphylicum

Linum pamphylicum subsp.

pamphylicum, commonly known as the Pamphylian flax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species that typically grows in dry, rocky soils.

The plant produces showy blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its linear leaves are narrow and often hairy, and it is characterized by its fibrous stems.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in fiber production.
