Lathyrus viciifolius

Lathyrus viciifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaved vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with broad, oval-shaped leaflets and clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces elongated pods that contain several elliptical, brown seeds. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.

Lathyrus viciifolius is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural use.
