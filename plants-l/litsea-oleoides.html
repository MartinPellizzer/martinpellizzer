Litsea oleoides

Litsea oleoides, commonly known as the Chinese tallow tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for birds. Its bark and leaves contain essential oils, which have been used in traditional medicine and for aromatic purposes.

Litsea oleoides is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various soil types.
