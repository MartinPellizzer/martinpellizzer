Lycium schaeferi

Lycium schaeferi, commonly known as Schaefer's boxthorn, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshade plants, and is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries, which are edible for some animals but toxic to humans. It is adapted to harsh environments, with thick leaves that reduce water loss and deep roots that access groundwater.

Lycium schaeferi plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
