Lepanthes crescentiformis

Lepanthes crescentiformis, commonly known as the crescent-shaped orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in photosynthesis.

Lepanthes crescentiformis thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
