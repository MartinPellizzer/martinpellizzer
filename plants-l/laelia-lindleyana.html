Laelia lindleyana

Laelia lindleyana, commonly known as the Lindley's orchid, is a species native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions. The plant features a pseudobulb with long, narrow leaves and produces strikingly colorful, fragrant flowers with three sepals and three petals. Its blooms are usually pink or white, with a prominent lip that is often yellow or orange.

Laelia lindleyana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to cultivation.
