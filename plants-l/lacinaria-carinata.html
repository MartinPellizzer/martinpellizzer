Lacinaria carinata

Lacinaria carinata, commonly known as the ridge-leaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, ribbed leaves and showy, pale pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its botanical features include hairy stems and capsules that contain small, round seeds.

Lacinaria carinata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
