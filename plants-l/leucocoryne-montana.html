Leucocoryne montana

Leucocoryne montana, commonly known as the mountain white lily, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina and Chile.

It is characterized by its striking white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, often forming a basal rosette.

Leucocoryne montana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
