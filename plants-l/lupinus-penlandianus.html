Lupinus penlandianus

Lupinus penlandianus, commonly known as Penland's lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. It has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds.

Lupinus penlandianus is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for ecological restoration projects.
