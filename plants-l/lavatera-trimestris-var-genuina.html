Lavatera trimestris var. genuina

Lavatera trimestris var. genuina, commonly known as the woolly sunhemp, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and showy, fluffy yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates. Its fibrous stems are often used in traditional agriculture for livestock feed and soil stabilization.

The botanical features of this plant make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable.
