Lupinus portae-westgardiae

Lupinus portae-westgardiae, commonly known as the Westgardia lupine, is a species of flowering plant native to the high Andes of South America.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and is adapted to thrive in cool, high-altitude environments.

Its root system forms symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
