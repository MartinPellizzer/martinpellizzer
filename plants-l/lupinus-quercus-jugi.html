Lupinus quercus-jugi

Lupinus quercus-jugi, commonly known as the oak-juniper lupine, is a perennial legume native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers. The plant has compound leaves with several oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and open areas.

Lupinus quercus-jugi plays a role in nitrogen fixation, benefiting the surrounding ecosystem.
