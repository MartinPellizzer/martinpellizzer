Lindera pulcherrima var. glauca

Lindera pulcherrima var. glauca, commonly known as the blueleaf sweet pepper, is a variant of the sweet pepper plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue-green foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and produces small, orange-yellow fruits that are edible. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild, sweet flavor in culinary applications.
