Lissochilus spathulifer

Lissochilus spathulifer, commonly known as the broad-leafed orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, broad, ovate leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and cloud forests, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its roots are often covered in a dense network of mycorrhizal fungi, which aid in nutrient absorption.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
