Launaea tomentella

Launaea tomentella, commonly known as the woolly dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features hairy, grayish-green leaves that help reduce water loss in dry environments. Its flowers are typically yellow, with a central disk surrounded by ray florets.

Launaea tomentella is often found growing in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low precipitation.
