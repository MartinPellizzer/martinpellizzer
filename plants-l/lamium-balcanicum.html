Lamium balcanicum

Lamium balcanicum, commonly known as Balkan dead-nettle, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and opposite leaves. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance.

Lamium balcanicum is often found in woodland areas and along the edges of forests, where it thrives in moist, shaded conditions.
