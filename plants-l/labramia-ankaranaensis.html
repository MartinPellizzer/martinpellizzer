Labramia ankaranaensis

Labramia ankaranaensis, commonly known as the Madagascar spider orchid, is a rare and endemic species found exclusively in the rainforests of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, intricate floral structure that resembles a spider in appearance. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree bark and deriving moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. Its flowers are small, usually white or pale green, and are adapted for specific pollinators, primarily certain species of flies.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, Labramia ankaranaensis is considered critically endangered and is a focus of conservation efforts in Madagascar.
