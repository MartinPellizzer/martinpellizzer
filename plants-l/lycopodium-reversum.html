Lycopodium reversum

Lycopodium reversum, commonly known as the reverse clubmoss, is a small, evergreen fern-like plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, creeping growth habit and its distinctive, scale-like leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern. The plant produces small, cone-like structures called strobila, which contain the spores for reproduction. Lycopodium reversum typically thrives in moist, shaded environments, such as forests or rocky crevices.

Its ancient lineage dates back to the Carboniferous period, making it a living fossil with significant botanical interest.
