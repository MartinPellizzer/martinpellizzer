Leucas montana

Leucas montana, commonly known as mountain leucas, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern and eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and clusters of small, pale blue or violet flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Leucas montana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
