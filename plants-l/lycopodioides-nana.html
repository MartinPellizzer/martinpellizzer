Lycopodioides nana

Lycopodioides nana, commonly known as the dwarf clubmoss, is a small evergreen fern-like plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its compact stature and needle-like leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem. This species typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures, known as strobila, are cone-like and contain spores.

Lycopodioides nana plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for small organisms in its native ecosystems.
