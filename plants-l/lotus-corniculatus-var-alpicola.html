Lotus corniculatus var. alpicola

Lotus corniculatus var. alpicola, commonly known as the alpine bird's-foot trefoil, is a flowering plant native to alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. Its small, hairy leaves and distinctive seed pods are characteristic of the genus Lotus.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil in alpine ecosystems and is often found in meadows and mountainous areas.
