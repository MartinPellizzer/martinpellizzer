Lespedeza harmsii

Lespedeza harmsii, commonly known as the Harms' lespedeza, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or open areas. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Lespedeza harmsii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
