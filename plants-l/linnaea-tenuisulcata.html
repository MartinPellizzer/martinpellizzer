Linnaea tenuisulcata

Linnaea tenuisulcata, commonly known as the narrow-ribbed twinflower, is a small evergreen shrub native to the wet, acidic soils of the northeastern United States.

It features opposite, oval-shaped leaves with a glossy dark green surface and prominent midribs. The plant produces delicate, pinkish-white flowers in pairs, which bloom in early spring. Its foliage remains evergreen throughout the year, providing year-round interest in woodland gardens.

Linnaea tenuisulcata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments.
