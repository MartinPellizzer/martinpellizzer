Lonicera japonica f. chinensis

Lonicera japonica f. chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of yellow and orange, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that ripens in late summer and is edible for both humans and wildlife.

This species is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and has traditional medicinal uses in Chinese herbal medicine.
