Lindera praetermissa

Lindera praetermissa, commonly known as the Carolina sweetshrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurel and avocado trees. The plant features opposite, elliptic leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by dark blue berries that are edible for some wildlife.

Lindera praetermissa thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
