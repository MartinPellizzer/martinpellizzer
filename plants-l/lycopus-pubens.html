Lycopus pubens

Lycopus pubens, commonly known as rough blueweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as stream banks and wet meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and have a distinctive blue-gray color.

It produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators.
