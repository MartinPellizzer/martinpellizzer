Lampranthus glaucus var. tortuosus

Lampranthus glaucus var. tortuosus, commonly known as the tortuous ice plant, is a succulent species native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water, adapting to arid environments. The plant produces vibrant pink to red flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its unique, twisted stems and striking floral display make it a popular ornamental plant.
