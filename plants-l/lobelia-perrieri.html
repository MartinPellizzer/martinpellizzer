Lobelia perrieri

Lobelia perrieri, commonly known as the Perrier's lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and swamps. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Lobelia perrieri is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
