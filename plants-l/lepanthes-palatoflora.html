Lepanthes palatoflora

Lepanthes palatoflora, commonly known as the pale-flowered orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid with delicate, showy flowers that typically have a pale or white coloration. The plant produces a single spike of flowers, each with three sepals and three petals, with the lip being a distinctive feature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Lepanthes palatoflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
