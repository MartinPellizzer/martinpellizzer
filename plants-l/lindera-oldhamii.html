Lindera oldhamii

Lindera oldhamii, commonly known as Chinese wild laurustan, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurel and avocado plants. The plant features simple, alternate, ovate to elliptic leaves with a rough texture and a distinctive aromatic scent. Its small, yellowish-green flowers appear in clusters and are followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for birds.

Lindera oldhamii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
