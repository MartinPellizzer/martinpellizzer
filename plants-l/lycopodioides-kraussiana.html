Lycopodioides kraussiana

Lycopodioides kraussiana, commonly known as the Cape clubmoss, is a species of lycophyte native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae and is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and spore-producing structures. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its reproductive structures, called strobila, are clustered at the tips of its stems.

Lycopodioides kraussiana plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
