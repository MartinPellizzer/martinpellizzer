Lupinus columbianus

Lupinus columbianus, commonly known as the Columbia lupine, is a species of flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open areas such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Lupinus columbianus plays an important role in soil stabilization and is a valuable plant for ecological restoration projects.
