Lepisorus asterolepis

Lepisorus asterolepis, commonly known as the star-leaved polypody, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are often covered with small, star-shaped glands. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizome is creeping and produces numerous short, upright shoots.

Lepisorus asterolepis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in shaded gardens.
