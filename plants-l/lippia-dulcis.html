Lippia dulcis

Lippia dulcis, commonly known as sweet lippia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Lippia dulcis is also valued for its use in aromatherapy and as a natural flavoring agent.
