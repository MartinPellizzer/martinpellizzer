Lobivia hertrichiana f. incaica

Lobivia hertrichiana f. incaica, commonly known as the Inca Lily, is a unique cactus native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and a dense covering of white, woolly hairs that help protect it from intense sunlight and dehydration. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in specialized collections.
