Linaria pancicii

Linaria pancicii, commonly known as the Pancetti's toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the genus Linaria, which includes various species of annual and perennial flowering plants. The plant produces striking, colorful flowers that resemble those of the common toadflax, often in shades of yellow and orange. Linaria pancicii is characterized by its slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

It is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.
