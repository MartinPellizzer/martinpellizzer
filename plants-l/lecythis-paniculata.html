Lecythis paniculata

Lecythis paniculata, commonly known as the yellow sacha in South America, is a tropical tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves and produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the rainy season. The tree is notable for its heavy, woody fruit, which contains a single large seed surrounded by a fleshy, yellow pulp. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local communities.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
