Lipozygis tenella

Lipozygis tenella, commonly known as the slender lily, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of ponds.

Lipozygis tenella is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in water gardens.
