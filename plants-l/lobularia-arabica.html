Lobularia arabica

Lobularia arabica, commonly known as sweet Alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and is often used in garden landscapes for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Lobularia arabica is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
