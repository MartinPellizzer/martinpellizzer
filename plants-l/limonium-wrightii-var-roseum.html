Limonium wrightii var. roseum

Limonium wrightii var. roseum, commonly known as the pink sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, pink to lavender-colored flowers. The plant produces bracts that are often mistaken for petals, giving it a unique appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and drought tolerance.
