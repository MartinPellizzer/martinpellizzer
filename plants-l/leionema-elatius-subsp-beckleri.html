Leionema elatius subsp. beckleri

Leionema elatius subsp.

beckleri, commonly known as the silver-leafed leionema, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia. It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery-white pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which appear in clusters during the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal regions and rocky habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
