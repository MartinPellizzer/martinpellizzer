Linum peyronii

Linum peyronii, commonly known as Peyron's flax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the genus Linum, which includes various species of flax known for their showy blue or white flowers. Peyron's flax typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has slender, branched stems with small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces delicate, five-petaled flowers that bloom in early spring, often attracting pollinators such as bees.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic contribution.
