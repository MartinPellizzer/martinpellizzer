Lathyrus bitlisicus

Lathyrus bitlisicus, commonly known as the Bitlis vetch, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers are usually purple or violet, adding ornamental value to its natural habitat.
