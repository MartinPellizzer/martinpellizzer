Litsea sebifera var. normalis

Litsea sebifera var. normalis, commonly known as the Indian bay leaf or wild bay leaf, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including parts of India, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, ovate leaves that are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark blue berries.

The leaves are typically harvested for their essential oils and medicinal properties.
