Leptadenia visciformis

Leptadenia visciformis, commonly known as the sticky leaf or African milkweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, opposite leaves that often have a sticky or waxy coating. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system to access water.

Leptadenia visciformis is also noted for its latex sap, which has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
