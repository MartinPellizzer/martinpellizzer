Lactuca surugensis

Lactuca surugensis, commonly known as the Japanese endive, is a perennial plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright, leafy stems and loose, bluish-green to white flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate climates and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks. Its leaves are edible and have a slightly bitter taste, making it a popular ingredient in salads and traditional dishes.

The plant also has medicinal uses in some cultures, though it is less commonly cultivated than other lettuce varieties.
