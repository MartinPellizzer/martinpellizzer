Laurus chinensis

Laurus chinensis, commonly known as Chinese laurel, is a broadleaf evergreen shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a fragrant aroma when crushed. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for ornamental gardening.

Due to its aromatic properties, it has been used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cuisines.
