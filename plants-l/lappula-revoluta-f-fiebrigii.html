Lappula revoluta f. fiebrigii

Lappula revoluta f. fiebrigii, commonly known as the Fiebrig's lappula, is a variant of the lappula plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The flowers are typically small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and bloom during the summer months. This form is distinguished by its unique leaf morphology and growth habit compared to the typical variety.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and agricultural fields.
