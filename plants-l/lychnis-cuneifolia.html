Lychnis cuneifolia

Lychnis cuneifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, pink or red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant features linear, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 2 inches in length. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed sites.

Lychnis cuneifolia is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
