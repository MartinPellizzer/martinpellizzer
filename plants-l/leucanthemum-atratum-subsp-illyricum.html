Leucanthemum atratum subsp. illyricum

Leucanthemum atratum subsp.

illyricum, commonly known as the Illyrian oxeye, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It is a perennial herb characterized by its white ray florets and yellow disc florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.

The plant features a basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves and a tall, branching stem that bears numerous flower heads. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value has made it a subject of interest in horticulture and botanical studies.
