Lastreopsis coriaceosquamata

Lastreopsis coriaceosquamata, commonly known as the rock spurge, is a perennial plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are covered in small, scale-like structures. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in cyathia, a unique floral structure typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

Due to its striking appearance and resilience, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in dry gardens.
