Lycopodium gramineum

Lycopodium gramineum, commonly known as the clubmoss, is a vascular plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky soils. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its spiral arrangement of needle-like leaves and a strobilus, or cone-like structure, that produces spores. Lycopodium gramineum is characterized by its primitive structure, which includes a root system, stems, and leaves, and it reproduces via spores rather than seeds.

This plant plays an important ecological role in forest ecosystems by contributing to soil stability and providing habitat for various microorganisms.
