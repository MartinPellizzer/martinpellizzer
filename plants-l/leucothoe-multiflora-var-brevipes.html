Leucothoe multiflora var. brevipes

Leucothoe multiflora var. brevipes, commonly known as the dwarf beauty bush, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features attractive, fragrant white flowers that appear in clusters during late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, elliptical leaves that provide dense foliage throughout the growing season. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its slow growth rate and ornamental value make it a popular choice for landscape design.
