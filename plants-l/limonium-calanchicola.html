Limonium calanchicola

Limonium calanchicola, commonly known as the Calanchicola sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and limestone formations. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, often covered in a fine layer of white hair.

Limonium calanchicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
