Lonicera javanica

Lonicera javanica, commonly known as the Java honeysuckle, is a vigorous evergreen climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy on the underside. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange-red, blooming in clusters. Its fruit is a small, red, fleshy berry that ripens in late summer.

Lonicera javanica is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and showy flowers.
