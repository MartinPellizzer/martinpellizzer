Lagerstroemia tomentosa var. caudata

Lagerstroemia tomentosa var. caudata, commonly known as the woolly crape myrtle, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, grayish-green foliage and showy, clustered flowers that range from pink to red in color. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive flowers and unique, hairy leaves.
