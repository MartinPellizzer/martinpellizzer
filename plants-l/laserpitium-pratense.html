Laserpitium pratense

Laserpitium pratense, commonly known as meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. It produces yellow flowers with a central disk surrounded by ray florets, and its leaves are lance-shaped with hairy surfaces.

Laserpitium pratense is often found in open habitats and is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed environments.
