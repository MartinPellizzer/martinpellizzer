Lobivia hertrichiana var. laui

Lobivia hertrichiana var. laui, commonly known as the Laui's Lobivia, is a rare and striking cactus native to the Andes Mountains of southern Peru.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and vibrant, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant features thick, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss and providing protection from herbivores.

This variety is highly valued by succulent enthusiasts for its unique appearance and challenging cultivation requirements.
