Lilium michiganense f. peramoenum

Lilium michiganense f. peramoenum, commonly known as the Michigan lily, is a rare and unique variant of the Michigan lily.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, and often recurved petals that give it a striking appearance. This cultivar is native to the Great Lakes region of North America and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pale pink.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in specialized gardens and botanical collections.
