Lucuma lecomtei

Lucuma lecomtei, commonly known as the Andean starfruit, is a species of flowering plant native to the high Andes of South America.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its striking, star-shaped yellow fruits that resemble small lanterns. The plant thrives in cool, humid climates at elevations between 2,500 and 4,000 meters. Its leaves are broad, leathery, and evergreen, while its flowers are typically white or pink.

The fruit is not only ornamental but also has traditional uses in local cultures.
