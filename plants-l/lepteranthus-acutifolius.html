Lepteranthus acutifolius

Lepteranthus acutifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pale yellow. It is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy or rocky soils.
