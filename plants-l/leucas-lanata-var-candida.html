Leucas lanata var. candida

Leucas lanata var. candida, commonly known as the white hairy leucas, is a variant of the hairy leucas species native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, white, woolly foliage that covers the stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or rocky areas. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in shades of pink or white.

This variant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique texture and appearance.
