Linum katiae

Linum katiae, commonly known as the Katia flax, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and delicate, blue to purple flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil. Linum katiae thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
