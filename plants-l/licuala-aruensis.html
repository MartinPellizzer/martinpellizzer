Licuala aruensis

Licuala aruensis, commonly known as the Aru palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Indonesia, particularly the Aru Islands. The plant is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach up to three meters in diameter. Its trunk is typically short and robust, with a smooth, grayish-brown surface.

Licuala aruensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
