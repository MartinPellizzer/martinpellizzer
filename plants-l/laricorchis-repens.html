Laricorchis repens

Laricorchis repens, commonly known as the creeping lily of the valley, is a rare and elusive orchid species native to the high elevations of the Sierra Nevada mountains in California.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a slender, creeping rhizome that produces a single, delicate white flower with a prominent lip. The plant is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves and is often found growing in shaded, moist forest soils. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered endangered and is protected under the Endangered Species Act.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique adaptations to high-altitude environments and its role in the ecological dynamics of its native range.
