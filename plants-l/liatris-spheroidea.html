Liatris spheroidea

Liatris spheroidea, commonly known as the globe thistle, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its spherical, spiky flower heads that resemble small thistles. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, while its flowers range in color from white to pink or purple.

Liatris spheroidea is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
