Liatris acidota var. mucronata

Liatris acidota var. mucronata, commonly known as the blunt-tipped blazing star, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely leafed stems that bear dense clusters of pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges. It produces small, achene-like seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread across suitable environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
