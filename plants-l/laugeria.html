Laugeria

Laugeria, commonly known as the African orchid or *Laugeria gerrardii*, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers clustered in spikes.

Laugeria is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
