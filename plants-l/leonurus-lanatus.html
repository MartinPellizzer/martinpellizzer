Leonurus lanatus

Leonurus lanatus, commonly known as the "hedgehog plant" or "lion's tail," is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its unique, spiky, purple flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which contribute to its rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy perennial in many climates.

Leonurus lanatus is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
