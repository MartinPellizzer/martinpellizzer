Lindsaea napaea

Lindsaea napaea, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air. Its rhizomes are creeping and often covered in a thick layer of moss-like tissue.

Lindsaea napaea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded, humid environments.
