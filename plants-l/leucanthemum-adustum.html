Leucanthemum adustum

Leucanthemum adustum, commonly known as the woodland oxeye, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its showy white flowers with yellow centers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Leucanthemum adustum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
