Laetia

Laetia, commonly known as the ghost plant, is a rare and unique species of liverwort.

It is a non-vascular plant that thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant's most distinctive feature is its translucent, leaf-like structures that resemble ghostly shapes, giving it its common name. Laetia lacks true roots, stems, and leaves, instead having a thalloid body composed of a single layer of cells.

Due to its elusive nature and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a challenging plant to study and cultivate.
