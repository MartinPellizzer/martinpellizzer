Lophochloa pumila var. glabrescens

Lophochloa pumila var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth lophochloa, is a grass species characterized by its compact growth habit and reduced leaf pubescence.

It typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in moist, well-drained soils. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its botanical significance lies in its morphological variation and ecological adaptability.

It is occasionally cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and resilience.
