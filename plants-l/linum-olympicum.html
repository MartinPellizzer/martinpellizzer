Linum olympicum

Linum olympicum, commonly known as Olympic flax, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue or white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Linum olympicum is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance, supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
