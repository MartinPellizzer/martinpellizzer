Lonchocarpus vallicola

Lonchocarpus vallicola, commonly known as the mountain senna, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, and the plant is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
