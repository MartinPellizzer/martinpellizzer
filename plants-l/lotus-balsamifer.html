Lotus balsamifer

Lotus balsamifer, commonly known as the balsam tree, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils. Its name derives from the balsamic scent of its flowers and the resinous properties of its bark.

The lotus balsamifer is also valued for its ornamental beauty and traditional medicinal uses.
