Lepanthes viridis

Lepanthes viridis, commonly known as the green orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking greenish-white flowers, which often have a subtle yellowish or pinkish hue. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its leaves are usually broad and strap-like, adapted to capture moisture from the air.

Lepanthes viridis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
