Linum austriacum subsp. mauritanicum

Linum austriacum subsp.

mauritanicum, commonly known as the Mauritanian flax, is a perennial herb native to North Africa. It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and small, blue to violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showing adaptations to drought conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its capsules contain numerous small seeds.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological niche and potential for medicinal or agricultural use.
