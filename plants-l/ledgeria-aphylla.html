Ledgeria aphylla

Ledgeria aphylla, commonly known as the leafless orchid, is a unique member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its lack of leaves, with photosynthesis occurring through its stems. Native to the cloud forests of Central and South America, it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of white and purple.

Due to its specialized habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
