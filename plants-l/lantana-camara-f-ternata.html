Lantana camara f. ternata

Lantana camara f. ternata, commonly known as the silverleaf lantana, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the Americas.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, depending on the variety. The leaves are typically narrow, lance-shaped, and covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving the plant its distinctive appearance. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and prolonged flowering period.

However, it can be invasive in certain regions, posing a threat to native plant communities.
