Linaria viscosa subsp. viscosa

Linaria viscosa subsp.

viscosa, commonly known as sticky toadflax, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in dry, rocky, or sandy soils and is characterized by its glandular, sticky leaves.

The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers in the spring, which are clustered in racemes. It is a member of the Scrophulariaceae family, now commonly classified under the Plantaginaceae family.

Its adaptability to arid environments makes it a common sight in Mediterranean and temperate regions.
