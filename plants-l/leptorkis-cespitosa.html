Leptorkis cespitosa

Leptorkis cespitosa, commonly known as the clustered broom, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and small, needle-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in ecological restoration projects.
