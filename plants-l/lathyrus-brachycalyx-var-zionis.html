Lathyrus brachycalyx var. zionis

Lathyrus brachycalyx var. zionis, commonly known as the Zion peavine, is a climbing legume native to the western United States.

It features slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers in shades of pink to violet, typically blooming in late spring. Its fruits are cylindrical pods containing several elongated seeds.

This variety is adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a hardy plant in xeriscaped gardens.
