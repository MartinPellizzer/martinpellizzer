Lobelia deckenii var. tayloriana

Lobelia deckenii var. tayloriana, commonly known as Taylor's lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude grasslands of East Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, tubular blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, arranged alternately along the stem. This variety is often found in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in cooler, moist environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
