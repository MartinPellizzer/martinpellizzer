Lophopetalum grandiflorum

Lophopetalum grandiflorum, commonly known as the Chinese hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It features opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in clusters, which bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that ripens in the fall.

Lophopetalum grandiflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
