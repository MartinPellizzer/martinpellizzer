Lampranthus rupestris

Lampranthus rupestris, commonly known as the rock daphne, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, upright stems that are often tinged with red or purple, and its leaves are small, oval, and arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces vibrant, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and striking appearance make it a valuable addition to arid landscape designs.
