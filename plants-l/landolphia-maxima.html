Landolphia maxima

Landolphia maxima, commonly known as the giant arrowroot or African arrowroot, is a climbing shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces a starchy tuberous root that is used as a food source in some regions. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Landolphia maxima is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional medicinal uses in various African cultures.
