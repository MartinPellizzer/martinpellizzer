Lasianthus perakensis var. kalimantanensis

Lasianthus perakensis var. kalimantanensis, commonly known as the Kalimantan flameflower, is a tropical shrub native to the Indonesian island of Kalimantan.

It is characterized by its vibrant red and orange floral bracts, which are often mistaken for petals. The plant typically grows in lowland rainforests and is adapted to high humidity and warm temperatures. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth, glossy surface.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
