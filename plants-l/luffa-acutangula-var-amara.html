Luffa acutangula var. amara

Luffa acutangula var. amara, commonly known as the bitter luffa, is a variant of the sponge gourd native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its fibrous, spongy fruit that matures into a rough, net-like texture. The plant thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy surface.

The fruit is typically harvested when fully mature, though it can be used at various stages of development depending on the desired texture.
