Leptospermum lineare

Leptospermum lineare, commonly known as the narrowleaf tea tree, is a small evergreen shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Leptospermum lineare is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in Indigenous Australian medicine.
