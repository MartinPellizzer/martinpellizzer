Lobelia harrisii

Lobelia harrisii, commonly known as Harris's lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats like woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Lobelia harrisii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
