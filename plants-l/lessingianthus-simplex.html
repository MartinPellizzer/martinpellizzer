Lessingianthus simplex

Lessingianthus simplex, commonly known as the yellow sunflower, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow ray florets and dark brown disk florets, which give it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological landscapes.
