Lycium subglobosum

Lycium subglobosum, commonly known as the globe boxthorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, orange-red berries that are edible when ripe. The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, and it is known for its hardy, drought-resistant nature.

Lycium subglobosum plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
