Limnanthes douglasii subsp. rosea

Limnanthes douglasii subsp.

rosea, commonly known as the pink marsh plant, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its distinctive pink to rose-colored flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and pond edges, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, and it produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
