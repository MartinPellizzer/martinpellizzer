Lupinus tauris var. tauris

Lupinus tauris var. tauris, commonly known as the Taurian lupine, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces cylindrical seed pods that mature into a distinctive, spiny form. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species adapted to temperate climates.

Lupinus tauris var. tauris is valued for its ornamental appeal and nitrogen-fixing capabilities, contributing to soil fertility in natural and agricultural settings.
