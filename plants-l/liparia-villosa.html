Liparia villosa

Liparia villosa, commonly known as the hairy liparia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing herbaceous perennial that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. Its small, white to pale blue flowers bloom in clusters during the summer months.

Liparia villosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
