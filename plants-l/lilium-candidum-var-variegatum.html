Lilium candidum var. variegatum

Lilium candidum var. variegatum, commonly known as the variegated white lily, is a cultivar of the white lily native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking white petals with distinctive green or yellow markings, creating a visually appealing contrast. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often variegated as well. It produces fragrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its historical use in floral arrangements and religious symbolism.
