Loranthus carsonii

Loranthus carsonii, commonly known as Carson's mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches itself to the branches of its host trees.

It belongs to the family Loranthaceae and is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. The plant has a distinctive appearance with small, oval leaves and clusters of white or yellowish flowers. It produces small, fleshy berries that are often consumed by birds, aiding in seed dispersal.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can significantly affect the growth and health of its host tree.
