Lupinus nanus var. latifolius

Lupinus nanus var. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf lupine, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features compact, bushy growth with clusters of pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The leaves are typically narrow and hairy, and the plant produces small, spiny pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
