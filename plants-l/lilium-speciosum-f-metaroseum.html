Lilium speciosum f. metaroseum

Lilium speciosum f. metaroseum, commonly known as the roseum lily, is a unique cultivar of the species Lilium speciosum.

It is characterized by its striking, double-flowered blooms that resemble roses, with a layered, petal-like structure. The plant is a perennial herbaceous species native to the mountainous regions of China. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette.

This variety is highly valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
