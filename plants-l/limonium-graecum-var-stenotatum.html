Limonium graecum var. stenotatum

Limonium graecum var. stenotatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting flowers, which are often used in dried floral arrangements.
