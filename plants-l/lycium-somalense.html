Lycium somalense

Lycium somalense, commonly known as the Somali boxthorn, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Somalia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible and often consumed by local communities. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and limited water availability.

Lycium somalense plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small animals in its native environment.
