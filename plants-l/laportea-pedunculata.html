Laportea pedunculata

Laportea pedunculata, commonly known as the white wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves with a distinctive white underside, and its flowers are small, white or pale pink, clustered in umbels. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks. It has a fibrous root system and produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds.

Laportea pedunculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
