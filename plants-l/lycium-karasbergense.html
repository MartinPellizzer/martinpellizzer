Lycium karasbergense

Lycium karasbergense, commonly known as Karasberg boxthorn, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family and is characterized by its dense, spiny growth habit. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Lycium karasbergense is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
