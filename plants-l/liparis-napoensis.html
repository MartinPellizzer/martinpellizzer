Liparis napoensis

Liparis napoensis, commonly known as the Napo Orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in the dense canopy. Its flowers are usually small, with three sepals and three petals, and it produces a single, elongated spur.

Liparis napoensis plays a vital role in the biodiversity of its ecosystem, supporting various pollinators and contributing to the rainforest's ecological balance.
