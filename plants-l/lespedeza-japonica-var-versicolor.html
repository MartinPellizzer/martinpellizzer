Lespedeza japonica var. versicolor

Lespedeza japonica var. versicolor, commonly known as Japanese clover, is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial native to East Asia.

It features trifoliate leaves with a distinctive trifoliate structure, and its flowers range from white to pink and red, often displaying a striking color variation. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental and erosion control purposes. Its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia makes it beneficial for soil fertility.

Lespedeza japonica var. versicolor is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
