Limonium obesifolium

Limonium obesifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and its ability to thrive in coastal and saline environments. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder, which helps reduce water loss. Limonium obesifolium is a perennial species that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
