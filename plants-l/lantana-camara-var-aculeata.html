Lantana camara var. aculeata

Lantana camara var. aculeata, commonly known as the spiked lantana, is a vigorous shrub native to the Americas.

It features opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are typically lance-shaped. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom throughout the growing season. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it both ornamental and invasive in many regions.

The variety is distinguished by its spiny stems and bracts, which contribute to its defensive characteristics.
