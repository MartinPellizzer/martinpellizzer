Lilium japonicum f. roseum

Lilium japonicum f. roseum, commonly known as the Japanese lily roseum form, is a cultivated variant of the Japanese lily.

It is characterized by its striking pink to red flowers, which are more intensely colored than those of the typical species. The plant is a herbaceous perennial with strap-like, lance-shaped leaves that are often a deep green. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, thriving in temperate climates.

This form is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and floral arrangements.
