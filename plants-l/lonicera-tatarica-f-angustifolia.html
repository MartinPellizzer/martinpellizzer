Lonicera tatarica f. angustifolia

Lonicera tatarica f. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub characterized by its slender, ascending stems and opposite, lanceolate leaves.

It produces fragrant, tubular flowers that range from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant is native to eastern Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators. Its berries, which are orange-red and edible, mature in late summer and are a food source for birds.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and adaptability to a variety of soil conditions.
