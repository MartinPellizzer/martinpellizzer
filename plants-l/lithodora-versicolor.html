Lithodora versicolor

Lithodora versicolor, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, blue to violet flowers. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
