Limonium fradinianum

Limonium fradinianum, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its blue to purple flowers and spiny, leaf-like bracts that surround the flower cluster. The plant thrives in coastal areas and saline soils, making it well adapted to harsh, maritime environments. Its flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Limonium fradinianum is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
