Lavandula × chaytoriae

Lavandula × chaytoriae, commonly known as Chaytoria lavender, is a hybrid lavender species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and spikes of purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping. It is valued for its use in aromatherapy, essential oils, and as an ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both gardeners and perfumers.
