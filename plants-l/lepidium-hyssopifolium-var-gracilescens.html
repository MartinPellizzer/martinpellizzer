Lepidium hyssopifolium var. gracilescens

Lepidium hyssopifolium var. gracilescens, commonly known as slender peppergrass, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture, and it produces small, yellowish-white flowers in early spring.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in arid conditions.
