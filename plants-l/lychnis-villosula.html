Lychnis villosula

Lychnis villosula, commonly known as the downy scabious, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, clustered flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers, which range in color from pink to white, bloom in late spring to early summer.

Lychnis villosula is known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
