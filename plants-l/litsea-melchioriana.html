Litsea melchioriana

Litsea melchioriana, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that appear in clusters, giving it a fiery appearance. The plant produces small, round fruits that are edible for some bird species but not typically for humans. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a glossy, dark green surface.

Litsea melchioriana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks due to its vibrant floral display.
