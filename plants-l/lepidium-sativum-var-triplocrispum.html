Lepidium sativum var. triplocrispum

Lepidium sativum var. triplocrispum, commonly known as the three-rowed cress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, edible leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30-60 cm and prefers cool, moist environments. Its seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

The variety triplocrispum is distinguished by its uniquely triple-crisped leaf margins.
