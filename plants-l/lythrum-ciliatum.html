Lythrum ciliatum

Lythrum ciliatum, commonly known as narrow-leaved catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Lythraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, pink to red flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with fine, ciliate margins, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, ditches, and along stream banks.

Lythrum ciliatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
