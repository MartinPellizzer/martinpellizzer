Lysiloma candidum

Lysiloma candidum, commonly known as the white lythrum, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, pink to white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments like woodlands and stream banks.

Botanically, it belongs to the Lythraceae family, which includes other flowering plants with similar floral structures.
