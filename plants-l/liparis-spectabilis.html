Liparis spectabilis

Liparis spectabilis, commonly known as the showy orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features long, strap-like leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, violet or purple flowers. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland habitats.

Liparis spectabilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinator populations.
