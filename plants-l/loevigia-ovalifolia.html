Loevigia ovalifolia

Loevigia ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaf loevigia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often blooming during the wet season. Loevigia ovalifolia thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ecological research.
