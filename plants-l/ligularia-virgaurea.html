Ligularia virgaurea

Ligularia virgaurea, commonly known as the heartleaf arrowhead, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive arrowhead base, often adorned with serrated edges. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters during late summer, which are followed by small, dry fruits. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its foliage is popular in ornamental gardening for its striking appearance and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
