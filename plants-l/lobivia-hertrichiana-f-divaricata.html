Lobivia hertrichiana f. divaricata

Lobivia hertrichiana f. divaricata, commonly known as the Variegated Lobivia, is a rare and striking hybrid succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It features a compact, rounded rosette of thick, fleshy leaves that are often adorned with distinctive white or yellow markings. The plant produces tall, branched stems that bear clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, requiring minimal water to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is highly sought after by collectors and requires careful cultivation.
