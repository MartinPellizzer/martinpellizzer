Lepidium oleraceum var. serrulatum

Lepidium oleraceum var. serrulatum, commonly known as the serrated cress, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, green, edible leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, fertile soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields. Its leaves are used in culinary applications, adding a peppery flavor to dishes.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
