Ligustrum myrtifolium

Ligustrum myrtifolium, commonly known as the myrtleleaf privet, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, oval leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, followed by black, fleshy berries that are edible for some birds. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often used in landscaping due to its adaptability and ornamental value.

The plant is also known for its ability to tolerate partial shade and is relatively low maintenance.
