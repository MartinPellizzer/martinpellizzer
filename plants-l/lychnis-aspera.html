Lychnis aspera

Lychnis aspera, commonly known as rough fireweed, is a flowering plant in the carnation family (Caryophyllaceae).

It is characterized by its erect stems and clusters of pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has hairy, rough-textured leaves that are typically lance-shaped and slightly serrated. Native to Europe, it is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
