Leontopodium × gracile

Leontopodium × gracile, commonly known as the edelweiss, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Alps.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its dense, woolly white foliage and small, showy yellow flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, harsh environments. Its unique appearance has made it a symbol of alpine beauty and resilience.
