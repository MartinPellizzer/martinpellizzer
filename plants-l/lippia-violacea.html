Lippia violacea

Lippia violacea, commonly known as violet verbena, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Lippia violacea is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
