Linaria amethystea f. broussonetii

Linaria amethystea f. broussonetii, commonly known as the Broussonet's throatwort, is a compact, mat-forming plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It features slender, branching stems covered in fine, hairy foliage. The plant produces striking, tubular flowers in shades of purple and pink, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly serrated edge.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.
