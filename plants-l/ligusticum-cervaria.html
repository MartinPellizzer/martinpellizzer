Ligusticum cervaria

Ligusticum cervaria, commonly known as woodland cowparsley, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its root system is fibrous, and it produces a mild, aromatic scent.

Ligusticum cervaria is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
