Liparis tipuloides

Liparis tipuloides, commonly known as the mosquito orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a slender, leafless stem that produces a single, showy flower. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with a distinctive lip that resembles a mosquito, hence its common name.

Liparis tipuloides is found in various regions of Asia, including China, Japan, and Southeast Asia.
