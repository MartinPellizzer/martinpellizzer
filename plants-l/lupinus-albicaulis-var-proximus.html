Lupinus albicaulis var. proximus

Lupinus albicaulis var. proximus, commonly known as the silverleaf lupine, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and erect, spindly stems that bear clusters of small, pea-like flowers in shades of white to pale purple. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open grasslands and along streambanks. Its root system includes nitrogen-fixing symbiotic relationships with rhizobia, enhancing soil fertility.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
