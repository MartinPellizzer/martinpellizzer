Leycesteria insignis

Leycesteria insignis, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, appearing in clusters during the spring. These flowers are pollinated by insects, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens in temperate climates.
