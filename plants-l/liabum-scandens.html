Liabum scandens

Liabum scandens, commonly known as the climbing hibiscus, is a tropical vine native to regions of Africa and Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, yellow, and orange. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to climb using its tendrils, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening. Its flowers bloom throughout the growing season, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
