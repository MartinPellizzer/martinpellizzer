Leptospermum myrsinoides var. latifolium

Leptospermum myrsinoides var. latifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved tea tree, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its aromatic foliage.
