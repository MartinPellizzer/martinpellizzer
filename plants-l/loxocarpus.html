Loxocarpus

Loxocarpus, commonly known as the "butterfly pea," is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

These plants are characterized by their distinctive, often colorful flowers that resemble butterflies, hence their common name. Loxocarpus species are typically shrubs or small trees, native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas. They have compound leaves and produce pods that contain seeds.

The genus is noted for its ornamental value and some species are used in traditional medicine.
