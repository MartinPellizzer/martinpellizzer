Lastrea pauperis

Lastrea pauperis, commonly known as the poor man's aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features rosettes of fleshy, gray-green leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers on slender stalks during the warmer months. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their soothing properties.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it an ideal choice for low-maintenance gardens.
