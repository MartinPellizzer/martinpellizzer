Lamium cymbalariifolium

Lamium cymbalariifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf deadnettle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy surface and produces clusters of small, two-lipped, pinkish-purple flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae), which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves.

Its flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
