Lecaniodiscus cupanioides

Lecaniodiscus cupanioides, commonly known as the golden star cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the deserts of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking visual contrast to its green foliage. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, a common adaptation for reducing water loss in arid environments.

Lecaniodiscus cupanioides is well-suited for xeriscaping and requires minimal water once established.
