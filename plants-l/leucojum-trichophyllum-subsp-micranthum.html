Leucojum trichophyllum subsp. micranthum

Leucojum trichophyllum subsp.

micranthum, commonly known as the small white bellflower, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe. It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers.

The plant is typically found in woodland and shaded areas, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate appearance and early spring blooming habit.
