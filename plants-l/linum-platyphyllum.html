Linum platyphyllum

Linum platyphyllum, commonly known as the broad-leaved flax, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Linaceae family, which includes other flax species. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fibrous stems and leaves have been used traditionally for their textile and medicinal properties.

Linum platyphyllum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitats.
