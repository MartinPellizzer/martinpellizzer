Linum leonii

Linum leonii, commonly known as the scarlet pimpernel, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the genus Linum, which includes various species of flax. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically pink or red, with five petals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Linum leonii is known for its rapid growth and short blooming period, typically from spring to early summer.
