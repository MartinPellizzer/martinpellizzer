Lonicera turcomanica var. subvillosa

Lonicera turcomanica var. subvillosa, commonly known as the Turkmen honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to Central Asia.

It features slender, arching stems covered with fine, soft hairs, giving it a somewhat velvety appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a slightly hairy surface.

This species is often found in dry, open habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
