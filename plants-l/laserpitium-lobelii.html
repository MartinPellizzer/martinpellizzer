Laserpitium lobelii

Laserpitium lobelii, commonly known as the Lobel's hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant features tall, slender stems with narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces bright yellow to orange composite flowers. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
