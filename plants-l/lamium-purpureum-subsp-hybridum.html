Lamium purpureum subsp. hybridum

Lamium purpureum subsp.

hybridum, commonly known as purple deadnettle, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive purple underside and produces small, two-lipped, pink or purple flowers.

The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along roadsides. It is a perennial species that spreads through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in gardens and disturbed soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it easily identifiable and ecologically significant in various habitats.
