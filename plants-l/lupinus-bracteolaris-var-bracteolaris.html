Lupinus bracteolaris var. bracteolaris

Lupinus bracteolaris var. bracteolaris, commonly known as the Sierra lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and showy blue to purple flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open meadows and rocky areas.
