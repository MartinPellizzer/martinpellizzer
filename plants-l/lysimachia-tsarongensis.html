Lysimachia tsarongensis

Lysimachia tsarongensis, commonly known as the Tsarong's chickweed, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan region of India.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and often prostrate.

Lysimachia tsarongensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
