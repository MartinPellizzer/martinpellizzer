Loranthus glaberrimus

Loranthus glaberrimus, commonly known as the smooth mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily in the genus Eucalyptus.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while it provides some benefits through its flowers and berries. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. Its berries are typically red or orange and contain sticky seeds that adhere to the bark of host trees, facilitating propagation.

Loranthus glaberrimus is found in various regions of Australia and is an important part of the ecological dynamics of its host species.
