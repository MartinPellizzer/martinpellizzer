Lolium temulentum var. semiglabrum

Lolium temulentum var. semiglabrum, commonly known as the smooth ryegrass, is a variant of the annual ryegrass species.

It is characterized by its reduced leaf pubescence, giving it a smoother appearance compared to other varieties. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and has a clump-forming habit. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

This variety is often found in disturbed soils and is used in forage production due to its nutritional value.
