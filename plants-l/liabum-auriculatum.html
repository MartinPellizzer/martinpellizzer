Liabum auriculatum

Liabum auriculatum, commonly known as the ear-shaped leaf vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that often have a distinctive ear-like projection, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by round, fleshy fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage.

Its rapid growth habit makes it suitable for covering trellises or creating green walls.
