Lavandula buchii var. gracilis

Lavandula buchii var. gracilis, commonly known as slender lavender, is a compact, low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces spikes of fragrant, pale purple flowers. This variety is well adapted to dry, sunny conditions and thrives in well-drained soil. It is often used in rock gardens and as a border plant due to its ornamental value.

Its essential oils are also valued for their aromatic and medicinal properties.
