Loranthus cinereus

Loranthus cinereus, commonly known as the ash mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches itself to the branches of ash trees.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own carbohydrates through photosynthesis using the light it captures. The plant has small, scale-like leaves and produces yellowish-white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its berries are red and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This species is often found in temperate regions and is considered a significant component of forest ecosystems.
