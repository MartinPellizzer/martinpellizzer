Lycopersicon solanum-lycopersicum

Lycopersicon solanum-lycopersicum, commonly known as the tomato, is a herbaceous annual plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems, alternate leaves, and showy flowers that are typically yellow or white. The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that are edible when mature. Its botanical features include a taproot system and the ability to thrive in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

The tomato is widely cultivated for its nutritious fruit, which is rich in vitamins and antioxidants.
