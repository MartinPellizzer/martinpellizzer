Lonicera acuminata var. japonica

Lonicera acuminata var. japonica, commonly known as Japanese honeysuckle, is a deciduous climbing vine native to East Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated margins and produces fragrant, tubular flowers that range from yellow to orange in color. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to twine around supports, making it both ornamental and invasive in some regions. Its fruit is a yellowish capsule that splits open to release small, black seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal but requires careful management due to its aggressive spreading habit.
