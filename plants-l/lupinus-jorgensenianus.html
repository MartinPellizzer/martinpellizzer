Lupinus jorgensenianus

Lupinus jorgensenianus, commonly known as the Jorgensen's lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its blue to violet flowers bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Lupinus jorgensenianus is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
