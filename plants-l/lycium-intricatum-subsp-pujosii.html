Lycium intricatum subsp. pujosii

Lycium intricatum subsp.

pujosii, commonly known as the Pujos' boxthorn, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands. It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, red berries that are edible.

The plant thrives in arid and rocky environments, showcasing adaptations to withstand drought. Its leaves are typically narrow and leathery, reducing water loss.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for birds and other wildlife.
