Lupinus vallicola subsp. apricus

Lupinus vallicola subsp.

apricus, commonly known as the Sierra lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of blue to violet pea-like flowers.

The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in mountainous regions. Lupinus vallicola subsp.

apricus plays an important ecological role by fixing nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plant communities.
