Linaria multicaulis subsp. pseudosupina

Linaria multicaulis subsp.

pseudosupina, commonly known as the narrowleaf toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and bright yellow, spurred flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its leaves are narrow and linear, arranged alternately along the stem.

This subspecies is part of the Plantaginaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
