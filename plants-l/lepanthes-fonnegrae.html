Lepanthes fonnegrae

Lepanthes fonnegrae, commonly known as the Black Orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking dark violet to black petals and sepals, which give it its distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are small but highly fragrant, often blooming during the rainy season.

Lepanthes fonnegrae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is a subject of interest in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
