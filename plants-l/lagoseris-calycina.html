Lagoseris calycina

Lagoseris calycina, commonly known as the slender sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble sunflowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

The showy yellow flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
