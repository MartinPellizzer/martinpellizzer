Lepidium virginicum subsp. virginicum

Lepidium virginicum subsp.

virginicum, commonly known as peppergrass, is a flowering plant native to North America. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.
