Linostoma scandens var. cambodianum

Linostoma scandens var. cambodianum, commonly known as the Cambodian climbing fern, is a rare and lesser-known variant of the Linostoma scandens species.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like fronds that exhibit a unique, intricate pattern of lobes and divisions. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine, often found in tropical and subtropical regions with high humidity. Its leaves are usually light green, with a slightly waxy texture that helps it retain moisture in its native environment.

This variant is of particular interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and potential ecological significance in its habitat.
