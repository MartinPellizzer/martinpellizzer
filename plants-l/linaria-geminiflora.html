Linaria geminiflora

Linaria geminiflora, commonly known as the twin-flowered toadflax, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces two flowers per stem, which are spurred at the base, a distinctive feature of the genus Linaria. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Linaria geminiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its bright, showy blooms.
