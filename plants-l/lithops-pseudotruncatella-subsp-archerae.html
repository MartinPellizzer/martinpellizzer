Lithops pseudotruncatella subsp. archerae

Lithops pseudotruncatella subsp.

archerae, commonly known as the Cape Town lithops, is a succulent plant native to South Africa. It is characterized by its compact, fleshy leaves that resemble rocks, allowing it to blend into its arid environment.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the winter months, adding a striking contrast to its otherwise muted appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
