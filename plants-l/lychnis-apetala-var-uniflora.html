Lychnis apetala var. uniflora

Lychnis apetala var. uniflora, commonly known as the single-flowered catchfly, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, hairy stems that grow from 10 to 30 centimeters in height. The plant produces small, pink to red flowers with five petals, arranged singly at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.
