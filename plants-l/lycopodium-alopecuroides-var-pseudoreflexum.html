Lycopodium alopecuroides var. pseudoreflexum

Lycopodium alopecuroides var. pseudoreflexum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf clubmoss, is a low-growing evergreen plant characterized by its slender, thread-like leaves and sporangia-bearing strobili.

It belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae and is native to certain regions of southern Africa. The plant typically forms dense mats on rocky or sandy soils, thriving in arid and semi-arid environments. Its reproductive structures are clustered at the tips of its stems, producing spores for propagation.

This variety is distinguished by its narrower leaf blades compared to the typical form of Lycopodium alopecuroides.
