Ligustrum coriaceum

Ligustrum coriaceum, commonly known as the thick-leaved privet, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, dark green, leathery leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by dark blue berries. It is often used in landscaping for its dense growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for hedges and ornamental planting.
