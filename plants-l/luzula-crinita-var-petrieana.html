Luzula crinita var. petrieana

Luzula crinita var. petrieana, commonly known as the silver hair grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and dense clusters of soft, silvery-white to gray-green leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in late summer, which are arranged in spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks, and is often found in the understory of coniferous forests.

Luzula crinita var. petrieana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
