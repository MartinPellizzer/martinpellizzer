Lautembergia coriacea var. perrieri

Lautembergia coriacea var. perrieri, commonly known as the Cape rose, is a succulent shrub native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, woody stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has thick, leathery leaves that help it retain water in arid conditions. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and seasonal blooming.
