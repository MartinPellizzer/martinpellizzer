Limonium ramosissimum subsp. siculum

Limonium ramosissimum subsp.

siculum, commonly known as the Sicilian sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers.

The plant thrives in coastal areas and sandy soils, often found in dune environments. Its flowering period typically occurs in late summer to early autumn.

The subspecies is distinguished by its more robust growth and slightly larger flowers compared to other members of the species.
