Lobivia haematantha subsp. chorrillosensis

Lobivia haematantha subsp.

chorrillosensis, commonly known as the Chorrillosensis Lobivia, is a succulent plant native to the coastal regions of Peru. It is characterized by its compact, rosette-forming growth habit and thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a reddish tinge.

The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice among cactus and succulent enthusiasts.
