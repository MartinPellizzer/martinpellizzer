Leptostemma fasciculatum

Leptostemma fasciculatum, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, woodlands and stream banks, preferring partial to full shade. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a violet or purple throat. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological value, supporting various pollinators.
