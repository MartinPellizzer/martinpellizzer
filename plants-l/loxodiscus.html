Loxodiscus

Loxodiscus, commonly known as the spider flower, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Malvaceae.

It is characterized by its showy, often multicolored bracts that resemble spider-like structures, which are not true flowers but modified leaves. The plants are native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, particularly in areas with warm, humid climates. Loxodiscus species typically have palmate leaves and produce small, inconspicuous flowers that are surrounded by the vibrant bracts.

These plants are often cultivated for their ornamental value in gardens and as houseplants due to their striking appearance.
