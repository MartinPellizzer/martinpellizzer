Lagunaria patersonia var. typica

Lagunaria patersonia var. typica, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, spiky blue or purple flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with silvery-gray foliage that reflects sunlight. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sandy or rocky coastal habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for coastal gardens and conservation efforts.
