Lacroixia minor

Lacroixia minor, commonly known as the lesser lacroixia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils. It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
