Lupinus dedeckerae

Lupinus dedeckerae, commonly known as Dedeckera's lupine, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers in shades of purple or white, which bloom during the warmer months. Lupinus dedeckerae is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system that helps it survive in dry conditions.

It plays a role in soil nitrogen fixation, contributing to ecosystem health in its native habitat.
