Lomelosia persica

Lomelosia persica, commonly known as Persian lily, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky and well-drained soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lanceolate and arranged in a basal rosette, while its flowers bloom in late summer.

Lomelosia persica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
