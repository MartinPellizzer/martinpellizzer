Liabum eremophilum

Liabum eremophilum, commonly known as the desert lantana, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring woody stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and low-maintenance plant for desert and semi-arid landscapes.
