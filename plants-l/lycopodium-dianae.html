Lycopodium dianae

Lycopodium dianae, commonly known as the Dianae clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is a small, evergreen herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or rocky areas. The plant features a slender, upright stem with whorled leaves that are often needle-like in appearance. Lycopodium dianae reproduces via spores rather than seeds, and its life cycle includes a distinct gametophyte stage.

This plant is of interest to botanists due to its primitive characteristics and its role in understanding the evolution of early land plants.
