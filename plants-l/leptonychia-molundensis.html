Leptonychia molundensis

Leptonychia molundensis, commonly known as the Molund's bladderwort, is a small aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in Central Africa.

It is characterized by its tiny, bladder-like structures called vesicles, which are used to trap small aquatic organisms. The plant has slender, branching stems and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color. It thrives in shallow, still waters with abundant sunlight and is often found in marshy areas.

Due to its specialized adaptations, it plays a role in the local ecosystem by contributing to nutrient cycling and providing habitat for microorganisms.
