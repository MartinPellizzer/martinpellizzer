Linnaea borealis f. breviloba

Linnaea borealis f. breviloba, commonly known as the dwarf twinflower, is a rare and diminutive variant of the twinflower plant.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring. This plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as boreal forests and rocky slopes. Its leaves are oval-shaped and have a distinctive waxy coating.

The species is noted for its low-growing habit and is often found in the northern regions of North America and Eurasia.
