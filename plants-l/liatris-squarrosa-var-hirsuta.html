Liatris squarrosa var. hirsuta

Liatris squarrosa var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy blazing star, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its showy, spiky flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant features upright, hairy stems that support its pink to purple flower clusters, which are composed of many small, tubular florets. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is often found in prairies, open woods, and disturbed sites, thriving in full sun and well-drained soils.
