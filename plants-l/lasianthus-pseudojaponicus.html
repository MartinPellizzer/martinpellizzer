Lasianthus pseudojaponicus

Lasianthus pseudojaponicus, commonly known as the Japanese flame flower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking red or orange tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth surface, while the flowers are clustered in terminal inflorescences.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
