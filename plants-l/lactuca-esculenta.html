Lactuca esculenta

Lactuca esculenta, commonly known as lettuce, is a cool-season annual plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its rosette growth habit, with spoon-shaped leaves that vary in color from light green to dark green. The plant typically grows to a height of 20–60 cm, with a fibrous root system and a flowering head that produces small yellow flowers. Lettuce is cultivated for its edible leaves, which are used in salads and other culinary dishes worldwide.

Its growth is influenced by temperature, with optimal conditions ranging between 15–20°C.
