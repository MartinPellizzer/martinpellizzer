Lavandula stoechas var. elongata

Lavandula stoechas var. elongata, commonly known as the Spanish lavender or elongated lavender, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features narrow, silver-green leaves and produces spikes of fragrant, violet-blue flowers. The plant is characterized by its unique, elongated flower spikes that are often adorned with glandular, hairy bracts. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and its ability to attract pollinators.
