Lycopodium porophilum

Lycopodium porophilum, commonly known as the porose clubmoss, is a small evergreen fern-like plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, scale-like leaves that form dense, cushion-like clusters. The plant produces spore-bearing structures called strobili, which are typically clustered at the tips of the stems. Lycopodium porophilum thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories or rocky crevices.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important component of certain forest ecosystems.
