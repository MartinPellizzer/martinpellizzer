Lagenocarpus adamantinus

Lagenocarpus adamantinus, commonly known as the hard-seeded tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its sturdy, woody structure and dense foliage. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that are encased in a hard, thick shell. Its leaves are typically elliptical and have a glossy surface.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
