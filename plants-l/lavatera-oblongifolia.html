Lavatera oblongifolia

Lavatera oblongifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallows, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It features large, showy flowers that range in color from pink to white, often with a subtle fragrance. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are typically narrow and oblong in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Lavatera oblongifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
