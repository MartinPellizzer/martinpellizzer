Lupinus polyphyllus var. laeteviridis

Lupinus polyphyllus var. laeteviridis, commonly known as the greenleaf lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of pea-like flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant has palmately compound leaves with numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open meadows and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant communities and a popular choice for ecological restoration projects.
