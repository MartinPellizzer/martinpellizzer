Lepisorus subhemionitideus

Lepisorus subhemionitideus, commonly known as the rock fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, creeping rhizomes and small, clustered fronds that often grow on rock surfaces. The leaves are typically lanceolate to ovate in shape, with a glossy green color and a smooth texture. It prefers humid, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
