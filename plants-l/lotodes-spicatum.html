Lotodes spicatum

Lotodes spicatum, commonly known as the spicate lotus, is a water plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its floating leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments. Its leaves are broad and rounded, often with a waxy surface to repel water.

The flowers are typically borne on long, slender stems and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
