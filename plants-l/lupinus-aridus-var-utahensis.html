Lupinus aridus var. utahensis

Lupinus aridus var. utahensis, commonly known as the Utah lupine, is a perennial legume native to the arid regions of the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, dry environments.

Lupinus aridus var. utahensis plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
