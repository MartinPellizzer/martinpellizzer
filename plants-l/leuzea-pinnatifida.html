Leuzea pinnatifida

Leuzea pinnatifida, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking yellow to orange inflorescences that resemble a crown. The plant features narrow, linear leaves arranged in a pinnate pattern, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats.

Leuzea pinnatifida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
