Leucanthemum oppositifolium

Leucanthemum oppositifolium, commonly known as the oppositeleaf whitelet, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy blooms. The plant features opposite leaf arrangement, giving it its scientific name, and has hairy, serrated leaves that are typically lance-shaped. Its flowers are white with yellow centers, resembling daisies, and bloom in late summer to early fall.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
