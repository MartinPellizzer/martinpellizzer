Ligustrum magnoliifolium

Ligustrum magnoliifolium, commonly known as the magnolia-leaf privet, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters, which bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a small, dark purple drupe that contains a single seed.

Ligustrum magnoliifolium is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
