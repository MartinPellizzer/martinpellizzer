Labordia kaalae

Labordia kaalae, commonly known as the Hawaiian pili leaf.

This plant is a flowering shrub native to the Hawaiian Islands, characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered white flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in coastal and lowland areas. The leaves are typically leathery and have a rough texture, while the plant produces small, edible berries.

Labordia kaalae plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various native wildlife species.
