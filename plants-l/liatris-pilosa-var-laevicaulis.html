Liatris pilosa var. laevicaulis

Liatris pilosa var. laevicaulis, commonly known as the smooth blazing star, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features upright, grass-like leaves and produces striking purple to pink flower clusters that bloom in late summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Its botanical structure supports pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it an important species in native plant gardens.
