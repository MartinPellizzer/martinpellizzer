Liparis odorata

Liparis odorata, commonly known as the sweet orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks. Its roots are fibrous and often grow in clusters, which help anchor it to rocky or wooded substrates.

The flowers are usually pink or white with a distinctive lip that enhances its ornamental appeal.
