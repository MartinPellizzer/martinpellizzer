Laurus barbusano

Laurus barbusano, commonly known as the narrow-leaved laurel, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dark green, elliptical leaves that are typically narrow and have a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into dark purple berries, which are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Laurus barbusano is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

It is also valued in traditional herbal medicine for its aromatic properties.
