Lotus hispidus var. odoratus

Lotus hispidus var. odoratus, commonly known as the fragrant lotus, is a perennial aquatic plant native to parts of Asia.

It features showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom above the water surface, often with a subtle, sweet fragrance. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, hairy textures, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its roots are adapted to thrive in muddy or shallow water environments.

This variety is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in wetland habitats.
