Lespedeza juncea f. variegata

Lespedeza juncea f. variegata, commonly known as the variegated lespedeza, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves with distinctive white or yellow variegation. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or red, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
