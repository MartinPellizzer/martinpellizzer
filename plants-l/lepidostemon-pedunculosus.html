Lepidostemon pedunculosus

Lepidostemon pedunculosus, commonly known as the clustered lepidostemon, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of white to pale purple. The leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is typically found in forest margins or rocky areas.

The plant is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
