Lycaste cruenta var. sulphurea

Lycaste cruenta var. sulphurea, commonly known as the sulphur-flowered lycaste, is a rare and striking orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow-orange flowers with intricate patterns. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine, white pubescence.

This variety is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts due to its unique coloration and rarity in cultivation.
