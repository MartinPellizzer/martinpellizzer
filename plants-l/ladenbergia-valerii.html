Ladenbergia valerii

Ladenbergia valerii, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is distinguished by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruit, a flattened pod, contains several seeds and is often used in traditional medicine.

The botanical features of Ladenbergia valerii make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
