Lindernia benthamii

Lindernia benthamii, commonly known as the bentham's lindernia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the family Linderniaceae and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, tubular flowers. The plant typically produces small, showy blooms in shades of purple and pink, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Lindernia benthamii is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of care.
