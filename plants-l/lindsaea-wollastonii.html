Lindsaea wollastonii

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are often clustered in dense tufts. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. Lindsaea wollastonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded, humid environments.
