Lathyrus frenchii

Lathyrus frenchii, commonly known as the French pea or French vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with tendrils that allow it to ascend supports such as fences or trellises. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in clusters. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three heart-shaped leaflets, and it has a fibrous root system.

Lathyrus frenchii is native to Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible seeds.
