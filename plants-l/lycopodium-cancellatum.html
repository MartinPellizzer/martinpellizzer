Lycopodium cancellatum

Lycopodium cancellatum, commonly known as the common clubmoss, is a primitive vascular plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its creeping, branched stems that produce small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces spore-bearing structures called strobila, which are clustered in dense, cone-like formations at the tips of the branches. Lycopodium cancellatum typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas.

It plays an important ecological role in soil stabilization and as a habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
