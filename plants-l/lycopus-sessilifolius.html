Lycopus sessilifolius

Lycopus sessilifolius, commonly known as narrow-leaved blueweed, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically narrow and sessile, giving it its scientific name.

Lycopus sessilifolius is often found in moist habitats like stream banks and wet meadows.
