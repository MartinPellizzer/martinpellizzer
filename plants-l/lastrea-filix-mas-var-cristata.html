Lastrea filix-mas var. cristata

Lastrea filix-mas var. cristata, commonly known as the crested snake plant, is a cultivar of the snake plant genus known for its distinctive, upward-curving leaves that form a rosette shape.

It is a succulent, native to tropical regions and is adapted to thrive in low-light conditions with minimal water. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that store water, making it highly drought-tolerant. It is often cultivated as a decorative indoor plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.

The varietal name "cristata" refers to the unique, crested growth pattern of its foliage.
