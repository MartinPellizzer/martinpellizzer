Leptospermum imbricatum

Leptospermum imbricatum, commonly known as the coastal tea tree, is a small evergreen shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in winter, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in coastal environments and is well adapted to sandy soils and salt spray.

Leptospermum imbricatum is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
