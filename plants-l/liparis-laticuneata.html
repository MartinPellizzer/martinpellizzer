Liparis laticuneata

Liparis laticuneata, commonly known as the broad-leaved marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Asia and North America.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in wet, marshy habitats and thrives in moist, acidic soils. It is a perennial species that produces its flowers in late spring to early summer.

The orchid has a complex root system and relies on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.
