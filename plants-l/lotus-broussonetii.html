Lotus broussonetii

Lotus broussonetii, commonly known as the African lotus, is a flowering plant native to Africa and parts of the Middle East.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven leaflets and produces pods that contain edible seeds. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.

The plant thrives in wet, marshy environments and is adapted to tolerate waterlogged soils.
