Lasiostoma oblongum

Lasiostoma oblongum, commonly known as the broadleaf marsh plant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh environments.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, exhibiting a rhizomatous growth habit that allows it to spread and colonize new areas. The plant features broad, ovate leaves that are often floating or partially submerged, aiding in photosynthesis in aquatic conditions. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually greenish-white and arranged in clusters, adapted for wind pollination.

Lasiostoma oblongum plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.
