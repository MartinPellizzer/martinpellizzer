Lithocarpus calophyllus

Lithocarpus calophyllus, commonly known as the broad-leaved stone oak, is a member of the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks and chestnuts.

It is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a glossy surface. Its fruit is a hard, woody nut enclosed in a persistent cupule, which is typical of the genus Lithocarpus.

This species is valued for its durable wood and is often used in construction and furniture making.
