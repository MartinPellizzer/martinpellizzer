Lindera praecox

Lindera praecox, commonly known as early spicebush, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes other aromatic plants like cinnamon and avocado. The plant is characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by orange-red berries that are edible for birds.

Lindera praecox thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and streambanks.
