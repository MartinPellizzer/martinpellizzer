Lychnis terminalis

Lychnis terminalis, commonly known as the red campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the carnation family (Caryophyllaceae) and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

Lychnis terminalis is often considered a weed but can also be grown as an ornamental plant in gardens.
