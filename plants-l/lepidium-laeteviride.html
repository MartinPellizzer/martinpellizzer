Lepidium laeteviride

Lepidium laeteviride, commonly known as the green horseradish, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and western Asia, often found in grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, and its leaves are pinnately lobed with a peppery scent. The root system is fibrous, and the plant is known for its strong, spicy odor.

It is sometimes cultivated as a herb or for its potential medicinal properties.
