Lamium maculatum lusus crenatum

Lamium maculatum lusus crenatum, commonly known as the speckled dead nettle, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish or purple mottling, and it produces clusters of small, two-lipped flowers in shades of pink or white. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks. It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.

Its foliage is also sometimes used in herbal remedies for its mild sedative properties.
