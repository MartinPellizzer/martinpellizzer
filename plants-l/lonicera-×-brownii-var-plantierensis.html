Lonicera × brownii var. plantierensis

Lonicera × brownii var. plantierensis, commonly known as the Chinese honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant produces red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its rapid growth and adaptability contribute to its widespread cultivation in temperate regions.
