Lasiospermum poterioides

Lasiospermum poterioides, commonly known as the mountain mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the high elevations of South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in rocky, alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are alternate, narrow, and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh habitat.

The showy yellow flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
