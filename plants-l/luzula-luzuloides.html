Luzula luzuloides

Luzula luzuloides, commonly known as the slender wood sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Luzula luzuloides is often found in temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland and forest ecosystems.
