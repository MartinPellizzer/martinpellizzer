Leopoldia principis

Leopoldia principis, commonly known as the royal leopoldia, is a rare and endangered orchid species native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that bloom in late spring, featuring a unique combination of colors and intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories where it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. Its small, strap-like leaves are adapted to conserve water, and it produces a single, elongated flower spike with a few blossoms.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, Leopoldia principis is critically endangered, making conservation efforts essential for its survival.
