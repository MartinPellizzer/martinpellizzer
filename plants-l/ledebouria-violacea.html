Ledebouria violacea

Ledebouria violacea, commonly known as the violet star lily, is a bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It produces striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer, adding a splash of color to its natural habitat. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense rosette, helping it store water and nutrients. Its bulbs are adapted to survive in arid conditions, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
