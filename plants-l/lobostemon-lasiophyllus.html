Lobostemon lasiophyllus

Lobostemon lasiophyllus, commonly known as the woolly bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, woolly leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is a perennial herb that produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
