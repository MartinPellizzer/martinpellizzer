Limonium globuliferum var. subglobosum

Limonium globuliferum var. subglobosum, commonly known as the globe limonium, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded growth habit and produces clusters of small, showy blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes. Its flowers bloom in late summer, adding a vibrant touch to gardens during the warmer months.

The species is known for its drought tolerance and ability to withstand salty air, which makes it well-suited for coastal environments.
