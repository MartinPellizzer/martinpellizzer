Lycaste virginalis f. cobanensis

Lycaste virginalis f. cobanensis, commonly known as the Coban lycaste, is a rare and elegant orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure with fleshy leaves. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink, often with a subtle greenish tinge. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its limited natural range and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable and challenging species for orchid enthusiasts.
