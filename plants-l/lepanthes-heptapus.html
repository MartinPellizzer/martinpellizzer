Lepanthes heptapus

Lepanthes heptapus, commonly known as the seven-lobed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in montane forests. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with a unique, intricate structure that aids in pollination by specific insect species.

Lepanthes heptapus is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological balance of its native habitats.
