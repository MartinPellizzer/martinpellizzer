Lepidium crassifolium var. dentatum

Lepidium crassifolium var. dentatum, commonly known as the toothed celtuce, is a variant of the celtuce plant native to China.

It is characterized by its broad, fleshy leaves with serrated edges, which give it its common name. The plant belongs to the Brassicaceae family, also known as the mustard family, and is known for its edible leaves and stems. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its mild, nutty flavor.

The leaves are typically harvested before they become too fibrous, making it a popular vegetable in Asian cuisines.
