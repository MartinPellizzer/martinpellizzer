Laelia russelliana

Laelia russelliana, commonly known as Russell's orchid, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces showy, yellow to orange blooms with a distinctive green throat, often appearing in late winter to early spring. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil for optimal growth.

Laelia russelliana is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
