Liparis harrisii

Liparis harrisii, commonly known as the Harris's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in a raceme, which are typically short-lived but attract various pollinators. Liparis harrisii thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a unique and interesting subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
