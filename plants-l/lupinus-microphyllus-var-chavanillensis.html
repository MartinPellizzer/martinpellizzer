Lupinus microphyllus var. chavanillensis

Lupinus microphyllus var. chavanillensis, commonly known as the Chavanilla lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, such as along coastal bluffs and in chaparral regions. It is a perennial herb that produces seed pods containing several small, hard seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
