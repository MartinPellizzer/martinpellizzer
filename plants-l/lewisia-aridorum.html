Lewisia aridorum

Lewisia aridorum, commonly known as the desert pink, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing form and vibrant pink or red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it store water, adapting it to arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a valuable addition to xeriscapic landscapes.
