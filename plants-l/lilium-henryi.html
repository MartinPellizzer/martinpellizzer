Lilium henryi

Lilium henryi, commonly known as the Chinese lily, is a species of bulbous perennial plant native to China.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer, producing clusters of white or pale pink petals with a yellow throat. Its leaves are grass-like and arranged in a fan shape, and it prefers well-drained soil in a sunny location.

Lilium henryi is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively hardy in temperate climates.
