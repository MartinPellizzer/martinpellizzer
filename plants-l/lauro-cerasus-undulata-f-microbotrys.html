Lauro-cerasus undulata f. microbotrys

Lauro-cerasus undulata f. microbotrys, commonly known as the dwarf cherry laurel, is a compact, evergreen shrub characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant flowers.

It belongs to the Prunus genus and is a natural hybrid between Prunus laurocerasus and Prunus cerasifera. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red or black in color. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its dense foliage and winter interest.

This cultivar is relatively low-maintenance and thrives in a variety of soil types.
