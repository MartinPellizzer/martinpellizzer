Leptophoenix pterophylla

Leptophoenix pterophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf date palm, is a species of palm native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, tall trunk and narrow, feather-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible dates, which are a significant agricultural product in its native region. It thrives in arid and semi-arid climates, requiring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its economic and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in regions with suitable environmental conditions.
