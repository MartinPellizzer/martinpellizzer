Lobelia filiformis f. multipilis

Lobelia filiformis f. multipilis, commonly known as the slender lobelia or the thread-leaf lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial with slender, cylindrical stems and small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wet meadows. Its leaves are narrow and linear, contributing to its delicate appearance.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
