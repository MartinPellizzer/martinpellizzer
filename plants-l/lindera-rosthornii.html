Lindera rosthornii

Lindera rosthornii, commonly known as the Chinese sweetwood, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurel and avocado trees. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, narrow leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. Its fruits are small, red drupes that ripen in the fall, attracting various bird species.

Lindera rosthornii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aromatic foliage and seasonal interest.
