Leucopogon juniperinus

Leucopogon juniperinus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a shrub native to the temperate regions of southern Australia.

It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas. The plant has small, needle-like leaves that are usually covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are small, clustered, and often pink or red in color, blooming in spring.

It is well adapted to dry conditions and is an important component of its native ecosystem.
