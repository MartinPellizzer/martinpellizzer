Linum juniperifolium

Linum juniperifolium, commonly known as the juniper-leaf flax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, round pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Linum juniperifolium typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and along streambanks.

Its leaves are narrow and resemble those of juniper, giving it its common name.
