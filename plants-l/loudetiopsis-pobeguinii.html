Loudetiopsis pobeguinii

Loudetiopsis pobeguinii, commonly known as the coastal dwarf lichen, is a species of lichen that thrives in coastal environments.

It is characterized by its dense, foliose growth form and a distinctive yellowish-green coloration. This lichen is typically found on rock surfaces in humid, maritime climates. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and contributing to nutrient cycling in its habitat.

Its ability to tolerate salt spray makes it an important indicator species for coastal ecosystems.
