Limosella aquatica var. diandra

Limosella aquatica var. diandra, commonly known as the water bladderwort, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically above water. The plant has slender, branching stems and is adapted to aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats. It possesses specialized structures called bladders, which are used to trap small aquatic organisms.

This variety is often found in shallow, freshwater areas and plays a role in aquatic ecosystems.
