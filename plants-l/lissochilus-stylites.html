Lissochilus stylites

Lissochilus stylites, commonly known as the slender-stemmed orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, upright stems and small, pale green to white flowers that bloom during the cooler months. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky areas, where it thrives in well-drained soil and moderate temperatures. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern to conserve moisture.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species, making conservation efforts crucial for its survival.
