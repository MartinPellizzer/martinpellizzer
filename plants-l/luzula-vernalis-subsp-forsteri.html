Luzula vernalis subsp. forsteri

Luzula vernalis subsp.

forsteri, commonly known as the spring sweetflag, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue, and it produces tiny, greenish flowers in early spring.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
