Liparis aureolabella

Liparis aureolabella, commonly known as the golden-labella orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers with a distinctive golden-orange lip. The plant typically thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, acidic soil. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Liparis aureolabella is valued in horticulture for its unique floral structure and adaptability to cultivated conditions.
