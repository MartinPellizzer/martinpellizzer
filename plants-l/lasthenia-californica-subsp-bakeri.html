Lasthenia californica subsp. bakeri

Lasthenia californica subsp.

bakeri, commonly known as Baker's tidy tips, is a low-growing annual plant native to California. It produces small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May.

The plant has hairy, linear leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas.

This subspecies is part of the Asteraceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and role in supporting local pollinators.
