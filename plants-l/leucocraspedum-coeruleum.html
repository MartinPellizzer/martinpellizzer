Leucocraspedum coeruleum

Leucocraspedum coeruleum, commonly known as the blue leucocraspedum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or alpine environments. It is a perennial species that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely commercially available.
