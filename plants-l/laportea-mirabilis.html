Laportea mirabilis

Laportea mirabilis, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Mexican velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Laportea mirabilis is also known for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural fibers.
