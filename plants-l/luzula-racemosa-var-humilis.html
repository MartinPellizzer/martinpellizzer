Luzula racemosa var. humilis

Luzula racemosa var. humilis, commonly known as the dwarf woodrush, is a low-growing perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the plantain family (Plantaginaceae) and is characterized by its dense clusters of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish or white flowers arranged in a spike, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Luzula racemosa var. humilis is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in wet areas.
