Lupinus × hybridus insignis

Lupinus × hybridus insignis, commonly known as the hybrid lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of showy, pea-like flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces elongated pods that contain several seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for native gardens and ecological restoration projects.
