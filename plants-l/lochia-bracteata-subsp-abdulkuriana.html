Lochia bracteata subsp. abdulkuriana

Lochia bracteata subsp.

abdulkuriana, commonly known as the Kharak Desert bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its erect stem and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink.

The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits that contain numerous seeds. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in desert ecosystems.
