Limonium connivens

Limonium connivens, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal areas of Europe and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of small, blue or purple flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in maritime environments. Its flowers are typically visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological importance.

Limonium connivens is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.
