Lepidium scandens

Lepidium scandens, commonly known as wild cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges. The plant has alternate, pinnately divided leaves that are covered in small, scale-like structures, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, white or yellowish flowers are arranged in racemes and bloom in early spring.

The plant is known for its peppery taste and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
