Lippia michoacana

Lippia michoacana, commonly known as Mexican blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. It is valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
