Linum tenuifolium var. ericoides

Linum tenuifolium var. ericoides, commonly known as the dwarf flax or heather flax, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in a compact, shrub-like form. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive and drought-tolerant addition to native plant landscapes.
