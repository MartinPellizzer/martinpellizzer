Laser panjutinii

Laser panjutinii, commonly known as the golden flame orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow and orange flowers, which resemble flames, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in dense, humid forests, often in shaded environments where it can thrive. Its botanical features include long, slender stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating.

Laser panjutinii is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
