Lysimachia amoena

Lysimachia amoena, commonly known as the pretty willowherb, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically features slender, creeping stems that root at the nodes, forming dense mats or clusters. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink to white, often arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and covered with fine hairs.

Lysimachia amoena thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.
