Loudonia cordigera

Loudonia cordigera, commonly known as the heartleaf orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks and branches in its natural habitat. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat destruction, Loudonia cordigera is considered endangered and requires careful preservation efforts.
