Limonia poggei var. latialata

Limonia poggei var. latialata, commonly known as the dwarf lobe lemon, is a small evergreen shrub native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, citrus-scented flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are edible but not commonly cultivated for commercial use. Its leaves are glossy and elliptical, with a leathery texture, and it thrives in well-drained, acidic soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to adapt to tropical and subtropical climates.
