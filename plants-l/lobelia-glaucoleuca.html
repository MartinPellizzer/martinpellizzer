Lobelia glaucoleuca

Lobelia glaucoleuca, commonly known as the blue-white lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often bluish-green in appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
