Lonicera yunnanensis

Lonicera yunnanensis, commonly known as Yunnan honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have serrated edges, while its stems are often covered in fine hairs.

Lonicera yunnanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
