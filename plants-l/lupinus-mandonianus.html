Lupinus mandonianus

Lupinus mandonianus, commonly known as the Mandon's lupine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces elongated pods that contain several seeds.

Lupinus mandonianus is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in open areas such as grasslands and disturbed sites.
