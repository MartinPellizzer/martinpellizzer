Lackeya viridiflora

Lackeya viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered lackeya, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant green flowers, which are typically borne in dense clusters. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in shaded undergrowth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some botanical gardens and is of interest to botanists studying tropical flora.
