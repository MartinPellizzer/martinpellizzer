Lycopodium serratum var. longipetiolatum

Lycopodium serratum var. longipetiolatum, commonly known as the serrated clubmoss, is a perennial evergreen plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems that bear small, scale-like leaves arranged in whorls. The plant produces spore-bearing structures called strobila, which are clustered at the tips of the branches. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or rocky crevices.

Its morphology reflects its ancient lineage, showcasing features similar to early vascular plants.
