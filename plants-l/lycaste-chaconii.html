Lycaste chaconii

Lycaste chaconii, commonly known as the Chacon's lycaste, is a species of orchid native to Mexico.

It belongs to the subfamily Epidendroideae and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often with a yellow lip. Its pseudobulbs are elongated and cylindrical, supporting the plant's structure.

Lycaste chaconii thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
