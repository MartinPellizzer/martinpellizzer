Lepironia articulata var. capitata

Lepironia articulata var. capitata, commonly known as the water lily of the genus Lepironia, is a tropical aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white or pink flowers that bloom on long, slender stalks. The plant has broad, floating leaves that provide shade and oxygen to the water. Its rhizomes grow beneath the water surface, anchoring the plant and supporting its growth.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
