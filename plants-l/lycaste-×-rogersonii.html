Lycaste × rogersonii

Lycaste × rogersonii, commonly known as the hybrid lycaste orchid, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Lycaste meleagris and Lycaste sanderi.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America. This plant is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink, white, and purple. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in greenhouses and as a houseplant.
