Lycopodium schlechteri

Lycopodium schlechteri, commonly known as the Schlechter's clubmoss, is a primitive vascular plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is a low-growing, evergreen plant that typically forms dense mats on the forest floor, often in moist, shaded environments. The plant has small, scale-like leaves arranged in whorls around the stem, which is segmented and resembles a club. It produces spore-bearing structures called strobila, which are clustered at the tips of the branches.

Lycopodium schlechteri plays an important ecological role in nutrient cycling and habitat provision for various forest floor organisms.
