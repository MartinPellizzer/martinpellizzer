Lathyrus hirsutus var. ecirrhosus

Lathyrus hirsutus var. ecirrhosus, commonly known as the hairy vetch, is a perennial legume native to Europe and western Asia.

It features trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant is characterized by its robust growth habit and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in agricultural settings. It thrives in temperate climates and is often used as a cover crop or forage.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
