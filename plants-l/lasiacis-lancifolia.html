Lasiacis lancifolia

Lasiacis lancifolia, commonly known as the sharpleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, thriving in moist to aquatic environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often sharply pointed, giving it its common name. Its inflorescence consists of dense clusters of small, three-lobed flowers that are usually greenish or brownish in color.

Lasiacis lancifolia plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
