Lissochilus kassnerianus

Lissochilus kassnerianus, commonly known as the Kassner's slipper orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, elongated floral structure that resembles a slipper. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, humid conditions with well-drained soil. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Due to its habitat specificity and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant plant.
