Liabum subglandulare

Liabum subglandulare, commonly known as the glandular liabum, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves with glandular hairs. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically white or pale purple in color. It is native to parts of Africa and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
