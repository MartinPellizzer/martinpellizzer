Lissochilus wakefieldii

Lissochilus wakefieldii, commonly known as the Wakefield's slipper orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped pouch that serves as a pollination trap. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky areas, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellow, with intricate patterns that aid in attracting specific pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and delicate species within its ecosystem.
