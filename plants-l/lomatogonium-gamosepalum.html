Lomatogonium gamosepalum

Lomatogonium gamosepalum, commonly known as the sticky everlasting pea, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical pea-like flowers and compound leaves. The plant is known for its small, yellow, pea-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet having a hairy surface that gives the plant its common name.
