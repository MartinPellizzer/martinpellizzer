Lamium lassithiense

Lamium lassithiense, commonly known as the Lassithi deadnettle, is a perennial herb native to the island of Crete in Greece.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal and culinary uses. The plant features square stems and opposite leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.
