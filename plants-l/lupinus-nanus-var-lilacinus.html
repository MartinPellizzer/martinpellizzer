Lupinus nanus var. lilacinus

Lupinus nanus var. lilacinus, commonly known as the dwarf lupine, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features small, showy flowers that range in color from pale blue to lilac, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has slender, hairy stems and pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive and hardy addition to native plant collections.
