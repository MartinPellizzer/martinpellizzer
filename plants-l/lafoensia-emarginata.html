Lafoensia emarginata

Lafoensia emarginata, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pink trumpet tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking pink to red tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and bats. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite and oval in shape. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, round seeds.

Lafoensia emarginata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also valued for its hard, durable wood.
