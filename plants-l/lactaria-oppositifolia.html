Lactaria oppositifolia

Lactaria oppositifolia, commonly known as the yellow milk cap, is a species of fungus that belongs to the family Tricholomataceae.

It is characterized by its yellow to orange cap, which often has a viscid or sticky surface. The stem is typically slender and may have a slight sheen, while the gills are initially yellow and darken with age. This edible mushroom is found in forested areas, particularly in regions with coniferous trees.

It is known for its mild flavor and is sometimes used in foraging for culinary purposes.
