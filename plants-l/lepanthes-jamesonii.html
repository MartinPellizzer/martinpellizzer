Lepanthes jamesonii

Lepanthes jamesonii, commonly known as the Jameson's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central and South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments within tropical forests. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green. Its flowers are often clustered in a spike and bloom during the rainy season.

Lepanthes jamesonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
