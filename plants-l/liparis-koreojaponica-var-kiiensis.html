Liparis koreojaponica var. kiiensis

Liparis koreojaponica var. kiiensis, commonly known as the Kii mountain orchid, is a rare orchid species native to Japan.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
