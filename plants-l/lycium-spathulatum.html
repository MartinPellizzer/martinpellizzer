Lycium spathulatum

Lycium spathulatum, commonly known as the broad-leaf boxthorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but have a bitter taste. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Lycium spathulatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
