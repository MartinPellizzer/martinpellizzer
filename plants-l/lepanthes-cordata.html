Lepanthes cordata

Lepanthes cordata, commonly known as the heart-shaped orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped petals and leaves, which are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often in shades of white, pink, and purple. Lepanthes cordata thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks in tropical rainforests.

Its unique morphology and ornamental value make it a popular choice for botanical gardens and orchid enthusiasts.
