Lonicera tatarica var. albiflora

Lonicera tatarica var. albiflora, commonly known as the whiteflower honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, arching stems that often grow along the ground or climb support structures. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible for birds. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
