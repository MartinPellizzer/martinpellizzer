Lepanthes zelenkoi

Lepanthes zelenkoi, commonly known as the Zelenko's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually small, with intricate patterns and vibrant colors that attract specific pollinators.

Lepanthes zelenkoi is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological balance of its native habitat.
