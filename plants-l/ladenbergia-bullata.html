Ladenbergia bullata

Ladenbergia bullata, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Leguminosae (Fabaceae) family and is characterized by its distinctive, flame-like red flowers that appear in late winter or early spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces long, slender pods that contain several seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can grow up to 15 meters tall in suitable conditions.
