Lactuca dentata subvar. angustifolia

Lactuca dentata subvar. angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved lettuce, is a wild lettuce species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are often serrated along the edges. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can reach heights of up to 1 meter. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in a composite head, and it produces small, spiky seeds.

This variety is often used in traditional medicine for its mild sedative properties.
