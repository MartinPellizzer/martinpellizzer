Linum narbonense var. latifolium

Linum narbonense var. latifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved flax, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of blue or purple flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oval seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in textile production.
