Lupinus castillonanus

Lupinus castillonanus, commonly known as the Castillon lupin, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of blue or violet flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.
