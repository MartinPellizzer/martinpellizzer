Lactuca viminea var. chondrilliflora

Lactuca viminea var. chondrilliflora, commonly known as the slender lettuce or chondrilliflora lettuce, is a variant of the common lettuce species.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically less bitter than those of other lettuce varieties. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in temperate climates. It is often cultivated for its edible leaves, which can be used in salads or cooked dishes.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens due to its delicate, fern-like foliage.
