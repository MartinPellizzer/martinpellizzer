Loricaria lycopodinea

Loricaria lycopodinea, commonly known as the slender lycopod, is a species of clubmoss that belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping stems that produce small, scale-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures consist of sporangia that are borne on strobila, which are cone-like formations.

Loricaria lycopodinea plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
