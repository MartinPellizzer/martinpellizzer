Leucothoe spicata

Leucothoe spicata, commonly known as the fragrant evergreen holly, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features lance-shaped leaves that turn reddish in the fall, adding visual interest to the landscape. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in spring, followed by bright red berries that persist into winter. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Leucothoe spicata is often used in woodland gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
