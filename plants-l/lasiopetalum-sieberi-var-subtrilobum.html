Lasiopetalum sieberi var. subtrilobum

Lasiopetalum sieberi var. subtrilobum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mulla mulla, is a small evergreen shrub native to eastern Australia.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pink or white flowers that bloom in spring and summer. It is often found in sandy or rocky soils in open woodlands and heathlands.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for garden planting in suitable climates.
