Lycopodium clavatum var. paucidivisum

Lycopodium clavatum var. paucidivisum, commonly known as the slender clubmoss, is a variant of the clubmoss family (Lycopodiaceae).

It is a small, evergreen fern-like plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky crevices. The plant features compact, spore-bearing cones called strobila, which are clustered at the tips of its branching stems. Its leaves are narrow and linear, arranged in whorls around the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This variety is notable for its reduced number of divisions in its sporophyte structure, which is a key botanical feature distinguishing it from other lycopodium species.
