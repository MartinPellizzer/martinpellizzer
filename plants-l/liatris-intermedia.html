Liatris intermedia

Liatris intermedia, commonly known as the tall blazing star, is a native North American perennial herb belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, supporting clusters of showy, spires of purple to pink flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Liatris intermedia is well adapted to full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
