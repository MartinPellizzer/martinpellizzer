Luzama patula

Luzama patula, commonly known as the Mexican bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, often found in dry, rocky soils. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture, while its stems are hairy and often branching.

Luzama patula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
