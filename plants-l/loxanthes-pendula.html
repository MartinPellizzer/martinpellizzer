Loxanthes pendula

Loxanthes pendula, commonly known as the weeping lantana, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its drooping clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has aromatic leaves and produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Loxanthes pendula is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
