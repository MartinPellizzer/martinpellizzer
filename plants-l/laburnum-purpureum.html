Laburnum purpureum

Laburnum purpureum, commonly known as the golden chain tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its long, drooping clusters of bright yellow flowers that appear in early spring. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. Its bark is smooth and reddish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

Laburnum purpureum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also known for its toxic seeds.
