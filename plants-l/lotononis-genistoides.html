Lotononis genistoides

Lotononis genistoides, commonly known as the African broom, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments.

The botanical features of Lotononis genistoides make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural studies.
