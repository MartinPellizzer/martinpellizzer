Lastrea scariosa

Lastrea scariosa, commonly known as the rock pear, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers, which are often followed by pear-shaped fruits. Its leaves are typically reduced to small, scale-like structures, which help minimize water loss.

Lastrea scariosa is often found growing on rocky outcrops and is well adapted to full sun and minimal rainfall.
