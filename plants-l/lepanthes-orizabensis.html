Lepanthes orizabensis

Lepanthes orizabensis, commonly known as the Orizaba orchid, is a species of orchid native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its epiphytic growth habit, often found growing on trees in tropical rainforests. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a rosette pattern, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
