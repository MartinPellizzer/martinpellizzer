Lepanthes reburrus

Lepanthes reburrus, commonly known as the red-tipped orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, hairy, greenish-brown pseudobulbs and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically white or pale pink with a distinctive red or orange tip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Lepanthes reburrus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
