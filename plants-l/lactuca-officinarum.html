Lactuca officinarum

Lactuca officinarum, commonly known as common lettuce, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are typically edible and used in various culinary applications. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. Its leaves contain a milky sap, which is a distinguishing feature of the genus Lactuca.

This plant is native to Europe and western Asia but has been widely cultivated around the world for its nutritional and medicinal properties.
