Lobelia acrochila

Lobelia acrochila, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Lobelia acrochila is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
