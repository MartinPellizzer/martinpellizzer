Lithospermum cyanochroum

Lithospermum cyanochroum, commonly known as blue root, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with blue or purple. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, blooming from late spring to early summer. Its root system is notable for containing compounds that have been studied for their potential medicinal properties.

Lithospermum cyanochroum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
