Logania angustifolia

Logania angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf logania, is a flowering plant belonging to the Loganiaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its slender, often climbing stems and narrow, elongated leaves. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, with a distinctive tubular shape and vibrant colors.

It is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value in warm climates.
