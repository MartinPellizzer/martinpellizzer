Lithophila muscoides subsp. macrantha

Lithophila muscoides subsp.

macrantha, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It typically grows on rocky outcrops and is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and green, which bloom during the cooler months. Its leaves are succulent and adapted to store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. Lithophila muscoides subsp.

macrantha is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in rocky gardens.
