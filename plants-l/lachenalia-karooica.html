Lachenalia karooica

Lachenalia karooica, commonly known as the Karoo lachenalia, is a perennial bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, tuberous roots and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, tubular flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its striking appearance and unique morphology, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
