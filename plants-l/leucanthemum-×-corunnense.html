Leucanthemum × corunnense

Leucanthemum × corunnense, commonly known as Shasta daisy, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, white ray florets surrounding a central yellow disk, giving it a daisy-like appearance. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and produces flowers throughout the summer months. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological gardens.
