Lepanthes ankistra

Lepanthes ankistra, commonly known as the ankistra orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces striking, fragrant flowers with a distinctive coloration. Its floral structure includes a spurred lip, which is characteristic of many orchid species.

Lepanthes ankistra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique beauty.
