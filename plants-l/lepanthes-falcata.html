Lepanthes falcata

Lepanthes falcata, commonly known as the sickle orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, elongated, sickle-shaped petals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are small and often white or pale green, with a distinctive lip that is broader than the other petals.

Lepanthes falcata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
