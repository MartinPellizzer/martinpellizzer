Lepidagathis heudelotiana

Lepidagathis heudelotiana, commonly known as the velvetleaf or African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its aesthetic appeal.

The flowers are primarily pollinated by insects, and the plant is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and vegetative reproduction.
