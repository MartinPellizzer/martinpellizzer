Luma poeppigiana

Luma poeppigiana, commonly known as the golden trumpet tree, is a tropical plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, featuring a dense, rounded canopy. The plant is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and its striking yellow flowers, which bloom in clusters during the rainy season. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.

Luma poeppigiana is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.
