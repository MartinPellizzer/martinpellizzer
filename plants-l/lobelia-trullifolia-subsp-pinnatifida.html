Lobelia trullifolia subsp. pinnatifida

Lobelia trullifolia subsp.

pinnatifida, commonly known as the narrow-leaf lobelia, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features opposite, linear to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy and have a distinct, narrow shape. Its flowers are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences, with tubular, violet to blue petals that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
