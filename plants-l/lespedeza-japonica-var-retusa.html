Lespedeza japonica var. retusa

Lespedeza japonica var. retusa, commonly known as Japanese lespeadeza, is a perennial legume native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, pink to red flowers in raceme clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Lespedeza japonica var. retusa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
