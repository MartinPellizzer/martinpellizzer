Lophospermum grandiflorum var. coccineum

Lophospermum grandiflorum var. coccineum, commonly known as the red-flowered glorybower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces vibrant red, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, warm climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in greenhouses.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
