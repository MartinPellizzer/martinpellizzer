Luina nardosmia

Luina nardosmia, commonly known as the sweet flag, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color, and it produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a loose panicle. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, slightly sweet scent, which is released when the leaves are crushed. Luina nardosmia is known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically in traditional medicine.

It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found near water bodies.
