Lucuma gomphiifolia

Lucuma gomphiifolia, commonly known as the golden fruit tree, is a member of the Lauraceae family native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and distinctive, elongated fruits that mature to a golden-yellow color. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are typically broad and lance-shaped, with a glossy surface that contributes to its ornamental appeal.

The fruit, while not widely consumed commercially, holds cultural significance in its native regions.
