Linum lignosum

Linum lignosum, commonly known as the silky oaks, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its woody structure and produces showy, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and forest areas.

Linum lignosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
