Laserpitium latifolium f. macrophyllum

Laserpitium latifolium f. macrophyllum, commonly known as the broadleaf lousewort, is a variant of the lousewort species native to North America.

This plant is characterized by its large, broad leaves that distinguish it from other forms of Laserpitium. It typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as meadows and stream banks. The flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in shades of pink or purple.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Orobanchaceae, which includes parasitic and hemiparasitic plants.
