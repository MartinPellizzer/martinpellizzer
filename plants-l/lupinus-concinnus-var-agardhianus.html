Lupinus concinnus var. agardhianus

Lupinus concinnus var. agardhianus, commonly known as the Sierra lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and clusters of showy blue or purple flowers. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces elongated pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and along streambanks.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for various wildlife species.
