Lasianthus bracteatus

Lasianthus bracteatus, commonly known as the velvetleaf or bracteate lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in some regions for horticultural purposes.
