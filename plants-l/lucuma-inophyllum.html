Lucuma inophyllum

Lucuma inophyllum, commonly known as the golden fruit tree, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a member of the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen shrub or small tree form. The plant produces yellow-orange berries that are rich in nutrients and have a sweet, nutty flavor. Its leaves are broad and glossy, and it thrives in cool, high-altitude climates.

The fruit is often used in traditional Andean cuisine and has gained popularity in modern health food markets.
