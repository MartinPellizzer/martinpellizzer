Lycium peruvianum

Lycium peruvianum, commonly known as the Peruvian wolfberry, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its spiny stems and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are ovate and slightly hairy, with a strong, aromatic scent.

Lycium peruvianum is adapted to high-altitude environments and thrives in well-drained soils.
