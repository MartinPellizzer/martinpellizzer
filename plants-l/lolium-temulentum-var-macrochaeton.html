Lolium temulentum var. macrochaeton

Lolium temulentum var. macrochaeton, commonly known as the tall cheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and spike-like inflorescences that bear small, two-lobed spikelets. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green color that helps distinguish it from other grasses.

It produces wind-pollinated flowers and is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize open areas.
