Lepanthes recurva

Lepanthes recurva, commonly known as the recurved orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. The plant has a compact growth habit with a short, slender stem and is often found growing in shaded, humid environments. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Lepanthes recurva is valued in horticulture for its beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
