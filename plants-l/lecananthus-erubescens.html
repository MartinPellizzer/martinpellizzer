Lecananthus erubescens

Lecananthus erubescens, commonly known as the red-flowered lecananthus, is a member of the Malvaceae family, characterized by its showy, velvety flowers.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, particularly in countries like Kenya and Tanzania. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub with hairy stems and broad, ovate leaves. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
