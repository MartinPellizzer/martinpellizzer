Limonium graecum

Limonium graecum, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking spikes of blue or purple flowers, which bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and saline soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Limonium graecum is widely cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
