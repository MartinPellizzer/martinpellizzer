Lightfootia elata

Lightfootia elata, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and dark, aromatic heartwood. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a glossy surface. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood, which has been historically used in furniture and musical instruments.
