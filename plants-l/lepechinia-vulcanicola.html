Lepechinia vulcanicola

Lepechinia vulcanicola, commonly known as the volcanic lepechinia, is a flowering plant native to the volcanic regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. This species is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in nutrient-poor, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or volcanic landscapes.

The plant's structure includes hairy stems and leaves, which help it retain moisture in its harsh environment.
