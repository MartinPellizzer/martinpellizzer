Lysimachia quadrifolia f. variegata

Lysimachia quadrifolia f. variegata, commonly known as the variegated wood sorrel, is a flowering plant characterized by its distinctive foliage.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a striking variegation of white and green patterns. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation in gardens.
