Lesquerella lasiocarpa var. heterochroma

Lesquerella lasiocarpa var. heterochroma, commonly known as the heterochromatic lesquerella, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, waxy, and glandular leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and desert scrub habitats.

This species is of interest for its potential in biochemistry and horticulture due to its unique chemical composition.
