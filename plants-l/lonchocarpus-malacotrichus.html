Lonchocarpus malacotrichus

Lonchocarpus malacotrichus, commonly known as the Malacotrichus or the Red Root.

It is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas, particularly Central and South America. The plant is characterized by its striking red roots and yellow flowers, which bloom in clusters. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or pea family, and is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability.

The plant is sometimes used in traditional medicine, though its toxic properties require caution in handling.
