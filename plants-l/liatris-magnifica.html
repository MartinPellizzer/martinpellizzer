Liatris magnifica

Liatris magnifica, commonly known as the showy blazing star, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescence composed of numerous tubular purple to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies, meadows, and woodland edges. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Liatris magnifica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
