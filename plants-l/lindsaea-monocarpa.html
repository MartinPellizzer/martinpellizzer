Lindsaea monocarpa

Lindsaea monocarpa, commonly known as the slender lindsaea, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, slender rhizomes and small, ovate to lanceolate leaves. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its fronds are usually clustered at the base, with a creeping growth habit.

Lindsaea monocarpa is notable for its unique reproductive structures, including sporangia that are often clustered in sori on the underside of the leaves.
