Lathyrus gracillimus

Lathyrus gracillimus, commonly known as slender vetch, is a flowering plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces trifoliate leaves and pods that contain small, elliptical seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

Lathyrus gracillimus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
