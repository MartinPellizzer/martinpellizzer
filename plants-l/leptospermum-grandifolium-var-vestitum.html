Leptospermum grandifolium var. vestitum

Leptospermum grandifolium var. vestitum, commonly known as the silver-leaved tea tree, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage that provides a striking visual contrast. The plant produces small, white, five-petaled flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is a popular choice for landscaping and conservation efforts.
