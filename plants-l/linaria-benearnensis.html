Linaria benearnensis

Linaria benearnensis, commonly known as the Benearn's toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant characterized by its slender stems and lance-shaped leaves. The flowers are typically yellow or orange, with four petals and a distinctive spur at the base. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
