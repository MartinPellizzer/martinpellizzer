Liparis bibullata

Liparis bibullata, commonly known as the spurred orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers with distinctive spurred petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil.

Liparis bibullata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with proper humidity and drainage.
