Luzula barbata

Luzula barbata, commonly known as the beard sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces spike-like inflorescences that bear small, three-lobed flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats.

Luzula barbata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.
