Lonchocarpus venustus

Lonchocarpus venustus, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a species of legume native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are typically divided into five to seven leaflets. The plant produces striking yellow-orange flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by distinctive pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Its roots contain compounds that have been studied for their potential medicinal properties.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
