Lindernia antipoda subsp. verbenifolia

Lindernia antipoda subsp.

verbenifolia, commonly known as New Zealand verbenaceae, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand. It belongs to the family Verbenaceae and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
