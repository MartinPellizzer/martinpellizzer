Liatris gracilis var. gholsonii

Liatris gracilis var. gholsonii, commonly known as the Gholson's narrowleaf blazingstar, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and showy, spiky flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and woodland edges. Its flowers are usually pink to purple in color and bloom during the summer months.

It plays an important role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
