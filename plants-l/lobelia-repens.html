Lobelia repens

Lobelia repens, commonly known as creeping lobelia, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple, arranged in clusters along its prostrate stems. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces seeds in elongated, slender capsules. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wet meadows.

Lobelia repens is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
