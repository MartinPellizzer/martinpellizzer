Lespedeza fasciculiflora

Lespedeza fasciculiflora, commonly known as Chinese clover, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its small, pink or white flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees.

Lespedeza fasciculiflora is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and improve nutrient content.
