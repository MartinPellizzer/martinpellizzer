Laggera crassifolia

Laggera crassifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf laggera, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with rough, hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the summer months. Its root system is fibrous, and it is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Laggera crassifolia thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
