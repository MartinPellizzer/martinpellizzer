Lessingianthus lanatus

Lessingianthus lanatus, commonly known as the woolly sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with a distinctive, woolly appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance contribute to its appeal in both wild and cultivated settings.
