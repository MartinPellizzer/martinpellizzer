Limniris chrysographes

Limniris chrysographes, commonly known as the golden leaf orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, golden-yellow leaves that are often marked with intricate patterns. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom during the rainy season. It thrives in humid, shady environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
