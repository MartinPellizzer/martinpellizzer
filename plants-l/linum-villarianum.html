Linum villarianum

Linum villarianum, commonly known as the common flax, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is often found in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed soils due to its adaptability.

Linum villarianum is also cultivated for its fibrous seeds, which have historical uses in textile production and oil extraction.
