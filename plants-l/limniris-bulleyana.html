Limniris bulleyana

Limniris bulleyana, commonly known as the Bulleyan gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by edible, red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for ornamental gardens.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional Chinese medicine.
