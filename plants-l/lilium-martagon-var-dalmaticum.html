Lilium martagon var. dalmaticum

Lilium martagon var. dalmaticum, commonly known as the Dalmatian Turk's Cap lily, is a striking perennial bulbous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It features showy, recurved, pink to lilac-colored flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces a dense cluster of bell-shaped blooms on a sturdy, upright stem, with lance-shaped, dark green leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in horticulture.
