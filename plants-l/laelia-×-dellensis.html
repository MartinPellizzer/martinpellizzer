Laelia × dellensis

Laelia × dellensis, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a naturally occurring hybrid between Laelia purpurea and Laelia anceps.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes, particularly in Ecuador and Peru. This plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white. The botanical features include pseudobulbs, which store water and nutrients, and long, strap-like leaves.

It thrives in cool, bright conditions with good air circulation, making it a popular choice for orchid enthusiasts.
