Lilium speciosum var. clivorum

Lilium speciosum var. clivorum, commonly known as the Alpine Lily, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, bearing large, showy flowers with six to eight petals. The flowers typically bloom in late summer, showcasing a range of colors from white to deep pink, depending on the specific cultivar. This variety is well adapted to cold, high-altitude environments, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for alpine gardens and rockeries.
