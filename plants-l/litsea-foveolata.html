Litsea foveolata

Litsea foveolata, commonly known as the fragrant mountain laurel, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurel, avocado, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by spherical, berry-like fruits.

Litsea foveolata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its fragrance and visual interest.
