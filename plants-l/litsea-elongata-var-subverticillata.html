Litsea elongata var. subverticillata

Litsea elongata var. subverticillata, commonly known as the clustered leafed litsea, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves that are arranged in whorls of three to five, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in terminal panicles. Its fruits are small, woody drupes that contain a single seed.

This variety is native to Southeast Asia and is often found in moist, shaded forest understories.
