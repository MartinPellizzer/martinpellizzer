Ligustrum candolleanum

Ligustrum candolleanum, commonly known as the Candolleanum privet, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter in color underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by black, spherical berries that are edible for birds. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its dense foliage and adaptability to various soil types.

Ligustrum candolleanum is also valued for its use in hedges and as a decorative element in gardens.
