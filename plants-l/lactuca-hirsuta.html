Lactuca hirsuta

Lactuca hirsuta, commonly known as hairy lettuce, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family, Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia, and is characterized by its hairy, lobed leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and along stream banks. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant also produces a milky sap, which is a common feature among the genus Lactuca.
