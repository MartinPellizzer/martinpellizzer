Lobivia einsteinii var. atrospinosa

Lobivia einsteinii var. atrospinosa, commonly known as the Black Spine Lobivia, is a succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It features a short, stocky stem with prominent, spiny lobes that give it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the growing season, typically in spring or early summer. It requires well-draining soil and minimal water to thrive, making it suitable for rock gardens or cactus collections.

Due to its unique morphology and hardiness, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
