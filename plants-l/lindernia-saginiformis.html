Lindernia saginiformis

Lindernia saginiformis, commonly known as the silverleaf lindernia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery coating, giving it its common name. Its showy, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom in the summer months.

Lindernia saginiformis is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
