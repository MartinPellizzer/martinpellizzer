Licuala waraguh

Licuala waraguh, commonly known as the "Copperleaf" or "Golden Lily," is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family, which includes palms, and is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach up to two meters in diameter. The plant produces striking yellow or orange flowers that are clustered in branched inflorescences, often emerging from the leaf axils. The fruit, which is a small, fleshy drupe, contains a single seed and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
