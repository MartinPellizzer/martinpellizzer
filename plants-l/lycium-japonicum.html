Lycium japonicum

Lycium japonicum, commonly known as Japanese boxthorn, is a shrub native to East Asia.

It features small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer, followed by red berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. The plant has spiny stems and is often found in rocky or dry environments. It is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its botanical characteristics make it adaptable to various soil types and climates.
