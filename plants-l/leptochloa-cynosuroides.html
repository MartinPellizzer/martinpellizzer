Leptochloa cynosuroides

Leptochloa cynosuroides, commonly known as the narrowleaf goosefoot, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded habitats, such as marshes and riverbanks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive blue-green coloration. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often clustered in dense, cylindrical arrangements.

Leptochloa cynosuroides is characterized by its fibrous root system and its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.
