Lantana prostrata

Lantana prostrata, commonly known as creeping lantana, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a low-growing shrub with opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it a common ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes.
