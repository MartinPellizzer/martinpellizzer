Liabum annuum

Liabum annuum, commonly known as the common sunflower, is an annual herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, straight stem and large, showy yellow flowers with a distinctive central disk. The plant produces numerous small, oval-shaped seeds in the center of the flower head. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered in a fine layer of hairs.

Liabum annuum is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and for the production of sunflower oil.
