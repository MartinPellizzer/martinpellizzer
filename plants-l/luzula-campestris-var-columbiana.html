Luzula campestris var. columbiana

Luzula campestris var. columbiana, commonly known as the Columbia sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, wetlands, and stream banks. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
