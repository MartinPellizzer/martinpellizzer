Lespedeza violacea var. sessiliflora

Lespedeza violacea var. sessiliflora, commonly known as the violetstem lespedeza, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and its clusters of small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for erosion control in dry, sunny habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species in native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
