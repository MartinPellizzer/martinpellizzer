Lactuca hirsuta var. sanguinea

Lactuca hirsuta var. sanguinea, commonly known as blood lettuce, is a variant of the common lettuce species characterized by its distinctive red pigmentation.

This plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features rosettes of hairy, lobed leaves that are often tinged with red or purple hues. The flowers are small and yellow, arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
