Lactuca cichoriifolia

Lactuca cichoriifolia, commonly known as Italian chicory, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its blue or purple flowers and lobed leaves. The plant has a rosette of basal leaves that are often curly and resemble those of endive. It is cultivated for its edible leaves and flowers, which are used in salads and as a garnish.

The plant also contains lactucine, a bitter compound that contributes to its distinctive flavor.
