Linum

Linum, commonly known as flax, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Linaceae.

It is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and showy blue or purple flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. Flax is particularly noted for its fibrous stalks, which have been used historically for textile production.

Additionally, the seeds are a source of oil and are used in food and medicine.
