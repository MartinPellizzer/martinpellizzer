Laelia × kautskyi

Laelia × kautskyi, commonly known as the Kautsky hybrid, is a hybrid orchid resulting from the cross between Laelia purpurea and Laelia speciosa.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of Central America, particularly in Mexico and Guatemala. The plant typically grows as a sympodial orchid with pseudobulbs and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. Its leaves are broad, fleshy, and arranged in two rows, while its roots are thick and fibrous.

This hybrid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms and resilience in various growing conditions.
