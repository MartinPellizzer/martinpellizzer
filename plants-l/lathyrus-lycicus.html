Lathyrus lycicus

Lathyrus lycicus, commonly known as the Cyprus vetch, is a perennial leguminous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas and sandy habitats.

Lathyrus lycicus is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
