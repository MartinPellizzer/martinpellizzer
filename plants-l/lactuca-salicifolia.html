Lactuca salicifolia

Lactuca salicifolia, commonly known as willowleaf lettuce, is a species of lettuce native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, willow-like leaves that are typically pale green with a slight bluish tinge. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a loose cluster, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are often used in salads and can be eaten raw or cooked.

Lactuca salicifolia is a cool-season crop that prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial shade.
