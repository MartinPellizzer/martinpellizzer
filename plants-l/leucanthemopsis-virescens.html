Leucanthemopsis virescens

Leucanthemopsis virescens, commonly known as the greenleaf oxeye, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a rosette of basal, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are typically glaucous and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white to pale yellow ray florets, blooming from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
