Libanotis patriniana

Libanotis patriniana, commonly known as the Cyprus bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Cyprus.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30-60 cm. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
