Lamium glandulosidens

Lamium glandulosidens, commonly known as the sticky woundwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in glandular hairs, giving the plant its common name. The flowers are typically pink to purple, arranged in whorled clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
