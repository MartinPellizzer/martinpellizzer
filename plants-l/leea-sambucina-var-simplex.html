Leea sambucina var. simplex

Leea sambucina var. simplex, commonly known as the common leea, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the family Leeaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is occasionally cultivated for its attractive foliage and floral display.
