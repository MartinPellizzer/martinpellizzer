Leea gracilis

Leea gracilis, commonly known as the slender leea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the family Leeaceae and is characterized by its climbing or trailing growth habit. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical or ovate in shape. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers are typically clustered in umbels, and it produces small, fleshy berries that contain the seeds.

Leea gracilis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
