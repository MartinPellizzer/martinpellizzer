Linaria origanifolia var. grandiflora

Linaria origanifolia var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered sand rocket, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that resemble those of oregano. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
