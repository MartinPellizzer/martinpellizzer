Lobelia viridiflora

Lobelia viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate foliage and unique floral display.
