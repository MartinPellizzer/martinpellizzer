Lepanthes micronyx

Lepanthes micronyx, commonly known as the miniature orchid, is a small epiphytic orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant has a compact growth habit with short, fleshy roots and narrow, strap-like leaves. Its flowers are usually pale green to white with a distinctive red or purple lip, making it a striking addition to botanical collections.

Lepanthes micronyx requires high humidity and indirect light to thrive, making it a popular choice for greenhouse cultivation.
