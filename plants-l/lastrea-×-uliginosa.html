Lastrea × uliginosa

Lastrea × uliginosa, commonly known as the marsh hibiscus, is a hybrid plant native to wetland areas in the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in swamps, marshes, and along riverbanks. The plant features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming from late summer to early fall. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture, while its stems are somewhat succulent.

Lastrea × uliginosa plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
