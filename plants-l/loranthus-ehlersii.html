Loranthus ehlersii

Loranthus ehlersii, commonly known as the Ehlers' mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using the light it captures. The plant has a distinctive appearance with small, oval-shaped leaves and small, white or yellowish flowers. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that contains seeds covered in a sticky substance to aid in attachment to host plants.

This species plays an important ecological role in its habitat by supporting various insect and bird populations.
