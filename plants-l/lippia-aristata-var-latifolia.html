Lippia aristata var. latifolia

Lippia aristata var. latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf lippia, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping and drought-tolerant gardens. It is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
