Limonium parosicum

Limonium parosicum, commonly known as the Cyprus sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing form and its clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils. It is often found along coastal areas and is adapted to withstand salty and windy conditions.

Limonium parosicum is a popular ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
