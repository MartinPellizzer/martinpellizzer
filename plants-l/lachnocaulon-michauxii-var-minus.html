Lachnocaulon michauxii var. minus

Lachnocaulon michauxii var. minus, commonly known as the lesser michaux's lichen, is a foliose lichen that exhibits a distinctive botanical structure.

It forms a thin, leaf-like thallus with a pale green to yellowish-green coloration. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found on tree bark or soil in temperate forests. Its morphology includes a well-developed medulla and a cortex that provides structural support.

This lichen is notable for its slow growth and is often used in ecological studies to monitor air quality and environmental changes.
