Lepidium iberis

Lepidium iberis, commonly known as Spanish pepper or Iberian cress, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and has hairy, branched stems. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately divided, and covered in fine hairs.

Lepidium iberis is often found in disturbed soils and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
