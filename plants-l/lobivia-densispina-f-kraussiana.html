Lobivia densispina f. kraussiana

Lobivia densispina f. kraussiana, commonly known as the Kraussiana Lobivia, is a succulent plant native to South America.

It features a compact, branching structure with thick, fleshy stems that store water, typical of desert plants. The leaves are usually small, spiny, and arranged in a rosette pattern, providing protection against herbivores. It produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of yellow or orange, often in late spring or early summer.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soil with minimal water.
