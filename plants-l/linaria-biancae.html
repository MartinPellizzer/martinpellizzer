Linaria biancae

Linaria biancae, commonly known as the white toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

Linaria biancae is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of care.
