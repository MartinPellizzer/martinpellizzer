Lupinaster depauperatus

Lupinaster depauperatus, commonly known as the poor lupin, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its small, delicate leaves and clusters of pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. Native to certain regions of southern Africa, this plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and full sun exposure. It is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil and supports other plant life.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
