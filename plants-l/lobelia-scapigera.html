Lobelia scapigera

Lobelia scapigera, commonly known as the slender bluebell, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. The plant features slender, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects.

Lobelia scapigera is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
