Leavenworthia alabamica var. brachystyla

Leavenworthia alabamica var. brachystyla, commonly known as the short-styled bladderpod, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and distinctive, inflated pods that resemble bladders. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along streambanks and in wetlands. It blooms with yellow flowers in the spring, and its seeds are dispersed by wind.

This variety is part of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants.
