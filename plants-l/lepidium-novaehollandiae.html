Lepidium novaehollandiae

Lepidium novaehollandiae, commonly known as the New Holland cress, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress species. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, and it features small, white or pale yellow flowers. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately divided, and often covered in a fine layer of hairs.

The fruit is a capsule that contains numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
