Lolium temulentum var. subbivalve

Lolium temulentum var. subbivalve, commonly known as the red brome, is a variant of the common brome grass.

It is characterized by its reddish coloration, particularly in the lemma and palea of its florets. This plant is typically found in disturbed soils and grasslands, often in temperate regions. Its botanical features include narrow, grass-like leaves and spikelets that are often arranged in a loose panicle.

The plant is known for its adaptability and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
