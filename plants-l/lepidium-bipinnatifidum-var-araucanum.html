Lepidium bipinnatifidum var. araucanum

Lepidium bipinnatifidum var. araucanum, commonly known as the Araucanian cress, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typical of the Brassicaceae family. Its seeds are small and often dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize various habitats.

The plant is often found in moist, temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
