Laserpitium

Laserpitium, commonly known as the narrowleaf gentian, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Gentianaceae.

These plants are characterized by their showy, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. They are typically found in alpine and subalpine regions across the Northern Hemisphere. Laserpitium species are perennial herbs with lance-shaped leaves and are often used in traditional medicine.

The genus includes around 40 species, many of which are valued for their ornamental and ecological significance.
