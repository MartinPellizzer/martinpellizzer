Linum angustifolium var. genuinum

Linum angustifolium var. genuinum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flax, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or inland regions. It is a perennial herb that produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in textile production.
