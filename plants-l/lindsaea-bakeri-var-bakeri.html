Lindsaea bakeri var. bakeri

Lindsaea bakeri var. bakeri, commonly known as the Baker's lindsaea, is a species of epiphytic fern native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Lindsaeaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that often grow in dense clusters. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found on tree trunks or branches in the understory of tropical forests. Its reproductive structures, including strobili, are small and clustered, contributing to its unique appearance.

Lindsaea bakeri var. bakeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in specialized botanical gardens.
