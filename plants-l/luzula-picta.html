Luzula picta

Luzula picta, commonly known as the picta sedge, is a flowering plant species belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically clustered at the ends of the stems. Luzula picta is often found in moist, shady habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage.
