Lorentzia pascaloides

Lorentzia pascaloides, commonly known as the purpleleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, violet-blue flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and forest edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a distinctive purple tinge, adding to its ornamental value.

Lorentzia pascaloides is valued for its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
