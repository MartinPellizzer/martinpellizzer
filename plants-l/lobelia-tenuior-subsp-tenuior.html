Lobelia tenuior subsp. tenuior

Lobelia tenuior subsp.

tenuior, commonly known as narrowleaf cardinal flower, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America. It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface.

The plant produces striking tubular flowers in shades of red and orange, typically blooming from June to September. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun, often found in wetlands and woodland edges.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and role in supporting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees.
