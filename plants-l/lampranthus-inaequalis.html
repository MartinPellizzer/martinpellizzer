Lampranthus inaequalis

Lampranthus inaequalis, commonly known as the variable firecracker, is a striking member of the Aizoaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, often appearing in clusters at the tips of the stems. The plant is succulent, with fleshy, ribbed leaves that help it store water in arid environments. It blooms during the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value, it is popular in rock gardens and xeriscaping, thriving in well-drained soil and full sun.
