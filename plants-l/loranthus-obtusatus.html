Loranthus obtusatus

Loranthus obtusatus, commonly known as the blunt-leaf mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that attaches itself to the branches of host trees, primarily in the genus Eucalyptus.

It belongs to the family Loranthaceae and is native to Australia, where it thrives in a variety of forest and woodland habitats. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers that are often clustered together, followed by small, fleshy berries that contain the seeds. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped and have a leathery texture, which helps it conserve water in its often arid environment.

Loranthus obtusatus plays an important ecological role by providing food for birds and other animals that consume its berries.
