Leontodon muricatus

Leontodon muricatus, commonly known as the rough leontodon, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the daisy family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and rough to the touch, contributing to its common name.

Leontodon muricatus is often found in temperate climates and is a relatively hardy species.
