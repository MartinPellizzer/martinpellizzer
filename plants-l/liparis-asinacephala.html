Liparis asinacephala

Liparis asinacephala, commonly known as the horse's head orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its unique, horse-head-shaped pseudobulbs and linear leaves. The plant produces showy, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinct, spurred lip. Native to Southeast Asia, it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
