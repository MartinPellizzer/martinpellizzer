Leianthus latifolius

Leianthus latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf leianthus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features large, broad leaves that are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It produces yellow flowers that are arranged in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
