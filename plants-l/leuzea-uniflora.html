Leuzea uniflora

Leuzea uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower leuzea, is a herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant features upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and serrated. Its most distinctive characteristic is the single, showy flower that blooms in late summer.

Leuzea uniflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
