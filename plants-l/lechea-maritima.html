Lechea maritima

Lechea maritima, commonly known as the sea lettuce, is a type of green algae that thrives in marine environments.

It is characterized by its flat, blade-like structure and is typically found in shallow coastal waters. This plant plays a vital role in marine ecosystems by serving as a food source for various marine organisms. It reproduces through sexual reproduction, releasing gametes into the water.

Lechea maritima is also used in some traditional medicine practices due to its nutritional and medicinal properties.
