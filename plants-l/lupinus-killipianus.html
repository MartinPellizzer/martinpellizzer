Lupinus killipianus

Lupinus killipianus, commonly known as the Killipian lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion.

Lupinus killipianus is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
