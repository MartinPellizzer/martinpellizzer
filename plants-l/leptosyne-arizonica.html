Leptosyne arizonica

Leptosyne arizonica, commonly known as Arizona hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in late summer. This plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open plains. Its fibrous root system helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion in its native habitat.

Leptosyne arizonica plays a minor but ecologically significant role in supporting local insect populations and maintaining desert ecosystem balance.
