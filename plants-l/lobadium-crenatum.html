Lobadium crenatum

Lobadium crenatum, commonly known as the serrated lobelia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spurred flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite and often have serrated edges, contributing to its common name.

Lobadium crenatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
