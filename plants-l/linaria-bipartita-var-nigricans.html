Linaria bipartita var. nigricans

Linaria bipartita var. nigricans, commonly known as the black-flowered toadflax, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces striking, two-lipped flowers that are typically deep purple to black in color, with a distinctive yellow throat. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and unique coloration, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
