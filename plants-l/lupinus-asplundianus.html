Lupinus asplundianus

Lupinus asplundianus, commonly known as the Asplund's lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and spiny stems. The plant produces showy, pea-like flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Lupinus asplundianus is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
