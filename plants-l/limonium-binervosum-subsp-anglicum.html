Limonium binervosum subsp. anglicum

Limonium binervosum subsp.

anglicum, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and North Africa. It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and showy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant thrives in saline and well-drained soils, often found in dunes and coastal grasslands. Its flowers are typically arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems. Limonium binervosum subsp.

anglicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize coastal soils.
