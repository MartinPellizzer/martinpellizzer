Lastrea orizabae

Lastrea orizabae, commonly known as the Mexican rock cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, spineless stems that store water. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
