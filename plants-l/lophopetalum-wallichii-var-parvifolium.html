Lophopetalum wallichii var. parvifolium

Lophopetalum wallichii var. parvifolium, commonly known as the small-leaved flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Himalayas.

It features compound leaves that are typically 5 to 9 pairs of oval, serrated leaflets. The plant produces bright orange to red flowers in panicles during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
