Lisianthius viscosus

Lisianthius viscosus, commonly known as the sticky lisianthus, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant has sticky hairs on its stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

The sticky lisianthus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
