Lactuca aculeata

Lactuca aculeata, commonly known as prickly lettuce, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, spiny leaves and a distinctive flower head that resembles a small dandelion. The plant is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and it is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. Its latex-rich stems and leaves can cause skin irritation, and it has been used historically in traditional medicine.

Prickly lettuce is an annual plant that blooms from summer to early autumn.
