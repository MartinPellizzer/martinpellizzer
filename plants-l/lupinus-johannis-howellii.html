Lupinus johannis-howellii

Lupinus johannis-howellii, commonly known as Howell's lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces pods that contain several elliptical seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops.

Lupinus johannis-howellii plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native environment.
