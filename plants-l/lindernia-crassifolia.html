Lindernia crassifolia

Lindernia crassifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf lindernia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom from summer to fall. The plant has opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
