Lachnaea dodecandra

Lachnaea dodecandra, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Lachnaea dodecandra is often cultivated for its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms in gardens.
