Leucopogon ozothamnoides

Leucopogon ozothamnoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a shrub native to the temperate regions of southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils in open forests or heathlands, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its flowers, which are usually pink or white, bloom in spring and are an important food source for native pollinators.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
