Lespedeza formosa var. australis

Lespedeza formosa var. australis, commonly known as the Australian lespedeza, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit. The plant produces small, pale yellow to greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late winter to early spring. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Lespedeza formosa var. australis is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
