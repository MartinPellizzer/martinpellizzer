Lathyrus alpestris var. regis-alexandri

Lathyrus alpestris var. regis-alexandri, commonly known as the royal Alexander grass vetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pale pink to white. The plant produces small, oval seeds that are typically dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
