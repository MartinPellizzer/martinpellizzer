Lindera sikkimensis

Lindera sikkimensis, commonly known as Sikkim Lindera, is a small evergreen shrub native to the eastern Himalayas.

It typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 3,000 meters. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. Its flowers are small, clustered in umbels, and are often yellow or greenish-yellow in color.

The fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed, and the plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional uses in local medicine.
