Larochea

Larochea, commonly known as the silver leaf plant, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which helps it reflect sunlight and reduce water loss. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. Larochea typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters.

It is well adapted to dry climates and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
