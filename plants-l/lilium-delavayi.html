Lilium delavayi

Lilium delavayi, commonly known as the Delavay lily, is a species of flowering plant in the Liliaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of western China, particularly in Sichuan and Yunnan provinces. The plant is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and red. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
