Lasiochloa adscendens

Lasiochloa adscendens, commonly known as the ascending hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often grow upright. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing minute, spikelet-based flowers. Its leaves are narrow, hairy, and often have a bluish-green hue.

Lasiochloa adscendens is typically found in moist, well-drained soils in grasslands and along streambanks.
