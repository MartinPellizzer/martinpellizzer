Leucas martinicensis var. schimperi

Leucas martinicensis var. schimperi, commonly known as the dwarf leucas, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly found in parts of Jamaica and the Greater Antilles.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant typically grows as a low-growing shrub with slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered in whorls, often displaying shades of pink or purple, and it produces small, dry fruits that contain several seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
