Luma philippii

Luma philippii, commonly known as the Chilean firetree, is a flowering plant native to southern Chile and northern Argentina.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal and mountainous regions. The plant is characterized by its bright red or orange flowers, which bloom in clusters during the spring. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a reddish or purple tint.

Luma philippii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
