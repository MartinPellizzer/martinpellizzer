Lotus wrightii

Lotus wrightii, commonly known as the plains lotus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the central and southern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, where it thrives in moist to wet soil conditions. Its root system is fibrous, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Lotus wrightii is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
