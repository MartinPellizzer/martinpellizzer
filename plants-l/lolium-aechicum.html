Lolium aechicum

Lolium aechicum, commonly known as red oat grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with reddish hues. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences, which contain both male and female flowers, making it a monoecious species. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and is often found in meadows and disturbed areas.

Lolium aechicum is also noted for its potential as a forage plant and its ornamental value in gardens.
