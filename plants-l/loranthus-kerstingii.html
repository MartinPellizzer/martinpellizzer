Loranthus kerstingii

Loranthus kerstingii, commonly known as the Kersting's mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on a variety of host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own carbohydrates through photosynthesis using the light it captures. The plant has small, scale-like leaves and produces white or yellowish flowers that are often clustered in umbels. Its berries are typically red or orange and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This species plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing food for various animals and contributing to the structure of the forest canopy.
