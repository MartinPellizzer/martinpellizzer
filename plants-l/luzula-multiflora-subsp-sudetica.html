Luzula multiflora subsp. sudetica

Luzula multiflora subsp.

sudetica, commonly known as the Sudetic woodrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features dense, grass-like foliage with narrow, dark green leaves that form a low-growing rosette. It produces small, clustered white or pale green flowers in late spring to early summer.

The subspecies is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often used in horticultural settings for its ornamental value.
