Linum corymbiferum subsp. lambesanum

Linum corymbiferum subsp.

lambesanum, commonly known as the lamb's ear plant, is a perennial herbaceous species native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its soft, velvety foliage that resembles lamb's ears, hence its common name.

The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue or white flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade. Linum corymbiferum subsp.

lambesanum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its decorative foliage and modest floral display.
