Lythrum floribundum

Lythrum floribundum, commonly known as the red rootflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Lythraceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in wet meadows and marshy areas. The plant is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of vibrant pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Lythrum floribundum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in moist environments.
