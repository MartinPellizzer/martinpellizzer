Luzuriaga marginata

Luzuriaga marginata, commonly known as the marginsnoot sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly hairy, and it produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers in early spring. Luzuriaga marginata is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, which allows it to spread vegetatively and form dense stands.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
