Lycopodium magnificum

Lycopodium magnificum, commonly known as the magnificent clubmoss, is a perennial evergreen plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its spore-bearing cones and its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its slender, branching stems and small, scale-like leaves. It is often found in temperate regions, particularly in forests and rocky outcrops.

Lycopodium magnificum plays an important ecological role by contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for various microorganisms.
