Lundellianthus jaliscensis

Lundellianthus jaliscensis, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant orange to yellow blooms. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sunlight. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Lundellianthus jaliscensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in arid environments.
