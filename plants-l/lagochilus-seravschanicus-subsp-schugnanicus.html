Lagochilus seravschanicus subsp. schugnanicus

Lagochilus seravschanicus subsp.

schugnanicus, commonly known as Schugnan mountain thyme, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and square stems.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing small, lance-shaped leaves with a fragrant, mint-like aroma. Its flowers are usually pink to purple, arranged in dense clusters, and bloom during the summer months.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local herbal medicine.
