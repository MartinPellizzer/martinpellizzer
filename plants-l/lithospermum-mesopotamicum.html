Lithospermum mesopotamicum

Lithospermum mesopotamicum, commonly known as Mesopotamic lithosperm, is a perennial herb native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its roots contain compounds with potential medicinal properties, including antioxidants and anti-inflammatory agents.
