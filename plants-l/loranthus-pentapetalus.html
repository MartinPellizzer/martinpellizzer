Loranthus pentapetalus

Loranthus pentapetalus, commonly known as the five-petaled mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own carbohydrates through photosynthesis using the sunlight it captures. The plant has small, scale-like leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that releases tiny seeds, which can adhere to the bark of host trees.

This species is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it plays a role in the ecological interactions of forest ecosystems.
