Lycoctonum pteropus

Lycoctonum pteropus, commonly known as the ghost plant, is a unique species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, translucent, waxy leaves that give it a ghostly appearance. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding to its ethereal look. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in dense rainforests.

Due to its rarity and unusual appearance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and plant enthusiasts alike.
