Lucuma serpentaria var. elliptica

Lucuma serpentaria var. elliptica, commonly known as the elliptical lucuma, is a unique member of the Lauraceae family, native to the cloud forests of South America.

This plant is characterized by its elongated, elliptical leaves that are often adorned with a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in its humid, shaded habitat. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by the development of its distinctive, spiky fruit. While not widely cultivated, it is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.

Its botanical features make it an intriguing subject for ecological and horticultural study.
