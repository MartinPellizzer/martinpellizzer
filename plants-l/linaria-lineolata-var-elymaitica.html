Linaria lineolata var. elymaitica

Linaria lineolata var. elymaitica, commonly known as the Elymaitic toadflax, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers with two distinct lobes. The leaves are linear and often have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
