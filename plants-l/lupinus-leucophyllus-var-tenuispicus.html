Lupinus leucophyllus var. tenuispicus

Lupinus leucophyllus var. tenuispicus, commonly known as the slender-leaved lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of bright yellow to orange flowers. The plant has narrow, pinnately compound leaves with small, pointed leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Lupinus leucophyllus var. tenuispicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
