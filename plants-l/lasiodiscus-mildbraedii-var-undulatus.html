Lasiodiscus mildbraedii var. undulatus

Lasiodiscus mildbraedii var. undulatus, commonly known as the undulated waxy fern, is a rare and unique plant species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, undulating fronds that are covered in a waxy, translucent cuticle, which helps protect it from excessive moisture loss. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in tropical rainforests. Its leaves are usually elongated and have a delicate, feathery appearance, making it a striking addition to botanical collections.
