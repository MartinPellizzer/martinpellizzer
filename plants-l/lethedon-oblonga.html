Lethedon oblonga

Lethedon oblonga, commonly known as the narrow-leaved daisy, is a flowering plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
