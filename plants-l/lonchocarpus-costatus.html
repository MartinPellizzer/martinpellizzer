Lonchocarpus costatus

Lonchocarpus costatus, commonly known as the yellow cluster bean or yellow senna, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with prominent veins and yellow, clustered flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces distinctive pod-like fruits that contain small, edible seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of leguminous protein.

Botanically, it belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes many nitrogen-fixing plants.
