Luzula rufescens

Luzula rufescens, commonly known as redstem woodrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, commonly referred to as the grass family, and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green to reddish in color, especially along the edges. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of grasses.

Luzula rufescens thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
