Lepidium montanum subsp. canescens

Lepidium montanum subsp.

canescens, commonly known as the mountain peppergrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

The plant features small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of whitish hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance.

The species is notable for its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient component of alpine ecosystems.
