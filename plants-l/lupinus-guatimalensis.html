Lupinus guatimalensis

Lupinus guatimalensis, commonly known as the Guatemalan lupin, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces spikes of pea-like flowers in shades of purple, pink, or white, which bloom in the spring. Lupinus guatimalensis is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and rocky areas.

It is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
