Leptospermum myrsinoides

Leptospermum myrsinoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tea tree, is a small evergreen shrub native to eastern Australia.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, typically blooming in spring. Its bark is smooth and greyish, and it has a distinctive aromatic scent when the leaves are crushed.

This species is well adapted to sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.
