Lobivia rossii var. hardeniana

Lobivia rossii var. hardeniana, commonly known as the "Harden's Lobivia," is a compact, low-growing cactus native to the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It features dense, cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly hairs that help reduce water loss in its arid environment. The plant produces pale yellow to orange flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring or early summer. It is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
