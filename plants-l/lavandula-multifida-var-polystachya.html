Lavandula multifida var. polystachya

Lavandula multifida var. polystachya, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lavender, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, gray-green foliage with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are densely packed along its stems. The plant produces tall spikes of fragrant, purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and mild essential oils, which are used in aromatherapy and fragrance products.
