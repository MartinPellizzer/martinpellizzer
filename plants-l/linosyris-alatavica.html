Linosyris alatavica

Linosyris alatavica, commonly known as the Armenian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It features upright stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Linosyris alatavica is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, often growing in alpine or subalpine environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
