Lychnophora mello-barretoi

Lychnophora mello-barretoi, commonly known as the Brazilian sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, often in coastal areas. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it is adapted to thrive in dry, well-drained conditions.

Lychnophora mello-barretoi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
