Lycium guayaquilense

Lycium guayaquilense, commonly known as the Guayaquil boxthorn, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Ecuador and Peru.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces red berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Lycium guayaquilense is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in tropical and subtropical regions.
