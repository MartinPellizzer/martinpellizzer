Liatris ligulistylis

Liatris ligulistylis, commonly known as the narrow-stemmed blazing star, is a herbaceous perennial native to the central and eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features tall, slender stems that bear whorled clusters of purple to pink flowers, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slight bluish tint.

Liatris ligulistylis is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for native gardens and pollinator habitats.
