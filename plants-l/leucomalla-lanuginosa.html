Leucomalla lanuginosa

Leucomalla lanuginosa, commonly known as the woolly leucomalla, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories. Its scientific name reflects its white, woolly appearance, with "lanuginosa" referring to the lanugo-like hairs covering its leaves.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
