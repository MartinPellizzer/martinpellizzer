Lycopus velutinus

Lycopus velutinus, commonly known as silky bugleweed, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and features small, blue to purple flowers. Its leaves are hairy and have a distinctive velvety texture, contributing to its common name.

Lycopus velutinus is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported calming and anti-inflammatory properties.
