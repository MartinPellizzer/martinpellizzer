Lathyrus miyabei

Lathyrus miyabei, commonly known as the Japanese vetch, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple, depending on the variety. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.

Lathyrus miyabei is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in ecological restoration projects.
