Lupinus lepidus var. constancei

Lupinus lepidus var. constancei, commonly known as the Constance's lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive blue to violet pea-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and open habitats, often in coastal areas. It has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance.
