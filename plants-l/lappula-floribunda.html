Lappula floribunda

Lappula floribunda, commonly known as the flowering lappula, is a flowering plant native to arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress plants. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the summer months. Its leaves are hairy and lobed, adapted to survive in cold, nutrient-poor environments.

Lappula floribunda is typically found growing on rocky slopes and in open tundra habitats.
