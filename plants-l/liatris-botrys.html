Liatris botrys

Liatris botrys, commonly known as the clustered blazing star, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, spikelike inflorescence composed of numerous small, showy purple to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in prairies, open woodlands, and disturbed sites, preferring full sun and well-drained soil. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy to the touch.

Liatris botrys is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
