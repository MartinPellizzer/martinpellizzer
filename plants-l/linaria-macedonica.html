Linaria macedonica

Linaria macedonica, commonly known as the Macedonian toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its linear, lance-shaped leaves are arranged oppositely along the stem, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Linaria macedonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its bright, showy blooms.
