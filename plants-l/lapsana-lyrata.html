Lapsana lyrata

Lapsana lyrata, commonly known as the common tansy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a biennial or short-lived perennial herb that grows up to 1 meter tall, featuring erect, branching stems covered in fine hairs. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and a strong, aromatic scent. Its yellow flowers are arranged in flat-topped clusters, blooming from June to September.

Lapsana lyrata is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and waste areas, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
