Linnaea triflora

Linnaea triflora, commonly known as the twinflower, is a small evergreen shrub native to the northern regions of North America and Eurasia.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in pairs, giving it the common name. The plant has opposite, oval-shaped leaves with a smooth, glossy surface. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Linnaea triflora is noted for its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in early spring.
