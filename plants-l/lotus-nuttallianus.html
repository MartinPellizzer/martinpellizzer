Lotus nuttallianus

Lotus nuttallianus, commonly known as Nuttall's lotus, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps. The plant features showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a waxy texture, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to waterlogged soils.

Nuttall's lotus is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland environments and supporting local biodiversity.
