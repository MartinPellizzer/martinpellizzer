Lycium parvifolium

Lycium parvifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved boxthorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically less than 2 centimeters in length. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Lycium parvifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
