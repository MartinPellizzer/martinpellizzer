Lachenalia odoratissima

Lachenalia odoratissima, commonly known as the sweet-scented lachenalia, is a South African bulbous plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces a strong, pleasant fragrance, which is most noticeable in the evening. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and unique scent, it is popular among gardeners and botanists alike.
