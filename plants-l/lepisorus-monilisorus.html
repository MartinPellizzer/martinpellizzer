Lepisorus monilisorus

Lepisorus monilisorus, commonly known as the bearded maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are often covered in small, hairy structures, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are wiry and creeping, allowing it to spread gradually over time.

Lepisorus monilisorus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with the right conditions.
