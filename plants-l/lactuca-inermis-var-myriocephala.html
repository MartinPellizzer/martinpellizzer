Lactuca inermis var. myriocephala

Lactuca inermis var. myriocephala, commonly known as the broad-headed lettuce, is a variant of the common lettuce species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad, and often lobed leaves that are typically smooth and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that form in a loose cluster at the top of the stem. It thrives in cool climates and is often cultivated for its edible leaves.

This variety is valued for its mild flavor and is used in various culinary applications.
