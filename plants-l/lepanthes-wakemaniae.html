Lepanthes wakemaniae

Lepanthes wakemaniae, commonly known as the Wakemania orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually small, with a unique coloration that includes shades of white, green, and purple.

Lepanthes wakemaniae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
