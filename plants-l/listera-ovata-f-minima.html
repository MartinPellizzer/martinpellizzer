Listera ovata f. minima

Listera ovata f. minima, commonly known as the miniature white wood sorrel, is a compact, low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive white underside, and its flowers are delicate, five-petaled white blooms that appear in early spring. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and garden beds. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.

Botanically, it belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its unique leaf morphology and floral structure.
