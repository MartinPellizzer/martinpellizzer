Liatris paniculata

Liatris paniculata, commonly known as the blazing star, is a native North American plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that bear dense clusters of pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in prairies, open woods, and disturbed sites, preferring full sun and well-drained soil. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often gray-green in appearance.

The flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
