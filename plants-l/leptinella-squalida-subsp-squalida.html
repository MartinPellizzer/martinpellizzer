Leptinella squalida subsp. squalida

Leptinella squalida subsp.

squalida, commonly known as the Alpine penny-cress, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained, rocky soils.

The plant features a low-growing, mat-forming structure with small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are pinnately divided, with a silvery-white underside, and the plant is known for its frost-resistant characteristics.

It is often used in rock gardens and alpine displays due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
