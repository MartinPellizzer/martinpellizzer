Lobivia densispina f. purpureostoma

Lobivia densispina f. purpureostoma, commonly known as the Purple-Flowered Lobivia, is a succulent plant native to South America, specifically Argentina.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, branching structure with thick, ribbed stems. The plant produces clusters of small, vibrant purple flowers during the spring and summer seasons. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, and it thrives in well-draining soil with minimal water.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to adapt to arid environments.
