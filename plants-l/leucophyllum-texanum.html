Leucophyllum texanum

Leucophyllum texanum, commonly known as Texas heather, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, which bloom in clusters during the spring and summer. The plant has gray-green foliage that is covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The plant's compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in arid regions.
