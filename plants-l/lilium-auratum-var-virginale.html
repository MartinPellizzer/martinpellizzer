Lilium auratum var. virginale

Lilium auratum var. virginale, commonly known as the Virginia lily, is a striking perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white to pale yellow in color, with a distinctive red or orange throat. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves that are arranged in a fan-like pattern at the base. It thrives in woodland environments and prefers partial to full shade with moist, well-drained soil.

The Virginia lily is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant blooms.
