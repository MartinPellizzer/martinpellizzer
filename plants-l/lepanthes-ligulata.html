Lepanthes ligulata

Lepanthes ligulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pale yellow. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing in the understory of tropical forests. Its flowers are usually short-lived but attract various pollinators such as bees and moths.

Lepanthes ligulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
