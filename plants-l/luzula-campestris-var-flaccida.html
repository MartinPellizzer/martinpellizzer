Luzula campestris var. flaccida

Luzula campestris var. flaccida, commonly known as the drooping woodrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, three-lobed flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Luzula campestris var. flaccida is often found in temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.
