Leucothoe ambigua

Leucothoe ambigua, commonly known as the Florida beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces showy, violet-pink berries in the fall, which are a food source for various birds. Leucothoe ambigua thrives in partial to full shade and moist, well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
