Ligusticum aromaticum

Ligusticum aromaticum, commonly known as Chinese lovage, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its aromatic, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in umbrella-like clusters called umbels. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Ligusticum aromaticum is valued for its distinctive fragrance and is cultivated both for ornamental purposes and culinary use.
