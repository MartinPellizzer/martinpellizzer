Lithospermum froebellii

Lithospermum froebellii, commonly known as the froebell's rock pepper, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky, arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and poor soil conditions. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are often pink to purple in color, arranged in clusters along the stems. Its roots contain compounds that have been studied for potential medicinal properties.

Lithospermum froebellii is also valued for its ornamental appeal in xeriscaping due to its hardy nature and distinctive foliage.
