Lobularia canariensis subsp. rosula-venti

Lobularia canariensis subsp.

rosula-venti, commonly known as the Canary Island candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands. It is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial that produces clusters of white or pink flowers.

The plant has small, rounded leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in rock gardens and coastal landscapes. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for Mediterranean climates.

Its delicate appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking drought-tolerant plants.
