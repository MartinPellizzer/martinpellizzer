Linaria setabensis

Linaria setabensis, commonly known as the Setabense toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the genus Linaria in the family Plantaginaceae, which includes various species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

Linaria setabensis is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun conditions.
