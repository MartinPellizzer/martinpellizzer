Lupinus stockii

Lupinus stockii, commonly known as the Sierra lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and showy blue to purple flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. Lupinus stockii is adapted to rocky and dry habitats, often found in mountainous regions.

It plays an important role in soil nitrogen fixation, contributing to ecosystem health.
