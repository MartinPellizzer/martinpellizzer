Lychnis umbellifera

Lychnis umbellifera, commonly known as the clustered pink, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of small, pink to reddish-purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Lychnis umbellifera is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
