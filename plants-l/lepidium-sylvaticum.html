Lepidium sylvaticum

Lepidium sylvaticum, commonly known as wild cress, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants like mustard and cabbage. The plant is characterized by its small, green, fern-like leaves and clusters of tiny white or yellow flowers. It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Lepidium sylvaticum is often used in herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
