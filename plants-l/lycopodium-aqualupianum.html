Lycopodium aqualupianum

Lycopodium aqualupianum, commonly known as the aquatic clubmoss, is a unique species of lycopod that thrives in wet, swampy environments.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and spore-producing structures called sporangia. This plant is typically found in tropical and subtropical regions where there is consistent moisture. Its root system helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion in aquatic habitats.

Lycopodium aqualupianum plays a vital role in maintaining the ecological balance of wetland ecosystems.
