Litsea ferruginea var. ferruginea

Litsea ferruginea var. ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty litsea, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often reddish-brown underneath, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by orange-red berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests and along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
