Leptotaenia dissecta var. multifida

Leptotaenia dissecta var. multifida, commonly known as the dissected multifid fern, is a delicate and intricately structured fern native to temperate regions.

Its leaves are deeply divided into narrow, feather-like segments, giving it a lacy appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands or rocky crevices. It has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread slowly through underground stems.

The fronds are usually a deep green, with a soft texture that contributes to its ornamental appeal.
