Lobelia burttii subsp. meruensis

Lobelia burttii subsp.

meruensis, commonly known as the Meru lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of Kenya. It is characterized by its erect growth habit and showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in montane grasslands and forest margins. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and branching.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
