Lobelia fulgens var. propinqua

Lobelia fulgens var. propinqua, commonly known as the scarlet lily, is a striking perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, tubular red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 3 feet and has narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like hummingbirds and butterflies.
