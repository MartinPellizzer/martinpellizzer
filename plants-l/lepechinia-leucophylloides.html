Lepechinia leucophylloides

Lepechinia leucophylloides, commonly known as silverleaf lepechinia, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in spikes during the summer months. Its leaves are typically silver or gray-green, covered in fine hairs that help reduce water loss in arid environments.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or open habitats.
