Limnophila trichophylla

Limnophila trichophylla, commonly known as the threadleaf water plantain, is a semi-aquatic flowering plant native to wetlands and marshes in Southeast Asia.

It features slender, ribbon-like leaves that are often submerged or floating, adapted to aquatic environments. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom above the water surface. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats in still or slow-moving water.

Limnophila trichophylla plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small organisms and stabilizing shoreline sediments.
