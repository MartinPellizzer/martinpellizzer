Lucuma obovata var. ruizii

Lucuma obovata var. ruizii, commonly known as the Ruiz lucuma, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly Peru and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its large, obovate leaves and produces yellow-orange fruits that are rich in nutrients. The fruit has a unique, sweet, and creamy flavor, making it a valuable crop for both local consumption and commercial use.

It thrives in high-altitude areas with cool, temperate climates and well-drained soils.
