Lotus corniculatus var. uniflorus

Lotus corniculatus var. uniflorus, commonly known as the single-flower sweet clover, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it valuable in soil enrichment. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and agricultural fields.

Its short stature and rapid growth make it a common weed in various habitats.
