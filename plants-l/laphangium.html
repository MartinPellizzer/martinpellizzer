Laphangium

Laphangium, commonly known as the golden lily, is a striking plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and grows in moist, shaded environments. Laphangium is often found in forest understories and along stream banks.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and ecological role in its native habitat.
