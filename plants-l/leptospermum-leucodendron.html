Leptospermum leucodendron

Leptospermum leucodendron, commonly known as the white-leaved tea tree, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often white or pale green in color. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine and are also a source of essential oils.
