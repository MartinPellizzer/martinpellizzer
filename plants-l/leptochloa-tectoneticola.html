Leptochloa tectoneticola

Leptochloa tectoneticola, commonly known as the narrowleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and compact inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and pastures. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers.

Leptochloa tectoneticola is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature and ability to stabilize soil.
