Luzula multiflora subsp. calabra

Luzula multiflora subsp.

calabra, commonly known as the Calabrian woodrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes.

The plant features dense, grass-like foliage with narrow, dark green leaves that are often clustered at the base. Its small, clustered flowers are usually white or pale green, appearing in early spring.

The subspecies is characterized by its compact growth habit and adaptability to humid, temperate climates.
