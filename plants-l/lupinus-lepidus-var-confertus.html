Lupinus lepidus var. confertus

Lupinus lepidus var. confertus, commonly known as the dense lupin, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers and narrow, hairy leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is often found in coastal regions. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
