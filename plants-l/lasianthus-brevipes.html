Lasianthus brevipes

Lasianthus brevipes, commonly known as the short-footed mistflower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically features lance-shaped leaves with a smooth texture and produces small, tubular flowers in shades of pink to purple. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded forest environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
