Lysimachia radicans

Lysimachia radicans, commonly known as creeping Jenny, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North America.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, and its leaves are heart-shaped with a smooth texture. The plant spreads via rhizomes, forming dense mats that can cover large areas. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or wetlands.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become invasive in certain regions if not managed properly.
