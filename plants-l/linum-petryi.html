Linum petryi

Linum petryi, commonly known as the Persian flax, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant produces small, elongated pods containing several seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber. Linum petryi thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
