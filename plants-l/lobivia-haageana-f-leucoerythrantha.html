Lobivia haageana f. leucoerythrantha

Lobivia haageana f. leucoerythrantha, commonly known as the White-veined Lobivia, is a rare and striking cactus native to the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in dense, white spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring. Its unique white veins on the stems make it easily distinguishable from other Lobivia species.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
