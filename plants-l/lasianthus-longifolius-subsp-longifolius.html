Lasianthus longifolius subsp. longifolius

Lasianthus longifolius subsp.

longifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved lasianthus, is a species of flowering plant in the Acanthaceae family. It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia.

The plant is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. It produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom during the wet season.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
