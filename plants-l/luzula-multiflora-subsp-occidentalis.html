Luzula multiflora subsp. occidentalis

Luzula multiflora subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as western wood rush, is a perennial herb native to western North America. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes.

The plant features grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy. It produces small, spike-like clusters of white or pale blue flowers in the spring.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies in suitable habitats.
