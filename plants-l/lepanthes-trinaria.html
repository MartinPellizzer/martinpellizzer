Lepanthes trinaria

Lepanthes trinaria, commonly known as the three-lobed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical forests. Its leaves are usually oval-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern.

Lepanthes trinaria is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouse environments.
