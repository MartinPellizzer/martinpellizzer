Lessingianthus tomentellus

Lessingianthus tomentellus, commonly known as the woolly sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, grayish foliage and bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces small, daisy-like blooms that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a notable species in its native ecosystems.
