Laurelia philippiana

Laurelia philippiana, commonly known as the Philippine laurel, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cinnamon and avocado. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall, featuring a straight trunk and broad, glossy dark green leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered, and it produces edible berries that are sometimes used in traditional medicine.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in its native habitat.
