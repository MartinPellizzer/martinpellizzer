Leandra capitata

Leandra capitata, commonly known as the clustered leandra, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stems. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm climates.
