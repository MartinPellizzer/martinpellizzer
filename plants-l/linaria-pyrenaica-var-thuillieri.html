Linaria pyrenaica var. thuillieri

Linaria pyrenaica var. thuillieri, commonly known as the Pyrenean toadflax, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Pyrenees region.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
