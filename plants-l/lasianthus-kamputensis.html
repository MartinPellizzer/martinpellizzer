Lasianthus kamputensis

Lasianthus kamputensis, commonly known as the Kamputensis Flaxflower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

The flowers, which range in color from white to pale pink, bloom during the rainy season and attract various pollinators.
