Lupinus allimicranthus

Lupinus allimicranthus, commonly known as the golden lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and showy yellow flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, hairy pods that contain several seeds. Lupinus allimicranthus is adapted to dry, sunny environments and is often found in sandy or gravelly soils.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
