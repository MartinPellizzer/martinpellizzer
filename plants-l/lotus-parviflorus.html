Lotus parviflorus

Lotus parviflorus, commonly known as the dwarf lotus, is a species of aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom during the summer months. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, supported by long, slender stems. Its roots are submerged, anchoring it in muddy or sandy substrates.

Lotus parviflorus is often cultivated in ponds and water gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
