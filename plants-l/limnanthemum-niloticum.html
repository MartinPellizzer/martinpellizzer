Limnanthemum niloticum

Limnanthemum niloticum, commonly known as the water mallow, is a flowering plant native to Africa and parts of the Middle East.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wet or marshy environments. The plant features showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
