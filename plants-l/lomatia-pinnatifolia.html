Lomatia pinnatifolia

Lomatia pinnatifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lomatia, is a shrub native to the south-western region of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, pinnately divided leaves that give it a distinctive, fern-like appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom during the cooler months. Lomatia pinnatifolia is adapted to grow in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or near-coastal areas.

It is an important plant in local ecosystems, providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
