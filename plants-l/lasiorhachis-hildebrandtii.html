Lasiorhachis hildebrandtii

Lasiorhachis hildebrandtii, commonly known as the Hildebrandt's feather grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, hair-like leaves that give it a feathery appearance, and it typically grows in open, sunny habitats. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are borne on slender stalks. It is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
