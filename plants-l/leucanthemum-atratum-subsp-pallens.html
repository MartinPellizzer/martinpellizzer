Leucanthemum atratum subsp. pallens

Leucanthemum atratum subsp.

pallens, commonly known as the dark-leaved whiteflower, is a perennial herb native to the northeastern United States. It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade.

The plant features dark green, slightly hairy leaves and produces daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. Its botanical characteristics include a basal rosette of leaves and flowering stems that rise above the foliage.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
