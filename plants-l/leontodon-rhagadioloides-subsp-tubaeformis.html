Leontodon rhagadioloides subsp. tubaeformis

Leontodon rhagadioloides subsp.

tubaeformis, commonly known as the tubular bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its tubular, bell-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color.

The plant has linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, bract-like structures at the base of its flower heads. It is native to southern Europe and thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open grasslands.

This subspecies is often found in Mediterranean regions and is valued for its ornamental appearance.
