Linaria olympica

Linaria olympica, commonly known as the Olympic linaria, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Greece.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, spire-like clusters of yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and well-drained soils, thriving in full sun conditions. Its slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves contribute to its distinctive appearance.

Linaria olympica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
