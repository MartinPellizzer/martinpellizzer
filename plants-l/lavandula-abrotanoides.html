Lavandula abrotanoides

Lavandula abrotanoides, commonly known as Spanish lavender, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems and narrow, gray-green leaves that are aromatic and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces spikes of small, fragrant purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its essential oils are valued for their calming properties and are used in aromatherapy and perfumery.
