Leucocoryne gayi

Leucocoryne gayi, commonly known as the silver leaf lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking silver-white leaves and delicate, bell-shaped white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as under forest canopies. Its foliage is often covered in a fine, powdery coating that gives it a distinctive appearance.

Leucocoryne gayi is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in shaded garden settings.
