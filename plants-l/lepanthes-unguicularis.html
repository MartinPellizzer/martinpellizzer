Lepanthes unguicularis

Lepanthes unguicularis, commonly known as the red-tipped orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red sepals and petals that are often adorned with a distinctive yellow or orange tip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are small but numerous, arranged in a dense inflorescence, and it blooms during the rainy season.

Lepanthes unguicularis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
