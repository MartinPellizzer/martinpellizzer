Lotononis involucrata subsp. digitata

Lotononis involucrata subsp.

digitata, commonly known as the digitate lotononis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue to purple in color.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slight zigzag or toothed margin.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in arid climates.
