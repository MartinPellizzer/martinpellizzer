Livistona lorophylla

Livistona lorophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pandanus, is a species of palm native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnately compound leaves that are narrow and often curved. The plant produces large, branched inflorescences with small, white flowers, followed by edible, fibrous fruits. It thrives in coastal and wetland environments, often forming dense stands in swampy areas.

Livistona lorophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and has cultural significance among Indigenous Australian communities.
