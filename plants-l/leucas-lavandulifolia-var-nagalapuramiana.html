Leucas lavandulifolia var. nagalapuramiana

Leucas lavandulifolia var. nagalapuramiana, commonly known as Nagalapuram lavender, is a rare and endemic plant species native to the southern Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic, square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or blue in color, arranged in dense spike-like clusters. Its foliage is noted for its strong, camphor-like fragrance, which is often used in traditional medicine.
