Limonium melitense

Limonium melitense, commonly known as the Maltese sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and saline soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powder.

Limonium melitense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and floral arrangements.
