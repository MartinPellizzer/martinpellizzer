Lonicera sempervirens f. superba

Lonicera sempervirens f. superba, commonly known as the Florida honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly pubescent underside. The plant produces fragrant, tubular yellow-orange flowers in the spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that matures in the fall and is edible for both humans and wildlife.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in garden settings.
