Leucospermum dregei

Leucospermum dregei, commonly known as the King protea, is a striking South African flowering plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous white to pale pink petals surrounding a central yellow cone. The plant is native to the Western Cape region and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils. It produces vibrant orange-red seed pods that split open to release its seeds.

Leucospermum dregei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a symbol of South Africa's floral heritage.
