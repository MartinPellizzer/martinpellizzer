Linum tauricum subsp. bosphori

Linum tauricum subsp.

bosphori, commonly known as the Bosphorus flax, is a flowering plant native to the Black Sea region. It is characterized by its slender stems and small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds. It is often found growing in sandy or gravelly soils near coastal areas.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Linaceae and is notable for its ornamental value and historical use in fiber production.
