Lagascea helianthifolia var. suaveolens

Lagascea helianthifolia var. suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented goldenbush, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It features aromatic, glandular leaves that emit a pleasant fragrance when crushed. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fibrous root system helps stabilize soil in arid environments.

The species is adapted to full sun and well-drained soils, making it a hardy plant in desert landscapes.
