Litobrochia ludens

Litobrochia ludens, commonly known as the Mediterranean stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun exposure, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Litobrochia ludens is also known for its ability to survive in harsh environments, contributing to its ecological importance in Mediterranean ecosystems.
