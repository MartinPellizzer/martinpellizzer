Lasiopetalum tomentosum

Lasiopetalum tomentosum, commonly known as the woolly matted bellflower, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple, with a woolly, hairy texture on its stems and leaves. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or heathlands. Its foliage is densely matted, providing a low-growing, ornamental appearance.

It is valued in horticulture for its drought tolerance and attractive floral display.
