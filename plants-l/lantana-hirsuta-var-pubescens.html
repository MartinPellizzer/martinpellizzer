Lantana hirsuta var. pubescens

Lantana hirsuta var. pubescens, commonly known as hairy lantana, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to orange and red. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which give it its scientific name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its showy blooms attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity.
