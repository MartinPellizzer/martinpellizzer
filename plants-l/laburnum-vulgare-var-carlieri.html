Laburnum vulgare var. carlieri

Laburnum vulgare var. carlieri, commonly known as the Carlier's golden chain, is a variant of the golden chain tree characterized by its striking yellow flowers.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region, particularly France and Italy. The plant features pinnate leaves with numerous small leaflets and produces long, drooping clusters of bright yellow blossoms in spring. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.

The varietal form is noted for its slightly different growth habit and flower structure compared to the typical Laburnum vulgare.
