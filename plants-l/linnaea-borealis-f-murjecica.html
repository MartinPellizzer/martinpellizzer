Linnaea borealis f. murjecica

Linnaea borealis f. murjecica, commonly known as the Murjecica twinflower, is a rare and unique variant of the twinflower plant.

It is characterized by its small, white, bilaterally symmetrical flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is a low-growing perennial with opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence. It thrives in shaded, moist environments, typically found in boreal and temperate forest understories.

This form is distinguished by its distinct floral morphology and is of particular interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and genetic uniqueness.
