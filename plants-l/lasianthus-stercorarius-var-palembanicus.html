Lasianthus stercorarius var. palembanicus

Lasianthus stercorarius var. palembanicus, commonly known as the Palembanese lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its attractive, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture, while the stems are often hairy.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens.
