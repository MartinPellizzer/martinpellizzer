Litsea albayana

Litsea albayana, commonly known as the Philippine laurel, is a deciduous tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by edible berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Litsea albayana is often found in lowland and montane forests, thriving in humid and shaded environments.

Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in construction and furniture-making.
