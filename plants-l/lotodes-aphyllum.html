Lotodes aphyllum

Lotodes aphyllum, commonly known as the leafless water lily, is a unique aquatic plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its absence of leaves, with only submerged stems and flowers emerging from the water's surface. The plant's flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive, boat-shaped structure. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds and lakes.

Due to its specialized morphology, it plays a niche role in aquatic ecosystems, providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
