Lisianthius campanulaceus

Lisianthius campanulaceus, commonly known as the bluebell of the genus Lisianthius, is a member of the Campanulaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States, particularly in the southeastern region. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, with a rough texture that distinguishes it from similar species.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.
