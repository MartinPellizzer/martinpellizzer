Loasa vulcanica

Loasa vulcanica, commonly known as the volcanic loasa, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of purple and violet. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions.

Loasa vulcanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in specialized gardens.
