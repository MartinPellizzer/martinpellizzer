Locheria hirsuta var. magnifica

Locheria hirsuta var. magnifica, commonly known as the magnificus hirsute locheria, is a striking member of the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine, hairy textures. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This variant is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
