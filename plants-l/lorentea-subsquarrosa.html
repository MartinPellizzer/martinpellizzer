Lorentea subsquarrosa

Lorentea subsquarrosa, commonly known as the squarrose lorentea, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, clustered inflorescences and small, often white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories or rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface.

Lorentea subsquarrosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
