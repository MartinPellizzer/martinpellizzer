Lupinus andersonii var. apertus

Lupinus andersonii var. apertus, commonly known as the Anderson's lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces cylindrical, hairy pods that contain several seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
