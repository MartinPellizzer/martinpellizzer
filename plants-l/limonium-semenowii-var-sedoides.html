Limonium semenowii var. sedoides

Limonium semenowii var. sedoides, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its blue to purple flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dune environments. It is a perennial species that blooms in late spring to early summer.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for coastal gardens and landscaping.
