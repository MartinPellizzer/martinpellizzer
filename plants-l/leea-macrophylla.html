Leea macrophylla

Leea macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved leea, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves that can grow up to 20 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm climates.
