Loasa ambrosiifolia

Loasa ambrosiifolia, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a fragrant, mint-like aroma.

Loasa ambrosiifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
