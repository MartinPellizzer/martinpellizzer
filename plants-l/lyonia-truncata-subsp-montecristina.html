Lyonia truncata subsp. montecristina

Lyonia truncata subsp.

montecristina, commonly known as the Montecristi lyonia, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America, particularly in Ecuador. It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen shrub form.

The plant features small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, and its leaves are leathery and glossy. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in cloud forests or high-altitude regions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
