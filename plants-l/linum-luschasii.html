Linum luschasii

Linum luschasii, commonly known as the Luschasii flax, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, delicate flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Linum luschasii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has limited agricultural use compared to other flax species.
