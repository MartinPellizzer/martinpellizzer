Laurus nacusua

Laurus nacusua, commonly known as the sacred laurel, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other well-known plants like bay laurel and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its aromatic evergreen leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. It produces dark blue berries that are edible but often used in traditional medicine.

Laurus nacusua is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in ancient cultures.
