Lathyrus polymorphus var. incanus

Lathyrus polymorphus var. incanus, commonly known as the silver flax or white flax, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds, which are typically brown or black in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
