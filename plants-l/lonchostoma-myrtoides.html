Lonchostoma myrtoides

Lonchostoma myrtoides, commonly known as the myrtleleaf lichen, is a foliose lichen that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It has a distinctive greenish-gray thallus with a finely wrinkled surface. The plant forms a symbiotic relationship with algae, which provides photosynthetic capabilities. It is often found on tree trunks and rocks in temperate forests.

Its slow growth and unique appearance make it a subject of interest in ecological and botanical studies.
