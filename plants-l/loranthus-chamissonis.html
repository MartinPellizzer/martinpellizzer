Loranthus chamissonis

Loranthus chamissonis, commonly known as the red-stemmed mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that grows on various host trees, primarily in the southeastern United States.

It has smooth, reddish stems and small, opposite leaves that are typically oval in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers, followed by berries that are red when mature. Its seeds are dispersed by birds, which eat the berries and excrete the seeds onto new host trees.

Loranthus chamissonis plays an important ecological role by providing food for wildlife and contributing to the biodiversity of forest ecosystems.
