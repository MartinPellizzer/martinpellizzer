Limonium pagasaeum

Limonium pagasaeum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. This plant thrives in coastal and saline environments, often found on dunes and rocky shores. It has a compact growth habit and is well adapted to withstand strong winds and salt spray.

Limonium pagasaeum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
