Loranthus dregei var. subcuneifolius

Loranthus dregei var. subcuneifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that attaches to host trees, primarily in the genus Acacia.

It possesses small, glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along its stems. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. Its berries are red or orange, containing seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This species is native to southern Africa and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.
