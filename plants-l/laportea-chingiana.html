Laportea chingiana

Laportea chingiana, commonly known as Chinese laportea, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive, aromatic scent.

Laportea chingiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses in some cultures.
