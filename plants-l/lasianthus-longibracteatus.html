Lasianthus longibracteatus

Lasianthus longibracteatus, commonly known as the long-bract lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy bracts that are often mistaken for petals. The plant typically grows as an herb or shrub, with opposite leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape. Its flowers are clustered in whorls and are often brightly colored, attracting various pollinators.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in tropical gardens for its visual interest.
