Lomatium farinosum var. farinosum

Lomatium farinosum var. farinosum, commonly known as the sandberg's lomatium, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in dry, open habitats such as sagebrush steppe and rocky slopes. Its seeds are small and dry, often dispersed by wind.

It is an important component of native plant communities in arid regions.
