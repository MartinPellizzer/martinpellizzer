Linum karataviense

Linum karataviense, commonly known as the Karatavi flax, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the genus Linum, which includes various species of flax known for their blue or white flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its slender stems and small, delicate flowers make it a distinctive member of the Plantaginaceae family.

Despite its ornamental appeal, it is not widely cultivated for commercial purposes.
