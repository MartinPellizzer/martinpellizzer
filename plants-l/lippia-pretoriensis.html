Lippia pretoriensis

Lippia pretoriensis, commonly known as the Pretoria hyssop, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or blue, arranged in whorls along the stem. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly fuzzy texture, and the plant is known for its strong, minty fragrance.

Lippia pretoriensis is often used in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant due to its hardy nature and visual appeal.
