Lycopodioides oaxacana

Lycopodioides oaxacana, commonly known as the Oaxaca clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the order Lycopodiales.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. The plant has a distinctive appearance with whorled leaves that form dense clusters around its stems. It produces sporophylls that bear spores, which are released into the air for reproduction.

This plant is of particular interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
