Lamium maculatum var. bourgaei

Lamium maculatum var. bourgaei, commonly known as the spotted dead nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive purple and white markings, and it produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or violet in color. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to spread through rhizomes, forming dense ground cover.
