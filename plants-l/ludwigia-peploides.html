Ludwigia peploides

Ludwigia peploides, commonly known as the red Ludwigia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking red or pink foliage, which becomes more vibrant in low-light conditions. The plant typically grows in shallow, slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and streams. It produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are often submerged.

Ludwigia peploides is popular in aquascaping due to its aesthetic appeal and ease of cultivation.
