Lepanthes wercklei

Lepanthes wercklei, commonly known as the Werckle's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central and South America.

It is a small epiphytic orchid, typically growing on trees in humid tropical forests. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with intricate markings. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Lepanthes wercklei is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
