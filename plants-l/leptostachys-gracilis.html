Leptostachys gracilis

Leptostachys gracilis, commonly known as the slender leek orchid, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and delicate, white or pale green flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, where it thrives in moist, sandy soils.

It is a perennial herb that produces small, clustered inflorescences, and its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in wetland ecosystems.
