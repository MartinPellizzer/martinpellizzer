Loranthus vaginatus

Loranthus vaginatus, commonly known as the common mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily in the genus Eucalyptus.

It lacks true roots and absorbs water and nutrients through its haustoria, which penetrate the host's vascular system. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers followed by small, fleshy berries that are often consumed by birds. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a waxy coating.

Loranthus vaginatus plays an important ecological role by providing food for various wildlife species and contributing to the biodiversity of forest ecosystems.
