Ligustrum japonicum var. syaryotense

Ligustrum japonicum var. syaryotense, commonly known as Japanese privet, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter green underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by black berries in the fall. It is often used in landscaping due to its adaptability and tolerance to various soil conditions.

The varietal form, syaryotense, is distinguished by its unique leaf shape and growth habit compared to other Ligustrum species.
