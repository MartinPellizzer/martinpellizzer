Lathyrus gorgoni subsp. alatus

Lathyrus gorgoni subsp.

alatus, commonly known as the winged vetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves with distinctive wing-like structures on the leaflets.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
