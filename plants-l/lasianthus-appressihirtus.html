Lasianthus appressihirtus

Lasianthus appressihirtus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
