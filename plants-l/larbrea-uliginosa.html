Larbrea uliginosa

Larbrea uliginosa, commonly known as the bog mallow, is a perennial herb native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and alternate leaves that are typically oval to lance-shaped. The plant produces showy, pink or red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs, marshes, and along stream banks.

Larbrea uliginosa plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
