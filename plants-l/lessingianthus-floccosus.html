Lessingianthus floccosus

Lessingianthus floccosus, commonly known as the woolly sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, fuzzy leaves and bright yellow flower heads. The plant produces daisy-like composite flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique appearance and adaptability contribute to its appeal in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
