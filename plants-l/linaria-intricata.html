Linaria intricata

Linaria intricata, commonly known as the intricate toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, branched stems and clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces slender, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Linaria intricata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
