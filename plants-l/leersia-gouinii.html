Leersia gouinii

Leersia gouinii, commonly known as the African bulrush, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, thriving in moist to aquatic environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Botanically, it is classified under the Poaceae family, which includes other grasses.
