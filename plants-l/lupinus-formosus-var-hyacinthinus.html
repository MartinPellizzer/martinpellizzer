Lupinus formosus var. hyacinthinus

Lupinus formosus var. hyacinthinus, commonly known as the hyacinth lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of showy, pea-like flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

The species is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain butterfly species.
