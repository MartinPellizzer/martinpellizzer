Lithospermum serrulatum

Lithospermum serrulatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rock pepper, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive, serrated leaves. The plant produces small, purple to reddish flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its roots contain compounds that have been traditionally used in herbal medicine.

Lithospermum serrulatum thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.
