Lobelia caerulea f. procera

Lobelia caerulea f. procera, commonly known as the tall blue lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that can grow up to 3 feet tall, making it a striking addition to gardens. The plant produces clusters of small, sky-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down.

This cultivar is well-suited for moist, partially shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
