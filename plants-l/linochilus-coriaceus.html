Linochilus coriaceus

Linochilus coriaceus, commonly known as the rough-stemmed orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its upright, often hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in a dense spike. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires moderate to high humidity for optimal growth.

Linochilus coriaceus is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks, where it can receive sufficient light without being exposed to direct sunlight.
