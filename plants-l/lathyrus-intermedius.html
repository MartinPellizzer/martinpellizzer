Lathyrus intermedius

Lathyrus intermedius, commonly known as the evergreen sweet pea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves, which are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pea-like flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is widely cultivated in temperate climates.
