Lathyrus aphaca var. affinis

Lathyrus aphaca var. affinis, commonly known as the slender sweet pea, is a climbing legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves with a serrated edge. The plant produces fragrant, pea-like flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are followed by elongated pods containing several seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and ability to attract pollinators.
