Listera ovata

Listera ovata, commonly known as the white toothwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves and produces delicate, bell-shaped white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and it is often found in clusters along the ground.

Listera ovata is a member of the Primulaceae family and is noted for its unique, tooth-like leaves.
