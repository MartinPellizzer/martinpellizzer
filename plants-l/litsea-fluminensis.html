Litsea fluminensis

Litsea fluminensis, commonly known as the Brazilian lauraceae or sweet bay, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, lowland areas and thrives in warm, moist climates. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a glossy surface.

The fruit is a small, dark berry that contains several seeds, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
