Lythrum ovalifolium

Lythrum ovalifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaf lythrum, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Lythraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of pink to purple flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and woodland edges.

Lythrum ovalifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
