Loxanthocereus madisonorum

Loxanthocereus madisonorum, commonly known as the Madison's night blooming cactus, is a species native to the Sonoran Desert in North America.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its ribbed stems and spiny areoles. The plant produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as bats and moths. Its adaptations include a deep root system for water storage and a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

Loxanthocereus madisonorum is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.
