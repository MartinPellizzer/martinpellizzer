Loudetia tisserantii

Loudetia tisserantii, commonly known as the Tisserant's lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-pollinated flowers. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought conditions.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing soils and providing forage for grazing animals.
