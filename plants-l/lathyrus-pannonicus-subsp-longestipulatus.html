Lathyrus pannonicus subsp. longestipulatus

Lathyrus pannonicus subsp.

longestipulatus, commonly known as the long-stippled vetch, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves with elongated stipules.

The flowers are typically pink or purple, arranged in racemes, and bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.
