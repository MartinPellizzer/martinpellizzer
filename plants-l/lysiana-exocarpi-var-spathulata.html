Lysiana exocarpi var. spathulata

Lysiana exocarpi var. spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leafed passionfruit, is a variant of the passionfruit plant characterized by its distinctive, broad, spatulate leaves.

It belongs to the family Passifloraceae and is native to tropical regions of South America. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that are often associated with its symbolic significance in religious and cultural contexts. Its fruits are typically orange or yellow, with a thick, aromatic rind and a sweet, tangy pulp.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its edible fruits, making it a popular choice in gardens and for commercial cultivation.
