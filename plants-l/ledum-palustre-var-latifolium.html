Ledum palustre var. latifolium

Ledum palustre var. latifolium, commonly known as broadleaf Labrador tea, is a variant of the narrow-leaved Labrador tea species.

It is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the boreal and subarctic regions of North America. The plant features broad, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and emit a strong, aromatic scent when crushed. It produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

This plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in peat bogs and wetlands.
