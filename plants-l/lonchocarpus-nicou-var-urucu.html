Lonchocarpus nicou var. urucu

Lonchocarpus nicou var. urucu, commonly known as urucu or blood tree, is a species native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is a legume belonging to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive red, fleshy pods. The plant is known for its deep red sap, which has been traditionally used by indigenous communities for various purposes. Its leaves are typically compound, with trifoliate or pinnate structures, and it produces small, clustered flowers.

The plant is also valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a source of natural dyes.
