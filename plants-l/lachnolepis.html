Lachnolepis

Lachnolepis, commonly known as the velvet fern, is a unique plant belonging to the family Lachnaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage that gives it a soft, almost fuzzy appearance. The plant is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are typically small and clustered, forming a compact rosette structure.

Lachnolepis is noted for its ability to store water, making it somewhat drought-tolerant once established.
