Lactuca hastata var. paniculata

Lactuca hastata var. paniculata, commonly known as the panicled lettuce, is a wild species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette of dark green, spoon-shaped leaves and a tall, branching stem that bears small, green flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, and it is often found in disturbed soils.

The species is of interest to botanists and ecologists due to its ecological role and potential for use in horticulture.
