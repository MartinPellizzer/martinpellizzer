Lantana triplinervia

Lantana triplinervia, commonly known as the three-veined lantana, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to orange and red. The leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a rough texture with three prominent veins. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

However, it can be invasive in some areas and may pose a risk to local ecosystems.
