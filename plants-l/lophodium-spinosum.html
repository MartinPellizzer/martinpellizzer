Lophodium spinosum

Lophodium spinosum, commonly known as the spiny lophodium, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically spiny along the edges, providing a defense mechanism against herbivores. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid climates.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in drought-tolerant gardens.
