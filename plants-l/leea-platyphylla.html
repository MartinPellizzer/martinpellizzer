Leea platyphylla

Leea platyphylla, commonly known as the flat-leaf leea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the family Leeaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically broad and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, often appearing in late spring or early summer. It is often found growing in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
