Lythrum schelkovnikovii

Lythrum schelkovnikovii, commonly known as the Siberian verbena, is a flowering plant native to the cold regions of Siberia and northern China.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, acidic soils, often found in tundra and taiga ecosystems. The plant features slender, erect stems and produces spikes of pink to purple flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

Lythrum schelkovnikovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
