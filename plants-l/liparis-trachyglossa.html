Liparis trachyglossa

Liparis trachyglossa, commonly known as the narrow-lipped orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. The plant has a slender, upright stem with several elongated, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and arranged in a raceme, often with a faint fragrance.

Liparis trachyglossa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
