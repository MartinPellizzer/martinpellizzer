Lupinus quitensis

Lupinus quitensis, commonly known as the Peruvian lupin, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and clusters of pea-like flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Lupinus quitensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential in ecological restoration projects.
