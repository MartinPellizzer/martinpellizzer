Laportea weddellii

Laportea weddellii, commonly known as Weddell's ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and dense clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant has palmate, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, rich soils and is often found in woodland borders or along stream banks.

Laportea weddellii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
