Ledebouria humifusa

Ledebouria humifusa, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale purple flowers. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often grown as a decorative succulent. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of care, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.
