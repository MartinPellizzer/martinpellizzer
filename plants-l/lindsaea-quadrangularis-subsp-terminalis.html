Lindsaea quadrangularis subsp. terminalis

Lindsaea quadrangularis subsp.

terminalis, commonly known as the slender lindsaea, is a species of epiphytic fern native to the tropical regions of Central and South America. It is characterized by its slender, wiry rhizomes and delicate, linear leaves that are often arranged in a spiral pattern.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically growing on trees or rocks in the understory of tropical forests.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists studying epiphytic plant adaptations.
