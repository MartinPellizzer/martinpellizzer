Lactuca scariola subsp. augustanae

Lactuca scariola subsp.

augustanae, commonly known as the Augustan sowthistle, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family. It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright stems and lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to various environments, though it can become invasive in some regions.
