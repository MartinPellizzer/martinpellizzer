Liparis mamillata

Liparis mamillata, commonly known as the spurred orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its distinctive spurred petals and small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and forest understories. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it produces a delicate, fragrant blossom.

Botanically, it belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitats.
