Larix gmelinii subsp. principis-rupprechtii

Larix gmelinii subsp.

principis-rupprechtii, commonly known as the Korean larch, is a coniferous tree native to the Korean Peninsula. It is characterized by its needle-like leaves that are typically yellow-green in color and turn golden in autumn.

The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and parks.
