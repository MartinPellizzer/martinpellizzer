Larrea intricata

Larrea intricata, commonly known as grevillea, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, feather-like flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant has small, leathery leaves that help it conserve water in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for drought-tolerant gardens.

Larrea intricata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract native pollinators.
