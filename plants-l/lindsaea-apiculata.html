Lindsaea apiculata

Lindsaea apiculata, commonly known as the spike-tipped maidenhair fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that often grow in a dense, cascading form. The plant typically attaches itself to trees or rocks, drawing moisture and nutrients from the air. Its spore-bearing structures, called sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.

Lindsaea apiculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded, humid environments.
