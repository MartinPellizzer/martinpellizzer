Lophopetalum oblongifolium

Lophopetalum oblongifolium, commonly known as the Texas mountain laurel, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southern United States.

It features compound leaves that are elliptical in shape, with a smooth, dark green upper surface and a lighter, fuzzy underside. The plant produces fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, adding ornamental value to the landscape. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for landscaping in arid and semi-arid regions.
