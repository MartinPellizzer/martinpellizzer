Lilium michiganense

Lilium michiganense, commonly known as the Michigan lily, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, cup-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer, producing pink to red flowers with a white throat. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Lilium michiganense prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
