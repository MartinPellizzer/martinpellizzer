Ligusticum bulbocastanum

Ligusticum bulbocastanum, commonly known as Chinese lovage, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its aromatic, fern-like leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in umbels, typical of the family, and its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is valued for its medicinal properties.

The leaves and roots contain volatile oils that contribute to its distinctive aroma and therapeutic uses.
