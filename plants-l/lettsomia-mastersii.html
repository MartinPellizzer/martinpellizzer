Lettsomia mastersii

Lettsomia mastersii, commonly known as the golden star orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow petals and a distinctive star-shaped lip. The plant typically grows in cool, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to thrive. Lettsomia mastersii is noted for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is protected in many regions.
