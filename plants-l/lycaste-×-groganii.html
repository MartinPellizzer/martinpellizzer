Lycaste × groganii

Lycaste × groganii, commonly known as the Grogan's lycaste, is a hybrid orchid species that combines traits from its parent species, Lycaste meleagris and Lycaste skinneri.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in warm, humid climates with high light exposure. The plant features pseudobulbs that support its climbing habit, and it produces fragrant, showy flowers in shades of yellow and orange. Lycaste × groganii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.

It requires well-draining soil and regular watering to maintain optimal growth and blooming.
