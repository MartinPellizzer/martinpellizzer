Lycopodium planum

Lycopodium planum, commonly known as the ground pine, is a low-growing evergreen plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae and is characterized by its compact, carpet-like growth habit. The plant features small, scale-like leaves that are typically dark green and arranged in a dense, mat-forming structure. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky areas.

Lycopodium planum is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to form dense ground cover.
