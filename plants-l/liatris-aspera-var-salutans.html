Liatris aspera var. salutans

Liatris aspera var. salutans, commonly known as the rough blazing star, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescence composed of purple to pinkish-red flowers. The plant has hairy, linear leaves that are often gray-green in color, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is adapted to well-drained soils and is often found in prairies, meadows, and open woods.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
