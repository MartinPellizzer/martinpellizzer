Lupinus cesaranus

Lupinus cesaranus, commonly known as the Sierra lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of pea-like flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in open habitats such as meadows and slopes. Lupinus cesaranus is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with soil bacteria.

Its ecological importance includes providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for various wildlife species.
