Lepanthes guanacastensis

Lepanthes guanacastensis, commonly known as the Guanacaste orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers that typically feature a white or pale pink coloration with intricate patterns. The plant grows as an epiphyte, often found on trees in humid tropical forests. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, adapted to capture moisture from the air.

Lepanthes guanacastensis plays a role in the ecosystem by providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
