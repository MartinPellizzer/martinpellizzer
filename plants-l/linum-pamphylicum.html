Linum pamphylicum

Linum pamphylicum, commonly known as the Pamphylian flax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the genus Linum, which includes various species of flax known for their blue or white flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is characterized by its slender stems and small, delicate flowers. It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands.

Linum pamphylicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical significance in traditional herbal medicine.
