Lagochilus chingii

Lagochilus chingii, commonly known as the Chinese thyme, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by aromatic essential oils and square stems. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, often with a strong, mint-like fragrance.

Lagochilus chingii is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses in Chinese herbal medicine.
