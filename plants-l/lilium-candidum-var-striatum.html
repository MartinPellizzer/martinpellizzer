Lilium candidum var. striatum

Lilium candidum var. striatum, commonly known as the white lily with streaked petals, is a cultivar of the white lily native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its pale white flowers with distinct, narrow streaks of a darker hue, often pink or red, running along the petals. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring upright stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. It blooms in late spring to early summer, producing fragrant, showy flowers that are popular in gardens and floral arrangements.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its delicate appearance and historical significance in horticulture.
