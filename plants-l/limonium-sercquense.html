Limonium sercquense

Limonium sercquense, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiky, blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has succulent, silver-gray leaves that help it retain moisture in coastal environments. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in maritime climates.

Limonium sercquense is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate salt spray.
