Lastrea notabilis

Lastrea notabilis, commonly known as the noble lastrea, is a rare and striking plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has a succulent structure, allowing it to store water in arid environments. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine, fuzzy texture.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Lastrea notabilis is considered endangered and is of great interest to botanists and conservationists.
