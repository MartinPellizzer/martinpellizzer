Luina hypoleuca

Luina hypoleuca, commonly known as the white-flowered larkspur, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, showy white or pale blue flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. Luina hypoleuca thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

It is often found in meadows and open woodlands, where it contributes to the local floral diversity.
