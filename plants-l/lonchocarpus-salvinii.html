Lonchocarpus salvinii

Lonchocarpus salvinii, commonly known as the purple bean or salvinii bean, is a tropical leguminous plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive purple or violet flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is part of the Fabaceae family.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
