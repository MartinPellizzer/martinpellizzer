Lamium moschatum subsp. moschatum

Lamium moschatum subsp.

moschatum, commonly known as sweet Cicely, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe. It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a distinct, spicy aroma.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or white, arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its aromatic foliage and delicate blossoms make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
