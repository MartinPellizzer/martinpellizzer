Linum transsilvanicum

Linum transsilvanicum, commonly known as the Transylvanian flax, is a flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and delicate blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its seeds are small and oval.

Linum transsilvanicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
