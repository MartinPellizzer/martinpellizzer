Lepanthes hispidosa

Lepanthes hispidosa, commonly known as the hairy lepanthes, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact, terrestrial growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The plant has fleshy, hairy leaves that help it retain moisture in its often arid or shaded habitats. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Lepanthes hispidosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance.
