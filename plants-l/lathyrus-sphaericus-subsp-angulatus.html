Lathyrus sphaericus subsp. angulatus

Lathyrus sphaericus subsp.

angulatus, commonly known as the angular globeflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe. It is characterized by its distinctive spherical, seed-like flowers that are typically purple or violet in color.

The plant produces small, angular pods that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
