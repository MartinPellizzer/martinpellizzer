Leobordea cytisoides

Leobordea cytisoides, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces seed heads that resemble small crowns, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
