Lochia bracteata

Lochia bracteata, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain numerous seeds.

Lochia bracteata is often found in disturbed habitats and is known for its rapid growth and adaptability.
