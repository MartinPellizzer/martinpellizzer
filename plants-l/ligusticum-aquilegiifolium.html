Ligusticum aquilegiifolium

Ligusticum aquilegiifolium, commonly known as the heartleaf angelica, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed, resembling the petals of a columbine flower. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in umbels, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its roots are aromatic and have been used in traditional herbal medicine.

The species is characterized by its vigorous growth and ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments.
