Lythrum purpureum

Lythrum purpureum, commonly known as purple loosestrife, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features upright, square stems that are often reddish in color, and it produces spikes of showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped, opposite leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly serrated edge. Its roots are fibrous and can spread aggressively, making it an invasive species in some regions.

Lythrum purpureum is commonly found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams.
