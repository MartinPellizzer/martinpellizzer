Lathyrus montanus f. emarginatus

Lathyrus montanus f. emarginatus, commonly known as the emarginated mountain vetch, is a botanical variant of the mountain vetch species.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with the terminal leaflet often having a notched or emarginated appearance, giving the plant its specific epithet. It is native to certain regions of Europe and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, contributing to its ornamental value in horticulture.
