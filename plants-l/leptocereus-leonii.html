Leptocereus leonii

Leptocereus leonii, commonly known as the Leon's cholla, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a spineless, columnar cactus that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant has a dense, branching structure with a ribbed stem that can reach up to 3 meters in height. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange and bloom in the spring.

Leptocereus leonii is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and low water availability, making it a resilient plant in desert ecosystems.
