Linnaea borealis f. concolor

Linnaea borealis f. concolor, commonly known as the white twinflower, is a rare and striking variant of the twinflower plant.

It is characterized by its small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is native to the boreal forests of North America and parts of Eurasia. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded, forested areas.

Its unique appearance and limited distribution make it a notable species in botanical studies.
