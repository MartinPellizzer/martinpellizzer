Lychnis dioica var. campestris

Lychnis dioica var. campestris, commonly known as the field sandwort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Carnation family (Caryophyllaceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pink or red flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds.

The species is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
