Leucanthemum persicifolium

Leucanthemum persicifolium, commonly known as the Persian buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of silvery-white, woolly leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its flowers are composed of ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
