Liparis imperatifolia

Liparis imperatifolia, commonly known as the imperial orchid, is a species of orchid native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elegant, slender stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Liparis imperatifolia is considered a conservation concern in many areas.
