Lactuca saligna var. ruppiana

Lactuca saligna var. ruppiana, commonly known as the Rupp's sowthistle, is a variant of the common sowthistle.

It is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in a loose cluster, and it produces achene seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This variety is often considered a weed but has some medicinal uses in traditional herbal medicine.
