Launaea massauensis

Launaea massauensis, commonly known as the African dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant is herbaceous, with a sprawling growth habit and hairy, lobed leaves. It produces yellow flowers that develop into fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds, aiding in its propagation across dry landscapes.

Launaea massauensis is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.
