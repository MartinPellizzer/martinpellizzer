Linum goulimyi

Linum goulimyi, commonly known as the Chinese flax or yellow flax, is a flowering plant native to China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Linum goulimyi is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine.

Its seeds are also harvested for industrial purposes, similar to other flax species.
