Lepanthes pteroglossa

Lepanthes pteroglossa, commonly known as the feather-lobed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers that typically feature white or pale pink petals and sepals. The plant grows as an epiphyte, often found on trees in humid tropical forests. Its leaves are usually dark green and strap-shaped, providing a striking contrast to its floral display.

Lepanthes pteroglossa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
