Leptospermum javanicum

Leptospermum javanicum, commonly known as the Java tea tree, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in spring. Its aromatic leaves have been traditionally used in herbal remedies, and the plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal.

Leptospermum javanicum prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in tropical and subtropical climates.
