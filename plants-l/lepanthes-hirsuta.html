Lepanthes hirsuta

Lepanthes hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy slipper orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, hairy, violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in the understory of tropical forests. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the forest floor.

Lepanthes hirsuta is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
