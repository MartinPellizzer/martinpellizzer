Larix gmelinii f. hsinganica

Larix gmelinii f. hsinganica, commonly known as the Hsingan larch, is a subspecies of the larch tree native to northeastern China.

It is characterized by its conical shape and needle-like leaves that turn golden in the fall. The tree produces small, woody cones that contain seeds, typical of the coniferous family. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens and parks.
