Lupinus sativus

Lupinus sativus, commonly known as the scarlet lupin, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of vibrant red or pink flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces elongated pods containing several seeds. Lupinus sativus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in ecological restoration due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a suitable choice for gardeners and landscapers.
