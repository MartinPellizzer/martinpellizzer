Lamium plebeium

Lamium plebeium, commonly known as Greater Dead-nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces small, pale purple to pink flowers in whorled clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs.

Lamium plebeium is often used in traditional medicine for its purported soothing and anti-inflammatory properties.
