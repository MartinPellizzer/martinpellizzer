Lepanthes uvallensis

Lepanthes uvallensis, commonly known as the Violet Orchid of the Andes, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the cloud forests of Central America, particularly in countries like Costa Rica and Panama. The plant is characterized by its small, brightly colored flowers that typically range from violet to purple. Lepanthes uvallensis thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
