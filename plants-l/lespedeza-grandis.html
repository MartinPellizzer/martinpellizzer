Lespedeza grandis

Lespedeza grandis, commonly known as largeleaf lespeza, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in open woodlands, stream banks, and disturbed sites.

Lespedeza grandis is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
