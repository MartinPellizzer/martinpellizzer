Lucaea schimperi

Lucaea schimperi, commonly known as the Ethiopian violet, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its small, showy violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showing adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, while its stems are often hairy.

Lucaea schimperi is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
