Layia glandulosa var. heterotricha

Layia glandulosa var. heterotricha, commonly known as the roughleaf golden aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy, rough-textured leaves, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and produces small, composite flower heads.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to arid environments and suitable for xeriscaping.
