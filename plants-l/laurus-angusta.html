Laurus angusta

Laurus angusta, commonly known as the narrow-leafed laurel, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dark green, glossy, narrow leaves that are aromatic and often used in culinary applications. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible but not commonly consumed. Laurus angusta thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a versatile plant for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.
