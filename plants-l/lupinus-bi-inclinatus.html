Lupinus bi-inclinatus

Lupinus bi-inclinatus, commonly known as the bentleaf lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves that are often curved or bent, giving it its common name. The plant produces spires of pea-like flowers in shades of purple, pink, or white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Lupinus bi-inclinatus is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is often found in coastal and inland grasslands.

It plays an important role in soil stabilization and is a valuable plant for ecological restoration projects.
