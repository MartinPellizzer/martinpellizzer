Leontodon taraxacon

Leontodon taraxacon, commonly known as the common dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves and a long, hollow stem that supports a flowering head. The plant produces a cluster of small, yellow flowers that develop into fluffy white seeds with parachute-like structures for wind dispersal. It is widely distributed across temperate regions and is often considered a weed in gardens and lawns.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
