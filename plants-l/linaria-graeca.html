Linaria graeca

Linaria graeca, commonly known as Greek toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and vibrant yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Linaria graeca is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained conditions.
