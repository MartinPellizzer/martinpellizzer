Laphamia coronopifolia

Laphamia coronopifolia, commonly known as the whiteleaf lichen, is a foliose lichen that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is characterized by its pale green to white thallus, which is covered in a layer of fine, powdery granules. The plant forms a symbiotic relationship with algae, which provides it with nutrients through photosynthesis. It is typically found on tree trunks and rocks in temperate regions.

Due to its delicate structure, it is sensitive to environmental changes and is often used as an indicator of air quality.
