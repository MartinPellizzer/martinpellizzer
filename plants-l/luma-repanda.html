Luma repanda

Luma repanda, commonly known as the silver flame tree, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its striking silver-blue flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a vibrant touch to its tropical habitat. The plant has palmate leaves with a soft, velvety texture, and its bark is often covered in a fine, powdery coating. Luma repanda thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

It is also valued for its ornamental beauty and has cultural significance in some island communities.
