Limonium carolinianum var. obtusilobum

Limonium carolinianum var. obtusilobum, commonly known as Carolina sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in sandy or gravelly soils. It is often found in dunes and salt marshes, where it tolerates saline and well-drained conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing coastal habitats.
