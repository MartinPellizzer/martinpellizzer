Linum platyphyllum subsp. platyphyllum

Linum platyphyllum subsp.

platyphyllum, commonly known as the flat-leaved flax, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy.

The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers clustered in loose spikes, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in fiber production, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
