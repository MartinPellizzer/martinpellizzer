Lilium punctatum

Lilium punctatum, commonly known as the spotted lily, is a species of flowering plant in the lily family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Russia and Mongolia. The plant features showy, white or pale pink flowers with distinctive red or purple spots on the petals. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a fan-like pattern, and it produces a tall stem with a single bloom.

Lilium punctatum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
