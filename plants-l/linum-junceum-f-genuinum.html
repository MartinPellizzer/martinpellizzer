Linum junceum f. genuinum

Linum junceum f. genuinum, commonly known as the true flax.

It is a botanical variety of the flax plant, characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to white flowers that bloom in clusters. It is native to regions of Europe and is often cultivated for its fibers, which are used in textile production.

This form is distinguished by its more compact growth habit compared to other flax varieties.
