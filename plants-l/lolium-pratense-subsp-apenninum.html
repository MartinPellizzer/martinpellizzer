Lolium pratense subsp. apenninum

Lolium pratense subsp.

apenninum, commonly known as the Apennine ryegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and dense, fine-textured leaves.

The plant produces spikelets that are typically arranged in clusters, with each spikelet containing two to three small, awned lemmas. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated for landscaping purposes.
