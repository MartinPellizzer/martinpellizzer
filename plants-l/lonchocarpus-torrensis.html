Lonchocarpus torrensis

Lonchocarpus torrensis, commonly known as the torren's senna, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow flowers. The plant produces pods that contain seeds enclosed in a fleshy, red aril, which is a specialized structure that aids in seed dispersal. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
