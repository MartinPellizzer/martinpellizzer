Litwinowia tatarica

Litwinowia tatarica, commonly known as the Tatarian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It features showy blue or violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and ability to attract pollinators.
