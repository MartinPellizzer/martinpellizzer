Lasianthus cyanocarpus var. asperatus

Lasianthus cyanocarpus var. asperatus, commonly known as the rough-leaved blue-fruited lasianthus, is a species of flowering plant in the Acanthaceae family.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and small, clustered blue or purple flowers. The fruit is typically a capsule containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Native to tropical regions of Asia, it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

This plant is often found in forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
