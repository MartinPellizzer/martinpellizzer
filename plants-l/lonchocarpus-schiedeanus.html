Lonchocarpus schiedeanus

Lonchocarpus schiedeanus, commonly known as the soursop or custard apple, is a tropical plant native to the Americas.

It is a small tree or shrub that produces large, heart-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant is characterized by its distinctive fruit, which has a rough, spiny exterior and a sweet, aromatic pulp. It belongs to the legume family (Fabaceae) and is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
