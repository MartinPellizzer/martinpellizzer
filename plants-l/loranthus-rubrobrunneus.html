Loranthus rubrobrunneus

Loranthus rubrobrunneus, commonly known as the red-brown mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily in the families Myrtaceae and Fabaceae.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using the light it captures. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces small, red berries that are dispersed by birds. It is native to regions of South America, particularly in Brazil and Argentina, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can have significant impacts on the health of its host trees if left unchecked.
