Lindsaea leptophylla

Lindsaea leptophylla, commonly known as the slender-leaf lindsaea, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family, characterized by its delicate, fern-like appearance.

It is an epiphytic plant, often found growing on trees in tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in Southeast Asia. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves that are often clustered in dense, overlapping rosettes. Its reproductive structures, known as sorus, are typically located on the underside of the fronds.

Lindsaea leptophylla thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires high levels of atmospheric moisture for optimal growth.
