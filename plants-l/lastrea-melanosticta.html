Lastrea melanosticta

Lastrea melanosticta, commonly known as the black-streaked milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that are pollinated by insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The latex sap of Lastrea melanosticta is milky and can be toxic to some animals.
