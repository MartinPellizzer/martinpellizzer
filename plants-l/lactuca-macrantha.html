Lactuca macrantha

Lactuca macrantha, commonly known as the giant lettuce or broadleaf lettuce, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family, Asteraceae.

It is native to the eastern United States and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, along stream banks, and in other damp habitats.

Lactuca macrantha is also known for its milky sap, which is a distinguishing feature of the genus Lactuca.
