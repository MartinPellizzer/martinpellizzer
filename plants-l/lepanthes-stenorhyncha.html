Lepanthes stenorhyncha

Lepanthes stenorhyncha, commonly known as the narrow-mouthed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers with a distinctive elongated lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are often white or pale yellow, and it blooms during the rainy season.

Lepanthes stenorhyncha is valued for its ornamental beauty and is a subject of interest in botanical research due to its unique floral structure.
