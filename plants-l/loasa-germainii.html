Loasa germainii

Loasa germainii, commonly known as the desert loasa, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh conditions. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of showy, pink to purple flowers during the spring and summer months. Its botanical features include a woody stem and a compact growth habit, which help it conserve water.

Loasa germainii plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in rocky soils and along streambanks.
