Lupsia galactites var. elegans

Lupsia galactites var. elegans, commonly known as the elegant milkweed, is a striking plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly hairy surface, and the plant produces follicles that contain the seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for gardens and natural habitats.

This species is also noted for its role in supporting local pollinators, particularly butterflies.
