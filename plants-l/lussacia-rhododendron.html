Lussacia rhododendron

Lussacia rhododendron, commonly known as the Chinese rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, humid climates. Its flowers range in color from white to pink and are often arranged in clusters.

Lussacia rhododendron is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
