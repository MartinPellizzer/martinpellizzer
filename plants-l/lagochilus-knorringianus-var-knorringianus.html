Lagochilus knorringianus var. knorringianus

Lagochilus knorringianus var. knorringianus, commonly known as the Knorring's rock hyacinth, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, tubular blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
