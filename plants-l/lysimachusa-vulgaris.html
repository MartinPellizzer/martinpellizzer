Lysimachusa vulgaris

Lysimachusa vulgaris, commonly known as the Chinese water chestnut, is a herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its underground tubers, which are edible and have a nutty flavor. The plant typically grows in wet, marshy environments and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, while its flowers are small and clustered in spikes.

Lysimachusa vulgaris is valued both for its culinary uses and its ornamental appeal in gardens.
