Linaria aeruginea subsp. cardonica

Linaria aeruginea subsp.

cardonica, commonly known as the Italian toadflax, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, linear to lanceolate leaves.

The plant produces bright yellow flowers with two lips, typical of the Scrophulariaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens but is considered invasive in some areas.
