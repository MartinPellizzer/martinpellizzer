Lychnis githago var. nicaensis

Lychnis githago var. nicaensis, commonly known as the Nicaean catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pinkish-red flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
