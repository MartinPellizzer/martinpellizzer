Lomaria gayana

Lomaria gayana, commonly known as the yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.
