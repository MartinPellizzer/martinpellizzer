Lagerstroemia anisoptera

Lagerstroemia anisoptera, commonly known as the Chinese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant, pale purple to white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces vibrant purple to red berries that persist into winter, providing food for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a smooth texture and a glossy appearance.

Lagerstroemia anisoptera is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
