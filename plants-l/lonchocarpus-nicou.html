Lonchocarpus nicou

Lonchocarpus nicou, commonly known as the purple bean or nicou tree, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and produces distinctive pods containing edible seeds. The plant is often cultivated for its nutritional value, as the seeds are a good source of protein. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Additionally, it has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
