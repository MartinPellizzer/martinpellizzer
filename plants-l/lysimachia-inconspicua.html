Lysimachia inconspicua

Lysimachia inconspicua, commonly known as the creeping Jenny, is a low-growing perennial herb native to North America.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, often forming dense clusters along its stems. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Lysimachia inconspicua is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wet areas.
