Limnophila chamaedrifolia

Limnophila chamaedrifolia, commonly known as the creeping water plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of streams. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette and are often covered in a fine, silky texture. Its small, white or pale pink flowers are clustered in spikes and bloom during the spring and summer months.

Limnophila chamaedrifolia plays an important ecological role by stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.
