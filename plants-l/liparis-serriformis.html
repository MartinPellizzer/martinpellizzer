Liparis serriformis

Liparis serriformis, commonly known as the serrated orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with each pseudobulb producing a single leaf and a flower spike. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with intricate patterns, and they bloom during the spring or early summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
