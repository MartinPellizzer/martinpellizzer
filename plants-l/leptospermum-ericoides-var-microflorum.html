Leptospermum ericoides var. microflorum

Leptospermum ericoides var. microflorum, commonly known as the dwarf tea tree, is a low-growing shrub native to the coastal regions of southeastern Australia.

It features small, narrow leaves that are typically covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is well adapted to sandy soils and can tolerate salt spray, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
