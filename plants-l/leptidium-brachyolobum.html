Leptidium brachyolobum

Leptidium brachyolobum, commonly known as the short-lobed bladderwort, is a small aquatic plant belonging to the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its tiny, bladder-like structures called pseudocarps, which are used for trapping small aquatic organisms. The plant typically grows in shallow, freshwater habitats such as ponds and ditches. It has small, green, egg-shaped leaves and produces small white or yellow flowers.

Leptidium brachyolobum is often found in temperate regions and is adapted to nutrient-rich waters.
