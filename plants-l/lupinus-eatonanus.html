Lupinus eatonanus

Lupinus eatonanus, commonly known as Eaton's lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.

Lupinus eatonanus plays an important role in nitrogen fixation, benefiting the surrounding ecosystem.
