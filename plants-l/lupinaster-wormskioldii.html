Lupinaster wormskioldii

Lupinaster wormskioldii, commonly known as the Antarctic lupin, is a flowering plant native to the subantarctic regions of New Zealand.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds.

It thrives in coastal environments and is adapted to the harsh climatic conditions of its native habitat.
