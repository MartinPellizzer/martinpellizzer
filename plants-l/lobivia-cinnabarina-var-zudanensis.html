Lobivia cinnabarina var. zudanensis

Lobivia cinnabarina var. zudanensis, commonly known as the Zudanensis Lobivia, is a compact, slow-growing cactus native to southern Africa.

It features a short, thick stem with prominent, ribbed structures that help it store water. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, typical of many desert-adapted cacti.

This variety is well-suited for rock gardens and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
