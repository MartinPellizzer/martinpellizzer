Lathyrus multiceps f. eurypetalus

Lathyrus multiceps f. eurypetalus, commonly known as the broad-petaled lathyrus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces showy, pea-like flowers in shades of pink to purple. Its blooming period typically occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
