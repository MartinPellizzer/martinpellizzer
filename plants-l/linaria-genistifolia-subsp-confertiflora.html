Linaria genistifolia subsp. confertiflora

Linaria genistifolia subsp.

confertiflora, commonly known as the clustered toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The leaves are typically narrow and opposite, with a slightly hairy texture. This subspecies is adapted to rocky and sandy soils, often growing in open habitats such as coastal areas and dry meadows.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
