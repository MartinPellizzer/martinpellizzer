Lepanthes lynniana

Lepanthes lynniana, commonly known as the Lynn's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid with a pseudobulb and slender, strap-shaped leaves. The plant produces delicate, showy flowers that are typically white or pale pink with intricate patterns. Lepanthes lynniana thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
