Liparis panduriformis

Liparis panduriformis, commonly known as the panduriform orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, rhizomatous growth habit and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understoreys. Its leaves are usually elongated and grass-like, and it has a distinctive pseudobulb structure.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in horticulture.
