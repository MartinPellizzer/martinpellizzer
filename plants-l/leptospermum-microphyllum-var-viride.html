Leptospermum microphyllum var. viride

Leptospermum microphyllum var. viride, commonly known as the green-leaved tea tree, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its small, glossy, dark green leaves that are densely packed along the stems. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, which are typically followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological habitats.
