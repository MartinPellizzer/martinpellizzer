Lepanthes hamulifera

Lepanthes hamulifera, commonly known as the hanging orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, pendulous flowers that often hang from the stems, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as rainforests, where it attaches itself to trees or other vegetation. Its flowers are usually small and brightly colored, ranging from white to pink or purple, and are pollinated by various insects and birds.

Lepanthes hamulifera is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts of tropical flora.
