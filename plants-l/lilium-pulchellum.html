Lilium pulchellum

Lilium pulchellum, commonly known as the showy lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that bloom in shades of white, pink, and red. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a fan-like pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Lilium pulchellum is valued for its beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
