Leucophae dendrochahorra

Leucophae dendrochahorra, commonly known as the silverwood tree, is a rare and unique species native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its striking silvery-white leaves and its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils. The plant has a slow growth rate and can live for several decades, making it a symbol of resilience in harsh environments. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically blooming in the spring, and it produces woody fruits that contain seeds.
