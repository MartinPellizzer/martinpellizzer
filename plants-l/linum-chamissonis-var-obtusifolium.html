Linum chamissonis var. obtusifolium

Linum chamissonis var. obtusifolium, commonly known as the blunt-leaf flax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves with rounded tips. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or inland dune environments.

Linum chamissonis var. obtusifolium is a member of the Linaceae family and is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates.
