Laetia lucida

Laetia lucida, commonly known as the golden flame orchid, is a strikingly beautiful flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, golden-yellow petals and elongated, spiky sepals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in late spring and early summer, releasing a subtle, sweet fragrance that attracts pollinators such as bees and moths.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, Laetia lucida is a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists alike.
