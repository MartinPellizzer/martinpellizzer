Lavandula latifolia

Lavandula latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf lavender, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, silver-green foliage and spikes of violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and compact, upright growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal use.

Its essential oils are valued for their calming properties and are widely used in aromatherapy and cosmetics.
