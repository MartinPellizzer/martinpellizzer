Laelia anceps subsp. hollidayana

Laelia anceps subsp.

hollidayana, commonly known as the Holliday's Laelia, is a hybrid orchid species native to Mexico. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, white, and yellow.

The plant typically blooms during the winter months, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation. Its pseudobulbs are stout and covered with a dense layer of white tomentum, which helps in water retention.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
