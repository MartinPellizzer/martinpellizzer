Lathyrus sylvestris var. heterophyllus

Lathyrus sylvestris var. heterophyllus, commonly known as the variable wood vetch, is a perennial legume native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaves, which vary in shape and size, giving it its varietal name "heterophyllus.". The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in woodland and hedgerow environments, often growing in partial shade.

This species is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a food source for various insects and birds.
