Linum mucronatum subsp. armenum

Linum mucronatum subsp.

armenum, commonly known as Armenian flax, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region. It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Its ornamental value and historical significance in textile production make it a notable species in botanical gardens and ecological studies.
