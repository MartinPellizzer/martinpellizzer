Laterifissum rivulare

Laterifissum rivulare, commonly known as the riverweed, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, thread-like leaves that grow along the stems. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often near water sources. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and wetlands.

Its botanical structure is adapted to aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats, with roots that help stabilize the soil and prevent erosion.
