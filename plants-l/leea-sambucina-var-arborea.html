Leea sambucina var. arborea

Leea sambucina var. arborea, commonly known as the tree elderberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to temperate regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale green flowers in spring, followed by edible berries that ripen to a dark purple color in late summer. The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
