Lepanthes sericinitens

Lepanthes sericinitens, commonly known as the silver-tipped orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, pale green to white flowers with a distinctive silvery sheen. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in the understory of tropical forests. Its leaves are usually elongated and glossy, contributing to its overall ornamental appeal.

Lepanthes sericinitens is valued in horticulture for its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
