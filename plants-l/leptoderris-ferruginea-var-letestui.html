Leptoderris ferruginea var. letestui

Leptoderris ferruginea var. letestui, commonly known as the red-stemmed ironwood, is a shrub or small tree native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its dark, fissured bark and dense, evergreen foliage. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a leathery texture with a bluish-green hue. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by spherical, woody fruits.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens for its unique appearance and resilience.
