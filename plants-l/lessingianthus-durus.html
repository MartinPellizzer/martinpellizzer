Lessingianthus durus

Lessingianthus durus, commonly known as the rugged sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and other composite flowers. The plant is characterized by its robust, upright stems and large, bright yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of smaller flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
