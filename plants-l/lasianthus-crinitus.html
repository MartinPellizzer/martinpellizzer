Lasianthus crinitus

Lasianthus crinitus, commonly known as the hairy lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as an herb or shrub, often found in moist, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
