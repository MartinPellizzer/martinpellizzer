Lycium megistocarpum

Lycium megistocarpum, commonly known as the large-fruited boxthorn, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces bright orange-red berries that are edible when ripe. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in coastal regions.

Lycium megistocarpum has adapted to withstand drought and saline conditions, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
