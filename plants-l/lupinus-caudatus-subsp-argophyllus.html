Lupinus caudatus subsp. argophyllus

Lupinus caudatus subsp.

argophyllus, commonly known as the silverleaf lupine, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers.

The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, rounded leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological restoration and ornamental gardening.
