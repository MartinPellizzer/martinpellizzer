Lythrum pubescens

Lythrum pubescens, commonly known as purple loosestrife, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and spikes of showy, lavender-pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stem. Its root system is fibrous and can spread aggressively, making it an invasive species in some regions.

Lythrum pubescens thrives in moist soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.
