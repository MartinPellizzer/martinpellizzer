Lepechinia glomerata

Lepechinia glomerata, commonly known as clustered lepechinia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as meadows and slopes. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence.

Lepechinia glomerata is known for its medicinal properties and has been used by indigenous peoples for various therapeutic purposes.
