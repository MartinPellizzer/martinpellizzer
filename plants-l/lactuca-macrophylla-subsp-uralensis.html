Lactuca macrophylla subsp. uralensis

Lactuca macrophylla subsp.

uralensis, commonly known as the Ural lettuce, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a loose panicle, which bloom during the summer months. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some historical use in traditional medicine.
