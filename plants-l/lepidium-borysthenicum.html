Lepidium borysthenicum

Lepidium borysthenicum, commonly known as the Crimean cress, is a flowering plant native to the steppes of southern Russia and Ukraine.

It is a low-growing annual herb with small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has slender, branched stems and produces small, oval-shaped seeds in pods. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open grasslands.

Botanically, it is characterized by its simple leaves and is part of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard family plants.
