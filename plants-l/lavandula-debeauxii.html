Lavandula debeauxii

Lavandula debeauxii, commonly known as the narrowleaf lavender, is a species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and spikes of purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. Its essential oils, rich in linalool and lavandin, are widely used in aromatherapy and perfumery.

Lavandula debeauxii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
