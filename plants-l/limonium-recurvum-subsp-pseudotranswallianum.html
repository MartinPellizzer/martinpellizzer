Limonium recurvum subsp. pseudotranswallianum

Limonium recurvum subsp.

pseudotranswallianum, commonly known as the Transwallian sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, showy blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in maritime environments. It has narrow, linear leaves that are adapted to withstand salt spray and strong coastal winds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with saline conditions.
