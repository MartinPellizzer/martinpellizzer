Leucas macrantha

Leucas macrantha, commonly known as the blue leucas, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often aromatic and have been used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Leucas macrantha is typically found in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils.
