Lobelia cardinalis var. alba

Lobelia cardinalis var. alba, commonly known as white cardinalflower, is a striking perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, tubular white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and butterflies. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 3 feet, with lance-shaped, dark green leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
