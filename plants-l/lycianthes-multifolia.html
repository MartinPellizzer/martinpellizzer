Lycianthes multifolia

Lycianthes multifolia, commonly known as the many-leaved physalis, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to the Americas, particularly found in regions of Mexico and Central America. The plant is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and clusters of small, yellowish flowers that develop into orange-red, lantern-like fruits. The leaves are typically lobed and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has also been used in traditional medicine.
