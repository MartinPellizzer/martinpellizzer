Lotus argophyllus var. fremontii

Lotus argophyllus var. fremontii, commonly known as the Fremont's lotus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features palmate leaves with five to seven leaflets, often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in arid and semi-arid regions.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to drought conditions and suitable for xeriscaping.
