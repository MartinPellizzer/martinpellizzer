Limonium gougetianum var. multiceps

Limonium gougetianum var. multiceps, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, often appearing in clusters atop slender, leafless stems. The plant is typically found in coastal areas, gravelly soils, and dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its long-lasting blooms.
