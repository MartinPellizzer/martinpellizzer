Linum narbonense

Linum narbonense, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and delicate blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas. It is an annual herb that can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters.

Its fibrous stems and leaves have been historically used for textile production, similar to other flax species.
