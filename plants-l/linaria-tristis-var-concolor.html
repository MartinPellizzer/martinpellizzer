Linaria tristis var. concolor

Linaria tristis var. concolor, commonly known as the narrowleaf toadflax, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, often with a bluish-green hue. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to various arid and semi-arid environments.

This plant is often found in rocky slopes and open woodlands, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
