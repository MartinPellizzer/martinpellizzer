Lobelia decipiens

Lobelia decipiens, commonly known as the Indian Tobacco, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, tubular blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture and a distinct aromatic scent. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Lobelia decipiens is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
