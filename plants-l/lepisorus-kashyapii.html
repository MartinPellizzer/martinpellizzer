Lepisorus kashyapii

Lepisorus kashyapii, commonly known as the Kashyapii polypody, is a fern species native to the Himalayan region.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories and rocky crevices. Its spores are released from small, clustered sporangia located on the underside of the fronds.

Lepisorus kashyapii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shady gardens.
