Lonicera caerulea canadensis

Lonicera caerulea canadensis, commonly known as the Canadian honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and have serrated edges. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to yellow, often appearing in clusters. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that ripens in late summer and is edible for both humans and wildlife.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
