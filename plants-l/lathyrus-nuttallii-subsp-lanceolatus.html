Lathyrus nuttallii subsp. lanceolatus

Lathyrus nuttallii subsp.

lanceolatus, commonly known as the lanceleaf sweet pea, is a flowering plant native to North America. It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves.

The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pea-like flowers in shades of pink and white. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in temperate climates.
