Loasa bergii

Loasa bergii, commonly known as the silverleaf loasa, is a shrub native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which results from dense, soft pubescence covering its leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in the spring. Loasa bergii is often found in rocky or dry habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an attractive ornamental plant in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
