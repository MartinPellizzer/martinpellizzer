Lysias bifolia

Lysias bifolia, commonly known as the two-leaved lysias, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its natural habitat.
