Lebeckia cuspidosa

Lebeckia cuspidosa, commonly known as the Cape ironweed, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright, branching stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal landscapes.

Lebeckia cuspidosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
