Lomariopsis longicaudata

Lomariopsis longicaudata, commonly known as the long-tailed fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its long, slender fronds that can grow up to several meters in length, giving it a dramatic and graceful appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories where it thrives in high humidity. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it to rocky or soil substrates, while its spore-producing structures are located on the underside of its fronds.

Lomariopsis longicaudata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to add a lush, tropical feel to gardens and indoor spaces.
