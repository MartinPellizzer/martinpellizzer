Lobelia cordata

Lobelia cordata, commonly known as heartleaf lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features small, tubular blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Lobelia cordata is often used in woodland gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
