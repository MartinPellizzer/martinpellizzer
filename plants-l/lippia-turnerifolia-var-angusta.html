Lippia turnerifolia var. angusta

Lippia turnerifolia var. angusta, commonly known as narrowleaf lippia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. It is a perennial herb with a woody stem that can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
