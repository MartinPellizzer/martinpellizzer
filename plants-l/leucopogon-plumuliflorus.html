Leucopogon plumuliflorus

Leucopogon plumuliflorus, commonly known as the silky-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, often resembling a plume or feather, which gives it its species name *plumuliflorus*. Its leaves are typically covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or pine forests.
