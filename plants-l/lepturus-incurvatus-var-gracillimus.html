Lepturus incurvatus var. gracillimus

Lepturus incurvatus var. gracillimus, commonly known as the slender thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, erect stems that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, spherical heads, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with serrated edges and a hairy surface.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.
