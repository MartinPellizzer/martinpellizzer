Larix larix f. pendula

Larix larix f. pendula, commonly known as the weeping larch, is a unique variant of the larch tree characterized by its pendulous, drooping branches.

This decorative form exhibits a distinctive cascading appearance, with needle-like leaves that create a soft, feathery texture. The plant is native to the northern regions of Europe and Asia, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. It produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and parks for its striking visual appeal.
