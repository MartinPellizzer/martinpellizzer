Linum gyaricum subsp. gyaricum

Linum gyaricum subsp.

gyaricum, commonly known as the Gyaric flax, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers with five petals, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
