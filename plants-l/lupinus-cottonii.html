Lupinus cottonii

Lupinus cottonii, commonly known as the cottonwood lupine, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as deserts and rocky soils. The plant produces tall, spindly stems with clusters of bright blue or purple pea-like flowers. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Lupinus cottonii is adapted to withstand drought conditions and is often found in areas with poor soil quality.
