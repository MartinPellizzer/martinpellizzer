Leucopogon dielsianus

Leucopogon dielsianus, commonly known as the Diels' heath, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of southern Australia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heaths and heather. The plant typically grows in dense, shrub-like formations, with small, leathery leaves and clusters of pink or white flowers. It is well adapted to nutrient-poor soils and is often found in swampy or seasonally waterlogged areas.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
