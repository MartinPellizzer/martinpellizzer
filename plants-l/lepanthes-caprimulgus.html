Lepanthes caprimulgus

Lepanthes caprimulgus, commonly known as the moon orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often nocturnal blooming habits and its unique, moon-like appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments such as cloud forests. Its flowers are usually fragrant and attract nocturnal pollinators like moths.

Lepanthes caprimulgus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
