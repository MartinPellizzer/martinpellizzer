Lantana undulata var. grandiflora

Lantana undulata var. grandiflora, commonly known as the giant lantana, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange and red. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces berries that are typically red when mature. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

However, it can be invasive in some areas and may pose a threat to native ecosystems.
