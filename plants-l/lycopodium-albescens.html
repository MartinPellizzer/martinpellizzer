Lycopodium albescens

Lycopodium albescens, commonly known as the white clubmoss, is a small, evergreen fern-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and scale-like leaves. The plant produces spore-bearing structures called strobili, which are clustered at the tips of its stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Lycopodium albescens has a slow growth rate and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
