Lophopetalum pedunculatum

Lophopetalum pedunculatum, commonly known as the Texas mountain laurel, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southern United States.

It features compound leaves composed of 8-20 oval-shaped leaflets, which are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces showy, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers in panicles during the spring. It is adapted to well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warmer climates.
