Limodorum imbricatum

Limodorum imbricatum, commonly known as the zigzag lily, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, spurred flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. The plant produces a single, showy bloom on a slender stem, which is often adorned with a distinctive zigzag pattern on its leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks, and is often found growing on rocky or humus-rich soils.

Limodorum imbricatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
