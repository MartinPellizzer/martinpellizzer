Lippia arechavaletae var. microphylla

Lippia arechavaletae var. microphylla, commonly known as the Mexican mint or mountain mint, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, clustered white or pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or rocky slopes. It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae), which is known for its fragrant foliage and medicinal properties.

The varietal name *microphylla* refers to its small leaves, distinguishing it from other varieties within the *Lippia* genus.
