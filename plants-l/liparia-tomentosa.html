Liparia tomentosa

Liparia tomentosa, commonly known as the wooly liparia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely hairy, gray-green foliage and small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers. Native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia, it thrives in moist, calcareous soils. The plant is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

Its delicate appearance and aromatic properties make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
