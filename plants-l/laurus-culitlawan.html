Laurus culitlawan

Laurus culitlawan, commonly known as the Philippine laurel, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other well-known plants like bay leaves and avocado. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by oval-shaped berries that are edible.

The tree is often valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
