Leptostachya lucida var. obtusifolia

Leptostachya lucida var. obtusifolia, commonly known as the glossy leaved grass, is a variant of the species Leptostachya lucida, native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, glossy green leaves that are often slightly rounded at the tips, giving it the varietal name *obtusifolia*. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, pale green to white flowers.

This grass is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in horticultural settings for its aesthetic qualities.
