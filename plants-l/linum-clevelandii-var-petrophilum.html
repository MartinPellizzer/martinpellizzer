Linum clevelandii var. petrophilum

Linum clevelandii var. petrophilum, commonly known as the Cleveland's flax, is a rare and endemic plant species native to the coastal regions of southern California.

It is characterized by its delicate, blue to violet flowers and linear, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along the shoreline, thriving in coastal climates with mild temperatures and moderate rainfall. It is a member of the Linaceae family, known for its fibrous seed capsules and historical use in textile production.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species requiring conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
