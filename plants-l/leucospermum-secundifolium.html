Leucospermum secundifolium

Leucospermum secundifolium, commonly known as the firewheel or yellow star flower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces bright yellow, star-shaped flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its botanical characteristics include a dense, compact growth habit and a preference for well-drained, sandy soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in coastal environments.
