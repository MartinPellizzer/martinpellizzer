Leontodon vernus

Leontodon vernus, commonly known as the spring gentian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture.

Leontodon vernus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
