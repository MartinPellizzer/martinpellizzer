Leucanthemum atratum subsp. coronopifolium

Leucanthemum atratum subsp.

coronopifolium, commonly known as the black-eyed Susan, is a flowering plant native to North America. It is characterized by its dark purple to black central disk surrounded by bright yellow ray florets.

The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and wildflower landscapes.

Its botanical name reflects its distinctive appearance and its classification within the Asteraceae family.
