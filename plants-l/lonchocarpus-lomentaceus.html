Lonchocarpus lomentaceus

Lonchocarpus lomentaceus, commonly known as the creeping cowpea, is a tropical shrub belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in moist, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. It is often found growing along riverbanks and in disturbed soils due to its preference for nutrient-rich environments.

The species is also valued for its potential in soil enrichment and as a forage crop.
