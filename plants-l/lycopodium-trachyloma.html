Lycopodium trachyloma

Lycopodium trachyloma, commonly known as the narrow-leaf clubmoss, is a small, evergreen fern-like plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its creeping rhizomes and small, scale-like leaves that form dense mats. The plant produces spore-bearing cones called strobili, which are typically clustered at the base of the foliage. Native to regions of southern Africa, it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Its unique morphology and ancient lineage make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
