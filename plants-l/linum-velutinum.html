Linum velutinum

Linum velutinum, commonly known as velvet flax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces showy blue to violet flowers. The plant has slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a soft, velvety texture. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Velvet flax is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including meadows and roadsides.
