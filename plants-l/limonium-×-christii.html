Limonium × christii

Limonium × christii, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in saline environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating.

Limonium × christii is widely cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
