Logania capensis

Logania capensis, commonly known as the Cape logania, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Loganiaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and showy, yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely distributed outside its native range.
