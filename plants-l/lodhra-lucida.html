Lodhra lucida

Lodhra lucida, commonly known as sacred fig or bodhi tree, is a species of the Ficus genus native to South and Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, featuring a broad, spreading canopy. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are smooth and glossy, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters. Its fruit is a small, round, greenish-yellow syconium, which turns brown when ripe.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and is widely used in traditional Ayurvedic medicine.
