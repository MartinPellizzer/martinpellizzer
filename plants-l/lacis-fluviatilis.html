Lacis fluviatilis

Lacis fluviatilis, commonly known as the river lacis, is a unique aquatic plant found in slow-moving freshwater environments.

It has long, slender leaves that float on the water surface, while its roots anchor it to the substrate. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically submerged, contributing to its reproductive cycle. Its structure allows it to absorb nutrients efficiently from the water, making it an important part of the aquatic ecosystem.

Due to its specialized adaptations, Lacis fluviatilis plays a role in maintaining water quality and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
