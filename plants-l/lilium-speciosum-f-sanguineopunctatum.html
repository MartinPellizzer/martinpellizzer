Lilium speciosum f. sanguineopunctatum

Lilium speciosum f. sanguineopunctatum, commonly known as the spotted red lily, is a striking variant of the species Lilium speciosum.

It is characterized by its vibrant red petals adorned with distinctive orange or reddish spots, which add to its ornamental appeal. The plant is a bulbous perennial that typically grows in temperate climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight. Its flowers bloom in late summer, producing a showy display that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This cultivar is prized in horticulture for its unique coloration and elegant floral structure.
