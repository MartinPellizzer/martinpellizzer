Liatris pycnostachya f. alba

Liatris pycnostachya f. alba, commonly known as the white blazing star, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, spires of white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in prairies, open woods, and disturbed sites. It is a perennial herb that thrives in full sun and well-drained soil.

Its botanical name reflects its dense staminal hairs and the white coloration of its flowers.
