Lindsaea kalimantanensis

Lindsaea kalimantanensis, commonly known as the Kalimantan lace fern, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family and is native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are often covered in fine, hair-like structures called trichomes. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks or branches in the canopy layer of the forest. Its rhizomes are short and thick, allowing it to anchor itself securely in its environment.

Lindsaea kalimantanensis plays a role in the ecological balance of its habitat by contributing to the biodiversity of the rainforest flora.
