Leptospermum epacridoideum

Leptospermum epacridoideum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tea tree, is a species of shrub native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalypts and other aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. It is often found in heathlands and open forests, thriving in well-drained soils.

The leaves are used in traditional Aboriginal medicine and are also valued for their aromatic properties.
