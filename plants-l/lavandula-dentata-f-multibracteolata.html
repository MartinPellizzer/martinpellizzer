Lavandula dentata f. multibracteolata

Lavandula dentata f. multibracteolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lavender, is a compact shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a silvery-gray underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces spikes of purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is valued for its aromatic foliage and is often used in gardens for its ornamental and aromatic qualities.

This variety is more cold-hardy than many other lavender types, making it suitable for a wider range of climates.
