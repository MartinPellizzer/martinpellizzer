Laurentia fluviatilis

Laurentia fluviatilis, commonly known as the river laurel, is a flowering plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often submerged or floating. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in spikes, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in slow-moving waters and is often found in marshes and along the edges of rivers.

Laurentia fluviatilis plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
