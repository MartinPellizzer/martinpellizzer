Lindera melastomacea

Lindera melastomacea, commonly known as the Florida melastoma, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as forest floors and wetlands. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and a distinctive, aromatic scent. Its showy, white to pink flowers bloom in late spring, followed by orange-red berries that are a food source for various birds.

Lindera melastomacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant landscapes.
