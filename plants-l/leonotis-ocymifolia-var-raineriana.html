Leonotis ocymifolia var. raineriana

Leonotis ocymifolia var. raineriana, commonly known as the Raineriana Lantana, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its clusters of tubular, salmon-pink to orange-red flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for coastal and Mediterranean climates.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
