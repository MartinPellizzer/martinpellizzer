Liparis eggersii

Liparis eggersii, commonly known as the Eggers' orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

Liparis eggersii is a relatively rare species, making it a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts.
