Lathyrus multijugus

Lathyrus multijugus, commonly known as the clustered vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features slender, branching stems and produces small, pea-like flowers in shades of purple or pink. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with oval-shaped leaflets.

Lathyrus multijugus is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and along coastal areas.
