Limniris hookeri

Limniris hookeri, commonly known as the Hooker's water lily, is a aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves that form a dense canopy on the water surface. The plant produces white, fragrant flowers that bloom in the early morning. Its roots are anchored in the mud at the bottom of ponds and lakes.

Limniris hookeri plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various water organisms.
