Leontodon hyoseroides var. pseudocrispus

Leontodon hyoseroides var. pseudocrispus, commonly known as the curly hair grass, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, finely divided, grass-like leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It is often found in regions with temperate climates and is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
