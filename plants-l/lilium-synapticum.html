Lilium synapticum

Lilium synapticum, commonly known as the Synaptic Lily, is a rare and striking member of the Liliaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Kyrgyzstan. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. Its foliage consists of long, narrow leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Lilium synapticum is considered a rare and endangered species.
