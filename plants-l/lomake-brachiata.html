Lomake brachiata

Lomake brachiata, commonly known as the hanging leaf plant, is a tropical climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, slender stems and heart-shaped, glossy leaves that often hang in clusters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers, followed by round, red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage.

Lomake brachiata requires support to climb and prefers partial shade to full sun.
