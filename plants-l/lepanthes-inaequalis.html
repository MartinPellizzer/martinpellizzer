Lepanthes inaequalis

Lepanthes inaequalis, commonly known as the unequal orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments within tropical forests. The plant features a slender rhizome and produces delicate, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. Its flowers are often fragrant and attract various pollinators such as bees and moths.

Lepanthes inaequalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
