Lindmania neogranatensis

Lindmania neogranatensis, commonly known as the Neogranatensis wax palm, is a species of palm native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant is known for its slow growth and ability to thrive in high-altitude environments. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts, and it holds cultural significance in some indigenous communities.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
