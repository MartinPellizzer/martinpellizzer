Leucas eenii

Leucas eenii, commonly known as the Indian chervil or narrowleaf horsegram, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by small, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It is often grown as a forage crop and has medicinal uses in traditional systems of medicine.

Leucas eenii thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.
