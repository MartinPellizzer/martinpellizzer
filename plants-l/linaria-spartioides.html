Linaria spartioides

Linaria spartioides, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is well adapted to arid environments. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Linaria spartioides is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
