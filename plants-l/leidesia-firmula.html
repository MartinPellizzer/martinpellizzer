Leidesia firmula

Leidesia firmula, commonly known as the slender leidesia, is a member of the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries.

It is a low-growing evergreen shrub characterized by its small, dark green leaves and bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. Native to the southeastern United States, it thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and partial shade. The plant is often found in moist, shaded woodlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
