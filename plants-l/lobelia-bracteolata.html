Lobelia bracteolata

Lobelia bracteolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf lobelia, is a herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces striking blue or purple tubular flowers. It is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.

Lobelia bracteolata is also a valuable nectar source for pollinators like bees and butterflies.
