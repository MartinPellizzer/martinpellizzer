Lopezia pauciflora

Lopezia pauciflora, commonly known as the few-flowered Lopezia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered inflorescences and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, dry fruits that contain seeds.

Lopezia pauciflora is often found in desert scrub and chaparral ecosystems.
