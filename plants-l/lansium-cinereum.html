Lansium cinereum

Lansium cinereum, commonly known as the hairy lansium, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and alternate, ovate leaves with hairy undersides. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into edible, sticky fruits with a sweet and sour flavor. It is often cultivated for its fruit, which is used in traditional cuisines and beverages.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in tropical landscapes.
