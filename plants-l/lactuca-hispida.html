Lactuca hispida

Lactuca hispida, commonly known as the hispid lettuce, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough leaves and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and prefers well-drained soils in full sun. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its milky sap.
