Lamium maculatum var. longifolium

Lamium maculatum var. longifolium, commonly known as the spotted dead nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves with distinctive red or purple markings, and it produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of pink or white. The plant thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks. It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.

The variety longifolium is characterized by its longer, more elongated leaves compared to other varieties of Lamium maculatum.
