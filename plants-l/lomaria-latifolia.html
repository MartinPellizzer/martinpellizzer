Lomaria latifolia

Lomaria latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf lomaria, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with lance-shaped leaves that are broad and fleshy. Its flowers are yellow and appear in clusters, blooming from late spring to early autumn.

Lomaria latifolia is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
