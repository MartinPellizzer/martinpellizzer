Lagenocarpus insignis

Lagenocarpus insignis, commonly known as the golden cup fern, is a tropical fern species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, cup-shaped fronds that are often bright yellow or golden in color. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its fronds are composed of multiple pinnae, which are arranged in a unique, fan-like pattern.

Lagenocarpus insignis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant foliage.
