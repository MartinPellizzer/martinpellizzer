Lagoseris taraxacoides

Lagoseris taraxacoides, commonly known as the false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are basal, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow and resemble those of true dandelions.

It is often mistaken for the common dandelion due to its similar appearance, but it lacks the characteristic puffball seeds.
