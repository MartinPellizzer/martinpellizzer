Leptacanthus

Leptacanthus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed daisy, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers with ray florets and a central disk of tubular flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and linear, often arranged alternately along the stem. Leptacanthus species are native to various regions in Africa, Asia, and Australia.
