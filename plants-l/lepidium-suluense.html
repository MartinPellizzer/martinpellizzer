Lepidium suluense

Lepidium suluense, commonly known as the Sulu cress, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia, particularly the Philippines.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and pinnately compound leaves. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions.

Lepidium suluense is sometimes cultivated for its edible leaves and has traditional uses in local cuisines.
