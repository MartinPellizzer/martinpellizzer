Linaria markotchensis

Linaria markotchensis, commonly known as the Armenian toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey.

It belongs to the genus Linaria in the family Plantaginaceae, characterized by its showy, often yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its narrow, linear leaves are arranged in a basal rosette, and it produces slender, branched stems that bear the colorful flowers.

Linaria markotchensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
