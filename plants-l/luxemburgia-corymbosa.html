Luxemburgia corymbosa

Luxemburgia corymbosa, commonly known as the clustered starflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Luxemburgia corymbosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
