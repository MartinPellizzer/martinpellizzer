Lilium liukiu

Lilium liukiu, commonly known as the Ryukyu lily, is a species of bulbous plant native to the Ryukyu Islands of Japan.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stem and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer, producing white or pinkish flowers that are often fragrant. Its leaves are long and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade.

Lilium liukiu is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
