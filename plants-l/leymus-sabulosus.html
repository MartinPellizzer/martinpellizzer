Leymus sabulosus

Leymus sabulosus, commonly known as sandberg bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal dunes and other dry, open habitats. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.

Its ecological importance includes stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for various small organisms.
