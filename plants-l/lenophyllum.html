Lenophyllum

These plants are characterized by their floating leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom above the water surface. Their roots are anchored in the mud at the bottom of ponds or lakes, while their leaves form a floating mat on the water's surface.

The flowers of Lenophyllum typically open in the morning and close in the late afternoon, a behavior known as nyctinasty.
