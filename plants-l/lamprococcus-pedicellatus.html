Lamprococcus pedicellatus

Lamprococcus pedicellatus, commonly known as the pedicellate lamprococcus, is a species of scale insect belonging to the family Coccidae.

It is characterized by its small, elongated body covered in a waxy, protective coating. The plant it infests is typically a member of the family Myrtaceae, such as eucalyptus trees. Its feeding habits cause visible damage to the foliage, leading to discoloration and reduced growth.

Due to its specific host preferences, it is often found in regions with a subtropical or tropical climate.
