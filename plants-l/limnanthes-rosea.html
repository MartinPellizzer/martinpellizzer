Limnanthes rosea

Limnanthes rosea, commonly known as the golden cupflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has a short, hairy stem and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Limnanthes rosea is a perennial species that is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits.
