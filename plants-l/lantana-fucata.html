Lantana fucata

Lantana fucata, commonly known as silverleaf lantana, is a perennial shrub native to the southern United States.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple, depending on the variety. The plant has aromatic, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

However, it can be invasive in some regions and may pose a risk to native ecosystems.
