Lobelia bialata

Lobelia bialata, commonly known as the Biala lobelia, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family, characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and wetlands. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.
