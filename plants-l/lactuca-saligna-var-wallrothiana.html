Lactuca saligna var. wallrothiana

Lactuca saligna var. wallrothiana, commonly known as the Wallroth's lettuce, is a variant of the prickly lettuce species.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy, lobed leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in a composite head, which are followed by small, spiny seed capsules. It is native to regions of southern Europe and parts of the Middle East.

This variety is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
