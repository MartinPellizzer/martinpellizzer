Leontodon pyrenaicus

Leontodon pyrenaicus, commonly known as the Pyrenean dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula and the Pyrenees.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Leontodon pyrenaicus is often found in open habitats such as alpine meadows and forest edges.
