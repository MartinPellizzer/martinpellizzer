Linaria striata

Linaria striata, commonly known as the striped toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a member of the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular, yellow-orange flowers with distinctive dark stripes. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves contribute to its ornamental appeal.

Linaria striata is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various growing conditions.
