Lapeirousia corymbosa

Lapeirousia corymbosa, commonly known as the Cape gold cup, is a striking member of the Iridaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that resemble miniature cups, typically blooming in late summer. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of flowers on tall, slender stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Lapeirousia corymbosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
