Ligustrum mayebaranum

Ligustrum mayebaranum, commonly known as the Mayebaran privet, is a species of flowering plant in the Oleaceae family.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. It typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters.

The fruit is a small, dark blue drupe that contains a single seed, and the plant is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
