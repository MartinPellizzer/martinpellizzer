Leptospermum pubescens var. nitidum

Leptospermum pubescens var. nitidum, commonly known as the silver tea tree, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its narrow, silvery-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or subalpine regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
