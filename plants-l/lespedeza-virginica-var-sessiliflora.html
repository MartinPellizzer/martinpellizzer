Lespedeza virginica var. sessiliflora

Lespedeza virginica var. sessiliflora, commonly known as narrowleaf bluecurls, is a perennial forb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as woodlands and grasslands. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its distinctive inflorescence consists of clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that resemble curls.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
