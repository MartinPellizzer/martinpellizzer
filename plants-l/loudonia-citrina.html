Loudonia citrina

Loudonia citrina, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a tropical climbing plant characterized by its striking yellow flowers that resemble small bells.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is native to regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, hairy texture. Its flowers are typically clustered in racemes and bloom during the warmer months.

Loudonia citrina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
