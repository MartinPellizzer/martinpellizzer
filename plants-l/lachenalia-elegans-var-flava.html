Lachenalia elegans var. flava

Lachenalia elegans var. flava, commonly known as the yellow lachenalia, is a striking member of the Amaryllidaceae family native to South Africa.

This perennial bulbous plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth. The plant is noted for its compact size and ornamental value, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its botanical characteristics reflect adaptations to its native arid environment, with a dormant period during the colder months.
