Landolphia rogersii

Landolphia rogersii, commonly known as the African morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces fleshy, berry-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and greenhouses.
