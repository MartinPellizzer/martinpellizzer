Luisia indica

Luisia indica, commonly known as the Indian lily, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to white. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its roots are fibrous and adapted for moisture retention.

Luisia indica is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
