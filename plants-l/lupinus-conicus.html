Lupinus conicus

Lupinus conicus, commonly known as the coneflower lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of violet or purple pea-like flowers. The plant has pinnately compound leaves and produces legume-like pods that contain several seeds. Lupinus conicus is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
