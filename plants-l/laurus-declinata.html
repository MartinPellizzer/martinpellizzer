Laurus declinata

Laurus declinata, commonly known as the drooping laurel, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other well-known plants like bay laurel and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its narrow, dark green leaves that are often curved or twisted, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Laurus declinata is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and aromatic properties.
