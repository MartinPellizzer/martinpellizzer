Lomatophyllum propaguliferum

Lomatophyllum propaguliferum, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers and silvery, succulent leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is adapted to saline and sandy soils. It produces small, rounded seed heads that are dispersed by the wind, aiding in its propagation.

Lomatophyllum propaguliferum is often used in coastal gardens for its ornamental value and hardiness.
