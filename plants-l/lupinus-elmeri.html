Lupinus elmeri

Lupinus elmeri, commonly known as Elmer's lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers. The plant has compound leaves made up of several small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Lupinus elmeri is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
