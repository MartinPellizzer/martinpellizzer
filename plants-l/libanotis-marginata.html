Libanotis marginata

Libanotis marginata, commonly known as the white margined bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with silver or white along the edges. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Libanotis marginata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
