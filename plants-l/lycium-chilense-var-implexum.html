Lycium chilense var. implexum

Lycium chilense var. implexum, commonly known as the Chilean boxthorn, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, demonstrating adaptability to harsh climatic conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
