Lonicera ruprechtiana var. xanthocarpa

Lonicera ruprechtiana var. xanthocarpa, commonly known as the yellow-fruited honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in spring. Its fruit is a small, yellow berry that ripens in late summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
