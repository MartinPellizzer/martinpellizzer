Lepanthes clandestina

Lepanthes clandestina, commonly known as the hidden orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that often have a strikingly intricate structure. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments such as rainforests. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insects.

Lepanthes clandestina is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its rarity and ecological significance.
