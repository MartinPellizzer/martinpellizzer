Lunania mexicana

Lunania mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. Its leaves are broad and lobed, and it produces small, red berries that contain the seeds.

The Mexican moonflower is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its unique nocturnal blooming habit.
