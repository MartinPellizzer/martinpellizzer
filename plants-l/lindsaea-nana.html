Lindsaea nana

Lindsaea nana, commonly known as the dwarf lindsaea, is a small, epiphytic fern native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It typically grows on tree trunks or branches, utilizing its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air. The plant has delicate, feather-like fronds that are usually less than 10 centimeters in length. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its small size and unique appearance, it is a popular subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
