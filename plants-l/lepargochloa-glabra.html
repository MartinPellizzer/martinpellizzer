Lepargochloa glabra

Lepargochloa glabra, commonly known as the smooth lepargochloa, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, flat leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in landscaping or as a ground cover in warmer climates.
