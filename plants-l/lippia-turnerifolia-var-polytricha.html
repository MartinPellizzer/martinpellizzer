Lippia turnerifolia var. polytricha

Lippia turnerifolia var. polytricha, commonly known as the hairy lippia, is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It features aromatic, narrow leaves with a hairy texture, which contribute to its common name. The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in the summer months. Its flowering stems are often branched and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
