Leptocereus nudiflorus

Leptocereus nudiflorus, commonly known as the nakedflower cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its smooth, ribbed stems and the absence of spines, giving it a distinctive, streamlined appearance. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom during the spring and summer months. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water requirements.

Its growth pattern and unique floral display make it a notable species in desert botany.
