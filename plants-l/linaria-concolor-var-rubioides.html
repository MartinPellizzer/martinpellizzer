Linaria concolor var. rubioides

Linaria concolor var. rubioides, commonly known as the red-flowered antirrhinum, is a variant of the common toadflax.

It is a herbaceous annual plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. The plant features slender, branched stems and produces clusters of bright red, tubular flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This variety is often cultivated for its vibrant color and ease of growth in gardens.
