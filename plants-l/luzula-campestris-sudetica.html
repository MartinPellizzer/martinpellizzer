Luzula campestris sudetica

Luzula campestris sudetica, commonly known as the Sudetic woodrush, is a member of the Poaceae family, though it is often classified within the Juncaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features grass-like leaves that are narrow and dark green, with a dense, tufted growth habit. Its small, inconspicuous flowers appear in early spring and are clustered in spike-like inflorescences.

Luzula campestris sudetica is native to Central and Eastern Europe and is often found in mountainous regions of the Sudetes.
