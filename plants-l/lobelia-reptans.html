Lobelia reptans

Lobelia reptans, commonly known as creeping lobelia, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom throughout the summer months. The plant has creeping stems that root at the nodes, allowing it to spread across the ground. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Lobelia reptans thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
