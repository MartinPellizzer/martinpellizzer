Leandra dispar

Leandra dispar, commonly known as the white leandra, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It belongs to the family Strelitziaceae and is characterized by its striking, white, funnel-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant features lance-shaped, glossy leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stems. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, fertile soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant in warmer climates.
