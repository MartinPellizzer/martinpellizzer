Ligeophila umbraticola

Ligeophila umbraticola, commonly known as the shadow fern, is a unique species of fern that thrives in low-light conditions.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that grow in a rosette pattern. The plant prefers moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate forests or along the edges of water bodies. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it to the substrate while absorbing nutrients.

Ligeophila umbraticola plays a vital role in maintaining the ecological balance of its habitat by contributing to soil stability and providing shelter for small organisms.
