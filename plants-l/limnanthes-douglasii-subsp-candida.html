Limnanthes douglasii subsp. candida

Limnanthes douglasii subsp.

candida, commonly known as the white buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States. It features showy, white, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant has hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and marshy areas.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.
