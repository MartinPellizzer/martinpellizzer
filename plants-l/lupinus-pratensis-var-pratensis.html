Lupinus pratensis var. pratensis

Lupinus pratensis var. pratensis, commonly known as the meadow lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of pea-like flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.

Lupinus pratensis var. pratensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
