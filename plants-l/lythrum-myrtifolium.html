Lythrum myrtifolium

Lythrum myrtifolium, commonly known as purple loosestrife, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, spire-like clusters of vibrant purple flowers that bloom from midsummer to early autumn. The plant has lance-shaped, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly rough texture. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in wetlands and disturbed soils.

However, it is considered an invasive species in many regions of North America, where it can outcompete native plants.
