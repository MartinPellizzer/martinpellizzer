Lonchocarpus huetamoensis

Lonchocarpus huetamoensis, commonly known as the Huetamo legume, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive pod fruits. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it ecologically significant in nutrient-poor environments.

Its flowers are typically small and clustered, often with vibrant colors that attract pollinators.
