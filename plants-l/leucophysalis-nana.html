Leucophysalis nana

Leucophysalis nana, commonly known as the dwarf leucophysalis, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is a sought-after plant among botanical enthusiasts.
