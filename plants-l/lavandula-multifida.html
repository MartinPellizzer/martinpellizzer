Lavandula multifida

Lavandula multifida, commonly known as the fringed lavender, is a species of flowering plant in the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its distinctive, narrow, and fringed leaves. The plant produces spikes of purple flowers that bloom in summer, making it a popular ornamental plant. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, thriving in dry, rocky conditions.

Lavandula multifida is also valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in herbal remedies.
