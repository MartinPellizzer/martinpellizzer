Limeum fenestratum var. perenne

Limeum fenestratum var. perenne, commonly known as the perennial fenestrated lime, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its distinctive fenestrated (perforated) leaves.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as woodland floors or wet meadows, where it thrives in partial to full shade. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers during the spring or early summer. Its leaves are often heart-shaped with a broad base and numerous leaflets, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The species is known for its adaptability to wet soils and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
