Lens cordata

Lens cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically lobed and have a velvety texture. The plant produces striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in the morning. Its stems are slender and twining, allowing it to climb and spread along supports.

Lens cordata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
