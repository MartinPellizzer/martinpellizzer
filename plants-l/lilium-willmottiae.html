Lilium willmottiae

Lilium willmottiae, commonly known as the Willmott's lily, is a rare and striking species native to the mountainous regions of central China.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and large, showy white flowers with a distinctive red or pink throat. The plant typically blooms in late summer, producing fragrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem, contributing to its elegant appearance.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Lilium willmottiae is considered a conservation concern.
