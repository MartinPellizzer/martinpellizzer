Larix olgensis f. viridis

Larix olgensis f. viridis, commonly known as the green larch, is a variant of the larch tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its unique foliage, featuring needle-like leaves that remain green throughout the growing season rather than turning yellow or gold in autumn. The plant belongs to the Pinaceae family and is known for its conical shape and adaptability to various soil conditions. Its cones are typically woody and contain seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This ornamental tree is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and resilience in different climates.
