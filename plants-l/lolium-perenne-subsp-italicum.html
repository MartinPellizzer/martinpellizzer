Lolium perenne subsp. italicum

Lolium perenne subsp.

italicum, commonly known as Italian ryegrass, is a cool-season grass native to Europe. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny flowers, which are typically green or pale yellow. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in forage production and turfgrass.

Its rapid growth and high nutritional value make it a valuable agricultural species.
