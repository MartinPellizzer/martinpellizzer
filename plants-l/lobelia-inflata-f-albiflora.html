Lobelia inflata f. albiflora

Lobelia inflata f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered Indian tobacco, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular white flowers that bloom in late summer. This variant of Lobelia inflata is distinguished by its pale white flowers, contrasting with the more common blue or purple-flowered forms.
