Lycopersicon pennellii

Lycopersicon pennellii, commonly known as the wild tomato, is a species native to South America.

It is a herbaceous plant that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually hairy and have a lobed or serrated margin. Its flowers are yellow and borne in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries that contain numerous seeds.

This species is often used in botanical research due to its genetic diversity and potential for breeding with cultivated tomatoes.
