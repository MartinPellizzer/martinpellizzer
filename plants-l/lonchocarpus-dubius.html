Lonchocarpus dubius

Lonchocarpus dubius, commonly known as the Brazilian cowpea, is a tropical legume native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by distinctive, inflated pods containing angular, yellow seeds. It is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural systems.

However, it can be invasive in certain regions and requires careful management.
