Lucuma laetevirens

Lucuma laetevirens, commonly known as the golden lucuma, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly in Peru and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its evergreen shrub form, with thick, leathery leaves that are dark green in color. Its fruit, which is oval-shaped and covered in a yellowish husk, contains edible seeds surrounded by a sweet, creamy pulp.

This plant is valued not only for its unique flavor but also for its potential medicinal properties.
