Lespedeza potaninii f. breviracemi

Lespedeza potaninii f. breviracemi, commonly known as the narrow-leaf lespedeza, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, clustered inflorescences. The leaves are typically narrow and pinnately divided, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This plant is native to parts of East Asia and is often found in dry, open habitats.

It produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, adding to its ornamental value.
