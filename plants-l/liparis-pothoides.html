Liparis pothoides

Liparis pothoides, commonly known as the Potho's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and delicate, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments, often in mountainous regions. Its roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption in poor soil conditions.

Liparis pothoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
