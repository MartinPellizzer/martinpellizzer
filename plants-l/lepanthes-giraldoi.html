Lepanthes giraldoi

Lepanthes giraldoi, commonly known as the Giraldo's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in montane forests. Its flowers are usually small, with intricate patterns and a variety of colors, including white, pink, and purple.

Lepanthes giraldoi is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
