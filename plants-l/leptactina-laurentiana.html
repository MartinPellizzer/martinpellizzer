Leptactina laurentiana

Leptactina laurentiana, commonly known as the Laurentian leek, is a perennial herb native to the northeastern United States and southeastern Canada.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its strap-like, grass-like leaves that grow from a bulbous base. The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers in the summer, which are followed by small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along streambanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
