Lessingianthus constrictus

Lessingianthus constrictus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features elongated, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its bright yellow flowers bloom in the summer months.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking drought-resistant ornamental plants.
