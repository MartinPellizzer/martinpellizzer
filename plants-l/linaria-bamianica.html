Linaria bamianica

Linaria bamianica, commonly known as the Bamian Valley horned poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Afghanistan.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers with prominent, horn-like petals that resemble the shape of a horn. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, thriving at high altitudes. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine layer of hair.

Linaria bamianica is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its historical and cultural significance in the region.
