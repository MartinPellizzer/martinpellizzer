Limnanthemum crenatum

Limnanthemum crenatum, commonly known as the water lily of the genus Limnanthemum, is a species of aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its floating leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in summer. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and marshes. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped with wavy edges, and the flowers are often pink or white.

Limnanthemum crenatum plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
