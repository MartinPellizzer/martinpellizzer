Lepidium crassifolium var. ramosissimum

Lepidium crassifolium var. ramosissimum, commonly known as the thick-leaved wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, branching growth habit and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to yellow. The plant has basal and stem leaves that are thick and fleshy, adapted to survive in arid conditions. It thrives in rocky soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as slopes and cliffs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual interest.
