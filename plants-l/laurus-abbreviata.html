Laurus abbreviata

Laurus abbreviata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved laurel, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dark green, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are aromatic and often used in culinary applications. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible but not commonly consumed. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and fragrance, it is often cultivated in gardens for both aesthetic and aromatic purposes.
