Laelia aurantiaca

Laelia aurantiaca, commonly known as the orange orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers, which are often adorned with contrasting green veins. This terrestrial orchid typically grows in high-altitude regions with cool, moist conditions. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in its native habitat.

Laelia aurantiaca is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its vibrant blooms and adaptability to cultivation.
