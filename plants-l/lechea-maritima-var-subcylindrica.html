Lechea maritima var. subcylindrica

Lechea maritima var. subcylindrica, commonly known as the narrow-cylindrical sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and its ability to thrive in saline and sandy soils. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically narrow and cylindrical, giving it a unique appearance.

It is often used in coastal landscaping due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
