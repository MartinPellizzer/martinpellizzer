Loranthus giorgii

Loranthus giorgii, commonly known as the Italian mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches itself to various host trees, primarily oaks and poplars.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own carbohydrates through photosynthesis using sunlight absorbed by its host's leaves. The plant produces small, white, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds and other animals. Its life cycle involves seed dispersal by birds that consume the berries and later excrete the seeds onto suitable host trees.

Loranthus giorgii is often found in temperate regions and is considered both a parasitic and ecologically significant species.
