Lobelia scabra

Lobelia scabra, commonly known as rough lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, slender stems and small, tubular blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are narrow and often have a rough texture, contributing to its common name.

Lobelia scabra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
