Lithospermum kotschyi

Lithospermum kotschyi, commonly known as the Kotschyi rock pepper, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, showcasing its adaptability to arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, aiding in water conservation.

The roots of Lithospermum kotschyi contain compounds that have been studied for their potential medicinal properties.
