Lupinus erectifolius

Lupinus erectifolius, commonly known as the upright-leaf lupin, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces tall spikes of bright yellow or orange flowers, which are clustered in racemes. Lupinus erectifolius is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

It is a member of the legume family, capable of fixing nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria.
