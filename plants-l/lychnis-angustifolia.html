Lychnis angustifolia

Lychnis angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, pink or red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Lychnis angustifolia is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
