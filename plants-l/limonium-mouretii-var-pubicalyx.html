Limonium mouretii var. pubicalyx

Limonium mouretii var. pubicalyx, commonly known as the coastal flax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in sandy or coastal soils, thriving in well-drained environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and is often used in rock gardens or as a coastal ornamental plant.
