Lobivia vanurkiana

Lobivia vanurkiana, commonly known as the Vanurkian Lobivia, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and dense covering of white to pale blue spines. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and thrives in full sun, exhibiting a slow growth rate. Its flowers, which appear in spring, are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, adding a subtle aesthetic to its otherwise spiky form.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is a sought-after specimen among cactus enthusiasts.
