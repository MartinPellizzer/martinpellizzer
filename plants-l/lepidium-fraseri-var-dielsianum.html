Lepidium fraseri var. dielsianum

Lepidium fraseri var. dielsianum, commonly known as the Fraser's cress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and finely divided leaves. It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and ecological adaptations to high-altitude habitats.
