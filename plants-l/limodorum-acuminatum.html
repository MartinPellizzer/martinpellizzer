Limodorum acuminatum

Limodorum acuminatum, commonly known as the spurred gentian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are typically tubular with prominent spurred lobes. The plant grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy texture.

Limodorum acuminatum is a member of the Gentianaceae family, known for its showy, often blue or purple flowers.
