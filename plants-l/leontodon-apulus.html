Leontodon apulus

Leontodon apulus, commonly known as the Italian dandelion, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate leaves and yellow composite flowers. The plant produces a distinctive, hollow, hairless stem that becomes fibrous with age. Its flowers are typically visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Leontodon apulus is often found in open habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and rocky areas.
