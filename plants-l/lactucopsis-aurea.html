Lactucopsis aurea

Lactucopsis aurea, commonly known as the golden lettuce, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped, bright yellow leaves. The plant produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grasslands.

Lactucopsis aurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
