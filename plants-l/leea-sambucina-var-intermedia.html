Leea sambucina var. intermedia

Leea sambucina var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate elderberry, is a variant of the elderberry plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces dark purple berries that are edible but should be harvested matured and properly prepared. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Botanically, it is notable for its aromatic foliage and its role in supporting local wildlife.
