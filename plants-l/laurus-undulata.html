Laurus undulata

Laurus undulata, commonly known as sweet bay laurel, is a flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to the Mediterranean region. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, dark green, glossy leaves that are often used in cooking and traditional medicine. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark, spherical berries.

The leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its distinctive fragrance and medicinal properties.
