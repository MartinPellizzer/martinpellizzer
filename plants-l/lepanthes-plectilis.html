Lepanthes plectilis

Lepanthes plectilis, commonly known as the zigzag orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored flowers that feature intricate, zigzagging patterns on the petals and sepals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical forests. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Lepanthes plectilis is valued for its ornamental beauty and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its native habitats.
