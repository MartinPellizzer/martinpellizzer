Lantana viburnoides var. richardii

Lantana viburnoides var. richardii, commonly known as the Richard's lantana, is a flowering shrub native to Mexico.

It features opposite, simple, and ovate to lanceolate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of yellow to orange, which bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that turns red when mature.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
