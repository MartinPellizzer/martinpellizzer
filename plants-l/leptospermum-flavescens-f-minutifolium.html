Leptospermum flavescens f. minutifolium

Leptospermum flavescens f. minutifolium, commonly known as the small-leaved tea tree, is a compact shrub native to Australia.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental gardening for its hardy nature and attractive foliage.

This form is a naturally occurring variant of the broader Leptospermum flavescens species.
