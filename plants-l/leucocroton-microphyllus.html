Leucocroton microphyllus

Leucocroton microphyllus, commonly known as the small-leaved croton, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically small and elliptical in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by bright orange or red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Leucocroton microphyllus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and seasonal color changes.
