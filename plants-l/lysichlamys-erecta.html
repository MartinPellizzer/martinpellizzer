Lysichlamys erecta

Lysichlamys erecta, commonly known as the upright lichen, is a foliose lichen that exhibits a distinctive, leaf-like structure.

It typically grows on bark, soil, or rock surfaces, forming a dense, crust-like layer. The thallus is usually pale green to yellowish in color, with a smooth or slightly wrinkled surface. It lacks distinct rhizines and has a simple, unbranched structure.

This lichen is often found in temperate regions and is known for its ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments.
