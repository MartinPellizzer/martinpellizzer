Ligustrum vulgare f. triphyllum

Ligustrum vulgare f. triphyllum, commonly known as the three-leaf privet, is a compact shrub characterized by its small, dark green leaves that often exhibit a trifoliate pattern.

This form of Ligustrum vulgare is typically found in shaded, moist environments, such as woodlands or along stream banks. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, which are followed by dark blue berries that are attractive to birds. Its dense growth habit makes it suitable for use as a hedging plant or in ornamental landscaping.

The botanical features of this cultivar make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a low-maintenance, visually appealing shrub.
