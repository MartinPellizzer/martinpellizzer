Limnia adsurgens

Limnia adsurgens, commonly known as the climbing water plant, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its ability to climb and spread along submerged surfaces.

It features slender, thread-like stems that can extend several meters, allowing it to anchor itself in mud or sand at the bottom of water bodies. The plant produces small, delicate green leaves that are often submerged, though some may emerge just above the water's surface. Its flowers are typically tiny and inconspicuous, often floating on the water's surface, and are adapted for pollination by wind or insects.

Limnia adsurgens thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments and plays a role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems by reducing erosion.
