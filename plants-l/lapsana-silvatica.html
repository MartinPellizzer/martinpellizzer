Lapsana silvatica

Lapsana silvatica, commonly known as the wood knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer.

Lapsana silvatica is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and can be found in various habitats across its native range.
