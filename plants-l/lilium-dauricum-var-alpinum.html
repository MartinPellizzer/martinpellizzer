Lilium dauricum var. alpinum

Lilium dauricum var. alpinum, commonly known as the alpine lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or red, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lanceolate and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This variety is well adapted to cold climates and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.
