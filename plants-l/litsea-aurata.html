Litsea aurata

Litsea aurata, commonly known as golden flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often appearing before or with the leaves. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its fruit is a small, spherical drupe that turns red when mature.

Litsea aurata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
