Lupinus perissophytus

Lupinus perissophytus, commonly known as the silver lupin, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets, and its root system is well-developed, often forming symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria. Lupinus perissophytus thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to coastal conditions with high salt exposure and strong winds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential as a forage crop in certain agricultural contexts.
