Ludwigia longifolia

Ludwigia longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved Ludwigia, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically bright yellow or orange in color. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Ludwigia longifolia is also cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and vibrant blooms.
