Lupinus horizontalis var. horizontalis

Lupinus horizontalis var. horizontalis, commonly known as the creeping lupine, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features slender, spreading stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats of foliage. The plant produces spikes of blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets.

This species is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.
