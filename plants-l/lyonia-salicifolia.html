Lyonia salicifolia

Lyonia salicifolia, commonly known as the willowleaf lyonia, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its slender, arching stems. The plant features small, willow-like leaves that are oval and pointed, with a glossy green surface. It produces white, bell-shaped flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by red berries in the fall.

Lyonia salicifolia is often found in moist, acidic soils in woodlands and streambanks.
