Litsea indoverticillata

Litsea indoverticillata, commonly known as the Indian litsea, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and avocado trees. The plant is characterized by its opposite, simple, and elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. It produces small, white, fragrant flowers followed by berries that are edible for some bird species.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
