Lupinus ccorilazensis

Lupinus ccorilazensis, commonly known as the Andean lupine, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces raceme-like inflorescences of vibrant blue or purple flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Lupinus ccorilazensis is adapted to cold and arid environments, often growing in rocky or alpine soils.

Its seeds are an important food source for local wildlife and have been used traditionally by indigenous communities.
