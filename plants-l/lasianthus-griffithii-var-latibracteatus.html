Lasianthus griffithii var. latibracteatus

Lasianthus griffithii var. latibracteatus, commonly known as the broad-bract lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy bracts that are broader than the actual flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with opposite, lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually clustered in terminal panicles and display a range of colors from pink to red.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
