Lathyrus luteus var. styriacus

Lathyrus luteus var. styriacus, commonly known as the Styrian yellow vetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Europe.

It is a perennial legume characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of yellowish-orange pea-like flowers. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain several oblong seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and traditional herbal uses.
