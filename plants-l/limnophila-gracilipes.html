Limnophila gracilipes

Limnophila gracilipes, commonly known as the slender water plantain, is a semi-aquatic herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shallow water or damp soil, with its slender, grass-like leaves floating just above the water surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or white, arranged in dense spikes. Its rhizomes form a network beneath the soil, allowing it to spread and colonize new areas.

Limnophila gracilipes plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
