Listera puberula

Listera puberula, commonly known as the hairy basketflower, is a small perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of white, star-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in rich, well-drained soils. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant produces small, round berries that mature to a dark blue color.

Listera puberula is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for various pollinators.
