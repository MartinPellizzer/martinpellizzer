Leptolobium glaziovianum

Leptolobium glaziovianum, commonly known as the Glaziov's legume, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and horticultural research.
