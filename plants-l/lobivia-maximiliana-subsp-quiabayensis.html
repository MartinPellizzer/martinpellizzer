Lobivia maximiliana subsp. quiabayensis

Lobivia maximiliana subsp.

quiabayensis, commonly known as the Quiabayensis Lobivia, is a succulent plant native to Argentina. It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, spiny growth habit.

The plant typically features short, cylindrical stems with areoles that bear clusters of white or yellow flowers. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it is well adapted to arid environments.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in dry conditions.
