Lepidium pratense

Lepidium pratense, commonly known as wild cress, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like mustard and cabbage. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and along riverbanks. Its small, yellow flowers are arranged in clusters, and it produces small, rounded seeds enclosed in a siliqua fruit.

Lepidium pratense is often used in herbal medicine and is also a food source for various insects and animals.
