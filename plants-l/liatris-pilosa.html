Liatris pilosa

Liatris pilosa, commonly known as blazing star, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescence composed of numerous purple or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in prairies, open woods, and disturbed sites, thriving in full sun and well-drained soil. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value.

Liatris pilosa is also a valuable nectar source for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
