Livistona endauensis

Livistona endauensis, commonly known as the Endau palm, is a species of palm native to Malaysia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant typically grows in swampy or wetland habitats, thriving in humid and tropical conditions. Its fruit is a small, edible drupe that is often consumed by local wildlife and humans.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to its wetland environment.
