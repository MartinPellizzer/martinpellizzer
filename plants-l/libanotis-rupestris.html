Libanotis rupestris

Libanotis rupestris, commonly known as the rock bellflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, thriving in well-drained environments. The plant features upright stems with lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of blue or violet flowers. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate dry conditions.

Libanotis rupestris is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its hardy nature.
