Lasiospermum eriospermum

Lasiospermum eriospermum, commonly known as the woolly mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that grows on the branches of various host trees.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly covering of white or grayish hairs, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, white or pale green berries that are often consumed by birds, aiding in seed dispersal. Its leaves are typically small, elliptical, and have a leathery texture.

Lasiospermum eriospermum is native to parts of South America and is often found in humid, forested environments.
