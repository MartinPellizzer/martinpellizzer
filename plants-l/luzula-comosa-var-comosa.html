Luzula comosa var. comosa

Luzula comosa var. comosa, commonly known as the tufted wood sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tufted at the base.

It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the sedge family.
