Linum catharticum var. annuum

Linum catharticum var. annuum, commonly known as the common flax, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are hairy along the edges. The plant produces blue or purple flowers with five petals, which are typically arranged in loose clusters. Its seeds are small and oval, with a high oil content, making it valuable for both industrial and medicinal purposes.

The plant is also known for its historical use in traditional medicine due to its cathartic properties.
