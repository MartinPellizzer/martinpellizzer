Leucosyris riparia

Leucosyris riparia, commonly known as the river sedge, is a flowering plant native to wetland and riparian habitats.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in marshy areas and along the edges of rivers and streams. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikes. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.

Leucosyris riparia is well adapted to fluctuating water levels and is often found in regions with high humidity.
