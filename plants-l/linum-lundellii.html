Linum lundellii

Linum lundellii, commonly known as the white flax or Lundell's flax, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Linaceae family, which includes other flax species. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

Linum lundellii is often found in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils.
