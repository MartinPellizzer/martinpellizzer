Lastrea equestris

Lastrea equestris, commonly known as the horse's tail plant, is a member of the Asparagaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features long, thin, grass-like leaves that resemble horse's tail, hence its common name. It produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are clustered in umbels.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
