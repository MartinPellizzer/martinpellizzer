Leontodon trivialis

Leontodon trivialis, commonly known as the common hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and linear, grass-like leaves. This perennial plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. The plant's name refers to its resemblance to a hawk's beak, due to the shape of its flower heads.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and is often found in open habitats.
