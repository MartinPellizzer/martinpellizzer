Lilium gazarubrum

Lilium gazarubrum, commonly known as the Gazarubrum lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, often fragrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that can range in color from white to pink and even red. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring narrow, grass-like leaves and a robust root system. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

The Gazarubrum lily is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
