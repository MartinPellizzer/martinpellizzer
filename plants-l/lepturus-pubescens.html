Lepturus pubescens

Lepturus pubescens, commonly known as the hairy leek, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of strap-shaped, hairy leaves. The plant produces tall, slender flower stalks that bear clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is occasionally grown in gardens.
