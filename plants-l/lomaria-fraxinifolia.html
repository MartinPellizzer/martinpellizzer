Lomaria fraxinifolia

Lomaria fraxinifolia, commonly known as the ash-leaf lomaria, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 meters. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are often described as resembling the leaves of the ash tree, hence its common name. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, winged fruits.

Lomaria fraxinifolia is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.
