Leptocarpus burchellii

Leptocarpus burchellii, commonly known as the Burchell's sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit and thrives in well-drained sandy or gravelly soils. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a fine layer of salt, which helps it adapt to coastal environments.

Leptocarpus burchellii is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardiness in maritime climates.
