Licuala dasyantha

Licuala dasyantha, commonly known as the broad-leafed pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palm-like leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and as thatching material. The plant typically grows in wet, swampy areas and thrives in humid climates. Its robust root system helps stabilize soil, making it useful in erosion control.

The plant's unique morphology and adaptability make it an important species in both ecological and cultural contexts.
