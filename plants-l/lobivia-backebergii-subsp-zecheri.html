Lobivia backebergii subsp. zecheri

Lobivia backebergii subsp.

zecheri, commonly known as the Zecher's lobivia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa. It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems and clusters of small, spiny leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
