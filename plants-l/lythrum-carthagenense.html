Lythrum carthagenense

Lythrum carthagenense, commonly known as purple loosestrife, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescences that produce showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it typically grows in moist environments such as wet meadows and marshes. Its root system is fibrous, allowing it to establish itself quickly in new areas.

However, it is considered an invasive species in North America, where it can outcompete native vegetation.
