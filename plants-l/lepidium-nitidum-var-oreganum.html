Lepidium nitidum var. oreganum

Lepidium nitidum var. oreganum, commonly known as Oregon wallflower, is a herbaceous plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many flowering plants with small, four-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky soils and is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of yellow or white flowers. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
