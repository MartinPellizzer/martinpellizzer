Linum capitatum subsp. capitatum

Linum capitatum subsp.

capitatum, commonly known as the meadow flax, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oval seeds. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Linum capitatum subsp.

capitatum is known for its ornamental value and has been used historically for fiber and dye production.
