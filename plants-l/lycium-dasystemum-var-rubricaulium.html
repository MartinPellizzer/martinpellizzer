Lycium dasystemum var. rubricaulium

Lycium dasystemum var. rubricaulium, commonly known as the red-stemmed boxthorn, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, red or orange berries that ripen in the autumn. The plant has woody stems that turn reddish with age, contributing to its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
