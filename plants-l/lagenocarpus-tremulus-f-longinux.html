Lagenocarpus tremulus f. longinux

Lagenocarpus tremulus f. longinux, commonly known as the longleaf lichen, is a unique fungal-plant composite that exhibits distinct botanical characteristics.

It is a foliose lichen, featuring a leaf-like thallus with a flat, lobed structure that often grows on tree bark or rocks. The thallus is typically greenish-gray in color, with a dense, crust-like surface that helps it retain moisture. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, commonly found in temperate forests.

This lichen is notable for its slow growth and ability to colonize nutrient-poor substrates.
