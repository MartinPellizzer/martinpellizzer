Lycopodioides brachyclada

Lycopodioides brachyclada, commonly known as the narrow-leaf clubmoss, is a small, evergreen fern-like plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and its production of microsporangia and megasporangia on separate structures. The plant exhibits a distinct alternation of generations, with the sporophyte stage being the dominant phase in its life cycle. Lycopodioides brachyclada typically thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Its structure and reproductive features make it an important subject in the study of early land plant evolution.
