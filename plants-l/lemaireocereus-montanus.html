Lemaireocereus montanus

Lemaireocereus montanus, commonly known as the Mexican star cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem and spiny areoles, which are typical features of the Cactaceae family. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, often at night. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Lemaireocereus montanus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in harsh conditions.
