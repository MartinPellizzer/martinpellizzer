Linaria amethystea subsp. broussonetii

Linaria amethystea subsp.

broussonetii, commonly known as the purple toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pale purple to deep violet.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, flat seeds. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats. Linaria amethystea subsp.

broussonetii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
