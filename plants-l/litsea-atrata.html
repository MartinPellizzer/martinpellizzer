Litsea atrata

Litsea atrata, commonly known as the blackleaf litsea, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and avocados. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. Its flowers are small and fragrant, typically appearing in clusters, and it produces small, dark blue berries.

Litsea atrata is often valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
