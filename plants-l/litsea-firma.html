Litsea firma

Litsea firma, commonly known as the fragrant leaf tree, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, elliptical leaves that emit a pleasant scent when crushed. Its flowers are small and white, appearing in clusters, and it produces small, orange-colored fruits.

Litsea firma is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its fragrant foliage and seasonal blooms.
