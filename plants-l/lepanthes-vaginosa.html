Lepanthes vaginosa

Lepanthes vaginosa, commonly known as the velvet slipper orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety purple or violet flowers that resemble small slippers, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in the understory of tropical forests. Its leaves are usually broad and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its colorful blooms.

Lepanthes vaginosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
