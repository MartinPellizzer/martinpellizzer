Lilac × persica var. dubia

Lilac × persica var. dubia, commonly known as the Persian lilac, is a hybrid shrub that exhibits characteristics of both lilacs and peaches.

It is native to the regions of Central Asia and is known for its fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 6 to 10 feet and has a spreading, rounded form. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside.

The flowers, which range from white to pale pink, are clustered in panicles and are highly attractive to pollinators.
