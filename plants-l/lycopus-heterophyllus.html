Lycopus heterophyllus

Lycopus heterophyllus, commonly known as narrow-leaved blueweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes and stream banks. Its small, blue to purple flowers bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators.

The leaves are usually narrow and have a slightly hairy surface, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
