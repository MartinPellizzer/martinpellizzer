Leandra paleacea

Leandra paleacea, commonly known as the paleaceae orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, showy flowers that typically feature a combination of white, pink, and green hues. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its leaves are usually broad and leathery, providing structural support for the delicate floral structures.

Leandra paleacea is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
