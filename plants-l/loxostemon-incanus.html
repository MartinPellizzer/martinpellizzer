Loxostemon incanus

Loxostemon incanus, commonly known as the woolly bush, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Loxostemon incanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
