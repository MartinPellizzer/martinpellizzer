Leptospermum retusum

Leptospermum retusum, commonly known as the Tasmanian tea tree, is a small evergreen shrub native to Tasmania.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, rounded inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.

The species is valued for its aromatic foliage and historical use in traditional medicine.
