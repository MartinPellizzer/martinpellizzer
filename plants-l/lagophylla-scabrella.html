Lagophylla scabrella

Lagophylla scabrella, commonly known as the hairy leaf everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and small, showy flowers that bloom in shades of pink and purple. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes. Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The species is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
