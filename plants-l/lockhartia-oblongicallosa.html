Lockhartia oblongicallosa

Lockhartia oblongicallosa, commonly known as the orchid with elongated calluses, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant produces distinctive callus structures on its roots, which are unique to the genus Lockhartia. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
