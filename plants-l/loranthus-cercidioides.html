Loranthus cercidioides

Loranthus cercidioides, commonly known as the silky mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily in the genus Acacia.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using the sunlight captured by its leaves. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces white or greenish flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. Its berries are typically red or yellow and contain sticky seeds that adhere to the bark of trees, facilitating its spread.

This species is native to regions of Africa and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.
