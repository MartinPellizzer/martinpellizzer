Lonchocarpus pubescens

Lonchocarpus pubescens, commonly known as the hairy senna, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, hairy stipules and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces cylindrical pods containing several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and herbal practices.
