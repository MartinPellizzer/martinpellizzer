Landolphia monteiroi

Landolphia monteiroi, commonly known as the Monteiro's moon vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a distinctive corolla shape. The plant produces large, fleshy berries that contain seeds surrounded by a sticky, aromatic pulp. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
