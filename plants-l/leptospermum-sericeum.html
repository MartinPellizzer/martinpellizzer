Leptospermum sericeum

Leptospermum sericeum, commonly known as silver bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and heathland environments. The plant is characterized by its narrow, silver-green leaves and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. It produces woody, cylindrical fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Leptospermum sericeum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its hardy and drought-tolerant nature.
