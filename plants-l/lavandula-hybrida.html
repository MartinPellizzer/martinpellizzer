Lavandula hybrida

Lavandula hybrida, commonly known as English lavender, is a flowering plant in the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and fragrant, spike-like inflorescences composed of purple or pinkish violet flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and has narrow, grey-green, aromatic leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

The botanical features of Lavandula hybrida make it both ornamental and useful in aromatherapy and herbal medicine.
