Languas graminea

Languas graminea, commonly known as the slender grass, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its inflorescence consists of small, clustered spikelets that produce wind-pollinated flowers.

Languas graminea is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
