Lavandula stricta

Lavandula stricta, commonly known as narrowleaf lavender, is a species of flowering plant in the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its upright, compact growth habit. The plant produces spikes of purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, with a strong aromatic scent.

Lavandula stricta is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and has some historical use in herbal remedies.
