Lonicera barbinervis

Lonicera barbinervis, commonly known as the Japanese honeysuckle, is a deciduous vine native to East Asia.

It features opposite, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive hairy underside. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its berries are red and contain seeds, which are dispersed by birds.

Lonicera barbinervis is often cultivated for its ornamental value but can become invasive in certain regions.
