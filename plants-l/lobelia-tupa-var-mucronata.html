Lobelia tupa var. mucronata

Lobelia tupa var. mucronata, commonly known as the scarlet lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features striking, tubular, bright red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and slightly serrated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to attract pollinators like hummingbirds and bees.
