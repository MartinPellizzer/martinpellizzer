Leptactina senegambica

Leptactina senegambica, commonly known as the Senegambian sand mat, is a flowering plant native to the dry regions of West Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with succulent leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or reddish in color. It is adapted to arid environments, with thick leaves that store water and reduce transpiration.

Leptactina senegambica is often found in sandy soils and is used in traditional medicine by local communities.
