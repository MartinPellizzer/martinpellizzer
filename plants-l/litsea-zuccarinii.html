Litsea zuccarinii

Litsea zuccarinii, commonly known as the yellow-flowered litsea, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and avocado trees. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that turns red when mature.

Litsea zuccarinii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
