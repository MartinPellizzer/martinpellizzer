Lippia adoensis var. pubescens

Lippia adoensis var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy lippia, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves covered in fine, fuzzy hairs, which give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers, often with a strong, mint-like fragrance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Lippia adoensis var. pubescens is valued in traditional medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
