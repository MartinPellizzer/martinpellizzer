Lobelia hypoleuca var. rockii

Lobelia hypoleuca var. rockii, commonly known as Rock's lobelia, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, tubular, and typically blue or violet in color, arranged in dense spikes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
