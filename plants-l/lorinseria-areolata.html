Lorinseria areolata

Lorinseria areolata, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, spotted leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its root system is fibrous, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Lorinseria areolata is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by supporting pollinators.
