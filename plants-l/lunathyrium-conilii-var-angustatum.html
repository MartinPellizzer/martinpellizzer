Lunathyrium conilii var. angustatum

Lunathyrium conilii var. angustatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf moonflower, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern California.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its white, star-shaped flowers that open at night. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, round berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when mature.
