Limnosipanea spruceana

Limnosipanea spruceana, commonly known as the Spruce's water-plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in the northern hemisphere.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant has broad, lance-shaped leaves that are usually submerged or floating, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. Its rhizomatous root system helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion in aquatic habitats.

Limnosipanea spruceana plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
