Liabum polymnioides

Liabum polymnioides, commonly known as the woolly blueweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that are typically arranged in clusters. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grassland and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
