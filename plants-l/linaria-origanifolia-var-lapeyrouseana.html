Linaria origanifolia var. lapeyrouseana

Linaria origanifolia var. lapeyrouseana, commonly known as the Lapeyrouse's toadflax, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and bright yellow to orange flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, often resembling those of oregano, which is reflected in its scientific name. This plant thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
