Limonium florentinum

Limonium florentinum, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and its clusters of blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the flowers are typically borne on long, slender stems.

Limonium florentinum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in gardens and floral arrangements.
