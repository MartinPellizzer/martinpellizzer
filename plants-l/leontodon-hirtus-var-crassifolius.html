Leontodon hirtus var. crassifolius

Leontodon hirtus var. crassifolius, commonly known as the hairy roughhead, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features a robust root system and erect, hairy stems that bear linear to lanceolate leaves. Its flowering heads are yellow and consist of numerous small florets arranged in a composite structure.

It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in environments with low nutrient availability.
