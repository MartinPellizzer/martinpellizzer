Lobelia setulosa

Lobelia setulosa, commonly known as the hairy lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite and often covered in fine, stiff hairs, contributing to its common name.

Lobelia setulosa is a member of the Campanulaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
