Lathyrus blepharicarpos var. cyprius

Lathyrus blepharicarpos var. cyprius, commonly known as the Cyprus vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its trailing growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is native to the Mediterranean region, including parts of Cyprus, and thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ability to fix nitrogen, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and for soil stabilization.
