Lantana lucida

Lantana lucida, commonly known as the silver lantana, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to yellow. The plant has opposite, narrow, and slightly hairy leaves that are often covered in a silvery-gray bloom. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Lantana lucida is also known for its ability to attract butterflies and other pollinators.
