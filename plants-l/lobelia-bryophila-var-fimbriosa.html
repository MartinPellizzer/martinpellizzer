Lobelia bryophila var. fimbriosa

Lobelia bryophila var. fimbriosa, commonly known as the fringed lobelia, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive fringed edge, and it produces clusters of blue to purple flowers. It belongs to the Campanulaceae family, which is known for its showy, bell-shaped blooms.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits to pollinators.
