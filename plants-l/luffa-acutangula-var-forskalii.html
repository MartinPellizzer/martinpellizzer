Luffa acutangula var. forskalii

Luffa acutangula var. forskalii, commonly known as the African sponge gourd, is a tropical vine native to Africa.

It is characterized by its fibrous, spongy fruit that develops from the flower and is often used for scrubbing. The plant produces large, heart-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The fruit matures to a pale yellow or greenish color and can be harvested when it is fully dry.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
