Leucas tricrenata

Leucas tricrenata, commonly known as the three-leaf leucas, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in shades of purple or white. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Leucas tricrenata is often used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
