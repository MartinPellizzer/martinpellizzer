Linum lewisii var. lepagei

Linum lewisii var. lepagei, commonly known as the Lepage's flax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and delicate blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
