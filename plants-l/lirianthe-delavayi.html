Lirianthe delavayi

Lirianthe delavayi, commonly known as the Delavay's lily, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southwest China.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in high-altitude forests and shrublands, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy texture, while its stems are sturdy and upright.

Lirianthe delavayi is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
