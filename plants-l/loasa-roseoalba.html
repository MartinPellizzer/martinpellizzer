Loasa roseoalba

Loasa roseoalba, commonly known as the white loasa, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andes mountains in South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in high-altitude regions, often in rocky or mountainous terrain. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the spring and summer seasons. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Loasa roseoalba is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in cooler climates.
