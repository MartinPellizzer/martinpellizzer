Limeum mossambicense

Limeum mossambicense, commonly known as the Mozambique lime moss, is a species of liverwort found in tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Ptilidiorrhizaceae and is characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth form. The plant has a simple thalloid structure with a green, photosynthetic surface covered in small, leaf-like structures called phyllidia. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often growing on rocks or decaying wood.

Limeum mossambicense plays a vital role in soil stabilization and moisture retention in its native habitats.
