Loranthus dregei var. kerenicus

Loranthus dregei var. kerenicus, commonly known as the Kerenicus mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily in the genus Acacia.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using the light it captures. The plant has small, scale-like leaves and produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. Its seeds are dispersed by birds, which consume the fleshy, red berries.

This species is native to southern Africa and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.
