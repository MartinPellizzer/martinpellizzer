Lapeirousia rivularis

Lapeirousia rivularis, commonly known as the river lily, is a striking member of the Iridaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows near water sources, such as rivers and streams, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a basal rosette.

Lapeirousia rivularis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens with adequate water and sunlight.
