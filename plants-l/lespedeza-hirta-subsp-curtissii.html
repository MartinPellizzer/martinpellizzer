Lespedeza hirta subsp. curtissii

Lespedeza hirta subsp.

curtissii, commonly known as curlytop lespedeza, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its hairy stems and small, clustered inflorescences that produce pink to reddish-purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as prairies, wetlands, and disturbed areas. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is valued in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide forage for wildlife.
