Lissochilus friderici

Lissochilus friderici, commonly known as the Friar's orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly in countries like Peru and Bolivia. The plant is characterized by its delicate, pendulous flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
