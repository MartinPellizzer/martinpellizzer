Lundinia

Lundinia, commonly known as the water crown, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by orange-red berries. Lundinia thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found near streams or in forest undergrowth.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural studies.
