Lathyrus undulatus

Lathyrus undulatus, commonly known as the wavy vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves with wavy edges. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Lathyrus undulatus is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
