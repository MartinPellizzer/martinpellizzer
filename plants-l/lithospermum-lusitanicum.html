Lithospermum lusitanicum

Lithospermum lusitanicum, commonly known as the Lusitanian rock pepper, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe.

It features upright stems covered in fine hairs and produces small, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and is adapted to grow in rocky or sandy soils. Its roots contain compounds that have been studied for potential medicinal properties.

It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and forest margins.
