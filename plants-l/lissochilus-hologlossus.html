Lissochilus hologlossus

Lissochilus hologlossus, commonly known as the silver-lipped orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, silver-veined leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky areas, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its unique morphology includes a slender stem and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are adapted to its harsh environment.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
