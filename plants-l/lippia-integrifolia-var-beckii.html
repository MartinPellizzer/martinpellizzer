Lippia integrifolia var. beckii

Lippia integrifolia var. beckii, commonly known as Mexican blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces small, blue or purple flowers arranged in spikes. It has a strong, aromatic scent and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
