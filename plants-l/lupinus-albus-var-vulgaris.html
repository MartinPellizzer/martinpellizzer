Lupinus albus var. vulgaris

Lupinus albus var. vulgaris, commonly known as white lupin, is a perennial legume native to Europe and western Asia.

It features upright stems with clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets, and its root system is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen, enriching the soil. It is often cultivated as a forage crop and has historical uses in traditional medicine.

The species is also valued in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature and soil-improving properties.
