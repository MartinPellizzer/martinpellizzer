Lycaste cobbiana

Lycaste cobbiana, commonly known as the Cobb's lycaste, is a tropical orchid native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, enabling it to survive in seasonal droughts. Its leaves are strap-like and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its unique appearance.

Lycaste cobbiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in the right growing conditions.
