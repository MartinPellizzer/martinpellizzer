Laburnum vulgare var. parkesii

Laburnum vulgare var. parkesii, commonly known as the golden chain tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the British Isles.

It is characterized by its long, drooping clusters of bright yellow flowers, which bloom in early spring. The plant has pinnate leaves composed of 10-20 oval-shaped leaflets. Its bark is smooth and greyish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.
