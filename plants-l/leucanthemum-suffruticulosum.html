Leucanthemum suffruticulosum

Leucanthemum suffruticulosum, commonly known as the shrub chamomile, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a woody base with soft, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, yellow-orange flower heads surrounded by white ray florets, resembling miniature daisies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Leucanthemum suffruticulosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild aromatic properties.
