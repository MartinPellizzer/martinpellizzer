Lupinus mackeyi

Lupinus mackeyi, commonly known as Mackey's lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes.

Lupinus mackeyi is also valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a food source for pollinators.
