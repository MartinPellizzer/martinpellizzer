Lucuma bonplandii

Lucuma bonplandii, commonly known as the golden cup, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange fruit, which resembles a small cup. The plant typically grows in high-altitude areas with cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are broad and glossy, and it produces fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters.

The fruit is often used in culinary applications and is valued for its unique flavor and nutritional content.
