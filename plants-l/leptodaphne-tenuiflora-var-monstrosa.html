Leptodaphne tenuiflora var. monstrosa

Leptodaphne tenuiflora var. monstrosa, commonly known as the variegated winterbry, is a variant of the winterbry plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form and striking variegated foliage with white and green patterns. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, arranged in whorls around the stems. It produces small, fragrant flowers in late winter to early spring, followed by dark blue berries.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a container plant.
