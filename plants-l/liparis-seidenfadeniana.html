Liparis seidenfadeniana

Liparis seidenfadeniana, commonly known as the Seidenfaden's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, grass-like foliage and small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant prefers shady, humid environments and is often found in forest understories. Its root system is fibrous, and it thrives in well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
