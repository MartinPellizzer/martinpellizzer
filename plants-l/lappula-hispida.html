Lappula hispida

Lappula hispida, commonly known as rough lappula, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its hairy, erect stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine pubescence, contributing to its rugged appearance.

Lappula hispida is an annual herb that is native to Europe and parts of Asia.
