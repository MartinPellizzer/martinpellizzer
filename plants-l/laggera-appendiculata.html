Laggera appendiculata

Laggera appendiculata, commonly known as the velvetleaf or wild sunhemp, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small yellow flowers that are followed by distinctive, spiny capsules containing numerous seeds. Laggera appendiculata is known for its rapid growth and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
