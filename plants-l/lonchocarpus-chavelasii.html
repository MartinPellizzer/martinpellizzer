Lonchocarpus chavelasii

Lonchocarpus chavelasii, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a tropical leguminous plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces pods that contain flat, bean-like seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some applications in ecological restoration projects.
