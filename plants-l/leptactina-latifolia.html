Leptactina latifolia

Leptactina latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf leptactina, is a species of flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
