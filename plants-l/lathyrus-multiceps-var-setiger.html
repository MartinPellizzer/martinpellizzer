Lathyrus multiceps var. setiger

Lathyrus multiceps var. setiger, commonly known as the hairy spurred lathyrus, is a perennial flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive hairy texture. The flowers are typically blue or purple, with spurred petals that give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade, often found in woodland or grassy areas.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
