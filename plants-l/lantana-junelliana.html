Lantana junelliana

Lantana junelliana, commonly known as the yellow lantana, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom throughout the year in warm climates. Lantana junelliana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Its adaptability to various soil types and drought tolerance make it a popular choice in landscaping.
