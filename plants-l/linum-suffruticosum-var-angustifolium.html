Linum suffruticosum var. angustifolium

Linum suffruticosum var. angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved flax, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or dry soils. The plant features slender, branched stems and narrow, linear leaves. Its flowers are small, blue or violet, and arranged in loose clusters.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in fiber production.
