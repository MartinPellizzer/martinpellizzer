Lupinus variegatus

Lupinus variegatus, commonly known as the variegated lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has palmately compound leaves with a distinctive variegated pattern of green and white. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or open habitats.

Lupinus variegatus is also known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
