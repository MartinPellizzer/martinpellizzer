Lepanthes aries

Lepanthes aries, commonly known as the red-tipped orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically feature bright red sepals and petals with a white or pale pink base. The plant grows as an epiphyte, often found on trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers bloom during the rainy season, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

Lepanthes aries is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
