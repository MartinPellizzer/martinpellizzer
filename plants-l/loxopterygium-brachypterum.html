Loxopterygium brachypterum

Loxopterygium brachypterum, commonly known as the narrow-winged grass.

It is a member of the Poaceae family, characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets.

It plays a minor role in local ecosystems but is often overlooked due to its unassuming appearance.
