Lilium oxypetalum var. insigne

Lilium oxypetalum var. insigne, commonly known as the showy lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern China.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, supporting large, showy, white to pale pink flowers with a distinctive, recurved petal structure. The plant produces elliptical, dark green leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It blooms in late summer to early autumn, producing fragrant flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
