Lilium cathayanum

Lilium cathayanum, commonly known as the Chinese lily, is a striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It produces large, showy flowers that can reach up to 15 centimeters in diameter, typically in shades of white, pink, and red. The plant features strap-like, dark green leaves that are arranged in a fan shape around the stem. It blooms in late spring to early summer, and its bulbs are used in traditional Chinese medicine.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and cultural significance in horticulture.
