Lobivia hoevenii

Lobivia hoevenii, commonly known as the Desert Rose, is a succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It features a compact, multi-stemmed structure with thick, fleshy leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces vibrant flowers in shades of pink, red, and purple, typically during the warmer months. Its leaves are usually oval or elliptical, with a slightly waxy texture to reduce water loss.

Lobivia hoevenii is well-suited for rock gardens or as a container plant due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
