Linnaea borealis f. trifasciata

Linnaea borealis f. trifasciata, commonly known as the three-banded twinflower, is a rare and striking variant of the twinflower plant.

It is characterized by its distinctive three-colored leaf patterns, which feature contrasting bands of green, white, and yellow. This plant is native to the boreal forests of North America and parts of northern Europe. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Its unique foliage makes it a subject of interest for botanists and plant enthusiasts alike.
