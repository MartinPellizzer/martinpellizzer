Lepanthes furcata

Lepanthes furcata, commonly known as the Forked Orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments within cloud forests. The plant features a slender, erect stem with several fleshy, ovate leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale green, with a distinctive forked shape in the lip, which gives it its common name.

Lepanthes furcata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
