Laelia × proserpini

Laelia × proserpini, commonly known as the Proserpine orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of pendulous or upright blooms in shades of white, pink, and violet, with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals. It prefers cool, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.

Due to its hybrid origin, it exhibits a wide range of morphological variations depending on the parent species.
