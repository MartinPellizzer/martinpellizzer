Linum mucronatum var. papilliferum

Linum mucronatum var. papilliferum, commonly known as the hairy flax, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are narrow and covered in fine, hairy bumps, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
