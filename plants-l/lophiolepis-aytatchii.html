Lophiolepis aytatchii

Lophiolepis aytatchii, commonly known as the Aytatchii grass, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
