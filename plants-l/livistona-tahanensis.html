Livistona tahanensis

Livistona tahanensis, commonly known as the Tahan palm, is a species of palm native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a branched inflorescence, followed by edible, round fruits that are clustered in bunches. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests or swampy areas.

The Tahan palm is valued for its ornamental beauty and has some traditional uses in local communities.
