Luzula haussknechtiana

Luzula haussknechtiana, commonly known as the alpine woodrush, is a perennial plant belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is native to high-altitude regions of Europe and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine soils, thriving in cool, moist environments. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Due to its habitat preferences, it is often found in mountainous areas and is considered a species of conservation interest.
