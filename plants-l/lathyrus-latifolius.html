Lathyrus latifolius

Lathyrus latifolius, commonly known as broad-leaved sweet pea, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and vibrant, pea-like flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant produces trifoliate leaves with broad, heart-shaped leaflets. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

Lathyrus latifolius is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
