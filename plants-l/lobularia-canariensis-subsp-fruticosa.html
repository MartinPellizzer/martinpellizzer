Lobularia canariensis subsp. fruticosa

Lobularia canariensis subsp.

fruticosa, commonly known as Canary Island lobularia, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands. It is a low-growing, evergreen shrub that produces small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers.

The plant has small, ovate leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to Mediterranean climates.
