Ligeophila clavigera var. rhodostachys

Ligeophila clavigera var. rhodostachys, commonly known as the red-stemmed willow, is a variant of the willow species native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive reddish stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing a light yellow to greenish hue, and it is often found near water sources.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
