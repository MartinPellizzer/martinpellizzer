Lobelia cordigera var. pyramidalis

Lobelia cordigera var. pyramidalis, commonly known as the pyramidal cardinal flower, is a strikingly ornamental plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, tubular red flowers that bloom in dense, pyramidal clusters, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 3 feet, with lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often slightly hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variant is prized for its vibrant color and is often used in native plant gardens for its ecological and aesthetic value.
