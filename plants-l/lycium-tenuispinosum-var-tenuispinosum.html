Lycium tenuispinosum var. tenuispinosum

Lycium tenuispinosum var. tenuispinosum, commonly known as the slender-spined boxthorn, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It features small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by red berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. The plant has dense, spiny foliage that provides habitat for various small animals. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine, hair-like structures.

It is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.
