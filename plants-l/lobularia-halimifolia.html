Lobularia halimifolia

Lobularia halimifolia, commonly known as sweet alyssum, is a low-growing annual plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant, white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has alternate, hairy leaves that are typically oval or lance-shaped. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and prolonged blooming period contribute to its ornamental value in gardens.
