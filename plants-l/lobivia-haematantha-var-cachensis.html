Lobivia haematantha var. cachensis

Lobivia haematantha var. cachensis, commonly known as the Cachanita, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded shape and fleshy, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green foliage. Its leaves are typically small, spiny, and arranged in a rosette pattern, providing protection against herbivores.

This variety is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
