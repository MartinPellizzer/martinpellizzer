Leptochloa ginae

Leptochloa ginae, commonly known as the Gina's grass, is a species of grass belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and compact growth habit, typically forming dense clumps. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its small, spike-like inflorescences contain numerous tiny flowers, which are adapted for wind pollination.

Leptochloa ginae is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
