Leucadendron stellatum

Leucadendron stellatum, commonly known as the star sugar bush, is a South African shrub belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded form and its striking, star-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a rough, leathery texture and a distinctive white or pale undersurface. It is well adapted to nutrient-poor soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
