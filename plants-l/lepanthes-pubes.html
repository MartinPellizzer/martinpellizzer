Lepanthes pubes

Lepanthes pubes, commonly known as the pubescent orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as cloud forests, where it thrives on the forest floor or on tree roots. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine, pubescent hairs, giving it its common name.

The flowers are small, with three sepals and three petals, and often display a range of colors from white to pink or purple.
