Loudetia baldwinii

Loudetia baldwinii, commonly known as Baldwin's lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It is often found in open grasslands and along rocky outcrops.

Loudetia baldwinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
