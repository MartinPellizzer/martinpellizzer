Leptopteris laxa

Leptopteris laxa, commonly known as the slender cliff fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinnately compound fronds that are typically green and have a leathery texture. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as rocky crevices and forest floors. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it to rocky substrates, while its spore-producing structures are located on the underside of the fronds.

Leptopteris laxa plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for small invertebrates in its native ecosystems.
