Leucas linifolia var. decipiens

Leucas linifolia var. decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive leucas, is a flowering plant native to parts of South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically pale blue or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem, and bloom during the summer months.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
