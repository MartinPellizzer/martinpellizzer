Larix potaninii var. australis

Larix potaninii var. australis, commonly known as the Australian larch, is a deciduous conifer native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and needle-like leaves that turn golden in autumn before shedding. The tree produces small, woody cones that release seeds in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in native landscapes.
