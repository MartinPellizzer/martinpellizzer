Lindsaea jarrettiana

Lindsaea jarrettiana, commonly known as the Jarrett's spikemoss, is a member of the family Lindsaeaceae and is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a small, evergreen fern-like plant that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features slender, branching stems covered with small, scale-like leaves, and it produces tiny, clustered spore capsules at the tips of its branches. Lindsaea jarrettiana is characterized by its unique morphology and is often found in humid, low-light conditions.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
