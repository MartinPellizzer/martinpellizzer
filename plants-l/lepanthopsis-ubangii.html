Lepanthopsis ubangii

Lepanthopsis ubangii, commonly known as the Ubangii orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces small, greenish-white to pale yellow blooms with a distinct lip petal. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
