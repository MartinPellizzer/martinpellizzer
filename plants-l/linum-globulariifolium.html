Linum globulariifolium

Linum globulariifolium, commonly known as the globular-leaved flax, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the genus Linum, which includes various species of flax known for their showy flowers. The plant is characterized by its rounded, globular leaves and produces small, delicate blue or purple flowers. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
