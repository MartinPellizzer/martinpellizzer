Lycianthes laevis subsp. inaequidens

Lycianthes laevis subsp.

inaequidens, commonly known as the rough lycianthes, is a species native to the Andes region of South America. It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves and showy yellow flowers with five petals.

The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when immature and turn red when mature. It thrives in temperate to subtropical climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
