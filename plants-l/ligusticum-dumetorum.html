Ligusticum dumetorum

Ligusticum dumetorum, commonly known as rough cowparsley, is a biennial or short-lived perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the carrot family (Apiaceae) and is characterized by its fern-like, compound leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and woodland edges.

Its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescence are typical of the Apiaceae family, and it is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
