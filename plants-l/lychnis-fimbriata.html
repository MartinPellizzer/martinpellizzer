Lychnis fimbriata

Lychnis fimbriata, commonly known as the fringed campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the carnation family (Caryophyllaceae) and is characterized by its delicate, pink or white flowers with fringed petals. The plant typically grows in meadows, woodland edges, and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. It produces small, oval seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread across various habitats.

Lychnis fimbriata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its attractive appearance and ease of care.
