Lotus thessalus

Lotus thessalus, commonly known as the Greek lotus, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has palmately compound leaves and produces pods that contain several seeds. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils near water bodies.

The lotus thessalus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has cultural significance in various regions.
