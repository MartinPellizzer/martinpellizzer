Lysipomia rhizomata

Lysipomia rhizomata, commonly known as the creeping marshplant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in South America.

It features a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread vegetatively through underground stems. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are slender and grass-like, often floating or submerged depending on the water level.

Lysipomia rhizomata plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for aquatic insects.
