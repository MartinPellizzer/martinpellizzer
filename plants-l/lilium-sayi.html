Lilium sayi

Lilium sayi, commonly known as the Say's lily, is a species of flowering plant in the Liliaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of western North America, particularly in Idaho and Nevada. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with a subtle fragrance. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

Lilium sayi is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it blooms in late summer.
