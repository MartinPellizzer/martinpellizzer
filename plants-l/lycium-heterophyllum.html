Lycium heterophyllum

Lycium heterophyllum, commonly known as the Chinese wolfberry or sandthorn, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces red berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. Lycium heterophyllum thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its botanical features make it a resilient species suitable for landscaping in dry climates.
