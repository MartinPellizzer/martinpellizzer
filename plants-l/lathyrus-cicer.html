Lathyrus cicer

Lathyrus cicer, commonly known as the sweet vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its slender stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. Its pods contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines.

Lathyrus cicer is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.
