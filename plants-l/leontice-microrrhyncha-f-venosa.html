Leontice microrrhyncha f. venosa

Leontice microrrhyncha f. venosa, commonly known as the variegated leontice, is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its distinctive foliage.

It features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often variegated with white or cream markings, enhancing its ornamental value. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a unique and low-maintenance ground cover.
