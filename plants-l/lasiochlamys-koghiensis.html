Lasiochlamys koghiensis

Lasiochlamys koghiensis, commonly known as the Koghi mountain bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which contribute to its common name, "lasiochlamys" meaning "hairy calyx.". It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
