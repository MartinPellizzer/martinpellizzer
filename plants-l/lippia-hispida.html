Lippia hispida

Lippia hispida, commonly known as Mexican mint or Mexican rue, is a perennial herb native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and small, white or pale purple flowers. The plant thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

Lippia hispida is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects.
