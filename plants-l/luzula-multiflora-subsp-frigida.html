Luzula multiflora subsp. frigida

Luzula multiflora subsp.

frigida, commonly known as the alpine wood sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to high-altitude regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It typically grows in moist, rocky or alpine habitats, often in mountainous areas with cold climates.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale blue or greenish flowers. It is adapted to harsh environmental conditions, including low temperatures and strong winds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
