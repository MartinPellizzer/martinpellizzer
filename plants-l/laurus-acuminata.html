Laurus acuminata

Laurus acuminata, commonly known as bay laurel, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are glossy and have a strong aromatic scent. Bay laurel produces small, white flowers followed by dark, berry-like fruits.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the aromatic leaves used in cooking and traditional medicine.
