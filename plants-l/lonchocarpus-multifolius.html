Lonchocarpus multifolius

Lonchocarpus multifolius, commonly known as the Brazilian peacock fern, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking, fern-like foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in tropical gardens for its unique appearance.
