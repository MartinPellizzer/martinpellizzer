Lythrum roseum-superbum

Lythrum roseum-superbum, commonly known as rose pink or tall loosestrife, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lythraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along streambanks.
