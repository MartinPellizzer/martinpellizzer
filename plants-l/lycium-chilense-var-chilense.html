Lycium chilense var. chilense

Lycium chilense var. chilense, commonly known as the Chilean boxthorn, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to coastal and mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
