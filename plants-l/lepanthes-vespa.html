Lepanthes vespa

Lepanthes vespa, commonly known as the wasp orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, wasp-like appearance, with dark green, velvety leaves and a showy, yellow-tinged flower. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are small but highly ornate, featuring intricate patterns that mimic the appearance of wasps.

Lepanthes vespa is valued for its unique morphology and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its aesthetic appeal.
