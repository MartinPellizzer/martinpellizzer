Lycopodioides brevifolia

Lycopodioides brevifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved clubmoss, is a small evergreen plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and produces spore-bearing structures called strobili. The plant has whorled leaves that are typically narrow and linear, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Lycopodioides brevifolia plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
