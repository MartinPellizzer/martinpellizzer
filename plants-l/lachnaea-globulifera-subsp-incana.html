Lachnaea globulifera subsp. incana

Lachnaea globulifera subsp.

incana, commonly known as the hairy globeflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its compact, globular inflorescence composed of numerous small, pinkish-white flowers.

The plant features hairy, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
