Lolium cristatum

Lolium cristatum, commonly known as the bentgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, narrow leaves. The plant produces spikelets that are often curved or twisted, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lawns, meadows, and along stream banks.

Lolium cristatum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
