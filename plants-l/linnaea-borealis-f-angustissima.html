Linnaea borealis f. angustissima

Linnaea borealis f. angustissima, commonly known as the narrowleaf twinflower, is a rare variant of the twinflower plant native to northern regions of North America and Eurasia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. Its flowers emit a sweet scent, attracting pollinators like bees and flies.

This subspecies is noted for its delicate appearance and is of interest to botanists and conservationists due to its limited distribution and ecological significance.
