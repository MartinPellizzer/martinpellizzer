Lantana bernardinensis

Lantana bernardinensis, commonly known as the silver lantana, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, depending on the variety. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a silvery or grayish underside, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Lantana bernardinensis is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
