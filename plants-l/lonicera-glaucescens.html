Lonicera glaucescens

Lonicera glaucescens, commonly known as the blue honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, arching stems with opposite, ovate leaves that are often tinged with a bluish hue. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in early summer, typically white or pale yellow, which attract pollinators. Its berries, which ripen to a dark blue or black color, are edible but not particularly flavorful.

Lonicera glaucescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
