Linum austriacum var. glaucescens

Linum austriacum var. glaucescens, commonly known as the Austrian flax, is a flowering plant native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with a bluish hue. The plant produces showy blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in flax cultivation.
