Limonium longispicatum

Limonium longispicatum, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers and spiny, silver-gray foliage. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, salt marshes, and sandy soils, showing a high tolerance for saline conditions. Its flowers bloom in summer, often forming dense, spike-like clusters.

Limonium longispicatum is widely cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and resilience in harsh environments.
