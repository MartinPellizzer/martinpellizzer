Lepanthes yubarta

Lepanthes yubarta, commonly known as the Yubarta orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically display vibrant colors such as white, yellow, and green. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees or other plants without deriving nutrients from them. Its foliage consists of narrow, strap-like leaves that help in photosynthesis and moisture retention.

Lepanthes yubarta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
