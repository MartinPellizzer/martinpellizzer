Lippia foliolosa

Lippia foliolosa, commonly known as Mexican mint or lemon verbena, is a member of the Verbenaceae family.

It is a perennial shrub native to Mexico and Central America, characterized by its aromatic, serrated leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale purple, arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for both ornamental and medicinal use.

Its essential oils contain compounds like citral, which contribute to its strong, lemony scent and various therapeutic properties.
