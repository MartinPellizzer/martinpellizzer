Luzula albida var. leucanthema

Luzula albida var. leucanthema, commonly known as the white tufted sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage with narrow, cylindrical leaves that are typically light green to white in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in clusters, which are often surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands, bogs, and along the edges of ponds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
