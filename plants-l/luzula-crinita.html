Luzula crinita

Luzula crinita, commonly known as the hairy woodrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are often covered in fine, hair-like structures, giving it its common name.

Luzula crinita is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
