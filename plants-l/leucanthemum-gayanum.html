Leucanthemum gayanum

Leucanthemum gayanum, commonly known as the Gamanthema, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Sri Lanka.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring basal leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy. Its flowers are white with a yellow center, creating a striking appearance that attracts pollinators.

Leucanthemum gayanum is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
