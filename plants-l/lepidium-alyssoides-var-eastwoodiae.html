Lepidium alyssoides var. eastwoodiae

Lepidium alyssoides var. eastwoodiae, commonly known as Eastwood's wall cress, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and pinnately divided leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its seeds are small and capsule-like, containing several tiny seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This variety is distinguished by its slightly larger leaves and more robust growth compared to the standard Lepidium alyssoides.
