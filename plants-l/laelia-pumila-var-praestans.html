Laelia pumila var. praestans

Laelia pumila var. praestans, commonly known as the miniature orchid, is a compact, terrestrial orchid native to the Andes Mountains.

It features a small, fleshy rhizome and produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green. The plant thrives in cool, humid environments with partial shade and well-draining soil. Its blooms are typically small, with three petals and a prominent lip, characteristic of the Laelia genus.

It is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its miniature size and ornamental appeal.
