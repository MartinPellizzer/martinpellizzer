Leea heterodoxa

Leea heterodoxa, commonly known as the broadleaf moonseed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It belongs to the family Leeaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in horticulture.
