Linaria juncea var. expansa

Linaria juncea var. expansa, commonly known as the broadleaf toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value, it is also sometimes cultivated as a garden plant.
