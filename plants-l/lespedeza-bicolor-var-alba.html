Lespedeza bicolor var. alba

Lespedeza bicolor var. alba, commonly known as white lespedeza, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade.

It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it a valuable plant for soil enrichment and erosion control.
