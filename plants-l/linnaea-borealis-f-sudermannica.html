Linnaea borealis f. sudermannica

Linnaea borealis f. sudermannica, commonly known as the southern twinflower, is a rare variant of the twinflower plant native to the northern hemisphere.

This low-growing perennial herb features small, delicate white flowers that bloom in early spring. It typically thrives in shaded, moist forest floors, often in areas with rich, acidic soil. The plant's leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in a fine, velvety texture.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and limited distribution, making it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
