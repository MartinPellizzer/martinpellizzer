Leucadendron comosum subsp. homaeophyllum

Leucadendron comosum subsp.

homaeophyllum, commonly known as the silverleaf leucadendron, is a shrub native to South Africa. It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and cone-like inflorescences that range in color from yellow to orange.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and Mediterranean climates. Its flowers are adapted for pollination by birds and insects, contributing to its ecological significance.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in dry conditions.
