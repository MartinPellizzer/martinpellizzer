Luma montevidensis

Luma montevidensis, commonly known as the Montevidense guava, is a small evergreen tree native to Uruguay and southern Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy white flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically consumed fresh or used in jams and beverages. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and for local consumption.
