Lathyrus nevadensis var. nevadensis

Lathyrus nevadensis var. nevadensis, commonly known as the Nevada vetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It typically grows in rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions. The plant has slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves with small, pea-like flowers that range in color from purple to pink. Its root system is fibrous, and it produces small, pod-like fruits containing several seeds.

This species is adapted to dry, harsh environments and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
