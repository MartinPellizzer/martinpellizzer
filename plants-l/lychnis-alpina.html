Lychnis alpina

Lychnis alpina, commonly known as the alpine campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky soils, thriving in cool, high-altitude environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and branching.

Lychnis alpina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
