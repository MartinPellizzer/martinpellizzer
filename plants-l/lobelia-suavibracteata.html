Lobelia suavibracteata

Lobelia suavibracteata, commonly known as the sweet bract lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a velvety texture.

Lobelia suavibracteata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
