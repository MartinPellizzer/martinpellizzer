Lippia virgata var. platyphylla

Lippia virgata var. platyphylla, commonly known as Mexican blueweed, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It features aromatic, narrow leaves with a slightly hairy texture and produces small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
