Lavandula angustifolia var. turolensis

Lavandula angustifolia var. turolensis, commonly known as the Turolensis lavender, is a subspecies of English lavender native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences of purple flowers. This variety is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping and rock gardens. Its aromatic properties make it valuable in both ornamental gardening and essential oil production.

The plant is also noted for its hardiness and ability to thrive in Mediterranean climates.
