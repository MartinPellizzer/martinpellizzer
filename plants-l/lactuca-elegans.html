Lactuca elegans

Lactuca elegans, commonly known as slender lettuce, is a flowering plant in the daisy family, Asteraceae.

It is native to the eastern United States and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It is a herbaceous perennial that produces a milky sap when the stems or leaves are broken.

Lactuca elegans is often found in disturbed soils and can spread through seeds dispersed by wind.
