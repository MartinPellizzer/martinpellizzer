Lavandula spica var. turolensis

Lavandula spica var. turolensis, commonly known as Turolensis lavender, is a variety of lavender native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense spikes of purple flowers. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that are aromatic and often used in herbal remedies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This cultivar is valued for its fragrance and ornamental appeal in both garden and commercial settings.
