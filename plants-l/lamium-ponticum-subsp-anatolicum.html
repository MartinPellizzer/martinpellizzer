Lamium ponticum subsp. anatolicum

Lamium ponticum subsp.

anatolicum, commonly known as Anatolian deadnettle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Turkey and the Caucasus. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a hairy surface, and it is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
