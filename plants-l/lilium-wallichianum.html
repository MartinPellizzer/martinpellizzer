Lilium wallichianum

Lilium wallichianum, commonly known as the Himalayan tiger lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stem and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The flowers are typically orange-red with a dark red throat, and they bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped, grass-like leaves and produces bulbous underground stems.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
