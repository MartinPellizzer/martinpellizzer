Lilium canadense var. coccineum

Lilium canadense var. coccineum, commonly known as the Canadian red lily, is a variant of the Canadian lily native to North America.

It is characterized by its striking red to orange flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a fan-like pattern.

The bulbs are edible when young and have been used traditionally by Indigenous peoples.
