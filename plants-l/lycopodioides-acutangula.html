Lycopodioides acutangula

Lycopodioides acutangula, commonly known as the narrow-leaved clubmoss, is a small evergreen shrub belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and needle-like leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, cone-like structures called strobili, which contain the reproductive organs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices.

Lycopodioides acutangula is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
