Leucas mollissima var. sebastiana

Leucas mollissima var. sebastiana, commonly known as the silky leucas, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. It is a perennial herb that blooms throughout the warmer months.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental species within its native habitat.
