Lycopodium kajewskii

Lycopodium kajewskii, commonly known as the Kajewskii clubmoss, is a species of lycophyte native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems that bear whorls of small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces spore-bearing cones at the tips of its branches, which are typically clustered in groups of three. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas.

Lycopodium kajewskii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological systems.
