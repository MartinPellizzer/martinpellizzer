Lindmania dendritica

Lindmania dendritica, commonly known as the clustered bamboo palm, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family, which includes palms, and is characterized by its dense, rosette-like arrangement of leaves. The plant features a short, unbranched stem with a cluster of long, narrow leaves that are often covered in a waxy layer. Its inflorescences produce small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by small, edible fruits.

Lindmania dendritica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
