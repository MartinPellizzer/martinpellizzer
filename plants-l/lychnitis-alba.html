Lychnitis alba

Lychnitis alba, commonly known as the white dead-nettle, is a flowering plant in the family Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white, two-lipped flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Lychnitis alba is often found in disturbed soils and is considered an indicator species for certain ecological conditions.
