Launaea wassavensis

Launaea wassavensis, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette growth habit and hairy, succulent leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by small, spiky fruits that disperse seeds by wind. Launaea wassavensis is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of desert flora.
