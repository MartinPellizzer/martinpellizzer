Litsea singaporensis

Litsea singaporensis, commonly known as Singapore laurier, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurel and avocado trees. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, narrow leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, dark purple drupe that contains a single seed.

Litsea singaporensis is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal properties.
