Ligusticopsis rechingeriana

Ligusticopsis rechingeriana, commonly known as the Kashmir mountain dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of many small florets. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes at high altitudes. Its leaves are basal, lanceolate, and covered with fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a single head.

Ligusticopsis rechingeriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
