Lespedeza rosea

Lespedeza rosea, commonly known as rose lespedeza, is a perennial flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of pink to red flowers. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its stems are often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woods, fields, and along roadsides.

Lespedeza rosea is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
