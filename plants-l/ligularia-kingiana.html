Ligularia kingiana

Ligularia kingiana, commonly known as the giant marsh plant, is a striking perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 inches in diameter, with a distinctive white to yellowish margin. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers in late summer, which are clustered in dense, terminal inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shady environments, often found near streams or in wetlands.

Its foliage is highly ornamental, making it a popular choice for water gardens and bog landscapes.
