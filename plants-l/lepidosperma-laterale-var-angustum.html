Lepidosperma laterale var. angustum

Lepidosperma laterale var. angustum, commonly known as narrowleaf paperbark sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to Australia.

It typically grows in wetland or swampy environments, often forming dense mats near water bodies. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a whitish, powdery coating. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are clustered in spikelets, and it produces small, three-lobed berries.

This species is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for native wildlife.
