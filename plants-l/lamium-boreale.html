Lamium boreale

Lamium boreale, commonly known as northern deadnettle, is a perennial herb native to northern regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces and small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Lamium boreale is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
