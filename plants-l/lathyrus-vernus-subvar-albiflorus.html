Lathyrus vernus subvar. albiflorus

Lathyrus vernus subvar. albiflorus, commonly known as the white-flowered vetch, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe.

It features slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in early spring. Its flowers are followed by elongated, bean-like pods containing several seeds.

This variety is often found in woodland edges and meadows, where it thrives in partial shade to full sun.
