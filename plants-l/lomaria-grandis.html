Lomaria grandis

Lomaria grandis, commonly known as the giant lomaria, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. This plant is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. Its flowers are showy, with vibrant purple or red hues, attracting various pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

Lomaria grandis is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained, sunny conditions.
