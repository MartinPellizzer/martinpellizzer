Limnetis juncea var. monogyna

Limnetis juncea var. monogyna, commonly known as the narrow-leaved water shield, is a aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in shallow, slow-moving waters and is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that float on the water surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are often submerged or partially submerged, contributing to its ecological role in supporting aquatic life. Its rhizomes help stabilize sediment and improve water clarity by filtering out particulate matter.

Limnetis juncea var. monogyna is an important species in wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
