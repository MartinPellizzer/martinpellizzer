Laurus semecarpifolia

Laurus semecarpifolia, commonly known as the Mediterranean laurel, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is a large evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and aromatic scent. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by black, egg-shaped berries. Its leaves are often used in cooking and traditional medicine due to their aromatic properties.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular ornamental and culinary plant.
