Lathyrus utahensis

Lathyrus utahensis, commonly known as Utah vetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three leaflets, and it has a sprawling or climbing growth habit.

Utah vetch is adapted to withstand drought conditions and is often found in desert scrub habitats.
