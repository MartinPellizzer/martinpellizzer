Lonicera fulva

Lonicera fulva, commonly known as the yellow honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are usually yellowish-green in color. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range from yellow to orange, typically blooming in early spring. Its red, berry-like fruits are edible and attract wildlife.

Lonicera fulva is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
