Leda radicans

Leda radicans, commonly known as the creeping bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a sprawling growth habit with rhizomatous stems that allow it to spread across the ground. The plant produces showy blue to purple bell-shaped flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, oval, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Leda radicans is often found in moist, woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
