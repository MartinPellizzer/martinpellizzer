Lavoisiera glandulifera var. genuina

Lavoisiera glandulifera var. genuina, commonly known as the glandular lavoisiera, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features upright, branched stems covered in small, glandular hairs that secrete sticky substances. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in dense, terminal clusters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky outcrops.
