Lippia schlimii var. glabrescens

Lippia schlimii var. glabrescens, commonly known as the glabrous lippia, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
