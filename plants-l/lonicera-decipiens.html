Lonicera decipiens

Lonicera decipiens, commonly known as the false honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features opposite, oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its berries are red and edible, though they are not as prized as those of other honeysuckle species.

Lonicera decipiens is often found in moist woodlands and along stream banks.
