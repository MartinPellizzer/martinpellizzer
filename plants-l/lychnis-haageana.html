Lychnis haageana

Lychnis haageana, commonly known as the red campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the carnation family (Caryophyllaceae) and is characterized by its striking pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, woodland edges, and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Lychnis haageana blooms from late spring to early autumn, adding vibrant color to its natural habitat.
