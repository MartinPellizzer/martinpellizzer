Lotus neoincanus

Lotus neoincanus, commonly known as the Andean lotus, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant with distinctive palmate leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as cloud forests and mountain meadows. Its flowers bloom in late summer and early autumn, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and has cultural significance in some indigenous communities.
