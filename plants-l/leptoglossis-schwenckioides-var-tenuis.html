Leptoglossis schwenckioides var. tenuis

Leptoglossis schwenckioides var. tenuis, commonly known as the slender-leaved leek orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in cloud forests or along riverbanks. Its unique morphology and habitat requirements make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered vulnerable and requires careful protection.
