Linaria salzmannii var. gracilis

Linaria salzmannii var. gracilis, commonly known as Salzmann's horned poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and bright orange to red flowers that resemble horns. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain the seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
