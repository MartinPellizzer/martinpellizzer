Lespedeza juncea f. kanaoriensis

Lespedeza juncea f. kanaoriensis, commonly known as the Kanaori lespedeza, is a subspecies native to Japan.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, such as nitrogen fixation and soil stabilization.
