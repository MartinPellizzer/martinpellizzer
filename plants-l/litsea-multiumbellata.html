Litsea multiumbellata

Litsea multiumbellata, commonly known as the many-fruited litsea, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocado, and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, narrow leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns red when mature, often attracting birds and other wildlife.

Litsea multiumbellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
