Leopoldia atropatana

Leopoldia atropatana, commonly known as the black leopoldia, is a rare and striking orchid native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black petals and sepals, which give it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
