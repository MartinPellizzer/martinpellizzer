Lochia bracteata f. ciliata

Lochia bracteata f. ciliata, commonly known as the hairy bract leek, is a variant of the species *Lochia bracteata*, which belongs to the family Amaryllidaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy bracts that enclose the inflorescence, giving it a unique appearance. The plant typically grows in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, white or pale purple flowers.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
