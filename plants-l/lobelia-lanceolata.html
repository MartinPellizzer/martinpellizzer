Lobelia lanceolata

Lobelia lanceolata, commonly known as the narrowleaf lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features slender, lance-shaped leaves and produces spikes of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.

Lobelia lanceolata is often used in gardens for its ability to thrive in partial shade and its attractive floral display.
