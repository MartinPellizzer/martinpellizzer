Lobelia minutula var. kiwuensis

Lobelia minutula var. kiwuensis, commonly known as the Kiwuense Lobelia, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the tropical rainforests of East Africa.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in mountainous regions with high humidity. The plant features delicate, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, attracting various pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth texture and a slightly hairy underside.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance and ecological significance.
