Linnaea borealis f. hilarula

Linnaea borealis f. hilarula, commonly known as the "Little Wintergreen," is a variant of the common wintergreen plant native to the northern regions of North America and Eurasia.

This low-growing evergreen shrub features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. Its flowers are fragrant, white, and borne in pairs, often appearing in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded forest understories.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
