Leonurus artemisia var. albiflorus

Leonurus artemisia var. albiflorus, commonly known as whiteflower hedgehog.

It is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia, characterized by its hairy stems and small, white flowers. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties. Its leaves are often lance-shaped and covered in fine pubescence, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grassy areas.
