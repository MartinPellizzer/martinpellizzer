Lonchocarpus neurophyllus var. oligophyllus

Lonchocarpus neurophyllus var. oligophyllus, commonly known as the yellow or red senna, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is notable for its striking yellow or red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape.

The plant also produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.
