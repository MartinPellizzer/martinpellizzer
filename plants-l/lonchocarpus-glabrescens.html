Lonchocarpus glabrescens

Lonchocarpus glabrescens, commonly known as the yellow cowpea, is a tropical legume native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces cylindrical pods containing several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is commonly found in disturbed soils and along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and agricultural purposes.
