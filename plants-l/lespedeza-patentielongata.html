Lespedeza patentielongata

Lespedeza patentielongata, commonly known as the narrowleaf lespedeza, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and disturbed sites, preferring well-drained soils. It is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.

Its flowering period usually occurs in late summer, adding ornamental value to the landscape.
