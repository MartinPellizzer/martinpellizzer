Lysimachia mauritiana var. rubida

Lysimachia mauritiana var. rubida, commonly known as the red-flowered yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red flowers that bloom in dense clusters, often contrasting with its bright yellow stamens. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or wetlands. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its stems are usually prostrate or ascending.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in horticulture due to its striking color contrast and adaptability to various soil conditions.
