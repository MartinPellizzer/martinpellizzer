Lepanthes carunculigera

Lepanthes carunculigera, commonly known as the red-tipped orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking red sepals and petals, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical forests. Its flowers are small but numerous, often blooming in clusters during the rainy season.

Lepanthes carunculigera is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
