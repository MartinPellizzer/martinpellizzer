Lippia costaricensis

Lippia costaricensis, commonly known as the Costa Rican verbena, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Lippia costaricensis is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
