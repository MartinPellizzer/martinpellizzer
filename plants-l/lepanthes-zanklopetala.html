Lepanthes zanklopetala

Lepanthes zanklopetala, commonly known as the Zanklopetala Orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a slender, wiry stem and a single, showy flower that typically blooms in the spring. The plant features elliptical, dark green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. Its flowers are characterized by their pale yellow to white petals and a distinctive, hooded lip.

Lepanthes zanklopetala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
