Lagophylla filipes

Lagophylla filipes, commonly known as the slender-leaved ghost flower, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, thread-like leaves and small, pale blue or white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its unique appearance and restricted habitat make it a rare and ecologically significant species.

Conservation efforts are ongoing to protect this plant from habitat loss and over-collection.
