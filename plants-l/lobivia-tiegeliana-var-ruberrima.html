Lobivia tiegeliana var. ruberrima

Lobivia tiegeliana var. ruberrima, commonly known as the Red-Flowered Lobivia, is a compact, succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It features fleshy, ribbed stems that store water, enabling it to thrive in dry conditions. The plant produces vibrant red flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. Its leaves are typically small and rounded, often covered in a fine, powdery coating.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ease of care, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and succulent collections.
