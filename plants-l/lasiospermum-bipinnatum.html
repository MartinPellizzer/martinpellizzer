Lasiospermum bipinnatum

Lasiospermum bipinnatum, commonly known as the two-pinnate mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that primarily inhabits the branches of certain trees in tropical and subtropical regions.

It possesses a unique structure with a short, woody stem and a dense covering of fine, white hairs, which give it its scientific name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by small, fleshy berries that serve as a food source for various birds and mammals. Its parasitic nature allows it to draw water and nutrients from its host, while still performing photosynthesis to some extent.

This species plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat, contributing to seed dispersal and providing sustenance for wildlife.
