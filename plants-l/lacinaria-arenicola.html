Lacinaria arenicola

Lacinaria arenicola, commonly known as the sand lichen, is a unique species of lichen that thrives in arid and coastal environments.

It is characterized by its crust-like growth form, which allows it to adhere tightly to rocky surfaces and withstand harsh conditions. The plant's thallus is typically pale to dark gray, with a granular texture that aids in moisture retention. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing microhabitats for other organisms in its ecosystem.

Due to its specialized habitat requirements, Lacinaria arenicola is often found in regions with low humidity and high salinity.
