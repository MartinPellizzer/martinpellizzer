Lupinus purosericeus

Lupinus purosericeus, commonly known as the silverleaf lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves and erect, sparsely branched stems. The plant produces showy, pea-like flowers in shades of purple and pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Lupinus purosericeus thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

It is a member of the legume family and has the ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
