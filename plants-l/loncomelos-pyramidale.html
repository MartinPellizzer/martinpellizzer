Loncomelos pyramidale

Loncomelos pyramidale, commonly known as the pyramidale tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, orange-colored fruits.

Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.
