Leucojum nicaeense

Leucojum nicaeense, commonly known as the Nicaean white squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers in early spring. The plant is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its bulbs are toxic and should be handled with care.

Leucojum nicaeense is valued in gardens for its elegant appearance and hardy nature.
