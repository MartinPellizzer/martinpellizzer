Linum caespitosum

Linum caespitosum, commonly known as spiked flax, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces fluffy, seed-containing pods that are often used in traditional herbal medicine. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Linum caespitosum is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its delicate beauty and easy care requirements.
