Lobivia pseudocachensis

Lobivia pseudocachensis, commonly known as the Cachanilla or Cachanilla Lobivia, is a species of flowering plant in the cactus family, Cactaceae.

It is native to the arid regions of central and southern Peru, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. It is a slow-growing, compact cactus that is highly valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in controlled environments to ensure its survival.
