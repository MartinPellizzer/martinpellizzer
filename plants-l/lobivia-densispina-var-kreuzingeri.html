Lobivia densispina var. kreuzingeri

Lobivia densispina var. kreuzingeri, commonly known as the Kreuzinger's dense spine lobivia, is a compact, low-growing cactus native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It features a short, cylindrical stem covered in dense, white to pale yellow spines, which provide protection from herbivores and intense sunlight. The plant produces small, vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and early summer, adding a striking contrast to its spiny structure. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts and collectors.
