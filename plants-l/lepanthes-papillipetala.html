Lepanthes papillipetala

Lepanthes papillipetala, commonly known as the papillate-lobed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink with intricate patterns. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually elliptical and covered in a dense, velvety pubescence.

Lepanthes papillipetala thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.
