Lobivia formosa var. bertramiana

Lobivia formosa var. bertramiana, commonly known as the Bertram's Lobivia, is a compact, low-growing succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It features short, cylindrical stems covered in dense, woolly white hair, which helps protect it from intense sunlight and water loss. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking contrast to its gray-green foliage. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth, it is a prized specimen among succulent enthusiasts.
