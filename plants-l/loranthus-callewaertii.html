Loranthus callewaertii

Loranthus callewaertii, commonly known as the Congo mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of various host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Loranthaceae family and has a unique symbiotic relationship with its host, drawing water and nutrients while providing some ecological benefits. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are often clustered together. Its berries are typically red or orange, containing seeds that are dispersed by birds.

Loranthus callewaertii plays a role in forest ecosystems by influencing host tree growth and supporting bird populations through its fruit.
