Lygodesmia aphylla var. texana

Lygodesmia aphylla var. texana, commonly known as Texas leathery greenbriar, is a low-growing, woody vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, opposite, elliptic leaves that are typically leathery in texture and have a dark green upper surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its fruits are small, dry, one-seeded capsules that mature in late summer.

This species is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and disturbed areas.
