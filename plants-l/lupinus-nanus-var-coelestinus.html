Lupinus nanus var. coelestinus

Lupinus nanus var. coelestinus, commonly known as the sky-blue lupine, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features small, clustered flowers in shades of blue to violet, which bloom in early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
