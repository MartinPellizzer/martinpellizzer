Lippia callicarpifolia

Lippia callicarpifolia, commonly known as purpleleaf lippia, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring aromatic leaves and showy purple or pink flowers. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture. Its flowers are clustered in terminal panicles and bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Lippia callicarpifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its vibrant foliage and fragrant properties.
