Linum komarovii subsp. komarovii

Linum komarovii subsp.

komarovii, commonly known as the Komarov's flax, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy.

The plant produces showy blue or violet flowers in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or arid environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential in ecological restoration projects.
