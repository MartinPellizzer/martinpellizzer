Lycium confertum

Lycium confertum, commonly known as the denseberry lycium, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, clustered berries. The plant produces yellowish flowers that develop into red or orange berries, which are edible but have a tart flavor. Lycium confertum is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, with deep roots that help it access water.

It is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental qualities in landscaping.
