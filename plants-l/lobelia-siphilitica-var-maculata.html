Lobelia siphilitica var. maculata

Lobelia siphilitica var. maculata, commonly known as the Indian blanket flower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, with a distinctive white or yellow spot near the base of each petal. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often spotted or streaked with white. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
