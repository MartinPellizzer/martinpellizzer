Lycium patagonicum

Lycium patagonicum, commonly known as the Patagonian boxthorn, is a shrub native to southern South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible and often used in local cuisine. Lycium patagonicum is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

Its botanical features make it an important plant for both ecological and traditional uses in the region.
