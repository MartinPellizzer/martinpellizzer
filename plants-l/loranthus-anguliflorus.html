Loranthus anguliflorus

Loranthus anguliflorus, commonly known as the angular-flowered mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on a variety of host trees.

It belongs to the Loranthaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, angular-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruits are typically berries that contain seeds covered in a sticky substance to aid in attachment to potential host trees.

Loranthus anguliflorus is found in tropical and subtropical regions, often thriving in humid, shaded environments.
