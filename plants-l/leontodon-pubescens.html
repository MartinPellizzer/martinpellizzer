Leontodon pubescens

Leontodon pubescens, commonly known as the hairy dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are hairy and alternate, with a basal rosette structure, and it produces yellow flowers that develop into fluffy seed heads.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
