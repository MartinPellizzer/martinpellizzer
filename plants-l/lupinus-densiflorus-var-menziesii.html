Lupinus densiflorus var. menziesii

Lupinus densiflorus var. menziesii, commonly known as the Menzies' lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, spike-like clusters of blue to violet flowers and compound, trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen through its root nodules.

This variety is often found in coastal regions and is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
