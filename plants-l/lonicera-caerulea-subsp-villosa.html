Lonicera caerulea subsp. villosa

Lonicera caerulea subsp.

villosa, commonly known as the hairy honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its subspecific name "villosa.".

The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its berries, which are typically red or yellow, are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor.

This subspecies is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and in traditional medicine.
