Luzula laetevirens

Luzula laetevirens, commonly known as the greenish woodrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves that are typically bright green. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, greenish-brown flowers. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Luzula laetevirens is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in wet areas.
