Lophiolepis turkestanica subsp. turkestanica

Lophiolepis turkestanica subsp.

turkestanica, commonly known as the Turkestan lophiolepis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or reddish in color. It is adapted to arid conditions and has a deep root system for water retention.

This subspecies is of interest in botanical studies due to its ecological resilience and potential medicinal properties.
