Limonium protohermaeum

Limonium protohermaeum, commonly known as the narrowleaf sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Limonium protohermaeum thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or sandy environments.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
