Lotus minoriflorus

Lotus minoriflorus, commonly known as the small-flowered lotus, is a species native to parts of Asia, particularly India and Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. The plant features a short stem with broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair. Its flowers are small and pale pink, with five petals and a prominent yellow stamen, blooming in late summer.

The roots of Lotus minoriflorus are edible and have been traditionally used in local cuisines and herbal remedies.
