Lycopersicon humboldtii

Lycopersicon humboldtii, commonly known as the Humboldt tomato, is a wild species native to South America.

It is a herbaceous plant with hairy stems and alternate, lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are typically less than 1 inch in diameter. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

This species is closely related to domesticated tomatoes and has been studied for its potential in breeding programs.
