Lemooria

Lemooria, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the misty highlands of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its translucent, waxy leaves that shimmer in the dim light, giving it an ethereal appearance. The plant produces delicate, pale-blue flowers that bloom only during the night, releasing a faint, sweet fragrance. Lemooria thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires specific soil conditions to flourish.

Its unique botanical features have made it a subject of fascination for botanists and herbalists alike.
