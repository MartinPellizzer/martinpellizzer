Linaria elegans var. gracilescens

Linaria elegans var. gracilescens, commonly known as the slender toadflax, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, branching stems and small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
