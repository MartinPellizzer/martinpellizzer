Lilium persicum

Lilium persicum, commonly known as the Persian lily, is a species of bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with broad, strap-like leaves arranged in a fan shape. Its bulbs are perennial, and it blooms in late summer to early autumn.

The Persian lily is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
