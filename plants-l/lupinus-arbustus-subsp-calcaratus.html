Lupinus arbustus subsp. calcaratus

Lupinus arbustus subsp.

calcaratus, commonly known as the Sierra lupine, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers.

The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces legume pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
