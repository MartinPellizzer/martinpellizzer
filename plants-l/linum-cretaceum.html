Linum cretaceum

Linum cretaceum, commonly known as the chalk flax or sea flax, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its delicate blue or purple flowers and linear, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. Linum cretaceum thrives in sandy or coastal soils and is often found in salty or wind-swept environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in traditional medicine.
