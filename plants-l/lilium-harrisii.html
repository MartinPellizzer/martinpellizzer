Lilium harrisii

Lilium harrisii, commonly known as the Harris' lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or open woodland habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, arranged in a basal rosette.

Lilium harrisii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
