Ludia spinosa

Ludia spinosa, commonly known as the spiked lantana, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring dense, spiny foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are usually yellow or orange in color, blooming throughout the warmer months. Ludia spinosa has small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

It is well adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
