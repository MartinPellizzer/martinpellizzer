Lachanodes prenanthiflora

Lachanodes prenanthiflora, commonly known as the "spiny leaf orchid," is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiny leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, with a unique, intricate structure.

Lachanodes prenanthiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
