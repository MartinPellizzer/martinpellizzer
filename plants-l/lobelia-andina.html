Lobelia andina

Lobelia andina, commonly known as Andean lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its delicate blue or violet flowers and hairy, often glandular leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Lobelia andina is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional Andean cultures.
