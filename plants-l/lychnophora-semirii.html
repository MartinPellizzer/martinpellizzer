Lychnophora semirii

Lychnophora semirii, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height, featuring dense, aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, fleshy berries. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed.

The plant is adapted to humid, tropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.
