Linum viscosum

Linum viscosum, commonly known as sticky flax, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Linum viscosum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
