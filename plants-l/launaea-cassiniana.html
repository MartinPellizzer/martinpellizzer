Launaea cassiniana

Launaea cassiniana, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and white. The plant has a succulent stem and leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powder, which helps reduce water loss. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Launaea cassiniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
