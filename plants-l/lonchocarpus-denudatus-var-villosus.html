Lonchocarpus denudatus var. villosus

Lonchocarpus denudatus var. villosus, commonly known as the hairy senna, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features prominently hairy stems and leaves, which contribute to its distinctive appearance. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, and it produces elongated, woody pods that contain several seeds.

It is often used in traditional medicine and has ecological importance as a nitrogen-fixing plant.
