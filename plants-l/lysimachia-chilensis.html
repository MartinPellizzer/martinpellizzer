Lysimachia chilensis

Lysimachia chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean yellow-eyed grass, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America, particularly Chile.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. Lysimachia chilensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
