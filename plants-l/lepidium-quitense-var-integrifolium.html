Lepidium quitense var. integrifolium

Lepidium quitense var. integrifolium, commonly known as the Peruvian mustard, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and pinnately compound leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are typically pollinated by insects. Its seeds are enclosed in siliques, which are elongated, pod-like fruits.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
