Lychnis drummondii

Lychnis drummondii, commonly known as Drummond's pink, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the carnation family, Caryophyllaceae, and is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches and features simple, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its delicate appearance and vibrant color make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
