Ladenbergia pedunculata

Ladenbergia pedunculata, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The tree produces elongated, woody pods that contain small, dark seeds. It is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in furniture and construction.

The plant also has traditional medicinal uses in some African cultures.
