Luzula campestris var. subspicata

Luzula campestris var. subspicata, commonly known as the spike grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, three-lobed flowers. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Luzula campestris var. subspicata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
