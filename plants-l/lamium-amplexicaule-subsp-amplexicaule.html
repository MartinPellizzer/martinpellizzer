Lamium amplexicaule subsp. amplexicaule

Lamium amplexicaule subsp.

amplexicaule, commonly known as greater deadnettle, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features heart-shaped leaves that are often clasping around the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it an important plant in ecological gardens.
