Lepidium transvaalense

Lepidium transvaalense, commonly known as the Transvaal cress, is a small flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant produces small white or yellow flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are typically pinnately divided. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

Lepidium transvaalense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
