Lamprocephalus montanus

Lamprocephalus montanus, commonly known as the mountain larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and forest edges. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in late spring to early summer, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive hairy texture, while its stem is usually hairy as well.
