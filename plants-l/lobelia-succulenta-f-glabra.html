Lobelia succulenta f. glabra

Lobelia succulenta f. glabra, commonly known as the smooth lobelia, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features fleshy, thick leaves that store water, an adaptation to its arid environment. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple. Its smooth, glabrous leaves distinguish it from other lobelia species.

It is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a decorative succulent due to its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.
