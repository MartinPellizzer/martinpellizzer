Lycopersicon esculentum var. bukasovii

Lycopersicon esculentum var. bukasovii, commonly known as the Bukasovii tomato, is a botanical variant of the domesticated tomato.

It is characterized by its unique fruit morphology, which includes a distinctive shape and coloration compared to standard tomatoes. This variety is native to the Caucasus region and exhibits traits adapted to local climatic conditions. Botanically, it belongs to the Solanaceae family and is closely related to other tomato species.

Its genetic makeup offers potential for breeding programs aimed at improving disease resistance and yield in tomato cultivation.
