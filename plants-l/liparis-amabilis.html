Liparis amabilis

Liparis amabilis, commonly known as the velvet orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and striking, orchid-like flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest undergrowth. Its roots are fibrous and often form symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi.

Liparis amabilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in tropical gardens.
