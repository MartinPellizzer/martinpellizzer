Laurus canariensis

Laurus canariensis, commonly known as the Canary Island laurel, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like bay laurel. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, spice-like scent. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark blue berries.

Laurus canariensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of essential oils.
