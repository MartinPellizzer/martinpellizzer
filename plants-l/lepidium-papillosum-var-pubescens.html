Lepidium papillosum var. pubescens

Lepidium papillosum var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy peppergrass, is a low-growing annual plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many flowering plants with small, cross-shaped flowers. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which contribute to its common name. It produces small, yellow flowers that are typically arranged in racemes.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
