Leontodon farinosus

Leontodon farinosus, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive white or pale pink border.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
