Lupinus densiflorus var. dudleyi

Lupinus densiflorus var. dudleyi, commonly known as Dudley's lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, showy clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance as a food source for native pollinators and wildlife.
