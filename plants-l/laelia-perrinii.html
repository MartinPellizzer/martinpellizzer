Laelia perrinii

Laelia perrinii, commonly known as the Perrin's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the high Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green. The plant typically blooms during the summer months, and its flowers are often clustered in racemes. Laelia perrinii prefers cool, bright conditions and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for orchid enthusiasts.

It is also noted for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of growing environments.
