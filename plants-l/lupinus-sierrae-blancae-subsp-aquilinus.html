Lupinus sierrae-blancae subsp. aquilinus

Lupinus sierrae-blancae subsp.

aquilinus, commonly known as the Sierra White Lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of white to pale blue flowers.

The plant has digitate leaves composed of several oval to elliptic leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a food source for native pollinators.
