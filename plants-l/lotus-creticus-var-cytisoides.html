Lotus creticus var. cytisoides

Lotus creticus var. cytisoides, commonly known as the yellow lotus or golden lotus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are trifoliate, meaning they consist of three leaflets, and the plant is often found in wetland habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
