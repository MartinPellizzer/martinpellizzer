Loranthus similis

Loranthus similis, commonly known as the similar mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches itself to the branches of host trees, primarily in the families Fabaceae and Rutaceae.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while it provides some organic compounds through photosynthesis using its own leaves. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers that are often clustered in short racemes. Its berries are typically red or yellow and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

Loranthus similis is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it plays a role in ecological interactions with its host and wildlife.
