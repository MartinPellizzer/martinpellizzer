Liatris punctata var. punctata

Liatris punctata var. punctata, commonly known as the purpletop blazingstar, is a native perennial wildflower found in the eastern and central United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, spires of purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often growing in prairies and open woodlands.

Liatris punctata var. punctata is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
