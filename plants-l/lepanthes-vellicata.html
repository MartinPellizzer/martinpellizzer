Lepanthes vellicata

Lepanthes vellicata, commonly known as the velvet orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety purple flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments, often on tree trunks or branches. Its leaves are elliptical and dark green, providing a lush backdrop to its vibrant blossoms.

Lepanthes vellicata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
