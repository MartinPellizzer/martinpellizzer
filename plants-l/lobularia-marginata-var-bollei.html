Lobularia marginata var. bollei

Lobularia marginata var. bollei, commonly known as the Bolle's lobularia, is a low-growing, flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often blooming throughout the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to thrive in well-drained, sandy soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
