Lythrum silenoides

Lythrum silenoides, commonly known as purple loosestrife, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, spires of vibrant purple flowers that bloom from midsummer to early autumn. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the square, hairy stems. Its root system consists of rhizomes, which allow it to spread rapidly and become invasive in some regions.

Lythrum silenoides is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and lakes.
