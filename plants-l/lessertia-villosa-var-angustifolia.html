Lessertia villosa var. angustifolia

Lessertia villosa var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved velvetleaf, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticultural practices.
