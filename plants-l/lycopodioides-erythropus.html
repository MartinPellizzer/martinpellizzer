Lycopodioides erythropus

Lycopodioides erythropus, commonly known as the red clubmoss, is a member of the Lycopodiaceae family and is characterized by its small, erect, spore-bearing cones.

It is a primitive vascular plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its reddish stems and scale-like leaves, which are arranged in whorls around the stem. Lycopodioides erythropus reproduces via spores rather than seeds, and its life cycle involves both a gametophytic and sporophytic stage.

This plant plays an important ecological role in maintaining soil stability and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
