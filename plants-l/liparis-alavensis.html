Liparis alavensis

Liparis alavensis, commonly known as the Alava orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and woodland areas. It has a rhizomatous root system and produces small, lance-shaped leaves.

Liparis alavensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
