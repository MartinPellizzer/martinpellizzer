Lonchocarpus brachypterus

Lonchocarpus brachypterus, commonly known as the small-winged senna, is a tropical legume native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom during the dry season. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential pharmaceutical applications.
