Ledenbergia rosea-aenea

Ledenbergia rosea-aenea, commonly known as the golden leaved orchid, is a unique epiphytic orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that display a combination of rose and aenea (bronze) hues, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts interested in rare orchids.
