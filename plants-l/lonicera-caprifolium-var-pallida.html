Lonicera caprifolium var. pallida

Lonicera caprifolium var. pallida, commonly known as pale honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, arching stems with opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are usually light green and slightly hairy. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its berries are small, red, and ornamental, often persisting into autumn.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
