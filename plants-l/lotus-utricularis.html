Lotus utricularis

Lotus utricularis, commonly known as the bladderwort, is a unique aquatic plant found in freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its small, bladder-like structures that trap small aquatic organisms, aiding in nutrient absorption. The plant has delicate, floating leaves and produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged. It is native to regions such as Africa, Asia, and Australia, thriving in still or slow-moving waters.

Due to its specialized adaptations, it plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by contributing to nutrient cycling and providing habitat for microorganisms.
