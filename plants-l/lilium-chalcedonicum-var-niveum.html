Lilium chalcedonicum var. niveum

Lilium chalcedonicum var. niveum, commonly known as the white tunicated lily, is a rare and striking variant of the tunicated lily.

It is characterized by its pale white to cream-colored flowers, which often have a slight pinkish tinge. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and thrives in cool, moist environments.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in specialized gardens.
