Lepanthes monoptera

Lepanthes monoptera, commonly known as the single-lobed slipper orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, hooded flowers. The plant typically features a short, erect stem with a few broad, elliptical leaves. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with a distinctive pouch-like structure at the base.

Lepanthes monoptera thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.
