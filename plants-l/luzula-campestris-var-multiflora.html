Luzula campestris var. multiflora

Luzula campestris var. multiflora, commonly known as the meadow sweetflag, is a perennial grass-like plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Poaceae and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, greenish flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
