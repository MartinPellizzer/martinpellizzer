Leontodon berinii

Leontodon berinii, commonly known as the Italian dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stem is erect and hairy.

The Italian dandelion is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
