Lobivia shaferi var. albiflora

Lobivia shaferi var. albiflora, commonly known as the Whiteflower Lobivia, is a succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rounded shape and thick, fleshy stems. The plant produces small, white flowers during the warmer months, which contrast beautifully with its green or bluish-green foliage. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
