Lomanthus putcalensis

Lomanthus putcalensis, commonly known as the African sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and compound leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by flat, pod-like fruits. It is often found in woodland areas and can reach heights of up to 20 meters.

Lomanthus putcalensis is valued for its hard, durable wood and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
