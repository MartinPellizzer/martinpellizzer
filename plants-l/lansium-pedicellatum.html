Lansium pedicellatum

Lansium pedicellatum, commonly known as the mangosteen tree, is a tropical evergreen plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces edible fruits with a bright red or yellow exterior and a white, segmented, sweet flesh. Its botanical features include a woody trunk, compound leaves, and small, winged seeds.

The plant is valued not only for its fruit but also for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
