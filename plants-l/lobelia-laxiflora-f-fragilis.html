Lobelia laxiflora f. fragilis

Lobelia laxiflora f. fragilis, commonly known as the slender-flowered lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features delicate, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its fragrant flowers and compact growth habit.
