Lamium flexuosum subsp. berengueri

Lamium flexuosum subsp.

berengueri, commonly known as the Canary Islands deadnettle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Canary Islands. It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its creeping growth habit and hairy stems.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stems. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and covered with fine hairs.

This subspecies is adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a resilient plant in its native habitat.
