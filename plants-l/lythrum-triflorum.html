Lythrum triflorum

Lythrum triflorum, commonly known as redroot sedgewort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lythraceae family and is characterized by its upright, herbaceous growth habit. The plant produces showy, clustered pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Lythrum triflorum is often found in moist soils along stream banks and in wet meadows.
