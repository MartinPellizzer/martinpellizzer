Lasianthus repens

Lasianthus repens, commonly known as the creeping lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, spreading habit. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy, and it produces small, tubular flowers in shades of pink or purple. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
