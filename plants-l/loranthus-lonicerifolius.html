Loranthus lonicerifolius

Loranthus lonicerifolius, commonly known as the Indian mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on a variety of host trees, primarily in the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Loranthaceae and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while it provides some benefits through the sugars it produces. It is often found in moist, shaded environments and is used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Its parasitic nature and unique ecological role make it an interesting subject in botanical studies.
