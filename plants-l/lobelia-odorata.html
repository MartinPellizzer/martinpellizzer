Lobelia odorata

Lobelia odorata, commonly known as sweet peppergrass, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in clusters atop slender stems. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it thrives in moist, shady environments. Lobelia odorata is known for its fragrant blooms, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
