Lotus corniculatus var. pratensis

Lotus corniculatus var. pratensis, commonly known as the meadow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a hairy texture, often with a bluish or purplish hue.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is commonly found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.
