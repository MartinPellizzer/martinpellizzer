Lamium nepetifolium

Lamium nepetifolium, commonly known as the pennywort or deadnettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive silver-white underside, which gives it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, two-lipped flowers in shades of purple or pink, typically arranged in whorls along the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Lamium nepetifolium is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
