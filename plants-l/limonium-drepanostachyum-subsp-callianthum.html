Limonium drepanostachyum subsp. callianthum

Limonium drepanostachyum subsp.

callianthum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and produces showy blue to purple flowers.

The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its flowers are typically borne in dense, spike-like clusters at the top of the stems.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate salty and windy conditions.
