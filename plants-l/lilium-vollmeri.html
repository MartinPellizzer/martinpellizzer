Lilium vollmeri

Lilium vollmeri, commonly known as the Vollmer's lily, is a species of bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its tall, straight stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces clusters of white or pale yellow blossoms that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a basal rosette.

Lilium vollmeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
