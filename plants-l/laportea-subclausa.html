Laportea subclausa

Laportea subclausa, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems covered in fine, hairy hairs. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a serrated margin and a slightly hairy surface.

It produces small, clustered purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
