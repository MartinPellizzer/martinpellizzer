Leucopogon pedicellatus

Leucopogon pedicellatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in heathlands and coastal areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces small, dark berries as fruit.

Leucopogon pedicellatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
