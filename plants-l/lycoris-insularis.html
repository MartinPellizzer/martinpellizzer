Lycoris insularis

Lycoris insularis, commonly known as the island lily, is a flowering plant native to Japan and parts of East Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking red flowers that bloom in the fall. The plant features strap-like, narrow leaves that are typically evergreen, and its bulbous root system allows it to store nutrients for seasonal growth. Lycoris insularis thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique blooming cycle and vibrant color make it a notable addition to botanical collections.
