Lindera griffithii

Lindera griffithii, commonly known as Griffith's spicebush, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern Himalayas.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and avocados, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and can reach heights of up to 2 meters. Its berries are dark blue to black and are an important food source for birds and small mammals.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its fragrant foliage.
