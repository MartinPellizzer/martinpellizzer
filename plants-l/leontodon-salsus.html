Leontodon salsus

Leontodon salsus, commonly known as the salt leontodon, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the dandelion family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and basal rosette of leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, saline soils and is often found in coastal areas or disturbed sites. It has a taproot system and produces seeds with pappus for wind dispersal.

Leontodon salsus is generally not considered invasive and plays a minor role in local ecosystems.
