Lychnis subintegra

Lychnis subintegra, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, pink to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are opposite or whorled along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and wildflower landscapes.
