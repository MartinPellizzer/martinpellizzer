Ligularia afghanica

Ligularia afghanica, commonly known as the Afghan ligularia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Afghanistan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads and often aromatic foliage. The plant features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with prominent veins, and it produces yellow-orange flower heads that bloom in the summer. Ligularia afghanica typically grows in moist, shady environments and is adapted to high-altitude conditions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
