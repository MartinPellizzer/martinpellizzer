Libinhania sphaerocephala

Libinhania sphaerocephala, commonly known as the globe-headed bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive spherical flower heads, which are composed of numerous small, bell-shaped blooms. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and thrives in temperate to subtropical climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine layer of white or grayish hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
