Lilium longiflorum subsp. wallichianum

Lilium longiflorum subsp.

wallichianum, commonly known as the Himalayan lily, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly smaller flowers and more robust foliage compared to the common Easter lily. It prefers cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and has cultural significance in various regions of Asia.
