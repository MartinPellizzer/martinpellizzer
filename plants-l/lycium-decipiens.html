Lycium decipiens

Lycium decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive lycium, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries, which are edible but can be mildly toxic in large quantities. Lycium decipiens is often found in coastal areas and thrives in well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ecological research.
