Lotus alpicola

Lotus alpicola, commonly known as the alpine lotus, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of the Himalayas and Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments. Its leaves are trifoliate, and the flowers are usually pink or purple, blooming in late summer.

Lotus alpicola plays a role in stabilizing alpine ecosystems and is of interest to botanists studying plant adaptation to extreme climates.
