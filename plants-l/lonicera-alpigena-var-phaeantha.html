Lonicera alpigena var. phaeantha

Lonicera alpigena var. phaeantha, commonly known as the alpine honeysuckle, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are typically hairy and have a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, which bloom in late spring to early summer. These flowers are followed by red, berry-like fruits that are attractive to birds.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.
