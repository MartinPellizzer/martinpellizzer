Linnaea parvisignata

Linnaea parvisignata, commonly known as the small-flowered twinflower, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, fragrant, white flowers with five petals, which bloom in early spring. Linnaea parvisignata is often found in moist, acidic soils in shaded forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the southeastern deciduous forest ecosystem.
