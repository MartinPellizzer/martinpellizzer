Luzula parviflora subsp. parviflora

Luzula parviflora subsp.

parviflora, commonly known as the narrowleaf woodrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features slender, grass-like leaves that are usually less than 1 millimeter wide. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Luzula parviflora subsp.

parviflora is often found in nutrient-rich soils and is characteristic of montane and subalpine habitats.
