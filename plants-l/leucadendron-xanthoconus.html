Leucadendron xanthoconus

Leucadendron xanthoconus, commonly known as the yellow conebush, is a flowering shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow to orange flower heads, which are surrounded by bracts that can range in color from green to red. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, and it is well adapted to coastal and inland fynbos habitats.

The yellow conebush is an important component of the fynbos biome and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
