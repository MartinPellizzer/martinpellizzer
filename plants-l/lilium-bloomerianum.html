Lilium bloomerianum

Lilium bloomerianum, commonly known as the Bloomer's lily, is a species of flowering plant in the Liliaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with tall, slender stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. Its blooms appear in late summer to early autumn, and the plant is known for its strong, sweet fragrance.

Lilium bloomerianum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
