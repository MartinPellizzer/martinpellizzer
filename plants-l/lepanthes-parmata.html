Lepanthes parmata

Lepanthes parmata, commonly known as the silver star orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking white to pale purple flowers with a distinctive yellow or orange center. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in the understory of tropical forests. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

Lepanthes parmata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
