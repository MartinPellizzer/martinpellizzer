Lepanthes bogariniana

Lepanthes bogariniana, commonly known as the Bogarin's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments. The plant produces delicate, showy flowers with a distinctive coloration, often featuring a mix of white, pink, and purple hues. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the forest floor, and it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.

Lepanthes bogariniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
