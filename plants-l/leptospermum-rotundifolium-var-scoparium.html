Leptospermum rotundifolium var. scoparium

Leptospermum rotundifolium var. scoparium, commonly known as silver honey bush, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its silver-gray, narrow leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are rich in antioxidants. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subalpine regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native habitats.
