Lotus hispidus proles castellanus

Lotus hispidus proles castellanus, commonly known as the Castellan lotus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright, sparsely hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces pink or reddish flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its historical use in herbal medicine is noted for its potential anti-inflammatory properties.
