Leucopogon pendulus

Leucopogon pendulus, commonly known as the weeping heath, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its drooping clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or acidic soils and thrives in coastal or heathland environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, evergreen leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white or grey hair.

The weeping heath is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
