Lycium salsum var. chilense

Lycium salsum var. chilense, commonly known as Chilean boxthorn, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to harsh climates.

Botanically, it belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshade plants.
