Leptocarpus thamnochortoides

Leptocarpus thamnochortoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered blue or purple flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in dune environments. It has a short, creeping stem and produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Leptocarpus thamnochortoides is adapted to salt spray and is an important component of coastal vegetation.
