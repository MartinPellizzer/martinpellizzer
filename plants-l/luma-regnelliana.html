Luma regnelliana

Luma regnelliana, commonly known as the silver birch, is a deciduous tree native to the southern regions of South America.

It is characterized by its white, peeling bark and delicate, pendulous catkins. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers in the spring, which are followed by winged seeds dispersed by the wind. Its leaves are oval-shaped and turn a striking golden color in the autumn.

Luma regnelliana is often found in dry, open habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
