Lunania cubensis

Lunania cubensis, commonly known as the Cuban moonflower, is a species of flowering plant native to Cuba.

It is a member of the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. Lunania cubensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its unique nocturnal blooming habit.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
