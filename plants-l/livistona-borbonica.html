Livistona borbonica

Livistona borbonica, commonly known as the Bourbon palm, is a species of cycad native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery, compound leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by hard, woody fruits containing seeds. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
