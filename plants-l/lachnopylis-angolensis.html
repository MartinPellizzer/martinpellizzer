Lachnopylis angolensis

Lachnopylis angolensis, commonly known as the Angolan lichen, is a unique plant species native to southern Africa.

It is a lichen, which is a symbiotic organism composed of a fungus and a photosynthetic partner, typically an alga or cyanobacterium. This species is characterized by its crust-like growth form, often adhering tightly to rock surfaces in arid or semi-arid environments. Lachnopylis angolensis plays a crucial role in soil stabilization and nutrient cycling in its native habitats.
