Linociera latifolia

Linociera latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf linociera, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its bark is fibrous and has been traditionally used in local medicine for its potential healing properties.

Linociera latifolia thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.
