Lindera meissneri

Lindera meissneri, commonly known as the Meissner's spicebush, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, elliptical leaves that are often tinged with red or purple on the underside. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by red, berry-like fruits.

Lindera meissneri is typically found in moist, shaded forest environments and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
