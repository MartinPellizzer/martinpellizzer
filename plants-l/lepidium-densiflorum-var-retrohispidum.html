Lepidium densiflorum var. retrohispidum

Lepidium densiflorum var. retrohispidum, commonly known as the denseflower wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense, branched inflorescences that produce small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine, glandular hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
