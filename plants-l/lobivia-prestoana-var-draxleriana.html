Lobivia prestoana var. draxleriana

Lobivia prestoana var. draxleriana, commonly known as the Draxler's Lobivia, is a succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It features compact, rounded rosettes of fleshy, blue-green leaves that are densely packed and often covered in a fine white powdery coating. The plant produces tall, branching stems that bear clusters of small, yellow flowers during the growing season. It is well adapted to hot, dry climates and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its slow growth and unique appearance make it a prized addition to many succulent collections.
