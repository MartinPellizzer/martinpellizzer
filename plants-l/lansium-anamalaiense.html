Lansium anamalaiense

Lansium anamalaiense, commonly known as the Anamalai lansium, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface and a lighter underside. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible fruits with a unique, sweet-sour flavor. Its fruit, known as the "star fruit" or "carambola," is prized for its culinary and medicinal uses.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in dense forest understories.
