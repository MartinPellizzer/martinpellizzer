Linum lecheoides

Linum lecheoides, commonly known as the milkweed flax or yellow flax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Linum lecheoides is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for pollinators like bees and butterflies.
