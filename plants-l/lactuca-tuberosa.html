Lactuca tuberosa

Lactuca tuberosa, commonly known as the Jerusalem artichoke, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, leafy stalks and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the late summer. The plant produces edible tubers that are rich in inulin, a type of dietary fiber. These tubers are often used in cooking and have a slightly sweet, nutty flavor.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
