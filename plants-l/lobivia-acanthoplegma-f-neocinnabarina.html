Lobivia acanthoplegma f. neocinnabarina

Lobivia acanthoplegma f. neocinnabarina, commonly known as the red-tipped lobivia, is a fascinating succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rounded growth habit. The plant features short, cylindrical stems that are often tinged with a reddish hue, especially at the tips. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, emerging from the axils of the leaves.

This variety is prized for its unique coloration and adaptability to dry, well-draining soil conditions.
