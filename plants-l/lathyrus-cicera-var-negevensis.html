Lathyrus cicera var. negevensis

Lathyrus cicera var. negevensis, commonly known as the Negev vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pink to purple, often appearing in clusters. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

This species is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.
