Lissochilus juncifolius

Lissochilus juncifolius, commonly known as the rushleaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and delicate, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as cloud forests and mountainous areas. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
