Leptopus orbicularis

Leptopus orbicularis, commonly known as the globe cactus, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its spherical shape and thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, blooming during the warmer months. Its leaves are reduced to spines, which help to minimize water loss and deter herbivores.

Overall, Leptopus orbicularis is an example of adaptive evolution in desert flora, showcasing specialized features for survival in extreme conditions.
