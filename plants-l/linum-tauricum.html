Linum tauricum

Linum tauricum, commonly known as the Tauric flax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of blue or purple flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. Linum tauricum is often found in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun.

It has been historically used for its fibers and seeds, though its ornamental value is also appreciated in gardens.
