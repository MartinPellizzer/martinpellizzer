Liparis ambohimangana

Liparis ambohimangana, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Madagascar.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a pseudobulb and long, narrow leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
