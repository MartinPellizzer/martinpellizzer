Lycium fuchsioides

Lycium fuchsioides, commonly known as the purple boxthorn, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its spiny stems and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal and sandy environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers, which range in color from white to violet, bloom in spring and are followed by small, red berries.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
