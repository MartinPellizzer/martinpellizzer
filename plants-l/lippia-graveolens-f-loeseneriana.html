Lippia graveolens f. loeseneriana

Lippia graveolens f. loeseneriana, commonly known as Mexican oregano, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves that emit a strong, pungent scent, particularly when crushed. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale purple, arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for both ornamental and culinary use.

Its essential oils contain compounds like thymol and carvacrol, contributing to its medicinal and aromatic properties.
