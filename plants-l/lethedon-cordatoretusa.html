Lethedon cordatoretusa

Lethedon cordatoretusa, commonly known as the heart-shaped leaf plant, is a unique species characterized by its distinctive cordate (heart-shaped) leaves.

This plant exhibits a compact growth habit with leaves that are often a vibrant green, sometimes with a slight variegation. It is native to tropical regions and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant's foliage is not only aesthetically pleasing but also plays a role in its photosynthetic efficiency.

While it is not widely cultivated, it is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and potential ecological adaptations.
