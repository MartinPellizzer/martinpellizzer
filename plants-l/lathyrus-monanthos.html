Lathyrus monanthos

Lathyrus monanthos, commonly known as the tall vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces striking blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and is often found in meadows and along roadsides. Lathyrus monanthos is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in temperate climates.

Its seeds are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines, though the plant contains compounds that can be toxic in large quantities.
