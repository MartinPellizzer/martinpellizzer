Lycium tweedyanum var. pruinosum

Lycium tweedyanum var. pruinosum, commonly known as the silverleaf boxthorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny growth and small, often reddish or yellowish berries. The leaves are typically covered in a fine, silvery or whitish pubescence, giving it its common name. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
