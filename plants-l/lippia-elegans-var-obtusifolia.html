Lippia elegans var. obtusifolia

Lippia elegans var. obtusifolia, commonly known as Mexican mint or sweet basil, is a herbaceous plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture. The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers that are clustered in terminal spikes, typical of the Lamiaceae family. It is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Lippia elegans var. obtusifolia thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy and adaptable plant.
