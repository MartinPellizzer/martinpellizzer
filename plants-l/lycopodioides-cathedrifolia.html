Lycopodioides cathedrifolia

Lycopodioides cathedrifolia, commonly known as the cathedral fern, is a species of clubmoss belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, feathery fronds that resemble the architecture of a cathedral, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its sporophytes are small and often clustered, while the gametophytes are delicate and short-lived.

Lycopodioides cathedrifolia plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
