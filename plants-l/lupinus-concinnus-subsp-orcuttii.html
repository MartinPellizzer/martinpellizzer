Lupinus concinnus subsp. orcuttii

Lupinus concinnus subsp.

orcuttii, commonly known as the Orcutt's lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of blue to violet pea-like flowers. The subspecies is distinguished by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and is often found in rocky soils and open habitats.

It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility, and is of interest in ecological and botanical studies.
