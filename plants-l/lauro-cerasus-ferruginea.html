Lauro-cerasus ferruginea

Lauro-cerasus ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty cherry or Prunus ferruginea, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its dark green, ovate leaves that turn reddish-brown in autumn. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, reddish-black berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or rocky habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
