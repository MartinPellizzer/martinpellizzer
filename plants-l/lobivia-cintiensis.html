Lobivia cintiensis

Lobivia cintiensis, commonly known as the Cinti's lobivia, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape with ribbed stems. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It has a slow growth rate and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is often cultivated by succulent enthusiasts.
