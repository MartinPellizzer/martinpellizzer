Lepistemon lignosus

Lepistemon lignosus, commonly known as the rough lepistemon, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers clustered in terminal spikes, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
