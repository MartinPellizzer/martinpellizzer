Lathyrus pannonicus subsp. austriacus

Lathyrus pannonicus subsp.

austriacus, commonly known as the Austrian vetch, is a perennial legume native to Central Europe. It features slender, upright stems with trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along forest edges.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen, benefiting soil fertility.
