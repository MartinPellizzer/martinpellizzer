Lobivia haematantha f. drijveriana

Lobivia haematantha f. drijveriana, commonly known as the Drijver's red lobivia, is a rare and striking succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, cylindrical stems that are typically reddish in color, especially when exposed to full sun. The plant produces clusters of tubular, bright orange flowers during the summer months, which are highly attractive to pollinators. Its leaves are small, fleshy, and often covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss.

This variety is prized by collectors for its unique coloration and relatively easy care requirements when provided with the right growing conditions.
