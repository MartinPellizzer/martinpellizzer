Listera flabellata

Listera flabellata, commonly known as the bell-shaped wintergreen, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Listera flabellata is often found growing in clusters, with its delicate appearance making it a notable wildflower in its native habitat.
