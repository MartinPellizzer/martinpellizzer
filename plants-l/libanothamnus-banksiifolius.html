Libanothamnus banksiifolius

Libanothamnus banksiifolius, commonly known as the Banks' flatleaf, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern regions of Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as heathlands and rocky slopes.
