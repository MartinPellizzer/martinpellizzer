Lacmellea armata

Lacmellea armata, commonly known as the coral tree, is a striking plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, branching structure and vibrant coral-colored flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique floral display and adaptability contribute to its appeal in both natural and cultivated settings.
