Lathyrus luteus var. gmelinii

Lathyrus luteus var. gmelinii, commonly known as the yellow vetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia and parts of the Middle East.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features trifoliate leaves with a yellowish-green color and produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. Its stems are usually hairy and can become woody with age, while its roots form a dense network that helps stabilize soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in arid environments.
