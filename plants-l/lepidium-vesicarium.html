Lepidium vesicarium

Lepidium vesicarium, commonly known as bladder mustard, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and pungent odor, typical of many members of its family. The plant produces small, round, bladder-like seed pods that are a distinctive feature. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Lepidium vesicarium is an annual herb that can be invasive in certain regions.
