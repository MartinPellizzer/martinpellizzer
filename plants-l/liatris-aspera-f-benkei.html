Liatris aspera f. benkei

Liatris aspera f. benkei, commonly known as the Benkei panicled grass, is a botanical variant of the panicled blazing star.

It is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States, characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescences and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, arranged in dense, branched clusters. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and prairies.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
