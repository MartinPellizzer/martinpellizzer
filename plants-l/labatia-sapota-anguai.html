Labatia sapota-anguai

Labatia sapota-anguai, commonly known as the Brazilian starfruit or golden star.

It is a flowering plant native to the Amazon rainforest, characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often consumed fresh or used in traditional recipes. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
