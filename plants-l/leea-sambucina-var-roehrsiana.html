Leea sambucina var. roehrsiana

Leea sambucina var. roehrsiana, commonly known as the Rosy Leea, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Leeaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely distributed outside its native range.
