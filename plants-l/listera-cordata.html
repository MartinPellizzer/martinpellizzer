Listera cordata

Listera cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf basketflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite and have a distinctive cordate shape, while its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

Listera cordata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
