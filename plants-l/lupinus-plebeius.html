Lupinus plebeius

Lupinus plebeius, commonly known as the common lupine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies, meadows, and disturbed soils. The plant features upright, branching stems covered in soft, hairy foliage. Its showy, pea-like flowers are usually blue, purple, or white, appearing in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Lupinus plebeius is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it an important plant in ecological restoration efforts.
