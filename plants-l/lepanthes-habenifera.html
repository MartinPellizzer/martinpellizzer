Lepanthes habenifera

Lepanthes habenifera, commonly known as the golden tailed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow petals and sepals, which contrast with its greenish-white dorsal sepal. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical forests. It produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom during the rainy season.

Lepanthes habenifera is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
