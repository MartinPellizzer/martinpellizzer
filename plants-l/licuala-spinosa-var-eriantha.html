Licuala spinosa var. eriantha

Licuala spinosa var. eriantha, commonly known as the velvet palm, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense, velvety texture, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows as an understory species in humid, shaded environments, often found in lowland rainforests. Its leaves are not only aesthetically pleasing but also have cultural and traditional uses in various indigenous communities.

The plant's botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm climates.
