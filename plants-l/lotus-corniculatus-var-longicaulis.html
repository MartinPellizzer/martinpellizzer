Lotus corniculatus var. longicaulis

Lotus corniculatus var. longicaulis, commonly known as the slender bird's-foot trefoil, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged on a slender stem. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
