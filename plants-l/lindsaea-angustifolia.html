Lindsaea angustifolia

Lindsaea angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf lindsaea, is a species of fern that belongs to the family Bromeliaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette-like pattern. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences. It is native to tropical regions of the Americas, particularly in Central and South America.

Lindsaea angustifolia thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
