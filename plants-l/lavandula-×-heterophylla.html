Lavandula × heterophylla

Lavandula × heterophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lavender, is a hybrid shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, spike-like inflorescences of purple to pink flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are gray-green in color, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Lavandula × heterophylla is valued for its ornamental beauty and mild aromatic properties.
