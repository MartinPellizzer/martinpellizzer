Lycopodium scandens

Lycopodium scandens, commonly known as the climbing clubmoss, is a primitive vascular plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its creeping, branching stems that often climb or trail along the ground. The plant produces small, spirally arranged leaves and produces spores in cone-like structures called strobila. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas.

Lycopodium scandens plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
