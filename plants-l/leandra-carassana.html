Leandra carassana

Leandra carassana, commonly known as the Mexican orchid or golden orchid, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow and green flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded forest environments. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the soil.

Leandra carassana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
