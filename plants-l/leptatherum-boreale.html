Leptatherum boreale

Leptatherum boreale, commonly known as northern woodland grass, is a perennial grass species native to the northern regions of North America.

It typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in boreal forests and wetlands. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually less than 10 cm in length. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers.

Leptatherum boreale plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
