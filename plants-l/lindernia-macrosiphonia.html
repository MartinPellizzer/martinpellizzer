Lindernia macrosiphonia

Lindernia macrosiphonia, commonly known as the large-lipped lindernia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its flowers are tubular with a distinctive two-lipped structure. It blooms in late spring to early summer, producing showy pink to purple flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Lindernia macrosiphonia is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
