Lobivia rossii var. carminata

Lobivia rossii var. carminata, commonly known as the Crimson Lobivia, is a succulent native to South America, specifically Argentina.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rounded shape and thick, ribbed stems. The plant produces vibrant red to orange flowers during the summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green foliage. Its leaves are typically short and cylindrical, adapted to store water in arid environments.

This variety is prized for its unique coloration and is often cultivated as a decorative cactus in gardens and greenhouses.
