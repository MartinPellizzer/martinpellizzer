Limnanthes alba var. detonsa

Limnanthes alba var. detonsa, commonly known as the white marsh plant or marsh milkweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features showy, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has a fibrous root system and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in wet or moist soils, often found in marshes, pond edges, and stream banks.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it an important plant for ecological diversity.
