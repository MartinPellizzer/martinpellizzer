Leucothoe revoluta var. blanchetii

Leucothoe revoluta var. blanchetii, commonly known as the Blanquet's leucothoe, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers in the spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
