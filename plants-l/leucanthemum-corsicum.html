Leucanthemum corsicum

Leucanthemum corsicum, commonly known as Corsican daisy, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features basal rosettes of silver-white, woolly leaves and produces daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate coastal conditions.
