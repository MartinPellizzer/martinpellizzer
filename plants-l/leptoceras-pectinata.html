Leptoceras pectinata

Leptoceras pectinata, commonly known as the fringed lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, hairy textures. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple, with a distinctive fringed appearance along the edges. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
