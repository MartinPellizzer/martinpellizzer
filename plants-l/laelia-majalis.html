Laelia majalis

Laelia majalis, commonly known as the Alpine orchid, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is a perennial herb with a compact growth habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant features a pseudobulb that stores water and nutrients, typical of many orchid species. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapted to withstand cold and harsh environmental conditions.

Laelia majalis thrives in well-drained soil and requires ample sunlight to bloom successfully.
