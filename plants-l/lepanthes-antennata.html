Lepanthes antennata

Lepanthes antennata, commonly known as the Antennate Orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist environments within tropical forests. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers with a unique shape that resembles an antenna, giving it its common name. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with intricate patterns that attract specific pollinators.

Lepanthes antennata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
