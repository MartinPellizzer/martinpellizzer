Liatris punctata f. coloradensis

Liatris punctata f. coloradensis, commonly known as the Colorado blazing star, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its tall, spiky flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, with a rough texture, and its flowers are usually pink to purple in color.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
