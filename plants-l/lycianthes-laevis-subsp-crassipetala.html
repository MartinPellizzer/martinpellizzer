Lycianthes laevis subsp. crassipetala

Lycianthes laevis subsp.

crassipetala, commonly known as the broadleaf nightshade, is a species native to Central and South America. It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its hairy stems and broad, ovate leaves.

The flowers are typically yellow with five petals and are borne in clusters. The fruit is a fleshy, orange-red berry that contains several seeds.

This plant is often found in disturbed habitats and is part of the nightshade family, Solanaceae.
