Lepidium campestre proles smithii

Lepidium campestre proles smithii, commonly known as the coastal peppergrass, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of North America.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, yellow flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along shorelines and dunes. It has small, pinnately divided leaves and produces numerous seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Lepidium campestre proles smithii is often found in disturbed coastal habitats and is adapted to withstand salt spray and strong winds.
