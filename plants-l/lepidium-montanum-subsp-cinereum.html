Lepidium montanum subsp. cinereum

Lepidium montanum subsp.

cinereum, commonly known as the dark lousewort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It is a small, herbaceous annual or biennial species that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, branched clusters, and its leaves are usually narrow and linear. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard family plants.

The subspecies is distinguished by its dark, almost black, stems and leaves, giving it its common name.
