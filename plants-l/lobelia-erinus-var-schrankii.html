Lobelia erinus var. schrankii

Lobelia erinus var. schrankii, commonly known as the dwarf red lobelia, is a compact, trailing plant native to South Africa.

It features small, tubular flowers that range in color from red to pink, often with a contrasting white throat. The plant has succulent, hairy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette or trailing fashion. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
