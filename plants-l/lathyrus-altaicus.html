Lathyrus altaicus

Lathyrus altaicus, commonly known as the Altai vetchling, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant features slender stems and clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. Its leaves are pinnately compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

Lathyrus altaicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
