Leptospermum porophyllum

Leptospermum porophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tea tree, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and small, white to pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant is part of the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.

The species is known for its aromatic foliage and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
