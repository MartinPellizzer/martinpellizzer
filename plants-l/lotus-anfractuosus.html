Lotus anfractuosus

Lotus anfractuosus, commonly known as the rock lotus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its robust, woody stems and dense clusters of small, pink or purple flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens with suitable climates.
