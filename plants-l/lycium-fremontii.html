Lycium fremontii

Lycium fremontii, commonly known as the fremont's boxthorn, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellow flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible for humans and wildlife. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along desert margins.

Lycium fremontii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert fauna.
