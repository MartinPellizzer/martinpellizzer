Licania parviflora var. conduplicata

Licania parviflora var. conduplicata, commonly known as the Brazilian flame tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking orange to red flowers that appear in clusters, typically during the rainy season. The plant is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, with compound leaves and a tendency to produce aerial roots. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in disturbed or secondary forest areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm regions.
