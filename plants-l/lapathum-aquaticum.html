Lapathum aquaticum

Lapathum aquaticum, commonly known as water betony, is a herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in Europe.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers arranged in spikes. The plant thrives in shallow water or damp soil, making it a common sight in aquatic environments. It is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported calming and healing properties.

Its presence indicates healthy wetland ecosystems due to its specific habitat requirements.
