Leucothoe mariana

Leucothoe mariana, commonly known as the Florida leucothoe, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in forest understories. The plant features small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red berries in the fall. Its leaves are elliptical and have a smooth texture, turning reddish in the autumn.

Leucothoe mariana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
