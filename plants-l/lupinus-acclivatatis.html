Lupinus acclivatatis

Lupinus acclivatatis, commonly known as the mountain lupine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds.

Lupinus acclivatatis is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.
