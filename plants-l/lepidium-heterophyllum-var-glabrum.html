Lepidium heterophyllum var. glabrum

Lepidium heterophyllum var. glabrum, commonly known as the smooth rockcress, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with fleshy, succulent stems and leaves that are typically covered in a fine, smooth layer of hair, giving it its varietal name "glabrum.". The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring, which are arranged in clusters along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Lepidium heterophyllum var. glabrum is adapted to withstand drought and extreme temperatures, making it a hardy species in its native environment.
