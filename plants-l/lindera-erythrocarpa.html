Lindera erythrocarpa

Lindera erythrocarpa, commonly known as the red-fruited spicebush, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes other aromatic plants like cinnamon and avocado. The plant is characterized by its opposite, simple, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small yellow flowers appear in early spring, followed by bright red, fleshy berries that mature in the fall.

The berries are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.
