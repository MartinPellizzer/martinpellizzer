Lythrum wilsonii

Lythrum wilsonii, commonly known as Wilson's loosestrife, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lythraceae family, which includes other flowering plants such as the poinsettia. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along streams, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. It produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stem, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
