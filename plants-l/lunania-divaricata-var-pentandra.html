Lunania divaricata var. pentandra

Lunania divaricata var. pentandra, commonly known as the red or orange moon cactus, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its vivid, showy flowers.

It is native to the Caribbean and features a distinct, swollen caudex that stores water and nutrients. The plant produces clusters of fleshy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its unique appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and containers.
