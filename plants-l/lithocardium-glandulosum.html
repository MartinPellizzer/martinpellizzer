Lithocardium glandulosum

Lithocardium glandulosum, commonly known as the woolly stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a woolly appearance. The plant produces small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Lithocardium glandulosum is a hardy, drought-tolerant species that is well adapted to arid environments.
