Lepidostemon penstemonoides

Lepidostemon penstemonoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved beardtongue, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces spikes of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typical of the Plantaginaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
