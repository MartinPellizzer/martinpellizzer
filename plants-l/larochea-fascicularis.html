Larochea fascicularis

Larochea fascicularis, commonly known as the clustered larochea, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically has woody stems that are covered in a thick, hairy layer, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. Its leaves are narrow and often arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Larochea fascicularis is adapted to survive in low-nutrient soils and can enter dormancy during prolonged dry periods.
