Lagerstroemia flos-reginae

Lagerstroemia flos-reginae, commonly known as the regal lagerstroemia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy bracts that resemble petals, which come in shades of red, pink, and purple. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are followed by pale blue to purple berries. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and have a smooth texture, turning red in the fall.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
