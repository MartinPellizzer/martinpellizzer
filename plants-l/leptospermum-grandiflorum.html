Leptospermum grandiflorum

Leptospermum grandiflorum, commonly known as the coastal tea tree, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from spring to autumn. It thrives in coastal environments and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils.

Leptospermum grandiflorum is also valued for its aromatic foliage and historical use in traditional medicine.
