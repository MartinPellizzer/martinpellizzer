Lippia whytei

Lippia whytei, commonly known as the Australian verbena, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or blue in color. Lippia whytei is often found in dry, open habitats such as woodlands and grasslands.
