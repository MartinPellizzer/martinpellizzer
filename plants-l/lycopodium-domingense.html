Lycopodium domingense

Lycopodium domingense, commonly known as the Dominican clubmoss, is a small evergreen fern-like plant native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae and is characterized by its spore-bearing cones and simple, needle-like leaves. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures, called strobila, are clustered at the tips of its stems.

Lycopodium domingense plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
