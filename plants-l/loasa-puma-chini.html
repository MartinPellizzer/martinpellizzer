Loasa puma-chini

Loasa puma-chini, commonly known as the Andean loasa, is a shrub native to the high Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at elevations ranging from 2,500 to 4,000 meters. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Loasa puma-chini is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in temperate climates.
