Lepanthes panisca

Lepanthes panisca, commonly known as the Panisca orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid with delicate, often fragrant flowers that typically bloom in the summer months. The plant features a short, fleshy rhizome and produces showy, spurred orchid flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its rarity and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable species in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
