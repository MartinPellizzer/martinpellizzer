Lilium giganteum

Lilium giganteum, commonly known as the giant lily, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter, often in shades of white, pink, or red. The plant features a robust stem that can grow up to six feet tall, supporting its impressive blooms. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, arranged in a fan-like pattern along the stem.

Lilium giganteum thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking dramatic floral displays.
