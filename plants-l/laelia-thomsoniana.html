Laelia thomsoniana

Laelia thomsoniana, commonly known as Thomson's orchid, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its elegant, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces pendulous, yellowish-green blooms with a distinctive red or orange lip. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

Laelia thomsoniana is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and adaptability to cultivation.
