Lauro-cerasus sphaerocarpa brasiliensis

Lauro-cerasus sphaerocarpa brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Eugenia brasiliensis*, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Brazil.

It features elliptical, glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, red, cherry-like berries. The berries are rich in antioxidants and are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in Brazilian flora.
