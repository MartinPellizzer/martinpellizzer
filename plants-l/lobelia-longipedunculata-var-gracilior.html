Lobelia longipedunculata var. gracilior

Lobelia longipedunculata var. gracilior, commonly known as slender-stemmed lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along streambanks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
