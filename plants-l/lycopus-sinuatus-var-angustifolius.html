Lycopus sinuatus var. angustifolius

Lycopus sinuatus var. angustifolius, commonly known as narrow-leaved water huckleberry, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. Its small, blue to purple flowers appear in clusters and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but have a bitter taste.
