Leontodon saxatilis var. arenarius

Leontodon saxatilis var. arenarius, commonly known as the sandy leontodon, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disk florets. This perennial herb typically grows in dry, sandy or gravelly soils, often in rocky or coastal areas. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, and its leaves are narrow and linear with a rough texture.

It is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
