Lyrochilus

Lyrochilus, commonly known as the lyre orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its unique lyre-shaped lip, which is a distinguishing botanical feature. The plants typically have elongated, strap-like leaves and produce small, fragrant flowers in a range of colors. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, Lyrochilus species are often found in shaded, moist environments.

These orchids are valued for their ornamental appeal and are cultivated for their beauty and ecological significance.
