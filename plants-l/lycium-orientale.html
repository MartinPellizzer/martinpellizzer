Lycium orientale

Lycium orientale, commonly known as Chinese boxthorn, is a shrub native to Asia, particularly China and the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, orange-red berries that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes and potatoes, and is known for its toxic alkaloids. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Lycium orientale thrives in temperate climates and is often found in rocky or arid environments.
