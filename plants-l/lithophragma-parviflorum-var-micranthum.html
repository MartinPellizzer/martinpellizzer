Lithophragma parviflorum var. micranthum

Lithophragma parviflorum var. micranthum, commonly known as the small-flowered rock beauty, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky slopes. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. It has a shallow root system and is adapted to thrive in cool, humid climates.
