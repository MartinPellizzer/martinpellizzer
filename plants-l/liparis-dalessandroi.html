Liparis dalessandroi

Liparis dalessandroi, commonly known as the Dalessandro's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forests or rocky slopes. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are often pale green or white with intricate patterns.

Liparis dalessandroi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
