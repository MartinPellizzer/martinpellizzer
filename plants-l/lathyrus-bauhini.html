Lathyrus bauhini

Lathyrus bauhini, commonly known as the Persian vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia, including parts of Turkey and the Caucasus. The plant features tendrils that help it climb and support its growth on other plants or structures. Its flowers are typically blue or violet, with a distinctive spurred petal, and it produces small, elongated pods containing several seeds.

Lathyrus bauhini is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
