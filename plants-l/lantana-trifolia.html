Lantana trifolia

Lantana trifolia, commonly known as wild lantana, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, colorful flowers that range from yellow to orange and red. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are usually hairy and have a somewhat serrated edge. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in disturbed soils.

Lantana trifolia is also known for its strong fragrance and is sometimes used in ornamental gardening.
