Lastrea robertiana var. longula

Lastrea robertiana var. longula, commonly known as the long-leaf lastrea, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a fine, powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss.

It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
