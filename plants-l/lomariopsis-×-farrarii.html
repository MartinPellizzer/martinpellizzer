Lomariopsis × farrarii

Lomariopsis × farrarii, commonly known as the hybrid staghorn fern, is a natural hybrid between Lomariopsis harrisonii and Lomariopsis filicaulis.

It is a member of the Polypodiaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, antler-like fronds that give it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees or other structures in tropical and subtropical regions. Its fronds are usually dark green with a leathery texture and can reach lengths of up to 2 meters.

Lomariopsis × farrarii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in shaded, humid environments.
