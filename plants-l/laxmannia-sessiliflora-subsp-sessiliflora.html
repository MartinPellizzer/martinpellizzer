Laxmannia sessiliflora subsp. sessiliflora

Laxmannia sessiliflora subsp.

sessiliflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved laxmannia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features upright, hairy stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to alpine gardens.
