Lachenalia ensifolia subsp. maughanii

Lachenalia ensifolia subsp.

maughanii, commonly known as the Maughan's sword lachenalia, is a species native to southern Africa. It is a bulbous perennial plant characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers.

The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of yellow to orange, often with a red or purple throat. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is widely cultivated by succulent enthusiasts.
