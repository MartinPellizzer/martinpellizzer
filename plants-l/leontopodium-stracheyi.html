Leontopodium stracheyi

Leontopodium stracheyi, commonly known as the edelweiss, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, woolly leaves and its distinctive, showy yellow flower with white petals. The plant thrives in high altitudes where the climate is cold and the soil is rocky. It is often found in mountainous areas of the Alps, Pyrenees, and Carpathians.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, it is protected in many regions.
