Libertia pulchella

Libertia pulchella, commonly known as the silver fern, is a striking evergreen shrub native to New Zealand.

It features slender, arching stems with bright green, lance-shaped leaves that have a distinctive silvery underside. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers in spring, which are followed by cylindrical seed pods. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its unique foliage and hardy nature contribute to its appeal in both horticulture and ecological settings.
