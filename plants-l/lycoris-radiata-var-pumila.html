Lycoris radiata var. pumila

Lycoris radiata var. pumila, commonly known as the dwarf Chinese lantern plant, is a compact variant of the common lycoris.

It features striking red-orange flowers that bloom in late summer, resembling small lanterns. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces bulbous underground stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or woodland settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique floral display and foliage structure.
