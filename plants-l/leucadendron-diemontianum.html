Leucadendron diemontianum

Leucadendron diemontianum, commonly known as the silver fire bush, is a South African shrub belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, silvery-gray foliage and striking, cone-shaped flower heads that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant is native to the Western Cape region and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to full sun conditions.

Leucadendron diemontianum is also valued for its role in supporting local biodiversity and its use in traditional medicine.
