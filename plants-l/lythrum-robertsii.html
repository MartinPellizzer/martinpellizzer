Lythrum robertsii

Lythrum robertsii, commonly known as purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Lythraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of showy, violet-pink flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands and moist soils, making it a common sight in prairie and wetland ecosystems. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slight hairy texture.

The flowers bloom in late summer and early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
