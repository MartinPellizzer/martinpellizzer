Lychnocephaliopsis suffruticulosa

Lychnocephaliopsis suffruticulosa, commonly known as the rock daisy, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces small, white or pale pink flowers clustered in dense heads. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that are often aromatic and adapted to dry, rocky environments. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and arid landscapes.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive addition to xerophytic plant collections.
