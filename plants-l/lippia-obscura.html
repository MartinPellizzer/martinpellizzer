Lippia obscura

Lippia obscura, commonly known as wild thyme or Mexican thyme, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, sunny environments such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant features small, fragrant leaves and produces clusters of tiny, white or pale pink flowers. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their aromatic and potentially therapeutic properties.

Lippia obscura is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in various climates.
