Leopoldia bormaniana

Leopoldia bormaniana, commonly known as the Borman's leopoldia, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pale yellow. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest undergrowth, where it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine, hairy texture that helps retain moisture.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Leopoldia bormaniana is considered a conservation concern.
