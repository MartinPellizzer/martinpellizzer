Landolphia nitens

Landolphia nitens, commonly known as the yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a woody vine that can grow up to 10 meters in length, with thick, fleshy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking yellow flowers that are often clustered in umbels, followed by orange-red berries containing seeds. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
