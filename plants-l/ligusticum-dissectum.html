Ligusticum dissectum

Ligusticum dissectum, commonly known as the Japanese parsley or Japanese celery, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It features finely dissected, fern-like leaves that are aromatic and often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family. Its roots are valued for their medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.

Ligusticum dissectum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.
