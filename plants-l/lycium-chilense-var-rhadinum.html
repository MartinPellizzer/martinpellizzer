Lycium chilense var. rhadinum

Lycium chilense var. rhadinum, commonly known as the Chilean boxthorn, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ornamental gardening and for erosion control.
