Lepisorus xiphiopteris

Lepisorus xiphiopteris, commonly known as the golden polypody, is a fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Polypodiaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are bi-lobed and have a distinctive golden hue on the underside, which becomes more prominent as the plant matures.

Lepisorus xiphiopteris is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in a humid, indoor setting.
