Lobelia laxiflora var. insignis

Lobelia laxiflora var. insignis, commonly known as the showy lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, tubular blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture. This variety thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

It is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and other moist habitats, contributing to the biodiversity of its native environment.
