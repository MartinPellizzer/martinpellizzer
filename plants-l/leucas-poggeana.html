Leucas poggeana

Leucas poggeana, commonly known as the pink leucas, is a flowering plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. Its leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and covered in a fine layer of hairs.

Leucas poggeana is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.
