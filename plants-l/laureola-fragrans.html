Laureola fragrans

Laureola fragrans, commonly known as the sweet laurel, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine for their aromatic properties. Laureola fragrans thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
