Liriope muscari var. variegata

Liriope muscari var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated woodland iris, is a perennial plant native to eastern Asia.

It features grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. The variegated foliage adds visual interest with its contrasting color patterns. It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

This plant is often used as a ground cover due to its dense growth habit and ornamental appeal.
