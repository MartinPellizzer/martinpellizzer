Lachnostylis bilocularis

Lachnostylis bilocularis, commonly known as the two-chambered lichen, is a species of foliose lichen that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is characterized by its leaf-like thallus with a distinctive two-chambered structure in its upper cortex. The plant forms a symbiotic relationship with algae, which provide photosynthetic energy, while the fungal component offers structural support and protection. It is typically found on tree trunks and rocks in temperate regions.

Lachnostylis bilocularis plays an important role in nutrient cycling and serves as a habitat for various microorganisms.
