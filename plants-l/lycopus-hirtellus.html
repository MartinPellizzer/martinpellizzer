Lycopus hirtellus

Lycopus hirtellus, commonly known as blueweed, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant features square stems and opposite leaves that are often hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small, blue to purple, and clustered in whorls, contributing to its ornamental value.

Lycopus hirtellus is typically found in moist habitats such as wet meadows and streambanks.
