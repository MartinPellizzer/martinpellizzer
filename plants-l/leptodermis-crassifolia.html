Leptodermis crassifolia

Leptodermis crassifolia, commonly known as the thick-leaved rock cress, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to high-altitude conditions.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
