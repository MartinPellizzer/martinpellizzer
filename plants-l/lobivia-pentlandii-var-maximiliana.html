Lobivia pentlandii var. maximiliana

Lobivia pentlandii var. maximiliana, commonly known as the Maximilian's lobivia, is a succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It features a compact, cylindrical stem with a distinctive ribbed structure that helps it store water. The plant produces clusters of small, pale green to blue-gray leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. Its flowering occurs in late spring to early summer, producing delicate, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange.

This species is well adapted to rocky and sandy soils, thriving in full sun and requiring minimal water.
