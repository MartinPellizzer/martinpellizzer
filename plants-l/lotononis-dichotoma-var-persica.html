Lotononis dichotoma var. persica

Lotononis dichotoma var. persica, commonly known as the Persian lotononis, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its small, delicate white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a creeping growth habit with slender, branched stems that often root at the nodes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, arranged alternately along the stems.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable ornamental plant for rock gardens or dry landscapes.
