Licuala pumila

Licuala pumila, commonly known as the dwarf palm or small-leaf palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. This plant is characterized by its compact growth habit, with short, clustered stems and narrow, elongated leaves. The leaves are typically light green and have a smooth, waxy texture.

Licuala pumila is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
