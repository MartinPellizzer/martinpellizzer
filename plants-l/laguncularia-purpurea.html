Laguncularia purpurea

Laguncularia purpurea, commonly known as the purple mangrove, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the Americas.

It is a halophyte, meaning it can thrive in saline environments, often found in mangrove swamps and tidal areas. The plant has specialized structures such as pneumatophores, which are root-like projections that facilitate gas exchange in waterlogged soil. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically white or purple, and it produces small, fleshy berries that are dispersed by water.

Laguncularia purpurea plays a crucial role in coastal ecosystems by stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial species.
