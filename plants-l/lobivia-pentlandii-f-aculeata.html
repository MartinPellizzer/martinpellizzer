Lobivia pentlandii f. aculeata

Lobivia pentlandii f. aculeata, commonly known as the Aculeate Lobivia, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its unique, spiny stems and compact growth habit. The plant features short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in white or pale green hairs. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, appearing at the base of the stems during the growing season.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained, sandy soils with minimal water.
