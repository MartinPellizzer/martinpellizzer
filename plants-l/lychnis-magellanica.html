Lychnis magellanica

Lychnis magellanica, commonly known as the Magellan's campion, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of pink or red flowers. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Lychnis magellanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
