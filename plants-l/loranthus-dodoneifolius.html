Loranthus dodoneifolius

Loranthus dodoneifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaved mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees.

It belongs to the family Loranthaceae and is characterized by its evergreen, leathery leaves that are often broader than long. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. Its fruit is a fleshy, red berry that contains one or two seeds, which are dispersed by birds.
