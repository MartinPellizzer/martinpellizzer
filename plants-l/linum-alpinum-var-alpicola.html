Linum alpinum var. alpicola

Linum alpinum var. alpicola, commonly known as the alpine flax, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a member of the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its fibrous stems and leaves are adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil in high-altitude ecosystems.

This variety is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
