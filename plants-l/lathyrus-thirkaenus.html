Lathyrus thirkaenus

Lathyrus thirkaenus, commonly known as the Thirkaenus vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves, which are typically heart-shaped. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows and along woodland edges.

Lathyrus thirkaenus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in nitrogen fixation in the soil.
