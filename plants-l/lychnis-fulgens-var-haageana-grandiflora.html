Lychnis fulgens var. haageana-grandiflora

Lychnis fulgens var. haageana-grandiflora, commonly known as the scarlet poppy, is a strikingly colorful plant native to southern Europe.

It features showy, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stem is hairy and upright. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable in native habitats.
