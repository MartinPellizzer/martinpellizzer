Lamyropsis cynaroides

Lamyropsis cynaroides, commonly known as the African crownbeard, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, yellow-orange flower heads that resemble a crown. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, tubular flowers arranged in dense, composite inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Lamyropsis cynaroides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
