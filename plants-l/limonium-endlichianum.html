Limonium endlichianum

Limonium endlichianum, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its striking, spiky flower heads that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in saline or sandy environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, gray-green leaves that are adapted to withstand coastal conditions.

Limonium endlichianum is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
