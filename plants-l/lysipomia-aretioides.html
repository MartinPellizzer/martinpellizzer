Lysipomia aretioides

Lysipomia aretioides, commonly known as the Andean bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Andes.

It features a rosette of narrow, silver-gray leaves that help it withstand cold and arid conditions. The plant produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue, violet, or white, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
