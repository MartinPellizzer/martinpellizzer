Lysiloma standleyanum

Lysiloma standleyanum, commonly known as the white trumpet tree, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking white, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape. It produces small, woody fruits that contain several seeds.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils in warm climates.
