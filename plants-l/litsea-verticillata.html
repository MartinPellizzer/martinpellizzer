Litsea verticillata

Litsea verticillata, commonly known as the clustered leafed litsea, is a flowering plant species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that often grow in whorls of three to five at the nodes. The plant produces small, fragrant yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by spherical, woody fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
