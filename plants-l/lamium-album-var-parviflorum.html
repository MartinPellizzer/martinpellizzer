Lamium album var. parviflorum

Lamium album var. parviflorum, commonly known as white dead-nettle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving them a soft texture. The plant produces small, white, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and early summer. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas or along the edges of forests.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
