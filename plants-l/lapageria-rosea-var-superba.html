Lapageria rosea var. superba

Lapageria rosea var. superba, commonly known as the Chilean bellflower, is a striking evergreen shrub native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from deep red to orange, depending on the variety. The plant features glossy, dark green leaves that are ovate and slightly hairy. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Lapageria rosea var. superba is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
