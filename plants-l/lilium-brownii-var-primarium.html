Lilium brownii var. primarium

Lilium brownii var. primarium, commonly known as the Primrose Lily, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, nodding flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its bulbs are adapted to store nutrients, allowing it to survive harsh winters.

The leaves are grass-like, and the plant is often found in high-altitude habitats where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
