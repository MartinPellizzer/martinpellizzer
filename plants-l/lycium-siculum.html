Lycium siculum

Lycium siculum, commonly known as the Sicilian boxthorn, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible. The plant has thorny stems and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and rocky habitats.

Lycium siculum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
