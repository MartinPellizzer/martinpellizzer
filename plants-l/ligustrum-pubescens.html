Ligustrum pubescens

Ligustrum pubescens, commonly known as the Chinese privet, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually oval in shape and covered in fine hairs. Its berries are dark blue to black when mature and are edible for birds but toxic to humans.

Ligustrum pubescens is often used in landscaping due to its fast growth and adaptability to various soil types.
