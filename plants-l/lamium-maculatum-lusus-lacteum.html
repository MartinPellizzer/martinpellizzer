Lamium maculatum lusus lacteum

Lamium maculatum lusus lacteum, commonly known as the spotted deadnettle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive white or silver markings, and its flowers are typically pink or white, arranged in whorls. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks. It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

The species is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
