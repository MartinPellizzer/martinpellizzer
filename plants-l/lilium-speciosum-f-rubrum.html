Lilium speciosum f. rubrum

Lilium speciosum f. rubrum, commonly known as the red-flowered tiger lily, is a cultivar of the species Lilium speciosum, which is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

This plant is characterized by its striking, large, and showy flowers that typically bloom in late summer to early autumn. The 'rubrum' form is distinguished by its deep red petals with a contrasting yellow throat, creating a dramatic visual effect. It is a herbaceous perennial that grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with strap-like leaves and a strong, upright stem.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
