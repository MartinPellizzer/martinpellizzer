Lycopodium debile

Lycopodium debile, commonly known as the slender clubmoss, is a small evergreen fern-like plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae and is characterized by its compact, creeping growth habit. The plant produces small, spirally arranged leaves and has distinctive strobila (cone-like structures) that bear spores. Lycopodium debile thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest floors or rocky areas.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
