Lepanthes janus

Lepanthes janus, commonly known as the Janus orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant has a delicate, sympodial growth habit with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale green, with intricate patterns that mimic the appearance of a face.

Lepanthes janus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
