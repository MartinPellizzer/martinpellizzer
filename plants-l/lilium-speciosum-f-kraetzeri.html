Lilium speciosum f. kraetzeri

Lilium speciosum f. kraetzeri, commonly known as the Kraetzer's showy lily, is a botanical variant of the showy lily, characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers.

This form exhibits larger, more vibrant blooms compared to the typical species, with petals that often display a mix of white and pink hues. It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, where it thrives in well-drained, loamy soils. The plant produces sword-shaped leaves and has a robust root system that helps it survive in its natural habitat.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections worldwide.
