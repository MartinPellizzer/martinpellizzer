Lepidium densiflorum var. pubicarpum

Lepidium densiflorum var. pubicarpum, commonly known as the pubescent peppergrass, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and sagebrush habitats. Its leaves are pinnately divided and covered with fine, pubescent hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The species is noted for its ability to thrive in arid environments, making it an important component of its native ecosystems.
