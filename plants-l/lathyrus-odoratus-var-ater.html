Lathyrus odoratus var. ater

Lathyrus odoratus var. ater, commonly known as the dark purple sweet pea, is a cultivar of the sweet pea plant characterized by its deep, velvety purple flowers.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, known for its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant typically grows as an annual vine, producing tendrils that help it climb supports. Its flowers are fragrant and bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The foliage consists of pinnate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
