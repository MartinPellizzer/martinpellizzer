Loreya riparia

Loreya riparia, commonly known as the riverbank bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands and riverbanks of Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale blue flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and lanceolate, arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
