Lessingianthus pusillus

Lessingianthus pusillus, commonly known as the dwarf sunflower, is a small flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of only a few inches, with small, yellow-orange flower heads surrounded by narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its compact size and vibrant blooms, it is often used in ornamental horticulture.
