Leontodon balansae

Leontodon balansae, commonly known as the Balansae hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are basal, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Leontodon balansae is often found in disturbed areas and is adapted to thrive in nutrient-poor environments.
