Linum jailicola

Linum jailicola, commonly known as the desert flax, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a member of the Linaceae family, which includes other flax species. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its flowers are small and usually blue or purple, and it produces small, oval seeds.

Linum jailicola is valued for its ecological role in supporting local pollinators and its resilience in harsh environments.
