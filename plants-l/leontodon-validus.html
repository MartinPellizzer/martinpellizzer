Leontodon validus

Leontodon validus, commonly known as the meadow bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the daisy family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges. Its leaves are basal, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowering stems bear clusters of small, tubular flowers.

Leontodon validus is noted for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for various herbivores.
