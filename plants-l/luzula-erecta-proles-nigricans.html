Luzula erecta proles nigricans

Luzula erecta proles nigricans, commonly known as the black-stemmed wood rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its upright, slender stems that are typically dark green to blackish in color, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing greenish to brownish flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks, often growing in acidic soils.

Luzula erecta proles nigricans is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
