Lasianthaea ceanothifolia var. gradata

Lasianthaea ceanothifolia var. gradata, commonly known as the mountain holly or mountain hollyleaf, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a glossy texture and a fragrant aroma. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and hardy addition to xeriscaped gardens.
