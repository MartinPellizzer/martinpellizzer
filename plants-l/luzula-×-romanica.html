Luzula × romanica

Luzula × romanica, commonly known as the silver false beardgrass, is a hybrid plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and distinctive silver-gray foliage due to the presence of fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, greenish-brown flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Luzula × romanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden settings for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
