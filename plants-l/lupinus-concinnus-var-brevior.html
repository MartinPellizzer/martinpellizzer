Lupinus concinnus var. brevior

Lupinus concinnus var. brevior, commonly known as the short-stemmed lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
