Lonicera rupicola var. thibetica

Lonicera rupicola var. thibetica, commonly known as the Tibetan honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of China.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its slender, woody stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring. The leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to rocky or alpine soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in traditional medicine in some regions.
