Lycoctonum gigas

Lycoctonum gigas, commonly known as the giant lycoperdon, is a striking member of the Solanaceae family, characterized by its large, showy flowers.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant produces strikingly large, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. Its leaves are broad and ovate, often with a velvety texture, while its stems are sturdy and branching.

The fruit, which resembles a large, fleshy tomato, contains numerous seeds and is an important food source for various wildlife.
