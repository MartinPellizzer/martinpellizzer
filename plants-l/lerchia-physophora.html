Lerchia physophora

Lerchia physophora, commonly known as the golden star lichen, is a foliose lichen belonging to the family Physciaceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange thallus, which often has a star-like appearance due to the arrangement of its lobes. The lichen thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found on tree trunks and soil in temperate regions. It is composed of a symbiotic association between a photosynthetic cyanobacterium and a fungal partner.

This species is of interest to ecologists due to its role in nutrient cycling and its sensitivity to environmental changes.
