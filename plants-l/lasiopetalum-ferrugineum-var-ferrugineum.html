Lasiopetalum ferrugineum var. ferrugineum

Lasiopetalum ferrugineum var. ferrugineum, commonly known as the rusty matted bellflower, is a low-growing shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features small, tubular flowers that are typically pink to reddish in color, often appearing in clusters. The plant has silver-gray foliage with a velvety texture, and its stems are covered in fine, rusty-colored hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract native pollinators.
