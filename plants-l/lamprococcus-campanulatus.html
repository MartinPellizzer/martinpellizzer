Lamprococcus campanulatus

Lamprococcus campanulatus, commonly known as the bluebell orchid, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters.

Lamprococcus campanulatus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and unique appearance.
