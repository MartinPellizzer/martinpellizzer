Linum alpinum var. provinciale

Linum alpinum var. provinciale, commonly known as the Alpine flax, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces capsules that contain numerous small, oval seeds. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Linum alpinum var. provinciale is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in fiber production.
