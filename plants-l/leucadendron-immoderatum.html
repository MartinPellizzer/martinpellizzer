Leucadendron immoderatum

Leucadendron immoderatum, commonly known as the yellow starling leucadendron, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is part of the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow to orange-pink flowers that resemble the plumage of a starling bird. The plant produces small, woody cones that contain the seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections worldwide.
