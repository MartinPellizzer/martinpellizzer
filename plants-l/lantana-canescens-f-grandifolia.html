Lantana canescens f. grandifolia

Lantana canescens f. grandifolia, commonly known as the largeflower lantana, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring dense, aromatic foliage with a grayish-green hue. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom throughout the growing season. Its leaves are covered in fine, white hairs, giving the plant a distinctive silvery appearance.

Lantana canescens f. grandifolia is well adapted to full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
