Lavandula santolinifolia

Lavandula santolinifolia, commonly known as the santolinifolia lavender, is a species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub characterized by its aromatic, slender leaves and spikes of purple flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or dry garden environments. Its flowers are used in perfumery and aromatherapy, similar to other lavender species.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in traditional herbal medicine.
