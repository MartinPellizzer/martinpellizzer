Liparis koreana

Liparis koreana, commonly known as the Korean orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Korean Peninsula.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and stream banks. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with three sepals and three petals, often with a distinctive lip.

Liparis koreana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
