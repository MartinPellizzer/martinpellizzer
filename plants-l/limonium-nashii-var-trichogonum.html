Limonium nashii var. trichogonum

Limonium nashii var. trichogonum, commonly known as the coastal flax or sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and the eastern Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found growing in sandy or rocky soils near the shore. It is well adapted to salty and windy environments, making it a hardy species suitable for coastal gardens.

Limonium nashii var. trichogonum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging maritime conditions.
