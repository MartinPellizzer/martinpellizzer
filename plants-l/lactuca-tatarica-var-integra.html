Lactuca tatarica var. integra

Lactuca tatarica var. integra, commonly known as Siberian endive, is a perennial plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette of spoon-shaped, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is often cultivated for its edible leaves, which have a slightly bitter taste.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
