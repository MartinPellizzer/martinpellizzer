Lobivia thionantha var. catamarcensis

Lobivia thionantha var. catamarcensis, commonly known as the Catamarca lobivia, is a succulent native to Argentina.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rounded shape with thick, ribbed stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the growing season, typically in spring. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright sunlight to flourish.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
