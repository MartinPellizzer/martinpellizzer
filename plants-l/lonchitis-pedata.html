Lonchitis pedata

Lonchitis pedata, commonly known as the creeping bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, creeping stems that root at the nodes, allowing it to spread across the ground. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, with five petals. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Lonchitis pedata thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
