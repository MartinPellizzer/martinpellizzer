Ludwigia peduncularis

Ludwigia peduncularis, commonly known as the red Ludwigia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Onagraceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its striking red-orange flowers and elongated, narrow leaves. Its flowers are often visited by insects, contributing to its role in pollination.

Ludwigia peduncularis is also popular in aquatic gardening due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
