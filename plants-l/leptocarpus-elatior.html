Leptocarpus elatior

Leptocarpus elatior, commonly known as the broad-leaved sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dune environments. It is adapted to salt spray and can tolerate moderate drought conditions.

Leptocarpus elatior is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in coastal gardens.
