Lasianthus pedicellatus

Lasianthus pedicellatus, commonly known as the slender flame flower, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically red or orange, with a distinctive two-lipped structure. These flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom during the rainy season, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes due to its vibrant floral display.
