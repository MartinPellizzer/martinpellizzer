Lonchocarpus roseus

Lonchocarpus roseus, commonly known as the pink pod senna, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking pink to red flowers and compound leaves with small, oval leaflets. The plant produces distinctive pod-like fruits that are typically hairy and contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and research settings.
