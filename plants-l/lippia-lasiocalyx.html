Lippia lasiocalyx

Lippia lasiocalyx, commonly known as the roughleaf lippia, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pale yellow. The plant has hairy, rough-textured foliage, which contributes to its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or disturbed areas.

Lippia lasiocalyx is valued for its potential medicinal properties and use in traditional herbal remedies.
