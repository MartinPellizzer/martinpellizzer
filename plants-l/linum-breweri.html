Linum breweri

Linum breweri, commonly known as the Brewer's flax, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and delicate blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as rocky soils and open grasslands. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds.

Linum breweri is often found in areas with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.
