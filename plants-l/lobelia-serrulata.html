Lobelia serrulata

Lobelia serrulata, commonly known as the zigzag lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging stems and small, tubular blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and often have serrated edges, contributing to its common name.

Lobelia serrulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
