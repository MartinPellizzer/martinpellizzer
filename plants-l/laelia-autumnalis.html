Laelia autumnalis

Laelia autumnalis, commonly known as the autumn orchid, is a species native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant that typically grows in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple during the autumn season. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to anchor in rocky or volcanic soil.

Laelia autumnalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
