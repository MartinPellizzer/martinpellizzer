Lilium philadelphicum f. wausharaicum

Lilium philadelphicum f. wausharaicum, commonly known as the Waushara lily, is a subspecies of the showy orchid lily native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its striking white to pale pink flowers with a distinctive red or orange throat, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, and it produces a tall, slender stem that supports its showy inflorescence.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
