Lomelosia pulsatilloides

Lomelosia pulsatilloides, commonly known as the Mexican pulsatilla, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or open habitats, often in mountainous regions. The plant features a short, stout stem with large, palmately divided leaves. Its flowers are yellow with prominent, showy stamens, and it produces follicles that contain the seeds.

It is native to Central America and parts of the southwestern United States.
