Loboptera subsessilis

Loboptera subsessilis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf loboptera, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often slightly hairy, with a distinctively narrow shape. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and native plant gardens.
