Linum capitatum

Linum capitatum, commonly known as the common flax, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of blue or white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Linum capitatum is often grown for ornamental purposes and has historical significance in textile production.
