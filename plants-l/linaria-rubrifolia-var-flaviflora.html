Linaria rubrifolia var. flaviflora

Linaria rubrifolia var. flaviflora, commonly known as the goldenleaf toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the genus Linaria, which includes various species of flowering plants known for their showy, often yellow or orange flowers. This particular variety is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and finely divided, fern-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
