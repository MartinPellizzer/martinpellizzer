Litsea longipetiolata

Litsea longipetiolata, commonly known as the long-petioled litsea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its simple, opposite, and often elliptical leaves with long petioles. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible berries.

Litsea longipetiolata is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its aromatic properties.
