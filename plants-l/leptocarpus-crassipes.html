Leptocarpus crassipes

Leptocarpus crassipes, commonly known as the broad-leaved sea bean, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

The plant thrives in saline and brackish environments, often found in mangrove forests and tidal mudflats. Its seeds are encased in a hard, pod-like fruit, which plays a role in its dispersal in coastal ecosystems.

Leptocarpus crassipes contributes to stabilizing coastal soils and provides habitat for various marine and terrestrial organisms.
