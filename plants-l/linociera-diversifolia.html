Linociera diversifolia

Linociera diversifolia, commonly known as the variable leaf linociera, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which exhibit a wide variation in shape and size across different parts of the plant. The species produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
