Libanotus asiaticus

Libanotus asiaticus, commonly known as the Asian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to regions of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to blue or purple. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slight pubescence, while the flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Libanotus asiaticus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, showy blooms.
