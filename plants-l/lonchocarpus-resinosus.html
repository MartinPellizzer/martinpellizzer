Lonchocarpus resinosus

Lonchocarpus resinosus, commonly known as the resinous legume, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant is notable for its distinctive, often resinous, and sometimes toxic seeds. Its leaves are typically pinnately compound, and it produces small, yellowish flowers.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
