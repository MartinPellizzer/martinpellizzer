Linum strictum subsp. spicatum

Linum strictum subsp.

spicatum, commonly known as the spike flax, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in spikes.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its fibrous stems and seeds have been used historically for various purposes, including fiber production and as a source of oil.
