Lonchocarpus neuroscapha

Lonchocarpus neuroscapha, commonly known as the scarlet bignay, is a tropical leguminous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, woody pods containing edible seeds that are rich in nutrients. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and nutritional content, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and agricultural purposes.
