Luzula campestris var. picta

Luzula campestris var. picta, commonly known as the painted woodrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, commonly known as the grass family, and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, white, and sometimes pink, giving it its common name. It typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The flowering stems produce small, spike-like clusters of pale blue or white flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer.
