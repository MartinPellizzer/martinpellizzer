Lamyra angustifolia

Lamyra angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf lamyra, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. Lamyra angustifolia is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in ecological studies of Mediterranean flora.
