Lycopersicon racemiforme

Lycopersicon racemiforme, commonly known as the wild tomato, is a species native to South America.

It is a herbaceous plant that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, giving it its scientific name. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

The fruit is a small, red, berry-like structure that contains numerous seeds.
