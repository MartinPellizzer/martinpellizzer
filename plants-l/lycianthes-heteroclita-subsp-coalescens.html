Lycianthes heteroclita subsp. coalescens

Lycianthes heteroclita subsp.

coalescens, commonly known as the purple eggplant, is a species native to Central America and northern South America. It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its hairy stems and alternate leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple, followed by round, fleshy fruits that resemble small eggs. Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes and peppers.

The subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and potential use in traditional medicine.
