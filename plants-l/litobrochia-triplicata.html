Litobrochia triplicata

Litobrochia triplicata, commonly known as the triple-leaf stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the rocky habitats of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, fleshy leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, typically with three lobes, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the warmer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
