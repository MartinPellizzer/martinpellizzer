Leontopodium maireanum

Leontopodium maireanum, commonly known as the Maire's edelweiss, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of China.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and showy, white to pale yellow flower heads. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions and well-drained soil. Its growth is slow, and it is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, it is protected in its native range.
