Lissochilus lindleyanus

Lissochilus lindleyanus, commonly known as the Lindley's orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, spurred flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or forested areas. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, while its flowers are usually pale green or white with a distinct lip.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
