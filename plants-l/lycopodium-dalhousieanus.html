Lycopodium dalhousieanus

Lycopodium dalhousieanus, commonly known as the Dalhousie clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its whorled arrangement of needle-like leaves and a short, upright stem. It produces sporophylls that bear spores, which are released into the air for dispersal.

Lycopodium dalhousieanus plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and small invertebrates.
