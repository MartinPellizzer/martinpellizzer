Linum cruciatum

Linum cruciatum, commonly known as common flax, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, blue or white flowers that are typically arranged in clusters, giving it a cross-shaped appearance. Its seeds are used for oil production, and the fibers from its stems have been historically used for textiles.

Linum cruciatum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a common ornamental and agricultural plant.
