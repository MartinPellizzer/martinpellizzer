Lepisorus preussii

Lepisorus preussii, commonly known as the Preuss's spleenwort, is a small, terrestrial fern belonging to the family Pterobryaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, creeping rhizomes and compact, evergreen foliage. The leaves are typically arranged in a rosette-like pattern, with lanceolate to ovate shapes and a smooth, glossy surface. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in rocky crevices or on the bark of trees.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in shaded garden settings.
