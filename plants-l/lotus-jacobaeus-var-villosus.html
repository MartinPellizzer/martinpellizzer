Lotus jacobaeus var. villosus

Lotus jacobaeus var. villosus, commonly known as the hairy lotus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features basal leaves that are hairy and lobed, with a distinctive violet to pinkish hue. The plant produces showy, butterfly-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and rocky areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
