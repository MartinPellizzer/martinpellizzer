Lycopus longifolius

Lycopus longifolius, commonly known as blueweed or long-leaved water-hemlock, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers arranged in whorls along the upper stems. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a fragrant, somewhat minty aroma.

Lycopus longifolius thrives in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and wet meadows.
