Liatris odoratissima

Liatris odoratissima, commonly known as the sweet peppergrass, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescence composed of numerous small, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slight woolly texture.

Liatris odoratissima is known for its pleasant, spicy scent, which is most noticeable when the flowers are in bloom.
