Lessingianthus longicuspis

Lessingianthus longicuspis, commonly known as the long-beaked sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. It is typically found in dry, open habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant in horticulture.
