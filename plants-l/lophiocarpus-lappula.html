Lophiocarpus lappula

Lophiocarpus lappula, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple, followed by narrow, pod-like fruits. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert environments.

Lophiocarpus lappula plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
