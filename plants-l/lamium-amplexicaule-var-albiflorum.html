Lamium amplexicaule var. albiflorum

Lamium amplexicaule var. albiflorum, commonly known as white-lipped deadnettle, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that clasp the stem, giving it its species name *amplexicaule*. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of white to pale pink, typically blooming from spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and along stream banks.

Its leaves and flowers are often used in herbal teas and have mild medicinal properties.
