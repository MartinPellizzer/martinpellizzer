Lippia angustifolia

Lippia angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to salt spray, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.

Lippia angustifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
