Lithospermum fimbriatum

Lithospermum fimbriatum, commonly known as the fringed rock pepper, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its hairy, square stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its small, tubular flowers are typically pink to purple in color and bloom in late summer.
