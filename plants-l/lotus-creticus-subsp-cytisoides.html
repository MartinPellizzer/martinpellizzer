Lotus creticus subsp. cytisoides

Lotus creticus subsp.

cytisoides, commonly known as the narrow-leafed lotus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, sunny environments such as rocky soils and open fields.

The plant features slender, upright stems with small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow or orange, with a distinctive spurred shape, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
