Lippia bothrioura

Lippia bothrioura, commonly known as Mexican blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and clusters of small blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

Its essential oils are also valued in aromatherapy and as a natural insect repellent.
