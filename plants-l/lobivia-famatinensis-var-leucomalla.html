Lobivia famatinensis var. leucomalla

Lobivia famatinensis var. leucomalla, commonly known as the White-veined Lobivia, is a succulent native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rounded growth habit. The plant features pale green to bluish-gray stems with prominent white veins, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are typically small and ephemeral, often falling off during periods of drought.

It produces yellow flowers in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its otherwise muted foliage.
