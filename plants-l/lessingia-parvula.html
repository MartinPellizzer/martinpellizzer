Lessingia parvula

Lessingia parvula, commonly known as the smallflower sandmat, is a low-growing annual plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its prostrate habit, with small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are usually covered in fine, glandular hairs. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.
