Lagophylla ramosissima subsp. congesta

Lagophylla ramosissima subsp.

congesta, commonly known as the coastal bladderwrack, is a member of the Fucaceae family and is found in intertidal zones along the coasts of southern Africa. This seaweed is characterized by its dark brown to black coloration and branched, filamentous thallus that forms dense mats on rocky substrates.

It thrives in high-energy environments and is often found in areas with strong wave action. The plant's structure is adapted to withstand desiccation and mechanical stress, with a leathery texture and a high concentration of algal pigments.

It plays a vital role in coastal ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various marine organisms.
