Lactuca quercina

Lactuca quercina, commonly known as oakleaf lettuce, is a species of lettuce native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its lobed, oak-like leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly bitter taste. The plant grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers cool, temperate climates. It is often cultivated for its edible leaves, which can be used in salads or cooked dishes.

Lactuca quercina is also valued for its ornamental qualities due to its distinctive foliage.
