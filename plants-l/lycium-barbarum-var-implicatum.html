Lycium barbarum var. implicatum

Lycium barbarum var. implicatum, commonly known as the Chinese wolfberry or goji berry, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of China.

It produces small, red berries that are rich in antioxidants, vitamins, and minerals. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is closely related to other Lycium species and is often cultivated for its nutritional and medicinal properties.
