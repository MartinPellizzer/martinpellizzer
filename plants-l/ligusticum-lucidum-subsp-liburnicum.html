Ligusticum lucidum subsp. liburnicum

Ligusticum lucidum subsp.

liburnicum, commonly known as the Liburnian angelica, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula. It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges and a strong, spicy scent.

The small white to greenish flowers appear in clusters and are followed by small, dry fruits.
