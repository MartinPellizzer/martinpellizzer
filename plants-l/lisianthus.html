Lisianthus

Lisianthus, commonly known as the "false lavender," is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, clustered blooms. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 36 inches and produces flowers in shades of pink, red, white, and purple. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are sturdy and upright.

Lisianthus thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
