Lycium scoparium lusus confertiflorum

Lycium scoparium lusus confertiflorum, commonly known as the dense-flowered boxthorn, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It features spiny stems and small, fleshy leaves adapted to conserve water. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red, fleshy berries. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in harsh conditions.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited for stabilizing soils in dry environments.
