Lycopodioides fendleri

Lycopodioides fendleri, commonly known as the Fendler's clubmoss, is a species of lycophyte belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in arid environments. The plant features a short, horizontal stem with clustered, needle-like leaves that are often spirally arranged. Its sporophylls are closely clustered and bear spores in small, round clusters.

This plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions and is often found in regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.
