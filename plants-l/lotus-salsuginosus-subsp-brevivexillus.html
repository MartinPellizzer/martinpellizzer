Lotus salsuginosus subsp. brevivexillus

Lotus salsuginosus subsp.

brevivexillus, commonly known as the dwarf lotus, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its compact growth habit, small pink or purple flowers, and succulent leaves adapted to conserve water.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats. It is a perennial species that produces seed pods containing small, hard seeds.

Due to its drought tolerance, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental or for ecological restoration projects.
