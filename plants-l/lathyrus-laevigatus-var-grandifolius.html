Lathyrus laevigatus var. grandifolius

Lathyrus laevigatus var. grandifolius, commonly known as the large-leaf sweet pea, is a perennial climbing legume native to the eastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with large, heart-shaped leaflets and produces fragrant, spiraled flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white. The plant has a fibrous root system and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun to partial shade. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting neighboring plants.

Its botanical structure supports its role as both a garden ornamental and a potential forage crop.
