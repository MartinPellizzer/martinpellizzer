Lonchocarpus purpusii

Lonchocarpus purpusii, commonly known as the yellow snakeroot, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow flowers. The plant produces pods that contain seeds with a hard, woody coating. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
