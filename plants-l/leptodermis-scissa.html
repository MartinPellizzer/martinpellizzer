Leptodermis scissa

Leptodermis scissa, commonly known as the brittlestem daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a fine, white pubescence, while the flowers are small and usually yellow or white.

The species is noted for its resilience to drought and its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
