Linaria capilliflora

Linaria capilliflora, commonly known as the slender toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, hair-like leaves and vibrant yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family, which includes other ornamental and medicinal plants.

Linaria capilliflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
