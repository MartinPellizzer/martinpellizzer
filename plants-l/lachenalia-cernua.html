Lachenalia cernua

Lachenalia cernua, commonly known as the hanging lachenalia, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces showy, often orange or yellow flowers that hang downward, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Lachenalia cernua is popular among succulent enthusiasts for its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
