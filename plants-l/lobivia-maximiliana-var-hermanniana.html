Lobivia maximiliana var. hermanniana

Lobivia maximiliana var. hermanniana, commonly known as the Hermann's lobivia, is a compact, slow-growing succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It features fleshy, ribbed stems that often remain close to the ground, with clusters of small, paddle-shaped leaves that are typically blue-gray in color. The plant produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the spring and summer months. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, requiring minimal water to prevent root rot.

Due to its rarity and specific care requirements, it is often cultivated by experienced growers.
