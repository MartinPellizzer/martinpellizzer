Lycopus pauciflorus

Lycopus pauciflorus, commonly known as narrow-leaved blueweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its slender stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its aromatic leaves and flowers are often used in traditional herbal medicine.
