Lygodium elegans

Lygodium elegans, commonly known as the elegant water fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Lygodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Americas. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its reproductive structures, including spore-producing sori, are located on the underside of its leaves.

Lygodium elegans is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and can thrive in both aquatic and semi-aquatic conditions.
