Lepia linifolia

Lepia linifolia, commonly known as the slender leaf lichen, is a foliose lichen that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It has a thin, leaf-like structure with a dark green to black upper surface and a white to pale yellow lower surface. The plant forms a symbiotic relationship with algae, which provides it with nutrients through photosynthesis. It is often found on tree bark, rocks, and soil in temperate regions.

Lepia linifolia plays an important role in nutrient cycling and serves as a habitat for various microorganisms.
