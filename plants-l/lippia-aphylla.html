Lippia aphylla

Lippia aphylla, commonly known as Mexican mint or wild mint, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils. Lippia aphylla is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its essential oils contain compounds like menthone and piperitone, which contribute to its distinctive minty scent.
