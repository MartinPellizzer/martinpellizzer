Lobivia haagei var. crassa

Lobivia haagei var. crassa, commonly known as the thick-leaved lobivia, is a succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its robust, fleshy stems and thick, waxy leaves that help it store water. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers during the warmer months. It is a low-maintenance plant that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and succulent collections.
