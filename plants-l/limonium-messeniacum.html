Limonium messeniacum

Limonium messeniacum, commonly known as the Greek sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, showcasing its adaptability to saline environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powder.

Limonium messeniacum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging coastal conditions.
