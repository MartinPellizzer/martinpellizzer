Lychnis brachypetala

Lychnis brachypetala, commonly known as the short-petaled catchfly, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are typically pink to red, with five petals that are short and rounded. The plant produces small, hairy capsules that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Lychnis brachypetala is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
