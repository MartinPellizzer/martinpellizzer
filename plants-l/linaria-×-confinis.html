Linaria × confinis

Linaria × confinis, commonly known as the hybrid wallflower, is a perennial flowering plant that belongs to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is a cross between Linaria vulgaris and Linaria maroccana, resulting in a plant with distinctive, colorful, and often intricate flowers. The species is native to the Mediterranean region but has been widely cultivated in gardens across temperate climates. It typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, featuring slender stems and lance-shaped leaves.

The flowers, which range in color from yellow to orange and red, bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
