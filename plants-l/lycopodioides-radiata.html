Lycopodioides radiata

Lycopodioides radiata, commonly known as the radiate lycopod, is a species of clubmoss belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping stems that produce small, scale-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or rocky areas. Its reproductive structures include spore-producing cones, which are clustered at the base of the stems.

Lycopodioides radiata plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
