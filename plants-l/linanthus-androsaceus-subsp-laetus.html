Linanthus androsaceus subsp. laetus

Linanthus androsaceus subsp.

laetus, commonly known as the Sierra primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a low-growing perennial with bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring.

The plant has a mat-forming habit, with hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to the harsh conditions of its native environment.
