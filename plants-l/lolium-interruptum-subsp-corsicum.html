Lolium interruptum subsp. corsicum

Lolium interruptum subsp.

corsicum, commonly known as Corsican ryegrass, is a perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, hairless stems.

The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration. It produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences containing numerous tiny florets.

This species is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open, sunny areas.
