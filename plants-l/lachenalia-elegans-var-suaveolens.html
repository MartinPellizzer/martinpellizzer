Lachenalia elegans var. suaveolens

Lachenalia elegans var. suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented lachenalia, is a South African bulbous plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and clusters of bell-shaped, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a subtle, sweet fragrance that attracts pollinators. The plant produces a basal cluster of bulbs, which are essential for its propagation and survival.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
