Lessertia affinis

Lessertia affinis, commonly known as the African orchid or the mountain orchid, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa, particularly South Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elegant, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant has strap-like leaves and produces clusters of small, colorful blossoms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden settings.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
