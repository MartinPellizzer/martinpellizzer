Lobelia reverchonii

Lobelia reverchonii, commonly known as the Reverchon's lobelia, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Lobelia reverchonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
