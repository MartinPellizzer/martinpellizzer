Lychnis dioica var. vespertina

Lychnis dioica var. vespertina, commonly known as evening snowflake, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the carnation family (Caryophyllaceae) and is characterized by its white, star-shaped flowers that open in the late afternoon. The plant typically grows in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm. Its foliage consists of oval, hairy leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette.

The species is known for its short blooming period, usually from June to August, and is often found in moist or shaded environments.
