Liparis alata

Liparis alata, commonly known as the winged orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, wing-like leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces delicate, greenish-yellow flowers with three sepals and three petals, often arranged in a spiral. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Liparis alata is a perennial herb that spreads through rhizomes, making it a hardy and adaptable species.
