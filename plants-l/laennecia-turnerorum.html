Laennecia turnerorum

Laennecia turnerorum, commonly known as the Turner's laennecia, is a species of liverworts belonging to the family Laeniaceae.

It is a small, leafy plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant has a thalloid structure with a central axis and lateral lobes, characteristic of many liverworts. Its reproductive structures, including male and female gametophores, are often found on the upper surface of the thallus.

Laennecia turnerorum plays a role in nutrient cycling in its ecosystem and is an indicator species for certain environmental conditions.
