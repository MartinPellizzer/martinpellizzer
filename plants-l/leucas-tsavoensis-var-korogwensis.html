Leucas tsavoensis var. korogwensis

Leucas tsavoensis var. korogwensis, commonly known as the Tsavo leucas, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Kenya.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It has narrow, linear leaves that help reduce water loss.

This variety is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique ecological adaptations and potential medicinal properties.
