Lobelia decurrens

Lobelia decurrens, commonly known as the slender lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Lobelia decurrens thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
