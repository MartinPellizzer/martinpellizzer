Loasa horrida

Loasa horrida, commonly known as the rough loasa, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows at high elevations, often in rocky or alpine environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink in color. Its leaves are characterized by their rough, hairy texture, which gives it its common name.

Loasa horrida is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
