Luffa racemosa

Luffa racemosa, commonly known as the sponge gourd, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, elongated fruits that mature into fibrous, spongy structures. The plant produces yellow flowers and thrives in warm, humid climates. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often with a hairy texture.

The fibrous interior of the mature fruit is used for sponges and natural cleaning tools.
