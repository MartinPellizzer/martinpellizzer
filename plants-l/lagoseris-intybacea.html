Lagoseris intybacea

Lagoseris intybacea, commonly known as the slender sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with small, fleshy leaves. The plant produces tiny, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability.

Its succulent leaves help store moisture, making it well-suited for xerophytic conditions.
