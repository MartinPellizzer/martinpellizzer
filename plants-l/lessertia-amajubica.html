Lessertia amajubica

Lessertia amajubica, commonly known as the Natal fan palm, is a distinctive plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, fan-shaped palm with a short trunk and broad, feather-like leaves that give it a unique appearance. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in coastal forests. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine and for thatching, highlighting its cultural significance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in its native habitat.
