Limonium speciosum

Limonium speciosum, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads, which are surrounded by spiny, leaf-like bracts. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, sandy soils, and saline environments, showing a high tolerance for salt and wind. Its flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Limonium speciosum is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and resilience.
