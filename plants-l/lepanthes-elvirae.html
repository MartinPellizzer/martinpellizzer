Lepanthes elvirae

Lepanthes elvirae, commonly known as the Elvirae Orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually small to medium in size, with intricate patterns and colors ranging from white to pink and purple.

Lepanthes elvirae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
