Limonium procerum var. paramedium

Limonium procerum var. paramedium, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Europe and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and striking, blue to purple flower heads that resemble small umbrellas. The plant thrives in saline and well-drained soils, often found in dunes and coastal grasslands. Its flowers are typically visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological importance.

Limonium procerum var. paramedium is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand coastal conditions.
