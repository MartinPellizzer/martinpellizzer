Lobelia bipinnatifida

Lobelia bipinnatifida, commonly known as the twin-lobed lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.

Lobelia bipinnatifida is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty and ecological benefits.
