Linaria ventricosa

Linaria ventricosa, commonly known as the balloon flower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its inflated, seed-filled pods that resemble small balloons, which burst to disperse seeds. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny environments. It produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the summer months.

Linaria ventricosa is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and easy care requirements.
