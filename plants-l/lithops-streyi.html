Lithops streyi

Lithops streyi, commonly known as the "Twin Rock Plant," is a member of the Aizoaceae family and is native to South Africa.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its two fleshy, leaf-like structures that resemble stones, helping it blend into its arid environment. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. Lithops streyi thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for rock gardens or indoor cultivation.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
