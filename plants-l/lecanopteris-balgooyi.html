Lecanopteris balgooyi

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows on tree trunks or branches, drawing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of its leaves.
