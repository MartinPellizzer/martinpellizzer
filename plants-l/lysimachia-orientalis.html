Lysimachia orientalis

Lysimachia orientalis, commonly known as the oriental yellow loosestrife, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and the Korean Peninsula.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Lysimachia orientalis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in garden landscapes for its ability to thrive in wet conditions.
