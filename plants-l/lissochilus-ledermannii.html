Lissochilus ledermannii

Lissochilus ledermannii, commonly known as the Ledermann's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or forested areas. Its leaves are usually narrow and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

This orchid is noted for its delicate appearance and is often found in humid, cool climates.
