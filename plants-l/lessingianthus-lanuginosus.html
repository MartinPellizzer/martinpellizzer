Lessingianthus lanuginosus

Lessingianthus lanuginosus, commonly known as the woolly sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a notable species in its native ecosystem.
