Lespedeza penduliflora

Lespedeza penduliflora, commonly known as hanging lespedeza, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces fluffy, pale yellow to brown seed pods that mature in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or disturbed areas.

Lespedeza penduliflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone regions.
