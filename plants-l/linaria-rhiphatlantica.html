Linaria rhiphatlantica

Linaria rhiphatlantica, commonly known as the narrowleaf toadflax, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slightly hairy texture, while its flowers have two lips, typical of the genus Linaria.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
