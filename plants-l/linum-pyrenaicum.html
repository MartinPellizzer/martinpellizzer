Linum pyrenaicum

Linum pyrenaicum, commonly known as the Pyrenean flax, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a member of the Linaceae family and is characterized by its delicate blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils. It has slender, branched stems and linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Linum pyrenaicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
