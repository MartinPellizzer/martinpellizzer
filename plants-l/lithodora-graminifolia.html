Lithodora graminifolia

Lithodora graminifolia, commonly known as the grass-leaved germander, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant blue or violet flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a dense, compact growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Lithodora graminifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
