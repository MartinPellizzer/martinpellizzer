Lecythis wullschlaegeliana

Lecythis wullschlaegeliana, commonly known as the giant lecythis, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves and produces massive, woody fruits that can weigh several kilograms. The fruit has a thick, spiny husk and contains a sweet, edible pulp with a high water content. The tree is valued for its timber and is also used in traditional medicine.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitats by providing food for various animals.
