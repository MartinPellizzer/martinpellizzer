Lupinus horizontalis

Lupinus horizontalis, commonly known as the creeping lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it has a fibrous root system.

Lupinus horizontalis is well adapted to rocky and sandy soils, making it a hardy plant in arid and semi-arid environments.
