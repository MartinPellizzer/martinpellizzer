Lychniothyrsus albus

Lychniothyrsus albus, commonly known as the white lychnis, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its simple, erect stems and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Lychniothyrsus albus is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
