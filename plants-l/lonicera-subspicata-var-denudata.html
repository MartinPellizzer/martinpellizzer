Lonicera subspicata var. denudata

Lonicera subspicata var. denudata, commonly known as the naked-stem honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, often hairless stems that are typically reddish-brown in color. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, yellowish-white flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are edible for birds. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly serrated margin.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting wildlife.
