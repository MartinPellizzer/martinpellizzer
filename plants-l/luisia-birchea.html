Luisia birchea

Luisia birchea, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It features delicate, bell-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, which bloom in clusters along its stems. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement and are often covered in fine hairs. Its rapid growth habit makes it suitable for trellises or as a ground cover in warm climates.

Luisia birchea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its vibrant floral display.
