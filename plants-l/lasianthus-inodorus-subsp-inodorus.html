Lasianthus inodorus subsp. inodorus

Lasianthus inodorus subsp.

inodorus, commonly known as the fragrant orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia. It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, fragrant flowers.

The flowers are typically white or pale pink and are arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and requires well-drained soil.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
