Leptochloa chinensis

Leptochloa chinensis, commonly known as Chinese narrowleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest floors or wetlands. It has a fibrous root system and reproduces through both sexual and asexual means.

Its botanical structure makes it an important component of certain ecosystems and a potential resource for ecological restoration.
