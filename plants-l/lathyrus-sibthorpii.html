Lathyrus sibthorpii

Lathyrus sibthorpii, commonly known as the Tibetan vetch, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple, often arranged in clusters. Its leaves are compound, with slender leaflets that help reduce water loss in its arid habitat.

Lathyrus sibthorpii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
