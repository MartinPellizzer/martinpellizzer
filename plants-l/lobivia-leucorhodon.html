Lobivia leucorhodon

Lobivia leucorhodon, commonly known as the White-Faced Lobivia, is a succulent native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly hair. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. It is a slow-growing species that requires well-draining soil and minimal water to thrive.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts.
