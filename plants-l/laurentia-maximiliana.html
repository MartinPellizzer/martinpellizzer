Laurentia maximiliana

Laurentia maximiliana, commonly known as the narrow-leaved marshwort, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Due to its habitat preferences, it plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
