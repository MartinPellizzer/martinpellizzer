Lupinus seifrizianus

Lupinus seifrizianus, commonly known as the Seifrizian lupin, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and clusters of pea-like flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces cylindrical seed pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates with moderate rainfall.

Lupinus seifrizianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential as a forage crop.
