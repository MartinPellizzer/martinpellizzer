Lathyrus niger f. heterophyllus

Lathyrus niger f. heterophyllus, commonly known as the black vetch variant with variable foliage, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive black pods and trifoliate leaves, which exhibit variation in shape and size, giving rise to its specific epithet "heterophyllus.". The plant typically grows in temperate regions and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides. Its flowers are usually purple or violet, and it produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds.

This form is notable for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
