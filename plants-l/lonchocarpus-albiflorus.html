Lonchocarpus albiflorus

Lonchocarpus albiflorus, commonly known as the whiteflower legume, is a tropical plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces distinctive pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is native to regions in South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in moist, tropical environments.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
