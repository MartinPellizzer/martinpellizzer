Lonchocarpus proteranthus

Lonchocarpus proteranthus, commonly known as the yellow spurred bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces striking yellow flowers with distinctive spurred petals. The plant is notable for its unique seed pods, which are elongated and often contain multiple seeds. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
