Limodorum roseum

Limodorum roseum, commonly known as the pink lily of the Nile, is a striking flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces vibrant pink or white blooms that are often fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Limodorum roseum is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value and is relatively easy to grow in warm, humid climates.
