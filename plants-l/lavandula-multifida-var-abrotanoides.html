Lavandula multifida var. abrotanoides

Lavandula multifida var. abrotanoides, commonly known as narrow-leaved lavender, is a compact, aromatic shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, linear leaves and produces spikes of pale purple to violet flowers. The plant is characterized by its dense growth habit and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal water once established.

This variety is valued for its fragrance and ornamental appeal, as well as its historical use in herbal medicine.
