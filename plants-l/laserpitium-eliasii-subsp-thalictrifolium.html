Laserpitium eliasii subsp. thalictrifolium

Laserpitium eliasii subsp.

thalictrifolium, commonly known as the alpine avens, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high alpine regions of Europe. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at elevations above 1,500 meters.

The plant has basal leaves that are pinnately divided, and its flowers are small, clustered in dense, racemose inflorescences. It produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The subspecies is distinguished by its leaf morphology and floral structure compared to other members of the species.
