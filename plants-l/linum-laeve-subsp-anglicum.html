Linum laeve subsp. anglicum

Linum laeve subsp.

anglicum, commonly known as English flax, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and blue to purple flowers.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain small, oval seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Its historical significance lies in the use of its fibers for textile production.
