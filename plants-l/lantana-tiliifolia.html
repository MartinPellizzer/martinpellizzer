Lantana tiliifolia

Lantana tiliifolia, commonly known as the white lantana, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant has opposite, simple, and usually hairy leaves that are often aromatic. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common ornamental plant in gardens.

Lantana tiliifolia is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
