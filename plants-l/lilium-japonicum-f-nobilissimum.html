Lilium japonicum f. nobilissimum

Lilium japonicum f. nobilissimum, commonly known as the Japanese glory of the snow, is a striking cultivar of the lily family.

It is characterized by its large, white, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is native to Japan and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This variety is prized for its elegant appearance and fragrant blooms.
