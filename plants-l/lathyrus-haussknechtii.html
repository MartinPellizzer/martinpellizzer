Lathyrus haussknechtii

Lathyrus haussknechtii, commonly known as the Hausknecht's vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds, typical of the legume family. It has compound leaves with several leaflets and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Lathyrus haussknechtii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in ecological restoration projects.
