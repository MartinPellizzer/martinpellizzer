Ligaria emarginata

Ligaria emarginata, commonly known as the Chilean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has hairy, glandular leaves that help it retain moisture. Its flowers are usually blue or purple and bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Ligaria emarginata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.
