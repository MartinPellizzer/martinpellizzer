Lophiolepis × ciliatiformis

Lophiolepis × ciliatiformis, commonly known as the hybrid rock cress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and linear, with a slightly hairy texture, while its flowers are white or pale yellow.

Lophiolepis × ciliatiformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a ground cover.
