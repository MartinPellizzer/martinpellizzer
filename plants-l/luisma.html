Luisma

Luisma, commonly known as the "ghost flower," is a striking plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, translucent petals that resemble delicate, ghostly veils, giving it its ethereal appearance. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its bioluminescent properties under certain conditions. Luisma typically thrives in high-altitude environments, often found in cloud forests and rocky terrains.

Its rare and unusual features make it a subject of fascination for botanists and plant enthusiasts alike.
