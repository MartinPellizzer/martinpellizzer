Leptatherum japonicum

Leptatherum japonicum, commonly known as Japanese fineleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically less than 2 mm wide. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grassy areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its fine texture and low maintenance requirements.
