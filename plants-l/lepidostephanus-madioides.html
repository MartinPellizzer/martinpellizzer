Lepidostephanus madioides

Lepidostephanus madioides, commonly known as the Madioides lily, is a striking plant native to South Africa.

It is a bulbous perennial that produces tall, upright stems with showy, white to pale purple flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in its arid environment. Its flowers are typically arranged in clusters at the top of the stem, adding to its ornamental value.

Lepidostephanus madioides is well-suited for rock gardens and dry landscapes due to its drought tolerance and unique appearance.
