Lucaea plumbea

Lucaea plumbea, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, blue-gray foliage and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and thrives in partial to full sun. Its foliage is often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, mist-like appearance.

Lucaea plumbea is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
