Liatris patens

Liatris patens, commonly known as the showy blazing star, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern and central United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, upright stems that bear dense, spike-like clusters of violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in prairies, meadows, and open woodlands. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually gray-green in color.

Liatris patens is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
