Lindernia dubia

Lindernia dubia, commonly known as the slender lindernia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, oval-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of purple or pink, typically blooming from spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Lindernia dubia is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
