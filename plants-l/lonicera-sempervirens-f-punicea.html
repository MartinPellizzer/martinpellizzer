Lonicera sempervirens f. punicea

Lonicera sempervirens f. punicea, commonly known as the redflower honeysuckle, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green and glossy on top with a lighter underside. The plant produces fragrant, tubular red flowers in clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to serve as a habitat for wildlife.
