Listera nipponica

Listera nipponica, commonly known as the Japanese basketwort, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Listera nipponica is valued both for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
