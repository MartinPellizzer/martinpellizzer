Linaria oblongifolia subsp. haenseleri

Linaria oblongifolia subsp.

haenseleri, commonly known as the narrow-leaf toadflax, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It features slender, branching stems that grow from 30 to 60 centimeters in height.

The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers in racemes, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are elongated and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
