Lobelia courtallensis

Lobelia courtallensis, commonly known as the golden lobelia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are erect and somewhat woody.

Lobelia courtallensis is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its traditional use in herbal medicine.
