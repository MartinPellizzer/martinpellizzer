Lindernia porphyrodinea

Lindernia porphyrodinea, commonly known as the purple mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodland floors and stream banks. The plant features slender, erect stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. Its showy, clustered inflorescences produce small, tubular flowers in shades of purple and pink, which bloom from late spring to early summer.

Lindernia porphyrodinea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partially shaded environments.
