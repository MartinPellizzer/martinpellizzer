Ligularia kaialpina

Ligularia kaialpina, commonly known as the Kailai fern, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs. The plant produces yellow-orange flower heads that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Ligularia kaialpina thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for shady garden settings.
