Loranthus eminii f. rufescens

Loranthus eminii f. rufescens, commonly known as the red-stemmed mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees.

It belongs to the family Loranthaceae and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, reddish berries. The plant lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while it can produce some of its own food through photosynthesis. It is typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, often growing on native trees such as eucalyptus and acacia.

This species plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing food for birds and insects.
