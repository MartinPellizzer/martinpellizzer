Loewia microphylla

Loewia microphylla, commonly known as the dwarf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and delicate, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky substance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Loewia microphylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
