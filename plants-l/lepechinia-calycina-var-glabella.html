Lepechinia calycina var. glabella

Lepechinia calycina var. glabella, commonly known as the glabrous lepechinia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
