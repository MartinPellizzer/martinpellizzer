Lasianthus plagiophyllus

Lasianthus plagiophyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth. Its flowers are often violet or purple in color and attract various pollinators.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
