Linaria pauciflora proles diffusa

Linaria pauciflora proles diffusa, commonly known as the spreading toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.

Linaria pauciflora proles diffusa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
