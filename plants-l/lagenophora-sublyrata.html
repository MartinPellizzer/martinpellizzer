Lagenophora sublyrata

Lagenophora sublyrata, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant has a woody stem and is often found growing in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate in shape and have a rough, hairy texture.

Lagenophora sublyrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
