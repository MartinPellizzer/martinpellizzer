Livistona benthamii

Livistona benthamii, commonly known as the Bentham palm, is a species of palm native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces yellowish-pink flowers that are arranged in clusters, followed by edible fruits that are roughly spherical in shape. It thrives in coastal and sandy soils, often found in humid environments.

Livistona benthamii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a landscape plant.
