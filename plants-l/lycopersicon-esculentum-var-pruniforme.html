Lycopersicon esculentum var. pruniforme

Lycopersicon esculentum var. pruniforme, commonly known as the purple tomato, is a botanical variant of the domesticated tomato.

It is characterized by its distinctive purple or violet-colored fruit, which develops from the flower and matures in late summer. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with hairy stems and alternate leaves. Its flowers are yellow and five-petaled, typical of the Solanaceae family.

The fruit is a fleshy berry containing numerous seeds, and it is cultivated for its unique color and flavor.
