Lomatium utriculatum var. papillatum

Lomatium utriculatum var. papillatum, commonly known as the papillate lomatium, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and umbellate flowers. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered with small, raised bumps or papillae, giving it its specific variety name. It produces small, yellowish-white flowers in clustered umbels, which bloom in early summer.

This species is typically found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks.
