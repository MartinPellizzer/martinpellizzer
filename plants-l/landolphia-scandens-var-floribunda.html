Landolphia scandens var. floribunda

Landolphia scandens var. floribunda, commonly known as the African Trumpet Vine, is a vigorous climbing plant native to tropical Africa.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves and produces large, showy yellow-orange flowers with a strong, sweet fragrance. The plant is characterized by its long, tubular corolla that attracts pollinators such as bats and birds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in subtropical regions.
