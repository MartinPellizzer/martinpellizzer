Lychnis californica

Lychnis californica, commonly known as the California pink, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. The plant features basal leaves that are oval to lance-shaped with hairy surfaces, and it produces showy, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are clustered in dense, rounded inflorescences, and the plant is often found in coastal and inland regions of California.

Lychnis californica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for native pollinators.
