Lepidium kabulicum

Lepidium kabulicum, commonly known as the Kabul cress, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like broccoli and mustard. The plant is characterized by its small, edible leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in rocky or disturbed soils.

Due to its medicinal and nutritional properties, it has been used in traditional medicine for centuries.
