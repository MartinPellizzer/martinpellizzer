Loranthus griseus var. grandiflorus

Loranthus griseus var. grandiflorus, commonly known as the large-flowered mistletoe, is a parasitic plant belonging to the Loranthaceae family.

It typically grows on the branches of host trees, deriving its nutrients through specialized structures called haustoria. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. Its berries are usually red or yellow, containing seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This species is native to parts of Asia and is often found in forested areas where suitable host trees are present.
