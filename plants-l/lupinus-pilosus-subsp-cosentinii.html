Lupinus pilosus subsp. cosentinii

Lupinus pilosus subsp.

cosentinii, commonly known as the silky lupine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its finely hairy foliage and distinctive seed pods that mature into a spiny, curved structure. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
