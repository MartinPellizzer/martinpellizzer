Libanotis gummifera

Libanotis gummifera, commonly known as the gum tree of Lebanon, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and leathery, evergreen leaves. The plant produces a sticky, resinous sap that has been historically used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in its native habitat.
