Lythrum petiolatum

Lythrum petiolatum, commonly known as narrow-leaf rosemary, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Lythraceae family, which includes other plants such as the poinsettia and the red root. The plant typically grows in wetlands and along stream banks, thriving in moist, sandy soils. It produces vibrant purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Its foliage is lance-shaped and often has a slightly aromatic scent when crushed.
