Lasiopus viridifolius

Lasiopus viridifolius, commonly known as the green-leaved lichen, is a unique organism that belongs to the Lecanoromycetes class of lichens.

It is characterized by its foliose thallus, which consists of a leaf-like structure with a distinct upper and lower surface. The upper surface is typically green to yellowish in color, while the lower surface is often more complex and contains the fungal hyphae. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often growing on bark, soil, or rocks.

This lichen is known for its ecological importance in nutrient cycling and as an indicator of air quality.
