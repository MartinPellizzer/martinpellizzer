Lobelia flaccidifolia

Lobelia flaccidifolia, commonly known as the flaccidleaf lobelia, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. Its leaves are usually oval-shaped and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Lobelia flaccidifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
