Lippia carterae

Lippia carterae, commonly known as Texas caryota or wild sage, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky soils. The plant features slender, branched stems and small, opposite leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are clustered in spikes and are usually violet or purple in color, attracting various pollinators.

Lippia carterae is valued for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
