Litsea teysmannii

Litsea teysmannii, commonly known as the Philippine laurier or Taysmann's litsea, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in the Philippines, Indonesia, and Malaysia. The plant is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree, typically reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and usually elliptic to ovate in shape, with a glossy upper surface and a lighter underside.

The flowers are small, fragrant, and arranged in clusters, followed by small, round berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.
