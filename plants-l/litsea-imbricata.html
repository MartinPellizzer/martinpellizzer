Litsea imbricata

Litsea imbricata, commonly known as the Indian laurier or sweetleaf, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, smooth, and often glossy dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by spherical, dark blue berries that are edible. Litsea imbricata thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties make it a significant species in both horticulture and traditional medicine.
