Lobelia secunda

Lobelia secunda, commonly known as the common lobelia, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring slender stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Lobelia secunda is often found in moist, shady habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking native plants that attract pollinators.
