Lychnis alpina var. typica

Lychnis alpina var. typica, commonly known as the alpine campion, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. The plant features erect stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are pink to red, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems, and bloom from late spring to early summer.

Lychnis alpina var. typica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
