Lepanthes alopex

Lepanthes alopex, commonly known as the silver-haired orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, such as cloud forests, where it thrives on tree roots or in rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, giving it its common name.

Lepanthes alopex is a miniature orchid, often reaching only a few centimeters in height, making it a unique and sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
