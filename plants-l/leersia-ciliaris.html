Leersia ciliaris

Leersia ciliaris, commonly known as beakgrass, is a perennial grass native to wetland areas in the Americas.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive beak-like tip on its seed heads. The plant typically grows in marshy or swampy environments, preferring saturated soil conditions. Its flowering occurs in late summer, producing small, spike-like inflorescences.

Leersia ciliaris plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
