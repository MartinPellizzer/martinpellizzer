Leandra ciliolata

Leandra ciliolata, commonly known as the velvetleaf orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers, which come in shades of white and pink, are clustered in racemes and bloom during the wet season.

Leandra ciliolata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
