Lespedeza × longifolia

Lespedeza × longifolia, commonly known as the Chinese clover, is a hybrid species within the legume family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is native to East Asia and is often found in grassy areas and disturbed soils. It has a fibrous root system that helps prevent soil erosion.

Lespedeza × longifolia is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in agricultural and ecological restoration settings.
