Limonium confusum subsp. psilocladum

Limonium confusum subsp.

psilocladum, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Europe and North Africa. It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dunes or along the shoreline. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, with a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are erect and sturdy.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate saline and windy conditions.
