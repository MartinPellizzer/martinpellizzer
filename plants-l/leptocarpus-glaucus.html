Leptocarpus glaucus

Leptocarpus glaucus, commonly known as the silverleaf lily, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Aroid family (Araceae) and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescence composed of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands, where it thrives in humid conditions. Its leaves are broad and often have a waxy, bluish-green appearance, contributing to its common name.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its unique floral display.
