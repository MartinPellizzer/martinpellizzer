Lycopodioides picta

Lycopodioides picta, commonly known as the painted clubmoss, is a species of lycophyte characterized by its distinctive, intricate foliage.

It features small, scale-like leaves arranged in whorls along its stems, which are typically low-growing and creeping. The plant produces spore-bearing cones, known as strobili, which are often clustered at the ends of its branches. Lycopodioides picta thrives in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it an important component of certain understory plant communities.
