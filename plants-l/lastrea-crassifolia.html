Lastrea crassifolia

Lastrea crassifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf lastrea, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family, which is characterized by its fleshy leaves and adaptability to arid environments. The plant features thick, fleshy leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water storage and reducing transpiration. It produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and xeriscapes.
