Lessingianthus pumilus

Lessingianthus pumilus, commonly known as the dwarf sunflower, is a small flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of only a few inches, with narrow, grass-like leaves and bright yellow flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its compact size and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
