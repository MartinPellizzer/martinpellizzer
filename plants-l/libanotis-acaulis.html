Libanotis acaulis

Libanotis acaulis, commonly known as the slender bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that are often hairless and can grow up to 30 centimeters in height. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically arranged in a basal rosette.

Libanotis acaulis is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as cliffs and slopes.
