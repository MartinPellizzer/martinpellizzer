Lotus aurantiacus

Lotus aurantiacus, commonly known as the red lotus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Nelumbonaceae family and is characterized by its striking, large, pink to red blooms. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the surface of water, and its stems are often submerged. The flowers typically open in the early morning and close in the late afternoon.

It is cultivated for its ornamental value and has cultural significance in various regions.
