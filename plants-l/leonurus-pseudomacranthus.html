Leonurus pseudomacranthus

Leonurus pseudomacranthus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hedgehog.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its hairy stems and small, tubular flowers. The plant is native to parts of Asia, particularly China and India, and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly serrated edge, while the flowers are typically purple or pink in color.

Leonurus pseudomacranthus is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ease of cultivation.
