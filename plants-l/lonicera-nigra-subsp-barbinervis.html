Lonicera nigra subsp. barbinervis

Lonicera nigra subsp.

barbinervis, commonly known as the black honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries and its distinctive, narrow, serrated leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. It produces fragrant, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

This subspecies is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
