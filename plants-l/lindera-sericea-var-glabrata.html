Lindera sericea var. glabrata

Lindera sericea var. glabrata, commonly known as silverleaf ligustrum, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features slender, silver-green leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, which are followed by orange-red berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and adaptability to partial shade.
