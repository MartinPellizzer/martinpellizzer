Linaria flava subsp. sardoa

Linaria flava subsp.

sardoa, commonly known as the Sardonic horned poppy, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that resemble horns, which bloom from spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in open habitats such as coastal cliffs and mountain slopes. Its leaves are linear and slightly hairy, while the stems are erect and branched.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and unique flower shape.
