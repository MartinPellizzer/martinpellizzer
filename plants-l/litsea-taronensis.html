Litsea taronensis

Litsea taronensis, commonly known as the Taronese-laurel, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter on the underside. It produces small, white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by spherical, red berries that are edible.

Litsea taronensis is often used in traditional medicine for its aromatic properties and is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
