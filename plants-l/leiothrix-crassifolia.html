Leiothrix crassifolia

Leiothrix crassifolia, commonly known as the thick-leafed mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees.

It has broad, leathery leaves that are usually dark green and glossy, which help it capture sunlight efficiently. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are often clustered together. Its berries are typically red or orange, providing a food source for birds and other animals.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can affect the health of its host tree if left unchecked.
