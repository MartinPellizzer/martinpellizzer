Larrea gracilis

Larrea gracilis, commonly known as the slender grevillea, is a shrub native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, linear leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, with a deep root system to access water. It is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental value.

Larrea gracilis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.
