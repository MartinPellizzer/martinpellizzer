Linaria × cornubiensis

Linaria × cornubiensis, commonly known as the Cornubian toadflax, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Linaria vulgaris and Linaria daphnifolia.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species native to southern England and parts of western Europe. The plant features showy, yellow-orange to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, and it often grows in disturbed soils along roadsides and waste areas.

Linaria × cornubiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
