Leocereus estevesii

Leocereus estevesii, commonly known as the Estevess's night blooming cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a member of the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its tall, columnar stems covered in white, woolly spines. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers that open at night, typically in shades of white and pink. It thrives in arid environments and requires well-draining soil and minimal water.

Due to its unique appearance and nocturnal blooming habit, it is a popular choice for collectors and enthusiasts of desert flora.
