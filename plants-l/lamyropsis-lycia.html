Lamyropsis lycia

Lamyropsis lycia, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive and hardy addition to gardens in temperate climates.
