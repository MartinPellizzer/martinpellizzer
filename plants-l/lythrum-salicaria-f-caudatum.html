Lythrum salicaria f. caudatum

Lythrum salicaria f. caudatum, commonly known as the purple loosestrife with long shoots, is a botanical variant of the common purple loosestrife.

It is characterized by its elongated, slender stems and clusters of vibrant purple flowers. This form exhibits a more compact growth habit compared to the typical species, with a greater emphasis on the elongated floral structures. It thrives in moist soils, often found in wetlands and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical features make it a notable ornamental plant in horticulture and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
