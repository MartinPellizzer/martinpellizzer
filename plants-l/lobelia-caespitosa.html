Lobelia caespitosa

Lobelia caespitosa, commonly known as the clustered lobelia, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant features small, tubular blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

Lobelia caespitosa is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
