Lilium thunbergianum var. bicolor

Lilium thunbergianum var. bicolor, commonly known as the Japanese Lily, is a striking bulbous perennial plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and red. The plant typically blooms in late summer to early autumn, producing large, recurved petals that resemble the shape of a lily. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This variety is valued in gardens for its vibrant color and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
