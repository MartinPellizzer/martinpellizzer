Lacistema pittieri

Lacistema pittieri, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Mexico and Central America.

It features heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant is part of the Convolvulaceae family, which includes many species of morning glories. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its rapid growth habit makes it suitable for trellises or as a ground cover in tropical gardens.
