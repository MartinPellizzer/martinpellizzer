Lantana triplinervia var. puberulenta

Lantana triplinervia var. puberulenta, commonly known as the velvetleaf lantana, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to yellow, orange, and red, depending on the variety. The leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and covered in a fine, velvety pubescence, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The plant is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
