Laurelia crenata

Laurelia crenata, commonly known as the coastal laurel, is a flowering plant native to the western coast of South America.

It is a member of the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants such as cinnamon and avocado. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are typically leathery and have a serrated edge, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Laurelia crenata thrives in coastal environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils along the shoreline.
