Lotus lamprocarpus

Lotus lamprocarpus, commonly known as the slender lotus, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, clay soils. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens.
