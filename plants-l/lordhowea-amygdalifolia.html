Lordhowea amygdalifolia

Lordhowea amygdalifolia, commonly known as the Lord Howe Island fig, is a tropical plant native to Lord Howe Island in the South Pacific.

It is a large, evergreen tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a broad, spreading canopy. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape, with a smooth, glossy surface. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, sweet-smelling fruits.

The species is notable for its ecological importance to the island's unique biodiversity.
