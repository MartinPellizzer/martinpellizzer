Lepanthes peruviana

Lepanthes peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments within cloud forests. The plant features a slender rhizome and produces delicate, showy flowers with intricate patterns on their petals and sepals. Its blossoms are often pale green or white with a slight pinkish hue, and they emit a subtle fragrance.

Lepanthes peruviana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and rarity.
