Loranthus pungwensis

Loranthus pungwensis, commonly known as the Pungwe mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of various host trees.

It belongs to the family Loranthaceae and is native to southern Africa, particularly Zimbabwe and South Africa. The plant has a distinctive appearance with small, oval-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in groups. It relies on its host for water and nutrients, while it provides nectar to attract pollinators like birds and insects.

The Pungwe mistletoe plays an important ecological role in its habitat by supporting biodiversity through its interactions with host trees and pollinators.
