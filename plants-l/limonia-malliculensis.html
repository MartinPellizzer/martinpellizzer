Limonia malliculensis

Limonia malliculensis, commonly known as the Madagascar tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by distinctive, fleshy, orange-red fruits. Its fruits are edible and have a tart, acidic flavor, often used in local cuisines.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
