Lotononis exstipulata

Lotononis exstipulata, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and showy, clustered flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant typically grows as a perennial herb, often with a short, thick stem and broad, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually borne on upright stems and bloom throughout the growing season.

It is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its preference for bright, indirect light and moderate watering.
