Luziola spruceana

Luziola spruceana, commonly known as the Spruce's lichen, is a species of lichen that belongs to the family Parmeliaceae.

It is characterized by its crustose growth form, which adheres tightly to the surfaces of trees, typically on coniferous bark. The thallus is pale to dark grey, often with a granular texture, and it contains a mix of photobionts, primarily green algae. It is found in temperate and boreal regions, growing on tree trunks and branches in shaded, humid environments.

This lichen is an important indicator species for forest health and air quality due to its sensitivity to environmental changes.
