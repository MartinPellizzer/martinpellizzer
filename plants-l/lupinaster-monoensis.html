Lupinaster monoensis

Lupinaster monoensis, commonly known as the Mono lupinaster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, butterfly-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, enriching the soil with nutrients.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
