Lycopodium piliferum

Lycopodium piliferum, commonly known as the clubmoss, is a small evergreen shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae and is characterized by its spore-bearing cones and scale-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its reproductive structures, called strobila, are distinctive and contain the spores necessary for propagation.

Lycopodium piliferum plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various small organisms.
