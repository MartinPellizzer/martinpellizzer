Lycopersicon esculentum provar. mikadofolium

Lycopersicon esculentum provar. mikadofolium, commonly known as the Mikado leaf tomato, is a botanical variant of the common tomato.

It is characterized by its distinctive, broad, and lobed leaves that give it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red when ripe. It is adapted to grow in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruits.
