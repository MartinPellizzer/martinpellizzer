Liparis flava

Liparis flava, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, often forming a rosette at the base.

Liparis flava is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
