Linaria melanogramma

Linaria melanogramma, commonly known as the black-leaved toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically dark green with a distinctive black or purple underside, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
