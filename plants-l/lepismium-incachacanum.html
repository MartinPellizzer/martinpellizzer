Lepismium incachacanum

Lepismium incachacanum, commonly known as the Andean flame vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to the Andes Mountains.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has woody stems that can grow quite long, often reaching several meters in height. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and typically have a velvety texture.

Lepismium incachacanum thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
