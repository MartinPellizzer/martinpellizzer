Lysimachia quadriflora f. albescens

Lysimachia quadriflora f. albescens, commonly known as the white-flowered four-flower chickweed, is a variant of the species Lysimachia quadriflora.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features small, opposite, ovate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters of four, giving it its common name.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
