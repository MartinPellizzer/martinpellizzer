Lantana fucata var. longipes

Lantana fucata var. longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed lantana, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange and red. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a woody, branching structure. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in coastal areas and disturbed habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
