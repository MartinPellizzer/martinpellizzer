Linanthus watsonii subsp. watsonii

Linanthus watsonii subsp.

watsonii, commonly known as the Watson's flaxflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and showy yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are clustered in terminal spikes.

It is a perennial herb that plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
