Lepisanthes burmanica

Lepisanthes burmanica, commonly known as the Burmese orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded forest environments. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters.

Lepisanthes burmanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic qualities.
