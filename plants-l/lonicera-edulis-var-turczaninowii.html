Lonicera edulis var. turczaninowii

Lonicera edulis var. turczaninowii, commonly known as the Turczaninow's honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically pubescent on the underside. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, blooming in the spring. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that ripens in late summer, containing several seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible berries.
