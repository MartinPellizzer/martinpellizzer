Lycopus cavaleriei

Lycopus cavaleriei, commonly known as Chinese bugleweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its small, blue to purple flowers bloom in late summer, and it produces small, nut-like fruits.

Lycopus cavaleriei is often used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.
