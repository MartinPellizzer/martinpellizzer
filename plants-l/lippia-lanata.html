Lippia lanata

Lippia lanata, commonly known as the woolly lippia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and small, fragrant white or pink flowers. The plant belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is often found in open woodlands and disturbed soils. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Lippia lanata is sometimes used in traditional medicine and is valued for its aromatic properties.
