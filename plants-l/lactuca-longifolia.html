Lactuca longifolia

Lactuca longifolia, commonly known as the slender lettuce or longleaf lettuce, is a species of flowering plant in the daisy family, Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and elongated, narrow leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typical of the Asteraceae family. Lactuca longifolia is native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia, and it is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides.

It is not typically cultivated for food but is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
