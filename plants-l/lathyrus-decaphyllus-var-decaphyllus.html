Lathyrus decaphyllus var. decaphyllus

Lathyrus decaphyllus var. decaphyllus, commonly known as the common blue flax, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, erect stems that bear alternate, pinnately compound leaves with a delicate, fern-like appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in early summer. Its seed pods are cylindrical and dehiscent, releasing tiny seeds when mature.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
