Lastrea coniifolia

Lastrea coniifolia, commonly known as the mountain holly, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, dark blue berries that persist through winter. The plant typically grows in rocky or wooded areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a leathery texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
