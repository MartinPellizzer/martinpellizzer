Lupinaster africanus

Lupinaster africanus, commonly known as the African lupin, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Lupinaster africanus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in some traditional herbal practices.
