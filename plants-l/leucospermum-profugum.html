Leucospermum profugum

Leucospermum profugum, commonly known as the silver candlestick, is a South African species of flowering plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, cylindrical stems that are covered in dense, white, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in rounded heads at the tips of the stems. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
