Lapathum × acutum var. minimum

Lapathum × acutum var. minimum, commonly known as the dwarf red deadnettle, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae and is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and distinctive pink or purple flowers. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowering period occurs in the spring and early summer, and it produces small, nutlet-like fruits.

The species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support pollinator populations.
