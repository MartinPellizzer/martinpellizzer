Lepidium villarsii subsp. anticarium

Lepidium villarsii subsp.

anticarium, commonly known as the Anticaria cress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family. It is a small, annual herb characterized by its slender stems and small, pinnately divided leaves.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, typical of the Brassicaceae family. Its fruits are short, cylindrical siliques that contain several seeds.

It is native to southern Europe and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
