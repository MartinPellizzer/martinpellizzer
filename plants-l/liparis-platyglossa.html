Liparis platyglossa

Liparis platyglossa, commonly known as the broad-leafed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its linear, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and grasslands. Its roots form a symbiotic relationship with mycorrhizal fungi, which aid in nutrient absorption.

Liparis platyglossa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
