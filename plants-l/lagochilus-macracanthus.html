Lagochilus macracanthus

Lagochilus macracanthus, commonly known as the large-armed rock hyssop, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are narrow and often covered with fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

Lagochilus macracanthus is adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
