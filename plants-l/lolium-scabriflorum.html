Lolium scabriflorum

Lolium scabriflorum, commonly known as roughleaf grass, is a flowering plant species native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant features narrow, rough-textured leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to blue flowers. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open, disturbed habitats.

Lolium scabriflorum is adapted to arid conditions and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
