Lycopodium mandiocanum var. mexicanum

Lycopodium mandiocanum var. mexicanum, commonly known as the Mexican clubmoss, is a species of lycopod that belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant characterized by its spore-bearing cones and needle-like leaves. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. It has a rhizomatous root system that helps it anchor and absorb nutrients from the soil.

The plant is notable for its ability to thrive in humid climates and its historical use in traditional medicine.
