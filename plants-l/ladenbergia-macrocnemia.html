Ladenbergia macrocnemia

Ladenbergia macrocnemia, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The tree produces pods that contain flat, seed-like structures, which are used in traditional medicine. Its wood is valued for its durability and fine grain, though it is also used in herbal remedies.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitats.
