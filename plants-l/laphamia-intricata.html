Laphamia intricata

Laphamia intricata, commonly known as the intricate lichen, is a unique species of lichen that exhibits a complex symbiotic relationship between a fungus and an alga.

It is typically found growing on the bark of trees in humid, shaded environments, particularly in temperate regions. The thallus of Laphamia intricata is leathery and crust-like, providing a stable structure for the symbiotic organisms within. Its coloration can range from pale green to dark green, depending on the environmental conditions and the health of the algal component.

This plant plays a vital role in nutrient cycling and serves as a bioindicator of air quality in its ecosystem.
