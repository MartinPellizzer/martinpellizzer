Lindera pulcherrima subsp. thomsonii

Lindera pulcherrima subsp.

thomsonii, commonly known as the Thomson's sweet pepper tree, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States. It features aromatic, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and dark green coloration.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during late spring, followed by orange-red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
