Lupinus platanophilus

Lupinus platanophilus, commonly known as the desert lupine, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces spikes of blue to purple flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along dry streambeds.

Lupinus platanophilus is adapted to withstand drought conditions and is an important component of desert ecosystems.
