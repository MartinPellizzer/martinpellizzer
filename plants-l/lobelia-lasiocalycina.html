Lobelia lasiocalycina

Lobelia lasiocalycina, commonly known as the rough calyx lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers. The plant has hairy, rough-textured leaves and bracts, which give it its common name. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.

Lobelia lasiocalycina is a valuable plant for pollinators and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
