Lespedeza gerardiana

Lespedeza gerardiana, commonly known as hairy lespedeza, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Lespedeza gerardiana is often found in dry, open habitats such as woodland edges and sandy soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for ecological restoration projects.
