Lophiolepis pseudobracteosa

Lophiolepis pseudobracteosa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lichen, is a unique plant characterized by its dense, crustose growth form.

It typically forms a thin, adherent layer on rock surfaces, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant lacks distinct leaves and instead relies on its thallus for photosynthesis. Its structure is composed of a complex network of fungal hyphae intertwined with algal cells.

This symbiotic relationship allows it to thrive in harsh, nutrient-poor conditions.
