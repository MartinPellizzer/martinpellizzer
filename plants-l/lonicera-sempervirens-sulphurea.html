Lonicera sempervirens sulphurea

Lonicera sempervirens sulphurea, commonly known as the golden honeysuckle, is a striking plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The leaves are typically oval-shaped and have a glossy appearance, adding to its ornamental value. This species is known for its fragrant, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
