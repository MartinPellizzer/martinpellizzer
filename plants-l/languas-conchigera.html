Languas conchigera

Languas conchigera, commonly known as the golden star vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling stars. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are glossy and arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Its botanical name reflects its unique appearance and ecological significance in its native habitat.
