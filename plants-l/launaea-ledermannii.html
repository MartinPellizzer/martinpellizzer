Launaea ledermannii

Launaea ledermannii, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant has a succulent, branching structure with hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. Its yellow flowers bloom in summer, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Launaea ledermannii is adapted to survive in drought conditions, making it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.
