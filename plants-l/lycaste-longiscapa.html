Lycaste longiscapa

Lycaste longiscapa, commonly known as the long-spurred lycaste, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers with long, slender spurs that give it its common name. The plant typically grows as a sympodial orchid, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

Lycaste longiscapa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts due to its unique floral structure.
