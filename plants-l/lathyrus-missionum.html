Lathyrus missionum

Lathyrus missionum, commonly known as the Mission pea, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial vine that produces showy, pea-like flowers in shades of pink and purple. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and bears small, edible pods containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
