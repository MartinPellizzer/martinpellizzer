Landolphia dulcis var. dulcis

Landolphia dulcis var. dulcis, commonly known as the sweet-scented arrowroot.

It is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia, characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces a starchy tuberous root that is edible and has a mild, sweet flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

The species is valued both for its culinary uses and as an ornamental plant in gardens.
