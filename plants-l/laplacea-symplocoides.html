Laplacea symplocoides

Laplacea symplocoides, commonly known as the silverleaf shrub, is a small evergreen shrub native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, silvery foliage and compact growth habit. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often used in landscaping and erosion control.
