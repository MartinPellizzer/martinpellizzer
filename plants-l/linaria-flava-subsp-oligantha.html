Linaria flava subsp. oligantha

Linaria flava subsp.

oligantha, commonly known as the yellow sandwort, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Europe and North Africa. It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, adapting to its harsh environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats. Linaria flava subsp.

oligantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
