Lagenocarpus eriopodus

Lagenocarpus eriopodus, commonly known as the hairy fruit tree, is a species native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its rough, bark-like trunk and dense foliage. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by fleshy, berry-like fruits that are edible. Its leaves are typically elliptical with a hairy texture, contributing to its common name.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in some cultures.
