Laxmannia roei

Laxmannia roei, commonly known as the Kashmir rose, is a perennial shrub native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stems. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in mountainous areas with well-drained soil.

Laxmannia roei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
