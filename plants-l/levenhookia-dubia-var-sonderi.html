Levenhookia dubia var. sonderi

Levenhookia dubia var. sonderi, commonly known as the Sonder's false heather, is a low-growing perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has aromatic, needle-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and unique botanical characteristics, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
