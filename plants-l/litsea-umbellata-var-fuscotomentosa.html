Litsea umbellata var. fuscotomentosa

Litsea umbellata var. fuscotomentosa, commonly known as the dark-tomentose litsea, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves, which are covered in a dense, dark gray pubescence. Its flowers are small and clustered in umbels, and it produces berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
