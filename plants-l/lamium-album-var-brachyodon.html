Lamium album var. brachyodon

Lamium album var. brachyodon, commonly known as white dead-nettle, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive white midrib and produces clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and gardens. Its flowers bloom from spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It is often used in herbal medicine for its purported calming and anti-inflammatory properties.
