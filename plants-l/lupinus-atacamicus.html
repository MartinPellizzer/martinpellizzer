Lupinus atacamicus

Lupinus atacamicus, commonly known as the Atacama lupine, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It is adapted to extreme drought conditions, with deep root systems that allow it to access groundwater. The plant produces tall, spindly stems with clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. Its leaves are typically narrow and hairy, reducing water loss through transpiration.

Lupinus atacamicus plays a vital role in stabilizing desert soils and is an important species for ecological studies in harsh environments.
