Lychnis dioica var. rubra

Lychnis dioica var. rubra, commonly known as red campion, is a flowering plant belonging to the carnation family (Caryophyllaceae).

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of pink to red, five-petaled flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy appearance. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in sunny, well-drained soils.

This plant is often found in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed areas, and is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
