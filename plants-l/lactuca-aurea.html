Lactuca aurea

Lactuca aurea, commonly known as golden lettuce, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, bright yellow leaves, which are hairy and slightly succulent. It produces yellow to orange flowers in a loose cluster, followed by small, dry fruits called achenes.

Lactuca aurea is often found in dry grasslands and along roadsides, thriving in full sun and well-drained soils.
