Lecythis ovata

Lecythis ovata, commonly known as the Brazil nut tree, is a large evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 3 feet in length. The tree produces large, woody fruits that contain multiple seeds, which are the edible Brazil nuts. The flowers are small and green, clustered in dense inflorescences, and the tree is known for its slow growth and long lifespan.

It plays a vital role in the rainforest ecosystem, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
