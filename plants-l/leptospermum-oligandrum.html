Leptospermum oligandrum

Leptospermum oligandrum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tea tree, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, dark brown to black seeds enclosed in woody capsules. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and dry inland regions.

Leptospermum oligandrum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
