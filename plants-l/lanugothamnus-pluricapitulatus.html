Lanugothamnus pluricapitulatus

Lanugothamnus pluricapitulatus, commonly known as the many-headed thamnus, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically gray-green in color, covered in fine hairs. The plant produces numerous small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense, compact clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for rocky or dry landscapes.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and ornamental gardening.
