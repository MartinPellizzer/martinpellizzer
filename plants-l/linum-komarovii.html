Linum komarovii

Linum komarovii, commonly known as Komarov's flax, is a flowering plant native to central Asia.

It belongs to the genus Linum, which includes various species of flowering plants known for their showy blue or white flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments and has a compact, upright growth habit. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in shades of blue or purple, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Linum komarovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
