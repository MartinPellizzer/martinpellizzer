Leucocroton cordifolius

Leucocroton cordifolius, commonly known as the heart-leaved croton, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically a deep green with prominent veins. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that are followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
