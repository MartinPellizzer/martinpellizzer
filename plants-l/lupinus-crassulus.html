Lupinus crassulus

Lupinus crassulus, commonly known as the dwarf lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant features small, narrow leaves and produces legume pods that contain several seeds. Lupinus crassulus is adapted to arid and rocky environments, making it a hardy species suitable for xeriscaping.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, such as nitrogen fixation in the soil.
