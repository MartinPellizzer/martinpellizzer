Lobivia versicolor

Lobivia versicolor, commonly known as the variable lobivia, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a compact, low-growing cactus with a ribbed stem that can reach up to 30 centimeters in height. The plant produces vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange and red, depending on the variety. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it is adapted to thrive in arid and rocky environments.

Lobivia versicolor is popular among cactus enthusiasts for its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
