Lepanthes intricata

Lepanthes intricata, commonly known as the intricate orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and purple. The plant has a compact growth habit with a short, thick rhizome and narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated by enthusiasts but remains relatively rare in the wild.
