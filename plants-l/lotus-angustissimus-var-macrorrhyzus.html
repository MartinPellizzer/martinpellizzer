Lotus angustissimus var. macrorrhyzus

Lotus angustissimus var. macrorrhyzus, commonly known as the narrowleaf lotus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white hairs. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland habitats.
