Limonium binervosum subsp. multiflorum

Limonium binervosum subsp.

multiflorum, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
