Litsea maluensis

Litsea maluensis, commonly known as the Maluensis laurier, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. Its fruit is a drupe, typically containing a single seed, and it is often found in lowland tropical forests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
