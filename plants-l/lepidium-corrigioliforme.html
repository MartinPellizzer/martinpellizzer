Lepidium corrigioliforme

Lepidium corrigioliforme, commonly known as the slender wallflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, and its leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
