Linum mcvaughii

Linum mcvaughii, commonly known as McVaugh's flax, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the genus Linum, which includes various species of flax known for their showy blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with five petals that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Linum mcvaughii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
