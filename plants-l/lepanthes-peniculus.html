Lepanthes peniculus

Lepanthes peniculus, commonly known as the slender star orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central and South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments within tropical forests. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers with a distinctive star-like shape. Its flowers are usually white or pale green with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insects.

Lepanthes peniculus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
