Lupinus × hybridus rubrocyaneus

Lupinus × hybridus rubrocyaneus, commonly known as the purple lupine, is a hybrid species within the genus Lupinus, characterized by its striking violet to purple flower clusters.

This plant is native to the western United States and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces tall, erect stems that support its showy, pea-like blossoms. The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.

Lupinus × hybridus rubrocyaneus is also known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility in agricultural settings.
