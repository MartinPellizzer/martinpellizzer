Lachemilla polylepis

Lachemilla polylepis, commonly known as the silverleaf cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and silver-gray foliage due to a thick layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Lachemilla polylepis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive appearance.
