Lonicera fudzimoriana

Lonicera fudzimoriana, commonly known as the Japanese honeysuckle, is a deciduous vine native to East Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to climb by twining around supports. It blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

However, it can become invasive in some regions if not properly managed.
