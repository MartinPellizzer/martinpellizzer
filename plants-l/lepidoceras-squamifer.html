Lepidoceras squamifer

Lepidoceras squamifer, commonly known as the squamiferous liverwort, is a type of non-vascular plant belonging to the family Lepidocerataceae.

It is characterized by its leaf-like structures called phyllidia, which are arranged in a rosette pattern and often covered in small, scale-like structures. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in rocky or forested areas. It reproduces through spores rather than seeds, and its life cycle includes both a gametophyte and sporophyte stage.

The plant's unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
