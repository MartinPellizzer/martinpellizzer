Lindera odorata

Lindera odorata, commonly known as sweet pepper tree, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, ovate leaves that emit a pleasant fragrance when crushed. Its small, yellowish-green flowers appear in clusters and are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits.

Lindera odorata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
