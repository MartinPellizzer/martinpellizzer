Liabum uniflorum

Liabum uniflorum, commonly known as the single-flower liabum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure. Its flowers are small and yellow, often arranged in a single bloom per stem, giving it its common name.

Liabum uniflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
