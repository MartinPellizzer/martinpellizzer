Leptotes beatricis

Leptotes beatricis, commonly known as the Bricoleur's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the island of Madeira.

It is a small, herbaceous plant with delicate, lance-shaped leaves and a single, slender stem that bears a single flower. The flower is typically pale green or white with a distinctive, elongated lip that is often marked with purple or brown streaks. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
