Linum tenuifolium var. differens

Linum tenuifolium var. differens, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flax variant, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Due to its delicate structure, it requires well-drained soil and moderate watering to flourish.
