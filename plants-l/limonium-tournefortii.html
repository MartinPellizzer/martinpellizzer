Limonium tournefortii

Limonium tournefortii, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, spike-like clusters of blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in coastal environments and well-drained soils, often found in dunes and rocky shores. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is known for its salt tolerance.

Limonium tournefortii is widely cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
