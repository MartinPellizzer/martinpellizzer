Larix × henryana

Larix × henryana, commonly known as the Chinese larch, is a hybrid coniferous tree native to China.

It belongs to the genus Larix, which includes various species of larches, and is characterized by its needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure. The tree typically grows in mountainous regions and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils. Its foliage turns a striking golden yellow in the fall, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Larix × henryana is valued for its hardiness and ability to adapt to a variety of environmental conditions.
