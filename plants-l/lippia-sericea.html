Lippia sericea

Lippia sericea, commonly known as silky lippia, is a perennial herb native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in spikes at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Lippia sericea is valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional herbal medicine.
