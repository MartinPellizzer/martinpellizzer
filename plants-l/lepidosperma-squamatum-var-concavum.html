Lepidosperma squamatum var. concavum

Lepidosperma squamatum var. concavum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedgeland sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant thrives in wetland and swampy environments, often found in coastal and freshwater habitats. Its flowers are typically white or pale green, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil in these moist areas.

Lepidosperma squamatum var. concavum is part of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants.
