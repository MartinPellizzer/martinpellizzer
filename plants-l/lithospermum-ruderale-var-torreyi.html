Lithospermum ruderale var. torreyi

Lithospermum ruderale var. torreyi, commonly known as the desert lousewort, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial with small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of pink or purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in dry, rocky soils.

The species is characterized by its ability to withstand drought and poor soil conditions, making it a common sight in desert environments.
