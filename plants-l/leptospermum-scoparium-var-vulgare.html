Leptospermum scoparium var. vulgare

Leptospermum scoparium var. vulgare, commonly known as manuka, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, golden-yellow berries that are rich in antioxidants and have medicinal properties. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Manuka is also known for its aromatic oils, which are used in both traditional and modern herbal medicine.
