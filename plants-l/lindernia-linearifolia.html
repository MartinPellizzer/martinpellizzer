Lindernia linearifolia

Lindernia linearifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf lindernia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pale purple to pink. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is a low-growing perennial that is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to native plant landscapes.
