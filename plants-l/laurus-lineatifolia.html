Laurus lineatifolia

Laurus lineatifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved laurel, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dark green, narrow, and leathery leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by black, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental gardening.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in hedges or as a specimen plant in landscaping.
