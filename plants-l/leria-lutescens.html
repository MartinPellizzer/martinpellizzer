Leria lutescens

Leria lutescens, commonly known as the yellow leria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Leria lutescens is often found in moist, shaded environments within forest understories.
