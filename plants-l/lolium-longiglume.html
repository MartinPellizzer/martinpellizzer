Lolium longiglume

Lolium longiglume, commonly known as the long-awned ryegrass, is a grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and elongated awns on its spikelets. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

Lolium longiglume plays a role in ecological systems by providing forage for livestock and supporting biodiversity in its habitat.
