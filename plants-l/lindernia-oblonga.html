Lindernia oblonga

Lindernia oblonga, commonly known as the common lindernia, is a herbaceous annual plant native to South America.

It features opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and slightly serrated. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of purple or pink, which bloom in clusters at the leaf axils. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Lindernia oblonga is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
