Lunaria annua f. dalmatica

Lunaria annua f. dalmatica, commonly known as the Dalmatian moonflower, is a botanical variant of the common helleborine.

It is characterized by its distinctive, orb-shaped seed pods that turn a striking blue-gray color when mature. The plant belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is native to the Mediterranean region, including parts of southern Europe and the Middle East. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and slightly hairy, while its flowers are small and white, often clustered in racemes.

This cultivar is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and unique seed pod structure.
