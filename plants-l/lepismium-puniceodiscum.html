Lepismium puniceodiscum

Lepismium puniceodiscum, commonly known as the red disc lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains in South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from red to pink, often with a distinctive disc-shaped structure at the center. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in a fine, hairy texture, which helps to retain moisture in its native habitat.

Lepismium puniceodiscum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens with specific climate requirements.
