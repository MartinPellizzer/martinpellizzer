Leontodon integerrimus

Leontodon integerrimus, commonly known as the rough hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant features basal leaves that are rough and hairy, with a distinctive yellow-orange flower head composed of numerous small florets. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.

Leontodon integerrimus is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance.
