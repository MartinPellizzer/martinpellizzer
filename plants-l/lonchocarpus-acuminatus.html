Lonchocarpus acuminatus

Lonchocarpus acuminatus, commonly known as the climbing senna, is a leguminous shrub or vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It has a deep root system that helps stabilize soil, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.

However, it can be invasive in certain environments if not managed properly.
