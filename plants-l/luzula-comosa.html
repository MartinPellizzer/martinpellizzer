Luzula comosa

Luzula comosa, commonly known as the tufted wood sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and slender, cylindrical stems that often bear small, triangular leaves. The plant produces spikelets that are typically arranged in clusters, with each spikelet containing several tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Luzula comosa thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of ecological conditions.
