Ligustrum punctifolium

Ligustrum punctifolium, commonly known as the spotted leaf privet, is a species of flowering plant in the Oleaceae family.

It is native to eastern Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with distinctive white or yellow spots. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters, followed by small, dark berries that are edible for some bird species. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various soil types.

Ligustrum punctifolium is also used in traditional medicine in some regions for its purported health benefits.
