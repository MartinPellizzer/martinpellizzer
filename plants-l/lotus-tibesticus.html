Lotus tibesticus

Lotus tibesticus, commonly known as the Tibetan lotus, is a rare aquatic plant native to the high-altitude lakes of the Tibetan Plateau.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom in summer, typically in shades of pink and white. The plant thrives in cold, clear waters and has adapted to the harsh environmental conditions of its habitat. Its leaves are broad and floating, while its roots anchor it to the lakebed.

The Tibetan lotus is not only ecologically significant but also holds cultural importance in local traditions.
