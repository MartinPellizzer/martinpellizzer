Lepanthes papilio

Lepanthes papilio, commonly known as the Papilio Orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its strikingly colorful flowers, often featuring a mix of blue, purple, and white hues. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are designed to attract specific pollinators, such as hummingbirds and bees, through their vibrant colors and nectar-rich structures.

Lepanthes papilio is valued for its ornamental beauty and plays a significant role in the biodiversity of its native ecosystems.
