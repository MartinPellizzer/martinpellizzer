Lepanthes crassifolia

Lepanthes crassifolia, commonly known as the thick-leaved orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its broad, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to its often arid or shaded habitats. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically during the rainy season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a popular subject in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
