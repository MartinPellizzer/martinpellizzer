Lycium isthmense

Lycium isthmense, commonly known as the Isthmus boxthorn, is a shrub native to the Isthmus of Panama.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible, though they are often used in local traditional medicine. Lycium isthmense thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, typically in well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in Central American ecosystems.
