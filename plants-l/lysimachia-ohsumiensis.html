Lysimachia ohsumiensis

Lysimachia ohsumiensis, commonly known as the Japanese spurge or golden thread, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in dense inflorescences, blooming in late spring to early summer.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
