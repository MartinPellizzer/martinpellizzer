Lobelia hirtipes

Lobelia hirtipes, commonly known as the wooly lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish foliage and spikes of blue or purple flowers. This perennial herb typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity.

The plant's unique appearance and ecological role make it a valuable addition to native gardens and conservation efforts.
