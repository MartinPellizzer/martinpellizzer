Linanthus dichotomus subsp. dichotomus

Linanthus dichotomus subsp.

dichotomus, commonly known as the two-branched flax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a member of the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are alternate and often divided into two parts, giving it the "two-branched" appearance in its name.

It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.
