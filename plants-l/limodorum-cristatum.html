Limodorum cristatum

Limodorum cristatum, commonly known as the crowned lily, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the forests of eastern North America, particularly in the southeastern United States. The plant is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy coating.

The species is notable for its unique floral structure, which includes a spurred lip and a distinctive crown-like arrangement of petals.
