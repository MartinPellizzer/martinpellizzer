Lobivia shaferi subsp. leucomalla

Lobivia shaferi subsp.

leucomalla, commonly known as the White-Flowered Lobivia, is a succulent native to the arid regions of South America. It is characterized by its compact, rounded shape and thick, fleshy stems that store water.

The plant produces small, pale yellow to white flowers during the warmer months. Its leaves are typically green and may bear a slight pubescence.

It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
