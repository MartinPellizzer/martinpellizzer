Limonium globulariifolium subsp. tommasinii

Limonium globulariifolium subsp.

tommasinii, commonly known as the Tommasini's sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its globular, spiny seed heads and blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in coastal areas, sandy soils, and disturbed sites, showing a preference for well-drained environments. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
