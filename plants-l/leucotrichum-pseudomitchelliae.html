Leucotrichum pseudomitchelliae

Leucotrichum pseudomitchelliae, commonly known as the silver hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-white hairs that cover the leaf blades, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its inflorescence consists of slender, spike-like clusters of small, greenish flowers.

Leucotrichum pseudomitchelliae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
