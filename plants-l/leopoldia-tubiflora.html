Leopoldia tubiflora

Leopoldia tubiflora, commonly known as the woolly leopoldia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are thick and covered in a fine, hairy coating, which helps retain moisture.

Leopoldia tubiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
