Lithospermum echioides

Lithospermum echioides, commonly known as purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its roots are deep and fibrous.

Lithospermum echioides is often found in prairies, open woods, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.
