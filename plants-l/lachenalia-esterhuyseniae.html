Lachenalia esterhuyseniae

Lachenalia esterhuyseniae, commonly known as the Esterhuysenia lachenalia, is a South African bulbous plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, tubular flowers in shades of white, yellow, or orange. The plant is native to the Western and Northern Cape provinces, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. It is typically found in open, sunny habitats and is adapted to seasonal rainfall patterns.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is often cultivated by succulent enthusiasts and conservationists.
