Lobelia scioensis

Lobelia scioensis, commonly known as Indian tobacco, is a herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple, arranged in spikes along the stem. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Lobelia scioensis is also notable for its medicinal uses by indigenous peoples.
