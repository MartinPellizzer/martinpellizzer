Ligustrum japonicum f. difformis

Ligustrum japonicum f. difformis, commonly known as the Japanese privet.

This ornamental shrub is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its clusters of small, white flowers that appear in the spring. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, with a glossy green surface and a slightly serrated margin. It is native to Japan and is often cultivated for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

The plant is also valued for its use in hedges and as a decorative element in gardens.
