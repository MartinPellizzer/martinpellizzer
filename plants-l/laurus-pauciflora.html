Laurus pauciflora

Laurus pauciflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved laurel, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by dark blue to black berries. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and pleasant fragrance.

Laurus pauciflora prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
