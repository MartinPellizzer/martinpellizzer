Lonicera tatarica var. morrowii

Lonicera tatarica var. morrowii, commonly known as Morrow's honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy on the underside. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of yellow to orange, which bloom in early summer. Its fruits are red, fleshy berries that mature in late summer and are edible for birds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
