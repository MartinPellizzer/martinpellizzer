Lasiorrhiza hieracioides

Lasiorrhiza hieracioides, commonly known as the hairy false dandelion, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous tiny florets. The plant features a basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in late spring to early summer, producing small, winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

The plant is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils and open habitats.
