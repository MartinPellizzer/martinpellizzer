Laurentia hederacea

Laurentia hederacea, commonly known as creeping laurel, is a perennial evergreen shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features small, glossy, dark green leaves that are typically arranged in a dense, mat-like growth pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers in early spring, followed by dark blue berries that are attractive to birds. Its compact form and ability to thrive in shaded environments make it a popular choice for ground cover in woodland gardens.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Ericaceae, which includes other well-known plants such as blueberries and heathers.
