Lobelia humistrata var. kingiana

Lobelia humistrata var. kingiana, commonly known as the King's lobelia, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, hairy stems and small, tubular blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a bluish-green hue with a slightly rough texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with high humidity and partial shade.
