Lathyrus pratensis subsp. hallersteinii

Lathyrus pratensis subsp.

hallersteinii, commonly known as the Hallerstein's vetch, is a flowering plant species belonging to the legume family Fabaceae. It is native to central and eastern Europe, where it typically grows in meadows and grasslands.

The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its trifoliate leaves consist of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces elongated pods containing lens-shaped seeds.

This subspecies is often noted for its ecological importance in supporting pollinators and maintaining soil fertility through nitrogen fixation.
