Liparis espeevijii

Liparis espeevijii, commonly known as the Espeev's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually grass-like, and the inflorescence bears a cluster of small, colorful flowers.

Liparis espeevijii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
