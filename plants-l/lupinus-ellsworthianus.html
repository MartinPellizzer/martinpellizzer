Lupinus ellsworthianus

Lupinus ellsworthianus, commonly known as the Ellsworth lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of blue or violet pea-shaped flowers. The plant has feathery, compound leaves that help it conserve water in arid environments. It plays an important role in ecological systems by fixing nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.

Lupinus ellsworthianus is often found in grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils.
