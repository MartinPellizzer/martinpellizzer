Lepidium hirtum var. emarginatum

Lepidium hirtum var. emarginatum, commonly known as the hairy cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, white or yellow flowers and hairy leaves, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in various habitats such as roadsides and fields. It has a low-growing habit with a mat-forming structure, and its seeds are dispersed by wind.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a range of environmental conditions.
