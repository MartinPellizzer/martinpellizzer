Lathyrus sylvestris var. oblongus

Lathyrus sylvestris var. oblongus, commonly known as the purple vetch, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces trifoliate leaves and pod-like fruits that contain several elliptical seeds. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and traditional herbal uses.
