Linaria fragosoi

Linaria fragosoi, commonly known as the fireweed or mountain flax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in mountainous areas. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it is known for its resilience in harsh environmental conditions.

Linaria fragosoi is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
