Lamium album subsp. sempervirens

Lamium album subsp.

sempervirens, commonly known as white dead-nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are generally pubescent and have a distinctive white or pale underside.

The plant produces clusters of small, white, bilaterally symmetric flowers with a spurred petal, typically blooming in spring. It prefers moist, shady habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its foliage is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its mild sedative and anti-inflammatory properties.
