Lantana obtusata

Lantana obtusata, commonly known as the broad-leaved lantana, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It features opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often hairy and have a waxy texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, which are followed by spherical, woody berries. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it adaptable to various garden environments.

Lantana obtusata is also known for its ability to attract birds and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity.
