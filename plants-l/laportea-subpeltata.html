Laportea subpeltata

Laportea subpeltata, commonly known as the broadleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy purple to pink composite flowers. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped to ovate leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive, aromatic scent. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as swamps and stream banks, and is often found in rich, organic soils.

Laportea subpeltata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
