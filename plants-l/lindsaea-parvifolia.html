Lindsaea parvifolia

Lindsaea parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved lindsaea, is a species of fern belonging to the family Bromeliaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often hidden within the foliage. Lindsaea parvifolia is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid, lowland forests.
