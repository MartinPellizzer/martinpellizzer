Lonicera caerulea subsp. borbasiana

Lonicera caerulea subsp.

borbasiana, commonly known as the Siberian honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its opposite, oval-shaped leaves and fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant produces small, edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tart, sweet flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary uses.

This subspecies is particularly hardy and can tolerate cold climates, making it suitable for northern gardens.
