Lindsaea falcatosora

Lindsaea falcatosora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spikemoss, is a species of fern ally native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Lindsaeaceae and is characterized by its elongated, linear leaves that are often slightly curved. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures, called sporangia, are clustered in spore-bearing spikes at the tips of its stems.

Lindsaea falcatosora plays a role in maintaining soil stability and providing habitat for small invertebrates in its native ecosystems.
