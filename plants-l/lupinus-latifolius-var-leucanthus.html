Lupinus latifolius var. leucanthus

Lupinus latifolius var. leucanthus, commonly known as the white-flowered bush lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and showy white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has a deep taproot system that helps it thrive in well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and nitrogen-fixing capabilities, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
