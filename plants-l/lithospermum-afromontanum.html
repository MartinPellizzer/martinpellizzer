Lithospermum afromontanum

Lithospermum afromontanum, commonly known as the East African rock pepper, is a perennial herb native to the highlands of East Africa.

It is characterized by its erect stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that contain the seeds. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops.

Lithospermum afromontanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
