Limonium tomentellum subsp. alutaceum

Limonium tomentellum subsp.

alutaceum, commonly known as the alpine sea holly, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its low-growing habit and produces clusters of blue to purple flowers.

The plant has hairy, silver-gray leaves that help it retain moisture in harsh alpine conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine collections.
