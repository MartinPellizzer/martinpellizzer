Lasiocephalus otophorus

Lasiocephalus otophorus, commonly known as the hairy-headed orchid, is a unique member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, yellowish-green stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. Native to South Africa, this plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and full sun exposure. It is often found in fynbos vegetation, which is a unique type of shrubland ecosystem.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
