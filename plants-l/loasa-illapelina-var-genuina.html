Loasa illapelina var. genuina

Loasa illapelina var. genuina, commonly known as the Illapel loasa, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of Chile.

It is characterized by its small, ovate leaves that are typically covered in fine, white pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its growth habit is compact, with multiple stems arising from the base.

Loasa illapelina var. genuina is adapted to sandy soils and is often found in maritime climates with high salt exposure.
