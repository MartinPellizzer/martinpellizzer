Leontodon squamosus

Leontodon squamosus, commonly known as the scaly leontodon, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and has a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are yellow and appear in late summer, forming a single head per stem.

The species is notable for its distinctive, scaly appearance on the stem and is often found in open, disturbed habitats.
