Lobelia michelii

Lobelia michelii, commonly known as Michel's lobelia, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and along stream banks. The plant features small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Lobelia michelii is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
