Leptasea chrysantha

Leptasea chrysantha, commonly known as the golden star orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers with a star-shaped pattern. The plant typically grows in tropical rainforests, thriving in moist, shaded environments. Its roots are often mycorrhizal, forming symbiotic relationships with fungi to enhance nutrient absorption.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
