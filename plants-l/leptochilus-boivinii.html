Leptochilus boivinii

Leptochilus boivinii, commonly known as the Boivin's thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically features small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, blooming in early summer. Its leaves are narrow and aromatic, often used in traditional herbal remedies.

Leptochilus boivinii thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable plant for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
