Lathyrus sanguineus

Lathyrus sanguineus, commonly known as the red bupleurum, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and showy, pea-like flowers. The plant produces small, round pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional herbal medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
