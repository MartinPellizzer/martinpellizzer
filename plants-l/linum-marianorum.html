Linum marianorum

Linum marianorum, commonly known as Mary's Tears, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Linaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and has linear, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are arranged in racemes and are often visited by bees and other pollinators.

Linum marianorum thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun.
