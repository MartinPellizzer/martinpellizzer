Linnaea chinensis

Linnaea chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese twinflower, is a small evergreen shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, elliptical leaves with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers in early spring, which are followed by bright red berries. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forests and rocky areas.

Linnaea chinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
