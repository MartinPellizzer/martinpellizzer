Lupinus aridus subsp. ashlandensis

Lupinus aridus subsp.

ashlandensis, commonly known as Ashland lupine, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of pea-like, violet to blue flowers.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.
