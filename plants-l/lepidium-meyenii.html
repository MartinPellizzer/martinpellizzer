Lepidium meyenii

Lepidium meyenii, commonly known as maca, is a tuberous plant native to the high Andes of Peru.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its dense, branched root system that produces edible tubers. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soil. Its tubers are rich in nutrients, including carbohydrates, proteins, and various bioactive compounds.

Maca is widely cultivated for its potential health benefits and use in traditional medicine.
