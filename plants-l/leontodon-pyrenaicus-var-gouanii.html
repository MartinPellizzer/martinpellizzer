Leontodon pyrenaicus var. gouanii

Leontodon pyrenaicus var. gouanii, commonly known as the Pyrenean dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and basal rosette of leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils at elevations ranging from 1,000 to 2,500 meters. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variety is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its resilience to alpine environmental conditions.
