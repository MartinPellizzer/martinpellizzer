Lippia tegulifera var. parvifolia

Lippia tegulifera var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved lippia, is a flowering plant native to southern Mexico and Central America.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, small, opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers, which are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Due to its ornamental value and aromatic properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
