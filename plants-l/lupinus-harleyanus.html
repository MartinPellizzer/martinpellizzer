Lupinus harleyanus

Lupinus harleyanus, commonly known as the California lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth and clusters of blue or purple flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. Lupinus harleyanus thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland grasslands.

It plays an important role in nitrogen fixation, enriching the soil for other plants.
