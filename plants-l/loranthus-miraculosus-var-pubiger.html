Loranthus miraculosus var. pubiger

Loranthus miraculosus var. pubiger, commonly known as the miraculous mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.

It lacks true roots and absorbs water and nutrients through its haustoria, which penetrate the host's vascular system. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers, followed by fleshy, berry-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its leaves are usually small, elliptical, and often have a waxy coating.

This species is notable for its unique ecological role and its ability to thrive in shaded, humid environments.
