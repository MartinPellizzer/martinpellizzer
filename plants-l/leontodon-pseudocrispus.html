Leontodon pseudocrispus

Leontodon pseudocrispus, commonly known as the false hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are linear to lanceolate, often with a rough texture, and it produces yellow flowers from late spring to early autumn.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
