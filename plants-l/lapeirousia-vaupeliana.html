Lapeirousia vaupeliana

Lapeirousia vaupeliana, commonly known as the Orange River cycad, is a distinctive cycad species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its stout, unbranched stem and large, pinnate leaves that can grow up to 2 meters in length. The plant produces cones rather than flowers, with male and female cones occurring on separate plants. It thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
