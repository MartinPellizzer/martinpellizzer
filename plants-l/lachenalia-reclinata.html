Lachenalia reclinata

Lachenalia reclinata, commonly known as the reclining lachenalia, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces clusters of pale yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and containers.
