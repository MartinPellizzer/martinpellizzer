Lavandula canariensis subsp. canariae

Lavandula canariensis subsp.

canariae, commonly known as the Canary Island lavender, is a perennial shrub native to the Canary Islands. It features aromatic, narrow linear leaves and produces spikes of pale purple to lilac-colored flowers.

The plant is well adapted to the arid and coastal climates of its native habitat. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for Mediterranean and subtropical gardens.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild essential oils.
