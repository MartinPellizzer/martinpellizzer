Lesemia coccinea

Lesemia coccinea, commonly known as the scarlet globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces vibrant red-orange flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its stems are often hairy. Lesemia coccinea thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a suitable candidate for xeriscaping.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
