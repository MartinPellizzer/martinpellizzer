Leucosyke brunnescens

Leucosyke brunnescens, commonly known as the brown leucosyke, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in grassy areas and along forest edges, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, pale yellow flowers are clustered in terminal spikes, blooming during the late summer to early autumn.

The species is noted for its resilience to drought and its role in supporting local insect populations.
