Ligeria digitaliflora

Ligeria digitaliflora, commonly known as the digitate ligeria, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, digitate (finger-like) leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
