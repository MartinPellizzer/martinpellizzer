Linaria hepaticifolia

Linaria hepaticifolia, commonly known as liverleaf toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of ovate, liver-shaped leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Linaria hepaticifolia is typically found in rocky or gravelly soils along coastal areas.

It is a perennial herb that is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
