Lepigonum depauperatum

Lepigonum depauperatum, commonly known as the poor man's bugloss, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine white or grayish hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a valuable addition to xeriscaped gardens.
