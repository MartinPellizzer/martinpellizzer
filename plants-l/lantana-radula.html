Lantana radula

Lantana radula, commonly known as the red lantana, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the verbena family (Verbenaceae) and is characterized by its clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to red. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are often hairy and have a serrated edge. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a common ornamental plant in warm climates.

Lantana radula is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
