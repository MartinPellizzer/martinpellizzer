Lindernia angustifolia

Lindernia angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf lindernia, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features slender, branching stems covered in soft, gray-green foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Lindernia angustifolia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
