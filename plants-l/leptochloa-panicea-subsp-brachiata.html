Leptochloa panicea subsp. brachiata

Leptochloa panicea subsp.

brachiata, commonly known as the slender panic grass, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions. It typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in sandy or well-drained soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
