Lobelia dealbata

Lobelia dealbata, commonly known as the white cardinal flower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Lobelia dealbata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and attracts pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.
