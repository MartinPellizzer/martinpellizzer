Litsea philippinensis

Litsea philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine laurier or Philippine litsea, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when mature, containing several seeds. It thrives in tropical rainforests and is often found in lowland to mid-elevation areas.

The tree is valued for its timber and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
