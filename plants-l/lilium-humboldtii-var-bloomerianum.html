Lilium humboldtii var. bloomerianum

Lilium humboldtii var. bloomerianum, commonly known as the Bloomer lily, is a striking bulbous plant native to the western United States.

It features tall, upright stems that bear large, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and red. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped, strap-like leaves that are typically green with a slight bluish tinge. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens but is also considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
