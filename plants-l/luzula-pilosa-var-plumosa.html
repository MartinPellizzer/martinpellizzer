Luzula pilosa var. plumosa

Luzula pilosa var. plumosa, commonly known as the silky wood sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and dense, tufted foliage that gives it a soft, feathery appearance. Its leaves are typically narrow and hairy, contributing to its common name.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and woodland edges.
