Lupinus densiflorus var. latidens

Lupinus densiflorus var. latidens, commonly known as the broad-flowered lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, spike-like inflorescence composed of showy, blue to purple pea-shaped flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Lupinus densiflorus var. latidens plays a role in nitrogen fixation and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
