Linaria dealbata

Linaria dealbata, commonly known as the silverweed or white-toothed flax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces distinctive, finely toothed leaves that are often covered in a silvery or grayish pubescence. Linaria dealbata typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
