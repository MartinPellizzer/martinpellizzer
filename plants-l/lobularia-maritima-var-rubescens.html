Lobularia maritima var. rubescens

Lobularia maritima var. rubescens, commonly known as the red-flowered sweet rocket, is a flowering plant native to coastal areas of Europe and North Africa.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along shorelines and is well adapted to salt-laden environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slight pubescence, while the stems are erect and branching.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
