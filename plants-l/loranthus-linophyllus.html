Loranthus linophyllus

Loranthus linophyllus, commonly known as the silky leaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to host trees, primarily in the genus Eucalyptus.

It belongs to the family Loranthaceae and is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and fleshy, sticky berries that contain seeds. The plant derives some of its nutrients from the host through specialized root structures called haustoria. It is native to Australia and is often found in forested areas.

Its ecological role includes providing food for birds and insects, contributing to the biodiversity of its habitat.
