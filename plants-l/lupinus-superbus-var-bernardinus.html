Lupinus superbus var. bernardinus

Lupinus superbus var. bernardinus, commonly known as the Bernard's lupine, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

Lupinus superbus var. bernardinus is known for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local pollinators.
