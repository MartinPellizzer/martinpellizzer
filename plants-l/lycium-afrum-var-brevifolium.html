Lycium afrum var. brevifolium

Lycium afrum var. brevifolium, commonly known as the short-leaved boxthorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny growth and small, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for humans and animals. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh conditions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in landscaping and for erosion control.
