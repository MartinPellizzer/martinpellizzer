Lobelia siphilitica f. hybrida

Lobelia siphilitica f. hybrida, commonly known as the hybrid blue cardinal.

This cultivar is a hybrid form of the blue cardinal flower, characterized by its striking cobalt-blue to violet flowers. It is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States, typically growing in moist, shaded environments. The plant features opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like butterflies and hummingbirds.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial to full shade.
