Lycoctonum gigas var. tatewakii

Lycoctonum gigas var. tatewakii, commonly known as the Tatewakii bellflower, is a rare and striking species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, showy blue or purple bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in specialty gardens.
