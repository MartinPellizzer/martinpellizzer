Lonicera saccata var. tangiana

Lonicera saccata var. tangiana, commonly known as the Tangian honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are often covered in fine, reddish hairs. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its showy floral display.

The species is also noted for its adaptability to a variety of soil types and its ability to thrive in full sun to partial shade.
