Luzula nivea var. rubella

Luzula nivea var. rubella, commonly known as the red-flowered wood rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves that are typically glaucous or bluish-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that are often tinged with red, giving it its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Luzula nivea var. rubella is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
