Lycoseris squarrosa

Lycoseris squarrosa, commonly known as the hairy lycoperd, is a species of fungus that belongs to the family Lycoperdaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spore-bearing structure known as a volva, which is often covered in a dense layer of hairs. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas, such as grasslands and disturbed soils, and is commonly found in temperate regions. It plays a role in the decomposition of organic matter and contributes to nutrient cycling in its ecosystem.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological function, it is of interest to mycologists and ecological researchers.
