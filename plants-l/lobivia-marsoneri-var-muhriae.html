Lobivia marsoneri var. muhriae

Lobivia marsoneri var. muhriae, commonly known as the Muhriae Lobivia, is a compact, low-growing cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It features a short, cylindrical stem that is typically green or bluish-green in color, with a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a delicate touch to its otherwise rugged appearance. It has thick, fleshy leaves that are modified into spines, which help protect it from herbivores and reduce transpiration.

This variety is well-suited for rock gardens and dry landscapes, requiring minimal water and full sun exposure.
