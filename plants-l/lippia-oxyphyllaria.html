Lippia oxyphyllaria

Lippia oxyphyllaria, commonly known as the narrow-leaf lippia, is a perennial shrub native to the warm regions of South America.

It belongs to the Mint family (Lamiaceae), which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in open woodlands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly serrated margin, and it produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters.

Lippia oxyphyllaria is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
