Lissochilus cucullatus

Lissochilus cucullatus, commonly known as the hooded orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to South America.

It is characterized by its unique, hood-shaped flower that gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in mountainous regions. Its flowers are usually small and come in a variety of colors, including white, yellow, and green.

Lissochilus cucullatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
