Lycopodium erectum

Lycopodium erectum, commonly known as the upright clubmoss, is a small, evergreen plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, moss-like appearance with numerous small, needle-like leaves arranged in whorls around its stem. The plant produces spore-bearing structures called strobila, which are clustered at the tips of the branches. Lycopodium erectum typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or rocky crevices.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and small invertebrates.
