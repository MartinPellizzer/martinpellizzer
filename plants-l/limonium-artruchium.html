Limonium artruchium

Limonium artruchium, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers and its ability to thrive in coastal and saline environments. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder, which helps reduce water loss. Limonium artruchium is a perennial species that blooms in summer, producing clusters of small, showy flowers.

It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its hardiness and visual appeal.
