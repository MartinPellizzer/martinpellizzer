Lepanthes angulata

Lepanthes angulata, commonly known as the angular lady's slipper, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its unique, pouch-like flower that is typically yellow or green with intricate patterns. The plant grows in humid, shaded environments, often in the understory of forests. Its leaves are typically elongated and dark green, contributing to its overall ornamental appeal.

Lepanthes angulata is valued for its beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its striking floral display.
