Lycopodium sydowiorum

Lycopodium sydowiorum, commonly known as the Sydow's clubmoss, is a small, evergreen fern-like plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its spore-bearing cones and needle-like leaves, which are typically less than 1 centimeter in length. This species is native to the tropical regions of Africa, particularly in countries like Kenya and Tanzania. Lycopodium sydowiorum thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories or on rocky outcrops.

Its reproductive structures are adapted for wind pollination, which is a key feature of its life cycle.
