Lolium felix

Lolium felix, commonly known as the golden grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and bright yellow inflorescences that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are narrow and blade-like, with a slightly hairy texture.

Lolium felix is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its vibrant color and ease of cultivation.
