Lupinus Micranthus Var. Pachylobus

Lupinus micranthus var. pachylobus is a striking member of the legume family known for its vibrant blue and purple flowers. This variety is native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping. The plant's unique thick, rounded leaves and showy blooms make it a standout in any garden. Its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil also makes it an ecologically valuable addition to the landscape.
