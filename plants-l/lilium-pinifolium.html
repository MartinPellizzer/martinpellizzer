Lilium pinifolium

Lilium pinifolium, commonly known as the pineleaf lily, is a species of flowering plant in the Liliaceae family.

It is native to the western United States and is characterized by its narrow, pine-like leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and woodlands. It produces bright red berries that are edible for birds and small mammals.

Lilium pinifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
