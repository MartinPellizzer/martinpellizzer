Lycoris longituba var. flava

Lycoris longituba var. flava, commonly known as the yellow spider lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China.

It features striking yellow flowers that bloom in the fall, often with a subtle red or purple hue at the base. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically evergreen, and it produces red berries that contain seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

This variety is closely related to other lycoris species and is valued for its ornamental beauty and cultural significance.
