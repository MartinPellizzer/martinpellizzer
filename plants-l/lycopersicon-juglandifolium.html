Lycopersicon juglandifolium

Lycopersicon juglandifolium, commonly known as the walnut-leaf tomato, is a species of wild tomato native to Central America.

It is characterized by its large, lobed leaves that resemble the shape of a walnut leaf, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into red, cherry-sized fruits. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded areas.

Due to its unique appearance and genetic diversity, it is of interest to botanists and plant breeders.
