Loranthus sandersonii

Loranthus sandersonii, commonly known as the sanderson's mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily in the genus Eucalyptus.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while it provides some benefits through nitrogen fixation. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers and red, fleshy berries that are attractive to birds. Its life cycle involves both asexual reproduction through vegetative growth and sexual reproduction via seeds dispersed by birds.

Loranthus sandersonii is an important component of the ecological dynamics in Australian forests.
