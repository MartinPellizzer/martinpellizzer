Linguella dilatata

Linguella dilatata, commonly known as the broad-leafed tongue plant, is a unique species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, ribbon-like leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom during the wet season. Its roots are fibrous and have been traditionally used in local medicine for their purported healing properties.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in the undergrowth of dense forests.
