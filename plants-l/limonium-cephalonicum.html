Limonium cephalonicum

Limonium cephalonicum, commonly known as the Greek sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, showcasing its adaptability to saline environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Limonium cephalonicum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained conditions.
