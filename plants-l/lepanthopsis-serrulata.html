Lepanthopsis serrulata

Lepanthopsis serrulata, commonly known as the serrated lepanthopsis, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its sympodial growth habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers with a distinctive coloration. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments within tropical forests. Its leaves are usually elliptical and covered in a fine layer of pubescence.

Lepanthopsis serrulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
