Linnaea longiflora

Linnaea longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered twinflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the temperate regions of North America.

It is characterized by its opposite, elliptic leaves that are often hairy and have a velvety texture. The plant produces small, fragrant, white flowers that bloom in pairs, typically in early spring. Its flowers have five petals and are arranged in a unique, clustered pattern.

Linnaea longiflora thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
