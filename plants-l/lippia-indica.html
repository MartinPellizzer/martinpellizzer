Lippia indica

Lippia indica, commonly known as Mexican mint or honey mint, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, slightly minty scent. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale purple, arranged in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Lippia indica is also popular in culinary applications, particularly in teas and herbal infusions.
