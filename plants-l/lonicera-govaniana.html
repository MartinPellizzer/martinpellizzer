Lonicera govaniana

Lonicera govaniana, commonly known as the Goianinha honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to yellow, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a glossy appearance.

The fruit is a small, red berry that ripens in the fall and is edible for both humans and wildlife.
