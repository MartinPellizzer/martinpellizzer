Loranthus umbelliflorus

Loranthus umbelliflorus, commonly known as the umbrellaflower mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees.

It belongs to the family Loranthaceae and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, white or greenish flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are often consumed by birds, which then disperse its seeds. It is native to parts of Africa and is often found growing on trees such as acacia and eucalyptus.

Its parasitic nature allows it to derive water and nutrients from its host, while still performing photosynthesis.
