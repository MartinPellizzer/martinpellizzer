Lonicera douglasii var. dasygyna

Lonicera douglasii var. dasygyna, commonly known as the Douglas honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly rough texture. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are usually yellow or orange, appearing in clusters during the spring. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that ripens in late summer and is an important food source for birds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
