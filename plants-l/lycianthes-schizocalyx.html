Lycianthes schizocalyx

Lycianthes schizocalyx, commonly known as the Mexican nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and broad, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange to red berries containing several seeds. Its botanical characteristics include a pubescent surface and a distinctive calyx that splits at maturity, giving it its scientific name.

It is often found in disturbed habitats and is part of the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades.
