Linum plotzii

Linum plotzii, commonly known as the desert flax, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pale blue or white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is adapted to survive in hot, dry environments with minimal water. Linum plotzii is a member of the Linaceae family, which includes other flax species.

Its seeds are sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil.
