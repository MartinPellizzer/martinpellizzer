Litsea velutina

Litsea velutina, commonly known as velvet tamarind, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark brown bark and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, orange-red drupes that contain a single seed. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its leaves and fruits have been used in traditional medicine and as a source of food in some regions.
