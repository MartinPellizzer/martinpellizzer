Lupinus nanus f. lilacinus

Lupinus nanus f. lilacinus, commonly known as the lilac-flowered dwarf lupine, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features small, clustered blue to violet flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves with a distinctive hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
