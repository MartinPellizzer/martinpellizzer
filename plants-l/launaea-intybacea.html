Launaea intybacea

Launaea intybacea, commonly known as the desert spinach, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in dry environments. The plant produces small yellow flowers arranged in composite heads, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines for their mild, spinach-like flavor.

Launaea intybacea is also valued for its ability to prevent soil erosion in harsh climates.
