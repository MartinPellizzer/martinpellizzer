Lobelia amoena var. obtusata

Lobelia amoena var. obtusata, commonly known as the broad-leaved lobelia, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are broad at the base and hairy on the underside. The plant produces clusters of tubular, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
