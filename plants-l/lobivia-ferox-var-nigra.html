Lobivia ferox var. nigra

Lobivia ferox var. nigra, commonly known as the Black Lobivia, is a succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It features a compact, barrel-shaped stem with a dark, almost black, velvety surface. The plant produces clusters of small, spiky, white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native desert environment.

It is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts for its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
