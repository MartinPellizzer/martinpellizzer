Lisianthius sempervirens

Lisianthius sempervirens, commonly known as the evergreen lisianthus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring narrow, grass-like leaves and showy blue to purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and ability to attract pollinators.
