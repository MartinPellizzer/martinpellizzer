Leonurus cardiaca var. canescens

Leonurus cardiaca var. canescens, commonly known as the hairy heartwort, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant features hairy, ovate leaves and produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitats.
