Lycopus europaeus var. subpinnatifidus

Lycopus europaeus var. subpinnatifidus, commonly known as European water horehound, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers are small, blue to purple, tubular blooms that appear in clusters. The plant thrives in moist environments such as wet meadows, stream banks, and marshes. It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and is often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Its botanical structure supports its role in wetland ecosystems and its historical medicinal uses.
