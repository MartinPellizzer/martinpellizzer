Lythrum salicaria var. glabricaule

Lythrum salicaria var. glabricaule, commonly known as narrow-leaved purple loosestrife, is a variant of the purple loosestrife plant native to Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and tall spikes of purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist soils along stream banks, wetlands, and other aquatic habitats. It is a perennial herb that can spread aggressively through rhizomes, making it an invasive species in some regions.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically significant.
