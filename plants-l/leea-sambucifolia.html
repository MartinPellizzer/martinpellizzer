Leea sambucifolia

Leea sambucifolia, commonly known as the sambucus-leaved leea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Leeaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that resemble those of the genus Sambucus. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
