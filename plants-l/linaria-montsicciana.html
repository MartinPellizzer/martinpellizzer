Linaria montsicciana

Linaria montsicciana, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and vibrant yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its flowers are tubular in shape, with two lips, and are designed to attract specific pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Linaria montsicciana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
