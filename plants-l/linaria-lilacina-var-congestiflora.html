Linaria lilacina var. congestiflora

Linaria lilacina var. congestiflora, commonly known as the clustered toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, lilac-colored flowers. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its prolonged blooming period, typically from spring to early summer.
