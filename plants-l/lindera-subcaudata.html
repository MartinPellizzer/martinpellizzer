Lindera subcaudata

Lindera subcaudata, commonly known as the narrow-tailed spicebush, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and avocado trees. The plant features opposite, elliptical leaves with a serrated margin and a distinctive narrow, tapering tip. Its fragrant flowers are yellow-green and appear in clusters, followed by small, dark purple berries.

Lindera subcaudata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and stream banks.
