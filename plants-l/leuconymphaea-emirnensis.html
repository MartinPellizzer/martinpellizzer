Leuconymphaea emirnensis

Leuconymphaea emirnensis, commonly known as the Emirnensis water lily, is a rare aquatic plant native to the freshwater ecosystems of the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves that are typically green with a waxy surface, adapted for buoyancy and light absorption. The plant produces showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom above the water surface, attracting pollinators such as bees and beetles. Its roots are anchored in the muddy substrate, enabling it to thrive in still or slow-moving water bodies.

Due to habitat loss and pollution, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
