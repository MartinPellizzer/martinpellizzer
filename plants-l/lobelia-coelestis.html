Lobelia coelestis

Lobelia coelestis, commonly known as blue lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, sky-blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are opposite and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

Lobelia coelestis is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to tolerate waterlogged conditions.
