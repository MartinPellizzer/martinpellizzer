Lycopsis sibthorpiana

Lycopsis sibthorpiana, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a member of the Apocynaceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant that typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. Its milky sap is a characteristic feature, which can be toxic to some animals.

Lycopsis sibthorpiana is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental qualities.
