Lysimachia lanceola

Lysimachia lanceola, commonly known as the narrowleaf chickweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. The plant has slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Lysimachia lanceola is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and flowers.
