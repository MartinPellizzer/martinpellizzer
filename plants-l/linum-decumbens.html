Linum decumbens

Linum decumbens, commonly known as creeping flax, is a low-growing perennial plant native to North America.

It produces delicate blue or white flowers and has slender, creeping stems that root at the nodes. The plant is characterized by its linear, grass-like leaves and is often found in moist, sandy soils. It is a member of the Linaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed or open habitats.

Linum decumbens is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in fiber production.
