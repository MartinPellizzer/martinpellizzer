Lathyrus palustris subsp. exalatus

Lathyrus palustris subsp.

exalatus, commonly known as the marsh vetch, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland habitats in North America. It features slender, ascending stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 60 cm in height.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, arranged in clusters along the stems. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with several pairs of oval-shaped leaflets.

This subspecies is adapted to moist soils and plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
