Lonicera orientalis subsp. kesselringii

Lonicera orientalis subsp.

kesselringii, commonly known as the Kesselring honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Europe. It features fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, typically in shades of yellow and orange.

The plant produces red berries that are edible for birds but not for humans. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and seasonal flowering.
