Lophopetalum wightianum

Lophopetalum wightianum, commonly known as the Wight's flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of bright orange to red flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking visual display to its foliage. The plant has pinnately compound leaves that are often used in traditional medicine in its native regions. Its fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that contains several seeds.

Lophopetalum wightianum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.
