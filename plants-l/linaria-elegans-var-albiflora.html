Linaria elegans var. albiflora

Linaria elegans var. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered toadflax, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, branching stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically yellow or orange with a distinctive bilateral symmetry. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often arranged in a whorled pattern along the stem.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
