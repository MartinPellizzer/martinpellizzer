Linanthus bigelovii var. jonesii

Linanthus bigelovii var. jonesii, commonly known as Jones' flaxvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are alternate and trifoliate, with small, rounded leaflets.

It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.
