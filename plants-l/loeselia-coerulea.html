Loeselia coerulea

Loeselia coerulea, commonly known as the bluebell of the south, is a flowering plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring season. The plant typically grows in open woodland and grassland habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Loeselia coerulea is valued not only for its beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
