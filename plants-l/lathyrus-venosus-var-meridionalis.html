Lathyrus venosus var. meridionalis

Lathyrus venosus var. meridionalis, commonly known as the southern vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It features climbing stems with trifoliate leaves composed of three ovate to lanceolate leaflets. The plant produces showy, pea-like flowers in shades of violet or purple, typically blooming in spring. Its small, elongated pods contain several seeds, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

It is often found in rocky or disturbed habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and nitrogen-fixing ability.
