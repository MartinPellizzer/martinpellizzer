Litsea tetranthera

Litsea tetranthera, commonly known as the fragrant lauraceous tree, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, elliptical leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits containing numerous seeds. Its essential oils are valued for their medicinal and aromatic properties.

Litsea tetranthera thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.
