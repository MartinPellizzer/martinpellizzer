Linaria sapphirina var. incarnata

Linaria sapphirina var. incarnata, commonly known as the Sapphire Flax or Blue Flax, is a striking annual plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branching stems covered in vibrant blue to violet tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and long blooming period from spring to early summer add color to gardens throughout the growing season.
