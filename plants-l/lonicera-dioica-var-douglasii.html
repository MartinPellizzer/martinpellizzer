Lonicera dioica var. douglasii

Lonicera dioica var. douglasii, commonly known as Douglas honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to western North America.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically hairy on the underside. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are yellowish-white with orange stamens, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its berries are red or orange, turning dark when mature, and are an important food source for birds.

This species is often found in moist, open habitats such as streambanks and meadows.
