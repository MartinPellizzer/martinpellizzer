Ligustrum japonicum var. spathulatum

Ligustrum japonicum var. spathulatum, commonly known as Japanese privet, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter in color underneath. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, which are followed by dark blue berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.

However, it can become invasive in some regions if not managed properly.
