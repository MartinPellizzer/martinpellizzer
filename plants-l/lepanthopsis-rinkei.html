Lepanthopsis rinkei

Lepanthopsis rinkei, commonly known as the Rink's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, brightly colored flowers that typically bloom in summer. The plant features long, slender stems with clustered leaves and produces fragrant, showy blossoms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Lepanthopsis rinkei thrives in warm, humid environments with well-draining soil and partial shade.

Its unique floral structure and vibrant appearance make it a prized addition to botanical collections and gardens.
