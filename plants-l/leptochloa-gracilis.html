Leptochloa gracilis

Leptochloa gracilis, commonly known as slender grass or slender flat sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded areas, such as marshes and wetlands. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in Southeast Asia.

Leptochloa gracilis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
