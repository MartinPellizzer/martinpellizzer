Lobelia goldmanii

Lobelia goldmanii, commonly known as the golden lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, branching stems covered in small, lance-shaped leaves. Its most striking characteristic is its clusters of bright yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

Lobelia goldmanii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
