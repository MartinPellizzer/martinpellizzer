Lonicera rupicola var. rupicola

Lonicera rupicola var. rupicola, commonly known as the rock honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, elliptic leaves with a rough texture and a distinctive yellow-green hue. The plant produces small, fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that ripens in the fall.

This species is characterized by its ability to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils and its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
