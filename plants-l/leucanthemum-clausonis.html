Leucanthemum clausonis

Leucanthemum clausonis, commonly known as the alpine chamomile, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. It is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.

Leucanthemum clausonis is a hardy perennial that blooms in late summer and early autumn.
