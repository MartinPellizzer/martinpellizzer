Lupinus × regalis

Lupinus × regalis, commonly known as the regal lupine, is a hybrid species within the legume family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and striking clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces elongated pods that contain several seeds. Native to the western United States, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Lupinus × regalis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil health.
