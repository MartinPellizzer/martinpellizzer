Lophocereus schottii f. mieckleyanus

Lophocereus schottii f. mieckleyanus, commonly known as the Mieckley's prickly pear, is a unique cactus species native to Mexico.

It is a low-growing, columnar cactus with a distinctive appearance, featuring a green body covered in clusters of spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in xeriscapes and rock gardens.
