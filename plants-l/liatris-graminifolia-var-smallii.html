Liatris graminifolia var. smallii

Liatris graminifolia var. smallii, commonly known as the narrowleaf blazingstar, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces showy, spiky flower heads composed of numerous small, tubular flowers that range in color from pale purple to pink. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
