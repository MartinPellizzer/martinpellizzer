Linaria aegyptiaca var. heterophylla

Linaria aegyptiaca var. heterophylla, commonly known as the Egyptian toadflax, is a perennial herb native to arid regions of North Africa and the Middle East.

It features slender, branched stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, often with a trailing or ascending habit. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers in racemes, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are typically linear or lanceolate, varying in shape depending on the growth conditions.

It is adapted to drought and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.
