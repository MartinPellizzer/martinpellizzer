Lonicera bulbalina

Lonicera bulbalina, commonly known as the fragrant honeysuckle, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range from white to yellow in color. The plant is known for its sweet, nectar-rich blossoms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a small, red berry that ripens in late summer.

Lonicera bulbalina is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
