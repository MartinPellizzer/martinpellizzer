Lomatium orientale

Lomatium orientale, commonly known as the oriental lomatium, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the family. Its root system is thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Lomatium orientale thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
