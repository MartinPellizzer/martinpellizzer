Leptogramma mollissima monstr. cristata

Leptogramma mollissima monstr.

cristata, commonly known as the crested moss fern, is a striking epiphytic plant native to tropical regions of South America. It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage that gives it a soft, moss-like appearance.

The plant features a rosette-shaped growth pattern with a distinctive, elongated crest that adds to its ornamental appeal. Its leaves are typically dark green and covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its lush texture.

This species thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its unique visual characteristics in botanical gardens and collections.
