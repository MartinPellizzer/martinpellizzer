Lactuca

Lactuca, commonly known as lettuce, is a genus of flowering plants in the daisy family, Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its edible leaves, which are widely consumed in various cuisines around the world. The plants typically have a rosette growth habit, with broad, fleshy leaves that vary in color from light green to dark green. Many species within the genus are cultivated for their crisp, refreshing leaves, while others are used in traditional medicine.

Lactuca species are native to temperate regions of Europe, Asia, and North America.
