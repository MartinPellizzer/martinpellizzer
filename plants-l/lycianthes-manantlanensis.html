Lycianthes manantlanensis

Lycianthes manantlanensis, commonly known as the Manantlan nightshade, is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, peppers, and potatoes.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and research settings.
