Luma nannophylla

Luma nannophylla, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a shrub or small tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It has small, elliptical leaves that are typically less than 2 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that contains a single seed.

Luma nannophylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other regions, where it can become invasive.
