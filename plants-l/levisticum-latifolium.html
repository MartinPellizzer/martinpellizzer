Levisticum latifolium

Levisticum latifolium, commonly known as broad-leaved parsley, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like carrots and dill. The plant is characterized by its broad, flat leaves and small yellow flowers that appear in umbels. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
