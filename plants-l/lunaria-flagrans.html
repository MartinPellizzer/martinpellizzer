Lunaria flagrans

Lunaria flagrans, commonly known as the spreading moonflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant is characterized by its delicate, pale blue or white flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Lunaria flagrans is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
