Luma expallens

Luma expallens, commonly known as the Christmasberry, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces bright orange-red berries that are edible and often consumed by birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Luma expallens is valued for its ornamental berries and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
