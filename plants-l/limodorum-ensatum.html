Limodorum ensatum

Limodorum ensatum, commonly known as the golden tongue orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that resemble a golden tongue. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters.

Limodorum ensatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
