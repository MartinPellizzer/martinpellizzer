Luisia truncata

Luisia truncata, commonly known as the cutleaf luisia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, truncate (cut-off) leaf shapes. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its showy, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, attracting pollinators such as butterflies and bees.

Luisia truncata is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.
