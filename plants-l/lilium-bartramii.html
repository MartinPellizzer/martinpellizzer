Lilium bartramii

Lilium bartramii, commonly known as the Bartram's lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial with showy, fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has strap-like, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland habitats.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, contributing to its ecological significance.
