Launaea kuriensis

Launaea kuriensis, commonly known as the Mexican dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a basal rosette of leaves and produces yellow flowers that develop into fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Launaea kuriensis is adapted to withstand drought conditions and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
