Lasiandra lutescens

Lasiandra lutescens, commonly known as the yellow mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Lasiandra lutescens thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
