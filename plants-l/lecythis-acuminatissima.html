Lecythis acuminatissima

Lecythis acuminatissima, commonly known as the Amazonian wax palm, is a large tropical tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, palm-like leaves that can reach up to three meters in length. The tree produces yellowish-green flowers that develop into woody, spiny fruits containing edible seeds. Its latex, which is highly viscous, has been used by indigenous communities for various purposes, including as a natural adhesive.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing food and shelter for numerous species of wildlife.
