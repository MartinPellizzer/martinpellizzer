Leptocarpaea loeselii

Leptocarpaea loeselii, commonly known as the Cape Verde morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Cape Verde Islands.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces striking blue or violet flowers with a distinctive spiral pattern on the petals. The plant has slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to the arid conditions of its native habitat.

Leptocarpaea loeselii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in tropical and subtropical regions.
