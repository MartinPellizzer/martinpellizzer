Lasiorrhiza candidissima

Lasiorrhiza candidissima, commonly known as the white-flowered larkspur, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers. The plant has palmate leaves with three to five lobes, and its root system consists of fibrous roots that help anchor it in rocky or dry soils. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in meadows and open woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
