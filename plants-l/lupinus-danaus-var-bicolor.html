Lupinus danaus var. bicolor

Lupinus danaus var. bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking bi-colored flowers, typically featuring a mix of purple and white. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces elongated seed pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed sites.

Lupinus danaus var. bicolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in nitrogen fixation, benefiting soil fertility.
