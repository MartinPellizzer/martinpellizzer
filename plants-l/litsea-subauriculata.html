Litsea subauriculata

Litsea subauriculata, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers that appear in clusters, giving it its common name. Its leaves are usually elliptical with a glossy surface and a distinctive auriculate base.

Litsea subauriculata is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.
