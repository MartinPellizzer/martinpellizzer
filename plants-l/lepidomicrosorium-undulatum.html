Lepidomicrosorium undulatum

Lepidomicrosorium undulatum, commonly known as the undulating moss, is a species of moss belonging to the family Microsoridae.

It is characterized by its delicate, branching structure and small, oval-shaped leaves that are often slightly undulating in appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in temperate regions where humidity is consistently high. Its reproductive structures, known as sporophytes, are relatively simple and consist of a capsule that produces spores for propagation.

Lepidomicrosorium undulatum plays a vital role in soil stabilization and moisture retention in its natural habitat.
