Lycopus americanus var. laurentianus

Lycopus americanus var. laurentianus, commonly known as American blueweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist environments such as wet meadows, stream banks, and marshes. The plant features opposite, hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, blue to purple flowers in clusters. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, and it is often found in the northeastern United States and southeastern Canada.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has historical use in traditional medicine.
