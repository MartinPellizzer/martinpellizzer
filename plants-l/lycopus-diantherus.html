Lycopus diantherus

Lycopus diantherus, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant typically grows in moist habitats such as meadows, wetlands, and stream sides. It produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.
