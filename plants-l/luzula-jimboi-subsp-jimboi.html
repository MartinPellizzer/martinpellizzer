Luzula jimboi subsp. jimboi

Luzula jimboi subsp.

jimboi, commonly known as the Jimbo sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia. It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest floors and stream banks.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often dark green and densely packed. Its flowering stems produce small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brown flowers.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
