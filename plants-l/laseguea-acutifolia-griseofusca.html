Laseguea acutifolia griseofusca

Laseguea acutifolia griseofusca, commonly known as the gray-leaved laseguea, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, grayish pubescence. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
