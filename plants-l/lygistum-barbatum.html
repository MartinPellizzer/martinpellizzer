Lygistum barbatum

Lygistum barbatum, commonly known as the Chinese lantern plant, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive red, inflated bracts that resemble small lanterns, which are often used in ornamental gardening. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and produces small yellow flowers followed by berries. Its leaves are ovate and have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are woody and branched.

Lygistum barbatum is often cultivated for its vibrant, showy bracts that add visual interest to gardens during the fall season.
