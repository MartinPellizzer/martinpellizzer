Lonicera lanata

Lonicera lanata, commonly known as the hairy honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its scientific name. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. These flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Lonicera lanata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in a variety of soil conditions.
