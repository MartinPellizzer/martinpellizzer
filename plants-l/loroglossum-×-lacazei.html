Loroglossum × lacazei

Loroglossum × lacazei, commonly known as the hybrid lantana, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered inflorescences that range in color from white to red. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with opposite, ovate leaves that have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
