Loudonia aurea var. angustifolia

Loudonia aurea var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved goldcup, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in wetlands. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in late spring. Its botanical structure includes a fibrous root system and a branched stem.

It is often found in wetland ecosystems and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role.
