Lavatera thuringiaca subsp. cachemiriana

Lavatera thuringiaca subsp.

cachemiriana, commonly known as Kashmir lavender, is a shrub native to the Himalayan regions of Pakistan and India. It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and showy, pink to white flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has a woody stem and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens in temperate climates.
