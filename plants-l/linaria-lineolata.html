Linaria lineolata

Linaria lineolata, commonly known as the narrowleaf toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features slender, upright stems and produces bright yellow, spurred flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and linear, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Linaria lineolata is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and gravelly soils.
