Lippia tegulifera var. ovata

Lippia tegulifera var. ovata, commonly known as the broadleaf lippia, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub characterized by its aromatic, narrow to broad leaves and small, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and ethnobotanical contexts.
