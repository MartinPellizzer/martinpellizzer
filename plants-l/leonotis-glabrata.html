Leonotis glabrata

Leonotis glabrata, commonly known as the Blanketflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It typically grows as an upright shrub with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves.

Leonotis glabrata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
