Lycopodioides douglasii

Lycopodioides douglasii, commonly known as Douglas's clubmoss, is a species of lycophyte native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its small, spore-bearing cones and a creeping, rhizomatous growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are linear and often arranged in whorls around the stem.

Lycopodioides douglasii plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
