Liparis ochrantha

Liparis ochrantha, commonly known as the orange orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers, which are typically borne on tall, erect stems. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces numerous small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Liparis ochrantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
