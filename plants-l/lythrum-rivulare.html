Lythrum rivulare

Lythrum rivulare, commonly known as narrow-leaved lythrum, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lythraceae family, which includes plants such as the poinsettia and the American beautyberry. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks. Its slender, upright stems are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces spikes of pale pink to purple flowers.

The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly toothed margin.
