Loasa Ambrosiifolia Var. Hispida

Loasa ambrosiifolia var. hispida is a unique and intriguing plant native to the mountainous regions of South America. This variant is known for its distinctive appearance, featuring hairy leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. It thrives in cooler climates and requires well-drained soil to flourish. Despite its ornamental value, it is often overlooked in favor of more common garden plants. Its resilience and adaptability make it a fascinating subject for botanical study and cultivation.
