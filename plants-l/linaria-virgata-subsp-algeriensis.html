Linaria virgata subsp. algeriensis

Linaria virgata subsp.

algeriensis, commonly known as Algerian toadflax, is a flowering plant native to North Africa. It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and clustered flowers.

The plant produces showy, yellow-orange to red-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as slopes and grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
