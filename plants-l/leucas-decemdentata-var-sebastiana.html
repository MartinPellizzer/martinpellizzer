Leucas decemdentata var. sebastiana

Leucas decemdentata var. sebastiana, commonly known as the white leucas, is a flowering plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, white or pale purple flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

It is often found in grasslands and along rocky outcrops, thriving in well-drained soils.
