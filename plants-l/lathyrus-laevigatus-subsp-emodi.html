Lathyrus laevigatus subsp. emodi

Lathyrus laevigatus subsp.

emodi, commonly known as the Himalayan vetch, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

The plant produces clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it has a fibrous root system.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
