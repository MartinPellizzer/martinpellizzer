Loranthus eucalyptifolius

Loranthus eucalyptifolius, commonly known as the eucalyptus mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of eucalyptus trees.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own carbohydrates through photosynthesis using its leaves. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves and produces white or yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that releases tiny seeds, which can adhere to the bark of host trees.

This species is native to Australia and is often found in regions with warm, dry climates.
