Lauro-cerasus samydoides

Lauro-cerasus samydoides, commonly known as the Japanese apricot, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Prunus genus and is characterized by its fragrant, white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, yellow or orange fruits that are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice in gardens and landscapes.
