Lactuca multifida

Lactuca multifida, commonly known as the broad-leaf lettuce, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that are often hairy and have a mild, slightly bitter taste. The plant produces small, dandelion-like yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is native to temperate regions of Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

Lactuca multifida is often grown as a leafy vegetable and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
