Lobelia poetica

Lobelia poetica, commonly known as the poet's flower, is a herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

Lobelia poetica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
