Lycopodium sintenisii

Lycopodium sintenisii, commonly known as the Sintenis' clubmoss, is a small, evergreen plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing form and its distinctive, spirally arranged leaves. The plant produces spore-bearing cones, known as strobili, which are typically found at the tips of its stems. Lycopodium sintenisii thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
