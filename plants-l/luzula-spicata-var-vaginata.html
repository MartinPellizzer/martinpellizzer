Luzula spicata var. vaginata

Luzula spicata var. vaginata, commonly known as the velvet grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often bear a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically greenish or reddish in color, with inconspicuous flowers. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often have a sheathing base, contributing to its ornamental appeal in gardens.

This variety is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and thrives in moist, shaded environments.
