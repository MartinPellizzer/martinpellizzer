Lupinus burkei subsp. caeruleomontanus

Lupinus burkei subsp.

caeruleomontanus, commonly known as the Sierra blue lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States. It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and finely divided, fern-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky soils and open habitats, such as meadows and forest edges. It has a robust root system that helps prevent soil erosion.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
