Lahaya latifolia

Lahaya latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its flowers bloom throughout the growing season, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
