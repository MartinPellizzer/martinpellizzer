Lilium yoshidae

Lilium yoshidae, commonly known as the Yoshida lily, is a species of flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stem and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer, producing vibrant pink or red petals that attract pollinators. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it thrives in well-drained, fertile soil.

Lilium yoshidae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
