Lilium maculatum var. maculatum

Lilium maculatum var. maculatum, commonly known as the spotted lily, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that typically feature a white or pale pink color with distinctive red or orange spots on the outer petals. The plant grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with strap-like, grassy leaves that are often bluish-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

The bulbs are perennial, and the plant is known for its showy blooms that appear in late spring to early summer.
