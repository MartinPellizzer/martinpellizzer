Licuala aurantiaca

Licuala aurantiaca, commonly known as the red-veined areca palm, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves with prominent red veins that contrast sharply with its dark green foliage. The plant typically grows as a solitary tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters, and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its trunk is smooth and slender, with a fibrous sheath that peels away as the plant matures.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to flourish.
