Linaria rossmaessleri

Linaria rossmaessleri, commonly known as the Rossmaessler's toadflax, is a perennial flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spurred flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Linaria rossmaessleri is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
