Lilium chalcedonicum subsp. gracile

Lilium chalcedonicum subsp.

gracile, commonly known as the slender tawny lily, is a subspecies native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Turkey. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of pale yellow to orange-red flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or open woodland habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in two rows along the stem.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, showy blooms.
