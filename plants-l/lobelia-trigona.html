Lobelia trigona

Lobelia trigona, commonly known as the zigzag lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive zigzagging stems and clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are opposite and elliptical, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Lobelia trigona is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
