Lobelia sylvicola

Lobelia sylvicola, commonly known as the woodland lobelia, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments, often found in rich, loamy soils. The plant features small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, often blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Lobelia sylvicola plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies in its native habitat.
