Lepanthes venezuelensis

Lepanthes venezuelensis, commonly known as the Venezuelan orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America, particularly Venezuela.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid with a distinctive appearance, featuring delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as forest understories, where it relies on mycorrhizal associations for nutrient uptake. Its flowers are usually pale green or white, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Lepanthes venezuelensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated by enthusiasts of rare orchids.
