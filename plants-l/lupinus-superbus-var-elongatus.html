Lupinus superbus var. elongatus

Lupinus superbus var. elongatus, commonly known as the tall lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in open habitats.

Lupinus superbus var. elongatus plays an important role in nitrogen fixation, enriching the soil for other plants.
