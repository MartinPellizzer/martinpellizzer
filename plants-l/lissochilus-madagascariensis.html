Lissochilus madagascariensis

Lissochilus madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar slipper orchid, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in mountainous regions. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the forest floor, and it thrives in nutrient-rich soils.

The orchid is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
