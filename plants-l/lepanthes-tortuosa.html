Lepanthes tortuosa

Lepanthes tortuosa, commonly known as the spiral orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, spiral-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of white and green. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments, often epiphytic on tree trunks or branches. Its leaves are usually elongated and arranged in a spiral pattern, contributing to its unique appearance.

Lepanthes tortuosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
