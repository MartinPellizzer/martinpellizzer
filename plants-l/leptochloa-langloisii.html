Leptochloa langloisii

Leptochloa langloisii, commonly known as the narrowleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along waterways. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a smooth texture. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-pollinated flowers.

Leptochloa langloisii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a ground cover in suitable climates.
