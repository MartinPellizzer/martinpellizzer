Lonicera praecox

Lonicera praecox, commonly known as early honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are usually hairy on the underside. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to yellow, typically blooming in early spring. Its berries are red and edible, though they are not commonly consumed by humans.

Lonicera praecox is often found in woodlands and along stream banks, where it thrives in partial shade to full sun.
