Linaria polyclada

Linaria polyclada, commonly known as the clustered toadflax, is a perennial herb native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has erect, branching stems and small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It is often found in dry, rocky soils and can thrive in full sun.

Linaria polyclada is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
