Lupinus albus subsp. albus

Lupinus albus subsp.

albus, commonly known as white lupin, is a leguminous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of white to pale yellow flowers.

The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces elongated pods containing several seeds. It is well-known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria.

This subspecies is often cultivated as a forage crop and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
