Lycium scoparium var. affine

Lycium scoparium var. affine, commonly known as the silverleaf lycium, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features small, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine, reflective hairs, which help reduce water loss in dry environments. The plant produces yellowish-orange berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun exposure.

Its hardy nature makes it a valuable plant for xeriscaping and habitat restoration in desert ecosystems.
