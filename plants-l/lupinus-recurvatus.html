Lupinus recurvatus

Lupinus recurvatus, commonly known as the curlleaf lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. Lupinus recurvatus thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

It is a member of the legume family and has the ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
