Lecythis melliana

Lecythis melliana, commonly known as the Brazil nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its large, buttressed roots and broad, palm-like leaves. The tree produces large, woody fruits that contain multiple edible seeds, known as Brazil nuts. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange and are pollinated by specific insects.

The plant plays a vital role in the ecosystem, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
