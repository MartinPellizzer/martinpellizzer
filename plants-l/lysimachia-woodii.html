Lysimachia woodii

Lysimachia woodii, commonly known as the mountain sorrel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of yellow flowers throughout the summer. The plant is characterized by its creeping growth habit and ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments. Lysimachia woodii is often found in rocky or woodland soils, where it forms dense mats.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in difficult conditions.
