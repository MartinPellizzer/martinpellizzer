Lacinaria scariosa var. virgata

Lacinaria scariosa var. virgata, commonly known as the silverleaf lacinaria, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green leaves that are often lobed or divided, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland or rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
