Lacinaria gracilis

Lacinaria gracilis, commonly known as the slender lacinaria, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, slender stems and small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often submerged or floating on the water surface. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.

Lacinaria gracilis is typically found in slow-moving or still freshwater environments.
