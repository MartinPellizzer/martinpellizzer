Lastrea × boottii

Lastrea × boottii, commonly known as the Boott's lastrea, is a hybrid plant in the Asclepiadaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, succulent stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are usually covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces distinctive, showy flowers with a crown-like structure, often in shades of yellow or orange. It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it requires full sun and minimal water.
