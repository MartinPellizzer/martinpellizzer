Lupinus latifolius var. columbianus

Lupinus latifolius var. columbianus, commonly known as the Columbia lupine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has palmately compound leaves with several leaflets, and its root system includes nitrogen-fixing nodules. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open meadows and grasslands.

The Columbia lupine is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
