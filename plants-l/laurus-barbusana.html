Laurus barbusana

Laurus barbusana, commonly known as the barbusan laurel, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves that are typically oval in shape and have a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by dark blue-black berries. Laurus barbusana is valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental appeal.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile addition to gardens and landscapes.
