Luzula rufa

Luzula rufa, commonly known as red tussock sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and dense, reddish-brown flowering spikes that appear in late summer. Luzula rufa typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats.
