Lasiacis lucida

Lasiacis lucida, commonly known as the shining lichen, is a foliose lichen that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It features a dense, crust-like thallus with a bright, glossy surface that reflects light. The plant's structure consists of a symbiotic association between a fungus and green algae or cyanobacteria. It is often found on bark, rocks, or soil in temperate regions.

Due to its slow growth and sensitivity to environmental changes, it serves as an indicator species for air quality and ecosystem health.
