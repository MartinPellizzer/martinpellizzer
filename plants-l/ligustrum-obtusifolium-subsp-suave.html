Ligustrum obtusifolium subsp. suave

Ligustrum obtusifolium subsp.

suave, commonly known as sweet privet, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States. It features opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are typically glossy and dark green.

The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters, which are followed by black, fleshy berries. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often used as an ornamental plant due to its dense growth habit.

Its botanical characteristics make it adaptable to different environmental conditions.
