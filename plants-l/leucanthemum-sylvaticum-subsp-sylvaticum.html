Leucanthemum sylvaticum subsp. sylvaticum

Leucanthemum sylvaticum subsp.

sylvaticum, commonly known as the woodland oxeye, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and along stream banks.

The plant features showy white to pale yellow flowers with a distinctive dark center, blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
