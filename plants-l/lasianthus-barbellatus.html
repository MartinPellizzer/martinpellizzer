Lasianthus barbellatus

Lasianthus barbellatus, commonly known as the hairy lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive hairy leaves and showy inflorescences. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its flowers are usually pink or red, with two lips, and are arranged in dense clusters.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
