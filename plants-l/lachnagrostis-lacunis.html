Lachnagrostis lacunis

Lachnagrostis lacunis, commonly known as the narrowleaf silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, silver-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscaping.
