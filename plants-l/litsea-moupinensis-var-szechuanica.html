Litsea moupinensis var. szechuanica

Litsea moupinensis var. szechuanica, commonly known as the Sichuan litsea, is a variant of the litsea genus native to China.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, woody drupe that contains a single seed.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
