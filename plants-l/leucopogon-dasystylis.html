Leucopogon dasystylis

Leucopogon dasystylis, commonly known as the broad-styled leucopogon, is a species of flowering plant in the heath family (Ericaceae).

It is native to Australia, particularly found in the southeastern regions, where it thrives in sandy soils and open habitats. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, which bloom during the spring and summer seasons. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, contributing to its adaptability in harsh environmental conditions.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic features.
