Liparis rheedei

Liparis rheedei, commonly known as the yellow lady's slipper, is a terrestrial orchid native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and grasslands. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with a rhizome that produces a single leaf and a flowering stem. Its flowers are distinctive, with a yellow pouch-shaped labellum and sepals that are often green or yellow.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
