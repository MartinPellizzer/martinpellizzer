Lapathum glomeratum

Lapathum glomeratum, commonly known as the clustered cow-wheat, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and clusters of small, pinkish to reddish-purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval to lance-shaped leaflets.

Lapathum glomeratum is known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for various pollinators.
