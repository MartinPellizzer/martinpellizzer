Lamiophlomis

Lamiophlomis, commonly known as Chinese valerian, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Lamiaceae.

These plants are native to Asia, particularly China, and are characterized by their square stems and opposite leaves. They typically produce small, tubular flowers that are often pink or purple in color. Lamiophlomis species are often found in grassy areas and rocky habitats, thriving in temperate climates.

The genus is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
