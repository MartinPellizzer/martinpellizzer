Leucadendron deasii

Leucadendron deasii, commonly known as the Deas's candle bush, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, cone-like inflorescences that range in color from yellow to orange, red, and purple. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically visited by birds and insects for nectar. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Leucadendron deasii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as part of native plant landscaping.
