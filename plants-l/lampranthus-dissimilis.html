Lampranthus dissimilis

Lampranthus dissimilis, commonly known as the firecracker flower, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and birds. The plant has fleshy, green to blue-green leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for xeriscaping and drought-tolerant landscaping.
