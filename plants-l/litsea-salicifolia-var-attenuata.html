Litsea salicifolia var. attenuata

Litsea salicifolia var. attenuata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved litsea, is a variant of the litsea species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub characterized by its elongated, lanceolate leaves that are often narrow and tapering. The leaves are typically glossy green on the upper surface and lighter in color on the underside, with a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed. It produces small, white to pale yellow flowers followed by oval-shaped, edible fruits that are favored by various wildlife species.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
