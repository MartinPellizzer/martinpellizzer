Liatris vittata

Liatris vittata, commonly known as the narrowleaf blazing star, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, spiky flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces purple to pinkish flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Liatris vittata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local ecosystems.
