Lupinus excubitus var. johnstonii

Lupinus excubitus var. johnstonii, commonly known as Johnston's lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and showy blue to purple flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

This species is also valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a food source for native pollinators.
