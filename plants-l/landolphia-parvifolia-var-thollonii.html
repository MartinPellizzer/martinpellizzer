Landolphia parvifolia var. thollonii

Landolphia parvifolia var. thollonii, commonly known as the small-leaved African morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions for its vibrant blooms.
