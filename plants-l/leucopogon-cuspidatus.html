Leucopogon cuspidatus

Leucopogon cuspidatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white in color. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.

Leucopogon cuspidatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
