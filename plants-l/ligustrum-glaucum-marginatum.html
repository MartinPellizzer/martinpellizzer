Ligustrum glaucum-marginatum

Ligustrum glaucum-marginatum, commonly known as the silverleaf privet, is a flowering plant belonging to the Oleaceae family.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with a waxy, silver-blue underside, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers in clusters, which are typically followed by small, dark berries. Native to East Asia, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various soil types.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions if not properly managed.
