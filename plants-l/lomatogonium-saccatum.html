Lomatogonium saccatum

Lomatogonium saccatum, commonly known as the sand mat, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its low-growing, sprawling habit with gray-green, woolly foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Lomatogonium saccatum is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.
