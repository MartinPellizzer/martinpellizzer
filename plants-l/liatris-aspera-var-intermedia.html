Liatris aspera var. intermedia

Liatris aspera var. intermedia, commonly known as the rough blazingstar, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features tall, upright stems covered in fine, bristly hairs, and its leaves are narrow, linear, and often hairy. It produces showy purple to pink flower heads that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This perennial herb thrives in dry, open habitats like prairies and woodland edges.
