Lagenocarpus guianensis var. typicus

Lagenocarpus guianensis var. typicus, commonly known as the Guianan bottle palm, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive bottle-shaped stem and pinnately arranged leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with yellowish-green flowers. Its fruits are typically small, fleshy berries that contain a single seed.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to humid, warm climates.
