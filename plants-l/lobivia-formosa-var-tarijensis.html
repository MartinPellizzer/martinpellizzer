Lobivia formosa var. tarijensis

Lobivia formosa var. tarijensis, commonly known as the Tarijensis Lobivia, is a succulent native to the Andes of South America.

It features a compact, rounded stem with thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-gray in color. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
