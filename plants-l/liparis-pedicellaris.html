Liparis pedicellaris

Liparis pedicellaris, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. The plant features a rhizome with erect, slender stems that bear lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, often arranged in a loose spike, and are pollinated by insects.

This orchid is valued for its delicate appearance and ecological role in its native habitats.
