Linum armenum

Linum armenum, commonly known as Armenian flax, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces delicate blue or purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branched stems and linear, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. Its seeds are small and contain high levels of oil, making it valuable for both ornamental and industrial purposes.

Linum armenum thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments.
