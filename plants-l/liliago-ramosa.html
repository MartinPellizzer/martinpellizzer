Liliago ramosa

Liliago ramosa, commonly known as the branching lily, is a herbaceous plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves arranged in a rosette pattern, and its flowers typically come in shades of white, pink, or purple. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Liliago ramosa is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
