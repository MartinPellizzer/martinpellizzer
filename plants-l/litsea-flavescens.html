Litsea flavescens

Litsea flavescens, commonly known as the yellow-flowered litsea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white to yellow flowers that appear in clusters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a mild aromatic scent.

Litsea flavescens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
