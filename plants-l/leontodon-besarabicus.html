Leontodon besarabicus

Leontodon besarabicus, commonly known as the Caucasian dandelion, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces yellow composite flowers that develop into elongated, cylindrical seed heads. It thrives in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
