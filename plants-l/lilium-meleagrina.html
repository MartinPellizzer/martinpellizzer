Lilium meleagrina

Lilium meleagrina, commonly known as the Turk's cap lily, is a species of flowering plant in the lily family.

It is native to the eastern and southeastern United States, where it thrives in woodland and moist environments. The plant is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with a dark center. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a fan-like pattern, and it produces a tall, sturdy stem.

Lilium meleagrina is a perennial herb that blooms in late summer to early fall, adding vibrant color to gardens and natural landscapes.
