Licuala coccinisedes

Licuala coccinisedes, commonly known as the red-veined palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves with distinctive red veins, which give it its common name. The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests. Its leaves are thick and waxy, adapted to retain moisture in its natural habitat.

The red-veined palm is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic qualities in gardens and landscapes.
