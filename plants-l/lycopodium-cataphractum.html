Lycopodium cataphractum

Lycopodium cataphractum, commonly known as the African clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Lycopodiales.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant has slender, branching stems that are covered in small, scale-like leaves, and it produces spores in specialized structures called sporangia. Lycopodium cataphractum is characterized by its ability to thrive in harsh environments, making it a resilient and hardy species.
