Lomatium idahoense

Lomatium idahoense, commonly known as Idaho lomatium, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of fern-like, compound leaves that are often grayish-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, typically in clusters at the top of the stem. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and sagebrush steppe.

Lomatium idahoense is part of the Apiaceae family, which includes parsley and carrots, and is known for its aromatic foliage.
