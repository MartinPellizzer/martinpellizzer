Lucuma venulosa

Lucuma venulosa, commonly known as the white lucuma, is a member of the Lauraceae family and is native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height, featuring smooth, dark green leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The fruit is a large, oval-shaped berry with a thick, leathery skin and a sweet, creamy pulp. It produces its fruit during the dry season, typically between May and September.

The plant thrives in high-altitude environments with well-drained soils and requires a cool, temperate climate for optimal growth.
