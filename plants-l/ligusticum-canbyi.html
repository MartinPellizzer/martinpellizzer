Ligusticum canbyi

Ligusticum canbyi, commonly known as Canby's angelica, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescences.

Native to the Pacific Northwest of North America, it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant features tall, slender stems with large, pinnately compound leaves that can reach up to 30 inches in length. Its flowers are small and white to pale green, arranged in umbrella-like clusters, and it produces small, dry fruits known as schizocarps.

Ligusticum canbyi is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
