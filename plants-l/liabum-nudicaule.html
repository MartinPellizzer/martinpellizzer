Liabum nudicaule

Liabum nudicaule, commonly known as the naked-stemmed catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial with slender, hairless stems that often lie close to the ground. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming in the spring. Its leaves are narrow and linear, arranged alternately along the stem.

Liabum nudicaule is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open meadows and disturbed soils.
