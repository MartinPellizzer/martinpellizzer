Lobelia violaceoaurantiaca

Lobelia violaceoaurantiaca, commonly known as the violet-tinged lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet and orange-pink flowers, which bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and features narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It prefers cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique coloration and adaptability to high-altitude conditions.
