Launaea resedifolia var. pulchella

Launaea resedifolia var. pulchella, commonly known as the purple sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, hairy stems. The plant has a succulent, creeping growth habit, allowing it to thrive in sandy and rocky soils. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The showy purple to pink flowers appear in clusters, making it a striking addition to dryland landscapes.
