Lantana tuxtlensis

Lantana tuxtlensis, commonly known as the Tuxtlans lantana, is a shrub native to the humid tropical regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, which attract various pollinators. Lantana tuxtlensis is adapted to thrive in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in its native habitat.
