Lauro-cerasus guanaiensis

Lauro-cerasus guanaiensis, commonly known as the Guanai cherry laurel, is a species of flowering plant in the Prunus genus.

It is native to the Andes mountains in South America, specifically in regions of Argentina and Chile. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruits are small, dark berries that mature in late summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
