Lespedeza daurica subsp. potaninii

Lespedeza daurica subsp.

potaninii, commonly known as Mongolian lespedeza, is a perennial flowering plant native to East Asia. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, pinkish to reddish flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has trifoliate leaves with three oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry grasslands and steppe environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
