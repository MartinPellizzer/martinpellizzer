Ligustrum ovalifolium f. argenteum

Ligustrum ovalifolium f. argenteum, commonly known as silverleaf privet, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the western United States.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are densely covered with a silvery-white to grayish-white powdery coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by dark blue-black berries that are edible for birds. It is often used in landscaping for its dense foliage and adaptability to various soil types.

Due to its invasive potential in some regions, it is sometimes considered a weed in natural areas.
