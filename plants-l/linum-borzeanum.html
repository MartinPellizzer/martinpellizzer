Linum borzeanum

Linum borzeanum, commonly known as the Borzean flax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the genus Linum, which includes various species of flax known for their ornamental and sometimes medicinal uses. The plant produces delicate blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Linum borzeanum is valued for its aesthetic appeal and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
