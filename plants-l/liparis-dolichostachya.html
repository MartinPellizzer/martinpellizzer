Liparis dolichostachya

Liparis dolichostachya, commonly known as the long-stalked marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense clusters of small, greenish or brownish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as swamps and wetlands. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern at the base of the plant.

Liparis dolichostachya belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its unique floral structure and adaptation to wetland habitats.
