Lazarum alismifolium

Lazarum alismifolium, commonly known as the water shield, is a aquatic plant native to North America.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and marshes. The plant has narrow, ribbon-like leaves that float on the water surface, with roots submerged in the mud. Its flowers are small and yellow, often clustered in loose inflorescences.

Lazarum alismifolium plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various invertebrates and fish.
