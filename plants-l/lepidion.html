Lepidion

Lepidion, commonly known as the common rock cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of pinnately lobed leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or arctic regions. Lepidion species are generally herbaceous and can be found in various parts of the Northern Hemisphere.

Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and flies.
