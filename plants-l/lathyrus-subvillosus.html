Lathyrus subvillosus

Lathyrus subvillosus, commonly known as the velvet vetchling, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in early summer. Its trifoliate leaves are covered in fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture.

Lathyrus subvillosus is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including meadows and woodland edges.
