Linaria genistifolia var. angustata

Linaria genistifolia var. angustata, commonly known as narrow-leaved toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are narrow and linear, giving the plant its common name, while the flowers are typically arranged in racemes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable plant for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
