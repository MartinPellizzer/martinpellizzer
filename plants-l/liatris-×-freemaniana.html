Liatris × freemaniana

Liatris × freemaniana, commonly known as the Freeman's blazing star, is a hybrid perennial grassland plant native to the central United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright, spikelike inflorescence that blooms with showy, pinkish-purple flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet and features narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a hardy choice for native plant gardens.

Its botanical structure supports pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
