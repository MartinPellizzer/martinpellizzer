Ligia

Ligia, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny leaves and showy, often blue or purple, bracts that resemble flowers. Ligia thrives in coastal areas and saline soils, making it well-adapted to harsh maritime environments. The plant is typically found in the Mediterranean region and parts of southern Europe.

Its unique morphology and ecological resilience make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
