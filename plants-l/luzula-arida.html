Luzula arida

Luzula arida, commonly known as the arid sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, rushes, and grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a dark green color, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters.

Luzula arida is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil in harsh environments.
