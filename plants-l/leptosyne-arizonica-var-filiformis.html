Leptosyne arizonica var. filiformis

Leptosyne arizonica var. filiformis, commonly known as the Arizona hairgrass, is a variant of the Arizona hairgrass species.

It is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. The plant is characterized by its slender, thread-like leaves that give it its common name. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in desert scrub and rocky soils.

Its botanical features include small, spike-like inflorescences and tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.
