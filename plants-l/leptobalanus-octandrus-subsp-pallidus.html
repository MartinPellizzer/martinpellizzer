Leptobalanus octandrus subsp. pallidus

Leptobalanus octandrus subsp.

pallidus, commonly known as the pale leathery bark tree, is a species native to the mountainous regions of South America. It is characterized by its thick, leathery bark that provides protection against environmental stressors.

The plant exhibits a shrub-like growth habit with small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. Its flowers are typically clustered in inflorescences and are adapted for pollination by wind or insects.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations and potential ecological roles in its native habitat.
