Laurelia aromatica

Laurelia aromatica, commonly known as the sweet laurel, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a member of the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like cinnamon and bay laurel. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are glossy and have a strong, spicy fragrance. It produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences.

The fruit is a small, dark purple drupe that contains a single seed.
