Limodorum turkestanicum

Limodorum turkestanicum, commonly known as the Turkestan lily, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or mountainous regions. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

The flowers are often fragrant and attract various pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.
