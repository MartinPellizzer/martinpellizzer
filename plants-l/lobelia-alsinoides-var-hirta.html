Lobelia alsinoides var. hirta

Lobelia alsinoides var. hirta, commonly known as the hairy lobelia, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
