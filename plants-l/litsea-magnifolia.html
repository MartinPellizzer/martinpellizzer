Litsea magnifolia

Litsea magnifolia, commonly known as the fragrant leaf tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Vietnam, Thailand, and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, ovate leaves that emit a pleasant scent when crushed. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by spherical, reddish berries.

Litsea magnifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
