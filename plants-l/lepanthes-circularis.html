Lepanthes circularis

Lepanthes circularis, commonly known as the circular orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its small, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, yellow, and green. The plant has a compact growth habit with a pseudobulb and is often found in shaded, humid environments. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a rosette pattern.

Lepanthes circularis is appreciated for its ornamental value and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its delicate beauty.
