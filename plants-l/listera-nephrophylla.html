Listera nephrophylla

Listera nephrophylla, commonly known as the kidneyleaf spring beauty, is a small perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of delicate white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and along stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, making it one of the first wildflowers to appear in its habitat.

Listera nephrophylla is an important species for pollinators and contributes to the biodiversity of its ecosystem.
