Liquidambar peregrina

Liquidambar peregrina, commonly known as the Chinese sweetgum, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive star-shaped leaves and showy, spiky fruit capsules. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall, with smooth, gray bark and a broad, rounded crown. Its leaves turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in the fall, making it a notable ornamental plant.

The species is valued for its timber and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
