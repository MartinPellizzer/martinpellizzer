Lobelia cardinalis

Lobelia cardinalis, commonly known as cardinalflower, is a striking perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, tubular red flowers that attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and butterflies. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments like woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Lobelia cardinalis is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting native wildlife.
