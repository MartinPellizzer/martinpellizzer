Laportea cuneata

Laportea cuneata, commonly known as the narrowleaf laportea, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive white or pale green underside, and it produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. Laportea cuneata is often found in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and along stream banks.
