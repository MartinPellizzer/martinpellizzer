Lepidaploa persicifolia

Lepidaploa persicifolia, commonly known as the Persian rose or Persian lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges, typically arranged in an alternate pattern along the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pink, which bloom in early summer. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.

Lepidaploa persicifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and aromatic qualities.
