Limonium lefroyi

Limonium lefroyi, commonly known as the coastal flax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, sandy soils, and saline environments, showing a high tolerance for salt and wind. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Limonium lefroyi is often used in coastal landscaping due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
