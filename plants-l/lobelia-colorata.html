Lobelia colorata

Lobelia colorata, commonly known as the scarlet lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Lobelia colorata is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and attracts pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
