Leucadendron involucratum

Leucadendron involucratum, commonly known as the silver curlew, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage and vibrant yellow-orange flowers that bloom in winter. The plant has a dense, compact growth habit and is often used in rock gardens due to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, requiring minimal water once established.

The species is also valued for its ecological role, providing habitat and nectar for local pollinators.
