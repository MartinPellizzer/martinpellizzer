Laurus culillaban

Laurus culillaban, commonly known as the sacred laurel, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other well-known species like bay laurel and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage, which is dark green and aromatic, and its small, white flowers that appear in clusters. Its fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and historical significance in traditional medicine, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
