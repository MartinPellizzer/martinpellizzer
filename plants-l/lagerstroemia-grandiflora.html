Lagerstroemia grandiflora

Lagerstroemia grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered crape myrtle, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its showy, pendulous flowers that range in color from pink to red and white, appearing in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its long flowering period and ability to withstand heat and drought contribute to its widespread cultivation.
