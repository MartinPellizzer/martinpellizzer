Lilium auratum var. tricolor

Lilium auratum var. tricolor, commonly known as the tricolor lily, is a striking perennial bulbous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant, three-colored petals that typically display red, orange, and yellow hues, creating a visually captivating bloom. The plant produces tall, upright stems that can reach up to 6 feet in height, adorned with lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
