Lepanthes callisto

Lepanthes callisto, commonly known as the violet orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue petals and a distinctive, showy floral structure. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, tropical environments. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to the undercanopy of its native habitat.

Lepanthes callisto is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
