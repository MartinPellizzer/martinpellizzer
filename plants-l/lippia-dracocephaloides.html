Lippia dracocephaloides

Lippia dracocephaloides, commonly known as dragonhead mint, is a perennial herb native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a serrated edge and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or pink in color. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a mild mint-like fragrance.

Lippia dracocephaloides is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
