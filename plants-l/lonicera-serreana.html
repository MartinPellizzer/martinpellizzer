Lonicera serreana

Lonicera serreana, commonly known as the creeping honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, arching stems that often root at the nodes, allowing it to spread along the ground or climb. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically blooming in late spring. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly rough texture.

Lonicera serreana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
