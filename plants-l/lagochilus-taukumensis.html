Lagochilus taukumensis

Lagochilus taukumensis, commonly known as the Tawkmun limestone thyme, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink and purple, typically in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Lagochilus taukumensis is often found growing in rocky or limestone soils, thriving in dry and sunny conditions.
