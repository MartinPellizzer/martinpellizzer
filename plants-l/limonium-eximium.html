Limonium eximium

Limonium eximium, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its spikes of blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in sandy or salty soils and is often found in dunes and coastal grasslands. It has narrow, linear leaves and is well adapted to harsh maritime conditions.

Limonium eximium is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in stabilizing coastal soils.
