Liabum alibum

Liabum alibum, commonly known as the white veldtgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open grasslands and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pale green to white flowers. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are usually hairless and have a bluish-green hue. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and resilience.

Liabum alibum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for livestock in its native habitat.
