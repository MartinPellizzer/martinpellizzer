Lupinus arvensis

Lupinus arvensis, commonly known as the field lupin, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species that typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields. The plant produces erect stems with compound leaves and spikes of blue to purple flowers. It is characterized by its nitrogen-fixing ability, which makes it beneficial for soil enrichment.

Lupinus arvensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
