Loranthus kraussianus

Loranthus kraussianus, commonly known as the African mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of host trees, primarily in the genus Acacia.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using the light it captures. The plant has small, scale-like leaves and produces white or yellowish flowers that are often visited by insects and birds. Its berries, which are toxic to humans, are an important food source for certain bird species.

This plant plays a significant role in the ecological dynamics of its native habitats in southern Africa.
