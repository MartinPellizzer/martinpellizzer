Lobivia calorubra var. megalocephala

Lobivia calorubra var. megalocephala, commonly known as the giant red lobivia, is a succulent native to the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It features thick, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. The plant produces vibrant red flowers during the cooler months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green foliage. Its leaves are typically broad and fleshy, adapted for minimizing water loss.

This variety is prized for its unique coloration and is often cultivated in rock gardens and arid landscapes.
