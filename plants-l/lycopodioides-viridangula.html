Lycopodioides viridangula

Lycopodioides viridangula, commonly known as the green clubmoss, is a member of the Lycopodiaceae family and is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and spore-bearing structures called strobila.

It is a vascular plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. The plant reproduces through spores rather than seeds, a feature common among primitive plants. Its structure includes a root system and a stem with whorled foliage, and it plays a role in soil stabilization and habitat provision for microorganisms.

Lycopodioides viridangula is often found in temperate regions and is considered an indicator species for certain ecological conditions.
