Linum angustifolium var. ambiguum

Linum angustifolium var. ambiguum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf flax or ambiguous flax, is a perennial herb native to parts of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal or inland regions. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.

Its fibrous stems and leaves are also used in the production of natural fibers.
