Lucuma ramiflora

Lucuma ramiflora, commonly known as the tree of life, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and ornamental flowers. The plant produces yellow-orange fruits that are rich in nutrients and have a unique, sweet flavor. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Lucuma ramiflora is valued both for its ecological role and its potential economic importance in local communities.
