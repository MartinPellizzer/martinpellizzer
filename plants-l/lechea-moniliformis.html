Lechea moniliformis

Lechea moniliformis, commonly known as the chain vine, is a climbing plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its long, slender stems that form a chain-like structure, giving it its common name. The plant has small, heart-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in humid environments.

Lechea moniliformis is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique appearance.
