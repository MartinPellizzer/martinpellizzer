Lamiostachys

Lamiostachys, commonly known as the narrowleaf mountain mint, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in woodland or wetland habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinct minty fragrance when crushed.

Lamiostachys produces small, clustered blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months.
