Lychnis scottii

Lychnis scottii, commonly known as the Scottish catchfly, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the British Isles.

It features delicate, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a low-growing habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland edges and meadows.

Lychnis scottii is known for its attractive appearance and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
