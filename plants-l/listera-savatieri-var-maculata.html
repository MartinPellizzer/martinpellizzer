Listera savatieri var. maculata

Listera savatieri var. maculata, commonly known as the spotted basket orchid, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, spurred orchid-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture, while the flowers are usually white or pale pink with a distinctive spur.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens with the right growing conditions.
