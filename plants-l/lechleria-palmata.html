Lechleria palmata

Lechleria palmata, commonly known as the Mexican fan palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical climates, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits.

Lechleria palmata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in landscaping due to its elegant appearance.
