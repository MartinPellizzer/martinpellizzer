Lysimachia westphalica

Lysimachia westphalica, commonly known as the Westphalian chickweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. The plant has wiry, prostrate stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are oval to lance-shaped with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Lysimachia westphalica is often found in moist soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in garden settings.
