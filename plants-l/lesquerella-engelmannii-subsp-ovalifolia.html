Lesquerella engelmannii subsp. ovalifolia

Lesquerella engelmannii subsp.

ovalifolia, commonly known as the ovalleaf lesquerella, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its woody stems and small, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

Lesquerella ovalifolia is valued for its potential in ecological restoration and as a source of natural oils.
