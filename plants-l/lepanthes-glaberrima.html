Lepanthes glaberrima

Lepanthes glaberrima, commonly known as the pale lepanthes, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments within forest understories. The plant features a slender, erect stem with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. Its flowers are small, white to pale green, and are clustered in a raceme, with a distinctive, hooded labellum.

Lepanthes glaberrima is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its delicate appearance.
