Lomaria feei

Lomaria feei, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are woody and branched.

Lomaria feei thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
