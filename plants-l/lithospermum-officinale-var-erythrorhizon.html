Lithospermum officinale var. erythrorhizon

Lithospermum officinale var. erythrorhizon, commonly known as Japanese erythroxylum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features erect, branching stems covered in fine hairs and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, capsule-like fruits containing reddish-brown seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in woodland edges and rocky areas.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly the presence of compounds like shikonin in its roots.
