Lupinus sparsiflorus subsp. sparsiflorus

Lupinus sparsiflorus subsp.

sparsiflorus, commonly known as the sparse-flowered lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States. It typically grows in open, disturbed sites such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant features upright stems with palmately compound leaves composed of three to five oval-shaped leaflets. Its showy, pea-like flowers are usually blue to purple and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The species is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a food source for native wildlife.
