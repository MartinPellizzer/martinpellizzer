Leptotaenia californica

Leptotaenia californica, commonly known as the California lespedeza, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. The plant features slender, erect stems with opposite, pinnately compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, yellowish-white flowers appear in clusters during the summer months.

It is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide forage for wildlife.
