Lipochaeta elliptica var. artialata

Lipochaeta elliptica var. artialata, commonly known as the narrowleaf lipochaeta, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southern United States.

It typically grows in wetland and marshy environments, thriving in moist, acidic soils. The plant features slender, erect stems with opposite, elliptic-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, yellow flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, contributing to its ornamental value.

It is valued in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
