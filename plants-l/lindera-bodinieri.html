Lindera bodinieri

Lindera bodinieri, commonly known as the Chinese spice bush, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, which are followed by dark blue, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Lindera bodinieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and its use in traditional Chinese medicine.
