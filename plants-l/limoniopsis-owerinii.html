Limoniopsis owerinii

Limoniopsis owerinii, commonly known as the desert daisy, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant is well adapted to harsh environments, with succulent leaves that store water and reduce transpiration. Its flowering period typically occurs during the rainy season, adding a splash of color to the landscape.

Limoniopsis owerinii is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
