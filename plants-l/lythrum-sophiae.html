Lythrum sophiae

Lythrum sophiae, commonly known as rosepink or purple loosestrife, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, clustered pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. Lythrum sophiae is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to various regions for garden use.
