Landolphia membranacea

Landolphia membranacea, commonly known as the African morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough texture.

Landolphia membranacea is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
