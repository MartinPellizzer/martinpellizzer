Limonium carnosum

Limonium carnosum, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in coastal areas and well-drained soils, often growing in sandy or rocky habitats. Its flowers are typically visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.

Limonium carnosum is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
