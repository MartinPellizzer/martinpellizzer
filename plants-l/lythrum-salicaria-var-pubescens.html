Lythrum salicaria var. pubescens

Lythrum salicaria var. pubescens, commonly known as purple loosestrife, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall spikes of vibrant purple flowers that bloom in midsummer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, preferring full sun and moist soil.

Due to its aggressive growth, it is considered an invasive species in many regions of North America.
