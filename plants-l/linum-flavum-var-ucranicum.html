Linum flavum var. ucranicum

Linum flavum var. ucranicum, commonly known as the Ucranic yellow flax, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a member of the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while the flowers are usually solitary or in small groups.

Linum flavum var. ucranicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in textile production.
