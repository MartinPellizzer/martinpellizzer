Laggera alata

Laggera alata, commonly known as the winged sunflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaf structure, with wing-like lobes that give it its common name. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Laggera alata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
