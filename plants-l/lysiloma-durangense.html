Lysiloma durangense

Lysiloma durangense, commonly known as the Durango acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant has bipinnate leaves, which are divided into multiple pairs of leaflets. It produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Lysiloma durangense is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in its native habitat.
