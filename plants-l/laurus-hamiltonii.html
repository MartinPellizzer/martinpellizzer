Laurus hamiltonii

Laurus hamiltonii, commonly known as the Hamilton's laurel, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the western coast of North America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional herbal practices. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Laurus hamiltonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its historical use in indigenous medicine.
