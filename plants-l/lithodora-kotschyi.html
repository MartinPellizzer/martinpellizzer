Lithodora kotschyi

Lithodora kotschyi, commonly known as the Armenian bugloss, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats of foliage, with small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of blue to violet flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Lithodora kotschyi thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice for horticultural use in temperate climates.
