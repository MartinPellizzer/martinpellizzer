Linum perenne subsp. lewisii

Linum perenne subsp.

lewisii, commonly known as Lewis's flax, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, blue to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and slightly hairy, while its flowers are clustered in terminal spikes.

Lewis's flax is valued for its ornamental appeal and its historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
