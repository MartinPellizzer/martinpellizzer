Lepidium silaifolium

Lepidium silaifolium, commonly known as silvery leafed peppergrass, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, white or pale yellow flowers and its finely divided, silvery-gray leaves. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It is native to regions of North America, particularly in the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

Lepidium silaifolium is often found in disturbed soils and can be an indicator of soil disturbance or degradation.
