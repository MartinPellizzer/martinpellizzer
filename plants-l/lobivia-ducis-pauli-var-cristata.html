Lobivia ducis-pauli var. cristata

Lobivia ducis-pauli var. cristata, commonly known as the cristate lobivia, is a rare and striking cactus native to the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly covering of white to grayish hairs, which helps it retain moisture in its arid environment. The plant typically grows in a compact, rounded shape with a distinctive cristate form, giving it a unique, almost otherworldly appearance. Its flowers, when they appear, are usually yellow or orange, adding a vibrant contrast to its otherwise muted foliage.

Due to its extreme rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is highly sought after by collectors and requires careful cultivation.
