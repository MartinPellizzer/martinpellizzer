Lupinus simulans

Lupinus simulans, commonly known as the mimic lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Lupinus simulans is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.
