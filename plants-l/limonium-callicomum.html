Limonium callicomum

Limonium callicomum, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads, which are surrounded by silver-gray bracts that resemble leaves. The plant typically grows in sandy or saline soils, thriving in coastal environments where it is exposed to salt spray. Limonium callicomum is a member of the Plumbagaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Its flowers bloom in summer, adding a vibrant touch to coastal landscapes.
