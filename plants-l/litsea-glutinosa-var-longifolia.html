Litsea glutinosa var. longifolia

Litsea glutinosa var. longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf litsea, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Vietnam, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, small, elliptic leaves that are often covered in a fine, sticky resin. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, and the fruit is a drupe that turns from green to red when mature.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
