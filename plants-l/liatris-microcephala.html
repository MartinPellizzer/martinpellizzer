Liatris microcephala

Liatris microcephala, commonly known as narrowleaf blazingstar, is a herbaceous perennial native to the central and eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, spiky flower heads. The plant typically blooms in late summer to early fall, producing purple to pinkish-purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade in well-drained soils.

Liatris microcephala is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
