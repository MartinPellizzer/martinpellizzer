Loranthus laxiflorus

Loranthus laxiflorus, commonly known as the slender-flowered mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant belonging to the Loranthaceae family.

It attaches itself to the branches of various host trees, such as eucalyptus and acacia, through a specialized root system called a haustellum. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its berries, which are often red or yellow, contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

Loranthus laxiflorus plays a role in ecosystem dynamics by influencing host tree growth and providing food for wildlife.
