Leianthus longifolius

Leianthus longifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved leianthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
