Lolium × fleischeri

Lolium × fleischeri, commonly known as the hybrid ryegrass, is a perennial grass species that results from the cross between Lolium perenne (perennial ryegrass) and Lolium multiflorum (Italian ryegrass).

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and dense growth habit, making it a popular choice for forage and turfgrass applications. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and produces spikelets that are often clustered at the base of the stems. It thrives in temperate climates and is known for its high nutritional value and rapid establishment.

Lolium × fleischeri is also valued for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to withstand grazing and mowing.
