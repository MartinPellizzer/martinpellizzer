Lipotriche pungens subsp. pungens

Lipotriche pungens subsp.

pungens, commonly known as the spiny pungent lichen, is a member of the family Pilocarpaceae. It is characterized by its unique morphology, featuring a thallus that is often crustose or foliose in structure.

The plant exhibits a distinct coloration, typically ranging from yellowish to orange, which helps in its identification. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found on bark or soil surfaces.

Its ecological role includes contributing to soil stabilization and serving as a microhabitat for various organisms.
