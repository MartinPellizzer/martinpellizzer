Lepanthes omnifera

Lepanthes omnifera, commonly known as the many-flowered slipper orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that often display a range of colors, including white, yellow, and purple. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as rainforests, where it thrives as an epiphyte on trees. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and debris around it.

Lepanthes omnifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
