Lobostemon trichotomus

Lobostemon trichotomus, commonly known as the three-parted bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are often divided into three parts, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Lobostemon trichotomus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
