Lycopodium alpinum proles issleri

Lycopodium alpinum proles issleri, commonly known as the Alpine clubmoss, is a small, evergreen plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and its distinctive, spirally arranged leaves that form a dense, cushion-like structure. The plant produces sporophytes that develop into spore-bearing cones, which are typically clustered at the base of the foliage. Native to high-altitude regions of the Alps and surrounding mountainous areas, it thrives in cool, moist, and well-drained soils.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it is often considered a relict species with historical significance in plant evolution.
