Lagochilus diacanthophyllus

Lagochilus diacanthophyllus, commonly known as the Persian bugloss, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces small, blue or violet flowers in late spring to early summer. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to access water from lower soil layers.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
