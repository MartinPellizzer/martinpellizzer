Lycopodium flabellum

Lycopodium flabellum, commonly known as the clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its branching, needle-like leaves that form a dense, cushion-like structure. It produces spores in small, cone-like structures called strobila, which are located at the tips of its branches.

Lycopodium flabellum plays an important ecological role in maintaining soil stability and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
