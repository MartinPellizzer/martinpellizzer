Lampocarya leptostachya

Lampocarya leptostachya, commonly known as the slender-stemmed lampocarya, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and heat. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The flowers are unisexual, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants, a feature common in many members of the Amaranthaceae family.
