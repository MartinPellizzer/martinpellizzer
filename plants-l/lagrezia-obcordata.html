Lagrezia obcordata

Lagrezia obcordata, commonly known as the red-flowered passion vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that feature five petals, a spurred corona, and a distinctive red coloration. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often consumed by birds and mammals. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are woody and twining.

Lagrezia obcordata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
