Lasiorrhiza lanata

Lasiorrhiza lanata, commonly known as the woolly lasiorrhiza, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, woolly leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky outcrops, preferring well-drained soils. It produces showy, tubular flowers that are often pink or purple in color, attracting pollinators like bees and birds.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in regions with a suitable climate.
