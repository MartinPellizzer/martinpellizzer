Loranthus heterochromus

Loranthus heterochromus, commonly known as the heterochromatic mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to host trees through its haustoria, drawing water and nutrients from the host.

It belongs to the family Loranthaceae and is native to regions of Africa, including parts of South Africa. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. Its berries, which are typically red or yellow, contain seeds covered in a sticky substance to aid in attachment to new hosts.

This species is of interest in ecological studies due to its parasitic nature and role in forest ecosystems.
