Linaria heterophylla subsp. galioides

Linaria heterophylla subsp.

galioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved toadflax, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

The plant features slender stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are bright yellow with a distinctive bilabiate structure, resembling those of other members of the Plantaginaceae family.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
