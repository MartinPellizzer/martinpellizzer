Lupinus arizonicus subsp. arizonicus

Lupinus arizonicus subsp.

arizonicus, commonly known as Arizona lupine, is a perennial legume native to the southwestern United States. It typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

The plant features erect stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces spiky, pea-like flowers in shades of blue, purple, or white. Its root system includes nitrogen-fixing nodules, which enhance soil fertility.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role and is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.
