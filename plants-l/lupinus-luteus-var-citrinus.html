Lupinus luteus var. citrinus

Lupinus luteus var. citrinus, commonly known as the yellow lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and compound leaves with small, oval leaflets. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas. It has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion.

The seeds are edible after proper preparation and are used in some traditional cuisines.
