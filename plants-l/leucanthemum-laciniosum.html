Leucanthemum laciniosum

Leucanthemum laciniosum, commonly known as the fringed leucanthemum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features basal leaves that are lobed and hairy, with stems that are often sparsely hairy as well. Its flowers are white with yellow centers, and they bloom in late summer to early autumn.

It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable addition to rock gardens and borders.
