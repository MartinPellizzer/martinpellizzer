Limonia diphylla

Limonia diphylla, commonly known as the two-leaved hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of eastern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, often near the shoreline. The plant features showy, yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in clusters. Its leaves are usually small, opposite, and have a rough texture.

Limonia diphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
