Lupinus luteus var. aureus

Lupinus luteus var. aureus, commonly known as the golden lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats. Its root system hosts nitrogen-fixing bacteria, contributing to soil fertility.

The golden lupine is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
