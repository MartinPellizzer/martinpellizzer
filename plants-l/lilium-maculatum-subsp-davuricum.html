Lilium maculatum subsp. davuricum

Lilium maculatum subsp.

davuricum, commonly known as the spotted leek lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features upright, linear leaves and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red with a white or yellow throat.

The plant is characterized by its distinctive spotted or mottled leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
