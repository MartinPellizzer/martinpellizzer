Leucopogon squamatus

Leucopogon squamatus, commonly known as the silverleaf heath, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers, which are typically borne on short, hairy stems. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

Leucopogon squamatus is adapted to dry conditions and is an important component of its native ecosystem.
