Laelia × elegans var. schilleriana

Laelia × elegans var. schilleriana, commonly known as the Schiller's orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, often fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, yellow, and pink. The plant typically produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, enabling it to thrive in its natural, often arid environment. Its leaves are usually broad and fleshy, adapted to conserve moisture.

This variety is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
