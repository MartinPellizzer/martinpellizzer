Lonicera symphoricarpos

Lonicera symphoricarpos, commonly known as coralberry, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It features clusters of small, red berries that persist through the winter, providing food for birds and other wildlife. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are usually oval to elliptic in shape. It produces fragrant, tubular flowers in the spring, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Coralberry is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
