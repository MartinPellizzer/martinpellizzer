Lilium japonicum

Lilium japonicum, commonly known as the Japanese lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan and Korea.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its tall, straight stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer, producing pink to red flowers that are often speckled with darker markings. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, and the plant produces bulbous roots that store nutrients.

Lilium japonicum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
