Larix gmelinii var. japonica

Larix gmelinii var. japonica, commonly known as Japanese larch, is a deciduous conifer native to Japan and parts of eastern Russia.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and needle-like leaves that turn golden in autumn before falling. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often growing in mountainous regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its seasonal color changes.
