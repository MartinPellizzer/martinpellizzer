Lycium martii var. glomeratum

Lycium martii var. glomeratum, commonly known as the Chinese wolfberry or Goji berry plant, is a shrub native to arid regions of China.

It produces small, red, fleshy berries that are rich in antioxidants and nutrients. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for semi-arid climates. Its leaves are alternate and ovate, with a slightly hairy texture.

The flowers are yellow and clustered, giving rise to the variety name "glomeratum," which means "clustered.".
