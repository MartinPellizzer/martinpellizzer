Lupinus magnificus var. glarecola

Lupinus magnificus var. glarecola, commonly known as the golden lupine, is a striking perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of South America.

This variant is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which contrast beautifully with its deep green foliage. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, producing tall, spindly stems covered in dense clusters of pea-like blooms. Its root system forms symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enriching the soil.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
