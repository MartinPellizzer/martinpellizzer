Lathyrus cyaneus subsp. digitatus

Lathyrus cyaneus subsp.

digitatus, commonly known as the blue bell vetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant has slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
