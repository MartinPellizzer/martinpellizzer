Lobularia intermedia var. gracilis

Lobularia intermedia var. gracilis, commonly known as sweet alyssum, is a low-growing annual plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in early summer. The plant has hairy, linear leaves and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. It is often used in rock gardens and as a border plant due to its compact form and continuous flowering.

Its botanical name reflects its intermediate growth habit and graceful appearance.
