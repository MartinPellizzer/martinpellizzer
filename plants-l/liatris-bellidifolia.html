Liatris bellidifolia

Liatris bellidifolia, commonly known as the pink gayfeather, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy pink to purple flower clusters. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, preferring moist to wet soil conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Liatris bellidifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
