Ledum glandulosum var. columbianum

Ledum glandulosum var. columbianum, commonly known as the Columbian rosebay, is a perennial shrub native to western North America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used by indigenous peoples for various traditional purposes.
