Limonium humile

Limonium humile, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its blue to purple flower heads, which are actually clusters of small, tubular flowers surrounded by white, papery bracts. The plant thrives in salty, well-drained soils and is often found in dunes and coastal grasslands. Limonium humile is a perennial species that blooms from late spring to early autumn.

Its hardy nature makes it a popular choice for coastal gardens and landscaping.
