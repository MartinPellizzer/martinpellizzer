Leucas capitata

Leucas capitata, commonly known as the hairy false dandelion, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its fluffy, yellowish-white flower heads that resemble dandelion blooms. The plant features basal leaves that are hairy and lance-shaped, with stems that are also covered in fine hairs. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Leucas capitata is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
