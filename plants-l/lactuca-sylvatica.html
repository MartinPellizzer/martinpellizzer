Lactuca sylvatica

Lactuca sylvatica, commonly known as woodland lettuce, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features a basal rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often bluish-green in color. Its flowering stems produce small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in a loose cluster.

Lactuca sylvatica is characterized by its milky sap, which is a common trait among plants in the Asteraceae family.
