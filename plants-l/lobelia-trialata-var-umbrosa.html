Lobelia trialata var. umbrosa

Lobelia trialata var. umbrosa, commonly known as the shade-loving lobelia, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features small, tubular blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to add color to woodland gardens.
