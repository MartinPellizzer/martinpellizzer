Lycopodium pittieri

Lycopodium pittieri, commonly known as the Mexican clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant features slender, branching stems covered in small, scale-like leaves. Lycopodium pittieri reproduces via spores rather than seeds, and its life cycle includes a distinct gametophyte stage.

This ancient plant is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, showcasing its adaptability to diverse ecological conditions.
