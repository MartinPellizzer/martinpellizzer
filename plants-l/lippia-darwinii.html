Lippia darwinii

Lippia darwinii, commonly known as Darwin's verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Lippia darwinii is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
