Lycaste cochleata

Lycaste cochleata, commonly known as the spiral orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, spiral-shaped flowers that resemble a coiled shell, giving it its common name. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are typically broad and grass-like, and it thrives in warm, humid environments with bright indirect light.

Lycaste cochleata is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
