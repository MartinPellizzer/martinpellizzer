Leandra fluminensis

Leandra fluminensis, commonly known as the Amazonian orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in a variety of colors, including white, yellow, and pink. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in the dense rainforest canopy. Its leaves are elongated and glossy, and it thrives in high humidity and indirect sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by horticulturists worldwide.
