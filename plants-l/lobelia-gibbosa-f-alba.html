Lobelia gibbosa f. alba

Lobelia gibbosa f. alba, commonly known as white lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale blue, blooming in late summer to early fall. The plant has finely divided, aromatic leaves that are often hairy to the touch. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wet meadows.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking native, ornamental plants.
