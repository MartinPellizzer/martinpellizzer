Leea sambucina var. biserrata

Leea sambucina var. biserrata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved elderberry, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Leeaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by dark purple berries. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses in local cultures.
