Lepidium lasiocarpum var. pubescens

Lepidium lasiocarpum var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy peppergrass, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and hairy, lobed leaves. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

The variety pubescens is distinguished by its dense pubescence, or fine hairs, covering its stems and leaves.
