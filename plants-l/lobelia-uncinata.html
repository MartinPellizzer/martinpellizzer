Lobelia uncinata

Lobelia uncinata, commonly known as the hooked lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and swamps. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Lobelia uncinata is often found in association with other moisture-loving plants, contributing to the biodiversity of its habitat.
