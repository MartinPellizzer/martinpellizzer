Lobivia caespitosa

Lobivia caespitosa, commonly known as the clustered lobivia, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is a low-growing, succulent plant with thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. Its leaves are typically small and cylindrical, reducing water loss through transpiration. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual element to its habitat.

It is well adapted to rocky soils and full sun exposure, making it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.
