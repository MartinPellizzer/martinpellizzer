Lobivia haageana f. durispina

Lobivia haageana f. durispina, commonly known as the Spiny Lobivia, is a compact, low-growing cactus native to the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It features a rounded, ribbed stem that is typically covered in spines, which help reduce water loss in its arid environment. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the cooler months, adding a striking contrast to its rugged appearance. Its fleshy stems store water, allowing it to survive prolonged dry periods.

This variety is prized by cactus enthusiasts for its unique morphology and resilience.
