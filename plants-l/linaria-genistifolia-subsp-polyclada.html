Linaria genistifolia subsp. polyclada

Linaria genistifolia subsp.

polyclada, commonly known as the clustered toadflax, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, branching stems that often grow in dense clusters, with small, yellow-orange flowers arranged in spikes.

The leaves are linear and slightly hairy, typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or disturbed habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
