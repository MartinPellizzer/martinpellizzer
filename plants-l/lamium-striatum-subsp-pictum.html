Lamium striatum subsp. pictum

Lamium striatum subsp.

pictum, commonly known as the painted dead nettle, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe. It features lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive purple and green mottling pattern, which gives it its common name.

The plant produces clusters of small, two-lipped, pink to purple flowers that bloom from spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking shade-tolerant plants.
