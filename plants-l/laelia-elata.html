Laelia elata

Laelia elata, commonly known as the Andean orchid, is a species native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or alpine regions. The plant produces striking, showy flowers with a distinctive coloration, usually featuring a mix of white, yellow, and green hues. It has a pseudobulb structure, which helps it store water and nutrients in its native arid climate.

Laelia elata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
