Liparis wenzelii

Liparis wenzelii, commonly known as the Wenzel's orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a rhizomatous growth habit, often found in moist, shaded forest understories. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically arranged in a rosette pattern.

Liparis wenzelii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
