Lysimachia eberhardtii

Lysimachia eberhardtii, commonly known as Eberhardt's bugleweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically features spreading stems covered in small, heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in the spring, which are characteristic of the Primulaceae family. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Lysimachia eberhardtii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wet areas.
