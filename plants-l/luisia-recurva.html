Luisia recurva

Luisia recurva, commonly known as the curled luisia, is a species of flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. Its flowers are usually pale blue or violet, with five petals and a tubular shape.

Luisia recurva thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
