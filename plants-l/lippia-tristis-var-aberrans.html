Lippia tristis var. aberrans

Lippia tristis var. aberrans, commonly known as the narrowleaf lemon verbena, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves that emit a distinct lemony scent when crushed. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping and ornamental gardening.

Its essential oils are used in aromatherapy and natural remedies due to their calming and antiseptic properties.
