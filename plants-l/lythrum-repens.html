Lythrum repens

Lythrum repens, commonly known as narrowleaf catchfly, is a flowering plant in the family Lythraceae.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features slender, branching stems covered with fine hairs and lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pink or red, with five petals and numerous stamens.

Lythrum repens is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and it thrives in moist, disturbed soils.
