Lechea intermedia var. laurentiana

Lechea intermedia var. laurentiana, commonly known as the Laurentian lechea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a bluish-green hue and produces small, white to pale pink flowers in the spring. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to nutrient-rich soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
