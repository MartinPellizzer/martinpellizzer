Ledum californicum

Ledum californicum, commonly known as California cowberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to coastal areas of California.

It features small, waxy, red berries that are edible but have a sharp, acidic flavor. The plant has aromatic, glandular leaves that release a strong, pine-like scent when crushed. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for local ecosystems and a subject of botanical study.
