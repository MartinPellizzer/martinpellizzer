Linaria tenuis var. peltieriana

Linaria tenuis var. peltieriana, commonly known as the narrowleaf toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are narrow and linear, often covered with fine hairs, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
