Limnanthemum hydrophyllum

Limnanthemum hydrophyllum, commonly known as the water buttercup, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its small, bright yellow flowers with five petals and a hairy, waxy appearance. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are usually submerged or floating, adapted to aquatic conditions.

It plays a role in supporting aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small invertebrates.
