Leiothrix rufula var. elatior

Leiothrix rufula var. elatior, commonly known as the greater flameback bamboo, is a variant of the flameback bamboo species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant has a dense, bushy appearance with a tufted crown, and its leaves are narrow and slightly curved. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in humid tropical and subtropical regions.

This bamboo is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its unique aesthetic appeal.
