Lessingianthus farinosus

Lessingianthus farinosus, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that bloom in shades of pink and purple. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens despite its preference for arid conditions.
