Lupinus laxiflorus var. arbustus

Lupinus laxiflorus var. arbustus, commonly known as the bush lupine, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of pea-like flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets, and its root system is well-developed, often forming symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in arid and semi-arid environments.

Lupinus laxiflorus var. arbustus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators and stabilizing soils in disturbed areas.
