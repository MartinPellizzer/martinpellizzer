Lycium cornifolium

Lycium cornifolium, commonly known as the horned lycium, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It features distinctive, waxy, spiny leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a rugged appearance. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are edible and have a slightly tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought and extreme temperatures.

Lycium cornifolium is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
