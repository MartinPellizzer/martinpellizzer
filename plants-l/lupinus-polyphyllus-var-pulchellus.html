Lupinus polyphyllus var. pulchellus

Lupinus polyphyllus var. pulchellus, commonly known as the silver lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-blue, fern-like foliage and tall, spindly stems that bear clusters of pea-like flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes. Its botanical features include compound leaves with numerous small leaflets and flowers that are typically arranged in dense, upright spikes.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plants.
