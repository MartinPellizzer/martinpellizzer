Lapsana minima

Lapsana minima, commonly known as lesser hawk's-beard, is a low-growing annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small yellow daisies. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often branched. It produces small, yellow ray flowers surrounded by numerous disk flowers, which are typically visited by various pollinators.

Lapsana minima thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.
