Luzula abyssinica var. simensis

Luzula abyssinica var. simensis, commonly known as the Abyssinian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to high-altitude regions of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and compact inflorescences. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in spikes, and it produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Luzula abyssinica var. simensis is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to its harsh ecological niche.
