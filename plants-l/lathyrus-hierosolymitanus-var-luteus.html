Lathyrus hierosolymitanus var. luteus

Lathyrus hierosolymitanus var. luteus, commonly known as the Jerusalem glory flower, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking yellow to orange flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with slender, branching stems and trifoliate leaves. Its flowers are borne in racemes and are known for their vibrant color and ornamental value.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation in temperate climates.
