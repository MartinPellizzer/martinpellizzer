Leptinella pectinata subsp. pectinata

Leptinella pectinata subsp.

pectinata, commonly known as the silverleaf penny-cress, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is a small annual herb with delicate, fern-like leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery pubescence.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically arranged in clusters, blooming in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or disturbed habitats.

Its botanical features make it a characteristic species of open, sunny environments.
