Linaria babensis

Linaria babensis, commonly known as the Babes' toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny environments. It has linear leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Linaria babensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
