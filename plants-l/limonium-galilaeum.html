Limonium galilaeum

Limonium galilaeum, commonly known as the Galilee limonium, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Israel and Palestine.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky and coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down.

Limonium galilaeum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its hardy nature and visual contrast in garden settings.
