Lupinus alsophilus

Lupinus alsophilus, commonly known as the Santa Cruz lupin, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of California.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and coastal bluffs. It has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds.

Lupinus alsophilus is an important part of the local ecosystem, providing food for pollinators and supporting biodiversity in its native range.
