Lithophragma glabrum var. ramulosum

Lithophragma glabrum var. ramulosum, commonly known as the silverleaf twinflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring, typically clustered in pairs. Its leaves are broad, ovate, and covered in a dense layer of fine, silver-white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky slopes.
