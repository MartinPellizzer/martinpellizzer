Lotus strigosus var. hirtellus

Lotus strigosus var. hirtellus, commonly known as the hairy lotus, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

The hairy lotus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
