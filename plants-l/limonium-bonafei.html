Limonium bonafei

Limonium bonafei, commonly known as the Sicilian sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powder.

Limonium bonafei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and floral arrangements.
