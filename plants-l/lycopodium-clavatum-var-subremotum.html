Lycopodium clavatum var. subremotum

Lycopodium clavatum var. subremotum, commonly known as the slender clubmoss, is a variant of the clubmoss genus Lycopodium.

It is a small, evergreen fern-like plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, thread-like leaves that form dense, low-growing mats. Its sporophytes produce spores in clusters, which are dispersed by wind.

This variety is often found in forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
