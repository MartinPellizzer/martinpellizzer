Lecythis elliptica

Lecythis elliptica, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a large evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its elongated, elliptical leaves and produces large, woody fruits that contain the edible nuts. The tree can grow up to 50 meters tall and has a dense canopy, contributing to the forest's biodiversity. Its fruits are an important food source for various wildlife species.

The tree is also valued for its hard, durable wood and is often found in mixed-species forests.
