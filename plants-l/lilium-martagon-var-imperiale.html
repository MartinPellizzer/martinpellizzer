Lilium martagon var. imperiale

Lilium martagon var. imperiale, commonly known as the imperial tiger lily, is a striking variety of the martagon lily.

It is characterized by its large, showy, fragrant flowers that are typically a deep red or orange with a distinctive, tiger-like pattern. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is known for its robust growth and resilience. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a loose spiral, while its stems are sturdy and often branched.

This variety is prized in gardens for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its dramatic floral display.
