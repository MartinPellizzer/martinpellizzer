Lindsaea finlaysoniana

Lindsaea finlaysoniana, commonly known as the Finlayson's lace fern, is a striking epiphytic fern native to the tropical rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that can grow up to 1 meter in length, with a distinctive appearance that resembles a lace-like pattern. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on tree trunks or branches in the canopy layer of the forest. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the undersides of its fertile fronds, contributing to its unique botanical appearance.

Lindsaea finlaysoniana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is a subject of interest in botanical studies due to its ecological and evolutionary significance.
