Limniris ensata

Limniris ensata, commonly known as the water lily of Japan, is a aquatic plant native to the temperate regions of East Asia.

It typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and lakes. The plant features broad, floating leaves that are usually green with a waxy surface, and its flowers are showy, often with white or pale pink petals. Limniris ensata plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Its roots help stabilize the sediment, contributing to water clarity and ecological balance.
