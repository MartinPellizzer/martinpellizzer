Lupinus maculatus

Lupinus maculatus, commonly known as the spotted lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of blue or violet pea-shaped flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, rounded leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Lupinus maculatus is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
