Limeum fruticosum

Limeum fruticosum, commonly known as the creeping thyme, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features small, fragrant leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant produces tiny white or pink flowers in the spring, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its woody stems and aromatic foliage make it a popular choice for rock gardens and as a ground cover.

Limeum fruticosum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy and versatile plant for various garden settings.
