Litsea salicifolia

Litsea salicifolia, commonly known as Chinese tallowtree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its opposite, simple, and serrated leaves that resemble willow leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by orange-yellow berries that contain smooth, oval seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other regions, sometimes becoming invasive.

The species is also valued for its wood, which is lightweight and used in various traditional applications.
