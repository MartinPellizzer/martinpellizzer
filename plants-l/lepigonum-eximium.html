Lepigonum eximium

Lepigonum eximium, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, rough, and often covered in fine hairs.

Lepigonum eximium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
