Lucuma pauciflora var. elliptica

Lucuma pauciflora var. elliptica, commonly known as the elliptic lucuma, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a small, evergreen tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 5 meters. The plant produces yellow-orange fruits that are rich in nutrients and have a sweet, creamy texture. Its leaves are elliptical in shape, leathery, and arranged alternately along the branches.

The plant thrives in cool, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.
