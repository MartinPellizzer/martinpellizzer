Lycopus sinuatus

Lycopus sinuatus, commonly known as narrow-leaved bugleweed, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant features opposite, narrow, serrated leaves and produces small, blue to purple flowers in whorled clusters. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Lycopus sinuatus is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
