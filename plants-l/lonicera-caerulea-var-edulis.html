Lonicera caerulea var. edulis

Lonicera caerulea var. edulis, commonly known as the edible honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It produces small, fragrant flowers that transition into berries, which are typically red or yellow when ripe. The plant is valued for its edible fruits, which are rich in antioxidants and have a sweet, tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for gardens and small-scale cultivation.

The berries are often used in jams, juices, and desserts due to their unique taste and nutritional benefits.
