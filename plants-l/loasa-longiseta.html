Loasa longiseta

Loasa longiseta, commonly known as the long-stemmed loasa, is a perennial shrub native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces, and its stems are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Loasa longiseta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
