Lepidium heterophyllum var. canescens

Lepidium heterophyllum var. canescens, commonly known as the hairy cress, is a herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and pinnately divided leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. It has a compact growth habit with hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name.

The species is part of the Brassicaceae family, known for its mustard-like flowers and edible seeds.
