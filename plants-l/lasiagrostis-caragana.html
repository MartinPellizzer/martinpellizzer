Lasiagrostis caragana

Lasiagrostis caragana, commonly known as the Caragana shrub, is a perennial plant native to the cold climates of Central Asia and Siberia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is known for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant is often used for erosion control due to its extensive root system.

Lasiagrostis caragana is also valued for its hardy nature and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
