Lathyrus dolichos

Lathyrus dolichos, commonly known as the long bean or horse gram, is a climbing legume native to the Indian subcontinent.

It produces distinctive, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, followed by elongated pods containing small, kidney-shaped seeds. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and sturdy, twining stems that allow it to climb. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional seeds, which are rich in protein.

Lathyrus dolichos is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in agriculture and cuisine.
