Lepanthes horrida

Lepanthes horrida, commonly known as the horrid lady's tresses, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and its distinctive, long, spiraling inflorescence of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments, often epiphytic on tree trunks or branches. Its flowers bloom during the spring and summer months, attracting various pollinators such as bees and moths.

Lepanthes horrida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
