Lycium americanum var. chrysocarpum

Lycium americanum var. chrysocarpum, commonly known as the golden fruit lycium, is a deciduous shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It features small, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow-orange berries that are rich in antioxidants. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates. Its berries are edible and have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in landscaping.
