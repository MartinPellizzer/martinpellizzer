Leipoldtia framesii

Leipoldtia framesii, commonly known as the Karoo sandveld orchid, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically greenish-yellow with a faint purple tinge. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
