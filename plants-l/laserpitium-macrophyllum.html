Laserpitium macrophyllum

Laserpitium macrophyllum, commonly known as the large-leaved hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads composed of many small florets. The plant features robust, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped basal leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy yellow flowers are typically arranged in a single terminal head, with ray florets that give it a daisy-like appearance.

Laserpitium macrophyllum is known for its ability to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes.
