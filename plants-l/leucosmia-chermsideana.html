Leucosmia chermsideana

Leucosmia chermsideana, commonly known as the Christmas lily, is a rare and striking orchid native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It features elegant, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring, often around the Christmas season, hence its common name. The plant has strap-like, dark green leaves that are narrow and arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its limited natural habitat and threats from habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species.
