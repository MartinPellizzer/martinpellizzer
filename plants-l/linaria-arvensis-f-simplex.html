Linaria arvensis f. simplex

Linaria arvensis f. simplex, commonly known as the simple weedflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features simple, opposite leaves that are often hairy and have a lanceolate shape. Its flowers are yellow with two lips, forming a distinctive two-lipped structure typical of the genus Linaria.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and gardens.
