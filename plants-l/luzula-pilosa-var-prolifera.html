Luzula pilosa var. prolifera

Luzula pilosa var. prolifera, commonly known as the hairy woodrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its dense, clump-forming growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, greenish-white flowers.

Luzula pilosa var. prolifera is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
