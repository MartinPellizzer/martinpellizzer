Litsea vang var. lobata

Litsea vang var. lobata, commonly known as the broadleaf litsea, is a variant of the Litsea genus native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub characterized by its simple, alternate, and ovate to elliptic leaves. The leaves are typically glossy green on the upper surface and lighter green underneath, with a smooth margin. It produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles, followed by small, dark blue berries.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic foliage and aromatic qualities.
