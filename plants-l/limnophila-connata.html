Limnophila connata

Limnophila connata, commonly known as the water hyssop, is a semi-aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in wetlands, ponds, and stream edges. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of purple or pink. Its root system helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion in aquatic environments.

Limnophila connata is also used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.
