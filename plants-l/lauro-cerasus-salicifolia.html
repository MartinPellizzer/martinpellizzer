Lauro-cerasus salicifolia

Lauro-cerasus salicifolia, commonly known as the cherry laurel, is a species of flowering plant in the Prunus genus.

It is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by dark purple berries. It is native to Europe and western Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Lauro-cerasus salicifolia is also valued for its fragrant foliage and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
