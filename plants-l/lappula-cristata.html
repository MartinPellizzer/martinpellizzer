Lappula cristata

Lappula cristata, commonly known as the crowned lappula, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant has a basal rosette of leaves and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a raceme. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

Lappula cristata is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in some agricultural settings.
