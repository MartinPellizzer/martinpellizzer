Lotus anfractuosus var. anfractuosus

Lotus anfractuosus var. anfractuosus, commonly known as the mountain lotus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant features palmate leaves with lobed margins, adapted to withstand harsh mountain climates. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
