Limonium bicolor

Limonium bicolor, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its blue or purple flowers and spiky, silver-green foliage. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in saline conditions. Limonium bicolor is a member of the Plumbagaceae family and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Its flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
