Lingoum esculentum

Lingoum esculentum, commonly known as the edible lily, is a herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It features strap-like, lance-shaped leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces tall, slender stems that bear clusters of white or pale pink flowers. Its edible parts include the tender leaves and young flower buds, which are used in various culinary preparations.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.
