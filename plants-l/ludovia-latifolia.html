Ludovia latifolia

Ludovia latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf ludovia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom during the wet season. Ludovia latifolia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a humid climate to flourish.

It is often found in dense undergrowth of rainforests and is valued for its ornamental qualities.
