Lactuca numidica

Lactuca numidica, commonly known as wild endive, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe and North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring hairy stems and lobed, dark green leaves. Its flowers are yellow and occur in dense clusters, attracting various pollinators.

The leaves are often used in salads, though they have a slightly bitter taste.
