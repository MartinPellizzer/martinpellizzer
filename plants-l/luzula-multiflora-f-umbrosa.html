Luzula multiflora f. umbrosa

Luzula multiflora f. umbrosa, commonly known as the shaded wood sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses and sedges, and is characterized by its clump-forming growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of understory vegetation in forest ecosystems.
