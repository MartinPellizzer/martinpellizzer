Lycopsis obtusifolia

Lycopsis obtusifolia, commonly known as the blunt-leaf lycopsis, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lycopsidaceae.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. It is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes.

Lycopsis obtusifolia is a perennial plant that plays a minor role in local ecosystems but is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological niche.
