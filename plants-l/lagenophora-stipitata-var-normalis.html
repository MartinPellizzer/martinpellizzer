Lagenophora stipitata var. normalis

Lagenophora stipitata var. normalis, commonly known as the slender-stemmed bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, branching stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly rough texture.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.
