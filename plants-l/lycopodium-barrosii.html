Lycopodium barrosii

Lycopodium barrosii, commonly known as the Barros' clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. The plant features whorled leaves that are narrow and strap-like, with a distinctive spiral arrangement. It produces sporophylls that bear spores, which are released into the air for dispersal.

Lycopodium barrosii plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and small invertebrates.
