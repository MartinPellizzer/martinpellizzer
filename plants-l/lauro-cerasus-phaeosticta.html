Lauro-cerasus phaeosticta

Lauro-cerasus phaeosticta, commonly known as the cherry laurel, is a species of flowering plant in the Prunus genus.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. Native to parts of Asia, it is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.

Its leaves are evergreen, and the plant is often used in landscaping for its dense growth habit.
