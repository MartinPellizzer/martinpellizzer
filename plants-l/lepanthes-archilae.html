Lepanthes archilae

Lepanthes archilae, commonly known as the golden star orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow petals and a distinctive, star-shaped lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in late spring and early summer, adding vibrant color to the forest canopy.

Lepanthes archilae is valued for its ornamental beauty and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its native habitat.
