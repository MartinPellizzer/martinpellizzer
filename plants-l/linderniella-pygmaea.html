Linderniella pygmaea

Linderniella pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf lindernia, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences and are usually pale blue or lavender in color.

Linderniella pygmaea is adapted to drought conditions and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.
