Lundia truncata

Lundia truncata, commonly known as the African bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from purple to blue. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Lundia truncata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
