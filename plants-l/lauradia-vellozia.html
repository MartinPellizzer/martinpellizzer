Lauradia vellozia

Lauradia vellozia, commonly known as the Brazilian mahogany, is a deciduous tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes other aromatic plants like cinnamon and avocado. The tree is characterized by its smooth, dark-brown bark and elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by edible, red berries.

Lauradia vellozia is valued for its hard, durable wood, which has been traditionally used in furniture and construction.
