Leucospermum ellipticum

Leucospermum ellipticum, commonly known as the Cape goldfix, is a South African native plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers, which are borne in dense, spherical clusters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

Leucospermum ellipticum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
