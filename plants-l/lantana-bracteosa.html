Lantana bracteosa

Lantana bracteosa, commonly known as the silverleaf lantana, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a silvery underside. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to yellow, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Lantana bracteosa is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardy nature and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for both gardeners and ecological studies.
