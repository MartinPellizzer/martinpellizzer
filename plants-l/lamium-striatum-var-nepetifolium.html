Lamium striatum var. nepetifolium

Lamium striatum var. nepetifolium, commonly known as the spotted deadnettle, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive white or pale green markings along the veins. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of pink or purple, typically arranged in whorls along the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable plant in ecological gardens.
