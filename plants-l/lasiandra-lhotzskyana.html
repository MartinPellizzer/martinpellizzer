Lasiandra lhotzskyana

Lasiandra lhotzskyana, commonly known as the rock daisy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for alpine environments. Its leaves are usually hairy and arranged in a basal rosette, providing a dense cover that helps retain moisture.

Lasiandra lhotzskyana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh conditions.
