Lycopus europaeus var. trichophora

Lycopus europaeus var. trichophora, commonly known as European water harebell, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Geraniaceae family and typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are hairy on the underside. Its flowers are small, blue or purple, and clustered in dense, bell-shaped inflorescences.

It is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported calming and antispasmodic properties.
