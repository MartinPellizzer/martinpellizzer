Lasiosiphon lampranthus

Lasiosiphon lampranthus, commonly known as the golden flame lily, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a fiery display. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its unique floral structure and vibrant color make it a standout in both cultivated and natural settings.
