Litsea resinosa

Litsea resinosa, commonly known as the fragrant leaf tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, leathery leaves that emit a strong, spicy scent when crushed. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are followed by oval, woody fruits containing a single seed.

Litsea resinosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine and as a source of aromatic compounds.
