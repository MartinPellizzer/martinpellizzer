Lycopodium × buttersii

Lycopodium × buttersii, commonly known as the buttercup lycopod, is a hybrid fern that exhibits unique botanical features.

It belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae and is characterized by its spore-bearing cones and needle-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its sporophyte stage is distinguished by the presence of sporangia arranged in clusters on the stems.

Lycopodium × buttersii is valued for its ecological role in soil stabilization and as a habitat for various microorganisms.
