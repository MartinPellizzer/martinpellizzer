Lemma minuta

Lemma minuta, commonly known as the tiny lemma, is a small, annual grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, narrow leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in open fields or along roadsides. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.

Botanically, it is notable for its simple morphology and ecological adaptability.
