Lissochilus thomsonii

Lissochilus thomsonii, commonly known as Thomson's beard-leaf, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, hairy leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or mountainous areas. Its unique morphology and habitat make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
