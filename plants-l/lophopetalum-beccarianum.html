Lophopetalum beccarianum

Lophopetalum beccarianum, commonly known as the Beccari's hibiscus, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features showy, pentagonal flowers with five petals, often in shades of yellow or red. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and usually ovate or elliptic in shape.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.
