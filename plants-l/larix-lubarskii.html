Larix lubarskii

Larix lubarskii, commonly known as the Siberian larch, is a species of deciduous conifer native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its needle-like leaves, which turn golden before falling in autumn, giving the tree a striking appearance. The tree produces small, woody cones that contain seeds dispersed by wind. It thrives in cold, dry climates and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in construction and furniture-making.
