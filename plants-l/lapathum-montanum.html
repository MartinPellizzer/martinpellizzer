Lapathum montanum

Lapathum montanum, commonly known as mountain sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in mountainous or alpine environments. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters. It belongs to the family Polygonaceae, which includes other plants like buckwheat and sorrel.

Lapathum montanum is valued for its edible leaves, which have a tangy, sour flavor.
