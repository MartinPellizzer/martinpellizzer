Lasianthus hispidus

Lasianthus hispidus, commonly known as the hairy lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or violet in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
