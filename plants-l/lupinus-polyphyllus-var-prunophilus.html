Lupinus polyphyllus var. prunophilus

Lupinus polyphyllus var. prunophilus, commonly known as the Prunus-loving lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, branched stems and clusters of pea-like flowers in shades of purple and pink. The plant has compound leaves with multiple pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and rocky slopes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
