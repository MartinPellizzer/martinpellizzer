Lonicera involucrata var. ledebourii

Lonicera involucrata var. ledebourii, commonly known as the desert honeysuckle, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features slender, aromatic stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a glossy green surface with a slightly hairy underside.

This drought-tolerant species is well adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soils.
