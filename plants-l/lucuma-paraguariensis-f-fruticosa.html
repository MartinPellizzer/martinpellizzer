Lucuma paraguariensis f. fruticosa

Lucuma paraguariensis f. fruticosa, commonly known as the Paraguayan lucuma, is a shrub-like variant of the lucuma plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, branching structure and is cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruits. The plant produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are rich in nutrients and have a sweet, nutty flavor. Its botanical features include hairy leaves and clusters of small, white flowers.

This form is often used in horticulture for its hardiness and adaptability to various growing conditions.
