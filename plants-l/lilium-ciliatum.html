Lilium ciliatum

Lilium ciliatum, commonly known as the fringed lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive fringed appearance along the edges.

Lilium ciliatum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking blooms.
