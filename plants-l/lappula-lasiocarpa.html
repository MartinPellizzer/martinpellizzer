Lappula lasiocarpa

Lappula lasiocarpa, commonly known as the rough lappula, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in early summer. Its fruit is a dry, dehiscent silique containing several seeds.

Lappula lasiocarpa is typically found in disturbed soils and can be a common weed in agricultural fields.
