Lisianthius viridiflorus

Lisianthius viridiflorus, commonly known as the greenflower lisianthus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, and its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun exposure, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
