Lonicera deflexicalyx var. xerocalyx

Lonicera deflexicalyx var. xerocalyx, commonly known as the snowberry honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by orange-red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. The plant has slender, arching stems and elliptical leaves with a glossy green surface. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions if not managed properly.
