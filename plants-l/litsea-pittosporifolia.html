Litsea pittosporifolia

Litsea pittosporifolia, commonly known as the sweet-scented litsea, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes other aromatic plants such as laurel and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually glossy and emit a pleasant fragrance when crushed. It produces small, white flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences, followed by small, fleshy berries.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine for its aromatic and therapeutic properties.
