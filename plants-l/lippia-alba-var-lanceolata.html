Lippia alba var. lanceolata

Lippia alba var. lanceolata, commonly known as silverleaf lippia, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping and ornamental gardens.

Its essential oils are often used in traditional medicine and aromatherapy for their antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.
