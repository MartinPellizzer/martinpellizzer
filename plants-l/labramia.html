Labramia

Labramia, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, blue to purple flower heads that resemble mist. The plant typically grows in moist, shady habitats such as woodlands and streambanks. Labramia has alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

It produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into fluffy, white seed heads, aiding in wind pollination.
