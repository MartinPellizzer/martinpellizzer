Ligusticum arafoe

Ligusticum arafoe, commonly known as the Armenian lovage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with fern-like leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in umbels. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
