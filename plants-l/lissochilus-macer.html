Lissochilus macer

Lissochilus macer, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, elongated stems and small, pale green to white flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or forested areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged along the stem, contributing to its overall slender appearance.

Lissochilus macer is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in specialized horticultural settings.
