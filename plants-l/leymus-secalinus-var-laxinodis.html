Leymus secalinus var. laxinodis

Leymus secalinus var. laxinodis, commonly known as the purple wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces spikelets with purple to reddish bracts, giving it its distinctive appearance. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed sites.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is cultivated in gardens and used for erosion control.
