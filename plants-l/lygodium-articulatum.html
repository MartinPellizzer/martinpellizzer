Lygodium articulatum

Lygodium articulatum, commonly known as the climbing fern, is a tropical fern species characterized by its unique, segmented fronds.

It belongs to the family Lygodiaceae and is native to regions across Asia, Africa, and the Pacific Islands. The plant exhibits a climbing growth habit, using its wiry stems to ascend trees and other structures. Its fronds are divided into numerous small, lance-shaped segments, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Lygodium articulatum thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
