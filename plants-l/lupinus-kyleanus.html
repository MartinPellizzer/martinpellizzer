Lupinus kyleanus

Lupinus kyleanus, commonly known as Kyle's lupine, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and clusters of pea-like flowers that range in color from purple to white. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Lupinus kyleanus is an important plant for pollinators and is also valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation.
