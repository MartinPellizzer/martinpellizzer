Lepicystis

Lepicystis, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its showy yellow flowers and hairy, often glandular leaves. The plants typically grow in dry, rocky soils and are found in various regions of the Americas. Lepicystis species are generally herbaceous, with some varieties producing small, fleshy fruits.

They are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes used in traditional medicine.
