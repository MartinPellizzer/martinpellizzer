Lychnis coeli-rosa var. pusilla

Lychnis coeli-rosa var. pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf flowering cranesbill, is a small perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of delicate, pink or white flowers resembling tiny bells. The leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

This variety is valued for its compact growth and prolonged blooming period, typically from late spring to early summer.
