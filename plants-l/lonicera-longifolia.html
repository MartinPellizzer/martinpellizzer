Lonicera longifolia

Lonicera longifolia, commonly known as the sweet flag or Japanese honeysuckle, is a deciduous vine native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a red, fleshy drupe that contains seeds.

Lonicera longifolia is often found in moist, shaded areas and can be invasive in some regions.
