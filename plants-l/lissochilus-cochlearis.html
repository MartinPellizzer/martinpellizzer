Lissochilus cochlearis

Lissochilus cochlearis, commonly known as the spiral orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique spiral-shaped inflorescence, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in cool, humid conditions. Its flowers are small, usually white or pale pink, and are arranged in a tight, curling pattern.

Lissochilus cochlearis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
