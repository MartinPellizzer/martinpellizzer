Lavandula × cadevallii

Lavandula × cadevallii, commonly known as the Spanish lavender, is a hybrid shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features silver-gray foliage and produces spikes of purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping. It has a strong aromatic scent and is often used in aromatherapy and herbal remedies.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for both garden landscapes and container planting.
