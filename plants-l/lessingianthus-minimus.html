Lessingianthus minimus

Lessingianthus minimus, commonly known as the small lessingia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a herbaceous annual that grows up to 30 centimeters tall, featuring slender stems and small, hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its ornamental value and compact size, it is often cultivated by gardeners interested in native flora.
