Leymus karataviensis

Leymus karataviensis, commonly known as the Karatavinskaya wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. This plant is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and can thrive in environments with low rainfall. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion in its native habitat.

Leymus karataviensis is also valued for its potential use in forage and ecological restoration projects.
