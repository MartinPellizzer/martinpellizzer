Linum simsii

Linum simsii, commonly known as the Persian flax or blue flax, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the genus Linum in the family Fabaceae, which includes other flax species. The plant is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Linum simsii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
