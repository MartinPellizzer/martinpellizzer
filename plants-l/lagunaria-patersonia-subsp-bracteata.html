Lagunaria patersonia subsp. bracteata

Lagunaria patersonia subsp.

bracteata, commonly known as the bracteate sea hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Australia. It is characterized by its distinctive, showy yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along the shoreline, where it thrives in saline environments. Its leaves are alternate and palmately lobed, often with a hairy texture.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes.
