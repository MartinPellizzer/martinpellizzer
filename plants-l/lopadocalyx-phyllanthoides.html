Lopadocalyx phyllanthoides

Lopadocalyx phyllanthoides, commonly known as the silverleaf stinkwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a waxy coating. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, round, red or orange fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and forested areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
