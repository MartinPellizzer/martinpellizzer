Limonium globulariifolium subsp. lusitanicum

Limonium globulariifolium subsp.

lusitanicum, commonly known as the Lusitanian sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its compact, globular growth habit and striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is typically found in coastal and sandy habitats. It is well adapted to saline and well-drained soils, often growing in dunes or along the edges of salt marshes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
