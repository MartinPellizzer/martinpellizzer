Lippia canescens

Lippia canescens, commonly known as silverleaf lippia, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny environments and is often found in grasslands and shrublands. It has aromatic leaves that are used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cuisines.

Lippia canescens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
