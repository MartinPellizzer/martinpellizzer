Lagarinthus peltigerus

Lagarinthus peltigerus, commonly known as the velvetleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from purple to blue, often with a velvety texture. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically hairy, contributing to its common name. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

The species is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
