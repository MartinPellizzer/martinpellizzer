Lathyrus tingitanus var. coruscans

Lathyrus tingitanus var. coruscans, commonly known as the glittering vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from blue to violet. The leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant produces small, elongated pods containing kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
