Lantana cubensis

Lantana cubensis, commonly known as the Cuban lantana, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, including Cuba, the Bahamas, and parts of Central America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 1 to 5 meters, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red, which bloom throughout the warm seasons. Lantana cubensis is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in full sun, making it a common ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
