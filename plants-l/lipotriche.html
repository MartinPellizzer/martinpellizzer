Lipotriche

Lipotriche, commonly known as the "crown vetch," is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three small, heart-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple, depending on the species. Lipotriche species are typically shrubs or perennial herbs, often found in temperate regions.

These plants are valued for their ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making them useful in ecological restoration projects.
