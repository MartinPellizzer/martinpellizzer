Lopezia

Lopezia, commonly known as the "lanceleaf desert lily," is a genus of flowering plants native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

These plants are characterized by their narrow, lance-shaped leaves and showy, often fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white, yellow, and pink. Lopezia species are typically perennial herbs that thrive in well-drained soils and full sun, adapting to harsh desert conditions. They play an important role in their ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to their unique appearance and ecological significance, Lopezia plants are often cultivated in xeriscapes and botanical gardens.
