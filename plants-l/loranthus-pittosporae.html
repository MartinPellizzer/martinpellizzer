Loranthus pittosporae

Loranthus pittosporae, commonly known as the Pittosporum mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the stems of its host, typically Pittosporum species.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own carbohydrates through photosynthesis using light captured by its leaves. The plant forms a dense, leafy crown that can grow up to several meters in length, often creating a tangled mass on the host tree. It is native to Australia and is often found in eucalyptus forests.

The berries it produces are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.
