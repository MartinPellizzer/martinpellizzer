Loranthus latifolius

Loranthus latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches itself to host trees such as eucalyptus and acacia.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using the light it captures. The plant has small, leathery leaves and produces white or yellowish flowers that are often visited by insects. Its berries are red or orange and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This species is found in various parts of Australia and is an important part of the ecosystem, supporting bird life and contributing to the biodiversity of its habitat.
