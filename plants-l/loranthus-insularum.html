Loranthus insularum

Loranthus insularum, commonly known as the island mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own carbohydrates through photosynthesis using the light it captures. The plant has small, scale-like leaves and produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are often clustered together. Its fruit is a fleshy, red berry that contains seeds covered in a sticky substance to aid in attachment to potential host trees.

Loranthus insularum plays a role in ecological systems by influencing host tree growth and providing food for certain bird species.
