Lepidium montanum subsp. davisii

Lepidium montanum subsp.

davisii, commonly known as Davis's cress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, herbaceous habit. It typically features small, bright yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in early spring.

The plant has alternate, linear to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine, glandular hairs. It is native to the western United States, particularly in regions with moist, rocky soils.

Davis's cress is often found in open woodlands and along streambanks, where it thrives in partial shade to full sun.
