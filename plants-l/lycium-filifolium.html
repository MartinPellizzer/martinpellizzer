Lycium filifolium

Lycium filifolium, commonly known as the threadleaf boxthorn, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It features small, needle-like leaves that are densely packed, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for humans and wildlife. It is adapted to survive in harsh conditions with minimal water requirements.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important plant in ecological restoration projects.
