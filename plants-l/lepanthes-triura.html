Lepanthes triura

Lepanthes triura, commonly known as the Three-lobed Orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments within the understory of tropical forests. The plant features delicate, strap-shaped leaves and produces fragrant, three-lobed flowers in shades of white and pink. Its flowers are often adorned with intricate patterns and are pollinated by specific insect species.

Lepanthes triura is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
