Leptadenia lancifolia var. scabra

Leptadenia lancifolia var. scabra, commonly known as rough-leaved arrowroot, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, rough-textured leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by capsule fruits containing minute, elongated seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

This species is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
