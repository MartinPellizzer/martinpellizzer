Lamium corsicum

Lamium corsicum, commonly known as Corsican dead-nettle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks. Its flowers bloom from spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Lamium corsicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
