Lilicella

Lilicella, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a species native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a deciduous tree that typically grows to a height of 10 to 20 meters. The leaves are alternate, simple, and have a distinctive silvery underside, giving the plant its common name. The flowers are small, clustered, and often white or pale yellow, while the fruit is a capsule containing several seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
