Lobelia pleotricha var. cacumiflora

Lobelia pleotricha var. cacumiflora, commonly known as the showy lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland edges and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
