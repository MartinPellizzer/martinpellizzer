Litsea rufa

Litsea rufa, commonly known as the red-flowered litsea, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white or red flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture with a strong aromatic scent.

Litsea rufa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
