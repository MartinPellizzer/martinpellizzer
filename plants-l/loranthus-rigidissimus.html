Loranthus rigidissimus

Loranthus rigidissimus, commonly known as the rigid mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using light reflected from the host. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds. It is native to regions of Asia, including China and Japan, and is often found on trees such as poplars and willows.

Its parasitic nature and unique ecological role make it an interesting subject in botanical studies.
