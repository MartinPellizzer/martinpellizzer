Leucadendron glaberrimum subsp. erubescens

Leucadendron glaberrimum subsp.

erubescens, commonly known as the red-stemmed sugar bush, is a shrub native to South Africa. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and striking red or orange flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant typically grows in coastal fynbos vegetation, thriving in well-drained, sandy soils. Its leaves are leathery and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are distinctly reddish in color.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
