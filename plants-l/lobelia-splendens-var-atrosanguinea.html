Lobelia splendens var. atrosanguinea

Lobelia splendens var. atrosanguinea, commonly known as the black blood lobelia, is a striking perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features dark purple to almost black flowers that contrast beautifully with its dark green foliage. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 feet, producing clusters of tubular flowers that attract pollinators like hummingbirds and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This variety is well-suited for woodland gardens and moist, shaded environments.
