Linaria frutescens

Linaria frutescens, commonly known as the common toadflax, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial that produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers with a distinctive spurred shape. The plant has thin, branching stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Linaria frutescens is known for its ornamental value and has been introduced to various parts of the world as a garden plant.
