Leptinella intricata

Leptinella intricata, commonly known as the intricate snowbell, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue, which bloom in early spring. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are densely packed along the stems. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

Leptinella intricata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and delicate floral display.
