Lonicera pyrenaica subsp. pyrenaica

Lonicera pyrenaica subsp.

pyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean honeysuckle, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula. It features opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are typically oval to elliptic in shape.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually yellow or orange, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments and is adapted to rocky or dry soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
