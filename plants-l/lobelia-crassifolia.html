Lobelia crassifolia

Lobelia crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is often found in the southeastern regions of the U.S. Lobelia crassifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
