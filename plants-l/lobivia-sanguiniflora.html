Lobivia sanguiniflora

Lobivia sanguiniflora, commonly known as the blood-flowered lobivia, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and thick, ribbed stems that store water. The plant produces striking red or pink flowers during the warmer months, which are often described as resembling blood. Its leaves are modified into spines, providing protection from herbivores and reducing water loss.

Lobivia sanguiniflora is well adapted to harsh desert environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil to thrive.
