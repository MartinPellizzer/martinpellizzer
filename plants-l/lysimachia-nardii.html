Lysimachia nardii

Lysimachia nardii, commonly known as sweet William or winter jasmine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves appear. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Lysimachia nardii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
