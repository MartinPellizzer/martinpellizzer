Lepidium suffruticosum

Lepidium suffruticosum, commonly known as wild cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous annual that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes, and its leaves are alternate, often lobed or toothed. It produces small, rounded fruits called siliques, which contain several seeds.

Lepidium suffruticosum is commonly found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in temperate climates.
