Lepidium meyenii f. rhombicum

Lepidium meyenii f. rhombicum, commonly known as the maca root, is a variant of the maca plant native to the high Andes of Peru.

It is characterized by its rhombic-shaped root system, which is typically harvested for its nutritional and medicinal properties. The plant belongs to the Brassicaceae family and thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures. Its roots are rich in bioactive compounds, including alkaloids, flavonoids, and carbohydrates.

This variety is cultivated for its potential health benefits and is used in traditional medicine and as a dietary supplement.
