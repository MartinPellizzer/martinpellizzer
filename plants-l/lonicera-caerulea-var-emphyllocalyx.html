Lonicera caerulea var. emphyllocalyx

Lonicera caerulea var. emphyllocalyx, commonly known as the honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant's leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge. It bears small, red or orange berries that are edible and often used in jams and wines.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in traditional medicine.
