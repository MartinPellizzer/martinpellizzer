Lycopersicon esculentum var. racemigerum

Lycopersicon esculentum var. racemigerum, commonly known as the potato tomato, is a botanical variant of the domesticated tomato.

It is characterized by its unique growth habit, where it produces a racemose inflorescence, resulting in a cluster of flowers and fruits along the stem. This variety is notable for its potential in breeding programs due to its genetic diversity and unique traits. The plant typically exhibits a more compact growth form compared to standard tomatoes, making it suitable for smaller garden spaces.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for agricultural research and horticultural experimentation.
