Lepanthes herpaga

Lepanthes herpaga, commonly known as the Herpaga orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its leaves are usually broad and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

Lepanthes herpaga is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
