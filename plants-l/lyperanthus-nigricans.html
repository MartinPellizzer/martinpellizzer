Lyperanthus nigricans

Lyperanthus nigricans, commonly known as the black-flowered bell heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen shrub form. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically dark purple or black, which gives it its common name. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
