Laportea annamica

Laportea annamica, commonly known as the Vietnamese false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers. The plant has hairy stems and leaves with a distinctive, aromatic scent. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Laportea annamica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
