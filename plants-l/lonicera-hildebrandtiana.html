Lonicera hildebrandtiana

Lonicera hildebrandtiana, commonly known as the Hildebrandt honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, blooming in late spring. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that ripens in the fall.

Lonicera hildebrandtiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and insects.
