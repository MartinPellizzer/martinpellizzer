Lockhartia parthenocomos var. crispula

Lockhartia parthenocomos var. crispula, commonly known as the Crisped Lockhartia, is a rare and unique orchid species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, velvety leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom in clusters along the stems. Its foliage is notable for the distinctive, wavy edges that give it its varietal name, "crispula.".

This orchid thrives in humid, shaded environments and is prized by collectors for its ornamental value and rarity.
