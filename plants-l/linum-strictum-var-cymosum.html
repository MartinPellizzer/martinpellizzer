Linum strictum var. cymosum

Linum strictum var. cymosum, commonly known as the clustered flax, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that are often branched and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that are clustered in cymes, giving it its varietal name. Its leaves are alternate, linear, and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in textile production.
