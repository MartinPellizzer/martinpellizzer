Leucas cephalantha

Leucas cephalantha, commonly known as the white leucas or mountain leucas, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered white or pale pink flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Leucas cephalantha is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
