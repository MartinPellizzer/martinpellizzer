Lychnis himalayensis

Lychnis himalayensis, commonly known as the Himalayan fireweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the carnation family, Caryophyllaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils at high altitudes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in clusters at the tips of the stems.

Lychnis himalayensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
