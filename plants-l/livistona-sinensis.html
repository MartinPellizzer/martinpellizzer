Livistona sinensis

Livistona sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese areca palm, is a species of palm native to southern China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by edible fruits resembling small dates. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Livistona sinensis is also valued for its ability to tolerate shade and its use in traditional medicine.
