Liatris squarrulosa

Liatris squarrulosa, commonly known as blazing star, is a herbaceous perennial native to the central and eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, spiky flower heads that range in color from pale pink to deep purple. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Liatris squarrulosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
