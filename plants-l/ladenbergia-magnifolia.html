Ladenbergia magnifolia

Ladenbergia magnifolia, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a vibrant display during the warmer months. The plant has pinnate leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets, and its bark is smooth with a reddish-brown color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The species is also valued for its rapid growth and ability to provide shade in tropical landscapes.
