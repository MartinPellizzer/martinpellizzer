Ligularia virgaurea var. pilosa

Ligularia virgaurea var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy ligularia, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern Asia.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive hairy texture on the upper surface. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers in late summer, which are clustered in dense, terminal inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its foliage is popular in ornamental horticulture for its striking visual appeal.
