Lepanthes rodrigogonzalesii

Lepanthes rodrigogonzalesii, commonly known as the Rodriguez-Gonzales orchid, is a rare and endemic species native to the cloud forests of Mexico.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, intricate flowers that typically feature a combination of white, green, and yellow hues. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks in the understory of tropical forests. Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and limited distribution, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss and its restricted range, it is considered vulnerable and requires careful preservation efforts.
