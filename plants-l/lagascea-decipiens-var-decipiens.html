Lagascea decipiens var. decipiens

Lagascea decipiens var. decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive lagascea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant features slender, branching stems covered in soft, hairy foliage. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with tubular corollas that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
