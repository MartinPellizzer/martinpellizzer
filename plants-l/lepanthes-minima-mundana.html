Lepanthes minima-mundana

Lepanthes minima-mundana, commonly known as the miniature orchid, is a small, epiphytic orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pale pink. The plant has slender, wiry stems and small, strap-like leaves that help it thrive in shaded, humid environments. It is often found growing on trees or rocks in the understory of rainforests.

Due to its size and beauty, it is a popular species among orchid enthusiasts and conservationists.
