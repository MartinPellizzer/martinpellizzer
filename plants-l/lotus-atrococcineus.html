Lotus atrococcineus

Lotus atrococcineus, commonly known as the crimson lotus, is a striking aquatic plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy pink to red flowers that bloom on long, slender stalks above the water. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically submerged or floating, depending on the water conditions. Its roots are fibrous and help stabilize the plant in muddy substrates.

Lotus atrococcineus is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant blooms and unique appearance.
