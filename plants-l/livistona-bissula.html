Livistona bissula

Livistona bissula, commonly known as the Bissul palm, is a species of palm native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers followed by edible, sweet fruits. It thrives in coastal and sandy soils, often found in open forests and heathlands.

Livistona bissula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
