Ladanum verum

Ladanum verum, commonly known as true ladanum, is a member of the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, white flowers. The plant produces a resin that has been historically used in incense and traditional medicine. Its leaves are often used in perfumery due to their strong, spicy scent.

Ladanum verum thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
