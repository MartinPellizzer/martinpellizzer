Lycaste × luciani

Lycaste × luciani, commonly known as the Luciani's lycaste, is a hybrid orchid species that results from the cross between Lycaste meleagris and Lycaste mackenii.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central America, where it thrives in warm, humid climates with well-draining soil. The plant produces large, showy flowers in shades of pink and white, often with a fragrant aroma. It is a sympodial orchid, meaning it grows in a horizontal manner with multiple stems emerging from a central rhizome.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and unique floral characteristics.
