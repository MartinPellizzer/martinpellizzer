Lupinus andersonii var. fulcratus

Lupinus andersonii var. fulcratus, commonly known as the Anderson's lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets, and its stems are often hairy.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.
