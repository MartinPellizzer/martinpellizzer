Lacinaria graminifolia var. pilosa

Lacinaria graminifolia var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy grass lacinaria, is a herbaceous plant characterized by its fine, hairy texture on its stems and leaves.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is native to certain regions of South America. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are elongated and often have a serrated margin, while its flowers are small and clustered in composite heads.

The variety 'pilosa' is distinguished by its dense pubescence, which gives it a distinctive appearance and aids in moisture retention.
