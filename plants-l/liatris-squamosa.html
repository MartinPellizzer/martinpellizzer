Liatris squamosa

Liatris squamosa, commonly known as marsh blazing star, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescence composed of numerous purple to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as swamps, marshes, and wet meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with a dense layer of fine, white hairs.

Liatris squamosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
