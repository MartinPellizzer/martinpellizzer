Linanthus breviculus subsp. royalis

Linanthus breviculus subsp.

royalis, commonly known as the royal globemallow, is a small annual herb native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
