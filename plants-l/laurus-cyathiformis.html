Laurus cyathiformis

Laurus cyathiformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved laurel, is a species of evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other well-known plants like bay laurel and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible for humans and birds.

Laurus cyathiformis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic foliage.
