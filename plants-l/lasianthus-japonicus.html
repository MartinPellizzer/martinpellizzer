Lasianthus japonicus

Lasianthus japonicus, commonly known as the Japanese flameflower, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.
