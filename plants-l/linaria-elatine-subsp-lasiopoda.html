Linaria elatine subsp. lasiopoda

Linaria elatine subsp.

lasiopoda, commonly known as the narrowleaf toadflax, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture. This species is often found in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and along streambanks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
