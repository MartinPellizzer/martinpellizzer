Lemna parodiana

Lemna parodiana, commonly known as the water clover, is a small, free-floating aquatic plant belonging to the family Araceae.

It is characterized by its rosette-like structure of tiny, overlapping leaves that form a dense mat on the water surface. Lemna parodiana is often found in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, contributing to water filtration and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
