Lycopodium linifolium var. subaristatum

Lycopodium linifolium var. subaristatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved clubmoss, is a variant of the clubmoss plant characterized by its slender, linear leaves.

It is a member of the Lycopodiaceae family and is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or rocky crevices. The plant produces small, spore-bearing cones at the tips of its stems, which are essential for its reproduction. Its foliage is often dense and forms low-growing mats, contributing to the understory vegetation in its habitat.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in maintaining soil stability.
