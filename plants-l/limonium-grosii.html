Limonium grosii

Limonium grosii, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in coastal environments and is well adapted to salty, well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of salt.

Limonium grosii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh maritime conditions.
