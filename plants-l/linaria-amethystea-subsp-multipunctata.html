Linaria amethystea subsp. multipunctata

Linaria amethystea subsp.

multipunctata, commonly known as the spotted toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and vibrant yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer.

The leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, often with a bluish-green hue. This subspecies is distinguished by the presence of small, dark spots on its leaves, giving it its common name.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
