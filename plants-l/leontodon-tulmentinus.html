Leontodon tulmentinus

Leontodon tulmentinus, commonly known as the Alpine dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive pappus for wind dispersal.

Leontodon tulmentinus is known for its hardy nature and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
