Lonicera fragilis

Lonicera fragilis, commonly known as the honeysuckle vine, is a deciduous climbing shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, twining stems that allow it to climb and trail along supports. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to yellow, typically blooming in late spring. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Lonicera fragilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
