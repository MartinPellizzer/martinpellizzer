Laguna tricolor

Laguna tricolor, commonly known as the three-color water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tri-colored petals that range from deep blue to purple and white, creating a visually captivating display. The plant thrives in shallow, freshwater environments and plays a vital role in maintaining water quality by filtering pollutants. Its leaves are broad and floating, providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Laguna tricolor is also valued for its ornamental beauty in water gardens and ponds.
