Lycopodioides pteryphyllos

Lycopodioides pteryphyllos, commonly known as the winged clubmoss, is a member of the Lycopodiaceae family and is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves.

It is a herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or rocky crevices. The plant produces sporophytes with distinctive, club-shaped spore cones that are often covered in tiny, wing-like structures. Its reproductive structures are adapted for spore dispersal, which is a key feature of its life cycle.

Lycopodioides pteryphyllos plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
