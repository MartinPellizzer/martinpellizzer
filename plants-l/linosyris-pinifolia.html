Linosyris pinifolia

Linosyris pinifolia, commonly known as the needleleaf linosyris, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial that typically forms dense mats of foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are needle-like, hence the species name "pinifolia," and are adapted to reduce water loss in dry environments.

Linosyris pinifolia is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is well-suited to harsh, drought-prone climates.
