Lindera umbellata var. hypoglauca

Lindera umbellata var. hypoglauca, commonly known as the white-flowered sweet pepper, is a variant of the sweet pepper plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are typically elliptical with a smooth texture and a slightly aromatic scent. This plant prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

It is often found in woodland edges and moist habitats, contributing to local biodiversity.
