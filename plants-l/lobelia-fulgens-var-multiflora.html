Lobelia fulgens var. multiflora

Lobelia fulgens var. multiflora, commonly known as the firecracker lily, is a strikingly colorful plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, tubular red flowers that are clustered in racemes, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 3 feet, with lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are opposite along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
