Lathyrus luteus var. grandifolius

Lathyrus luteus var. grandifolius, commonly known as the giant yellow vetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces long, slender pods containing small, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
