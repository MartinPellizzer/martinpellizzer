Lepanthes tortilis

Lepanthes tortilis, commonly known as the curled lepanthe, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically display a mix of white, yellow, and green hues. The plant grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical rainforests. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

Lepanthes tortilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
