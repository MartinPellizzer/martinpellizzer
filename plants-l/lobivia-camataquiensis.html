Lobivia camataquiensis

Lobivia camataquiensis, commonly known as the Camataquiensis Lobivia, is a member of the Crassulaceae family native to South America.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its compact, rounded growth habit and fleshy, thick leaves that store water. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or purple during the warmer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
