Lasianthus rhinocerotis subsp. pedunculatus

Lasianthus rhinocerotis subsp.

pedunculatus, commonly known as the hornbill plant, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to purple.

The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.
