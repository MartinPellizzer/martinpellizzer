Lagochilus hindukushi

Lagochilus hindukushi, commonly known as Hindukush limestone hyssop, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit, hairy stems, and small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, often found in alpine and subalpine zones.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
