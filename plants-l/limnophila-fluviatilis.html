Limnophila fluviatilis

Limnophila fluviatilis, commonly known as water thyme, is a floating aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It has slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically 10–30 cm long and 1–2 cm wide, arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish or yellowish in color. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often forming dense mats on the water surface.

Limnophila fluviatilis is known for its rapid growth and ability to provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.
