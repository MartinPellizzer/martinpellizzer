Leptocereus undulosus

Leptocereus undulosus, commonly known as the Mexican jumping bean, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stem that can grow up to 1.5 meters in length. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the evening, followed by the development of fruiting bodies called "jumping beans.". These beans contain a larvae, which moves inside the pod, causing it to bounce when disturbed.

The plant is adapted to arid environments and is drought-tolerant, making it a hardy species in desert regions.
