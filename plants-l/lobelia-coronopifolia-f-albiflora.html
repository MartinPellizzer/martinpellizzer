Lobelia coronopifolia f. albiflora

Lobelia coronopifolia f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered cardinal flower, is a striking perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems covered in lance-shaped, dark green leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink, contrasting with its deep green foliage. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like hummingbirds and butterflies.
