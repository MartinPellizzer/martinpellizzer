Laxmannia acuta

Laxmannia acuta, commonly known as the sharpleaf marsh grass, is a flowering plant native to wetland and marsh environments in Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that typically bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in coastal areas and tidal zones. Laxmannia acuta plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
