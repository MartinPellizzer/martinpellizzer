Loxomorchis maculata

Loxomorchis maculata, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. The plant features a slender, upright stem with lance-shaped leaves that are often spotted or streaked with dark coloration. Its flowers are small and usually pale green or yellow, with a distinctive three-lobed lip.

Loxomorchis maculata is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
