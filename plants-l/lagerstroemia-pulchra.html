Lagerstroemia pulchra

Lagerstroemia pulchra, commonly known as the pink lacebark tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark that reveals layers of pink and white underneath, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pink flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, fuzzy fruits. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Lagerstroemia pulchra is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
