Linaria khorasanensis

Linaria khorasanensis, commonly known as the Persian toadflax, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It belongs to the genus Linaria in the family Plantaginaceae, characterized by its showy, brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its flowers are often yellow or orange, with a distinctive spurred shape at the base.

Linaria khorasanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
