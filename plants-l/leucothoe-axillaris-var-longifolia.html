Leucothoe axillaris var. longifolia

Leucothoe axillaris var. longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf leucothoe, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features dense, upright growth with small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide habitat for wildlife.
