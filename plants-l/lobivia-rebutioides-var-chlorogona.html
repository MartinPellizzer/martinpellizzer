Lobivia rebutioides var. chlorogona

Lobivia rebutioides var. chlorogona, commonly known as the green-flowered lobivia, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape with thick, ribbed stems. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its greenish-gray foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
