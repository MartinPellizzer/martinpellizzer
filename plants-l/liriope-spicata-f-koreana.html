Liriope spicata f. koreana

Liriope spicata f. koreana, commonly known as the Korean lilyturf, is a low-growing perennial plant native to East Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically evergreen and form dense clumps. The plant produces tall spikes of small, white or pale blue flowers in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and delicate floral display.
