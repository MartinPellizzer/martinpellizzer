Linum gallicum var. abyssinicum

Linum gallicum var. abyssinicum, commonly known as the Abyssinian flax, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous small, oval seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in rocky or arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
