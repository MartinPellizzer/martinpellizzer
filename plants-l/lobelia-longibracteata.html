Lobelia longibracteata

Lobelia longibracteata, commonly known as the long bract lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, tubular blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Lobelia longibracteata is valued for its ornamental appeal and serves as a food source for various pollinators.
