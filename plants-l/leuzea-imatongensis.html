Leuzea imatongensis

Leuzea imatongensis, commonly known as the Imatong grass, is a flowering plant native to the highland regions of South Sudan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous roots. The plant is known for its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and along riverbanks.

Due to its hardy nature, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
