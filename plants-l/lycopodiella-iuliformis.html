Lycopodiella iuliformis

Lycopodiella iuliformis, commonly known as the slender clubmoss, is a small, evergreen plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces small, scale-like leaves. The plant forms dense clusters of spore-bearing structures called strobila, which are typically found at the tips of its stems. L. iuliformis thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest floors or rocky crevices.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important component of certain temperate ecosystems.
