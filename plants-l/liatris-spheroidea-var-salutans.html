Liatris spheroidea var. salutans

Liatris spheroidea var. salutans, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features tall, upright stems that bear whorled clusters of lavender to purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
