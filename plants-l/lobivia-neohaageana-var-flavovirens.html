Lobivia neohaageana var. flavovirens

Lobivia neohaageana var. flavovirens, commonly known as the Golden Green Lobivia, is a compact, low-growing succulent native to South America.

It features thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically green with a subtle yellowish or golden hue. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for arid environments.

Its unique coloration and compact form make it a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.
