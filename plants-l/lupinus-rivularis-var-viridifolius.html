Lupinus rivularis var. viridifolius

Lupinus rivularis var. viridifolius, commonly known as the greenleaf water lupine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and pinnately compound leaves with a bright green coloration. The plant produces spikes of blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and riparian areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and native plant gardens.
