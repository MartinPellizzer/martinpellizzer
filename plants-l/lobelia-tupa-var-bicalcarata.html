Lobelia tupa var. bicalcarata

Lobelia tupa var. bicalcarata, commonly known as the purple mountain lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in dense clusters. The plant typically grows in rocky, high-altitude environments, preferring cool and moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable climatic conditions.
