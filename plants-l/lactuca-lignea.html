Lactuca lignea

Lactuca lignea, commonly known as woody lettuce, is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its woody stems and rough, hairy leaves that are typically lance-shaped. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, oval-shaped seeds. It is often found in dry, rocky soils and can thrive in disturbed habitats.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
