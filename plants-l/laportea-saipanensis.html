Laportea saipanensis

Laportea saipanensis, commonly known as the Saipan ironwood, is a flowering plant native to the island of Saipan in the Northern Mariana Islands.

It is a small shrub or tree that typically grows in coastal areas and rocky soils. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are ovate and slightly hairy, with a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed.

Laportea saipanensis is valued for its hardy nature and is often used in local landscaping due to its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
