Lantana chiapasensis

Lantana chiapasensis, commonly known as the Chiapas lantana, is a flowering plant native to Central America, particularly Mexico.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, brightly colored flowers, typically in shades of yellow, orange, or red. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. It is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various soil types.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions if not managed properly.
