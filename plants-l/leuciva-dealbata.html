Leuciva dealbata

Leuciva dealbata, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a species native to South Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with a rounded crown and smooth, silvery-gray bark. The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a distinctive silvery-white underside, giving the plant its common name. It produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by small, red, fleshy fruits.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to a variety of soil types and climatic conditions.
