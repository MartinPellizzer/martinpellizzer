Lavandula pedunculata

Lavandula pedunculata, commonly known as the Spanish lavender, is a perennial shrub native to southern Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that bear spikes of purple to pink flowers. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are gray-green in color, contributing to its drought-tolerant nature. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Lavandula pedunculata is valued for its aromatic foliage and is often used in horticulture and essential oil production.
