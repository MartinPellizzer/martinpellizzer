Limonia lacourtiana

Limonia lacourtiana, commonly known as the African hibiscus, is a flowering shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and savannah regions.

Limonia lacourtiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
