Libanotis laserpitifolia

Libanotis laserpitifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf bellflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features silver-gray, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in arid regions.
