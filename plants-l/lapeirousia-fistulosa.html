Lapeirousia fistulosa

Lapeirousia fistulosa, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a striking member of the Xanthorrhoeaceae family native to South Africa.

This perennial plant features tall, spiky inflorescences that are typically bright red or orange, giving it its common name. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a rosette at the base. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

It is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
