Lespedeza floribunda var. fasciculiflora

Lespedeza floribunda var. fasciculiflora, commonly known as the clustered lespedeza, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and its clusters of small, fluffy white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its stems are often hairy. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in open woodlands, fields, and along roadsides.

Lespedeza floribunda var. fasciculiflora is a valuable plant for wildlife, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for small insects.
