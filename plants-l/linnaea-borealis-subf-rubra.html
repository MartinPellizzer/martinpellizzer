Linnaea borealis subf. rubra

Linnaea borealis subf.

rubra, commonly known as the red twinflower, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the northern regions of North America and Eurasia. It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant features dark green, leathery leaves and produces red or pink flowers that are often borne in pairs. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is typically found in boreal and subarctic environments.

This plant is noted for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local wildlife.
