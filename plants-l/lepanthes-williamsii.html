Lepanthes williamsii

Lepanthes williamsii, commonly known as the Williams's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a rosette pattern, adapting to the humid, shaded environments it inhabits.

Lepanthes williamsii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
