Lobelia madagascariensis

Lobelia madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar lily, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a whorled pattern around the stem.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.
