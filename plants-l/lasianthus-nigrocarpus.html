Lasianthus nigrocarpus

Lasianthus nigrocarpus, commonly known as black-fruited lasianthus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, dark purple to black berries. The plant typically grows as an understory shrub, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

The flowers are tubular and usually pink or red, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.
