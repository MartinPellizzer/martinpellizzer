Lobivia ferox f. cerdana

Lobivia ferox f. cerdana, commonly known as the Cerdana Lobivia, is a compact, low-growing succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It features dense, fleshy stems that are often tinged with red or purple, especially when exposed to full sun. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are typically rounded and thick, adapted to store water in its native environment.

This variety is prized for its unique coloration and compact growth habit, making it a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
