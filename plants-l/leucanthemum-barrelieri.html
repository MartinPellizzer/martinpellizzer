Leucanthemum barrelieri

Leucanthemum barrelieri, commonly known as the Barrelier's white ragwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with a central disc of smaller florets.

It is often found in coastal areas and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
