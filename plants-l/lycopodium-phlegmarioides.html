Lycopodium phlegmarioides

Lycopodium phlegmarioides, commonly known as the false clubmoss, is a member of the Lycopodiaceae family and is characterized by its spore-bearing cones and needle-like leaves.

It is a vascular plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or rocky crevices. The plant produces sporophylls that enclose the spores, which are released into the air for dispersal. Its structure resembles that of true clubmosses, but it lacks the true roots and vascular system found in more advanced plants.

Lycopodium phlegmarioides plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
