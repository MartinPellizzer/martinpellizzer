Luzula deflexa

Luzula deflexa, commonly known as the drooping wood rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a dense, upright clump. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, greenish to yellowish flowers. Luzula deflexa thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

It is often found in temperate regions across the Northern Hemisphere.
