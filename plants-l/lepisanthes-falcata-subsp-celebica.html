Lepisanthes falcata subsp. celebica

Lepisanthes falcata subsp.

celebica, commonly known as the Celebes lepisanthes, is a member of the Araceae family. It is native to parts of Indonesia, particularly the Moluccas and surrounding islands.

The plant is characterized by its striking, arrow-shaped leaves that are often deep green with a glossy texture. It produces inflorescences consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe, typical of the arum family.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
