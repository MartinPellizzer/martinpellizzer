Lamium garganicum subsp. glabratum

Lamium garganicum subsp.

glabratum, commonly known as the smooth deadnettle, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically hairless or nearly so, giving it its subspecies name *glabratum*.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its leaves and stems are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported calming and soothing properties.
