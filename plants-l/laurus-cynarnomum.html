Laurus cynarnomum

Laurus cynarnomum, commonly known as sweet bay or Laurier rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to the Mediterranean region. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, dark purple drupe that contains a single seed.

It is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves used in cooking and traditional medicine.
