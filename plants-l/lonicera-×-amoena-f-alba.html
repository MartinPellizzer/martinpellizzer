Lonicera × amoena f. alba

Lonicera × amoena f. alba, commonly known as the white honeysuckle, is a hybrid plant in the Caprifoliaceae family.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is a deciduous vine that can grow up to 10 meters in length, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for gardens and natural habitats.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
