Lonicera nigra

Lonicera nigra, commonly known as black honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries that ripen in the fall. The plant has opposite, simple, and elliptical leaves that turn yellow in autumn. Its fragrant, tubular flowers bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Lonicera nigra is often found in moist woodlands and along stream banks.
