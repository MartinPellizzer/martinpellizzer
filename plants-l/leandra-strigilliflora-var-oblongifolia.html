Leandra strigilliflora var. oblongifolia

Leandra strigilliflora var. oblongifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed leandra, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine, hairy texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in warm climates as a houseplant.
