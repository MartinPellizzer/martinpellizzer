Lasiopogon molluginoides

Lasiopogon molluginoides, commonly known as the slender leaf speedwell, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, often blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, helping it retain moisture in arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-adapted to Mediterranean climates with mild, wet winters and hot, dry summers.
