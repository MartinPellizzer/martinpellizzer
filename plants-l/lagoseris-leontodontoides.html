Lagoseris leontodontoides

Lagoseris leontodontoides, commonly known as the woolly goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant features hairy, gray-green leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its botanical characteristics include a taproot system and small, linear leaves with a fuzzy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in arid gardens.
