Licuala lanata

Licuala lanata, commonly known as the woolly pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, pinnately compound leaves that are covered in dense, soft, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and as thatching material due to their durability.

The plant thrives in humid, coastal environments and is adapted to tolerate salt spray and sandy soils.
