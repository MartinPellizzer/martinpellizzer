Leonia caudata

Leonia caudata, commonly known as the golden thread vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a mild, aromatic scent. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for hanging baskets and trellis displays.
