Lythrum salicaria f. orgyalis

Lythrum salicaria f. orgyalis, commonly known as the purple loosestrife, is a flowering plant characterized by its striking spikes of violet to purple flowers.

It belongs to the Lythraceae family and is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a reddish or purple hue, adding to its ornamental appeal.

However, it is considered an invasive species in many regions due to its rapid growth and ability to outcompete native vegetation.
