Linaria praetermissa

Linaria praetermissa, commonly known as the common toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are tubular with two lips, a feature common in many species of the genus Linaria.

Linaria praetermissa is often found in disturbed habitats and is considered a pioneer species in ecological succession.
