Lychnitis boerhavii

Lychnitis boerhavii, commonly known as the Boerhavia bluemink, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in dense clusters. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or arid environments.

Lychnitis boerhavii is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
