Lansium aqueum

Lansium aqueum, commonly known as the water longan, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The fruit is a small, round drupe with a smooth, thin skin and a sweet, juicy pulp. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The plant is valued not only for its edible fruit but also for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
