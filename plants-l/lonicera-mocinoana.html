Lonicera mocinoana

Lonicera mocinoana, commonly known as the Mocino's honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and woodland edges. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are usually yellow or orange in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Lonicera mocinoana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
