Laennecia filaginoides

Laennecia filaginoides, commonly known as the yellow flag iris, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland areas in North America.

It features tall, slender stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, supporting large, showy yellow flowers with a distinctive purple throat. The plant has strap-like, dark green leaves that are arranged in a fan shape at the base. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important indicator species for wetland ecosystems.
