Lonicera chlamydophora

Lonicera chlamydophora, commonly known as the golden honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It produces red berries that are edible for birds but not for humans.

Lonicera chlamydophora is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
