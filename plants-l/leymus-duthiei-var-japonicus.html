Leymus duthiei var. japonicus

Leymus duthiei var. japonicus, commonly known as Japanese needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, needle-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
