Limnanthes versicolor var. parishii

Limnanthes versicolor var. parishii, commonly known as the yellow marsh plant, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in wet meadows, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
