Lilium inyoense

Lilium inyoense, commonly known as the Inyo lily, is a rare and endemic species native to the high elevations of the Sierra Nevada mountain range in California.

This perennial herbaceous plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in alpine environments. It produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often with a distinct red or purple blotch at the base. The plant is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and a single stem that supports its floral display.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Lilium inyoense is considered a vulnerable species, facing threats from climate change and habitat loss.
