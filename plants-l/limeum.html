Limeum

Limeum, commonly known as the common lime, is a deciduous tree native to Europe, western Asia, and north Africa.

It belongs to the genus Tilia in the family Tiliaceae, characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits called limes, which are commonly used in culinary and medicinal applications. Limeum is valued for its ornamental qualities, providing shade and a pleasant canopy in gardens and parks.

Its wood is also used in furniture-making due to its fine texture and workability.
