Lupinus bicolor subsp. marginatus

Lupinus bicolor subsp.

marginatus, commonly known as the pink lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves.

The plant produces showy, pea-like flowers that range in color from pink to purple, often with a contrasting throat. Its root system hosts nitrogen-fixing bacteria, contributing to soil fertility.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.
