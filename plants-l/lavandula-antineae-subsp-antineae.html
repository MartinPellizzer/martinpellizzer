Lavandula antineae subsp. antineae

Lavandula antineae subsp.

antineae, commonly known as the Armenian lavender, is a perennial shrub native to the high-altitude regions of the Caucasus. It features aromatic, spike-like inflorescences composed of narrow, purple-blue flowers.

The plant has gray-green, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, fuzzy layer. It thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and requires full sun.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in perfumery and herbal medicine.
