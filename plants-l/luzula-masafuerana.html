Luzula masafuerana

Luzula masafuerana, commonly known as the Masafuera sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and cloud forests. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the sedge family.

Luzula masafuerana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
