Lasianthus strigillosus

Lasianthus strigillosus, commonly known as the hairy lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its small, showy flowers that typically bloom in clusters. The plant has hairy leaves and stems, which give it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Lasianthus strigillosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
