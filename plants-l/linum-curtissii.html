Linum curtissii

Linum curtissii, commonly known as the Curtiss flax, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the genus Linum, which includes various species of flax known for their showy blue or purple flowers. This particular species is characterized by its slender stems and small, delicate flowers that bloom in the spring. Linum curtissii thrives in rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

It is a perennial plant that plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
