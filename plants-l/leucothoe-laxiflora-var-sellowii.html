Leucothoe laxiflora var. sellowii

Leucothoe laxiflora var. sellowii, commonly known as the Sellow's leatherleaf, is a woody shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing year-round interest in the landscape.

It is often used in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to shaded garden environments.
