Lonicera giraldii

Lonicera giraldii, commonly known as the Chinese honeysuckle, is a deciduous climbing vine native to China.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, fragrant flowers that range from white to yellow in color. The flowers are typically followed by red berries that contain seeds, contributing to its ornamental value. This species is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.
