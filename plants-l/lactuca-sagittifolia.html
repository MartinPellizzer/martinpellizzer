Lactuca sagittifolia

Lactuca sagittifolia, commonly known as arrowleaf lettuce, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It features a rosette of arrow-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, green flowers that are clustered in a terminal head, followed by slender, elongated seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and valuable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
