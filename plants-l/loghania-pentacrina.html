Loghania pentacrina

Loghania pentacrina, commonly known as the five-lobed loghania, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Loganiaceae and is characterized by its unique, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
