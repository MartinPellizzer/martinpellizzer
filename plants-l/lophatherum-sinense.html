Lophatherum sinense

Lophatherum sinense, commonly known as Chinese love grass, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, three-lobed flowers. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

Lophatherum sinense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
