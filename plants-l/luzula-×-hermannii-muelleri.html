Luzula × hermannii-muelleri

Luzula × hermannii-muelleri, commonly known as the Hermann's woodrush, is a hybrid species within the plantain family (Plantaginaceae).

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of white or pale blue flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded woodland environments.

Luzula × hermannii-muelleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland areas.
