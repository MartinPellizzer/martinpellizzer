Lupinus palmeri var. clawsonianus

Lupinus palmeri var. clawsonianus, commonly known as the desert lupine, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant produces small, hairy pods that contain several elliptical seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.

Lupinus palmeri var. clawsonianus is adapted to withstand drought conditions and is an important component of desert ecosystems.
