Laseguea acutifolia f. guilleminiana

Laseguea acutifolia f. guilleminiana, commonly known as the Guilleminian form of the sharp-leaved laseguea, is a rare and distinctive variant of the species.

It is characterized by its finely serrated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a silvery underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its limited distribution and unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
