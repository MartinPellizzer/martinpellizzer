Limonium × insigne var. rossmaessleri

Limonium × insigne var. rossmaessleri, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking, spike-like flower heads composed of numerous small, blue or purple flowers. The plant has narrow, silver-gray foliage that is often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to tolerate salt spray and drought conditions.
