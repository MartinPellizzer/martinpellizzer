Leptorhynchos pulchellus

Leptorhynchos pulchellus, commonly known as the beautiful leafy orchid, is a species of orchid native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows in shady, moist environments such as forest understories or rocky outcrops. Its roots form symbiotic relationships with specific fungi, which aid in nutrient absorption.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
