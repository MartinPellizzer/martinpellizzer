Lupinus cosentinii

Lupinus cosentinii, commonly known as the Cosentin's lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in dry, rocky habitats. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through symbiotic bacteria.

Lupinus cosentinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
