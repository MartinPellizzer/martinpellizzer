Loasa fulva

Loasa fulva, commonly known as the yellow loasa, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in cloud forests. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are usually alternate, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Loasa fulva is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and subtle floral display.
