Leucolaena rotundifolia

Leucolaena rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaf leucolaena, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It features small, rounded leaves that are typically arranged alternately along its stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, which are followed by small, woody pods containing seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Leucolaena rotundifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
