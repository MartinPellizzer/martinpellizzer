Lathyrus occidentalis

Lathyrus occidentalis, commonly known as Western pea or wild vetch, is a flowering plant native to western North America.

It is a perennial legume characterized by its trailing growth habit and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces erect, slender stems that bear clusters of pea-like flowers, typically ranging in color from white to purple. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several small, rounded seeds.

Lathyrus occidentalis is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas, meadows, and along roadsides.
