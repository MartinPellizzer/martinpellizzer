Lobivia hyalacantha

Lobivia hyalacantha, commonly known as the glassy lobivia, is a succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its slow growth and unique appearance, it is a popular choice among cactus and succulent enthusiasts.
