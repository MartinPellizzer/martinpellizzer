Lotus neomexicanus

Lotus neomexicanus, commonly known as the New Mexico lotus, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its striking pink or red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces small, bean-like pods that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This species is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its unique adaptations and ornamental value.
