Lasiacis divaricata var. austroamericana

Lasiacis divaricata var. austroamericana, commonly known as the Brazilian flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It features compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets that are often arranged in a feathery pattern. The plant produces vibrant orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its bark is smooth and often exhibits a reddish or brownish hue, and it can grow up to 10 meters in height under optimal conditions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in warm climates for its striking floral display.
