Lobelia mcvaughii

Lobelia mcvaughii, commonly known as McVaugh's lobelia, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, tubular blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils in woodland or canyon habitats. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Lobelia mcvaughii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
