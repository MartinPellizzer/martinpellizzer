Linum crenatum

Linum crenatum, commonly known as hairy flax, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate blue or purple flowers and hairy stems, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have wavy edges, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Linum crenatum is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and historical significance in textile production.
