Lyonia maestrensis

Lyonia maestrensis, commonly known as the Maestrense Lyonia, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen shrub form. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in the spring. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a dense canopy that helps it thrive in cooler, high-altitude environments.

Lyonia maestrensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
