Leptochloa plumosa

Leptochloa plumosa, commonly known as the silky grass or plumose grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its fine, silky hairs that cover its leaves and stems, giving it a soft, feathery appearance. This perennial plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of slender spikelets that are often arranged in a panicle-like structure.

The plant is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental due to its delicate and decorative foliage.
