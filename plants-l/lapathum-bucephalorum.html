Lapathum bucephalorum

Lapathum bucephalorum, commonly known as the broad-leaved woundwort, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and can be found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks. The plant features dark green, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a hairy underside, and its small, clustered flowers are usually pink to purple in color. It blooms from June to August and is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments.

Lapathum bucephalorum is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
