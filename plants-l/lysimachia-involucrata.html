Lysimachia involucrata

Lysimachia involucrata, commonly known as goldenstar, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, often surrounded by whorled leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Lysimachia involucrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
