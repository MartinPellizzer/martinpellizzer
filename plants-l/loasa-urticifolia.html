Loasa urticifolia

Loasa urticifolia, commonly known as the stinging nettle bush, is a perennial shrub native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, stinging leaves that can cause skin irritation upon contact. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in shades of pink or white.

Loasa urticifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
