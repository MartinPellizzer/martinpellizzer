Lampranthus tenuis

Lampranthus tenuis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ice plant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features small, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its foliage. It thrives in well-draining soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ability to withstand drought conditions makes it a hardy and low-maintenance plant for gardeners in dry climates.
