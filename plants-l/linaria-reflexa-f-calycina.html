Linaria reflexa f. calycina

Linaria reflexa f. calycina, commonly known as the calycine toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The calycine toadflax is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
