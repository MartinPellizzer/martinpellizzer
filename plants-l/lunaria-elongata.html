Lunaria elongata

Lunaria elongata, commonly known as the moonflower or nightshade, is a flowering plant belonging to the nightshade family.

It is characterized by its elongated, cylindrical seed pods that turn a striking blue when mature. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is native to parts of Europe and Asia. Its flowers are usually small and white or pale blue, opening at night.

The seed pods are often used in traditional medicine and as decorative elements due to their unique appearance.
