Leontodon salzmannii

Leontodon salzmannii, commonly known as Salzmann's bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a yellow center. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is native to regions in Europe and western Asia. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, bract-like structures beneath its flowers.

Leontodon salzmannii is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats, thriving in cooler, high-altitude environments.
