Lobelia ovata

Lobelia ovata, commonly known as the oval lobelia, is a flowering plant native to parts of the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The leaves are ovate, hence the species name, and are usually arranged opposite along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
