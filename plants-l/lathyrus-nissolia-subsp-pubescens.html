Lathyrus nissolia subsp. pubescens

Lathyrus nissolia subsp.

pubescens, commonly known as the hairy grass vetch, is a perennial legume native to temperate regions of Asia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pale blue to purple.

The subspecies is distinguished by its hairy foliage and stems, which provide a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed sites.

This plant plays a role in nitrogen fixation and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
