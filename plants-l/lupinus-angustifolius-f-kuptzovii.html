Lupinus angustifolius f. kuptzovii

Lupinus angustifolius f. kuptzovii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lupine, is a botanical variant characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers.

It is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in Kazakhstan and Kyrgyzstan. The plant thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils and is adapted to harsh, arid environments. Its foliage and flowers are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in stabilizing soils in its native habitat.
