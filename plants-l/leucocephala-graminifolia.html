Leucocephala graminifolia

Leucocephala graminifolia, commonly known as the silverhead grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pale blue or white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along riverbanks.

Leucocephala graminifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its unique foliage and floral display.
