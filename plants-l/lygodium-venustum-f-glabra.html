Lygodium venustum f. glabra

Lygodium venustum f. glabra, commonly known as the smooth climbing fern, is a variant of the genus Lygodium, which belongs to the family Lygodiaceae.

This fern is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and pinnately compound leaves that are typically smooth and glabrous, giving it its specific epithet "glabra.". It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in shaded, moist areas such as forest undergrowth. The fronds are usually bipinnate, with small, ovate pinnae that are arranged closely along the rachis.

This plant is notable for its rapid growth and ability to form dense mats, which can contribute to its ecological role in soil stabilization and habitat provision.
