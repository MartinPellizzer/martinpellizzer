Limnanthes douglasii var. nivea

Limnanthes douglasii var. nivea, commonly known as the white marsh marigold, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features showy, yellow flowers that are often surrounded by white bracts, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant has heart-shaped, succulent leaves that are covered in a fine, white waxy coating. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
