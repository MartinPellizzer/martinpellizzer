Lomatium californicum

Lomatium californicum, commonly known as California lomatium, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its fern-like leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine by Native American tribes.

Lomatium californicum is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing soil.
