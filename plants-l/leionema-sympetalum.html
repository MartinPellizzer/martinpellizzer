Leionema sympetalum

Leionema sympetalum, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its soft, silvery-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy regions.

Leionema sympetalum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in gardens and landscaping.
