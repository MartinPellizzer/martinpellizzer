Lippia heterophylla var. ciliatifolia

Lippia heterophylla var. ciliatifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf lippia, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves with ciliate margins, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale purple, arranged in clusters. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or dry soils.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
