Lepanthes gunningiana

Lepanthes gunningiana, commonly known as the Gunning's slipper orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns and a lip that resembles a slipper. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its botanical features include linear, grass-like leaves and a compact growth habit.

Lepanthes gunningiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
