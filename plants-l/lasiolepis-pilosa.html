Lasiolepis pilosa

Lasiolepis pilosa, commonly known as the hairy lasiolepis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological adaptations, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
