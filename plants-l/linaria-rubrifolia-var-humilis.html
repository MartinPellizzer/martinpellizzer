Linaria rubrifolia var. humilis

Linaria rubrifolia var. humilis, commonly known as the dwarf red-leaf toadflax, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features slender, branching stems covered in small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically reddish in color. The plant produces clusters of delicate, yellow-orange flowers with two prominent lobes, resembling the shape of a toadflax. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant.
