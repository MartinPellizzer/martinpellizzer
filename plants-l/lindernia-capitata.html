Lindernia capitata

Lindernia capitata, commonly known as the whorled lindernia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of pink to purple. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Lindernia capitata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
