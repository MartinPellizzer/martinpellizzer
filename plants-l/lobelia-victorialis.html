Lobelia victorialis

Lobelia victorialis, commonly known as the blue lobelia, is a herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and has opposite, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

Lobelia victorialis is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and woodland edges, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
