Lepidium bonannianum

Lepidium bonannianum, commonly known as the Bonannia cress, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many cruciferous vegetables and wild cresses. The plant is characterized by its small, delicate leaves and clusters of tiny, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It produces small, round seeds that are often dispersed by wind.

Lepidium bonannianum is typically found in moist, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
