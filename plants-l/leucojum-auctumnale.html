Leucojum auctumnale

Leucojum auctumnale, commonly known as the autumn crocus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a bulbous base. Its flowers emit a mild, sweet fragrance and are pollinated by insects.

The autumn crocus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
