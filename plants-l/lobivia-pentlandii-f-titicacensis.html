Lobivia pentlandii f. titicacensis

Lobivia pentlandii f. titicacensis, commonly known as the Titiacan Lobivia, is a rare and striking cactus native to the Andes mountains of Peru.

It features short, cylindrical stems with distinctive, ribbed, and spiny structures that help it survive in its arid environment. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring, adding a splash of color to its rocky habitat. It is highly valued by cactus enthusiasts for its unique appearance and challenging cultivation requirements.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat threats, it is considered a vulnerable species in the wild.
