Luzula parviflora var. densiflora

Luzula parviflora var. densiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered wood rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, commonly known as the grass family, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to whitish flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as forest floors and rocky outcrops.

Its dense foliage and ability to form clumps make it a suitable ornamental plant in shaded garden settings.
