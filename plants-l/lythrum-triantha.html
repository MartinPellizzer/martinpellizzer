Lythrum triantha

Lythrum triantha, commonly known as narrow-leaved verbena, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lythraceae family, which includes other species such as the common redroot and the American beautyberry. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Lythrum triantha is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
