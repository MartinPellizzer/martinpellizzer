Licuala stipitata

Licuala stipitata, commonly known as the umbrella palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves that radiate from a central stem, giving it a distinctive umbrella-like appearance. The plant typically grows in wet, shaded environments such as rainforests. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and as thatching material.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
