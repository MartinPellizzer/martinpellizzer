Lycianthes heterodonta

Lycianthes heterodonta, commonly known as the purpleleaf nightshade, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its alternate, simple, and usually hairy leaves. The flowers are typically white or purple, with five petals and a distinctive, spurred calyx. The fruit is a fleshy, orange or yellow berry that contains several seeds.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and potatoes.
