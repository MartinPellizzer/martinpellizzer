Lilium carniolicum subsp. albanicum

Lilium carniolicum subsp.

albanicum, commonly known as the Albanian red lily, is a rare and endemic subspecies native to the Balkan region. It is characterized by its striking red flowers with a white throat, which bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as mountain forests and rocky slopes. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces a tall stem with a single flower.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
