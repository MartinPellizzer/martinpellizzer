Leptopus diplospermus

Leptopus diplospermus, commonly known as the blackwood tree, is a species of flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by dark purple berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal regions and dry forests.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood and is also used in traditional Aboriginal practices.
