Lepanthes marahuacensis

Lepanthes marahuacensis, commonly known as the Marahuaca orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America, particularly found in Guatemala and northern Honduras.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that grows on trees in humid, shaded forest environments. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers with white petals and a distinctive yellow lip. Its leaves are typically dark green and arranged in a rosette pattern.

Lepanthes marahuacensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
