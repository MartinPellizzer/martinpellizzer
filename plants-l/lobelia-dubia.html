Lobelia dubia

Lobelia dubia, commonly known as the slender lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that are usually blue or purple in color. Lobelia dubia is known for its ability to thrive in wet conditions, making it a common sight in swampy areas.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
