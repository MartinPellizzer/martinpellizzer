Lilium leucanthum

Lilium leucanthum, commonly known as the white lily, is a species of flowering plant in the Liliaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Afghanistan and Pakistan. The plant is characterized by its large, showy white flowers with a pale pink throat, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, and it produces a tall, slender stem.

Lilium leucanthum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
