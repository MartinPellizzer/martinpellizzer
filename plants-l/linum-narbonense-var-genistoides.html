Linum narbonense var. genistoides

Linum narbonense var. genistoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf flax, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branching stems that often grow along the ground, and its leaves are linear to lanceolate in shape, typically with a hairy surface. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, and it is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

It is often found in rocky or dry habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in fiber production.
