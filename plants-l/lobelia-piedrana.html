Lobelia piedrana

Lobelia piedrana, commonly known as the Andean lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in high-altitude regions with cool, moist climates. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. It is often found in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
