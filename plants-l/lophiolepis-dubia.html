Lophiolepis dubia

Lophiolepis dubia, commonly known as the slender leaved grass, is a species of grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It is a perennial herb that produces small, inconspicuous flowers, which are often wind-pollinated.

Lophiolepis dubia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticultural settings.
