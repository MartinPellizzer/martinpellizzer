Lepidium draba subsp. chalepense

Lepidium draba subsp.

chalepense, commonly known as the Armenian cress, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region. It is a small annual herb with a basal rosette of leaves and slender, branched stems.

The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers arranged in racemes, which are typically visited by pollinators such as bees. Its seeds are encased in a hard, triangular capsule, and the plant is adapted to grow in rocky and arid environments. Lepidium draba subsp.

chalepense is often found in mountainous areas and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
