Lobivia zecheri

Lobivia zecheri, commonly known as the Zecher's lobivia, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded shape and produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months. The plant has thick, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, which help minimize water loss.

Lobivia zecheri is well-suited for rock gardens and requires minimal care once established.
