Lupinus fraxinetorum

Lupinus fraxinetorum, commonly known as the Fraxinetum lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Lupinus fraxinetorum plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native environment.
