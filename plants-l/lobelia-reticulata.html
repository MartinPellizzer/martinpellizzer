Lobelia reticulata

Lobelia reticulata, commonly known as the net-veined lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a distinctive net-like vein pattern, contributing to its ornamental value.

Lobelia reticulata is often used in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and its ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
