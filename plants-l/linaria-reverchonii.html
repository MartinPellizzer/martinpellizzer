Linaria reverchonii

Linaria reverchonii, commonly known as the Reverchon's toadflax, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of yellow-orange flowers. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Linaria reverchonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
