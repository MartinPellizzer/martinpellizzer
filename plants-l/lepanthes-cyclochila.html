Lepanthes cyclochila

Lepanthes cyclochila, commonly known as the cyclochila orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid with delicate, pale green to white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has a pseudobulb structure typical of many orchids, which helps it store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a rosette pattern, and it thrives in shaded, humid environments.

Lepanthes cyclochila is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
