Loudetia anomala

Loudetia anomala, commonly known as the African grass or false lovegrass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, inconspicuous flowers. It is often used as a forage plant for livestock due to its nutritional value and adaptability.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in various environmental conditions.
