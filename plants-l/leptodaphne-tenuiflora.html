Leptodaphne tenuiflora

Leptodaphne tenuiflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved daphne, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features lance-shaped, aromatic leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem and opposite leaf arrangement.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and distinctive scent.
