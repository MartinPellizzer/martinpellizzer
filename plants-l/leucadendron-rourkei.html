Leucadendron rourkei

Leucadendron rourkei, commonly known as the King Protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that resemble a crown of white flowers, giving it its common name. The plant is native to the Cape Province and thrives in coastal and mountainous regions with well-drained soils. It produces distinctive spherical flower heads composed of numerous small, white to pinkish flowers.

Leucadendron rourkei is also known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
