Lagerstroemia carinata

Lagerstroemia carinata, commonly known as the crested crape myrtle, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, ridged bark that becomes flaky with age, and its showy, clustered inflorescences that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, round fruits that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its adaptability and ornamental value make it a favored choice in horticulture.
