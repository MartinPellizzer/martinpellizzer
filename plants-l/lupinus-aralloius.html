Lupinus aralloius

Lupinus aralloius, commonly known as the Sierra lupine, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant has pinnately compound leaves and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open meadows and rocky areas.

Lupinus aralloius plays an important role in soil nitrogen fixation due to its symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.
