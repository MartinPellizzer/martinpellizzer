Lavandula rotundifolia

Lavandula rotundifolia, commonly known as English lavender, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and gray-green, rounded leaves. The plant produces spikes of purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is widely cultivated for its aromatic properties and is used in perfumery, aromatherapy, and herbal medicine.

Its essential oils contain compounds such as linalool and lavandin, which contribute to its calming effects.
