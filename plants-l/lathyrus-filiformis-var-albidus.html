Lathyrus filiformis var. albidus

Lathyrus filiformis var. albidus, commonly known as the white threadflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces elongated, bean-like pods that contain several small, elliptical seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive addition to gardens and natural landscapes.
