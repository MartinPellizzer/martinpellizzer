Lemna tenera

Lemna tenera, commonly known as the lesser water clover, is a small, floating aquatic plant belonging to the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its rosette-like structure of oval, waxy leaves that are typically less than 1 cm in diameter. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by bright yellow berries containing seeds. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments and is often found in ponds, lakes, and wetlands.

Lemna tenera is an important part of aquatic ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
