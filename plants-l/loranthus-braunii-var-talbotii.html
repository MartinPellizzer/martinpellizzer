Loranthus braunii var. talbotii

Loranthus braunii var. talbotii, commonly known as the Talbot's mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily in the family Fabaceae.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using the light it captures. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. Its berries are typically red or orange, containing sticky seeds that adhere to the bark of the host tree.

This species is found in various regions of Southeast Asia, where it plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its host forest communities.
