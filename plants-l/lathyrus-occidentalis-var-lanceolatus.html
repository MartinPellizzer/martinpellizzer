Lathyrus occidentalis var. lanceolatus

Lathyrus occidentalis var. lanceolatus, commonly known as the western peavine, is a perennial climbing legume native to North America.

It features slender, twining stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval, lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, followed by elongated, bean-like pods containing angular seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or riparian habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal practices.
