Laurus mauritiana

Laurus mauritiana, commonly known as the bay laurel or sweet bay, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often used in cooking and traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by black, egg-shaped berries. Its leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its distinctive fragrance and medicinal properties.

Laurus mauritiana thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for ornamental and practical purposes.
