Layia pentachaeta subsp. pentachaeta

Layia pentachaeta subsp.

pentachaeta, commonly known as the narrowleaf cosmos, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the western United States. It features slender, branching stems covered in fine, white to pale yellow hairs.

The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center, blooming from summer to early fall. Its leaves are linear to lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.
