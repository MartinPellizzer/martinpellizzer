Lepidium bipinnatifidum var. curicoanum

Lepidium bipinnatifidum var. curicoanum, commonly known as the Curicoan rocket, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and aromatic foliage. The plant produces slender, branching stems with small, pinnately divided leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its seeds are typically small and capsule-like, typical of the Brassicaceae family.

It is often cultivated for its peppery flavor and used in culinary applications.
