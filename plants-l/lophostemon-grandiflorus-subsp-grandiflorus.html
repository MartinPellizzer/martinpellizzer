Lophostemon grandiflorus subsp. grandiflorus

Lophostemon grandiflorus subsp.

grandiflorus, commonly known as the Sydney red gum, is a species native to eastern Australia. It is a tall, deciduous tree that can reach heights of up to 30 meters.

The tree is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and long, narrow leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by woody, spherical fruits.

This species is well adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in dry, open forests and woodlands.
