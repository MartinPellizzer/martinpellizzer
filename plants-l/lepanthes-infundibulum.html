Lepanthes infundibulum

Lepanthes infundibulum, commonly known as the hooded orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring a hood-like lip that protects the reproductive organs. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are often small, fragrant, and display a range of colors, including white, pink, and purple.

Lepanthes infundibulum is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its native habitats.
