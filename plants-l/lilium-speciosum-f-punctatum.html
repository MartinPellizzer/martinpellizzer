Lilium speciosum f. punctatum

Lilium speciosum f. punctatum, commonly known as the spotted lily, is a botanical variant of the species Lilium speciosum.

It is characterized by its striking, spotted patterns on the petals, which are often a combination of white, pink, and red. This cultivar is typically found in the mountainous regions of western North America, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant produces tall, slender stems that bear showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in late summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
