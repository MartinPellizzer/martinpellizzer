Limonium lausianum

Limonium lausianum, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, herbaceous growth habit and its striking clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, salt marshes, and sandy soils, showing a high tolerance for saline conditions. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Limonium lausianum is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in challenging environments.
