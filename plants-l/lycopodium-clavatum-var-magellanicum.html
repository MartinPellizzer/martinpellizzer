Lycopodium clavatum var. magellanicum

Lycopodium clavatum var. magellanicum, commonly known as the Magellanic clubmoss, is a low-growing evergreen plant native to southern South America.

It belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae and is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and spore-bearing strobila. The plant typically forms dense mats on rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal or mountainous regions. It is adapted to harsh environmental conditions, including high winds and salt spray.

This variety is distinguished from other lycopodium species by its specific geographic distribution and morphological features.
