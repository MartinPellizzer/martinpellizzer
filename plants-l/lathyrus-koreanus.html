Lathyrus koreanus

Lathyrus koreanus, commonly known as the Korean vetch, is a flowering plant native to Korea and neighboring regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, followed by pod fruits containing several seeds. Lathyrus koreanus is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
