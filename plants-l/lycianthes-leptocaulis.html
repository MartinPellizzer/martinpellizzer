Lycianthes leptocaulis

Lycianthes leptocaulis, commonly known as the slender-stemmed physalis, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to Central and South America, where it typically grows in dry, open habitats. The plant features hairy, slender stems and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into inflated, green to orange calyces. Its fruit, which is a type of capsule, encloses numerous seeds and is often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
