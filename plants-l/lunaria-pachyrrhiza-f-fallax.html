Lunaria pachyrrhiza f. fallax

Lunaria pachyrrhiza f. fallax, commonly known as the fallax form of the broad-leaved moonflower, is a variant of the moonflower species native to North America.

This plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and its striking, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The flowers are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported calming effects. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and valuable addition to both ecological and horticultural settings.
