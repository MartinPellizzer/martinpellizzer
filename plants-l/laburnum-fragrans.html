Laburnum fragrans

Laburnum fragrans, commonly known as the sweet-scented golden chain, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its long, drooping clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, emitting a sweet, honey-like fragrance. The plant has pinnate leaves composed of several small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Laburnum fragrans is also valued for its ornamental value and is often used in landscaping for its vibrant floral display.
