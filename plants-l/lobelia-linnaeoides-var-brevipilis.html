Lobelia linnaeoides var. brevipilis

Lobelia linnaeoides var. brevipilis, commonly known as the short-stalked lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial with slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a suitable choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value and ecological benefits make it a valuable addition to native plant landscapes.
