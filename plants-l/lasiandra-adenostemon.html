Lasiandra adenostemon

Lasiandra adenostemon, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its basal rosette of gray-green, woolly leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, capsule-like fruits containing seeds with silky hairs. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments through its succulent leaves and deep root system.

Lasiandra adenostemon is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
