Larnax pilosa

Larnax pilosa, commonly known as the hairy larnax, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which provide a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Larnax pilosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
