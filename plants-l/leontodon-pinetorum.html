Leontodon pinetorum

Leontodon pinetorum, commonly known as the pinehill bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and grassy areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, producing showy yellow-orange flowers.

Leontodon pinetorum is often found in association with pine trees, hence its common name.
