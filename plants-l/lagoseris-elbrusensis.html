Lagoseris elbrusensis

Lagoseris elbrusensis, commonly known as the Elbrus aster, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Caucasus Mountains.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, adapting to harsh climatic conditions. Its flowers are usually blue or violet, with ray florets that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and fragile habitat, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
