Limonium macrophyllum

Limonium macrophyllum, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking, spike-like inflorescence composed of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and saline soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in a fine layer of salt.

Limonium macrophyllum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
