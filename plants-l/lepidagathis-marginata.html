Lepidagathis marginata

Lepidagathis marginata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pittosporum, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow, leathery, and often have a reddish or purple hue when young.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and fragrant blooms.
