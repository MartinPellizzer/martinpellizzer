Linaria alba

Linaria alba, commonly known as the white toadflax, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. The plant has slender, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, rocky areas, and along roadsides.

Linaria alba is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
