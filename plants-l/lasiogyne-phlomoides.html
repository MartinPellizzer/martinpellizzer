Lasiogyne phlomoides

Lasiogyne phlomoides, commonly known as narrowleaf ironweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in prairies, meadows, and open woodlands.

Lasiogyne phlomoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
