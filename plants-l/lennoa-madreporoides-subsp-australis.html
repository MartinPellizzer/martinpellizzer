Lennoa madreporoides subsp. australis

Lennoa madreporoides subsp.

australis, commonly known as the Australian rock daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia. It is a perennial herb characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and succulent, aiding in water conservation.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens in suitable climates.
