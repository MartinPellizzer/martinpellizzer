Linanthus pusillus var. californicus

Linanthus pusillus var. californicus, commonly known as the California dwarf flax, is a small annual plant native to California.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has small, lance-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
