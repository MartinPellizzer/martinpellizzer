Lopimia

Lopimia, commonly known as the sacred lotus of Southeast Asia, is a tropical plant native to regions such as India, China, and Indonesia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom on long, winding stems that rise from water. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, while its roots anchor it in the mud at the bottom of ponds or lakes. Lopimia is also known for its symbolic significance in various cultural and religious traditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and spiritual contexts.
