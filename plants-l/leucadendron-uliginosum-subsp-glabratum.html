Leucadendron uliginosum subsp. glabratum

Leucadendron uliginosum subsp.

glabratum, commonly known as the silver leaf leucadendron, is a shrub native to South Africa. It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and conical or cylindrical flower heads.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal fynbos vegetation. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
