Lonicera oiwakensis

Lonicera oiwakensis, commonly known as the Japanese honeysuckle, is a deciduous vine native to East Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of yellow and orange, which bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a small, red berry that ripens in the fall.

Lonicera oiwakensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
