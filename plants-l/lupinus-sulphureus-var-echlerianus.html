Lupinus sulphureus var. echlerianus

Lupinus sulphureus var. echlerianus, commonly known as the yellow lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, improving soil fertility.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
