Leucaena confertiflora var. confertiflora

Leucaena confertiflora var. confertiflora, commonly known as the denseflower leucaena, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets that are typically arranged in pairs. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers, which are followed by elongated, flat pods containing seeds. It is often cultivated for its nitrogen-fixing ability and as a forage crop.

The species is also valued for its rapid growth and adaptability to various soil conditions.
