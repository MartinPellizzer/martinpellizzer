Litsea acrantha

Litsea acrantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved litsea, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including parts of India, China, and Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically narrow and elliptical in shape. Its small, fragrant flowers are usually white or pale yellow and are clustered in axillary inflorescences.

The fruit is a small, spherical drupe that turns from green to red when mature.
