Lupinus excubitus subsp. medius

Lupinus excubitus subsp.

medius, commonly known as the desert lupine, is a perennial legume native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple.

The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and is adapted to arid environments. It plays an important ecological role by fixing nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation. Lupinus excubitus subsp.

medius is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
