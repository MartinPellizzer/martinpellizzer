Lactuca brachyrrhyncha

Lactuca brachyrrhyncha, commonly known as the short-beaked lettuce, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family, Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its rosette of spoon-shaped, pale green leaves and small, yellow flowers that appear in the summer months. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is a herbaceous annual that can reach heights of up to 30 centimeters.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
