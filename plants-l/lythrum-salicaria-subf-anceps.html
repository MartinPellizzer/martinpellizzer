Lythrum salicaria subf. anceps

Lythrum salicaria subf.

anceps, commonly known as narrow-leaved loosestrife, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Lythraceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and spikes of purple or pink flowers.

The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, sunny environments such as wet meadows and stream banks.

The plant is often found in wetland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
