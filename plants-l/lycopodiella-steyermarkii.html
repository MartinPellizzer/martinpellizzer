Lycopodiella steyermarkii

Lycopodiella steyermarkii, commonly known as the Steyermark's spikemoss, is a small, evergreen fern ally native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae and is characterized by its compact, moss-like appearance with tiny, needle-like leaves. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures, known as strobila, are cone-like and contain spores, which are dispersed by wind.

Lycopodiella steyermarkii plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
