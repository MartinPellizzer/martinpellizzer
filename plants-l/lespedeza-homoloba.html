Lespedeza homoloba

Lespedeza homoloba, commonly known as narrowleaf Lespedeza, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in open woods, fields, and along stream banks, thriving in well-drained soils. Its trifoliate leaves and yellow to reddish-purple blooms make it a distinctive member of the genus Lespedeza.

It is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
