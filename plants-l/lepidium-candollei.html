Lepidium candollei

Lepidium candollei, commonly known as the Andean rocket, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and its ability to thrive in high-altitude environments. Its seeds are edible and have been used in traditional Andean cuisine.

Lepidium candollei is also valued for its potential medicinal properties and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
