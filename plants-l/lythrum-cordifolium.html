Lythrum cordifolium

Lythrum cordifolium, commonly known as purple loosestrife, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescences that produce vibrant purple flowers, which bloom from June to September. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist environments such as wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Lythrum cordifolium is also known for its ability to spread rapidly, making it an invasive species in some regions.
