Lappa major subsp. pubens

Lappa major subsp.

pubens, commonly known as the pubescent hogweed, is a herbaceous plant characterized by its hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and vegetables.

The plant produces tall flowering stems with small, white to greenish flowers arranged in umbrella-like clusters. It is native to parts of Europe and Asia and is often found in moist, disturbed habitats.
