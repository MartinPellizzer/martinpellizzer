Loudetia cerata

Loudetia cerata, commonly known as the spiked grass or red grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often reddish in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

Loudetia cerata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its distinctive appearance.
