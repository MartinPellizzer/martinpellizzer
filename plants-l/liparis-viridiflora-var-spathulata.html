Liparis viridiflora var. spathulata

Liparis viridiflora var. spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leaf green orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of greenish-white flowers with a distinctive, often yellowish lip. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is typically found in forest understories. It has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
