Lepanthes disjuncta

Lepanthes disjuncta, commonly known as the Disjunct Lady's Slipper, is a striking orchid species native to the cloud forests of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that feature a distinctive slipper-shaped pouch, which is a key feature of the orchid family. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insects.

Lepanthes disjuncta is valued for its ornamental beauty and plays an important role in the biodiversity of its native ecosystems.
