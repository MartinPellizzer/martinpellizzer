Lavandula canescens

Lavandula canescens, commonly known as silver lavender, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and spikes of fragrant, violet-blue flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping. Its essential oils, derived from the flowers, are widely used in aromatherapy and perfumery.

Lavandula canescens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
