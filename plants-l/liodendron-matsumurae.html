Liodendron matsumurae

Liodendron matsumurae, commonly known as the Japanese magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Magnoliaceae family and is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

Liodendron matsumurae is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
