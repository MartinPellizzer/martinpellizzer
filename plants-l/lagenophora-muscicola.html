Lagenophora muscicola

Lagenophora muscicola, commonly known as the mossy orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. The plant has a unique morphology, featuring a short rhizome and a single, erect stem that bears a few lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, often clustered in a spike, and are adapted for pollination by small insects.

Lagenophora muscicola is found primarily in tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and is known for its delicate appearance and ecological significance in its native habitats.
