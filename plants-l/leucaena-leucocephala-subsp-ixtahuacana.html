Leucaena leucocephala subsp. ixtahuacana

Leucaena leucocephala subsp.

ixtahuacana, commonly known as the Mexican tree of heaven, is a shrub or small tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, white, spherical flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant produces legume pods containing small, flat, brown seeds. It is a nitrogen-fixing species, making it valuable for soil enrichment and reforestation efforts.

This subspecies is often used in agroforestry and as a forage crop due to its fast growth and adaptability.
