Lepanthes athena

Lepanthes athena, commonly known as the Athena orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that often display a striking combination of colors. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually elongated and strap-like, adapted for capturing moisture from the air.

Lepanthes athena is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
