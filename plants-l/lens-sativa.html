Lens sativa

Lens sativa, commonly known as the chickpea, is an annual leguminous plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It produces pods containing numerous seeds, which are widely used in cuisines around the world. The plant has a bushy growth habit with trifoliate leaves and small white or pink flowers. Its seeds are rich in protein and are a staple in many diets, particularly in South Asian and Middle Eastern countries.

The botanical name reflects its classification within the Fabaceae family, known for nitrogen-fixing capabilities.
