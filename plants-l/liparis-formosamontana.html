Liparis formosamontana

Liparis formosamontana, commonly known as the Formosa mountain orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, greenish-white flowers with a distinct lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments, often on rocky or humus-rich substrates. Its rhizomes are fibrous, and it produces small, clustered pseudobulbs that support its growth.

Liparis formosamontana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
