Lithophragma australe

Lithophragma australe, commonly known as the coastal saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the western coast of North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky, coastal areas and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are usually basal, forming a rosette, and are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Lithophragma australe is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging, coastal climates.
