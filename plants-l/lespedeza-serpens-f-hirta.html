Lespedeza serpens f. hirta

Lespedeza serpens f. hirta, commonly known as hairy lespedeza, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are usually pink or reddish in color, blooming in late summer. Its hairy stems and leaves give it a distinctive appearance, and it is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in certain environments.
