Liparis millei

Liparis millei, commonly known as the Chinese orchid or the golden orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a distinctive appearance, featuring a single, showy flower that is typically yellow or orange in color. The plant has a pseudobulb structure, which helps it store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it often grows in shaded, moist environments.

Liparis millei is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
