Lannea stolzii

Lannea stolzii, commonly known as the African velvet tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that give it a velvety appearance, and its distinctive, spiny fruits that contain edible seeds. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in regions with low rainfall. It plays an important ecological role by providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.

Additionally, its wood and leaves have been used traditionally for medicinal and practical purposes.
