Lamium multifidum

Lamium multifidum, commonly known as the greater calamint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are usually gray-green in color and have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The plant is known for its aromatic leaves and has been used historically in herbal remedies.
