Leucopogon abnormis

Leucopogon abnormis, commonly known as the variable leucopogon, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, often arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal and sandy environments.

Leucopogon abnormis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
