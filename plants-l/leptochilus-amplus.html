Leptochilus amplus

Leptochilus amplus, commonly known as the broadleaf marsh orchid, is a species of orchid native to wetland and marsh habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its elongated, grass-like leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, adapting to its wetland environment with specialized root structures. Its reproductive strategy involves forming symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi to enhance nutrient uptake.

Leptochilus amplus plays an important ecological role in maintaining the biodiversity of its wetland ecosystem.
