Lonicera catalinensis

Lonicera catalinensis, commonly known as the Catalina honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to the island of Catalina in California.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically blooming in spring. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that contains several seeds, and it is adapted to the coastal climate of its native habitat.

Lonicera catalinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
