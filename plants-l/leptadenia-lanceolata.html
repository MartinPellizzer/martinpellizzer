Leptadenia lanceolata

Leptadenia lanceolata, commonly known as the African milk vine, is a climbing shrub native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is known for its milky sap, which can be toxic if ingested.

Leptadenia lanceolata is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.
