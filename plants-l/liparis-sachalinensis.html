Liparis sachalinensis

Liparis sachalinensis, commonly known as the Sakhalin orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Russian Far East.

It is characterized by its delicate, grass-like foliage and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its unique floral structure.

Liparis sachalinensis is relatively rare and is of interest to botanists due to its specific ecological requirements.
