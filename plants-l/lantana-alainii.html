Lantana alainii

Lantana alainii, commonly known as the Hawaiian lantana, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange and red, depending on the stage of bloom. Lantana alainii is adapted to the tropical climate of Hawaii, thriving in full sun and well-drained soils.

Its flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and birds, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
