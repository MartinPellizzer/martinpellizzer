Linaria weilleri var. antiatlantica

Linaria weilleri var. antiatlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of western Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along the shoreline. The plant features slender, branching stems and produces clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers with two lips. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

It is well adapted to saline and wind-swept environments, making it a hardy species found in coastal ecosystems.
