Lycopodioides willdenowii

Lycopodioides willdenowii, commonly known as the slender clubmoss, is a member of the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features slender, upright stems covered in small, scale-like leaves. It produces spores in cone-like structures called strobili, which are borne at the tips of the branches.

Lycopodioides willdenowii is characterized by its primitive morphology, reflecting its ancient evolutionary lineage within the lycophyte group.
