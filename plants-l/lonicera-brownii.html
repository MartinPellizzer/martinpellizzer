Lonicera brownii

Lonicera brownii, commonly known as the Brown's honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces fragrant, yellowish-white flowers in the spring. The plant bears red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Lonicera brownii typically grows in moist, wooded areas and can reach heights of up to 6 feet.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in natural habitats.
