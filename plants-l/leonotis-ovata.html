Leonotis ovata

Leonotis ovata, commonly known as the lion's tail, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that resemble the tail of a lion, hence its common name. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a soft, velvety texture.

Leonotis ovata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
