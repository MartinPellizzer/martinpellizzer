Lamium maculatum f. alpinum

Lamium maculatum f. alpinum, commonly known as the Alpine spotted deadnettle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with dark green, heart-shaped leaves that often have distinctive white or silver markings. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in cool, moist, and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for shaded garden environments.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is widely cultivated in horticulture.
