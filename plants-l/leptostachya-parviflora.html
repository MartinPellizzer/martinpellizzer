Leptostachya parviflora

Leptostachya parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered lepidothamnus, is a member of the Poaceae family, native to Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a grass-like texture.

The flowers are tiny and clustered, producing light seeds that are dispersed by wind.
