Lobivia cruciaureispina

Lobivia cruciaureispina, commonly known as the Cruciate Spine Lobivia, is a species of cactus native to the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems and clusters of spiny, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a compact growth habit and is well adapted to arid environments with minimal water requirements. Its spines are modified leaves that help reduce water loss and provide protection from herbivores.

Lobivia cruciaureispina is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
