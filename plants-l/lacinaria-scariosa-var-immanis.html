Lacinaria scariosa var. immanis

Lacinaria scariosa var. immanis, commonly known as the broad-leaved lacinaria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white, silky hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its striking foliage and delicate floral display.
