Lotus glaucus subsp. salvagensis

Lotus glaucus subsp.

salvagensis, commonly known as the Salvage lotus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features erect, slender stems that bear palmately divided leaves with a bluish-gray hue.

The flowers are typically pink to purple, with a distinctive spurred petal at the base. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
