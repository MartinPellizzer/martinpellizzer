Loranthus hexasepalus

Loranthus hexasepalus, commonly known as the six-lobed mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to host trees through its haustoria, drawing water and nutrients from the host.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia. The plant has simple, alternate leaves and produces small, white or yellowish flowers. Its fruit is a fleshy, red berry that contains several seeds.

The species is notable for its ecological role in supporting birdlife, as the berries are a food source for various frugivorous birds.
