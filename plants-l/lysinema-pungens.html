Lysinema pungens

Lysinema pungens, commonly known as the purple tansy, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of purple to pinkish-yellow flowers. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Lysinema pungens is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
