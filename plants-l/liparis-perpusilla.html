Liparis perpusilla

Liparis perpusilla, commonly known as the dwarf orchid, is a small, herbaceous orchid native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky crevices. The plant has a slender, often branched stem and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple. Its roots are fibrous and adapted for absorbing moisture from the soil.

Liparis perpusilla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its subtle beauty.
