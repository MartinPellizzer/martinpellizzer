Lindernia uvens

Lindernia uvens, commonly known as the velvetleaf lindernia, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 feet. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a velvety texture, and it produces spikes of showy, tubular flowers in shades of pink to purple. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
