Lepidium subulatum

Lepidium subulatum, commonly known as prickly rocket, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants. The plant is characterized by its small, serrated leaves and clusters of tiny, yellow flowers. It thrives in arid and rocky environments, often growing in coastal areas.

Lepidium subulatum is known for its ability to withstand harsh conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
