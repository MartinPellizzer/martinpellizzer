Lotodes leucanthum

Lotodes leucanthum, commonly known as the white water lily, is a aquatic plant native to parts of Asia.

It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. The plant features broad, floating leaves that are often covered in a waxy layer to repel water. Its flowers are white with a yellow center, blooming during the summer months.

The roots of Lotodes leucanthum help stabilize the sediment in aquatic ecosystems.
