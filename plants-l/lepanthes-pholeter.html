Lepanthes pholeter

Lepanthes pholeter, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers, which often have a subtle greenish or reddish tinge. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical forests. Its leaves are usually long and narrow, adapted for capturing moisture from the air.

Lepanthes pholeter is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
