Lobelia cordigera var. fatalis

Lobelia cordigera var. fatalis, commonly known as the death's-head hawkmoth plant, is a striking member of the Campanulaceae family.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its vibrant purple to blue flowers, which are tubular in shape and attract various pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and ecological significance in supporting local wildlife.
