Lophochloa cristata var. pseudolobulata

Lophochloa cristata var. pseudolobulata, commonly known as the crested grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tufted growth habit and the presence of a prominent, often branched inflorescence. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a smooth surface and a bluish-green coloration. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
