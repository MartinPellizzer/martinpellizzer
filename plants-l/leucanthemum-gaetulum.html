Leucanthemum gaetulum

Leucanthemum gaetulum, commonly known as the Gaetulan daisy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its white ray florets surrounding a central yellow disk, giving it a daisy-like appearance. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and prefers well-drained soil in full sun. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Leucanthemum gaetulum is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative plant in horticulture due to its ornamental value.
