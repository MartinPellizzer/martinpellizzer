Lilium japonicum var. brownii

Lilium japonicum var. brownii, commonly known as the Brown's Japanese lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with a slight fragrance. Its leaves are narrow and lanceolate, arranged in a basal rosette, and its bulbs are used in traditional horticulture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant blooms.
