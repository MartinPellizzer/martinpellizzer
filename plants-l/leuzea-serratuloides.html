Leuzea serratuloides

Leuzea serratuloides, commonly known as the serrated leuzea, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, giving it its common name. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in loose clusters, and bloom in the summer months.

Leuzea serratuloides is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
