Lotodes tenax

Lotodes tenax, commonly known as the hardy water lily, is a perennial aquatic plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and lakes. The plant features broad, floating leaves that are usually green with a waxy surface, and its flowers are typically white or pale pink, blooming in summer. It has a robust root system that helps stabilize the sediment and improve water quality.

Lotodes tenax is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in aquatic ecosystems.
