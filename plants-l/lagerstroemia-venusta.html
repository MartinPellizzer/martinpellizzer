Lagerstroemia venusta

Lagerstroemia venusta, commonly known as the velvetwood or rose-of-Sharon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and its showy, panicle-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and lavender. The plant produces small, round, red or orange fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Lagerstroemia venusta is also valued for its ability to tolerate urban conditions and its resistance to many pests and diseases.
