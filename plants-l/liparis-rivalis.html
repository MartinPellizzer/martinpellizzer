Liparis rivalis

Liparis rivalis, commonly known as the rival orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, grass-like leaves and small, often fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or mountainous regions. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil.

Liparis rivalis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation under suitable conditions.
