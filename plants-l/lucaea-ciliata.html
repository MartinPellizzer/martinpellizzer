Lucaea ciliata

Lucaea ciliata, commonly known as the hairy leaf orchid, is a tropical plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its elongated, hairy leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. It prefers well-drained soil and consistent moisture, making it a popular choice for greenhouse cultivation.

Due to its ornamental value, Lucaea ciliata is often sought after by plant enthusiasts and horticulturists.
