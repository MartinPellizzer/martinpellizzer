Lycopodium obovalifolium

Lycopodium obovalifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaf clubmoss, is a species of clubmoss belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant characterized by its erect, branching stems and scale-like leaves. The plant produces spore-bearing cones, which are typically clustered at the tips of the branches. Native to certain regions of North America, it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the understory vegetation in its native habitat.
