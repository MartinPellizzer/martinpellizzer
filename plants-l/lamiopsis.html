Lamiopsis

Lamiopsis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lamiaceae, is a genus of flowering plants in the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, often fragrant flowers. The plants typically have a herbaceous growth habit and are found in various regions across the Northern Hemisphere. Lamiopsis species are often used in traditional medicine for their purported therapeutic properties.

They thrive in moist, well-drained soils and are often found in woodland or rocky habitats.
