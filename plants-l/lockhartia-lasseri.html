Lockhartia lasseri

Lockhartia lasseri, commonly known as the orchid with the striking purple and white flowers, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a unique coloration pattern. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts for its beauty and rarity.
