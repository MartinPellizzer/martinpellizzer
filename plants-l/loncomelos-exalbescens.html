Loncomelos exalbescens

Loncomelos exalbescens, commonly known as the white-flowered morning glory, is a member of the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, trumpet-shaped white flowers that open in the morning and close by midday. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its rapid growth and climbing habit make it both a decorative and sometimes invasive species in certain environments.
