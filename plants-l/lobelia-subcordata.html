Lobelia subcordata

Lobelia subcordata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf lobelia, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The leaves are narrow and often have a slightly heart-shaped base, giving the plant its scientific name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
