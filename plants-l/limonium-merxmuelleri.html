Limonium merxmuelleri

Limonium merxmuelleri, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its blue to purple flowers and spiny, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. It is a perennial species that produces its flowers in summer, often forming dense clusters.

Limonium merxmuelleri is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate salt spray.
