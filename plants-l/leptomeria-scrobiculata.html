Leptomeria scrobiculata

Leptomeria scrobiculata, commonly known as the mountain thimbleweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. Its fruit is a small, dry capsule that contains several seeds.

Leptomeria scrobiculata is often found in shaded environments and is valued for its ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
