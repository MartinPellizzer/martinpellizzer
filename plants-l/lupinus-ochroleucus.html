Lupinus ochroleucus

Lupinus ochroleucus, commonly known as the yellow lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and bright yellow flowers. The plant produces compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and has a fibrous root system. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.

Lupinus ochroleucus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for ecological restoration projects.
