Lilium lateritium

Lilium lateritium, commonly known as the alpine lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of showy, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Lilium lateritium is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in alpine gardens.
