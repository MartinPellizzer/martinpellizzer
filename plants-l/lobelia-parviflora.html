Lobelia parviflora

Lobelia parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered lobelia, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.

Lobelia parviflora is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
