Lasiocladus anthospermifolius var. linearifolius

Lasiocladus anthospermifolius var. linearifolius, commonly known as the linear-leaved sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its succulent, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in desert botany and xerophytic plant communities.
