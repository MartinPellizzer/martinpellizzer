Limonium komarovii

Limonium komarovii, commonly known as the Komarov's sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces spikes of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas. It is a member of the Plumbagaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Limonium komarovii is drought-tolerant and prefers full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
