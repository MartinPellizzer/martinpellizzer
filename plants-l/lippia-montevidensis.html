Lippia montevidensis

Lippia montevidensis, commonly known as the Montevidense verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Uruguay and parts of Argentina.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, small, tubular flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant has hairy, narrow leaves and thrives in well-drained soils, often found in dry, open habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
