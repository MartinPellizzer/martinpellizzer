Lonicera robertsonii

Lonicera robertsonii, commonly known as the Japanese honeysuckle, is a vigorous climbing vine native to East Asia.

It features opposite, heart-shaped leaves and produces fragrant, tubular yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and runners. Its berries, which are orange-red and edible, mature in the fall.

However, it is considered an invasive species in many regions due to its competitive nature and ability to outcompete native vegetation.
