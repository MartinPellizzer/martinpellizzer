Lycium chinense subsp. rhombifolium

Lycium chinense subsp.

rhombifolium, commonly known as Chinese wolfberry or Goji berry, is a shrub native to China and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its spiny stems, small yellow flowers, and red berries that are rich in antioxidants.

The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are ovate to rhombic in shape, and the plant is often cultivated for its edible fruits.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
