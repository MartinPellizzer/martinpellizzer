Leucanthemum zaianicum

Leucanthemum zaianicum, commonly known as the Zaian tansy, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branched.

Leucanthemum zaianicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
