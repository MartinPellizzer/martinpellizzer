Lagotis glauca var. lanceolata

Lagotis glauca var. lanceolata, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy blue to purple blooms. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland environments and has lance-shaped, bluish-green leaves. It produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
