Lagerstroemia noei var. longifolia

Lagerstroemia noei var. longifolia, commonly known as the longifolia crape myrtle, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It features showy, panicle-shaped flowers that range from pink to red, appearing in summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that turn red or orange in the fall. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem with smooth bark and fragrant blooms that attract pollinators.
