Lathyrus pratensis f. pallidiflorus

Lathyrus pratensis f. pallidiflorus, commonly known as the pale-flowered vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, branching stems and trifoliate leaves. The flowers are typically pale pink or white, with a distinctive spurred petal, and appear in clusters along the stems. This plant is often found in meadows and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils.

It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility through its symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.
