Lepidium pubescens var. fallax

Lepidium pubescens var. fallax, commonly known as the hairy cress, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like mustard and cabbage. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and has small, white or yellow flowers. Its leaves are hairy and lobed, and it produces small, rounded pods that contain several seeds.

This species is often found in wetland areas and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
