Lophiolepis byzantina

Lophiolepis byzantina, commonly known as the Byzantine grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in dense, spike-like clusters, which are often surrounded by bracts. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas.

Lophiolepis byzantina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
