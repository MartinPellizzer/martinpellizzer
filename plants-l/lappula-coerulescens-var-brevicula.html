Lappula coerulescens var. brevicula

Lappula coerulescens var. brevicula, commonly known as the short-stemmed lappula, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, yellow flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky soils and is often found in tundra and alpine environments. Its life cycle is adapted to the short growing seasons of its habitat, with rapid growth and flowering.

The varietal form brevicula distinguishes it by its shorter stems and smaller flowers compared to the typical variety.
