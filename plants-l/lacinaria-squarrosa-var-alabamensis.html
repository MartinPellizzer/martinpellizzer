Lacinaria squarrosa var. alabamensis

Lacinaria squarrosa var. alabamensis, commonly known as the Alabama lacinaria, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, erect stems that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The leaves are lance-shaped with a distinctive, wavy margin and are typically arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are small and clustered in loose inflorescences, with pale blue to purple petals.

This plant thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open woods or along streambanks.
