Lespedeza juncea f. subsessilis

Lespedeza juncea f. subsessilis, commonly known as narrowleaf lespedeza, is a perennial flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often hairless stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellow to reddish flowers in late summer, which are followed by elongated, bean-like pods containing small seeds.

It is often found in open woodlands, meadows, and disturbed sites, and is valued for its ornamental appeal and nitrogen-fixing ability.
