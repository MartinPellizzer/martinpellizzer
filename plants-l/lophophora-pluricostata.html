Lophophora pluricostata

Lophophora pluricostata, commonly known as the Mexican jumping bean, is a small cacti species native to the Sierra Madre mountains of Mexico.

It is characterized by its ribbed, cylindrical stems that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by small, round fruits that contain seeds. Lophophora pluricostata is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

It is also known for its unique seed pods, which are often used in traditional practices.
