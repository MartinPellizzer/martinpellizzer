Lupinus amplus

Lupinus amplus, commonly known as the broadleaf lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces showy, pea-like flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, which bloom in summer. Lupinus amplus has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion and fixes nitrogen, enriching the soil.

It is often used in restoration projects due to its ecological benefits and ornamental value.
