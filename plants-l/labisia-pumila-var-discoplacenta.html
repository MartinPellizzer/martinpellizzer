Labisia pumila var. discoplacenta

Labisia pumila var. discoplacenta, commonly known as the red-flowered labisia, is a compact evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically dark green with a reddish or purplish underside. The plant produces small, fragrant, tubular flowers that are predominantly red or orange, often arranged in clusters at the tips of the branches. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a ornamental plant due to its vibrant floral display.

The variety discoplacenta is distinguished by its unique floral structure and coloration compared to other forms of Labisia pumila.
