Laurembergia repens var. brachypoda

Laurembergia repens var. brachypoda, commonly known as the dwarf laurembergia, is a low-growing perennial plant native to specific regions of South Africa.

It features small, clustered leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces tiny, pale blue or purple flowers during the summer months, which are clustered in umbels. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its compact size and ornamental foliage make it a popular plant among succulent enthusiasts.
