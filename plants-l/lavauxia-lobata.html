Lavauxia lobata

Lavauxia lobata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its small, often hairy leaves and clusters of fragrant, pink or white flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Lavauxia lobata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
