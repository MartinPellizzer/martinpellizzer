Lotus hispidus subsp. angustissimus

Lotus hispidus subsp.

angustissimus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lotus, is a perennial aquatic plant native to parts of Asia. It typically grows in slow-moving freshwater habitats such as ponds and lakes.

The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, hairy textures. Its flowers are pink to reddish in color and bloom during the summer months.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in wetland ecosystems.
