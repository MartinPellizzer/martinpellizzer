Lupinus latifolius var. canadensis

Lupinus latifolius var. canadensis, commonly known as the Canada lupine, is a perennial legume native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has palmately divided, pinnately veined leaves that are typically broad and lobed. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairies, open woods, and disturbed sites.

As a member of the legume family, it has the ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
