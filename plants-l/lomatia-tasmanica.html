Lomatia tasmanica

Lomatia tasmanica, commonly known as the Tasmanian lomatia, is a shrub native to Tasmania, characterized by its distinctive, woody, branched stems and aromatic leaves.

It features small, white, tubular flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has a dense, leathery foliage with a strong, spicy scent, which is particularly noticeable when the leaves are crushed. Lomatia tasmanica is adapted to grow in moist, shaded environments, often found in cool temperate rainforests.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a valuable component of Tasmania's native flora.
