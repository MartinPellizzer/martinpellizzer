Libanotis taurica

Libanotis taurica, commonly known as the Crimean bellflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, erect stems that bear showy blue to violet flowers resembling bells. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Libanotis taurica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
