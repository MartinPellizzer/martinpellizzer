Liatris elegans var. flabellata

Liatris elegans var. flabellata, commonly known as the flabellate blazing star, is a perennial herb native to the central and eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of narrow, spiky flower heads. The plant produces pink to purple flower spikes that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade in well-drained soils.

This species is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
