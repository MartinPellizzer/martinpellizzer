Lewisia disepala

Lewisia disepala, commonly known as the two-lobed Lewisia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is part of the Montiaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of fleshy, blue-green leaves. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Due to its limited range, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
