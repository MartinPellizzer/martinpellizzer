Lingnania tsiangii

Lingnania tsiangii, commonly known as the Chinese yew, is a rare and critically endangered species native to China.

It is a member of the Taxaceae family and is characterized by its slow growth and evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, reddish berries that contain the seeds, which are dispersed by birds. Due to its limited distribution and habitat destruction, it is highly vulnerable to extinction.

Conservation efforts are underway to protect and propagate this unique botanical species.
