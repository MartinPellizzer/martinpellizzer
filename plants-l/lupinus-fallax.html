Lupinus fallax

Lupinus fallax, commonly known as the slender lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of pea-like flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant has compound leaves with several oval-shaped leaflets and produces legume pods that contain several seeds. Lupinus fallax thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

It is a member of the legume family and has the ability to fix nitrogen through its root nodules, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
