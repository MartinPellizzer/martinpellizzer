Lonicera sieversiana

Lonicera sieversiana, commonly known as the Sievers' honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to yellow, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its red to orange berries are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

Lonicera sieversiana is often found in moist, woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
