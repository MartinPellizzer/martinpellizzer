Lepanthes smaragdina

Lepanthes smaragdina, commonly known as the Emerald Lady orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking greenish-yellow flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns and a prominent, spurred lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its leaves are usually dark green and strap-like, providing a lush backdrop to its showy blooms.

Lepanthes smaragdina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and rarity.
