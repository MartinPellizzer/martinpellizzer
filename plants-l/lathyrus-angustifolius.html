Lathyrus angustifolius

Lathyrus angustifolius, commonly known as narrow-leaved vetch, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in grasslands and disturbed soils, and it produces pods containing edible seeds.

It is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a valuable plant in agricultural settings.
