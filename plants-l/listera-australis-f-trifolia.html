Listera australis f. trifolia

Listera australis f. trifolia, commonly known as the three-leafed wintergreen, is a herbaceous perennial belonging to the Primulaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval to lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically valuable component of temperate woodland ecosystems.
