Leucothoe pulchra

Leucothoe pulchra, commonly known as the pink azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of showy, pink to white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has opposite, elliptical leaves that are dark green and glossy on top and lighter underneath. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

Leucothoe pulchra is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to form dense, compact growth.
