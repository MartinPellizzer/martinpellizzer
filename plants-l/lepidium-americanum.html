Lepidium americanum

Lepidium americanum, commonly known as peppergrass, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and hairy stems. Peppergrass has small, edible seeds that are sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.

It is an annual plant that often thrives in open fields and along roadsides.
