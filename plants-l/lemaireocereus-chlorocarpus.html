Lemaireocereus chlorocarpus

Lemaireocereus chlorocarpus, commonly known as the green fruit cactus, is a species native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a small, columnar cactus with a green, spiny stem that often branches at the base. The plant produces small, pale green fruits that are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it thrives in well-drained soil with minimal water.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
