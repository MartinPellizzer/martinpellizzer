Lindera erythrocarpa var. longipes

Lindera erythrocarpa var. longipes, commonly known as the long-peduncled red-fruited ligusticum, is a variant of the Lindera erythrocarpa species native to eastern Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces distinctive red, fleshy berries that persist through winter, providing food for various wildlife. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of understory vegetation in its native habitat.
