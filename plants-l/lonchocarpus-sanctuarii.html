Lonchocarpus sanctuarii

Lonchocarpus sanctuarii, commonly known as the sacred flame tree, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy red flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a dramatic visual display. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and possesses compound leaves with an odd number of leaflets. Its seeds are encased in hard, woody pods, and the plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, nutrient-rich soils.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
