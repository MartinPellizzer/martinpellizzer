Lycianthes maxonii var. appendiculata

Lycianthes maxonii var. appendiculata, commonly known as the Mexican nightshade, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange to red berries, which are edible but have a bitter taste. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open woods.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and potential use in traditional medicine.
