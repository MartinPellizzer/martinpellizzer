Lobelia fervens var. asperulata

Lobelia fervens var. asperulata, commonly known as the roughleaf lobelia, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and clusters of small, tubular blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.

Its flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.
