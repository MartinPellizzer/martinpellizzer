Licuala borneensis

Licuala borneensis, commonly known as the Bornean palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Borneo and is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests. Its trunk is typically slender and covered with persistent leaf bases, giving it a unique appearance.

The Bornean palm is valued for its ornamental qualities and has also been studied for its potential in ecological restoration projects.
