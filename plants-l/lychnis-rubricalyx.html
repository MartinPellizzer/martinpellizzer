Lychnis rubricalyx

Lychnis rubricalyx, commonly known as the red-stemmed campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, reddish stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with purple. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or borders.

Lychnis rubricalyx is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
