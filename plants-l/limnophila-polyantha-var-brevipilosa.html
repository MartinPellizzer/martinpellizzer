Limnophila polyantha var. brevipilosa

Limnophila polyantha var. brevipilosa, commonly known as the narrow-stemmed water hyssop, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, arranged in dense spikes, and attract various pollinators.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
