Luffa cordifolia

Luffa cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf luffa, is a tropical vine belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and hairy stems, which are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces large, pale yellow flowers that are typically unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant. Its fruit, which resembles a sponge gourd, matures to a light green color and can be harvested when mature for culinary or cosmetic use.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
