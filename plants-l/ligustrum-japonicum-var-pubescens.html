Ligustrum japonicum var. pubescens

Ligustrum japonicum var. pubescens, commonly known as Japanese privet, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are pubescent, or hairy, on both surfaces, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are followed by dark blue berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.

However, it can become invasive in some regions if not properly managed.
