Lasianthus maculatus

Lasianthus maculatus, commonly known as the spotted leaf lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, spotted leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as an herb or shrub, often found in moist, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually bright red or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
