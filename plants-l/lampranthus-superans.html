Lampranthus superans

Lampranthus superans, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that bloom in summer. The plant features fleshy, upright stems and leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its striking appearance and drought tolerance make it a valuable addition to xeriscaped landscapes.
