Lupinus subvexus var. phoeniceus

Lupinus subvexus var. phoeniceus, commonly known as the purple lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of vivid purple or pink flowers. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to native plant gardens.

Its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil makes it beneficial for soil enrichment and ecological restoration.
