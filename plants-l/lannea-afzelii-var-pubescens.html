Lannea afzelii var. pubescens

Lannea afzelii var. pubescens, commonly known as the African rock apple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and compound leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers followed by small, fleshy, orange-red fruits that are edible for humans and animals. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in dry savanna and woodland habitats.

Lannea afzelii var. pubescens plays an important ecological role in supporting local wildlife and is also valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
