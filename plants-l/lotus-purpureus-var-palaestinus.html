Lotus purpureus var. palaestinus

Lotus purpureus var. palaestinus, commonly known as the Palestinian lotus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and produces striking pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is adapted to grow in rocky or sandy soils and is drought-tolerant. It is often found in coastal areas and along the edges of cultivated fields.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.
