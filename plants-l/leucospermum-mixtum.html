Leucospermum mixtum

Leucospermum mixtum, commonly known as the African firecracker, is a striking evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It features showy, cone-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in spring and summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are adapted to withstand dry conditions, making it drought-tolerant. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often growing in coastal or mountainous regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in mild climates.
