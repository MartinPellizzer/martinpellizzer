Laurus nobilis var. floribunda

Laurus nobilis var. floribunda, commonly known as the fragrant laurel or sweet bay laurel, is a variant of the bay tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark green, glossy leaves that are widely used in cooking and traditional medicine. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by dark blue berries, which are ornamental and edible. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and hedging.

Its historical significance dates back to ancient times, where it was revered for its symbolic and practical uses.
