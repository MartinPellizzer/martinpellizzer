Lycopodium sitchense

Lycopodium sitchense, commonly known as the Sichuan clubmoss, is a species of vascular plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and rocky areas. The plant features a short, stout stem with whorled leaves that are linear and often covered in tiny, scale-like structures. Lycopodium sitchense reproduces via spores, which are released from specialized structures called sporangia located on the underside of its leaves.
