Liabum mikanioides

Liabum mikanioides, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth habit and ability to climb or trail along surfaces. Its flowers typically open in the morning and close by midday, hence its common name.

Liabum mikanioides is often used in ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
