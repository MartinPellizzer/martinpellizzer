Loranthus paniculatus

Loranthus paniculatus, commonly known as the mistletoe of the genus Loranthus, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while it provides some organic compounds through photosynthesis. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often pollinated by insects. Its berries are typically red or yellow and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This species is found in various regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia, often growing in tropical and subtropical climates.
