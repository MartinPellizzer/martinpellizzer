Lupinus mollisifolius

Lupinus mollisifolius, commonly known as the soft-leaved lupine, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and tall spikes of blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in open, dry habitats. Its flowers are highly attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Lupinus mollisifolius is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
