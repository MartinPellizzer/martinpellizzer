Lasianthus appressus

Lasianthus appressus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. The plant has opposite, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Lasianthus appressus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
