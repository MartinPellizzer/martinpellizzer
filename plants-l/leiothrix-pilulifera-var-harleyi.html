Leiothrix pilulifera var. harleyi

Leiothrix pilulifera var. harleyi, commonly known as the Harlequin orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, multicolored flowers that resemble a harlequin's costume, hence its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded forest environments. Its flowers are small but highly ornamental, often displaying a mix of red, yellow, and green hues.

The species is valued for its unique appearance and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic and ecological significance.
