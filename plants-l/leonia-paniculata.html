Leonia paniculata

Leonia paniculata, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous clusters of bright yellow flowers that resemble showers of golden beads. The plant has compound leaves with multiple leaflets, and it produces small, orange fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Leonia paniculata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
