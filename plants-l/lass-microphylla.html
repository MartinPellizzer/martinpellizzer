Lass microphylla

Lass microphylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lillypillly, is a species of evergreen shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its small, glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically less than 2 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers during the spring and summer months. It is often found in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions.

Lass microphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in gardens and landscaping.
