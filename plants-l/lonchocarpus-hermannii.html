Lonchocarpus hermannii

Lonchocarpus hermannii, commonly known as the Hermann's legume, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces striking orange to red flowers, which are often clustered in dense inflorescences. Its roots contain alkaloids, making it a subject of interest in pharmacological research.

However, it is also known to be toxic to livestock, requiring caution in agricultural settings.
