Laurus cuipala

Laurus cuipala, commonly known as the Cuipala laurel, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other well-known plants like laurel and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are typically dark green and glossy. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by small, round berries.

The botanical features of Laurus cuipala make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
