Lapathum arborescens

Lapathum arborescens, commonly known as the tall white toothcup, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant features opposite, heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of small, white flowers in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Lapathum arborescens is often found in the eastern United States and is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
