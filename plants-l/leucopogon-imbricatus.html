Leucopogon imbricatus

Leucopogon imbricatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in heathlands and dry forests, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom in spring and attract various pollinators.

Leucopogon imbricatus is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
