Lotus arabicus

Lotus arabicus, commonly known as the Arabian lotus, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking pink or purple flowers that bloom during the summer months. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, bean-like pods that contain its seeds. It is adapted to thrive in dry, saline soils and is often found in coastal areas and desert oases.

The Arabian lotus is also valued for its ornamental beauty and has cultural significance in some regions.
