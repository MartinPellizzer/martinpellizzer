Lantana cujabensis var. parvifolia

Lantana cujabensis var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-flowered lantana, is a species native to South America, particularly found in regions of Colombia and Venezuela.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring dense, aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to yellow, depending on the stage of bloom. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adaptable to various soil types, though it prefers well-drained conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
