Linum berlandieri var. berlandieri

Linum berlandieri var. berlandieri, commonly known as desert flax, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is adapted to arid environments, making it drought-tolerant. It produces capsules containing numerous small seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Linaceae and is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators in desert ecosystems.
