Lolium rigidum var. longiglume

Lolium rigidum var. longiglume, commonly known as rigid fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its stiff, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in a variety of habitats, including grasslands, roadsides, and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often clustered in dense, cylindrical formations.

It is well-adapted to arid and semi-arid conditions, making it a resilient species in dry environments.
