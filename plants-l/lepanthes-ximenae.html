Lepanthes ximenae

Lepanthes ximenae, commonly known as the Ximena's slipper orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that are typically pale green to white in color. The plant produces showy, slipper-shaped petals and a distinctive, hairy lip that aids in pollination. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Lepanthes ximenae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is a subject of interest in botanical research.
