Lactuca matsumurae var. dissecta

Lactuca matsumurae var. dissecta, commonly known as the Japanese lettuce or dissected lettuce, is a variant of the lactuca genus native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves that give it a delicate, lettuce-like appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are edible and have a mild, slightly bitter taste, making it a minor vegetable in some Asian cuisines.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and other composite flowers.
