Lobivia zecheri f. fungiflora

Lobivia zecheri f. fungiflora, commonly known as the Fungiflora Lobivia, is a unique cactus species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and dense covering of white to pale blue spines. The plant produces striking, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange, blooming during the warmer months. Its foliage is minimal, with only a few short, woolly leaves at the base.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and adaptability to dry, well-drained soils.
