Luma seriatoramosa

Luma seriatoramosa, commonly known as the hairy luma, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a hairy texture, contributing to its common name.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
