Lopimia dasypetala

Lopimia dasypetala, commonly known as the rough-leaved lopimia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a rough texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of rainforests.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
