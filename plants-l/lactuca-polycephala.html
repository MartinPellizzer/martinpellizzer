Lactuca polycephala

Lactuca polycephala, commonly known as the many-headed lettuce, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it typically grows in disturbed soils and along roadsides. The plant features a rosette of pale green, spoon-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in a single head, resembling a dandelion.

Lactuca polycephala is not commonly cultivated for food but is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
