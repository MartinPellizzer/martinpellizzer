Lathraea phelypaea

Lathraea phelypaea, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a unique succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, leaf-like structures that store water, enabling it to survive in arid environments. The plant produces small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in spring, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. Its growth habit is epiphytic, meaning it grows on other plants without being parasitic.

Lathraea phelypaea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and as a houseplant.
