Loranthus liquidambaricola

Loranthus liquidambaricola, commonly known as the sweet gum mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the roots of sweet gum trees (Liquidambar styraciflua).

It derives some of its nutrients from the host plant through its root system, while also performing photosynthesis using its leaves. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers and red berries that are dispersed by birds. It is often found in the southeastern United States, where it thrives in forested areas.

Its presence can impact the health of its host tree, sometimes leading to reduced growth or even mortality in severe cases.
