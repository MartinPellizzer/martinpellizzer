Lithospermum macromeria

Lithospermum macromeria, commonly known as the largeleaf lithosperm, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as woodland edges and riverbanks. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces showy, violet-blue flowers in the summer months. Its root system is notable for containing compounds used in traditional medicine.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and maintaining soil stability.
