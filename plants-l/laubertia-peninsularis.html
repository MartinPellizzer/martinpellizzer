Laubertia peninsularis

Laubertia peninsularis, commonly known as the Peninsula grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green in color. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is adapted to dry, rocky soils and can thrive in full sun conditions.

Laubertia peninsularis is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
