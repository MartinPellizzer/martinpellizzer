Lasianthus ellipsoideus

Lasianthus ellipsoideus, commonly known as the oval-leaved lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and spikes of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its flowers are often pale blue or purple, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.

The species is relatively easy to cultivate, making it a popular choice for gardeners in tropical and subtropical regions.
