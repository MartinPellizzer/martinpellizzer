Lilium martagon subsp. alternifolium

Lilium martagon subsp.

alternifolium, commonly known as the Turk's Cap Lily, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its striking, recurved, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are alternately arranged along the stem. Its bulbs are typically small and are adapted to survive in cold, high-altitude environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
