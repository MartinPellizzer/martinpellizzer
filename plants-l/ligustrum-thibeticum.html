Ligustrum thibeticum

Ligustrum thibeticum, commonly known as Tibetan privet, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayan regions of India, Nepal, and Bhutan.

It typically grows in mountainous areas at elevations ranging from 2,000 to 4,000 meters. The plant features small, oval-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter in color underneath. Its flowers are clustered in small umbels and are usually white or pale yellow, followed by small, fleshy berries.

Ligustrum thibeticum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
