Lupinus lyleianus

Lupinus lyleianus, commonly known as the Lyle's lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of spiny, erect stems. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

Lupinus lyleianus is adapted to rocky and dry soils, making it a hardy member of the legume family.
