Lundinia plumbea

Lundinia plumbea, commonly known as the blue lundinia, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological importance.

Lundinia plumbea is typically found in moist, woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and native biodiversity support.
