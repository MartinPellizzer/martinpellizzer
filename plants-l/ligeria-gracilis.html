Ligeria gracilis

Ligeria gracilis, commonly known as the slender ligeria, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a member of the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its delicate, hairy stems and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
