Lespedeza virginica var. typica

Lespedeza virginica var. typica, commonly known as the Virginia lespedeza, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval, serrated leaflets. The plant produces small, pinkish to reddish flower clusters in late summer, which are arranged in dense panicles. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common sight in open woodlands and disturbed areas.

Lespedeza virginica var. typica is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
