Leptostachya secundiflora

Leptostachya secundiflora, commonly known as the mountain bamboo, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high elevations of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its clumping growth habit and slender, bamboo-like stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, inconspicuous flowers. Leptostachya secundiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
