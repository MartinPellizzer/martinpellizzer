Litsea pallida

Litsea pallida, commonly known as the white-flowered litsea, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and avocados. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth surface. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in panicles, making it an attractive ornamental plant.

The fruit is a small, spherical drupe that turns from green to red when mature.
