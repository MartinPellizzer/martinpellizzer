Lespedeza stuevei var. neglecta

Lespedeza stuevei var. neglecta, commonly known as the slender lespedeza, is a perennial herb native to parts of East Asia, including China and Korea.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pink to red flowers in clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for various ecological conditions.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it beneficial for soil enrichment in native plant gardens.
