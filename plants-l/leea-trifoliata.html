Leea trifoliata

Leea trifoliata, commonly known as the three-leaved vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged symmetrically. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. It is often found growing in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, shaded environments.

Leea trifoliata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
