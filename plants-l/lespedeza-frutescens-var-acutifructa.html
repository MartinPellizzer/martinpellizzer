Lespedeza frutescens var. acutifructa

Lespedeza frutescens var. acutifructa, commonly known as the sharp-fruited lespedeza, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems that are often branched and covered in fine hairs. Its small, pea-like flowers are usually pink to reddish in color and appear in clusters.

The plant produces small, oblong fruits that are sharply pointed, giving it its common name.
