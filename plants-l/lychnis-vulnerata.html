Lychnis vulnerata

Lychnis vulnerata, commonly known as the woundwort, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the carnation family (Caryophyllaceae) and typically grows in moist, grassy areas. The plant features upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually pink or red, with five petals and a distinctive central white spot.

Lychnis vulnerata is known for its medicinal properties, historically used to treat wounds and skin conditions.
