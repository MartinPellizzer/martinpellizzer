Litsea glutinosa var. platyphylla

Litsea glutinosa var. platyphylla, commonly known as the broad-leafed litsea, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and avocado trees. The plant is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves that are glossy and often have a slight waxy coating. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its attractive foliage and seasonal interest.
