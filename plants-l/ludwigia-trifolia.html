Ludwigia trifolia

Ludwigia trifolia, commonly known as the three-leaf water primrose, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its small, bright green, trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, making it a common aquatic or semi-aquatic species. It is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams.

Ludwigia trifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in water garden settings.
