Lepidium flavum var. typicum

Lepidium flavum var. typicum, commonly known as the yellow rocket, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and fern-like leaves, which are often used in herbal medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

The seeds of Lepidium flavum var. typicum are small and can be dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.
