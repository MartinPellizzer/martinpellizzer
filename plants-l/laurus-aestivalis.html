Laurus aestivalis

Laurus aestivalis, commonly known as summer laurel, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like bay laurel. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate, and often serrated leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, followed by small, round berries that are edible but not commonly consumed.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various soil conditions.
