Lachnanthes caroliana

Lachnanthes caroliana, commonly known as the Carolina lily, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Lachnanthes caroliana is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
