Lobelia imperialis var. kanitzii

Lobelia imperialis var. kanitzii, commonly known as the Kanitz's royal lobelia, is a striking plant native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence composed of numerous small, tubular blue or violet flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. This variety thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a desirable addition to rock gardens or alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant color and unique floral structure.
