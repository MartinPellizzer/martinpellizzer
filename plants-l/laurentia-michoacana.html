Laurentia michoacana

Laurentia michoacana, commonly known as the Michoacan laurel, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are typically glossy and elliptical in shape. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by small, dark berries.

This species is often found in moist, shaded areas of forests and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
