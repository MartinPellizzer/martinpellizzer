Lippia asperrima var. longipedunculata

Lippia asperrima var. longipedunculata, commonly known as the rough lippia, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It features small, aromatic leaves with a serrated margin and produces clusters of pink to purple flowers. The plant is characterized by its branched, woody stems and is often found in dry, open habitats. It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is valued for its medicinal properties.

The variety longipedunculata is distinguished by its elongated peduncles, which support its flowering clusters.
