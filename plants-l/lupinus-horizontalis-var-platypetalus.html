Lupinus horizontalis var. platypetalus

Lupinus horizontalis var. platypetalus, commonly known as the flat-petaled lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of showy, pea-like flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and has a fibrous root system. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive and drought-tolerant addition to native plant landscapes.
