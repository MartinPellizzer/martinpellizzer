Lasianthus lei

Lasianthus lei, commonly known as the mountain bluebell, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments, often in mountainous regions. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a velvety texture.

Lasianthus lei is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
