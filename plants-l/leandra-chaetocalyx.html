Leandra chaetocalyx

Leandra chaetocalyx, commonly known as the velvetleaf orchid, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety green leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its flowers, which bloom in clusters, are often pink or white with a distinctive, hairy texture.

Leandra chaetocalyx is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant in warmer climates.
