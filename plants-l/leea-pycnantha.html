Leea pycnantha

Leea pycnantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved leeah, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its small, clustered white flowers and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are usually opposite and have a smooth, glossy surface.

Leea pycnantha is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
