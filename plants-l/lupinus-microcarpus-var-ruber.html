Lupinus microcarpus var. ruber

Lupinus microcarpus var. ruber, commonly known as the red burr lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compact, spiny seed pods and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats. Its foliage consists of trifoliate leaves, and it is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
