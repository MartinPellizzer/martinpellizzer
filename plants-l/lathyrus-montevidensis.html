Lathyrus montevidensis

Lathyrus montevidensis, commonly known as the Montevideo pea, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Uruguay and Argentina.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows in grasslands and disturbed areas, showcasing its adaptability to various soil types. Its flowers are usually blue or violet, with a distinctive pea-like shape, and it produces small, elongated pods containing several seeds.

L. montevidensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
