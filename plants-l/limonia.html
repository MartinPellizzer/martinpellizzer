Limonia

Limonia, commonly known as the lemon tree, is a small evergreen tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, citrus-like fruits. The plant produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are rich in vitamin C and used in culinary and medicinal applications. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and have a leathery texture.

Limonia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in warm, sunny climates.
