Linaria farsensis

Linaria farsensis, commonly known as Persian toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces seed pods that split open to release tiny seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Linaria farsensis is often found in rocky or sandy habitats, making it a resilient species in challenging environments.
