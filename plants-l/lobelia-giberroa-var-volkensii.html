Lobelia giberroa var. volkensii

Lobelia giberroa var. volkensii, commonly known as the Volckens' lobelia, is a flowering plant native to East Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to violet, which bloom during the wet season. It thrives in well-drained, moist soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This species is often found in rocky or forested areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
