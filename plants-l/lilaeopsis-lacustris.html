Lilaeopsis lacustris

Lilaeopsis lacustris, commonly known as the aquatic grass of lakes, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family.

It is native to temperate regions and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of lakes and rivers. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in spikes. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.

Lilaeopsis lacustris is adapted to waterlogged conditions and can tolerate a range of nutrient levels in its environment.
