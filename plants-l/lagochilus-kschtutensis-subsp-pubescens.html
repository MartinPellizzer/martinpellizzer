Lagochilus kschtutensis subsp. pubescens

Lagochilus kschtutensis subsp.

pubescens, commonly known as the hairy Persian hyssop, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant is distinguished by its hairy, glandular leaves and spikes of violet or blue flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
