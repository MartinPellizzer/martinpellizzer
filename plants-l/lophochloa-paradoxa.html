Lophochloa paradoxa

Lophochloa paradoxa, commonly known as the paradox grass, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and forest margins. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often reddish in color.

Lophochloa paradoxa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
