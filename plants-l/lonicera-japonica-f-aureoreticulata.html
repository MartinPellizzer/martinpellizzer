Lonicera japonica f. aureoreticulata

Lonicera japonica f. aureoreticulata, commonly known as the golden reticulated honeysuckle, is a compact shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, which features a golden-green hue with a reticulated or net-like pattern of darker green veins. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it requires careful management in certain regions.
