Lilium longifolium

Lilium longifolium, commonly known as the tawny lily, is a species of flowering plant in the Liliaceae family.

It is native to the western United States and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, orange-red flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are long and narrow, often reaching up to 20 cm in length, and are arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

The bulbs of Lilium longifolium are edible when young but become more fibrous as the plant matures.
