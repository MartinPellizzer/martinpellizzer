Lappula floribunda var. jessicae

Lappula floribunda var. jessicae, commonly known as the pink lappula, is a flowering plant native to arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to white. The plant produces oblong, ribbed seed pods that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to cold climates.

This variety is often found in open habitats such as tundra and mountain meadows.
