Lonicera macrantha var. heterotricha

Lonicera macrantha var. heterotricha, commonly known as the variable honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to parts of East Asia.

It is characterized by its twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that ripens in late summer.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in a variety of soil conditions.
