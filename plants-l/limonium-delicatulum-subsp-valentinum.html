Limonium delicatulum subsp. valentinum

Limonium delicatulum subsp.

valentinum, commonly known as Valentine's limonium, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its delicate, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
