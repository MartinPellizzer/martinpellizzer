Linanthus filipes

Linanthus filipes, commonly known as the threadleaf flax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, thread-like leaves and delicate, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has small, capsule-like fruits that release tiny seeds when mature.

Linanthus filipes plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is of interest in ecological and botanical studies.
