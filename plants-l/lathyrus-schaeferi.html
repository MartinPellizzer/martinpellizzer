Lathyrus schaeferi

Lathyrus schaeferi, commonly known as the Schaefer's vetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, pea-like flowers in shades of purple or white. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along roadsides.

Lathyrus schaeferi is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
