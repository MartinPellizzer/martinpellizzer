Lavandula setifera

Lavandula setifera, commonly known as spike lavender, is a species of flowering plant in the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its tall, spikelike inflorescences of purple flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. Its fragrant flowers are used in aromatherapy and as a natural remedy, while its foliage is often used in floral arrangements.

Lavandula setifera is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
