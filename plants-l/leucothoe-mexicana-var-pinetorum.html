Leucothoe mexicana var. pinetorum

Leucothoe mexicana var. pinetorum, commonly known as the Mexican leatherleaf, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a leathery texture, giving it its common name.

It produces small, bell-shaped white flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are attractive to birds.
