Lycianthes geminata

Lycianthes geminata, commonly known as the twinleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and tobacco. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb with simple, opposite leaves. Its small, yellow flowers are followed by round, green berries that turn red when mature.
