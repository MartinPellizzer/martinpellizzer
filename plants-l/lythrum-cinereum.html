Lythrum cinereum

Lythrum cinereum, commonly known as purple loosestrife, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, spike-like inflorescence composed of numerous purple to pink flowers. The plant has lance-shaped, opposite leaves that are often hairy and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its fibrous root system helps stabilize soil, making it an important plant in wetland ecosystems.
