Linum tauricum subsp. linearifolium

Linum tauricum subsp.

linearifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flax, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, erect stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, adorned with linear, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color.

The plant produces clusters of blue or purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fibrous stems and leaves are characteristic of the Linaceae family, known for their industrial and historical uses.

This subspecies is often found in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in full sun conditions.
