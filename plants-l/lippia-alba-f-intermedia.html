Lippia alba f. intermedia

Lippia alba f. intermedia, commonly known as silverleaf lippia, is a hybrid form of the genus Lippia native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping and ornamental gardening. Its leaves contain essential oils, contributing to its use in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent.

The species is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
