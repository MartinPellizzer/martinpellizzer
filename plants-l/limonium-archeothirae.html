Limonium archeothirae

Limonium archeothirae, commonly known as the sea heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and produces showy, violet-blue or white flowers. The plant thrives in coastal areas and sandy soils, often growing in saline environments. Its flowers are typically clustered in dense, rounded inflorescences, and it blooms during the summer months.

Limonium archeothirae is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate coastal conditions.
