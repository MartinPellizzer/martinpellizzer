Lippia suffruticosa

Lippia suffruticosa, commonly known as Mexican mint or wild mint, is a perennial shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green leaves and small white or purple flowers. The plant is often found in moist, shaded areas such as forests and along stream banks. It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is valued for its medicinal and aromatic properties.

Lippia suffruticosa is also used in traditional medicine for its antiseptic and digestive benefits.
