Lastrea velata

Lastrea velata, commonly known as the velvet lastrea, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly covering of fine hairs, which gives it a soft, velvety appearance. The plant typically forms a low-growing, sprawling rosette of fleshy, blue-green leaves. It produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by pale blue or white fruits.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in arid and semi-arid regions.
