Leontodon hispidulus subsp. muelleri

Leontodon hispidulus subsp.

muelleri, commonly known as the hairy toothed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and produces yellowish-green flower heads in the summer. Its inflorescence consists of a single flower per stem, with small, tubular florets surrounded by bracts.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
