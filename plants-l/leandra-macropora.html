Leandra macropora

Leandra macropora, commonly known as the large-leafed leandra, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Leandra macropora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
