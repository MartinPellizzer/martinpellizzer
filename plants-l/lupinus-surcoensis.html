Lupinus surcoensis

Lupinus surcoensis, commonly known as the Surco lupine, is a perennial herb native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has palmate leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in mountainous and semi-arid environments.

Lupinus surcoensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
