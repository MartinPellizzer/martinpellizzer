Lupinus equi-collis

Lupinus equi-collis, commonly known as the horse-collar lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and showy, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Lupinus equi-collis is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
