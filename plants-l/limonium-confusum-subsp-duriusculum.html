Limonium confusum subsp. duriusculum

Limonium confusum subsp.

duriusculum, commonly known as the rugged sea lavender, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in coastal and saline environments.

The plant produces spikes of small, blue to purple flowers during the summer months. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of salt. Limonium confusum subsp.

duriusculum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its resilience to harsh maritime conditions.
