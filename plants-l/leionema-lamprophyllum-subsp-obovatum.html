Leionema lamprophyllum subsp. obovatum

Leionema lamprophyllum subsp.

obovatum, commonly known as the silver-leaved leionema, is a shrub native to New Zealand. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of silvery-white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers a sunny to partially shaded location.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively low maintenance.
