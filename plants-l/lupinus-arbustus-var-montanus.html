Lupinus arbustus var. montanus

Lupinus arbustus var. montanus, commonly known as the mountain lupine, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval to lance-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Lupinus arbustus var. montanus plays an important ecological role by fixing nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plant life.
