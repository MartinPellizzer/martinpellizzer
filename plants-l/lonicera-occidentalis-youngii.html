Lonicera occidentalis youngii

Lonicera occidentalis youngii, commonly known as the Western honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, blooming in late spring to early summer. These flowers are adapted for pollination by bees and other insects.

The plant's berries, which ripen in late summer, are edible and often consumed by birds and small mammals.
