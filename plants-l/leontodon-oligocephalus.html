Leontodon oligocephalus

Leontodon oligocephalus, commonly known as the narrow-headed hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with prominent central disks.

Leontodon oligocephalus is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
