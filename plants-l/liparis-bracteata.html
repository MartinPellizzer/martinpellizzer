Liparis bracteata

Liparis bracteata, commonly known as the bracteate orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers that are typically pale green to yellow in color. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually elongated and grass-like, and it thrives in shaded, moist environments.

Liparis bracteata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
