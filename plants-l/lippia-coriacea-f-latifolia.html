Lippia coriacea f. latifolia

Lippia coriacea f. latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf lippia, is a variant of the species Lippia coriacea, belonging to the Verbenaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are usually covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in clusters. It is native to regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

This plant is often used in traditional medicine and as an ornamental due to its aromatic foliage and compact growth habit.
