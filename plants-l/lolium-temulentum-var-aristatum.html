Lolium temulentum var. aristatum

Lolium temulentum var. aristatum, commonly known as the tall ryegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, making it a monoecious species. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and it thrives in disturbed soils, often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Lolium temulentum var. aristatum is also known for its potential as a forage crop due to its high nutritional value.
