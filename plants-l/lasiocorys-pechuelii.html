Lasiocorys pechuelii

Lasiocorys pechuelii, commonly known as the pechuelii orchid, is a species of orchid native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers with a distinctive violet hue. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, adapted to the harsh conditions of its native range.

Lasiocorys pechuelii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
