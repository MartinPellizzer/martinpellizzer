Lasianthaea zinnioides

Lasianthaea zinnioides, commonly known as the Mexican zinnia, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its bright, showy flowers that come in shades of red, yellow, and orange, resembling those of the common zinnia. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 3 feet, with lance-shaped leaves and a branching stem. Its flowers are composed of two types of petals—showy ray florets and smaller tubular disc florets—that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Lasianthaea zinnioides is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
