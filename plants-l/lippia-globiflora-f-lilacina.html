Lippia globiflora f. lilacina

Lippia globiflora f. lilacina, commonly known as the lilac-flowered lippia, is a compact shrub native to Central America.

It features small, oval leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, showy lilac-pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. These flowers are borne on short pedicels and are arranged in dense, globular inflorescences.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a mild, aromatic scent.
