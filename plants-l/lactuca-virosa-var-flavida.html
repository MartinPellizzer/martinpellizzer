Lactuca virosa var. flavida

Lactuca virosa var. flavida, commonly known as yellow bitter lettuce, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its pale yellow to greenish-yellow leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in clusters. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. It contains lactucarium, a milky sap with sedative properties, and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

However, it is generally considered toxic and should be avoided for consumption.
