Leucanthemum ircutianum subsp. leucolepis

Leucanthemum ircutianum subsp.

leucolepis, commonly known as the white leucanthemum, is a flowering plant native to regions of Asia, including China and Korea. It is characterized by its bright white petals and yellow-orange centers, forming daisy-like flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a compact growth habit with basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
