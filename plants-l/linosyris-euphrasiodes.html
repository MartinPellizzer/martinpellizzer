Linosyris euphrasiodes

Linosyris euphrasiodes, commonly known as the European bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features attractive, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Linosyris euphrasiodes is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
