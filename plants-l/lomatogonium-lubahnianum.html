Lomatogonium lubahnianum

Lomatogonium lubahnianum, commonly known as the lubahn's lomatogonium, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows as a shrub, featuring hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the branches.

Lomatogonium lubahnianum is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal or semi-arid environments.
