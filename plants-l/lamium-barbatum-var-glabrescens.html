Lamium barbatum var. glabrescens

Lamium barbatum var. glabrescens, commonly known as the hairy dead nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with a hairy texture, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of pink or purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and gardens.

Lamium barbatum var. glabrescens is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and modest floral display.
