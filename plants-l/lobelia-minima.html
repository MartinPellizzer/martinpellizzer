Lobelia minima

Lobelia minima, commonly known as the dwarf lobelia, is a small flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of less than 6 inches, featuring delicate, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland areas and along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Lobelia minima is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
