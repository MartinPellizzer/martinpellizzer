Lysiloma watsonii subsp. thornberi

Lysiloma watsonii subsp.

thornberi, commonly known as the Thornber's screwtree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to the southwestern United States. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The plant features compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces small, fragrant white to yellowish flowers in clusters. Its fruit is a dry, three-lobed capsule that releases seeds when mature.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
