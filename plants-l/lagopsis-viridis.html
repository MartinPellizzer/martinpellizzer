Lagopsis viridis

Lagopsis viridis, commonly known as green lagopsis, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands. It has a compact growth habit and is well adapted to arid conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens in regions with a Mediterranean climate.
