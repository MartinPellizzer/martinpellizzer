Lessingianthus

Lessingianthus, commonly known as the "butterfly flower," is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the Asteraceae family.

These plants are native to South America, particularly Brazil, and are characterized by their vibrant, showy inflorescences that resemble butterflies. The flowers typically have tubular corollas with five lobes, and the leaves are usually alternate and pinnately divided. Lessingianthus species are often cultivated for their ornamental value in gardens due to their striking colors and unique foliage.

They prefer full sun and well-drained soil, making them suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
