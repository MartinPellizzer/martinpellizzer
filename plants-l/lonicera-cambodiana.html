Lonicera cambodiana

Lonicera cambodiana, commonly known as Cambodian honeysuckle, is a deciduous vine native to Southeast Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth habit and ability to climb using its tendrils. Its fruit is a small, red berry that contains seeds, and it is often found in moist, forested areas.

Lonicera cambodiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
