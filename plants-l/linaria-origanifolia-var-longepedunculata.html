Linaria origanifolia var. longepedunculata

Linaria origanifolia var. longepedunculata, commonly known as the long pedunculate toadflax, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branching stems that bear clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers with distinctive spurred petals. The leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, often resembling those of oregano, giving it its species name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

This variant is noted for its extended flowering period and ornamental value in horticultural settings.
