Luzuriaga radicans

Luzuriaga radicans, commonly known as the creeping luzuriaga, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged in a dense, mat-like formation along the ground. The plant produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often appearing in late spring or early summer. Luzuriaga radicans thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found in cloud forests or rocky slopes.

Its unique growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a desirable plant for shaded garden settings.
