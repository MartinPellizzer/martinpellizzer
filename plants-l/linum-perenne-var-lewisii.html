Linum perenne var. lewisii

Linum perenne var. lewisii, commonly known as Lewis's flax, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant has trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet being oval and slightly hairy. It produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Lewis's flax is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.
