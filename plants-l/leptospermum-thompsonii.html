Leptospermum thompsonii

Leptospermum thompsonii, commonly known as Thompson's tea tree, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds, typical of the Myrtaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some historical use in traditional medicine.
