Leptonychia multiflora

Leptonychia multiflora, commonly known as the many-flowered rockrose, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or coastal environments. Its flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Leptonychia multiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
