Lauridia rupicola

Lauridia rupicola, commonly known as the rock laurel, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen foliage and small, clustered white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to coastal conditions with high salt exposure. Its leaves are leathery and often have a distinct, spicy scent.

Lauridia rupicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in coastal landscaping.
