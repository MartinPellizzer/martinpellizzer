Liparis krameri var. nikkoensis

Liparis krameri var. nikkoensis, commonly known as the Nikko orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Japan.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and delicate, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. It produces small, clustered inflorescences with lip-like petals that are often adorned with intricate patterns.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
