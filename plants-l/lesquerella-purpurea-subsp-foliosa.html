Lesquerella purpurea subsp. foliosa

Lesquerella purpurea subsp.

foliosa, commonly known as the purple lesquerella, is a species native to the southwestern United States. It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its slender stems and alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The subspecies is notable for its potential use in biofuel production due to its high oil content.
