Lamium moschatum subsp. micranthum

Lamium moschatum subsp.

micranthum, commonly known as cat's ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white.

The plant typically blooms from late spring to early summer, and its foliage remains decorative throughout the growing season. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable habitats.
