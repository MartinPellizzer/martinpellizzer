Lidia yukonensis

Lidia yukonensis, commonly known as Yukon lily, is a perennial herb native to the Yukon and Alaska.

It features upright, slender stems that bear lance-shaped leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Lidia yukonensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
