Leucanthemopsis heywoodii

Leucanthemopsis heywoodii, commonly known as the Heywood's leucanthemum, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
