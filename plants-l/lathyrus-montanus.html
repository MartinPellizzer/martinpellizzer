Lathyrus montanus

Lathyrus montanus, commonly known as the mountain larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of showy blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds.

Lathyrus montanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
