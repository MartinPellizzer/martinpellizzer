Lilium sinensium

Lilium sinensium, commonly known as the Chinese lily, is a species native to China and belongs to the Liliaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and red. The plant produces a bulbous underground stem, which serves as a storage organ for nutrients. Its leaves are long and narrow, arranged in a grass-like pattern along the stem.

Lilium sinensium is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking floral display and fragrance.
