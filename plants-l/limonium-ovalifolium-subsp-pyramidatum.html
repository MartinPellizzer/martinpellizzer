Limonium ovalifolium subsp. pyramidatum

Limonium ovalifolium subsp.

pyramidatum, commonly known as the pyramidal sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking, spiky blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and forms dense, compact clumps. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
