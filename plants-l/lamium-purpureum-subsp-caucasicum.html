Lamium purpureum subsp. caucasicum

Lamium purpureum subsp.

caucasicum, commonly known as Caucasian deadnettle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Caucasus region. It features opposite, heart-shaped leaves with a fuzzy texture and produces clusters of pink or purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a sprawling growth habit and is often found in disturbed soils.

Its flowers bloom from spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
