Lepechiniella alaica

Lepechiniella alaica, commonly known as the Alaskan lepechiniella, is a small, mat-forming perennial herb native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of North America.

The plant has tiny, hairy leaves that are arranged in a dense rosette, helping to conserve moisture in harsh climates. Its flowers are small, pink to purple, and bloom in late summer, adding a splash of color to the landscape.

Lepechiniella alaica is adapted to cold temperatures and has a slow growth rate, making it a resilient component of polar ecosystems.
