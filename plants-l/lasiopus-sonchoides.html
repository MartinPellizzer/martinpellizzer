Lasiopus sonchoides

Lasiopus sonchoides, commonly known as the common sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It has a mat-forming growth habit with numerous branched stems that lie close to the ground. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a cluster at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in dry environments.

Lasiopus sonchoides is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in harsh, sandy conditions.
