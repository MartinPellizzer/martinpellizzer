Lepanthes anfracta

Lepanthes anfracta, commonly known as the zigzag orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that often display a striking combination of white and purple hues. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as cloud forests, where it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

Lepanthes anfracta is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
