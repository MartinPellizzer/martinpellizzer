Leiopyxis sumatrana

Leiopyxis sumatrana, commonly known as the Sumatran tamarind, is a member of the Fabaceae family.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, pale bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing flat, edible seeds. It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Sumatra and Borneo, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its timber and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
