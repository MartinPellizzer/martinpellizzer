Lithodora consobrina

Lithodora consobrina, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces clusters of small, sky-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine white hairs, giving them a fuzzy appearance.

Lithodora consobrina thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
