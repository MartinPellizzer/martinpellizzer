Litrisa carnosa

Litrisa carnosa, commonly known as the fleshy leaf plant, is a species native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, succulent leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. Its leaves are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, providing structural support and maximizing sunlight absorption.

Litrisa carnosa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
