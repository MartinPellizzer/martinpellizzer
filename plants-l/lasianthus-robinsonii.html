Lasianthus robinsonii

Lasianthus robinsonii, commonly known as the Robinson's lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with a smooth surface and a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
