Lobelia serpyllacea var. odorata

Lobelia serpyllacea var. odorata, commonly known as sweet lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in fine hairs.

The variety 'odorata' is distinguished by its fragrant foliage, which emits a mild, sweet scent.
