Lithospermum murasaki

Lithospermum murasaki, commonly known as Japanese rock pepper, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that often grow in rocky or dry soils. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

The roots of Lithospermum murasaki contain compounds that have been studied for their potential medicinal properties.
