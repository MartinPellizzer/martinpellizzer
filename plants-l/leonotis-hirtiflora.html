Leonotis hirtiflora

Leonotis hirtiflora, commonly known as the woolly lantern or red-hot poker, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in coastal and sandy habitats. The plant produces striking, tubular flowers that are usually red or orange in color, which attract pollinators such as birds and insects. Its leaves are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a woolly appearance.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
