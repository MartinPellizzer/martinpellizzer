Leontodon hastilis subsp. hispidus

Leontodon hastilis subsp.

hispidus, commonly known as the hairy hawk's-beard, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous root system.

The plant features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, with bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, often forming dense colonies.

Its subspecies, hispidus, is distinguished by its more pronounced hairy texture compared to other varieties.
