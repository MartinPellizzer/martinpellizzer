Lagenocarpus pauciflorus var. triqueter

Lagenocarpus pauciflorus var. triqueter, commonly known as the three-angled burrgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive three-angled seed pods, which are adapted for wind dispersal. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and along roadsides, exhibiting a sprawling growth habit. Its leaves are lanceolate and hairy, while its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in clusters.

The botanical features of this plant make it well-suited for ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature and ability to stabilize soil.
