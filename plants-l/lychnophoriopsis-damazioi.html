Lychnophoriopsis damazioi

Lychnophoriopsis damazioi, commonly known as the Damazio's lychnophoriopsis, is a rare and lesser-known species within the Bromeliaceae family.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a water-holding cup, typical of many bromeliads. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, often hidden within the leaf rosette.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
