Leptochloa ligulata

Leptochloa ligulata, commonly known as the narrowleaf grass, is a species of grass native to various regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, fields, and grasslands. Its flowers are wind-pollinated, and it produces small, dry fruits known as caryopses.

Leptochloa ligulata is often used in ecological studies due to its adaptability and role in soil stabilization.
