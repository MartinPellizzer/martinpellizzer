Lupinus douglasii var. fallax

Lupinus douglasii var. fallax, commonly known as the Douglas's lupine, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to violet pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

Lupinus douglasii var. fallax is adapted to nutrient-poor soils and is often found in areas with full sun exposure.
