Lysimachia aspera

Lysimachia aspera, commonly known as rough yellow loosestrife, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving them a rough texture. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences.

Lysimachia aspera is often found in wet meadows and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
