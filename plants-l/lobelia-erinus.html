Lobelia erinus

Lobelia erinus, commonly known as the moss rose, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial that produces clusters of small, vibrant blue or purple flowers. The plant has delicate, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Lobelia erinus is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
