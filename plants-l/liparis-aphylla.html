Liparis aphylla

Liparis aphylla, commonly known as the leafless orchid, is a unique member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its lack of leaves, relying instead on its roots for photosynthesis. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that are often white or pale pink in color. Native to certain regions of Asia, it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its specialized growth habits, it is considered a rare and delicate species in its natural habitat.
