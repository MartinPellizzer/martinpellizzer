Leucaena salvadorensis

Leucaena salvadorensis, commonly known as the Salvadoran leucaena, is a shrub or small tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to dry and semi-arid climates, making it a resilient species in tropical and subtropical regions.

It has a deep root system that helps it survive in poor soil conditions, and it is often used for forage and soil stabilization.
