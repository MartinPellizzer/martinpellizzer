Lepanthes rutrum

Lepanthes rutrum, commonly known as the red-leaved orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking red or purple flower petals and sepals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are often fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

Lepanthes rutrum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts worldwide.
