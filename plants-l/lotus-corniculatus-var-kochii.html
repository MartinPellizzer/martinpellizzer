Lotus corniculatus var. kochii

Lotus corniculatus var. kochii, commonly known as the Koch's bird's-foot trefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow-orange flowers. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, making it valuable for soil enrichment.
