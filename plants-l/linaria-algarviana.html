Linaria algarviana

Linaria algarviana, commonly known as the Algarve toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe, particularly Portugal and Spain.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and sunny habitats. The plant produces vibrant yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its slender, branching stems and narrow leaves are adapted to withstand arid conditions.

Linaria algarviana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
