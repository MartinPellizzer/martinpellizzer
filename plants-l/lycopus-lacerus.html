Lycopus lacerus

Lycopus lacerus, commonly known as rough water-hemlock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its small, white to pale blue flowers are clustered in whorls along the upper stems.

However, it is important to note that all parts of this plant are toxic and should not be ingested.
