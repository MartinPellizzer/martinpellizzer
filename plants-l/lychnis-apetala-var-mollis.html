Lychnis apetala var. mollis

Lychnis apetala var. mollis, commonly known as the soft-stemmed pinkflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the carnation family, Caryophyllaceae, and is characterized by its simple, often hairy stems and showy, pinkish-red flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. Its flowers bloom in early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
