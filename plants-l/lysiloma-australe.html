Lysiloma australe

Lysiloma australe, commonly known as the Australian wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance through its deep root system. It plays an important role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for native wildlife.

Lysiloma australe is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
