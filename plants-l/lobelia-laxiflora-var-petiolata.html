Lobelia laxiflora var. petiolata

Lobelia laxiflora var. petiolata, commonly known as the slenderleaf lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, tubular blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically narrow and have a petiole, giving the plant its varietal name. It thrives in moist, woodland environments and is often found in shaded, well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
