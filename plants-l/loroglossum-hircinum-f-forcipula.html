Loroglossum hircinum f. forcipula

Loroglossum hircinum f. forcipula, commonly known as the forcipulate larkspur, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers and has hairy stems and leaves. The plant is native to the western United States and grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. It produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by wind.
