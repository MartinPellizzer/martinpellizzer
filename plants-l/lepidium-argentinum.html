Lepidium argentinum

Lepidium argentinum, commonly known as the Argentine cress, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many edible and economically important plants. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate regions and has small, pinnately divided leaves. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in clusters, and it produces small, round fruits known as siliques.

Lepidium argentinum is sometimes used in traditional medicine and has potential nutritional value.
