Leandra circumscissa

Leandra circumscissa, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in shaded or partially shaded environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it has a fibrous root system adapted to its arid habitat.

Leandra circumscissa is a rare and endangered species, requiring specific conditions for successful growth and reproduction.
