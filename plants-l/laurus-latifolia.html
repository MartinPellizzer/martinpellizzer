Laurus latifolia

Laurus latifolia, commonly known as sweet bay or laurel, is a broad-leaved evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dark green, glossy, lance-shaped leaves that are aromatic and often used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark blue berries, which are edible but less commonly consumed. Its essential oils, derived from the leaves, are valued for their fragrance and therapeutic properties.

Laurus latifolia is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes and as a source of aromatic compounds.
