Laelia superbiens var. decorata

Laelia superbiens var. decorata, commonly known as the Decorated Laelia, is a hybrid orchid native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, long-lasting flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and rocks in its natural habitat. Its pseudobulbs are thick and fleshy, storing water to help it survive in its high-altitude environment.

The species is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
