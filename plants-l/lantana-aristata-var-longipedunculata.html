Lantana aristata var. longipedunculata

Lantana aristata var. longipedunculata, commonly known as the long-pedunculate lantana, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, tubular flowers that are usually yellow or orange in color. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to gardens.
