Lepidium impressum

Lepidium impressum, commonly known as the narrowleaf rocket, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard plants. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its small, yellow flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in the spring.

The leaves are narrow and often have a slightly bitter taste, making it a plant of interest for both ecological and culinary purposes.
