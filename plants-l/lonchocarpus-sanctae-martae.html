Lonchocarpus sanctae-martae

Lonchocarpus sanctae-martae, commonly known as the sacred bignon, is a tropical legume native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces cylindrical pods containing several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and ethnobotanical studies.
