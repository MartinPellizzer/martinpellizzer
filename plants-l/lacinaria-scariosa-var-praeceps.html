Lacinaria scariosa var. praeceps

Lacinaria scariosa var. praeceps, commonly known as the scarious lacinaria, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the summer months. Its leaves are often covered in a thin, papery layer that gives the plant its "scarious" designation.

This species is typically found in open, sandy or rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.
