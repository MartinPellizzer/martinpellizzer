Leea mastersii var. siamensis

Leea mastersii var. siamensis, commonly known as the Siam leea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Leeaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
