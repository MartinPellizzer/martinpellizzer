Lotus corniculatus subsp. preslii

Lotus corniculatus subsp.

preslii, commonly known as Presli's bird's-foot trefoil, is a flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade. It has small, hairy stems and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This subspecies is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
