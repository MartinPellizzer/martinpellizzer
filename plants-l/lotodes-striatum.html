Lotodes striatum

Lotodes striatum, commonly known as the striped water lily, is a aquatic plant native to parts of Africa.

It features broad, floating leaves with a distinctive waxy coating that helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom above the water surface. Its roots anchor it in muddy substrates, while its rhizomes spread horizontally to form dense colonies.

Lotodes striatum plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
