Lacistema oblongum

Lacistema oblongum, commonly known as the broadleaf nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in the evening. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, poisonous seeds.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
