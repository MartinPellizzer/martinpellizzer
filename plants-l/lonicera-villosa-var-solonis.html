Lonicera villosa var. solonis

Lonicera villosa var. solonis, commonly known as the Solon's honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features slender, arching stems covered in fine, white pubescence, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring. Its berries are red, fleshy fruits that mature in late summer and are attractive to birds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade to full sun.
