Lampujang majus

Lampujang majus, commonly known as the sacred lotus of Java, is a tropical aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom in shades of pink, red, and white, often floating on the surface of still water. The plant has thick, fleshy roots that anchor it in muddy substrates, while its broad, heart-shaped leaves provide a habitat for various aquatic life. Lampujang majus is known for its symbolic significance in Javanese culture, representing purity and spiritual enlightenment.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and cultural contexts.
