Lycopus uniflorus f. flagellaris

Lycopus uniflorus f. flagellaris, commonly known as narrow-leaved bugleweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and small, opposite leaves that are often lance-shaped. The plant produces clusters of pale blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically valuable species in its native habitats.
