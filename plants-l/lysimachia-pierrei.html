Lysimachia pierrei

Lysimachia pierrei, commonly known as the alpine yellowflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing habit and vibrant yellow flowers. The plant typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates. Lysimachia pierrei is often used in alpine gardens for its ornamental value and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
