Ligustrum deciduum

Ligustrum deciduum, commonly known as the deciduous privet, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, opposite leaves that turn yellow before falling in autumn, showcasing its deciduous nature. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by dark blue berries. Its adaptability to various soil types and climates makes it a common ornamental plant.

However, it can become invasive in some regions if not managed properly.
