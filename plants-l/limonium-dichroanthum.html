Limonium dichroanthum

Limonium dichroanthum, commonly known as the two-colored sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and its striking, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features silver-gray foliage with a velvety texture, and its flowers are often adorned with a distinctive two-toned appearance. Limonium dichroanthum thrives in coastal environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to withstand salty air and coastal conditions.
