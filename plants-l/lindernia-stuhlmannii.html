Lindernia stuhlmannii

Lindernia stuhlmannii, commonly known as the Stuhlmann's lindernia, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the family Linderniaceae and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Lindernia stuhlmannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
