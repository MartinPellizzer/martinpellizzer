Logania vaginalis var. longifolia

Logania vaginalis var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved logania, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Loganiaceae and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically red or orange in color. The plant has long, slender leaves that give it its common name, and it often grows in moist, shaded environments. Its flowers are borne in clusters and attract various pollinators, including bees and birds.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
