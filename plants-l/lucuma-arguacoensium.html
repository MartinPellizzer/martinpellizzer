Lucuma arguacoensium

Lucuma arguacoensium, commonly known as the golden fruit tree, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange fruits that resemble small, rounded citrus fruits. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, temperate climates with well-drained soil. Its leaves are broad and lobed, and it produces small white flowers before the fruit develops.

The fruit is prized for its sweet, nutty flavor and is often used in culinary applications.
