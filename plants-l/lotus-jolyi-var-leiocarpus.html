Lotus jolyi var. leiocarpus

Lotus jolyi var. leiocarpus, commonly known as the white lotus, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of India and Bangladesh.

It is a herbaceous plant that typically grows in aquatic or semi-aquatic environments, such as ponds and wetlands. The plant features large, showy white flowers that bloom in summer, often with a subtle fragrance. Its leaves are heart-shaped and float on the water surface, while its roots are submerged, aiding in nutrient absorption.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and has cultural significance in various traditional practices.
