Lachenalia albida

Lachenalia albida, commonly known as the white lachenalia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white and pink during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
