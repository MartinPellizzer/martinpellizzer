Licuala triphylla var. integrifolia

Licuala triphylla var. integrifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed pandan, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad, and often glossy green leaves that are arranged in a fan shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically followed by clustered, edible berries. Its leaves are widely used in cooking, especially in Southeast Asian cuisines, for their aromatic flavor.

The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
