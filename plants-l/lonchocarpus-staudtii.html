Lonchocarpus staudtii

Lonchocarpus staudtii, commonly known as the Brazilian senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces striking yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is known for containing alkaloids, which have been studied for potential medicinal properties.

The plant's growth habit includes both shrub and tree forms, depending on environmental conditions.
