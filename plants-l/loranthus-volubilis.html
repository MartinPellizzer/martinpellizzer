Loranthus volubilis

Loranthus volubilis, commonly known as the mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of host trees, primarily in the genus Eucalyptus.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using light absorbed by its leaves. The plant has small, scale-like leaves and produces white or yellowish flowers that are often visited by birds and insects. Its berries are typically red or yellow and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

Loranthus volubilis plays an important ecological role in forest ecosystems, influencing host tree growth and providing food for wildlife.
