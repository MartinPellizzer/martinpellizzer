Liabellum cervinum

Liabellum cervinum, commonly known as the golden tassel orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in countries like Argentina and Chile. The plant is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which are clustered in dense, tassel-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in a fine, hairy texture.

Liabellum cervinum thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found growing in rocky or forested areas.
