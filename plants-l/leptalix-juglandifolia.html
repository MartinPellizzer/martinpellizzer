Leptalix juglandifolia

Leptalix juglandifolia, commonly known as the goldenleaf fern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that emit a subtle golden hue under sunlight. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil. Its leaves are typically compound, with small, lance-shaped leaflets arranged in a spiral pattern.

Leptalix juglandifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and air-purifying properties.
