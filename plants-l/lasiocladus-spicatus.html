Lasiocladus spicatus

Lasiocladus spicatus, commonly known as the spiny broomstick cactus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cactaceae.

It is native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico. This plant is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to desert environments.
