Lepanthes laciniata

Lepanthes laciniata, commonly known as the slit-leaf orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, yellow, and pink. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical forests. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a distinctive, serrated edge.

Lepanthes laciniata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
