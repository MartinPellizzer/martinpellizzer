Linaria lapeyrouseana

Linaria lapeyrouseana, commonly known as the Lapeyrouse's toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the plantain family, Plantaginaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and slopes. It has slender, branching stems with lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Linaria lapeyrouseana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its bright, colorful display.
