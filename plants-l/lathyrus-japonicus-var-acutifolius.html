Lathyrus japonicus var. acutifolius

Lathyrus japonicus var. acutifolius, commonly known as Japanese vetch, is a perennial legume native to East Asia.

It features slender, climbing stems with pinnately compound leaves composed of elliptical, acuminate leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers in shades of purple or pink, followed by elongated, cylindrical pods containing lens-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and soil stabilization.
