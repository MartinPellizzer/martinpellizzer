Lepanthes inamoena

Lepanthes inamoena, commonly known as the fragrant orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that often have a pink or white hue with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical rainforests. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Lepanthes inamoena is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
