Lamium formosanum

Lamium formosanum, commonly known as the Japanese violet, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces clusters of violet-blue flowers in the spring. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecosystem biodiversity.
