Luzula congesta var. subglabra

Luzula congesta var. subglabra, commonly known as the glabrous wood rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, commonly referred to as the grass family. The plant is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves that are typically glabrous, or smooth, as indicated by its varietal name. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
