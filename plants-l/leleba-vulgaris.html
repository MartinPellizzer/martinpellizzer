Leleba vulgaris

Leleba vulgaris, commonly known as the common leleba, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters and has narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Leleba vulgaris is often used in ornamental gardening for its delicate appearance and ease of cultivation.
