Lepanthes leporina

Lepanthes leporina, commonly known as the rabbit orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid with a delicate appearance, featuring a short, fleshy stem and a single, showy flower. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with intricate patterns that resemble a rabbit's face, giving it its common name.

Lepanthes leporina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
