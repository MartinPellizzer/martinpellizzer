Lycium edgeworthii

Lycium edgeworthii, commonly known as the Himalayan boxthorn, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Lycium edgeworthii is adapted to high-altitude environments and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.
