Lilium mandshuricum

Lilium mandshuricum, commonly known as the Manchurian lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental value.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.
