Lacinaria

Lacinaria, commonly known as the lace fern, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Araceae.

It is characterized by its intricate, lacy foliage that gives it its common name. The leaves are typically divided into numerous narrow segments, creating a delicate, feathery appearance. Lacinaria species are native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

They thrive in moist, shaded environments and are often cultivated for their ornamental value.
