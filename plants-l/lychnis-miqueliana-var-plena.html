Lychnis miqueliana var. plena

Lychnis miqueliana var. plena, commonly known as the full-flowered bluemink, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value comes from its abundant flowering and compact growth habit.
