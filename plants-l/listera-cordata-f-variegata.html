Listera cordata f. variegata

Listera cordata f. variegata, commonly known as the variegated heartleaf.

This plant is a variant of the heartleaf (Listera cordata), characterized by its striking variegated leaves that display white or yellow markings on a dark green background. It is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia, typically found in moist, shaded woodland environments. The flowers are small, white, and clustered in umbels, adding a delicate appearance to the plant.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for shaded garden beds and rock gardens.
