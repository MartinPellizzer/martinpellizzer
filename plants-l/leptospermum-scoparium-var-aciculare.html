Leptospermum scoparium var. aciculare

Leptospermum scoparium var. aciculare, commonly known as silver furze, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that give it a pine-like appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in coastal and subalpine environments, often growing on rocky or sandy soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been introduced to other parts of the world for horticultural purposes.
