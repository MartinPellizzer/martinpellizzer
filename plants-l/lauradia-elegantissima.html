Lauradia elegantissima

Lauradia elegantissima, commonly known as the elegant lauracia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes other aromatic plants like cinnamon and avocado. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, narrow leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its limited natural range, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
