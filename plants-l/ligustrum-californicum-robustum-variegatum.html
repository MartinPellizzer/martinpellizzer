Ligustrum californicum robustum-variegatum

Ligustrum californicum robustum-variegatum, commonly known as the California privet, is a shrub native to coastal regions of California.

It features small, dark green leaves with white or variegated markings, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by dark blue berries that are edible for birds. It is often used in landscaping due to its adaptability and ornamental value.

However, it can become invasive in certain areas if not managed properly.
