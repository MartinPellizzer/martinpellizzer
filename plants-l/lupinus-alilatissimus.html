Lupinus alilatissimus

Lupinus alilatissimus, commonly known as the silver lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and tall spires of blue or purple flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Lupinus alilatissimus is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
