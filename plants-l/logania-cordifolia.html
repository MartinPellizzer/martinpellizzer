Logania cordifolia

Logania cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf logania, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Loganiaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. It typically grows as an erect shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant in some regions.
