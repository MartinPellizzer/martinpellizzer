Lilium wallichianum var. superbum

Lilium wallichianum var. superbum, commonly known as the Himalayan tiger lily, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, often with a distinctive black eye pattern. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a tall, sturdy stem that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to the harsh conditions of mountainous regions.

The bulbs are perennial and can be propagated through division or seed, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
