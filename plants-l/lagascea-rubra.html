Lagascea rubra

Lagascea rubra, commonly known as red sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant red, pink, or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has slender, branched stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in arid and semi-arid environments.

Lagascea rubra is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
