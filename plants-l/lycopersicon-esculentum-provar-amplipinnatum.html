Lycopersicon esculentum provar. amplipinnatum

Lycopersicon esculentum provar. amplipinnatum, commonly known as the large-leaf tomato, is a botanical variant of the domesticated tomato.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves and larger fruit size compared to standard tomato varieties. The plant belongs to the Solanaceae family and is cultivated for its edible, fleshy fruits. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variant is often used in horticultural research to study genetic diversity and trait improvement in tomato species.
