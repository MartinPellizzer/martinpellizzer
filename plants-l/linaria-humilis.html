Linaria humilis

Linaria humilis, commonly known as the common toadflax, is a low-growing annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, branching stems and produces showy, yellow-orange flowers with two lips, typical of the Scrophulariaceae family. The plant has small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Linaria humilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
