Linaria ikonnikovii

Linaria ikonnikovii, commonly known as the Ikonnikov's toadflax, is a perennial flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the genus Linaria in the family Plantaginaceae, characterized by its showy, spurred flowers that typically range in color from yellow to orange. The plant features slender, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
