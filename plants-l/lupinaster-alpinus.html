Lupinaster alpinus

Lupinaster alpinus, commonly known as the alpine lupinaster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming in late summer. Lupinaster alpinus is well adapted to harsh alpine environments, thriving in poor, well-drained soils.

It is often found in rocky slopes and meadows at high altitudes.
