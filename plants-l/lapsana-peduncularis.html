Lapsana peduncularis

Lapsana peduncularis, commonly known as the common cudweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is an annual or biennial herb that grows up to 60 cm tall, featuring hairy stems and alternate leaves that are oval to lance-shaped. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in loose clusters, which bloom from June to August. Its seeds are small, winged, and dispersed by wind, aiding in its widespread distribution.

Lapsana peduncularis is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in waste areas.
