Lyginia tenax

Lyginia tenax, commonly known as the rigid water plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes, ponds, and slow-moving streams. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often submerged or floating, with long, slender stems. Its small, greenish-white flowers appear in clusters during the summer months.

Lyginia tenax plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
