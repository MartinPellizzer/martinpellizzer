Leandra longisetosa

Leandra longisetosa, commonly known as the long-hair leandra, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, elongated fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
