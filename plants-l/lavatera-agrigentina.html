Lavatera agrigentina

Lavatera agrigentina, commonly known as the Sicilian woolflower, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, fluffy flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in clusters. The plant has silvery-gray foliage with soft, fuzzy textures, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Lavatera agrigentina is valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators.
