Lupinus aliumbellatus

Lupinus aliumbellatus, commonly known as the slender lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets, and its root system forms symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.

Lupinus aliumbellatus is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
