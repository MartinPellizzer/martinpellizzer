Leucopogon marginatus

Leucopogon marginatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heaths and heathers, and is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in heathlands and open forests, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are usually pink or white, and it produces small, leathery fruits that are dispersed by animals.

Leucopogon marginatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
