Lythrum salicaria f. albiflora

Lythrum salicaria f. albiflora, commonly known as white-flowered loosestrife, is a variant of the common loosestrife plant.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers, which contrast with the typical deep red or purple blooms of the species. This plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in wetlands and along stream banks. Its foliage is lance-shaped and slightly hairy, with a reddish stem.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in moist conditions.
