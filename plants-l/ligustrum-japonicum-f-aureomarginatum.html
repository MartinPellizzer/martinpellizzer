Ligustrum japonicum f. aureomarginatum

Ligustrum japonicum f. aureomarginatum, commonly known as the golden-margin privet, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that have a distinctive yellow margin, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by dark blue berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for hedging and ornamental landscaping.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in gardens for its aesthetic value and low maintenance requirements.
