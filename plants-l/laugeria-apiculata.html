Laugeria apiculata

Laugeria apiculata, commonly known as the slender tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests. Its rhizome system supports the growth of its tall, unbranched trunk.

Laugeria apiculata plays a significant role in its ecosystem by providing habitat and contributing to forest biodiversity.
