Lissochilus ramosus

Lissochilus ramosus, commonly known as the branched orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and purple. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with multiple stems arising from a central rhizome, and its leaves are usually lanceolate and covered in a fine pubescence. It thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found in cloud forests at high altitudes.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
