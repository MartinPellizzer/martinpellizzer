Lantana lavandulacea

Lantana lavandulacea, commonly known as lavender bluemont, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces opposite leaves that are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Lantana lavandulacea is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
