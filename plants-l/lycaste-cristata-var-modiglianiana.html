Lycaste cristata var. modiglianiana

Lycaste cristata var. modiglianiana, commonly known as the Modigliani's crowned lycaste, is a hybrid orchid species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, densely packed foliage that forms a rosette-like structure, giving it a unique and compact appearance. The plant produces fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white and pink, often with a slight blush of purple. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
