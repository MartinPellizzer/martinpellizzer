Laxmannia grandiflora subsp. stirlingensis

Laxmannia grandiflora subsp.

stirlingensis, commonly known as the Stirling's laxmannia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, clustered inflorescences.

The plant produces small, pink to reddish flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
