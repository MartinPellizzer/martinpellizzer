Lobelia longiscapa

Lobelia longiscapa, commonly known as the long-stemmed lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, tubular blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Lobelia longiscapa is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and serves as a nectar source for various pollinators.
