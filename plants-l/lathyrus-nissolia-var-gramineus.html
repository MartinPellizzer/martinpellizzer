Lathyrus nissolia var. gramineus

Lathyrus nissolia var. gramineus, commonly known as the grass-leaved sweet pea, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of fragrant, pea-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. Native to the mountainous regions of southern and eastern Asia, this plant thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The plant's growth habit is typically upright, with tendrils that help it climb and support its flowering stems.
