Lasianthera africana var. africana

Lasianthera africana var. africana, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and savanna habitats.

Due to its aromatic properties, it has been traditionally used in various cultural and medicinal practices.
