Lobivia steinmannii var. tuberculata

Lobivia steinmannii var. tuberculata, commonly known as the Tuberculate Lobivia, is a compact, succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It features a short, cylindrical stem with dense, tuberculate (bumpy) ridges that give it a unique textured appearance. The plant produces small, pale green to blue-gray leaves that are adapted to store water, typical of its desert habitat. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, emerging from the axils of the leaves.

It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
