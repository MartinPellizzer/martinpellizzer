Liparis koreojaponica

Liparis koreojaponica, commonly known as the Korean-Japanese orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and delicate, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. It has a pseudobulb structure that helps store water and nutrients, typical of many orchid species.

Liparis koreojaponica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
