Lavandula stoechas f. rosea

Lavandula stoechas f. rosea, commonly known as the rose-lavender, is a compact, herbaceous perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking, violet-pink flowers with distinctive, glandular, purple bracts that resemble small, fuzzy leaves. The plant features silver-gray, linear, aromatic leaves that are covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This cultivar is valued for its unique floral display and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
