Lespedeza thunbergii f. miyagialba

Lespedeza thunbergii f. miyagialba, commonly known as the Japanese stragglerbine, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and forest edges. The plant produces small, pink to reddish-purple flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is characterized by its ability to thrive in partial shade and its role in ecological restoration projects.
