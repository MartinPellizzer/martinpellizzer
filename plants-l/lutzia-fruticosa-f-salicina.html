Lutzia fruticosa f. salicina

Lutzia fruticosa f. salicina, commonly known as the silverleaf lutzia, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, silver-gray leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters, which bloom in the spring. Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for garden use.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade conditions.
