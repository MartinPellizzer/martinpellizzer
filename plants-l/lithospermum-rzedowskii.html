Lithospermum rzedowskii

Lithospermum rzedowskii, commonly known as the Mexican burning bush, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. Its roots contain compounds that have been used in traditional medicine for their potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

Lithospermum rzedowskii thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or disturbed habitats.
