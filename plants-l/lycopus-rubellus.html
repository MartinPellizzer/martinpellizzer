Lycopus rubellus

Lycopus rubellus, commonly known as red huckleweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often reddish underneath, giving it its common name. Its flowers are small, blue to purple, and clustered in spikes, blooming in late summer.

The plant is known for its aromatic leaves and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported calming properties.
