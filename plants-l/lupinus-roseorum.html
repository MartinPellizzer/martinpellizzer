Lupinus roseorum

Lupinus roseorum, commonly known as the rose lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of showy, rose-purple to pink blossoms. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to full sun exposure.

Lupinus roseorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
