Lazarolus pinnatifida

Lazarolus pinnatifida, commonly known as the featherleaf Lazarolus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery leaves that are pinnately divided, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in horticultural settings.
