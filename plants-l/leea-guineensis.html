Leea guineensis

Leea guineensis, commonly known as the African star gooseberry, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Leeaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by berry-like fruits that are edible. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
