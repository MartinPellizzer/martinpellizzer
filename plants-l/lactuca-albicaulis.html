Lactuca albicaulis

Lactuca albicaulis, commonly known as the white-stemmed lettuce, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family.

It is characterized by its slender, white-veined stems and pale green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hair. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. It is native to regions of Europe and Asia and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Lactuca albicaulis is a herbaceous plant that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters.
