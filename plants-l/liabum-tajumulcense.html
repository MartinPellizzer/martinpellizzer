Liabum tajumulcense

Liabum tajumulcense, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, vibrant blue or purple flowers that open in the morning. The plant has climbing stems that twine around supports, and its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive if not managed properly.
