Lantana limensis

Lantana limensis, commonly known as the Jamaican lantana, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Lantana limensis is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in full sun.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and use in traditional medicine.
