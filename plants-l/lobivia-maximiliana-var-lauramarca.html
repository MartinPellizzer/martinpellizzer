Lobivia maximiliana var. lauramarca

Lobivia maximiliana var. lauramarca, commonly known as the "Lauramarca Lobivia," is a compact, flowering cactus native to the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It features a short, thick stem with prominent areoles and produces clusters of small, yellow to orange flowers during the spring. The plant has a dense, woolly coating of white to silver hairs, which helps reduce water loss in its arid environment. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its limited natural range, it is often cultivated in controlled environments to preserve its genetic diversity.
