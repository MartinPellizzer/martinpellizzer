Leucanthemum gussonei

Leucanthemum gussonei, commonly known as the Gussone's Marguerite, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, gray-green leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
