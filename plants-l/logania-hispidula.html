Logania hispidula

Logania hispidula, commonly known as the rough logania, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Loganiaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its fruits are follicles that release seeds with tufts of hair, aiding in wind dispersal.

Logania hispidula is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.
