Lapathum arvense

Lapathum arvense, commonly known as field heal, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Boraginaceae.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often glandular. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers arranged in a spike, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in disturbed soils and is commonly found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Lapathum arvense has a long history of use in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
