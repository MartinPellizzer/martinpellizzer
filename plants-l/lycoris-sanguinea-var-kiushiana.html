Lycoris sanguinea var. kiushiana

Lycoris sanguinea var. kiushiana, commonly known as the Japanese rain lily, is a flowering plant native to Japan and Korea.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers that bloom in late summer, often following rainfall. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

The flowers are not only visually appealing but also attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
