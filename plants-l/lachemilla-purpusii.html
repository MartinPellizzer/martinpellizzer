Lachemilla purpusii

Lachemilla purpusii, commonly known as the purple mossy orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in cloud forests. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, with a unique, moss-like appearance. Its roots form symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi, which aid in nutrient absorption.

Lachemilla purpusii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in specialized horticultural settings.
