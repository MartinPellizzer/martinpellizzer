Lepidomicrosorium lanceolatum

Lepidomicrosorium lanceolatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water fern, is a delicate aquatic plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It features finely divided, lance-shaped fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in shallow, freshwater environments with soft, nutrient-rich substrates. Its reproductive structures are small and inconspicuous, with spores produced in clusters on the underside of its fronds.

Lepidomicrosorium lanceolatum plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
