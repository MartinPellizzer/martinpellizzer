Lupinus micheneri

Lupinus micheneri, commonly known as Michener's lupine, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to the western United States, particularly found in California and Oregon. This plant is characterized by its erect growth habit and clusters of pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The leaves are compound, typically consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

Lupinus micheneri is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.
