Lamium galeobdolon subsp. montanum

Lamium galeobdolon subsp.

montanum, commonly known as mountain deadnettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are typically pubescent and have a distinctive reddish or purple underside.

The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers with two lips, typically in shades of pink or purple, which bloom from spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and rocky slopes.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and native plant gardens.
