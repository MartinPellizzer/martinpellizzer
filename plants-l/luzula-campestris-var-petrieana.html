Luzula campestris var. petrieana

Luzula campestris var. petrieana, commonly known as the purple moor grass, is a perennial grass species native to northern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, dark green leaves that often display a striking purple tinge, especially in shaded conditions. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer, which are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs, heaths, and along the edges of ponds.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
