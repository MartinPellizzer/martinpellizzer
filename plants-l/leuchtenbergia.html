Leuchtenbergia

Leuchtenbergia, commonly known as the "Ghost Plant," is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, leaf-like structures that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating called farina. The plant's leaves are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps it conserve water in its harsh environment. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or yellow in color.

Due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
