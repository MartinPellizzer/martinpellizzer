Lagochilium bahiense

Lagochilium bahiense, commonly known as the coastal morning glory, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in sandy or coastal soils. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Lagochilium bahiense is adapted to saline environments and is often found in dune ecosystems.
