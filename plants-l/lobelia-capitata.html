Lobelia capitata

Lobelia capitata, commonly known as the clustered lobelia, is a herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, heart-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Lobelia capitata is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
