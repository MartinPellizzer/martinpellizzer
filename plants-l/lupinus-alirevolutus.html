Lupinus alirevolutus

Lupinus alirevolutus, commonly known as the Alirevolutus lupine, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of vibrant blue to purple flowers. The plant has pinnate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds. Native to specific regions in South America, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and nitrogen-fixing abilities, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and ecological restoration projects.
