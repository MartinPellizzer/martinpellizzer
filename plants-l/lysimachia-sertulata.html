Lysimachia sertulata

Lysimachia sertulata, commonly known as silverweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to North America.

It features small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has distinctive, hairy, spoon-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine silvery coating. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges.

Lysimachia sertulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
