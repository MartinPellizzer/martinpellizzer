Lucuma multiflora var. urbanii

Lucuma multiflora var. urbanii, commonly known as the urban lucuma, is a lesser-known variant of the lucuma plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish-orange fruits that are typically harvested when fully mature. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, temperate climates and well-drained soil. Its leaves are broad and glossy, often arranged in a rosette pattern, while its flowers are delicate and pale green.

This variety is valued for its unique flavor and is sometimes cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
