Lindsaea alexandri var. bipinnata

Lindsaea alexandri var. bipinnata, commonly known as the Alexandrian lindsaea, is a species of epiphytic fern native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are typically divided into multiple pinnae, giving it a finely dissected appearance. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on tree trunks or branches in the understory of rainforests. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are located on the undersides of the fronds, typical of the Polypodiaceae family.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in botanical gardens.
