Licuala pachycalyx

Licuala pachycalyx, commonly known as the giant pandanus, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces large, spadix-like inflorescences that are often used in floral arrangements. Its leaves are thick and waxy, adapted to withstand humid and sometimes saline environments.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
