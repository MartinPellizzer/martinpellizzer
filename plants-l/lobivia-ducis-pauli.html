Lobivia ducis-pauli

Lobivia ducis-pauli, commonly known as the Pauli's lobivia, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems and clusters of small, spiny leaves. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments.

Due to its unique appearance and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated by succulent enthusiasts.
