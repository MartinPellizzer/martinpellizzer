Lupinus micranthus var. congdonii

Lupinus micranthus var. congdonii, commonly known as the Congdon's lupine, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
