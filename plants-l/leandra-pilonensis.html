Leandra pilonensis

Leandra pilonensis, commonly known as the Pilon's treefern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant has a symbiotic relationship with mycorrhizal fungi, which aid in nutrient absorption from the soil. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine by local communities.

Leandra pilonensis plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
