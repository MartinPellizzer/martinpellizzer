Lychnis furcata subsp. affinis

Lychnis furcata subsp.

affinis, commonly known as the common mouse-ear, is a flowering plant belonging to the carnation family, Caryophyllaceae. It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and features small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has hairy leaves that are often lobed or notched, and its stems are typically covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

This subspecies is closely related to other members of the Lychnis genus and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
