Lobelia divaricata

Lobelia divaricata, commonly known as the spreading lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Lobelia divaricata is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
