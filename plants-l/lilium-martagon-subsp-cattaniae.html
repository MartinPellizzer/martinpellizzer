Lilium martagon subsp. cattaniae

Lilium martagon subsp.

cattaniae, commonly known as the Sicilian martagon lily, is a rare and ornamental species native to southern Italy. It is characterized by its striking, recurved petals that form a bell-shaped bloom, typically in shades of pink and white.

The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in woodland or shaded environments, preferring well-drained, humus-rich soil.

This subspecies is valued for its unique appearance and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
