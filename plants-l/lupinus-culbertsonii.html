Lupinus culbertsonii

Lupinus culbertsonii, commonly known as Culbertson's lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces showy, pea-like flowers in shades of purple and blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Lupinus culbertsonii plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native environment.
