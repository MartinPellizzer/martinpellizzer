Liparis cordifolia

Liparis cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments, often on tree trunks or rocks. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, often clustered in a loose inflorescence.

Liparis cordifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
