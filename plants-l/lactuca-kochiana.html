Lactuca kochiana

Lactuca kochiana, commonly known as Koch's lettuce, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family, Asteraceae.

It is native to regions of Central Asia and is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

Lactuca kochiana is not typically cultivated for food but is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
