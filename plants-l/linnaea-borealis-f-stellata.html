Linnaea borealis f. stellata

Linnaea borealis f. stellata, commonly known as the star-form alpine beauty, is a rare and striking variant of the twinflower.

It is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the northern hemisphere, particularly found in alpine and boreal regions. The plant features small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring, adding a delicate touch to its foliage. Its leaves are oval and dark green, often covered in a fine layer of white wax.

This unique form is prized for its ornamental value and ecological significance in its native habitats.
