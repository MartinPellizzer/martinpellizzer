Lasianthera africana var. microphylla

Lasianthera africana var. microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved African star, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in open woodland or along riverbanks. The plant features small, elliptical leaves with a smooth surface and a pale green color. Its flowers are clustered in umbels and are usually white or pale yellow, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
