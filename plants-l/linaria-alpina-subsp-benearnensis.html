Linaria alpina subsp. benearnensis

Linaria alpina subsp.

benearnensis, commonly known as the Alpine toadflax, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.

The plant features slender, erect stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence. Its flowers are bright yellow, with two lips, resembling the shape of a toadflax, and bloom in late summer.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
