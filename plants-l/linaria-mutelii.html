Linaria mutelii

Linaria mutelii, commonly known as the Mutel's toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and northern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, bright yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs.

Linaria mutelii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
