Laggera makarikariensis

Laggera makarikariensis, commonly known as the Kapinga daisy, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific islands of French Polynesia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows as a shrub with erect, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are yellow and appear in clusters, often blooming during the dry season.

It is adapted to coastal and sandy soils, making it a hardy species in tropical environments.
