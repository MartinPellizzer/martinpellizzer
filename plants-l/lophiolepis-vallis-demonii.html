Lophiolepis vallis-demonii

Lophiolepis vallis-demonii, commonly known as the Demon's Valley grass, is a perennial grass species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the grass family Poaceae. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in rocky or disturbed habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
