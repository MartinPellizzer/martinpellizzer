Lycopus pensylvanicus

Lycopus pensylvanicus, commonly known as Pennsylvania bugleweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, blue to purple flowers in clusters. Its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional herbal remedies.

The species is part of the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage.
