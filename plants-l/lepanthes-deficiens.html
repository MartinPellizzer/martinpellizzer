Lepanthes deficiens

Lepanthes deficiens, commonly known as the deficient lady's slipper, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically feature a pale yellow or white coloration with intricate patterns. The plant grows in shaded, humid environments, often in the understory of tropical forests. Its leaves are usually elliptical and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its floral display.

Lepanthes deficiens is notable for its unique morphology and is a subject of interest in botanical studies due to its ecological and evolutionary significance.
