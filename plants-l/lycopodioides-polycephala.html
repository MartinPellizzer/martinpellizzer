Lycopodioides polycephala

Lycopodioides polycephala, commonly known as the many-headed clubmoss, is a member of the Lycopodiaceae family.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features a short, horizontal stem with numerous small, spirally arranged leaves. Its reproductive structures include sporophylls that bear spores, typical of its evolutionary lineage.

Lycopodioides polycephala plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
