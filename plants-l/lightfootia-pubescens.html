Lightfootia pubescens

Lightfootia pubescens, commonly known as the silky spurge, is a herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features slender, upright stems covered in soft, white pubescence, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Lightfootia pubescens is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
