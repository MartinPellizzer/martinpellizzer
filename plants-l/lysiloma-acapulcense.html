Lysiloma acapulcense

Lysiloma acapulcense, commonly known as the Acapulco trumpet tree, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds, typical of many leguminous species. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in dry forests and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warmer regions.
