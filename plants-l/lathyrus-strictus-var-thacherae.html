Lathyrus strictus var. thacherae

Lathyrus strictus var. thacherae, commonly known as the narrowleaf sweet pea, is a perennial climbing plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, twining stems and produces clusters of small, fragrant, violet-blue flowers. The leaves are pinnately compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
