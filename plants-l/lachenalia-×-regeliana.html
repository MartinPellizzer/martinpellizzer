Lachenalia × regeliana

Lachenalia × regeliana, commonly known as the hybrid lachenalia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are typically fleshy and covered in a powdery coating. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red during the warmer months. It requires well-draining soil and full sun to thrive, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its hybrid nature, it often exhibits a wide range of color variations and growth forms.
