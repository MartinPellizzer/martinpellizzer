Lupinus meionanthus

Lupinus meionanthus, commonly known as the small-flowered lupine, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with numerous small leaflets, and its root system includes nitrogen-fixing nodules. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry grasslands and open woodlands.

Lupinus meionanthus is an important plant for supporting pollinators and is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
