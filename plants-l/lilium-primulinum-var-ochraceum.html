Lilium primulinum var. ochraceum

Lilium primulinum var. ochraceum, commonly known as the yellow lily, is a rare and striking variant of the primrose lily.

It is native to the mountainous regions of western North America, particularly in California and Oregon. The plant features showy, orange-yellow flowers with a distinctive red throat, blooming in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, while the stems are sturdy and upright.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and unique appearance.
