Lophatherum

Lophatherum, commonly known as the wild rice grass, is a member of the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain minute, wind-pollinated flowers. Lophatherum is native to Asia and thrives in wetland and marsh environments.

It is often used in traditional medicine and has ecological significance in its native habitats.
