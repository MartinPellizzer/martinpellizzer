Lobelia kirkii var. microphylla

Lobelia kirkii var. microphylla, commonly known as the dwarf lobelia, is a perennial herb native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories or rocky crevices. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
