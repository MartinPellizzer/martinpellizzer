Lathyrus filiformis

Lathyrus filiformis, commonly known as the slender vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Lathyrus filiformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in nitrogen fixation, benefiting soil fertility.
