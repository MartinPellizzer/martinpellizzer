Linum tenellum

Linum tenellum, commonly known as the lesser flax, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces capsules containing numerous small, oval seeds. It is often found in grasslands, wastelands, and along roadsides.

Linum tenellum is part of the Linaceae family and is known for its fibrous stalks, which are similar to those of the flax plant.
