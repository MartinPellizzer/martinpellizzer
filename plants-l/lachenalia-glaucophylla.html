Lachenalia glaucophylla

Lachenalia glaucophylla, commonly known as the silver-leaf lachenalia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are covered in a powdery white coating, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which are clustered in terminal inflorescences. It is adapted to arid conditions and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Lachenalia glaucophylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique foliage.
