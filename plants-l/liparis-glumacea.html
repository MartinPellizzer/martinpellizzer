Liparis glumacea

Liparis glumacea, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are linear and often clustered at the base, while its roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption in poor soils.

Liparis glumacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
