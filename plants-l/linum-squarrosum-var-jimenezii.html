Linum squarrosum var. jimenezii

Linum squarrosum var. jimenezii, commonly known as the Jimenez's flax, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it well adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations and limited distribution.
