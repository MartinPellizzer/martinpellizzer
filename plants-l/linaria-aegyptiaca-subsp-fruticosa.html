Linaria aegyptiaca subsp. fruticosa

Linaria aegyptiaca subsp.

fruticosa, commonly known as Egyptian toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a perennial shrub characterized by its woody stems and dense, compact growth habit.

The plant produces bright yellow or orange tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant nature. Linaria aegyptiaca subsp.

fruticosa is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
