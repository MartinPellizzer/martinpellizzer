Lycaste macrophylla subsp. puntarenasensis

Lycaste macrophylla subsp.

puntarenasensis, commonly known as the Puntarenas orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central America. It is characterized by its large, showy flowers with a distinctive yellow lip and greenish-white petals.

The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, tropical environments. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native habitat.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm, shaded gardens.
