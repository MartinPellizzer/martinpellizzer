Linkia marginata

Linkia marginata, commonly known as the coastal hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in sandy or saline soils near the shoreline. The plant features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually pink or red, with five petals and a prominent central stamen. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Linkia marginata is adapted to salt spray and is often used in coastal landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness.
