Leptochloa laurentii

Leptochloa laurentii, commonly known as the slender grass or African grass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Leptochloa laurentii is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various small organisms.
