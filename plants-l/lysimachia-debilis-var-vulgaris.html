Lysimachia debilis var. vulgaris

Lysimachia debilis var. vulgaris, commonly known as lesser yellow archangel, is a low-growing perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe.

It features slender, spreading stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, golden-yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in whorls along the stems. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and covered with fine hairs.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
