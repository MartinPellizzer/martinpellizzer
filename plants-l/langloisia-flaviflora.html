Langloisia flaviflora

Langloisia flaviflora, commonly known as the yellow-flowered langlois, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow petals and compact, bushy growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a glossy texture, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Langloisia flaviflora is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
