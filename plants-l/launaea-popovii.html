Launaea popovii

Launaea popovii, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant features a cylindrical, succulent stem covered in fine hairs, adapted to reduce water loss in dry environments. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in a waxy cuticle to minimize evaporation.

Launaea popovii produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by fluffy seeds dispersed by the wind.
