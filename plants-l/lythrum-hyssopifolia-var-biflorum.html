Lythrum hyssopifolia var. biflorum

Lythrum hyssopifolia var. biflorum, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features striking spikes of deep violet to purple flowers that bloom from summer to fall. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

Its botanical structure includes a fibrous root system and a branching stem that supports its showy floral display.
