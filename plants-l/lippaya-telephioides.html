Lippaya telephioides

Lippaya telephioides, commonly known as the silver leaf cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage that reflects sunlight, helping it retain moisture in harsh environments. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the warmer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice for desert gardens and rockeries.
