Lonicera involucrata var. involucrata

Lonicera involucrata var. involucrata, commonly known as the rough honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with hairy undersides and produces fragrant, tubular flowers in the spring. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and can reach heights of up to 6 feet. Its berries are orange-red and edible, providing food for birds and small mammals.

It is valued in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.
