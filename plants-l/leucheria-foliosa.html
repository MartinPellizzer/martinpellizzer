Leucheria foliosa

Leucheria foliosa, commonly known as the leafy orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, strap-like leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its botanical features include a short rhizome and clustered inflorescences that produce numerous tiny white or pale yellow blooms.

Leucheria foliosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or shaded gardens.
