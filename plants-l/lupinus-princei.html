Lupinus princei

Lupinus princei, commonly known as the Prince's lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of blue or purple pea-like flowers. The plant has feathery, compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and open woodland habitats.

Lupinus princei is a member of the legume family and has the ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for ecosystem health.
