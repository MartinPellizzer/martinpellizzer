Liriodendron truncatifolium

Liriodendron truncatifolium, commonly known as the tulip tree, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are truncate at the base, giving it a distinctive appearance. The tree produces showy, tulip-like yellow flowers in the spring, which are followed by elongated, woody fruits. Its bark is smooth and light gray, becoming fissured with age.

The tulip tree is valued for its fast growth and ornamental qualities, making it a popular choice in landscaping.
