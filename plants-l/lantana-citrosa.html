Lantana citrosa

Lantana citrosa, commonly known as orange lantana, is a perennial shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It features opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are typically green with a slightly aromatic scent. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to yellow, orange, or red, depending on the variety. Its fruit is a small, fleshy capsule that contains numerous tiny seeds.

Lantana citrosa is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in full sun, making it a common ornamental plant in warmer climates.
