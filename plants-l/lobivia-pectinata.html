Lobivia pectinata

Lobivia pectinata, commonly known as the Pectinata Lobivia, is a succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It features a compact, cylindrical stem with distinctive ribbed structures that help it store water. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the growing season, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically fleshy and covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Lobivia pectinata is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
