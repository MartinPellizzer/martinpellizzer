Loranthus alatus

Loranthus alatus, commonly known as the winged mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that attaches itself to host trees, primarily in the genus Eucalyptus.

It lacks true roots and absorbs water and nutrients through its stems, which are often covered in small, leaf-like structures called phyllodes. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically visited by insects for pollination. Its berries are red and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

Loranthus alatus is native to Australia and is often found in coastal and inland regions where its host trees thrive.
