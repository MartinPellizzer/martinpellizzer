Lepanthopsis abbreviata

Lepanthopsis abbreviata, commonly known as the dwarf orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist environments within forest understories. The plant features a compact growth habit with short, fleshy roots and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with a distinctive white or pale pink coloration.

Lepanthopsis abbreviata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouse settings due to its specific environmental requirements.
