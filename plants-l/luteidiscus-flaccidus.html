It is characterized by its yellow to orange-brown thallus, which is typically crustose or foliose in growth form. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found on bark or rock surfaces. Its chemical composition includes various secondary metabolites, such as atranorin and lupeol.

Luteidiscus flaccidus plays a role in ecological systems by contributing to soil stabilization and serving as a food source for certain invertebrates.
