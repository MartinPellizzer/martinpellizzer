Larix europaea var. multicaulis

Larix europaea var. multicaulis, commonly known as the European larch, is a deciduous conifer native to Europe.

It is characterized by its needle-like leaves that turn golden in autumn before shedding. The tree produces cone-like structures that contain its seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It typically grows in well-drained soils and prefers full sun exposure.

This variety is known for its tall, slender form and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.
