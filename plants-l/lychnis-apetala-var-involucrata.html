Lychnis apetala var. involucrata

Lychnis apetala var. involucrata, commonly known as the woolly mouse-ear, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green foliage and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. Its leaves are basal and form a rosette, while the stems are upright and branched.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in alpine gardens.
