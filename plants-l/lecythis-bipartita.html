Lecythis bipartita

Lecythis bipartita, commonly known as the two-part lecythium, is a tropical tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in diameter. The tree produces heavy, woody fruits that contain a single, large seed surrounded by a sticky, orange pulp. Its bark is thick and often covered in a layer of resinous exudate.

The plant is valued for its ornamental potential and has some traditional uses in local communities.
