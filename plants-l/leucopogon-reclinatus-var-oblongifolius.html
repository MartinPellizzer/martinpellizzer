Leucopogon reclinatus var. oblongifolius

Leucopogon reclinatus var. oblongifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in dense, silvery-white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or red, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
