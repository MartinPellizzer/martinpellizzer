Loranthus glaucophyllus

Loranthus glaucophyllus, commonly known as the glaucous mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily eucalyptus species.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using the light it captures. The plant has small, leathery leaves and produces white or pink flowers that attract insects for pollination. Its berries, which are typically yellow or red, contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, contributing to the biodiversity of forest ecosystems.
