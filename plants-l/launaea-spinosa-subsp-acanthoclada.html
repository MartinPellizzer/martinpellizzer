Launaea spinosa subsp. acanthoclada

Launaea spinosa subsp.

acanthoclada, commonly known as the spiny launaea, is a shrub-like plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East. It is characterized by its succulent, spiny stems and narrow, fleshy leaves that help reduce water loss in harsh environments.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming during the rainy season. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access deep water sources, making it drought-resistant.

It is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to high temperatures and low precipitation.
