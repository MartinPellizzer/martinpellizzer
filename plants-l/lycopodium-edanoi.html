Lycopodium edanoi

Lycopodium edanoi, commonly known as the giant clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and dense clusters of small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces spores in specialized structures called sporangia, which are typically clustered in strobila, or cone-like formations. L. edanoi is native to tropical and subtropical regions, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its unique morphology and reproductive structures make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
