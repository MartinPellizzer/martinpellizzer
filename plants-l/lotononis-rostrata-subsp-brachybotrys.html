Lotononis rostrata subsp. brachybotrys

Lotononis rostrata subsp.

brachybotrys, commonly known as the short-stemmed lotononis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple or blue.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces seed pods that mature into distinctive, three-lobed structures. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable conditions.
