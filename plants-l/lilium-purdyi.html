Lilium purdyi

Lilium purdyi, commonly known as the Pud's lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white to pink. The plant produces a single, upright stem with lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

Lilium purdyi is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
