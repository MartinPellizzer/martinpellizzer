Lythrum thesioides

Lythrum thesioides, commonly known as narrowleaf lythrum, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lythraceae family, which includes other flowering plants such as the poinsettia. The plant features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy, pink to red flowers are clustered in spikes and bloom in late summer.

Lythrum thesioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.
