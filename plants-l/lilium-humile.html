Lilium humile

Lilium humile, commonly known as the dwarf lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 15 to 30 centimeters. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, often with a distinct red or orange stigma. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, arranged in a basal rosette.

Lilium humile is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
