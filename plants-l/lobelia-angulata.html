Lobelia angulata

Lobelia angulata, commonly known as the angular lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments like woodlands and stream banks.

Lobelia angulata is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
