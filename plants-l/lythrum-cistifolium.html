Lythrum cistifolium

Lythrum cistifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved catchfly, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lythraceae family, which includes other flowering plants such as the poinsettia. The plant is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Lythrum cistifolium is adapted to grow in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.
