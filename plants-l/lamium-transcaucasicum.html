Lamium transcaucasicum

Lamium transcaucasicum, commonly known as Caucasian deadnettle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic and medicinal plants. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces small, tubular flowers in shades of pink or purple. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
