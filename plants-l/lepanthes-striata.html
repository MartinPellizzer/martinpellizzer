Lepanthes striata

Lepanthes striata, commonly known as the streaked orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist forest understories. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with a short rhizome and several erect, lanceolate leaves. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and often white or pale yellow with intricate patterns.

Lepanthes striata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
