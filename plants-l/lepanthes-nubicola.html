Lepanthes nubicola

Lepanthes nubicola, commonly known as the cloud forest orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, fragrant flowers with white petals and a distinctive yellow lip. Its flowers bloom in the summer and are often pollinated by bees and other insects.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, Lepanthes nubicola is considered a rare and endangered species.
