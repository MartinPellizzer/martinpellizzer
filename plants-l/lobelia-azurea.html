Lobelia azurea

Lobelia azurea, commonly known as blue lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Lobelia azurea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
