Limonium vulgare subsp. serotinum

Limonium vulgare subsp.

serotinum, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its blue or purple flowers and linear, grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in coastal areas, sandy soils, and saline environments. It blooms from late summer to early autumn, adding vibrant color to coastal landscapes.

Its hardy nature makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal gardens.
