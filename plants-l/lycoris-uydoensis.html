Lycoris uydoensis

Lycoris uydoensis, commonly known as the Korean rain lily, is a flowering plant native to Korea and parts of China.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking red and white flowers that bloom in the fall. The plant features strap-like, narrow leaves that emerge from a bulbous stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often found in woodland areas.

Lycoris uydoensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and cultural significance in traditional Korean gardens.
