Lindernia petrensis

Lindernia petrensis, commonly known as the rock lindernia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy flowers, which range in color from purple to pink, are borne in dense spikes and bloom during the spring and summer months.

Lindernia petrensis thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.
