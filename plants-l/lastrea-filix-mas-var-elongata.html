Lastrea filix-mas var. elongata

Lastrea filix-mas var. elongata, commonly known as the snake plant or mother-in-law's tongue, is a striking evergreen perennial plant native to tropical regions.

It features tall, upright leaves that are typically sword-shaped and can grow up to several feet in length. The plant is characterized by its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and requires minimal watering, making it highly suitable for indoor environments. Its foliage is often variegated with shades of green, yellow, and white, adding visual interest to any space.

The plant is also noted for its air-purifying qualities, contributing to improved indoor air quality.
