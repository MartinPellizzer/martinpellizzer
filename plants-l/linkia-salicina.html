Linkia salicina

Linkia salicina, commonly known as the white coral tree, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling coral in appearance. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a feathery pattern. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal or disturbed soils.

Linkia salicina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value.
