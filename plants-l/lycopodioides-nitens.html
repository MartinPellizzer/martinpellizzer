Lycopodioides nitens

Lycopodioides nitens, commonly known as the slender clubmoss, is a small, evergreen fern-like plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces spore-bearing cones called strobila, which are typically clustered at the tips of its stems. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a spiral pattern, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Lycopodioides nitens is often found in rocky or forested areas, where it plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for small invertebrates.
