Lettsomia festiva

Lettsomia festiva, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, giving it a compact appearance. Lettsomia festiva thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value has made it a popular choice among gardeners seeking drought-resistant plants.
