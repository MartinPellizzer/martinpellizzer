Leymus racemosus var. sabulosus

Leymus racemosus var. sabulosus, commonly known as the sandreed grass, is a perennial grass species native to coastal and sandy environments.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, fibrous root system that helps stabilize sandy soils. The plant produces spikelets that are typically arranged in racemes, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in well-drained, saline soils and is often found in dunes and coastal prairies.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in preventing erosion and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
