Leptorumohra × takayamensis

Leptorumohra × takayamensis, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a naturally occurring hybrid between Leptorumohra yamashitanii and Leptorumohra kiusiana.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and attractive, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant prefers shaded, moist environments and is often found in the understory of forests in Japan. Its botanical significance lies in its role as a hybrid species that demonstrates genetic diversity within the Leptorumohra genus.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated by enthusiasts of rare orchids.
