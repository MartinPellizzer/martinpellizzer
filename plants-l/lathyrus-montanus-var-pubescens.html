Lathyrus montanus var. pubescens

Lathyrus montanus var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent grass vetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features slender, erect stems covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its trifoliate leaves are heart-shaped with a hairy surface, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

This species is adapted to well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
