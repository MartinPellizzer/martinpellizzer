Ladenbergia coriacea

Ladenbergia coriacea, commonly known as the hard-stemmed legume, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody, thorny stems and compound leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, followed by cylindrical, woody pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.
