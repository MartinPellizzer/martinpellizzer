Leschenaultia longiloba

Leschenaultia longiloba, commonly known as the long-stemmed leschenaultia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Leschenaultia longiloba is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
