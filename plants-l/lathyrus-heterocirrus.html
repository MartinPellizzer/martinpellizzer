Lathyrus heterocirrus

Lathyrus heterocirrus, commonly known as the purple vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves, which are typically green with a slight pubescence. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pale purple to deep violet, often appearing in clusters. Its fruit is a legume pod containing several elliptical seeds.

Lathyrus heterocirrus is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in well-drained soils in sunny to partially shaded environments.
