Lolium husnotii

Lolium husnotii, commonly known as the narrowleaf ryegrass, is a species of grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Lolium husnotii is valued for its use in forage and as a cover crop due to its rapid growth and hardiness.
