Lepidium tetraspermum

Lepidium tetraspermum, commonly known as four-seeded rocket, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of South America.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant produces small, white to yellowish flowers that are typically arranged in racemes. Its leaves are pinnately divided, and the fruit is a dry, segmented capsule containing four seeds.

Lepidium tetraspermum is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is sometimes used in herbal remedies.
