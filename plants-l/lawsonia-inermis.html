Lawsonia inermis

Lawsonia inermis, commonly known as the henna plant, is a shrub native to North Africa, the Middle East, and parts of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces a reddish-brown dye from its dried leaves, which has been used for centuries in hair and skin treatments. It thrives in arid and semi-arid climates, preferring well-drained soils.

Lawsonia inermis is also valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional remedies.
