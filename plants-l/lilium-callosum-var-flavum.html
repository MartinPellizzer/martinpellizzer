Lilium callosum var. flavum

Lilium callosum var. flavum, commonly known as the yellow bell lily, is a rare and striking member of the Liliaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of China, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. The plant is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which are typically bell-shaped and emit a subtle fragrance. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a slightly waxy texture.

This variety is highly valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts due to its rarity and beauty.
