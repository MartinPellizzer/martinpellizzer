Lepidagathis hyssopifolia

Lepidagathis hyssopifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved false hyssop, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers in clusters. Its botanical characteristics include hairy stems and showy blue or violet flowers that attract pollinators.

It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, contributing to local biodiversity.
