Lotus tenuis subsp. decumbens

Lotus tenuis subsp.

decumbens, commonly known as the creeping lotus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern China and Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its slender, sprawling stems that often lie close to the ground, forming dense mats.

The plant produces small, pale pink to white flowers with five petals and a yellowish center, typical of the legume family. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in moist, sunny environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
