Lycopodioides rionegrensis

Lycopodioides rionegrensis, commonly known as the Rionegro clubmoss, is a small, evergreen fern-like plant native to the cloud forests of Colombia.

It belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae and is characterized by its creeping, rhizomatous growth habit and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces spore-bearing cones, which are typically clustered at the base of the stems. Lycopodioides rionegrensis thrives in humid, shaded environments and plays a role in maintaining the ecological balance of its native habitat.

Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it an important species for botanical studies in tropical ecosystems.
