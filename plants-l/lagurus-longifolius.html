Lagurus longifolius

Lagurus longifolius, commonly known as the woollypod or silky grass, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, feathery foliage and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a distinctive pod-like structure that resembles a fluffy seed pod. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
