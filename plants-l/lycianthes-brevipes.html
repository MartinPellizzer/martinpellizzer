Lycianthes brevipes

Lycianthes brevipes, commonly known as the short-stemmed nightshade, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its hairy stems and small, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are typically clustered in the leaf axils, followed by orange or red berries containing several seeds. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and peppers.

Lycianthes brevipes is often found in dry, open habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid conditions.
