Laurus robusta

Laurus robusta, commonly known as the Turkish laurel, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like bay laurel. The plant is characterized by its dense, dark green, leathery leaves that are often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb. Laurus robusta produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by dark blue-black berries.

It thrives in well-drained soil and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
