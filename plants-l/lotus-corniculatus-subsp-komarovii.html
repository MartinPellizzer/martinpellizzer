Lotus corniculatus subsp. komarovii

Lotus corniculatus subsp.

komarovii, commonly known as the Komarov's grass vetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia. It features slender, branched stems and small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in spikes.

The leaves are trifoliate, with three oval to lanceolate leaflets. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as steppes and rocky soils.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological adaptability and is often found in arid regions.
