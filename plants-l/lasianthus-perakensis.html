Lasianthus perakensis

Lasianthus perakensis, commonly known as the Bornean flame flower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia, particularly Borneo.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture, while its stems are often hairy.

Lasianthus perakensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
