Lonchocarpus pycnophyllus

Lonchocarpus pycnophyllus, commonly known as the dense-leaved legume, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, forested environments. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation due to its symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.

The species is also valued for its potential in ecological restoration and as a source of traditional medicinal compounds.
