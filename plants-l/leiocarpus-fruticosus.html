Leiocarpus fruticosus

Leiocarpus fruticosus, commonly known as the coastal morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue, violet, and purple. The plant thrives in sandy soils and is often found in mangrove ecosystems along the shorelines. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are woody and covered in fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize coastal dunes.
