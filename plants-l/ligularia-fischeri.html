Ligularia fischeri

Ligularia fischeri, commonly known as the giant ragwort, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in a dense, flat-topped inflorescence. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Due to its size and striking appearance, it is a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
