Lobelia falcata

Lobelia falcata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and spikes of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. It is a perennial herb that produces showy blooms from late spring to early summer.

Lobelia falcata is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
