Lycaste tyrianthina

Lycaste tyrianthina, commonly known as the Tyrian lycaste, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, enabling it to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates. Its roots are fibrous and adapted for anchoring in loose, well-draining soil.

Lycaste tyrianthina is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
