Lessertia dubia

Lessertia dubia, commonly known as the African milk tree, is a small, slow-growing shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray-green bark and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by distinctive orange or red berries.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
