Lessingianthus rugulosus

Lessingianthus rugulosus, commonly known as the roughleaf lessingia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features hairy, rugose (rough) leaves that give it its common name, and it produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a compact cluster. It thrives in dry, open habitats and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
