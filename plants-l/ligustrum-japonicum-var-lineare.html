Ligustrum japonicum var. lineare

Ligustrum japonicum var. lineare, commonly known as Japanese privet, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a linear pattern along the stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by dark blue berries in the fall. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

However, it can be invasive in some regions outside its native habitat.
