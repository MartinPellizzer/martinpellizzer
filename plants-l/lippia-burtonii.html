Lippia burtonii

Lippia burtonii, commonly known as Mexican mint or burton's lippia, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant features opposite, oval-shaped leaves with a fragrant, mint-like aroma. It produces small, clustered white or pale purple flowers that bloom during the summer months.

Lippia burtonii is often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb due to its aromatic properties.
