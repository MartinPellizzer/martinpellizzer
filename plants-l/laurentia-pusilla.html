Laurentia pusilla

Laurentia pusilla, commonly known as the small laurentia, is a small flowering plant native to the wetlands and marshes of eastern North America.

It typically grows to a height of about 10-20 centimeters and has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often submerged or floating. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It is an important component of aquatic ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various invertebrates.

Laurentia pusilla is often found in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.
