Leucopogon vieillardii

Leucopogon vieillardii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant features small, dense clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, adapted to thrive in acidic, well-drained soils.

It is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
