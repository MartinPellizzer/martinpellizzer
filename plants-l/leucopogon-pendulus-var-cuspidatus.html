Leucopogon pendulus var. cuspidatus

Leucopogon pendulus var. cuspidatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved weeping heath, is a shrub native to the eastern regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its pendulous clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are narrow, linear, and often have a pointed tip, giving it the specific epithet "cuspidatus.". It typically grows in well-drained sandy or rocky soils in open forests or heathlands.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, cascading floral display.
