Lycium mediterraneum var. cinnamomeum

Lycium mediterraneum var. cinnamomeum, commonly known as the Mediterranean boxthorn, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often bitter. The plant has dense, spiny foliage that provides habitat for various insects and birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for rocky or arid environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and ornamental plant in Mediterranean and coastal gardens.
