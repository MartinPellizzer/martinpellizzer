Lockhartia ivainae

Lockhartia ivainae, commonly known as the ivory orchid, is a rare and exquisite species native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking white and green floral structures. The plant produces fragrant, pendulous flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a subtle ivory hue. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, acidic soil.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is highly valued in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
