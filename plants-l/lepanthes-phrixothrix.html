Lepanthes phrixothrix

Lepanthes phrixothrix, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees in humid, tropical environments. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with intricate patterns that attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Lepanthes phrixothrix is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
