Leptolobium paradisi

Leptolobium paradisi, commonly known as the paradise everlet, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are often dispersed by wind or animals.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
