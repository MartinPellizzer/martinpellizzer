Lonchocarpus bussei

Lonchocarpus bussei, commonly known as the African senna, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces distinctive pods containing seeds that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to warm, humid climates.

Due to its nitrogen-fixing ability, it plays a role in soil enrichment in its native habitats.
