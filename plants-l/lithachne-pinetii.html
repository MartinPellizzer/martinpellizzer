Lithachne pinetii

Lithachne pinetii, commonly known as the Florida panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in saturated soils and its role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.

Lithachne pinetii is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and resilience.
