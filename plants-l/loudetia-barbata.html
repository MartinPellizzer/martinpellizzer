Loudetia barbata

Loudetia barbata, commonly known as the hairy grass or barbed grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to southern Africa, where it typically grows in open, sandy or rocky soils. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a hairy texture. Its inflorescence consists of a spikelet arrangement, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Loudetia barbata is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
