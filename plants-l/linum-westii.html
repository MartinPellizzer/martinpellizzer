Linum westii

Linum westii, commonly known as the western flax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds.

Linum westii is often found in meadows and along dry streambeds, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments.
