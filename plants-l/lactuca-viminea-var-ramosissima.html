Lactuca viminea var. ramosissima

Lactuca viminea var. ramosissima, commonly known as the curly endive, is a cultivated variety of the common endive plant.

It is characterized by its tightly curled, pale green leaves that form a dense, compact head. The plant is typically grown for its edible leaves, which have a slightly bitter taste and are often used in salads or cooked dishes. It thrives in cool climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other leafy greens and medicinal plants.
