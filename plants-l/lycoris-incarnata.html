Lycoris incarnata

Lycoris incarnata, commonly known as the Chinese lantern flower, is a perennial bulbous plant native to East Asia.

It features striking red-orange flowers that bloom in late summer, resembling small lanterns. The plant has strap-like, narrow leaves that emerge from a bulbous base. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant blooms and long-lasting foliage.
