Leucas dinteri

Leucas dinteri, commonly known as Dinter's leucas, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
