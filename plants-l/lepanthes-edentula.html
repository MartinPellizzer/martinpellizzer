Lepanthes edentula

Lepanthes edentula, commonly known as the toothless orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees in humid tropical forests. The plant produces delicate, white or pale pink flowers with intricate patterns on the petals. Its leaves are usually ovate and arranged in a rosette formation.

Lepanthes edentula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
