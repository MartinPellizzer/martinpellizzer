Lippia grandiflora

Lippia grandiflora, commonly known as Mexican blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its essential oils contain compounds like thymol and carvacrol, which contribute to its medicinal and aromatic properties.

Lippia grandiflora is also valued in traditional medicine for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory benefits.
