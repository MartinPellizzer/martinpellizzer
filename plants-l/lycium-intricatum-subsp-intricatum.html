Lycium intricatum subsp. intricatum

Lycium intricatum subsp.

intricatum, commonly known as the intricate boxthorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and small, often yellow or orange berries.

The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are small, elliptical, and usually covered in fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
