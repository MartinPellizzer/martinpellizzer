Leonohebe ciliolata

Leonohebe ciliolata, commonly known as the velvetleaf hebe, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in some regions.
