Lupinus formosus var. bridgesii

Lupinus formosus var. bridgesii, commonly known as the Bridge's lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and showy blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Lupinus formosus var. bridgesii plays a role in soil nitrogen fixation due to its symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.
