Lampranthus caespitosus

Lampranthus caespitosus, commonly known as the rock garden geranium, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features compact, bushy growth with dense clusters of small, bright pink to red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.

Its drought tolerance and vibrant floral display make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and garden ornamental use.
