Lockhartia bennettii

Lockhartia bennettii, commonly known as the Bennett's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments within the understory of forests. The plant features a compact, upright growth habit with dark green, strap-like leaves. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and usually appear in clusters, displaying a range of colors from white to pink and purple.

Lockhartia bennettii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
