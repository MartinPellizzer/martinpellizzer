Lolium perenne var. pacyanum

Lolium perenne var. pacyanum, commonly known as the blue grass variant, is a variant of perennial ryegrass characterized by its distinctive blue-green foliage.

It is a cool-season grass that thrives in temperate climates, often found in lawns and pastures. The plant has fine, erect blades and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. It is valued for its dense growth habit and high wear tolerance, making it suitable for turfgrass applications.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental and agricultural purposes.
