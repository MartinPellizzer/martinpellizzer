Leucas montana var. wightii

Leucas montana var. wightii, commonly known as the Kashmir leucas, is a flowering plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to the Himalayan regions of India, Pakistan, and Afghanistan, where it thrives in high-altitude environments. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, square stems and opposite leaves with a serrated margin. Its small, clustered flowers are typically pale blue to violet, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported antipyretic and anti-inflammatory properties.
