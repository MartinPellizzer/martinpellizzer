Lupinus tafiensis

Lupinus tafiensis, commonly known as the Tafiensian lupin, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant produces cylindrical, spiny pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Lupinus tafiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in soil stabilization.
