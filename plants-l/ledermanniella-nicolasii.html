Ledermanniella nicolasii

Ledermanniella nicolasii, commonly known as the Nicolasii lichen, is a rare and specialized lichen species belonging to the family Lichenes.

It is characterized by its unique thallus structure, which is typically crustose and tightly adherent to its substrate. The organism forms a symbiotic relationship between a fungus and a green alga or cyanobacterium, which contributes to its ability to survive in nutrient-poor environments. It is often found on bark or stone in temperate regions, where it plays a role in ecological succession and soil formation.
