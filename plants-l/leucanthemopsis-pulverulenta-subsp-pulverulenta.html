Leucanthemopsis pulverulenta subsp. pulverulenta

Leucanthemopsis pulverulenta subsp.

pulverulenta, commonly known as the dusty leucanthemum, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and features small, daisy-like flowers with white petals and yellow centers.

The plant produces pale green, hairy leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in landscaping.
