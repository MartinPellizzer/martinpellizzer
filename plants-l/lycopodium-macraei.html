Lycopodium macraei

Lycopodium macraei, commonly known as the giant clubmoss, is a species of lycophyte native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, cone-like structures called strobila, which contain spores for reproduction. Its leaves are narrow and spirally arranged, typical of the Lycopodiaceae family.

Lycopodium macraei thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical forests.
