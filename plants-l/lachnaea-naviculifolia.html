Lachnaea naviculifolia

Lachnaea naviculifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed water flea, is a small aquatic plant found in freshwater environments.

It belongs to the family Lachnaceae and is characterized by its delicate, thread-like leaves that float on the water's surface. The plant produces tiny, translucent flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. It plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and small invertebrates.

Due to its fragile nature, it is often found in still or slow-moving waters with moderate light conditions.
