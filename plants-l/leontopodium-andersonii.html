Leontopodium andersonii

Leontopodium andersonii, commonly known as the Anderson's edelweiss, is a striking alpine plant native to the high mountains of central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly white bracts that surround a central cluster of small, yellowish flowers. The plant thrives in harsh, rocky environments at high altitudes, often above the tree line. Its growth is slow, and it is adapted to cold, sunny conditions with poor soil.

Due to its beauty and rarity, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and protected areas.
