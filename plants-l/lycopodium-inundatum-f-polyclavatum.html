Lycopodium inundatum f. polyclavatum

Lycopodium inundatum f. polyclavatum, commonly known as the swamp spike moss, is a variant of the common clubmoss.

It is a perennial evergreen plant characterized by its dense, low-growing clusters of spore-bearing cones. The plant thrives in moist, acidic environments such as bogs and wetlands. Its leaves are typically narrow and arranged in whorls around the stem.

Botanically, it is notable for its unique morphology and ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
