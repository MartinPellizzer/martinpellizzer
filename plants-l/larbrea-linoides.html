Larbrea linoides

Larbrea linoides, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its flowers are small and clustered, often white or pale yellow, and it produces woody fruits containing several seeds.

Larbrea linoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
