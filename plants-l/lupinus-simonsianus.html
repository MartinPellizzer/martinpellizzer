Lupinus simonsianus

Lupinus simonsianus, commonly known as the California lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and clusters of blue or violet flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland grasslands.

Lupinus simonsianus plays an important role in soil nitrogen fixation due to its symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.
