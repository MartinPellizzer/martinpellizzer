Lasianthus walkerianus var. lanceolatus

Lasianthus walkerianus var. lanceolatus, commonly known as the lanceolate lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its flowers are often brightly colored, ranging from red to orange, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
