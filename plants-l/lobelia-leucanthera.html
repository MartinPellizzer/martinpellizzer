Lobelia leucanthera

Lobelia leucanthera, commonly known as white cardinal flower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet. The plant features showy, tubular flowers that are predominantly white with a slight pink or red tinge. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Lobelia leucanthera thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and woodland areas.
