Lacosteopsis maxima

Lacosteopsis maxima, commonly known as the giant lace fern, is a striking member of the Davalliaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, delicate fronds that can reach lengths of up to 2 meters, with a feathery, lacy appearance. Native to tropical regions of South America, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its rhizomes form dense colonies, contributing to its ability to spread and establish itself in suitable habitats.

The plant's unique morphology and growth habit make it a notable species in botanical studies and horticulture.
