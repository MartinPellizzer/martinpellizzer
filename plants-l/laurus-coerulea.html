Laurus coerulea

Laurus coerulea, commonly known as the blue laurel, is a flowering shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in spring, adding a vibrant touch to gardens. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves that are aromatic and often used in perfumery and culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Laurus coerulea is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
