Ludolfia macrosperma

Ludolfia macrosperma, commonly known as the large-seeded water plantain, is a aquatic plant native to Africa.

It typically grows in shallow waters and is characterized by its long, ribbon-like leaves that float on the water surface. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in summer, often arranged in clusters. Its roots are fibrous and help stabilize the sediment in aquatic environments.

Ludolfia macrosperma is valued for its ability to provide habitat for aquatic life and improve water quality.
