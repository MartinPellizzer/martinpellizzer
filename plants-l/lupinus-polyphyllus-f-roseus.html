Lupinus polyphyllus f. roseus

Lupinus polyphyllus f. roseus, commonly known as the rose-flowered lupine, is a striking perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and its showy, rose-pink to lavender-colored flower spikes. The plant features compound leaves with numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets, and its flowers are typically arranged in dense, terminal clusters. Lupinus polyphyllus f. roseus is well adapted to full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

It also plays an important role in ecological systems by fixing nitrogen in the soil.
