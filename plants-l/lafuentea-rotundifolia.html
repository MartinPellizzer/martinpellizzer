Lafuentea rotundifolia

Lafuentea rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leafed lafuentea, is a unique plant species native to specific regions in South America.

It is characterized by its rounded, succulent leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, adding a delicate aesthetic to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
