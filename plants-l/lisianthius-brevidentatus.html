Lisianthius brevidentatus

Lisianthius brevidentatus, commonly known as the short-toothed lisianthus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in horticultural gardens.
