Lindera officinalis

Lindera officinalis, commonly known as wild ginger, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features aromatic, heart-shaped leaves and produces yellowish-green flowers in the spring. The plant is characterized by its underground rhizomes, which are the primary source of its medicinal properties. Its essential oils contain compounds like asaricin and linderaline, which have been used in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
