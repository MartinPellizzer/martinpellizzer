Lychnis grandiflora var. sieboldii

Lychnis grandiflora var. sieboldii, commonly known as the Siebold's catchfly, is a herbaceous perennial native to East Asia.

It features showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
