Licania apetala var. pendula

Licania apetala var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping licania, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous inflorescences, which give it a distinctive weeping appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, hanging clusters. Its leaves are typically opposite and elliptical, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in shaded gardens and as a houseplant.
