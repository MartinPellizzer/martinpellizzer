Lepidium virginicum var. robinsonii

Lepidium virginicum var. robinsonii, commonly known as Robinson's peppergrass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often covered in fine hairs.

It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by the development of dry, three-angled seed pods.
