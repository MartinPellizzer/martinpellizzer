Leptocereus ekmanii

Leptocereus ekmanii, commonly known as the Ekman's night-blooming cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of central and northern Mexico.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its spiny stems and nocturnal flowering habit. The plant produces large, white flowers that open at night, attracting specific pollinators such as moths. Its adaptations include thick, fleshy stems for water storage and a shallow root system to thrive in harsh desert conditions.

Leptocereus ekmanii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
