Lasiopetalum Bracteatum

Lasiopetalum bracteatum is a small, flowering shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia. It is known for its delicate, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in spring and summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and a compact growth habit that makes it well suited for rock gardens and coastal landscapes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice among gardeners in arid climates. Despite its beauty, it is relatively uncommon in cultivation, which has led to increased interest in its care and propagation.
