Litsea laxiflora

Litsea laxiflora, commonly known as the broadleaf litsea, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and avocado trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate, and often aromatic leaves that are elliptical to ovate in shape. Its small, white flowers are typically clustered in umbels, and it produces small, round berries that are edible for some bird species.

Litsea laxiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
