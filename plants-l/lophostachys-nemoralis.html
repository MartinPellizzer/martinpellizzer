Lophostachys nemoralis

Lophostachys nemoralis, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers that range from white to pink in color. The plant produces small, reddish-brown fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in woodworking, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
