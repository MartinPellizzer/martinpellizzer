Lyperia canariensis

Lyperia canariensis, commonly known as the Canary Islands lily, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, often forming a rosette at the base.

Lyperia canariensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
