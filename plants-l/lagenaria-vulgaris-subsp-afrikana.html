Lagenaria vulgaris subsp. afrikana

Lagenaria vulgaris subsp.

afrikana, commonly known as the African bottle gourd, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family. It is a climbing vine that produces large, thick-walled fruits that can be used as containers.

The plant is native to Africa and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, while its flowers are yellow and typically unisexual.

The fruit's versatility in both culinary and practical uses makes it an important plant in many traditional cultures.
