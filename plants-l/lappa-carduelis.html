Lappa carduelis

Lappa carduelis, commonly known as the common lappa, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in late summer. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Lappa carduelis is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.
