Layia platyglossa

Layia platyglossa, commonly known as the narrowleaf golden aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, and its flowers bloom in shades of yellow to orange in the summer months.

Layia platyglossa is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to arid environments.
