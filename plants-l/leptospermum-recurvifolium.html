Leptospermum recurvifolium

Leptospermum recurvifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tea tree, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often drooping leaves that are typically narrow and recurved at the edges. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open woodlands.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract native pollinators.
