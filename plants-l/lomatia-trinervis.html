Lomatia trinervis

Lomatia trinervis, commonly known as the silky oaks, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately divided into narrow, silky-textured segments. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in spring. It is often found in woodland and forest areas, thriving in well-drained soils.

Lomatia trinervis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional Aboriginal medicine.
