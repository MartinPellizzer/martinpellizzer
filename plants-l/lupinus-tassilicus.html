Lupinus tassilicus

Lupinus tassilicus, commonly known as the Tassilicus lupin, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds.

It is adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots that help it survive in poor soil and low moisture conditions.
