Leontodon lucidus

Leontodon lucidus, commonly known as the shining dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring well-drained conditions. Its bright yellow flowers are followed by fluffy seed heads that disperse easily by wind.

The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
