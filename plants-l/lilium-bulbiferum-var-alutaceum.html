Lilium bulbiferum var. alutaceum

Lilium bulbiferum var. alutaceum, commonly known as the alutaceum lily, is a rare and ornamental variety of the lily family.

It is characterized by its striking, pale pink to white flowers with a distinctive, recurved shape. The plant produces bulb-like structures called bulbils, which are used for vegetative propagation. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, it thrives in cool, moist environments.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is highly valued in botanical gardens and by horticulturists.
