Lactuca gracilipetiolata

Lactuca gracilipetiolata, commonly known as slender-petioled lettuce, is a species of lettuce native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thin, elongated leaves that are typically less than 10 cm in length. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a compact inflorescence, which is typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are often used in salads and have a mild, slightly bitter taste.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary use in traditional dishes.
