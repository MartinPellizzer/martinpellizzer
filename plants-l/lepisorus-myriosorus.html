Lepisorus myriosorus

Lepisorus myriosorus, commonly known as the broad-leafed polypody, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy, evergreen foliage and compact growth habit. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest undergrowth or rocky crevices. Its fronds are bi-lobed and can reach lengths of up to 30 centimeters.

Lepisorus myriosorus is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
