Lepidium campestre var. glabratum

Lepidium campestre var. glabratum, commonly known as the glabrous peppergrass, is a low-growing annual plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other economically important plants such as cabbage and mustard. The plant typically has small, green, lobed leaves and produces tiny white or pale yellow flowers in clusters. Its fruit is a dry, segmented capsule that contains several small, rounded seeds.

This variety is characterized by its smooth, hairless surface, distinguishing it from other varieties of Lepidium campestre.
