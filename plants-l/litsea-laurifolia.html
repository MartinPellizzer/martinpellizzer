Litsea laurifolia

Litsea laurifolia, commonly known as the Chinese evergreen or Chinese laurel, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to southeastern China, Japan, and Korea, and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in umbels, followed by small, orange-red berries. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and pleasant scent.

Litsea laurifolia prefers moist, well-drained soils and is commonly found in shaded to semi-shaded environments.
