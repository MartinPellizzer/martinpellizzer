Leonotis

Leonotis, commonly known as lion's tail, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Lamiaceae.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its distinctive, showy, tubular flowers that resemble the tail of a lion. The plants typically grow as shrubs or subshrubs, with hairy stems and opposite leaves. Leonotis species are often cultivated for their ornamental value and are also used in traditional medicine.

They thrive in well-drained soil and full sun, making them popular in gardens and landscapes.
