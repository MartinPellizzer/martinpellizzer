Ledum groenlandicum subsp. glandulosum

Ledum groenlandicum subsp.

glandulosum, commonly known as the glandular labrador tea, is a perennial shrub native to arctic and subarctic regions of North America. It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves that emit a strong, often unpleasant odor.

The plant produces small, bell-shaped white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. Its evergreen foliage remains dense throughout the year, and it thrives in acidic, well-drained soils.

This species is adapted to cold climates and is often found in tundra and boreal forest ecosystems.
