Lauro-cerasus phaeosticta f. puberula

Lauro-cerasus phaeosticta f. puberula, commonly known as the hairy cherry laurel, is a cultivar of the Prunus genus.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage that is covered with fine, hairy pubescence. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by red, fleshy berries in the summer. It is a compact shrub that thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
