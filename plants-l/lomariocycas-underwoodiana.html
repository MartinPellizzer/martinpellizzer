Lomariocycas underwoodiana

Lomariocycas underwoodiana, commonly known as the Underwood cycad, is a rare and ancient plant species belonging to the Cycadaceae family.

It is native to the tropical rainforests of Queensland, Australia, and is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and pinnate, fern-like leaves. The plant produces large, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs, typical of cycads. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
