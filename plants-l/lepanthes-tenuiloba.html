Lepanthes tenuiloba

Lepanthes tenuiloba, commonly known as the narrow-leafed orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink. It is often found in cloud forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
