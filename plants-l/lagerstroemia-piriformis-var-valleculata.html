Lagerstroemia piriformis var. valleculata

Lagerstroemia piriformis var. valleculata, commonly known as the Mexican crape myrtle, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered inflorescences that range in color from white to pink and purple, appearing in late spring to early summer. The plant has small, ovate leaves that are typically gray-green in color and turn reddish in the fall. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its compact size and seasonal blooms make it a versatile addition to gardens and landscapes.
