Lasiagrostis

Lasiagrostis, commonly known as silvergrass, is a genus of grasses native to various parts of the world, including North America, Europe, and Asia.

These plants are characterized by their fine, silver-gray foliage, which gives them their common name. They typically grow in moist or wet environments such as marshes, swamps, and stream banks. Lasiagrostis species often form dense, low-growing clumps with narrow, grass-like leaves.

The flowers are usually small and clustered, forming spikelets that are often wind-pollinated.
