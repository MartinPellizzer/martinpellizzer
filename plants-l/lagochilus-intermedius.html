Lagochilus intermedius

Lagochilus intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate rock hyssop, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in temperate to cold climates. Its small, tubular flowers are usually purple or blue, arranged in spikes, and bloom in early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
