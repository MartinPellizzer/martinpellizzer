Lavandula pedunculata subsp. cariensis

Lavandula pedunculata subsp.

cariensis, commonly known as the Carian lavender, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces spikes of fragrant purple flowers.

The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is well adapted to dry, sunny conditions. It is often found in rocky soils and coastal areas.

Its essential oils are valued for their aromatic and medicinal properties.
