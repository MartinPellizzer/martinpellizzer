Lysimachia melampyroides var. melampyroides

Lysimachia melampyroides var. melampyroides, commonly known as yellow water pepper, is a low-growing perennial herb native to North America.

It typically features creeping stems that root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and forest floors.
