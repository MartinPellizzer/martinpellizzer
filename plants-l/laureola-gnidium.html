Laureola gnidium

Laureola gnidium, commonly known as the rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in dry and sunny environments. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pink or white flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, often covered in a fine layer of white hair.

Laureola gnidium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
