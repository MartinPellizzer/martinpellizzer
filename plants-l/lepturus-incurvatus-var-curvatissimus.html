Lepturus incurvatus var. curvatissimus

Lepturus incurvatus var. curvatissimus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of North America.

It typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are usually narrow and slightly curved, giving it its scientific name. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, which are characteristic of the Cyperaceae family.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to wet environments and its role in stabilizing soil in aquatic ecosystems.
