Leptospermum scoparium var. rotundifolium

Leptospermum scoparium var. rotundifolium, commonly known as the coastal tea tree, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its rounded, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and aromatic. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in coastal and sandy soils, often growing in open, sunny habitats.

Its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions makes it a resilient and ecologically important species.
