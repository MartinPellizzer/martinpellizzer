Lepidagathis lanceolata

Lepidagathis lanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed lily or mountain lily, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and striking white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its flowering period usually occurs during the warmer months, and it is often found in mountainous regions.

Lepidagathis lanceolata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
