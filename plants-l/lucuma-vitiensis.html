Lucuma vitiensis

Lucuma vitiensis, commonly known as the golden apple or lucuma, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its large, yellow-orange, sweet-tasting fruits. The plant produces clusters of berries that are typically harvested when fully ripe, which is usually in the dry season. The fruit's pulp is rich in nutrients, including vitamins and minerals, making it a valued crop in traditional and modern diets.

Its botanical features, such as its evergreen shrub form and aromatic leaves, contribute to its ecological and agricultural significance.
