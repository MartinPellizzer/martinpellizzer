Leandra fimbriata

Leandra fimbriata, commonly known as the velvetleaf orchid, is a tropical plant native to regions of South America.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in humid, warm environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
