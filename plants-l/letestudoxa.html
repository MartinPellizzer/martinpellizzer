Letestudoxa

Letestudoxa, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of small, golden-yellow flowers that appear in early summer. The tree has a rapid growth rate and can reach heights of up to 30 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated edge.

The bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.
