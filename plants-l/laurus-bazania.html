Laurus bazania

Laurus bazania, commonly known as the Persian laurel, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes well-known plants like laurel and avocado. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves that are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible but not commonly consumed.

Laurus bazania thrives in temperate climates and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
