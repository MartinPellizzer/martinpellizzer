Lomandra filiformis subsp. filiformis

Lomandra filiformis subsp.

filiformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lomandra, is a perennial herb native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color and have a fibrous texture.

The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers arranged in dense spikes during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas and dry heathlands.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in low-maintenance garden environments.
