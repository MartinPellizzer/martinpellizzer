Leontodon asper subsp. biscutellifolius

Leontodon asper subsp.

biscutellifolius, commonly known as the rough hawk's-beard, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It features a basal rosette of linear, hairy leaves that are often rough to the touch.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers arranged in a single head, which is surrounded by a series of bracts. Its flowering period typically occurs in late spring to early summer.

The subspecies is distinguished by its distinctive leaf morphology and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.
