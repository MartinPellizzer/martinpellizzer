Lysimachia taliensis var. breviloba

Lysimachia taliensis var. breviloba, commonly known as the Taliensis bugleweed, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
