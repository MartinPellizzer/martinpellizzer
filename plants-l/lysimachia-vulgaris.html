Lysimachia vulgaris

Lysimachia vulgaris, commonly known as yellow loosestrife, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread readily, forming dense mats.

Lysimachia vulgaris is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wet habitats.
