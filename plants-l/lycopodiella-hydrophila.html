Lycopodiella hydrophila

Lycopodiella hydrophila, commonly known as the water spike moss, is a small, terrestrial moss belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems covered in small, scale-like leaves. This species thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest floors or along streams. Its reproductive structures, known as sporophytes, develop from the base of the plant and produce spores for propagation.

Lycopodiella hydrophila plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for microorganisms in its native ecosystems.
