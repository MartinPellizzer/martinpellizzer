Ledonia heterophylla

Ledonia heterophylla, commonly known as the variable leaf ledonia, is a unique plant species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking variation in leaf morphology, which can range from lanceolate to ovate shapes depending on environmental conditions. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist habitats such as forest understories or rocky crevices. Its flowers are small and clustered, often displaying a range of colors from white to pale yellow.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological specificity, Ledonia heterophylla is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
