Linum angustifolium subvar. nanum

Linum angustifolium subvar. nanum, commonly known as the dwarf flax or narrow-leaved flax, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Australia.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 2 cm wide, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in spring, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological restoration and horticultural use.
