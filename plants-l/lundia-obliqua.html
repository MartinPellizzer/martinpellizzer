Lundia obliqua

Lundia obliqua, commonly known as the slender lily, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are lanceolate and arranged alternately along the stem, while its flowers bloom in late spring or early summer.

Lundia obliqua is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
