Lepanthes ibarrensis

Lepanthes ibarrensis, commonly known as the Ibarren's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is a terrestrial herb with a short, rhizomatous stem and a single, showy flower. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments within tropical forests. Its flowers are usually small and white or pale pink, with a distinctive lip that is often marked with purple or red streaks.

Lepanthes ibarrensis is part of the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological significance.
