Lithops umdausensis

Lithops umdausensis, commonly known as the Umdaus Lithops, is a species of stone plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, fleshy, and often colorful leaves that resemble stones, which help it blend into its arid environment. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the summer months, typically in the afternoon. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
