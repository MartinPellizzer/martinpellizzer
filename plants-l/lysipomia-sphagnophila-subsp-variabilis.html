Lysipomia sphagnophila subsp. variabilis

Lysipomia sphagnophila subsp.

variabilis, commonly known as the variable moss gentian, is a herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of South America. It is characterized by its small, showy blue or purple flowers that bloom during the summer months.

The plant thrives in moist, acidic soil and is often found in sphagnum moss-dominated habitats. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, and it has a compact growth habit.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and maintaining the biodiversity of its wetland environment.
