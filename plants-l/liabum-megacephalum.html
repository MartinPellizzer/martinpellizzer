Liabum megacephalum

Liabum megacephalum, commonly known as the giant-headed liabum, is a unique flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that resemble a crown, giving it its common name. The plant features elongated, slender stems that can grow up to several meters in height. Its leaves are typically narrow and arranged alternately along the stem.

While not widely cultivated, it is of interest to botanists due to its distinctive morphology and potential ecological role in its native habitat.
