Lobelia jamesoniana

Lobelia jamesoniana, commonly known as Jameson's lobelia, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Lobelia jamesoniana is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
