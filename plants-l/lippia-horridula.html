Lippia horridula

Lippia horridula, commonly known as rough lippia, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in open woodlands or along roadsides. It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is known for its aromatic properties.

Lippia horridula is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
