Lactuca tatarica var. heterophylla

Lactuca tatarica var. heterophylla, commonly known as Tatarian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and flowering heads that produce small, yellow disk flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in wetlands. Its leaves are usually alternate and vary in shape, which is reflected in its scientific name "heterophylla," meaning "different leaves.".

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common weed in agricultural and natural areas.
