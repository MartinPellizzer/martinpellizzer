Leptospermum aciculare

Leptospermum aciculare, commonly known as the silver bottlebrush, is a shrub native to Tasmania.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and clusters of bright red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and forested areas.

Leptospermum aciculare is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to native wildlife.
