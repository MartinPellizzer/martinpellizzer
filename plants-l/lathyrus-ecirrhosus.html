Lathyrus ecirrhosus

Lathyrus ecirrhosus, commonly known as the slender vetch, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep purple. Lathyrus ecirrhosus is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, where it thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Its seeds are used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.
