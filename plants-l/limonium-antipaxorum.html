Limonium antipaxorum

Limonium antipaxorum, commonly known as the Antipaxos sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the island of Antipaxos in Greece.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and well-drained soils. It blooms in late summer, adding vibrant color to its natural habitat.

Limonium antipaxorum is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and resilience.
