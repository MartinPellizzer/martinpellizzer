Lysimachia brevipes

Lysimachia brevipes, commonly known as the short-stemmed yellow loosestrife, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring to early summer. Its roots are fibrous and can form dense colonies, making it an effective ground cover in suitable habitats.

Lysimachia brevipes is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
