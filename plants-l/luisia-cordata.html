Luisia cordata

Luisia cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf luisia, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescences.

It is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida and the Caribbean. The plant features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped lobes, and its flowers are typically white or pale green, arranged in clusters. Luisia cordata thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical structure supports its role in local ecosystems, providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
