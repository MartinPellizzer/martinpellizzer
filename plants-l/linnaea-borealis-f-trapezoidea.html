Linnaea borealis f. trapezoidea

Linnaea borealis f. trapezoidea, commonly known as the narrow-leaf twinflower, is a rare botanical variant of the twinflower plant.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are distinctly narrower than those of the typical species. This form is native to northern regions of North America and Eurasia, thriving in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring.

Its unique morphology makes it a subject of interest in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
