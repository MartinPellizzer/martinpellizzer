Limonium brunneri

Limonium brunneri, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiky, blue to purple flower heads and silver-gray foliage. The plant thrives in coastal and saline environments, making it well adapted to coastal dunes and rocky shores. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Limonium brunneri is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
