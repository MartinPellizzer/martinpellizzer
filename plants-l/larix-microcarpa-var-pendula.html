Larix microcarpa var. pendula

Larix microcarpa var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping tamarack, is a variant of the tamarack tree characterized by its distinctive drooping branches.

It is a deciduous conifer native to the western United States, particularly in California and Oregon. The plant has needle-like leaves that turn golden in the fall before shedding, and its cones are small and woody. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones.

The weeping tamarack is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
