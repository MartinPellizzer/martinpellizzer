Lespedeza reticulata var. sessiliflora

Lespedeza reticulata var. sessiliflora, commonly known as the sessile-flowered lespedeza, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its small, clustered pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, opposite leaves and produces small, three-angled seed pods. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and disturbed areas.

Lespedeza reticulata var. sessiliflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
