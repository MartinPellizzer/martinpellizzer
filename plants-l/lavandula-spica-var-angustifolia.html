Lavandula spica var. angustifolia

Lavandula spica var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf lavender, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are gray-green in color. The plant produces spikes of fragrant, violet-blue flowers during the summer months. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its aromatic properties.

This variety is well-suited to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
