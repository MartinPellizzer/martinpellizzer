Lotus pedunculatus subsp. granadensis

Lotus pedunculatus subsp.

granadensis, commonly known as the Granada lotus, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and palmate leaves, which are typically trifoliate or pinnate.

The plant produces showy pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
