Lobivia walteri

Lobivia walteri, commonly known as the Walter's lobivia, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, spherical shape and spiny stems. The plant produces bright yellow flowers during the warmer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and moths. Lobivia walteri is well adapted to drought conditions, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Its slow growth rate and unique appearance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
