Lonchitis hirsuta var. ghiesbreghtii

Lonchitis hirsuta var. ghiesbreghtii, commonly known as the Ghiesbreght's hairy sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and hairy texture, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands or along the edges of water bodies. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often reddish or brownish in color.

This variant is noted for its distinct morphology and is often found in temperate regions with suitable wetland habitats.
