Lonicera utahensis

Lonicera utahensis, commonly known as Utah honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features slender, arching stems that often grow along the ground or climb low structures. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Utah honeysuckle is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
