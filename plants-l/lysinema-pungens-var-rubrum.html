Lysinema pungens var. rubrum

Lysinema pungens var. rubrum, commonly known as the red-stemmed tansy, is a variant of the tansy plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its reddish stems and small, yellowish-green leaves with a strong, aromatic scent. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, round seed heads. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and waste grounds.

Due to its toxic properties, it is considered a harmful plant to livestock and should be handled with care.
