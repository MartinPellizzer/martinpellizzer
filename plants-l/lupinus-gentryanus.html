Lupinus gentryanus

Lupinus gentryanus, commonly known as Gentry's lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, often in areas with well-drained soil. Its flowers are usually blue or purple, with a distinctive spurred petal that aids in pollination by bees and other insects.

Lupinus gentryanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
