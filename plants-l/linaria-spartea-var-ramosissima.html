Linaria spartea var. ramosissima

Linaria spartea var. ramosissima, commonly known as the branched toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain areas.
