Lindsaea trapeziformis var. falcata

Lindsaea trapeziformis var. falcata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf lindsaea, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, strap-like fronds that are typically narrow and elongated. The plant produces small, clustered sporangia on the underside of its leaves, which are responsible for reproduction through spores. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories where humidity is high.

Its unique morphology and ecological niche make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
