Launaea cervicornis

Launaea cervicornis, commonly known as the horned dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy foliage. The plant features a distinctive, spiny stem and a rosette of narrow, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water. Its flowers are yellow and produce small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Launaea cervicornis is adapted to survive in harsh environments, making it a resilient and hardy species.
