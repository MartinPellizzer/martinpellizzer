Lechea tenuifolia var. occidentalis

Lechea tenuifolia var. occidentalis, commonly known as the western threadleaf sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, thread-like leaves and yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into achene fruits.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
