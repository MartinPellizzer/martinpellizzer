Lobelia siphilitica var. alba

Lobelia siphilitica var. alba, commonly known as white cardinal flower, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its striking white to pale pink tubular flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet and has lance-shaped, hairy leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wet meadows.

The species is part of the Campanulaceae family and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance.
