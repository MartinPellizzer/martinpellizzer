Leucadendron reticulatum

Leucadendron reticulatum, commonly known as the King Protea, is a striking South African shrub belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy, spherical flower heads composed of numerous white to pinkish bracts. The plant typically grows in coastal fynbos vegetation, thriving in well-drained, acidic soils. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by birds, particularly the cape sugarbird.

Leucadendron reticulatum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique and elegant appearance.
