Lautembergia coriacea

Lautembergia coriacea, commonly known as the roughleaf sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, narrow leaves that are covered in a rough, textured surface. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Lautembergia coriacea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
