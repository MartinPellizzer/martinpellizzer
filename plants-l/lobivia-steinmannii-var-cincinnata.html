Lobivia steinmannii var. cincinnata

Lobivia steinmannii var. cincinnata, commonly known as the "Cinnamon Lobivia," is a compact, low-growing succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It features fleshy, ribbed stems that are typically green to bluish-green in color, with a tendency to develop a reddish hue when exposed to full sun. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of yellow to orange during the spring and early summer. Its leaves are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water, making it well-suited for dry environments.

It is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its hardiness and ornamental appeal.
