Lacinaria scariosa var. ramea

Lacinaria scariosa var. ramea, commonly known as the rough lacinaria, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat preferences, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
