Lepanthes laevis

Lepanthes laevis, commonly known as the smooth lepanthus, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers that typically feature white or pale pink petals and a distinctive, elongated lip. The plant grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical forests. Its leaves are usually broad and fleshy, adapted to store moisture in its natural habitat.

Lepanthes laevis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
