Lycopodium alopecuroides var. rigidum

Lycopodium alopecuroides var. rigidum, commonly known as the rigid clubmoss, is a perennial evergreen plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant features small, scale-like leaves that form a mat-like structure on the ground. It produces spore-bearing cones, which are typically clustered at the base of the plant.

This variety is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathland and open forest environments.
