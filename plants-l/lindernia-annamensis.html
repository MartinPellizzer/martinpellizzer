Lindernia annamensis

Lindernia annamensis, commonly known as the Annam lindernia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Linderniaceae and is characterized by its erect, slender stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Lindernia annamensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
