Libertia recta

Libertia recta, commonly known as the straight libertia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Amaryllidaceae.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its upright, narrow leaves and striking, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces white or pale pink flowers that are often fragrant, adding ornamental value to gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for rock gardens or containers.

Libertia recta is relatively low maintenance, requiring minimal water and pruning once established.
