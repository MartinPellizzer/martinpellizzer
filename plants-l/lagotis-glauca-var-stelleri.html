Lagotis glauca var. stelleri

Lagotis glauca var. stelleri, commonly known as the Steller's bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery-blue waxy coating.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
