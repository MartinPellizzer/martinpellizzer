Liatris mucronata var. interrupta

Liatris mucronata var. interrupta, commonly known as the narrowleaf blazingstar, is a flowering plant native to the central and southern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, spikelike inflorescence composed of numerous purple to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and grasslands. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a grayish or blue-green hue due to a dense covering of fine hairs.

It is a perennial herb that produces seeds in small, dry fruits called achenes.
