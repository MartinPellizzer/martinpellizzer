Ligustrum bodinieri

Ligustrum bodinieri, commonly known as Chinese privet, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features opposite, simple, and usually elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by dark purple berries. It is often used in landscaping due to its adaptability and fast growth rate.

However, it is considered invasive in some regions, posing a threat to native plant communities.
