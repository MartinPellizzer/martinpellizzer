Lysiana miniata

Lysiana miniata, commonly known as the dwarf lily of the Nile, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped blooms. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.

Lysiana miniata prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
