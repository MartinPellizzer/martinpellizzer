Lupinus polyphyllus f. albus

Lupinus polyphyllus f. albus, commonly known as the white lupine, is a variant of the lupine species native to western North America.

It is characterized by its white to pale yellow flowers and compound leaves with numerous small leaflets. This plant is typically found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. It has a taproot system and produces seed pods that contain several seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in nitrogen fixation in soil.
