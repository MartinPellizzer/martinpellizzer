Lindmania candelabriforma

Lindmania candelabriforma, commonly known as the candleplant, is a striking member of the Bromeliaceae family native to the rainforests of South America.

This plant is characterized by its tall, upright inflorescence that resembles a candle, hence its common name. Its leaves are long, narrow, and arranged in a rosette, forming a water-holding structure that supports epiphytic life. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom sequentially along the inflorescence, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Lindmania candelabriforma is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and air-purifying properties.
