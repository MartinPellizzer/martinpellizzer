Liatris novae-angliae f. trilisioides

Liatris novae-angliae f. trilisioides, commonly known as the trilisioides form of the New England blazing star, is a variant of the native North American flowering plant.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem and clusters of purple to pinkish-purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges. It produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

This form is notable for its distinctive foliage and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
