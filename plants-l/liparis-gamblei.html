Liparis gamblei

Liparis gamblei, commonly known as the Gambier orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the island of Borneo.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the soil.

The orchid's flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insects.
