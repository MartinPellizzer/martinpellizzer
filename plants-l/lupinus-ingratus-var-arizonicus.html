Lupinus ingratus var. arizonicus

Lupinus ingratus var. arizonicus, commonly known as Arizona lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.

Lupinus ingratus var. arizonicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for native pollinators.
