Lampranthus glaucus

Lampranthus glaucus, commonly known as the silver chalice, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, cylindrical stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces vibrant orange or yellow flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are typically small and covered in a waxy, bluish coating that helps reduce water loss.

Lampranthus glaucus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance.
