Lacaena

Lacaena, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and the presence of compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The flowers are typically small and clustered, often in racemes, and are usually yellow or greenish in color. The fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

Lacaena species are native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.
