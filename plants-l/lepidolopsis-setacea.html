Lepidolopsis setacea

Lepidolopsis setacea, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Lepidolopsis setacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
