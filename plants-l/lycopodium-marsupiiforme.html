Lycopodium marsupiiforme

Lycopodium marsupiiforme, commonly known as the marsh holly or ground holly, is a species of clubmoss belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. The plant features whorled leaves that are usually dark green and have a leathery texture. Its reproductive structures, known as strobila, are cone-like and contain spores, which are released for dispersal.

Lycopodium marsupiiforme plays an important ecological role in providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and small animals.
