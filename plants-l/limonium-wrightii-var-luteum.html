Limonium wrightii var. luteum

Limonium wrightii var. luteum, commonly known as the yellow sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of California.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hair.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in coastal gardens and xeriscaping.
