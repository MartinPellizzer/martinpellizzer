Lobelia spicata var. originalis

Lobelia spicata var. originalis, commonly known as the original lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, tubular blue to violet flowers. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
