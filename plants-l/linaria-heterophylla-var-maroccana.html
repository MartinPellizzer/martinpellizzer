Linaria heterophylla var. maroccana

Linaria heterophylla var. maroccana, commonly known as the Moroccan toadflax, is a perennial flowering plant native to North Africa.

It features slender, branching stems that often grow in a prostrate or ascending manner. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically linear to lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to well-drained soils in sunny conditions.
