Leptochloa contracta

Leptochloa contracta, commonly known as narrowleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, fields, and sandy soils. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves with a distinct, narrow shape and a dense growth habit. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of spikelets, which are small and tightly arranged.

Leptochloa contracta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardy nature and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
