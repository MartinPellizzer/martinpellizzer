Luzula campestris var. pallescens

Luzula campestris var. pallescens, commonly known as the pale sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically grayish-green in color. This variety is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows, wetlands, and rocky slopes. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer, which are arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

The plant is notable for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and its role in stabilizing soil in various ecological settings.
