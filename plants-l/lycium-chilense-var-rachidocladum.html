Lycium chilense var. rachidocladum

Lycium chilense var. rachidocladum, commonly known as the Chilean boxthorn, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Chile.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, red berries that are edible. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers during the spring. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it adaptable to harsh conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in local ecosystems and traditional medicine.
