Lobelia homophylla

Lobelia homophylla, commonly known as the purple lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple to blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Lobelia homophylla is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
