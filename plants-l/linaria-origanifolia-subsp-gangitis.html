Linaria origanifolia subsp. gangitis

Linaria origanifolia subsp.

gangitis, commonly known as the Gangiti stickseed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
