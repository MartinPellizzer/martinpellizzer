Lavandula canariensis

Lavandula canariensis, commonly known as the Canary island lavender, is a species native to the Canary Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring aromatic, gray-green leaves and spikes of purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its fragrant herbs and ornamental plants. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for Mediterranean climates.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardens and for the production of essential oils.
