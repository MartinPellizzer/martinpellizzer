Leptochloa polystachya

Leptochloa polystachya, commonly known as the slender grass or hill grass, is a perennial grass species native to various regions of Asia, Africa, and Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands, wetlands, and along riverbanks. It has a fibrous root system and is adapted to a range of environmental conditions.

Leptochloa polystachya is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a ground cover.
