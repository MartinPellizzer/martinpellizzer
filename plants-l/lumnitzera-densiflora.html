Lumnitzera densiflora

Lumnitzera densiflora, commonly known as the mangrove buttonwood, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It thrives in brackish and saline water environments, often found in mangrove ecosystems. The plant has small, opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in spikes, while the fruit is a capsule containing numerous seeds.

Lumnitzera densiflora plays a vital role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial species.
