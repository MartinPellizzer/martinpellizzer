Leucadendron

Leucadendron, commonly known as the protea, is a genus of flowering plants native to South Africa.

These plants are characterized by their striking, often brightly colored bract-like leaves that surround the true flowers. Leucadendrons are members of the Proteaceae family and are known for their diverse forms and vibrant hues. They typically thrive in well-drained soils and require full sun to partial shade.

Many species are cultivated for their ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
