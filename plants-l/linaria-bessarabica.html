Linaria bessarabica

Linaria bessarabica, commonly known as the Bessarabian toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers are tubular with two lips, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Linaria bessarabica is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its vibrant color and delicate appearance.
