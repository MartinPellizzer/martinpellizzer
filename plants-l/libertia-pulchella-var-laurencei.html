Libertia pulchella var. laurencei

Libertia pulchella var. laurencei, commonly known as the Lawrence's flax lily, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces striking, showy flowers in shades of purple and pink, arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
