Lasallea oblongifolia

Lasallea oblongifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf lasallea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. Lasallea oblongifolia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and native plant gardening.
