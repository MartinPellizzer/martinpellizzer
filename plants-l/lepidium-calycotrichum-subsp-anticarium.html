Lepidium calycotrichum subsp. anticarium

Lepidium calycotrichum subsp.

anticarium, commonly known as the Anticaria cress, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America. It is a small annual herb with erect, branched stems and small, clustered leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, which are followed by small, hairy fruits called siliques. It thrives in high-altitude environments and is adapted to cool, moist conditions.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological niche.
