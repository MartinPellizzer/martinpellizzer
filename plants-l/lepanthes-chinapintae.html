Lepanthes chinapintae

Lepanthes chinapintae, commonly known as the Chinese nightshade orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that typically bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique beauty and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
