Liparis lacerata

Liparis lacerata, commonly known as the broken lip orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, pale green or white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its flowers are often fragrant and attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Liparis lacerata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
