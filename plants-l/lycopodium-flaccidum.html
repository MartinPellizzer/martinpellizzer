Lycopodium flaccidum

Lycopodium flaccidum, commonly known as the flaccid clubmoss, is a species of lycophyte that belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its small, creeping stems that produce spore-bearing cones called strobili. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its leaves are linear and arranged in whorls around the stem, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Lycopodium flaccidum plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
