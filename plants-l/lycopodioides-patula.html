Lycopodioides patula

Lycopodioides patula, commonly known as the slender clubmoss, is a species of lycophyte that belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its small, needle-like leaves and a creeping rhizome that produces sporophylls. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. It reproduces via spores rather than seeds, a trait typical of its ancient lineage.

Its simple structure and primitive morphology reflect its evolutionary origins from early vascular plants.
