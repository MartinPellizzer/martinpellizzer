Leontodon radiatus

Leontodon radiatus, commonly known as the yellow puccoon, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stem is upright and branching.

Leontodon radiatus is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally by Indigenous peoples for various purposes.
