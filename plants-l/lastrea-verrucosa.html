Lastrea verrucosa

Lastrea verrucosa, commonly known as the rough lastrea, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and clusters of small, greenish flowers. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its spiny, warty tubercles that provide protection against herbivores. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy leaves.

Due to its unique morphology and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
