Ludwigia decurrens

Ludwigia decurrens, commonly known as the water spinach or river hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often reddish in color, especially when grown in low-light conditions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of stems. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil and is often used in aquatic gardens or as a decorative plant in ponds.

Ludwigia decurrens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and can be grown both in water and as a terrestrial plant.
