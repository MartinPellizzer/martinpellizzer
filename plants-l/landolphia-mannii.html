Landolphia mannii

Landolphia mannii, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night, emitting a strong, sweet fragrance. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces long, fleshy fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is notable for its nocturnal blooming habit.
