Lilium bulbiferum var. croceum

Lilium bulbiferum var. croceum, commonly known as the yellow bulbous lily, is a striking perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a distinctive red or orange throat. The plant produces bulbous structures that serve as storage organs and can propagate new plants. It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with narrow, grass-like leaves.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
