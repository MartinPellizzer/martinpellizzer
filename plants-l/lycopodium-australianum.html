Lycopodium australianum

Lycopodium australianum, commonly known as the Australian clubmoss, is a small evergreen plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae and is characterized by its spore-bearing strobila, which resembles a cone. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and scale-like, arranged in whorls around the stem.

Lycopodium australianum plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for small invertebrates.
