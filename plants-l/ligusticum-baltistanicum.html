Ligusticum baltistanicum

Ligusticum baltistanicum, commonly known as the Baltistan lovage, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of northern Pakistan and parts of Afghanistan.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and umbel-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving in cool and moist conditions. Its leaves are broad and pinnately divided, with a strong, spicy aroma.

The flowers are small and white or greenish, appearing in late summer, contributing to its ecological role in mountainous regions.
