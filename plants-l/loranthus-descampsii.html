Loranthus descampsii

Loranthus descampsii, commonly known as the African mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on a variety of host trees, primarily in the savannas of sub-Saharan Africa.

It belongs to the Loranthaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers that are often white or yellowish in color. The plant derives some of its nutrients from its host through specialized root structures called haustoria, while it performs photosynthesis using its leaves.

It plays an ecological role in supporting biodiversity by providing food and shelter for various insects and birds.
