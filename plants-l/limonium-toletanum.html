Limonium toletanum

Limonium toletanum, commonly known as the Toletan sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Plumbagaceae family and is characterized by its striking, spike-like inflorescences that bear blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and saline soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a fine layer of salt, which helps in reducing water loss.

Limonium toletanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
