Linum nervosum var. aucheri

Linum nervosum var. aucheri, commonly known as the Alpes flax or also called the Alpine flax, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous small, brown seeds. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Its fibrous stems and leaves have been historically used in traditional textile production and herbal medicine.
