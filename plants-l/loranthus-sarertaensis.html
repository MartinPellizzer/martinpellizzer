Loranthus sarertaensis

Loranthus sarertaensis, commonly known as the Saraterra mistletoe, is a parasitic plant belonging to the Loranthaceae family.

It primarily attaches itself to the branches of host trees, such as those in the genus Ficus, through haustoria that penetrate the host's vascular system. The plant produces small, clustered greenish-yellow flowers that are often inconspicuous and lack showy petals. Its fruits are fleshy berries that contain seeds covered in a sticky substance to aid in seed dispersal by birds.

Due to its specialized parasitic lifestyle, it plays a unique role in the ecological dynamics of its native habitat.
