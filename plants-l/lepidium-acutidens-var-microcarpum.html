Lepidium acutidens var. microcarpum

Lepidium acutidens var. microcarpum, commonly known as the dwarf cress, is a small annual herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as stream banks and meadows. The plant has slender, branched stems and small, alternate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in loose racemes, and it produces tiny, capsule-like fruits.

The species is notable for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed environments.
