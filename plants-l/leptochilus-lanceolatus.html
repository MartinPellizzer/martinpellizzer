Leptochilus lanceolatus

Leptochilus lanceolatus, commonly known as the lanceolate leek, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its strap-like, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or borders.
