Lepanthes purpurata

Lepanthes purpurata, commonly known as the purple orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central and South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments such as cloud forests. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, violet to purple flowers with intricate patterns. Its root system is specialized for absorbing nutrients from the forest floor, and it often forms symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi.

Lepanthes purpurata is prized for its ornamental value and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts worldwide.
