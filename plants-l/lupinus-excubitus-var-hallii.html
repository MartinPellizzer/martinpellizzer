Lupinus excubitus var. hallii

Lupinus excubitus var. hallii, commonly known as the Hall's lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces cylindrical seed pods that contain several seeds.

This species is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a nectar source for pollinators.
