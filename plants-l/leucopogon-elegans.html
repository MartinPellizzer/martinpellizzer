Leucopogon elegans

Leucopogon elegans, commonly known as the slender leucopogon, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in sandy or rocky habitats.

Leucopogon elegans is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to coastal environments.
