Lobivia haageana f. cinnabarina

Lobivia haageana f. cinnabarina, commonly known as the cinnabarina lobivia, is a rare and striking succulent native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, cylindrical stem and clusters of fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves that often display a vibrant red or orange hue. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is a sought-after species among cactus and succulent enthusiasts.
