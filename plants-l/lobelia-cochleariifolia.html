Lobelia cochleariifolia

Lobelia cochleariifolia, commonly known as the spiral lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking spiral arrangement of leaves, which gives it a unique and ornamental appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a suitable choice for woodland gardens.

Its foliage and floral structure are adapted to its native habitat, showcasing the plant's evolutionary traits.
