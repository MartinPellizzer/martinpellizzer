Lycopodioides parkeri

Lycopodioides parkeri, commonly known as the African clubmoss, is a member of the Lycopodiaceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces cone-like structures called strobili, which contain the reproductive organs. Its leaves are linear and arranged in whorls around the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Lycopodioides parkeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
