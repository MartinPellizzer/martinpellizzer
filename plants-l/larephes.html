Larephes

Larephes, commonly known as the silverleaf shrub, is a perennial plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It features silvery-gray leaves that are covered in fine, fuzzy hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access groundwater in dry soils.

Larephes is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
