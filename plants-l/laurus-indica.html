Laurus indica

Laurus indica, commonly known as the bay laurel, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves that are aromatic and often used in cooking. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by black, berry-like fruits. Its leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its distinctive scent and medicinal properties.

Laurus indica is widely cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
