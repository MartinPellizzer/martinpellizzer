Lippia argyrophylla

Lippia argyrophylla, commonly known as silverleaf lippia, is a shrub native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant is often found in dry, sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is known for its aromatic leaves.

Lippia argyrophylla is sometimes used in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance.
