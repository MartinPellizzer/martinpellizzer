Linaria caelesyriaca

Linaria caelesyriaca, commonly known as the Syrian toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Linaria caelesyriaca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its bright, delicate blooms.
