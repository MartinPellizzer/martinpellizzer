Lobelia coriacea

Lobelia coriacea, commonly known as the rough lobelia, is a herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features hairy, rough-textured leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant thrives in moist, woodland environments and is often found in shaded or partially shaded areas. It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy floral displays.

Lobelia coriacea is valued in native plant gardens for its ecological benefits and ornamental appeal.
