Linum alpinum var. pycnophyllum

Linum alpinum var. pycnophyllum, commonly known as the alpine flax, is a perennial flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and produces small, blue or violet flowers. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is adapted to thrive in cold, alpine environments. It is often found in rocky soils and high-altitude meadows.

Linum alpinum var. pycnophyllum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
