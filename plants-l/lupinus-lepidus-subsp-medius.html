Lupinus lepidus subsp. medius

Lupinus lepidus subsp.

medius, commonly known as the coastal lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers.

The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal dunes and grasslands. Lupinus lepidus subsp.

medius plays a vital role in nitrogen fixation, enriching the soil for other plant species.
