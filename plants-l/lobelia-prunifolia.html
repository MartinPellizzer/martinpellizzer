Lobelia prunifolia

Lobelia prunifolia, commonly known as the cherry-leaf lobelia, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple, giving it a striking appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Lobelia prunifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like butterflies and bees.
