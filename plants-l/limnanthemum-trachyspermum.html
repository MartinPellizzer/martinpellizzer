Limnanthemum trachyspermum

Limnanthemum trachyspermum, commonly known as the wild marigold, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its yellow-orange flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, while the stems are erect and branching.
