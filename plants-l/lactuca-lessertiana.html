Lactuca lessertiana

Lactuca lessertiana, commonly known as the lesser lettuce, is a flowering plant species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a cluster, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits.

Lactuca lessertiana is often found in meadows and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.
