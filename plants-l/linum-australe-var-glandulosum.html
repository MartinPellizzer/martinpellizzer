Linum australe var. glandulosum

Linum australe var. glandulosum, commonly known as the Australian flax or glandular flax, is a herbaceous perennial native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in glandular hairs. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer to autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally by Indigenous communities for various purposes.
