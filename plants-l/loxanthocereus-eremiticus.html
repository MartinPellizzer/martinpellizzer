Loxanthocereus eremiticus

Loxanthocereus eremiticus, commonly known as the night-blooming cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to Mexico.

It is a small, spiny cactus that typically grows in rocky or arid environments. The plant is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers that open at night. Its flowers are fragrant and attract nocturnal pollinators such as bats and moths.

The species is adapted to survive in extreme desert conditions with minimal water requirements.
