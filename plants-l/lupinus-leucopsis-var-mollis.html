Lupinus leucopsis var. mollis

Lupinus leucopsis var. mollis, commonly known as the soft white lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy foliage and clusters of white to pale pink flowers. The plant has compound leaves with oval to lance-shaped leaflets and produces slender, erect pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
