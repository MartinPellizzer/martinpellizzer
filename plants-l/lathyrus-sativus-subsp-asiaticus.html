Lathyrus sativus subsp. asiaticus

Lathyrus sativus subsp.

asiaticus, commonly known as the Asiatic vetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, followed by elongated pods containing lens-shaped seeds.

It is often cultivated for its edible seeds, which are used in traditional cuisines, and it has also been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
