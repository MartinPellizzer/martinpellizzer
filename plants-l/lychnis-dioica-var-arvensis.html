Lychnis dioica var. arvensis

Lychnis dioica var. arvensis, commonly known as the field sandwort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the carnation family (Caryophyllaceae) and typically grows in dry, disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It has a low-growing habit with hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.
