Lonicera alpigena var. watanabeana

Lonicera alpigena var. watanabeana, commonly known as the Watanabe's honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features slender, arching stems that often grow in a climbing or trailing habit. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a glossy surface with serrated edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its showy flowers and adaptability to various soil conditions.
