Lathyrus philippii

Lathyrus philippii, commonly known as the Philippi's vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of southern Europe.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

Lathyrus philippii is often found in rocky or disturbed soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
