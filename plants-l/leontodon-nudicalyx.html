Leontodon nudicalyx

Leontodon nudicalyx, commonly known as the nakedleaf hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its leaves are usually basal, with a rough, hairy texture, and the flower heads are yellow with prominent central disk flowers.

It is a perennial species that produces achenes as its seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
