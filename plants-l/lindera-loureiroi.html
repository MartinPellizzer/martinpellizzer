Lindera loureiroi

Lindera loureiroi, commonly known as the Chinese wild laurier, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, simple leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by elliptical, dark blue berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Lindera loureiroi is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in Chinese medicine.
