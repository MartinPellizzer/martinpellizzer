Lychnis alba

Lychnis alba, commonly known as white campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stem.

Lychnis alba is often found in disturbed habitats and can spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
