Lolium perenne var. aristatum

Lolium perenne var. aristatum, commonly known as the tufted ryegrass, is a variant of perennial ryegrass characterized by its distinctive tufted or rosette-like growth habit.

This grass is native to Europe and is often found in temperate regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its foliage is typically narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration that becomes more pronounced during the growing season. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It is valued for its resilience and is used in both agricultural and horticultural settings.
