Lupinus luteus f. ucrainicus

Lupinus luteus f. ucrainicus, commonly known as the Ukrainian yellow lupine, is a variant of the yellow lupine native to Eastern Europe.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves with numerous small leaflets. The flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in dense spikes, and bloom in late spring to early summer. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or disturbed habitats.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in ecological restoration projects.
