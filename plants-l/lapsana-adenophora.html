Lapsana adenophora

Lapsana adenophora, commonly known as the common cudweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, hairy leaves that are typically opposite in arrangement. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in composite flower heads, which are typical of the Asteraceae family. It is native to Europe and western Asia but has been introduced to other regions, including North America.

Lapsana adenophora is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is considered a weed in some areas.
