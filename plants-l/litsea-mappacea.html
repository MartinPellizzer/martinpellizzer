Litsea mappacea

Litsea mappacea, commonly known as the Chinese tallowtree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurel and avocado trees. The plant is characterized by its opposite, simple, ovate leaves that are often glossy and emit a pleasant fragrance. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are followed by orange to red berries that contain several seeds.

Litsea mappacea is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
