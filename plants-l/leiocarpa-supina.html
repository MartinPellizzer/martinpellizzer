Leiocarpa supina

Leiocarpa supina, commonly known as the groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches a height of about 10-20 centimeters. The plant features small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in spring and summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

Leiocarpa supina is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
