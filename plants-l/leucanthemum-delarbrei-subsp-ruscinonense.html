Leucanthemum delarbrei subsp. ruscinonense

Leucanthemum delarbrei subsp.

ruscinonense, commonly known as the alpine chamomile, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe. It is a perennial herb characterized by its compact growth habit and daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of gray-green, hairy leaves that are usually basal and rosette-forming.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to flourish in challenging alpine environments.
