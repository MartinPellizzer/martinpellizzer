Lotus corniculatus f. vulgaris

Lotus corniculatus f. vulgaris, commonly known as the common birdsfoot trefoil, is a flowering plant native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed areas. It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it valuable in soil enrichment.

The species is also used in forage and as a ornamental plant in gardens.
