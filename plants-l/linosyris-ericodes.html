Linosyris ericodes

Linosyris ericodes, commonly known as the heath lichen, is a foliose lichen that thrives in acidic, nutrient-poor environments.

It is composed of a symbiotic association between a green alga and a fungal partner, which allows it to survive in harsh, exposed habitats. The plant typically grows on rocks, tree bark, or soil in heathlands and pine forests. Its flattened, leaf-like thallus is often covered in a dense layer of fine, hair-like structures.

Linosyris ericodes plays an important role in soil stabilization and serves as a habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
