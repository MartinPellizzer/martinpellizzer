Limonium hypanicum

Limonium hypanicum, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking, spiky blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in coastal environments, often growing on sandy or gravelly soils. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

Limonium hypanicum is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
