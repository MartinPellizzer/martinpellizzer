Lupinus venustus

Lupinus venustus, commonly known as the beautiful lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and showy, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the legume family. Lupinus venustus thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open meadows and rocky areas.

Its ecological importance includes nitrogen fixation, which enriches the soil for other plants.
