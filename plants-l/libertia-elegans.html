Libertia elegans

Libertia elegans, commonly known as the elegant libertia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family, which includes many ornamental plants such as irises and gladioli. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable in its native habitat.
