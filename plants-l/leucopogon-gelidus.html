Leucopogon gelidus

Leucopogon gelidus, commonly known as the Antarctic snowbell, is a flowering plant native to the Southern Hemisphere, particularly found in New Zealand and South America.

It is characterized by its small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in alpine or subantarctic environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

Leucopogon gelidus plays a significant role in its ecosystem, providing nectar for pollinators and contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitats.
