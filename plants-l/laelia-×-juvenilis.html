Laelia × juvenilis

Laelia × juvenilis, commonly known as the juvenile laelia, is a hybrid orchid native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that typically come in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in high-altitude environments. It thrives in cool, bright conditions with moderate humidity and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
