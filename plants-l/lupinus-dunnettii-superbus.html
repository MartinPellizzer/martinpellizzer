Lupinus dunnettii superbus

Lupinus dunnettii superbus, commonly known as the Dunnet Island lupine, is a species of flowering plant native to the British Isles.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of pea-like flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant produces spiny pods that contain several seeds, which are adapted for dispersal by wind and animals. It thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils and is often found in coastal grasslands and heathlands.

Lupinus dunnettii superbus is an important component of the local flora, contributing to biodiversity and ecological stability in its habitat.
