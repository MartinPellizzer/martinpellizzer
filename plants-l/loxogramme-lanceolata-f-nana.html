Loxogramme lanceolata f. nana

Loxogramme lanceolata f. nana, commonly known as the dwarf water clover, is a compact, aquatic fern characterized by its small, finely divided leaves.

It thrives in shallow, still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in marshes and ponds. The plant's rhizomes form dense mats, which help stabilize sediment and provide habitat for aquatic organisms. Its leaves are typically bright green, with a delicate, lace-like appearance that enhances its ornamental value.

This fern is often used in aquatic gardening for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
