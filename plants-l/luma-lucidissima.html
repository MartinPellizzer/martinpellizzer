Luma lucidissima

Luma lucidissima, commonly known as the silver mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its striking silver-white foliage, which reflects light and gives it a luminous appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the rainy season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental value.
