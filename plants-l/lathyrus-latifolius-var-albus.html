Lathyrus latifolius var. albus

Lathyrus latifolius var. albus, commonly known as the white broad-leafed pea, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical pods containing small, round seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in meadows and along roadsides.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
