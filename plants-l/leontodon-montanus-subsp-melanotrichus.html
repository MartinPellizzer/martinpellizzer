Leontodon montanus subsp. melanotrichus

Leontodon montanus subsp.

melanotrichus, commonly known as the black-haired mountain bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flower heads surrounded by pale green to brown bracts.

The subspecies is distinguished by its dark, hairy stems and leaves, which contribute to its common name. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, often in alpine or subalpine environments.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
