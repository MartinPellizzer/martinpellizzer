Lasiosiphon kraussianus var. angustifolius

Lasiosiphon kraussianus var. angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lasiosiphon, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers with five petals, typical of the Boraginaceae family. Its foliage is often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance.

This species thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
