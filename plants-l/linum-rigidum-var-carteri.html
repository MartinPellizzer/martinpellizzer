Linum rigidum var. carteri

Linum rigidum var. carteri, commonly known as Carter's flax, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Linaceae and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
