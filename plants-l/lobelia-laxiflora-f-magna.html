Lobelia laxiflora f. magna

Lobelia laxiflora f. magna, commonly known as the giant lobelia, is a striking perennial plant native to the high altitudes of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of blue to purple tubular flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky areas. Its large, showy flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, white bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance.
