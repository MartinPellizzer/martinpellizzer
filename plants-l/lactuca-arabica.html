Lactuca arabica

Lactuca arabica, commonly known as wild lettuce, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its distinctive blue or purple flowers. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy and serrated, and it produces small, green fruits that contain seeds. Lactuca arabica is known for its milky sap, which contains lactucine, a compound with mild sedative properties.

It is often found in meadows and along roadsides, thriving in temperate climates.
