Leptorkis delicatula

Leptorkis delicatula, commonly known as the delicate orchid, is a species of orchid native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elegant stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest undergrowth, where it can access the necessary humidity and indirect light. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, often covered in a fine, waxy coating that helps reduce water loss.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts of rare orchids.
