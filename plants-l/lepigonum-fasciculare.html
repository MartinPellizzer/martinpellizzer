Lepigonum fasciculare

Lepigonum fasciculare, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has upright, branching stems covered in soft, hairy foliage. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in clusters along the stems.

Lepigonum fasciculare thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.
