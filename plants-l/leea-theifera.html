Leea theifera

Leea theifera, commonly known as the silverleaf creeper, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It belongs to the family Leeaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that often have a silvery underside. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, which are typically followed by small, round fruits. Leea theifera is often cultivated as a ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various soil types.

It is also used in traditional medicine in some regions for its purported health benefits.
