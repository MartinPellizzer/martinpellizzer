Leandra debilis var. grandifolia

Leandra debilis var. grandifolia, commonly known as the giant leandra, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a woody shrub or small tree that typically grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that are often glossy and dark green. Its flowers are showy, with bright yellow petals and a distinctive red or orange center, attracting various pollinators.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
