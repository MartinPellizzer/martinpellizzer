Loranthus maidenii

Loranthus maidenii, commonly known as the Australian mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of various host trees, primarily eucalyptus and acacia species.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using the light it captures. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces white or pale green flowers that are often clustered in umbels. Its fruit is a fleshy, red berry that contains seeds covered in a sticky substance to aid in attachment to the host.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing food for birds and other animals.
