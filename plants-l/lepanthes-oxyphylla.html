Lepanthes oxyphylla

Lepanthes oxyphylla, commonly known as the sharp-lobed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in the summer months. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually elliptical and covered in a fine layer of white pubescence.

Lepanthes oxyphylla is often found in humid, shaded environments within tropical forests.
