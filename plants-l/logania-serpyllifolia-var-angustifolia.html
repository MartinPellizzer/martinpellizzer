Logania serpyllifolia var. angustifolia

Logania serpyllifolia var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved logania, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Loganiaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
