Lychnis conica

Lychnis conica, commonly known as the upright fireweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features upright, branching stems that are often reddish in color, and it produces showy, pink to red flowers in clusters at the tips of the stems. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Lychnis conica is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
