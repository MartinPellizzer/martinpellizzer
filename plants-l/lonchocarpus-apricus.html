Lonchocarpus apricus

Lonchocarpus apricus, commonly known as the yellow spurred bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers with spurred petals. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and agricultural studies.
