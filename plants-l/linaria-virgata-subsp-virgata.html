Linaria virgata subsp. virgata

Linaria virgata subsp.

virgata, commonly known as the slender toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and pairs of opposite, linear leaves.

The plant produces bright yellow or orange flowers that are typically tubular in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats. Linaria virgata subsp.

virgata is a perennial species that is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
