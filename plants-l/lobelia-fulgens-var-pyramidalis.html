Lobelia fulgens var. pyramidalis

Lobelia fulgens var. pyramidalis, commonly known as the pyramidal cardinal flower, is a striking perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features tall, upright stems that bear clusters of bright red, tubular flowers, which are highly attractive to pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet, with lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for wetland gardens.

Its vibrant color and ecological benefits make it a valuable addition to native plant landscapes.
