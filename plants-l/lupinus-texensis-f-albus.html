Lupinus texensis f. albus

Lupinus texensis f. albus, commonly known as the white plains lupine, is a flowering plant native to the central and southern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. The plant features upright stems with clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and its role in ecological restoration due to its nitrogen-fixing capabilities.
