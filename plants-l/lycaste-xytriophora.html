Lycaste xytriophora

Lycaste xytriophora, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a hybrid of several species within the Schlumbergera genus.

It is native to the tropical rainforests of Brazil, where it grows as an epiphytic plant on trees. The plant is characterized by its segmented, fleshy stems that bear vibrant flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Lycaste xytriophora is popular as a houseplant due to its long blooming period and ease of care.
