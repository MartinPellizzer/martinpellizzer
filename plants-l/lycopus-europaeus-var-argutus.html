Lycopus europaeus var. argutus

Lycopus europaeus var. argutus, commonly known as narrow-leaved water-hobble, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It typically grows in moist habitats such as stream banks, marshes, and wet meadows. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinct serrated edge. Its small, blue to purple flowers are clustered in spikes and bloom in late summer.

It is characterized by its aromatic foliage and is often used in traditional herbal medicine.
