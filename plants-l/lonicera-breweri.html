Lonicera breweri

Lonicera breweri, commonly known as the Brewer's honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, elliptic leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly rough texture. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are yellowish-orange in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that ripens in the fall, attracting various wildlife species.

Lonicera breweri is often found in moist, open habitats such as woods and streambanks.
