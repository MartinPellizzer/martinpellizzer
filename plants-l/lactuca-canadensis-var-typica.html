Lactuca canadensis var. typica

Lactuca canadensis var. typica, commonly known as Canadian lettuce, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features a rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and have a mild, slightly bitter taste. The plant produces yellow flowers in a composite head, which are followed by small, oval-shaped seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This species is also known for its latex, which can cause skin irritation upon contact.
