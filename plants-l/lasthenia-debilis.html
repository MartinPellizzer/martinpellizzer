Lasthenia debilis

Lasthenia debilis, commonly known as the brittlestem everlasting, is a delicate annual plant native to California.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellowish to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal and inland habitats.
