Lactuca tysonii

Lactuca tysonii, commonly known as the Tyson's lettuce, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, followed by small, spiky seed heads. It is native to the western United States and thrives in dry, rocky habitats.

Lactuca tysonii is often considered a weed but has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
