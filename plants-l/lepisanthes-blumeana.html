Lepisanthes blumeana

Lepisanthes blumeana, commonly known as the Blume's orchid, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that come in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and elliptical, often with a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Lepisanthes blumeana is prized for its ornamental value and is cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts worldwide.
