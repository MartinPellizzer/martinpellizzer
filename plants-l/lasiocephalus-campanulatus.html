Lasiocephalus campanulatus

Lasiocephalus campanulatus, commonly known as the bell-shaped heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it requires well-drained soil and minimal water.
