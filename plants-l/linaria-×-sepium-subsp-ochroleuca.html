Linaria × sepium subsp. ochroleuca

Linaria × sepium subsp.

ochroleuca, commonly known as the yellow toadflax, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family. It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers.

The leaves are typically linear and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green hue. Native to southern Europe, it thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for garden use.
