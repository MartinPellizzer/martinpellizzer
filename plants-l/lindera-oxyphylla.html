Lindera oxyphylla

Lindera oxyphylla, commonly known as the fragrant bladdernut, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurel trees and other aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in catkins, while the fruit is a follicle that splits open to release winged seeds.

Lindera oxyphylla thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and streamside habitats.
