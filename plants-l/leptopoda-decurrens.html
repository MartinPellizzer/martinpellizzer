Leptopoda decurrens

Leptopoda decurrens, commonly known as the creeping bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features a low-growing, spreading habit with slender stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are bell-shaped, ranging in color from white to pale blue, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Leptopoda decurrens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
