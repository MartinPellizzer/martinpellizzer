Linum alexeenkoanum

Linum alexeenkoanum, commonly known as the Alexandrine flax, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the genus Linum, which includes various species of flax known for their showy blue or purple flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and agricultural settings.

Its ornamental value and potential for seed production make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
