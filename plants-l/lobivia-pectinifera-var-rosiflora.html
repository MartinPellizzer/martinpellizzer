Lobivia pectinifera var. rosiflora

Lobivia pectinifera var. rosiflora, commonly known as the Rosy Flora Lobivia, is a compact, low-growing succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It features fleshy, ribbed stems that are typically green to bluish-green in color, with a tendency to develop a reddish hue when exposed to full sun. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom in late spring or early summer. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

This variety is prized by collectors for its unique coloration and resilience to drought.
