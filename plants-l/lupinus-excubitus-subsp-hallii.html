Lupinus excubitus subsp. hallii

Lupinus excubitus subsp.

hallii, commonly known as the desert lupine, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces spires of blue to purple flowers. It is adapted to survive in harsh conditions with minimal water availability. Lupinus excubitus subsp.

hallii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators in its native habitat.
