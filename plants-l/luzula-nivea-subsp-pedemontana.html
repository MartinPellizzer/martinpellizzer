Luzula nivea subsp. pedemontana

Luzula nivea subsp.

pedemontana, commonly known as the alpine woodrush, is a perennial herb native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist, rocky or alpine habitats, often forming dense cushions or mats.

The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are usually white or pale blue in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, greenish-white flowers in spike-like clusters during the summer months.

This subspecies is adapted to cold climates and is often found in high-altitude environments.
