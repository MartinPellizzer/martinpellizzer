Leucas candicans

Leucas candicans, commonly known as white leucas, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in whorls along the stem, giving it a delicate appearance.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
