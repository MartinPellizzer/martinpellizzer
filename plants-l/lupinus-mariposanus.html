Lupinus mariposanus

Lupinus mariposanus, commonly known as the Mariposa larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. Lupinus mariposanus is adapted to grow in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and meadows.
