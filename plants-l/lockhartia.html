Lockhartia

Lockhartia, commonly known as the orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It consists of about 15 species, primarily native to Southeast Asia, including countries like Thailand, Indonesia, and Malaysia. These plants are epiphytic, meaning they grow on the surfaces of trees and other structures, absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. Lockhartia orchids are prized for their fragrant, showy flowers, which often bloom in clusters and come in a range of colors, including white, pink, and purple.

They require bright, indirect light and high humidity to thrive, making them popular among tropical plant enthusiasts.
