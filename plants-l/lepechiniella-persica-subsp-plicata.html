Lepechiniella persica subsp. plicata

Lepechiniella persica subsp.

plicata, commonly known as Persian carpet grass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia. It features dense, mat-forming foliage with narrow, slightly hairy leaves that are often curled or folded.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and can thrive in full sun.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in harsh environments.
