Lactuca dentata

Lactuca dentata, commonly known as the toothed lettuce, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family, Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it typically grows in moist, grassy areas. The plant has basal leaves that are broadly lanceolate with toothed margins, and it produces yellow flowers in a composite head. It is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain regions.

The species is known for its milky sap, which is characteristic of the genus Lactuca.
