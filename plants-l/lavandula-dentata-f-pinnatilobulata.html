Lavandula dentata f. pinnatilobulata

Lavandula dentata f. pinnatilobulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lavender, is a cultivar of the lavender plant characterized by its distinctive, narrow, and lobed leaves.

It produces fragrant, spike-like flower clusters that range in color from purple to pink, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.

Its foliage and flowers are both utilized in herbal remedies and essential oil production.
