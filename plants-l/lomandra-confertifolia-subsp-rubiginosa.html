Lomandra confertifolia subsp. rubiginosa

Lomandra confertifolia subsp.

rubiginosa, commonly known as the red-flowered lomandra, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southeastern Australia. It features dense, grass-like foliage with narrow, evergreen leaves that are typically bright green to reddish in color.

The plant produces striking, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, red or pink flowers, which are particularly noticeable in spring. It is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and heathlands.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
