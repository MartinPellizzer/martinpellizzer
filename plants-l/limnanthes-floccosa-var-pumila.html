Limnanthes floccosa var. pumila

Limnanthes floccosa var. pumila, commonly known as the dwarf swamp milkweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features soft, fuzzy, gray-green leaves and produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wet meadows and marshy areas. It is a valuable nectar source for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its unique appearance and ecological importance make it a notable species in wetland ecosystems.
