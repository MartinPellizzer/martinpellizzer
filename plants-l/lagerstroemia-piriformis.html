Lagerstroemia piriformis

Lagerstroemia piriformis, commonly known as the Crape Myrtle, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that bloom in summer, often in shades of pink, red, or white. The plant has smooth, mottled bark that becomes more pronounced with age, and its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Lagerstroemia piriformis is also known for its ability to withstand drought and urban conditions, enhancing its adaptability in various landscapes.
