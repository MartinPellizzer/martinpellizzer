Lepidium fletcheri

Lepidium fletcheri, commonly known as Fletcher's cress, is a small annual herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats and has small, oval leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, rounded pods that contain several seeds.

Botanically, it is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and its ability to thrive in arid or semi-arid environments.
