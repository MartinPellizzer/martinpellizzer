Liparis rhombea

Liparis rhombea, commonly known as the rhombic orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to various regions of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It is characterized by its rhombic-shaped pseudobulbs and linear, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer or early autumn. Liparis rhombea is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountain slopes.

It is valued in horticulture for its unique appearance and adaptability to different growing conditions.
