Litsea sericea var. trichocarpa

Litsea sericea var. trichocarpa, commonly known as the velvetleaf litsea, is a flowering plant species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, silvery-green leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often followed by elliptical, dark blue berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
