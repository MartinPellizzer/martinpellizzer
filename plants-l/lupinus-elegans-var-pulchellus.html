Lupinus elegans var. pulchellus

Lupinus elegans var. pulchellus, commonly known as the elegant lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking blue to violet pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces seed pods that split open to release its seeds.

Lupinus elegans var. pulchellus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in nitrogen fixation in soil.
