Lupinus bilineatus

Lupinus bilineatus, commonly known as the two-lined lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of blue or purple pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces legume pods that contain several seeds. Lupinus bilineatus is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
