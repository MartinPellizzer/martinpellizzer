Luzula jimboi subsp. atrotepala

Luzula jimboi subsp.

atrotepala, commonly known as the dark-pink sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia. It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses and sedges, and is characterized by its cylindrical, grass-like stems.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wet meadows. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets.

The subspecies is distinguished by its dark pink to purple floral bracts, which give it its common name.
