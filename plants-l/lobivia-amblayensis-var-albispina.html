Lobivia amblayensis var. albispina

Lobivia amblayensis var. albispina, commonly known as the White-spined Lobivia, is a compact, spineless cactus native to the arid regions of Argentina.

It features a short, cylindrical stem with a distinctive white, woolly coating that helps reflect sunlight and reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring, adding a delicate touch to its otherwise rugged appearance. Its dense, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts and collectors.
