Lepidium foliosum

Lepidium foliosum, commonly known as peppergrass, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like mustard and cabbage. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is characterized by its small, pinnately divided leaves. It produces small white or yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, and it is often found in prairies and open fields.

Lepidium foliosum is also known for its edible seeds, which have a peppery flavor similar to that of mustard.
