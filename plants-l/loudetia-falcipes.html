Loudetia falcipes

Loudetia falcipes, commonly known as the sickleleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of a single, spike-like cluster of small, pale green to brownish flowers.

Loudetia falcipes is often used in ornamental gardening for its graceful appearance and drought tolerance.
