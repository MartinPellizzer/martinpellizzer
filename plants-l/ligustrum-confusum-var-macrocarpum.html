Ligustrum confusum var. macrocarpum

Ligustrum confusum var. macrocarpum, commonly known as the large-fruited privet, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It features opposite, simple, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters, followed by elliptical berries that turn from green to dark purple as they mature. It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.

The variety is distinguished by its notably larger fruits compared to other ligustrum species.
