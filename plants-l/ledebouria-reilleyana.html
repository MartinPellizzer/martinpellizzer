Ledebouria reilleyana

Ledebouria reilleyana, commonly known as the Reilley's bulbous lily, is a striking bulbous plant native to the arid regions of Namibia and Angola.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to its drought-prone environment. The plant produces clusters of striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the warmer months. Its bulbs are perennial, allowing it to survive harsh conditions by entering dormancy.

Ledebouria reilleyana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and resilience.
