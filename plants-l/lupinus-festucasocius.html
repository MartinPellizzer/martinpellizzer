Lupinus festucasocius

Lupinus festucasocius, commonly known as the coastal lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western coast of North America.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal dunes and rocky habitats.

Lupinus festucasocius plays an important ecological role by fixing nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plant life.
