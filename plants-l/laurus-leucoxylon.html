Laurus leucoxylon

Laurus leucoxylon, commonly known as the white-wood laurel, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other well-known plants like bay laurel and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional herbal medicine. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, and it produces black, oval berries that are edible but not commonly consumed.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
