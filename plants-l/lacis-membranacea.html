Lacis membranacea

Lacis membranacea, commonly known as the membranous lacis, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, membranous leaves that give it its scientific name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
