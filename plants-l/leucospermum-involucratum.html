Leucospermum involucratum

Leucospermum involucratum, commonly known as the "star flower" or "spear flower," is a South African native shrub belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, cone-shaped inflorescences that consist of numerous small, white to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal and mountainous regions, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, needle-like leaves that are usually evergreen.

Leucospermum involucratum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
