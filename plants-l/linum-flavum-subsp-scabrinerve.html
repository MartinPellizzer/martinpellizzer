Linum flavum subsp. scabrinerve

Linum flavum subsp.

scabrinerve, commonly known as the scabrous-nerved flax, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a rough, scabrous texture.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically clustered in loose racemes. It is often found in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in textile production, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
