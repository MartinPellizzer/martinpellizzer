Linum suffruticosum var. virgatum

Linum suffruticosum var. virgatum, commonly known as the Virgin's bower or sweet william, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its climbing habit and showy, clustered flowers. The flowers are typically blue or purple, with five petals and a prominent central stamen, which adds to its ornamental appeal. The plant produces small, spherical fruits called follicles that contain several seeds.

It thrives in partial shade to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
