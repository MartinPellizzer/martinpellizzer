Lycopodium selago f. alpinum

Lycopodium selago f. alpinum, commonly known as the alpine clubmoss, is a fern-like plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in rocky, alpine environments. The plant has whorled, scale-like leaves that form dense, cushion-like structures. It reproduces via spores, which are produced in small, stalked clusters called strobila.

This subspecies is adapted to cold, high-altitude climates and is often found in mountainous regions across the Northern Hemisphere.
