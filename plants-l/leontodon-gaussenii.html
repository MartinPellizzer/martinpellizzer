Leontodon gaussenii

Leontodon gaussenii, commonly known as the Gaussen's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, linear leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in the summer months. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, it thrives in alpine and subalpine habitats. The plant has a fibrous root system and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of high-altitude ecosystems.
