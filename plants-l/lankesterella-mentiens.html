Lankesterella mentiens

Lankesterella mentiens, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and elusive epiphytic plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its lack of leaves, relying instead on its roots for photosynthesis. The plant produces small, pale green or white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is highly sensitive to environmental changes and is listed as endangered.

Its unique appearance and ecological significance make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
