Leontopodium melanolepis

Leontopodium melanolepis, commonly known as the black-leaved edelweiss, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly white flowers surrounded by a cluster of dark brown bracts. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soil. Its unique appearance makes it a symbol of resilience and beauty in harsh mountain climates.

Due to its limited distribution and conservation status, it is often protected in its natural habitats.
