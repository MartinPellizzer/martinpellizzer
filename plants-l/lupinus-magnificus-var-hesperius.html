Lupinus magnificus var. hesperius

Lupinus magnificus var. hesperius, commonly known as the Desert Lupine, is a perennial legume native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It features erect, branching stems covered in soft, hairy foliage and produces clusters of vibrant blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets, and its root system is well adapted to nutrient-poor soils. It plays an important role in nitrogen fixation, enriching the soil for other plants.

This species is also valued for its ecological significance in supporting pollinators and stabilizing sandy soils.
