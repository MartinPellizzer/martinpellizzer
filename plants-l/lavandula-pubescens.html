Lavandula pubescens

Lavandula pubescens, commonly known as woolly lavender, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its gray-green, fuzzy leaves and spikes of purple or pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is well adapted to dry, sunny conditions and thrives in well-drained soils. Its aromatic foliage and flowers make it a popular choice for gardens and herbal use.

Lavandula pubescens is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
