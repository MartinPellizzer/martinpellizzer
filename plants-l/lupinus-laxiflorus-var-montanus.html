Lupinus laxiflorus var. montanus

Lupinus laxiflorus var. montanus, commonly known as the mountain lupine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

This species is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a food source for wildlife.
