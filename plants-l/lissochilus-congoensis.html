Lissochilus congoensis

Lissochilus congoensis, commonly known as the Congo orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous plant that typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest understories. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the acidic, nutrient-poor soils of its habitat.

Lissochilus congoensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral characteristics.
