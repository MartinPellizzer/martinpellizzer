Lunaria diffusa

Lunaria diffusa, commonly known as the moonflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant is characterized by its delicate, bi-lobed seed pods that turn a striking blue-gray color when mature. Its flowers are small, white, and bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators.

Lunaria diffusa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique seed pods.
