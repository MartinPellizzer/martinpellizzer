Lycopus laurentianus

Lycopus laurentianus, commonly known as Laurentian bugleweed, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers in clusters, which bloom from June to August. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Lycopus laurentianus is part of the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.
