Leucothoe stenophylla

Leucothoe stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved leucothoe, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit. The plant features small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath, with a leathery texture. It produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers in the spring, followed by small, bright red berries in the fall.

Leucothoe stenophylla thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist woodland habitats.
