Lagochilus pubescens

Lagochilus pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent lips, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or pink in color. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
