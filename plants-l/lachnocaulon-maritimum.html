Lachnocaulon maritimum

Lachnocaulon maritimum, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flower heads, which are surrounded by white, woolly bracts. Native to coastal areas of Europe and North Africa, this plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils. It typically grows in maritime climates, often found along sea cliffs and dunes.

The plant is well adapted to salt spray and strong winds, making it a resilient species in harsh coastal environments.
