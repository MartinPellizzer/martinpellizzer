Leptaspis urceolata

Leptaspis urceolata, commonly known as the cup-shaped fern, is a small, delicate fern species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, cup-like fronds that are often clustered at the base of the plant. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually dark green and have a leathery texture, with a distinctive, compact growth habit.

Leptaspis urceolata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
