Laurus geniculata

Laurus geniculata, commonly known as the sweet bay tree, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves that are often used in cooking and traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark purple berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.
