Lonicera sachalinensis var. barbinervis

Lonicera sachalinensis var. barbinervis, commonly known as the Sachalin honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated margins and a distinctive reddish-brown bark. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, which bloom in early spring. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that ripens in late summer, attracting various bird species.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in gardens and landscapes.
