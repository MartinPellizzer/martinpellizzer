Languas ptychanthera

Languas ptychanthera, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a unique and rare plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, translucent, waxy flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions, hence its common name. The plant has a short, succulent stem with few leaves, adapted to store water in its harsh environment. Its flowers open at night, attracting specific nocturnal pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Languas ptychanthera is considered vulnerable and is of great interest to botanists and conservationists.
