Lepidium reticulatum var. austroamericanum

Lepidium reticulatum var. austroamericanum, commonly known as the Andean cress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family and is native to South America.

It is a low-growing annual herb with small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its seeds are small and contain compounds that have been studied for potential medicinal properties.
