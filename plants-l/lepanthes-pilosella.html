Lepanthes pilosella

Lepanthes pilosella, commonly known as the silky orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, hairy petals and a compact, sympodial growth habit. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually small and fragrant, with a distinctive coloration that varies by subspecies.

Lepanthes pilosella is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
