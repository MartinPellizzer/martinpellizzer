Limonium aureum var. maduoense

Limonium aureum var. maduoense, commonly known as the golden lantana, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and profuse display of bright yellow or golden flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer to early autumn, producing clusters of small, showy flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun exposure, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

The species is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
