Lotus cytisoides subsp. collinus

Lotus cytisoides subsp.

collinus, commonly known as the coastal lotus, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean. It is characterized by its delicate, pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has slender, branching stems and small, trifoliate leaves. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dunes or coastal scrub habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain areas.
