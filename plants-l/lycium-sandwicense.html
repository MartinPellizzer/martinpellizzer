Lycium sandwicense

Lycium sandwicense, commonly known as the sandwicense boxthorn, is a shrub native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, orange-red berries that are edible when ripe. The plant thrives in coastal and dry forest environments, often growing on rocky or sandy soils. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and clustered in small inflorescences.

Lycium sandwicense plays an important ecological role in providing habitat and food for native Hawaiian wildlife.
