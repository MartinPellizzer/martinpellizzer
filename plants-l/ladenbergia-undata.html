Ladenbergia undata

Ladenbergia undata, commonly known as the yellow flame tree, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is notable for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a popular ornamental species. Its seeds are enclosed in flat, bean-like pods and can remain viable for several years.

The tree is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
