Linum perenne var. saxosum

Linum perenne var. saxosum, commonly known as the saxifrage flax, is a hardy perennial plant native to rocky and alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and small, blue or purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in crevices and on rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its capsules produce numerous small seeds.

This variety is valued for its resilience and ornamental appeal in rock gardens and alpine settings.
