Lasianthus malaccensis

Lasianthus malaccensis, commonly known as the Malacca lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to purple. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
