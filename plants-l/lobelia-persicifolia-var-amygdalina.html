Lobelia persicifolia var. amygdalina

Lobelia persicifolia var. amygdalina, commonly known as the almond-leaf lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, almond-scented leaves and clusters of blue to violet tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a compact growth habit and is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators like butterflies and bees.
