Lemurangis floribunda

Lemurangis floribunda, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pendulous, bell-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant produces clusters of fragrant blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth, glossy surface.

Lemurangis floribunda is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant flowers and cascading growth habit.
