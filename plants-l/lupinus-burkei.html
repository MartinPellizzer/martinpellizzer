Lupinus burkei

Lupinus burkei, commonly known as the desert lupine, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and is adapted to arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.

Lupinus burkei plays an important ecological role by fixing nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plant life.
