Lobelia velutina

Lobelia velutina, commonly known as velvetleaf lobelia, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a velvety texture. Its showy blue to violet flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Lobelia velutina thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
