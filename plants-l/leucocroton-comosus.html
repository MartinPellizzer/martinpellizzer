Leucocroton comosus

Leucocroton comosus, commonly known as the white croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are often green with a waxy, white underside. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, spiny capsules containing seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its unique foliage and floral display.
