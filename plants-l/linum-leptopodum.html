Linum leptopodum

Linum leptopodum, commonly known as the slender flax, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, adapting well to arid conditions. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs.

Linum leptopodum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
