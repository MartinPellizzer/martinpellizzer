Lachnaea burchellii

Lachnaea burchellii, commonly known as the Burchell's lichen, is a species of lichen that belongs to the family Lichenes.

It is characterized by its crust-like growth form and is typically found on rock surfaces in arid or semi-arid environments. The thallus of Lachnaea burchellii is usually pale yellow to orange in color, and it lacks distinct lobes or fissures. This lichen is often used as an indicator species for environmental conditions due to its sensitivity to air pollution.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms.
