Lepanthes bitriangularis

Lepanthes bitriangularis, commonly known as the Triangular Orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive triangular-shaped petals and sepals, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within the understory of tropical forests. Its flowers are often small and delicate, with a variety of colors including white, yellow, and pink.

Lepanthes bitriangularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
