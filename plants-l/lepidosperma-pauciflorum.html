Lepidosperma pauciflorum

Lepidosperma pauciflorum, commonly known as the slender sedges, is a member of the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flowers. Native to Australia, it typically grows in wetland or swampy environments. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and establish in moist soils.

Its flowers are usually pale green to brown, and it produces small, three-lobed fruits.
