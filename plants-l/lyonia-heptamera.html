Lyonia heptamera

Lyonia heptamera, commonly known as the seven-flowered lyonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters of seven, giving it its common name. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and covered in fine, soft hairs.

Lyonia heptamera typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats.
