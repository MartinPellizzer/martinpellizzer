Lobivia acanthoplegma f. patula

Lobivia acanthoplegma f. patula, commonly known as the Patula Lobivia, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rounded growth habit and spiny, ribbed stems. The plant features small, paddle-shaped leaves that are often covered in a whitish, powdery coating, known as farina, which helps reduce water loss. It produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer.

Due to its slow growth and unique appearance, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts and collectors.
