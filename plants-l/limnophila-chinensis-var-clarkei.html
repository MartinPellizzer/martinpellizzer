Limnophila chinensis var. clarkei

Limnophila chinensis var. clarkei, commonly known as the water starwort, is a aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater habitats such as ponds and slow-moving streams. The plant has slender, branching stems that are often submerged, with small, bright green leaves arranged in whorls. It produces tiny, floating flowers that are usually white or pale yellow.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in aquatic ecosystems.
