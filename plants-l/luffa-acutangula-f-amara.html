Luffa acutangula f. amara

Luffa acutangula f. amara, commonly known as bitter luffa, is a variant of the common luffa plant characterized by its hairy, spiny stems and serrated leaves.

It produces pale green, lobed leaves and large, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The fruit is a fibrous, sponge-like structure that matures to a light brown color. This plant thrives in warm, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun.

It is often cultivated for its edible young fruits and its fibrous mature fruit, which is used in various culinary and practical applications.
