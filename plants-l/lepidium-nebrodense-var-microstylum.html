Lepidium nebrodense var. microstylum

Lepidium nebrodense var. microstylum, commonly known as the Sicilian cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in early spring. The plant has a low-growing habit with lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is native to the Mediterranean region.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its natural habitat.
