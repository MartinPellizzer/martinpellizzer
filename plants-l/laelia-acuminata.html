Laelia acuminata

Laelia acuminata, commonly known as the acute-angled laelia, is a species of orchid native to the high-altitude regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, often fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, yellow, and pink. The plant typically features long, narrow leaves and produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. It thrives in cool, humid environments with bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Laelia acuminata is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts due to its resilience and ornamental value.
