Lespedeza eriocarpa f. leiocarpa

Lespedeza eriocarpa f. leiocarpa, commonly known as silky lespedeza, is a variant of the lespedeza genus native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in woodland areas and along stream banks. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are usually pink to reddish in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its characteristic silky texture.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
