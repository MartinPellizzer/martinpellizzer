Lilium andinum

Lilium andinum, commonly known as the Andean lily, is a species native to the high altitudes of the Andes Mountains.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically blooms in the summer months and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces a unique, fragrant scent.

Lilium andinum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in temperate climates.
