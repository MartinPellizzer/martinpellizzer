Lobularia canariensis subsp. marginata

Lobularia canariensis subsp.

marginata, commonly known as Canary Island lobularia, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its small, white or pale yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It has linear, grass-like leaves that are often marginally hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
