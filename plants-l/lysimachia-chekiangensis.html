Lysimachia chekiangensis

Lysimachia chekiangensis, commonly known as the Chinese yellow loosestrife, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia, particularly China.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. The plant features slender stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in dense inflorescences, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Lysimachia chekiangensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
