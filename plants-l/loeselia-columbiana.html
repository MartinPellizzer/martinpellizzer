Loeselia columbiana

Loeselia columbiana, commonly known as the Columbia loeselia, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. The plant features small, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring, often in shades of white and pale pink. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its unique appearance.

Loeselia columbiana plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators and serving as habitat for various small organisms.
