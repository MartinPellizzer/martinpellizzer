Larix olgensis var. koreana

Larix olgensis var. koreana, commonly known as the Korean larch, is a deciduous conifer native to the Korean Peninsula.

It is characterized by its needle-like leaves that turn golden yellow in autumn before falling off. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, typical of the Pinaceae family. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to adapt to various environmental conditions.
