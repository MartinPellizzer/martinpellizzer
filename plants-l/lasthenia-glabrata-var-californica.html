Lasthenia glabrata var. californica

Lasthenia glabrata var. californica, commonly known as the California sticky geranium, is a low-growing annual plant native to California.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from February to April. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are often covered in fine, sticky hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is commonly found in coastal and inland grasslands.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
