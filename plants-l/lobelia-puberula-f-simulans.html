Lobelia puberula f. simulans

Lobelia puberula f. simulans, commonly known as the silverleaf lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features small, tubular blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, silver-green foliage that gives it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
