Lippia polystachya

Lippia polystachya, commonly known as Mexican mint or wild mint, is a perennial herb native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its aromatic, creeping stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.

Lippia polystachya is valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
