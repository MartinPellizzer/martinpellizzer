Lychnis coronata var. sieboldii

Lychnis coronata var. sieboldii, commonly known as the Siebold's fireweed, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and showy, pink to red bracts that resemble petals. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers in clusters at the base of the bracts. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
