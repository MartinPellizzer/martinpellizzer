Lupinus munzianus

Lupinus munzianus, commonly known as the Munz's lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of blue or violet pea-shaped flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Lupinus munzianus plays an important ecological role by fixing nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plant life.
