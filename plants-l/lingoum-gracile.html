Lingoum gracile

Lingoum gracile, commonly known as the slender lily, is a herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features slender, strap-like leaves and produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white or pale pink. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soil with partial to full sun exposure. Its botanical classification places it within the family Liliaceae, which includes other familiar plants like tulips and onions.

Lingoum gracile is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively low maintenance in garden settings.
