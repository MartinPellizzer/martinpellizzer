Lobelia bryophila

Lobelia bryophila, commonly known as the mossy lobelia, is a small herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features delicate, hairy stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly curled, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Lobelia bryophila is often found growing in association with mosses, which is reflected in its common name.
