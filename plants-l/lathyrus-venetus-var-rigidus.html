Lathyrus venetus var. rigidus

Lathyrus venetus var. rigidus, commonly known as the rigid climbing pea, is a perennial legume native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems that climb or trail along the ground. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and it has a fibrous root system.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
