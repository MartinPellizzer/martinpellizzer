Lamium amplexicaule var. album

Lamium amplexicaule var. album, commonly known as white dead-nettle, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that clasp the stems, giving it its species name *amplexicaule*. The plant produces clusters of showy, white, bilaterally symmetrical flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and garden borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological habitats.
