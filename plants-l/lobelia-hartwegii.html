Lobelia hartwegii

Lobelia hartwegii, commonly known as Hartweg's lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a hairy texture.

Lobelia hartwegii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
