Litsea citriodora

Litsea citriodora, commonly known as the lemon grass or citronella tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves that emit a strong lemon scent when crushed. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers followed by oval, orange-yellow fruits containing aromatic seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its essential oils used in perfumes and traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both horticulture and aromatic industries.
