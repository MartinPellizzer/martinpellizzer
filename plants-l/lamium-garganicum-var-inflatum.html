Lamium garganicum var. inflatum

Lamium garganicum var. inflatum, commonly known as the inflated deadnettle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive, inflated, purple to reddish stems that resemble small, inflated tubes. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
