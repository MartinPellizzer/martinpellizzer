Limnochloa obsoleta

Limnochloa obsoleta, commonly known as the slender watergrass, is a perennial aquatic plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It thrives in shallow, still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in marshes and ponds. The plant has narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are typically submerged, with small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge above the water surface. Its rhizomes form dense mats, helping to stabilize sediment and improve water quality.

Limnochloa obsoleta plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various invertebrates and fish.
