Ledebouria pusilla

Ledebouria pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf bulbous hyacinth, is a small, bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a compact rosette of narrow, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces clusters of delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple during the spring. It is often grown as a rock garden plant due to its low maintenance and ornamental appeal.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited for xerophytic environments and container gardening.
