Limonium britannicum var. celticum

Limonium britannicum var. celticum, commonly known as the Scottish sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the British Isles.

It is characterized by its striking, spiky blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, where it thrives in sandy or gravelly soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in a fine white coating.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand salty sea air.
