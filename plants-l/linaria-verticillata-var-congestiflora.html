Linaria verticillata var. congestiflora

Linaria verticillata var. congestiflora, commonly known as the clustered toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers. The leaves are narrow and opposite, often forming a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
