Linum squamulosum

Linum squamulosum, commonly known as the squamulous flax, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb with slender, branched stems that often grow close to the ground. The plant produces small, delicate blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are covered in fine, scale-like hairs, giving it its common name.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
