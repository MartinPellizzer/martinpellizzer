Lysimachia vittiformis

Lysimachia vittiformis, commonly known as the yellow-eyed grass, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It features slender, upright stems that bear small, bright yellow flowers clustered in dense inflorescences. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its delicate appearance. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Lysimachia vittiformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
