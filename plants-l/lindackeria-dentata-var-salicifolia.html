Lindackeria dentata var. salicifolia

Lindackeria dentata var. salicifolia, commonly known as the willow-leaved lindackeria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that resemble those of willow trees, hence its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically serrated along the edges, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Lindackeria dentata var. salicifolia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
