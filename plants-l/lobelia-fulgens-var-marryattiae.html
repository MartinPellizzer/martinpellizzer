Lobelia fulgens var. marryattiae

Lobelia fulgens var. marryattiae, commonly known as the Maryatt's flameflower, is a strikingly colorful plant native to the wetlands of Florida.

It is characterized by its vibrant red to orange tubular flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in a fine, velvety texture.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to attract pollinators like hummingbirds and butterflies.
