Lepidium rotundum var. phlebopetalum

Lepidium rotundum var. phlebopetalum, commonly known as the narrowleaf peppergrass, is a flowering plant native to western North America.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, white or yellow flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and has a low, spreading growth habit. Its leaves are narrow and pinnately divided, and it produces numerous small seeds that are dispersed by wind.
