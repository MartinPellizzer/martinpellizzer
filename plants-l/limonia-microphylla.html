Limonia microphylla

Limonia microphylla, commonly known as the sour orange, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or orange in color, with a sour taste. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of citrus compounds.

Limonia microphylla is relatively drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils.
