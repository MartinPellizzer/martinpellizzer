Lapathum tingitanum

Lapathum tingitanum, commonly known as the Canary Island betony, is a perennial herb native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, two-lipped flowers in shades of purple and blue. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
