Leonohebe densifolia

Leonohebe densifolia, commonly known as the dense-leaved rock hebe, is a perennial shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tubular flowers, typically in shades of blue or purple, during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Leonohebe densifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
