Linaria quadrifolia

Linaria quadrifolia, commonly known as the four-leafed toadflax, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Linaria quadrifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
