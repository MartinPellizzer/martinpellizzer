Lobivia wrightiana

Lobivia wrightiana, commonly known as the Wright's lobivia, is a succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, cylindrical stem that often branches at the base. The plant features spiny areoles with clusters of white or yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, and it stores water in its thick, fleshy stem.

Lobivia wrightiana thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
