Limonium delicatulum subsp. formenterae

Limonium delicatulum subsp.

formenterae, commonly known as the Formentera sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly found on the island of Formentera. It is a low-growing perennial that produces showy, blue to purple flowers with a distinctive central disc.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is adapted to coastal and sandy environments. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy species suitable for coastal gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to bloom throughout the summer months.
