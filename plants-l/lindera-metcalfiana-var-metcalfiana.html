Lindera metcalfiana var. metcalfiana

Lindera metcalfiana var. metcalfiana, commonly known as the Metcalf's spicebush, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees, and is characterized by its aromatic, opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by dark blue berries that are an important food source for birds. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of native plant ecosystems.
