Lesquerella occidentalis subsp. cinerascens

Lesquerella occidentalis subsp.

cinerascens, commonly known as the western lesquerella, is a member of the Brassicaceae family. It is native to the western United States, particularly in regions with rocky soils and arid climates.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers and has linear, hairless leaves. It is characterized by its distinctive seed capsule that splits open to release its seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its potential in industrial applications due to its oil-rich seeds.
