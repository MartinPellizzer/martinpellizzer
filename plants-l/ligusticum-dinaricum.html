Ligusticum dinaricum

Ligusticum dinaricum, commonly known as Dinaric lovage, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and umbellate flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as forests and riverbanks. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and have a strong, spicy scent.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
