Lonicera sublabiata

Lonicera sublabiata, commonly known as the Chinese honeysuckle, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, fragrant, yellowish-white flowers in the spring. The plant is characterized by its tubular corolla and is often found in moist, shaded environments. Its fruits are red berries that ripen in the fall and are edible for birds and some mammals.

Lonicera sublabiata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in gardens and natural habitats.
