Licania sclerophylla var. myristicoides

Licania sclerophylla var. myristicoides, commonly known as the myristicoides variant of the sclerophyllous licania, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to several meters. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in axillary inflorescences, and it produces fruit that is capsule-like in structure.

This variant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its resilience in challenging climates.
