Laelia fulva

Laelia fulva, commonly known as the orange orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky soils. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its flowers are showy, with bright orange petals and a distinctive yellow lip.

Laelia fulva is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses due to its specific environmental requirements.
