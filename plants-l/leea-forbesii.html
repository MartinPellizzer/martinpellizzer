Leea forbesii

Leea forbesii, commonly known as the Forbes' leeah, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Leeaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on short pedicels. It is a woody shrub that can grow up to several meters in height, and it thrives in humid, tropical environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
