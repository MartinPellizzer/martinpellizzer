Lycopsis taurica

Lycopsis taurica, commonly known as the Taurian lycopsis, is a rare and ancient plant species native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the family Lycopsidaceae and is characterized by its small, spiral-arranged leaves and root-like structures called rhizomes. The plant exhibits a primitive morphology, resembling early land plants, and is often found in moist, shaded environments. Lycopsis taurica is of significant interest to botanists due to its evolutionary significance and limited distribution.

Its conservation status is vulnerable due to habitat loss and environmental changes.
