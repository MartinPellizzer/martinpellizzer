Linum smallii

Linum smallii, commonly known as the Sierra flax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and delicate blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky habitats and is adapted to dry, sunny conditions. Its leaves are alternate and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

Linum smallii plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is valued for its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
