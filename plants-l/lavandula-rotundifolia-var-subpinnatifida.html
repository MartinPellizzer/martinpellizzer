Lavandula rotundifolia var. subpinnatifida

Lavandula rotundifolia var. subpinnatifida, commonly known as narrow-leaved lavender, is a variety of lavender native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are slightly serrated along the edges. The plant produces spikes of pale purple to lilac flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in herbal remedies and essential oil production.
