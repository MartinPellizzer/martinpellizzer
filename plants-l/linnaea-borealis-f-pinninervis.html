Linnaea borealis f. pinninervis

Linnaea borealis f. pinninervis, commonly known as the slender twinflower, is a rare variant of the twinflower plant native to northern regions of North America and Eurasia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, opposite, pinnately veined leaves, which set it apart from the typical form of Linnaea borealis. The plant produces small, fragrant, white flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves fully develop. It thrives in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes.

Due to its limited distribution and unique morphology, it is of particular interest to botanists and conservationists.
