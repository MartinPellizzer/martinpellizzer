Lucuma lasiocarpa

Lucuma lasiocarpa, commonly known as the golden apple or lucuma, is a South American plant native to the Andes.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its large, yellow-orange fruits that resemble small apples. The fruit has a hard, spiky exterior and a sweet, creamy pulp when ripe.

It is valued for its nutritional content, including high levels of carbohydrates and vitamin C. The plant is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
