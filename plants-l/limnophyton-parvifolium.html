Limnophyton parvifolium

Limnophyton parvifolium, commonly known as the small-leaved water plant, is a unique aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and marshes, where it anchors itself to submerged substrates. The plant has delicate, narrow leaves that are typically less than 2 centimeters in length, giving it a feathery appearance. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, often clustered in dense inflorescences, and are adapted for pollination by water insects.

Limnophyton parvifolium plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
