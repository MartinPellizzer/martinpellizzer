Linaria kantschavelii

Linaria kantschavelii, commonly known as the Kantschavel's toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant characterized by its slender stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces vibrant yellow to orange flowers, which are typically tubular in shape and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Linaria kantschavelii thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the genus Linaria, known for its ornamental value and ecological significance.
