Lepanthes lymphosa

Lepanthes lymphosa, commonly known as the lymphose orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the cloud forests of Central America.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pale green. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, adapted to the humid, shaded conditions of its natural habitat.

Lepanthes lymphosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
