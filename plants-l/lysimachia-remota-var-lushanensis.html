Lysimachia remota var. lushanensis

Lysimachia remota var. lushanensis, commonly known as the Lushan Lysimachia, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and small, bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and stream banks. Its leaves are usually oval-shaped with a glossy surface, and it produces numerous tiny, clustered flowers during the summer months.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural settings for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
