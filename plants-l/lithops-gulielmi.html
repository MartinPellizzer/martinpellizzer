Lithops gulielmi

Lithops gulielmi, commonly known as the "Guelich's ice plant," is a succulent native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, paired leaves that resemble stones, which help it blend into its rocky habitat. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers during the summer months. It stores water in its thick, fleshy leaves, allowing it to survive in arid conditions.

Lithops gulielmi is a popular choice for rock gardens due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
