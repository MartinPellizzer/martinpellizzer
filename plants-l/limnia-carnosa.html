Limnia carnosa

Limnia carnosa, commonly known as the fleshy moss, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its soft, succulent stems and leaves.

It thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in wetlands and along the edges of ponds. The plant's structure consists of rhizoids that anchor it to substrates, and its leaves are typically broad and fleshy, aiding in water retention. Limnia carnosa plays a role in stabilizing sediment and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.

Its botanical features make it an important component of aquatic ecosystems.
