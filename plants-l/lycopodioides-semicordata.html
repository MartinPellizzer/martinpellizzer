Lycopodioides semicordata

Lycopodioides semicordata, commonly known as the heartleaf clubmoss, is a member of the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is a small, herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features spirally arranged leaves that are often lanceolate or ovate in shape. It produces small, spore-bearing structures called strobila, which are typically clustered at the tips of its branches.

Lycopodioides semicordata plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
