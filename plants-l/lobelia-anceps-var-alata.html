Lobelia anceps var. alata

Lobelia anceps var. alata, commonly known as the winged lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden settings for its delicate, cascading growth habit.
