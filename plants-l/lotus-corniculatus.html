Lotus corniculatus

Lotus corniculatus, commonly known as the bird's-foot trefoil, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with trifoliate leaves and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a creeping habit, often forming dense mats, and is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils. It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it a valuable plant in ecological restoration.

The species is also cultivated as a forage crop for livestock due to its nutritional value.
