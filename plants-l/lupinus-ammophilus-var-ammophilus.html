Lupinus ammophilus var. ammophilus

Lupinus ammophilus var. ammophilus, commonly known as the sand lupine, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often found in coastal dunes and inland sand plains. The plant features erect, branched stems and produces clusters of pea-like flowers in shades of blue, purple, or white. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, narrow leaflets.

Lupinus ammophilus var. ammophilus is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.
