Lespedeza buergeri var. oldhamii

Lespedeza buergeri var. oldhamii, commonly known as Oldham's lespeza, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
