Lobelia pygmaea

Lobelia pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf lobelia, is a small herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of only a few inches, featuring delicate blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. It is a member of the Campanulaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with showy blooms.
