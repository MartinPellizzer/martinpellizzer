Linanthus maculatus

Linanthus maculatus, commonly known as the spotted flax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Linaceae family, which includes other flax species. The plant is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Linanthus maculatus is adapted to arid environments and is often found in dry grasslands and rocky soils.
