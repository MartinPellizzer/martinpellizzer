Lycopodioides madagascariensis

Lycopodioides madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar clubmoss, is a species of lycophyte belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its small, spirally arranged leaves and a distinctive, erect, spore-bearing cone at the tip of its stem. Native to Madagascar, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its reproductive structures are adapted for spore dispersal, typical of its evolutionary lineage.

The plant exhibits a unique morphology that reflects its ancient origins and ecological adaptations.
