Lepanthes pulcherrima

Lepanthes pulcherrima, commonly known as the beautiful orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that often display vibrant colors such as red, yellow, and orange. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are usually borne on terminal racemes and are known for their intricate, often fragrant structure.

Lepanthes pulcherrima is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique and colorful blooms.
