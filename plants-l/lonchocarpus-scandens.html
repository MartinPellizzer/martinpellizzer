Lonchocarpus scandens

Lonchocarpus scandens, commonly known as the climbing cowpea, is a tropical legume native to Central and South America.

It is a vigorous climber with pinnately compound leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial in soil enrichment. Its seeds are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
