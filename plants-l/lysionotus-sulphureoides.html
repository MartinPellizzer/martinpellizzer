Lysionotus sulphureoides

Lysionotus sulphureoides, commonly known as the golden star orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange inflorescence with star-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or terrestrial species, often found in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects attracted to its vibrant color and scent.

Lysionotus sulphureoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts for its unique appearance.
