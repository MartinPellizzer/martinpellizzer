Lemaireocereus godingianus

Lemaireocereus godingianus, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stem and is typically found in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant produces striking white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as bats and moths. Its growth is slow, and it requires well-draining soil and minimal water to thrive.

Due to its unique blooming habit and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
