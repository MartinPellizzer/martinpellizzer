Lonicera gracilipes var. glandulosa

Lonicera gracilipes var. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features slender, arching stems covered in small, glandular hairs, which give it its varietal name. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy surface.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
