Linum flavum var. campanulatum

Linum flavum var. campanulatum, commonly known as the yellow flax or campanulate flax, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, yellow, bell-shaped flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a soft appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
