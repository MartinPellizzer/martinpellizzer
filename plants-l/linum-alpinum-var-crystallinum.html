Linum alpinum var. crystallinum

Linum alpinum var. crystallinum, commonly known as the Alpine flax, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows.

This variety is noted for its delicate appearance and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
