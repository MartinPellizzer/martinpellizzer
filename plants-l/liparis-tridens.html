Liparis tridens

Liparis tridens, commonly known as the three-toothed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers with three distinct teeth on each petal. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Liparis tridens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
