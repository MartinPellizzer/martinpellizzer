Lamium album var. kitadakense

Lamium album var. kitadakense, commonly known as the Japanese white dead-nettle, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture. The plant produces showy, white, spurred flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental species within the Lamiaceae family.
