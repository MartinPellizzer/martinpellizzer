Linaria aurasiaca

Linaria aurasiaca, commonly known as the aurasiaca toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers, which are clustered in racemes and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Linaria aurasiaca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
