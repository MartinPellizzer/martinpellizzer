Lobelia amabilis

Lobelia amabilis, commonly known as the sweet lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 feet, featuring upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Lobelia amabilis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
