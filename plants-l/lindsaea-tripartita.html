Lindsaea tripartita

Lindsaea tripartita, commonly known as the three-part lindsaea, is a species of epiphytic fern native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that often grow in a rosette pattern. The plant typically thrives in shaded, humid environments, such as on tree trunks or in forest undergrowth. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are clustered in specialized indusia on the underside of its fronds.

Lindsaea tripartita is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
