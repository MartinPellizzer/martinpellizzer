Lavandula × intermedia nothosubsp. leptostachya

Lavandula × intermedia nothosubsp.

leptostachya, commonly known as English lavender, is a hybrid plant that combines traits from Lavandula angustifolia and Lavandula × intermedia. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and aromatic, silver-gray foliage.

The plant produces spikes of fragrant purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It is well-suited for cultivation in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal use.

This subspecies is noted for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of climates.
