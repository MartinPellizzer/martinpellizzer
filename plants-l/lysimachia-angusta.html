Lysimachia angusta

Lysimachia angusta, commonly known as narrowleaf chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features small, opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, star-shaped white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Lysimachia angusta is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and early blooming habit.
