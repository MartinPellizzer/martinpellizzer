Linum flavum var. lanceolatum

Linum flavum var. lanceolatum, commonly known as the lanceolate flax, is a variant of the flax species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. It is a herbaceous perennial with a fibrous root system, and its stems are often hairy.

The flowers are clustered in racemes and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
