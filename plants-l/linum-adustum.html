Linum adustum

Linum adustum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and delicate blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. It is a herbaceous perennial that produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Linum adustum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in textile production.
