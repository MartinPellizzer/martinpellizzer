Lepanthes bicornuta

Lepanthes bicornuta, commonly known as the two-horned orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, pendulous flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually ovate and arranged in a basal rosette.

Lepanthes bicornuta is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
