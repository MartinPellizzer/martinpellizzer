Lonicera tenuipes var. tomentosa

Lonicera tenuipes var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, grayish-green leaves that provide a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and the sweet scent of its flowers.
