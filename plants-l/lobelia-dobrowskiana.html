Lobelia dobrowskiana

Lobelia dobrowskiana, commonly known as the Siberian lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features erect, slender stems that bear whorled clusters of small, blue to violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
