Leea macropus

Leea macropus, commonly known as the broad-leaved leea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Leeaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often broad and elliptical. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, inflated fruit capsules. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural studies.
