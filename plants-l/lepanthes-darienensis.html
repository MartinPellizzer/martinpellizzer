Lepanthes darienensis

Lepanthes darienensis, commonly known as the Darien orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a compact, pseudobulbous structure and produces small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests. Its flowers are short-lived but attract various pollinators, including bees and moths.

Lepanthes darienensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is a subject of interest in botanical research due to its ecological and evolutionary significance.
