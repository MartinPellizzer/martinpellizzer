Leycesteria glaucophylla var. thibetica

Leycesteria glaucophylla var. thibetica, commonly known as the Tibetan honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers in late summer. Its growth habit is compact, with a tendency to form dense thickets.

It is well adapted to cold, arid environments and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
