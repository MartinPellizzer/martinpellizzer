Luma ruiziana

Luma ruiziana, commonly known as the Andean nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in high-altitude regions, often in rocky or arid environments. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or purple in color. Its leaves are simple and opposite, with a slightly hairy texture.

Luma ruiziana is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
