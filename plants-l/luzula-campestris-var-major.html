Luzula campestris var. major

Luzula campestris var. major, commonly known as the tall wood rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, commonly referred to as the grass family, and is characterized by its upright, grass-like appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks, where it thrives in well-drained soil. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with silver or white along the edges.

The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences of greenish-white flowers during the late spring and early summer.
