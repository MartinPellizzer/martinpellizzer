Lagerstroemia thomsonii

Lagerstroemia thomsonii, commonly known as the Thomson's crape myrtle, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, panicle-like inflorescences that bloom in summer, typically in shades of pink, red, or white. The plant has smooth, greyish bark that peels in thin layers, and its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a rough texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

The species is also valued for its adaptability and resistance to pests and diseases.
