Laportea amplissima

Laportea amplissima, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowwood, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
