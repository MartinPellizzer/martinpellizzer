Lonicera nigra var. campaniflora

Lonicera nigra var. campaniflora, commonly known as the bellflower honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, blooming in early spring. Its fruit is a red, fleshy drupe that matures in late summer, attracting various bird species.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
