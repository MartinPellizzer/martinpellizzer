Lychnis chubutensis

Lychnis chubutensis, commonly known as the Chubut carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Patagonian region of Argentina.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink or red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to the harsh climate of its native habitat. It has simple, linear leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that attract pollinators.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
