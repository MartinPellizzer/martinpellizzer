Lobelia × makoyi

Lobelia × makoyi, commonly known as the hybrid lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a cross between Lobelia cardinalis and Lobelia sanguinea, resulting in a species with distinctive botanical features. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with lance-shaped leaves and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to purple. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it an important component of local ecosystems.
