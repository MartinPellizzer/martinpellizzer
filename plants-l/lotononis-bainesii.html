Lotononis bainesii

Lotononis bainesii, commonly known as the Baines' daisy, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow ray florets and dense, compact growth habit. The plant typically grows from 10 to 30 centimeters in height, with narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its small, daisy-like flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
