Lagascea media

Lagascea media, commonly known as the common redstem, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces vibrant red or purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a hardy plant for xeriscaping.

Its fibrous roots help prevent soil erosion, contributing to its ecological importance in its native habitat.
