Lippia hermannioides

Lippia hermannioides, commonly known as Hermann's verbena, is a flowering plant native to Central America and northern South America.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically has hairy, aromatic leaves and produces showy inflorescences in shades of purple or pink. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Lippia hermannioides is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
