Lobelia pulchella

Lobelia pulchella, commonly known as the showy lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Lobelia pulchella is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
