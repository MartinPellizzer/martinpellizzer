Laurus elongata

Laurus elongata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed bay tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen leaves that are elongated and lance-shaped. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by dark, glossy berries. Its leaves are often used in cooking, particularly in Mediterranean cuisine, for their distinctive flavor.

Laurus elongata thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.
