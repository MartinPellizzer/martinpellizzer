Lonicera sempervirens var. flava

Lonicera sempervirens var. flava, commonly known as the golden honeysuckle, is a deciduous vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy surface. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its berries are orange-red and serve as a food source for birds.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade to full sun.
