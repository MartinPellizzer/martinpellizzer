Lepironia mucronata

Lepironia mucronata, commonly known as the purple swamp saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to wetland and marsh environments in Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and clusters of small, purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in waterlogged soils and is often found in swampy areas near rivers and lakes. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats, which help stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Lepironia mucronata plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing food and shelter for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
