Lithospermum pinetorum

Lithospermum pinetorum, commonly known as the eastern purple coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces showy, tubular purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Its flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity.
