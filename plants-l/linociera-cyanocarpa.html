Linociera cyanocarpa

Linociera cyanocarpa, commonly known as the blue-fruited liana, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves with a serrated margin. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its most distinctive feature is the blue-black berries that appear in clusters, which are edible but have a tart flavor.

Linociera cyanocarpa is often found in humid lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
