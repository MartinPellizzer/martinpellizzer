Laurus culilawang

Laurus culilawang, commonly known as the Philippine laurel, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in lowland to montane forests, reaching heights of up to 20 meters. Its bark and leaves have been traditionally used in local medicine for their purported health benefits.

The fruit, which is a small, dark berry, is often used in traditional culinary practices.
