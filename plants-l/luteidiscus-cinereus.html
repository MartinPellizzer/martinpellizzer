Luteidiscus cinereus

Luteidiscus cinereus, commonly known as the ash-gray lichen, is a foliose lichen that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It features a dark grey to ash-colored thallus with a leathery texture. The plant's structure consists of a symbiotic association between a fungus and algae or cyanobacteria. It is often found on tree bark, rocks, and soil in temperate regions.

Luteidiscus cinereus plays a role in nutrient cycling and serves as a habitat for various microorganisms.
