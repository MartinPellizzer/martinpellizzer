Lonchocarpus robustus

Lonchocarpus robustus, commonly known as the giant soursop or wild soursop, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet flowers. The plant produces long, woody pods that contain several large, kidney-shaped seeds. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.

However, it contains toxic compounds and requires careful handling.
