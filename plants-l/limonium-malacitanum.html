Limonium malacitanum

Limonium malacitanum, commonly known as the Sicilian sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, preferring sandy or gravelly soils. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Limonium malacitanum is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained conditions.
