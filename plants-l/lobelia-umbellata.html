Lobelia umbellata

Lobelia umbellata, commonly known as the whorled lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, wooded areas. The plant features upright stems with whorled leaves and produces clusters of blue to purple flowers. Its flowers are arranged in umbels, giving it its scientific name.

Lobelia umbellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
