Leucadendron lineare

Leucadendron lineare, commonly known as the silver leaf candle bush, is a South African shrub belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, silvery-green leaves that are arranged in a linear fashion, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by distinctive, cone-like seed heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and Mediterranean climates.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and public landscapes.
