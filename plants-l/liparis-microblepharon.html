Liparis microblepharon

Liparis microblepharon, commonly known as the small-eyed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers with distinctive, hooded petals and a narrow, spurred labellum. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and mountainous regions.

Botanically, it belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is noted for its delicate floral structure and specialized pollination mechanisms.
