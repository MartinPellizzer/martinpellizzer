Lomariopsis boryana

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, characterized by its unique floral structures and milky sap. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in high-altitude environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

Lomariopsis boryana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
