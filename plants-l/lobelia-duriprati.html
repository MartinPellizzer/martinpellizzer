Lobelia duriprati

Lobelia duriprati, commonly known as the dwarf lobelia, is a herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

Lobelia duriprati is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
