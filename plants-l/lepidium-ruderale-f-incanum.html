Lepidium ruderale f. incanum

Lepidium ruderale f. incanum, commonly known as the hoary cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely hairy, grayish-green foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, typical of the family. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and fields.

Its seed pods are slender and dehiscent, releasing seeds through a mechanism that aids in its dispersal.
