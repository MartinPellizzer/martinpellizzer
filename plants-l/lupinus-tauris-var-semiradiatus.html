Lupinus tauris var. semiradiatus

Lupinus tauris var. semiradiatus, commonly known as the Caucasian lupine, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and clusters of vibrant blue to purple flowers. The plant features pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species adapted to temperate climates.

Lupinus tauris var. semiradiatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and nitrogen-fixing abilities, contributing to soil fertility.
