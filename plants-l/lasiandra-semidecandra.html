Lasiandra semidecandra

Lasiandra semidecandra, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rock heath, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in rocky, arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention. Its flowers are usually pale pink to white, appearing in clusters along the stems.

Lasiandra semidecandra is valued for its drought tolerance and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low maintenance requirements.
