Lilium washingtonianum var. rubescens

Lilium washingtonianum var. rubescens, commonly known as the red-flowered Washington lily, is a striking perennial plant native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red, depending on the variety. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet, with narrow, grass-like leaves that form a basal rosette. Its bulbs are adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
