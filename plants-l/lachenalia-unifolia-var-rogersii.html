Lachenalia unifolia var. rogersii

Lachenalia unifolia var. rogersii, commonly known as the Rogers' single-leaf lachenalia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a rosette of narrow, linear leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, adapted to store water in arid conditions. The plant produces tall, branching stems with clusters of bright yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
