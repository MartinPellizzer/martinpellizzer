Leschenaultia tubiflora var. purpurea

Leschenaultia tubiflora var. purpurea, commonly known as the purple-flowered leschenaultia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white or silver hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
