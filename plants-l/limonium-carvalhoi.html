Limonium carvalhoi

Limonium carvalhoi, commonly known as the Carvalho's sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, typically in coastal areas. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, bract-like structures that surround the flower heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Limonium carvalhoi is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
