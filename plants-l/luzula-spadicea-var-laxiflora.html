Luzula spadicea var. laxiflora

Luzula spadicea var. laxiflora, commonly known as the slender-flowered wood rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features grass-like leaves that are narrow and often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, greenish-white flowers.

It is characterized by its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and its ecological importance in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
