Leucopsis tweediei var. pilosa

Leucopsis tweediei var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy leucopsis, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is a perennial shrub that can reach heights of up to 30 centimeters.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in gardens with well-draining soil.
