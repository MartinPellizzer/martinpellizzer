Lythrum boraei

Lythrum boraei, commonly known as the Arctic lythrum, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is a member of the Lythraceae family and is characterized by its erect, herbaceous stems and showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is adapted to harsh, cold climates. Its small, ovate leaves are arranged alternately along the stem, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Lythrum boraei plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and provides nectar for pollinators in its brief growing season.
