Lindelofia stylosa var. hispida

Lindelofia stylosa var. hispida, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed lindelofia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems covered in fine, stiff hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
