Lamium rubrum var. lacteum

Lamium rubrum var. lacteum, commonly known as the milky deadnettle, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features square, hairy stems that are often reddish or purplish in color. The plant produces opposite, heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small, tubular, and typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
