Leptochilus fluviatilis

Leptochilus fluviatilis, commonly known as the river water clover, is a small aquatic plant that thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves that are often submerged or floating at the water's surface. The plant has tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are typically borne in clusters, contributing to its reproductive success in aquatic habitats. Its root system helps stabilize sediment and improve water clarity by filtering particulate matter.

Leptochilus fluviatilis plays a vital role in maintaining the ecological balance of freshwater ecosystems.
