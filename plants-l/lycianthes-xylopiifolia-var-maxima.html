Lycianthes xylopiifolia var. maxima

Lycianthes xylopiifolia var. maxima, commonly known as the giant yellow bell, is a species native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange to red fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in dry forests and along rocky slopes.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
