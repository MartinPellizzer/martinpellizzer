Lupinus argenteus var. prati-harti

Lupinus argenteus var. prati-harti, commonly known as silver lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and tall spires of blue or purple flowers. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically beneficial.
