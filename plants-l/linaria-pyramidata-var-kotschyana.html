Linaria pyramidata var. kotschyana

Linaria pyramidata var. kotschyana, commonly known as the Kotschy's beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of tubular, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or alpine environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
