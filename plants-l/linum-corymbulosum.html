Linum corymbulosum

Linum corymbulosum, commonly known as the clustered flax, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, pale blue or white flowers. The plant produces small, hairy pods that contain several seeds, which are adapted for wind dispersal. Linum corymbulosum is often found in grasslands and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its delicate appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in suitable climates.
