Lycium torreyi var. filiforme

Lycium torreyi var. filiforme, commonly known as the filiform lycium, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces red berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to harsh conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in desert ecosystems.
