Leptopetalum paniculatum

Leptopetalum paniculatum, commonly known as the Chinese snowbell, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching structure and clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are opposite in arrangement, and its flowers typically bloom in early spring. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its delicate, cascading appearance.

The fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, winged seeds.
