Lepidostachys roxburghii

Lepidostachys roxburghii, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a deciduous shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by bright orange, round fruits that are rich in vitamin C. Its botanical features make it an important species in both traditional medicine and modern horticulture.

The plant's adaptability to various soil types and climate conditions contributes to its widespread cultivation.
