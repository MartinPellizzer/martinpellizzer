Lycopodium pachystachyum

Lycopodium pachystachyum, commonly known as the giant clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and dense clusters of small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces sporophylls that bear spores, which are released into the air for dispersal. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Its unique morphology and reproductive structures make it an important subject in the study of ancient plant lineages.
