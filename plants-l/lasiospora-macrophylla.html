Lasiospora macrophylla

Lasiospora macrophylla, commonly known as the giant moss cushion, is a unique species of moss that forms dense, cushion-like colonies.

It is characterized by its large, elongated leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in mountainous regions and humid forests. Its reproductive structures, called sporophytes, are relatively large compared to other mosses, contributing to its ecological significance.

Lasiospora macrophylla plays a vital role in soil stabilization and moisture retention in its native habitats.
