Lobivia densispina f. citriflora

Lobivia densispina f. citriflora, commonly known as the Citrus-spined Lobivia, is a rare and striking succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its dense spines and compact, globular shape. The plant features a short, thick stem with numerous spines that are often yellow or green in color. Its leaves are typically reduced to tubercles, and it produces small, yellow flowers during the growing season.

This cultivar is prized for its unique appearance and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a container plant.
