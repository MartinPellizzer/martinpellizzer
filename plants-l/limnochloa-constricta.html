Limnochloa constricta

Limnochloa constricta, commonly known as the narrowleaf water clover, is a herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its distinctive, narrow leaves that are often arranged in a rosette formation. The plant typically grows in wet, marshy environments and is adapted to waterlogged soils. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in shades of white or pale pink.

Limnochloa constricta plays an important role in its native ecosystem, providing habitat and food for various invertebrates.
