Lolium edwardii

Lolium edwardii, commonly known as Edward's ryegrass, is a species of grass native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous pale green to yellowish flowers.

Lolium edwardii is often used in ecological studies due to its adaptability and role in soil stabilization.
