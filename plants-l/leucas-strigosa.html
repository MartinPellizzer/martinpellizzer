Leucas strigosa

Leucas strigosa, commonly known as hairy leucas, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its hairy, square stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. It produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters.

Leucas strigosa is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
