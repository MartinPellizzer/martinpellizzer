Lacistema guyanense

Lacistema guyanense, commonly known as the Guyana lacistema, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by orange or red berries containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understorey of tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
