Listera nepalensis

Listera nepalensis, commonly known as the Nepal basketflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal and northern India.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. It has a short rhizomatous root system and produces small, oval-shaped berries that contain the seeds.

Listera nepalensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
