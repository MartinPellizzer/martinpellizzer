Lonicera tatarica f. sibirica

Lonicera tatarica f. sibirica, commonly known as the Siberian honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, arching stems with opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, yellowish-white flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its botanical characteristics make it suitable for use in gardens and as a hedging plant.
