Lindera pulcherrima var. hemsleyana

Lindera pulcherrima var. hemsleyana, commonly known as the Hemsley's beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are a striking feature, often attracting birds and other wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in garden settings.
