Lycopodium pichinchense

Lycopodium pichinchense, commonly known as the Pichincha clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is native to the Andean regions of Ecuador and Colombia, where it thrives in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant features a slender, branching stem covered in small, scale-like leaves. Its reproductive structures, called strobila, are cone-like and produce spores for propagation.

Lycopodium pichinchense plays a role in maintaining the ecological balance of its habitat by contributing to soil stability and providing microhabitats for various organisms.
