Lophostemon grandiflorus subsp. riparius

Lophostemon grandiflorus subsp.

riparius, commonly known as the river paperbark, is a small to medium-sized tree native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark that reveals a mottled pattern of green and grey, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody, cone-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found along riverbanks and in wetlands.

Its ornamental value and ability to tolerate waterlogged conditions make it a popular choice for landscaping in suitable climates.
