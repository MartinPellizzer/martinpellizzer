Lagenocarpus laniger

Lagenocarpus laniger, commonly known as the woolly pod sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Lagenocarpus laniger thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
