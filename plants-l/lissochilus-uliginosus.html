Lissochilus uliginosus

Lissochilus uliginosus, commonly known as the bog orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom during the cooler months. The plant typically grows in wet, boggy habitats, thriving in moist and acidic soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces a short, horizontal rhizome that helps it spread vegetatively.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often found in high-altitude regions and is considered a rare species in some areas.
