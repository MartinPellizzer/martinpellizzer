Lobelia comosa var. foliosa

Lobelia comosa var. foliosa, commonly known as the hairy lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
