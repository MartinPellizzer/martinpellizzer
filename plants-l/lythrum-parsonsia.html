Lythrum parsonsia

Lythrum parsonsia, commonly known as parson's lythrum, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Lythrum.

It is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands or along stream banks. The plant produces striking spikes of violet to purple flowers, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Lythrum parsonsia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
