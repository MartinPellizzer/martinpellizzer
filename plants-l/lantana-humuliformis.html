Lantana humuliformis

Lantana humuliformis, commonly known as the golden lantana, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, showy yellow to orange flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture with a strong aromatic scent.

Lantana humuliformis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
