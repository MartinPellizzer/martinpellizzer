Lantana glandulosissima var. grandis

Lantana glandulosissima var. grandis, commonly known as the grand lantana, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to yellow, orange, and red, depending on the variety. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, glandular hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

The plant is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
