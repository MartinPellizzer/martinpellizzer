Lycium chubutense

Lycium chubutense, commonly known as the Chubut lycium, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Argentina.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its small, spiny stems and yellow flowers. The plant produces red berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with limited water availability.

Lycium chubutense plays an important role in the ecological balance of its native habitat.
