Lobelia phyllostachya

Lobelia phyllostachya, commonly known as the purpletop lobelia, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs. This species thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
