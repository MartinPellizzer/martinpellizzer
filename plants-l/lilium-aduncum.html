Lilium aduncum

Lilium aduncum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and striking, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soil and full sun. Its bulbs are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines, while its ornamental value makes it a sought-after garden plant.

Lilium aduncum plays an important ecological role in its native habitats by supporting pollinators and contributing to biodiversity.
