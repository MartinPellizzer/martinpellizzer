Lycopodium dalhousieanum

Lycopodium dalhousieanum, commonly known as the Dalhousie clubmoss, is a species of vascular plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant features upright, branched stems covered with small, scale-like leaves. Its sporophytes produce spores in clusters called strobili, which are borne on the upper parts of the stems.

Lycopodium dalhousieanum is often found in the northeastern regions of India and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
