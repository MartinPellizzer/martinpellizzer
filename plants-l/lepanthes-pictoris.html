Lepanthes pictoris

Lepanthes pictoris, commonly known as the painted orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that often display intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its leaves are usually dark green and fleshy, adapted to store moisture in its natural habitat.

Lepanthes pictoris is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and unique floral display.
