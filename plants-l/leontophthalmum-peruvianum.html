Leontophthalmum peruvianum

Leontophthalmum peruvianum, commonly known as the Peruvian sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble sunflowers. The plant features tall, slender stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy yellow to orange flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
