Lathyrus splendens

Lathyrus splendens, commonly known as the golden pea or scarlet flax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red flowers and slender, branching stems. The plant produces small, bean-like pods containing several seeds. Lathyrus splendens thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
