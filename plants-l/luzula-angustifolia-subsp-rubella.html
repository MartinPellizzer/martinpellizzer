Luzula angustifolia subsp. rubella

Luzula angustifolia subsp.

rubella, commonly known as the red-stemmed wood rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features slender, grass-like leaves that are often dark green with a waxy coating. Its flowering stems bear small, clustered spikes of white or pale blue flowers, which appear in early summer.

This subspecies is distinguished by its reddish stems and is often used in horticultural settings for its ornamental value.
