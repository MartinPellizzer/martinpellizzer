Lonchocarpus sinaloensis

Lonchocarpus sinaloensis, commonly known as the Sinaloan legume, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes and related plants known for their nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow to orange flowers. It produces pods that contain seeds, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical environments.

Due to its ecological importance and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
