Libertia azurea

Libertia azurea, commonly known as the blue-libertia, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves with a silvery-blue underside. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and ability to attract pollinators.
