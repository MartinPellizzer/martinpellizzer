Lycopodium taxifolium var. nitens

Lycopodium taxifolium var. nitens, commonly known as the nitid-leaf clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, scale-like leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern. The plant produces sporophylls that bear microsporangia and megasporangia, which release spores for reproduction. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories or rocky crevices.

This variety is distinguished by its shiny, smooth leaves, which give it its common name.
