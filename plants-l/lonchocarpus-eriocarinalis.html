Lonchocarpus eriocarinalis

Lonchocarpus eriocarinalis, commonly known as the velvet pod senna, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant is notable for its hairy, velvety appearance, particularly on its stems and leaves, which give it its common name. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, and it produces elongated pods that contain several seeds.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine and has potential ecological benefits in certain environments.
