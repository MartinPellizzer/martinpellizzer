Lagenosocereus luetzelburgii

Lagenosocereus luetzelburgii, commonly known as the Luetzelburg's cactus, is a species of cactus native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stem that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces clusters of white to pale yellow flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it has a deep root system adapted to arid conditions.

This species is well-suited for xerophytic environments and requires minimal water once established.
