Lotus pacificus

Lotus pacificus, commonly known as the Pacific lotus, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy pink or white petals and a velvety, dark purple center. The plant typically grows in shallow, brackish water or wetlands, thriving in saline environments. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often floating on the water's surface.

The Pacific lotus is valued for its ornamental beauty and has cultural significance in various Asian traditions.
