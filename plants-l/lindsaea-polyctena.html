Lindsaea polyctena

Lindsaea polyctena, commonly known as the hairy shield fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its dense, hairy fronds that give it its common name. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. Its rhizomes are short and often covered in fine, soft hairs.

Lindsaea polyctena is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
