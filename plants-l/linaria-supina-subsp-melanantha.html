Linaria supina subsp. melanantha

Linaria supina subsp.

melanantha, commonly known as the prostrate toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms mats of narrow, linear leaves.

The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for rock gardens and as a decorative ground cover.
