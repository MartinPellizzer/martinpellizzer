Lilium cernuum var. candidum

Lilium cernuum var. candidum, commonly known as the white fawn lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its drooping, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, rounded berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
