Lavoisiera angustifolia

Lavoisiera angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lavoisiera, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun. It is often found in grasslands and open woodlands, where it contributes to the local biodiversity.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
