Lophiolepis turkestanica subsp. pseudolappacea

Lophiolepis turkestanica subsp.

pseudolappacea, commonly known as the Turkestan lichen-leaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs.

The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with spikelets that are typically pale green to brown in color. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky or sandy soils.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its ecological adaptations and potential role in stabilizing soil in dry regions.
