Limnanthemum albiflorum

Limnanthemum albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered waterstar, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous species that typically grows near water bodies, such as ponds and wetlands. The plant features small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and slightly hairy, with a rounded shape.

Limnanthemum albiflorum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in aquatic gardens.
