Lycopodium complanatum subsp. montellii

Lycopodium complanatum subsp.

montellii, commonly known as the mountain clubmoss, is a small evergreen fern-like plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae and is characterized by its compact, creeping growth habit and scale-like leaves.

The plant produces spore-bearing cones, which are typically clustered at the base of the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the understory vegetation in its native habitat.
