Lepanthes isabeliae

Lepanthes isabeliae, commonly known as Isabel's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking violet and white flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks or branches in humid tropical environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

Lepanthes isabeliae is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and rarity in cultivation.
