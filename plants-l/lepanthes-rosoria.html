Lepanthes rosoria

Lepanthes rosoria, commonly known as the Rosy Pteridium Orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with a short rhizome and a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with a distinctive lip that is often marked with purple or red spots.

Lepanthes rosoria is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
