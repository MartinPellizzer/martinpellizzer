Lagerstroemia ruffordii

Lagerstroemia ruffordii, commonly known as the Rufford's crape myrtle, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern China.

It is characterized by its showy, panicle-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, appearing in summer. The plant has smooth, gray bark that becomes fissured with age, and its leaves are ovate to elliptic, often with a reddish tint when young. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

The fruit is a small, woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.
