Loeselia glandulosa subsp. nepetifolia

Loeselia glandulosa subsp.

nepetifolia, commonly known as the glandular loeselia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its small, clustered inflorescences and hairy, glandular leaves that give it its specific epithet.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are usually pale blue to white, with five petals and a distinctive glandular structure on the stamens.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
