Lychnis patagonica f. glabriuscula

Lychnis patagonica f. glabriuscula, commonly known as the Patagonian fireweed, is a variant of the fireweed species native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and showy pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
