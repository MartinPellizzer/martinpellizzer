Ligusticum carnosulum

Ligusticum carnosulum, commonly known as the Italian cow parsnip, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and umbellate flowers.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, often found in moist, calcareous soils. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring a hollow, ridged stem and pinnately lobed leaves. Its yellowish-green flowers appear in umbrella-like clusters and are followed by small, dry fruits.

The plant is known for its strong, spicy scent and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
