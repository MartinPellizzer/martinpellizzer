Linaria supina var. pyrenaica

Linaria supina var. pyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean upright toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slight hairy texture. This variety is typically found in rocky or gravelly soils at higher elevations.

It is a perennial plant that thrives in full sun and is valued for its ornamental appeal in alpine gardens.
