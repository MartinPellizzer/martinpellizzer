Lotus sativus

Lotus sativus, commonly known as the eddo or water caltrop, is a aquatic plant native to Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiky, floating fruits that contain a single, hard seed. The plant has broad, arrow-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

The fruit is often harvested for its starchy, nutty-tasting seed, which is used in various culinary and medicinal applications.
