Lithops olivacea var. nebrownii

Lithops olivacea var. nebrownii, commonly known as the Brown's Lithops, is a species of succulent native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its pair of fleshy, ribbed leaves that resemble stones, helping it blend into its arid environment. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
