Linociera sangda

Linociera sangda, commonly known as the Sangda tree, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a broad, spreading canopy. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and elliptical in shape, often with a slightly waxy texture. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, round fruits.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
