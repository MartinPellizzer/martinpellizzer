Lagophylla hillmanii

Lagophylla hillmanii, commonly known as the Hillman's ghost flower, is a unique plant native to the arid regions of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in winter, adding a rare splash of color to the landscape. The plant has a low-growing, mat-like habit with succulent leaves that store water, adapting it to its harsh environment. Its flowers are typically clustered in dense inflorescences, and the species is known for its ephemeral blooming pattern.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Lagophylla hillmanii is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
