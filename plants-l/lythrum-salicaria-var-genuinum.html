Lythrum salicaria var. genuinum

Lythrum salicaria var. genuinum, commonly known as purple loosestrife, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescences that produce vibrant purple flowers, which bloom from June to September. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are opposite and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

However, it is considered an invasive species in North America, where it outcompetes native vegetation.
