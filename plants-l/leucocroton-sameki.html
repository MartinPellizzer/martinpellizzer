Leucocroton sameki

Leucocroton sameki, commonly known as the white croton, is a shrub native to the island of Socotra.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are often bright green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to harsh conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
