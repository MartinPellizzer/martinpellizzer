Lonicera infundibulum

Lonicera infundibulum, commonly known as the funnel honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive funnel-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. Its leaves are opposite and oval, with a smooth texture and a slightly serrated margin.

Lonicera infundibulum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
