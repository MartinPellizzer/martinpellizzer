Lamprolobium grandiflorum

Lamprolobium grandiflorum, commonly known as the giant spider orchid, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that resemble spiders, with long, slender petals and a distinctive, elongated lip. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It produces bright yellow or orange flowers during the summer months, attracting specific pollinators like bees and wasps.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
