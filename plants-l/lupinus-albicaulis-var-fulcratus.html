Lupinus albicaulis var. fulcratus

Lupinus albicaulis var. fulcratus, commonly known as the white lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of white to pale pink flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to grow in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and coastal scrub habitats.

This species is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability and potential as a forage crop.
