Launaea

Launaea, commonly known as the sandmat, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and flowering heads that resemble daisies. The plant is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, the Middle East, and parts of Asia. Launaea species often grow in sandy or rocky soils and are adapted to drought conditions.

They are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
