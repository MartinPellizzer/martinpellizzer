Loudetia arundinacea var. trichantha

Loudetia arundinacea var. trichantha, commonly known as the hairy bamboo, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, fibrous growth habit, which makes it suitable for erosion control and as a decorative plant. The leaves are narrow and often covered with fine hairs, giving it its common name. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in panicles, which are typically wind-pollinated.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a range of soil conditions.
