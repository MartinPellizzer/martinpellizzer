Luzula caespitosa subsp. sanabriae

Luzula caespitosa subsp.

sanabriae, commonly known as Sanabrian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its dense, clump-forming growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, three-lobed flowers. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland habitats.
