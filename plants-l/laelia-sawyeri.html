Laelia sawyeri

Laelia sawyeri, commonly known as the Sawyer's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, brightly colored flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant features a pseudobulb, which stores water and nutrients, and produces one or two showy, fragrant flowers per stem. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapted to the cool, moist conditions of its natural habitat.

Laelia sawyeri is appreciated by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and relative ease of cultivation.
