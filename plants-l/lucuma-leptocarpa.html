Lucuma leptocarpa

Lucuma leptocarpa, commonly known as the small lucuma, is a species of plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its yellow, sweet, and creamy fruit. The plant thrives in high-altitude regions with cool temperatures and well-drained soil. Its fruit is typically harvested when it reaches maturity, which occurs several months after flowering.

The botanical features of Lucuma leptocarpa make it a unique and valuable species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
