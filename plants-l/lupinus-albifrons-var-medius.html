Lupinus albifrons var. medius

Lupinus albifrons var. medius, commonly known as the white-fronted lupine, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant has digitate leaves composed of several small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open grasslands and rocky areas.

Lupinus albifrons var. medius is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it valuable in ecological restoration projects.
