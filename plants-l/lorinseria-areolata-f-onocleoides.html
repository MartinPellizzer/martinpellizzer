Lorinseria areolata f. onocleoides

Lorinseria areolata f. onocleoides, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a rare and distinctive orchid species characterized by its unique floral morphology and specialized habitat requirements.

It features showy, often brightly colored flowers with intricate patterns, typically arranged in clustered inflorescences. The plant exhibits a complex life cycle, with a reliance on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake, which is crucial for its survival. Its habitat is usually limited to moist, shaded environments in temperate regions, where it thrives in nutrient-poor soils.
