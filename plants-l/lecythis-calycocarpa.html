Lecythis calycocarpa

Lecythis calycocarpa, commonly known as the yellow lecythium, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Lecythidaceae and is characterized by its large, gourd-like fruits that turn yellow when mature. The plant produces showy, white to yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are typically large, elliptical, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.
