Lucuma pallida

Lucuma pallida, commonly known as the white lucuma, is a species of flowering plant in the family Anacardiaceae.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly Peru and Ecuador. The plant produces fruit that is typically yellow or orange when ripe, though the variety known as Lucuma pallida has a distinctive white flesh. The fruit is valued for its sweet, nutty flavor and is often used in culinary applications.

It thrives in high-altitude environments and requires well-drained soil and a warm climate.
