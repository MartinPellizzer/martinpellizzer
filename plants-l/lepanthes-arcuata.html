Lepanthes arcuata

Lepanthes arcuata, commonly known as the curved-lobed orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments. The plant features a short, erect stem with a few leaves and produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. Its flowers are characterized by their unique, curved sepals and petals, which contribute to its ornamental appeal.

Lepanthes arcuata is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
