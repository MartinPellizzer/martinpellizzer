Lycopodioides karsteniana

Lycopodioides karsteniana, commonly known as the Karsten's clubmoss, is a small, evergreen plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, prostrate growth habit and its production of small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces spore-bearing structures called strobili, which are clustered at the tips of its stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in temperate regions with high humidity.

Lycopodioides karsteniana is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and its ability to form dense, low-growing mats.
