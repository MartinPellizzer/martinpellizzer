Loasa vitifolia

Loasa vitifolia, commonly known as the grapeleaf loasa, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, woodland habitats. The plant features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, white flowers bloom in clusters and are followed by elongated, woody fruits.

Loasa vitifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
