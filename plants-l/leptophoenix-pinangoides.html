Leptophoenix pinangoides

Leptophoenix pinangoides, commonly known as the pinang tree, is a member of the Arecaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a medium-sized palm tree characterized by its slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in panicles, which are followed by elongated, feather-like fruits. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated for conservation and horticultural purposes.
