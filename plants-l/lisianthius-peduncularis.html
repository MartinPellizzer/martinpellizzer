Lisianthius peduncularis

Lisianthius peduncularis, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and roadside ditches. The plant produces clusters of small, blue-to-purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
