Lycopodium simulans

Lycopodium simulans, commonly known as the ground pine, is a small evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae and is characterized by its dense, needle-like foliage that resembles the appearance of pine needles. The plant produces small, cone-like structures called strobila, which contain spores rather than seeds. Lycopodium simulans thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded, woodland environments.

Its unique morphology and ancient lineage make it a fascinating subject for botanical study.
