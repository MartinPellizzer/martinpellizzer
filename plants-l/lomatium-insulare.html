Lomatium insulare

Lomatium insulare, commonly known as the island lomatium, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of fern-like, compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in umbels during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

Lomatium insulare is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
