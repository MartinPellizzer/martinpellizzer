Leptochilus acuminatus

Leptochilus acuminatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It typically features compact, mat-forming growth with small, fleshy, often blue-green leaves that are adapted to conserve water. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in clusters during the spring and early summer. Its leaves are characterized by their narrow shape and acute tips, which help reduce water loss.

Leptochilus acuminatus thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sandy or rocky habitats.
