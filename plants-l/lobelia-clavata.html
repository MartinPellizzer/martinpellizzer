Lobelia clavata

Lobelia clavata, commonly known as the clustered lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, tubular blue to purple flowers. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, woodland environments and is often found in shaded to partially sunny areas.

This plant is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators like butterflies and bees.
