Lonchocarpus minimiflorus

Lonchocarpus minimiflorus, commonly known as the small-flowered legume, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, where it typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, and its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small leaflets. It produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.
