Lilium parviflorum

Lilium parviflorum, commonly known as the Turk's cap lily, is a species of flowering plant in the Liliaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and the Caucasus. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically red or pink in color. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and grows from a bulbous underground stem.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
