Lepidium aucheri var. borszczowii

Lepidium aucheri var. borszczowii, commonly known as Borszczowii cress, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It features small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes, typical of the Brassicaceae family. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible leaves, which have a peppery flavor similar to watercress.
