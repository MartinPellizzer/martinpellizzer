Lepironia palustris

Lepironia palustris, commonly known as the swamp lantern, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, lantern-shaped inflorescences that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and swamps, where it forms dense colonies. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often float on the water surface.

Lepironia palustris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in aquatic gardens.
