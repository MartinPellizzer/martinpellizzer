Leimanthium virginicum

Leimanthium virginicum, commonly known as the Virginia leimanthium, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features a rhizomatous root system and has alternate, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small and clustered in loose spikes, usually appearing in late spring to early summer.

The plant is often found in association with other wetland flora and plays a role in maintaining ecological balance in its habitat.
