Liparis saltucola

Liparis saltucola, commonly known as the salt marsh orchid, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in saline or brackish soils, often found in wetlands and coastal dunes. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it produces a basal rosette of foliage.

Liparis saltucola is an important species for coastal ecosystems, providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
