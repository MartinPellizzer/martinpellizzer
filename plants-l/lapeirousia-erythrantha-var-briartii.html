Lapeirousia erythrantha var. briartii

Lapeirousia erythrantha var. briartii, commonly known as the Orange River orchid, is a member of the Iridaceae family and is native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers with a distinctive yellow throat, which bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and birds.

This variant is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
