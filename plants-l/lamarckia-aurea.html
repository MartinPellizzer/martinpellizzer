Lamarckia aurea

Lamarckia aurea, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets and produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The botanical features of Lamarckia aurea make it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
