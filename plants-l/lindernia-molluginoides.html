Lindernia molluginoides

Lindernia molluginoides, commonly known as the Mexican wallflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Verbenaceae and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, tubular flowers. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of pink, red, and purple, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
