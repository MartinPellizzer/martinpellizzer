Lagenocarpus ciliatus

Lagenocarpus ciliatus, commonly known as the hairy sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family.

Lagenocarpus ciliatus is often found in moist environments such as wetlands and marshes.
