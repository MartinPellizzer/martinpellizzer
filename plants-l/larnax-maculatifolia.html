Larnax maculatifolia

Larnax maculatifolia, commonly known as the spotted leaf larnax, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmately lobed leaves that are often adorned with white or yellow spots. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its foliage is valued for its ornamental appeal, making it a popular choice in horticulture.

However, it requires well-drained soil and partial shade to thrive optimally.
