Lupinus pulcher

Lupinus pulcher, commonly known as the beautiful lupin, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of vibrant blue or purple flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Lupinus pulcher is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
