Lasianthus rufus

Lasianthus rufus, commonly known as the red-flowered lasianthus, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking red or pink tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a glossy surface, while the flowers are clustered in whorls along the stems.

Lasianthus rufus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
