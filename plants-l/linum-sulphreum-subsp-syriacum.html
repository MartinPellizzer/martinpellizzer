Linum sulphreum subsp. syriacum

Linum sulphreum subsp.

syriacum, commonly known as the Syrian flax, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of southern Europe. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture.

The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
