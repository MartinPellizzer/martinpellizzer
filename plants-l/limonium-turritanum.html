Limonium turritanum

Limonium turritanum, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking, spiky blue or purple flower heads that resemble small towers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, thriving in sandy or gravelly soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in a fine layer of salt.

Limonium turritanum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate saline conditions.
