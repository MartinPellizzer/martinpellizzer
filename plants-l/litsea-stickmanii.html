Litsea stickmanii

Litsea stickmanii, commonly known as the dwarf litsea, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. It produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by orange-red berries that are edible but not widely cultivated.

Litsea stickmanii is often used in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and compact size.
