Laurus odorifera

Laurus odorifera, commonly known as the bay laurel, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fragrant, dark green leaves that are widely used in cooking and traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by red, fleshy berries. Its essential oils, derived from the leaves, contain compounds like eucalyptol and linalool, which contribute to its aromatic properties.

The bay laurel has been historically valued for its ornamental beauty and practical uses in various cultures.
