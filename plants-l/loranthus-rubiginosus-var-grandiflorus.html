Loranthus rubiginosus var. grandiflorus

Loranthus rubiginosus var. grandiflorus, commonly known as the large-flowered mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to host trees through its haustoria.

It is native to regions of Africa and is characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, reddish-brown berries that are a food source for birds and other animals. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can affect the health of its host trees if left unchecked.
