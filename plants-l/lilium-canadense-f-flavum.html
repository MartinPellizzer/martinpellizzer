Lilium canadense f. flavum

Lilium canadense f. flavum, commonly known as the Canadian white lily, is a cultivar of the native North American lily species.

It is characterized by its pale yellow to white flowers, which are typically larger and more showy than those of the typical species. The plant features strap-like, grassy leaves and produces bulbous underground storage organs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
