Lomaria spissa

Lomaria spissa, commonly known as the rigid lomaria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are small and yellow, often arranged in a compact cluster.

Lomaria spissa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its resilience and low maintenance requirements.
