Linum saxicola

Linum saxicola, commonly known as the rock flax, is a flowering plant native to arid and rocky environments in the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate blue or violet flowers and linear, grass-like leaves. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well adapted to harsh, dry conditions. Linum saxicola is often found growing on limestone or sandy soils in mountainous areas.

Its small, showy flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
