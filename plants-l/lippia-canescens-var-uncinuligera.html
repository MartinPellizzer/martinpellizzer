Lippia canescens var. uncinuligera

Lippia canescens var. uncinuligera, commonly known as the hairy lippia, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring aromatic, hairy leaves with a grayish-green color. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

The species is characterized by its branched structure and the presence of glandular hairs on its foliage.
