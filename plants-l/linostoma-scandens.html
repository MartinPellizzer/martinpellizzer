Linostoma scandens

Linostoma scandens, commonly known as the climbing lily, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Liliaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit, with tendrils that allow it to ascend supports. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with six petals. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stems.

Linostoma scandens is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
