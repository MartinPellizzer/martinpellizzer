Lotus corniculatus subsp. ruprechtii

Lotus corniculatus subsp.

ruprechtii, commonly known as the Ruprecht's bird's-foot trefoil, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is a low-growing perennial herb with trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands, meadows, and along roadsides. It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice for ecological restoration projects.
