Lonicera coreana

Lonicera coreana, commonly known as the Korean honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to yellow, often blooming in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that matures in the fall, providing food for birds and small mammals.

Lonicera coreana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
