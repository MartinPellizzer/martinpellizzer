Liparis loeselii subsp. orientalis

Liparis loeselii subsp.

orientalis, commonly known as the oriental lady's tresses, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea. It is characterized by its dense, spiraling inflorescence of small, white to pale pink flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it produces a single, slender flower stem.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
