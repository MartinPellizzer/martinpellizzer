Lunasia pubifolia

Lunasia pubifolia, commonly known as the hairy moonflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, giving it its common name. Its flowers emit a strong fragrance to attract nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

Lunasia pubifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
