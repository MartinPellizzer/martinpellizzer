Laurus nervosa

Laurus nervosa, commonly known as sweet bay or laurel, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy, elongated leaves that have a subtle aromatic scent when crushed. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by dark, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are often used in cooking, particularly in Mediterranean cuisine, and have been historically associated with symbolism and ritual.

Botanically, it belongs to the Lauraceae family and is valued for its ornamental and aromatic qualities.
