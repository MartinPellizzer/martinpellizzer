Lamprococcus fulgens

Lamprococcus fulgens, commonly known as the golden shield bug, is a species of scale insect belonging to the family Coccidae.

It is characterized by its glossy, golden-brown exoskeleton, which provides it with a striking appearance. The plant it infests is typically a host within the genus Eucalyptus, where it feeds on sap from the leaves and stems. Its life cycle includes several stages, with females laying eggs on the underside of leaves.

This pest can cause significant damage to eucalyptus trees, affecting their growth and overall health.
