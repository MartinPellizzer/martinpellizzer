Lilium speciosum var. fasciatum-album

Lilium speciosum var. fasciatum-album, commonly known as the White-Fasciated Lily, is a striking variation of the species Lilium speciosum.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers with distinctive yellow or cream-colored fasciation patterns on the petals. This variety is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant is a perennial bulbous herb, with narrow, grass-like leaves and a tall stem that supports its ornamental blooms.

Its unique coloration makes it a prized addition to gardens and botanical collections.
