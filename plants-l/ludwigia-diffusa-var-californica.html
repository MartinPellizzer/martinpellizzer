Ludwigia diffusa var. californica

Ludwigia diffusa var. californica, commonly known as California Ludwigia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often reddish or maroon in color. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in riparian zones and wetlands.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in aquatic and wetland habitats.
