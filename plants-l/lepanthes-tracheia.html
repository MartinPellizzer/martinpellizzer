Lepanthes tracheia

Lepanthes tracheia, commonly known as the Trachea Orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Lepanthes tracheia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
