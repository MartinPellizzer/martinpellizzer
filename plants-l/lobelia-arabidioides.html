Lobelia arabidioides

Lobelia arabidioides, commonly known as the Arabian lobelia, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its dry environment.

Lobelia arabidioides is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low rainfall, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
