Leucanthemum vulgare var. asperulum

Leucanthemum vulgare var. asperulum, commonly known as the rough leucanthemum, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features white to light yellow ray florets surrounding a central cluster of dark brown to black disk florets. The plant has rough, hairy leaves that are typically lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
