Lychnis laeta

Lychnis laeta, commonly known as the sweet william, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the carnation family (Caryophyllaceae) and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and produces small, rounded leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Lychnis laeta is also known for its pleasant, spicy fragrance, which adds to its ornamental value.
