Lonchocarpus pyxidarius

Lonchocarpus pyxidarius, commonly known as the yellow flame acacia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces distinctive pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its growth habit includes both shrub and tree forms, depending on environmental conditions.
