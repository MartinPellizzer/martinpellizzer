Lycium schreiteri

Lycium schreiteri, commonly known as Schreiter's boxthorn, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. Lycium schreiteri is adapted to harsh environments, with thick leaves and a deep root system to conserve water.

It plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing food for wildlife in its native habitat.
