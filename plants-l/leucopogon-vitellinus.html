Leucopogon vitellinus

Leucopogon vitellinus, commonly known as the yellow leucopogon, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its small, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in moist, shaded environments such as pine flatwoods and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Leucopogon vitellinus is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
