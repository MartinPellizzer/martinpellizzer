Lupinus cyaneus

Lupinus cyaneus, commonly known as the blue lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Lupinus cyaneus is also valued for its role in nitrogen fixation, improving soil fertility.
