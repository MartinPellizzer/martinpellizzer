Laretia

Laretia, commonly known as the blueweed, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling a cluster of tiny stars. The plants typically have slender, branching stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Laretia species are native to various regions in the Americas and are often found in dry, open habitats.

They are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes used in wildflower gardens.
