Leucothoe axillaris var. ambigens

Leucothoe axillaris var. ambigens, commonly known as the Appalachian leatherleaf, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features narrow, elongated leaves that are often tinged with red or purple on the underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in the spring, which are clustered in umbels. Its slow growth habit and dense foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

It thrives in moist, acidic soils and prefers partial to full shade.
