Laurus pallens

Laurus pallens, commonly known as the sacred laurel or white bay, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries. Its leaves are glossy and dark green, often used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

The botanical features of Laurus pallens make it both ornamental and ecologically significant in its native habitat.
