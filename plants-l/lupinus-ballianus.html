Lupinus ballianus

Lupinus ballianus, commonly known as the Ballian's lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its erect, spindly stems and clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers. The plant produces cylindrical pods that contain several small, hard seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Lupinus ballianus is a member of the legume family and has the ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it ecologically significant.
