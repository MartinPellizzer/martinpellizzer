Lomatocarpa albomarginata

Lomatocarpa albomarginata, commonly known as the white-margin desert parsley, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as rocky soils and open plains. Its root system is adapted to conserve moisture, allowing it to thrive in drought conditions.

The plant is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.
