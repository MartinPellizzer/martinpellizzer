Leptochloa parviflora

Leptochloa parviflora, commonly known as small-flowered leptochloa, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along waterways. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to white flowers. It is a perennial species that thrives in warm climates and is often used in restoration projects due to its erosion control capabilities.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of wetland and riparian ecosystems.
