Layia pentachaeta var. hansenii

Layia pentachaeta var. hansenii, commonly known as the Hansen's golden aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

This variety is often found in California and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
