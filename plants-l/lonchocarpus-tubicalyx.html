Lonchocarpus tubicalyx

Lonchocarpus tubicalyx, commonly known as the golden tamarind, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant is notable for its striking yellow flowers and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria.

Its seeds are enclosed in a thick, woody pod, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
