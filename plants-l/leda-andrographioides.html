Leda andrographioides

Leda andrographioides, commonly known as Indian borage, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in spikes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Leda andrographioides is also valued for its medicinal properties, used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antipyretic effects.
