Leptospermum myrtifolium

Leptospermum myrtifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tea tree, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a shrub characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of white to pale yellow flowers. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known species like eucalyptus and tea tree. It is often found in heathlands and coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils.

The plant is notable for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
