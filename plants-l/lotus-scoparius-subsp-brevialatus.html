Lotus scoparius subsp. brevialatus

Lotus scoparius subsp.

brevialatus, commonly known as the short-stemmed lotus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking pink to red flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has pinnately compound leaves with serrated margins, and its stems are typically shorter and more robust compared to other lotus species. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
