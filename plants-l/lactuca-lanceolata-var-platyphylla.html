Lactuca lanceolata var. platyphylla

Lactuca lanceolata var. platyphylla, commonly known as the broadleaf lettuce, is a variant of the common lettuce species native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically smooth and slightly wavy in texture. The plant produces small, green flowers that are clustered in a loose head, and it is often cultivated for its edible leaves. It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for both garden and agricultural settings.

This variety is valued for its mild flavor and crisp texture, making it a popular choice in salads and fresh culinary applications.
