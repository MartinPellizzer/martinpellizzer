Lathyrus laevigatus subsp. transsylvanicus

Lathyrus laevigatus subsp.

transsylvanicus, commonly known as the Transylvanian vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Carpathian region. It features slender, climbing stems with trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and rocky habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
