Leptoschoenus prolifer

Leptoschoenus prolifer, commonly known as the swamp reed, is a herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a rosette at the base.

Leptoschoenus prolifer plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
