Laurus rigida

Laurus rigida, commonly known as Mediterranean laurel, is a evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dark green, glossy leaves that are often used in cooking, particularly in the preparation of certain traditional dishes. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible but not commonly consumed. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Due to its aromatic properties, it has been historically valued for both culinary and medicinal uses.
