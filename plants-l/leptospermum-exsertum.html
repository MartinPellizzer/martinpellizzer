Leptospermum exsertum

Leptospermum exsertum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tea tree, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are typically grey-green in color, and it produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in spring. The plant is characterized by its aromatic foliage, which contains essential oils and is used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.

Leptospermum exsertum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
