Laggera viscosa

Laggera viscosa, commonly known as sticky laggera, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a sticky texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming during the warm season. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family.
