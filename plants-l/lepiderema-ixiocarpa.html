Lepiderema ixiocarpa

Lepiderema ixiocarpa, commonly known as the red-spotted nettle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes other stinging nettles. The plant is characterized by its slender stems, opposite leaves with serrated edges, and small greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

The leaves and stems are covered in fine hairs that can cause skin irritation upon contact.
