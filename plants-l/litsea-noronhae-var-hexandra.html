Litsea noronhae var. hexandra

Litsea noronhae var. hexandra, commonly known as the Malabar litsea, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles, followed by elliptical, smooth, and glossy fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
