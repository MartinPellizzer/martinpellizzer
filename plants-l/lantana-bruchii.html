Lantana bruchii

Lantana bruchii, commonly known as the African lantana, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, brightly colored flowers, usually in shades of yellow, orange, or red, which attract pollinators like bees and birds. Its fruits are small, dry capsules that contain numerous seeds, facilitating its spread in suitable habitats.

Lantana bruchii is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in full sun.
