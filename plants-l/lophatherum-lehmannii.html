Lophatherum lehmannii

Lophatherum lehmannii, commonly known as Chinese wild rice, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, slender stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, clustered flowers. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in wetlands and marshy areas.

Lophatherum lehmannii is valued for its ecological role and has been used in traditional medicine.
