Leptodermis velutiniflora var. tenera

Leptodermis velutiniflora var. tenera, commonly known as the velvetleaf *Leptodermis*, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its smooth, often velvety bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody capsules containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

The species is noted for its adaptability and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
