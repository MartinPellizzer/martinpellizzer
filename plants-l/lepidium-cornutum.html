Lepidium cornutum

Lepidium cornutum, commonly known as the horned pepper or horned cress, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like mustard and cabbage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and has leaves that are pinnately divided, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and it is often found growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Lepidium cornutum is also known for its peppery flavor, which makes it a popular herb in some cuisines.
