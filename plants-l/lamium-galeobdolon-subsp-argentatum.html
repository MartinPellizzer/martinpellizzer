Lamium galeobdolon subsp. argentatum

Lamium galeobdolon subsp.

argentatum, commonly known as silver nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It features heart-shaped, silver-velvety leaves with a distinctive netted pattern on the underside.

The plant produces small, pale purple to pink flowers arranged in whorls along the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and garden beds.

Its foliage is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
