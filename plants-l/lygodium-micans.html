Lygodium micans

Lygodium micans, commonly known as the slender climbing fern, is a tropical fern species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds and a unique climbing habit, using rhizomes to anchor itself to trees and other structures. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its leaves, which release spores for reproduction. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
