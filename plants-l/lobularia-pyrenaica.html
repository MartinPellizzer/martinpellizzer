Lobularia pyrenaica

Lobularia pyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean lobularia, is a flowering plant native to the Pyrenees mountain range.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are linear and often covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Lobularia pyrenaica produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
