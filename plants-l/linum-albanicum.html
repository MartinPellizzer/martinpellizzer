Linum albanicum

Linum albanicum, commonly known as the Albanian flax, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is often found in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun.

Due to its ornamental value and historical significance in fiber production, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
