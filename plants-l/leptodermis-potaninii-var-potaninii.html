Leptodermis potaninii var. potaninii

Leptodermis potaninii var. potaninii, commonly known as the Sikkim bellflower, is a herbaceous plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments at elevations between 2,000 and 3,500 meters. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered with fine hairs, while its stems are slender and often branched.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
