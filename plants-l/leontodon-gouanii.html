Leontodon gouanii

Leontodon gouanii, commonly known as the Gouan's hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe and North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are basal, linear, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central dark disc.

Leontodon gouanii is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ornamental value in horticulture.
