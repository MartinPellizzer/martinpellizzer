Lathyrus mulkak

Lathyrus mulkak, commonly known as the Kashmir pea, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Lathyrus mulkak is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
