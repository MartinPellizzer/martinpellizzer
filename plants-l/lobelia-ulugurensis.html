Lobelia ulugurensis

Lobelia ulugurensis, commonly known as the Uluguru lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the Uluguru Mountains in Tanzania.

It is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, which are adapted for pollination by birds and insects. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest understories, thriving in humid and cool environments. Its leaves are opposite and oval-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Lobelia ulugurensis is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological niche and potential medicinal properties.
