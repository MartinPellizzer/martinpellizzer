Lissochilus vaginatus

Lissochilus vaginatus, commonly known as the velvet orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in cooler and drier climates. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in a dense, velvety coating, which helps in reducing water loss.

The orchid is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
