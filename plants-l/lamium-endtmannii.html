Lamium endtmannii

Lamium endtmannii, commonly known as the Endtmann's deadnettle, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and opposite leaves. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in whorls along the stem. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture.

Lamium endtmannii is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
