Lepisorus minor

Lepisorus minor, commonly known as the small wall fern, is a species of fern native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and delicate, lance-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows on rocky surfaces or in shaded, moist environments. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Lepisorus minor is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
