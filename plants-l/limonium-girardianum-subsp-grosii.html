Limonium girardianum subsp. grosii

Limonium girardianum subsp.

grosii, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers and silver-green foliage, which are adapted to coastal environments.

The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Limonium girardianum subsp.

grosii is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
