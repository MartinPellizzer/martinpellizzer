Lepanthes homotaxis

Lepanthes homotaxis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments within montane forests. The plant features a slender, upright stem with several fleshy, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually pale green to white, with a distinctive lip that is often adorned with intricate patterns.

Lepanthes homotaxis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
