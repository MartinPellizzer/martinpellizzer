Lepanthes setifera

Lepanthes setifera, commonly known as the thread-veined orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pale green. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers that are often fragrant, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing in the understory of tropical forests.

Lepanthes setifera is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological significance.
