Linaria Origanifolia Subsp. Euoriganifolia

Orangeflower, scientifically known as Linaria origanifolia subsp. euoriganifolia, is a strikingly beautiful flowering plant native to the western United States. This member of the Scrophulariaceae family is celebrated for its vibrant orange-yellow blooms that resemble tiny lanterns. Its unique appearance and ecological significance make it a popular subject among botanists and gardeners alike. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats. With its delicate petals and adaptability, Orangeflower plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and biodiversity.
