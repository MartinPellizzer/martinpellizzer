Ligustrum myrsinites

Ligustrum myrsinites, commonly known as the silverleaf privet, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are typically silvery-white underneath, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, followed by dark blue berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

Ligustrum myrsinites is also known for its ability to tolerate a variety of soil types and environmental conditions.
