Litsea polyantha f. glabra

Litsea polyantha f. glabra, commonly known as the smooth litsea, is a flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous bark and lanceolate leaves that are typically glossy green. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers clustered in panicles, which are followed by elliptical, red-brown berries. Native to Southeast Asia, it thrives in humid, shaded environments within tropical and subtropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value and aromatic properties, it is sometimes cultivated in horticulture.
