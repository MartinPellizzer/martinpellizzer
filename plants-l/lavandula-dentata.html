Lavandula dentata

Lavandula dentata, commonly known as woolly lavender, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its gray-green, fuzzy leaves and spikes of purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is well adapted to dry, sunny conditions and thrives in well-drained soil. Its aromatic oils, found in the flowers and leaves, are widely used in perfumes and aromatherapy.

Lavandula dentata is also valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
