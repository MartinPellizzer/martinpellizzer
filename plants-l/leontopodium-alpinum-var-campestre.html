Leontopodium alpinum var. campestre

Leontopodium alpinum var. campestre, commonly known as the alpine edelweiss, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly white bracts that surround the small yellow flowers, giving it a distinctive and fluffy appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool and sunny conditions. Its foliage consists of basal leaves that are spoon-shaped and covered in a thick layer of fine hairs.

This variety is closely related to other members of the Leontopodium genus, which are all known for their unique and showy floral structures.
