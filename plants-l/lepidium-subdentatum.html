Lepidium subdentatum

Lepidium subdentatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wallcress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, pinnately divided leaves and clusters of tiny white or yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or disturbed soils and is native to Europe and parts of Asia. It produces small, round seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Lepidium subdentatum is often found in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.
