Leea pinnata

Leea pinnata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed leea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the family Leeaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical or lance-shaped. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Leea pinnata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
