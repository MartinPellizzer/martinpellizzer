Lupinus albicaulis var. shastensis

Lupinus albicaulis var. shastensis, commonly known as Shasta lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its white to pale blue flowers and hairy stems, which are typical of the lupine genus. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance as a food source for pollinators and wildlife.
