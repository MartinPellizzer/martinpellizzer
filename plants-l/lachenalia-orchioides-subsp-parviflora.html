Lachenalia orchioides subsp. parviflora

Lachenalia orchioides subsp.

parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered lachenalia, is a species native to southern Africa. It is a bulbous geophyte with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers.

The flowers are typically pale yellow to orange in color, and the plant is adapted to arid and rocky habitats. It blooms in spring and is often found in rocky outcrops and sandy soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
