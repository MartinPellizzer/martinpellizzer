Lonicera simulatrix

Lonicera simulatrix, commonly known as the false honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves. Its red or orange berries are an important food source for birds and small mammals.

Lonicera simulatrix is often mistaken for true honeysuckles due to its similar appearance, but it lacks the strong, sweet scent associated with the genus Lonicera.
