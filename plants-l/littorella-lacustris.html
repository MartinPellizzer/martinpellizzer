Littorella lacustris

Littorella lacustris, commonly known as the lake shore plant, is a halophytic species that thrives in brackish and freshwater environments.

It is a member of the plantain family (Plantaginaceae) and is characterized by its rosette-like growth pattern. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically greenish or yellowish in color. It has a fibrous root system that helps it anchor in muddy or sandy substrates.

Littorella lacustris is often found in coastal marshes, estuaries, and shallow lakes, where it plays a role in stabilizing shorelines and supporting aquatic ecosystems.
