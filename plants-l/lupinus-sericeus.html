Lupinus sericeus

Lupinus sericeus, commonly known as silky lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of soft, silvery-blue flowers. The leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets with a silky texture. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in some ecological restoration projects.
