Leucas coleae

Leucas coleae, commonly known as the blue leucas, is a flowering plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. Its leaves are typically narrow and opposite, often with a slight pubescence.

Leucas coleae is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops.
