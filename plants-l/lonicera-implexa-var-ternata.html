Lonicera implexa var. ternata

Lonicera implexa var. ternata, commonly known as the Japanese honeysuckle, is a climbing vine native to East Asia.

It features opposite, oval-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a red, fleshy capsule that matures in the fall.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value but can become invasive in certain regions.
