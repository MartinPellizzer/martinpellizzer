Lonicera caucasica

Lonicera caucasica, commonly known as Caucasian honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It features opposite, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to yellow, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its fruits are red, fleshy berries that mature in late summer and are edible for birds and some mammals.

Lonicera caucasica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
