Loranthus stemmato-beccarii

Loranthus stemmato-beccarii, commonly known as the African mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily in the genus *Acacia*.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using the light it captures. The plant has a smooth, woody stem with small, scale-like leaves that are often hidden beneath its dense, fleshy berries. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, with white or pink petals, and it produces a sticky substance to aid in seed dispersal by birds.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitats by influencing host tree growth and providing food for various wildlife species.
