Lachnaea grandiflora

Lachnaea grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered lachnaea, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered inflorescences that produce small, tubular flowers in shades of pink to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Lachnaea grandiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
