Lepanthes × castillo-montii

Lepanthes × castillo-montii, commonly known as the Castillo-Monti orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, often fragrant flowers with a distinctive coloration pattern. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical rainforests. Its foliage consists of narrow, strap-like leaves that are adapted for efficient water absorption.

This hybrid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and rarity.
