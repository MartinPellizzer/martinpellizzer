Lupinus elongatus

Lupinus elongatus, commonly known as the slender lupin, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of pea-like flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats.

Lupinus elongatus is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
