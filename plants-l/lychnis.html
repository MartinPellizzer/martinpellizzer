Lychnis

Lychnis, commonly known as bloody cranesbill, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Caryophyllaceae.

These plants are characterized by their showy, often pink or red flowers and hairy stems. They are native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and many species are found in moist or woodland habitats. Lychnis species typically have basal leaves that are lance-shaped or ovate, and their flowers are usually arranged in clusters.

The genus includes both annual and perennial species, some of which are cultivated for their ornamental value.
