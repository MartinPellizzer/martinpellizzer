Lathyrus gmelinii var. cirrhosus

Lathyrus gmelinii var. cirrhosus, commonly known as the curly-stemmed grass vetch, is a perennial climbing legume native to Central Asia.

It features slender, wiry stems that are often covered in fine, curly hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom in late spring. Its trifoliate leaves are typically heart-shaped with a hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.
