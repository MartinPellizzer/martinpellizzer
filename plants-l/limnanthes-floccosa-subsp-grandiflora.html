Limnanthes floccosa subsp. grandiflora

Limnanthes floccosa subsp.

grandiflora, commonly known as the giant swamp milkweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States. It features showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The plant has soft, fuzzy leaves and grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes and pond edges. It is adapted to tolerate waterlogged soils and has a shallow root system.

Its fibrous roots and hairy foliage are distinctive botanical characteristics.
