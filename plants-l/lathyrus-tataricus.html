Lathyrus tataricus

Lathyrus tataricus, commonly known as the Tatarian vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It is a perennial legume that produces striking blue or violet pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Lathyrus tataricus is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting other plants in the vicinity.
