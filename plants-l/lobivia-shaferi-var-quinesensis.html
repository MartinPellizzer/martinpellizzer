Lobivia shaferi var. quinesensis

Lobivia shaferi var. quinesensis, commonly known as the Quinesensis Lobivia, is a succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It features a compact, rounded rosette of fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder, known as farina. The plant produces tall, branching stems that bear clusters of small, yellow flowers during the spring and early summer. It is well adapted to drought conditions, storing water in its thick leaves and stems.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is a sought-after specimen among succulent enthusiasts.
