Lomagramma leucolepis

Lomagramma leucolepis, commonly known as the white-stemmed rock fern, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family.

It is a small, epiphytic fern that typically grows on rocks or trees in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has delicate, linear leaves that are usually bright green with a distinctive white or pale underside. Its rhizomes are wiry and help it anchor to its substrate.

Lomagramma leucolepis is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in shaded, humid environments.
