Lobivia andalgalensis

Lobivia andalgalensis, commonly known as the Andalusian lobivia, is a succulent native to southern Spain.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rounded shape and thick, fleshy stems. The plant features small, spiny areoles and produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Lobivia andalgalensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh climates.
