Lepidium lasiocarpum subsp. lasiocarpum

Lepidium lasiocarpum subsp.

lasiocarpum, commonly known as the roughpod wallflower, is a flowering plant native to North America. It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release the seeds.

It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in disturbed soils.
