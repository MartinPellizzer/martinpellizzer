Lilium philadelphicum var. pulchrum

Lilium philadelphicum var. pulchrum, commonly known as the showy lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink to red in color, with six to eight petals. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves that are arranged in a fan-like pattern. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The bulbs of this plant are used in traditional medicine and are also cultivated for ornamental purposes.
