Lathyrus ochrus var. ochroides

Lathyrus ochrus var. ochroides, commonly known as the golden pea or yellow vetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, and its seed pods are slender and contain several small, brown seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas, along roadsides, and in dry grasslands.
