Leptactina mannii

Leptactina mannii, commonly known as the African milk tree, is a shrub native to the savannas of sub-Saharan Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, making it drought-resistant. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers and has milky sap that can cause skin irritation. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Leptactina mannii is also valued for its traditional uses in medicine and as a source of fiber.
