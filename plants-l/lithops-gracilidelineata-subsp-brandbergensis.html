Lithops gracilidelineata subsp. brandbergensis

Lithops gracilidelineata subsp.

brandbergensis, commonly known as the Brandberg's narrow-line lithops, is a species of succulent native to Namibia and South Africa. It is characterized by its pair of fleshy, triangular leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder, known as farina.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
