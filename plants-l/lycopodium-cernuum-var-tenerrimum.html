Lycopodium cernuum var. tenerrimum

Lycopodium cernuum var. tenerrimum, commonly known as the drooping clubmoss, is a variant of the clubmoss plant native to North America.

It is a low-growing, evergreen herb that typically forms dense mats on the forest floor. The plant features small, scale-like leaves and produces spore-bearing structures called sporophytes. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Botanically, it is characterized by its unique morphology and reproductive structures typical of the Lycopodiaceae family.
