Lathyrus litvinovii

Lathyrus litvinovii, commonly known as the Litvinov's vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in Kazakhstan and Kyrgyzstan. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, pea-like flowers that bloom in shades of purple and pink. Its leaves are pinnately divided, and it produces elongated pods that contain several seeds.

Lathyrus litvinovii is often found in rocky or semi-arid habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
