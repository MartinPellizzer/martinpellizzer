Linum kingii var. sedoides

Linum kingii var. sedoides, commonly known as the coastal flax, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along the shoreline. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom from late winter to early spring. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and it has a fibrous root system adapted to coastal conditions.

Linum kingii var. sedoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize dunes and prevent erosion.
