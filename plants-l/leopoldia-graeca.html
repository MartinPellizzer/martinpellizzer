Leopoldia graeca

Leopoldia graeca, commonly known as the Greek leopoldia, is a rare and striking flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Greece.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, spurred petals and vibrant, often purple or violet, flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its delicate appearance contrasts with its resilience, making it a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists.
