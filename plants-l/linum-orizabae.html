Linum orizabae

Linum orizabae, commonly known as the Mexican flax, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and blue to purple flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. Linum orizabae is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.

It is primarily cultivated for its ornamental value and has limited use in traditional medicine.
