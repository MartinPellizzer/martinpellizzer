Lonchocarpus kanurii

Lonchocarpus kanurii, commonly known as the Kanurii or African senna, is a leguminous plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural systems.

However, some parts of the plant contain toxic alkaloids, requiring caution in handling and use.
