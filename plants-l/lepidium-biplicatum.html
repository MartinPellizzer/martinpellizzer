Lepidium biplicatum

Lepidium biplicatum, commonly known as the double-leafed cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, white or yellowish flowers and pinnately compound leaves that are often folded or pleated. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments and is found in parts of Europe, Asia, and North America. Its seeds are small and contain compounds that have been studied for potential medicinal properties.

Lepidium biplicatum is often used in herbal medicine for its purported diuretic and anti-inflammatory effects.
