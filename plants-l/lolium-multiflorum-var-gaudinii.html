Lolium multiflorum var. gaudinii

Lolium multiflorum var. gaudinii, commonly known as Gaudin's ryegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and bright green foliage. The plant produces spikelets that are typically two-ranked, with several florets per spikelet. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.

Gaudin's ryegrass is valued for its forage quality and is used in agricultural settings for livestock grazing.
