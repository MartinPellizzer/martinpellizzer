Linaria caesia var. broteri

Linaria caesia var. broteri, commonly known as the broteri toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are linear and slightly hairy, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
