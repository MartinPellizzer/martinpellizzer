Lissochilus krebsii var. purpuratus

Lissochilus krebsii var. purpuratus, commonly known as the purple-flowered bell orchid, is a species of orchid native to the high Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from deep purple to violet. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming dense clumps.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
