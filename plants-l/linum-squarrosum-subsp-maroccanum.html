Linum squarrosum subsp. maroccanum

Linum squarrosum subsp.

maroccanum, commonly known as the Moroccan flax, is a flowering plant native to North Africa. It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, pale blue or white flowers.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens in regions with a Mediterranean climate.
