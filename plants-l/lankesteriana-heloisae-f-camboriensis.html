Lankesteriana heloisae f. camboriensis

Lankesteriana heloisae f. camboriensis, commonly known as the Camboriensis lankesteriana, is a rare and striking orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

This hybrid orchid is characterized by its large, showy flowers with intricate patterns and vibrant colors, often featuring a mix of purple, pink, and white. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments at high altitudes, where it thrives in the cool, misty conditions of cloud forests. Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and limited distribution, making it a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts.

Due to habitat destruction and limited natural propagation, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
