Laelia × triophthalma

Laelia × triophthalma, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a naturally occurring hybrid between Laelia purpurea and Laelia tatei.

It is a terrestrial orchid native to the high Andes of South America, where it thrives in cool, humid environments. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers with a distinctive three-lobed lip, which is a key feature in its classification. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a hybrid that demonstrates the genetic diversity within the Laelia genus.
