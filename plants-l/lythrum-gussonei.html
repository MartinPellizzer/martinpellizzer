Lythrum gussonei

Lythrum gussonei, commonly known as the narrowleaf lythrum, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lythraceae family, which includes other plants such as the poinsettia and the persimmon. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and spikes of vibrant purple or pink flowers. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist, acidic soils.
