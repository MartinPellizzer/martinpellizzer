Leptaloe myriacantha

Leptaloe myriacantha, commonly known as the many-thorned milkweed, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its branched stems and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that is toxic to some animals, including livestock. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in arid or semi-arid environments.

Leptaloe myriacantha is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
