Liabum melastomoides

Liabum melastomoides, commonly known as the pink-stemmed melastome, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Melastomataceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers with five petals, typically in shades of pink or white, which bloom during the wet season. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and is often found in humid, tropical climates.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
