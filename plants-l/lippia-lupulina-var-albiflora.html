Lippia lupulina var. albiflora

Lippia lupulina var. albiflora, commonly known as whiteflower lippia, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, serrated leaves and showy white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its flowers are borne in dense clusters and are often visited by pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.
