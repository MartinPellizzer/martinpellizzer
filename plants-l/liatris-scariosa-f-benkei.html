Liatris scariosa f. benkei

Liatris scariosa f. benkei, commonly known as the Benkei blazing star, is a variant of the blazing star genus native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and showy, spike-like inflorescences. The flowers are typically purple to pink in color, with narrow, grass-like bracts that give the plant its distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
