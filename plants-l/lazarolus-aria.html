Lazarolus aria

Lazarolus aria, commonly known as the silverleaf orchid, is a striking plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, pale green to silver-white flowers. The plant's leaves are narrow and covered in a fine, silvery down, which helps reflect sunlight and reduce water loss. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
