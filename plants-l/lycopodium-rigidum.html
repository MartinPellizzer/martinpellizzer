Lycopodium rigidum

Lycopodium rigidum, commonly known as the clubmoss, is a primitive vascular plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and a sporophyte structure that consists of a stem with whorls of leaves. The plant produces spores rather than seeds, and its reproductive structures are called strobila, which are cone-like formations. Lycopodium rigidum typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
