Limnanthemum calycinum

Limnanthemum calycinum, commonly known as the golden star or starflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is a low-growing herbaceous plant with bright yellow flowers that resemble small stars, typically blooming in clusters. The plant features simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded habitats.

Limnanthemum calycinum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
