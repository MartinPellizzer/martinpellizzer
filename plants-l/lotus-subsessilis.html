Lotus subsessilis

Lotus subsessilis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved water lily, is a species native to parts of Asia, including China and India.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a waxy surface that repels water.

The flowers are usually pink to white, with a central yellow stamen, and are pollinated by insects.
