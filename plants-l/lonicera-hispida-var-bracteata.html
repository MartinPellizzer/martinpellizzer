Lonicera hispida var. bracteata

Lonicera hispida var. bracteata, commonly known as the bracteate honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Asia.

It features opposite, oval-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its berries are red or orange and are edible, though often used in traditional medicine.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
