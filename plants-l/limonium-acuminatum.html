Limonium acuminatum

Limonium acuminatum, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and spikes of blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in saline and well-drained soils, often found in dunes and coastal grasslands. Its flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Limonium acuminatum is valued for its ornamental use in gardens and its ability to stabilize coastal soils.
