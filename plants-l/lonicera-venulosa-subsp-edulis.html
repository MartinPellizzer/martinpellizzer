Lonicera venulosa subsp. edulis

Lonicera venulosa subsp.

edulis, commonly known as the edible honeysuckle, is a shrub native to eastern Asia, particularly China. It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant produces red berries that are edible for humans and some bird species. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a glossy texture.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of food in traditional cuisines.
