Lateropora santafeensis

Lateropora santafeensis, commonly known as the Santa Fe sponge coral, is a marine species belonging to the family Dendrophylliidae.

It is characterized by its calcareous skeleton and colonial growth form, with polyps arranged in a distinctive pattern. The coral exhibits a symbiotic relationship with zooxanthellae, which contribute to its coloration and provide energy through photosynthesis. It thrives in shallow, warm waters and is often found in reef environments.

Due to its ecological significance, it plays a vital role in coral reef ecosystems.
