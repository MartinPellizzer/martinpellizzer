Ligularia parvifolia

Ligularia parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved ligularia, is a herbaceous perennial native to East Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive yellowish-green color and a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a panicle, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found near streams or in woodland areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a shade-tolerant plant with unique foliage.
