Lathyrus spectabilis

Lathyrus spectabilis, commonly known as the showy larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmately divided leaves and produces long, slender pods that contain small, black seeds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Lathyrus spectabilis is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
