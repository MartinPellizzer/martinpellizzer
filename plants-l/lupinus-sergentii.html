Lupinus sergentii

Lupinus sergentii, commonly known as the desert lupine, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds.

Lupinus sergentii is adapted to arid environments and plays a role in nitrogen fixation, improving soil fertility in its native habitat.
