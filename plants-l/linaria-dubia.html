Linaria dubia

Linaria dubia, commonly known as the common toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant that typically grows up to 60 cm tall, featuring upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy yellow or orange flowers with two lips, typical of the Scrophulariaceae family. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and wastelands.
