Lupinus Sulphureus Subsp. Whithamii

Lupinus sulphureus subsp. whithamii is a rare and striking plant native to the western United States. Known for its bright yellow flowers and distinctive foliage, it thrives in dry, rocky habitats. This subspecies is closely related to other lupine species but is distinguished by its unique coloration and growth patterns. Due to its limited range and habitat specificity, it is considered endangered. Conservation efforts are crucial to protect this beautiful and ecologically important plant.
