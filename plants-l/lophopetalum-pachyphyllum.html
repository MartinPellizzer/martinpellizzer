Lophopetalum pachyphyllum

Lophopetalum pachyphyllum, commonly known as the thickleaf hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are usually dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by edible berries that are often used in jams and jellies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and open woods.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
