Linaria balansae

Linaria balansae, commonly known as the Balansae toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats. The plant produces striking yellow to orange flowers in late spring to early summer, which are borne in racemes along the stems. Its leaves are linear to lanceolate, often with a slightly hairy texture, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in harsh, arid conditions.

Linaria balansae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
