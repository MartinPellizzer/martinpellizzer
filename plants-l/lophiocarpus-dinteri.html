Lophiocarpus dinteri

Lophiocarpus dinteri, commonly known as the Dinter's legume, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are bipinnate, meaning they are divided into smaller leaflets, and it produces small, yellowish flowers.

The fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.
