Loranthus myrsinifolius

Loranthus myrsinifolius, commonly known as the mistletoe of the genus Loranthus, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to host trees through its aerial roots.

It belongs to the family Loranthaceae and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for nutrients, while still performing some photosynthesis. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in Southeast Asia.

Its ecological role includes providing food for birds and insects, which help in seed dispersal.
