Liabum andrieuxii

Liabum andrieuxii, commonly known as the Andrieux's liabum, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. Its root system is notable for nitrogen-fixing capabilities, contributing to soil fertility.

Despite its ecological significance, it remains a relatively obscure species within botanical studies.
