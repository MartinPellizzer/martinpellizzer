Linaria munbyana var. leiosperma

Linaria munbyana var. leiosperma, commonly known as the smooth-flowered toadflax, is a perennial flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers. The leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a smooth margin, and the plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds, aiding in its propagation.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
