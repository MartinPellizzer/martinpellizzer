Lysiloma guachapele

Lysiloma guachapele, commonly known as the Guachapele tree, is a species native to Central America, particularly found in countries like Guatemala and Honduras.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall, featuring a straight trunk and a broad, rounded canopy. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters.

The fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind and animals.
