Laportea ternatense

Laportea ternatense, commonly known as the Java false nettle, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Laportea ternatense thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
