Lepanthes ferrelliae

Lepanthes ferrelliae, commonly known as the Ferrell's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Costa Rica.

It is characterized by its small, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pale pink. The plant has a compact growth habit with a short, sturdy stem and produces multiple blooms during the rainy season. Its leaves are dark green and ovate, often with a waxy texture adapted to the humid, shaded environments it inhabits.

Lepanthes ferrelliae is a rare and endangered species, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts in its native habitat.
