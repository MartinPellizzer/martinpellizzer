Lepanthes orbella

Lepanthes orbella, commonly known as the orbella orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid with a delicate, nodding flower that typically features white or pale pink petals. The plant has a short, fleshy rhizome and produces showy, fragrant blooms during the rainy season. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a rosette pattern.

Lepanthes orbella is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
