Licuala rotundifolia

Licuala rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaf pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family, characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant produces large, fragrant inflorescences that attract pollinators such as birds and insects. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and for thatching.

The plant thrives in humid, coastal environments and is adapted to salt spray and sandy soils.
