Litsea zeylanica var. venosa

Litsea zeylanica var. venosa, commonly known as the veined litsea, is a variant of the litsea zeylanica species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub characterized by its fragrant, aromatic leaves. The leaves are typically ovate or elliptic, with a distinctive veining pattern that gives the plant its common name. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers followed by small, round berries.

This plant is often valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
