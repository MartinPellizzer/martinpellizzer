Lepanthes dikoensis

Lepanthes dikoensis, commonly known as the Dikoense Orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist forest understories. The plant features a short, rhizomatous stem with a single, lance-shaped leaf. Its flowers are delicate, usually white or pale pink, with a distinctive lip that is often darker in color.

Lepanthes dikoensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
