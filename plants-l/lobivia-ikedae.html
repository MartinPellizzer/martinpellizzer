Lobivia ikedae

Lobivia ikedae, commonly known as the Ikedae lobivia, is a species of flowering plant native to the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It is a low-growing, succulent plant with thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are adapted to store water. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers during the cooler months. It is notable for its ability to thrive in extremely arid conditions, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens.

Its unique appearance and resilience make it a popular plant among cactus enthusiasts.
