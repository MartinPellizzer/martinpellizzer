Lupinus peruvianus var. spragueanus

Lupinus peruvianus var. spragueanus, commonly known as the Peruvian lupine, is a perennial legume native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and clusters of bright blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has narrow, serrated leaves and produces elongated pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or mountainous terrain.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in nitrogen fixation in soil.
