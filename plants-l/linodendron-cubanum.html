Linodendron cubanum

Linodendron cubanum, commonly known as the Cuban yellowwood, is a small evergreen tree native to Cuba.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
