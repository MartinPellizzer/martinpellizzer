Lepanthes plurifolia

Lepanthes plurifolia, commonly known as the many-flowered orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as cloud forests and rainforests. Its leaves are usually broad and lance-shaped, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers with a distinctive lip.

Lepanthes plurifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its native habitats.
