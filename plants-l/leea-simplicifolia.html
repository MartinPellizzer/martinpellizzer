Leea simplicifolia

Leea simplicifolia, commonly known as the simple-leafed leeah, is a tropical shrub native to regions of Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the family Leeaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by small, berry-like fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in coastal forests or disturbed sites.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
