Lepidium coronopifolium f. puberulum

Lepidium coronopifolium f. puberulum, commonly known as the hairy heartleaf peppergrass, is a botanical variant of the species Lepidium coronopifolium.

It is a flowering plant native to North America, particularly found in the western United States. The plant is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a pubescent appearance. It produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early spring.

This subspecies is typically found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks.
