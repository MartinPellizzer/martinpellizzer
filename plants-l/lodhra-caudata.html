Lodhra caudata

Lodhra caudata, commonly known as the long-stemmed ash tree, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, round fruits. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates.
