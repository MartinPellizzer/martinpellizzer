Limonium chrysocomum subsp. semenovii

Limonium chrysocomum subsp.

semenovii, commonly known as the Semenov's sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, typically on slender, upright stems.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

This subspecies is noted for its hardiness and ability to withstand saline conditions.
