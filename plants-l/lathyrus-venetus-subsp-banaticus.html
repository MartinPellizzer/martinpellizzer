Lathyrus venetus subsp. banaticus

Lathyrus venetus subsp.

banaticus, commonly known as the Banat vetch, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Europe. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in grasslands and disturbed soils.

The plant features slender, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers that are usually violet or purple in color. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blossoms.
