Lupinus missionum

Lupinus missionum, commonly known as Mission lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal scrub and grassland habitats.

Lupinus missionum plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
