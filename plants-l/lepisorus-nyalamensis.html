Lepisorus nyalamensis

Lepisorus nyalamensis, commonly known as the Nyalam polypody, is a fern species native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal and India.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizome is creeping and produces numerous short, upright shoots.

Lepisorus nyalamensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shady garden settings.
