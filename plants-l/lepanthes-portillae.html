Lepanthes portillae

Lepanthes portillae, commonly known as the Portilla's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the humid forests of Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically feature vibrant colors such as red, orange, and yellow. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and has specialized roots that help it absorb nutrients from the forest floor. Its flowers are often short-lived but attract various pollinators, including bees and hummingbirds.

Lepanthes portillae is a rare and endangered species, making conservation efforts crucial for its survival.
