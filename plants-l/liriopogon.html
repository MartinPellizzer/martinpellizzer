Liriopogon

Liriopogon, commonly known as the purple lily of the Nile, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Iridaceae.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its striking, often violet or purple, tubular flowers. The plants typically grow in wet, swampy areas and have long, narrow leaves that form a rosette. Liriopogon species are known for their ornamental value and are sometimes cultivated in gardens for their vibrant blooms.

They are also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
