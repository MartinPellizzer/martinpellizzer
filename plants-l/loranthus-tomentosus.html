Loranthus tomentosus

Loranthus tomentosus, commonly known as the silky mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that attaches itself to the branches of host trees, primarily in the genus Eucalyptus.

It lacks true roots and absorbs water and nutrients through its leaves, which are arranged in a distinctive spiral pattern. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that are often clustered together, followed by small, fleshy berries. Its leaves are covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs, giving it a silky appearance.

Loranthus tomentosus plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for birds and insects, despite its parasitic nature.
