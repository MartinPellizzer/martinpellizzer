Lobelia palmaris

Lobelia palmaris, commonly known as the palmate lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its palmately lobed leaves, which give it its common name, and its spikes of tubular blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It is a member of the Campanulaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like bellflowers.

Lobelia palmaris is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
