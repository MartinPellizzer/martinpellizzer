Lamium garganicum subsp. longiflorum

Lamium garganicum subsp.

longiflorum, commonly known as the long-flowered deadnettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces clusters of pale pink to lilac flowers with two lips. Its flowers are borne on erect, slender stems and are visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
