Lespedeza × maruyamana

Lespedeza × maruyamana, commonly known as the Japanese clover.

It is a hybrid plant belonging to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is native to East Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Lespedeza × maruyamana is also valued for its role in erosion control and as a forage plant for livestock.
