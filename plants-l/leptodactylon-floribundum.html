Leptodactylon floribundum

Leptodactylon floribundum, commonly known as the clustered mossy fern, is a species of flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, clustered growth habit and its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or brown in color.

Leptodactylon floribundum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to form lush, compact ground covers.
