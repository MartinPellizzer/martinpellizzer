Lycium spencerae

Lycium spencerae, commonly known as Spencerae boxthorn, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Australia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its woody stems and spiny foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically followed by red, fleshy berries. It is adapted to harsh environments, showing resistance to drought and poor soil conditions.

Lycium spencerae plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and stabilizing sandy soils.
