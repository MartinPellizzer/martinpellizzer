Linum berlandieri var. filifolium

Linum berlandieri var. filifolium, commonly known as the filifolium flax, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open woodlands. Its fibrous stems and leaves were historically used by indigenous peoples for textile production.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
