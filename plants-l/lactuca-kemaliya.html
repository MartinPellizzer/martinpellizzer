Lactuca kemaliya

Lactuca kemaliya, commonly known as Turkish lettuce or Persian lettuce, is a leafy plant native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its rosette growth pattern. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, followed by seeds. Its leaves are typically broad, slightly hairy, and have a mild, slightly bitter taste.

It is often cultivated for its edible leaves and is valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
