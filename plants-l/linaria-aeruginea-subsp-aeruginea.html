Linaria aeruginea subsp. aeruginea

Linaria aeruginea subsp.

aeruginea, commonly known as the silverweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with silver-gray foliage that gives it its common name.

The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Linaria aeruginea subsp.

aeruginea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
