Lycopodium erythrocaulon

Lycopodium erythrocaulon, commonly known as the red-stem clubmoss, is a small, evergreen plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, reddish stems and small, scale-like leaves that form dense, carpet-like growths. The plant produces spore-bearing structures called sporophytes, which are typically clustered at the base of the stems. Lycopodium erythrocaulon thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas.

Its unique morphology and reproductive structures make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
