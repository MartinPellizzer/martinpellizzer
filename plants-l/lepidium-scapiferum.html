Lepidium scapiferum

Lepidium scapiferum, commonly known as the slender cress, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like mustard and cabbage. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, streambanks, and along roadsides. It has small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, and its leaves are alternate, linear, and often hairy.

The fruit is a small, dry pod that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
