Lepanthes concinna var. obtusipetala

Lepanthes concinna var. obtusipetala, commonly known as the obtuse-peduncled flame orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery red flowers with intricate patterns and a compact growth habit. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in the understory of tropical forests. Its leaves are broad and elliptical, providing a lush green backdrop to its vibrant blooms.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
