Liatris lancifolia

Liatris lancifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf blazingstar, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its spires of purple or pink tubular flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy flower heads that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Liatris lancifolia is a perennial herb that plays an important role in supporting local ecosystems and is valued in native plant gardens.
