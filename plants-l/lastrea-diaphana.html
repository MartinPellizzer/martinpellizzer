Lastrea diaphana

Lastrea diaphana, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a rare and delicate succulent native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its translucent, waxy leaves that resemble ghostly apparitions, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white and pale pink, which bloom during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited to xerophytic environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Lastrea diaphana is considered a vulnerable species.
