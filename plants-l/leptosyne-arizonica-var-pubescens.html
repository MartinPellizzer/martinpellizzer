Leptosyne arizonica var. pubescens

Leptosyne arizonica var. pubescens, commonly known as the Arizona hairysage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and yellow flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, demonstrating adaptability to harsh conditions. Its small, daisy-like flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is noted for its aromatic foliage and is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats.
