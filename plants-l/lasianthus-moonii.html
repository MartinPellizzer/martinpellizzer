Lasianthus moonii

Lasianthus moonii, commonly known as the Moon's Lantern, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy bracts that resemble small lanterns. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its flowers are usually pink or red, with a distinctive two-lipped structure, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
