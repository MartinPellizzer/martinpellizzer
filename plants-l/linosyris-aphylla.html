Linosyris aphylla

Linosyris aphylla, commonly known as the leafless flax, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, herbaceous annual that grows in sandy or rocky soils. The plant is characterized by its absence of leaves, with only a few bracts present. It produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring.

Its unique morphology makes it an interesting subject for botanical study and ecological research.
