Leucanthemopsis alpina subsp. cuneifolia

Leucanthemopsis alpina subsp.

cuneifolia, commonly known as the alpine leucanthemopsis, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit.

The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in a single head, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, which is reflected in its subspecies name *cuneifolia*.

It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and mountainous regions.
