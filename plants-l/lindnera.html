Lindnera

Lindnera, commonly known as the mountain holly, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Ericaceae.

It consists of several species, primarily found in the mountainous regions of South America. These plants are evergreen shrubs with small, leathery leaves that are often arranged in whorls. The flowers are typically white or pink and are clustered in racemes, adding ornamental value to the plant.

Lindnera species are valued for their hardiness and ability to thrive in acidic, well-drained soils.
