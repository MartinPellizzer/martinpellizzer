Linum capitatum subsp. serrulatum

Linum capitatum subsp.

serrulatum, commonly known as the serrated flax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers arranged in a dense, spike-like cluster at the top of the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in textile production.
