Limonium helenae

Limonium helenae, commonly known as the Greek sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal and saline environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Limonium helenae is part of the Plumbagaceae family, which includes other salt-tolerant species.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
