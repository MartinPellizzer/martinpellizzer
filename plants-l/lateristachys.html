Lateristachys

Lateristachys, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a genus of flowering plants in the Apocynaceae family.

It is native to various regions in Africa and is characterized by its showy, often pink or red flowers. The plants typically have opposite, ovate leaves and produce a milky sap that can be toxic. Many species within this genus are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

They also serve as a food source for certain butterfly species, contributing to local biodiversity.
