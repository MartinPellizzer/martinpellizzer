Lychnis affinis

Lychnis affinis, commonly known as the common sand-verbena, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, pink or red flowers. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped. It thrives in sandy or well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and botanical gardens.
