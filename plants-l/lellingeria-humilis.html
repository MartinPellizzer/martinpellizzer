Lellingeria humilis

Lellingeria humilis, commonly known as the dwarf lellingeria, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to South America.

It typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and wetlands. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves that are usually arranged in a basal rosette. Its flowers are inconspicuous, consisting of small, greenish-white blooms that appear in clusters.

Lellingeria humilis is known for its ability to form dense mats, which helps in soil stabilization and moisture retention.
