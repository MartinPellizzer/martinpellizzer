Limodorum venosum

Limodorum venosum, commonly known as the veiny lily of the valley, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters at the top of its slender stems. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often veined, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Limodorum venosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
