Leandra purpurascens var. ovata

Leandra purpurascens var. ovata, commonly known as the pink-veined orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to parts of Central and South America.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant pink and green foliage with distinctive veining. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom during the warm season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for collectors and horticulturists.
