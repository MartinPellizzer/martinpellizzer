Limonium confusum proles laxissimum

Limonium confusum proles laxissimum, commonly known as the looseflower sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss in saline environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dunes or along the edges of salt marshes.

Limonium confusum proles laxissimum is valued in horticulture for its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
