Lupinus microcarpus var. densiflorus

Lupinus microcarpus var. densiflorus, commonly known as the dense-flowered lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces elongated, cylindrical pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and inland regions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in nitrogen fixation in soil.
