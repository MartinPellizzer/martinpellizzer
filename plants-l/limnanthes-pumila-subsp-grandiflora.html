Limnanthes pumila subsp. grandiflora

Limnanthes pumila subsp.

grandiflora, commonly known as the giant marsh plant or California marsh plant, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and pond edges.

The plant features showy yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in moist environments.
