Lemurella culicifera

Lemurella culicifera, commonly known as the mosquito vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in ornamental gardening.

However, it can become invasive in certain environments if not properly managed.
