Lilium brownii var. australe

Lilium brownii var. australe, commonly known as the Australian white lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It features showy, white to pale yellow flowers with six petals and a prominent central stamen. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in spring and are often found in open woodlands or grassy areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
