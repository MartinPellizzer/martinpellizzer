Linaria arvensis subsp. micrantha

Linaria arvensis subsp.

micrantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved toadflax, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species characterized by its slender stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves.

The flowers are typically yellow or orange, with two lips, and are arranged in spikes along the stem. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

The plant is known for its ability to hybridize with other Linaria species, contributing to its variability in morphology and distribution.
