Leea expansa

Leea expansa, commonly known as the broadleaf leea, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the family Leeaceae, characterized by its simple, alternate leaves. The leaves are usually broad and elliptical, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, spherical fruits.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along riverbanks.
