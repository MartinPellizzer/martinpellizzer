Lasianthaea fruticosa var. villosa

Lasianthaea fruticosa var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy lasianthaea, is a shrub native to certain regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant has woody stems that become more branched with age, and its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or sandy habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
