Lobelia cavanillesiana

Lobelia cavanillesiana, commonly known as the mountain lobelia, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with slender stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

Lobelia cavanillesiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
