Leucocroton leprosus

Leucocroton leprosus, commonly known as the ghost tree, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking white, waxy flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a ghostly appearance. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill forest areas.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
