Lolium arvense var. minor

Lolium arvense var. minor, commonly known as lesser ryegrass, is a cool-season grass that typically grows in disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields.

It has fine, tufted leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous florets. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. It is often considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its competitive nature.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to different environments, contributing to its widespread distribution.
