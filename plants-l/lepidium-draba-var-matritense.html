Lepidium draba var. matritense

Lepidium draba var. matritense, commonly known as the Spanish wallflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in temperate climates. It has a compact growth habit with hairy stems and leaves, and its flowers are often yellow or orange in color.

Lepidium draba var. matritense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
