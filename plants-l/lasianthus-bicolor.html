Lasianthus bicolor

Lasianthus bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored beauty, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking bi-colored flowers, which typically feature a white base with a deep purple or red upper part. The plant produces small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
