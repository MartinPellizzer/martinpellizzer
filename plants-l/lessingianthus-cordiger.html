Lessingianthus cordiger

Lessingianthus cordiger, commonly known as the Brazilian sunflower, is a striking annual herb native to Brazil.

It features bright yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of smaller, darker flowers, giving it a sunflower-like appearance. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its botanical interest lies in its unique floral structure and adaptability to tropical climates.
