Lesliea mirabilis

Lesliea mirabilis, commonly known as the golden star bush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its slender, evergreen branches and small, glossy leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, golden-yellow flowers that bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
