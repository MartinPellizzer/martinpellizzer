Lotus corniculatus var. arvensis

Lotus corniculatus var. arvensis, commonly known as the field poppy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and disturbed soils, thriving in sunny conditions. Its small, trifoliate leaves and papery petals make it easily identifiable.

It is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to its ecological importance.
