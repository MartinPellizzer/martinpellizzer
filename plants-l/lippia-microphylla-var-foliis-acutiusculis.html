Lippia microphylla var. foliis-acutiusculis

Lippia microphylla var. foliis-acutiusculis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lippia, is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It features small, lance-shaped leaves that are sharply pointed, giving it its specific epithet. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers, which are often used in herbal teas. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its aromatic properties make it valuable in both culinary and medicinal applications.
