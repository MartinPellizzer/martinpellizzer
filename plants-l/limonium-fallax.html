Limonium fallax

Limonium fallax, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flower heads, which are surrounded by soft, fuzzy bracts. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. Limonium fallax is a perennial species that produces showy blooms from late spring to early summer.

Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.
