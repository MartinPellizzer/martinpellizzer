Lycopus coreanus

Lycopus coreanus, commonly known as Korean bugleweed, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its small, blue to purple flowers are clustered in terminal spikes and bloom in late summer.

Lycopus coreanus is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
