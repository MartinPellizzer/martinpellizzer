Lobivia bridgesii subsp. bridgesii

Lobivia bridgesii subsp.

bridgesii, commonly known as the Bridges' Lobivia, is a compact, short-lived perennial cactus native to the arid regions of southern Africa. It features a short, cylindrical stem that is typically covered in white or pale green spines, which help reduce water loss and provide protection from herbivores.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the cooler months, usually in spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its slow growth and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in succulent gardens and rockeries.
