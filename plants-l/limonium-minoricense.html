Limonium minoricense

Limonium minoricense, commonly known as the coastal flax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, blue to purple flower heads that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, sandy soils, and saline environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of salt.

Limonium minoricense is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging landscapes.
