Lilacina papposa

Lilacina papposa, commonly known as the hairy papposa, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds that aid in its propagation across dry landscapes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique adaptations, Lilacina papposa is an important component of its native ecosystem.
