Ligustrum ovalifolium var. hisauchii

Ligustrum ovalifolium var. hisauchii, commonly known as the Japanese privet, is a variant of the common privet, native to Japan.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by dark purple berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes.

Due to its adaptability, it is often used in hedges and as a specimen plant.
