Lobelia zeylanica var. walkeri

Lobelia zeylanica var. walkeri, commonly known as the slender Indian toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are usually blue or violet in color, arranged in spikes. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
