Lycopodioides tenuifolia

Lycopodioides tenuifolia, commonly known as the slender clubmoss, is a species of lycophyte belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its small, needle-like leaves and a creeping, rhizomatous growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its sporophyte produces spores in specialized structures called sporangia, which are borne on the underside of the leaves.

Lycopodioides tenuifolia plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
