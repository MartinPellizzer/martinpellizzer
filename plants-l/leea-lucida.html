Leea lucida

Leea lucida, commonly known as the shining leaf vine, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves that are glossy and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in spikes. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Leea lucida is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
