Lacinaria chapmanii

Lacinaria chapmanii, commonly known as the Chapman's lacinaria, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, with a hairy texture.

Lacinaria chapmanii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
