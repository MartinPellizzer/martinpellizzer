Ligularia soldanella

Ligularia soldanella, commonly known as the Alpine ligularia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped, hairy leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in a single head, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and habitat specificity, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
