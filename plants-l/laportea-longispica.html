Laportea longispica

Laportea longispica, commonly known as the long-spined ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, purple to pinkish flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture, and its stems are often covered in fine, bristly hairs. It thrives in moist to wet soils in woodlands and swampy areas, and it plays a role in supporting local pollinator populations.

Laportea longispica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in wetland ecosystems.
