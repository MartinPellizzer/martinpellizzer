Lissochilus petiolatus

Lissochilus petiolatus, commonly known as the long-stemmed orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in moist, shaded environments within cloud forests. The plant features elongated, slender stems and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white and pink. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the rich, organic soil of its habitat.

Lissochilus petiolatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts of rare orchids.
