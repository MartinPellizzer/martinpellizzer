Linum campanulatum var. heterophyllum

Linum campanulatum var. heterophyllum, commonly known as the variable bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological landscapes.
