Ludwigia pubescens var. ligustrifolia

Ludwigia pubescens var. ligustrifolia, commonly known as the redroot sedum, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, bright red flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has hairy, reddish stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in wetlands and along the edges of ponds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in moist environments.
