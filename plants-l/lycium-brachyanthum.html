Lycium brachyanthum

Lycium brachyanthum, commonly known as the slender boxthorn, is a shrub native to Australia.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for humans and animals. The plant has dense, spiny foliage, which provides shelter for various wildlife species. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Lycium brachyanthum plays an important role in local ecosystems by supporting biodiversity and contributing to the landscape's ecological balance.
