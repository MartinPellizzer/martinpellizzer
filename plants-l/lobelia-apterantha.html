Lobelia apterantha

Lobelia apterantha, commonly known as the slender lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features small, tubular blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It is often found in the Appalachian region and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.

Lobelia apterantha is also known for its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
