Linum corymbiferum var. gomezii

Linum corymbiferum var. gomezii, commonly known as the Gomez's flax, is a rare and endemic plant species native to specific regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Linaceae family, which includes other flax species known for their fibrous seeds and ornamental value. The plant is characterized by its delicate, blue to violet flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling small umbels. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its unique botanical appearance.
