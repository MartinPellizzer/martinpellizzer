Litsea diospyrifolia

Litsea diospyrifolia, commonly known as the Chinese tallow tree, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its smooth, light-gray bark and opposite, simple leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange to red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other parts of the world, where it sometimes becomes invasive.
