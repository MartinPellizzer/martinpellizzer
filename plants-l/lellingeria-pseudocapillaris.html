Lellingeria pseudocapillaris

Lellingeria pseudocapillaris, commonly known as the slender spike-rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that often grow in dense clumps, typically reaching heights of 60 to 120 centimeters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spike-like inflorescences, which are usually greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist environments such as wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Lellingeria pseudocapillaris is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
