Lithospermum kamczaticum

Lithospermum kamczaticum, commonly known as the Crimean rock pepper, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It features upright stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by dark red, fleshy berries. Its roots contain compounds such as lithospermic acid, which have been studied for potential medicinal properties.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.
