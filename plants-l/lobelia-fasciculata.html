Lobelia fasciculata

Lobelia fasciculata, commonly known as the clustered lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Lobelia fasciculata is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
