Ligustrum salicinum

Ligustrum salicinum, commonly known as the white privet, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. The plant has dark green, elliptical leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It produces small, dark purple berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful.

Ligustrum salicinum is often used in landscaping due to its adaptability and tolerance to various soil conditions.
