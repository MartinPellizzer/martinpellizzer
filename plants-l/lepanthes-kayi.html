Lepanthes kayi

Lepanthes kayi, commonly known as the Kay's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as montane forests, where it thrives on the forest floor or on tree roots. Its flowers are usually small, with intricate patterns and a wide range of colors, including white, pink, and purple.

Lepanthes kayi is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
