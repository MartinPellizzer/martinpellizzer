Lilaeopsis sinuata

Lilaeopsis sinuata, commonly known as the sinuous grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to regions of Asia, including China and India, and thrives in moist, temperate climates. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves with a distinctive wavy or sinuous edge, giving it its common name. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically arranged in spikelets, a characteristic feature of grasses.

Lilaeopsis sinuata is often found in wetlands and along streambanks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
