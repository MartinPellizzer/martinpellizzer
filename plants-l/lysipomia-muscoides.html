Lysipomia muscoides

Lysipomia muscoides, commonly known as the moss-like grass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant has small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a moss-like appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in forest understories.

Lysipomia muscoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
