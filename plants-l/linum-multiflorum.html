Linum multiflorum

Linum multiflorum, commonly known as the common flax, is a flowering plant in the family Linaceae.

It is native to Europe, western Asia, and northwestern Africa, and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, blue or purple flowers. The plant produces capsules that contain numerous small, oval seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a hairy texture.

Linum multiflorum is cultivated for its fibers, which are used in the production of linen, and for its seeds, which are used in food and oil production.
