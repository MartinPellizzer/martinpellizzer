Lagurus dimorphus

Lagurus dimorphus, commonly known as the woolly bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its fluffy, white to pale pink flowers that resemble small bells, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has basal leaves that are woolly and hairy, providing a distinctive texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Lagurus dimorphus is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and easy care requirements.
