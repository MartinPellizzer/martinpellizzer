Lepanthes obovata

Lepanthes obovata, commonly known as the broad-ovate slipper orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid with a delicate, showy flower that typically features a distinctive slipper-shaped pouch. The plant has a short, stocky stem and lanceolate leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a subtle fragrance that attracts specific pollinators.
