Lonicera ibotiformis

Lonicera ibotiformis, commonly known as the creeping honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its twining stems that can climb or spread along the ground. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, blooming in early spring. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly serrated edge.

Lonicera ibotiformis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
