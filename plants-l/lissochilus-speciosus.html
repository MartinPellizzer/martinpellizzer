Lissochilus speciosus

Lissochilus speciosus, commonly known as the Andean orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically feature vibrant colors such as purple, pink, and white. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, enabling it to thrive in its high-altitude environment. Its flowers are often fragrant and attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Lissochilus speciosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts for its unique beauty.
