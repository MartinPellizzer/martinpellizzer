Leucopogon microphyllus var. pilibundus

Leucopogon microphyllus var. pilibundus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is a shrub characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in heathland and dry sclerophyll forests, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowering period occurs during the spring and early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, contributing to biodiversity and soil stability.
