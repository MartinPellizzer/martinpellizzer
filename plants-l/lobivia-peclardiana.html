Lobivia peclardiana

Lobivia peclardiana, commonly known as the Peclard's lobivia, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, multi-stemmed growth habit. The plant features thick, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, appearing in late spring or early summer.

Lobivia peclardiana is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental appeal.
