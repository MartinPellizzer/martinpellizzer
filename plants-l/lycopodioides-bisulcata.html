Lycopodioides bisulcata

Lycopodioides bisulcata, commonly known as the two-ribbed clubmoss, is a small, evergreen fern-like plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems covered in tiny, scale-like leaves. The plant produces sporangia in clusters along the stems, which are responsible for releasing spores for reproduction. Native to moist, shaded environments in tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in humid conditions.

Its unique morphology and ancient lineage make it an important subject in the study of early plant evolution.
