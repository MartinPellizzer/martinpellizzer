Lupinus perennis var. gracilis

Lupinus perennis var. gracilis, commonly known as slender lupine, is a perennial legume native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairie and open woodland habitats.

Lupinus perennis var. gracilis plays a role in nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
