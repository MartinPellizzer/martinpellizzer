Linum suecicum

Linum suecicum, commonly known as Swedish flax, is a flowering plant native to northern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas.

Linum suecicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
