Lasianthus constrictus

Lasianthus constrictus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lasianthus, is a species of flowering plant in the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Asia, including parts of India, China, and Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are typically small, clustered in whorls, and come in shades of pink or red, adding ornamental value.

Lasianthus constrictus is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and as a decorative plant.
