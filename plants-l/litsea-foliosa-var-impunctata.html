Litsea foliosa var. impunctata

Litsea foliosa var. impunctata, commonly known as the smooth-leafed litsea, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves that are smooth and often glossy on the upper surface. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by berries that are typically orange-red in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its aromatic foliage.
