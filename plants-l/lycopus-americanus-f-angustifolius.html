Lycopus americanus f. angustifolius

Lycopus americanus f. angustifolius, commonly known as narrow-leaved blueweed, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces blue to purple flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported calming and antispasmodic properties.
