Linaria badachschanica

Linaria badachschanica, commonly known as the Badakhshan scabious, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia, particularly Afghanistan and Tajikistan.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, yellowish-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late summer.

Linaria badachschanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators in its native habitat.
