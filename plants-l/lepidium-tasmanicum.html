Lepidium tasmanicum

Lepidium tasmanicum, commonly known as Tasmanian cress, is a flowering plant native to Tasmania, Australia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many edible and economically important plants. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are typically oval and slightly hairy. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.

Lepidium tasmanicum is also known for its peppery taste, making it a potential candidate for culinary use.
