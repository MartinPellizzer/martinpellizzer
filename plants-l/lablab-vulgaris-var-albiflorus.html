Lablab vulgaris var. albiflorus

Lablab vulgaris var. albiflorus, commonly known as the white-flowered lablab, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive white or pale pink flowers, which bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant produces elongated, edible pods that contain kidney-shaped seeds, often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun for optimal growth.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it a valuable crop for soil enrichment and sustainable agriculture.
