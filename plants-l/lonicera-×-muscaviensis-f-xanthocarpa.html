Lonicera × muscaviensis f. xanthocarpa

Lonicera × muscaviensis f. xanthocarpa, commonly known as the yellow-fruited honeysuckle, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Caprifoliaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a small, yellow berry that ripens in late summer, adding ornamental value to the plant.

This variety is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits in gardens and natural habitats.
