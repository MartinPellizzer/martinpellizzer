Limonium tamaricoides

Limonium tamaricoides, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, showy blue or violet flowers. The plant thrives in coastal environments, tolerating saline conditions and strong winds. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, adapted to reduce water loss in arid climates.

Limonium tamaricoides is often used in ornamental gardening for its hardy nature and attractive blooms.
