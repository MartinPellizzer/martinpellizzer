Loasa tricolor var. mendocina

Loasa tricolor var. mendocina, commonly known as the Mendocino loasa, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically gray-green in color with a fuzzy texture. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to diverse environmental conditions.
