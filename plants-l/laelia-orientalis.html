Laelia orientalis

Laelia orientalis, commonly known as the oriental laelia, is a species of orchid native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and the presence of pseudobulbs, which store water and nutrients. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange, often with intricate patterns on the petals. It thrives in cool, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Laelia orientalis is relatively easy to cultivate, making it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
