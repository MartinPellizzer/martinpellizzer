Lobelia melsetteria

Lobelia melsetteria, commonly known as the Melsetter lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually blue or violet in color, arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in well-drained, moist soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Lobelia melsetteria is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and hummingbirds.
