Lobanilia crotonoides

Lobanilia crotonoides, commonly known as the African croton, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including savannas and woodland edges.

Its latex sap is toxic and can cause skin irritation, making it important to handle with care.
