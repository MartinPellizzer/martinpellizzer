Loudetia angolensis

Loudetia angolensis, commonly known as the Angola grass, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. Its leaves are narrow and blade-like, often with a bluish-green hue.

Loudetia angolensis is typically found in grasslands and along riverbanks, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
