Linaria rubrifolia var. intermedia

Linaria rubrifolia var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate redleaf toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and vibrant red-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are narrow and often have a reddish tinge, especially on the underside, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is known for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation in temperate climates.
