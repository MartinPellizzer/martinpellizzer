Leandra barbinervis

Leandra barbinervis, commonly known as the velvetleaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, velvety green leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its flowers, which bloom in clusters, are often pink or purple and attract various pollinators such as bees and birds.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant in tropical climates.
