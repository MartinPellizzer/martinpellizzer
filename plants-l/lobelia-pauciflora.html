Lobelia pauciflora

Lobelia pauciflora, commonly known as the sparse-flowered lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear small, tubular flowers in shades of blue or violet. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.

Lobelia pauciflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
