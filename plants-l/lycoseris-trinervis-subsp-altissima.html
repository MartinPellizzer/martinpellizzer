Lycoseris trinervis subsp. altissima

Lycoseris trinervis subsp.

altissima, commonly known as the tall slender grass, is a member of the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and fine, grass-like leaves. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and marshy areas, preferring temperate climates.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of grasses. Its root system is fibrous, aiding in soil stabilization and nutrient uptake.

Due to its delicate structure, it is often found in areas with consistent moisture and moderate sunlight.
