Linum ucranicum subsp. uralense

Linum ucranicum subsp.

uralense, commonly known as the Ural flax, is a perennial herb native to the Ural Mountains region. It typically grows in moist, loamy soils and thrives in temperate climates.

The plant features slender stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height, adorned with lance-shaped leaves. Its most striking characteristic is the production of blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in fiber production.
