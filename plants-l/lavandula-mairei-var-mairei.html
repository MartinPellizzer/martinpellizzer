Lavandula mairei var. mairei

Lavandula mairei var. mairei, commonly known as the Maire lavender, is a compact, upright shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces spikes of fragrant, violet-blue flowers during the summer months. The plant is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and full sun, making it a drought-tolerant choice for gardens. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.

This variety is also valued for its use in essential oil production and traditional herbal remedies.
