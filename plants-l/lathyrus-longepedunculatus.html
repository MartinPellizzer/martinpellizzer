Lathyrus longepedunculatus

Lathyrus longepedunculatus, commonly known as the long pedunculate vetch, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, and it bears pods containing lens-shaped seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along roadsides.
