Lycopersicon esculentum provar. scopigerum

Lycopersicon esculentum provar. scopigerum, commonly known as the wild tomato, is a botanical variant of the domesticated tomato.

It is characterized by its small, often yellow or orange fruits and hairy stems. This plant is native to South America and thrives in warm, sunny climates. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, sharing close relatives with potatoes and peppers.

Its botanical features make it an important subject in genetic studies of tomato domestication.
