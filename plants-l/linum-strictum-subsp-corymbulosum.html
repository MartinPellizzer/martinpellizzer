Linum strictum subsp. corymbulosum

Linum strictum subsp.

corymbulosum, commonly known as the clustered flax, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, erect stems that often branch towards the top, supporting clusters of small, blue to violet flowers.

The leaves are alternate, linear, and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable plant for rock gardens or borders.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in fiber production.
