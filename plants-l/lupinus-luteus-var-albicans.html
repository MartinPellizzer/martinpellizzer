Lupinus luteus var. albicans

Lupinus luteus var. albicans, commonly known as the white lupin, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces cylindrical pods containing several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a legume for soil enrichment.

This variety is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.
