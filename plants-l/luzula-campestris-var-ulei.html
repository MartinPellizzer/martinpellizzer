Luzula campestris var. ulei

Luzula campestris var. ulei, commonly known as the meadow sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, marshes, and streambanks. The plant has slender, triangular stems that are often slightly hairy, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like in appearance. It produces small, clustered spikelets that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to wet conditions and its role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
