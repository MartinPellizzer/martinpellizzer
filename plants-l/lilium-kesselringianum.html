Lilium kesselringianum

Lilium kesselringianum, commonly known as the Kesselringian lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking red and orange flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with strap-like leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. Its bulbs are an important part of its growth cycle, storing nutrients for the flowering process.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
