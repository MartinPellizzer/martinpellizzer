Licuala olivifera

Licuala olivifera, commonly known as the olive-leaf pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family, commonly referred to as the palm family. The plant is characterized by its large, pinnately compound leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. Its trunk is typically short and robust, with a fibrous outer layer.

Licuala olivifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and has cultural significance in various regions.
