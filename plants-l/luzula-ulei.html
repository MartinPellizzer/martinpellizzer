Luzula ulei

Luzula ulei, commonly known as silver grass, is a perennial flowering plant native to northern regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, silver-blue foliage. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers arranged in dense spikelets, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Luzula ulei thrives in moist, shady environments such as forest floors and stream banks.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive, silvery leaves and its ability to form dense, low-growing clumps.
