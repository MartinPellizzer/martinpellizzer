Ludwigia lagunae

Ludwigia lagunae, commonly known as the marsh Ludwigia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland habitats in the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in shallow, still waters or moist soils, often in marshes and pond edges. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually reddish or purple in color, adding visual interest to its aquatic environment. Its small, yellowish flowers bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and flies.

Ludwigia lagunae plays a role in stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
