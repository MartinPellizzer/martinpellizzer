Linosyris mexicana var. tomentosa

Linosyris mexicana var. tomentosa, commonly known as the hairy linosyris, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant is distinguished by its dense, woolly foliage, which gives it its common name. It typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing adaptations to dry conditions.

Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
