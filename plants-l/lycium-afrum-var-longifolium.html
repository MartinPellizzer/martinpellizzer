Lycium afrum var. longifolium

Lycium afrum var. longifolium, commonly known as the African boxthorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features spiny stems and lance-shaped leaves with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to withstand drought and poor soil conditions.
