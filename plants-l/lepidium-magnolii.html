Lepidium magnolii

Lepidium magnolii, commonly known as the Mexican wallflower, is a herbaceous plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with small, cross-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or disturbed soils and is often found in open areas with full sun exposure. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, and it produces small, rounded seeds in pods.

Lepidium magnolii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
