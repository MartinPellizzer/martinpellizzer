Lysimachia thyrsiflora var. verticillata

Lysimachia thyrsiflora var. verticillata, commonly known as the spiral-leaved loosestrife, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its whorled arrangement of leaves, which give it a distinctive, spiral-like appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a shade-tolerant, low-maintenance plant.
