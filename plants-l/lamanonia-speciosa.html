Lamanonia speciosa

Lamanonia speciosa, commonly known as the showy lamanonia, is a striking member of the Ericaceae family, native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, often with a distinctive red or purple throat. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens. Its leaves are elliptical and dark green, providing a lush backdrop to its showy blooms.

Lamanonia speciosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
