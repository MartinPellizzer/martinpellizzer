Lycopodium homocarpum

Lycopodium homocarpum, commonly known as the clubmoss, is a primitive vascular plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its small, spore-bearing cones and a unique growth habit that includes both foliage and reproductive structures. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are spirally arranged and often have a scale-like appearance.

Lycopodium homocarpum plays an important ecological role in soil stabilization and as a habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
