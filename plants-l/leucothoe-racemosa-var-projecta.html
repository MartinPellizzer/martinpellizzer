Leucothoe racemosa var. projecta

Leucothoe racemosa var. projecta, commonly known as the spreading leucothoe, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features dense, upright growth with elliptical, dark green leaves that turn reddish in the fall. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers in late spring, followed by dark blue berries in the summer. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for woodland gardens and erosion control.
