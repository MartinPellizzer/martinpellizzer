Lycianthes mozinoana var. mozinoana

Lycianthes mozinoana var. mozinoana, commonly known as the Mozino's bitterleaf, is a shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its spiny stems and lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by orange to red berries containing several seeds. It is often found in dry, disturbed habitats and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.

The species is also valued for its ornamental potential due to its distinctive foliage and colorful fruits.
