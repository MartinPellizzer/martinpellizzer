Lepidagathis amaranthoides

Lepidagathis amaranthoides, commonly known as the red-flowered lepidagathis, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often bright red or orange flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, which attract various pollinators such as bees and birds.

Lepidagathis amaranthoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
