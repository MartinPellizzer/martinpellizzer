Lavandula stoechas subsp. maderensis

Lavandula stoechas subsp.

maderensis, commonly known as the Portuguese lavender, is a subspecies native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers and aromatic foliage.

This plant typically grows in rocky or sunny habitats, preferring well-drained soils. It has narrow, linear leaves and is often found in Mediterranean climates.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and essential oil production.
