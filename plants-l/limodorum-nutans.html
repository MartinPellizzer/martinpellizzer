Limodorum nutans

Limodorum nutans, commonly known as the nodding lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, downward-facing flowers that resemble lilies. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with a distinct lip.

Limodorum nutans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
