Laurus maxima

Laurus maxima, commonly known as the bay laurel, is a flowering evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fragrant, dark green, elongated leaves that are widely used in cooking and traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by black, berry-like fruits. Its essential oils, derived from the leaves, contain compounds such as eucalyptol and cineole, which contribute to its aromatic properties.

Laurus maxima is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in various cultures.
