Larix gmelinii var. principis-rupprechtii

Larix gmelinii var. principis-rupprechtii, commonly known as the Korean larch, is a variant of the Siberian larch native to the Korean Peninsula.

It is a coniferous tree characterized by its needle-like leaves and cones, typical of the genus Larix. The tree produces yellowish to reddish-brown cones that mature over several years. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
