Lobivia maximiliana f. pseudocariquinensis

Lobivia maximiliana f. pseudocariquinensis, commonly known as the Pseudocariquinensis Lobivia, is a rare and distinctive cactus native to the arid regions of northern Chile.

It features a compact, globular stem with a dark green to bluish-gray coloration, often adorned with white or pale markings. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring, which are typically short-lived but striking in appearance. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, requiring minimal water to survive.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a valuable and challenging plant for collectors and botanists alike.
