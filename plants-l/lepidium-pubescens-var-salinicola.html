Lepidium pubescens var. salinicola

Lepidium pubescens var. salinicola, commonly known as the saltbush, is a halophyte plant adapted to thrive in saline environments.

It possesses small, rounded leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which help reduce water loss and reflect sunlight. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in early spring. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers in arid conditions.

This species is often found in coastal areas, salt marshes, and other saline habitats across North America.
