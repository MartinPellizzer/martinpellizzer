Laricorchis maldonadoensis

Laricorchis maldonadoensis, commonly known as the Maldonado's lily, is a rare and endemic orchid species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom during the cooler months. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and forest edges, where it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, adapted to the cold and dry conditions of its habitat.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species in conservation efforts.
