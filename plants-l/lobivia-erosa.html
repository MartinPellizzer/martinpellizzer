Lobivia erosa

Lobivia erosa, commonly known as the laceleaf lobivia, is a succulent native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and small, clustered leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish coating. The plant produces delicate, yellowish flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth, it is a popular choice among cactus and succulent enthusiasts.
