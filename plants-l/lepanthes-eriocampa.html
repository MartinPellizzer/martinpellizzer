Lepanthes eriocampa

Lepanthes eriocampa, commonly known as the velvet-leaf orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its small, showy flowers that are typically white or pale pink with intricate patterns. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in tropical environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in a fine, velvety pubescence.

Lepanthes eriocampa thrives in shaded, humid conditions and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.
