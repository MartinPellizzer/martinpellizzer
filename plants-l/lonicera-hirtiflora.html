Lonicera hirtiflora

Lonicera hirtiflora, commonly known as hairy honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are hairy on both surfaces, giving it its common name. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a red, fleshy drupe that matures in the fall, attracting birds and other wildlife.

Lonicera hirtiflora is often found in moist woodlands and along stream banks, where it thrives in partial shade to full sun.
