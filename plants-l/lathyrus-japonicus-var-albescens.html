Lathyrus japonicus var. albescens

Lathyrus japonicus var. albescens, commonly known as the white-flowered Japanese vetch, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a perennial legume characterized by its slender, climbing stems and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets and has small, hairy pods that contain lens-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
