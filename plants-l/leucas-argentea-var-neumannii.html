Leucas argentea var. neumannii

Leucas argentea var. neumannii, commonly known as silver leucas, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name. It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or violet in color.

This species is often found in dry, open habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
