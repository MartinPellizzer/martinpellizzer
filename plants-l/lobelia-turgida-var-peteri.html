Lobelia turgida var. peteri

Lobelia turgida var. peteri, commonly known as the Peter's lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. It is a herbaceous perennial that produces its flowers during the summer months.

Its unique appearance and habitat make it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine collections.
