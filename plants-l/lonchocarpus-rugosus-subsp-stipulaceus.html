Lonchocarpus rugosus subsp. stipulaceus

Lonchocarpus rugosus subsp.

stipulaceus, commonly known as the hairy senna, is a leguminous shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia. It is characterized by its compound leaves with stipules, and its distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, and it is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

It has medicinal uses in traditional practices and is also valued for its ornamental qualities.
