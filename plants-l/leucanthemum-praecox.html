Leucanthemum praecox

Leucanthemum praecox, commonly known as the early white daisy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its white petals surrounding a yellow center, forming a daisy-like appearance. The plant typically grows to a height of 10 to 30 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It blooms in early spring, often before the leaves fully develop, and is known for its delicate, fragrant flowers.

Leucanthemum praecox is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is also valued in traditional herbal medicine.
