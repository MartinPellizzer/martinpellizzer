Litsea multiflora

Litsea multiflora, commonly known as the many-flowered litsea, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by small, round berries that are edible for some bird species.

The plant is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is also valued in traditional medicine for its aromatic properties.
