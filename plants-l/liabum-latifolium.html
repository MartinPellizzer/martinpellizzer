Liabum latifolium

Liabum latifolium, commonly known as broadleaf liabum, is a flowering plant species native to parts of East Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale pink, arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
