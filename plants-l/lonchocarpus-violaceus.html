Lonchocarpus violaceus

Lonchocarpus violaceus, commonly known as purple senna, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is notable for its striking violet to purple flowers, which bloom in dense clusters. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being elliptical and hairy.

The seed pods are elongated and contain several seeds, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.
