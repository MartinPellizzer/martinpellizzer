Litsea cambodiana

Litsea cambodiana, commonly known as Cambodian laurier or Cambodian laurel, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like avocado and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. It produces small, white, fragrant flowers followed by spherical, dark blue berries.

Litsea cambodiana is often valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
