Litsea longepedunculata

Litsea longepedunculata, commonly known as the long pedunculate litsea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Vietnam, Cambodia, and Laos. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a dark purple drupe, containing a single seed, and it is often found in moist, shaded forest environments.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
