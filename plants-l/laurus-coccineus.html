Laurus coccineus

Laurus coccineus, commonly known as the red bay tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to the southeastern United States and parts of the Caribbean, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruits are red, fleshy berries that contain the seeds.

The red bay tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its aromatic leaves, which have been used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
