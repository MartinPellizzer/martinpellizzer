Linaria aeruginea subsp. pruinosa

Linaria aeruginea subsp.

pruinosa, commonly known as the purple toadflax, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe. It features slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating.

The plant produces striking, tubular flowers that range in color from pale purple to deep violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens despite its natural habitat being more restricted.
