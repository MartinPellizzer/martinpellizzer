Lobostemon pilicaulis

Lobostemon pilicaulis, commonly known as the hairy-leaved lobostemon, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often found in moist, shaded environments. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and showy inflorescences that attract pollinators.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid climates.
