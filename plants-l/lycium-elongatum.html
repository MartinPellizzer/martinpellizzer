Lycium elongatum

Lycium elongatum, commonly known as Chinese wolfberry or goji berry, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny branches, small yellow flowers, and red berries that turn orange-red when ripe. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it adaptable to various climates. Its berries are rich in antioxidants and nutrients, contributing to its popularity in traditional medicine and cuisine.

The plant's botanical features make it both ornamental and economically valuable.
