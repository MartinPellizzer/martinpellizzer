Lantana corymbosa

Lantana corymbosa, commonly known as the shrub lantana, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, brightly colored flowers that range from yellow to orange and red. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are typically lance-shaped. Lantana corymbosa is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and climates, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

However, it can be invasive in some regions and is considered a weed in certain areas.
