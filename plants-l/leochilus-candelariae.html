Leochilus candelariae

Leochilus candelariae, commonly known as the candlestick cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stem that resembles a candlestick, often reaching heights of up to 6 feet. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking visual contrast to its green stem. It has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in well-drained, sandy soils.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptations to harsh environments, it is often cultivated in desert gardens for its ornamental value.
