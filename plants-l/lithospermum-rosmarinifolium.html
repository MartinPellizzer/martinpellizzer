Lithospermum rosmarinifolium

Lithospermum rosmarinifolium, commonly known as Chinese burnet, is a perennial herb native to Asia.

It features upright stems covered in fine hairs and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in summer. Its roots contain a red dye called lac, which has been used historically for coloring textiles.

Lithospermum rosmarinifolium is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.
