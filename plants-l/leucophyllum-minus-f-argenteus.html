Leucophyllum minus f. argenteus

Leucophyllum minus f. argenteus, commonly known as silverleaf redstem, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are densely covered with white, woolly hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces clusters of pink to purple flowers during the spring and summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping in arid regions.

Its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements make it an ideal plant for rock gardens and landscape designs.
