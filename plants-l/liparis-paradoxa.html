Liparis paradoxa

Liparis paradoxa, commonly known as the paradox orchid, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, pendulous flowers that resemble small, delicate orchids. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories where it relies on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it produces showy, fragrant blooms that attract various pollinators.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Liparis paradoxa is considered a conservation concern in its native range.
