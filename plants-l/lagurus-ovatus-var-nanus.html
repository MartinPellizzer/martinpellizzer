Lagurus ovatus var. nanus

Lagurus ovatus var. nanus, commonly known as the dwarf sowing grass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features delicate, feathery plumes that resemble small ears, giving it a unique and ornamental appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact size and airy foliage make it a popular choice for adding texture and movement to garden landscapes.
