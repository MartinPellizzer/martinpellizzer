Leptocarpus crebriculmis

Leptocarpus crebriculmis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, succulent-like growth habit and produces small, blue or violet flowers. The plant has fleshy, narrow leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in its arid environment. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found growing in dunes or along the shoreline.

Leptocarpus crebriculmis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize coastal soils.
