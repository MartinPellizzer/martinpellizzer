Lasiorrhiza rosea

Lasiorrhiza rosea, commonly known as the rose thornvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pink or purple flowers. The plant is typically found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in nutrient-poor conditions.

Lasiorrhiza rosea is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, which makes it an important plant in soil enrichment.
