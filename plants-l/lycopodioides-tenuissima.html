Lycopodioides tenuissima

Lycopodioides tenuissima, commonly known as the slender clubmoss, is a small, herbaceous plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its creeping stems that produce small, scale-like leaves and erect, spore-bearing cones. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or rocky areas. Its reproductive structures, known as strobila, contain the spores necessary for its propagation.

Lycopodioides tenuissima plays a role in maintaining soil stability and providing habitat for microorganisms in its native ecosystems.
