Licuala tansachana

Licuala tansachana, commonly known as the golden palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of yellowish spines. It produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, which eventually develop into edible fruits.

Licuala tansachana is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in some local cultures.
