Lepanthes oscillifera

Lepanthes oscillifera, commonly known as the oscillating orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, pendulous flowers that sway with the movement of air, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in the understory of tropical rainforests. Its flowers are usually small and white or pale green, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Lepanthes oscillifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
