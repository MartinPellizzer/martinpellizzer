Lappula ciliata

Lappula ciliata, commonly known as the hairy lappula, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in disturbed soils and along roadsides, preferring dry, sandy or gravelly habitats. The plant has erect stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a hairy texture. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in dense, cylindrical spikes.

The fruit is a three-lobed capsule that releases tiny seeds when mature.
