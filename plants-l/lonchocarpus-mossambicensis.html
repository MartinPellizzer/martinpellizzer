Lonchocarpus mossambicensis

Lonchocarpus mossambicensis, commonly known as the African trumpet tree, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tubular, yellow or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces woody pods containing seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Its leaves are compound, with several pairs of oval-shaped leaflets.
