Linaria paradoxa

Linaria paradoxa, commonly known as the paradox flower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its striking, elongated yellow-orange flowers that resemble small beaks. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

Linaria paradoxa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
