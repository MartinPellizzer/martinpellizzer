Leontodon hellenicus

Leontodon hellenicus, commonly known as the Greek dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe, particularly Greece.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and deeply lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces a distinctive seed head with pappus that aids in wind dispersal.

Leontodon hellenicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been traditionally used in herbal medicine.
