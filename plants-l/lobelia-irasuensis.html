Lobelia irasuensis

Lobelia irasuensis, commonly known as the Ethiopian bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and open habitats at elevations above 2,000 meters. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered with fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branched.
