Lychnis miqueliana

Lychnis miqueliana, commonly known as the Miquel's catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or arctic regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branched.

Lychnis miqueliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
