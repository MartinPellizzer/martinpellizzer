Lucuma neriifolia

Lucuma neriifolia, commonly known as the black lucuma, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The fruit is typically a dark yellow or orange berry, which contains edible seeds surrounded by a sweet, sticky pulp. This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.

Its growth habit includes a shrub or small tree form, adapting well to the cool, high-altitude environments of its native habitat.
