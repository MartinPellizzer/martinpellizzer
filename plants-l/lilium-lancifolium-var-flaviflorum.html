Lilium lancifolium var. flaviflorum

Lilium lancifolium var. flaviflorum, commonly known as the yellow-flowered fritillary, is a variant of the asiatic lily that exhibits distinctive botanical features.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and striking yellow-orange flowers with a distinctive fragrance. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with sturdy stems and showy, bell-shaped blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens.

This variety is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its vibrant, long-lasting flowers.
