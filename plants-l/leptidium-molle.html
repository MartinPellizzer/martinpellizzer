Leptidium molle

Leptidium molle, commonly known as the slender bladderwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, herbaceous stature and is often found in moist, sandy soils along coastal areas. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate and linear, often with a bluish-green hue.

Leptidium molle is known for its ability to thrive in saline environments, making it a notable species in coastal botany.
