Lobivia haageana var. cinnabarina

Lobivia haageana var. cinnabarina, commonly known as the Crimson Lobivia, is a succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It features compact, rounded stems that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, white pubescence. The plant produces striking, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, often with a distinctive orange or yellow throat. Its leaves are usually small, fleshy, and arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted to store water in its native environment.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation in well-draining soil.
