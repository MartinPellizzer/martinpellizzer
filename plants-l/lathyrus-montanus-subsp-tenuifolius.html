Lathyrus montanus subsp. tenuifolius

Lathyrus montanus subsp.

tenuifolius, commonly known as the slender-leafed sweet pea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and clusters of fragrant, pea-like flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep violet.

The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and thrives in well-drained soils in full sun. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
