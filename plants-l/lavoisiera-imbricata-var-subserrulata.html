Lavoisiera imbricata var. subserrulata

Lavoisiera imbricata var. subserrulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lavoisiera, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the Acanthaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often slightly serrated along the margins. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is native to tropical regions of South America and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
