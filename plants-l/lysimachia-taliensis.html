Lysimachia taliensis

Lysimachia taliensis, commonly known as Taliensis chickweed, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing habit with opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
