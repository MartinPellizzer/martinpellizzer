Lepidium parodii f. integriusculum

Lepidium parodii f. integriusculum, commonly known as the smooth wall rocket, is a variant of the common wall rocket, characterized by its smooth, undivided leaves.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family, known for its small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are edible and have a peppery flavor, often used in culinary applications.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and ornamental value in gardens.
