Lonchocarpus angulatus

Lonchocarpus angulatus, commonly known as the angular senna, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with angular leaflets and produces distinctive yellow flowers. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. Its seeds are encased in a hard, woody pod, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine.

However, it contains toxic compounds and requires careful handling.
