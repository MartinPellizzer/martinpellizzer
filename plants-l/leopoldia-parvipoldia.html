Leopoldia parvipoldia

Leopoldia parvipoldia, commonly known as the dwarf leopoldia, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of tiny, greenish-white or pale yellow flowers that bloom during the wet season. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a delicately endangered species in its native range.
