Lathyrus lusseri

Lathyrus lusseri, commonly known as the Corsican vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, pea-like flowers in shades of purple or pink. Its pods contain seeds that are often used in traditional agriculture.

Lathyrus lusseri is adapted to rocky and dry soils, making it a resilient species in arid environments.
