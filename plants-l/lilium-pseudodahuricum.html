Lilium pseudodahuricum

Lilium pseudodahuricum, commonly known as the Siberian lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It features tall, straight stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, adorned with broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces large, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, or red, typically with a distinctive red or orange throat. It blooms in late summer to early autumn, and its bulbs are used in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
