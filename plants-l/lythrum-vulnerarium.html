Lythrum vulnerarium

Lythrum vulnerarium, commonly known as the woundwort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lythraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Lythrum vulnerarium has been historically used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
