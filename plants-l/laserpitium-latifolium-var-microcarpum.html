Laserpitium latifolium var. microcarpum

Laserpitium latifolium var. microcarpum, commonly known as the narrow-fruited broadleaf gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is a member of the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and meadows. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, and the flowers bloom in late summer.

The variety microcarpum is distinguished by its smaller, more compact fruits compared to the typical form.
