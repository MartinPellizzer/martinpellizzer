Lepismium cruciforme

Lepismium cruciforme, commonly known as the crucifix lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that resemble crosses, hence its common name. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a velvety texture, while its flowers are usually pink or red.

Lepismium cruciforme is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
