Linum gracile

Linum gracile, commonly known as slender flax, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the genus Linum, which includes other species of flax known for their blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is characterized by its slender stems and small, delicate flowers. Linum gracile has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring.

It is often found in habitats such as grasslands and open woodlands.
