Lolium temulentum var. ramosum

Lolium temulentum var. ramosum, commonly known as the slender ryegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny flowers, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers moist, well-drained conditions.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it is often found in disturbed areas and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
