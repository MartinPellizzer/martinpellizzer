Lonicera tatarica f. leroyana

Lonicera tatarica f. leroyana, commonly known as the Tatarian honeysuckle, is a compact shrub native to eastern Europe and western Asia.

It features fragrant, tubular yellow to orange flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and it produces red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
