Lobelia caerulea var. glabrescens

Lobelia caerulea var. glabrescens, commonly known as blue lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches and has narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This variety is notable for its smooth, hairless stems and is often used in gardens for its ornamental value.
