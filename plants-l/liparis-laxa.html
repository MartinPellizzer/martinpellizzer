Liparis laxa

Liparis laxa, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky areas. The plant produces slender, grass-like leaves and bears small, greenish-yellow flowers that are often clustered in a spike. It has a fibrous root system and is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Liparis laxa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
