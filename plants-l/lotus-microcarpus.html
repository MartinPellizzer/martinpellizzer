Lotus microcarpus

Lotus microcarpus, commonly known as the dwarf lotus, is a species of aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shallow waters and is characterized by its small, floating leaves and showy pink or red flowers. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that helps it anchor in muddy substrates. Its flowers bloom from June to September, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Lotus microcarpus is valued for its ornamental beauty and has cultural significance in traditional medicine and water gardening.
