Linnaea umbellata

Linnaea umbellata, commonly known as the twinflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its distinctive pair of opposite flowers that bloom in early spring, typically in shades of white or pink. The plant has opposite, simple, and usually ovate or elliptic leaves that are arranged in a whorled pattern along the stem. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and forest understories.

Linnaea umbellata is known for its fragrant flowers and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
