Lippia cuneifolia var. incisa

Lippia cuneifolia var. incisa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf lippia, is a perennial shrub native to the warm climates of South America.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often serrated along the edges. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for ornamental gardens and herbal cultivation.

Its aromatic properties make it valuable in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
