Leontodon ludovici

Leontodon ludovici, commonly known as the Ludovician dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous root system. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a single head.

Leontodon ludovici is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
