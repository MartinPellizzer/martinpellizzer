Lotus hispidus var. major

Lotus hispidus var. major, commonly known as the rough lotus, is a perennial aquatic plant native to parts of Asia.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater habitats such as ponds and slow-moving streams. The plant features a thick, fleshy rhizome that helps anchor it in the substrate. Its leaves are broad, floating, and covered with fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture.

The flowers are pink or reddish, with a showy appearance that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.
