Lippia africana var. sessilis

Lippia africana var. sessilis, commonly known as African blue verbena, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in shrubland and forest margins, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are often sessile, meaning they lack stalks, and are covered in fine hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
