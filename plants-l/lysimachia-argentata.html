Lysimachia argentata

Lysimachia argentata, commonly known as silver woundwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, and its foliage is characterized by silvery-gray undersides, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or along stream banks. It has a fibrous root system and spreads through stolons, forming dense mats.

Lysimachia argentata is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and ease of cultivation.
