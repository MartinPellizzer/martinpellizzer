Leucojum trichophyllum var. grandiflorum

Leucojum trichophyllum var. grandiflorum, commonly known as the giant white squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features broad, strap-like leaves and produces large, showy white flowers with a distinctive greenish hue. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or woodland settings. Its flowers bloom in spring, adding a striking visual element to gardens.

It is relatively low maintenance and resistant to pests and diseases, making it a popular choice for gardeners.
