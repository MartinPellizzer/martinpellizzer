Loasa acerifolia

Loasa acerifolia, commonly known as the mapleleaf loasa, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant, maple-like leaves that turn red or orange in the fall. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters during the spring. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal.

Loasa acerifolia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.
