Lolium pluriflorum

Lolium pluriflorum, commonly known as the purple grass or purple clover, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and vibrant purple inflorescences that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in temperate climates and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along roadsides. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are small and clustered in panicles.

Lolium pluriflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and as a forage crop.
