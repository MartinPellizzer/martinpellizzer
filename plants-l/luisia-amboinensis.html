Luisia amboinensis

Luisia amboinensis, commonly known as the Amboina lily, is a flowering plant native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows as a bulbous perennial, with strap-like leaves that are broad and fleshy. It thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil and partial shade for optimal growth.

Luisia amboinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
