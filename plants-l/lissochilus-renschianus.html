Lissochilus renschianus

Lissochilus renschianus, commonly known as the Rensch's hairless orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, elongated floral structure. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or alpine habitats. Its flowers are usually small and have a distinctive, intricate arrangement of petals and sepals.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
