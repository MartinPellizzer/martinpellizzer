Lonicera etrusca var. brownii

Lonicera etrusca var. brownii, commonly known as the Brown's honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy on the upper surface. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are yellowish-white with orange stamens, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruits are red berries that develop in late summer, providing food for birds and small mammals.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
