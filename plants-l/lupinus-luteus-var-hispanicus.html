Lupinus luteus var. hispanicus

Lupinus luteus var. hispanicus, commonly known as the Spanish yellow lupin, is a perennial legume native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas. It has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion.

The plant is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it beneficial in ecological restoration projects.
