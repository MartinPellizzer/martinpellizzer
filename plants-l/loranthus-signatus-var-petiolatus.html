Loranthus signatus var. petiolatus

Loranthus signatus var. petiolatus, commonly known as the petioled mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to its host through haustoria.

It belongs to the family Loranthaceae and is characterized by its leaf-like branches and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows on the branches of various host trees, such as eucalyptus and acacia. Its berries are usually red or yellow and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This species plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its host trees by influencing their growth and nutrient uptake.
