Ligustrina reticulata var. mandshurica

Ligustrina reticulata var. mandshurica, commonly known as the Far Eastern ligustrum, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features small, dark green leaves with a slightly rough texture and a distinct network of veins. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by black, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value and adaptability contribute to its popularity in horticulture.
