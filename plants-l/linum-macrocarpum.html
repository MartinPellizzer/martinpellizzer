Linum macrocarpum

Linum macrocarpum, commonly known as the large-seeded flax, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its seeds are edible and have been used historically for their oil content.

Linum macrocarpum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.
