Leucadendron filamentosum

Leucadendron filamentosum, commonly known as the Filamentous Coneflower, is a striking shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and vibrant, cone-shaped inflorescences that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are pollinated by insects, particularly bees and butterflies. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs.

Leucadendron filamentosum is well-suited for cultivation in warm climates and is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience.
