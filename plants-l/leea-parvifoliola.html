Leea parvifoliola

Leea parvifoliola, commonly known as the small-leaved leea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Leeaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, which are typically borne on short peduncles. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Leea parvifoliola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and flowers.
