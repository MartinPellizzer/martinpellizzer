Lagerstroemia poilanei var. poilanei

Lagerstroemia poilanei var. poilanei, commonly known as the Purple Flame Crape Myrtle, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, panicle-like inflorescences that bloom in late spring to early summer, displaying shades of pink, red, and purple. The plant features smooth, peeling bark that becomes increasingly fissured with age, adding to its ornamental value. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate, with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental flowers and adaptability to various soil types.
