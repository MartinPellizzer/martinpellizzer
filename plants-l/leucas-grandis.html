Leucas grandis

Leucas grandis, commonly known as the large-leaved leucas, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia, particularly India and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic and medicinal plants. The plant is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested or grassy areas.

Due to its aromatic properties, it has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
