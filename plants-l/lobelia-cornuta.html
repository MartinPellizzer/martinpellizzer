Lobelia cornuta

Lobelia cornuta, commonly known as the horned lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tubular, red or orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spiny fruits that contain the seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and swampy areas.

Lobelia cornuta is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
