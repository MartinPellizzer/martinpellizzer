Lonicera periclymenum var. longipetiolata

Lonicera periclymenum var. longipetiolata, commonly known as the long-petioled honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture with a slightly serrated margin. This variety is distinguished by its elongated petioles, which give it a unique appearance compared to other honeysuckle species.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
