Laportea bulbifera subsp. dielsii

Laportea bulbifera subsp.

dielsii, commonly known as the bulbiferous false dandelion, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of South America. It is characterized by its basal rosette of soft, hairy leaves and the production of bulbils along the stems, which can give rise to new plants.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in a cluster at the tip of the stem, and it blooms during the spring and early summer.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique reproductive strategy and ecological adaptations.
