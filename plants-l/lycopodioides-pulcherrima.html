Lycopodioides pulcherrima

Lycopodioides pulcherrima, commonly known as the beautiful clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the order Lycopodiales.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves and a creeping or upright growth habit. The plant produces small, cone-like structures called strobili, which contain the spores for reproduction. Lycopodioides pulcherrima thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest floors or rocky crevices.

Its ancient lineage dates back to the Paleozoic era, making it a living fossil with significant ecological and evolutionary importance.
