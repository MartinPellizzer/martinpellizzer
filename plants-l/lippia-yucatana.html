Lippia yucatana

Lippia yucatana, commonly known as Mexican blueweed, is a perennial shrub native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Lippia yucatana is also valued for its medicinal properties, with traditional uses in herbal remedies.
