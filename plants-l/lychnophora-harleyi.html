Lychnophora harleyi

Lychnophora harleyi, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with smooth, grayish bark and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that are followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in coastal areas.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive tendencies, it is considered a problematic species in some regions.
