Lyciopsis cuneata

Lyciopsis cuneata, commonly known as the narrowleaf lycium, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and oval-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its fruit is a small, dry capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Lyciopsis cuneata is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
