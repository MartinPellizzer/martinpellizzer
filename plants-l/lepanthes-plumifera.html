Lepanthes plumifera

Lepanthes plumifera, commonly known as the feather-leaf orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, pendulous flowers that often display a range of colors, including white, yellow, and pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as cloud forests, where it thrives on tree roots or in rocky crevices. Its leaves are elongated and slender, giving it the "feather-leaf" moniker, and it produces small, fragrant blooms throughout the wet season.

Due to its striking appearance and ecological significance, Lepanthes plumifera is a subject of interest for both horticulturists and conservationists.
