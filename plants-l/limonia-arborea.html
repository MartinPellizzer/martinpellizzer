Limonia arborea

Limonia arborea, commonly known as the Java hibiscus, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, lance-shaped leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, round, yellow-orange fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisines. Limonia arborea thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for ornamental and subtropical gardens.

Its ornamental value and adaptability contribute to its popularity in horticulture.
