Lycopersicon esculentum provar. flammatum

Lycopersicon esculentum provar. flammatum, commonly known as the fire tomato, is a variant of the domesticated tomato characterized by its distinctive red-orange to deep red fruit.

This plant belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is typically an annual herb with hairy stems and alternate leaves. The fruit develops from a flower and is often used in culinary applications due to its vibrant color and flavor.

Its botanical features include a thick, fleshy pericarp and a central core with seeds.
