Lasianthus bractescens var. rosulatus

Lasianthus bractescens var. rosulatus, commonly known as the clustered lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or subshrub, with lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers, which are usually pink or red, bloom in terminal clusters, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
