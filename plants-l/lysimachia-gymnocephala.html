Lysimachia gymnocephala

Lysimachia gymnocephala, commonly known as the naked-headed chickweed, is a herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is characterized by its low, spreading growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the spring and early summer. Its leaves are usually opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

Lysimachia gymnocephala is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.
