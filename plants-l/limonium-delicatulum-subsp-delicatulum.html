Limonium delicatulum subsp. delicatulum

Limonium delicatulum subsp.

delicatulum, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and spikes of small, blue to purple flowers.

The plant thrives in saline and well-drained soils, often found in dunes and coastal grasslands. It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens with a coastal or Mediterranean theme.
