Libanotis riviniana

Libanotis riviniana, commonly known as the river libanotis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. The plant features basal rosettes of lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often tinged with purple. It produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in loose clusters during late spring to early summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
