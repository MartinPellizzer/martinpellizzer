Lobivia abrantha

Lobivia abrantha, commonly known as the silver cholla, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in dense, white spines, which help to reflect sunlight and reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and early summer, adding a striking contrast to its gray-green foliage. Lobivia abrantha is adapted to survive in extreme desert conditions with minimal water and high temperatures.

Its unique morphology and resilience make it a notable example of desert flora.
