Leschenaultia formosa

Leschenaultia formosa, commonly known as the pink fairy lantern, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces vibrant pink to purple flowers that resemble small lanterns, hence its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Leschenaultia formosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
