Laburnum platycarpum

Laburnum platycarpum, commonly known as the golden chain tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern and eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its long, drooping clusters of bright yellow flowers that appear in spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces woody, pod-like fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

The golden chain tree is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
