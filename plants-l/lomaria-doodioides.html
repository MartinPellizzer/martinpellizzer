Lomaria doodioides

Lomaria doodioides, commonly known as the desert lomaria, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant has a low-growing, spreading habit with succulent leaves that help it retain moisture in dry environments. It produces small, wind-dispersed seeds, aiding in its propagation across sandy and rocky soils.

Lomaria doodioides is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
