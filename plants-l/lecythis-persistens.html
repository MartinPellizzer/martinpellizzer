Lecythis persistens

Lecythis persistens, commonly known as the red cedar or Brazilian walnut, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Lecythidaceae and is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and showy yellow flowers. The tree produces a heavy, oily fruit with a hard, woody endocarp that encases the seeds. Its bark and wood are valued for their durability and have been used traditionally for various purposes.

The plant also plays an important role in the ecosystems of the Amazon rainforest.
