Ligustrum obtusifolium f. leiocalyx

Ligustrum obtusifolium f. leiocalyx, commonly known as the glossy privet, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters, followed by dark blue berries that are edible for some bird species. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its dense growth habit and attractive foliage.

This variety is known for its compact form and is suitable for hedges or as a foundation plant in gardens.
