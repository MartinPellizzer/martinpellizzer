Lilium bulbiferum var. fulgens

Lilium bulbiferum var. fulgens, commonly known as the fiery lily, is a striking member of the Liliaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is characterized by its vibrant red and orange flowers. The plant produces bulb-like structures called bulbils, which are used for vegetative propagation. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its dramatic floral display.
