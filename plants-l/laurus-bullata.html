Laurus bullata

Laurus bullata, commonly known as the bay laurel or sweet bay, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark, spherical berries. Its leaves are often used in cooking and have a strong, spicy aroma.

Laurus bullata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a hedge or ornamental plant.
