Laurus catesbaei

Laurus catesbaei, commonly known as the Florida laurel, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other well-known species like bay laurel. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are glossy and aromatic, and its small white flowers that appear in clusters. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or swampy areas.

The Florida laurel is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
