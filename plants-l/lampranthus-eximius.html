Lampranthus eximius

Lampranthus eximius, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a striking member of the Aizoaceae family native to South Africa.

It is a herbaceous plant that produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows from a basal rosette of fleshy, blue-green leaves that help it conserve water in arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in regions with a Mediterranean climate.
