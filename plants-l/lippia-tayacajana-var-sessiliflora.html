Lippia tayacajana var. sessiliflora

Lippia tayacajana var. sessiliflora, commonly known as the yellow verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellow flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and opposite, with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
