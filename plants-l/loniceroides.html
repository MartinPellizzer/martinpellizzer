Loniceroides

Loniceroides, commonly known as trailing jostleberry, is a species of flowering plant in the honeysuckle family (Caprifoliaceae).

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces red, orange, or yellow berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is native to parts of Asia, particularly China.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and naturalized areas.
