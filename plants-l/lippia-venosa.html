Lippia venosa

Lippia venosa, commonly known as the Mexican mint or wild mint, is a herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments and can be found in regions such as Mexico, Central America, and parts of the southern United States. Lippia venosa has opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple in color.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb due to its pleasant scent and medicinal properties.
