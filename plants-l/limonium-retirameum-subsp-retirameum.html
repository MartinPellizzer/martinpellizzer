Limonium retirameum subsp. retirameum

Limonium retirameum subsp.

retirameum, commonly known as the sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and its clusters of blue to violet flowers.

The plant thrives in coastal areas and sandy soils, often found in dunes and salt marshes. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are drought-tolerant.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various insects.
