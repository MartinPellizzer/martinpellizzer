Leochilus macrantherus

Leochilus macrantherus, commonly known as the large-flowered leechgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
