Lithospermum hispidulum

Lithospermum hispidulum, commonly known as hairy rock pepper, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and small, pinkish to reddish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically red when mature. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Boraginaceae and is noted for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
