Lupinus caudatus var. microphyllus

Lupinus caudatus var. microphyllus, commonly known as the dwarf lupine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of pea-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant has small, compound leaves with numerous narrow, pointed leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and native gardens.
