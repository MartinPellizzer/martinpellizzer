Limonium binervosum subsp. dodartii

Limonium binervosum subsp.

dodartii, commonly known as the Narrow-leaved Sea Lavender, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in coastal areas, sandy soils, and disturbed sites, showing a preference for well-drained environments. Its leaves are narrow and often have a distinct two-veined appearance, contributing to its botanical identification.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes.
