Leontice leontopetaloides

Leontice leontopetaloides, commonly known as the white leontice, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It prefers moist, rich soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive and relatively easy-to-grow plant for gardens with suitable conditions.
