Lupinus ermineus

Lupinus ermineus, commonly known as the ermine lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of blue to violet pea-shaped flowers. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Lupinus ermineus is often found in dry, open areas such as grasslands and rocky slopes, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
