Lepanthes hystrix

Lepanthes hystrix, commonly known as the spiny lepanthes, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments within tropical forests. The plant is characterized by its striking, spurred flowers that range in color from white to pink, with a distinctive spiny structure on the lip. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom during the wet season.

Lepanthes hystrix is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
