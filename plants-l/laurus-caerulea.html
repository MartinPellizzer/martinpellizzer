Laurus caerulea

Laurus caerulea, commonly known as sweet bay or Laurier rose, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and fragrant, small white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, blue-black berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic leaves.

Laurus caerulea prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile addition to gardens and landscapes.
