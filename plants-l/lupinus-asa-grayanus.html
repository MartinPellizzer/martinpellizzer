Lupinus asa-grayanus

Lupinus asa-grayanus, commonly known as the Asa-Gray lupine, is a species native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds.

Lupinus asa-grayanus is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils, where it plays a role in nitrogen fixation.
