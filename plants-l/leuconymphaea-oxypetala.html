Leuconymphaea oxypetala

Leuconymphaea oxypetala, commonly known as the slender water lily, is a species of aquatic plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It features broad, floating leaves that are typically light green with a waxy surface, adapted to reduce water loss. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in the early morning and close by midday. Its roots are anchored in the mud at the bottom of water bodies, allowing it to thrive in still or slow-moving waters.

The plant plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
