Lysimachia × doerfleri

Lysimachia × doerfleri, commonly known as golden groundsel, is a hybrid perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features oval, toothed leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, contributing to its textured appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Lysimachia × doerfleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to spread through rhizomes, forming dense ground covers.
