Licania duckei

Licania duckei, commonly known as the Brazilian licania, is a member of the Malvaceae family.

It is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its flowers are typically clustered in racemes. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation projects.
