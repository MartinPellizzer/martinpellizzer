Linum congestum var. confertum

Linum congestum var. confertum, commonly known as the clustered flax or broad-leaved flax, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the genus Linum, which includes various species of flax known for their showy blue or white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in clusters, giving it its common name.

The flowers are usually blue or purple and bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
