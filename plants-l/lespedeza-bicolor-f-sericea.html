Lespedeza bicolor f. sericea

Lespedeza bicolor f. sericea, commonly known as silverleaf lespedeza, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable ornamental plant for gardens and landscapes.

Its fibrous root system helps prevent soil erosion, contributing to its ecological value in native plant gardens.
