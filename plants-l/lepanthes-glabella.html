Lepanthes glabella

Lepanthes glabella, commonly known as the glabrous orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central and South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments such as forests and cloud forests. The plant features a slender, often hairless stem and produces small, delicate flowers with intricate patterns on their petals. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and they emit a faint, sweet fragrance.

Lepanthes glabella is noted for its unique morphology and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value.
