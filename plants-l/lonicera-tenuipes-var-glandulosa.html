Lonicera tenuipes var. glandulosa

Lonicera tenuipes var. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features slender, twining stems that bear opposite, ovate leaves with a hairy underside. The plant produces small, fragrant, yellowish-green flowers in late spring, followed by red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and adaptability to various garden conditions.
