Linderniella nana

Linderniella nana, commonly known as the dwarf lindernia, is a small, herbaceous annual plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of less than 10 centimeters, with small, opposite leaves and clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed sites.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and relatively easy-to-grow plant for gardeners interested in native flora.
