Lantana chamissonis

Lantana chamissonis, commonly known as the yellow lantana, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring narrow, opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Lantana chamissonis is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
