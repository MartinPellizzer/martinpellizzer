Lissochilus papilionaceus

Lissochilus papilionaceus, commonly known as the purple orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, violet-colored flowers with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or forested areas. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the thin, nutrient-poor soils of its habitat.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
