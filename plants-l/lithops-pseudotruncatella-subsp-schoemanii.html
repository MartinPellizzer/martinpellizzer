Lithops pseudotruncatella subsp. schoemanii

Lithops pseudotruncatella subsp.

schoemanii, commonly known as the "ice plant" or "living stone," is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its fleshy, triangular leaves that resemble stones, helping it blend into its arid environment.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers during the winter months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for rocky or sandy habitats.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
