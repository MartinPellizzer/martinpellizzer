Lespedeza buergeri var. kinashii

Lespedeza buergeri var. kinashii, commonly known as Kinashii lespedza, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, which makes it a valuable plant for soil enrichment.

The varietal name "kinashii" refers to its historical use in traditional medicine.
