Lavandula pannosa f. cadevallii

Lavandula pannosa f. cadevallii, commonly known as the Cadevallii lavender, is a compact, low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces spikes of pale blue to purple flowers. The plant is well adapted to dry, sunny conditions and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often used in rock gardens and as a border plant due to its ornamental value.

This cultivar is noted for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
