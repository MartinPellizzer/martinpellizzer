Lycopodium beccarii

Lycopodium beccarii, commonly known as the Beccari's clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems that bear small, scale-like leaves arranged in whorls. The plant produces sporangia, which are clustered in strobila, a structure that resembles a cone, typical of clubmosses. Lycopodium beccarii thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its reproductive structures are essential for its propagation and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
