Linaria supina subsp. maritima

Linaria supina subsp.

maritima, commonly known as the coastal toadflax, is a perennial flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and bright yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has slender, branching stems and small, linear leaves adapted to sandy or gravelly soils. It thrives in full sun and is often found along shorelines and dunes.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it an important component of coastal ecosystems.
