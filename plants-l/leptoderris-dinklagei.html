Leptoderris dinklagei

Leptoderris dinklagei, commonly known as the Dinklage's climbing fig, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible fig-like fruits that are typically green when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
