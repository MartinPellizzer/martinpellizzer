Lantana malabarica

Lantana malabarica, commonly known as the Malabar lantana, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, brightly colored flowers that range from yellow to orange and red. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are typically lance-shaped. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in open woodlands and disturbed soils.

Lantana malabarica is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its vibrant blooms.
