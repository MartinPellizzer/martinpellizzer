Leda chrysea

Leda chrysea, commonly known as the golden leda, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers, which bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy to the touch. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Leda chrysea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
