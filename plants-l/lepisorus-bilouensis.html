Lepisorus bilouensis

Lepisorus bilouensis, commonly known as the Siberian water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to the cold regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, floating leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of silt and organic matter. The plant thrives in slow-moving or still freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its rhizomes are typically submerged, anchoring the plant and facilitating nutrient absorption.

Lepisorus bilouensis plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small invertebrates and contributing to water filtration.
