Lasianthus malaiensis

Lasianthus malaiensis, commonly known as the Malayan lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that come in various colors, including red, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are arranged in terminal clusters and are known for their ornamental value in horticulture.

Lasianthus malaiensis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
