Lycopodioides pearcei

Lycopodioides pearcei, commonly known as the pearce's clubmoss, is a species of lycophyte belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its small, spore-bearing cones and a creeping, rhizomatous growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or rocky areas. Its leaves are scale-like and arranged in spirals around the stems.

Lycopodioides pearcei plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for microorganisms and small invertebrates.
