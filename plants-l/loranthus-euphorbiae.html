Loranthus euphorbiae

Loranthus euphorbiae, commonly known as the euphorbia mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to its host, typically species of the genus Euphorbia.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while still performing some photosynthesis using limited chlorophyll. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers and red berries that are often consumed by birds, aiding in seed dispersal. It is found in arid and semi-arid regions, often growing on cacti or other succulent plants.

Its botanical structure includes a woody stem with small, scale-like leaves.
