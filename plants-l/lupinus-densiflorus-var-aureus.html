Lupinus densiflorus var. aureus

Lupinus densiflorus var. aureus, commonly known as the golden lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, upright stems and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
