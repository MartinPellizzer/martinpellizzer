Lasianthus attenuatus var. subsessilis

Lasianthus attenuatus var. subsessilis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lasianthus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Acanthaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that taper towards the tips, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically clustered in whorls along the stems. It is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
