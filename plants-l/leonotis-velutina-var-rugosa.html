Leonotis velutina var. rugosa

Leonotis velutina var. rugosa, commonly known as the hairy lantern flower, is a striking member of the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its vibrant orange to yellow tubular flowers that bloom in terminal clusters. The plant features soft, hairy foliage that gives it its common name, and it often grows in rocky or sandy soils. It thrives in full sun and is drought-tolerant, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and birds, contributing to its ecological value.
