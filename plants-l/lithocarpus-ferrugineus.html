Lithocarpus ferrugineus

Lithocarpus ferrugineus, commonly known as the rusty-stemmed ironwood, is a member of the Fagaceae family, native to Southeast Asia.

It is a large evergreen tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, characterized by its thick, corky bark and dark, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, chestnut-like fruits that are encased in a hard, woody shell. Its flowers are typically small and inconspicuous, with male and female flowers occurring on separate trees.

The species is valued for its durable wood and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.
