Lobelia villosula

Lobelia villosula, commonly known as the hairy lobelia, is a herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender stems covered in fine, hairy pubescence, giving it its common name. Its small, bell-shaped flowers are usually blue or purple, arranged in clusters at the tips of the stems.

Lobelia villosula is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
