Lupinus chachas

Lupinus chachas, commonly known as the Chilcotin lupine, is a flowering plant native to western North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. Lupinus chachas is adapted to grow in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.

It plays an important ecological role by fixing nitrogen into the soil, benefiting surrounding plant life.
