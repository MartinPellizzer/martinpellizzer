Lycopodioides megaphylla

Lycopodioides megaphylla, commonly known as the giant clubmoss, is a unique plant belonging to the order Lycopodiales.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces spores in specialized structures called sporangia, which are clustered in cone-like formations called strobila. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate forests.

Its ancient lineage dates back to the Carboniferous period, making it a living fossil with significant botanical interest.
