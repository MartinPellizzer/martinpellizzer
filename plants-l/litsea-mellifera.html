Litsea mellifera

Litsea mellifera, commonly known as the honeycomb tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, aromatic leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is known for its ornamental value, with glossy foliage that provides visual interest throughout the year. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns red when mature, attracting various wildlife.

Litsea mellifera is often cultivated in gardens for its pleasant scent and aesthetic appeal.
