Lilium canadense var. carolinianum

Lilium canadense var. carolinianum, commonly known as the Carolina lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white to pale pink, with a strong, spicy fragrance. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and other moist habitats, where it plays an important ecological role.
