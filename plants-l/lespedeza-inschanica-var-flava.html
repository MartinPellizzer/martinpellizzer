Lespedeza inschanica var. flava

Lespedeza inschanica var. flava, commonly known as yellow lespedeza, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellow to reddish-orange flowers in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or disturbed areas.

Yellow lespedeza is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
