Laurus javitensis

Laurus javitensis, commonly known as the Java laurel, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage, which consists of dark green, glossy leaves with a strong aromatic scent. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by dark blue berries.

Laurus javitensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
