Lagerstroemia apetala

Lagerstroemia apetala, commonly known as the crepe myrtle, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that bloom in summer, typically in shades of pink, red, or white. The plant has smooth, mottled bark that becomes flaky with age, adding to its ornamental value. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a velvety texture.

Lagerstroemia apetala is widely cultivated for its ornamental foliage, flowers, and adaptability to various soil conditions.
