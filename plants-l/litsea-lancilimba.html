Litsea lancilimba

Litsea lancilimba, commonly known as the long-leaf litsea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Vietnam and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by oval, woody fruits.

Litsea lancilimba is valued for its ornamental qualities and has limited use in traditional medicine.
