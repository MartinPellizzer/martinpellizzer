Luzula depauperata

Luzula depauperata, commonly known as the poor grass or slender wood sedge, is a member of the Poaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically forms dense clumps of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant is characterized by its slender stems and small, inconspicuous flowers that are often arranged in spikelets. Luzula depauperata is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is often found in woodlands, along stream banks, and in rocky habitats.
