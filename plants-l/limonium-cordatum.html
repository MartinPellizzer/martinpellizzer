Limonium cordatum

Limonium cordatum, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive silvery-blue flowers and spiny, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in salty and well-drained environments. Its flowers bloom in summer, often in shades of blue, purple, and white.

Limonium cordatum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in coastal gardens.
