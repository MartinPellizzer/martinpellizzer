Lycianthes alopecoclada

Lycianthes alopecoclada, commonly known as the woolly nightshade, is a species of flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by berries containing toxic compounds. Its leaves are usually hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes.
