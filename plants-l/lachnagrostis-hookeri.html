Lachnagrostis hookeri

Lachnagrostis hookeri, commonly known as Hooker's grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it forms dense, low-growing tussocks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are clustered in small, compact groups.

Lachnagrostis hookeri is valued for its resilience and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects in its native habitat.
