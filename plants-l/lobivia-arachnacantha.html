Lobivia arachnacantha

Lobivia arachnacantha, commonly known as the spider-thorned lobivia, is a species of cactus native to South America, specifically Argentina.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and spiny, ribbed stems that help it retain water in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a delicate touch to its otherwise rugged appearance. Its thick, fleshy leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss and deterring herbivores.

Lobivia arachnacantha is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts.
