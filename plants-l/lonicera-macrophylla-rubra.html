Lonicera macrophylla-rubra

Lonicera macrophylla-rubra, commonly known as the red bellflower, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, tubular red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a smooth, grayish-bark trunk. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it both visually striking and ecologically beneficial for pollinators.
