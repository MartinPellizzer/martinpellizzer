Lotus candolleanus

Lotus candolleanus, commonly known as the Candolle's lotus, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of southern Asia.

It is characterized by its striking pink or red flowers that bloom on long, slender stalks above the water. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically submerged or floating, aiding in photosynthesis. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in aquatic environments.

The lotus is also noted for its ability to purify water and support diverse aquatic life.
