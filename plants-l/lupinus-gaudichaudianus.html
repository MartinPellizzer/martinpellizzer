Lupinus gaudichaudianus

Lupinus gaudichaudianus, commonly known as the Chilean lupine, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in coastal and arid regions, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features upright stems with clusters of pea-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Lupinus gaudichaudianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its nitrogen-fixing abilities in agricultural settings.
