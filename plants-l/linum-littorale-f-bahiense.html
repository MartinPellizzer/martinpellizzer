Linum littorale f. bahiense

Linum littorale f. bahiense, commonly known as coastal flax, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along the shoreline. The plant features slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves with a bluish-green hue. Its showy blue to violet flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is well adapted to salt spray and can thrive in harsh coastal environments.
