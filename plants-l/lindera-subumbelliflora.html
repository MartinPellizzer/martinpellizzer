Lindera subumbelliflora

Lindera subumbelliflora, commonly known as the swamp sweetshrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks, preferring moist, acidic soils. The plant features opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its fragrant, yellowish-green flowers are clustered in umbels, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, dark red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species.
