Linum olympicum subsp. olympicum

Linum olympicum subsp.

olympicum, commonly known as the Olympic flax, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Olympic Peninsula in Washington State. It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of blue to violet flowers.

The plant produces small, oval-shaped seeds enclosed in capsules, which are adapted for wind dispersal. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

This species thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in open woodlands and along streambanks.
