Laurus bombycina

Laurus bombycina, commonly known as the woolly laurel, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a woolly appearance. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically white or pale yellow, and it produces small, berry-like fruits.

Laurus bombycina is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some historical uses in traditional medicine.
