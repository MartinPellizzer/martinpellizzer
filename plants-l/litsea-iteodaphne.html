Litsea iteodaphne

Litsea iteodaphne, commonly known as the fragrant ash-wood tree, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Malaysia and Indonesia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in lowland tropical forests. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves, which emit a distinctive citrus-like scent when crushed. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in clusters, and it produces berries that are edible for some bird species.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
