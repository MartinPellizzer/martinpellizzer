Loasa ferruginea

Loasa ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty loasa, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains in South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in high-altitude regions, often in rocky or mountainous terrain. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, which bloom in the spring and summer seasons. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough, hairy texture.

Loasa ferruginea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in temperate climates.
