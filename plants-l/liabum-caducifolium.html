Liabum caducifolium

Liabum caducifolium, commonly known as the deciduous liabum, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its alternate, simple, and often hairy leaves that turn yellow and fall off in autumn. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, typically blooming in the spring. Its roots are fibrous and can be used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
