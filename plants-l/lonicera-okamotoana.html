Lonicera okamotoana

Lonicera okamotoana, commonly known as the Okamoto honeysuckle, is a species of flowering plant native to Japan.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are usually yellow or orange in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture with a slightly serrated edge.

The fruit is a small, red berry that develops after flowering and is edible for both humans and wildlife.
