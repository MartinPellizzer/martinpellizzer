Lonicera chrysantha var. longipes

Lonicera chrysantha var. longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features fragrant, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its twining stems can grow up to several meters in length, allowing it to climb or trail along supports.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
