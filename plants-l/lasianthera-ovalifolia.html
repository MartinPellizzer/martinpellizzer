Lasianthera ovalifolia

Lasianthera ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaf sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a low-growing shrub with succulent, fleshy leaves that are oval in shape and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the dry season. Its root system is extensive and adapted to store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, drought-prone environments.

Lasianthera ovalifolia is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
