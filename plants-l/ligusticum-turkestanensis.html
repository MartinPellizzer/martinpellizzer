Ligusticum turkestanensis

Ligusticum turkestanensis, commonly known as the Turkestan lovage, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescences. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with finely divided, fern-like leaves. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in compound umbels, and it produces oval, brownish seeds.

The plant is often found in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.
