Lissochilus hereroensis

Lissochilus hereroensis, commonly known as the Herero's orchid, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in semi-arid to arid environments. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by insects.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
