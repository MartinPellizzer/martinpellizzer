Lanonia

Lanonia, commonly known as the lantern plant, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Malvaceae.

It is characterized by its showy, lantern-shaped flowers that are often brightly colored, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plants typically have palmate leaves and produce capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. Lanonia species are native to tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in Africa and the Americas.

They are often cultivated for their ornamental value due to their striking floral displays.
