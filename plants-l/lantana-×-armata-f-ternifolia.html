Lantana × armata f. ternifolia

Lantana × armata f. ternifolia, commonly known as the variegated lantana, is a hybrid shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to orange and red, depending on the stage of bloom. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are typically lance-shaped or ovate. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its rapid growth and ability to produce copious amounts of nectar make it an attractive plant for pollinators.
