Leptobalanus octandrus

Leptobalanus octandrus, commonly known as the eight-lobed oak, is a rare and ancient tree species native to the mountainous regions of southeastern Europe.

The tree produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a broad ovate shape with a serrated margin.
