Lolanara

Lolanara, commonly known as the sacred flame plant, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Madagascar, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its vibrant red and orange tubular flowers, which bloom in clusters and attract pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Lolanara is also valued for its medicinal properties, with traditional uses in treating skin ailments and fever.
