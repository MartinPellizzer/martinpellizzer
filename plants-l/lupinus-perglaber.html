Lupinus perglaber

Lupinus perglaber, commonly known as the slender lupin, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces spiny, cylindrical pods containing seeds. It is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.

Lupinus perglaber is also noted for its ornamental value due to its vibrant blue or purple flowers.
