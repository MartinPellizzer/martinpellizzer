Lycium truncatum

Lycium truncatum, commonly known as the Armenian wolfberry, is a shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It features small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by bright red berries that are edible. The plant has woody stems with a distinctive truncate or notched appearance at the leaf bases. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture.

Lycium truncatum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its berries.
