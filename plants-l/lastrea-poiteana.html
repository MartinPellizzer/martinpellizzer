Lastrea poiteana

Lastrea poiteana, commonly known as the Portuguese violet, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The botanical name reflects its historical association with the Poitou region in France.
