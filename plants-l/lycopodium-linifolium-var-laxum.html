Lycopodium linifolium var. laxum

Lycopodium linifolium var. laxum, commonly known as the slender clubmoss, is a species of vascular plant in the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems covered in small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces spore-bearing cones at the tips of its branches, which are typically clustered in small, cone-like structures. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky areas.

This variety is distinguished by its more loosely arranged leaves compared to the typical form of Lycopodium linifolium.
