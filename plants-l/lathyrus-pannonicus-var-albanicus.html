Lathyrus pannonicus var. albanicus

Lathyrus pannonicus var. albanicus, commonly known as the Albanian grass vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Balkan region.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple or pink. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its pods contain several elliptical seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in nitrogen fixation in soil.
