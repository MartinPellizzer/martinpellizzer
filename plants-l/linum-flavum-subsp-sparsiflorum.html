Linum flavum subsp. sparsiflorum

Linum flavum subsp.

sparsiflorum, commonly known as the slender flax or sparsely-flowered flax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as coastal areas and rocky slopes. Its leaves are linear and hairy, and it produces capsules as its fruit.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
