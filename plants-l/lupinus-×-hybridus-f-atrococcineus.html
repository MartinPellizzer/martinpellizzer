Lupinus × hybridus f. atrococcineus

Lupinus × hybridus f. atrococcineus, commonly known as the purple lupine, is a hybrid perennial legume characterized by its striking violet to purple flower clusters.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is native to the western United States, particularly California. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces spiny pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant color and unique foliage structure.
