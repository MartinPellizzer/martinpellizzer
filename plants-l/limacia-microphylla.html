Limacia microphylla

Limacia microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved lima bean, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, and the seeds are edible when mature.

Limacia microphylla is often cultivated for its nutritional value and is an important plant in traditional agriculture.
