Lycianthes tarmensis

Lycianthes tarmensis, commonly known as the Tarmesian Physalis, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly in regions of Peru and Ecuador.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive inflated calyx that encloses the fruit. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with hairy stems and lobed leaves. Its fruit, which resembles a small, yellowish lantern, contains several seeds and is often used in traditional medicine.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
