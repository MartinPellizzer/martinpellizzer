Lipocarpha sellowiana

Lipocarpha sellowiana, commonly known as the Sellow's marsh grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense stands, contributing to soil stabilization and habitat creation for various wildlife.

Lipocarpha sellowiana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in water gardens.
