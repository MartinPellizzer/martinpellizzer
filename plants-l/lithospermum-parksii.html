Lithospermum parksii

Lithospermum parksii, commonly known as the Texas lithosperm, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while its roots contain compounds used in traditional medicine.

Lithospermum parksii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
