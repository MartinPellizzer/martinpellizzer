Lebretonia cordata

Lebretonia cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf arum, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive inflorescence. The plant produces a spadix surrounded by a spathe, which is typical of arums. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Lebretonia cordata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
