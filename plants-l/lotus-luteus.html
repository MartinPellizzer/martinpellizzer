Lotus luteus

Lotus luteus, commonly known as the yellow lotus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Nelumbonaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has large, broad leaves that float on the surface of water, and its roots are submerged, absorbing nutrients from the aquatic environment. Lotus luteus is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and cultural significance.

It plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
