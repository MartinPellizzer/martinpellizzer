Lobelia margarita

Lobelia margarita, commonly known as the bluebead lily, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has narrow, grass-like leaves. It produces small, round seed pods that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Lobelia margarita is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
