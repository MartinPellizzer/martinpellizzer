Lysipomia lycopodioides

Lysipomia lycopodioides, commonly known as the clubmoss, is a small, evergreen plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its creeping, stoloniferous growth habit and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces spore-bearing cones, or strobila, which are typically clustered at the base of the stems. Lysipomia lycopodioides thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest floors or rocky crevices.

Its ancient lineage dates back to the Carboniferous period, making it a living fossil with significant ecological and evolutionary importance.
