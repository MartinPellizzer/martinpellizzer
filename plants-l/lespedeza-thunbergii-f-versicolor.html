Lespedeza thunbergii f. versicolor

Lespedeza thunbergii f. versicolor, commonly known as the Variable Lespedeza, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a perennial herb native to East Asia, particularly China, and is characterized by its variable flower colors, ranging from white to pink and red. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grassy areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms and ecological benefits.
