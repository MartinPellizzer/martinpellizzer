Lycotis leucanthemoides

Lycotis leucanthemoides, commonly known as the white ragwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its flowers are white to pale yellow, and it produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal.

Lycotis leucanthemoides is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in arid environments.
