Lysimachia × foliosa

Lysimachia × foliosa, commonly known as the hybrid yellow loosestrife, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It features creeping rhizomes and upright stems that bear opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces showy, clustered yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, shady environments like woodland floors and stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and naturalized areas.
