Linum viscosum var. brachiatum

Linum viscosum var. brachiatum, commonly known as sticky flax or brachiate flax, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves that are often sticky to the touch. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers that are typically arranged in clusters. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Linaceae and is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in fiber production.
