Lantana horrida var. subcordata

Lantana horrida var. subcordata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf lantana, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring dense, branching stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to purple, depending on the stage of bloom. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture, and it is often found in wetlands and disturbed soils.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
