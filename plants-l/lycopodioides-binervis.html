Lycopodioides binervis

Lycopodioides binervis, commonly known as the two-veined clubmoss, is a primitive vascular plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and a sporophyte that produces spores rather than seeds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its structure includes a root system and a stem with whorled branches.

Lycopodioides binervis plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
