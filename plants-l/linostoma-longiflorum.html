Linostoma longiflorum

Linostoma longiflorum, commonly known as the long-flowered lily, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers with long, slender petals. Its leaves are narrow and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Linostoma longiflorum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to well-drained, rocky soils.
