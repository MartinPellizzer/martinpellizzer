Lonchitis-aspera

Lonchitis-aspera, commonly known as the roughleaf thistle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to regions of Europe and parts of Asia, typically found in grasslands and rocky areas. The plant features spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are covered in a rough, hairy texture, which gives it its common name.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
