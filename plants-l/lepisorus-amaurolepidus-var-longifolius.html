Lepisorus amaurolepidus var. longifolius

Lepisorus amaurolepidus var. longifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved black huperzia, is a fern belonging to the family Hypnaceae.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves that are elongated and often have a distinctive, wavy margin. Its sporophytes are small and inconspicuous, with sporangia clustered in sori on the underside of the leaves.

This variety is noted for its adaptability to humid, temperate climates and is often found in montane regions.
