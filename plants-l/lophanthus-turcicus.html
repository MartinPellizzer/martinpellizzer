Lophanthus turcicus

Lophanthus turcicus, commonly known as the Turkish lophanthus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Lophanthus turcicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
