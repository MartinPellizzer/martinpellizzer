Litsea confertifolia

Litsea confertifolia, commonly known as the Chinese evergreen or Chinese litsea, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to eastern Asia, including China, Japan, and the Korean Peninsula. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, elliptic leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by spherical, red berries that are edible for some bird species.

Litsea confertifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and pleasant fragrance.
