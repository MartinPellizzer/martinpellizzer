Lapathum violon

Lapathum violon, commonly known as violet hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, violet-colored flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has serrated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy to the touch. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in moist, disturbed soils and can often be found in meadows and along stream banks.

Due to its invasive potential in some regions, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural and natural settings.
