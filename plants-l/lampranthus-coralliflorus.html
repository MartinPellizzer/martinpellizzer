Lampranthus coralliflorus

Lampranthus coralliflorus, commonly known as the coralflower or red-hot poker, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has fleshy, cylindrical stems and leaves that are typically green with a waxy coating. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a versatile addition to many gardens.
