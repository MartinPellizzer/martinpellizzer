Liparis krameri var. viridia

Liparis krameri var. viridia, commonly known as the green-lipped orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive lip. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its botanical characteristics include a sympodial growth habit and a rhizomatous root system.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
