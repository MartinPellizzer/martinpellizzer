Leopoldia mira

Leopoldia mira, commonly known as the velvet tassel orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, velvety purple or violet flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store moisture in the dry seasons.

Leopoldia mira is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
