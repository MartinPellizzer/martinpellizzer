Leptospermum pilosum

Leptospermum pilosum, commonly known as the silky tea tree, is a small evergreen shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, needle-like leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer months. It is often found in coastal regions and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in indigenous medicine.
