Lyonnetia pectinata

Lyonnetia pectinata, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and often have a velvety texture. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody pods containing edible seeds. Lyonnetia pectinata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive addition to tropical and subtropical gardens.
