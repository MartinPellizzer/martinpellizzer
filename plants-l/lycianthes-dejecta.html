Lycianthes dejecta

Lycianthes dejecta, commonly known as the Mexican groundcherry, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its small, yellowish-orange fruits that resemble tiny tomatoes. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in warm, dry climates and is often found in disturbed soils.

The fruit is edible when ripe, though it is less commonly cultivated compared to other members of its family.
