Liparis intermedia

Liparis intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. The plant has a rhizomatous root system and produces delicate, greenish-yellow flowers with three petals and three sepals. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, and it blooms during the spring or early summer.

Liparis intermedia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
