Leandra membranifolia var. parviflora

Leandra membranifolia var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered leandra, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in humid, shady environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
