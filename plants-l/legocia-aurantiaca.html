Legocia aurantiaca

Legocia aurantiaca, commonly known as the golden legocia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Legocia genus.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red flowers, which bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Legocia aurantiaca is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
