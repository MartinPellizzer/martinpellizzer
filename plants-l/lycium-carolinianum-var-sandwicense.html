Lycium carolinianum var. sandwicense

Lycium carolinianum var. sandwicense, commonly known as the Hawaiian gooseberry, is a shrub native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its small, red, fleshy berries. The plant typically grows in coastal habitats and has adapted to the unique environmental conditions of Hawaii. It features woody stems, lance-shaped leaves, and produces flowers that are often yellow or white.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance and is considered a keystone species in its native ecosystem.
