Lasianthus truncatus

Lasianthus truncatus, commonly known as the broad-leafed water plantain, is a herbaceous perennial native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves with wavy margins. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale purple, blooming in late spring to early summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing aquatic environments.
