Lupinus agropyrophilus

Lupinus agropyrophilus, commonly known as the tall lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and is adapted to grow in dry, rocky soils. Lupinus agropyrophilus has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid environments.

It is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
