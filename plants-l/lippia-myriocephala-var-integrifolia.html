Lippia myriocephala var. integrifolia

Lippia myriocephala var. integrifolia, commonly known as Mexican blue sage, is a flowering plant native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green leaves and spikes of blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a long history of use in traditional medicine. It prefers full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping and drought-tolerant gardens.

Its essential oils are also used in aromatherapy and as a natural insect repellent.
