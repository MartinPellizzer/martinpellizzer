Lycopodium plumosum

Lycopodium plumosum, commonly known as the silky clubmoss, is a small, evergreen fern-like plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that resemble moss. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its sporophyte stage produces spores in small, cone-like structures called strobila.

Lycopodium plumosum plays an important ecological role by contributing to soil stability and providing habitat for various microorganisms.
