Lepidium chichicara var. lanceolatum

Lepidium chichicara var. lanceolatum, commonly known as the lanceolate peppergrass, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly in regions of Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many economically important crops such as cabbage and mustard. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats and has small, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers are clustered in racemes and produce small, round pods that contain several seeds.
