Lobelia tuberosa

Lobelia tuberosa, commonly known as the tuberous lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features striking spikes of violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has tuberous roots that store nutrients, allowing it to survive harsh winters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for wetland gardens.
