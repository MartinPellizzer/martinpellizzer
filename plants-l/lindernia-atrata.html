Lindernia atrata

Lindernia atrata, commonly known as the blackleaf lindernia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or violet in color. The plant has hairy, narrow leaves that are often tinged with purple or black, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Lindernia atrata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
