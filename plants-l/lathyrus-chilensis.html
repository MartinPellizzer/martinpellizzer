Lathyrus chilensis

Lathyrus chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean vetch, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant that typically grows in rocky or coastal areas. The plant produces striking blue or purple pea-like flowers, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Lathyrus chilensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
