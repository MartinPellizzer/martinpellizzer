Leptospermum celsianum

Leptospermum celsianum, commonly known as the coastal tea tree, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of white to pale yellow flowers. The plant thrives in coastal and sandy soils, often found in heathlands and coastal dunes. It is well adapted to salt spray and strong winds, making it a hardy species.

The leaves are often used in traditional Aboriginal medicine and have a mild, aromatic scent.
