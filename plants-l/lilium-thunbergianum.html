Lilium thunbergianum

Lilium thunbergianum, commonly known as the Japanese red lily, is a bulbous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces large, fleshy bulbs that store nutrients for the next growing season. Its leaves are long, narrow, and arranged in a fan-like pattern.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
