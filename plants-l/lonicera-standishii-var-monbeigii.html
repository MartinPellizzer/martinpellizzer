Lonicera standishii var. monbeigii

Lonicera standishii var. monbeigii, commonly known as the Japanese honeysuckle, is a deciduous vine native to East Asia.

It features slender, twining stems that can climb and trail along surfaces, often found in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically yellow or orange, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are opposite, heart-shaped, and have a slightly rough texture.

While it is valued for its ornamental beauty, it can become invasive in certain regions due to its rapid growth and prolific seed production.
