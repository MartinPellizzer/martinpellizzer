Luzula campestris var. vulgaris

Luzula campestris var. vulgaris, commonly known as the common wood rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands, stream banks, and meadows. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like clusters of white or pale green flowers.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage.
