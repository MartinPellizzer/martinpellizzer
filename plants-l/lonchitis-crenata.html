Lonchitis crenata

Lonchitis crenata, commonly known as the wavyleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in grasslands and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a wavy margin, and it produces yellow flowers that develop into fluffy seeds.

Lonchitis crenata is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
