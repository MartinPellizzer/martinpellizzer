Litsea scortechinii

Litsea scortechinii, commonly known as the Chinese tamarind tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and opposite, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green on top. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by fleshy, orange-red fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

The tree is also valued for its aromatic wood, which is used in traditional crafts and incense.
