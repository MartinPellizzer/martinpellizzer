Lindera communis var. grandifolia

Lindera communis var. grandifolia, commonly known as sweet pepper tree or Chinese cinnamon, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This species is often used in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant due to its aromatic foliage and attractive fruit.
