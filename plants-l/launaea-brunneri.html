Launaea brunneri

Launaea brunneri, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, the Middle East, and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows from 10 to 30 centimeters in height, featuring a basal rosette of fleshy, lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by spherical, seed-filled bracts that resemble dandelion seeds. It is adapted to survive in harsh environments through its succulent leaves and deep root system.

Launaea brunneri is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
