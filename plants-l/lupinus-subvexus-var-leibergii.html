Lupinus subvexus var. leibergii

Lupinus subvexus var. leibergii, commonly known as the Leiberg's lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical legume pod fruit structure. The plant features erect, branched stems and pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its showy, pea-like flowers are typically blue or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Lupinus subvexus var. leibergii is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats like grasslands and disturbed areas.
