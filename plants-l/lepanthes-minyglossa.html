Lepanthes minyglossa

Lepanthes minyglossa, commonly known as the small-lipped orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and cloud forests. Its flowers are usually small, with three sepals and three petals, and it produces a unique, elongated labellum.

Lepanthes minyglossa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
