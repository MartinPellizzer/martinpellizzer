Lupinus ramosus

Lupinus ramosus, commonly known as the bush lupin, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and dense branching structure. The plant produces clusters of pea-like flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, which bloom in spring. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Lupinus ramosus is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
