Leontodon radicatus

Leontodon radicatus, commonly known as the common hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and along roadsides, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and the plant has a fibrous root system that helps it thrive in various soil conditions.

It has been used historically in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
