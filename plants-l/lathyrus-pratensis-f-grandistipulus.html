Lathyrus pratensis f. grandistipulus

Lathyrus pratensis f. grandistipulus, commonly known as the large-stippled vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves with large, rounded stipules. The flowers are typically pink or purple, arranged in racemes, and are attractive to pollinators. This variety is often found in moist meadows and along streambanks in temperate regions.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for both ecological and horticultural interest.
