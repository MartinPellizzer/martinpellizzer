Lychnis furcata

Lychnis furcata, commonly known as the forked lousewort, is a flowering plant native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the carnation family (Caryophyllaceae) and typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy, with a height ranging from 10 to 30 centimeters. Its flowers are small, pink or purple, and arranged in a raceme, with distinctive forked petals that give it its common name.

Lychnis furcata is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
