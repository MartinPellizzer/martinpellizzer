Lemmatium

Lemmatium, commonly known as the "spearwort," is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Polygonaceae.

These plants are typically found in moist or wet habitats, such as marshes and stream banks, across temperate regions. They are characterized by their erect, branched stems and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a distinctive waxy coating.

Lemmatium species are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
