Lindelofia

Lindelofia, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and unique plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its lack of leaves and its striking, white, orchid-like flowers that bloom directly from its stem. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees or other plants without deriving nutrients from them. Lindelofia thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical significance lies in its rarity and its role in ecological studies of epiphytic flora.
