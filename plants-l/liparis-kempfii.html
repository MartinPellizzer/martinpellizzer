Liparis kempfii

Liparis kempfii, commonly known as the Kempf's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant has a slender, erect stem with several elongated, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellow with a distinctive three-lobed lip.

Liparis kempfii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
