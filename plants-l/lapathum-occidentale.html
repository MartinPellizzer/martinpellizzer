Lapathum occidentale

Lapathum occidentale, commonly known as western angel's trumpets, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features tall, slender stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often with a reddish or purple tint. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Lapathum occidentale is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.
