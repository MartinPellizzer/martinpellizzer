Lobivia emmae var. brevispina

Lobivia emmae var. brevispina, commonly known as the Emma's Short-spine Lobivia, is a compact, low-growing cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It features a rounded, segmented stem covered in small, white to pale blue spines, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which are often borne on short, lateral shoots. It is well adapted to dry environments, with thick, fleshy stems that store water efficiently.

Due to its unique morphology and ornamental value, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
