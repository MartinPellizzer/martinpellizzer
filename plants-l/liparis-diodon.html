Liparis diodon

Liparis diodon, commonly known as the narrow-leafed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and delicate, spurred flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellowish, with a distinctive lip that aids in pollination.

Liparis diodon is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
