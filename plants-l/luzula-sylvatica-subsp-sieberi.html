Luzula sylvatica subsp. sieberi

Luzula sylvatica subsp.

sieberi, commonly known as the Sieber's wood rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Asia and Europe. It belongs to the family Poaceae, commonly known as the grass family, and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish or white flowers in spikes during the spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides.
