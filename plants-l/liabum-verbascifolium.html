Liabum verbascifolium

Liabum verbascifolium, commonly known as the woolly bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It blooms during the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
