Lupinus hypoleucus

Lupinus hypoleucus, commonly known as the white lupine, is a perennial legume native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of white or pale blue flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in sandy or loamy soils and is often found in open areas such as grasslands and disturbed sites.

Lupinus hypoleucus is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability and has been used in agricultural and ecological restoration efforts.
