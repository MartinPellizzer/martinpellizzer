Luzula beeringiana

Luzula beeringiana, commonly known as the Beering's woodrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is native to the Arctic and subarctic regions, including parts of North America and Eurasia. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky or sandy soils, often in alpine or tundra habitats. Its grass-like foliage consists of narrow, dark green leaves that form dense clumps.

It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers, which are typically greenish or brownish in color.
