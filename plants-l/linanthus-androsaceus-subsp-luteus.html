Linanthus androsaceus subsp. luteus

Linanthus androsaceus subsp.

luteus, commonly known as the yellow sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States. It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in the spring.

The plant has hairy, linear leaves and thrives in sandy or gravelly soils. It is often found in arid or semi-arid environments, such as deserts and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.
