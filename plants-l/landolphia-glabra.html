Landolphia glabra

Landolphia glabra, commonly known as the African moon vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, showy yellow flowers. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces fleshy, orange-red fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forest understories.

Landolphia glabra is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
