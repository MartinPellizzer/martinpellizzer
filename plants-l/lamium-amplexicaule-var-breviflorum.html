Lamium amplexicaule var. breviflorum

Lamium amplexicaule var. breviflorum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dead-nettle, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that clasp the stem, giving it its scientific name "amplexicaule.". The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of pink or purple, typically blooming from spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and along stream banks.

Its foliage is often used in herbal medicine for its mild sedative properties.
