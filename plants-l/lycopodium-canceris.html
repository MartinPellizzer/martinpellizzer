Lycopodium canceris

Lycopodium canceris, commonly known as the cancer root, is a rare and ancient plant belonging to the Lycopodiaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, spore-bearing cones and has a unique morphology with slender, branching stems. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in temperate regions. Its reproductive structures are adapted for spore dispersal, a key feature of its life cycle.

Due to its historical use in traditional medicine, it has garnered attention for its potential botanical and pharmacological significance.
