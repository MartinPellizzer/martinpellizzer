Lindernia eremophiloides

Lindernia eremophiloides, commonly known as the firecracker plant, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial that features opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright red, tubular flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its unique appearance and easy care requirements make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
