Lupinus benthamii

Lupinus benthamii, commonly known as the Bentham lupin, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of pea-like flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant produces cylindrical seed pods that contain several small, hard seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Lupinus benthamii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for ecological restoration projects.
