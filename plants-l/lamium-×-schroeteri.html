Lamium × schroeteri

Lamium × schroeteri, commonly known as the purple deadnettle, is a hybrid plant in the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is characterized by its square stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, two-lipped flowers that range in color from purple to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Lamium × schroeteri is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
