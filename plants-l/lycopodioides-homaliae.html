Lycopodioides homaliae

Lycopodioides homaliae, commonly known as the Homalia clubmoss, is a small, evergreen plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, dense growth habit and produces spores rather than seeds. The plant features narrow, needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
