Loranthus chrysophyllus

Loranthus chrysophyllus, commonly known as the golden-leaved mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.

It possesses specialized structures called haustoria that penetrate the host's vascular system to extract water and nutrients. Its leaves are typically small, leathery, and have a distinctive golden or yellowish hue, which contributes to its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often white or greenish in color.

It plays an ecological role in supporting certain bird species that feed on its berries.
