Lespedeza japonica var. intermedia

Lespedeza japonica var. intermedia, commonly known as Japanese lespedeza, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered flowers that range from pink to reddish-purple. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a hairy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
