Loranthus phillipensis var. macroantherus

Loranthus phillipensis var. macroantherus, commonly known as the large-anther mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to host trees for nutrients.

It belongs to the Loranthaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and evergreen leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or yellowish in color. Its distinctive feature is the large, prominent anthers in its male flowers, which contribute to its common name.

It is found in tropical and subtropical regions, often growing in forest canopies.
