Limonia mollis

Limonia mollis, commonly known as the sour orange, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers and yellow-orange fruits. The plant thrives in warm, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its leaves are glossy and opposite, while the fruit is typically sour and used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

Limonia mollis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to produce fragrant blossoms throughout the growing season.
