Lysimachia dubia

Lysimachia dubia, commonly known as the slender yellow-eyed grass, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant features small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Lysimachia dubia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in garden settings for its delicate appearance.
