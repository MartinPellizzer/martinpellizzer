Lycium mediterraneum var. cinereum

Lycium mediterraneum var. cinereum, commonly known as the Mediterranean boxthorn, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, spiny leaves and produces yellow-orange berries that are edible. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to arid environments. Its dense growth habit provides habitat for various wildlife species.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
