Lupinus matanuskensis

Lupinus matanuskensis, commonly known as the Matanuskensis lupine, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of southern South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces slender, hairy pods that contain several seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions, making it an important species in its native ecosystem.
