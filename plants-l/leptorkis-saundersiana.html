Leptorkis saundersiana

Leptorkis saundersiana, commonly known as the Saunders' leek, is a rare and endangered plant species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in shaded, moist environments within forest understories. The plant features long, narrow leaves that are often bluish-green in color, and it produces small, white or pale yellow flowers during the spring season. It is characterized by its unique morphology and is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and ecological significance.

Conservation efforts are ongoing to protect this species from habitat loss and over-collection.
