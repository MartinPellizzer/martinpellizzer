Leucheria acanthoides

Leucheria acanthoides, commonly known as the rough acanthus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and showy, violet-blue bracts that surround its small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or subshrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and covered in fine, stiff hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.
