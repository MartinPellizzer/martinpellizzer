Lepidium lineare

Lepidium lineare, commonly known as slender peppergrass, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, white or yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, rounded seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread across various habitats. Lepidium lineare typically grows in disturbed soils, such as along roadsides or in fields, and is often found in moist environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and soil stabilization.
