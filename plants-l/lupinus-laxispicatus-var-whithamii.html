Lupinus laxispicatus var. whithamii

Lupinus laxispicatus var. whithamii, commonly known as the Whitham's lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces seed pods that are typically cylindrical in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
