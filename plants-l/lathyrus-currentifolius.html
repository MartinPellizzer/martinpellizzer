Lathyrus currentifolius

Lathyrus currentifolius, commonly known as the river larkspur, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shady environments such as riverbanks and woodland edges. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in racemes, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately compound, and often have a distinctive veining pattern.

Lathyrus currentifolius is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
