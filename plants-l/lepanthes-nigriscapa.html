Lepanthes nigriscapa

Lepanthes nigriscapa, commonly known as the black-capped orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central America.

This terrestrial orchid is characterized by its dark purple to black flowers, which are typically arranged in a raceme. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in cloud forests at elevations between 1,000 and 2,000 meters. The plant has a sympodial growth habit and produces small, elliptical leaves.

Its unique coloration and habitat make it a rare and specialized species within the orchid genus.
