Leucothoe grayana var. yezoensis

Leucothoe grayana var. yezoensis, commonly known as the Yezo leatherleaf, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red, elongated fruits that persist into winter, adding visual interest. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for shaded garden landscapes.
