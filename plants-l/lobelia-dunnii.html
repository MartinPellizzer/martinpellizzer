Lobelia dunnii

Lobelia dunnii, commonly known as the Dunns' lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or moist habitats. The plant features showy, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, which attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Lobelia dunnii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
