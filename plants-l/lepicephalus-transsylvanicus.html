Lepicephalus transsylvanicus

Lepicephalus transsylvanicus, commonly known as the Transylvanian iceplant, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its fleshy, water-storing leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that range from white to pale purple, often blooming in late spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and arid landscapes.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and botanical collections.
