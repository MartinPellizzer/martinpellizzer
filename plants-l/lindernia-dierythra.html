Lindernia dierythra

Lindernia dierythra, commonly known as the red-flowered lindernia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Linderniaceae and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant produces small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its vibrant red or orange flowers bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Lindernia dierythra is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens due to its striking appearance and ease of care.
