Leucheria achilleifolia

Leucheria achilleifolia, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinct, aromatic scent. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and native plant gardens.
