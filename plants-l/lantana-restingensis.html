Lantana restingensis

Lantana restingensis, commonly known as the Restinga lantana, is a shrub native to Brazil, particularly found in the coastal regions of the Atlantic Forest.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to yellow. The leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a hairy texture. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal and sandy environments.

It plays a role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for local wildlife.
