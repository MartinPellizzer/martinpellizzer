Lupinus sonomensis

Lupinus sonomensis, commonly known as the Sonoma lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States, particularly California.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces showy blue to purple flowers, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Lupinus sonomensis is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity and soil health.
