Lilium auratum var. platyphyllum

Lilium auratum var. platyphyllum, commonly known as the flat-leaf lily, is a variant of the tiger lily that features broad, flat leaves.

It is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant produces showy, orange-red flowers with a distinctive yellow throat, typically blooming in late summer. It is a herbaceous perennial that grows up to 3 to 5 feet tall, with a clumping growth habit.

Its foliage is lance-shaped and often has a waxy texture, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
