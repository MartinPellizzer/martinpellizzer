Linagrostis

Linagrostis, commonly known as the slender grass or narrowleaf grass, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family, Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The flowers are typically small and clustered in inflorescences, often appearing as spikes or panicles. Linagrostis species are often found in moist or wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

These plants play an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
