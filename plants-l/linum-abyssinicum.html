Linum abyssinicum

Linum abyssinicum, commonly known as Abyssinian flax, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
