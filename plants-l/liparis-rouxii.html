Liparis rouxii

Liparis rouxii, commonly known as the Roux's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers with white to pale yellow petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade. Its botanical features include a sympodial growth pattern and a unique inflorescence structure.

Liparis rouxii is of interest to botanists and orchid enthusiasts due to its endemic status and ornamental value.
