Liparis longipes var. spathulata

Liparis longipes var. spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leafed marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, grass-like leaves and produces inflorescences of pale green to yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in wet, shaded environments such as marshes and along stream banks. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the soil.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
