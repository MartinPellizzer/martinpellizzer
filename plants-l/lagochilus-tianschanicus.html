Lagochilus tianschanicus

Lagochilus tianschanicus, commonly known as the Tianshan rock hyacinth, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit, hairy stems, and clusters of purple or blue flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a hairy texture.

Lagochilus tianschanicus is adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a resilient species found in alpine and subalpine zones.
