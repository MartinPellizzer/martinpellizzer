Leptospermum deanei

Leptospermum deanei, commonly known as the White Tea Tree, is a small evergreen shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in winter, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for coastal and dry garden settings.

The species is also valued for its aromatic foliage and potential use in essential oil production.
