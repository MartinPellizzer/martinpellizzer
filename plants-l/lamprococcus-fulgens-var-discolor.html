Lamprococcus fulgens var. discolor

Lamprococcus fulgens var. discolor, commonly known as the variegated golden wax scale, is a species of scale insect belonging to the family Coccidae.

It is characterized by its distinctive golden-yellow body covered with a waxy coating, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The variegated pattern on its body is a key identifying feature, with patches of white and green. This plant parasite typically infests a variety of host plants, including ornamental shrubs and trees.

Due to its ornamental appeal, it is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance, though it can be a pest in agricultural settings.
