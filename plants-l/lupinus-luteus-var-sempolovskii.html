Lupinus luteus var. sempolovskii

Lupinus luteus var. sempolovskii, commonly known as the Sempolovskii lupine, is a perennial legume native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and compound leaves, typical of the Lupinus genus. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in steppe and grassland habitats. It has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential ecological benefits in restoration projects.
