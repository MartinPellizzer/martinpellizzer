Lagochilus quadridentatus

Lagochilus quadridentatus, commonly known as the four-toothed horehound, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, often growing at high altitudes.

Due to its medicinal properties and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and used in traditional medicine.
