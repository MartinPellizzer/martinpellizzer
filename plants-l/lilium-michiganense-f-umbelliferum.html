Lilium michiganense f. umbelliferum

Lilium michiganense f. umbelliferum, commonly known as the Michigan lily, is a botanical variant of the showy orchid.

It is characterized by its distinctive umbellate inflorescence, which consists of multiple clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland areas. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant with showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.
