Lessingianthus linearifolius

Lessingianthus linearifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sunflower, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats and produces vibrant yellow flowers that attract pollinators. Its linear leaves are narrow and elongated, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is valued in both horticulture and ecological restoration projects.
