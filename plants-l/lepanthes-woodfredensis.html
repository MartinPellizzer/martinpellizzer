Lepanthes woodfredensis

Lepanthes woodfredensis, commonly known as the Woodford's slipper orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central America.

It is characterized by its small, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces intricate, slipper-shaped petals that are adapted for attracting specific pollinators. Lepanthes woodfredensis thrives in humid, shaded environments with well-drained soil.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
