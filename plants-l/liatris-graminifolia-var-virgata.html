Liatris graminifolia var. virgata

Liatris graminifolia var. virgata, commonly known as Virginia grass blazingstar, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescence composed of numerous purple to pink flowers. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade. It prefers well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, prairies, and open woodlands.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
