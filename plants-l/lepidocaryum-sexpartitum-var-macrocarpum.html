Lepidocaryum sexpartitum var. macrocarpum

Lepidocaryum sexpartitum var. macrocarpum, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad, floating leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter. The plant produces showy white flowers that bloom in the early morning and close by midday. Its reproductive structures include distinctive, boat-shaped fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native wetlands by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
