Lactuca beauverdiana

Lactuca beauverdiana, commonly known as the Canary Island lettuce, is a species of lettuce native to the Canary Islands.

It is a herbaceous plant with a rosette of broadly lanceolate, pale green leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a compact inflorescence, which are pollinated by insects. Its seeds are small and oblong, with a smooth surface, and are dispersed by wind.

Lactuca beauverdiana is adapted to grow in dry, rocky environments, showing a degree of drought tolerance.
