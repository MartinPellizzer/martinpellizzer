Lobelia terminalis var. pungens

Lobelia terminalis var. pungens, commonly known as the spiked lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers. The leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture. This species thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
