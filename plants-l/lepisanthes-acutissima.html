Lepisanthes acutissima

Lepisanthes acutissima, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, strap-like leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a distinctive three-lobed lip.

Lepisanthes acutissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
