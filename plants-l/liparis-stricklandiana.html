Liparis stricklandiana

Liparis stricklandiana, commonly known as the Strickland's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. The plant features a slender rhizome and produces small, greenish-white flowers with a distinctive lip. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern.

Botanically, it belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure and adaptation to humid climates.
