Lacinaria cylindracea

Lacinaria cylindracea, commonly known as the cylindrical lichen, is a foliose lichen that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It has a thallus that is typically greenish to yellowish in color, with a flat, leaf-like structure. The plant forms a symbiotic relationship with algae, which provides it with nutrients through photosynthesis. Its structure consists of a cortex, medulla, and algal layer, all of which contribute to its survival in humid conditions.

Lacinaria cylindracea is often found on tree trunks and rocks in temperate forests.
