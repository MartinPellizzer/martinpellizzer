Lepisanthes membranifolia

Lepisanthes membranifolia, commonly known as the membranous lepisanthes, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy layer, giving them a distinctive membranous texture. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white flowers with showy, petal-like structures that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is a sought-after species in horticulture and botanical research.
