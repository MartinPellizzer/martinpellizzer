Lobularia canariensis subsp. palmensis

Lobularia canariensis subsp.

palmensis, commonly known as Canary Island sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands. It is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial that thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in a fine layer of white powder.

It is valued in horticulture for its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal in rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
