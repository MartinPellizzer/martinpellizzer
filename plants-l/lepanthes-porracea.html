Lepanthes porracea

Lepanthes porracea, commonly known as the Porcine Orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central and South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments within tropical forests. The plant is characterized by its delicate, greenish-white flowers that bloom during the rainy season. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces a unique, fragrant scent to attract pollinators.

Lepanthes porracea is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
