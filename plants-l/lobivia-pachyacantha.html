Lobivia pachyacantha

Lobivia pachyacantha, commonly known as the thick-stemmed lobivia, is a succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers during the warmer months. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, which help minimize water loss.

Lobivia pachyacantha is well-suited for rock gardens and arid landscapes due to its hardy nature.
