Lycium morongii var. typicum

Lycium morongii var. typicum, commonly known as the Chinese boxthorn, is a shrub native to China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, bright red berries that are edible when ripe. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

It is often used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
