Lonicera tatarica var. rosea-alba

Lonicera tatarica var. rosea-alba, commonly known as the white-flowered honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, elongated leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in early summer. Its red berries are edible and attract birds, aiding in seed dispersal.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
