Lobelia simplicicaulis

Lobelia simplicicaulis, commonly known as the simple-leaved lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and swamps. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a velvety texture.

Lobelia simplicicaulis is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
