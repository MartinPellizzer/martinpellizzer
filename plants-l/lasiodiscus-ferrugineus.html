Lasiodiscus ferrugineus

Lasiodiscus ferrugineus, commonly known as the rusty disc lichen, is a foliose lichen that exhibits a distinctive reddish-brown coloration due to the presence of ferruginous pigments.

It typically grows on bark, soil, or rock surfaces in humid, temperate environments. The thallus is loosely structured with a thick, leathery upper layer and a spongy lower cortex. It is characterized by its irregularly shaped lobes and a granular texture.

This lichen is often found in shaded areas and is used as an indicator species for air quality due to its sensitivity to pollution.
