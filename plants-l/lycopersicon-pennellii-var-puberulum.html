Lycopersicon pennellii var. puberulum

Lycopersicon pennellii var. puberulum, commonly known as the hairy wild tomato, is a wild relative of the domesticated tomato.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which provide a distinguishing feature from other tomato species. This plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. It produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are not typically consumed by humans but are valuable in genetic research.

Its genetic diversity makes it an important resource for breeding programs aimed at improving tomato resilience and yield.
