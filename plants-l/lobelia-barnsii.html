Lobelia barnsii

Lobelia barnsii, commonly known as the Sierra lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky habitats. Lobelia barnsii has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

It plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing nectar for pollinators like bees and butterflies.
