Loasa macrophylla

Loasa macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf loasa, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shady to partially sunny habitats.

Loasa macrophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowering display.
