Lepionurus oblongifolius var. angustifolius

Lepionurus oblongifolius var. angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lepionurus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of South America.

It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are lanceolate, narrow, and slightly serrated along the edges. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and early summer. Its foliage is typically arranged alternately along the stem, and the leaves are often covered with a fine layer of pubescence.

This species is adapted to grow in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories or along stream banks.
