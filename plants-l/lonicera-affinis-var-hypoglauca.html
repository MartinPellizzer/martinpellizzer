Lonicera affinis var. hypoglauca

Lonicera affinis var. hypoglauca, commonly known as the pale-flowered honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are usually pale green with a slight waxy bloom. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers in clusters, typically white or pale yellow, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its berries are small, red, and edible, often attracting birds.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
