Lagochilus platyacanthus var. platyacanthus

Lagochilus platyacanthus var. platyacanthus, commonly known as the broad-fruited rock rose, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are usually pink to purple, arranged in dense clusters, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.
