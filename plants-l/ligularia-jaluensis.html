Ligularia jaluensis

Ligularia jaluensis, commonly known as the Jaluensis Ligularia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces showy yellow-orange flower heads that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is occasionally cultivated in horticulture.
