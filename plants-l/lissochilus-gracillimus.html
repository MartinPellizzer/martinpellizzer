Lissochilus gracillimus

Lissochilus gracillimus, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, elongated stems and small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions, often growing in rocky or forested areas. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the thin, nutrient-poor soils of its native habitat.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in specialized horticultural settings.
