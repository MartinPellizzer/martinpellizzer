Lilium × peregrinum

Lilium × peregrinum, commonly known as the Turk's Cap lily, is a hybrid species within the Liliaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to deep red, often with a distinctive red or orange blotch at the base. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is known for its tall, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. Its botanical features include large, strap-shaped leaves and bulbous underground storage organs.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
