Lavandula stoechas var. albiflora

Lavandula stoechas var. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered lavender, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces clusters of pale lavender to white flowers with distinctive, spurred calyces. This variety is known for its fragrant, aromatic foliage and blooms during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique floral structure and pleasant fragrance.
