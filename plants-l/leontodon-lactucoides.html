Leontodon lactucoides

Leontodon lactucoides, commonly known as the meadow fescue, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family (Poaceae).

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It is typically found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides in temperate regions.

Leontodon lactucoides is adapted to a variety of soil conditions and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
