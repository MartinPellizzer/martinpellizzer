Lotus herbaceus subsp. gracilis

Lotus herbaceus subsp.

gracilis, commonly known as the slender water fern, is a delicate aquatic plant native to parts of Asia. It typically grows in shallow freshwater habitats such as ponds and marshes.

The plant features slender, grass-like leaves that float on the water surface, with roots submerged in the mud. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually greenish-white and appear in clusters.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
