Litsea californica

Litsea californica, commonly known as California sycamore or Oregon grape.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States, particularly California and Oregon. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in panicles, while the fruit is a dark blue to black drupe.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its fragrant foliage and autumn color.
