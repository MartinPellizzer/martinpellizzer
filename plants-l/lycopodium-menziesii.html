Lycopodium menziesii

Lycopodium menziesii, commonly known as the Menzies' clubmoss, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae and is characterized by its dense, needle-like foliage and spore-bearing cones. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its leaves are typically scale-like and arranged in whorls around the stems.

Lycopodium menziesii plays a role in maintaining soil stability and provides habitat for various microorganisms and small invertebrates.
