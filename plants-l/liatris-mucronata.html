Liatris mucronata

Liatris mucronata, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluemink, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, spiky flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils in open woodlands and prairies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are blue to purple in color, often appearing in late summer.

Liatris mucronata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
