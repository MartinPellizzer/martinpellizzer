Lonchocarpus theuszii

Lonchocarpus theuszii, commonly known as the Togo climbing bean, is a leguminous plant native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and partial shade.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has gained attention for potential pharmaceutical applications.
