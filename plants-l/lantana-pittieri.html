Lantana pittieri

Lantana pittieri, commonly known as the Hawaiian lantana, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring dense, branched stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom throughout the year. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Lantana pittieri is adapted to thrive in coastal and dry forest environments, showcasing its resilience to the tropical climate of Hawaii.
