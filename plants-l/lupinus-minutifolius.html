Lupinus minutifolius

Lupinus minutifolius, commonly known as the small-leaved lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers. The plant has small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Lupinus minutifolius is adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a hardy species that thrives in well-drained soils.
