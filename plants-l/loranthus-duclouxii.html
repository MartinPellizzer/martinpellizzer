Loranthus duclouxii

Loranthus duclouxii, commonly known as the Ducloux's mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily in the genus Ficus.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using the light it captures. The plant has a distinctive appearance with small, oval-shaped leaves and small, white or yellowish flowers that appear in clusters. Its fruit is a fleshy capsule that contains numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by birds.

This species is native to Madagascar and is an example of the unique ecological relationships found in tropical ecosystems.
