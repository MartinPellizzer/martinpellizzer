Lobelia comosa var. secundata

Lobelia comosa var. secundata, commonly known as the trailing lobelia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in hanging baskets or as a ground cover.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
