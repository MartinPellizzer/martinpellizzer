Lobostemon cinereus

Lobostemon cinereus, commonly known as the ash-colored lobostemon, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green foliage and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are often reddish in color.

Lobostemon cinereus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid climates.
