Landolphia glaberrima

Landolphia glaberrima, commonly known as the African morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the morning. The plant produces large, fleshy berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and have a smooth, glabrous surface.

Landolphia glaberrima is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and riverbanks.
