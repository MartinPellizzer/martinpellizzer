Lyonia compta

Lyonia compta, commonly known as the small lyonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen shrub form. The plant features small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers in the spring, which are followed by small, oval berries.

Lyonia compta thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland habitats.
