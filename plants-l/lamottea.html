Lamottea

Lamottea, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to tropical Africa.

These plants are characterized by their rosette-shaped leaves and showy, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors. Lamottea species typically thrive in warm, humid environments and require well-draining soil to prevent root rot. They are popular houseplants due to their attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements.

The flowers are often used in horticultural displays and are prized for their ornamental value.
