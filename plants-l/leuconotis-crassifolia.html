Leuconotis crassifolia

Leuconotis crassifolia, commonly known as the thick-leaved daisy, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features a rosette of thick, fleshy, hairy leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces showy, daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white petals, blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its unique foliage and attractive flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
