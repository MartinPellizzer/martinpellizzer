Limonium dolihiense

Limonium dolihiense, commonly known as the coastal flax, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dune environments. It has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Limonium dolihiense is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize coastal soils.
