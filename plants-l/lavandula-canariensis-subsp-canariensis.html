Lavandula canariensis subsp. canariensis

Lavandula canariensis subsp.

canariensis, commonly known as the Canary lavender, is a perennial shrub native to the Canary Islands. It features aromatic, spike-like inflorescences composed of purple to pinkish-lavender flowers.

The plant has lance-shaped, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant nature. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild essential oils used in aromatherapy.
