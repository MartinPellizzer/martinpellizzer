Lepidium acutidens var. cornigerum

Lepidium acutidens var. cornigerum, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada cress, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and pinnately divided leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. It produces small, edible seeds that are often used in traditional herbal practices.

Its botanical features include a basal rosette of leaves and a flowering stem that bears clusters of yellow flowers.
