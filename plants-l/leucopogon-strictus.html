Leucopogon strictus

Leucopogon strictus, commonly known as the narrowleaf heath, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in heathlands and dry forests, characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually white or pale pink, arranged in clusters along the stems. It has a fibrous root system and is adapted to nutrient-poor soils.

Leucopogon strictus plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing food and shelter for various native wildlife species.
