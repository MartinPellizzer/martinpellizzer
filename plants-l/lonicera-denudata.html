Lonicera denudata

Lonicera denudata, commonly known as the naked honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically hairless and have a smooth texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to yellow, blooming in early spring. Its fruits are red berries that mature in late summer, providing food for birds and other wildlife.

Lonicera denudata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
