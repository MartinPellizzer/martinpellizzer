Linaria segetalis

Linaria segetalis, commonly known as the field toadflax, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the genus Linaria in the family Plantaginaceae, characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and has a compact, bushy growth habit. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in racemes.

Linaria segetalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
