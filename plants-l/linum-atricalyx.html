Linum atricalyx

Linum atricalyx, commonly known as the dark-leaf flax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, pale blue to purple flowers. The leaves are typically dark green and have a distinctive, narrow shape. This species belongs to the Linaceae family, which includes other flax species.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or rocky areas.
