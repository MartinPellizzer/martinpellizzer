Lysimachia crassifolia

Lysimachia crassifolia, commonly known as broadleaf starwort, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of Asia and Europe.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy and have a broad, rounded shape. Its small, white or yellowish flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in early summer.

Lysimachia crassifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
