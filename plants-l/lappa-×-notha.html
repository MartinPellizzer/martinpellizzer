Lappa × notha

Lappa × notha, commonly known as the common hogweed, is a hybrid plant in the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, umbrella-like inflorescence and tall, hollow stems that can reach up to 3 meters in height. The plant has broad, pinnately compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is native to Europe and parts of Asia.

Due to its toxic sap and phototoxic compounds, it is considered a noxious weed in many regions.
