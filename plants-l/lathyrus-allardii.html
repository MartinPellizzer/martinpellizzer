Lathyrus allardii

Lathyrus allardii, commonly known as the white-flowered grass vetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring.
