Lagoa calcarata

Lagoa calcarata, commonly known as the rock lagoon plant, is a unique succulent native to specific arid regions.

It features a compact, rounded growth habit with thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange, typically during the warmer months. Its root system is shallow and adapted to absorb moisture from the surface layer of soil.

Lagoa calcarata is often found growing on rocky outcrops, where it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.
