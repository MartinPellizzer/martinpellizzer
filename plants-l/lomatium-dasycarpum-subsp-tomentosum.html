Lomatium dasycarpum subsp. tomentosum

Lomatium dasycarpum subsp.

tomentosum, commonly known as the woolly lomatium, is a member of theApiaceae family, characterized by its branched, aromatic foliage. It is native to the Pacific Northwest of North America, particularly in regions of Washington and Oregon.

The plant features a basal rosette of soft, hairy leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white pubescence. Its flowering stems are usually taller and bear small, yellowish flowers arranged in compound umbels.

This subspecies is adapted to moist, shaded environments and is often found in coniferous forests.
