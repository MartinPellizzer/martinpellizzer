Lastrea patens

Lastrea patens, commonly known as the desert honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it well adapted to desert environments. Its flowering period typically occurs in spring and early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging climates.
