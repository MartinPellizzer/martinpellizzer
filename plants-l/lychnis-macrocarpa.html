Lychnis macrocarpa

Lychnis macrocarpa, commonly known as the showy campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its flowers are typically arranged in racemes. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.

Lychnis macrocarpa is a perennial herb that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.
