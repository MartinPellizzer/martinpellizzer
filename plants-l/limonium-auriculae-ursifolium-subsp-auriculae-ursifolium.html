Limonium auriculae-ursifolium subsp. auriculae-ursifolium

Limonium auriculae-ursifolium subsp.

auriculae-ursifolium, commonly known as the alpine sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in alpine meadows.

Its ornamental value has made it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine gardens.
