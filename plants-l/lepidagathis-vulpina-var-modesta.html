Lepidagathis vulpina var. modesta

Lepidagathis vulpina var. modesta, commonly known as the dwarf foxglove, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It features slender, erect stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of tubular, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale purple to deep red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
