Linum platyphyllum subsp. guvensenii

Linum platyphyllum subsp.

guvensenii, commonly known as the Guvensen's flax, is a member of the Linaceae family and is native to specific regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pale blue or white flowers.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oval seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
