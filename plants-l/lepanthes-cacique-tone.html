Lepanthes cacique-tone

Lepanthes cacique-tone, commonly known as the cacique-tone orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its strikingly colorful flowers, often featuring vibrant red, yellow, and green hues. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical forests. Its flowers are small but highly ornate, with intricate patterns that attract specific pollinators.

The species is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
