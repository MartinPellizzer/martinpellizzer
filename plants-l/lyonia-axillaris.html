Lyonia axillaris

Lyonia axillaris, commonly known as the evergreen huckleberry, is a low-growing shrub native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, followed by edible berries that are dark blue to black in color. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats.

Lyonia axillaris is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for wildlife.
