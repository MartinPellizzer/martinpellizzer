Linum brachypetalum

Linum brachypetalum, commonly known as the short-petaled flax, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Linaceae family, which includes other flax species. The plant produces small, delicate blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it typically grows in sandy or rocky soils.

Linum brachypetalum is often found in open woodlands and along riverbanks, where it thrives in full sun.
