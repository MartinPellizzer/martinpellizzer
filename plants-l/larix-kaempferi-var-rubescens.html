Larix kaempferi var. rubescens

Larix kaempferi var. rubescens, commonly known as the red Japanese larch, is a deciduous conifer native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and its striking reddish-brown bark, which becomes more pronounced with age. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain winged seeds, typical of the pine family. Its needles are short, flat, and often turn a vibrant yellow before falling in autumn.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens for its unique color and seasonal changes.
