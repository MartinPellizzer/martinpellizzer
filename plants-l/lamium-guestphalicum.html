Lamium guestphalicum

Lamium guestphalicum, commonly known as the broadleaf dead-nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive purple underside and produces clusters of small, two-lipped flowers in shades of pink to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and hedgerows. It is part of the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic properties and often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Its flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
