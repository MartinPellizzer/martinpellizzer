Lepidagathis laguroidea

Lepidagathis laguroidea, commonly known as the golden star grass, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, slender stems and small, clustered leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense, spherical inflorescences, which give it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Lepidagathis laguroidea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its unique floral display.
