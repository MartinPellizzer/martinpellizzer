Leucas ciliata

Leucas ciliata, commonly known as the blue milkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including India and China.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.

Leucas ciliata is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
