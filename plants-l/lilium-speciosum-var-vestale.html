Lilium speciosum var. vestale

Lilium speciosum var. vestale, commonly known as the White Lily, is a striking perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with a strong, sweet fragrance. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, and it produces a single stem that bears the floral display. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

This variety is prized for its elegant appearance and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
