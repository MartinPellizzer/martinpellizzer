Lagophylla diabolensis

Lagophylla diabolensis, commonly known as the Devil's Thorn, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, fleshy stems and small, clustered leaves that help it retain water in harsh environments. The plant produces bright yellow flowers during the warmer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its unique morphology and adaptations make it well-suited for survival in extreme conditions.

Lagophylla diabolensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience.
