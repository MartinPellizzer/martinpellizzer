Loranthus speciosus

Loranthus speciosus, commonly known as the beauty fruit or Indian mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of host trees, primarily in the genus Ficus.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while it provides nectar to attract insects for pollination. The plant produces small, red or yellow berries that are edible for some bird species, aiding in seed dispersal. Its flowers are often clustered and have a distinctive, fragrant appearance.

Loranthus speciosus is found in tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
