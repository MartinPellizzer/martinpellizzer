Ledonia peduncularis var. salviifolia

Ledonia peduncularis var. salviifolia, commonly known as the Salvia-leaved Ledonia, is a rare and unique plant species characterized by its distinctive foliage.

It features lanceolate leaves that are often a deep green with a silvery underside, giving it a striking visual appeal. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of purple and blue, which bloom in late spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a conservation concern in its native habitat.
