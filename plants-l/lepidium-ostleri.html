Lepidium ostleri

Lepidium ostleri, commonly known as the "Peruvian rocket," is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a small annual herb that grows up to 30 centimeters in height, characterized by its branched stems and small, alternate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, and it is known for its peppery flavor, similar to that of mustard. Lepidium ostleri is often cultivated as a leafy green vegetable and is valued for its nutritional content and culinary use.

It thrives in cool, temperate climates and is adapted to high-altitude environments.
