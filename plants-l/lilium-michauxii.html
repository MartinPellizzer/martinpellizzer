Lilium michauxii

Lilium michauxii, commonly known as the Michaux's lily, is a rare and elegant flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking white to pale pink flowers with a distinctive red or purple blotch at the base of each petal. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and prefers partial to full shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a fan-like pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Lilium michauxii is valued for its botanical significance and is often cultivated in specialty gardens.
