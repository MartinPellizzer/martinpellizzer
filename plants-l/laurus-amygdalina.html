Laurus amygdalina

Laurus amygdalina, commonly known as the sweet bay tree, is a flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white flowers that develop into dark, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are widely used in cooking for their distinctive, slightly sweet and peppery flavor.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
