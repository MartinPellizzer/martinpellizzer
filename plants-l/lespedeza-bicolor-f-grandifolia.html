Lespedeza bicolor f. grandifolia

Lespedeza bicolor f. grandifolia, commonly known as the large-flowered lespedeza, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for ecological restoration projects.
