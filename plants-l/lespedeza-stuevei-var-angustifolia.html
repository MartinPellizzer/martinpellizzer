Lespedeza stuevei var. angustifolia

Lespedeza stuevei var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf lespedeza, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are alternate, simple, and narrow, with a serrated margin. This plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in open woodlands and disturbed soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
