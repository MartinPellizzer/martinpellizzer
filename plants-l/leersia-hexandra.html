Leersia hexandra

Leersia hexandra, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in Asia.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in dense clusters during the summer months. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands in moist environments.

Leersia hexandra plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
