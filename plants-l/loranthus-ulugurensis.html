Loranthus ulugurensis

Loranthus ulugurensis, commonly known as the Uluguru mistletoe, is a parasitic plant belonging to the Loranthaceae family.

It grows on the branches of host trees, primarily in the Uluguru Mountains of Tanzania. The plant has small, opposite leaves and produces white or yellowish flowers that attract insects for pollination. Its berries are typically red or orange, containing seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This species plays a role in the local ecosystem by providing food for wildlife and contributing to the biodiversity of its habitat.
