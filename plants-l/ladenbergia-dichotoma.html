Ladenbergia dichotoma

Ladenbergia dichotoma, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bright red flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has compound leaves that are opposite and pinnate, with small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its bark is smooth and greyish, often marked with vertical fissures.

Ladenbergia dichotoma is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in tropical and subtropical regions.
