Lophiolepis richteriana subsp. giraudiasii

Lophiolepis richteriana subsp.

giraudiasii, commonly known as the Cape sugarbush, is a shrub native to the Western Cape region of South Africa. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color.

The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers during the winter months, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates with dry summers and wet winters.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in local habitats.
