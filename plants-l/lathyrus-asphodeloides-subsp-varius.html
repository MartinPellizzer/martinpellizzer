Lathyrus asphodeloides subsp. varius

Lathyrus asphodeloides subsp.

varius, commonly known as the variable vetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant features erect stems with alternate, pinnately compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are borne in racemes and are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive spurred petal.

The subspecies varius is distinguished by its variable leaf morphology and floral characteristics compared to other subspecies of Lathyrus asphodeloides.
