Lichtensteinia elegans

Lichtensteinia elegans, commonly known as the elegant lichen, is a species of lichen that belongs to the family Lichens.

It is characterized by its intricate symbiotic relationship between a fungus and a photosynthetic partner, typically a green alga or cyanobacterium. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found on tree trunks or rocks in forested areas. Its structure consists of a thallus, which is the composite body of the lichen, and it lacks the typical root, stem, and leaf structures of vascular plants.

Lichtensteinia elegans plays an important ecological role in nutrient cycling and serves as an indicator species for environmental conditions.
