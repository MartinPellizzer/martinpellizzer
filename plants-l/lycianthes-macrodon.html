Lycianthes macrodon

Lycianthes macrodon, commonly known as the large-fruited physalis, is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades.

It is a herbaceous annual plant native to Central and South America, characterized by its hairy stems and lobed leaves. The plant produces clusters of inflated, balloon-like calyces that enclose its small, orange-red berries. These berries are edible when ripe and are often used in culinary applications.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical regions.
