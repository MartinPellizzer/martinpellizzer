Leibnitzia

Leibnitzia, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to tropical regions of Africa.

These plants are characterized by their rosette-shaped leaves and showy, often colorful flowers that bloom in spring and summer. Leibnitzia species typically have smooth, waxy foliage that helps them retain moisture in their native habitats. They are popular as houseplants due to their ability to thrive in indirect light and their ornamental value.

The genus includes several species, such as Leibnitzia africana and Leibnitzia flava, which are prized for their vibrant blooms and ease of care.
