Lachnagrostis

Lachnagrostis, commonly known as silvergrass, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family, Poaceae.

These plants are characterized by their fine, silver-gray foliage, which gives them their common name. They typically grow in wet or marshy environments, often found in temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. Lachnagrostis species are perennial herbs that produce small, spike-like inflorescences.

Their botanical features make them valuable in ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
