Lobivia maximiliana subsp. caespitosa

Lobivia maximiliana subsp.

caespitosa, commonly known as the clustered lobivia, is a compact, low-growing cactus native to South America. It features dense clusters of cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green to bluish-green in color.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its foliage. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or arid landscapes.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
