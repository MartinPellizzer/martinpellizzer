Loranthus grahamii

Loranthus grahamii, commonly known as the Graham's mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily oaks and eucalyptus.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using light reflected from the host's leaves. The plant has small, scale-like leaves and produces white or yellowish flowers that attract insects for pollination. Its berries are typically red or yellow and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This species plays a unique role in ecosystem dynamics by influencing host tree growth and providing food for wildlife.
