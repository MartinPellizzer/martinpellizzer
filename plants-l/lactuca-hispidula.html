Lactuca hispidula

Lactuca hispidula, commonly known as hairy lettuce, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as wet meadows and streambanks. Its leaves are hairy and somewhat succulent, with a mild, slightly bitter taste.

It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.
