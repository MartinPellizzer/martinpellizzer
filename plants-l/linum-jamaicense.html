Linum jamaicense

Linum jamaicense, commonly known as Jamaican flax, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean islands, particularly Jamaica.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and delicate, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its seeds are small and encased in a fibrous husk.

Jamaican flax is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
