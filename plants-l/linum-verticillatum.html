Linum verticillatum

Linum verticillatum, commonly known as blue flax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves arranged in whorls around the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Linum verticillatum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in dye production.
