Lactuca hirsuta var. genuina

Lactuca hirsuta var. genuina, commonly known as the hairy lettuce or wild lettuce, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which are typically lobed and serrated. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

This plant is known for its milky sap, which contains lactucin and other compounds.
