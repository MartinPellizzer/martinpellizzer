Loranthus buchwaldii

It belongs to the family Loranthaceae and is native to Australia, where it thrives in subtropical and temperate regions. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers and red berries that contain seeds dispersed by birds. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

Loranthus buchwaldii plays a role in ecological systems by providing food for various bird species and contributing to the biodiversity of its habitat.
