Liparis nesophila

Liparis nesophila, commonly known as the alpine orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is native to high-altitude regions of the Himalayas and is adapted to cold, moist environments. The plant produces delicate, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive lip that is often darker in color. It thrives in rocky or mountainous terrain where it can receive adequate sunlight and drainage.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
