Lotononis carnosa

Lotononis carnosa, commonly known as the woolly thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, hairy stems and leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
