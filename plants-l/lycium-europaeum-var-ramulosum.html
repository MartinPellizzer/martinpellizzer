Lycium europaeum var. ramulosum

Lycium europaeum var. ramulosum, commonly known as the European wolfberry or redberry, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its small, red, fleshy berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. The plant has dense, spiny branches and small, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to survive in harsh climates with minimal water availability.
