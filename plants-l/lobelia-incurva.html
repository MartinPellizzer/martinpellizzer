Lobelia incurva

Lobelia incurva, commonly known as the bent lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features whorled leaves and produces spikes of small, tubular blue or purple flowers. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by hummingbirds and bees, making it an important species for pollinators.

Lobelia incurva is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
