Lepanthes laxiflora

Lepanthes laxiflora, commonly known as the slender-lobed orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as forest understories, where it thrives in the dappled light. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a basal rosette, while its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences.

Lepanthes laxiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
