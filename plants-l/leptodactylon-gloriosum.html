Leptodactylon gloriosum

Leptodactylon gloriosum, commonly known as the golden star fern, is a striking epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features long, arching fronds that are typically bright green with a golden or yellowish hue, giving it its common name. The plant has a compact, rosette-like growth habit, with small, clustered leaves that form a dense, ornamental appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires high levels of moisture and indirect light.

Leptodactylon gloriosum is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its unique and eye-catching foliage.
