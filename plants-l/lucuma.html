Lucuma

Lucuma, commonly known as the "golden apple," is a tropical fruit native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the genus *Pouteria* in the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its yellow, oval-shaped fruit with a sweet, creamy flesh. The fruit typically ripens during the summer months and is often harvested by hand from trees that can grow up to 15 meters tall. Lucuma is rich in nutrients, including vitamins A and C, and is valued for its unique flavor and culinary versatility.

Its botanical features make it an important crop in both traditional and modern agricultural systems.
