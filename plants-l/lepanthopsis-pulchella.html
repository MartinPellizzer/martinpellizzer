Lepanthopsis pulchella

Lepanthopsis pulchella, commonly known as the elegant orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees or other plants without deriving nutrients from them. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Lepanthopsis pulchella is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
