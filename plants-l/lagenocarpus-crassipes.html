Lagenocarpus crassipes

Lagenocarpus crassipes, commonly known as the broad-leaved water fern, is a species of aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, floating leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread across water surfaces. Its fronds are usually divided into segments, and it produces spores rather than seeds for reproduction.

Lagenocarpus crassipes plays a role in stabilizing water ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
