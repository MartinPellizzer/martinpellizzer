Leucanthemum pallens subsp. leucolepis

Leucanthemum pallens subsp.

leucolepis, commonly known as the white leucanthemum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features basal rosettes of dark green, velvety leaves and produces showy white to pale yellow flowers with prominent central disc florets.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
