Lavandula brevidens var. moulouyana

Lavandula brevidens var. moulouyana, commonly known as the narrowleaf lavender, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fragrant, purple-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that are densely packed, contributing to its drought-tolerant nature. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its aromatic properties make it a popular choice for both ornamental and essential oil purposes.
