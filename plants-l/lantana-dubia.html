Lantana dubia

Lantana dubia, commonly known as the pale lantana, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to yellow, often blooming in summer. Lantana dubia is known for its ability to thrive in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a hardy species in tropical and subtropical climates.

It is also noted for its use in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant.
