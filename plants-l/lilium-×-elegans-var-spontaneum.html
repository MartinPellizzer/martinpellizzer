Lilium × elegans var. spontaneum

Lilium × elegans var. spontaneum, commonly known as the wild lily, is a naturally occurring variant of the Asian lily.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink. The plant is native to regions of Asia, including China and Korea, and thrives in temperate climates. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a hardy perennial in suitable environments.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental gardens and ecological restoration projects.
