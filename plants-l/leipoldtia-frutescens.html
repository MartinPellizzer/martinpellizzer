Leipoldtia frutescens

Leipoldtia frutescens, commonly known as the Cape rose, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has simple, elliptical leaves and produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens in suitable climates.
