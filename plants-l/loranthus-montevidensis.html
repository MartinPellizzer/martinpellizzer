Loranthus montevidensis

Loranthus montevidensis, commonly known as the Montevidense mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of various host trees.

It belongs to the family Loranthaceae and is native to South America, particularly in regions of Uruguay and southern Brazil. The plant has woody stems and produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are often clustered together. Its leaves are typically scale-like, and it relies on its host for water and nutrients while performing photosynthesis.

This species plays an ecological role in its habitat by providing food for certain birds and insects.
