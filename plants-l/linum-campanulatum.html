Linum campanulatum

Linum campanulatum, commonly known as the bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Linum campanulatum is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of care.
