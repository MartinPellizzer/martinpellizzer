Leptoclinium fruticosum

Leptoclinium fruticosum, commonly known as the creeping gentian, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, spreading stems that root at the nodes, forming dense mats. Its flowers are blue to purple in color, with five petals and prominent yellow stamens.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
