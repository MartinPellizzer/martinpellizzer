Lasianthus leucocarpus

Lasianthus leucocarpus, commonly known as the white-fruited lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its fruits are distinctive, being white and fleshy, which gives it its common name.

Lasianthus leucocarpus is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.
