Lobelia melleri

Lobelia melleri, commonly known as the Meller's lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems and produces clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Lobelia melleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
