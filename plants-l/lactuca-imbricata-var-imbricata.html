Lactuca imbricata var. imbricata

Lactuca imbricata var. imbricata, commonly known as the common lettuce, is a species of flowering plant in the daisy family.

It is characterized by its rosette of spoon-shaped, dark green leaves that are often curly or frilly in appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped cluster, which bloom in the summer months. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been naturalized in various regions around the world.

This plant is typically found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
