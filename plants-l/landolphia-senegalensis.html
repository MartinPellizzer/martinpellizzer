Landolphia senegalensis

Landolphia senegalensis, commonly known as the African morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range from yellow to orange. The plant produces berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when ripe. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses in warm climates.
