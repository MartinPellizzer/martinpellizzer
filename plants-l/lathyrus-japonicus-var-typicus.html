Lathyrus japonicus var. typicus

Lathyrus japonicus var. typicus, commonly known as Japanese vetch, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a perennial legume that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces clusters of pea-like flowers in shades of pink or purple. Its pods contain small, edible seeds that are sometimes used in traditional cuisine.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility.
