Lobelia trinitensis

Lobelia trinitensis, commonly known as the Trinidad morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean islands, particularly Trinidad and Tobago.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in the early morning, hence its common name. The plant belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is known for its trumpet-shaped petals and vibrant coloration. Lobelia trinitensis typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
