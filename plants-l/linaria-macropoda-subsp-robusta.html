Linaria macropoda subsp. robusta

Linaria macropoda subsp.

robusta, commonly known as the robust wallflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical name reflects its close relation to the genus Linaria, which includes various species of flowering plants known for their showy blooms.
