Laplacea quinoderma

Laplacea quinoderma, commonly known as the six-lobed bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale blue to violet. The plant has lanceolate leaves with a hairy surface, and its stems are typically hairy as well. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Laplacea quinoderma is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
