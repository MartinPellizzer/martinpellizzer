Lepidium medium var. pubescens

Lepidium medium var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy cress, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, white or pink flowers and hairy stems and leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is found in various regions across North America. It has a compact growth habit and produces small, edible seeds.

Lepidium medium var. pubescens is often used in herbal medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
