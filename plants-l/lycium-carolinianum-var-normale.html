Lycium carolinianum var. normale

Lycium carolinianum var. normale, commonly known as the Carolina wolfberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It produces small, red berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

The berries are rich in antioxidants and have been historically valued for their nutritional and medicinal properties.
