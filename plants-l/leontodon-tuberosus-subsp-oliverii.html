Leontodon tuberosus subsp. oliverii

Leontodon tuberosus subsp.

oliverii, commonly known as the Oliver's bulbous hawk's-beard, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its subspecies status highlights its distinct morphological features compared to the main species, including differences in leaf shape and flower structure.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
