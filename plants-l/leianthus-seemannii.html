Leianthus seemannii

Leianthus seemannii, commonly known as the African bluebell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slight hairy texture.

Leianthus seemannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
