Linharia tinctoria

Linharia tinctoria, commonly known as the redwood plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the coastal regions of southern Brazil and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in sandy, well-drained soils and its tolerance to salt spray, making it suitable for coastal environments. Linharia tinctoria has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its ornamental value also makes it a popular choice for horticultural purposes.
