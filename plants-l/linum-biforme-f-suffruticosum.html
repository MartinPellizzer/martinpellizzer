Linum biforme f. suffruticosum

Linum biforme f. suffruticosum, commonly known as the shrub flax, is a compact, woody perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. Its stems are erect and somewhat branched, contributing to its shrub-like form.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
