Lophochloa obtusiflora

Lophochloa obtusiflora, commonly known as the broad-flowered lophochloa, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brownish flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Lophochloa obtusiflora is often found in wetlands and along stream banks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
