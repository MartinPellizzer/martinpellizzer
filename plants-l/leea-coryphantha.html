Leea coryphantha

Leea coryphantha, commonly known as the yellow leea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Leeaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in racemes, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
