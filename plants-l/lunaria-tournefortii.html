Lunaria tournefortii

Lunaria tournefortii, commonly known as the moonflower of the desert, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers. The plant produces distinctive spherical seed pods that split open to release seeds, a feature that aids in its dispersal. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its blooming period typically occurs in the evening, adding a unique element to its ecological role.
