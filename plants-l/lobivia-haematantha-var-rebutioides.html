Lobivia haematantha var. rebutioides

Lobivia haematantha var. rebutioides, commonly known as the Rebuttioides Lobivia, is a compact, low-growing succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It features a dense rosette of thick, fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. The plant produces tall, branched stems that bear clusters of yellow flowers during the warmer months. It is well adapted to dry conditions, storing water in its leaves and stems.

Due to its slow growth and ornamental appeal, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
