Leucas elliotii

Leucas elliotii, commonly known as Elliot's leucas, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and clusters of small, pale blue or purple flowers. It is often found in grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils.

Leucas elliotii is used in traditional medicine for its purported antispasmodic and analgesic properties.
