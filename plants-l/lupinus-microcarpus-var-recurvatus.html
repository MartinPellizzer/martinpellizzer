Lupinus microcarpus var. recurvatus

Lupinus microcarpus var. recurvatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
