Lavatera olbia var. hispida

Lavatera olbia var. hispida, commonly known as the rough lavender tree, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It features soft, silvery-gray leaves that are covered in fine, hairy textures, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

The variety 'hispida' is characterized by its rough, hairy foliage, which distinguishes it from other varieties of Lavatera.
