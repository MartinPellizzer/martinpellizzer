Lippia imbricata

Lippia imbricata, commonly known as Mexican blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
