Lepanthes triangularis

Lepanthes triangularis, commonly known as the triangular orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. The plant produces aerial roots and thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on trees or rocks. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern.

Lepanthes triangularis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and unique floral structure.
