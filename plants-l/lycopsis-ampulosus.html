Lycopsis ampulosus

Lycopsis ampulosus, commonly known as the giant lycopsis, is a rare and unique plant species belonging to the family Lycopsida.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and dense clusters of small, scale-like leaves. This plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its reproductive structures are simple, consisting of sporangia that release spores for propagation.
