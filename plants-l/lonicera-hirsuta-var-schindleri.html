Lonicera hirsuta var. schindleri

Lonicera hirsuta var. schindleri, commonly known as the Schindler's honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features slender, arching stems that are often covered in short, glandular hairs. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture with serrated edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
