Lycopodioides subcaulescens

Lycopodioides subcaulescens, commonly known as the slender clubmoss, is a species of lycophyte characterized by its small, needle-like leaves and compact, club-shaped sporophytes.

It is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant exhibits a unique reproductive structure, with sporangia clustered in strobila at the tips of its branches. Its vascular system is relatively simple, consisting of a central axis with scattered vascular bundles.

Lycopodioides subcaulescens plays a minor role in ecological succession but is valued for its historical significance in paleobotanical studies.
