Lathyrus sativus var. pulchrus

Lathyrus sativus var. pulchrus, commonly known as the beautiful vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a legume characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces pea-like flowers in shades of pink, purple, or white, which bloom in early summer. Its seeds are encased in a hard pod and are often used as a food source for livestock.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and nitrogen-fixing capabilities in soil.
