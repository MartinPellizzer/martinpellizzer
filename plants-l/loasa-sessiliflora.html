Loasa sessiliflora

Loasa sessiliflora, commonly known as the sessile-flower loasa, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or mountainous terrain. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink in color. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy surface.

Loasa sessiliflora is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
