Limnanthes montana

Limnanthes montana, commonly known as the California poppy, is a flowering plant native to western North America.

It is characterized by its showy, orange to red-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is an annual herb with narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open areas.

Limnanthes montana is also known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
