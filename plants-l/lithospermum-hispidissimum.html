Lithospermum hispidissimum

Lithospermum hispidissimum, commonly known as hairy lithosperm, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, which help reduce water loss. Its flowers are usually violet to purple, with tubular corollas that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Lithospermum hispidissimum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
