Limniris pinetorum

Limniris pinetorum, commonly known as the silverleaf pine, is a deciduous shrub native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silver-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early spring, followed by clusters of red berries that attract various bird species. Limniris pinetorum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
