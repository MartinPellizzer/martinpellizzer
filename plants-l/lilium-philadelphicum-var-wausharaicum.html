Lilium philadelphicum var. wausharaicum

Lilium philadelphicum var. wausharaicum, commonly known as the Waushara lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the United States, particularly found in the Great Lakes region.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with lance-shaped leaves and a robust root system. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and woodland edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
