Lasianthus tubifer

Lasianthus tubifer, commonly known as the water lily of the genus Lasianthus, is a member of the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its floating leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom above the water surface. The plant typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats, such as ponds and lakes. Its leaves are broad and rounded, often with a waxy coating to repel water.

The flowers are hermaphroditic, meaning they contain both male and female reproductive structures, facilitating self-pollination.
