Lonicera splendida

Lonicera splendida, commonly known as the golden honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and have a smooth texture. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that range from yellow to orange, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its red, fleshy berries are an important food source for birds and small mammals.

Lonicera splendida is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
