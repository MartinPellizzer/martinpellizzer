Lobelia salicina subsp. brachyantha

Lobelia salicina subsp.

brachyantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lobelia, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States. It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Campanulaceae family.
