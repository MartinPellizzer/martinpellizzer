Lobelia brassiana

Lobelia brassiana, commonly known as the golden lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and prefers partial to full shade. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Lobelia brassiana is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
