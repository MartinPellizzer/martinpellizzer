Laelia dayana

Laelia dayana, commonly known as the Mexican orchid, is a species native to the highlands of Mexico.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy flowers. The plant typically produces intricate, fragrant blooms in shades of white, yellow, and green, often with a distinctive pattern resembling a sunflower. Laelia dayana thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

It is a popular choice for both indoor cultivation and botanical gardens due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
