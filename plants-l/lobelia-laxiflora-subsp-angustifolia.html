Lobelia laxiflora subsp. angustifolia

Lobelia laxiflora subsp.

angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, tubular flowers are usually blue or purple and bloom in late spring to early summer.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
