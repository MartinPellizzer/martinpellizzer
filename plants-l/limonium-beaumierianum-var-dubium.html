Limonium beaumierianum var. dubium

Limonium beaumierianum var. dubium, commonly known as the coastal flax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, blue to violet flowers. The plant thrives in coastal areas and sandy soils, often found along shorelines and dunes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is known for its ability to tolerate salt spray and harsh coastal conditions.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in stabilizing coastal environments.
