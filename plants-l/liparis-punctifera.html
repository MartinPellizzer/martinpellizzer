Liparis punctifera

Liparis punctifera, commonly known as the punctate orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, greenish-yellow flowers with distinct purple spots. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form colonies in suitable habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
