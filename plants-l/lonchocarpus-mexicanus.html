Lonchocarpus mexicanus

Lonchocarpus mexicanus, commonly known as Mexican sensitiveplant, is a member of the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves.

It produces striking yellow flowers that are followed by distinctive, inflated seed pods. The plant is native to tropical regions of Mexico and is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats. It contains toxic alkaloids, which can cause skin irritation and are used in traditional medicine.

Its rapid growth and nitrogen-fixing ability make it an important component of certain ecosystems.
