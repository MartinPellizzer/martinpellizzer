Lebetanthus myrsinites

Lebetanthus myrsinites, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in woodworking, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
