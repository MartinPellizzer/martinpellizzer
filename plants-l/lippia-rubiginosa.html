Lippia rubiginosa

Lippia rubiginosa, commonly known as Mexican tea or red lippia, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring aromatic, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, often arranged in clusters at the tips of the branches. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The leaves and flowers are used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb, known for their aromatic and slightly spicy flavor.
