Leucas aequistylosa

Leucas aequistylosa, commonly known as blue leucas, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and showy blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
