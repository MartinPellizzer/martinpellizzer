Lamium demirizii

Lamium demirizii, commonly known as the Turkish deadnettle, is a perennial herb native to Turkey.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, hairy seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Lamium demirizii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
