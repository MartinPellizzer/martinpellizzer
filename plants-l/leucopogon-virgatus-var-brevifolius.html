Leucopogon virgatus var. brevifolius

Leucopogon virgatus var. brevifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy or well-drained soils, often in coastal dunes or open woodlands. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
