Lucuma caimito

Lucuma caimito, commonly known as the golden apple or lucuma, is a fruit-bearing plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its unique, elongated fruit that resembles a small apple. The fruit has a sweet, nutty flavor and is often used in both culinary and traditional medicinal applications. Its botanical features make it a valuable crop for both local and international markets.
