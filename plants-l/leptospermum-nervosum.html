Leptospermum nervosum

Leptospermum nervosum, commonly known as the narrow-veined tea tree, is a small evergreen shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its woody structure and aromatic foliage make it a hardy and ornamental plant suitable for coastal and sandy soils.

Leptospermum nervosum is also valued for its potential medicinal properties and use in traditional herbal remedies.
