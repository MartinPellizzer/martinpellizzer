Lippia myriocephala var. ovatifolia

Lippia myriocephala var. ovatifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf lippia, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It features aromatic, lanceolate leaves that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. The plant produces small, clustered white or pink flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species in both horticulture and ethnobotanical studies.
