Lophopetalum dubium

Lophopetalum dubium, commonly known as the desert holly, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. The plant produces small, white, tubular flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are thick and waxy, reducing water loss through transpiration.

The plant's resilience to heat and low rainfall makes it an important species in xeriscaping and ecological restoration projects.
