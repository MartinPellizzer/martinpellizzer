Lythrum alatum var. ovalifolium

Lythrum alatum var. ovalifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf purple coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, branching stems that are often tinged with red or purple, adding visual interest to gardens. The plant produces showy, spiky inflorescences composed of numerous small, tubular pink to purple flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, with a distinct central vein.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is well-suited for moist, well-drained soils.
