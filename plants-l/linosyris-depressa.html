Linosyris depressa

Linosyris depressa, commonly known as the woolly sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in dry environments. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Linosyris depressa plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates in its native range.
