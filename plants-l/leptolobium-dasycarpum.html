Leptolobium dasycarpum

Leptolobium dasycarpum, commonly known as the rough fruit tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, woody pods. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers followed by rough, hairy pods that contain several seeds.

It is adapted to dry and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species often found in rocky or coastal areas.
