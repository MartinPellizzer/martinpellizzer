Loudetia bequaertii

Loudetia bequaertii, commonly known as the African grass or red grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and distinctive reddish-purple inflorescence. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Loudetia bequaertii is often used in ornamental gardening for its striking visual appeal and drought tolerance.
