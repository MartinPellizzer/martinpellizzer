Lithops pseudotruncatella var. elisabethiae

Lithops pseudotruncatella var. elisabethiae, commonly known as the "Living Stone," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, leaf-like structures that resemble stones, helping it blend into its arid environment. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking contrast to its rocky appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, requiring minimal water to survive.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and succulent collections.
