Lithospermum arizonicum

Lithospermum arizonicum, commonly known as Arizona rock pepper, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are a source of food for various wildlife species. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often woody at the base.

Lithospermum arizonicum is known for its medicinal properties, with compounds used in traditional herbal remedies.
