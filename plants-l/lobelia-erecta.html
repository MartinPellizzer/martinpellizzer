Lobelia erecta

Lobelia erecta, commonly known as the upright lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slight hairy texture.

Lobelia erecta is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks, where it contributes to the biodiversity of its habitat.
