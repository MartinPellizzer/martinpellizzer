Lantana concinna var. macrophylla

Lantana concinna var. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved lantana, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of small, brightly colored flowers, typically in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The leaves are broad, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal. This variety is notable for its ability to thrive in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its botanical features make it both aesthetically pleasing and ecologically valuable, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
