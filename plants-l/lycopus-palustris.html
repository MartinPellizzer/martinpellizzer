Lycopus palustris

Lycopus palustris, commonly known as marsh woundwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces small, blue to purple flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its flowering stems are often branched and can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters.

Lycopus palustris is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
