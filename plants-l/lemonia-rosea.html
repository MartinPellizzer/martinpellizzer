Lemonia rosea

Lemonia rosea, commonly known as the rose-lemon tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are oval-shaped with a slightly hairy surface. Its fruit, which resembles a small lemon, is edible and has a tart, citrusy flavor.

Lemonia rosea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for its use in traditional medicine.
