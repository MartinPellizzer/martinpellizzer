Laburnum triflorum

Laburnum triflorum, commonly known as the yellow chain tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and clusters of bright yellow, pendulous flowers that appear in early spring. The plant has compound leaves composed of 10-20 oval-shaped leaflets, and its bark is smooth and light grey. Laburnum triflorum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
