Lavoisiera bradeana

Lavoisiera bradeana, commonly known as the Brazilian water lily, is a tropical aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves and showy white or pink flowers that bloom above the water surface. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds and marshes. Its roots anchor it to the substrate, while its leaves provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Lavoisiera bradeana plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by supporting biodiversity and contributing to water quality.
