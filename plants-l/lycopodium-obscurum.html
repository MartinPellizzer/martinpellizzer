Lycopodium obscurum

Lycopodium obscurum, commonly known as the dark clubmoss, is a small evergreen shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae and is characterized by its dense clusters of spore-bearing cones. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are scale-like and arranged in whorls around the stem, giving it a compact appearance.

Lycopodium obscurum plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various small organisms.
