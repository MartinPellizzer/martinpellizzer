Luzula spadicea f. carpatica

Luzula spadicea f. carpatica, commonly known as the Carpathian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically glaucous in appearance. The inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of pale green to brownish flowers, which are usually clustered in three rows. It prefers moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and is often found in temperate regions of Europe.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and ecological role in maintaining soil stability in wetland habitats.
