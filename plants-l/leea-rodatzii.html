Leea rodatzii

Leea rodatzii, commonly known as the African moonvine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Leeaceae and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are typically clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruit is a fleshy, berry-like structure that turns red when mature.

Leea rodatzii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some African countries.
