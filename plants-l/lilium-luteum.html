Lilium luteum

Lilium luteum, commonly known as the golden lion's mouth, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It produces showy, orange-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, and its stems are sturdy and upright. Lilium luteum is characterized by its large, trumpet-shaped blooms that can reach up to 10 centimeters in diameter.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a striking addition to rock gardens and alpine settings.
