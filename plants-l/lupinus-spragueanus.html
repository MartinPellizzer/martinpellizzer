Lupinus spragueanus

Lupinus spragueanus, commonly known as Sprague's lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves composed of oval to lance-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

Lupinus spragueanus plays an important role in ecological systems by fixing nitrogen through its root nodules.
