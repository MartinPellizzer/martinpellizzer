Lithotoma anethifolia

Lithotoma anethifolia, commonly known as the anise-leaved lithotoma, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually pale blue or violet in color. Its leaves are finely divided and resemble those of anise, giving it its common name.

Lithotoma anethifolia is adapted to survive in harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
