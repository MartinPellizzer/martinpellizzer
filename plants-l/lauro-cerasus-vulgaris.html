Lauro-cerasus vulgaris

Lauro-cerasus vulgaris, commonly known as the cherry laurel, is a species of flowering plant in the family Prunus.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible but not typically consumed by humans. Native to Europe and western Asia, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
