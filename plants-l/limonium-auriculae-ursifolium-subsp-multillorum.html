Limonium auriculae-ursifolium subsp. multillorum

Limonium auriculae-ursifolium subsp.

multillorum, commonly known as the purple sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean. It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, often appearing in clusters at the ends of its stems.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are adapted to withstand saline and windy conditions. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dunes or along the coast.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
