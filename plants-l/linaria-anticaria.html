Linaria anticaria

Linaria anticaria, commonly known as the common toadflax, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky, or sandy soils. The plant features slender stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are linear and often covered with fine hairs, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Linaria anticaria is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in gardens for its vibrant color and drought tolerance.
