Lathyrus pannonicus subsp. hispanicus

Lathyrus pannonicus subsp.

hispanicus, commonly known as the Spanish vetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in grasslands and disturbed soils.

The plant produces clusters of pea-like flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and it has a climbing or trailing growth habit.

The seeds are contained in pods and are adapted for dispersal by wind or animals.
