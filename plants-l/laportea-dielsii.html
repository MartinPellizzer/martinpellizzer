Laportea dielsii

Laportea dielsii, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a perennial herb native to Central America and parts of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, purple to pinkish flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It produces small, tubular flowers arranged in composite inflorescences, which attract various pollinators.

Laportea dielsii is often found in dry to moist woodlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
