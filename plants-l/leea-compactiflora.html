Leea compactiflora

Leea compactiflora, commonly known as the compact leaf leea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the family Leeaceae and is characterized by its small, clustered leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to several meters. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with five petals, and it produces small, fleshy fruits.

Leea compactiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to humid, warm climates.
