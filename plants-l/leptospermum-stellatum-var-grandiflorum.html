Leptospermum stellatum var. grandiflorum

Leptospermum stellatum var. grandiflorum, commonly known as the large-flowered tea tree, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage and showy, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, with a distinctive tea-like scent when crushed.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its hardiness and visual interest.
