Lindelofia pterocarpa

Lindelofia pterocarpa, commonly known as the African butter tree, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, deciduous tree or shrub that typically grows in coastal regions. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in racemes. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains edible seeds, which are used in traditional cuisine.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly hairy surface, contributing to its distinctive botanical appearance.
