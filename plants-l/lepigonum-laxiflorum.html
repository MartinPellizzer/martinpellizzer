Lepigonum laxiflorum

Lepigonum laxiflorum, commonly known as the slender-flowered lepigonum, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae, which includes plants commonly known as buckwheats. The plant features slender, erect stems with simple, alternate leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. Its small, showy flowers are typically pink or red and bloom in late summer.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.
