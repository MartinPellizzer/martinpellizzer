Lophidium

Lophidium, commonly known as the common lophidium, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its composite flower heads, which consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and is often found in meadows and along roadsides. Lophidium species are known for their aromatic leaves and showy yellow or orange flowers.

These plants are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
