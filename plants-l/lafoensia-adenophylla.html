Lafoensia adenophylla

Lafoensia adenophylla, commonly known as the white-leafed lafoensia, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
