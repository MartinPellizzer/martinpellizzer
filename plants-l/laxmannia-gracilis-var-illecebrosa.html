Laxmannia gracilis var. illecebrosa

Laxmannia gracilis var. illecebrosa, commonly known as the sweet laxmannia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems with small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
