Liquiritia

Liquiritia, commonly known as licorice, is a perennial herb native to Asia, particularly China, Russia, and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into pods containing several seeds. Its roots are the primary part used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent.

Liquiritia contains compounds like glycyrrhizin, which contribute to its medicinal properties and distinct sweet taste.
