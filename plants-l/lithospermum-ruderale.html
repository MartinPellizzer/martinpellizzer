Lithospermum ruderale

Lithospermum ruderale, commonly known as red sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and open habitats. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its roots contain compounds that have been used traditionally for their medicinal properties.

The plant's ability to thrive in harsh environments makes it an important species in ecological studies.
