Ligustrina amurensis var. japonica

Ligustrina amurensis var. japonica, commonly known as Japanese privet, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter in color underneath. The plant produces clusters of white, fragrant flowers in the spring, which are followed by small, black berries in the fall. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions if not properly managed.
