Leiphaimos aurantiaca

Leiphaimos aurantiaca, commonly known as the golden flame orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange and yellow flowers, which bloom in clusters during the warmer months. The plant features long, strap-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, aiding in water retention. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in moist, well-drained soil.

This orchid is prized for its striking appearance and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
