Lembotropis nigricans subsp. sericeus

Lembotropis nigricans subsp.

sericeus, commonly known as the silky lembotropis, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance.

The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers, which are typically fragrant and attract various pollinators. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique foliage and aesthetic appeal.
