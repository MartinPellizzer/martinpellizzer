Lasianthus tashiroi

Lasianthus tashiroi, commonly known as the Tashiro's flameflower, is a species of flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows in tropical rainforests, thriving in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

Lasianthus tashiroi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
