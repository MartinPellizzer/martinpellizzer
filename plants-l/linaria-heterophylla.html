Linaria heterophylla

Linaria heterophylla, commonly known as the common toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It has a sprawling growth habit with branching stems and narrow, linear leaves.

Linaria heterophylla is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is also considered an invasive species in some regions.
