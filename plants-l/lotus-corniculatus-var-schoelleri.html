Lotus corniculatus var. schoelleri

Lotus corniculatus var. schoelleri, commonly known as the Schoeller's bird's-foot trefoil, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that features bright yellow flowers clustered in small, branched inflorescences. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its stems are often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
