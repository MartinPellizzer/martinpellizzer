Lasiorrhiza rosea var. andryaloides

Lasiorrhiza rosea var. andryaloides, commonly known as the pink sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in well-drained sandy or rocky soils. It is often found in arid or semi-arid environments, such as deserts and coastal dunes.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
