Lamium maculatum var. macrophyllum

Lamium maculatum var. macrophyllum, commonly known as purple shamrock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often speckled with purple or red markings. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often used in gardens for its ornamental value.

Its foliage remains attractive throughout the growing season, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.
