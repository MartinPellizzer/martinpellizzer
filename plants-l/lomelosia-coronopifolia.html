Lomelosia coronopifolia

Lomelosia coronopifolia, commonly known as the crownleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive crown-like arrangement of leaves at the base of the stem. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Lomelosia coronopifolia is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other moist habitats.
