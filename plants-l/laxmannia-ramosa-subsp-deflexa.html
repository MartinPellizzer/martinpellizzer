Laxmannia ramosa subsp. deflexa

Laxmannia ramosa subsp.

deflexa, commonly known as the flexible willow-herb, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to temperate regions of Asia. It features slender, branching stems that often grow in a prostrate or decumbent manner, allowing it to spread across the ground.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense spikes, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, linear, and often covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wet meadows or along riverbanks.
