Leptosomus natans

Leptosomus natans, commonly known as the floating bladderwort, is a free-floating aquatic plant found in freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its filamentous, leafless stems that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically submerged or just at the water's surface. It is a member of the Lentibulariaceae family, known for its carnivorous tendencies.
