Lobelia cardamines

Lobelia cardamines, commonly known as the spring gentian, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often have a slightly toothed margin.

Lobelia cardamines is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
