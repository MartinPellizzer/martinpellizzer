Lysimachia navillei

Lysimachia navillei, commonly known as Naville's chickweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It features small, delicate yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Lysimachia navillei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
