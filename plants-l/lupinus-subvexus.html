Lupinus subvexus

Lupinus subvexus, commonly known as the slender lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Lupinus subvexus plays a role in nitrogen fixation, making it valuable for soil enrichment.
