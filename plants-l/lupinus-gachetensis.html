Lupinus gachetensis

Lupinus gachetensis, commonly known as the Gachet's lupin, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its foliage consists of compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces spikes of colorful, pea-like flowers.

Lupinus gachetensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
