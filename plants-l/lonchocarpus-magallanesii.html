Lonchocarpus magallanesii

Lonchocarpus magallanesii, commonly known as the yellow senna, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, bean-like pods containing the seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
