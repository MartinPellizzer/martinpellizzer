Libidibia ferrea var. leiostachya

Libidibia ferrea var. leiostachya, commonly known as ironwood or Brazilian ironwood, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood and thick, dark green foliage. The plant has compound leaves with long, narrow leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody pods containing flat, bean-like seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its strong, durable wood and has ecological importance in its native habitat.
