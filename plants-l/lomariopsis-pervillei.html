Lomariopsis pervillei

Lomariopsis pervillei, commonly known as the Perville's fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are often arching or drooping. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it to substrates, while its spore-producing structures are located on the underside of its leaves.

Lomariopsis pervillei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage.
