Lonchocarpus bracteolatus

Lonchocarpus bracteolatus, commonly known as the bracteolate poinsettia, is a species of flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive, colorful bracts that resemble poinsettia flowers. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are often overlooked due to the showy foliage. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight.

The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough texture, contributing to its ornamental value.
