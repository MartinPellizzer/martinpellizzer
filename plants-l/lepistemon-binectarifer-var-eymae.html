Lepistemon binectarifer var. eymae

Lepistemon binectarifer var. eymae, commonly known as the Eyma's twin-flower, is a unique variant of the Lepistemon genus native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, twin-like flowers that bloom in pairs, often in shades of violet or purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while the stems are slender and upright.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens for its distinctive floral display.
