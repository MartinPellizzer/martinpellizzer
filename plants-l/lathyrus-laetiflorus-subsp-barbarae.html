Lathyrus laetiflorus subsp. barbarae

Lathyrus laetiflorus subsp.

barbarae, commonly known as the Barbadan sweet pea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its climbing habit and produces clusters of fragrant, pea-like flowers in shades of pink and purple.

The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are typically hairy. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental value.

Due to its attractive blooms and historical use in traditional medicine, it holds both horticultural and cultural significance.
