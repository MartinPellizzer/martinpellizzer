Leucospermum rodolentum

Leucospermum rodolentum, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescences that bear bright orange to red tubular flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in full sun with well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its striking appearance and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

The flowers attract birds and insects, making it a valuable addition to wildlife-friendly gardens.
