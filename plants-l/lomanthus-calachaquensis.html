Lomanthus calachaquensis

Lomanthus calachaquensis, commonly known as the Calachaqui lomatium, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or arid soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss.

The flowers are small and yellow, often arranged in a cluster, and bloom during the warmer months of the year.
