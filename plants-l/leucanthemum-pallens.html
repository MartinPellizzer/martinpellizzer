Leucanthemum pallens

Leucanthemum pallens, commonly known as the pale coneflower, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its white petals and a yellow central cone, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and thrives in partial to full shade. It produces small, showy flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Leucanthemum pallens is valued for its ornamental beauty and its ability to support local ecosystems.
