Libocedrus cupressoides

Libocedrus cupressoides, commonly known as the California incense cedar, is a coniferous tree native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Cupressaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, scale-like leaves and reddish-brown bark. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of fragrant wood.

The species is well adapted to dry, rocky environments and can thrive in a range of soil types.
