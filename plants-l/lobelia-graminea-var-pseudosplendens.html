Lobelia graminea var. pseudosplendens

Lobelia graminea var. pseudosplendens, commonly known as the slender lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spikes of small, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in wetland habitats.
