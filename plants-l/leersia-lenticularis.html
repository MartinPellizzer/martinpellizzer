Leersia lenticularis

Leersia lenticularis, commonly known as the lens-shaped sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and three-sided stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, three-lobed flowers. It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions.

Leersia lenticularis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
