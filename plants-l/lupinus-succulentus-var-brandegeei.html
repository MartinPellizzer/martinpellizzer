Lupinus succulentus var. brandegeei

Lupinus succulentus var. brandegeei, commonly known as the Brandegee's lupine, is a succulent perennial plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems and clusters of blue to violet flowers. The plant has adapted to arid environments with its water-storing stems and reduced leaf surfaces. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an important species in its native habitat.
