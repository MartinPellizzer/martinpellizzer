Lobularia canariensis subsp. microsperma

Lobularia canariensis subsp.

microsperma, commonly known as Canary Island lobularia, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands. It is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial that produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale blue.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its delicate appearance and ability to attract pollinators.

The subspecies is distinguished by its smaller, more compact flowers compared to other Lobularia species.
