Linum pycnophyllum

Linum pycnophyllum, commonly known as the dense-flowered flax, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant features linear, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Linum pycnophyllum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in textile production.
