Litsea sulavesiana

Litsea sulavesiana, commonly known as the Moluccan laurier, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Indonesia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, glossy, elliptical leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
