Laurus cordata

Laurus cordata, commonly known as sweet bay or Turkish bay laurel, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green, elliptical leaves that are aromatic when crushed. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Laurus cordata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of aromatic leaves used in cooking and traditional medicine.
