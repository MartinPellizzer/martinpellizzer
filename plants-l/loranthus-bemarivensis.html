Loranthus bemarivensis

Loranthus bemarivensis, commonly known as the Madagascar mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on a variety of host trees in Madagascar.

It belongs to the Loranthaceae family and has a complex life cycle involving both vegetative and reproductive phases. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers and red berries that are dispersed by birds. Its roots penetrate the host's vascular system to obtain water and nutrients, while it produces its own chlorophyll for partial photosynthesis.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting biodiversity through its interactions with birds and other organisms.
