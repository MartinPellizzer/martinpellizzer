Lippia globiflora var. geminata

Lippia globiflora var. geminata, commonly known as the twin-flowered lippia, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and is often found in dry, sunny habitats. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale pink flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. Its leaves are typically aromatic, with a mild mint-like scent, and the plant is often used in traditional herbal remedies.

Lippia globiflora var. geminata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual and aromatic qualities.
