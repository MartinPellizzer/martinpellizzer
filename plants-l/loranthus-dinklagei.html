Loranthus dinklagei

Loranthus dinklagei, commonly known as the Dinklage's mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily in the genus Ficus.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while also deriving some photosynthetic ability from its own leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale green berries that are often consumed by birds, which then disperse the seeds. It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines.

Due to its parasitic nature and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized species within the Loranthaceae family.
