Leea grandifolia

Leea grandifolia, commonly known as the large-flowered leeah, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing shrub characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are green when unripe and turn red when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation in warm climates.
