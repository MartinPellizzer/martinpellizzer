Leptorkis affinis

Leptorkis affinis, commonly known as the slender rock fern, is a small, epiphytic fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows on tree trunks or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant has delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are usually less than 10 centimeters in length. Its rhizomes are short and often covered in a fine, white coating.

Leptorkis affinis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
