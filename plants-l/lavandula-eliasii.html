Lavandula eliasii

Lavandula eliasii, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada lavender, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the western United States.

It features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces spikes of pale blue to violet flowers. This species is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and full sun, making it drought-tolerant. It is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.

The plant's aromatic foliage and flowers make it a popular choice for both garden and landscape design.
