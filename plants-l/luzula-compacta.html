Luzula compacta

Luzula compacta, commonly known as dwarf wood rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, commonly known as the grass family, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, greenish-white flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often covered in a fine layer of silky hairs.

Luzula compacta is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.
