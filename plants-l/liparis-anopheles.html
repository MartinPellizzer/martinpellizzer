Liparis anopheles

Liparis anopheles, commonly known as the Anopheles orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its compact appearance.

Botanically, it belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
