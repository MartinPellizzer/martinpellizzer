Lomariocycas cycadifolia

Lomariocycas cycadifolia, commonly known as the slender cycad, is a unique cycad species native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and a crown of pinnately compound leaves that are typically green and somewhat leathery. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by woody, cone-like structures containing seeds. It thrives in tropical climates with well-drained soil and requires minimal water once established.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native environment.
