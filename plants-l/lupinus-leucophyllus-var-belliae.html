Lupinus leucophyllus var. belliae

Lupinus leucophyllus var. belliae, commonly known as the silverleaf lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-white, narrow leaves and tall spikes of blue or purple flowers. The plant has a deep taproot system, allowing it to thrive in well-drained, sandy soils. It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.

Its flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity.
