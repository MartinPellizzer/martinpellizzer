Luehea eichleri

Luehea eichleri, commonly known as the white cedar, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, light-colored bark and elongated, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by round, woody fruits containing single seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Luehea eichleri is valued for its hard, durable wood and has traditional uses in local communities.
