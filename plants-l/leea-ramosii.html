Leea ramosii

Leea ramosii, commonly known as the hairy leea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Leeaceae and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
