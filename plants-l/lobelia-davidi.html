Lobelia davidi

Lobelia davidi, commonly known as the Chinese toadflax, is a flowering plant native to China.

It is characterized by its erect, slender stems and clusters of small, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Lobelia davidi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
