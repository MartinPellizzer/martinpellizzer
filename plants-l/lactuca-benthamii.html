Lactuca benthamii

Lactuca benthamii, commonly known as the Bentham's lettuce, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family, Asteraceae.

It is native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina and Chile. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, bluish-green leaves and can grow up to 30 centimeters in height. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, followed by small, elongated seeds.

Lactuca benthamii is often found in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.
