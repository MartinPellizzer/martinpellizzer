Lobelia urens var. longebracteata

Lobelia urens var. longebracteata, commonly known as the long-bract lobelia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant produces distinctive bracts that are longer than the flowers themselves, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
