Leycesteria formosa var. stenosepala

Leycesteria formosa var. stenosepala, commonly known as the narrow-sepaled honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern Himalayas.

It features fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters along the stems. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly serrated margin. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its showy blooms.
