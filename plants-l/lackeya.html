Lackeya

Lackeya, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Lackeya is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
