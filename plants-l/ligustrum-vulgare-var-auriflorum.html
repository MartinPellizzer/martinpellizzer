Ligustrum vulgare var. auriflorum

Ligustrum vulgare var. auriflorum, commonly known as the goldenflower privet, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its distinctive golden-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. The leaves are typically dark green and glossy, providing an attractive appearance throughout the growing season. This variety is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.

It is also valued for its use in hedges and as a foundation plant in landscaping.
