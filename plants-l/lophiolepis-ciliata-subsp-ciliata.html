Lophiolepis ciliata subsp. ciliata

Lophiolepis ciliata subsp.

ciliata, commonly known as the coastal lichen grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa. It typically grows in coastal and sandy environments, adapting to saline and well-drained soils.

The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, spikelet-bearing flowers.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes and supporting local biodiversity.
