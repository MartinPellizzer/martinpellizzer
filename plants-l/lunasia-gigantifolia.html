Lunasia gigantifolia

Lunasia gigantifolia, commonly known as the giant moonflower, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night and close during the day. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its rapid growth and impressive size. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, while its stems are thick and woody.

The flowers emit a sweet scent that attracts nocturnal pollinators such as moths.
