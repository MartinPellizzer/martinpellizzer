Lycianthes somniculenta var. lanceolata

Lycianthes somniculenta var. lanceolata, commonly known as the nightshade leaf vine, is a member of the Solanaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red berries that are edible but have a bitter taste. Native to tropical regions of South America, it thrives in warm, humid climates.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
