Lonicera altaica

Lonicera altaica, commonly known as the Altai honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its slender, arching stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its berries are red or orange and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

Lonicera altaica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
