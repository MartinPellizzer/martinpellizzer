Lotus bryantii

Lotus bryantii, commonly known as the Bryant's lotus, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high elevations. The plant features palmate leaves with a waxy surface, and its flowers are usually pink to purple in color, with a distinctively shaped calyx. It is adapted to cold climates and can withstand harsh environmental conditions.

The Bryant's lotus is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
