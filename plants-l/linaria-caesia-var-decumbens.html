Linaria caesia var. decumbens

Linaria caesia var. decumbens, commonly known as the prostrate toadflax, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features slender, creeping stems that often lie close to the ground, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, yellow-orange flowers with two lips, typical of the Linaria genus. Its leaves are linear and slightly hairy, adapted to reduce water loss in arid environments.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
