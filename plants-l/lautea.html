Lautea

Lautea, commonly known as the blueberry bush, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and vitamins. Lautea thrives in acidic soils and requires well-drained conditions to flourish.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping and horticultural gardens.
