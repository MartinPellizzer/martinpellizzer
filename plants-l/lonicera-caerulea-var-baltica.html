Lonicera caerulea var. baltica

Lonicera caerulea var. baltica, commonly known as the Baltic honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Europe and parts of Russia.

It produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring. The plant is characterized by its opposite, oval-shaped leaves and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible berries.

The berries, which are typically yellow or red, are used in jams, wines, and traditional foods.
