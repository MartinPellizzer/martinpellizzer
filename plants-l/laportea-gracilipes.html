Laportea gracilipes

Laportea gracilipes, commonly known as the slender leaf false nettle, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are typically borne on slender stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland understories and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
