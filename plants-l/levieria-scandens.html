Levieria scandens

Levieria scandens, commonly known as the climbing lily, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its woody stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
