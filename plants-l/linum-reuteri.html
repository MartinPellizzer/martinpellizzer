Linum reuteri

Linum reuteri, commonly known as the narrowleaf flax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Linaceae family, which includes other flax species. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Linum reuteri is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.
