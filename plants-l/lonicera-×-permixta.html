Lonicera × permixta

Lonicera × permixta, commonly known as the crossvine, is a hybrid evergreen shrub that exhibits a combination of traits from its parent species, Lonicera japonica and Lonicera hongkongensis.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth and the production of long, twisting vines that can climb or trail along surfaces. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, which bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. Its foliage consists of opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that are often glossy and dark green.

Lonicera × permixta is commonly cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
