Licuala longipedunculata

Licuala longipedunculata, commonly known as the long-pedunculate palm, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length, making it a striking ornamental plant. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and landscapes.

The palm produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, coconut-like fruits.
