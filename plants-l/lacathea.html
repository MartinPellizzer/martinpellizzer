Lacathea

Lacathea, commonly known as the golden bamboo or *Phyllostachys aurea*, is a species of grass in the Poaceae family.

It is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and golden-yellow coloration. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and ability to form dense clumps. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for landscaping.

Lacathea is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of bamboo for crafts and construction.
