Lonicera dioica subsp. glaucescens

Lonicera dioica subsp.

glaucescens, commonly known as the blue honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia. It features opposite, simple, and usually ovate leaves that are often covered in a waxy, bluish-white bloom, giving it its common name.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically yellow or white, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators and providing food for wildlife.
