Laelia anceps var. roeblingiana

Laelia anceps var. roeblingiana, commonly known as the Roeblingiana orchid, is a hybrid orchid native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces large, showy flowers with intricate patterns of purple, pink, and white. The plant typically blooms during the summer months, and its flowers are highly prized for their elegance and fragrance. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

This variety is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
