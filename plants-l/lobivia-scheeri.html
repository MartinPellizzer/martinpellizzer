Lobivia scheeri

Lobivia scheeri, commonly known as the Scheeri's lobivia, is a succulent plant native to South America, specifically Argentina.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rounded shape and fleshy, ribbed stems. The plant produces small, vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. Its leaves are often reduced to spines, which help it conserve water in arid environments.

Lobivia scheeri is well-suited for rock gardens and requires minimal watering, making it a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
