Lippia geminata var. lockhartii

Lippia geminata var. lockhartii, commonly known as the Lockhart's lippia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Central America and the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and along stream banks. The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid climates.
