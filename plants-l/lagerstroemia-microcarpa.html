Lagerstroemia microcarpa

Lagerstroemia microcarpa, commonly known as the small-fruited crape myrtle, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to red bract clusters that appear in summer, providing ornamental value. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and turn reddish in autumn. Its small, capsule-like fruits contain seeds that are dispersed by wind.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
