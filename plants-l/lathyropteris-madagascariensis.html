Lathyropteris madagascariensis

Lathyropteris madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its delicate, floating fronds that are often used in traditional medicine by local communities. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and streams, where it can anchor itself to submerged surfaces. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, typical of many fern species.

The plant's unique morphology and ecological role make it an important component of Madagascar's freshwater ecosystems.
