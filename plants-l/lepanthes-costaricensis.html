Lepanthes costaricensis

Lepanthes costaricensis, commonly known as the Costa Rican orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pale pink. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in its natural habitat. It prefers shaded, humid environments and is often found growing in the understory of tropical forests.

Lepanthes costaricensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its native regions.
