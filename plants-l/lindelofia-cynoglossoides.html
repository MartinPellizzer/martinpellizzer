Lindelofia cynoglossoides

Lindelofia cynoglossoides, commonly known as the narrow-leafed lindelofia, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in the spring. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and swampy areas. It is characterized by its fibrous root system and its ability to form dense colonies.

Lindelofia cynoglossoides is often found in wetlands and is adapted to tolerate periodic flooding.
