Lepanthes odontolabis

Lepanthes odontolabis, commonly known as the toothed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that thrives in humid, shaded environments within tropical forests. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers with a distinctive tooth-like feature on the lip, which is a characteristic trait of many Lepanthes species. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and surrounding substrates.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often sought after by orchid enthusiasts and botanists alike.
