Leptochloa monticola

Leptochloa monticola, commonly known as mountain grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous or hilly areas. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets.

Leptochloa monticola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a ground cover.
