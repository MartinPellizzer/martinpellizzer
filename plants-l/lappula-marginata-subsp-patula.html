Lappula marginata subsp. patula

Lappula marginata subsp.

patula, commonly known as the narrowleaf lappia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to various arid and semi-arid environments.

This species is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats, contributing to the biodiversity of its native range.
