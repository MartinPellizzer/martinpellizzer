Laelia anceps subsp. sanderiana

Laelia anceps subsp.

sanderiana, commonly known as the Sander's laelia, is a species of orchid native to Mexico. It is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange.

The plant typically blooms in winter and early spring, with flowers arranged in a raceme. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Its botanical features make it a prized specimen among orchid enthusiasts.
