Linaria triphylla var. minor

Linaria triphylla var. minor, commonly known as the lesser flaxflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender stems and small, yellow-orange flowers arranged in racemes. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy, and it thrives in sunny, well-drained soils. It is valued in horticulture for its delicate appearance and ability to attract pollinators.

Its botanical name reflects its close relationship to the genus Linaria, which includes several species of flowering plants.
