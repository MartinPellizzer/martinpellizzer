Liparis bituberculata

Liparis bituberculata, commonly known as the two-ribbed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically arranged in a loose inflorescence. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Botanically, it belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its delicate structure and unique floral morphology.
