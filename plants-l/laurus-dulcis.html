Laurus dulcis

Laurus dulcis, commonly known as laurel, is a flowering evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its aromatic leaves, which are widely used as a spice and in culinary applications. Its fruit, a small drupe, contains a single seed and is also used in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.
