Lepanthes silverstonei

Lepanthes silverstonei, commonly known as the Silverstone orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the highlands of Mexico.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces delicate, showy flowers with a distinctive white and green coloration, often with a yellow lip. Its growth habit is characterized by a short, upright stem with a few leaves and a single or few floral spikes.

Lepanthes silverstonei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
