Listrostachys welwitschii

Listrostachys welwitschii, commonly known as the Welwitschia mirabilis, is a unique succulent plant native to the Namib Desert in Namibia.

It is characterized by its thick, woody trunk and large, leathery leaves that can grow up to 3 meters in length. The plant has a distinctive root system that allows it to survive in arid conditions by storing water. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by insects, and its seeds are dispersed by wind.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, it is protected in its natural habitat.
