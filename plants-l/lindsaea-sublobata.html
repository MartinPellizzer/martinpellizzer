Lindsaea sublobata

Lindsaea sublobata, commonly known as the slender lindsaea, is a species of epiphytic fern native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows on tree trunks or branches, drawing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and produce spores for propagation.

Lindsaea sublobata is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in shaded, humid environments.
