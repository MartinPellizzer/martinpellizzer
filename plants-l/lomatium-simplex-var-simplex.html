Lomatium simplex var. simplex

Lomatium simplex var. simplex, commonly known as the western lomatium, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features a basal rosette of deeply lobed, fern-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine by indigenous peoples.

This species thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.
