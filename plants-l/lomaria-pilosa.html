Lomaria pilosa

Lomaria pilosa, commonly known as the hairy lomaria, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and produces small, daisy-like flowers that are typically yellow or white. Native to South America, this plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in open fields or along roadsides. Its fibrous roots and robust stems allow it to withstand various environmental conditions.

The plant is also noted for its potential medicinal uses in traditional herbal practices.
