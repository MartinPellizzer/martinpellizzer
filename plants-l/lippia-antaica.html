Lippia antaica

Lippia antaica, commonly known as the Andean mint, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and digestive properties.

Lippia antaica is valued for its essential oils, which contribute to its distinctive scent and potential therapeutic uses.
