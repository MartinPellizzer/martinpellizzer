Lonicera caerulea var. venulosa

Lonicera caerulea var. venulosa, commonly known as the sweet birch or red honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, red-orange flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible and have a tart, honey-like flavor. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a glossy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.
