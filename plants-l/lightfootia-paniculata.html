Lightfootia paniculata

Lightfootia paniculata, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, daisy-like flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

Lightfootia paniculata is drought-tolerant and prefers well-drained soil.
