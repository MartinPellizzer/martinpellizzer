Leptostachya comata

Leptostachya comata, commonly known as the hairy leaved bamboo, is a perennial grass native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and long, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the grass family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Leptostachya comata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage.
