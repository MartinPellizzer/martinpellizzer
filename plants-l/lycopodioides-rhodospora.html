Lycopodioides rhodospora

Lycopodioides rhodospora, commonly known as the red-spore lycopod, is a species of clubmoss belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its small, erect, and often sparsely branched stems that bear spirally arranged leaves. The plant produces spores in capsules that are reddish in color, giving it its common name. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or rocky crevices.

Lycopodioides rhodospora plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms.
