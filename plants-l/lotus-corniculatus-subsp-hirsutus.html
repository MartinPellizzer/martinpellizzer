Lotus corniculatus subsp. hirsutus

Lotus corniculatus subsp.

hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy grass violet, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, violet or blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as meadows and roadsides. It has small, heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
