Lunania buchii

Lunania buchii, commonly known as the white moonflower, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night and close by dawn. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires support for its vine-like growth habit.

Due to its unique flowering pattern, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and greenhouses.
