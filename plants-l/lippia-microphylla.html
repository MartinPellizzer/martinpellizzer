Lippia microphylla

Lippia microphylla, commonly known as Mexican blueweed, is a perennial herb native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of pale blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in wetlands, prairies, and along stream banks.

Lippia microphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
