Lycium chinense subf. lanceolatum

Lycium chinense subf.

lanceolatum, commonly known as the Chinese boxthorn, is a shrub native to eastern Asia. It features slender, spiny stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs.

The plant produces yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.

The species is also used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
