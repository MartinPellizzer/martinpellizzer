Liparis glaucescens

Liparis glaucescens, commonly known as the blue orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are typically borne on tall, slender stems. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, giving them a glossy appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Liparis glaucescens is a perennial herb that is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
