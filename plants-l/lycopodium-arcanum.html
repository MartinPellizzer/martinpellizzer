Lycopodium arcanum

Lycopodium arcanum, commonly known as the ghost fern, is a rare and enigmatic member of the Lycopodiaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, intricate fronds that resemble ghostly shadows, hence its common name. This plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its sporophytic structures are highly specialized, with sporangia clustered in strobila, a feature typical of many clubmosses.

Due to its rarity and unique morphology, Lycopodium arcanum is of significant interest to botanists and conservationists.
