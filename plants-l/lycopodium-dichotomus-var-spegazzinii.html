Lycopodium dichotomus var. spegazzinii

Lycopodium dichotomus var. spegazzinii, commonly known as the Spegazzini's clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems covered in small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces sporangia in clusters at the tips of the branches, which release spores for reproduction. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or rocky crevices.

This variety is notable for its morphological distinctiveness and is often found in specific geographic regions of South America.
