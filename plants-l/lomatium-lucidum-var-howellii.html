Lomatium lucidum var. howellii

Lomatium lucidum var. howellii, commonly known as Howell's shining lomatium, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to the carrot family (Apiaceae) and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It produces small, dry fruits called schizocarps, which disperse via wind.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its delicate foliage and flowering habit.
