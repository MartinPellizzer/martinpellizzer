Ligustrum chenaultii

Ligustrum chenaultii, commonly known as Chenault's privet, is a species of flowering plant in the Oleaceae family.

It is native to the island of Madagascar and is characterized by its small, opposite leaves and clusters of white flowers. The plant produces small, dark blue to black berries that are edible for some bird species. It typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
