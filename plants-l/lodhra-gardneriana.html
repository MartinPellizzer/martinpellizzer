Lodhra gardneriana

Lodhra gardneriana, commonly known as the Indian privet, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, simple, and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring season. Its fruit is a small, spherical drupe that turns from green to yellow or red as it matures.

Lodhra gardneriana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional medicinal uses in Ayurveda.
