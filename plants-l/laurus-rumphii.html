Laurus rumphii

Laurus rumphii, commonly known as the Nutmeg Laurel, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Indonesian archipelago.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like laurel and cinnamon. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall, featuring glossy, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant, yellowish flowers. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that contains a single seed, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties.

The plant is also valued for its essential oils, which are used in perfumery and traditional medicine.
