Lasiosiphon calocephalus

Lasiosiphon calocephalus, commonly known as the velvetleaf morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. The flowers are trumpet-shaped, typically blue or violet, and bloom in clusters. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, round seeds.

It is often found in disturbed habitats and is considered an invasive species in some areas.
