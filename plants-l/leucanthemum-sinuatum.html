Leucanthemum sinuatum

Leucanthemum sinuatum, commonly known as the white marguerite, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features showy white or pale yellow flowers with prominent yellow centers, surrounded by white ray florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 10 to 30 centimeters, with finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden borders and rock gardens.

Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
