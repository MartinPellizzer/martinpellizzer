Lisianthius cordifolius

Lisianthius cordifolius, commonly known as the heart-leaved lisianthius, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically a deep green and have a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
