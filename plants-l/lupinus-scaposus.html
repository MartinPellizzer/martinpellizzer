Lupinus scaposus

Lupinus scaposus, commonly known as the slender lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has pinnately compound leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Lupinus scaposus is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes, and it plays a role in nitrogen fixation due to its symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.
