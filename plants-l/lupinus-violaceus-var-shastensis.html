Lupinus violaceus var. shastensis

Lupinus violaceus var. shastensis, commonly known as Shasta lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of violet to purple flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open meadows and grasslands.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
