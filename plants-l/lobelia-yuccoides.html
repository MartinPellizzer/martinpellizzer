Lobelia yuccoides

Lobelia yuccoides, commonly known as the yucca lily, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features striking, sword-shaped leaves that are blue-gray in color and have a rough texture. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for native gardens and arid landscapes.
