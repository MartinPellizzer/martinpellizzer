Lilium concolor

Lilium concolor, commonly known as the white lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, white, cup-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its bulbs are edible and have been used in traditional culinary practices.

Lilium concolor is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens.
