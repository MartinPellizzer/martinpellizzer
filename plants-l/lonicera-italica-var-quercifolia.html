Lonicera italica var. quercifolia

Lonicera italica var. quercifolia, commonly known as the oakleaf honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, maple-like leaves that are lobed and turn vibrant red in the fall. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers in the spring, which are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in partial to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

This species is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
