Lysinema attenuatum

Lysinema attenuatum, commonly known as the slender lichen, is a species of lichen that belongs to the family Lecanoraceae.

It is a crustose lichen, meaning it forms a thin, crust-like layer on its substrate, typically growing on rock surfaces or bark. The thallus is usually pale to dark gray, and it lacks distinct lobes, giving it a smooth appearance. It is often found in temperate regions and is characterized by its slow growth rate and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.

This lichen is of interest to ecologists due to its role in soil formation and as an indicator of air quality.
