Liatris squarrosa f. alba

Liatris squarrosa f. alba, commonly known as the white gayfeather, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, spikelike inflorescence composed of numerous white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in prairies, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are linear and grayish-green, often with a rough texture.

The white gayfeather is a valuable plant for pollinators, attracting bees and butterflies with its nectar-rich blooms.
