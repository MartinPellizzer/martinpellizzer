Lepidium auritum

Lepidium auritum, commonly known as the "ear-shaped cress," is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like mustard and cabbage. The plant is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and smooth, edible leaves that have a peppery flavor. Lepidium auritum thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks.

Its seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cuisines.
