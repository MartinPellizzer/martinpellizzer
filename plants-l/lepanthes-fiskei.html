Lepanthes fiskei

Lepanthes fiskei, commonly known as the Fiske's slipper orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that resemble small slippers, with intricate patterns of purple and white. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded forest environments. Its flowers bloom during the wet season, often in clusters, and are pollinated by specific insect species.

Lepanthes fiskei is valued for its ornamental beauty and is a subject of interest in botanical research due to its unique morphology and ecological role.
