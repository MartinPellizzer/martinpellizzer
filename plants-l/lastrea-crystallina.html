Lastrea crystallina

Lastrea crystallina, commonly known as the glassy dandelion, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of South Africa.

It features fleshy, cylindrical stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy, white seed heads. Its leaves are typically small, narrow, and covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

The species is prized for its unique appearance and drought tolerance, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
