Lagochilus inebrians

Lagochilus inebrians, commonly known as the drunk fennel, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported sedative and analgesic properties.

The plant's name reflects its historical use in rituals and its association with intoxication in some cultural contexts.
