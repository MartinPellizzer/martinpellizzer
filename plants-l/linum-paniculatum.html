Linum paniculatum

Linum paniculatum, commonly known as wild flax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, blue or violet flowers. The plant produces oval, smooth seeds that are often used in traditional herbal medicine. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Linum paniculatum is often found in dry, open habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
