Lepanthes excavata

Lepanthes excavata, commonly known as the pit orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its unique, shallow pit-like structure at the base of its flowers, which is a distinguishing botanical feature. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually small and come in shades of white, pink, or purple.

Lepanthes excavata is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
