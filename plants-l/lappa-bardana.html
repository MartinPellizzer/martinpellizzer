Lappa bardana

Lappa bardana, commonly known as greater burdock, is a biennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces purple flowers in clusters, which are followed by spiky, brown seed heads. Its root is thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Lappa bardana thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.
