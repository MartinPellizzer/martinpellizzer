Lissochilus purpuratus

Lissochilus purpuratus, commonly known as the purple-lipped orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America, particularly in regions of Peru and Ecuador.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet and purple floral hues. The plant typically grows in humid, forested environments, often at higher elevations. Its flowers are small to medium in size, with a distinctive lip that is often purplish in color.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
