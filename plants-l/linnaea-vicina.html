Linnaea vicina

Linnaea vicina, commonly known as the twinflower, is a small evergreen shrub native to the northern regions of North America and Eurasia.

It is characterized by its delicate, paired flowers that bloom in early spring, typically in shades of white or pink. The plant features opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green, often with a slightly hairy texture. Its roots are fibrous and help it thrive in moist, acidic soils.

Linnaea vicina is often found in shaded woodlands and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance.
