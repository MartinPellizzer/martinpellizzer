Litsea formanii

Litsea formanii, commonly known as the Forman's litsea, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a glossy green surface. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing a fragrant aroma, and it produces small, fleshy berries that are edible for some bird species.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
