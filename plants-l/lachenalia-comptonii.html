Lachenalia comptonii

Lachenalia comptonii, commonly known as the Compton's lachenalia, is a bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and as a container plant.
