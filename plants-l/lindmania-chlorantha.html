Lindmania chlorantha

Lindmania chlorantha, commonly known as the silver leaf fern, is a species of fern native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, bright green fronds that have a silvery sheen due to the presence of tiny, scale-like structures on the leaf surface. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks or branches in humid, shaded environments. Its rhizomes form a dense network that helps anchor it to its host and absorb moisture from the air.

Lindmania chlorantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
