Limonium binervosum subsp. sarniense

Limonium binervosum subsp.

sarniense, commonly known as the Sardinian sea holly, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Sardinia. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in coastal areas and saline soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, while the stems are erect and often branched.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in coastal landscaping due to its resilience and beauty.
