Lamium longiflorum var. numidicum

Lamium longiflorum var. numidicum, commonly known as the Numidian dead nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and produces clusters of tubular flowers that are typically pink to purple in color. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
