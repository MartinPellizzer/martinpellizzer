Lacara triplinervia

Lacara triplinervia, commonly known as the three-veined lacara, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves with three prominent veins, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
