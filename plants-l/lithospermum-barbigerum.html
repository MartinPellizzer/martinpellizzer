Lithospermum barbigerum

Lithospermum barbigerum, commonly known as the bearded rock pepper, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, sprawling growth habit and produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various purposes.
