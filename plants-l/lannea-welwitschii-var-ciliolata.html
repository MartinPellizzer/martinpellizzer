Lannea welwitschii var. ciliolata

Lannea welwitschii var. ciliolata, commonly known as the African snowball tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and its striking, spherical clusters of white flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and small, globose fruits that turn red when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique floral display.
