Leptochloa lindleyana

Leptochloa lindleyana, commonly known as the slender grass or lindley's grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing minute flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of grassland ecosystems and a subject of study in plant taxonomy.
