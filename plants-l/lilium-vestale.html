Lilium vestale

Lilium vestale, commonly known as the white lily, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the Liliaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and large, showy white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces sword-shaped leaves that are typically broad and pointed. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Lilium vestale is often cultivated for its ornamental value and symbolic significance in various cultures.
