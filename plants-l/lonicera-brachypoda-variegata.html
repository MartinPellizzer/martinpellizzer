Lonicera brachypoda variegata

Lonicera brachypoda variegata, commonly known as the variegated honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features slender, arching stems that produce small, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and white during the spring. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a distinctive variegation pattern of white and green. This plant thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
