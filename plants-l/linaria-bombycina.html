Linaria bombycina

Linaria bombycina, commonly known as the woolly toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or dry soils, often found in mountainous regions. The plant produces bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, giving it a woolly appearance.

Linaria bombycina is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
