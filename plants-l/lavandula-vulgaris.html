Lavandula vulgaris

Lavandula vulgaris, commonly known as English lavender, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, spike-like inflorescences composed of numerous purple to pinkish-blue flowers. The plant has narrow, gray-green, aromatic leaves that are densely packed along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal use.

Lavandula vulgaris is widely cultivated for its essential oils, which are used in perfumery, aromatherapy, and medicinal applications.
