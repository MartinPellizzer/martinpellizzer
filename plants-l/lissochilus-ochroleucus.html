Lissochilus ochroleucus

Lissochilus ochroleucus, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers that bloom during the spring and summer seasons. The plant typically grows in high-altitude cloud forests, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence.

The orchid's unique floral structure and vibrant color make it a popular subject in botanical studies and horticultural collections.
