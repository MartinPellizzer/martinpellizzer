Lycium chinense

Lycium chinense, commonly known as Chinese wolfberry or Goji berry, is a shrub native to China and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its reddish berries that are rich in antioxidants. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid regions and has small, yellowish flowers. Its leaves are ovate and slightly hairy, while its stems are woody and branched.

The fruit, which ripens to a bright orange-red color, is widely used in traditional medicine and as a food supplement.
