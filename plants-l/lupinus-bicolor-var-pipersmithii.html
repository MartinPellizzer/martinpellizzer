Lupinus bicolor var. pipersmithii

Lupinus bicolor var. pipersmithii, commonly known as the Pipersmith's lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking bicolor flowers, typically featuring a white upper petal and a purple lower petal. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces cylindrical seed pods that mature into a dark brown color.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
