Lilium longiflorum var. vittatum

Lilium longiflorum var. vittatum, commonly known as the variegated tiger lily, is a cultivar of the lily family (Liliaceae) characterized by its striking, variegated foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers.

The plant typically features broad, lance-shaped leaves with distinct white or yellow markings, adding visual interest to gardens. Its flowers are usually orange to red in color, with a distinctive black or brown blotch at the base of the tepals. It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in floral arrangements due to its vibrant colors and long-lasting blooms.
