Lupinus leptophyllus

Lupinus leptophyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has narrow, pinnately compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Lupinus leptophyllus is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.
