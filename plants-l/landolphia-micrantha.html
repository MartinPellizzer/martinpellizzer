Landolphia micrantha

Landolphia micrantha, commonly known as the African morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces fleshy, orange-red berries that contain seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Landolphia micrantha is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in some African cultures.
