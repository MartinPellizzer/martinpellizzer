Lonicera leycesterioides

Lonicera leycesterioides, commonly known as the Japanese honeysuckle, is a deciduous vine native to East Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to climb or trail along structures. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value, though it can become invasive in certain regions.

The berries that follow the flowers are small, red, and edible, attracting birds and other wildlife.
