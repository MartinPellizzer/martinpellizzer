Ligustrum lodense

Ligustrum lodense, commonly known as the swamp privet, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as swamps and riverbanks, and can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its small, fragrant flowers are usually white or pale yellow and appear in clusters, followed by small, dark berries.

Ligustrum lodense is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
