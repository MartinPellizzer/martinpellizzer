Loranthus albertisii

Loranthus albertisii, commonly known as the Albert's mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that attaches itself to the branches of its host trees.

It belongs to the Loranthaceae family and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often green or yellow in color. Its berries are typically red or orange and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

The leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss.
