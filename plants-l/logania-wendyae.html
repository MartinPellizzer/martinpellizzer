Logania wendyae

Logania wendyae, commonly known as the Wendy's logania, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, specifically Brazil.

It belongs to the family Loganiaceae and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant is an evergreen shrub that can grow up to 2 meters in height, featuring opposite, simple leaves with a rough texture.

Its flowers are clustered in umbels and are pollinated primarily by insects, making it an important species in its native ecosystem.
