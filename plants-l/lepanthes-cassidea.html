Lepanthes cassidea

Lepanthes cassidea, commonly known as the Cassidea orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers and slender, upright stems. The plant typically produces small, greenish-white to pale yellow blooms during the spring and early summer. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern at the base of the plant.

Lepanthes cassidea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm, humid environments.
