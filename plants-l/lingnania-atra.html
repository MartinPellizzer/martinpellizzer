Lingnania atra

Lingnania atra, commonly known as the black lingnania, is a rare and elusive plant species native to the dense, humid forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, almost blackish-green foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found growing on decaying wood or in the undergrowth of tropical rainforests. Its botanical significance lies in its unique adaptations to low-light conditions and its role in the ecological balance of its habitat.

Due to habitat loss and limited study, much about its biology and conservation status remains unknown.
