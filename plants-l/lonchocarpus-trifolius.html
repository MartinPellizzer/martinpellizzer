Lonchocarpus trifolius

Lonchocarpus trifolius, commonly known as the three-leaved senna, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellowish to orange flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by distinctive pod fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. It contains toxic alkaloids, making it important to handle with care.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the Fabaceae family.
