Larix × pendula var. repens

Larix × pendula var. repens, commonly known as the creeping larch, is a hybrid coniferous evergreen shrub that exhibits unique botanical characteristics.

It is a natural cross between Larix decidua (European larch) and Larix gmelinii (Siberian larch), resulting in a plant with distinctive, cascading foliage. The leaves are needle-like, bright green, and often hang in long, drooping clusters, giving the plant its ornamental appeal. It produces small, cone-like structures that mature into woody cones, typical of the Larix genus.

This variety is well-suited for rock gardens and alpine settings due to its compact growth habit and cold hardiness.
