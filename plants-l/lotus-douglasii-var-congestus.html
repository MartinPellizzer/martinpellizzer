Lotus douglasii var. congestus

Lotus douglasii var. congestus, commonly known as the Sierra lotus, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, pink or purple flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, showy flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
