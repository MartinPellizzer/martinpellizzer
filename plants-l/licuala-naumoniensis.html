Licuala naumoniensis

Licuala naumoniensis, commonly known as the Naumon Island palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found on the island of Naumoni. This palm is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in botanical gardens and as a decorative plant in some tropical landscapes.
