Lycium caespitosum

Lycium caespitosum, commonly known as the silver berry or creeping lycium, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny growth habit and small, dark blue to black berries that are edible. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas. Its leaves are narrow and silvery-gray, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

The berries are rich in antioxidants and have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
