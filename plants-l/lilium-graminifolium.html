Lilium graminifolium

Lilium graminifolium, commonly known as the grass-leaved lily, is a species of flowering plant in the Liliaceae family.

It is native to regions of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, where it thrives in forested and mountainous areas. The plant is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and tall, slender stems that bear showy, white or pale pink flowers. It blooms in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
