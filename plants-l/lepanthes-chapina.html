Lepanthes chapina

Lepanthes chapina, commonly known as the Chapin's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments within the understory of forests. The plant features delicate, linear leaves and produces intricate, fragrant flowers with a distinctive white and purple coloration. Its flowers are usually arranged in loose clusters and bloom during the warm season.

Lepanthes chapina is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance.
