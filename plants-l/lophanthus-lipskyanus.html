Lophanthus lipskyanus

Lophanthus lipskyanus, commonly known as the Siberian lophanthus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its woody stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing fragrant, pale yellow to white petals. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence.

Lophanthus lipskyanus is well adapted to cold climates and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.
