Lepanthes venusta

Lepanthes venusta, commonly known as the showy orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often colorful flowers that range from white to violet, with intricate patterns on the petals. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments, relying on air roots for moisture and nutrients. Its leaves are usually dark green and strap-like, aiding in photosynthesis.

Lepanthes venusta is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
