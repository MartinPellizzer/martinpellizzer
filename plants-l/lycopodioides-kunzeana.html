Lycopodioides kunzeana

Lycopodioides kunzeana, commonly known as the Kunze's clubmoss, is a small, evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae and is characterized by its compact, needle-like leaves and small, cone-like structures that produce spores. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky areas. Its foliage is often used in horticultural settings for its ornamental value.

Lycopodioides kunzeana plays a role in maintaining soil stability and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
