Lophiolepis veruta

Lophiolepis veruta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rock heath, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern regions of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Lophiolepis veruta is often found in rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in native plant communities.
