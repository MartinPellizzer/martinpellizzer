Lindernia elata var. floribunda

Lindernia elata var. floribunda, commonly known as the showy lindernia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture and produces clusters of tubular, violet to purple flowers. Its blooming period occurs from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and natural landscapes.
