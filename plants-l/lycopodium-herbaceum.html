Lycopodium herbaceum

Lycopodium herbaceum, commonly known as the ground clubmoss, is a small, evergreen plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its creeping, branched stems that produce small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces sporophytes, which are cone-like structures that release spores for reproduction. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas.

Lycopodium herbaceum plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
