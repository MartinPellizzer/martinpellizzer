Lolium italicum var. ramosum

Lolium italicum var. ramosum, commonly known as Italian ryegrass, is a cool-season grass native to Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny flowers, which are typically greenish to yellowish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in forage and turfgrass applications.

Its rapid growth and high nutritional value make it a valuable plant in agricultural settings.
