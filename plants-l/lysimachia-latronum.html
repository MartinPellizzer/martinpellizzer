Lysimachia latronum

Lysimachia latronum, commonly known as the common yellow loosestrife, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, heart-shaped, and have a smooth or slightly hairy texture.

Lysimachia latronum is often found in wetland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
