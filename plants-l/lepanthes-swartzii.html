Lepanthes swartzii

Lepanthes swartzii, commonly known as the Swartz's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees in humid tropical environments. The plant features delicate, showy flowers that are often white or pale pink, with a distinctively shaped lip. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Lepanthes swartzii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
