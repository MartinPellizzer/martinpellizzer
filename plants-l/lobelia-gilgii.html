Lobelia gilgii

Lobelia gilgii, commonly known as the African bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Lobelia gilgii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
