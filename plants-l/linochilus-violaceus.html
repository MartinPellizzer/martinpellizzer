Linochilus violaceus

Linochilus violaceus, commonly known as the violet-stemmed orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, violet-colored stems and delicate, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces small, fragrant blooms during the spring season.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, Linochilus violaceus is considered a rare and endangered species.
