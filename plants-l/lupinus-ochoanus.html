Lupinus ochoanus

Lupinus ochoanus, commonly known as the Ochoan lupine, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves composed of several small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Lupinus ochoanus is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with soil bacteria.
