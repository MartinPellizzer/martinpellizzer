Lactuca dentata f. partita

Lactuca dentata f. partita, commonly known as the split-leaved lettuce, is a variant of the common lettuce species characterized by its distinctive, lobed leaves.

This plant is native to temperate regions of Europe and is often cultivated for its edible leaves. The leaves are typically pale green with a crisp texture and a mild, slightly bitter flavor. It thrives in cool climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities, making it a popular choice in gardens.
