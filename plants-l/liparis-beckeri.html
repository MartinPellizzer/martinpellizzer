Liparis beckeri

Liparis beckeri, commonly known as the Becker's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid with a slender, erect stem and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that are typically arranged in a loose inflorescence. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its delicate appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
