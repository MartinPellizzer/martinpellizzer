Ledebouria parvifolia

Ledebouria parvifolia, commonly known as the dwarf African bulb, is a small, bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appeal, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and rockeries.
