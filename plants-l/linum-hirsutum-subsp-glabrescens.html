Linum hirsutum subsp. glabrescens

Linum hirsutum subsp.

glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth flax or glabrous flax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

This subspecies is distinguished by its reduced hair coverage compared to the typical form of Linum hirsutum.
