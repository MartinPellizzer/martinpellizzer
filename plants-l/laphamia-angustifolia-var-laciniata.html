Laphamia angustifolia var. laciniata

Laphamia angustifolia var. laciniata, commonly known as the narrowleaf laphamia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often deeply lobed or notched, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its foliage is sometimes used in ornamental gardening due to its unique leaf structure.
