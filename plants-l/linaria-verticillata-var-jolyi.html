Linaria verticillata var. jolyi

Linaria verticillata var. jolyi, commonly known as the Joly's spirea, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces narrow, linear leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
