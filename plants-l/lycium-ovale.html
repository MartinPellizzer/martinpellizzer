Lycium ovale

Lycium ovale, commonly known as the Chinese boxthorn, is a shrub native to China and other parts of Asia.

It features small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces red berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. Lycium ovale is characterized by its spiny stems and dark green, oval-shaped leaves.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and has been used in traditional medicine.
