Lupinus succulentus

Lupinus succulentus, commonly known as the succulent lupin, is a perennial plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and vibrant purple or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has adapted to harsh environments with its succulent leaves, which store water and reduce transpiration. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access deep soil moisture.

Lupinus succulentus plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and supporting local pollinators in its native habitat.
