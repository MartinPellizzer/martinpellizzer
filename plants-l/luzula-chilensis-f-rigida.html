Luzula chilensis f. rigida

Luzula chilensis f. rigida, commonly known as the rigid sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America, particularly Chile.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its stiff, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It typically grows in moist, temperate environments such as wetlands and forest floors.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and horticultural applications.
