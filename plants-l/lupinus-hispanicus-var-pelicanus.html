Lupinus hispanicus var. pelicanus

Lupinus hispanicus var. pelicanus, commonly known as the Pelican lupine, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has nitrogen-fixing abilities through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules.

This variety is often found in rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
