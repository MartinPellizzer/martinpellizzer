Leptopogon chrysostachyus

Leptopogon chrysostachyus, commonly known as the golden-stemmed leekgrass, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces panicles of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Leptopogon chrysostachyus is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
