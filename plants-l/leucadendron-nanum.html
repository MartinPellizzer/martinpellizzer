Leucadendron nanum

Leucadendron nanum, commonly known as the silver coin bush, is a small shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silvery-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, adding a subtle touch of color to its overall look. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its compact size and ornamental leaves make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
