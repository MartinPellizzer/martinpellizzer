Linociera mildbraedii

Linociera mildbraedii, commonly known as the KwaZulu-Natal wild pear, is a shrub native to the eastern regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white flowers that are typically clustered in the axils of the leaves. Its fruits are small, round pomes that ripen to a yellowish color, and they are edible but not widely cultivated.

Linociera mildbraedii is adapted to grow in sandy or loamy soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas.
