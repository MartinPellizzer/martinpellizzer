Lasiagrostis variegata

Lasiagrostis variegata, commonly known as the variegated grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that exhibit a striking variegation of green and white or yellow. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear delicate, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and low maintenance requirements.
