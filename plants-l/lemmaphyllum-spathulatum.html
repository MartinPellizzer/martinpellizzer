Lemmaphyllum spathulatum

Lemmaphyllum spathulatum, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowhead, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green, with a distinctive arrowhead shape. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and wetland habitats.
