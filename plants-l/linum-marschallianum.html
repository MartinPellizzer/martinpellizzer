Linum marschallianum

Linum marschallianum, commonly known as the marshmallow flax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and clusters of small, pale blue or white flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Linum marschallianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
