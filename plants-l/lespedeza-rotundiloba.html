Lespedeza rotundiloba

Lespedeza rotundiloba, commonly known as roundleaf lespedeza, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks. Lespedeza rotundiloba is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

It blooms in late spring to early summer, producing pink to purple flowers that attract pollinators.
