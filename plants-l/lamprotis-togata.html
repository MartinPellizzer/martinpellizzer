Lamprotis togata

Lamprotis togata, commonly known as the Togata orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that often feature vibrant colors such as red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, tropical environments. Its flowers are borne on lateral spikes and can last for several weeks.

The orchid has a relatively short blooming period, making it a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
