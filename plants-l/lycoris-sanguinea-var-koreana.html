Lycoris sanguinea var. koreana

Lycoris sanguinea var. koreana, commonly known as the Korean red lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Korea and parts of eastern Asia.

It features striking crimson flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from August to September. The plant has strap-like, narrow leaves that emerge from a bulbous root system. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant color and unique flowering habit.
