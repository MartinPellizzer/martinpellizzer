Lamproconus aurantiacus

Lamproconus aurantiacus, commonly known as the golden star orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bright orange flowers with a star-shaped pattern. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are elongated and often covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

The flowers bloom in clusters and are pollinated by specific insects, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
