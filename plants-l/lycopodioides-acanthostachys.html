Lycopodioides acanthostachys

Lycopodioides acanthostachys, commonly known as the spike moss, is a type of primitive vascular plant belonging to the order Lycopodiales.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and a spore-bearing structure called a strobilus. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or rocky crevices. Its root system is specialized for anchoring in soil and absorbing nutrients.

Lycopodioides acanthostachys plays an important ecological role in maintaining soil stability and providing habitat for microorganisms.
