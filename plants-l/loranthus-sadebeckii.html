Loranthus sadebeckii

Loranthus sadebeckii, commonly known as the Sadebeck's mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily in the genus Eucalyptus.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while also producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using light reflected from the host. The plant produces small, white or pale green berries that are consumed by birds, which then disperse the seeds. It is native to Australia and is often found in coastal regions where its host trees thrive.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can cause significant stress to its host if left unchecked.
