Lobelia giberroa var. mionandra

Lobelia giberroa var. mionandra, commonly known as the mountain lobelia, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of South America.

It features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in cloud forests or mountainous regions. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with appropriate conditions.
