Lepidium papillosum var. drummondii

Lepidium papillosum var. drummondii, commonly known as Drummond's cress, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard plants. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has small, clustered white or yellow flowers. Its leaves are pinnately divided, and the fruits are small, dry pods containing several seeds.

The species is characterized by its distinctive papillae on the seed surface, which aid in seed dispersal.
