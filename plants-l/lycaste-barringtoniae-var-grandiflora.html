Lycaste barringtoniae var. grandiflora

Lycaste barringtoniae var. grandiflora, commonly known as the Barrington's lycaste, is a hybrid orchid species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 centimeters in diameter, typically in shades of white and pink. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, often arranged in a fan shape, and it produces fragrant blooms during the warmer months.

This variety is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation under proper conditions.
