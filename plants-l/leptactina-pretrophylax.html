Leptactina pretrophylax

Leptactina pretrophylax, commonly known as the silverleaf orchid, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the subtropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, pale green leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of tiny, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring, typically in shades of white and pale yellow. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and requires careful cultivation due to its specific humidity and temperature needs.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is protected in several conservation programs.
