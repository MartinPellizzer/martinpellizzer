Leptobalanus stevensii

Leptobalanus stevensii, commonly known as the Stevens' alder, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its small, cone-like fruits and its ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. It plays an important role in stabilizing streambanks and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Leptobalanus stevensii is often found in riparian zones and is adapted to the cool, humid climate of its native range.
