Leptinella calcarea

Leptinella calcarea, commonly known as the limestone cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to rocky and alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and clusters of white or pinkish flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and is often found in mountainous areas with harsh climatic conditions. Its flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Leptinella calcarea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
