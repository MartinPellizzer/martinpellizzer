Leucanthemum atratum subsp. corsicum

Leucanthemum atratum subsp.

corsicum, commonly known as Corsican ragwort, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its basal rosette of dark green, hairy leaves and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. It has a taproot system and produces a milky sap that is characteristic of the Asteraceae family.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
