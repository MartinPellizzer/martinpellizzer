Launaea integrifolia

Launaea integrifolia, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant has a rosette of basal leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. Its stems are upright and can grow up to 60 cm tall, supporting the inflorescence.

Launaea integrifolia is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
