Lucuma stahliana

Lucuma stahliana, commonly known as the golden fruit tree, is a tropical plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces a unique, sweet, and nutty fruit with a creamy texture when ripe. Its leaves are broad and glossy, often used in traditional medicine by local communities.

Lucuma stahliana is valued not only for its culinary uses but also for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
