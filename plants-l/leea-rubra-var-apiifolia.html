Leea rubra var. apiifolia

Leea rubra var. apiifolia, commonly known as the white-leaved leea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Leeaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often white or pale green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically followed by small, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value is appreciated in horticulture due to its unique foliage.
