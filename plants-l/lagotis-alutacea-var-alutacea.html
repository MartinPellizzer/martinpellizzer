Lagotis alutacea var. alutacea

Lagotis alutacea var. alutacea, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces seeds in small, dry fruits.

This species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ornamental appeal in wetland gardens.
