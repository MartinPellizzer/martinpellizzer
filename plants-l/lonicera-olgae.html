Lonicera olgae

Lonicera olgae, commonly known as the creeping honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features slender, arching stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense ground cover. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in early spring. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a glossy green appearance with serrated edges.

Lonicera olgae is valued for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
