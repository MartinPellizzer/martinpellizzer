Limonium scoparium var. meyeri

Limonium scoparium var. meyeri, commonly known as Meyer's sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden landscapes and coastal plantings.
