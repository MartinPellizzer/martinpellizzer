Lolium perenne var. viviparum

Lolium perenne var. viviparum, commonly known as the viviparous perennial ryegrass, is a variant of the perennial ryegrass species.

It is characterized by its ability to produce live seeds, a trait known as viviparity, which allows for rapid germination and establishment. This plant typically grows in temperate climates and is often found in grasslands and pastures. Its botanical features include narrow, grass-like leaves and inflorescences that produce small, wind-dispersed seeds.

The viviparous trait makes it an interesting subject for studies in plant reproduction and adaptation.
