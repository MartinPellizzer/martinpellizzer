Lonicera pyrenaica subsp. majoricensis

Lonicera pyrenaica subsp.

majoricensis, commonly known as the Majorica honeysuckle, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and clusters of yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or scrubland habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
