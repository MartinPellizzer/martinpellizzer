Litsea chinpingensis

Litsea chinpingensis, commonly known as the Guangxi litsea, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to southern China.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, ovate leaves that are usually dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its flowers are small and yellowish-green, typically appearing in clusters, and it produces small, round berries that are edible.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
