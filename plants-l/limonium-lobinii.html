Limonium lobinii

Limonium lobinii, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flower heads, which are surrounded by narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in saline conditions. Its flowers bloom in summer and are often used in ornamental gardening.

Limonium lobinii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
