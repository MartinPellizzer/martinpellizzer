Lycopodium brongniartii

Lycopodium brongniartii, commonly known as the African clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in Africa. The plant has needle-like leaves arranged in whorls around the stem, and it produces cone-like structures that contain spores. Lycopodium brongniartii is characterized by its ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important component of certain ecosystems, contributing to soil stability and biodiversity.
