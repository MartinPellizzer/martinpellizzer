Lonchocarpus densiflorus var. moritzianus

Lonchocarpus densiflorus var. moritzianus, commonly known as the Moritz's denseflower legume, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant produces distinctive, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
