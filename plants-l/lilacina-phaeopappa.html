Lilacina phaeopappa

Lilacina phaeopappa, commonly known as the purple-flowered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale purple to deep violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a valuable species for ecological studies and horticultural diversity.
