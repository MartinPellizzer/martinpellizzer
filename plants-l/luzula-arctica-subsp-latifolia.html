Luzula arctica subsp. latifolia

Luzula arctica subsp.

latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved arctic woodrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Arctic and alpine regions. It belongs to the family Poaceae, commonly known as the grass family, and is characterized by its dense, low-growing clusters of narrow, grass-like leaves.

Its ability to survive in extreme cold makes it an important component of Arctic ecosystems.
