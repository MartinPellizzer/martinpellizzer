Lepisorus distans

Lepisorus distans, commonly known as the distant fern, is a species of polypod fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce small, clustered sporangia on the undersides of its leaves.

Lepisorus distans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded gardens.
