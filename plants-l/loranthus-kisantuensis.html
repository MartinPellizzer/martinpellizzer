Loranthus kisantuensis

Loranthus kisantuensis, commonly known as the Kisantu mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches itself to the branches of its host trees.

It belongs to the Loranthaceae family and is native to Central and Eastern Africa. The plant has small, leathery leaves and produces white or yellowish flowers that are often visited by insects for pollination. Its berries are typically red or orange and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This species plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat by influencing host tree growth and providing food for wildlife.
