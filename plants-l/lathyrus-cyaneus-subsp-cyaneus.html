Lathyrus cyaneus subsp. cyaneus

Lathyrus cyaneus subsp.

cyaneus, commonly known as the blue flax or blue bean, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant features slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves with stipules. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a forage plant and its ornamental value in gardens.
