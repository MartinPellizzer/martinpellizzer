Lobelia nelsonii var. fragilis

Lobelia nelsonii var. fragilis, commonly known as the Nelson's lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant features slender stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
