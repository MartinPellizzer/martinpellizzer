Lepidium iberideum

Lepidium iberideum, commonly known as the Iberian cress, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous species. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers are arranged in clusters, and it produces small, rounded seeds.

Lepidium iberideum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
