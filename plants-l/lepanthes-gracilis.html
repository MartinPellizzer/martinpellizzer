Lepanthes gracilis

Lepanthes gracilis, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. Lepanthes gracilis is part of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate floral structure.

Due to its beauty and rarity, it is often sought after by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
