Loricaria complanata var. tatamensis

Loricaria complanata var. tatamensis, commonly known as the Tatamensis water lily, is a species of aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves that provide habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom above the water surface, contributing to its ornamental value. Its roots anchor in the substrate, helping to stabilize the soil and prevent erosion.

This variety is often cultivated in ponds and water gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
