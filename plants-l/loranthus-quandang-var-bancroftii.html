Loranthus quandang var. bancroftii

Loranthus quandang var. bancroftii, commonly known as the Bancroft's mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that attaches itself to the branches of its host trees.

It belongs to the family Loranthaceae and is characterized by its hemiparasitic nature, drawing water and nutrients from its host while performing some photosynthesis. The plant typically has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often white or greenish in color. It is native to parts of Africa and is often found in forested areas where its host trees thrive.

The berries it produces are usually red or yellow and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.
