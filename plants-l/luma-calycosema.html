Luma calycosema

Luma calycosema, commonly known as the coral tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, featuring a straight trunk and a broad, rounded crown. The plant is characterized by its showy, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the dry season. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical in shape, with a glossy surface.

The coral tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
