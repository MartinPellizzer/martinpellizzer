Lysimachia violascens var. xerophila

Lysimachia violascens var. xerophila, commonly known as the desert chickweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It features small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring, often in clusters at the ends of its stems. The plant has succulent-like leaves that are thick and fleshy, adapted to conserve water in dry environments. Its fibrous root system helps it anchor in loose, sandy soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging, drought-prone landscapes.
