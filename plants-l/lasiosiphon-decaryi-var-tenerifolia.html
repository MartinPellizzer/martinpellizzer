Lasiosiphon decaryi var. tenerifolia

Lasiosiphon decaryi var. tenerifolia, commonly known as the Canary Island morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a herbaceous vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas with well-drained soil. Its flowers open in the morning and close in the late afternoon, hence its common name.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
