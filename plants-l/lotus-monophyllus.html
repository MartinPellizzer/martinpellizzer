Lotus monophyllus

Lotus monophyllus, commonly known as the sacred lotus, is a water-lily species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom on long, buoyant stalks above the water surface. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water, with a waxy surface that repels water. Its flowers are typically pink or white, with a distinctive cup-like shape and numerous stamens.

Lotus monophyllus is known for its symbolic significance in various cultures and its use in traditional medicine and cuisine.
