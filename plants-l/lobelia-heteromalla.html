Lobelia heteromalla

Lobelia heteromalla, commonly known as the slender blue lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, tubular blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces seeds in elongated capsules.

Lobelia heteromalla is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
