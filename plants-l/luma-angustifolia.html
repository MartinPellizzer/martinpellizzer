Luma angustifolia

Luma angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf luma, is a flowering plant native to the southern Andes of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in humid, subtropical climates. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. It is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, thriving in well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
