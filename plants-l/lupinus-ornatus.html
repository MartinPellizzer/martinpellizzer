Lupinus ornatus

Lupinus ornatus, commonly known as the California lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of showy purple or blue flowers. The plant has compound leaves composed of several small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Lupinus ornatus plays an important role in ecological systems by fixing nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plant life.
