Lacinaria chlorolepis

Lacinaria chlorolepis, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange tubular flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a hairy surface, and its stems are often branched and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in warmer climates.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
