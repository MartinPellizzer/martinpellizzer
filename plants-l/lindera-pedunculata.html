Lindera pedunculata

Lindera pedunculata, commonly known as sweet pepper bush, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant features opposite, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green, often with a slight aromatic scent. Its small, yellowish-green flowers appear in clusters and are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits.

Lindera pedunculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade to full sun.
