Loranthus microphyllus

Loranthus microphyllus, commonly known as the small-leaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to host trees through its haustoria, drawing water and nutrients from the host.

It is native to eastern Australia and typically grows on eucalyptus and other native trees. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces small, white or pale green flowers followed by fleshy, red berries. It plays a role in the ecosystem by providing food for birds and other animals.

Its parasitic nature and unique adaptations make it an interesting subject in botanical studies.
