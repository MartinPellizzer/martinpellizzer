Linum rupestre

Linum rupestre, commonly known as the rock flax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue or violet flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is often found growing in rocky or gravelly soils. It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Linum rupestre is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
