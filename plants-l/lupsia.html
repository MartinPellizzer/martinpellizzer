Lupsia

Lupsia, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, violet-colored petals and a distinctive cone-shaped center. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom from mid-summer to early fall.

Lupsia is known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
