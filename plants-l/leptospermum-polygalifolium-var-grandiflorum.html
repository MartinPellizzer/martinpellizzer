Leptospermum polygalifolium var. grandiflorum

Leptospermum polygalifolium var. grandiflorum, commonly known as the broad-leaved tea tree, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage composed of narrow, leathery leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant white or pink flowers in clusters, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

The botanical features of this plant make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
