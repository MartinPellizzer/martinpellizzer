Litsea exsudens

Litsea exsudens, commonly known as the fragrant lauraceous tree, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves contain essential oils, which contribute to its distinctive scent and various medicinal properties.

Litsea exsudens is often found in moist, shaded environments in tropical forests.
