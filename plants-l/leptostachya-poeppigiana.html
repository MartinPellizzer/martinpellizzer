Leptostachya poeppigiana

Leptostachya poeppigiana, commonly known as the Mexican grass or velvet grass, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically borne in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Leptostachya poeppigiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
