Leymus secalinus var. qinghaicus

Leymus secalinus var. qinghaicus, commonly known as the Qinghai wild rye, is a perennial grass native to the Qinghai region of China.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky areas, adapting to harsh environmental conditions. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous florets.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for grazing animals.
