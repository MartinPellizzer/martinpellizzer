Lindsaea guianensis

Lindsaea guianensis, commonly known as the Guianan lace fern, is a member of the family Polypodiaceae and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a epiphytic fern, meaning it grows on the surface of other plants without deriving nutrients from them. The plant features delicate, lace-like fronds with a distinctive, intricate pattern of veins. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the canopy of tropical rainforests.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular specimen in botanical gardens and among fern enthusiasts.
