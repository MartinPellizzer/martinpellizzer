Linosyris rupestris

Linosyris rupestris, commonly known as the rock larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in sunny, well-drained environments. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are erect and branching.

Linosyris rupestris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
