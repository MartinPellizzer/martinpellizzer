Lapathum sanguineum

Lapathum sanguineum, commonly known as the bloodroot, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb with a distinctive red-orange flower that blooms in early spring. The plant has a single, sturdy stem that supports its showy, five-petaled flowers. Its leaves are basal, forming a rosette and are typically heart-shaped with a hairy texture.

Lapathum sanguineum is often found in woodland areas and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
