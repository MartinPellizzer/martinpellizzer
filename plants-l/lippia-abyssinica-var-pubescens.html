Lippia abyssinica var. pubescens

Lippia abyssinica var. pubescens, commonly known as Ethiopian basil or wild basil, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which give it its specific variety name "pubescens.". It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects.
