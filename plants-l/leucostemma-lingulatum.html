Leucostemma lingulatum

Leucostemma lingulatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf leucostemma, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as stream banks and meadows. The plant features slender, ascending stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in dense, terminal inflorescences.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
