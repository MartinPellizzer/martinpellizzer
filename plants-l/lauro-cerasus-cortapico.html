Lauro-cerasus cortapico

Lauro-cerasus cortapico, commonly known as the cherry laurel, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Prunus genus and is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and is often used as a hedge or screen due to its dense growth habit.
