Luzula lactea f. velutina

Luzula lactea f. velutina, commonly known as the velvet wood sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered in a fine, silky pubescence, giving it a velvety texture. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in spike-like inflorescences, and it plays a role in supporting local wildlife and soil stabilization.
