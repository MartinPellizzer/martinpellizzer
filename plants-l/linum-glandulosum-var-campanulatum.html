Linum glandulosum var. campanulatum

Linum glandulosum var. campanulatum, commonly known as the campanulate flax, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and produces showy, bell-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which are typical of the Linum genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which extends from spring to early autumn.
