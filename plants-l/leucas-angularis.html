Leucas angularis

Leucas angularis, commonly known as the angular leucas, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features upright, branching stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are usually lance-shaped with serrated edges. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
