Lippia pickelii

Lippia pickelii, commonly known as Mexican blue sage, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and clusters of small, blue or violet flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping and drought-tolerant gardens. Its essential oils, derived from the leaves, are used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine for their calming properties.

Lippia pickelii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
