Litsea casearioides

Litsea casearioides, commonly known as the Malayan litsea, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and avocado trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by spherical berries that are yellow when mature.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
