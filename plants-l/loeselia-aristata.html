Loeselia aristata

Loeselia aristata, commonly known as the mountain bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high elevations of South America.

It features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking blue to violet bell-shaped flowers. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions. Its botanical structure is adapted to withstand harsh mountain conditions, including strong winds and low temperatures.

Loeselia aristata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
