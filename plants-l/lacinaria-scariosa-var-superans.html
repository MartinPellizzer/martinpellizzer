Lacinaria scariosa var. superans

Lacinaria scariosa var. superans, commonly known as the scarious lacinaria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a whitish, waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in loose inflorescences, typically blooming during the warmer months. Its leaves and stems are covered in a distinctive, papery layer that helps reduce water loss in arid environments.

This plant is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to thrive in semi-arid to dry conditions.
