Linum squamatum

Linum squamatum, commonly known as the woolly flax or squamose flax, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Linaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green foliage. The plant produces showy blue or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Linum squamatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
