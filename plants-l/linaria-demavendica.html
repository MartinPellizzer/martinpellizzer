Linaria demavendica

Linaria demavendica, commonly known as the Demavend sandflower, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its slender stems and small, lance-shaped leaves are well-suited for conserving water.

Linaria demavendica is also noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
