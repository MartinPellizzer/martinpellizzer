Ligustrum ciliatum var. heterophyllum

Ligustrum ciliatum var. heterophyllum, commonly known as the rough-leaf privet, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often used in ornamental landscaping.

Its adaptability makes it a common species in tropical and subtropical regions.
