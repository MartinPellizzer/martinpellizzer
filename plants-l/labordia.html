Labordia

Labordia, commonly known as the African orchid, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

These plants are native to southern Africa and are characterized by their showy, often fragrant flowers. Labordia species typically feature tall, erect stems with clustered blooms that can range in color from white to pink and purple. They are often cultivated for their ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses due to their striking appearance.

However, they require specific growing conditions, including well-drained soil and bright, indirect light.
