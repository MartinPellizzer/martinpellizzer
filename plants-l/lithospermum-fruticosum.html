Lithospermum fruticosum

Lithospermum fruticosum, commonly known as red sandalwood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branched stems covered in fine hairs and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color. The plant's leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy surface. Its fruits are small, capsule-like structures that release tiny seeds when mature.

Lithospermum fruticosum is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
