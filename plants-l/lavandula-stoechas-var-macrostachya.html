Lavandula stoechas var. macrostachya

Lavandula stoechas var. macrostachya, commonly known as the Spanish lavender or the purple lavender, is a perennial shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and spikes of violet or purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and Mediterranean climates. Its flowers are used in perfumery and herbal remedies due to their essential oil content.

This variety is distinguished by its larger, more showy flower spikes compared to other lavender species.
