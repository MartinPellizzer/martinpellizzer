Loranthus jackianus

Loranthus jackianus, commonly known as the Jack's mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees.

It belongs to the family Loranthaceae and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant derives some of its nutrients from the host through specialized structures called haustoria. It is typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, often growing in dense forests.

Its berries, which are usually red or yellow, are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.
