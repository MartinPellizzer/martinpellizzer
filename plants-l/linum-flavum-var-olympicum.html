Linum flavum var. olympicum

Linum flavum var. olympicum, commonly known as the Olympic yellow flax, is a variant of the flax species native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers and slender, branched stems. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is lance-shaped with a smooth texture, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its bright, eye-catching blooms.
