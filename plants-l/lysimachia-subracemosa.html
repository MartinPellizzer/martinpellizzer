Lysimachia subracemosa

Lysimachia subracemosa, commonly known as the slender yellow loosestrife, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist habitats such as woodlands, stream banks, and wet meadows. The plant features opposite, linear to lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring to early summer. Its stems are often prostrate or ascending, and it can form dense mats in suitable conditions.

Lysimachia subracemosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting various pollinators.
