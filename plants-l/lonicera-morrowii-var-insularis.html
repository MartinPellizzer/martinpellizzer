Lonicera morrowii var. insularis

Lonicera morrowii var. insularis, commonly known as the Japanese honeysuckle, is a vigorous, climbing vine native to East Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value, though it can become invasive in certain regions.

The berries it produces are small, red, and edible, attracting birds and other wildlife.
