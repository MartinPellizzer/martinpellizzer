Ligustrum lucidum var. tricolor

Ligustrum lucidum var. tricolor, commonly known as the variegated privet, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that exhibit a striking tricolor pattern of green, white, and gold. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by dark blue berries in the fall. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.

The variegation in its foliage makes it a popular choice for hedges and ornamental gardening.
