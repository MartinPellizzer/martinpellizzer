Ligusticum indicum

Ligusticum indicum, commonly known as Indian cumin or wild cumin, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayas and parts of South Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the family. Its aromatic seeds are used in traditional medicine and culinary practices.

Ligusticum indicum thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.
