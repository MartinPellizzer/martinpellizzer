Lespedeza densiflora

Lespedeza densiflora, commonly known as silverleaf lespedeza, is a perennial flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage and small, pea-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in open woodlands and disturbed soils. It has fibrous roots that help prevent soil erosion, making it a valuable plant in ecological restoration.

Lespedeza densiflora is also known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to tolerate drought conditions.
