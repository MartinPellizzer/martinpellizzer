Lychnis macrosperma f. albida

Lychnis macrosperma f. albida, commonly known as the white-flowered fireweed, is a variant of the common fireweed species.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to northern regions of Europe and Asia. The plant features showy, clustered pink or white flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in disturbed soils.

It is often found in meadows, roadsides, and other open habitats.
