Lonchocarpus utilis

Lonchocarpus utilis, commonly known as the climbing cowpea, is a tropical legume native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with long, twining stems that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers followed by pods containing several elliptical seeds. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with 5 to 7 pairs of leaflets, and it has a fibrous root system.

The plant is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability and has been used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop.
