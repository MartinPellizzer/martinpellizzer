Lathyrus humilis

Lathyrus humilis, commonly known as the creeping vetch, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, pea-like flowers in shades of pink or purple. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along streambanks.

Its seeds are an important food source for various bird species.
