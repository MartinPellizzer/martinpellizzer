Lagrezia madagascariensis

Lagrezia madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, fragrant blooms. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its flowers are often brightly colored, with intricate patterns that attract specific pollinators.

Due to its limited habitat and rarity, it is considered a valuable and endangered species in conservation efforts.
