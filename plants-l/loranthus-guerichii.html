Loranthus guerichii

Loranthus guerichii, commonly known as the Guerich's mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily in the genus Ficus.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while still performing some photosynthesis using its scale-like leaves. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers and red berries that are dispersed by birds. It is native to parts of Africa and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for studying plant parasitism and ecological interactions.
