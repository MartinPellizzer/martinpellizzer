Lycopodium capellae

Lycopodium capellae, commonly known as the Capella clubmoss, is a small evergreen plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and produces spore-bearing cones called strobili. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are linear and spirally arranged, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Lycopodium capellae plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
