Leea pubescens

Leea pubescens, commonly known as the hairy leea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Leeaceae and is characterized by its small, simple leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically borne on short pedicels. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Leea pubescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and floral display.
