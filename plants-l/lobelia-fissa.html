Lobelia fissa

Lobelia fissa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems with opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and stream banks.

Lobelia fissa is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
