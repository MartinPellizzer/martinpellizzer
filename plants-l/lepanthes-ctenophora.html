Lepanthes ctenophora

Lepanthes ctenophora, commonly known as the feather-leaf orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments within tropical forests. Its roots are often mycorrhizal, forming symbiotic relationships with fungi to enhance nutrient absorption.

Lepanthes ctenophora is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
