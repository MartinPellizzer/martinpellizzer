Lycium paucifolium

Lycium paucifolium, commonly known as the sparse-leaved boxthorn, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in pairs. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, followed by red berries that are edible for humans and animals. Lycium paucifolium is adapted to survive in harsh conditions with minimal water, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
