Limonium × neumanii

Limonium × neumanii, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its striking, spiky flower heads that range in color from white to blue and purple. The plant thrives in saline and well-drained soils, making it well-suited for coastal environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating.

Limonium × neumanii is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate harsh maritime conditions.
