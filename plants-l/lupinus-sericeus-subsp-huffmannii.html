Lupinus sericeus subsp. huffmannii

Lupinus sericeus subsp.

huffmannii, commonly known as the silver lupine, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and tall, sparsely branched stems that bear clusters of blue to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are compound, with small, hairy leaflets that help reduce water loss. Lupinus sericeus subsp.

huffmannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in stabilizing soils in its native habitat.
