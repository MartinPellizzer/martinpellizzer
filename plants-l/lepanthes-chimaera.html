Lepanthes chimaera

Lepanthes chimaera, commonly known as the Chimaera orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually small, with a distinctive coloration that ranges from white to pale yellow.

Lepanthes chimaera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
