Lobelia mutisiana

Lobelia mutisiana, commonly known as the Andean lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, herbaceous stems and clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as cloud forests and rocky slopes. Lobelia mutisiana belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.

Its flowers attract pollinators like bees and hummingbirds, contributing to its ecological significance.
