Lugaion ferox

Lugaion ferox, commonly known as the Crimson Viper, is a rare and striking plant native to the misty highlands of the Andes.

It features vibrant red and orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. The plant has a thick, waxy leaf structure that helps it retain moisture in its arid environment. Its roots are deep and fibrous, allowing it to access water from underground sources.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is considered a protected species in its native habitat.
