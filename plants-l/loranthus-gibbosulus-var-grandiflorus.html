Loranthus gibbosulus var. grandiflorus

Loranthus gibbosulus var. grandiflorus, commonly known as the large-flowered mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to host trees through its haustoria.

It belongs to the family Loranthaceae and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are often consumed by birds, aiding in seed dispersal. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in secondary forests and disturbed habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of ecological systems where it interacts with both host trees and wildlife.
