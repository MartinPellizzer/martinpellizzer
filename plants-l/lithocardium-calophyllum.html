Lithocardium calophyllum

Lithocardium calophyllum, commonly known as the golden rock fig, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically consumed fresh or used in local cuisines. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing on rocky or limestone soils.

Lithocardium calophyllum is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its ecological and cultural significance in its native regions.
