Ludwigia octovalvis subsp. macropoda

Ludwigia octovalvis subsp.

macropoda, commonly known as the red Ludwigia, is a flowering plant native to South America. It is characterized by its striking red or orange bracts that surround its small white flowers.

The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, and it often forms dense clusters.

It is popular in aquascaping due to its vibrant color and ease of cultivation.
