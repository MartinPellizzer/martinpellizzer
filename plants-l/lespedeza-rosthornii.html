Lespedeza rosthornii

Lespedeza rosthornii, commonly known as the slender lespedeza, is a perennial flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or red, appearing in clusters during the summer months. Its trifoliate leaves are compound, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

Lespedeza rosthornii is often found in dry, open habitats such as woodland edges and sandy soils.
