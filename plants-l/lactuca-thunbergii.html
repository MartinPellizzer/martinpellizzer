Lactuca thunbergii

Lactuca thunbergii, commonly known as Japanese lettuce or mountain lettuce, is a flowering plant in the dandelion family (Compositae).

It is characterized by its upright stem, alternate leaves that are usually lance-shaped, and small yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant is native to East Asia and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. It is a herbaceous perennial that can grow up to 1 meter in height, with a fibrous root system.

Lactuca thunbergii is sometimes cultivated for its edible leaves, which have a mild, slightly bitter taste.
