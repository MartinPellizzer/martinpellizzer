Loeselia glandulosa var. ramosissima

Loeselia glandulosa var. ramosissima, commonly known as the glandular loeselia, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and hairy, glandular leaves that give it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its stems are often branched and can develop multiple shoots, contributing to its varietal name "ramosissima," meaning highly branched.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphological features and limited distribution.
