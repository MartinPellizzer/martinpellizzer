Leptospermum fastigiatum

Leptospermum fastigiatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tea tree, is a shrub native to Tasmania.

It belongs to the myrtle family (Myrtaceae) and is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and small, urn-shaped flowers. The plant produces a fragrant, honey-scented nectar that attracts a variety of pollinators, including bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and montane regions.

The species is also valued for its aromatic oils and has been used in traditional and modern herbal applications.
