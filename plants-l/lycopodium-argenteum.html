Lycopodium argenteum

Lycopodium argenteum, commonly known as the silver clubmoss, is a small evergreen shrub native to the temperate regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage and small, clustered spore cones. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or rocky outcrops. Its leaves are narrow and scale-like, often covered in a silvery or whitish coating, giving it its common name.

Lycopodium argenteum plays a role in maintaining soil stability and provides habitat for various small invertebrates.
