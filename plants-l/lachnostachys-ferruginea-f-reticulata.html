Lachnostachys ferruginea f. reticulata

Lachnostachys ferruginea f. reticulata, commonly known as the reticulated ironweed, is a variant of the ironweed species native to certain regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive reticulated leaf patterns, which give it its botanical name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a reddish or rust-colored hue, contributing to its ornamental value.

It produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
