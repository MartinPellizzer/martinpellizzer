Lilium odorum

Lilium odorum, commonly known as the sweet william lily, is a species of bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of trumpet-shaped blossoms. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Lilium odorum is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its historical use in perfumery and medicine.
