Lactuca acanthifolia f. amorgina

Lactuca acanthifolia f. amorgina, commonly known as the Amorgian prickly lettuce, is a rare botanical variant of the prickly lettuce species.

It is characterized by its robust, spiny leaves and is native to the island of Amorgos in the Aegean Sea. The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments, showcasing adaptations to its harsh habitat. Its leaves are often deeply lobed and covered in fine, stiff hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
