Linum alpinum var. ruscinonense

Linum alpinum var. ruscinonense, commonly known as the Alpine flax or Ruscinon flax, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and delicate blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are linear and covered with fine hairs, while its seeds are small and oval-shaped.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in textile production.
