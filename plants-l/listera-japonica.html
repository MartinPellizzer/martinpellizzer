Listera japonica

Listera japonica, commonly known as the Japanese basketwort, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a member of the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and woodlands. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Listera japonica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
