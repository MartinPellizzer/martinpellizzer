Lilium matangense

Lilium matangense, commonly known as the Matangense lily, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant belongs to the Liliaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its aesthetic appeal.

The flowers are often fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
