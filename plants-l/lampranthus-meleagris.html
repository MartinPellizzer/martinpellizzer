Lampranthus meleagris

Lampranthus meleagris, commonly known as the spotted lampranthus, is a member of the Aizoaceae family, native to South Africa.

It is a succulent shrub characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems and vibrant, showy flowers that range from red to orange in color. The plant produces small, bead-like fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
