Linaria gobantesiana

Linaria gobantesiana, commonly known as the desert snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant yellow or orange flowers. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces elongated seed pods that split open to release its seeds. It is adapted to survive in dry, sandy soils and is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.

Linaria gobantesiana is an important species for pollinators and contributes to the ecological diversity of its native range.
