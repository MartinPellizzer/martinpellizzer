Lepanthes caluffii

Lepanthes caluffii, commonly known as the Caluffi's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often colorful flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its leaves are usually fleshy and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

The flowers bloom in spring and are notable for their intricate structure and vibrant hues.
