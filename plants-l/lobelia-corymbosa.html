Lobelia corymbosa

Lobelia corymbosa, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet, featuring slender stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Lobelia corymbosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
