Loliolum

Loliolum, commonly known as the lesser calamity, is a small, herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and along stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems with small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are tiny, greenish-white blooms that appear in clusters, and it produces small, rounded fruits containing several seeds.

Loliolum is often considered a weed but can serve as a food source for certain insects and small animals.
