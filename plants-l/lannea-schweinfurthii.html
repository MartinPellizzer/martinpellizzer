Lannea schweinfurthii

Lannea schweinfurthii, commonly known as the African bristlecone, is a deciduous tree native to eastern and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fissured bark and large, leathery leaves that are arranged in a distinctive spiral pattern. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, spiny fruits containing edible seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.

Lannea schweinfurthii plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
