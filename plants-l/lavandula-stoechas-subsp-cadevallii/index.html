Lavandula Stoechas Subsp. Cadevallii

Lavandula stoechas subsp. cadevallii is a lesser-known member of the lavender family native to the Iberian Peninsula. This subspecies is distinguished by its striking violet-blue flower spikes and unique, twisted leaves. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in sunny, Mediterranean climates. Despite its beauty, it remains underappreciated compared to more common lavender varieties. Its ornamental value and hardy nature make it a valuable addition to gardens and landscapes.
