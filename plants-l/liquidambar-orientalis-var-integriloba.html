Liquidambar orientalis var. integriloba

Liquidambar orientalis var. integriloba, commonly known as the Chinese sweetgum, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and palmate leaves that turn vibrant yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, spiky samaras that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular ornamental species.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically and aesthetically valuable in landscaping.
