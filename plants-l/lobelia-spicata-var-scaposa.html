Lobelia spicata var. scaposa

Lobelia spicata var. scaposa, commonly known as the scapose lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features slender, erect stems that bear clusters of small, tubular blue or violet flowers. The leaves are typically alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
