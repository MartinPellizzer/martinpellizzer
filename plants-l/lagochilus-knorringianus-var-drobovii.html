Lagochilus knorringianus var. drobovii

Lagochilus knorringianus var. drobovii, commonly known as the Drobovii heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, violet-colored flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a woolly texture, while its roots are used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its ecological role in its native habitat.
