Loranthus butayei

Loranthus butayei, commonly known as the Siberian mistletoe, is a parasitic plant belonging to the Loranthaceae family.

It grows on various host trees, primarily in the temperate regions of Asia. The plant has small, opposite leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. Its berries are red or orange and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

Loranthus butayei is notable for its ecological role in supporting bird populations through its fruit.
