Lomaria lanceolata

Lomaria lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf lomariella, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, white or pale blue flowers bloom in clusters during the spring and early summer.

Lomaria lanceolata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
