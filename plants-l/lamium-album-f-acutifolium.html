Lamium album f. acutifolium

Lamium album f. acutifolium, commonly known as white dead-nettle with acute leaves, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features opposite, lanceolate leaves that are often hairy and have a distinct serrated margin. The plant produces small, white, tubular flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides.

Its leaves and flowers are used in traditional herbal medicine for their mild sedative and antispasmodic properties.
