Lasiococca symphyllifolia

Lasiococca symphyllifolia, commonly known as the silkyleaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees.

It has small, leathery leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its berries are typically red or yellow and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

It is native to parts of southern Africa and is often found in forested areas.
