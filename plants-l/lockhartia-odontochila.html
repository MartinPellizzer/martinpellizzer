Lockhartia odontochila

Lockhartia odontochila, commonly known as the toothed-leaf orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often with a distinctive yellow or greenish throat. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a serrated edge, contributing to its common name.

This orchid thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
