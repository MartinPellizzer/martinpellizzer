Lockhartia latilabris

Lockhartia latilabris, commonly known as the broad-lipped orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elegant, pendulous flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant features strap-like leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern, and its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the humid environment.

Lockhartia latilabris thrives in shaded, moist conditions and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking floral display.
