Lobelia graminea var. phyllostachya

Lobelia graminea var. phyllostachya, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lobelia, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
