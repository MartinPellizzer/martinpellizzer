Loranthus undulatus var. sagittifolius

Loranthus undulatus var. sagittifolius, commonly known as the arrowleaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees.

It belongs to the family Loranthaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often arrow-shaped. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its berries are usually red or orange, containing seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This species is found in various regions of Southeast Asia, where it plays a role in the ecological interactions of its host trees.
