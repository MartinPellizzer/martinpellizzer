Lepanthes longiloba

Lepanthes longiloba, commonly known as the long-lipped orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers that typically feature a white or pale pink coloration with intricate patterns. The plant grows as an epiphyte, often found on trees in humid tropical forests. Its leaves are usually oval and arranged in a rosette, while its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture from the air.

Lepanthes longiloba is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
