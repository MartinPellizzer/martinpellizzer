Lathyrus stipulaceus

Lathyrus stipulaceus, commonly known as the stipule pea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces clusters of pea-like flowers in shades of pink to purple. The plant has trifoliate leaves with stipules, which are small, leaf-like structures at the base of the leaf stalks. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Lathyrus stipulaceus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
