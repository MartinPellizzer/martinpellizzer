Libanotis ugoensis var. japonica

Libanotis ugoensis var. japonica, commonly known as the Japanese bellflower, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lanceolate leaves that are often hairy, and it prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils. It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in temperate climates.

The species is part of the Campanulaceae family, which includes other familiar genera such as Bellis and Lobelia.
