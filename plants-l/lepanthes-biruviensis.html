Lepanthes biruviensis

Lepanthes biruviensis, commonly known as the Blue Violet Orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers, which are typically small and highly fragrant. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in a fine, waxy coating.

Lepanthes biruviensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
