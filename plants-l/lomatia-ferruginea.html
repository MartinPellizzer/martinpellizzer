Lomatia ferruginea

Lomatia ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty lomatia, is a shrub native to southwestern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, glandular-hairy leaves that are often reddish-brown in color. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in winter and early spring. Its fruits are woody follicles that contain several seeds.

Lomatia ferruginea is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and open woodlands.
