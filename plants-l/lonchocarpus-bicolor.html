Lonchocarpus bicolor

Lonchocarpus bicolor, commonly known as the yellow velvet tamarind, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often hairy and have a distinctive yellowish-green color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by pod-like fruits containing flat, dark brown seeds. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some indigenous cultures.

Botanically, it belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its nitrogen-fixing abilities.
