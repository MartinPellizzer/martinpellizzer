Lonicera xylosteum var. macrocalyx

Lonicera xylosteum var. macrocalyx, commonly known as the large-flowered honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features opposite, simple, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are usually yellow or orange-red, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its berries are red or orange and contain seeds, which are dispersed by birds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
