Lithospermum chamaecistus

Lithospermum chamaecistus, commonly known as the creeping rock pepper, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and adapted to arid conditions. The plant produces bright red or orange flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its roots contain compounds with potential medicinal properties, including antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
