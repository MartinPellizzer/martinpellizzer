Lathyrus latidentatus

Lathyrus latidentatus, commonly known as the broad-leaved peavine, is a flowering plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It is a climbing legume characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, woody pods that contain several elliptical seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
