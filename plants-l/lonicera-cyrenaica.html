Lonicera cyrenaica

Lonicera cyrenaica, commonly known as the Cape honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant produces tubular, fragrant flowers that are usually yellow or orange in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Lonicera cyrenaica is well adapted to dry climates and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
