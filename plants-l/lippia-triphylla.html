Lippia triphylla

Lippia triphylla, commonly known as Mexican mint or wild mint, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Lippia triphylla is known for its aromatic leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.

It has a compact growth habit and is often found in clusters, making it a distinctive and recognizable species in its native range.
