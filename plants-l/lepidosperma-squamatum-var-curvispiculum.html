Lepidosperma squamatum var. curvispiculum

Lepidosperma squamatum var. curvispiculum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedgeland sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to Australia.

It typically grows in wetland or swampy areas, where it thrives in waterlogged soils. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often slightly curved, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowers are typically greenish or brownish, and it is often found in coastal or inland wetlands.

This variety is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology and is an important component of the local flora in its habitat.
