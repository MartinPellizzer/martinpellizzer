Linum austriacum var. mauritanicum

Linum austriacum var. mauritanicum, commonly known as the Mauritanian flax, is a flowering plant native to North Africa.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and historical use in fiber production.
