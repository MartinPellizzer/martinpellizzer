Lobelia kalmii

Lobelia kalmii, commonly known as Kalm's lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, tubular blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and prefers partial to full shade. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Lobelia kalmii is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like butterflies and bees.
