Lygodium polystachyum

Lygodium polystachyum, commonly known as the giant water fern, is a tropical climbing fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lygodiaceae and is characterized by its large, pinnately compound leaves that can reach lengths of over two meters. The plant has a unique reproductive system, producing sporangia on the underside of its fronds, which release spores for propagation. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in wetlands and along riverbanks.

Its intricate leaf structure and rapid growth make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
