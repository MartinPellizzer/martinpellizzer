Landolphia tenuifolia

Landolphia tenuifolia, commonly known as the slender leaf African buttercup, is a climbing plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its thin, elongated leaves and showy, yellowish-orange flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, smooth seeds. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
