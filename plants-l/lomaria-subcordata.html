Lomaria subcordata

Lomaria subcordata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf lomaria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its small, blue to purple flowers bloom in late spring or early summer.

Lomaria subcordata is adapted to drought conditions and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and slopes.
