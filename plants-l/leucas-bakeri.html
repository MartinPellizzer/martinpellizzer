Leucas bakeri

Leucas bakeri, commonly known as the Baker's leucas, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It has hairy leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, although it is not widely distributed outside its native habitat.
