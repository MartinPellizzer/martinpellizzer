Lotus salsuginosus

Lotus salsuginosus, commonly known as the saltbush lotus, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is well adapted to saline and drought-prone environments, with succulent leaves that help retain moisture. It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.

Its ability to thrive in harsh conditions makes it a valuable species for rehabilitation projects in degraded landscapes.
