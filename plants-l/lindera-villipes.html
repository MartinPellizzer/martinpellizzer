Lindera villipes

Lindera villipes, commonly known as the Chinese wild ginger, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, smooth, and often reddish-barked stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds and small mammals. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a distinctive scent when crushed.

Lindera villipes is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.
