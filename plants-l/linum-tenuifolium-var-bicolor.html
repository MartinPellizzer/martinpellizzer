Linum tenuifolium var. bicolor

Linum tenuifolium var. bicolor, commonly known as the slender-leaved flax with two colors, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and showy, two-toned flowers that range from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, colorful blooms.
