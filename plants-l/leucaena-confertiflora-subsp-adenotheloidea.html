Leucaena confertiflora subsp. adenotheloidea

Leucaena confertiflora subsp.

adenotheloidea, commonly known as the narrowleaf leucaena, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas. It has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets arranged in pairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in dense clusters, which are followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It is adapted to various soil types but thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

This species is often used for forage, soil stabilization, and in traditional medicine.
