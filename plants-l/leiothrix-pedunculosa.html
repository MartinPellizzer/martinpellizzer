Leiothrix pedunculosa

Leiothrix pedunculosa, commonly known as the slender leiothrix, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear small, clustered flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually arranged in a whorled pattern along the stem. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its floral structure is simple, with four petals and five sepals, typical of the Rubiaceae family.
