Lyonia calyculata

Lyonia calyculata, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its showy clusters of blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has small, narrow leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery or grayish pubescence. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

Lyonia calyculata is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
