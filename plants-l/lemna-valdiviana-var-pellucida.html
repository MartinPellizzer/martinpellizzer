Lemna valdiviana var. pellucida

Lemna valdiviana var. pellucida, commonly known as the translucent water clover, is a species of floating leafless aquatic plant belonging to the family Araceae.

It is characterized by its small, translucent, rounded leaves that allow light to pass through, giving it a unique appearance. This variety is native to South America and thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments. The plant reproduces vegetatively through the production of tubers and fragments, which can quickly colonize water bodies.

It plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small organisms and contributing to water filtration.
