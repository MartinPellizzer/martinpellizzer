Linum medium var. typicum

Linum medium var. typicum, commonly known as the common flax.

It is a flowering plant in the family Linaceae, characterized by its slender, branching stems and delicate blue or purple flowers. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and slightly hairy, while its flowers are typically arranged in loose clusters.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in fiber production.
