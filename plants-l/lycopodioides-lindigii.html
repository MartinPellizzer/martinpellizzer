Lycopodioides lindigii

Lycopodioides lindigii, commonly known as the Lindig's clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the order Lycopodiales.

It is characterized by its small, needle-like leaves and a creeping, rhizomatous growth habit. This plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests or rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures, known as strobila, are cone-like and produce spores for propagation.

Lycopodioides lindigii plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
