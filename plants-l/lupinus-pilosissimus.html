Lupinus pilosissimus

Lupinus pilosissimus, commonly known as the silky lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has soft, hairy leaves that give it its common name, and it thrives in well-drained soils. Lupinus pilosissimus is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

It plays an important role in nitrogen fixation, enriching the soil for other plants.
