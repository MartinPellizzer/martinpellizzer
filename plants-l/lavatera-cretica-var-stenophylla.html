Lavatera cretica var. stenophylla

Lavatera cretica var. stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leafed lavatera, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and showy, fluffy white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural displays.

The species is also noted for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
