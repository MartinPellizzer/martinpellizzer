Lepidium gerloffianum

Lepidium gerloffianum, commonly known as the Gerloff's cress, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its small, white or yellow flowers are arranged in clusters, and it produces small, round seeds.

Lepidium gerloffianum is often found in high-altitude regions, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
