Lavandula Coronopifolia Var. Subtropica

Spanish lavender, scientifically known as Lavandula coronopifolia var. subtropica, is a resilient and aromatic plant native to the Mediterranean region. This variety of lavender thrives in warm climates and is prized for its fragrant purple flowers and silver-green foliage. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and for its calming essential oils. The plant's adaptability makes it a popular choice for both landscaping and herbal applications. Its unique appearance and pleasant scent make it a favorite among gardeners and aromatherapy enthusiasts alike.
