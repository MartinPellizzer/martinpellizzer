Luzula multiflora subsp. thracica

Luzula multiflora subsp.

thracica, commonly known as the Thrace rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often densely arranged around the stem. Its flowering spikes bear small, greenish-white flowers that appear in late spring to early summer.

The subspecies is distinguished by its specific geographic distribution and subtle morphological differences from the nominate form.
