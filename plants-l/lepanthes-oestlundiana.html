Lepanthes oestlundiana

Lepanthes oestlundiana, commonly known as the Oestlund's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant features a slender, often branched stem with numerous lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale green, with a distinctive, hooded lip.

Lepanthes oestlundiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
