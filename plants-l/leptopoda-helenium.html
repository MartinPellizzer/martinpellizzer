Leptopoda helenium

Leptopoda helenium, commonly known as the narrowleaf helenium, is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

It is native to North America and typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. The plant features slender, upright stems with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its showy yellow-orange flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Leptopoda helenium is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
