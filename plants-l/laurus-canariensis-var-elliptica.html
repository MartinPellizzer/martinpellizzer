Laurus canariensis var. elliptica

Laurus canariensis var. elliptica, commonly known as the Canary Island laurel, is a compact, evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Canary Islands.

It features dark green, elliptical-shaped leaves that are glossy and have a strong aromatic scent when crushed. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by spherical, black berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its dense growth habit and aromatic foliage make it a valuable addition to both landscape designs and indoor spaces.
