Licuala hospita

Licuala hospita, commonly known as the Philippine palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the Philippines and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and as thatching material.

The flowers are small and clustered, producing fruits that are edible and used in local cuisines.
