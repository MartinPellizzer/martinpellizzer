Lisianthius exsertus

Lisianthius exsertus, commonly known as the Hawaiian ironwood, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a compact growth habit and is well adapted to the humid, tropical climate of Hawaii. It thrives in well-drained soil and is often found in coastal and lowland forests.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
