Lippia elliptica var. silvicola

Lippia elliptica var. silvicola, commonly known as the swamp lippia, is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along riverbanks, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant features opposite, elliptic leaves with serrated margins and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white to pale purple. Its aromatic foliage and showy blooms make it a notable species in botanical gardens and wetland ecosystems.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
