Lolium multiflorum subsp. ramosum

Lolium multiflorum subsp.

ramosum, commonly known as Italian ryegrass, is a cool-season grass native to Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, finely textured foliage.

The plant produces numerous spikelets along its stems, which bear small, two-lobed flowers. It is widely used in forage production and turfgrass applications due to its rapid growth and high nutritional value.

Its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types makes it a versatile agricultural and horticultural species.
