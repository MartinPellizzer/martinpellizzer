Lasiocroton prunifolius

Lasiocroton prunifolius, commonly known as the cherry-leaved lasiocroton, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub characterized by its lanceolate, leathery leaves that are often reddish or purple in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
