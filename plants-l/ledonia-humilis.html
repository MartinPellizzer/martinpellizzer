Ledonia humilis

Ledonia humilis, commonly known as the dwarf ledonia, is a small herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows to a height of less than 10 centimeters, featuring delicate, grass-like leaves and small, pale blue or purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats. It is known for its low maintenance and ability to withstand drought conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.
