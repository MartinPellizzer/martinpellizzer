Lassonia

Lassonia, commonly known as the false lily of the valley, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asparagaceae.

It consists of several species, most of which are perennial herbs native to southern Africa. The plants typically have narrow, grass-like leaves and produce clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white or pink. Lassonia species are often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrive in well-drained environments.

They are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes cultivated in gardens for their delicate appearance.
