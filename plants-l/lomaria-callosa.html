Lomaria callosa

Lomaria callosa, commonly known as the rough lomaria, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has hairy, rough-textured leaves that give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens.

Lomaria callosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
