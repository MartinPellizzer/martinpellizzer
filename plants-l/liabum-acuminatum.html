Liabum acuminatum

Liabum acuminatum, commonly known as the sharp-leaved liabum, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Fabaceae.

It is native to certain regions of South America, particularly in Argentina and Bolivia. The plant is characterized by its narrow, acuminate (sharply pointed) leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple. It thrives in dry, open habitats and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
