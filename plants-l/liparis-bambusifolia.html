Liparis bambusifolia

Liparis bambusifolia, commonly known as the bamboo orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, bamboo-like stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are elongated and often resemble bamboo in appearance, contributing to its common name.

Liparis bambusifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
