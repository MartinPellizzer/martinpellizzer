Limonium tabernense

Limonium tabernense, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in maritime environments. Its aromatic foliage and showy blooms make it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Limonium tabernense is also valued for its ability to tolerate salt spray and harsh maritime conditions.
