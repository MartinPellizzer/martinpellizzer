Lucinaea membranacea

Lucinaea membranacea, commonly known as the membranous wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features delicate, trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped lobes and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and has a fibrous root system. Its leaves are covered in fine, white hairs, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Lucinaea membranacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in native plant gardens.
