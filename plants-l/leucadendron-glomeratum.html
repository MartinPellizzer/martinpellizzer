Leucadendron glomeratum

Leucadendron glomeratum, commonly known as the silver leaf cycad, is a member of the Cycadaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched stem and large, pinnately compound leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white, waxy bloom. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by woody, cone-like structures containing seeds. It is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is a valued plant in both horticulture and conservation efforts.
