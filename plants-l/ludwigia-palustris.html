Ludwigia palustris

Ludwigia palustris, commonly known as swamp Ludwigia, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in the Americas.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant has elongated, narrow leaves that are often reddish in color, especially under bright light conditions. Its small, yellowish flowers are clustered in spikes and bloom during the summer months.

Ludwigia palustris is often used in aquatic gardens and as a decorative plant in ponds due to its attractive appearance and adaptability.
