Lycianthes urnigera

Lycianthes urnigera, commonly known as the sweet potato leaf vine, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by orange or red berries that contain several seeds. Its leaves are edible and have a mild, slightly sweet flavor, making it a valuable plant in traditional cuisines.

The plant is also cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
