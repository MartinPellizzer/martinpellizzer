Loxocalyx urticifolius var. urticifolius

Loxocalyx urticifolius var. urticifolius, commonly known as the stinging nettle tree, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the family Urticaceae, which includes many species known for their stinging hairs. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are covered in fine, irritating trichomes. It produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, and its roots are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
