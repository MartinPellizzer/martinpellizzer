Lupinus elegans var. quercetorum

Lupinus elegans var. quercetorum, commonly known as the oakleaf lupine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking, fern-like foliage and tall spikes of blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its unique leaf structure, resembling oak leaves, gives it its common name.

Lupinus elegans var. quercetorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
