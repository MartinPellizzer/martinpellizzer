Linum chironioides

Linum chironioides, commonly known as the golden flax, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces slender, branched stems and has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Linum chironioides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
