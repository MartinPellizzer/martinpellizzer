Leptodermis ovata

Leptodermis ovata, commonly known as the oval-leaved daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are ovate, with a smooth surface, and the flowers are small, white or pale yellow, arranged in a composite head.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
