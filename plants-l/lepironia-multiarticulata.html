Lepironia multiarticulata

Lepironia multiarticulata, commonly known as the Indian arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of India and Southeast Asia.

It features a rhizomatous root system that allows it to thrive in waterlogged soils. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are often submerged or floating, adapting to its aquatic environment. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically arranged in spikes, and it produces berries that contain numerous seeds.

Lepironia multiarticulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
