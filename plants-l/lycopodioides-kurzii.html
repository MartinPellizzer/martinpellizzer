Lycopodioides kurzii

Lycopodioides kurzii, commonly known as the Kurz's clubmoss, is a small, evergreen plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, dense growth habit and its production of small, scale-like leaves. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. It reproduces via spores rather than seeds, a feature common among primitive vascular plants.
