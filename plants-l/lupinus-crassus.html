Lupinus crassus

Lupinus crassus, commonly known as the thickleaf lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers. The plant has palmately compound leaves with several leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained soils. Lupinus crassus is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria.

It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.
