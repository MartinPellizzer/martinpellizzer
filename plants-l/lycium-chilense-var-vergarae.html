Lycium chilense var. vergarae

Lycium chilense var. vergarae, commonly known as the Chilote wolfberry, is a shrub native to southern Chile.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, red berries. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils and maritime climates. Its berries are edible and have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is of interest for its potential in horticulture and conservation due to its limited distribution.
