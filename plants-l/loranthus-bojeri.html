Loranthus bojeri

Loranthus bojeri, commonly known as the Bojer's mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees.

It belongs to the family Loranthaceae and is native to parts of Africa, particularly South Africa. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are often clustered together. Its berries are red and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This species plays an important ecological role by providing food for various bird species and contributing to the biodiversity of its ecosystem.
