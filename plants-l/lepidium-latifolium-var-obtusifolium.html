Lepidium latifolium var. obtusifolium

Lepidium latifolium var. obtusifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard family plants.

Its structure and growth habits make it well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
