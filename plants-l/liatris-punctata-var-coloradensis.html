Liatris punctata var. coloradensis

Liatris punctata var. coloradensis, commonly known as the Colorado blazingstar, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely leafed stems that bear dense clusters of pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy addition to native plant gardens.
