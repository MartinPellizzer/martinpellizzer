Limonium dictyophorum

Limonium dictyophorum, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiky flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and saline soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of salt.

Limonium dictyophorum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained, sunny conditions.
