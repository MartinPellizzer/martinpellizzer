Leandra melastomoides var. paulina

Leandra melastomoides var. paulina, commonly known as the Paulina's leandra, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically features opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and prominent veins. Its flowers are usually clustered in inflorescences and can range in color from white to pink or purple.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and compact growth habit.
