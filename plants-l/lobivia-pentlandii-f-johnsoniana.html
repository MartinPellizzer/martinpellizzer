Lobivia pentlandii f. johnsoniana

Lobivia pentlandii f. johnsoniana, commonly known as the Johnson's lobivia, is a rare and striking cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It features a compact, globular stem with ribbed structures that help it store water efficiently. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in specialized collections.
