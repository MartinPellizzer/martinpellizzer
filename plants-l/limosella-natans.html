Limosella natans

Limosella natans, commonly known as floating bladderwort, is a small aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tiny, floating leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white, and it is known for its ability to trap small aquatic organisms in its bladder-like structures. Limosella natans thrives in still or slow-moving waters and is often found in ponds, ditches, and wetlands.

Its unique adaptations make it an important part of aquatic ecosystems.
