Liparis minuta

Liparis minuta, commonly known as the dwarf orchid, is a small, terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, greenish-yellow flowers with a faint fragrance. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact size and unique floral structure.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often considered a conservation concern.
