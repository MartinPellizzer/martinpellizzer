Linkia falcata

Linkia falcata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed kikar, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often prostrate stems and narrow, falcate (sickle-shaped) leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Linkia falcata is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, with mechanisms to store water and reduce transpiration.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of forage for livestock.
