Luzula multiflora subsp. comosa

Luzula multiflora subsp.

comosa, commonly known as the tufted woodrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks, where it thrives in well-drained, acidic soils.

The plant features dense, grass-like foliage with narrow, dark green leaves that form a compact, cushion-like rosette. Its flowering stems bear clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers, which are often arranged in spike-like inflorescences. Luzula multiflora subsp.

comosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various invertebrates.
