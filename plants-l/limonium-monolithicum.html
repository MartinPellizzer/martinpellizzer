Limonium monolithicum

Limonium monolithicum, commonly known as the rock sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are adapted to withstand salty air and strong winds.

Limonium monolithicum is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
