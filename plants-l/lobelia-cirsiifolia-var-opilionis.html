Lobelia cirsiifolia var. opilionis

Lobelia cirsiifolia var. opilionis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, tubular blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
