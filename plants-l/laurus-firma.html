Laurus firma

Laurus firma, commonly known as the Mediterranean laurel, is a broadleaf evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dark green, glossy, oval-shaped leaves that are aromatic and often used in culinary applications. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible but not widely consumed. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its hardy nature and attractive foliage make it a valuable addition to both landscape design and traditional herbal practices.
