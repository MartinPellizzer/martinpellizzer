Lhotskya alpestris var. bracteosa

Lhotskya alpestris var. bracteosa, commonly known as the Alpine bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are adapted to cold and arid conditions. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant for rock gardens and alpine collections.
