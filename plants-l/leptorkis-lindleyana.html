Leptorkis lindleyana

Leptorkis lindleyana, commonly known as the Lindley's orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elegant, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are often epiphytic, meaning they grow on the surface of other plants rather than in soil.

The flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish in color, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.
