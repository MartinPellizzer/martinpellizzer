Lathyrus nissolia

Lathyrus nissolia, commonly known as the Nissol's vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet being ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in racemose inflorescences. Its pods are slender and contain several elliptical seeds.

Lathyrus nissolia is native to regions of southern Europe and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.
