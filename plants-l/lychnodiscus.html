Lychnodiscus

Lychnodiscus, commonly known as the African buttercup, is a genus of flowering plants native to southern Africa.

It consists of several species, most of which are herbaceous perennials with showy, yellow or orange flowers. The leaves are typically basal, arranged in a rosette, and are often covered in fine hairs. The plant's flowers are actinomorphic, with five petals that are usually fused at the base.

Lychnodiscus species are often found in grasslands and open habitats, and they play a role in supporting local pollinator populations.
