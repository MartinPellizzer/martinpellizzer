Luzula rigida

Luzula rigida, commonly known as rigid woodrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its upright, grass-like foliage and produces small, clustered inflorescences. Native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, it thrives in moist, acidic soils, often found in bogs and wetlands. The plant has narrow, rigid leaves that are typically green to blue-green in color.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging wetland environments.
