Lycopodium watsonianum

Lycopodium watsonianum, commonly known as the Watson's clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is a small, evergreen shrub characterized by its compact growth habit and scale-like leaves. The plant produces cone-like structures called strobila, which contain the sporangia that release spores for reproduction. Native to certain regions of Southeast Asia, it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
