Lythrum acuminatum

Lythrum acuminatum, commonly known as narrow-leaved loosestrife, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lythraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and spikes of violet to pink flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and marshy areas.

Lythrum acuminatum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
