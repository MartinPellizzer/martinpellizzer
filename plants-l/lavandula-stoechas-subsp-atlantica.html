Lavandula stoechas subsp. atlantica

Lavandula stoechas subsp.

atlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic lavender, is a subspecies native to the Iberian Peninsula and North Africa. It is a perennial shrub characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and showy, violet-blue flower spikes.

The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are used in perfumery and herbal medicine, similar to other lavender species.

It is also valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
