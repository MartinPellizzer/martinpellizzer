Lycopodiella glaucescens

Lycopodiella glaucescens, commonly known as the glaucous clubmoss, is a small, evergreen plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its production of small, scale-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its spore-bearing structures, called strobili, are clustered at the tips of its branches.

Lycopodiella glaucescens plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and small invertebrates.
