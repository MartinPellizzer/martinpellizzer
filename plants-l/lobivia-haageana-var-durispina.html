Lobivia haageana var. durispina

Lobivia haageana var. durispina, commonly known as the Spiny Lobivia, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It features dense, cylindrical stems covered in spiny tubercles, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers during the warmer months. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, and it stores water in its fleshy stems.

This variety is prized for its unique appearance and resilience in low-water conditions.
