Lobelia lindblomii f. nanella

Lobelia lindblomii f. nanella, commonly known as the dwarf lobelia, is a rare and distinctive plant native to the high elevations of the Sierra Nevada mountain range in California.

This fern-like plant features delicate, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence, giving it a soft, almost silvery appearance. Its small, tubular flowers are typically blue or violet, arranged in clusters that add a striking visual contrast to its foliage. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments, often found in shaded, rocky habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a conservation concern.
