Ligularia tongkyukensis

Ligularia tongkyukensis, commonly known as the Korean ligularia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Korea.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often deeply lobed. The plant produces yellowish-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
