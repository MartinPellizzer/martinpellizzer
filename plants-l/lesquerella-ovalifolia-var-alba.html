Lesquerella ovalifolia var. alba

Lesquerella ovalifolia var. alba, commonly known as the white lesquerella, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its oval-shaped leaves and white, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces a unique seed oil that contains high levels of erucic acid, making it valuable for industrial and medicinal applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.
