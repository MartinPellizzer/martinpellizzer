Lepanthes dasyphylla

Lepanthes dasyphylla, commonly known as the showy slipper orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive slipper-shaped pouch. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually elongated and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

The orchid's delicate appearance and unique floral structure make it a popular subject in botanical studies and horticulture.
