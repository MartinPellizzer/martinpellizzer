Lupinus ingratus var. confertifolius

Lupinus ingratus var. confertifolius, commonly known as the dense-leaved lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets, and its root system forms symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
