Lycopodioides arenaria

Lycopodioides arenaria, commonly known as the sand lycopod, is a small, spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its simple, branching structure and tiny, scale-like leaves. This plant thrives in sandy or well-drained soils, often found in arid or coastal environments. Lycopodioides arenaria reproduces through spores rather than seeds, and it plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils.

Its presence indicates a specific ecological niche within dry, nutrient-poor habitats.
