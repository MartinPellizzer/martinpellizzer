Lawia ceylanica var. malabarica

Lawia ceylanica var. malabarica, commonly known as the Malabar lawia, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments within tropical forests. The plant features small, elliptical leaves with a smooth surface and a distinct, aromatic scent. Its flowers are clustered in inflorescences and are usually pale yellow or greenish-white in color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage and fragrance.
