Loranthus dekindtianus

Loranthus dekindtianus, commonly known as the De Kindt's mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily in the genus *Eucalyptus*.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using light absorbed by its leaves. The plant has a distinctive appearance with small, oval-shaped leaves and small, white or yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. Its berries are typically red or orange and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This species is native to southern Australia and is often found in coastal and dry forest regions.
