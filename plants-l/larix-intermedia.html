Larix intermedia

Larix intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate larch, is a deciduous conifer native to eastern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and its ability to shed its needle-like leaves in the fall, revealing a striking silver appearance. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its bark is smooth and reddish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

Larix intermedia is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
