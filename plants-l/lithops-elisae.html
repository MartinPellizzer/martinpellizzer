Lithops elisae

Lithops elisae, commonly known as the "Cape Town flower," is a unique succulent native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its pair of fleshy, leaf-like structures that resemble stones, helping it blend into its arid environment. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water to prevent root rot.

Lithops elisae is prized for its striking appearance and ability to store water efficiently.
