Lobelia dopatrioides var. dopatrioides

Lobelia dopatrioides var. dopatrioides, commonly known as the dwarf lobelia, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, branching stems that produce small, tubular flowers in shades of blue to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and wet meadows. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

This species is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
