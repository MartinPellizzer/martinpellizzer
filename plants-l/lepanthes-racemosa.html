Lepanthes racemosa

Lepanthes racemosa, commonly known as the clustered slipper orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that often feature a lip or labellum. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical rainforests. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by insects.

Lepanthes racemosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the biodiversity of its native ecosystems.
