Linanthus bigelovii subsp. bigelovii

Linanthus bigelovii subsp.

bigelovii, commonly known as the desert flax, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a member of the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish-green flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and it produces capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This species is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing food for local wildlife.
