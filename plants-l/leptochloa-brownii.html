Leptochloa brownii

Leptochloa brownii, commonly known as the Brown's needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its resilience to drought.

Leptochloa brownii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for livestock in its native range.
