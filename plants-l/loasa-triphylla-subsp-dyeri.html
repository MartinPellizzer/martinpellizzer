Loasa triphylla subsp. dyeri

Loasa triphylla subsp.

dyeri, commonly known as the Sierra loasa, is a shrub native to the western United States and parts of Mexico. It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged in whorls of three.

The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of interest in horticultural studies.
