Lauro-cerasus lusitanica f. variegata

Lauro-cerasus lusitanica f. variegata, commonly known as the variegated cherry laurel, is a hybrid plant that exhibits a unique combination of traits from both Prunus laurocerasus and Prunus lusitanica.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with striking white or yellow variegation, which adds visual interest to gardens. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in spring, followed by edible berries that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a versatile ornamental plant.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is widely cultivated in temperate regions for landscaping purposes.
