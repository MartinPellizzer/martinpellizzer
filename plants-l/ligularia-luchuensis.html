Ligularia luchuensis

Ligularia luchuensis, commonly known as the Luchuan ligularia, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Ligularia luchuensis thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique foliage and ornamental value make it a sought-after plant for botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
