Linum strictum proles cymosum

Linum strictum proles cymosum, commonly known as the clustered flax, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, slender stems and dense clusters of small, blue or purple flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and herbal medicine.
