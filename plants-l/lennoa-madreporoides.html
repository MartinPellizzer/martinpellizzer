Lennoa madreporoides

Lennoa madreporoides, commonly known as the rock wallaby orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its compact, hairy stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or heathland environments.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species.
