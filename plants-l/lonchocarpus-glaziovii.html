Lonchocarpus glaziovii

Lonchocarpus glaziovii, commonly known as the Brazilian burr bean, is a tropical legume native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, spiny pods that contain several seeds. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it valuable in agricultural systems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological and potential medicinal studies.
