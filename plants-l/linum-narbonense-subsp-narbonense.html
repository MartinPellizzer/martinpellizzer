Linum narbonense subsp. narbonense

Linum narbonense subsp.

narbonense, commonly known as the Narrow-leaved flax, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Linum. It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces showy blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to coastal and inland habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in fiber production.
