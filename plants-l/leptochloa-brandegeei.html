Leptochloa brandegeei

Leptochloa brandegeei, commonly known as the Brandegee's hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands, wetlands, and along riverbanks. The plant has slender, tufted shoots with narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually smooth and slightly hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are small and often clustered at the tips of the stems.

Leptochloa brandegeei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its fine texture and adaptability.
