Liparis filiformis

Liparis filiformis, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, grass-like leaves and small, intricate flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its flowers are usually pale green or white, with a unique structure that includes a spurred lip.

Liparis filiformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
