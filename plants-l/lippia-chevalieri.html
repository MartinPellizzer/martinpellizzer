Lippia chevalieri

Lippia chevalieri, commonly known as the Mexican blue sage, is a flowering plant native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It belongs to the verbena family (Verbenaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and showy blue to purple flowers. The plant is typically shrub-like, with branching stems that often grow in a compact, rounded form. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Lippia chevalieri is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
