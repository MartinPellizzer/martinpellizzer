Lepistachya

Lepistachya, commonly known as the spike grass, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family, Poaceae.

These plants are typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, often growing in moist or wetland environments. Lepistachya species are characterized by their dense, spike-like inflorescences, which are often composed of small, clustered flowers. The leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, with a prominent midrib.

They play an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
