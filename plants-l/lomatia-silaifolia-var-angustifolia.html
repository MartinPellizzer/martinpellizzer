Lomatia silaifolia var. angustifolia

Lomatia silaifolia var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf lomatia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, angular stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-white flowers, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. Its foliage is often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This variety is adapted to grow in dry, rocky environments and is often found in coastal regions.
