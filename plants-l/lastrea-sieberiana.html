Lastrea sieberiana

Lastrea sieberiana, commonly known as the Sieber's lastrea, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically red or pink in color. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
