Lycoctonum pseudolaeve

Lycoctonum pseudolaeve, commonly known as the smooth nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and potatoes. The plant features hairy stems and broad, ovate leaves with a slightly serrated edge. Its flowers are typically violet to purple in color, and it produces small, round berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

Due to its toxic properties, it is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
