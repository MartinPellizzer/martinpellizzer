Lipochaeta umbellata var. conferta

Lipochaeta umbellata var. conferta, commonly known as the denseflower ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in open woodlands or heathlands. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
