Lachenalia versicolor var. purpurea

Lachenalia versicolor var. purpurea, commonly known as the purple-flowered lachenalia, is a striking member of the Amaryllidaceae family native to South Africa.

This succulent plant is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are typically green with a reddish or purplish hue, especially under bright light conditions. The flowers, which bloom in late spring or early summer, are showy and come in shades of pink, purple, and white, often with a distinctive striped pattern. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated by succulent enthusiasts.
