Lobelia ehrenbergii subsp. gracilens

Lobelia ehrenbergii subsp.

gracilens, commonly known as slenderleaf lobelia, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States. It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves with a smooth, glossy surface. Its flowers are small, tubular, and typically blue or purple, arranged in dense spikes.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
