Liparis maboroensis

Liparis maboroensis, commonly known as the Maboro orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the island of Maboro in Japan.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the surrounding soil.

Liparis maboroensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
