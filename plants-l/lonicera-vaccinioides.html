Lonicera vaccinioides

Lonicera vaccinioides, commonly known as sweet honey honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features opposite, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy on the underside. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are usually yellow or orange, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its berries are red or orange, containing small seeds, and are an important food source for birds.

Lonicera vaccinioides is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.
