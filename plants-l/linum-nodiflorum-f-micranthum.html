Linum nodiflorum f. micranthum

Linum nodiflorum f. micranthum, commonly known as the dwarf blue flax, is a compact, herbaceous perennial belonging to the Linaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, clustered blue flowers and linear, lance-shaped leaves. This cultivar is a compact form of the parent species, Linum nodiflorum, which is native to Europe and western Asia. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
