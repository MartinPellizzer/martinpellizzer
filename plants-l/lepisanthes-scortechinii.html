Lepisanthes scortechinii

Lepisanthes scortechinii, commonly known as the golden star orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow and green flowers with a distinctive star-shaped pattern. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native habitat.

Lepisanthes scortechinii is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or conservatories.
