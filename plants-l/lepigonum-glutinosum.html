Lepigonum glutinosum

Lepigonum glutinosum, commonly known as sticky lespedeza, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and produces small, pink to purple flowers. The plant has sticky hairs on its stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and disturbed areas.

Lepigonum glutinosum is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide forage for wildlife.
