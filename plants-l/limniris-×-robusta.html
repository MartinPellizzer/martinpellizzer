Limniris × robusta

Limniris × robusta, commonly known as the hybrid water lily, is a botanical hybrid resulting from the cross between Limniris natalensis and Limniris tenuis.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter, providing a lush surface on the water. The plant produces showy white or pale yellow flowers with a distinctive spiral arrangement of petals. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds and lakes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in water gardens and wetland habitats.
