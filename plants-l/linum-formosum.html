Linum formosum

Linum formosum, commonly known as the Chinese flax or Chinese blue flax, is a flowering plant native to China and Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual that grows up to 1 meter tall, featuring slender stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers, which are typically arranged in racemes. It belongs to the Linaceae family, which includes other flax species.

Linum formosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional Chinese medicine.
