Limnanthemum calthifolium

Limnanthemum calthifolium, commonly known as the marsh mallow or water mallow, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial with showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, and its stems are often reddish in color. It thrives in moist soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Limnanthemum calthifolium plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining the ecological balance of wetland ecosystems.
