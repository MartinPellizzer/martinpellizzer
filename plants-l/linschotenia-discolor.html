Linschotenia discolor

Linschotenia discolor, commonly known as the spotted lichen, is a foliose lichen that thrives in humid, shaded environments.

It is characterized by its distinct, irregularly shaped thallus with a pale green to yellowish upper surface and a darker, often reddish lower surface. The plant lacks true roots, stems, and leaves, instead forming a symbiotic association between algae and fungi. It is typically found on tree trunks and rocks in temperate forests.

Its unique coloration and growth patterns make it a notable species in ecological studies.
