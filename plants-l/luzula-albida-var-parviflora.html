Luzula albida var. parviflora

Luzula albida var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered wood rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features upright, grass-like stems that bear dense clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and often have a bluish-gray sheen, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is characterized by its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and its role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.
