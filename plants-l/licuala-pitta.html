Licuala pitta

Licuala pitta, commonly known as the pitta palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach several meters in diameter. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by dark blue or black berries.

Licuala pitta is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
