Lonicera gynochlamydea

Lonicera gynochlamydea, commonly known as the narrowleaf honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish-white flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its berries are small, red, and edible, often attracting birds and other wildlife.

Lonicera gynochlamydea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in gardens and natural habitats.
