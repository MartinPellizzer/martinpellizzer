Legnephora minutiflora

Legnephora minutiflora, commonly known as the tiny-flower woodvine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, delicate flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
