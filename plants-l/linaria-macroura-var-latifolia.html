Linaria macroura var. latifolia

Linaria macroura var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf toadflax, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of yellow-orange flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and often broader than those of the typical variety, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
