Lippia oligophylla

Lippia oligophylla, commonly known as Mexican blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its slender, aromatic stems and small, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Lippia oligophylla is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of streams.

Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
