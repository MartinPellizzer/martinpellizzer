Loranthus pendulus var. melaleucae

Loranthus pendulus var. melaleucae, commonly known as the mistletoe of the honey gum, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host, typically the honey gum (Eucalyptus viminalis).

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using light absorbed by its leaves. The plant forms a dense, bushy growth with small, oval-shaped leaves and produces small, white or pale green flowers. It is often found in coastal regions of southeastern Australia, where it thrives in a variety of eucalyptus species.

Its presence can have both ecological and economic impacts on its host trees.
