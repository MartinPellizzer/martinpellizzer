Lepidium ruderale var. crispum

Lepidium ruderale var. crispum, commonly known as curly cress, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant is characterized by its small, white or yellowish flowers and hairy, lobed leaves. It typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

The variety *crispum* is distinguished by its curled or crisped leaf margins, giving it a unique appearance.
