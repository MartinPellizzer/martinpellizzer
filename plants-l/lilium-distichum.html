Lilium distichum

Lilium distichum, commonly known as the two-row lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in two rows along the stem. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a basal rosette.

Lilium distichum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
