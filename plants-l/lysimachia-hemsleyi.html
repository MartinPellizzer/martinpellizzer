Lysimachia hemsleyi

Lysimachia hemsleyi, commonly known as the Hemsley's bugleweed, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its creeping growth habit and dense clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant features opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Lysimachia hemsleyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
