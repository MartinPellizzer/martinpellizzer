Leiothrix hirsuta f. vivipara

Leiothrix hirsuta f. vivipara, commonly known as the viviparous hirsute leiothrix, is a rare and unique variant of the leiothrix genus.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and a distinctive growth habit. This plant exhibits viviparity, a reproductive trait where the plant produces live offspring rather than seeds, making it a fascinating subject in botanical studies. It thrives in shaded, moist environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical significance lies in its unusual reproductive method and potential adaptations to specific ecological niches.
