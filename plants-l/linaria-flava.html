Linaria flava

Linaria flava, commonly known as the yellow toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and gravelly soils. It has slender, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Linaria flava is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its historical use in traditional medicine.
