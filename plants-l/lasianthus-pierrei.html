Lasianthus pierrei

Lasianthus pierrei, commonly known as the Philippine star orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers with white and purple markings, which are often arranged in a star-like pattern. The plant typically grows in forest understories, thriving in humid and shaded environments. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the forest floor, and it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.

Lasianthus pierrei is a rare and endangered species, making conservation efforts crucial for its survival.
