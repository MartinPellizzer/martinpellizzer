Leucanthemum atratum subsp. tridactylites

Leucanthemum atratum subsp.

tridactylites, commonly known as the black-eyed Susan, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America. It features showy yellow ray florets surrounding a dark central disk, giving it its characteristic appearance.

The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and produces daisy-like flowers in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and wildflower habitats.
