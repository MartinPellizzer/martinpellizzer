Lupinus carchiensis

Lupinus carchiensis, commonly known as the Carcharhine lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, spindly growth habit. The plant produces showy, pea-like flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Lupinus carchiensis is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
