Lathyrus floribundus

Lathyrus floribundus, commonly known as the sweet pea, is a climbing legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and clusters of fragrant, pea-like flowers that come in a wide range of colors. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are typically borne in racemes, and the plant produces small, flat pods that contain several angular seeds.

Lathyrus floribundus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
