Lindmania lateralis

Lindmania lateralis, commonly known as the silver fern, is a striking plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its unique, sword-shaped leaves that form a rosette pattern. The plant's foliage is covered in a fine, silvery-white powder, which gives it its common name and helps reflect excess sunlight. It produces small, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Lindmania lateralis is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its aesthetic value and air-purifying qualities.
