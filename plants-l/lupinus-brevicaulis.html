Lupinus brevicaulis

Lupinus brevicaulis, commonly known as the short-stalked lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant features upright stems with narrow, pinnately compound leaves. Its flowers are usually blue or purple, arranged in dense spikes, and are attractive to pollinators.

Lupinus brevicaulis is adapted to arid conditions and has a deep root system for water absorption.
