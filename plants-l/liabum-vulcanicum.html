Liabum vulcanicum

Liabum vulcanicum, commonly known as the fire vine, is a unique plant species native to volcanic regions.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and vibrant, often red or orange, tubular flowers. The plant thrives in nutrient-rich, well-drained soils found in areas with volcanic activity. Its leaves are typically small and opposite, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and specialized flora of volcanic landscapes.
