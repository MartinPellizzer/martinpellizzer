Lycopus rubellus var. lanceolatus

Lycopus rubellus var. lanceolatus, commonly known as red hairy herb, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features opposite, lanceolate leaves that are often hairy and have a reddish tinge, especially along the veins. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers in clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it identifiable and useful in ecological studies and herbal medicine.
