Lefebvrea longipedicellata var. levisticifolia

Lefebvrea longipedicellata var. levisticifolia, commonly known as the lemon verbena, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, lemon-scented leaves that are opposite and pinnately compound. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its botanical features include woody stems and hairy foliage, which contribute to its distinctive appearance.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
