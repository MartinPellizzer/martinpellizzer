Lepanthes subdimidiata

Lepanthes subdimidiata, commonly known as the narrow-lobed slipper orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its delicate, spurred flowers that are typically white or pale green in color. The plant grows in shaded, humid environments, often in montane forests. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, and it produces small, fragrant blooms during the rainy season.

Lepanthes subdimidiata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
