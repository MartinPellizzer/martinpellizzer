Lonicera × xylosteoides

Lonicera × xylosteoides, commonly known as the yellow honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and clusters of fragrant, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds but not typically consumed by humans. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its rapid growth and ability to climb or trail make it versatile for various landscape applications.
