Litsea meyeri

Litsea meyeri, commonly known as Meyer's litsea, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurel and avocado trees. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, round seeds.

Litsea meyeri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
