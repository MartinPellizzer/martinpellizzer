Lucuma dictyoneura

Lucuma dictyoneura, commonly known as the golden lucuma, is a species of tree native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its large, egg-shaped fruits that are typically harvested when fully mature. The fruit has a unique, sweet, and nutty flavor, with a texture similar to a mix between a banana and a sweet potato. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, temperate climates and well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value, combined with its nutritional benefits, has made it a subject of interest for both horticulture and food industries.
