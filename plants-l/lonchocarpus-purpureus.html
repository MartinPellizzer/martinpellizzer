Lonchocarpus purpureus

Lonchocarpus purpureus, commonly known as the purple senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its legume-like pods and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces vibrant yellow flowers. Its seed pods are distinctive, being elongated and often containing several seeds.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking floral display.
