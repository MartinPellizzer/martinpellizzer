Lilium coridion

Lilium coridion, commonly known as the Turk's cap lily, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant produces a single, large, recurved bloom that resembles a turban, giving it its common name. Lilium coridion is a bulbous perennial with narrow, grass-like leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.
