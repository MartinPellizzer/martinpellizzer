Lonicera × muscaviensis f. rhodocarpa

Lonicera × muscaviensis f. rhodocarpa, commonly known as the red-berried honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value comes from its colorful foliage and showy berries, making it a popular choice for gardeners.
