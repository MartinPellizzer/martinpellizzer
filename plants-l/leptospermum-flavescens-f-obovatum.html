Leptospermum flavescens f. obovatum

Leptospermum flavescens f. obovatum, commonly known as the golden tea tree, is a shrub native to Australia, specifically found in the eastern regions.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically a bright yellow-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are often visited by bees and other pollinators. It is a member of the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and tea tree.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its aromatic foliage.
