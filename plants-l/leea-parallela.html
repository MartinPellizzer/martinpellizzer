Leea parallela

Leea parallela, commonly known as the parallelleaf leea, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Leeaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
