Lysionotus gamosepalus var. gamosepalus

Lysionotus gamosepalus var. gamosepalus, commonly known as the common lythrum, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, arranged in spikes along the stem. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are square in cross-section, a characteristic feature of the mint family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
