Liatris spicata var. typica

Liatris spicata var. typica, commonly known as blazing star, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features upright, spiky flower heads composed of numerous narrow, tubular flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with linear, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in prairies, meadows, and open woodlands.

The showy inflorescence attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
