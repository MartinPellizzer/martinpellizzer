Lagopus glaber

Lagopus glaber, commonly known as the purple sandparsley, is a flowering plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and small, purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in cold, dry environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. Its distinctive appearance makes it well adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

Lagopus glaber plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small organisms in its native ecosystems.
