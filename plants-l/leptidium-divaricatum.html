Leptidium divaricatum

Leptidium divaricatum, commonly known as the slender bladderpod, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant is characterized by its small, white or pale yellow flowers and its capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils along stream banks and roadsides.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
