Leonurus marrubifolius

Leonurus marrubifolius, commonly known as greater motherwort, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features upright stems covered in fine hairs and lance-shaped leaves that are often grayish-green in appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves and flowers are used in traditional medicine for their purported calming and antispasmodic properties.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.
