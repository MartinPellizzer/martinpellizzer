Lastrea chrysolepis

Lastrea chrysolepis, commonly known as the golden-leaved lastrea, is a member of the Asparagaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its striking golden-yellow leaves. The plant typically grows as a succulent shrub, often found in rocky or arid environments. Its leaves are fleshy and arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps it conserve water.

It produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that bloom in summer, adding a subtle contrast to its vibrant foliage.
