Lysimachia azorica

Lysimachia azorica, commonly known as the Azorean chickweed, is a flowering plant native to the Azores, a group of islands in the North Atlantic Ocean.

It is a low-growing herb with small, delicate leaves and yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest undergrowth or along stream banks. Its leaves are typically hairy and have a slightly succulent texture, while its flowers are clustered in loose inflorescences.

Lysimachia azorica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
