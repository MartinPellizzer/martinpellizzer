Lysimachia rhodesica

Lysimachia rhodesica, commonly known as the Rhodes groundsel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright stems covered in fine, hairy hairs and produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in woodland or scrubland habitats. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Lysimachia rhodesica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
