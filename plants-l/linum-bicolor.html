Linum bicolor

Linum bicolor, commonly known as the yellow flax, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers and linear, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. It produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Linum bicolor is a member of the Linaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
