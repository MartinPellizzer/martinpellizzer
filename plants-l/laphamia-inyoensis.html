Laphamia inyoensis

Laphamia inyoensis, commonly known as the Inyo laphamia, is a rare flowering plant native to the Mojave Desert in California.

It is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to arid conditions. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
