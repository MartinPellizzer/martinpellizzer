Lonchocarpus spectabilis

Lonchocarpus spectabilis, commonly known as the showy legume, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and striking purple-pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces distinctive pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
