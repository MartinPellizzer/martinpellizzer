Linum usitatissimum var. hirsutiseptum

Linum usitatissimum var. hirsutiseptum, commonly known as the hairy seven-sepaled flax, is a variant of the common flax plant.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, as well as its distinctive floral structure with seven sepals. This variety is typically found in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers, which are often used in ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in plant morphology and horticulture.
