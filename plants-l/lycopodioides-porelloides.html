Lycopodioides porelloides

Lycopodioides porelloides, commonly known as the slender clubmoss, is a small, evergreen shrub belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and scale-like leaves that form dense, moss-like clusters. The plant produces small, cone-like structures called strobili, which contain spores for reproduction. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Lycopodioides porelloides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in maintaining ecological balance in its native habitat.
