Leandra cordigera var. transiens

Leandra cordigera var. transiens, commonly known as the Transient Leandra, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, leathery, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, fertile soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
