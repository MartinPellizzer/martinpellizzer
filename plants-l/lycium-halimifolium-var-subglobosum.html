Lycium halimifolium var. subglobosum

Lycium halimifolium var. subglobosum, commonly known as the globular boxthorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny growth and small, fleshy berries that are typically red when mature. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats. Its leaves are usually small, oval, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are pale yellow and clustered.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide habitat for local wildlife.
