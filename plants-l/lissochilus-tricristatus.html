Lissochilus tricristatus

Lissochilus tricristatus, commonly known as the three-crested orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America, particularly in regions of Argentina and Chile.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant has thin, erect stems and produces small, greenish-white or pale yellow flowers arranged in a raceme. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
