Lecythis compressa

Lecythis compressa, commonly known as the compressed lecythium, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers. The tree produces a heavy, oily fruit with a hard, spiky outer shell containing a single large seed. Its latex is used in traditional medicine and for making adhesives.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in rainforest ecosystems.
