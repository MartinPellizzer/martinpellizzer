Lycium cestroides var. normale

Lycium cestroides var. normale, commonly known as the common wolfberry, is a shrub native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, orange-red berries that ripen in the autumn. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to a variety of climates. Its leaves are oval and have a slightly serrated edge, while its flowers are yellow and clustered.

The berries are edible and often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
