Lonicera chamissoi f. albiflora

Lonicera chamissoi f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered honeysuckle, is a unique variant of the honeysuckle genus.

It is characterized by its pale yellow to white flowers, which contrast with the typical orange-red hues of other honeysuckle species. This plant is native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its foliage consists of opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but not as commonly consumed as those of other honeysuckle varieties.
