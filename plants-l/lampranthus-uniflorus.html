Lampranthus uniflorus

Lampranthus uniflorus, commonly known as the single-flower ice plant, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces a single, showy flower per stem, typically in shades of pink or red, during the summer months. It belongs to the Aizoaceae family, known for its adaptability to dry conditions.

Lampranthus uniflorus is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and ornamental value.
