Luzula campestris subsp. nevadensis

Luzula campestris subsp.

nevadensis, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in alpine and subalpine habitats. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spike-like clusters, which are typically greenish or brownish in color.

This subspecies is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments and is often found in meadows and rocky slopes.
