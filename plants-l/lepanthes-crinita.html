Lepanthes crinita

Lepanthes crinita, commonly known as the curly lip orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that often have a distinctive curling lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, adapting to its canopy-dwelling lifestyle.

Lepanthes crinita is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and unique floral structure.
