Lomatophyllum tormentorii

Lomatophyllum tormentorii, commonly known as the desert daisy, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in a fine, white powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss.

Lomatophyllum tormentorii is well adapted to harsh, dry environments and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
