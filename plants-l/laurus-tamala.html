Laurus tamala

Laurus tamala, commonly known as bay laurel or sweet bay, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its dark green, shiny leaves that have a distinctive aromatic scent. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by dark purple berries. Its leaves are widely used in cooking, particularly in Mediterranean cuisine, and have historical significance in ancient Greek and Roman cultures.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its fragrance and visual beauty.
