Lolium halleri

Lolium halleri, commonly known as alpine ryegrass, is a flowering plant species native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant typically produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it thrives in cool, moist environments.

Lolium halleri is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
