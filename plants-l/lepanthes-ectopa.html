Lepanthes ectopa

Lepanthes ectopa, commonly known as the Ecuadorian violet, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its small, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pale pink. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually elliptical and covered in a fine, velvety texture.

Lepanthes ectopa thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires specific conditions to flourish.
