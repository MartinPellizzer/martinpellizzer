Lonicera japonica f. halliana

Lonicera japonica f. halliana, commonly known as the Hall's honeysuckle, is a cultivar of the Japanese honeysuckle.

It is a deciduous vine characterized by its rapid growth and trailing habit. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, appearing in clusters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate with a slightly hairy surface.

This variety is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
