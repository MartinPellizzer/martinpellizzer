Lonchocarpus crassispermus

Lonchocarpus crassispermus, commonly known as the broad-seeded legume, is a tropical plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and produces distinctive pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. The plant is native to regions in Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. It is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks, contributing to the biodiversity of these ecosystems.

Due to its nitrogen-fixing ability, it plays a role in soil enrichment and ecological balance.
