Lonchocarpus hondurensis

Lonchocarpus hondurensis, commonly known as the Honduran urdane, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with bright colors that attract pollinators.

The species is noted for its potential medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
