Lobelia bellidifolia

Lobelia bellidifolia, commonly known as the bellflower lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, linear to lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Lobelia bellidifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
