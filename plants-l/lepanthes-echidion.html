Lepanthes echidion

Lepanthes echidion, commonly known as the echidion orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers that typically feature a yellow or greenish hue with intricate patterns. The plant grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical forests. Its leaves are usually broad and strap-like, adapted to capture moisture from the air.

Lepanthes echidion is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its native habitats.
