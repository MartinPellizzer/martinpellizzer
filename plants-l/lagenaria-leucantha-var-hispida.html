Lagenaria leucantha var. hispida

Lagenaria leucantha var. hispida, commonly known as the hairy bottle gourd, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, thick-walled, bottle-shaped fruit covered in rough, spiny projections. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and is cultivated for its edible fruit. The leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a hairy texture.

It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
