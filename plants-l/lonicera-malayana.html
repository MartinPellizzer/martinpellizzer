Lonicera malayana

Lonicera malayana, commonly known as the Malay honeysuckle, is a deciduous climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for some bird species but toxic to humans. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in tropical and subtropical regions.
