Leptospermum juniperifolium

Leptospermum juniperifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tea tree, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its small, needle-like leaves that are densely packed along the stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves contain volatile oils and are used in traditional and modern herbal remedies.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits in native gardens.
