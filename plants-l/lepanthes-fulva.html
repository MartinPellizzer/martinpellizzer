Lepanthes fulva

Lepanthes fulva, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is native to Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that grows on trees in tropical rainforests, deriving moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. The plant produces striking yellow flowers with intricate patterns, often resembling a sunflower in appearance. Its roots are adapted to absorb water and nutrients from the surrounding environment, and it thrives in humid, shaded conditions.

Lepanthes fulva is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
