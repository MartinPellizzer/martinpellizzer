Lepidaria pulchella

Lepidaria pulchella, commonly known as the pretty lepidaria, is a small flowering plant native to the wetlands of eastern North America.

It produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, with a distinctive, waxy texture. The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in swampy areas or along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is known for its ability to tolerate waterlogged conditions.

Lepidaria pulchella is an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
