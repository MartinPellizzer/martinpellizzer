Lonchocarpus pallidus

Lonchocarpus pallidus, commonly known as the pale urara, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its palmate leaves and distinctive yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated, woody pods that contain several seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
