Ligustrum obtusifolium

Ligustrum obtusifolium, commonly known as the blunt-leaf privet, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern China.

It features opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and have a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by black, berry-like fruits. It is often used in landscaping for its dense growth and adaptability to various soil types.

Ligustrum obtusifolium is also valued for its ability to tolerate pruning, making it suitable for hedges and topiaries.
