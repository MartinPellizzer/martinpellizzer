Lepanthes imbricata

Lepanthes imbricata, commonly known as the silver-veined orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments within tropical forests. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces intricate, fragrant flowers with white petals and a distinctive yellow lip. Its flowers bloom during the rainy season, often in clusters at the base of the plant.

Lepanthes imbricata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance.
