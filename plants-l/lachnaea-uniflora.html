Lachnaea uniflora

Lachnaea uniflora, commonly known as the single-flowered lichen, is a species of lichen that belongs to the family Lachnaceae.

It is characterized by its unique morphology, featuring a single apothecium per thallus, which is a distinguishing trait among lichens. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found on decaying wood or rock surfaces. Its thallus is typically crust-like, and it may exhibit a variety of colors depending on environmental conditions.

L. uniflora plays a role in nutrient cycling and serves as a habitat for various microorganisms.
