Lupinus sparsiflorus subsp. pondii

Lupinus sparsiflorus subsp.

pondii, commonly known as the pond larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and meadows.

The plant features erect stems with narrow, pinnately compound leaves. Its showy flowers are usually blue or purple, arranged in dense spikes, and bloom in early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
