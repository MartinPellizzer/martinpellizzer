Lolium appenninum

Lolium appenninum, commonly known as Italian ryegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, elongated leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences containing numerous tiny florets, which are pollinated by wind. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils, meadows, and along roadsides.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it is both a valuable forage crop and a potential weed in agricultural settings.
