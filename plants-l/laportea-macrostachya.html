Laportea macrostachya

Laportea macrostachya, commonly known as the broadleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, purple to pinkish-red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has palmately lobed leaves with serrated edges, and its foliage is often covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as swamps and marshes.

Laportea macrostachya is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
