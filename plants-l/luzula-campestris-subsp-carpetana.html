Luzula campestris subsp. carpetana

Luzula campestris subsp.

carpetana, commonly known as the carpet rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often arranged in dense clusters, giving it a mat-like appearance. Its flowering stems produce small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny greenish or brownish flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and its role in ecological restoration projects.
