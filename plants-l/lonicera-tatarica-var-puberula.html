Lonicera tatarica var. puberula

Lonicera tatarica var. puberula, commonly known as the Siberian honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, arching stems covered in fine, soft pubescence, giving it a distinctive velvety texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in early summer, typically yellow or orange, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly serrated margin, with a glossy green surface.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
