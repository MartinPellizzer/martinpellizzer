Lithocarpus dunnii

Lithocarpus dunnii, commonly known as the dwarf ironwood, is a member of the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks and chestnuts.

It is native to the Philippines and is characterized by its dense, hard wood and evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, cupule-enclosed nuts that are typically dispersed by animals. Its leaves are typically elliptical with a leathery texture and a glossy surface.

Lithocarpus dunnii thrives in moist, shaded environments within tropical and subtropical forests.
