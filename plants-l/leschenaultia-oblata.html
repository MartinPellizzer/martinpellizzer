Leschenaultia oblata

Leschenaultia oblata, commonly known as the silverleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-gray, woolly leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy seeds dispersed by the wind. Leschenaultia oblata is often found in rocky soils and coastal areas, where it thrives in full sun.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a notable species in its native habitat.
