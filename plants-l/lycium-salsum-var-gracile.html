Lycium salsum var. gracile

Lycium salsum var. gracile, commonly known as sea buckthorn, is a shrub native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its small, yellow-orange berries that are rich in vitamins and antioxidants. The plant has small, needle-like leaves and produces yellow flowers in the spring. It thrives in saline and well-drained soils, often growing in coastal dunes or near the sea.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils and its nutritional benefits.
