Lycioserissa

Lycioserissa, commonly known as the sea hibiscus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is native to coastal regions of the Indo-Pacific, where it thrives in sandy or rocky shores. The plant features fleshy, succulent leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to red. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Lycioserissa plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for marine life.
