Leucopogon maccraei

Leucopogon maccraei, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of Western Australia.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant thrives in sandy soils and is adapted to the dry, Mediterranean climate of its native habitat. It plays an important ecological role in its environment by providing nectar for native pollinators.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species and is often found in conservation areas.
