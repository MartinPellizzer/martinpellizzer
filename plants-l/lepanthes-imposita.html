Lepanthes imposita

Lepanthes imposita, commonly known as the showy orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are usually showy and fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

Lepanthes imposita is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in greenhouses and botanical gardens.
