Lysimachia vietnamensis

Lysimachia vietnamensis, commonly known as Vietnamese chickweed, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia, particularly Vietnam.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pink. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly rough texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
