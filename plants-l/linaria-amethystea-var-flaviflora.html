Linaria amethystea var. flaviflora

Linaria amethystea var. flaviflora, commonly known as the golden foxglove, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features slender, branching stems that bear clusters of bright orange to yellow flowers with a distinctive spurred shape. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its vibrant color and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
