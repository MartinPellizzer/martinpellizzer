Lonchocarpus variabilis

Lonchocarpus variabilis, commonly known as variable senna, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant features trifoliate leaves with serrated margins and produces yellow to orange flowers. Its seeds are enclosed in long, narrow pods that contain toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to livestock.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is sometimes cultivated in warm climates.
