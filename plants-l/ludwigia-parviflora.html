Ludwigia parviflora

Ludwigia parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered Ludwigia, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in wetlands and along stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers clustered in dense inflorescences. It is often found in shallow water or moist soil, thriving in environments with ample sunlight.

Ludwigia parviflora is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant in water gardens due to its attractive foliage and blooms.
