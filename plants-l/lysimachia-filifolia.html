Lysimachia filifolia

Lysimachia filifolia, commonly known as threadleaf chickweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Asia.

It features small, opposite, thread-like leaves that give it its common name, and it produces clusters of tiny, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or stream banks. It is part of the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its fibrous root system and creeping growth habit.

Lysimachia filifolia is often used in ornamental gardening for its delicate appearance and ability to spread easily in suitable conditions.
