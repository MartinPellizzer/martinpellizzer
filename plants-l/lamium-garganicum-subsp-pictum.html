Lamium garganicum subsp. pictum

Lamium garganicum subsp.

pictum, commonly known as the spotted dead-nettle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to central and southern Europe. It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves with purple markings and its clusters of tubular, pinkish-white flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.

Its flowering period occurs from spring to early summer, making it a notable component of early season floral ecosystems.
