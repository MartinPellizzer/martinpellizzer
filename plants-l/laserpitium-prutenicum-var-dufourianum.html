Laserpitium prutenicum var. dufourianum

Laserpitium prutenicum var. dufourianum, commonly known as the Dufour's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It produces small, wind-dispersed seeds and is often found in disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
