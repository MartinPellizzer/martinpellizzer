Lechea leggettii var. intermedia

Lechea leggettii var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate milkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring and early summer. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.

This species plays an important role in supporting local pollinators, including butterflies and bees.
