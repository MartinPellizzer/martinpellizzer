Laelia lucasiana

Laelia lucasiana, commonly known as the Lucas's orchid, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of South America, particularly in Ecuador and Peru.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its sympodial growth habit and the presence of pseudobulbs. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, with a distinctive lip that is often darker. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil for optimal growth.

Laelia lucasiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
