Lythrum arnhemicum

Lythrum arnhemicum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lythrum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Lythraceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and showy spikes of pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along stream banks, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a reddish or purple tinge on the underside.

Lythrum arnhemicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
