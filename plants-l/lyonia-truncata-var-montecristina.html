Lyonia truncata var. montecristina

Lyonia truncata var. montecristina, commonly known as the Montecristi lyonia, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen shrub form. The plant features small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy, with a truncate or notched leaf tip. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, clustered in dense, rounded inflorescences, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats.
