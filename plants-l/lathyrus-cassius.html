Lathyrus cassius

Lathyrus cassius, commonly known as the Cassius pea, is a perennial climbing legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, twining stems and heart-shaped, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces showy, pea-like flowers that range in color from violet to white, often with a distinctive purple throat. Its fruit is a slender pod containing several elliptical seeds.

Lathyrus cassius is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
