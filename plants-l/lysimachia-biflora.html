Lysimachia biflora

Lysimachia biflora, commonly known as the twin-flowered yellow loosestrife, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in pairs along the stems. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is often found in moist soils.

Lysimachia biflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
