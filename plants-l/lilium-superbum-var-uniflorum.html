Lilium superbum var. uniflorum

Lilium superbum var. uniflorum, commonly known as the single-flower tiger lily, is a variant of the showy lady's slipper lily.

It is native to the eastern United States, particularly in forested areas and along stream banks. The plant features tall, erect stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its most distinguishing characteristic is the single, striking orange-red flower with a prominent yellow throat.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
