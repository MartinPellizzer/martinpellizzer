Luzula effusa var. chinensis

Luzula effusa var. chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese woodrush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Poaceae and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, greenish flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices.

Luzula effusa var. chinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its ability to form compact, attractive clumps.
