Laelia sanguiloba

Laelia sanguiloba, commonly known as the blood orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, dark red to purple flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often on rock faces or in shaded forest areas. Its leaves are long, narrow, and grass-like, and it produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

Laelia sanguiloba is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
