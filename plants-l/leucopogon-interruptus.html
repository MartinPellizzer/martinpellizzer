Leucopogon interruptus

Leucopogon interruptus, commonly known as the interrupted heath, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heaths and heathers, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils in open woodlands and sandy habitats. Its flowers, which are usually pink or white, bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Leucopogon interruptus is often found in association with other ericaceous plants in the understory of pine and hardwood forests.
