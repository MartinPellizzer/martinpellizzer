Liparis persimilis

Liparis persimilis, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, grass-like leaves and small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellowish with a distinctive lip that is narrower than the other petals.

Liparis persimilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with appropriate conditions.
