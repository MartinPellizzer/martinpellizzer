Litophila

Litophila, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

These plants are typically found in rocky or coastal environments, where they thrive in well-drained soils. Litophila species are characterized by their compact growth habit and showy, often fragrant flowers. They are native to regions such as southern Africa, Australia, and parts of Southeast Asia.

The genus includes several species, each adapted to specific ecological niches, making them an interesting subject for botanical study.
