Luma apiculata

Luma apiculata, commonly known as the Chilean firetree, is a flowering plant native to Chile and Argentina.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and dry forests. The plant produces striking red, orange, or yellow flowers that bloom in spring and summer. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, adapted to the arid conditions of its native habitat.

Luma apiculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
