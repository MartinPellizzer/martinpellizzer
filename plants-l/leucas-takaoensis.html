Leucas takaoensis

Leucas takaoensis, commonly known as the Takao leucas, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky areas.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
