Lilium leichtlinii f. pseudotigrinum

Lilium leichtlinii f. pseudotigrinum, commonly known as the tigroid lily, is a rare and striking variant of the Leichtlin's lily.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tiger-like pattern on its petals, which features bold, contrasting streaks and spots. This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is often grown in specialized gardens due to its unique appearance. It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is native to specific regions in Central Asia.

The plant typically blooms in late spring or early summer, producing showy, fragrant flowers that attract pollinators.
