Linum heterostylum

Linum heterostylum, commonly known as the narrowleaf flax, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are linear and often have a hairy texture, while the seeds are small and oval in shape.

Linum heterostylum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in textile production.
