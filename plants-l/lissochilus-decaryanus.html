Lissochilus decaryanus

Lissochilus decaryanus, commonly known as the Decary's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually elongated and grass-like, while the flowers are borne on lateral inflorescences.

This species is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
