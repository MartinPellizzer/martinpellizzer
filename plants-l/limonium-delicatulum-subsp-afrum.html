Limonium delicatulum subsp. afrum

Limonium delicatulum subsp.

afrum, commonly known as African sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers.

The plant thrives in well-drained sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or inland dune environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators.
