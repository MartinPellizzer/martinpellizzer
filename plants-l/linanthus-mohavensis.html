Linanthus mohavensis

Linanthus mohavensis, commonly known as Mohave flax, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils in arid environments, showcasing its adaptation to drought conditions. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and it plays a role in stabilizing desert soils.

Mohave flax is often found in scrublands and along riverbanks in its native range.
