Lepanthes schizocardia

Lepanthes schizocardia, commonly known as the split-heart orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale green with intricate markings. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without deriving nutrients from them. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Its unique floral structure and coloration make it a notable species among orchid enthusiasts.
