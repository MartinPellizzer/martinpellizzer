Lycopodium novae-zelandicum

Lycopodium novae-zelandicum, commonly known as the New Zealand clubmoss, is a small, evergreen plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae and is characterized by its spore-bearing cones and needle-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. It plays a role in the ecological balance of its habitat by providing shelter and food for various organisms.

Despite its small size, it is an important indicator species for the health of native ecosystems.
