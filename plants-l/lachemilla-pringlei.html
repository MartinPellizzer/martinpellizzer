Lachemilla pringlei

Lachemilla pringlei, commonly known as the Pringle's cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early spring, accompanied by narrow, elongated leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
