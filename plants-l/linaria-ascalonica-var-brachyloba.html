Linaria ascalonica var. brachyloba

Linaria ascalonica var. brachyloba, commonly known as the short-stemmed toadflax, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that are often branched and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers with two lips, typical of the Plantaginaceae family. Its leaves are linear to lanceolate, with a slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
