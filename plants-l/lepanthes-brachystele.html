Lepanthes brachystele

Lepanthes brachystele, commonly known as the short-stemmed urn orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America, particularly found in humid tropical forests. The plant typically grows from 10 to 30 centimeters in height, with a compact, often leafless stem. Its flowers are small, usually white or pale yellow, and are arranged in a loose inflorescence.

The species is characterized by its unique, urn-shaped petals and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
