Lodicularia laxa

Lodicularia laxa, commonly known as the slender waterplantain, is a perennial aquatic plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater habitats such as ponds, streams, and wetlands. The plant has narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are usually floating or semi-submerged, with a smooth, waxy surface. Its small, greenish-white flowers appear in clusters and are adapted for wind pollination.

Lodicularia laxa plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and amphibians.
