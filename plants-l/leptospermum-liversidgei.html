Leptospermum liversidgei

Leptospermum liversidgei, commonly known as the Liversidge's tea tree, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the myrtle family (Myrtaceae) and is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal or sandplain habitats, preferring well-drained soils. It is a shrub that can reach up to 2 meters in height, with a dense, compact growth habit.

The species is named after the botanist A. Liversidge, who contributed significantly to the study of Australian flora.
