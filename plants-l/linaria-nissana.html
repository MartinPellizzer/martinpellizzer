Linaria nissana

Linaria nissana, commonly known as the yellow toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and bright yellow, spurred flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation. Linaria nissana is often found in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
