Lysimachia candida var. microphylla

Lysimachia candida var. microphylla, commonly known as white wood sorrel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine white hairs, giving the plant a soft, fuzzy appearance. The flowers are usually pale yellow or white, with five petals and a distinctive central stamen. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in gardens for its delicate foliage and blooming habit.
