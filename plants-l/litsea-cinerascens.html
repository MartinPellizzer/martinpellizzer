Litsea cinerascens

Litsea cinerascens, commonly known as the silver flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its smooth, light gray bark and pinnately compound leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible berries that are often consumed by birds and small mammals.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
