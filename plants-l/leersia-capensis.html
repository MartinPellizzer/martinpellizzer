Leersia capensis

Leersia capensis, commonly known as the African ricegrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with pale green to brown spikelets, which are typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along waterways.

Leersia capensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.
