Linaria mamorensis

Linaria mamorensis, commonly known as the Mamore flaxflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the plantain family, Plantaginaceae, and is characterized by its striking, spurred flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its slender stems and linear leaves contribute to its distinctive appearance, while its nectar-rich flowers attract various pollinators.

Linaria mamorensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its native environment.
