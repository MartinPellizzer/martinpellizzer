Laurus coruscans

Laurus coruscans, commonly known as the golden laurel, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in umbels. Its fruits are small, round drupes that turn red when mature.

Laurus coruscans is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
