Lespedeza × manniana

Lespedeza × manniana, commonly known as the Chinese clover, is a hybrid shrub native to East Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It typically grows in a variety of habitats, including woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Lespedeza × manniana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and flowering display.
