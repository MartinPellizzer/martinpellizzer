Licuala confusa

Licuala confusa, commonly known as the giant pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its large, pinnately compound leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant belongs to the Arecaceae family, which includes palms, and is often found in coastal and wetland habitats. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and as thatching material.

The plant also produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by fibrous, coconut-like fruits.
