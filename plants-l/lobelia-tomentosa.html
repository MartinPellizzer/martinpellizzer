Lobelia tomentosa

Lobelia tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish foliage and clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland environments and thrives in partial to full shade. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces showy blooms from late spring to early summer.

Lobelia tomentosa is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
