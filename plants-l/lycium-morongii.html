Lycium morongii

Lycium morongii, commonly known as the Morong boxthorn, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its woody stems and spiny leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along coastal areas.

Lycium morongii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
