Lupinus velutinus

Lupinus velutinus, commonly known as the velvet lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of purple or blue flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, hairy leaflets, giving it a velvety texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Lupinus velutinus is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
