Luma estrellensis

Luma estrellensis, commonly known as the Estrella guava, is a small evergreen tree native to the Andes mountains of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant white flowers and edible, sweet fruits. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in cloud forests at high elevations. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a glossy surface, while its bark is smooth and reddish in color.

Luma estrellensis is valued not only for its ornamental qualities but also for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
