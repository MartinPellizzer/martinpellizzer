Lepanthes ferax

Lepanthes ferax, commonly known as the crimson lady orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically feature vibrant red or orange hues with intricate patterns. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its delicate, epiphytic growth habit, often growing on trees or rocks in humid environments. Its flowers are short-lived but highly attractive to pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

Lepanthes ferax is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in warm, moist climates.
