Laserpitium canescens

Laserpitium canescens, commonly known as the hoary gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy, grayish leaves that give it a distinctive appearance, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in alpine meadows and rocky areas, where it contributes to the biodiversity of its habitat.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
