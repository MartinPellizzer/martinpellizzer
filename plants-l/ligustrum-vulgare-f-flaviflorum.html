Ligustrum vulgare f. flaviflorum

Ligustrum vulgare f. flaviflorum, commonly known as the yellowflower privet, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to southern Europe.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers in the spring, which are followed by dark blue berries. It is often used in landscaping for its dense foliage and adaptability to various soil types.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and as a hedge plant.
