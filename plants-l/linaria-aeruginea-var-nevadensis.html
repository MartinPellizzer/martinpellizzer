Linaria aeruginea var. nevadensis

Linaria aeruginea var. nevadensis, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada toadflax, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of California.

It features slender, upright stems and produces clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers with four petals. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high elevations. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators in its native habitat.
