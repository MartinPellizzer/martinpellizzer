Lycopodium berggrenii

Lycopodium berggrenii, commonly known as the Berggren's clubmoss, is a small, evergreen plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and needle-like leaves that form dense, mat-like structures. Native to the moist, shaded environments of the Pacific Northwest, this species thrives in cool, humid climates. The plant produces spore-bearing cones, which are clustered at the tips of its branches.

Lycopodium berggrenii plays a significant role in forest ecosystems by contributing to soil stability and providing habitat for various microorganisms.
