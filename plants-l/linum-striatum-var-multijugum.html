Linum striatum var. multijugum

Linum striatum var. multijugum, commonly known as the multijugum flax, is a variant of the flax species characterized by its unique morphological features.

It is a herbaceous plant with slender, upright stems and narrow, linear leaves. The flowers are typically blue or purple, with five petals arranged in a showy, spurred shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in temperate regions.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and historical use in fiber production.
