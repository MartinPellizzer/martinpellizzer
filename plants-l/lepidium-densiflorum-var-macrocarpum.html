Lepidium densiflorum var. macrocarpum

Lepidium densiflorum var. macrocarpum, commonly known as the large-seeded wallcress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, branched inflorescences and small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or disturbed soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. Its leaves are pinnately divided, and the fruit is a short, cylindrical silique containing several seeds.

This variety is notable for its robust growth and adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
