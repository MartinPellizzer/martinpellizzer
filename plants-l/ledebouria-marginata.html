Ledebouria marginata

Ledebouria marginata, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a yellow or orange center. The plant has fleshy, strap-like leaves that are typically green with red or purple margins. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
