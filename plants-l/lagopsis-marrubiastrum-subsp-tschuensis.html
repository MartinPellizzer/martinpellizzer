Lagopsis marrubiastrum subsp. tschuensis

Lagopsis marrubiastrum subsp.

tschuensis, commonly known as the Chinese lagopsis, is a flowering plant native to central and eastern China. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the summer months.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
