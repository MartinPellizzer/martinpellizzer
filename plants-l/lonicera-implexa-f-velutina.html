Lonicera implexa f. velutina

Lonicera implexa f. velutina, commonly known as the velvet honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, velvety foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, appearing in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture with a slightly waxy feel.

The plant is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
