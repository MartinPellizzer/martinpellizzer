Lycium grevilleanum

Lycium grevilleanum, commonly known as the Greville's boxthorn, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible but should be consumed in moderation. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and coastal areas.

Lycium grevilleanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
