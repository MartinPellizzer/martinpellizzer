Lycaste lasioglossa

Lycaste lasioglossa, commonly known as the golden lycaste, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically feature a yellow or orange coloration with intricate patterns. The plant has pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and leathery, providing structural support and aiding in photosynthesis.

Lycaste lasioglossa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in the right conditions.
