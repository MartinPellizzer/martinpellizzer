Lupinus cusickii subsp. brachypodus

Lupinus cusickii subsp.

brachypodus, commonly known as the shortpod lupine, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland habitats. Lupinus cusickii subsp.

brachypodus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.
