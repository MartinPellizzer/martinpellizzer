Lotus corniculatus subsp. major

Lotus corniculatus subsp.

major, commonly known as the greater bird's-foot trefoil, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of up to 30 centimeters.

The plant features trifoliate leaves with a distinctive bird's-foot shape, and its flowers are yellow with a distinctive central vein. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands, meadows, and along roadsides.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
