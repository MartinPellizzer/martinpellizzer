Lycium marlothii

Lycium marlothii, commonly known as the Marloth's boxthorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to harsh conditions.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the Lycium genus, with unique ecological and medicinal significance.
