Liparis stricklandiana var. longibracteata

Liparis stricklandiana var. longibracteata, commonly known as the long-bract orchid, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated bracts that give it its specific epithet, and it typically grows in moist, shaded environments. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
