Lumbricidia legalis

Lumbricidia legalis, commonly known as the legal vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is often found in moist, shaded environments. Its fruit is a fleshy capsule that contains numerous seeds, which are dispersed by animals.

Lumbricidia legalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
