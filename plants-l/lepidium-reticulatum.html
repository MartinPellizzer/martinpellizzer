Lepidium reticulatum

Lepidium reticulatum, commonly known as the nettleleaf rocket, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It typically grows in moist, temperate environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

Lepidium reticulatum is valued for its peppery flavor and is sometimes used in culinary applications.
