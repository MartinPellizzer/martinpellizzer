Logfia paradoxa

Logfia paradoxa, commonly known as the paradoxical logfia, is a rare and enigmatic plant species native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its striking, iridescent leaves that change color with the time of day, a phenomenon attributed to its unique pigmentation. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and requires specific soil conditions rich in organic matter. Its flowers, which bloom only during the winter months, are small but highly fragrant, attracting a variety of pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Logfia paradoxa is considered a conservation concern.
