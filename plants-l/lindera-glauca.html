Lindera glauca

Lindera glauca, commonly known as glossy privet, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are typically a glossy green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by yellowish-orange berries in the fall. It thrives in partial to full sun and prefers well-drained soils.

Lindera glauca is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
