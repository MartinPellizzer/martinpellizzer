Lindera pulcherrima

Lindera pulcherrima, commonly known as the fragrant sumac, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that are opposite and often have a fragrant aroma when crushed. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring, which are followed by orange-red berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodlands and stream banks.

Lindera pulcherrima is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
