Lockhartia variabilis

Lockhartia variabilis, commonly known as the variable orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often colorful flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually elongated and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

Lockhartia variabilis is appreciated for its ornamental value and is cultivated in greenhouses and gardens worldwide.
