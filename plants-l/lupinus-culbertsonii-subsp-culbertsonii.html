Lupinus culbertsonii subsp. culbertsonii

Lupinus culbertsonii subsp.

culbertsonii, commonly known as the Culbertson's lupine, is a member of the legume family Fabaceae. It is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States, particularly found in Arizona and New Mexico.

The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, exhibiting a low, sprawling growth habit. Its flowers are usually blue to purple, arranged in dense spikes, and are adapted for pollination by bees and other insects.

The species is notable for its ecological importance in stabilizing soils and supporting local pollinator populations.
