Lonicera biflora var. brachytricha

Lonicera biflora var. brachytricha, commonly known as the short-stalked honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually yellow or white, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that ripens in the fall and is attractive to birds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
