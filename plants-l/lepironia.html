Lepironia

Lepironia, commonly known as the sacred lotus, is a genus of aquatic plants native to Southeast Asia.

It features showy, pink or white flowers that bloom above the water surface, while its leaves are broad and heart-shaped. The plant thrives in shallow, still or slow-moving freshwater habitats. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine, and it plays a significant role in cultural and religious practices.

Lepironia is also valued for its ornamental appeal in water gardens.
