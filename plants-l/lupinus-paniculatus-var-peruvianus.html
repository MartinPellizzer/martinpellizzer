Lupinus paniculatus var. peruvianus

Lupinus paniculatus var. peruvianus, commonly known as the Peruvian lupine, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of pea-like flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces cylindrical seed pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or mountainous habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.
