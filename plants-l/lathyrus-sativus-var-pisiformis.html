Lathyrus sativus var. pisiformis

Lathyrus sativus var. pisiformis, commonly known as the sweet pea, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with slender stems that twine around supports. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pea-like flowers in a range of colors, including pink, red, and white. Its leaves are pinnate, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The seeds are enclosed in pods and are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some cultures.
