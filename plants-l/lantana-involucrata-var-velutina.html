Lantana involucrata var. velutina

Lantana involucrata var. velutina, commonly known as velvet lantana, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and orange. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically eaten by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Velvet lantana is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
