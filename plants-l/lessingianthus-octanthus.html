Lessingianthus octanthus

Lessingianthus octanthus, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant purple petals and spiky yellow center. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats and produces small, seed-filled cones at the center of its flowers. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
