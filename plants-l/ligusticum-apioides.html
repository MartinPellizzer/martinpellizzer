Ligusticum apioides

Ligusticum apioides, commonly known as the lesser calamint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes plants like parsley and carrots, and is characterized by its aromatic, often fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the family. Its roots and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.

Ligusticum apioides thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
