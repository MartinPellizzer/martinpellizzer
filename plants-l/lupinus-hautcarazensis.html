Lupinus hautcarazensis

Lupinus hautcarazensis, commonly known as the Hautcaraz lupine, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of vibrant blue or purple flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces legume pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to grow in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in high-altitude environments.

Lupinus hautcarazensis plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
