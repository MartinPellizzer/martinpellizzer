Liparis clypeolum subsp. cuspidata

Liparis clypeolum subsp.

cuspidata, commonly known as the horned orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, horn-like floral structures. It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions such as Indonesia and the Philippines.

The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or terrestrial herb, often in moist, shaded forest environments. Its flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

The subspecies is noted for its unique morphology and ecological adaptations, making it a subject of interest in botanical research.
