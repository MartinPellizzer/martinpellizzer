Leptohyptis

Leptohyptis, commonly known as the "false basil" or "scented hyptis," is a genus of flowering plants in the Lamiaceae family.

These plants are native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, particularly in Central and South America. Leptohyptis species are characterized by their aromatic leaves and showy, often violet or blue flowers. They typically grow as shrubs or herbs, often found in moist, shaded environments.

The genus includes several species, some of which are used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
