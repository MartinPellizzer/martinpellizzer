Labatia macrocarpa

Labatia macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited labatia, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its fruit is a large, fleshy capsule that splits open to release numerous seeds.

Labatia macrocarpa is primarily valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and greenhouses.
