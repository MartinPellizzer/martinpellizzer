Loranthus ambiguus

Loranthus ambiguus, commonly known as the mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily in the genus Eucalyptus.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while also producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using light absorbed by the host's leaves. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers that are often inconspicuous, followed by fleshy, berry-like fruits that attract birds for seed dispersal. Its parasitic nature makes it a significant ecological factor in forest ecosystems, influencing host tree growth and biodiversity.

Loranthus ambiguus is also culturally significant, often associated with festive traditions and symbolic meanings.
