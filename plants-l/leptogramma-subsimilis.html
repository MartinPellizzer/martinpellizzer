Leptogramma subsimilis

Leptogramma subsimilis, commonly known as the similar leptogramma, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its small, delicate fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant exhibits a rhizomatous growth habit, with creeping rhizomes that allow it to spread along the forest floor. Its leaves are pinnately divided, with a serrated margin and a pale green coloration.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
