Lonicera aureoreticulata

Lonicera aureoreticulata, commonly known as the golden honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy underside and produces red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Lonicera aureoreticulata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
