Lathyrus latifolius subsp. algericus

Lathyrus latifolius subsp.

algericus, commonly known as Algerian broad-leafed vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of pea-like flowers that range in color from pink to purple.

The plant produces elongated, cylindrical pods containing small, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or disturbed habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial in ecological restoration projects.
