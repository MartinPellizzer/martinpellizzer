Limonium confusum subsp. densissimum

Limonium confusum subsp.

densissimum, commonly known as the denseflower sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Europe and northern Africa. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dunes or along the shoreline. Its leaves are linear and succulent, adapted to withstand salty air and strong winds.

This subspecies is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate coastal conditions.
