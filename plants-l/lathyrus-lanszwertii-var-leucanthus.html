Lathyrus lanszwertii var. leucanthus

Lathyrus lanszwertii var. leucanthus, commonly known as the white-flowered sweet pea, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe.

It produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features slender, climbing stems with compound leaves consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are typically borne in racemes and are attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
