Leschenaultia agrostophylla

Leschenaultia agrostophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf leschenaultia, is a species of flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, yellowish-green flowers appear in clusters and bloom during the spring and early summer.

Leschenaultia agrostophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
