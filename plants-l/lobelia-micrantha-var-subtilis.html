Lobelia micrantha var. subtilis

Lobelia micrantha var. subtilis, commonly known as the slender lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It is often found in rich, loamy soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in garden settings.

The species is also noted for its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
