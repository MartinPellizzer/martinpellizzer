Lycium pubitubum

Lycium pubitubum, commonly known as the hairy boxthorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces red berries that are edible for humans and birds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Lycium pubitubum has been used traditionally in local medicine for its purported health benefits.
