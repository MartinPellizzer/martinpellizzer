Lachnagrostis retrofracta

Lachnagrostis retrofracta, commonly known as the narrowleaf wheatgrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is typically found in dry, sandy or gravelly soils in arid and semi-arid regions.

Lachnagrostis retrofracta is often used for erosion control and as a forage plant for livestock.
