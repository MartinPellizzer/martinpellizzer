Lycopodium acerosum var. filiforme

Lycopodium acerosum var. filiforme, commonly known as the slender clubmoss, is a perennial evergreen plant native to the Appalachian region of the United States.

It belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae and is characterized by its small, spore-bearing cones and needle-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its foliage forms dense mats, and it plays a role in soil stabilization and habitat provision for microorganisms.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in forest ecosystems.
