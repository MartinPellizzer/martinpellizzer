Lysimachia elliptica

Lysimachia elliptica, commonly known as the narrowleaf chickweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically less than 2 centimeters in length, giving it a compact appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Lysimachia elliptica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in garden settings for its delicate foliage and flowering habit.
