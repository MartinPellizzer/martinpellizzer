Lycopodium fuertesii

Lycopodium fuertesii, commonly known as the Fuertes' clubmoss, is a species of lycophyte belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features slender, branching stems covered with tiny, scale-like leaves. Its spore-producing structures, called strobili, are clustered at the tips of the branches.

Lycopodium fuertesii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
