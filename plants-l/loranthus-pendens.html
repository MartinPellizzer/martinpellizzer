Loranthus pendens

Loranthus pendens, commonly known as the hanging mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using the light it captures. The plant has small, scale-like leaves and produces small, white or pink flowers that attract insects for pollination. Its berries are typically red or orange and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This species is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in a pendulous manner from its host.
