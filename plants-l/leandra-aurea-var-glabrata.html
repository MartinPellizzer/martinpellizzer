Leandra aurea var. glabrata

Leandra aurea var. glabrata, commonly known as the golden leandra, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, golden-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a vibrant touch to its natural habitat. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a lush backdrop to its showy blooms.

This plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests or along riverbanks.
