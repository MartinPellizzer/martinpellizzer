Linum julicum

Linum julicum, commonly known as the summer flax, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender stems and delicate blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. Linum julicum is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
