Lyginia barbata

Lyginia barbata, commonly known as the slender arrowhead, is a semi-aquatic perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are slightly notched and often have a velvety texture. Its small, white flowers appear in late spring and are clustered in spikes, contributing to its ornamental value.

Lyginia barbata is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and improve water quality in wetland ecosystems.
