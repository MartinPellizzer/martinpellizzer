Lonicera gracilipes f. albiflora

Lonicera gracilipes f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered honeysuckle, is a variant of the slender honeysuckle.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its slender, twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are predominantly white, blooming in early spring. Its fruit is a small, red berry that matures in late summer.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its delicate floral display.
