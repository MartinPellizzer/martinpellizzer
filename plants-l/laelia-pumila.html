Laelia pumila

Laelia pumila, commonly known as the dwarf laelia, is a species of orchid native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils at high altitudes. The plant produces compact, pseudobulbous stems with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are usually small and white or pale green, with a distinctive spurred lip.

Laelia pumila is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
