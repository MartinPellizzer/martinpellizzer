Lomatia ilicifolia var. axillaris

Lomatia ilicifolia var. axillaris, commonly known as the narrow-leafed lomatia, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with narrow, leathery leaves that have a distinctive, prickly texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences, typically in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal heathlands and sandplain areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is a species of interest in both horticulture and conservation efforts.
