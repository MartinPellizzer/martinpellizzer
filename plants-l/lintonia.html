Lintonia

Lintonia, commonly known as the silverleaf lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white flowers during the spring and early summer. Lintonia thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate foliage and prolonged blooming period.
