Lupinus luteus var. leucospermus

Lupinus luteus var. leucospermus, commonly known as the white-flowered lupin, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It features upright, branching stems covered in soft, hairy foliage. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that range from white to pale yellow, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system forms nitrogen-fixing nodules, enhancing soil fertility.

This variety is valued in ecological restoration and ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
