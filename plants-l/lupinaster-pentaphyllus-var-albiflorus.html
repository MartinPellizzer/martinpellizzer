Lupinaster pentaphyllus var. albiflorus

Lupinaster pentaphyllus var. albiflorus, commonly known as the white-flowered lupinaster, is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits.

It is native to southern Africa, where it thrives in sandy or rocky soils. The plant features showy, clustered white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
