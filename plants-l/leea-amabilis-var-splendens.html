Leea amabilis var. splendens

Leea amabilis var. splendens, commonly known as the splendour leea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and showy, fragrant yellow flowers. The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests or along riverbanks.

Its botanical features include smooth, glabrous bark and small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.
