Lagerstroemia caudata

Lagerstroemia caudata, commonly known as the Chinese dogwood, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, pink or red bract-based inflorescences that appear in late spring to early summer. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate to elliptic leaves that are typically dark green and turn reddish in autumn. Its flowers are actually small, pale yellow to white flowers that are clustered within the colorful bracts.

Lagerstroemia caudata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant foliage and long-lasting floral display.
