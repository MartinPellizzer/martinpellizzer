Lepidium lasiocarpum subsp. georginum

Lepidium lasiocarpum subsp.

georginum, commonly known as the woolly cress, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes, followed by distinctive, inflated, greenish-brown pods. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and botanical gardens.
