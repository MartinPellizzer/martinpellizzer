Lysipomia muscoides subsp. delicatula

Lysipomia muscoides subsp.

delicatula, commonly known as the slender hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America. It is characterized by its delicate, hair-like leaves and small, clustered inflorescences.

This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as wetlands and forest floors. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense mats, which help stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

The plant's botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
