Ligularia sibirica subsp. abakanica

Ligularia sibirica subsp.

abakanica, commonly known as the Siberian ligularia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Eastern Siberia. It features large, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive greenish-white underside, which are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a dense, terminal inflorescence, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forested areas along riverbanks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental foliage and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with high humidity.
