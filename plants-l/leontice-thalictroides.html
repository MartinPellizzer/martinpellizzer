Leontice thalictroides

Leontice thalictroides, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in moist, rich soils and is often found in woodland habitats.

Leontice thalictroides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for early pollinators.
