Lepanthes sinuosa

Lepanthes sinuosa, commonly known as the sinuous orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, greenish-white flowers with intricate patterns and a distinctive, recurved lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, tropical environments. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a rosette, aiding in water retention.

Lepanthes sinuosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its native habitats.
