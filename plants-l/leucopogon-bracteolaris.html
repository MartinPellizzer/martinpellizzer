Leucopogon bracteolaris

Leucopogon bracteolaris, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in heathlands and open forests, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features narrow, leathery leaves and produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters. It is part of the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries.

Leucopogon bracteolaris is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
