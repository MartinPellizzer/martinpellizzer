Lycium odonellii

Lycium odonellii, commonly known as the Odonell's boxthorn, is a shrub native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible for both humans and animals. Lycium odonellii is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils and can tolerate extreme temperature fluctuations.

Its botanical features make it an important species in desert ecosystems.
