Lotononis pumila var. micrantha

Lotononis pumila var. micrantha, commonly known as the dwarf African daisy, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features small, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center, blooming throughout the warmer months. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, helping it conserve water in arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its compact size and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
