Lepidium dioscaridis

Lepidium dioscaridis, commonly known as the Sinai cress, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant is characterized by its small, white or yellow flowers and pinnately divided leaves. It thrives in sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Lepidium dioscaridis is often found in rocky or coastal areas, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
