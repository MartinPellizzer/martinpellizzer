Lasianthus filipedunculatus

Lasianthus filipedunculatus, commonly known as the thread-stemmed lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, thread-like stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The flowers are usually pink or purple, adding ornamental value to its natural habitat.
