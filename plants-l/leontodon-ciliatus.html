Leontodon ciliatus

Leontodon ciliatus, commonly known as the hairy leontodon, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the daisy family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its distinctive, hairy leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. The species is often found in open habitats and is relatively hardy, though it is not commonly cultivated for ornamental purposes.
