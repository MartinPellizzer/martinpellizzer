Lespedeza veitchii

Lespedeza veitchii, commonly known as Veitch's lespeadeza, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands. It produces seed pods that contain several small, elliptical seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Lespedeza veitchii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
