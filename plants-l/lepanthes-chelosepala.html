Lepanthes chelosepala

Lepanthes chelosepala, commonly known as the pink-stemmed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually elongated and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in photosynthesis.

Lepanthes chelosepala thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.
