Linnaea borealis f. decora

Linnaea borealis f. decora, commonly known as the decorated twinflower, is a rare and beautiful flowering plant native to the boreal forests of North America.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its delicate, twin-like flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive, spurred shape that aids in pollination by insects.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in shaded gardens for its unique appearance.
