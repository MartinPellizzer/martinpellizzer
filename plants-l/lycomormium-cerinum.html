Lycomormium cerinum

Lycomormium cerinum, commonly known as the candleplant, is a unique succulent native to South Africa.

It features a cylindrical, candle-like stem covered in a waxy, white coating that helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, green, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are thick and fleshy, adapted for storing water in arid environments.

Lycomormium cerinum is often cultivated for its unusual appearance and low maintenance requirements.
