Lasianthus longicaudus

Lasianthus longicaudus, commonly known as the long-tailed flame vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and a woody stem, typical of many species in the Acanthaceae family. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing along riverbanks or in moist, shaded areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and green spaces.
