Lathyrus annuus subsp. cassius

Lathyrus annuus subsp.

cassius, commonly known as the Cassius vetch, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical pea-like flowers and compound leaves.

The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by elongated, bean-like pods containing several seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in sunny, well-drained environments.

Its botanical features make it a useful plant for soil stabilization and as a forage crop in certain regions.
