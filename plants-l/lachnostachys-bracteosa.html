Lachnostachys bracteosa

Lachnostachys bracteosa, commonly known as the bracteosa grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically clustered at the base of the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or rocky habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as a ornamental or for erosion control in suitable environments.
