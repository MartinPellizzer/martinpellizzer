Lyonia marginata var. rubra

Lyonia marginata var. rubra, commonly known as the redleaf lyonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, acidic soils, often found in wetlands and along stream banks. The plant features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. Its leaves are distinctive, with a red-tinged margin that gives it its common name.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
