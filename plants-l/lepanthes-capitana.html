Lepanthes capitana

Lepanthes capitana, commonly known as the hooded orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, pendant-like flowers that often have a distinctive hooded shape. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments, often epiphytically on trees. Its flowers are usually small, fragrant, and come in a range of colors including white, yellow, and pink.

Lepanthes capitana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
