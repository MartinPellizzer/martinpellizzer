Lonicera myrtilloides

Lonicera myrtilloides, commonly known as the blue honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, which are usually white or pale yellow, followed by edible blue-black berries. Lonicera myrtilloides thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or streambanks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and wildlife.
