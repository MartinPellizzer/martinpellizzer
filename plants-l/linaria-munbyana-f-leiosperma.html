Linaria munbyana f. leiosperma

Linaria munbyana f. leiosperma, commonly known as the smooth-flowered toadflax, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellow-orange flowers. The leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in a fine, silky hair that gives the plant its "leiosperma" designation, meaning "smooth seed-bearing.". This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often grown in gardens for its delicate, cascading blooms.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a bedding plant.
