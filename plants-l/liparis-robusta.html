Liparis robusta

Liparis robusta, commonly known as the robust orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its erect, leafy stems and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and shrublands. Its leaves are usually elongated and grass-like, and it produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

Liparis robusta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
