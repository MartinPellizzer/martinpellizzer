Leucadendron sphaerocephalum

Leucadendron sphaerocephalum, commonly known as the silver coin bush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage and spherical, yellow-orange flower heads that resemble small coins. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping. Its compact growth habit and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

The species is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
