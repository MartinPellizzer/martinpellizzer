Lotus creticus var. bonifaciensis

Lotus creticus var. bonifaciensis, commonly known as the Cretan lotus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves with a distinctive serrated margin. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Its historical and cultural significance adds to its botanical interest.
