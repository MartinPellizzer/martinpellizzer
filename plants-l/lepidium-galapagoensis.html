Lepidium galapagoensis

Lepidium galapagoensis, commonly known as the Galápagos cress, is a flowering plant native to the Galápagos Islands.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many economically important crops such as mustard and cabbage. The plant is characterized by its small, white or yellow flowers and pinnately divided leaves. It thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to the unique ecological conditions of the Galápagos.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
