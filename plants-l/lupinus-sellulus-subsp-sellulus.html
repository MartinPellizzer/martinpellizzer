Lupinus sellulus subsp. sellulus

Lupinus sellulus subsp.

sellulus, commonly known as the narrowleaf lupine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces spikes of blue to purple flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its root system forms symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, making it valuable for soil enrichment in ecological restoration projects.
