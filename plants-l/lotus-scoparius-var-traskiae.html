Lotus scoparius var. traskiae

Lotus scoparius var. traskiae, commonly known as the Trask's lotus, is a perennial aquatic plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater habitats such as ponds and marshes. The plant features showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are floating or semi-submerged, with a waxy surface that helps repel water.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in wetland ecosystems.
