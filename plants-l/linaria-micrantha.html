Linaria micrantha

Linaria micrantha, commonly known as the narrowleaf toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Linaria micrantha is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
