Linaria virgata f. riffea

Linaria virgata f. riffea, commonly known as the Rif carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Rif Mountains of northern Africa.

It belongs to the plant family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun conditions. It is a perennial herb with slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.
