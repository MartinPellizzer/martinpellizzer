Lobivia fallax

Lobivia fallax, commonly known as the rock lobster cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, fleshy stems that form a compact, globular shape, often resembling a cluster of small lobes. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. It is well adapted to dry environments, with thick, water-storing stems and spiny protection against herbivores.

Lobivia fallax is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and attractive appearance.
