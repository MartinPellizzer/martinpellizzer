Lycaste sebastianii

Lycaste sebastianii, commonly known as the Sebastian's lycaste, is a rare and striking orchid native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water, enabling it to survive in arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts in controlled environments.
