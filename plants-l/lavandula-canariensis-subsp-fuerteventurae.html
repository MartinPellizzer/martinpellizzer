Lavandula canariensis subsp. fuerteventurae

Lavandula canariensis subsp.

fuerteventurae, commonly known as Fuerteventura lavender, is a subspecies native to the Canary Islands. It is a perennial shrub characterized by its aromatic, spike-like inflorescences of purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it well adapted to the arid climate of its native habitat. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its fragrance and visual appeal.
