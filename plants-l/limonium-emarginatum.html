Limonium emarginatum

Limonium emarginatum, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers and its ability to thrive in coastal and saline environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

Limonium emarginatum is also valued for its role in coastal dune stabilization and its historical use in herbal medicine.
