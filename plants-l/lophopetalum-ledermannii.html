Lophopetalum ledermannii

Lophopetalum ledermannii, commonly known as the Ledermann's hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible for wildlife but not typically consumed by humans.

Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
