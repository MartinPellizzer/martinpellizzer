Laurus punctata

Laurus punctata, commonly known as bay laurel, is a evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fragrant, dark green, elliptical leaves that are often used in cooking and traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by spherical, black berries. Its aromatic oils, primarily composed of cineole, are responsible for its distinctive scent and medicinal properties.

Bay laurel is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
