Luffa vittata

Luffa vittata, commonly known as the spotted luffa, is a flowering plant in the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and is characterized by its hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that give way to elongated, fibrous fruits, which are harvested when mature. The fibrous interior of the fruit is used as a natural sponge, while the outer layer can be used as a poultice.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
