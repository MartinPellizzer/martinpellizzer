Lonicera linderifolia

Lonicera linderifolia, commonly known as the Florida honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy on the underside. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in spring. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that ripens in late summer.

Lonicera linderifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
