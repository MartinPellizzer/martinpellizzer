Lamium maculatum var. elatius

Lamium maculatum var. elatius, commonly known as the silver spotted dead nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive silver or white spots, and its flowers are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
