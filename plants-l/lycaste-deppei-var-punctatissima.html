Lycaste deppei var. punctatissima

Lycaste deppei var. punctatissima, commonly known as the spotted lycaste, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as a sympodial orchid, forming pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a compact rosette.

This variety is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
