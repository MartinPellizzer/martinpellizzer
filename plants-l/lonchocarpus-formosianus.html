Lonchocarpus formosianus

Lonchocarpus formosianus, commonly known as the Taiwan legume, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant is notable for its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its seeds are encased in hard, woody pods, which contribute to its ecological role in seed dispersal.
