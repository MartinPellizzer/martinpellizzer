Lobelia bracteata

Lobelia bracteata, commonly known as the bracted lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, tubular blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches and has narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to native gardens.
