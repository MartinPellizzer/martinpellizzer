Luzula divulgata

Luzula divulgata, commonly known as the common wood rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its upright, grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves that are usually dark green in color. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that contain both male and female flowers, typical of grasses. Luzula divulgata is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
