Lindsaea canaliculatipes

Lindsaea canaliculatipes, commonly known as the channel-veined fern, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are often adorned with small, linear channels on the underside of the leaves. Native to tropical regions of Central and South America, this fern thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it to substrates such as tree trunks or rocks.

The plant's unique venation pattern and intricate morphology make it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
