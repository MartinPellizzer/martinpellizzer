Leonurus mexicanus

Leonurus mexicanus, commonly known as Mexican hedgehog, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in whorls along the stems. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance.

Leonurus mexicanus is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
