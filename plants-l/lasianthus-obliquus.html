Lasianthus obliquus

Lasianthus obliquus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf lasianthus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Acanthaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries such as Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. Its flowers are small and clustered in whorls, producing a striking display of purple or pink hues.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
