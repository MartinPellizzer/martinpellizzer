Lappa bardana var. major

Lappa bardana var. major, commonly known as greater lappa, is a biennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, hairy stems. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with large, heart-shaped leaves that are often lobed. Its flowers are yellow and occur in a dense cluster, attracting various pollinators.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
