Lepidium Montanum Subsp. Papilliferum

Lepidium montanum subsp. papilliferum is a lesser-known plant species that thrives in high-altitude environments. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many familiar edible and medicinal plants. This subspecies is characterized by its unique papillate surfaces on its leaves and stems. Due to its limited distribution, it is often overlooked in botanical studies. Understanding this plant can provide valuable insights into alpine flora and ecological adaptation.
