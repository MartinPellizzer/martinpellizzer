Limonium saxicola

Limonium saxicola, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, thriving in well-drained environments. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often branched.

Limonium saxicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and ability to tolerate salt spray.
