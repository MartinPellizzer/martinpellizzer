Leptospermum acerosum

Leptospermum acerosum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tea tree, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and dry forest habitats.

Leptospermum acerosum is valued for its aromatic foliage and historical use in traditional medicine.
