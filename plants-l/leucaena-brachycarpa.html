Leucaena brachycarpa

Leucaena brachycarpa, commonly known as the small-fruited leucaena, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, spherical pods. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in poor soils, making it useful for soil stabilization and as a forage crop.

Its flowers are typically yellow or white, and it produces small, edible seeds that are sometimes used in traditional medicine.
