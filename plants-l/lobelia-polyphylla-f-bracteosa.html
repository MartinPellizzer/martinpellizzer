Lobelia polyphylla f. bracteosa

Lobelia polyphylla f. bracteosa, commonly known as the bracteate lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial characterized by its erect stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a valuable addition to woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate foliage and showy inflorescences.
