Lansium

Lansium, commonly known as the kaffir lime, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, lime-green leaves and small, yellowish-green fruits that resemble limes. The plant belongs to the Rutaceae family and is valued for its aromatic zest and fruit, which are used in both culinary and medicinal applications. Its flowers are white and fragrant, often attracting pollinators like bees.

The tree thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
