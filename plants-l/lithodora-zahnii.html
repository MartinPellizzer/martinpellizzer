Lithodora zahnii

Lithodora zahnii, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, sky-blue to violet flowers. The plant has a mat-forming habit, with creeping stems that root at the nodes. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine, soft hairs.

Lithodora zahnii thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
