Lippia sclerophylla var. loretensis

Lippia sclerophylla var. loretensis, commonly known as Loretto lippia, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. It produces small, four-lobed fruits that are dispersed by wind.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
