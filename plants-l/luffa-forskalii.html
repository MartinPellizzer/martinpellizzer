Luffa forskalii

Luffa forskalii, commonly known as the African sponge gourd, is a tropical climbing vine native to Africa.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other gourds and cucurbits. The plant produces large, fibrous fruits that mature into spongy, lightweight structures used for scrubbing. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, while its flowers are typically yellow with five petals.

The plant thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
