Lavandula elegans

Lavandula elegans, commonly known as English lavender, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, woody stems and narrow, gray-green leaves that are aromatic and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces spikes of fragrant, violet-blue flowers during the summer months. It is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and essential oils used in aromatherapy and cosmetics.

Lavandula elegans thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
