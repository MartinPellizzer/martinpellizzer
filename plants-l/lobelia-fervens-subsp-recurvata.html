Lobelia fervens subsp. recurvata

Lobelia fervens subsp.

recurvata, commonly known as the recurved lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, tubular blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
