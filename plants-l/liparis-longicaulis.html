Liparis longicaulis

Liparis longicaulis, commonly known as the long-stemmed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a slender, wiry rhizome and produces tall, erect stems with lance-shaped leaves. The plant blooms with delicate, greenish-yellow flowers that have three sepals and three petals, typically arranged in a loose raceme. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in horticulture.
