Laelia hispidula

Laelia hispidula, commonly known as the hairy laelia, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and hairy stems, which give it its common name. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange, often with a dark throat. Laelia hispidula thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
