Linum strictum var. laxiflorum

Linum strictum var. laxiflorum, commonly known as the slender flax or laxiflorous flax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while its flowers are clustered in loose inflorescences.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its delicate appearance.
