Lepanthes hortensis

Lepanthes hortensis, commonly known as the orchid of the genus Lepanthes, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. Lepanthes hortensis thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, organic-rich substrates.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and its role in the biodiversity of tropical ecosystems.
