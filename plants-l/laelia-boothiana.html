Laelia boothiana

Laelia boothiana, commonly known as the Booth's orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically blooms in the spring and produces clusters of violet or purple flowers with yellow markings. Laelia boothiana thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soil.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
