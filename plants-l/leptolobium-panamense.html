Leptolobium panamense

Leptolobium panamense, commonly known as the Panamanian sycamore, is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits.

It is native to Central America, particularly Panama, and thrives in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 15 meters. Its leaves are bipinnate, with numerous small leaflets arranged in a feathery pattern.

The flowers are usually clustered in inflorescences and are adapted for pollination by insects.
