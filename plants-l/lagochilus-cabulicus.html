Lagochilus cabulicus

Lagochilus cabulicus, commonly known as Afghan bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to thrive in arid and rocky environments.

Lagochilus cabulicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
