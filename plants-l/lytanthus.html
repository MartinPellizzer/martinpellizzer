Lytanthus

Lytanthus, commonly known as the sweet flag, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Araceae.

It is characterized by its arrow-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or greenish flowers. The plant is native to wetland areas in Asia, Africa, and the Americas. It often grows in marshy or aquatic environments, thriving in moist soil conditions.
