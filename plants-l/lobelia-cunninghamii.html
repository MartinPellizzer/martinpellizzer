Lobelia cunninghamii

Lobelia cunninghamii, commonly known as the Cunningham's lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands and swamps of southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dense clusters and is often found in areas with consistently moist soil. Its flowers bloom in late winter to early spring, attracting a variety of pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Lobelia cunninghamii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with appropriate water conditions.
