Leymus kirghisorum

Leymus kirghisorum, commonly known as the Kirghiz grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and coarse, hairy leaves. This plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as steppes and rocky soils. It has a deep root system, which allows it to thrive in arid conditions.

Leymus kirghisorum plays an important role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems.
