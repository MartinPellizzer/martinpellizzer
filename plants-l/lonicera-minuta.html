Lonicera minuta

Lonicera minuta, commonly known as the small-flowered honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or white, followed by red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in partial to full shade and is often found in moist woodland habitats.

Lonicera minuta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for various wildlife species.
