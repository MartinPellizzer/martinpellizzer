Lippia alba var. carterae

Lippia alba var. carterae, commonly known as white sage, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and small white to pale purple flowers. The plant thrives in dry, well-drained soils and full sun, making it well adapted to arid environments. It is often used in traditional medicine and as a ceremonial plant due to its strong scent and purported healing properties.

Its essential oils contain compounds like thymol and carvacrol, which contribute to its medicinal and aromatic uses.
