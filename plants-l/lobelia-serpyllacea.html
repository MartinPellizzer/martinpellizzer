Lobelia serpyllacea

Lobelia serpyllacea, commonly known as common lobelia, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, arranged in dense spikes. The plant has hairy, linear leaves that are often covered in fine white hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, along stream banks, and in woodland edges.

Lobelia serpyllacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
