Lotus racemosus

Lotus racemosus, commonly known as the sacred lotus, is a herbaceous plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear clusters of fragrant, pale yellow flowers. The plant thrives in wet, marshy environments and is often found near water bodies. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, while its roots are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The lotus is also significant in various cultural and religious contexts.
