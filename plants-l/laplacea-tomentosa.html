Laplacea tomentosa

Laplacea tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly laplacea, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, white, woolly foliage that covers its stems and leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid tropical regions.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
