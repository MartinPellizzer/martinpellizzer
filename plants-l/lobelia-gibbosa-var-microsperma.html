Lobelia gibbosa var. microsperma

Lobelia gibbosa var. microsperma, commonly known as the dwarf lobelia, is a compact, flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, tubular blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. It is a member of the Campanulaceae family, known for its showy, bell-shaped blooms.

This variety is distinguished by its smaller seeds compared to other lobelia species, contributing to its unique botanical classification.
