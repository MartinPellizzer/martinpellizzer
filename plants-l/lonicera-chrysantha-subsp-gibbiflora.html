Lonicera chrysantha subsp. gibbiflora

Lonicera chrysantha subsp.

gibbiflora, commonly known as the yellow honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its fragrant, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that matures in the fall.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and streambanks.
