Lonchocarpus hydrophilus

Lonchocarpus hydrophilus, commonly known as water senna, is a tropical legume native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers, which bloom in clusters. The plant produces distinctive pod fruits that contain flat, oblong seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

Due to its nitrogen-fixing ability, it plays a role in soil enrichment in its natural habitat.
