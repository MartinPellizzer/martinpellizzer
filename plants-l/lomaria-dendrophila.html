Lomaria dendrophila

Lomaria dendrophila, commonly known as the tree lomaria, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties, with various parts used in traditional remedies.
