Liparis nyassana

Liparis nyassana, commonly known as the Nyassa orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to East Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky habitats. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellowish with intricate patterns, and it produces small, round pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

Liparis nyassana is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by supporting pollinators.
