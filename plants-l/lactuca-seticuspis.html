Lactuca seticuspis

Lactuca seticuspis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lettuce, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family, Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and narrow, serrated leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a composite head, which are followed by slender, elongated seed pods. Native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa, it is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists studying drought-resistant plants.
