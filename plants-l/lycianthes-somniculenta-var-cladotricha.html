Lycianthes somniculenta var. cladotricha

Lycianthes somniculenta var. cladotricha, commonly known as the velvetleaf nightshade, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It features broad, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a distinctive, often purple-tinged stem. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into fleshy, orange or red berries containing several seeds. It is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.

Botanically, it belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and peppers.
