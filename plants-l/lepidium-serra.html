Lepidium serra

Lepidium serra, commonly known as the serrated peppergrass, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many economically important crops such as cabbage and mustard. The plant is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, elongated leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, round pods that contain several seeds.

Lepidium serra is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, showcasing its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
