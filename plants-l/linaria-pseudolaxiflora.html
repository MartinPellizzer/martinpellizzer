Linaria pseudolaxiflora

Linaria pseudolaxiflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Linaria pseudolaxiflora is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
