Lolium × elongatum f. aristatum

Lolium × elongatum f. aristatum, commonly known as the tufted ryegrass, is a botanical variant characterized by its distinctive tufts of elongated leaf blades.

This form is a natural hybrid between Lolium perenne and Lolium multiflorum, exhibiting features of both parent species. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and is often found in grasslands and pastures. It is valued for its high forage quality and rapid establishment.

The tufted growth habit makes it easily distinguishable from other ryegrass species.
