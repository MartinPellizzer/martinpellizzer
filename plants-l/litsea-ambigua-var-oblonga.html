Litsea ambigua var. oblonga

Litsea ambigua var. oblonga, commonly known as the broadleaf litsea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Vietnam and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate, and elliptic to ovate leaves that are usually glossy and dark green on top. It produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by oval, woody fruits.

This variant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its aromatic properties.
