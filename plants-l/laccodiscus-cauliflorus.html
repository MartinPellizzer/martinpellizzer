Laccodiscus cauliflorus

Laccodiscus cauliflorus, commonly known as the red-flowered hibiscus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically red or pink in color. The plant exhibits cauliflorous growth, meaning its flowers and fruits develop directly from the trunk or branches. Its leaves are usually large, heart-shaped, and have a velvety texture.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
