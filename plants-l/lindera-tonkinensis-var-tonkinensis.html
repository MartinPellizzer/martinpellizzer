Lindera tonkinensis var. tonkinensis

Lindera tonkinensis var. tonkinensis, commonly known as Vietnamese sweetwood, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as mountain forests and riverbanks. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, while its fruit is a small, dark purple drupe.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
