Lobivia pugionacantha var. boedekeriana

Lobivia pugionacantha var. boedekeriana, commonly known as the Boedeker's lobivia, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It features a short, thick, branching stem covered in dense, gray-green foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring or early summer. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its slow growth and unique appearance, it is a prized specimen among cactus and succulent enthusiasts.
