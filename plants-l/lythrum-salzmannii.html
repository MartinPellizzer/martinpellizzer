Lythrum salzmannii

Lythrum salzmannii, commonly known as purple loosestrife, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy spikes of violet to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are opposite and covered in fine hairs. Its root system is fibrous, and it produces a large number of seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Lythrum salzmannii is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and lakes.
