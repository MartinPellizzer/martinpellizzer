Lactuca intricata

Lactuca intricata, commonly known as the curled lettuce, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family.

It is characterized by its tightly curled leaves that form a compact rosette, often resembling a small cabbage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in dry, sunny environments.

Lactuca intricata is often found in rocky soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
