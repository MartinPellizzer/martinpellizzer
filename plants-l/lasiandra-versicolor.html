Lasiandra versicolor

Lasiandra versicolor, commonly known as the variable lasiandra, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture.

Lasiandra versicolor is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
