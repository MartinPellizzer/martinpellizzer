Lythrum hyssopifolia subsp. thymifolia

Lythrum hyssopifolia subsp.

thymifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf lythrum, is a flowering plant in the genus Lythrum of the family Lythraceae. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture. Native to regions of Europe and western Asia, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
