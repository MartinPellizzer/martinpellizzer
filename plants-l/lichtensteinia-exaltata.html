Lichtensteinia exaltata

Lichtensteinia exaltata, commonly known as the Cape sugarbush, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded form and dark green, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
