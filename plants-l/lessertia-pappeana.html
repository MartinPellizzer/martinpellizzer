Lessertia pappeana

Lessertia pappeana, commonly known as the Natal wild bean, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its nitrogen-fixing ability through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by pod-like fruits containing seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grassland and thicket habitats.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
