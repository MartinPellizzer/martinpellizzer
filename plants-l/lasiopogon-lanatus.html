Lasiopogon lanatus

Lasiopogon lanatus, commonly known as the woolly bladderpod, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas. Its fruit is a pod that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Lasiopogon lanatus is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with similar structural features.
