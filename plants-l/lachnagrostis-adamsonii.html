Lachnagrostis adamsonii

Lachnagrostis adamsonii, commonly known as the Adamson's silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and compact growth habit, which helps it conserve water in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain minute, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well adapted to desert and semi-desert conditions.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological resilience.
