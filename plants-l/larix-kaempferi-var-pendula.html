Larix kaempferi var. pendula

Larix kaempferi var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping larch, is a variant of the Japanese larch.

It is a deciduous conifer that features drooping branches, giving it a distinctive, cascading appearance. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds. Its needles are needle-like and turn yellow in the fall before shedding.

This ornamental tree is often cultivated for its unique visual appeal in gardens and parks.
