Liparis cathcartii

Liparis cathcartii, commonly known as the Cathcart's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in well-drained, loamy soils. The plant features a short, stocky rhizome with erect, linear leaves. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in a loose inflorescence, often blooming in late spring.

Liparis cathcartii is a perennial species that produces a single spike of flowers, making it a distinctive yet understated member of the orchid family.
