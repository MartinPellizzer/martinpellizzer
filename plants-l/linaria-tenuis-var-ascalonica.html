Linaria tenuis var. ascalonica

Linaria tenuis var. ascalonica, commonly known as the ascalonic toadflax, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that bear bright yellow flowers with four petals, typical of the Scrophulariaceae family. The leaves are linear and slightly hairy, contributing to its drought-resistant nature. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
