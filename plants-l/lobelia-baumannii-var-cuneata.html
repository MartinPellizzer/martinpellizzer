Lobelia baumannii var. cuneata

Lobelia baumannii var. cuneata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf lobelia, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and stream banks, where it thrives in well-drained, loamy soils. The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are small, tubular, and usually blue or purple, arranged in spikes at the top of the stems.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
