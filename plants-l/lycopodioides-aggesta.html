Lycopodioides aggesta

Lycopodioides aggesta, commonly known as the African clubmoss, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and needle-like leaves. The plant produces spore-bearing cones, which are clustered at the tips of its branches. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the region's biodiversity and ecological systems.
