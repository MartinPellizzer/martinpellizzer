Lespedeza pilosa var. pedunculata

Lespedeza pilosa var. pedunculata, commonly known as hairy lespedeza, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges. The plant features slender, hairy stems and small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered with fine hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in disturbed areas.
