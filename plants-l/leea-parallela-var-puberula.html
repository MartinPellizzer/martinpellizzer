Leea parallela var. puberula

Leea parallela var. puberula, commonly known as the hairy leea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small shrub characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that split open to release tiny seeds.

This species is valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
