Loudetia acuminata

Loudetia acuminata, commonly known as the spike grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces spike-like inflorescences that bear small, two-lipped flowers, which are typically purple or pink in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Loudetia acuminata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its graceful appearance.
