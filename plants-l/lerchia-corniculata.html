Lerchia corniculata

Lerchia corniculata, commonly known as the horned poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, spurred petals that resemble horns, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving at high altitudes. Its flowers are usually bright red or orange, and it blooms during the summer months.

Lerchia corniculata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
