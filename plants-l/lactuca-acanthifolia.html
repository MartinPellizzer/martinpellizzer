Lactuca acanthifolia

Lactuca acanthifolia, commonly known as the spiny lettuce, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family.

It is native to regions of Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and is characterized by its hairy, spiny leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant is also known for its milky sap, which is a characteristic feature of the genus Lactuca.
