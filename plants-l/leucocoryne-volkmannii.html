Leucocoryne volkmannii

Leucocoryne volkmannii, commonly known as the White-veined Orchid, is a striking plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its unique, white-veined leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often appearing in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts for its aesthetic appeal.
