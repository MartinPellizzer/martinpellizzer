Lychnidia coeli-rosa var. aspera

Lychnidia coeli-rosa var. aspera, commonly known as the rough lychnis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe.

It features upright stems that are often hairy, giving it a rough texture. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
