Liriodendron liliiferum

Liriodendron liliiferum, commonly known as the tulip tree, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, large, yellow-green flowers that resemble tulips, which bloom in early spring. The tree has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. Its bark is smooth and light gray, with longitudinal ridges that become more pronounced with age.

The tulip tree is known for its rapid growth and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.
