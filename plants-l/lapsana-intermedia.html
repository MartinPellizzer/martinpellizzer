Lapsana intermedia

Lapsana intermedia, commonly known as common cudweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and wastelands. Its leaves are alternate, hairy, and have a serrated edge, while its flowers are yellow with pale pink centers.

Lapsana intermedia is often considered a weed but has historical uses in traditional medicine.
