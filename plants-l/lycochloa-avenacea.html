Lycochloa avenacea

Lycochloa avenacea, commonly known as the narrowleaf hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to various regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or inland dune environments. Lycochloa avenacea is adapted to arid and semi-arid conditions, demonstrating resilience to drought and salt spray.

Its ecological importance includes stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
