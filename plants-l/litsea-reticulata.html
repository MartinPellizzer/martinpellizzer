Litsea reticulata

Litsea reticulata, commonly known as the fragrant ash tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries such as India, China, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by small, dark blue berries.

The plant is valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
