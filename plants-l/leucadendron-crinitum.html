Leucadendron crinitum

Leucadendron crinitum, commonly known as the "Cape tulip," is a striking evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant, cone-shaped flower heads that range in color from yellow to red, depending on the variety. The plant features narrow, needle-like leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a unique, fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Leucadendron crinitum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
