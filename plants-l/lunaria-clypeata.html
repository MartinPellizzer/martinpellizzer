Lunaria clypeata

Lunaria clypeata, commonly known as the moonflower or nightshade, is a member of the Solanaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that open at night. The plant produces round, papery seed pods that contain small, shiny seeds. It is native to the southern United States and thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its nocturnal blooming habit, it is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and ecological value.
