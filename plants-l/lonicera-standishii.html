Lonicera standishii

Lonicera standishii, commonly known as the standish honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to the island of Honshu in Japan.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and clusters of fragrant, tubular yellow flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds but not for humans. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight, making it a suitable ornamental plant in temperate gardens.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and horticultural interest.
