Liparis rhodochila

Liparis rhodochila, commonly known as the red-lipped orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red lips on the petals, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its flowers are small but numerous, often arranged in a loose inflorescence.

Liparis rhodochila is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
