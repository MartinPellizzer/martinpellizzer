Leucaena lempirana

Leucaena lempirana, commonly known as the lempira tree, is a fast-growing leguminous shrub or small tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable for soil enrichment. It produces small, flat pods containing seeds that are often used in traditional medicine.

Leucaena lempirana is also cultivated for its forage value and as a source of timber.
