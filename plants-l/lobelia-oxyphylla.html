Lobelia oxyphylla

Lobelia oxyphylla, commonly known as the mountain lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool and moist conditions. Its flowers are tubular and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Lobelia oxyphylla is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
