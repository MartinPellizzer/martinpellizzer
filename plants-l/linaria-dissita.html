Linaria dissita

Linaria dissita, commonly known as the scattered toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant features slender, branched stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are linear to lance-shaped with a hairy texture. Its flowers are yellow with a distinctive orange-red throat, forming in clusters along the stems.

Linaria dissita is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soils.
