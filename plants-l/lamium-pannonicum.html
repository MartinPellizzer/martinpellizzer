Lamium pannonicum

Lamium pannonicum, commonly known as greater dead-nettle, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and opposite leaves. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of small, two-lipped flowers in shades of pink or purple. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Lamium pannonicum is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and can also serve as a food source for certain pollinators.
