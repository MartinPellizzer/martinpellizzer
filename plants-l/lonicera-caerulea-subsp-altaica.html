Lonicera caerulea subsp. altaica

Lonicera caerulea subsp.

altaica, commonly known as the Siberian honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern China. It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pale yellow, which bloom in early summer. Its fruit is a red, juicy berry that is highly valued for its tart flavor and nutritional content.

This subspecies is well adapted to harsh environments and is often used in ornamental gardening and as a source of edible berries.
