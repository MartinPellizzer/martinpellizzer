Liriope koreana

Liriope koreana, commonly known as Korean lily-turf, is a perennial evergreen plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its clumping growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple during late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial to full shade and is adaptable to a variety of soil types, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Liriope koreana is also valued for its ability to suppress weeds and its low maintenance requirements.
