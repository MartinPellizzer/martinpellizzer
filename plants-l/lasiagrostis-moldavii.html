Lasiagrostis moldavii

Lasiagrostis moldavii, commonly known as Moldavian hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowers are wind-pollinated, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in wet or semi-aquatic environments.

Lasiagrostis moldavii plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native range.
