Lindernia pyxidaria

Lindernia pyxidaria, commonly known as the Mexican flameflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the family Verbenaceae and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and often covered in fine hairs. The flowers, which are typically pink to red, bloom in summer and attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for container gardening and hanging baskets.
