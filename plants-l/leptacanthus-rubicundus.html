Leptacanthus rubicundus

Leptacanthus rubicundus, commonly known as the red-veined orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves with deep red veins and its fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is known for its adaptability to moist, shaded environments. Its unique coloration and ornamental value make it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation.

The species is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional remedies.
