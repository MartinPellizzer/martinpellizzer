Limacia sagittata

Limacia sagittata, commonly known as the arrowhead plant, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its arrow-shaped leaves and striking spadix inflorescence. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found near water bodies. Its foliage is broad and glossy, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe.
