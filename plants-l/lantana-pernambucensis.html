Lantana pernambucensis

Lantana pernambucensis, commonly known as Brazilian lantana, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a rough texture.

Lantana pernambucensis is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.
