Lodhra racemosa

Lodhra racemosa, commonly known as the sacred ash tree or the Indian privet, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae and is characterized by its opposite, simple, and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by small, round, black berries. It is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional Ayurvedic practices.

The bark and leaves are used in herbal remedies for their astringent and anti-inflammatory effects.
