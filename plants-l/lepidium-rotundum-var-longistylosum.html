Lepidium rotundum var. longistylosum

Lepidium rotundum var. longistylosum, commonly known as the long-styled cress, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic, and covered with fine hairs, while the stems are upright and branched.

The variety longistylosum is distinguished by its elongated stamens, which are longer than those of the typical form.
