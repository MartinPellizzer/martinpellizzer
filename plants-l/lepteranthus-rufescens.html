Lepteranthus rufescens

Lepteranthus rufescens, commonly known as the red-flowered rock orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking red or pink flowers. The plant typically grows on rocky outcrops and is adapted to survive in dry, well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
