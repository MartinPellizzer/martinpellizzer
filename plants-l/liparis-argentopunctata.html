Liparis argentopunctata

Liparis argentopunctata, commonly known as the silver-spotted orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, grass-like foliage and small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale green with faint silver markings. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
