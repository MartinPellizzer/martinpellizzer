Liatris punctata var. nebraskana

Liatris punctata var. nebraskana, commonly known as the Nebraska blazing star, is a perennial herb native to the central United States.

It features tall, unbranched stems that bear whorls of narrow, grass-like leaves and spikes of purple to pink flowers. The plant is adapted to dry, sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it a common sight in prairie habitats. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
