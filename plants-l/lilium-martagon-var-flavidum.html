Lilium martagon var. flavidum

Lilium martagon var. flavidum, commonly known as the golden Turk's cap lily, is a rare and striking variant of the Turk's cap lily.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that are typically a pale yellow to orange in color. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and is known for its elegant, nodding blooms that appear in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often dark green and arranged in a fan-like pattern.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
