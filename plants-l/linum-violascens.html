Linum violascens

Linum violascens, commonly known as blue flax, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, delicate blue or violet flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are rich in oil, making it valuable for both ornamental and agricultural purposes. Linum violascens thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Its historical use dates back to ancient times for dye production and medicinal applications.
