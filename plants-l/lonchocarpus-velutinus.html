Lonchocarpus velutinus

Lonchocarpus velutinus, commonly known as velvet senna, is a tropical legume native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces distinctive, spiny pods that contain seeds covered in a fine, velvety coating. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for horticultural and research purposes.
