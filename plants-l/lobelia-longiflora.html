Lobelia longiflora

Lobelia longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Campanulaceae family and typically grows in moist, wooded areas. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of blue to purple tubular flowers. It is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other damp habitats.

Lobelia longiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
