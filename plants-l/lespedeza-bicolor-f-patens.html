Lespedeza bicolor f. patens

Lespedeza bicolor f. patens, commonly known as the pink burr medic, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its seeds are encased in hairy, spiny pods, which aid in seed dispersal.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is used in forage and soil stabilization efforts.
