Lindera aggregata f. playfairii

Lindera aggregata f. playfairii, commonly known as the Playfair's spicebush, is a variant of the spicebush species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and small yellowish-green flowers that appear in the spring. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Botanically, it belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants such as cinnamon and avocado.

Its horticultural value lies in its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
