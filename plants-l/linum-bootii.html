Linum bootii

Linum bootii, commonly known as the Armenian flax or Persian flax, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It is a member of the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its fibrous stems were historically used for fiber production, similar to other flax species.

Linum bootii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
