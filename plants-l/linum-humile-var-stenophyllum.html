Linum humile var. stenophyllum

Linum humile var. stenophyllum, commonly known as narrow-leaved flax, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its fibrous stalks and leaves are used in traditional herbal medicine and for fiber production.
