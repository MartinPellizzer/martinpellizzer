Linum humile f. vaccaeorum

Linum humile f. vaccaeorum, commonly known as the lesser flax or a variant of the common flax, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It features slender stems and small, delicate flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its fibrous stalks and seeds have been historically used for textile and nutritional purposes.
