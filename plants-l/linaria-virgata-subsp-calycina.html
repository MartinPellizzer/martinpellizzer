Linaria virgata subsp. calycina

Linaria virgata subsp.

calycina, commonly known as the narrowleaf toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. Linaria virgata subsp.

calycina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in horticulture for its vibrant blooms.
