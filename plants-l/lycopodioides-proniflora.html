Lycopodioides proniflora

Lycopodioides proniflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaf clubmoss, is a species of lycophyte belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and a distinct, upright stem that bears clustered sporophylls. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures are specialized, with microsporangia and megasporangia arranged on separate sporophylls.

Lycopodioides proniflora plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
