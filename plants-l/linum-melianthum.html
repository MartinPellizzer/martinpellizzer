Linum melianthum

Linum melianthum, commonly known as the yellow flax or wild flax, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has slender, branched stems covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slight pubescence.

Linum melianthum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive, sun-loving nature.
