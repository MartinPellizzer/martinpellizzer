Lobivia haematantha subvar. pectinifera

Lobivia haematantha subvar. pectinifera, commonly known as the Crimson Lobivia, is a succulent native to the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It features compact, rounded stems with thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with red or purple hues. The plant produces clusters of vibrant orange to red flowers during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to arid conditions, storing water in its leaves and stems.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
