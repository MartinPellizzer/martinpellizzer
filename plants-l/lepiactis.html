Lepiactis

Lepiactis, commonly known as the common rockcress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of fleshy, lobed leaves and small, cruciform flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant is native to temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia, often found growing in rocky or gravelly soils. Lepiactis produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

It is a hardy perennial that thrives in well-drained conditions and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.
