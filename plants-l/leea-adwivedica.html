Leea adwivedica

Leea adwivedica, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Leeaceae and is characterized by its nocturnal blooming habit, with large, fragrant white flowers that open at night. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, fleshy fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique blooming pattern and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
