Lonicera × bella f. rosea

Lonicera × bella f. rosea, commonly known as the rose-flowered honeysuckle, is a hybrid shrub characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that range from pink to rose-red.

It is native to eastern Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 6 feet and features opposite, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
