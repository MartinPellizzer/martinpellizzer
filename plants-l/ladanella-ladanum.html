Ladanella ladanum

Ladanella ladanum, commonly known as the Palestine jessamine, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fragrant flowers.
