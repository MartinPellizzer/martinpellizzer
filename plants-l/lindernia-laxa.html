Lindernia laxa

Lindernia laxa, commonly known as the slender lindernia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial plant characterized by its slender, branching stems and opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale purple to lavender in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Lindernia laxa thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in dry, open habitats.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
