Leonocassia

Leonocassia, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Lamiaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of the Americas and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flower clusters. The leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and often have a hairy texture. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
