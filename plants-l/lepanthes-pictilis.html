Lepanthes pictilis

Lepanthes pictilis, commonly known as the Painted Orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that often display intricate patterns and vivid hues. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, such as cloud forests, where it thrives in the understory. Its flowers are usually small to medium in size, with three sepals and three petals, and it produces a distinctive, fragrant scent.

Lepanthes pictilis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and rarity.
