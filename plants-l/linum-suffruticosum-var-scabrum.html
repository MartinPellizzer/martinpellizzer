Linum suffruticosum var. scabrum

Linum suffruticosum var. scabrum, commonly known as rough flax, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, blue to purple flowers. The leaves are narrow and covered with a rough, hairy texture, which gives the plant its common name. It belongs to the Linaceae family and is known for its fibrous stems, which were historically used for textile production.

This plant is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
