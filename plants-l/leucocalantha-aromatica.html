Leucocalantha aromatica

Leucocalantha aromatica, commonly known as the fragrant white flower.

This plant is characterized by its delicate, white petals that emit a subtle, sweet fragrance. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands or along stream banks. The leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Its blooming season occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
