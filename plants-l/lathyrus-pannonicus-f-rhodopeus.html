Lathyrus pannonicus f. rhodopeus

Lathyrus pannonicus f. rhodopeus, commonly known as the Rhodope vetch, is a botanical variant of the common vetch.

It is a perennial leguminous plant characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The flowers are typically pink to purple, arranged in racemes, and are attractive to pollinators. It thrives in rocky or calcareous soils, often found in mountainous regions of the Balkans.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and its role in traditional herbal medicine.
