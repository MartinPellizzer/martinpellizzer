Lonicera chrysantha f. crassipes

Lonicera chrysantha f. crassipes, commonly known as the broad-leaved honeysuckle, is a hybrid shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically broader than those of the parent species, Lonicera chrysantha. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often blooming in late spring. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that matures in late summer, attracting various birds and small mammals.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to different soil types.
