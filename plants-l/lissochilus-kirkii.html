Lissochilus kirkii

Lissochilus kirkii, commonly known as the Kirk's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to South America.

It is a terrestrial herb with a slender, wiry stem that often branches at the base. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in a loose inflorescence. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in cloud forests at high altitudes.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is sometimes cultivated by enthusiasts and conservationists.
