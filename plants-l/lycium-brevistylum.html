Lycium brevistylum

Lycium brevistylum, commonly known as the short-stemmed boxthorn, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces red berries that are edible for birds and small mammals, contributing to its role in local ecosystems. Lycium brevistylum is adapted to hot, dry environments and has a deep root system for water conservation.

Its hardy nature makes it a valuable plant for erosion control in desert landscapes.
