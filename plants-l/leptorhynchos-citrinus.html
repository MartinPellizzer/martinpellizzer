Leptorhynchos citrinus

Leptorhynchos citrinus, commonly known as the lemon thyme, is a flowering plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its fragrant, lemon-scented leaves and small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. Native to the Mediterranean region, this plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It is often used in culinary and aromatic applications due to its essential oils.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
