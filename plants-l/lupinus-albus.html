Lupinus albus

Lupinus albus, commonly known as white lupin, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important crop in agricultural systems.

Lupinus albus is also valued for its nutritional content and use in traditional medicine.
