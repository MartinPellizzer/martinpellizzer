Lagochilus iliensis

Lagochilus iliensis, commonly known as the Ili mountain hyssop, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a valuable species in its native habitat.
