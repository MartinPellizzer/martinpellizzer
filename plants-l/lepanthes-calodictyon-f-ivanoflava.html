Lepanthes calodictyon f. ivanoflava

Lepanthes calodictyon f. ivanoflava, commonly known as the Ivanoff's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant features delicate, showy flowers with a distinctive coloration pattern, often in shades of white and pink. Its leaves are usually basal, arranged in a rosette, and are covered in a fine, velvety texture.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its beauty and rarity.
