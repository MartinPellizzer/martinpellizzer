Linaria reflexa f. flava

Linaria reflexa f. flava, commonly known as the yellow sandwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, while the stems are prostrate or ascending.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal in rock gardens and as a drought-tolerant plant in arid landscapes.
