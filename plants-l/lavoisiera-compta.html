Lavoisiera compta

Lavoisiera compta, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white and pale pink. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in shaded, humid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
