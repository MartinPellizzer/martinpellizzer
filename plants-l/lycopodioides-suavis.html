Lycopodioides suavis

Lycopodioides suavis, commonly known as the sweet lycopod, is a small, evergreen shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae, which includes club mosses and related plants. The plant is characterized by its dense, needle-like leaves that form compact, mat-like growths on the ground. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystem, supporting various insect and fungal life forms.
