Lupinus nevadensis

Lupinus nevadensis, commonly known as the Sierra lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Lupinus nevadensis plays an important role in its ecosystem by fixing nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plant life.
