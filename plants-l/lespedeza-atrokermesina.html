Lespedeza atrokermesina

Lespedeza atrokermesina, commonly known as black lespedeza, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and dry soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade. Its seeds are small and pod-like, containing nutrients that can be used in soil enrichment.

Lespedeza atrokermesina is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to support local pollinators.
