Lippia affinis

Lippia affinis, commonly known as Mexican mint or honey herb, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, slender leaves and small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic and digestive properties.

Lippia affinis is also valued in culinary applications, particularly in Mexican cuisine, where it is used to flavor beverages and dishes.
