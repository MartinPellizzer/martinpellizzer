Leymus divaricatus subsp. fasciculatus

Leymus divaricatus subsp.

fasciculatus, commonly known as the tufted wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States. It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of spikelets, which are often tightly packed.

This subspecies is valued for its drought tolerance and is used in restoration projects and as a forage plant.
