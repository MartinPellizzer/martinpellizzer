Linnaea borealis f. sulcata

Linnaea borealis f. sulcata, commonly known as the wrinkled twinflower, is a rare and intriguing variant of the twinflower plant.

It is characterized by its distinctive, wrinkled leaves that give it a unique appearance compared to the typical smooth leaves of the species. This plant is native to boreal and subarctic regions, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in pairs, resembling the shape of a twin, hence its common name.

The f. sulcata form is particularly rare, making it a subject of interest for botanists and plant enthusiasts alike.
