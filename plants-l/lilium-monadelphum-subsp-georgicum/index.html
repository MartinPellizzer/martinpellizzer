Lilium Monadelphum Subsp. Georgicum

Georgian lily, scientifically known as Lilium monadelphum subsp. georgicum, is a rare and elegant flower native to the mountainous regions of Georgia. This subspecies is distinguished by its striking bell-shaped blooms and vibrant colors that range from white to deep pink. It thrives in cool, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas with moist, well-drained soil. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, the Georgian lily is considered a vulnerable species. Conservation efforts are crucial to preserving this beautiful plant for future generations.
