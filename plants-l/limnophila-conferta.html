Limnophila conferta

Limnophila conferta, commonly known as the water thyme, is a submerged aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It has slender, branching stems that are often covered in small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or brown in color. Limnophila conferta thrives in slow-moving to still freshwater environments.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
