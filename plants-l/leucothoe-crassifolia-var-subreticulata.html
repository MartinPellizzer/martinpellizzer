Leucothoe crassifolia var. subreticulata

Leucothoe crassifolia var. subreticulata, commonly known as the variegated leatherleaf, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features dense, glossy, dark green foliage with prominent veins that give it its variegated appearance. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries in the fall. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
