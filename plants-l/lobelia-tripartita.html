Lobelia tripartita

Lobelia tripartita, commonly known as the three-part lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 feet. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Lobelia tripartita is often found in moist, shaded woodlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
