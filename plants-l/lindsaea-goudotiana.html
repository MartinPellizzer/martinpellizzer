Lindsaea goudotiana

Lindsaea goudotiana, commonly known as the Goudot's spikemoss, is a member of the family Lindsaeaceae and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features a creeping stem with whorled leaves that are often lance-shaped and have a leathery texture. Lindsaea goudotiana produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in spike-like structures, which are characteristic of the genus.

It plays a role in its ecosystem by providing habitat for microorganisms and small invertebrates.
