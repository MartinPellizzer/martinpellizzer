Loncomelos tardum

Loncomelos tardum, commonly known as the slow-growing shrub, is a perennial plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, woody structure and small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white hair. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers during the dry season, which are followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to harsh, semi-arid environments.

Due to its slow growth and unique appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in xeriscaping.
