Lippia graveolens

Lippia graveolens, commonly known as Mexican mint or green mint, is a flowering plant native to Central America and northern South America.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its fragrant foliage and ornamental value. Its essential oils, extracted from the leaves, are used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine.

Lippia graveolens is also valued in culinary applications, particularly in the preparation of teas and sauces.
