Lycopodium trigonum

Lycopodium trigonum, commonly known as the clubmoss, is a small evergreen plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its creeping rhizomes and erect, spore-bearing cones called strobili. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its leaves are needle-like and arranged in whorls around the stem.

Lycopodium trigonum plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and small invertebrates.
