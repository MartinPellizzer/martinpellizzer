Leucaena doylei

Leucaena doylei, commonly known as the Doyle leucaena, is a leguminous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to fix nitrogen, making it valuable for soil enrichment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in agroforestry systems.

Its flowers are typically yellow or white, and it is sometimes cultivated for forage or ornamental purposes.
