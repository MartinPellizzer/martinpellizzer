Lobelia rosulata

Lobelia rosulata, commonly known as the clustered lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its rosette-like growth habit, with leaves arranged in dense clusters around the base. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Lobelia rosulata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

It is a perennial species that is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
