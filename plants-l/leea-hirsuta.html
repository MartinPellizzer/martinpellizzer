Leea hirsuta

Leea hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy leea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Southeast Asia, and the Pacific Islands.

It is a woody shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height. The plant features simple, opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, white or yellowish flowers are clustered in axillary inflorescences, and it produces small, fleshy fruits that contain a single seed.

Leea hirsuta is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
