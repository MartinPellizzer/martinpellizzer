Lotus glareosus var. villosus

Lotus glareosus var. villosus, commonly known as the hairy lotus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces striking white or pink flowers with a strong, sweet fragrance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
