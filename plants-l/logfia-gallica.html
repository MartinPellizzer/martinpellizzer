Logfia gallica

Logfia gallica, commonly known as the French lavender, is a perennial shrub native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and spikes of purple flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is closely related to the genus Lavandula and shares similar medicinal and aromatic properties. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal use.

Its essential oils are widely used in perfumery and traditional medicine.
