Luma myrcianthes

Luma myrcianthes, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of Brazil.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that can grow up to 10 meters tall, featuring dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight.

The species is notable for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
