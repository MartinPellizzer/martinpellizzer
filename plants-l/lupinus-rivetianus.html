Lupinus rivetianus

Lupinus rivetianus, commonly known as the Riviera lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of pea-like flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Lupinus rivetianus is also valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a food source for local wildlife.
