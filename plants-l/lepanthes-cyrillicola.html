Lepanthes cyrillicola

Lepanthes cyrillicola, commonly known as the Cyrillic orchid, is a species of orchid native to the highlands of Central America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments. The plant features a slender, erect stem with a single or few flowers that are usually pale green or white in color. Its flowers are characterized by their intricate structure and are often adorned with delicate, intricate patterns.

Lepanthes cyrillicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
