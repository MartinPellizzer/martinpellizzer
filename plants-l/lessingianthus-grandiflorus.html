Lessingianthus grandiflorus

Lessingianthus grandiflorus, commonly known as the large-flowered lessingia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy, yellow to orange ray flowers surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers, making it visually striking. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Lessingianthus grandiflorus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
