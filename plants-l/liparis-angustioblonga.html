Liparis angustioblonga

Liparis angustioblonga, commonly known as the narrow-ovate orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive lip. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky areas. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with a three-lobed lip that is often hairy.

Liparis angustioblonga is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with appropriate humidity and light conditions.
