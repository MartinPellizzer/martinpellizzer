Lycopus bracteatus

Lycopus bracteatus, commonly known as blueweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, blue to purple, and clustered in dense inflorescences.

Lycopus bracteatus is known for its aromatic foliage and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
