Lonchocarpus longifolius

Lonchocarpus longifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved senna, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bipinnately compound leaves with numerous small leaflets, and its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in racemes. It produces elongated pods that contain several seeds, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine.

However, some parts of the plant contain toxic alkaloids, requiring caution in handling and use.
