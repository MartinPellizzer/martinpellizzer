Lathyrus japonicus f. spectabilis

Lathyrus japonicus f. spectabilis, commonly known as the Japanese pea vine, is a flowering plant characterized by its trailing growth habit and showy, pea-like flowers.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is native to Japan, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant features trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets and produces clusters of vibrant purple or pink flowers that bloom in spring.

Its botanical significance lies in its ornamental value and its role in ecological systems as a nitrogen-fixing plant.
