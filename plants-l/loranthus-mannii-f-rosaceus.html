Loranthus mannii f. rosaceus

Loranthus mannii f. rosaceus, commonly known as the rose-leaved mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own carbohydrates through photosynthesis using the light it captures. The plant features small, alternate leaves that are often pinkish or reddish in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow and are borne in clusters, and it produces small, fleshy berries that are dispersed by birds.

This species is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique parasitic lifestyle and ecological interactions.
