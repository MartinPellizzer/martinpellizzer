Linnaea borealis f. hemisema

Linnaea borealis f. hemisema, commonly known as the hemisemic twinflower, is a rare and distinctive variant of the twinflower plant.

It is characterized by its unique hemispherical arrangement of flowers, which sets it apart from the typical linear growth pattern of the species. This form is typically found in moist, shaded forest environments, often in the northern regions of North America and Europe. The plant produces small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in early spring.

Its botanical significance lies in its rarity and its role in the study of plant morphology and genetic variation.
