Lathyrus subandinus

Lathyrus subandinus, commonly known as the Andean vetch, is a climbing legume native to South America.

It features trifoliate leaves and produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant is characterized by its slender, twining stems and is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats. It thrives in temperate climates and is adapted to poor soils.

Lathyrus subandinus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential ecological and medicinal properties.
