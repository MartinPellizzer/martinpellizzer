Litsea lakshmammaniana

Litsea lakshmammaniana, commonly known as the Lakshmi Mamaniana-laurel, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. It produces small, white, fragrant flowers followed by spherical, blue-black berries.

Litsea lakshmammaniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
