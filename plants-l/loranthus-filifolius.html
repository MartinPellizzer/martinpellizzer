Loranthus filifolius

Loranthus filifolius, commonly known as the filifoliate mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to its host tree through a specialized structure called a haustellum.

It belongs to the family Loranthaceae and is native to various regions in Asia, including China, India, and Southeast Asia. The plant has small, scale-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are often clustered together. Its berries are red or orange and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds, aiding in its propagation.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can affect the health of its host tree if left unchecked.
