Lycium spina-christi

Lycium spina-christi, commonly known as the Christ's thorn or Christ's crown, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, red berries that resemble blood droplets. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is often found in rocky or arid environments. Its berries are toxic if consumed raw but have been used in traditional medicine.

The plant's unique appearance and historical associations have made it a symbol in various cultural and religious contexts.
