Lepanthes suarezii

Lepanthes suarezii, commonly known as the Suarez's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually small, with intricate patterns and vibrant colors, often in shades of white, pink, and purple.

Lepanthes suarezii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
