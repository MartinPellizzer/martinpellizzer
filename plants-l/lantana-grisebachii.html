Lantana grisebachii

Lantana grisebachii, commonly known as the silver lantana, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The leaves are typically gray-green, covered in fine, silvery hairs, which give the plant its common name. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Lantana grisebachii is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
