Lomaria cycadifolia

Lomaria cycadifolia, commonly known as the cycad-like fern, is a unique plant characterized by its distinctive, palm-like appearance.

It belongs to the family Lomariaceae and is native to certain regions of Southeast Asia. The plant features a stout, unbranched stem with a crown of pinnately compound leaves that resemble those of cycads. Its foliage is typically dark green and glossy, with a leathery texture.

Lomaria cycadifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
