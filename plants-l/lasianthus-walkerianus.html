Lasianthus walkerianus

Lasianthus walkerianus, commonly known as the Walker's flame flower, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are clustered in whorls along the stems.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in tropical gardens for its striking floral display.
