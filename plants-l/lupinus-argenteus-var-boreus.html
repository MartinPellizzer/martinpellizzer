Lupinus argenteus var. boreus

Lupinus argenteus var. boreus, commonly known as the silver lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its silvery-blue to lavender flowers and hairy, compound leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance.
