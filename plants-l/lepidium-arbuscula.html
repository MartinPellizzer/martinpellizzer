Lepidium arbuscula

Lepidium arbuscula, commonly known as dwarf wall rocket, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is characterized by its dense, low-growing habit. The plant features small, fern-like leaves and produces tiny, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to thrive in harsh, sunny conditions.

Lepidium arbuscula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
