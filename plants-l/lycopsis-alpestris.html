Lycopsis alpestris

Lycopsis alpestris, commonly known as the alpine lycopsis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence. Its flowers are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in whorls along the stem, and bloom during the summer months.

Lycopsis alpestris thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.
