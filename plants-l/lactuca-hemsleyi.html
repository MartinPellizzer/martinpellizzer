Lactuca hemsleyi

Lactuca hemsleyi, commonly known as Hemsley's lettuce, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family, Asteraceae.

It is native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico. The plant is characterized by its rosette of spoon-shaped, hairy leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in the summer. It is adapted to survive in dry, sandy soils and can tolerate high temperatures.

Lactuca hemsleyi is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and dry fields.
