Leucophae canariensis var. orotavensis

Leucophae canariensis var. orotavensis, commonly known as the Canary Island hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The species is adapted to the humid, subtropical climate of its native habitat, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.
