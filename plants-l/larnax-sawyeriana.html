Larnax sawyeriana

Larnax sawyeriana, commonly known as the Sawyer's larnax, is a rare and unique plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, spiral-arranged leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Due to habitat loss, Larnax sawyeriana is considered endangered and is the subject of conservation efforts.
