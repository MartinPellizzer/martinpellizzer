Lagerstroemia subcostata var. fauriei

Lagerstroemia subcostata var. fauriei, commonly known as the Japanese or Chinese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, violet-blue to purple berries that appear in late summer and persist into winter. The plant has small, ovate leaves that are usually arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. It produces panicles of white or pale pink flowers in the spring, which are often followed by the showy berries.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
