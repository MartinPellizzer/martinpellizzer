Leria albicans

Leria albicans, commonly known as the white leria, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and prefers full sun exposure. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine layer of white pubescence.

Leria albicans is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
