Litsea cambodiana var. cambodiana

Litsea cambodiana var. cambodiana, commonly known as Cambodian litsea, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by oval, woody fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils in lowland areas.

The tree is valued for its aromatic wood and has traditional uses in local medicine and as a source of essential oils.
