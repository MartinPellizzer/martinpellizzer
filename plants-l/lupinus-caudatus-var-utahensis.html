Lupinus caudatus var. utahensis

Lupinus caudatus var. utahensis, commonly known as the Utah lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to violet pea-like flowers. The plant has feathery, compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sagebrush habitats.

Lupinus caudatus var. utahensis plays an important role in nitrogen fixation and supports local pollinator populations.
