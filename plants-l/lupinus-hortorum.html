Lupinus hortorum

Lupinus hortorum, commonly known as the garden lupine, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth and clusters of pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. Lupinus hortorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or disturbed areas.
