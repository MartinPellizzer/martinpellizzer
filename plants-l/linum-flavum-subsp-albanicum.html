Linum flavum subsp. albanicum

Linum flavum subsp.

albanicum, commonly known as the Albanian flax, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan region. It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its yellow flowers and linear leaves.

The plant typically grows in dry, calcareous soils and is often found in rocky or grassy habitats. It produces small, oval seeds that are used for both ornamental and potential agricultural purposes.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare species in its native range.
