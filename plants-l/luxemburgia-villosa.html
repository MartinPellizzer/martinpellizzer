Luxemburgia villosa

Luxemburgia villosa, commonly known as the woolly luxemburgia, is a flowering plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, or villosa, stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, rugged appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is a popular choice for drought-resistant landscaping.
