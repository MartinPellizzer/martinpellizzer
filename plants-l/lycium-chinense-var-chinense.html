Lycium chinense var. chinense

Lycium chinense var. chinense, commonly known as Chinese wolfberry or goji berry, is a shrub native to China and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces red berries that are rich in antioxidants and nutrients, making them popular in traditional medicine and cuisine. It thrives in arid and semi-arid climates, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

The berries are typically harvested when fully ripe, and the plant is valued for both its ornamental and medicinal properties.
