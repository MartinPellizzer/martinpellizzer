Linaria repens var. alba

Linaria repens var. alba, commonly known as the white sand verbena, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features slender, branching stems that produce small, tubular flowers in shades of white to pale yellow. The plant's leaves are linear and slightly hairy, adapted to thrive in arid and rocky environments. It is typically found in sandy or gravelly soils, often in desert or coastal regions.

Its flowering period occurs in the spring and early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
