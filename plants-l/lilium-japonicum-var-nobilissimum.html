Lilium japonicum var. nobilissimum

Lilium japonicum var. nobilissimum, commonly known as the Japanese lily or noble lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring long, strap-like leaves and a strong, aromatic scent. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

This variety is prized for its elegance and is often cultivated for both its aesthetic value and its use in traditional floral arrangements.
