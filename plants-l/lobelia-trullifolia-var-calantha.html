Lobelia trullifolia var. calantha

Lobelia trullifolia var. calantha, commonly known as the calantha lobelia, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, erect stems that bear whorled leaves, which are typically lance-shaped and hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of blue to purple, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in native plant gardens.
