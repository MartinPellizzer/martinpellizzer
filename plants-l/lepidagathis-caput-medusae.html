Lepidagathis caput-medusae

Lepidagathis caput-medusae, commonly known as the crown-of-thorns or Medusa's head, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its spiny, fleshy leaves and showy yellow flowers.

It is native to the Indo-Pacific region and thrives in coastal and brackish water environments. The plant is often found growing on mangrove roots or in sandy soils, demonstrating its adaptability to saline conditions. Its unique appearance, with its dense clusters of spines and bright blooms, makes it a notable ornamental species.

However, it can be invasive in certain areas, posing challenges to local ecosystems.
