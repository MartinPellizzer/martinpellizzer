Leptostachya martiana var. macrophylla

Leptostachya martiana var. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leafed bamboo, is a variant of the Leptostachya martiana species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to several meters in height, forming dense clumps. The plant produces long, narrow leaves that are often larger than those of the typical variety, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation in tropical and subtropical regions.
