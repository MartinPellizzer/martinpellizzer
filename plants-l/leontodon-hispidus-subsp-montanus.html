Leontodon hispidus subsp. montanus

Leontodon hispidus subsp.

montanus, commonly known as the alpine hawk's-beard, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and yellow composite flowers.

The subspecies montanus is distinguished by its more robust growth and increased hairiness compared to other subspecies. It typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes.

This plant is often found in alpine meadows and is adapted to cold, harsh environments.
