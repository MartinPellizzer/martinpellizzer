Leonurus tibeticus

Leonurus tibeticus, commonly known as the Tibetan mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Tibetan Plateau.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant features small, hairy leaves and produces clusters of pale blue to purple flowers. It thrives in cold, arid environments and is adapted to the extreme conditions of its native habitat.

Due to its unique characteristics, it is of interest to botanists and herbalists alike.
