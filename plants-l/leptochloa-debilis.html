Leptochloa debilis

Leptochloa debilis, commonly known as slender grass or slender lespedeza, is a low-growing perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 10 cm in length, with a bluish-green color and a fine texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are often overlooked due to their subtle appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in ecological studies and habitat restoration efforts.
