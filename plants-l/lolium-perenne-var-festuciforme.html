Lolium perenne var. festuciforme

Lolium perenne var. festuciforme, commonly known as sheep's fescue, is a cool-season grass species characterized by its fine, dense foliage and tufted growth habit.

It is native to Europe and is often found in moist, well-drained soils, particularly in grasslands and meadows. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and turfgrass mixes.

Due to its adaptability, it can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a versatile choice for both ecological and horticultural applications.
