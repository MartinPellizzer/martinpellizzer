Lobivia rebutioides var. citriniflora

Lobivia rebutioides var. citriniflora, commonly known as the Citrine-flowered Lobivia, is a succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It features compact, cylindrical stems with a dark green to bluish-gray coloration. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are typically small, fleshy, and arranged in a rosette pattern.

It is well adapted to harsh desert conditions, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.
