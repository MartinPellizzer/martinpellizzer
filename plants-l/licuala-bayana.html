Licuala bayana

Licuala bayana, commonly known as the Philippine palm, is a species native to the Philippines.

It is a tall, upright palm with a slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal areas and lowland forests. Its leaves are used in traditional crafts and as thatching material.

The plant has a moderate growth rate and requires well-drained soil and ample sunlight to flourish.
