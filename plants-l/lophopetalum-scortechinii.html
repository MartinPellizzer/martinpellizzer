Lophopetalum scortechinii

Lophopetalum scortechinii, commonly known as the Texas mountain laurel, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are opposite and pinnately arranged, and its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces woody pods that contain several seeds, and it is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
