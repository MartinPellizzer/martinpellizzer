Leiothamnus anomalus

Leiothamnus anomalus, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, bell-shaped flowers that typically range in color from white to pale blue. The plant thrives in rocky, alpine environments and is adapted to cold, high-altitude conditions. Its leaves are generally narrow and covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, which helps to reflect sunlight and reduce water loss.

Leiothamnus anomalus plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing food and shelter for various insect species.
