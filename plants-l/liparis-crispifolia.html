Liparis crispifolia

Liparis crispifolia, commonly known as the crinkled orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, crinkled leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky areas. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its unique leaf morphology.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
