Luehea fiebrigii

Luehea fiebrigii, commonly known as the red sandalwood tree, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, reddish bark and dense, aromatic wood. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by elongated, woody pods containing several seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic in shape, with a smooth margin.

Luehea fiebrigii is valued for its hard, durable wood, which has been traditionally used in construction and craftsmanship.
