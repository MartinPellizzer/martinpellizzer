Lantana lamiifolia

Lantana lamiifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf lantana, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

However, it can become invasive in certain environments if not properly managed.
