Loranthus leptopetalus

Loranthus leptopetalus, commonly known as the slender-petaled mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to its host tree through a specialized root system called a haustellum.

It lacks true roots and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while obtaining some of its own through photosynthesis. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. Its berries are typically red or yellow and contain sticky pulp that aids in seed dispersal by birds.

Loranthus leptopetalus is found in various regions of Australia, where it thrives in a range of host species, including eucalyptus and acacia.
