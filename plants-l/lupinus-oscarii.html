Lupinus oscarii

Lupinus oscarii, commonly known as the Oscarii lupin, is a species of flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of pea-like flowers. The plant produces trifoliate leaves and has a deep taproot system, which helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Lupinus oscarii is also notable for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
