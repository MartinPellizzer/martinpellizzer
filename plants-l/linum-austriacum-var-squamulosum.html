Linum austriacum var. squamulosum

Linum austriacum var. squamulosum, commonly known as the Austrian flax or squamulous flax, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its slender stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, scaly texture. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers arranged in loose spikes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in fiber production.
