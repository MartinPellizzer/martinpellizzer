Lauro-cerasus mexicana

Lauro-cerasus mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican cherry laurel, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Prunus genus and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in temperate climates and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

However, its berries can be toxic to humans and domestic animals if ingested in large quantities.
