Limonium migjornense

Limonium migjornense, commonly known as the coastal flax, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils along coastal areas and is often found in dunes or salt marshes. It is well adapted to saline and windy environments, showing resilience to harsh coastal conditions.

Limonium migjornense is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in coastal ecosystems.
