Linum triflorum

Linum triflorum, commonly known as the three-flowered flax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

Linum triflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators.
