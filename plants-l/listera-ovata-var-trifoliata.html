Listera ovata var. trifoliata

Listera ovata var. trifoliata, commonly known as the three-leaved woodland star, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged in a rosette. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded woodland environments and has a fibrous root system.
