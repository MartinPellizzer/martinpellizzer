Lobelia crassiuscula

Lobelia crassiuscula, commonly known as the dwarf lobelia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of only a few inches. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its compact size and ornamental value, it is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover.
