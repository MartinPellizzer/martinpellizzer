Lonicera serpyllifolia

Lonicera serpyllifolia, commonly known as creeping honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub with slender, prostrate stems that often form dense mats. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or white, blooming in early spring. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Lonicera serpyllifolia is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil.
