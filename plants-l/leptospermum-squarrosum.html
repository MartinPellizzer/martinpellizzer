Leptospermum squarrosum

Leptospermum squarrosum, commonly known as the squarrosum tea tree, is a small evergreen shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is part of the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known species like eucalyptus and tea tree. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

The leaves are used in traditional Aboriginal medicine and have antimicrobial properties.
