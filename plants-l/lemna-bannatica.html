Lemna bannatica

Lemna bannatica, commonly known as the Bannatic watermeal, is a small, free-floating aquatic plant belonging to the Lemnaceae family.

It is characterized by its tiny, oval-shaped leaves that are usually less than 1 centimeter in diameter. The plant lacks true roots and stems, and its reproduction is primarily through vegetative propagation via the production of daughter plants. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds, ditches, and marshes.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become a nuisance in aquatic ecosystems if not managed properly.
