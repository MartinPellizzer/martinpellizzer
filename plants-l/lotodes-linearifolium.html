Lotodes linearifolium

Lotodes linearifolium, commonly known as the slender-leaved water lily, is a aquatic plant native to parts of Asia.

It features broad, floating leaves that are typically linear in shape, with a waxy surface to repel water. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the early morning and close by midday. Its roots anchor in the mud at the bottom of ponds and lakes, while its stems extend upward to support the foliage.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various water organisms.
