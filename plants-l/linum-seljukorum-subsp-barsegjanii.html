Linum seljukorum subsp. barsegjanii

Linum seljukorum subsp.

barsegjanii, commonly known as the Armenian flax, is a flowering plant species native to the highlands of Armenia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces clusters of blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique genetic makeup and limited distribution.
