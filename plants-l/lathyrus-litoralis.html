Lathyrus litoralis

Lathyrus litoralis, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and North Africa.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive spiny leaves and showy blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky coastal habitats, thriving in saline and well-drained soils. Its growth habit is upright, with stems that can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

Lathyrus litoralis plays a role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
