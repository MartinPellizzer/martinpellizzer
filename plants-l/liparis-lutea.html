Liparis lutea

Liparis lutea, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and the Himalayas, and thrives in temperate to subtropical climates. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces vibrant yellow flowers with a distinct, often fragrant scent. Liparis lutea is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or rocky slopes.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for both ornamental gardening and botanical study.
