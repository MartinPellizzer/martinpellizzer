Lobelia griffithii var. dopatrioides

Lobelia griffithii var. dopatrioides, commonly known as the Griffith's lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its small, tubular blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety hair.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
