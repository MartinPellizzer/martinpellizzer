Littonia obscura

Littonia obscura, commonly known as the obscure littonia, is a flowering plant native to the subtropical regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in shades of pale yellow to white. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its limited distribution and unique floral structure, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
