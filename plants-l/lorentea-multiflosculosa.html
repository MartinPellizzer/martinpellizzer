Lorentea multiflosculosa

Lorentea multiflosculosa, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to purple. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy addition to alpine gardens. Its flowering period typically occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Lorentea multiflosculosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in natural and cultivated landscapes.
