Lathyrus lycicus subsp. phaselitanus

Lathyrus lycicus subsp.

phaselitanus, commonly known as the Phaselitanus pea, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in early summer. Its pods contain several seeds, which are adapted for dispersal by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
