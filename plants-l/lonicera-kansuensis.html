Lonicera kansuensis

Lonicera kansuensis, commonly known as the Kansu honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but not typically consumed by humans. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

Lonicera kansuensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
