Lonchocarpus caribaeus

Lonchocarpus caribaeus, commonly known as the Caribbean senna, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers, which are typical of the Fabaceae family. The plant produces distinctive pod-like fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a fleshy, red aril. It is often found in moist, shaded areas such as forest understories and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for horticultural purposes.
