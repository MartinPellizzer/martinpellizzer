Luzula leptophylla

Luzula leptophylla, commonly known as slender-leaf wood sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its narrow, grass-like leaves are arranged in a triangular cross-section, a characteristic feature of many sedges.

Luzula leptophylla produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spike-like clusters, which are often pollinated by wind.
