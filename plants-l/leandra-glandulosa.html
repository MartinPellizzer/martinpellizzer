Leandra glandulosa

Leandra glandulosa, commonly known as the sticky leandra, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its glandular hairs that cover its stems and leaves, giving it a sticky texture. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
