Laricorchis mapiriensis

Laricorchis mapiriensis, commonly known as the Mapiri orchid, is a rare and endemic species native to the cloud forests of northern South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments with well-drained soil.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
