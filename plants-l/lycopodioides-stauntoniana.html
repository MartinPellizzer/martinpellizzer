Lycopodioides stauntoniana

Lycopodioides stauntoniana, commonly known as the Staunton's clubmoss, is a species of lycophyte belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is a small, evergreen shrub characterized by its dense, needle-like leaves and spore-producing cones. Native to the southeastern United States, it thrives in moist, acidic soils in forest understories. The plant exhibits a unique reproductive system, with sporophytes producing spores in specialized structures called sporangia.

Its morphology reflects ancient evolutionary traits, making it a valuable subject for botanical study.
