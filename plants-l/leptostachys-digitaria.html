Leptostachys digitaria

Leptostachys digitaria, commonly known as the slender-stemmed grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its narrow, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces panicles of small, greenish flowers, which are arranged in a loose, open cluster. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in landscaping or as a forage crop in certain regions.
