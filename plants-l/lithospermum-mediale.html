Lithospermum mediale

Lithospermum mediale, commonly known as the narrowleaf lithospermum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy, violet to purple flowers bloom in clusters during the summer months.

The roots of Lithospermum mediale contain compounds that have been studied for their potential medicinal properties.
