Linaria lasiopoda

Linaria lasiopoda, commonly known as the hairy toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Linaria lasiopoda is often found in alpine environments and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
