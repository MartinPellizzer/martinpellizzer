Lepisorus luchunensis

Lepisorus luchunensis, commonly known as the Luchun moss fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, creeping growth habit and small, ovate to lanceolate leaves. The plant typically forms dense, mat-like colonies on rocky substrates, often in shaded, humid environments. Its fronds are usually glossy and have a distinct midrib, with serrated or toothed margins.

Lepisorus luchunensis is native to China and is often found in mountainous regions with cool, moist conditions.
