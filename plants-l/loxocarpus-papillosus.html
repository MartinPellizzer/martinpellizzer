Loxocarpus papillosus

Loxocarpus papillosus, commonly known as the silky sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as swamps, bogs, and stream margins.

Loxocarpus papillosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
