Lupinus splendens

Lupinus splendens, commonly known as the golden lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of bright yellow or orange flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces legume pods that contain several seeds. Lupinus splendens is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats.

It is a member of the legume family and has the ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
