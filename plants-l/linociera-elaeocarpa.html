Linociera elaeocarpa

Linociera elaeocarpa, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and rich color, though it is also listed as an endangered species due to overharvesting.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is known for its ecological importance in native forests.
