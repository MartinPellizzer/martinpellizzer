Lobivia steinmannii var. christinae

Lobivia steinmannii var. christinae, commonly known as the Christina Lobivia, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded shape and fleshy, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the growing season. Its leaves are typically spine-tipped and arranged in a rosette pattern, providing structural support and reducing water loss.

This variety is prized for its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, making it a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
