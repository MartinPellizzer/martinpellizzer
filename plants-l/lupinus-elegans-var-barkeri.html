Lupinus elegans var. barkeri

Lupinus elegans var. barkeri, commonly known as Barker's lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces legume pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Barker's lupine is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
