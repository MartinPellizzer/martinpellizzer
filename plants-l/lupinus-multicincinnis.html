Lupinus multicincinnis

Lupinus multicincinnis, commonly known as the clustered lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of small, pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets, and its root system includes nitrogen-fixing nodules, which enhance soil fertility.

Lupinus multicincinnis is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
