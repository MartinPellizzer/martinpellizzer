Lecythis usitata

Lecythis usitata, commonly known as the Brazil nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and produces a heavy, woody fruit that contains up to 20 seeds, which are the edible Brazil nuts. The tree can grow up to 50 meters tall and has a dense canopy that provides habitat for various wildlife species.

Its flowers are yellowish-green and are pollinated by insects, while its seeds are dispersed by animals that consume the nuts.
