Lupinus neocotus

Lupinus neocotus, commonly known as the New Zealand lupin, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, spindly growth habit. The plant produces clusters of pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Lupinus neocotus is adapted to grow in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions of New Zealand.
