Lythrum salicaria f. virescens

Lythrum salicaria f. virescens, commonly known as the green-stem lythrum, is a variant of the common loosestrife.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and showy pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. It has a robust root system that helps stabilize soil in moist environments.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for wetland restoration projects.
