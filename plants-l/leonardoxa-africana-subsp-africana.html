Leonardoxa africana subsp. africana

Leonardoxa africana subsp.

africana, commonly known as the African sycamore, is a large, deciduous tree native to tropical Africa. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellow flowers.

The tree produces long, flat pods that contain seeds dispersed by wind. It is often found in moist, fertile soils along riverbanks and in lowland forests.

Its rapid growth and hardy nature make it an important species for reforestation and ecological restoration.
