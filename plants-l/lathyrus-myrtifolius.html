Lathyrus myrtifolius

Lathyrus myrtifolius, commonly known as the myrtle-leaved pea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves that resemble those of myrtle, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, pea-like flowers in shades of pink or white, which bloom in late spring. Lathyrus myrtifolius is typically found in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and flowers.
