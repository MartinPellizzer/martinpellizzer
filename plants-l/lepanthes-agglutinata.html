Lepanthes agglutinata

Lepanthes agglutinata, commonly known as the sticky leech orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its unique, often hairy or sticky floral structures that aid in pollination by specific insects. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, tropical environments. Its flowers are usually small and fragrant, displaying a range of colors from white to yellow.

Lepanthes agglutinata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitats.
