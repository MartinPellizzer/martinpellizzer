Lepanthes serriola

Lepanthes serriola, commonly known as the serrated orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant grows as an epiphyte, often found on trees in humid tropical environments. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a rosette pattern, helping it to retain moisture.

Lepanthes serriola is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
