Lupinus vandykeae

Lupinus vandykeae, commonly known as Vandyke's lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States, particularly found in California.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils. It has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds.

Lupinus vandykeae is also noted for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for certain wildlife species.
