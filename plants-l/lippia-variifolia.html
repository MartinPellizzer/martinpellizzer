Lippia variifolia

Lippia variifolia, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its delicate, bluish-gray foliage and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils. Lippia variifolia is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.

Its foliage can be used in herbal teas, though it is more commonly appreciated for its aesthetic qualities in gardens.
