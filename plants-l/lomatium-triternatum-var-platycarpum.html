Lomatium triternatum var. platycarpum

Lomatium triternatum var. platycarpum, commonly known as the broad-fruited lomatium, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and forest edges.
