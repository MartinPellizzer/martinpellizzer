Lomaria egenolfioides

Lomaria egenolfioides, commonly known as the Egenolf's lomaria, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky areas. The plant features slender, upright stems with small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually white or pale blue, clustered in dense inflorescences, and bloom during the warmer months.

Lomaria egenolfioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in alpine gardens.
