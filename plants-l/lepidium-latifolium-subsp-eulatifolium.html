Lepidium latifolium subsp. eulatifolium

Lepidium latifolium subsp.

eulatifolium, commonly known as broadleaf wallflower, is a herbaceous plant native to western North America. It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
