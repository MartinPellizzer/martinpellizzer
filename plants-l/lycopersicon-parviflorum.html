Lycopersicon parviflorum

Lycopersicon parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flower tomato, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshade plants like potatoes and peppers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring hairy stems and alternate leaves with serrated edges. Its small, white flowers are followed by bright red, oval-shaped fruits that are edible and used in culinary applications.

The plant thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
