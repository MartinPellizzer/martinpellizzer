Lycopus europaeus f. glabrescens

Lycopus europaeus f. glabrescens, commonly known as European bugleweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant features small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments like woodlands and stream banks.

Its leaves are usually hairy but can be smooth in this form, distinguishing it from other varieties.
