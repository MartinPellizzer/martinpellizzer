Leucaena sinaloensis

Leucaena sinaloensis, commonly known as the Sinaloan leucaena, is a shrub or small tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions, making it adaptable to different environments. It is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities, which improve soil fertility.

Additionally, its leaves are used as forage for livestock and in traditional medicine.
