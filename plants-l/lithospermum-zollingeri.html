Lithospermum zollingeri

Lithospermum zollingeri, commonly known as Zollinger's rock pepper, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and small, pale purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to harsh, dry conditions.
