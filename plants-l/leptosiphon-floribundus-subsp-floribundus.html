Leptosiphon floribundus subsp. floribundus

Leptosiphon floribundus subsp.

floribundus, commonly known as the desert honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments.

The plant features small, tubular flowers that are usually pink to purple in color, often blooming in clusters. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry climates.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging soil conditions.
