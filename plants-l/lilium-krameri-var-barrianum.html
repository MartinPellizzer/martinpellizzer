Lilium krameri var. barrianum

Lilium krameri var. barrianum, commonly known as the Turk's cap lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically bright red or orange, which contrast strikingly with its narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces a bulbous underground stem, which serves as a storage organ and helps it survive in drought conditions. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, and the plant is adapted to thrive in well-drained, sandy soils.

Lilium krameri var. barrianum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in suitable climates.
