Lobelia tenuior subsp. dictyosperma

Lobelia tenuior subsp.

dictyosperma, commonly known as the narrowleaf lobelia, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, tubular, and typically blue or purple, arranged in dense spikes.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
