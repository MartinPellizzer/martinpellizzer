Lindernia hirta

Lindernia hirta, commonly known as hairy lindernia, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Verbenaceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches. Lindernia hirta thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
