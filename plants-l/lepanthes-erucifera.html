Lepanthes erucifera

Lepanthes erucifera, commonly known as the dragon blood orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often red or orange-colored flowers that resemble drops of blood, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within tropical forests. Its inflorescence consists of a single, elongated spike bearing several showy flowers.

Lepanthes erucifera is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
