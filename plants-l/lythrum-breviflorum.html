Lythrum breviflorum

Lythrum breviflorum, commonly known as narrowleaf common loosestrife, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lythraceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, with a distinctive bilabiate corolla. It thrives in moist, sunny habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks.

Lythrum breviflorum is often found in temperate regions and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain areas.
