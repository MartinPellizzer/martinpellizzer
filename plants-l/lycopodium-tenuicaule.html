Lycopodium tenuicaule

Lycopodium tenuicaule, commonly known as the slender clubmoss, is a small, evergreen fern-like plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, thread-like leaves that grow in dense clusters, giving it a feathery appearance. This plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices, where it can absorb sufficient humidity. Its spore-producing structures, called strobili, are borne on the upper parts of the plant and are essential for its reproductive cycle.

Lycopodium tenuicaule plays a role in soil stabilization and provides microhabitats for various small organisms.
