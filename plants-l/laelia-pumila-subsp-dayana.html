Laelia pumila subsp. dayana

Laelia pumila subsp.

dayana, commonly known as the dwarf laelia, is a small orchid native to the Andes Mountains of South America. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and yellow.

The plant prefers cool, humid environments and is often found growing at high altitudes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces a single, upright inflorescence with several flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
