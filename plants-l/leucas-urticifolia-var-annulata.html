Leucas urticifolia var. annulata

Leucas urticifolia var. annulata, commonly known as the ringed leucas, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and spikes of small, pale blue or purple flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ethnobotanical studies.
