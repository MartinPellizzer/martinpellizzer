Leucadendron prostratum

Leucadendron prostratum, commonly known as the coastal sunflower, is a low-growing shrub native to South Africa.

It features dense, silver-gray foliage that reflects sunlight, helping it thrive in coastal environments. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers with a distinctive, spiky center, which attract pollinators like bees and birds. It is well adapted to sandy soils and salt spray, making it a hardy coastal plant.

Leucadendron prostratum is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
