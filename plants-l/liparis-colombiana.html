Liparis colombiana

Liparis colombiana, commonly known as the Colombian orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Andean regions of Colombia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and clustered flowering stems. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive lip that is often darker in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is adapted to the mountainous terrain of its native habitat.

Liparis colombiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
