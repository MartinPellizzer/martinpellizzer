Lingoum indicum

Lingoum indicum, commonly known as the Indian garlic chives, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its long, narrow, grass-like leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers in the summer months. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, often used in traditional cuisines.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental and culinary plant.
