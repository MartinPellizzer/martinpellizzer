Leptecophylla abietina

Leptecophylla abietina, commonly known as the silver pine or white pine, is a coniferous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves that are typically silver-white in color, giving it its common name. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Leptecophylla abietina is valued for its timber and has cultural significance in some indigenous communities.
