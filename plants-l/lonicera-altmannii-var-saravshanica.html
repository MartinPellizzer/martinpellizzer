Lonicera altmannii var. saravshanica

Lonicera altmannii var. saravshanica, commonly known as the Saravshan honeysuckle, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. These flowers are attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The fruit is a red, fleshy berry that ripens in late summer and is edible for both humans and wildlife.
