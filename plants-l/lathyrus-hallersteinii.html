Lathyrus hallersteinii

Lathyrus hallersteinii, commonly known as the Haller's pea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, pea-like flowers in shades of pink or purple. Its pods contain several seeds, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Botanically, it belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its nitrogen-fixing ability through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules.
