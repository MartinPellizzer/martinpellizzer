Laburnum vulgare var. aureum

Laburnum vulgare var. aureum, commonly known as the golden chain tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe.

It is characterized by its long, drooping clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces elongated pods that contain flattened, winged seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to garden environments.
