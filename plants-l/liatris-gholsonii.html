Liatris gholsonii

Liatris gholsonii, commonly known as Gholson's blazing star, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small clusters of individual flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it well adapted to xeric environments. Its showy purple to pink flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Liatris gholsonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
