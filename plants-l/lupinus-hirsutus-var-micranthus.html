Lupinus hirsutus var. micranthus

Lupinus hirsutus var. micranthus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of pea-like, blue to purple flowers. The plant has narrow, hairy leaves and produces legume pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Lupinus hirsutus var. micranthus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in nitrogen fixation in soil.
