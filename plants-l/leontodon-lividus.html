Leontodon lividus

Leontodon lividus, commonly known as the purple leontodon, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, grassy habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. The plant features basal leaves that are often hairy and have a bluish-green hue, while its flowering stems bear yellow flowers with five petals. Its flowering period usually occurs in late spring to early summer.

The species is characterized by its distinctive seed heads, which are often covered in fine, bristly hairs.
