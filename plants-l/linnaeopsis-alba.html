Linnaeopsis alba

Linnaeopsis alba, commonly known as the white lily of the Nile, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, showy white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a long history of use in traditional medicine.

Linnaeopsis alba prefers full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for gardens in warmer climates.
