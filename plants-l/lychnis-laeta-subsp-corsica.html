Lychnis laeta subsp. corsica

Lychnis laeta subsp.

corsica, commonly known as Corsican campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or limestone soils.

The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained conditions.
