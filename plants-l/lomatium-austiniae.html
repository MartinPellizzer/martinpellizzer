Lomatium austiniae

Lomatium austiniae, commonly known as Austin's lomatium, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and umbellate flowers.

Native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States, it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and meadows. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of soft, woolly hair. Its flowers are small and clustered in umbrella-like clusters, producing a delicate, yellowish-green hue.

Lomatium austiniae is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
