Ligusticum brunonis

Ligusticum brunonis, commonly known as the Italian lovage, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with fern-like, deeply lobed leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family.

Ligusticum brunonis is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
