Layia pentachaeta subsp. albida

Layia pentachaeta subsp.

albida, commonly known as the white globe mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its showy, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often branched and upright.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive, globe-like flower heads.
