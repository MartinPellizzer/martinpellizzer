Loranthus leptanthus

Loranthus leptanthus, commonly known as the slender mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches itself to the branches of its host trees, primarily in the genus Eucalyptus.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using the light it captures. The plant has small, scale-like leaves and produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are often visited by insects for pollination. Its berries are typically red or orange and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

Loranthus leptanthus is native to Australia and is an example of a hemiparasitic species that plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its host trees.
