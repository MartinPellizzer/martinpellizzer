Litsea foliosa var. puncticulata

Litsea foliosa var. puncticulata, commonly known as the punctuated leaf litsea, is a variant of the litsea genus native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology, featuring small, raised punctures along the leaf surface. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are usually elliptical or ovate, with a smooth upper surface and a hairy underside.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
