Lophochloa phleoides var. trichantha

Lophochloa phleoides var. trichantha, commonly known as the hairy lophochloa, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves with a distinct hairy texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the vegetation in its native ecosystems.
