Litsea aureosericea

Litsea aureosericea, commonly known as the golden-haired litsea, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its delicate, hair-covered leaves that give it its common name. Its flowers are small and white, often clustered in panicles, and it produces small, orange berries.

Litsea aureosericea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and fragrant aroma.
