Lycianthes inconspicua

Lycianthes inconspicua, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous annual with slender, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the morning, giving it its common name. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain several seeds.

Lycianthes inconspicua is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.
