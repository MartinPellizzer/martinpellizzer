Linaria reflexa var. alba

Linaria reflexa var. alba, commonly known as the white toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and often appear in pairs along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Its ornamental value comes from its delicate blooms and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
