Lepanthes systole

Lepanthes systole, commonly known as the systole orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, tropical environments. Its flowers are usually pale green to white with intricate patterns, and it blooms during the rainy season.

Lepanthes systole is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
