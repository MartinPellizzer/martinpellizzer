Lobelia angolensis

Lobelia angolensis, commonly known as the Angola lily, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. It belongs to the Campanulaceae family, which includes other lobelias and bellflowers.

Lobelia angolensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
