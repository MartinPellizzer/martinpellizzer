Lilium philadelphicum var. angustifolium

Lilium philadelphicum var. angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved fawn lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features grass-like basal leaves that are narrow and strap-shaped, often with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, typically appearing in early spring. It thrives in woodland habitats and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
