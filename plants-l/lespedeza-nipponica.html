Lespedeza nipponica

Lespedeza nipponica, commonly known as Japanese lespedeza, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in forest understories and along stream banks, contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for various insects.

Lespedeza nipponica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
