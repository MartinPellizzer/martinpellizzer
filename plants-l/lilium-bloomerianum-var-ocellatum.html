Lilium bloomerianum var. ocellatum

Lilium bloomerianum var. ocellatum, commonly known as the spotted lily, is a striking bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, often with a distinctive red or orange spot at the base of the petals. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. It is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.

The variety's unique spotting adds to its visual appeal, making it a standout in floral displays.
