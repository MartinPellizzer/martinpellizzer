Lodhra nervosa

Lodhra nervosa, commonly known as the sacred Indian lotus, is a woody shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Nyctaginaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by yellowish fruits containing seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded areas near water bodies.

Due to its cultural and medicinal significance, it is widely revered in traditional Ayurvedic practices.
