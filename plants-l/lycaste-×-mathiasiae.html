Lycaste × mathiasiae

Lycaste × mathiasiae, commonly known as the hybrid cyclamen, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Lycaste and Mathiasia species.

It is characterized by its attractive, velvety leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant is native to the Andes Mountains and thrives in cool, humid environments. It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ornamental value and ease of care.

Lycaste × mathiasiae prefers well-draining soil and partial shade, making it suitable for indoor or outdoor cultivation in temperate climates.
