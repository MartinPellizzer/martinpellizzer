Lepanthes corazonis

Lepanthes corazonis, commonly known as the Heart of the Forest orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that often feature intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually small to medium in size, with a unique structure that includes a spurred lip, a feature common in many orchid species.

Lepanthes corazonis is valued for its ornamental beauty and plays a significant role in the biodiversity of its native ecosystems.
