Launaea nana

Launaea nana, commonly known as the dwarf launaea, is a small, succulent shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and fleshy, lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in spherical heads, typical of the daisy family. It is well adapted to drought conditions, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems and leaves.

Launaea nana is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its resilience in harsh environments.
