Lathyrus debilis var. arvensis

Lathyrus debilis var. arvensis, commonly known as the slender vetch, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces trifoliate leaves and small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and taxonomic studies.
