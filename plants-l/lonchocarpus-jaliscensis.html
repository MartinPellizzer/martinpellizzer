Lonchocarpus jaliscensis

Lonchocarpus jaliscensis, commonly known as the Mexican sensitive plant, is a species of legume native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves that fold shut when touched. The plant produces distinctive yellow flowers followed by elongated, spiny pods. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its sensitivity to touch, it is sometimes referred to as a "shy" plant.
