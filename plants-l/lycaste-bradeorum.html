Lycaste bradeorum

Lycaste bradeorum, commonly known as the Brade's lycaste, is a rare and striking orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers with intricate patterns and vibrant colors. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its pseudobulbs are thick and segmented, supporting the plant's growth and storing moisture.

Lycaste bradeorum is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and challenging cultivation requirements.
