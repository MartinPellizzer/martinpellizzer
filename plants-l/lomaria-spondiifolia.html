Lomaria spondiifolia

Lomaria spondiifolia, commonly known as the spindling lomaria, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky soils, thriving in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are elongated and often have a woolly texture, while its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in dense clusters.

Lomaria spondiifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
