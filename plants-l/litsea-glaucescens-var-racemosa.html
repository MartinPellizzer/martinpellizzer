Litsea glaucescens var. racemosa

Litsea glaucescens var. racemosa, commonly known as the racemose litsea, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, elliptical leaves that are often glossy and dark green. It produces small, white, fragrant flowers in racemose inflorescences, which are clusters of flowers arranged along a central axis.

The fruit is a small, dark berry that contains a single seed, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
