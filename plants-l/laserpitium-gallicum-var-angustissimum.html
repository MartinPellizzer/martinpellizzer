Laserpitium gallicum var. angustissimum

Laserpitium gallicum var. angustissimum, commonly known as the narrowleaf gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface.

It produces small, tubular flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
