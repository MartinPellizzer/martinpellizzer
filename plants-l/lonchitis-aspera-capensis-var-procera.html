Lonchitis-aspera capensis var. procera

Lonchitis-aspera capensis var. procera, commonly known as the Cape roughbark, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its rough, asperous bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat preferences, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
