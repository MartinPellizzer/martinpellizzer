Linum piligerum

Linum piligerum, commonly known as the hairy flax, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the genus *Linum* in the family Plantaginaceae. The plant is characterized by its slender stems and small, blue or purple flowers. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Linum piligerum is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.
