Lobelia chamissoniana

Lobelia chamissoniana, commonly known as the dwarf blue lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of less than 10 inches, featuring delicate blue or violet flowers. The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are clustered in dense whorls.

Lobelia chamissoniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
