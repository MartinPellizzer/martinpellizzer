Licuala simplex

Licuala simplex, commonly known as the "sago palm," is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves that can grow up to several meters in diameter. The plant has a short trunk and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into black, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and as a decorative element in landscaping.

Botanically, it belongs to the Arecaceae family and is valued for its ornamental and practical uses in tropical regions.
