Lycopodium caudifolium

Lycopodium caudifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant features whorled, needle-like leaves that are often reddish in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its reproductive structures, called strobila, are cone-like and produce spores for propagation.

Lycopodium caudifolium plays a role in maintaining soil stability and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
