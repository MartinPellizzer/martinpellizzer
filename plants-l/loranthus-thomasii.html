Loranthus thomasii

Loranthus thomasii, commonly known as the Thomas' mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of host trees, primarily in the genus Eucalyptus.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using the light it captures. The plant has small, scale-like leaves and produces white or yellowish flowers that attract insects for pollination. Its berries are usually red or orange and contain sticky seeds that adhere to the bark of host trees, facilitating propagation.

This species is native to Australia and is often found in woodland and forested areas.
