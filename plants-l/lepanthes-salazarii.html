Lepanthes salazarii

Lepanthes salazarii, commonly known as the Salazar's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers with a distinctive coloration, often featuring white and green hues. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Lepanthes salazarii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
