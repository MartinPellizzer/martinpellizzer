Lupinus famelicus

Lupinus famelicus, commonly known as the poor man's lupin, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of bright yellow to orange flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces elongated pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Lupinus famelicus is a member of the legume family and has the ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
