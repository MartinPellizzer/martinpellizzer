Linaria leptoceras

Linaria leptoceras, commonly known as the slender toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its delicate, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its slender stems and narrow leaves contribute to its common name, while its nectar-rich flowers attract a variety of pollinators.

Linaria leptoceras is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and easy care requirements.
