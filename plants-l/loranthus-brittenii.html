Loranthus brittenii

Loranthus brittenii, commonly known as the brittle loranthus, is a parasitic plant belonging to the Loranthaceae family.

It attaches itself to the branches of its host trees, typically using haustoria to extract water and nutrients. The plant produces small, red or orange berries that contain seeds dispersed by animals. It is native to regions of East Asia, including China and Japan.

Its botanical features include opposite, simple leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers.
