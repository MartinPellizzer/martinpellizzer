Linanthus harknessii var. septentrionalis

Linanthus harknessii var. septentrionalis, commonly known as the northern flaxvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pale blue or white in color. It has small, compound leaves and is adapted to grow in dry, rocky soils.

This variety is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe.
