Leucas zeylanica var. walkeri

Leucas zeylanica var. walkeri, commonly known as the purple leucas, is a flowering plant native to southern India.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
