Leymus obvipodus

Leymus obvipodus, commonly known as the smooth brome, is a perennial grass species native to Eurasia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, hairless stems. The plant produces dense clusters of spikelets, each containing two to three small, awned flowers. Its leaves are narrow, flat, and often have a bluish-green hue.

Leymus obvipodus is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed areas.
