Leptotaenia multifida var. eatonii

Leptotaenia multifida var. eatonii, commonly known as the Eaton's splitleaf, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves that are deeply divided, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in clusters during the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique addition to wetland and woodland habitats.
