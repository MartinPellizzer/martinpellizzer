Laurus leucocarpa

Laurus leucocarpa, commonly known as white bay or white laurier, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the coastal regions of southern Africa, particularly South Africa. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, glossy, dark green leaves and small white flowers that appear in clusters. Its fruits are small, round, and initially green, turning yellow or orange as they mature.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
