Lepanthes byfieldii

Lepanthes byfieldii, commonly known as the Byfield's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and purple. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a basal rosette.

Lepanthes byfieldii thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in tropical forests.
