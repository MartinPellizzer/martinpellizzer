Lasiacis guaranitica

Lasiacis guaranitica, commonly known as the Guarani palm, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fruits that are typically consumed by local communities.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
