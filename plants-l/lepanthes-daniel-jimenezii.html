Lepanthes daniel-jimenezii

Lepanthes daniel-jimenezii, commonly known as the Daniel Jiménez orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments within montane forests. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces intricate, fragrant flowers with a distinctive purple and white coloration. It is named in honor of the botanist Daniel Jiménez, who contributed significantly to the study of orchids in the region.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
