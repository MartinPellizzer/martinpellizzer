Lycaste macrophylla subsp. viridescens

Lycaste macrophylla subsp.

viridescens, commonly known as the green-veined orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America. It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pale yellow, with prominent green veins on the petals.

The plant has strap-like leaves that are often broad and lance-shaped, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This subspecies is valued for its beauty and is often cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
