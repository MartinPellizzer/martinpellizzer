Lepanthes eruca

Lepanthes eruca, commonly known as the orchid mantis, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, orchid-like flowers that resemble the appearance of a mantis, a unique form of mimicry. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within tropical forests. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with intricate patterns that help it blend into its surroundings.

Lepanthes eruca is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated by enthusiasts of rare and exotic plants.
