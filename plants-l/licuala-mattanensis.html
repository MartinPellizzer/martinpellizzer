Licuala mattanensis

Licuala mattanensis, commonly known as the Malay palm, is a species of palm native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter. The plant typically grows in wet, tropical environments, often near water bodies. Its trunk is relatively short and covered with persistent leaf bases.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic qualities in gardens.
