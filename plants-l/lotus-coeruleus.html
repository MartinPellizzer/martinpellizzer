Lotus coeruleus

Lotus coeruleus, commonly known as the blue lotus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue petals and a white inner bloom, which resemble a lotus flower. The plant thrives in aquatic or moist environments and is often found in ponds and wetlands. Its flowers open at night and close during the day, a unique circadian behavior.

The botanical name reflects its distinctive blue coloration, making it a popular subject in horticulture and traditional medicine.
