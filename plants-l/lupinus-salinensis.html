Lupinus salinensis

Lupinus salinensis, commonly known as the saltbush lupine, is a perennial herb native to coastal regions of California and Baja California.

It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces sparsely branched clusters of pea-like flowers in shades of pink to purple. Lupinus salinensis plays a vital role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for native wildlife.

Its ecological significance makes it an important species for coastal restoration projects.
