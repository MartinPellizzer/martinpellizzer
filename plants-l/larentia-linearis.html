Larentia linearis

Larentia linearis, commonly known as the slender larentia, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and small, delicate flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or along stream banks. Its root system is fibrous, allowing it to anchor firmly in soil while absorbing nutrients efficiently.

Larentia linearis plays a role in supporting local pollinators and contributes to the biodiversity of its ecosystem.
