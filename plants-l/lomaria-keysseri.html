Lomaria keysseri

Lomaria keysseri, commonly known as the Easter lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and has strap-like, evergreen leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

The Easter lily is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and is a symbol of renewal and hope.
