Lamprocaulos grandis

Lamprocaulos grandis, commonly known as the grand leek orchid, is an epiphytic plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, leek-like foliage. The plant produces tall, erect flowering stems with clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically growing on trees or rocks.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
