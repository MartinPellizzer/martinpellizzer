Lonicera sempervirens f. sulphurea

Lonicera sempervirens f. sulphurea, commonly known as the golden honeysuckle, is a unique variant of the native American honeysuckle.

This plant is characterized by its striking yellow flowers, which contrast beautifully with its dark green foliage. It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet, producing fragrant, tubular flowers in the spring. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a glossy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
