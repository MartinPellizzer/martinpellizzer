Linaria spuria var. toussaintii

Linaria spuria var. toussaintii, commonly known as the toussaint's toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a perennial herb with slender, branched stems and small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
