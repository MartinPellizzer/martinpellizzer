Lolium remotum var. oliganthum

Lolium remotum var. oliganthum, commonly known as the slender ryegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils.

Due to its hardiness and rapid growth, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
