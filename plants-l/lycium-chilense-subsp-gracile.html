Lycium chilense subsp. gracile

Lycium chilense subsp.

gracile, commonly known as the Chilean boxthorn, is a shrub native to southern Chile and parts of Argentina. It typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often in coastal areas and lowland deserts.

The plant features small, spiny leaves and produces yellowish-orange berries that are edible but have a bitter taste. Its botanical structure includes woody stems with thorns and small, ovate leaves with a leathery texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
