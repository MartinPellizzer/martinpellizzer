Limonium relicticum

Limonium relicticum, commonly known as the relic sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, thriving in sandy or gravelly soils with good drainage. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slightly succulent texture.

Limonium relicticum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
