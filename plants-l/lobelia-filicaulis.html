Lobelia filicaulis

Lobelia filicaulis, commonly known as the velvetleaf lobelia, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in spikes at the top of the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands.

Lobelia filicaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
