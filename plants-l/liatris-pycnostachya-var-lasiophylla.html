Liatris pycnostachya var. lasiophylla

Liatris pycnostachya var. lasiophylla, commonly known as the showy blazing star, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small clusters of flowers. The plant features tall, upright stems covered in narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces striking purple to pink flower spikes in late summer. Its botanical structure supports pollination by bees and other insects, contributing to its ecological importance.

The variety lasiophylla is distinguished by its dense, showy flower heads and slightly hairy leaves.
