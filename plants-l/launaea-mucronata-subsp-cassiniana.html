Launaea mucronata subsp. cassiniana

Launaea mucronata subsp.

cassiniana, commonly known as the narrowleaf launaea, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its erect, sparsely branched stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in composite heads, typical of the Asteraceae family. It is typically found in arid or semi-arid regions, often growing in rocky soils or along dry riverbeds.

This subspecies is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient plant in harsh environments.
