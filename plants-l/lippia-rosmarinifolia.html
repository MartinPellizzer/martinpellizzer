Lippia rosmarinifolia

Lippia rosmarinifolia, commonly known as lemon verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring aromatic, dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are widely used in herbal teas and culinary applications due to their citrusy fragrance.

Lippia rosmarinifolia thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal cultivation.
