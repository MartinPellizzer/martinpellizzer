Lobivia acanthoplegma

Lobivia acanthoplegma, commonly known as the Canary Island cactus, is a species of cactus native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and is often covered in dense, white to grayish spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
