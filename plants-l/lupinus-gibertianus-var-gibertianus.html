Lupinus gibertianus var. gibertianus

Lupinus gibertianus var. gibertianus, commonly known as the Desert Lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and clusters of showy blue to violet flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces legume pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Lupinus gibertianus var. gibertianus is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for ecological restoration projects.
