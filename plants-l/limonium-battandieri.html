Limonium battandieri

Limonium battandieri, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spikes of blue or purple flowers. The plant thrives in coastal environments and well-drained soils, often growing in sandy or rocky habitats. It is a perennial species that produces small, showy flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Limonium battandieri is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate salt and wind.
