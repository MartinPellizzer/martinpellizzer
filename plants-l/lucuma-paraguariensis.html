Lucuma paraguariensis

Lucuma paraguariensis, commonly known as the "peach of the Andes," is a South American fruit tree native to the Andean regions of Peru and Bolivia.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and yellow-orange fruit that ripens in the dry season. The fruit has a sweet, nutty flavor and is often used in traditional Andean cuisine. It is also valued for its nutritional content, including high levels of carbohydrates and dietary fiber.

The plant's ornamental value and unique fruit make it a significant species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
