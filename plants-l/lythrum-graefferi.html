Lythrum graefferi

Lythrum graefferi, commonly known as the redroot lythrum, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Lythraceae family, which includes plants known for their showy flowers and often aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks. Its striking red or purple flowers bloom in summer, and the plant is characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves.

Lythrum graefferi is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
