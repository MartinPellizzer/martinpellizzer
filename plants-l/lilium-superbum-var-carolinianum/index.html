Lilium Superbum Var. Carolinianum

Lilium superbum var. carolinianum is a striking variant of the Turk's cap lily native to the southeastern United States. This plant is known for its large, showy flowers that bloom in late spring and early summer. It thrives in woodland habitats and prefers moist, well-drained soil. The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance. Its unique characteristics make it a notable subject in botanical studies and garden cultivation.
