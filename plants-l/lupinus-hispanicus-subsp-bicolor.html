Lupinus hispanicus subsp. bicolor

Lupinus hispanicus subsp.

bicolor, commonly known as the Spanish lupine, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and North Africa. It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of blue or purple pea-shaped flowers.

The plant has compound leaves composed of several small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in rocky or coastal areas. Lupinus hispanicus subsp.

bicolor is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
