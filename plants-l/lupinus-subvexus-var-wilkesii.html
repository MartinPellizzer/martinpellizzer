Lupinus subvexus var. wilkesii

Lupinus subvexus var. wilkesii, commonly known as the Wilkes' lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Lupinus subvexus var. wilkesii plays a role in nitrogen fixation, making it ecologically significant in its native range.
