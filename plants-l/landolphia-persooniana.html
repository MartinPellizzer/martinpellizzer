Landolphia persooniana

Landolphia persooniana, commonly known as the African morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a climbing vine that produces large, showy flowers with five petals, often in shades of yellow or orange. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and thrives in warm, humid climates. It is often found in moist woodlands and along riverbanks.

Its botanical significance includes its use in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant.
