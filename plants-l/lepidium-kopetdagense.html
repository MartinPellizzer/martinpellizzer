Lepidium kopetdagense

Lepidium kopetdagense, commonly known as the Kopetdag cress, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia, particularly found in regions of Kazakhstan and Turkmenistan.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other well-known mustard plants. The plant is characterized by its small, white or pale yellow flowers and its pinnately divided leaves. It typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Lepidium kopetdagense is often found in open, sunny habitats and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
