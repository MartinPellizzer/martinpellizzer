Lepanthes calopetala

Lepanthes calopetala, commonly known as the showy orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, spurred flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical forests. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a rosette pattern, facilitating water retention.

Lepanthes calopetala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
