Lepanthes caroli-lueri

Lepanthes caroli-lueri, commonly known as the Caroli-Lueri orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in late summer. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in moisture retention.

The flowers are often pale yellow to white, with intricate patterns that attract specific pollinators.
