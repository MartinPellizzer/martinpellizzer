Lithocarpus Dodonaeifolius

Ironwood, scientifically known as Lithocarpus dodonaeifolius, is a remarkable evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia. This species is renowned for its exceptionally dense and durable wood, which has been historically valued for its strength and resistance to decay. Despite its ecological and economic importance, the Ironwood tree faces threats from deforestation and habitat loss. Its unique foliage and slow growth rate make it a key component of diverse forest ecosystems. Conservation efforts are essential to ensure the survival of this valuable and ecologically significant plant.
