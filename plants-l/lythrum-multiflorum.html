Lythrum multiflorum

Lythrum multiflorum, commonly known as purple loosestrife, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, spires of showy purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies.

Due to its aggressive growth, it is considered an invasive species in many regions of North America.
