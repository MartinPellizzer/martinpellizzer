Lupinus sericeus subsp. sabinii

Lupinus sericeus subsp.

sabinii, commonly known as the silver lupine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its silvery-blue flowers and hairy, slender stems.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets. Lupinus sericeus subsp.

sabinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
