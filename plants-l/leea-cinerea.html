Leea cinerea

Leea cinerea, commonly known as silverleaf leea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Pacific.

It is a small shrub or tree that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant has opposite, elliptical leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery or grayish pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in dense inflorescences, often blooming in summer.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, dry seeds.
