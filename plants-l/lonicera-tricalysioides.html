Lonicera tricalysioides

Lonicera tricalysioides, commonly known as the Japanese honeysuckle, is a deciduous climbing vine native to East Asia.

It features opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that ripens in the fall.

Lonicera tricalysioides is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
