Lophatherum pilosulum

Lophatherum pilosulum, commonly known as hairy lemongrass, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the Poaceae family. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their aromatic and medicinal properties.

Lophatherum pilosulum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.
