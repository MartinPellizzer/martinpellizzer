Lonicera watanabeana

Lonicera watanabeana, commonly known as the Watanabe honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Lonicera watanabeana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its role in supporting pollinators.
