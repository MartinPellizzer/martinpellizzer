Lechea maritima var. interior

Lechea maritima var. interior, commonly known as the inland milkweed, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in disturbed or open habitats. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and the stems are often covered in fine, white hairs.

This species is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a hardy component of its native ecosystem.
