Leptosiphon oblanceolatus

Leptosiphon oblanceolatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant features opposite, oblanceolate leaves that are hairy and have a distinctive blue-green color. Its flowers are trumpet-shaped, with five petals, and are usually pale blue or lavender in color.

It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.
