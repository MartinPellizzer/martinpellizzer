Lerchenfeldia flexuosa subsp. montana

Lerchenfeldia flexuosa subsp.

montana, commonly known as the mountain lerchenfeldia, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of South America. It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions.

The plant features slender stems and small, clustered leaves that are adapted to withstand harsh mountain climates. Its flowers are usually pale blue or white, with five petals and a distinctive, spurred shape.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to high-altitude ecosystems.
