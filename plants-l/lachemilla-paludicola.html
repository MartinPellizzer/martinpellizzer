Lachemilla paludicola

Lachemilla paludicola, commonly known as the bog sedum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs, fens, and other wetland habitats. The plant features small, fleshy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces clusters of tiny, star-shaped flowers in the spring. It is adapted to nutrient-poor conditions and plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.

Lachemilla paludicola is an important species for both ecological and botanical studies due to its specialized habitat and unique morphology.
