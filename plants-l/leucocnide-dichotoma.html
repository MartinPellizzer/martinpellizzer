Leucocnide dichotoma

Leucocnide dichotoma, commonly known as the dichotomous leucocnide, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to twine around supports, often found in dense forest understories. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate or elliptical in shape. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically greenish-white in color, and it produces small, berry-like fruits.

Due to its aggressive growth habit, it can become invasive in certain environments if not managed properly.
