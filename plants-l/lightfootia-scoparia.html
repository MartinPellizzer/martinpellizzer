Lightfootia scoparia

Lightfootia scoparia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sandwort, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a small, perennial herb with succulent, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the cooler months. Its root system is shallow and fibrous, allowing it to absorb moisture from the soil efficiently.

Lightfootia scoparia is adapted to sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal or inland dune environments.
