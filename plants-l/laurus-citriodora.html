Laurus citriodora

Laurus citriodora, commonly known as lemon bay or citronella laurel, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glossy, dark green leaves that emit a strong citrus scent. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, lemon-scented berries. Its leaves are often used in cooking and traditional medicine due to their aromatic properties.

Laurus citriodora thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sun.
