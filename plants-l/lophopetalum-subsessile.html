Lophopetalum subsessile

Lophopetalum subsessile, commonly known as the Mexican flame bush, is a deciduous shrub native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It features showy, bright orange to red flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has small, elliptical leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are often reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Lophopetalum subsessile is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in harsh, arid environments.
