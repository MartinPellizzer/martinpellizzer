Lens esculenta var. macrosperma

Lens esculenta var. macrosperma, commonly known as the large-seeded lentil, is a cultivated variety of the lentil plant.

It is characterized by its larger, more robust seeds compared to other lentil varieties. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being oval-shaped and slightly hairy.

The pods are typically green, containing several seeds that are rich in protein and nutrients.
