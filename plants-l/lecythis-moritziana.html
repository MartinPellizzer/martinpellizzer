Lecythis moritziana

Lecythis moritziana, commonly known as the Amazonian Brazil nut tree, is a large evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and large, palmately lobed leaves. The tree produces a unique, woody capsule that contains the edible Brazil nuts. Its flowers are small and greenish-yellow, typically appearing in clusters.

The plant plays a vital role in the ecosystem, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
