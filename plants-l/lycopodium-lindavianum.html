Lycopodium lindavianum

Lycopodium lindavianum, commonly known as the Lindau clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its creeping, branched stems that bear small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces sporangia in clusters called strobila, which are typically found at the tips of the branches. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices.

Lycopodium lindavianum plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
