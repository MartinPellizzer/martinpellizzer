Lophosoria polypodioides

Lophosoria polypodioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water fern, is a small, aquatic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater habitats such as ponds and streams. The plant has delicate, finely divided fronds that are often floating or submerged. Its reproductive structures include sporangia that produce spores for propagation.

Lophosoria polypodioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in aquatic ecosystems.
