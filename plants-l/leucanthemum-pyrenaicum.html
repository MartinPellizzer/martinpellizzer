Leucanthemum pyrenaicum

Leucanthemum pyrenaicum, commonly known as the Pyrenean ragwort, is a flowering plant native to the Pyrenees mountain range.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to white, with a central yellow disk, and it blooms from late spring to early autumn.

Leucanthemum pyrenaicum is often found in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands at high altitudes.
