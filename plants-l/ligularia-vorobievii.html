Ligularia vorobievii

Ligularia vorobievii, commonly known as the Vorobievii ligularia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often green with a distinctive white or yellow margin. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped cluster, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its foliage is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
