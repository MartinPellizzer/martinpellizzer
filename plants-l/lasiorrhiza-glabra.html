Lasiorrhiza glabra

Lasiorrhiza glabra, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has a shallow root system and is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to fix nitrogen.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
