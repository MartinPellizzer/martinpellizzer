Linanthus tularensis

Linanthus tularensis, commonly known as the Tulare eveningflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the eveningflower family, known for its showy, often pink or red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its small, narrow leaves are arranged oppositely along the stems.

Linanthus tularensis plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is of interest to botanists due to its ecological and evolutionary significance.
