Leucostegia amplissima

Leucostegia amplissima, commonly known as the giant marsh orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the wetlands and marshes of South America, particularly in Brazil and Argentina. This plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy white or pale pink flowers. The leaves are long and narrow, often emerging from the base of the plant.

It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in areas with standing water.
