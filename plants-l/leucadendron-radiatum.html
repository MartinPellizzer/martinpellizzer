Leucadendron radiatum

Leucadendron radiatum, commonly known as the king protea, is a striking South African shrub belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy flower heads composed of numerous pink or white ray florets surrounding a central cluster of smaller tubular flowers. The plant is native to the Cape Province and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils with full sun exposure. Its striking appearance makes it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and floral arrangements.

Leucadendron radiatum is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for pollinators and supporting local biodiversity.
