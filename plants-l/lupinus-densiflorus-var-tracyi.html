Lupinus densiflorus var. tracyi

Lupinus densiflorus var. tracyi, commonly known as Tracy's lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, fluffy clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Tracy's lupine plays an important role in ecological systems by fixing nitrogen into the soil, benefiting surrounding plant life.
