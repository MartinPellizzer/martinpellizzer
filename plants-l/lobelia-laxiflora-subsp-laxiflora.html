Lobelia laxiflora subsp. laxiflora

Lobelia laxiflora subsp.

laxiflora, commonly known as the slender lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color.

The leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem, often with a hairy texture. This species thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and garden cultivation.
