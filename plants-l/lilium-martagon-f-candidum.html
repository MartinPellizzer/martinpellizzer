Lilium martagon f. candidum

Lilium martagon f. candidum, commonly known as the white martagon lily, is a botanical variant of the martagon lily.

It is characterized by its pale white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features showy, recurved petals and a distinctive, clustered inflorescence. It is native to regions of Europe and is often found in woodland habitats.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
