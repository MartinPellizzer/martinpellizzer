Luzula plumosa var. acuminata

Luzula plumosa var. acuminata, commonly known as the slender wood rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, commonly referred to as the grass family, and is characterized by its upright, cylindrical stems and dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. The leaves are typically narrow, grass-like, and often covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This variety is noted for its slender growth habit and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
