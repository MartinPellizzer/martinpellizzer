Lobelia grimesiana

Lobelia grimesiana, commonly known as Grimes' lobelia, is a rare flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as pine forests and along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slight pubescence.

Lobelia grimesiana plays an important role in its ecosystem, providing nectar for pollinators and contributing to the biodiversity of its habitat.
