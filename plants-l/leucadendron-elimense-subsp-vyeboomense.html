Leucadendron elimense subsp. vyeboomense

Leucadendron elimense subsp.

vyeboomense, commonly known as the Vyeboom leucadendron, is a shrub native to South Africa. It is characterized by its dense, needle-like leaves and vibrant yellow to orange flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant is part of the Proteaceae family, which is known for its horticultural value and ecological importance. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental appeal and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
