Liparis elliptica

Liparis elliptica, commonly known as the elliptical orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and delicate, pendulous flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its roots form symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi, which aid in nutrient absorption.

Liparis elliptica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
