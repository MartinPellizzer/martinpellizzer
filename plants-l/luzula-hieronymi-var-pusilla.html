Luzula hieronymi var. pusilla

Luzula hieronymi var. pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf wood rush, is a small, herbaceous perennial belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of grass-like leaves and spikes of small, pale green to brownish flowers. Native to high-altitude regions of the Himalayas, this plant thrives in cool, moist environments. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form compact clumps, often found in rocky or alpine soils.

The plant is notable for its ornamental value and ecological role in stabilizing mountainous terrain.
