Lupinus littoralis var. variicolor

Lupinus littoralis var. variicolor, commonly known as the coastal lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Pacific coast of North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking, colorful flower spikes that range from purple to pink and white. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along coastal dunes and rocky shores. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

This species is well adapted to harsh coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.
