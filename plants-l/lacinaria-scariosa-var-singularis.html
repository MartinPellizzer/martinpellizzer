Lacinaria scariosa var. singularis

Lacinaria scariosa var. singularis, commonly known as the singular lacinaria, is a rare and striking member of the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, narrow, and often twisted leaves that are covered in a thin, papery layer. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish or reddish in color. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its limited distribution and unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
