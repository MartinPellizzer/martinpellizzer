Leucophyta multiflora

Leucophyta multiflora, commonly known as the silverleaf flax, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Leucophyta multiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
