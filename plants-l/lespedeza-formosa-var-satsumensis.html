Lespedeza formosa var. satsumensis

Lespedeza formosa var. satsumensis, commonly known as the Satsuma lespeza, is a flowering plant native to East Asia, particularly Japan.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring slender stems and compound leaves with oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, clustered pink to reddish-purple flowers in late spring to early summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and the ability to thrive in well-drained soils, making it suitable for ornamental and ecological landscaping.
