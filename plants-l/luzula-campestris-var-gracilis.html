Luzula campestris var. gracilis

Luzula campestris var. gracilis, commonly known as slender wood rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, commonly referred to as the grass family, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and grass-like foliage. The plant typically features slender, cylindrical stems that bear small, spike-like clusters of pale green to white flowers. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and often have a slight sheen, contributing to its ornamental appeal in moist, shaded garden settings.

Luzula campestris var. gracilis is adapted to wet or damp environments and is often found in meadows, stream banks, and woodland areas.
