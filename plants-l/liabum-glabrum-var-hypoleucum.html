Liabum glabrum var. hypoleucum

Liabum glabrum var. hypoleucum, commonly known as the white-flowered liabum, is a variant of the liabum plant characterized by its pale or white flowers.

It is a herbaceous perennial native to certain regions of southern Africa. The plant typically features slender stems and lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairless. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in late spring to early summer.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and its adaptability to various soil conditions.
