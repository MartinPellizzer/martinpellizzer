Lasiosiphon sericocephalus

Lasiosiphon sericocephalus, commonly known as the silky-headed morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces long, slender stems that often climb or trail. Its flowers open in the morning and close in the late afternoon, a trait typical of many morning glory species.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
