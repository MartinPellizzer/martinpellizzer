Leptospermum citratum

Leptospermum citratum, commonly known as lemon myrtle, is a shrub native to Australia, characterized by its aromatic, lemon-scented leaves.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is known for its small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, round, yellow fruits that are edible and often used in culinary applications. Its leaves contain high concentrations of citral, a compound responsible for its distinctive citrus fragrance.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
