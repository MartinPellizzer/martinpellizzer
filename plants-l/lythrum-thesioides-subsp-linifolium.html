Lythrum thesioides subsp. linifolium

Lythrum thesioides subsp.

linifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved lythrum, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States. It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along streambanks, preferring moist to wet soils.

The plant features slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. Its showy, pink to purple flowers are clustered in spikes and bloom from June to September.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
