Lacinaria lancifolia

Lacinaria lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lacinaria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features linear, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Lacinaria lancifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
