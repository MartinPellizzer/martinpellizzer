Lepanthes linguifera

Lepanthes linguifera, commonly known as the tongue orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, pendulous flowers that often display a striking combination of white and green hues. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as cloud forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are usually elongated and grass-like, and it produces small, fragrant blooms throughout the year.

Lepanthes linguifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
