Lavandula officinalis var. pyrenaica

Lavandula officinalis var. pyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean lavender, is a subspecies native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its aromatic, grey-green foliage and spikes of purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, sunny soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. Its essential oils, rich in linalool and lavandin, are widely used in aromatherapy and perfumery.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental beauty and its medicinal properties.
