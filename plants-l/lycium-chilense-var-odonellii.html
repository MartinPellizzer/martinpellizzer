Lycium chilense var. odonellii

Lycium chilense var. odonellii, commonly known as the Chilean boxthorn, is a shrub native to southern Chile and northern Argentina.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, orange-red berries. The plant typically grows in coastal and semi-arid regions, adapting to saline and well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellowish with five petals.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
