Lupinaster pentaphyllus var. albus

Lupinaster pentaphyllus var. albus, commonly known as the white lupinaster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with numerous small leaflets, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun exposure.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
