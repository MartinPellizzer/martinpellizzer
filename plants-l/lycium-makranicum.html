Lycium makranicum

Lycium makranicum, commonly known as the Makran boxthorn, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of Pakistan and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its small, spiny leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces red berries that are edible and have a tart flavor, often used in local cuisines. Lycium makranicum thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing high tolerance to drought and saline soils.

It plays a role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
