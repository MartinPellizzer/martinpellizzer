Lilium speciosum var. tametomo

Lilium speciosum var. tametomo, commonly known as the Japanese showy lily, is a striking perennial plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear large, showy flowers. The blooms, which are typically pink to red in color, are trumpet-shaped and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

It requires full sun to partial shade and is relatively low maintenance once established.
