Libinhania suffruticosa

Libinhania suffruticosa, commonly known as the golden bell tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woody structure and clusters of yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed, and its leaves are typically elliptical with a smooth surface. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland forests or along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
