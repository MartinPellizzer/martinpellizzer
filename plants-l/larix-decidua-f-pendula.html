Larix decidua f. pendula

Larix decidua f. pendula, commonly known as the weeping larch, is a unique variant of the European larch.

It is characterized by its drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant is a deciduous conifer, shedding its needle-like leaves in the autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in mountainous regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping and garden design.
