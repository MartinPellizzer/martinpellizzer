Lasianthus wallichii var. penangensis

Lasianthus wallichii var. penangensis, commonly known as the Penang lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy inflorescences. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers in shades of pink and purple. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
