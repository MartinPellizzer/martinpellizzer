Lupulus humulus

Lupulus humulus, commonly known as hops, is a climbing plant belonging to the Cannabaceae family.

It is native to Europe, Asia, and North America, and is characterized by its conical, glandular spikes that produce a distinctive aroma. The female inflorescences, or cones, are the primary part used in brewing beer, imparting bitterness and flavor. The plant has serrated leaves and can grow up to 6 meters in height.

It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
