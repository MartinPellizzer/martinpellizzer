Lactuca axillaris

Lactuca axillaris, commonly known as the lesser lettuce, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, spoon-shaped leaves and is native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, followed by fluffy seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and meadows.

Lactuca axillaris is typically herbaceous and has a mild, slightly bitter taste when eaten.
