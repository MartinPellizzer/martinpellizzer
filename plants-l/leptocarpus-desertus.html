Leptocarpus desertus

Leptocarpus desertus, commonly known as the desert pandanus, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Australia.

It belongs to the family Pandanaceae and is characterized by its strap-like, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-gray in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, which are often hidden among the foliage. It is adapted to survive in dry, sandy soils and can tolerate high temperatures and low water availability.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an important component of desert ecosystems.
