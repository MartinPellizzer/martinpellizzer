Lepidium chichicara

Lepidium chichicara, commonly known as wild cress, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard family plants. The plant is characterized by its small, white or pink flowers and pinnately divided leaves. It typically grows in rocky or disturbed soils and is often found in high-altitude environments.

Lepidium chichicara is valued for its nutritional content and has been used in traditional medicine.
