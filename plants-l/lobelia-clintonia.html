Lobelia clintonia

Lobelia clintonia, commonly known as Clinton's lobelia, is a delicate perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking blue to purple flowers in late spring. Its flowers are clustered in spikes and are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects.

Lobelia clintonia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
