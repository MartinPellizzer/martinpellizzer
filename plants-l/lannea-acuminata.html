Lannea acuminata

Lannea acuminata, commonly known as the African wild olive, is a deciduous tree native to eastern and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and alternate, simple, elliptic leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits that contain a single seed. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its hardy nature and medicinal properties.

The tree plays an important ecological role in its native regions, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
