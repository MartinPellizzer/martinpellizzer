Luzula erecta var. calabra

Luzula erecta var. calabra, commonly known as the Calabrian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often dark green and slightly hairy. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like clusters of white to pale green flowers.

Luzula erecta var. calabra is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in wetland habitats.
