Lychnis fulgens var. typica

Lychnis fulgens var. typica, commonly known as the fireweed or willow herb, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, sunny environments such as meadows and riverbanks. The plant features upright stems with lance-shaped leaves and produces striking red or orange flowers in clusters at the tips of the branches. Its showy blooms attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities in garden settings.
