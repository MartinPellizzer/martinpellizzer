Lavandula aristibracteata

Lavandula aristibracteata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lavender, is a species native to southern Europe, particularly Spain and Portugal.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and spikes of purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and Mediterranean climates. Its fragrant flowers are used in essential oils and aromatherapy, while its ornamental value makes it a popular garden plant.

The species is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
