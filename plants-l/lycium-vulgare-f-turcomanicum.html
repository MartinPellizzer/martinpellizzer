Lycium vulgare f. turcomanicum

Lycium vulgare f. turcomanicum, commonly known as the Turkestan boxthorn, is a shrub native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, reddish berries that are edible. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to harsh conditions. Its leaves are typically small and elliptical, with a thick, waxy coating to reduce water loss.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to withstand drought.
