Lockhartia integra

Lockhartia integra, commonly known as the flame orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with a contrasting yellow or white throat. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its inflorescence is a spike that bears several blooms, which are often clustered together.

The leaves are elongated and arranged in a spiral pattern, contributing to its unique appearance.
