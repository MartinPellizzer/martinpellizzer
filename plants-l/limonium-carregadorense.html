Limonium carregadorense

Limonium carregadorense, commonly known as the Azores sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to the Azores archipelago.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, violet-blue flowers. The plant thrives in coastal environments, often found on sandy or rocky soils. It is adapted to withstand salty air and strong winds typical of its maritime habitat.

Limonium carregadorense plays a role in stabilizing coastal dunes and is of interest for its ornamental value and ecological significance.
