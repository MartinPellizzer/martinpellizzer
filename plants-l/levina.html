Levina

Levina, commonly known as the Levantine iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers bloom in early spring.

Levina is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant colors.
