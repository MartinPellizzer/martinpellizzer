Lathyrus aphaca

Lathyrus aphaca, commonly known as the alpine larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive spurred petals and erect growth habit. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its flowers, which range in color from blue to purple, bloom in early summer, adding vibrant color to high-altitude landscapes.

Lathyrus aphaca is also noted for its toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to livestock and humans if ingested.
