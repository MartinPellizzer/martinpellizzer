Leochilus inconspicuus

Leochilus inconspicuus, commonly known as the inconspicuous leechplant, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the southern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or white in color.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
