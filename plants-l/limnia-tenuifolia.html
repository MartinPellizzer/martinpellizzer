Limnia tenuifolia

Limnia tenuifolia, commonly known as the slender water plant, is a small aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Asia.

It typically grows in still or slow-moving waters, such as ponds and streams, and is characterized by its delicate, thread-like leaves. The plant has a simple morphology with no true roots, relying instead on rhizomes for anchorage and nutrient absorption. Limnia tenuifolia is often found in shallow waters where it can receive adequate sunlight for photosynthesis.

Its presence is an indicator of clean, well-oxygenated water in its habitat.
