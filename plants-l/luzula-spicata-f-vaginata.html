Luzula spicata f. vaginata

Luzula spicata f. vaginata, commonly known as the slender woodrush, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, commonly referred to as the grass family, and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a whitish bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, greenish to brownish flowers.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is often used in landscaping for its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments.
