Linaria galioides var. pseudosupina

Linaria galioides var. pseudosupina, commonly known as the narrowleaf toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb with slender, branching stems that often grow in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that are typically arranged in clusters along the stem. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slightly hairy surface.

The species is characterized by its ability to thrive in arid conditions and its role in stabilizing soil in disturbed areas.
