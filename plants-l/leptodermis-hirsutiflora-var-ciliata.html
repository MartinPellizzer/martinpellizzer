Leptodermis hirsutiflora var. ciliata

Leptodermis hirsutiflora var. ciliata, commonly known as the hairy-leaved leptodermis, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves that are often covered with fine, ciliated margins, giving it its specific epithet "ciliata.". The plant produces small, clustered flowers with five petals, typically in shades of white or pale pink. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforest undergrowth.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and research settings.
