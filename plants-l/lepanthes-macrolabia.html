Lepanthes macrolabia

Lepanthes macrolabia, commonly known as the showy slipper orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers with a distinctive lip that resembles a slipper. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical forests. Its flowers are often brightly colored, ranging from white to pink and purple, and are adapted for pollination by specific insects.

Lepanthes macrolabia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts worldwide.
