Lavandula pinnata var. pubescens

Lavandula pinnata var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy lavender, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, gray-green leaves covered in fine, silky hairs, which contribute to its common name. The plant produces spikes of purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its essential oils are valued for their fragrance and use in aromatherapy and herbal remedies.
