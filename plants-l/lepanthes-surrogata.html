Lepanthes surrogata

Lepanthes surrogata, commonly known as the surrogate orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that grows on trees in humid tropical forests. The plant produces delicate, showy flowers with intricate patterns, often in shades of white, pink, and purple. It thrives in high light conditions and requires well-draining, loose soil to prevent root rot.

Lepanthes surrogata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
