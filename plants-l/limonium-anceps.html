Limonium anceps

Limonium anceps, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in coastal environments and is often found in sandy or gravelly soils. Its salt tolerance makes it well-suited for maritime climates.

Limonium anceps is a member of the Plumbagaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
