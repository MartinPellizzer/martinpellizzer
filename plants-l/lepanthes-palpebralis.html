Lepanthes palpebralis

Lepanthes palpebralis, commonly known as the "Eyelid Orchid," is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central and South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments within tropical forests. The plant features delicate, greenish-white flowers with intricate patterns that resemble eyelids, giving it its common name. Its pseudobulbs are short and stout, supporting a single, narrow leaf.

Lepanthes palpebralis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance.
