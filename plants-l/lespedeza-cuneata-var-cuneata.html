Lespedeza cuneata var. cuneata

Lespedeza cuneata var. cuneata, commonly known as sericea lespedeza, is an annual legume native to Asia.

It features trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces small, yellow to reddish flowers arranged in dense spikes. The plant has a fibrous root system and is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields. It is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen and is used in erosion control and as forage for livestock.

Its adaptability to various climates makes it a common invasive species in some regions.
