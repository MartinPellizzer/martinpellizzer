Luzama lynniana

Luzama lynniana, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the cloud forests of Central America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Luzama lynniana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
