Lepanthes dorsalis

Lepanthes dorsalis, commonly known as the Spotted Pteridium Orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often colorful floral structures. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are usually small to medium in size, with intricate patterns and vibrant hues that attract pollinators.

Lepanthes dorsalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
