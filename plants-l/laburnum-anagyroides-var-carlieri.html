Laburnum anagyroides var. carlieri

Laburnum anagyroides var. carlieri, commonly known as the Carlier's golden chain, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features delicate, drooping clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has pinnate leaves composed of several small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its ornamental value lies in its vibrant floral display and graceful form.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
