Lessonia sarcophyllum

Lessonia sarcophyllum, commonly known as the giant petiolar vine, is a striking plant native to the temperate rainforests of southern South America.

It is characterized by its massive, woody stems that can grow up to several meters in length, often climbing or cascading from trees. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically green with a waxy surface, adapted to the humid, shaded environments it inhabits. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually white or pale yellow, and it produces dark, fleshy berries as its fruit.

Lessonia sarcophyllum plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
