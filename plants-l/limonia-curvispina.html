Limonia curvispina

Limonia curvispina, commonly known as the sour orange or kaffir lime, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, narrow leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits with a thick, bumpy rind and a segmented interior filled with aromatic, juicy segments. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

Limonia curvispina is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
