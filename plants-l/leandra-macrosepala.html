Leandra macrosepala

Leandra macrosepala, commonly known as the giant leandra, is a striking plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It is a member of the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red and purple. The plant has broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing along stream banks or in dense undergrowth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
