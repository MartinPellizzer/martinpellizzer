Lathyrus nissolia f. gramineus

Lathyrus nissolia f. gramineus, commonly known as the grass-like pea, is a botanical variant of the species Lathyrus nissolia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple or pink. This plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure. It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its delicate appearance and ability to attract pollinators.

However, it contains toxic alkaloids and should be handled with care.
