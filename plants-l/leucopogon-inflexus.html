Leucopogon inflexus

Leucopogon inflexus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen nature and dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

Leucopogon inflexus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
