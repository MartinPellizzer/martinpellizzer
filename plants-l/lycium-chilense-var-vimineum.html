Lycium chilense var. vimineum

Lycium chilense var. vimineum, commonly known as the Chilean wolfberry, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family and is characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit, with slender, often hairy stems. The plant produces small, yellow-orange berries that are edible and have a tart, slightly sweet flavor. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and covered in fine pubescence, while its flowers are typically white or pale yellow.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal uses in traditional medicine.
