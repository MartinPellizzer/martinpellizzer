Laurus champa

Laurus champa, commonly known as Indian laurel or champa, is a small evergreen tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like cinnamon and bay laurel. The plant is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is a dark, woody drupe that contains a single seed.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the essential oils extracted from its leaves and flowers.
