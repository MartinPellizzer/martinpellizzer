Lupinus laxiflorus var. tenellus

Lupinus laxiflorus var. tenellus, commonly known as the slender-flowered lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, loosely arranged blue to violet flowers and slender, branching stems. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are compound, consisting of small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Lupinus laxiflorus var. tenellus plays an important role in soil stabilization and provides nectar for pollinators.
