Lepanthes comadresina

Lepanthes comadresina, commonly known as the "Coral Orchid," is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, brightly colored flowers that range from pink to red, often with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a compact rosette.

Lepanthes comadresina is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
