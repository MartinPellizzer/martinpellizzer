Lonicera racemosa

Lonicera racemosa, commonly known as redbank honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its opposite, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to yellow. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds and small mammals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.

Lonicera racemosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native habitats.
