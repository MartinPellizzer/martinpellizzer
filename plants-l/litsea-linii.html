Litsea linii

Litsea linii, commonly known as the Chinese sandalwood or mountain laurel, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, glossy leaves and small white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, dark reddish-brown drupe containing a single seed.

Litsea linii is valued for its aromatic wood and is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
