Lagerstroemia collinsiae

Lagerstroemia collinsiae, commonly known as the Collins' crape myrtle, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern China.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red, appearing in late spring to early summer. The plant has smooth, gray bark that becomes flaky with age, and its leaves are broad, ovate, and turn red or orange in the fall. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Lagerstroemia collinsiae is also valued for its ability to withstand drought and urban conditions.
