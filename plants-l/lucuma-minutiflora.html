Lucuma minutiflora

Lucuma minutiflora, commonly known as the dwarf lucuma, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its compact growth habit and yellow-orange fruits. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruit, which matures to a sweet, custard-like texture, is often used in desserts and traditional medicine.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
