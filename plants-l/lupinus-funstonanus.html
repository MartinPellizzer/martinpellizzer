Lupinus funstonanus

Lupinus funstonanus, commonly known as Funston's lupine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has palmately divided leaves with a hairy texture, and its root system consists of nitrogen-fixing nodules. Lupinus funstonanus thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

It plays an important role in ecological systems by supporting pollinators and contributing to soil fertility.
