Libanotis aurea

Libanotis aurea, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that resemble small bells, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often found in coastal areas and mountainous regions.

Libanotis aurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
