Lepanthes urotepala

Lepanthes urotepala, commonly known as the violet orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees in tropical rainforests. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, violet-colored flowers with a distinctive three-lobed lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires high air humidity and indirect light for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
