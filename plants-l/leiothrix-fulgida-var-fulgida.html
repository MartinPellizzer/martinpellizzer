Leiothrix fulgida var. fulgida

Leiothrix fulgida var. fulgida, commonly known as the golden thread orchid, is a striking orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to golden yellow. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are notable for their intricate patterns and are highly valued in horticulture for their ornamental appeal.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
