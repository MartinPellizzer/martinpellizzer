Loranthus avicularis

Loranthus avicularis, commonly known as the mistletoe of the genus Loranthus, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using the light it captures. The plant has small, scale-like leaves and produces white or yellowish flowers that are often visited by insects. Its berries are typically red or orange and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This species is found in various parts of Africa, Asia, and Australia, often growing in tropical and subtropical regions.
