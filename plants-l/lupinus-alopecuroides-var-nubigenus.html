Lupinus alopecuroides var. nubigenus

Lupinus alopecuroides var. nubigenus, commonly known as the cloud lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and showy blue to purple flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets, and its root system includes nitrogen-fixing nodules, which contribute to soil fertility.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open meadows and rocky areas.
