Lepanthes caudata

Lepanthes caudata, commonly known as the long-tailed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated floral spurs that can reach up to 10 centimeters in length. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical forests. Its flowers are usually pale green or white with a slight purple tinge, and they bloom during the rainy season.

Lepanthes caudata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
