Lorentea linearis

Lorentea linearis, commonly known as the slender loranth, is a parasitic plant belonging to the Loranthaceae family.

It typically attaches itself to the branches of host trees, such as eucalyptus or acacia, through specialized structures called haustoria. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are linear and narrow, contributing to its common name.

Lorentea linearis plays a role in ecological interactions by influencing the growth and health of its host plants.
