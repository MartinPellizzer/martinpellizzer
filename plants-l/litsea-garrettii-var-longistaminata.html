Litsea garrettii var. longistaminata

Litsea garrettii var. longistaminata, commonly known as the long-stamen litsea, is a variant of the litsea genus native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, small, and usually white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a smooth or slightly serrated margin.

The fruit is a small, capsule-like structure that contains several seeds.
