Loranthus lanceolatus

Loranthus lanceolatus, commonly known as the lanceolate mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using light absorbed by its leaves. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are often visited by insects. Its berries are typically red or yellow and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This species is found in various regions of Africa and is often seen in forested areas where it forms a symbiotic relationship with its host.
