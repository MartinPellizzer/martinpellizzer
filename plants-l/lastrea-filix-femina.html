Lastrea filix-femina

Lastrea filix-femina, commonly known as the snake plant, is a member of the Asparagaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a hardy, evergreen perennial that features upright, sword-shaped leaves with a variegated pattern of green and yellow. The plant is characterized by its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and requires minimal watering, making it an ideal indoor plant. Its leaves are succulent, storing water efficiently, and it produces small, inconspicuous white flowers under optimal conditions.

The plant is also noted for its air-purifying qualities, contributing to improved indoor air quality.
