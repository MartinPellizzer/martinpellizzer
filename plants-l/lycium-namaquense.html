Lycium namaquense

Lycium namaquense, commonly known as the Namakwa bush tomato, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshade plants. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

Its botanical features include woody stems, alternate leaves, and flowers that are typically yellow or white.
