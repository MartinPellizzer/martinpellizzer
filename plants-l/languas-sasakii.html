Languas sasakii

Languas sasakii, commonly known as the Sasakii orchid, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain, and it thrives in warm, moist climates with high humidity.

The flowers bloom in clusters and are often used in traditional floral arrangements due to their beauty and fragrance.
