Livistona altissima

Livistona altissima, commonly known as the giant palm or Queensland palm, is a striking tropical plant native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is well adapted to salty air and sandy soils. Its reproductive structures include small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible, sweet fruits.

Livistona altissima is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in coastal landscapes.
