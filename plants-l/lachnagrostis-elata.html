Lachnagrostis elata

Lachnagrostis elata, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. It produces small, greenish-white flowers in late summer, which are clustered in spikelets.

Lachnagrostis elata is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
