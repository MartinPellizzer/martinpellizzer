Lotus anfractuosus var. vanuatensis

Lotus anfractuosus var. vanuatensis, commonly known as the Vanuatu lotus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pacific island of Vanuatu.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, palmately compound leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale pink to white in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or lowland areas.

This species is notable for its ornamental value and potential ecological importance in its native habitat.
