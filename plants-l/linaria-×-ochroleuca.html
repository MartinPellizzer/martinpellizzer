Linaria × ochroleuca

Linaria × ochroleuca, commonly known as the hybrid Snapdragon, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Linaria vulgaris and Linaria daphnifolia.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems and clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its vibrant blooms and ease of care.

Linaria × ochroleuca is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
