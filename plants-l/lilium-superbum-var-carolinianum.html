Lilium superbum var. carolinianum

Lilium superbum var. carolinianum, commonly known as the Carolina lily, is a striking perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its tall, upright stems and large, showy white or pink flowers with a distinctive red-brown throat. The plant typically grows in moist, rich soils along stream banks and forest edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
