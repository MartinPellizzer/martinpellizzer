Lacinaria spicata var. albiflora

Lacinaria spicata var. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered lacinaria, is a flowering plant characterized by its delicate, white petals and slender, branching stems.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks, where it thrives in partial to full shade. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological role in the ecosystem. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth, slightly hairy texture, adding to its distinctive appearance.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant, ethereal blooms.
