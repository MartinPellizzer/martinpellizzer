Lippia diamantinensis

Lippia diamantinensis, commonly known as diamond leaf or Brazilian verbena, is a shrub native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Lippia diamantinensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine.
