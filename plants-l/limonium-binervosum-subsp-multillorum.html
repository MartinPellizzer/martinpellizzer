Limonium binervosum subsp. multillorum

Limonium binervosum subsp.

multillorum, commonly known as the coastal flax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in coastal areas, sandy soils, and saline environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are linear and often have a slightly hairy texture, while the flowers bloom in late summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes.
