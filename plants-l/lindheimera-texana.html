Lindheimera texana

Lindheimera texana, commonly known as Texas Lindheimer, is a flowering shrub native to the Southwestern United States.

It features dense, aromatic foliage with small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, showy white or pale pink flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Lindheimera texana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
