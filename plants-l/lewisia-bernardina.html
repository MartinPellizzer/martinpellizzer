Lewisia bernardina

Lewisia bernardina, commonly known as the Bernard's lewisia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Montiaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of blue-green, fleshy leaves. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers in spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Lewisia bernardina is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to bloom reliably in cooler climates.
