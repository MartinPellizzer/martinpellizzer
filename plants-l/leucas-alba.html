Leucas alba

Leucas alba, commonly known as white leucas, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Leucas alba is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
