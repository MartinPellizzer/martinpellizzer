Lythrum salicaria subsp. anceps

Lythrum salicaria subsp.

anceps, commonly known as narrowleaf purple loosestrife, is a perennial herb native to Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and spikes of vibrant purple flowers that bloom in midsummer.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually narrower than those of the typical purple loosestrife. It thrives in moist soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
