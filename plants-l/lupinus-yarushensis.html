Lupinus yarushensis

Lupinus yarushensis, commonly known as Yarush's lupin, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces showy, pea-like flowers in shades of purple and white, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Lupinus yarushensis is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Its ecological importance includes nitrogen fixation, which enriches the soil for other plant species.
