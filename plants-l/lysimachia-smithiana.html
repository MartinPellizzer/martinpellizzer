Lysimachia smithiana

Lysimachia smithiana, commonly known as Smith's yellow-eyed grass, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, creeping stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

Lysimachia smithiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
