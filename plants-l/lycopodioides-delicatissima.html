Lycopodioides delicatissima

Lycopodioides delicatissima, commonly known as the delicate clubmoss, is a small evergreen fern-like plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, scale-like leaves and compact growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its spore-producing structures, called strobili, are clustered at the tips of its branches.

Lycopodioides delicatissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in maintaining ecological balance in its native habitat.
