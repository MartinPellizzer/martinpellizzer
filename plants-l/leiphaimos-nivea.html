Leiphaimos nivea

Leiphaimos nivea, commonly known as the snow leek, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, rosette-forming structure with silvery-white, fleshy leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue flowers during the summer months, which are clustered in umbels. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been traditionally used in herbal remedies for its purported health benefits.
