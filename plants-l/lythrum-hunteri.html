Lythrum hunteri

Lythrum hunteri, commonly known as the redroot lythrum, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lythraceae family and is characterized by its striking pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks, preferring moist to wet soil conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a reddish hue, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Lythrum hunteri is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
