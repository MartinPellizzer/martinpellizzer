Lamprococcus laurentianus

Lamprococcus laurentianus, commonly known as the Laurentian scale, is a species of armored scale insect native to North America.

It primarily infests coniferous trees, such as spruce and fir, feeding on sap through its specialized, waxy shield. The plant's botanical significance lies in its role as a host for various natural enemies, including parasitoid wasps. Its presence can indicate the health of forest ecosystems, as it thrives in areas with high tree density.

Due to its specific host preferences, it is often used in biological control programs to manage invasive plant species.
