Lepanthes cymbium

Lepanthes cymbium, commonly known as the showy slipper orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that resemble small slippers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are often fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

Lepanthes cymbium is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
