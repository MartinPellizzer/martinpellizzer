Lachenalia rosea

Lachenalia rosea, commonly known as the roseal lachenalia, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of narrow, fleshy leaves. The plant produces tall, branched stems with clusters of bright pink to red flowers, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
