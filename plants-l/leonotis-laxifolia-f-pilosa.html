Leonotis laxifolia f. pilosa

Leonotis laxifolia f. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy leonotis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant is distinguished by its hairy foliage, which gives it its specific epithet "pilosa.". It typically grows as a shrub or subshrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters.

This species is adapted to dry conditions and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
