Lonicera kesselringii

Lonicera kesselringii, commonly known as Kesselring's honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces red berries that are edible for birds but not typically for humans. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Lonicera kesselringii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
