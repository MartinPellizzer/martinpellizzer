Luculia

Luculia, commonly known as the Chinese bellflower, is a genus of flowering plants in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

These plants are native to various regions in Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea. They typically feature showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet, often with a contrasting throat. The leaves are usually simple, alternate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Luculia species are often cultivated for their ornamental value and are also used in traditional Chinese medicine.
