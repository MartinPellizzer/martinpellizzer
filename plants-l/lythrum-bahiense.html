Lythrum bahiense

Lythrum bahiense, commonly known as the Bahia lythrum, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Lythraceae family, which includes other flowering plants such as the willows and poinsettias. The plant typically grows in wetlands and moist soils, often found in swampy areas and along streambanks. It produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Its foliage is lance-shaped and often has a reddish tinge, adding visual interest to its natural habitat.
