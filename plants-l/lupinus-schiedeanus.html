Lupinus schiedeanus

Lupinus schiedeanus, commonly known as the Schiedea lupine, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of blue to violet pea-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, leathery pods that contain several elliptical seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Lupinus schiedeanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
