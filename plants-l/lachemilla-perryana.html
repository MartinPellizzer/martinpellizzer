Lachemilla perryana

Lachemilla perryana, commonly known as the Perry's cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine, soft hairs.

Lachemilla perryana thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories and along stream banks.
