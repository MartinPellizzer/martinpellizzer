Lachenalia bowieana

Lachenalia bowieana, commonly known as Bowie's lachenalia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and as a container plant.
