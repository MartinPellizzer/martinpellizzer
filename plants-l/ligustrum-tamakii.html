Ligustrum tamakii

Ligustrum tamakii, commonly known as the Chinese privet, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant features opposite, simple, ovate leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by black, spherical berries.

Ligustrum tamakii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal.
