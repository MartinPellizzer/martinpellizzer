Lonchocarpus epigaeus

Lonchocarpus epigaeus, commonly known as the hairy triggerplant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its unique, spurred petals that resemble a trigger mechanism. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
