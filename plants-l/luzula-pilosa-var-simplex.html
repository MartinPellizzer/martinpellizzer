Luzula pilosa var. simplex

Luzula pilosa var. simplex, commonly known as the silky woodrush, is a perennial flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, which includes grasses and sedges, and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, silky appearance. It produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brownish flowers during the summer months.

Luzula pilosa var. simplex typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes.
