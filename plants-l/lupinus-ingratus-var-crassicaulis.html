Lupinus ingratus var. crassicaulis

Lupinus ingratus var. crassicaulis, commonly known as the thick-stemmed lupine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its robust, cylindrical stems and clusters of blue to violet pea-shaped flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small, oval leaflets, and its root system includes nitrogen-fixing nodules. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry grasslands and rocky slopes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
