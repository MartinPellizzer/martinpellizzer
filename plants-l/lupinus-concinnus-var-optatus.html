Lupinus concinnus var. optatus

Lupinus concinnus var. optatus, commonly known as the golden lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and upright growth habit. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open areas with full sun exposure.

The variety optatus is distinguished by its more robust growth and showier floral display compared to other varieties of Lupinus concinnus.
