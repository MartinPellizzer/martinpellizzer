Lilium nevadense var. shastense

Lilium nevadense var. shastense, commonly known as the Shasta lily, is a species native to the western United States, particularly found in high-elevation areas of California and Oregon.

It is a bulbous perennial plant that produces showy, white to pale pink flowers with a distinctive recurved shape. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a fan-like pattern, and it thrives in well-drained soil.

The Shasta lily is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
