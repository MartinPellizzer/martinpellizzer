Linum catharticum subsp. suecicum

Linum catharticum subsp.

suecicum, commonly known as Swedish flax, is a perennial herb native to northern Europe. It features slender, branching stems that grow up to 60 cm tall, covered with fine, silky hairs.

The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a slightly rough texture.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
