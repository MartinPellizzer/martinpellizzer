Luffa sphaerica

Luffa sphaerica, commonly known as the sponge gourd, is a tropical vine belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, spherical, fibrous fruit that develops from the flower. The fruit's interior becomes spongy and fibrous as it matures, making it useful for scrubbing. The plant thrives in warm climates and requires a long growing season.

It is often cultivated for its edible young fruits and the fibrous material used in household items.
