Lonicera villosa var. fulleri

Lonicera villosa var. fulleri, commonly known as the Fulleri honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive velvety appearance. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its berries are orange-red and edible, attracting birds and providing food for wildlife.

This species is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
