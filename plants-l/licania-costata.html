Licania costata

Licania costata, commonly known as the Brazilian palm or the golden palm, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, coconut-like fruits. Licania costata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its growth habit and adaptability make it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
