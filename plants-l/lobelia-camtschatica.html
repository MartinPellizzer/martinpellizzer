Lobelia camtschatica

Lobelia camtschatica, commonly known as the "scarlet lily" or "red lily," is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking spikes of bright red or orange tubular flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and grow in a basal rosette. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Lobelia camtschatica is popular in gardens for its vibrant color and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
