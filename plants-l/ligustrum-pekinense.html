Ligustrum pekinense

Ligustrum pekinense, commonly known as Chinese privet, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, simple, ovate leaves that are dark green and glossy on top with a lighter underside. The plant produces small, fragrant, white flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by black, spherical berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to various soil types.

However, it can be invasive in certain regions outside its native range.
