Lycium trothae

Lycium trothae, commonly known as the Troth's boxthorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible but have a tart flavor. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along riverbanks.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ornamental gardening and for erosion control.
