Lilium martagon var. albiflorum

Lilium martagon var. albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered Turk's cap lily, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in a loose, rounded cluster at the top of the stem. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in two rows along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a striking visual element to the landscape.
