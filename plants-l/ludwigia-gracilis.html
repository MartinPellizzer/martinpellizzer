Ludwigia gracilis

Ludwigia gracilis, commonly known as the slender Ludwigia, is a species of flowering plant in the Onagraceae family.

It is native to South America and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Ludwigia gracilis is also cultivated as an ornamental plant in water gardens and pond environments.
