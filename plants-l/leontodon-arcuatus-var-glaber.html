Leontodon arcuatus var. glaber

Leontodon arcuatus var. glaber, commonly known as the smooth bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually smooth to the touch. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer, which are clustered in spikelets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, dunes, and along coastal areas.

Due to its adaptability, it is considered an important species in ecological restoration projects.
