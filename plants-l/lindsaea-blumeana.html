Lindsaea blumeana

Lindsaea blumeana, commonly known as the Blume's spikemoss, is a species of fern ally native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lindsaeaceae and is characterized by its erect, sparsely branched stems and small, ovate leaves. The plant produces sporangia in terminal clusters, which are often protected by a hood-like structure called a indusium. Lindsaea blumeana typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
