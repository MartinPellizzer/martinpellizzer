Lysimachia montana var. platyphylla

Lysimachia montana var. platyphylla, commonly known as broad-leaved mountain jewelflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, bright yellow flowers throughout the summer months. The leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
