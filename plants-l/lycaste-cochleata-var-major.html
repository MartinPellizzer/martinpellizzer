Lycaste cochleata var. major

Lycaste cochleata var. major, commonly known as the large-leafed lycaste, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America. This plant is characterized by its broad, strap-like leaves and produces fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white and yellow. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright indirect light.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in greenhouse settings.
