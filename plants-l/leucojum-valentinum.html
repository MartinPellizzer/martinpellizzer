Leucojum valentinum

Leucojum valentinum, commonly known as the St. Valentine's lily, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves that emerge from a bulbous underground stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate appearance and the timing of its bloom, which coincides with Valentine's Day.
