Lycianthes bimensis

Lycianthes bimensis, commonly known as the two-month tomato, is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers.

It is a shrub native to Central and South America, characterized by its hairy stems and lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into orange-red berries, each containing several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some medicinal uses in traditional medicine.

Due to its compact size and drought tolerance, it is well-suited for container gardening and xeriscaping.
