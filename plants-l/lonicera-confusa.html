Lonicera confusa

Lonicera confusa, commonly known as the fuzzy honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are covered in fine, fuzzy hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to yellow, typically blooming in late spring. Its berries are small, red, and edible, often attracting birds and other wildlife.

Lonicera confusa is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
