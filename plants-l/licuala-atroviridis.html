Licuala atroviridis

Licuala atroviridis, commonly known as the black-veined palm, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves with dark green veins that contrast sharply with the lighter green background. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and as a decorative element in horticulture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in warm climates for its aesthetic and practical uses.
