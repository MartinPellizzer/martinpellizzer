Lavatera cretica proles stenophylla

Lavatera cretica proles stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leafed lavatera, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and showy, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are borne in clusters and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
