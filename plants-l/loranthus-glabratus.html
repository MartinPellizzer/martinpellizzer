Loranthus glabratus

Loranthus glabratus, commonly known as the smooth mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that attaches itself to the branches of host trees, primarily in the genus Eucalyptus.

It lacks true roots and absorbs water and nutrients through its stems and leaves, which are adapted for this parasitic lifestyle. The plant produces small, white or pale green berries that contain sticky seeds, facilitating bird-mediated dispersal. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped and have a waxy surface, which helps reduce water loss.

Loranthus glabratus is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives in warm, humid climates.
