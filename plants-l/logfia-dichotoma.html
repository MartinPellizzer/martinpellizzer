Logfia dichotoma

Logfia dichotoma, commonly known as the forked fern, is a species of flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dichotomous branching pattern, where each stem splits into two equal branches. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are typically narrow and linear, contributing to its overall delicate appearance.

Logfia dichotoma is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.
