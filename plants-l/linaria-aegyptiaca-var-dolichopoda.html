Linaria aegyptiaca var. dolichopoda

Linaria aegyptiaca var. dolichopoda, commonly known as the Egyptian toadflax, is a flowering plant native to regions of North Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the summer. The leaves are linear and grass-like, often arranged in a whorled pattern along the stems. This variety is closely related to other members of the Plantaginaceae family and is known for its adaptability to arid environments.

It is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
