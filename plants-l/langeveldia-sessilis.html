Langeveldia sessilis

Langeveldia sessilis, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a waxy texture, while its flowers are borne in terminal inflorescences.

Langeveldia sessilis is well adapted to humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
