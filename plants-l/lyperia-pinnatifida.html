Lyperia pinnatifida

Lyperia pinnatifida, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lyperia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and generally herbaceous growth habit. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in dense clusters, and bloom during the warmer months.

Lyperia pinnatifida is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.
