Leleba floribunda f. viridistriata

Leleba floribunda f. viridistriata, commonly known as the green-striated leleba, is a variant of the leleba plant characterized by its distinct foliage patterns.

It is a flowering plant native to specific regions in Africa, particularly in the eastern part of the continent. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with prominent green stripes, which set it apart from other leleba varieties. It produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers, typically in shades of white or pale yellow.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
