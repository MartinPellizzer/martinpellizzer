Loasa volubilis var. alpina

Loasa volubilis var. alpina, commonly known as the alpine loasa, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is a trailing shrub that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, often at high elevations. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers during the spring and early summer. Its leaves are usually alternate, simple, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Loasa volubilis var. alpina is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to harsh, high-altitude conditions.
