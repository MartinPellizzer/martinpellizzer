Limonium subviolaceum

Limonium subviolaceum, commonly known as the blueflower sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, showcasing its adaptability to saline environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a basal rosette.

Limonium subviolaceum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
