Lepanthopsis lingulata

Lepanthopsis lingulata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The flowers are typically white or pale yellow, with a distinctive lip that is often darker in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on trees or rocks in tropical forests.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
