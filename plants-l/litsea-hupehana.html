Litsea hupehana

Litsea hupehana, commonly known as Chinese sandalwood, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray-barked trunk and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruit is a small, dark purple drupe containing a single seed.

Litsea hupehana is valued for its aromatic wood and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
