Lobelia speculum

Lobelia speculum, commonly known as the sacred lotus, is a striking herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, often with a distinctive white or yellow throat. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it grows in moist, woodland habitats. Lobelia speculum is known for its ability to thrive in partial shade and its role in attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for native gardens and ecological restoration projects.
