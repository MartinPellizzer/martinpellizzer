Lycopodioides chrysoleuca

Lycopodioides chrysoleuca, commonly known as the golden-leaved clubmoss, is a small evergreen plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems that bear small, scale-like leaves arranged in whorls. The plant produces spore-bearing cones, which are typically clustered at the base of the stem. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it is considered an important indicator species for certain forest ecosystems.
