Lonicera aemulans

Lonicera aemulans, commonly known as the golden honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish-orange flowers in late spring to early summer. Its berries are red and edible, often attracting birds.

Lonicera aemulans is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
