Liriope graminifolia var. variegata

Liriope graminifolia var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated liriope, is a perennial grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically variegated with white or yellow markings along the edges. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale blue flowers in late summer. It thrives in partial to full shade and is often used in ornamental landscaping for its attractive foliage.

Its foliage remains evergreen, providing visual interest throughout the year.
