Lychnis mexicana

Lychnis mexicana, commonly known as Mexican catchfly, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, pink to red flowers. The plant features hairy stems and leaves that are often covered in fine, white pubescence. Its flowers bloom in clusters at the ends of the stems, typically from late spring to early summer.

Lychnis mexicana is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.
