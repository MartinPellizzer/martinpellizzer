Lysinema ewartianum

Lysinema ewartianum, commonly known as the Ewart's lichen, is a species of lichen that belongs to the family Lichenes.

It is a symbiotic organism composed of a fungus and a photosynthetic partner, typically a green alga or cyanobacterium. This lichen is often found on rock surfaces in cool, moist environments, such as forest floors or mountainous regions. Its structure consists of a thallus, which is the composite body of the lichen, and it lacks true roots, stems, or leaves.

Lysinema ewartianum plays an important role in nutrient cycling and soil stabilization in its habitat.
