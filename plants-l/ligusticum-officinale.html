Ligusticum officinale

Ligusticum officinale, commonly known as Chinese lovage, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its aromatic, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the family. Its rhizomes and roots are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Ligusticum officinale is often cultivated for its culinary and medicinal properties.
