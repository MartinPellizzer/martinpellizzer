Leptochloa subdigitata

Leptochloa subdigitata, commonly known as the digitate grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and ecological restoration projects.
