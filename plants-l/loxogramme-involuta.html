Loxogramme involuta

Loxogramme involuta, commonly known as the curled water fern, is a small aquatic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that curl tightly around the stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats, such as ponds and marshes. Its leaves are usually submerged or floating, and it reproduces through spores rather than seeds.

Loxogramme involuta plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small invertebrates and contributing to water quality through its biological activity.
