Lasianthus sogerensis

Lasianthus sogerensis, commonly known as the Soger's flameflower, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in horticultural settings.
