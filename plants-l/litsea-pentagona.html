Litsea pentagona

Litsea pentagona, commonly known as the five-angled litsea, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and avocados. The plant is characterized by its simple, opposite, and elliptical leaves that are usually dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its small, white flowers are typically clustered in umbels, and it produces small, round berries that are edible.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its aromatic properties.
