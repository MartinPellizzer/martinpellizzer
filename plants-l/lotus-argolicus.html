Lotus argolicus

Lotus argolicus, commonly known as the Argolic lotus, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy pink or purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing along coastal areas. Its root system is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.

The seeds are encased in a tough pod, which splits open to release the seeds when mature.
