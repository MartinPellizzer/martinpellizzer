Leucophae massoniana

Leucophae massoniana, commonly known as the Masson's white-flowered tree, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elegant, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. The tree has a tall, slender trunk with smooth, gray bark and lance-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and pale underneath. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks due to its striking appearance.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and a warm, humid climate to thrive.
