Linum monogynum var. grandiflorum

Linum monogynum var. grandiflorum, commonly known as the common flax or blue flax, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are used in various industries. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Linum monogynum var. grandiflorum is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and ease of growth.
