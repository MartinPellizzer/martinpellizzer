Lonicera caerulea subsp. subarctica

Lonicera caerulea subsp.

subarctica, commonly known as the Sakhalin honeysuckle, is a shrub native to the Russian Far East and northern Japan. It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant produces edible berries that are typically dark blue to black in color, similar to blueberries. It thrives in cool, temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential nutritional benefits.
