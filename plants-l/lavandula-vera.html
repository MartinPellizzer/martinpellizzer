Lavandula vera

Lavandula vera, commonly known as true lavender, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fragrant, spike-like inflorescences composed of purple or violet flowers. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that are aromatic and often used in essential oil production. Lavandula vera thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal use.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and valuable in aromatherapy and medicine.
