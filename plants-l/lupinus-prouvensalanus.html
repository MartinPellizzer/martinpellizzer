Lupinus prouvensalanus

Lupinus prouvensalanus, commonly known as the Provencal lupine, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Lupinus prouvensalanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
