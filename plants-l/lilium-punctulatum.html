Lilium punctulatum

Lilium punctulatum, commonly known as the spotted lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking, white flowers with red or purple spots, which are typically borne on slender, erect stems. The plant produces sword-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It is often found in open, dry habitats such as meadows and woodlands.

Lilium punctulatum is a perennial herb that blooms in late spring to early summer, adding ornamental value to its natural environment.
