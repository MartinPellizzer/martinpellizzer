Lycopodium sprucei

Lycopodium sprucei, commonly known as the Spruce clubmoss, is a species of clubmoss belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. The plant has a creeping stem that produces small, scale-like leaves and erect, spore-bearing cones. Lycopodium sprucei is characterized by its distinctive, clustered spore cones that are often reddish in color.

This species plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
