Lactuca macrophylla

Lactuca macrophylla, commonly known as the broad-leaf lettuce, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often used in culinary applications. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, and it is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. Lactuca macrophylla is typically found in meadows, fields, and along roadsides.

Its leaves contain lactucine, a compound that can have mild sedative effects.
