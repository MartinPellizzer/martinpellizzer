Leontodon psilocalix

Leontodon psilocalix, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its yellow flowers bloom in late summer.

The species is notable for its ability to tolerate various environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
