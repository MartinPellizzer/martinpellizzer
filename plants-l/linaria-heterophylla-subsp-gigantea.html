Linaria heterophylla subsp. gigantea

Linaria heterophylla subsp.

gigantea, commonly known as the giant common toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its tall, branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

The plant produces yellow-orange flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom from spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.
