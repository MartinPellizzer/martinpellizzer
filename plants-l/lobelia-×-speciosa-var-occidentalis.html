Lobelia × speciosa var. occidentalis

Lobelia × speciosa var. occidentalis, commonly known as the western cardinal flower, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking red-orange tubular flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens and wetland restoration projects.
