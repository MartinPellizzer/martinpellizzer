Litsea acuminatissima

Litsea acuminatissima, commonly known as the Chinese holly or narrow-leaved litsea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, Japan, and Korea, and is often found in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green on top, with a lighter underside. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by berries that are red when mature.

Litsea acuminatissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its aromatic properties.
