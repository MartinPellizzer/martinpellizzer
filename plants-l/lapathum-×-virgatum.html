Lapathum × virgatum

Lapathum × virgatum, commonly known as the purple meadow cranesbill, is a hybrid perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of deep purple to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are often hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

Lapathum × virgatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
