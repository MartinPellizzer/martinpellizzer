Lupinus latifolius var. wigginsii

Lupinus latifolius var. wigginsii, commonly known as Wiggins' broadleaf lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves and showy blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, enriching the soil with nitrogen.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
