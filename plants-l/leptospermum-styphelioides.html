Leptospermum styphelioides

Leptospermum styphelioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tea tree, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in spring. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

Leptospermum styphelioides is also valued for its aromatic foliage and historical use in traditional herbal remedies.
