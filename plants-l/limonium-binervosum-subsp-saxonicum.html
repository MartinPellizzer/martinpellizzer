Limonium binervosum subsp. saxonicum

Limonium binervosum subsp.

saxonicum, commonly known as the saxonic sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Europe. It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and showy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in maritime environments. It is a perennial species that is well adapted to salt spray and strong winds.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for coastal gardens and landscaping.
