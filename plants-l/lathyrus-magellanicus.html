Lathyrus magellanicus

Lathyrus magellanicus, commonly known as the Magellanic pea, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, followed by elongated pods containing lens-shaped seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
