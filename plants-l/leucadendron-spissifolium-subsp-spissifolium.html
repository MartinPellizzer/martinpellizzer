Leucadendron spissifolium subsp. spissifolium

Leucadendron spissifolium subsp.

spissifolium, commonly known as the dense-leaved sugar bush, is a shrub native to South Africa. It is characterized by its dense, needle-like leaves that give it its common name.

The plant produces cone-like inflorescences that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture.
