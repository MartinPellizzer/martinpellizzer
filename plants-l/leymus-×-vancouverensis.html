Leymus × vancouverensis

Leymus × vancouverensis, commonly known as Vancouver needlegrass, is a sterile hybrid grass species native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant forms dense tussocks and is often found in moist, well-drained soils in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates and small mammals.

Due to its sterility, it does not produce seeds and relies on vegetative reproduction for propagation.
