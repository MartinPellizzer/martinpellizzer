Loreya huberi

Loreya huberi, commonly known as the desert lily, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and striking white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. Loreya huberi is adapted to survive in extreme heat and drought, storing water in its bulbous underground stem.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it a notable species in desert flora.
