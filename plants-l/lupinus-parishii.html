Lupinus parishii

Lupinus parishii, commonly known as the Parish lupine, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the western United States, particularly found in California and Oregon. This plant is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers. The leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

Lupinus parishii plays an important ecological role by fixing nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plant life.
