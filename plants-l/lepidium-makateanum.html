Lepidium makateanum

Lepidium makateanum, commonly known as the Makatea cress, is a flowering plant native to the island of Makatea in French Polynesia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard family plants. The plant is characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and pinnately divided leaves. It grows in rocky, coastal areas and is adapted to the unique environmental conditions of its habitat.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
