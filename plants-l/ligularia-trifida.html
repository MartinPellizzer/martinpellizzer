Ligularia trifida

Ligularia trifida, commonly known as the giant leopard plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern Asia.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins and a distinctive yellow-orange flower spike. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its foliage is thick and leathery, providing a striking visual contrast to its delicate flowers.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
