Lisianthius ramosissimus

Lisianthius ramosissimus, commonly known as the common lisianthius, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features narrow, grass-like leaves. It produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This species is often found in moist, sandy soils along streambanks and in open woods.
