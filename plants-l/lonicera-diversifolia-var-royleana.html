Lonicera diversifolia var. royleana

Lonicera diversifolia var. royleana, commonly known as the Kashmir honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan.

It features slender, arching stems with opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are typically dark green and slightly pubescent. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers in clusters, usually yellow or orange, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a small, yellowish berry that ripens in autumn, containing seeds enclosed in a fleshy pulp.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
