Lupinus magnificus var. magnificus

Lupinus magnificus var. magnificus, commonly known as the magnificent lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and striking blue to purple flower clusters. The plant features compound leaves with numerous small leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for native gardens and ecological restoration projects.
