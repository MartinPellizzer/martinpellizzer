Lysiana exocarpi subsp. tenuis

Lysiana exocarpi subsp.

tenuis, commonly known as the slender-fruited lychee, is a subtropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, glossy leaves and small, fragrant flowers.

The fruit is typically oval-shaped with a thin, red or yellow rind and a sweet, aromatic pulp. This subspecies is distinguished by its slender, elongated fruits compared to other lychee varieties.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
