Lithocardium corymbosum f. glabriusculum

Lithocardium corymbosum f. glabriusculum, commonly known as the glabrous form of the clustered rock-cress, is a variant of this flowering plant native to certain regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow. The leaves are generally smooth and glabrous, distinguishing it from other forms of the species. This plant thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in plant morphology and adaptation to harsh climates.
