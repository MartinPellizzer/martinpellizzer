Lewisia cotyledon

Lewisia cotyledon, commonly known as the "Cotyledon Lewisii," is a succulent plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and purple during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is widely cultivated by gardeners in dry climates.
