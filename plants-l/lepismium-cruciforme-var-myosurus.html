Lepismium cruciforme var. myosurus

Lepismium cruciforme var. myosurus, commonly known as the Myosurus Lepismium, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains.

It is a member of the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spiny stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces bright yellow or orange blooms during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.

Its unique appearance and ornamental value make it a sought-after plant among succulent enthusiasts.
