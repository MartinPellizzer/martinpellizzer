Limnanthemum rautanenii

Limnanthemum rautanenii, commonly known as the velvetleaf moonflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom at night, emitting a sweet fragrance to attract nocturnal pollinators. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, smooth seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
