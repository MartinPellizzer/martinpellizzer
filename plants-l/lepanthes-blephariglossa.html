Lepanthes blephariglossa

Lepanthes blephariglossa, commonly known as the "Crimson Lip" orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers with a distinctive red or pink lip. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in the understory of tropical forests. Its flowers are usually short-lived but highly attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Lepanthes blephariglossa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
