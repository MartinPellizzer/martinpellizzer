Lippia bromleyana

Lippia bromleyana, commonly known as Brazilian pepper tree, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, small white flowers and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are edible but often have a spicy flavor. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

However, it can be invasive in some regions, posing a threat to native ecosystems.
