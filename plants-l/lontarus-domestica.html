Lontarus domestica

Lontarus domestica, commonly known as the domestic lontaru, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The fruit, which is edible, has a mild flavor and is sometimes used in local cuisines.
