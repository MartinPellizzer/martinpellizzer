Lathyrus cilicicus

Lathyrus cilicicus, commonly known as the Cilician vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Turkey and Syria.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features slender, climbing stems with small, pea-like flowers that bloom in shades of pink or purple. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, heart-shaped leaflets.

Lathyrus cilicicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential ecological and medicinal properties.
