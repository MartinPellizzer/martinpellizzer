Lobelia dioica

Lobelia dioica, commonly known as Indian tobacco, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple, arranged in dense spikes. It is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Lobelia dioica is notable for its medicinal properties and has been used by indigenous peoples for various therapeutic purposes.
