Linum pycnophyllum subsp. kurdicum

Linum pycnophyllum subsp.

kurdicum, commonly known as Kurdish flax, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East. It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, blue to violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant traits.

Kurdish flax is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in local horticulture.
