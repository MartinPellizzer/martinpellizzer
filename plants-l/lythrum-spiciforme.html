Lythrum spiciforme

Lythrum spiciforme, commonly known as rosepink or spiked lythrum, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright, sparsely branched stems. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers in dense spikes, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Lythrum spiciforme is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ability to attract pollinators.
