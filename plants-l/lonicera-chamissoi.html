Lonicera chamissoi

Lonicera chamissoi, commonly known as the Chamisso honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture with a slightly serrated margin.

Lonicera chamissoi thrives in partial to full sun and is often found in moist, woodland environments.
