Lasianthus iteophyllus

Lasianthus iteophyllus, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf balsam, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that are often a deep green or purplish hue. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink, white, or violet, typically blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its aesthetic appeal.
