Lobelia polyphylla var. besseriana

Lobelia polyphylla var. besseriana, commonly known as the mountain lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its unique beauty and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
