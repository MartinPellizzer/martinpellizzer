Ladenbergia obovata

Ladenbergia obovata, commonly known as the African wild bean, is a leguminous plant native to Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of protein.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in woodland and savanna habitats.
