Linum austriacum subsp. tommasinii

Linum austriacum subsp.

tommasinii, commonly known as the Austrian flax or Tommasin's flax, is a flowering plant belonging to the Linaceae family. It is native to Central and Eastern Europe, particularly found in mountainous regions of the Alps.

The plant produces showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees. It has linear leaves and a slender stem, typical of the flax genus.

This subspecies is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role.
