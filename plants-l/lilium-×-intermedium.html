Lilium × intermedium

Lilium × intermedium, commonly known as the Intermediate Lily, is a hybrid species belonging to the Liliaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette.

Lilium × intermedium is native to the mountainous regions of Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
