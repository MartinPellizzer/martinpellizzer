Lithocarpus spicatus var. brevipetiolatus

Lithocarpus spicatus var. brevipetiolatus, commonly known as the short-stalked ironwood, is a member of the Fagaceae family, characterized by its hard, dense wood and evergreen foliage.

It typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in mountainous or forested areas with high humidity. The plant produces small, leathery leaves that are glossy on top and lighter in color underneath. Its fruit is a one-seeded nut enclosed in a thick, woody husk, which is typical of the genus Lithocarpus.

This species is valued for its durable timber and plays a role in local ecosystems as a food source for various wildlife.
