Limonium sitiacum

Limonium sitiacum, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in saline and sandy soils, making it well adapted to coastal environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powder.

Limonium sitiacum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and floral arrangements.
