Lycium quitense

Lycium quitense, commonly known as the Peruvian wolfberry, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes and potatoes, and is characterized by its yellow-orange berries. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in arid to semi-arid climates. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture, and it produces small, bell-shaped flowers.

The berries are edible and have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
