Lobelia fistulosa

Lobelia fistulosa, commonly known as the redstem lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a hairy texture.

Lobelia fistulosa is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
