Lesquerella macrocarpa

Lesquerella macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-seeded lesquerella, is a species native to the southwestern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its hairy stems and alternate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous small, elliptical seeds. Lesquerella macrocarpa is known for its high oil content, which makes it valuable in industrial applications.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and economic studies.
