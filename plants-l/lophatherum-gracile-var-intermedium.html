Lophatherum gracile var. intermedium

Lophatherum gracile var. intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate sweetflag, is a perennial grass native to Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as marshes and along stream banks. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly hairy. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, greenish flowers.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits and is also used in ornamental horticulture.
