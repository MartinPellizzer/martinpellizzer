Leea sundaica var. fuliginosa

Leea sundaica var. fuliginosa, commonly known as the sooty leea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in Indonesia.

It belongs to the family Leeaceae and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and dark green, often hairy leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as tropical forests. Its leaves are usually leathery and have a distinctive sooty appearance due to a dense covering of fine hairs.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
