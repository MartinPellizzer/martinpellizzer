Lespedeza bicolor var. higoensis

Lespedeza bicolor var. higoensis, commonly known as the purpletop sandbur, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open, sandy, or disturbed soils. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a hairy texture, while its stems are erect and often branched.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
