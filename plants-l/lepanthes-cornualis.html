Lepanthes cornualis

Lepanthes cornualis, commonly known as the candlelight orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, pale yellow to white flowers that resemble candle flames, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in the understory of tropical forests. Its flowers are small and clustered, with three sepals and three petals, and it lacks a visible labellum.

Lepanthes cornualis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
