Linum usitatissimum subsp. vulgare

Linum usitatissimum subsp.

vulgare, commonly known as flax, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and lanceolate, hairy leaves.

The plant produces blue or white flowers with five petals, which are typically arranged in loose clusters. Its most notable feature is the capsule fruit that contains numerous small, oval seeds.

Flax is widely cultivated for its oil-rich seeds and fibers used in textile and culinary applications.
