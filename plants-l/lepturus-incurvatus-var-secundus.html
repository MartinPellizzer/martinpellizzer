Lepturus incurvatus var. secundus

Lepturus incurvatus var. secundus, commonly known as the zigzag fern, is a unique vascular plant characterized by its distinctive, undulating fronds.

This variety belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions. Its fronds are typically narrow and curling, giving the plant its common name. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found growing on rocks or tree trunks.
