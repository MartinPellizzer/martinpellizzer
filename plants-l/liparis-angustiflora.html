Liparis angustiflora

Liparis angustiflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinct, intricate structure. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its delicate appearance and specialized pollination mechanisms.

Liparis angustiflora is often found in montane regions and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
