Litsea polyneura

Litsea polyneura, commonly known as the Chinese sandalwood or mountain laurel, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, white flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically red or yellow when mature, containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its wood is valued for its fragrance and is used in traditional crafts and perfumery.
