Lobivia caespitosa var. altiplani

Lobivia caespitosa var. altiplani, commonly known as the Andean rock rose, is a succulent native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It features dense, compact growth with short, fleshy stems and small, rounded leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating. The plant produces clusters of vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and early summer. It is well adapted to harsh, arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Its unique appearance and resilience make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
