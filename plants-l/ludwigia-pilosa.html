Ludwigia pilosa

Ludwigia pilosa, commonly known as the hairy Ludwigia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Onagraceae.

It is native to South America and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, pink to red flowers. The plant has finely hairy stems and leaves that are opposite and ovate in shape. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Ludwigia pilosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
