Lactuca indica var. laciniata

Lactuca indica var. laciniata, commonly known as the shiso or Japanese perilla, is a leafy annual plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its finely cut, serrated leaves that range in color from light green to deep purple. The plant produces small, green flowers that develop into slender, cylindrical fruits containing tiny seeds. Its leaves are often used in culinary applications, adding a distinctive peppery flavor to dishes.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family and is closely related to other members of the lettuce genus.
