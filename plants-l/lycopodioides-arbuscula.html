Lycopodioides arbuscula

Lycopodioides arbuscula, commonly known as the clubmoss, is a small, evergreen plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, moss-like appearance with tiny, scale-like leaves arranged in whorls around its stem. The plant produces spores in cone-like structures called strobila, which are typically found at the tips of its branches. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices.

Lycopodioides arbuscula plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
