Lechea leggettii var. typica

Lechea leggettii var. typica, commonly known as the desert milkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in spring and early summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils.

Lechea leggettii var. typica is adapted to withstand drought conditions and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
