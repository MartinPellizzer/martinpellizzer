Lychnis incarnata

Lychnis incarnata, commonly known as the bloody cranesbill, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its striking pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and grows to a height of about 1 to 2 feet. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or meadows.

Lychnis incarnata is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
