Lasiochloa hispida var. longifolia

Lasiochloa hispida var. longifolia, commonly known as the hairy hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and hairy leaves that are often reddish in color. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, preferring well-drained environments. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, which are often covered in fine hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in arid landscapes.
