Leptactina hexamera

Leptactina hexamera, commonly known as the six-armed sea anemone, is a marine cnidarian characterized by its unique morphology.

It possesses six primary tentacles, which are arranged symmetrically around its central oral disk. The body structure consists of a gastrovascular cavity surrounded by a ring of muscle fibers. Its reproductive system includes both sexual and asexual methods, contributing to its adaptability in marine environments.

The species is typically found in shallow coastal waters, where it thrives on rocky substrates.
