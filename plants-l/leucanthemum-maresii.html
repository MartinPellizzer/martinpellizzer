Leucanthemum maresii

Leucanthemum maresii, commonly known as the alpine oxeye, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, high-altitude environments. It produces white to pale yellow flowers with a central yellow disk, which bloom in late summer.

Leucanthemum maresii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
