Lycaste fimbriata

Lycaste fimbriata, commonly known as the fringe-leaf orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves with a distinctive fringed or toothed edge, which gives it its scientific name. The plant produces fragrant, showy flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Lycaste fimbriata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in the right conditions.
