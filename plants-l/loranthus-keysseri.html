Loranthus keysseri

Loranthus keysseri, commonly known as the Kessie's mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that relies on host trees for water and nutrients.

The plant produces small, fleshy berries that contain seeds dispersed by birds. Its leaves are usually scale-like and lack true stomata, which is a characteristic feature of many mistletoe species.

Loranthus keysseri plays a role in forest ecosystems by providing food for various wildlife species.
