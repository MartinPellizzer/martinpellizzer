Loranthus pustulatus

Loranthus pustulatus, commonly known as the rough-stemmed mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that attaches to the branches of host trees.

It belongs to the Loranthaceae family and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are often eaten by birds, which then disperse its seeds. It is found in various regions of Africa and is adapted to thrive in a range of host species.

Its botanical features make it an important component of forest ecosystems.
