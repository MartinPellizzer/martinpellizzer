Leandra lepidota

Leandra lepidota, commonly known as the velvetleaf hibiscus, is a tropical flowering plant native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are velvety to the touch, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Leandra lepidota is typically found in coastal areas and lowland forests, where it contributes to the biodiversity of its ecosystem.
