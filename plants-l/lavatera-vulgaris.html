Lavatera vulgaris

Lavatera vulgaris, commonly known as the common lavatera, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, featuring soft, hairy leaves and showy, cup-shaped flowers. The flowers are typically pink, white, or purple and bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
