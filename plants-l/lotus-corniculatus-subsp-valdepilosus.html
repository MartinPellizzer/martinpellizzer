Lotus corniculatus subsp. valdepilosus

Lotus corniculatus subsp.

valdepilosus, commonly known as the hairy horned vetch, is a perennial legume native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is often found in temperate climates. Its hairy stems and leaves give it a distinctive appearance, and it is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it a valuable plant for soil enrichment.

It is also used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature and adaptability.
