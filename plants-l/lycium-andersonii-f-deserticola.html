Lycium andersonii f. deserticola

Lycium andersonii f. deserticola, commonly known as the desert lycium, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, yellow-orange berries that ripen in late summer. The plant has adapted to harsh desert conditions with thick, waxy leaves that reduce water loss. It produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring.

The species is part of the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshade plants.
