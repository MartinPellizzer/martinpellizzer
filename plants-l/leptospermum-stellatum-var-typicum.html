Leptospermum stellatum var. typicum

Leptospermum stellatum var. typicum, commonly known as the coastal tea tree, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of white to pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is well adapted to coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils. It is often used in coastal landscaping for its hardiness and ornamental value.

The essential oils from its leaves are also used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine.
