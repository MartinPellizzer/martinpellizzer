Lonchophaca kaibensis

Lonchophaca kaibensis, commonly known as the Kaibab lily, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its stems are erect and often branched.
