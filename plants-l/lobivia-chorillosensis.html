Lobivia chorillosensis

Lobivia chorillosensis, commonly known as the Chorillos lobivia, is a species of flowering plant in the cactus family, Cactaceae.

It is native to the arid regions of southern Peru, where it thrives in rocky and well-drained soils. The plant is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It has adapted to survive in harsh desert conditions with minimal water and high sunlight exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and cactus enthusiasts alike.
