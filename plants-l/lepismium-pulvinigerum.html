Lepismium pulvinigerum

Lepismium pulvinigerum, commonly known as the red-tipped nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces striking red-tipped flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a velvety texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
