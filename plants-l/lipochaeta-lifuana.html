Lipochaeta lifuana

Lipochaeta lifuana, commonly known as the New Zealand rock dandelion, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow daisy-like flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in coastal and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It has small, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Lipochaeta lifuana plays an important role in its native ecosystem, providing nectar for pollinators and contributing to soil stability.
