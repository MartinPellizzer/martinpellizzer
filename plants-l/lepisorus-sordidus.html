Lepisorus sordidus

Lepisorus sordidus, commonly known as the sordid fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. The plant has delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are often covered in fine hairs. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Lepisorus sordidus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
