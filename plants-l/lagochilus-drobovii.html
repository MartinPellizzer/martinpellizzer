Lagochilus drobovii

Lagochilus drobovii, commonly known as Drobovii's mountain thyme, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their essential oils. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in temperate to cold climates. Its small, fragrant flowers are usually purple or pink, and it produces small, oval-shaped leaves.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
