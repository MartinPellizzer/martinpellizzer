Luzula pilosa

Luzula pilosa, commonly known as hairy wood rush, is a perennial plant belonging to the family Plantaginaceae.

It is characterized by its upright, grass-like foliage with soft, hairy textures on the stems and leaves. Native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense spikes during the summer months.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense colonies, making it a common component of moist forest understories.
