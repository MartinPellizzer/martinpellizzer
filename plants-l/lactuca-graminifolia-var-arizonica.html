Lactuca graminifolia var. arizonica

Lactuca graminifolia var. arizonica, commonly known as Arizona leaf lettuce, is a variant of the common lettuce species native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant grows in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along riverbanks. It has a low-growing habit and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or yellow.

This variety is valued for its edible leaves, which have a mild, slightly bitter taste.
