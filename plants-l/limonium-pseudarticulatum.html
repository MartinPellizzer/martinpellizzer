Limonium pseudarticulatum

Limonium pseudarticulatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and its clusters of blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is often found in sandy or coastal soils. It is a member of the Plumbagaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
