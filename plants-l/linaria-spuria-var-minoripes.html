Linaria spuria var. minoripes

Linaria spuria var. minoripes, commonly known as the lesser toadflax, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers bloom from spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
