Leymus chinensis

Leymus chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and slender, wiry leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is commonly found in steppe and meadow ecosystems. Leymus chinensis is known for its ability to form extensive grasslands and is an important component of the vegetation in northern China.

It plays a significant role in soil stabilization and provides forage for grazing animals.
