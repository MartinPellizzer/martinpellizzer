Linum monopetalum

Linum monopetalum, commonly known as the Persian poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep red, often with a distinctive black center. The plant has a slender, upright stem and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Linum monopetalum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
