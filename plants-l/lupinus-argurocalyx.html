Lupinus argurocalyx

Lupinus argurocalyx, commonly known as the silverleaf lupine, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of bright yellow to orange flowers. The leaves are compound, consisting of multiple small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in desert scrub and rocky habitats.

It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for soil enrichment in arid environments.
