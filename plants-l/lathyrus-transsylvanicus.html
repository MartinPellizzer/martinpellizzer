Lathyrus transsylvanicus

Lathyrus transsylvanicus, commonly known as the Transylvanian vetchling, is a flowering plant native to eastern Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in grassy areas and along forest edges. The plant features slender stems and trifoliate leaves, with small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pale blue to purple. Its fruit is a legume pod containing several elliptical seeds.

Lathyrus transsylvanicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in nitrogen fixation in soil.
