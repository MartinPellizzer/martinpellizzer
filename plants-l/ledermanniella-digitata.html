Ledermanniella digitata

Ledermanniella digitata, commonly known as the digitate leaf fern, is a small, delicate fern native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and along stream banks. The plant features finely divided, finger-like leaves that give it its common name. It has a rhizomatous growth habit and produces spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its fronds.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often found in areas with high humidity and well-drained soil.
