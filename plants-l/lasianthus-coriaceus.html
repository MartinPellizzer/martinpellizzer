Lasianthus coriaceus

Lasianthus coriaceus, commonly known as the rough-leaved lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that give it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale purple or white in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
