Lupinus × macounii

Lupinus × macounii, commonly known as the Macoun's lupine, is a hybrid species of lupine native to western North America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and gravelly slopes. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

Lupinus × macounii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in ecological restoration projects due to its nitrogen-fixing abilities.
