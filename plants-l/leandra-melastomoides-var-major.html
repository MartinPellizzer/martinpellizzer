Leandra melastomoides var. major

Leandra melastomoides var. major, commonly known as the giant leandra, is a striking tropical plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, waxy, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom in clusters at the leaf axils. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, rich soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
