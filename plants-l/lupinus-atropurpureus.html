Lupinus atropurpureus

Lupinus atropurpureus, commonly known as the dark purple lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of violet to purple flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Lupinus atropurpureus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for ecological restoration projects.
