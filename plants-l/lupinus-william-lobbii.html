Lupinus william-lobbii

Lupinus william-lobbii, commonly known as the Lobb's lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, such as along stream banks and in open woodlands. The plant features compound leaves with numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces spires of blue to purple flowers. Its root system includes nitrogen-fixing nodules, which help improve soil fertility.

Lupinus william-lobbii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
