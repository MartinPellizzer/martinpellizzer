Lotus olgae

Lotus olgae, commonly known as the African lotus, is a perennial aquatic plant native to the wetlands of Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, with long, slender stems that support its showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in marshes and ponds. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The lotus olgae is valued for its ornamental beauty and has cultural significance in various African traditions.
