Licuala sallehana

Licuala sallehana, commonly known as the golden palm, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of golden-colored spines. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fruits that contain black seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a decorative plant in tropical landscapes.
