Leonurus sibiricus f. niveus

Leonurus sibiricus f. niveus, commonly known as the white-hairy leonurus, is a variant of the common leonurus found in temperate regions.

This plant is characterized by its dense, white pubescence that covers its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands.
