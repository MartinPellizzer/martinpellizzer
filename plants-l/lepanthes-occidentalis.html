Lepanthes occidentalis

Lepanthes occidentalis, commonly known as the western urn-lobed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its delicate, urn-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow with intricate markings. The plant grows as an epiphyte, often found on trees in humid tropical forests. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Lepanthes occidentalis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique floral structure.
