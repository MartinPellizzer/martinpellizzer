Lysimachia rubiginosa var. glabra

Lysimachia rubiginosa var. glabra, commonly known as smooth wood sorrel, is a low-growing perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves with a smooth texture and produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its stems are often reddish in color, and it has a fibrous root system.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
