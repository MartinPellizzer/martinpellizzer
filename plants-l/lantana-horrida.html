Lantana horrida

Lantana horrida, commonly known as the rough lantana, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring dense, woody stems covered in fine, bristly hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, red, and purple, depending on the variety. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a rough texture with serrated edges.

Lantana horrida is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in full sun, making it a common sight in gardens and natural habitats.
