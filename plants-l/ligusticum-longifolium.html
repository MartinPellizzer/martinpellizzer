Ligusticum longifolium

Ligusticum longifolium, commonly known as Chinese lovage, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It features aromatic, fern-like leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and is often found in moist, shaded environments. It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes parsley and carrots.

Its rhizomes and roots are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
