Lupinus luteus subsp. orientalis

Lupinus luteus subsp.

orientalis, commonly known as the oriental yellow lupin, is a perennial legume native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces elongated pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or dry environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and nitrogen-fixing abilities, which benefit soil fertility.
