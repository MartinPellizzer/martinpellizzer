Limonium puberulum

Limonium puberulum, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and North Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spikes of blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in salty, well-drained soils and is often found in dunes and coastal grasslands. Its flowers bloom from late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Limonium puberulum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize coastal soils.
