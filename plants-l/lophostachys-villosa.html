Lophostachys villosa

Lophostachys villosa, commonly known as the desert globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, demonstrating high drought tolerance.

Its fibrous root system helps it survive in poor soil conditions, making it well adapted to desert landscapes.
