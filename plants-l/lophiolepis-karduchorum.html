Lophiolepis karduchorum

Lophiolepis karduchorum, commonly known as the Karoo lichen, is a unique lichen species that thrives in the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Lecanoraceae and is characterized by its crustose growth form, adhering tightly to rock surfaces. The thallus is typically pale to dark grey, with a smooth or slightly granular texture. It plays a vital role in soil stabilization and nutrient cycling in its harsh habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and specialized environment, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
