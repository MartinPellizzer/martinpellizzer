Lespedeza chinensis var. nokoensis

Lespedeza chinensis var. nokoensis, commonly known as the Noko lespedeza, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or reddish in color, blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
