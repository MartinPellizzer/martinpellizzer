Lotus elisabethae

Lotus elisabethae, commonly known as the Elizabeth's lotus, is a rare and striking flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, showy pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant features palmately lobed leaves and produces pods that contain edible seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded, humid environments.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is highly sought after by botanists and horticulturists.
