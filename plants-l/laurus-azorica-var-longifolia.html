Laurus azorica var. longifolia

Laurus azorica var. longifolia, commonly known as the Azorean laurel, is a species native to the Azores and Madeira Islands.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, blue-black berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in humid, temperate climates and is often found in coastal areas.

The leaves are used in traditional herbal remedies and for their aromatic properties.
