Lilaeopsis schaffneriana var. aquatica

Lilaeopsis schaffneriana var. aquatica, commonly known as the aquatic lily, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. This plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. It has a rhizomatous root system that helps it anchor in waterlogged soils.

The plant is adapted to aquatic conditions, with leaves that can float or remain submerged depending on the water level.
