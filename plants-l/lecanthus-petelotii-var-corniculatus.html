Lecanthus petelotii var. corniculatus

Lecanthus petelotii var. corniculatus, commonly known as the horned lecanthus, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, horn-like inflorescences that give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical structure includes fibrous roots and a short, woody stem with opposite, lance-shaped leaves.
