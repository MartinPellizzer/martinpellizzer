Lespedeza formosa var. velutina

Lespedeza formosa var. velutina, commonly known as velvet lespedeza, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, upright stems and dense clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine, velvety hairs, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils.

Velvet lespedeza is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
