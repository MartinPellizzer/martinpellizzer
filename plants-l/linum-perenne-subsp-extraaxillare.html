Linum perenne subsp. extraaxillare

Linum perenne subsp.

extraaxillare, commonly known as the extraaxillary flax, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It features slender, upright stems that bear alternate, lanceolate leaves with smooth margins.

The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its fibrous roots and delicate foliage make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological landscapes.
