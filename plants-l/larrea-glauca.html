Larrea glauca

Larrea glauca, commonly known as grevillea, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates. Its leaves are typically small and linear, reducing water loss through transpiration.

Larrea glauca is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
