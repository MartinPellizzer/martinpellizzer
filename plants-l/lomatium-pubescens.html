Lomatium pubescens

Lomatium pubescens, commonly known as the hairy lomatium, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its fern-like leaves and umbel-shaped flowers.

It is native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States, where it thrives in moist, forested environments. The plant features a thick, hairy stem that supports its delicate, yellowish-green flowers. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Lomatium pubescens is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
