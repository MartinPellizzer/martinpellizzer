Leptochloa pringlei

Leptochloa pringlei, commonly known as the Pringle's hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands or along stream banks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
