Lotus creticus subsp. commutatus

Lotus creticus subsp.

commutatus, commonly known as the Cretan lotus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, typically arranged in a rosette pattern.

The plant produces bright pink to purple flowers with a distinctive spurred petal, which is a characteristic feature of the legume family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local herbal medicine.
