Liriope muscari var. exiliflora

Liriope muscari var. exiliflora, commonly known as the slender-flowered martagon lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It features grass-like foliage that forms dense clumps and produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to purple. The flowers are typically borne on slender, upright stems and bloom in late spring to early summer. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade and well-drained soil.

It is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic value and low maintenance requirements.
