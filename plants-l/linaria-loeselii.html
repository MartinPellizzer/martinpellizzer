Linaria loeselii

Linaria loeselii, commonly known as the Loesel's toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the genus Linaria in the family Plantaginaceae, characterized by its showy, often yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. Its flowers are tubular with two lips, a feature common in many species of the genus.

Linaria loeselii is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and along coastal areas.
