Lycopodium selago var. patens

Lycopodium selago var. patens, commonly known as the creeping clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming plant that thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. The plant features small, scale-like leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its reproductive structures, called strobila, are cone-like and contain the spores necessary for propagation.

This variety is notable for its adaptability and is often used in horticultural settings for its ornamental value.
