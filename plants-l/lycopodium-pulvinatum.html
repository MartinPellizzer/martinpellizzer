Lycopodium pulvinatum

Lycopodium pulvinatum, commonly known as the clubmoss, is a vascular fern that belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its erect, branched stems and small, scale-like leaves. Its sporophyte stage produces spores in specialized structures called sporangia, which are clustered in strobila.

Lycopodium pulvinatum plays an important ecological role by contributing to soil stability and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
