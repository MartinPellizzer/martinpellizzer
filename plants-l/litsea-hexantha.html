Litsea hexantha

Litsea hexantha, commonly known as the fragrant leafed litsea, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and avocados, and is characterized by its aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by oval, woody fruits containing smooth, dark seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a leathery texture with a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed.

Litsea hexantha is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
