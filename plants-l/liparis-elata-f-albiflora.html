Liparis elata f. albiflora

Liparis elata f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered marsh orchid, is a striking orchid species native to wetland and marsh habitats in Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers, which bloom during the summer months. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in environments with partial to full sunlight. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the waterlogged soil, and it plays a role in maintaining the ecological balance of its habitat.

Due to habitat loss, this rare orchid is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
