Lucaea

Lucaea, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Acanthaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its striking, often bioluminescent flowers that emit a faint glow at night. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a velvety texture.

Lucaea species are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for their unique visual characteristics.
