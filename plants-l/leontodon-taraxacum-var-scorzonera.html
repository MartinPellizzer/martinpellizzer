Leontodon taraxacum var. scorzonera

Leontodon taraxacum var. scorzonera, commonly known as the Scorzonera dandelion, is a variant of the common dandelion characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology and flowering habits.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, typically found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The leaves are lanceolate and often have a rough texture, while the flowers consist of a yellow composite head surrounded by bracts. It produces a taproot that can reach considerable depth, aiding in its survival in various soil conditions.

The plant is noted for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its historical use in traditional medicine.
