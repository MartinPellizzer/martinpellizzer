Lupinus aliamandus

Lupinus aliamandus, commonly known as the aliamandus lupine, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the western United States, particularly California, and thrives in dry, rocky soils. The plant features erect, spindly stems with clusters of violet or blue-purple pea-shaped flowers. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Lupinus aliamandus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for ecological restoration projects.
