Lonicera linearis

Lonicera linearis, commonly known as the redbank honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features slender, arching stems that often grow along the ground or climb over other vegetation. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, elongated, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Lonicera linearis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
