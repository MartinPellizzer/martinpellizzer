Leucothoe oleifolia var. longifolia

Leucothoe oleifolia var. longifolia, commonly known as the fragrant leucothoe, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features dense, upright growth with lance-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and pale underneath. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers in early spring, followed by red berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value lies in its attractive foliage, fragrant blossoms, and seasonal fruit display.
