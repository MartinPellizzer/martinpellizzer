Lantana viburnoides subsp. richardii

Lantana viburnoides subsp.

richardii, commonly known as the richardii lantana, is a shrub native to parts of Africa, particularly South Africa. It features small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, which bloom in the spring and summer months.

The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
