Lupinus sparsiflorus subsp. inopinatus

Lupinus sparsiflorus subsp.

inopinatus, commonly known as the sparse-flowered lupine, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval leaflets.

This subspecies is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
