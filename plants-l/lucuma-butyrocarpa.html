Lucuma butyrocarpa

Lucuma butyrocarpa, commonly known as the lucuma tree, is a native species of the Andean region, particularly in Peru.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and yellow-orange fruit. The fruit, known as lucuma, is encased in a hard shell and contains a sweet, creamy pulp with a unique flavor. The plant is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses, with the fruit being a popular ingredient in desserts and traditional dishes.

It thrives in dry, temperate climates and is adapted to high-altitude environments.
