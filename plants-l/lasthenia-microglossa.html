Lasthenia microglossa

Lasthenia microglossa, commonly known as the narrowleaf sunshine flower, is a small annual herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It features slender, branching stems and small, yellowish-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The flowers bloom in the spring, producing tiny, clustered flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
