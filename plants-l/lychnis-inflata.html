Lychnis inflata

Lychnis inflata, commonly known as the inflated bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, inflated, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soil. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Lychnis inflata blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
