Lysimachia fistulosa var. wulingensis

Lysimachia fistulosa var. wulingensis, commonly known as the Wuling yellowthread, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptical, and often have a hairy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional Chinese medicine.
