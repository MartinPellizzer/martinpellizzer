Lotus procumbens var. jepsonii

Lotus procumbens var. jepsonii, commonly known as the Jepson's lotus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, pink to purple flowers. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and is adapted to grow in wetland or riparian habitats. It is often found in areas with moist soil, such as marshes and stream banks.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
