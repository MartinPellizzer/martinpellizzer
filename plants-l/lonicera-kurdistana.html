Lonicera kurdistana

Lonicera kurdistana, commonly known as the Kurdistani honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and northern Iraq.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces red to orange berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. It typically grows in rocky and well-drained soils, often in alpine or subalpine environments.

Lonicera kurdistana is valued for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local wildlife.
