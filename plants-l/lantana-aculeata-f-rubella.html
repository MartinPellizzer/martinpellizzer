Lantana aculeata f. rubella

Lantana aculeata f. rubella, commonly known as the red-leaf lantana, is a compact shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and, in some forms, red. The leaves are typically ovate to lance-shaped and often have a reddish tinge, especially when mature. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in sunny, well-drained conditions.

It is also known for its resistance to pests and diseases, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
