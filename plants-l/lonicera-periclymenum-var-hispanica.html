Lonicera periclymenum var. hispanica

Lonicera periclymenum var. hispanica, commonly known as Spanish honeysuckle, is a deciduous vine native to southern Europe.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange-red. The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in woodland edges or along hedgerows. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, while its berries provide food for birds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in garden settings.
