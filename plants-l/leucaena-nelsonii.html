Leucaena nelsonii

Leucaena nelsonii, commonly known as the Nelson leucaena, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it valuable for soil enrichment.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in agroforestry systems due to its hardy nature and rapid growth.
