Leucanthemum gaudinii

Leucanthemum gaudinii, commonly known as the white marguerite, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy white ray florets surrounding a central disc of yellow tubular flowers, giving it a daisy-like appearance. It typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, thriving in partial to full sunlight.

Leucanthemum gaudinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
