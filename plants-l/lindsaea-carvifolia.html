Lindsaea carvifolia

Lindsaea carvifolia, commonly known as the serrated lindsaea, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with serrated edges, contributing to its common name.

Lindsaea carvifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
