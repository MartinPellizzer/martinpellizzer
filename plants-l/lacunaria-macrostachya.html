Lacunaria macrostachya

Lacunaria macrostachya, commonly known as the giant lacunaria, is a striking member of the Amaranthaceae family.

It is native to the arid regions of South America, particularly in Argentina and Chile. This plant is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy inflorescences that can reach several meters in height. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for dryland gardening.
