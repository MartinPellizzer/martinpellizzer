Lymania marantoides

Lymania marantoides, commonly known as the arrowroot plant, is a tropical perennial herb native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Marantaceae family and is characterized by its arrow-shaped leaves and rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that are typically borne on upright inflorescences. Its roots are the primary economic product, used as a starchy food source and thickening agent in cooking.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible roots.
