Lathyrus venosus var. venosus

Lathyrus venosus var. venosus, commonly known as the veiny pea or wild pea, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with slender leaflets and prominent veins, giving it its common name.

The plant is often found in woodland edges, meadows, and disturbed soils, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
