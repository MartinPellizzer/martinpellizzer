Linaria argillicola

Linaria argillicola, commonly known as the clay larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species that thrives in well-drained, often calcareous soils. The plant features upright, branched stems and produces clusters of small, delicate flowers in shades of blue, purple, or white. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Linaria argillicola is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
