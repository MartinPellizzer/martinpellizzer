Linum bracei

Linum bracei, commonly known as the desert flax, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the genus Linum, which includes over 150 species of flowering plants, many of which are known for their showy blue or purple flowers. Linum bracei is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, delicate flowers that bloom in shades of white, pink, or purple. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, with deep roots that help it access water from deeper soil layers.

Its fibrous stems and leaves make it a potential source of natural fibers, similar to its more well-known relatives like flax.
