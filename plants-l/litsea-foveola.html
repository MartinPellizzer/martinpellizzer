Litsea foveola

Litsea foveola, commonly known as the narrow-leafed litsea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its simple, elliptic leaves that are often slightly hairy on the underside. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, round berries.

Litsea foveola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
