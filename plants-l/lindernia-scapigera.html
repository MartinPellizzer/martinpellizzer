Lindernia scapigera

Lindernia scapigera, commonly known as the scaly lindernia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, scaly hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces spikes of small, tubular flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming from summer to fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, ditches, and along stream banks.

Lindernia scapigera is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
