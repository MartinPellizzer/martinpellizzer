Lycopodium pallescens

Lycopodium pallescens, commonly known as the pale clubmoss, is a species of lycophyte belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its small, spore-bearing strobila that forms at the tip of its erect, branching stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its leaves are scale-like and arranged in whorls around the stem.

Lycopodium pallescens plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
