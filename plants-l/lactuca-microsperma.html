Lactuca microsperma

Lactuca microsperma, commonly known as the dwarf lettuce, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family, Asteraceae.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, typically growing in dry, rocky habitats. The plant has small, spoon-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. Its flowering heads are small and yellow, consisting of numerous tiny florets.

Lactuca microsperma is not commonly cultivated for food but is occasionally used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory properties.
