Lycaste micheliana

Lycaste micheliana, commonly known as the Micheliana Lycaste, is a tropical orchid native to Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. It produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in humid, shaded environments.

Lycaste micheliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
