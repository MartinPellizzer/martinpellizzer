Lockhartia lamellosa

Lockhartia lamellosa, commonly known as the wax plant, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, waxy, and often brightly colored flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its arid environment.

Lockhartia lamellosa is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
