Linaria boissieri

Linaria boissieri, commonly known as the Boissier's toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the genus Linaria in the family Plantaginaceae, characterized by its showy, spurred flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny conditions. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with a distinctive hooded structure that resembles a toad's head.

Linaria boissieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
