Leonurus crispus

Leonurus crispus, commonly known as cranesbill, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its hairy, square stems and opposite leaves that are often crinkled or wavy. The plant produces small, two-lipped flowers that are typically pink or violet in color, arranged in whorls along the stem. It is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and thrives in a variety of habitats including meadows and woodland edges.

Leonurus crispus is often used in traditional medicine and is also a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
