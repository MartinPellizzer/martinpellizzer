Lucuma dissepala

Lucuma dissepala, commonly known as the golden fruit or lucuma, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly Peru, Ecuador, and Colombia. The plant produces a large, oval-shaped fruit with a yellow-orange pulp that is rich in nutrients such as beta-carotene and antioxidants. Its botanical structure includes a thick, leathery exocarp, a fleshy mesocarp, and a central hard endocarp containing the edible seeds.

The plant is often cultivated for its unique flavor and nutritional value, making it a valuable crop in traditional and modern agriculture.
