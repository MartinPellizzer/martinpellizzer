Lithocardium longipedum var. glabrifolium

Lithocardium longipedum var. glabrifolium, commonly known as the smooth-leaved lithocardium, is a rare and striking plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom during the late spring or early summer. Its growth habit is generally low and spreading, making it well-suited for rocky or arid environments.
