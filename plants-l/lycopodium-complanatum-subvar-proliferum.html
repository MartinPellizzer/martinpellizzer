Lycopodium complanatum subvar. proliferum

Lycopodium complanatum subvar. proliferum, commonly known as the club moss, is a small, evergreen fern-like plant that belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, low-growing clusters of scale-like leaves and produces spore-bearing cones at the base of the plant. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. It has a unique reproductive system involving both sexual and asexual propagation through spores and vegetative growth.

This species plays an important ecological role in soil stabilization and as a habitat for small organisms.
