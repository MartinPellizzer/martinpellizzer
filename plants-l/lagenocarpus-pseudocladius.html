Lagenocarpus pseudocladius

Lagenocarpus pseudocladius, commonly known as the false bamboo grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with pale green to brown flowers, typical of many grass species. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
