Lobelia mucronata

Lobelia mucronata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive, narrow appearance.

Lobelia mucronata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
