Letestudoxa grandifolia

Letestudoxa grandifolia, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a tropical species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy red flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting a variety of pollinators. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets that provide substantial shade. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Letestudoxa grandifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can grow quite tall, reaching heights of up to 30 meters in its natural habitat.
