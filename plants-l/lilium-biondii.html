Lilium biondii

Lilium biondii, commonly known as the Biondi lily, is a species of flowering plant in the lily family, Liliaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe, particularly in Italy and the Balkans. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, or red. Its bulbs are perennial, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

The Biondi lily is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
