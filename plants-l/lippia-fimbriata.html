Lippia fimbriata

Lippia fimbriata, commonly known as Mexican mint or silverleaf sage, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and small, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is known for its strong, mint-like scent. Lippia fimbriata is often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.
