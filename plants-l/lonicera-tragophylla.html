Lonicera tragophylla

Lonicera tragophylla, commonly known as the fragrant honeysuckle, is a deciduous climbing shrub native to East Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to yellow, often attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that ripens in late summer.

Lonicera tragophylla is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
