Ledebouriella

Ledebouriella, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a genus of flowering plants in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It comprises several species, most of which are native to southern Africa. These plants are characterized by their silver-gray foliage, which gives them their common name, and their small, often showy flowers. The fruits are usually berries that contain toxic compounds, making them unpalatable to most animals.
