Lonchocarpus cultratus

Lonchocarpus cultratus, commonly known as the swordleaf senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by distinctive pod fruits that are often curved or twisted. Its botanical features include stipules, which are leaf-like structures at the base of the leaf stalks.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its foliage and floral display.
