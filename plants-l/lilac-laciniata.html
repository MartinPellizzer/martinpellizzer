Lilac laciniata

Lilac laciniata, commonly known as the cutleaf lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive, almost fern-like appearance. The plant produces fragrant, clustered flowers in shades of white, purple, and pink, which bloom in early spring. Lilac laciniata thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its botanical features make it both visually striking and ecologically valuable.
