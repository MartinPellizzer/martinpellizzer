Laserpitium garganicum var. scabrum

Laserpitium garganicum var. scabrum, commonly known as the rough scabrous gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained, rocky soils. The plant features basal leaves that are rough and hairy, with narrow, lance-shaped blades. Its flowers are blue to violet in color, with five petals and prominent yellow-orange spots near the base.

It is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
