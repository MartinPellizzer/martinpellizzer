Leptecophylla juniperina subsp. parvifolia

Leptecophylla juniperina subsp.

parvifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved juniper, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves that are typically arranged in dense clusters.

The plant produces blue or white flowers in compact clusters, which are followed by small, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in dry climates.
