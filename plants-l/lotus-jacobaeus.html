Lotus jacobaeus

Lotus jacobaeus, commonly known as the meadow milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and along riverbanks, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually pale pink to white, and it produces small, hairy pods that contain several seeds.

The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
