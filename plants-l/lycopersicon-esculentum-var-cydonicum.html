Lycopersicon esculentum var. cydonicum

Lycopersicon esculentum var. cydonicum, commonly known as the cherry tomato, is a botanical variant of the domesticated tomato.

It is characterized by its small, round, and often red or yellow fruits, which are typically harvested when fully ripe. The plant is a herbaceous annual with hairy stems and alternate leaves that are broadly ovate. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, sharing genetic traits with other nightshade plants.

This variety is prized for its flavor and versatility in culinary applications.
