Lupinus davisianus

Lupinus davisianus, commonly known as Davis' lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of pea-like flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant has pinnately compound leaves and produces elliptical pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Lupinus davisianus plays a role in nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
