Lotus corniculatus f. microphyllus

Lotus corniculatus f. microphyllus, commonly known as the dwarf cornflower, is a compact, low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers with a distinctive pinkish or reddish hue, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs, and the plant produces small, elongated seed pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
