Lobelia nudicaulis

Lobelia nudicaulis, commonly known as the naked-stemmed lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, erect stems that are often hairless, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple, arranged in dense spikes at the top of the stems. Lobelia nudicaulis thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its delicate appearance and ecological importance make it a valuable species in native plant gardens.
