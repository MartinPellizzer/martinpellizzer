Laurus chloroxylon

Laurus chloroxylon, commonly known as Indian laurel or wild bay, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces dark blue to black berries that are edible but often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are widely used in culinary applications, particularly in Indian and Southeast Asian cuisines.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates.
