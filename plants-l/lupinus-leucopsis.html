Lupinus leucopsis

Lupinus leucopsis, commonly known as the white lupin, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of white to pale pink flowers. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Lupinus leucopsis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
