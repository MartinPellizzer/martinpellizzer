Leiothrix vivipara var. longipila

Leiothrix vivipara var. longipila, commonly known as the long-stalked velvet tangle, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters. The plant features soft, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its tactile appeal. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique viviparous reproduction, where seeds germinate while still attached to the parent plant, is a notable botanical feature.
