Lathyrus lancifolius

Lathyrus lancifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leafed pea, is a perennial legume native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of pea-like flowers that range from pink to violet in color. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical pods that contain several small, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas.

Due to its nitrogen-fixing ability, it plays a role in soil enrichment and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
