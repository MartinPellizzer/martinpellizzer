Lissochilus gracilior var. angusta

Lissochilus gracilior var. angusta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lissochilus, is a species of orchid native to the Andean region of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pale pink. The plant thrives in cool, humid environments, often growing in shaded forest understories. Its flowers are often clustered in racemes, and it is prized for its ornamental value in horticulture.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
