Lythrum ramosum

Lythrum ramosum, commonly known as rosemary bush or red root, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Lythraceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red, which bloom in summer. Lythrum ramosum is often found in moist soils along stream banks and in wet meadows.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
