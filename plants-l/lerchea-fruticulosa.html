Lerchea fruticulosa

Lerchea fruticulosa, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, aromatic wood that is highly valued for its durability and fine grain. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in well-drained soils. Its leaves are small, opposite, and often have a reddish tint, while its flowers are small and clustered.

The plant is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
