Laportea kusaiana

Laportea kusaiana, commonly known as Kusaian false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its foliage is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
