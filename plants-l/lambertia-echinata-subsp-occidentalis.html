Lambertia echinata subsp. occidentalis

Lambertia echinata subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as the Western African lambertia, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and clusters of bright red, lantern-like flowers.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and rocky habitats. Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly smaller size and more compact growth compared to other Lambertia species.
