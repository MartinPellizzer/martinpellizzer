Leptopus polypetalus

Leptopus polypetalus, commonly known as the African bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

Leptopus polypetalus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
