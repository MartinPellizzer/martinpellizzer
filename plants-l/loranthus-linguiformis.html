Loranthus linguiformis

Loranthus linguiformis, commonly known as the tongue-shaped mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that attaches itself to the branches of host trees.

It belongs to the family Loranthaceae and is characterized by its leaf-like stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces white or yellowish berries that contain seeds covered in a sticky substance to aid in dispersal by birds. It is native to regions of Asia, including China, Japan, and the Philippines.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject in the study of parasitic plant interactions and ecological relationships.
