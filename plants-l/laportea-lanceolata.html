Laportea lanceolata

Laportea lanceolata, commonly known as the arrowleaf false nettle, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive arrowhead shape, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, and the plant is known for its mild, slightly bitter taste.

Laportea lanceolata is also valued for its ecological role, providing food and habitat for various insects and wildlife.
