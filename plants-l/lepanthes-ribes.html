Lepanthes ribes

Lepanthes ribes, commonly known as the red-tipped orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking red-tipped petals and is often found growing in shaded, humid environments. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom during the summer months. Lepanthes ribes is a terrestrial orchid with a sympodial growth habit, forming pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

Its unique appearance and ecological significance make it a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
