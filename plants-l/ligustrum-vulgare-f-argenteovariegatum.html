Ligustrum vulgare f. argenteovariegatum

Ligustrum vulgare f. argenteovariegatum, commonly known as the silver-leaved privet, is a varietal form of the common privet.

It is characterized by its striking variegated leaves that feature white and green patterns. The plant is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and western Asia. It typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters and has small, dark blue berries.

The foliage is often used in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
