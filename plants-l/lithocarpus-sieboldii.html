Lithocarpus sieboldii

Lithocarpus sieboldii, commonly known as the Japanese ironwood, is a species of evergreen tree belonging to the Fagaceae family.

It is native to East Asia, particularly Japan and parts of China. The plant is characterized by its hard, dense wood and leathery, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green in color. It produces small, chestnut-like fruits that are encased in a thick, woody shell.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its durability and aesthetic features.
