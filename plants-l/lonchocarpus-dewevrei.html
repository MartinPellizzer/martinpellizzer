Lonchocarpus dewevrei

Lonchocarpus dewevrei, commonly known as the African senna, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated, woody pods that contain several seeds. It is often found in savannah and woodland habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
