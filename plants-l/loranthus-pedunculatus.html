Loranthus pedunculatus

Loranthus pedunculatus, commonly known as the mistletoe of the genus Loranthus, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches itself to the branches of host trees.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using light absorbed by the host's leaves. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers that are often inconspicuous, followed by berries that contain sticky seeds. It is found in various regions of the world, including parts of Asia, Africa, and the Americas.

Its parasitic nature and unique reproductive strategy make it an interesting subject in botanical studies.
