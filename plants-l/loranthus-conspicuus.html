Loranthus conspicuus

Loranthus conspicuus, commonly known as the shining mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on a variety of host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.

It possesses scale-like leaves and produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. The plant relies on its host for water and some nutrients, while it provides nectar to attract pollinators such as birds and insects. Its seeds are dispersed by birds that consume the fleshy, red berries.

Loranthus conspicuus plays an important ecological role in its habitat by influencing host tree health and supporting biodiversity.
