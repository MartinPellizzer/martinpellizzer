Leontodon keretinus

Leontodon keretinus, commonly known as the mountain bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, preferring well-drained and sunny environments. The plant features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and produces tall, slender flowering stems with bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, and it is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
