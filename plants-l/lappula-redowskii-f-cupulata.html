Lappula redowskii f. cupulata

Lappula redowskii f. cupulata, commonly known as the cupulate lappula, is a variant of the lappula plant characterized by its distinctive cup-like structures on its stems.

This plant belongs to the family Chenopodiaceae and is native to certain regions of Europe. Its botanical features include small, greenish-yellow flowers that are arranged in clusters, and it typically grows in dry, sandy soils. This unique morphological trait is an adaptation to its arid habitat, aiding in water retention and reducing desiccation.
