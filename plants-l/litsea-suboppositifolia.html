Litsea suboppositifolia

Litsea suboppositifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved litsea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically borne in clusters and are followed by small, berry-like fruits.

Litsea suboppositifolia is valued for its ornamental qualities and has also been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
