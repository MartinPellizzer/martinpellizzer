Lasianthus fasciculus

Lasianthus fasciculus, commonly known as the clustered lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, often found in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The flowers are usually pink or purple, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
