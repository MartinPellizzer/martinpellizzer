Lasiopogon muscoides

Lasiopogon muscoides, commonly known as the mossy leaf orchid, is a small, terrestrial orchid native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. The plant has a slender, creeping stem with small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, moss-like coating. Its flowers are pale green to brownish in color, with three sepals and three petals, and are arranged in a spike.

Lasiopogon muscoides is notable for its unique appearance and its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.
