Lathyrus strictus var. barbarae

Lathyrus strictus var. barbarae, commonly known as the Armenian vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Caucasus region.

It features slender, erect stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, adorned with trifoliate leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several elliptical seeds. It is adapted to rocky and dry environments, showcasing a deep root system for water absorption.

Its botanical characteristics make it both an ornamental plant and a potential forage species.
