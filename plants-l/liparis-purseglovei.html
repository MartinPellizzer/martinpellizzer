Liparis purseglovei

Liparis purseglovei, commonly known as the Pursglove's orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in areas with high altitude and cool temperatures. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellow with intricate patterns, and it is pollinated by specific insects.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Liparis purseglovei is considered endangered and is of great interest to botanists and conservationists.
