Lupinus nuttallii

Lupinus nuttallii, commonly known as Nuttall's lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands, disturbed soils, and rocky slopes. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces cylindrical seed pods that contain several seeds.

Lupinus nuttallii is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in ecological restoration efforts.
