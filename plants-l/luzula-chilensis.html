Luzula chilensis

Luzula chilensis, commonly known as Chilean woodrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, commonly known as grasses, and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, greenish flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Luzula chilensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
