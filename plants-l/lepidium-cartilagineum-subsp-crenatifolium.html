Lepidium cartilagineum subsp. crenatifolium

Lepidium cartilagineum subsp.

crenatifolium, commonly known as the alpine cress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family. It is characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and hairy, glandular leaves.

The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, thriving in rocky or gravelly soils. Its leaves are often crenate or serrate, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is noted for its ability to tolerate cold climates and is often found in mountainous areas across Europe and Asia.
