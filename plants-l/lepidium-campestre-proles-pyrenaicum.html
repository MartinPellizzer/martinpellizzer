Lepidium campestre proles pyrenaicum

Lepidium campestre proles pyrenaicum, commonly known as the Pyrenean cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its finely divided leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

This species is of interest in botanical studies due to its ecological adaptations and potential medicinal properties.
