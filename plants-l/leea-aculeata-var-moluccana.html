Leea aculeata var. moluccana

Leea aculeata var. moluccana, commonly known as the Moluccan leea, is a tropical climbing plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for tropical gardens and greenhouses.
