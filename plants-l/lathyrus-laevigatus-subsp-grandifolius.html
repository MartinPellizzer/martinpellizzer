Lathyrus laevigatus subsp. grandifolius

Lathyrus laevigatus subsp.

grandifolius, commonly known as the large-leafed vetch, is a perennial legume native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pale blue to violet, typically blooming in late spring. Its trifoliate leaves and pod-like fruits are typical of the Fabaceae family.

It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
