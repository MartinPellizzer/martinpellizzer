Luzula kjellmaniana subsp. sibirica

Luzula kjellmaniana subsp.

sibirica, commonly known as the Siberian wood rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Poaceae. It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear greenish to reddish flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wet meadows. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

The leaves are usually narrow, grass-like, and have a bluish-green hue, contributing to its ornamental appeal in horticultural settings.
