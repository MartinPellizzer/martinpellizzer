Lycium berlandieri f. parviflorum

Lycium berlandieri f. parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered boxthorn, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to provide habitat for local wildlife.
