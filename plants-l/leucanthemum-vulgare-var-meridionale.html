Leucanthemum vulgare var. meridionale

Leucanthemum vulgare var. meridionale, commonly known as the southern oxeye daisy, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features showy white ray florets surrounding a central yellow disc, giving it a daisy-like appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces numerous small, yellow flowers in the summer months.

This variety is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
