Leontodon scorzonera

Leontodon scorzonera, commonly known as the meadow bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are sturdy and branching.

Leontodon scorzonera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
