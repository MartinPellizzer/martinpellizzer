Lepanthes wendlandii

Lepanthes wendlandii, commonly known as the Wendland's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments. The plant features a slender, often reddish stem and produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the forest floor, and it thrives in humid, well-drained conditions.

Lepanthes wendlandii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
