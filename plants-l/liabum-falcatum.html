Liabum falcatum

Liabum falcatum, commonly known as the flatleaf liabum, is a herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a smooth or slightly hairy texture.

Liabum falcatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
