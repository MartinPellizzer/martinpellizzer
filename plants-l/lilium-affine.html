Lilium affine

Lilium affine, commonly known as the eastern fritillary, is a species of lily native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink, white, and red. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves and produces capsules that contain numerous small, dark seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Lilium affine is a perennial herb that plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
