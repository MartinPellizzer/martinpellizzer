Lespedeza bicolor f. nakaiana

Lespedeza bicolor f. nakaiana, commonly known as the Japanese lespedeza, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its delicate appearance and ability to attract pollinators.

Additionally, it has been used in ecological restoration projects for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities.
