Linaria floridana

Linaria floridana, commonly known as the Florida toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the plantain family, Plantaginaceae, and is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or disturbed soils and is often found in open habitats such as roadsides and pine flatwoods. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and its flowers are arranged in dense spikes.

Linaria floridana is an herbaceous perennial that plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
