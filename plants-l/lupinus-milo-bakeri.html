Lupinus milo-bakeri

Lupinus milo-bakeri, commonly known as Baker's lupin, is a rare flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and spiny stems. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of purple and blue, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Lupinus milo-bakeri is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils in desert regions.
