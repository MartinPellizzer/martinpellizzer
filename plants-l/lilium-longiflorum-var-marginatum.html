Lilium longiflorum var. marginatum

Lilium longiflorum var. marginatum, commonly known as the white tiger lily, is a variant of the Asiatic lily characterized by its striking white petals with distinctive red-orange margins.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 3 feet and has strap-like, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

The flowers are fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
