Leuconotis gigantea

Leuconotis gigantea, commonly known as the giant leuconotis, is a striking flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 centimeters in diameter, typically displaying vibrant shades of yellow and orange. The plant has a woody stem and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Leuconotis gigantea is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
