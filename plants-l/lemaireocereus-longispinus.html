Lemaireocereus longispinus

Lemaireocereus longispinus, commonly known as the long-spined cactus, is a species native to the Canary Islands.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and long, sharp spines. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, where it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, appearing in late spring or early summer.

Lemaireocereus longispinus is often cultivated for its striking appearance and drought tolerance.
