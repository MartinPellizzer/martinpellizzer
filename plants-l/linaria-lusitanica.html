Linaria lusitanica

Linaria lusitanica, commonly known as the Portuguese toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and North Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial plant characterized by its slender stems and lance-shaped leaves. The flowers are typically yellow or orange, with a distinctive two-lipped structure, and bloom from spring to summer. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and coastal areas.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
