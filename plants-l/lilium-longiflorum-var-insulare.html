Lilium longiflorum var. insulare

Lilium longiflorum var. insulare, commonly known as the Japanese white lily, is a botanical variant of the Easter lily.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with narrow, strap-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. Native to Japan, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it a valuable subject for both horticultural study and garden cultivation.
