Lilium × elegans var. incomparabile

Lilium × elegans var. incomparabile, commonly known as the Giant White Lily, is a hybrid perennial bulbous plant native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its large, white, trumpet-shaped flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in length. The plant typically grows to a height of 4 to 6 feet, with strap-like, dark green foliage. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

The flowers emit a sweet, fragrant aroma, making it a popular choice for floral arrangements.
