Limonium × pericotii

Limonium × pericotii, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a hybrid species within the Limonium genus, characterized by its showy, spike-like inflorescences that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Limonium × pericotii is also valued for its ability to tolerate saline environments, making it a resilient choice for coastal landscaping.
