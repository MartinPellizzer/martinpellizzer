Leontodon × kaiseri

Leontodon × kaiseri, commonly known as the Kaiseri dandelion, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and toothed, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in meadows and grasslands, thriving in temperate climates. It is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in some areas.

Botanically, it exhibits features of both parent species, Leontodon taraxacoides and Leontodon alpinus, showcasing genetic diversity.
