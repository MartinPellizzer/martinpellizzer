Lotononis peduncularis var. meyeri

Lotononis peduncularis var. meyeri, commonly known as Meyer's lotononis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant produces yellow disc florets surrounded by white to pale pink ray florets, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for low-maintenance landscaping.
