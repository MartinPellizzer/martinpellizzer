Lanaria plumosa

Lanaria plumosa, commonly known as the woolly morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in the morning and open fully by midday. The plant has a trailing growth habit and produces heart-shaped leaves with a soft, hairy texture. Its flowers come in a range of colors, including pink, purple, and white, and are often used in ornamental gardening.

Lanaria plumosa is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
