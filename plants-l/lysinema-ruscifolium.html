Lysinema ruscifolium

Lysinema ruscifolium, commonly known as the red-tipped plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are typically reddish at the base, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in the early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a useful plant for ecological studies and habitat restoration.
