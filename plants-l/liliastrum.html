Liliastrum

Liliastrum, commonly known as the star of Bethlehem, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Liliaceae.

These plants are characterized by their showy, bell-shaped flowers that often display a striking white or pink color. Liliastrum species are typically perennial herbs with lanceolate leaves and are native to various regions in Europe, Asia, and North Africa. They thrive in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making them suitable for gardens and natural landscapes.

The genus is also noted for its ornamental value and historical significance in herbal medicine.
