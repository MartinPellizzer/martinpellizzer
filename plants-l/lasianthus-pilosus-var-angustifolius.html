Lasianthus pilosus var. angustifolius

Lasianthus pilosus var. angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
