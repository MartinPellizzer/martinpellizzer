Linaria supina var. font-queri

Linaria supina var. font-queri, commonly known as the prostrate toadflax, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, creeping stems that form a dense mat of foliage, often reaching up to 30 cm in height. The plant produces small, yellow-orange flowers with two lips, typical of the Plantaginaceae family. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptical, and covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
