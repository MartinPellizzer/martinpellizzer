Lathyrus latifolius var. algericus

Lathyrus latifolius var. algericus, commonly known as Algerian broadleaf vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of blue or purple pea-like flowers. The plant produces elongated, slender pods that contain several small, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
