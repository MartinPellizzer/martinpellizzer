Lilium canadense subsp. michiganense

Lilium canadense subsp.

michiganense, commonly known as the Michigan lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America. It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear large, showy white to pinkish flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly different floral structure and geographic distribution compared to other members of the *Lilium canadense* species complex.
