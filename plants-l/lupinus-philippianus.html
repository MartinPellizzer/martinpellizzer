Lupinus philippianus

Lupinus philippianus, commonly known as the Philippine lupine, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Lupinus philippianus plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native environment.
