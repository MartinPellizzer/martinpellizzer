Languas padacanca

Languas padacanca, commonly known as the golden flame orchid, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It features striking, brightly colored flowers that range from orange to red, with intricate patterns resembling flames. The plant has strap-like leaves that are long and narrow, often growing in a rosette formation. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the rich, moist soil of its natural habitat.

Languas padacanca is primarily cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant and unique floral display.
