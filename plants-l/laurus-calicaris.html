Laurus calicaris

Laurus calicaris, commonly known as the Calycary laurel, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features simple, alternate, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and aromatic foliage, it is often cultivated in horticultural settings.
