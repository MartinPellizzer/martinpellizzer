Lonicera depressa var. myrtillus

Lonicera depressa var. myrtillus, commonly known as the dwarf honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features small, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring, typically in shades of yellow and white. The plant has slender, arching stems and elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
