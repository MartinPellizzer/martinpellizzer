Liparis verrucosa

Liparis verrucosa, commonly known as the warty orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, often yellowish stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish or brownish flowers that are clustered in a spike. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky areas.

Liparis verrucosa is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its unique, warty texture on its stems.
