Lachnospermum ericifolium

Lachnospermum ericifolium, commonly known as the heath rose, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils, often in alpine or subalpine regions. The plant produces small, pink or red flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are leathery and evergreen, adapted to the harsh environmental conditions of its habitat.

Lachnospermum ericifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in cooler climates.
