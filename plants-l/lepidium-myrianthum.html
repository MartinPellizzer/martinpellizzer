Lepidium myrianthum

Lepidium myrianthum, commonly known as the clustered cress, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard plants. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along stream banks. Its small, white or pink flowers are clustered in racemes, and the leaves are pinnately divided.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
