Litsea amara var. attenuata

Litsea amara var. attenuata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved litsea, is a variant of the litsea amara species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features lanceolate, aromatic leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by elliptical, red or orange berries. Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ornamental horticulture and ecological studies.

The plant is also noted for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
