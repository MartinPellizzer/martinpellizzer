Lipocarpha prieuriana var. crassicuspis

Lipocarpha prieuriana var. crassicuspis, commonly known as the broad-ciliate spikerush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical inflorescence that resembles a spiky structure, typical of many sedges. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered with fine, bristly hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting aquatic ecosystems.
