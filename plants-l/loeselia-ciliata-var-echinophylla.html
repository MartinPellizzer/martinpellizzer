Loeselia ciliata var. echinophylla

Loeselia ciliata var. echinophylla, commonly known as the hairy leaved loeselia, is a perennial herb native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticultural and ecological studies.
