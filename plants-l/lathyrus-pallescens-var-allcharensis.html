Lathyrus pallescens var. allcharensis

Lathyrus pallescens var. allcharensis, commonly known as the pale lathyris, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, pea-like flowers that typically range in color from pale pink to white. The plant produces small, elongated pods containing several seeds, and its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
