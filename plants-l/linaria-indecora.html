Linaria indecora

Linaria indecora, commonly known as the common toadflax, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow or orange flowers that resemble small toads, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It produces slender, branching stems and has small, opposite leaves.

Linaria indecora is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate.
