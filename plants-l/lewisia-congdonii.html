Lewisia congdonii

Lewisia congdonii, commonly known as the Congdon's Lewisia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water, adapting it to its arid environment. The plant produces striking pink to purple flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a desirable addition to native plant landscapes.
