Leitneria pilosa

Leitneria pilosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves that are often tinged with red or purple on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in loose inflorescences. Its fruit is a three-lobed capsule that contains several seeds.

Leitneria pilosa is often found in disturbed areas and is considered an invasive species in some parts of the world.
