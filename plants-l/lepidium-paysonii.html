Lepidium paysonii

Lepidium paysonii, commonly known as Payson's cress, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as stream banks and meadows. It produces small, yellow flowers and has slender, branching stems.

Its leaves are alternate and often have a slightly hairy texture.
