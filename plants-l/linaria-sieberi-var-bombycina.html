Linaria sieberi var. bombycina

Linaria sieberi var. bombycina, commonly known as the silky toadflax, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems covered in soft, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that are tubular in shape and attract various pollinators. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.
