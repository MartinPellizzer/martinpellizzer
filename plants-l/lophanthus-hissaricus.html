Lophanthus hissaricus

Lophanthus hissaricus, commonly known as the Caucasian lophanthus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in rocky and dry habitats. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a distinctive glandular appearance.

Lophanthus hissaricus is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
