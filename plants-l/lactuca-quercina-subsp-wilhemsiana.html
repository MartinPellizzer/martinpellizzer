Lactuca quercina subsp. wilhemsiana

Lactuca quercina subsp.

wilhemsiana, commonly known as the oakleaf lettuce, is a wild lettuce species native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its lanceolate to ovate leaves that are often lobed or toothed, resembling the leaves of oak trees.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in a loose cluster, followed by small, oval-shaped seed capsules.

This subspecies is valued for its potential medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
