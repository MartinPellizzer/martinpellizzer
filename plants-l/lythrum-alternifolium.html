Lythrum alternifolium

Lythrum alternifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf redroot, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lythraceae family, which includes other plants such as the poinsettia and the common willow. The plant is characterized by its slender, alternate leaves and clusters of small, pinkish to red flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
