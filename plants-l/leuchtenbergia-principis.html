Leuchtenbergia principis

Leuchtenbergia principis, commonly known as the "moon cactus," is a unique hybrid plant formed by grafting a Hylocereus species onto a Gymnocalycium species.

It is native to the arid regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in hot, dry climates. The plant is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that bloom at night, typically in shades of white, pink, or yellow. Its foliage consists of thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh conditions.

Due to its striking appearance and ease of care, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and indoor gardening.
