Leionema lamprophyllum subsp. orbiculare

Leionema lamprophyllum subsp.

orbiculare, commonly known as the silver-leaved rock heath, is a low-growing shrub native to eastern Australia. It features dense, silver-gray foliage with small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in coastal or dry inland areas.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
