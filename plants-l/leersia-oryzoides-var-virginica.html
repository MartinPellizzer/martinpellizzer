Leersia oryzoides var. virginica

Leersia oryzoides var. virginica, commonly known as Virginia wild rice, is a grass species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and small, spike-like clusters of pale green to golden brown flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slight sheen.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in supporting wetland biodiversity and its historical significance as a food source for indigenous peoples.
