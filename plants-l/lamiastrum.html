Lamiastrum

Lamiastrum, commonly known as dead-nettle, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, often two-lipped flowers. The plants are typically herbaceous and can be found in a variety of habitats, including meadows and woodlands. Lamiastrum species are known for their ability to thrive in moist environments and are sometimes considered weeds.

They are also valued for their ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
