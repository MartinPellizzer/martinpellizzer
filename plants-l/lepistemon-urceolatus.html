Lepistemon urceolatus

Lepistemon urceolatus, commonly known as the bottlebrush plant, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tubular flowers that resemble a bottlebrush, typically arranged in dense clusters. The plant has a shrub-like growth habit with woody stems and small, narrow leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The flowers are often visited by birds and insects, contributing to its ecological significance.
