Lonicera androsaemifolia

Lonicera androsaemifolia, commonly known as the Andros false honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically yellow or white, which bloom in early spring. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that ripens in the fall, attracting various wildlife.

It is often found in moist woodlands and along stream banks, where it thrives in partial to full shade.
