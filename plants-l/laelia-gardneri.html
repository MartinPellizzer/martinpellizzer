Laelia gardneri

Laelia gardneri, commonly known as the Gardner's laelia, is a species of orchid native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, often fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant typically produces multiple pseudobulbs, each supporting a pair of opposite leaves. Its flowers are borne on terminal inflorescences and are known for their intricate, spurred petals.

Laelia gardneri is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to cultivation under controlled conditions.
