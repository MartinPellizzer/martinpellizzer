Laurus triplinervia

Laurus triplinervia, commonly known as the three-veined laurel, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other well-known plants like laurel and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves, which contain essential oils and have been used traditionally in various cultural practices. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically white or pale yellow, and it produces drupes that are often used in local cuisines.

Laurus triplinervia is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its potential medicinal properties.
