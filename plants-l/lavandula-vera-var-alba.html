Lavandula vera var. alba

Lavandula vera var. alba, commonly known as white lavender, is a variety of the lavender plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its pale to white flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves and a compact, bushy growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its delicate appearance, it is often used in ornamental gardening and for its subtle, aromatic fragrance.
