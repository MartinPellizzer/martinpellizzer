Lycopodium cuernavacence

Lycopodium cuernavacence, commonly known as the Cuernavaca clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or rocky crevices. The plant features a stem with whorled leaves that are often scale-like in appearance. It produces sporophylls that bear spores, which are dispersed by wind.

Lycopodium cuernavacence plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and small invertebrates.
