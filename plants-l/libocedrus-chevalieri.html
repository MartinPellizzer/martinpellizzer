Libocedrus chevalieri

Libocedrus chevalieri, commonly known as the Sierra Madre cedar, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Cupressaceae family and is characterized by its scale-like leaves and small, rounded cones. The tree typically grows in mountainous regions, preferring well-drained soils and cool, moist climates. This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
