Lycopodioides labordei

Lycopodioides labordei, commonly known as the Texas clubmoss, is a small, evergreen fern-like plant native to the south-central United States.

It belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant has needle-like leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded, forested areas.

Lycopodioides labordei plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates in its native environment.
