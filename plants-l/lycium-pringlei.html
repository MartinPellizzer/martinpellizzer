Lycium pringlei

Lycium pringlei, commonly known as the Pringle's boxthorn, is a shrub native to arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. Lycium pringlei is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water.

Its ecological importance includes providing food for wildlife and stabilizing soil in desert ecosystems.
