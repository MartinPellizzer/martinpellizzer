Lupinus chrysocalyx

Lupinus chrysocalyx, commonly known as the golden-hued lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers and upright growth habit. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Lupinus chrysocalyx is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
