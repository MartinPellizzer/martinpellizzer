Linaria spuria var. dentata

Linaria spuria var. dentata, commonly known as the toothed toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are narrow and often have serrated edges, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This plant prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
