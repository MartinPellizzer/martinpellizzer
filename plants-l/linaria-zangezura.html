Linaria zangezura

Linaria zangezura, commonly known as the Persian toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the genus Linaria in the family Plantaginaceae, characterized by its spurred flowers and lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its bright yellow or orange flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
