Lenticularia monorhiza

Lenticularia monorhiza, commonly known as the single-rooted lens-shaped plant, is a unique species characterized by its distinctive lenticular or lens-shaped root system.

This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are usually narrow and elongated, adapted to maximize light absorption in low-light conditions. The plant's root structure is specialized for nutrient uptake and water retention, making it resilient in wetland habitats.

It plays a minor but ecologically significant role in maintaining soil stability and supporting local biodiversity.
