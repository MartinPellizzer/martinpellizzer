Lepanthes carinata

Lepanthes carinata, commonly known as the spurred orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, greenish-white flowers with prominent spurred petals. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in the understory of tropical forests. Lepanthes carinata has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, fragrant blooms.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
