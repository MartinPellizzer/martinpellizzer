Lupinus minimus var. hellerae

Lupinus minimus var. hellerae, commonly known as the Hellerae lupine, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
