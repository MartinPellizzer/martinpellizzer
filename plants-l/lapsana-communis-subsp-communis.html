Lapsana communis subsp. communis

Lapsana communis subsp.

communis, commonly known as common cudweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, western Asia, and northern Africa. It is an annual or biennial herb that grows up to 60 cm tall, with slender, hairy stems and alternate, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming from June to August. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides, in fields, and near waste areas.

The species is characterized by its ability to spread through seeds and its resilience in various environmental conditions.
