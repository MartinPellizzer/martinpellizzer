Lonicera caerulea var. glabrescens

Lonicera caerulea var. glabrescens, commonly known as the early black honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in the spring, followed by edible berries that ripen to a deep black color. These berries are rich in antioxidants and are often used in jams, wines, and traditional medicines.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is commonly cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
