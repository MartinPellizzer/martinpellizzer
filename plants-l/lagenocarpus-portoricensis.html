Lagenocarpus portoricensis

Lagenocarpus portoricensis, commonly known as the Puerto Rican fig tree, is a species native to the Caribbean island of Puerto Rico.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows in coastal areas and forested regions. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth texture. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in clusters, while the fruit is a fleshy, edible fig-like structure.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food for various wildlife species.
