Laennecia artemisiifolia

Laennecia artemisiifolia, commonly known as wormwood, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe, Asia, and North America.

It features a rosette of gray-green, hairy leaves that are lance-shaped and often have a strong, aromatic scent. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clustered inflorescences, which bloom in the summer months. Its roots are rich in volatile oils and have been used traditionally in herbal medicine.

The plant is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its role in traditional ecological systems.
