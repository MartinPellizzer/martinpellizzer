Lessingianthus dichrous

Lessingianthus dichrous, commonly known as the two-faced sunflower, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its distinctive two-toned flowers. The plant features vibrant yellow petals that transition to deep red near the center, creating a visually appealing contrast. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often growing in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
