Larix gmelinii f. genhensis

Larix gmelinii f. genhensis, commonly known as the Korean larch, is a variant of the Siberian larch, characterized by its distinct botanical features.

It is a deciduous conifer that exhibits needle-like leaves which turn golden before falling in autumn. This subspecies is native to the Korean Peninsula and parts of northern China, thriving in temperate climates. The cones are typically small and woody, containing two to three seeds, and the tree produces a unique, aromatic resin.

Its growth habit is generally upright, with a conical shape, making it a notable ornamental and ecological plant in its native habitat.
