Lobelia siphilitica var. candida

Lobelia siphilitica var. candida, commonly known as white cardinal flower, is a striking perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear clusters of tubular, white to pale pink flowers. The leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs. This variety is a cultivar of the common cardinal flower, distinguished by its lighter floral coloration.

It thrives in moist, rich soils and is often found in woodland habitats.
