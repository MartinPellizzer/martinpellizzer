Lepanthes papyrophylla

Lepanthes papyrophylla, commonly known as the papery-leaved orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, papery petals and sepals, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually small and fragrant, often displaying a range of colors including white, pink, and purple.

Lepanthes papyrophylla is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
