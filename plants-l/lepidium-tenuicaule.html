Lepidium tenuicaule

Lepidium tenuicaule, commonly known as slender peppergrass, is a small annual herb native to North America.

It typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in prairies, roadsides, and open woodlands. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters. Its leaves are alternate, linear, and often have a slight pubescence.

Lepidium tenuicaule is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard plants.
