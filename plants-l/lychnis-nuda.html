Lychnis nuda

Lychnis nuda, commonly known as the naked-stemmed catchfly, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that are often hairless, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and alternate, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Lychnis nuda is adapted to grow in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.
