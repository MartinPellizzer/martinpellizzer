Latouria oncidiochila

Latouria oncidiochila, commonly known as the orchid of the genus Latouria, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its intricate floral structure, featuring a prominent lip that is often brightly colored. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are usually small to medium in size, with a complex arrangement of petals and sepals.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
