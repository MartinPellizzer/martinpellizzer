Lupinus alimanens

Lupinus alimanens, commonly known as the coastal lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western coast of the United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and spiny stems. The plant produces showy, pea-like flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, which bloom in summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Lupinus alimanens is adapted to coastal environments, thriving in sandy or gravelly soils and tolerating salt spray.
