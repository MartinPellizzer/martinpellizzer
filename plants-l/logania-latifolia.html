Logania latifolia

Logania latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved logania, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a member of the Loganiaceae family, which includes several genera with showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
