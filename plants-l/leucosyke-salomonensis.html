Leucosyke salomonensis

Leucosyke salomonensis, commonly known as the Solomon Islands tree fern, is a tropical plant native to the islands of the Solomon archipelago.

It is a member of the Cyatheaceae family and is characterized by its large, feather-like fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant has a tall, unbranched trunk covered in a layer of persistent, fibrous roots. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The fronds are used in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber by local communities.
