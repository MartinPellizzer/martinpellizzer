Lathyrus wilsonii

Lathyrus wilsonii, commonly known as Wilson's vetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial legume characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
