Lycopodioides cuspidata

Lycopodioides cuspidata, commonly known as the spike moss, is a species of clubmoss belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant has a distinctive appearance with a slender, upright stem and small, scale-like leaves arranged in whorls. Its reproductive structures, called strobila, are cone-like and contain the plant's spores.

Lycopodioides cuspidata plays an important role in maintaining soil stability and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
