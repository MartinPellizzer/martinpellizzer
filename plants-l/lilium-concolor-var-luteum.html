Lilium concolor var. luteum

Lilium concolor var. luteum, commonly known as the yellow camellia lily, is a variant of the concolor lily native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and can grow up to 3 feet tall. It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
