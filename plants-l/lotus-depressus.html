Lotus depressus

Lotus depressus, commonly known as the marsh lotus, is a perennial aquatic plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that float on the water surface, with long, slender stems that support its showy pink or white flowers. The plant has a distinctive underground rhizome system that helps it anchor and store nutrients. Its flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The lotus is valued for its ornamental beauty and has cultural significance in various regions.
