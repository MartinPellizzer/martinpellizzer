Ludwigia myrtifolia

Ludwigia myrtifolia, commonly known as the myrtle-leaved Ludwigia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Onagraceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, particularly in countries like Brazil and Mexico. Its flowers are small, yellowish, and arranged in clusters, typical of the Onagraceae family.

Ludwigia myrtifolia is often grown as an ornamental plant in aquatic or bog gardens due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to moist environments.
