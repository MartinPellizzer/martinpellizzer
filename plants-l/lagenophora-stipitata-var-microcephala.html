Lagenophora stipitata var. microcephala

Lagenophora stipitata var. microcephala, commonly known as the small-headed cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and clusters of tiny, white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats and is found in various regions of southern Africa. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and flies.

The species is notable for its compact growth habit and distinctive floral morphology.
