Lilium bulbiferum var. chaixii

Lilium bulbiferum var. chaixii, commonly known as the Chaix's bulbous lily, is a rare and striking member of the Liliaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, where it thrives in cool, temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pale pink, with a distinctive, recurved petal structure. Its bulbous rhizomes are an essential part of its growth cycle, enabling it to store nutrients and survive in harsh environmental conditions.
