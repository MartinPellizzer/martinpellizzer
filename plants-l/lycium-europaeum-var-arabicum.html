Lycium europaeum var. arabicum

Lycium europaeum var. arabicum, commonly known as the desert nightshade, is a shrub native to arid regions of the Mediterranean and Middle East.

It is characterized by its small, red berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in sandy or rocky soils.

Its berries contain antioxidants and have been used for their purported health benefits in various cultures.
