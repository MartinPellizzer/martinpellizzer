Laserpitium paradoxum

Laserpitium paradoxum, commonly known as the paradox grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, inconspicuous yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a hairy texture that helps reduce water loss.

Laserpitium paradoxum is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to high-altitude habitats.
