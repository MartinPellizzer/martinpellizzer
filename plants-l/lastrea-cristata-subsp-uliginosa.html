Lastrea cristata subsp. uliginosa

Lastrea cristata subsp.

uliginosa, commonly known as the bog lastrea, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its compact, rosette-forming growth habit and thick, fleshy leaves that store water.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by distinctive, spiky fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or bog environments.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping in suitable climates.
