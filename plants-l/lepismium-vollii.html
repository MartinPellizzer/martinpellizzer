Lepismium vollii

Lepismium vollii, commonly known as the velvet tangle, is a climbing plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing along forest floors or in rocky crevices. Its leaves are typically thick and waxy, which helps it retain moisture in its natural habitat.

Lepismium vollii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and unique texture.
