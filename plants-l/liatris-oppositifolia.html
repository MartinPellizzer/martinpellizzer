Liatris oppositifolia

Liatris oppositifolia, commonly known as the oppositeleaf bluetail, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, spike-like inflorescence composed of numerous blue to purple flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are opposite in arrangement, giving it its scientific name, and it blooms during the summer months.

Liatris oppositifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
