Lathyrus alberjilla

Lathyrus alberjilla, commonly known as the alpine vetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial legume that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from purple to pink, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its trifoliate leaves consist of three leaflets, and it has a climbing or trailing growth habit.

Lathyrus alberjilla is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in soil stabilization in its native habitats.
