Lonchocarpus pterocarpus

Lonchocarpus pterocarpus, commonly known as the yellow trumpet tree, is a tropical leguminous plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves, which are typical of the Fabaceae family. The plant produces large, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.

Its growth habit includes a tall, slender trunk with a dense canopy, making it a notable species in tropical forests.
