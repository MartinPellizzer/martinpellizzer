Lilium rubrum

Lilium rubrum, commonly known as the red lily, is a species of flowering plant in the Liliaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is characterized by its striking red or reddish-pink petals. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with tall, sturdy stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in late summer, producing a rich fragrance that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The bulbs of Lilium rubrum are also used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
