Lasianthus virgatus

Lasianthus virgatus, commonly known as the slender leaved gentian, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its showy blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth. It has opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Lasianthus virgatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens.
