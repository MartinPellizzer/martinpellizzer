Linociera courtallensis

Linociera courtallensis, commonly known as the Ceylon myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to Sri Lanka.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, spherical berries. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded areas of forests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
