Lonicera nigra f. changbaishana

Lonicera nigra f. changbaishana, commonly known as the Changbai Mountain honeysuckle, is a rare flowering plant native to the forests of the Changbai Mountains in China.

It is a shrub characterized by its dark purple to black berries and fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to the high-altitude conditions of its native habitat. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a hairy texture.

This form is distinguished by its unique floral structure and coloration, making it a notable species within the honeysuckle family.
