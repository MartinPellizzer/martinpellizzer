Leptodermis lanata

Leptodermis lanata, commonly known as the woolly skin vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, lanate (woolly) leaves and stems, which provide a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The species is valued both for its ornamental appearance and its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
