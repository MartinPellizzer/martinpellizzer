Lobivia grandiflora

Lobivia grandiflora, commonly known as the giant lobivia, is a succulent native to the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that store water. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for arid garden environments.

Due to its slow growth and specific care requirements, it is often cultivated as a prized ornamental plant.
