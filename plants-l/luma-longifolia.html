Luma longifolia

Luma longifolia, commonly known as the Chilean firetree, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of bright red, orange, or yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Luma longifolia is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its striking floral display.

It also has historical and cultural significance in the regions where it is found.
