Lupinus lepidus var. sellulus

Lupinus lepidus var. sellulus, commonly known as the Sierra lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and woodland edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
