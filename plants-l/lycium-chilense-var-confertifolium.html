Lycium chilense var. confertifolium

Lycium chilense var. confertifolium, commonly known as the Chilean boxthorn, is a shrub native to southern Chile and parts of Argentina.

It features dense, spiny foliage and produces small, orange-red berries that are edible. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and thrives in coastal and arid environments. It is often used in landscaping due to its hardy nature and ornamental value.

The berries are a food source for birds and can be used in jams or wines.
