Lonicera apodantha

Lonicera apodantha, commonly known as the slender honeysuckle, is a deciduous climbing vine native to China.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The plant is characterized by its slender, twining stems and is often found in woodland or moist habitats. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The fruit is a red, fleshy berry that ripens in late summer, providing food for birds and small mammals.
