Lycaste campbellii

Lycaste campbellii, commonly known as the Campbell's lycaste, is a species of orchid native to the humid forests of Central America.

It is characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves and produces fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows as a sympodial orchid, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. It thrives in warm, moist environments with high humidity and partial shade.

Lycaste campbellii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
