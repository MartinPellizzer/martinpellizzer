Leontodon saxatilis subsp. longirostris

Leontodon saxatilis subsp.

longirostris, commonly known as the long-beaked bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to rocky and mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and basal leaves that are typically lanceolate in shape.

The flowers are blue to violet in color, arranged in a loose raceme, and have a distinctive bell-shaped corolla. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as cliffs and grasslands.

This subspecies is notable for its morphological adaptations to its harsh, alpine environment.
