Lonicera periclymenum var. quercina-variegata

Lonicera periclymenum var. quercina-variegata, commonly known as the variegated honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub characterized by its distinctive variegated foliage.

It features heart-shaped leaves with white and green patterns, adding visual interest to gardens. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for a variety of garden settings.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
