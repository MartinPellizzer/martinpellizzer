Leleba multiplex f. variegata

Leleba multiplex f. variegata, commonly known as the variegated leleba, is a striking ornamental plant native to parts of Africa.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage with distinct variegation patterns. The plant typically features lance-shaped leaves that are often edged with white or yellow, creating a striking visual contrast. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its aesthetic appeal, it is popular in tropical and subtropical gardens as a decorative plant.
