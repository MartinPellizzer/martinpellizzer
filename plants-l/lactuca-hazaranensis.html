Lactuca hazaranensis

Lactuca hazaranensis, commonly known as the Hazarani lettuce, is a species of flowering plant in the daisy family, Asteraceae.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of the Caucasus, particularly in eastern Turkey and northern Iran. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous environments, where it thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are usually elongated and slightly hairy, with a mild, slightly bitter taste.

Lactuca hazaranensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
