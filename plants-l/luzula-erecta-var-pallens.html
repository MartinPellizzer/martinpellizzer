Luzula erecta var. pallens

Luzula erecta var. pallens, commonly known as silver hair grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and dense clusters of pale blue to silver-green flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its common name.

Luzula erecta var. pallens is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partially shaded conditions.
