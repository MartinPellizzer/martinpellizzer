Linaria saxatilis var. glutinosa

Linaria saxatilis var. glutinosa, commonly known as sticky toadflax, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of bright yellow, tubular flowers. The leaves are linear and often covered in a sticky, glandular substance, which is a distinguishing feature of this variety. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous areas.

This plant is adapted to harsh environments and is considered a hardy, drought-tolerant species.
