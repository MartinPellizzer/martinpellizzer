Lysimachia stenosepala var. lutea

Lysimachia stenosepala var. lutea, commonly known as the yellow wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a creeping rhizome that allows it to spread and form dense mats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
