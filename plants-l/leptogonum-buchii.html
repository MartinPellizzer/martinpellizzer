Leptogonum buchii

Leptogonum buchii, commonly known as the silverweed, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant features small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late winter to early spring. Its leaves are characterized by their silvery-white undersides and a rough, hairy texture.

Leptogonum buchii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and early bloom.
