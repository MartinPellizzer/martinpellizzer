Lycopus europaeus f. velutina

Lycopus europaeus f. velutina, commonly known as velvet leaf water-hobble, is a flowering plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety foliage, which gives it its specific epithet "velutina.". The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It typically grows in moist, shady environments, such as woodlands and stream banks.

This subspecies is often found in Europe and parts of Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates.
