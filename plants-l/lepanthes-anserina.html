Lepanthes anserina

Lepanthes anserina, commonly known as the silver-veined orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking white petals with silver veins, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments within tropical forests. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its delicate, fragrant flowers.

Lepanthes anserina is often found epiphytically on trees, where it absorbs moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.
