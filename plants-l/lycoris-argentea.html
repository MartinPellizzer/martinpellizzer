Lycoris argentea

Lycoris argentea, commonly known as the white spider lily, is a striking perennial plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear clusters of white, spider-like flowers in the autumn. The plant's leaves are narrow and grass-like, appearing in the spring before the flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Lycoris argentea is also noted for its ability to naturalize and create dense, elegant displays over time.
