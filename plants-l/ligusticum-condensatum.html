Ligusticum condensatum

Ligusticum condensatum, commonly known as Chinese lovage, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate flowers. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small white to greenish-yellow flowers in clustered umbels. It is often cultivated for its medicinal properties and as a culinary herb.

Its rhizomes and leaves contain volatile oils that contribute to its distinctive aroma and therapeutic uses.
