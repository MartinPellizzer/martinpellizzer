Lonicera boczkarnikovae

Lonicera boczkarnikovae, commonly known as the Boczkarnikovae honeysuckle, is a rare and critically endangered species native to the mountainous regions of southeastern Europe.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its slender stems, opposite leaves, and clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. Due to its limited distribution and habitat destruction, it is considered one of the most threatened honeysuckle species.

Conservation efforts are ongoing to protect and propagate this unique botanical specimen.
