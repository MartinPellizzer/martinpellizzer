Lucuma nitida

Lucuma nitida, commonly known as the velvet tamarillo, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, velvety red fruits. The plant typically grows in subtropical and tropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils and warm climates. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a slightly hairy texture, while its flowers are small and yellowish-green.

The fruit, which matures in late summer, is prized for its sweet, tangy flavor and nutritional value.
