Lithospermum aucheri

Lithospermum aucheri, commonly known as Persian rock pepper, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus and parts of Turkey.

It features erect, branched stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 60 cm in height. The plant produces small, pale pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from June to August. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with toothed margins, and the plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

Lithospermum aucheri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowering display.
