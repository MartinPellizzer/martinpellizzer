Lamprodithyros russegeri

Lamprodithyros russegeri, commonly known as the Russian thyme.

This plant is a member of the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is found in temperate regions. The small, fragrant flowers are usually pink or purple, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Its aromatic leaves are often used in traditional herbal remedies.
