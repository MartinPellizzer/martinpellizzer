Leonurus cardiaca subsp. persicus

Leonurus cardiaca subsp.

persicus, commonly known as Persian heartweed, is a perennial herb native to regions of the Middle East and Central Asia. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in whorled clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or semi-arid habitats.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly different morphology compared to the more widely known L. cardiaca.
