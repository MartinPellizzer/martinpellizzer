Lupinus misticola

Lupinus misticola, commonly known as the misty lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of violet or blue-purple flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and its root system includes nitrogen-fixing nodules. Lupinus misticola thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny areas such as meadows and rocky slopes.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
