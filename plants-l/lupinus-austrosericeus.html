Lupinus austrosericeus

Lupinus austrosericeus, commonly known as the southern silky lupin, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its soft, silky leaves and showy purple or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has a taproot system and produces pods that contain several small, edible seeds.

Lupinus austrosericeus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its nitrogen-fixing ability in agricultural settings.
