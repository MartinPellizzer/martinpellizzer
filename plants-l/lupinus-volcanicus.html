Lupinus volcanicus

Lupinus volcanicus, commonly known as the volcanic lupin, is a flowering plant native to the volcanic regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of blue or purple pea-like flowers. The plant thrives in nutrient-poor, volcanic soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. Its leaves are compound, consisting of small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Lupinus volcanicus plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems in its native habitat.
