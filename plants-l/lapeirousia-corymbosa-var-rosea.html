Lapeirousia corymbosa var. rosea

Lapeirousia corymbosa var. rosea, commonly known as the pink-flowered marsh daisy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of pink to magenta flowers during the spring and summer months. The plant thrives in wet, marshy environments and is often found in coastal areas. It has a fibrous root system and is adapted to tolerate saline conditions.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, contributing to its ecological importance in its native habitat.
