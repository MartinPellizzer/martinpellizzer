Lophochloa rohlfsii

Lophochloa rohlfsii, commonly known as the Rohlfs' sedge, is a grass-like plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread vegetatively.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland and riparian ecosystems.
