Lobivia pentlandii f. brunneorosea

Lobivia pentlandii f. brunneorosea, commonly known as the Brunneorose Lobivia, is a compact, branching cactus native to the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It features pale green to bluish-green stems with a distinctive reddish-brown or pinkish hue, giving it its varietal name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring. It is well adapted to arid conditions, with thick, fleshy stems that store water.

This rare and attractive species is often grown by cactus enthusiasts for its unique appearance and hardy nature.
