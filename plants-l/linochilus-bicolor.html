Linochilus bicolor

Linochilus bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its strikingly contrasting colors on its petals and sepals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in cloud forests. Its flowers are small but showy, with a unique coloration pattern that aids in attracting specific pollinators.

Due to its habitat specificity, Linochilus bicolor is considered a rare and endangered species.
