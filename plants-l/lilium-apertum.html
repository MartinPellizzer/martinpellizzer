Lilium apertum

Lilium apertum, commonly known as the Turk's cap lily, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking, showy flowers that are typically pink or red, with a distinctive, cup-like shape. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a single stem that supports its vibrant blooms. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or dry habitats.

Lilium apertum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
