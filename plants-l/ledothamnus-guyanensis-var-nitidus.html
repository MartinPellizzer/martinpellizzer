Ledothamnus guyanensis var. nitidus

Ledothamnus guyanensis var. nitidus, commonly known as the glossy ledothamnus, is a shrub native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage, with leaves that are glossy and often arranged in opposite pairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, fleshy fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of primary forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
