Lonicera acutifolia

Lonicera acutifolia, commonly known as the Japanese honeysuckle, is a deciduous vine native to East Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces fragrant, tubular flowers that range from yellow to orange in color. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to climb or trail along surfaces. Its fruit is a small, red berry that contains several seeds.

Lonicera acutifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value but can become invasive in some regions.
