Lastrea paradoxa

Lastrea paradoxa, commonly known as the paradoxical lastrea, is a unique succulent native to South Africa.

It features a cylindrical stem covered in dense, spiny tubercles that protect it from herbivores. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the warmer months. Its leaves are reduced to small, scale-like structures, typical of many arid-adapted plants.

Due to its unusual appearance and limited distribution, it is a subject of interest in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
