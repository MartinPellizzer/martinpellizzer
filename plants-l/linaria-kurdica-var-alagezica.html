Linaria kurdica var. alagezica

Linaria kurdica var. alagezica, commonly known as the Kurdish toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
