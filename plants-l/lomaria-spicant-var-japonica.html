Lomaria spicant var. japonica

Lomaria spicant var. japonica, commonly known as Japanese spirea, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing pink or white flowers that attract pollinators. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
