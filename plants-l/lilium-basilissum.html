Lilium basilissum

Lilium basilissum, commonly known as the imperial lily, is a species of flowering plant in the Liliaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. Its bulbs are used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
