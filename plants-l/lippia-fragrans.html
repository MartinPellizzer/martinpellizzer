Lippia fragrans

Lippia fragrans, commonly known as sweet verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soil and full sun. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Lippia fragrans is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
