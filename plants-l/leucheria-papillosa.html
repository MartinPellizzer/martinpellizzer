Leucheria papillosa

Leucheria papillosa, commonly known as the silky lichen, is a foliose lichen that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It has a distinctive, soft, velvety upper surface covered in tiny, branched structures called rhizines. The plant's thallus is typically pale green to yellowish in color, with a dense, layered structure. It is often found on tree trunks and rocks in temperate regions.

Leucheria papillosa plays a vital role in nutrient cycling and provides habitat for various microorganisms.
