Lobivia rossii var. bustilloensis

Lobivia rossii var. bustilloensis, commonly known as the Bustillo's Lobivia, is a compact, low-growing cactus native to the arid regions of northern Chile.

It features a short, cylindrical stem with a gray-green color and is covered in dense, white to pale blue spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It is highly drought-tolerant and requires minimal watering, making it suitable for rock gardens or arid landscapes.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
