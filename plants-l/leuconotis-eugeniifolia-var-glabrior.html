Leuconotis eugeniifolia var. glabrior

Leuconotis eugeniifolia var. glabrior, commonly known as the silky sedge, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of South America.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are smooth and glabrous, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
