Lycopus exaltatus

Lycopus exaltatus, commonly known as blueweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from June to September. Lycopus exaltatus thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

It is often used in herbal medicine for its purported calming and respiratory benefits.
