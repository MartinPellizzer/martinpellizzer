Lolium rigidum var. lepturoides

Lolium rigidum var. lepturoides, commonly known as slender ryegrass, is a cool-season grass native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in compact, dense tufts and has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil. It is often found in disturbed sites such as roadsides and agricultural fields.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it is both a valuable forage crop and a potential weed in cultivated areas.
