Ligustrum ovalifolium multiflorum

Ligustrum ovalifolium multiflorum, commonly known as the evergreen privet, is a dense, shrub-like plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy, providing a lush appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by dark blue berries that are attractive to birds. It is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and ability to form thick hedges.

Botanically, it belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic foliage and adaptability to various soil conditions.
