Lisianthius meianthus

Lisianthius meianthus, commonly known as the small-flowered lisianthus, is a member of the Campanulaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
