Linanthus grandiflorus

Linanthus grandiflorus, commonly known as the largeflower larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are lance-shaped and serrated.

Linanthus grandiflorus is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
