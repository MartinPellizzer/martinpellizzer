Lycium oxycarpum

Lycium oxycarpum, commonly known as the Chinese boxthorn, is a shrub native to China and neighboring regions.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces red berries that are edible but have a tart flavor. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky soils.

Lycium oxycarpum is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional medicinal uses in Chinese herbal medicine.
