Lorenzanea ira

Lorenzanea ira, commonly known as the silverleaf lichen, is a unique symbiotic organism composed of a fungus and a cyanobacterium.

It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocks or soil surfaces. The plant's structure consists of a dense, crust-like thallus that reflects light, giving it a silvery appearance. It plays a vital role in soil stabilization and nutrient cycling in its ecosystem.

Due to its specialized habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and sensitive indicator of environmental health.
