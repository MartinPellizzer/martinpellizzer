Lupinus barbiger

Lupinus barbiger, commonly known as the bearded lupine, is a flowering plant native to western North America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of pea-like blue or purple flowers. The plant has feathery, compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Lupinus barbiger is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through its root nodules.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
