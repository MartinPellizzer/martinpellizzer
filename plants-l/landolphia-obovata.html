Landolphia obovata

Landolphia obovata, commonly known as the African buttercup, is a climbing plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces a distinctive, fleshy fruit that contains numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
