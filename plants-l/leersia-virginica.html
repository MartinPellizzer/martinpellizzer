Leersia virginica

Leersia virginica, commonly known as common leersia, is a species of grass native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swales, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny white or greenish flowers. It is often found in moist soils and can thrive in partial to full shade.

Leersia virginica plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various insects and wildlife.
