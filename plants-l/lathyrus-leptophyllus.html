Lathyrus leptophyllus

Lathyrus leptophyllus, commonly known as the slender-leaf pea, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its small, pea-like flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces slender, elongated pods containing small, edible seeds.

Lathyrus leptophyllus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
