Limnosipanea palustris

Limnosipanea palustris, commonly known as the marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It typically grows in shallow, waterlogged soils and is often found in marshes and swampy areas. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikes. Its botanical features are adapted to tolerate prolonged periods of flooding.

Limnosipanea palustris plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
