Lepanthes argentata

Lepanthes argentata, commonly known as the silver orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking white to silver-colored flowers, which often have a metallic sheen. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as cloud forests, where it thrives in the understory. Its leaves are usually broad and lance-shaped, and it produces small, delicate blooms that are highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts.

Due to its beauty and rarity, Lepanthes argentata is often cultivated in specialized horticultural settings.
