Lycium salsum var. patagonicum

Lycium salsum var. patagonicum, commonly known as the Patagonian boxthorn, is a shrub native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible and often consumed by local wildlife. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local flora in Patagonia.
