Litsea maclurei

Litsea maclurei, commonly known as Maclure's litsea, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to the Pacific coastal regions of the United States.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, elliptical leaves that are often glossy and dark green. Its small white flowers are typically clustered in axillary inflorescences, and it produces small, round berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Litsea maclurei thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal woodlands and mixed evergreen forests.
