Lactuca bracteata

Lactuca bracteata, commonly known as the bracted lettuce, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and a single, upright flowering stalk that bears yellow flowers. The plant is native to regions of Europe and Asia and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are typically smooth and succulent, with a mild, slightly bitter taste.

Lactuca bracteata is often found in disturbed soils and can be cultivated as a leafy vegetable in some regions.
