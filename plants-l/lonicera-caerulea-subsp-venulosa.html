Lonicera caerulea subsp. venulosa

Lonicera caerulea subsp.

venulosa, commonly known as the sweet birch or red honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its fragrant, red to orange flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant produces small, edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a sweet, tart flavor. Its leaves are opposite and oval-shaped, with a smooth texture and a slightly serrated edge.

This subspecies is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and for its culinary uses in traditional medicine and food.
