Lathyrus napoleonis

Lathyrus napoleonis, commonly known as Napoleon's pea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a climbing legume that produces small, pea-like flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces elongated pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
