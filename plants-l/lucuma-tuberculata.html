Lucuma tuberculata

Lucuma tuberculata, commonly known as the Peruvian chestnut or lucuma, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a member of the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its large, leathery leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces a fruit that is oval-shaped and has a sweet, nutty flavor, often used in culinary applications. Its fruit contains a single large seed enclosed in a fleshy pulp.

Lucuma tuberculata is valued not only for its edible fruit but also for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
