Lobivia potosina

Lobivia potosina, commonly known as the Potosi lobivia, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and thick, ribbed stems. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which bloom at the ends of the stems. Lobivia potosina is well adapted to drought conditions, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in sandy, well-drained soils.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
