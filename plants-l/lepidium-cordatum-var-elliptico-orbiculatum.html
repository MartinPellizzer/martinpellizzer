Lepidium cordatum var. elliptico-orbiculatum

Lepidium cordatum var. elliptico-orbiculatum, commonly known as the broad-leafed wallflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its elliptical to orbicular leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions. It has a compact growth habit and is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
