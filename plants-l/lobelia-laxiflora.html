Lobelia laxiflora

Lobelia laxiflora, commonly known as the slenderflower lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features delicate, tubular blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors. It is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks.

Lobelia laxiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
