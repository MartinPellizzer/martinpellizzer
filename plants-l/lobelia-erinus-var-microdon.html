Lobelia erinus var. microdon

Lobelia erinus var. microdon, commonly known as the dwarf lobelia, is a compact, trailing plant native to South America.

It features small, tubular flowers that are typically blue, purple, or white, arranged in dense clusters. The plant has fine, hairy foliage and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun to partial shade. It is often used in hanging baskets and containers due to its cascading growth habit.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to bloom continuously throughout the growing season.
