Lavoisiera pulcherrima var. major

Lavoisiera pulcherrima var. major, commonly known as the large beauty lavoisiera, is a striking member of the Araliaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, palmately compound leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter, giving it a dramatic visual presence. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that are followed by bright red berries. Native to tropical regions of South America, it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens in warm climates.
