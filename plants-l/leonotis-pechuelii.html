Leonotis pechuelii

Leonotis pechuelii, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescences that range in color from red to orange. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically visited by bees and other pollinators. It is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance and ability to thrive in full sun.

Leonotis pechuelii is drought-tolerant and prefers well-drained soil, making it a suitable choice for xeriscaping.
