Lippia lacunosa var. acutifolia

Lippia lacunosa var. acutifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved lippia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow, and pointed leaves, which are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ornamental gardening and traditional herbal uses.
