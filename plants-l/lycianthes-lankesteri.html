Lycianthes lankesteri

Lycianthes lankesteri, commonly known as the Mexican beautyberry, is a tropical shrub native to Central America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces showy, violet-blue berries that are typically eaten by birds, aiding in seed dispersal. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture, while its flowers are usually white or pale purple.

Lycianthes lankesteri is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
