Libanotis condensata subsp. arctica

Libanotis condensata subsp.

arctica, commonly known as Arctic bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions. It is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in cold, harsh environments. It has a low-growing habit with hairy leaves that help insulate it from extreme temperatures.

This subspecies is adapted to long periods of snow cover and has a short growing season.
