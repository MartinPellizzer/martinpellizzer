Lonicera macrantha var. biflora

Lonicera macrantha var. biflora, commonly known as the twin-flowered honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, simple, ovate leaves that are often hairy beneath. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, with two prominent lobes. It blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The fruit is a small, red, fleshy drupe that ripens in late summer.
