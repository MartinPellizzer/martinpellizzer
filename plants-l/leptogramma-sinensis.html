Leptogramma sinensis

Leptogramma sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to China.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a finely divided structure, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Leptogramma sinensis is valued both for its aesthetic qualities and its ecological role in forest ecosystems.
