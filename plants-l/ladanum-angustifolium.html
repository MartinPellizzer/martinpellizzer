Ladanum angustifolium

Ladanum angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ladanum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in temperate to alpine climates. Its blooming period occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Ladanum angustifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
