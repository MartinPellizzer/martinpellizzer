Lagenaria sagittata

Lagenaria sagittata, commonly known as the bottle gourd, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It produces large, hollow fruits that can be used as containers, and its leaves are heart-shaped, contributing to its ornamental value. The plant thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil and requires full sun for optimal growth. Its flowers are typically yellow and attract pollinators such as bees.

The fruit's versatility in both culinary and practical applications makes it a valuable plant in many cultures.
