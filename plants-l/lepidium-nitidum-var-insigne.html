Lepidium nitidum var. insigne

Lepidium nitidum var. insigne, commonly known as the silverleaf whitlowgrass, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. Its leaves are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The species is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.
