Linum hirsutum var. anatolicum

Linum hirsutum var. anatolicum, commonly known as Anatolian flax, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces blue or purple flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in textile production.
