Linum macraei var. macraei

Linum macraei var. macraei, commonly known as the Macrae's flax, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and delicate, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and prefers full sun exposure. Its leaves are linear and slightly hairy, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Linum macraei var. macraei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
