Loranthus ogowensis

Loranthus ogowensis, commonly known as the Ogowensis mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that attaches itself to the branches of its host trees.

It belongs to the Loranthaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa. The plant has a hemiparasitic lifestyle, meaning it derives some of its nutrients from the host while also performing photosynthesis. Its small, glossy leaves are often clustered near the base of the stems, and it produces small, white or yellowish flowers.

The fruit is a capsule that releases sticky seeds, which adhere to the bark of trees, facilitating its spread.
