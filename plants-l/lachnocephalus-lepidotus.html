Lachnocephalus lepidotus

Lachnocephalus lepidotus, commonly known as the purple ant plant, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to New Zealand and is characterized by its striking purple and red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a short, stocky stem with dense clusters of small, spiny leaves. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant colors and unique foliage.

However, it is toxic and should be handled with care.
