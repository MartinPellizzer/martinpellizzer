Lycium parishii var. parishii

Lycium parishii var. parishii, commonly known as the Parish's boxthorn, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features small, spiny leaves and produces yellow-orange berries that are edible for both humans and animals. The plant is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it resilient in harsh environments. It is often found in rocky soils and along coastal areas.

Its ornamental value and hardy nature make it a popular choice for landscaping in dry climates.
