Lepanthes sarcopetala

Lepanthes sarcopetala, commonly known as the flesh-colored orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often pale or reddish flowers that resemble flesh, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in the understory of tropical rainforests. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with intricate petal structures that contribute to its ornamental appeal.

Lepanthes sarcopetala is valued in horticulture for its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
