Lycium martii

Lycium martii, commonly known as the Chinese wolfberry or goji berry, is a shrub native to arid regions of China, Mongolia, and Tibet.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that are rich in antioxidants and nutrients, making them popular in traditional medicine and cuisine. Lycium martii thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in desert or semi-desert environments.

Its ecological importance includes providing food for wildlife and stabilizing soil in harsh climates.
