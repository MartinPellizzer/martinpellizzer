Luzula confusa var. subspicata

Luzula confusa var. subspicata, commonly known as the slender woodrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices.

Luzula confusa var. subspicata is often found in temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.
