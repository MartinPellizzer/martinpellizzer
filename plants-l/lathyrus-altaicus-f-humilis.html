Lathyrus altaicus f. humilis

Lathyrus altaicus f. humilis, commonly known as the low larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, creeping stems and small, clustered blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being ovate to lanceolate in shape.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
