Lasianthus xanthospermus

Lasianthus xanthospermus, commonly known as the goldenseed lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
