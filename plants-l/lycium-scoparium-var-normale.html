Lycium scoparium var. normale

Lycium scoparium var. normale, commonly known as the common wolfberry, is a shrub native to parts of Asia, particularly China.

It is characterized by its small, red berries that are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source. The plant has woody stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

The berries are rich in antioxidants and have been used for their purported health benefits for centuries.
