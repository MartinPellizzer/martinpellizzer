Lonicera implexa var. valentina

Lonicera implexa var. valentina, commonly known as the Valentine honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring, typically in shades of yellow and orange. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

The berries it produces are edible but generally not favored by humans.
