Lupinus mexiae

Lupinus mexiae, commonly known as Mexican lupine, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, where it typically grows in dry, rocky soils. The plant features erect stems with palmately compound leaves and produces spikes of blue to purple flowers. Lupinus mexiae is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it ecologically significant in arid environments.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
