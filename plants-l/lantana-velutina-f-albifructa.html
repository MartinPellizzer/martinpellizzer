Lantana velutina f. albifructa

Lantana velutina f. albifructa, commonly known as the white-fruited lantana, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that typically range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are usually white or yellow when mature. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

However, it can be invasive in some areas, posing a threat to native plant species.
