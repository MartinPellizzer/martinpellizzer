Linanthus campanulatus

Linanthus campanulatus, commonly known as the bellflower linanthus, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has hairy stems and leaves. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Linanthus campanulatus is often found in open woodlands and along streambanks, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
