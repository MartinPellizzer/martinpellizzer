Luzula elata

Luzula elata, commonly known as the tall wood sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Luzula elata produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense spikes.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
