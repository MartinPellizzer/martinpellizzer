Linaria salzmannii var. violacea

Linaria salzmannii var. violacea, commonly known as Salzmann's toadflax, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of violet to purple flowers. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
