Lewisia crenulata

Lewisia crenulata, commonly known as the Sierra snowplant, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Montiaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers. The flowers, which range in color from white to pink, are adapted for pollination by insects such as bees and butterflies.
