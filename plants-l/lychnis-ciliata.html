Lychnis ciliata

Lychnis ciliata, commonly known as the pink campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of pink or red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits called capsules, which contain numerous tiny seeds. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges, and the stems are often hairy.

Lychnis ciliata is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal.
