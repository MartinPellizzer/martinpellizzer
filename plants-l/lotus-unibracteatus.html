Lotus unibracteatus

Lotus unibracteatus, commonly known as the single bract lotus, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking pink or red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features a robust root system and broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface. Its unique structure includes a single bract that encloses the flower bud, giving it its common name.

Lotus unibracteatus is also valued for its ecological role in aquatic environments and its traditional uses in herbal medicine.
