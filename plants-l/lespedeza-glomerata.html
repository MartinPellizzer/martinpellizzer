Lespedeza glomerata

Lespedeza glomerata, commonly known as clustered lespedeza, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and grassy areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, trifoliate, and often have a hairy texture.

Lespedeza glomerata is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment and ecological restoration projects.
