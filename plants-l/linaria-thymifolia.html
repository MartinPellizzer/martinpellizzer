Linaria thymifolia

Linaria thymifolia, commonly known as the thyme-leaved toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of bright yellow, spurred flowers. The leaves are narrow and often covered in a fine, fuzzy coating, resembling the texture of thyme. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
