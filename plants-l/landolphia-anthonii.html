Landolphia anthonii

Landolphia anthonii, commonly known as the African morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically bright yellow or orange, opening in the morning and closing at dusk. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and can grow up to several meters in length. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

The fruit is a capsule that contains numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.
