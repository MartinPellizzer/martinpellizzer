Lolium osiridis

Lolium osiridis, commonly known as the sacred grass of ancient Egypt, is a perennial grass species characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. The plant is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, though it can tolerate partial shade. Its historical significance is linked to its use in religious rituals and as a symbol of resurrection and renewal.

Lolium osiridis is also noted for its ornamental value and potential as a forage crop.
