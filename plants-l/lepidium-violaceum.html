Lepidium violaceum

Lepidium violaceum, commonly known as purple cress, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, violet-colored flowers and pinnately divided leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments and is often found in wet meadows or along stream banks. Its seeds are small and capsule-like, containing several tiny seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Lepidium violaceum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
