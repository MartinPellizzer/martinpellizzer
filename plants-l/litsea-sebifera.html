Litsea sebifera

Litsea sebifera, commonly known as the wax leaf litsea, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and avocado trees. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, glossy, waxy leaves that give it its common name. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Litsea sebifera is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
