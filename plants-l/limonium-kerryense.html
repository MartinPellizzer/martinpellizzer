Limonium kerryense

Limonium kerryense, commonly known as Kerry sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of Ireland.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and produces clusters of small, blue to violet flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in maritime environments. Its flowers are typically visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological role.

Limonium kerryense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
