Lycopodium clavatum var. jamaicense

Lycopodium clavatum var. jamaicense, commonly known as the Jamaican clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, cone-like structures called strobila, which contain the spores necessary for reproduction. Its leaves are typically narrow and spirally arranged, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is native to the Caribbean and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
