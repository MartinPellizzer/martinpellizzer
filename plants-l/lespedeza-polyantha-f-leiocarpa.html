Lespedeza polyantha f. leiocarpa

Lespedeza polyantha f. leiocarpa, commonly known as the white lespedeza, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or open areas. It has trifoliate leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for ecological restoration.
