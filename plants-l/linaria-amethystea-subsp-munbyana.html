Linaria amethystea subsp. munbyana

Linaria amethystea subsp.

munbyana, commonly known as the Munby's toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pale purple to white.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
