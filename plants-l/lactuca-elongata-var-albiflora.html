Lactuca elongata var. albiflora

Lactuca elongata var. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered lettuce, is a variant of the common lettuce species.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and white flowers that bloom in clusters. This plant is native to temperate regions and thrives in cool climates. It is often cultivated for its edible leaves, which have a mild, slightly bitter taste.

The white flowers are not only ornamental but also attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
