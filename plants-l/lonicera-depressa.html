Lonicera depressa

Lonicera depressa, commonly known as the creeping honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are usually hairy on the underside. Its fragrant, tubular flowers are typically yellow or orange and bloom in early summer.

Lonicera depressa is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
