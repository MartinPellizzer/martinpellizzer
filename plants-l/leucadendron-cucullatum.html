Leucadendron cucullatum

Leucadendron cucullatum, commonly known as the silver bush, is a striking evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silvery-gray foliage and vibrant, cone-like inflorescences that range in color from yellow to red. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and full sun exposure, making it well-suited for coastal gardens. Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Additionally, it plays a role in supporting local wildlife, including birds and insects.
