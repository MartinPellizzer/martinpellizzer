Lindernia purpurea

Lindernia purpurea, commonly known as purple lindernia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Verbenaceae and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant purple flowers. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically arranged in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Lindernia purpurea thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a low-maintenance, colorful ground cover.
