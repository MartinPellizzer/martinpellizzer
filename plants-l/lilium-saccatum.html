Lilium saccatum

Lilium saccatum, commonly known as the sacrum lily, is a species of bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink to red in color, with a distinctive sac-like structure at the base of the petals. The plant produces a tall, upright stem that can reach up to 1 meter in height, adorned with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its bulbs are perennial, and it blooms in late summer to early autumn.

Lilium saccatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
