Lobelia cyphia

Lobelia cyphia, commonly known as the Cyphia lobelia, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Lobelia cyphia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
