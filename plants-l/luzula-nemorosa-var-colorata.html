Luzula nemorosa var. colorata

Luzula nemorosa var. colorata, commonly known as the variegated wood rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and dense clusters of grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale green to white flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks, and is often found in nutrient-rich soils.

The variegated leaf patterns of this variety make it an attractive addition to shaded garden settings.
