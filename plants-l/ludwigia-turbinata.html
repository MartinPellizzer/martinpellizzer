Ludwigia turbinata

Ludwigia turbinata, commonly known as the turban bluemouth, is a striking aquatic plant native to South America.

It features elongated, dark green leaves with a distinctive reddish or purple coloration along the edges, which intensifies under strong lighting. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically submerged, though they may emerge slightly above the water surface. It thrives in tropical and subtropical freshwater environments, often found in slow-moving streams and ponds.

Ludwigia turbinata is popular in aquariums for its ornamental value and ability to provide habitat for aquatic life.
