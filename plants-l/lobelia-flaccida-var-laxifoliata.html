Lobelia flaccida var. laxifoliata

Lobelia flaccida var. laxifoliata, commonly known as the slenderleaf lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a suitable choice for woodland gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
