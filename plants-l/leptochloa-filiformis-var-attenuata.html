Leptochloa filiformis var. attenuata

Leptochloa filiformis var. attenuata, commonly known as the slender threadgrass, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its narrow, thread-like leaves and compact growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle with small, spikelet-like flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticultural settings.
