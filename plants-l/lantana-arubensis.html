Lantana arubensis

Lantana arubensis, commonly known as the yellow lantana, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its dense, sprawling growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Lantana arubensis is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to drought conditions.
