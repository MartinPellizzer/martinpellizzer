Leucanthemum heterophyllum

Leucanthemum heterophyllum, commonly known as the blue-eyed Mary, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy blue or purple flowers surrounded by white ray florets, with a central disk of yellow tubular flowers. Its leaves are typically alternate, lance-shaped, and have a fuzzy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or disturbed habitats.
