Lythrum sagittifolium

Lythrum sagittifolium, commonly known as rosemary weed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It is a member of the Lythraceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows in moist soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. It has a creeping growth habit with hairy stems and produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Lythrum sagittifolium is often considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its aggressive spread and ability to thrive in disturbed environments.
