Laserpitium gallicum var. platyphyllum

Laserpitium gallicum var. platyphyllum, commonly known as the broad-leaved lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features basal rosettes of broad, waxy, silver-green leaves that are deeply lobed and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces upright flowering stems topped with clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

The leaves and flowers are used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported soothing and anti-inflammatory properties.
