Lotus campylocladus subsp. hillebrandii

Lotus campylocladus subsp.

hillebrandii, commonly known as the Hillebrand's lotus, is a rare and unique plant species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its striking, large, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has a distinctive, spiraled leaf structure that gives it a visually appealing appearance. It thrives in wet, swampy habitats and is often found in association with water bodies.
