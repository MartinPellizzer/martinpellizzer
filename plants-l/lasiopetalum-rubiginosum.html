Lasiopetalum rubiginosum

Lasiopetalum rubiginosum, commonly known as the rusty petal lillypillly, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are typically narrow and leathery, with a distinctive rusty or reddish underside. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract native pollinators.
