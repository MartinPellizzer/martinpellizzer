Lactuca acanthifolia f. integrifolia

Lactuca acanthifolia f. integrifolia, commonly known as the smoothleaf prickly lettuce, is a variant of the prickly lettuce species.

It is a biennial or annual herb native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia. The plant features upright, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with smooth, entire margins. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.
