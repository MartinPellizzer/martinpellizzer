Lilium biligulatum

Lilium biligulatum, commonly known as the white lily, is a species of flowering plant in the Liliaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and Mongolia. The plant produces showy, white to pale pink flowers with six petals and a distinctive scent. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Lilium biligulatum is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been cultivated for centuries in horticulture.
