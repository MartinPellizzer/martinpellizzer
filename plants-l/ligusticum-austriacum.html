Ligusticum austriacum

Ligusticum austriacum, commonly known as Austrian lovage, is a perennial herb native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the family. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its aromatic foliage and historical use in traditional medicine highlight its botanical and cultural significance.
