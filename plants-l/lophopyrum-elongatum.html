Lophopyrum elongatum

Lophopyrum elongatum, commonly known as the slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. Lophopyrum elongatum is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
