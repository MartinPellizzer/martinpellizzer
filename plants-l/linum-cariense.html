Linum cariense

Linum cariense, commonly known as the Carpathian flax, is a flowering plant native to the Carpathian Mountains in Europe.

It belongs to the genus Linum, which includes various species of flax plants. The plant is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of blue or purple flowers. It typically grows in rocky or forested areas and prefers well-drained soils.

Linum cariense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has also been studied for its potential ecological benefits.
