Loxoscaphe theciferum var. concinnum

Loxoscaphe theciferum var. concinnum, commonly known as the concinnum thistle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and produces yellow flowers in early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments. Its foliage is covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
