Liparis letouzeyana

Liparis letouzeyana, commonly known as the Letouzey's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in humid climates.

The flowers usually bloom in spring, displaying a range of colors from white to pink, depending on the specific variety.
