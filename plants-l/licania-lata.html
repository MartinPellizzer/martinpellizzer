Licania lata

Licania lata, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Erythroxylum licania*, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible and often consumed by local communities. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded areas of the rainforest.

Licania lata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
