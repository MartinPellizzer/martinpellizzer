Licuala oxleyi

Licuala oxleyi, commonly known as the giant pandan, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, paddle-shaped leaves that can grow up to 3 meters in length. The plant is a member of the Araceae family, which includes other familiar plants like philodendrons and peace lilies. Its leaves are often used in traditional cuisine and for making baskets and thatching.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for both its ornamental and practical uses.
