Lotus hispidus subsp. levieri

Lotus hispidus subsp.

levieri, commonly known as the hairy lotus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of southern Africa. It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name.

The plant produces showy pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes and along riverbanks.

The subspecies is notable for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
