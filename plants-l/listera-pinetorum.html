Listera pinetorum

Listera pinetorum, commonly known as the pine forest violet, is a small perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and is often found beneath the canopy of pine trees. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces delicate, violet-colored flowers in late spring. It has a fibrous root system and spreads through rhizomes, forming dense colonies.

Listera pinetorum is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
