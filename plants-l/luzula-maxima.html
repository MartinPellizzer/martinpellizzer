Luzula maxima

Luzula maxima, commonly known as the giant woodrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like stems and clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are linear and often have a bluish-green hue, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Luzula maxima is valued for its ability to thrive in wet conditions and its ecological role in stabilizing soil in riparian zones.
