Loranthus ophiodes

Loranthus ophiodes, commonly known as the red mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to its host tree through a specialized structure called a haustellum.

It primarily grows on the stems and branches of various species of eucalyptus and acacia. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that are typically visited by insects for pollination. Its berries are red and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

Loranthus ophiodes plays an important ecological role by providing food for wildlife and contributing to the biodiversity of its habitat.
