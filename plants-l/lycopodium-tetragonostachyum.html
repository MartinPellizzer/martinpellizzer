Lycopodium tetragonostachyum

Lycopodium tetragonostachyum, commonly known as the four-angled clubmoss, is a species of lycophyte characterized by its distinctive, four-sided stems.

It belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae and is native to tropical regions of Asia, including India and Southeast Asia. The plant produces small, spore-bearing cones called strobili, which are typically clustered at the tips of its branches. Lycopodium tetragonostachyum thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest undergrowth or along stream banks.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
