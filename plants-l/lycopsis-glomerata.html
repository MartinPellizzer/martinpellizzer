Lycopsis glomerata

Lycopsis glomerata, commonly known as the clustered lycopus, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Lycopsis glomerata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
