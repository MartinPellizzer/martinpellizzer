Lilium linearifolianum

Lilium linearifolianum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lily, is a species of flowering plant in the Liliaceae family.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves and tall, slender stems that bear showy, fragrant flowers. Native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Asia, this plant thrives in cool, moist environments. The flowers, which typically bloom in summer, come in shades of white, pink, and red, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for cultivation in gardens with well-drained soil and partial shade.
