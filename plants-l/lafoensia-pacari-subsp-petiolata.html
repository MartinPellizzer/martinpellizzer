Lafoensia pacari subsp. petiolata

Lafoensia pacari subsp.

petiolata, commonly known as the Brazilian hibiscus, is a shrub native to the Amazon rainforest. It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and red.

The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural use in warm regions.
