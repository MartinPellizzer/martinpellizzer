Liabum platylepis

Liabum platylepis, commonly known as the broad-leaved liabum, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, tropical environments and is often found in forest understories. Its seeds are enclosed in pods, a common feature among leguminous plants.
