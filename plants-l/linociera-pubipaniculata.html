Linociera pubipaniculata

Linociera pubipaniculata, commonly known as the hairy panicled mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, hairy glands, which help in absorbing nutrients from the host. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers arranged in panicles, which are clustered inflorescences. Its seeds are dispersed by birds, which eat the fleshy berries.

Linociera pubipaniculata is native to tropical regions of South America and is often found in humid, shaded forest environments.
