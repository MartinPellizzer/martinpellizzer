Lilium pardalinum subsp. shastense

Lilium pardalinum subsp.

shastense, commonly known as the Shasta lily, is a species native to the western United States. It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and large, showy white flowers with a distinctive red or orange throat.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a fan-like pattern.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
