Lupinus parodianus

Lupinus parodianus, commonly known as Parod's lupine, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy blue to violet flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to grow in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas.

Lupinus parodianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
