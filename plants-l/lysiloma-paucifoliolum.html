Lysiloma paucifoliolum

Lysiloma paucifoliolum, commonly known as the sparse-leaved lythrum, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Lythraceae family, which includes other species such as the common lythrum. The plant is characterized by its slender stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are typically pink or red, forming clusters that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Lysiloma paucifoliolum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
