Larnax hunzikeriana

Larnax hunzikeriana, commonly known as the Zambian lily, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy flowers that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant typically blooms in summer, producing clusters of bright pink or red petals that are highly attractive to pollinators. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy texture, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections worldwide.
