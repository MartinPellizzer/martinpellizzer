Lycopus uniflorus var. ovatus

Lycopus uniflorus var. ovatus, commonly known as narrow-leaved blueweed, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It typically grows in moist habitats such as wet meadows, stream banks, and marshy areas. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually less than 2 inches long. Its flowers are small, blue to purple, and clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences.

It is often found in the eastern United States and has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties.
