Linnaea borealis f. rhodoides

Linnaea borealis f. rhodoides, commonly known as the red-flowered twinflower, is a rare and striking variant of the twinflower plant.

It is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to northern regions of North America and Eurasia. The plant features small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pink in the standard form, but in this form, they exhibit a distinctive reddish hue. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a leathery texture.

This unique form is characterized by its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unusual coloration.
