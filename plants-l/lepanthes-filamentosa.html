Lepanthes filamentosa

Lepanthes filamentosa, commonly known as the filament orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, linear leaves and intricate, fragrant flowers that often display a mix of white and purple hues. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as rainforests, where it attaches itself to trees or rocks. Its flowers are notable for their slender, thread-like petals, which give the species its common name.

Lepanthes filamentosa is appreciated by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and unique floral structure.
