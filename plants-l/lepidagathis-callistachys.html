Lepidagathis callistachys

Lepidagathis callistachys, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are usually pink to red in color, with five petals and a prominent central column. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
