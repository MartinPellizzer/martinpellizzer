Laurus pucheri

Laurus pucheri, commonly known as the Mediterranean laurel, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features broad, dark green leaves that are glossy and have a subtle aromatic scent. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for Mediterranean climates.

Due to its ornamental value and fragrance, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a hedge.
