Lobelia nannae

Lobelia nannae, commonly known as the mountain lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, delicate blue or purple flowers and hairy, narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers are often clustered in dense inflorescences, and it is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Lobelia nannae plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
