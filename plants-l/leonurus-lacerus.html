Leonurus lacerus

Leonurus lacerus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hedgehog plant, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant features small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in whorls along the stems. Its leaves are narrow, serrated, and often covered in fine hairs.

It is often grown as an ornamental plant for its unique appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
