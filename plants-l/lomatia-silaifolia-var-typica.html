Lomatia silaifolia var. typica

Lomatia silaifolia var. typica, commonly known as the silky lomatia, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its soft, silvery foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine, silky hairs.

This species is well adapted to sandy soils and coastal conditions, making it a resilient plant in its native habitat.
