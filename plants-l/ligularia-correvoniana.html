Ligularia correvoniana

Ligularia correvoniana, commonly known as the African arrowhead, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive arrowhead shape, often with a waxy surface and dark green coloration. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in a spike, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found near streams or in damp woodlands.

Its foliage is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
