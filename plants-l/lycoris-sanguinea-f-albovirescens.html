Lycoris sanguinea f. albovirescens

Lycoris sanguinea f. albovirescens, commonly known as the White-flowered Red Lily, is a striking variation of the red lily known for its unique floral characteristics.

This plant belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is native to regions of East Asia, including China and Japan. Its flowers typically bloom in late summer, featuring a combination of white petals and red stamens that create a visually appealing contrast. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its foliage consists of narrow, strap-like leaves that emerge in early spring, adding to its aesthetic value throughout the growing season.
