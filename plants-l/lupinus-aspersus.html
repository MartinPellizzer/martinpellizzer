Lupinus aspersus

Lupinus aspersus, commonly known as the hairy lupin, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and pinnately compound leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces showy, pea-like flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Lupinus aspersus has a deep taproot system, allowing it to thrive in well-drained, sandy soils.

It is also known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
