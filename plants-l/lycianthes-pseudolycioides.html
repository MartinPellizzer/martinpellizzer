Lycianthes pseudolycioides

Lycianthes pseudolycioides, commonly known as the Mexican nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and peppers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring hairy stems and oval, dark green leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are usually yellow or white, and it produces small, rounded berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

The plant is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
