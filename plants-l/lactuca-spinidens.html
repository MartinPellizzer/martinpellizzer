Lactuca spinidens

Lactuca spinidens, commonly known as the prickly lettuce, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and a distinctive, hairy stem. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Lactuca spinidens is also an annual plant that thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in various habitats such as fields and roadsides.
