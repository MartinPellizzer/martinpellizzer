Libanotis cycloloba

Libanotis cycloloba, commonly known as the mountain daffodil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers during the spring season.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and protected areas.
