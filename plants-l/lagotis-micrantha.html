Lagotis micrantha

Lagotis micrantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soils. Lagotis micrantha is often found in grasslands and rocky outcrops, where it contributes to local biodiversity.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for both ecological and horticultural interest.
