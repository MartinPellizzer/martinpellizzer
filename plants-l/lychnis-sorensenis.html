Lychnis sorensenis

Lychnis sorensenis, commonly known as the Sorensen's catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes other flowering plants such as carnations and snapdragons. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are pink or red, with five petals and a distinctive appearance that resembles a small, delicate star.

It is known for its short growing season and is often found in disturbed or sunny habitats.
