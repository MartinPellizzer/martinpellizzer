Linaria triphylla var. neglecta

Linaria triphylla var. neglecta, commonly known as the three-leaved toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often trifoliate, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

This variety is often found in rocky areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
