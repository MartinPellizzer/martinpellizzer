Loranthus preissii

Loranthus preissii, commonly known as the mistletoe of Australia, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of eucalyptus and other host trees.

It lacks true roots and absorbs water and nutrients through its stems, which are embedded into the host's bark. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers and red berries that are important for local wildlife. It is often found in coastal and temperate regions of Australia.

Its parasitic nature and unique ecological role make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
