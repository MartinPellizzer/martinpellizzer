Lindsaea crenulata

Lindsaea crenulata, commonly known as the serrate lindsaea, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, where it typically grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant features a rhizomatous growth habit with erect, branching fronds that are often serrated along the edges. Its leaves are usually leathery and have a distinctive, intricate venation pattern.

Lindsaea crenulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
