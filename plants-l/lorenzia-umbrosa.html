Lorenzia umbrosa

Lorenzia umbrosa, commonly known as the shadow lorenzia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often found in the understory of dense forests. Its flowers bloom throughout the rainy season, attracting various pollinators such as bees and moths.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
