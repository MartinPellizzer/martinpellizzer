Lavandula officinalis var. delphinensis

Lavandula officinalis var. delphinensis, commonly known as the Delphinensis lavender, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and fragrant, spike-like inflorescences of purple to blue-lilac flowers. The leaves are narrow, linear, and covered in fine, silvery hairs, contributing to its aromatic properties. This variety is prized for its essential oils, which are used in aromatherapy, perfumery, and medicinal applications.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
