Lappa × ritschliana

Lappa × ritschliana, commonly known as the giant hogweed, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Lappa sativa and Lappa ritschliana.

It is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its large, umbrella-shaped flowers and tall, hollow stems. The plant can grow up to 5 meters in height, making it one of the tallest members of its family. Its leaves are broad, lobed, and can reach lengths of up to 2 meters.

The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been introduced to other regions, where it can become invasive.
