Lavigeria salutaris

Lavigeria salutaris, commonly known as the sacred water plant, is a unique aquatic plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its long, ribbon-like leaves that float on the water's surface, often forming dense mats. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its botanical features include small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom underwater, contributing to its ecological role in aquatic ecosystems.

Lavigeria salutaris is also noted for its cultural significance in traditional medicine and rituals.
