Lathyrus nigrivalvis f. puberulus

Lathyrus nigrivalvis f. puberulus, commonly known as the pubescent black-valved vetch, is a variant of the Lathyrus nigrivalvis species, characterized by its hairy foliage.

This perennial legume belongs to the Fabaceae family and is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep purple, followed by elongated, bean-like pods.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for ecological and horticultural study.
