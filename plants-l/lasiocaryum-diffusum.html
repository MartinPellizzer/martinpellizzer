Lasiocaryum diffusum

Lasiocaryum diffusum, commonly known as the hairy podded tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stem and dense, hairy foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
