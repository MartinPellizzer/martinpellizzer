Lycopus integrifolius

Lycopus integrifolius, commonly known as blueweed or water horehound, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, serrated leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist environments such as wet meadows, stream banks, and marshes.

Lycopus integrifolius is often found in the eastern and central United States, where it plays a role in supporting local biodiversity.
