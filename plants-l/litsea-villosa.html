Litsea villosa

Litsea villosa, commonly known as hairy litsea, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries such as China, India, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by oval, red or orange berries.

Litsea villosa is often found in moist, shaded areas of forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
