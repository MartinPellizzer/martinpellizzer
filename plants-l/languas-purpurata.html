Languas purpurata

Languas purpurata, commonly known as the purple tamarisk, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to deep purple. The plant produces small, winged fruits that aid in wind dispersal. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
