Loranthus nyikensis

Loranthus nyikensis, commonly known as the African mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily in the savanna regions of Africa.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while still performing some photosynthesis using its small leaves. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are often visited by insects for pollination. Its berries, which contain sticky seeds, are dispersed by birds that eat the fruit.

This species plays an important ecological role in its habitat by influencing host tree growth and providing food for wildlife.
