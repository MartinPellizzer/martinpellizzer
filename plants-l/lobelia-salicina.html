Lobelia salicina

Lobelia salicina, commonly known as the slender lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces striking blue to purple tubular flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Lobelia salicina is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
