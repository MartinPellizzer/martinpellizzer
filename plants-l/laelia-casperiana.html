Laelia casperiana

Laelia casperiana, commonly known as the Casper's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows at high altitudes, often in rocky or forested areas, and prefers cool, humid conditions. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a distinctive yellowish throat, and it has a short-lived bloom period.

Laelia casperiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
