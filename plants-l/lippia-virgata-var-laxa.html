Lippia virgata var. laxa

Lippia virgata var. laxa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lippia, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It features aromatic, narrow leaves and small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is often found in open woodlands, grasslands, and along riverbanks, thriving in well-drained soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
