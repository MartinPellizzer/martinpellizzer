Ligusticum govanianum

Ligusticum govanianum, commonly known as the Indian hemlock, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated margins, and it produces small white to greenish flowers in clustered umbels.

The roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported therapeutic properties.
