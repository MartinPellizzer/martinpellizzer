Laurus nitida

Laurus nitida, commonly known as bay laurel or sweet bay, is a evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green, aromatic leaves that are widely used in cooking and traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible but less commonly consumed. Laurus nitida thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value and historical significance make it a popular choice in gardens and landscapes.
