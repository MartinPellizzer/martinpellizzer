Lupinus confertus var. wrightii

Lupinus confertus var. wrightii, commonly known as Wright's lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of pea-shaped, violet-blue flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

As a member of the legume family, it has the ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, benefiting soil fertility.
