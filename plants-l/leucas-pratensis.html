Leucas pratensis

Leucas pratensis, commonly known as the meadow leucas, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, thriving in temperate climates. Its small, white or pale blue flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
