Lagochilus gypsaceus

Lagochilus gypsaceus, commonly known as the gypsum rockhyacinth, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its narrow, gray-green leaves and spikes of blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found growing on rocky or gypsum-rich substrates.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in dry gardens and rockeries.
