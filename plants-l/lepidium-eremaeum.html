Lepidium eremaeum

Lepidium eremaeum, commonly known as the desert cress, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant is characterized by its small, clustered white or yellow flowers and its ability to thrive in harsh, dry environments. Its leaves are typically narrow and succulent, adapted to conserve water.

Lepidium eremaeum is often found in rocky soils and can serve as a food source for certain desert animals.
