Lathyrus phaselitanus

Lathyrus phaselitanus, commonly known as the Greek vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, followed by lens-shaped pods containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
