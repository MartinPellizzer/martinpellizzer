Leptocarpus membranaceus

Leptocarpus membranaceus, commonly known as the paperleaf daisy, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine by Indigenous communities.
