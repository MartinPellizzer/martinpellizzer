Languas flabellata var. major

Languas flabellata var. major, commonly known as the broad-leafed fan fern, is a species native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped fronds that can reach up to 2 meters in length, giving it a dramatic appearance. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread and form dense clumps. Its leaves are typically bright green and slightly glossy, with a soft texture.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often used as an ornamental plant in tropical gardens.
