Lophochloa villosa

Lophochloa villosa, commonly known as the hairy lemongrass, is a perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fibrous roots, slender cylindrical stems, and dense foliage with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, greenish flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Lophochloa villosa is valued for its aromatic leaves, which are used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.
