Leuconymphaea lasiophylla

Leuconymphaea lasiophylla, commonly known as the velvetleaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers, which bloom in clusters, are often pink or purple and attract various pollinators.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts for its unique appearance and ecological significance.
