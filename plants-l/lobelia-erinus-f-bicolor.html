Lobelia erinus f. bicolor

Lobelia erinus f. bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored dwarf lantana, is a compact, flowering plant native to South America.

It features small, tubular flowers that are typically pink and white, creating a striking bicolor effect. The plant is often cultivated as a bedding plant due to its ornamental value and ability to thrive in various conditions. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Its foliage is broad and dark green, adding contrast to its colorful blooms.
