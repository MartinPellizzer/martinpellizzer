Lyonia obtusa var. longipes

Lyonia obtusa var. longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed leatherleaf, is a variant of the evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and compact growth habit. The leaves are typically elliptical, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. It produces small, fragrant flowers in spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible for birds.

This plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often used in horticultural settings for its ornamental value.
