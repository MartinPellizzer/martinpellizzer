Ligustrum vulgare var. macrophyllum

Ligustrum vulgare var. macrophyllum, commonly known as the large-leaved privet, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, oval-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters, which are followed by black berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a common ornamental plant in landscaping.
