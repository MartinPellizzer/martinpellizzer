Lupinus weberbaueri

Lupinus weberbaueri, commonly known as the Weberbauer lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of blue or purple pea-shaped flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds.

Lupinus weberbaueri is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
