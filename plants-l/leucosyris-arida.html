Leucosyris arida

Leucosyris arida, commonly known as the desert leucosyris, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a small shrub that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters, featuring narrow, gray-green leaves adapted to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the cooler months. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Leucosyris arida is well-suited to harsh environments and plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils in its native habitat.
