Lotus glaucus

Lotus glaucus, commonly known as the blue lotus, is a flowering plant native to southern and eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple petals and a yellow center, which bloom in early summer. The plant thrives in wetland environments and is often found near rivers and lakes. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, typically floating on the water's surface.

Lotus glaucus is also valued for its ornamental beauty and has cultural significance in various regions.
