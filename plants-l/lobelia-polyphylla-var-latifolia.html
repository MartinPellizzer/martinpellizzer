Lobelia polyphylla var. latifolia

Lobelia polyphylla var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are broad and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
