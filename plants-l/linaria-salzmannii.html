Linaria salzmannii

Linaria salzmannii, commonly known as Salzmann's toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and vibrant yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Linaria salzmannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
