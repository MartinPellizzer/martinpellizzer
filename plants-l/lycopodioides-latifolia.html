Lycopodioides latifolia

Lycopodioides latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf clubmoss, is a member of the Lycopodiaceae family and is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and creeping growth habit.

It typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices, where it forms dense, low-growing mats. The plant produces spores in specialized structures called sporangia, which are clustered in strobila, a feature typical of many primitive vascular plants. Its root system is fibrous and helps anchor it to the substrate while absorbing nutrients from the soil.

Lycopodioides latifolia plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
