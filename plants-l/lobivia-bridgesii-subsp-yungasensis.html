Lobivia bridgesii subsp. yungasensis

Lobivia bridgesii subsp.

yungasensis, commonly known as the Yungas lobivia, is a succulent plant native to the Andean regions of Bolivia and Argentina. It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes, often in semi-shade conditions. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by cactus enthusiasts.
