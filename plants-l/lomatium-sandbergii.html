Lomatium sandbergii

Lomatium sandbergii, commonly known as Sandberg's lomatium, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to the carrot family (Apiaceae) and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and forest edges. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional herbal medicine.

It plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for small wildlife.
