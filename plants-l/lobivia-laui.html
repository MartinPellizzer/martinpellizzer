Lobivia laui

Lobivia laui, commonly known as the Laui's lobivia, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded shape and short, cylindrical stems that are typically ribbed. The plant produces small, vibrant flowers, often in shades of yellow or orange, during the warmer months. Lobivia laui is well adapted to harsh desert conditions, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

It is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and aesthetic appeal.
