Linanthus ouellatus

Linanthus ouellatus, commonly known as the desert flax, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Camelineae tribe within the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard plants. The plant is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers and linear leaves that are adapted to arid environments. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in desert scrub habitats.

Linanthus ouellatus plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is of interest in ecological and botanical studies.
