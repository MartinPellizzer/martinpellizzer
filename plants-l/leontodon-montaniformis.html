Leontodon montaniformis

Leontodon montaniformis, commonly known as the mountain leontodon, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer, adding a splash of color to high-altitude environments.

Leontodon montaniformis is also known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in harsh, cold climates.
