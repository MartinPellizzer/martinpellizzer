Latania rubra

Latania rubra, commonly known as the red latania, is a striking member of the Arecaceae family, native to the tropical regions of the Indo-Pacific.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of long, arching leaves that give it a graceful appearance. The plant produces large, spadix-like inflorescences that are typically yellow or orange in color, which are followed by dark purple to black fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and as ornamental foliage in landscaping.

Latania rubra thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
