Leptolobium aurantiacum

Leptolobium aurantiacum, commonly known as the orange-flowered billygoatweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, orange-red flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its fruit is a legume pod that splits open to release seeds, and it is often found in disturbed soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
