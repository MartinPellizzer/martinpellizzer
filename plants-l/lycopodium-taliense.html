Lycopodium taliense

Lycopodium taliense, commonly known as the Taliense clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems that bear small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces sporophylls that enclose the sporangia, which release spores for reproduction. Lycopodium taliense is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or rocky areas.

Its ancient lineage dates back to the Paleozoic era, making it a living fossil with significant ecological and evolutionary importance.
