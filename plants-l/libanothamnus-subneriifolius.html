Libanothamnus subneriifolius

Libanothamnus subneriifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved libanothamnus, is a species native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its gray-green, narrow, and leathery leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers during the summer months, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to thrive in poor, sandy soils.

This plant is often found in dry steppe and desert environments, showcasing its resilience to harsh climatic conditions.
