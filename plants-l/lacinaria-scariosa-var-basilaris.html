Lacinaria scariosa var. basilaris

Lacinaria scariosa var. basilaris, commonly known as the basilar lacinaria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of southern Africa.

It features slender, erect stems that are often hairy, with alternate leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape. The flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences, typically displaying a pale yellow to greenish hue. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland or rocky habitats.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its delicate floral display.
