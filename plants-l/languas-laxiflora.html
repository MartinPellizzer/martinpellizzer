Languas laxiflora

Languas laxiflora, commonly known as the soft-flowered bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, erect stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a soft, fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or gravelly habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
