Limosella drummondii

Limosella drummondii, commonly known as Drummond's sandflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing, annual herb that typically reaches heights of less than 10 centimeters. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to drought conditions and high sunlight exposure.
