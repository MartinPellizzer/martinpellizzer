Lepanthes stenoscleros

Lepanthes stenoscleros, commonly known as the narrow-leaf orchid, is a species native to Central America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure and compact growth habit.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
