Lithospermum ochroleucum

Lithospermum ochroleucum, commonly known as the white-stemmed rock pepper, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, hairy stems that often grow along the ground, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. Its roots contain compounds that have been studied for potential medicinal properties.

Lithospermum ochroleucum thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.
