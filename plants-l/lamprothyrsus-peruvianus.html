Lamprothyrsus peruvianus

Lamprothyrsus peruvianus, commonly known as the Peruvian candleplant, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly Peru and Bolivia. The plant is characterized by its striking, candle-like inflorescences that are often brightly colored, ranging from red to orange. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

The leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its unique appearance.
