Linaria purpurascens

Linaria purpurascens, commonly known as the purple toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of yellow to orange. Its leaves are linear and slightly hairy, often arranged in opposite pairs along the stem.

Linaria purpurascens is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
