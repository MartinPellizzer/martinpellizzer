Ludovia lancifolia

Ludovia lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ludovia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, with a subtle fragrance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, Ludovia lancifolia is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
