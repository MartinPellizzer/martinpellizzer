Lathyrus speciosus

Lathyrus speciosus, commonly known as the showy larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its erect, branched stems and showy, spurred flowers. The plant produces small, pea-like pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Lathyrus speciosus typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and is also studied for its potential ecological roles.
