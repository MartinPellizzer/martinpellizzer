Lellingeria organensis

Lellingeria organensis, commonly known as the Organ Mountains desert parsley, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, silvery-green leaves and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is adapted to harsh desert conditions, with a deep root system that allows it to access water from deeper soil layers. It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing nectar for pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in some regions.
