Loasa ambrosiifolia var. hispida

Loasa ambrosiifolia var. hispida, commonly known as the hispid loasa, is a species native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a shrub characterized by its erect growth habit and hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, with serrated margins and a pubescent surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink, blooming in spring.

It thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found in moist, shaded forest understories.
