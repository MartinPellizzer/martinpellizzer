Lonicera mochidzukiana var. mochidzukiana

Lonicera mochidzukiana var. mochidzukiana, commonly known as the Japanese honeysuckle, is a deciduous vine native to East Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that ripens in the fall.

This species is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
