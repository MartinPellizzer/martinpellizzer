Lycopodiella lateralis

Lycopodiella lateralis, commonly known as the slender clubmoss, is a small, evergreen fern-like plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, scale-like leaves that give it a unique appearance. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Lycopodiella lateralis reproduces via spores, which are released from specialized structures called sporangia.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
