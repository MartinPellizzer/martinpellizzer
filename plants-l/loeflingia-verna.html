Loeflingia verna

Loeflingia verna, commonly known as the spring bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe.

It typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring slender stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, blooming in early spring. Loeflingia verna thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or shaded gardens.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking early-season color.
