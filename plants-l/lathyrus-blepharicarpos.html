Lathyrus blepharicarpos

Lathyrus blepharicarpos, commonly known as the eye-leaf pea, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features trifoliate leaves with small, heart-shaped leaflets, and its flowers are usually pale blue or violet, arranged in racemes. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are adapted for dispersal by animals.

This species is notable for its ornamental value and its role in ecological systems.
