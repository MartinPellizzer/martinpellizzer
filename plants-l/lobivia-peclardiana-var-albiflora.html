Lobivia peclardiana var. albiflora

Lobivia peclardiana var. albiflora, commonly known as the Whiteflower Lobivia, is a succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rounded growth habit with thick, ribbed stems. The plant produces showy, white to pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, and it stores water in its fleshy stems.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
