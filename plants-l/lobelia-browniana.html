Lobelia browniana

Lobelia browniana, commonly known as the Brown's lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems that bear small, tubular flowers in shades of blue to purple. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture.

Lobelia browniana is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
