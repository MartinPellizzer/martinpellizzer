Loranthus henriquesii

Loranthus henriquesii, commonly known as the Henriques' mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using light reflected from the host. The plant has small, scale-like leaves and produces white or yellowish flowers that are often visited by insects for pollination. Its seeds are dispersed by birds that eat the fleshy, red berries.

This species plays a significant ecological role in its habitat by influencing host tree growth and providing food for various wildlife.
