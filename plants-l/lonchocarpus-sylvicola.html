Lonchocarpus sylvicola

Lonchocarpus sylvicola, commonly known as the red root or wild senna, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its medicinal properties, with various parts used in traditional medicine. Its roots contain alkaloids and are sometimes used for their purported anti-inflammatory effects.

However, it is also considered toxic in certain forms, requiring careful handling.
