Leucolaena huegelii

Leucolaena huegelii, commonly known as the yellow serissa, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to yellow, and its elliptical, leathery leaves. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for warm climates. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate coastal conditions.

Leucolaena huegelii is also valued for its role in erosion control and as a source of browse for livestock.
