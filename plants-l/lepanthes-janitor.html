Lepanthes janitor

Lepanthes janitor, commonly known as the janitor orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that often display vibrant colors. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as cloud forests, where it thrives on the forest floor or on tree trunks. Its flowers are usually short-lived, opening in the early morning and closing by midday.

Lepanthes janitor is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its native habitats.
