Lilium bosniacum

Lilium bosniacum, commonly known as the Bosnian lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in mountainous regions with cool, moist conditions. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. Its bulbs are often used in traditional medicine, and the plant is valued for its ornamental beauty.

Lilium bosniacum plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting various pollinators and contributing to local biodiversity.
