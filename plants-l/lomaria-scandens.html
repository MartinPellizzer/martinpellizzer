Lomaria scandens

Lomaria scandens, commonly known as the climbing lomaria, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Polygonaceae.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its slender, twining stems that climb by means of tendrils. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color, which bloom in early to mid-summer. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a distinctive reddish or purple tinge when young.

Lomaria scandens thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
