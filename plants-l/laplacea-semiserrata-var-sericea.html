Laplacea semiserrata var. sericea

Laplacea semiserrata var. sericea, commonly known as the silverleaf sedge, is a perennial plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves that have a silvery sheen due to fine, silky hairs. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, thriving in moist soils along stream banks or in swampy areas. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, and it produces dark brown, three-sided seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland habitats.
