Lecomtedoxa

Lecomtedoxa, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a climbing plant native to South America.

It features showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a woody stem with tendrils that help it climb and attach to supports. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Lecomtedoxa is valued in gardens for its vibrant floral display and ability to thrive in full sun.
