Linum corymbiferum

Linum corymbiferum, commonly known as the common flax or wild flax, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, blue or purple flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oval seeds. Linum corymbiferum is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in textile production.
