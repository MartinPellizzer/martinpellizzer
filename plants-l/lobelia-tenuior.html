Lobelia tenuior

Lobelia tenuior, commonly known as slenderleaf lobelia, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, tubular flowers are usually blue or purple and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Lobelia tenuior is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
