Leucanthemum meridionale

Leucanthemum meridionale, commonly known as the southern oxeye daisy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a robust root system and erect, branched stems that can grow up to 60 cm in height. The plant produces white to pale yellow flowers with a central yellow disc, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
