Lamiophlomis rotata subsp. bhutanica

Lamiophlomis rotata subsp.

bhutanica, commonly known as Bhutanese lamiophlomis, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayan regions of Bhutan and northern India. It typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 3,000 meters.

The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its small, tubular flowers are usually arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional medicinal uses in local herbal practices.
