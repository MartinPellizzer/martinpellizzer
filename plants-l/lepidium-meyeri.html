Lepidium meyeri

Lepidium meyeri, commonly known as the mountain cress, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and the Caucasus. The plant is characterized by its small, white or yellowish flowers and its pinnately divided leaves. It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions.

Lepidium meyeri is often found in meadows and along stream banks at high elevations.
