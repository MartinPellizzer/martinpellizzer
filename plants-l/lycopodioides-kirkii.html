Lycopodioides kirkii

Lycopodioides kirkii, commonly known as Kirk's clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the order Lycopodiales.

It is characterized by its small, needle-like leaves and a compact, mat-forming growth habit. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures, including strobila, are borne on the upper portions of the stems.

Lycopodioides kirkii plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
