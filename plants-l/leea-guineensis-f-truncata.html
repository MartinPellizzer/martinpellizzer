Leea guineensis f. truncata

Leea guineensis f. truncata, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Leeaceae.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and showy, white, night-blooming flowers that emit a sweet fragrance. The plant is native to tropical Africa and thrives in humid, shady environments. Its unique morphology, including the truncate (cut-off) leaf shape, distinguishes it from other members of the species.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nocturnal blooming habit.
