Liatris punctata var. mucronata

Liatris punctata var. mucronata, commonly known as the spike gentian, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence composed of numerous small blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

This variety is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
