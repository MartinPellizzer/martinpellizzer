Lomatia polymorpha

Lomatia polymorpha, commonly known as the silky oaks, is a unique plant native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height, characterized by its silvery-gray bark and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, followed by woody pods containing flat, seed-like structures. Lomatia polymorpha is adapted to sandy soils and is often found in dune environments, showcasing its resilience to coastal conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in the ecological dynamics of its native habitat.
