Lippia lamiana

Lippia lamiana, commonly known as Mexican mint or wild mint, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, sunny environments. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a strong, aromatic scent. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or violet and bloom in clusters.

Lippia lamiana is often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.
