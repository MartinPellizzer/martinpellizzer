Luzula tristachya

Luzula tristachya, commonly known as the slender wood rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, upright stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish or brownish flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Luzula tristachya is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its delicate foliage and ability to thrive in partial shade.
