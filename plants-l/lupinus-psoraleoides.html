Lupinus psoraleoides

Lupinus psoraleoides, commonly known as the sand lupin, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of western North America.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces violet to purple flowers in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

Lupinus psoraleoides is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.
