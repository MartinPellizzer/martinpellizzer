Lupinaster pentaphyllus subsp. angustifolius

Lupinaster pentaphyllus subsp.

angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lupinaster, is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. It is native to southern Africa, particularly found in regions with Mediterranean climates.

The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with hairy stems and small, clustered flowers. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and the flowers are usually pale blue or violet, attracting pollinators like bees.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
