Lasianthus cyanocarpus

Lasianthus cyanocarpus, commonly known as the blue-fruited lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple berries. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub with opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually clustered in whorls and display a range of colors from white to pink.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant foliage and unique fruiting structures.
