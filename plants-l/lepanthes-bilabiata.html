Lepanthes bilabiata

Lepanthes bilabiata, commonly known as the pink lady's tresses, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and showy, pinkish-white flowers arranged in a dense, three-rowed spike. Native to the humid forests of Central and South America, this species thrives in shaded, moist environments. The plant produces small, tuberous roots and has a short, upright stem.

Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
