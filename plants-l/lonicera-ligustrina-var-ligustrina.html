Lonicera ligustrina var. ligustrina

Lonicera ligustrina var. ligustrina, commonly known as the Chinese honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are usually yellow or orange-red, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that ripens in late summer and is edible for both humans and birds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine.
