Lycium berlandieri var. andersonii

Lycium berlandieri var. andersonii, commonly known as the Anderson's boxthorn, is a shrub native to the Chihuahuan Desert in northern Mexico and southern Texas.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems and small, fleshy berries that are typically red or yellow when ripe. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, demonstrating resilience to drought and high temperatures. Its leaves are usually small, oval-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

This species plays a role in supporting local wildlife, particularly birds and small mammals, by providing both food and shelter.
