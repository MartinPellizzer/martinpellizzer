Lasianthus pseudostipularis

Lasianthus pseudostipularis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically arranged in spikes, with colors ranging from pale purple to white. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in tropical gardens.
