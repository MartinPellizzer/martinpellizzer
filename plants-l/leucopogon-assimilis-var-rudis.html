Leucopogon assimilis var. rudis

Leucopogon assimilis var. rudis, commonly known as the rough leucopogon, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is a small shrub characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or violet flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in heathland and coastal dunes. It is adapted to tolerate salt spray and is an important component of the local flora in its habitat.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
