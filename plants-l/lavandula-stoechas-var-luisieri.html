Lavandula stoechas var. luisieri

Lavandula stoechas var. luisieri, commonly known as the Spanish lavender or Luisieri lavender, is a compact, aromatic shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features striking violet-blue flower spikes that are often adorned with distinctive, fuzzy bracts, giving it a unique appearance. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and Mediterranean climates.

Its fragrance and ornamental value make it a valued addition to both gardens and floral arrangements.
