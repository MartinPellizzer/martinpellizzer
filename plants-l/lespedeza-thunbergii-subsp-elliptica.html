Lespedeza thunbergii subsp. elliptica

Lespedeza thunbergii subsp.

elliptica, commonly known as the elliptic lespedeza, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia. It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It produces small, pea-like flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
