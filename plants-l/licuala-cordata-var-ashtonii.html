Licuala cordata var. ashtonii

Licuala cordata var. ashtonii, commonly known as the Ashton's palm, is a variant of the broad-leafed palm native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach up to 2 meters in diameter, giving it a striking visual presence. The plant typically grows in humid, tropical environments, often near rivers or in swampy areas. Its trunk is short and stout, with a fibrous, rough texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
