Lilium alternans

Lilium alternans, commonly known as the alternate lily, is a species of flowering plant in the Liliaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, often fragrant flowers that come in shades of white, pink, and red. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and prefers moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem, giving it its common name.

Lilium alternans is known for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
