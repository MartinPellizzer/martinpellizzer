Lacinaria scariosa var. nieuwlandii

Lacinaria scariosa var. nieuwlandii, commonly known as the Newland's lacinaria, is a rare and striking plant native to specific regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, hairy, and often lobed leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a valuable species for botanical study and conservation efforts.
