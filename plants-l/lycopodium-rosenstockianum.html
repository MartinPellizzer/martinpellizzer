Lycopodium rosenstockianum

Lycopodium rosenstockianum, commonly known as the Rosenstock's clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features a creeping stem with whorled leaves that are often scale-like in appearance. Its reproductive structures, called strobila, are cone-like and contain the spores necessary for propagation.

Lycopodium rosenstockianum plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
