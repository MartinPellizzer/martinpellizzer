Loranthus molliflorus

Loranthus molliflorus, commonly known as the soft-flowered mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of host trees.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using the light it captures. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces white or pinkish flowers that are often clustered in small groups. Its berries are typically red or yellow and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This species is found in various regions of Africa and is often associated with specific host trees like acacias and eucalyptus.
