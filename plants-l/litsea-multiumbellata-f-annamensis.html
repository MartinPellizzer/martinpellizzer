Litsea multiumbellata f. annamensis

Litsea multiumbellata f. annamensis, commonly known as the Annam leafy-laurel, is a botanical variant native to Southeast Asia, particularly Vietnam.

It is a member of the Lauraceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are usually elliptical, with a glossy surface and a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its foliage in horticultural settings.
