Lindernia crustacea var. verticillata

Lindernia crustacea var. verticillata, commonly known as the clustered lindernia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The leaves are opposite and ovate, with a slightly hairy texture. This species thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
