Laelia albida var. brunnea

Laelia albida var. brunnea, commonly known as the white-veined orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking white petals with dark brown veins, which give it a distinctive and elegant appearance. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. It produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, often in shades of white and yellow.

This variety is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
