Lappa janczewskii

Lappa janczewskii, commonly known as the narrowleaf hogweed, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its umbellate inflorescence and aromatic foliage.

Its flowers are small and white, arranged in compound umbels, and it produces small, dry fruits known as schizocarps.

Lappa janczewskii is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and riverbanks.
