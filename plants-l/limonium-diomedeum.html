Limonium diomedeum

Limonium diomedeum, commonly known as the sea helene, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form and produces spikes of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils. Its flowering period typically occurs in late summer to early autumn.

Limonium diomedeum is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
