Lobelia crispa

Lobelia crispa, commonly known as the crested lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland areas and along stream banks.

Lobelia crispa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
