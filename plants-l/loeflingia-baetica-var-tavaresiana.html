Loeflingia baetica var. tavaresiana

Loeflingia baetica var. tavaresiana, commonly known as the Tavaresian stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, adapting to arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically during the summer months. It thrives in rocky soils and full sun exposure, making it a hardy species for Mediterranean climates.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
