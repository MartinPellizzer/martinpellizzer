Lupinus sinaloensis

Lupinus sinaloensis, commonly known as the Sinaloan lupine, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds.

Lupinus sinaloensis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species that thrives in well-drained soils.
