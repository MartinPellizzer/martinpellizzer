Loranthus claviceps

Loranthus claviceps, commonly known as the mistletoe of the genus Loranthus, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using light reflected from the host's leaves. The plant has a distinctive appearance with small, scale-like leaves and small, white or yellowish flowers. Its berries are typically red or orange and contain sticky seeds that adhere to the bark of trees.

This species is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it plays a role in ecological interactions with its host plants.
