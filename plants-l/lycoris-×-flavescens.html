Lycoris × flavescens

Lycoris × flavescens, commonly known as the golden lily-of-the-valley, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant features strap-like, narrow leaves that emerge from a bulbous base, typical of many bulbous flowering plants. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant color and unique floral structure.
