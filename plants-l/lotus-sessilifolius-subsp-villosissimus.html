Lotus sessilifolius subsp. villosissimus

Lotus sessilifolius subsp.

villosissimus, commonly known as the woolly lotus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia. It is characterized by its basal rosette of large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in dense, soft white hairs.

The plant produces showy pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer, often with a distinctive scent. Its roots are edible and have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

This subspecies is adapted to wetland environments and thrives in moist, clay soils.
