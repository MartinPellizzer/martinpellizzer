Lycopodium mimosoides

Lycopodium mimosoides, commonly known as the mimic fern, is a species of clubmoss that belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, fern-like foliage composed of small, scale-like leaves arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces spore-bearing structures called strobila, which are typically clustered at the tips of the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Lycopodium mimosoides is valued for its ornamental appearance and its role in ecological systems.
