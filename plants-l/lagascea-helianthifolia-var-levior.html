Lagascea helianthifolia var. levior

Lagascea helianthifolia var. levior, commonly known as the pale lagascea, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, branched stems and narrow, linear leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its yellow-orange flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is distinguished by its lighter coloration compared to other forms of Lagascea helianthifolia.
