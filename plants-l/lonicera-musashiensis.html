Lonicera musashiensis

Lonicera musashiensis, commonly known as the Japanese honeysuckle, is a deciduous climbing vine native to Japan.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to climb using its twining stems. It blooms in the spring and is often used in ornamental gardening.

However, it can be invasive in certain regions if not properly managed.
