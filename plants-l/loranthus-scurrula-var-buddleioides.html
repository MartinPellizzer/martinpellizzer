Loranthus scurrula var. buddleioides

Loranthus scurrula var. buddleioides, commonly known as the Indian mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily in the family Rutaceae.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while still performing some photosynthesis using modified leaves. It is native to regions of India and Southeast Asia, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can cause stress to its host trees if left unchecked.
