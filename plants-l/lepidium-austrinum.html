Lepidium austrinum

Lepidium austrinum, commonly known as the Australian cress, is a small annual herb native to Australia.

It has slender, branched stems and small, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, oval-shaped pods containing several seeds. Lepidium austrinum is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and gardens.

It is valued for its peppery taste and is sometimes used in culinary applications.
