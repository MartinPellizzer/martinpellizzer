Linaria virgata var. calycina

Linaria virgata var. calycina, commonly known as the California sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous annual with slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and inland habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species for pollinators and a representative of the Plantaginaceae family.
