Lupinus pickeringii

Lupinus pickeringii, commonly known as Pickering's lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Lupinus pickeringii plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native environment.
