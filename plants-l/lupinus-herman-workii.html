Lupinus herman-workii

Lupinus herman-workii, commonly known as Herman-Work's lupine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its erect stems and clusters of blue to violet pea-like flowers. The plant produces compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Lupinus herman-workii is part of the legume family and has the ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
