Lyperia phlogiflora

Lyperia phlogiflora, commonly known as the flameflower, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red and orange inflorescences, which resemble flames, hence its common name. The plant belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is known for its showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a glossy appearance, contributing to its ornamental value.

Lyperia phlogiflora thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate to flourish.
