Limonium lacostei

Limonium lacostei, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flower heads, which are surrounded by spiny, leaf-like bracts. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is well adapted to salt spray and coastal conditions. Its flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Limonium lacostei is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
