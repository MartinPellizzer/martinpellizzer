Lachnospermum umbellatum

Lachnospermum umbellatum, commonly known as the mountain morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soil.

Lachnospermum umbellatum is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
