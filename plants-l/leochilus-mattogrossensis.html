Leochilus mattogrossensis

Leochilus mattogrossensis, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid, is a rare and endemic species native to the Brazilian state of Mato Grosso.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments within the Atlantic Forest biome. Its growth is slow, and it requires specific soil conditions rich in organic matter and good drainage.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered threatened and is often found in conservation areas.
