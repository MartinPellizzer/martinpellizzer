Lobelia erinus var. bracteolata

Lobelia erinus var. bracteolata, commonly known as the dwarf red-leaf lobelia, is a compact, trailing plant native to South America.

It features small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple, with striking red or maroon leaves that add visual interest. The plant has a fibrous root system and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for container gardening. It is often used as a bedding plant due to its ornamental value and ability to bloom profusely in full sun.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for rock gardens and hanging baskets.
