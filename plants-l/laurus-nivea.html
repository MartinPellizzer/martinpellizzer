Laurus nivea

Laurus nivea, commonly known as the white bay laurel, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are followed by edible, black berries. Its leaves are often used in cooking and have a mild, sweet aroma similar to bay leaves.

Laurus nivea is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and historical significance.
