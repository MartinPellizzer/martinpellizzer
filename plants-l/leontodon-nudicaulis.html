Leontodon nudicaulis

Leontodon nudicaulis, commonly known as the narrowleaf hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and rocky soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a hairy texture, and its flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is often found in open habitats and is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed environments.
