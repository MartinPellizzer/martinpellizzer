Lantana triplinervia var. longibracteolata

Lantana triplinervia var. longibracteolata, commonly known as the broad-leaved lantana, is a species native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to yellow, pink, or red, depending on the variety. Its inflorescences are typically surrounded by bracts, which are often longer than the flowers themselves.

The species is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in full sun, making it a common ornamental plant in warmer climates.
