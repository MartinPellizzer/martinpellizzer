Leptopus lanceolatus

Leptopus lanceolatus, commonly known as the lanceolate bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to freshwater habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, bladder-like structures that trap aquatic invertebrates. The plant thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and is often found in wetlands and ponds. Its leaves are typically narrow and elongated, adapted for efficient nutrient absorption from both water and prey.

The species plays an important role in its ecosystem by controlling insect populations and contributing to nutrient cycling.
