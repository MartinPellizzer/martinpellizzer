Lagochilus glaberrimus

Lagochilus glaberrimus, commonly known as the smooth-lipped rock rose, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, hairy seeds in its fruit.

Lagochilus glaberrimus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and drought tolerance.
