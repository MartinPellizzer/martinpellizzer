Lepidium scopulorum var. spathulatum

Lepidium scopulorum var. spathulatum, commonly known as the broadleaf wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, showy yellow flowers clustered in dense inflorescences. The leaves are typically broad and spatulate, giving the plant its specific epithet "spathulatum.". It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
