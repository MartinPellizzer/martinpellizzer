Lupinus yakimensis

Lupinus yakimensis, commonly known as Yakimen's lupine, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and showy, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, exhibiting a range of flower colors including blue, purple, and white.

Lupinus yakimensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and also plays a role in soil nitrogen fixation due to its symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.
