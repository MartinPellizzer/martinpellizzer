Lathyrus japonicus f. albescens

Lathyrus japonicus f. albescens, commonly known as the white-flowered Japanese vetch, is a variant of the Japanese vetch that exhibits distinctive white flowers.

This plant is a perennial legume native to Japan and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves. The flowers are typically small and borne in racemes, with a pale pink to white coloration. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its unique floral display.
