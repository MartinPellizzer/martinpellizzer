Leandra deflexa var. furfuracea

Leandra deflexa var. furfuracea, commonly known as the woolly leandra, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy, and hairy foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of rainforests.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
