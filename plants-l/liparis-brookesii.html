Liparis brookesii

Liparis brookesii, commonly known as the Brooks' orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and grasslands. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil efficiently.

Liparis brookesii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
