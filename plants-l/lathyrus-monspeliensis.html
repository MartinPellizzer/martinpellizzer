Lathyrus monspeliensis

Lathyrus monspeliensis, commonly known as the spring vetchling, is a flowering plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruit. The plant typically grows in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its flowers are usually pale blue or violet, with a distinctive spurred petal.

The seeds are enclosed in a hairy, inflated pod and are dispersed by wind or animals.
