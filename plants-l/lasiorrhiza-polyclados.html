Lasiorrhiza polyclados

Lasiorrhiza polyclados, commonly known as the red-flowered rock orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive red petals and a compact growth habit, often found in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapted to conserve water in arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and is valued for its ornamental qualities.
