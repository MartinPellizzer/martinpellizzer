Lotodes glandulosum var. albidum

Lotodes glandulosum var. albidum, commonly known as the white-flowered water lily, is a perennial aquatic plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves and white, showy flowers that bloom above the water surface. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds and lakes. Its leaves are typically rounded with a waxy surface, and its stems are long and flexible, allowing it to spread across the water's surface.

The species is noted for its ecological importance in supporting aquatic life and providing habitat for various water organisms.
