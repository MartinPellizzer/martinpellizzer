Lepanthes ovalis

Lepanthes ovalis, commonly known as the oval orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a rosette pattern.

Lepanthes ovalis is often found in shaded, humid environments, such as cloud forests, where it thrives in the understory.
