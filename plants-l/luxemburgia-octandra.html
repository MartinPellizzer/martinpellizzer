Luxemburgia octandra

Luxemburgia octandra, commonly known as the eight-angled luxemburgia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, angular stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.

Luxemburgia octandra is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
