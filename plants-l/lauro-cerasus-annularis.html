Lauro-cerasus annularis

Lauro-cerasus annularis, commonly known as the cherry laurel, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Prunus genus and is characterized by its glossy, dark green elliptical leaves and showy white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to a variety of soil types, though it prefers well-drained conditions.
