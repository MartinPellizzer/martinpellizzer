Lipochaeta pinnatifida

Lipochaeta pinnatifida, commonly known as the slender mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant belonging to the Onagraceae family.

It is native to Australia and thrives in coastal and sandy environments. The plant has small, opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces tiny, white to pink flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that releases seeds with tufts of hair for wind dispersal.

Lipochaeta pinnatifida is often found growing on the branches of eucalyptus and other woody plants.
