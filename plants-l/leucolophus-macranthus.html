Leucolophus macranthus

Leucolophus macranthus, commonly known as the large-flowered orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 centimeters in diameter, featuring a combination of white and green hues. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its native habitat.
