Lythrum salicaria var. gracilius

Lythrum salicaria var. gracilius, commonly known as narrow-leaved purple loosestrife, is a variant of the purple loosestrife plant native to Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and spikes of vibrant purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, wetlands, and along stream banks. It is a herbaceous perennial that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, with a fibrous root system.

Due to its invasive potential, it is sometimes considered a noxious weed in certain regions.
