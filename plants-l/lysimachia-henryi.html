Lysimachia henryi

Lysimachia henryi, commonly known as Henry's chickweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern Asia.

It features small, star-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or white, blooming in early summer. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and it often grows in moist, shaded environments. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.

Lysimachia henryi is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
