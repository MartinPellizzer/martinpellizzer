Linum usitatissimum var. macrocarpum

Linum usitatissimum var. macrocarpum, commonly known as the giant flax, is a variant of the common flax species characterized by its larger seeds and robust growth habit.

It is a herbaceous annual plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia. The plant produces showy blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are erect and often branched.

This variety is valued for its seed yield and historical use in textile and oil production.
