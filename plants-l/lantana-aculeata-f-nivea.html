Lantana aculeata f. nivea

Lantana aculeata f. nivea, commonly known as the white-flowered lantana, is a compact shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, clustered white flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has hairy, opposite leaves that are typically oval to lance-shaped. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and drought-tolerant plant suitable for various landscaping applications.
