Lagenaria leucantha var. clavata

Lagenaria leucantha var. clavata, commonly known as the bottle gourd or calabash, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, elongated, bottle-shaped fruits that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions and thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil. Its leaves are broad and lobed, while the flowers are typically yellow and unisexual.

The fruit is used in various culinary and practical applications due to its versatility and durability.
