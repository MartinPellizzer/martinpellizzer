Lupinus pisacensis

Lupinus pisacensis, commonly known as the Pisac lupine, is a flowering plant native to the high Andes of Peru.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of blue or violet pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and is adapted to thrive in high-altitude, rocky environments. Lupinus pisacensis plays a role in nitrogen fixation, enriching the soil with nutrients.

Its unique appearance and ecological significance make it an important species in its native habitat.
