Lycopodioides lindenii

Lycopodioides lindenii, commonly known as the Lindenberg's clubmoss, is a species of vascular plant belonging to the order Lycopodiales.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and a creeping rhizome system, typical of many clubmosses. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate forests and rocky areas. Its reproductive structures include sporangia that produce spores, which are dispersed by wind.

Lycopodioides lindenii plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
