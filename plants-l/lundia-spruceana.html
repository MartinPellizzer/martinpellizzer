Lundia spruceana

Lundia spruceana, commonly known as the Spruce-leaved Lundia, is a rare flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its evergreen coniferous-like appearance, featuring needle-like leaves that are dense and arranged in spirals. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by the development of cone-like structures. Lundia spruceana thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and requires a cool, humid climate to flourish.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
