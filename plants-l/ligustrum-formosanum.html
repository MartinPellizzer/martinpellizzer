Ligustrum formosanum

Ligustrum formosanum, commonly known as the Formosan privet, is a deciduous shrub native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, simple, and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by dark blue-black berries. It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.

However, it can become invasive in some regions if not properly managed.
