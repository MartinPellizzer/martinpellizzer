Lupinus bolivianus

Lupinus bolivianus, commonly known as the Bolivian lupine, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Bolivia and Argentina.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth and compound leaves. The plant produces spikes of showy, often blue or purple flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Lupinus bolivianus is adapted to grow in arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

Its seeds are used by local communities for food and traditional medicine.
