Linaria gracilis

Linaria gracilis, commonly known as slender toadflax, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its slender stems and small, lance-shaped leaves contribute to its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Linaria gracilis is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
