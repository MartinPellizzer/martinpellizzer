Leymus bruneostachyus

Leymus bruneostachyus, commonly known as the purple-stemmed grass, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces panicles of small, pale blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. Leymus bruneostachyus is adapted to well-drained soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including meadows and open woodlands.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
