Lobelia flexicaulis

Lobelia flexicaulis, commonly known as the flexible lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that often exhibit a flexible or even drooping growth habit. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in dense clusters at the tips of the stems. Lobelia flexicaulis thrives in moist, shady environments, often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs.
