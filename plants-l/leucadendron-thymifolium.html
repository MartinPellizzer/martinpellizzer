Leucadendron thymifolium

Leucadendron thymifolium, commonly known as the silverleaf leucadendron, is a South African shrub belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and its cone-like inflorescences that range in color from yellow to red. The plant is adapted to nutrient-poor soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by insects.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in horticulture for its hardy and drought-resistant nature.
