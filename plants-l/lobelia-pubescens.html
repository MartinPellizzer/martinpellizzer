Lobelia pubescens

Lobelia pubescens, commonly known as the hairy lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and spikes of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Lobelia pubescens is a herbaceous perennial that blooms from late spring to early summer.

It is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
