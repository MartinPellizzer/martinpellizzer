Linaria verticillata var. gaditana

Linaria verticillata var. gaditana, commonly known as the Spanish toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and coastal areas. Its leaves are opposite, linear, and often covered in fine hairs.

It is a perennial species that is relatively drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils.
