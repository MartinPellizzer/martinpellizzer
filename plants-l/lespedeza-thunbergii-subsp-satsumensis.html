Lespedeza thunbergii subsp. satsumensis

Lespedeza thunbergii subsp.

satsumensis, commonly known as the Japanese lespedeza, is a perennial shrub native to East Asia. It features small, pink to purple flower clusters that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, hairy seeds in dry pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or open areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
