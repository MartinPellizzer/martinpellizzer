Lapsana communis subsp. ramosissima

Lapsana communis subsp.

ramosissima, commonly known as hairy yellow toadflax, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is a member of the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems.

The subspecies is distinguished by its more branched growth habit compared to other forms of Lapsana communis. It typically grows in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
