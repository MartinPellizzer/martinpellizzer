Linum teneriffae

Linum teneriffae, commonly known as the Canary Island flax, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the genus Linum, which includes various species of flax plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically blue or purple, with five petals arranged in a symmetrical pattern.

Linum teneriffae is adapted to the arid and coastal environments of its native habitat.
