Litsea leefeana

Litsea leefeana, commonly known as the white-flowered litsea, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, round berries.

The leaves and fruits are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cuisines.
