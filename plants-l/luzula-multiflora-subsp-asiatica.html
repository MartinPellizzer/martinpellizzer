Luzula multiflora subsp. asiatica

Luzula multiflora subsp.

asiatica, commonly known as the Asian wood rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes.

The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of white or greenish flowers. It belongs to the family Poaceae, which includes grasses and sedges.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies in suitable habitats.
