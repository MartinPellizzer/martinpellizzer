Lepanthes pabloi

Lepanthes pabloi, commonly known as Pablo's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale pink in color. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

Lepanthes pabloi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
