Lepanthes corrugata

Lepanthes corrugata, commonly known as the zigzag orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its intricate, often zigzag-shaped petals and sepals, which contribute to its distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are usually small and fragrant, often appearing in clusters during the rainy season.

Lepanthes corrugata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is a subject of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
