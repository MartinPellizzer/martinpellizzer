Lathyrus magellanicus var. magellanicus

Lathyrus magellanicus var. magellanicus, commonly known as the Magellanic pea, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant features slender, climbing stems and produces small, pea-like flowers in shades of pink to purple. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The Magellanic pea is adapted to withstand harsh coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.
