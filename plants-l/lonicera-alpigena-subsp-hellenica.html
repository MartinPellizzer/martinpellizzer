Lonicera alpigena subsp. hellenica

Lonicera alpigena subsp.

hellenica, commonly known as the Greek honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Greece. It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its slender, arching stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. These flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested habitats at higher elevations.
