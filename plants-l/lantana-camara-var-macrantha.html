Lantana camara var. macrantha

Lantana camara var. macrantha, commonly known as the giant lantana, is a shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinct aromatic scent. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom throughout the growing season. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it both ornamental and invasive in some areas.

The plant is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of nectar for pollinators.
