Lepanthes elaminata

Lepanthes elaminata, commonly known as the white-veined orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically feature white petals with green or yellow veining. The plant grows as an epiphyte, often found on trees in humid tropical forests. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Lepanthes elaminata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or shaded gardens.
