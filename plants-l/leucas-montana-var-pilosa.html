Leucas montana var. pilosa

Leucas montana var. pilosa, commonly known as hairy mountain leucas, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant is distinguished by its dense, white to grayish pubescence, which covers the stems and leaves, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It produces small, pale blue or purple flowers arranged in whorls along the stems.

This species is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
