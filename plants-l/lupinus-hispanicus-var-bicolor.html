Lupinus hispanicus var. bicolor

Lupinus hispanicus var. bicolor, commonly known as the Spanish lupin, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces spikes of bright pink or purple flowers, which are typically bilaterally symmetrical. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Lupinus hispanicus var. bicolor is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
