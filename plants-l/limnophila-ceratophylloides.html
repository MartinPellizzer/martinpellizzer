Limnophila ceratophylloides

Limnophila ceratophylloides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water starwort, is a aquatic plant native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and wetlands. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves that are often submerged. It produces tiny, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Limnophila ceratophylloides plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
