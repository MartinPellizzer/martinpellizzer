Lilium bakerianum var. bakerianum

Lilium bakerianum var. bakerianum, commonly known as the Baker's lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, and the plant has a fibrous root system.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
