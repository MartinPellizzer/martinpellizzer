Lythrum tuxtlense

Lythrum tuxtlense, commonly known as Tuxtlans rosemary, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lythraceae family and is characterized by its erect, herbaceous stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
