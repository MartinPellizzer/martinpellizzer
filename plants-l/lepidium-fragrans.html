Lepidium fragrans

Lepidium fragrans, commonly known as sweet rocket, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in clusters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly peppery aroma.

Lepidium fragrans is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
