Limodorum generale

Limodorum generale, commonly known as the noble orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to various regions in Asia, including China, Japan, and the Himalayas.

It is a terrestrial herb with a slender, erect stem that bears a single, showy flower with a distinctive hooded lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors and mountainous regions. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Limodorum generale is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
