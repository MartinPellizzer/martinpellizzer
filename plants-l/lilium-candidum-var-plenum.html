Lilium candidum var. plenum

Lilium candidum var. plenum, commonly known as the full-flowered white lily, is a cultivar of the white lily species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its densely packed, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green in color. Its bulbs are perennial, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
