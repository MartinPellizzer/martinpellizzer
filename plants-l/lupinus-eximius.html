Lupinus eximius

Lupinus eximius, commonly known as the exalted lupine, is a member of the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to the western United States, particularly found in California and Oregon. This perennial plant typically grows in rocky soils and open habitats, such as chaparral and coastal scrub. It features tall, erect stems with clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility.
