Lactuca serriola f. integrifolia

Lactuca serriola f. integrifolia, commonly known as prickly lettuce, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features a rosette of deeply lobed, hairy leaves with serrated edges, and it produces yellow flowers in a loose cluster. The plant has a taproot and is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in fields. It is characterized by its distinctive prickly leaves and is sometimes considered a weed.

The species is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
