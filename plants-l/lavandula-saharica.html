Lavandula saharica

Lavandula saharica, commonly known as the Sahara lavender, is a flowering plant native to North Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates. The plant produces spikes of fragrant purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, with a strong aromatic scent reminiscent of lavender.

Lavandula saharica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
