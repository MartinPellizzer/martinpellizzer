Lotodes venustum

Lotodes venustum, commonly known as the water lily, is a perennial aquatic plant native to temperate regions.

It features broad, floating leaves that provide shade for aquatic life and support a variety of insects. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pink, blooming in summer. Its roots anchor it in muddy substrates, while its rhizomes spread horizontally to form colonies.

Lotodes venustum plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by improving water quality and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
