Lissochilus taylorii

Lissochilus taylorii, commonly known as Taylor's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers are usually small and come in shades of white, pink, and purple, making it a striking addition to botanical gardens.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
