Lilium dauricum f. batemanniae

Lilium dauricum f. batemanniae, commonly known as the Batemann's lily, is a rare and ornamental plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and has lance-shaped, grass-like leaves. It is valued for its beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern in some regions.
