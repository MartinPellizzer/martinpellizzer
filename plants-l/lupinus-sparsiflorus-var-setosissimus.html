Lupinus sparsiflorus var. setosissimus

Lupinus sparsiflorus var. setosissimus, commonly known as the hairy lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its sparsely flowered, erect stems and compound leaves. The plant features small, pea-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, often appearing in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This variety is notable for its hairy foliage and its role in nitrogen fixation, benefiting the surrounding ecosystem.
