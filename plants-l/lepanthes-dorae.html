Lepanthes dorae

Lepanthes dorae, commonly known as the Dorae's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale purple in color. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a rosette pattern.

Lepanthes dorae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
