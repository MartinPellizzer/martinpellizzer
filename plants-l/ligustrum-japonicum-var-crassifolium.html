Ligustrum japonicum var. crassifolium

Ligustrum japonicum var. crassifolium, commonly known as Japanese privet, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that are thick and leathery, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by dark blue berries in the fall. It is often used in landscaping for its dense growth and adaptability to various soil types.

However, it can become invasive in some regions if not properly managed.
