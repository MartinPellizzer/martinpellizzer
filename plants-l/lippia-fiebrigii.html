Lippia fiebrigii

Lippia fiebrigii, commonly known as Mexican blue sage, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and basil. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and showy blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

Lippia fiebrigii is also cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.
