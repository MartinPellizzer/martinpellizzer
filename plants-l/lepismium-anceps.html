Lepismium anceps

Lepismium anceps, commonly known as the red-veined orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves with deep red veins. The plant produces fragrant, showy flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

Lepismium anceps is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
