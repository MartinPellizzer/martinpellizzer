Lycaste skinneri var. albaviridis

Lycaste skinneri var. albaviridis, commonly known as the white-green lycaste, is a rare and striking orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that display a unique combination of white and green hues, giving it its varietal name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its foliage consists of broad, strap-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle, aiding in moisture retention.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
