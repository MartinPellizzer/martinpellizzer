Lepanthes saccosepala

Lepanthes saccosepala, commonly known as the sac-like lip orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored flowers with a distinctive sac-like structure on the lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical forests. Its leaves are usually elongated and strap-like, adapted for efficient water absorption.

Lepanthes saccosepala is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique floral morphology.
