Lactuca sikkimensis

Lactuca sikkimensis, commonly known as Sikkim lettuce, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India, Nepal, and Bhutan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and dark green, slightly serrated leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the dandelion family. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and forest clearings.

Lactuca sikkimensis is not commonly cultivated for food but is occasionally used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
