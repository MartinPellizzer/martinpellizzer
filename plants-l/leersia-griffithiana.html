Leersia griffithiana

Leersia griffithiana, commonly known as the purple leersia, is a perennial grass species native to Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with purple or reddish hues. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale green to brown flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Leersia griffithiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting wildlife.
