Lippia cardiostegia

Lippia cardiostegia, commonly known as Mexican mint or blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to Central America and the southern United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, mint-scented leaves and clusters of small, blue or purple flowers. The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Lippia cardiostegia is also a popular ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to attract pollinators.
