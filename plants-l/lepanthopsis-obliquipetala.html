Lepanthopsis obliquipetala

Lepanthopsis obliquipetala, commonly known as the oblique-lobed orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its sympodial growth habit and produces pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowers. The plant typically features lanceolate leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, and its flowers are small, clustered in inflorescences with a distinctive coloration. Lepanthopsis obliquipetala is known for its intricate floral structure, which includes a labellum that is often adorned with intricate markings.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires careful cultivation to mimic its natural habitat.
