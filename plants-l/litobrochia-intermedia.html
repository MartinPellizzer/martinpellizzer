Litobrochia intermedia

Litobrochia intermedia, commonly known as the rock brochome, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to rocky and arid environments.

It typically grows in regions with well-drained soils, often found in mountainous or coastal areas. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or pale in color. It is adapted to withstand drought and high levels of sunlight.

Its botanical structure reflects its ability to thrive in harsh, nutrient-poor conditions.
