Lobelia surrepens

Lobelia surrepens, commonly known as the hidden lobelia, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple, arranged in spikes along the stems. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy and slightly succulent, adapted to thrive in moist, shaded environments. It is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other damp habitats.

Lobelia surrepens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
