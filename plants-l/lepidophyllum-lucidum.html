Lepidophyllum lucidum

Lepidophyllum lucidum, commonly known as the shining leaf cactus, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the family Cactaceae and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and spiny stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in the spring. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to survive in dry conditions.

Lepidophyllum lucidum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
