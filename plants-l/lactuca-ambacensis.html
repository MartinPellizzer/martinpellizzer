Lactuca ambacensis

Lactuca ambacensis, commonly known as the Ethiopian lettuce, is a species of lettuce native to Ethiopia.

It is a flowering plant in the dandelion family, Asteraceae, and is characterized by its rosette of broad, wavy leaves. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, adapting to harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are edible and have a mild, slightly bitter taste, making it a traditional food in its native region.

Lactuca ambacensis is also valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal uses.
