Lepanthes apiculifera

Lepanthes apiculifera, commonly known as the apiculate orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees in humid tropical forests. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces strikingly colorful, fragrant flowers with intricate patterns. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow with red or purple markings, attracting various pollinators.

Lepanthes apiculifera is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
