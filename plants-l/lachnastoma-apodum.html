Lachnastoma apodum

Lachnastoma apodum, commonly known as the leafless mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that primarily inhabits the branches of host trees in tropical and subtropical regions.

It lacks true leaves and instead absorbs water and nutrients through its specialized structures called haustoria, which penetrate the host's vascular system. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often white or greenish in color, followed by small, berry-like fruits. Lachnastoma apodum plays a role in the ecosystem by providing food for certain bird species and contributing to the biodiversity of its host trees.
