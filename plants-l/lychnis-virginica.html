Lychnis virginica

Lychnis virginica, commonly known as the pink campion, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Lychnis virginica is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
