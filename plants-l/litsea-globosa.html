Litsea globosa

Litsea globosa, commonly known as the globular-laurel, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, ovate leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. Its fruits are spherical, hence the species name "globosa," and are typically orange-red when mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its aromatic properties.
