Leucophae marmorea

Leucophae marmorea, commonly known as the marble queen peace lily, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, waxy leaves that display a distinctive white and green mottling pattern, giving it its common name. The plant produces elegant white flowers that are often surrounded by modified leaves called spathes. It thrives in low-light conditions and requires consistent moisture and humidity to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
