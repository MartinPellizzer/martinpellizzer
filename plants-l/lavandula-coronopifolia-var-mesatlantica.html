Lavandula coronopifolia var. mesatlantica

Lavandula coronopifolia var. mesatlantica, commonly known as the Mexican lavender, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features narrow, silver-gray foliage with a distinctive, compact growth habit. The plant produces spikes of pale purple to lavender flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

This variety is valued for its aromatic properties and ornamental appeal in gardens.
