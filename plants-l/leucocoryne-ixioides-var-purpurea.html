Leucocoryne ixioides var. purpurea

Leucocoryne ixioides var. purpurea, commonly known as the Purple-leafed Spider Orchid, is a striking plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, spider-like appearance with elongated, narrow leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soil. Its foliage is notable for its distinctive purple hue, which contrasts beautifully with its white or pale pink floral displays.

This variety is prized by collectors for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouse settings due to its specific environmental requirements.
