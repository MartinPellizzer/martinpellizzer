Lathyrus numidicus

Lathyrus numidicus, commonly known as the Numidian vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in Mediterranean regions, characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in clusters. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are an important food source for various wildlife.

Lathyrus numidicus is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.
