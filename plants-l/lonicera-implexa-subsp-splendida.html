Lonicera implexa subsp. splendida

Lonicera implexa subsp.

splendida, commonly known as the golden honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to southeastern China. It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-orange flowers.

The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy beneath, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to climb or trail through structures.

The subspecies is closely related to other honeysuckle species but is distinguished by its vibrant floral color and unique growth form.
