Lobivia pentlandii f. carminantha

Lobivia pentlandii f. carminantha, commonly known as the Carmine Lobivia, is a unique and striking member of the Cactaceae family.

It is a compact, short-stemmed cactus with a dense covering of white to pale blue spines. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers that range from pink to deep red, often blooming in late spring or early summer. Its foliage consists of fleshy, ribbed stems that store water, adapting it to arid environments.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a container plant.
