Loricaria thuyoides var. laxifolia

Loricaria thuyoides var. laxifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a species native to South America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant has opposite, elliptical leaves that are often hairy on the underside. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in panicles, followed by red, fleshy berries.

The plant is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
