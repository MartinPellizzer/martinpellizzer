Lerchia maritima var. fruticosa

Lerchia maritima var. fruticosa, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its silver-gray, woolly leaves and striking blue or purple flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is highly adapted to saline and windy environments. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscaping.

It blooms from late spring to early summer, adding ornamental value to coastal gardens.
