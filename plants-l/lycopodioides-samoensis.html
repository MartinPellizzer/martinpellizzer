Lycopodioides samoensis

Lycopodioides samoensis, commonly known as the Samoan clubmoss, is a species of lycophyte native to the Pacific Islands.

It is a small, herbaceous plant characterized by its spore-bearing cones and needle-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures are adapted for wind pollination, a common trait among lycophytes.

Lycopodioides samoensis plays a role in maintaining soil stability and providing habitat for small organisms in its native ecosystem.
