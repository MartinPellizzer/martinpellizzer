Limonium lychnidifolium var. corymbosum

Limonium lychnidifolium var. corymbosum, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit and its clusters of small, blue to violet flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to salt spray and coastal conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are drought-tolerant.

This variety is often used in coastal landscaping due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
