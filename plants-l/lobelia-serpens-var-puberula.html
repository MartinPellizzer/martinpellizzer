Lobelia serpens var. puberula

Lobelia serpens var. puberula, commonly known as the hairy threadleaf lobelia, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, trailing stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and slightly serrated, with a bluish-green hue. Small, tubular flowers in shades of blue or purple bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

This plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded, woodland environments.
