Lepanthes moorei

Lepanthes moorei, commonly known as the Moore's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid with a delicate appearance, featuring a slender stem and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically blooms in the spring, producing white or pale pink flowers with intricate patterns. Lepanthes moorei thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical significance lies in its ornamental value and its role in the ecological diversity of tropical ecosystems.
