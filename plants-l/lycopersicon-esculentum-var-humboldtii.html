Lycopersicon esculentum var. humboldtii

Lycopersicon esculentum var. humboldtii, commonly known as the Humboldt tomato, is a wild relative of the domesticated tomato.

It is native to the Andes region of South America, specifically Peru and Ecuador. This variety is characterized by its small, yellow or orange fruits that are typically less than 2 centimeters in diameter. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, and it produces small, yellow flowers.

It is valued for its genetic diversity and potential use in breeding programs to improve cultivated tomato varieties.
