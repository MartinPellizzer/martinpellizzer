Lycium berlandieri var. longiflorum

Lycium berlandieri var. longiflorum, commonly known as the longflower lycium, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its woody stems, small evergreen leaves, and clusters of yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces red berries that are edible, though they are generally not cultivated for commercial use. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
