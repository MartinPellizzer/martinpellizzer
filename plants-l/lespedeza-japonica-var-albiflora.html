Lespedeza japonica var. albiflora

Lespedeza japonica var. albiflora, commonly known as whiteflower Japanese lespeadeza, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces clusters of white to pale yellow flowers, which are borne in elongated racemes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value and nitrogen-fixing ability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and ecological restoration projects.
