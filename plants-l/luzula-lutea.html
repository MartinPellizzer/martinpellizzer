Luzula lutea

Luzula lutea, commonly known as yellow woodrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, commonly known as the grass family, and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bright green, with a slight yellowish tinge, especially on the undersides. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Luzula lutea is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes.
