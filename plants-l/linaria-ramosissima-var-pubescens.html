Linaria ramosissima var. pubescens

Linaria ramosissima var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy toadflax, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branched stems covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces bright yellow, snapdragon-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and also covered in a fuzzy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
