Linconia deusta

Linconia deusta, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds, often used in traditional medicine. Its roots are also valued for their nitrogen-fixing properties, contributing to soil fertility.

Linconia deusta is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and riverbanks.
