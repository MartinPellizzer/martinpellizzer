Lagochilus lorestanicus

Lagochilus lorestanicus, commonly known as the Lorestan hyssop, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square, hairy stems and small, tubular flowers that typically bloom in shades of blue or purple. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, often growing at high altitudes.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
