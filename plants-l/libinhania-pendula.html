Libinhania pendula

Libinhania pendula, commonly known as the hanging sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves that are often reddish in color, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are arranged in spike-like clusters. Libinhania pendula is characterized by its drooping inflorescence, which gives it its common name.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
