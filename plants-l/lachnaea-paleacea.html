Lachnaea paleacea

Lachnaea paleacea, commonly known as the pale spurge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in a cup-like structure called a cyathium. It is often found in dry, disturbed soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Lachnaea paleacea is known for its latex-like sap, which can be irritating to the skin.
