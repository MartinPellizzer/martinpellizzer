Lolium remotum

Lolium remotum, commonly known as the slender ryegrass, is a cool-season grass native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny florets.

Lolium remotum is often used in forage and erosion control due to its hardiness and adaptability.
