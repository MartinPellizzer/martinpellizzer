Leucopsis diffusa

Leucopsis diffusa, commonly known as the silverleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its leaves are arranged in a basal rosette, and the stems are upright with a rough texture.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal use.
