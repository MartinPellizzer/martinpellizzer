Lespedeza angustifolia

Lespedeza angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf clover, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and disturbed sites, thriving in well-drained soils. It is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment and as a forage plant.

Its delicate appearance and ecological benefits make it a notable species in native plant gardens.
