Laurus nobilis var. undulata

Laurus nobilis var. undulata, commonly known as the undulating laurel, is a variant of the sweet bay laurel.

It is characterized by its distinctive, wavy-edged leaves that give it a unique appearance. The plant is evergreen, with a dense, bushy growth habit that makes it suitable for ornamental purposes. Its leaves are aromatic, similar to the standard laurel, and are often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a versatile addition to gardens and landscapes.
