Lepanthes multiflora

Lepanthes multiflora, commonly known as the many-flowered slipper orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers that often feature intricate patterns and vibrant colors. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as forest understories, where it relies on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. Its flowers are usually small to medium in size and arranged in clusters, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Lepanthes multiflora is valued in horticulture for its beauty and adaptability to cultivated conditions.
