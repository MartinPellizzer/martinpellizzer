Litsea sandakanensis

Litsea sandakanensis, commonly known as the Bornean litsea, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and avocado trees, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill dipterocarp forests. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and have a distinct, spicy scent when crushed.

The fruit is a small, round drupe that ripens to a dark red or black color, providing food for various wildlife species.
