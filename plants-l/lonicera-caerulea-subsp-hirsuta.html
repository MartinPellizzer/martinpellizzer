Lonicera caerulea subsp. hirsuta

Lonicera caerulea subsp.

hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia. It features slender, often hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that are typically yellow or orange in color. These berries are rich in antioxidants and are used in traditional foods and herbal remedies.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.
