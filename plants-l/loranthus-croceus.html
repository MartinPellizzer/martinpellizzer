Loranthus croceus

Loranthus croceus, commonly known as the yellow mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to host trees such as eucalyptus and acacia.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using the light it captures. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. Its berries are orange-red and contain sticky seeds that adhere to the bark of host trees, facilitating propagation.

This species is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its host ecosystems.
