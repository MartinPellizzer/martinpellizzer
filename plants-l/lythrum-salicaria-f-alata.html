Lythrum salicaria f. alata

Lythrum salicaria f. alata, commonly known as the scarlet rosemallow, is a striking variant of the common loosestrife.

This plant is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in spikes during the summer months. The foliage is typically lance-shaped and has a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and marshy areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, native plants.
