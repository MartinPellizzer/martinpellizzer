Leptodactylon tenuilobum

Leptodactylon tenuilobum, commonly known as the narrow-lobed fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The leaves are usually slender and have finely divided, lance-shaped lobes, giving the plant a refined appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
