Loasa sclareifolia var. brachycarpa

Loasa sclareifolia var. brachycarpa, commonly known as the narrow-fruit loasa, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The leaves are typically alternate, ovate, and covered with a fine pubescence. The fruit is a capsule that is shorter and more compact compared to other varieties of Loasa.

This species is often found in moist, temperate forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
