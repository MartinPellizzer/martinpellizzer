Leucanthemum vulgare subsp. leucolepis

Leucanthemum vulgare subsp.

leucolepis, commonly known as white marguerite, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its white or pale yellow flowers with a central disc of darker florets.

The plant typically grows in meadows, woodland edges, and rocky soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are basal, spoon-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in gardens.
