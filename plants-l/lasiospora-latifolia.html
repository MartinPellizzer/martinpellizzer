Lasiospora latifolia

Lasiospora latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved lasiospora, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and role in local ecosystems.
