Lasiocladus linearifolius

Lasiocladus linearifolius, commonly known as the linear-leaved cushion plant, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by small, dry fruits. It is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions, including high temperatures and low rainfall.

Lasiocladus linearifolius plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various desert organisms.
