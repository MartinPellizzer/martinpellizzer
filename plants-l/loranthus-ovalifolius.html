Loranthus ovalifolius

Loranthus ovalifolius, commonly known as the oval-leaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of host trees, primarily in the genus Eucalyptus.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while also producing its own carbohydrates through photosynthesis using the light it captures. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are often visited by insects. Its fruit is a small, fleshy capsule that contains numerous seeds, which are dispersed by birds.

This species plays a significant ecological role in its native habitats by influencing host tree growth and providing food for various wildlife species.
