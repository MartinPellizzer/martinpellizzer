Luzula patagonica

Luzula patagonica, commonly known as Patagonian woodrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, which includes grasses and sedges. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky areas. It has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters.
