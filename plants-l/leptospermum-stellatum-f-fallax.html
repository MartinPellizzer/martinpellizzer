Leptospermum stellatum f. fallax

Leptospermum stellatum f. fallax, commonly known as the silver-leaved tea tree, is a rare and ornamental variant of the species.

It is characterized by its striking silvery-white undersides on the leaves, which give it a unique appearance. The plant is native to Australia and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils. It produces small, white flowers that bloom in spring, adding visual interest to gardens.

Due to its rarity and aesthetic appeal, it is often cultivated in specialized horticultural settings.
