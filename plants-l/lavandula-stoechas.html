Lavandula stoechas

Lavandula stoechas, commonly known as the Spanish lavender or the scented lavender, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking, spiky purple flower spikes that are often adorned with distinctive, fuzzy bracts. The plant has aromatic silver-gray foliage that is both ornamental and useful in aromatherapy. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its essential oils, extracted from the flowers and leaves, are widely used in perfumes, cosmetics, and medicinal applications.
