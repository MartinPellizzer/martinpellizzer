Lobivia pugionacantha subsp. rossii

Lobivia pugionacantha subsp.

rossii, commonly known as the Rosse's lobivia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its compact, rounded rosettes of blue-green, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax.

The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which emerge from the center of the rosette. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal water to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
