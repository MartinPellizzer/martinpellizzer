Lobelia hookeri

Lobelia hookeri, commonly known as Hooker's lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Lobelia hookeri is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
