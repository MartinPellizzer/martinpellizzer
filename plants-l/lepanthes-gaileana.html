Lepanthes gaileana

Lepanthes gaileana, commonly known as the Gailean orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in the summer. The plant has a short, stout rhizome and produces linear, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are usually pale green to white with intricate patterns, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a threatened species in its native range.
