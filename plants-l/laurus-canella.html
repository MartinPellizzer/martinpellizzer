Laurus canella

Laurus canella, commonly known as bay leaf or *Laurus nobilis*, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark green, leathery leaves that are widely used in cooking and traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark, fleshy berries. Its leaves contain essential oils, primarily cineole, which contribute to its distinctive scent and medicinal properties.

Laurus canella thrives in well-drained soil and is often cultivated for its ornamental and aromatic value.
