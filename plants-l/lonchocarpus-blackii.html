Lonchocarpus blackii

Lonchocarpus blackii, commonly known as the black bean tree, is a tropical leguminous plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves and showy, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several bean-like seeds, which are used in traditional medicine. Its roots and leaves contain alkaloids, making it an important species in ethnobotanical studies.

The plant is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in agricultural systems.
