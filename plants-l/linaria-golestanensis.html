Linaria golestanensis

Linaria golestanensis, commonly known as the Golestan scabious, is a flowering plant native to Iran.

It belongs to the plantain family, Amaranthaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellowish-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers are often clustered in spikes, and it produces small, dry fruits that contain seeds.

Linaria golestanensis is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
