Lacinaria pilosa

Lacinaria pilosa, commonly known as the hairy lacinaria, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry or semi-arid environments.

Lacinaria pilosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
