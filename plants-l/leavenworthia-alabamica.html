Leavenworthia alabamica

Leavenworthia alabamica, commonly known as the Alabama spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant features showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
