Linaria coutinhoi

Linaria coutinhoi, commonly known as the Portuguese toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the island of Madeira.

It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers have two lips, typical of the genus Linaria.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
