Lomagramma bipinnata

Lomagramma bipinnata, commonly known as the two-pinnate fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its intricate, bipinnate fronds that are divided into multiple pairs of pinnae. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it to the soil while absorbing nutrients.

Lomagramma bipinnata is valued for its ornamental foliage and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
