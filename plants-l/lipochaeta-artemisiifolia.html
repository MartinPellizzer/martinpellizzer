Lipochaeta artemisiifolia

Lipochaeta artemisiifolia, commonly known as field ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in late summer. It is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as fields and roadsides.

Lipochaeta artemisiifolia is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
