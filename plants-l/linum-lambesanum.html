Linum lambesanum

Linum lambesanum, commonly known as the Persian flax, is a flowering plant belonging to the Linaceae family.

It is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia, including parts of Turkey and the Mediterranean. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

Linum lambesanum is primarily cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in textile production.
