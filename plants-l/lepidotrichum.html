Lepidotrichum

Lepidotrichum, commonly known as the velvet fern, is a unique plant belonging to the family Lepidotrichaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage that gives it a soft, almost fuzzy appearance. The plant is native to South America, particularly in regions of Brazil and Paraguay. Lepidotrichum thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
