Lepidium persicum subsp. macrocarpum

Lepidium persicum subsp.

macrocarpum, commonly known as Persian cress, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes, and its leaves are typically lobed or divided.

The plant produces small, smooth, oval-shaped seeds that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
