Lepanthes barclaya

Lepanthes barclaya, commonly known as the Barclaya orchid, is a species native to Central America, particularly found in countries like Costa Rica and Panama.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers that typically feature a white or pale pink coloration. The plant grows as an epiphyte, often found on trees in humid tropical forests, where it absorbs moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Its botanical features include narrow, strap-like leaves and a compact growth habit, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation due to its ornamental appeal.
