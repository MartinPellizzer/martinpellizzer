Lacinaria scariosa var. squarrulosa

Lacinaria scariosa var. squarrulosa, commonly known as the rough lacinaria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, bract-like leaves that are often covered in a dry, scaly texture. The plant produces yellow to orange composite flowers, which are typically arranged in a dense, spherical inflorescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
