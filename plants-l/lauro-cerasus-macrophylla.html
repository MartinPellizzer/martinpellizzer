Lauro-cerasus macrophylla

Lauro-cerasus macrophylla, commonly known as the Japanese cherry laurel, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically elongated and have a waxy texture. The plant produces fragrant white or pink flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that are red when mature. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to a variety of soil types.

However, it can be toxic to humans and animals if ingested.
