Lobivia fungiflora

Lobivia fungiflora, commonly known as the "fungus-flowered lobivia," is a succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rounded shape and fleshy, ribbed stems. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are often white or pale yellow, which bloom in spring. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, a common adaptation to conserve water in dry environments.

Lobivia fungiflora is prized by collectors for its unique appearance and resilience in harsh conditions.
