Lobelia berlandieri subsp. berlandieri

Lobelia berlandieri subsp.

berlandieri, commonly known as the Texas bluebell, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its striking cobalt-blue, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or prairie habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.
