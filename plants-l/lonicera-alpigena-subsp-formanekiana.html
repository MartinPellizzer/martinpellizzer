Lonicera alpigena subsp. formanekiana

Lonicera alpigena subsp.

formanekiana, commonly known as the alpine honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and pairs of opposite, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
