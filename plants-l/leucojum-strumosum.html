Leucojum strumosum

Leucojum strumosum, commonly known as the nodding white squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, white flowers that droop downward. The plant is characterized by its fragrant blooms, which appear in late winter to early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shady garden settings.

Leucojum strumosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize over time.
