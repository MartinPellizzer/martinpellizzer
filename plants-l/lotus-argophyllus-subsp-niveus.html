Lotus argophyllus subsp. niveus

Lotus argophyllus subsp.

niveus, commonly known as the white lotus, is a species of aquatic plant native to parts of Asia. It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom during the summer months.

The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, while its roots are submerged, anchoring it in shallow waters. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments and is often found in ponds and lakes.

The white lotus is also valued for its ornamental beauty and has cultural significance in various regions.
