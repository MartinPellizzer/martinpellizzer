Loasa atriplicifolia

Loasa atriplicifolia, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a rough texture.

Loasa atriplicifolia produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences.
