Lobivia cinnabarina subsp. acanthoplegma

Lobivia cinnabarina subsp.

acanthoplegma, commonly known as the Red Lobivia, is a compact, spineless cactus native to the coastal regions of southern Africa. It features a short, cylindrical stem that is typically reddish-brown in color, with a dense covering of white, woolly hairs.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking contrast to its textured foliage. It is well adapted to arid conditions, requiring minimal water and full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
