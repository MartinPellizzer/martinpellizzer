Leucadendron barkerae

Leucadendron barkerae, commonly known as the silver tree, is a striking shrub or small tree native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which results from dense, fine hairs covering the leaves. The plant produces conical or cylindrical flower heads that range in color from yellow to red, depending on the species. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in coastal regions.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is a popular choice for gardens and conservation areas.
