Lithospermum hirsutissimum

Lithospermum hirsutissimum, commonly known as the hairy lithosperm, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from pale pink to red, typically blooming in late summer. Its root system is known for containing lithospermic acid, a compound with potential medicinal properties.

This species thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone-rich habitats.
