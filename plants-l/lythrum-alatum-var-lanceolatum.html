Lythrum alatum var. lanceolatum

Lythrum alatum var. lanceolatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bog whorlflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to wetland habitats in North America.

It belongs to the Lythraceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy pink to red flowers that bloom in whorls along the stems. The plant has linear, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators and wetland biodiversity.
