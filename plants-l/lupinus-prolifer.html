Lupinus prolifer

Lupinus prolifer, commonly known as the spreading lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and clusters of vibrant blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has palmately compound leaves with several oval-shaped leaflets. Lupinus prolifer thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

It also plays an important role in nitrogen fixation, enriching the soil for other plants.
