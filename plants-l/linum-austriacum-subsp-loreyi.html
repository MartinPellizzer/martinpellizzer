Linum austriacum subsp. loreyi

Linum austriacum subsp.

loreyi, commonly known as the Austrian flax, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central Europe. It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and small, blue to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. Its seeds are enclosed in a capsule and are adapted for wind dispersal. Linum austriacum subsp.

loreyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in textile production.
