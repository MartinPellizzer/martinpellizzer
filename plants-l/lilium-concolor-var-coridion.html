Lilium concolor var. coridion

Lilium concolor var. coridion, commonly known as the red-lilac or red lily, is a variant of the concolor lily native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking red to reddish-purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, while the stems are usually slender and upright.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
