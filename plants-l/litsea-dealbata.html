Litsea dealbata

Litsea dealbata, commonly known as the white-flowered litsea, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite, and often hairy leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are typically clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruits are small, spherical berries that turn from green to red when mature.

Litsea dealbata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its aromatic properties.
