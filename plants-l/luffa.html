Luffa

Luffa, commonly known as sponge gourd, is a tropical vine belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its hollow, fibrous fruit that matures into a spongy structure. The plant produces large, lobed leaves and yellow flowers, and it thrives in warm, humid climates. The fruit is often used in cooking or as a natural sponge after drying.

Its rapid growth and unique texture make it a distinctive and useful plant in both agriculture and household applications.
