Lycopodium poseidonis

Lycopodium poseidonis, commonly known as the sea fern, is a marine plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is a type of clubmoss that thrives in coastal environments, particularly in intertidal zones. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its branching, leaf-like structures that resemble ferns. It plays a vital role in stabilizing coastal sediments and providing habitat for small marine organisms.

Lycopodium poseidonis is adapted to withstand saltwater exposure and fluctuating tidal conditions.
