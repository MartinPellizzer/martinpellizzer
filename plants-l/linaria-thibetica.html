Linaria thibetica

Linaria thibetica, commonly known as the Tibetan toadflax, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of Asia, including Tibet, Nepal, and Bhutan.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool and moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, while its flowers have a distinctive two-lipped structure.

Linaria thibetica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
