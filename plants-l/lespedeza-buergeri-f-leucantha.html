Lespedeza buergeri f. leucantha

Lespedeza buergeri f. leucantha, commonly known as white lespedeza, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered white to pale pink flowers. The leaves are alternate, pinnate, and consist of several pairs of oval-shaped leaflets. This variety is native to East Asia and is often found in open woodlands and disturbed soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
