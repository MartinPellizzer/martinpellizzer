Lepidium pumilum

Lepidium pumilum, commonly known as dwarf cress, is a small annual herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions. The plant features slender, branching stems covered with small, alternate leaves that are usually hairy. Its flowers are small, white or greenish-yellow, and arranged in clusters, producing tiny, edible seeds.

Lepidium pumilum is part of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other members like mustard and cabbage.
