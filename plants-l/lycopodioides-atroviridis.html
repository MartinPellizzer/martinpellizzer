Lycopodioides atroviridis

Lycopodioides atroviridis, commonly known as the black moss, is a species of clubmoss belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. The plant has a dense, mat-like growth habit with slender, thread-like stems covered in tiny, scale-like leaves. It produces spores in small, cone-like structures called strobili, which are located at the tips of the stems.

Lycopodioides atroviridis plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
