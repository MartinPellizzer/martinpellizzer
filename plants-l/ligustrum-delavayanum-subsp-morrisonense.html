Ligustrum delavayanum subsp. morrisonense

Ligustrum delavayanum subsp.

morrisonense, commonly known as the Morrison's privet, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy.

The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that are followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
