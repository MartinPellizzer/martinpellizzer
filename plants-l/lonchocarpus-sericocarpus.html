Lonchocarpus sericocarpus

Lonchocarpus sericocarpus, commonly known as the silky pod senna, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive pod-like fruits. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by elongated, silky-hairy pods that contain several seeds. It is often found in wet tropical forests and is adapted to thrive in humid, nutrient-rich soils.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
