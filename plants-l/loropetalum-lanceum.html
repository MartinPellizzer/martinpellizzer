Loropetalum lanceum

Loropetalum lanceum, commonly known as the lanceleaf loropetalum, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia, particularly China.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often a vibrant green, turning reddish in the fall. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its compact growth habit and seasonal color changes make it a valuable addition to gardens and landscapes.
