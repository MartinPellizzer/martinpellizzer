Litsea elongata var. acutivena

Litsea elongata var. acutivena, commonly known as the narrow-leafed litsea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of China, Vietnam, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often glossy and have a strong aromatic scent. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences.

The fruit is a small, woody drupe that contains a single seed, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
