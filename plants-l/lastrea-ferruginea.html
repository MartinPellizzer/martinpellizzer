Lastrea ferruginea

Lastrea ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty lastrea, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a reddish or rust-colored coating. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
