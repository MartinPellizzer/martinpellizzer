Leucopogon gibbosus

Leucopogon gibbosus, commonly known as the globular heath, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, spherical shape formed by numerous small, leathery leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
