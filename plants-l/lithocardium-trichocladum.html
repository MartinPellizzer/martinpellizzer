Lithocardium trichocladum

Lithocardium trichocladum, commonly known as the hairy leaf lithocardium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, hairy structures, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale pink flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or sandy habitats.

Lithocardium trichocladum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
