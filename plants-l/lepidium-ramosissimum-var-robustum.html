Lepidium ramosissimum var. robustum

Lepidium ramosissimum var. robustum, commonly known as the California wallflower, is a herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its branched stems and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a popular choice for native landscaping.
