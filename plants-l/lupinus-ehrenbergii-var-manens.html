Lupinus ehrenbergii var. manens

Lupinus ehrenbergii var. manens, commonly known as the manzanita lupine, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of blue or purple flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats, making it well adapted to arid environments.
