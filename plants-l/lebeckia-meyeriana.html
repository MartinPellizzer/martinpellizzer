Lebeckia meyeriana

Lebeckia meyeriana, commonly known as the Meyer's lebeckia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like appearance with yellow disc florets surrounded by white ray florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It produces small, cylindrical fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Lebeckia meyeriana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
