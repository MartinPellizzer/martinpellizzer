Limnophila myriophylloides

Limnophila myriophylloides, commonly known as the broadleaf water plantain, is a aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and marshes. The plant has broad, ribbon-like leaves that float on the water surface or extend underwater. Its flowering stems produce small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

Limnophila myriophylloides plays a role in water filtration and provides habitat for aquatic organisms.
