Lippia aegyptiaca

Lippia aegyptiaca, commonly known as Egyptian thyme, is a flowering plant native to Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, four-lobed fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Lippia aegyptiaca is also used in traditional medicine for its purported antimicrobial and antioxidant properties.
