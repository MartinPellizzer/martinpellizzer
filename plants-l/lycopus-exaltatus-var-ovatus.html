Lycopus exaltatus var. ovatus

Lycopus exaltatus var. ovatus, commonly known as blueweed or blue false nettle, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a bluish-green hue. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pink to purple, and it produces small, round berries that are edible but have a slightly bitter taste.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
