Lightfootia rupestris

Lightfootia rupestris, commonly known as the rock daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features slender, upright stems and produces clusters of small, daisy-like white flowers with yellow centers. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Lightfootia rupestris is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its hardy nature and ornamental appeal.
