Lecanorchis japonica f. lutea

Lecanorchis japonica f. lutea, commonly known as the yellow lady's tresses, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and long, spirally arranged inflorescences. The flowers are typically yellow or orange, with a distinctive three-lobed lip that is often adorned with intricate patterns. This orchid is native to parts of East Asia, including Japan, and thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, fragrant blooms.
