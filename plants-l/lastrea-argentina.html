Lastrea argentina

Lastrea argentina, commonly known as the Argentine lastrea, is a succulent plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family, which is characterized by its fleshy leaves and adaptations to arid environments. The plant typically features cylindrical or club-shaped stems with small, spiny leaves that help reduce water loss. It produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the warmer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and as a houseplant.
