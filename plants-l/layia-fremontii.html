Layia fremontii

Layia fremontii, commonly known as the Fremont's golden aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has narrow, gray-green leaves and a compact growth habit.

Layia fremontii is often found in areas with well-drained soils and full sun exposure.
