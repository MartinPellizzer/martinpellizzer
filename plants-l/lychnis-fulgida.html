Lychnis fulgida

Lychnis fulgida, commonly known as the fireweed or scarlet pimpernel, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is often found in temperate climates. Its leaves are ovate and hairy, with a distinctive reddish or purple coloration on the underside.

Lychnis fulgida is a short-lived perennial that thrives in full sun and well-drained soil.
