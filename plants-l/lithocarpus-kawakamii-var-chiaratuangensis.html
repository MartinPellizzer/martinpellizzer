Lithocarpus kawakamii var. chiaratuangensis

Lithocarpus kawakamii var. chiaratuangensis, commonly known as the Chiaratuangensis stone oak, is a variant of the stone oak genus native to the Philippines.

It is a deciduous evergreen tree characterized by its thick, leathery leaves and hard, woody fruits. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils in tropical and subtropical climates. It is often found in lowland to mid-elevation forests, contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitat.

The species is valued for its durable wood and ecological significance in forest ecosystems.
