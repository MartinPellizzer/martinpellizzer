Lysimachia phyllocephala var. polycephala

Lysimachia phyllocephala var. polycephala, commonly known as the clustered yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial with slender, upright stems that bear small, bright yellow flowers clustered in dense inflorescences. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
