Linaria concolor f. serpens

Linaria concolor f. serpens, commonly known as the snake's head flower, is a compact, trailing plant characterized by its bright orange to yellow flowers that resemble small snakes.

It is a succulent perennial native to southern Europe and western Asia, thriving in well-drained soil and full sun. The plant features fleshy, opposite leaves that are often tinged with red or purple, adding visual interest. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation in rock gardens or as a container plant.
