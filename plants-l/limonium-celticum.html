Limonium celticum

Limonium celticum, commonly known as the Scottish sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of Scotland and northern England.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking blue to purple flower heads that resemble small holly berries. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in maritime environments. Limonium celticum produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
