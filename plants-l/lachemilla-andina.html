Lachemilla andina

Lachemilla andina, commonly known as Andean stonecrop, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Andes Mountains.

It features fleshy, succulent leaves that are typically gray-green in color, adapted to retain moisture in its high-altitude environment. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

Lachemilla andina is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
