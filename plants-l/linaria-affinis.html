Linaria affinis

Linaria affinis, commonly known as the narrowleaf toadflax, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The plant produces bright yellow or orange flowers that are clustered in racemes, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically narrow and linear, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Linaria affinis is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.
