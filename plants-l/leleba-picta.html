Leleba picta

Leleba picta, commonly known as the painted bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that come in shades of blue, purple, and white, often with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, providing a textured appearance.

Leleba picta is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its cultural significance in local communities.
