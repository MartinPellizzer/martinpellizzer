Leptochilus wallii

Leptochilus wallii, commonly known as the Wall's leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its strap-shaped, grass-like leaves that form a dense rosette. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in a spike during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
