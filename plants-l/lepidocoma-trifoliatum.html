Lepidocoma trifoliatum

Lepidocoma trifoliatum, commonly known as the three-leaved lichen, is a species of lichen that exhibits a unique symbiotic relationship between a fungus and an alga.

It is characterized by its foliose structure, which consists of leaf-like lobes that are usually green or yellowish in color. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices, where it can absorb moisture and nutrients from the air. Its thallus, the composite body of the lichen, is often covered in small, rounded lobes that help increase surface area for photosynthesis.

Lepidocoma trifoliatum plays an important ecological role in nutrient cycling and serves as a habitat for various microorganisms.
