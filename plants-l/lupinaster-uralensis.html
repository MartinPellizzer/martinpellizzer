Lupinaster uralensis

Lupinaster uralensis, commonly known as the Siberian lupin, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the cold climates of Siberia and Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and spherical, yellowish flower clusters. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it valuable for soil enrichment. Lupinaster uralensis thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in steppe and forest-steppe ecosystems.

Its hardy nature allows it to survive in harsh, temperate environments.
