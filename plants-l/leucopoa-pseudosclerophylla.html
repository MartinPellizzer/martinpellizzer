Leucopoa pseudosclerophylla

Leucopoa pseudosclerophylla, commonly known as the coastal leucopoa, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale blue flowers during the spring and summer seasons. It is adapted to sandy or rocky soils and thrives in coastal environments with high salinity and strong winds.

Due to its unique ecological role, it is often found in coastal dunes and is an important component of the local flora.
