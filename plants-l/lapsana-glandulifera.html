Lapsana glandulifera

Lapsana glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and linear, hairy leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is found in various regions across Europe and Asia. It produces small, glandular-tipped seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Lapsana glandulifera is often considered a weed but has historical uses in herbal medicine.
