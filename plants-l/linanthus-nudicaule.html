Linanthus nudicaule

Linanthus nudicaule, commonly known as the naked-stem flax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Linaceae family, which includes other flax species. The plant is characterized by its slender, often hairless stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. It produces clusters of tiny, pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer.

Linanthus nudicaule is adapted to arid environments and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
