Lupinus hirsutus

Lupinus hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy lupine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces showy, pea-like flowers in shades of purple, pink, or white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its root system forms nodules with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, contributing to soil fertility.

Lupinus hirsutus is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to a variety of environmental conditions.
