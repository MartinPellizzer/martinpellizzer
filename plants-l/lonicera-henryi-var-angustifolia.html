Lonicera henryi var. angustifolia

Lonicera henryi var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy underneath. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, blooming in late spring. Its berries are small, dark red, and edible, though they are not commonly consumed by humans.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
