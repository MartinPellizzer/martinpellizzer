Lobivia jajoana var. fleischeriana

Lobivia jajoana var. fleischeriana, commonly known as the Fleischer's Lobivia, is a compact, low-growing succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It features fleshy, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are typically small and cylindrical, reducing water loss through transpiration.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ease of care, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor collections.
