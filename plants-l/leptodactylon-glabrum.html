Leptodactylon glabrum

Leptodactylon glabrum, commonly known as the smooth leathery fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery fronds that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant has a creeping rhizome that allows it to spread across the forest floor. Its leaves are bipinnate, with small, rounded pinnae that give it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
