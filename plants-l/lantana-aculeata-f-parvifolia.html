Lantana aculeata f. parvifolia

Lantana aculeata f. parvifolia, commonly known as the dwarf lantana, is a compact shrub native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to yellow, red, and purple, depending on the variety. The plant has hairy, opposite leaves that are typically oval to lance-shaped. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens, though it can be invasive in some areas.
