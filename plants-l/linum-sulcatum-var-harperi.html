Linum sulcatum var. harperi

Linum sulcatum var. harperi, commonly known as the Harp's flax, is a rare variant of the flax plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, blue to purple flowers. The leaves are linear and often have a slightly serrated edge, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is typically found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
