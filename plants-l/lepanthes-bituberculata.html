Lepanthes bituberculata

Lepanthes bituberculata, commonly known as the two-lobed orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant features delicate, greenish-white flowers with a distinctively shaped lip. Its roots are often clustered and adapted to absorb moisture from the forest floor.

Lepanthes bituberculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
