Lepanthes nanegalensis

Lepanthes nanegalensis, commonly known as the Ecuadorian violet, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of Ecuador.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid with delicate, pale green to white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a smooth texture, and it produces a single flower per stem.

Lepanthes nanegalensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
