Leontopodium villosum

Leontopodium villosum, commonly known as the "Edelweiss," is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, white, woolly foliage and showy, yellow-centered flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in rocky, high-altitude environments where it forms compact, cushion-like growths. Its unique appearance and habitat make it a symbol of resilience and alpine beauty.

Due to its limited distribution and sensitivity to environmental changes, it is also a protected species in many regions.
