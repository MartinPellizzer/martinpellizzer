Lazarolus

Lazarolus, commonly known as the ghost plant, is a rare and enigmatic species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its translucent, waxy leaves that appear almost ethereal in appearance, often resembling a ghostly outline against the backdrop of its rocky habitat. The plant produces small, pale blue flowers during the brief summer months, which are pollinated by specific species of moths. Lazarolus thrives in cool, moist environments with minimal sunlight, making it a challenging plant to cultivate outside its natural range.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a botanical curiosity and a subject of scientific interest.
