Lyroglossa bicolor

Lyroglossa bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored lyreleaf, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive lyre-shaped leaves that are typically green on the upper surface and silver-white on the underside. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the spring and summer months. It is native to parts of North America and thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
