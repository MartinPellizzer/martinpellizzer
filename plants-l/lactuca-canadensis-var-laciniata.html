Lactuca canadensis var. laciniata

Lactuca canadensis var. laciniata, commonly known as the cutleaf lettuce, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It features a rosette of large, deeply lobed, and often toothed leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in a loose cluster, which are followed by slender, cylindrical seed pods. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
