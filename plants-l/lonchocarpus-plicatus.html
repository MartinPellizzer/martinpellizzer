Lonchocarpus plicatus

Lonchocarpus plicatus, commonly known as the zigzag senna, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves with a zigzag pattern along the petiole. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by distinctive, inflated pods that contain several seeds. It is often found in moist, disturbed habitats such as riverbanks and forest edges.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
