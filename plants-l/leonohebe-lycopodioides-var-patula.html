Leonohebe lycopodioides var. patula

Leonohebe lycopodioides var. patula, commonly known as the Patula clubmoss, is a low-growing evergreen plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae and is characterized by its dense, compact clusters of small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces spore-bearing cones, which are typically clustered at the base of the foliage. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
