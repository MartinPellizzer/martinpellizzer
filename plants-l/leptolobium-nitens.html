Leptolobium nitens

Leptolobium nitens, commonly known as the slender false indigo, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and cylindrical, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. Its flowers are usually pink to purple in color and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Leptolobium nitens is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
