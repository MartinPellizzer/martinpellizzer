Laurus macrophylla

Laurus macrophylla, commonly known as bay laurel, is a broadleaf evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dark green, glossy, elliptical leaves that are aromatic and often used in cooking. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by black, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for both ornamental and culinary purposes.

Its historical significance dates back to ancient civilizations, where it was revered for its fragrance and symbolic value.
