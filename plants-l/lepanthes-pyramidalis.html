Lepanthes pyramidalis

Lepanthes pyramidalis, commonly known as the Pyramidal Orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, pyramidal-shaped flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. The plant has a compact growth habit with a short, thick rhizome and is often found in shaded, humid environments. Its flowers bloom during the rainy season, attracting various pollinators such as bees and moths.

Lepanthes pyramidalis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
