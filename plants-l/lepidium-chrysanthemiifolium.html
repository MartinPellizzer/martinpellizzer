Lepidium chrysanthemiifolium

Lepidium chrysanthemiifolium, commonly known as yellow rocket, is a herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many cruciferous plants. The plant features bright yellow flowers that resemble those of mustard, and its leaves are pinnately divided with a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a food source for various insects and its potential ecological benefits in wetland restoration.
