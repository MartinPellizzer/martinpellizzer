Ligustrum longipedicellatum

Ligustrum longipedicellatum, commonly known as the long-pedicellate privet, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by dark blue berries. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its dense foliage and adaptability to various soil types.

Ligustrum longipedicellatum is also valued for its use in hedges and as a landscape plant in tropical and subtropical regions.
