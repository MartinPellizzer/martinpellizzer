Leipoldtia jacobseniana

Leipoldtia jacobseniana, commonly known as the Jacobsema lily, is a striking perennial plant native to the Western Cape of South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically blooms in late winter to early spring, adding a unique ornamental value to gardens. Its leaves are long, narrow, and grass-like, often forming a basal rosette.

Leipoldtia jacobseniana prefers well-drained, sandy soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
