Lycopodioides pringlei

Lycopodioides pringlei, commonly known as the Pringle's clubmoss, is a species of lycophyte belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and a distinctive, often branched sporophyte structure. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures, including microsporangia and megasporangia, are located on the sporophyte, which is the dominant phase in its life cycle.

Lycopodioides pringlei plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
