Lomatium utriculatum

Lomatium utriculatum, commonly known as the bladder lomatium, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lobed, hairy leaves and a tall, branching stem that produces small, yellowish flowers. The plant thrives in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its unique feature is the presence of bladder-like structures on its stems, which are thought to aid in water retention.

Lomatium utriculatum is also known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
