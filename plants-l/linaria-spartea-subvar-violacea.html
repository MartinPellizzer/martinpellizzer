Linaria spartea subvar. violacea

Linaria spartea subvar. violacea, commonly known as the violet-flowered sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces spikes of violet to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

This subvariety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
