Lepanthes marielana

Lepanthes marielana, commonly known as the Marielana orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually small, with intricate patterns and a variety of colors, including white, pink, and purple.

Lepanthes marielana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
