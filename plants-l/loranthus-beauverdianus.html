Loranthus beauverdianus

Loranthus beauverdianus, commonly known as the red mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on a variety of host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while performing photosynthesis using its own leaves. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are often visited by insects for pollination. Its berries are typically red or orange and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This species plays an important ecological role in its habitat by supporting biodiversity through its interactions with host trees and wildlife.
