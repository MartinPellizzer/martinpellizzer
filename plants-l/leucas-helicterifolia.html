Leucas helicterifolia

Leucas helicterifolia, commonly known as the Indian helicteris, is a shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

The flowers, which are usually pale purple or white, attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
