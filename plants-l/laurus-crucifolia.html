Laurus crucifolia

Laurus crucifolia, commonly known as the cross-leaved laurel, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically white or pale yellow, and it produces small, round berries.

The plant prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.
