Lepanthes tectorum

Lepanthes tectorum, commonly known as the roof orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that grows on trees in tropical rainforests. The plant has delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. Lepanthes tectorum is known for its unique floral structure, which includes a spurred lip that aids in pollination.

Due to its habitat and appearance, it is often sought after by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists.
