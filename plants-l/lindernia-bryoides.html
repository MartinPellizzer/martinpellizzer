Lindernia bryoides

Lindernia bryoides, commonly known as the mossy lindernia, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-like growth habit. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically lavender to purple in color, arranged in clusters along the stems. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in a fine pubescence.

Lindernia bryoides thrives in moist, shaded environments, making it a common sight in woodland areas and along stream banks.
