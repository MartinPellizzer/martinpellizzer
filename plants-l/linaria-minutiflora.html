Linaria minutiflora

Linaria minutiflora, commonly known as the dwarf toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial plant that typically reaches heights of less than 10 centimeters. The plant features bright yellow flowers with four petals, arranged in racemes, and possesses a distinctive spurred petal at the base of each bloom. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and often covered in fine hairs.

Linaria minutiflora thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and dry fields.
