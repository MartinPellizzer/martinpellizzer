Loranthus quandang var. amplexifolius

Loranthus quandang var. amplexifolius, commonly known as the broad-leafed mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to host trees for water and nutrients.

It belongs to the Loranthaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color. Its berries are usually red or yellow and serve as a food source for various bird species.

This variety is native to parts of Asia and is often found growing on the branches of hardwood trees.
