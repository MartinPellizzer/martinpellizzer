Lycium potaninii

Lycium potaninii, commonly known as the Tibetan boxthorn, is a shrub native to the high altitudes of the Tibetan Plateau.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces red berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in harsh, arid environments and is adapted to cold temperatures.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in alpine regions.
