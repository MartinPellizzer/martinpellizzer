Leucocasia gigantea

Leucocasia gigantea, commonly known as the giant leucocasia, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, waxy, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to several feet in diameter. The plant produces clusters of small, pale green flowers that are often overlooked due to their subtle appearance. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Despite its ornamental appeal, it is not commonly cultivated due to its specific care requirements.
