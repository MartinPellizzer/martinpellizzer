Liabum lechleri

Liabum lechleri, commonly known as the caucau or rubber tree, is a tropical plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces a milky latex sap that has been historically used for rubber production. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments with well-drained soil. Its bark and latex contain compounds with potential medicinal properties.

However, due to overharvesting and habitat loss, it is now considered endangered in the wild.
