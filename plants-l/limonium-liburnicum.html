Limonium liburnicum

Limonium liburnicum, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and silver-gray foliage, which are adapted to coastal environments. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sunlight. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Limonium liburnicum is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
