Lactuca tricostata

Lactuca tricostata, commonly known as the three-ribbed lettuce, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family.

It is native to southern Europe and western Asia and is characterized by its hairy, lobed leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in fields and along roadsides. It is a biennial plant, meaning it completes its life cycle over two growing seasons.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
