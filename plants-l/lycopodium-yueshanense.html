Lycopodium yueshanense

Lycopodium yueshanense, commonly known as the Yueshan clubmoss, is a rare and endangered species native to China.

It belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae and is characterized by its spore-bearing strobila, which is typically clustered at the tips of its stems. The plant has a creeping growth habit with small, scale-like leaves that form a dense mat. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in mountainous regions with acidic soil.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
