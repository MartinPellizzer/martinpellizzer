Liliago ciliata

Liliago ciliata, commonly known as the fringed lily, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the family Liliaceae, which includes many well-known ornamental and medicinal plants. The plant features showy, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with delicate, thread-like petals. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a soft, velvety texture.

Liliago ciliata is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental qualities.
