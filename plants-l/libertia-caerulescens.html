Libertia caerulescens

Libertia caerulescens, commonly known as the blue libertia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features strap-like, grassy leaves that are often blue-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in various regions with a Mediterranean climate.
