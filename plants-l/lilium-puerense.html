Lilium puerense

Lilium puerense, commonly known as the Puerian lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan Province in China.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white, pink, and red. The plant produces bulbous roots and has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found at high elevations.

Lilium puerense is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine.
