Leptochloa floribunda

Leptochloa floribunda, commonly known as the flowering grass, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, narrow leaves. The plant produces compact inflorescences with numerous spikelets, each containing two to three florets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along streambanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
