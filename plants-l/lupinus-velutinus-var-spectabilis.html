Lupinus velutinus var. spectabilis

Lupinus velutinus var. spectabilis, commonly known as the showy lupine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and clusters of showy, pea-like flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets, and its root system includes nitrogen-fixing nodules. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for native plant gardens.

This species plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and enhancing biodiversity in its native habitat.
