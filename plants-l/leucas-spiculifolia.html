Leucas spiculifolia

Leucas spiculifolia, commonly known as the spiky leucas, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, particularly India and Nepal.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. It produces clusters of tiny, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer.

Leucas spiculifolia is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
