Lobivia rossii var. sanguinea

Lobivia rossii var. sanguinea, commonly known as the Crimson Lobivia, is a succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It features compact, cylindrical stems that are typically covered in a thick layer of white to grayish-blue wax. The plant produces vibrant red or pink flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are usually small, fleshy, and arranged in a rosette pattern.

It is well adapted to harsh, dry environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.
