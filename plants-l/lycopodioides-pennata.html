Lycopodioides pennata

Lycopodioides pennata, commonly known as the slender clubmoss, is a member of the Lycopodiaceae family.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. The plant features whorled, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair. Its reproductive structures are specialized spore-producing cones, which are clustered at the base of the plant.

Lycopodioides pennata plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
