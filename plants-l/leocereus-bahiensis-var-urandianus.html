Leocereus bahiensis var. urandianus

Leocereus bahiensis var. urandianus, commonly known as the Urandi Cereus, is a species of cactus native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that are typically covered in white to pale green spines. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are usually white or pale pink, blooming at night. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, requiring minimal water once established.

This variety is well-suited for arid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
