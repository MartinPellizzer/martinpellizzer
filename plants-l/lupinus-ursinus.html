Lupinus ursinus

Lupinus ursinus, commonly known as the bear clover, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant produces small, hairy pods that contain several seeds. Lupinus ursinus is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions.

It is a member of the legume family and has nitrogen-fixing abilities that benefit the surrounding soil.
