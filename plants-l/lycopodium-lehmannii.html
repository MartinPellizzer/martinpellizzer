Lycopodium lehmannii

Lycopodium lehmannii, commonly known as the clubmoss, is a vascular plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. The plant produces spore-bearing cones called strobili, which are clustered at the tips of its branches. Its leaves are spirally arranged and have a scale-like appearance, contributing to its distinctive look.

Lycopodium lehmannii is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and can thrive in containers or garden beds with proper care.
