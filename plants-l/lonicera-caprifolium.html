Lonicera caprifolium

Lonicera caprifolium, commonly known as honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe, western Asia, and northwestern Africa.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant produces red or orange berries that are edible for humans and birds. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture.

Lonicera caprifolium is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and can also be found in traditional herbal medicine.
