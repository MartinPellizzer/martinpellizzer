Luzula pallescens var. umbrosa

Luzula pallescens var. umbrosa, commonly known as the shadowy wood sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are usually bluish-green in color. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer, which are clustered in spike-like inflorescences.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting biodiversity in woodland habitats.
