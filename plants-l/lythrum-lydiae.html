Lythrum lydiae

Lythrum lydiae, commonly known as Lydia's lythrum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lythraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, pink to red flowers. The plant typically blooms in summer, producing clusters of small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Lythrum lydiae is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
