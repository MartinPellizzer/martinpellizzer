Lycopodium complanatum subsp. tristachyum

Lycopodium complanatum subsp.

tristachyum, commonly known as the flat mossy clubmoss, is a small, evergreen fern-like plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes.

The plant features slender, creeping stems that produce small, scale-like leaves. Its spore-bearing structures, called strobila, are clustered in tight, cone-like formations.

This subspecies is characterized by its low-growing habit and is often found in temperate regions across the northern hemisphere.
