Lycianthes crassipetala

Lycianthes crassipetala, commonly known as the thick-leaved nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, thick, ovate leaves that are usually pubescent. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange to red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along forest edges.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in tropical regions.
