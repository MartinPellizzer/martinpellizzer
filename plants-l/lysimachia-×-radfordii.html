Lysimachia × radfordii

Lysimachia × radfordii, commonly known as the Radford's chickweed, is a hybrid plant in the Primulaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with prostrate or ascending stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in early spring. Its leaves are usually opposite, ovate to elliptical in shape, and covered with fine hairs.

Lysimachia × radfordii is native to the southeastern United States and thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors.
