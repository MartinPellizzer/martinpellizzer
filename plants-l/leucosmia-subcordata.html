Leucosmia subcordata

Leucosmia subcordata, commonly known as the velvet orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has strap-like, dark green leaves that are often used in floral arrangements. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Leucosmia subcordata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
