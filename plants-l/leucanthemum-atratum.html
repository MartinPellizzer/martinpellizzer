Leucanthemum atratum

Leucanthemum atratum, commonly known as the black-eyed Susan, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow ray florets surrounding a deep black center, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It has lance-shaped leaves that are gray-green in color and are covered in fine hairs.

Leucanthemum atratum is a hardy perennial that is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
