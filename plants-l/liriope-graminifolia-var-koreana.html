Liriope graminifolia var. koreana

Liriope graminifolia var. koreana, commonly known as Korean lilyturf, is a perennial grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that form dense clumps, and it produces small, bell-shaped white or purple flowers in late summer. The plant is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments. It prefers moist, well-drained soil and is relatively low maintenance.

Its foliage remains attractive throughout the growing season, making it a popular choice for ground cover in gardens.
