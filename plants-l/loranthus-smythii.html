Loranthus smythii

Loranthus smythii, commonly known as the red mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily in the genus Eucalyptus.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while it provides some benefits by stimulating growth in the host. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red berries containing sticky seeds.

Its parasitic nature and unique ecological role make it an important species in certain Australian ecosystems.
