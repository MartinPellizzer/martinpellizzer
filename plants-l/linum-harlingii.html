Linum harlingii

Linum harlingii, commonly known as Harling's flax, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are slender and erect.

Harling's flax is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and along rocky outcrops.
