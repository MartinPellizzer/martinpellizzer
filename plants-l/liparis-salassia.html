Liparis salassia

Liparis salassia, commonly known as the Salassian orchid, is a species of orchid native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, grass-like foliage and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pale pink. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found growing in rocky or forested areas. Its root system is adapted to absorb nutrients from the thin soil of its habitat.

Liparis salassia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
