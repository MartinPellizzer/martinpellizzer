Lonchocarpus izabalanus

Lonchocarpus izabalanus, commonly known as the Izu balanus legume, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces distinctive yellow to orange flowers. Its root system is known for containing toxic alkaloids, which have been studied for potential medicinal properties.
