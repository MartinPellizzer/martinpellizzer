Lodhra leucantha

Lodhra leucantha, commonly known as white ash tree, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, alternate leaves. The tree produces small, white flowers that appear in clusters, followed by small, oval-shaped fruits. It is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional Ayurvedic practices.

The plant is also used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and seasonal changes.
