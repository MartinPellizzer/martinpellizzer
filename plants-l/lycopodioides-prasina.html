Lycopodioides prasina

Lycopodioides prasina, commonly known as the small clubmoss, is a member of the Lycopodiaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage.

It is a small, creeping plant that forms dense mats, typically growing in moist, shaded environments such as forests or rocky crevices. The plant features spirally arranged leaves that are often scale-like, and it produces spore-bearing cones rather than flowers. Lycopodioides prasina is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and its role in stabilizing soil in woodland ecosystems.

Its historical significance in paleobotany also highlights its evolutionary importance in the development of vascular plants.
