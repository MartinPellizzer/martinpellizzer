Lycium andersonii var. andersonii

Lycium andersonii var. andersonii, commonly known as the Anderson's boxthorn, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, orange-red berries that ripen in the fall. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along canyon walls. Its leaves are typically narrow and evergreen, providing year-round structure.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to stabilize soil in harsh landscapes.
