Lysimachia liukiuensis

Lysimachia liukiuensis, commonly known as the Ryukyu chickweed, is a flowering plant native to the Ryukyu Islands of Japan.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies in its native habitat.
