Lightfootia gracilis var. lavandulifolia

Lightfootia gracilis var. lavandulifolia, commonly known as the lavenderleaf lightfootia, is a rare and delicate plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and small, lance-shaped leaves that emit a subtle lavender scent. The plant produces tiny, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically white or pale pink, which bloom during the warm season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.
