Lycium schweinfurthii var. aschersonii

Lycium schweinfurthii var. aschersonii, commonly known as the Ascherson's boxthorn, is a shrub native to arid regions of North America.

It features small, spiny leaves and produces red berries that are edible for both humans and wildlife. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to drought conditions. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, adding a vibrant touch to its habitat.

This species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing food for various animals.
