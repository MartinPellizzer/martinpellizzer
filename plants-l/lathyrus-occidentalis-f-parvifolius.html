Lathyrus occidentalis f. parvifolius

Lathyrus occidentalis f. parvifolius, commonly known as the dwarf western peavine, is a compact variant of the western peavine.

It is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States, characterized by its small, trifoliate leaves and slender, climbing stems. The plant produces pea-like flowers in shades of purple or pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or open woodland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
