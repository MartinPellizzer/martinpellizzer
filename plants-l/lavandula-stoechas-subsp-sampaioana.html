Lavandula stoechas subsp. sampaioana

Lavandula stoechas subsp.

sampaioana, commonly known as the Portuguese lavender, is a subspecies native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its distinctive violet-blue flowers with prominent purple bracts that resemble small leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure. It has a compact, shrub-like growth habit with aromatic, gray-green foliage.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and herbal applications.
