Loranthus roseus

Loranthus roseus, commonly known as the rose mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to host trees such as eucalyptus and acacia.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using sunlight absorbed by its host's leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red, fleshy berries that contain the seeds. Its parasitic nature makes it an important component of ecological systems, influencing host tree health and biodiversity.

Loranthus roseus is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
