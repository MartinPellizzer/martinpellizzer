Loranthus sogerensis

Loranthus sogerensis, commonly known as the Soger's mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches itself to host trees, primarily in the genus Eucalyptus.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using light reflected from the host. The plant has small, scale-like leaves and produces white or yellowish flowers that attract insects for pollination. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that contains seeds covered in a sticky substance to aid in attachment to new hosts.

This species is native to parts of Australia and is of interest in ecological studies due to its unique parasitic relationships.
