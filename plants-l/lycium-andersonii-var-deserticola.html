Lycium andersonii var. deserticola

Lycium andersonii var. deserticola, commonly known as the desert lycium, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its small, spiny stems and yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces red berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and limited water availability.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for xeriscaping and ecological restoration projects in desert environments.
