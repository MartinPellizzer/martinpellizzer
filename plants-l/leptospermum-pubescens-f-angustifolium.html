Leptospermum pubescens f. angustifolium

Leptospermum pubescens f. angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved tea tree, is a variant of the tea tree species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.

This form is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to tolerate coastal conditions.
