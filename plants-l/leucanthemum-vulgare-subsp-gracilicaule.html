Leucanthemum vulgare subsp. gracilicaule

Leucanthemum vulgare subsp.

gracilicaule, commonly known as slenderleaf oxeye daisy, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features a basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces numerous white to pale yellow flowers with a prominent central disk, blooming from late spring to early autumn. It prefers full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This subspecies is characterized by its slender stems and is often found in grasslands and woodland edges.
