Lavoisiera gentianoides

Lavoisiera gentianoides, commonly known as the blue gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stem is usually unbranched and somewhat woody.

Lavoisiera gentianoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and unique foliage.
