Larnalles

Larnalles, commonly known as the "larnalles tree," is a rare and lesser-known plant species found in specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, waxy leaves that have a distinctive blue-green hue. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom during the dry season. Its fruits are hard, spiky pods that contain several seeds.

Due to habitat loss, the larnalles is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to survive.
