Lathyrus magellanicus var. heterocirrus

Lathyrus magellanicus var. heterocirrus, commonly known as the Magellanic vetch, is a flowering plant native to southern South America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open, grassy habitats. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

The plant is adapted to temperate climates and is often found in disturbed or marginal soils.
