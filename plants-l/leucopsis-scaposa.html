Leucopsis scaposa

Leucopsis scaposa, commonly known as the slender bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, branching stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Leucopsis scaposa is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
