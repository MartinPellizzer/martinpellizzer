Lysimachia congestiflora var. kwangtungensis

Lysimachia congestiflora var. kwangtungensis, commonly known as the Kwangtung yellow creeper, is a flowering plant native to southern China.

It is a perennial herb with trailing stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The plant produces clusters of small, golden-yellow flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a soft texture.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant blooms.
