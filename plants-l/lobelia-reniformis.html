Lobelia reniformis

Lobelia reniformis, commonly known as the kidneyleaf lobelia, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its kidney-shaped leaves and clusters of blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has small, hairy stems and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Lobelia reniformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
