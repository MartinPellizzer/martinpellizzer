Leucas stelligera

Leucas stelligera, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers and silver-gray foliage, which help it withstand salty sea air and strong winds. The plant has a compact growth habit and thrives in well-drained sandy or gravelly soils. Its flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Leucas stelligera is often used in coastal gardens for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize dunes.
