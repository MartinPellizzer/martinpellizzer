Lamourouxia longiflora var. hyssopifolia

Lamourouxia longiflora var. hyssopifolia, commonly known as the woolly bluebell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring, often in clusters at the ends of its stems. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long, slender flowers and drought tolerance.
