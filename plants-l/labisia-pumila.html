Labisia pumila

Labisia pumila, commonly known as the "stone root" or "hairy labisia," is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features small, opposite leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs, giving it a textured appearance. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, often with a yellowish or greenish hue.

Labisia pumila is known for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
