Lychniothyrsus hygrophilus

Lychniothyrsus hygrophilus, commonly known as the water hygrophil, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in marshy areas, characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy white or pale purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

It plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
