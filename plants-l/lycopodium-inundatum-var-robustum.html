Lycopodium inundatum var. robustum

Lycopodium inundatum var. robustum, commonly known as the giant clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems covered in small, scale-like leaves. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate forests and wetlands. Its sporophyte stage produces spores in specialized structures called sporangia, which are clustered in strobila.

The plant plays an important ecological role in nutrient cycling and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
