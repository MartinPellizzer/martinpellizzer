Lepidium hirtum subsp. calycotrichum

Lepidium hirtum subsp.

calycotrichum, commonly known as the hairy wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and ability to attract pollinators.
