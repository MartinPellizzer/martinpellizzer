Lobelia oxyphylla subsp. cacuminis

Lobelia oxyphylla subsp.

cacuminis, commonly known as the pink lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its striking pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.
