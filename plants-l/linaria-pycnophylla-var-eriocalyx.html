Linaria pycnophylla var. eriocalyx

Linaria pycnophylla var. eriocalyx, commonly known as the narrow-leaved toadflax, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are densely arranged along the stem. The plant produces clusters of yellow-orange flowers with two prominent lobes, typical of the Linaria genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
