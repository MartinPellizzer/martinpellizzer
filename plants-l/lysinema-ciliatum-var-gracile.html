Lysinema ciliatum var. gracile

Lysinema ciliatum var. gracile, commonly known as the slender ciliate lichen, is a foliose lichen that exhibits a distinct, leaf-like structure.

It typically grows on tree trunks and branches in moist, shaded environments, particularly in temperate regions. The thallus is pale to dark green, with a smooth or slightly wrinkled surface, and it often has a distinctive ciliate margin. It is characterized by its slow growth and ability to thrive in nutrient-poor conditions.

This lichen is often used in ecological studies to monitor air quality due to its sensitivity to pollution.
