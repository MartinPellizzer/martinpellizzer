Lindera strychnifolia var. velutina

Lindera strychnifolia var. velutina, commonly known as the velvet leaf lindera, is a variant of the lindera species native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and woodlands. Its small, yellowish-green flowers appear in the spring, followed by red berries that are edible for some bird species.

The plant has a woody stem and is part of the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees.
