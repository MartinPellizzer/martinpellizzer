Liparis casseabria

Liparis casseabria, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a rhizomatous root system and produces erect, leafy stems that support its showy inflorescences. Its flowers are typically clustered in racemes and feature three petals, with the lip being prominently marked.

Liparis casseabria thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
