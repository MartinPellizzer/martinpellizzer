Landolphia heudelotii

Landolphia heudelotii, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a climbing shrub belonging to the family Asclepiadaceae.

It is native to tropical Africa and is characterized by its large, showy, night-blooming flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. The plant has a woody stem with tendrils that help it climb and can reach heights of several meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a velvety texture.

The flowers emit a strong fragrance at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.
