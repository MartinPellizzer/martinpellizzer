Leontodon taraxacoides subsp. hispidus

Leontodon taraxacoides subsp.

hispidus, commonly known as the hairy hawk's-beard, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and rocky areas, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters.

The plant features a basal rosette of hairy, dark green leaves and produces yellow composite flowers with a distinctive, rough, and hairy involucre. Its flowers are arranged in a single head, with both ray and disk florets.

This subspecies is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
