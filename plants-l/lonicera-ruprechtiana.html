Lonicera ruprechtiana

Lonicera ruprechtiana, commonly known as the Ruprecht's honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy on the underside. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its berries are red or orange and contain small, edible seeds.

Lonicera ruprechtiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and wildlife benefits.
