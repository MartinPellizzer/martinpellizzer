Lapageria rosea var. rubra

Lapageria rosea var. rubra, commonly known as the crimson bell of Ecuador, is a striking evergreen shrub native to the Andean cloud forests of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its large, pendulous, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from deep red to orange-red, depending on the variety. The plant features glossy, dark green, leathery leaves that are opposite and elliptical in shape. It thrives in cool, humid environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
