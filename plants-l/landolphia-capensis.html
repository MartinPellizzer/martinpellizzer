Landolphia capensis

Landolphia capensis, commonly known as the African morning glory, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces berries that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for certain animals. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in woodland and coastal areas.

Landolphia capensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and traditional medicinal uses.
