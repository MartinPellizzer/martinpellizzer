Liparis merapiensis

Liparis merapiensis, commonly known as the Merapi orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Indonesian island of Java.

It is characterized by its delicate, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom during the rainy season. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky areas. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the surrounding soil.

Liparis merapiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
