Linaria cyparissias

Linaria cyparissias, commonly known as the upright toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and bright yellow, spurred flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in dry, rocky soils along coastal areas. It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Linaria cyparissias is also noted for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its drought tolerance.
