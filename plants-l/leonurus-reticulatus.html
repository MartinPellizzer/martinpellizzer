Leonurus reticulatus

Leonurus reticulatus, commonly known as the lesser hedgehog plant, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features distinctive, hairy, oval-shaped leaves arranged in a basal rosette, and its flowers are small, pink or white, and clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, often found in grassy areas and along roadsides.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
