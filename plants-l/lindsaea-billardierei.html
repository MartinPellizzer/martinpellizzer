Lindsaea billardierei

Lindsaea billardierei, commonly known as the slender lindsaea, is a species of epiphytic fern native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, linear leaves that grow in a rosette formation. The plant typically thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found growing on tree trunks or branches in the understory of rainforests. Its reproductive structures, including spore-producing sorus, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Lindsaea billardierei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
