Lonchocarpus punctatus

Lonchocarpus punctatus, commonly known as the scarlet bower vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers and distinctive, spiny pods that contain the seeds. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is known for its nitrogen-fixing abilities. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are often hairy and heart-shaped.

The plant is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.
