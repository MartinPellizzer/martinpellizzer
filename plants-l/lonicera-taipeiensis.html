Lonicera taipeiensis

Lonicera taipeiensis, commonly known as the Taipei honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 meters, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers, which range from white to yellowish-orange, attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The fruit is a small, red drupe that matures in the fall, contributing to its ecological role in supporting wildlife.
