Lobelia hypnodes

Lobelia hypnodes, commonly known as the narrow-leaf lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, woodland habitats. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers. It is often found in shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

The species is also used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to thrive in disturbed soils.
