Loranthus ituriensis var. coriaceifolius

Loranthus ituriensis var. coriaceifolius, commonly known as the Iuriensis mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily in the tropical rainforests of Central and West Africa.

It possesses small, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical in shape, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are often clustered together, followed by small, fleshy berries that serve as a food source for various birds and mammals. As a parasitic species, it derives some of its nutrients from the host plant, while also performing photosynthesis to some extent.

Its ecological role is significant in maintaining the balance of forest ecosystems by influencing host tree growth and providing sustenance for wildlife.
