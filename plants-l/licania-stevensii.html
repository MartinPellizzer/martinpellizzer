Licania stevensii

Licania stevensii, commonly known as the Brazilian evergreen tree, is a species native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is a large, deciduous tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, characterized by its thick, buttressed roots and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody, cylindrical fruits containing numerous seeds. Its bark is thick and flaky, often used in traditional medicine by local communities.

Licania stevensii plays a vital role in the ecosystem by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
