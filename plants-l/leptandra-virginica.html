Leptandra virginica

Leptandra virginica, commonly known as sweet pepperbush, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has opposite, elliptical leaves with serrated edges and produces small, red drupes in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.

Sweet pepperbush is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for pollinators and birds.
