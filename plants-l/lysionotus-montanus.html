Lysionotus montanus

Lysionotus montanus, commonly known as the mountain lythrum, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lythraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, pinkish-red flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in clusters at the ends of its stems.

Lysionotus montanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
