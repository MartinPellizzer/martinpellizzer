Loranthus glaucescens

Loranthus glaucescens, commonly known as the green mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily in the families Myrtaceae and Eucalyptaceae.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using the light it captures. The plant has small, scale-like leaves and produces white or pink flowers that are often visited by birds and insects. Its berries are typically red or yellow and contain seeds that are dispersed by animals.

This species is found in tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.
