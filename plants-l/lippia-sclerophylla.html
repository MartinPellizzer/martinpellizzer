Lippia sclerophylla

Lippia sclerophylla, commonly known as the silverleaf lippia, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, which bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Lippia sclerophylla thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
