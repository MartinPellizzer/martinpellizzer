Leontodon hispidus subsp. alpicola

Leontodon hispidus subsp.

alpicola, commonly known as the alpine hawk's-beard, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has rough, hairy leaves that are typically lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes.

Due to its ecological significance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in alpine gardens.
