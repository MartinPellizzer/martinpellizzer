Leptophytus leyseroides

Leptophytus leyseroides, commonly known as the Cape leopard's ear, is a small, evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, adding ornamental value to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique foliage and delicate flowers, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
