Leucanthemum armenum

Leucanthemum armenum, commonly known as Armenian white ragwort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disc florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun, often found in alpine or subalpine environments. Its bright yellow flowers are surrounded by white ray florets, giving it a striking appearance.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
