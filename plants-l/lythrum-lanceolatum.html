Lythrum lanceolatum

Lythrum lanceolatum, commonly known as narrow-leaved rosemary, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lythraceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, pale pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Lythrum lanceolatum is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and flowering display.
