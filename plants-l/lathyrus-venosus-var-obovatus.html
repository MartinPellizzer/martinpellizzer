Lathyrus venosus var. obovatus

Lathyrus venosus var. obovatus, commonly known as the broad-leafed grass vetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, erect stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are obovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy upper surface. The flowers are typically blue to purple, arranged in dense spikes, and bloom in early summer. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.
