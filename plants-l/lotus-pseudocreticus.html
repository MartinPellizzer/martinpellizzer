Lotus pseudocreticus

Lotus pseudocreticus, commonly known as the Cretan lotus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking pink or red flowers. The plant features palmately compound leaves and produces pods that contain edible seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

The Cretan lotus is valued for its ornamental beauty and traditional uses in local cultures.
