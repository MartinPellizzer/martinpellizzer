Lepidium trifurcum

Lepidium trifurcum, commonly known as peppergrass, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other economically important plants. The plant is characterized by its small, white or yellowish flowers and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. Peppergrass typically grows in open habitats such as fields and roadsides.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardiness and ability to stabilize soil.
