Lepidium khalkhalicum

Lepidium khalkhalicum, commonly known as Khalkhal cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to Iran and is characterized by its small, edible leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice in local cuisines.

Lepidium khalkhalicum plays a role in supporting local biodiversity and is of interest to botanists studying plant resilience.
