Lippia nodiflora var. repens

Lippia nodiflora var. repens, commonly known as creeping lippia, is a low-growing perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It features aromatic, narrow leaves and produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover. Its essential oils contain compounds like thymol and carvacrol, which contribute to its medicinal and aromatic properties.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.
