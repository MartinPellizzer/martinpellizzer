Lecythis pneumatophora

Lecythis pneumatophora, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a large aquatic plant native to the Amazon Basin.

It possesses broad, floating leaves that can reach up to three meters in diameter, providing a habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant has a distinctive inflorescence with showy, fragrant flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and bats. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the water, and it plays a vital role in the ecological balance of its environment.

The plant's unique adaptations allow it to thrive in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats.
