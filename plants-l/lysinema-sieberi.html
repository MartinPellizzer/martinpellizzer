Lysinema sieberi

Lysinema sieberi, commonly known as the Siberian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. The plant features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Lysinema sieberi is valued for its ornamental qualities and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
