Lycaste chrysoptera

Lycaste chrysoptera, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow and green flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and surrounding substrate.

Lycaste chrysoptera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or warm climates.
