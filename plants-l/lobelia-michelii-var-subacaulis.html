Lobelia michelii var. subacaulis

Lobelia michelii var. subacaulis, commonly known as the slender lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
