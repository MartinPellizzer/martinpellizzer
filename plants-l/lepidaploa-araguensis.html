Lepidaploa araguensis

Lepidaploa araguensis, commonly known as the Araguani orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, terrestrial growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in cloud forests or mountainous regions. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil.

Lepidaploa araguensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
