Linaria × sepium var. intermedia

Linaria × sepium var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate toadflax, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, often with a bluish-green hue. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to various garden conditions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
