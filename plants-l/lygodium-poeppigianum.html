Lygodium poeppigianum

Lygodium poeppigianum, commonly known as the climbing fern, is a tropical plant belonging to the family Lygodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are often used in horticulture for their ornamental value. The plant is a climbing vine that can grow up to several meters in height, using its aerial roots to attach to supports. Its spores are produced in clusters called sori, which are located on the undersides of its leaves.

This species is native to parts of Asia and is often found in moist, shaded environments.
