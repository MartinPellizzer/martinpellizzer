Lens esculenta var. microsperma

Lens esculenta var. microsperma, commonly known as the small-seeded lentil, is a variety of the lentil plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, round seeds that are typically red or brown in color. The plant is an annual legume that thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its leaves are trifoliate, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain the edible seeds.

This variety is valued for its nutritional content and is often cultivated for both human consumption and animal feed.
