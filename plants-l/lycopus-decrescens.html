Lycopus decrescens

Lycopus decrescens, commonly known as narrow-leaved blueweed, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Its small, blue to purple flowers appear in clusters during the summer months.

Lycopus decrescens is part of the mint family and has a creeping growth habit with rhizomes that help it spread.
