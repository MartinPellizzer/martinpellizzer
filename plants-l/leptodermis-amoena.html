Leptodermis amoena

Leptodermis amoena, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are often clustered in spikes. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous tiny seeds.

Leptodermis amoena is often found in wetland areas and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
