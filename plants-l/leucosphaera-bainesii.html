Leucosphaera bainesii

Leucosphaera bainesii, commonly known as the Baines' ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic orchid species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, ghostly appearance, with pale, translucent petals and a striking, almost ethereal bloom. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surfaces of trees rather than in soil, and relies on specific environmental conditions to thrive. Its flowers are typically small and white, with intricate patterns that resemble ghostly shapes, giving it its common name.

Due to habitat loss and its limited distribution, Leucosphaera bainesii is considered a critically endangered species.
