Lathyrus frolovii var. parviflora

Lathyrus frolovii var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered grass vetch, is a perennial leguminous plant native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often found in steppes and rocky soils. The plant has slender, creeping stems and small, pea-like flowers that are usually pale pink or white. Its trifoliate leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
