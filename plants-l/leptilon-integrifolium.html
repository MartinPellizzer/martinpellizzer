Leptilon integrifolium

Leptilon integrifolium, commonly known as the smoothleaf leptilon, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually smooth or only slightly hairy. Its flowers are small and clustered in racemes, often appearing in shades of white or pale pink.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting local pollinators and its ornamental appeal in native gardens.
