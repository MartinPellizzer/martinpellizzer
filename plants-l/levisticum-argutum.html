Levisticum argutum

Levisticum argutum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved parsley.

It is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, characterized by its finely divided, feather-like leaves that are aromatic and often used in culinary applications. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that appear in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family. Its root system is fibrous and contains volatile oils that contribute to its distinctive flavor.

Levisticum argutum is valued both for its culinary uses and as a medicinal herb in traditional systems.
