Leucocoryne inclinata

Leucocoryne inclinata, commonly known as the leaning white lily, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 cm, featuring narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is occasionally cultivated by gardeners interested in exotic flora.
