Lonicera arborea var. persica

Lonicera arborea var. persica, commonly known as the Persian honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Asia.

It features opposite, elliptic leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter in color underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range from white to pink, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that ripens in the fall and is attractive to birds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
