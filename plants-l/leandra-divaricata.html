Leandra divaricata

Leandra divaricata, commonly known as the silverleaf orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive silver-green foliage. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, typically blooming during the summer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Leandra divaricata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
