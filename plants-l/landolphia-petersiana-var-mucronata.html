Landolphia petersiana var. mucronata

Landolphia petersiana var. mucronata, commonly known as the African buttercup, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy yellow flowers with a distinctive red calyx. The plant produces large, fleshy berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae, which includes other milkweed-related species.
