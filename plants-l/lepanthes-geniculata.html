Lepanthes geniculata

Lepanthes geniculata, commonly known as the curling orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers that display a range of colors, typically white, pink, or purple. The plant has a short, erect stem with a few leaves and produces small, clustered blooms. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Lepanthes geniculata is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is appreciated for its ornamental value in horticulture.
