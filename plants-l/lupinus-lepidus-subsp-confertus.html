Lupinus lepidus subsp. confertus

Lupinus lepidus subsp.

confertus, commonly known as the Sierra lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its erect growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its potential use in restoration projects.
