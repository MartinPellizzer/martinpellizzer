Leucopogon elegans var. glaucifolius

Leucopogon elegans var. glaucifolius, commonly known as the silverleaf leucopogon, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is a shrub characterized by its narrow, silvery-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or mauve in color, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
