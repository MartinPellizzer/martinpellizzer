Lactuca verdickii

Lactuca verdickii, commonly known as the desert lettuce, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant has slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, and its seeds are dispersed by wind.

Lactuca verdickii is adapted to survive in hot, dry environments and is often found in rocky soils.
