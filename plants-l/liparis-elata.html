Liparis elata

Liparis elata, commonly known as the tall lady's tresses, is a perennial orchid native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, grass-like leaves. The plant produces a long, spiraled inflorescence of small, greenish-white flowers with a subtle fragrance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as bogs and wet meadows.

Liparis elata is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
