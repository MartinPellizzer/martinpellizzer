Leontice chrysogonia

Leontice chrysogonia, commonly known as golden lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, branching stems that are covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a distinctive waxy coating.

Leontice chrysogonia is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks.
