Lonchocarpus sericeus subsp. palmeri

Lonchocarpus sericeus subsp.

palmeri, commonly known as the silky senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its legume fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability.

The plant features trifoliate leaves with silky, hairy surfaces, and it produces yellow to orange flowers. Its pods are elongated and contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
