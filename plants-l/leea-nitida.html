Leea nitida

Leea nitida, commonly known as the silky leaf vine, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in tropical gardens for its attractive foliage and unique fruit.
