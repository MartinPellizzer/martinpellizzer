Lilium × hollandicum

Lilium × hollandicum, commonly known as the Dutch hybrid lily, is a cultivated variety resulting from crosses between different lily species.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that come in a range of colors, including white, pink, red, and orange. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with strap-like leaves arranged in a fan shape. Its bulbs are adapted for storage and propagation, and it thrives in well-drained soil with ample sunlight.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding ornamental value to gardens and landscapes.
