Luxemburgia neglecta

Luxemburgia neglecta, commonly known as the neglected luxembourg plant, is a rare and delicate flowering species native to the mountainous regions of Luxembourg.

It features small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in early summer, adding a subtle elegance to its foliage. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green color, providing a striking contrast to its pale blooms. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

Due to its limited habitat and threatened status, Luxemburgia neglecta is considered a conservation priority in its native environment.
