Lomandra longifolia subsp. hystrix

Lomandra longifolia subsp.

hystrix, commonly known as the stiff-leaved lomandra, is a perennial herb native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage with stiff, sword-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in summer, which are clustered in spike-like inflorescences. It is typically found in coastal heathlands and sandy soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
