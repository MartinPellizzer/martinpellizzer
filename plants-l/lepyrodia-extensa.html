Lepyrodia extensa

Lepyrodia extensa, commonly known as the broadleaf cowpea, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple, followed by pods containing edible seeds. Lepyrodia extensa is often cultivated for its seeds, which are used in culinary and nutritional contexts.

It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun for optimal growth.
