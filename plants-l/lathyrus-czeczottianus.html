Lathyrus czeczottianus

Lathyrus czeczottianus, commonly known as the Czeczottian sweet pea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of many legumes. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, heart-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained, loamy soils.

Lathyrus czeczottianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
