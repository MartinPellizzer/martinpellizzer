Lycopodium trichiatum var. desvauxianum

Lycopodium trichiatum var. desvauxianum, commonly known as the desert clubmoss, is a low-growing evergreen plant characterized by its dense, scale-like leaves and spore-producing cones.

It belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae and is native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions due to its succulent stems and leaves. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small organisms in its native environment.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
