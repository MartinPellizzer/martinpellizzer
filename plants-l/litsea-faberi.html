Litsea faberi

Litsea faberi, commonly known as the Chinese sandalwood or fragrant laurier, is a small evergreen tree native to southern China and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic trees like cinnamon and avocado. The plant is characterized by its simple, opposite, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically white or pale yellow, and it produces small, round, dark blue berries.

The tree is valued for its aromatic wood and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and pleasant scent.
