Luzula rufescens var. rufescens

Luzula rufescens var. rufescens, commonly known as the red-stemmed wood sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base, giving it its characteristic appearance. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, each bearing small, three-lobed flowers.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens for its ecological benefits.
