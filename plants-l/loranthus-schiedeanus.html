Loranthus schiedeanus

Loranthus schiedeanus, commonly known as the mistletoe of the genus Loranthus, is a parasitic plant that attaches itself to the branches of its host trees.

It lacks true roots and absorbs nutrients and water through its stems and leaves, which are embedded into the host's bark. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant. Its berries are dark red or black and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

Loranthus schiedeanus is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it forms a symbiotic relationship with various tree species.
