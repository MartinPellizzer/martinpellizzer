Lepidium crassum

Lepidium crassum, commonly known as broad-leaved peppergrass, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many edible and economically important plants. Lepidium crassum is often found in rocky or gravelly soils, growing in dry, open habitats.

Its seeds are small and can be dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize new areas.
