Lipskya insignis

Lipskya insignis, commonly known as the pink fairy fan, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its vibrant pink to purple tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is popular in gardens and collections of exotic plants.
