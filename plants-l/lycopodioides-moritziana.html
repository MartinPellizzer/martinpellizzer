Lycopodioides moritziana

Lycopodioides moritziana, commonly known as the Moritz's clubmoss, is a species of lycophyte native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, spore-bearing cones and its preference for moist, shaded environments. The plant has a low-growing habit with dense, needle-like leaves that form a compact mat. Lycopodioides moritziana plays a role in forest ecosystems by contributing to soil stability and providing habitat for microorganisms.

Its conservation status is currently listed as vulnerable due to habitat loss and degradation.
