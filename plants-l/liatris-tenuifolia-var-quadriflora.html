Liatris tenuifolia var. quadriflora

Liatris tenuifolia var. quadriflora, commonly known as narrowleaf blazingstar, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and clusters of pale purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and woodlands. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

This variant is distinguished by its four-flowered clusters, which give it its specific epithet "quadriflora.".
