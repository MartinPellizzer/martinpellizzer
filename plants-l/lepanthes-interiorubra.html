Lepanthes interiorubra

Lepanthes interiorubra, commonly known as the interior red lepanthus, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red and orange floral hues, which are particularly striking in its naturally occurring habitats. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are relatively small but possess intricate structures typical of the orchid family, including specialized petals and sepals.

Lepanthes interiorubra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
