Leptochloa virgata var. gracilis

Leptochloa virgata var. gracilis, commonly known as slender spikegrass, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of aquatic and riparian ecosystems.
