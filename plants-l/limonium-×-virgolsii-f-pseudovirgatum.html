Limonium × virgolsii f. pseudovirgatum

Limonium × virgolsii f. pseudovirgatum, commonly known as the sea holly, is a hybrid species within the Limonium genus, characterized by its striking, spiky flower heads.

It is native to coastal regions of southern Europe and is adapted to saline and sandy soils. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with narrow, gray-green leaves and blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

The inflorescence consists of dense clusters of small, blue or purple flowers, giving it a distinctive appearance.
