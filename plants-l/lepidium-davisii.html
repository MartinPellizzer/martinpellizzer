Lepidium davisii

Lepidium davisii, commonly known as Davis's cress, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard plants. The plant is characterized by its small, delicate leaves and clusters of tiny white or yellow flowers. It typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as stream banks and mountain meadows.

Lepidium davisii is often found in regions with cool, temperate climates and is adapted to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.
