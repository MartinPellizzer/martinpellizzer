Luzula luzuloides subsp. cuprina

Luzula luzuloides subsp.

cuprina, commonly known as the cuprona grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. Luzula luzuloides subsp.

cuprina is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in rocky environments.
