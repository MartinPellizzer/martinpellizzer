Lithocarpus lemeeanus subsp. lemeeanus

Lithocarpus lemeeanus subsp.

lemeeanus, commonly known as the Philippine stone oak, is a member of the Fagaceae family, characterized by its hard, dense wood and evergreen foliage. It is native to the Philippines and thrives in tropical and subtropical rainforests, often found in mountainous regions.

The plant produces small, cupule-enclosed nuts, which are an important food source for various wildlife species. Its leaves are typically thick, leathery, and have a prominent midrib, contributing to its ecological role in forest ecosystems.

This subspecies is valued for its timber and is also studied for its potential in ecological restoration projects.
