Lacostea

Lacostea, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, drooping clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has compound leaves with many small leaflets and produces small, winged fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Lacostea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
