Linum squamulosum subsp. collinum

Linum squamulosum subsp.

collinum, commonly known as the mountain flax, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves covered in fine, silky hairs.

The plant produces clusters of blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
