Leptospermum subargenteum

Leptospermum subargenteum, commonly known as silver tea tree, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine, whitish hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
