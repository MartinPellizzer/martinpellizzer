Lonicera edulis

Lonicera edulis, commonly known as the edible honeysuckle, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia.

It produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant bears small, red, berry-like fruits that are edible for humans and animals. These fruits are rich in vitamin C and have a sweet, tangy flavor.

Lonicera edulis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for its edible fruits in traditional cuisines.
