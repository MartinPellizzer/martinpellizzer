Lepanthes chrysosepala

Lepanthes chrysosepala, commonly known as the golden-sepal orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow sepals and petals, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within tropical forests. Its flowers are small but colorful, often appearing in clusters.

Lepanthes chrysosepala is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
