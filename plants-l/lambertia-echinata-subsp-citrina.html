Lambertia echinata subsp. citrina

Lambertia echinata subsp.

citrina, commonly known as the yellow lambertia, is a striking shrub native to South Africa. It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a vibrant splash of color to its natural habitat.

The plant features woody stems with smooth, greyish bark, and its leaves are narrow, leathery, and evergreen. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and adaptability to Mediterranean climates.
