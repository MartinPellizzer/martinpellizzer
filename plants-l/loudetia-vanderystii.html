Loudetia vanderystii

Loudetia vanderystii, commonly known as the Vanderyst's lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are wind-pollinated.

This species is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
