Lobivia haematantha var. fechseri

Lobivia haematantha var. fechseri, commonly known as the Red-veined Lobivia, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It features compact, cylindrical stems covered in dense, woolly white or grayish hairs. The plant produces vibrant orange to red flowers during the warmer months, which are typically short-lived but striking. Its leaves are small, fleshy, and often turn reddish in response to sunlight.

This variety is prized for its unique coloration and adaptability to dry, well-drained soils.
