Linochilus saxatilis

Linochilus saxatilis, commonly known as the rock linochilus, is a flowering plant species native to the high altitudes of East Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pale yellow. The plant thrives in rocky and well-drained soils, often growing in mountainous regions with cool, humid climates. Its unique morphology includes narrow, grass-like leaves and a compact growth habit, which help it adapt to its challenging environment.

Linochilus saxatilis is of interest to botanists due to its ecological significance and potential for conservation efforts in its native habitat.
