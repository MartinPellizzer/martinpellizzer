Laurus ophthalmicus

Laurus ophthalmicus, commonly known as the eye laurel, is a flowering plant belonging to the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are glossy and emit a strong, spicy scent when crushed.

Laurus ophthalmicus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine.
