Leptocarpus fruticosus

Leptocarpus fruticosus, commonly known as the coastal daisy or sea daisy, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along the shoreline. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in dense, daisy-like clusters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in a fine layer of salt-tolerant hairs.

Leptocarpus fruticosus is adapted to withstand salt spray and strong coastal winds, making it a resilient species in harsh maritime environments.
