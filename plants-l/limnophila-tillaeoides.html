Limnophila tillaeoides

Limnophila tillaeoides, commonly known as the slender water fern, is a small aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in shallow, still or slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and marshes. The plant has delicate, floating leaves that are often divided into narrow segments, giving it a feathery appearance. Its rhizomes spread along the substrate, allowing it to colonize new areas.

Limnophila tillaeoides plays a role in water filtration and provides habitat for small aquatic organisms.
