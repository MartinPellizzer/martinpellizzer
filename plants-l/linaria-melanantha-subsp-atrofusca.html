Linaria melanantha subsp. atrofusca

Linaria melanantha subsp.

atrofusca, commonly known as the dark-flowered toadflax, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers.

The flowers are typically dark purple to black, with two prominent lobes at the tip, and are often arranged in dense racemes. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, oval seeds in dry, capsule-like fruits.

It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
