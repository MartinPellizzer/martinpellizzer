Linum strictum proles spicatum

Linum strictum proles spicatum, commonly known as the spiked flax, is a flowering plant belonging to the Linaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers arranged in spikes, which are typical of its species. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grassy areas.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in fiber production, it is cultivated in gardens and agricultural settings.
