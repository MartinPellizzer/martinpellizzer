Lessertia obtusata

Lessertia obtusata, commonly known as the African snowball, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Lessertia obtusata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
