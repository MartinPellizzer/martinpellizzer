Lessingianthus xanthophyllus

Lessingianthus xanthophyllus, commonly known as the golden ray flower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy yellow ray flowers surrounding a central disk of smaller florets, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in grasslands and open habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and delicate structure.
