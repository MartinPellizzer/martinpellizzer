Lablab purpureus subsp. purpureus

Lablab purpureus subsp.

purpureus, commonly known as hyacinth bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. It is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces.

The plant produces clusters of purple or violet flowers that are followed by elongated, flat pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its nutritional value and ornamental appeal.

The species is also valued for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
