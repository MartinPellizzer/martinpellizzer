Lathyrus pratensis f. sepium

Lathyrus pratensis f. sepium, commonly known as the purple vetch, is a variant of the common vetch.

It is a climbing legume characterized by its slender, twining stems and trifoliate leaves. The flowers are typically pale purple to violet, with a distinctive spurred petal. This plant is often found in meadows and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.

It plays a role in nitrogen fixation and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
