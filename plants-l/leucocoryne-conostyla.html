Leucocoryne conostyla

Leucocoryne conostyla, commonly known as the silver-flowered lily, is a species of bulbous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery coating, giving it its common name. It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its elegant and unique floral display.
