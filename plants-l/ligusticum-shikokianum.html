Ligusticum shikokianum

Ligusticum shikokianum, commonly known as the Shikoku lovage, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbel-shaped flower clusters. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

Its rhizomes and leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.
