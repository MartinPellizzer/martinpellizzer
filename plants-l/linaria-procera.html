Linaria procera

Linaria procera, commonly known as the tall toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and slender, branching stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that are typically arranged in dense clusters at the tips of the branches. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Linaria procera is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
