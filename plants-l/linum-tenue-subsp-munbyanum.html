Linum tenue subsp. munbyanum

Linum tenue subsp.

munbyanum, commonly known as the narrowleaf flax, is a flowering plant species native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It is a member of the Linaceae family, which includes other flax species.

The subspecies munbyanum is distinguished by its narrower leaves and slightly different floral morphology compared to the broader-leaved varieties of Linum tenue.
