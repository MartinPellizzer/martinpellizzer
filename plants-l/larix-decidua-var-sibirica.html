Larix decidua var. sibirica

Larix decidua var. sibirica, commonly known as the Siberian larch, is a variant of the European larch native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a coniferous tree characterized by its needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure. The plant exhibits a unique adaptation to cold climates, with needle-like foliage that reduces water loss in harsh environments. Its cones are typically woody and release seeds in the autumn.

This species is often used in reforestation projects due to its resilience and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
