Lobelia ingrata

Lobelia ingrata, commonly known as the slender lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Lobelia ingrata is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
