Lupinus convencionensis

Lupinus convencionensis, commonly known as the Convencion lupine, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers are usually blue or purple, with a distinctive spurred shape that aids in pollination by specific insects.

Lupinus convencionensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
