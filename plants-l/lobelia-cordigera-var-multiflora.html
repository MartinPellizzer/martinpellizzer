Lobelia cordigera var. multiflora

Lobelia cordigera var. multiflora, commonly known as the Carolina bluebell, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces clusters of striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
