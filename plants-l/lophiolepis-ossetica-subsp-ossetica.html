Lophiolepis ossetica subsp. ossetica

Lophiolepis ossetica subsp.

ossetica, commonly known as the Ossetian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region. It is characterized by its tubular, bell-shaped flowers that typically range in color from violet to blue.

The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are usually lance-shaped and arranged oppositely. It thrives in rocky or limestone soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
