Lepargochloa

Lepargochloa, commonly known as the silver hairgrass, is a genus of grasses belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-tinged leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. These plants typically thrive in moist, well-drained soils and are often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Lepargochloa species are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes used in landscaping.
