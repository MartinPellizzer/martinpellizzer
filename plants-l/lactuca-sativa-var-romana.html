Lactuca sativa var. romana

Lactuca sativa var. romana, commonly known as romaine lettuce, is a cultivated variety of lettuce characterized by its crisp, upright leaves and a mild, slightly bitter taste.

It is a leafy green vegetable that belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 10 to 30 centimeters and has a rosette-like growth pattern with elongated, dark green leaves. Romaine lettuce is prized for its nutritional value, containing significant amounts of vitamins A and K, as well as dietary fiber.

It is widely used in salads and is a popular choice for its fresh, crunchy texture.
