Linum nelsonii

Linum nelsonii, commonly known as Nelson's flax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the genus Linum, which includes various species of flax known for their showy blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while its flowers are solitary and have five petals.

Linum nelsonii is often found in arid regions and is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.
