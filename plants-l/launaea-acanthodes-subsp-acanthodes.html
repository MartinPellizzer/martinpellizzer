Launaea acanthodes subsp. acanthodes

Launaea acanthodes subsp.

acanthodes, commonly known as the spiny dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright, sparsely branched stems and basal leaves that are lobed and hairy.

The plant produces yellow flowers that are surrounded by a series of bracts, which are often mistaken for petals. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
