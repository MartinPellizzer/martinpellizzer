Lycopodium guatemalense

Lycopodium guatemalense, commonly known as the Guatemalan clubmoss, is a small evergreen plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and small, scale-like leaves that form dense mats on the forest floor. The plant produces spore-bearing cones, which are typically clustered at the base of the stems. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Lycopodium guatemalense plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
