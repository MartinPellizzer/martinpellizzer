Lepanthes osiris

Lepanthes osiris, commonly known as the sacred orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central and South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments such as cloud forests. The plant features delicate, spurred flowers that are often pale green or white, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insects. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the forest floor, and it relies on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.

Lepanthes osiris is valued for its ornamental beauty and cultural significance in some regions.
