Limnanthemum microphyllum

Limnanthemum microphyllum, commonly known as the small-leaved water geranium, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of bright yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its flowers are often pollinated by insects, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soil.

Limnanthemum microphyllum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in aquatic habitats.
