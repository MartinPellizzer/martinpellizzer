Lumnitzera ocimoides

Lumnitzera ocimoides, commonly known as the mangrove apple or sea hibiscus, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to coastal regions of the Indo-Pacific.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often pubescent on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by fleshy, orange-red berries that contain several seeds. It thrives in brackish water environments and is often found in mangrove ecosystems.

Lumnitzera ocimoides plays a vital role in coastal stabilization and provides habitat for various marine and terrestrial organisms.
