Littaea geminiflora

Littaea geminiflora, commonly known as the twin-flowered rock cress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers, which are arranged in pairs along the stems. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly succulent texture.

Littaea geminiflora is adapted to cold, dry environments and is often found at high elevations.
