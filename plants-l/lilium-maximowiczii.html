Lilium maximowiczii

Lilium maximowiczii, commonly known as the Korean tiger lily, is a species of perennial herb native to Korea and parts of northeastern China.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers with dark red spots, which resemble a tiger's stripes, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with narrow, linear leaves arranged in a basal rosette. Its bulbs are bulbous and store nutrients, supporting the plant's growth during the growing season.

Lilium maximowiczii is a member of the Liliaceae family and is valued for its ornamental beauty in horticulture.
