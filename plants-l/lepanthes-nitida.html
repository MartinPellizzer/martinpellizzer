Lepanthes nitida

Lepanthes nitida, commonly known as the silver orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking white or pale green flowers with a distinctive, glossy appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in the understory of tropical forests. Its leaves are usually elliptical and covered in a fine, waxy coating that gives it a silvery sheen.

Lepanthes nitida is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
