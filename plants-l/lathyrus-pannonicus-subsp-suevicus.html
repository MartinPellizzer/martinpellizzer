Lathyrus pannonicus subsp. suevicus

Lathyrus pannonicus subsp.

suevicus, commonly known as the Baltic vetch, is a perennial legume native to parts of Europe, including the Baltic region. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

The plant features slender, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from purple to blue. Its trifoliate leaves are pinnately divided, and it produces elongated pods containing several seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
