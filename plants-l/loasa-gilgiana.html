Loasa gilgiana

Loasa gilgiana, commonly known as the Mexican ironwood, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in poor soils.
