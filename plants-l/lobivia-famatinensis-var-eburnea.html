Lobivia famatinensis var. eburnea

Lobivia famatinensis var. eburnea, commonly known as the White-veined Lobivia, is a succulent native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, branching structure and pale green to white foliage with distinct veining. The plant produces tall, sparsely branched stems that bear clusters of yellow flowers during the spring. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth, it is a sought-after species among cactus and succulent enthusiasts.
