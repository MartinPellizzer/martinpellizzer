Loxanthocereus piscoensis

Loxanthocereus piscoensis, commonly known as the Pisco cactus, is a species of cactus native to the coastal regions of Peru.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are typically sparsely armed with spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as bats and moths. It thrives in arid environments with well-drained soil, showcasing adaptations to extreme desert conditions.

Its structure and flowering habits reflect its evolutionary response to the harsh climate of its natural habitat.
