Lycium fimbriatum

Lycium fimbriatum, commonly known as the fringed lycium, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and red berries. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, while its stems are woody and often branched.

Lycium fimbriatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
