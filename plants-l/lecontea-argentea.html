Lecontea argentea

Lecontea argentea, commonly known as the silverleaf bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-white, velvety foliage and bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale blue to lavender. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is often found in forest understories.

Lecontea argentea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
