Lantana brittonii

Lantana brittonii, commonly known as the Britton's lantana, is a flowering shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant is evergreen, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

Lantana brittonii is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
