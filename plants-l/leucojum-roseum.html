Leucojum roseum

Leucojum roseum, commonly known as the Spanish snowflake, is a bulbous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of white, star-shaped flowers with a distinctive pinkish hue. The plant is characterized by its fragrant blooms, which appear in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Leucojum roseum is also known for its hardiness and ability to naturalize over time.
