Lucilia violacea

Lucilia violacea, commonly known as the purple blowfly, is a species of fly that belongs to the family Calliphoridae.

It is characterized by its metallic purple coloration, which is most prominent on its thorax and abdomen. The plant is not a botanical entity but rather an insect, so it does not have botanical aspects in the traditional sense. However, it is often found near decaying organic matter, which can be part of a broader ecological discussion.

Its presence can indicate the decomposition stage of organic material in an ecosystem.
