Liquidambar obovata

Liquidambar obovata, commonly known as the Japanese sweetgum, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It features opposite, ovate to obovate leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, spiky spherical fruits that contain seeds. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

Liquidambar obovata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens and parks.
