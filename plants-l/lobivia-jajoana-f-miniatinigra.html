Lobivia jajoana f. miniatinigra

Lobivia jajoana f. miniatinigra, commonly known as the miniature lobivia, is a compact and low-growing cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It features short, cylindrical stems that are typically greenish-gray in color, covered in sparse, white to pale blue hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a delicate touch of color to its otherwise muted appearance. It is well adapted to drought conditions, storing water in its thickened stems.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts and collectors.
