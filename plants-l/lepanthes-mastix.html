Lepanthes mastix

Lepanthes mastix, commonly known as the mastix orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, brightly colored flowers that often feature intricate patterns and vivid hues. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, tropical environments. Its leaves are usually elongated and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

Lepanthes mastix is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique floral displays.
