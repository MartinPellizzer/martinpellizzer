Lomanthus cerrateae

Lomanthus cerrateae, commonly known as the Brazilian flame tree, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy red flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a striking visual display. The plant has compound leaves with long, narrow leaflets that help it thrive in warm, humid climates. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant blooms and rapid growth.

Lomanthus cerrateae prefers well-drained soil and full sun exposure for optimal growth.
