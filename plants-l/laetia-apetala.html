Laetia apetala

Laetia apetala, commonly known as the leafy mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.

It lacks true leaves and instead relies on its green stems for photosynthesis, while its roots penetrate the host's vascular system to absorb water and nutrients. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers, which are often clustered and lack petals, giving it its scientific name. Its seeds are dispersed by birds that feed on the fleshy, red berries it produces.

Laetia apetala plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for certain wildlife and influencing the growth of its host trees.
