Lupinus clarkei var. maxonianus

Lupinus clarkei var. maxonianus, commonly known as the Maxonian lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in nitrogen fixation in soil.
