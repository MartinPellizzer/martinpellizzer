Lathyrus torreyi var. tenellus

Lathyrus torreyi var. tenellus, commonly known as the slender vetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with slender, hairy leaflets.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
