Lomatia bidwillii

Lomatia bidwillii, commonly known as the silky oak, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows up to 15 meters in height. The plant features compound leaves with a distinctive silky texture, giving it its common name. It produces small, yellowish-white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer seasons.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and seasonal blooms.
