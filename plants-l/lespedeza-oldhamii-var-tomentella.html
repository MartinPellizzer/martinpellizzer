Lespedeza oldhamii var. tomentella

Lespedeza oldhamii var. tomentella, commonly known as Oldham's lespezea, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its finely hairy stems and leaves, which are typically narrow and lance-shaped. The plant produces small, fluffy flower clusters that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grassy areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is cultivated in some regions for landscaping and erosion control.
