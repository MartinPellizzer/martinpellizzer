Landolphia crassipes var. boivinii

Landolphia crassipes var. boivinii, commonly known as the African morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features large, showy flowers that are typically trumpet-shaped and come in shades of yellow to orange. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces fleshy, berry-like fruits that are edible when ripe. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
