Larix vulgaris

Larix vulgaris, commonly known as the tamarack, is a deciduous conifer native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves that turn golden in the fall before shedding. The tree produces cone-like structures that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its bark is thick and scaly, providing protection against harsh environmental conditions.

The tamarack is often found in wetland areas and is valued for its timber and ecological importance.
