Lactuca napifolia

Lactuca napifolia, commonly known as leaf lettuce, is a cool-season annual plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its loose, leafy head and crisp, edible leaves that are widely used in salads and as a garnish. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with broad, dark green leaves that are often ribbed. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, and it is often cultivated for its nutritional value and mild flavor.

Lactuca napifolia is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes other familiar plants like daisies and sunflowers.
