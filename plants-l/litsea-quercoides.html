Litsea quercoides

Litsea quercoides, commonly known as the Indian persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often glossy and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by orange to reddish berries that are edible. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and its fruit, which has a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor.
