Lepidium heterophyllum

Lepidium heterophyllum, commonly known as the dwarf cress, is a small flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like broccoli and mustard. The plant typically grows to a height of less than 10 centimeters and features small, white or pink flowers. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Lepidium heterophyllum is often found in moist, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in cooler climates.
