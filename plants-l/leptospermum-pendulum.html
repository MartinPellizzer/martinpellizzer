Leptospermum pendulum

Leptospermum pendulum, commonly known as the coastal tea tree, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its small, needle-like leaves and clusters of white to pale yellow flowers. The plant thrives in coastal environments and is often found in sandy or rocky soils. It is drought-tolerant and can withstand salt spray, making it well-suited for coastal regions.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
