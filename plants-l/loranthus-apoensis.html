Loranthus apoensis

Loranthus apoensis, commonly known as the Philippine mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees in the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Loranthaceae and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant relies on its host for water and nutrients, while it provides some ecological benefits by supporting bird populations that feed on its berries. Loranthus apoensis is often found in lowland and montane forests, contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitat.

Its presence is an indicator of healthy forest ecosystems in the region.
