Lepanthes ruthiana

Lepanthes ruthiana, commonly known as the Ruth's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments within the understory of forests. The plant produces delicate, showy flowers with a distinctive coloration, often featuring a mix of white, purple, and green hues. Its flowers are usually short-lived, blooming for only a few days before wilting.

Lepanthes ruthiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique beauty.
