Luzula juncoides

Luzula juncoides, commonly known as the common wood rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowering occurs in early spring, producing tiny, greenish to brownish flowers that are often hidden among the foliage.

Luzula juncoides is adapted to cool, temperate climates and is often found in nutrient-rich, well-drained soils.
