Leptochloa virgata

Leptochloa virgata, commonly known as slender grass or narrowleaf grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family, Poaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. It produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with two to three florets.

Leptochloa virgata is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
