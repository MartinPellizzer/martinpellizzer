Larix gmelinii f. macrocarpa

Larix gmelinii f. macrocarpa, commonly known as the Siberian larch, is a variant of the Siberian larch tree native to the cold climates of Siberia.

It is a deciduous conifer, characterized by its needle-like leaves that turn golden before falling in autumn. The tree produces cone-like structures that contain large, winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its wood is durable and has been historically used for construction and furniture.

This subspecies is adapted to harsh environments, showcasing resilience to extreme cold and poor soil conditions.
