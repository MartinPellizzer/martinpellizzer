Lagerstroemia noei

Lagerstroemia noei, commonly known as the Chinese crape myrtle, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to red bracts that appear in late spring to early summer, giving the plant its ornamental appeal. The leaves are opposite, elliptical, and turn vibrant red or orange in the fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
