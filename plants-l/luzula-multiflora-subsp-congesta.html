Luzula multiflora subsp. congesta

Luzula multiflora subsp.

congesta, commonly known as the clustered wood rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Poaceae, commonly referred to as the grass family, though it is often mistaken for a sedge due to its triangular stems and narrow leaves.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, grass-like appearance.
