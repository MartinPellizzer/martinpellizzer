Leibnitzia serotina

Leibnitzia serotina, commonly known as the late-flowering leibnitzia, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in rich, loamy soils. The plant features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves with a hairy surface. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in late summer to early autumn.

The species is notable for its unique flowering habit and is often found in association with other woodland flora.
