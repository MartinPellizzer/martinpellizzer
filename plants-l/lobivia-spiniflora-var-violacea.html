Lobivia spiniflora var. violacea

Lobivia spiniflora var. violacea, commonly known as the violet-flowered lobivia, is a succulent native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, branching structure with thick, fleshy stems. The plant produces vibrant violet or purple flowers during the warmer months, which are typically short-lived but striking. Its leaves are usually reduced to spines, and it thrives in well-drained soil with minimal water.

It is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and ornamental appeal.
