Litsea ripidion

Litsea ripidion, commonly known as the narrow-leafed litsea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves that are usually narrow and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by small, fleshy berries.

The plant is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its aromatic properties and potential medicinal uses.
