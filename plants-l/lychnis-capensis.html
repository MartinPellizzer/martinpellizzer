Lychnis capensis

Lychnis capensis, commonly known as the Cape sandcatcher, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with fleshy, succulent leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens in regions with a Mediterranean climate.
