Lepanthes salpiginosa

Lepanthes salpiginosa, commonly known as the "Cape of Good Hope orchid," is a species of orchid native to South America, particularly found in Brazil and Argentina.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid with a distinctive appearance, featuring a showy, spurred flower that is often violet or purple in color. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowers are characterized by their intricate structure, including a long, slender column and three sepals.

Lepanthes salpiginosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique beauty.
