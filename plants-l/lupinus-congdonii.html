Lupinus congdonii

Lupinus congdonii, commonly known as Congdon's lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States, particularly found in California.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and showy blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Lupinus congdonii plays an important ecological role by fixing nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plant life.
