Landolphia gummifera

Landolphia gummifera, commonly known as the gum tree or African rubber plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a woody shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by distinctive orange or red berries. Its latex sap is viscous and has been used traditionally for various purposes.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitats.
