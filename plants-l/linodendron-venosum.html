Linodendron venosum

Linodendron venosum, commonly known as the veiny orchid, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, veined leaves and fragrant, showy flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees without deriving nutrients from them. Its flowers are usually pale green or white with a slight purple tint, and they bloom during the wet season.

Linodendron venosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique botanical features.
