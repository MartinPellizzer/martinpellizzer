Lactuca alpina

Lactuca alpina, commonly known as alpine lettuce, is a flowering plant species native to mountainous regions of Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually basal, forming a rosette, and are often hairy and succulent.

Lactuca alpina is known for its ability to withstand harsh climates and is sometimes cultivated for its edible leaves.
