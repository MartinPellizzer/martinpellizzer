Laelia autumnalis var. xanthotrophis

Laelia autumnalis var. xanthotrophis, commonly known as the golden-tongued laelia, is a hybrid orchid native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated pseudobulbs and showy, golden-yellow flowers with a distinctive orange lip. This variety thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and moderate humidity. The plant produces fragrant blooms during the late summer and early autumn months.

It is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its vibrant color and relatively easy care requirements.
