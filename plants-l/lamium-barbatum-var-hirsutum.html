Lamium barbatum var. hirsutum

Lamium barbatum var. hirsutum, commonly known as hairy deadnettle, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, two-lipped flowers in shades of pink or purple, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its hairy leaves and showy flowers make it a distinctive and attractive addition to gardens.
