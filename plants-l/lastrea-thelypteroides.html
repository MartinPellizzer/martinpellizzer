Lastrea thelypteroides

Lastrea thelypteroides, commonly known as the rock fern, is a species of fern native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, delicate fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in sunny, coastal areas. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and as a decorative plant.
