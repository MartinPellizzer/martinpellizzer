Lastrea filix-mas

Lastrea filix-mas, commonly known as the sacred fig or bodhi tree, is a tropical plant belonging to the Moraceae family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish flowers that develop into edible fruits. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has cultural significance in various traditions. Its roots and leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The tree can grow quite large, reaching heights of up to 30 meters in its native habitat.
