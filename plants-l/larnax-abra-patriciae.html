Larnax abra-patriciae

Larnax abra-patriciae, commonly known as the Patrician's Larnax, is a rare flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It belongs to the Larnaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet-blue petals and serrated leaf margins. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil. Its blooming season occurs during the late spring, producing clusters of fragrant flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
