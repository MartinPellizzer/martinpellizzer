Launaea capitata

Launaea capitata, commonly known as the spiny groundsel, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, spiny stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and clustered in a single head.

Launaea capitata is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
