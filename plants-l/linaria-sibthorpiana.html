Linaria sibthorpiana

Linaria sibthorpiana, commonly known as the Sibthorp's toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stem.

Linaria sibthorpiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
