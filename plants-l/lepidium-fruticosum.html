Lepidium fruticosum

Lepidium fruticosum, commonly known as prickly rocket, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage. The plant is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in coastal areas.

Lepidium fruticosum is used in traditional medicine and is also valued as a forage plant for livestock.
