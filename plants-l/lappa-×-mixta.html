Lappa × mixta

Lappa × mixta, commonly known as the hybrid giant hogweed, is a tall biennial or perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in diameter, with a hairy underside. The plant produces clusters of white flowers in umbrella-like inflorescences, which are highly visible and attract pollinators. Lappa × mixta is known for its toxic sap, which contains phototoxic compounds that can cause severe skin irritation.

Due to its invasive nature, it is often considered a noxious weed in many regions.
