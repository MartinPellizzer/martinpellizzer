Lippia nodiflora var. tarraconensis

Lippia nodiflora var. tarraconensis, commonly known as the Tarraconensis mint, is a perennial herb native to southern Spain and northern Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly minty leaves and small white to pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported digestive and antispasmodic properties.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
