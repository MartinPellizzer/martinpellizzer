Lomatium triternatum var. brevifolium

Lomatium triternatum var. brevifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved lomatium, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to theApiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbel-like flower clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and forest edges. Its leaves are finely divided and have a distinctive three-part structure, giving it a fern-like appearance.

The small, yellowish flowers appear in early summer and are followed by small, dry fruits that disperse seeds by wind.
