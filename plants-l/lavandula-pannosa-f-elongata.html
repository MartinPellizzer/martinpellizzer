Lavandula pannosa f. elongata

Lavandula pannosa f. elongata, commonly known as the elongated lavender, is a compact, low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, upright stems covered in dense clusters of small, fragrant lavender-blue flowers. The leaves are gray-green, linear, and aromatic, contributing to its ornamental value. This cultivar is prized for its hardiness and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.

It is often used in rock gardens and as a border plant due to its attractive appearance and pleasant scent.
