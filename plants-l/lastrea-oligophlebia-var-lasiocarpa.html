Lastrea oligophlebia var. lasiocarpa

Lastrea oligophlebia var. lasiocarpa, commonly known as the woolly lastrea, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems covered in dense, white, woolly hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring or early summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
