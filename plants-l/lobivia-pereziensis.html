Lobivia pereziensis

Lobivia pereziensis, commonly known as the Peruvian lobivia, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andean region of Peru.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rounded stem and clusters of small, vibrant yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes, often in mountainous regions. It is a slow-growing succulent that requires minimal water and full sun exposure to thrive.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated by succulent enthusiasts for its ornamental value.
