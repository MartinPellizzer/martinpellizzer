Lonchocarpus hockii

Lonchocarpus hockii, commonly known as the Malayan strangle vine, is a climbing legume native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated, woody pods that contain several seeds. It is often found in tropical rainforests and is known for its rapid growth habit.

Due to its invasive potential, it can pose a threat to native ecosystems in certain regions.
