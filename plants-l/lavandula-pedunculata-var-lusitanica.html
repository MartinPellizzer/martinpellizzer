Lavandula pedunculata var. lusitanica

Lavandula pedunculata var. lusitanica, commonly known as the Portuguese lavender, is a compact, shrub-like plant native to southern Europe.

It features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces spikes of fragrant, violet-blue flowers. This variety is well adapted to dry, sunny conditions and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often used in rock gardens and as a border plant due to its ornamental value.

The plant is also valued for its essential oils, which are used in aromatherapy and perfumery.
