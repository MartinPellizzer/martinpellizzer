Leersia oryzoides subsp. japonica

Leersia oryzoides subsp.

japonica, commonly known as Japanese leersia, is a perennial grass species native to East Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and wetlands.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a rhizomatous growth habit.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
