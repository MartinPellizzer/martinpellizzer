Ligustrum macrocarpum

Ligustrum macrocarpum, commonly known as the large-fruited privet, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, simple, ovate leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters, followed by elliptical, black berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

However, it can become invasive in some regions if not managed properly.
