Lyonia glandulosa subsp. revolutifolia

Lyonia glandulosa subsp.

revolutifolia, commonly known as the hairy lyonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States. It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves.

The species produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape and have a distinctive revolute margin.

This plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded or partially shaded woodland environments.
