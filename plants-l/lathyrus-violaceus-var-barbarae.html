Lathyrus violaceus var. barbarae

Lathyrus violaceus var. barbarae, commonly known as the purple vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, climbing stems with trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces clusters of small, violet to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. Its seeds are encased in elongated pods, typical of the Fabaceae family.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
