Luma dimorpha

Luma dimorpha, commonly known as the golden trumpet or golden glory, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Luma dimorpha is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
