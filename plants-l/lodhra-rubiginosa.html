Lodhra rubiginosa

Lodhra rubiginosa, commonly known as the red-stained wood lot, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its rough, bark-like trunk and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It is often found in moist, tropical, and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils.

The plant has traditional medicinal uses in Ayurveda and is valued for its therapeutic properties.
