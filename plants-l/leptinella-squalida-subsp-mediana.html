Leptinella squalida subsp. mediana

Leptinella squalida subsp.

mediana, commonly known as the Alpine snowbell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit and small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink.

The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, silvery-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, providing insulation against harsh weather.

This subspecies is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging garden conditions.
