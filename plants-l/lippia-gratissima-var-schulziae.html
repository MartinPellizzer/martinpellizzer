Lippia gratissima var. schulziae

Lippia gratissima var. schulziae, commonly known as sweet basil or lemon basil, is a variant of the Lippia genus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves that emit a distinct citrus-like fragrance. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with hairy stems and opposite, oval-shaped leaves. It produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters.

This variety is often cultivated for its culinary uses and ornamental value in gardens.
