Lathyrus leucanthus var. laetivirens

Lathyrus leucanthus var. laetivirens, commonly known as the scarlet flaxvetch, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bright red to orange flowers, which bloom in early summer. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval, serrated leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
