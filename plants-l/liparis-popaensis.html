Liparis popaensis

Liparis popaensis, commonly known as the Păușa orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to Romania.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, greenish-yellow flowers with a prominent lip. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are fibrous, and it produces small, clustered pseudobulbs.

Liparis popaensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
