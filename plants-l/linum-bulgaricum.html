Linum bulgaricum

Linum bulgaricum, commonly known as Bulgarian flax, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, blue or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along riverbanks.

Linum bulgaricum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in fiber production.
