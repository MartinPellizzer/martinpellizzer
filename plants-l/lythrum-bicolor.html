Lythrum bicolor

Lythrum bicolor, commonly known as the pink willow or redroot, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and woodland edges.

Lythrum bicolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
