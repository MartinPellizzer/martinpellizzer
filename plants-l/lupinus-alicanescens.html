Lupinus alicanescens

Lupinus alicanescens, commonly known as the silver lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-blue flowers and feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has a taproot system that helps it survive in nutrient-poor conditions.

Lupinus alicanescens is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility.
