Lupinus argenteus var. krauchianus

Lupinus argenteus var. krauchianus, commonly known as the silver lupine, is a variant of the silver lupin native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and tall, spiky inflorescences that bear pink to purple flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It is a perennial legume that plays a role in nitrogen fixation, benefiting surrounding vegetation.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens and restoration projects.
