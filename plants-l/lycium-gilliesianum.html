Lycium gilliesianum

Lycium gilliesianum, commonly known as the gilliesian boxthorn, is a shrub native to South America, particularly found in Argentina, Chile, and Peru.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a rough texture, and it thrives in dry, arid environments.

Lycium gilliesianum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
