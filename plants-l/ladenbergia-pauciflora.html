Ladenbergia pauciflora

Ladenbergia pauciflora, commonly known as the slender legume, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
