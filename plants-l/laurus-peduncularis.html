Laurus peduncularis

Laurus peduncularis, commonly known as the Turkish laurel or Persian laurel, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It features dark green, glossy leaves that are oval in shape and have a spicy, aromatic scent. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clustered inflorescences, followed by small, dark blue berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

The species is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
