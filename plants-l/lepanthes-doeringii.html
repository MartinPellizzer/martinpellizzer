Lepanthes doeringii

Lepanthes doeringii, commonly known as the Doering's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers with white and purple petals, often resembling miniature orchids. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and surrounding substrate.

Lepanthes doeringii is appreciated for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique appearance.
