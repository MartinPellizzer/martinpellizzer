Libanotis incana

Libanotis incana, commonly known as the silverweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which results from dense covering of fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Libanotis incana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
