Lavandula lanata

Lavandula lanata, commonly known as woolly lavender, is a species of flowering plant in the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a unique, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces spikes of purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its aromatic properties and ornamental value make it both a decorative and potentially useful plant in horticulture.
