Lopezia oaxacana

Lopezia oaxacana, commonly known as the Oaxaca lupine, is a flowering plant native to the Mexican state of Oaxaca.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are pinnately compound, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Lopezia oaxacana plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and contributing to soil stabilization in its native habitat.
