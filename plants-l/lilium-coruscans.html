Lilium coruscans

Lilium coruscans, commonly known as the glittering lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant produces showy, fragrant blooms that typically appear in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Lilium coruscans is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
