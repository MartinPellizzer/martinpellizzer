Lepanthes crucipetala

Lepanthes crucipetala, commonly known as the Crossed-leaf Orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically range in color from white to pale pink. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually linear and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

Lepanthes crucipetala is often found in shaded, humid environments such as cloud forests.
