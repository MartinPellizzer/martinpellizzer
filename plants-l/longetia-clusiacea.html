Longetia clusiacea

Longetia clusiacea, commonly known as the glossy buckthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the family Clusiaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly the Congo and Gabon. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are typically glossy and dark green in color. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange or red berries.

Longetia clusiacea is often found in humid, lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
