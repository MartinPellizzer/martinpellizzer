Leea scabra

Leea scabra, commonly known as roughleaf leea, is a species of flowering plant in the family Leeaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Asia, including India, China, and Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often rough or hairy to the touch. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences.

Leea scabra is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
