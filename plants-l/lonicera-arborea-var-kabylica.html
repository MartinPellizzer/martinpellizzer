Lonicera arborea var. kabylica

Lonicera arborea var. kabylica, commonly known as the Kabylica honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It features slender, arching stems and is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.
