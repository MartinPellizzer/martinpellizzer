Laportea oleracea

Laportea oleracea, commonly known as the edible groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, lobed leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is often found in moist, disturbed soils such as along roadsides or in wetlands. Its leaves are edible and have a slightly bitter taste, making it a useful plant in foraging.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
