Liparis greenwoodiana

Liparis greenwoodiana, commonly known as the Greenwood's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest understories. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, often with a waxy surface.

Botanically, it belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
