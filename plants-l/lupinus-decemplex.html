Lupinus decemplex

Lupinus decemplex, commonly known as the nine-leaf lupine, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its spiny stems and compound leaves with nine pairs of leaflets. The plant produces tall, erect spikes of blue or violet flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Lupinus decemplex has adapted to grow in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats like grasslands and shrublands.

Its seeds are encased in pods and are an important food source for various wildlife species.
