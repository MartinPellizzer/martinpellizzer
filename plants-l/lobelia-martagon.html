Lobelia martagon

Lobelia martagon, commonly known as the cardinal flower, is a striking perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear clusters of tubular, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and purple. The flowers are bilaterally symmetrical, with two prominent lobes that give the plant its genus name. Lobelia martagon thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

It is a popular choice for gardeners due to its vibrant color and ecological value, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and butterflies.
