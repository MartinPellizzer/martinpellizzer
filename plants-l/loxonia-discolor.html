Loxonia discolor

Loxonia discolor, commonly known as the white-flowered loxonia, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Apiaceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. Native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant is often found in woodland edges and along streambanks.

Its botanical features make it an attractive addition to gardens and a valuable species for ecological studies.
