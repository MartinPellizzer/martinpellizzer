Lycoctonum chrysopilum

Lycoctonum chrysopilum, commonly known as the golden-hair lily, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with long, hairy stamens that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, preferring well-drained soils and cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are erect and slender.

Lycoctonum chrysopilum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
