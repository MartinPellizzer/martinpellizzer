Lobelia ignea

Lobelia ignea, commonly known as the fireweed or red lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features striking red to orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Lobelia ignea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
