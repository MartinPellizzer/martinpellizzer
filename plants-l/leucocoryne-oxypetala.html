Leucocoryne oxypetala

Leucocoryne oxypetala, commonly known as the silver leaf lily, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking white, waxy petals that resemble small lilies, and its leaves are broad and often have a silvery sheen. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments and prefers well-drained soil. It produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, adding ornamental value to gardens.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance, it is a popular choice for indoor and outdoor cultivation.
