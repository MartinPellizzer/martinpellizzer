Luzula multiflora var. lutescens

Luzula multiflora var. lutescens, commonly known as the yellow woodrush, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, commonly known as the grass family, and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in spikelets along the stem. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often have a bluish-green hue.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover in shaded areas.
