Lupinus alcis-temporis

Lupinus alcis-temporis, commonly known as the temporal lupine, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of vibrant blue or purple flowers. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Lupinus alcis-temporis is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for various ecological niches.

Its botanical features make it an important plant for both ecological studies and potential agricultural applications.
