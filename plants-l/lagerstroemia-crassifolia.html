Lagerstroemia crassifolia

Lagerstroemia crassifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf lagerstroemia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, broad, ovate to elliptical leaves that are usually dark green and glossy on top. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, pale pink to white flowers in summer, which are followed by round, fuzzy, red or purple fruits. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

The species is also valued for its role in traditional medicine in some regions.
