Lathyrus albus subvar. gracilis

Lathyrus albus subvar. gracilis, commonly known as the slender white vetch, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several small, edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value and nitrogen-fixing ability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and agricultural settings.
