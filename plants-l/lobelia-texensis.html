Lobelia texensis

Lobelia texensis, commonly known as Texas bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking blue to violet bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or prairie habitats. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Lobelia texensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant spring blooms.
