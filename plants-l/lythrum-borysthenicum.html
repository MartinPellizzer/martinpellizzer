Lythrum borysthenicum

Lythrum borysthenicum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lythrum, is a flowering plant native to the steppes of Eastern Europe and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lythraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and sandy soils. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in some regions for garden use.
