Lolium siculum

Lolium siculum, commonly known as Sicilian grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems with narrow, elongated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is adapted to well-drained soils and can thrive in full sun to partial shade.

Lolium siculum is often found in disturbed areas and is used in some traditional herbal practices.
