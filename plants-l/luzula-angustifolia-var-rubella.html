Luzula angustifolia var. rubella

Luzula angustifolia var. rubella, commonly known as the red-tipped wood rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, commonly referred to as the grass family, and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with reddish hues, particularly at the base, giving it its common name. It produces small, greenish or brownish spikelets that are typically arranged in clusters at the top of the stems.

This variety is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
