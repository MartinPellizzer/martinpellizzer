Lissochilus aurantiacus

Lissochilus aurantiacus, commonly known as the orange-hued orchid, is a species native to the Andean highlands of South America.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas at elevations ranging from 2,000 to 4,000 meters. Its flowers are small, clustered, and often have a velvety texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
