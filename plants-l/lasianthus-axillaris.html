Lasianthus axillaris

Lasianthus axillaris, commonly known as the slender mistflower, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale blue, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
