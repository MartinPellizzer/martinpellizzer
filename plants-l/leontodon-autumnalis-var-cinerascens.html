Leontodon autumnalis var. cinerascens

Leontodon autumnalis var. cinerascens, commonly known as the autumn leontodon, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn.

The variety cinerascens is distinguished by its pale, ash-colored leaves, which set it apart from other varieties of the species.
