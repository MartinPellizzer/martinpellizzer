Lycoseris oblongifolia

Lycoseris oblongifolia, commonly known as the oblong-leaved lycoseris, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in dense clusters, contributing to its ornamental value.

Lycoseris oblongifolia is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
