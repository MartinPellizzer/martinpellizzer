Lathyrus intricatus

Lathyrus intricatus, commonly known as the intricate pea, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, pea-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks. Its seed pods contain small, rounded seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Lathyrus intricatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
