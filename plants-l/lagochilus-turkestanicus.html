Lagochilus turkestanicus

Lagochilus turkestanicus, commonly known as the Turkestan lungwort, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and clusters of blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
