Lobivia densispina f. haematantha

Lobivia densispina f. haematantha, commonly known as the Crimson Lobivia, is a succulent plant native to South America, specifically Argentina.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rounded growth habit and dense, spiny stems. The leaves are typically reduced to small, tubular structures, and the plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red and orange.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in arid gardens due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
