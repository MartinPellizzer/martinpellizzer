Liparis nervosa f. aureovariegata

Liparis nervosa f. aureovariegata, commonly known as the golden variegated orchid, is a hybrid orchid species characterized by its striking variegated foliage.

It features lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive golden-yellow margin, contrasting with its deep green central portion. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant orchid flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. Native to the temperate regions of East Asia, it thrives in cool, humid environments with well-drained soil.

This ornamental plant is prized for its unique appearance and is often cultivated in shaded garden settings or as a potted plant.
