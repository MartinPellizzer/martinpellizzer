Ledonia populifolia var. cordifolia

Ledonia populifolia var. cordifolia, commonly known as the heart-leaf ledonia, is a rare and unique plant species native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of silver hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its growth habit is typically shrub-like, with a dense canopy that provides habitat for various small organisms.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
