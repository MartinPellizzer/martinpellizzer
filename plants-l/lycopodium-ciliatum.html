Lycopodium ciliatum

Lycopodium ciliatum, commonly known as the clubmoss, is a small evergreen shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae and is characterized by its spore-bearing cones and scale-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its sporophyte structure includes a stem with whorled branches and small, scale-like leaves.

Lycopodium ciliatum plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
