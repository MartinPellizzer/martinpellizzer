Laseguea erecta f. griseo-olivacea

Laseguea erecta f. griseo-olivacea, commonly known as the gray-green form of the erect laseguea, is a variant of the species Laseguea erecta.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The leaves are typically covered in a fine, grayish-green pubescence, giving the plant its distinctive appearance. This form is often found in rocky or calcareous soils, thriving in well-drained environments.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate dry conditions.
