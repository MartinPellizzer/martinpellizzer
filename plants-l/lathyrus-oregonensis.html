Lathyrus oregonensis

Lathyrus oregonensis, commonly known as the Oregon pea, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical pea-like flowers and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and meadow habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of study in plant ecology.
