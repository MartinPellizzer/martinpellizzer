Leontodon saxatilis var. pristis

Leontodon saxatilis var. pristis, commonly known as the rock leontodon, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often on cliffs or slopes. The plant features a basal rosette of linear, grass-like leaves and produces yellow flower heads in the summer months. Its flowers are composed of ray florets and are surrounded by a dense involucral bract.

This variety is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.
