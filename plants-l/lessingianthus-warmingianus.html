Lessingianthus warmingianus

Lessingianthus warmingianus, commonly known as the Brazilian sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow ray florets and dark brown disk florets. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and is known for its ability to thrive in full sun. It has hairy stems and leaves that are opposite in arrangement, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
