Lepanthes odobenella

Lepanthes odobenella, commonly known as the purple-veined orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers that typically feature a violet or purple hue with intricate veining. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its epiphytic growth habit, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without being parasitic. Its flowers are often fragrant and attract various pollinators such as bees and moths.

Lepanthes odobenella is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
