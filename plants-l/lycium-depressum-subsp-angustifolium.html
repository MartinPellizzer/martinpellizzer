Lycium depressum subsp. angustifolium

Lycium depressum subsp.

angustifolium, commonly known as narrowleaf boxthorn, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of western North America. It features small, spiny stems and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant has small, narrow leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It is adapted to dry conditions and can thrive in sandy or rocky soils.

The berries it produces are edible and have been used by indigenous peoples for food and medicinal purposes.
