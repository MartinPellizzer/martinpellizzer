Lasianthus seseensis

Lasianthus seseensis, commonly known as the Sese Island lasianthus, is a species of flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its attractive, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as mountain forests and understorey habitats. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with a smooth texture and a mild scent.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
