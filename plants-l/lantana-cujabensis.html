Lantana cujabensis

Lantana cujabensis, commonly known as the Brazilian lantana, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows up to 1.5 meters tall, featuring opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red, which bloom throughout the year in warm climates. Lantana cujabensis is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in full sun.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and use in landscaping.
