Limnochloa densa

Limnochloa densa, commonly known as the dense swamp sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and is typically found in peat bogs and marshy areas. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that often grow in dense clusters, and its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy. Limnochloa densa plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.

Due to its restricted habitat, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
