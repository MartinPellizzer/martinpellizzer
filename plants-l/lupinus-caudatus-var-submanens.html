Lupinus caudatus var. submanens

Lupinus caudatus var. submanens, commonly known as the Sierra lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open grasslands and rocky slopes.

The Sierra lupine plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.
