Leontodon croceus

Leontodon croceus, commonly known as the yellow hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it typically grows in meadows and grasslands.

Leontodon croceus is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
