Licuala cameronensis

Licuala cameronensis, commonly known as the Cameron's palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the Philippines and is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach up to two meters in diameter. The plant typically grows as a solitary stem with a crown of leaves, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries.

Licuala cameronensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
