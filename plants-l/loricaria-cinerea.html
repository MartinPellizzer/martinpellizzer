Loricaria cinerea

Loricaria cinerea, commonly known as the silverleaf loach, is a freshwater fish native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender body and a silvery sheen that reflects light, giving it its common name. The species belongs to the family Loricariidae, which includes many armored catfish. Loricaria cinerea typically inhabits slow-moving rivers and streams with sandy or muddy substrates.

Its diet mainly consists of algae, detritus, and small invertebrates, making it an important part of aquatic ecosystems.
