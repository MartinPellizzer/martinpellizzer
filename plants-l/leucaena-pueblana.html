Leucaena pueblana

Leucaena pueblana, commonly known as Mexican golden shrub, is a shrub native to Central America and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for reforestation and land restoration.

Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine, and it can fix atmospheric nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
