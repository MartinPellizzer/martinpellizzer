Lycopodium assurgens var. schwackei

Lycopodium assurgens var. schwackei, commonly known as the Schwacke's clubmoss, is a species of lycophyte belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and the presence of sporophylls that bear spores. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures are clustered in strobila, which are cone-like formations.

The species is notable for its historical use in traditional medicine and its ecological role in soil stabilization.
