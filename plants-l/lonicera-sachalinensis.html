Lonicera sachalinensis

Lonicera sachalinensis, commonly known as the Sakhalin honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are usually yellow or orange in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its fruits are red berries that mature in late summer, attracting various birds and small mammals.

Lonicera sachalinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
