Luzula spicata var. kjellmanii

Luzula spicata var. kjellmanii, commonly known as Kjellman's woodrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, greenish flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

This variety is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
