Lobivia pentlandii f. schneideriana

Lobivia pentlandii f. schneideriana, commonly known as the Schneider's Lobivia, is a rare and compact cactus native to southern Africa.

It features a short, cylindrical stem with a greenish-gray color and sparse, white to pale yellow spines. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or violet in color, blooming in late spring or early summer. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in controlled environments by succulent enthusiasts.
