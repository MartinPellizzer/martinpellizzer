Lampranthus erratus

Lampranthus erratus, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a striking succulent native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its dense, upright clusters of bright red or orange tubular flowers. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. It blooms primarily during the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
