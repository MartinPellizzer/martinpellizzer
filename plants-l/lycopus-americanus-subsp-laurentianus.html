Lycopus americanus subsp. laurentianus

Lycopus americanus subsp.

laurentianus, commonly known as Canadian blueweed, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and stream banks.

The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, blue to purple flowers in clusters. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, and it is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
