Lycium kraussii

Lycium kraussii, commonly known as the coastal gooseberry, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, yellow-orange berries that ripen in late summer. The plant thrives in coastal environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils. Its leaves are elliptical and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are typically pale yellow.

Lycium kraussii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
