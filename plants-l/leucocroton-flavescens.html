Leucocroton flavescens

Leucocroton flavescens, commonly known as the golden croton, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has striking, palmate leaves that are typically green with a reddish or purple underside.
