Lupinus bartlettianus

Lupinus bartlettianus, commonly known as the Bartlett's lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Lupinus bartlettianus is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through its root nodules.

It plays an important ecological role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing soil in its native environment.
