Lepidium oxycarpum var. acutidens

Lepidium oxycarpum var. acutidens, commonly known as the sharp-leaved wall cress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its slender stems and small, pinnately divided leaves. The flowers are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in racemes, and are composed of four petals. The plant produces small, spherical fruits known as siliques, which contain several seeds.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in coastal areas and rocky habitats.
