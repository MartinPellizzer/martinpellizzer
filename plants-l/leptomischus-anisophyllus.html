Leptomischus anisophyllus

Leptomischus anisophyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a robust, upright stem that is often hairy and covered in spines. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough, serrated margin and a bluish-gray underside. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a dense, globular head, typical of the Asteraceae family.

The plant is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
