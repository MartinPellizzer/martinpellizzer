Loeseneriella chesseana

Loeseneriella chesseana, commonly known as the Chessean lichen, is a species of lichen that belongs to the family Parmeliaceae.

It is characterized by its crustose growth form, forming a thin, tightly adherent layer on rock surfaces. The thallus is typically pale green to yellowish in color, with a finely granular texture. It is often found in humid, shaded environments, particularly on limestone or granite substrates.

This lichen is of interest to mycologists and botanists due to its ecological significance and potential role in bioindication.
