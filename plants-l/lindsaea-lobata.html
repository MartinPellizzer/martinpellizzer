Lindsaea lobata

Lindsaea lobata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that often form a compact, rosette-like structure. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically growing on trees or rocks in the understory of forests. Its spores are produced in small, clustered structures called sori, which are located on the underside of the fertile fronds.

Lindsaea lobata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant due to its unique appearance and air-purifying qualities.
