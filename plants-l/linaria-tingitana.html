Linaria tingitana

Linaria tingitana, commonly known as the Moroccan toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow-orange flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is typically found in rocky or sandy soils. It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Linaria tingitana is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.
