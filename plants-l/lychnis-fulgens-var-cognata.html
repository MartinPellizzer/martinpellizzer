Lychnis fulgens var. cognata

Lychnis fulgens var. cognata, commonly known as the showy campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in dense, terminal clusters.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
