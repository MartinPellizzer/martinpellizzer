Lupinus latifolius var. subalpinus

Lupinus latifolius var. subalpinus, commonly known as the Alpine broadleaf lupine, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves and tall spires of blue to purple flowers. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. It plays an important ecological role by fixing nitrogen into the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.

Its striking appearance makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens and restoration projects.
