Lippia boliviana

Lippia boliviana, commonly known as Bolivian blueweed, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Bolivia and Peru.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, clustered blue or purple flowers. The plant is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has been used traditionally in folk medicine for its purported healing properties.

Lippia boliviana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential as a source of essential oils.
