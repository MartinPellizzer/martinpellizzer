Lonicera obovata

Lonicera obovata, commonly known as the broad-leaf honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, ovate to obovate leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly hairy underside. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that range from yellow to orange, often appearing in clusters. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that ripens in late summer.

Lonicera obovata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
