Lactuca serriola subsp. integrifolia

Lactuca serriola subsp.

integrifolia, commonly known as prickly lettuce, is a perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. It is characterized by its hairy, lobed leaves and a distinctive, spiny stem.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, followed by seed heads that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in disturbed soils and is commonly found along roadsides and in dry fields.

Its botanical features make it a resilient and adaptable species within its native range.
