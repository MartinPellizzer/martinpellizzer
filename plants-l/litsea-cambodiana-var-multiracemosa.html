Litsea cambodiana var. multiracemosa

Litsea cambodiana var. multiracemosa, commonly known as the Cambodian litsea, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, small, elliptical leaves that are often hairy beneath. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, round berries. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
