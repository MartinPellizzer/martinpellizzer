Laelia perrinii f. alba

Laelia perrinii f. alba, commonly known as the white-flowered Laelia, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Andes Mountains.

It is characterized by its striking white petals and a pale yellow lip, which give it a delicate and elegant appearance. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. It produces fragrant, long-lasting blooms that can last several weeks during the flowering season.

This variety is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
