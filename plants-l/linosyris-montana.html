Linosyris montana

Linosyris montana, commonly known as the mountain linosyris, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and linear, while its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in shades of yellow or white.

Linosyris montana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
