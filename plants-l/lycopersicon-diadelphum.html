Lycopersicon diadelphum

Lycopersicon diadelphum, commonly known as the purple tomato, is a species of nightshade plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple to red fruits, which are typically smaller than those of the common tomato. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that are pollinated by insects. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

The fruit is edible and has a slightly tart flavor, making it a unique addition to culinary uses.
