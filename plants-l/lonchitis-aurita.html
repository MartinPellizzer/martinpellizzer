Lonchitis aurita

Lonchitis aurita, commonly known as the golden ear orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, ear-shaped petals. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with a distinctive, elongated spur.

Botanically, it is noted for its delicate structure and specialized pollination mechanisms.
