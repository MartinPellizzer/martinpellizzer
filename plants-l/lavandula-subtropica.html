Lavandula subtropica

Lavandula subtropica, commonly known as Spanish lavender, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and spikes of purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for Mediterranean climates. It is often used in herbal remedies and as a decorative plant in gardens.

Its essential oils are valued for their calming properties and use in aromatherapy.
