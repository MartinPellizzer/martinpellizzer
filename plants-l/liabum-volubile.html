Liabum volubile

Liabum volubile, commonly known as the climbing honeysuckle, is a vigorous vine native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces fragrant, tubular flowers that range from yellow to orange in color. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to climb various structures, making it both ornamental and invasive in some regions. Its fruit is a small, red berry that ripens in late summer and is edible but often bitter.

Liabum volubile is often cultivated for its ornamental value but requires careful management due to its aggressive growth habit.
