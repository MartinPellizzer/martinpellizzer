Lonicera caerulea var. solonis

Lonicera caerulea var. solonis, commonly known as the Solonos honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in early summer, typically in shades of yellow to orange. The plant produces small, edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tart, honey-like flavor. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and hedgerows.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its potential use in culinary and medicinal applications.
