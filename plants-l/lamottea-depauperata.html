Lamottea depauperata

Lamottea depauperata, commonly known as the poor man's begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink and red. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of care, it is widely cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
