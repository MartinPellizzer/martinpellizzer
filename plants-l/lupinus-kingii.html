Lupinus kingii

Lupinus kingii, commonly known as King's lupin, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bright blue to purple flowers. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces legume pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils.

Lupinus kingii is a member of the legume family and has the ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
