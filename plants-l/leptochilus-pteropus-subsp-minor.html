Leptochilus pteropus subsp. minor

Leptochilus pteropus subsp.

minor, commonly known as the lesser rock orchid, is a small perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often on slopes or cliffs.

The plant features a slender stem with a single, narrow lance-shaped leaf. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and arranged in a spike, blooming in early spring.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.
