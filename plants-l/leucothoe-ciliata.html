Leucothoe ciliata

Leucothoe ciliata, commonly known as silky alder, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant features small, elliptical leaves with a distinctive silvery-white underside, giving it its common name. Its flowers are white and clustered in upright racemes, appearing in late spring.

The berries that follow are dark blue and serve as a food source for birds and other wildlife.
