Launaea acanthoclada

Launaea acanthoclada, commonly known as the spiny leaf sunflower, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, spiny leaves. The plant features a thick, succulent stem that helps it retain water in dry environments. Its leaves are typically covered in fine hairs and have a distinctive spiny margin, which serves as a defense mechanism against herbivores.

Launaea acanthoclada is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.
