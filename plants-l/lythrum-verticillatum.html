Lythrum verticillatum

Lythrum verticillatum, commonly known as purple fringed loosestrife, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in dense spikes during the summer months. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in wetland habitats such as marshes and pond edges.

Its botanical name reflects its clustered inflorescence and the structure of its flowers.
