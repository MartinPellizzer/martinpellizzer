Lamium purpureum f. albiflorum

Lamium purpureum f. albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered deadnettle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with a hairy texture and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks. It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its spreading growth habit and ability to form dense mats.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
