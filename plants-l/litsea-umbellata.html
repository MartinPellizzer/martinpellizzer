Litsea umbellata

Litsea umbellata, commonly known as the fragrant wild lime, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions of India, China, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white flowers that emit a strong, pleasant scent. Its fruit is a small, yellowish drupe that ripens in the summer.

Litsea umbellata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the essential oils extracted from its leaves and fruits.
