Lycurus muticus

Lycurus muticus, commonly known as the silent lycurus, is a rare and elusive plant native to the dense, humid forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, waxy leaves that range in color from deep green to almost metallic silver. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom at night, relying on nocturnal pollinators such as moths and bats. Its unique ability to absorb moisture through its leaves makes it well adapted to its moist, shaded habitat.

Due to its rarity and specialized ecological requirements, Lycurus muticus is considered a vulnerable species.
