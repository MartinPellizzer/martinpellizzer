Limnanthemum minimum

Limnanthemum minimum, commonly known as the small water violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh environments.

It features small, delicate flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, with five petals and a yellow center. The plant has slender, spreading stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often submerged or floating. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it an important species in aquatic ecosystems.

Limnanthemum minimum plays a role in supporting aquatic insect life and is often used in restoration projects for wetland habitats.
