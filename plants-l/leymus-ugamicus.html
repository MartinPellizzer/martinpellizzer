Leymus ugamicus

Leymus ugamicus, commonly known as the Mongolian needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. This plant thrives in dry, semi-arid environments and is often found in steppe and grassland habitats. Leymus ugamicus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for grazing animals.

Its ability to withstand harsh conditions makes it a resilient component of its ecosystem.
