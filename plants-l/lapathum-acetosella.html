Lapathum acetosella

Lapathum acetosella, commonly known as Lesser Deadnettle, is a low-growing herbaceous plant belonging to the family Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are hairy and often have a reddish or purple tinge. The plant produces small, two-lipped flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stems. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and meadows.

Lapathum acetosella is often found in temperate regions of Europe, Asia, and North America.
