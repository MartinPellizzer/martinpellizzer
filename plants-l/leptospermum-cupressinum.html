Leptospermum cupressinum

Leptospermum cupressinum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tea tree, is a shrub native to Tasmania.

It is characterized by its aromatic, needle-like leaves and small, pink or white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, woody fruits called capsules, which contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.

Leptospermum cupressinum is valued for its essential oils and has traditional uses in both medicine and aromatherapy.
