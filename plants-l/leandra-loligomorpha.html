Leandra loligomorpha

Leandra loligomorpha, commonly known as the golden star orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers that resemble stars, which bloom in clusters during the warmer months. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts of exotic plants.
