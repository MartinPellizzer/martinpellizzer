Lasionema pubescens

Lasionema pubescens, commonly known as the hairy bamboo or Chinese bamboo, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that often form dense clumps, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in panicles, which are typically borne on the upper parts of the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Lasionema pubescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
