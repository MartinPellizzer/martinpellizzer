Lupinus jacob-andersonii

Lupinus jacob-andersonii, commonly known as Jacob-Anderson's lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and showy, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Lupinus jacob-andersonii is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and unique ecological adaptations.
