Lathyrus erectus

Lathyrus erectus, commonly known as the upright vetch, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, followed by elongated pods containing lens-shaped seeds. Lathyrus erectus is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a valuable plant in ecological and agricultural contexts.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed habitats.
