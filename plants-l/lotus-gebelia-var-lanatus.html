Lotus gebelia var. lanatus

Lotus gebelia var. lanatus, commonly known as the woolly lotus, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is cultivated in gardens and natural habitats alike.
