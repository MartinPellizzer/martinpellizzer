Lexipyretum

Lexipyretum, commonly known as the feverfew plant, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dark green, slightly hairy leaves and yellow-orange flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant is known for its medicinal properties, particularly its use in treating headaches and fever. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a hardy perennial in temperate climates.

Its unique appearance and historical significance make it a notable addition to botanical gardens and herbal collections.
