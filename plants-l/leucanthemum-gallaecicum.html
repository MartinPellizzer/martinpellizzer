Leucanthemum gallaecicum

Leucanthemum gallaecicum, commonly known as the Galician white marguerite, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of basal leaves that are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs.

Leucanthemum gallaecicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
