Linnaea borealis f. leucoloba

Linnaea borealis f. leucoloba, commonly known as the white-flowered twinflower, is a rare and striking variant of the twinflower plant.

It is characterized by its pale white flowers, which contrast with the typical pink or red hues of the species. The plant is a deciduous shrub native to northern regions of North America and Eurasia. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in boreal and subarctic environments.

Its botanical features make it a unique subject for ecological and horticultural study.
