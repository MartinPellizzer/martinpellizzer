Lepidium pratense var. ramburei

Lepidium pratense var. ramburei, commonly known as the Rambure's cress, is a variant of the common cress species native to certain regions of Europe.

It is a small, annual herb that typically grows in moist, disturbed soils such as roadside banks and wet meadows. The plant features alternate, pinnately lobed leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters. Its fruits are slender, elongated siliques that contain several seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and early spring blooms.
