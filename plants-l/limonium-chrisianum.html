Limonium chrisianum

Limonium chrisianum, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in sandy or saline soils and is often found near coastal areas. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Limonium chrisianum is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
