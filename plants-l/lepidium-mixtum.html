Lepidium mixtum

Lepidium mixtum, commonly known as wild cress, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other plants like mustard and cabbage. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in meadows. Its small, white or yellowish flowers are followed by rounded, seed-containing pods.

Lepidium mixtum is known for its peppery flavor and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
