Lepidium tofaceum

Lepidium tofaceum, commonly known as the rock cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of pinnately divided, hairy leaves and small, white or yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or arctic regions. It produces small, rounded pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Lepidium tofaceum is a hardy perennial that is often found in high-altitude environments across the northern hemisphere.
