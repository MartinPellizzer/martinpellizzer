Lilio-hyacinthus

Lilio-hyacinthus, commonly known as the hyacinth, is a bulbous plant belonging to the Asparagaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its dense clusters of colorful, fragrant flowers. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and has a fibrous root system. Hyacinths bloom in early spring and come in shades of blue, purple, white, and pink.

They are often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and floral arrangements.
