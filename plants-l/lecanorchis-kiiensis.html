Lecanorchis kiiensis

Lecanorchis kiiensis, commonly known as the Kii orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Japan.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its roots are mycorrhizal, forming symbiotic relationships with fungi to enhance nutrient absorption.

Lecanorchis kiiensis is a rare species, often found in specific microhabitats, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
