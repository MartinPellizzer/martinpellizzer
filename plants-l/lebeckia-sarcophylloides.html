Lebeckia sarcophylloides

Lebeckia sarcophylloides, commonly known as the golden lebeckia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has a low-growing, mat-forming habit with silvery-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
