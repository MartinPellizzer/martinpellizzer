Lisianthius pendulus

Lisianthius pendulus, commonly known as the weeping lantana, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its drooping clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 feet. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
