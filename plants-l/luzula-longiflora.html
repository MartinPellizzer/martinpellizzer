Luzula longiflora

Luzula longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered wood rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, commonly known as the grass family, and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage. The plant produces tall, slender flowering stems that bear clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowers are typically greenish or pale blue in color and are arranged in dense, cylindrical spikes.

Luzula longiflora is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
