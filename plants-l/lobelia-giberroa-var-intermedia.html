Lobelia giberroa var. intermedia

Lobelia giberroa var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and swampy areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partially shaded conditions.
