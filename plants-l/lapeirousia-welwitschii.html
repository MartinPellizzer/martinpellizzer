Lapeirousia welwitschii

Lapeirousia welwitschii, commonly known as the Welwitsch's orchid, is a striking bulbous plant native to the Western Cape of South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stem and showy, pale pink to white flowers. The plant produces a single, upright spike bearing several flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
