Leandra raimondiana

Leandra raimondiana, commonly known as the Raimond's arum, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean, particularly Cuba.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and striking, spadix-like inflorescence that resembles a torch. The plant belongs to the Araceae family, which includes other familiar genera such as philodendron and peace lily. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil.

Leandra raimondiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique foliage.
