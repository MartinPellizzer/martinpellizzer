Lithops pseudotruncatella var. riehmerae

Lithops pseudotruncatella var. riehmerae, commonly known as the "ice plant" or "living stone," is a succulent native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that resemble stones, helping it blend into its arid environment. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers during the spring or summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for xerophytic gardens.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
