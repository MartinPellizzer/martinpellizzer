Linaria dissita var. parviflora

Linaria dissita var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered sand rocket, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
