Linaria delphinioides

Linaria delphinioides, commonly known as the Delphinium-like toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its showy, spurred flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Linaria delphinioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
