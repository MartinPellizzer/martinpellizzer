Lupinus austrorientalis var. jujuyensis

Lupinus austrorientalis var. jujuyensis, commonly known as the Jujuy lupin, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and clusters of pea-like flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant produces cylindrical pods containing several seeds, which are adapted for dispersal by wind. It thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential as a forage crop.
