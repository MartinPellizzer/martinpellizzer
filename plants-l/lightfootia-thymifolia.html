Lightfootia thymifolia

Lightfootia thymifolia, commonly known as the thyme-leaved lightfootia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features small, narrow leaves that resemble those of thyme, giving it its common name. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Lightfootia thymifolia is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
