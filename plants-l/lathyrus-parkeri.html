Lathyrus parkeri

Lathyrus parkeri, commonly known as the Persian vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is a perennial legume that typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pale blue to purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its trifoliate leaves are compound, with each leaflet being oval-shaped and slightly hairy.

Lathyrus parkeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
