Lobelia fastigiata

Lobelia fastigiata, commonly known as the upright lobelia, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Lobelia fastigiata is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
