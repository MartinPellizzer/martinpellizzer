Lonicera ciliosa var. occidentalis

Lonicera ciliosa var. occidentalis, commonly known as the western honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features slender, arching stems that produce fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a hairy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adapted to well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value comes from its showy blooms and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
