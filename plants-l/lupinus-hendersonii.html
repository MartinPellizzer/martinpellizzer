Lupinus hendersonii

Lupinus hendersonii, commonly known as Henderson's lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Lupinus hendersonii plays an important role in nitrogen fixation, enriching the soil for other plants.
