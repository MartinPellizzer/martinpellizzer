Livistona merrillii

Livistona merrillii, commonly known as the Queensland sheoak, is a species of palm native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clustered inflorescences, followed by edible, black, spherical fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland areas with a subtropical climate.

Livistona merrillii is valued for its ornamental appearance and its role in local ecosystems.
