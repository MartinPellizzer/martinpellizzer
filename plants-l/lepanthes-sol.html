Lepanthes sol

Lepanthes sol, commonly known as the sun orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, yellow, and pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and cloud forests. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern.

Lepanthes sol is known for its unique floral structure, which includes a prominent lip used to attract pollinators.
