Lippia hypoleia var. ovatifolia

Lippia hypoleia var. ovatifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf lippia, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, clustered white to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its foliage contains essential oils that contribute to its distinctive scent and potential medicinal properties.

It is often used in traditional herbal practices for its purported health benefits.
