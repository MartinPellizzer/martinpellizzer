Lathyrus pseudocicera

Lathyrus pseudocicera, commonly known as the Turkish vetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial legume that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and can thrive in arid conditions. The plant features slender, climbing stems and produces small, pea-like flowers in shades of pink or purple. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Lathyrus pseudocicera is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural purposes.
