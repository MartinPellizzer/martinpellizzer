Lobelia mucronata var. berteroi

Lobelia mucronata var. berteroi, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lobelia, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant features slender stems and small, tubular blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with appropriate moisture conditions.
