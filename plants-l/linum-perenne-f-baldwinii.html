Linum perenne f. baldwinii

Linum perenne f. baldwinii, commonly known as the Baldwin's flax, is a perennial flowering plant native to the northeastern United States.

It belongs to the genus Linum, which includes various species of flax known for their showy blue or white flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The flowers are borne in clusters and are characterized by their five petals, with the inner two petals often being larger and more showy.
