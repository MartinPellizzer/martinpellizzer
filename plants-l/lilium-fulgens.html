Lilium fulgens

Lilium fulgens, commonly known as the fiery lily, is a striking perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep red, often with a distinctive orange or yellow throat. The plant typically blooms in late summer to early autumn, producing fragrant, trumpet-shaped blossoms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a fan-like pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Lilium fulgens prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and cottage landscapes.
