Lactuca sonchus

Lactuca sonchus, commonly known as prickly lettuce, is a flowering plant in the sunflower family (Asteraceae).

It is characterized by its hairy, lobed leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia, and it has been naturalized in various regions around the world. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

The species is known for its milky sap, which can cause skin irritation upon contact.
