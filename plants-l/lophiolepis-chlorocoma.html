Lophiolepis chlorocoma

Lophiolepis chlorocoma, commonly known as the yellow-flowered grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in panicles, which are typically visible during the summer months. Lophiolepis chlorocoma is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance through its deep root system and reduced leaf surface area.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for livestock in its native habitat.
