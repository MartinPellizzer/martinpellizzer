Lactuca reviersii

Lactuca reviersii, commonly known as the narrow-leaf lettuce, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is often found in meadows and grassy areas.

Lactuca reviersii is not typically cultivated for food but is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
