Lobelia psilophylla

Lobelia psilophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf lobelia, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, tubular flowers are usually blue or purple, arranged in spikes, and bloom in the spring.

Lobelia psilophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
