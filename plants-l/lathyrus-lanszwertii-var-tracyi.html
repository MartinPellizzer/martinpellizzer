Lathyrus lanszwertii var. tracyi

Lathyrus lanszwertii var. tracyi, commonly known as Tracy's grass vetch, is a perennial legume native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in open woodlands and disturbed sites, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features slender, trailing stems with small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. Its trifoliate leaves are compound, with three oval-shaped leaflets.

This species is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a food source for native wildlife.
