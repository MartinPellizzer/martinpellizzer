Loudetia thomasii

Loudetia thomasii, commonly known as Thomas's lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, typical of grasses. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly hairy surface, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Loudetia thomasii is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species suitable for dryland restoration projects.
