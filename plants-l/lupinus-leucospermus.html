Lupinus leucospermus

Lupinus leucospermus, commonly known as the white-flowered lupin, is a perennial legume native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Lupinus leucospermus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
