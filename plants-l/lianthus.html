Lianthus

Lianthus, commonly known as the "flowering grass," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Poaceae.

These plants are native to various regions of the world, including parts of Africa, Asia, and the Americas. Lianthus species are characterized by their tall, grass-like appearance and their ability to produce showy, often colorful flowers. They are typically herbaceous perennials that thrive in a variety of soil types and climates.

The genus includes several species that are cultivated for ornamental purposes due to their striking floral displays.
