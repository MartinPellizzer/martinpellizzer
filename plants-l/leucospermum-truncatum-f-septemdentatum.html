Leucospermum truncatum f. septemdentatum

Leucospermum truncatum f. septemdentatum, commonly known as the seven-toothed sugarbush, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, serrated leaf margins. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its foliage is evergreen, with leathery leaves that help it conserve water in its native arid environment.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
