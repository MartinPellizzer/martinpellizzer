Ligusticum peloponense

Ligusticum peloponense, commonly known as the Peloponnesian lovage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and umbellate flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a strong, spicy scent, while its flowers are small and white, forming clusters at the top of the stems.

Ligusticum peloponense is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.
