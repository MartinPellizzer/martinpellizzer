Loranthus lindensis

Loranthus lindensis, commonly known as the Lind's mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while it provides some carbohydrates through photosynthesis using its own leaves. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers and red berries that are important for bird dispersal. Its parasitic nature makes it an ecologically significant component of forest ecosystems.

Loranthus lindensis is often found in areas with high humidity and is a subject of interest in botanical studies due to its unique growth habits.
