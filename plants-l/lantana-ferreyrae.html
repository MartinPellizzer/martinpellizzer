Lantana ferreyrae

Lantana ferreyrae, commonly known as the Mexican lantana, is a flowering shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, brightly colored flowers, typically in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Lantana ferreyrae is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
