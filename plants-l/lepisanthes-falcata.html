Lepisanthes falcata

Lepisanthes falcata, commonly known as the sickle-shaped orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive sickle-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
