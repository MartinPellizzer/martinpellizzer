Lupinus cusickii subsp. cusickii

Lupinus cusickii subsp.

cusickii, commonly known as the Cusick's lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States. It is characterized by its erect growth habit and clusters of pea-like blue to violet flowers.

The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, hairy pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Lupinus cusickii subsp.

cusickii plays an important role in nitrogen fixation and supports local pollinator populations.
