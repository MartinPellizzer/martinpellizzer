Lipochaeta umbellata

Lipochaeta umbellata, commonly known as the clustered ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect, herbaceous stems and clusters of small, pinkish to reddish flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.

Lipochaeta umbellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
