Lobivia caespitosa var. violacea

Lobivia caespitosa var. violacea, commonly known as the violet lobivia, is a compact, low-growing succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It features dense, clustered rosettes of fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often tinged with a striking violet hue. The plant produces tall, branched stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of violet or purple during the warmer months. It is well adapted to harsh, dry environments and requires minimal water once established.

Due to its unique coloration and compact form, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and container cultivation.
