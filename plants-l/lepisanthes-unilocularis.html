Lepisanthes unilocularis

Lepisanthes unilocularis, commonly known as the single-chambered orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, adapting to its humid, shaded forest habitat.

Lepisanthes unilocularis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and unique floral structure.
