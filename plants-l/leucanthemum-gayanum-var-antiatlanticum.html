Leucanthemum gayanum var. antiatlanticum

Leucanthemum gayanum var. antiatlanticum, commonly known as the Atlantic leucanthemum, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the Atlantic Ocean.

It is characterized by its white to pale yellow flowers and dark green, lance-shaped leaves. This variety thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dune environments. It is a low-growing perennial that produces a profusion of blooms from late spring to early summer.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize coastal soils.
