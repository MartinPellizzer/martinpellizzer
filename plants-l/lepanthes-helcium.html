Lepanthes helcium

Lepanthes helcium, commonly known as the golden star orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow petals and a central white or yellowish callus. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in the understory of tropical forests. Its flowers are relatively small but highly ornamental, with a complex structure typical of orchids.

Lepanthes helcium is valued in horticulture for its beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
