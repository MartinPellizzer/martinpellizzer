Lophopetalum sessilifolium

Lophopetalum sessilifolium, commonly known as the Chinese mahonia, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features compound leaves with elliptical, sessile leaflets that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its adaptability and aesthetic appeal contribute to its use in gardens and landscapes.
