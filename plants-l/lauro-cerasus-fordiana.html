Lauro-cerasus fordiana

Lauro-cerasus fordiana, commonly known as the Chinese cherry laurel, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Prunus genus and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that turn from red to black as they mature.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and aromatic qualities.
