Limodorum abortivum var. anatolicum

Limodorum abortivum var. anatolicum, commonly known as the Anatolian adder's mouth, is a rare and distinctive orchid species native to Turkey.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers with a dark purple throat, resembling the mouth of a snake. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are elongated and lance-shaped, often with a waxy texture.

This variety is noted for its limited distribution and is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
