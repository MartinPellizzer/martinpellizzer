Licuala bankae

Licuala bankae, commonly known as the Malayan palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a large, fan-shaped palm with a robust trunk and long, narrow leaves. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests. Its leaves are used in traditional crafts and as thatching material.

The plant has a slow growth rate and is valued for its ornamental and practical uses.
