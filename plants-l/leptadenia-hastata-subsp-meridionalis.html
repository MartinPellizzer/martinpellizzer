Leptadenia hastata subsp. meridionalis

Leptadenia hastata subsp.

meridionalis, commonly known as the Southern Spiny Caper, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its spiny stems and oval, waxy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along riverbanks.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a ornamental plant.
