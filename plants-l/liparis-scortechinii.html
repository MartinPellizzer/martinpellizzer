Liparis scortechinii

Liparis scortechinii, commonly known as the Scortechini's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, often forming a rosette at the base.

Liparis scortechinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
