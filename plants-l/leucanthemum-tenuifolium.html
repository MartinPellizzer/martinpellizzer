Leucanthemum tenuifolium

Leucanthemum tenuifolium, commonly known as slenderleaf oxeye, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are white with a yellow center, and it blooms from late summer to early autumn.

The leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its common name.
