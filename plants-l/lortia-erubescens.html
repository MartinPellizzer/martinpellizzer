Lortia erubescens

Lortia erubescens, commonly known as the red-flowered lortia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces showy, clustered inflorescences of pink to red flowers during the spring and summer seasons. Its flowers are characterized by five petals and numerous stamens, making it a visually striking species.

Lortia erubescens thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded garden settings.
