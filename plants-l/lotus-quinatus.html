Lotus quinatus

Lotus quinatus, commonly known as the white lotus, is a perennial aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features showy, white flowers that bloom above the water surface, often with a subtle pink or purple hue. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, supported by long, slender stems. Its flowers are known for their fragrance and are used in traditional medicine and religious ceremonies.

Lotus quinatus thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.
