Leopoldia wallii

Leopoldia wallii, commonly known as the Wallis leopoldia, is a rare and unique flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, orchid-like flowers with a distinctive coloration. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and has a compact, herbaceous growth habit. Its botanical significance lies in its limited distribution and its role in the study of orchid diversity.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered an endangered species, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
