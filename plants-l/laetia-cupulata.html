Laetia cupulata

Laetia cupulata, commonly known as the hooded fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hood-like fronds that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and stream banks. Its fronds are usually dark green and have a leathery texture.

Laetia cupulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
