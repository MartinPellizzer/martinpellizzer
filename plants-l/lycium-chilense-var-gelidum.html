Lycium chilense var. gelidum

Lycium chilense var. gelidum, commonly known as the Chilean boxthorn, is a shrub native to the southern regions of South America.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, red berries that are typically consumed by birds. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, demonstrating a high degree of drought tolerance. Its leaves are usually small and elliptical, with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss.

This variety is often used in landscaping due to its hardy nature and ornamental value.
