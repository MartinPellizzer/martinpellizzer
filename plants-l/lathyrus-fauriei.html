Lathyrus fauriei

Lathyrus fauriei, commonly known as the Faurie's vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces clusters of pea-like flowers in shades of purple or pink. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several small, hard seeds.

Lathyrus fauriei is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
