Lathyrus brachypterus

Lathyrus brachypterus, commonly known as the dwarf sweet pea, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its slender stems and clusters of fragrant, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several elliptical seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, often found in woodland areas.

Lathyrus brachypterus is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
