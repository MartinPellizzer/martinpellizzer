Leea amabilis

Leea amabilis, commonly known as the sweetleaf tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that are usually dark green and glossy on the upper surface. The plant produces small, fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red berries. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
