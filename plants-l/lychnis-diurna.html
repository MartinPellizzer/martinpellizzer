Lychnis diurna

Lychnis diurna, commonly known as the morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the bindweed family (Convolvulaceae).

It is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, showy flowers that open in the morning and close by midday, hence its common name. The plant is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and it thrives in a variety of soil types, including sandy and clay soils. Its stems are typically twining or trailing, and it produces small, oval fruits that contain seeds.

Lychnis diurna is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
