Linum venustum

Linum venustum, commonly known as the velvetleaf flax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, blue-violet flowers and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sunlight. Its seeds are small and oval, containing oils that have been historically used in traditional medicine.

Linum venustum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
