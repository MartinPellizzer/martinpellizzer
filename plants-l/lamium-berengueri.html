Lamium berengueri

Lamium berengueri, commonly known as the Canary Island deadnettle, is a perennial herb native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of purple or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. It is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to the mild, humid climate of its native habitat.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners interested in native plant species.
