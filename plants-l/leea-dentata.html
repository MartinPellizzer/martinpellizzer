Leea dentata

Leea dentata, commonly known as the toothed leea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Leeaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles, often blooming in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

Leea dentata is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
