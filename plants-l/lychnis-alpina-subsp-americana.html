Lychnis alpina subsp. americana

Lychnis alpina subsp.

americana, commonly known as the American fireweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America. It belongs to the carnation family (Caryophyllaceae) and is characterized by its striking pink to red flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and streambanks, and can reach heights of up to 1 meter. Its basal leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the upper leaves are smaller and more elliptical.

It is known for its showy inflorescences that bloom from late summer to early fall.
