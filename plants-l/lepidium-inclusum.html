Lepidium inclusum

Lepidium inclusum, commonly known as the narrowleaf wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its small, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and it produces pods that contain tiny seeds.

Lepidium inclusum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
