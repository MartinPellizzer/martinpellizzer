Loxogramme acroscopa

Loxogramme acroscopa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water clover, is a small aquatic fern native to parts of Asia.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and streams. The plant has delicate, feathery fronds that are divided into small, lance-shaped segments. Its reproductive structures include sporangia that produce spores for propagation.
