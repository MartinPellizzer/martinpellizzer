Leucas stachydiformis

Leucas stachydiformis, commonly known as the sticky leucas, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its opposite, hairy leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
