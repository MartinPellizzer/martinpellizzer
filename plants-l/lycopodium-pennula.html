Lycopodium pennula

Lycopodium pennula, commonly known as the clubmoss, is a small, evergreen plant that belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its spore-bearing cones and its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its whorled arrangement of leaves and a creeping rhizome system. Lycopodium pennula is often found in forest floors and rocky areas, contributing to soil stabilization.

Its historical significance in traditional medicine and its role in ecological systems make it an important botanical subject.
