Leandra simplicicaulis

Leandra simplicicaulis, commonly known as the simple-leaved leandra, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its attractive, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually ovate or elliptical in shape. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
