Litsea mysorensis

Litsea mysorensis, commonly known as the Mysore litsea, is a small evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible for some bird species.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded areas of tropical forests and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
