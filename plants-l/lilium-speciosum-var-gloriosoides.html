Lilium speciosum var. gloriosoides

Lilium speciosum var. gloriosoides, commonly known as the glory lily, is a striking perennial plant native to southern China.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has narrow, strap-like leaves that form a rosette at the base, and its stems are often adorned with bracts that resemble petals. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

The flowers are not only ornamental but also attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
