Libanotis cachroides

Libanotis cachroides, commonly known as the Cape honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
