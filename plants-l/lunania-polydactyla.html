Lunania polydactyla

Lunania polydactyla, commonly known as the starry moonflower, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its large, white, star-shaped flowers that open at night and close during the day. The plant has a climbing habit and produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and slightly hairy.

Lunania polydactyla is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nocturnal blooming habit.
