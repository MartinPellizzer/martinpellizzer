Lactuca tatarica f. stevensii

Lactuca tatarica f. stevensii, commonly known as Tatarian lettuce, is a variant of the common sow thistle.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Central Asia and parts of Eastern Europe. The plant features basal rosettes of dark green, spoon-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. It produces small, yellow flowers in composite inflorescences, typical of the Asteraceae family.

This form is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some historical use in traditional medicine.
