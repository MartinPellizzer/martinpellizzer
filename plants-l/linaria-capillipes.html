Linaria capillipes

Linaria capillipes, commonly known as the slender toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stem, giving it a delicate appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Linaria capillipes is a perennial herb that is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
