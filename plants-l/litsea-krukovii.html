Litsea krukovii

Litsea krukovii, commonly known as the Malayan laurustin, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and bay trees. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, elliptical leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. It produces small, white, fragrant flowers followed by spherical, yellowish fruits.

Litsea krukovii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
