Lucuma elongata

Lucuma elongata, commonly known as the elongated lucuma, is a member of the Lauraceae family native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a small evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height, characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant flowers. The fruit is a distinctive elongated capsule that turns from green to yellow-orange when mature, containing numerous seeds. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and nutritional value, it is gaining interest for cultivation and culinary use.
