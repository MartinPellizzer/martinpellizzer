Linum vuralianum

Linum vuralianum, commonly known as the Armenian flax, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are often clustered in the leaf axils. It is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in temperate climates.

Linum vuralianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in textile production.
