Lepidium rotundifolium

Lepidium rotundifolium, commonly known as peppergrass, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is characterized by its small, round leaves and clusters of tiny white flowers. It produces small, round, brown seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Peppergrass is often found in arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in disturbed soils and open habitats.
