Lippia ligustrina var. schulziae

Lippia ligustrina var. schulziae, commonly known as the Schulz's lippia, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale purple, arranged in clusters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and fragrance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
