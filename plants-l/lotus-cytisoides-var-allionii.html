Lotus cytisoides var. allionii

Lotus cytisoides var. allionii, commonly known as the yellow lotus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being oval and slightly hairy.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
