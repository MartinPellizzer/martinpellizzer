Leonurus kudrjaschevii

Leonurus kudrjaschevii, commonly known as the Siberian hedgehog, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants, and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers arranged in whorls, which bloom in summer. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Due to its unique appearance and medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and herbalists alike.
