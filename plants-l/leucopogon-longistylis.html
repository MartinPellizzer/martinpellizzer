Leucopogon longistylis

Leucopogon longistylis, commonly known as the long-styled leucopogon, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red in color, with prominent stamens that extend beyond the petals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal heathlands and sandy areas.

Leucopogon longistylis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
