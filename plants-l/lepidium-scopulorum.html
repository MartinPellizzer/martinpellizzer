Lepidium scopulorum

Lepidium scopulorum, commonly known as rock pepper or stone cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, white or yellowish flowers and is typically found growing in rocky or gravelly soils. The plant has a low-growing habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its fruit is a slender, cylindrical silique that contains several small, black seeds.

Lepidium scopulorum is often used in herbal medicine and is valued for its potential medicinal properties.
