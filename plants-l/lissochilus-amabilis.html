Lissochilus amabilis

Lissochilus amabilis, commonly known as the pink orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom during the spring and summer seasons. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or forested areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

The orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
