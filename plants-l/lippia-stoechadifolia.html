Lippia stoechadifolia

Lippia stoechadifolia, commonly known as Mexican blueweed, is an aromatic herb native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and it often grows in dry, sunny habitats. Lippia stoechadifolia is known for its pungent scent and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

It is also a popular plant in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
