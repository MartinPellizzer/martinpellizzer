Lastrea divisa

Lastrea divisa, commonly known as the divided lastrea, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that come in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
