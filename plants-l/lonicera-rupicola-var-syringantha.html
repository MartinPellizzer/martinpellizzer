Lonicera rupicola var. syringantha

Lonicera rupicola var. syringantha, commonly known as the rock honeysuckle, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually yellow or orange in color, blooming in the spring. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that contains several seeds.

This species is adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a hardy plant in dry, well-drained soils.
