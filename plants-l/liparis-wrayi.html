Liparis wrayi

Liparis wrayi, commonly known as the Wray's orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, and it produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white, yellow, or green.

Liparis wrayi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
