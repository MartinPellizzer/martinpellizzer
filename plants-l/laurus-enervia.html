Laurus enervia

Laurus enervia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved laurel, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and aromatic. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by black, berry-like fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Laurus enervia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
