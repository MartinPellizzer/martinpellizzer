Lysimachia punctata var. tomentosa

Lysimachia punctata var. tomentosa, commonly known as hairy yellow-eyed grass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to parts of Asia.

It features small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, creeping stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological restoration and ornamental gardening.
