Lettsomia scortechinii

Lettsomia scortechinii, commonly known as the red-flowered orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, deep red petals and a distinctive, elongated flower spike. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within the understory of rainforests. Its flowers bloom during the warmer months, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Botanically, it belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological significance.
