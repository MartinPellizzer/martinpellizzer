Lantana velutina f. violacea

Lantana velutina f. violacea, commonly known as the violet velvet lantana, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from violet to deep purple. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant blooms and hardy nature. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for coastal and tropical gardens.

However, it can be invasive in some regions, requiring careful management to prevent ecological disruption.
