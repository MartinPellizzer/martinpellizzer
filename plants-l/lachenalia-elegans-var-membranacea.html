Lachenalia elegans var. membranacea

Lachenalia elegans var. membranacea, commonly known as the parchment lachenalia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and fleshy, often blue-green leaves that are covered in a thin, waxy layer. The plant produces tall, spindly stems with clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires ample sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance, it is often cultivated by succulent enthusiasts.
