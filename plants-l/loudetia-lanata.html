Loudetia lanata

Loudetia lanata, commonly known as the woolly sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage with soft, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, preferring moist soils and full sun to partial shade. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that bear small, three-lobed flowers, which are usually greenish or brownish in color.

Loudetia lanata is often used in ornamental gardening for its unique texture and ability to thrive in waterlogged conditions.
