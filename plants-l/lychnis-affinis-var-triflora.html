Lychnis affinis var. triflora

Lychnis affinis var. triflora, commonly known as the three-flowered catchfly, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in meadows, woodlands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are hairy along the edges. Its flowers are pink to red, with five petals and numerous stamens, blooming from June to August.

It is a perennial species that spreads through rhizomes and is often found in moist, well-drained habitats.
