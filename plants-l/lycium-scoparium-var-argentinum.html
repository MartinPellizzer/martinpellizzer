Lycium scoparium var. argentinum

Lycium scoparium var. argentinum, commonly known as the Argentine boxthorn, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its woody stems and small, spiny leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are typically followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in dry, sunny conditions and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in landscaping for erosion control.
