Lonchocarpus trinitensis

Lonchocarpus trinitensis, commonly known as the Trinidad legume, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly Trinidad and Tobago.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes and beans, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is notable for its striking yellow flowers and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it ecologically significant.

It is often found in moist, tropical habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
