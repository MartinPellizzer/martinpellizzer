Lockhartiopsis

Lockhartiopsis, commonly known as the silverleaf daisy, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. The leaves are typically silver or gray-green, covered in fine hairs that give them a distinctive appearance. Native to southern Africa, these plants are often found in rocky or sandy soils.

They are popular in gardens for their ornamental value and ability to thrive in dry conditions.
