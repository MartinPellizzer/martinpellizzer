Lonicera tatarica var. sibirica

Lonicera tatarica var. sibirica, commonly known as the Siberian honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy on the underside. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of yellow to orange, which bloom in late spring to early summer. These flowers are pollinated by insects and attract bees and butterflies.

The plant is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
