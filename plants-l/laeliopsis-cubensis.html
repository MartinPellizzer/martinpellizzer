Laeliopsis cubensis

Laeliopsis cubensis, commonly known as the Cuban orchid, is a species native to Cuba and parts of the Caribbean.

It is a herbaceous plant with fleshy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts interested in rare orchids.
