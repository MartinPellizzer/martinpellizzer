Lobivia pentlandii f. higginsiana

Lobivia pentlandii f. higginsiana, commonly known as the Higgins' Lobivia, is a compact, low-growing succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It features thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically blue-green in color, adapted to store water. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers during the spring or early summer. It is known for its slow growth and is often cultivated as a decorative succulent.

Its unique appearance and resilience make it a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
