Lilium oxypetalum

Lilium oxypetalum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink to red in color, with six narrow petals. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental value.

Lilium oxypetalum is valued in horticulture for its striking blooms and adaptability to well-drained soils.
