Licuala paniculata

Licuala paniculata, commonly known as the giant cycad or the Philippine cycad, is a striking evergreen plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its large, pinnately compound leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces distinctive cone-like structures, with male and female cones on separate plants, a feature typical of cycads. It thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil and ample sunlight.

Due to its slow growth and ecological significance, it is also considered a conservation concern in its native habitat.
