Lemna umbonata

Lemna umbonata, commonly known as the broad-leafed duckweed, is a small, floating aquatic plant native to temperate regions.

It has broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically 1 to 3 centimeters in diameter. The plant reproduces rapidly through vegetative propagation, with new plants forming from the nodes along the stems. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

Its simple structure and fast growth make it an important species in aquatic ecosystems.
