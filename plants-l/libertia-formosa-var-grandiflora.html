Libertia formosa var. grandiflora

Libertia formosa var. grandiflora, commonly known as the grandiflora lilies, is a striking perennial plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It features tall, slender stems that bear large, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are arranged in a fan-like pattern at the base. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its prolonged blooming period, typically from late spring to early summer.
