Lysimachia franchetii

Lysimachia franchetii, commonly known as the Chinese chickweed or Franchet's loosestrife, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from spring to early summer. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Lysimachia franchetii is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.
