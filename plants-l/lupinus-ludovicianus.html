Lupinus ludovicianus

Lupinus ludovicianus, commonly known as the plains lupine, is a perennial legume native to the central and western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces cylindrical seed pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Lupinus ludovicianus plays an important role in nitrogen fixation, improving soil fertility for other plants.
