Lepidium persicum subsp. persicum

Lepidium persicum subsp.

persicum, commonly known as Persian cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family. It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species native to Central Asia, particularly Iran and Afghanistan.

The plant features small, delicate white or yellow flowers arranged in racemes, and its leaves are pinnately divided with a peppery aroma. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in rocky or arid soils.

The seeds are small and can be used as a spice, similar to mustard.
