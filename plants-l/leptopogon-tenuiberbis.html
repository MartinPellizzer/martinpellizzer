Leptopogon tenuiberbis

Leptopogon tenuiberbis, commonly known as the slender-stemmed feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and fine, feathery leaves that give it a delicate appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and dry slopes. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in loose panicles, which are often overlooked due to their modest size.

Leptopogon tenuiberbis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
