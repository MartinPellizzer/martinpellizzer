Litsea cuspidata

Litsea cuspidata, commonly known as the Chinese evergreen or mountain laurel, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, elliptical leaves with a pointed tip. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by round, red or orange berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Litsea cuspidata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
