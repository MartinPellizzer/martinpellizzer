Lepanthes linealis

Lepanthes linealis, commonly known as the slender lady's tresses, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, linear leaves and its distinctive, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a subtle fragrance that attracts pollinators like bees and moths.

Lepanthes linealis is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological significance.
