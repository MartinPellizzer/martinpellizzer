Leandra cornoides

Leandra cornoides, commonly known as the velvetleaf orchid, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, often with intricate patterns. The plant has broad, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a soft, velvety texture. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Leandra cornoides is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
