Leptodactylon lilacinum f. pulchriflorum

Leptodactylon lilacinum f. pulchriflorum, commonly known as the lilac-flowered form of the common fern, is a varietal form of a fern species native to certain regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lanceolate leaves that exhibit a distinctive lilac or pale violet hue on the underside. The fronds are typically bipinnate, with a graceful, arching growth habit that adds ornamental value to gardens. This variety is prized for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated for its visual interest in shaded garden settings.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique coloration and its role in the study of fern morphology and horticultural variation.
