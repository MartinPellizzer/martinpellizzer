Lilium sibiricum

Lilium sibiricum, commonly known as the Siberian lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the cold regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate to subarctic climates. Its flowers, which can be white, pink, or red, bloom in summer and are often used in traditional medicine.

The bulbs of Lilium sibiricum are also edible when properly prepared.
