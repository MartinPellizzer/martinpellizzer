Laportea peduncularis subsp. peduncularis

Laportea peduncularis subsp.

peduncularis, commonly known as the creeping buttercup, is a flowering plant native to North America. It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with prostrate stems that root at the nodes.

The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy texture.

This species thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands, ditches, and along stream banks.
