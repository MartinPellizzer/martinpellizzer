Ledebouria ovatifolia subsp. ovatifolia

Ledebouria ovatifolia subsp.

ovatifolia, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a bulbous geophyte native to South Africa. It features striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a yellow throat.

The plant has linear, grass-like leaves that form a rosette around the bulb. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires periods of dormancy to bloom successfully.

This species is popular in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low water needs and ornamental value.
