Leucadendron pedunculatum

Leucadendron pedunculatum, commonly known as the silver tree, is a striking South African shrub or small tree characterized by its silvery, feathery foliage.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is native to the coastal regions of KwaZulu-Natal. The plant produces showy, cone-like inflorescences that range in color from yellow to red, depending on the species. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
