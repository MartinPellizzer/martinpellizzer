Listrostachys pynaertii

Listrostachys pynaertii, commonly known as the African rock fig, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and woody stems that form a compact, bushy structure. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spherical inflorescences, which later develop into edible, yellowish fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and used in traditional medicine.
