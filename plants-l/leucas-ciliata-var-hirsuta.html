Leucas ciliata var. hirsuta

Leucas ciliata var. hirsuta, commonly known as hairy leucas, is a flowering plant native to regions of southern Africa.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It produces small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
