Linnaea serrata var. hegii

Linnaea serrata var. hegii, commonly known as the Alaska snowbell, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It features small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges, which give it its scientific name. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in tundra and alpine habitats.

Linnaea serrata var. hegii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in cold climates.
