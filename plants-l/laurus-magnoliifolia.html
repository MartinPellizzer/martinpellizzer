Laurus magnoliifolia

Laurus magnoliifolia, commonly known as the bay laurel, is a flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark green, glossy leaves that are often used in cooking and traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark, edible berries. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in temperate climates and well-drained soil.

Its historical significance dates back to ancient times, where it was revered for its symbolic and practical uses.
