Lycium martii var. obovatum

Lycium martii var. obovatum, commonly known as the Chinese boxthorn, is a shrub native to China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it adaptable to various soil types.

Botanically, it belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshade plants.
