Lobivia famatinensis var. subcarnea

Lobivia famatinensis var. subcarnea, commonly known as the pink lobivia, is a succulent native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its rounded, ribbed stems that can store water. The plant produces vibrant pink or red flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, and it thrives in well-drained soil with plenty of sunlight.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
