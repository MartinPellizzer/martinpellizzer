Lonchocarpus guatemalensis var. fendleri

Lonchocarpus guatemalensis var. fendleri, commonly known as the Guatemalan sensitiveplant, is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits.

It is native to Central America and exhibits a climbing or shrub growth habit. The plant produces bright yellow flowers, which are often followed by elongated, woody pods containing several seeds. It is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight.

Due to its sensitive nature, it may wilt or curl in response to touch or environmental stress.
