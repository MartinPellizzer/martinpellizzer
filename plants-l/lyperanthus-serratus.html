Lyperanthus serratus

Lyperanthus serratus, commonly known as the serrated starflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive star-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are serrated and lance-shaped, contributing to its common name.

Lyperanthus serratus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
