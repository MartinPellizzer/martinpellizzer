Lathyrus venosus subsp. arkansanus

Lathyrus venosus subsp.

arkansanus, commonly known as the Arkansas vetch, is a perennial legume native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and slender, climbing stems that often produce small, pea-like flowers.

The plant has a deep root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adapted to a variety of soil types.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological restoration and wildlife habitat.
