Loranthus luzonensis

Loranthus luzonensis, commonly known as the Luzon mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that grows on the branches of various host trees in the Philippines.

It belongs to the Loranthaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, white or yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are often consumed by birds, which in turn help disperse its seeds. Its parasitic nature means it derives nutrients from its host, potentially affecting the host's growth and health.

Despite its parasitic habits, Loranthus luzonensis plays a role in local ecosystems and is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
