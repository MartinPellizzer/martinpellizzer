Lupinus ynesiae

Lupinus ynesiae, commonly known as the Ynesia lupine, is a species of flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and spiny stems. The plant produces showy, pea-like flowers that range in color from purple to pink, typically blooming in late spring. Lupinus ynesiae is adapted to arid and rocky environments, often growing in open woodlands and slopes.

Its seed pods contain small, hard seeds that are well-suited for dispersal by wind or animals.
