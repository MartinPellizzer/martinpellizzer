Lobivia haematantha var. amblayensis

Lobivia haematantha var. amblayensis, commonly known as the Amblay's lobivia, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded shape and thick, fleshy leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in drought conditions. The plant produces vibrant yellow to orange flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green foliage. Its leaves are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, and the stems are short and often covered in a fine, fuzzy coating.

This variety is prized by collectors for its unique appearance and resilience in challenging environments.
