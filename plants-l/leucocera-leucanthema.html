Leucocera leucanthema

Leucocera leucanthema, commonly known as the white-flowered orchid, is a species native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family, characterized by its delicate, white blooms. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, and the flowers are arranged in a spike-like cluster.

Leucocera leucanthema is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
