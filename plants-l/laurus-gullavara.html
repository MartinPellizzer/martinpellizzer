Laurus gullavara

Laurus gullavara, commonly known as the narrow-leaved laurel, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like bay laurel and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green, narrow leaves that are highly aromatic when crushed. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by spherical, dark blue berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Laurus gullavara is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for its fragrant foliage.
