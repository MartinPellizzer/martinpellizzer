Lessingianthus teyucuarensis

Lessingianthus teyucuarensis, commonly known as the Teyucuarensis sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and daisy-like appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its bright yellow to orange flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
