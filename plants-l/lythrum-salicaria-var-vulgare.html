Lythrum salicaria var. vulgare

Lythrum salicaria var. vulgare, commonly known as purple loosestrife, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and showy spikes of purple flowers that bloom from midsummer to early autumn. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are opposite along the stem and are often covered in fine hairs. Its roots are fibrous and can form dense colonies, making it a strong competitor in wetland environments.

It is often considered an invasive species in North America due to its rapid growth and ability to outcompete native vegetation.
