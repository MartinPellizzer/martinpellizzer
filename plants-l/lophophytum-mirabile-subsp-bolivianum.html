Lophophytum mirabile subsp. bolivianum

Lophophytum mirabile subsp.

bolivianum, commonly known as the Bolivian velvet plant, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the cloud forests of South America. It is characterized by its thick, velvety leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance.

The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white and pink, which bloom during the warm season. It thrives in high humidity and indirect light, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Its unique foliage and ornamental value make it a sought-after plant among collectors and enthusiasts.
