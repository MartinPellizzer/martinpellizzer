Lepidium lasiocarpum var. wrightii

Lepidium lasiocarpum var. wrightii, commonly known as Wright's peppergrass, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many mustard plants. The plant is characterized by its low-growing habit, with small, alternate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its fruit is a short, cylindrical silique, typical of the Brassicaceae family, and it produces small, rounded seeds.
