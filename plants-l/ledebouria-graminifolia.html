Ledebouria graminifolia

Ledebouria graminifolia, commonly known as the grass-leaved bulbous plant, is a species of flowering plant in the Asparagaceae family.

It is native to South Africa, where it typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils. The plant features a compact rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in arid conditions. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, often in shades of white or pale yellow.

Ledebouria graminifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in rock gardens and as a houseplant.
