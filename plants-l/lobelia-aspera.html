Lobelia aspera

Lobelia aspera, commonly known as rough lobelia, is a herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features hairy, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

Lobelia aspera is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other moist habitats.
