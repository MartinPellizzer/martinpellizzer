Leea saxatilis

Leea saxatilis, commonly known as the rock leeah, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Pacific.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree that typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or yellow, arranged in clusters along the stems. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Leea saxatilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
