Lepanthes hirtzii

Lepanthes hirtzii, commonly known as the hairy lepanthes, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant features a compact, terrestrial growth habit with a short, thick rhizome. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine, hairy structures, giving it its common name.

Lepanthes hirtzii thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
