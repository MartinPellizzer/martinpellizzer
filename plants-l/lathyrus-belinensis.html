Lathyrus belinensis

Lathyrus belinensis, commonly known as the Belin's vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are adapted for dispersal by wind.

Lathyrus belinensis is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including rocky areas and along roadsides.
