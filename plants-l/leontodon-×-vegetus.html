Leontodon × vegetus

Leontodon × vegetus, commonly known as the hybrid dandelion, is a composite plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a cross between Leontodon tectorum (common dandelion) and Leontodon alpinus (alpine dandelion). The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows. It produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by fluffy seed heads.

Its botanical characteristics include a basal rosette of leaves and a hollow, leafless stem.
