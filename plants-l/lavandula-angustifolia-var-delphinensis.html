Lavandula angustifolia var. delphinensis

Lavandula angustifolia var. delphinensis, commonly known as the Delphinensis lavender, is a compact, aromatic shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces spikes of fragrant, pale blue to lavender-colored flowers. This variety is prized for its strong scent and is often used in herbal teas and aromatherapy. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal water once established.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
