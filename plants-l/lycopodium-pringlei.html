Lycopodium pringlei

Lycopodium pringlei, commonly known as the Pringle's clubmoss, is a species of lycophyte belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its small, needle-like leaves and a sporophyte that produces spores in specialized structures called strobila. Native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its reproductive structures are prominent, with the strobila typically appearing at the tip of the stem.

Lycopodium pringlei plays a role in forest ecosystems by contributing to soil stability and providing microhabitats for various organisms.
