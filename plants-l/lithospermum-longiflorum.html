Lithospermum longiflorum

Lithospermum longiflorum, commonly known as the long-flowered rock pepper, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. The plant features slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. Its showy, tubular flowers are usually pink to purple and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Lithospermum longiflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
