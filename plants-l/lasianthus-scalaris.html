Lasianthus scalaris

Lasianthus scalaris, commonly known as the mountain balm, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in horticulture.
