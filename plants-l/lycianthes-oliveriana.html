Lycianthes oliveriana

Lycianthes oliveriana, commonly known as the Oliver's physalis, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its distinctive lantern-shaped, orange-red calyces that enclose the fruit. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with hairy stems and alternate leaves. Its flowers are small and yellow, with five petals, and it produces small, white to yellow fruits enclosed in a fleshy, persistent calyx.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has also been used in traditional medicine.
