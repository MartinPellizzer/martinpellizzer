Luma pitanga

Luma pitanga, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pitanga, is a small evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It produces bright red, cherry-like fruits that are rich in vitamin C and antioxidants. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves and white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its botanical name, *Solanum luma*, reflects its classification within the nightshade family.

The fruit is often used in traditional medicine and as a flavorful ingredient in local cuisines.
