Lobelia tenuifolia

Lobelia tenuifolia, commonly known as slenderleaf lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems covered with small, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of delicate, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland gardens or stream banks.

Lobelia tenuifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
