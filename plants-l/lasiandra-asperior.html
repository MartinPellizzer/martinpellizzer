Lasiandra asperior

Lasiandra asperior, commonly known as the rough lasiandra, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features upright, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in dense clusters at the tips of the stems.

Lasiandra asperior is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
