Lepanthes conchyliata

Lepanthes conchyliata, commonly known as the shell orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as forest understories, where it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a basal rosette, while its inflorescence bears small, colorful flowers.

Lepanthes conchyliata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
