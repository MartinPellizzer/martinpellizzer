Lophodium callipteris

Lophodium callipteris, commonly known as the mountain holly, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, glossy leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs. The plant produces bright red berries that are an important food source for various bird species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in humid, temperate climates.

Lophodium callipteris is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate partial shade.
