Loranthus baronii

Loranthus baronii, commonly known as the Baron's mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant belonging to the Loranthaceae family.

It attaches itself to host trees, primarily oaks and elms, through its haustoria to extract water and nutrients. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are often visited by insects for pollination. Its berries, which are typically red or orange, contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This species is native to parts of Europe and Asia and is often found in forested areas.
