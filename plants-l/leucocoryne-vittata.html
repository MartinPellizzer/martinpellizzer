Leucocoryne vittata

Leucocoryne vittata, commonly known as the Striped Leucocoryne, is a bulbous plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a distinctive white or pale stripe running along the central vein. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
