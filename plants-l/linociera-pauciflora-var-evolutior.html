Linociera pauciflora var. evolutior

Linociera pauciflora var. evolutior, commonly known as the slender thornapple, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It features small, pale green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, adapted to reduce water loss in dry environments. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by spherical, woody fruits that contain several seeds. Its thorny stems provide protection from herbivores, while its deep root system allows it to access groundwater.

This species is valued for its resilience and is often used in ecological restoration projects in its native habitat.
