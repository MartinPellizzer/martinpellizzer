Lonchitis-aspera capensis var. chilensis

Lonchitis-aspera capensis var. chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean roughleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered with fine, rough hairs. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open woodland or grassland habitats.
