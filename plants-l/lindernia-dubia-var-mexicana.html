Lindernia dubia var. mexicana

Lindernia dubia var. mexicana, commonly known as Mexican flameflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving them a soft texture. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract hummingbirds and other pollinators.
