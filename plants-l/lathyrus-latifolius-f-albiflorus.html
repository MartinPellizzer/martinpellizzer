Lathyrus latifolius f. albiflorus

Lathyrus latifolius f. albiflorus, commonly known as the white-flowered broadleaf pea, is a variant of the broadleaf pea plant characterized by its pale white or almost white flowers.

It is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically growing in moist, woodland habitats. The plant features compound leaves with oval to ovate leaflets and produces elongated pods containing small, edible seeds.

Its botanical significance lies in its ornamental value and its role in ecological studies related to plant genetics and hybridization.
