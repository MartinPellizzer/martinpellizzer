Lachnanthes tinctoria var. major

Lachnanthes tinctoria var. major, commonly known as the redroot false indigo, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmately divided leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in dyeing, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
