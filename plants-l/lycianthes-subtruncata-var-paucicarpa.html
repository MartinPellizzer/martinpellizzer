Lycianthes subtruncata var. paucicarpa

Lycianthes subtruncata var. paucicarpa, commonly known as the few-seeded physalis, is a species native to Central America.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its hairy stems and broad, lobed leaves. The plant produces clusters of orange, lantern-shaped fruits that enclose the seeds. Its flowers are typically yellow with five petals and are borne in the leaf axils.

This variety is notable for its reduced seed capsule, which is a distinguishing feature from other varieties within the Lycianthes genus.
