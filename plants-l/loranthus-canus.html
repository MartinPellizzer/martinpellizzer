Loranthus canus

Loranthus canus, commonly known as the white mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily in the genus Eucalyptus.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using the light it captures. The plant has small, scale-like leaves and produces white, fleshy berries that contain seeds dispersed by birds. It is native to Australia and is often found in coastal regions.

Its parasitic nature and unique ecological role make it an interesting subject in botanical studies.
