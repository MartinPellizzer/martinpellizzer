Lisianthius fistulosus

Lisianthius fistulosus, commonly known as the slender lisianthus, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it is considered a weed in some agricultural settings.
