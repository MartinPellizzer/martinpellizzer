Lithocarpus lappaceus var. perakensis

Lithocarpus lappaceus var. perakensis, commonly known as the Bornean tallow tree, is a member of the Fagaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, cupule-like fruits that contain a single, edible nut. It thrives in tropical rainforests and is often found in lowland to hill forest ecosystems.

Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in construction and furniture-making.
