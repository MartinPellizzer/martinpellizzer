Lessingianthus kuntzei

Lessingianthus kuntzei, commonly known as the yellow sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow ray flowers and green bracts. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant appearance.
