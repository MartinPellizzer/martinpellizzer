Lupinus albicaulis var. bridgesii

Lupinus albicaulis var. bridgesii, commonly known as the Bridges' lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces elongated pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

This variety is notable for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and its value as a food source for local wildlife.
