Leonotis kwebensis

Leonotis kwebensis, commonly known as the Cape lantern plant, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its striking, tubular, orange-red flowers that resemble lanterns. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often woody and branched.

Leonotis kwebensis is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
