Lepidium densipuberulum

Lepidium densipuberulum, commonly known as the dense-hairy wallflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard plants. The plant is characterized by its dense, glandular hairs that cover its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The species is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
