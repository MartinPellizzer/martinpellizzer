Lycopodium buesii

Lycopodium buesii, commonly known as the Buesii clubmoss, is a small evergreen plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems that bear small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces sporophylls that enclose the spores, a feature typical of clubmosses. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest undergrowth.

Lycopodium buesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological habitats.
