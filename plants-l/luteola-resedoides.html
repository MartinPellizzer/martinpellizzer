Luteola resedoides

Luteola resedoides, commonly known as the yellow everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, preferring full sun and well-drained soil. Its small, narrow leaves are usually arranged in a basal rosette, and it produces numerous small seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Luteola resedoides is often used in floral arrangements due to its long-lasting blooms and vibrant color.
