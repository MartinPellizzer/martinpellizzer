Linum micranthum

Linum micranthum, commonly known as small-flower flax, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and small, blue to purple flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Linum micranthum thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
