Limnanthemum congestiflorum

Limnanthemum congestiflorum, commonly known as the clustered water lily, is a aquatic plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its floating leaves that are typically heart-shaped and covered in a waxy cuticle to prevent water loss. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats, often found in ponds and marshes.

Its botanical structure supports its role in aquatic ecosystems by providing shelter for aquatic life and contributing to water filtration.
