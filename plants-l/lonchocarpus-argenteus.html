Lonchocarpus argenteus

Lonchocarpus argenteus, commonly known as silver senna, is a tropical leguminous plant characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is native to parts of Central and South America. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
