Lantana × albopurpurea

Lantana × albopurpurea, commonly known as the purple lantana, is a hybrid shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening. Its foliage is typically glossy and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral displays.

Lantana × albopurpurea is also noted for its strong fragrance, which attracts a variety of pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
