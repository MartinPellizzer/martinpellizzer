Lupinophyllum lupinifolium

Lupinophyllum lupinifolium, commonly known as the blue lupin, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, butterfly-like flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Its nitrogen-fixing ability makes it beneficial for soil enrichment, and it is often used in ecological restoration projects.
