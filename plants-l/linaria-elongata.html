Linaria elongata

Linaria elongata, commonly known as the slender toadflax, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that are tubular in shape and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Linaria elongata thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade conditions.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in various climates.
