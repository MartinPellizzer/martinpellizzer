Lirianthe

Lirianthe, commonly known as the white lily, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Liliaceae.

These plants are characterized by their showy, often fragrant flowers that typically feature six petals and numerous stamens. Lirianthe species are native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, including parts of Europe, Asia, and North America. They prefer well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making them suitable for gardens and natural habitats.
