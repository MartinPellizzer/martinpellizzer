Leptospermum erubescens

Leptospermum erubescens, commonly known as the red-flowered bottlebrush, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and vibrant red or pink flowers that bloom in dense, brush-like clusters. The plant typically grows in coastal regions and is adapted to sandy or gravelly soils. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in full sun.

The species is also valued for its hardiness and low maintenance requirements.
