Lupinus albifrons var. douglasii

Lupinus albifrons var. douglasii, commonly known as Douglas's lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of white to pale blue flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, hairy pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and meadows.

This species is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a food source for wildlife.
