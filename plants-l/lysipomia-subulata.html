Lysipomia subulata

Lysipomia subulata, commonly known as the slender tussock grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, needle-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale green or white flowers that bloom in late summer, contributing to its ornamental value in alpine gardens. Lysipomia subulata thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

It is often used in native plant landscaping due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
