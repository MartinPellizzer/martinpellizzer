Limeum sulcatum var. sulcatum

Limeum sulcatum var. sulcatum, commonly known as the horned poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, horn-like bracts that surround the flower cluster. The plant produces striking orange-red flowers with a unique, spiky appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
