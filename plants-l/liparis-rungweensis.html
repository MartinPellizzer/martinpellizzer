Liparis rungweensis

Liparis rungweensis, commonly known as the Rungwe orchid, is a rare and endemic orchid species found in the Rungwe area of Tanzania.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces small, greenish-yellow flowers with intricate patterns, often blooming during the rainy season. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often growing on rocky substrates or in forest undergrowth.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
