Lithocardium leucocomum

Lithocardium leucocomum, commonly known as the white-flowered rock cress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or arctic regions.

Lithocardium leucocomum is adapted to cold climates and has a low-growing habit to survive harsh environmental conditions.
