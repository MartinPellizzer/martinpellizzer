Leptospermum × violipurpureum

Leptospermum × violipurpureum, commonly known as the purple viola myrtle, is a hybrid plant native to Australia.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers. The leaves are narrow and dark green, often with a slight blue or purple tinge, giving the plant its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract native pollinators.
