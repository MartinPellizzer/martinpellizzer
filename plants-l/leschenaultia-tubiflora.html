Leschenaultia tubiflora

Leschenaultia tubiflora, commonly known as the woolly sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited to arid environments. Its growth habit is low and spreading, with multiple stems that produce numerous small, daisy-like flowers.

Leschenaultia tubiflora is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
