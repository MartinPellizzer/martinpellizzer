Lantana montevidensis

Lantana montevidensis, commonly known as the yellow lantana, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it adaptable to various climates.

Lantana montevidensis is often used in ornamental gardening due to its vibrant blooms and hardy nature.
