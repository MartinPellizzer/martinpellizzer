Lipochaeta lavarum var. hillebrandiana

Lipochaeta lavarum var. hillebrandiana, commonly known as the Hillebrand's narrowleaf lipochaeta, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dune environments.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
