Litsea racemosa

Litsea racemosa, commonly known as the fragrant sumac or Indian bay leaf, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, ovate leaves that emit a strong, spicy scent when crushed. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in racemes, followed by berries that turn from red to dark purple as they mature. Litsea racemosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in perfumery and medicine.

Its adaptability to various soil types makes it a popular choice for horticultural applications in tropical and subtropical regions.
