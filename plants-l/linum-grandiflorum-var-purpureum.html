Linum grandiflorum var. purpureum

Linum grandiflorum var. purpureum, commonly known as the purple flax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate foliage and vibrant floral display.
