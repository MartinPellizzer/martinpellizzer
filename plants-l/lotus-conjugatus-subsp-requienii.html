Lotus conjugatus subsp. requienii

Lotus conjugatus subsp.

requienii, commonly known as the mountain lotus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to temperate climates with distinct seasonal changes.

The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are palmately lobed and often covered in a fine, silky hair.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
