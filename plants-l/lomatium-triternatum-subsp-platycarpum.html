Lomatium triternatum subsp. platycarpum

Lomatium triternatum subsp.

platycarpum, commonly known as the broad-fruited lomatium, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in umbel-like clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional herbal medicine.

This subspecies is distinguished by its broader, more rounded fruits compared to other Lomatium species.
