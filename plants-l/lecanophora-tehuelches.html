Lecanophora tehuelches

Lecanophora tehuelches, commonly known as the Tehuelche thistle, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of southern South America.

It is characterized by its spiny, woody stems and leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with poor soil conditions.

Its ecological importance includes providing shelter and food for native wildlife.
