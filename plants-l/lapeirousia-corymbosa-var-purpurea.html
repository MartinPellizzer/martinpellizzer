Lapeirousia corymbosa var. purpurea

Lapeirousia corymbosa var. purpurea, commonly known as the purple-flowered rain lily, is a striking member of the Amaryllidaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, showy clusters of pale purple to pink flowers that bloom in response to rainfall. The plant features strap-like, grassy leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is adapted to seasonal rainfall patterns.

This variant is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, fleeting blooms.
