Lasianthus ovatus var. oligoneurus

Lasianthus ovatus var. oligoneurus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories. Its flowers are often pale blue or purple, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.

The varietal form, oligoneurus, is distinguished by its more elongated leaves compared to the typical species.
