Leonotis pentadentata

Leonotis pentadentata, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescences that bear bright orange to red tubular flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for gardens in warm climates.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and birds, contributing to its ecological significance.
