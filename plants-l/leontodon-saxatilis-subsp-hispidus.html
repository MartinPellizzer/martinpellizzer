Leontodon saxatilis subsp. hispidus

Leontodon saxatilis subsp.

hispidus, commonly known as the hairy rock dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in open habitats such as cliffs, slopes, and dry meadows.

The plant features basal rosettes of hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow composite flowers with a distinctive pappus for wind dispersal. It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its subspecific name "hispidus.".

This species is often found in disturbed or nutrient-poor environments, showcasing its adaptability to challenging conditions.
