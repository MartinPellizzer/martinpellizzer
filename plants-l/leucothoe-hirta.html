Leucothoe hirta

Leucothoe hirta, commonly known as the hairy leucothoe, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland habitats. The plant features small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by dark blue berries in the fall. Its leaves are elliptical, with a hairy texture on the upper surface, giving it its common name.

Leucothoe hirta is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
