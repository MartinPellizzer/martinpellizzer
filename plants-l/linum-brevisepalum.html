Linum brevisepalum

Linum brevisepalum, commonly known as the short-epal wild flax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oblong seeds. Linum brevisepalum is typically found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential ecological benefits.
