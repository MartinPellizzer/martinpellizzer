Lansium dubium

Lansium dubium, commonly known as the Java longan, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and fragrant white flowers. The fruit is a drupe with a smooth, yellowish skin and a sweet, juicy pulp containing a single seed. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruit.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental features and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
