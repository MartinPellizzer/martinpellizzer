Liparis loeselii var. cracoviensis

Liparis loeselii var. cracoviensis, commonly known as the Cracow orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and delicate, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. It produces small, fragrant blooms that attract pollinators like bees and moths.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
