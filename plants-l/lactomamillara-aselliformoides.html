Lactomamillara aselliformoides

Lactomamillara aselliformoides, commonly known as the milk mill lichen, is a species of lichen that belongs to the family Parmeliaceae.

It is characterized by its crustose growth form, forming a thin, tightly adherent layer on rock surfaces. The thallus is typically pale yellow to whitish in color, with a granular texture and distinct rhizines. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found on bark or soil in temperate regions.

The lichen's chemical composition includes various secondary metabolites, contributing to its ecological role in nutrient cycling and habitat diversity.
