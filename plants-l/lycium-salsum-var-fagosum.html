Lycium salsum var. fagosum

Lycium salsum var. fagosum, commonly known as the desert boxthorn, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, red or yellow berries that are edible for wildlife but generally unpalatable to humans. The plant has small, oval leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces yellow flowers in the spring. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water.

Its botanical features make it an important component of desert ecosystems, providing shelter and food for various animals.
