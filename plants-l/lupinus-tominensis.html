Lupinus tominensis

Lupinus tominensis, commonly known as the Mexican lupine, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and showy blue or purple flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds. Lupinus tominensis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils and along dry slopes.

It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
