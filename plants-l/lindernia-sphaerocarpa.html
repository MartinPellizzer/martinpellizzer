Lindernia sphaerocarpa

Lindernia sphaerocarpa, commonly known as the globe-lindernia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple, arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs.

Lindernia sphaerocarpa thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
