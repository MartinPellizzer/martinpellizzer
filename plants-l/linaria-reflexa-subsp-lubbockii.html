Linaria reflexa subsp. lubbockii

Linaria reflexa subsp.

lubbockii, commonly known as the Lubbock flaxflower, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, bright yellow or orange flowers.

The plant produces slender, linear seeds that are adapted for dispersal by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats. Linaria reflexa subsp.

lubbockii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
