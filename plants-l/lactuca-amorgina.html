Lactuca amorgina

Lactuca amorgina, commonly known as the Amorgos lettuce, is a species of lettuce native to the island of Amorgos in the Aegean Sea.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly bitter taste. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and other flowering plants. It produces small yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Lactuca amorgina is often grown in home gardens for its edible leaves and ornamental value.
