Lysinema ciliatum

Lysinema ciliatum, commonly known as the creeping bellflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are often covered in fine, thread-like hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Lysinema ciliatum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
