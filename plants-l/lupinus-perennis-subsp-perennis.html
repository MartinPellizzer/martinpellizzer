Lupinus perennis subsp. perennis

Lupinus perennis subsp.

perennis, commonly known as the tall lupine, is a perennial legume native to North America. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers.

The plant has compound leaves with numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in prairies and open woodlands. Lupinus perennis subsp.

perennis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
