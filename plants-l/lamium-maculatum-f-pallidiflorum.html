Lamium maculatum f. pallidiflorum

Lamium maculatum f. pallidiflorum, commonly known as the pale-flowered deadnettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and silver-white veins, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pale pink or white flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking shade-tolerant plants.
