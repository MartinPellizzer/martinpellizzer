Lupinus sericeus subsp. marianus

Lupinus sericeus subsp.

marianus, commonly known as the silver lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its silvery-blue to lavender flowers and narrow, glaucous leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal or inland desert regions. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.

Its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soils make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
