Lycium propinquum

Lycium propinquum, commonly known as the Chinese boxthorn, is a shrub native to China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are typically clustered in inflorescences.

Lycium propinquum is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential health benefits.
