Lepanthes excedens

Lepanthes excedens, commonly known as the tall orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored flowers that are typically borne on tall, erect stems. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its intricate floral structure, which includes a spurred lip and showy petals. Lepanthes excedens thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on trees or rocks in tropical forests.

Its botanical significance lies in its ornamental value and its role in the ecological diversity of its native habitats.
