Lychnis rotundifolia

Lychnis rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaved catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette of rounded, velvety leaves and produces clusters of small, pink or red flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and gardens. It blooms from late spring to early summer and is known for its ornamental value.

The species is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
