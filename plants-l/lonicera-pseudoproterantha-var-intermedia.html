Lonicera pseudoproterantha var. intermedia

Lonicera pseudoproterantha var. intermedia, commonly known as the Chinese honeysuckle, is a deciduous vine native to East Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that ripens in late summer, containing small seeds.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine.
