Leucopogon acuminatus

Leucopogon acuminatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heaths and heathers, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in heathlands and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained, acidic conditions. Leucopogon acuminatus is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various garden environments.
