Lupinus isabelianus

Lupinus isabelianus, commonly known as Isabel's lupin, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and showy spicate inflorescences. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it has a deep taproot system that helps it thrive in well-drained soils.

Lupinus isabelianus is often found in rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance.
