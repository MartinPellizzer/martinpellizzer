Lepanthes pseudoprofusa

Lepanthes pseudoprofusa, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a lithophytic plant, typically growing on tree trunks or branches in humid tropical environments. The plant is characterized by its small, white or pale green flowers with intricate, spurred petals. It is native to Central and South America, where it thrives in shaded, moist habitats.
