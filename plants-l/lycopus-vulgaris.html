Lycopus vulgaris

Lycopus vulgaris, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from July to September. Lycopus vulgaris is often found in wet or moist environments such as marshes and stream sides.

It is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
