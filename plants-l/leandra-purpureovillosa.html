Leandra purpureovillosa

Leandra purpureovillosa, commonly known as the purple-hair leandra, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple flowers and soft, hairy stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in the understory of tropical forests. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with a glossy surface.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
