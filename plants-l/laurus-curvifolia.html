Laurus curvifolia

Laurus curvifolia, commonly known as sweet bay or Turkish laurel, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves that are aromatic and often used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible but not commonly consumed. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental gardening.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care, especially around children and pets.
