Libanotis merkulowiczii

Libanotis merkulowiczii, commonly known as the Armenian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or mountainous habitats. The plant features showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
