Lotus corniculatus subsp. glacialis

Lotus corniculatus subsp.

glacialis, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a flowering plant native to alpine and arctic regions. It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves adapted to cold climates.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils at high altitudes. It has a low-growing habit and produces small, capsule-like fruits.

This subspecies is often found in mountainous areas of Europe and Asia, where it thrives in harsh, nutrient-poor environments.
