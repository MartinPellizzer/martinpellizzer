Leptophoenix densiflora

Leptophoenix densiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered fan palm, is a species native to the island of Madagascar.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its fan-shaped leaves that are densely packed and covered in small, sharp spines. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, which are typically found at the base of the leaves. Its fruit is a dark red drupe containing a single seed, and it thrives in humid, tropical environments with well-drained soil.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
