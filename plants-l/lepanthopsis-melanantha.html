Lepanthopsis melanantha

Lepanthopsis melanantha, commonly known as the black-stemmed orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark green to black stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Lepanthopsis melanantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated by enthusiasts of rare orchids.
