Lepironia foliosa

Lepironia foliosa, commonly known as the water fern, is a tropical aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that float on the water's surface. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in marshes and ponds. Its rhizomes form dense mats, which can help stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Lepironia foliosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in some cultures.
