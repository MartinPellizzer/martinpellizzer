Lachnospermum fasciculatum

Lachnospermum fasciculatum, commonly known as the clustered sandmat, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it its common name. Its leaves are usually fleshy and succulent, adapted to conserve water in dry environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
