Linaria genistifolia var. orbelica

Linaria genistifolia var. orbelica, commonly known as the Orbelica toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture. This variety is adapted to grow in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in coastal and mountainous regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
