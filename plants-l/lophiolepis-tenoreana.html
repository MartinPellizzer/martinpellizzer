Lophiolepis tenoreana

Lophiolepis tenoreana, commonly known as the coastal larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant nature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sandy or rocky coastal habitats.

Lophiolepis tenoreana is an important species for local pollinators and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
