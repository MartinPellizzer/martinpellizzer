Limonium transwallisnum

Limonium transwallisnum, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers and silver-gray foliage. The plant thrives in coastal areas and well-drained soils, often growing in saline environments. Its flowers are typically arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, and it blooms during the summer months.

Limonium transwallisnum is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
