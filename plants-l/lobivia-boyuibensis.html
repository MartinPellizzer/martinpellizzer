Lobivia boyuibensis

Lobivia boyuibensis, commonly known as the Boyuibensis Lobivia, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to the arid regions of South America.

It is a small, compact cactus with a short, cylindrical stem and sparse, spiny foliage. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the warmer months, which are typically brief in duration. Its foliage and flowers are adapted to thrive in low-water environments, showcasing the plant's resilience.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
