Liatris punctata f. alba

Liatris punctata f. alba, commonly known as the white gayfeather, is a cultivar of the native North American plant Liatris punctata.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of white to pale pink, spire-like flowers. The plant is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and it is known for its showy, long-lasting blooms. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens and pollinator habitats.

The white coloration of its flowers distinguishes it from the more common purple or pink varieties of the species.
