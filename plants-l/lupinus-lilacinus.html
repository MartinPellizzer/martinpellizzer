Lupinus lilacinus

Lupinus lilacinus, commonly known as the blue lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces elongated pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Lupinus lilacinus plays a role in nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
