Linum affine

Linum affine, commonly known as the slender flax, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the genus *Linum* in the family Fabaceae, which includes other species such as the common flax (*Linum usitatissimum*). The plant is characterized by its slender stems and small, blue or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a hairy texture.

Linum affine is typically found in grassy areas and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.
