Liatris carinata

Liatris carinata, commonly known as the narrowleaf blazingstar, is a native North American flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its upright, sparsely foliated stems and showy, spikelike clusters of violet-blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies, woodlands, and sandy soils. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Liatris carinata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
