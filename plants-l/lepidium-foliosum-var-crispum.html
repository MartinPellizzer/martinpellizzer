Lepidium foliosum var. crispum

Lepidium foliosum var. crispum, commonly known as the curly cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and hairy, lobed leaves that are often curled or crinkled, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate regions and is often found in disturbed soils or along streambanks. Its seeds are small and can be dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize new areas.

The species is also valued for its potential in herbal medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
