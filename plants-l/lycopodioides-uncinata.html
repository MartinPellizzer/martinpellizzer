Lycopodioides uncinata

Lycopodioides uncinata, commonly known as the hooked spike moss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its slender, branching stems and small, scale-like leaves. It produces spores in specialized structures called sporangia, which are clustered in cone-like formations at the tips of its branches.

Lycopodioides uncinata plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
