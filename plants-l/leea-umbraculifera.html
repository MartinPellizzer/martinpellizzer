Leea umbraculifera

Leea umbraculifera, commonly known as the umbrella leaf vine, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter, giving it a distinctive umbrella-like appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for hanging baskets and shaded garden areas.
