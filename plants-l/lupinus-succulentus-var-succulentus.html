Lupinus succulentus var. succulentus

Lupinus succulentus var. succulentus, commonly known as the succulent lupine, is a member of the legume family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and spikes of blue to purple flowers. Native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant has a deep root system that helps it survive in dry conditions.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it beneficial for soil enrichment in its native habitat.
