Lobelia thesiodes

Lobelia thesiodes, commonly known as the Sierra lobelia, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant features slender stems and small, tubular blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

Lobelia thesiodes is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
