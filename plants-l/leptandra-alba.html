Leptandra alba

Leptandra alba, commonly known as the white sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, white or pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or well-drained soils, often in open woodlands or along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Leptandra alba is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
