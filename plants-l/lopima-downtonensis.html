Lopima downtonensis

Lopima downtonensis, commonly known as the Downton thornbush, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers during the summer months, which are followed by small, woody fruits. Lopima downtonensis is often found in rocky soils and is well-suited to withstand drought conditions.

Its unique morphology makes it an important component of its native ecosystem.
