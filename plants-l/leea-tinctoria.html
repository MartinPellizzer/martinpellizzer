Leea tinctoria

Leea tinctoria, commonly known as the red-flowered Leea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the family Leeaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to red, depending on the species. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that release seeds when mature.

Leea tinctoria is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.
