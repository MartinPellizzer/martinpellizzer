Loasa triphylla subsp. papaverifolia

Loasa triphylla subsp.

papaverifolia, commonly known as the pink loasa, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has palmate leaves with three to five lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and attractive foliage.
