Larnax xalapensis

Larnax xalapensis, commonly known as the Mexican larkspur, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, greenish flowers in early spring.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be toxic if ingested.
