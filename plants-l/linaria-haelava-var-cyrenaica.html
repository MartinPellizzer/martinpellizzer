Linaria haelava var. cyrenaica

Linaria haelava var. cyrenaica, commonly known as the Cyrenaic toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and vibrant yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs to reduce water loss.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its bright, cheerful blossoms.
