Lupinus perplexus

Lupinus perplexus, commonly known as the enigmatic lupin, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of vibrant blue or purple pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces elongated pods that contain several seeds.

Lupinus perplexus is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.
