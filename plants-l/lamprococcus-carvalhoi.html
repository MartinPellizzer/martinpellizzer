Lamprococcus carvalhoi

Lamprococcus carvalhoi, commonly known as the Carvalho's lamprococcus, is a species of scale insect belonging to the family Coccidae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is typically found on the leaves of certain host plants. The plant hosts of L. carvalhoi include species of the genus Erythroxylum, which are part of the nightshade family. This insect is characterized by its small, elongated body covered in a waxy, protective coating.

Due to its specialized habitat and limited distribution, L. carvalhoi is of interest to botanists and entomologists studying plant-insect interactions.
