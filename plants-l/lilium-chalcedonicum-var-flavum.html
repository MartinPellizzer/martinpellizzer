Lilium chalcedonicum var. flavum

Lilium chalcedonicum var. flavum, commonly known as the golden lily, is a rare and striking variant of the Chalcedony lily.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which contrast beautifully with its green, strap-like leaves. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, this plant thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil. Its bulbs are perennial, and it blooms in late spring to early summer.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is highly sought after by botanists and gardeners alike.
