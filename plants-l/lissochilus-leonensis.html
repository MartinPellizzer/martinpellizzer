Lissochilus leonensis

Lissochilus leonensis, commonly known as the Leonensis orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its intricate floral structure and vibrant colors. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in cloud forests or rocky areas. Its flowers are usually small to medium in size, with a distinctive lip that aids in pollination by specific insect species.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
