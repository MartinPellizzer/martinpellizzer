Leucas sivadasaniana

Leucas sivadasaniana, commonly known as Sivadasan's leucas, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
