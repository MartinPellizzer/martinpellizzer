Lonicera × plantierensis f. youngii

Lonicera × plantierensis f. youngii, commonly known as the youngii honeysuckle, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Caprifoliaceae family.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth and the production of fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and can reach heights of up to 6 meters. It is native to China and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

Due to its adaptability, it thrives in a variety of soil types and is relatively easy to maintain in garden settings.
