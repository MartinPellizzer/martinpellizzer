Leucas sebaldiana

Leucas sebaldiana, commonly known as the narrow-leaved leucas, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale purple, arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
