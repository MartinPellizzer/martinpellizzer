Leptospermum thea

Leptospermum thea, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tea tree, is a small evergreen shrub native to Tasmania.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant is known for its aromatic oils, which are used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.

Leptospermum thea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
