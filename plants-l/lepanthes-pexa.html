Lepanthes pexa

Lepanthes pexa, commonly known as the Pexa orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant features a slender, erect stem with several elongated, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are showy, with a distinctive white or pale blue coloration and a yellow throat.

Lepanthes pexa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
