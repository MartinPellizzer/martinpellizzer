Lannea obovata

Lannea obovata, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to eastern and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and large, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds. Lannea obovata is adapted to dry climates and can thrive in well-drained soils.

It is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often used in traditional craftsmanship.
