Lilium × elegans

Lilium × elegans, commonly known as the noble lily, is a hybrid species within the lily family (Liliaceae).

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with strap-like, grassy leaves that are arranged in a fan shape. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

The bulbs of Lilium × elegans are perennial, and the plant is known for its fragrant blooms that appear in late spring to early summer.
