Lonicera flava

Lonicera flava, commonly known as the yellow honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are elliptical in shape. Its berries are orange-red and contain small, hard seeds.

Lonicera flava is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
