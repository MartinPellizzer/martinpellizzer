Lemotrys

Lemotrys, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble clusters of small flowers. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its most distinctive feature is its bright yellow inflorescence that blooms in late summer. Lemotrys is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a source of nectar for pollinators.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.
