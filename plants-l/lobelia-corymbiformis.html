Lobelia corymbiformis

Lobelia corymbiformis, commonly known as the clustered lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features delicate, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in shades of blue or purple. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Lobelia corymbiformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
