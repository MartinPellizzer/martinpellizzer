Lobelia caerulea

Lobelia caerulea, commonly known as blue lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often slightly hairy, and it grows in moist, well-drained soils. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and can thrive in partial to full sun.

Lobelia caerulea is also valued for its ability to help prevent soil erosion in wetland areas.
