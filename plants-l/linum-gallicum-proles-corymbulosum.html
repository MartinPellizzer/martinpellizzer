Linum gallicum proles corymbulosum

Linum gallicum proles corymbulosum, commonly known as the French flax or garden flax, is a flowering plant belonging to the Linaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in early summer. It is native to Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Linum gallicum proles corymbulosum prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for garden borders and rock gardens.
