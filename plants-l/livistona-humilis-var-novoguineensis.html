Livistona humilis var. novoguineensis

Livistona humilis var. novoguineensis, commonly known as the New Guinea palm, is a species native to the tropical regions of New Guinea.

It is a medium-sized palm characterized by its slender trunk and pinnately compound leaves that are typically green and glossy. The plant produces inflorescences that bear small, white flowers, which are followed by round, edible fruits. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
