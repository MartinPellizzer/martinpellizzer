Lysimachia lanceolata

Lysimachia lanceolata, commonly known as narrowleaf chickweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to North America.

It features small, opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in early summer. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its stems are typically prostrate or ascending, and it has a fibrous root system.

Lysimachia lanceolata is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
