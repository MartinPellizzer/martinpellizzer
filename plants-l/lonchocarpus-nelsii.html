Lonchocarpus nelsii

Lonchocarpus nelsii, commonly known as the Brazilian peacock fern, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces striking purple and yellow flowers, followed by pods containing seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for horticultural purposes.
