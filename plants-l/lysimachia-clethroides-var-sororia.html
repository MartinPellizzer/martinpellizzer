Lysimachia clethroides var. sororia

Lysimachia clethroides var. sororia, commonly known as golden thread, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has slender, creeping stems that root at the nodes, allowing it to spread along the ground. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.
