Lychnocephalus mello-barretoi

Lychnocephalus mello-barretoi, commonly known as the Brazilian candleplant, is a unique succulent native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its distinctive candle-like shape, with a single central stem topped by a dense cluster of fleshy, waxy leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is adapted to thrive in arid conditions, storing water in its thick, fleshy leaves.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered an endangered species.
