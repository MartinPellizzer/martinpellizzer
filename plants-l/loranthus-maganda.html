Loranthus maganda

Loranthus maganda, commonly known as the beautiful mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches itself to the branches of host trees, primarily in the genus Ficus.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using light absorbed by its leaves. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by small, fleshy berries that attract birds and other animals. Its roots form haustoria, specialized structures that penetrate the host's vascular system to extract resources.

Loranthus maganda is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, where it plays a role in both ecological interactions and traditional medicine.
