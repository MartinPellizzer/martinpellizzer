Lactuca capillaris

Lactuca capillaris, commonly known as slender lettuce, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It is often found in moist meadows and along stream banks, preferring temperate climates.

Lactuca capillaris is a perennial herb that can grow up to 1 meter tall, with a fibrous root system that helps it thrive in wet soils.
