Lamium tomentosum

Lamium tomentosum, commonly known as woolly deadnettle, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are covered in soft, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale purple to white flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming from spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Its hairy foliage and clusters of flowers make it a notable plant in botanical gardens and natural habitats.
