Lepigonum caespitosum

Lepigonum caespitosum, commonly known as the woolly foxglove, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and tall spikes of tubular, violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Lepigonum caespitosum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
