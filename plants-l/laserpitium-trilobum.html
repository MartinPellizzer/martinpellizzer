Laserpitium trilobum

Laserpitium trilobum, commonly known as the three-lobed gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine or subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and cool climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped with three distinct lobes, contributing to its common name.

Laserpitium trilobum is often found in rocky or meadow environments at high elevations.
