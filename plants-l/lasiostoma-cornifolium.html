Lasiostoma cornifolium

Lasiostoma cornifolium, commonly known as the horned leaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, leaf-like floral bracts that resemble leaves. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are small and clustered, with a distinctive shape that sets it apart from other orchid species.

Lasiostoma cornifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
