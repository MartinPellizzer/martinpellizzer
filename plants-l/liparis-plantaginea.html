Liparis plantaginea

Liparis plantaginea, commonly known as the broad-leafed orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, and thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant features slender, upright stems that bear lance-shaped leaves with a waxy texture. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and arranged in a loose spike, often with a subtle fragrance.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
