Lagenocarpus brevirostris

Lagenocarpus brevirostris, commonly known as the broad-beaked pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia and is characterized by its robust, fibrous leaves and distinctive, broad, beak-like fruit. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to several meters. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber, while the fruit is edible and has a sweet, fibrous texture.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and cultural contexts.
