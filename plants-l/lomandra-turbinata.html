Lomandra turbinata

Lomandra turbinata, commonly known as the Turbinated Lomandra, is a perennial evergreen plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage. The plant features narrow, strap-shaped leaves that are often blue-green in color, with a waxy coating. It produces small, white or pale blue flowers in summer, which are clustered in racemes.

Lomandra turbinata is well adapted to dry conditions and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover in arid landscapes.
