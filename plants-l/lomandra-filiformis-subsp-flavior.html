Lomandra filiformis subsp. flavior

Lomandra filiformis subsp.

flavior, commonly known as the golden lomandra, is a low-growing, evergreen perennial plant native to eastern Australia. It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color and densely packed along the stems.

The plant produces striking golden-yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for native gardens and restoration projects.
