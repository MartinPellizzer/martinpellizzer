Lonicera tatarica var. pyrenaica

Lonicera tatarica var. pyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the Pyrenees region.

It features opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant, yellowish-green flowers in umbel-like clusters, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. It is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators and providing food for birds.
