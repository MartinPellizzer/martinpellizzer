Lobelia longipedunculata

Lobelia longipedunculata, commonly known as the long peduncle lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, erect stems that bear clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of blue to purple. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
