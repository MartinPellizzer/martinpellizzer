Leptodactylon patens

Leptodactylon patens, commonly known as the creeping fern, is a species of fern characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit.

It features delicate, pinnately compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures include sori that are clustered on the underside of the fronds.

Leptodactylon patens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in maintaining soil stability.
