Loranthus pseudoperfoliatus

Loranthus pseudoperfoliatus, commonly known as the false mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily in the families Fabaceae and Myrtaceae.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own carbohydrates through photosynthesis using the light it captures. The plant has small, oval leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that disperses seeds through wind, allowing the plant to propagate without direct contact with the host.

It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, contributing to the ecological dynamics of its host trees.
