Luvunga nitida

Luvunga nitida, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, white, night-blooming flowers that emit a strong fragrance, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has a slender, woody stem with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its botanical name, *Luvunga nitida*, reflects its smooth, shiny appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soil and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.
