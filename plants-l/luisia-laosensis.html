Luisia laosensis

Luisia laosensis, commonly known as the Laos orchid, is a rare flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant blooms. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in montane forests. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the forest floor, and it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.

Due to habitat loss, Luisia laosensis is considered endangered and is of significant interest to botanists and conservationists.
