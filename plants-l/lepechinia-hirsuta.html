Lepechinia hirsuta

Lepechinia hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy lepechinia, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
