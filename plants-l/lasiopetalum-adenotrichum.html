Lasiopetalum adenotrichum

Lasiopetalum adenotrichum, commonly known as the woolly matted bellflower, is a small, evergreen shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, soft-textured appearance. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
