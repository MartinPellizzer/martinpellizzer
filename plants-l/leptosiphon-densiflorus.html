Leptosiphon densiflorus

Leptosiphon densiflorus, commonly known as the denseflower bluebottle, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to violet flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for xeriscaping in arid regions.
