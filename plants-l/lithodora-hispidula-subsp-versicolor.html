Lithodora hispidula subsp. versicolor

Lithodora hispidula subsp.

versicolor, commonly known as the variable heath, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southeastern United States. It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit.

The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are hairy and often have a slightly aromatic scent, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

This species is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
