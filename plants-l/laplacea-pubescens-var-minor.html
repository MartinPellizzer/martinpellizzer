Laplacea pubescens var. minor

Laplacea pubescens var. minor, commonly known as the lesser laplacea, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and clusters of pale yellow or white flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in humid tropical forests. Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and limited distribution.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered, making conservation efforts crucial for its survival.
