Lindera pulcherrima var. attenuata

Lindera pulcherrima var. attenuata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sweet pepper tree, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are dark green and have a slightly glossy appearance. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish-white flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by small, red, fleshy fruits. Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive addition to native plant gardens.

It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.
