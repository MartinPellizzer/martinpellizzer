Lycium lasiopetalum

Lycium lasiopetalum, commonly known as the woolly boxthorn, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny branches and small, fleshy berries that are typically red or orange in color. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a woolly appearance. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and coastal areas.

Lycium lasiopetalum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardy nature.
