Lasianthus biflorus

Lasianthus biflorus, commonly known as the two-flowered lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers. The plant typically grows as an herb or shrub, with opposite leaves that are often lance-shaped. Its flowers are usually clustered in whorls and display vibrant colors, attracting various pollinators.

Lasianthus biflorus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
