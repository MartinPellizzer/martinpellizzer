Leptospermum anfractum

Leptospermum anfractum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tea tree, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal or heathland environments.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardening in suitable climates.
