Lessingianthus irwinii

Lessingianthus irwinii, commonly known as the Irwin's sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant features bright yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers, typical of the sunflower genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a potential source of new medicinal compounds.
