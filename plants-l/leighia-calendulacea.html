Leighia calendulacea

Leighia calendulacea, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a tropical plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that resemble small sunflowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are often pollinated by bees and other insects.

Leighia calendulacea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in greenhouses and botanical gardens around the world.
