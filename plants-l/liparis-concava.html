Liparis concava

Liparis concava, commonly known as the concave orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its erect, flowering stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in a raceme, often with a distinctive concave shape. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Liparis concava is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
