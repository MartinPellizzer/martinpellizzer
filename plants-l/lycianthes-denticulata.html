Lycianthes denticulata

Lycianthes denticulata, commonly known as the prickly sida, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are usually ovate with serrated edges, and the stems are covered in fine hairs.

Lycianthes denticulata is often found in disturbed habitats and is adapted to thrive in dry conditions.
