Lycium dispermum

Lycium dispermum, commonly known as the two-seeded boxthorn, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but have a bitter taste. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

Lycium dispermum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
