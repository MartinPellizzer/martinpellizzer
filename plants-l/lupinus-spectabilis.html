Lupinus spectabilis

Lupinus spectabilis, commonly known as the showy lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking spires of violet to blue-purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmately compound leaves with several oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and open woodlands.

Lupinus spectabilis is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
