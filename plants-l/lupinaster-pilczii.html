Lupinaster pilczii

Lupinaster pilczii, commonly known as the Pilczynski's lupinaster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, butterfly-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers are usually purple or blue, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Lupinaster pilczii is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential ecological and medicinal benefits.
