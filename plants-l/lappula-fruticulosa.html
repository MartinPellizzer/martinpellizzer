Lappula fruticulosa

Lappula fruticulosa, commonly known as the prickly lettuce, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found along roadsides and in fields. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Lappula fruticulosa is often considered a weed but has historical uses in traditional medicine.
