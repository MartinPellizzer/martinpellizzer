Lepidospartum burgessii

Lepidospartum burgessii, commonly known as Burgess rockrose, is a low-growing shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It features dense clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has small, scale-like leaves that are gray-green in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

This plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
