Lucuma deplanchei

Lucuma deplanchei, commonly known as the Andean sweet potato or yellow lucuma, is a member of the Solanaceae family.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly Peru and Ecuador. The plant produces yellow-orange fruits that are rich in nutrients, including vitamins A and C, and dietary fiber. The fruit has a sweet, creamy texture and is often used in desserts and beverages.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable crop for both culinary and medicinal purposes.
