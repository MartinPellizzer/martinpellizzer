Lycium fremontii var. bigelovii

Lycium fremontii var. bigelovii, commonly known as the Fremont's boxthorn, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, red berries that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, demonstrating drought tolerance and adaptability to poor soils. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped and have a hairy texture, while its flowers are yellow and appear in clusters.

This species plays a role in supporting local wildlife, particularly birds and small mammals, by providing food and shelter.
