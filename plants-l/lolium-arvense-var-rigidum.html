Lolium arvense var. rigidum

Lolium arvense var. rigidum, commonly known as rigid ryegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a resilient plant in various environmental conditions.
