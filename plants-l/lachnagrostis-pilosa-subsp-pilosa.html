Lachnagrostis pilosa subsp. pilosa

Lachnagrostis pilosa subsp.

pilosa, commonly known as hairy bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and hairy leaves that are often covered with fine, soft hairs.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along riverbanks. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, two-lobed spikelets. Lachnagrostis pilosa subsp.

pilosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
