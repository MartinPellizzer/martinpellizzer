Linum mysurense

Linum mysurense, commonly known as the Persian flax or blue flax, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant has slender, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are hairy to the touch. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Linum mysurense is also valued for its historical use in textile production, similar to its more famous relative, flax.
