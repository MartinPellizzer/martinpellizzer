Leucopogon strongylophyllus

Leucopogon strongylophyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in heathlands and sandy soils, often in regions with a Mediterranean climate. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in spring. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, adapted to withstand dry conditions.

Leucopogon strongylophyllus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native gardens.
