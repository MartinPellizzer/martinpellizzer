Lonchocarpus amarus

Lonchocarpus amarus, commonly known as the bitter senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its toxic properties, containing various alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Despite its toxicity, it has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
