Lindera balansae

Lindera balansae, commonly known as Chinese Lindera, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and features aromatic, opposite leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible for certain bird species. Its leaves contain essential oils and have been used in traditional medicine for their aromatic and antiseptic properties.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.
