Lupinus latifolius

Lupinus latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf lupine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has palmately divided, broad leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Lupinus latifolius thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

It is a member of the legume family and has the ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through its root nodules.
