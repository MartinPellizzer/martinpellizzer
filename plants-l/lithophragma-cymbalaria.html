Lithophragma cymbalaria

Lithophragma cymbalaria, commonly known as the mountain stonecrop, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are usually thick and arranged in a rosette, aiding in water retention.

Lithophragma cymbalaria is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in harsh, arid conditions.
