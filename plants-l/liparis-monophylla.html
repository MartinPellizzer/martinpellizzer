Liparis monophylla

Liparis monophylla, commonly known as the single-leaf orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique morphology, featuring a single, lance-shaped leaf and a tall, branched inflorescence. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often blooming during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions for horticultural purposes.
