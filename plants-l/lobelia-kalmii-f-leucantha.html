Lobelia kalmii f. leucantha

Lobelia kalmii f. leucantha, commonly known as the white-flowered lobelia, is a striking perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, tubular, white to pale blue flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine, downy coating. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and wetland habitats.
