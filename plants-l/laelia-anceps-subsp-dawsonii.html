Laelia anceps subsp. dawsonii

Laelia anceps subsp.

dawsonii, commonly known as Dawson's laelia, is a species of orchid native to the Andes Mountains of South America. It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and lavender.

The plant typically blooms during the summer months, with flowers appearing in clusters along the stem. It prefers cool, humid environments and is often cultivated as a hybrid in orchid gardens.

Its foliage consists of broad, dark green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern.
