Lepanthes fimbriata

Lepanthes fimbriata, commonly known as the fringed orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central and South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist environments within tropical forests. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces intricate, fragrant flowers with a distinctive fringed appearance. Its flowers are usually pale green to white with a yellowish throat, and they bloom during the rainy season.

Lepanthes fimbriata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
