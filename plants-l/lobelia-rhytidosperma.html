Lobelia rhytidosperma

Lobelia rhytidosperma, commonly known as the sand verbena lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in spring and early summer. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that help reduce water loss in its arid habitat. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy areas.
