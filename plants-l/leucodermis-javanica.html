Leucodermis javanica

Leucodermis javanica, commonly known as the Javanese white skin fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Dryopteridaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically light green to pale yellow in color. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its rhizome is creeping and produces both sterile and fertile fronds.

Leucodermis javanica is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in shaded garden settings.
