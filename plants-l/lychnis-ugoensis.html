Lychnis ugoensis

Lychnis ugoensis, commonly known as the Ugo's catchfly, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and features clustered stems covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a reddish or purplish tinge.

Lychnis ugoensis is adapted to dry, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
