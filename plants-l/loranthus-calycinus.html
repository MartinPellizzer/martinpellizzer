Loranthus calycinus

Loranthus calycinus, commonly known as the sacred fig or the Indian banyan, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches itself to the roots of other trees.

It belongs to the Loranthaceae family and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia. The plant has small, green leaves and produces yellowish or reddish flowers that are often used in traditional medicine. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that contains seeds enclosed in a hard, woody layer.

Loranthus calycinus plays an important ecological role by providing food for various insects and birds.
