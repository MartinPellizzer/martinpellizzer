Liparis androchilus

Liparis androchilus, commonly known as the golden lip orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of showy, yellowish-green to golden flowers with a distinctive lip that is often darker in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

The orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable conditions.
