Leptotes paranaensis

Leptotes paranaensis, commonly known as the Parana orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in moist, shaded environments within the Atlantic Forest biome. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its leaves and flowers. Its flowers are usually small and white or pale pink, arranged in a raceme.

Leptotes paranaensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
