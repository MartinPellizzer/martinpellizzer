Lessertia candida

Lessertia candida, commonly known as the white-leaved milkwood, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale green to white leaves that give it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in summer, followed by small, spherical fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
