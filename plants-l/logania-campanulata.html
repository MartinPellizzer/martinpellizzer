Logania campanulata

Logania campanulata, commonly known as the bell-shaped logania, is a striking flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Loganiaceae and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections for its unique floral display.
