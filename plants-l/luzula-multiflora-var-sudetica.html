Luzula multiflora var. sudetica

Luzula multiflora var. sudetica, commonly known as the Sudetic wood sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, rushes, and other wetland plants. This variety is characterized by its slender, triangular stems and dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides.
