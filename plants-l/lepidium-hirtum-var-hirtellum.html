Lepidium hirtum var. hirtellum

Lepidium hirtum var. hirtellum, commonly known as the hairy whitlow grass, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit with hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in early spring. It is native to regions of Europe and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural and urban environments.
