Lomatium graveolens

Lomatium graveolens, commonly known as poison hemlock, is a biennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant contains toxic compounds, including cicutoxin, which can be harmful to humans and animals. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Due to its toxicity, it is important to avoid ingestion or contact with the plant.
