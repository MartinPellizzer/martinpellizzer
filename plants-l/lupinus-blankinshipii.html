Lupinus blankinshipii

Lupinus blankinshipii, commonly known as the Blankinship lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of showy blue to purple flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.
