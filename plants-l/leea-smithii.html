Leea smithii

Leea smithii, commonly known as the Smith's leea, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Leeaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow and bloom in the spring.

Leea smithii is often found in association with other shade-tolerant flora in its native habitat.
