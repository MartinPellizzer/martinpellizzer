Leptospermum tuberculatum var. subenerve

Leptospermum tuberculatum var. subenerve, commonly known as the narrow-veined tea tree, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with prominent, parallel veins, giving it its botanical name. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in spring. It is often found in heathlands and coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils.

The variety subenerve is distinguished by its reduced venation compared to the typical form of the species.
