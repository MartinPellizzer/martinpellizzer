Lycopersicon esculentum provar. persicoides

Lycopersicon esculentum provar. persicoides, commonly known as the cherry tomato, is a botanical variant of the domesticated tomato.

It is characterized by its small, round, and often colorful fruits, typically red, yellow, or orange. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is native to South America. It has hairy stems and alternate leaves with a slightly serrated edge.

This variety is widely cultivated for its flavor and use in culinary applications.
