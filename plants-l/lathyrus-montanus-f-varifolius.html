Lathyrus montanus f. varifolius

Lathyrus montanus f. varifolius, commonly known as the variable-leaf mountain vetch, is a flowering plant species belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and delicate, trifoliate leaves that vary in shape and size, giving it its specific epithet "varifolius.". The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowering display.
