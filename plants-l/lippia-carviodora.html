Lippia carviodora

Lippia carviodora, commonly known as Mexican mint or carviodora mint, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant leaves that emit a strong mint-like scent. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. It produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches.

Lippia carviodora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of essential oils used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine.
