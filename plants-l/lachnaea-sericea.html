Lachnaea sericea

Lachnaea sericea, commonly known as the silky lichen, is a foliose lichen that thrives in humid, shaded environments.

It is characterized by its thin, leathery thallus with a distinctive silvery or whitish coating on the upper surface. The plant forms a symbiotic relationship with algae, which provide nutrients through photosynthesis. Its growth is often found on tree trunks and rocks in temperate regions.

Lachnaea sericea plays a role in soil stabilization and serves as a habitat for various microorganisms.
