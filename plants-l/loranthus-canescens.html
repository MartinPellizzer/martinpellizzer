Loranthus canescens

Loranthus canescens, commonly known as the silver mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily in the genus Eucalyptus.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using light reflected from the host. The plant has small, scale-like leaves and produces small, white or pale flowers that are often inconspicuous. Its fruit is a capsule that contains tiny seeds with a sticky coating, which adhere to the feathers of birds or the fur of animals for dispersal.

This species is found in Australia and is an important part of the ecosystem, supporting various insect and bird life.
