Lepturus pubescens var. persicus

Lepturus pubescens var. persicus, commonly known as Persian velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its hairy, broad leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions. It is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it both a common weed and a subject of ecological study.
