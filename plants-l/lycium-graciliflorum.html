Lycium graciliflorum

Lycium graciliflorum, commonly known as slenderflower boxthorn, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, typically blooming in spring. The plant has thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention. Its fruit is a small, red berry that ripens in late summer and is edible for both humans and wildlife.

Lycium graciliflorum is adapted to harsh environments, making it a resilient and hardy plant in its native habitat.
