Lasiorrhiza eriochlaena

Lasiorrhiza eriochlaena, commonly known as the woolly sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Australia.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its hairy, silver-green foliage that helps reduce water loss in dry environments. Due to its adaptations to harsh conditions, it plays an important role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
