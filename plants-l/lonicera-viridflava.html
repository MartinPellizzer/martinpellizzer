Lonicera viridflava

Lonicera viridflava, commonly known as the golden honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically blooming in late spring. Its fruit is a small, yellow berry that ripens in early summer.

Lonicera viridflava is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
