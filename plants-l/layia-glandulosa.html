Layia glandulosa

Layia glandulosa, commonly known as the sticky layia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant has hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which contribute to its common name. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Layia glandulosa is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions and is often found in regions with Mediterranean climates.
