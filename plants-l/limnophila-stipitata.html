Limnophila stipitata

Limnophila stipitata, commonly known as the water willow, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as marshes and wetlands. The plant has slender, ribbon-like leaves that are often submerged or floating, with roots that help stabilize the soil. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually greenish or yellowish in color.

Limnophila stipitata plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
