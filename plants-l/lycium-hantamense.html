Lycium hantamense

Lycium hantamense, commonly known as the Hantam boxthorn, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible for some animals but toxic to humans. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along coastal areas.

Lycium hantamense plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
