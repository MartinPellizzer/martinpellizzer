Lobelia deckenii var. cacuminum

Lobelia deckenii var. cacuminum, commonly known as the African lobelia, is a flowering plant native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, herbaceous stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a hairy texture.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
