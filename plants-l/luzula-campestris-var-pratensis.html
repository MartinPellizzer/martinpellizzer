Luzula campestris var. pratensis

Luzula campestris var. pratensis, commonly known as the meadow woodrush, is a perennial plant belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, upright stems and dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, marshes, and woodland edges. It has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair, and it produces small, greenish-brown flowers in early summer.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
