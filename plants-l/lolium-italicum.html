Lolium italicum

Lolium italicum, commonly known as Italian ryegrass, is a cool-season grass native to Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, dark green leaves and dense growth habit, making it a popular choice for turfgrass and forage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in agricultural settings due to its high nutritional value for livestock.

Lolium italicum is also valued for its rapid establishment and ability to withstand various environmental conditions.
