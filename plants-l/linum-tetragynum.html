Linum tetragynum

Linum tetragynum, commonly known as the four-awned flax, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the genus Linum, which includes various species of flax plants. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Linum tetragynum is often found in meadows and along roadsides, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
