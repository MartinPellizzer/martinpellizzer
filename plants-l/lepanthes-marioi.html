Lepanthes marioi

Lepanthes marioi, commonly known as the Mario's slipper orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically display a range of colors including white, yellow, and green. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its unique slipper-shaped pouch, which is a distinctive feature in many Lepanthes species. Lepanthes marioi thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
