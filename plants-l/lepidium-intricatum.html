Lepidium intricatum

Lepidium intricatum, commonly known as the clustered wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant is characterized by its dense clusters of small, yellow flowers and its finely divided, fern-like leaves. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

Lepidium intricatum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive appearance and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
