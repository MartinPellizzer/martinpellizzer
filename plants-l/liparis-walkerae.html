Liparis walkerae

Liparis walkerae, commonly known as the Walkera's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil.

Liparis walkerae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
