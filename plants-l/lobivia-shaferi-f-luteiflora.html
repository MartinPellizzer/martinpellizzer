Lobivia shaferi f. luteiflora

Lobivia shaferi f. luteiflora, commonly known as the Yellow Flowered Lobivia, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, cylindrical stems that are often ribbed and covered in a layer of fine white hair. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are typically borne at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts and collectors.
