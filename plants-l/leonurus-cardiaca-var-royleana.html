Leonurus cardiaca var. royleana

Leonurus cardiaca var. royleana, commonly known as the heart-leaved fleabane, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, hairy stems that branch near the base, supporting clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The leaves are heart-shaped, with a distinctive lobed margin, and are covered in fine hairs. This variant is often found in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
