Ladenbergia chapadensis

Ladenbergia chapadensis, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its striking red-orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The tree has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces elongated pods that contain seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display and adaptability to various soil types.
