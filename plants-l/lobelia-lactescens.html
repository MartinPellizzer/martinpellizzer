Lobelia lactescens

Lobelia lactescens, commonly known as the milky lobelia, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are usually lance-shaped with serrated edges. Its milky sap, which gives it its name, is a distinctive feature and can be toxic if ingested.

Lobelia lactescens is often found in shaded environments and is a member of the Campanulaceae family.
