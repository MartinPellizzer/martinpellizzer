Lobularia strigulosa

Lobularia strigulosa, commonly known as the woolly lobularia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a low-growing herbaceous plant that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are covered in fine, velvety hairs, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
