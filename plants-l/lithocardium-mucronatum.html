Lithocardium mucronatum

Lithocardium mucronatum, commonly known as the sharp-tipped stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the summer months. It typically grows on rocky outcrops and is well adapted to poor soil conditions.

Lithocardium mucronatum is a hardy, low-maintenance plant popular among succulent enthusiasts.
