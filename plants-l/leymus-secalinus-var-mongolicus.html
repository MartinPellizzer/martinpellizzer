Leymus secalinus var. mongolicus

Leymus secalinus var. mongolicus, commonly known as Mongolian wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often covered with fine hairs. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brownish flowers.

This plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species for ecological restoration projects.
