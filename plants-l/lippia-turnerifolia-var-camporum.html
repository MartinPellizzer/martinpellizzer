Lippia turnerifolia var. camporum

Lippia turnerifolia var. camporum, commonly known as Camp's lippia, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features aromatic, narrow leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its small, tubular flowers are typically pale blue to violet in color. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and potential medicinal research.
