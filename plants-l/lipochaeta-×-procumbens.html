Lipochaeta × procumbens

Lipochaeta × procumbens, commonly known as the prostrate lipochaeta, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its sprawling growth habit and small, showy yellow flowers. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or semi-arid regions.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens in suitable climates.
