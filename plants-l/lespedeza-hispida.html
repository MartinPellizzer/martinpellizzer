Lespedeza hispida

Lespedeza hispida, commonly known as hispid lespedeza, is a perennial shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces fluffy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its root system has nitrogen-fixing capabilities, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

Lespedeza hispida is often found in open woodlands and disturbed sites, thriving in full sun to partial shade.
