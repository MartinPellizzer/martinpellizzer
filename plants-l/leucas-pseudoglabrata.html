Leucas pseudoglabrata

Leucas pseudoglabrata, commonly known as the hairy leucas, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple. Its leaves are usually hairy, giving it the common name, and it often grows in moist, shaded environments.

Leucas pseudoglabrata is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
