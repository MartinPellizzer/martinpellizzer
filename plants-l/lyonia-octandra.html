Lyonia octandra

Lyonia octandra, commonly known as the red mountain beauty, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its small, bell-shaped, pink to red flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are evergreen, leathery, and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems.

Lyonia octandra thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.
