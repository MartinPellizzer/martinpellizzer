Linoma

Linoma, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Gesneriaceae and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically produces vibrant blooms in shades of purple, pink, and white, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Linoma prefers well-draining soil and indirect sunlight, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its compact growth habit and ease of care contribute to its widespread cultivation as a decorative plant.
