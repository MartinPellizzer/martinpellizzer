Lolium agreste

Lolium agreste, commonly known as goat's rue, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of grasses. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in fields, along roadsides, and in meadows.

Lolium agreste is also known for its use in traditional herbal medicine and as a forage crop for livestock.
