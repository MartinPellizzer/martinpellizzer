Lycopodium fissidentoides

Lycopodium fissidentoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf clubmoss, is a small evergreen plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, scale-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls. The plant produces spore-bearing cones, known as strobila, which are clustered at the tips of its branches. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas.

Lycopodium fissidentoides plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
