Lyonia foliosa

Lyonia foliosa, commonly known as the goldenleaf azalea, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other shrubs and flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy clusters of fragrant, yellowish-orange flowers. It typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade.

Lyonia foliosa is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in moist, woodland environments.
