Lilium martagon f. albiflorum

Lilium martagon f. albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered Turk's cap lily, is a distinctive cultivar of the Turk's cap lily species.

It is characterized by its broad, recurved petals that are predominantly white, giving it a striking appearance. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It produces fragrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variety is well-suited for garden cultivation and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.
