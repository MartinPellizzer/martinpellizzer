Lavandula bipinnata var. burmanniana

Lavandula bipinnata var. burmanniana, commonly known as Burmann's lavender, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, narrow leaves that are gray-green in color and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces spikes of purple to pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its compact growth habit and fragrant foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
