Lagerstroemia ovalifolia var. novoguineensis

Lagerstroemia ovalifolia var. novoguineensis, commonly known as the New Guinea rosewood, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and clusters of showy, pink to red bracts that appear during the dry season. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by elongated, woody fruits containing flat, winged seeds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes in warm climates.
