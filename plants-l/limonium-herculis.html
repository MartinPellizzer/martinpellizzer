Limonium herculis

Limonium herculis, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, preferring sandy or gravelly soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often used in floral arrangements due to its long-lasting blooms.

Limonium herculis is also valued in horticulture for its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
