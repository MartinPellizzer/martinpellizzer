Lupinus tricolor

Lupinus tricolor, commonly known as the blue lupin, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. It has a short life cycle, often completing its growth and reproduction within a single growing season.

Lupinus tricolor is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
