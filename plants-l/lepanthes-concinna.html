Lepanthes concinna

Lepanthes concinna, commonly known as the pale violet orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically range in color from white to pale purple. The plant produces a single, showy flower per stem, which is often adorned with intricate patterns and a distinct lip. Lepanthes concinna thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Its botanical features make it a popular subject for both horticultural study and conservation efforts.
