Lobivia cinnabarina subvar. walterspielii

Lobivia cinnabarina subvar. walterspielii, commonly known as the Walt's Lobivia, is a compact, low-growing succulent native to South America.

It features thick, fleshy stems that are often tinged with red or purple hues, especially under bright light conditions. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique coloration and compact form, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and container cultivation.
