Ligularia nigro-pilosa

Ligularia nigro-pilosa, commonly known as the black-hairy ligularia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive dark green color and dense, blackish hairs on the underside. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers arranged in a panicle inflorescence, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
