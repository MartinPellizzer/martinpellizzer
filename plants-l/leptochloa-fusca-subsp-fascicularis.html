Leptochloa fusca subsp. fascicularis

Leptochloa fusca subsp.

fascicularis, commonly known as the fasciculate grass, is a perennial grass species native to various regions of Asia. It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like blades.

The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along water bodies. Its inflorescence consists of a spikelet-like structure with small, spikelet-like flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
