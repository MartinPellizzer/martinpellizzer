Lobelia bicolor lilacina

Lobelia bicolor lilacina, commonly known as the lilac-flowered lobelia, is a striking perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers in shades of lilac and white, which bloom from late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, blue-black berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking native, low-maintenance plants.
