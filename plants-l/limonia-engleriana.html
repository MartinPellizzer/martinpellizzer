Limonia engleriana

Limonia engleriana, commonly known as the Engler's lime, is a small evergreen shrub native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its fragrant, narrow leaves and small white flowers. The plant produces yellow-orange fruits that are edible and have a tart flavor, similar to limes. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and citrus-like aroma.

Limonia engleriana is also valued for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
