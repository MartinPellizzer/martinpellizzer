Litsea cuttingiana

Litsea cuttingiana, commonly known as the fragrant leaf tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that emit a subtle, aromatic scent when crushed. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red berries. Litsea cuttingiana thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
