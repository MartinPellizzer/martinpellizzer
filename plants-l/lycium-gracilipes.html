Lycium gracilipes

Lycium gracilipes, commonly known as the slender goji, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshade plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer. Its berries, which are typically red or orange, are edible and contain high levels of antioxidants.

Lycium gracilipes is often cultivated for its nutritional value and ornamental appeal in dry climates.
