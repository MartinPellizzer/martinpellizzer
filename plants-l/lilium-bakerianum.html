Lilium bakerianum

Lilium bakerianum, commonly known as the Baker's lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, supporting large, showy flowers with six petal-like lobes. The flowers are typically white or pale pink and have a strong, spicy fragrance. This perennial plant thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
