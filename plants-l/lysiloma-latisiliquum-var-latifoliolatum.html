Lysiloma latisiliquum var. latifoliolatum

Lysiloma latisiliquum var. latifoliolatum, commonly known as the broad-leaved lythrum, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lythraceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating, and its stems are often hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral displays.
