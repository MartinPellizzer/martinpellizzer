Ligustrum japonicum var. leucocarpum

Ligustrum japonicum var. leucocarpum, commonly known as the white-fruited privet, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features small, opposite leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, followed by white, fleshy berries that are distinctive in appearance. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often used in landscaping due to its hardiness and adaptability.

The white fruits are a notable characteristic, setting it apart from other privet varieties.
