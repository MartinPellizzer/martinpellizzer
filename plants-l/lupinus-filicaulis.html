Lupinus filicaulis

Lupinus filicaulis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf lupine, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of pea-like flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small, narrow leaflets, giving it a feathery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Lupinus filicaulis is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
