Lithophragma brevilobum

Lithophragma brevilobum, commonly known as the Alaska rock beauty, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the primrose family (Primulaceae) and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs.

Lithophragma brevilobum thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
