Lepechinia salviifolia var. salviifolia

Lepechinia salviifolia var. salviifolia, commonly known as the bluecurls, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and dense clusters of sky-blue to purple flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy texture. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
