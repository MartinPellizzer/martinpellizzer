Lotus confinis

Lotus confinis, commonly known as the narrowleaf water-lily, is a perennial aquatic plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, supported by long, slender petioles. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in the early morning and close by midday. Its roots system helps stabilize the soil and improve water quality.

Lotus confinis is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and has cultural significance in traditional medicine.
