Linum diversifolium

Linum diversifolium, commonly known as the variable flax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the genus Linum, which includes various species of flax known for their showy blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is characterized by its variable leaf shapes, which range from lance-shaped to broad and oval. It produces small, delicate flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Linum diversifolium is often found in open areas such as grasslands and along streambanks.
