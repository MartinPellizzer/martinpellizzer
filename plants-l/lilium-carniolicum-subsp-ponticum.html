Lilium carniolicum subsp. ponticum

Lilium carniolicum subsp.

ponticum, commonly known as the Crimean lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus and parts of southern Europe. It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height.

The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink to red in color, with a white throat. Its leaves are lanceolate and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental value.

This species is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
