Licuala crassiflora

Licuala crassiflora, commonly known as the broad-leaved palm, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, pinnately compound leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for tropical gardens and indoor spaces.

The leaves are also used in traditional crafts and have cultural significance in some regions.
