Lagochilium maculatum

Lagochilium maculatum, commonly known as the spotted bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, spotted flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a rough texture.
