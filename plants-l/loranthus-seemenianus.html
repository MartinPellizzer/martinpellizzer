Loranthus seemenianus

Loranthus seemenianus, commonly known as the African mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily in the genus Acacia.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using the light it captures. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces white or yellowish flowers that are often clustered together. Its fruits are small, fleshy berries that contain seeds covered in a sticky substance to aid in seed dispersal by birds.

This species is found in various parts of Africa and plays a role in local ecosystems despite its parasitic nature.
