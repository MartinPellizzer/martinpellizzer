Leontopodium alpinum var. perinicum

Leontopodium alpinum var. perinicum, commonly known as the edelweiss, is a perennial plant native to the Alps.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly white foliage and a single flower that blooms in the summer. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments, often found in rocky or alpine meadows. Its flowers are typically surrounded by a cluster of white bracts, which give it a distinctive appearance.

Due to its habitat and appearance, it is often associated with alpine landscapes and has cultural significance in various regions.
