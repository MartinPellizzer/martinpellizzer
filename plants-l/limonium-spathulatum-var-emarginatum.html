Limonium spathulatum var. emarginatum

Limonium spathulatum var. emarginatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are narrow and slightly lobed, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate salt spray.
