Loranthus gomesii

Loranthus gomesii, commonly known as the Gomes' mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily in the family Fabaceae.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while still performing some photosynthesis using its leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers, followed by spherical berries that contain sticky seeds. It is native to regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Its parasitic nature and unique reproductive strategy make it an interesting subject in botanical studies.
