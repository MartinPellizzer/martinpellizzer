Laportea crenulata var. nitida

Laportea crenulata var. nitida, commonly known as the silky leaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its finely toothed, silvery-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving them a distinctive, velvety appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers in late summer, which are arranged in dense, spherical inflorescences. It thrives in moist, rich soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both ecological and ornamental purposes in native gardens.
