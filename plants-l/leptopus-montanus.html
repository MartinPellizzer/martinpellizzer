Leptopus montanus

Leptopus montanus, commonly known as the mountain leptopus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments with cool temperatures. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, providing some protection against harsh weather conditions.

Leptopus montanus is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations and limited distribution.
