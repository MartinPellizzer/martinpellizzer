Linum indehiscens proles elongatum

Linum indehiscens proles elongatum, commonly known as the elongated flax, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the Linaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in loose inflorescences. Its seeds are enclosed in a capsule that does not split open upon maturity, a trait known as indehiscence.

This species is native to certain regions of South America and is often found in dry, open habitats.
