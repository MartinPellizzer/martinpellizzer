Leucas montana f. parviflora

Leucas montana f. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered mountain leucas, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers arranged in whorled clusters along the upper stem. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats at higher elevations.
