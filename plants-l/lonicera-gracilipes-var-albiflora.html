Lonicera gracilipes var. albiflora

Lonicera gracilipes var. albiflora, commonly known as the whiteflower honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features slender, arching stems that bear opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy green surface. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by red berries in the fall. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and adaptability to various soil types.
