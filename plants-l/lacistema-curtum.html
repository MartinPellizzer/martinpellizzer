Lacistema curtum

Lacistema curtum, commonly known as the Mexican nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with hairy stems and alternate leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape. Its small, white or purple flowers bloom in clusters and are followed by berries that turn from green to yellow or red as they mature.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
