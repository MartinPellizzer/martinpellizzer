Linaria rubrifolia var. imintalensis

Linaria rubrifolia var. imintalensis, commonly known as the Imintalensis toadflax, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers with a distinctive spurred petal. The leaves are typically narrow and linear, often with a reddish or purple tinge along the edges. This variety thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and relatively low-maintenance ornamental plant.
