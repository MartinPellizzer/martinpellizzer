Leidesia procumbens var. genuina

Leidesia procumbens var. genuina, commonly known as the goldenleaf or yellowleaf, is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, prostrate stems that root at the nodes, forming dense mats of foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a distinctive yellowish-green hue with prominent veins.

It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded, wetland habitats.
