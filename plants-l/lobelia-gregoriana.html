Lobelia gregoriana

Lobelia gregoriana, commonly known as Gregor's lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in mountainous regions with cool temperatures. Its flowers bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Lobelia gregoriana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
