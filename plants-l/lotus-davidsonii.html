Lotus davidsonii

Lotus davidsonii, commonly known as the Davidson's lotus, is a rare flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking pink or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has palmately compound leaves with a waxy texture, and its roots are often used in traditional Chinese medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a cool, temperate climate to flourish.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is protected in several conservation programs.
