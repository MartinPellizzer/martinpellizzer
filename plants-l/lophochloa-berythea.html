Lophochloa berythea

Lophochloa berythea, commonly known as the broad-leafed grass, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, tropical climates. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle with small, spikelet-like flowers.

Lophochloa berythea plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
