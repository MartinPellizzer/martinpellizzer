Lysimachia alfredi var. alfredi

Lysimachia alfredi var. alfredi, commonly known as the Alaskan chickweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the northern regions of North America.

It features small, bright green leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces delicate, yellowish flowers during the summer months. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments such as forest floors and streambanks. It has a fibrous root system and is often found in areas with rich, organic soil.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
