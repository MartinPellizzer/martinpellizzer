Lavandula abrotanoides var. elegans

Lavandula abrotanoides var. elegans, commonly known as narrowleaf lavender, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces spikes of fragrant, pale purple flowers. The plant is well adapted to dry, well-drained soils and full sun exposure. It is often used in rock gardens and as a border plant due to its ornamental value and hardy nature.

Its essential oils are also valued for their aromatic and medicinal properties.
