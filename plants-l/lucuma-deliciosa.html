Lucuma deliciosa

Lucuma deliciosa, commonly known as the sweet potato or lucuma, is a South American plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is native to the Andes region and is cultivated for its nutrient-rich, orange-colored fruit. The fruit has a sweet, creamy texture and is often used in desserts and beverages. The plant produces showy yellow flowers and has hairy, ovate leaves.

It thrives in cool, high-altitude climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
