Landolphia comorensis

Landolphia comorensis, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white, night-blooming flowers that emit a strong fragrance. The plant produces bright red berries that contain toxic seeds, making it important to handle with care. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture.

Landolphia comorensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
