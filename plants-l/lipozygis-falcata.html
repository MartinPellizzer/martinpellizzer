Lipozygis falcata

Lipozygis falcata, commonly known as the narrowleaf lipozygis, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
