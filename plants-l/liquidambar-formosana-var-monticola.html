Liquidambar formosana var. monticola

Liquidambar formosana var. monticola, commonly known as the mountain sweetgum, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, palmately lobed leaves. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in catkins, followed by spherical, spiky fruit clusters. Its leaves turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in autumn, making it a striking ornamental plant.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions with moderate climates.
