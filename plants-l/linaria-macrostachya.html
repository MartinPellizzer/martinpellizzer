Linaria macrostachya

Linaria macrostachya, commonly known as the tall yellow toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of bright yellow, spurred flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Linaria macrostachya is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of care.
