Lycianthes geminiflora

Lycianthes geminiflora, commonly known as the twin-flowered physalis, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is native to Central and South America, where it typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces distinctive yellow-orange calyces that enclose its small, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Lycianthes geminiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique fruiting structures.
