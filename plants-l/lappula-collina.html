Lappula collina

Lappula collina, commonly known as the mountain lappula, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in harsh, cold environments. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in a fine pubescence.

Lappula collina is known for its ability to withstand extreme temperatures and is often found in mountainous and tundra habitats.
