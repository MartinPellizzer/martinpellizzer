Lobivia formosa var. grandis

Lobivia formosa var. grandis, commonly known as the Grand Lobivia, is a compact cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It features a short, thick stem with prominent, ribbed tubercles that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces showy, yellowish-green flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are modified into spines, which provide protection from herbivores and aid in reducing transpiration.

This variety is prized for its unique appearance and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a container plant.
