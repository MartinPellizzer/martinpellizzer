Lobivia shaferi subsp. rubriflora

Lobivia shaferi subsp.

rubriflora, commonly known as the red-flowered lobivia, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America. It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rounded stem and vibrant red flowers.

The plant features spiny, cylindrical stems that store water, adapted to its harsh, dry environment. Its flowers are typically showy, with a bright red coloration that attracts pollinators.

It requires well-draining soil and minimal water, making it a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts.
