Linaria rubrifolia f. bianorii

Linaria rubrifolia f. bianorii, commonly known as the Bianorii toadflax, is a compact, mat-forming perennial native to the mountainous regions of southeastern Europe.

It features slender, upright stems with small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of bright orange to yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its compact growth habit and vibrant floral display make it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine plantings.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained, rocky soils.
