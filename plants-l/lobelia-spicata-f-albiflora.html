Lobelia spicata f. albiflora

Lobelia spicata f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered whorled lobelia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, arranged in whorls around the stem, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in native plant gardens.
