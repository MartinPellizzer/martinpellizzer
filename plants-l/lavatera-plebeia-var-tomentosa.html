Lavatera plebeia var. tomentosa

Lavatera plebeia var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly lavatera, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy, grayish foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, fluffy white or pink flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators.
