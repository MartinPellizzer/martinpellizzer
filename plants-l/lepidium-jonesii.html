Lepidium jonesii

Lepidium jonesii, commonly known as Jones' cress, is a small annual herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard plants. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats and has small, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in racemes, and it produces small, rounded pods that contain several seeds.

Lepidium jonesii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
