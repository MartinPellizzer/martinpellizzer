Lupinus argenteus var. argophyllus

Lupinus argenteus var. argophyllus, commonly known as silver lupine, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features silver-gray foliage and produces tall spikes of blue to purple flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
