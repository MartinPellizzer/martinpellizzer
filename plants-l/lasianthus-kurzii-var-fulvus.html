Lasianthus kurzii var. fulvus

Lasianthus kurzii var. fulvus, commonly known as the golden lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partially shaded conditions.
