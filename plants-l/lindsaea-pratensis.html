Lindsaea pratensis

Lindsaea pratensis, commonly known as the marsh lindsaea, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet, shaded environments such as marshes and swamps. The plant features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense basal rosette. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, white to greenish flowers.

Lindsaea pratensis is characterized by its ability to thrive in moist, nutrient-poor soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
