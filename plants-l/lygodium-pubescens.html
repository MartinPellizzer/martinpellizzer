Lygodium pubescens

Lygodium pubescens, commonly known as the hairy water fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lygodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found near streams or in humid forests. Its reproductive structures include sporangia that release spores for propagation.

The hairy water fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
