Leucopogon glaucifolius

Leucopogon glaucifolius, commonly known as the silver-leafed heath, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southeastern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, often in heathlands and coastal dunes. The plant is characterized by its narrow, silvery-green leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or red and bloom in spring, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects.

Leucopogon glaucifolius is well adapted to coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.
