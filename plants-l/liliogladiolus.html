Liliogladiolus

Liliogladiolus, commonly known as the gladiolus, is a genus of flowering plants in the iris family, Iridaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, sword-shaped leaves and showy, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous brightly colored flowers. The plant is native to southern Europe, North Africa, and the Middle East, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight. Liliogladiolus species are often cultivated for their ornamental value, with varieties displaying a wide range of colors including white, pink, red, and yellow.

The flowers are typically used in floral arrangements and are also popular in gardens for their vibrant appearance and long-lasting blooms.
