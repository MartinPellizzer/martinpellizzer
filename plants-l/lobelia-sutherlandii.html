Lobelia sutherlandii

Lobelia sutherlandii, commonly known as Sutherland's lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture.

Lobelia sutherlandii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
