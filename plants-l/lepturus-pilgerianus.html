Lepturus pilgerianus

Lepturus pilgerianus, commonly known as the desert lily, is a perennial plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. The plant produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. Its root system is extensive and deep, allowing it to access groundwater during drought periods.

Lepturus pilgerianus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators in its native habitat.
