Lupinus setifolius

Lupinus setifolius, commonly known as the silky lupin, is a perennial legume native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to violet pea-like flowers. The plant has feathery, finely divided leaves that give it a delicate appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Lupinus setifolius is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
