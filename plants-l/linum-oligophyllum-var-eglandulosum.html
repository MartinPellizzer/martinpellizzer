Linum oligophyllum var. eglandulosum

Linum oligophyllum var. eglandulosum, commonly known as the glandless flax, is a variant of the flax plant characterized by its reduced glandular structures on the stems and leaves.

It is a herbaceous annual plant native to the eastern United States, typically found in moist, disturbed habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. The plant produces slender, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a hairy texture.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its delicate floral display.
