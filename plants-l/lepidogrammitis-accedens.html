Lepidogrammitis accedens

Lepidogrammitis accedens, commonly known as the narrow-leaf fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, narrow fronds that are typically less than 30 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on tree trunks or rocks in tropical rainforests. Its rhizomes are wiry and help anchor it to its substrate while absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air.

The spores are produced in clusters on the undersides of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy in its natural habitat.
