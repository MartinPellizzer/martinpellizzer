Lycopodioides trichobasis

Lycopodioides trichobasis, commonly known as the hairy clubmoss, is a small, evergreen plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and needle-like leaves that are often covered in fine, hairy structures. The plant produces sporophytes with distinct, club-shaped strobili that bear spores. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in temperate regions.

Its unique morphology and reproductive structures make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
