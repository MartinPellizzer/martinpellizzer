Lepidium lacerum subsp. lacerum

Lepidium lacerum subsp.

lacerum, commonly known as the slender wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America. It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds, typical of the Brassicaceae family. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its delicate appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and somewhat elusive species.
