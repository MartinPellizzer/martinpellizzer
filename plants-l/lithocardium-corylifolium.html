Lithocardium corylifolium

Lithocardium corylifolium, commonly known as the broad-leafed lithocardium, is a member of the Araliaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in umbels, followed by red berries that are edible for birds. Native to Southeast Asia, it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
