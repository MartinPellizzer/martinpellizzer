Laserpitium krapffii subsp. gaudinii

Laserpitium krapffii subsp.

gaudinii, commonly known as the Gaudin's lasur, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of East Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect, sparsely branched stems and small, yellowish to brownish flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats at elevations above 2,000 meters. Its leaves are usually narrow and linear, with a hairy surface that helps reduce water loss in its arid environment.
