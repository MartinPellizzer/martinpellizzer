Lilium davidii f. macranthum

Lilium davidii f. macranthum, commonly known as the giant lily or David's lily, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter. The plant features strap-like, grassy leaves and produces tall, sturdy stems that support its impressive blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.

Its ornamental value and unique floral structure make it a notable species in horticultural and botanical studies.
