Luffa fluminensis

Luffa fluminensis, commonly known as the Amazon luffa, is a tropical climbing vine native to South America.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes cucumbers and gourds. The plant produces large, lobed leaves and elongated, cylindrical fruits that mature to a yellowish or orange color. Its fruit is characterized by a spongy interior that becomes fibrous when dried, making it useful for sponges and natural exfoliants.

Luffa fluminensis thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
