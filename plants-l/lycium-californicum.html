Lycium californicum

Lycium californicum, commonly known as the California boxthorn, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. The plant has spiny stems and leaves that are typically alternate and ovate in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats.

Lycium californicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
