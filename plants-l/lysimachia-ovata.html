Lysimachia ovata

Lysimachia ovata, commonly known as the broadleaf hillshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and North America.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are usually smooth or slightly hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and streambanks.

Lysimachia ovata is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and subtle floral display.
