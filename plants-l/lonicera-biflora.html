Lonicera biflora

Lonicera biflora, commonly known as the twin-flowered honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. These flowers are usually yellow or orange with red markings, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Lonicera biflora is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
