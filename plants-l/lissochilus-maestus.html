Lissochilus maestus

Lissochilus maestus, commonly known as the Maestus orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and shrublands, thriving in cool and humid climates. Its flowers are usually pale pink or white, with a unique, intricate pattern on the petals.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
