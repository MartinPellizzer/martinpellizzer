Litsea variabilis var. oblonga

Litsea variabilis var. oblonga, commonly known as the broadleaf litsea, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and avocado trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate, elliptical leaves that are usually dark green on top and lighter underneath. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by yellowish drupes.

This species is often valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
