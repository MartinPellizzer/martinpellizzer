Lycaste cruenta var. longibracteata

Lycaste cruenta var. longibracteata, commonly known as the Crimson Lycaste, is a hybrid orchid species native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking red and orange flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces elongated bracts that give it its specific epithet, "longibracteata.". It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its vibrant color and unique floral structure.
