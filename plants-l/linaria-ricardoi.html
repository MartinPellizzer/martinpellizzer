Linaria ricardoi

Linaria ricardoi, commonly known as the Ricard's toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of bright yellow to orange-red flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are tubular and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Linaria ricardoi is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
