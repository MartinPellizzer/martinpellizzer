Lilium cordatum

Lilium cordatum, commonly known as the heart-leaved lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade. It is a perennial herb that produces showy blooms in late spring to early summer.

Lilium cordatum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
