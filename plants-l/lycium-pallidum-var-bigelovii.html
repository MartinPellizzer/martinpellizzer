Lycium pallidum var. bigelovii

Lycium pallidum var. bigelovii, commonly known as the Bigelov's lycium, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, yellowish-green berries that ripen in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grayish-green, often covered in a fine, powdery coating.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscapic gardens.
