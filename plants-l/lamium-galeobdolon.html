Lamium galeobdolon

Lamium galeobdolon, commonly known as the purple deadnettle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features square stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, two-lipped flowers in shades of purple and white, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Lamium galeobdolon is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
