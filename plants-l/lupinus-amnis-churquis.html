Lupinus amnis-churquis

Lupinus amnis-churquis, commonly known as the Churquise lupine, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves with a feathery appearance. The plant produces spikes of showy, usually blue or purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
