Loranthus lamborayi

Loranthus lamborayi, commonly known as the parasitic mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches itself to the branches of host trees, primarily in the genus Eucalyptus.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while it provides some benefits by stimulating growth in the host. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers and red, fleshy berries that are dispersed by birds. It is native to Australia and is often found in coastal and inland regions where its host trees thrive.

Its presence can have both positive and negative impacts on the health of its host tree.
