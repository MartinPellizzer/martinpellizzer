Lippia hirsuta var. glabrescens

Lippia hirsuta var. glabrescens, commonly known as the velvetleaf lippia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy, often glandular leaves and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
