Lespedeza formosa f. versicolor

Lespedeza formosa f. versicolor, commonly known as the rainbow lespedeza, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful bracts that range from red to orange to yellow. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is trifoliate, with small, oval-shaped leaves, and it produces small, pea-like flowers in clusters.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
