Lepidium reichei

Lepidium reichei, commonly known as the Peruvian rocket, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and pinnately divided leaves. It is often found growing in arid and semi-arid regions, thriving in rocky soils.

Lepidium reichei is also valued for its peppery taste, making it a potential candidate for culinary and medicinal uses.
