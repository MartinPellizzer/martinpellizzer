Lagochilus brachyacanthus

Lagochilus brachyacanthus, commonly known as the short-spined rock hyssop, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in dry, sunny environments. Its small, tubular flowers are usually blue or purple and bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
