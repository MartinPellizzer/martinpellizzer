Lotus ehrenbergii

Lotus ehrenbergii, commonly known as the desert lotus, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has adapted to harsh environments with its deep root system and ability to store water efficiently. Its leaves are typically small and hairy, reducing water loss through transpiration.

The seeds are enclosed in pods that split open when mature, dispersing the seeds through the wind.
