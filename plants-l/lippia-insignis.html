Lippia insignis

Lippia insignis, commonly known as Mexican mint or wild mint, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant features opposite, hairy leaves that are often fragrant and have a mint-like scent. It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale purple, arranged in clusters.

Lippia insignis is often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb due to its aromatic properties.
