Lespedeza paradoxa

Lespedeza paradoxa, commonly known as the paradox lespedeza, is a perennial flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in woodland or open areas. Its trifoliate leaves and distinctive seed pods contribute to its ornamental value in gardens.

Lespedeza paradoxa is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
