Lepanthes dolabriformis

Lepanthes dolabriformis, commonly known as the sickle-shaped orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated pseudobulbs and narrow, lanceolate leaves. The flowers are typically small and white or pale green, with three sepals and three petals, often arranged in a loose inflorescence. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in cloud forests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate with proper care.
