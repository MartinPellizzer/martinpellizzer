Lippia magentea

Lippia magentea, commonly known as Mexican blueweed, is a herbaceous plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly hairy leaves and small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Lippia magentea is often found in wetlands, prairies, and along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
