Limonia schweinfurthii

Limonia schweinfurthii, commonly known as the Madagascar tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves and fragrant, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow when ripe, containing a single seed. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

Limonia schweinfurthii is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dye.
