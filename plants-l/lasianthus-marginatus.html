Lasianthus marginatus

Lasianthus marginatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth margin and a distinctive silver-green hue. Its showy, tubular flowers are typically blue or purple, arranged in whorled clusters at the tips of the stems.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
