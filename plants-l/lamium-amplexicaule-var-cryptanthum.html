Lamium amplexicaule var. cryptanthum

Lamium amplexicaule var. cryptanthum, commonly known as purple deadnettle, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that clasping the stem, with purple flowers that bloom in clusters during spring. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks. Its flowers are tubular and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to suppress weeds.
