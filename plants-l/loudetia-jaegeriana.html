Loudetia jaegeriana

Loudetia jaegeriana, commonly known as the redroot lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
