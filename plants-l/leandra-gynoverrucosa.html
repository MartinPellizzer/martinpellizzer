Leandra gynoverrucosa

Leandra gynoverrucosa, commonly known as the woolly leandra, is a member of the Melastomataceae family and is native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, woody stems and broad, ovate leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
