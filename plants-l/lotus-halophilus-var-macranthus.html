Lotus halophilus var. macranthus

Lotus halophilus var. macranthus, commonly known as the salt marsh lotus, is a halophytic plant adapted to thrive in saline environments.

It possesses thick, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss in its arid habitat. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is capable of filtering out excess salt, allowing it to flourish in coastal and estuarine areas.

This species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
