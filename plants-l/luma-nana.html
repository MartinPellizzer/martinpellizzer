Luma nana

Luma nana, commonly known as the dwarf luma, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit. The plant features small, oval-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. It produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, berry-like fruits.

Luma nana thrives in coastal areas and is often used in traditional landscaping due to its hardy nature and ornamental value.
