Lupinus neglectus var. graminocamporum

Lupinus neglectus var. graminocamporum, commonly known as the grassland lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. The plant features erect, spindly stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are clustered in spikes, producing vibrant purple or pink pea-shaped blooms.

It plays an important ecological role by fixing nitrogen into the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
