Lupinus perennis subsp. plattensis

Lupinus perennis subsp.

plattensis, commonly known as the plains lupine, is a perennial legume native to the central United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to violet pea-like flowers.

The plant has trifoliate leaves with stipules, and its root system includes nitrogen-fixing nodules. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairie and open woodland habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and improving soil fertility.
