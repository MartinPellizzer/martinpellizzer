Ligularia sibirica subsp. sibirica

Ligularia sibirica subsp.

sibirica, commonly known as Siberian ligularia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Siberia. It features broad, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive wavy margin, often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in a terminal inflorescence, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

Its foliage is prized for its ornamental value in garden settings.
