Lophiolepis ossetica subsp. abkhasica

Lophiolepis ossetica subsp.

abkhasica, commonly known as the Abkhazian lophiolepis, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region. It features slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy.

The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale blue to purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its range.
