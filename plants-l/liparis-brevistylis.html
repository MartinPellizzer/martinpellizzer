Liparis brevistylis

Liparis brevistylis, commonly known as the short-styled orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in a loose inflorescence, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it has a short, stout rhizome.

Liparis brevistylis is a rare and endangered species, requiring specific habitat conditions for successful growth.
