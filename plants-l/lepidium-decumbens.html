Lepidium decumbens

Lepidium decumbens, commonly known as wild cress, is a low-growing, herbaceous plant native to various regions of North America.

It typically thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, stream banks, and along roadsides. The plant has small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by tiny, rounded seeds. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in small, raised dots, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Lepidium decumbens is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like broccoli and mustard.
