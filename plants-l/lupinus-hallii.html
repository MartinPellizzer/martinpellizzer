Lupinus hallii

Lupinus hallii, commonly known as the Hall's lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often used as forage for livestock. Lupinus hallii is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in open areas such as meadows and grasslands.

Its presence is an indicator of healthy, undisturbed ecosystems.
