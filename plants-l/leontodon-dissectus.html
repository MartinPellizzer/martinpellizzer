Leontodon dissectus

Leontodon dissectus, commonly known as the dissected hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant features finely dissected leaves that give it its common name, and it produces yellow ray flowers surrounded by pale yellow disk flowers.
