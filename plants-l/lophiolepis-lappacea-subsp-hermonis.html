Lophiolepis lappacea subsp. hermonis

Lophiolepis lappacea subsp.

hermonis, commonly known as the Hermon's lappaceae, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, pale green blades.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, white to pale yellow flowers. It thrives in well-drained sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal and inland fynbos vegetation.

Due to its ornamental appeal and ecological value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
