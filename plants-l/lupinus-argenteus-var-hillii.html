Lupinus argenteus var. hillii

Lupinus argenteus var. hillii, commonly known as the silver lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and tall spires of blue to violet flowers. The plant is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to native plant gardens.

Its striking appearance and ecological benefits make it a valuable species for conservation and landscaping efforts.
