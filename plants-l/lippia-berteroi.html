Lippia berteroi

Lippia berteroi, commonly known as Mexican mint or wild mint, is a perennial herb native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny environments such as meadows and stream banks. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

Lippia berteroi is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
