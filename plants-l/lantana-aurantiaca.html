Lantana aurantiaca

Lantana aurantiaca, commonly known as the orange lantana, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, brightly colored flowers, typically in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant has opposite, simple, and usually hairy leaves that are aromatic when crushed. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

However, it can be invasive in some regions and may pose a risk to native ecosystems.
