Lachnagrostis pilosa

Lachnagrostis pilosa, commonly known as hairy wiregrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and hairy, leaf-like blades that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as prairies and dunes.

Lachnagrostis pilosa plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
