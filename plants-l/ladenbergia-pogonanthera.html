Ladenbergia pogonanthera

Ladenbergia pogonanthera, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles. The plant is typically shrub-like, with a woody stem and can grow up to several meters in height. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, and it is often found in moist, shaded habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
