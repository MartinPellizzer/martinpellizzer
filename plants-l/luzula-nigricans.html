Luzula nigricans

Luzula nigricans, commonly known as black tuft sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features grass-like leaves that are dark green and often have a bluish tint, with dense, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowers are small, greenish-brown, and clustered in spikes, typical of the Cyperaceae family.

Luzula nigricans is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging, wet conditions.
