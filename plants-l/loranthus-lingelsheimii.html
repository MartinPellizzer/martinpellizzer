Loranthus lingelsheimii

Loranthus lingelsheimii, commonly known as the African mistletoe, is a parasitic plant belonging to the Loranthaceae family.

It grows on various host trees, primarily in Africa, and relies on its host for water and nutrients. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces white or yellowish flowers that attract insects for pollination. Its berries are red or orange and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitats by supporting bird populations and contributing to forest biodiversity.
