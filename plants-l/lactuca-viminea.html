Lactuca viminea

Lactuca viminea, commonly known as creeping lettuce, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family, Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is characterized by its low-growing, sprawling habit. The plant features slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine white hairs. Its small yellow flowers are arranged in a cluster and bloom in the summer months.

Lactuca viminea is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain regions.
