Limonium binervosum var. aurigniense

Limonium binervosum var. aurigniense, commonly known as the Aurigny sea holly, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to salty and windy conditions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
