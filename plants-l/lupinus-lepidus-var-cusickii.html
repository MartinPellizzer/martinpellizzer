Lupinus lepidus var. cusickii

Lupinus lepidus var. cusickii, commonly known as the Cusick's lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a soft, hairy coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Lupinus lepidus var. cusickii is also valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a food source for pollinators.
