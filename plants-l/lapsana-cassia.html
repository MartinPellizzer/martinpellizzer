Lapsana cassia

Lapsana cassia, commonly known as common cow-wheat, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and grows up to 1 meter tall, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. It is an annual or biennial herb that produces small, dry fruits called achenes.

The species is also known for its medicinal uses in traditional herbal remedies.
