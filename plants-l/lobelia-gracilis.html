Lobelia gracilis

Lobelia gracilis, commonly known as slender lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features delicate, whorled leaves and spikes of blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.

Lobelia gracilis is also a nectar source for various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
