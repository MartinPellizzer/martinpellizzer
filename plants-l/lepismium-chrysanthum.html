Lepismium chrysanthum

Lepismium chrysanthum, commonly known as the golden flame cactus, is a species of cactus native to the deserts of Central America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are covered in dense, white spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking visual contrast to its green foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
