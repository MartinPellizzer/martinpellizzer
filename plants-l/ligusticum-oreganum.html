Ligusticum oreganum

Ligusticum oreganum, commonly known as Oregon grape, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in umbels. The plant produces dark blue to black berries that are edible but often bitter. It thrives in woodland areas and has a preference for moist, well-drained soils.

Oregon grape is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
