Leptospermum inelegans

Leptospermum inelegans, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tea tree, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in winter. The plant is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas. It produces small, woody fruits that contain several seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological communities.
