Lophochloa salzmannii

Lophochloa salzmannii, commonly known as Salzmann's grass, is a perennial grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, tropical environments, often found in wetlands or along riverbanks. Its inflorescence consists of a spikelet cluster, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Lophochloa salzmannii plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
