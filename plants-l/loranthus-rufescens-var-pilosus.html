Loranthus rufescens var. pilosus

Loranthus rufescens var. pilosus, commonly known as the hairy mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to host trees such as eucalyptus and acacia.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own carbohydrates through photosynthesis using its leaves. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, hairy structures, giving it its common name. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are a food source for birds.

This species is often found in arid and semi-arid regions, contributing to the ecological dynamics of its host trees.
