Leucothoe catesbaei

Leucothoe catesbaei, commonly known as the Appalachian inkberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and dark green, glossy leaves that turn reddish in the fall. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by blue-black berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland habitats.

Leucothoe catesbaei is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
