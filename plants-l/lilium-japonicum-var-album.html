Lilium japonicum var. album

Lilium japonicum var. album, commonly known as the Japanese white lily, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its tall, upright stems and large, showy white flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer to early autumn, producing fragrant, trumpet-shaped blooms that can reach up to 10 centimeters in diameter. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, arranged in a basal rosette, and the plant prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
