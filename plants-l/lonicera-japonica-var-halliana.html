Lonicera japonica var. halliana

Lonicera japonica var. halliana, commonly known as the Hall's honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy on the underside. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are usually yellow or orange in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its berries are red and edible, though they are generally not consumed by humans.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
