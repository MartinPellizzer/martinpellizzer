Lophia phoenicea

Lophia phoenicea, commonly known as the red lily, is a striking plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy red flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern at the base. Lophia phoenicea is a perennial herb that thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
