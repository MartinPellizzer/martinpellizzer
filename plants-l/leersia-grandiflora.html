Leersia grandiflora

Leersia grandiflora, commonly known as broadleaf arrowgrass, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often have a slight sheen, while its inflorescence consists of a single, spike-like cluster of flowers.

Leersia grandiflora plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various insects and wildlife.
