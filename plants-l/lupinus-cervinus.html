Lupinus cervinus

Lupinus cervinus, commonly known as the red lupin, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features upright stems with clusters of pea-like flowers that range in color from pink to deep red. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Lupinus cervinus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
