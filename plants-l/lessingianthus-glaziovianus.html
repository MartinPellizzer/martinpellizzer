Lessingianthus glaziovianus

Lessingianthus glaziovianus, commonly known as the Glaziov's sunflower, is a herbaceous plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers and linear leaves. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and is adapted to full sun exposure. It produces small, cylindrical flower heads that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
