Lecananthus peduncularis

Lecananthus peduncularis, commonly known as the slender lecananthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
