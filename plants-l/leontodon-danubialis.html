Leontodon danubialis

Leontodon danubialis, commonly known as the Danube hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils along riverbanks and wet meadows. It features a basal rosette of leaves and produces yellow flowers with ray florets.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
