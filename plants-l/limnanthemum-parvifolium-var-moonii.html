Limnanthemum parvifolium var. moonii

Limnanthemum parvifolium var. moonii, commonly known as the Moon's Lilies, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, star-shaped white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has small, narrow leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
