Lachnopylis gracilis

Lachnopylis gracilis, commonly known as the slender lichen grass, is a member of the Poaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, grass-like appearance.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or along stream banks, where it thrives in nutrient-rich soil. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its unique texture. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually clustered in dense inflorescences, and it reproduces through wind-pollinated flowers.

Lachnopylis gracilis plays a vital role in maintaining ecological balance by providing habitat and food for various small invertebrates.
