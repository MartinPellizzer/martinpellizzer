Linum perenne subsp. anglicum

Linum perenne subsp.

anglicum, commonly known as English flax, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It is a herbaceous perennial that produces delicate blue or purple flowers, typically in late spring to early summer.

The plant has linear, hairy leaves and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils. It is closely related to the flax species used in textile production.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
