Lycopodioides leptophylla

Lycopodioides leptophylla, commonly known as the slender clubmoss, is a member of the family Lycopodiaceae and is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and erect, branching stems.

It is a primitive vascular plant that exhibits a sporophyte-dominated life cycle, with spores produced in specialized structures called sporangia. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or rocky crevices, where it thrives in humid conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and linear, and it produces both microspores and megaspores, contributing to its reproductive diversity.

Lycopodioides leptophylla plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
