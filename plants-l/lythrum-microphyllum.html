Lythrum microphyllum

Lythrum microphyllum, commonly known as narrow-leaved catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Lythraceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its flowers, which are usually pink or purple, bloom in late spring to early summer.

The leaves are narrow and opposite, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
