Lepanthanthe cruttwellii

Lepanthanthe cruttwellii, commonly known as the Cruttwell's orchid, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pale pink. The plant has slender, wiry stems and produces small, clustered inflorescences. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, often with a waxy surface that helps retain moisture in its humid habitat.

Lepanthanthe cruttwellii is primarily grown as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and unique floral structure.
