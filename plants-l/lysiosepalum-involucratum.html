Lysiosepalum involucratum

Lysiosepalum involucratum, commonly known as the velvetleaf sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers with a distinctive violet or purple central disc. The plant has palmate leaves that are hairy and lobed, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated as a garden plant.

The fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, black seeds.
