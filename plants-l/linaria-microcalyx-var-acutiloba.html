Linaria microcalyx var. acutiloba

Linaria microcalyx var. acutiloba, commonly known as the narrow-leaf toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, branched stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces bright yellow, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators make it a notable species in native plant gardens.
