Leucheria aurita

Leucheria aurita, commonly known as the golden star moss, is a small, delicate moss species that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to golden-green coloration, which is particularly noticeable in its leaf-like structures. The plant forms dense, carpet-like mats on the forest floor, often found in temperate and subtropical regions. Its reproductive structures, known as sporophytes, are typically small and inconspicuous, emerging from the gametophyte stage.

Leucheria aurita plays a vital role in soil stabilization and moisture retention in its native ecosystems.
