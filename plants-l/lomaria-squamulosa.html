Lomaria squamulosa

Lomaria squamulosa, commonly known as the squamulose lomaria, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are usually covered in a layer of fine, scale-like structures, giving it its scientific name.

Lomaria squamulosa is often found in coastal areas and is known for its resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
