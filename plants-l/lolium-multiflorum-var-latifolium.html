Lolium multiflorum var. latifolium

Lolium multiflorum var. latifolium, commonly known as broadleaf ryegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its wide, flat leaves and dense, tufted inflorescences that produce small, spikelet-like flowers. This plant thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, fertile ground. It is often used in forage production and as a cover crop due to its rapid growth and high nutritional value.

Additionally, it plays a role in ecological systems by preventing soil erosion and supporting pollinator populations.
