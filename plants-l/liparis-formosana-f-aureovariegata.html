Liparis formosana f. aureovariegata

Liparis formosana f. aureovariegata, commonly known as the golden variegated lady's tresses, is a variegated form of the orchid species Liparis formosana.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated leaves with a mix of green and golden hues. The plant produces dense clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in a loose, horseshoe-shaped inflorescence. It is native to Taiwan and thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in mountainous regions.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated in specialty orchid collections.
