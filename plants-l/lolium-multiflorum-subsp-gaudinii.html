Lolium multiflorum subsp. gaudinii

Lolium multiflorum subsp.

gaudinii, commonly known as the Gaudini's ryegrass, is a cool-season grass native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed areas.

Due to its adaptability, it is both a valuable forage crop and a potential weed in agricultural settings.
