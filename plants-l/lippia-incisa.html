Lippia incisa

Lippia incisa, commonly known as Mexican mint or wild mint, is a perennial shrub native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, serrated leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady areas and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are widely used in traditional medicine and culinary applications due to their strong, mint-like aroma.

Lippia incisa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
