Liparis subplicata

Liparis subplicata, commonly known as the pleated orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pleated leaves and small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant prefers shady, moist environments and is often found in forest understories. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from decaying organic matter.

Liparis subplicata is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its role in ecological systems.
