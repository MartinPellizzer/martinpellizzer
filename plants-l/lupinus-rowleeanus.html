Lupinus rowleeanus

Lupinus rowleeanus, commonly known as the Rowleean lupine, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and clusters of pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowers, which range in color from white to violet, bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Lupinus rowleeanus is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in ecological restoration efforts.
