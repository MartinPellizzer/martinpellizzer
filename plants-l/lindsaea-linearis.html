Lindsaea linearis

Lindsaea linearis, commonly known as the slender lindsaea, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, linear fronds that are typically less than 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the undersides of its leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Lindsaea linearis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
