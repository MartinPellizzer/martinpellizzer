Lobelia australiensis

Lobelia australiensis, commonly known as the Australian lobelia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces showy inflorescences that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Lobelia australiensis is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
