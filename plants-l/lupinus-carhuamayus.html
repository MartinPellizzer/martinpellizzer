Lupinus carhuamayus

Lupinus carhuamayus, commonly known as the Andean lupine, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of blue or purple flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to grow in high-altitude environments and is often found in rocky or semi-arid habitats.

Lupinus carhuamayus is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in ecological restoration efforts.
