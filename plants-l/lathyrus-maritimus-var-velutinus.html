Lathyrus maritimus var. velutinus

Lathyrus maritimus var. velutinus, commonly known as the silky field bean, is a flowering plant native to coastal areas of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of pea-like flowers that range in color from pink to purple, followed by pod-like fruits containing edible seeds.

It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dune environments, where it plays a role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems.
