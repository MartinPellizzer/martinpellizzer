Lippia nodiflora var. suborbicularis

Lippia nodiflora var. suborbicularis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lippia, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that are typically lavender to pink in color, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems. The leaves are narrow and oval, with a slightly hairy surface, and the plant often grows in moist, sunny environments. It is a member of the verbena family (Verbenaceae) and is known for its aromatic properties.

This variety is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine and as a ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and fragrance.
