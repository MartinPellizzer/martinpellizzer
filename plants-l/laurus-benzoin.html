Laurus benzoin

Laurus benzoin, commonly known as Indian bay leaf, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, ovate leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark, spherical berries. Its essential oils, extracted from the leaves and bark, contain compounds like cineole and linalool, which contribute to its fragrance and therapeutic properties.

Laurus benzoin thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its aromatic value.
