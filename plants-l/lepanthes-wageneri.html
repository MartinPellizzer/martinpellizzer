Lepanthes wageneri

Lepanthes wageneri, commonly known as the Wagener's slipper orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that resemble small slippers, typically in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has aerial roots and thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on trees or rocks. Its flowers bloom during the wet season, attracting various pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

Lepanthes wageneri is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
