Lantana camara f. varia

Lantana camara f. varia, commonly known as the variegated lantana, is a compact, shrub-like plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, branching habit and small, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to orange and red. The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate, with a glossy surface and often exhibit variegation patterns of green, white, and yellow. This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in full sun to partial shade.

It is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
