Leptandra japonica

Leptandra japonica, commonly known as Japanese leptandra, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in clusters during the spring. Its fruits are dry, capsule-like structures that release seeds in the autumn.

Leptandra japonica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to a variety of soil types.
