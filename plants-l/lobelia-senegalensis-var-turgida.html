Lobelia senegalensis var. turgida

Lobelia senegalensis var. turgida, commonly known as the Senegal lobelia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a velvety texture, while its stems are erect and branching.

This species is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
