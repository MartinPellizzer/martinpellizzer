Lycopodium acutum

Lycopodium acutum, commonly known as the sharp clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. The plant features erect, branching stems covered with small, scale-like leaves. Its reproductive structures, called strobila, are cone-like and produce spores for propagation.

Lycopodium acutum plays a role in maintaining soil stability and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
