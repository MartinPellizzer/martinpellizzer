Luzula banksiana var. rhadina

Luzula banksiana var. rhadina, commonly known as the silver woodrush, is a perennial plant belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage that forms low, compact clumps. The leaves are typically silver-gray in color due to a dense covering of fine hairs, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer, arranged in spike-like clusters.

This variety is often found in rocky or gravelly soils in alpine and subalpine regions.
