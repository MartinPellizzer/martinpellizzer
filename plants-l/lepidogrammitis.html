Lepidogrammitis

Lepidogrammitis, commonly known as the golden fern, is a type of epiphytic fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, intricate fronds that are often covered in a fine, scaly texture. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it to trees or rocks.
