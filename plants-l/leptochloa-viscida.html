Leptochloa viscida

Leptochloa viscida, commonly known as sticky bluemink, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and dense, mat-like growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue to purplish flowers. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a sticky texture.

Leptochloa viscida is typically found in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments.
