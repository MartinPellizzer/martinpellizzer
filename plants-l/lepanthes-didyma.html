Lepanthes didyma

Lepanthes didyma, commonly known as the twin-veined orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically range in color from white to pale pink. The plant features a slender, erect stem with several leaves and produces flowers in clusters. Lepanthes didyma thrives in shaded, humid environments, often growing in the understory of tropical forests.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and its role in the ecological diversity of orchid habitats.
