Lachnaea diosmoides

Lachnaea diosmoides, commonly known as the desert lichen, is a unique plant that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments.

It is a lichen, which is a symbiotic organism composed of a fungus and a photosynthetic partner, typically a green alga or cyanobacterium. This plant is characterized by its crust-like growth form, allowing it to cling to rocks and withstand extreme temperatures and drought. Lachnaea diosmoides plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms in its harsh environment.
