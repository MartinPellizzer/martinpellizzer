Lampranthus roseus

Lampranthus roseus, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features striking red or orange tubular flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has fleshy, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that help it store water. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its vibrant flowers attract pollinators like bees and birds, adding both aesthetic and ecological value to the landscape.
