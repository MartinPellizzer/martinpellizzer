Lepanthes ptyxis

Lepanthes ptyxis, commonly known as the narrow-leafed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically range in color from white to pale pink. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing in the understory of tropical forests. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, adapted to capture limited light in its natural habitat.

Lepanthes ptyxis is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
