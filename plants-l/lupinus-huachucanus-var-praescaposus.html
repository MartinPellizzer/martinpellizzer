Lupinus huachucanus var. praescaposus

Lupinus huachucanus var. praescaposus, commonly known as the Huachuca lupine, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces cylindrical, spiny seed pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats, particularly in desert or semi-desert regions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
