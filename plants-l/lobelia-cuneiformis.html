Lobelia cuneiformis

Lobelia cuneiformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and spikes of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It belongs to the Campanulaceae family, which includes other lobelias and bellflowers.

Lobelia cuneiformis is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
