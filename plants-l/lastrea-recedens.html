Lastrea recedens

Lastrea recedens, commonly known as the creeping lastrea, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family, also known as the ice plant family, and is characterized by its fleshy, water-storing leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its unique ability to store water in its leaves allows it to survive in arid conditions with minimal care.
