Luzula acutifolia

Luzula acutifolia, commonly known as sharpleaf wood sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodlands, meadows, and streambanks. Its slender, triangular stems are often adorned with grass-like leaves that have sharp, pointed tips.

Luzula acutifolia produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spike-like clusters, which are typically pollinated by wind.
