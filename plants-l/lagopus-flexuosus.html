Lagopus flexuosus

Lagopus flexuosus, commonly known as the slender snowgrass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of North America.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer, and its leaves are typically narrow and covered in a fine layer of white hairs. It thrives in cold, nutrient-poor soils and is well adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

The slender snowgrass plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various Arctic and alpine organisms.
