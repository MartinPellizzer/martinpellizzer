Lactuca azurea

Lactuca azurea, commonly known as blue lettuce, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers and rosette of dark green, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its milky sap. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Lactuca azurea is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ecological value.
