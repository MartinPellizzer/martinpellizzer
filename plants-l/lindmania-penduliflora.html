Lindmania penduliflora

Lindmania penduliflora, commonly known as the hanging-flowered lindmania, is a species of flowering plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the family Bromeliaceae, which includes many epiphytic plants that thrive in humid environments. The plant is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a water-holding structure, typical of bromeliads. Its pendulous inflorescence bears small, showy flowers that are often visited by pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is a subject of interest in botanical research and conservation efforts.
