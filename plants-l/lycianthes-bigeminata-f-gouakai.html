Lycianthes bigeminata f. gouakai

Lycianthes bigeminata f. gouakai, commonly known as the pink-flowered lycianthes, is a variant of the species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive pink or reddish flowers, which are typically borne in clusters. The plant is a shrub or small tree with opposite, ovate leaves that have a slightly hairy texture. It produces small, round fruits that are edible when ripe.

This form is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
