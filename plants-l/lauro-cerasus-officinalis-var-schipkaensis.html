Lauro-cerasus officinalis var. schipkaensis

Lauro-cerasus officinalis var. schipkaensis, commonly known as the Schipka cherry laurel, is a botanical variant native to the Carpathian region.

It is a small evergreen shrub or tree with dense, dark green, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in spring, followed by edible red berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Its leaves and berries have been historically used in traditional medicine for their aromatic and mild medicinal properties.
