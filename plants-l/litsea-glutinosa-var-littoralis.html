Litsea glutinosa var. littoralis

Litsea glutinosa var. littoralis, commonly known as the coastal litsea, is a shrub or small tree native to coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, elliptical leaves that are often glandular-dotted. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by spherical, sticky fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in mangrove or brackish water environments.

Its adaptability to coastal conditions makes it a valuable species for ecological restoration projects.
