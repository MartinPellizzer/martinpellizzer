Lonchitis sinuata

Lonchitis sinuata, commonly known as the Sinuous Bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is native to regions of East Asia, including China, Korea, and Japan. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly wavy or sinuous margin, giving the plant its scientific name.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.
