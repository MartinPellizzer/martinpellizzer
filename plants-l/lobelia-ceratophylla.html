Lobelia ceratophylla

Lobelia ceratophylla, commonly known as the flame azalea, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, often appearing in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade. It is a member of the Campanulaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
