Leptochilus cuspidatus var. normalis

Leptochilus cuspidatus var. normalis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features small, opposite, ovate leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers in terminal clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or borders.

Its aromatic foliage and compact growth habit make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
