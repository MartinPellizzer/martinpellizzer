Lacostea rhomboidea

Lacostea rhomboidea, commonly known as the diamond-shaped orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, rhombus-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often with a distinct yellow or greenish hue at the base. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts for its aesthetic appeal.
