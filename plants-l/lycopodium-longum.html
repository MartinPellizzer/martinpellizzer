Lycopodium longum

Lycopodium longum, commonly known as the club moss, is a primitive vascular plant belonging to the family Lycopodiales.

It is characterized by its small, spore-bearing cones and its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its upright, branching stems and scale-like leaves. Lycopodium longum is often found in forest floors and rocky areas, contributing to soil stabilization.

Its historical use in traditional medicine and its role in ecological systems highlight its botanical significance.
