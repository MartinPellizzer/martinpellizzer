Ludovia palmata

Ludovia palmata, commonly known as the red-veined palm, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its distinctive, deeply veined leaves that display a vibrant red coloration.

Native to the humid lowland forests of Southeast Asia, it thrives in warm, moist environments with high humidity. The plant features a slender, unbranched stem that can grow up to several meters in height, supporting its large, pinnately compound leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in axillary inflorescences, and it produces small, round fruits that are edible.

Due to its ornamental value, Ludovia palmata is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
