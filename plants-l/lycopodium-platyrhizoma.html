Lycopodium platyrhizoma

Lycopodium platyrhizoma, commonly known as the clubmoss, is a primitive vascular plant belonging to the order Lycopodiales.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and a sporophytic structure that resembles a club, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and rocky areas. Its root system is extensive and fibrous, which helps it anchor in nutrient-poor soils.

Lycopodium platyrhizoma reproduces via spores rather than seeds, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms.
