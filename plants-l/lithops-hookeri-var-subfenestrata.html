Lithops hookeri var. subfenestrata

Lithops hookeri var. subfenestrata, commonly known as the "Bald Mountain Lithops," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its paired, fleshy leaves that resemble stones, which help it survive in arid environments. The leaves are typically pale green with distinct white or yellow markings, especially around the edges. During the flowering season, it produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange.

This variety is prized for its unique appearance and ease of care, making it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
