Leptalix teres

Leptalix teres, commonly known as the slender leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of southern Africa.

It typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in a dense, umbel-like cluster, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its root system consists of a fibrous network that helps it thrive in well-drained soils.

Leptalix teres is often found in open grasslands and along the edges of woodlands, where it plays a minor role in local ecosystems.
