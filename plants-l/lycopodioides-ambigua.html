Lycopodioides ambigua

Lycopodioides ambigua, commonly known as the ambiguous clubmoss, is a species of lycophyte belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and a creeping, rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces spore-bearing strobili, which are typically clustered at the tips of its stems. Lycopodioides ambigua is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or rocky crevices.

Its reproductive structures are adapted for dispersal by wind, contributing to its ecological role in nutrient cycling and habitat structure.
