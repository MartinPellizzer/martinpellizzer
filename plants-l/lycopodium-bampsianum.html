Lycopodium bampsianum

Lycopodium bampsianum, commonly known as the Bampsian clubmoss, is a small, evergreen plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems covered in tiny, scale-like leaves. The plant produces spores in small, cone-like structures called strobila, which are typically found at the tips of the branches. Native to specific regions in South America, it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
