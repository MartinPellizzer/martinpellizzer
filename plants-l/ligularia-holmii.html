Ligularia holmii

Ligularia holmii, commonly known as the giant marsh bellflower, is a herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter. The plant produces showy, yellowish-orange flowers with prominent, tongue-like lips. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as wetlands and stream banks.

Ligularia holmii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens with high humidity.
