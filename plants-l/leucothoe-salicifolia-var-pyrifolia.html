Leucothoe salicifolia var. pyrifolia

Leucothoe salicifolia var. pyrifolia, commonly known as the goldenleaf leucothoe, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features broad, ovate leaves that are typically a deep green with a yellowish or golden underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white to pale green flowers in clusters during early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This ornamental plant is valued for its attractive foliage and is often used in woodland gardens.
