Leochilus labiatus

Leochilus labiatus, commonly known as the pink rock orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in open woodland or along riverbanks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the flowers are clustered in racemes.

Leochilus labiatus is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions.
