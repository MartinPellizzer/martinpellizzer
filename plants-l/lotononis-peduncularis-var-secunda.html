Lotononis peduncularis var. secunda

Lotononis peduncularis var. secunda, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in the early morning, hence its common name. The plant features slender, twining stems that climb or trail along the ground. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
