Linanthus floribundus subsp. glaber

Linanthus floribundus subsp.

glaber, commonly known as the slender flaxflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers.

The subspecies glaber has smooth, hairless leaves and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It blooms in late spring to early summer, producing showy inflorescences that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

This plant is adapted to arid environments and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native range.
