Lobivia huascha var. purpureominiata

Lobivia huascha var. purpureominiata, commonly known as the Purple Minaret Cactus, is a compact, spineless cactus native to the Andes of Peru.

It features a short, cylindrical stem with a distinctive purple to reddish hue, particularly on the upper parts. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it thrives in well-draining soil with full sun exposure.

It is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its hardy nature and ornamental appeal.
