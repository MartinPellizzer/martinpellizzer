Limonium gummiferum var. muticum

Limonium gummiferum var. muticum, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, typically from July to September. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of salt, adapting to its saline environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dunes or along rocky shores.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for coastal gardens and landscaping.
