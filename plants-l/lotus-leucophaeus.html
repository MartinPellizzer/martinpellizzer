Lotus leucophaeus

Lotus leucophaeus, commonly known as the white lotus, is a aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom during the early morning. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface. Its roots are submerged, anchoring it in shallow waters.

The white lotus is known for its symbolic significance in various cultures and its use in traditional medicine.
