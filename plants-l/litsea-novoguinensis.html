Litsea novoguinensis

Litsea novoguinensis, commonly known as the New Zealand laurifolium, is a small evergreen tree native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its simple, lanceolate leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that appear in clusters, followed by elliptical, yellowish-orange berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland forest habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
