Laelia harpophylla var. xanthina

Laelia harpophylla var. xanthina, commonly known as the yellow-leaved laelia, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Andes Mountains.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange floral bracts and pendulous inflorescences. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually small and fragrant, appearing in late winter to early spring.

This variety is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its striking coloration and ease of cultivation.
