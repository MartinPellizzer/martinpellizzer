Linaria yusufeliensis

Linaria yusufeliensis, commonly known as the Yusufeli's toadflax, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of bright yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its flowers are tubular and bilabiate, typical of the genus Linaria, and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
