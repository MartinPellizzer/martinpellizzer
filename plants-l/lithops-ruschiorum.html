Lithops ruschiorum

Lithops ruschiorum, commonly known as the Cape Town stone plant, is a member of the Aizoaceae family and is native to South Africa.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its paired, stone-like leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers during the summer months, typically in late spring. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
