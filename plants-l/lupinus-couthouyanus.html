Lupinus couthouyanus

Lupinus couthouyanus, commonly known as the Couthouy's lupin, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of bright yellow or orange flowers. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for rocky or sandy habitats.

Lupinus couthouyanus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
