Lupinus stipaphilus

Lupinus stipaphilus, commonly known as the sticky lupin, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, stiff leaflets that give it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Lupinus stipaphilus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
