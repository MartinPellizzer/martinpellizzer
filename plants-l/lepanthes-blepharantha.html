Lepanthes blepharantha

Lepanthes blepharantha, commonly known as the showy orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that often feature intricate patterns and vibrant hues. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments within the understory of forests. Its flowers are usually small to medium in size, with three sepals and three petals, one of which is modified into a lip.

Lepanthes blepharantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its native habitats.
