Laurus tigricida

Laurus tigricida, commonly known as the tiger laurel, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other well-known species like bay laurel. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is a bright red drupe that contains a single seed.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional medicine practices.
