Lathyrus pubescens var. acutifolius

Lathyrus pubescens var. acutifolius, commonly known as the acutely leafed grass violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that bear alternate, lanceolate leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of purple or blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or woodland borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant collections and pollinator habitats.
