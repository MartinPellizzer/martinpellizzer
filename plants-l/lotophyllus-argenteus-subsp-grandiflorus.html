Lotophyllus argenteus subsp. grandiflorus

Lotophyllus argenteus subsp.

grandiflorus, commonly known as the silverleaf lotus, is a rare and striking plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its large, showy white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The leaves are typically silver or white-veined, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
