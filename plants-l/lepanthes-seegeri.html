Lepanthes seegeri

Lepanthes seegeri, commonly known as the Seeger's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as cloud forests, where it thrives on tree bark or moss. Lepanthes seegeri has small, strap-like leaves and produces flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts and horticulturists.
