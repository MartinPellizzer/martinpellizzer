Lessertia vesicaria

Lessertia vesicaria, commonly known as the bladderleaf or bladderleaf fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, pinnately divided fronds. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Its unique leaf morphology and reproductive structures make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
