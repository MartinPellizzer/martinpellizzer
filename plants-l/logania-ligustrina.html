Logania ligustrina

Logania ligustrina, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Loganiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture, while its fruits are follicles that split open to release seeds.

Logania ligustrina is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant blooms and unique appearance.
