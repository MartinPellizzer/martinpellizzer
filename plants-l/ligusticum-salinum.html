Ligusticum salinum

Ligusticum salinum, commonly known as sea holly, is a perennial herb native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It features a rosette of blue-gray, fuzzy leaves and produces striking yellow flowers with prominent, spiky bracts. The plant thrives in salty, well-drained soils and is often found in dunes and coastal grasslands. Its aromatic foliage and unique floral structure make it a distinctive member of the Apiaceae family.

Ligusticum salinum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
