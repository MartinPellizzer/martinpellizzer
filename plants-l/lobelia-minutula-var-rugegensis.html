Lobelia minutula var. rugegensis

Lobelia minutula var. rugegensis, commonly known as the dwarf lobelia, is a small herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of only a few inches, with delicate, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This variety is known for its ability to bloom throughout the growing season, adding continuous color to shaded landscapes.
