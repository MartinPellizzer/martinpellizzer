Lagochilus androssowii

Lagochilus androssowii, commonly known as the Tibetan thyme, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers and hairy, narrow leaves. It thrives in high-altitude environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its unique ecological role and medicinal potential, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
