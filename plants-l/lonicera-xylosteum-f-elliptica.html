Lonicera xylosteum f. elliptica

Lonicera xylosteum f. elliptica, commonly known as the elliptic honeysuckle, is a variant of the honeysuckle plant characterized by its elliptical-shaped leaves.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in temperate regions, featuring fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds but generally not for humans. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it an attractive addition to gardens and natural habitats.
