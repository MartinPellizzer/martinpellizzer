Leptochloa halei

Leptochloa halei, commonly known as Hale's grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is a low-growing perennial grass characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and compact growth habit. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the grass family.

It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open fields.
