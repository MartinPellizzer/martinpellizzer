Lycopodioides conferta

Lycopodioides conferta, commonly known as the clustered clubmoss, is a small, evergreen plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of needle-like leaves. The plant produces sporophytes that are typically small and inconspicuous, with spore-bearing structures called strobila. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices.

Its botanical features make it an important component of understory vegetation in temperate and tropical regions.
