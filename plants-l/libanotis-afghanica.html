Libanotis afghanica

Libanotis afghanica, commonly known as Afghan bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia, particularly Afghanistan and surrounding regions.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. It has hairy, narrow leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer.

Libanotis afghanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and hardy nature.
