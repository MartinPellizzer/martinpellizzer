Lycianthes chrysothrix subsp. dolichopoda

Lycianthes chrysothrix subsp.

dolichopoda, commonly known as the long-fruited goldenleaf, is a member of the Solanaceae family. It is native to Central America and is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and elongated fruits.

The plant typically grows as an annual herb with hairy stems and opposite leaves. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

The subspecies is distinguished by its longer fruiting structures compared to other members of the species.
