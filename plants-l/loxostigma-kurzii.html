Loxostigma kurzii

Loxostigma kurzii, commonly known as the Kashmir rose, is a shrub native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan.

It is characterized by its fragrant pink or white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, red berries that are edible. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Loxostigma kurzii is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
