Leontodon laconicus

Leontodon laconicus, commonly known as the Laconic hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while its yellow flowers bloom from late spring to early summer.

Leontodon laconicus is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
