Ligustrum myrianthum

Ligustrum myrianthum, commonly known as the many-flowered privet, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, opposite leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, followed by small, black berries in the fall. It is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and hardy nature.

Ligustrum myrianthum is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and light conditions.
