Leonotis mollissima var. carnea

Leonotis mollissima var. carnea, commonly known as the red lantern bush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters, resembling small lanterns. The plant has soft, velvety leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

This species is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
