Lippia salicifolia

Lippia salicifolia, commonly known as silverleaf lippia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine white hairs, giving them a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale purple, arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Lippia salicifolia is often used in herbal medicine for its potential aromatic and medicinal properties.
