Lychnophora haplopappa

Lychnophora haplopappa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along streambanks.

Lychnophora haplopappa has been used traditionally for its purported medicinal benefits, though scientific validation remains limited.
