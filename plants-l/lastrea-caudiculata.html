Lastrea caudiculata

Lastrea caudiculata, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant has a short, thick stem and is adapted to arid environments, making it a hardy succulent. Its leaves are usually fleshy and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water storage.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
