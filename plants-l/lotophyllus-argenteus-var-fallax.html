Lotophyllus argenteus var. fallax

Lotophyllus argenteus var. fallax, commonly known as the silverleaf lotophyllus, is a rare and unique plant species characterized by its striking silver-green foliage.

The leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine, waxy coating that gives them their distinctive appearance. This variety thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires ample sunlight to maintain its vibrant coloration. It is native to arid regions and is adapted to withstand periods of drought.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by horticultural enthusiasts.
