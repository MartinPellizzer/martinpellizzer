Lappula divaricata

Lappula divaricata, commonly known as the broadleaf lappula, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas. The plant has alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy, and it produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters. Its fruit is a dry, three-lobed capsule that contains several seeds.

Lappula divaricata is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
