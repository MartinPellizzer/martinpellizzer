Linum pannonicum

Linum pannonicum, commonly known as the Hungarian flax or Pannonian flax, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Europe.

It belongs to the genus Linum in the family Linaceae, which includes other species of flax. The plant is characterized by its slender stems and small, delicate blue or purple flowers. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Linum pannonicum is often found in meadows and along riverbanks, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.
