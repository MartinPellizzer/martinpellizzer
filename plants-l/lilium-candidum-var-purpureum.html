Lilium candidum var. purpureum

Lilium candidum var. purpureum, commonly known as the purple lily, is a variant of the white lily native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its pale pink to deep purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with broad, lance-shaped leaves. Its bulbs are adapted for storage and propagation, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in perfumery and medicine.
