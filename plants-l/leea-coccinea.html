Leea coccinea

Leea coccinea, commonly known as the red-leaved leea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red foliage, which contrasts with its small, white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

Leea coccinea is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded gardens.
