Lithophila muscoides var. longifolia

Lithophila muscoides var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved lithophila, is a member of the Ericaceae family and is native to South Africa.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows on rocky outcrops and quartzitic soils. The plant features narrow, leathery leaves that are often clustered at the base, and it produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white. It is adapted to arid conditions and requires well-drained soil to thrive.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens.
