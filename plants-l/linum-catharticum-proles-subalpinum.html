Linum catharticum proles subalpinum

Linum catharticum proles subalpinum, commonly known as the subalpine flax, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and cool climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its seeds are encased in a capsule that splits open to release the seeds.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its ecological adaptations and potential medicinal properties.
