Linum subasperifolium

Linum subasperifolium, commonly known as the rough-leaved flax, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, rough-textured leaves. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Linum subasperifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in fiber production.
