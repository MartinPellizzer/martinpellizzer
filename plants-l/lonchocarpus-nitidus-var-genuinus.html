Lonchocarpus nitidus var. genuinus

Lonchocarpus nitidus var. genuinus, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces distinctive yellow-orange pods that contain flat, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local cultures.
