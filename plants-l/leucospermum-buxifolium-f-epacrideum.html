Leucospermum buxifolium f. epacrideum

Leucospermum buxifolium f. epacrideum, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a compact shrub native to the Western Cape of South Africa.

It features bright orange to red flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are dark green and resemble those of boxwood, giving it its specific epithet "buxifolium.". It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

The floral display is particularly striking, with its cup-shaped blooms resembling miniature sunflowers.
