Lupinus × hybridus f. albococcineus

Lupinus × hybridus f. albococcineus, commonly known as the white lupin, is a hybrid species within the lupin genus.

It is characterized by its distinctive white to pale yellow flowers and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

However, it contains toxic alkaloids and should be handled with care.
