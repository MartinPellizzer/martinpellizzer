Laurus pseudocassia

Laurus pseudocassia, commonly known as the Chinese bay leaf, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its aromatic evergreen leaves, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its aromatic properties.

The leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its distinctive fragrance and various traditional uses.
