Luzula caespitosa subsp. iberica

Luzula caespitosa subsp.

iberica, commonly known as the Iberian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and mountainous regions.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in dense clusters during the spring. It has a clump-forming growth habit, with rhizomes that help it spread and establish in suitable habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in the study of alpine and temperate flora.
