Lodhra grandiflora

Lodhra grandiflora, commonly known as the Indian privet, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Oleaceae and is characterized by its opposite, simple, and ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by dark blue, fleshy berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional medicinal uses in Ayurveda.

The bark and leaves are used in herbal remedies for their astringent and anti-inflammatory properties.
