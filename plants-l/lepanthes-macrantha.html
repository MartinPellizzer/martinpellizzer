Lepanthes macrantha

Lepanthes macrantha, commonly known as the large-flowered slipper orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its showy, violet or purple flowers with a distinctive lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical forests. Its botanical features include slender pseudobulbs and elongated, lance-shaped leaves.

Lepanthes macrantha is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
