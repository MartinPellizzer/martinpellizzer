Linostrophum

Linostrophum, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in racemes. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release its seeds.

Linostrophum is often found in disturbed soils and is considered an invasive species in some regions.
