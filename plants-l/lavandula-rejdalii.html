Lavandula rejdalii

Lavandula rejdalii, commonly known as the Rejdal lavender, is a species of flowering plant in the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe, particularly in Spain and Portugal. This perennial shrub is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and spikes of purple or pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is well adapted to dry, sunny climates and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.

Its essential oils, extracted from the flowers, are valued for their fragrance and medicinal properties.
