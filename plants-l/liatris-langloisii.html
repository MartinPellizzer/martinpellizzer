Liatris langloisii

Liatris langloisii, commonly known as the Langlois blazingstar, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and showy purple or pink flower heads. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically visited by bees and other pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairies.

Liatris langloisii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinator populations.
