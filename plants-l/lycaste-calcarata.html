Lycaste calcarata

Lycaste calcarata, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, spurred orchid flowers. The plant typically grows on rocky substrates and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, fragrant blooms in shades of white and pink.

Lycaste calcarata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological significance in its native habitat.
