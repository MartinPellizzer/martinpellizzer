Lepteranthus linifolius

Lepteranthus linifolius, commonly known as the slender-leaved orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, linear leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its root system is fibrous, and it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.

The botanical features of Lepteranthus linifolius make it an important subject for studies on orchid ecology and conservation.
