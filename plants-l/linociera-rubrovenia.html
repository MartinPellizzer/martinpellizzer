Linociera rubrovenia

Linociera rubrovenia, commonly known as the red-veined liana, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and heart-shaped leaves with prominent red veins. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its unique vascular patterns and vibrant fruit make it a notable species in botanical studies.
