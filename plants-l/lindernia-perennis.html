Lindernia perennis

Lindernia perennis, commonly known as the common lindernia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its trailing stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of purple, pink, or white, which bloom from late spring to early fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, ditches, and along streambanks.

Lindernia perennis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates.
