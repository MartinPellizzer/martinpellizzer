Lepanthes ingramii

Lepanthes ingramii, commonly known as the Ingram's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid with delicate, showy flowers that typically bloom in the spring. The plant features a compact rhizome and produces pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowering structures. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals.

Lepanthes ingramii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and rarity.
