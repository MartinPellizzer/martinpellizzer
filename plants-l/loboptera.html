Loboptera

Loboptera, commonly known as the common lichen, is a type of symbiotic organism composed of a fungus and a photosynthetic partner, typically a green alga or cyanobacterium.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found on tree bark, rocks, or soil surfaces. The structure of Loboptera consists of a dense, crust-like thallus that can vary in color depending on the species and environmental conditions. It plays a crucial role in nutrient cycling and serves as a habitat for various microorganisms.

Due to its sensitivity to air pollution, Loboptera is often used as an indicator of environmental health.
