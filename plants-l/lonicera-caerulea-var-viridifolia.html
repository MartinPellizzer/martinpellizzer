Lonicera caerulea var. viridifolia

Lonicera caerulea var. viridifolia, commonly known as the green-leaf honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in the spring, followed by edible berries that are often used in jams and wines. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a suitable candidate for ornamental and edible gardening.

Its unique foliage and fruit make it a valuable addition to both landscapes and food gardens.
