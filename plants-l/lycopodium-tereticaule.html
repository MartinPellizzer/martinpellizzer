Lycopodium tereticaule

Lycopodium tereticaule, commonly known as the slender clubmoss, is a species of lycophyte belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is a small, herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. The plant features a short, erect stem with spirally arranged leaves that are narrow and elongated. Its sporophyte produces spore-bearing structures called strobila at the tip of the stem.

Lycopodium tereticaule plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
