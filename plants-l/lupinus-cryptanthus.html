Lupinus cryptanthus

Lupinus cryptanthus, commonly known as the cryptanthus lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, rounded leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. Lupinus cryptanthus is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is often found in coastal and inland desert regions.

It plays an important ecological role by fixing nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plant life.
