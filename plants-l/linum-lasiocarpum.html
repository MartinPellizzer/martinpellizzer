Linum lasiocarpum

Linum lasiocarpum, commonly known as the woolly flax.

It is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves. The plant produces showy blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. It belongs to the Linaceae family, which includes other flax species.

Linum lasiocarpum is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
