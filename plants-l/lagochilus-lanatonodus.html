Lagochilus lanatonodus

Lagochilus lanatonodus, commonly known as the woolly bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves. The plant produces clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and steppe habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal medicine.
