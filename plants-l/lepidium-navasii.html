Lepidium navasii

Lepidium navasii, commonly known as Navas' cress, is a small annual herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress species, and is characterized by its tiny, clustered flowers and pinnately divided leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or disturbed soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. It produces small, round seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize new areas.

Lepidium navasii is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptability.
