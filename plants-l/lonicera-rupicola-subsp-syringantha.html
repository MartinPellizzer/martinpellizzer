Lonicera rupicola subsp. syringantha

Lonicera rupicola subsp.

syringantha, commonly known as the rock honeysuckle, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States. It features slender, woody stems that are often covered in fine, pubescent hairs.

The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy surface.

It is adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a hardy species suitable for xeriscaping.
