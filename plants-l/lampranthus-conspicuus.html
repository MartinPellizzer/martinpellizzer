Lampranthus conspicuus

Lampranthus conspicuus, commonly known as the showy trugan, is a striking member of the Aizoaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with contrasting purple or red markings. The plant typically grows as a succulent shrub, featuring fleshy, hairy leaves that help it conserve water in arid conditions. It blooms during the summer months, producing clusters of showy flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and as a houseplant.
