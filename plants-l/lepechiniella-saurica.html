Lepechiniella saurica

Lepechiniella saurica, commonly known as the hairy lepechiniella, is a small, perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. The plant has small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss. It produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its resilience and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens in suitable climates.
