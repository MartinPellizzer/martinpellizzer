Lupinus wolfianus

Lupinus wolfianus, commonly known as the wolf's lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of blue or purple pea-shaped flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and is typically found in rocky or open habitats. Lupinus wolfianus is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, which benefits surrounding vegetation.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
