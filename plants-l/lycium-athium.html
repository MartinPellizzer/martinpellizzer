Lycium athium

Lycium athium, commonly known as Chinese wolfberry or goji berry, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces red berries that are rich in antioxidants and nutrients, making them popular in traditional medicine and cuisine. Lycium athium thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Its berries are often harvested when fully ripe, typically in the fall, and are used in various culinary and medicinal applications.
