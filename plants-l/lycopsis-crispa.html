Lycopsis crispa

Lycopsis crispa, commonly known as the curly lycopus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, opposite leaves that are often curled or crinkled, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in whorled clusters. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides.

Lycopsis crispa is often used in herbal medicine for its potential calming and sedative properties.
