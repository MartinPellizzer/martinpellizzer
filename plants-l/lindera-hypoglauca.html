Lindera hypoglauca

Lindera hypoglauca, commonly known as Chinese wild laurier, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its small, oval leaves that are typically a dull green on top and lighter underneath. It produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by small, dark berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Lindera hypoglauca is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
