Lindernia dinteri

Lindernia dinteri, commonly known as Dinter's lindernia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Linderniaceae and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Lindernia dinteri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
