Lycopodium nitidum

Lycopodium nitidum, commonly known as the slender clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant features upright, needle-like leaves and produces cone-like structures called strobili that contain spores. Lycopodium nitidum is characterized by its compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small organisms in its native range.
