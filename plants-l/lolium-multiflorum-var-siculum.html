Lolium multiflorum var. siculum

Lolium multiflorum var. siculum, commonly known as Sicilian ryegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with two to three florets per spikelet. Sicilian ryegrass is often used in forage production due to its high nutritional value and adaptability to various soil types.

It is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in moderate climates.
