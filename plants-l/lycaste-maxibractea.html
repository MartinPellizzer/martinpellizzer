Lycaste maxibractea

Lycaste maxibractea, commonly known as the giant spider orchid, is a striking tropical orchid native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter, with vibrant yellow and orange petals. The plant has a pseudobulb structure, which stores water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in humid, tropical environments. It typically grows in shaded, moist areas with well-drained soil.

The species is noted for its unique floral morphology and is a popular subject in botanical studies and horticulture.
