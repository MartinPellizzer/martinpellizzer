Leucopogon obtusatus var. elachophyllus

Leucopogon obtusatus var. elachophyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heaths and heathers, and is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in heathlands and dry, sandy soils, thriving in nutrient-poor environments. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces small, elliptical berries that are an important food source for local wildlife.

This variety is distinguished by its narrow, leathery leaves and is often found in coastal and inland regions of New South Wales and Victoria.
