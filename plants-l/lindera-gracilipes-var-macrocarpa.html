Lindera gracilipes var. macrocarpa

Lindera gracilipes var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited sweetshrub, is a variant of the sweetshrub species native to eastern Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces large, red-orange berries that are an important food source for various bird species. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in suitable climates.
