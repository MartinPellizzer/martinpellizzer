Liparis japonica

Liparis japonica, commonly known as the Japanese marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to East Asia.

It typically grows in wet, marshy environments and has long, slender stems with narrow leaves. The plant produces delicate, greenish-white flowers with a single lip that is often marked with purple or brown streaks. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with moist soil.

Liparis japonica is part of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its adaptability to humid conditions.
