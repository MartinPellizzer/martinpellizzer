Liparis draculoides

Liparis draculoides, commonly known as the dragon's blood orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, red-brown flower spikes that resemble dried blood, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are borne on elongated stems and can last for several weeks.

Liparis draculoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
