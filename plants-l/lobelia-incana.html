Lobelia incana

Lobelia incana, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and clusters of small, sky-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, whitish hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Lobelia incana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
