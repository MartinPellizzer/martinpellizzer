Lastrea setosa

Lastrea setosa, commonly known as the hairy leaf cactus, is a species native to South America.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems and dense covering of fine, white hairs. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its succulent structure. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, appearing in clusters at the ends of the stems.

The hairy leaf cactus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
