Laurus iteophylla

Laurus iteophylla, commonly known as the sweet bay laurel, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible but less commonly consumed. Its leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its distinctive fragrance and therapeutic properties.

Laurus iteophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in various cultures.
