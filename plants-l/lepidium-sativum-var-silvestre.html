Lepidium sativum var. silvestre

Lepidium sativum var. silvestre, commonly known as wild cress, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, edible leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is native to parts of Europe and Asia. It has a short life cycle and is often found in fields, along roadsides, and in woodland edges.

Wild cress is valued for its peppery flavor and is used in salads and as a garnish.
