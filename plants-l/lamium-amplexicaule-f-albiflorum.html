Lamium amplexicaule f. albiflorum

Lamium amplexicaule f. albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered dead nettle, is a flowering plant characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves that clasping the stems.

It produces clusters of small, white, tubular flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is native to Europe and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and gardens. It is a perennial herb that spreads through rhizomes, forming dense mats.

The flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
