Lepidosperma costale

Lepidosperma costale, commonly known as the coastal sedgeland sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to coastal regions of southern Australia.

It typically grows in wetland or saline environments, often found in saltmarshes and estuaries. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine, scale-like structures, giving it its genus name. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are clustered in spikelets, and it produces small, three-lobed fruits.

Lepidosperma costale plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
