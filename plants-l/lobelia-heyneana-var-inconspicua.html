Lobelia heyneana var. inconspicua

Lobelia heyneana var. inconspicua, commonly known as the small lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 cm. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are usually blue or violet in color, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and slightly hairy, contributing to its overall compact appearance.

This variety is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of care.
