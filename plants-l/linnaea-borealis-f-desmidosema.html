Linnaea borealis f. desmidosema

Linnaea borealis f. desmidosema, commonly known as the dwarf twinflower, is a rare and distinctive variant of the twinflower plant.

It is a low-growing shrub native to the northern regions of North America and parts of Europe. The plant features small, white, bilaterally symmetrical flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and often have a waxy coating.

This form is characterized by its reduced size and unique floral structure, making it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
