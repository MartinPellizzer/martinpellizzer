Lobivia ferox f. aureolilacina

Lobivia ferox f. aureolilacina, commonly known as the Aureolilacine Lobivia, is a rare and striking cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, spiny stems that are often covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair. The plant produces vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that range from pale yellow to deep lilac, adding a dramatic contrast to its rugged appearance. Its foliage is typically reduced to tubercles, which help it conserve water in its harsh environment.

This unique cultivar is highly sought after by collectors for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to specific growing conditions.
