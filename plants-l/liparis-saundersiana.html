Liparis saundersiana

Liparis saundersiana, commonly known as the Saunders' orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and produces delicate, greenish-white flowers with a subtle fragrance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the soil.

Liparis saundersiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
