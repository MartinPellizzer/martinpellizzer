Lathyrus annuus var. latifolius

Lathyrus annuus var. latifolius, commonly known as broadleaf sweet pea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are broad and trifoliate, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces tendrils that help it climb and support its growth on structures. Its flowers are typically pink or purple, with a pea-like shape, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
