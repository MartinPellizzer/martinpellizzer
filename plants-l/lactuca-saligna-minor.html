Lactuca saligna minor

Lactuca saligna minor, commonly known as the slender milkweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and alternate leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded areas with well-drained soil.

Lactuca saligna minor is also known for its milky sap, which can be irritating to the skin.
