Linaria elatine var. calvipes

Linaria elatine var. calvipes, commonly known as the smooth yellow toadflax, is a flowering plant native to western North America.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender, branching stems and small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and the plant typically reaches a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as fields and roadsides.

The flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
