Linum lyallanum

Linum lyallanum, commonly known as Lyall's flax, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, delicate blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a soft, hairy texture.

Linum lyallanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by supporting pollinators.
