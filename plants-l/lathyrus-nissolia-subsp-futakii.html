Lathyrus nissolia subsp. futakii

Lathyrus nissolia subsp.

futakii, commonly known as the Japanese vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family. It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in clusters. It is native to Japan and thrives in moist, temperate environments.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural studies.
