Limnophila polyantha subsp. brevipilosa

Limnophila polyantha subsp.

brevipilosa, commonly known as the narrow-stemmed water plantain, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia. It typically grows in shallow, freshwater habitats such as ponds and wetlands.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often submerged or floating, with a smooth surface. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually in spikes, and are pollinated by insects.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in water ecosystems.
