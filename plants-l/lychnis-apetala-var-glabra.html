Lychnis apetala var. glabra

Lychnis apetala var. glabra, commonly known as the naked-stemmed catchfly, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pink or red flowers with five petals, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

This species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
