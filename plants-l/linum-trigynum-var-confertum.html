Linum trigynum var. confertum

Linum trigynum var. confertum, commonly known as the clustered flax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds, which are typically dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
