Lavandula massonii

Lavandula massonii, commonly known as the Cape lavender, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and aromatic, gray-green leaves. The plant produces spikes of purple flowers, which bloom in the spring and summer months. Its botanical features include narrow, linear leaves and showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers with distinct petals and stamens.

Lavandula massonii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
