Lobelia phyteuma

Lobelia phyteuma, commonly known as the purple lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to blue, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and prefers partial to full shade. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Lobelia phyteuma is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
