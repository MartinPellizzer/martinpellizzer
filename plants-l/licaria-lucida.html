Licaria lucida

Licaria lucida, commonly known as the shining licaria, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically pink or white, with five petals and a prominent central stigma. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving them a soft texture.

Licaria lucida is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
