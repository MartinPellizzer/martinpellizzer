Lecanorchis purpurea

Lecanorchis purpurea, commonly known as the purple twayblade, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to various regions of North America.

This terrestrial orchid typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and forest floors. It has a slender, leafless stem with two opposite leaves that are narrow and lance-shaped. The plant produces small, pale purple to white flowers in a dense spike, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

Lecanorchis purpurea is a perennial herb that relies on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake, showcasing a unique symbiotic relationship.
