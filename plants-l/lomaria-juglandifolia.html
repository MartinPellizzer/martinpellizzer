Lomaria juglandifolia

Lomaria juglandifolia, commonly known as the walnut-leaved lomaria, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in dry, rocky soils. The plant has alternate, simple, and often leathery leaves that resemble the shape of a walnut leaf. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually greenish-white and appear in clusters.

The fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that contains several seeds.
