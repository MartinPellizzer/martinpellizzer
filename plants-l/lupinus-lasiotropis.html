Lupinus lasiotropis

Lupinus lasiotropis, commonly known as the woolly lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, erect stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Lupinus lasiotropis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.
