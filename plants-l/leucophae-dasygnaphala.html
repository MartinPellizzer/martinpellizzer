Leucophae dasygnaphala

Leucophae dasygnaphala, commonly known as the narrow-leafed ghost tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped, glossy green leaves that are often covered in fine white hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. It is typically found in dry sclerophyll forests and is adapted to withstand drought and poor soil conditions.

The species is of interest in ecological studies due to its role in supporting local biodiversity.
