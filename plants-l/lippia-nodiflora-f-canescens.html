Lippia nodiflora f. canescens

Lippia nodiflora f. canescens, commonly known as silverleaf lippia, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its grayish-silvery foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its aromatic properties, it is sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a ornamental plant.
