Lycopersicon esculentum var. myrobalaneum

Lycopersicon esculentum var. myrobalaneum, commonly known as the myrobalan tomato, is a botanical variant of the common tomato.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish fruit that matures to a reddish hue, often used in traditional medicine and as a spice. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with hairy stems and lobed leaves. Its flowers are yellow with five petals, and it produces fruits that are smaller and more acidic than standard tomatoes.

This variety is native to South America and has been cultivated for its unique flavor and medicinal properties.
