Laelia × exoniensis

Laelia × exoniensis, commonly known as the Exeter orchid, is a hybrid orchid resulting from the cross between Laelia purpurea and Laelia alba.

It is native to the Andes mountains in South America and is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces large, brightly colored blooms in shades of pink, white, and purple, which appear during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Laelia × exoniensis is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts due to its beauty and relative ease of cultivation.
