Leucopogon florulentus

Leucopogon florulentus, commonly known as the showy leucopogon, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant floral display, which can range from white to pink or red.

Leucopogon florulentus is often used in native plant gardens for its ecological benefits and aesthetic appeal.
