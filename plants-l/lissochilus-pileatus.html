Lissochilus pileatus

Lissochilus pileatus, commonly known as the hairy leaf orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves and small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pale pink. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions, often growing in rocky or forested areas. Its unique morphology and habitat make it a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
