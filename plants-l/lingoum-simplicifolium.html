Lingoum simplicifolium

Lingoum simplicifolium, commonly known as the simple-leafed wonder, is a rare flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It features a compact, bushy growth habit with small, lance-shaped leaves that are densely packed along its stems. The plant produces clusters of delicate, white flowers with a subtle fragrance, which bloom during the late spring and early summer. Its leaves are covered in a fine, silvery waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its arid environment.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is highly sought after by botanists and plant collectors.
