Lepanthes cyrtostele

Lepanthes cyrtostele, commonly known as the golden star orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central and South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in moist, shaded forest understories. The plant features a short, fleshy rhizome and produces delicate, showy flowers with yellow petals and a prominent, golden lip. Its flowers are often fragrant and attract various pollinators, including bees and moths.

Lepanthes cyrtostele is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
