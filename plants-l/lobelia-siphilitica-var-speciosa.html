Lobelia siphilitica var. speciosa

Lobelia siphilitica var. speciosa, commonly known as the Indian blanket flower, is a striking perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, clustered flowers that range from red to orange, with a distinctive yellow center, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
