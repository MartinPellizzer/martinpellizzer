Larix olgensis var. komarovii

Larix olgensis var. komarovii, commonly known as the Korean larch, is a deciduous conifer native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its needle-like leaves that turn yellow before falling in autumn. The plant produces cones that are typically reddish-brown and contain winged seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
