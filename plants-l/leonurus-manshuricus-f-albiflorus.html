Leonurus manshuricus f. albiflorus

Leonurus manshuricus f. albiflorus, commonly known as the white-flowered hedgehog.

This variant of the hedgehog plant is characterized by its pale or white flowers, which contrast with the typical purple or pink hues of the species. It is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Asia, particularly in the Russian Far East and parts of China. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, and it produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in the summer.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique coloration and delicate appearance.
