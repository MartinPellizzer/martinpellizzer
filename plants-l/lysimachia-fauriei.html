Lysimachia fauriei

Lysimachia fauriei, commonly known as the Faurie's yellow loosestrife, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features upright stems covered with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in dense, terminal inflorescences, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Lysimachia fauriei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in wetland restoration projects.
