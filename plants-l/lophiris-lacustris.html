Lophiris lacustris

Lophiris lacustris, commonly known as the lake lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to freshwater lakes and ponds.

It features broad, waxy leaves that float on the water's surface, with long, slender stems that extend downward into the water. The plant produces showy, white or pink flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its roots help stabilize the sediment, preventing erosion in shallow waters.

Lophiris lacustris plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen to aquatic life.
