Linaria caesia subsp. polygalifolia

Linaria caesia subsp.

polygalifolia, commonly known as the clustered toadflax, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The leaves are linear and often arranged in a whorled pattern along the stems. This subspecies is adapted to rocky and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments.

It is often found in dry, open habitats such as coastal cliffs and woodland edges.
