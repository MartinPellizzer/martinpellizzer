Lavandula × spica-latifolia

Lavandula × spica-latifolia, commonly known as Spanish lavender, is a hybrid shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, narrow lance-shaped leaves and spikes of purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is known for its strong fragrance, which is primarily due to its high concentration of essential oils. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal use.

Its botanical characteristics make it both ornamental and valuable in aromatherapy and traditional medicine.
