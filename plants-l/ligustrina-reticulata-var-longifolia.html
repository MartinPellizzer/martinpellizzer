Ligustrina reticulata var. longifolia

Ligustrina reticulata var. longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf ligustrum, is a shrub native to parts of East Asia.

It features small, oval leaves with a reticulate venation pattern, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that are typically followed by dark blue berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.

The species is also valued for its use in traditional medicine in some regions.
