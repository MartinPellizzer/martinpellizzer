Leucococcus vimineus

Leucococcus vimineus, commonly known as the silver vine, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when mature. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and long blooming period.
