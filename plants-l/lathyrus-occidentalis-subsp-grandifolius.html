Lathyrus occidentalis subsp. grandifolius

Lathyrus occidentalis subsp.

grandifolius, commonly known as the western peavine, is a flowering plant native to North America. It is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet being ovate to elliptic in shape.

The plant produces showy, pea-like flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

The subspecies is distinguished by its larger, more prominent leaves compared to other members of the Lathyrus genus.
