Lepigonum medium var. macrocarpum

Lepigonum medium var. macrocarpum, commonly known as the large-fruited honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically hairy on the underside, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are usually pink or red, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its berries are dark red to black, and they persist into winter, providing food for birds.

The plant is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
