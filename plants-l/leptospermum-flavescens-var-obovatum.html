Leptospermum flavescens var. obovatum

Leptospermum flavescens var. obovatum, commonly known as the golden tea tree, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, obovate leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery-white pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting a variety of pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or heathland environments.

This variety is noted for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
