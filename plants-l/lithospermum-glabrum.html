Lithospermum glabrum

Lithospermum glabrum, commonly known as smooth lithosperm, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, whitish bloom. The plant produces showy, violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its roots contain compounds that have been studied for their potential medicinal properties.

Lithospermum glabrum thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.
