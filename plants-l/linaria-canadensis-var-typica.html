Linaria canadensis var. typica

Linaria canadensis var. typica, commonly known as Canadian toadflax, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, disturbed soils such as riverbanks and meadows. The plant features slender stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of yellow-orange, tubular flowers. It is part of the Plantaginaceae family and is often found in wetland habitats.

Due to its attractive flowers and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain regions.
