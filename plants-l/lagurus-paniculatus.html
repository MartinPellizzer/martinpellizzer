Lagurus paniculatus

Lagurus paniculatus, commonly known as the silky oat or velvet grass, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, silky hairs that cover its leaves and inflorescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a paniculate inflorescence, which is a complex, branched cluster. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Lagurus paniculatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
