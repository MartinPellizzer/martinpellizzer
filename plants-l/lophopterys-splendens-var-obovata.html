Lophopterys splendens var. obovata

Lophopterys splendens var. obovata, commonly known as the golden fern, is a striking ornamental plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features large, glossy, obovate-shaped leaves that are typically bright green with a metallic sheen. The plant produces delicate, spore-bearing fronds that contribute to its unique appearance. It thrives in humid, shady environments and requires well-drained, fertile soil.

Due to its aesthetic appeal, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
