Lupinus concinnus subsp. optatus

Lupinus concinnus subsp.

optatus, commonly known as the silver lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its silvery-blue foliage and tall, spindly stems that bear clusters of pea-like flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces legumes as its fruit. Lupinus concinnus subsp.

optatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
