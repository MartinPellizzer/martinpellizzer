Lagotis yesoensis

Lagotis yesoensis, commonly known as the Yeso lily, is a perennial herb native to the Korean Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Liliaceae and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are erect and bear the showy inflorescence.

The Yeso lily is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
