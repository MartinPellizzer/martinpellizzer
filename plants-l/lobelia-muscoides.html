Lobelia muscoides

Lobelia muscoides, commonly known as the musk lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial with slender, hairy stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and wet meadows. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Lobelia muscoides is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
