Lonicera strophiophora var. glabra

Lonicera strophiophora var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are smooth and glossy, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that ripens in the fall.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
