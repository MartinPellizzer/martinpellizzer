Lycopodium × sulcinervium

Lycopodium × sulcinervium, commonly known as the velvet fern, is a hybrid fern that exhibits unique botanical features.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and its ability to thrive in arid environments. The plant's fronds are typically small, delicate, and covered in a fine, velvety coating of spores. It belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae and is native to southern Africa.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in rocky or sandy soils in regions with mild climates.
