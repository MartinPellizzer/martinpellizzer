Leucosceptrum sinense

Leucosceptrum sinense, commonly known as the Chinese snowbell, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its elegant appearance and seasonal interest.
