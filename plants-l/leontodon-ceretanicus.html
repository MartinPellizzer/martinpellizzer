Leontodon ceretanicus

Leontodon ceretanicus, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves and produces bell-shaped, violet or blue flowers during the summer months. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Leontodon ceretanicus is characterized by its hardy nature and ability to withstand cold climates.
