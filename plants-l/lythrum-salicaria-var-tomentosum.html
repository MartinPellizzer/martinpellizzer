Lythrum salicaria var. tomentosum

Lythrum salicaria var. tomentosum, commonly known as woolly loosestrife, is a variant of the common loosestrife plant.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and tall spikes of pink to purple flowers. This perennial herb is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been introduced to various regions around the world. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
