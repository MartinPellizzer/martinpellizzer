Loranthus lichtensteinii

Loranthus lichtensteinii, commonly known as the Lichtenstein's mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily in the African savanna.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using the light it captures. The plant has small, scale-like leaves and produces white or yellowish flowers that attract insects for pollination. Its berries are typically red or orange, containing seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by influencing host tree growth and providing food for various wildlife.
