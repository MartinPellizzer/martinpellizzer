Lophomyrtus bullata

Lophomyrtus bullata, commonly known as the silver mistflower, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its small, white, bell-shaped flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often covered in a fine, silvery coating.

Lophomyrtus bullata is typically found in coastal and lowland areas, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.
