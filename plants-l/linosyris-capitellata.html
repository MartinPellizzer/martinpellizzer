Linosyris capitellata

Linosyris capitellata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sandwort, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically forms dense mats of foliage. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in compact, spherical clusters called capitula. Its leaves are narrow, fleshy, and adapted to conserve water, reflecting its xerophytic nature.

Linosyris capitellata is often found in sandy or gravelly soils, thriving in environments with limited moisture.
