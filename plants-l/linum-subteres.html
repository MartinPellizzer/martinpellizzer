Linum subteres

Linum subteres, commonly known as slender flax, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Linum subteres is often used in traditional medicine and has potential applications in modern herbal remedies.
