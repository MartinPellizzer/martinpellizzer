Linanthus dianthiflorus subsp. farinosus

Linanthus dianthiflorus subsp.

farinosus, commonly known as the farina linanthus, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, white to pale pink flowers.

The plant produces a distinctive powdery coating on its leaves and stems, which is composed of fine, white hairs. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open habitats.

This subspecies is part of the evening primrose family (Onagraceae) and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
