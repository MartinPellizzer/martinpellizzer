Lotus douglasii var. nevadensis

Lotus douglasii var. nevadensis, commonly known as the Nevada lotus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces small, edible seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and conservation efforts.
