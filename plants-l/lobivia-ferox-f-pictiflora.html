Lobivia ferox f. pictiflora

Lobivia ferox f. pictiflora, commonly known as the Painted Flower Lobivia, is a striking succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, cylindrical stems that are often tinged with shades of green and blue. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of orange and red, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are typically small and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native environment.

This fascinating plant is prized by collectors for its unique appearance and resilience in harsh conditions.
