Lycopus lucidus subsp. americanus

Lycopus lucidus subsp.

americanus, commonly known as American bugleweed, is a flowering plant native to North America. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces and produces small, blue to purple flowers in clusters. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional herbal medicine.

It is also valued in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
