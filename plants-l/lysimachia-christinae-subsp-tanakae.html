Lysimachia christinae subsp. tanakae

Lysimachia christinae subsp.

tanakae, commonly known as Japanese hillbilly weed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to East Asia. It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a creeping growth habit. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it adaptable to various soil types, though it prefers well-drained conditions.
