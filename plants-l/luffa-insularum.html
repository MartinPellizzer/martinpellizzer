Luffa insularum

Luffa insularum, commonly known as the island luffa, is a tropical climbing vine belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and long, cylindrical fruits that mature to a fibrous, sponge-like texture. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires full sun for optimal growth. Its fruits are typically harvested when they are fully mature, around 80 to 100 days after planting.

The fibrous interior of the fruit is used as a natural sponge for personal care or household purposes.
