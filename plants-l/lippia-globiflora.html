Lippia globiflora

Lippia globiflora, commonly known as globeflower, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that resemble tiny globes. The plant produces aromatic, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Lippia globiflora is also valued for its essential oils, which are used in aromatherapy and natural remedies.
