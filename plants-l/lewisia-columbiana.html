Lewisia columbiana

Lewisia columbiana, commonly known as the Sierra snowplant, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, gray-green leaves. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Due to its striking appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
