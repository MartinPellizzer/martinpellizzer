Lilium verticillatum

Lilium verticillatum, commonly known as the clustered lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking white or pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a whorl at the base of the stem.

The clustered lily is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
