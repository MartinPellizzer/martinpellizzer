Lasiosiphon polyanthus var. microcalyx

Lasiosiphon polyanthus var. microcalyx, commonly known as the small-flowered bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to New Zealand.

It features slender, branching stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue and purple. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in a fine, silky hair, contributing to its distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

It is valued for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
