Lacinaria spicata var. montana

Lacinaria spicata var. montana, commonly known as the mountain lacinaria, is a perennial flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh habitat.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with similar environmental conditions.
