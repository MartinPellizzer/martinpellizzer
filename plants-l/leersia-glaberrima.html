Leersia glaberrima

Leersia glaberrima, commonly known as broadleaf arrowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairless. Its small, spike-like inflorescence produces numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Leersia glaberrima plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
