Lepturus hirtulus

Lepturus hirtulus, commonly known as the hairy leek, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, cylindrical stems covered in fine, hairy structures. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slightly rough texture.

Lepturus hirtulus is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
