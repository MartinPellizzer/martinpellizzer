Lipozygis erubescens var. microphylla

Lipozygis erubescens var. microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved red lipozygis, is a shrub native to the eastern regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces clusters of bright red, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring, adding a striking visual contrast to its foliage. Its leaves are often covered in tiny, hair-like structures that help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance and hardiness.
