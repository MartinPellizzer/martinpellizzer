Leucas mollissima subsp. scaberula

Leucas mollissima subsp.

scaberula, commonly known as rough leucas, is a perennial herb native to parts of southern Africa. It typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils.

The plant features hairy, glandular leaves that are often rough to the touch, giving it its common name. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pink or purple, blooming in the spring.

It is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
