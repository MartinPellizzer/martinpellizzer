Linaria tarhunensis

Linaria tarhunensis, commonly known as the Tarhun's toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey.

It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of bright yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptation to arid and harsh environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are tubular with two lips, typical of the genus Linaria.

Linaria tarhunensis is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
