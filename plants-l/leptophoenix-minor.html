Leptophoenix minor

Leptophoenix minor, commonly known as the dwarf fan palm, is a small, slow-growing palm native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact size, typically reaching only about 2 meters in height, and its distinctive fan-shaped leaves that are light green and slightly serrated. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into red fruits, which are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Leptophoenix minor thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in coastal areas or near water sources.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in tropical and subtropical climates.
