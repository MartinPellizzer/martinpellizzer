Lepidium cartilagineum subsp. caespitosum

Lepidium cartilagineum subsp.

caespitosum, commonly known as the cushioned peppergrass, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a low-growing perennial that forms dense, mat-like growths, often covering rocky or gravelly soils.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typical of the Brassicaceae family. Its leaves are usually small, linear, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is adapted to harsh environmental conditions, making it a common sight in alpine and arid habitats.
