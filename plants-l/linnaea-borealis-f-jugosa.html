Linnaea borealis f. jugosa

Linnaea borealis f. jugosa, commonly known as the fatlinnet or the bearberry.

This variant of the common twinflower is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to northern regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It features small, dark green leaves and produces clusters of white, fragrant flowers in early spring. The plant is characterized by its distinctive two-lipped flowers and is often found in shaded, moist forest floors.

It is a member of the family Ericaceae and is adapted to grow in acidic, nutrient-poor soils.
