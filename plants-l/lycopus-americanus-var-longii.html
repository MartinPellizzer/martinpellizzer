Lycopus americanus var. longii

Lycopus americanus var. longii, commonly known as American blueweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist habitats such as wet meadows, stream banks, and woodlands. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive blue-green hue and small, clustered blue flowers. Its flowering period occurs in late summer, and it produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
