Leptospermum madidum subsp. madidum

Leptospermum madidum subsp.

madidum, commonly known as the coastal tea tree, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its small, needle-like leaves and produces yellow flowers in late spring to early summer.

The plant is typically found growing in coastal heathlands and sandy soils. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it well adapted to coastal environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses by Indigenous communities.
