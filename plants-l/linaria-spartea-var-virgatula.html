Linaria spartea var. virgatula

Linaria spartea var. virgatula, commonly known as the slender toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slightly hairy texture. This variety is typically found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to thrive in sunny, well-drained environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and drought tolerance.
