Linum intercursum

Linum intercursum, commonly known as the common flax, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and linear, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Linum intercursum is a member of the Linaceae family and is known for its fibrous seeds, which are used in the production of flax fiber.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
