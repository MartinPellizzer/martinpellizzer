Lepanthopsis hirtzii

Lepanthopsis hirtzii, commonly known as the hairy lepanthopsis, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant features elongated, linear leaves that are covered in fine, hairy pubescence, giving it its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks in the understory of rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
