Lepidium montanum subsp. papilliferum

Lepidium montanum subsp.

papilliferum, commonly known as the rough wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard plants.

The plant is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and hairy, lobed leaves. It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its subspecies name, *papilliferum*, refers to the papillate (bumpy) structures on its leaves, a distinctive botanical feature.
