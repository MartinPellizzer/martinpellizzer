Lysimachia baoxingensis

Lysimachia baoxingensis, commonly known as Baoxing knotweed, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its creeping growth habit and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically features heart-shaped leaves and produces yellow or white flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
