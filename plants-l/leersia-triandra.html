Leersia triandra

Leersia triandra, commonly known as the white leersia, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist to aquatic environments. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are usually narrow and pointed, with a smooth texture. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that bear small, white to pale yellow flowers, which are often clustered together.

Leersia triandra plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
