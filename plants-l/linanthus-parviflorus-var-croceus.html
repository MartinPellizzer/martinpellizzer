Linanthus parviflorus var. croceus

Linanthus parviflorus var. croceus, commonly known as the yellow globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, yellow, globular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grassland and desert environments. It has hairy stems and leaves that are narrow and lance-shaped.
