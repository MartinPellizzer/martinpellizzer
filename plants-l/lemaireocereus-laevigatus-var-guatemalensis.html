Lemaireocereus laevigatus var. guatemalensis

Lemaireocereus laevigatus var. guatemalensis, commonly known as the Guatemalan star cactus, is a species of cactus native to Central America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are typically covered in white or pale blue spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the rainy season, which are often followed by red or orange fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure.

This variety is appreciated for its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
