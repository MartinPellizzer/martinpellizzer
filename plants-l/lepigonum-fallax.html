Lepigonum fallax

Lepigonum fallax, commonly known as the redstem filaree, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, bright red flowers clustered in dense, fluffy heads. The plant has slender, branching stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Lepigonum fallax is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
