Lepidium halleri

Lepidium halleri, commonly known as hoary cress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a frosty appearance.

Lepidium halleri is also known for its ability to tolerate harsh climatic conditions and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
