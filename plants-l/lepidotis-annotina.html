Lepidotis annotina

Lepidotis annotina, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and showy, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of purple, pink, and white. The plant typically grows as a small shrub or herb, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are arranged in a rosette pattern, and the flowers are borne on short stalks rising from the center of the plant.

Lepidotis annotina is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
