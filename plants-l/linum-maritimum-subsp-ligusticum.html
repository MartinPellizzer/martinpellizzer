Linum maritimum subsp. ligusticum

Linum maritimum subsp.

ligusticum, commonly known as the Ligurian flax, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves.

The plant produces showy, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in coastal areas and sandy soils, often found along cliffs and dunes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in fiber production.
