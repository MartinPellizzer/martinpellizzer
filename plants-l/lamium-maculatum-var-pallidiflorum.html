Lamium maculatum var. pallidiflorum

Lamium maculatum var. pallidiflorum, commonly known as the pale-flowered dead nettle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive silver or white mottling on the upper surface. The plant produces clusters of small, pale pink or white flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking shade-tolerant plants.
