Lens cyanea

Lens cyanea, commonly known as the blue bean, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive blue or violet flowers. The plant produces small, bean-like pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

The botanical features of Lens cyanea make it both ornamental and agriculturally significant in its native habitat.
