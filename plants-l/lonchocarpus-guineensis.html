Lonchocarpus guineensis

Lonchocarpus guineensis, commonly known as African senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow to orange flowers. Its root system is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The seeds are enclosed in a hard, woody pod that splits open when mature.
