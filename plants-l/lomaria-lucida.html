Lomaria lucida

Lomaria lucida, commonly known as the brightleaf lomaria, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its arid habitat. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to anchor the plant in rocky soils.

Lomaria lucida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and seasonal blooms.
