Lysimachia scandens

Lysimachia scandens, commonly known as creeping Jenny, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North America.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer, and its foliage is typically bright yellow or variegated. The plant has prostrate or ascending stems that spread along the ground, often forming dense mats. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or near streams.

Lysimachia scandens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wet conditions.
