Limonium commune var. minus

Limonium commune var. minus, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has silver-gray foliage with a fuzzy texture, which helps it retain moisture in saline environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dunes and rocky shores.

Limonium commune var. minus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize coastal soils.
