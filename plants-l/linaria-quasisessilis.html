Linaria quasisessilis

Linaria quasisessilis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in grassland habitats. Its leaves are narrow and sessile, giving it a distinctive appearance.
