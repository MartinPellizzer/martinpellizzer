Lapsana macrocarpa

Lapsana macrocarpa, commonly known as greater bur marigold, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and distinctive, prickly seed heads that resemble small burrs. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils along waterways and fields. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.

Lapsana macrocarpa is also used in phytoremediation due to its ability to absorb heavy metals from the soil.
