Lissochilus buettneri

Lissochilus buettneri, commonly known as the Andean orchid, is a species native to the high altitudes of the Andes Mountains.

It is a small, herbaceous plant with delicate, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with multiple stems arising from a rhizome. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapted to the cool, humid conditions of its mountainous habitat.

Lissochilus buettneri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in specialized orchid collections.
