Lotus hispidus var. villosus

Lotus hispidus var. villosus, commonly known as the hairy lotus, is a perennial aquatic plant native to parts of Asia.

It typically grows in shallow water and is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves and showy pink or red flowers. The plant has a robust root system that helps stabilize sediment in wetland environments. Its flowers bloom from June to September, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The hairy lotus is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits in aquatic habitats.
