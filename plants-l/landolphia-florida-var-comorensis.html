Landolphia florida var. comorensis

Landolphia florida var. comorensis, commonly known as the Comorian flame vine, is a climbing plant native to the Comoros Islands.

It is characterized by its striking, showy orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and partial shade.

Its rapid growth habit makes it suitable for ornamental use in gardens and as a climbing plant in greenhouses.
