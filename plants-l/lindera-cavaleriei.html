Lindera cavaleriei

Lindera cavaleriei, commonly known as Chinese wild laurustree, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, followed by berries that are dark purple when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
