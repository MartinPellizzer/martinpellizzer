Lycopodioides bakeriana

Lycopodioides bakeriana, commonly known as the Baker's clubmoss, is a small, evergreen fern-like plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments. The plant produces tiny, spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are clustered in strobila, a distinctive feature of clubmosses. Lycopodioides bakeriana is often found in rocky soils and forest understories, where it contributes to soil stability and moisture retention.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important component of certain temperate ecosystems.
