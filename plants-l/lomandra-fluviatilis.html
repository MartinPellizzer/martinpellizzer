Lomandra fluviatilis

Lomandra fluviatilis, commonly known as the river lomandra, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southeastern Australia.

It features dense, sword-shaped leaves that are often bluish-green in color, arranged in a rosette formation. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers in late summer, which are clustered in spikelets. It is well adapted to wetland and riparian environments, thriving in moist soils.

Lomandra fluviatilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing riverbanks.
