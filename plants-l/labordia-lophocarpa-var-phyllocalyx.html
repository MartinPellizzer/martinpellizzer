Labordia lophocarpa var. phyllocalyx

Labordia lophocarpa var. phyllocalyx, commonly known as the broad-leaf labordia, is a variant of the Labordia lophocarpa species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves that are often glossy and dark green in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

This variant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens for its unique foliage.
