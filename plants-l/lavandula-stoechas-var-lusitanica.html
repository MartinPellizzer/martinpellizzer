Lavandula stoechas var. lusitanica

Lavandula stoechas var. lusitanica, commonly known as the Portuguese lavender, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to southern Europe.

It features narrow, gray-green leaves and spikes of purple flowers with distinctive, fuzzy bracts. This variety is known for its strong, spicy fragrance, which is characteristic of the lavender genus. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

The plant is also valued for its use in aromatherapy and as a medicinal herb.
