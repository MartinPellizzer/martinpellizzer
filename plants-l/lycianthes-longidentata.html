Lycianthes longidentata

Lycianthes longidentata, commonly known as the long-toothed bitterleaf, is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades.

It is native to Central and South America and is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves with prominent, elongated teeth along the margins. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red berries containing several seeds. It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its distinctive foliage and colorful fruits.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
