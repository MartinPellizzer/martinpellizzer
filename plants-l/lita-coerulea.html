Lita coerulea

Lita coerulea, commonly known as the blue lita, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white hair. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Lita coerulea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
