Leucanthemum praecox var. alpicola

Leucanthemum praecox var. alpicola, commonly known as the alpine oxeye, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its white ray florets surrounding a central yellow disc, forming daisy-like flowers. This perennial herb typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are basal, lobed, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

The plant is well adapted to high-altitude conditions, demonstrating resilience to harsh climatic conditions.
