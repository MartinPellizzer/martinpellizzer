Limnanthes alba subsp. parishii

Limnanthes alba subsp.

parishii, commonly known as the white marsh plant, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its delicate, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in wet meadows and marshy areas, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its subspecies status highlights subtle differences in morphology compared to the broader species.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
