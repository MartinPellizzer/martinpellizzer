Lupinus digitatus

Lupinus digitatus, commonly known as the digitate lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are compound, with small, finger-like leaflets, giving it its common name.

Lupinus digitatus is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in ecological restoration projects.
