Ligusticum rhizomaticum

Ligusticum rhizomaticum, commonly known as Chinese lovage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and small yellowish-green flowers that appear in umbels. The plant produces a rhizomatous root system, which is often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves and roots contain volatile oils, including ligustilide, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Ligusticum rhizomaticum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shady areas.
