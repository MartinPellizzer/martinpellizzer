Lobivia amblayensis

Lobivia amblayensis, commonly known as the Amblay's lobivia, is a species of cactus native to the Andes Mountains in South America.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded body and spiny, fleshy stems that store water, typical of desert plants. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss and protecting it from herbivores.

Lobivia amblayensis is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it a resilient choice for rock gardens or xeriscaping.
