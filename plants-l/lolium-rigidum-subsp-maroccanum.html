Lolium rigidum subsp. maroccanum

Lolium rigidum subsp.

maroccanum, commonly known as Moroccan rigid ryegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with prominent veins.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous pale green to brownish florets. It is adapted to dry, disturbed soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical features make it a valuable forage plant in certain agricultural settings.
