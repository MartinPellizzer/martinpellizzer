Lysimachia mellico

Lysimachia mellico, commonly known as yellow loosestrife, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and thrives in partial to full shade. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Lysimachia mellico is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
