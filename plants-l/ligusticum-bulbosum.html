Ligusticum bulbosum

Ligusticum bulbosum, commonly known as the bulbous lovage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate flowers. The plant features a thick, fleshy rhizome and leaves that are deeply lobed and have a strong, spicy scent. Its yellow flowers appear in late summer and are clustered in umbrella-like inflorescences.

Ligusticum bulbosum is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
