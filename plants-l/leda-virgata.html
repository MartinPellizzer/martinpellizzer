Leda virgata

Leda virgata, commonly known as the mountain holly or false holly, is a flowering plant native to the high elevations of eastern Asia.

It is a member of the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas, preferring cool and moist conditions. Its leaves are dark green and glossy, while the flowers are usually white or pale pink, adding ornamental value to its natural habitat.

Leda virgata is often used in horticulture for its hardiness and aesthetic appeal in shaded gardens.
