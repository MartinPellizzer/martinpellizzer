Loranthus pauciflorus

Loranthus pauciflorus, commonly known as the sparse-flowered mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to host trees through its haustoria, drawing water and nutrients from the host.

It is native to parts of Africa, particularly in countries like Kenya and Tanzania, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-yellow in color, and its fruit is a small, fleshy capsule that contains seeds. Loranthus pauciflorus is often found growing in shaded, humid environments, typically on the branches of acacia or other woody plants.

Its botanical significance lies in its parasitic nature and its role in the ecological dynamics of its native habitats.
