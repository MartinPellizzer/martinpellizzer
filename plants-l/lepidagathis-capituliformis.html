Lepidagathis capituliformis

Lepidagathis capituliformis, commonly known as the Cape jasmine, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in dense, rounded clusters at the ends of its branches. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a slightly rough texture.

It is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils and saline conditions.
