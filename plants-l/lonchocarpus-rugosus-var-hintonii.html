Lonchocarpus rugosus var. hintonii

Lonchocarpus rugosus var. hintonii, commonly known as Hinton's senna, is a tropical shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features palmate leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically yellow or orange, attracting pollinators. It produces distinctive, woody pods that contain several seeds, and it is often found in moist, tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
