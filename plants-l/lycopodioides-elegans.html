Lycopodioides elegans

Lycopodioides elegans, commonly known as the slender clubmoss, is a member of the Lycopodiaceae family and is characterized by its small, needle-like leaves.

It is a primitive vascular plant that exhibits a unique reproductive structure called a strobilus, which contains the sporangia. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizome is short and often produces a dense carpet of foliage.

Lycopodioides elegans plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
