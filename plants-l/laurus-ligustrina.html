Laurus ligustrina

Laurus ligustrina, commonly known as sweet bay, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to the Mediterranean region. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small white flowers that appear in clusters. Its fruit is a dark purple drupe, and the plant is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves used in cooking and traditional medicine.

Laurus ligustrina is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
