Linaria texana

Linaria texana, commonly known as Texas toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. The plant produces bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the spring and summer months. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and it has a branching stem that supports its showy inflorescence.

Linaria texana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant colors.
