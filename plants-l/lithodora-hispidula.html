Lithodora hispidula

Lithodora hispidula, commonly known as the hairy bluebell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, sky-blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, hairy, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Lithodora hispidula is also valued for its aromatic foliage and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
