Leea macrophylla var. oxyphylla

Leea macrophylla var. oxyphylla, commonly known as the large-leaved Leea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, ovate, glossy green leaves that can grow up to 20 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in umbels, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental leaves and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
