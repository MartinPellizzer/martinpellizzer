Loudetia flavida

Loudetia flavida, commonly known as the yellow marsh grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and along the edges of water bodies. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters. It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, which allows it to spread vegetatively.

Loudetia flavida is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and improve water quality.
