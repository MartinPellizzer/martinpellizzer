Lotus coccineus

Lotus coccineus, commonly known as the crimson lotus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family and is characterized by its striking red or pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has palmately compound leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

The lotus coccineus is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
