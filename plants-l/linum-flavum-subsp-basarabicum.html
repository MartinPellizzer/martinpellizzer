Linum flavum subsp. basarabicum

Linum flavum subsp.

basarabicum, commonly known as the Caucasian flax, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lanceolate leaves that are typically hairy.

The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in steppe and forest-steppe habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and historical significance in flax cultivation, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
