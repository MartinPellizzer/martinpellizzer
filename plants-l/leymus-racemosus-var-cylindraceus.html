Leymus racemosus var. cylindraceus

Leymus racemosus var. cylindraceus, commonly known as the cylindrical wild rye, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and cylindrical, spike-like inflorescences that are often reddish in color. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
