Laportea diffusa

Laportea diffusa, commonly known as the broadleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features upright stems that are hairy and can grow up to 2 meters tall. The plant produces large, showy purple to pink flower heads that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a distinctive texture.

Laportea diffusa is often found in moist woodlands and along stream banks, where it thrives in partial to full shade.
