Lippia sclerophylla var. subintegra

Lippia sclerophylla var. subintegra, commonly known as the narrow-leaf lippia, is a perennial shrub native to the southern United States.

It features aromatic, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or blue in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for xeriscaping.

Its aromatic properties make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and medicinal gardens.
