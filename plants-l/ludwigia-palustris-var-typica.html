Ludwigia palustris var. typica

Ludwigia palustris var. typica, commonly known as the swamp Ludwigia, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in the Americas.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its rapid growth and ability to thrive in nutrient-rich waters make it a common sight in aquatic habitats.

It is also sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant in water gardens.
