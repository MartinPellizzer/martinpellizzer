Lasiopetalum parviflorum var. occidentale

Lasiopetalum parviflorum var. occidentale, commonly known as the Western Grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features narrow, needle-like leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
