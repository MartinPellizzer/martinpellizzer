Lycium argentinoelongatum

Lycium argentinoelongatum, commonly known as the Argentine elongated boxthorn, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible but have a bitter taste. Its leaves are usually elliptical and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Lycium argentinoelongatum is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand arid conditions.
