Lessingia germanorum var. glandulifera

Lessingia germanorum var. glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular lessingia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender stems and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually covered in fine, glandular hairs, which contribute to its common name.

This variant is part of the Caryophyllaceae family, known for its showy, often colorful flowers.
