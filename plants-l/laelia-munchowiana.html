Laelia munchowiana

Laelia munchowiana, commonly known as the Munchow orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or mountainous terrain. The plant features a pseudobulb that supports its foliage and flowering structure, with lance-shaped leaves that are dark green in color. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with a distinctive fragrance, and bloom during the cooler months.

Laelia munchowiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
