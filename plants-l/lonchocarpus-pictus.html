Lonchocarpus pictus

Lonchocarpus pictus, commonly known as the painted legume, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, often colorful pods. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated, woody pods that split open when mature.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and floral display.
