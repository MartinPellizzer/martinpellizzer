Lachnastoma viridiflorum

Lachnastoma viridiflorum, commonly known as the green-flowered lichen, is a unique member of the Lecanoromycetes class.

It is characterized by its foliose thallus, which is typically greenish in color due to the presence of chlorophyll. The plant forms a symbiotic relationship with algae, which contributes to its photosynthetic capabilities. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found on bark or soil surfaces.

Its morphology and ecological niche make it an important indicator species for certain forest ecosystems.
