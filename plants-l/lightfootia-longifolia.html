Lightfootia longifolia

Lightfootia longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved lightfoot, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stem and elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Lightfootia longifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
