Lauro-cerasus martabanica

Lauro-cerasus martabanica, commonly known as the Martaban cherry laurel, is a species of flowering plant in the Prunus genus.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, particularly Myanmar and Thailand. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a small, cherry-like drupe that turns red when mature.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in landscaping due to its hardiness and adaptability.
