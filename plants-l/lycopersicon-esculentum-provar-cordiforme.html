Lycopersicon esculentum provar. cordiforme

Lycopersicon esculentum provar. cordiforme, commonly known as the heart-shaped tomato, is a botanical variant of the domesticated tomato.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped fruit, which differs from the typical round or oval forms of standard tomatoes. The plant belongs to the Solanaceae family, sharing genetic relationships with other nightshade plants. It exhibits typical tomato growth habits, including herbaceous stems and compound leaves.

This variety is primarily cultivated for its unique fruit shape and is often used in ornamental gardening.
