Lupinus francis-whittieri

Lupinus francis-whittieri, commonly known as the Francis Whittier lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to violet pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with thread-like leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Lupinus francis-whittieri plays a role in nitrogen fixation, benefiting the surrounding ecosystem.
