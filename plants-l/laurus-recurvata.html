Laurus recurvata

Laurus recurvata, commonly known as the Turkish laurel or winter laurel, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense, dark green, oval-shaped leaves that are often aromatic and used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by black, egg-shaped berries that are edible but not commonly consumed. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in various parts of the world.
