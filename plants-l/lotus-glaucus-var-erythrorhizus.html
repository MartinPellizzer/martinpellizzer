Lotus glaucus var. erythrorhizus

Lotus glaucus var. erythrorhizus, commonly known as the red-rooted lotus, is a perennial aquatic plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking pink to purple flowers and distinctive red roots that give it its common name. The plant thrives in shallow water and wetlands, where it forms dense colonies. Its leaves are typically rounded and floating, while its stems are often submerged.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been cultivated for both decorative and medicinal purposes.
