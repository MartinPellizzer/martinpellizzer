Lysimachia scopulensis

Lysimachia scopulensis, commonly known as the rock yellowtop, is a perennial herb native to rocky and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as limestone soils and rocky outcrops. The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are clustered in terminal spikes, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

Lysimachia scopulensis is known for its resilience to harsh environmental conditions and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
