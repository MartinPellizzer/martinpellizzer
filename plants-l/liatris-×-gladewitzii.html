Liatris × gladewitzii

Liatris × gladewitzii, commonly known as the Gladewitz blazing star, is a hybrid perennial grassland plant native to the central United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, spiky flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in prairies and open woodlands. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Liatris × gladewitzii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
