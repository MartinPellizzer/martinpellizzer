Lepanthes bustyla

Lepanthes bustyla, commonly known as the violet orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically range in color from white to pale purple. The plant grows as an epiphyte, often found on trees in humid tropical forests. Its leaves are usually clustered at the base and are broad and fleshy to store moisture.

Lepanthes bustyla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
