Lepisanthes assamica

Lepisanthes assamica, commonly known as the Assam orchid, is a herbaceous plant native to the northeastern region of India.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils.

Lepisanthes assamica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
