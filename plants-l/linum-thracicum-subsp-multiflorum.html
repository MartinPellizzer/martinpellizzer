Linum thracicum subsp. multiflorum

Linum thracicum subsp.

multiflorum, commonly known as the Thrace flax, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Europe. It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in textile production.
