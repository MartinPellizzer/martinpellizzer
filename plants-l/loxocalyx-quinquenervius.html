Loxocalyx quinquenervius

Loxocalyx quinquenervius, commonly known as the five-veined loxocalyx, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have five distinct veins, giving it its scientific name.

It prefers well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.
