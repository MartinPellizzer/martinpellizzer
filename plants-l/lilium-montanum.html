Lilium montanum

Lilium montanum, commonly known as the mountain lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a basal rosette, and it produces bulbous underground stems called bulbs.

Lilium montanum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
