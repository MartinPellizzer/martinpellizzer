Laurus ludoviciana

Laurus ludoviciana, commonly known as California laurel, is a deciduous evergreen tree native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and aromatic properties. The plant has a smooth, grey-bark trunk and can grow up to 30 meters tall in its natural habitat. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, followed by dark blue, berry-like fruits that are edible but not widely consumed by humans.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
