Lipochaeta succulenta var. angustata

Lipochaeta succulenta var. angustata, commonly known as narrowleaf lipochaeta, is a succulent plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are narrow and often reduced, minimizing water loss through transpiration.

This species is well adapted to harsh environments and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
