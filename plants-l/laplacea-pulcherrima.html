Laplacea pulcherrima

Laplacea pulcherrima, commonly known as the golden flame orchid, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bright orange and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. The plant has strap-like, dark green leaves that are arranged in a fan shape, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its vibrant colors and unique appearance, it is a popular choice for ornamental gardening and indoor cultivation.
