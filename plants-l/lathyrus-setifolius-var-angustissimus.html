Lathyrus setifolius var. angustissimus

Lathyrus setifolius var. angustissimus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved grass vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, angular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as fields and rocky slopes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
