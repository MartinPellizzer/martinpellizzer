Leandra vesiculosa

Leandra vesiculosa, commonly known as the bubbleleaf orchid, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its unique, inflated, bubble-like leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
