Lythrum anatolicum subsp. anatolicum

Lythrum anatolicum subsp.

anatolicum, commonly known as Anatolian loosestrife, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny environments.

The plant features slender, erect stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height. Its flowers are pink to purple, arranged in dense spikes, and bloom from late spring to early summer.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
