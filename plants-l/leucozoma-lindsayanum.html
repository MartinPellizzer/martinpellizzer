Leucozoma lindsayanum

Leucozoma lindsayanum, commonly known as the Lind's ghost orchid, is a rare and elusive orchid species native to the high-altitude cloud forests of Central America.

It is characterized by its delicate, pale greenish-white flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions, hence its common name. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without deriving nutrients from them. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, with a unique structure that aids in pollination by specific insect species.

Due to habitat destruction and limited distribution, Leucozoma lindsayanum is considered endangered and is of great interest to botanists and conservationists.
