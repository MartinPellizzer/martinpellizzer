Lolium gaudinii

Lolium gaudinii, commonly known as the Gaudin's ryegrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted leaves and dense, compact growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate climates and is often found in grasslands and along stream banks. Its flowering stems bear spikelets that are usually pale green to reddish in color.

Lolium gaudinii is valued for its forage quality and has been introduced to various regions for agricultural purposes.
