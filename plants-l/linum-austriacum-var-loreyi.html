Linum austriacum var. loreyi

Linum austriacum var. loreyi, commonly known as the Austrian flax or Lorey's flax, is a flowering plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its fibrous seeds are also used for ornamental purposes in gardens.
