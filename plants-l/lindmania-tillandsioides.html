Lindmania tillandsioides

Lindmania tillandsioides, commonly known as the bromeliad, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a water-holding structure. The plant produces vibrant, showy inflorescences that attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. Its leaves are typically covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help in water absorption and reduce transpiration.

Lindmania tillandsioides is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and air-purifying qualities.
