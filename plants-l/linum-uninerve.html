Linum uninerve

Linum uninerve, commonly known as the narrow-veined flax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves with a single central vein. The plant produces small, blue or white flowers that bloom in late spring. Linum uninerve is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

It is related to other members of the Linaceae family, which includes other flax species.
