Laxmannia pauciflora

Laxmannia pauciflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved laxmannia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, branched stems covered in dense, woolly hair, which helps protect it from harsh environmental conditions. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a bluish-gray hue due to a dense covering of fine hairs.

Laxmannia pauciflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and delicate floral display.
