Lophostemon lactifluus

Lophostemon lactifluus, commonly known as the silky gum, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to eastern Australia and is characterized by its smooth, light-colored bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by woody capsules containing seeds. It is often found in open forests and woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils.

The silky gum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine by Indigenous Australians.
