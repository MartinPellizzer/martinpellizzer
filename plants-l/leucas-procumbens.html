Leucas procumbens

Leucas procumbens, commonly known as dwarf heliotrope, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in spring and summer. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are often glandular, giving it a distinct texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it a valued addition to both cultivated and natural landscapes.
