Lupinus edward-palmeri

Lupinus edward-palmeri, commonly known as Edward Palmer's lupine, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the southwestern United States, particularly found in Arizona and New Mexico. This plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant produces tall spikes of blue to purple flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its growth habit includes erect stems and compound leaves, typical of many lupine species.
