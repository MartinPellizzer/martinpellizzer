Linnaea pyrrosema

Linnaea pyrrosema, commonly known as the red-flowered twinflower, is a small evergreen shrub native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces delicate, red or pinkish flowers in pairs, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Linnaea pyrrosema is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
