Liparis anthericoides

Liparis anthericoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf lady's tresses, is a terrestrial orchid native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It features a slender, creeping rhizome and produces dense, dark green leaves that are narrow and linear. The plant blooms with a spike of small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in a loose spiral, typical of the Orchidaceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The species is characterized by its delicate appearance and is often found in association with other orchid species in its habitat.
