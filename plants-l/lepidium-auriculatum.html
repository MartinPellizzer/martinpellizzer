Lepidium auriculatum

Lepidium auriculatum, commonly known as the golden weed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like mustard and cabbage. The plant is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and hairy, branched stems. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Lepidium auriculatum is often found in regions with temperate climates and can tolerate a range of soil conditions.
