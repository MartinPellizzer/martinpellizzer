Lepervenchea

Lepervenchea, commonly known as the silver leaf vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and striking silver-green foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Lepervenchea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
