Lithospermum tournefortii

Lithospermum tournefortii, commonly known as red sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in dry environments. The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its roots contain compounds that have been used in traditional medicine.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
