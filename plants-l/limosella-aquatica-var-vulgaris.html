Limosella aquatica var. vulgaris

Limosella aquatica var. vulgaris, commonly known as marsh sandwort, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 5 to 15 centimeters. The plant features small, white or pinkish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and often covered in fine hairs, adapted to thrive in waterlogged soils.

This species is often found in marshes, pond margins, and other moist environments, contributing to the ecological diversity of these habitats.
