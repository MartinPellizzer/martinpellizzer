Lithocardium sessilifolium

Lithocardium sessilifolium, commonly known as the rock spurge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, succulent stems and leaves that are often clustered at the base. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are arranged in clusters, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Lithocardium sessilifolium is often used in rock gardens due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
