Lambertia rariflora subsp. lutea

Lambertia rariflora subsp.

lutea, commonly known as the yellow lambertia, is a shrub native to South Africa. It is characterized by its dense, spiny branches and clusters of bright yellow flowers.

The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for some animals but toxic to humans. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Its unique appearance and seasonal blooming make it a notable species in its native habitat.
