Lavatera maritima subsp. rupestris

Lavatera maritima subsp.

rupestris, commonly known as the rock lavender, is a perennial shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Europe and northern Africa. It is characterized by its silvery-green, woolly leaves and clusters of soft pink to white flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in coastal dunes and cliffs. It is drought-tolerant and adapted to salty sea air, making it a hardy species suited to maritime climates.

Its botanical features make it an attractive addition to xeriscapic gardens and coastal landscapes.
