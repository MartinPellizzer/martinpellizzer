Loasa hastata

Loasa hastata, commonly known as the silverleaf loasa, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It features small, oval leaves that are densely covered with silvery-white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Loasa hastata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
