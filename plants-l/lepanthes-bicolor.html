Lepanthes bicolor

Lepanthes bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its strikingly contrasting colors, with white petals and a deep purple or violet lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical forests. Its flowers are relatively small but highly fragrant, often blooming in clusters during the rainy season.

Lepanthes bicolor is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly cultivated in greenhouses and botanical gardens.
