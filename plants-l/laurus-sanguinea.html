Laurus sanguinea

Laurus sanguinea, commonly known as the blood laurel, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy fruits that are edible and often used in culinary applications. Its aromatic properties make it valuable in both traditional medicine and perfumery.

Laurus sanguinea thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a hardy plant suitable for ornamental gardening.
