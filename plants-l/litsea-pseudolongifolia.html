Litsea pseudolongifolia

Litsea pseudolongifolia, commonly known as the Chinese tallow tree or Chinese tallow, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite, and often aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange-red berries that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other regions, sometimes becoming invasive.
