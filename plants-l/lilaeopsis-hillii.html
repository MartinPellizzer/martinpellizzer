Lilaeopsis hillii

Lilaeopsis hillii, commonly known as the Hill's sedge, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants.

It is a perennial grass-like plant characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Native to the wetlands of eastern North America, it thrives in moist, often saturated soils. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
