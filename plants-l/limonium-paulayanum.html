Limonium paulayanum

Limonium paulayanum, commonly known as the coastal flax, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dune environments. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Limonium paulayanum is valued for its ability to stabilize coastal soils and its use in horticulture.
