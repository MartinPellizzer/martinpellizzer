Lepidosperma squamatum var. pyramidatum

Lepidosperma squamatum var. pyramidatum, commonly known as the pyramid sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in wet or waterlogged soils, often found in swampy areas or along the edges of water bodies.

This variety is distinguished by its pyramidal inflorescence, which gives it its common name.
