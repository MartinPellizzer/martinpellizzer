Lactuca chitralensis

Lactuca chitralensis, commonly known as Chitral endive, is a species of lettuce native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is a biennial plant that typically grows in rocky, arid environments. The plant has a rosette of dark green, curly leaves that are often used in local cuisines. It is closely related to other members of the Asteraceae family, sharing similar structural and reproductive features.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
