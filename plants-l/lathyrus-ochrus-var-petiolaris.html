Lathyrus ochrus var. petiolaris

Lathyrus ochrus var. petiolaris, commonly known as the golden pea or yellow vetch, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with slender stems and clusters of small, bright yellow pea-like flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.

The seeds are enclosed in pods and are used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.
