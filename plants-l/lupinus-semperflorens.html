Lupinus semperflorens

Lupinus semperflorens, commonly known as the everblooming lupine, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of vibrant blue or purple flowers. The plant has compound leaves composed of several small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Lupinus semperflorens is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plants.
