Lycopodium microstachyum

Lycopodium microstachyum, commonly known as the clubmoss, is a small evergreen plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, dense growth habit and produces spore-bearing cones called strobili. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its leaves are scale-like and arranged in whorls around the stems.

Lycopodium microstachyum plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and small invertebrates.
