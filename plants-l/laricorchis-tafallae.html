Laricorchis tafallae

Laricorchis tafallae, commonly known as the Tafalla lily, is a rare and endemic orchid species native to the Pyrenees Mountains.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. It has a short, upright stem with a single, showy flower that blooms in late spring.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
