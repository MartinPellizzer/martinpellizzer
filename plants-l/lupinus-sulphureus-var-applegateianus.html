Lupinus sulphureus var. applegateianus

Lupinus sulphureus var. applegateianus, commonly known as the yellow lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant typically grows in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. It has a taproot system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in nitrogen fixation in the soil.
