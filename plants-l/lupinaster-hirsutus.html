Lupinaster hirsutus

Lupinaster hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy lupinaster, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is native to certain regions of Australia and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, where it plays a role in nitrogen fixation due to its symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.

Lupinaster hirsutus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
