Lactuca tatarica subsp. pulchella

Lactuca tatarica subsp.

pulchella, commonly known as the Siberian endive, is a perennial herb native to the cold climates of Asia. It features a rosette of dark green, spoon-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy and have a mild, slightly bitter taste.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a loose cluster, which are followed by slender, elongated seed pods. It is adapted to grow in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in cooler temperatures.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its edible leaves and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
