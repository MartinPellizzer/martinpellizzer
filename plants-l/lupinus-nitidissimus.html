Lupinus nitidissimus

Lupinus nitidissimus, commonly known as the silver lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of bright yellow to orange flowers. The plant has compound leaves composed of several narrow, lance-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Lupinus nitidissimus is also noted for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain butterfly species.
