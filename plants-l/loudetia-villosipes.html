Loudetia villosipes

Loudetia villosipes, commonly known as the woolly flatsedge, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a hairy surface that gives the plant its common name.

Loudetia villosipes is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along water bodies, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.
