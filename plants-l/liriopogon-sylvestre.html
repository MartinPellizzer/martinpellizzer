Liriopogon sylvestre

Liriopogon sylvestre, commonly known as the forest lily, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories. The plant features lance-shaped, dark green leaves and produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters. Its flowering period occurs during the wet season, and it is often found in humid, lowland areas.

Liriopogon sylvestre is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
