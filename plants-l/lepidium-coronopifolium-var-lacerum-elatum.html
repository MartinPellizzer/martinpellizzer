Lepidium coronopifolium var. lacerum-elatum

Lepidium coronopifolium var. lacerum-elatum, commonly known as the fringed cress, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many economically important crops such as cabbage and mustard. The plant is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in stream banks and moist meadows.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in its native habitat.
