Lactuca madatapensis

Lactuca madatapensis, commonly known as the Madatapensis lettuce, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family, Asteraceae.

It is native to specific regions in southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in grassy and rocky habitats. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are generally narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Lactuca madatapensis is not widely cultivated for food but is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations.
