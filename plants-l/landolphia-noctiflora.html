Landolphia noctiflora

Landolphia noctiflora, commonly known as the night-flowering star apple, is a climbing plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant produces a fleshy, yellow or orange fruit that contains several seeds. Its leaves are typically large, heart-shaped, and have a rough texture.

Landolphia noctiflora is often found in humid, shaded environments within rainforests.
