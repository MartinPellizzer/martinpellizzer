Leucas parviflora

Leucas parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered leucas, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant features slender, branched stems and small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

It is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
