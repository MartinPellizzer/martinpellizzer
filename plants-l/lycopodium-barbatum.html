Lycopodium barbatum

Lycopodium barbatum, commonly known as the hairy clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. The plant features whorled leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its reproductive structures, called strobila, are cone-like and produce spores for propagation.

Lycopodium barbatum plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various small organisms.
