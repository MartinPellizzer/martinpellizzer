Lasiorrhiza lanigera

Lasiorrhiza lanigera, commonly known as the woolly sand verbena, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its grayish-green, woolly foliage and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. It has a compact growth habit and is well adapted to drought conditions.

Lasiorrhiza lanigera is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental appeal.
