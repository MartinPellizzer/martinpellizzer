Lobivia tiegeliana f. albiflora

Lobivia tiegeliana f. albiflora, commonly known as the White-flowered Lobivia, is a rare and striking cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and thick, ribbed stems. The plant produces clusters of showy, white flowers during the cooler months, which are borne on short, branched pedicels. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to survive in nutrient-poor, well-drained soils.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated by enthusiasts in controlled environments.
