Lotononis biflora

Lotononis biflora, commonly known as the African bluebell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, cylindrical seed pods that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Lotononis biflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
