Linum microphyllum

Linum microphyllum, commonly known as small-leaved flax, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces delicate blue or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Linum microphyllum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in fiber production.
