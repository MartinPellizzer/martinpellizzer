Leptodermis tomentella

Leptodermis tomentella, commonly known as the woolly skin vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, tomentose stems and leaves, which provide a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, often blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain areas.
