Lipotriche rhombifolia

Lipotriche rhombifolia, commonly known as the rhombic lipotriche, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, sandy soils in woodland or coastal areas. The plant features opposite, rhomboid-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and it produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments.

Lipotriche rhombifolia is characterized by its delicate appearance and is sometimes used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value.
