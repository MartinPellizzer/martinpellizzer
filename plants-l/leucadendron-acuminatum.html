Leucadendron acuminatum

Leucadendron acuminatum, commonly known as the silver tree, is a striking evergreen shrub or small tree native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-blue foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance, and its cone-like inflorescences that range in color from yellow to red. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping and coastal gardens. It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural displays.

The species is known for its resilience to drought and salt spray, which contributes to its popularity in coastal regions.
