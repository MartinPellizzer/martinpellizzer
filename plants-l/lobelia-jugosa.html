Lobelia jugosa

Lobelia jugosa, commonly known as the purple lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple to blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Lobelia jugosa has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
