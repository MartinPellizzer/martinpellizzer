Lappago biflora

Lappago biflora, commonly known as the twin-flowered lappago, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and produces small, white or pale blue flowers in clusters. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude grasslands. Its flowers are typically visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Lappago biflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
