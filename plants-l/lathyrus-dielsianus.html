Lathyrus dielsianus

Lathyrus dielsianus, commonly known as the Diels' vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features slender, trailing stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are usually blue to purple in color, arranged in racemes, and bloom from late spring to early summer.

Lathyrus dielsianus is characterized by its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.
