Lavandula × alportelensis

Lavandula × alportelensis, commonly known as the Alportelensis lavender, is a hybrid shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and fragrant, spike-like clusters of purple flowers. The plant produces aromatic essential oils in its flowering stems, which are widely used in perfumery and aromatherapy. Its leaves are narrow, gray-green, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Lavandula × alportelensis thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
