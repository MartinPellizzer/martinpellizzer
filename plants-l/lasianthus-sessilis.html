Lasianthus sessilis

Lasianthus sessilis, commonly known as the sessile lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The leaves are typically opposite, ovate to elliptic in shape, and have a smooth surface. The flowers, which are usually pink to purple in color, bloom in clusters and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
