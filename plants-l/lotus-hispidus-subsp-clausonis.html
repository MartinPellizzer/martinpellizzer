Lotus hispidus subsp. clausonis

Lotus hispidus subsp.

clausonis, commonly known as the hairy lotus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and trifoliate leaves.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically clustered in spherical heads. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it an important species in soil enrichment and ecological restoration.
