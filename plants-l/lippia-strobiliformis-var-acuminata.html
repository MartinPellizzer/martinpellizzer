Lippia strobiliformis var. acuminata

Lippia strobiliformis var. acuminata, commonly known as the acuminata lippia, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, small, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has slender, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its fragrance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardens and herbal use.
