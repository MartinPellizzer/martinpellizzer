Lycopodium affine

Lycopodium affine, commonly known as the slender clubmoss, is a member of the Lycopodiaceae family and is native to various regions in the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a small, evergreen perennial plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant features a short, horizontal stem with numerous whorled leaves that are narrow and pointed. Lycopodium affine produces spore-bearing structures called strobila, which are clustered at the tips of its stems.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
