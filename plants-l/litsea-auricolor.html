Litsea auricolor

Litsea auricolor, commonly known as golden litsea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, yellowish-green leaves that emit a strong citrus-like aroma. Its small, white flowers are followed by elliptical, yellow fruits that are edible.

Litsea auricolor is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
