Linum coulterianum

Linum coulterianum, commonly known as Coulter's flax, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the genus Linum, which includes various species of flax known for their showy blue or white flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowers are characterized by five narrow petals and are often visited by pollinators such as bees.

Coulter's flax is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
