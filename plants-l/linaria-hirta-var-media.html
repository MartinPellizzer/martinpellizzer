Linaria hirta var. media

Linaria hirta var. media, commonly known as the hairy toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces bright yellow, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.

Linaria hirta var. media is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping due to its hardy nature.
