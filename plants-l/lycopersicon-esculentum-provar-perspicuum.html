Lycopersicon esculentum provar. perspicuum

Lycopersicon esculentum provar. perspicuum, commonly known as the tomato, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshade plants. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that develop into bright red, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in a fine hair layer.

The fruit is rich in nutrients and contains lycopene, a powerful antioxidant.
