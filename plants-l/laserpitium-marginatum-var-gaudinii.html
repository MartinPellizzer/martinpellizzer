Laserpitium marginatum var. gaudinii

Laserpitium marginatum var. gaudinii, commonly known as the Gaudin's hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers that resemble a hawk's beak. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a distinctive white or pale margin.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy and resilient nature.
