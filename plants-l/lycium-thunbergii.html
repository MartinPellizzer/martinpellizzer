Lycium thunbergii

Lycium thunbergii, commonly known as Chinese wolfberry or goji berry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family and is characterized by its yellow flowers and red berries that ripen in late summer. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid regions and is adapted to thrive in poor soils. Its berries are rich in antioxidants and have been used in traditional medicine for centuries.

Lycium thunbergii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated for both commercial and medicinal purposes.
