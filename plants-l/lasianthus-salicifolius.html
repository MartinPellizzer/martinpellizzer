Lasianthus salicifolius

Lasianthus salicifolius, commonly known as the willow-leaf lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that resemble those of willow trees. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale purple or white, arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
