Limniris sanguinea var. tobataensis

Limniris sanguinea var. tobataensis, commonly known as the Tobata blood fern, is a variant of the blood fern species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red undersides on its fronds, which become more pronounced as the plant matures. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in tropical rainforests. Its leaves are large, leathery, and can reach lengths of up to two meters.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
