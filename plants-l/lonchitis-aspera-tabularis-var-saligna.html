Lonchitis-aspera tabularis var. saligna

Lonchitis-aspera tabularis var. saligna, commonly known as the narrowleaf spurge.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States, characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in wetland and woodland ecosystems.
