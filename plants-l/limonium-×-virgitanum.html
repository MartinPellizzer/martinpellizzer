Limonium × virgitanum

Limonium × virgitanum, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, silver-gray leaves that are adapted to saline and windy environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dunes and coastal cliffs.

Limonium × virgitanum is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh coastal conditions.
