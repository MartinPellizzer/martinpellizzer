Lamprolobium fruticosum

Lamprolobium fruticosum, commonly known as the coastal beard grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial grass that thrives in coastal and sandy environments, often growing in dunes or along the shoreline. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It is adapted to withstand salt spray and strong winds, making it a hardy species in harsh coastal conditions.

Lamprolobium fruticosum plays a role in stabilizing dune ecosystems and is often used in restoration projects.
