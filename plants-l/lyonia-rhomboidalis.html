Lyonia rhomboidalis

Lyonia rhomboidalis, commonly known as the mountain laurel, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, dark green leaves that are typically oval or rhomboid in shape, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in clusters during the spring, often in shades of white, pink, or red. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its hardiness and adaptability contribute to its widespread cultivation in gardens and natural habitats.
