Lupinus mutabilis var. cruckshanksii

Lupinus mutabilis var. cruckshanksii, commonly known as the Andean lupin, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking purple to red flowers and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant is adapted to high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, temperate climates with well-drained soils. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional use in local communities.
