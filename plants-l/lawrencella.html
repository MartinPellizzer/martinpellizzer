Lawrencella

Lawrencella, commonly known as the mountain flax, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Amaranthaceae.

These plants are native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. Lawrencella species are characterized by their erect growth habit, narrow leaves, and showy, often blue or purple flowers. They are adapted to thrive in harsh, dry environments with poor soils.

The genus includes several species, such as Lawrencella arizonica and Lawrencella tenuifolia, which are often used in xeriscaping due to their low water requirements.
