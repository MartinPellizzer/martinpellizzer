Lotus peczoricus

Lotus peczoricus, commonly known as the Peczori lotus, is a rare flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking pink to purple petals and a delicate, fragrant aroma. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine, waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

Due to its limited habitat and susceptibility to environmental changes, the Peczori lotus is considered endangered.
