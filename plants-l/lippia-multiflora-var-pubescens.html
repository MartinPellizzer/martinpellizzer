Lippia multiflora var. pubescens

Lippia multiflora var. pubescens, commonly known as Mexican mint or blue mist shrub, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and Central America.

It features aromatic, hairy leaves that are often tinged with blue or silver, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental and medicinal gardening.

The leaves are commonly used in traditional medicine for their purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.
