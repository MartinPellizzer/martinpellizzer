Lepanthes oblivia

Lepanthes oblivia, commonly known as the silver star orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking white and green flowers with a distinctive yellow center. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in the understory of tropical forests. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and it produces small, fragrant blooms during the rainy season.

Lepanthes oblivia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
