Lapeirousia teretifolia

Lapeirousia teretifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and delicate, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is a perennial herb that produces its flowers during the spring and early summer months.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts of rare flora.
