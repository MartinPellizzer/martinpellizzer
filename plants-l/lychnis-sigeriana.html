Lychnis sigeriana

Lychnis sigeriana, commonly known as the red campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the carnation family (Caryophyllaceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Lychnis sigeriana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
