Lupinus sicco-silvae

Lupinus sicco-silvae, commonly known as the dry forest lupine, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in sunny, open habitats. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces spikes of blue or purple flowers. Its root system forms symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enriching the soil.

Lupinus sicco-silvae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
