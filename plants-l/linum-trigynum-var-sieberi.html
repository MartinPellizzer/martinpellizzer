Linum trigynum var. sieberi

Linum trigynum var. sieberi, commonly known as the Sieber's flax, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered with fine hairs, while the flowers are clustered in loose inflorescences.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its delicate blooms.
