Lasianthaea podocephala

Lasianthaea podocephala, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leaf arrangement. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces purple to pinkish flower heads that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as swamps and marshes.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a valuable component of wetland ecosystems.
