Lepisorus megasorus

Lepisorus megasorus, commonly known as the giant water fern, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family.

It is a perennial fern characterized by its large, floating fronds that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes, where it attaches itself to submerged surfaces. Its fronds are divided into numerous small, oval-shaped pinnae, which help in maximizing light absorption for photosynthesis.

The spores are produced in clusters on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy in aquatic habitats.
