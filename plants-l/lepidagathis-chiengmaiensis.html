Lepidagathis chiengmaiensis

Lepidagathis chiengmaiensis, commonly known as the Chiengmai mountain balm, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of northern Thailand.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. It typically grows in shaded, humid environments at higher elevations.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
