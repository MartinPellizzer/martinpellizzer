Linaria munbyana var. hirtella

Linaria munbyana var. hirtella, commonly known as the woolly toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy foliage, which gives it its common name, and it thrives in sandy or rocky soils. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and slopes.

Linaria munbyana var. hirtella is a perennial species that is adapted to arid environments.
