Linum inaequale

Linum inaequale, commonly known as unequal flax, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the genus Linum, which includes various species of flax known for their showy blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its flowers are characterized by their five-petaled structure and are often visited by pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Linum inaequale is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
