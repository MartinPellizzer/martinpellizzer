Lilium longiflorum

Lilium longiflorum, commonly known as the Easter lily, is a species of flowering plant in the lily family.

It is native to the eastern United States and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, white, fragrant flowers. The plant typically blooms in the spring, with showy, trumpet-shaped petals that are often used in floral arrangements. Its bulbs are perennial, and it thrives in well-drained soil with partial to full sunlight.

The Easter lily is also valued for its ornamental beauty and cultural significance, especially during the Easter season.
