Lonicera tenuipes var. subglabra

Lonicera tenuipes var. subglabra, commonly known as the smooth honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, arching stems that are often covered in a fine, smooth hairless coating, giving it its varietal name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in the spring, typically yellow or white, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.
