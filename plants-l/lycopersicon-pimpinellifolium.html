Lycopersicon pimpinellifolium

Lycopersicon pimpinellifolium, commonly known as the cherry tomato, is a species of tomato native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, round fruits that are typically red, though they can also be yellow or orange. The plant has hairy stems and lobed leaves, which are typical of the Solanaceae family. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its flavor and is a popular choice in both home gardens and commercial agriculture.
