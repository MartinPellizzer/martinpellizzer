Lacinaria shortii

Lacinaria shortii, commonly known as the Narrow-leafed Lacinaria, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is sometimes cultivated in arid landscapes.
