Leucothoe pulchella var. cordifolia

Leucothoe pulchella var. cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf leucothoe, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features glossy, elliptical to heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, followed by red berries in the fall. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide habitat for wildlife.
