Laurus cyathifera

Laurus cyathifera, commonly known as the narrow-leaved laurel, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other well-known plants like bay laurel and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped, aromatic leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible but not commonly consumed.

Laurus cyathifera thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
