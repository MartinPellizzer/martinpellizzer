Luxemburgia polyandra

Luxemburgia polyandra, commonly known as the velvetleaf.

It is a tropical plant native to parts of Africa, characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes, and it is often found in moist, shaded environments. Luxemburgia polyandra is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.

Its botanical features make it a notable plant in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
