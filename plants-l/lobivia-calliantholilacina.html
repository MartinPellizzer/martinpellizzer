Lobivia calliantholilacina

Lobivia calliantholilacina, commonly known as the lilac-flowered lobivia, is a succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems and dense, woolly foliage. The plant produces vibrant violet or lilac-colored flowers during the warmer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for dry climates.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
