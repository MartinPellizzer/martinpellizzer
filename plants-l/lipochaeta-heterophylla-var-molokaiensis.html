Lipochaeta heterophylla var. molokaiensis

Lipochaeta heterophylla var. molokaiensis, commonly known as the Molokai ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect growth habit and distinctive, often hairy foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on long, slender stems. It thrives in coastal and lowland habitats, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
