Lupinus sericeus var. wallowensis

Lupinus sericeus var. wallowensis, commonly known as the Wallowa lupine, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces spikes of blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain butterfly species.
