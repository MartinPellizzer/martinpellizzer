Lupinus densiflorus var. stenopetalus

Lupinus densiflorus var. stenopetalus, commonly known as the narrow-petaled lupine, is a member of the legume family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, fluffy flower clusters that typically range in color from white to pink. The plant has palmate leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in nitrogen fixation in the ecosystem.
