Labichea teretifolia

Labichea teretifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf labichea, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
