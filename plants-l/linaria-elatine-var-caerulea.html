Linaria elatine var. caerulea

Linaria elatine var. caerulea, commonly known as the blue toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial herb with slender, branched stems and small, showy flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The flowers are typically tubular with two lips, a characteristic feature of the Plantaginaceae family. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands, roadsides, and waste areas.
