Lepidium montanum var. jonesii

Lepidium montanum var. jonesii, commonly known as Jones' peppergrass, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as stream banks and meadows. It produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.
