Lychnitis nigra

Lychnitis nigra, commonly known as the black nightshade, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are typically oval in shape and covered in a fine pubescence. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate or elliptic with a slightly serrated margin. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and woodland edges.

The flowers are small, star-shaped, and typically white or pale green, appearing in clusters.
