Lupinus perennis var. occidentalis

Lupinus perennis var. occidentalis, commonly known as western lupine, is a perennial herb native to western North America.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and clusters of pea-like, often blue or purple, flowers. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

This species is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a food source for wildlife.
