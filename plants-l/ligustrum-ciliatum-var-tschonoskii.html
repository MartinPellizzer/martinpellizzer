Ligustrum ciliatum var. tschonoskii

Ligustrum ciliatum var. tschonoskii, commonly known as the Tschonoskii privet, is a shrub native to parts of East Asia.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy underside, which gives it its species name *ciliatum*. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers followed by dark blue berries. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its dense growth habit and adaptability.

Botanically, it belongs to the Oleaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like lilacs and jasmine.
