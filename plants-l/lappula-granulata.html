Lappula granulata

Lappula granulata, commonly known as the granulated lappula, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Eurasia.

It typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas. The plant has hairy stems and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Lappula granulata is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
