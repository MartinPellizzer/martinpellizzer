Lapathum obtusatum

Lapathum obtusatum, commonly known as the bluntleaf betony, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are oval-shaped with rounded tips, and it produces small, pale blue or violet flowers in whorls along the stem.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has a long history of use in traditional herbal medicine.
