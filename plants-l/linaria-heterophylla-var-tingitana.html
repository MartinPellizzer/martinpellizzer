Linaria heterophylla var. tingitana

Linaria heterophylla var. tingitana, commonly known as the Moroccan toadflax, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branched stems that often grow along the ground or in low mounds. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers with two lips, typical of the Scrophulariaceae family. Its leaves are usually linear or lance-shaped, varying in size depending on the growing conditions.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
