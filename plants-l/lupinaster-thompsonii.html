Lupinaster thompsonii

Lupinaster thompsonii, commonly known as Thompson's lupinaster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Lupinaster thompsonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in nitrogen fixation.
