Lepanthes aeora

Lepanthes aeora, commonly known as the silver-tipped orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, pale green to white flowers with distinctive silver or pale blue markings. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded forest environments. Its leaves are usually ovate and glossy, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Lepanthes aeora is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in tropical greenhouses and gardens.
