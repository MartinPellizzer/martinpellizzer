Lupinus albicaulis var. albicaulis

Lupinus albicaulis var. albicaulis, commonly known as the white lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in nitrogen fixation in the ecosystem.
