Linum perenne var. pyrenaicum

Linum perenne var. pyrenaicum, commonly known as the Pyrenean flax, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pyrenees mountain range.

It features slender, upright stems that bear lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces delicate blue to purple flowers in early summer, which are arranged in loose racemes. It thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitat.
