Laccosperma laurentii

Laccosperma laurentii, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a tropical tree native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, flame-like red flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has compound leaves with long, slender leaflets and produces woody pods containing seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its timber and medicinal properties.
