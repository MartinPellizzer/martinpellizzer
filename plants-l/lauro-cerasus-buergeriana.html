Lauro-cerasus buergeriana

Lauro-cerasus buergeriana, commonly known as the Japanese cherry laurel, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Prunus genus and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy white or pink flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans and livestock.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
