Lobelia gruina f. flava

Lobelia gruina f. flava, commonly known as the yellow-flowered lobelia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, ovate leaves that have a slightly hairy texture. Its most distinguishing characteristic is its clusters of small, tubular yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partially shaded garden settings.
