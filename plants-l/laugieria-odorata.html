Laugieria odorata

Laugieria odorata, commonly known as the sweet sarsaparilla, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves with a glossy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its roots are valued for their aromatic properties and have been used in traditional medicine.

Laugieria odorata is also cultivated for its ornamental appeal and as a source of natural flavoring.
