Lepidium crispum

Lepidium crispum, commonly known as curly cress, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage. The plant is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and fern-like, curly leaves. It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and fields.

Lepidium crispum is often used in herbal medicine and is also a food source for certain insects.
