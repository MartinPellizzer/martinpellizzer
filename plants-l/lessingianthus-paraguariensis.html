Lessingianthus paraguariensis

Lessingianthus paraguariensis, commonly known as yerba mate, is a perennial shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil, Paraguay, and Argentina.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its serrated, lance-shaped leaves and yellow-orange flowers. The plant is well adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Its most notable feature is the use of its leaves in the preparation of a traditional caffeinated beverage.
