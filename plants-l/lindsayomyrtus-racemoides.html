Lindsayomyrtus racemoides

Lindsayomyrtus racemoides, commonly known as the coastal hibiscus, is a shrub native to coastal regions of eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in spring and summer. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dune environments. It has a compact growth habit and is well adapted to salt spray and strong coastal winds.

Lindsayomyrtus racemoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in stabilizing coastal ecosystems.
