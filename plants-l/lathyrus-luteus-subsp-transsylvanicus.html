Lathyrus luteus subsp. transsylvanicus

Lathyrus luteus subsp.

transsylvanicus, commonly known as the Transylvanian vetch, is a flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves.

The plant produces yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and forest edges.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
