Laxmannia geoides

Laxmannia geoides, commonly known as the broad-leaved saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are broad, fleshy, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

Laxmannia geoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
