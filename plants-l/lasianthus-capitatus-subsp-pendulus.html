Lasianthus capitatus subsp. pendulus

Lasianthus capitatus subsp.

pendulus, commonly known as the hanging lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its pendant inflorescences, which give it its common name.

The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
