Lepidium robustum

Lepidium robustum, commonly known as wild cress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family and is native to various regions across Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It is an annual or biennial herb that typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields. The plant features small, white or yellowish flowers arranged in racemes, and its leaves are usually pinnately divided. Lepidium robustum has a taproot system and produces small, rounded seeds that are dispersed by wind.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
