Lavatera julii

Lavatera julii, commonly known as the Mediterranean woolly bush, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its soft, woolly leaves and showy, fluffy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in sunny, well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant once established. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Lavatera julii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
