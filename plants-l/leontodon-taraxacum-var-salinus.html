Leontodon taraxacum var. salinus

Leontodon taraxacum var. salinus, commonly known as the salt meadow hawk's-beard, is a perennial flowering plant native to saline environments.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy, lobed leaves. The plant thrives in coastal and saline soils, showcasing adaptations to high salt concentrations. Its growth habit includes a rosette of basal leaves with a central flowering stem.

It plays a role in stabilizing coastal dunes and supporting local biodiversity.
