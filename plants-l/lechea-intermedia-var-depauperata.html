Lechea intermedia var. depauperata

Lechea intermedia var. depauperata, commonly known as the slender lechea, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is often grown in gardens and landscapes.
