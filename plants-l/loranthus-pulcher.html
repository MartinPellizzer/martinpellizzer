Loranthus pulcher

Loranthus pulcher, commonly known as the beautiful mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that attaches itself to the branches of host trees, primarily in the genus Eucalyptus.

It lacks true roots and absorbs water and nutrients through its stems and leaves, which are adapted for this parasitic lifestyle. The plant produces small, white or pale green berries that contain seeds covered in a sticky substance to aid in attachment to new hosts. Its foliage is dense and often forms a compact, bushy growth on the host tree.

Loranthus pulcher plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for birds and insects, despite its parasitic nature.
