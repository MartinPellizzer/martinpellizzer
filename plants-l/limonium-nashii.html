Limonium nashii

Limonium nashii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and spikes of blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dune environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the flowers are typically borne on elongated stems.

Limonium nashii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize coastal soils.
