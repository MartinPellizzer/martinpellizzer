Lastrea pozoi

Lastrea pozoi, commonly known as the desert hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant has a robust root system that allows it to survive in dry conditions by storing water. Its flowers bloom during the rainy season, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Lastrea pozoi is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental beauty in landscaping.
