Lycium anatolicum

Lycium anatolicum, commonly known as Turkish boxthorn, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species found in rocky soils.

Lycium anatolicum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
