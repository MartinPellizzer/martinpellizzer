Limodorum aloifolium

Limodorum aloifolium, commonly known as the African lily or the yellow lily of the valley, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a slender, upright stem that bears lance-shaped, waxy leaves arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. Its foliage and flowers are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Limodorum aloifolium is adapted to grow in moist, shaded environments, typically in forest understories.
