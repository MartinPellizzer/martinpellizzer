Lycium brevipes var. brevipes

Lycium brevipes var. brevipes, commonly known as the short-stemmed boxthorn, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It features dense, spiny foliage and produces small, red berries that are edible for both humans and wildlife. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves and is adapted to thrive in low-nutrient, well-drained soils. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited for xeriscaping and erosion control in dry environments.
