Lotus corniculatus var. fallax

Lotus corniculatus var. fallax, commonly known as the autumn lotus or fallax grass violet, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive spurred petal. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and it is known for its ability to tolerate a variety of environmental conditions.

The flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, making it a valuable nectar source for pollinators during the transitional seasons.
