Licuala telifera

Licuala telifera, commonly known as the giant pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its large, feather-like leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. It produces small, fragrant flowers arranged in panicles, which are often used in traditional crafts and as a source of aromatic oils.

The plant thrives in humid, coastal environments and is often found in mangrove ecosystems.
