Lupinus yaulyensis

Lupinus yaulyensis, commonly known as the Yauly endemic lupine, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of blue to violet pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces elongated pods that contain several seeds. Lupinus yaulyensis is adapted to high-altitude environments and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in mountainous ecosystems.
