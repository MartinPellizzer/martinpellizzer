Laurus serrata

Laurus serrata, commonly known as the serrated laurel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 2 meters. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by blue-black berries. Its leaves are elliptical with serrated edges, giving it its common name.

Laurus serrata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
