Lupinus wyethii subsp. tetonensis

Lupinus wyethii subsp.

tetonensis, commonly known as the Teton lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the northern Great Plains of North America. It is characterized by its erect growth habit and clusters of sparsely arranged blue to purple flowers.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairie and steppe habitats. Lupinus wyethii subsp.

tetonensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
