Lantana velutina var. longifolia

Lantana velutina var. longifolia, commonly known as the velvet lantana, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, textured appearance.

The flowers are borne in terminal inflorescences and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
