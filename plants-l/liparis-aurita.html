Liparis aurita

Liparis aurita, commonly known as the aurite orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are strap-like and often have a waxy coating, which helps in retaining moisture.

Liparis aurita is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
