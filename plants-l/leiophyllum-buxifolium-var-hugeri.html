Leiophyllum buxifolium var. hugeri

Leiophyllum buxifolium var. hugeri, commonly known as the Huger's boxleaf lily, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage that resembles boxwood, with small, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and low-maintenance plant for ornamental use.
