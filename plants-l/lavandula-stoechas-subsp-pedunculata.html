Lavandula stoechas subsp. pedunculata

Lavandula stoechas subsp.

pedunculata, commonly known as the Spanish lavender, is a perennial shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula. It features aromatic, gray-green leaves and spikes of violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant is characterized by its distinctive, elongated flower clusters, which are often adorned with red or orange bracts. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and Mediterranean climates.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and essential oil content, used in aromatherapy and perfumery.
