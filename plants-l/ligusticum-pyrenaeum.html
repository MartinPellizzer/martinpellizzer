Ligusticum pyrenaeum

Ligusticum pyrenaeum, commonly known as the Pyrenean lovage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula and southern France.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at elevations ranging from 500 to 2,000 meters. Its leaves are deeply lobed and emit a strong, spicy scent when crushed.

Ligusticum pyrenaeum is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
