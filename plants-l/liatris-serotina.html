Liatris serotina

Liatris serotina, commonly known as purpletop blazingstar, is a herbaceous perennial native to the central and eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescence composed of numerous purple to pinkish flower heads. The plant typically grows in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in prairies, meadows, and open woodlands. Its striking appearance makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.

Liatris serotina is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
