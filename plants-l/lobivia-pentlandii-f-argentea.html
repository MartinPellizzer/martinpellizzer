Lobivia pentlandii f. argentea

Lobivia pentlandii f. argentea, commonly known as the silver lobivia, is a succulent plant native to the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It features a compact, branching structure with thick, fleshy stems that store water, typical of desert plants. The leaves are usually small and cylindrical, often covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair that reflects sunlight and reduces water loss. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, appearing in late spring or early summer.

This variety is prized for its unique appearance and adaptability to arid environments.
