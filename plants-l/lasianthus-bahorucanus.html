Lasianthus bahorucanus

Lasianthus bahorucanus, commonly known as the Madagascar flame flower, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its vibrant red and orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Lasianthus bahorucanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens for its dramatic floral display.
