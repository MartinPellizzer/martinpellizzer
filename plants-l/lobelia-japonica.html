Lobelia japonica

Lobelia japonica, commonly known as Japanese lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to East Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear whorled clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of blue to purple. The leaves are typically oval to lance-shaped, with a rough, hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a ground cover.
