Leucopogon conchifolius

Leucopogon conchifolius, commonly known as the coastal heath, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southeastern Australia.

It is a shrub characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds. It plays an important role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for native wildlife.

Leucopogon conchifolius is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
