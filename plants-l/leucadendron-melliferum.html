Leucadendron melliferum

Leucadendron melliferum, commonly known as the honeycomb leucadendron, is a striking evergreen shrub native to the Western Cape of South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, conical clusters of yellow to orange flower heads, which resemble a honeycomb, hence its common name. The plant produces small, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Leucadendron melliferum is a popular ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
