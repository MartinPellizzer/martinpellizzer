Lepanthes uxoria

Lepanthes uxoria, commonly known as the Orchid of the Andes, is a species of orchid native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that often display vibrant colors such as red, purple, and white. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas, thriving in cool and humid conditions. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in its challenging mountainous habitat.

Lepanthes uxoria is also noted for its delicate, fragrant blooms that attract pollinators like bees and hummingbirds.
