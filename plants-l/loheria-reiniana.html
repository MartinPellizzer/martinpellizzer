Loheria reiniana

Loheria reiniana, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or alpine regions. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery-white pubescence.

Loheria reiniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable climates.
