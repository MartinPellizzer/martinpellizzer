Lass

Lass, commonly known as *Lassia* or *Lassia tenuifolia*, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Gesneriaceae and is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, tubular flowers. The leaves are typically ovate or lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a ornamental plant.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
