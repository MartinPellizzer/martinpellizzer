Lathyrus decaphyllus var. minor

Lathyrus decaphyllus var. minor, commonly known as the lesser bird's-foot trefoil, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny environments. The plant features delicate, trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. Its botanical characteristics include a slender stem and a fibrous root system, which help it adapt to arid conditions.

It is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and modest blooms.
