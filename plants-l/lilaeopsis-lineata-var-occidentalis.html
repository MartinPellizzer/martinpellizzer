Lilaeopsis lineata var. occidentalis

Lilaeopsis lineata var. occidentalis, commonly known as the western threadleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and compact growth habit, often forming dense, low-growing mats. The plant typically thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early spring, which are arranged in spikelets.

This species is valued for its drought tolerance and is often used in restoration projects and landscaping in suitable climates.
