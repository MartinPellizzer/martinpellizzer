Lomenia borbonica

Lomenia borbonica, commonly known as the Bourbon lomenta, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its seeds are encased in hairy pods, which aid in seed dispersal.

Lomenia borbonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
