Layia calliglossa

Layia calliglossa, commonly known as the golden coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow petals and a distinctive, spiky central cone that resembles a crown. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Layia calliglossa is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
