Luehea uniflora var. gracilis

Luehea uniflora var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow or orange. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, hard seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or riverine habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for tropical gardens and as a specimen plant.
