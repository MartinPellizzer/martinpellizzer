Lotus pusillus var. macranthus

Lotus pusillus var. macranthus, commonly known as the giant lotus, is a striking aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 centimeters in diameter, making it a popular choice for ornamental ponds. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in marshes and wetlands. Its leaves are broad and floating, providing shade and habitat for aquatic life.

The flowers typically bloom in late summer and early fall, adding vibrant color to the water's surface.
