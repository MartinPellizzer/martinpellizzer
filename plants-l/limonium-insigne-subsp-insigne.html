Limonium insigne subsp. insigne

Limonium insigne subsp.

insigne, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, often covered in a fine layer of salt.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate saline environments.
