Lobelia gelida

Lobelia gelida, commonly known as the hardy lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender stems and small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in the summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Lobelia gelida is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging, wet conditions.
