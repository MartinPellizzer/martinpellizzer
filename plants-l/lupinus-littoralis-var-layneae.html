Lupinus littoralis var. layneae

Lupinus littoralis var. layneae, commonly known as Layne's coastal lupine, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific coast of North America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of violet-blue to purple pea-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal dunes and sandy soils, adapted to salt spray and strong winds. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval to lance-shaped leaflets.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing dune ecosystems and providing nectar for pollinators.
