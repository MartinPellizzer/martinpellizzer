Lantana sellowiana var. lanceolata

Lantana sellowiana var. lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf lantana, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and have a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

The foliage and flowers are often used in ornamental gardening for their vibrant colors and hardy nature.
