Lavrania cactiformis

Lavrania cactiformis, commonly known as the spiky leaf cactus, is a unique succulent native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It features a shrub-like structure with fleshy, spiny leaves that store water, adapting to its dry environment. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the rainy season, adding a striking contrast to its rugged appearance. Its root system is shallow and widespread, allowing it to absorb moisture efficiently from the soil.

Lavrania cactiformis is valued for its resilience and ornamental appeal in xerophytic gardens.
