Lepanthes phalicus

Lepanthes phalicus, commonly known as the "phallic orchid," is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant produces delicate, showy flowers with intricate patterns and a distinctive, elongated spur. Its botanical features include a slender stem and lance-shaped leaves.

Lepanthes phalicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
