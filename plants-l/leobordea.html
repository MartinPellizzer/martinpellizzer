Leobordea

Leobordea, commonly known as the rainbow orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plants are characterized by their striking, colorful flowers that often display a range of hues, including red, orange, and yellow. Leobordea species typically grow as epiphytes or lithophytes, attaching themselves to trees or rocks.

They require high humidity and indirect light to thrive, making them popular among orchid enthusiasts.
