Lonchocarpus fendleri var. pubescens

Lonchocarpus fendleri var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy triggerplant, is a perennial shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is notable for its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its hairy texture, as suggested by the variety name, is a distinguishing feature of its foliage.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits in tropical gardens.
