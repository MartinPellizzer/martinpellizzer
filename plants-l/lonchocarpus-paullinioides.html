Lonchocarpus paullinioides

Lonchocarpus paullinioides, commonly known as the yellow cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and produces pods containing yellow to orange seeds. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it valuable in agricultural settings. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal uses, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
