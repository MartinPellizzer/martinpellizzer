Lantana angustibracteata

Lantana angustibracteata, commonly known as the narrow bract lantana, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to yellow, orange, or red, depending on the variety. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.

The plant is also known for its ability to attract butterflies and other pollinators.
