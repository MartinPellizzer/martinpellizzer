Lythrum salicaria var. glabrum

Lythrum salicaria var. glabrum, commonly known as purple loosestrife, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, spire-like clusters of vibrant purple flowers that bloom from June to September. The plant has lance-shaped, opposite leaves that are typically smooth and glabrous, giving it its varietal name. It thrives in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, preferring moist to wet soils.

However, it is considered an invasive species in North America, where it outcompetes native vegetation and disrupts wetland ecosystems.
