Larsenia decumbens

Larsenia decumbens, commonly known as the creeping larsenia, is a low-growing perennial plant native to parts of South America.

It features fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating, known as farina. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Larsenia decumbens is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
