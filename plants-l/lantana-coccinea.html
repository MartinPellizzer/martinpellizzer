Lantana coccinea

Lantana coccinea, commonly known as the red lantana, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, brightly colored flowers, typically red, orange, or yellow. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

However, it can be invasive in some regions and may pose a risk to native ecosystems.
