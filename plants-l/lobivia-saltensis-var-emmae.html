Lobivia saltensis var. emmae

Lobivia saltensis var. emmae, commonly known as the Emma's Lobivia, is a succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It features compact, rounded rosettes with thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a bluish or grayish hue. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the warmer months, which are borne on tall, slender stems. It is well adapted to hot, dry climates and requires minimal water, making it ideal for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice among cactus and succulent enthusiasts.
