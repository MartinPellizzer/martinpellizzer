Lithocardium neowiedianum

Lithocardium neowiedianum, commonly known as the rock spurge, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a succulent shrub characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and yellow flowers. The plant thrives in arid and rocky environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its genus name, Lithocardium, refers to its growth on limestone, while the specific name honors the botanist who first described it.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in xerophytic landscapes.
