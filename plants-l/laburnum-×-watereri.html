Laburnum × watereri

Laburnum × watereri, commonly known as the golden chain tree, is a hybrid deciduous shrub or small tree native to the British Isles.

It is characterized by its long, drooping clusters of bright yellow flowers that appear in early spring. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets, and its bark is smooth and greyish-brown. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental planting.

The golden chain tree is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
