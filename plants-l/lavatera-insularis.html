Lavatera insularis

Lavatera insularis, commonly known as the island lavatera, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and pinnate leaves. The plant produces showy, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and rocky habitats.

Lavatera insularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
