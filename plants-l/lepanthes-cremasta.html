Lepanthes cremasta

Lepanthes cremasta, commonly known as the cream-stemmed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, pale green to white flowers that often have a yellow or orange lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, tropical environments. Its leaves are usually elongated and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native habitat.

Lepanthes cremasta is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
