Limnochloa calyptrata

Limnochloa calyptrata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and is often found in wetland or riparian habitats. The plant features small, heart-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. Its flowers are tiny and clustered in spikes, with pale green to brownish hues.

Limnochloa calyptrata plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for invertebrates in its native environment.
