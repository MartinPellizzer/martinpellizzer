Lilium davidii var. macranthum

Lilium davidii var. macranthum, commonly known as the Chinese tiger lily, is a perennial bulbous plant native to China.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of orange, red, and yellow, often with a distinctive red throat. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a rosette at the base.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
