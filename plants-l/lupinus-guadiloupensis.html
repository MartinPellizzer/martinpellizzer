Lupinus guadiloupensis

Lupinus guadiloupensis, commonly known as the Guadalupe lupine, is a flowering plant native to the island of Guadalupe in the Caribbean.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and clusters of pea-like flowers. The plant produces small, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds, typical of many leguminous species. Lupinus guadiloupensis thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native environment.
