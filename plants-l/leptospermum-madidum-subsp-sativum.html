Leptospermum madidum subsp. sativum

Leptospermum madidum subsp.

sativum, commonly known as the coastal tea tree, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its small, needle-like leaves and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers.

The plant thrives in coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils. It produces small, edible berries that are used in traditional and modern culinary applications.

Its aromatic foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
