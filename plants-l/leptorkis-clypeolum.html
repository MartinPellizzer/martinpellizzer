Leptorkis clypeolum

Leptorkis clypeolum, commonly known as the golden cup fern, is a small, delicate fern native to Southeast Asia.

It features a compact, rosette-like structure with fronds that are typically bright yellow to golden in color. The plant has a short, creeping rhizome and produces sporangia on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental appeal, it is occasionally cultivated as a houseplant.
