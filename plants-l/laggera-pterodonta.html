Laggera pterodonta

Laggera pterodonta, commonly known as the Chinese dandelion, is a herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its upright stems and lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers that develop into fluffy, parachute-like seeds, facilitating wind dispersal. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Laggera pterodonta is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional Chinese medicine.
