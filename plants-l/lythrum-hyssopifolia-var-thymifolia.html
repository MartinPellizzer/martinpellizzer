Lythrum hyssopifolia var. thymifolia

Lythrum hyssopifolia var. thymifolia, commonly known as thyme-leaved loosestrife, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and wetland habitats.
