Leucothoe subcordata

Leucothoe subcordata, commonly known as the narrowleaf inkberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark green, elliptical leaves that turn reddish in the fall. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers in the spring, which are followed by dark blue berries that persist into winter. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Leucothoe subcordata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide year-round interest in the landscape.
