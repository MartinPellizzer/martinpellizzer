Lilium elatum

Lilium elatum, commonly known as the Turk's cap lily, is a striking bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems that bear large, showy flowers with six recurved petals. The plant typically blooms in late summer to early autumn, producing vibrant red or orange hues that attract pollinators. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Lilium elatum is valued in horticulture for its dramatic appearance and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
