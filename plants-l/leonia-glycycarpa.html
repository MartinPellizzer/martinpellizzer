Leonia glycycarpa

Leonia glycycarpa, commonly known as the sweet fruit tree, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant produces small, round fruits with a thick, smooth skin that turns from green to yellow when ripe. The fruit contains a single seed surrounded by a sweet, aromatic pulp.

It is valued for its edible fruit and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
