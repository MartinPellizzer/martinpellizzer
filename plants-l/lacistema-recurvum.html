Lacistema recurvum

Lacistema recurvum, commonly known as the yellow bell vine, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its long, trailing stems and showy, yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and it produces small, green, fleshy fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
