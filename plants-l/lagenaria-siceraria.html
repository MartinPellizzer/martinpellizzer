Lagenaria siceraria

Lagenaria siceraria, commonly known as the bottle gourd, is a flowering plant in the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to Africa and is characterized by its large, hollow, bottle-shaped fruits. The plant has large, heart-shaped leaves and yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers. It is cultivated for its edible young fruits, which are used in various cuisines.

The plant is also used in traditional medicine and for making containers due to its strong, durable rind.
