Lasianthus sylvestris f. latifolius

Lasianthus sylvestris f. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf lasianthus, is a variant of the species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green in color. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with appropriate conditions.
