Lissochilus palmicola

Lissochilus palmicola, commonly known as the palm-like orchid, is a species of orchid native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its slender, palm-like leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous habitats, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions. Its unique morphology and habitat make it a notable species in the Orchidaceae family.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered plant in its native range.
