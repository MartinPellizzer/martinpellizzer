Ligusticum caeruleomontanum

Ligusticum caeruleomontanum, commonly known as blue mountain lovage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features tall, slender stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, often adorned with finely divided, aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in late summer. Its root system is fibrous and contains compounds that have been used in traditional medicine.

Ligusticum caeruleomontanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its potential medicinal properties.
