Lippia alba f. macrophylla

Lippia alba f. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved white sage, is a variant of the white sage plant native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale purple, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in arid and semi-arid climates.

This variety is valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in traditional herbal practices.
