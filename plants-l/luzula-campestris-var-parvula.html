Luzula campestris var. parvula

Luzula campestris var. parvula, commonly known as the dwarf woodrush, is a low-growing perennial plant belonging to the family Plantaginaceae.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a rosette shape. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, greenish-white flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

This variety is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil.
