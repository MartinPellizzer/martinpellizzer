Lecanorchis amethystea

Lecanorchis amethystea, commonly known as the amethyst orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking violet or amethyst-colored flowers, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments, often near streams or in rich, organic soil. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it produces a single, spike-like inflorescence with several flowers.

Lecanorchis amethystea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
