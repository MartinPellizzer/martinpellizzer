Lasianthus africanus subsp. biokoensis

Lasianthus africanus subsp.

biokoensis, commonly known as the Bioko flame flower, is a species of flowering plant native to the island of Bioko in Equatorial Guinea. It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that display vibrant red and orange hues.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories and rocky areas. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture, while the flowers are clustered in dense spikes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in tropical gardens.
