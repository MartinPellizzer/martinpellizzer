Ligusticum albanicum

Ligusticum albanicum, commonly known as Albanian lovage, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbel-shaped flower clusters. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with finely divided, fern-like leaves that emit a strong, spicy scent. Its flowers are small and white to pale green, appearing in late spring to early summer.

The roots and leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.
