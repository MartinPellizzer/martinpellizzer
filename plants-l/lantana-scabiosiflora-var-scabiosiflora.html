Lantana scabiosiflora var. scabiosiflora

Lantana scabiosiflora var. scabiosiflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lantana, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It features opposite, narrow, and slightly hairy leaves that are typically green with a slightly grayish underside. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, which bloom in the summer months. It is often found in dry to moist woodland habitats and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowering display.
