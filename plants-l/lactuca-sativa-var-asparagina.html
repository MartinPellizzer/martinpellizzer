Lactuca sativa var. asparagina

Lactuca sativa var. asparagina, commonly known as asparagus lettuce, is a leafy green vegetable with a distinctive flavor profile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tender, asparagus-like stems and slightly bitter leaves. The plant is typically grown for its edible leaves and young shoots, which are used in various culinary applications. It thrives in cool climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its nutritional content and unique taste, making it a popular choice in salads and cooked dishes.
