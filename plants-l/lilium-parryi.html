Lilium parryi

Lilium parryi, commonly known as Parry's lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking white to pale pink flowers with a distinctive red or purple blotch at the base. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Its bulbs are adapted to survive in harsh environments, and it blooms in late spring to early summer.

Lilium parryi is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
