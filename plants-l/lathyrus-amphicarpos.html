Lathyrus amphicarpos

Lathyrus amphicarpos, commonly known as the two-seed cowpea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant's pods contain two seeds each, which are often used as a food source in some parts of the world. Lathyrus amphicarpos is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
