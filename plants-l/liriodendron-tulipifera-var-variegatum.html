Liriodendron tulipifera var. variegatum

Liriodendron tulipifera var. variegatum, commonly known as the variegated tulip tree, is a cultivar of the tulip tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive variegated leaves that display a mix of green, white, and yellow hues. The plant produces showy, tulip-shaped yellow flowers in the spring, followed by elongated, woody fruits. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental tree in gardens.

Its unique foliage and seasonal blooms contribute to its ornamental value in landscape design.
