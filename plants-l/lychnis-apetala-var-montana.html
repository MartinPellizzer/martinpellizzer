Lychnis apetala var. montana

Lychnis apetala var. montana, commonly known as the mountain everlasting, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically pink or red, which bloom in late summer. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

It is well adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, making it a hardy plant suitable for alpine gardens.
