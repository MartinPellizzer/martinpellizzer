Linum fasciculatum

Linum fasciculatum, commonly known as clustered flax, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Linum fasciculatum is primarily cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
