Luzula forsteri subsp. caspica

Luzula forsteri subsp.

caspica, commonly known as the Caspian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in wetlands or along the edges of water bodies. It produces small, clustered spikelets that are usually green or brown in color, and it flowers in the spring or early summer. Luzula forsteri subsp.

caspica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
