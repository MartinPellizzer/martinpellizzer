Liatris pauciflora var. pauciflora

Liatris pauciflora var. pauciflora, commonly known as the few-flowered blazing star, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that bear clusters of purple to pinkish-purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and sandy soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
