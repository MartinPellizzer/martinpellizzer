Lonicera pyrenaica var. grandifolia

Lonicera pyrenaica var. grandifolia, commonly known as the large-flowered honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly rough texture. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are usually yellow or orange, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its fruits are red berries that mature in the fall, attracting various bird species.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
