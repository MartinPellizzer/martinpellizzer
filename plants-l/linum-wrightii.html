Linum wrightii

Linum wrightii, commonly known as plains flax, is a flowering plant native to the central and western United States.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
