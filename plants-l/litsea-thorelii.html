Litsea thorelii

Litsea thorelii, commonly known as the Thai litsea or Thai sweet pepper tree, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, yellowish drupe that turns red when mature, and it is often used in traditional medicine.

The plant's essential oils are valued for their aromatic properties and potential medicinal uses.
