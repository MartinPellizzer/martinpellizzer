Limonia alternifolia

Limonia alternifolia, commonly known as the West Indian hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 2 to 4 meters. The plant features alternate, elliptic leaves that are usually glossy and dark green in color. Its flowers are showy, with five petals and a prominent yellow stamen, often blooming in clusters.

Limonia alternifolia is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
