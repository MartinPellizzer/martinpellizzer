Lactuca decipiens var. multifida

Lactuca decipiens var. multifida, commonly known as the curly leaf lettuce, is a variant of the common lettuce species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, lobed, and often curly leaves that give it a unique appearance. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and prefers cool, moist environments. Its leaves are edible and have a mild, slightly bitter taste, making it a popular choice in salads and culinary applications.

The plant also has a long flowering stem that produces small, green flowers, which can attract pollinators like bees.
