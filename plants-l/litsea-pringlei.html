Litsea pringlei

Litsea pringlei, commonly known as Pringle's litsea, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in secondary forests. Its fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for various wildlife species.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine in some regions for their purported therapeutic properties.
