Lavera nodiflora

Lavera nodiflora, commonly known as the clustered lavender.

It is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and spikes of purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Lavera nodiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
