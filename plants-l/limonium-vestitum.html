Limonium vestitum

Limonium vestitum, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flower heads surrounded by silvery, velvety bracts. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, salt marshes, and sandy soils, showing a high tolerance for saline conditions. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Limonium vestitum is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought-resistant properties.
