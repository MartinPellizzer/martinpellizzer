Leonotis velutina

Leonotis velutina, commonly known as the velvet lantern or velvet trumpet, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from orange to red. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are velvety to the touch, giving the plant its common name, and it produces showy, lantern-like flower clusters.

Leonotis velutina is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
