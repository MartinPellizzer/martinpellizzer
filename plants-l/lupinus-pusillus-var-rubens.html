Lupinus pusillus var. rubens

Lupinus pusillus var. rubens, commonly known as the red dwarf lupine, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features small, clustered leaves and produces vivid red or purple pea-like flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats. Its small size makes it an attractive addition to native plant gardens.

The species is notable for its role in nitrogen fixation, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
