Loliolum orientale

Loliolum orientale, commonly known as the Eastern lily, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in the spring. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white, pink, or yellow. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for both its beauty and its use in traditional medicine.
