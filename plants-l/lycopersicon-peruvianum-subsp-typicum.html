Lycopersicon peruvianum subsp. typicum

Lycopersicon peruvianum subsp.

typicum, commonly known as the common tomato, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America. It is characterized by its hairy stems, alternate leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of yellow flowers that develop into bright red, fleshy fruits. The plant thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its fruit is a key component in many cuisines worldwide and is rich in vitamins and antioxidants.
