Ligustrum ibota f. obtusifolium

Ligustrum ibota f. obtusifolium, commonly known as the obtuse-leaved privet, is a variant of the privet shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are typically smooth and have a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by dark blue-black berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its foliage provides year-round interest, and it is often used in landscaping for its dense growth habit.
