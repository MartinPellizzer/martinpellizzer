Limon vulgare

Limon vulgare, commonly known as meadow sweet, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North America.

It typically grows in moist meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges, reaching heights of 1 to 3 meters. The plant features basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish and branched. Its flowers are yellow and clustered in rounded umbels, attracting pollinators like bees and flies.

The plant is known for its aromatic leaves and has been used historically in herbal medicine.
