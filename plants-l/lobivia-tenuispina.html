Lobivia tenuispina

Lobivia tenuispina, commonly known as the narrow-spined lobivia, is a species of cactus native to South America, specifically Argentina.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are typically ribbed and covered in sparse, narrow spines. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. Lobivia tenuispina is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
