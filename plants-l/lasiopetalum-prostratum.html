Lasiopetalum prostratum

Lasiopetalum prostratum, commonly known as the prostrate lasiopetalum, is a low-growing shrub native to Australia.

It features small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance. It is often found in sandy or rocky soils in coastal and semi-arid regions.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
