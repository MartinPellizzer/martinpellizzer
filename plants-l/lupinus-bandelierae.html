Lupinus bandelierae

Lupinus bandelierae, commonly known as the Bandelier lupine, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stalks and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, typically found in rocky or gravelly habitats. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Lupinus bandelierae plays an important role in stabilizing soils and supporting local pollinator populations.
