Lonchestigma bipinnatifidum

Lonchestigma bipinnatifidum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved star sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage with a distinctive, finely divided, bipinnatifid leaves that give it a delicate, feathery appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically found in dense clusters along the stems. It thrives in moist, swampy environments and is often found in freshwater marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Due to its unique leaf structure and habitat preferences, it is of interest to botanists studying wetland flora.
