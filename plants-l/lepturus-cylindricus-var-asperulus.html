Lepturus cylindricus var. asperulus

Lepturus cylindricus var. asperulus, commonly known as the rough-stemmed lepturus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. The plant features slender, cylindrical stems that are covered in a rough, hairy texture. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly serrated edge.

The flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in early summer.
