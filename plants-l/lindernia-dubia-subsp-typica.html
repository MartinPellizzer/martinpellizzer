Lindernia dubia subsp. typica

Lindernia dubia subsp.

typica, commonly known as the slender lindernia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It features slender, branching stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple.

The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to dry conditions. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss. Lindernia dubia subsp.

typica is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
