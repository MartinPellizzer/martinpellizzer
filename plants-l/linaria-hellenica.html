Linaria hellenica

Linaria hellenica, commonly known as the Greek toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and clusters of bright yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its leaves are narrow and opposite, with a slightly hairy texture.

Linaria hellenica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
