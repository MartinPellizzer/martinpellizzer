Lantana maxima

Lantana maxima, commonly known as the shrub lantana, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its dense, bushy growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to red. The plant produces small, oval berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. Lantana maxima is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in warm, sunny climates.

It is also known for its strong fragrance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
