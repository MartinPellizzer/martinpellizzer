Leucaena collinsii subsp. zacapana

Leucaena collinsii subsp.

zacapana, commonly known as Zacapana leucaena, is a shrub or small tree native to Mexico. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers.

The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant. It produces small, woody pods that contain edible seeds, which are a source of protein and can be used in traditional diets.

Its rapid growth and nitrogen-fixing ability make it valuable for soil stabilization and livestock forage.
