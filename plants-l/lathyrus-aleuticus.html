Lathyrus aleuticus

Lathyrus aleuticus, commonly known as the Aleutian vetch, is a flowering plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It is a perennial vine that typically grows in moist, rocky habitats along the coasts. The plant produces clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

Lathyrus aleuticus plays a role in stabilizing coastal soils and provides habitat for various insects and small animals.
