Lycaste × niesseniae var. rosea

Lycaste × niesseniae var. rosea, commonly known as the pink-tinged lycaste, is a hybrid orchid species native to Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elegant, pendulous inflorescences. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers with a pinkish hue, often with white or cream-colored markings. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining, organic-rich soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or tropical gardens.
