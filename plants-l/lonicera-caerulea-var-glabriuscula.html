Lonicera caerulea var. glabriuscula

Lonicera caerulea var. glabriuscula, commonly known as the dwarf honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a sweet, tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a suitable candidate for ornamental and edible landscaping.

This variety is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and its culinary uses.
