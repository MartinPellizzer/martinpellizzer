Loranthus brieyi

Loranthus brieyi, commonly known as the Brazilian mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of host trees, primarily in the Amazon rainforest.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using sunlight absorbed by its leaves. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces white or pink flowers that attract pollinators like bees and birds. Its berries are red or yellow and contain seeds that are dispersed by animals.

Loranthus brieyi plays a role in the ecosystem by providing food for wildlife and contributing to the biodiversity of its forest habitat.
