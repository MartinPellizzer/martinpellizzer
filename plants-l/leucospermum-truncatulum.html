Leucospermum truncatulum

Leucospermum truncatulum, commonly known as the yellow star jelly, is a member of the Proteaceae family native to South Africa.

It is a shrub that produces striking yellow flowers with a star-like appearance, which bloom in spring and summer. The plant has needle-like leaves and is adapted to grow in well-drained, sandy soils. It is often found in fynbos vegetation and is valued for its ornamental qualities.

The species is also known for its ability to thrive in full sun and is relatively drought-tolerant.
