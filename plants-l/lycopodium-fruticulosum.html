Lycopodium fruticulosum

Lycopodium fruticulosum, commonly known as the clubmoss, is a small, evergreen plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its spore-bearing cones and its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its clustered, needle-like leaves and a creeping or upright growth habit. Lycopodium fruticulosum is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Botanically, it plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms.
