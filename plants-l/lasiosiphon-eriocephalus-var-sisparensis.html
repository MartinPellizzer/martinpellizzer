Lasiosiphon eriocephalus var. sisparensis

Lasiosiphon eriocephalus var. sisparensis, commonly known as the Sisparensis morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers, which bloom in the early morning and close by midday. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces tendrils that help it climb and support its growth. Its botanical features include a hairy stem and small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in cool, moist environments.
