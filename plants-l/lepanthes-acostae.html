Lepanthes acostae

Lepanthes acostae, commonly known as the Acosta's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees in humid, tropical environments. The plant features showy, fragrant flowers that are often pale green or yellow with red markings. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Lepanthes acostae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
