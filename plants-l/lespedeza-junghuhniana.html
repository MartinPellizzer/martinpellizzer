Lespedeza junghuhniana

Lespedeza junghuhniana, commonly known as Chinese strangleweed, is a perennial shrub native to East Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow to reddish-pink flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, which can make it invasive in certain regions. It has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in a variety of soil types and environmental conditions.

Lespedeza junghuhniana is also valued for its potential in erosion control and as a forage plant for livestock.
