Lepanthes bibarbullata

Lepanthes bibarbullata, commonly known as the barred twayblade, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a slender, leafless stem that produces small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist habitats such as forests and woodlands. Its flowers are characterized by their unique, two-lobed structure and lack of a typical orchid lip.

Lepanthes bibarbullata is known for its delicate appearance and is often found in association with mycorrhizal fungi.
