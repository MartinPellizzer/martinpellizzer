Lindsaea terrae-reginae

Lindsaea terrae-reginae, commonly known as the queen's earth fern, is a rare and delicate epiphytic fern native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on tree trunks or rocks. The plant features intricate, delicate fronds with a unique, lace-like appearance. Its reproductive structures, called sporangia, are clustered in specialized leaf axils.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is highly valued in botanical conservation efforts.
