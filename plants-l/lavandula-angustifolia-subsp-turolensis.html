Lavandula angustifolia subsp. turolensis

Lavandula angustifolia subsp.

turolensis, commonly known as the Turol lavender, is a subspecies native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, gray-green leaves.

The plant produces spikes of fragrant, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

This subspecies is valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in essential oil production and ornamental horticulture.
