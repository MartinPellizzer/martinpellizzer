Lythrum alatum var. breviflorum

Lythrum alatum var. breviflorum, commonly known as narrowflower redroot, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, erect stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, showy pink to purple flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and woodland edges.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species for wetland restoration and ecological studies.
