Lophatherum gracile var. elatum

Lophatherum gracile var. elatum, commonly known as the tall bulbostachyum, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces inflorescences of small, spike-like clusters that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
