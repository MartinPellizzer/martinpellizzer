Limnia cuprea

Limnia cuprea, commonly known as the copperhead water flea, is a small aquatic crustacean belonging to the order Cladocera.

It is characterized by its translucent exoskeleton that often appears reddish or copper-colored due to pigmentation. This species is typically found in freshwater habitats such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. Limnia cuprea plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by serving as a primary food source for larger organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality and a healthy ecosystem.
