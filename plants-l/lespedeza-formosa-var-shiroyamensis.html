Lespedeza formosa var. shiroyamensis

Lespedeza formosa var. shiroyamensis, commonly known as the Japanese lespedeza, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forests and stream banks. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

However, it can also be invasive in certain regions outside its native range.
