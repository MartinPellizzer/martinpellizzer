Lobelia siphilitica var. bollii

Lobelia siphilitica var. bollii, commonly known as the Great Blue Lobelia, is a striking perennial wildflower native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, tubular blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its stems are upright with a fibrous texture.

This variant is notable for its vibrant color and ecological importance in wetland ecosystems.
