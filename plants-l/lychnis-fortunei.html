Lychnis fortunei

Lychnis fortunei, commonly known as the Chinese firecracker, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
