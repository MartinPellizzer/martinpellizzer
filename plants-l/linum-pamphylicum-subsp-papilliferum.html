Linum pamphylicum subsp. papilliferum

Linum pamphylicum subsp.

papilliferum, commonly known as the papillate flax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This subspecies is distinguished by the presence of papillae on its leaves, giving it its common name.
