Leea talbotii

Leea talbotii, commonly known as the Talbot's leeah, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Leeaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on short pedicels. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely commercially available.
