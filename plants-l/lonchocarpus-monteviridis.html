Lonchocarpus monteviridis

Lonchocarpus monteviridis, commonly known as the greenheart tree, is a tropical leguminous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green heartwood, which is highly valued for its durability and resistance to decay. The plant typically grows as a large evergreen tree, reaching heights of up to 30 meters. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces yellow flowers followed by pod-like fruits.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in wet, humid environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.
