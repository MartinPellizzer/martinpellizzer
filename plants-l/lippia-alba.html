Lippia alba

Lippia alba, commonly known as white verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, white to light purple flowers. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Lippia alba is also valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
