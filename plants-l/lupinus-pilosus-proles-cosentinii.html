Lupinus pilosus proles cosentinii

Lupinus pilosus proles cosentinii, commonly known as the Cosentin's lupine, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically trifoliate, with small, rounded leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Lupinus pilosus proles cosentinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
