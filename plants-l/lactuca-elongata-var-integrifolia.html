Lactuca elongata var. integrifolia

Lactuca elongata var. integrifolia, commonly known as the prickly lettuce, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and leaves that are deeply lobed with wavy edges. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been introduced to other regions.

The species is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
