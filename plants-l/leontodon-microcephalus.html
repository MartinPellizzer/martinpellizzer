Leontodon microcephalus

Leontodon microcephalus, commonly known as the small-headed bellflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads composed of many small florets. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually pale blue or white, with a distinctive bell-shaped appearance.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
