Liparis cyperifolia

Liparis cyperifolia, commonly known as the grass-leaf orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and delicate, often fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky areas. Its inflorescence consists of a raceme of small, greenish-white to pale yellow flowers.

Liparis cyperifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with appropriate humidity and light conditions.
