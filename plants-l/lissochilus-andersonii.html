Lissochilus andersonii

Lissochilus andersonii, commonly known as the Anderson's rock orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and unique floral structure. The plant typically produces small, intricate flowers with a distinctive coloration pattern, often featuring a combination of white, yellow, and green hues. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
