Lavandula multifida f. glabrifolia

Lavandula multifida f. glabrifolia, commonly known as the smoothleaf lavender, is a cultivar of the lavender plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are smooth and slightly hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces spikes of purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is often grown for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.

This variety is well-suited for rock gardens and dry, well-drained soils.
