Leontopodium fauriei var. fauriei

Leontopodium fauriei var. fauriei, commonly known as the edelweiss, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly white bracts that surround the small yellow flowers, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in high altitudes with cool, dry climates and well-drained soils. Its leaves are basal, rosette-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Due to its habitat and appearance, it is often associated with resilience and purity in cultural symbolism.
