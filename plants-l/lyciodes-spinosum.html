Lyciodes spinosum

Lyciodes spinosum, commonly known as the spiny lycopus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its small, purple or blue flowers and hairy, spiny leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
