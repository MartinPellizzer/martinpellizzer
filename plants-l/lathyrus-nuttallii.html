Lathyrus nuttallii

Lathyrus nuttallii, commonly known as Nuttall's vetch, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple, often appearing in clusters. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are adapted for dispersal by wind or animals.

Lathyrus nuttallii is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and open woodlands.
