Lotus aduncus

Lotus aduncus, commonly known as the slender lotus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, pale pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, often near water bodies. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while its stems are slender and often submerged.

The seeds of Lotus aduncus are enclosed in pods and can remain viable for several years in the soil.
