Larix dahurica var. japonica

Larix dahurica var. japonica, commonly known as Japanese larch, is a deciduous conifer native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and its ability to thrive in cold climates. The tree produces small, spherical cones that release winged seeds, aiding in its dispersal. Its needle-like leaves turn golden in autumn before falling, contributing to its seasonal beauty.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its elegant form and adaptability to various soil types.
