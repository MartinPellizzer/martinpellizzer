Loxogramme blumeana

Loxogramme blumeana, commonly known as the Blume's water clover, is a small aquatic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, floating leaves that are divided into small, clover-like segments. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds and marshes. Its rhizomes are submerged, anchoring the plant while absorbing nutrients from the water.

Loxogramme blumeana plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and small aquatic life.
