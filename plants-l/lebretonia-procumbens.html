Lebretonia procumbens

Lebretonia procumbens, commonly known as the creeping lebletonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial that typically forms dense mats of foliage. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often covered in fine hairs.

Lebretonia procumbens is adapted to wetland environments and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.
