Lepanthes branchifera

Lepanthes branchifera, commonly known as the branched-leaf orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has a compact growth habit with a pseudobulb and produces multiple inflorescences along its stems. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Lepanthes branchifera thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.
