Lavatera acerifolia var. hariensis

Lavatera acerifolia var. hariensis, commonly known as the Kashmiri rose or Kashmiri lavatera, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of Jammu and Kashmir.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, often with a subtle fragrance. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are hairy and resemble those of maple trees, giving it its species name. It thrives in cool, temperate climates and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
