Lamium ochroleucum

Lamium ochroleucum, commonly known as yellow archangel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of small, tubular, yellow flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks. It is a member of the mint family (Lamiaceae), which is characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves.

Lamium ochroleucum is also known for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
