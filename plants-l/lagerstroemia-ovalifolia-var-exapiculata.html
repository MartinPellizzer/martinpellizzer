Lagerstroemia ovalifolia var. exapiculata

Lagerstroemia ovalifolia var. exapiculata, commonly known as the broad-leafed crape myrtle, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its oval-shaped, glossy green leaves that turn vibrant red or orange in the fall. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in summer, typically in shades of pink, red, or white. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
