Liparis scaposa

Liparis scaposa, commonly known as the scapose orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its erect, leafy stems and clusters of small, greenish or white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its roots are often mycorrhizal, forming symbiotic relationships with fungi to enhance nutrient uptake.

Liparis scaposa is known for its delicate appearance and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
