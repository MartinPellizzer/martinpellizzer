Lallemantia azurea

Lallemantia azurea, commonly known as blue lantana, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It produces small, clustered blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Lallemantia azurea is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
