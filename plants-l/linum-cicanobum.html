Linum cicanobum

Linum cicanobum, commonly known as the common flax.

It is a herbaceous plant native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa. The plant features slender, upright stems with alternate leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy. Its flowers are blue or purple, with five petals and a prominent central stamen.

Linum cicanobum is often found in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed soils, and it is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
