Linum gallicum var. aureum

Linum gallicum var. aureum, commonly known as the golden flax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces slender, branched stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and slightly hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

The golden flax is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in textile production.
