Lycoseris triplinervia

Lycoseris triplinervia, commonly known as the three-veined lycoperd, is a species of fungi that belongs to the family Lycoperdaceae.

It is characterized by its unique cap shape and the presence of a distinct ring around the stem. The fruiting bodies are typically small and resemble mushrooms, with a smooth, often reddish-brown cap. This species is often found in grassy areas and is associated with decaying organic matter.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the puffball family.
