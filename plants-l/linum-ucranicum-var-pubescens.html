Linum ucranicum var. pubescens

Linum ucranicum var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy flax, is a variant of the flax plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Linaceae and is closely related to other flax species used for fiber and oil production.
