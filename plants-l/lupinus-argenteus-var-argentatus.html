Lupinus argenteus var. argentatus

Lupinus argenteus var. argentatus, commonly known as silver lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-white foliage and sparsely branched stems. The plant produces showy, pea-like flowers in shades of purple or blue, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value and nitrogen-fixing ability, it is cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
