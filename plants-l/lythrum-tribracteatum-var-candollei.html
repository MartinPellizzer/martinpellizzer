Lythrum tribracteatum var. candollei

Lythrum tribracteatum var. candollei, commonly known as the purple loosestrife, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy spikes of purple to pink flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist environments such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and ornamental horticulture.
