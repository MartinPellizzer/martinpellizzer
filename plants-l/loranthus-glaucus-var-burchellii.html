Loranthus glaucus var. burchellii

Loranthus glaucus var. burchellii, commonly known as the Burchell's mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that attaches itself to the branches of its host trees.

It belongs to the family Loranthaceae and is characterized by its dense, glaucous foliage and small, white or greenish flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are often consumed by birds, which then disperse its seeds. It is native to southern Africa and is often found on acacia and other hardwood trees.

Its parasitic nature means it draws nutrients and water from its host, sometimes causing stress or damage to the host plant.
