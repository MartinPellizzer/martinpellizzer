Lupinus arbustus subsp. arbustus

Lupinus arbustus subsp.

arbustus, commonly known as the bush lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers.

The plant has compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces legume pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas. Lupinus arbustus subsp.

arbustus is valued for its ornamental appeal and nitrogen-fixing ability, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
