Landolphia owariensis

Landolphia owariensis, commonly known as the African flame bean, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces large, woody pods that contain numerous seeds embedded in a sticky, resinous pulp. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
