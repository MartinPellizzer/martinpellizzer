Lathyrus laxiflorus subsp. angustifolius

Lathyrus laxiflorus subsp.

angustifolius, commonly known as narrow-leaved grass vetch, is a perennial legume native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, often forming dense colonies.

The plant features slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its trifoliate leaves consist of three narrow, lance-shaped leaflets.

This subspecies is valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for livestock.
