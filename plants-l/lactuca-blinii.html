Lactuca blinii

Lactuca blinii, commonly known as the Siberian endive, is a perennial plant native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern Russia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and lettuces, and is characterized by its rosette growth habit. The plant produces a single, large, purple or green flower head that blooms in late summer. Its leaves are typically curly and have a slightly bitter taste, similar to other endive varieties.

Lactuca blinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible leaves in traditional cuisine.
