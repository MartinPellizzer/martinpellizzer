Lupinus concinnus var. desertorum

Lupinus concinnus var. desertorum, commonly known as the desert lupine, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in well-drained soils, often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats. The plant features erect, spiny stems and clusters of pea-like blue or purple flowers. Its leaves are compound, consisting of small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Lupinus concinnus var. desertorum is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.
