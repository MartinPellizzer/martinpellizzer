Limonium sinuatum subsp. bonduellei

Limonium sinuatum subsp.

bonduellei, commonly known as the Bonduelle's sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of blue to purple, bell-shaped flowers.

The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in saline and well-drained conditions. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slight waxy coating.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
