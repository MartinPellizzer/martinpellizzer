Lyperia filicaulis

Lyperia filicaulis, commonly known as the slender feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or along riverbanks.

Lyperia filicaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, feathery appearance.
