Loranthus incarnatiflorus

Loranthus incarnatiflorus, commonly known as the red-flowered mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees.

It belongs to the family Loranthaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia. The plant has small, leathery leaves and produces bright red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds. Its parasitic nature allows it to derive water and nutrients from its host, while still performing photosynthesis.

The species is often found in secondary forests and is valued in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
