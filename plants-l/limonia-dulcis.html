Limonia dulcis

Limonia dulcis, commonly known as the sour orange, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant white flowers and oval, glossy leaves. The fruit is typically small, round, and has a thick, yellowish rind with a sour taste. It is often used in culinary applications, particularly in jams and marmalades, due to its high pectin content.

The plant is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
