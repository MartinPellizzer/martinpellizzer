Lobivia taratensis

Lobivia taratensis, commonly known as the Taratensis Lobivia, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains in South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and spiny stems. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure to flourish.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
