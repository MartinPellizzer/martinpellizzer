Lonchocarpus brenesii

Lonchocarpus brenesii, commonly known as the Caribbean coral tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive coral-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain seeds enclosed in a hard, woody covering. It is often found in coastal areas and can thrive in sandy or limestone soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
