Leochilus herbaceus

Leochilus herbaceus, commonly known as the woolly bellflower, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and gardeners alike.
