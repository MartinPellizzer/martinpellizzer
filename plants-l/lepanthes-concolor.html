Lepanthes concolor

Lepanthes concolor, commonly known as the plain white orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments. The plant features a slender rhizome and produces delicate, white or pale green flowers with a distinctive, elongated shape. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern.

Lepanthes concolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
