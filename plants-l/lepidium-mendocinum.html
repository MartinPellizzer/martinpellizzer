Lepidium mendocinum

Lepidium mendocinum, commonly known as Mendo cress, is a flowering plant native to California.

It is a low-growing annual herb with small, white or greenish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has slender, branched stems and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often hairy, with a slightly succulent texture.

Lepidium mendocinum is valued for its peppery flavor and is sometimes used in culinary applications.
