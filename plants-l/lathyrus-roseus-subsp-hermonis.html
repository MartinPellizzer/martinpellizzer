Lathyrus roseus subsp. hermonis

Lathyrus roseus subsp.

hermonis, commonly known as the Hermon flaxvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of pea-like flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep violet.

The plant produces small, oblong pods containing several seeds, and its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or calcareous habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
