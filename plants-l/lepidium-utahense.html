Lepidium utahense

Lepidium utahense, commonly known as Utah peppergrass, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many familiar plants such as mustard and cabbage. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small white or pale yellow flowers and has a distinctive, branched stem.

Its leaves are usually linear and covered in fine, hair-like structures, which help reduce water loss.
