Loranthus garcianus

Loranthus garcianus, commonly known as the Garcianus mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while still performing photosynthesis using its own leaves. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are often clustered together, and its berries are typically red or orange, containing sticky seeds. Loranthus garcianus plays a role in ecological systems by providing food for birds and other animals.

Its parasitic nature and unique reproductive strategies make it an interesting subject in botanical studies.
