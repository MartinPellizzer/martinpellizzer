Lupinus subvexus var. fluviatilis

Lupinus subvexus var. fluviatilis, commonly known as the river lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils along riverbanks and stream sides.

Lupinus subvexus var. fluviatilis plays a role in nitrogen fixation, improving soil fertility in its native habitat.
