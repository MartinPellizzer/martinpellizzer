Lycopersicon esculentum provar. finiens

Lycopersicon esculentum provar. finiens, commonly known as the cultivated tomato, is a herbaceous annual plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems, lobed leaves, and clusters of small white flowers. The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that are rich in nutrients and widely used in culinary applications. Its botanical classification has been revised over time, with the genus Lycopersicon now considered part of the genus Solanum.

This variety is a key example of domestication in agriculture, showcasing significant genetic diversity due to selective breeding.
