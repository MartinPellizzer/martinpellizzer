Leontodon hispidus var. pseudocrispus

Leontodon hispidus var. pseudocrispus, commonly known as the hairy leopard's-bane, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that are typically arranged in a dense, rounded cluster. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its name reflects both its physical appearance and its historical use in traditional medicine.
