Lobivia elongata

Lobivia elongata, commonly known as the elongated lobivia, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual element to its desert habitat. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, a common adaptation to minimize water loss in arid environments.

Lobivia elongata is well-suited for cultivation in dry climates and requires minimal care once established.
