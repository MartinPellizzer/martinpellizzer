Linum corymbiferum var. parviflorum

Linum corymbiferum var. parviflorum, commonly known as the dwarf flax or parviflorum flax, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous small seeds.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal practices.
