Lotus caucasicus

Lotus caucasicus, commonly known as the Caucasian lotus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in summer, followed by flat, bean-like pods containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
