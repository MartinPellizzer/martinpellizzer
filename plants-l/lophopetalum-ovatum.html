Lophopetalum ovatum

Lophopetalum ovatum, commonly known as the Texas mountain-laurel, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter, sometimes white, underside. The plant produces fragrant, showy clusters of pale yellow to white flowers in the spring. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, brown seeds.

Lophopetalum ovatum is well adapted to full sun and well-drained soils, making it a hardy and ornamental plant in native landscapes.
