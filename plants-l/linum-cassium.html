Linum cassium

Linum cassium, commonly known as yellow flax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Linum cassium is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant color and delicate appearance.
