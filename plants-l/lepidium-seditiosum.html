Lepidium seditiosum

Lepidium seditiosum, commonly known as the "seditious cress," is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is a small annual herb that typically grows in disturbed soils and moist environments. The plant has slender, branched stems and small, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and borne in clusters, and it produces small, rounded pods that contain several seeds.

Lepidium seditiosum is native to regions of Europe and Asia but has been introduced to other parts of the world.
