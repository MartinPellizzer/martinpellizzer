Leptocoryphium villaregalis

Leptocoryphium villaregalis, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces small, clustered blooms with intricate patterns on its petals, which are adapted for pollination by specific insects. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
