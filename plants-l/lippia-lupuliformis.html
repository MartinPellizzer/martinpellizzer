Lippia lupuliformis

Lippia lupuliformis, commonly known as the bitterleaf sage, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered white to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun, often found in desert and scrubland habitats.

Its leaves are used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported medicinal properties, and it is also valued as a source of nectar for pollinators.
