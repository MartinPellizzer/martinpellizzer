Lamium truncatum

Lamium truncatum, commonly known as purple deadnettle, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with purple veins and produces clusters of small, two-lipped flowers in shades of purple and white. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and gardens. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, velvety texture.

Lamium truncatum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in herbal medicine.
