Lithospermum circumscissum

Lithospermum circumscissum, commonly known as the scarlet pimpernel, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its small, bright red flowers that open in the morning and close in the evening. The plant has slender, branching stems and hairy, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. Its flowers are typically borne in clusters and are pollinated by insects.

Lithospermum circumscissum is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils.
