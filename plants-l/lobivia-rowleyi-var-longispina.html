Lobivia rowleyi var. longispina

Lobivia rowleyi var. longispina, commonly known as the Long-spined Lobivia, is a compact, short-lived perennial cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It features a dense rosette of cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces tall, branching inflorescences that bear yellow to orange flowers during the spring and early summer. Its spines are long and slender, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is well adapted to harsh, dry environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.
