Linum suffruticosum var. ramosissimum

Linum suffruticosum var. ramosissimum, commonly known as the hairy flax or hairy linum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub-like species characterized by its branched stems and hairy foliage. The plant produces small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its fibrous stems and leaves have been used historically for textile and medicinal purposes.
