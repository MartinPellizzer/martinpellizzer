Lobivia longiseta

Lobivia longiseta, commonly known as the long-spined lobivia, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rounded shape and spiny, ribbed stems. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, a feature that helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

Lobivia longiseta is well adapted to drought conditions and thrives in full sun with minimal water.
