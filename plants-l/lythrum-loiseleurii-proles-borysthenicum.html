Lythrum loiseleurii proles borysthenicum

Lythrum loiseleurii proles borysthenicum, commonly known as the Borysthenian lythrum, is a herbaceous plant native to the steppes of southern Russia and Ukraine.

It belongs to the Lythraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are lanceolate and often covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in dense, terminal spikes.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations and historical significance in botanical studies.
