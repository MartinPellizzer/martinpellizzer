Lippia contermina var. hirsuta

Lippia contermina var. hirsuta, commonly known as hairy lippia, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid climates.
