Lampra

Lampra, commonly known as the sacred lotus, is a aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that emerge above the water surface. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface. Its roots are submerged, anchoring it in the muddy bottom of ponds and lakes.

Lampra is known for its symbolic significance in various cultures and is also valued for its medicinal properties.
