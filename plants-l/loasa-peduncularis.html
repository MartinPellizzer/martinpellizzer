Loasa peduncularis

Loasa peduncularis, commonly known as the slender loasa, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in the spring. Its botanical features include hairy stems and pinnately compound leaves, which are typical of the Loasaceae family.

Loasa peduncularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive flowers.
