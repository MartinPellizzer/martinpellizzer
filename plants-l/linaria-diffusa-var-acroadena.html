Linaria diffusa var. acroadena

Linaria diffusa var. acroadena, commonly known as the narrow-leaved toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and delicate, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slight hairy texture. This plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
