Leontodon kerneri

Leontodon kerneri, commonly known as the Kern's false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing a compact growth habit with basal leaves. Its yellow flowers bloom in spring, and the plant produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal.

Leontodon kerneri is often found in alpine meadows and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
