Lippia alba f. scabra

Lippia alba f. scabra, commonly known as rough lemon verbena, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant leaves and small, clustered white flowers. The plant has rough, hairy foliage, which gives it its specific epithet "scabra.". It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often used in herbal medicine for its purported calming and digestive properties.

Its essential oils are also utilized in aromatherapy and as a flavoring agent in food and beverages.
