Lepidadenia tuberculata

Lepidadenia tuberculata, commonly known as the African milk bush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its woody stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are opposite and oval-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Lepidadenia tuberculata is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought conditions.
