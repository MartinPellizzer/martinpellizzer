Lisianthius

Lisianthius, commonly known as the everlasting or everlasting daisy, is a genus of flowering plants in the daisy family, Asteraceae.

These plants are native to South America and are characterized by their showy, daisy-like flowers that remain intact for a long time after blooming. The flowers typically consist of a central disk surrounded by ray florets, which give the plant its distinctive appearance. Lisianthius species are often cultivated for their ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
