Lasiandra strigillosa

Lasiandra strigillosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of various host trees.

It possesses specialized structures called haustoria that allow it to penetrate the host's vascular system and extract water and nutrients. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are usually covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences.

The fruit is a capsule that releases tiny seeds, which can adhere to the bark of host trees, facilitating further colonization.
