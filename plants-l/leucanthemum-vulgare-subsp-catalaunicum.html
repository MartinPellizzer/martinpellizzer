Leucanthemum vulgare subsp. catalaunicum

Leucanthemum vulgare subsp.

catalaunicum, commonly known as the Catalan marguerite, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula. It features a basal rosette of silver-gray, hairy leaves and produces daisy-like flowers with white ray florets surrounding a yellow disk.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to Mediterranean climates. It blooms from late spring to early summer, adding ornamental value to gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family.
