Lagenocarpus adamantinus var. bracteosus

Lagenocarpus adamantinus var. bracteosus, commonly known as the bracteose lagenocarpus, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
