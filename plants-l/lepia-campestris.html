Lepia campestris

Lepia campestris, commonly known as the field lepia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions.

It features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in umbels. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Lepia campestris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
