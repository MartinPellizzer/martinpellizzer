Licuala acuminata

Licuala acuminata, commonly known as the broadleaf pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, fibrous fruits.

Licuala acuminata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber.
