Lathyrus venosus var. californicus

Lathyrus venosus var. californicus, commonly known as the California larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive spurred petals and climbing or trailing growth habit. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, compound, and often have a veiny appearance, which contributes to its scientific name.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.
