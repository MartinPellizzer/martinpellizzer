Lobelia divergens

Lobelia divergens, commonly known as the spreading lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features delicate, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade.

Lobelia divergens is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
