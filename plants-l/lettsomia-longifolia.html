Lettsomia longifolia

Lettsomia longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf lettuce, is a herbaceous plant native to certain regions of South America.

It features slender, erect stems that are often covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are elongated and slightly pointed, with a smooth texture and a mild, peppery flavor. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family.

It is often cultivated for its edible leaves and has a preference for moist, well-drained soils.
