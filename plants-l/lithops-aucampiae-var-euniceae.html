Lithops aucampiae var. euniceae

Lithops aucampiae var. euniceae, commonly known as the "Cape Town Lithops," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its pair of fleshy, leaf-like structures that resemble stones, helping it blend into its arid environment. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers during the summer months. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, requiring minimal water to survive.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
