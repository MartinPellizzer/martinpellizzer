Lampranthus mutatus

Lampranthus mutatus, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and birds. The plant has fleshy, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that help it store water. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its drought tolerance and vibrant floral display make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
