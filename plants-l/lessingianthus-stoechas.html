Lessingianthus stoechas

Lessingianthus stoechas, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking purple to violet petals and a prominent central cone-like structure. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas and thrives in well-drained soil. Its flowers bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
