Lupinus diversalpicola

Lupinus diversalpicola, commonly known as the alpine lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to violet flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds. Lupinus diversalpicola is adapted to high-altitude environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators in alpine ecosystems.
