Ligularia longifolia

Ligularia longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf ligularia, is a herbaceous perennial native to East Asia.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive yellowish-green color and prominent veins. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers in late summer, which are clustered in terminal inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens with appropriate humidity and soil conditions.
