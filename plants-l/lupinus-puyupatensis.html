Lupinus puyupatensis

Lupinus puyupatensis, commonly known as the Puyupatensis lupine, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of showy purple or blue flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces elongated pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments, often found in mountainous areas with cool, temperate climates.

Lupinus puyupatensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential ecological benefits in nitrogen fixation.
