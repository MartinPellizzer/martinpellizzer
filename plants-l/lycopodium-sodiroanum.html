Lycopodium sodiroanum

Lycopodium sodiroanum, commonly known as the African clubmoss, is a member of the Lycopodiaceae family and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is a small, herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its erect, branching stems and small, scale-like leaves. It produces spores in cone-like structures called strobila, which are typically found at the tips of its branches.

Lycopodium sodiroanum plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
