Lathyrus pannonicus var. hispanicus

Lathyrus pannonicus var. hispanicus, commonly known as the Spanish vetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pale blue to violet. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is cultivated in gardens and used in ecological restoration projects.
