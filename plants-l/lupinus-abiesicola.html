Lupinus abiesicola

Lupinus abiesicola, commonly known as the alpine lupine, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high elevations. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Lupinus abiesicola is adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine meadows and subalpine forests.
