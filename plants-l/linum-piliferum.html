Linum piliferum

Linum piliferum, commonly known as the hairy flax, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems covered in fine, hairy texture. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and also bear a hairy coating.

Linum piliferum is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
