Lindsaea sectorifolia

Lindsaea sectorifolia, commonly known as the segmented lindsaea, is a species of epiphytic fern native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, linear leaves that are often divided into segments, giving it a unique, lace-like appearance. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are borne on specialized leaf tips called indusia.

Lindsaea sectorifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its native habitats.
