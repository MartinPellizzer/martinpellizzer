Languas densispicata

Languas densispicata, commonly known as the dense-hair plant, is a species characterized by its dense covering of fine, hairy structures across its foliage and stems.

It typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, where it has adapted to withstand drought conditions. The leaves are often small and narrow, reducing water loss through transpiration. Its flowers, when present, are usually small and clustered, often in inflorescences that enhance pollination efficiency.
