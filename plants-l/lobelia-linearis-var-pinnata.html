Lobelia linearis var. pinnata

Lobelia linearis var. pinnata, commonly known as the narrowleaf lobelia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, linear leaves that are often arranged in a pinnate pattern, giving it its varietal name. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
