Lachnagrostis barbuligera

Lachnagrostis barbuligera, commonly known as the hairy grass or silky grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its fine, hairy leaves and slender, tufted growth habit. Native to regions of Europe and Asia, this plant thrives in moist, temperate climates. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Lachnagrostis barbuligera is often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along riverbanks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
