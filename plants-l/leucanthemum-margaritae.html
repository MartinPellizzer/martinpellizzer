Leucanthemum margaritae

Leucanthemum margaritae, commonly known as the pearlbutton thistle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and features dense, silvery-white foliage. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
