Leersia hexandra subsp. grandiflora

Leersia hexandra subsp.

grandiflora, commonly known as the broad-leaved leersia, is a grass-like plant native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and wetlands.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a native, low-maintenance plant.
