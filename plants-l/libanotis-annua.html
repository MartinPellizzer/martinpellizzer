Libanotis annua

Libanotis annua, commonly known as the annual libanotis, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Libanotis annua is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in full sun to partial shade.
