Lavandula officinalis

Lavandula officinalis, commonly known as English lavender, is a flowering plant in the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its aromatic, spike-like inflorescences of purple or pink flowers. The plant has narrow, gray-green, aromatic leaves that are often used in herbal remedies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and aromatherapy.

Lavandula officinalis is valued for its essential oils, which contain compounds like linalool and lavandin, known for their calming and antiseptic properties.
