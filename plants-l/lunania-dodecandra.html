Lunania dodecandra

Lunania dodecandra, commonly known as the moonflower, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant white flowers that open at night and close by morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, round fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its nocturnal blooming habit, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens.
