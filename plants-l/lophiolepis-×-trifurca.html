Lophiolepis × trifurca

Lophiolepis × trifurca, commonly known as the three-branch mountain orchid, is a hybrid species native to South Africa.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often with a red or purple lip. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
