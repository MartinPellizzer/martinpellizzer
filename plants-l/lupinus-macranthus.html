Lupinus macranthus

Lupinus macranthus, commonly known as the large-flowered lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and its striking clusters of violet to purple flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets and produces long, slender pods that contain several seeds. Lupinus macranthus thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in dry, open habitats.

It plays an important role in nitrogen fixation, benefiting the surrounding ecosystem.
