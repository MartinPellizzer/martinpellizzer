Larix pyramidalis

Larix pyramidalis, commonly known as the Japanese larch, is a deciduous conifer native to Japan.

It is characterized by its pyramidal shape and needle-like leaves that turn golden in autumn before falling. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping and garden use.
