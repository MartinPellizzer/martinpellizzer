Leucas masukuensis

Leucas masukuensis, commonly known as African feverfew, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

The small yellow flowers appear in clusters and bloom during the warmer months.
