Leucanthemum montanum subsp. atratum

Leucanthemum montanum subsp.

atratum, commonly known as the black-eyed Susan, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its bright yellow ray florets surrounding a dark, central disk, giving it its common name.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It produces small, daisy-like flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn.

The subspecies is noted for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
