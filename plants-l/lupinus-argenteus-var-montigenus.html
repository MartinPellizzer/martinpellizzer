Lupinus argenteus var. montigenus

Lupinus argenteus var. montigenus, commonly known as silver lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-blue foliage and spires of violet or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as sagebrush steppe and mountain slopes. It has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for ecological restoration.
