Lippia acuminata

Lippia acuminata, commonly known as Mexican blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Central America and northern South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, sunny environments such as grasslands and disturbed areas. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slight aromatic scent.

Lippia acuminata is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
