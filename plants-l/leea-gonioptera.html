Leea gonioptera

Leea gonioptera, commonly known as the warty leaf vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Leeaceae and is characterized by its distinctive warty, glandular structures on its leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by red, fleshy fruits containing several seeds. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped with a rough texture, and the stems are often hairy.

Leea gonioptera is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
