Loranthus betchei

Loranthus betchei, commonly known as the African mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on a variety of host trees, primarily in sub-Saharan Africa.

It belongs to the family Loranthaceae and has a hemiparasitic lifestyle, meaning it derives some of its nutrients from the host plant while also performing photosynthesis. The plant has small, opposite leaves and produces white or yellowish flowers that are often clustered together. Its fruit is a small, fleshy capsule that contains seeds covered in a sticky substance to aid in attachment to the host.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitats by influencing host tree growth and providing food for various bird species.
