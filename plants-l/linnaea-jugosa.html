Linnaea jugosa

Linnaea jugosa, commonly known as the twinflower, is a small evergreen shrub native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It features opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green, with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, fragrant, pinkish-white flowers in pairs, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Linnaea jugosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
