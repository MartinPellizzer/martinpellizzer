Lasiorrhiza glabriuscula

Lasiorrhiza glabriuscula, commonly known as the smoothleaf sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, hairless stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Its fibrous roots help stabilize soil, making it a valuable plant for erosion control in desert landscapes.
