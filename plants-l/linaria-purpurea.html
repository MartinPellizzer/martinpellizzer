Linaria purpurea

Linaria purpurea, commonly known as the purple toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and vibrant purple to reddish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its spread in suitable habitats. Linaria purpurea prefers well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or sandy environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking colorful, low-maintenance plants.
