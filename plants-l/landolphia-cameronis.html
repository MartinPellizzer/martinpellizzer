Landolphia cameronis

Landolphia cameronis, commonly known as the Cameron's morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in the morning, hence its common name. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Botanically, it belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other flowering plants with similar morphological features.
