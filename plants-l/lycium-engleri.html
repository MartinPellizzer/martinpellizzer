Lycium engleri

Lycium engleri, commonly known as the Chinese boxthorn, is a shrub native to China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, red berries that are edible when ripe. The plant belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes and potatoes, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional Chinese medicine. Its leaves are ovate and hairy, while its flowers are typically yellow or orange.
