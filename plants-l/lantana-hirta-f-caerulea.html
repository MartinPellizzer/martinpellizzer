Lantana hirta f. caerulea

Lantana hirta f. caerulea, commonly known as blue lantana, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The leaves are typically hairy and opposite, providing a textured appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

This plant is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
