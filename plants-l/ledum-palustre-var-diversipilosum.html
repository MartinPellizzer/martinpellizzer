Ledum palustre var. diversipilosum

Ledum palustre var. diversipilosum, commonly known as the hairy Labrador tea, is a variant of the Labrador tea plant native to northern regions of North America.

It is a low-growing evergreen shrub characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves that are covered in fine, hairy hairs. The plant produces small, white, clustered flowers in late spring to early summer, which are followed by red berries. It thrives in acidic, wet soils and is often found in bogs and swampy areas.

This variety is notable for its unique foliage texture and its role in supporting local wildlife.
