Lobivia aurea var. callochrysea

Lobivia aurea var. callochrysea, commonly known as the golden leaf lobivia, is a succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It features a compact, cylindrical stem with thick, fleshy leaves that are typically a vibrant green or yellowish-green. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the warmer months. It is well adapted to drought conditions, storing water in its leaves and stems.

Due to its striking appearance and ease of care, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and succulent collections.
