Lachenalia nervosa

Lachenalia nervosa, commonly known as the narrow-leafed lachenalia, is a South African bulbous plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with a dormant summer period and active growth during the cooler, wetter months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Lachenalia nervosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
