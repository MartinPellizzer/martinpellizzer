Linum tenuifolium var. jimenezii

Linum tenuifolium var. jimenezii, commonly known as Jimenez's flax, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and delicate blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
