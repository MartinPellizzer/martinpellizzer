Lippia elegans var. macrophylla

Lippia elegans var. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved lippia, is a perennial herb native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its aromatic, opposite leaves that are typically broad and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in terminal clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping and ornamental gardening.

Its essential oils are often used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.
