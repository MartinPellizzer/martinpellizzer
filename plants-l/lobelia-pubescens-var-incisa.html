Lobelia pubescens var. incisa

Lobelia pubescens var. incisa, commonly known as the hairy lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces spikes of small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Lobelia pubescens var. incisa is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
