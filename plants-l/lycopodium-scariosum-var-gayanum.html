Lycopodium scariosum var. gayanum

Lycopodium scariosum var. gayanum, commonly known as the Sri Lankan clubmoss, is a small evergreen plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces spore-bearing cones called strobili. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are narrow and spirally arranged, with a waxy surface that aids in water retention.

The species is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
