Lupinus densiflorus var. mcgregorii

Lupinus densiflorus var. mcgregorii, commonly known as McGregor's lupine, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, showy clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has slender, branching stems and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky slopes.

McGregor's lupine is a member of the legume family and plays a role in nitrogen fixation in its ecosystem.
