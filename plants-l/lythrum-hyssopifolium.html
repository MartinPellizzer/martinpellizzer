Lythrum hyssopifolium

Lythrum hyssopifolium, commonly known as rosepink or purple loosestrife, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, herbaceous growth habit and produces showy, spike-like inflorescences of pink or purple flowers. The leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other regions, sometimes becoming invasive.
