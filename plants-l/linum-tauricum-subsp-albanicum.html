Linum tauricum subsp. albanicum

Linum tauricum subsp.

albanicum, commonly known as the Albanian flax, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and blue to purple flowers.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oval seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
