Lamium amplexicaule var. orientale

Lamium amplexicaule var. orientale, commonly known as Persian shield, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often adorned with striking purple markings. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of pink, red, and violet, typically blooming from spring to early summer. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its foliage, which remains attractive throughout the growing season.
