Lycopsis mollis

Lycopsis mollis, commonly known as the soft lycopsis, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal and culinary uses. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are soft to the touch and often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, clustered flowers that are typically purple or violet in color, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

Lycopsis mollis is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices.
