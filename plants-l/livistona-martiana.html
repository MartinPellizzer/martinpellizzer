Livistona martiana

Livistona martiana, commonly known as the Australian fan palm, is a species of palm native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter. The plant typically grows as a solitary tree with a short trunk and a crown of broad, feather-like fronds. It thrives in coastal and subtropical regions, often found in sandy soils near the shoreline.

Livistona martiana is valued for its ornamental appearance and is commonly cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
