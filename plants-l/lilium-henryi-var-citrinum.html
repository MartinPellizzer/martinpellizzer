Lilium henryi var. citrinum

Lilium henryi var. citrinum, commonly known as the yellow-flowered fritillary, is a rare and striking variant of the Henry's lily.

It is native to the mountainous regions of western China, where it thrives in cool, humid climates. The plant features large, showy flowers with a distinctive yellow-orange coloration, which sets it apart from other lily species. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine, silvery down.

This variant is highly valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by specialty growers.
