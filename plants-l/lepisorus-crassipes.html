Lepisorus crassipes

Lepisorus crassipes, commonly known as the broad-leaved rock fern, is a perennial fern native to East Asia.

It typically grows on rocky substrates in shaded, moist environments. The plant features rhizomatous growth and has broad, leathery, lance-shaped leaves. Its spore-bearing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Lepisorus crassipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
