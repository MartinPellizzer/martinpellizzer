Leycestria

Leycestria, commonly known as the white-leafed mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that primarily grows on the branches of host trees.

Leycestria is typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives in warm, humid climates.

Its unique ecological role as both a parasite and a food source for wildlife makes it an interesting subject in botanical studies.
