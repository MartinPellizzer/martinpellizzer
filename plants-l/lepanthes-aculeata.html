Lepanthes aculeata

Lepanthes aculeata, commonly known as the spiky orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, spiky appearance, with elongated, pointed leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale green, often arranged in a raceme. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated by enthusiasts but remains relatively rare in the wild.
