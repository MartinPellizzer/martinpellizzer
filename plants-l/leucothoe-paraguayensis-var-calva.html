Leucothoe paraguayensis var. calva

Leucothoe paraguayensis var. calva, commonly known as the Paraguay waxleaf, is a species of evergreen shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by bright red berries in the fall. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and seasonal color changes.

This variety is particularly valued for its winter interest and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
