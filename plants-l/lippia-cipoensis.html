Lippia cipoensis

Lippia cipoensis, commonly known as Cipo mint, is a perennial herb native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, aromatic leaves and small white to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. It is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional remedies.

Its essential oils contain compounds like limonene and pinocarvyl acetate, which contribute to its distinctive fragrance and potential therapeutic uses.
