Leucanthemum vulgare var. alpicola

Leucanthemum vulgare var. alpicola, commonly known as the alpine oxeye, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its white ray florets surrounding a central yellow disk, giving it a daisy-like appearance. This variety thrives in alpine environments, often growing in rocky soils at high elevations. It is a perennial plant that produces showy flowers from late summer to early autumn.

The species is adapted to cold climates and is often found in meadows and grasslands above the tree line.
