Lythrum

Lythrum, commonly known as rosemary or redroot, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Lythraceae.

These plants are native to Europe, Asia, and Africa, and they typically feature showy, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red. The leaves of lythrum are often lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are usually square or angular. Many species are grown for their ornamental value, and some are used in traditional medicine.

The genus includes both annual and perennial species, with some being adapted to wet or marshy environments.
