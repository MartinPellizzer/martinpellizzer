Lepanthes pallandrei

Lepanthes pallandrei, commonly known as the Palland's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in the understory of tropical rainforests. Its flowers are usually white or pale blue with intricate patterns, and it produces small, greenish-yellow fruits.

Lepanthes pallandrei is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
