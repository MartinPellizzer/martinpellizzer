Limonium peucetium

Limonium peucetium, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in coastal areas and saline soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in a fine, powdery coating.

Limonium peucetium is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging conditions.
