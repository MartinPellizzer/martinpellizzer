Lecanorchis malaccensis

Lecanorchis malaccensis, commonly known as the Malacca orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its delicate, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest undergrowth. Its rhizomes are fibrous, and it has linear, grass-like leaves.

Lecanorchis malaccensis is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests, where it relies on mycorrhizal associations for nutrient uptake.
