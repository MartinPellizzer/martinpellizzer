Lychnitis pulverulenta

Lychnitis pulverulenta, commonly known as the dusty miller, is a flowering plant characterized by its silvery-white, fuzzy leaves and small yellow flowers.

It is native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. The plant is often used in gardens for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Lychnitis pulverulenta is also known for its ability to attract beneficial insects like ladybugs and lacewings.
