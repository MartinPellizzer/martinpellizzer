Lactuca dregeana

Lactuca dregeana, commonly known as the Desert Lettuce, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family, Asteraceae.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa, particularly in Namibia and South Africa. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder. It produces small, yellow composite flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Lactuca dregeana is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, with deep roots that allow it to access water from deeper soil layers.
