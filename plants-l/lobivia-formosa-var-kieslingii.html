Lobivia formosa var. kieslingii

Lobivia formosa var. kieslingii, commonly known as the Kiesling's lobivia, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It features compact, rounded growth with thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces striking, vibrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are usually small and spoon-shaped, arranged in a rosette pattern to minimize water loss.

This variety is prized for its unique appearance and adaptability to rocky, well-drained soils.
