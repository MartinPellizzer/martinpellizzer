Lindera sericea var. lancea

Lindera sericea var. lancea, commonly known as the narrowleaf sweetshrub, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by red, fleshy berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Its ornamental value and ecological benefits make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
