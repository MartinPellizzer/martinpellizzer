Lupinus nootkatensis var. unalaskensis

Lupinus nootkatensis var. unalaskensis, commonly known as the Unalaska lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Aleutian Islands.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to violet pea-like flowers. The plant has feathery, bipinnate leaves that are adapted to coastal environments. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in coastal dunes and tundra habitats.

This variety is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local pollinator populations.
