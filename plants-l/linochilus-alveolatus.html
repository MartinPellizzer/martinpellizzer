Linochilus alveolatus

Linochilus alveolatus, commonly known as the honeycomb orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive honeycomb-like pattern on its leaves, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in shades of white and yellow.

Linochilus alveolatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts for its unique appearance.
