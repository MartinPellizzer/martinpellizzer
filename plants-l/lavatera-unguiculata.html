Lavatera unguiculata

Lavatera unguiculata, commonly known as the silky oaks or silky tree, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its soft, silvery-green leaves and showy, pink or white flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and dry regions of southern Australia.

Lavatera unguiculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
