Lupinus albifrons var. fissicalyx

Lupinus albifrons var. fissicalyx, commonly known as the fissicalyx lupine, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and clusters of bright yellow to orange flowers. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, and its stems are often branched. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in arid and semi-arid regions.

The species is known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain butterfly species.
