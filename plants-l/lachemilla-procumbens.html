Lachemilla procumbens

Lachemilla procumbens, commonly known as creeping cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to North America.

It features small, rounded leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of yellow flowers in early summer. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.

Lachemilla procumbens is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and support local pollinators.
