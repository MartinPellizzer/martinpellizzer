Linum angustifolium proles ambiguum

Linum angustifolium proles ambiguum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flax or ambiguous flax, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and delicate blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in coastal and inland regions, where it contributes to local biodiversity.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
