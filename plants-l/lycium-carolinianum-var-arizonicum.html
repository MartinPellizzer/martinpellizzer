Lycium carolinianum var. arizonicum

Lycium carolinianum var. arizonicum, commonly known as the Arizona wolfberry, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, red, fleshy berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. The plant has woody stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in desert scrub and rocky soils.

Its botanical features make it well-adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.
