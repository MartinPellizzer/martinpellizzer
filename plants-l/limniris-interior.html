Limniris interior

Limniris interior, commonly known as the interior marsh hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow to orange. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in moist, acidic soils. Limniris interior is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical features make it an important species for wetland ecosystems and a valuable plant for ecological restoration projects.
