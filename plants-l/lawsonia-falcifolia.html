Lawsonia falcifolia

Lawsonia falcifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed henna, is a shrub native to the Arabian Peninsula and parts of the Middle East.

It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves that are often used in traditional dyeing practices. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils.

Due to its historical and cultural significance, it is also valued for its medicinal properties.
