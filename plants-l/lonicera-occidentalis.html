Lonicera occidentalis

Lonicera occidentalis, commonly known as western honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that ripens in the fall and is attractive to birds.

Lonicera occidentalis is often found in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and in open woodlands.
