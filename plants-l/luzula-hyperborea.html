Luzula hyperborea

Luzula hyperborea, commonly known as the alpine wood sedge, is a perennial plant native to cold climates in the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves that are typically dark green in color. This species thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine tundra and boreal forest edges. Luzula hyperborea has a robust root system that helps it survive in harsh, nutrient-poor environments.

Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, forming in dense clusters at the base of the plant.
