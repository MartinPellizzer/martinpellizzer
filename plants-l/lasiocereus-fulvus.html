Lasiocereus fulvus

Lasiocereus fulvus, commonly known as the golden crown cactus, is a species native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its smooth, pale green stems and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has a distinctive crown-like appearance due to its dense arrangement of spines and flowering stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Lasiocereus fulvus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to adapt to arid environments.
