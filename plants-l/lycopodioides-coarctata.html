Lycopodioides coarctata

Lycopodioides coarctata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved clubmoss, is a small, evergreen plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, dense growth habit and produces small, cone-like structures called strobili that bear spores. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or rocky crevices. Its leaves are narrow and linear, arranged in whorls around the stem.

Lycopodioides coarctata is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ecological role in soil stabilization and habitat provision.
