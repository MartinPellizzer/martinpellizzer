Liparis cornicaulis

Liparis cornicaulis, commonly known as the horned orchid, is a species of orchid native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, horn-like sepals that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, often in shades of white, green, or yellow.

Liparis cornicaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
