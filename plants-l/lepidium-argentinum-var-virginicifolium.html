Lepidium argentinum var. virginicifolium

Lepidium argentinum var. virginicifolium, commonly known as the Virginia leafed rocket, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many mustard plants. The plant is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that appear in clusters. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
