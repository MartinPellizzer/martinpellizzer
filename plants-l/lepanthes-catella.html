Lepanthes catella

Lepanthes catella, commonly known as the cat's tail orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid with delicate, pale green to white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are characterized by their narrow, elongated shape, resembling a cat's tail, hence its common name.

Lepanthes catella is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
