Lycoctonum alboviolaceum var. purpurascens

Lycoctonum alboviolaceum var. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-flowered lycosperma, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of South America.

It features striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are often reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or containers.

Botanically, it is noted for its unique floral structure and potential ornamental value in horticultural settings.
