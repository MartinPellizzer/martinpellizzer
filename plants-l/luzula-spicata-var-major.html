Luzula spicata var. major

Luzula spicata var. major, commonly known as the tall wood sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. This variety is characterized by its tall, grass-like foliage and produces spike-like inflorescences that bear small, three-lobed flowers. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats.

Luzula spicata var. major is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
