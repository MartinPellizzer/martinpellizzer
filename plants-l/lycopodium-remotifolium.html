Lycopodium remotifolium

Lycopodium remotifolium, commonly known as the distant-leaved clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features whorled leaves that are narrow and lance-shaped, with a distinctive, creeping growth habit. Its sporophylls are clustered in strobili, which produce spores for reproduction.

Lycopodium remotifolium plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
