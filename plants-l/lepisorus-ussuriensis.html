Lepisorus ussuriensis

Lepisorus ussuriensis, commonly known as the Ussuri fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, creeping rhizomes and small, clustered fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its fronds are usually lanceolate or ovate, with a smooth or finely serrated margin.

Lepisorus ussuriensis is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in shady garden settings.
