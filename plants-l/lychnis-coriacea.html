Lychnis coriacea

Lychnis coriacea, commonly known as the rough hawksbeard, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Lychnis coriacea is often considered a weed but can add visual interest to natural landscapes.
