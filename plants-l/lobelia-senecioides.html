Lobelia senecioides

Lobelia senecioides, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy blue to purple flower clusters that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Lobelia senecioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
