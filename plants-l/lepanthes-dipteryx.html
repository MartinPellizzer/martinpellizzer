Lepanthes dipteryx

Lepanthes dipteryx, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers and is often found in humid, tropical environments. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in the canopy of rainforests. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Lepanthes dipteryx is valued for its ornamental beauty and plays a role in the ecological balance of its habitat.
