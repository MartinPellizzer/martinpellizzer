Lonicera incana

Lonicera incana, commonly known as gray honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the United States.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems that are often covered in a silvery-gray pubescence. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in the spring, which are typically yellow or white in color. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough, textured surface.

Lonicera incana is often found in moist, open woods and along stream banks, where it thrives in partial shade to full sun.
