Lippia aristata var. angustifolia

Lippia aristata var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf lemon grass, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features aromatic, narrow leaves with a citrussy scent, often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowering stems produce small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Botanically, it belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae), characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves.
