Loranthus kisaguka

Loranthus kisaguka, commonly known as the African mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily in the genus *Acacia*.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while also producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using the light it captures. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers, followed by fleshy, berry-like fruits that are dispersed by birds. It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing food for various wildlife species.

Its parasitic nature and unique reproductive strategies make it an interesting subject in botanical studies.
