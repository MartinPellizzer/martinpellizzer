Laelia esalqueana

Laelia esalqueana, commonly known as the Esalquean orchid, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of South America, particularly in Ecuador.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in rocky or forested areas at elevations above 2,000 meters. The plant features a pseudobulb with a single or few leaves and produces intricate, showy flowers with a distinct color pattern. Its blooms are usually pink or purple, with a yellow lip, and it flowers during the cooler months.

Laelia esalqueana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
