Lycopersicon esculentum var. commune

Lycopersicon esculentum var. commune, commonly known as the common tomato, is a herbaceous annual plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems, alternate leaves, and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces fleshy, berry-like fruits that typically range in color from red to yellow or orange. Its botanical features include a taproot system and a complex inflorescence structure that supports its reproductive cycle.

The variety is widely cultivated for its nutrient-rich fruits, which are used in a variety of culinary applications.
