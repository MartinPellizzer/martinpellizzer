Lindernia ruelloides

Lindernia ruelloides, commonly known as the mountain lindernia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the family Linderniaceae and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, tubular flowers. The plant typically produces violet to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.
