Leucanthemopsis flaveola subsp. alpestris

Leucanthemopsis flaveola subsp.

alpestris, commonly known as the alpine oxeye, is a member of the Asteraceae family. It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and basal rosette of leaves.

Native to alpine regions of Europe, it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils at high elevations. The plant produces small, composite flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its horticultural value lies in its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
