Lathyrus latifolius var. purpureus

Lathyrus latifolius var. purpureus, commonly known as the purple-flowered broadleaf pea, is a perennial legume native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three ovate to lanceolate leaflets, and its stems are often hairy. It produces small, cylindrical pods that contain several elliptical seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in ecological and agricultural settings.
