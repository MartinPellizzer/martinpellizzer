Leontodon brachyglossus

Leontodon brachyglossus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a hairy texture, while its flowers are yellow and resemble those of a dandelion.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual interest.
