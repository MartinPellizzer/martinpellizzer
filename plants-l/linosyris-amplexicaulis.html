Linosyris amplexicaulis

Linosyris amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clasping leaf sandwort, is a small, herbaceous plant belonging to the family Caryophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of elliptic, clasping leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that are typically arranged in a raceme. It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

Linosyris amplexicaulis is adapted to thrive in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky habitats.
