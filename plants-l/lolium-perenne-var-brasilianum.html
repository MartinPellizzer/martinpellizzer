Lolium perenne var. brasilianum

Lolium perenne var. brasilianum, commonly known as Brazilian perennial ryegrass, is a grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, dark green leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. This variety is often used in forage production due to its high nutritional value and adaptability to various soil types. It prefers temperate climates and can thrive in both full sun and partial shade.

The plant is also valued for its rapid establishment and ability to recover quickly after grazing or mowing.
