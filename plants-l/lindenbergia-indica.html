Lindenbergia indica

Lindenbergia indica, commonly known as the Indian lily, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. The plant typically grows in wet, marshy areas and thrives in humid tropical climates. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often with a waxy surface.

Lindenbergia indica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
