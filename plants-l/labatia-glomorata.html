Labatia glomorata

Labatia glomorata, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous coating. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Labatia glomorata thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant floral display.
