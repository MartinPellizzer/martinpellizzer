Lozania nunkui

Lozania nunkui, commonly known as the golden sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, oval-shaped fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in full sunlight and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
