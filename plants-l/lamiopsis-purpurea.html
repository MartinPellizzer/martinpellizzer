Lamiopsis purpurea

Lamiopsis purpurea, commonly known as purple lamiopsis, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, adding visual interest to gardens. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Lamiopsis purpurea thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.
