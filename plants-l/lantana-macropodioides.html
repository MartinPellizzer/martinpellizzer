Lantana macropodioides

Lantana macropodioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf lantana, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to yellow, orange, and red, depending on the variety. The plant has opposite, narrow, and often hairy leaves that are typically less than 5 cm in length. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common ornamental in gardens.

Lantana macropodioides is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
