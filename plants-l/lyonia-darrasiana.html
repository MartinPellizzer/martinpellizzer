Lyonia darrasiana

Lyonia darrasiana, commonly known as the Darrasiana lyonia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its evergreen shrub form, with small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green. Its flowers are usually pink or white, appearing in clusters and blooming in late spring to early summer.

Lyonia darrasiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained, acidic soils.
