Laurophyllus capensis

Laurophyllus capensis, commonly known as the Cape laurel.

It is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa, characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils. It produces small, edible berries that are sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Laurophyllus capensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in coastal environments.
