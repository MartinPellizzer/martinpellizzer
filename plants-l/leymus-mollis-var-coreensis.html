Leymus mollis var. coreensis

Leymus mollis var. coreensis, commonly known as Corean silky grass, is a perennial grass species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, silky hairs that cover the leaves and stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and wet meadows. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
