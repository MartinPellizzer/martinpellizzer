Lepanthes hondurensis

Lepanthes hondurensis, commonly known as the Honduran orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers with a distinctive white and green coloration. Its roots are mycorrhizal, forming symbiotic relationships with fungi to enhance nutrient absorption.

Lepanthes hondurensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
