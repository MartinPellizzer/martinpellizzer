Lasianthus inodorus subsp. pubescens

Lasianthus inodorus subsp.

pubescens, commonly known as the hairy lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its pubescent (hairy) stems and leaves.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
