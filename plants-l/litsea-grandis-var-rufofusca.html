Litsea grandis var. rufofusca

Litsea grandis var. rufofusca, commonly known as the red-barked litsea, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and avocado trees. The plant is characterized by its smooth, reddish-barked trunk and simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing a fragrant scent that attracts pollinators.

The fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that ripens to a yellow or orange color, containing a single seed.
