Linnaea borealis f. umensis

Linnaea borealis f. umensis, commonly known as the umenskaya twinflower, is a rare variant of the twinflower plant native to the boreal forests of northern Europe and Siberia.

It is characterized by its small, white, bilaterally symmetrical flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often on forest floors or in rocky crevices. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a leathery texture, adapted to the cool, humid conditions of its habitat.

This form is distinguished by its unique floral morphology and geographic distribution, making it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
