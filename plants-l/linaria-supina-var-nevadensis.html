Linaria supina var. nevadensis

Linaria supina var. nevadensis, commonly known as the Nevada sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the western United States.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers. The flowers are typically yellow or orange, with five petals and a distinctive spurred lower petal. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to full sun and drought conditions.

This variety is often found in disturbed areas and is valued for its resilience and ornamental appeal.
