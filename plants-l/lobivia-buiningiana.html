Lobivia buiningiana

Lobivia buiningiana, commonly known as the Buning's lobivia, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rounded shape and spiny, ribbed stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. Its leaves are modified into spines, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

Lobivia buiningiana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and adaptability to dry conditions.
