Lupinus lelandsmithii

Lupinus lelandsmithii, commonly known as Smith's lupine, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. The plant features slender, upright stems with palmately compound leaves that have five to seven leaflets. Its showy, blue to purple flowers are clustered in racemes and bloom in early summer.

Lupinus lelandsmithii is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by supporting pollinators.
