Liparis macrosepala

Liparis macrosepala, commonly known as the large-sepal orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated sepals that are often a vivid yellow or greenish-yellow in color. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are clustered in spikes and bloom during the wet season, adding to its ornamental value.

Botanically, it belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is noted for its complex floral structure and adaptability to various substrates.
