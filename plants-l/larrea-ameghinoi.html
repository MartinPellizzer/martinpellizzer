Larrea ameghinoi

Larrea ameghinoi, commonly known as the Argentine burro bush, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Argentina.

It belongs to the Zygophyllaceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and pinnate leaves. The plant is well adapted to harsh environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and a deep root system. Its bark and leaves have been used traditionally for their medicinal properties.

Larrea ameghinoi plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
