Leucaena leucocephala subsp. glabrata

Leucaena leucocephala subsp.

glabrata, commonly known as white leadtree, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions. It is characterized by its compound leaves and showy white or pale yellow flower clusters.

The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions. It has been widely used for forage, soil stabilization, and as a source of nitrogen-fixing legume.

However, it can become invasive in some areas if not managed properly.
