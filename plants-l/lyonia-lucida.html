Lyonia lucida

Lyonia lucida, commonly known as the small-flowered lyonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially sunny conditions. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, and it produces small, fragrant blooms in white or pink during the spring.

Lyonia lucida is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
