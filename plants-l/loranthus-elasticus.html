Loranthus elasticus

Loranthus elasticus, commonly known as the elastic mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while also producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using the light it captures. The plant has a distinctive appearance with small, oval-shaped leaves and white or yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its berries are typically red or orange, containing seeds that are dispersed by birds.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can have significant impacts on the health of its host trees.
