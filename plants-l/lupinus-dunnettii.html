Lupinus dunnettii

Lupinus dunnettii, commonly known as the Dunnett's lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed sites.

Lupinus dunnettii plays an important role in ecological systems by fixing nitrogen in the soil, benefiting other plant life in the area.
