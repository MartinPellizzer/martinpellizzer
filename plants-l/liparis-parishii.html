Liparis parishii

Liparis parishii, commonly known as the Paraguay orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to South America, particularly Paraguay and Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and delicate, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and grasslands. It has a short rhizome and produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
