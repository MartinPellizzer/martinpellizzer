Laurus australis

Laurus australis, commonly known as the Australian laurel, is a species of evergreen tree or shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like laurel and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. It produces berries that are typically blue or black when mature.

Laurus australis is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
