Leucanthemum cantabricum

Leucanthemum cantabricum, commonly known as the Cantabrian chrysanthemum, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features a basal rosette of gray-green, hairy leaves and produces daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

Leucanthemum cantabricum is also noted for its resilience and ability to withstand coastal conditions.
