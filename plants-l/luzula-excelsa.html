Luzula excelsa

Luzula excelsa, commonly known as the tall sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its upright, grass-like appearance with narrow, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Luzula excelsa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
