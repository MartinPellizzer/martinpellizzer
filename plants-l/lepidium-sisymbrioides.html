Lepidium sisymbrioides

Lepidium sisymbrioides, commonly known as Indian cabbages or wild mustard, is a flowering plant native to western North America.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard plants. The plant typically grows in open areas such as grasslands and disturbed soils, and it produces small, yellow flowers that attract pollinators. Its leaves are alternate, lobed, and often covered in a fine layer of white powder, which is a characteristic feature of the genus Lepidium.

The plant is also known for its edible leaves and seeds, which have been used in traditional cuisines.
