Lysiloma marchianum

Lysiloma marchianum, commonly known as the red sand dollar tree, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by distinctive red, fleshy fruits that resemble sand dollars. Its botanical features make it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates due to its attractive foliage and unique fruit.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
