Lonicera savranica

Lonicera savranica, commonly known as the yellow honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces fragrant, yellow flowers in early summer, which are followed by red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its rapid growth and ability to climb or trail make it versatile for various landscaping applications.
