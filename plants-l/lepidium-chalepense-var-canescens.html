Lepidium chalepense var. canescens

Lepidium chalepense var. canescens, commonly known as wild cress, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has hairy, linear leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

This variety is native to southern Europe and has been introduced to various parts of the world.
