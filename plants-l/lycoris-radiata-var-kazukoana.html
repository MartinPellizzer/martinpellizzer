Lycoris radiata var. kazukoana

Lycoris radiata var. kazukoana, commonly known as the Japanese rain lily, is a variant of the evergreen lily that exhibits distinct botanical features.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in the autumn, typically from September to November. The plant has narrow, strap-like leaves that are dark green and evergreen throughout the year. Its bulbs are adapted to thrive in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

This variety is native to Japan and is valued for its ornamental beauty and seasonal flowering.
