Limeum subnudum

Limeum subnudum, commonly known as the nakedleaf lichen, is a foliose lichen that thrives in humid, shaded environments.

It has a distinctive appearance with a pale green to yellowish thallus and a leathery texture. The plant lacks true leaves, instead having a flattened, lobed structure that absorbs nutrients from the substrate. It is often found on tree trunks and rocks in tropical and subtropical regions.

Limeum subnudum plays a vital role in nutrient cycling and serves as a habitat for various microorganisms.
