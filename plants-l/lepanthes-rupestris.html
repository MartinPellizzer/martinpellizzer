Lepanthes rupestris

Lepanthes rupestris, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant grows in rocky or mountainous habitats, often epiphytic on tree trunks or cliffs. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the air and substrate.

Lepanthes rupestris is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
