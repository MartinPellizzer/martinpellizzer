Lussacia hispida

Lussacia hispida, commonly known as the rough lussacia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features hairy, ribbed stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers bloom in late spring, and it produces capsule fruits containing minute seeds.

Lussacia hispida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xerophytic gardens.
