Lunania mauritii

Lunania mauritii, commonly known as the Mauritius moonflower, is a flowering plant native to the island of Mauritius.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous small seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers a tropical climate.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
