Lupinus caldasensis

Lupinus caldasensis, commonly known as the Caldas lupine, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of Colombia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces showy, sparsely branched flower clusters that range in color from white to violet, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Lupinus caldasensis is adapted to high-altitude environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.

Its ecological role includes nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
