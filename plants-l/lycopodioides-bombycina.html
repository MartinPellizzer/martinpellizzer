Lycopodioides bombycina

Lycopodioides bombycina, commonly known as the silver fir moss, is a species of clubmoss belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, thread-like leaves that form dense, cushion-like structures. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its sporophyte stage is relatively short-lived, and it relies on its gametophyte for long-term survival.

Lycopodioides bombycina plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for microorganisms and small invertebrates.
