Lasianthus attenuatus var. attenuatus

Lasianthus attenuatus var. attenuatus, commonly known as the slender lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale purple, arranged in whorled clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in tropical gardens.
