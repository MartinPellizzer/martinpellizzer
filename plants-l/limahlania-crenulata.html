Limahlania crenulata

Limahlania crenulata, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has rough, hairy leaves that are often lobed or notched, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Limahlania crenulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens in regions with a Mediterranean climate.
