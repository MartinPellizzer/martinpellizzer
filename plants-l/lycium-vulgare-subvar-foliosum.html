Lycium vulgare subvar. foliosum

Lycium vulgare subvar. foliosum, commonly known as the common boxthorn, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features small, spiny leaves and produces red berries that are edible but have a tart flavor. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas. Its botanical characteristics include woody stems and a dense growth habit.

It is sometimes used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness.
