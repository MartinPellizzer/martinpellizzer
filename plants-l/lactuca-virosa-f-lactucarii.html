Lactuca virosa f. lactucarii

Lactuca virosa f. lactucarii, commonly known as the bitter lettuce or wild lettuce, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, lobed leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant contains lactucin and other sesquiterpene lactones, which contribute to its bitter taste and potential medicinal properties. It is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, growing in a variety of habitats.

Its leaves are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported sedative and anti-inflammatory effects.
