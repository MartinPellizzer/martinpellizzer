Lucuma glaucescens

Lucuma glaucescens, commonly known as the blue lucuma, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to the Andean region of South America, particularly Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its large, leathery leaves and clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. Its fruit, which resembles a small, elongated citrus, is edible and has a sweet, nutty flavor.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and potential culinary uses.
