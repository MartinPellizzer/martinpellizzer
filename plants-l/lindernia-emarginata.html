Lindernia emarginata

Lindernia emarginata, commonly known as the Mexican verbena, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the family Verbenaceae and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically produces vibrant purple or blue flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.
