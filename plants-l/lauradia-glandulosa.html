Lauradia glandulosa

Lauradia glandulosa, commonly known as the hairy laurel, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features simple, opposite, and usually hairy leaves that are elliptical to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles. Its fruit is a small, smooth, dark blue drupe that matures in the fall.

Lauradia glandulosa is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodlands and along stream banks.
