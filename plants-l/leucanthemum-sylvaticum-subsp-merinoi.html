Leucanthemum sylvaticum subsp. merinoi

Leucanthemum sylvaticum subsp.

merinoi, commonly known as the Merino's wood aster, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America. It is characterized by its basal rosette of gray-green, hairy leaves and produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers with dark centers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or open woodland habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly smaller flowers and more compact growth compared to other members of the genus.
