Ligea secundiflora

Ligea secundiflora, commonly known as the slender leaf ligea, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, elongated leaves that grow in a spiral pattern along its stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale green to white in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists studying plant adaptation and biodiversity.
