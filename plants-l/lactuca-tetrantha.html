Lactuca tetrantha

Lactuca tetrantha, commonly known as the four-angled lettuce, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. Its seeds are small and wind-dispersed, contributing to its ability to spread in various habitats.

Lactuca tetrantha is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a range of environmental conditions.
