Lissochilus mechowii

Lissochilus mechowii, commonly known as the Mechow's slipper orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated, and often colorful pouch-like structure, which is a defining feature of the Orchidaceae family. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Due to its habitat specificity and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
