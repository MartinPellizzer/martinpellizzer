Lobelia heyneana var. parviflora

Lobelia heyneana var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered lobelia, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of South America.

It typically grows to a height of about 30-60 centimeters, featuring slender stems and alternate, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are usually blue or purple in color, arranged in spikes at the top of the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid forest understories.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with high humidity and partial shade.
