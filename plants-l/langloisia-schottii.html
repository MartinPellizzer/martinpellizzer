Langloisia schottii

Langloisia schottii, commonly known as the Schott's langloisia, is a member of the Araceae family, characterized by its tropical origin and distinctive floral structure.

This plant is native to Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. It features a short, rhizomatous stem with broad, arrow-shaped leaves that are often variegated in color. The inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe, typical of many arums.

Due to its ornamental value and specific care requirements, it is occasionally cultivated in greenhouses or botanical gardens.
