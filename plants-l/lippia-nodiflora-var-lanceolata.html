Lippia nodiflora var. lanceolata

Lippia nodiflora var. lanceolata, commonly known as Mexican blueweed, is a perennial herb native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its lanceolate leaves and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. It is often used in traditional medicine for its aromatic properties.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
