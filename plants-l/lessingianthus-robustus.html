Lessingianthus robustus

Lessingianthus robustus, commonly known as the robust sunflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers with a distinctive red center. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring upright stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowering period occurs during the summer months, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
