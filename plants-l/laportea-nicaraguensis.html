Laportea nicaraguensis

Laportea nicaraguensis, commonly known as the Nicaraguan false indigo, is a herbaceous perennial native to Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, aromatic leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
