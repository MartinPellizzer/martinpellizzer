Lilium bodinieri

Lilium bodinieri, commonly known as the Chinese lily, is a species native to China and is characterized by its striking pink and white flowers.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is known for its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces large, showy blooms with six to eight petals, often with a distinctive red or pink streak at the base. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

This species is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional Chinese medicine.
