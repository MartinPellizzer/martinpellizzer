Lunaria pachyrrhiza f. longecarpophorata

Lunaria pachyrrhiza f. longecarpophorata, commonly known as the long-carpophore moonflower, is a variant of the moonflower plant characterized by its elongated fruiting structures.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is native to certain regions of North America. The plant produces distinctive, orb-shaped seed pods that remain attached to the plant for an extended period. Its flowers are typically white or pale blue, opening at night and closing during the day.

This form is notable for its unique morphological features, which set it apart from other Lunaria species.
