Lamium pisidicum

Lamium pisidicum, commonly known as Persian nettle, is a flowering plant belonging to the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in whorled clusters along the stems. Native to regions of Europe and western Asia, it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its aromatic leaves and flowers make it a notable species within its ecological habitat.
