Leontodon salzmannii var. hispanicus

Leontodon salzmannii var. hispanicus, commonly known as the Spanish false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and northern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by distinctive, elongated bracts that give it its common name. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
