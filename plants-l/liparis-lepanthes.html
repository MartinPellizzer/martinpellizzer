Liparis lepanthes

Liparis lepanthes, commonly known as the orchid of the Andes, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a short, creeping rhizome and a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers that are typically pale yellow to white with a dark purple or violet lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments at high altitudes in South America.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated by enthusiasts and botanists alike.
