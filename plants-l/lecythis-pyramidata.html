Lecythis pyramidata

Lecythis pyramidata, commonly known as the Brazil nut tree, is a large evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The tree produces distinctive, woody fruits that contain up to 20 edible seeds, known as Brazil nuts. These nuts are rich in nutrients, particularly selenium and protein.

The Brazil nut tree plays a vital role in the ecosystem, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
