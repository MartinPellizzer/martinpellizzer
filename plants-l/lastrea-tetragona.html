Lastrea tetragona

Lastrea tetragona, commonly known as the four-angled lastrea, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy stems that are typically four-sided, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique morphology and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
