Leucopogon cunninghamii

Leucopogon cunninghamii, commonly known as the slender leucopogon, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its slender, woody stems and small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils in open habitats such as heaths and woodlands. It produces pink or red flowers that attract nectar-feeding birds and insects.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
