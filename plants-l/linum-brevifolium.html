Linum brevifolium

Linum brevifolium, commonly known as the short-leaved flax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces delicate blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Linum brevifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in fiber production.
