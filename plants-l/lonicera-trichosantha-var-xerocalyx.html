Lonicera trichosantha var. xerocalyx

Lonicera trichosantha var. xerocalyx, commonly known as the sandbar honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually hairy on the underside, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in sandy or well-drained soils and is often found in coastal dunes and wetlands.

Its adaptability to harsh environments makes it an important species for ecological restoration projects.
