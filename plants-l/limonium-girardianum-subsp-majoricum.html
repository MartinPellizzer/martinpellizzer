Limonium girardianum subsp. majoricum

Limonium girardianum subsp.

majoricum, commonly known as the coastal flax or sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in coastal areas, preferring sandy or gravelly soils and full sun exposure. It is adapted to salty and windy environments, making it a hardy species often found along shorelines.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in gardens, particularly in coastal and seaside landscapes.
