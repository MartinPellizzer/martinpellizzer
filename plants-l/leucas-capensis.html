Leucas capensis

Leucas capensis, commonly known as Cape leucas, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant features square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are often grayish-green in color. It produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Leucas capensis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
