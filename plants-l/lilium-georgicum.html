Lilium georgicum

Lilium georgicum, commonly known as the Georgian lily, is a species of flowering plant in the Liliaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus, including parts of Georgia and Turkey. This plant is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and red. The leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Lilium georgicum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
