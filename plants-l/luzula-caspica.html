Luzula caspica

Luzula caspica, commonly known as the Caspian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Eurasia.

It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands, stream banks, and wet meadows. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and arranged in a dense rosette. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Luzula caspica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
