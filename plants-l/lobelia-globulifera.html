Lobelia globulifera

Lobelia globulifera, commonly known as the globe lobelia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Lobelia globulifera is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
