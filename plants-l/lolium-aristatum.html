Lolium aristatum

Lolium aristatum, commonly known as the tufted grass or arrowgrass, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and fields.

Lolium aristatum is also valued as a forage crop for livestock due to its nutritional content and hardiness.
