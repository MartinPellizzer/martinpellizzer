Lycium floribundum var. grisebachii

Lycium floribundum var. grisebachii, commonly known as the grey-fruited boxthorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, spiny stems and produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant's berries are typically red or orange, turning dark when mature. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
