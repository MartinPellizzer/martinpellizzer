Lepanthes jugum

Lepanthes jugum, commonly known as the narrow-lip orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically range in color from white to pale pink. The plant grows as an epiphyte, often found on trees in humid, tropical environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern.

The genus Lepanthes is known for its diverse floral morphology, and Lepanthes jugum is no exception, with its unique lip structure that distinguishes it from other orchid species.
