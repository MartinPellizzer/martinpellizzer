Laserpitium ternatum

Laserpitium ternatum, commonly known as the three-leaved gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine or subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered with fine hairs, while the stems are erect and branched.

Laserpitium ternatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
