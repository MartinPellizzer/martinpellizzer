Lindera umbellata subsp. membranacea

Lindera umbellata subsp.

membranacea, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sweet pepper, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth, membranous texture on the underside. Its fragrant, yellowish-green flowers appear in umbrella-like clusters called umbels.

The fruit is a small, orange-red drupe that ripens in the fall, attracting various wildlife species.
