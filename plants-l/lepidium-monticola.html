Lepidium monticola

Lepidium monticola, commonly known as mountain cress, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant is characterized by its small, white or yellowish flowers and compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Lepidium monticola thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows.

Its botanical features make it an important component of high-altitude ecosystems.
