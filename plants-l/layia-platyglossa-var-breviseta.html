Layia platyglossa var. breviseta

Layia platyglossa var. breviseta, commonly known as the short-hairy layia, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, typically arranged in a flat-topped cluster. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are lance-shaped with a bluish-green hue. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

This variety is characterized by its shorter, more compact growth compared to other varieties of Layia platyglossa.
