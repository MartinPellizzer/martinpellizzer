Lagerstroemia amabilis

Lagerstroemia amabilis, commonly known as the crepe myrtle, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, papery flowers that appear in summer, often in shades of pink, red, or white. The plant has smooth, mottled bark that peels in thin, papery layers, adding to its ornamental appeal. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate, turning vibrant red or orange in the fall.

Lagerstroemia amabilis is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
