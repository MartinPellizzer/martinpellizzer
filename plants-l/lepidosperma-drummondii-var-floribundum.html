Lepidosperma drummondii var. floribundum

Lepidosperma drummondii var. floribundum, commonly known as the coastal sedgeland sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It typically grows in coastal sedgelands and wetlands, preferring moist to saline soils. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are often pink or reddish in color. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in brackish environments and its role in stabilizing coastal soils.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
