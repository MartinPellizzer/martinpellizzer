Leandra niangaeformis

Leandra niangaeformis, commonly known as the African velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, velvety leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, which bloom during the rainy season. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often found in moist woodland areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
