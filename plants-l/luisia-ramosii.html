Luisia ramosii

Luisia ramosii, commonly known as the mountain mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a perennial herb with slender, hairy stems that often branch out into a shrub-like form. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Luisia ramosii is adapted to high-altitude environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.
