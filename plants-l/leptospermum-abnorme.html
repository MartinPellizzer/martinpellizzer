Leptospermum abnorme

Leptospermum abnorme, commonly known as the variable tea tree, is a shrub native to Tasmania, Australia.

It is characterized by its small, needle-like leaves and clusters of white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and forested areas.

Leptospermum abnorme is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in indigenous cultures.
