Lavandula × intermedia

Lavandula × intermedia, commonly known as English lavender, is a hybrid shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fragrant, spike-like clusters of purple flowers and gray-green, aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and prefers well-drained soil and full sun. It is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and essential oils, which are used in aromatherapy and perfumery.

Lavandula × intermedia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
