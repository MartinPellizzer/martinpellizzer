Lepidotis clavata

Lepidotis clavata, commonly known as the clustered starflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, star-shaped white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Lepidotis clavata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native plant gardens.
