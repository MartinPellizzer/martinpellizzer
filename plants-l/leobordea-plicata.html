Leobordea plicata

Leobordea plicata, commonly known as the zigzag plant, is a member of the Aizoaceae family, native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, zigzagging stems that help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are fleshy and succulent, adapted for water storage.

Leobordea plicata is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its distinctive appearance and drought tolerance.
