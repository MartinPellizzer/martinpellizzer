Lycium ellenbeckii

Lycium ellenbeckii, commonly known as Ellenbeck's boxthorn, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and poor soil conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in its native habitat.
