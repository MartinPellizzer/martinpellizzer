Lespedeza retusa

Lespedeza retusa, commonly known as slender lespedeza, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves and small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides forage for wildlife.

Its seeds are often dispersed by water, contributing to its spread in wetland environments.
