Leucadendron coriaceum

Leucadendron coriaceum, commonly known as the silverleaf candle bush, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage and conical, candle-like flower heads. The plant belongs to the Proteaceae family and is known for its drought tolerance and hardy nature. Its leaves are leathery and covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking visual appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
