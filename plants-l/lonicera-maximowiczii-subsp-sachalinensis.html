Lonicera maximowiczii subsp. sachalinensis

Lonicera maximowiczii subsp.

sachalinensis, commonly known as the Sakhalin honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the Russian Far East and northeastern China. It features opposite, ovate leaves that are usually hairy on the underside, and it produces clusters of fragrant, tubular yellow-orange flowers in late spring to early summer.

The plant is characterized by its slender, arching stems and is often found in moist, shaded forest environments. Its berries, which are dark red when mature, are edible and attract various bird species.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
