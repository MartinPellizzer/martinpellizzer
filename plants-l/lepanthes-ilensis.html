Lepanthes ilensis

Lepanthes ilensis, commonly known as the Ecuadorian orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant has a slender, erect stem with a few leaves and produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink. Its flowers are often clustered in a spike and have a unique, intricate structure typical of many orchids.

Lepanthes ilensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
