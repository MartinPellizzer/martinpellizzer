Lasthenia macrantha var. bakeri

Lasthenia macrantha var. bakeri, commonly known as Baker's stickyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy basal rosette. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the spring, often accompanied by glandular hairs that give it a sticky texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it an interesting subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
