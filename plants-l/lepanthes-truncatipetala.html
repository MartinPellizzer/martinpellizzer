Lepanthes truncatipetala

Lepanthes truncatipetala, commonly known as the truncated-petaled orchid, is a species native to Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in montane forests. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish with intricate patterns, and it produces small, elongated capsules as fruit.

Lepanthes truncatipetala is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
