Lardizabala ternata

Lardizabala ternata, commonly known as the Andean arrowroot, is a perennial plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary edible part of the plant. The plant thrives in cool, high-altitude environments and requires well-drained soil. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and often have a waxy texture.

Lardizabala ternata is valued for its starchy, nutrient-rich tubers, which are used in traditional Andean cuisine.
