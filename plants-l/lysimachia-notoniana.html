Lysimachia notoniana

Lysimachia notoniana, commonly known as the Southern wood sorrel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southern United States.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive yellowish-green color and produces clusters of delicate, five-petaled white flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded areas. It is a member of the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit.

Lysimachia notoniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
