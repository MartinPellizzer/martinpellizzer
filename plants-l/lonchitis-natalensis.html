Lonchitis natalensis

Lonchitis natalensis, commonly known as Natal grass or Natal flat sedge, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and riverbanks, where it thrives in moist to aquatic conditions. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, which allows it to spread and form dense vegetation.

Lonchitis natalensis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
