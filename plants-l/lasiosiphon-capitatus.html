Lasiosiphon capitatus

Lasiosiphon capitatus, commonly known as the clustered siphon.

It is a flowering plant native to New Zealand, characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often growing in dense tufts.

Lasiosiphon capitatus is typically found in coastal and lowland areas, thriving in well-drained soils.
