Leucogenes tarahaoa

Leucogenes tarahaoa, commonly known as the Tahaa lily, is a flowering plant native to the Society Islands in French Polynesia.

It is characterized by its striking white petals and a prominent yellow throat, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to the humid, tropical climate of its native habitat. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of flowers that bloom throughout the year.

Due to its rarity and unique beauty, it is considered an important species in the local flora and conservation efforts.
