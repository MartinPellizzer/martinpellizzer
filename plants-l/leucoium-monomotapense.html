Leucoium monomotapense

Leucoium monomotapense, commonly known as the Natal lily, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a rhizomatous root system and produces striking white to pale yellow flowers with a distinctive spurred petal. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grassland or forest margins. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair.

The Natal lily is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its elegant blooms.
