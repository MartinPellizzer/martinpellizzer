Liatris walteri

Liatris walteri, commonly known as Walter's blazing star, is a herbaceous perennial native to the central and southern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small clusters of flowers. The plant features upright, sparsely leafed stems that bear showy, purple to pinkish-red bract-like flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Walter's blazing star is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
