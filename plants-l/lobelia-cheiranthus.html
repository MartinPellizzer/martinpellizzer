Lobelia cheiranthus

Lobelia cheiranthus, commonly known as the redstem lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, hairy stems and produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. Its foliage consists of alternate, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture.

Lobelia cheiranthus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partially shaded conditions.
