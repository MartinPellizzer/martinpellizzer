Lepturus repens var. palmyrensis

Lepturus repens var. palmyrensis, commonly known as the Palmyrene creeping lichen, is a foliose lichen that thrives in temperate and subtropical climates.

It features a dense, mat-like growth form with a pale green to yellowish thallus that often develops a darker, more leathery texture as it ages. The plant is characterized by its branched, leaf-like lobes and is typically found on tree trunks, branches, or rocks in shaded environments. It is a symbiotic organism composed of a fungal component and algae or cyanobacteria, contributing to nutrient cycling in forest ecosystems.

Due to its slow growth and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a sensitive indicator of environmental health.
