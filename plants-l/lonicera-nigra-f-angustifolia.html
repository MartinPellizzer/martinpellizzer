Lonicera nigra f. angustifolia

Lonicera nigra f. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf honeysuckle, is a compact shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, fragrant, yellowish-white flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, juicy berries that attract birds. Its foliage remains attractive throughout the growing season, making it a valuable addition to gardens.

It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, preferring moist environments.
