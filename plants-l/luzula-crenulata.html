Luzula crenulata

Luzula crenulata, commonly known as the notched woodrush, is a species of flowering plant in the family Poaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive notched or serrated edge. It produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters during the spring and early summer.

Luzula crenulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging, wetland conditions.
