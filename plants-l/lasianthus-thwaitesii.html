Lasianthus thwaitesii

Lasianthus thwaitesii, commonly known as the slender leaved lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
