Leontodon permiatus

Leontodon permiatus, commonly known as the Permius dandelion, is a flowering plant native to eastern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, woodlands, and disturbed soils, preferring moist to moderately dry conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture, and the flowers are yellow, resembling those of dandelions.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
