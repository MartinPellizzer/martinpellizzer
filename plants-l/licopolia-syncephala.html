Licopolia syncephala

Licopolia syncephala, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a striking member of the Malvaceae family native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, white, night-blooming flowers that emit a sweet fragrance, attracting nocturnal pollinators like moths. The plant features palmate leaves with a velvety texture and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique blooming habit and aesthetic appeal, it is a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
