Lophophora diffusa subsp. fricii

Lophophora diffusa subsp.

fricii, commonly known as the "Crazy Daisy," is a small, spineless cactus native to the Chihuahuan Desert. It features a short, cylindrical stem with dense clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant is characterized by its lack of spines and its ability to thrive in well-drained, sandy soils. It is often found in rocky areas and is adapted to arid environments with minimal rainfall.

Due to its striking appearance and unique growth habits, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
