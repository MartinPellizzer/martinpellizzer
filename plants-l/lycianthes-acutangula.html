Lycianthes acutangula

Lycianthes acutangula, commonly known as the sharp-fruited physalis, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to Central and South America and is characterized by its distinctive, inflated calyces that enclose the fruit. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered with fine hairs, while the flowers are small and yellowish-white.

The fruit, a type of berry, is surrounded by a fleshy, papery husk that turns from green to orange or red as it matures.
