Laportea peduncularis subsp. latidens

Laportea peduncularis subsp.

latidens, commonly known as the broad-leaved ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy purple to reddish-purple flowers.

The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves with a waxy surface, and its flowering period typically occurs in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in moist to wet soil conditions and is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
