Linum austriacum subsp. gaetulum

Linum austriacum subsp.

gaetulum, commonly known as the Austrian flax, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Central Europe. It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and lanceolate leaves.

The plant produces blue or violet flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is often found in dry meadows and rocky habitats.
