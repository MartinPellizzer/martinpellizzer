Lupinus procerus

Lupinus procerus, commonly known as the tall lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds.

Lupinus procerus is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment and ecological restoration projects.
