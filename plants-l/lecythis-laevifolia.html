Lecythis laevifolia

Lecythis laevifolia, commonly known as the smooth-leaved lecythis, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Lecythidaceae and is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The tree produces a heavy, oily fruit with a thick, spiky exterior, which contains a single large seed. Its growth habit includes a straight trunk and a dense crown, making it a notable species in rainforest ecosystems.

The plant is valued for its wood, which is used in construction and furniture, and for its potential medicinal properties.
