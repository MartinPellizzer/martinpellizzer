Limonium reniforme

Limonium reniforme, commonly known as the heart-shaped sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, showing a preference for well-drained conditions. Limonium reniforme is a perennial herb that thrives in full sun and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Its blooms attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity.
