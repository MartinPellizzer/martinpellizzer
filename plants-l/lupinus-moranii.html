Lupinus moranii

Lupinus moranii, commonly known as Moran's lupine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of pea-like flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant has compound leaves composed of several small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Lupinus moranii is part of the legume family and has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria.
