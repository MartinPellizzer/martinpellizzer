Litsea obscura

Litsea obscura, commonly known as the dark litsea, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate, ovate leaves that are usually dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its small, white flowers are typically clustered in axillary inflorescences, and it produces small, fleshy fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
