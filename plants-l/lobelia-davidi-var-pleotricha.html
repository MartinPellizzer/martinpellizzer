Lobelia davidi var. pleotricha

Lobelia davidi var. pleotricha, commonly known as the Chinese bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to China.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, tubular, violet-blue flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
