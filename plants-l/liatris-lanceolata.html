Liatris lanceolata

Liatris lanceolata, commonly known as narrowleaf blazingstar, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of pink to purple flower heads. The plant produces showy, spiky inflorescences that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Liatris lanceolata is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
