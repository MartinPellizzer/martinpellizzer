Lyonia rubrovenia

Lyonia rubrovenia, commonly known as the red-veined lyonia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its leaves are typically elliptical with distinct red veins, giving it its common name.

Lyonia rubrovenia thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in forest understories.
