Loxanthocereus nanus

Loxanthocereus nanus, commonly known as the miniature night-blooming cactus, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a small, slow-growing plant that typically reaches a height of less than 10 inches. The plant features a dense cluster of cylindrical stems covered in small, white spines. Its flowers, which appear at night, are pale yellow to orange and emit a sweet fragrance.

Loxanthocereus nanus is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil to thrive.
