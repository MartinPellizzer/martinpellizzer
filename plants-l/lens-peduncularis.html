Lens peduncularis

Lens peduncularis, commonly known as the spurred gentian, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or rocky slopes. Its flowers have a unique spurred structure at the base, which is an adaptation for pollination by specific insects.

The leaves are usually alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in a fine pubescence.
