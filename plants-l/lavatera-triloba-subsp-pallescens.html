Lavatera triloba subsp. pallescens

Lavatera triloba subsp.

pallescens, commonly known as the pale lavender bush, is a shrub native to Australia. It features soft, silvery-gray foliage and produces showy, pink or lavender-colored flowers.

The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
