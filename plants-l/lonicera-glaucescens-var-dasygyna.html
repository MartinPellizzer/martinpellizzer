Lonicera glaucescens var. dasygyna

Lonicera glaucescens var. dasygyna, commonly known as the glossy honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically glossy and dark green, with a slightly hairy underside. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually yellow or orange, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its fragrant flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
