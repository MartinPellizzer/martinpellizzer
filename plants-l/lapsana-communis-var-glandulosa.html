Lapsana communis var. glandulosa

Lapsana communis var. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular common cudweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this plant thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. It has a taproot system and can spread through both seeds and vegetative means.

The plant's glandular structures contribute to its distinctive appearance and may play a role in its ecological interactions.
