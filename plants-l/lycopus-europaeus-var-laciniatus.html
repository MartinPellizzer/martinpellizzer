Lycopus europaeus var. laciniatus

Lycopus europaeus var. laciniatus, commonly known as the cut-leaved water horehound, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its finely toothed, dark green leaves that are often lobed or divided. The plant produces small, blue to violet flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream sides.

Its aromatic leaves and flowers have been used traditionally in herbal medicine for their purported soothing and antiseptic properties.
