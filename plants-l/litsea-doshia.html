Litsea doshia

Litsea doshia, commonly known as the fragrant leaf tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and avocados, and is characterized by its aromatic, opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and for their pleasant scent, while the tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal.

Litsea doshia thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in forest understories or as a pioneer species in disturbed areas.
