Lepanthes hymenoptera

Lepanthes hymenoptera, commonly known as the hummingbird orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers that often resemble the shape and color of hummingbird feathers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments within tropical forests. Its flowers are specialized to attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.

Lepanthes hymenoptera exhibits typical orchid features, including a sympodial growth habit and a complex, highly modified root system.
