Larrea tridentata subsp. glutinosa

Larrea tridentata subsp.

glutinosa, commonly known as greasewood, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico. It is characterized by its small, yellowish-green leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant has a deep root system that allows it to survive in harsh, dry environments. Its leaves are coated with a resinous substance that helps reduce water loss.

This species plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
