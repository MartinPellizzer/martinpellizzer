Luzula spicata var. erecta

Luzula spicata var. erecta, commonly known as the upright wood rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and dense clusters of grass-like leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, spike-like flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in suitable climates.
