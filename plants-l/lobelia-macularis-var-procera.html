Lobelia macularis var. procera

Lobelia macularis var. procera, commonly known as the tall lobelia, is a striking flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 6 feet, making it a notable presence in wetland environments. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, narrow, and slightly hairy, often with a bluish tint.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in moist, sunny conditions.
