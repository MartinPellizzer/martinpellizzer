Lanugothamnus chionolaenoides

Lanugothamnus chionolaenoides, commonly known as the snow cushion thorn, is a low-growing shrub native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and silver-gray foliage, which helps it survive in harsh, cold environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, adding a striking contrast to its pale leaves. Its adaptability to extreme conditions makes it a resilient species in alpine and arid habitats.

Lanugothamnus chionolaenoides is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.
