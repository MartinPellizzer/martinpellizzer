Ladenbergia sericea

Ladenbergia sericea, commonly known as the velvetwood, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and compound leaves with a silvery underside. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range from white to pale yellow, followed by flat, bean-like pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and savanna habitats.

The tree is valued for its durable wood and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
