Lepidium demissum

Lepidium demissum, commonly known as the slender wallflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other well-known plants such as cabbages and mustards. The plant is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and slender, branched stems. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in open, sunny environments.

Lepidium demissum is often found in disturbed habitats and is considered a low-growing, perennial species.
