Lythrum salicaria subvar. gracilius

Lythrum salicaria subvar. gracilius, commonly known as narrow-leaved common loosestrife, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically narrower than those of the typical variety. The plant produces spikes of showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist to wet habitats, including marshes, wet meadows, and along stream banks.

This subspecies is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support diverse wildlife.
