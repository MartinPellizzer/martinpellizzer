Lithops bromfieldii f. sulphurea

Lithops bromfieldii f. sulphurea, commonly known as the yellow stone plant, is a member of the Aizoaceae family and is native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, fleshy, and often yellowish-green leaves that resemble stones, providing effective camouflage in its natural habitat. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the summer months, which are typically borne on slender stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Due to its striking appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
