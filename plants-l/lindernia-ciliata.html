Lindernia ciliata

Lindernia ciliata, commonly known as the velvetleaf lindernia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with ciliate margins, giving it a distinctive fuzzy appearance. It produces clusters of small, tubular, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

Lindernia ciliata is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and along stream banks.
