Lupinus bingenensis var. roseus

Lupinus bingenensis var. roseus, commonly known as the pink lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of showy pink or lavender flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds.

It is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
