Linaria rubioides subsp. rubioides

Linaria rubioides subsp.

rubioides, commonly known as the redstem toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender, branched stems that often have a reddish tinge.

The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that are typically arranged in racemes, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soils in sunny to partially shaded environments. Linaria rubioides subsp.

rubioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
