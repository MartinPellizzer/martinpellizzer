Linum marginale

Linum marginale, commonly known as the narrowleaf flax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces striking blue or purple flowers with five petals. The plant has slender, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Linum marginale is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in textile production.
