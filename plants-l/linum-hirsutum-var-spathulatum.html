Linum hirsutum var. spathulatum

Linum hirsutum var. spathulatum, commonly known as the broadleaf flax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, hairy leaves and produces clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant is a member of the Linaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Its seeds are also used in traditional herbal remedies.
