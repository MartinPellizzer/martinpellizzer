Leontodon liveus

Leontodon liveus, commonly known as the live alpine, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the daisy family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish to brownish flower heads that bloom in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species in many areas.
