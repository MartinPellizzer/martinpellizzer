Lychnanthos

Lychnanthos, commonly known as the African bellflower, is a genus of flowering plants native to southern Africa.

These plants are characterized by their showy, bell-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, yellow, and purple. They typically grow in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in full sun. The leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, while the stems are often erect and branching.

Lychnanthos species are popular in horticulture for their ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
