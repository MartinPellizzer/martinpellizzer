Lycopodioides cladorrhizans

Lycopodioides cladorrhizans, commonly known as the clubmoss, is a small, evergreen plant that belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit and the presence of microphylls, which are small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces sporangia on specialized structures called strobila, which are typically clustered at the tips of the branches. Lycopodioides cladorrhizans is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices.
