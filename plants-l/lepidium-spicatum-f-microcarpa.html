Lepidium spicatum f. microcarpa

Lepidium spicatum f. microcarpa, commonly known as the dwarf celtuce, is a compact, herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, rounded leaves that are often used in culinary applications, particularly in Asian cuisine. The plant produces tiny, pale yellow flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. Its botanical characteristics include a short, upright stem and a fibrous root system.

This variety is valued for its mild flavor and nutritional content, making it a popular choice in specialty gardening and cooking.
