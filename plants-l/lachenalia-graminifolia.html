Lachenalia graminifolia

Lachenalia graminifolia, commonly known as the grass-like lachenalia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces tall, branched stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale yellow flowers. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil to thrive.

Lachenalia graminifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
