Lomatium observatorium

Lomatium observatorium, commonly known as the observatory lomatium, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its fern-like foliage and umbel-shaped inflorescences.

It is native to the Pacific Northwest of North America, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky slopes. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and flies. Its leaves are deeply lobed and often have a bluish-gray hue due to a dense covering of fine hairs.

The roots of Lomatium observatorium are fibrous and can be used in traditional herbal medicine, though the plant is generally not cultivated for commercial purposes.
