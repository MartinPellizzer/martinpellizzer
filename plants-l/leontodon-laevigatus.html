Leontodon laevigatus

Leontodon laevigatus, commonly known as the smooth leontodon, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble single blooms. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are basal, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with ray florets.

Leontodon laevigatus is often found in open habitats and is relatively easy to cultivate in gardens.
