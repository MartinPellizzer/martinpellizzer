Lebidiera pallida

Lebidiera pallida, commonly known as the pale lepidium, is a herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant features small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Lebidiera pallida is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is characterized by its low-growing habit and delicate appearance.
