Lisianthius arcuatus

Lisianthius arcuatus, commonly known as the goldenbush, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
