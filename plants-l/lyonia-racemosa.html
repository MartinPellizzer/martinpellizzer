Lyonia racemosa

Lyonia racemosa, commonly known as the common lyonia, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in forest understories. The plant features small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in racemes during the spring. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Lyonia racemosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
