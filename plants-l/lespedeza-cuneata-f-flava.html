Lespedeza cuneata f. flava

Lespedeza cuneata f. flava, commonly known as yellow lespedeza, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow to orange flowers. The plant is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of soil types, including sandy and loamy soils. It is a perennial herb that grows up to 1 to 2 meters tall, with slender stems and small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Yellow lespedeza is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
