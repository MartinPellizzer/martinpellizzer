Lilium henrici

Lilium henrici, commonly known as the Henrici's lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its tall, upright stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are long and narrow, arranged in a basal rosette, and its bulbs are used for propagation.

Lilium henrici is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
