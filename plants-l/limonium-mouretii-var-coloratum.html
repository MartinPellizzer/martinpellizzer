Limonium mouretii var. coloratum

Limonium mouretii var. coloratum, commonly known as the pink sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer, typically from June to August. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads that resemble a sea-holly appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand coastal conditions.
