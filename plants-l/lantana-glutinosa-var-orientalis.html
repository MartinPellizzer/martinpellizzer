Lantana glutinosa var. orientalis

Lantana glutinosa var. orientalis, commonly known as the oriental lantana, is a flowering shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are usually covered in a sticky substance, giving it its scientific name "glutinosa.". It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

However, it can be invasive in some regions and requires careful management to prevent spread.
