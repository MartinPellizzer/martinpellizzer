Leontice armeniaca

Leontice armeniaca, commonly known as Armenian leontice, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the family. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or woodland soils.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
