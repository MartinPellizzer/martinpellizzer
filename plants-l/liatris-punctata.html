Liatris punctata

Liatris punctata, commonly known as the showy blazing star, is a herbaceous perennial native to the central and eastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and spikes of showy, pinkish-purple flowers. The plant typically grows in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in prairies, meadows, and open woodlands. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense, showy inflorescences.

Liatris punctata is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
