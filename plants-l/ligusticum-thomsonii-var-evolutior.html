Ligusticum thomsonii var. evolutior

Ligusticum thomsonii var. evolutior, commonly known as the Thomson's lovage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its umbellate inflorescences and aromatic foliage. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a silvery-gray pubescence. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in compound umbels, and it produces small, dry fruits known as schizocarps.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
