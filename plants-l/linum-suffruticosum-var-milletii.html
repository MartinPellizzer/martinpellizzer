Linum suffruticosum var. milletii

Linum suffruticosum var. milletii, commonly known as the milletii flax, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that are clustered in loose inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
