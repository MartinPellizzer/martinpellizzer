Lamyropsis carpini

Lamyropsis carpini, commonly known as the mountain heather, is a small evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Lamyropsis carpini thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and long blooming period.
