Liatris cylindracea var. solitaria

Liatris cylindracea var. solitaria, commonly known as the slender blazing star, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small clusters of individual flowers. The plant typically grows in prairies, open woodlands, and disturbed sites, preferring well-drained soils. Its striking purple to pink flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is noted for its upright, slender stems and its role in native plant restoration projects.
