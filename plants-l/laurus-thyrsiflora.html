Laurus thyrsiflora

Laurus thyrsiflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved laurel, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dark green, narrow, leathery leaves that are aromatic and often used in culinary applications. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by spherical, berry-like fruits that are edible. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal, it is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
