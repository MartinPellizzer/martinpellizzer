Laurus borbonia

Laurus borbonia, commonly known as the bay laurel or sweet bay, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and parts of southern Europe, North Africa, and the Middle East. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are aromatic and often used in culinary applications. It produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by dark blue berries.

Laurus borbonia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and historical significance.
