Lagocodes cernua

Lagocodes cernua, commonly known as the drooping bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, branching stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland environments and thrives in partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Lagocodes cernua is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
