Leea venkobarowii

Leea venkobarowii, commonly known as the Venkobarowii leaf vine, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Leeaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine, often found in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are glossy and can reach up to 10 centimeters in diameter, making it a striking addition to botanical gardens.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated for its unique foliage.
