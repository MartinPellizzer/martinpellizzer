Linaria serpyllifolia

Linaria serpyllifolia, commonly known as the common sandwort, is a low-growing annual plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically features slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The flowers are showy, with two lips and five petals, arranged in a spike-like inflorescence. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

This species is known for its resilience and ability to colonize areas with poor soil conditions.
