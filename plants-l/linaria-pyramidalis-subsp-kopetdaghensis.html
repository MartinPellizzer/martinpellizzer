Linaria pyramidalis subsp. kopetdaghensis

Linaria pyramidalis subsp.

kopetdaghensis, commonly known as the Kopetdagh horned poppy, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of Central Asia. It features striking, spurred flowers that are typically orange or red in color, with a distinctive horn-like projection at the base of each petal.

The plant has slender, branched stems and narrow, linear leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
