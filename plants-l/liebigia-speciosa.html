Liebigia speciosa

Liebigia speciosa, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, waxy, floating leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter. The plant has a unique ability to trap air in its leaves, allowing it to float on water surfaces. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a strong, sweet fragrance that attracts pollinators.

Liebigia speciosa plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
