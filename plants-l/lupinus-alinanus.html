Lupinus alinanus

Lupinus alinanus, commonly known as the alpine lupine, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and is adapted to thrive in high-altitude environments. Lupinus alinanus is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through symbiotic relationships with bacteria.

It is often found in rocky or alpine habitats where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
