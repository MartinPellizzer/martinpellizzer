Laserpitium eliasii var. oscense

Laserpitium eliasii var. oscense, commonly known as the purple mountain everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes, preferring well-drained soils. Its striking purple to violet flowers bloom in late summer, adding vibrant color to alpine environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
