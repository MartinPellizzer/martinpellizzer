Lysimachia rubiginea

Lysimachia rubiginea, commonly known as red willow or red sorrel, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a low-growing herbaceous perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves that are often reddish or purplish in color, contributing to its ornamental appeal. Its flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences and are usually yellow or orange, blooming in early spring.

Lysimachia rubiginea is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
