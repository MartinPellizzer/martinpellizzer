Liparis halconensis

Liparis halconensis, commonly known as the Halcon Orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. The plant features a sympodial growth pattern, with rhizomes that produce new shoots each year. Its flowers are usually small and greenish-white, with intricate petal structures typical of orchids.

Liparis halconensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in specialty gardens.
