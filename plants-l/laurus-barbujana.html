Laurus barbujana

Laurus barbujana, commonly known as the Armenian laurel, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like laurel and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruits are small, black berries that ripen in late summer.

Laurus barbujana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in shaded environments.
