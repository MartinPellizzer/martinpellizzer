Lilium puniceum

Lilium puniceum, commonly known as the red lily, is a species of bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red to orange flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a tall stem that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. Its bulbs are adapted to survive in harsh, rocky environments.

Lilium puniceum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
