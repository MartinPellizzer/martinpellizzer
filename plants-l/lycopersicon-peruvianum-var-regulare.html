Lycopersicon peruvianum var. regulare

Lycopersicon peruvianum var. regulare, commonly known as the Peruvian tomato, is a botanical variant of the domesticated tomato.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, round fruits that typically range in color from red to orange. This variety is native to the Andean regions of South America and has been cultivated for its unique flavor and nutritional profile. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that are pollinated by insects, leading to the development of its distinctive fruit.

It is often used in traditional Andean cuisine and has gained interest for its potential in sustainable agriculture.
