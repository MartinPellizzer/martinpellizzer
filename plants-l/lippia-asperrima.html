Lippia asperrima

Lippia asperrima, commonly known as rough lippia, is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often hairy leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and digestive properties.

Lippia asperrima is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
