Lactuca ovatifolia

Lactuca ovatifolia, commonly known as the ovate-leaf lettuce, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family, Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically arranged in a composite head, a common feature among members of its family. It is native to regions of Europe and Asia and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Lactuca ovatifolia is not typically cultivated for consumption but is studied for its potential medicinal properties.
