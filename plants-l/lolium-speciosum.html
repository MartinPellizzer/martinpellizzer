Lolium speciosum

Lolium speciosum, commonly known as the showy ryegrass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its ornamental, spike-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas and is often found in disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are small and clustered in dense, showy spikes.

Lolium speciosum is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and landscapes.
