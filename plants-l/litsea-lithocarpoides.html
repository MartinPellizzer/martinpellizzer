Litsea lithocarpoides

Litsea lithocarpoides, commonly known as the rock-pear tree, is a member of the Lauraceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a glossy upper surface. Its flowers are small and clustered, and it produces drupes that are often used in traditional medicine.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and its potential ecological benefits in forest ecosystems.
