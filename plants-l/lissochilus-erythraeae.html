Lissochilus erythraeae

Lissochilus erythraeae, commonly known as the Red Sea orchid, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Arabian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, spurred petals and a compact, terrestrial growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or reddish in color, which bloom during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to its harsh environment.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
