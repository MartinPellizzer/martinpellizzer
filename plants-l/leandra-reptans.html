Leandra reptans

Leandra reptans, commonly known as the climbing hibiscus, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough texture.

Leandra reptans thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
