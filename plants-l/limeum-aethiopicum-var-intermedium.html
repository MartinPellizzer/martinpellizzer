Limeum aethiopicum var. intermedium

Limeum aethiopicum var. intermedium, commonly known as the African violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are typically velvety and green, with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white, which bloom throughout the year under optimal conditions. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Its compact growth habit makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and ornamental purposes.
