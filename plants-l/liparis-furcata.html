Liparis furcata

Liparis furcata, commonly known as the forked orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial herb with a short, upright stem and linear, grass-like leaves. The plant produces delicate, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in a raceme, often with a distinctive forked shape. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Liparis furcata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in some horticultural collections.
