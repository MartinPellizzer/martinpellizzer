Lonicera sullivantii

Lonicera sullivantii, commonly known as Sullivant's honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The plant is characterized by its red, fleshy berries that mature in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.

Lonicera sullivantii is valued for its ornamental value and ecological importance as a food source for wildlife.
