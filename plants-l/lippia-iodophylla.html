Lippia iodophylla

Lippia iodophylla, commonly known as the iodine leaf plant, is a species of flowering plant in the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its unique appearance and fragrance, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
