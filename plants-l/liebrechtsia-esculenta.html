Liebrechtsia esculenta

Liebrechtsia esculenta, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Liebrechtsia esculenta is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
