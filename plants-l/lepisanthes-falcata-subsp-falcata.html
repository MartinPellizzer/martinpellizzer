Lepisanthes falcata subsp. falcata

Lepisanthes falcata subsp.

falcata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed orchid, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and delicate, often fragrant flowers.

The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories. Its flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, with a distinctive lip that aids in pollination by insects.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
