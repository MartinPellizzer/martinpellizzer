Lycopodiella pseudocurvata

Lycopodiella pseudocurvata, commonly known as the dwarf clubmoss, is a small, evergreen plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces sporophytes that develop into spore-bearing structures called strobila, which are typically clustered at the base. Lycopodiella pseudocurvata thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest floors or rocky crevices.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important component of certain ecosystems.
