Lilium candidum f. striatum

Lilium candidum f. striatum, commonly known as the striped white lily, is a botanical variant of the white lily characterized by its distinctive striped petals.

This cultivar features pale white petals with narrow, darker stripes running along their length, creating a striking visual contrast. It is native to the Mediterranean region and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with lance-shaped leaves and fragrant, trumpet-shaped flowers.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for floral arrangements and landscape design.
