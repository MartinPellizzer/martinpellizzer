Lepisorus clathratus var. papakensis

Lepisorus clathratus var. papakensis, commonly known as the Papakansis water fern, is a rare and delicate aquatic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, bright green fronds that float on the water's surface. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its rhizomes form dense colonies, helping to stabilize the substrate and provide habitat for small aquatic organisms.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
