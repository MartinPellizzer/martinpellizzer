Leucanthemum pachyphyllum

Leucanthemum pachyphyllum, commonly known as the thick-leaved oxeye, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and yellow-orange flowers that resemble daisies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
