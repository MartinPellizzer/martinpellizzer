Litsea citrifolia

Litsea citrifolia, commonly known as the citrus leaf tree or Indian bay leaf, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, elliptical leaves that emit a citrus-like aroma, particularly when crushed. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by oval, leathery fruits that contain a single seed. Its leaves are widely used in traditional medicine and as a spice in various cuisines.

The plant thrives in humid climates and is often cultivated for its aromatic foliage and ornamental value.
