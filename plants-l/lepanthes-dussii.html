Lepanthes dussii

Lepanthes dussii, commonly known as the Dussi's slipper orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees in humid tropical forests. The plant features a delicate, showy flower with a distinctive slipper-shaped pouch, which is a characteristic trait of many orchids in the genus Lepanthes. Its flowers are usually pale green or white, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insects.

Lepanthes dussii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
