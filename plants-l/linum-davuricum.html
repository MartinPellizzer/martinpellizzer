Linum davuricum

Linum davuricum, commonly known as the Siberian flax or narrowleaf flax, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the genus Linum in the family Fabaceae, which includes many legumes. The plant is characterized by its slender stems and small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Linum davuricum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
