Lonchocarpus araripensis

Lonchocarpus araripensis, commonly known as the Brazilian legume or araripense bean, is a tropical shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow flowers. The plant produces pods containing small, kidney-shaped seeds that are often used in traditional medicine.

It is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil, making it ecologically significant in its native habitat.
