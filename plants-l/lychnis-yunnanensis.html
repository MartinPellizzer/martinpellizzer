Lychnis yunnanensis

Lychnis yunnanensis, commonly known as the Yunnan campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan Province in China.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink or red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, thriving at elevations between 2,000 and 4,000 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
