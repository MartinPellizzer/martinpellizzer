Lauro-cerasus officinalis f. mischeana

Lauro-cerasus officinalis f. mischeana, commonly known as the cherry laurel or Prunus laurocerasus 'Mischeana', is a hybrid shrub or small tree belonging to the Prunus genus.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and showy, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It is native to the Mediterranean region and is widely cultivated for its ornamental value.

This variety is known for its compact growth habit and resistance to diseases, making it a popular choice in gardens and landscapes.
