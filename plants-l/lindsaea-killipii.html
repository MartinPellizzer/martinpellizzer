Lindsaea killipii

Lindsaea killipii, commonly known as the Killip's spikemoss, is a species of fern ally native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Lindsaeaceae and is characterized by its erect, branched stems covered with small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are clustered in specialized leaves called sporophylls. Lindsaea killipii typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices.
