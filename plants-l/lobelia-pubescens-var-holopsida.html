Lobelia pubescens var. holopsida

Lobelia pubescens var. holopsida, commonly known as the hairy lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its scientific name. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Lobelia pubescens var. holopsida is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
