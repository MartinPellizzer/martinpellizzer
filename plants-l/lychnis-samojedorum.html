Lychnis samojedorum

Lychnis samojedorum, commonly known as the Siberian fireweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Eurasia.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in cold climates. The plant features upright stems with lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, pink to red flowers in late summer. Its flowers are arranged in dense, terminal clusters and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Lychnis samojedorum is adapted to harsh environments and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
