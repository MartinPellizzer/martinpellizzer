Lessingianthus cipoensis

Lessingianthus cipoensis, commonly known as the Cipo sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features bright yellow ray flowers surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers, typical of the genus Lessingianthus. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
