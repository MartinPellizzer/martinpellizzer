Ligustrum wallichii

Ligustrum wallichii, commonly known as the Chinese privet, is a deciduous shrub native to Asia.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, simple leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by black, oval berries. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its adaptability and rapid growth.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions, outcompeting native vegetation.
