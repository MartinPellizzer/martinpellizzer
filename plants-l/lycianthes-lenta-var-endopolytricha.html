Lycianthes lenta var. endopolytricha

Lycianthes lenta var. endopolytricha, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into globose, orange-red berries. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

The berries are toxic to humans and animals, containing solanine and other alkaloids.
