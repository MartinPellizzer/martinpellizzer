Licuala wrayi

Licuala wrayi, commonly known as the Wray's palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its large, pinnately compound leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. Its inflorescences are typically clustered at the base of the plant, with both male and female flowers present on the same inflorescence.

Licuala wrayi is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
