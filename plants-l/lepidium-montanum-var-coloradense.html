Lepidium montanum var. coloradense

Lepidium montanum var. coloradense, commonly known as the Colorado cress, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small, white or yellowish flowers and has pinnately divided leaves.

Its botanical features make it an important component of high-altitude ecosystems.
