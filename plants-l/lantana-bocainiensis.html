Lantana bocainiensis

Lantana bocainiensis, commonly known as the Brazilian lantana, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, brightly colored flowers, typically in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant is shrub-like, with opposite leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Lantana bocainiensis is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
