Loxocarya striata

Loxocarya striata, commonly known as the striped morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, adhesive substance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Loxocarya striata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
