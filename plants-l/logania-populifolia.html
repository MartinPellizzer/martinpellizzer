Logania populifolia

Logania populifolia, commonly known as the maple-leaved logania, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Loganiaceae and is characterized by its palmate leaves that resemble those of maple trees. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by capsule fruits containing seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
