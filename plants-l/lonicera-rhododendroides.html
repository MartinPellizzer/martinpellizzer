Lonicera rhododendroides

Lonicera rhododendroides, commonly known as the creeping honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and have a smooth texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and white, which bloom in early spring. Its red berries are edible for birds but not recommended for human consumption.

The plant is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
