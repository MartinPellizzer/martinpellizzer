Lepanthopsis prolifera

Lepanthopsis prolifera, commonly known as the orchid vine, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and the production of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows along forest canopies and thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems.

Lepanthopsis prolifera is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
