Laburnum laburnum subsp. jacquinianum

Laburnum laburnum subsp.

jacquinianum, commonly known as the golden chain tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe. It is characterized by its long, drooping clusters of bright yellow flowers that appear in spring.

The leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces elongated, woody pods that contain flat, winged seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to a variety of soil types.
