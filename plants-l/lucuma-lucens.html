Lucuma lucens

Lucuma lucens, commonly known as the golden fruit tree, is a South American plant native to the Andes region.

It produces a large, yellow-orange fruit with a sweet, creamy pulp that is rich in nutrients. The plant is a member of the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses. Its leaves are broad and lobed, and it thrives in high-altitude, temperate climates.

The fruit is often used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent in various dishes.
