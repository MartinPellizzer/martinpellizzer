Lythrum thesioides subsp. geminiflorum

Lythrum thesioides subsp.

geminiflorum, commonly known as the twin-flower lythrum, is a herbaceous plant native to North America. It belongs to the Lythraceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers.

The plant produces pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in moist, sunny habitats like wet meadows and streambanks.
