Lepidium lasiocarpum var. rotundum

Lepidium lasiocarpum var. rotundum, commonly known as the round-seeded cress, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant features small, white or pale yellow flowers and has a distinctive, rounded seed pod that gives it its varietal name. Its leaves are typically alternate and pinnately divided, with a slightly hairy surface.

This species is often found in rocky or arid environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
