Liquidambar styraciflua var. mexicana

Liquidambar styraciflua var. mexicana, commonly known as Mexican sweetgum, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its star-shaped leaves and distinctive, spiky fruit capsules. The plant produces a fragrant resin, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a source of gum.
