Leucanthemum atratum var. incisum

Leucanthemum atratum var. incisum, commonly known as the black-eyed Susan variety with incised leaves, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black central disk surrounded by yellow ray florets. The leaves are typically lobed and have a serrated or incised margin, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

This variety is known for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
