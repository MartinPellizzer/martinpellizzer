Lupinus hartmannii

Lupinus hartmannii, commonly known as Hartmann's lupine, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showing adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds.

Lupinus hartmannii plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
