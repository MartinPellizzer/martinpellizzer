Leptorkis atropurpurea

Leptorkis atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple-leaved kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that are typically purple or reddish in color, which help it store water and withstand arid conditions. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the winter months. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal, it is widely cultivated as a decorative plant.
