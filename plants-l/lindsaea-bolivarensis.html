Lindsaea bolivarensis

Lindsaea bolivarensis, commonly known as the Bolivian lace fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are often covered in fine, hair-like structures. The plant typically grows on tree trunks or branches in humid, shaded environments. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and produce spores for propagation.

Lindsaea bolivarensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
