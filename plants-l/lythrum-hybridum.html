Lythrum hybridum

Lythrum hybridum, commonly known as purple loosestrife, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy spikes of violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stem, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in wetland habitats such as marshes, ponds, and ditches, often outcompeting native vegetation.

Due to its aggressive growth, it is considered an invasive species in many parts of North America.
