Lonchocarpus crassicalyx

Lonchocarpus crassicalyx, commonly known as the broad-leaved senna, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces distinctive pod fruits that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

It is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in ecological and agricultural contexts.
