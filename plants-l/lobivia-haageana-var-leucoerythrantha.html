Lobivia haageana var. leucoerythrantha

Lobivia haageana var. leucoerythrantha, commonly known as the White-Flowered Lobivia, is a succulent plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rounded stems and dense, spiny foliage. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its striking appearance and slow growth rate, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
