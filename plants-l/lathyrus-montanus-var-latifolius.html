Lathyrus montanus var. latifolius

Lathyrus montanus var. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf mountain vetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three ovate to lanceolate leaflets. The plant produces clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
