Lycopodium underwoodianum

Lycopodium underwoodianum, commonly known as the Underwood's clubmoss, is a small, evergreen plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, creeping growth habit and dense clusters of needle-like leaves. The plant produces spore-bearing cones, known as strobili, which are typically reddish in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Lycopodium underwoodianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological systems.
