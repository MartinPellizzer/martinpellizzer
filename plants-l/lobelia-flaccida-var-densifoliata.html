Lobelia flaccida var. densifoliata

Lobelia flaccida var. densifoliata, commonly known as the dense-leaved lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, erect stems covered in soft, hairy foliage. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
