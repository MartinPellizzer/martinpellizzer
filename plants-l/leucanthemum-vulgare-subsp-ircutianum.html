Leucanthemum vulgare subsp. ircutianum

Leucanthemum vulgare subsp.

ircutianum, commonly known as the common oxeye daisy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its white ray florets surrounding a yellow disc flower, giving it a daisy-like appearance.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun. It produces numerous small, yellow disk flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is known for its hardy nature and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
