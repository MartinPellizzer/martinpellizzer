Lupinus lyallii subsp. lyallii

Lupinus lyallii subsp.

lyallii, commonly known as the Lyall's lupine, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of pea-like flowers that range in color from blue to purple.

The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Lupinus lyallii subsp.

lyallii is also valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation, supporting local biodiversity.
