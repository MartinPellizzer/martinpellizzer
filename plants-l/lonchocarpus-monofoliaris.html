Lonchocarpus monofoliaris

Lonchocarpus monofoliaris, commonly known as the single-leaf legume, is a tropical plant native to regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique foliage, featuring a single, large leaf that emerges from the stem. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes many leguminous species known for nitrogen-fixing capabilities. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, producing pods that contain the seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and research settings.
