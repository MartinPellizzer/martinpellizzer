Lehmannia ocymoidea

Lehmannia ocymoidea, commonly known as the lemon thyme or lemon-scented thyme, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, lemon-scented leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, adding visual interest to gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its essential oils are also used in aromatherapy and natural remedies.
