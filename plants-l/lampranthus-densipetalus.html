Lampranthus densipetalus

Lampranthus densipetalus, commonly known as the dense-petaled ice plant, is a succulent species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and purple, typically during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its resilience to drought and ability to bloom repeatedly make it a hardy and attractive addition to arid landscapes.
