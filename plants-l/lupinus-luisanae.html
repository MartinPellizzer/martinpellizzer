Lupinus luisanae

Lupinus luisanae, commonly known as Luisana's lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States, particularly California.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of blue or purple pea-shaped flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and is adapted to grow in dry, rocky soils.

Lupinus luisanae plays an important ecological role by fixing nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plant life.
