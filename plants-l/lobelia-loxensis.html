Lobelia loxensis

Lobelia loxensis, commonly known as the swamp lobelia, is a herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant features showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Lobelia loxensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
