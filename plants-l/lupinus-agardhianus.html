Lupinus agardhianus

Lupinus agardhianus, commonly known as the Agardh's lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of showy blue to purple flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Lupinus agardhianus is also valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation, which benefits surrounding plant life.
