Lepicaulon

Lepicaulon, commonly known as the narrow-leaved water lily, is a aquatic plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It features broad, waxy leaves that float on the water's surface, supported by long, slender petioles. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the early morning and close by midday. Its root system helps stabilize the soil and provides habitat for aquatic organisms.

Lepicaulon plays a vital role in maintaining the ecological balance of its wetland environment.
