Limonium sewerzowii

Limonium sewerzowii, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads, which are surrounded by bracts that resemble leaves. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along the shoreline, where it is adapted to salty and windy conditions. Limonium sewerzowii is a perennial species that thrives in full sun and is often used in coastal landscaping for its ornamental value.

Its ability to tolerate salt spray makes it an important plant in coastal ecosystems.
