Lactuca czerepanovii

Lactuca czerepanovii, commonly known as the Czerepanov's lettuce, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family, Asteraceae.

It is native to Central Asia and is characterized by its basal rosette of pale green, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in composite flower heads, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
