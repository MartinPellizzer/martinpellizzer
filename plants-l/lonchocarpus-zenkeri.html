Lonchocarpus zenkeri

Lonchocarpus zenkeri, commonly known as the African senna, is a tropical shrub native to Africa and parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is notable for its striking yellow flowers and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. It is also recognized for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of leguminous protein.

However, some parts of the plant contain toxic compounds, requiring caution in handling and consumption.
