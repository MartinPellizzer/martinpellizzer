Lophocarpus bonia

Lophocarpus bonia, commonly known as the pink billygoat pea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers that are clustered in racemes, which are typically pollinated by insects.

Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
