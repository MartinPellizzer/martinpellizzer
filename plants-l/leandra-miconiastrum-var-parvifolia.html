Leandra miconiastrum var. parvifolia

Leandra miconiastrum var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved miconia, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically red or yellow when mature, containing numerous small seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
