Lychnis pauciflora

Lychnis pauciflora, commonly known as the few-flowered catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its pink or red flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, thriving in temperate climates. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Lychnis pauciflora is known for its compact growth habit and is often found in open, disturbed habitats.
