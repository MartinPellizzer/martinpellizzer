Leptosyne pinnata var. integrifolia

Leptosyne pinnata var. integrifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf leptosyne, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with smooth margins, which give it its varietal name "integrifolia.". The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters during the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Its foliage is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to support local wildlife.
