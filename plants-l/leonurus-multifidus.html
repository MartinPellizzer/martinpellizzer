Leonurus multifidus

Leonurus multifidus, commonly known as hedgehog's breakfast, is a flowering plant native to southern and eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and small, opposite leaves that are often lobed or toothed. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is often found in dry grasslands, wastelands, and along roadsides.

Leonurus multifidus is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
