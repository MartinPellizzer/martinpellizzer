Lycopodioides balfourii

Lycopodioides balfourii, commonly known as the Balfour's clubmoss, is a species of lycophyte belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is a small, herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant features a short, erect stem with whorled leaves that are narrow and linear in shape. It produces sporophytes that bear microsporangia and megasporangia, typical of its evolutionary lineage.

Lycopodioides balfourii plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
