Laurus cupularis

Laurus cupularis, commonly known as the bay laurel, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a small evergreen shrub or tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its aromatic, dark green, glossy leaves. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a strongly aromatic scent when crushed. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by dark blue, fleshy berries.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in culinary and medicinal applications.
