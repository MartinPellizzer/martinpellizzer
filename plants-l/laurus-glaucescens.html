Laurus glaucescens

Laurus glaucescens, commonly known as the sweet bay or Turkish bay leaf, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a small evergreen shrub or tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by dark blue berries. Its leaves are widely used in culinary applications and are also valued for their aromatic properties.

The botanical features of Laurus glaucescens make it a popular ornamental and medicinal plant.
