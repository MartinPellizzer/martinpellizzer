Lonicera guebriantiana

Lonicera guebriantiana, commonly known as the Guebriant honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are usually yellow or orange in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that matures in late summer, attracting various bird species.

Lonicera guebriantiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
