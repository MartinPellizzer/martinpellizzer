Laportea cuspidata f. bulbifera

Laportea cuspidata f. bulbifera, commonly known as the bulbiferous thimbleweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, daisy-like flowers. The plant produces bulb-like structures called bulbils, which are used for vegetative propagation. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

The species is adapted to moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
