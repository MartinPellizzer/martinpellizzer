Lepanthes laxa

Lepanthes laxa, commonly known as the loose lepanthes, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, pendulous flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and green. The plant has a compact growth habit with short, fleshy roots and is often found growing in shaded, humid environments. Its leaves are usually elliptical and covered in a fine, velvety texture.

Lepanthes laxa is valued in horticulture for its elegant appearance and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
