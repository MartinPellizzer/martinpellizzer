Labatia standleyana

Labatia standleyana, commonly known as the purple orchid vine, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant produces small, elongated leaves that are typically arranged alternately along its stems. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Labatia standleyana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
