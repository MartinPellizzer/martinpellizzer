Lychnis alpina var. americana

Lychnis alpina var. americana, commonly known as the American fireweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. The plant features erect, hairy stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its showy, pink to red flowers appear in clusters at the tips of the stems during the summer months.

It is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils, making it a common sight in roadside areas and along riverbanks.
