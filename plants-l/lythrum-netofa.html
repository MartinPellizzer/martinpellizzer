Lythrum netofa

Lythrum netofa, commonly known as the red root sedges, is a flowering plant belonging to the Lythraceae family.

It is native to regions of Africa and is characterized by its upright, grass-like stems and clusters of small, showy red or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, often in tropical and subtropical areas. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Lythrum netofa is often used in ornamental gardening due to its vibrant floral display and adaptability to various soil conditions.
