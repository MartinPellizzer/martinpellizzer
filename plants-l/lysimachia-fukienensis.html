Lysimachia fukienensis

Lysimachia fukienensis, commonly known as the Fukien chickweed, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, delicate leaves. The plant produces clusters of white or pinkish flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
