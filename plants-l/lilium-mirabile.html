Lilium mirabile

Lilium mirabile, commonly known as the miraculous lily, is a striking perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 15 centimeters in diameter, typically in shades of white, pink, or red. The plant features a robust rhizomatous root system and strap-like, lanceolate leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained, loamy soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and protected in its natural habitat.
