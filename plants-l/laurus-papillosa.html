Laurus papillosa

Laurus papillosa, commonly known as the silky laurel, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, glossy, dark green leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. It produces small, white, fragrant flowers followed by dark blue berries.

Laurus papillosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its use in traditional medicine.
