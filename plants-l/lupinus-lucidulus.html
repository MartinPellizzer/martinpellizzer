Lupinus lucidulus

Lupinus lucidulus, commonly known as the silver lupin, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its silvery, hairy leaves and tall spikes of purple or blue flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in dry conditions, and it is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Lupinus lucidulus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in restoration projects due to its ecological benefits.
