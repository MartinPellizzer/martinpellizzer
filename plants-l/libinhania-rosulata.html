Libinhania rosulata

Libinhania rosulata, commonly known as the rose-leaved flax, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep orange. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a sunny to partially shaded environment.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
