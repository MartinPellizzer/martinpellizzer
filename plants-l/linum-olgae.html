Linum olgae

Linum olgae, commonly known as the Persian flax, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of blue or purple flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Linum olgae is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically for fiber and oil production.
