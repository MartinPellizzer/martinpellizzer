Lesia

Lesia, commonly known as the "lesia plant," is a rare flowering species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pale blue petals and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Lesia flowers in late spring, producing clusters of small, fragrant blooms that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited habitat and susceptibility to environmental changes, the lesia plant is considered a conservation concern.
