Ligularia kanaitzensis var. subnudicaulis

Ligularia kanaitzensis var. subnudicaulis, commonly known as the Kanaitz Mountain Ligularia, is a perennial plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers in the summer, which are clustered in a terminal inflorescence. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in forested areas along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and valuable component of its native ecosystem.
