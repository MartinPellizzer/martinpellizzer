Laportea glandulosa

Laportea glandulosa, commonly known as the roughleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy purple to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its foliage is often covered with small, sticky glands, which give it a distinctive texture and appearance.

Laportea glandulosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
