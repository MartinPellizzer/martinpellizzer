Lotus australis var. laevigatus

Lotus australis var. laevigatus, commonly known as the smooth lotus, is a perennial aquatic plant native to the eastern coast of Australia.

It features a dense network of rhizomes that anchor it in muddy substrates, allowing it to thrive in freshwater environments. The plant produces strikingly large, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple, with a distinctive veined pattern on the petals. Its leaves are broad and floating, often with a waxy surface that helps repel water.

The smooth lotus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
