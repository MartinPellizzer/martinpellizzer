Lupinus multitinctus

Lupinus multitinctus, commonly known as the multicolored lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Lupinus multitinctus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological systems as a nitrogen-fixing plant.
