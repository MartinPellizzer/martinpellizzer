Linaria turcomanica

Linaria turcomanica, commonly known as the Turcoman toadflax, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and vibrant yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, adapting to harsh environmental conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that reduce water loss, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain the seeds.

Linaria turcomanica is often found in rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
