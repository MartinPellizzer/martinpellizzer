Lupinus eriocalyx

Lupinus eriocalyx, commonly known as the coastal lupine, is a perennial herb native to the western coast of North America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, glandular stipules. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils along coastal dunes and cliffs.

Lupinus eriocalyx plays an important role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems and is a valuable plant for ecological restoration projects.
