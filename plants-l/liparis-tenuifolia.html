Liparis tenuifolia

Liparis tenuifolia, commonly known as the slender leaved orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, grass-like foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pale green or white, often with a slight purple tinge. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions for horticultural purposes.
