Lonchitis adscensionis

Lonchitis adscensionis, commonly known as the climbing bellflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically features opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces clusters of blue or violet flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
