Libanotis korovinii

Libanotis korovinii, commonly known as the Siberian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern Mongolia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, pale blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Libanotis korovinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native ecosystems.
