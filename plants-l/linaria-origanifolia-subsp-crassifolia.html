Linaria origanifolia subsp. crassifolia

Linaria origanifolia subsp.

crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved toadflax, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, branched stems that grow from 30 to 60 centimeters in height.

The leaves are lance-shaped, with a rough texture and a strong, aromatic scent. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in clusters and blooming from late spring to early summer.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in rocky or gravelly habitats.
