Lobelia hypoleuca var. heterocarpa

Lobelia hypoleuca var. heterocarpa, commonly known as the narrowleaf lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially shaded woodland areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
