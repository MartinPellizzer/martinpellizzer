Leonotis pole-evansii

Leonotis pole-evansii, commonly known as the African heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its tubular, violet-blue flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered with a fine, fuzzy texture.

Leonotis pole-evansii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
