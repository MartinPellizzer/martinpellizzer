Laplacea fruticosa var. pulcherrima

Laplacea fruticosa var. pulcherrima, commonly known as the purple flame shrub, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and vibrant purple-pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant features small, narrow leaves and produces fruits that are edible but typically not consumed by humans. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its striking appearance, it is often cultivated in coastal regions where it can withstand salty air and coastal winds.
