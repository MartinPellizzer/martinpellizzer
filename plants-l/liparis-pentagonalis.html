Liparis pentagonalis

Liparis pentagonalis, commonly known as the pentagonal orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, pentagonal-shaped leaves that are often dark green with a glossy appearance. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, which bloom during the rainy season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
