Lactuca amurensis

Lactuca amurensis, commonly known as Amur lettuce, is a species of lettuce native to eastern Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and flowering heads. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments and is often found in river valleys and mountainous regions. Its leaves are edible and have a mild, slightly bitter taste, making it a cultivated vegetable in some parts of Asia.

The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes other members like daisies and chicory.
