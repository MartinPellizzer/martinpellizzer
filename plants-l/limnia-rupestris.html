Limnia rupestris

Limnia rupestris, commonly known as the rock water flea, is a small aquatic plant found in freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its delicate, floating leaves that are typically less than 1 cm in diameter. The plant has a simple, branching stem and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged. Limnia rupestris thrives in still or slow-moving waters with high light exposure.

Its presence is an indicator of good water quality and ecological balance in its habitat.
