Litsea insignis

Litsea insignis, commonly known as the fragrant evergreen tree, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand and Vietnam.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that can reach heights of up to 10 meters, characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically yellow or orange when mature. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed.

Litsea insignis is valued not only for its ornamental qualities but also for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
