Lepanthes helgae

Lepanthes helgae, commonly known as the Helgae's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored flowers that are typically borne on slender, erect stems. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its intricate floral structure, which includes specialized sepals and petals. Lepanthes helgae thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing as an epiphyte on trees or rocks.

Its botanical significance lies in its role within the diverse orchid flora of the Neotropics.
