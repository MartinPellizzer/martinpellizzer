Lamprothyrsus hieronymi var. nervosus

Lamprothyrsus hieronymi var. nervosus, commonly known as the velvetleaf sedge, is a perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that have a distinctive velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically borne on slender, branching stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of aquatic and semi-aquatic ecosystems.
