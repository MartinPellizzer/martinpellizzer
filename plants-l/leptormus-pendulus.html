Leptormus pendulus

Leptormus pendulus, commonly known as the hanging orchid, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its pendulous growth habit, with flowers hanging from its stems. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
