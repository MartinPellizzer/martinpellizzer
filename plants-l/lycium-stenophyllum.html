Lycium stenophyllum

Lycium stenophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved boxthorn, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its berries, which are typically red or orange, are edible and used in traditional medicine.

Lycium stenophyllum plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
