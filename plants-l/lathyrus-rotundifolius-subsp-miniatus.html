Lathyrus rotundifolius subsp. miniatus

Lathyrus rotundifolius subsp.

miniatus, commonly known as the dwarf red vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its small, rounded leaves and clusters of pink or red pea-like flowers.

The plant produces small, oblong pods that contain several angular seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
