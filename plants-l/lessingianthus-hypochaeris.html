Lessingianthus hypochaeris

Lessingianthus hypochaeris, commonly known as the yellow oxalis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that resemble those of the oxalis genus. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has alternate, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish or purplish hue along the veins.

The flowers are small and clustered in a composite head, which is a common feature among members of the Asteraceae family.
