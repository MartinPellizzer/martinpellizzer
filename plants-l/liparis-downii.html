Liparis downii

Liparis downii, commonly known as the Down's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. The plant features a pseudobulb with a single leaf and produces a spike of fragrant, greenish-yellow flowers. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil.

Liparis downii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
