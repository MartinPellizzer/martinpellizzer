Lepanthes attenboroughii

Lepanthes attenboroughii, commonly known as the Attenborough's slipper orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the cloud forests of Costa Rica.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful flowers that resemble small slippers. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments, showcasing its epiphytic nature. Its unique floral structure includes a pouch-like lip that aids in pollination by attracting specific insects.

Due to its rarity and beauty, it is highly sought after by botanists and conservationists alike.
