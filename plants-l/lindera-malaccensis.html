Lindera malaccensis

Lindera malaccensis, commonly known as the Malacca woodbine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.

The fruit is a small, berry-like structure that contains several seeds.
