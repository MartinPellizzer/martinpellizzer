Leea herbacea

Leea herbacea, commonly known as the creeping leaf vine, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in umbels. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain numerous seeds.

Leea herbacea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in shaded garden settings.
