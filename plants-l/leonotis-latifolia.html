Leonotis latifolia

Leonotis latifolia, commonly known as the blanket flower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or perennial herb, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a rough texture, while its flowers range in color from yellow to orange.

Leonotis latifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
