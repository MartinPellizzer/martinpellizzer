Laurus umbellata

Laurus umbellata, commonly known as sweet bay or Turkish bay leaf, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves that are aromatic and widely used in cooking. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers followed by dark, spherical berries. Its leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.

Laurus umbellata thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental and functional plant in gardens.
