Lycium infaustum

Lycium infaustum, commonly known as the bitter lycium, is a shrub native to regions of China and the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces red berries that are edible but have a bitter taste, often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture, while its flowers are typically clustered in inflorescences.

Lycium infaustum is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh climates.
