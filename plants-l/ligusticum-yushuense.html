Ligusticum yushuense

Ligusticum yushuense, commonly known as Yushu lovage, is a perennial herb native to the Qinghai-Tibet Plateau in China.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescences. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions with cool, moist climates, often found in rocky or alpine environments. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a strong, pungent odor, while its flowers are small and white or pale yellow.

Ligusticum yushuense is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional Tibetan medicine.
