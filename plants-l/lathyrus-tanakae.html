Lathyrus tanakae

Lathyrus tanakae, commonly known as the Tanaka's vetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in the summer months. Lathyrus tanakae is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing its resilience to harsh conditions.

Its seeds are encased in a hard pod, which aids in dispersal by wind or animals.
