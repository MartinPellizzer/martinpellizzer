Lobivia zecheri var. fungiflora

Lobivia zecheri var. fungiflora, commonly known as the Fairy Castle Cactus, is a unique succulent native to the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and dense, branching structure that resembles a fairy castle. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to arid conditions.

It requires minimal water and well-draining soil to thrive.
