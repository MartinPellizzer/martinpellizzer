Laelia albida f. sulphurea

Laelia albida f. sulphurea, commonly known as the sulphur-yellow laelia, is a hybrid orchid native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, sulphur-yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows as a compact, sympodial orchid with fleshy, dark green pseudobulbs. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, and it thrives in cool, high-altitude environments with ample sunlight.

This cultivar is prized for its vibrant color and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value.
