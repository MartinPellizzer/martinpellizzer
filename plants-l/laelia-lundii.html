Laelia lundii

Laelia lundii, commonly known as the Lund's orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments within the Atlantic Forest biome. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals.

Laelia lundii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
