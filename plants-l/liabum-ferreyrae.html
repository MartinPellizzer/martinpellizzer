Liabum ferreyrae

Liabum ferreyrae, commonly known as the Ferreyra's liabum, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family, characterized by its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.
