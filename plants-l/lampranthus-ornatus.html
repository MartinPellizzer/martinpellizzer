Lampranthus ornatus

Lampranthus ornatus, commonly known as the "Rainbow Bush," is a strikingly colorful plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family, known for its succulent characteristics and ability to store water in its fleshy leaves and stems. The plant produces vibrant, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom in late winter to early spring. Its foliage is typically bright green with a waxy coating, aiding in water retention.

Lampranthus ornatus thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
