Lamium tetrahit

Lamium tetrahit, commonly known as purple deadnettle, is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs and have a distinctive purple underside. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Lamium tetrahit is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
