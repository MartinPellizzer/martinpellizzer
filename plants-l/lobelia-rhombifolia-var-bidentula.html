Lobelia rhombifolia var. bidentula

Lobelia rhombifolia var. bidentula, commonly known as the two-toothed lobelia, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems with opposite, rhombic-shaped leaves that have two distinct teeth at the base. The plant produces showy, tubular blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its foliage and floral structure make it a distinctive and ornamental species within the Lobelioideae family.
