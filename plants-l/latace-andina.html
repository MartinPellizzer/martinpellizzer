Latace andina

Latace andina, commonly known as the Andean latace, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountain range.

It belongs to the Lataceae family, which is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions, where it thrives in cool, moist climates. Its leaves are often fragrant and used in traditional medicine by local communities.

The flowers, which bloom in shades of white and pink, attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
