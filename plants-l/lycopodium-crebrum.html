Lycopodium crebrum

Lycopodium crebrum, commonly known as the clubmoss, is a vascular plant belonging to the family Lycopodiales.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and a creeping or erect growth habit. The plant produces spores in specialized structures called strobila, which are typically clustered at the tips of its stems. Lycopodium crebrum thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forests or along stream banks.

Its ancient lineage dates back to the Carboniferous period, making it a primitive plant with significant evolutionary importance.
