Limonium roborowskii

Limonium roborowskii, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flower heads, which are surrounded by bracts that resemble leaves. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is well adapted to saline environments. Its flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Limonium roborowskii is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
