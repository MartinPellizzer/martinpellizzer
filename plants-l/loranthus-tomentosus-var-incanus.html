Loranthus tomentosus var. incanus

Loranthus tomentosus var. incanus, commonly known as the silver-haired mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that grows on various host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.

It has small, leathery leaves that are densely covered with fine, white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. Its berries are usually red or orange and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This species is often found in humid, shaded environments and plays a role in the ecological interactions of its host trees.
