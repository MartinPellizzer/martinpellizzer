Liparis dolichopoda

Liparis dolichopoda, commonly known as the long-stem orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces pendulous, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive lip that is longer than the rest of the flower. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Botanically, it belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is noted for its unique floral morphology and ecological adaptability.
