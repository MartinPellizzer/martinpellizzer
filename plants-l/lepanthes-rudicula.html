Lepanthes rudicula

Lepanthes rudicula, commonly known as the small-leaf orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom during the rainy season. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it often grows in shaded, humid environments.

Lepanthes rudicula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
