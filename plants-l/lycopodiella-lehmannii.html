Lycopodiella lehmannii

Lycopodiella lehmannii, commonly known as the Lehmann's clubmoss, is a small evergreen plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and its production of spore-bearing cones. The plant typically forms dense mats on rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are needle-like and arranged in whorls around the stem, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Lycopodiella lehmannii is adapted to survive in harsh conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
