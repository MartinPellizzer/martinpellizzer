Lobelia illota

Lobelia illota, commonly known as the African bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa.

It features striking blue to violet tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows to a height of 30-60 cm, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant color and compact growth habit.
