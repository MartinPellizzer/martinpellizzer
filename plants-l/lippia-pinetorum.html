Lippia pinetorum

Lippia pinetorum, commonly known as mountain mint, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, often gray-green leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale purple, arranged in dense clusters. Lippia pinetorum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and streamside habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
