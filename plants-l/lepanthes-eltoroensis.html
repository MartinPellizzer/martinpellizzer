Lepanthes eltoroensis

Lepanthes eltoroensis, commonly known as the El Toro orchid, is a species of orchid native to the mountainous regions of Costa Rica.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant features delicate, strap-like leaves and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. It is part of the Epidendroideae subfamily and is characterized by its unique floral structure.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
