Lychnis haageana var. hybrida

Lychnis haageana var. hybrida, commonly known as the hybrid fireweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with purple or red, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
