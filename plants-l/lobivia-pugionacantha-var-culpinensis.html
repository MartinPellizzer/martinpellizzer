Lobivia pugionacantha var. culpinensis

Lobivia pugionacantha var. culpinensis, commonly known as the Culpin's lobivia, is a compact, low-growing succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It features a short, thick stem with dense clusters of fleshy, cylindrical leaves that are typically green to blue-green in color. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its unique morphology and drought tolerance make it a desirable plant for collectors and gardeners in dry climates.
