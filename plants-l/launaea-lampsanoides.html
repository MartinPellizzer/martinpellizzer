Launaea lampsanoides

Launaea lampsanoides, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions. The plant features woody stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in dense inflorescences.

Launaea lampsanoides is known for its milky sap, which can be toxic and is used in traditional medicine.
