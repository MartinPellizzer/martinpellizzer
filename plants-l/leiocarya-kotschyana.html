Leiocarya kotschyana

Leiocarya kotschyana, commonly known as the Kashmir snowbell, is a perennial shrub native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers. The plant produces small, oval berries that are typically green when immature and turn red upon maturity. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions.

Leiocarya kotschyana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in temperate regions.
