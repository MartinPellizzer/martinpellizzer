Lemaireocereus thurberi

Lemaireocereus thurberi, commonly known as the Thornless Hedgehog Cactus, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming cactus with cylindrical stems that are typically green and covered in small, white spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are pollinated by various insects and birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in xeriscaping and as a decorative plant in desert gardens.
