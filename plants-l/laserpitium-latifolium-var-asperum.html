Laserpitium latifolium var. asperum

Laserpitium latifolium var. asperum, commonly known as the roughleaf hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets. The plant features basal and upright stems with lance-shaped, rough-textured leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
