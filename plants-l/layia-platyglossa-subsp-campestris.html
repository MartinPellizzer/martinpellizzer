Layia platyglossa subsp. campestris

Layia platyglossa subsp.

campestris, commonly known as the field layia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its yellow flowers bloom in clusters.

It is often found in California and parts of Nevada, thriving in full sun and well-drained soil.
