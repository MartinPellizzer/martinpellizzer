Linaria biebersteinii

Linaria biebersteinii, commonly known as the Beiberstein's toadflax, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and delicate, yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its flowers are tubular with two lips, a feature common in the genus Linaria, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Linaria biebersteinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
