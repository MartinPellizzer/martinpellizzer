Leucanthemum vulgare subsp. alpicola

Leucanthemum vulgare subsp.

alpicola, commonly known as the alpine oxeye, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist conditions. It produces white ray florets surrounded by yellow disk florets, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This subspecies is adapted to high-altitude environments, exhibiting a compact growth habit and frost resistance.
