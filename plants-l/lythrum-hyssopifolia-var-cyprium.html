Lythrum hyssopifolia var. cyprium

Lythrum hyssopifolia var. cyprium, commonly known as the Cyprus lythrum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright stems that are often covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces clusters of vibrant pink to purple flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rocky or dry environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
