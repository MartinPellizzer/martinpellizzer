Linum polygaloides

Linum polygaloides, commonly known as the narrowleaf flax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and delicate, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Linum polygaloides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
