Lundia rubicunda

Lundia rubicunda, commonly known as the red-hued orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant red petals and sepals, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. Native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments. The flowers typically bloom in late spring, producing a sweet, fragrant aroma that attracts pollinators.

Its unique morphology and vivid coloration make it a popular subject in botanical research and horticulture.
