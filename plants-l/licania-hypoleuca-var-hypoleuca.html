Licania hypoleuca var. hypoleuca

Licania hypoleuca var. hypoleuca, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry tree, is a species native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with a dense canopy and smooth, dark bark. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red, fleshy fruits that are edible for both humans and animals. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, with a glossy surface and a pointed tip.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
