Lasianthus rhinocerotis var. pedunculatus

Lasianthus rhinocerotis var. pedunculatus, commonly known as the hornbill lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, pale yellow to white flowers. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture. This species is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for small fauna.
