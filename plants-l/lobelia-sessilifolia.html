Lobelia sessilifolia

Lobelia sessilifolia, commonly known as the sessile-leaved lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant has oval, sessile leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.

Lobelia sessilifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
