Lamium album var. maculatum

Lamium album var. maculatum, commonly known as white dead-nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features opposite, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive white or pale variegation pattern. The plant produces small, pale purple to white flowers arranged in whorls along the stems. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a shade-tolerant, low-maintenance plant.
