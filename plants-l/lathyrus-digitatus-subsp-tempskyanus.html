Lathyrus digitatus subsp. tempskyanus

Lathyrus digitatus subsp.

tempskyanus, commonly known as the Caucasian vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Caucasus region. It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves with small, heart-shaped leaflets.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers in shades of pink to purple, followed by elongated, cylindrical pods containing several elliptical seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or open habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local agriculture.
