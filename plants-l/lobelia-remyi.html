Lobelia remyi

Lobelia remyi, commonly known as the Florida lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as swamps and wetlands. The plant features small, tubular blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Lobelia remyi plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by supporting local wildlife and contributing to biodiversity.
