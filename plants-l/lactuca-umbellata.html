Lactuca umbellata

Lactuca umbellata, commonly known as the common chicory, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of blue to violet leaves and a tall, branching stem that bears yellow flowers. The plant is native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia, and it thrives in a variety of soil types. It has a milky sap that is characteristic of the Lactuca genus, and it is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

The plant is also cultivated for its edible leaves and as a source of ornamental value.
