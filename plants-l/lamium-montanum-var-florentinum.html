Lamium montanum var. florentinum

Lamium montanum var. florentinum, commonly known as the Florentine deadnettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture and produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaf arrangement.

Its flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
