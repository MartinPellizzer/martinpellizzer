Lycoseris mexicana

Lycoseris mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican lycoperd, is a species of fungi that belongs to the family Lycoperdaceae.

It is characterized by its unique fruiting body, which resembles a small, dry, and often spiky structure. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid regions, particularly in Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Due to its ecological role in nutrient cycling, it plays an important part in the ecosystems where it is found.
