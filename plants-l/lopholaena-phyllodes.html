Lopholaena phyllodes

Lopholaena phyllodes, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that are often mistaken for flowers due to their delicate appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are followed by elongated, bean-like pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy regions.

Lopholaena phyllodes is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and restoration projects.
