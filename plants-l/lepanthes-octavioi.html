Lepanthes octavioi

Lepanthes octavioi, commonly known as the Octavio's orchid, is a rare and exquisite member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the cloud forests of Mexico, specifically in the state of Oaxaca, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often with intricate patterns on the petals. It typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees without deriving nutrients from them.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Lepanthes octavioi is considered a vulnerable species, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts in preserving its natural habitat.
