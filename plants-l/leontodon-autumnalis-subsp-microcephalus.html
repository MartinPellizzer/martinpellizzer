Leontodon autumnalis subsp. microcephalus

Leontodon autumnalis subsp.

microcephalus, commonly known as the small-headed autumn dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and pinnately lobed leaves.

The subspecies is distinguished by its smaller flower heads compared to other members of the species. It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows and roadsides, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant is known for its early flowering period, often blooming in late summer to early autumn.
