Lilaeopsis brisbanica

Lilaeopsis brisbanica, commonly known as the Brisbane grass, is a perennial herb native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along watercourses.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
