Lemna globosa

Lemna globosa, commonly known as the floating bladderwort, is a small, aquatic plant belonging to the family Araceae.

It is characterized by its inflated, bladder-like leaves that help it float on the water's surface. The plant is typically found in still or slow-moving freshwater environments. Its reproductive structures, called spathes, contain numerous tiny flowers that produce both male and female gametes.
