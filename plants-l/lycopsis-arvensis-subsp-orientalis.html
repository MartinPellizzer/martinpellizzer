Lycopsis arvensis subsp. orientalis

Lycopsis arvensis subsp.

orientalis, commonly known as the eastern field lycopsis, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Asia and Europe. It typically reaches heights of up to 20 centimeters and has slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a slightly rough texture.

It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open fields and along riverbanks.
