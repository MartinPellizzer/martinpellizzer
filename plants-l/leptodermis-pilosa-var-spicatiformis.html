Leptodermis pilosa var. spicatiformis

Leptodermis pilosa var. spicatiformis, commonly known as the spicatiform leathery vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and distinctive, spiky inflorescences. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine, utilizing its tendrils to support itself on other vegetation. Its flowers are small and clustered, often displaying a combination of white and green hues.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in warm climates for its unique foliage and floral display.
