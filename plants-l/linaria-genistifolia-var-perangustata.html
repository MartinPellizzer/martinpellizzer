Linaria genistifolia var. perangustata

Linaria genistifolia var. perangustata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved toadflax, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features slender, branching stems and small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, giving the plant a delicate, grass-like appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
