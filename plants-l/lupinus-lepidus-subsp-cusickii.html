Lupinus lepidus subsp. cusickii

Lupinus lepidus subsp.

cusickii, commonly known as the Cusick's lupine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and clusters of blue to violet pea-like flowers.

The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a dense coating of fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky slopes. Lupinus lepidus subsp.

cusickii is an important nectar source for pollinators and is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
