Leucopoa pulchella

Leucopoa pulchella, commonly known as the silverleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and bright yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs that reflect sunlight.

Leucopoa pulchella is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
