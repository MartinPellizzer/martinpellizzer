Leptocionium magellanicum

Leptocionium magellanicum, commonly known as the Magellanic lichen, is a foliose lichen that thrives in cool, moist environments.

It is characterized by its bright green to yellowish thallus, which is typically covered in a fine, powdery layer. The plant forms a symbiotic relationship with algae, which provide the primary source of nutrients through photosynthesis. It is often found on tree trunks and rocks in temperate rainforests.

Due to its sensitivity to environmental changes, it serves as an indicator species for ecological health.
