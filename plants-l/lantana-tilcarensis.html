Lantana tilcarensis

Lantana tilcarensis, commonly known as the Hawaiian lantana, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom throughout the year. Its flowers are borne on terminal panicles, and the plant is adapted to thrive in coastal and dry forest environments.

Lantana tilcarensis is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
