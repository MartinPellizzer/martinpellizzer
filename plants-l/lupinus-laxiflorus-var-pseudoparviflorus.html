Lupinus laxiflorus var. pseudoparviflorus

Lupinus laxiflorus var. pseudoparviflorus, commonly known as the slender-flowered lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for native pollinators.
