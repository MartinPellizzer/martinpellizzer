Leucas melissifolia

Leucas melissifolia, commonly known as honey-leaved shrub, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that emit a mild honey-like fragrance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, which bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is often cultivated for both aesthetic and therapeutic purposes.
