Loranthus umbellatus

Loranthus umbellatus, commonly known as the Indian mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that attaches itself to the branches of host trees.

It belongs to the family Loranthaceae and is native to regions of Asia, including India, China, and Southeast Asia. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are often clustered in umbels, giving it its scientific name. Its leaves are usually ovate or lanceolate and are arranged opposite along the stems.

Loranthus umbellatus is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
