Lobelia tupa var. pavonii

Lobelia tupa var. pavonii, commonly known as the purple lobelia, is a striking perennial herb native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple to violet flowers that bloom in dense spikes, typically from late spring to early summer. The plant features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in native plant landscaping.
