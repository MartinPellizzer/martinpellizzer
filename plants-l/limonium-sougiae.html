Limonium sougiae

Limonium sougiae, commonly known as the Greek sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to salty, well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of salt.

Limonium sougiae is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in gardens and floral arrangements.
