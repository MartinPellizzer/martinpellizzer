Lagenocarpus giganteus

Lagenocarpus giganteus, commonly known as the giant sedge, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like stems and broad, sword-shaped leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown, which develop into fleshy, berry-like fruits. It thrives in wetland or swampy environments, often forming dense stands that help stabilize soil.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it is sometimes considered an invasive species in non-native habitats.
