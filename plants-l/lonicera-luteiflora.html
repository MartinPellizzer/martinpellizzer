Lonicera luteiflora

Lonicera luteiflora, commonly known as the yellow honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are elliptical to ovate in shape. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that ripens in the fall.

Lonicera luteiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant flowers and adaptability to various soil types.
