Lupinus mantaroensis

Lupinus mantaroensis, commonly known as the Mantaro lupine, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky areas, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
