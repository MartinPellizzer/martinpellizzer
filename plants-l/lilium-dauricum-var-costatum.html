Lilium dauricum var. costatum

Lilium dauricum var. costatum, commonly known as the Daurian lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It features striking, showy flowers that are typically pink or red, with a distinctive white throat and prominent stamens. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, and it produces a tall, slender stem that bears the floral display. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or forested habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
