Lasiacis maculata

Lasiacis maculata, commonly known as the spotted leaf orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its striking, spotted leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its foliage is often variegated with white or yellow spots, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Lasiacis maculata prefers indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
