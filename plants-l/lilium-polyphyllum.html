Lilium polyphyllum

Lilium polyphyllum, commonly known as the Chinese death lily, is a species of lily native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its striking white or pale pink flowers with a distinctive red or purple spot at the base of each petal. The plant has broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in two rows along the stem. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Lilium polyphyllum is valued for its ornamental beauty and has cultural significance in traditional Chinese medicine.
