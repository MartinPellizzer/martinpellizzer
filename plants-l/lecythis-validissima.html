Lecythis validissima

Lecythis validissima, commonly known as the Amazonian butter tree, is a tropical tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, smooth, pale green bark and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces distinctive, yellow, fleshy fruits that contain a single large seed surrounded by a creamy, edible pulp. The fruit is highly valued for its rich, buttery texture and is used in traditional medicine and cuisine.

It plays an important ecological role in the rainforest by providing food for various animals and supporting biodiversity.
