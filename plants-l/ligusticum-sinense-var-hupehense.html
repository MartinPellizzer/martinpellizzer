Ligusticum sinense var. hupehense

Ligusticum sinense var. hupehense, commonly known as the Hupeh lovage, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescences. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with slender, hollow stems and finely divided, fern-like leaves. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, arranged in compound umbels, and it produces small, dry fruits.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional Chinese medicine.
