Lexipyretum punctatum

Lexipyretum punctatum, commonly known as the fireweed or redstem filaree, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its distinctive red stems and small, clustered white or pink flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in open fields.

Lexipyretum punctatum is a member of the Polygonaceae family, which includes other plants such as buckwheat and sorrel.
