Lycopodium selago var. varium

Lycopodium selago var. varium, commonly known as the northern clubmoss, is a small, evergreen fern-like plant that belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. The plant features slender, branching stems covered in tiny, scale-like leaves. It produces spore-bearing cones at the tips of its stems, which are essential for its reproduction.

This variety is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in northern regions of North America.
