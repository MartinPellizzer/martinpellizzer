Lyroglossa euglossa

Lyroglossa euglossa, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns and a faintly fragrant scent. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, such as rainforests, where it thrives in the understory. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the rich, organic soil of its habitat.

The orchid's unique floral structure and pollination mechanism make it an important subject in botanical studies.
