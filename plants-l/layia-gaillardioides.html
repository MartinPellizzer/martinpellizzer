Layia gaillardioides

Layia gaillardioides, commonly known as the Gaillardioides daisy, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall.

Layia gaillardioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and drought tolerance.
