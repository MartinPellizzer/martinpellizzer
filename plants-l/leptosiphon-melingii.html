Leptosiphon melingii

Leptosiphon melingii, commonly known as the desert morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually pink to purple in color, blooming in the early morning. Its slender stems and small, alternate leaves are characteristic of the genus Leptosiphon.
