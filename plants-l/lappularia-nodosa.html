Lappularia nodosa

Lappularia nodosa, commonly known as the nodular lichen, is a foliose lichen that thrives in temperate and subarctic regions.

It is characterized by its leaf-like thallus, which is usually pale green to yellowish in color. The plant forms a symbiotic relationship with algae and fungi, with the fungal component providing structure and the algal partner carrying out photosynthesis. It often grows on bark, soil, and rocks, and is typically found in shaded, moist environments.

Lappularia nodosa is an important indicator species for environmental conditions and biodiversity in its habitat.
