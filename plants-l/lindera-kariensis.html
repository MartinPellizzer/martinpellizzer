Lindera kariensis

Lindera kariensis, commonly known as Japanese spicebush, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes other aromatic plants like cinnamon and avocado. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth texture and a strong, spicy fragrance. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries.

Lindera kariensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.
