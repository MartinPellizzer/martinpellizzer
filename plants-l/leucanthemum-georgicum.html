Leucanthemum georgicum

Leucanthemum georgicum, commonly known as the Georgia coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, daisy-like flowers with white petals surrounding a prominent yellow disc floret. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and has lance-shaped, hairy leaves. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Leucanthemum georgicum is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
