Laelia × euspatha

Laelia × euspatha, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a cross between Laelia and a species of Cattleya, resulting in a plant with unique botanical characteristics.

It typically features vibrant, showy flowers with a complex structure, including a spurred lip and a column, typical of orchids. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on other plants without being parasitic, and thrives in warm, humid environments. Its foliage consists of broad, leathery leaves that are adapted for water retention.

Laelia × euspatha is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
