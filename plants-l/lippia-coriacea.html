Lippia coriacea

Lippia coriacea, commonly known as rough hibiscus, is a perennial shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils. It is commonly found in wetlands, stream banks, and forest edges.

Lippia coriacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
