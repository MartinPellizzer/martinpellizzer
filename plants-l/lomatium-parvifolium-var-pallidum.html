Lomatium parvifolium var. pallidum

Lomatium parvifolium var. pallidum, commonly known as the pallid lomatium, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces yellow flowers in early spring. The plant thrives in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional herbal medicine.

It is an important component of the local flora and supports various pollinators.
