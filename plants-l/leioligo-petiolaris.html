Leioligo petiolaris

Leioligo petiolaris, commonly known as the slender leioligo, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that typically bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss, Leioligo petiolaris is considered endangered and is often found in protected botanical gardens.
