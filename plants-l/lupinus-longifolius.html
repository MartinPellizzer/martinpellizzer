Lupinus longifolius

Lupinus longifolius, commonly known as the tall lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces spiny, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds, which are often used as forage for livestock. Lupinus longifolius is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

Its showy blue or purple flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
