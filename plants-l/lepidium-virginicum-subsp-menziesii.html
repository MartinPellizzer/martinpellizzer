Lepidium virginicum subsp. menziesii

Lepidium virginicum subsp.

menziesii, commonly known as the slender peppergrass, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family. It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters.

The plant has slender, branched stems and small, pale green leaves that are opposite or alternate in arrangement. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and arranged in racemes, typical of the Brassicaceae family.

The fruit is a dry, two-lobed silique that contains several seeds.
