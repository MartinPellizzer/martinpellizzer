Leymus arjinshanicus

Leymus arjinshanicus, commonly known as the Caucasian needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, needle-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers.

Leymus arjinshanicus plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest in ecological and agricultural studies.
