Lupinus palmeri var. humilis

Lupinus palmeri var. humilis, commonly known as the Palmer's lupine, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptation to harsh conditions. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

Lupinus palmeri var. humilis is also notable for its role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
