Lepidolopha komarowii

Lepidolopha komarowii, commonly known as the Komarow's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its unique appearance.

Lepidolopha komarowii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in specialty gardens.
