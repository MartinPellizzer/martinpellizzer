Libidibia monosperma

Libidibia monosperma, commonly known as the African mahogany, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and compound leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by pod-like fruits containing a single seed. It is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in furniture and construction.

Additionally, it plays an important role in local ecosystems and traditional medicine.
