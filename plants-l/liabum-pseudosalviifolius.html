Liabum pseudosalviifolius

Liabum pseudosalviifolius, commonly known as false sage.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves that are typically lance-shaped. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually purple or violet in color, arranged in whorled clusters. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

Liabum pseudosalviifolius is often found in dry, open habitats such as woodlands and fields.
