Lippia modesta

Lippia modesta, commonly known as Mexican tea, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring narrow, aromatic leaves with a slightly fuzzy texture. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually white or pale purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches. Lippia modesta is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for both gardeners and herbalists.
