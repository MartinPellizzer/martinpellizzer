Lespedeza × oblongifolia

Lespedeza × oblongifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf lespedeza, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Lespedeza virginica and Lespedeza × tenuifolia, characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink to reddish in color, arranged in dense spikes. It is native to the eastern United States and thrives in well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and along roadsides.

Lespedeza × oblongifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
