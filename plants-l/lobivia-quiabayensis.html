Lobivia quiabayensis

Lobivia quiabayensis, commonly known as the Quiabayensis Lobivia, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of northern Chile.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and a dense covering of spines that provide protection against herbivores and intense sunlight. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and early summer, adding a striking contrast to its green body. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it stores water in its thick, fleshy stems, allowing it to survive in harsh desert conditions.

Lobivia quiabayensis is a slow-growing plant that requires minimal water and is well-suited for rock gardens or arid landscapes.
