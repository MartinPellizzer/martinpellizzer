Leptonychia melanocarpa var. grandifolia

Leptonychia melanocarpa var. grandifolia, commonly known as the large-flowered rock cress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which is characterized by its cross-shaped flowers and siliqua fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are basal, pinnately divided, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

The showy yellow flowers appear in early summer and are followed by elongated, segmented pods that contain small, rounded seeds.
