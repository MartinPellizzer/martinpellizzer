Lysimachia zavadskii

Lysimachia zavadskii, commonly known as the Zavadsky's yellow loosestrife, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, sunny environments such as meadows and stream banks. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs.

Lysimachia zavadskii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
