Lycopodioides ovifolia

Lycopodioides ovifolia, commonly known as the ovate clubmoss, is a species of lycophyte that belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and produces spores in specialized structures called sporangia. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures are arranged in cone-like clusters, which are borne on specialized branches called strobila.

Lycopodioides ovifolia plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
