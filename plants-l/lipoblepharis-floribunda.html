Lipoblepharis floribunda

Lipoblepharis floribunda, commonly known as the fragrant bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and has narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its sweet, spicy fragrance, which attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.
