Lepechinia subhastata

Lepechinia subhastata, commonly known as the narrowleaf lepechinia, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It belongs to the heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of delicate, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Lepechinia subhastata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
