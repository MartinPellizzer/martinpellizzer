Leucadendron salignum

Leucadendron salignum, commonly known as the silver tree, is a striking shrub or small tree native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces cone-like structures known as infructescences, which are often brightly colored and attract pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Leucadendron salignum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique visual characteristics.
