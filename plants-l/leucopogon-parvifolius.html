Leucopogon parvifolius

Leucopogon parvifolius, commonly known as the small-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heaths and heathers, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and compact growth habit. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils in woodland or coastal areas. Its small, white or pink flowers appear in clusters during the spring and summer months.

It is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
