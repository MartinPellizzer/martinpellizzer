Lupinus lacus-payetti

Lupinus lacus-payetti, commonly known as the Lake Payette lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume pods and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its flowers are usually blue or purple, with a distinctive spurred petal that aids in pollination by specific insects.

Lupinus lacus-payetti plays an important ecological role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining soil fertility.
