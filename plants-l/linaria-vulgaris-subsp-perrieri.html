Linaria vulgaris subsp. perrieri

Linaria vulgaris subsp.

perrieri, commonly known as the yellow toadflax, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has slender, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in rocky or disturbed habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain areas.
