Lotus roseus

Lotus roseus, commonly known as the pink lotus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Nelumbonaceae family and is characterized by its striking pink or white petals and a distinctive, showy flower structure. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in ponds and wetlands. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and float on the water's surface, while its roots are submerged.

Lotus roseus is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its cultural and medicinal significance in various regions.
