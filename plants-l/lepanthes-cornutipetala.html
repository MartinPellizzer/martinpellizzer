Lepanthes cornutipetala

Lepanthes cornutipetala, commonly known as the horned-petalled orchid, is a species native to Central America.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure. The plant features showy, brightly colored flowers with elongated, horn-like petals. It typically grows in humid, shaded forest environments.

Lepanthes cornutipetala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
