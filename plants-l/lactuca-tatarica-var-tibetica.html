Lactuca tatarica var. tibetica

Lactuca tatarica var. tibetica, commonly known as Tibetan lettuce, is a perennial plant native to the high altitudes of the Tibetan Plateau.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette growth pattern and hairy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in cool, temperate climates and is adapted to high-altitude environments with limited soil nutrients.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
