Limonium xerocamposicum

Limonium xerocamposicum, commonly known as the desert sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking blue to purple flower heads that resemble small holly berries. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of tightly packed, tubular flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and coastal environments.

Limonium xerocamposicum is often used in ornamental gardening for its drought tolerance and visual appeal.
