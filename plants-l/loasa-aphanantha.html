Loasa aphanantha

Loasa aphanantha, commonly known as the small-leaved loasa, is a shrub native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine, hairy structures. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Its growth habit is compact, with multiple stems forming a dense, bushy appearance.

Loasa aphanantha is often found in moist, shaded environments within its native range.
