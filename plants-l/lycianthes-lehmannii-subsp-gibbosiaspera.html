Lycianthes lehmannii subsp. gibbosiaspera

Lycianthes lehmannii subsp.

gibbosiaspera, commonly known as the rough-leaved physalis, is a species native to Central and South America. It is a herbaceous annual plant that typically grows in dry, open habitats.

The plant produces distinctive yellow flowers followed by inflated, spiny calyces that enclose its orange-red berries. Its leaves are characterized by their rough, waxy texture and serrated margins.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique fruiting structures.
