Leptandra meyeri

Leptandra meyeri, commonly known as Meyer's leptandra, is a small evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. The plant features opposite, elliptical leaves that are dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter underside. Its small, fragrant white flowers bloom in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits.

It is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
