Lithospermum tenellum

Lithospermum tenellum, commonly known as narrowleaf rock pepper, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Lithospermum tenellum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential medicinal properties.
