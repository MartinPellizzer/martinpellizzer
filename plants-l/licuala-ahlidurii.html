Licuala ahlidurii

Licuala ahlidurii, commonly known as the Philippine palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the Philippines and is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach up to three meters in diameter. The plant typically grows in humid, tropical environments and thrives in well-drained soil. It produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into woody fruits containing several seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
