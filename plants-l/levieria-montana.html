Levieria montana

Levieria montana, commonly known as the mountain levieria, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests and alpine meadows. Its leaves are typically narrow and elongated, with a slightly hairy texture that helps reduce water loss.

Levieria montana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
