Lychnis apetala subsp. montana

Lychnis apetala subsp.

montana, commonly known as the mountain touch-me-not, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe. It features upright, branched stems that are covered in fine, soft hairs.

The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
