Lasianthus elevatineurus

Lasianthus elevatineurus, commonly known as the mountain lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically thrives in shaded, moist environments within tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs, while the flowers are usually pink or purple in color.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
