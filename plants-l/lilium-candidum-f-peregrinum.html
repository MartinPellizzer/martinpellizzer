Lilium candidum f. peregrinum

Lilium candidum f. peregrinum, commonly known as the white lily, is a botanical variant of the Easter lily.

It is characterized by its large, white, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It is a perennial herb with long, strap-like leaves and a robust root system.

This cultivar is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in floral arrangements.
