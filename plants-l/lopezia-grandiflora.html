Lopezia grandiflora

Lopezia grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered Lopezia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as deserts and rocky soils. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are often pink or red in color.

Lopezia grandiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
