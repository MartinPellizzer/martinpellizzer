Liparis indifferens

Liparis indifferens, commonly known as the indifferent orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in a raceme, often blooming in late spring or early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its delicate appearance and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
