Leucas argentea var. argentea

Leucas argentea var. argentea, commonly known as silver leucas, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months.

This species is often used in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and medicinal properties.
