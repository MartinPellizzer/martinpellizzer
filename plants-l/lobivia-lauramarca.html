Lobivia lauramarca

Lobivia lauramarca, commonly known as the "Lauramarca Lobivia," is a member of the Cactaceae family native to the arid regions of Argentina.

It is a small, compact cactus with a short, cylindrical stem that often branches at the base. The plant produces delicate, white to pale pink flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are modified into spines, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

Lobivia lauramarca is prized for its unique appearance and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a decorative indoor plant.
