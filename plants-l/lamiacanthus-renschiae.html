Lamiacanthus renschiae

Lamiacanthus renschiae, commonly known as the Rensch's lamiaceae, is a flowering plant belonging to the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. Its leaves are often aromatic, and the flowers may range in color from white to pale purple.

While not widely cultivated, it is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological niche.
