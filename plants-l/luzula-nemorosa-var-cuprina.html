Luzula nemorosa var. cuprina

Luzula nemorosa var. cuprina, commonly known as the cuprona or cuprin, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems with whorled leaves that are often a striking blue-green color. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, three-lobed flowers.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partially shaded conditions.
