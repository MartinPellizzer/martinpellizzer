Landolphia macrantha

Landolphia macrantha, commonly known as the giant African lily, is a striking herbaceous plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can grow up to 15 cm in diameter, typically in shades of yellow and orange. The plant has long, strap-like leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces berries that contain toxic seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions for its dramatic floral display.
