Lepanthes calocodon

Lepanthes calocodon, commonly known as the golden star orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in humid, shaded forest environments. The plant features a short, upright stem with a single, showy flower that is often yellow or orange in color. Its leaves are ovate and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its compact appearance.

Lepanthes calocodon is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
