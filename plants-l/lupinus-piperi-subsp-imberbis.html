Lupinus piperi subsp. imberbis

Lupinus piperi subsp.

imberbis, commonly known as the narrowleaf lupine, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers.

The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, linear leaflets, and its root system consists of nitrogen-fixing nodules. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. Lupinus piperi subsp.

imberbis is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its potential use in restoration projects.
