Lilium thunbergianum var. nigropunctatum

Lilium thunbergianum var. nigropunctatum, commonly known as the Black-spotted Lily, is a rare and ornamental plant native to China.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its striking, large, and showy flowers. The plant features dark purple to black spots on its white or pale pink petals, which give it its distinctive appearance. It typically grows in woodland areas and thrives in partial shade with well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its unique floral patterns and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
