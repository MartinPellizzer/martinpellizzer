Lathyrus ledebourii

Lathyrus ledebourii, commonly known as the desert pea, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking violet or purple pea-shaped flowers and slender, often hairy stems. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and is adapted to survive in dry, sandy soils. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Lathyrus ledebourii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
