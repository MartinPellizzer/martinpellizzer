Limonium sinense var. spinulosum

Limonium sinense var. spinulosum, commonly known as the spiked sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of China.

It is characterized by its blue to violet flowers and spiny, hairy stems. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in maritime environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
