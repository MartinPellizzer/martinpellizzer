Loasella rupestris

Loasella rupestris, commonly known as the rock loasaceae, is a succulent plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is characterized by its fleshy, often hairy leaves that help it store water in its arid habitat. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a delicate touch to its rugged appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is often cultivated by succulent enthusiasts.
