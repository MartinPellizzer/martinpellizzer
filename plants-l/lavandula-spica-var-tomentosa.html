Lavandula spica var. tomentosa

Lavandula spica var. tomentosa, commonly known as woolly lavender, is a variety of lavender native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and spikes of purple flowers. The plant is a perennial shrub that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. Its aromatic leaves and flowers are used in aromatherapy, perfumery, and as a natural remedy.

The tomentosa variety is particularly noted for its soft, fuzzy texture and compact growth habit.
