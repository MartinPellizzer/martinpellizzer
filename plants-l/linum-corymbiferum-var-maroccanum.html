Linum corymbiferum var. maroccanum

Linum corymbiferum var. maroccanum, commonly known as the Moroccan flax, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in dense, branched clusters. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is well adapted to hot, dry climates. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water once established.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in traditional medicine.
