Lepanthes voodoo

Lepanthes voodoo, commonly known as the Voodoo orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually small, with intricate patterns and vivid colors, making it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.

Lepanthes voodoo thrives in warm, moist conditions with high humidity and indirect light.
