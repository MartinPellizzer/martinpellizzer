Lonicera caprifolium pauciflora

Lonicera caprifolium pauciflora, commonly known as the slender honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and moist soils, thriving in partial shade. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and have a smooth texture.

The fruit is a small, red berry that develops after flowering and is often consumed by birds.
