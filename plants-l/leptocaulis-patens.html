Leptocaulis patens

Leptocaulis patens, commonly known as the spreading leathery fern, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features a creeping rhizome that produces erect, lance-shaped leaves with a leathery texture. The leaves are typically dark green and have a glossy appearance, with finely divided, serrated margins. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to form dense ground cover.
