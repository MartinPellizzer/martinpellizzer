Lychnis ischnopetala

Lychnis ischnopetala, commonly known as the narrow-leaved catchfly, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, pink or red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Lychnis ischnopetala is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
