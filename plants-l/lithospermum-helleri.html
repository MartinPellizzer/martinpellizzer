Lithospermum helleri

Lithospermum helleri, commonly known as Heller's rock pepper, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from red to purple. The plant typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and it is known for its ability to tolerate drought conditions.

Lithospermum helleri is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
