Lysimachia peduncularis

Lysimachia peduncularis, commonly known as yellow-eyed grass, is a herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a reddish or purple tint along the veins.

Lysimachia peduncularis is a member of the Primulaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
