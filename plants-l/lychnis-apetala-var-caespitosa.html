Lychnis apetala var. caespitosa

Lychnis apetala var. caespitosa, commonly known as the tufted sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to sandy and rocky soils in Europe.

It features small, pink to white flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. The plant has a compact, mat-forming growth habit, with hairy, succulent stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are actinomorphic, with five petals and numerous stamens, typical of the Caryophyllaceae family.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or dry habitats.
