Liabum acaule

Liabum acaule, commonly known as the stemless liabum, is a small, herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are small and yellow, often appearing in clusters, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Liabum acaule is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
