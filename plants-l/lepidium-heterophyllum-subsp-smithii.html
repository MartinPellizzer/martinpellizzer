Lepidium heterophyllum subsp. smithii

Lepidium heterophyllum subsp.

smithii, commonly known as the Smith's celtuce, is a member of the Brassicaceae family and is native to western North America. It is a biennial or perennial herb characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and flowering stems that bear small, yellow flowers.

The plant has a fibrous root system and produces small, spherical fruits called siliques. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a mild, peppery flavor, making it a cultivated vegetable in some regions.

It thrives in cool climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.
