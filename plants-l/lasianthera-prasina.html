Lasianthera prasina

Lasianthera prasina, commonly known as the slender leaf sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, succulent leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the cooler months. It has a fibrous root system that allows it to anchor firmly in sandy or rocky soils.

Lasianthera prasina is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
