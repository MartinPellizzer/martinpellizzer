Lopezia racemosa subsp. moelchenensis

Lopezia racemosa subsp.

moelchenensis, commonly known as the Mojave desert honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico. It is a shrub characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, opposite leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting various pollinators. It is adapted to arid environments, with deep roots that help it survive in dry conditions.

Its botanical features reflect its evolutionary response to the harsh, desert climate of its native habitat.
