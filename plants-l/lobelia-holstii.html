Lobelia holstii

Lobelia holstii, commonly known as the Holst's lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of blue and purple. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

Lobelia holstii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
