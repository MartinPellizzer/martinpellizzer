Larryleachia picta

Larryleachia picta, commonly known as the painted lily, is a striking plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful flowers that range from red to orange and yellow, often with intricate patterns resembling paint splashes. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, thriving in forest understories. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy surface.

Larryleachia picta is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
