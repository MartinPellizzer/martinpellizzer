Laserpitium latifolium subsp. merinoi

Laserpitium latifolium subsp.

merinoi, commonly known as Merino's broadleaf larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers.

The leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture, typical of the Larkspur genus. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

The plant is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
