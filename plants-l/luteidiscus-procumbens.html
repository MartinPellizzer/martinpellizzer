Luteidiscus procumbens

Luteidiscus procumbens, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to parts of Africa.

It features a sprawling habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with yellow. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring. Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and ecological role in its native habitat.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to arid conditions.
