Lupinus bassett-maguirei

Lupinus bassett-maguirei, commonly known as the Maguire lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of blue or purple pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces elongated pods that contain several seeds.

Lupinus bassett-maguirei is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in dry, rocky habitats.
