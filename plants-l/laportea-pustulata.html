Laportea pustulata

Laportea pustulata, commonly known as the roughleaf wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive rough texture and a waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellow, spurred flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and native plant gardens.
