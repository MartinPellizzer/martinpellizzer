Leptorkis cumingii

Leptorkis cumingii, commonly known as the Cuming's lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped bulbs that store nutrients and help it survive in its natural habitat. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, contributing to its overall delicate appearance.

Leptorkis cumingii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its floral display.
