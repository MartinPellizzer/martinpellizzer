Lepidium montanum var. diffusum

Lepidium montanum var. diffusum, commonly known as mountain peppergrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its small, white or yellowish flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically thrives in alpine and subalpine environments, where it forms dense mats on rocky or gravelly soils. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often covered with fine pubescence.

The species is adapted to cold climates and is often found in mountainous regions of western North America.
