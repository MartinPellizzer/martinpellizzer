Ledum hypoleucum

Ledum hypoleucum, commonly known as white labrador tea, is a perennial herb native to the boreal forests of North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves that emit a strong, resinous scent. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its roots and leaves contain various bioactive compounds, including flavonoids and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

However, due to its toxic compounds, it is not recommended for human consumption.
