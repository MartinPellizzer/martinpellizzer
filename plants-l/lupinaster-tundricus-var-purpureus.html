Lupinaster tundricus var. purpureus

Lupinaster tundricus var. purpureus, commonly known as the purple tundra lupin, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its legume-like pods and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features erect, hairy stems and small, clustered purple flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with hairy surfaces adapted to cold climates.

This variety is notable for its resilience in harsh environments and its role in stabilizing soil in tundra ecosystems.
