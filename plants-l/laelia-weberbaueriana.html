Laelia weberbaueriana

Laelia weberbaueriana, commonly known as the Weberbauer's orchid, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of South America, particularly in Ecuador and Peru.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a pseudobulb that supports its growth in cool, moist environments. The plant produces tall, slender stems with multiple flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. Its leaves are strap-like and arranged in a rosette pattern around the pseudobulb.

Laelia weberbaueriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
