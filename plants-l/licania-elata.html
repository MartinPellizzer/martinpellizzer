Licania elata

Licania elata, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its straight, dark reddish-brown heartwood, which is highly valued for its durability and fine grain. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth texture. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by small, round fruits containing a single seed.

Licania elata is often found in lowland rainforests and is known for its ecological importance in supporting various wildlife species.
