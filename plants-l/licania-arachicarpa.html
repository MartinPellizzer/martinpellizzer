Licania arachicarpa

Licania arachicarpa, commonly known as the Brazil nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into woody pods containing the edible Brazil nuts. The tree can grow up to 50 meters tall and has a dense canopy that provides habitat for various wildlife species. Its fruit is a critical resource for both local ecosystems and human consumption, with the seeds being harvested for their nutritional value.

The plant plays a vital role in maintaining the biodiversity and ecological balance of its native environment.
