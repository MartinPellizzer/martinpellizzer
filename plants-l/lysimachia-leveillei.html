Lysimachia leveillei

Lysimachia leveillei, commonly known as the creeping jenny, is a low-growing perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has rounded, glossy leaves that are typically less than 2 inches in length. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for wetland gardens.

Lysimachia leveillei is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
