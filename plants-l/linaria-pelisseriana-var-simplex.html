Linaria pelisseriana var. simplex

Linaria pelisseriana var. simplex, commonly known as the simple toadflax, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, branching stems that produce clusters of bright yellow, tubular flowers with two lips. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
