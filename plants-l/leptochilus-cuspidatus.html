Leptochilus cuspidatus

Leptochilus cuspidatus, commonly known as the pointed-leaved thistle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. Native to the western United States, this plant thrives in dry, rocky habitats. Its leaves are typically narrow and pointed, while its stems are erect and often covered in fine hairs.

The plant plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
