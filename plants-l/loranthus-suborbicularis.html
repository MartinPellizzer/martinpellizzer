Loranthus suborbicularis

Loranthus suborbicularis, commonly known as the suborbicular mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to its host tree through a specialized structure called a haustellum.

It primarily grows on the branches of eucalyptus trees, where it derives some of its nutrients from the host. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are elliptical to suborbicular in shape, hence the species name, and are often covered in a fine layer of hair.

Loranthus suborbicularis plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for birds and insects, despite its parasitic nature.
