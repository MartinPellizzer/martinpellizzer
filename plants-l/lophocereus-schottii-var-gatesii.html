Lophocereus schottii var. gatesii

Lophocereus schottii var. gatesii, commonly known as the Gates' night blooming cactus, is a succulent cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features a columnar stem with prominent areoles, from which spines and flowers emerge. The plant produces vibrant white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as bats and moths. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Its striking appearance and unique blooming habit make it a popular choice for desert gardens.
