Lyonia acuminata

Lyonia acuminata, commonly known as the trailing arbutus, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring, often in clusters along the stems. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and have a subtle evergreen scent.

Lyonia acuminata thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded, forested areas.
