Lagochilus macrodontus

Lagochilus macrodontus, commonly known as the large-toothed rock hyacinth, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of violet or blue flowers. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, adapted to the dry, rocky environments it inhabits.

Lagochilus macrodontus is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
