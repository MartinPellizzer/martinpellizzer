Lasiocorys hephaestis

Lasiocorys hephaestis, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery red flowers that appear in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has compound leaves with long, slender leaflets and produces woody pods that contain flat, winged seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The botanical features of Lasiocorys hephaestis make it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
