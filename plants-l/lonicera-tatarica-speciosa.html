Lonicera tatarica speciosa

Lonicera tatarica speciosa, commonly known as the showy honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to yellow and red, often appearing in clusters. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds but not typically consumed by humans. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Lonicera tatarica speciosa is also known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and runners.
