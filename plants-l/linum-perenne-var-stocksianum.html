Linum perenne var. stocksianum

Linum perenne var. stocksianum, commonly known as the Persian Flax, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of blue to purple flowers with five petals. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine layer of silky hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in textile production.
