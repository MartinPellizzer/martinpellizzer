Laburnum vulgare var. quercifolium

Laburnum vulgare var. quercifolium, commonly known as the oak-leaved laburnum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive, drooping clusters of bright yellow flowers that appear in early spring. The leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and resemble those of an oak tree, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in woodland edges and gardens.

This species is valued for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
