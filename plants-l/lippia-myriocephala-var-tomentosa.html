Lippia myriocephala var. tomentosa

Lippia myriocephala var. tomentosa, commonly known as silverleaf lippia, is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, silvery-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale purple, arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

This species is often used in traditional medicine and as a ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and aromatic properties.
