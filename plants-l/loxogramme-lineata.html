Loxogramme lineata

Loxogramme lineata, commonly known as the linear water clover, is a small aquatic fern native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and streams. The plant has finely divided, linear leaves that form a dense, floating mat on the water surface. Its rhizomes are submerged, anchoring the plant and facilitating nutrient absorption.

Loxogramme lineata plays a role in water filtration and provides habitat for small aquatic organisms.
