Lantana lundiana f. albida

Lantana lundiana f. albida, commonly known as the white-flowered lantana, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are typically lance-shaped. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its showy blooms attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
