Leiothrix fluminensis var. puberula

Leiothrix fluminensis var. puberula, commonly known as the Brazilian thornapple, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, pubescent leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and riverbanks. It produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible but have a mild, slightly tart flavor.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
