Lupinus polyphyllus f. floribundus

Lupinus polyphyllus f. floribundus, commonly known as the Pacific lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western coast of North America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of vibrant blue to purple pea-shaped flowers. The plant has compound leaves composed of multiple small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in coastal regions and open woodlands.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
