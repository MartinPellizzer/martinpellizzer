Litsea discocalyx

Litsea discocalyx, commonly known as the broadleaf litsea, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and avocados. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate, and usually elliptical leaves that are often fragrant. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by spherical, green berries that mature to a dark purple color.

Litsea discocalyx is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
