Lasiacis grisebachii

Lasiacis grisebachii, commonly known as the gray leaved lichen, is a species of lichen that belongs to the family Parmeliaceae.

It is characterized by its grayish to pale green thallus, which is typically covered with a thin, powdery layer of epicarp. This lichen is often found growing on tree bark, rocks, or soil in temperate and boreal forest environments. It is a relatively slow-growing organism that thrives in humid, shaded conditions.

Due to its ecological importance in nutrient cycling and as an indicator of air quality, it is often studied in ecological and environmental research.
