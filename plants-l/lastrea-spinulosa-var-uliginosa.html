Lastrea spinulosa var. uliginosa

Lastrea spinulosa var. uliginosa, commonly known as the bog lastrea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in marshes and fens. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive hairy texture.

The species is notable for its ability to tolerate waterlogged conditions, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
