Ligustrina japonica

Ligustrina japonica, commonly known as Japanese privet, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan and parts of East Asia.

It features small, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement and have a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by dark blue berries in the fall. Ligustrina japonica is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.

It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it adaptable to a variety of garden settings.
