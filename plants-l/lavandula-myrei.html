Lavandula myrei

Lavandula myrei, commonly known as the Canary Islands lavender, is a species native to the volcanic soils of the Canary Islands.

It is a small shrub characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and spikes of pale purple to violet flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, sunny conditions and is drought-tolerant once established. Its essential oils, rich in linalool and lavandine, are valued for their fragrance and medicinal properties.

Lavandula myrei plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting local pollinators and contributing to the region's biodiversity.
