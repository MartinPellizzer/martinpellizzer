Lupinus caespitosus var. utahensis

Lupinus caespitosus var. utahensis, commonly known as Utah prickly lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny growth habit and is typically found in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets.

This variety is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to fix nitrogen.
