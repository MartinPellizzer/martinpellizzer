Lantana wightiana

Lantana wightiana, commonly known as the silver lantana, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
