Lonicera alpigena var. nana

Lonicera alpigena var. nana, commonly known as the dwarf honeysuckle, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, arching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its compact size and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.
