Lonchocarpus vittatus

Lonchocarpus vittatus, commonly known as the yellow cowpea, is a tropical legume native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It has a deep root system that helps in nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

However, it can be invasive in some regions, posing a challenge to local ecosystems.
