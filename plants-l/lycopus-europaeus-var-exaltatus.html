Lycopus europaeus var. exaltatus

Lycopus europaeus var. exaltatus, commonly known as European bugleweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, stream banks, and woodland edges. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinct reddish or purple hue. Its flowers are small, blue to purple, and clustered in dense spikes, blooming from June to August.

It is valued in traditional herbal medicine for its potential calming and sedative properties.
