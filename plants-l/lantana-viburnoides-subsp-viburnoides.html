Lantana viburnoides subsp. viburnoides

Lantana viburnoides subsp.

viburnoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf lantana, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States. It features opposite, narrow, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath.

The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white to yellowish flowers in the summer, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Verbenaceae family.
