Lagochilum repandum

Lagochilum repandum, commonly known as the New Zealand rock daisy, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in dense, spherical heads. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a valued species in New Zealand's native flora.
