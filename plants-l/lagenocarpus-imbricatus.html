Lagenocarpus imbricatus

Lagenocarpus imbricatus, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds covered in a fibrous husk. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its showy flowers and adaptability to various soil types.

However, it can become invasive in some regions if not managed properly.
