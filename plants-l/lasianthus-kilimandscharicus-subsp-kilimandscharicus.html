Lasianthus kilimandscharicus subsp. kilimandscharicus

Lasianthus kilimandscharicus subsp.

kilimandscharicus, commonly known as the Kilimanjaro flame tree, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of East Africa. It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often giving the tree a fiery appearance.

The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and elliptical, with a smooth surface and a glossy sheen.

This species is well adapted to the cool, moist climates of its native habitat.
