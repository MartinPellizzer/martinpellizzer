Lonchocarpus comosus

Lonchocarpus comosus, commonly known as the hairy senna, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or pea family, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

It is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil, making it an important plant in ecological restoration projects.
