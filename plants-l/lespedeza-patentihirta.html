Lespedeza patentihirta

Lespedeza patentihirta, commonly known as narrow-leaved lespedeza, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to fix nitrogen and prevent soil erosion.
