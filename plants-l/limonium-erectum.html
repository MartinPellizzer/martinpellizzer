Limonium erectum

Limonium erectum, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking blue to purple flower heads, which are composed of numerous small, petal-like bracts. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and saline soils, showing a high tolerance for salt and drought. Its flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Limonium erectum is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is also valued in traditional herbal medicine.
