Lupinus fissicalyx

Lupinus fissicalyx, commonly known as the narrow-leaf lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces tall spikes of blue to purple flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Lupinus fissicalyx has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion and fixes nitrogen, improving soil fertility.

It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ecological and ornamental significance.
