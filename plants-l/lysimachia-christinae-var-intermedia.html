Lysimachia christinae var. intermedia

Lysimachia christinae var. intermedia, commonly known as golden thread, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes, allowing it to spread across moist, shaded areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It is often used in garden landscapes for its vibrant color and ability to thrive in woodland environments.
