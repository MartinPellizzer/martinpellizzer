Lampranthus dilutus

Lampranthus dilutus, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a member of the Aizoaceae family and is native to South Africa.

It is a succulent shrub that produces striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has fleshy, cylindrical stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss.

Lampranthus dilutus is popular in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and vibrant floral display.
