Linaria virgata

Linaria virgata, commonly known as the slender toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs.

Linaria virgata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
