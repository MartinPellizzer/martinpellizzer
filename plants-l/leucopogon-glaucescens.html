Leucopogon glaucescens

Leucopogon glaucescens, commonly known as the bluebells, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in heathlands and open woodlands. It has slender, evergreen leaves and produces small, bell-shaped flowers that attract pollinators like bees and birds.

Leucopogon glaucescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native ecosystems.
