Linanthus androsaceus subsp. micranthus

Linanthus androsaceus subsp.

micranthus, commonly known as the Sierra starflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a low-growing perennial herb with small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring.

The plant has a basal rosette of leaves and produces numerous slender stems that bear clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to the harsh conditions of its native environment.
