Longetia carunculata

Longetia carunculata, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown heartwood and is often found in tropical rainforests. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Due to its valuable wood, it is also considered an endangered species in many regions.
