Lepanthes amplior

Lepanthes amplior, commonly known as the broad-leaved orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that often display vibrant colors such as white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in cloud forests or montane regions. Its leaves are usually broad and strap-like, contributing to its common name.

Lepanthes amplior is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
