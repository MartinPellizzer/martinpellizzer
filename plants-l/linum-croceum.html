Linum croceum

Linum croceum, commonly known as yellow flax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or borders.

Linum croceum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in dye production.
