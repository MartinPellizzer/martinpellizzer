Laggera angustifolia

Laggera angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as fields and roadsides. Its small, blue to purple flowers bloom in the summer months, attracting various pollinators.

Laggera angustifolia is known for its aromatic foliage and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
