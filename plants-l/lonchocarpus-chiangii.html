Lonchocarpus chiangii

Lonchocarpus chiangii, commonly known as the yellow senna, is a tropical legume native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers, which bloom in clusters. The plant produces distinctive pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for horticultural and research purposes.
