Lamium amplexicaule var. clandestinum

Lamium amplexicaule var. clandestinum, commonly known as the hidden-leaved deadnettle, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe.

It features heart-shaped, opposite leaves that clasp the stems, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, two-lipped flowers in shades of pink or purple, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and along stream banks.

Its foliage is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported soothing properties.
