Limonium duriusculum subsp. bianorii

Limonium duriusculum subsp.

bianorii, commonly known as the pink sea holly, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its striking pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, typically from July to September.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is often found growing in coastal areas or saline soils. It is well adapted to coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils.

This subspecies is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardiness.
