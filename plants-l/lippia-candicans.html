Lippia candicans

Lippia candicans, commonly known as silverleaf lippia, is a flowering plant native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring aromatic leaves with a silvery underside. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

Lippia candicans thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
