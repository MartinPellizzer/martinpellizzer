Litsea mogol

Litsea mogol, commonly known as mountain laurel, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like avocado and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers and elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

The fruit is a small, dark purple drupe that contains a single seed.
