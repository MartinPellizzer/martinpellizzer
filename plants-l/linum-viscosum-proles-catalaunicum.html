Linum viscosum proles catalaunicum

Linum viscosum proles catalaunicum, commonly known as the Catalaunic gum plant, is a species native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its sticky, glandular leaves and slender, branching stems. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in early summer. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and contain a viscous, resinous substance that has been historically used in traditional medicine.

This species is adapted to dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or disturbed soils.
