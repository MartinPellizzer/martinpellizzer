Lasianthus cyanocarpus var. bracteatus

Lasianthus cyanocarpus var. bracteatus, commonly known as the blue-fruited lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple berries, which are a distinctive feature of the species. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The flowers are small and clustered, often in dense inflorescences, contributing to its ornamental value.
