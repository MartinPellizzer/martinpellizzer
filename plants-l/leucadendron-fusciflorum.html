Leucadendron fusciflorum

Leucadendron fusciflorum, commonly known as the silver tree, is a South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive silvery-gray foliage and showy, cone-like inflorescences that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant is adapted to nutrient-poor soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation. It produces small, pale flowers that are pollinated by insects, and its seeds are dispersed by wind.

The silver tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
