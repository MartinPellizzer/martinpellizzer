Leontodon saxatilis subsp. saxatilis

Leontodon saxatilis subsp.

saxatilis, commonly known as the rock leontodon, is a flowering plant native to rocky and mountainous regions of Europe. It is a perennial herb with a basal rosette of stiff, linear leaves and produces a single, tall flowering stem.

The flowers are typically yellow, with five petals and prominent central stamens, and bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

This subspecies is characterized by its compact growth form and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions.
