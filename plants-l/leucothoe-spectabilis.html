Leucothoe spectabilis

Leucothoe spectabilis, commonly known as the showy leucothoe, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, slender stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white to pink, typically blooming in late spring. The plant produces dark blue, waxy berries that persist through the winter, adding ornamental value. Its foliage is dark green and glossy, providing year-round visual interest.

Leucothoe spectabilis thrives in partial to full shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.
