Leucas javanica var. montana

Leucas javanica var. montana, commonly known as mountain leucas, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories. Its flowers are small, clustered in whorls, and range in color from white to pale purple.

The leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its ornamental value in horticulture.
