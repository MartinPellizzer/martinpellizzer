Lonicera caerulea var. pallasii

Lonicera caerulea var. pallasii, commonly known as the Sakhalin honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to northeastern Asia.

It produces small, edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a sweet-tart flavor. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun. Its foliage is dark green with a slightly hairy texture, and it blooms with yellowish-white flowers in early summer.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its use in traditional cuisine and herbal remedies.
