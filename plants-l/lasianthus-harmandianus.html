Lasianthus harmandianus

Lasianthus harmandianus, commonly known as the Harmand's flame vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a rough texture.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight.
