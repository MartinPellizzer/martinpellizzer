Linosyris viscidiflora var. serrulata

Linosyris viscidiflora var. serrulata, commonly known as the serrated viscid-flowered linosyris, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its glandular, sticky leaves and its distinctive, serrated leaf margins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in desert or semi-arid environments.

Linosyris viscidiflora var. serrulata is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
