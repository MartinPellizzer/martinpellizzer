Leichhardtia connivens

Leichhardtia connivens, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads that resemble mist, with ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or swampy areas. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Leichhardtia connivens is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
