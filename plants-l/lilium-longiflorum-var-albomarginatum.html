Lilium longiflorum var. albomarginatum

Lilium longiflorum var. albomarginatum, commonly known as the white-edged lily, is a cultivated variety of the Easter lily.

It is characterized by its large, trumpet-shaped white flowers with distinctive cream or white margins. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 3 feet, featuring strap-like, grassy leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in religious and ceremonial contexts.
