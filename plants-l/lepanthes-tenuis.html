Lepanthes tenuis

Lepanthes tenuis, commonly known as the slender lepanthus, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pale pink. The plant grows as an epiphyte, often found on tree branches in humid tropical forests. Its leaves are usually narrow and strap-like, adapted for efficient water absorption in its natural habitat.

Lepanthes tenuis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
