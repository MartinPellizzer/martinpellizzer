Lyonia ligustrina var. salicifolia

Lyonia ligustrina var. salicifolia, commonly known as the willowleaf lyonia, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features slender, willow-like leaves that are alternate and lanceolate, often with a silvery underside. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters during the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sun, making it adaptable to various garden conditions.

Its ornamental value comes from its delicate foliage and showy blooms, making it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
