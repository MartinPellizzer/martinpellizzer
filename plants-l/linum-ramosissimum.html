Linum ramosissimum

Linum ramosissimum, commonly known as desert flax, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, pale blue or white flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oval seeds. It has adapted to survive in dry environments with deep roots and reduced leaf surface area.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential agricultural applications in drought-prone areas.
