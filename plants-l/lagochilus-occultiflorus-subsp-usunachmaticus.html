Lagochilus occultiflorus subsp. usunachmaticus

Lagochilus occultiflorus subsp.

usunachmaticus, commonly known as the hidden-flower rock rose, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with silver-gray foliage.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or violet, blooming in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
