Lecanthus major

Lecanthus major, commonly known as the common lecanthaceum, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Lecanthaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The genus Lecanthus is notable for its unique floral structure and limited distribution across the globe.
