Lobelia obconica

Lobelia obconica, commonly known as the narrowleaf lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features lance-shaped, slightly hairy leaves that are often reddish at the base. The plant produces striking blue to violet tubular flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Lobelia obconica is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
