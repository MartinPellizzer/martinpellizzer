Landolphia crassipes subvar. obtusiuscula

Landolphia crassipes subvar. obtusiuscula, commonly known as the broad-leaved African morning glory, is a tropical climbing vine native to Central and Southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces fleshy, orange-red berries that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Malvaceae and is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in some regions.
