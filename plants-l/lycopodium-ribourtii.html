Lycopodium ribourtii

Lycopodium ribourtii, commonly known as the ribourt's clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant features needle-like leaves arranged in whorls around its slender, branching stems. It produces cone-like structures called strobila, which contain the spores for reproduction.

Lycopodium ribourtii plays a role in maintaining soil stability and providing habitat for various microorganisms and small invertebrates.
