Lippia intermedia var. parvifolia

Lippia intermedia var. parvifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved lippia, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It features aromatic, small, opposite leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a dense covering of fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple, blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in gardens and as a medicinal plant.

Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
