Lycium ciliatum var. heteromorphum

Lycium ciliatum var. heteromorphum, commonly known as the variable boxthorn, is a shrub native to parts of Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces red berries that are edible but should be consumed in moderation. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in arid conditions.
