Lomanthus subcandidus

Lomanthus subcandidus, commonly known as the white-leaf lomanthus, is a species of flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its broad, waxy leaves that often have a silvery or white underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom in the spring. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Lomanthus subcandidus is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
