Lemaireocereus pruinosus

Lemaireocereus pruinosus, commonly known as the velvet cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in a dense, velvety coating of white to grayish hairs. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the evening, typically during the warmer months. Its growth habit is typically solitary or in small clusters, and it is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
