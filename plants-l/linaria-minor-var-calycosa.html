Linaria minor var. calycosa

Linaria minor var. calycosa, commonly known as the narrowleaf toadflax, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, hairy texture, and produces bright yellow, spurred flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slightly hairy surface, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable plant for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and adaptability to various garden conditions.
