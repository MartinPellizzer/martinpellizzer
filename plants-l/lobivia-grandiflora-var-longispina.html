Lobivia grandiflora var. longispina

Lobivia grandiflora var. longispina, commonly known as the Long-spined Lobivia, is a succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It features a compact, rounded rosette of thick, fleshy, blue-gray leaves that are covered in fine white hairs. The plant produces tall, branched stems that bear clusters of bright yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to dry conditions, storing water in its leaves and stems.

Due to its striking appearance and slow growth, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and cactus collections.
