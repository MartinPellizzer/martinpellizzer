Lepanthes similis

Lepanthes similis, commonly known as the similar orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant features delicate, usually green or pale green leaves and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. Its flowers are often clustered in inflorescences and are known for their intricate, ornate structure.

Lepanthes similis is valued in horticulture for its beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
