Linum perenne

Linum perenne, commonly known as perennial flax, is a flowering plant belonging to the Linaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in temperate climates. The plant produces striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its slender, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves are characteristic of its morphology.

Linum perenne is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in textile production.
