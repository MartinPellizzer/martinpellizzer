Libyella maroccana

Libyella maroccana, commonly known as the Moroccan rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North Africa.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves adapted to conserve water. The plant produces vibrant pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant in dry gardens.
