Leptodactylon californicum f. glandulosum

Leptodactylon californicum f. glandulosum, commonly known as the glandular leathery fern, is a variant of the leathery fern native to California.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery fronds that are covered with small, glandular dots, giving it a distinct appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky areas. Its fronds are evergreen and can reach lengths of up to 30 inches, contributing to its ornamental value.

This fern is well adapted to the Mediterranean climate of California, thriving in cool, humid conditions.
