Lobelia × speciosa var. schneckii

Lobelia × speciosa var. schneckii, commonly known as the Schneck's lobelia, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and ecologically valuable addition to native plant landscapes.
