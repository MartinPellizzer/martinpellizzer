Linum asiaticum

Linum asiaticum, commonly known as Asian flax, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Himalayas and parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and showy blue or purple flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

Linum asiaticum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
