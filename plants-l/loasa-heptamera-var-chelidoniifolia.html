Loasa heptamera var. chelidoniifolia

Loasa heptamera var. chelidoniifolia, commonly known as the yellow-flowered loasa, is a perennial shrub native to the Andes of South America.

It features compound leaves with a distinctive, palmate arrangement and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has a woody stem and produces small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant in horticulture, particularly for its vibrant floral display.
