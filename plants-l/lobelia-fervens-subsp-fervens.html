Lobelia fervens subsp. fervens

Lobelia fervens subsp.

fervens, commonly known as the fiery lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its vibrant red-orange flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall.

The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features lance-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Botanically, it belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is noted for its showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
