Lupinus clokeyanus

Lupinus clokeyanus, commonly known as the Clokey's lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, spiny pods. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats. It produces violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Lupinus clokeyanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
