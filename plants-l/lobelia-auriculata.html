Lobelia auriculata

Lobelia auriculata, commonly known as the ear-shaped lobe, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial with lance-shaped leaves and striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are clustered in dense whorls, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Lobelia auriculata is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
