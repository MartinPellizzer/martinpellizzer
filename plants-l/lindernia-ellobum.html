Lindernia ellobum

Lindernia ellobum, commonly known as the velvetleaf lindernia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a velvety texture. Its flowers are small, tubular, and usually pink or purple, arranged in spikes at the tips of the stems.

Lindernia ellobum is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
