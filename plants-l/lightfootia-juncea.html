Lightfootia juncea

Lightfootia juncea, commonly known as the slender sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It typically forms dense mats of succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in harsh environments. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are covered in a waxy cuticle, which reduces water loss and enhances its drought tolerance.

Lightfootia juncea is often found in sandy soils and is well adapted to thrive in conditions with limited water availability.
