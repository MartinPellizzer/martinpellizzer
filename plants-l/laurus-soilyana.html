Laurus soilyana

Laurus soilyana, commonly known as the Turkish laurel or Turkish bay leaf, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants such as cinnamon and avocado. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that emit a strong, spicy aroma when crushed. It typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to its more famous relative, Laurus nobilis.
