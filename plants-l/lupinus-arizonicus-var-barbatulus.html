Lupinus arizonicus var. barbatulus

Lupinus arizonicus var. barbatulus, commonly known as the Arizona lupine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Lupinus arizonicus var. barbatulus is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing soil in arid environments.
