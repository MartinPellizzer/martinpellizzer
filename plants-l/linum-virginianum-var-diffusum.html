Linum virginianum var. diffusum

Linum virginianum var. diffusum, commonly known as the spreading flax, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, branching stems that often lie prostrate or spread along the ground, forming a dense mat. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
