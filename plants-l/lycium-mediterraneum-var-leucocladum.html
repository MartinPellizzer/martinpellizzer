Lycium mediterraneum var. leucocladum

Lycium mediterraneum var. leucocladum, commonly known as the white-flowered boxthorn, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense, spiny foliage and produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has small, fleshy berries that are typically red when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and adaptability to arid conditions.
