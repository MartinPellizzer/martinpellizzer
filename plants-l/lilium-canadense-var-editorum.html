Lilium canadense var. editorum

Lilium canadense var. editorum, commonly known as the Canadian lily, is a botanical variety native to North America.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and features lance-shaped leaves with a smooth texture. It produces a single stem that bears several flower buds, which open sequentially.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
