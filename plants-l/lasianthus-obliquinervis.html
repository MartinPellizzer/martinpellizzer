Lasianthus obliquinervis

Lasianthus obliquinervis, commonly known as the broad-leafed lasianthus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Acanthaceae family.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often broad and have a distinctive veining pattern. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
