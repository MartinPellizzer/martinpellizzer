Limnocitrus

Limnocitrus, commonly known as the water lime, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant, yellowish-white flowers. The fruit is similar to a citrus fruit, with a thick, leathery rind and a juicy, acidic pulp. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for its edible fruit.
