Lycium acnistoides

Lycium acnistoides, commonly known as the prickly lycium, is a shrub native to regions of Asia, particularly China and the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces red berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a rough texture, while its flowers are typically clustered in inflorescences.

Lycium acnistoides is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh climates.
