Luzula acutifolia subsp. acutifolia

Luzula acutifolia subsp.

acutifolia, commonly known as sharpleaf woodrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features slender, grass-like leaves that are sharply pointed, giving it its common name. Its inflorescence consists of dense clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that appear in late spring to early summer. Luzula acutifolia subsp.

acutifolia is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
