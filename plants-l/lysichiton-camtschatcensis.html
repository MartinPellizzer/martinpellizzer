Lysichiton camtschatcensis

Lysichiton camtschatcensis, commonly known as the giant cowplant, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the coastal regions of the Russian Far East.

It is characterized by its large, broad, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces showy, yellowish-green flowers arranged in a dense, umbel-like cluster. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in tidal zones and along shorelines.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a notable species in its native habitat.
