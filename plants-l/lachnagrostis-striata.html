Lachnagrostis striata

Lachnagrostis striata, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-tinged foliage and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its small, spike-like inflorescences produce wind-pollinated flowers, and it is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal.

Lachnagrostis striata is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity in wetland habitats.
