Limodorum rubrum

Limodorum rubrum, commonly known as the red hellebore, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, dark red to purple-colored spathe that encloses a spadix of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and often have a waxy texture.

Limodorum rubrum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in traditional medicine.
