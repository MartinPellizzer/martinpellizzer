Lupinus plattensis

Lupinus plattensis, commonly known as the plains lupine, is a flowering plant native to the central United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairie and grassland habitats.

Lupinus plattensis plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native environment.
