Liparis hildebrandtiana

Liparis hildebrandtiana, commonly known as the Hildebrandt's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, grass-like foliage and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with a distinctive lip that aids in pollination.

Liparis hildebrandtiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
