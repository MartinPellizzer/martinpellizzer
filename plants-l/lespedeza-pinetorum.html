Lespedeza pinetorum

Lespedeza pinetorum, commonly known as the sand hill bluecurls, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in open woodlands or pine forests. It produces small, trifoliate leaves and is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Lespedeza pinetorum is also valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a nectar source for pollinators.
