Lysinema purpurascens var. rubrum

Lysinema purpurascens var. rubrum, commonly known as the red-stemmed tansy, is a perennial herb native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems that turn reddish in color as the plant matures. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters called cymes. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This species is adapted to harsh environments and is often found in moist, rocky soils.
