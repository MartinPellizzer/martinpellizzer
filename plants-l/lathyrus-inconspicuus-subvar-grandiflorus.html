Lathyrus inconspicuus subvar. grandiflorus

Lathyrus inconspicuus subvar. grandiflorus, commonly known as the large-flowered grass vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and trifoliate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces showy, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or violet in color, blooming in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed sites.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in nitrogen fixation in soil ecosystems.
