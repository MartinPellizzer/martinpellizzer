Lissochilus livingstoneanus

Lissochilus livingstoneanus, commonly known as the Livingstone's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to the high-altitude regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in late summer. The plant produces showy, pale yellow to greenish-white flowers with a distinct lip that is often adorned with intricate markings. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or grassy habitats.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
