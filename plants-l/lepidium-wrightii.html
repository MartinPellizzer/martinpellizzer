Lepidium wrightii

Lepidium wrightii, commonly known as Wright's cress, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and slopes. Its small, white or yellow flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in the spring.

The leaves are usually alternate and pinnately divided, giving the plant a distinctive appearance.
