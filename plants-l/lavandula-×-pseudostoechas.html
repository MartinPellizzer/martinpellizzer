Lavandula × pseudostoechas

Lavandula × pseudostoechas, commonly known as Portuguese lavender, is a hybrid shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and aromatic, silver-green foliage. The plant produces spikes of purple or lilac flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its essential oils, derived from the flowers, are widely used in aromatherapy and perfumery.
