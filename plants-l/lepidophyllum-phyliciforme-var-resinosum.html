Lepidophyllum phyliciforme var. resinosum

Lepidophyllum phyliciforme var. resinosum, commonly known as the resinous lepidophyllum, is a member of the Scrophulariaceae family.

It is a low-growing, evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern California. The plant is characterized by its small, opposite leaves that are often covered in a waxy, resinous coating. This species is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal scrub habitats.
