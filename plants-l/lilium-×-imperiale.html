Lilium × imperiale

Lilium × imperiale, commonly known as the imperial lily, is a hybrid perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, supporting large, showy flowers with six to nine petals. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive red or orange throat, and bloom in late summer to early autumn. This plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

It is also valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in horticulture.
