Leucothoe laxiflora var. hookeriana

Leucothoe laxiflora var. hookeriana, commonly known as Hooker's leucothoe, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green, with showy, bell-shaped white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. Its fruit is a small, dark purple drupe that ripens in the fall.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide year-round interest in woodland gardens.
