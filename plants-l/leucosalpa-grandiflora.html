Leucosalpa grandiflora

Leucosalpa grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered snowbell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has smooth, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas at high elevations.

Leucosalpa grandiflora is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable climates.
