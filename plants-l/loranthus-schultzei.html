Loranthus schultzei

Loranthus schultzei, commonly known as the Schultze's mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using the light it captures. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces small, white or yellow flowers that are often clustered together. Its berries are typically red or orange and contain sticky seeds that adhere to the bark of trees, facilitating its spread.

This species plays an important ecological role in its habitat by influencing host tree growth and providing food for various animals.
