Lavoisiera pulchella

Lavoisiera pulchella, commonly known as the beautiful lavoisiera, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
