Lupinus laxiflorus var. multitinctus

Lupinus laxiflorus var. multitinctus, commonly known as the multicolored lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of pea-shaped flowers that display a range of colors, including purple, blue, and white. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open grasslands and rocky areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
