Libocedrus chilensis

Libocedrus chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean red cedar, is a coniferous tree native to central and southern Chile.

It belongs to the cypress family (Cupressaceae) and is characterized by its reddish bark and aromatic foliage. The plant has scale-like leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the branches. Its cones are small and woody, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Libocedrus chilensis is valued for its durable wood and is often found in temperate rainforests and mountainous regions.
