Leptorkis dendrochilum

Leptorkis dendrochilum, commonly known as the tree-lipped orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive, showy flowers that typically bloom in summer. The plant features a sympodial growth pattern, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually lanceolate and arranged in a rosette, while the flowers are often fragrant and attract various pollinators.

Leptorkis dendrochilum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
