Linum austriacum subsp. squamulosum

Linum austriacum subsp.

squamulosum, commonly known as the Austrian flax or squamulous flax, is a flowering plant native to Central Europe. It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces showy blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassy areas or along forest edges.

Its fibrous stalks were historically used for textile production, similar to other flax species.
