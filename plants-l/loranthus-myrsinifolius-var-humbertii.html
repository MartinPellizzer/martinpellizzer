Loranthus myrsinifolius var. humbertii

Loranthus myrsinifolius var. humbertii, commonly known as the Humbert's mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to its host tree through a specialized root system called a haustellum.

It belongs to the family Loranthaceae and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and showy, often red or yellow flowers. The plant primarily grows on the branches of host trees such as eucalyptus and acacia, drawing water and nutrients from them. Its reproductive strategy involves producing small, fleshy berries that are dispersed by birds.

This species is found in various regions of Australia and is of interest to botanists due to its unique parasitic lifestyle and ecological role.
