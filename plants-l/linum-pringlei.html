Linum pringlei

Linum pringlei, commonly known as the Pringle's flax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and delicate blue to purple flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. Linum pringlei is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in its native range.
