Liparis cuspidata

Liparis cuspidata, commonly known as the spike orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest undergrowth. Its leaves are linear and often have a slight sheen, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Liparis cuspidata is valued in horticulture for its unique appearance and relatively easy cultivation requirements.
