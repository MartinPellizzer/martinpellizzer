Liparis carnicolor

Liparis carnicolor, commonly known as the pink lady's tresses, is a terrestrial orchid belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and long, spiraling inflorescences that bear small, pinkish-white flowers. Native to parts of Europe and Asia, it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has a rhizomatous root system and is often found growing in colonies.

Its flowers are typically pollinated by bees and other insects, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitats.
