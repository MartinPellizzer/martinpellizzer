Lupinus bonplandianus

Lupinus bonplandianus, commonly known as the Bonpland lupine, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules.

Lupinus bonplandianus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in ecological restoration projects.
