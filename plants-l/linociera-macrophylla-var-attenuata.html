Linociera macrophylla var. attenuata

Linociera macrophylla var. attenuata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tamarind, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated, woody pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine and cuisine.
