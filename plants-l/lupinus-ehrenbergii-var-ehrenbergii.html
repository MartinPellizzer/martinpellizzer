Lupinus ehrenbergii var. ehrenbergii

Lupinus ehrenbergii var. ehrenbergii, commonly known as the desert lupine, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to violet pea-shaped flowers. The plant has compound leaves composed of several small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Lupinus ehrenbergii var. ehrenbergii is also known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.
