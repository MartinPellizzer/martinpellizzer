Lonchitis coriacea

Lonchitis coriacea, commonly known as the roughleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant typically grows in open, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in grassland habitats. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions.
