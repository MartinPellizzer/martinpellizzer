Lespedeza bicolor subsp. formosa

Lespedeza bicolor subsp.

formosa, commonly known as the pink burr medic, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America. It is a herbaceous perennial that produces small, pink to purple flower clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or disturbed areas. Lespedeza bicolor subsp.

formosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
