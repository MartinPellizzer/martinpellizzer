Lupinus californicus

Lupinus californicus, commonly known as California lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats.

Lupinus californicus plays an important role in nitrogen fixation, enriching the soil for other plants.
