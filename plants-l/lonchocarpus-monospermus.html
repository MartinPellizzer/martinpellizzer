Lonchocarpus monospermus

Lonchocarpus monospermus, commonly known as the yellow cowpea, is a tropical leguminous plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and produces distinctive yellow to orange pods that contain several seeds. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in agricultural systems. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often grown as a forage crop or for its edible seeds.

The species is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
