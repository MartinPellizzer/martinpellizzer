Lupinus sanctae-annae

Lupinus sanctae-annae, commonly known as the Saint Anne's lupin, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces spiny pods that contain several elliptical seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
