Laurus subscandens

Laurus subscandens, commonly known as the creeping laurel or sweet bay, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are aromatic and often used in culinary applications. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible but not commonly consumed. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a hedge.

Its ornamental value and fragrance make it a popular choice in horticulture.
