Lupinus elegantulus

Lupinus elegantulus, commonly known as the elegant lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and showy pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces clusters of vibrant blue or purple blooms. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Lupinus elegantulus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
