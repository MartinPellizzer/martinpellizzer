Lycopus amplectens var. pubens

Lycopus amplectens var. pubens, commonly known as hairy water huckleberry, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its fruit is a dark blue berry that ripens in late summer and is often consumed by birds and small mammals.

Lycopus amplectens var. pubens is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in wetland ecosystems.
