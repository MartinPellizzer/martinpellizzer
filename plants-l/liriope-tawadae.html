Liriope tawadae

Liriope tawadae, commonly known as the Japanese woodland lily, is a perennial evergreen grass-like plant native to Japan.

It features dense clumps of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a low, compact rosette. The plant produces tall, slender flower stalks bearing small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple. It thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Liriope tawadae is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive foliage and delicate floral display.
