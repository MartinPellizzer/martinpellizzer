Linaria purpurea subsp. cossonii

Linaria purpurea subsp.

cossonii, commonly known as the purple toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its striking purple to pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its slender, branching stems are covered in fine hairs, and the leaves are narrow and lance-shaped.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ease of care.
