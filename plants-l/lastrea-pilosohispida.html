Lastrea pilosohispida

Lastrea pilosohispida, commonly known as the hairy hispid lastrea, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family, commonly referred to as the ice plant family, and is characterized by its fleshy, water-storing leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a delicate touch to its succulent foliage. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
