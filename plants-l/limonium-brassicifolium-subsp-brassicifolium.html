Limonium brassicifolium subsp. brassicifolium

Limonium brassicifolium subsp.

brassicifolium, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal areas of the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers and linear, grass-like leaves that are adapted to salty, windy environments.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dunes and rocky shores. Its flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Limonium brassicifolium subsp.

brassicifolium is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in coastal gardens.
