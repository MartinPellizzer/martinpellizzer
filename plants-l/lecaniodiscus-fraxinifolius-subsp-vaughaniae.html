Lecaniodiscus fraxinifolius subsp. vaughaniae

Lecaniodiscus fraxinifolius subsp.

vaughaniae, commonly known as the Mexican ironwood, is a member of the Rutaceae family. It is a small to medium-sized tree characterized by its rough, fissured bark and dense, evergreen foliage.

The leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and have a leathery texture with a smooth margin. The flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and arranged in axillary clusters, while the fruit is a globose capsule that splits into three valves.

This species is native to Mexico and is valued for its hard, heavy wood used in traditional crafts and construction.
