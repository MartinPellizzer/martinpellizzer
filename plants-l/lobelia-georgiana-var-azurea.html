Lobelia georgiana var. azurea

Lobelia georgiana var. azurea, commonly known as blue lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking cobalt-blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland areas and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
