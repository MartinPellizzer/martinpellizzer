Liatris squarrosa var. typica

Liatris squarrosa var. typica, commonly known as the purpletop blazing star, is a native North American plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescence composed of purple to pinkish-purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies, woodlands, and disturbed soils. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine, white hairs, giving them a fuzzy appearance.

It is a perennial herb that plays an important role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
