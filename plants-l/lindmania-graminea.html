Lindmania graminea

Lindmania graminea, commonly known as the grass-like bromeliad, is a species native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central tank to collect rainwater. The plant has narrow, grass-like foliage that gives it its common name, and it produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white and purple. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Lindmania graminea is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its unique appearance and adaptability.
