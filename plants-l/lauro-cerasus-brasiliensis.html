Lauro-cerasus brasiliensis

Lauro-cerasus brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or Brazilian laurel, is a species of flowering plant in the Prunus genus.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various soil types.

Lauro-cerasus brasiliensis is also valued for its ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
