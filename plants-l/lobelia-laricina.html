Lobelia laricina

Lobelia laricina, commonly known as bluebeard, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Lobelia laricina is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used by Indigenous peoples for various health purposes.
