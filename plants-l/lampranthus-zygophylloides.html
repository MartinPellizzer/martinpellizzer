Lampranthus zygophylloides

Lampranthus zygophylloides, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features striking red, tubular flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like birds and insects. The plant has fleshy, ribbed leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. It belongs to the Aizoaceae family, known for their diverse and often ornamental species.

Lampranthus zygophylloides is often cultivated for its vibrant appearance and low maintenance requirements.
