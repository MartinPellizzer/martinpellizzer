Loranthus gaudichaudii

Loranthus gaudichaudii, commonly known as the mistletoe of the Pacific, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of native Australian eucalyptus trees.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using the light it captures. The plant has small, scale-like leaves and produces white or pink flowers that attract insects for pollination. Its berries are red and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by influencing host tree growth and providing food for wildlife.
