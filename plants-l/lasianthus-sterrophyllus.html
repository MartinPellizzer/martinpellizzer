Lasianthus sterrophyllus

Lasianthus sterrophyllus, commonly known as the silver-leafed bellflower, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, typically blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments in tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections for its unique foliage and delicate floral display.
