Lilium pardalinum var. parviflorum

Lilium pardalinum var. parviflorum, commonly known as the dwarf tiger lily, is a rare and delicate variant of the tiger lily species.

It is characterized by its smaller, pale yellow to orange flowers with intricate red and brown markings, resembling a tiger's stripes. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30-60 centimeters, making it more compact than its larger relatives. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem.

This variety is native to the eastern United States and thrives in well-drained, loamy soils in partial to full sun.
