Luzula erecta

Luzula erecta, commonly known as upright woodrush, is a perennial plant belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, greenish to brownish flowers.

Luzula erecta is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded conditions.
