Lonicera leptantha

Lonicera leptantha, commonly known as the slender honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Lonicera leptantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to wildlife.
