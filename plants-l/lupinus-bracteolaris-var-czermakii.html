Lupinus bracteolaris var. czermakii

Lupinus bracteolaris var. czermakii, commonly known as the Czermak's lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and clusters of spiky, pea-like flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces legume pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
