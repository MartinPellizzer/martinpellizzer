Liatris borealis f. albiflora

Liatris borealis f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered blazing star, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence composed of numerous white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and sandy soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette.

The flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
