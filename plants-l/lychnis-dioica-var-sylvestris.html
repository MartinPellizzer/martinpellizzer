Lychnis dioica var. sylvestris

Lychnis dioica var. sylvestris, commonly known as the woodland campion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in woodland edges, meadows, and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soil. The plant features simple, oval-shaped leaves and produces clusters of pink or red, five-petaled flowers. It blooms from late spring to early summer, and its stems are often hairy.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant flowers.
