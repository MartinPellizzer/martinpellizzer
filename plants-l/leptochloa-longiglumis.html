Leptochloa longiglumis

Leptochloa longiglumis, commonly known as the long glume grass, is a species of grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-like inflorescences with distinctive elongated glumes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or marshy habitats.

Leptochloa longiglumis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
