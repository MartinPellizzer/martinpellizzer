Lappula occidentalis var. occidentalis

Lappula occidentalis var. occidentalis, commonly known as western lappula, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often covered in fine hairs.

The fruit is a dry, dehiscent silique containing several seeds.
