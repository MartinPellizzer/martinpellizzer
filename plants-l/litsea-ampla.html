Litsea ampla

Litsea ampla, commonly known as the Chinese tallowtree, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces berries that contain a single seed, which is dispersed by animals. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other parts of the world.

However, it can become invasive in some regions, outcompeting native vegetation.
