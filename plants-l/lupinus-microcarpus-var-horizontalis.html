Lupinus microcarpus var. horizontalis

Lupinus microcarpus var. horizontalis, commonly known as the coastal lupine, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of pea-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, hairy pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils along coastal dunes and rocky shores.

This variety is adapted to coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.
