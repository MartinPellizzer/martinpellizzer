Limonium minutum subsp. revolutum

Limonium minutum subsp.

revolutum, commonly known as the narrowleaf sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its small, delicate blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in coastal areas, sandy soils, and rocky habitats. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly curled edge, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained conditions.
