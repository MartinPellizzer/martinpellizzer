Lupinus luteus var. sulphureus

Lupinus luteus var. sulphureus, commonly known as the sulphur yellow lupine, is a variant of the yellow lupine native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and hairy stems, which are typical features of the Lupinus genus. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for arid and semi-arid climates. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces cylindrical seed pods containing several seeds.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has also been studied for its potential in nitrogen fixation and soil improvement.
