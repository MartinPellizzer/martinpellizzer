Lobivia kupperiana

Lobivia kupperiana, commonly known as the Kupper's lobivia, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and ribbed structure. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for rocky or sandy habitats.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
