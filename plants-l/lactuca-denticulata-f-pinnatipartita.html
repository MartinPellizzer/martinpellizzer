Lactuca denticulata f. pinnatipartita

Lactuca denticulata f. pinnatipartita, commonly known as the finely toothed lettuce, is a botanical variant of the common lettuce species.

It is characterized by its finely serrated leaves that are typically narrow and elongated. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is native to regions of Europe and western Asia. Its foliage is often used in salads due to its crisp texture and mild flavor.

This form is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary versatility.
