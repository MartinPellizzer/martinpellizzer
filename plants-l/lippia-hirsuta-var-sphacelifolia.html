Lippia hirsuta var. sphacelifolia

Lippia hirsuta var. sphacelifolia, commonly known as the hairy lippia, is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It features aromatic, hairy leaves that are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
