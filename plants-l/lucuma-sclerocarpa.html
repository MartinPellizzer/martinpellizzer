Lucuma sclerocarpa

Lucuma sclerocarpa, commonly known as the golden fruit or lucuma, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

The fruit develops from an inflorescence called a cyme, which consists of multiple small, fleshy berries.
