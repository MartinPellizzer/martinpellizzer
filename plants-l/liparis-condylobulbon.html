Liparis condylobulbon

Liparis condylobulbon, commonly known as the narrow-leafed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories or rocky crevices. It has a rhizomatous root system and produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
