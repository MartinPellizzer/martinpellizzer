Ladenbergia malacophylla

Ladenbergia malacophylla, commonly known as the velvetwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, glossy bark and dense, leathery leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is a popular choice for horticultural cultivation.
