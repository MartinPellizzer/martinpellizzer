Lasianthus maingayi var. subglaber

Lasianthus maingayi var. subglaber, commonly known as the rough-leaved lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, stiff hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it is more commonly encountered in its natural habitat.
