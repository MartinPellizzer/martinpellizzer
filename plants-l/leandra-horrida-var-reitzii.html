Leandra horrida var. reitzii

Leandra horrida var. reitzii, commonly known as the Zulu lily, is a striking flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, elegant stems and clusters of bright white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces large, showy inflorescences that bloom in late winter to early spring, making it a popular choice for gardens in mild climates. This variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to thrive in well-drained soil with partial shade.
