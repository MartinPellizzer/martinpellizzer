Lathyrus sandbergii

Lathyrus sandbergii, commonly known as Sandberg's vetch, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a legume, belonging to the Fabaceae family, and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple, and it is often found in prairie and woodland habitats. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.

Lathyrus sandbergii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
