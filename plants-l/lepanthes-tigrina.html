Lepanthes tigrina

Lepanthes tigrina, commonly known as the tiger orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, brightly colored flowers that often feature intricate patterns resembling a tiger's stripes. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments such as cloud forests and rainforests. Its leaves are usually dark green and broad, adapted to capture as much light as possible in its low-light habitat.

Lepanthes tigrina is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
