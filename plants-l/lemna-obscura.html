Lemna obscura

Lemna obscura, commonly known as the dark duckweed, is a small aquatic plant belonging to the family Araceae.

It is characterized by its tiny, oval-shaped leaves that are typically darker in color compared to other duckweed species. The plant lacks true roots and instead has root-like structures called rhizoids that anchor it to submerged surfaces. It reproduces both sexually and asexually, with new plants forming from the fragmentation of existing ones.

Lemna obscura is often found in slow-moving freshwater environments and can play a role in water filtration and as a food source for aquatic organisms.
