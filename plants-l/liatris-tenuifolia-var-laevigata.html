Liatris tenuifolia var. laevigata

Liatris tenuifolia var. laevigata, commonly known as narrowleaf blazingstar, is a flowering plant native to the central and southern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and tall, spiky flower heads composed of numerous small, pinkish to purple flowers. The leaves are narrow, linear, and often covered with fine hairs, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for native plant gardens.

This variety is known for its ornamental value and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
