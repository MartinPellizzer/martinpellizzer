Lepicephalus succisa

Lepicephalus succisa, commonly known as the silverleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-gray, hairy leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy white seeds dispersed by the wind. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along roadsides.

Lepicephalus succisa is a hardy perennial that is both ornamental and ecologically important for local wildlife.
