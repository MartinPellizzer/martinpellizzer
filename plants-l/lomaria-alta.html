Lomaria alta

Lomaria alta, commonly known as the high lomaria, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate to subtropical climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range in color from white to pale yellow.

Lomaria alta is often found in shaded forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
