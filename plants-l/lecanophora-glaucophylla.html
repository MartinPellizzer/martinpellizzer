Lecanophora glaucophylla

Lecanophora glaucophylla, commonly known as the glaucophylla mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of eucalyptus trees in Australia.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a waxy, whitish coating, which helps reduce water loss in its arid habitat. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are often clustered together, followed by berries that are consumed by birds. Its root system forms haustoria, which penetrate the host tree's vascular system to absorb nutrients and water.

This plant plays an important ecological role in its native environment, supporting various bird species and contributing to the biodiversity of eucalyptus forests.
