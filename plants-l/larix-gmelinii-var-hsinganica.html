Larix gmelinii var. hsinganica

Larix gmelinii var. hsinganica, commonly known as the Hsian Mountains larch, is a variant of the Siberian larch native to northeastern China.

It is a deciduous conifer characterized by its needle-like leaves that turn golden before falling in autumn. The tree produces cone-like structures that contain winged seeds, typical of the Pinaceae family. It thrives in cold, temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
