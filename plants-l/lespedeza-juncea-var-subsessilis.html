Lespedeza juncea var. subsessilis

Lespedeza juncea var. subsessilis, commonly known as narrowleaf lespedeza, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in late summer. The leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. This plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and disturbed areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone regions.
