Lespedeza bracteolata

Lespedeza bracteolata, commonly known as the narrowleaf lespedeza, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late summer. Its trifoliate leaves are alternate and have a delicate, fern-like appearance.

Lespedeza bracteolata is often found in open woodlands and disturbed soils, and it plays a role in soil stabilization and wildlife habitat.
