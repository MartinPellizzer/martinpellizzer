Layia glandulosa var. hispida

Layia glandulosa var. hispida, commonly known as the hispid layia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. Its structure includes a low-growing, spreading habit with multiple stems and small, lance-shaped leaves.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and ecological importance to pollinators.
