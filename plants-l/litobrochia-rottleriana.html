Litobrochia rottleriana

Litobrochia rottleriana, commonly known as the mountain geranium, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are adapted to withstand cold and arid conditions. It thrives in rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows.

Litobrochia rottleriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable climates.
