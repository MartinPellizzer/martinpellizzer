Lupinus sparsiflorus var. pondii

Lupinus sparsiflorus var. pondii, commonly known as the pond's lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats.

Lupinus sparsiflorus var. pondii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
