Lagerstroemia subcostata

Lagerstroemia subcostata, commonly known as the Japanese pagoda tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and striking, peeling bark that reveals layers of different colors. The plant produces clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a serrated margin, turning red or orange in the fall.

It thrives in well-drained soil and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various climates.
