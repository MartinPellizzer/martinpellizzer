Laelia autumnalis alba

Laelia autumnalis alba, commonly known as the white winter orchid, is a species native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its elegant, white floral spikes that bloom during the cooler months, typically from late autumn to early spring. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers with intricate patterns on their petals and sepals. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and high humidity.

Botanically, it belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is prized for its ornamental value in horticulture.
