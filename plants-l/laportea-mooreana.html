Laportea mooreana

Laportea mooreana, commonly known as the Moore's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and can reach heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are hairy and have a distinctive, slightly fuzzy texture.

The showy purple flowers appear in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
