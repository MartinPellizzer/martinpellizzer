Lepicephalus medius

Lepicephalus medius, commonly known as the silver-leafed fern, is a rare and delicate plant native to the humid understories of tropical rainforests.

It features finely divided, silvery-green fronds that shimmer in the dappled light of its natural habitat. The plant's rhizome system is extensive, allowing it to anchor itself in moist, nutrient-rich soil. It reproduces through spores rather than seeds, a characteristic common among primitive vascular plants.

Due to its specific environmental requirements, it is highly sensitive to habitat disruption and is considered a conservation concern.
