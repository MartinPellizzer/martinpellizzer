Leptostachya wallichii

Leptostachya wallichii, commonly known as the Himalayan feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and fine, feathery foliage that gives it a delicate appearance. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its inflorescence consists of a narrow, spike-like cluster of small, greenish flowers.

Leptostachya wallichii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in ecological restoration projects in its native range.
