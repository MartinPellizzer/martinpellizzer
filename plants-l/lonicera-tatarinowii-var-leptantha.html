Lonicera tatarinowii var. leptantha

Lonicera tatarinowii var. leptantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaf honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, arching stems covered in opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, yellowish-white flowers in the spring, which are followed by red berries that attract birds. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in gardens and natural habitats.
