Lablab purpureus

Lablab purpureus, commonly known as hyacinth bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with trifoliate leaves and purple or white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical pods containing edible seeds that are rich in protein and nutrients. Its roots have also been traditionally used in herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.

Lablab purpureus is often cultivated for both ornamental and nutritional purposes.
