Lupinus sinus-meyersi

Lupinus sinus-meyersi, commonly known as the Sinus Meyers lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces spiny, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds, typical of the genus Lupinus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and sometimes endangered species.
