Linaria glauca subsp. olcadium

Linaria glauca subsp.

olcadium, commonly known as the blue toadflax, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, powdery blue or gray bloom, which gives it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable, supporting various pollinators.
