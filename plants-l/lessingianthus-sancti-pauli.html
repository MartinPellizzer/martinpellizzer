Lessingianthus sancti-pauli

Lessingianthus sancti-pauli, commonly known as the Pauli's sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and it produces small, brownish seeds in its composite flower heads.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
