Leucopogon richei var. acutifolius

Leucopogon richei var. acutifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is a shrub with dense, evergreen foliage and produces small, urn-shaped flowers that are typically pink or pale red. The leaves are narrow and lanceolate, with a leathery texture and a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract native pollinators.
