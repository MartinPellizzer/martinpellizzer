Lobelia gigantea

Lobelia gigantea, commonly known as giant lobelia, is a striking perennial plant native to the high elevations of the Appalachian Mountains.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems that can reach heights of up to 10 feet, adorned with clusters of tubular, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to purple. The plant produces showy, showy inflorescences that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as butterflies and bees. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged, wild appearance.

Lobelia gigantea thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.
