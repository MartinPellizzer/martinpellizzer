Lecythis barnebyi

Lecythis barnebyi, commonly known as the Barneby's lecythium, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Lecythidaceae and is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and showy, orange-yellow flowers. The tree produces a heavy, oily fruit with a thick, fibrous outer layer. Its seeds are encased in a hard, woody shell and are often dispersed by animals.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local communities.
