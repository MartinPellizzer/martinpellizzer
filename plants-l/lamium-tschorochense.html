Lamium tschorochense

Lamium tschorochense, commonly known as the alpine deadnettle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are usually oval to lance-shaped. The plant produces small, two-lipped flowers in shades of pink or purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and slopes.

Lamium tschorochense is adapted to cold climates and is a member of the mint family, Lamiaceae.
