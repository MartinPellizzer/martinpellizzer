Loudetia pilgeriana

Loudetia pilgeriana, commonly known as the Pilger's lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and as a ground cover.
