Lisianthius campanuloides

Lisianthius campanuloides, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, sky-blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, bell-shaped blooms that resemble a misty cloud. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This species is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
