Lippia adoensis var. multicaulis

Lippia adoensis var. multicaulis, commonly known as the hairy lippia, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale purple, arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
