Lantana canescens f. parvifolia

Lantana canescens f. parvifolia, commonly known as the smallflower lantana, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

However, it can be invasive in some regions and may require control measures to prevent overgrowth.
