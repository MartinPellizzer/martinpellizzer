Liparis dolichobulbon

Liparis dolichobulbon, commonly known as the long-bulb orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its terrestrial growth habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white in color. The plant features a rhizomatous structure with long, fleshy bulbs that store nutrients, aiding in its survival in seasonal environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it blooms during the warmer months.

Liparis dolichobulbon is often found in moist, shaded forest understories where it thrives in well-drained soil.
