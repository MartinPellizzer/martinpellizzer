Linanthus demissus

Linanthus demissus, commonly known as the slender flax or desert flax, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of pale yellow to greenish flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Linanthus demissus is adapted to arid environments and thrives in sandy or gravelly soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of desert ecosystems.
