Lepanthes pectinata

Lepanthes pectinata, commonly known as the pectinate orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its leaves are usually broad and lance-shaped, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Lepanthes pectinata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and adaptability to cultivated conditions.
