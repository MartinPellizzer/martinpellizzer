Lachnaea buxifolia var. virens

Lachnaea buxifolia var. virens, commonly known as the green-leaved boxleaf lichen, is a foliose lichen that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is characterized by its dense, leaf-like thallus with a smooth, bright green upper surface and a darker, more textured underside. The plant forms symbiotic relationships with algae, which provide nutrients through photosynthesis. It is often found growing on bark, soil, or rocks in temperate forests.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and sensitive indicator of environmental health.
