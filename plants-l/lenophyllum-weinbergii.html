Lenophyllum weinbergii

Lenophyllum weinbergii, commonly known as the Weinberg's flax lily, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its strap-shaped, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
