Lavatera grandiflora

Lavatera grandiflora, commonly known as the silky oaks, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, featuring compound leaves with soft, silvery undersides. The plant produces showy, fluffy white or pink flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant once established.

Lavatera grandiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
