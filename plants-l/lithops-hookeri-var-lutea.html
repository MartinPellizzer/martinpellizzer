Lithops hookeri var. lutea

Lithops hookeri var. lutea, commonly known as the Yellow Stone Lithops, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, stone-like appearance, with two closely fused leaves that form a distinctive, rounded structure. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers during the summer months, adding a striking contrast to its foliage. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
