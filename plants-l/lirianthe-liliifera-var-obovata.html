Lirianthe liliifera var. obovata

Lirianthe liliifera var. obovata, commonly known as the broad-leaf lirianthe, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The leaves are broad and ovate, with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy appearance. This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its striking floral display and adaptability to various growing conditions.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.
