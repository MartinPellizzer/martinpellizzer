Lepanthes cloesii

Lepanthes cloesii, commonly known as the Cloes's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist environments within the understory of tropical forests. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces fragrant, white or pale green flowers with intricate, spurred petals. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure and subtle coloration.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
