Litsea petiolata

Litsea petiolata, commonly known as the fragrant leaf tree, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, ovate leaves that are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils. Its small, yellowish-white flowers are followed by elliptical, red or orange fruits that contain a single seed.

Litsea petiolata is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitats.
