Lipostoma capitatum

Lipostoma capitatum, commonly known as the purple fringed gentian, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its showy, violet-blue flowers with distinctive fringed petals. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.
