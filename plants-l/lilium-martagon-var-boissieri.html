Lilium martagon var. boissieri

Lilium martagon var. boissieri, commonly known as the Turk's Cap Lily, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Turkey.

It is characterized by its striking, recurved, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped blooms on long, upright stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in horticulture.
