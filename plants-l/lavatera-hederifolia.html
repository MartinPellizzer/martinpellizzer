Lavatera hederifolia

Lavatera hederifolia, commonly known as the ivy-leaved hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves that resemble ivy, giving it its common name. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Lavatera hederifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
