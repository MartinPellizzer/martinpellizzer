Lobelia goetzei

Lobelia goetzei, commonly known as the Goetze's lobelia, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and wetlands. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. Lobelia goetzei is part of the Campanulaceae family, known for its showy, bell-shaped blooms.

It is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
