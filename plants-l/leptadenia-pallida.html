Leptadenia pallida

Leptadenia pallida, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a flowering plant native to Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, with slender, branched stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its latex is milky and has been used traditionally for medicinal purposes.

The plant is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils.
