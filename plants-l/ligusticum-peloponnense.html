Ligusticum peloponnense

Ligusticum peloponnense, commonly known as the Peloponnesian angelica, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring finely divided leaves and yellowish-white flowers that bloom in summer. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and rocky areas.
