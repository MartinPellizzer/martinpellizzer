Liparis nepalensis

Liparis nepalensis, commonly known as the Nepal orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal and northern India.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. It has a rhizomatous root system that helps it anchor in rocky or soil substrates.

Liparis nepalensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage and subtle floral display.
