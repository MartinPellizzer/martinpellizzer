Linaria tingitana var. ochroleuca

Linaria tingitana var. ochroleuca, commonly known as the yellow beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellow to orange flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture. This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its vibrant color and compact size.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.
