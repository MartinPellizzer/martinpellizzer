Leersia brasiliensis

Leersia brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian leersia, is a grass species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a perennial plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its botanical features include narrow, linear leaves and tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil in wetland areas.
