Leea pauciflora

Leea pauciflora, commonly known as the few-flowered leea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Leeaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, typically found at the base of the leaves. It is often found in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage.
