Luzula nemorosa hylogiton

Luzula nemorosa hylogiton, commonly known as the silver moss grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit. The plant features slender, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a fine, silvery bloom, giving it its common name. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Luzula nemorosa hylogiton typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
