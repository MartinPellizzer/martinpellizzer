Limonium coelicolor

Limonium coelicolor, commonly known as the sky blue eryngo, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Limonium coelicolor is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
