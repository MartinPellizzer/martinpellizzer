Lampsana edulis

Lampsana edulis, commonly known as the edible arrowhead, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, which includes plants like taro and calla lilies. The plant features arrowhead-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary practices. Its flowering structure consists of a spadix surrounded by a spathe, a characteristic common in the Araceae family.

The tubers of Lampsana edulis are edible and have a starchy, slightly sweet flavor.
