Limonium africanum

Limonium africanum, commonly known as African sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, showy blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in fine hairs.

Limonium africanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy and drought-resistant nature.
