Limonium dufourii

Limonium dufourii, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking, spiky blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, sandy soils, and saline environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of salt.

Limonium dufourii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
