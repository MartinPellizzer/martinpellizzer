Lithocardium ochnaceum

Lithocardium ochnaceum, commonly known as the rock caryota, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the family Ochnaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and dense, leathery leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its small, clustered flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, followed by small, round fruits.

Lithocardium ochnaceum is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
