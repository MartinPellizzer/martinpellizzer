Lesquerella mendocina f. nana

Lesquerella mendocina f. nana, commonly known as the dwarf lesquerella, is a compact, low-growing plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces distinctive seed pods that contain oily seeds used in industrial applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and economic purposes.
