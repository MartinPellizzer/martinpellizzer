Luzula campestris subsp. multiflora

Luzula campestris subsp.

multiflora, commonly known as the field woodrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features dense, grass-like foliage with narrow, dark green leaves arranged in a rosette. It produces small, inconspicuous white or pale green flowers in clusters during the spring or early summer.

The subspecies multiflora is distinguished by its numerous flower clusters, which contribute to its ornamental value in gardens.
