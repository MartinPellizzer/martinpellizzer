Lagotis decumbens

Lagotis decumbens, commonly known as the creeping bellflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It features creeping stems that root at the nodes, forming dense mats of foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. Its leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, adapting to arid environments.

Lagotis decumbens is often found in rocky soils and dry habitats, showcasing its resilience to harsh conditions.
