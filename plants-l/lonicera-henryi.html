Lonicera henryi

Lonicera henryi, commonly known as the Chinese honeysuckle, is a deciduous climbing shrub native to China.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange-red. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to climb using twining stems. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Lonicera henryi is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
