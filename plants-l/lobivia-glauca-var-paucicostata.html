Lobivia glauca var. paucicostata

Lobivia glauca var. paucicostata, commonly known as the Desert Rose, is a succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It features a compact, branching structure with thick, fleshy stems that store water, typical of desert plants. The leaves are usually small, rounded, and often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, appearing in clusters and blooming during the warmer months.

This variety is prized for its resilience and ornamental value in dry climates.
