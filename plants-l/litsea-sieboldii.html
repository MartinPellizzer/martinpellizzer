Litsea sieboldii

Litsea sieboldii, commonly known as Japanese wax tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, elliptical leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by round, woody fruits containing single seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Litsea sieboldii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
