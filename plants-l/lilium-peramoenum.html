Lilium peramoenum

Lilium peramoenum, commonly known as the Peramoenum lily, is a species of flowering plant in the Liliaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and China. This perennial herb typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring strap-like leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or red. The plant produces berries that contain seeds, which are dispersed by birds.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been cultivated for its striking floral display.
