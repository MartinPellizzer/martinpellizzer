Lilium auratum var. rubrovittatum

Lilium auratum var. rubrovittatum, commonly known as the red-striped lily, is a striking variant of the tiger lily.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems that bear large, showy flowers with bright orange petals and distinctive red streaks. The plant is native to eastern Asia and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. It is a perennial herb that produces bulbous roots and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers typically bloom in late summer, adding vibrant color to gardens and natural landscapes.
