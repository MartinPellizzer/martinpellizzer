Lapiedra gracilis

Lapiedra gracilis, commonly known as the slender rock plant, is a succulent species native to arid regions of South America.

It features a compact, low-growing structure with fleshy, narrow leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the warmer months. Its root system is shallow and adapted to anchor it in rocky or sandy soils.

Lapiedra gracilis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
