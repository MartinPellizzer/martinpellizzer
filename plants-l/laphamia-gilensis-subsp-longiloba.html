Laphamia gilensis subsp. longiloba

Laphamia gilensis subsp.

longiloba, commonly known as the long-stemmed laphamia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its succulent, cylindrical stems that store water, typical of arid environments.

The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pale green to yellow in color. It has a rosette growth habit, with leaves arranged in a tight, circular pattern around the stem.

This subspecies is adapted to rocky soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in dry, warm climates.
