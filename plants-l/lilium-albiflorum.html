Lilium albiflorum

Lilium albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features tall, slender stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, supporting large, showy white flowers with a subtle fragrance. The plant's leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its elegant appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a striking visual element to the landscape.
