Lepanthes schlechteriana

Lepanthes schlechteriana, commonly known as the Schlechter's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, such as cloud forests, where it thrives in the understory. Its flowers are usually small to medium in size, with intricate patterns and a distinctive structure that facilitates pollination by specific insect species.

Lepanthes schlechteriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
