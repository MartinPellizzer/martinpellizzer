Loranthus atkinsonae

Loranthus atkinsonae, commonly known as the Atkinson's mistletoe, is a parasitic plant belonging to the Loranthaceae family.

It grows on the branches of host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has small, leathery leaves and produces white or yellow flowers that attract insects for pollination. Its fruit is a fleshy capsule that disperses seeds through bird droppings.

This species plays an important ecological role by providing food for various wildlife species.
