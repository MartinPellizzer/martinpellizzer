Lunathyrium wilsonii var. incisoserratum

Lunathyrium wilsonii var. incisoserratum, commonly known as the Wilson's moonflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, white, moon-shaped flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with finely serrated edges, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological role in supporting nighttime pollinators.
