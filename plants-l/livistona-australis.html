Livistona australis

Livistona australis, commonly known as the Australian palm, is a species of palm native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, black, berry-like fruits. It thrives in coastal and subtropical regions, often growing in sandy or well-drained soils.

Livistona australis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
