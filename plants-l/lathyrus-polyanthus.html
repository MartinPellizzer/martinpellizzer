Lathyrus polyanthus

Lathyrus polyanthus, commonly known as the sweet pea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and delicate, fragrant flowers that come in a wide range of colors. The plant produces tendrils that allow it to climb and support itself on structures. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, heart-shaped leaflets.

Lathyrus polyanthus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
