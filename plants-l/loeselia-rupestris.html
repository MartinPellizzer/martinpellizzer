Loeselia rupestris

Loeselia rupestris, commonly known as the rock lily, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing form and fleshy, bluish-green leaves that are adapted to store water. The plant produces delicate, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for gardeners in dry climates.
