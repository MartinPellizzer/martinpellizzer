Lobelia douglasiana

Lobelia douglasiana, commonly known as Douglas's lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem.

Lobelia douglasiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
