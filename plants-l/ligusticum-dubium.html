Ligusticum dubium

Ligusticum dubium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved angelica, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots, parsley, and celery, and is characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbel-shaped flowers. Its roots are often harvested for their medicinal properties, containing compounds such as volatile oils and sesquiterpenes.

Ligusticum dubium is also valued in traditional herbal medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and digestive benefits.
