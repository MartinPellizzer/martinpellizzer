Leucanthemum coronopifolium subsp. coronopifolium

Leucanthemum coronopifolium subsp.

coronopifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leafed oxeye daisy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States. It features showy white ray flowers with a yellow center, surrounded by basal, narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with a fibrous root system and upright, branching stems. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

This species is adapted to moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and open fields.
