Lycopodium malacophyllum

Lycopodium malacophyllum, commonly known as the Malacophyllum clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant features a creeping stem with small, scale-like leaves that are tightly clustered around the stem. Lycopodium malacophyllum produces sporophylls that bear microsporangia and megasporangia, facilitating the reproduction of spores.

This ancient lineage of plants plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
