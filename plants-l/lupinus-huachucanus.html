Lupinus huachucanus

Lupinus huachucanus, commonly known as the Huachuca lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval to lance-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Lupinus huachucanus is a member of the legume family and has the ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
