Lepanthes whittenii

Lepanthes whittenii, commonly known as the Whitten's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its flowers are usually small and come in a variety of colors, including white, yellow, and pink.

Lepanthes whittenii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
