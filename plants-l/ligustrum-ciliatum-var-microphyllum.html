Ligustrum ciliatum var. microphyllum

Ligustrum ciliatum var. microphyllum, commonly known as the small-leaved privet, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically less than 1 inch in length, giving it its varietal name "microphyllum.". The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are followed by dark blue berries. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or swampy areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
