Liatris quadriflora

Liatris quadriflora, commonly known as the four-flowered bluetail, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features tall, upright stems that bear clusters of blue to purple spike-like flowers, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, arranged in a basal rosette.

Liatris quadriflora is often found in moist, open habitats such as prairies and wetlands.
