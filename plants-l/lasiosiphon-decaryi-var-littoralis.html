Lasiosiphon decaryi var. littoralis

Lasiosiphon decaryi var. littoralis, commonly known as the coastal lasiosiphon, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in maritime climates with high humidity and salt spray. Its leaves are opposite and oval-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface.

This variant is adapted to coastal environments, making it a resilient and hardy species.
