Lupinus albus subsp. graecus

Lupinus albus subsp.

graecus, commonly known as Greek white lupin, is a perennial legume native to southern Europe and parts of the Mediterranean. It is characterized by its erect growth habit and clusters of white or pale pink flowers.

The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces pods containing several seeds. It is adapted to well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability and potential use in sustainable agriculture.
