Lissochilus heteroglossus

Lissochilus heteroglossus, commonly known as the Andean sun orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, sun-like flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or alpine regions. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, adapted to the harsh conditions of its habitat.

The orchid produces small, delicate flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
