Lupinus hartwegii var. albus

Lupinus hartwegii var. albus, commonly known as the white lupine, is a perennial legume native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its white to pale blue flowers and palmate leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showing adaptability to harsh conditions. It has small, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in forage and soil enrichment.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological restoration and sustainable agriculture.
