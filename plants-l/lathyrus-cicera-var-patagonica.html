Lathyrus cicera var. patagonica

Lathyrus cicera var. patagonica, commonly known as the Patagonian vetch, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. Its flowers are usually blue or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for livestock.
