Lampranthus tanquanus

Lampranthus tanquanus, commonly known as the Crimson Carpet, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family, commonly referred to as the ice plant family, and is characterized by its fleshy, water-storing leaves. The plant produces vibrant red or orange flowers during the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
