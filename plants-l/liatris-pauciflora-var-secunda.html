Liatris pauciflora var. secunda

Liatris pauciflora var. secunda, commonly known as the slender blazing star, is a herbaceous perennial native to the central and eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and clusters of showy, lavender-pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges. Its narrow leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, while its inflorescence consists of numerous small, tubular florets.

This variety is noted for its ecological value, providing nectar for pollinators and seeds for birds.
