Leonia crassicarpa

Leonia crassicarpa, commonly known as the thick-fruited leonia, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant produces large, fleshy fruits that are typically red or orange when mature, containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and savanna environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it requires full sun and moderate watering.
