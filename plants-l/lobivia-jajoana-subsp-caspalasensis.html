Lobivia jajoana subsp. caspalasensis

Lobivia jajoana subsp.

caspalasensis, commonly known as the Caspalasensis Lobivia, is a compact, low-growing succulent native to the arid regions of northern Chile. It features a short, cylindrical stem with thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a bluish tinge.

The plant produces tall, branching stems that bear clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers during the spring and early summer. It is well adapted to drought and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and resilience make it a popular plant among succulent enthusiasts.
