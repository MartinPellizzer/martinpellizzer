Lycopodium billardierei

Lycopodium billardierei, commonly known as the narrow-leaf clubmoss, is a member of the Lycopodiaceae family and is native to Australia.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. The plant features whorled leaves that are narrow and linear, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces spore-bearing cones at the tips of its stems, which are essential for its reproductive cycle.

Lycopodium billardierei plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms in its ecosystem.
