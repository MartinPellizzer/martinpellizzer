Lycianthes holocalyx

Lycianthes holocalyx, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to Central America and is characterized by its showy, yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with hairy stems and alternate leaves. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, black seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its bright, cheerful blossoms.
