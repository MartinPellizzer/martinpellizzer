Lobivia pentlandii

Lobivia pentlandii, commonly known as the Pentland's lobivia, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and spiny stems. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. Its leaves are usually reduced to spines, which help it conserve water in its harsh environment.

Lobivia pentlandii is well adapted to extreme conditions, making it a resilient and fascinating species for botanical study.
