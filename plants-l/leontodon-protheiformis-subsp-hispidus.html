Leontodon protheiformis subsp. hispidus

Leontodon protheiformis subsp.

hispidus, commonly known as the hairy leontodon, is a perennial grass-like plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and hairy foliage.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows. Its inflorescence consists of a single, elongated flower head with yellow disc flowers surrounded by pale green ray florets.

It is often found in temperate regions and is noted for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.
