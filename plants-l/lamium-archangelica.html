Lamium archangelica

Lamium archangelica, commonly known as greater witchhound, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with a hairy texture and emits a strong, mint-like aroma. The plant produces clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers that are typically pink to purple in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Lamium archangelica is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
