Limonia trifolia

Limonia trifolia, commonly known as the Chinese hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring woody stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom throughout the summer. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.

Limonia trifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
