Lampranthus leipoldtii

Lampranthus leipoldtii, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a striking succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features vibrant red or orange tubular flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like birds and insects. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for gardeners in Mediterranean climates.
