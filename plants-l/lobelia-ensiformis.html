Lobelia ensiformis

Lobelia ensiformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers. This species typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is often found in wetland habitats.

Lobelia ensiformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
