Lonicera trichosantha

Lonicera trichosantha, commonly known as the hairy honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in the spring, typically yellow or white, which attract pollinators. Its berries are red or orange and are edible for birds and some mammals.

Lonicera trichosantha is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
