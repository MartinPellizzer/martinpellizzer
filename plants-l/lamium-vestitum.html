Lamium vestitum

Lamium vestitum, commonly known as the hairy deadnettle, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are covered in fine, hairy glands, giving the plant its common name. The flowers are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stems, and bloom from spring to early summer. It thrives in shady, moist environments such as woodlands and gardens.

The plant is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported soothing properties.
