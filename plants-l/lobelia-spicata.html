Lobelia spicata

Lobelia spicata, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its spikes of small, sky-blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and woodland edges.

Lobelia spicata is valued for its ornamental beauty and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
