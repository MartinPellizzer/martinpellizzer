Licuala glabra var. selangorensis

Licuala glabra var. selangorensis, commonly known as the Selangorensis palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia, and thrives in tropical rainforest environments. The plant is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves that are smooth and glabrous, giving it its scientific name. It produces small, yellowish-pink flowers that develop into edible, fibrous fruits.

This palm is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local communities.
