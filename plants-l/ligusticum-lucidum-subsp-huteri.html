Ligusticum lucidum subsp. huteri

Ligusticum lucidum subsp.

huteri, commonly known as the Chinese lovage, is a perennial herb native to China. It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescences.

The plant features slender, hollow stems and lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive pungent odor. Its flowers are small, white to pale yellow, and arranged in compound umbels.

It is often used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
