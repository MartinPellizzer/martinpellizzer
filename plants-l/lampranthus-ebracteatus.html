Lampranthus ebracteatus

Lampranthus ebracteatus, commonly known as the firecracker flower, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange, often resembling tiny firecrackers. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. It produces its flowers in late winter to early spring, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

The species is known for its ability to go dormant during dry periods, resuming growth with the onset of rain.
