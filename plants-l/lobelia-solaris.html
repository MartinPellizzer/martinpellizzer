Lobelia solaris

Lobelia solaris, commonly known as the sun lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sunlight. It is a member of the Campanulaceae family, which includes other familiar genera like Bellis and Lobelia.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
