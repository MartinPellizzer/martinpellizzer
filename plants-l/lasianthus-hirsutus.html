Lasianthus hirsutus

Lasianthus hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
