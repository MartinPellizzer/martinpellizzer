Litsea papillosa

Litsea papillosa, commonly known as the silky litsea, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and avocados. The plant is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark and fragrant, glossy leaves that are often covered in fine, silvery hairs. Its flowers are small and white, typically appearing in clusters, and it produces small, round berries that are edible.

Litsea papillosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
