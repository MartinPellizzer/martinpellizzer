Lobelia atrosanguinea

Lobelia atrosanguinea, commonly known as black blood lobelia, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features dark green, heart-shaped leaves and produces striking blue to purple tubular flowers. It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
