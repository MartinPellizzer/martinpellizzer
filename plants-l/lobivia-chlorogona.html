Lobivia chlorogona

Lobivia chlorogona, commonly known as the green-flowered lobivia, is a species of cactus native to the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in dense, white spines. The plant produces small, vibrant green flowers that bloom during the cooler months. It thrives in arid conditions with minimal rainfall and well-draining soil.

Lobivia chlorogona is a popular choice for rock gardens due to its hardiness and unique appearance.
