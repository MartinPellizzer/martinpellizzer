Lupinus subalpinus

Lupinus subalpinus, commonly known as the alpine lupine, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant produces spiny, upright stems that bear clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Lupinus subalpinus plays an important role in stabilizing soils in high-altitude ecosystems and is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
