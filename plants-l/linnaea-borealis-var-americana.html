Linnaea borealis var. americana

Linnaea borealis var. americana, commonly known as the American twinflower, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to North America.

It features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in pairs, typically in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, oval-shaped leaves with a waxy texture and a bluish-green hue. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded forest understories.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
