Lysimachia christinae var. pubescens

Lysimachia christinae var. pubescens, commonly known as hairy yellow loosestrife, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, creeping stems covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it its varietal name. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
