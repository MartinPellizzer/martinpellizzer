Lachnaea burchellii var. latifolia

Lachnaea burchellii var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved lachnaea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local flora and a subject of interest in ecological studies.
