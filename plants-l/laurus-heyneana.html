Laurus heyneana

Laurus heyneana, commonly known as the Brazilian laurel, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant bark. The plant typically grows in humid, tropical climates and can reach heights of up to 20 meters. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their aromatic and medicinal properties.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
