Lonchocarpus oliganthus

Lonchocarpus oliganthus, commonly known as the red root or sarsaparilla, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant produces distinctive, curved pods that contain several seeds. It is known for its deep red roots, which are often used in traditional medicine.

The species is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in ecological restoration projects.
