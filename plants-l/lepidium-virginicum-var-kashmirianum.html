Lepidium virginicum var. kashmirianum

Lepidium virginicum var. kashmirianum, commonly known as Kashmir cress, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats and has hairy, lobed leaves. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by elongated seed pods.

This variant is distinguished by its geographic distribution and morphological traits compared to the typical Lepidium virginicum.
