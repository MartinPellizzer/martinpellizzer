Lonicera anisotricha

Lonicera anisotricha, commonly known as the rough honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and can grow up to 6 feet tall. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its berries are orange-red and contain seeds, which are dispersed by birds.

Lonicera anisotricha is often found in moist woodlands and along streambanks.
