Lasiandra cordiformis

Lasiandra cordiformis, commonly known as the heart-shaped mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in wetlands. It is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to thrive in waterlogged conditions.

Lasiandra cordiformis is an important plant for pollinators and contributes to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
