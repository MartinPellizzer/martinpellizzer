Leontodon hirtus var. humilis

Leontodon hirtus var. humilis, commonly known as the hairy leontodon, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the dandelion family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosette of leaves. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered with fine, stiff hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
