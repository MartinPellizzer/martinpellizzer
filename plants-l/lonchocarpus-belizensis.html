Lonchocarpus belizensis

Lonchocarpus belizensis, commonly known as the Belize legume, is a flowering plant native to Central America, particularly Belize and adjacent regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes characterized by their pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters, with compound leaves and showy, violet to purple flowers. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated, woody pods, and the plant is often found in tropical dry forests and savannas.

Due to its ecological role and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
