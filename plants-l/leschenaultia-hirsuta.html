Leschenaultia hirsuta

Leschenaultia hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy leschenaultia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Leschenaultia hirsuta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
