Lilac vulgaris

Lilac vulgaris, commonly known as common lilac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, clustered flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, typically blooming in the spring. The plant has simple, opposite, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the autumn. Its bark is smooth and gray, becoming fissured with age.

Lilac vulgaris is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
