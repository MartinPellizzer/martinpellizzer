Luzula spicata var. petraea

Luzula spicata var. petraea, commonly known as the rock rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to rocky and alpine environments.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces spikes of small, dark blue to violet flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy species suitable for rock gardens.

Its ability to tolerate harsh conditions makes it a valuable addition to horticultural landscapes in cooler climates.
