Lithops glaudinae

Lithops glaudinae, commonly known as the Cape Town houseling, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, stone-like leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in arid environments. The plant produces tall, branched stems with small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange. Lithops glaudinae typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and requires minimal water.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts.
