Litsea cupularis

Litsea cupularis, commonly known as the fragrant flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by yellowish-orange fruits resembling capsules. Litsea cupularis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its aromatic properties make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm climates.
