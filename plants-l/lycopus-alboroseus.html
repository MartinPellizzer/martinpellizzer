Lycopus alboroseus

Lycopus alboroseus, commonly known as white hedge hyssop, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a member of the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in whorls along the stems. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded areas near streams or in wet meadows.

Lycopus alboroseus is also known for its aromatic foliage and has been used historically in herbal medicine.
