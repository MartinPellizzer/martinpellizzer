Lablab uncinatus

Lablab uncinatus, commonly known as the hairy lablab, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces small, yellow to orange flowers. The plant bears pods that contain edible, kidney-shaped seeds, which are rich in protein. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm climate for optimal growth.

Lablab uncinatus is often cultivated for its nutritious seeds and as a forage crop.
