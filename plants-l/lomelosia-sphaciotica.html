Lomelosia sphaciotica

Lomelosia sphaciotica, commonly known as the Mexican fireweed, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats and is known for its upright, branched stems. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Lomelosia sphaciotica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has cultural significance in some indigenous communities.
