Lycopodioides breynii

Lycopodioides breynii, commonly known as the African clubmoss, is a small evergreen plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, densely branched structure and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces spore-bearing cones, which are typically clustered at the tips of its branches. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Lycopodioides breynii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for microorganisms in its native ecosystems.
