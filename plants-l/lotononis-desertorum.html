Lotononis desertorum

Lotononis desertorum, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically clustered in dense heads, followed by fluffy, white seed heads that aid in wind dispersal. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, adapted to conserve water in dry environments.

Lotononis desertorum is often found in sandy soils and is an important component of desert ecosystems.
