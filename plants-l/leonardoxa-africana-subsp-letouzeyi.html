Leonardoxa africana subsp. letouzeyi

Leonardoxa africana subsp.

letouzeyi, commonly known as the African sycamore, is a member of the Fabaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa. It is a large, deciduous tree that can grow up to 30 meters in height, characterized by its smooth, grey bark and alternate, pinnate leaves. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemose inflorescences, followed by legume pods containing flat, winged seeds.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas, contributing to local biodiversity.
