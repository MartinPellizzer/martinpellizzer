Lathyrus erythrinus

Lathyrus erythrinus, commonly known as the red vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical pods that contain several oblong seeds. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Lathyrus erythrinus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and blooms.
