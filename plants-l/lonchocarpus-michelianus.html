Lonchocarpus michelianus

Lonchocarpus michelianus, commonly known as the red root or bloodroot, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its striking red roots and yellow flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and traditional uses, it is cultivated in some regions for horticultural purposes.
