Lathyrus cicera var. genuinus

Lathyrus cicera var. genuinus, commonly known as the Armenian vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is a perennial legume characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. Its seeds are typically small and oblong, often used in traditional medicine and as a food source.

Lathyrus cicera var. genuinus is adapted to grow in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its resilience in challenging conditions.
