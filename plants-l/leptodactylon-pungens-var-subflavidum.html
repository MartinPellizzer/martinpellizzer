Leptodactylon pungens var. subflavidum

Leptodactylon pungens var. subflavidum, commonly known as the yellow-flowered clubmoss, is a variant of the clubmoss species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and produces sporophytes that bear spores for reproduction. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or rocky crevices. Its leaves are often a vibrant yellow or green, and the plant can form dense mats or low shrubs.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in humid ecosystems.
