Lindera racemiflora

Lindera racemiflora, commonly known as sweet pepperbush, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that appear in clusters, typically in early spring. Its leaves are elliptical, smooth, and have a slightly aromatic scent when crushed.

Lindera racemiflora is often found in moist, woodlands and thrives in partial to full shade.
