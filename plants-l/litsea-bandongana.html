Litsea bandongana

Litsea bandongana, commonly known as the Java citrus or wild lime, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces yellow or green berries that contain a high concentration of citric acid, making them useful in traditional medicine and culinary applications. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
