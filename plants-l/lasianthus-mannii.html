Lasianthus mannii

Lasianthus mannii, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its durability and strength. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in spikes, which are followed by capsule fruits.

Lasianthus mannii is often found in lowland and hill forests, thriving in humid, shaded environments.
