Larix sudetica

Larix sudetica, commonly known as the Sudetic larch, is a species of larch native to Central Europe.

It is a deciduous conifer that sheds its needle-like leaves in the fall. The tree typically grows in mountainous regions and is adapted to cool, temperate climates. Its cones are small and woody, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

The bark is thick and fissured, providing protection against environmental stressors.
