Lindernia veronicifolia

Lindernia veronicifolia, commonly known as the common lindernia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive reddish or purple underside. The plant produces spikes of small, tubular flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom from summer to fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or wetlands.

Lindernia veronicifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
