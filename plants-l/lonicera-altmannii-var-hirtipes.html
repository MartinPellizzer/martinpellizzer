Lonicera altmannii var. hirtipes

Lonicera altmannii var. hirtipes, commonly known as the hairy honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, typically in shades of yellow and white. The plant has opposite, simple, and hairy leaves that are elliptical in shape. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
