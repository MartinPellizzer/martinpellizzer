Lomaria lenormandii

Lomaria lenormandii, commonly known as the golden star cactus, is a flowering plant native to the deserts of northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and thrives in full sun, exhibiting drought tolerance. Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for succulent gardens and xeriscaping.

Lomaria lenormandii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
