Lythrum subhastatum

Lythrum subhastatum, commonly known as purple loosestrife, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy spikes of violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in moist environments such as wetlands, ditches, and riverbanks.

Its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes make it an invasive species in many regions outside its native range.
