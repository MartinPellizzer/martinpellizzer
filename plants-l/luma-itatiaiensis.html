Luma itatiaiensis

Luma itatiaiensis, commonly known as the golden trumpet or itatiaia, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 6 meters in height, characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by animals.

Luma itatiaiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
