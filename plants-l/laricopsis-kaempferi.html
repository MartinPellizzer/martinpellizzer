Laricopsis kaempferi

Laricopsis kaempferi, commonly known as the Japanese larch, is a deciduous conifer native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Pinaceae and is characterized by its needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions and thrives in cool, moist climates. Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in construction and furniture-making.

The species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
