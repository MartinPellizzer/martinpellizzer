Lampranthus watermeyeri

Lampranthus watermeyeri, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features striking, torch-like clusters of bright red or orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has fleshy, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that help it store water. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its vibrant flowers attract pollinators like bees and birds, adding both beauty and ecological value to gardens.
