Lycopodium setaceum var. brasiliense

Lycopodium setaceum var. brasiliense, commonly known as the Brazilian clubmoss, is a small evergreen fern-like plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae and is characterized by its compact, moss-like appearance with dense clusters of spore-producing strobila. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its leaves are needle-like and arranged in whorls around the stem, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Lycopodium setaceum var. brasiliense plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and small invertebrates.
