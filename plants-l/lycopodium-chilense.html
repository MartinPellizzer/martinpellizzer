Lycopodium chilense

Lycopodium chilense, commonly known as the Chile clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae and is characterized by its small, needle-like leaves and a creeping growth habit. The plant produces sporophylls that bear spores, which are released into the air for dispersal. Lycopodium chilense thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Its ancient lineage dates back to the Paleozoic era, making it a living fossil with significant botanical interest.
