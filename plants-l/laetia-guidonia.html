Laetia guidonia

Laetia guidonia, commonly known as the yellow bell.

It is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands, characterized by its striking yellow tubular flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Laetia guidonia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
