Linum annotinum

Linum annotinum, commonly known as the common flax.

It is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe, Western Asia, and North Africa. The plant features slender, upright stems with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and have a rough texture. Its most distinctive feature is the blue to purple flowers, which are typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

The plant is known for its fibrous seeds, which have been historically used for fiber production and oil extraction.
