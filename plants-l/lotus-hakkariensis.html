Lotus hakkariensis

Lotus hakkariensis, commonly known as the Hakkari lotus, is a rare aquatic plant native to the Hakkari region of Turkey.

It is characterized by its striking pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in shallow, freshwater lakes and wetlands, where it thrives in sunny conditions. Its leaves are broad and floating, while its stems are long and flexible.

Lotus hakkariensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and has cultural significance in local traditions.
