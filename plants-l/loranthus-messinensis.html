Loranthus messinensis

Loranthus messinensis, commonly known as the Messina mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that grows on various host trees, primarily in the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Santalaceae family and is characterized by its hemiparasitic nature, meaning it derives some of its nutrients from the host while also performing photosynthesis. The plant has small, scale-like leaves and produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are often clustered together. Its berries are typically red or orange, containing seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, contributing to biodiversity and supporting various wildlife species.
