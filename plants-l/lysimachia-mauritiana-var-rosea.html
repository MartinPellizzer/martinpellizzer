Lysimachia mauritiana var. rosea

Lysimachia mauritiana var. rosea, commonly known as the pink mossy grass, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of pink or white. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are often covered in a fine, moss-like coating, which contributes to its common name.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
