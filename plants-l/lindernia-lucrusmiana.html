Lindernia lucrusmiana

Lindernia lucrusmiana, commonly known as the golden lindernia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces small, oval leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Lindernia lucrusmiana is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
