Leucas pechuelii

Leucas pechuelii, commonly known as the yellow leucas, is a flowering plant native to the eastern regions of Australia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open woodland and grassland habitats. The plant features slender, erect stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Leucas pechuelii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
