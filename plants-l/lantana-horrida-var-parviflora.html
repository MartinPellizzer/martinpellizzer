Lantana horrida var. parviflora

Lantana horrida var. parviflora, commonly known as the smallflowered lantana, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, brightly colored flowers that range from yellow to orange and red. The plant typically grows as a shrub or subshrub, reaching heights of up to 3 feet. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a rough texture.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a common sight in coastal and sandy environments.
