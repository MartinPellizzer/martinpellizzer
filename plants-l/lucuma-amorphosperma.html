Lucuma amorphosperma

Lucuma amorphosperma, commonly known as the golden fruit or lucuma, is a species native to the Andean region of South America.

It is a member of the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its large, yellow-orange, egg-shaped fruits. The fruit has a dense, starchy pulp that is rich in nutrients such as carbohydrates, fiber, and minerals. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters.

It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soils.
