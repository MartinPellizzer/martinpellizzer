Lupinaster baicalensis

Lupinaster baicalensis, commonly known as the Baikal lupin, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces showy, butterfly-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet, typically blooming in late summer. Lupinaster baicalensis is known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
