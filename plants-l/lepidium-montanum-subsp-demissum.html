Lepidium montanum subsp. demissum

Lepidium montanum subsp.

demissum, commonly known as the alpine cress, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of the northern hemisphere. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many economically important crops and weeds.

The plant is characterized by its small, white or pale yellow flowers and pinnately divided leaves. It typically grows in rocky or alpine soils, thriving in cool, moist environments.

Due to its habitat, it is often found in mountainous areas of Europe, Asia, and North America.
