Liparis brachyglottis

Liparis brachyglottis, commonly known as the broad-lipped orchid, is a terrestrial orchid belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions such as Indonesia, Malaysia, and the Philippines. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments, often on rocky or humus-rich substrates. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, with a distinctive broad lip that sets it apart from other orchid species.

The plant's morphology is adapted to its specific ecological niche, with specialized roots that help it absorb nutrients from the substrate.
