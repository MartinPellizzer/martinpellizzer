Leucocoryne foetida

Leucocoryne foetida, commonly known as the stinking onion, is a bulbous plant native to South America.

It produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a strong, garlic-like odor, which is particularly noticeable when the flowers open. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Leucocoryne foetida is often cultivated for its ornamental flowers and unique fragrance.
