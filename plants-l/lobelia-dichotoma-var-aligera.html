Lobelia dichotoma var. aligera

Lobelia dichotoma var. aligera, commonly known as the alpine lobelia, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial with slender, branching stems that often grow in rocky or alpine environments. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and hairy, adapted to withstand cold and windy conditions.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging climates.
