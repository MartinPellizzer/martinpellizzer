Lonchocarpus tenorioi

Lonchocarpus tenorioi, commonly known as the Hawaiian climbing legume, is a tropical plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its climbing growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces distinctive yellow flowers followed by pod fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in coastal and lowland habitats, thriving in sandy soils.

Due to its ecological significance, it plays a role in local biodiversity and traditional practices.
