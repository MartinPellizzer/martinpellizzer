Linaria pyramidalis subsp. lenkoranica

Linaria pyramidalis subsp.

lenkoranica, commonly known as the Lenkoran wallflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Caucasus region. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and profuse display of yellow-orange flowers.

The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and ecological role in its native habitat.
