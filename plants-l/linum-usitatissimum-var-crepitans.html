Linum usitatissimum var. crepitans

Linum usitatissimum var. crepitans, commonly known as the crackling flax, is a variant of the common flax plant.

It is characterized by its distinctive, crackling seed pods that emit a sound when touched, a feature that sets it apart from other flax varieties. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soil. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are blue or purple.

This variety is primarily cultivated for its seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil.
