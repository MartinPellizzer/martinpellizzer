Linaria bicornis

Linaria bicornis, commonly known as the two-horned toad lily, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The flowers are typically yellow or orange with two prominent lobes that resemble horns, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
