Leucospermum conocarpodendron subsp. conocarpodendron

Leucospermum conocarpodendron subsp.

conocarpodendron, commonly known as the king protea, is a striking member of the Proteaceae family native to South Africa. It is characterized by its large, showy, red flowers with prominent white stamens that resemble a crown, giving it its common name.

The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are leathery and glossy, often with a bluish-green hue, and it thrives in well-drained, acidic soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its dramatic floral display.
