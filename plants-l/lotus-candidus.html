Lotus candidus

Lotus candidus, commonly known as the white lotus, is a striking aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features showy, white flowers that bloom in the early morning and close by midday. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the surface of still water. Its roots are submerged, anchoring it in muddy or sandy substrates.

Lotus candidus is valued for its ornamental beauty and has cultural significance in various regions.
