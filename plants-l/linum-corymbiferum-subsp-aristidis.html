Linum corymbiferum subsp. aristidis

Linum corymbiferum subsp.

aristidis, commonly known as the purple flax, is a perennial plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves with a soft, fuzzy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a host plant for certain butterfly species and its ornamental value in landscaping.
