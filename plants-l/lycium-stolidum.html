Lycium stolidum

Lycium stolidum, commonly known as Indian wolfberry or nightshade, is a shrub native to the arid regions of India and Pakistan.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshade plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its spiny stems, yellow flowers, and red or orange berries that contain small, hard seeds. Lycium stolidum is adapted to survive in dry climates, with deep roots that help it access groundwater.

Its botanical features make it a hardy plant with potential medicinal uses in traditional systems of medicine.
