Leonotis mollissima var. fulva

Leonotis mollissima var. fulva, commonly known as the golden heliotrope, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves and clusters of tubular, orange-red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun. It is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and birds, contributing to local biodiversity.
