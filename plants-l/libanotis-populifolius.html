Libanotis populifolius

Libanotis populifolius, commonly known as the poplar-leaved bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that resemble those of poplar trees, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Libanotis populifolius is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
