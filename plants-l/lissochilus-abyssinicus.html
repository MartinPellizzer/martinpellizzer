Lissochilus abyssinicus

Lissochilus abyssinicus, commonly known as the Abyssinian orchid, is a rare and delicate species native to the high-altitude regions of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous habitats, where it thrives in cool and moist conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces a single spike of white or pale pink flowers.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
