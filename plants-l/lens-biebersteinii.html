Lens biebersteinii

Lens biebersteinii, commonly known as the desert pea, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive pod-like fruit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces vibrant purple or red flowers, which are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its seeds are enclosed in a leathery pod that splits open when mature, dispersing the seeds through mechanical means.

Lens biebersteinii is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, demonstrating resilience through deep root systems and water conservation strategies.
