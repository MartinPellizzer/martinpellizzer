Launaea crassifolia

Launaea crassifolia, commonly known as the thick-leaved launaea, is a succulent plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in drought conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, adding a subtle ornamental value. Its root system is deep, enabling it to access moisture from lower soil layers.

Launaea crassifolia is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and drought tolerance.
