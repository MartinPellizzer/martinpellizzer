Liparis kappleri

Liparis kappleri, commonly known as the Kappler's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and produces delicate, greenish-yellow flowers with a faint fragrance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and prefers well-drained, humus-rich soil. It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its unique floral structure and subtle coloration.

Liparis kappleri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in the right conditions.
