Lonicera marylandica

Lonicera marylandica, commonly known as the Maryland honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of yellow and orange, which bloom in late spring to early summer. These flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It also bears red berries that are edible for birds and can be used in jams and jellies.
