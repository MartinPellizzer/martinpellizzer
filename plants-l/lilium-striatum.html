Lilium striatum

Lilium striatum, commonly known as the spotted lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white with distinctive red or purple spots. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces red or orange bulbs that store nutrients for future growth. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

Lilium striatum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
