Lupinus cookianus

Lupinus cookianus, commonly known as Cook's lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and clusters of showy blue to purple flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. Lupinus cookianus is adapted to grow in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

It plays an important role in ecological systems by fixing nitrogen into the soil, benefiting surrounding plant life.
