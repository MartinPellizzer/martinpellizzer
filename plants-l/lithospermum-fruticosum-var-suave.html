Lithospermum fruticosum var. suave

Lithospermum fruticosum var. suave, commonly known as sweet lithosperm, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features erect, branched stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lanceolate with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its roots contain alkaloids and are used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rocky or dry environments.
