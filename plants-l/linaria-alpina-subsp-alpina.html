Linaria alpina subsp. alpina

Linaria alpina subsp.

alpina, commonly known as the alpine toadflax, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers in racemes, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. Linaria alpina subsp.

alpina is often found in alpine meadows and is a hardy plant that can tolerate harsh climatic conditions.
