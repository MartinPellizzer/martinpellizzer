Lysipomia gracilis

Lysipomia gracilis, commonly known as the slender tussock grass, is a perennial herb native to the southern Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form compact clumps. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in spikelets, which are a common feature in the grass family. Lysipomia gracilis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of high-altitude ecosystems.
