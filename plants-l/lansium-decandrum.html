Lansium decandrum

Lansium decandrum, commonly known as the kaffir lime, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, glossy, dark green leaves and small, yellow, citrus-like fruits. The plant belongs to the Rutaceae family and is valued for its aromatic zest, which is widely used in cooking and traditional medicine. Its flowers are white and fragrant, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

The tree is also cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to attract pollinators.
