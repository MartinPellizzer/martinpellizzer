Lycopodium distinctissimum

Lycopodium distinctissimum, commonly known as the slender clubmoss, is a species of vascular plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its small, upright, branching stems covered with tiny, scale-like leaves. The plant produces spores in cone-like structures called strobila, which are typically found at the tips of the branches. L. distinctissimum is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
