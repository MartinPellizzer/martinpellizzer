Lepidium parodii f. diviusculum

Lepidium parodii f. diviusculum, commonly known as the dwarf wall rocket, is a compact, herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features small, edible leaves that are often used in culinary applications, particularly in Mediterranean cuisine. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early spring. Its growth habit is low and spreading, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Botanically, it belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its four-petaled flowers and seed pods that split open to release seeds.
