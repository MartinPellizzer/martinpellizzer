Liabum nubigenum

Liabum nubigenum, commonly known as the cloud mist plant, is a unique species native to high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, hair-like leaves that trap moisture from the air, allowing it to thrive in humid, misty environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or pale green, adapted for pollination by wind or specialized insects. Its root system is shallow and fibrous, enabling it to absorb nutrients from the thin soil of its alpine habitat.

Due to its specialized adaptations, Liabum nubigenum is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
