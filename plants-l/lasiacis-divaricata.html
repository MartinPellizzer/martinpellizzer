Lasiacis divaricata

Lasiacis divaricata, commonly known as the silverleaf daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soil. It is a perennial herb that can reach up to 30 cm in height, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it an important plant in local ecosystems.
