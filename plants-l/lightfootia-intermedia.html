Lightfootia intermedia

Lightfootia intermedia, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, velvety leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Lightfootia intermedia is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
