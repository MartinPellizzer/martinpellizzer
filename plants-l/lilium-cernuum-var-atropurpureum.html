Lilium cernuum var. atropurpureum

Lilium cernuum var. atropurpureum, commonly known as the purple-flowered fawn lily, is a variant of the western fawn lily.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States, particularly found in moist meadows and forested areas. The plant features a basal rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces drooping, bell-shaped flowers with a distinctive purple hue. Its blooms typically appear in early spring, adding a striking visual element to its natural habitat.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
