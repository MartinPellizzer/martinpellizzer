Lyonia myrtilloides var. ovalifolia

Lyonia myrtilloides var. ovalifolia, commonly known as the ovalleaf lyonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its botanical features include hairy stems and small, dark blue to black berries that appear in the fall.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
