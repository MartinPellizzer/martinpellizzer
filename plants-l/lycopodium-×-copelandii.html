Lycopodium × copelandii

Lycopodium × copelandii, commonly known as the hybrid clubmoss, is a low-growing evergreen plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae and is characterized by its dense, needle-like foliage and spore-producing cones. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for use in rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Lycopodium × copelandii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to form dense mats that suppress weeds.
