Licuala terengganuensis

Licuala terengganuensis, commonly known as the Terengganu palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves that can grow up to several meters in diameter. The plant typically grows in swampy or wetland areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soil. Its trunk is relatively short and stout, often covered with a fibrous sheath.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential ecological and economic importance in its native habitat.
