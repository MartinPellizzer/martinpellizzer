Lychnis kiusiana

Lychnis kiusiana, commonly known as the Japanese fireweed, is a herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats like meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Lychnis kiusiana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
