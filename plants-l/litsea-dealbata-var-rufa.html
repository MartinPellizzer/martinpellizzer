Litsea dealbata var. rufa

Litsea dealbata var. rufa, commonly known as the red-leafed litsea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Lauraceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and small, fragrant flowers. The leaves of this variety are distinctly red or reddish-purple when mature, contrasting with the green leaves of the typical variety. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests and along stream banks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
