Luzula effusa

Luzula effusa, commonly known as the common wood rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features tufted, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like clusters of white or pale blue flowers, which appear in late spring to early summer.

Luzula effusa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging soil conditions.
