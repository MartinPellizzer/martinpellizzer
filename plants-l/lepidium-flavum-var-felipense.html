Lepidium flavum var. felipense

Lepidium flavum var. felipense, commonly known as the Mexican wallflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with small, four-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. Its yellow flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and the leaves are usually pinnately divided.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
