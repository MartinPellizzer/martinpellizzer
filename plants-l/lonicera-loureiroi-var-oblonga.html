Lonicera loureiroi var. oblonga

Lonicera loureiroi var. oblonga, commonly known as the Chinese honeysuckle, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The flowers are typically borne in clusters and are pollinated by insects, particularly bees. The plant bears small, red, fleshy berries that mature in the fall.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
