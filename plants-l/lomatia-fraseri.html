Lomatia fraseri

Lomatia fraseri, commonly known as the mountain devil, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. The plant has distinctive, glandular, and often sticky leaves that help reduce water loss. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and appear in clusters, often in late winter to early spring.

Lomatia fraseri is also known for its ability to form dense thickets, which can be both ecologically significant and potentially invasive in certain areas.
