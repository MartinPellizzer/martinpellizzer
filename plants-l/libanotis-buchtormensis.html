Libanotis buchtormensis

Libanotis buchtormensis, commonly known as the Buchtor Mountains bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Carpathian region.

It is characterized by its delicate blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine environments. It has small, lance-shaped leaves and is part of the Campanulaceae family.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
