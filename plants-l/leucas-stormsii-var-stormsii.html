Leucas stormsii var. stormsii

Leucas stormsii var. stormsii, commonly known as the African bluebottle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and along riverbanks, thriving in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are often woody at the base.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
