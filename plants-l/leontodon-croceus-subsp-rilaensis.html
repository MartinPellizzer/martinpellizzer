Leontodon croceus subsp. rilaensis

Leontodon croceus subsp.

rilaensis, commonly known as the Rila mountain buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the Rila Mountains in Bulgaria. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its bright yellow ray florets and a central disk of tubular flowers.

The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky areas at high elevations. It has a rosette of basal leaves that are hairy and often covered in white or pale pubescence.

This subspecies is notable for its limited distribution and is considered a rare and endemic plant in its native range.
