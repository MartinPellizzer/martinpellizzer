Lonicera pamirica

Lonicera pamirica, commonly known as the Pamir honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and opposite leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are usually yellow or orange in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its fruits are red berries that mature in late summer, containing small seeds.

Lonicera pamirica is often found in rocky or forested areas at high elevations.
