Limonia missionis

Limonia missionis, commonly known as the Madagascar citron, is a small evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, lemon-scented leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are often clustered together. Its fruits are round, leathery, and contain a single seed, resembling a miniature citrus fruit.

Limonia missionis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
