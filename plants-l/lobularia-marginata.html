Lobularia marginata

Lobularia marginata, commonly known as the sweet Alyssum, is a low-growing flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters, often covering the entire plant. The leaves are linear and gray-green, with a slightly fuzzy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
