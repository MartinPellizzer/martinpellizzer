Lepanthes matudana

Lepanthes matudana, commonly known as the Matudana orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant grows as an epiphyte, often found on trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

Lepanthes matudana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
