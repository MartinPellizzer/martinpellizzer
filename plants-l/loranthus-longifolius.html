Loranthus longifolius

Loranthus longifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily in the family Myrtaceae.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own carbohydrates through photosynthesis using the light it captures. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces white or yellowish flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. Its berries are typically red or orange, containing seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This species is found in tropical and subtropical regions, often growing in humid, shaded environments.
