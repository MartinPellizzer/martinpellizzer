Loranthus xestophyllus

Loranthus xestophyllus, commonly known as the mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of host trees, such as oaks and apple trees.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using the light it captures. The plant has small, scale-like leaves and produces white or yellowish berries that contain seeds. It plays an important ecological role by providing food for birds and other animals.

Its parasitic nature and unique reproductive strategy make it an interesting subject in botanical studies.
