Litsea subcoriacea var. stenophylla

Litsea subcoriacea var. stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved litsea, is a variant of the litsea genus native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub characterized by its narrow, elliptical leaves that are often slightly serrated. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by orange-red berries that are an important food source for various birds and mammals. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Its ornamental value and ecological significance make it a notable species in horticulture and conservation efforts.
