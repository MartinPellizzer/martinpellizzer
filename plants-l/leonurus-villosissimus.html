Leonurus villosissimus

Leonurus villosissimus, commonly known as the hairy leonurus, is a flowering plant native to regions of Asia, including China, India, and parts of the Middle East.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a soft, velvety texture. It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale purple or white, arranged in whorls along the stems.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has also been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
