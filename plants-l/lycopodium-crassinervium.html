Lycopodium crassinervium

Lycopodium crassinervium, commonly known as the broad-veined clubmoss, is a species of vascular plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its small, spore-bearing cones and a system of roots and stems that are typically found in moist, shaded environments. The plant exhibits a unique structure with dichotomous branching, where each stem splits into two equal parts. Its leaves are narrow and strap-like, often with prominent veins that contribute to its common name.

Lycopodium crassinervium plays a role in forest ecosystems, contributing to soil stability and providing habitat for various microorganisms.
