Lespedeza macrophylla

Lespedeza macrophylla, commonly known as largeleaf lespedeza, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and trifoliate leaves, which are broad and elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, pink to reddish-purple flowers in dense clusters, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and disturbed areas.

Lespedeza macrophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
