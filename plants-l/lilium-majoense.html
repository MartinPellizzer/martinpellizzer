Lilium majoense

Lilium majoense, commonly known as the Mexican tiger lily, is a striking bulbous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It features tall, sturdy stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, adorned with vibrant orange and red flowers that have a distinctive tiger-like pattern. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped blooms in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a fan-like pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Lilium majoense thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in temperate climates.
