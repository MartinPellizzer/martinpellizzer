Lepanthes loboauriculatus

Lepanthes loboauriculatus, commonly known as the golden-ear orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow petals and sepals, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, tropical environments. Its flowers are small but highly fragrant, attracting various pollinators such as bees and moths.

Lepanthes loboauriculatus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
