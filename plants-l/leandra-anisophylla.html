Leandra anisophylla

Leandra anisophylla, commonly known as the waxy leaf orchid, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, waxy-textured leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with a complex structure typical of orchids. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Leandra anisophylla is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
