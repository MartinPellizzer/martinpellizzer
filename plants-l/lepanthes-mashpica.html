Lepanthes mashpica

Lepanthes mashpica, commonly known as the Mashpi orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the cloud forests of Costa Rica.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that often display vibrant red, orange, and yellow hues. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and requires high humidity and well-drained soil. Its flowers are small but numerous, arranged in a spike that can reach several centimeters in length.

Lepanthes mashpica is a rare and endangered species, making it a subject of interest for botanical conservation efforts.
