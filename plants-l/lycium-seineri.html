Lycium seineri

Lycium seineri, commonly known as the Tibetan wolfberry, is a shrub native to the high-altitude regions of Asia, including Tibet, Nepal, and Bhutan.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshade plants, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and red berries. The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments and is adapted to cold climates. Its berries are edible and have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Lycium seineri is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in stabilizing soil in mountainous regions.
