Lauro-cerasus lusitanica subsp. hixa

Lauro-cerasus lusitanica subsp.

hixa, commonly known as the Iberian cherry laurel, is a shrub or small tree native to the Iberian Peninsula. It belongs to the Prunus genus and is characterized by its glossy evergreen leaves and fragrant white flowers.

The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark red to black in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural reserves.
