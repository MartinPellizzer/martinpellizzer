Leonotis engleri

Leonotis engleri, commonly known as the African heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. It typically grows as a shrub or subshrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters.

The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
