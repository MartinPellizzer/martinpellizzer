Lysimachia caloneura

Lysimachia caloneura, commonly known as the narrow-leaved yellow loosestrife, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in dense, terminal spikes, blooming in early summer.

Lysimachia caloneura is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and flowering habit.
