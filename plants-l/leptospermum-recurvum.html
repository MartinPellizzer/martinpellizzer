Leptospermum recurvum

Leptospermum recurvum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tea tree, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in heathland and coastal environments, characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves contain compounds with antimicrobial properties, contributing to its traditional use in herbal medicine.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
