Linaria saposhnikovii

Linaria saposhnikovii, commonly known as the Siberian toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces vibrant yellow-orange flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Linaria saposhnikovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
