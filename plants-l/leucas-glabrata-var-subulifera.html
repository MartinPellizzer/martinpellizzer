Leucas glabrata var. subulifera

Leucas glabrata var. subulifera, commonly known as the narrow-leaved leucas, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of pale blue to purple flowers. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
