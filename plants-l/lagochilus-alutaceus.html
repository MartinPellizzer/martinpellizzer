Lagochilus alutaceus

Lagochilus alutaceus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rock hyssop, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its small, tubular flowers are usually violet or blue, and they bloom in late spring to early summer.

Lagochilus alutaceus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
