Lonchocarpus fluvialis

Lonchocarpus fluvialis, commonly known as the river senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is known for its striking yellow flowers and is often found near water bodies. Its root system is adapted to aquatic or semi-aquatic environments, allowing it to thrive in moist soils.

The plant has been used traditionally in some regions for its medicinal properties.
