Leucas micrantha

Leucas micrantha, commonly known as small-flowered leucas, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia, particularly India and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and basil. The plant is characterized by its small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters, often giving it a delicate appearance. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Leucas micrantha is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
