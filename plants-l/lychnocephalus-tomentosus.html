Lychnocephalus tomentosus

Lychnocephalus tomentosus, commonly known as the woolly sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It typically grows in dry, open habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
