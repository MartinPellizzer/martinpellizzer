Lepidium sativum var. australe

Lepidium sativum var. australe, commonly known as Australian cress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family and is native to Australia.

It is an annual herb characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, cruciform flowers. The plant produces small, edible pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are typically lobed and have a peppery flavor, similar to other members of the mustard family.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.
