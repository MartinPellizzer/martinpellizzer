Linaria supina var. odoratissima

Linaria supina var. odoratissima, commonly known as the fragrant sandrocket, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial species characterized by its slender, sprawling stems and small, bright yellow flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as sandy soils and rocky areas.

Its strong, pleasant fragrance is most noticeable when the flowers are in full bloom.
