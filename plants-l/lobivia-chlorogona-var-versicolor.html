Lobivia chlorogona var. versicolor

Lobivia chlorogona var. versicolor, commonly known as the Rainbow Lobivia, is a succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It features a compact, branching structure with thick, fleshy stems that store water, typical of desert plants. The leaves are usually blue-green in color, though they can vary in hue depending on environmental conditions. Its flowers are small and typically yellow or orange, appearing in clusters during the growing season.

This variety is prized for its vibrant coloration and adaptability to dry climates.
