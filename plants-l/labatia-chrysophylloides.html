Labatia chrysophylloides

Labatia chrysophylloides, commonly known as the golden-leaf labatia, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that are predominantly yellow or golden in color, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, often blooming during the warmer months. Its foliage is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its vibrant color and unique leaf shape.

Labatia chrysophylloides thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to flourish.
