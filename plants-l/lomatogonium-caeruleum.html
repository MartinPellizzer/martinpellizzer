Lomatogonium caeruleum

Lomatogonium caeruleum, commonly known as blue lomatogonium, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically hairy and lance-shaped, with a grayish-green hue.

Lomatogonium caeruleum thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.
