Lauro-cerasus jenkinsii

Lauro-cerasus jenkinsii, commonly known as the Japanese apricot, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Prunus genus and is characterized by its fragrant, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, yellow or orange fruits that are edible but often tart. Its leaves are dark green and oval-shaped, and it has a dense, compact growth habit.

Lauro-cerasus jenkinsii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
