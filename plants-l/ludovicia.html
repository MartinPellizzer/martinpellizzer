Ludovicia

Ludovicia, commonly known as the sacred lotus, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas and is characterized by its large, showy flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value, with species such as Ludovicia tetragona being particularly popular. Its flowers typically bloom in shades of pink, red, or white, and they emit a sweet fragrance.
