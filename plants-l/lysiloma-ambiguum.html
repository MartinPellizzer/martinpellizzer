Lysiloma ambiguum

Lysiloma ambiguum, commonly known as the ambiguous acacia, is a deciduous shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant's ability to thrive in coastal and dry environments makes it an important component of local ecosystems.
