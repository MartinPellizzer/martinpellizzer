Lathyrus petiolaris

Lathyrus petiolaris, commonly known as the creeping vetch, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and streambanks. The plant features slender, trailing stems with trifoliate leaves composed of three ovate to lanceolate leaflets. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, borne in clusters and arranged in racemes, adding ornamental value to gardens.

Lathyrus petiolaris is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a beneficial plant in ecological restoration projects.
