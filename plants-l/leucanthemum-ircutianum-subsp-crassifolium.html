Leucanthemum ircutianum subsp. crassifolium

Leucanthemum ircutianum subsp.

crassifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf white wood aster, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia. It features a rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of white to pale yellow flowers with prominent yellow centers, blooming from late summer to early autumn. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
