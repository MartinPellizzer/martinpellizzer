Laseguea acutifolia griseo-olivacea

Laseguea acutifolia griseo-olivacea, commonly known as the silver-olive laseguea, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, grayish-green pubescence. The plant produces small, pale green to yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
