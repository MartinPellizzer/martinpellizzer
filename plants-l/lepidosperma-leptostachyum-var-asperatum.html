Lepidosperma leptostachyum var. asperatum

Lepidosperma leptostachyum var. asperatum, commonly known as the rough spike rush, is a perennial wetland plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green to brown in color. It thrives in shallow water or moist soils and is often found in swampy areas or along the edges of water bodies.

Its structure and growth habits make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
