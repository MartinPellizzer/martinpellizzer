Lobivia haematantha var. pectinifera

Lobivia haematantha var. pectinifera, commonly known as the Pectinifera Lobivia, is a compact, slow-growing cactus native to the arid regions of Argentina.

It features a short, thick stem with a distinctive red or pinkish hue, often covered in a layer of white, powdery bloom. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring. Its leaves are modified into spines, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

This variety is prized for its unique coloration and compact growth habit, making it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
