Lavandula spica var. vulgaris

Lavandula spica var. vulgaris, commonly known as English lavender, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, spike-like flower clusters that are typically purple or lilac in color. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves with a fuzzy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight. It is widely cultivated for its fragrant oils, which are used in perfumes, aromatherapy, and medicinal applications.

English lavender is also popular in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
