Leucas eriostoma var. lanata

Leucas eriostoma var. lanata, commonly known as the woolly leucas, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage, which gives it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and unique texture, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
