Lauro-cerasus macrophylla var. oxycarpa

Lauro-cerasus macrophylla var. oxycarpa, commonly known as the Japanese flowering cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, often in shades of pink and white. The plant has elliptical, dark green leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly planted in gardens and public spaces.

The fruit, though edible, is not typically cultivated for consumption.
