Linaria tristis subsp. lurida

Linaria tristis subsp.

lurida, commonly known as the pale toadflax, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in dry, open habitats such as scrublands and coastal areas.

The plant features slender, branching stems and produces showy yellow-orange flowers with two lips, characteristic of the Plantaginaceae family. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
