Lippia riedeliana

Lippia riedeliana, commonly known as the Mexican blue sage, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and showy blue to purple flowers. The plant is known for its strong, spicy scent, which is attributed to the presence of essential oils in its leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Lippia riedeliana is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
