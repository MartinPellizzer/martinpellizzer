Lomatium oreganum

Lomatium oreganum, commonly known as Oregon lomatium, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its fern-like leaves and umbel-shaped flowers.

Native to the Pacific Northwest of North America, it thrives in dry, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are deeply lobed and often have a slightly aromatic scent.

Lomatium oreganum is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally by Indigenous peoples for various health purposes.
