Lamium laevigatum var. glabratum

Lamium laevigatum var. glabratum, commonly known as silver nettle, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features heart-shaped, silver-velvet leaves with a soft, fuzzy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a low-maintenance, decorative plant.
