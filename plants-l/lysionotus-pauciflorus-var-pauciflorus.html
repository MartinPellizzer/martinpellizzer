Lysionotus pauciflorus var. pauciflorus

Lysionotus pauciflorus var. pauciflorus, commonly known as the few-flowered lythrum, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and native plant collections.
