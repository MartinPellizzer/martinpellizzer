Leonotis malacophylla

Leonotis malacophylla, commonly known as the African heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and have a distinctive blue-green color. Its showy, tubular flowers are usually pink to purple and are arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches.

The plant is adapted to dry conditions and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
