Laurus neesiana

Laurus neesiana, commonly known as the narrow-leafed laurel, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like cinnamon and bay laurel. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped, glossy dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters.

Its fruits are small, dark berries that mature in clusters, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
