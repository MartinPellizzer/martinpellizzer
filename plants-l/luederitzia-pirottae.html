Luederitzia pirottae

Luederitzia pirottae, commonly known as the Natal tree, is a deciduous tree native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, followed by flat, pod-like fruits.

Luederitzia pirottae is often found in moist, sandy soils and is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance.
