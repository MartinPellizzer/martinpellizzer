Lupinus sellulus var. ursinus

Lupinus sellulus var. ursinus, commonly known as the California lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of pea-like flowers. The plant has pinnately compound leaves and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions with a Mediterranean climate.

Lupinus sellulus var. ursinus plays an important role in nitrogen fixation and is a valuable plant for ecological restoration projects.
