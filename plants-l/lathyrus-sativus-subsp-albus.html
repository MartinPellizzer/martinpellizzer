Lathyrus sativus subsp. albus

Lathyrus sativus subsp.

albus, commonly known as white field bean, is a flowering plant in the legume family Fabaceae. It is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet being ovate to elliptic in shape.

The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are typically clustered in racemes. Its pods are cylindrical, containing several oval, flat seeds that are used as a food source in some regions.

The species is adapted to temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along field margins.
