Leucadendron pendunculatum

Leucadendron pendunculatum, commonly known as the silver leaf candle bush, is a member of the Proteaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage and upright, candle-like flower heads that range in color from yellow to red. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a desirable ornamental plant in various climates.
