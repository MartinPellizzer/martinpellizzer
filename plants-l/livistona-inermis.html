Livistona inermis

Livistona inermis, commonly known as the slender papyrus palm, is a species of palm native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by orange-red berries that are an important food source for native wildlife. It thrives in wetland and swampy environments, often forming dense stands in still water.

Livistona inermis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its natural habitat.
