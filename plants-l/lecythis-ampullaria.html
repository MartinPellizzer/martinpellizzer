Lecythis ampullaria

Lecythis ampullaria, commonly known as the giant Amazonian palm, is a tropical tree native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its large, buttressed roots and towering height, often reaching over 50 meters. The plant produces large, woody fruits that contain a single, edible seed surrounded by a thick, oily pulp. Its leaves are broad and palmate, providing significant canopy cover in the forest.

The tree is valued for its timber and has ecological significance in supporting diverse wildlife.
