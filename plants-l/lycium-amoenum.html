Lycium amoenum

Lycium amoenum, commonly known as the Chinese boxthorn, is a shrub native to China, Korea, and Japan.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces red berries that are edible when ripe, though they are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits. Lycium amoenum thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
