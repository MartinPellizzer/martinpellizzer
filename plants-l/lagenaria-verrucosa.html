Lagenaria verrucosa

Lagenaria verrucosa, commonly known as the horned bottle gourd, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes cucumbers, pumpkins, and squash. The plant produces large, inflated fruits that can grow up to 3 meters in length, often with a rough, warty surface. The fruit is typically green when unripe and turns yellow or orange when mature.

It is cultivated for its edible young fruits and has traditional uses in various cultures for both food and ornamental purposes.
