Leptochilus nicotianifolius var. simplex

Leptochilus nicotianifolius var. simplex, commonly known as the simple leafy spurge, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves and produces small, yellow flowers in early spring. The plant has a deep taproot system, making it highly resilient and difficult to eradicate. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and weed management.
