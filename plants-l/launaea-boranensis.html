Launaea boranensis

Launaea boranensis, commonly known as the Boran dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the Horn of Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, exhibiting adaptations to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, while the flowers are yellow and borne in clusters.

Launaea boranensis is often found in sandy soils and can thrive in disturbed environments.
