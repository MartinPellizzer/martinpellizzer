Lathyrus grandifolius

Lathyrus grandifolius, commonly known as the great leaf pea, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant features compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets and produces striking violet or purple flowers in late spring. Its fruit is a pod containing several elongated seeds, which are often used in traditional herbal remedies.

Lathyrus grandifolius is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens due to its attractive foliage and blooms.
