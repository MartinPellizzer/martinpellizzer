Leucanthemum sibiricum var. acutilobum

Leucanthemum sibiricum var. acutilobum, commonly known as Siberian tansy, is a perennial herb native to the colder regions of Eurasia.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers with white ray florets, typically blooming in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows or along stream banks.

Its aromatic foliage and showy flowers make it a valuable addition to wetland gardens.
