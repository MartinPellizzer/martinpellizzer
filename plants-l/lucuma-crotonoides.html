Lucuma crotonoides

Lucuma crotonoides, commonly known as the lucuma fruit tree, is a tropical plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, spiny leaves and produces yellow-orange fruits with a sweet, creamy pulp. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its fruit is rich in nutrients, including vitamins and minerals, making it a valuable crop.

The tree is also noted for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage and unique fruit structure.
