Ligularia tabularis

Ligularia tabularis, commonly known as the broadleaf ligularia, is a herbaceous perennial native to East Asia.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers in late summer, which are clustered in dense, terminal inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its foliage is prized for its ornamental value in garden settings.
