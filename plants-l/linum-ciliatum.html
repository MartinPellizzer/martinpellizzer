Linum ciliatum

Linum ciliatum, commonly known as the silky flax or woolly flax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the genus Linum, which includes other flax species, and is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Linum ciliatum is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in fiber production.
