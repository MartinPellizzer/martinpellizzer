Loranthus dregei

Loranthus dregei, commonly known as the African mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily in the genus Acacia.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using light reflected from the host's leaves. The plant forms a dense, bushy growth with small, oval-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into fleshy, red berries. It is often found in dry, arid regions of southern Africa, where it plays a role in the local ecosystem by providing food for birds and insects.

Its parasitic nature and unique adaptations make it an interesting subject in botanical studies.
