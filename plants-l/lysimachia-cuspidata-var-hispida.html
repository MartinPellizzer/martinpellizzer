Lysimachia cuspidata var. hispida

Lysimachia cuspidata var. hispida, commonly known as hairy yellow archangel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems that are hairy, giving it its common name, and it produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers during the summer months. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to spread through rhizomes.
