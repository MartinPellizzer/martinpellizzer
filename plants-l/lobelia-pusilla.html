Lobelia pusilla

Lobelia pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf lobelia, is a small flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of only a few inches, featuring delicate, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments, often found in woodland areas or along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and branching.

Lobelia pusilla is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
