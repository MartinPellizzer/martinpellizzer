Lupinus martensis

Lupinus martensis, commonly known as the Martens lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. Lupinus martensis is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, often found in open habitats such as grasslands and slopes.

Its foliage consists of trifoliate leaves, which are compound structures made up of three leaflets.
