Lepanthes quadrispatulata

Lepanthes quadrispatulata, commonly known as the four-leafed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical rainforests. Its leaves are broad and spatulate, giving it the species name "quadrispatulata," which refers to its four-spade-shaped leaves.

The flowers are usually fragrant and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
