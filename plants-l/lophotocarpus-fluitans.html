Lophotocarpus fluitans

Lophotocarpus fluitans, commonly known as the floating bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to freshwater habitats in tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its small, floating leaves that are often covered in specialized structures called bladder-like organs. These bladders trap and digest small aquatic animals, allowing the plant to obtain nutrients from its nutrient-poor environment. The plant's roots are typically absent, and it relies on its buoyant leaves for support and nutrient absorption.

Its unique adaptations make it an interesting subject in the study of aquatic plant ecology.
