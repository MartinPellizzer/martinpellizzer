Lepanthes durikaensis

Lepanthes durikaensis, commonly known as the Durika's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, showy flowers that are typically white or pale green with intricate patterns. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is often found in humid, shaded forest environments. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by small insects, and it thrives in well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts.
