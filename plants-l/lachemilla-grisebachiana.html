Lachemilla grisebachiana

Lachemilla grisebachiana, commonly known as the gray leaved mountain heather, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America.

It features dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery-gray pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

Lachemilla grisebachiana is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
