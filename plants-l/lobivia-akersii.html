Lobivia akersii

Lobivia akersii, commonly known as the Aker's lobivia, is a species of cactus native to the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded body and spiny, ribbed stems that help reduce water loss in its arid environment. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring. It is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated by succulent enthusiasts.
