Lathyrus quadrimarginatus subsp. tetrapterus

Lathyrus quadrimarginatus subsp.

tetrapterus, commonly known as the four-winged vetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, compound leaves with four pairs of leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple, followed by pods that contain several seeds.

It is often found in dry, calcareous soils and can thrive in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and wastelands.
