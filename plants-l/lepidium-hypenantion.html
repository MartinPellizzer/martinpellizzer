Lepidium hypenantion

Lepidium hypenantion, commonly known as the mountain cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, white or yellowish flowers and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold climates. Its leaves are usually alternate and pinnately divided, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Lepidium hypenantion is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
