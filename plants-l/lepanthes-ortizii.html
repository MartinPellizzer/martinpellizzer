Lepanthes ortizii

Lepanthes ortizii, commonly known as the Ortiz's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid with a delicate, showy flower that typically features a white or pale pink coloration. The plant has a short, underground rhizome and produces a single, slender stem that bears a single flower. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted to the humid, shaded environments where it grows.

Lepanthes ortizii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
