Laserpitium glaucum

Laserpitium glaucum, commonly known as the blue gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine or subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs, giving them a silvery appearance.

Laserpitium glaucum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
