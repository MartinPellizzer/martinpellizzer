Lespedeza hirta var. dissimulans

Lespedeza hirta var. dissimulans, commonly known as the hairy lespedeza, is a perennial legume native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has small, ovate leaves with fine hairs, giving it a distinctive, rugged appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and disturbed areas.

Lespedeza hirta var. dissimulans is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
