Lathyrus flaccidus

Lathyrus flaccidus, commonly known as the slender vetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

Lathyrus flaccidus is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility.
