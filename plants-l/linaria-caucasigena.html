Linaria caucasigena

Linaria caucasigena, commonly known as the Caucasian toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
