Leontodon pinnatifidus

Leontodon pinnatifidus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and deeply lobed, with a rough texture, and its yellow flowers appear in late spring to early summer.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and can thrive in a variety of conditions.
