Lithocardium cylindrostachyum var. platyphyllum

Lithocardium cylindrostachyum var. platyphyllum, commonly known as the broad-leafed lithocardium, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
