Laurus hypericifolia

Laurus hypericifolia, commonly known as the sweet bay laurel, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen leaves that are rich in essential oils. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark, glossy berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular ornamental and culinary plant.

Its historical use in ancient Roman cuisine and as a symbol of victory further highlights its cultural significance.
