Lycopodioides erectifolia

Lycopodioides erectifolia, commonly known as the upright fern, is a member of the Lycopodiaceae family, characterized by its primitive vascular structure.

It features a slender, upright stem with small, scale-like leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern. The plant produces microsporangia and megasporangia on separate structures, typical of its evolutionary lineage. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate regions.

Its reproductive structures are essential for its propagation and ecological role in forest understories.
