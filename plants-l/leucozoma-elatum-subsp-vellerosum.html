Leucozoma elatum subsp. vellerosum

Leucozoma elatum subsp.

vellerosum, commonly known as the silky leucozoma, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America. It is characterized by its dense, soft, and hairy foliage that gives it a velvety appearance.

The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a unique and attractive species for botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
