Lipoblepharis urticifolia

Lipoblepharis urticifolia, commonly known as the stinging nettle tree, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its stinging hairs on the leaves, which can cause irritation upon contact with the skin. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in the spring. Its leaves are often heart-shaped and have a rough texture.

The species is often found in moist, shaded areas and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
