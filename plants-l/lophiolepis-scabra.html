Lophiolepis scabra

Lophiolepis scabra, commonly known as the rough-leaved lophiolepis, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are often rough to the touch, giving it its scientific name, while its flowering period usually occurs during the summer months.

Lophiolepis scabra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
