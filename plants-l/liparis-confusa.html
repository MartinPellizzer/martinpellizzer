Liparis confusa

Liparis confusa, commonly known as the confused orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including parts of Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with a short rhizome and a rosette of fleshy, strap-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with three sepals and three petals, often displaying a range of colors from white to pink.

Liparis confusa prefers shady, moist environments and is often found growing in forest understories or on rock faces.
