Leontopodium × thomsonianum

Leontopodium × thomsonianum, commonly known as the edelweiss, is a flowering plant native to the high alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly white or pale yellow flowers surrounded by a cluster of bracts that resemble a crown. The plant thrives in harsh, rocky environments with cool temperatures and high altitudes. Its flowers typically bloom in late summer to early autumn, and it is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.

Due to its ecological significance and aesthetic appeal, it is also a symbol of resilience and is protected in many areas.
