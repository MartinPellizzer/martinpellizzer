Luzula multiflora subsp. thianshanica

Luzula multiflora subsp.

thianshanica, commonly known as the Tian Shan sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It typically grows in moist, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and forest understories.

The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, greenish flowers. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in cold climates and its adaptability to high-altitude environments.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological niche and potential medicinal properties.
