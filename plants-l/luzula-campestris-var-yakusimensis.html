Luzula campestris var. yakusimensis

Luzula campestris var. yakusimensis, commonly known as the Yakushiman grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountainous regions and forest floors. Its leaves are narrow and usually have a bluish-green hue, while its inflorescence consists of spikelets.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
