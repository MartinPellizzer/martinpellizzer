Lycopodium tetragonum

Lycopodium tetragonum, commonly known as the four-cornered clubmoss, is a small evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and scale-like leaves. The plant produces spore-bearing cones, which are typically clustered at the base of the stems. Lycopodium tetragonum thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitat.
