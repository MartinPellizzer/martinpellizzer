Lupinus albus var. vavilovii

Lupinus albus var. vavilovii, commonly known as the Vavilov's white lupin, is a variant of the white lupin native to Central Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves with small, elliptical leaflets. The flowers are typically white or pale blue, arranged in dense spikes, and are borne on slender peduncles. This variety is known for its robust growth and is often used in agricultural settings for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates.
