Leonurus turkestanicus

Leonurus turkestanicus, commonly known as the Turkestan hedgehog, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and small, purple or pink flowers that bloom in the summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered with fine hairs.

It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
