Linaria alpina subsp. aciculifolia

Linaria alpina subsp.

aciculifolia, commonly known as the alpine toadflax, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in full sun. Its flowers are tubular and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
