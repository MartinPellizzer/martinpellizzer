Leandra salicifolia

Leandra salicifolia, commonly known as the silky tree or silky-leaved hibiscus, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces large, showy flowers with five petals, often in shades of pink or red, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Leandra salicifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
