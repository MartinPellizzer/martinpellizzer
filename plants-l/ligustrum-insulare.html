Ligustrum insulare

Ligustrum insulare, commonly known as the island privet, is a flowering plant native to the Ryukyu Islands of Japan.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 2 to 5 meters. The plant features opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. Its small, fragrant white flowers appear in clusters during the spring, followed by black, berry-like fruits.

Ligustrum insulare is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
