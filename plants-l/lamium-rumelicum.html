Lamium rumelicum

Lamium rumelicum, commonly known as greater plantain, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of white or pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in fields, along roadsides, and in gardens. It has a creeping rhizome that allows it to spread easily and form dense mats.

Lamium rumelicum is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
