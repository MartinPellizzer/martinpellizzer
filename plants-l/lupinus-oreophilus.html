Lupinus oreophilus

Lupinus oreophilus, commonly known as the Sierra lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas and along streambanks. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

Lupinus oreophilus is a member of the legume family and has the ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to nutrient cycling in its ecosystem.
