Ligustrum gamblei

Ligustrum gamblei, commonly known as the Gambier privet, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 3 to 5 meters. The plant features simple, opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. Its small, fragrant white flowers are clustered in racemes and appear in the spring.

The fruit is a small, black drupe that contains a single seed, and the plant is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability.
