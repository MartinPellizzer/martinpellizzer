Litsea piperita

Litsea piperita, commonly known as wild betel, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, aromatic leaves and small, dark purple berries. The plant is valued for its essential oils, which are extracted from its leaves and used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent. Its leaves are often used in the preparation of betel quids, which are chewed for their stimulant effects.

The tree thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring moist, well-drained soils.
