Linaria ascalonica

Linaria ascalonica, commonly known as the Ascalonic toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and vibrant yellow-orange flowers that resemble small toadflax. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Linaria ascalonica is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
