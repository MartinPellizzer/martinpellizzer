Leontodon montanus subsp. pseudotaraxaci

Leontodon montanus subsp.

pseudotaraxaci, commonly known as the alpine dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cold, dry conditions. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a serrated edge, while its flowers are borne on long, slender stems.

This subspecies is often found in mountainous areas and is adapted to the harsh environmental conditions of its habitat.
