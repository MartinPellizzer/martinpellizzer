Laserpitium podolicum

Laserpitium podolicum, commonly known as the Podolic grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its narrow, grass-like leaves are arranged in a basal rosette, and it produces small, yellowish flower heads in the summer.

Laserpitium podolicum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
