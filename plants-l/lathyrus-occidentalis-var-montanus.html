Lathyrus occidentalis var. montanus

Lathyrus occidentalis var. montanus, commonly known as the Sierra lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant has pinnately compound leaves and produces pods that contain several small, elliptical seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.
