Lastrea subquinquefida

Lastrea subquinquefida, commonly known as the five-lobed rockrose, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a compact, branching stem covered in small, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-gray in color. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves and stems.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and xeriscapes.
