Lupinus extrarius

Lupinus extrarius, commonly known as the desert lupine, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds.

Lupinus extrarius is adapted to harsh, dry environments and plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils through its extensive root system.
