Lonchocarpus dipteroneurus

Lonchocarpus dipteroneurus, commonly known as the red root or yellow root, is a tropical leguminous plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces distinctive yellow or orange flowers that are clustered in racemes. Its fruit is a legume pod containing several seeds, and the roots are often harvested for their medicinal properties.

The plant is known for containing alkaloids, which have been studied for potential pharmaceutical applications.
