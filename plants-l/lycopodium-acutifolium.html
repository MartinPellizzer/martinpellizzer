Lycopodium acutifolium

Lycopodium acutifolium, commonly known as the sharp-leaf clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant features upright, branching stems covered in tiny, scale-like leaves. Its reproductive structures, called strobila, are cone-like and produce spores for propagation.

Lycopodium acutifolium plays a role in maintaining soil stability and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
