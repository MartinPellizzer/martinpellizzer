Luma sellowiana

Luma sellowiana, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that appear in the spring. The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various birds and wildlife. Luma sellowiana thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

Its ornamental value has made it a popular plant in horticulture, although it can become invasive in certain environments.
