Lomaria punctata

Lomaria punctata, commonly known as the spotted lomaria, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow and white. Lomaria punctata is often found in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a nectar source for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
