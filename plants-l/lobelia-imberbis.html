Lobelia imberbis

Lobelia imberbis, commonly known as the slender lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, tubular blue or purple flowers. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands.

Lobelia imberbis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
