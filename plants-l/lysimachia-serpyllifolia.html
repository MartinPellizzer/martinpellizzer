Lysimachia serpyllifolia

Lysimachia serpyllifolia, commonly known as common chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features small, bright green leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, with a hairy texture. The plant produces tiny, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lawns, gardens, and along pathways.

Lysimachia serpyllifolia is a hardy species that is easy to cultivate and is sometimes used in ornamental gardening.
