Lonicera coccinea

Lonicera coccinea, commonly known as the red trumpet honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant red tubular flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves that are dark green and glossy on top. Its berries, which appear in late summer, are bright orange and serve as a food source for birds.

Lonicera coccinea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits in gardens and natural habitats.
