Linaria pedunculata var. typica

Linaria pedunculata var. typica, commonly known as the common toadflax, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of bright yellow or orange tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as fields and roadsides. Its leaves are narrow and opposite, with a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to various regions as a garden plant.
