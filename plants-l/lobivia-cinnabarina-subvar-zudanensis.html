Lobivia cinnabarina subvar. zudanensis

Lobivia cinnabarina subvar. zudanensis, commonly known as the Zudanensis Lobivia, is a succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It features compact, cylindrical stems that are typically covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair. The plant produces vibrant orange to red flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are fleshy and often turn reddish when exposed to full sun.

This variety is well adapted to dry climates and requires minimal water to thrive.
