Liparis bistriata var. robusta

Liparis bistriata var. robusta, commonly known as the robust lady's tresses, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and a single spike of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and stream banks. Its flowers are usually pale green to brownish in color, with a distinctive three-lobed lip.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with the right conditions.
