Lagochilopsis pungens

Lagochilopsis pungens, commonly known as the horned poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny seed pods and vibrant red to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Lagochilopsis pungens is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
