Lycopodium clavatum var. monostachyum

Lycopodium clavatum var. monostachyum, commonly known as the clubmoss, is a low-growing evergreen fern that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It features small, scale-like leaves arranged in whorls around the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces spore-bearing cones, or strobili, at the tips of its stems, which are typically clustered in a single spike. It is native to various regions in the Northern Hemisphere and is often found in forest floors or rocky crevices.

This variety is notable for its compact growth habit and its role in ecological systems as a ground cover plant.
