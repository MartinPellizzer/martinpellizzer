Loliolum subulatum

Loliolum subulatum, commonly known as the slender leek, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to withstand salt spray.

Loliolum subulatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
