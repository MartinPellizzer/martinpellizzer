Lusekia laurina

Lusekia laurina, commonly known as the golden laurel, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are oval in shape and have a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers in the spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic value and ability to attract pollinators.
