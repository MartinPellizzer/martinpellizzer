Lupinus bernardinus

Lupinus bernardinus, commonly known as the desert lupine, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils.

Lupinus bernardinus is adapted to withstand drought conditions and is an important component of its native ecosystem.
