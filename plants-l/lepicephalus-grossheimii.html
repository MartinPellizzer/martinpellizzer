Lepicephalus grossheimii

Lepicephalus grossheimii, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter, providing a unique habitat for various aquatic life forms. The plant has a thick, spongy stem that helps it float on the water's surface while anchoring itself in the mud below. Its flowers, which are typically white or pink, bloom in the early morning and close by midday, a behavior known as nyctinasty.

Lepicephalus grossheimii plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by offering shelter and oxygen to the surrounding environment.
