Lactuca thunbergii var. albiflora

Lactuca thunbergii var. albiflora, commonly known as white-flowered lettuce, is a variant of the common lettuce species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its pale or white flowers, which contrast with its dark green, loosely packed leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments and is often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are edible and have a mild, slightly bitter taste, making it a cultivated vegetable in some Asian cuisines.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its distinctive floral display.
