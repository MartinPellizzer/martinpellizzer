Lathyrus verae

Lathyrus verae, commonly known as the Verne's vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with slender stems that twine around supports. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it is native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

Lathyrus verae is often found in grassy areas, along roadsides, and in disturbed soils, where it thrives in full sun to partial shade.
