Leucorchis straminea

Leucorchis straminea, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, yellow flowers with a subtle fragrance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its blooming period occurs during the spring and early summer months.

Leucorchis straminea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
