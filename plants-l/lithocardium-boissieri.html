Lithocardium boissieri

Lithocardium boissieri, commonly known as the rock cress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers bloom in early spring, and it produces small, rounded seed pods.

Lithocardium boissieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens.
