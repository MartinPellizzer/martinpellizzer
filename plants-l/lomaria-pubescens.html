Lomaria pubescens

Lomaria pubescens, commonly known as the hairy lomaria, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes parsley and carrots, and is characterized by its aromatic foliage. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. It produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family.

Lomaria pubescens is often grown as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and subtle floral display.
