Lissochilus dilectus

Lissochilus dilectus, commonly known as the golden bell orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers that resemble small bells, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky slopes, thriving in cool and humid conditions. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the thin soil layers typical of its habitat.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by enthusiasts of rare orchids.
