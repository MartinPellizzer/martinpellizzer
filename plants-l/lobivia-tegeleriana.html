Lobivia tegeleriana

Lobivia tegeleriana, commonly known as the Tegeler's lobivia, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rounded shape and spiny stems. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, which bloom during the warmer months. Lobivia tegeleriana is well adapted to drought conditions, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

It is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and ornamental value.
