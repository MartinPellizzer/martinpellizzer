Lappula intermedia

Lappula intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate lappula, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and prefers full sun to partial shade. The plant has erect, branching stems that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, yellow flowers are clustered in dense, spherical heads, and the leaves are alternate, ovate, and usually hairy.

Lappula intermedia is known for its adaptability and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
