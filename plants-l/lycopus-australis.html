Lycopus australis

Lycopus australis, commonly known as southern blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small, blue to purple, and clustered in whorls.

Lycopus australis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
