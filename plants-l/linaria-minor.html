Linaria minor

Linaria minor, commonly known as the lesser toadflax, is a flowering plant native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features slender, branching stems and bright yellow to orange flowers that resemble small toads, giving it its common name. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Linaria minor thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.
