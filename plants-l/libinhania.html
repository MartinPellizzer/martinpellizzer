Libinhania

Libinhania, commonly known as the African orchid, is a genus of flowering plants native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, often fragrant flowers. The plants typically have tall, erect stems with lance-shaped leaves and produce showy blooms in shades of yellow, orange, and red. Libinhania species are often found in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in full sun.

Due to their ornamental value, they are cultivated in gardens and greenhouses around the world.
