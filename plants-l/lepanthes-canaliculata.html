Lepanthes canaliculata

Lepanthes canaliculata, commonly known as the narrow-ribbed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a rosette pattern, facilitating efficient photosynthesis.

Lepanthes canaliculata is often found in humid, shaded environments such as cloud forests and is valued for its ornamental beauty in horticulture.
