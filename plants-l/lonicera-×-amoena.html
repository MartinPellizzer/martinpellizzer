Lonicera × amoena

Lonicera × amoena, commonly known as the common honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges, thickets, and disturbed areas.

Lonicera × amoena is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
