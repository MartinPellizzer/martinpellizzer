Laelia alvaroana

Laelia alvaroana, commonly known as the Alvaro's orchid, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces dark green, strap-like leaves and clusters of pink to violet blossoms. It thrives in cool, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in controlled greenhouse conditions.
