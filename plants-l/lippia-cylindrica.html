Lippia cylindrica

Lippia cylindrica, commonly known as the cylindrical lippia, is a perennial herb native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches. Lippia cylindrica is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

It is valued for its aromatic foliage and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
