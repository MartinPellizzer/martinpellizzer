Ledonia peduncularis

Ledonia peduncularis, commonly known as the silverleaf orchid, is a striking plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, pendulous stems and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom during the wet season. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and covered in a waxy, silver-gray coating that helps reflect excess sunlight. This unique foliage gives the plant its common name and aids in its survival in high-light environments.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
