Licania silvatica

Licania silvatica, commonly known as the Brazilian wild cashew, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, clustered flowers. The tree produces a fruit that resembles a cashew apple, though it is not related to the true cashew. Its bark and leaves contain compounds with potential medicinal properties.
