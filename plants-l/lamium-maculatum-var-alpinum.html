Lamium maculatum var. alpinum

Lamium maculatum var. alpinum, commonly known as the alpine dead nettle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive purple or reddish markings, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, two-lipped flowers in shades of pink or purple, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking shade-tolerant plants.
