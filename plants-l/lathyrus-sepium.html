Lathyrus sepium

Lathyrus sepium, commonly known as goat's-rue, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple, followed by elongated pods containing several seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in meadows.

Due to its toxic properties, it is considered a noxious weed in some regions.
