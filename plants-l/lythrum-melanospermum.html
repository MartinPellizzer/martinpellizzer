Lythrum melanospermum

Lythrum melanospermum, commonly known as blackseed lythrum, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lythraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and spikes of purple or pink flowers. The plant produces small, dark seeds that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly rough texture.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.
