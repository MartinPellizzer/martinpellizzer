Leucaena esculenta

Leucaena esculenta, commonly known as the velvetbean tree, is a fast-growing leguminous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features trifoliate leaves with a distinctive velvety texture and produces small, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. The plant is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural systems. It is also cultivated for its high-protein seeds and as a forage crop for livestock.

Additionally, its wood is used in construction and as a source of charcoal.
