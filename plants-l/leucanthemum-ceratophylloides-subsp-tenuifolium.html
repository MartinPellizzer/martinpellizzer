Leucanthemum ceratophylloides subsp. tenuifolium

Leucanthemum ceratophylloides subsp.

tenuifolium, commonly known as the slenderleaf tansy, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It features a compact, bushy growth habit with finely divided, gray-green leaves that are narrow and linear.

The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry grasslands and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
