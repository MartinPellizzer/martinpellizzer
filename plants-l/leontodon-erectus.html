Leontodon erectus

Leontodon erectus, commonly known as the upright leontodon, is a flowering plant belonging to the daisy family (Asteraceae).

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and distinctive yellow-orange ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. Its leaves are linear and slightly hairy, while its stem is usually unbranched and sturdy.

Leontodon erectus is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its bright, cheerful blooms.
