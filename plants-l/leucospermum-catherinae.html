Leucospermum catherinae

Leucospermum catherinae, commonly known as the King protea, is a striking evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous white to pinkish flowers arranged in a spherical or conical shape. The plant features narrow, strap-like leaves that are typically evergreen and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
