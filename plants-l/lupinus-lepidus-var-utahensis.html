Lupinus lepidus var. utahensis

Lupinus lepidus var. utahensis, commonly known as the Utah lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and clusters of blue to violet pea-shaped flowers. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open habitats.

Lupinus lepidus var. utahensis plays a role in nitrogen fixation, improving soil fertility in its native range.
