Leea biserrata

Leea biserrata, commonly known as the serrated leea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Leeaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in axillary inflorescences. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental potential and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
