Lindera strychnifolia var. limprichtii

Lindera strychnifolia var. limprichtii, commonly known as the Chinese sweetgum, is a variant of the sweetgum tree native to China.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by spherical, spiny fruit capsules. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
