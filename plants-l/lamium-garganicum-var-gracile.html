Lamium garganicum var. gracile

Lamium garganicum var. gracile, commonly known as slender dead-nettle, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features oval, hairy leaves that are usually arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom from spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its creeping habit and attractive foliage make it a useful ground cover in shady garden settings.
