Ligustrum foliosum f. ovale

Ligustrum foliosum f. ovale, commonly known as the oval-leaf privet, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green, arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by black berries that are attractive to birds. It is often used in landscaping for its dense growth and adaptability to various soil conditions.

The foliage remains evergreen, providing year-round visual interest in gardens.
