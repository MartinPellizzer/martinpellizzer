Lysimachia crista-galli

Lysimachia crista-galli, commonly known as the crown vetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its creeping growth habit and clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, and usually hairy leaves that are lance-shaped and slightly serrated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas.

Lysimachia crista-galli is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it a useful plant in ecological restoration projects.
