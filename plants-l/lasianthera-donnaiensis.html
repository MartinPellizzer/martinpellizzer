Lasianthera donnaiensis

Lasianthera donnaiensis, commonly known as the Brazilian starfruit, is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is native to Brazil.

It is a small evergreen tree that typically grows in tropical rainforests. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, fragrant flowers that are often white or pale pink. Its fruit is a small, yellowish berry that ripens in the dry season.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional medicinal uses.
