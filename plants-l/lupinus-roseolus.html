Lupinus roseolus

Lupinus roseolus, commonly known as the roseleaf lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and clusters of showy pink or purple flowers. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets, and its root system includes nitrogen-fixing nodules that benefit the soil.

Lupinus roseolus is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological contributions.
