Leonurus cardiaca var. intermedius

Leonurus cardiaca var. intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate heartwort, is a variant of the heart-leaved speedwell.

It is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive veining pattern. It produces small blue or purple flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

This variant is often found in moist meadows and along stream banks, where it thrives in partial shade to full sun.
