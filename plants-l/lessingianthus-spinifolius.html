Lessingianthus spinifolius

Lessingianthus spinifolius, commonly known as the spiked leaf sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features bright yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers, typical of the genus Lessingianthus. Its leaves are serrated and covered in fine hairs, giving them a rough texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
