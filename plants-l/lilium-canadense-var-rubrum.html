Lilium canadense var. rubrum

Lilium canadense var. rubrum, commonly known as the red Canada lily, is a striking perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red, with a distinctive red or orange stripe down the throat. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet, with strap-like, grassy leaves arranged in a fan-like pattern. Its bulbs are underground storage organs that help it survive winter and regenerate each growing season.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
