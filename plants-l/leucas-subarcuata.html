Leucas subarcuata

Leucas subarcuata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf leucas, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
