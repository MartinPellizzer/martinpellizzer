Lolium adzharicum

Lolium adzharicum, commonly known as Armenian ryegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences containing numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Lolium adzharicum is valued for its forage quality and ecological resilience.
