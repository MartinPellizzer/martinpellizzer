Lepidium peregrinum var. glabripes

Lepidium peregrinum var. glabripes, commonly known as the smooth wall rocket, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and its clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers are borne on slender stems and are visited by various pollinators, including bees and flies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its drought tolerance and visual interest.
