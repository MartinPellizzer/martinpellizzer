Lycopodium laxum

Lycopodium laxum, commonly known as the slender clubmoss, is a small evergreen perennial plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and produces spore-bearing cones rather than flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky areas. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem.

Lycopodium laxum plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
