Leucothoe revoluta var. arrabidae

Leucothoe revoluta var. arrabidae, commonly known as the Arrabida azalea, is a shrub native to the Azores.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are dark green and glossy, with a distinctive rolled margin.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid climates.
