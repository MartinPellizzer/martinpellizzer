Lippia pumila

Lippia pumila, commonly known as dwarf lippia, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches a height of less than 30 centimeters. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of tiny, white to pale purple flowers. It is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Lippia pumila is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
