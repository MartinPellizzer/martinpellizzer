Lamium sandrasicum

Lamium sandrasicum, commonly known as sandrasicum deadnettle, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

The leaves are hairy and have a slightly minty aroma when crushed.
