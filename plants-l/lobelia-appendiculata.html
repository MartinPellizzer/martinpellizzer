Lobelia appendiculata

Lobelia appendiculata, commonly known as the appendiculate lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Lobelia appendiculata is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
