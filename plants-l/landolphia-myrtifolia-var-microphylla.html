Landolphia myrtifolia var. microphylla

Landolphia myrtifolia var. microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved African morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses in warm climates.
