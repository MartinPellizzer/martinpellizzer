Leptolaena diospyroidea var. tampoketsensis

Leptolaena diospyroidea var. tampoketsensis, commonly known as the Tampoketsi persimmon, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in parts of Indonesia and the Philippines.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, orange-red fruits that are similar in appearance to persimmons.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the consumption of its fruit, which has a sweet, slightly astringent flavor.
