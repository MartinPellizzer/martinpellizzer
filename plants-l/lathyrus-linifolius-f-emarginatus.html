Lathyrus linifolius f. emarginatus

Lathyrus linifolius f. emarginatus, commonly known as the emarginated grass vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and small, trifoliate leaves with a distinctive notched or emarginated base. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
