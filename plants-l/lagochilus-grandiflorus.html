Lagochilus grandiflorus

Lagochilus grandiflorus, commonly known as the large-flowered mountain hyacinth, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit, hairy stems, and showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

Lagochilus grandiflorus is often found in rocky or gravelly soils at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 3,000 meters.
