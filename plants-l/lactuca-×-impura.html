Lactuca × impura

Lactuca × impura, commonly known as wild lettuce, is a hybrid species within the dandelion family (Asteraceae).

It is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often lobed. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into prickly, spherical seed heads. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in fields.

Botanically, it is notable for its milky sap, which contains lactucine, a compound with mild sedative properties.
