Liparis luteola

Liparis luteola, commonly known as the yellow lady's tresses, is a herbaceous orchid native to parts of Asia and North America.

It features a tall, slender stem with dense, spirally arranged leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption in poor soils.

Liparis luteola is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
