Lasiochlamys coriacea

Lasiochlamys coriacea, commonly known as the rough-leaved woodruff, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dark, leathery leaves that are covered in a rough, textured surface. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
