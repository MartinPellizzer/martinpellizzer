Ledebouria papillata

Ledebouria papillata, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a species of bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tulip-like flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. Its flowers are typically pink or purple, with a distinctive papillate (bumpy) texture on the petals.

Ledebouria papillata is well adapted to rocky, arid environments and is often found growing in crevices or on rocky outcrops.
