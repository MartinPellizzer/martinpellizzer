Loxogramme lanceolata

Loxogramme lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf water clover, is a perennial aquatic plant native to parts of Asia.

It features delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are often submerged or floating, with a distinctive network of veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and streams.

Its botanical structure supports its role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen through photosynthesis.
