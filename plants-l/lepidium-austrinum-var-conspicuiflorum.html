Lepidium austrinum var. conspicuiflorum

Lepidium austrinum var. conspicuiflorum, commonly known as the showy peppergrass, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that are borne on elongated peduncles. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

The species is part of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other economically important plants.
