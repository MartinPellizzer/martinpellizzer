Lupinus canescens subsp. amblyophyllus

Lupinus canescens subsp.

amblyophyllus, commonly known as the woolly lupine, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Mexico. It is characterized by its gray-green foliage and clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets. Lupinus canescens subsp.

amblyophyllus is adapted to arid conditions and has a deep root system to access moisture.
