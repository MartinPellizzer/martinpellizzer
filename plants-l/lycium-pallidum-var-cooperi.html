Lycium pallidum var. cooperi

Lycium pallidum var. cooperi, commonly known as the Cooper's lycium, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its pale yellow to orange-red berries, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant has small, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
