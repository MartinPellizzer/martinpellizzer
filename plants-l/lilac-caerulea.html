Lilac caerulea

Lilac caerulea, commonly known as the blue lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pale blue to lavender-colored flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy on top. It typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and prefers well-drained soil and full sun.

Lilac caerulea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in gardens for its showy spring display.
