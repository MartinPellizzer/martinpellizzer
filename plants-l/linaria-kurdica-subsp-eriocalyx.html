Linaria kurdica subsp. eriocalyx

Linaria kurdica subsp.

eriocalyx, commonly known as the Kurdish toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East. It belongs to the plant family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its yellow-orange tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The subspecies features lance-shaped leaves and a compact growth habit, often forming dense mats on rocky or arid soils. It is well adapted to harsh environments, thriving in full sun and poor soil conditions.

This plant is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance.
