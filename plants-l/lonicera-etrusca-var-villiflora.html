Lonicera etrusca var. villiflora

Lonicera etrusca var. villiflora, commonly known as the Etruscan honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to yellow, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in sunny to partially shaded locations with well-drained soil.

This variety is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and floral display.
