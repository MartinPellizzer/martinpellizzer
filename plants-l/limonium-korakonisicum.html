Limonium korakonisicum

Limonium korakonisicum, commonly known as the Greek sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in coastal and saline environments, making it well adapted to coastal dunes and rocky shores. It is often found in areas with poor soil and high salinity, demonstrating its resilience to harsh conditions.

Limonium korakonisicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ecological role in stabilizing coastal landscapes.
