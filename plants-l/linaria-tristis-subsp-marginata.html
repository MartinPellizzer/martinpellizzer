Linaria tristis subsp. marginata

Linaria tristis subsp.

marginata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender stems and lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive yellow-green margin.

The plant produces clusters of small, brightly colored flowers, typically yellow or orange, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in suitable climates.
