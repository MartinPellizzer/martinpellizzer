Lyonia vaccinioides

Lyonia vaccinioides, commonly known as swamp lily or blueberry bush, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. The plant has alternate, elliptic leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its berries, which are edible, are small and dark blue when mature.

Lyonia vaccinioides thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
