Lysipomia laricina

Lysipomia laricina, commonly known as the tussock grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It forms dense tussocks with a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, nutrient-poor soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Lysipomia laricina plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for Arctic wildlife.
