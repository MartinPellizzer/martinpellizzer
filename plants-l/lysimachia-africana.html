Lysimachia africana

Lysimachia africana, commonly known as African yellow loosestrife, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in dense, terminal spikes, producing a striking visual effect.

Lysimachia africana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its ability to thrive in wet conditions.
