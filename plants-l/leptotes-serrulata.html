Leptotes serrulata

Leptotes serrulata, commonly known as the serrated lespedeza, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pink to reddish-purple flowers. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade.
