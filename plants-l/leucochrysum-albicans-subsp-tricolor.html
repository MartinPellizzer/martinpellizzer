Leucochrysum albicans subsp. tricolor

Leucochrysum albicans subsp.

tricolor, commonly known as the three-colored everlasting flower, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its striking, tri-colored flowers that range from white to yellow and orange, creating a vibrant display.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers arranged in dense, compact inflorescences, which are often used in floral arrangements due to their longevity. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and drought tolerance.
