Linum schiedeanum var. coulterianum

Linum schiedeanum var. coulterianum, commonly known as the Coulter's flax, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the genus Linum, which includes various species of flax known for their showy blue or purple flowers. This particular variety is characterized by its slender, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.
