Linaria multicaulis subsp. panormitana

Linaria multicaulis subsp.

panormitana, commonly known as the Balkan toadflax, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, branched stems that often grow from a basal rosette of leaves.

The plant produces vibrant yellow-orange flowers in racemes, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are linear and slightly hairy, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
