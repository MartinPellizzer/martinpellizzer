Limnophila torenioides

Limnophila torenioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved water plantain, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that grow from a rhizomatous stem, often submerged or partially submerged in water. The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers that are typically clustered in dense spikes. It thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and the edges of ponds, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic life.

Limnophila torenioides is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in aquatic ecosystems.
