Litsea erectinervia

Litsea erectinervia, commonly known as the narrow-veined litsea, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and avocado trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate, and often elliptical leaves with prominent midribs. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by berries that are edible for some bird species.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and its potential use in traditional medicine.
