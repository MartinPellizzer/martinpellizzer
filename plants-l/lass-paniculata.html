Lass paniculata

Lass paniculata, commonly known as the sweet flag, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland areas in North America.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that grow in dense clusters, and it produces small, fragrant, yellowish-green flowers arranged in panicles. The plant is often found in marshy soils and can thrive in standing water, making it a common sight in wetland ecosystems. It is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to aquatic environments.
