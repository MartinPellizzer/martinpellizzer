Lobelia dolichothyrsa

Lobelia dolichothyrsa, commonly known as the long-stalked lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady woodlands and is often found in rich, loamy soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Lobelia dolichothyrsa is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
