Lamium mauritanicum

Lamium mauritanicum, commonly known as Persian shield, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often marked with silver or purple veins. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of pink, red, or purple, typically blooming from spring to early summer. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

Lamium mauritanicum is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and vibrant floral display.
