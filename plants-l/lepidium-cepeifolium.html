Lepidium cepeifolium

Lepidium cepeifolium, commonly known as the bladder campion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, pink or purple flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is found in various regions of North America. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine, sticky hairs.

Lepidium cepeifolium is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
