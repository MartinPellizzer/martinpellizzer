Leandra cremersii

Leandra cremersii, commonly known as the Mexican orchid or Cremers' orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows in shady, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces intricate, showy inflorescences.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
