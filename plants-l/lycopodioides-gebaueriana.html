Lycopodioides gebaueriana

Lycopodioides gebaueriana, commonly known as the Gebauer's clubmoss, is a species of lycophyte belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant features a short, upright stem with whorled leaves that are narrow and lance-shaped. Its reproductive structures include sporangia that produce spores, which are dispersed by wind.

Lycopodioides gebaueriana is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
