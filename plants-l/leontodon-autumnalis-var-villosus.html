Leontodon autumnalis var. villosus

Leontodon autumnalis var. villosus, commonly known as the hairy autumn hawk's-beard, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant features hairy stems and basal leaves that are often lobed or toothed. Its yellow flowers bloom in the fall, attracting various pollinators.

It typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils.
