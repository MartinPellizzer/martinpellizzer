Leea stipulosa

Leea stipulosa, commonly known as the stipulate leea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It belongs to the family Leeaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves with small stipules at the base of each leaf stalk. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in spring or early summer. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

Leea stipulosa is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
