Lavandula pedunculata subsp. ambigua

Lavandula pedunculata subsp.

ambigua, commonly known as the Spanish lavender, is a perennial shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula. It features aromatic, linear leaves and produces spikes of purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. Its essential oils are valued for their fragrance and medicinal properties.

This subspecies is closely related to other lavender species but is distinguished by its unique floral structure and habitat preferences.
