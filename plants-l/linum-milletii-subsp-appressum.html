Linum milletii subsp. appressum

Linum milletii subsp.

appressum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flax, is a flowering plant belonging to the Linaceae family. It is characterized by its slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers that are clustered in racemes. It is native to regions of Europe and Asia, often found in grassy areas and along roadsides.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in textile production.
