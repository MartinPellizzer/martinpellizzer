Lantana venosa

Lantana venosa, commonly known as the silver lantana, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, brightly colored flowers that range from yellow to red. The plant belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Lantana venosa thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees adds to its ecological importance.
