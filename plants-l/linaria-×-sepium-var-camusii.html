Linaria × sepium var. camusii

Linaria × sepium var. camusii, commonly known as the hybrid goldenweed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branched stems that often grow in open, sunny habitats. The plant produces showy, bright yellow flowers in racemes, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
