Litsea filipedunculata

Litsea filipedunculata, commonly known as the hairy-fruit litsea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its fruit is a small, yellowish drupe that develops from a green, hairy calyx.

Litsea filipedunculata is typically found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its aromatic properties.
