Leuzea dahurica

Leuzea dahurica, commonly known as Siberian cow-wheat, is a perennial herb native to the cold climates of Eastern Europe and Asia.

It features a robust root system and lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive hairy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, disturbed habitats.

Botanically, it is noted for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal medicine.
