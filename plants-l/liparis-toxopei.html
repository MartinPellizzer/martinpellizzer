Liparis toxopei

Liparis toxopei, commonly known as the toxic orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small, terrestrial herb with a slender, wiry stem and a cluster of narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces delicate, greenish-yellow flowers that are often clustered in a spike, and it is known for its toxic properties. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure and growth habits.

Due to its toxicity, it is important to handle the plant with care.
