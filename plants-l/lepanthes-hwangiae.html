Lepanthes hwangiae

Lepanthes hwangiae, commonly known as the Hwang's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Mexico.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, spurred flowers that often display vibrant colors. The plant typically grows as a small epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are often fragrant and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Lepanthes hwangiae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is a subject of interest in botanical research due to its unique floral morphology.
