Linum suffruticosum subsp. salsoloides

Linum suffruticosum subsp.

salsoloides, commonly known as the desert flax, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America. It is a low-growing, perennial herb with slender, branched stems and small, greenish-white flowers.

The plant has hairy, linear leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

This species is adapted to sandy soils and is often found in desert scrub and coastal dunes.
