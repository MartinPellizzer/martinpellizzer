Lotodes lupinellus

Lotodes lupinellus, commonly known as the lesser lupin, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. It is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil, making it beneficial in ecological restoration projects.

The seeds of Lotodes lupinellus are often used as forage for livestock and in traditional herbal medicine.
