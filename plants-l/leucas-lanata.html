Leucas lanata

Leucas lanata, commonly known as hairy leucas, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including India and Nepal.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant is distinguished by its soft, hairy foliage and clusters of small, pale blue or purple flowers. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.

Leucas lanata is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
