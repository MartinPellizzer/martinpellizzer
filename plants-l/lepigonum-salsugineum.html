Lepigonum salsugineum

Lepigonum salsugineum, commonly known as sea rocket, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and Atlantic coasts.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial that thrives in saline and sandy soils. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Lepigonum salsugineum is adapted to withstand harsh coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.
