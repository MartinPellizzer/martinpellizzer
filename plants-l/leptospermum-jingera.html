Leptospermum jingera

Leptospermum jingera, commonly known as the pink tea tree, is a small evergreen shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally for its aromatic properties.
