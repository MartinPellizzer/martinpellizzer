Lavandula brevidens var. bastricha

Lavandula brevidens var. bastricha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lavender, is a compact, aromatic shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grey-green leaves and produces spikes of purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is well adapted to dry, sunny conditions and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often used in ornamental gardening for its fragrance and visual appeal.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for rock gardens and as a cut flower.
