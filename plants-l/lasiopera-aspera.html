Lasiopera aspera

Lasiopera aspera, commonly known as the rough-leaved helleborine, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and inconspicuous, greenish flowers that bloom in late spring. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in shaded, moist woodlands and rocky habitats. The plant has a rhizomatous root system and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

It is often found growing in association with other shade-tolerant flora in its native environments.
