Loncoxis sulphurea

Loncoxis sulphurea, commonly known as the sulphur yellow orchid, is a striking plant native to the high Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which feature intricate patterns of orange and green. The plant typically grows in rocky, alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in a fine, silvery coating.

This orchid is known for its unique ability to withstand harsh climatic conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
