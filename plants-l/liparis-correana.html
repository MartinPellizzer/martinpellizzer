Liparis correana

Liparis correana, commonly known as the Korean orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to East Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a compact rhizome and produces small, greenish or white flowers that are often clustered in a spike. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are usually broad and grass-like, and it has a short flowering period in late spring to early summer.

Liparis correana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral structure.
