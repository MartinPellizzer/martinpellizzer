Lasianthus chryseus

Lasianthus chryseus, commonly known as the golden flame or golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in dense, showy clusters. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its foliage consists of opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth texture and a slightly glossy appearance.

Lasianthus chryseus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes due to its vibrant floral display.
