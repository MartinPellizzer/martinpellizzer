Lychnis bungeana

Lychnis bungeana, commonly known as the Bunge's catchfly, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the carnation family (Caryophyllaceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pink to red flowers. The plant produces hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white pubescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Lychnis bungeana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
