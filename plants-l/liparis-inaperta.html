Liparis inaperta

Liparis inaperta, commonly known as the unopened lip orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, greenish-white flowers that remain tightly closed even when mature, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories where it can receive adequate light without being exposed to direct sun. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, and it has a rhizomatous root system that helps it anchor in rocky or soil-rich substrates.

Due to its limited distribution and fragile nature, Liparis inaperta is considered a rare and protected species in its native habitat.
