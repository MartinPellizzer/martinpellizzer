Lupinus holosericeus var. utahensis

Lupinus holosericeus var. utahensis, commonly known as the Utah lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant has finely hairy stems and leaves composed of small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Lupinus holosericeus var. utahensis plays a role in soil stabilization and provides nectar for pollinators.
