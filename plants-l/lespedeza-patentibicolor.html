Lespedeza patentibicolor

Lespedeza patentibicolor, commonly known as the purpletop leafcup, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet being ovate and hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Lespedeza patentibicolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
