Lycopodioides sichuanica

Lycopodioides sichuanica, commonly known as the Sichuan clubmoss, is a species of lycophyte native to the mountainous regions of Sichuan, China.

It belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae and is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and spore-producing structures called strobila. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest undergrowth, where it can thrive in the cool, humid conditions. Its reproductive structures are essential for its propagation, and it plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat.
