Lepanthes jackinpyxa

Lepanthes jackinpyxa, commonly known as the Jack-in-Pyx orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically display vibrant colors such as purple and pink. The plant grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually long and narrow, adapted for capturing moisture from the air.

Lepanthes jackinpyxa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
