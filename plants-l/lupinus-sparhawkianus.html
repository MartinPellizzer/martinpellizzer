Lupinus sparhawkianus

Lupinus sparhawkianus, commonly known as the Sparhawk's lupine, is a species of flowering plant native to specific regions of the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Lupinus sparhawkianus is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in grasslands and open woodlands.
