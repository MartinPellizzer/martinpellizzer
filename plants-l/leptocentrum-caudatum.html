Leptocentrum caudatum

Leptocentrum caudatum, commonly known as the long-tailed balm, is a herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in marshy or swampy areas.

Leptocentrum caudatum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with appropriate water conditions.
