Linum scabridum

Linum scabridum, commonly known as rough flax, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and rocky habitats.

Linum scabridum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in fiber production.
