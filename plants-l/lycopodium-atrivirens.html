Lycopodium atrivirens

Lycopodium atrivirens, commonly known as the dark green clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its small, slender, evergreen foliage and produces spores in cone-like structures called strobila. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. It has a unique reproductive system that relies on spores rather than seeds.

The plant's structure includes both stems and leaves, with the stems often remaining underground.
