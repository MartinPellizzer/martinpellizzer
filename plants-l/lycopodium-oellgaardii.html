Lycopodium oellgaardii

Lycopodium oellgaardii, commonly known as the Oellgaard's clubmoss, is a small, evergreen fern-like plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae and is characterized by its scale-like leaves and spore-producing structures called strobili. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for small invertebrates.

Lycopodium oellgaardii is also of interest to botanists due to its primitive features and evolutionary significance.
