Leptodermis buxifolia

Leptodermis buxifolia, commonly known as the box-leaf daphne, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves that resemble those of boxwood, giving it its common name. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental species within its native habitat.
