Lagochilus hirsutus

Lagochilus hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy lips' herb, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color.

Lagochilus hirsutus is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
