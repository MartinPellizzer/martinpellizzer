Lamium striatum subsp. nivale

Lamium striatum subsp.

nivale, commonly known as snow groundsel, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features oval, slightly hairy leaves that are often tinged with silver, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, pale purple to white flowers clustered in whorls along the stems, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a hardy, shade-tolerant ground cover.
