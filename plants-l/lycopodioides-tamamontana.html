Lycopodioides tamamontana

Lycopodioides tamamontana, commonly known as the Tamamontan clubmoss, is a species of lycophyte belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant characterized by its dense, needle-like leaves arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces microspores and megaspores on separate cones, typical of its evolutionary lineage. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices.

Its presence often indicates a healthy, undisturbed ecosystem due to its sensitivity to environmental changes.
