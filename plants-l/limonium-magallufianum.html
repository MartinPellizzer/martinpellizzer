Limonium magallufianum

Limonium magallufianum, commonly known as the sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, thriving in salty, well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of salt.

Limonium magallufianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate harsh coastal conditions.
