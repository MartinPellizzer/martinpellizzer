Lawrencella lanceolata

Lawrencella lanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf lawrencella, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands. It has a fibrous root system and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Lawrencella lanceolata is valued for its ecological role in supporting local insect populations and its adaptability to arid environments.
