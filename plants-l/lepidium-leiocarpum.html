Lepidium leiocarpum

Lepidium leiocarpum, commonly known as the smooth-capsule wallflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant is characterized by its small, yellow or white flowers and hairy, branching stems. Its leaves are alternate and pinnately divided, often with a slightly succulent texture.

Lepidium leiocarpum typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as fields and woodland edges.
