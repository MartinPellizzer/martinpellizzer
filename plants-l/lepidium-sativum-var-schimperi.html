Lepidium sativum var. schimperi

Lepidium sativum var. schimperi, commonly known as Ethiopian cress, is a variant of the common cress species native to Ethiopia.

It is an annual herbaceous plant characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and small, white to yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions. Its seeds are edible and have a peppery flavor similar to other members of the Brassicaceae family.

The plant is also valued for its nutritional content and has been used in traditional medicine.
