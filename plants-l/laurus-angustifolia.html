Laurus angustifolia

Laurus angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved laurel, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and aromatic, often used in culinary applications. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark purple berries that are edible but not commonly consumed. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited for Mediterranean climates and garden landscapes.
