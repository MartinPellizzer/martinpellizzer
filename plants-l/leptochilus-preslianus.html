Leptochilus preslianus

Leptochilus preslianus, commonly known as the Presli's rockcress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically thrives in rocky or alpine environments. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and covered with a fine layer of white hairs.

It is native to regions in Europe and is often found in limestone soils.
