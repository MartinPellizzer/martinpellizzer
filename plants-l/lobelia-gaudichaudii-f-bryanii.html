Lobelia gaudichaudii f. bryanii

Lobelia gaudichaudii f. bryanii, commonly known as the Bryan's lobelia, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to New Zealand.

It features delicate, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in spring and summer. The plant has hairy, narrow leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested or rocky habitats.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
