Lycopodium × sabinifolium

Lycopodium × sabinifolium, commonly known as the clubmoss fern, is a hybrid plant that exhibits characteristics of both its parent species, Lycopodium and Sabina.

It is a small, evergreen shrub with a dense, compact growth habit, often forming low, mat-like structures. The plant produces needle-like leaves that are typically dark green and arranged in whorls around the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Lycopodium × sabinifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate a range of soil conditions.
