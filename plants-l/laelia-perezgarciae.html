Laelia perezgarciae

Laelia perezgarciae, commonly known as the Perez Garciae orchid, is a species native to the high Andes of Ecuador.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a compact growth habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The plant features a pseudobulb that stores water and nutrients, typical of many orchid species. Its flowers are borne on racemes and are adapted to the cool, moist conditions of its mountainous habitat.

Laelia perezgarciae is valued for its ornamental qualities and is a subject of interest in botanical research.
