Linum squarrosum

Linum squarrosum, commonly known as squarrose flax, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous small, oval seeds. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine, white hairs.

Linum squarrosum is often found in grassy areas, along roadsides, and in disturbed soils.
