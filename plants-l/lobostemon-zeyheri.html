Lobostemon zeyheri

Lobostemon zeyheri, commonly known as the Cape honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular, yellow-orange flowers. The plant is typically shrub-like, with a woody stem and can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and birds, contributing to its ecological significance.
