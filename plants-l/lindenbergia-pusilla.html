Lindenbergia pusilla

Lindenbergia pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf lindenbergia, is a small, herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It typically grows to a height of only a few centimeters, with compact, fleshy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, and it is often found in moist, shaded environments. Lindenbergia pusilla is valued in horticulture for its unique appearance and ability to thrive in humid conditions.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in plant morphology and adaptation.
