Lupinus pilosus f. albus

Lupinus pilosus f. albus, commonly known as the white hairy lupine, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant is native to the western United States, particularly California, and thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
