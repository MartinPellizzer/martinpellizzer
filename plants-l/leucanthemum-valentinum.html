Leucanthemum valentinum

Leucanthemum valentinum, commonly known as Valentine's Day Shasta Daisy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its white to pale yellow flowers with a central dark brown or purple disc, which are typically borne on sturdy, upright stems. The plant has lance-shaped to oval leaves that are usually hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its blooming period extends from late spring to early autumn, adding a delicate floral display to gardens.
