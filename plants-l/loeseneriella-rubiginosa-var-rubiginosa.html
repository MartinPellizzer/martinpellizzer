Loeseneriella rubiginosa var. rubiginosa

Loeseneriella rubiginosa var. rubiginosa, commonly known as the red-stemmed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of southern Africa.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in marshy areas or along the edges of water bodies. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. Its reddish stems are a distinguishing characteristic, especially during the growing season.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland habitats.
