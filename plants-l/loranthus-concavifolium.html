Loranthus concavifolium

Loranthus concavifolium, commonly known as the concave-leaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily in the genus Eucalyptus.

It lacks true roots and absorbs water and nutrients through its haustoria, which penetrate the host's vascular system. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers, followed by fleshy, red berries that contain seeds covered in a sticky substance to aid in dispersal. It is native to Australia and is often found in dry, open forests.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the ecosystem, supporting various bird species that feed on its berries.
