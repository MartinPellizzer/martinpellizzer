Lecythis tenuifolia

Lecythis tenuifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian walnut or yellow tallow tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and produces a heavy, oily fruit with a thick, waxy coating. The fruit contains a hard, edible kernel that is rich in oil and nutrients. The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in local communities.
