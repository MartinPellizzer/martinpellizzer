Linaria crassifolia

Linaria crassifolia, commonly known as the thick-leaved toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is well adapted to arid environments. It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.

Linaria crassifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
