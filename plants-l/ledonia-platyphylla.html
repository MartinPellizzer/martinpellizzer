Ledonia platyphylla

Ledonia platyphylla, commonly known as the flat-leaf ledonia, is a rare flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, flat leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps reduce water loss in its humid environment. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom during the wet season. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils, allowing it to thrive in shaded undergrowth.

Due to habitat destruction, Ledonia platyphylla is considered endangered and is protected in several conservation areas.
