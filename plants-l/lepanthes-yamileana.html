Lepanthes yamileana

Lepanthes yamileana, commonly known as the Yamileana orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often colorful flowers that bloom during the rainy season. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

Lepanthes yamileana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
