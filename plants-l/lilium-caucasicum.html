Lilium caucasicum

Lilium caucasicum, commonly known as the Caucasian lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in mountainous areas and prefers well-drained, loamy soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Lilium caucasicum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking blooms.
