Linaria fontanesii

Linaria fontanesii, commonly known as the rock snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bright yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Linaria fontanesii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
