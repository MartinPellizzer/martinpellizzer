Lindmania brachyphylla var. angustior

Lindmania brachyphylla var. angustior, commonly known as the narrow-leaved arrowroot, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its rhizomes are the primary part used, containing starch that is harvested for culinary purposes.

This variety is noted for its adaptability to humid, shaded environments and its role in traditional agriculture.
