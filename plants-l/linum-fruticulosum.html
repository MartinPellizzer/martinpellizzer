Linum fruticulosum

Linum fruticulosum, commonly known as the prickly poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and striking blue or purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds. Linum fruticulosum is often found in dry, rocky soils and can thrive in full sun.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it a notable species in its native habitat.
