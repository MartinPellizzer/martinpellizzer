Larix leptolepis f. pendula

Larix leptolepis f. pendula, commonly known as the weeping Japanese larch, is a unique form of the Japanese larch characterized by its pendulous branches that droop gracefully, giving it an ornamental appearance.

It is a deciduous conifer, shedding its needle-like foliage in the autumn. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, typical of the Pinaceae family. Native to Japan, it thrives in temperate climates and well-drained soils.

Its distinctive shape makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and landscape design.
