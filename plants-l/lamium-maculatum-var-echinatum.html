Lamium maculatum var. echinatum

Lamium maculatum var. echinatum, commonly known as the spotted deadnettle, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive purple and white markings, and its flowers are typically pink or white, forming in clusters. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to spread through rhizomes.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions if not managed properly.
