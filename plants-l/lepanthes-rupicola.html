Lepanthes rupicola

Lepanthes rupicola, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found growing on rocky slopes or in shaded forest understories. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pale green, with intricate patterns on the petals. Lepanthes rupicola is a terrestrial orchid that thrives in humid, tropical environments.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
