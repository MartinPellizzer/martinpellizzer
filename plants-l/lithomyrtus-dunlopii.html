Lithomyrtus dunlopii

Lithomyrtus dunlopii, commonly known as the Dunlop's myrtle, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and white or pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It is often found in coastal dunes and is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience.

Lithomyrtus dunlopii plays a role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems and provides habitat for various insects and birds.
