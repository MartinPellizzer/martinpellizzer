Lycopodium inundatum var. diversifolium

Lycopodium inundatum var. diversifolium, commonly known as the variable clubmoss, is a variant of the clubmoss genus Lycopodium.

It is a small, evergreen shrub characterized by its dense, needle-like foliage and spore-bearing structures called strobili. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in temperate forests and along stream banks. Its leaves are often variable in shape and size, giving rise to its specific epithet "diversifolium.".

The plant plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various small organisms.
