Lycopus riparius

Lycopus riparius, commonly known as water horehound, is a perennial herb native to wetland habitats in North America.

It typically grows in marshes, stream banks, and other moist environments, often forming dense colonies. The plant has square, hairy stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its small, blue to purple flowers appear in clusters and bloom in late summer.

Lycopus riparius is valued for its aromatic foliage and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
