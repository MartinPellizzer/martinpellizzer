Lithospermum czernjajevii

Lithospermum czernjajevii, commonly known as the Czech lithosperm, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and forest edges. The plant features upright stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly serrated margin. It produces small, pinkish to purple flowers in clusters, which bloom in the summer months.

The roots of Lithospermum czernjajevii contain various secondary metabolites, some of which have been studied for their potential medicinal properties.
