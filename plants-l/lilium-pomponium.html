Lilium pomponium

Lilium pomponium, commonly known as the Turk's cap lily, is a species of flowering plant in the Liliaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that are typically red or orange in color. The plant produces showy, clustered blooms that are often used in ornamental gardening. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

The Turk's cap lily is also known for its fragrant flowers and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic value.
