Lachnostachys rodwayana

Lachnostachys rodwayana, commonly known as the Natal grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems that bear narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, pale blue to purple flowers. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open grasslands and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in some regions for landscaping purposes.
