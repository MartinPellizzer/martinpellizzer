Lindsaea attenuata

Lindsaea attenuata, commonly known as the slender lindsaea, is a fern indigenous to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, creeping rhizomes and small, lance-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its reproductive structures include sporangia that produce spores, which are dispersed by wind.

Lindsaea attenuata is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate in humid conditions.
