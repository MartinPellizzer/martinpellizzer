Lepidium latifolium subsp. sibiricum

Lepidium latifolium subsp.

sibiricum, commonly known as Siberian cress, is a herbaceous plant native to the colder regions of Asia. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

The plant features small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly hairy texture. It produces small, oval seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other parts of the world for horticultural purposes.
