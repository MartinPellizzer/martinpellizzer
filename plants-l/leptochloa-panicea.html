Leptochloa panicea

Leptochloa panicea, commonly known as Panicled leptochloa, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences composed of numerous tiny florets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Leptochloa panicea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
