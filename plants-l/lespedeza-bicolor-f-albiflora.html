Lespedeza bicolor f. albiflora

Lespedeza bicolor f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered lespedeza, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, making it visually distinct. It is native to parts of Asia and is often found in open woodlands and grassy areas.

Lespedeza bicolor f. albiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
