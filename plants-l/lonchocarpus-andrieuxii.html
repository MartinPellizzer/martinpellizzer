Lonchocarpus andrieuxii

Lonchocarpus andrieuxii, commonly known as the African senna, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
