Lepanthes revoluta

Lepanthes revoluta, commonly known as the curled-leaf orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are often curled at the tips, giving it its common name. Its flowers are usually pale green or white, with a distinctive three-lobed lip.

Lepanthes revoluta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm, humid climates.
