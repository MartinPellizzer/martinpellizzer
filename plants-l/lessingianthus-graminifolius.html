Lessingianthus graminifolius

Lessingianthus graminifolius, commonly known as the grass-leaved sunflower, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces vibrant yellow flowers with a distinctive ray floret structure. It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to gardens and ecological restoration projects.
