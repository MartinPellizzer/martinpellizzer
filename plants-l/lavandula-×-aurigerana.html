Lavandula × aurigerana

Lavandula × aurigerana, commonly known as English lavender, is a hybrid shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, spike-like inflorescences of purple or pink flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that are densely packed along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and aromatherapy.

Its essential oils, extracted from the flowering tops, are widely used in perfumery and medicinal applications.
