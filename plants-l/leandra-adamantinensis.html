Leandra adamantinensis

Leandra adamantinensis, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid, is a species native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family, characterized by its intricate and often showy floral structures. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with intricate patterns, and it produces small, fleshy fruits.

Leandra adamantinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the biodiversity of its native habitat.
