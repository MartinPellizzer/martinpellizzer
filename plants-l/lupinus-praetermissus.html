Lupinus praetermissus

Lupinus praetermissus, commonly known as the Mexican lupine, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and is adapted to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.

It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for soil enrichment in native plant communities.
