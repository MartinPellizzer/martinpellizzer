Lavandula austroapennina

Lavandula austroapennina, commonly known as the Southern Apennine lavender, is a perennial shrub native to southern Italy.

It is characterized by its aromatic, spike-like inflorescences composed of purple or pink flowers. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for Mediterranean climates.

Lavandula austroapennina is valued for its essential oils, which are used in aromatherapy and herbal medicine.
