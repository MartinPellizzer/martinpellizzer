Lolium Adzharicum

Lolium adzharicum is a lesser-known grass species native to the Caucasus region. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks. This plant is notable for its fine, dense foliage and its ability to form dense mats. Though not widely cultivated, it holds ecological significance in its native habitat. Its study contributes to understanding plant adaptation and biodiversity in mountainous regions.
