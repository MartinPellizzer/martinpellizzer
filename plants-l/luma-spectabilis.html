Luma spectabilis

Luma spectabilis, commonly known as the Christmasberry, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southeastern Brazil.

It is characterized by its bright red berries that appear during the Southern Hemisphere's summer, typically from December to February. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, fragrant white flowers. It thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal dunes and scrublands.

The berries are a significant food source for various birds and are also used in traditional medicine.
