Laserpitium silaifolium

Laserpitium silaifolium, commonly known as the silvery leaf gentian, is a flowering plant belonging to the Gentianaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are typically tubular and arranged in clusters. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is adapted to high-altitude environments. Its leaves are covered in a dense layer of silvery hairs, giving it its common name.

Laserpitium silaifolium is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions.
