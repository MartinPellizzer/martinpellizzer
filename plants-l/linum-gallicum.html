Linum gallicum

Linum gallicum, commonly known as French flax, is a herbaceous annual plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It produces attractive blue or white flowers that bloom in summer, and its slender, branched stems are covered in fine hairs. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are alternate and often have a slightly hairy texture. It is cultivated for its seeds, which are used to produce linseed oil, and for its ornamental value in gardens.

Linum gallicum thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a versatile addition to both wild and cultivated landscapes.
