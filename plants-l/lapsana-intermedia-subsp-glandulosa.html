Lapsana intermedia subsp. glandulosa

Lapsana intermedia subsp.

glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular milkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, glandular hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically visited by various pollinators. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and other open habitats.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
