Leucorchis albida var. breviloba

Leucorchis albida var. breviloba, commonly known as the dwarf white orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. The plant grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest undergrowth where it thrives on the forest floor. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces a single spike of flowers during the rainy season.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
