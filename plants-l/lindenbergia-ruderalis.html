Lindenbergia ruderalis

Lindenbergia ruderalis, commonly known as the coastal morning glory, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along the shorelines. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are usually blue or violet in color, which bloom in the morning and close in the afternoon. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Lindenbergia ruderalis is adapted to salt spray and can tolerate harsh coastal conditions.
