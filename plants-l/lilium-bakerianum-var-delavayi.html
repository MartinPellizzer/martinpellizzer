Lilium bakerianum var. delavayi

Lilium bakerianum var. delavayi, commonly known as the Delavay's lily, is a species of perennial herbaceous plant native to the high altitudes of western China.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink to red. The plant features long, grass-like leaves and produces tall, slender stems that support its vibrant, trumpet-shaped blossoms. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
