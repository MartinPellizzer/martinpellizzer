Litobrochia decurrens

Litobrochia decurrens, commonly known as the creeping brochobranch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, often in coastal or arid regions. The plant features a low-growing, spreading habit with slender, hairy stems that root at the nodes. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds.

Litobrochia decurrens is adapted to withstand drought and salt spray, making it a resilient species in challenging environments.
