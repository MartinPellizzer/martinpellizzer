Lupinus laxiflorus var. lyleianus

Lupinus laxiflorus var. lyleianus, commonly known as the Lyle's lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and clusters of pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Lupinus laxiflorus var. lyleianus plays a role in nitrogen fixation and is of interest in ecological and botanical studies.
