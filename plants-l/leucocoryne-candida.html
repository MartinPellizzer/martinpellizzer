Leucocoryne candida

Leucocoryne candida, commonly known as the white leucocoryne, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features strap-like, evergreen leaves that are often used in ornamental gardening. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate sunlight to flourish.

Due to its unique appearance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and as a potted plant.
