Lupinus paruroensis

Lupinus paruroensis, commonly known as the Paruro lupine, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. Lupinus paruroensis is adapted to high-altitude environments and is often found in rocky or grassland habitats.

Its ecological importance includes nitrogen fixation, which enriches the soil in its native range.
