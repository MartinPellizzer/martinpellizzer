Lysimachia japonica subsp. papuana

Lysimachia japonica subsp.

papuana, commonly known as the Papuan goldenthread, is a perennial herb native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.

The plant features small, opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and produces clusters of yellow flowers in the spring. Its flowering stems are usually slender and branched, with the flowers arranged in racemes.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
