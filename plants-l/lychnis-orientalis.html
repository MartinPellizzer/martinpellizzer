Lychnis orientalis

Lychnis orientalis, commonly known as the oriental pink, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 feet, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, and it produces small, dry fruits called capsules.

Lychnis orientalis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
