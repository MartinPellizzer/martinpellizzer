Lythrum virgultosum

Lythrum virgultosum, commonly known as narrow-leaved catchfly, is a herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lythraceae family, which includes other flowering plants such as poinsettias and willows. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats like wet meadows and stream banks. It features slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves, with showy pink to red flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support pollinators.
