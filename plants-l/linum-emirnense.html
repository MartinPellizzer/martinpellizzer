Linum emirnense

Linum emirnense, commonly known as the Emirnense flax, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and delicate, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats and is known for its small, linear leaves. Its seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil.

Linum emirnense is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
