Lythrum portula var. longidentatum

Lythrum portula var. longidentatum, commonly known as the long-toothed lythrum, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lythraceae family, which includes plants known for their showy flowers and ecological importance. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with serrated margins, and its spikes of pink to purple flowers bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variety is distinguished by its elongated, tooth-like lobes on the leaves, giving it its specific epithet.
