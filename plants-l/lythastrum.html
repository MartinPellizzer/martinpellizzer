Lythastrum

Lythastrum, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is native to various regions of the Americas and is characterized by its showy, often violet or purple flowers. The plants typically have hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and they produce small, spiky seed heads that persist through the winter. Lythastrum species are often cultivated for their ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

They thrive in full sun and well-drained soil, making them suitable for gardens and natural landscapes.
