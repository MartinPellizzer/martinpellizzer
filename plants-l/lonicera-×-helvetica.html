Lonicera × helvetica

Lonicera × helvetica, commonly known as the Dutch honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe.

It features opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are typically elliptical to ovate in shape. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are usually yellow or orange, appearing in clusters. Its berries are small, red, and edible, though they are not as commonly consumed as other honeysuckle fruits.

Lonicera × helvetica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
