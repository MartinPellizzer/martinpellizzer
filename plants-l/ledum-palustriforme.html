Ledum palustriforme

Ledum palustriforme, commonly known as swamp laburnum, is a flowering plant native to northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in wet, acidic soils and is often found in bogs and swamps. Its small, leathery leaves and clusters of pendulous yellow blossoms make it distinctive in its habitat.

It plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators and shelter for small wildlife.
