Lupinus concinnus subsp. concinnus

Lupinus concinnus subsp.

concinnus, commonly known as the slender lupine, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets. Lupinus concinnus subsp.

concinnus plays an important role in soil stabilization and is often found in coastal and inland desert habitats.
