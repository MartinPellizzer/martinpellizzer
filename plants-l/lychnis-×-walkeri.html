Lychnis × walkeri

Lychnis × walkeri, commonly known as the garden erythronium, is a hybrid perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the carnation family (Caryophyllaceae) and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–30 cm, featuring lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its blooming period occurs in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to shaded landscapes.
