Lupinus poopoensis

Lupinus poopoensis, commonly known as Poopo lupine, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes, particularly in Bolivia and Peru.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and clusters of blue or purple flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. Lupinus poopoensis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional agriculture.
