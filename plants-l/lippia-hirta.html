Lippia hirta

Lippia hirta, commonly known as Mexican mint or wild mint, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its aromatic, aromatic leaves and small white to purple flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Lippia hirta is often used in traditional medicine for its purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.

Its creeping growth habit makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ground cover.
