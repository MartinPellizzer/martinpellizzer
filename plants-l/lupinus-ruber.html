Lupinus ruber

Lupinus ruber, commonly known as the scarlet lupin, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of bright red or orange flowers. The plant has palmately compound leaves with three to seven leaflets, and its root system includes nitrogen-fixing nodules. Lupinus ruber thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
