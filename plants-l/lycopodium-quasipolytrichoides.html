Lycopodium quasipolytrichoides

Lycopodium quasipolytrichoides, commonly known as the Quasi-polytrichoid moss, is a species of moss belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its small, erect, and often branching stems covered with leaf-like structures called microphylls. The plant exhibits a simple yet efficient structure, with leaves that are usually linear and arranged in two rows. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in temperate regions.

This moss plays a role in soil stabilization and nutrient cycling in its habitat.
