Lilium dauricum var. tigrinum

Lilium dauricum var. tigrinum, commonly known as the tiger lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically orange or red with a black or brown throat, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant has slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often marked with dark veins. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

The bulbs are edible when young and have a mild, sweet flavor.
