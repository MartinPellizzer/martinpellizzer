Linaria supina subsp. haenseleri

Linaria supina subsp.

haenseleri, commonly known as the prostrate toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is a low-growing perennial species that typically forms dense mats of foliage.

The plant produces bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

It is often found in rocky or coastal areas, where it demonstrates adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
