Linaria canadensis var. texana

Linaria canadensis var. texana, commonly known as Texas toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairies. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and it has a fibrous root system.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
