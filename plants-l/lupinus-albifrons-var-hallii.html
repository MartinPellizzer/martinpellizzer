Lupinus albifrons var. hallii

Lupinus albifrons var. hallii, commonly known as the Hall's white lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and clusters of white to pale pink flowers. The plant has pinnately compound leaves and produces cylindrical pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Lupinus albifrons var. hallii plays a role in nitrogen fixation and is of interest for its potential in ecological restoration and forage production.
