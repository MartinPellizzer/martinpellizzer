Lupinus lutescens

Lupinus lutescens, commonly known as the yellow lupine, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often in rocky soils. Its root system forms symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.

Lupinus lutescens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
