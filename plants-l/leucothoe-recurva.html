Leucothoe recurva

Leucothoe recurva, commonly known as silverleaf honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that turn reddish-brown in the fall, and it produces small, fragrant white flowers in the spring. The plant is characterized by its erect, branching structure and is often found in moist, woodland habitats. It has showy red berries that persist into winter, providing food for birds.

Leucothoe recurva is valued in landscaping for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
