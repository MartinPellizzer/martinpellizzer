Lonchocarpus speciosus

Lonchocarpus speciosus, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a tropical legume native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers and compound leaves, which are typical of the Fabaceae family. The plant produces distinctive pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its growth habit includes both tree and shrub forms, making it adaptable to various landscape settings.
