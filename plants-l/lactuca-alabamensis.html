Lactuca alabamensis

Lactuca alabamensis, commonly known as Alabama lettuce, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 feet, with lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and have a slightly bitter taste. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in the summer, producing small seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Lactuca alabamensis is often found in moist, open habitats such as fields and woodland edges.
