Leucas javanica var. littoralis

Leucas javanica var. littoralis, commonly known as the coastal leucas, is a perennial herb native to coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It thrives in sandy or muddy soils along shorelines, exhibiting salt tolerance and adaptability to tidal fluctuations. The plant features slender, branched stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowering period typically occurs during the warmer months, producing clusters of pale blue to purple flowers.

This variant is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial species.
