Lavandula viridis

Lavandula viridis, commonly known as green lavender, is a species of flowering plant in the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils and sunny locations. The plant produces small, fragrant blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its narrow, lance-shaped leaves are typically a deep green, giving the plant its common name.

Lavandula viridis is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is also valued for its essential oils in aromatherapy and herbal medicine.
