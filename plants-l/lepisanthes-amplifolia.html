Lepisanthes amplifolia

Lepisanthes amplifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf piper, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by oval, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its unique foliage and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
