Loxocarpus longipetiolatus

Loxocarpus longipetiolatus, commonly known as the long-petioled sea bean, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody, climbing habit. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, and its leaves are compound, typically with several pairs of leaflets.

It thrives in sandy or muddy soils along tidal zones and is adapted to withstand saline conditions.
