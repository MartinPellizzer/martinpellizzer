Lippia rotundifolia var. bahiensis

Lippia rotundifolia var. bahiensis, commonly known as Bahian mint, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of South America.

It features aromatic, slender stems and opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale blue, arranged in terminal or axillary clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional remedies for its antiseptic and digestive benefits.
