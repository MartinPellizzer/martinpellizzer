Launaea obtusa

Launaea obtusa, commonly known as the broadleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, the Middle East, and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant has a sprawling growth habit with fleshy, succulent leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically yellow and bloom in clusters, producing small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Launaea obtusa is adapted to drought conditions, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.
