Lysimachia limprichtii

Lysimachia limprichtii, commonly known as the Chinese chickweed, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its small, white or pinkish flowers and heart-shaped leaves. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. It has a low-growing habit and produces numerous stolons, allowing it to spread and form dense mats.

Lysimachia limprichtii is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
