Lactuca wildemaniana

Lactuca wildemaniana, commonly known as Wildeman's lettuce, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family, Asteraceae.

It is native to southern Europe and western Asia, often found in grassy areas and along roadsides. The plant has alternate, serrated leaves that are typically hairy, and it produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a composite head. It is an annual or biennial herb that grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters.

The latex sap of this plant is milky and can be used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory properties.
