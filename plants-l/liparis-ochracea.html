Liparis ochracea

Liparis ochracea, commonly known as the yellow lady's slipper, is a species of orchid native to parts of Asia and North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow, pouch-like flower that resembles a slipper, which is a key feature in its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette.

Liparis ochracea is known for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
