Lactuca canescens

Lactuca canescens, commonly known as hoary莴苣, is a flowering plant in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is native to temperate regions of Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. The plant has silver-gray leaves covered in fine hairs, which give it its common name. It produces small, yellow flowers arranged in composite heads, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Lactuca canescens is sometimes considered a weed but can also be used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
