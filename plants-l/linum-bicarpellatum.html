Linum bicarpellatum

Linum bicarpellatum, commonly known as the two-chambered flax, is a species of flowering plant in the family Linaceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically found in grassy areas and along roadsides. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The seeds of Linum bicarpellatum are small and contain a high amount of oil, making them valuable for both culinary and industrial uses.
