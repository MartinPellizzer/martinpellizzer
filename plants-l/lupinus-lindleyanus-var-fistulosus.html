Lupinus lindleyanus var. fistulosus

Lupinus lindleyanus var. fistulosus, commonly known as the hollowstem lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that are often hollow, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of blue to violet pea-like flowers, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

This species is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodland and chaparral habitats.
