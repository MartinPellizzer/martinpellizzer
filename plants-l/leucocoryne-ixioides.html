Leucocoryne ixioides

Leucocoryne ixioides, commonly known as the silver leaf lily, is a bulbous plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It features a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery sheen, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate watering, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
