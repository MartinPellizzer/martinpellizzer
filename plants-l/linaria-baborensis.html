Linaria baborensis

Linaria baborensis, commonly known as the Baborensis toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its slender, branching stems support clusters of small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Linaria baborensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its colorful display.
