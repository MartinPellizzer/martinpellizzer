Lathyrus niger f. protensus

Lathyrus niger f. protensus, commonly known as the black vetch, is a variant of the black vetch species native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial legume characterized by its dark purple to black flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in grasslands and wastelands. It produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are adapted for dispersal by wind.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in soil nitrogen fixation.
