Liparis dolabella

Liparis dolabella, commonly known as the Dolabella's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, grass-like foliage and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with a distinctive lip that is often adorned with intricate patterns.

Liparis dolabella is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
