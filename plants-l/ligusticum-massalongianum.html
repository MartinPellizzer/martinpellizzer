Ligusticum massalongianum

Ligusticum massalongianum, commonly known as the Massalongo lovage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescences. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer. It is often cultivated for its medicinal properties and as an ornamental plant.

The roots and leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported digestive and anti-inflammatory benefits.
