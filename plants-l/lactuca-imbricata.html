Lactuca imbricata

Lactuca imbricata, commonly known as the fringed lettuce, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its hairy, lobed leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, elongated fruits that contain numerous seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Lactuca imbricata is considered an ornamental plant due to its distinctive appearance.
