Lourya campanulata

Lourya campanulata, commonly known as the blue bell flower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, particularly India and Nepal.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem, while the flowers are often clustered in racemes.

Lourya campanulata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
