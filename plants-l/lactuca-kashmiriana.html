Lactuca kashmiriana

Lactuca kashmiriana, commonly known as Kashmir endive, is a species of lettuce native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is a biennial plant characterized by its rosette of broad, wavy, and sometimes curly leaves. The plant produces a tall, branching stem with small, green flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are often used in traditional cuisine, particularly in South Asian countries.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and other composite flowers.
