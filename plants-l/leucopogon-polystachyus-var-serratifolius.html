Leucopogon polystachyus var. serratifolius

Leucopogon polystachyus var. serratifolius, commonly known as the serrated-leaved heath, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The leaves are narrow, serrated, and covered in fine, white hairs. It produces small, bell-shaped flowers in clusters, typically pink or white, during the spring and summer.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.
