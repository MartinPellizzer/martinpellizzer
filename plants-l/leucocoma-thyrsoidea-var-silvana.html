Leucocoma thyrsoidea var. silvana

Leucocoma thyrsoidea var. silvana, commonly known as the silvan thyrsoide, is a lesser-known variant of the thyrsoide species.

It is characterized by its delicate, white to pale green foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in early spring. This plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as woodland floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are often covered in a fine, waxy coating, which helps retain moisture in humid conditions.

The plant is relatively low maintenance but requires well-drained soil and partial to full shade for optimal growth.
