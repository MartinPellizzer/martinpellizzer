Lavandula latifolia var. vulgaris

Lavandula latifolia var. vulgaris, commonly known as English lavender, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, spike-like inflorescences composed of purple to pinkish-blue flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped, gray-green, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal use.

The plant is valued for its essential oils, which are used in aromatherapy, perfumery, and traditional medicine.
