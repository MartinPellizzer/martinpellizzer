Lagerstroemia indica var. grafii

Lagerstroemia indica var. grafii, commonly known as the 'Grafii' crepe myrtle, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern China.

It is characterized by its showy, long-lasting flowers that range from pink to red, appearing in summer. The plant has smooth, mottled bark that becomes flaky with age, adding visual interest throughout the year. Its lance-shaped leaves are dark green and turn reddish in the fall.

It is well-suited for warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
