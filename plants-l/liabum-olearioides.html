Liabum olearioides

Liabum olearioides, commonly known as the African butterbean, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, bean-like pods that contain edible seeds, which are rich in nutrients. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and savanna habitats.

Due to its adaptability and nutritional value, it is both ecologically and agriculturally significant in its native region.
