Leptospermum acutifolium

Leptospermum acutifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tea tree, is a shrub native to Tasmania and southern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces a resinous sap that has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various native wildlife species.
