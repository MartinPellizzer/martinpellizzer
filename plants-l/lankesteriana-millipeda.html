Lankesteriana millipeda

Lankesteriana millipeda, commonly known as the giant spider orchid, is a striking orchid native to the cloud forests of Colombia.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence that resembles a spider, with long, slender petals and a distinctive, elongated lip. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a dark red or purple base.

Lankesteriana millipeda is a rare and endangered species, making it a subject of interest for conservation efforts.
