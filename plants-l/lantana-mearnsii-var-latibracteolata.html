Lantana mearnsii var. latibracteolata

Lantana mearnsii var. latibracteolata, commonly known as the broad-bract lantana, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring dense, aromatic foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are usually yellow or orange in color, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The plant is known for its rapid growth and adaptability to different soil types, making it a common ornamental species in warm climates.
