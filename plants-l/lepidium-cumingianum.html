Lepidium cumingianum

Lepidium cumingianum, commonly known as the Cuming's cress, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains in South America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many edible and economically important plants. The plant produces small, white or yellow flowers and has a compact, herbaceous growth habit. Its leaves are typically alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

Lepidium cumingianum is often found in moist, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
