Lepidium austrinum var. orbiculare

Lepidium austrinum var. orbiculare, commonly known as the roundleaf peppergrass, is a flowering plant native to parts of Australia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress plants. The plant typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, often in disturbed or open habitats. Its small, white flowers are arranged in racemes, and the leaves are alternate, with a slightly hairy texture.

The varietal name "orbiculare" refers to its round or orb-like leaves, distinguishing it from other varieties of Lepidium.
