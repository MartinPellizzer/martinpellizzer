Landolphia robusta

Landolphia robusta, commonly known as the giant African morning glory, is a climbing shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces fleshy, berry-like fruits that are typically red or orange when mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Landolphia robusta is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in some African cultures.
