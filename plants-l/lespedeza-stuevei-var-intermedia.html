Lespedeza stuevei var. intermedia

Lespedeza stuevei var. intermedia, commonly known as the eastern lespezeza, is a perennial legume native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils in woodlands and along stream banks. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, pink to reddish-purple flowers in late summer. Its seeds are encased in hairy pods and are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for wildlife.
