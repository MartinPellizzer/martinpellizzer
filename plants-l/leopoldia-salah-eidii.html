Leopoldia salah-eidii

Leopoldia salah-eidii, commonly known as the Sudanese orchid, is a rare and unique species native to the arid regions of Sudan.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, often colorful flowers. The plant typically features a single, upright stem with a few leaves and produces small, delicate blooms that are highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species, making it a subject of conservation interest.

Its botanical significance lies in its adaptation to extreme desert conditions and its role in the biodiversity of its native region.
