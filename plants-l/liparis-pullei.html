Liparis pullei

Liparis pullei, commonly known as the purple lady's tresses, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, spiral arrangement of pale purple to pink flowers along a flowering stem. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a rosette at the base.

Liparis pullei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
