Luzula campestris luteola

Luzula campestris luteola, commonly known as the yellow woodrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the grass family (Poaceae).

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves that are typically bright green to yellowish in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are borne on slender panicles during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks, often forming dense colonies.

Luzula campestris luteola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and ecological benefits.
