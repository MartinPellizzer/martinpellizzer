Lonchocarpus margaritensis

Lonchocarpus margaritensis, commonly known as the pearlpod senna, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow flowers. The plant produces pods that are inflated and contain seeds with a hard, glossy coating. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal uses, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and research settings.
