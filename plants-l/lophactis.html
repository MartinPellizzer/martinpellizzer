Lophactis

Lophactis, commonly known as the golden ragwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Lophactis has alternate, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

It is native to parts of Europe and Asia and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
