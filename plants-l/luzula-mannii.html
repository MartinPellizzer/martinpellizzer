Luzula mannii

Luzula mannii, commonly known as the common wood rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the grass family (Poaceae).

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing pale green to brownish flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Luzula mannii is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant landscaping.
