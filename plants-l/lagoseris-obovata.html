Lagoseris obovata

Lagoseris obovata, commonly known as the broad-ovate lagoseris, is a flowering plant native to regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and forest edges. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and its flowers are small and yellowish-white in color.

Lagoseris obovata is valued for its ornamental potential and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
