Lepanthes erythrocles

Lepanthes erythrocles, commonly known as the red-stemmed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, often clustered in a raceme. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is typically found in tropical forests.

Lepanthes erythrocles is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
