Leucanthemum vulgare var. amplifolium

Leucanthemum vulgare var. amplifolium, commonly known as the Shasta daisy, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its large, white ray florets surrounding a central cluster of yellow disc florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its showy flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a bright, cheerful presence to gardens.
