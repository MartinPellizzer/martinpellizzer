Lycopodioides hookeri

Lycopodioides hookeri, commonly known as the Hooker's clubmoss, is a small evergreen plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and produces spore-bearing structures called strobili. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its leaves are narrow and spirally arranged, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Lycopodioides hookeri plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
