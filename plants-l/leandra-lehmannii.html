Leandra lehmannii

Leandra lehmannii, commonly known as the Lehmann's tree fern, is a striking evergreen tree fern native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feathery fronds that can reach lengths of up to 3 meters. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture and well-draining soil. Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in tropical and subtropical climates.

Leandra lehmannii plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and shelter for various forest organisms.
