Loeseneriella concinna

Loeseneriella concinna, commonly known as the slender sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It is characterized by its small, fleshy leaves and clusters of tiny, white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to cold, harsh environments. Its growth habit is prostrate, allowing it to spread across the ground and form dense mats.

Loeseneriella concinna plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats and is often found in tundra and mountainous areas.
