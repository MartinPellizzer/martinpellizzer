Litobrochia papyracea

Litobrochia papyracea, commonly known as the paper-leaf brochidium, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems that are covered in a waxy, pale green cuticle, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and the plant stores water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Litobrochia papyracea is well adapted to survive in harsh desert environments with minimal rainfall.
