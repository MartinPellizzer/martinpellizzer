Lycaste bruncana

Lycaste bruncana, commonly known as the Brazilian night-blooming orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom at night. The plant has a pseudobulb structure typical of many orchids, which stores water and nutrients. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, often arranged in a fan shape.

Lycaste bruncana prefers warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
