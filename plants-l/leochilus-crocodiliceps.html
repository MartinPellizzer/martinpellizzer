Leochilus crocodiliceps

Leochilus crocodiliceps, commonly known as the crocodile-head orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated flowers that resemble a crocodile's head, with intricate patterns and vibrant colors. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in tropical or subtropical regions. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
