Lycium berlandieri subvar. brachyanthum

Lycium berlandieri subvar. brachyanthum, commonly known as the Sonoran desert honeysuckle, is a shrub native to the Sonoran Desert.

It is characterized by its small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has small, elliptical leaves and produces red berries that are edible for both humans and animals. It thrives in arid environments and is well adapted to withstand high temperatures and limited water availability.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing food and shelter for various desert wildlife.
