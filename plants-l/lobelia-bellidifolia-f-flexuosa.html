Lobelia bellidifolia f. flexuosa

Lobelia bellidifolia f. flexuosa, commonly known as the flexible lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb with slender, branching stems that often grow in a prostrate or ascending manner. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, arranged in clusters at the tips of the stems. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic in shape, and often have a slightly hairy surface.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
