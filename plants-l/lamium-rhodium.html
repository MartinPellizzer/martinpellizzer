Lamium rhodium

Lamium rhodium, commonly known as silverleaf deadnettle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive silvery underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, two-lipped flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its attractive foliage and flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
