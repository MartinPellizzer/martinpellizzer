Licuala amplifrons

Licuala amplifrons, commonly known as the broad-leaf pandanus, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palm-like leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant has a robust, woody stem with a fibrous trunk, typical of the Arecaceae family. Its leaves are usually fan-shaped, with a waxy surface and a distinctive, often colorful, midrib.

Licuala amplifrons is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional crafts and as a source of thatching material.
