Lathyrus aphaca var. modestus

Lathyrus aphaca var. modestus, commonly known as the dwarf sweet pea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces clusters of fragrant, pea-like flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white. The plant has slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it both visually appealing and ecologically beneficial to pollinators.
