Lepanthes carvii

Lepanthes carvii, commonly known as the Carvii orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as cloud forests, where it attaches itself to trees or rocks. Its flowers are usually small, with intricate patterns and colors ranging from white to pale pink.

Lepanthes carvii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
