Lindelofia cerinthoides

Lindelofia cerinthoides, commonly known as the red-tipped nettle, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes other stinging nettles. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with reddish veins and a dense covering of fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikes, typically greenish-white in color.

Lindelofia cerinthoides is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.
