Lippia

Lippia, commonly known as Mexican mint or winter savory, is a genus of flowering plants in the mint family, Lamiaceae.

These plants are native to the Americas and are characterized by their aromatic, often mint-scented leaves. Lippia species typically have square stems, opposite leaves, and small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. Many species are cultivated for their culinary and medicinal uses, including as a seasoning or for digestive health.

The genus includes over 100 species, with Lippia alba and Lippia dulcis being among the most widely recognized.
