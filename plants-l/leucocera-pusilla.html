Leucocera pusilla

Leucocera pusilla, commonly known as the small white moth.

This small moth species belongs to the family Noctuidae and is characterized by its pale, almost translucent wings. It is typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it feeds on nectar from various flowering plants. The larvae of Leucocera pusilla are often herbivorous, feeding on leaves of plants in the families Asteraceae and Fabaceae.

Its life cycle is closely tied to the seasonal availability of its host plants, making it an important indicator species for ecosystem health.
