Lappula revoluta

Lappula revoluta, commonly known as the revolute lappula, is a flowering plant in the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on short pedicels. Lappula revoluta is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

It is an annual or biennial plant that thrives in temperate climates.
