Lilium japonicum var. colchesteri

Lilium japonicum var. colchesteri, commonly known as the Colchester lily, is a rare and ornamental plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer, producing pink to white flowers with a subtle fragrance. It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Due to its rarity, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
