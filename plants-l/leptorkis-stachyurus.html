Leptorkis stachyurus

Leptorkis stachyurus, commonly known as the Chinese honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. It produces small, red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Leptorkis stachyurus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional Chinese medicine.
