Lonicera pubescens

Lonicera pubescens, commonly known as the hairy honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are pubescent, giving the plant its common name. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, blooming in early spring. Its berries are red and edible, often consumed by birds.

Lonicera pubescens is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
