Lithops verruculosa

Lithops verruculosa, commonly known as the "pebble plant," is a succulent native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, stone-like leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in arid environments. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are typically green with brown or purple markings, resembling rocks.

Lithops verruculosa is popular among cactus and succulent enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
