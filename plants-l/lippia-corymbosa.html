Lippia corymbosa

Lippia corymbosa, commonly known as wild mint or Mexican mint, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its opposite, hairy leaves and small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Lippia corymbosa is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
