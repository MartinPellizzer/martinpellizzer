Lobelia hypoleuca

Lobelia hypoleuca, commonly known as the white cardinalflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink to white in color, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant grows in moist to wet habitats, including swamps, bogs, and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Lobelia hypoleuca is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
