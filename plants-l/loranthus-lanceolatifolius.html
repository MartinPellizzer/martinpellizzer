Loranthus lanceolatifolius

Loranthus lanceolatifolius, commonly known as the lanceolate mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to host trees through its haustoria, drawing water and nutrients from the host.

It is native to regions of Africa, particularly in countries like Kenya and Tanzania, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along its stems. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are often clustered together, followed by small, berry-like fruits.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can have a significant impact on the health of its host trees if left unchecked.
