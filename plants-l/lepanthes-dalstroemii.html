Lepanthes dalstroemii

Lepanthes dalstroemii, commonly known as the Dalstrom's slipper orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish in color. The plant grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded forest environments. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Lepanthes dalstroemii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
