Lycium angustifolium

Lycium angustifolium, commonly known as the narrowleaf lycium, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It features small, narrow leaves that are typically arranged alternately along its stems. The plant produces yellowish-orange berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

Lycium angustifolium is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
