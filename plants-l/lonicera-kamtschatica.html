Lonicera kamtschatica

Lonicera kamtschatica, commonly known as the Kamchatka honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy on the underside. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers in clusters, usually yellow or orange, which bloom in early summer. Its berries are red and edible, often attracting birds and other wildlife.

Lonicera kamtschatica is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
