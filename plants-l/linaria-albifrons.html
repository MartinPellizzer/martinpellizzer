Linaria albifrons

Linaria albifrons, commonly known as the white-fronted toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and bright yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, elongated seed pods that release tiny seeds when mature. Linaria albifrons thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
