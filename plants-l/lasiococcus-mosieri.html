Lasiococcus mosieri

Lasiococcus mosieri, commonly known as the silverleaf mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that primarily grows on the branches of eucalyptus trees.

It has small, scale-like leaves that are covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that are often clustered together, followed by tiny, berry-like fruits. It plays a role in the ecosystem by providing food for certain birds and insects.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can weaken its host tree if left unchecked.
