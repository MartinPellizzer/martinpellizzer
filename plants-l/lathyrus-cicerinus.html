Lathyrus cicerinus

Lathyrus cicerinus, commonly known as the sweet pea, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with slender stems that twine around supports. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pea-like flowers in a range of colors, including pink, purple, and white. Its leaves are pinnately compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

Lathyrus cicerinus is native to the Mediterranean region and is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in horticulture.
