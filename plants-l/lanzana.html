Lanzana

Lanzana, commonly known as the cherry laurel, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically eaten raw or used in culinary applications. Lanzana thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
