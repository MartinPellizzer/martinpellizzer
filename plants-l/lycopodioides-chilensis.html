Lycopodioides chilensis

Lycopodioides chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean clubmoss, is a species of lycophyte native to South America, particularly Chile.

It belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae and is characterized by its small, spore-bearing cones and scale-like leaves. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its reproductive structures are adapted for dispersal via wind, which is typical for many spore-producing plants.

Lycopodioides chilensis plays a role in maintaining soil stability and providing habitat for small invertebrates in its native ecosystem.
