Linaria concolor var. serpens

Linaria concolor var. serpens, commonly known as the snake's head flower, is a striking annual plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branching stems that produce clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a linear shape that gives the plant its common name. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
