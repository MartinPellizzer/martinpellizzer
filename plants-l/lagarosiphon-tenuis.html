Lagarosiphon tenuis

Lagarosiphon tenuis, commonly known as the slender water plantain, is a small aquatic plant native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and lakes. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are often submerged, with small white flowers that emerge above the water surface. It is characterized by its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in nutrient-rich waters.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions, outcompeting native aquatic vegetation.
