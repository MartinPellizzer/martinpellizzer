Lepanthes lilijae

Lepanthes lilijae, commonly known as the Lilijae orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually small, with intricate patterns and a wide range of colors, including white, pink, and purple.

Lepanthes lilijae is valued for its ornamental appeal and contributes to the biodiversity of its native ecosystems.
