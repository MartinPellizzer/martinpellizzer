Leptosiphon ciliatus subsp. ciliatus

Leptosiphon ciliatus subsp.

ciliatus, commonly known as the silky bluebell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States. It features slender, branching stems that produce clusters of small, blue to purple flowers.

The leaves are narrow, linear, and often covered with fine, silky hairs, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, typically found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
