Leucadendron pubibracteolatum

Leucadendron pubibracteolatum, commonly known as the silver leaf leucadendron, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to South Africa, specifically the Western Cape province, where it grows in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant is characterized by its silver-gray, hairy leaves and conical, yellowish-orange flower heads. It is often found in fynbos vegetation, which is a unique biome known for its biodiversity.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking foliage and seasonal blooms.
