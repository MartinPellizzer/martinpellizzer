Limniris brevicaulis

Limniris brevicaulis, commonly known as the short-stemmed water plant, is a species of aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It typically grows in shallow, still waters such as ponds and wetlands, where it thrives in muddy or sandy substrates. The plant has slender, branching stems that are often only a few centimeters tall, with small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually less than 1 inch in length. Its flowers are tiny and inconspicuous, typically greenish-white in color, and it reproduces both sexually and asexually through rhizomes.

Limniris brevicaulis plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and amphibians.
