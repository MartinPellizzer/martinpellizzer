Lychnis dioica var. diurna

Lychnis dioica var. diurna, commonly known as the evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that open in the evening and close by morning. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oily seeds.

The species is known for its medicinal properties, particularly due to the high content of gamma-linolenic acid in its seeds.
