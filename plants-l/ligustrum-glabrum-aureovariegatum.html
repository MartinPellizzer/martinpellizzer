Ligustrum glabrum aureovariegatum

Ligustrum glabrum aureovariegatum, commonly known as the golden variegated privet, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with distinctive yellow or white variegation. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters during the spring. Its berries are dark blue and edible, though often consumed by birds.

It is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various soil types.
