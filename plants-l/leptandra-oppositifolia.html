Leptandra oppositifolia

Leptandra oppositifolia, commonly known as the oppositeleaf false indigo, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, blue-violet flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by narrow, elongated seed pods. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and native plant gardens.
