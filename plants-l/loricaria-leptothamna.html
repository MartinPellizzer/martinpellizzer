Loricaria leptothamna

Loricaria leptothamna, commonly known as the slender-leaf water lily, is a species of aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves that provide shade and habitat for aquatic life. The plant has a distinctive white flower that blooms above the water surface, typically during the warmer months. Its roots anchor in the muddy substrate, while its rhizomes spread horizontally to form dense colonies.

Loricaria leptothamna plays an important role in maintaining water quality and supporting local ecosystems.
