Lithospermum arvense var. punctatum

Lithospermum arvense var. punctatum, commonly known as spotted knawel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. The plant has distinctive, hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often spotted with purple or reddish markings. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in meadows, wastelands, and along roadsides.

The roots of this plant contain compounds that have been used in traditional medicine for their potential antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.
