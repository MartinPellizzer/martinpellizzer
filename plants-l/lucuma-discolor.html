Lucuma discolor

Lucuma discolor, commonly known as the white lucuma, is a tropical plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is closely related to the common lucuma (Lucuma lucuma). The plant produces large, yellowish-orange fruits that are covered in a hairy, waxy coating. The fruit's pulp is rich in nutrients, including carbohydrates, fiber, and various minerals.

It is valued both for its culinary uses and its ornamental appeal in gardens.
