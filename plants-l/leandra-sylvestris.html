Leandra sylvestris

Leandra sylvestris, commonly known as the wild leandra, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its attractive, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and a mildly aromatic scent. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
