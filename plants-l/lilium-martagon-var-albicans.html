Lilium martagon var. albicans

Lilium martagon var. albicans, commonly known as the white martagon lily, is a striking perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive, recurved petal structure that gives the plant its common name. It produces oval, brown seeds within a capsule and prefers well-drained, fertile soil in full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
