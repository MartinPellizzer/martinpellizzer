Lobelia pedunculata

Lobelia pedunculata, commonly known as the slender lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear whorled clusters of small, tubular blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Lobelia pedunculata is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
