Liatris novae-angliae f. albiflora

Liatris novae-angliae f. albiflora, commonly known as the whiteflower blazing star, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features tall, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, white to pale pink flower clusters. Its botanical structure supports pollination by bees and other insects, contributing to its ecological significance.

The cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
