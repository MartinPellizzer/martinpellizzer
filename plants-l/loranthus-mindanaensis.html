Loranthus mindanaensis

Loranthus mindanaensis, commonly known as the Mindanao mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant belonging to the Loranthaceae family.

It grows on various host trees, primarily in the Philippines, and relies on its host for water and nutrients while performing photosynthesis. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces yellowish-green flowers that are often clustered in small inflorescences. Its fruits are fleshy berries that contain seeds dispersed by birds.

Due to its parasitic nature and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
