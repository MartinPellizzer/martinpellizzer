Lobelia fatiscens

Lobelia fatiscens, commonly known as the showy lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Lobelia fatiscens is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and naturalized areas.
