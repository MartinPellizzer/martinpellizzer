Ligustridium japonicum

Ligustridium japonicum, commonly known as Japanese privet, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features opposite, simple, ovate leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant, white flowers in clustered inflorescences during the spring. These flowers are followed by small, dark blue to black berries that are edible for some bird species.

It is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
