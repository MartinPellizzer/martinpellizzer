Linum suffruticosum var. tegendense

Linum suffruticosum var. tegendense, commonly known as the Tegendens flax, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant has hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
