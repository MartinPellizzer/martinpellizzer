Lachenalia schlechteri

Lachenalia schlechteri, commonly known as the Schlechter's lachenalia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are typically blue-gray in color. The plant produces tall, branched stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
