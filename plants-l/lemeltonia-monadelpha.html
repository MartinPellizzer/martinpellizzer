Lemeltonia monadelpha

Lemeltonia monadelpha, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow petals that resemble small bells, which are arranged in clusters at the top of its slender, woody stems. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.
