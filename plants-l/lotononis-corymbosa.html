Lotononis corymbosa

Lotononis corymbosa, commonly known as the Natal grass or Natal wild indigo, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in grasslands and along riverbanks, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces pods that contain small, hard seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
