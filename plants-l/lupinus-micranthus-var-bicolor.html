Lupinus micranthus var. bicolor

Lupinus micranthus var. bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces tall spikes of pea-like flowers, typically in shades of pink and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
