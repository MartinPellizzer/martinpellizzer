Laurus culilawan

Laurus culilawan, commonly known as the Philippine laurel, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and aromatic scent. The plant typically grows in lowland to mid-elevation forests and can reach heights of up to 20 meters. Its flowers are small and clustered, followed by dark, glossy berries that are edible.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local medicine.
