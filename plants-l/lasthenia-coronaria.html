Lasthenia coronaria

Lasthenia coronaria, commonly known as the crownless common dandelion, is a flowering plant native to California.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into fluffy, white seed heads. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture.
