Lepanthes brownii

Lepanthes brownii, commonly known as the Brown's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid with a compact growth habit and produces delicate, showy flowers. The plant typically features green to reddish stems and possesses pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its flowers are often white or pale yellow with intricate patterns, and it blooms during the wet season.

Lepanthes brownii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
