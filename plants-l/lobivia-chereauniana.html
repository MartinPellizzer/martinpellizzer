Lobivia chereauniana

Lobivia chereauniana, commonly known as the Chereaunian Lobivia, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and ribbed structure. The plant typically features spiny areoles and produces yellow or orange flowers during the warmer months. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it stores water in its thick, fleshy stems.

This species is well adapted to harsh, dry environments and requires minimal watering to thrive.
