Lotodes jaubertianum

Lotodes jaubertianum, commonly known as the Jaubert's water lily, is a aquatic plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves that provide a habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant produces white flowers that bloom in the early morning and close during the heat of the day. Its roots are anchored in the mud at the bottom of still or slow-moving water bodies.

Lotodes jaubertianum plays an important role in water ecosystems by contributing to oxygenation and supporting biodiversity.
