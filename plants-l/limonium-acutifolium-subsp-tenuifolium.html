Limonium acutifolium subsp. tenuifolium

Limonium acutifolium subsp.

tenuifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean. It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and clusters of blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant thrives in sandy or saline soils and is often found in dune environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

Its ability to tolerate salt and wind makes it an important component of coastal ecosystems.
