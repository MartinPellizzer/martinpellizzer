Laportea humblotii

Laportea humblotii, commonly known as the yellow ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a distinctive, aromatic scent when crushed.

Laportea humblotii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
