Leptochloa falcata

Leptochloa falcata, commonly known as the hookleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, where it forms dense mats near the water's edge. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive hook-shaped tip, which gives it its scientific name. Its flowering stems are slender and often produce small, spike-like inflorescences.

Leptochloa falcata is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
