Lophopetalum curtisii

Lophopetalum curtisii, commonly known as the Mexican beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, bright purple berries that appear in the fall, which are a striking feature of the plant. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical, with a smooth texture and a glossy appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
