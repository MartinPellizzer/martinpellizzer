Lobelia urens var. longibracteata

Lobelia urens var. longibracteata, commonly known as the long-bract lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. The plant features slender stems and small, tubular flowers that are usually blue or purple in color. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is distinguished by its elongated bracts, which are modified leaves that surround the flower clusters.
