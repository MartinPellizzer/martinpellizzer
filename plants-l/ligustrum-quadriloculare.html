Ligustrum quadriloculare

Ligustrum quadriloculare, commonly known as the Chinese privet, is a shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, simple, and usually elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, followed by dark blue berries that are edible for birds. It is often used in landscaping due to its adaptability and dense growth habit.

Botanically, it belongs to the Oleaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like jasmine and lilac.
