Liliorhiza

Liliorhiza, commonly known as the white lotus, is a aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the surface of the water. Its roots are submerged, absorbing nutrients from the water.

Liliorhiza is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and has cultural significance in many Asian countries.
