Lyonia myrtilloides

Lyonia myrtilloides, commonly known as the dwarf blueberry, is a low-growing shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many acid-loving plants. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically blue or black in color. Its leaves are evergreen, leathery, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Lyonia myrtilloides thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and swampy areas.
