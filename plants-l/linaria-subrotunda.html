Linaria subrotunda

Linaria subrotunda, commonly known as the broadleaf toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers with a distinctive two-lipped structure. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Linaria subrotunda is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
