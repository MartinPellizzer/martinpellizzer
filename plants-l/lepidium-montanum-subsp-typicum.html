Lepidium montanum subsp. typicum

Lepidium montanum subsp.

typicum, commonly known as mountain cress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and cool climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are usually white or pale yellow. Lepidium montanum subsp.

typicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
