Lycium carolinianum

Lycium carolinianum, commonly known as the Carolina wolfberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red, fleshy berries that ripen in the summer. The plant has woody stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in woodland edges or disturbed areas.

The berries are edible and have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
