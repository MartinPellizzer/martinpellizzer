Lobivia hualfinensis var. fechseri

Lobivia hualfinensis var. fechseri, commonly known as the Fechser's Lobivia, is a compact, low-growing succulent native to the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It features short, cylindrical stems that often branch at the base, covered in dense, white to grayish-blue woolly hair. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are typically fleshy and succulent, adapted to store water in its arid habitat.

This variety is prized by collectors for its unique appearance and resilience in dry conditions.
