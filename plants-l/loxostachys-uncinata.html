Loxostachys uncinata

Loxostachys uncinata, commonly known as the hairy spiny grass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and hairy leaves, which help reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with bract-like structures that bear the flowering heads. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread vegetatively and stabilize soil in degraded landscapes.

Loxostachys uncinata is adapted to low rainfall and high temperatures, making it a resilient component of steppe and desert ecosystems.
