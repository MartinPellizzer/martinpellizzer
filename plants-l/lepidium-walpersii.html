Lepidium walpersii

Lepidium walpersii, commonly known as the alpine cress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are alternate, usually pinnately divided, and have a peppery aroma when crushed.

Lepidium walpersii is often found in meadows and along stream banks at high elevations.
