Lathyrus japonicus f. pubescens

Lathyrus japonicus f. pubescens, commonly known as the Japanese vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it the specific epithet *pubescens*. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is native to Japan and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
