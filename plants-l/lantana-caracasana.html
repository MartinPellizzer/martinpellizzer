Lantana caracasana

Lantana caracasana, commonly known as the yellow lantana, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, which bloom throughout the growing season. Its flowers are followed by small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

Lantana caracasana is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in full sun to partial shade.
