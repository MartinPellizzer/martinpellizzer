Lychnis flos-cuculi subsp. cyrilli

Lychnis flos-cuculi subsp.

cyrilli, commonly known as the Siberian campion, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of Asia. It features erect, slender stems that bear simple, alternate leaves with a slightly hairy texture.

The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers in clusters, typically blooming from June to August. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments.

This subspecies is distinguished by its smaller flowers and more compact growth compared to the main species.
