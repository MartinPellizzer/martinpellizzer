Lomatium dissectum var. multifidum

Lomatium dissectum var. multifidum, commonly known as the Pacific silverbush, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features finely dissected, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of silky white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, which are clustered in umbels. Its root system is fibrous and can be harvested for medicinal purposes.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
