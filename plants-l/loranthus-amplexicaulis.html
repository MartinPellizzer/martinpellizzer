Loranthus amplexicaulis

Loranthus amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clasping mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches itself to the branches of host trees, primarily in the genus Eucalyptus.

It lacks true roots and absorbs water and nutrients through its stems and leaves, which are embedded into the host's bark. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers, followed by red, fleshy berries that contain seeds. It is native to Australia and is often found in coastal regions where its host trees thrive.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can have a significant impact on the health of its host plants if left unchecked.
