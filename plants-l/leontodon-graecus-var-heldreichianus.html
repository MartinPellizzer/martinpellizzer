Leontodon graecus var. heldreichianus

Leontodon graecus var. heldreichianus, commonly known as the Balkan bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the daisy family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its distinctive blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. It has lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces and a compact growth habit.
