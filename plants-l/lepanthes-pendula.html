Lepanthes pendula

Lepanthes pendula, commonly known as the pendant orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous, showy flowers that often hang from the stems, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as forest understories, where it thrives in partial shade. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a distinctive, intricate pattern on the petals and sepals.

Lepanthes pendula is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental beauty in horticulture.
