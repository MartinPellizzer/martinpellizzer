Lithospermum worthingtonii

Lithospermum worthingtonii, commonly known as Wordington's rock pepper, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer, followed by dark red to black berries. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Lithospermum worthingtonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
