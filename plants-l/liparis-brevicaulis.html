Liparis brevicaulis

Liparis brevicaulis, commonly known as the short-stem orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant features a slender rhizome and produces small, greenish-white flowers with three sepals and three petals. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often found in association with other epiphytic orchids.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
