Lupinus rivularis var. barbatus

Lupinus rivularis var. barbatus, commonly known as the barbed lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, serrated leaflets, and its distinctive feature is the barbed bracts that surround the flower clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones and streambanks.

Lupinus rivularis var. barbatus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.
