Lunaria annua subsp. pachyrrhiza

Lunaria annua subsp.

pachyrrhiza, commonly known as the thick-ribbed moonflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants.

The plant is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that open at night, emitting a faint fragrance. Its fruit is a dry, saccate capsule that splits open to release seeds, a feature typical of the Brassicaceae family.

The leaves are alternate, pinnately lobed, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its unique botanical appearance.
