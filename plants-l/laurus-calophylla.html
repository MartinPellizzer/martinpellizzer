Laurus calophylla

Laurus calophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved laurel, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like laurel and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by edible, black berries.

Laurus calophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
