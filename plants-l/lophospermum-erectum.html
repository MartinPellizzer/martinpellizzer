Lophospermum erectum

Lophospermum erectum, commonly known as the evergreen morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped, waxy leaves. The plant produces vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often appearing in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Lophospermum erectum is valued for its rapid growth and ability to cover trellises or fences with its showy blooms.
