Lippia hederifolia

Lippia hederifolia, commonly known as Mexican mint or wild mint, is a perennial herb native to the Americas.

It features aromatic, serrated leaves that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale purple, arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially sunny environments.

Lippia hederifolia is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in herbal remedies.
