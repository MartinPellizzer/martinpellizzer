Lagenaria vulgaris f. gourda

Lagenaria vulgaris f. gourda, commonly known as the calabash gourd, is a variant of the calabash plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a fast-growing, annual vine that produces large, hard-shelled fruits that mature to a pale yellow or greenish color. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, requiring moderate water and warm temperatures for optimal growth. Its fruits are widely used in traditional crafts, cooking, and as containers due to their durability.

The plant's botanical features include heart-shaped leaves and a robust, branching stem that supports its vigorous growth.
