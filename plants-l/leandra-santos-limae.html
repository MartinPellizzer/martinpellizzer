Leandra santos-limae

Leandra santos-limae, commonly known as the Santos-Limae orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers with a complex structure typical of many orchids. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insects.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
