Lippia geminata var. suffruticosa

Lippia geminata var. suffruticosa, commonly known as Mexican blue sage, is a shrub-like perennial plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It features aromatic, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional herbal remedies. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem and a compact growth habit, which contribute to its ornamental value.
