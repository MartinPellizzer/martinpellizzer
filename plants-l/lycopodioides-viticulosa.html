Lycopodioides viticulosa

Lycopodioides viticulosa, commonly known as the vine-like clubmoss, is a member of the Lycopodiaceae family, characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and spore-producing structures.

It is a perennial evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests or rocky crevices. The plant has a rhizomatous stem system that allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense mats or small colonies. Its sporophyte phase is dominant, with the gametophyte stage being short-lived and dependent on the sporophyte for nutrition.

Lycopodioides viticulosa plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
