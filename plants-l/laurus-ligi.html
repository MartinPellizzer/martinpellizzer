Laurus ligi

Laurus ligi, commonly known as the bay laurel, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are aromatic and have a strong, spicy scent. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by dark, edible berries. Its leaves are widely used in cooking and traditional medicine for their aromatic and medicinal properties.

The bay laurel is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens.
