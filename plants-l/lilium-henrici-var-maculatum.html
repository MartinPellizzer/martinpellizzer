Lilium henrici var. maculatum

Lilium henrici var. maculatum, commonly known as the spotted lily, is a hybrid variety of the lily family (Liliaceae).

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and often feature distinctive red or purple spots. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of East Asia, particularly China, and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a rosette at the base.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
