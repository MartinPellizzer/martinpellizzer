Linum austriacum subsp. euxinum

Linum austriacum subsp.

euxinum, commonly known as the Crimean flax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas. It belongs to the Linaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in fiber production.

Its subspecies status highlights its distinct ecological and morphological traits compared to other members of the Linum genus.
