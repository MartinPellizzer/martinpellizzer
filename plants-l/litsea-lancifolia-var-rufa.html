Litsea lancifolia var. rufa

Litsea lancifolia var. rufa, commonly known as the red-leafed litsea, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Malaysia and Indonesia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows in tropical rainforests. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves, which are often reddish in color when young, giving it its common name. Its small, fragrant flowers are usually white or pale yellow, and it produces small, fleshy berries that are edible.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has also been used in traditional medicine for its aromatic properties.
