Loxostigma begoniifolium

Loxostigma begoniifolium, commonly known as the begonia-leaved loxostigma, is a unique species within the Loxostigma genus.

It is characterized by its striking, begonia-like leaves that are deeply lobed and often have a velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is native to parts of Southeast Asia.

Due to its ornamental foliage, it is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic value in botanical gardens.
