Lepidium hirtum subsp. oxyotum

Lepidium hirtum subsp.

oxyotum, commonly known as the hairy wallflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny locations. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is often found in alpine or subalpine regions, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
