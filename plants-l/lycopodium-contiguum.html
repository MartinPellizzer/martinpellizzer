Lycopodium contiguum

Lycopodium contiguum, commonly known as the clubmoss, is a species of vascular plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. The plant features spore-bearing cones called strobila, which are clustered at the tips of its stems. Its leaves are scale-like and arranged in whorls, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Lycopodium contiguum plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various small organisms.
