Leucothoe intermedia

Leucothoe intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate leatherleaf, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features glossy, dark green elliptical leaves that turn reddish-brown in the fall. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained, acidic soil.

Leucothoe intermedia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
