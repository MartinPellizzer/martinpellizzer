Lobelia appendiculata var. gattingeri

Lobelia appendiculata var. gattingeri, commonly known as the Gattinger's lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems covered with hairy foliage and produces clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other small insects.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
