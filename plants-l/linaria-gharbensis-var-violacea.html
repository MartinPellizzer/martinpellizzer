Linaria gharbensis var. violacea

Linaria gharbensis var. violacea, commonly known as the purple toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North Africa.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant purple or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its slender stems and lance-shaped leaves are covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
