Lycium senticosum

Lycium senticosum, commonly known as the Indian wolfberry or sandthorn, is a shrub native to the arid regions of South and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its spiny stems and yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are rich in antioxidants and nutrients. Its leaves are ovate and have a slightly hairy texture, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in dry soils.

Lycium senticosum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
