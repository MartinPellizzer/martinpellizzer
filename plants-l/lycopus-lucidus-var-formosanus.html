Lycopus lucidus var. formosanus

Lycopus lucidus var. formosanus, commonly known as the Formosan water horehound, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and wet meadows.

Its aromatic foliage and medicinal properties have made it of interest in traditional herbal practices.
