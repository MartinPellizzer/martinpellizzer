Litsea oblonga var. microphylla

Litsea oblonga var. microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved litsea, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, elliptical leaves that are typically less than 3 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by orange-red berries that are edible but often used in traditional medicine. Its aromatic foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The species is also valued for its potential in ecological restoration due to its adaptability to various soil types.
