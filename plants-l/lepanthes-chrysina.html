Lepanthes chrysina

Lepanthes chrysina, commonly known as the golden lepanthes, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its strikingly colorful, showy flowers that often feature a vibrant yellow or orange hue. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments such as cloud forests, where it thrives in the understory. Lepanthes chrysina has slender, erect pseudobulbs and linear leaves, and its flowers are often borne in clusters.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts worldwide.
