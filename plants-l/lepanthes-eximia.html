Lepanthes eximia

Lepanthes eximia, commonly known as the elegant orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that often display vibrant colors such as red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks or branches in humid, shaded environments. Its foliage consists of broad, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence.

Lepanthes eximia requires specific conditions, including high humidity and indirect light, to thrive in cultivation.
