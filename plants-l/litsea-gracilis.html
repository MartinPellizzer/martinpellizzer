Litsea gracilis

Litsea gracilis, commonly known as the slender-laurel, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, narrow leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. It produces small, red berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful.

Litsea gracilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in shaded environments.
