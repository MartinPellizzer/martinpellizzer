Loranthus tubulosus

Loranthus tubulosus, commonly known as the tubular mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to its host tree through a specialized root system called a haustellum.

It belongs to the family Loranthaceae and is native to various regions in Africa, Asia, and Australia. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. Its berries, which are typically red or orange, contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in a wide range of host species, including eucalyptus and acacia trees.
