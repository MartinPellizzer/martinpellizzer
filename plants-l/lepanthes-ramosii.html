Lepanthes ramosii

Lepanthes ramosii, commonly known as the fragrant orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as rainforests, where it thrives as an epiphyte on trees. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, facilitating water retention.

Lepanthes ramosii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
