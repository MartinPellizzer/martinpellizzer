Lycopodium stamineum

Lycopodium stamineum, commonly known as the clubmoss, is a primitive vascular plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and sporophyte structures that produce spores. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its life cycle involves alternation of generations, with the sporophyte phase being dominant.

Lycopodium stamineum plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
