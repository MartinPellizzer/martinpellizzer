Lagenophora erici

Lagenophora erici, commonly known as the Ericaceous Lagenophora, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist climates. Its botanical features include hairy stems and elliptical leaves with a leathery texture.

Lagenophora erici is often found in cloud forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
