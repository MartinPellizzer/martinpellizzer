Lepanthes barbosae

Lepanthes barbosae, commonly known as the Barbosa's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments within tropical forests. The plant features delicate, greenish-white flowers with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals. It belongs to the Epidendroideae subfamily and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit.

Lepanthes barbosae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
