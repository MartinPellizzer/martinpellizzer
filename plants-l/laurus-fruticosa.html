Laurus fruticosa

Laurus fruticosa, commonly known as bay laurel, is a evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are widely used in cooking and traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by black, berry-like fruits. Its leaves contain essential oils, particularly cineole, which contribute to its distinctive fragrance.

Laurus fruticosa is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and as a source of aromatic foliage.
