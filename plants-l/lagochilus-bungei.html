Lagochilus bungei

Lagochilus bungei, commonly known as Bunge's rock hyssop, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained conditions. Its small, tubular flowers are usually violet or purple, and it produces small, nutlet-like fruits.

Lagochilus bungei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and drought tolerance.
