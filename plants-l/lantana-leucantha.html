Lantana leucantha

Lantana leucantha, commonly known as white lantana, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

However, it can be invasive in some regions, outcompeting native vegetation.
