Lonicera roeseri

Lonicera roeseri, commonly known as the evergreen honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces red berries that are edible for birds but not for humans. It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.

Lonicera roeseri is a deciduous shrub that can grow up to 6 feet tall, thriving in full sun to partial shade.
