Lophochloa amblyantha

Lophochloa amblyantha, commonly known as the narrow-leafed ricegrass, is a species of grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and compact growth habit. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are small, flowering structures typical of the grass family.

Lophochloa amblyantha plays a minor role in local ecosystems but is sometimes used in traditional practices or as a forage plant.
