Lembotropis emeriflora

Lembotropis emeriflora, commonly known as the golden dawnflower, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow and orange flowers that bloom in clusters, resembling small suns. The plant has a woody stem with smooth, dark green leaves that are oval in shape. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
