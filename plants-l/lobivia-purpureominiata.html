Lobivia purpureominiata

Lobivia purpureominiata, commonly known as the Purple-Fringed Lobivia, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair. The plant produces striking clusters of violet or purple flowers during the warmer months. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, which help it conserve water in its harsh environment.

Lobivia purpureominiata is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
