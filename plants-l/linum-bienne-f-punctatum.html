Linum bienne f. punctatum

Linum bienne f. punctatum, commonly known as the spotted flax.

This plant is a herbaceous perennial belonging to the Linaceae family, characterized by its slender stems and lance-shaped leaves. It produces showy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The varietal form 'punctatum' is distinguished by its distinctive spots on the petals.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
