Lobelia tupa var. berteroi

Lobelia tupa var. berteroi, commonly known as the scarlet lily, is a striking member of the Campanulaceae family native to the Andes.

This variant is characterized by its vibrant red or orange flowers, which bloom in clusters atop slender, erect stems. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude regions of South America. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections worldwide.
