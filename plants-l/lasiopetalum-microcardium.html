Lasiopetalum microcardium

Lasiopetalum microcardium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved matted bellflower, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive hooded structure. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are densely arranged, giving it a matted appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal heathlands.

Lasiopetalum microcardium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
