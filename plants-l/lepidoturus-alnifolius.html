Lepidoturus alnifolius

Lepidoturus alnifolius, commonly known as the alderleaf hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters, followed by red or orange berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in natural habitats.
