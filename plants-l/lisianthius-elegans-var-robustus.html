Lisianthius elegans var. robustus

Lisianthius elegans var. robustus, commonly known as the robust lisianthius, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and vibrant purple to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Due to its hardiness and showy blooms, it is often cultivated for both its aesthetic value and its ability to attract pollinators.
