Lepanthes petalopteryx

Lepanthes petalopteryx, commonly known as the velvet tumbler orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety purple petals and a unique, tumbling growth habit. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments within the understory of tropical forests. Its flowers are small but highly fragrant, attracting various pollinators such as bees and moths.

The orchid's botanical structure includes a short, erect stem with fleshy, lanceolate leaves that help it thrive in low-light conditions.
