Lysimachia myrtifolia

Lysimachia myrtifolia, commonly known as rosemary moss, is a low-growing evergreen plant native to China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine, silky hairs. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Lysimachia myrtifolia is often used in ornamental gardening for its lush, green appearance and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
