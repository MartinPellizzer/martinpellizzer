Liparis gregaria

Liparis gregaria, commonly known as the clustered orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a terrestrial herbaceous plant characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and dense clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Native to Southeast Asia, it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and mountainous regions. The plant has elliptical to lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern.

Its flowers are typically short-lived, blooming in spring or early summer, and it relies on mycorrhizal associations for nutrient uptake.
