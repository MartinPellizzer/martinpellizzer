Lepanthes blepharistes

Lepanthes blepharistes, commonly known as the showy orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, brightly colored flowers that often feature intricate patterns and a unique, elongated shape. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by specific insects, and it thrives in high-light conditions with well-draining soil.

Lepanthes blepharistes is appreciated for its ornamental value and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts worldwide.
