Landolphia madagascariensis

Landolphia madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar morning glory, is a climbing plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant produces fleshy, berry-like fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, viscid coating. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in humid, shaded environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and greenhouses.
