Leucophae nervosa

Leucophae nervosa, commonly known as the slender white fern, is a delicate flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It features slender, arching stems that bear small, pale green leaves with a finely veined pattern. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and somewhat elusive species.
