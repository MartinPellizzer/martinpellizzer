Leucas mwingensis

Leucas mwingensis, commonly known as the African bluebottle, is a flowering plant native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and clusters of small, blue or purple flowers. It is often found in grasslands and along roadsides, thriving in dry to semi-arid conditions.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
