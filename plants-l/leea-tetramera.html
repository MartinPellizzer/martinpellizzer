Leea tetramera

Leea tetramera, commonly known as the four-lobed leaf vine, is a tropical plant native to regions of Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its opposite, simple, and often lobed leaves that can vary in shape depending on the species. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

Leea tetramera is often found growing in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
