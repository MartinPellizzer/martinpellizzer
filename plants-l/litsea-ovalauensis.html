Litsea ovalauensis

Litsea ovalauensis, commonly known as the Ovalau litsea, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of the South Pacific, particularly the islands of Vanuatu and Fiji. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth or slightly serrated margin. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by oval, woody berries.

The species is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
