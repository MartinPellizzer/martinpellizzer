Liatris gracilis

Liatris gracilis, commonly known as slender blazing star, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features tall, slender stems that bear showy, pink to purple spike-like flower clusters. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, typically arranged in a whorled pattern along the stem.

Liatris gracilis is often found in open woods, meadows, and sandy soils, and it serves as a valuable nectar source for pollinators.
