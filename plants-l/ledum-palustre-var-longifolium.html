Ledum palustre var. longifolium

Ledum palustre var. longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved Labrador tea, is a variant of the Labrador tea plant native to northern regions of North America.

It is a low-growing evergreen shrub characterized by its aromatic, dark green, and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves contain aromatic compounds, including phenolic substances, which contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.

It thrives in wet, acidic soils and is often found in bogs and swampy areas.
