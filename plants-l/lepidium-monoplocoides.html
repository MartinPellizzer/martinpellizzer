Lepidium monoplocoides

Lepidium monoplocoides, commonly known as the single-seeded cress, is a small flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants like cabbages and mustards. The plant has tiny, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. Its fruit is a small, segmented capsule that contains a single seed, a characteristic that gives it its scientific name.

This species is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
