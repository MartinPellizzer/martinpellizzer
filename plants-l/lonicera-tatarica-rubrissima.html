Lonicera tatarica rubrissima

Lonicera tatarica rubrissima, commonly known as the red honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and clusters of small, fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to red. The plant produces red or orange berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
