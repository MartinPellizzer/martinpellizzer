Lodhra bractealis

Lodhra bractealis, commonly known as the sacred lotus of the Himalayas, is a deciduous shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental, palmately lobed leaves and showy, pale yellow to white flowers. The plant is often found in moist, shady areas such as forests and stream sides. It produces small, round, brown fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Lodhra bractealis is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional Ayurvedic medicine.
