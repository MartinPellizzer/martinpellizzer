Lactuca hirsuta var. albiflora

Lactuca hirsuta var. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered hairy lettuce, is a variant of the hairy lettuce species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which are typically dark green with a slightly bitter taste. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the summer months, adding a delicate floral element to its appearance. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Due to its unique appearance and mild flavor, it is occasionally cultivated for ornamental or culinary purposes.
