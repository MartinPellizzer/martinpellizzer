Linum suaedifolium

Linum suaedifolium, commonly known as the desert flax, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Linaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its fibrous stems and leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Linum suaedifolium plays an important ecological role in stabilizing desert soils and providing nectar for pollinators.
