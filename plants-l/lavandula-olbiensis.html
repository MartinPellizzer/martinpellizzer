Lavandula olbiensis

Lavandula olbiensis, commonly known as the Albanian lavender, is a perennial shrub native to the Balkans, particularly Albania.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and spikes of pale purple to lilac flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for Mediterranean climates. Its essential oils, rich in linalool and lavandulol, are valued in aromatherapy and perfumery.

Lavandula olbiensis is also appreciated for its ornamental value and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
