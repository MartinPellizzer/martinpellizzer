Lobostemon capitiformis

Lobostemon capitiformis, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern at the base. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which extends from spring to early summer.
