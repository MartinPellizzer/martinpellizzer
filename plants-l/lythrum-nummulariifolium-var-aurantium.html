Lythrum nummulariifolium var. aurantium

Lythrum nummulariifolium var. aurantium, commonly known as the golden lythrum, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe.

It features striking, showy red to orange flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes and wetland restoration projects.
