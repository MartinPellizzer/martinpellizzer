Lupinus citrinus var. deflexus

Lupinus citrinus var. deflexus, commonly known as the yellow lupine or yellow grass lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through its root nodules.

This variety is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature and ability to improve soil fertility.
