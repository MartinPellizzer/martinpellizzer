Lobelia alata var. cuneiformis

Lobelia alata var. cuneiformis, commonly known as the slender cardinal flower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces striking, tubular red flowers that are clustered in whorls along the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland edges and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for wetland gardens and native plant collections.
