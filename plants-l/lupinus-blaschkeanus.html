Lupinus blaschkeanus

Lupinus blaschkeanus, commonly known as Blaschke's lupine, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit. The plant produces showy, sparsely branched flower clusters that range in color from purple to pink. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it has a deep taproot system.

Lupinus blaschkeanus is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and plays a role in nitrogen fixation in its ecosystem.
