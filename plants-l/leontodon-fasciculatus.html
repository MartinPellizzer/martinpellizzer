Leontodon fasciculatus

Leontodon fasciculatus, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, yellowish flowers in the summer. Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological role.

The species is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive floral arrangement.
