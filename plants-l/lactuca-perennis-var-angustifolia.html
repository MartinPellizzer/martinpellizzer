Lactuca perennis var. angustifolia

Lactuca perennis var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved lettuce, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features rosettes of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, green flowers in a compact cluster, which are followed by slender, cylindrical seed heads. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
