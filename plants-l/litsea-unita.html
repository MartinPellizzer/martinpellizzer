Litsea unita

Litsea unita, commonly known as the narrow-leaved litsea, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including parts of India, China, and Indonesia. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, round berries.

The leaves and fruits are often used in traditional medicine for their aromatic and therapeutic properties.
