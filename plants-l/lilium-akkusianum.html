Lilium akkusianum

Lilium akkusianum, commonly known as the Armenian lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, or red. The plant produces sword-shaped leaves and has a bulbous root system typical of many lily species. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a cool, temperate climate to flourish.

This plant is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its cultural and historical significance in the regions where it is found.
