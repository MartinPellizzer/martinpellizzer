Laurus sailiana

Laurus sailiana, commonly known as the Mediterranean laurel, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves that are aromatic and often used in culinary applications. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible but not widely consumed. Laurus sailiana thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its botanical features make it both aesthetically pleasing and ecologically valuable in its native habitat.
