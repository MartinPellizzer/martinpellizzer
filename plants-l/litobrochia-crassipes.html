Litobrochia crassipes

Litobrochia crassipes, commonly known as the thick-stemmed brochium, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its robust, fleshy stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves.

It is native to freshwater habitats in southern Africa, where it thrives in slow-moving or still waters. The plant's structure allows it to anchor itself in muddy or sandy substrates, providing stability in its environment. Its flowers, which are small and inconspicuous, are typically greenish-yellow and appear in clusters.

Litobrochia crassipes plays a role in stabilizing sediments and offering habitat for aquatic invertebrates.
