Laserpitium petrophilum var. polypterum

Laserpitium petrophilum var. polypterum, commonly known as the rock spurge, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces small, yellowish flowers clustered in dense inflorescences. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh alpine environments. Its leaves are typically succulent, which helps it retain moisture in arid conditions.

It is often found in high-altitude meadows and is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations.
