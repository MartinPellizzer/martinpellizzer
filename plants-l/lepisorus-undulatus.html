Lepisorus undulatus

Lepisorus undulatus, commonly known as the wavy fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that have a distinctive undulating or wavy appearance. Native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged in a compact rosette.

Its reproductive structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and are covered by a protective indusium.
