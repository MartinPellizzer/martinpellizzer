Liparis pterostyloides

Liparis pterostyloides, commonly known as the wing-stem orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and its delicate, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a rosette, helping to conserve moisture.

The species is noted for its unique floral structure, which includes a spurred labellum and a distinctive column.
