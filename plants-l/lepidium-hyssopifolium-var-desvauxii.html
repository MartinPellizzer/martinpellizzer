Lepidium hyssopifolium var. desvauxii

Lepidium hyssopifolium var. desvauxii, commonly known as the desert peppergrass, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is well adapted to drought conditions. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

This variety is distinguished by its unique leaf morphology, resembling that of hyssop, and is of interest in ecological and botanical studies.
