Lyonia parabolica

Lyonia parabolica, commonly known as the parabolica lily, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Liliaceae and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland areas and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are often reddish in color.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
