Lycopodium hookeri

Lycopodium hookeri, commonly known as the Hooker's clubmoss, is a small, evergreen plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit and is native to the mountainous regions of South America. The plant produces spore-bearing cones, known as strobili, which are typically clustered at the tips of its branching stems. Lycopodium hookeri thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ancient lineage dates back to the Carboniferous period, making it a living fossil with significant botanical interest.
