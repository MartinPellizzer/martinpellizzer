Lonicera caerulea var. villosa

Lonicera caerulea var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces edible berries that are typically yellow or orange in color, which are rich in antioxidants and vitamins. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a suitable candidate for ornamental and edible gardens.

This variety is valued for both its aesthetic appeal and its nutritional benefits.
