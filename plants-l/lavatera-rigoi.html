Lavatera rigoi

Lavatera rigoi, commonly known as the Woolly Sunhemp, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, often appearing in late winter to early spring. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it has a fibrous, woody stem.

Lavatera rigoi is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas and inland regions with a Mediterranean climate.
