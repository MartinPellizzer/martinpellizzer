Lepanthes tachirensis

Lepanthes tachirensis, commonly known as the Tachira orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America, particularly Venezuela and Colombia.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that thrives in humid, shaded environments within the tropical rainforests. The plant features delicate, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow with intricate patterns. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapted for absorbing moisture from the air.

Lepanthes tachirensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
