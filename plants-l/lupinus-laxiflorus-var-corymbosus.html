Lupinus laxiflorus var. corymbosus

Lupinus laxiflorus var. corymbosus, commonly known as the clustered lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant has feathery, compound leaves and produces cylindrical seed pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, including nitrogen fixation and support for pollinators.
