Luzula ignivoma

Luzula ignivoma, commonly known as fireweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of spike-like inflorescences that typically bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, greenish to purplish flowers that are often clustered in dense, cylindrical spikes. Luzula ignivoma is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

It is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
