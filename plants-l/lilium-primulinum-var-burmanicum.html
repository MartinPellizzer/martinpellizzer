Lilium primulinum var. burmanicum

Lilium primulinum var. burmanicum, commonly known as the Burman lily, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. Its bulbs are adapted for storage and survival in its natural habitat, and it thrives in well-drained, fertile soil.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
