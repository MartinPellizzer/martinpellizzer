Lobelia × speciosa

Lobelia × speciosa, commonly known as the Indian blanket flower, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant, daisy-like flowers that range in color from red to orange and yellow, often with a distinctive central purple eye. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Botanically, it belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is known for its showy blooms that attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
