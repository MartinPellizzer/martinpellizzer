Lotononis rara

Lotononis rara, commonly known as the rare African bellflower, is a striking plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling bells, hence its common name. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with hairy, narrow leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to dry conditions.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is highly valued in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
