Lobelia dortmanna f. terrestris

Lobelia dortmanna f. terrestris, commonly known as the creeping lobelia, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It features small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple, arranged in dense spikes. The plant has a mat-forming growth habit with prostrate stems that root at the nodes. Its leaves are opposite, oval to lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
