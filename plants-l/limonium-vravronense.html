Limonium vravronense

Limonium vravronense, commonly known as the Greek sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and saline soils, showing a high tolerance for salt and wind. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

Limonium vravronense is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
