Luzula gigantea

Luzula gigantea, commonly known as giant woodrush, is a perennial plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the plantain family (Plantaginaceae) and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in early summer, adding subtle floral interest to its habitat.

Luzula gigantea is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging, wet conditions.
