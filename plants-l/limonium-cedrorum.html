Limonium cedrorum

Limonium cedrorum, commonly known as the rock sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hair.

Limonium cedrorum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate coastal conditions.
