Lonicera harae var. tashiroi

Lonicera harae var. tashiroi, commonly known as the Tashiro's honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features slender, arching stems that often grow along the ground or climb supports. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and white, typically blooming in late spring. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a glossy surface.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.
