Luffa sepium

Luffa sepium, commonly known as the sponge gourd, is a flowering plant in the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and is characterized by its large, fibrous, sponge-like fruit that matures into a rough, woody structure. The plant produces yellow flowers and has hairy stems and leaves. Its fruit is used in various culinary and cosmetic applications due to its spongy texture.

The plant thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
