Lepanthes deutera

Lepanthes deutera, commonly known as the violet orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its leaves are usually broad and strap-like, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Lepanthes deutera is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
