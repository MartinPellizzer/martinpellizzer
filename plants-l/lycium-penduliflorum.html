Lycium penduliflorum

Lycium penduliflorum, commonly known as the hanging-flower boxthorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, yellowish-green flowers that hang in clusters, giving it its common name. The plant has spiny stems and leaves that are typically oval in shape. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Lycium penduliflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
