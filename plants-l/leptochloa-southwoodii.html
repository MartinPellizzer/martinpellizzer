Leptochloa southwoodii

Leptochloa southwoodii, commonly known as the Southwood's grass, is a perennial grass species native to the tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and along roadsides. It has narrow, linear leaves with a smooth texture and produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Leptochloa southwoodii is often used in ecological studies due to its adaptability and role in soil stabilization.
