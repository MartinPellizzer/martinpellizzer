Limnas malyschevii

Limnas malyschevii, commonly known as the Malyshev's water lily, is a rare aquatic plant native to specific regions in Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves that provide habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant produces white, fragrant flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the water, contributing to the ecosystem's balance.

Due to habitat loss and pollution, Limnas malyschevii is considered endangered in its natural range.
