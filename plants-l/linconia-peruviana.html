Linconia peruviana

Linconia peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine layer of white hair, which helps reduce water loss.

Linconia peruviana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
