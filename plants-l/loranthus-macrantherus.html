Loranthus macrantherus

Loranthus macrantherus, commonly known as the large-flowered mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees.

It belongs to the family Loranthaceae and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and showy, white or pinkish flowers. The plant derives some of its nutrients from the host through specialized roots called haustoria. It produces small, fleshy berries that are often consumed by birds, which aid in seed dispersal.

This species is typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, often inhabiting rainforests and other humid environments.
