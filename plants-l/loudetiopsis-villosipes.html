Loudetiopsis villosipes

Loudetiopsis villosipes, commonly known as the hairy strobilanthes, is a flowering plant belonging to the Acanthaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its specific epithet "villosipes.". The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
