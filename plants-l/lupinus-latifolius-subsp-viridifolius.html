Lupinus latifolius subsp. viridifolius

Lupinus latifolius subsp.

viridifolius, commonly known as the greenleaf lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States. It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green, contributing to its common name.

The plant produces tall spikes of blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands. Lupinus latifolius subsp.

viridifolius is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in nitrogen fixation, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
