Lymania corallina

Lymania corallina, commonly known as the coral tree, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive coral-colored flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a woody stem and produces small, green, seed-containing pods. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Lymania corallina is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
