Lupinus rusbyanus

Lupinus rusbyanus, commonly known as Rusby's lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of pea-like flowers. The plant has pinnately compound leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions.

Lupinus rusbyanus plays an important ecological role by fixing nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
