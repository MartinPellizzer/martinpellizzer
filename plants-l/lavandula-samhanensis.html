Lavandula samhanensis

Lavandula samhanensis, commonly known as the Korean lavender, is a flowering plant native to South Korea.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, spike-like inflorescences of purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, with a strong, pleasant fragrance.

Lavandula samhanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
