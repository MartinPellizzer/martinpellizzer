Larix kaempferi f. rubescens

Larix kaempferi f. rubescens, commonly known as the red-leaved larch, is a variant of the Japanese larch characterized by its striking red foliage.

This ornamental tree is native to Japan and is prized for its vibrant autumn coloration. The leaves are needle-like and turn a deep crimson in the fall, making it a popular choice for landscaping. It is a deciduous conifer that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

The plant exhibits typical coniferous growth habits, with a conical shape and upright branches.
