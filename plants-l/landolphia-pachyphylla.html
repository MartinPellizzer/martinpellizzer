Landolphia pachyphylla

Landolphia pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved arrowroot, is a tropical shrub native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, starchy tubers that are used as a food source. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

The tubers are an important resource for local communities and have been traditionally used in various culinary and medicinal applications.
