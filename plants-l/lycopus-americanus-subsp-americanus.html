Lycopus americanus subsp. americanus

Lycopus americanus subsp.

americanus, commonly known as American bugleweed, is a perennial herb native to North America. It typically grows in moist, woodland environments and has opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its roots are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported calming effects.

It is a member of the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves.
