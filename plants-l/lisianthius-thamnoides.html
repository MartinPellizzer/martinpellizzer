Lisianthius thamnoides

Lisianthius thamnoides, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically thrives in rocky or dry environments. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, with a compact inflorescence of yellow flowers. Its flowers are typically short-lived, opening in the morning and closing by midday.

The plant is often used in rock gardens due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
