Limodorum × speciosum

Limodorum × speciosum, commonly known as the hybrid bluebell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the forests of Europe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, nodding blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30–60 cm, with strap-like leaves that are dark green and glossy. Its flowers bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Limodorum × speciosum thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
