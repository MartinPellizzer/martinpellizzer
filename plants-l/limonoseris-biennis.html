Limonoseris biennis

Limonoseris biennis, commonly known as the two-yearly lemon herb, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly aromatic scent.

Limonoseris biennis is typically found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to thrive in coastal and inland areas with mild climates.
