Leptogramma totta var. pilosissima

Leptogramma totta var. pilosissima, commonly known as the hairy leptogramma, is a variant of the leptogramma species native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage, which gives it its common name and provides protection against herbivores and excessive moisture loss. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with a leathery texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered near the base of the plant.

This variant is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphological features and potential ecological adaptations.
