Lobelia ambigua

Lobelia ambigua, commonly known as the ambiguous lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to violet. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Lobelia ambigua is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
