Lolium montevidense

Lolium montevidense, commonly known as the Argentine ryegrass, is a cool-season grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted blades and dense growth habit, making it a common weed in agricultural fields. The plant typically grows in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions. It has a short stature, usually reaching heights of 20 to 50 centimeters.

Botanically, it is recognized for its ability to thrive in disturbed environments and its potential as a forage crop.
