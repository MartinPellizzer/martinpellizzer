Lomatogoniopsis galeiformis

Lomatogoniopsis galeiformis, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with gray-green, fuzzy leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces yellow, dandelion-like flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and limited water availability.

Due to its resilience, it is often found in disturbed soils and is considered an indicator species of desert ecosystems.
