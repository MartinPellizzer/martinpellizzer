Linaria × brangensis

Linaria × brangensis, commonly known as the hybrid sweet William, is a cross between Linaria vulgaris and Linaria maroccana.

It is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Mediterranean region. The plant features bright, showy flowers in shades of red, yellow, and orange, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped, serrated leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy.

Linaria × brangensis is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
