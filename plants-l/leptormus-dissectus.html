Leptormus dissectus

Leptormus dissectus, commonly known as the dissected leaf euphorbia, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, deeply lobed leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique foliage and resilience, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens.
