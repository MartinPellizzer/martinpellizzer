Lauro-cerasus javanica

Lauro-cerasus javanica, commonly known as the Java cherry or Javanese cherry, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Prunus genus and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red, cherry-like fruits that are edible but often have a tart flavor.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and landscapes.
