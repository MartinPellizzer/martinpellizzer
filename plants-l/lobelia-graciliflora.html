Lobelia graciliflora

Lobelia graciliflora, commonly known as slender-flowered lobelia, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers. It belongs to the Campanulaceae family, which is characterized by its bell-shaped flowers.

Lobelia graciliflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits to pollinators.
