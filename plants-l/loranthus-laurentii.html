Loranthus laurentii

Loranthus laurentii, commonly known as the European mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that attaches to the branches of host trees such as oaks and pines.

It lacks true roots and absorbs water and nutrients through its stems, which are embedded into the host's bark. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers and red, fleshy berries that are often consumed by birds. Its life cycle involves the dispersal of seeds via bird droppings, allowing it to colonize new hosts.

Despite its parasitic nature, Loranthus laurentii plays a role in supporting biodiversity by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
