Lonicera subspicata var. johnstonii

Lonicera subspicata var. johnstonii, commonly known as Johnston's honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to China.

It is characterized by its twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. Its berries are red and edible, often attracting birds and other wildlife.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
