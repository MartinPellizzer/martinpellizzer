Lathyrus aphaca subsp. cyprius

Lathyrus aphaca subsp.

cyprius, commonly known as Cyprus vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, including Cyprus. It is a perennial legume that produces small, pea-like flowers in shades of pink or purple.

The plant has trifoliate leaves and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and nitrogen-fixing ability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
