Lobelia mucronata f. ovalifolia

Lobelia mucronata f. ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaved cardinal flower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear whorled clusters of striking red-orange tubular flowers. The leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

This cultivar is valued for its vibrant blooms and ability to attract pollinators like hummingbirds and bees.
