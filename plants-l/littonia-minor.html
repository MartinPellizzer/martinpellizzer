Littonia minor

Littonia minor, commonly known as the lesser littonia, is a small herbaceous plant native to specific regions of South America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its small, white flowers bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months.

Littonia minor is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by supporting pollinators.
