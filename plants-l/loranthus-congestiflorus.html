Loranthus congestiflorus

Loranthus congestiflorus, commonly known as the clustered mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on a variety of host trees.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant relies on its host for water and nutrients, while it provides nectar to attract pollinators. Its berries are typically red or orange, containing seeds that are dispersed by birds.

Loranthus congestiflorus is found in tropical and subtropical regions, often in forested areas.
