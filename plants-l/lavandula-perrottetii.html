Lavandula perrottetii

Lavandula perrottetii, commonly known as the Perrottet's lavender, is a species of flowering plant in the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region, particularly found in southern France, Italy, and Spain. This plant is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and produces spikes of purple or pink flowers. The leaves are narrow, aromatic, and gray-green in color, which contribute to its ornamental value.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
