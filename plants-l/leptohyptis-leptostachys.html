Leptohyptis leptostachys

Leptohyptis leptostachys, commonly known as the narrow-stalked hyptis, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically clustered in whorls along the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.
