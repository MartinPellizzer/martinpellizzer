Linaria saxatilis var. rubella

Linaria saxatilis var. rubella, commonly known as the red-flowered sandlinaria, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often found in coastal areas. The plant features slender, branching stems and produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to deep red. Its flowers are adapted for pollination by insects, particularly bees, and the plant is known for its drought tolerance.

The variety rubella is distinguished by its more vibrant and intensely colored flowers compared to the typical form.
