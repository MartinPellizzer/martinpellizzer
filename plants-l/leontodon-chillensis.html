Leontodon chillensis

Leontodon chillensis, commonly known as the Chilean dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, adapting to harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a single head.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local pollinators.
