Lindsaea parvipinnula

Lindsaea parvipinnula, commonly known as the small-leaved lindsaea, is a species of epiphytic fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, linear leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern along its creeping rhizome. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on tree trunks or branches in tropical and subtropical regions. Its fronds are usually short and narrow, with a soft, velvety texture.

Lindsaea parvipinnula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
