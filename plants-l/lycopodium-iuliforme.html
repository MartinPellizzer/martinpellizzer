Lycopodium iuliforme

Lycopodium iuliforme, commonly known as the slender clubmoss, is a small, evergreen fern-like plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, thread-like leaves that form dense, compact clusters near the ground. The plant produces small, cone-like structures called strobili, which contain the spores for reproduction. Lycopodium iuliforme typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological systems as a ground cover plant.
