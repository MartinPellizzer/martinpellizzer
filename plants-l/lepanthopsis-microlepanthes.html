Lepanthopsis microlepanthes

Lepanthopsis microlepanthes, commonly known as the miniature lepanthopsis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features narrow, strap-like leaves and produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. Its flowers are typically arranged in a raceme and bloom during the wet season.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
