Lobelia subulata

Lobelia subulata, commonly known as the slender lobelia, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It is often found in the southeastern regions of the U.S., including states like Georgia and Florida.

Lobelia subulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
