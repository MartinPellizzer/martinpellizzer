Lepanthes carrilloi

Lepanthes carrilloi, commonly known as the Carrillo's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as cloud forests, where it thrives on tree trunks or in the undergrowth. Its flowers are usually small and colorful, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by insects.

Lepanthes carrilloi is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
