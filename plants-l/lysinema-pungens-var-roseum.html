Lysinema pungens var. roseum

Lysinema pungens var. roseum, commonly known as the rose-flowered lichen, is a type of foliose lichen that exhibits a distinctive pinkish or rose-colored thallus.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found on tree trunks and branches in temperate regions. The plant's structure consists of a symbiotic association between a fungus and algae or cyanobacteria, which allows it to absorb nutrients from the air and rain. Its slow growth rate and specialized habitat make it a sensitive indicator of environmental conditions.

Due to its delicate nature, it is often overlooked but plays an important role in ecological systems.
