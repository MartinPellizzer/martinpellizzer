Lupinus ledigianus

Lupinus ledigianus, commonly known as the ledig's lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and clusters of showy blue to purple flowers. The plant has pinnately compound leaves and produces pods that contain several seeds. It is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to full sun conditions.

Lupinus ledigianus is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility.
