Lebidiera stipularis

Lebidiera stipularis, commonly known as the narrow-leafed thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads and typically showsy blooms. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
