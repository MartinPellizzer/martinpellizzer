Lathyrus pannonicus f. rumelicus

Lathyrus pannonicus f. rumelicus, commonly known as the Rumelic grass vetch, is a flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in grasslands and along roadsides, characterized by its slender stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces slender, winding pods containing small, elongated seeds.

It is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinator populations.
