Linaria heterophylla subsp. tartessiana

Linaria heterophylla subsp.

tartessiana, commonly known as the Canary Island toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces bright yellow or orange flowers in clusters.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are usually linear or lance-shaped, and the subspecies is known for its variability in leaf morphology.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
