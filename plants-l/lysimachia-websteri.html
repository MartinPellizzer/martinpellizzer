Lysimachia websteri

Lysimachia websteri, commonly known as the Webster's chickweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features small, delicate white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has hairy, upright stems and alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Lysimachia websteri is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
