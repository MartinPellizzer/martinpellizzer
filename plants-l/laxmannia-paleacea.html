Laxmannia paleacea

Laxmannia paleacea, commonly known as the paleaceae or the white saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats of foliage, with small, white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates with short growing seasons. Its leaves are usually succulent, which helps it retain moisture in harsh environments.

Laxmannia paleacea is often found in tundra and subalpine habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems.
