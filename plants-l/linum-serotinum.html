Linum serotinum

Linum serotinum, commonly known as evening flax, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It produces striking blue or white flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has slender, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy. Its flowers are typically clustered in racemes and are known for their vibrant color and long-lasting nature.

Linum serotinum is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and has been used historically in traditional medicine.
