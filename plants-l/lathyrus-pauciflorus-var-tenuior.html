Lathyrus pauciflorus var. tenuior

Lathyrus pauciflorus var. tenuior, commonly known as the slender-flowered grass vetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and along roadsides. It has small, trifoliate leaves and produces pods containing several seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
