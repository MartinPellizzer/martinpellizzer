Linaria dichondrifolia

Linaria dichondrifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and bright yellow flowers that resemble those of the common toadflax. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in pairs along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Linaria dichondrifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
