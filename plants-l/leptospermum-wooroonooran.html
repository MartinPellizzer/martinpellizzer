Leptospermum wooroonooran

Leptospermum wooroonooran, commonly known as the Wooroonooran tea tree, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is often found in heathlands and along coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its foliage is frequently used in traditional bush tinctures for its aromatic properties.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native wildlife.
