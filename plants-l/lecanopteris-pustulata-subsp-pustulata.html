Lecanopteris pustulata subsp. pustulata

Lecanopteris pustulata subsp.

pustulata, commonly known as the pustulate shield fern, is a fern species native to tropical regions of Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds that are often covered in small, raised dots or pustules.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually leathery and dark green, with a rugged texture.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated in horticulture for its unique foliage.
