Leucocoryne valparadisea

Leucocoryne valparadisea, commonly known as the Valparaiso lily, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking white petals and a distinctive, elongated flower spike. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a basal rosette.

Leucocoryne valparadisea is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable conditions.
