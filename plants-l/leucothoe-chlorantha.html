Leucothoe chlorantha

Leucothoe chlorantha, commonly known as the greenleaf alder, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant features small, oval-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter green underneath. Its flowers are white and clustered in racemes, appearing in late spring.

The berries it produces are red and edible, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
