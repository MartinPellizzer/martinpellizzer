Lellingeria pinnata

Lellingeria pinnata, commonly known as the creeping bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Lellingeria pinnata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
