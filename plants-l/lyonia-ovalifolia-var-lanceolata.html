Lyonia ovalifolia var. lanceolata

Lyonia ovalifolia var. lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf lyonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen shrub form. The plant features lance-shaped, leathery leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath, providing a striking visual contrast. Its small, bell-shaped flowers are typically white or pale pink, appearing in clusters during the spring and early summer.

The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.
