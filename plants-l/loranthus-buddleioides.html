Loranthus buddleioides

Loranthus buddleioides, commonly known as the mistletoe of the genus Loranthus, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using light absorbed by its leaves. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often pollinated by insects. Its berries are typically red or yellow and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This species is found in various regions of the Americas and is an important part of the ecological dynamics of its host ecosystems.
