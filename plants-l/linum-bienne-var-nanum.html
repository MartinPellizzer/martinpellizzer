Linum bienne var. nanum

Linum bienne var. nanum, commonly known as dwarf flax, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, delicate blue to purple flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette, giving it a compact appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for both ornamental and ecological gardening.
