Lupinus verjonensis

Lupinus verjonensis, commonly known as the Verjone lupine, is a flowering plant native to the island of Sardinia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces elongated pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
