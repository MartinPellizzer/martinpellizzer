Lepigonum rupicola

Lepigonum rupicola, commonly known as the rock spurge, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant thrives in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It has a shallow root system that helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Lepigonum rupicola is often used in rock gardens due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
