Leptomeria cunninghamii

Leptomeria cunninghamii, commonly known as the mountain wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its yellow flowers and feathery leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in coastal and inland regions. It is often found in heathlands and is adapted to withstand drought and poor soil conditions.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
