Linaria multicaulis

Linaria multicaulis, commonly known as the clustered toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features slender, branched stems and produces bright yellow or orange flowers in clusters. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant characteristics.

Linaria multicaulis is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
