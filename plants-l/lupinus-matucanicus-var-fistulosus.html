Lupinus matucanicus var. fistulosus

Lupinus matucanicus var. fistulosus, commonly known as the Matucan lupine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of blue to violet pea-like flowers. The plant has palmately divided leaves with a hairy texture, and its root system includes nitrogen-fixing nodules. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky slopes.

This species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
