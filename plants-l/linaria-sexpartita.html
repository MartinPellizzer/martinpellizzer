Linaria sexpartita

Linaria sexpartita, commonly known as the six-parted toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. Linaria sexpartita is often found in rocky soils and dry habitats, thriving in well-drained environments.

Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs.
