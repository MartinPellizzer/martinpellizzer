Lukea quentinii

Lukea quentinii, commonly known as the Quentin's lily, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking white petals and a tall, slender stem that can reach up to two meters in height. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests. Its blooming period occurs during the spring and early summer months.

Due to its rarity and habitat specificity, it is considered an endangered species.
