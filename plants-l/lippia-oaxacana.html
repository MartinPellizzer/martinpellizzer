Lippia oaxacana

Lippia oaxacana, commonly known as Mexican blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is a member of the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

Lippia oaxacana is also valued as a source of essential oils and has been studied for its potential in herbal remedies.
