Leontodon autumnalis subsp. pratensis

Leontodon autumnalis subsp.

pratensis, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in open grasslands, meadows, and woodland edges, preferring moist to moderately dry soils.

The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces yellow flowers in late summer. Its inflorescence consists of a single, terminal flower head surrounded by bracts.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaf arrangement and is often found in disturbed or nutrient-rich habitats.
