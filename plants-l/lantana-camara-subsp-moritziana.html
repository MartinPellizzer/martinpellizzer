Lantana camara subsp. moritziana

Lantana camara subsp.

moritziana, commonly known as the Moritz's lantana, is a flowering plant native to South America. It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange and red.

The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are typically oval to lance-shaped. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it adaptable to various garden environments.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
