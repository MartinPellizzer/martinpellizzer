Loranthus acuminatissimus

Loranthus acuminatissimus, commonly known as the narrow-pointed mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily in the family Fabaceae.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using the light it captures. The plant has small, ovate leaves and produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that releases tiny seeds, which can adhere to the bark of host trees.

This species is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, contributing to the ecological dynamics of its host ecosystems.
