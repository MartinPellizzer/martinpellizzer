Lupinus × cymba-egressus

Lupinus × cymba-egressus, commonly known as the hybrid lupine, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of spiny, pea-like flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant produces trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its root system forms nodules containing nitrogen-fixing bacteria. Lupinus × cymba-egressus is native to regions of North America and is often found in disturbed soils such as roadsides and open woodlands.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in soil enrichment.
