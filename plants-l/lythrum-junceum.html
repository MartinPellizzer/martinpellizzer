Lythrum junceum

Lythrum junceum, commonly known as narrowleaf lythrum, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lythraceae family, which includes other plants such as the pomegranate and the willow herb. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and spikes of pale pink to purple flowers. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Lythrum junceum is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, where it thrives in moist soil conditions.
