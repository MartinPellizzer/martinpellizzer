Lycaste harrisoniae

Lycaste harrisoniae, commonly known as Harrison's lycaste, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant has a pseudobulb structure, which helps it store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in dry conditions. Its leaves are usually broad and strap-like, and it prefers bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its vibrant blooms.
