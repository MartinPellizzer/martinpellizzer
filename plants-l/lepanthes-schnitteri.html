Lepanthes schnitteri

Lepanthes schnitteri, commonly known as the Schmitter's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest understories. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination.

Lepanthes schnitteri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
