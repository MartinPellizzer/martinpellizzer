Lepanthes sijmii

Lepanthes sijmii, commonly known as the Sijm's slipper orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually small and showy, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insects.

Lepanthes sijmii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
