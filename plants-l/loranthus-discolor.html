Loranthus discolor

Loranthus discolor, commonly known as the white mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that relies on host trees for water and nutrients.

It belongs to the family Loranthaceae and is native to regions of Asia, including China and Japan. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces tiny, white or pale yellow flowers. Its berries are typically red or yellow and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

Loranthus discolor is often found growing on the branches of various host trees, such as elms and poplars.
