Liparis disticha

Liparis disticha, commonly known as the two-branched orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a raceme. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and rocky crevices. Its leaves are linear and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

Liparis disticha is valued in horticulture for its delicate appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
