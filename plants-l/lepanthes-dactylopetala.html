Lepanthes dactylopetala

Lepanthes dactylopetala, commonly known as the spider orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often colorful flowers that resemble spiders, with long, slender petals and sepals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical forests. Its flowers bloom during the rainy season, often in shades of white, yellow, or orange.

Lepanthes dactylopetala is valued for its ornamental beauty and is a subject of interest in botanical research due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
