Luisia jarawana

Luisia jarawana, commonly known as the Jarawana orchid, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean region.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its leaves are broad and glossy, and it produces showy, colorful blooms that attract pollinators.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
