Lycopsis cretica

Lycopsis cretica, commonly known as the Cretan lycopsis, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant features small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy texture. Its flowers are typically blue or purple, arranged in whorled clusters at the ends of the stems.

Lycopsis cretica is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and limestone areas.
