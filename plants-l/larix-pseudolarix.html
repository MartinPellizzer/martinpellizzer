Larix pseudolarix

Larix pseudolarix, commonly known as the Chinese larch, is a deciduous conifer native to China.

It is characterized by its needle-like leaves that turn golden before falling in autumn. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds surrounded by a papery wing. It typically grows in mountainous regions and prefers well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other parts of the world for landscaping purposes.
