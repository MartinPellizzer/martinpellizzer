Lens culinaris var. faurei

Lens culinaris var. faurei, commonly known as the Persian lentil, is a variety of the common lentil native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, round seeds that are typically brown or yellow in color. The plant is an annual legume that thrives in temperate climates and is cultivated for its high protein content. It has a bushy growth habit and produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers.

This variety is valued for its adaptability and nutritional benefits, making it a staple in traditional cuisines.
