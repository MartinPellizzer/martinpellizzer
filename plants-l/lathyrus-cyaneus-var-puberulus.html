Lathyrus cyaneus var. puberulus

Lathyrus cyaneus var. puberulus, commonly known as the pubescent blue flax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with slender, branching stems. Its leaves are pinnately compound, and the flowers are borne in racemes, adding to its ornamental value.

The variety puberulus is distinguished by its hairy or pubescent foliage, which sets it apart from other varieties of Lathyrus cyaneus.
