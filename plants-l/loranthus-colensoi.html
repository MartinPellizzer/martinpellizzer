Loranthus colensoi

Loranthus colensoi, commonly known as the Colenso's mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on a variety of host trees, primarily in the southern regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Loranthaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces fleshy, red berries that contain seeds covered in a sticky substance to facilitate attachment to potential host plants.

Its parasitic nature allows it to draw water and nutrients from its host, while also contributing to ecological interactions within forest ecosystems.
