Litsea segregata

Litsea segregata, commonly known as the fragrant leaf tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and avocados, and is characterized by its aromatic foliage. The plant features simple, alternate, ovate leaves that are often fragrant when crushed. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in panicles, while the fruit is a small, dark purple drupe.

Litsea segregata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
