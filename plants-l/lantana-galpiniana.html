Lantana galpiniana

Lantana galpiniana, commonly known as the Cape lantana, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring dense, branched stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often blooming throughout the year. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to survive in arid conditions.

Lantana galpiniana is well-suited to coastal and sandy soils, making it a hardy plant in Mediterranean climates.
