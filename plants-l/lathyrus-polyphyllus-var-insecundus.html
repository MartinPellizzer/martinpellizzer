Lathyrus polyphyllus var. insecundus

Lathyrus polyphyllus var. insecundus, commonly known as the lesser purple hopper, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
