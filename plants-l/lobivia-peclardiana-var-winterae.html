Lobivia peclardiana var. winterae

Lobivia peclardiana var. winterae, commonly known as the Winter's lobivia, is a succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It features compact, rounded rosettes of fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water, adapting to its harsh environment. The plant produces tall, spindly stems with clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the growing season. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, requiring minimal water to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
