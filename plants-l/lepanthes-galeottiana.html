Lepanthes galeottiana

Lepanthes galeottiana, commonly known as the Galeotti's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments. The plant features delicate, greenish-white flowers with intricate patterns, often resembling a miniature orchid. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit.

Lepanthes galeottiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
