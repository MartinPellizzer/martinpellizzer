Leymus triticoides subsp. multiflorus

Leymus triticoides subsp.

multiflorus, commonly known as the wheatgrass meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States. It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, including sandy and loamy soils.

The plant produces spikelets that are often clustered in dense inflorescences, with small, spikelet-like flowers that are typically green or pale yellow. It has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

This species is often found in open grasslands, disturbed sites, and along roadsides, contributing to its ecological versatility.
