Lobelia spicata var. hirtella

Lobelia spicata var. hirtella, commonly known as the hairy lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered with fine hairs, giving the plant its "hairy" appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
