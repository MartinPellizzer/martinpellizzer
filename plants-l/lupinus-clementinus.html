Lupinus clementinus

Lupinus clementinus, commonly known as the Clementine lupin, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of pea-like flowers. The plant produces small, bean-like pods that contain several seeds, which are often used as a food source by various animals.

Lupinus clementinus is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species that thrives in well-drained soils.
