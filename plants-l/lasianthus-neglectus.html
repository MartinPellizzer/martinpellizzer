Lasianthus neglectus

Lasianthus neglectus, commonly known as the neglected lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and purple. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical forests. It is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and blooms.

However, it is not widely known or commercially cultivated compared to other species in its family.
