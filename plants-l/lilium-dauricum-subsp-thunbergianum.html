Lilium dauricum subsp. thunbergianum

Lilium dauricum subsp.

thunbergianum, commonly known as the Siberian lily, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white to pink.

The plant typically grows in rocky or forested habitats and prefers well-drained soil. It is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in cooler climates.

The subspecies is closely related to other lilies in the genus Lilium, sharing similar botanical characteristics.
