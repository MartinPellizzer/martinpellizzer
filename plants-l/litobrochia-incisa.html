Litobrochia incisa

Litobrochia incisa, commonly known as the narrow-leafed brochidium, is a unique succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, fleshy, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a layer of white, waxy bloom. The plant produces small, pale green flowers that bloom in the early morning, typically during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and tend to wither quickly, giving way to the stems as the plant conserves water.

Litobrochia incisa is adapted to survive in extreme drought conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
