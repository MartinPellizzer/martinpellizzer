Lantana depressa var. floridana

Lantana depressa var. floridana, commonly known as Florida lantana, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and vibrant, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, showy blooms in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Florida lantana is adapted to warm, humid climates and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
