Lycium torreyi var. wrightii

Lycium torreyi var. wrightii, commonly known as the Wright's boxthorn, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish-orange berries that mature in the fall. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as rocky soils and desert plains. Its leaves are usually small, narrow, and covered in a fine layer of white, waxy hairs.

The species is part of the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshade plants.
