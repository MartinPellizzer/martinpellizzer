Lepidium divaricatum

Lepidium divaricatum, commonly known as the slender cress, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows to a height of about 20 to 40 centimeters, with small, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are white or pale yellow, arranged in racemes, and it produces small, triangular pods that contain several seeds.

The plant is often found in moist, calcareous soils and is sometimes cultivated as a weed in agricultural fields.
