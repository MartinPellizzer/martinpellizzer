Lens esculenta

Lens esculenta, commonly known as the lentil, is an annual legume belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, lens-shaped seeds that come in a variety of colors, including green, yellow, red, and black. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that develop into pods containing the edible seeds. Lentils are rich in protein, fiber, and essential nutrients, making them a valuable crop in agriculture.

They thrive in temperate climates and are cultivated worldwide for both human consumption and animal feed.
