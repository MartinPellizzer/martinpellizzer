Liparis harketii

Liparis harketii, commonly known as the Harket's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of Turkey.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas, preferring well-drained soils and partial shade. Its rhizomes are fibrous, and it produces small, lance-shaped leaves.

Liparis harketii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral structure.
