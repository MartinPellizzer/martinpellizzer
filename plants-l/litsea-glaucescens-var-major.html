Litsea glaucescens var. major

Litsea glaucescens var. major, commonly known as the broadleaf litsea, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including parts of China, India, and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its large, ovate leaves that are usually glossy and dark green on top with a lighter underside. It produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, reddish berries.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its aromatic properties.
