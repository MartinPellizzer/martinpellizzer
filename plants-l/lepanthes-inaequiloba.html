Lepanthes inaequiloba

Lepanthes inaequiloba, commonly known as the unequal-lobed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically feature a vivid mix of purple, pink, and white hues. The plant grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in moisture retention.

Lepanthes inaequiloba is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
