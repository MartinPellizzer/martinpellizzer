Lathyrus triopolitanus

Lathyrus triopolitanus, commonly known as the Triopian pea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

Lathyrus triopolitanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
