Libanotis hirsuta

Libanotis hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy libanotis, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Campanulaceae family, characterized by its bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its hairy leaves and stems are adaptations that help reduce water loss.
