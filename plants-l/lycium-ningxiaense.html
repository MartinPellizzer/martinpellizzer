Lycium ningxiaense

Lycium ningxiaense, commonly known as the Ningxia boxthorn, is a shrub native to the arid regions of northern China.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes and potatoes, and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces red berries that are edible and often used in traditional Chinese medicine. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperature fluctuations.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in various parts of Asia.
