Leucopogon macrocarpus

Leucopogon macrocarpus, commonly known as the broad-fruited leucopogon, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, which bloom in spring. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, and it thrives in well-drained, acidic soils.

Leucopogon macrocarpus is often found in heathlands and coastal regions, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
