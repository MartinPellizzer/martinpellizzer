Ligustrina pekinensis

Ligustrina pekinensis, commonly known as the Peking privet, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features opposite, simple, ovate leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by black, berry-like fruits. It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.

However, it can be invasive in some regions outside its native range.
