Lacaitaea calycosa

Lacaitaea calycosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a perennial herb native to the wetlands of North America.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional medicinal practices.

Lacaitaea calycosa thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found near streams or in swampy areas.
