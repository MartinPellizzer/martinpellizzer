Liabum homogamum

Liabum homogamum, commonly known as the common liabum, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple or pink. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals. It is native to regions in Europe and Asia and is often found in grassy areas or along roadsides.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
