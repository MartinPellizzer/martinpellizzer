Lilium Longiflorum Var. Alexandrae

Lilium longiflorum var. alexandrae is a rare and beautiful hybrid of the Easter lily known for its striking white petals and elegant form. This variant was developed in the early 20th century by horticulturists in the United States and is prized for its superior bloom quality and fragrance. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements. The plant is often associated with Easter celebrations due to its traditional white color and symbolic meaning. Despite its popularity, it remains a relatively uncommon cultivar in the wild.
