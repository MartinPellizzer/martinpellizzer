Leptosiphon filipes

Leptosiphon filipes, commonly known as the slender-flowered bluebonnet, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, blue to purple flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, ovate leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in prairie and rocky habitats.

Leptosiphon filipes is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
