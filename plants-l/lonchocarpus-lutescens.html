Lonchocarpus lutescens

Lonchocarpus lutescens, commonly known as yellow senna, is a tropical leguminous plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers, which bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in soil enrichment.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and reforestation projects.
