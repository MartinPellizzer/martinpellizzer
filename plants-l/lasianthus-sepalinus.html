Lasianthus sepalinus

Lasianthus sepalinus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flame flower, is a species of flowering plant in the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its striking red or orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Lasianthus sepalinus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
