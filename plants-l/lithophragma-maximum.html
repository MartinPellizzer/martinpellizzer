Lithophragma maximum

Lithophragma maximum, commonly known as the Oregon grape, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its evergreen leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. Lithophragma maximum thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.
