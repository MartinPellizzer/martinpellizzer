Lemaireocereus matucanensis

Lemaireocereus matucanensis, commonly known as the Matucanensis cactus, is a species of cactus native to the Philippines.

It is a small, columnar cactus with a distinctive appearance, featuring a pale green body covered in white to yellow spines. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone habitats, thriving in well-drained soil and full sun. It produces vibrant yellow flowers during the rainy season, which attract pollinators such as bats and insects.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat threats, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
