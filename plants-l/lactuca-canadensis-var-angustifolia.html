Lactuca canadensis var. angustifolia

Lactuca canadensis var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf lettuce, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It features slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, green flowers in clusters, which are followed by cylindrical seed pods. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

This variety is closely related to other lettuce species and is sometimes cultivated for its edible leaves.
