Lepanthes pseudocaulescens

Lepanthes pseudocaulescens, commonly known as the pseudocaulescent orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that often display vibrant colors. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as cloud forests, where it thrives on the forest floor or as an epiphyte. Its leaves are usually elongated and have a waxy surface to reduce water loss.

Lepanthes pseudocaulescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
