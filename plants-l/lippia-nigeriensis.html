Lippia nigeriensis

Lippia nigeriensis, commonly known as African verbena, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its erect, often branched stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. Lippia nigeriensis is adapted to grow in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance.
