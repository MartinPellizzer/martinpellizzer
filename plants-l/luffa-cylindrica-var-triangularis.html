Luffa cylindrica var. triangularis

Luffa cylindrica var. triangularis, commonly known as the triangular luffa, is a tropical vine belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive triangular-shaped fruits, which develop from the female flowers and mature into fibrous sponges. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires a long growing season to produce its edible or fibrous fruit. Its leaves are broad and lobed, and the stems are hairy and trailing.

The triangular luffa is often cultivated for its sponges, which are used in personal care and as natural cleaning tools.
