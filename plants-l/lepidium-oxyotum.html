Lepidium oxyotum

Lepidium oxyotum, commonly known as the narrowleaf wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard family plants. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to yellow. Its leaves are typically narrow and linear, giving it a distinct appearance.

Lepidium oxyotum is often found in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
