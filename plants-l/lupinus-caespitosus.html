Lupinus caespitosus

Lupinus caespitosus, commonly known as the bush lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to western North America.

It is characterized by its upright, bushy growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval to lance-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Lupinus caespitosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration due to its nitrogen-fixing capabilities.
