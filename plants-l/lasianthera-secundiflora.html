Lasianthera secundiflora

Lasianthera secundiflora, commonly known as the clustered sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its low-growing, sprawling habit and hairy, succulent leaves that help reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which bloom during the rainy season. Its root system is extensive and adapted for water storage, making it resilient to drought.

Lasianthera secundiflora is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal in dry landscapes.
