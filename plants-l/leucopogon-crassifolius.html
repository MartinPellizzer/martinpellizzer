Leucopogon crassifolius

Leucopogon crassifolius, commonly known as the broad-leafed heath, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to Australia and is typically found in heathlands and open forests. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy clusters of white or pale pink flowers. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often adapted to coastal or sandy environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
