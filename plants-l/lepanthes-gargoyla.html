Lepanthes gargoyla

Lepanthes gargoyla, commonly known as the gargoyle orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that often resemble gargoyle-like faces, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments within tropical forests. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with intricate patterns and a strong, sweet fragrance.

Lepanthes gargoyla is a terrestrial orchid that relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake, highlighting its ecological complexity.
