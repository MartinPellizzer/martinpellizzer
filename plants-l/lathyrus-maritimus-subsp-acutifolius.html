Lathyrus maritimus subsp. acutifolius

Lathyrus maritimus subsp.

acutifolius, commonly known as the sharp-leaved grass pea, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and parts of Europe. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or saline soils, often near the shorelines.

The plant produces clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. Its leaves are narrow and pointed, giving it the subspecies name *acutifolius*.

The plant is part of the legume family and has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize coastal dunes.
