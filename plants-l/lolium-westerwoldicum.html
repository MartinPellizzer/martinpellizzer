Lolium westerwoldicum

Lolium westerwoldicum, commonly known as Western meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, fields, and meadows. The plant has slender, tufted stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Lolium westerwoldicum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
