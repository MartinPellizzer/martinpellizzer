Loasa xanthiifolia

Loasa xanthiifolia, commonly known as the yellow-leaf loasa, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in high-altitude regions with cool, moist climates. The plant features palmate leaves with a yellowish-green hue, which give it its common name. Its small, fragrant flowers are usually white or pale yellow and bloom in clusters.

Loasa xanthiifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in temperate zones.
