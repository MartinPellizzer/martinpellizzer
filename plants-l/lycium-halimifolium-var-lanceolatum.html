Lycium halimifolium var. lanceolatum

Lycium halimifolium var. lanceolatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved boxthorn, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by red, fleshy berries. The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rocky or coastal environments.

Its ornamental value and hardy nature make it a popular choice for landscaping in arid climates.
