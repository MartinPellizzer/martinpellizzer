Leontodon longifolius

Leontodon longifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its yellow flowers bloom in late summer.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
