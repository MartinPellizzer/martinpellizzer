Lonicera pyrenaica f. majoricensis

Lonicera pyrenaica f. majoricensis, commonly known as the Majorica honeysuckle, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
