Lotus gebelia var. villosus

Lotus gebelia var. villosus, commonly known as the hairy lotus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, particularly India and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, showy pink or purple flowers. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name, and it often grows in moist, open habitats such as riverbanks and wetlands.

Its root system is notable for its ability to thrive in waterlogged soils, and it plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.
