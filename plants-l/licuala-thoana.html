Licuala thoana

Licuala thoana, commonly known as the giant swamp taro, is a large aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, arrowhead-shaped leaves that can grow up to 2 meters in length. The plant thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes and along the edges of slow-moving water bodies. Its rhizomes are sometimes used in traditional medicine, while its leaves are utilized in local cuisines.

Licuala thoana plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
