Lathyrus gmelinii

Lathyrus gmelinii, commonly known as the Siberian vetch, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple, followed by pods containing lens-shaped seeds. Lathyrus gmelinii is adapted to harsh environments, exhibiting frost resistance and the ability to thrive in poor soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of cold-region flora.
