Lilium medeoloides var. kurilense

Lilium medeoloides var. kurilense, commonly known as the Kuril lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Russian Far East and the Kuril Islands.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white to pale pink. The plant typically blooms in late summer, with the flowers often appearing in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with appropriate conditions.
