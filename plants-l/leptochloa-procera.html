Leptochloa procera

Leptochloa procera, commonly known as tall slender grass, is a species of grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets.

Leptochloa procera is often used in ecological studies due to its adaptability and role in soil stabilization.
