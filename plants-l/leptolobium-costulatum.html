Leptolobium costulatum

Leptolobium costulatum, commonly known as the narrow-ribbed everlet, is a member of the Fabaceae family and is native to South America.

It is a shrub characterized by its slender, often woody stems and trifoliate leaves with finely serrated edges. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pale yellow to cream in color, arranged in dense clusters. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several small, dark seeds.

Leptolobium costulatum is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
