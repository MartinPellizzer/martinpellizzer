Lupinus argyraeus

Lupinus argyraeus, commonly known as silver lupine, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-blue to white flowers and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or arid environments. Its seed pods are typically slender and contain several small, hard seeds.

Lupinus argyraeus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
