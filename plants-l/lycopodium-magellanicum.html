Lycopodium magellanicum

Lycopodium magellanicum, commonly known as the Magellanic clubmoss, is a small evergreen plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae and is characterized by its compact, moss-like appearance with spirally arranged leaves. The plant produces spore-bearing cones at the tips of its stems, which are typically clustered in small groups. Lycopodium magellanicum thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rock crevices.

Its historical significance includes use in traditional medicine and as a source of spores for scientific study.
