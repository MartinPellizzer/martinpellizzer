Leptochloa mexicana

Leptochloa mexicana, commonly known as Mexican narrowleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has small, spikelet-based inflorescences and produces wind-dispersed seeds.

Leptochloa mexicana is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soils.
