Lippia savoryi

Lippia savoryi, commonly known as Mexican bush bean, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of South America.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Lippia savoryi is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects.
