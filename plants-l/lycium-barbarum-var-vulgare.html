Lycium barbarum var. vulgare

Lycium barbarum var. vulgare, commonly known as Chinese wolfberry or goji berry, is a shrub native to China and other parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its red, fleshy berries that are rich in antioxidants. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture, while its flowers are small and yellowish-white.

The berries are often cultivated for their nutritional value and traditional medicinal uses.
