Leucopogon gracillimus

Leucopogon gracillimus, commonly known as the slender leucopogon, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils in open woodlands and pine savannas. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing seeds.

Leucopogon gracillimus is often found in wetland habitats and is adapted to tolerate periodic flooding.
