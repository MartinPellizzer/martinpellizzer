Ligularia tangutica

Ligularia tangutica, commonly known as the Tangut giant bellflower, is a striking herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its large, showy yellow-orange flowers that resemble bells. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive white margin, giving it an ornamental appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas at higher altitudes.

Ligularia tangutica is valued for its unique floral display and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
