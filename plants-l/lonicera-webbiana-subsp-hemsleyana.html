Lonicera webbiana subsp. hemsleyana

Lonicera webbiana subsp.

hemsleyana, commonly known as the Hemsley honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to China. It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, fragrant, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun, making it a suitable ornamental plant in gardens.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and horticultural use.
