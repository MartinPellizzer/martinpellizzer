Loricaria ferruginea

Loricaria ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty loricaria, is a species of aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its floating leaves that are typically oval or lance-shaped and have a reddish-brown coloration. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or yellowish in color. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes.

Loricaria ferruginea is often used in aquariums due to its ornamental appearance and ease of care.
