Lycopsis variegata

Lycopsis variegata, commonly known as the variegated lycopsis, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Lycopsidaceae.

It is characterized by its small, delicate leaves that often display a variegated pattern of green and white or yellow. The plant produces tiny, clustered flowers that are typically green or pale yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Lycopsis variegata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
