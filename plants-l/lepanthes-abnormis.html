Lepanthes abnormis

Lepanthes abnormis, commonly known as the bizarre orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure and vibrant colors. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are notable for their intricate petal arrangements and often display shades of purple and white.

Lepanthes abnormis is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the biodiversity of its native ecosystems.
