Leontodon glaucescens

Leontodon glaucescens, commonly known as the blue-haired hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped with rough textures, and the flowers are yellow with a distinctive blue-gray hue on the bracts.

Leontodon glaucescens is often found in open habitats and can tolerate a range of environmental conditions.
