Lepanthes cauda-avis

Lepanthes cauda-avis, commonly known as the "Bird's Tail Orchid," is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated flower spikes that resemble a bird's tail, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within tropical forests. Its flowers are usually white or pale green, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Lepanthes cauda-avis is a terrestrial orchid that thrives in well-drained soil and requires consistent moisture for optimal growth.
