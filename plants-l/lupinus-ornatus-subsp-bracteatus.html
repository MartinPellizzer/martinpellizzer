Lupinus ornatus subsp. bracteatus

Lupinus ornatus subsp.

bracteatus, commonly known as the Sierra lupine, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers.

The plant features pinnately compound leaves with numerous small leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or open habitats.

Its showy flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.
