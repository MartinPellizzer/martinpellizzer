Lasianthus microphyllus

Lasianthus microphyllus, commonly known as the small-leaf lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its flowers are often pale blue or purple and attract various pollinators.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
