Lessingianthus foliosus

Lessingianthus foliosus, commonly known as the Brazilian sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its foliage is lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking blooms.
