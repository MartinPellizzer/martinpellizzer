Lippia densispicata

Lippia densispicata, commonly known as Mexican mint or blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, mint-scented leaves. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is relatively easy to cultivate in gardens.
