Loranthus tschintschochensis

Loranthus tschintschochensis, commonly known as the Tschintschochensis mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees.

It belongs to the Loranthaceae family and lacks true roots, relying on its host for water and nutrients. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or greenish in color. Its seeds are dispersed by birds, which consume the fleshy, yellowish berries.

This species is found in the tropical and subtropical regions of Central and South America.
