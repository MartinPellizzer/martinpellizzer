Launaea almahrahensis

Launaea almahrahensis, commonly known as the Mahrah dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are usually basal, arranged in a rosette, and covered with a fine pubescence.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in spring, producing small, wind-dispersed seeds.
