Lavatera venosa

Lavatera venosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Lavatera venosa is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
