Linaria aegyptiaca var. villosa

Linaria aegyptiaca var. villosa, commonly known as the Egyptian toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving the plant a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
