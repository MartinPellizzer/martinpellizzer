Lepidium ruderale var. simplicissimum

Lepidium ruderale var. simplicissimum, commonly known as slender shepherd's purse, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, triangular seed pods that resemble a purse, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is found in various regions across Europe and Asia. It has simple, alternate leaves that are usually lance-shaped and hairy.

The flowers are small and yellow, arranged in racemes, and bloom during the spring and early summer.
