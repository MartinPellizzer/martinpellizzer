Laetia floribunda

Laetia floribunda, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces small, elongated fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardens and greenhouses.
