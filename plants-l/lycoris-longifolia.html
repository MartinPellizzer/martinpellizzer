Lycoris longifolia

Lycoris longifolia, commonly known as the Chinese lantern lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that resemble small lanterns, which bloom in the autumn season. The plant has strap-like, narrow leaves that are typically green and emerge from a bulbous underground stem. Lycoris longifolia is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and visually appealing addition to the landscape.
