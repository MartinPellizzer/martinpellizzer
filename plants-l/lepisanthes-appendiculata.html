Lepisanthes appendiculata

Lepisanthes appendiculata, commonly known as the appendiculate orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, pendulous inflorescences that bear small, fragrant flowers with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are elongated and often covered in fine, soft hairs, which help in moisture retention.

The orchid's unique floral structure and adaptability to specific ecological niches make it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
