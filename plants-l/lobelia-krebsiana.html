Lobelia krebsiana

Lobelia krebsiana, commonly known as the purple lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Lobelia krebsiana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
