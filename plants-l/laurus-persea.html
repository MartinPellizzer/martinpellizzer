Laurus persea

Laurus persea, commonly known as the avocado tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces a fruit with a large, oily seed. The plant has a shallow root system and thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil. Its leaves are aromatic and often used in traditional medicine.

The fruit is rich in nutrients, particularly monounsaturated fats, making it a valuable agricultural crop.
