Linum scabrellum

Linum scabrellum, commonly known as rough flax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe habitats.

Linum scabrellum is not widely cultivated but is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological role.
