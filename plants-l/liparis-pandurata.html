Liparis pandurata

Liparis pandurata, commonly known as the pandurata orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, linear leaves and intricate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant prefers shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories. Its flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow with a distinctive lip that is broader than the other petals.

Liparis pandurata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
