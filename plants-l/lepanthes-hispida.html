Lepanthes hispida

Lepanthes hispida, commonly known as the hairy lepanthes, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive, hairy leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale green with intricate patterns. Lepanthes hispida thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a popular subject for both horticultural enthusiasts and botanical researchers.
