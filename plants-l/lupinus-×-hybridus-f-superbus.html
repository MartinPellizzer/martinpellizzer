Lupinus × hybridus f. superbus

Lupinus × hybridus f. superbus, commonly known as the tall lupine, is a hybrid perennial plant characterized by its upright growth habit and tall, branching stems.

It features showy, pea-like flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant is native to the western United States and thrives in well-drained soils, often found in coastal and mountainous regions. It has lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive blue-green hue and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a variety of morphological traits that make it adaptable to different environmental conditions.
