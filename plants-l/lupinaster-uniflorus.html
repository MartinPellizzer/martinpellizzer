Lupinaster uniflorus

Lupinaster uniflorus, commonly known as the single-flower lupinaster, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, solitary flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple or pink. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Lupinaster uniflorus is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to thrive in arid or semi-arid environments.
