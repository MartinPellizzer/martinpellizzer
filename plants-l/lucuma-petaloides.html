Lucuma petaloides

Lucuma petaloides, commonly known as the golden fruit tree, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its distinctive, elongated fruits that resemble small, golden eggs. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a leathery texture with a strong aromatic scent.

The fruit is typically harvested when it reaches maturity, revealing a sweet, creamy pulp with a unique, mild flavor.
