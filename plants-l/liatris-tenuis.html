Liatris tenuis

Liatris tenuis, commonly known as narrowleaf blazing star, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and showy, spiky flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges. Its narrow, linear leaves are arranged alternately along the stem, and the flowers are usually pink to purple in color.

Liatris tenuis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
