Lespedeza capitata var. typica

Lespedeza capitata var. typica, commonly known as the common lespezea, is a perennial herb native to Asia and parts of the United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pinkish to reddish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or disturbed sites.

Lespedeza capitata var. typica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
