Leucopogon lanceolatus var. gracilis

Leucopogon lanceolatus var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender leucopogon, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is a perennial herb with slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in dense spikes, typically blooming in spring. It prefers well-drained sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as heaths and woodland edges.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Ericaceae family.
