Lupinus bimaculatus

Lupinus bimaculatus, commonly known as the two-spotted lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces showy, pea-like flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Lupinus bimaculatus has small, rounded seed pods that contain several seeds, and it is adapted to grow in dry, well-drained soils.

This species is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.
