Lespedeza daurica var. shimadae

Lespedeza daurica var. shimadae, commonly known as Daurian lespeza, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

Its adaptability and ecological importance make it a notable species in its native range.
