Lycopsis elongata

Lycopsis elongata, commonly known as the slender lycopus, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in whorls along the stems. Lycopsis elongata is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in herbal medicine.
