Linociera mannii

Linociera mannii, commonly known as the African white wood, is a tropical tree native to Central and West Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and large, simple leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by woody, elongated fruits containing several seeds. Linociera mannii is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture making.

It plays an important role in the ecosystems of its native regions, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
