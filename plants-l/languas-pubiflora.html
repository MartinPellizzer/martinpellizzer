Languas pubiflora

Languas pubiflora, commonly known as the woolly leaf plant, is a species native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to bright indirect light.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens with suitable conditions.
