Lachnastoma

Lachnastoma, commonly known as the woolly aphid, is a genus of aphids belonging to the family Aphididae.

These small, soft-bodied insects are characterized by their dense, waxy coating, which gives them a fuzzy appearance. They are primarily found on various plants, where they feed on sap using their specialized mouthparts. Lachnastoma species are known for their ability to produce honeydew, which can lead to the growth of sooty mold on host plants.

Their life cycle often includes winged and wingless forms, with some species overwintering as eggs.
