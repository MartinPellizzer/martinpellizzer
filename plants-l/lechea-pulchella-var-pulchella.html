Lechea pulchella var. pulchella

Lechea pulchella var. pulchella, commonly known as the showy milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

This species is also a host plant for the monarch butterfly, making it ecologically significant in its native range.
