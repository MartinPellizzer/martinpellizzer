Liatris spicata var. savannensis

Liatris spicata var. savannensis, commonly known as the tall blueweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, spikelike inflorescence composed of numerous blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and sandy soils. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, often forming a basal rosette.

It is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
