Luma fuscovelutina

Luma fuscovelutina, commonly known as the velvet tree, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in the Andean regions of Peru and Bolivia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, dark bark and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind and animals.

Luma fuscovelutina is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
