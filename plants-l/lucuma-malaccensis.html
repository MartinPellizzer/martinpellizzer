Lucuma malaccensis

Lucuma malaccensis, commonly known as the Malacca lucuma, is a species of flowering plant in the family Ericaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are encased in a fibrous husk, resembling a citrus fruit in appearance. The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
