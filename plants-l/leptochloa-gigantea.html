Leptochloa gigantea

Leptochloa gigantea, commonly known as the giant flatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant has slender, flat leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, greenish-brown flowers.

Leptochloa gigantea is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
