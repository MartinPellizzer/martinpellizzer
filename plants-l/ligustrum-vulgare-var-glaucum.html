Ligustrum vulgare var. glaucum

Ligustrum vulgare var. glaucum, commonly known as the whitewood privet, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically a bluish-green color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are followed by dark blue berries. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its compact growth habit and seasonal interest.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions, outcompeting native vegetation.
