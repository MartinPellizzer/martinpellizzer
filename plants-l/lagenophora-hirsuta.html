Lagenophora hirsuta

Lagenophora hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, often with a hairy texture on the petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Lagenophora hirsuta is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
