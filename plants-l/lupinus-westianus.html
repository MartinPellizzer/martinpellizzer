Lupinus westianus

Lupinus westianus, commonly known as the western lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces spiny, cylindrical seed pods that contain several small, bean-like seeds. Lupinus westianus is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

It plays an important ecological role by fixing nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plant life.
