Lasianthus harveyanus var. longifolius

Lasianthus harveyanus var. longifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved Harvey's lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are elongated and often have a velvety texture, while the flowers are usually pink or purple in color.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
