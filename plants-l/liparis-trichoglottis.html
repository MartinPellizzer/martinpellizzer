Liparis trichoglottis

Liparis trichoglottis, commonly known as the hairy-lip orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale green flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil.

The orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
