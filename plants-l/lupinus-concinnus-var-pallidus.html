Lupinus concinnus var. pallidus

Lupinus concinnus var. pallidus, commonly known as the pale lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.
