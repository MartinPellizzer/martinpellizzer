Leontopodium nivale subsp. alpinum

Leontopodium nivale subsp.

alpinum, commonly known as the alpine edelweiss, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its dense, woolly white foliage and showy, yellow-centered flower heads that bloom in late summer.

The plant thrives in cold, rocky environments and is adapted to withstand harsh mountain conditions. Its flowers are typically visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ecological significance and aesthetic appeal, it is often protected in its native habitats.
