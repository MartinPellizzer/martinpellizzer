Lycium chilense var. filifolium

Lycium chilense var. filifolium, commonly known as the Chilean boxthorn, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny growth habit and small, red berries that are edible. The plant has small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to harsh climates.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
