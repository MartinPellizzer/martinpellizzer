Linaria elatine

Linaria elatine, commonly known as the lesser flaxflower, is a low-growing annual plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features slender, branching stems that bear small, yellow-orange flowers with two lips, typical of the Scrophulariaceae family. The plant has opposite, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along riverbanks.

Linaria elatine is known for its ability to self-seed and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
