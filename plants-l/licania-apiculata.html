Licania apiculata

Licania apiculata, commonly known as the Brazilian candlewood, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth texture. It produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits.

Licania apiculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
