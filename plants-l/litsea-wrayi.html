Litsea wrayi

Litsea wrayi, commonly known as the Wray's litsea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by small, spherical berries.

Litsea wrayi is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal uses.
