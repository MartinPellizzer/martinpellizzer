Lens monantha

Lens monantha, commonly known as the slender cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that attract pollinators. Its seeds are edible and often used in various culinary traditions.

Botanically, it is valued for its adaptability and role in sustainable agriculture.
