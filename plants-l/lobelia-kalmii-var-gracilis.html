Lobelia kalmii var. gracilis

Lobelia kalmii var. gracilis, commonly known as slender lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems covered in small, lance-shaped leaves that are often slightly hairy. The plant produces clusters of tubular, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in wetland gardens or as a ground cover in shady areas.
