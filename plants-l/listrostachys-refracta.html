Listrostachys refracta

Listrostachys refracta, commonly known as the brittlestemmed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of South America.

It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant features grass-like leaves that are narrow and linear, with a distinctive bluish-green color. Its inflorescence consists of small, clustered flowers that are often concealed within bracts.

The species is characterized by its brittle stems, which contribute to its common name and make it somewhat challenging to handle.
