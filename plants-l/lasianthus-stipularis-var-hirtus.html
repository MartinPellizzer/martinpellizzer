Lasianthus stipularis var. hirtus

Lasianthus stipularis var. hirtus, commonly known as the hairy lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs, giving the plant its "hairy" appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
