Limonium ikaricum

Limonium ikaricum, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers and spiny, leaf-like bracts that surround the flower heads. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and saline soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Limonium ikaricum is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
