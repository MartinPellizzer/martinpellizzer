Lupinus urcoensis

Lupinus urcoensis, commonly known as the Andean lupine, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue or purple pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to the harsh conditions of mountainous regions.

Lupinus urcoensis plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
