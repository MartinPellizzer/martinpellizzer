Lasianthus tomentosus

Lasianthus tomentosus, commonly known as the woolly lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, or tomentose, foliage. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are clustered in whorled inflorescences and are often pink or purple in color.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
