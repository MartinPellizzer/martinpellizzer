Luzula antarctica

Luzula antarctica, commonly known as the Antarctic sedge, is a flowering plant native to the cold regions of the Southern Hemisphere.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. It is adapted to survive in harsh, nutrient-poor soils and extreme temperatures.

Luzula antarctica plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems in its native habitat.
