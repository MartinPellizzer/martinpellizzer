Lepidium virginicum f. pusillum

Lepidium virginicum f. pusillum, commonly known as small-leaved peppergrass, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of tiny, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils such as roadside ditches and wet meadows. It produces small, oval seeds that are dispersed by wind.

The species is part of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many edible and economically important plants.
