Loasa volubilis

Loasa volubilis, commonly known as the creeping loasa, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its showy, tubular flowers are usually pink or violet and bloom in spring.

Loasa volubilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
