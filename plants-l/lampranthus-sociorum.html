Lampranthus sociorum

Lampranthus sociorum, commonly known as the firecracker flower, is a member of the Aizoaceae family, native to southern Africa.

It is a succulent shrub that produces vibrant, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom in late summer. Its unique flower structure attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.

Lampranthus sociorum is also valued for its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal in arid landscapes.
