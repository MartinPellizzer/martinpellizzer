Linaria supina subsp. ajmasiana

Linaria supina subsp.

ajmasiana, commonly known as the prostrate toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a low-growing perennial that produces vibrant yellow-orange flowers resembling those of the common toadflax.

The plant features slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs and has narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
