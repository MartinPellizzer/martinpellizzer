Laportea conduplicata

Laportea conduplicata, commonly known as the velvetleaf or broadleaf cudweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features soft, fuzzy leaves that are typically heart-shaped or ovate in form. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its root system is fibrous and often grows close to the soil surface.

Laportea conduplicata is often found in moist, disturbed soils along roadsides and in woodland edges.
