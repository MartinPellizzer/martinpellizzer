Lysimachia assamica

Lysimachia assamica, commonly known as the Assam yellow mosquito plant, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal.

It is a member of the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are clustered in racemes.

Lysimachia assamica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
