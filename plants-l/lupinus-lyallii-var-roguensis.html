Lupinus lyallii var. roguensis

Lupinus lyallii var. roguensis, commonly known as the Roguenes lupine, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open meadows and disturbed sites.

Lupinus lyallii var. roguensis plays a role in soil stabilization and is a valuable species for ecological restoration projects.
