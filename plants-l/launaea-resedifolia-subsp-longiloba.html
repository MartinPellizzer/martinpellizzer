Launaea resedifolia subsp. longiloba

Launaea resedifolia subsp.

longiloba, commonly known as the long-stemmed desert leaf plant, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East. It features a robust root system that allows it to thrive in poor soil conditions and withstand drought.

The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that store water, and its stems are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This species is adapted to extreme environments and plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils in its native habitat.
