Lupinus lechlerianus

Lupinus lechlerianus, commonly known as the yellow lupine, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and bright yellow flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. Lupinus lechlerianus is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils and open habitats.

It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native range.
