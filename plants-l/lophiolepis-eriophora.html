Lophiolepis eriophora

Lophiolepis eriophora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lichen grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette formation. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, often with a reddish or purple tinge. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Lophiolepis eriophora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
