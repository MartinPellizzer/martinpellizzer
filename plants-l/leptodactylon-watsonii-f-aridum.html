Leptodactylon watsonii f. aridum

Leptodactylon watsonii f. aridum, commonly known as the desert spurge, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers during the rainy season, which are followed by capsule fruits containing seeds. Its leaves are typically reduced to small scales, minimizing water loss through transpiration.

This species is well adapted to harsh, low-nutrient soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
