Lilium chalcedonicum var. polyanthos

Lilium chalcedonicum var. polyanthos, commonly known as the polyanthus lily, is a striking bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its clustered, fragrant flowers that bloom in a range of colors from white to deep red, often with a distinctive bicolor pattern. The plant features strap-like, grassy leaves that form a dense rosette, and its stems are sturdy and upright. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and unique floral display, it is valued in horticulture for its beauty and resilience.
