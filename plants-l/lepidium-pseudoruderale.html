Lepidium pseudoruderale

Lepidium pseudoruderale, commonly known as the narrowleaf shepherd's purse, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, erect growth habit and pinnately divided leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are typically borne on slender stems. Its fruit is a dry, segmented capsule that dehisces to release tiny seeds.

Lepidium pseudoruderale is often found in disturbed soils and is native to regions of Europe and Asia.
