Lotus anthylloides

Lotus anthylloides, commonly known as the white lotus, is a perennial aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, supported by long, slender stems. Its roots are anchored in the mud at the bottom of ponds and lakes.

Lotus anthylloides is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and is also used in traditional medicine.
