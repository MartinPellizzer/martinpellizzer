Lantana mearnsii

Lantana mearnsii, commonly known as the Mexican firebush, is a shrub native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, brightly colored flowers, typically in shades of yellow, orange, or red, which bloom throughout the year in many regions. Lantana mearnsii is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in full sun.

However, it can be invasive in some areas, outcompeting native vegetation.
