Lacinaria scariosa var. inconcinna

Lacinaria scariosa var. inconcinna, commonly known as the scarious lacinaria, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered in a distinctive, papery sheath. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough, textured surface.

This variant is notable for its unique morphology and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
