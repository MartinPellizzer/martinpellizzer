Lamium garganicum subsp. grandiflorum

Lamium garganicum subsp.

grandiflorum, commonly known as the large-flowered dead-nettle, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It features heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces clusters of violet or purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It is part of the mint family (Lamiaceae) and has a creeping growth habit with stolons.

The flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
