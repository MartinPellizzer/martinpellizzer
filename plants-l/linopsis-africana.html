Linopsis africana

Linopsis africana, commonly known as the African ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the moist, shaded undergrowth of tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, translucent leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant thrives in humid environments, often growing on tree trunks or rocks, and relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient absorption. Its flowers, though not showy, emit a faint fragrance that attracts specific pollinators.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, Linopsis africana is considered endangered and is protected in many regions.
