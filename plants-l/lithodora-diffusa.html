Lithodora diffusa

Lithodora diffusa, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, sky-blue to violet flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a delicate ornamental appeal. The plant has a compact, mat-forming growth habit with hairy, oval-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Lithodora diffusa is also valued for its aromatic foliage and ability to attract pollinators.
