Lupinus angustifolius subvar. maissurianii

Lupinus angustifolius subvar. maissurianii, commonly known as the Maissurian lupine, is a perennial legume native to the Caucasus region.

It features slender, upright stems and pinnately compound leaves with narrow, linear leaflets. The plant produces tall spikes of blue to violet flowers, which are typically arranged in dense clusters. It is adapted to rocky and dry environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and nitrogen-fixing capabilities through its root nodules.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in soil enrichment.
