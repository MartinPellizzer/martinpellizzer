Lycium pilosum

Lycium pilosum, commonly known as the hairy boxthorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features woody stems covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red, fleshy berries. Its leaves are typically elliptical and have a slightly hairy texture.

Lycium pilosum is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
