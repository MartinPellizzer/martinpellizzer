Listera smallii

Listera smallii, commonly known as the Sierra dwarf white violet, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with heart-shaped leaves and small, white or pale purple flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of delicate flowers that attract pollinators. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky stream banks.

Listera smallii is characterized by its compact size and is often found in montane and subalpine regions.
