Ligularia paradoxa

Ligularia paradoxa, commonly known as the Paradox Ligularia, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in diameter, often with a dark green color and prominent veins. The plant produces showy yellow-orange flowers in the summer, which are borne on tall, slender stems. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is a popular choice for shade gardens.
