Lophatherum gracile f. elatum

Lophatherum gracile f. elatum, commonly known as the tall slender lemongrass, is a variant of the lemongrass plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like appearance with narrow, aromatic leaves that emit a citrusy scent. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb, similar to other lemongrass species.

The botanical features of this plant make it valuable for both ornamental and practical purposes.
