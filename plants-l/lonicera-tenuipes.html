Lonicera tenuipes

Lonicera tenuipes, commonly known as the slender honeysuckle, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and white, typically blooming in late spring. Its fruit is a red, fleshy capsule that ripens in summer, containing several seeds.

Lonicera tenuipes is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.
