Lithophila scirpoides

Lithophila scirpoides, commonly known as the rock rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows on rocky outcrops and in dry, sandy soils, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers. Lithophila scirpoides is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
