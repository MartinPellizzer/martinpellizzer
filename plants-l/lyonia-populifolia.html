Lyonia populifolia

Lyonia populifolia, commonly known as the paperleaf lyonia, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, white-veined leaves that resemble paper, hence its common name. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in clusters during the spring, which are typically pink or purple in color. Lyonia populifolia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
