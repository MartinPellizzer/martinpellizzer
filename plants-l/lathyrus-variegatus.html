Lathyrus variegatus

Lathyrus variegatus, commonly known as the variable vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it typically grows in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils. The plant features compound leaves with a distinctive variegated pattern, and its flowers are usually purple or pink, arranged in racemes. It produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds.

Lathyrus variegatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in ecological and agricultural contexts.
