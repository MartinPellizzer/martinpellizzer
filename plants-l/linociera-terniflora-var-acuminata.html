Linociera terniflora var. acuminata

Linociera terniflora var. acuminata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved linociera, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticultural gardens.
