Larnax ramosa

Larnax ramosa, commonly known as the branched larnax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and dense, branching structure. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. Larnax ramosa thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
