Lagochilus acutilobus

Lagochilus acutilobus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved helenia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly pointed tip. Its flowers are typically small, clustered in whorls, and range in color from pale blue to violet.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
