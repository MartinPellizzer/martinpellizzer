Linaria origanifolia var. glareosa

Linaria origanifolia var. glareosa, commonly known as the golden tickseed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branching stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
