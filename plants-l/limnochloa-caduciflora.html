Limnochloa caduciflora

Limnochloa caduciflora, commonly known as the weeping flax, is a herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its drooping, slender stems that bear small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces delicate, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Limnochloa caduciflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native ecosystems.
