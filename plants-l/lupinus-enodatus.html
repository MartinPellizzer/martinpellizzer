Lupinus enodatus

Lupinus enodatus, commonly known as the slender lupin, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces legume pods that contain several seeds. Lupinus enodatus is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
