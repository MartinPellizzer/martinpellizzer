Leymus racemosus subsp. crassinervius

Leymus racemosus subsp.

crassinervius, commonly known as the broad-veined wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States. It is characterized by its tall, erect growth and dense, tufted foliage.

The plant has coarse, parallel-veined leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. It produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

This subspecies is often found in disturbed soils and is valued for its role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for livestock.
