Loranthus geminatus

Loranthus geminatus, commonly known as the twinleaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees.

It belongs to the family Loranthaceae and is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and yellowish or reddish berries. The plant lacks true roots and absorbs water and nutrients from its host through specialized structures called haustoria. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in the Americas.

Its presence can have both positive and negative effects on the health of its host tree.
