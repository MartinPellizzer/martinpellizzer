Lepidagathis incurva var. ustulata

Lepidagathis incurva var. ustulata, commonly known as the scarlet bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tubular, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in a fine, waxy coating.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
