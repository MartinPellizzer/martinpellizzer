Ladenbergia cujabensis

Ladenbergia cujabensis, commonly known as the Cuja bean tree, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in regions of Colombia and Venezuela.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant is notable for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.

It produces small, fragrant flowers that attract pollinators, and its seeds are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
