Linaria azerbaijanensis

Linaria azerbaijanensis, commonly known as Azerbaijani toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region, particularly Azerbaijan.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and bright yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its flowers are tubular with two lips, a feature common in many species of the genus Linaria.

It is a relatively small plant, usually reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters.
