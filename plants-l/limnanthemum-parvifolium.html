Limnanthemum parvifolium

Limnanthemum parvifolium, commonly known as the small-leaved water violet, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in eastern North America.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant features small, heart-shaped leaves that are usually less than 2 inches in diameter, with a smooth, waxy surface. Its flowers are pale blue or violet, with five petals and are typically less than an inch in size.

Limnanthemum parvifolium plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
