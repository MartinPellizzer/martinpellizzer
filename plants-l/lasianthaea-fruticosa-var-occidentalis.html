Lasianthaea fruticosa var. occidentalis

Lasianthaea fruticosa var. occidentalis, commonly known as the western everlet, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has small, elliptical leaves with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental appeal make it a valuable addition to native plant collections.
