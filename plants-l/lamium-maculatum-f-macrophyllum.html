Lamium maculatum f. macrophyllum

Lamium maculatum f. macrophyllum, commonly known as the large-leaved deadnettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or silver markings. The plant produces clusters of small, two-lipped, pink or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or gardens.

Its foliage is frequently used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
