Lastrea tristis

Lastrea tristis, commonly known as the weeping fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite and ovate in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers followed by orange-red fruits containing one to three seeds. It is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in a variety of indoor environments.

However, it is important to note that it can be toxic if ingested, making it unsuitable for households with pets or young children.
