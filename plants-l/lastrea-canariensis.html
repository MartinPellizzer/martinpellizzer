Lastrea canariensis

Lastrea canariensis, commonly known as the Canary Islands rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or coastal environments, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions. The plant produces showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are small, fleshy, and often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
