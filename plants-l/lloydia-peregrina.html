Lloydia peregrina

Lloydia peregrina, commonly known as the wandering lily, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Liliaceae and is characterized by its striking white or pink flowers with a subtle fragrance. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and colonize new areas effectively.

Lloydia peregrina is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological significance.
