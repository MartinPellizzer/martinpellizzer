Lastrea muzensis

Lastrea muzensis, commonly known as the Muzungo cactus, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that store water for survival in dry environments. The plant produces small, white flowers during the rainy season, adding a striking contrast to its rugged appearance. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss and deterring herbivores.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, Lastrea muzensis is considered a rare and endangered species.
