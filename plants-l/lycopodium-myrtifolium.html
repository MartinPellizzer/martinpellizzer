Lycopodium myrtifolium

Lycopodium myrtifolium, commonly known as the myrtleleaf clubmoss, is a small, evergreen plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, moss-like growth habit and bright green, scale-like leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, spore-bearing cones called strobili, which are typically clustered at the base of the plant. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories or on rocky slopes.

Lycopodium myrtifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological systems as a habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
