Leucadendron penicillatum

Leucadendron penicillatum, commonly known as the silver leaf leucadendron, is a member of the Proteaceae family native to South Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and cone-shaped inflorescences that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are often visited by birds and insects, contributing to its ecological importance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for horticultural use.
