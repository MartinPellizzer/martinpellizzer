Licaria terminalis

Licaria terminalis, commonly known as the Brazilian licorice or licorice tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Malvaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil and is characterized by its woody structure and palmate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Licaria terminalis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.
