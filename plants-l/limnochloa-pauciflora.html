Limnochloa pauciflora

Limnochloa pauciflora, commonly known as the few-flowered watergrass, is a member of the Poaceae family, characterized by its aquatic or semi-aquatic growth habit.

It typically features slender, grass-like stems that are often submerged or floating in wetland environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered near the base of the plant, contributing to its low floral display. Its leaves are usually narrow and linear, adapted for efficient water uptake and photosynthesis in moist conditions.

Limnochloa pauciflora plays a role in stabilizing aquatic sediments and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
