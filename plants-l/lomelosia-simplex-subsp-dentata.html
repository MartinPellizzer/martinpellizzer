Lomelosia simplex subsp. dentata

Lomelosia simplex subsp.

dentata, commonly known as the toothed lomelosia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central America. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in cloud forests and rocky slopes.

The plant features lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, giving it its subspecific name "dentata.". Its flowers are small and clustered, usually in inflorescences that bloom in spring.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
