Lonchocarpus polystachyus

Lonchocarpus polystachyus, commonly known as the giant senna, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in racemes. The plant produces large, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.

Due to its nitrogen-fixing ability, it plays a role in soil enrichment in ecological systems.
