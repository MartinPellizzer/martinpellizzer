Leptomeria billardierei

Leptomeria billardierei, commonly known as the narrow-leafed rock heath, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale pink flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and open woodlands.

Leptomeria billardierei is adapted to dry conditions and is an important component of the local flora in its native habitat.
