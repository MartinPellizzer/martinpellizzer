Lupinus perennis subsp. latifolius

Lupinus perennis subsp.

latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf lupine, is a perennial herb native to North America. It features upright, branched stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height.

The plant produces dense clusters of pea-like, blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and are often covered in fine hairs.

This species thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.
