Lupinus sericeus var. fikerianus

Lupinus sericeus var. fikerianus, commonly known as the silver lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its finely hairy stems and clusters of blue to violet pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets.

It plays an important role in nitrogen fixation, improving soil fertility in its native habitat.
