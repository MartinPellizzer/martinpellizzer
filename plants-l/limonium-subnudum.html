Limonium subnudum

Limonium subnudum, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flower heads, which are surrounded by narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and saline soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Limonium subnudum is often used in ornamental gardening due to its drought tolerance and aesthetic appeal.
