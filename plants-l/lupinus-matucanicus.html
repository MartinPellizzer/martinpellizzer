Lupinus matucanicus

Lupinus matucanicus, commonly known as Matucan lupine, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and along stream banks.

Lupinus matucanicus plays an important role in soil stabilization and provides nectar for pollinators.
