Lysipomia reniformis

Lysipomia reniformis, commonly known as the kidneyleaf, is a small flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its kidney-shaped leaves and small, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments. Its flowering period occurs during the summer months, and it produces seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Lysipomia reniformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
