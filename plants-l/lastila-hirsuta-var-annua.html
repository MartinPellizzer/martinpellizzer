Lastila hirsuta var. annua

Lastila hirsuta var. annua, commonly known as the hairy lastila, is a perennial herb native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It features hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with reddish or purple hues. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical interest lies in its unique foliage and potential ecological role in its native habitat.
