Lasianthaea lundellii

Lasianthaea lundellii, commonly known as the desert bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and thrives in full sun, exhibiting a low, sprawling growth habit. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in a fine layer of white hair, which helps reduce water loss.

The species is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a resilient component of desert ecosystems.
