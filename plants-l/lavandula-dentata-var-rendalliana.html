Lavandula dentata var. rendalliana

Lavandula dentata var. rendalliana, commonly known as Rendall's lavender, is a fragrant shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces spikes of pale purple to lilac flowers, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic properties make it valuable in both ornamental gardening and essential oil production.
