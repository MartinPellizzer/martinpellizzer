Lavandula × cavanillesii

Lavandula × cavanillesii, commonly known as the narrow-leaf lavender, is a hybrid shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and fragrant, spike-like flowers that range in color from pale purple to deep violet. The plant produces aromatic essential oils in its foliage and flowers, which are widely used in perfumery and aromatherapy. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in many parts of the world.
