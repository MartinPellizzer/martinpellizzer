Lepidoceras peruvianum

Lepidoceras peruvianum, commonly known as the Peruvian cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the family Cactaceae and is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with thick, fleshy stems that store water. It typically grows at high altitudes, often in rocky or sandy soils.

Lepidoceras peruvianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in regions with suitable climates.
