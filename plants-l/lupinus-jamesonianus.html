Lupinus jamesonianus

Lupinus jamesonianus, commonly known as the Jameson's lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of pea-like blue or purple flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Lupinus jamesonianus is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with soil bacteria.

It plays an important ecological role by enriching soil fertility and providing nectar for pollinators.
