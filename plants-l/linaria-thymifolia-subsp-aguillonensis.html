Linaria thymifolia subsp. aguillonensis

Linaria thymifolia subsp.

aguillonensis, commonly known as the Aguillon's toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its aromatic scent. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in rocky or sandy habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
