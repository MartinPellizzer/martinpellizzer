Linnaea brachystemon

Linnaea brachystemon, commonly known as the twinflower, is a small evergreen shrub native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, bell-shaped white or pink flowers in pairs, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Linnaea brachystemon is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local insect populations.
