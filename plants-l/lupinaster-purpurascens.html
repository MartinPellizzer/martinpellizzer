Lupinaster purpurascens

Lupinaster purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-flowered lupinaster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, purple-pink flowers. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in rocky or well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate to subtropical climates.

Lupinaster purpurascens is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plant life.
