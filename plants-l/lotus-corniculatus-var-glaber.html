Lotus corniculatus var. glaber

Lotus corniculatus var. glaber, commonly known as the slender bird's-foot trefoil, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves, which consist of three small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in some ecological restoration projects.
