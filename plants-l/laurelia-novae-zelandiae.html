Laurelia novae-zelandiae

Laurelia novae-zelandiae, commonly known as the New Zealand laurel, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes other aromatic plants like laurel and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are typically glossy and elliptical in shape. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by small, spherical berries.

Laurelia novae-zelandiae is often found in coastal and lowland forest habitats, where it thrives in moist, temperate conditions.
