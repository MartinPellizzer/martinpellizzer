Lapageria hookeri

Lapageria hookeri, commonly known as the Andean bellflower, is a striking plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a perennial shrub that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features thick, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in its high-altitude habitat. Lapageria hookeri is known for its ability to thrive in cool, shaded environments with well-drained soil.

Its unique floral structure and vibrant colors make it a popular choice for botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
