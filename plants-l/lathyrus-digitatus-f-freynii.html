Lathyrus digitatus f. freynii

Lathyrus digitatus f. freynii, commonly known as the Freyn's spurred vetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive spurred petals and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats. Its flowers, which range in color from pink to purple, bloom in early summer, and it produces small, elongated pods containing several seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
