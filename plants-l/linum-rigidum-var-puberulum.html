Linum rigidum var. puberulum

Linum rigidum var. puberulum, commonly known as the woolly flax, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for low-maintenance gardens.
