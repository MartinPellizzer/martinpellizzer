Linconia tamariscina

Linconia tamariscina, commonly known as the scarlet hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Linconia tamariscina is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
