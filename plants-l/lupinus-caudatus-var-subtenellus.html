Lupinus caudatus var. subtenellus

Lupinus caudatus var. subtenellus, commonly known as the slender lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Lupinus caudatus var. subtenellus plays a role in nitrogen fixation and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
