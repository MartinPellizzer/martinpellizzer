Landolphia calabarica

Landolphia calabarica, commonly known as the African morning glory, is a climbing plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces a milky sap that contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are woody and can grow quite long.

It is often found in moist, shaded areas and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
