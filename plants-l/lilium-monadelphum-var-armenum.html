Lilium monadelphum var. armenum

Lilium monadelphum var. armenum, commonly known as the Armenian lily, is a striking perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey.

It features tall, slender stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, bearing large, showy flowers with six to eight petals. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, often with a distinctive red or purple tinge at the base. This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is cultivated in gardens for its elegance and fragrance.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, thriving in temperate climates.
