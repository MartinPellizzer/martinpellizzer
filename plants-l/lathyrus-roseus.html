Lathyrus roseus

Lathyrus roseus, commonly known as the pink pea or sweet pea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features climbing stems with trifoliate leaves and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white. Lathyrus roseus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking both aesthetic and ecological benefits.
