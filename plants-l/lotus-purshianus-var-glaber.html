Lotus purshianus var. glaber

Lotus purshianus var. glaber, commonly known as the naked lotus, is a herbaceous perennial native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has smooth, glabrous stems and leaves that are typically trifoliate, with heart-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to hot, dry climates.

This species is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
