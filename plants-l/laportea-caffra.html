Laportea caffra

Laportea caffra, commonly known as African violet, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 cm and thrives in well-drained soil with partial shade. Its leaves are hairy and have a distinctive, aromatic scent, while the flowers are borne in clusters at the tips of the stems.

Laportea caffra is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
