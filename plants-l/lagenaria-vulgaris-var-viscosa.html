Lagenaria vulgaris var. viscosa

Lagenaria vulgaris var. viscosa, commonly known as the sponge gourd, is a flowering plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, inflated, sponge-like fruits that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces yellow flowers and has a trailing or climbing growth habit. Its leaves are heart-shaped and hairy, while the stems are covered in fine, sticky hairs.

This species is often cultivated for its edible fruits, which are used in various culinary traditions.
