Lobelia ardisiandroides

Lobelia ardisiandroides, commonly known as the red-veined lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its striking, violet-blue flowers with prominent red veins, which are clustered in dense, showy inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Lobelia ardisiandroides is valued in horticulture for its vibrant color and ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates.
