Lycoseris crocata

Lycoseris crocata, commonly known as the crocus lily, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family, which includes irises and related genera. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a distinctive crocus-like appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Lycoseris crocata is known for its early spring blooming habit, adding vibrant color to gardens when many other plants are still dormant.
