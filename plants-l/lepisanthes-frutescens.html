Lepisanthes frutescens

Lepisanthes frutescens, commonly known as the purple passionvine, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible but often contain toxic compounds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
