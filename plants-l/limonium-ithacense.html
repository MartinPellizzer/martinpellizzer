Limonium ithacense

Limonium ithacense, commonly known as the Ithaca sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and saline soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that help reduce water loss.

Limonium ithacense is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained conditions.
