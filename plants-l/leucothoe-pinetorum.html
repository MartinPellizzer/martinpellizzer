Leucothoe pinetorum

Leucothoe pinetorum, commonly known as the pineleaf leucothoe, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage. The plant features narrow, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, which are followed by red berries in the fall.

Leucothoe pinetorum thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
