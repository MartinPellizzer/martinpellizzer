Limonia oligandra

Limonia oligandra, commonly known as the Natal plum, is a small evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture.

Limonia oligandra is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
