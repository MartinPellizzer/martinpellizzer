Lepanthes octopus

Lepanthes octopus, commonly known as the octopus orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central and South America.

This epiphytic plant is characterized by its unique, intricate flower structure that resembles the tentacles of an octopus, giving it its common name. It typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, tropical environments. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, purple, and pink, which bloom during the rainy season.

Lepanthes octopus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
