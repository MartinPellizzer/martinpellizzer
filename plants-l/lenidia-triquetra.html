Lenidia triquetra

Lenidia triquetra, commonly known as the three-cornered leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically three-angled in cross-section, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in loose clusters, which bloom in early summer. Lenidia triquetra thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens or wildflower borders.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
