Lupinus venustus tricolor

Lupinus venustus tricolor, commonly known as the tri-color lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its strikingly colorful flowers that typically display three distinct hues. The plant features palmately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and its stems are often hairy and upright. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for native gardens and restoration projects.

Lupinus venustus tricolor also plays a role in nitrogen fixation, benefiting the surrounding soil ecosystem.
