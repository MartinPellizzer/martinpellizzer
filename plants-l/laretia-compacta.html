Laretia compacta

Laretia compacta, commonly known as the desert hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptation to drought conditions. The plant features small, showy yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and early summer. Its leaves are small, oval, and often covered in a fine pubescence, which helps reduce water loss.

Laretia compacta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in low-water landscapes.
