Lobelia propinqua

Lobelia propinqua, commonly known as Indian tobacco, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces striking blue to purple tubular flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Lobelia propinqua is also valued for its historical use in traditional medicine.
