Lepidanthemum triplinervium

Lepidanthemum triplinervium, commonly known as the three-veined rockcress, is a small flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many edible and ornamental plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a distinct three-veined pattern on the underside.

The flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and arranged in clusters, making it a notable species for rock gardens and alpine habitats.
