Lepidium flavum var. apterum

Lepidium flavum var. apterum, commonly known as the yellow cress, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and leaves that are often covered in fine, hair-like structures. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is found in temperate regions. It has a compact growth habit and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and horticultural applications.
