Logfia lanceolata

Logfia lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf logfia, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It features elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along its stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often blooming in late spring. Its root system is fibrous and has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its purported tonic properties.

Logfia lanceolata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial shade, making it a suitable addition to woodland gardens.
