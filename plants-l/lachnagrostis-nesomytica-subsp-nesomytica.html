Lachnagrostis nesomytica subsp. nesomytica

Lachnagrostis nesomytica subsp.

nesomytica, commonly known as the Nesomytic grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa. It typically grows in coastal dunes and sandy soils, exhibiting a low, spreading growth habit.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is adapted to harsh environmental conditions, including salt spray and sandy substrates.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of coastal vegetation in its native range.
