Lampranthus paardebergensis

Lampranthus paardebergensis, commonly known as the Paardeberg ice plant, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, water-storing leaves and showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically blooms in winter, producing bright yellow or orange flowers that attract pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its common name reflects the appearance of its frost-like flowers and its habitat in the Paardeberg region.
