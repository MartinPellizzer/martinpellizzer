Leucopogon angustatus var. hirsutus

Leucopogon angustatus var. hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy narrowleaf heath, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is a shrub characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of pale pink to white flowers, which bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to coastal conditions with high salinity and wind exposure.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
