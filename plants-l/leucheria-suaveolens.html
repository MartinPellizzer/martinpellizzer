Leucheria suaveolens

Leucheria suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented leucheria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its pleasant aroma.
