Lilium superbum f. plenum

Lilium superbum f. plenum, commonly known as the full-flowered Turk's-cap lily, is a botanical variant of the Turk's-cap lily.

It is characterized by its large, showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink to reddish in color. The plant is native to the eastern United States and thrives in woodland habitats. It features strap-like leaves and produces bulbous underground stems.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
