Lecanorchis nigricans var. nigricans

Lecanorchis nigricans var. nigricans, commonly known as the black orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black flowers, which are typically clustered in a dense spike. The plant has a slender, erect stem with few leaves, and its roots are fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils. It thrives in moist, shaded woodlands and is often found in association with decaying organic matter.

The species is part of the Orchidaceae family and is notable for its unique coloration and ecological niche.
