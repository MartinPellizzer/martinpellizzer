Lobivia albolanata

Lobivia albolanata, commonly known as the White-Lobed Lithops, is a species of succulent native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its unique, stone-like appearance with rounded, fleshy leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers during the spring season. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its striking appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice among cactus and succulent enthusiasts.
