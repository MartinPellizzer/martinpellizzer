Lythrum americanum

Lythrum americanum, commonly known as American rosemary, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Lythraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, clustered pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows in wet soils along stream banks and marshes, showcasing its adaptability to moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

The plant is also valued for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in herbal remedies.
