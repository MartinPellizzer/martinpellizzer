Lunasia amara

Lunasia amara, commonly known as the bitter moonflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly found in regions of Brazil, Argentina, and Paraguay. The plant produces striking white flowers that open at night, emitting a mild fragrance to attract nocturnal pollinators. Its leaves are ovate with a rough texture, and the stems are hairy, typical of many solanaceous species.

The fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, hard seeds.
