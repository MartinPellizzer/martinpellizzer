Lonicera shintenensis

Lonicera shintenensis, commonly known as the Japanese honeysuckle, is a deciduous climbing vine native to East Asia.

It features opposite, simple, and often heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a small, red berry that ripens in the fall, containing several seeds.

Lonicera shintenensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
