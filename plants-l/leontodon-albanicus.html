Leontodon albanicus

Leontodon albanicus, commonly known as the Albanian dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Leontodon albanicus is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
