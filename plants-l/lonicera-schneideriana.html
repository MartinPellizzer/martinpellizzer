Lonicera schneideriana

Lonicera schneideriana, commonly known as the Schneider's honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces red berries that are an important food source for birds and small mammals. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it both a valuable ecological plant and a potential invasive species in some regions.
