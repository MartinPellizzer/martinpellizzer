Loranthus flavidus

Loranthus flavidus, commonly known as the yellow mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of various host trees.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using the light it captures. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring. Its berries are small and red, containing seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This species is often found in temperate regions and is considered an important part of the ecosystem for both its host trees and wildlife.
