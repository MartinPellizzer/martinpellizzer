Lysimachia glaucina

Lysimachia glaucina, commonly known as silverleaf threadleaf, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Asia and North America.

It features small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery-white bloom, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellowish-green flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Lysimachia glaucina is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
