Lyonia buchii

Lyonia buchii, commonly known as the dwarf lyonia, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy, with a leathery texture. It produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

Lyonia buchii is well adapted to rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
