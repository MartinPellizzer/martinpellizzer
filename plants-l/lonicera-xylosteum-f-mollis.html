Lonicera xylosteum f. mollis

Lonicera xylosteum f. mollis, commonly known as the fuzzy honeysuckle, is a unique variant of the honeysuckle plant characterized by its soft, hairy foliage.

It is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically growing in woodland edges and hedgerows. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in the spring, which are followed by orange-red berries in the fall. Its leaves are thick and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This form is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to attract pollinators.
