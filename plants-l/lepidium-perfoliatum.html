Lepidium perfoliatum

Lepidium perfoliatum, commonly known as Indian cress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky soils and is often found in alpine environments. Its leaves are alternate, lobed, and sometimes have a peppery taste, while its stems are often perforated, giving it the species name "perfoliatum.".

It is also known for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of edible seeds.
