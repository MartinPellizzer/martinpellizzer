Lathyrus montanus var. tenuifolius

Lathyrus montanus var. tenuifolius, commonly known as the slender-leaved sweet pea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and thrives in well-drained, loamy soils. It is a member of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing ability through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in temperate climates.
