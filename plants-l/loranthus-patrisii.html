Loranthus patrisii

Loranthus patrisii, commonly known as the Chinese mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of various host trees.

It belongs to the family Loranthaceae and is native to regions of East Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are often clustered together. Its berries are typically red or orange and contain several seeds that are dispersed by birds.

Loranthus patrisii plays a role in forest ecosystems by influencing host tree growth and providing food for wildlife.
