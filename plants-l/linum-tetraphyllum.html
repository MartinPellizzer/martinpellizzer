Linum tetraphyllum

Linum tetraphyllum, commonly known as the four-leaf flax, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is a low-growing herb with slender stems and small, blue or violet flowers. The plant has four petals, which give it its common name, and it typically blooms in late summer. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Linum tetraphyllum is adapted to cold climates and has a short growing season.
