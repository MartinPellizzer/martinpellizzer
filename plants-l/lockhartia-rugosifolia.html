Lockhartia rugosifolia

Lockhartia rugosifolia, commonly known as the wrinkled-leaf orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, wrinkled leaves that give it its scientific name. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often with a yellow or orange lip. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
