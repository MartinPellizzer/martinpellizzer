Limonium suworowii

Limonium suworowii, commonly known as the coastal blueflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and produces clusters of small, sky-blue to white flowers. The plant thrives in coastal environments, tolerating salty air and sandy soils. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Limonium suworowii is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
