Linociera intermedia

Linociera intermedia, commonly known as the Mexican beautyberry, is a shrub native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, bright purple berries that remain on the plant throughout the winter. The plant has opposite, simple, and elliptic-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Linociera intermedia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.
