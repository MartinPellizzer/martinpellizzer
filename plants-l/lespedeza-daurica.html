Lespedeza daurica

Lespedeza daurica, commonly known as Daurian lespedeza, is a perennial flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks. It produces small, pink to purple flowers in spikes, and its leaves are alternate, trifoliate, with a hairy texture.

Lespedeza daurica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, including soil stabilization and wildlife habitat support.
