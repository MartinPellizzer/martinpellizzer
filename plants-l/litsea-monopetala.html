Litsea monopetala

Litsea monopetala, commonly known as the single-flower litsea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of China, India, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and solitary, fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a small, dark-colored drupe that ripens in the summer.

Litsea monopetala is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
