Lobivia haematantha subvar. sublimiflora

Lobivia haematantha subvar. sublimiflora, commonly known as the snow-flowered lobivia, is a succulent plant native to the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It features a compact, branching stem with thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that are often white or pale pink, blooming in late winter or early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal water due to its arid origin.

This subvariety is prized for its ornamental value and resilience in harsh desert conditions.
