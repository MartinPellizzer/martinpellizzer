Lathyrus mutabilis

Lathyrus mutabilis, commonly known as the variable vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to parts of Europe and Asia and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, depending on environmental conditions. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

Lathyrus mutabilis is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural purposes.
