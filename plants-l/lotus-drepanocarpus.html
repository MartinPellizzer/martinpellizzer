Lotus drepanocarpus

Lotus drepanocarpus, commonly known as the sicklepod lotus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including India and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive sickle-shaped pods. The plant typically grows in wetland areas and is often found near water bodies such as ponds and marshes. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
