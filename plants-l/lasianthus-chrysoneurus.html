Lasianthus chrysoneurus

Lasianthus chrysoneurus, commonly known as the golden-horned flameflower, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically orange to red in color. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
