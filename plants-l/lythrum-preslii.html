Lythrum preslii

Lythrum preslii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Lythraceae family, which includes other genera such as Lagerstroemia and Vaccinium. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats like meadows and woodland edges. It produces striking pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its foliage is characterized by narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.
