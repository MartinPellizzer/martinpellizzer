Leandra australis var. phaeotricha

Leandra australis var. phaeotricha, commonly known as the golden leandra, is a striking evergreen shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage that exhibits a range of colors from deep green to reddish-brown, depending on the season. The plant produces showy, fragrant yellow or golden-orange flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for coastal and garden landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in horticultural settings.
