Lycopodium reflexointegrum

Lycopodium reflexointegrum, commonly known as the ground pine, is a species of clubmoss that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, scale-like leaves that are often green or reddish in color. The plant produces spore-bearing cones, which are typically clustered at the base of the stems. Lycopodium reflexointegrum is a perennial plant that can live for many years, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil in woodland areas.

Its historical use in traditional medicine has also contributed to its recognition in botanical studies.
