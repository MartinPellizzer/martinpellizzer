Leontice leontopetalum subsp. ewersmannii

Leontice leontopetalum subsp.

ewersmannii, commonly known as the Ewersmann's leontice, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers that range from white to pale pink in color.

The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open woodlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained conditions.
