Lychnis grandiflora var. haageana

Lychnis grandiflora var. haageana, commonly known as the Haage's catchfly, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its succulent leaves and showy, pink or red flowers. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. Its flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
