Lachnagrostis perennis

Lachnagrostis perennis, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-hued leaves and compact growth habit, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer, adding subtle visual interest. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, requiring minimal maintenance once established.

Lachnagrostis perennis is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and support local pollinators.
