Languas angustifolia

Languas angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed lanugo, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
