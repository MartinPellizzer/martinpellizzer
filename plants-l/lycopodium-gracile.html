Lycopodium gracile

Lycopodium gracile, commonly known as the slender clubmoss, is a small, evergreen fern-like plant that belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing form and its production of spore-bearing cones called strobila. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are narrow and linear, arranged in whorls around the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Lycopodium gracile plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and small invertebrates.
