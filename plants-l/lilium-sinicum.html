Lilium sinicum

Lilium sinicum, commonly known as the Chinese lily, is a species of flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions and has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern. Its bulbous roots are used in traditional Chinese medicine, and the flowers are often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

Lilium sinicum is known for its resilience and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
