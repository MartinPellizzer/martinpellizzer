Lonicera × tellmanniana

Lonicera × tellmanniana, commonly known as the evergreen honeysuckle, is a hybrid shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to yellow. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds but not for humans. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

Lonicera × tellmanniana is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
