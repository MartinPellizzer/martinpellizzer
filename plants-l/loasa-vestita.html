Loasa vestita

Loasa vestita, commonly known as the velvetleaf loasa, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 meters. The plant features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are usually covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. Its flowers are small, clustered in panicles, and often have five petals with a distinctive yellowish or greenish hue.

Loasa vestita is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage and floral display.
