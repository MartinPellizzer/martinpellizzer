Litsea josephi

Litsea josephi, commonly known as the Chinese tallow tree, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange-red berries that contain sticky, oily seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other parts of the world, though it can become invasive in some regions.
