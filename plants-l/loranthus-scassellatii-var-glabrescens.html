Loranthus scassellatii var. glabrescens

Loranthus scassellatii var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smoothleaf mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees.

It lacks true roots and absorbs nutrients and water through its haustoria, which penetrate the host's vascular system. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers and red berries that are dispersed by birds. It is native to parts of Africa and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the ecosystems where it grows.
