Loranthus mayombensis var. acutifolius

Loranthus mayombensis var. acutifolius, commonly known as the African mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees.

It has small, elliptical leaves that are usually arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are often clustered together. Its berries are typically red or orange, containing seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This species is native to parts of Africa and is often found in forested areas.
