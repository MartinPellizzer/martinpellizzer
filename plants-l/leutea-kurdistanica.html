Leutea kurdistanica

Leutea kurdistanica, commonly known as the Kurdish bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Kurdistan.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, hairy texture, which helps reduce water loss.

Leutea kurdistanica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
