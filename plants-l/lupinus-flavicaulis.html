Lupinus flavicaulis

Lupinus flavicaulis, commonly known as the yellowstem lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellowish stems. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush areas.

Lupinus flavicaulis is valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a potential forage plant for livestock.
