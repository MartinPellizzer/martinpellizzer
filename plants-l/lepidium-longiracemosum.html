Lepidium longiracemosum

Lepidium longiracemosum, commonly known as the long-stemmed peppergrass, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces elongated, racemose inflorescences that bear tiny, four-petaled flowers. Its leaves are alternate, linear, and often covered with fine hairs.

Lepidium longiracemosum is typically found in disturbed soils and arid regions, thriving in full sun.
