Laurus foetens

Laurus foetens, commonly known as the stinking laurel, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small white flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically red or black when mature. It is often found in rocky soils and coastal areas, thriving in mild, temperate climates.

Due to its aromatic leaves and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
