Lundia corymbifera

Lundia corymbifera, commonly known as the clustered lantern plant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, lantern-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Lundia corymbifera thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its unique floral structure and drought tolerance make it an interesting addition to xeriscapes.
