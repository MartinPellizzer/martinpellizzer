Licania diamenia

Licania diamenia, commonly known as the Brazilian white senna, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Licania diamenia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
