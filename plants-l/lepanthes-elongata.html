Lepanthes elongata

Lepanthes elongata, commonly known as the elongated slipper orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pendant flowers that often display vibrant colors such as pink, white, or yellow. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories where it relies on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. Its flowers are specialized for pollination by specific insects, and it is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relatively easy cultivation.

Lepanthes elongata is a member of the Orchidaceae family, which includes over 25,000 species of flowering plants.
