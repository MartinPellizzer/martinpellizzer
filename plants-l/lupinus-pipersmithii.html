Lupinus pipersmithii

Lupinus pipersmithii, commonly known as Pipersmith's lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of blue or purple flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces cylindrical seed pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Lupinus pipersmithii plays an important role in its ecosystem by fixing nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plant life.
