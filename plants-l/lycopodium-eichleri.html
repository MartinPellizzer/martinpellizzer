Lycopodium eichleri

Lycopodium eichleri, commonly known as the Eichleri clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. The plant features a creeping stem with whorls of narrow, needle-like leaves. It produces sporophylls that bear spores, which are released into the air for reproduction.

Lycopodium eichleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological systems.
