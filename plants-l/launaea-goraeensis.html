Launaea goraeensis

Launaea goraeensis, commonly known as the African groundsel, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as a low shrub with hairy, gray-green foliage. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces achenes as its fruit.

Launaea goraeensis is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.
