Lamium michaelianum

Lamium michaelianum, commonly known as Michael's dead nettle, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, two-lipped flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorled clusters along the stem. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, often with a reddish or purple tinge on the underside.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
