Lupinus louise-grisetae

Lupinus louise-grisetae, commonly known as the Louise Griseta lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds.

This species is of interest to botanists and gardeners due to its ornamental value and ecological significance.
