Lotus halophilus

Lotus halophilus, commonly known as the saltbush lotus, is a halophytic plant adapted to thrive in saline environments.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its ability to tolerate high concentrations of salt in its habitat. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, marshes, and saline soils, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion. Its small, pink or white flowers are followed by pods that contain edible seeds.

Lotus halophilus is also valued for its nutritional content and is used in traditional cuisines in certain regions.
