Lonchocarpus maculatus

Lonchocarpus maculatus, commonly known as the spotted legume, is a tropical plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive flowers with bright yellow petals. The plant produces elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is native to regions in South America.

Due to its toxic properties, it requires careful handling and is not typically cultivated for ornamental purposes.
