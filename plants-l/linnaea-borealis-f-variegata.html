Linnaea borealis f. variegata

Linnaea borealis f. variegata, commonly known as the variegated twinflower, is a rare and striking variant of the twinflower plant.

It is characterized by its unique foliage, which displays a mix of green and white or yellow patterns, giving it a distinctive appearance. This plant is native to the boreal forests of northern North America and parts of Eurasia. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The flowers are small, pinkish-white, and emit a subtle fragrance, attracting pollinators such as bees and flies.
