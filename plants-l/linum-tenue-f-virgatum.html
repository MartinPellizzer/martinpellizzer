Linum tenue f. virgatum

Linum tenue f. virgatum, commonly known as the slender flax or narrow-leaved flax, is a flowering plant belonging to the Linaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces delicate blue or purple flowers that bloom in early summer. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in fiber production.
