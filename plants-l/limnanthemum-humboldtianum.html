Limnanthemum humboldtianum

Limnanthemum humboldtianum, commonly known as the Humboldt's water lily, is a tropical aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves that form a dense canopy on the water surface. The plant produces showy, white to pink flowers that bloom throughout the wet season. Its roots are anchored in the mud at the bottom of still or slow-moving waters.

Limnanthemum humboldtianum plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.
