Lepidium draba

Lepidium draba, commonly known as whitlow grass, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in alpine and arctic regions, often found in rocky or gravelly soils. The plant has small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are alternate, linear, and often covered in fine hairs.

Lepidium draba is known for its ability to thrive in harsh environments, making it an important component of high-altitude ecosystems.
