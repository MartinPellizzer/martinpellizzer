Luzula campestris var. elata

Luzula campestris var. elata, commonly known as the tall marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with greenish-yellow flowers. It is often found in association with other wetland vegetation and plays a role in stabilizing soil in these environments.

Luzula campestris var. elata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland ecosystems.
