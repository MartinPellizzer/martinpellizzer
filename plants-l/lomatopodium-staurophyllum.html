Lomatopodium staurophyllum

Lomatopodium staurophyllum, commonly known as the star-flowered lomatopodium, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has a low, spreading growth habit. Its leaves are usually linear and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and clustered in a star-like arrangement.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
