Loeflingia hispanica var. pentandra

Loeflingia hispanica var. pentandra, commonly known as the Spanish loeflingia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky and calcareous soils, often found in dry, sunny environments. The plant features upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its small, yellowish flowers are clustered in umbels and bloom in the spring.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
