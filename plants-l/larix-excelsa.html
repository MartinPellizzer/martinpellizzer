Larix excelsa

Larix excelsa, commonly known as the dawn redwood, is a deciduous conifer native to China.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and conical shape, with slender, pendulous branches. The tree produces small, reddish-brown cones that mature in the fall, and its needles are bright green and shed annually. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its historical rarity and unique appearance, it is considered a valuable ornamental and ecological species.
