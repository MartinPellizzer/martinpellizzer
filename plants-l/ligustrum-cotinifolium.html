Ligustrum cotinifolium

Ligustrum cotinifolium, commonly known as the fragrant privet, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It features opposite, simple, ovate leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by small, dark berries in the fall. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

The species is also valued for its use in hedges and as a landscape plant due to its adaptability and low maintenance requirements.
