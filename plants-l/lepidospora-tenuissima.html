Lepidospora tenuissima

Lepidospora tenuissima, commonly known as the slender lepidospora, is a rare and delicate fern-like plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

The plant has a short, creeping rhizome and produces small, inconspicuous spore-bearing structures called sorus. Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered endangered and is often found in protected areas.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and ecological role in tropical understory ecosystems.
