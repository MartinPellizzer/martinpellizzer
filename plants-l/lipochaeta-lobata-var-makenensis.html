Lipochaeta lobata var. makenensis

Lipochaeta lobata var. makenensis, commonly known as the KwaZulu-Natal ironweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal and sandy habitats, often in open vegetation. It has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and is known for its hardy nature.

This variant is distinguished by its specific geographic distribution and morphological traits.
