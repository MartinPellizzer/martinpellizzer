Lithocardium cujabense

Lithocardium cujabense, commonly known as the Cujabense stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in arid environments. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, often in shades of yellow or orange.

Lithocardium cujabense is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to drought conditions.
