Leonurus turkestanicus var. turkestanicus

Leonurus turkestanicus var. turkestanicus, commonly known as the Turkestan hedgehog.

It is a perennial herb native to Central Asia, characterized by its hairy stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and it produces distinctive, spiny seed pods that burst to disperse seeds. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or semi-arid habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
