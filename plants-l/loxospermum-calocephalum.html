Loxospermum calocephalum

Loxospermum calocephalum, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, blue-violet flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in moist to wet habitats such as swamps and marshes.

Loxospermum calocephalum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for pollinators.
