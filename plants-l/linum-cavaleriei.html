Linum cavaleriei

Linum cavaleriei, commonly known as the Cavalerie flax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and delicate, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its seeds are small and oval.

Linum cavaleriei is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in fiber production.
