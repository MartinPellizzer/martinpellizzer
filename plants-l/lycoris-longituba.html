Lycoris longituba

Lycoris longituba, commonly known as the Chinese sacred lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking red flowers that bloom in the autumn. The plant features strap-like leaves that emerge from a bulbous underground stem. Its flowers are often associated with symbolic meanings in Chinese culture, representing farewell and remembrance.

Lycoris longituba thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
