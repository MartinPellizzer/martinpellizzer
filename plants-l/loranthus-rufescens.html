Loranthus rufescens

Loranthus rufescens, commonly known as the red-stemmed mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of host trees, primarily in the genus Eucalyptus.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using sunlight absorbed by its leaves. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its parasitic nature and ecological role in forest ecosystems make it a significant component of its native habitats.

Loranthus rufescens is often found in regions of Australia and is adapted to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
