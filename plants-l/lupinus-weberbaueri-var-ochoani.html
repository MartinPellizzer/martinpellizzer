Lupinus weberbaueri var. ochoani

Lupinus weberbaueri var. ochoani, commonly known as the Ochoan lupine, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and clusters of blue to violet pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Lupinus weberbaueri var. ochoani is often found in chaparral and desert scrub ecosystems.
