Lawsonia

Lawsonia, commonly known as the henna plant, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Lythraceae.

It is native to regions of Africa, the Middle East, and southern Asia, and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The most well-known species, Lawsonia inermis, produces a red dye from its dried leaves, which has been used for centuries in hair and body art. The plant typically features small, fragrant flowers and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

Its botanical significance extends to its use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural pigments.
