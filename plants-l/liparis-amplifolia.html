Liparis amplifolia

Liparis amplifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern, providing a striking visual appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for shaded garden environments.

Liparis amplifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
