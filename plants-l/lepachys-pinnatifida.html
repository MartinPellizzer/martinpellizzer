Lepachys pinnatifida

Lepachys pinnatifida, commonly known as the narrow-leafed dandelion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellow composite flowers that develop into fluffy seed heads. The plant is native to regions of southern Africa and is often found in grassy or disturbed habitats. It has a taproot system and is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

The species is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value or used in traditional medicine.
