Limonium anatolicum

Limonium anatolicum, commonly known as the Anatolian sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spikes of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, often covered in fine hairs.

Limonium anatolicum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate saline environments.
