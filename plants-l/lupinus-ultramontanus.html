Lupinus ultramontanus

Lupinus ultramontanus, commonly known as the Sierra lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Lupinus ultramontanus plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain butterfly species.
