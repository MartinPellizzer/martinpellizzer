Lycopodioides bryopteris

Lycopodioides bryopteris, commonly known as the fern moss, is a small, epiphytic plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, moss-like growth habit and its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments. The plant features a root system that anchors it to surfaces such as tree bark or soil, and its sporophyte stage produces spores in specialized structures called sporangia. Lycopodioides bryopteris is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, contributing to the biodiversity of forest ecosystems.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as an indicator species for humid, undisturbed habitats.
