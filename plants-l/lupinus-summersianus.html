Lupinus summersianus

Lupinus summersianus, commonly known as the Summer lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. It produces vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets.
