Lagerstroemia subcostata var. glabra

Lagerstroemia subcostata var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth crape myrtle, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark that reveals layers of different colors, ranging from gray to reddish-brown. The plant produces showy clusters of pink to red flowers in summer, which are followed by small, persistent fruits. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly serrated margin.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
