Lappula occidentalis var. cupulata

Lappula occidentalis var. cupulata, commonly known as the western lappula, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense, cup-like structures, giving it its common name. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

This variant is often found in disturbed areas and is considered a member of the Brassicaceae family.
