Luzula multiflora var. colchica

Luzula multiflora var. colchica, commonly known as the Colchic Meadow Sedge, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is a perennial herb with grass-like foliage, characterized by its narrow, dark green leaves and dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces tiny, three-lobed flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and forested areas.

Luzula multiflora var. colchica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
