Lonchocarpus lanceolatus

Lonchocarpus lanceolatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf poinciana, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with lance-shaped leaflets and clusters of bright red or orange flowers. The plant produces distinctive pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its showy blooms.

Botanically, it belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
