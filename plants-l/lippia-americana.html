Lippia americana

Lippia americana, commonly known as American verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and prairies. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are aromatic and have been used in traditional herbal remedies.

Lippia americana is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
