Lamium capitatum

Lamium capitatum, commonly known as purple deadnettle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features opposite, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive purple underside, and its flowers are small, tubular, and clustered in dense whorls at the ends of the stems. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks. It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
