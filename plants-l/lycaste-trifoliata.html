Lycaste trifoliata

Lycaste trifoliata, commonly known as the three-leaf orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, yellow, and pink. The plant has strap-like leaves that are usually arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Lycaste trifoliata thrives in humid, warm environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for with proper light and watering conditions.
