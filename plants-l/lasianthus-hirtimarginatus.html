Lasianthus hirtimarginatus

Lasianthus hirtimarginatus, commonly known as the hairy-margin gentian, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with hairy margins, giving it its common name.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
