Leucas mollis

Leucas mollis, commonly known as white leucas, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are usually opposite, oval-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Leucas mollis is often grown for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
