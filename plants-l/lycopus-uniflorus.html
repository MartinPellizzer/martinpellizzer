Lycopus uniflorus

Lycopus uniflorus, commonly known as blueweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North America.

It typically grows in moist habitats such as wet meadows, marshes, and stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive blue-green color. Its small, tubular flowers are usually blue or purple and bloom in clusters during the summer months.

Lycopus uniflorus is known for its aromatic foliage and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported calming effects.
