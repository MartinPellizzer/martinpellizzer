Leptormus rivalis

Leptormus rivalis, commonly known as the red-bellied bowerbird, is a striking bird native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant plumage, with males displaying a combination of red, black, and blue feathers to attract mates. The species is known for its elaborate courtship displays, where males construct intricate bower structures to impress females. Its habitat includes dense forests and undergrowth, where it forages for fruits, insects, and small vertebrates.

Leptormus rivalis plays a role in seed dispersal and contributes to the ecological balance of its ecosystem.
