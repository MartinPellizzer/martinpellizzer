Lightfootia tenella var. longivalvis

Lightfootia tenella var. longivalvis, commonly known as the longivalve lightfootia, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, lance-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil.
