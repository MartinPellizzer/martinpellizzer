Lathyrus hygrophilus

Lathyrus hygrophilus, commonly known as water pea, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to Europe and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several elliptical, brown seeds. It thrives in moist environments, often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.

Lathyrus hygrophilus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
