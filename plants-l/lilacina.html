Lilacina

Lilacina, commonly known as the sweet shrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, clustered flowers that bloom in the spring, typically in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy on the underside. Lilacina thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its botanical name, *Coleonema polyphylla*, reflects its diverse leaf structure and ornamental value.
