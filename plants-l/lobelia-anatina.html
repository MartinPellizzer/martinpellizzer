Lobelia anatina

Lobelia anatina, commonly known as the water lobelia, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant thrives in moist to aquatic environments, often found in marshes, pond edges, and along streams. Its leaves are typically alternate, oval-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Lobelia anatina is known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
