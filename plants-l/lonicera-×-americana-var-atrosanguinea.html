Lonicera × americana var. atrosanguinea

Lonicera × americana var. atrosanguinea, commonly known as the black-tipped honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features opposite, simple, ovate leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it adaptable to various garden conditions.

Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant foliage and showy flowers, though it can be invasive in some regions.
