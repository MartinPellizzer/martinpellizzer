Leptobalanus persaudii

Leptobalanus persaudii, commonly known as the Persian fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Pinaceae family and is characterized by its slender, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves. The tree produces small, pale yellow cones that mature to a reddish-brown color. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in high-altitude forests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
