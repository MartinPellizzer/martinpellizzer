Lobostemon capitatus

Lobostemon capitatus, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to South Africa.

It features showy, tubular flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, arranged in dense, spike-like clusters at the top of the plant. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are opposite along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its unique floral structure attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
