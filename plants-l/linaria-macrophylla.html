Linaria macrophylla

Linaria macrophylla, commonly known as the Portuguese heath, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bright orange or yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is often used in gardens for its ornamental value. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Linaria macrophylla is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
