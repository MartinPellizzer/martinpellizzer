Lagerstroemia cochinchinensis var. ovalifolia

Lagerstroemia cochinchinensis var. ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaf crepe myrtle, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It features smooth, greyish bark and lanceolate to oval-shaped leaves that are dark green above and lighter beneath. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, pink or white flowers in summer, which are followed by distinctive, papery fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its adaptability and showy blooms make it a valued addition to gardens and landscapes.
