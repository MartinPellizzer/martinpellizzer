Lonicera arborea

Lonicera arborea, commonly known as the hazel honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring. Its berries are small, red, and edible, providing food for birds and wildlife.

Lonicera arborea is often found in moist woodlands and stream banks, where it thrives in partial shade.
