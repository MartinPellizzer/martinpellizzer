Lavandula buchii var. tolpidifolia

Lavandula buchii var. tolpidifolia, commonly known as the sweet lavender or fragrant lavender, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, dark green leaves that are densely packed and emit a mild, sweet fragrance. The plant produces spikes of pale purple to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Due to its aromatic properties, it is often used in aromatherapy and as a decorative plant.
