Lithospermum caeruleum

Lithospermum caeruleum, commonly known as blue rock pepper, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, reddish-brown berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Lithospermum caeruleum thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for rocky or arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both ecological and medicinal purposes.
