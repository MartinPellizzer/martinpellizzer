Luzula borealis

Luzula borealis, commonly known as the northern woodrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, commonly known as the grass family, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are typically narrow, grass-like, and arranged in a rosette formation.

Luzula borealis thrives in cold, moist environments and is often found in tundra and boreal forest ecosystems.
