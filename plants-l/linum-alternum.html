Linum alternum

Linum alternum, commonly known as alternate flax, is a species of flowering plant in the family Linaceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically growing in moist, temperate regions. The plant features alternate leaves that are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture. Its flowers are usually blue or purple, with five petals, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Linum alternum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in textile production.
