Lycopodiella duseniana

Lycopodiella duseniana, commonly known as the Dusen's clubmoss, is a small, terrestrial fern-like plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its diminutive, spore-bearing cones and its ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments. This plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas, often in the understory where light is limited. Its leaves are narrow and linear, arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Lycopodiella duseniana plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
