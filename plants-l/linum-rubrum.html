Linum rubrum

Linum rubrum, commonly known as wild flax, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, branching stems that often grow up to 60 cm in height. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Linum rubrum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in fiber production.
