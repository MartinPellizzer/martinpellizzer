Lobelia ocimoides

Lobelia ocimoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf lobelia, is a herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant features slender stems and small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Lobelia ocimoides is valued in native gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
