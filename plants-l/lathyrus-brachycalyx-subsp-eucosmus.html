Lathyrus brachycalyx subsp. eucosmus

Lathyrus brachycalyx subsp.

eucosmus, commonly known as the short-calyx vetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of North America. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands.

The plant features slender, climbing stems with trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are small, pea-like, and usually pink or purple in color, arranged in clusters.

It produces small, hairy pods that contain several elliptical seeds.
