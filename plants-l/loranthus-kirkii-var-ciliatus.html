Loranthus kirkii var. ciliatus

Loranthus kirkii var. ciliatus, commonly known as the African mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of host trees, primarily in the genus Acacia.

It lacks true roots and absorbs water and nutrients through its haustoria, which penetrate the host's vascular system. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers and red, fleshy berries that contain the seeds. It is native to southern Africa and thrives in warm, humid climates.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the ecological interactions in its native habitat.
