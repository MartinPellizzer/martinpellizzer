Lodhra marginalis

Lodhra marginalis, commonly known as the marginal loofah, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Malvaceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and alternate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces showy, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its roots and leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a warm climate for optimal growth.
