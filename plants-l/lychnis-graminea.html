Lychnis graminea

Lychnis graminea, commonly known as the grass pink, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the carnation family (Caryophyllaceae) and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Lychnis graminea is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in partial to full sunlight.
