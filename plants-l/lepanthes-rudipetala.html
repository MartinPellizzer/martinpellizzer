Lepanthes rudipetala

Lepanthes rudipetala, commonly known as the pale-petaled orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically feature white or pale yellow petals and sepals. The plant grows as an epiphyte, often found on trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Lepanthes rudipetala is valued for its ornamental appeal and contributes to the biodiversity of its native ecosystems.
