Livistona melanocarpa

Livistona melanocarpa, commonly known as the black palm, is a species of palm native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers followed by edible, black, spherical fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rainforest areas.

Livistona melanocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
