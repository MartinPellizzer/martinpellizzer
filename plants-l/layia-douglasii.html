Layia douglasii

Layia douglasii, commonly known as Douglas's golden bush, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in coastal regions. It has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered blooms.

Layia douglasii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
