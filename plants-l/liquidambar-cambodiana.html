Liquidambar cambodiana

Liquidambar cambodiana, commonly known as Cambodian sweetgum, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly Cambodia and southern Vietnam.

It belongs to the genus Liquidambar in the family Altingiaceae, characterized by its aromatic sap and distinctive foliage. The tree produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by spiky, spherical fruits that contain several seeds. Its leaves are alternate, palmately lobed, and turn vibrant yellow in the fall, adding to its ornamental value.

The bark is smooth and light gray, often exuding a sticky resin when damaged.
