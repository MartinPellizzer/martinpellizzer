Laurus sylvestris

Laurus sylvestris, commonly known as sweet bay or laurel, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its aromatic, dark green, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible but often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are widely used as a spice and in traditional medicine due to their essential oils and antibacterial properties.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental and aromatic plant.
