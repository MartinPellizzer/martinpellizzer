Leontopodium × intermedium

Leontopodium × intermedium, commonly known as the edelweiss, is a flowering plant native to the Alps.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly white bracts that surround the small yellow flowers. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions. Its unique appearance makes it a symbol of resilience and alpine beauty.

It is also valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
