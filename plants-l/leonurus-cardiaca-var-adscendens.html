Leonurus cardiaca var. adscendens

Leonurus cardiaca var. adscendens, commonly known as the ascending heartwort, is a variant of the heartwort plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic, hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, two-lipped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

This variant is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported tonic and sedative properties.
