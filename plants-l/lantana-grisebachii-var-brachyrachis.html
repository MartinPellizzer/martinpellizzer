Lantana grisebachii var. brachyrachis

Lantana grisebachii var. brachyrachis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lantana, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to yellow, often blooming in late summer and early fall. The plant has opposite, narrow, and lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy beneath. It is adapted to well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it a common sight in coastal areas.

Its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators make it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
