Lotus maroccanus var. eriosolen

Lotus maroccanus var. eriosolen, commonly known as the Moroccan lotus, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive pink or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which help it retain moisture in its harsh environment. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
