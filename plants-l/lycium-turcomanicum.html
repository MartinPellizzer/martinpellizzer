Lycium turcomanicum

Lycium turcomanicum, commonly known as the Turkestan boxthorn, is a shrub native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, orange-red berries that are edible. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, thriving in sandy or rocky soils. Its leaves are usually narrow and evergreen, providing some shade in its natural habitat.

Lycium turcomanicum is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
