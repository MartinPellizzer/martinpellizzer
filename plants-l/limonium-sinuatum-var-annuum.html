Limonium sinuatum var. annuum

Limonium sinuatum var. annuum, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal areas of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate salt and wind.

Limonium sinuatum var. annuum is also valued for its role in attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
