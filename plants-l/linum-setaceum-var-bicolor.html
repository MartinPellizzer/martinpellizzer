Linum setaceum var. bicolor

Linum setaceum var. bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored flax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, two-toned flowers that range from white to pink and purple, often with a distinct color contrast between the upper and lower petals. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
