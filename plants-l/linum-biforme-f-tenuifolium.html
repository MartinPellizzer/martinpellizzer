Linum biforme f. tenuifolium

Linum biforme f. tenuifolium, commonly known as the slender-leaved flax, is a flowering plant belonging to the Linaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial characterized by its delicate, linear leaves and showy blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun. Its flowers are borne on slender stems and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is noted for its smaller, narrower leaves compared to the typical form of Linum biforme.
