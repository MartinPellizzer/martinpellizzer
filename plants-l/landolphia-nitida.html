Landolphia nitida

Landolphia nitida, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a climbing plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces distinctive, spiny fruits that contain toxic seeds. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often found in forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
