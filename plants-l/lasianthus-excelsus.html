Lasianthus excelsus

Lasianthus excelsus, commonly known as the white-flowered lasianthus, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth surface, while its inflorescences are clustered in terminal panicles.

Lasianthus excelsus is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
