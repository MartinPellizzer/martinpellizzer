Lonicera caerulea var. altaica

Lonicera caerulea var. altaica, commonly known as the Sakhalin honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the Russian Far East and northeastern China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces edible berries that are typically yellow or orange in color, with a sweet and tangy flavor. These berries are rich in antioxidants and are often used in jams, juices, and traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
