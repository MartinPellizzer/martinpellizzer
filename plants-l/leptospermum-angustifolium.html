Leptospermum angustifolium

Leptospermum angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved tea tree, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is evergreen and typically grows in coastal or sandy soils. It is part of the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus.

The leaves are often used in traditional herbal remedies due to their antimicrobial properties.
