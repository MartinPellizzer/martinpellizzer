Lophopyrum corsicum

Lophopyrum corsicum, commonly known as Corsican wild rye, is a perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Lophopyrum corsicum is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
