Lilaeopsis tenuis

Lilaeopsis tenuis, commonly known as the slender leek grass, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks. It has a fibrous root system and is often found in disturbed or transitional vegetation.

Lilaeopsis tenuis plays a minor role in local ecosystems but is of interest to botanists due to its ecological niche and morphological features.
