Leucaena bolivarensis

Leucaena bolivarensis, commonly known as Bolivian leucaena, is a shrub or small tree native to South America, particularly Bolivia and Peru.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a valuable species for soil stabilization and reforestation.

It also has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.
