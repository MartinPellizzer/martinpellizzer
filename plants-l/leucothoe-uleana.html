Leucothoe uleana

Leucothoe uleana, commonly known as the Florida leucothoe, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant features attractive, glossy evergreen leaves that remain on the plant throughout the winter. Its showy, fragrant white flowers appear in spring, followed by red, fleshy berries in the fall.

It is valued in landscaping for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
