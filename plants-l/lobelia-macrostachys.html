Lobelia macrostachys

Lobelia macrostachys, commonly known as the showy lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Lobelia macrostachys is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is also valued for its ecological benefits.
