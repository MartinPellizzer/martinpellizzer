Leontodon libanoticus

Leontodon libanoticus, commonly known as the Lebanon bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention.

Leontodon libanoticus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
