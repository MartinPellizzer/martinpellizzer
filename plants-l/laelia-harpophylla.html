Laelia harpophylla

Laelia harpophylla, commonly known as the Harpophylla orchid, is a species native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in high-altitude regions with cool, humid climates. The plant features a pseudobulb that supports its growth and stores water, which is essential for its survival in its native habitat. Its flowers are usually pale green to white, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insects.

Laelia harpophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
