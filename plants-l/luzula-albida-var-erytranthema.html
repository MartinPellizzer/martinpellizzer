Luzula albida var. erytranthema

Luzula albida var. erytranthema, commonly known as the pink tufted sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage with narrow, cylindrical leaves that are typically pale green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikes, which are usually pink or reddish in hue, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Luzula albida var. erytranthema is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
