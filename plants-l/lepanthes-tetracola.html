Lepanthes tetracola

Lepanthes tetracola, commonly known as the four-flowered slipper orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. The plant grows as an epiphyte, often found in the canopy of tropical rainforests. Its flowers are known for their intricate structure, featuring a pouch-like lip that aids in pollination.

Lepanthes tetracola is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
