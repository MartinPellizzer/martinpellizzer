Lindelofia angustifolia

Lindelofia angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lindelofia, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence. Its small, white flowers bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months.

Lindelofia angustifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
