Lilium japonicum var. insulare

Lilium japonicum var. insulare, commonly known as the Japanese mountain lily, is a rare and ornamental plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, such as mountainous regions, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces a single bloom per stem.

This variety is valued for its unique appearance and is often cultivated in specialized gardens.
