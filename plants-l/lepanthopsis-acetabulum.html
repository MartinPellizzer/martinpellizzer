Lepanthopsis acetabulum

Lepanthopsis acetabulum, commonly known as the Acetabulum orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded forest understories, where it thrives in humid environments. The plant features a slender, wiry stem with a single, lance-shaped leaf. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and usually white or pale green, with a distinctive lip that is often darker in color.

Lepanthopsis acetabulum is known for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in tropical gardens.
