Lavatera acerifolia

Lavatera acerifolia, commonly known as the maple leaf tree, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves that resemble maple leaves, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, fluffy yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique foliage and delicate blooms make it a striking addition to gardens and landscapes.
