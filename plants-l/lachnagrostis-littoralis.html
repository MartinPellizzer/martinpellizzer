Lachnagrostis littoralis

Lachnagrostis littoralis, commonly known as saltgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to coastal regions.

It thrives in saline and brackish environments, often found along shorelines and in salt marshes. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-like inflorescences. It is adapted to withstand harsh coastal conditions, including wind and salt spray.

Its ecological importance lies in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various coastal wildlife.
