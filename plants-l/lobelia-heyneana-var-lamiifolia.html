Lobelia heyneana var. lamiifolia

Lobelia heyneana var. lamiifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf lobelia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and spikes of small, tubular blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or stream sides. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture.

The flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
