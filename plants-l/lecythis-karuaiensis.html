Lecythis karuaiensis

Lecythis karuaiensis, commonly known as the golden tucan tree, is a tropical tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and distinctive, spiny fruits that contain edible seeds. The tree produces a yellowish latex that has been used by indigenous communities for various purposes. Its flowers are typically greenish-yellow and are pollinated by insects.

The plant is valued for its ecological role and potential economic importance in sustainable forestry.
