Liparis clemensiae

Liparis clemensiae, commonly known as the Clemens's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom during the rainy season. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

Liparis clemensiae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral structure.
