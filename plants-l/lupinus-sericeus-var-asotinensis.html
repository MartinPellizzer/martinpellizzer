Lupinus sericeus var. asotinensis

Lupinus sericeus var. asotinensis, commonly known as the Asotin lupine, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and hairy stems that are often silver or gray in appearance. The plant produces clusters of blue to violet pea-like flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets.

This variety is adapted to moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian areas and along streams.
