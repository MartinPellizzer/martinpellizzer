Linum strictum subsp. cymosum

Linum strictum subsp.

cymosum, commonly known as the clustered flax, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, erect stems that are often branched and covered in fine, silky hairs.

The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that are typically arranged in clusters, giving it its common name. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy surface, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
