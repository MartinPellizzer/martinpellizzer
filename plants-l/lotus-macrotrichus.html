Lotus macrotrichus

Lotus macrotrichus, commonly known as the hairy lotus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, hairy leaves and showy, pink or red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes and along riverbanks. Its roots are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The hairy lotus is also valued for its ornamental appeal in aquatic gardens.
