Leucaena blancii

Leucaena blancii, commonly known as white leucaena, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it useful for erosion control and livestock forage. Its flowers are typically pale yellow to white, and it produces nitrogen-fixing nodules on its roots, enhancing soil fertility.

Leucaena blancii is also valued for its hardiness and adaptability to various climates.
