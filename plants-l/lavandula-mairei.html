Lavandula mairei

Lavandula mairei, commonly known as Maire's lavender, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, narrow leaves and spikes of purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Lavandula mairei is also valued for its essential oils, which are used in aromatherapy and perfumery.
