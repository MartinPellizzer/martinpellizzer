Lepanthes longiacuminata

Lepanthes longiacuminata, commonly known as the long-armed slipper orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces a single, showy flower per stem, which is often adorned with intricate patterns and a spurred lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing as an epiphyte on trees or rocks.

The botanical features of Lepanthes longiacuminata make it a striking example of orchid diversity in tropical ecosystems.
