Lycaste longipetala

Lycaste longipetala, commonly known as the long-petaled lycaste, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its elegant, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pale pink. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in tropical climates. Its leaves are typically long and narrow, arranged in a rosette pattern around the pseudobulbs.

This orchid is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or warm, humid environments.
