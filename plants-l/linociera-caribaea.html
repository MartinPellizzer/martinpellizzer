Linociera caribaea

Linociera caribaea, commonly known as the Caribbean gumbo limbo, is a tropical tree native to the Caribbean region.

It is characterized by its smooth, light-colored bark that peels in thin layers, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a slightly hairy texture. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds.

Linociera caribaea is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and sandy soils.
