Lepanthes fusiformis

Lepanthes fusiformis, commonly known as the fusiform orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stem and relatively small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals. Lepanthes fusiformis thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing in the understory of tropical forests.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
