Lachenalia

Lachenalia, commonly known as the "rainbow bulb," is a genus of flowering plants native to southern Africa.

These plants are characterized by their striking, often colorful inflorescences that resemble a crown of flowers. Lachenalia species typically grow from bulbs and are adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. They produce showy, tubular flowers in a variety of hues, including red, orange, yellow, and white.

The plants are popular in horticulture for their ornamental value and unique appearance.
