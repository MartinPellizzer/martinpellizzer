Lophochloa phleoides

Lophochloa phleoides, commonly known as the slender spikegrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes and seasonally flooded areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense mats that stabilize soil and provide habitat for various organisms.

Lophochloa phleoides plays a role in wetland ecosystems by contributing to soil structure and supporting biodiversity.
