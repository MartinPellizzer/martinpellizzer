Lotus corniculatus var. norvegicus

Lotus corniculatus var. norvegicus, commonly known as the Norwegian bird's-foot trefoil, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, resembling a bird's foot in shape. The plant has trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet being oval and slightly hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along roadsides.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in ecological restoration projects.
