Lasia spinosa

Lasia spinosa, commonly known as the spiny lasia, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its succulent, spiny stems and small, clustered leaves that help reduce water loss. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers during the rainy season, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Lasia spinosa is well adapted to survive in harsh environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
