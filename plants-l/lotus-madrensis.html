Lotus madrensis

Lotus madrensis, commonly known as the Mexican lotus, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland areas and has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. Its roots are used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in its native habitat.
