Leucanthemum ceratophylloides var. barrelieri

Leucanthemum ceratophylloides var. barrelieri, commonly known as the Barrelier's coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, daisy-like flowers with white ray florets surrounding a central yellow disc, which attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to both horticultural collections and ecological landscapes.
