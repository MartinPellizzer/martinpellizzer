Litsea calophylla

Litsea calophylla, commonly known as the Chinese tallow tree, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and opposite, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and pale underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange to red berries that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other regions, sometimes becoming invasive.

The tree is also valued for its wood, which is lightweight and used in various crafts and construction.
