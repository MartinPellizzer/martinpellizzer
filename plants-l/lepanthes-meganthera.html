Lepanthes meganthera

Lepanthes meganthera, commonly known as the Meganthera orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically feature vibrant colors such as red, orange, and yellow. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees or other structures without being parasitic. Its flowers are often fragrant and attract various pollinators, including bees and hummingbirds.

The genus Lepanthes is known for its diversity, with many species exhibiting unique floral structures and adaptations to their specific habitats.
