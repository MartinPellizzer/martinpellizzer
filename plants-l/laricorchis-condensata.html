Laricorchis condensata

Laricorchis condensata, commonly known as the pine orchid, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its compact, grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, often resembling pine cones, hence its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in coniferous forests.

Laricorchis condensata is a perennial herb that plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its native habitat.
