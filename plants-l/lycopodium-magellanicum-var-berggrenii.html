Lycopodium magellanicum var. berggrenii

Lycopodium magellanicum var. berggrenii, commonly known as the Magellan clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae and is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and erect, branching stems. This variety is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. The plant produces spores in specialized structures called sporangia, which are clustered in strobila.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
