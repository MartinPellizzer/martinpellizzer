Luteola tinctoria

Luteola tinctoria, commonly known as the yellowweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant is known for its yellow-orange flowers, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. Luteola tinctoria has been historically used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Its leaves and flowers are often harvested for use in herbal remedies and dyeing processes.
