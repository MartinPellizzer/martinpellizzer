Lasianthus vulcanicus

Lasianthus vulcanicus, commonly known as the volcanic flameflower, is a unique flowering plant native to the volcanic regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red bracts that resemble flames, which are actually modified leaves rather than true flowers. The plant thrives in nutrient-rich, volcanic soil and is often found in areas with high humidity and moderate temperatures. Its growth is closely associated with volcanic activity, as it can colonize areas affected by recent eruptions.

Due to its specialized habitat, Lasianthus vulcanicus is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
