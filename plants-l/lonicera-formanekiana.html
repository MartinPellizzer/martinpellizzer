Lonicera formanekiana

Lonicera formanekiana, commonly known as the Formanek's honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for some bird species but not for humans. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Lonicera formanekiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
