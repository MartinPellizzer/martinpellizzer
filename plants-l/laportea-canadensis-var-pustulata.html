Laportea canadensis var. pustulata

Laportea canadensis var. pustulata, commonly known as Canadian roughleaf or roughleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It features a hairy, upright stem with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white pubescence. The plant produces showy, purple to pink composite flowers in late summer, arranged in flat-topped clusters. It thrives in moist to wet soils in open woodlands and wetlands.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
