Ledebouria undulata

Ledebouria undulata, commonly known as the undulating bulbous plant, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is a bulbous geophyte characterized by its fleshy, underground storage structures called bulbs. The plant produces clusters of narrow, grass-like leaves and bears small, white to pale green flowers during the rainy season. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience.
