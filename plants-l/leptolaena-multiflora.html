Leptolaena multiflora

Leptolaena multiflora, commonly known as the many-flowered leptolaena, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Leptolaena multiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
