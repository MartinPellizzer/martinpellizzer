Leptospermum grandifolium var. compactum

Leptospermum grandifolium var. compactum, commonly known as the coastal tea tree, is a compact shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features dense, rounded growth with small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, adding a subtle ornamental value. It is adapted to coastal conditions, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils.

This variety is prized for its hardiness and ability to thrive in coastal gardens.
