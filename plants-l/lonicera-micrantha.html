Lonicera micrantha

Lonicera micrantha, commonly known as the small-flowered honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, fragrant, tubular flowers that are usually yellow or white, blooming in early summer. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that ripens in late summer, attracting birds and other wildlife.

Lonicera micrantha is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
