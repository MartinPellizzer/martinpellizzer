Lauro-cerasus officinalis f. schipkaensis

Lauro-cerasus officinalis f. schipkaensis, commonly known as the Schipka cherry laurel, is a rare and cultivated form of the common cherry laurel.

It is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and partial shade.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in botanical gardens.
