Lippia nodiflora var. minor

Lippia nodiflora var. minor, commonly known as Mexican blueweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It features aromatic, narrow leaves and produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is often found in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.

Its essential oils contain compounds like thymol, which contribute to its medicinal and aromatic properties.
