Loranthus rubrostamineus

Loranthus rubrostamineus, commonly known as the red-stemmed mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that attaches itself to the branches of various host trees.

It belongs to the family Loranthaceae and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and red-colored stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are often visited by insects for pollination. Its berries, which are typically red, contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This species is found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it plays a role in the ecological interactions of its host trees.
