Lolium × elongatum f. pseudoglyceria

Lolium × elongatum f. pseudoglyceria, commonly known as the tall ryegrass, is a perennial grass species characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, blade-like leaves.

It is native to Europe and has been widely introduced to other temperate regions for agricultural and ornamental purposes. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with inflorescences composed of spikelets that bear small, two-lobed florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along roadsides.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it is both a valuable forage crop and a potential weed in agricultural settings.
