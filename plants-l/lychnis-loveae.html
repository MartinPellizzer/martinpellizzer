Lychnis loveae

Lychnis loveae, commonly known as the Love's Lychnis, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the northeastern United States.

It features showy, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has basal leaves that are kidney-shaped with toothed edges, and its stems are hairy and upright. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Lychnis loveae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
