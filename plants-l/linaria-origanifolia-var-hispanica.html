Linaria origanifolia var. hispanica

Linaria origanifolia var. hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish toadflax, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces vibrant yellow-orange flowers in raceme clusters, typically blooming from spring to summer. Its flowers have a distinctive spurred shape, typical of the Linaria genus, and are adapted for pollination by insects.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.
