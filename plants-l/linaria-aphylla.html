Linaria aphylla

Linaria aphylla, commonly known as the leafless toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its absence of leaves, with photosynthesis occurring through its stems. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Linaria aphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
