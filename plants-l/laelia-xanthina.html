Laelia xanthina

Laelia xanthina, commonly known as the yellow laelia, is a hybrid orchid species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which often feature intricate patterns and a sweet fragrance. The plant typically grows on rock faces or in exposed areas, adapting to harsh environmental conditions. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in dry periods.

Laelia xanthina is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and resilience in cultivation.
