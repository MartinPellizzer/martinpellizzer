Lonchocarpus galeottianus

Lonchocarpus galeottianus, commonly known as the red mangrove or *Pongamia glabra*, is a leguminous shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its woody stems, compound leaves, and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in terminal clusters. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by water or animals. It thrives in saline and brackish environments, making it an important species in coastal ecosystems.

Additionally, it has been studied for its potential medicinal and industrial applications due to its chemical composition.
