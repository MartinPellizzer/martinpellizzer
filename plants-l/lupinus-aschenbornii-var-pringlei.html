Lupinus aschenbornii var. pringlei

Lupinus aschenbornii var. pringlei, commonly known as the Pringle's lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and clusters of pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or open habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration due to its nitrogen-fixing abilities.
