Lycium mediterraneum var. ramulosum

Lycium mediterraneum var. ramulosum, commonly known as the Mediterranean boxthorn, is a shrub native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features small, dark green leaves that are typically arranged in a dense, compact manner. The plant produces yellow flowers in the spring, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible but often used more for ornamental purposes. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for Mediterranean climates.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and attractive addition to gardens and landscapes.
