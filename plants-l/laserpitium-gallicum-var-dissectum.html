Laserpitium gallicum var. dissectum

Laserpitium gallicum var. dissectum, commonly known as the French bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple. The leaves are lance-shaped and often deeply lobed, giving the plant a delicate, feathery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
