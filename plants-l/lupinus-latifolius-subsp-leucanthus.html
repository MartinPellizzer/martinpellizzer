Lupinus latifolius subsp. leucanthus

Lupinus latifolius subsp.

leucanthus, commonly known as the white-flowered lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States. It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and showy white or pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has a fibrous root system and produces pods that contain several seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in nitrogen fixation in soil.
