Liparis amphibius

Liparis amphibius, commonly known as the water lily, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It features broad, floating leaves that provide shade and habitat for aquatic life. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that bloom above the water surface. Its roots are anchored in the mud at the bottom of ponds or lakes.

Liparis amphibius plays an important role in maintaining water quality and supporting biodiversity in aquatic ecosystems.
