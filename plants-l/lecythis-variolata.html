Lecythis variolata

Lecythis variolata, commonly known as the spotted lecythium, is a tropical tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. The tree produces yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by large, woody fruits with a thick, spiky rind. The fruit contains a single, large, edible seed enclosed in a fleshy, sweet pulp.

This species is valued for its ornamental potential and has also been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
