Ligusticopsis glaucescens

Ligusticopsis glaucescens, commonly known as the glaucous ligustic, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy, bluish-gray coating. The plant produces tiny, pale yellow flowers arranged in dense, terminal clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a hardy species adapted to arid environments.

Due to its limited distribution and unique appearance, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
