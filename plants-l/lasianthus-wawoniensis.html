Lasianthus wawoniensis

Lasianthus wawoniensis, commonly known as the Wawon Island flameflower, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking red-orange bracts that resemble flowers. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture with a slightly glossy appearance.

Lasianthus wawoniensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant display.
