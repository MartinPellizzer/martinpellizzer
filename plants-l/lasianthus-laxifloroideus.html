Lasianthus laxifloroideus

Lasianthus laxifloroideus, commonly known as the slender-flowered lasianthus, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of pale blue to violet flowers, which are typically arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in tropical gardens.
