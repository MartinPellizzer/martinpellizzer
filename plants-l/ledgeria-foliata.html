Ledgeria foliata

Ledgeria foliata, commonly known as the leafy ledgeria, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The species is valued for both its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
