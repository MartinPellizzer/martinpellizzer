Loranthella volkensii

Loranthella volkensii, commonly known as the African mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily in the African region.

It lacks true roots and absorbs water and nutrients through its haustoria, which penetrate the host's vascular system. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or green flowers, followed by small, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds and other animals. It plays a role in the ecological balance by influencing host tree growth and providing resources for wildlife.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable in some areas.
