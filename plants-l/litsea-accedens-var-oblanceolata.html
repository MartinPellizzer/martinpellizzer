Litsea accedens var. oblanceolata

Litsea accedens var. oblanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed litsea, is a variant of the litsea genus native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub, typically reaching heights of up to 10 meters. The leaves are elliptical to oblanceolate in shape, with a glossy, dark green upper surface and a lighter, often hairy underside. It produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by spherical, yellowish fruits.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its aromatic properties.
