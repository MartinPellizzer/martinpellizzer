Lagenocarpus ciliatus f. humilis

Lagenocarpus ciliatus f. humilis, commonly known as the low-growing form of the silky bower vine, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of Asia.

It features slender, trailing stems that produce small, white or pale yellow flowers clustered in racemes. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered with fine, soft hairs, contributing to its delicate appearance. This variety is characterized by its compact growth habit, making it suitable for ground cover or hanging baskets.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal.
