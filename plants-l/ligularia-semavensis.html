Ligularia semavensis

Ligularia semavensis, commonly known as the Semavensis Buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of East Africa.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive yellowish-green coloration and a rough texture. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with a central greenish-brown disk, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
