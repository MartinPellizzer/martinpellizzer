Lanugothamnus albolanosus

Lanugothamnus albolanosus, commonly known as the white-flowered thamnus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing, shrub-like structure with silvery-gray foliage. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and traditional herbal uses.
