Lamium wettsteinii

Lamium wettsteinii, commonly known as Wettstein's dead-nettle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a slightly hairy surface, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Lamium wettsteinii is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats at high elevations.
