Leptorkis resupinata

Leptorkis resupinata, commonly known as the creeping lily, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Liliaceae.

It is characterized by its low-growing, rhizomatous habit and produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, and it thrives in moist, shady environments. It is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks, where it contributes to the understory vegetation.
