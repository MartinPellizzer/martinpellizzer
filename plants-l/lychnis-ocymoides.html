Lychnis ocymoides

Lychnis ocymoides, commonly known as the scarlet pimpernel, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a low-growing herbaceous plant with slender stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that open in the morning and close by midday, a behavior known as nyctinasty. Its flowers are typically surrounded by pale green bracts, and the plant is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Lychnis ocymoides is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
