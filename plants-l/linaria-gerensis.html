Linaria gerensis

Linaria gerensis, commonly known as the mountain sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, adapted to withstand harsh alpine conditions.

Linaria gerensis thrives in rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and scree slopes.
