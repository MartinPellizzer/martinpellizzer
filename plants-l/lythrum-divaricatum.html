Lythrum divaricatum

Lythrum divaricatum, commonly known as redroot lythrum, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the Lythraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist soils along streambanks and wetlands, thriving in partial to full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slight hairy texture, while the stems are reddish-brown and often branched.

Lythrum divaricatum is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
