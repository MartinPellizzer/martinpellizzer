Lycium pallidum var. pallidum

Lycium pallidum var. pallidum, commonly known as the pale wolfberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and red, fleshy berries. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in temperate to subtropical climates. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a slightly hairy surface, and it produces clusters of berries that are often used in traditional Chinese medicine.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
