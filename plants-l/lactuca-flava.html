Lactuca flava

Lactuca flava, commonly known as yellow lettuce, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, dark green leaves with a yellowish tinge and a mild, slightly bitter taste. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Lactuca flava is also known for its medicinal properties, traditionally used for its calming and sedative effects.
