Lycianthes arrazolensis

Lycianthes arrazolensis, commonly known as the Mexican nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and tobacco. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with opposite, ovate leaves that are hairy and have a distinctive scent. Its flowers are yellowish-white with five petals and are followed by small, orange-red berries.

The fruit is edible when ripe but contains toxic compounds in its raw form.
