Lathyrus eucosmus

Lathyrus eucosmus, commonly known as the purple vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial legume characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple, depending on the variety. Its seeds are encased in pods and are often used as forage for livestock.

Lathyrus eucosmus is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.
