Lycopodium scariosum var. holtonii

Lycopodium scariosum var. holtonii, commonly known as the Texas clubmoss, is a perennial evergreen plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Lycopodiaceae family and is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and spore-bearing strobila. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. It has a low-growing habit, forming dense mats that help prevent soil erosion.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important component of its native habitat.
