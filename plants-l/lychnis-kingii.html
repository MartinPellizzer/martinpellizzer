Lychnis kingii

Lychnis kingii, commonly known as King's catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. The plant features upright stems with opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its showy, pinkish-red flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Lychnis kingii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local ecosystems.
