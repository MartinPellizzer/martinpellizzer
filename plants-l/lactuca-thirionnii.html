Lactuca thirionnii

Lactuca thirionnii, commonly known as Thirion's lettuce, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family, Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, spoon-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a mild, slightly bitter taste. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, which are followed by fluffy, white seeds. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible leaves, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
