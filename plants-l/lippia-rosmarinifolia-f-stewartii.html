Lippia rosmarinifolia f. stewartii

Lippia rosmarinifolia f. stewartii, commonly known as the Stewart's lemon verbena, is a compact shrub native to South America.

It features aromatic, gray-green leaves with a distinctive lemony scent, reminiscent of its close relative, lemon verbena. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of essential oils.

This variety is particularly valued for its hardiness and adaptability to various growing conditions.
