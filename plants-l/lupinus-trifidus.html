Lupinus trifidus

Lupinus trifidus, commonly known as the three-lobed lupin, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and spikes of blue or purple flowers. The plant is often found in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny environments. Lupinus trifidus is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which makes it useful in soil enrichment.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in gardens and landscapes.
