Lepanthes isosceles

Lepanthes isosceles, commonly known as the narrow-leaf orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pale pink. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing as an epiphyte on trees or rocks. Its flowers are highly fragrant and attract various pollinators, including bees and moths.

Lepanthes isosceles is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
