Lecythis ovata var. longifolia

Lecythis ovata var. longifolia, commonly known as the longpod sacha inchi, is a tropical tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and distinctive long, woody pods that contain edible seeds. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that are often clustered in terminal panicles. Its bark and seeds have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
