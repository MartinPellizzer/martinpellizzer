Lobelia verbascifolia

Lobelia verbascifolia, commonly known as woolly lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, silver-green foliage and clusters of small, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and thrives in full sun. It is part of the Campanulaceae family and is often found in alpine and arid regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
