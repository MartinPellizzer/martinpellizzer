Lamium macrodon

Lamium macrodon, commonly known as the greater dead-nettle, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides.

Its flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
