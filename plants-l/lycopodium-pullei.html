Lycopodium pullei

Lycopodium pullei, commonly known as the clubmoss, is a small, evergreen plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its spore-bearing cones and its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its erect, branched stems and tiny, scale-like leaves. Lycopodium pullei is often found in forest understories and is adapted to nutrient-poor soils.

Its reproductive structures are essential for its propagation and ecological role in woodland ecosystems.
