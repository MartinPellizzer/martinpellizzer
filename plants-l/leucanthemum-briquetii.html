Leucanthemum briquetii

Leucanthemum briquetii, commonly known as the Briquet's oxeye, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats and thrives in temperate climates. Its flowers are white with a yellow center, resembling a dandelion, and bloom from late spring to early summer.

Leucanthemum briquetii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
