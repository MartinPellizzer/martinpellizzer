Lycopsis flavescens

Lycopsis flavescens, commonly known as the yellow lycopsis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Lycopsidaceae and is characterized by its small, delicate leaves and inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky habitats. Its reproductive structures are adapted for wind pollination, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.
