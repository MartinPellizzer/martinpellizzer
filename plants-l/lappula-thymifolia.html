Lappula thymifolia

Lappula thymifolia, commonly known as thymeleaf flax, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Chenopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and its leaves that resemble those of thyme, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid regions, often in rocky or sandy soils. It has a low-growing habit with slender stems and is often found in Mediterranean climates.

Lappula thymifolia is known for its resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.
