Lupinus breweri var. clokeyanus

Lupinus breweri var. clokeyanus, commonly known as Clokey's lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod-bearing fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers are usually blue or purple, with prominent yellow markings, and it produces erect, spiny stems.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance in supporting local pollinators and soil health.
