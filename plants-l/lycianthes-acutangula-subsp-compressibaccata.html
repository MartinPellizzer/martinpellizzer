Lycianthes acutangula subsp. compressibaccata

Lycianthes acutangula subsp.

compressibaccata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Central America. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers.

The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape, with a hairy surface. Its flowers are typically yellow with a distinctive purple calyx, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for certain wildlife.

This subspecies is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and abandoned fields.
