Leiothrix argyroderma var. brevipes

Leiothrix argyroderma var. brevipes, commonly known as the silver-leaved shrub, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive silvery-white undersides on its leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
