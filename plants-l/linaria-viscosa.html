Linaria viscosa

Linaria viscosa, commonly known as sticky toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a sticky texture. The plant produces bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Linaria viscosa is a perennial herb that can spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
