Lithops hallii var. ochracea

Lithops hallii var. ochracea, commonly known as the ochraceous bilberry lithops, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, fleshy leaves that resemble small stones, which help it blend into its arid environment. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
