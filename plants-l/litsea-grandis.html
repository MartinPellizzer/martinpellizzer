Litsea grandis

Litsea grandis, commonly known as the Chinese tallow tree, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its opposite, simple, and often aromatic leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange-red berries. Its bark is smooth and light gray, and it can grow up to 20 meters tall in its native habitat.

The plant is also known for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other parts of the world.
