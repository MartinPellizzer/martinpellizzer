Luzula plumosa var. albida

Luzula plumosa var. albida, commonly known as the silver feather grass, is a variant of the common wood sedge.

It is characterized by its silvery-white, feathery foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. It has narrow, grass-like leaves that are densely packed along its stem.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and natural landscapes.
