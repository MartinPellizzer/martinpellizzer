Lepilaena marina

Lepilaena marina, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that thrives in sandy or gravelly soils, often found along the seashore. The plant features succulent, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of blue or purple flowers. It is adapted to salt spray and strong winds, making it a resilient species in harsh maritime environments.

Lepilaena marina is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes.
