Lonicera oreodoxa

Lonicera oreodoxa, commonly known as the Japanese honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a capsule that contains small, black seeds.

Lonicera oreodoxa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of nectar for pollinators.
