Lepanthes quadrata

Lepanthes quadrata, commonly known as the quadrata orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid with a delicate appearance, featuring a slender stem and a single, showy flower. The plant has a complex root system that helps it thrive in shaded, moist environments. Its flowers are typically white or pale green, with intricate patterns that attract specific pollinators.

Lepanthes quadrata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
