Lampranthus antemeridianus

Lampranthus antemeridianus, commonly known as the morning glory of the fynbos, is a member of the Aizoaceae family, native to South Africa.

It is a succulent plant with fleshy, often hairy leaves and produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically red or orange in color. The plant blooms in the early morning, hence its common name, and is well adapted to arid environments. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
