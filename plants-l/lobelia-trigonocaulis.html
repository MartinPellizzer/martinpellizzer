Lobelia trigonocaulis

Lobelia trigonocaulis, commonly known as the three-lobed lobelia, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves with three distinct lobes, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Lobelia trigonocaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
