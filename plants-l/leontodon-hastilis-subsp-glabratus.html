Leontodon hastilis subsp. glabratus

Leontodon hastilis subsp.

glabratus, commonly known as the glabrous hawk's-beak, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and typical sunflower-like appearance.

The plant features linear, grass-like leaves and produces yellow disk flowers surrounded by pale yellow to brown ray florets. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

The subspecies glabratus is distinguished by its smooth, hairless stems and leaves, setting it apart from other varieties within the species.
