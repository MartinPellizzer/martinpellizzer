Lantana hispida var. ternata

Lantana hispida var. ternata, commonly known as the hairy lantana, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a red or purple calyx. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that are typically opposite in arrangement. It is adapted to thrive in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and use in traditional medicine.
