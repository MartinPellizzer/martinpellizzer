Linaria origanifolia subsp. cadevallii

Linaria origanifolia subsp.

cadevallii, commonly known as the Cadevall's toadflax, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and bright yellow flowers.

The leaves are linear and often resemble those of oregano, giving the species its common name. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as slopes and cliffs.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
