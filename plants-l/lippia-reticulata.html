Lippia reticulata

Lippia reticulata, commonly known as Mexican mint or wild mint, is a perennial herb native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly minty leaves and small white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny environments and is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils. It has a compact growth habit with hairy stems and is well adapted to warm climates.

Lippia reticulata is valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.
