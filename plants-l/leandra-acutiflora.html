Leandra acutiflora

Leandra acutiflora, commonly known as the flame flower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that resemble flames, with long, tubular petals that are typically red or orange in color. The plant thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.

Leandra acutiflora is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.
