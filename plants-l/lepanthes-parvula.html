Lepanthes parvula

Lepanthes parvula, commonly known as the dwarf orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant has delicate, grass-like leaves and produces small, showy flowers in shades of white and pink. Lepanthes parvula is known for its short stature and is often found in moist, forested areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for collectors and enthusiasts of miniature orchids.
