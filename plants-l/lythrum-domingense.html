Lythrum domingense

Lythrum domingense, commonly known as the Dominican lythrum, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Lythraceae family, which includes other flowering plants such as the common willow herb. The plant features showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in spikes, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, often with a hairy texture.

Lythrum domingense is typically found in moist, well-drained soils in tropical and subtropical regions.
