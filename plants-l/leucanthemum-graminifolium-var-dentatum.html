Leucanthemum graminifolium var. dentatum

Leucanthemum graminifolium var. dentatum, commonly known as the toothed leucanthemum, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, toothed leaves and produces showy white or pale yellow flowers with dark centers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. It is a perennial species that blooms in late summer and early fall.

Its foliage and flowers make it an attractive addition to native plant gardens.
