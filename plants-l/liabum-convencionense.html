Liabum convencionense

Liabum convencionense, commonly known as the Convenção bean, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with stipules and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and potential agricultural uses, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and agroforestry systems.
