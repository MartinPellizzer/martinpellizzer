Lathyrus montanus lusus variegatus

Lathyrus montanus lusus variegatus, commonly known as the variegated mountain pea, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking variegated foliage, which features a mix of green, white, and sometimes yellow patterns on its leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from purple to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in horticultural settings.
