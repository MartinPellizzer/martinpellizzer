Lindsaea decrescens, commonly known as the creeping lindsaea, is a species of fern belonging to the family Bromeliaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and is native to tropical regions of South America. The plant features long, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. It produces small, clustered inflorescences with showy, violet-colored flowers.

Lindsaea decrescens is often found in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices.
