Lycianthes pilifera var. pilosiuscula

Lycianthes pilifera var. pilosiuscula, commonly known as the hairy piquant leaf.

This variant is a member of the Solanaceae family, characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers. It is native to Central and South America and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines.

Its botanical features include glandular hairs on the leaves and stems, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
