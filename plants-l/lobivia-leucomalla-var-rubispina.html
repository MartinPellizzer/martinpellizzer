Lobivia leucomalla var. rubispina

Lobivia leucomalla var. rubispina, commonly known as the red-spined lobivia, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in white woolly hairs. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are typically followed by the development of red spines along its stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
