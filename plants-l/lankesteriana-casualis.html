Lankesteriana casualis

Lankesteriana casualis, commonly known as the Colombian orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of Colombia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually a vibrant mix of purple and white, with intricate patterns that attract pollinators.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
