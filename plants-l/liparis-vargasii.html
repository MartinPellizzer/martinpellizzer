Liparis vargasii

Liparis vargasii, commonly known as the Vargas orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its roots are often mycorrhizal, forming symbiotic relationships with fungi to enhance nutrient uptake.

Liparis vargasii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
