Lycopodium biforme

Lycopodium biforme, commonly known as ground pine, is a species of clubmoss that belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky areas. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its needle-like leaves arranged in whorls around the stem. It produces spores in cone-like structures called strobila, which are released during the spring and summer months.

Lycopodium biforme is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and can thrive in containers or as part of a woodland garden.
