Lilium lancifolium var. fortunei

Lilium lancifolium var. fortunei, commonly known as the Chinese tiger lily, is a variant of the tiger lily species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers with prominent black spots, which resemble a tiger's coat. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with long, sword-shaped leaves that are lanceolate in shape. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

This variety is also known for its robust growth and ability to naturalize in suitable climates.
