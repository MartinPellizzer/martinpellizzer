Lactuca marunguensis

Lactuca marunguensis, commonly known as the Namibian lettuce, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid regions, adapting to harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are usually succulent, providing moisture retention, while its flowers are small and clustered.

Lactuca marunguensis is often found in rocky soils and can thrive in low-nutrient environments.
