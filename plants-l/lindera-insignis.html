Lindera insignis

Lindera insignis, commonly known as the fragrant woodbine, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, opposite leaves that are usually elliptic to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and streambanks.

Lindera insignis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for various wildlife species.
