Lantana splendens

Lantana splendens, commonly known as the golden lantana, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces opposite, simple leaves that are usually hairy and have a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Lantana splendens is also known for its ability to attract birds and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.
