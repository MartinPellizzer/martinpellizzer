Laurus cinnamomea

Laurus cinnamomea, commonly known as the Chinese bay leaf, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, elliptical leaves that emit a spicy scent when crushed. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark, oval berries. Its leaves are used in traditional Chinese medicine and as a spice in culinary applications.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental and practical value.
