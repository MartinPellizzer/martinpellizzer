Lupinus bicolor subsp. tridentatus

Lupinus bicolor subsp.

tridentatus, commonly known as the three-lobed lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States. It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves with three sharply toothed lobes and vibrant blue to purple flowers.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry grasslands and open woodlands. Its root system hosts nitrogen-fixing bacteria, contributing to soil fertility.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
