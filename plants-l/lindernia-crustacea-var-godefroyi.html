Lindernia crustacea var. godefroyi

Lindernia crustacea var. godefroyi, commonly known as the Godefroy's crusted lindernia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its prostrate growth habit and hairy, fleshy stems. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine pubescence, while the flowers are small, tubular, and typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
