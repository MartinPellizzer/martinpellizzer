Leucaena colombiana

Leucaena colombiana, commonly known as Colombian leucaena, is a fast-growing shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant is adapted to various soil types but thrives best in well-drained, sandy soils. It is often used for forage, soil stabilization, and as a source of nitrogen-fixing benefits in agricultural systems.

Its seeds and foliage are also utilized in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
