Leucas volkensii var. volkensii

Leucas volkensii var. volkensii, commonly known as the African bluebottle, is a flowering plant native to eastern and southern Africa.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes in warm climates.

Its ornamental value and ability to attract wildlife make it a popular choice for horticultural use.
