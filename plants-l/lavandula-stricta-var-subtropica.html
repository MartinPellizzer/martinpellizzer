Lavandula stricta var. subtropica

Lavandula stricta var. subtropica, commonly known as the narrowleaf lavender, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems covered in spikes of fragrant, pale blue to violet flowers. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are gray-green in color and slightly hairy. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and essential oil content, which is used in aromatherapy and perfumery.
