Lilium albanicum subsp. jankae

Lilium albanicum subsp.

jankae, commonly known as the Albanian lily, is a species native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink.

The plant prefers well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This subspecies is valued for its horticultural potential and ecological role in its native habitat.
