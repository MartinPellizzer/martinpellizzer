Lysiella

Lysiella, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It consists of a single species, Lysiella monilifera, which is native to South Africa. The plant is characterized by its unique, bell-shaped flowers that appear in clusters, often resembling small, ghostly forms. Lysiella thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Its striking appearance and limited distribution make it a rare and interesting subject in botanical study.
