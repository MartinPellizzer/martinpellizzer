Limonium peregrinum

Limonium peregrinum, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and saline soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating.

Limonium peregrinum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in coastal gardens.
