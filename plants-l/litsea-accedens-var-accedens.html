Litsea accedens var. accedens

Litsea accedens var. accedens, commonly known as the white-flowered litsea, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Vietnam, Thailand, and Malaysia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and usually have a glossy surface with a distinct aromatic scent.

The fruit is a small, round drupe that turns from green to red when mature, containing a single seed.
