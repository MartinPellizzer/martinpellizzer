Lagochilus diacanthophyllus subsp. karataviensis

Lagochilus diacanthophyllus subsp.

karataviensis, commonly known as Karatavian thyme, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, often in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink to purple in color, and it produces small, wrinkled seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
