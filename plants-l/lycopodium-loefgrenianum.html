Lycopodium loefgrenianum

Lycopodium loefgrenianum, commonly known as the Loefgren's clubmoss, is a small evergreen fern-like plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments. The plant produces small, spore-bearing cones rather than flowers, which are typically found at the base of its foliage. L. loefgrenianum is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including China, Japan, and the Philippines.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it is often found in forest undergrowth where it contributes to soil stabilization and biodiversity.
