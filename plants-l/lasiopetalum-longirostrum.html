Lasiopetalum longirostrum

Lasiopetalum longirostrum, commonly known as the long-beaked leek-orchid, is an herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, often with a distinctive elongated spur. It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils in arid or semi-arid regions.

Lasiopetalum longirostrum is adapted to withstand drought and is often found in open habitats such as plains and woodlands.
