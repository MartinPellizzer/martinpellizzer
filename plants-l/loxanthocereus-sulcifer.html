Loxanthocereus sulcifer

Loxanthocereus sulcifer, commonly known as the Mexican night bloomer, is a cacti species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are ribbed and covered in spines. The plant produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It thrives in arid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Loxanthocereus sulcifer is a slow-growing plant that is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
