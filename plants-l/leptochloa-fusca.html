Leptochloa fusca

Leptochloa fusca, commonly known as slender grass or slender bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to various regions of Asia and Africa.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, which allows it to spread and form dense mats.

Leptochloa fusca is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
