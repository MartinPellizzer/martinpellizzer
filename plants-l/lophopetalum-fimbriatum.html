Lophopetalum fimbriatum

Lophopetalum fimbriatum, commonly known as the fringe-flowered hawthorn, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils. The plant features simple, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers in the spring. Its fruit is a small, yellowish drupe that ripens in the summer.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping for its attractive foliage and floral display.
