Lonicera germanica

Lonicera germanica, commonly known as the German honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its berries, which are red when mature, are edible and often consumed by birds.

Lonicera germanica is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
