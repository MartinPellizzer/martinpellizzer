Lipara

Lipara, commonly known as the sacred lotus of ancient Egypt, is a flowering plant belonging to the Nymphaeaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom on long, slender stalks rising from water. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, providing a habitat for various aquatic organisms. Its flowers typically open in the early morning and close by midday, a behavior known as nyctinasty.

Lipara plays a significant role in both ecological and cultural contexts, symbolizing purity and rebirth in many ancient traditions.
