Loxanthes

Loxanthes, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to various regions in Africa.

These plants are characterized by their upright growth habit and showy, often velvety flowers that come in a range of colors. The leaves are typically thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in arid environments. Loxanthes species are popular in horticulture due to their ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.

They thrive in well-drained soil and prefer bright, indirect light.
