Linaria aragonensis

Linaria aragonensis, commonly known as the Aragon snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

Linaria aragonensis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
