Limonium aureum

Limonium aureum, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and silver-gray foliage. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, showing a high tolerance for salt and wind. Its flowers are composed of tubular petals that open in the morning and close in the evening.

Limonium aureum is often used in ornamental gardening for its drought-resistant nature and aesthetic appeal.
