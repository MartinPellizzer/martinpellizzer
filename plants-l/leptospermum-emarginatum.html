Leptospermum emarginatum

Leptospermum emarginatum, commonly known as the coastal tea tree, is a small evergreen shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and small, urn-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in coastal and sandy soils, often found in heathlands and dunes. It is well adapted to salt spray and can withstand moderate drought conditions.

Leptospermum emarginatum is also valued for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in horticulture for its ornamental qualities.
