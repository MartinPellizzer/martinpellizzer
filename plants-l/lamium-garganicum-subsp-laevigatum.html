Lamium garganicum subsp. laevigatum

Lamium garganicum subsp.

laevigatum, commonly known as the smooth-limbed deadnettle, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically smooth or only slightly hairy.

The plant produces clusters of small, two-lipped flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom from spring to early summer. It thrives in woodland or shaded areas, preferring moist, well-drained soils.

This subspecies is often found in the undergrowth of forests and along stream banks.
