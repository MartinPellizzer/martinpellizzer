Lunathyrium gillespiei

Lunathyrium gillespiei, commonly known as the desert gentian, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows from 10 to 30 centimeters in height, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are adapted to conserve water. Its root system is fibrous and helps it survive in sandy or rocky soils.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in harsh desert conditions, making it an important part of its native ecosystem.
