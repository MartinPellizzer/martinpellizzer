Lolium distachyon

Lolium distachyon, commonly known as switchgrass, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, making it a dioecious species. Switchgrass is well adapted to a variety of soil types and is often used in ecological restoration projects.

Its rapid growth and high biomass potential make it an important candidate for biofuel production.
