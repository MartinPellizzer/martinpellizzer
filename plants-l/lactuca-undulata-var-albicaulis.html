Lactuca undulata var. albicaulis

Lactuca undulata var. albicaulis, commonly known as the white-stemmed lettuce, is a variant of the lettuce species characterized by its pale, often bluish-green leaves and white or pale green stems.

It is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and is typically found in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields. The plant has a rosette growth habit, with leaves that are usually undulating or wavy in shape. It produces small, green flowers in clusters, which are followed by slender, elongated seed pods.

This variety is valued for its mild flavor and is sometimes cultivated as a leafy vegetable.
