Lasianthus stipularis var. novaguineensis

Lasianthus stipularis var. novaguineensis, commonly known as the Madagascar flame flower, is a species native to Madagascar.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its striking red-orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a prominent stipule, which is a leaf-like structure at the base of the leaf stalk. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers attract pollinators such as birds and insects, contributing to its ecological significance.
