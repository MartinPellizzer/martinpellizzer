Leandra marigotiana

Leandra marigotiana, commonly known as the Guiana skyflower, is a striking climbing plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white or pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters, often reaching up to 12 inches in diameter. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves and is known for its rapid growth habit, making it a popular choice for vertical gardens. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-draining soil and partial to full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
