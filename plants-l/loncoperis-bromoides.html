Loncoperis bromoides

Loncoperis bromoides, commonly known as the slender false dandelion, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters, featuring hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its yellow flowers bloom in spring, followed by small, capsule-like fruits that disperse seeds by wind.

It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas.
