Lysiana subfalcata

Lysiana subfalcata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with six petals and prominent stamens. Lysiana subfalcata thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists alike.
