Lindera queenslandica

Lindera queenslandica, commonly known as the Queensland loropetalum, is a flowering plant native to northeastern Australia.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows in coastal and wetland areas. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green, with a slightly aromatic scent. It produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers in spring, followed by red berries that attract birds.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to moist, well-drained soils.
