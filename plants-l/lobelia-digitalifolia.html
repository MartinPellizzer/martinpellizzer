Lobelia digitalifolia

Lobelia digitalifolia, commonly known as the digitate lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, showy blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a distinctive notched or toothed margin, giving it a unique appearance.

Lobelia digitalifolia is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
