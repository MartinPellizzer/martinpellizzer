Liparis sasakii

Liparis sasakii, commonly known as the Sasakii orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Japan.

It is characterized by its erect, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers with a subtle fragrance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Liparis sasakii is also noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in partial shade.
