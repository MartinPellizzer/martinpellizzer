Leucothoe crassifolia var. macrophylla

Leucothoe crassifolia var. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved leucothoe, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and its attractive, elongated leaves that are thick and leathery. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, which are followed by dark blue berries in the fall. Its foliage remains vibrant throughout the growing season, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.
