Lilium philadelphicum

Lilium philadelphicum, commonly known as the showy lady's slipper, is a species of flowering plant in the lily family.

It is native to the eastern United States and is characterized by its large, showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has strap-like, lance-shaped leaves. It produces a tall stem with a single flower that blooms in late summer.

The bulbs of this plant are used for propagation and are an important part of its botanical structure.
