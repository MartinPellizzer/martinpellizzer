Laurus ferruginea

Laurus ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty laurel, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves with a distinctive reddish-brown underside. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, black berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Laurus ferruginea is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and aromatic properties.
