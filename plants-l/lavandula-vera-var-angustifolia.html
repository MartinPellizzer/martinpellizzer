Lavandula vera var. angustifolia

Lavandula vera var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved lavender, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, aromatic stems covered in spikes of purple or pink flowers. The leaves are narrow, linear, and covered in fine, silvery hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal use.

This variety is valued for its essential oils, which are used in perfumery, aromatherapy, and traditional medicine.
