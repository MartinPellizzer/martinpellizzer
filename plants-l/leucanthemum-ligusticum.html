Leucanthemum ligusticum

Leucanthemum ligusticum, commonly known as the Ligusticum sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small sunflowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring narrow, lance-shaped leaves and yellow-orange flower heads. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
