Lisianthius carinatus

Lisianthius carinatus, commonly known as the mountain lisianthus, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive central disc surrounded by narrow, strap-like petals.

Lisianthius carinatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
