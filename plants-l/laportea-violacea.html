Laportea violacea

Laportea violacea, commonly known as violetleaf or purpleleaf, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, violet-hued leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale purple flowers during the spring and summer months. Its aromatic foliage has been used in traditional herbal remedies.

Laportea violacea thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in shaded, well-drained soils.
