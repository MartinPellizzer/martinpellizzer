Lycianthes stellata

Lycianthes stellata, commonly known as the starfruit or golden star, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into the edible, star-shaped fruits. The fruits are typically 3 to 5 centimeters in diameter and have a sweet, tangy flavor when ripe.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental and culinary value.
