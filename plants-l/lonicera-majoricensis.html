Lonicera majoricensis

Lonicera majoricensis, commonly known as the Greater Caper spurge, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean.

It is a shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in disturbed soils or along roadsides. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It has opposite, simple leaves that are usually oval or elliptic in shape.

Lonicera majoricensis is characterized by its milky sap, which can be toxic if ingested.
