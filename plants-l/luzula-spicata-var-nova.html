Luzula spicata var. nova

Luzula spicata var. nova, commonly known as the New Zealand tufted sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color and arranged in dense, compact tufts. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spike-like clusters, which are usually borne on elongated stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and wetlands.

Luzula spicata var. nova is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
