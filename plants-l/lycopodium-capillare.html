Lycopodium capillare

Lycopodium capillare, commonly known as the slender clubmoss, is a small evergreen plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, thread-like leaves that form dense, low-growing mats. This plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its sporophyte structure consists of a short stem with whorls of leaves and small, cone-like strobila that produce spores.

Lycopodium capillare plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
