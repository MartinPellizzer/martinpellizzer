Linanthus maculatus subsp. emaculatus

Linanthus maculatus subsp.

emaculatus, commonly known as the spotted flax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its delicate, often hairy stems and small, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces clusters of showy, yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This subspecies is noted for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
