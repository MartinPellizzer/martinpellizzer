Leandra tomentosa var. major

Leandra tomentosa var. major, commonly known as the woolly leandra, is a species of flowering plant in the family Malvaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and showy, pendulous flowers that bloom in shades of pink to red. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
