Linaria boushehrensis

Linaria boushehrensis, commonly known as the Boushehren's toadflax, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in the Middle East.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its slender stems and lance-shaped leaves are typical of the genus Linaria.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
