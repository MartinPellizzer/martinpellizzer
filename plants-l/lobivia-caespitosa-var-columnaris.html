Lobivia caespitosa var. columnaris

Lobivia caespitosa var. columnaris, commonly known as the columnar lobivia, is a succulent plant native to South America.

It features a compact, cylindrical stem that grows upward, often forming a single, upright column. The plant produces small, paddle-shaped leaves that are typically blue-green in color. It blooms with yellow flowers during the spring and summer months.

This variety is well-suited for rock gardens and arid environments due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
