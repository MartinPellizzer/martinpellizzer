Lepanthes crassicaulis

Lepanthes crassicaulis, commonly known as the thick-stemmed urn orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It typically grows in humid, shaded environments within tropical rainforests. The plant features a short, stout stem and produces small, showy flowers in shades of white, yellow, or green. Its leaves are usually broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native habitat.

Lepanthes crassicaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
