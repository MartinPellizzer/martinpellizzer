Lysimachia daphnoides

Lysimachia daphnoides, commonly known as creeping Jenny, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North America.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from May to July. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy green leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish coating. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Lysimachia daphnoides is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered invasive in certain regions.
