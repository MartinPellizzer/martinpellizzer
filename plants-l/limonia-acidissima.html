Limonia acidissima

Limonia acidissima, commonly known as the Indian sour orange, is a small evergreen tree native to southern Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, waxy leaves and yellow-orange fruits that are highly acidic. The plant belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes other citrus plants. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The fruit is used in traditional medicine and as a source of citric acid.
