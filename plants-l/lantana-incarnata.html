Lantana incarnata

Lantana incarnata, commonly known as the Blazing Star, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange to red. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 feet. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Lantana incarnata thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
