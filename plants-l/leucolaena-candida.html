Leucolaena candida

Leucolaena candida, commonly known as the white leucolaena, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its small, white flowers and trifoliate leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments. Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.

Leucolaena candida is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential in phytoremediation.
