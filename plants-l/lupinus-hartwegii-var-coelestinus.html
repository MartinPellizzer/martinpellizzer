Lupinus hartwegii var. coelestinus

Lupinus hartwegii var. coelestinus, commonly known as the sky blue lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking blue to violet pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces cylindrical seed pods that contain several seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological role in nitrogen fixation.
