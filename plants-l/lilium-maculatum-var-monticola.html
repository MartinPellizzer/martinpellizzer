Lilium maculatum var. monticola

Lilium maculatum var. monticola, commonly known as the spotted lily, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant, and often bi-colored flowers that range from white to pink and red. The plant features strap-like, dark green leaves that are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique spotting and the dramatic display of its blooms.
