Leptodactylon nuttallii var. floribundum

Leptodactylon nuttallii var. floribundum, commonly known as the flowering false hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features basal rosettes of elliptic, dark green leaves with a waxy texture and a distinctive reddish-purple underside. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped, white or pale green flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique addition to native plant gardens and ecological studies.
