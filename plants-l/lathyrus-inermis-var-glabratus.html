Lathyrus inermis var. glabratus

Lathyrus inermis var. glabratus, commonly known as the smooth vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple or pink. The leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being ovate and slightly hairy. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for soil enrichment in agricultural settings.
