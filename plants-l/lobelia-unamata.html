Lobelia unamata

Lobelia unamata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf lobelia, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, tubular blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has heart-shaped to ovate leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Lobelia unamata is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
