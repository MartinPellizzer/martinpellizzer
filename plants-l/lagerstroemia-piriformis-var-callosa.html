Lagerstroemia piriformis var. callosa

Lagerstroemia piriformis var. callosa, commonly known as the pink musk-fruit tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive pink or red flowers that bloom in summer, followed by showy, papery fruits. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly waxy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical climates.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
