Lotus corniculatus subsp. japonicus

Lotus corniculatus subsp.

japonicus, commonly known as the Japanese bird's-foot trefoil, is a flowering plant native to East Asia. It is characterized by its small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has trifoliate leaves, each composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
