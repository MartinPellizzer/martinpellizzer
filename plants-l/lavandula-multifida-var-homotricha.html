Lavandula multifida var. homotricha

Lavandula multifida var. homotricha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lavender, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and fragrant purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are narrow and linear, with a silvery-gray underside, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in herbal remedies and aromatherapy.
