Leptospermum nichollsii

Leptospermum nichollsii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tea tree, is a small evergreen shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the myrtle family (Myrtaceae) and is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas and heathlands. It produces small, oval fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Leptospermum nichollsii is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
