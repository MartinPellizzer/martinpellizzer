Lagochilus seravschanicus subsp. nuristanicus

Lagochilus seravschanicus subsp.

nuristanicus, commonly known as the Nuristan thyme, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Afghanistan and Pakistan. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage.

The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving at high altitudes. Its small, pink to purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
