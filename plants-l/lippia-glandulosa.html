Lippia glandulosa

Lippia glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular lippia, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, small, white to pink flowers. The plant has opposite, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its glandular appearance. Lippia glandulosa is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
