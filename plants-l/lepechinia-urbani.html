Lepechinia urbani

Lepechinia urbani, commonly known as the urban lepechinia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, nutlet-like fruits.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
