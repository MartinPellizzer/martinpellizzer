Labichea eremaea

Labichea eremaea, commonly known as the desert labichea, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, adapting to harsh environmental conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom during the cooler months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are small and often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Labichea eremaea is valued for its resilience and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects in dry landscapes.
