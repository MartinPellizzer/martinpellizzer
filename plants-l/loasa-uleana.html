Loasa uleana

Loasa uleana, commonly known as the Brazilian ironwood, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, leathery fruits that contain several seeds. Loasa uleana is valued for its hard, durable wood, which has been traditionally used in craftsmanship and construction.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.
