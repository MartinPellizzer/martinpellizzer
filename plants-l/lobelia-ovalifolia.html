Lobelia ovalifolia

Lobelia ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaf lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its oval-shaped leaves and delicate blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is often found in the Appalachian region.

Lobelia ovalifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
