Larrea balsamica

Larrea balsamica, commonly known as balsam tree or foothill burscht, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, compound leaves and yellowish flowers. The plant is well adapted to harsh environments, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in dry, rocky soils. Its bark contains a resinous substance that gives it a distinctive balsamic aroma, which has been used in traditional medicine.

Larrea balsamica is also known for its slow growth and long lifespan, making it a resilient component of desert ecosystems.
