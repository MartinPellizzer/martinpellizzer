Linum loreyi

Linum loreyi, commonly known as the Lorey's flax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs.

Linum loreyi is part of the Linaceae family, which includes other flax species known for their fibrous seeds and ornamental value.
