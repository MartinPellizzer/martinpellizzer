Luzula cascadensis

Luzula cascadensis, commonly known as the cascade sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, three-lobed flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and rocky slopes.

Luzula cascadensis is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in wetland areas.
