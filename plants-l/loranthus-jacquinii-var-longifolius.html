Loranthus jacquinii var. longifolius

Loranthus jacquinii var. longifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of host trees, primarily in the genus *Eucalyptus*.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own carbohydrates through photosynthesis using the light it captures. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are often clustered at the base of its stems. Its flowers are typically white or greenish-yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, fleshy berries that are dispersed by birds.

This species is native to Australia and is often found in coastal regions where its host trees thrive.
