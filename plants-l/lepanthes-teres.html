Lepanthes teres

Lepanthes teres, commonly known as the dwarf violet orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid with a delicate, often fragrant flower. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as cloud forests. Its leaves are usually dark green and broad, while its flowers are characterized by their violet or purple coloration.

Lepanthes teres is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
