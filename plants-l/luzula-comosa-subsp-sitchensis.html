Luzula comosa subsp. sitchensis

Luzula comosa subsp.

sitchensis, commonly known as Sitka sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern hemisphere. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas and alpine environments.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in spike-like clusters. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in cool, temperate climates and its preference for partial to full shade.

This subspecies is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil in wetland habitats.
