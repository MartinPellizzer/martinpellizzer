Lophopyrum curvifolium

Lophopyrum curvifolium, commonly known as the bent-leaf wild rye, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, curved leaves and produces dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant thrives in a variety of habitats, including prairies, woodlands, and disturbed soils. It is valued for its erosion control capabilities and is often used in restoration projects.

Lophopyrum curvifolium is also an important forage plant for livestock.
