Leandra cuspidata

Leandra cuspidata, commonly known as the hookleaf hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a prominent yellow or red throat. The plant has simple, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun.

Leandra cuspidata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
