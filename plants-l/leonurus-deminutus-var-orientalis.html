Leonurus deminutus var. orientalis

Leonurus deminutus var. orientalis, commonly known as the lesser hedgehog plant, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia and the Middle East.

It features small, hairy, heart-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice among gardeners.
