Leuconotis bullata

Leuconotis bullata, commonly known as the swollen bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a distinctive swollen base. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its unique appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
