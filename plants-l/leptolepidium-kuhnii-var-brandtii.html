Leptolepidium kuhnii var. brandtii

Leptolepidium kuhnii var. brandtii, commonly known as the Brandt's rockcress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and features small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pale yellow. The plant produces small, elongated fruits called siliques, which contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or arctic environments.

This variety is notable for its resilience to harsh climatic conditions and is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
