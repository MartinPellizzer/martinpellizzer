Lachnagrostis punicea subsp. filifolia

Lachnagrostis punicea subsp.

filifolia, commonly known as the purple moor grass, is a low-growing perennial grass species native to the British Isles. It typically forms dense mats of foliage with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with purple or reddish hues.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in heathland and coastal areas. Lachnagrostis punicea subsp.

filifolia plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
