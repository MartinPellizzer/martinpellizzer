Lucuma zapota

Lucuma zapota, commonly known as the sweet sop or custard apple, is a tropical fruit tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is characterized by its large, spiky green fruit that turns yellow when ripe. The fruit has a creamy, aromatic pulp with a texture similar to custard, containing numerous seeds. It is valued for its nutritional content, including high levels of vitamins and minerals.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
