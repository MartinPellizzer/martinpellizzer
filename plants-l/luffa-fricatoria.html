Luffa fricatoria

Luffa fricatoria, commonly known as the sponge gourd, is a tropical vine belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, lobed leaves and large, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces a unique, spongy fruit that becomes fibrous and usable as a natural sponge after drying. The fruit's structure consists of a thick outer rind and a hollow, fibrous interior.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires a long growing season for optimal development.
