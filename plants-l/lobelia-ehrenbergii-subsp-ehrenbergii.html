Lobelia ehrenbergii subsp. ehrenbergii

Lobelia ehrenbergii subsp.

ehrenbergii, commonly known as the desert lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture.

The plant produces striking blue to violet tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to arid environments, with a deep root system to access moisture.
