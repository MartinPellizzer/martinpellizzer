Linnaea borealis f. superba

Linnaea borealis f. superba, commonly known as the "sweet-scented wintergreen," is a variant of the twinflower plant native to northern regions of North America and Eurasia.

This low-growing evergreen shrub features small, white, bilaterally symmetric flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a glossy, dark green appearance. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and flowers, which emit a subtle, sweet fragrance.

It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in boreal and temperate forest understories.
