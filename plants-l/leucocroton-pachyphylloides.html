Leucocroton pachyphylloides

Leucocroton pachyphylloides, commonly known as the thick-leaved leucocroton, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, thick, and often glossy leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that are typically followed by bright red or orange berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

However, it is important to note that some species within the genus Leucocroton can be toxic, so caution is advised when handling it.
