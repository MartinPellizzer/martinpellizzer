Lobelia dunbariae

Lobelia dunbariae, commonly known as Dunbar's lobelia, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its erect stems and showy blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

Lobelia dunbariae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for native pollinators.
