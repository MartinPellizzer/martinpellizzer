Lepanthes lobiserrata

Lepanthes lobiserrata, commonly known as the lobed-leaf orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized epiphytic orchid, typically found growing on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are often lobed, giving it its scientific name. Its flowers are usually white or pale green, with intricate patterns and a subtle fragrance.

Lepanthes lobiserrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
