Lathyrus inermis subsp. angustifolius

Lathyrus inermis subsp.

angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leafed pea, is a perennial legume native to Europe and western Asia. It features slender, erect stems with alternate, pinnately compound leaves composed of narrow, linear leaflets.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers in shades of purple or pink, typically in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a cylindrical pod containing several elliptical, dark brown seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
