Lycianthes fugax var. flexuosa

Lycianthes fugax var. flexuosa, commonly known as the flexible false huito, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to Central and South America, where it typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its trailing or climbing habit, with simple, ovate leaves and showy yellow flowers. It produces small, fleshy berries that are often eaten by birds and other wildlife.

This variant is distinguished by its more flexible stems compared to the typical form of Lycianthes fugax.
