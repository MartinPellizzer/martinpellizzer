Lobelia melleri var. grossidens

Lobelia melleri var. grossidens, commonly known as the giant lobelia, is a striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescences that bear numerous small, tubular blue or purple flowers. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous areas with well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
