Lucuma ramiflora var. lanceolata

Lucuma ramiflora var. lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceolate lucuma, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly Peru and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow-orange berries that are rich in nutrients. The fruit has a sweet, creamy texture and is often used in culinary applications.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil and moderate humidity.
