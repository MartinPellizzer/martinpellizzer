Leucosmia pubiflora

Leucosmia pubiflora, commonly known as the hairy-flowered leucosmia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The plant features striking, white, waxy flowers that are often covered in fine, hairy structures, giving it its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are typically broad and heart-shaped, contributing to its lush appearance.
