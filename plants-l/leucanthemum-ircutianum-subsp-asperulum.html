Leucanthemum ircutianum subsp. asperulum

Leucanthemum ircutianum subsp.

asperulum, commonly known as the roughleaf white ragwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia. It features basal rosettes of rough, hairy, gray-green leaves that are typically covered in fine, stiff hairs.

The plant produces yellow flowers with white ray florets, resembling a daisy, which bloom in summer. It thrives in dry, rocky, or gravelly soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
