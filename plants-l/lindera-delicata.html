Lindera delicata

Lindera delicata, commonly known as sweet pepper bush, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, aromatic stems and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces elliptical, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its fruit is a red, fleshy drupe that ripens in the fall, attracting various wildlife species.

Lindera delicata thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soils, making it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
