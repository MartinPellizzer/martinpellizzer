Ludwigia hyssopifolia

Ludwigia hyssopifolia, commonly known as the purple Ludwigia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking purple to red flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and streambanks, where it thrives in moist, sunny conditions. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Ludwigia hyssopifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant floral display and ability to add color to aquatic or bog gardens.
