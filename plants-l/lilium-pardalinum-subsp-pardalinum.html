Lilium pardalinum subsp. pardalinum

Lilium pardalinum subsp.

pardalinum, commonly known as the red tiger lily, is a striking ornamental plant native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

The plant produces large, showy flowers with bright red petals and a yellow-orange throat, often with a distinctive tiger-like pattern. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

This subspecies is valued for its vibrant blooms and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
