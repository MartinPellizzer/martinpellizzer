Licuala acutifida

Licuala acutifida, commonly known as the broadleaf pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palm-like leaves that are typically broad and pointed. The plant has a stout, unbranched stem that can grow up to several meters in height. Its leaves are arranged in a fan shape and are often used in traditional crafts and as thatching material.

The plant thrives in humid, coastal environments and is adapted to salt spray and sandy soils.
