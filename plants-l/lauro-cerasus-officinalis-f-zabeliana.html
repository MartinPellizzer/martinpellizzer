Lauro-cerasus officinalis f. zabeliana

Lauro-cerasus officinalis f. zabeliana, commonly known as the Zabel's cherry laurel, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Prunus genus.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are edible but typically not consumed by humans. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in temperate climates.
