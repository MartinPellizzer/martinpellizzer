Linum hirsutum subsp. platyphyllum

Linum hirsutum subsp.

platyphyllum, commonly known as the broadleaf flax, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and broad, ovate leaves that are typically hairy on both surfaces.

The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in textile production.
