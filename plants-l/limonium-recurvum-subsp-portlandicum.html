Limonium recurvum subsp. portlandicum

Limonium recurvum subsp.

portlandicum, commonly known as the Portland lavender, is a flowering plant native to the British Isles. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in coastal and maritime environments, thriving in sandy or gravelly soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, fragrant blooms.
