Lophopetalum winkleri

Lophopetalum winkleri, commonly known as the Winkler's lophopetalum, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, showy flowers that are typically pink or white, arranged in clusters along the stems. The plant has thick, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss in its dry habitat. It produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds dispersed by animals.

Lophopetalum winkleri is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
