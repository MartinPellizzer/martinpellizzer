Larix dahurica var. koreana

Larix dahurica var. koreana, commonly known as Korean larch, is a deciduous conifer native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its needle-like leaves that turn golden in autumn before shedding. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, typical of coniferous species. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in mountainous regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in temperate climates.
