Lychnis kigesiensis

Lychnis kigesiensis, commonly known as the Japanese fireweed, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland areas. The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy, pink to red flowers bloom in dense clusters during the summer months.

Lychnis kigesiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
