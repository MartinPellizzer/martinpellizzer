Leucothoe rivularis

Leucothoe rivularis, commonly known as the rivular rosebay, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, acidic soils along stream banks and forest edges. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. Its showy white or pink flowers appear in clusters during late spring to early summer.

Leucothoe rivularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
