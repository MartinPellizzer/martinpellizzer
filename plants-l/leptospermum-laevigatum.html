Leptospermum laevigatum

Leptospermum laevigatum, commonly known as the white tea tree, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red or orange berries. It is a member of the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea tree species.

This plant is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
