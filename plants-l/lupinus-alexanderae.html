Lupinus alexanderae

Lupinus alexanderae, commonly known as the Alexandrine lupine, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and showy, pea-like flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces cylindrical, hairy pods that contain several seeds.

Lupinus alexanderae is adapted to thrive in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats.
