Limnanthes douglasii subsp. douglasii

Limnanthes douglasii subsp.

douglasii, commonly known as the marshmallow plant, is a perennial herbaceous species native to western North America. It is characterized by its soft, woolly leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant thrives in moist, sandy soils near water bodies such as marshes and ponds. Its fruit is a capsule that releases small, wind-dispersed seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
