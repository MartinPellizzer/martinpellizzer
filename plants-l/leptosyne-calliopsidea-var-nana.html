Leptosyne calliopsidea var. nana

Leptosyne calliopsidea var. nana, commonly known as the dwarf leptosyne, is a low-growing, succulent plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It features small, fleshy, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It is well adapted to hot, dry climates and can store water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appeal, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in rock gardens or as a houseplant.
