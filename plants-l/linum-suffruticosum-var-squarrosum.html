Linum suffruticosum var. squarrosum

Linum suffruticosum var. squarrosum, commonly known as the rough flax or squarrosum flax, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and distinctive blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a rough texture. This species thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
