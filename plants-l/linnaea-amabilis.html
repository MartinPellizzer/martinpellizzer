Linnaea amabilis

Linnaea amabilis, commonly known as the twinflower, is a small evergreen shrub native to the temperate regions of North America and Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, oval-shaped leaves and its clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The flowers are typically white or pink and are borne on short, hairy pedicels. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful.

Linnaea amabilis is often found in moist, shaded woodlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
