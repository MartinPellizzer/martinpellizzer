Ligularia discoidea

Ligularia discoidea, commonly known as the heartleaf arrowhead, is a striking aquatic plant native to North America.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often bright green with a waxy texture, and it produces small, yellowish flowers in the summer. The plant thrives in shallow, still waters and is often found in ponds and wetlands. Its roots help stabilize the soil, making it an important species for aquatic ecosystems.

Ligularia discoidea is also popular in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and ease of cultivation.
