Linum medium

Linum medium, commonly known as common flax, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces blue or white flowers with five petals, which are arranged in a loose raceme. Its seeds are widely used for oil production and fiber extraction.

Linum medium thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for both agricultural and ornamental purposes.
