Leucanthemopsis pallida var. bilbilitanum

Leucanthemopsis pallida var. bilbilitanum, commonly known as the white leucanthemum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. The plant has a compact growth habit with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
