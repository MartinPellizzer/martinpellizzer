Limnophila pulcherrima

Limnophila pulcherrima, commonly known as the water hyssop, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and ponds, where it thrives in moist to aquatic conditions. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are often blue or purple in color. It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage.

Limnophila pulcherrima is often used in water gardens and can also serve as a habitat for various aquatic organisms.
