Lysiloma chiapense

Lysiloma chiapense, commonly known as the Chiapanensis trumpet tree, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain seeds covered in a fibrous membrane. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation in warmer regions.
