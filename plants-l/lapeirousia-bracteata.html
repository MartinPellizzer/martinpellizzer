Lapeirousia bracteata

Lapeirousia bracteata, commonly known as the Cape goldcup, is a striking member of the Amaryllidaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Lapeirousia bracteata is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in rock gardens and containers.
