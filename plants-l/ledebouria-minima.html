Ledebouria minima

Ledebouria minima, commonly known as the dwarf bulbous plant, is a small, hardy succulent native to southern Africa.

It features a compact rosette of fleshy, grass-like leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white or pale yellow during the spring or early summer. It is often grown as a decorative indoor or outdoor plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.

Ledebouria minima is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping, making it an ideal choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
