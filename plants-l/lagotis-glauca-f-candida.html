Lagotis glauca f. candida

Lagotis glauca f. candida, commonly known as the white-flowered bluebell, is a botanical variant of the species Lagotis glauca, characterized by its pale blue to white petals.

It is a herbaceous perennial native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere, typically found in woodland and meadow habitats. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
