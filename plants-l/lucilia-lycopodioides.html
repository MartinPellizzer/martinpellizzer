Lucilia lycopodioides

Lucilia lycopodioides, commonly known as the clubmoss, is a small, evergreen plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, scale-like leaves and has a unique reproductive structure known as a strobilus, which contains the spores. Lucilia lycopodioides is often found in forest floors or rocky habitats, where it plays a role in soil stabilization.

Its historical significance in traditional medicine and as a model organism in botanical research further highlights its ecological and scientific importance.
