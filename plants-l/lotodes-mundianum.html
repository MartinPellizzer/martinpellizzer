Lotodes mundianum

Lotodes mundianum, commonly known as the water lily of the Amazon, is a striking aquatic plant native to South America.

It features broad, floating leaves that provide shade and habitat for aquatic life. Its flowers are typically white or pink, with a distinctive yellow center, blooming in the early morning. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that anchors it in the muddy substrate of slow-moving water bodies.

It plays an important ecological role in maintaining water quality and supporting biodiversity in its native habitat.
