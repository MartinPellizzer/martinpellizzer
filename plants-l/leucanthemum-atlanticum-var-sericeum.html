Leucanthemum atlanticum var. sericeum

Leucanthemum atlanticum var. sericeum, commonly known as the silverleaf oxeye daisy, is a perennial plant native to the coastal regions of the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on the leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces daisy-like flowers with white petals surrounding a yellow center, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dunes or along the coastlines.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens in coastal areas.
