Lobelia decurrens var. jaensis

Lobelia decurrens var. jaensis, commonly known as the Jaen's lobelia, is a perennial herb native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or wetlands. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
