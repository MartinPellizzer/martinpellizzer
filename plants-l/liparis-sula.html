Liparis sula

Liparis sula, commonly known as the Sula orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are clustered in inflorescences with a distinctive coloration.

Liparis sula is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by supporting pollinators.
