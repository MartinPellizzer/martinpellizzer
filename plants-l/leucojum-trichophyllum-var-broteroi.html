Leucojum trichophyllum var. broteroi

Leucojum trichophyllum var. broteroi, commonly known as the Broter's white squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers in early spring. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, recurved petals and is often found in woodland habitats. It is relatively low-maintenance and thrives in well-drained soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
