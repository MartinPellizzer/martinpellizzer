Linaria anticaria subsp. rossmaessleri

Linaria anticaria subsp.

rossmaessleri, commonly known as the Rossmaessler's toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers.

The plant produces slender, cylindrical seed pods that release tiny seeds when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and rockeries.
