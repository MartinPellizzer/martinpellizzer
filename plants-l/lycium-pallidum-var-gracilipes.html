Lycium pallidum var. gracilipes

Lycium pallidum var. gracilipes, commonly known as the slender boxthorn, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It features small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for both humans and wildlife. The plant has woody stems and small, narrow leaves adapted to conserve water in dry environments. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its hardy nature makes it a resilient plant in challenging climates.
