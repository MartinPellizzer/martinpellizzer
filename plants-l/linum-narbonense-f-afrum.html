Linum narbonense f. afrum

Linum narbonense f. afrum, commonly known as the African flax, is a variant of the flax plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its slender stems and lance-shaped leaves. The flowers are typically blue or purple, with five petals, and bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for Mediterranean climates.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in textile production.
