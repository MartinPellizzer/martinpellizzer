Lobelia biserrata

Lobelia biserrata, commonly known as the serrated lobelia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, tubular blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are opposite and have a serrated edge, giving it its common name.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ease of care.
