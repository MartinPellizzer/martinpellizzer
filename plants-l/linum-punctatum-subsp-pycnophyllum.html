Linum punctatum subsp. pycnophyllum

Linum punctatum subsp.

pycnophyllum, commonly known as the dense-flowered flax, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, blue to purple flowers.

The leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a velvety texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
