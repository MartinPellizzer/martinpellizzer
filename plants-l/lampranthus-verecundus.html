Lampranthus verecundus

Lampranthus verecundus, commonly known as the "Cape fuchsia," is a striking South African plant belonging to the Aizoaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, often appearing in clusters at the ends of its stems. The plant is a succulent, featuring fleshy, often hairy leaves that help it store water in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Lampranthus verecundus is also known for its short blooming period, typically in late summer to early autumn.
