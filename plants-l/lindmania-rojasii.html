Lindmania rojasii

Lindmania rojasii, commonly known as the red-veined lindmania, is a species of flowering plant in the family Bromeliaceae.

It is native to the rainforests of Ecuador and is characterized by its striking, ornamental foliage with deep red veins against a green background. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, often forming a rosette that can hold water, a common feature among bromeliads.

Lindmania rojasii is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in tropical gardens.
