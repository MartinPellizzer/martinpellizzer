Lycium chilense f. glaberrimum

Lycium chilense f. glaberrimum, commonly known as the Chilean wolfberry, is a shrub native to southern South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered with a fine, pubescent layer.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to arid environments and its role in traditional medicine.
