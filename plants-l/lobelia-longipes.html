Lobelia longipes

Lobelia longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.

Lobelia longipes is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
