Lachrymaria

Lachrymaria, commonly known as the weeping willow, is a deciduous tree belonging to the Salicaceae family.

It is characterized by its long, drooping branches that create a cascading effect, resembling tears, hence its common name. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a bright green upper surface and a lighter underside. It thrives in moist environments, often found near rivers, lakes, and wetlands.

The tree is also noted for its ability to filter water and stabilize soil, making it ecologically significant.
