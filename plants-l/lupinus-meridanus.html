Lupinus meridanus

Lupinus meridanus, commonly known as the desert lupine, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. Lupinus meridanus is adapted to arid environments, with deep roots that help it survive in dry conditions.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various desert wildlife.
