Landolphia crassipes var. perrieri

Landolphia crassipes var. perrieri, commonly known as the African morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant is characterized by its long, woody stems and its ability to climb by twining around supports. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist woodland areas.

The flowers emit a strong fragrance, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
