Laportea pterostigma

Laportea pterostigma, commonly known as the broadleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its robust stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy purple to pink flowers in composite flower heads, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage has a distinctive scent and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.

Laportea pterostigma thrives in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
