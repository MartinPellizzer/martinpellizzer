Leucas eriostoma var. longifolia

Leucas eriostoma var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved leucas, is a variant of the leucas plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
