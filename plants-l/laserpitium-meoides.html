Laserpitium meoides

Laserpitium meoides, commonly known as the meadow bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a basal rosette, while its stems are erect and branched.

Laserpitium meoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
