Lauro-cerasus officinalis f. caucasica

Lauro-cerasus officinalis f. caucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian cherry laurel, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Prunus genus.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, which consists of dark green, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, followed by edible berries that are typically red or black in color. It is native to the Caucasus region and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is often cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
