Lucuma laeteviridis

Lucuma laeteviridis, commonly known as the green lucuma, is a member of the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly Peru and Ecuador. The plant produces large, yellow-orange fruits that are encased in a thick, waxy coating. The fruit's pulp is rich in nutrients, including beta-carotene and various antioxidants.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique and valuable species in both traditional and modern horticulture.
