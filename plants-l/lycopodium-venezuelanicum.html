Lycopodium venezuelanicum

Lycopodium venezuelanicum, commonly known as the Venezuelan clubmoss, is a small, evergreen plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and the presence of spore-bearing strobila at the tips of its branches. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its leaves are narrow and spirally arranged, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Lycopodium venezuelanicum plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various small organisms in its native range.
