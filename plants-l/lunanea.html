Lunanea

Lunanea, commonly known as the moonflower, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, white, night-blooming flowers that emit a sweet fragrance. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces fruits that are edible when ripe. Lunanea thrives in warm, humid climates and requires partial shade for optimal growth.

Its unique blooming pattern makes it a popular choice for evening gardens and floral displays.
