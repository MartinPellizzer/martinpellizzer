Legenere limosa

Legenere limosa, commonly known as the marsh hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to wetland areas in the Americas.

It features showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its roots are adapted to thrive in waterlogged soils, making it a common sight in marshes and swamps.

Legenere limosa plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining wetland ecosystems.
