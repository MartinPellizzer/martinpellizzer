Lepanthes ordonezii

Lepanthes ordonezii, commonly known as the Ordóñez's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments within tropical forests. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure and ephemeral blooming period.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
