Liparis nutans

Liparis nutans, commonly known as the nodding orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It features a rhizomatous growth habit and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that hang downward, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often clustered at the base of the plant.

The flowers are pollinated by insects, and the plant is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
