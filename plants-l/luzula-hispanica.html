Luzula hispanica

Luzula hispanica, commonly known as Spanish woodrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, commonly known as the grass family, and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit. The plant features slender, cylindrical stems that are often slightly hairy, and its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and arranged in a rosette. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing greenish or brownish flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated.

Luzula hispanica thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes, and is often found in mountainous regions.
