Liatris scariosa var. squarrulosa

Liatris scariosa var. squarrulosa, commonly known as the rough bluetail, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of purple to pinkish-purple flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white or grayish hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, prairies, and along stream banks.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it an important species in native plant gardens and wetland restoration projects.
