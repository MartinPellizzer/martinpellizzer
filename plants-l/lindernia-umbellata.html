Lindernia umbellata

Lindernia umbellata, commonly known as the whorled lindernia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Lindernia umbellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
