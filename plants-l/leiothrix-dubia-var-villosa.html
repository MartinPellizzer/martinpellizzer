Leiothrix dubia var. villosa

Leiothrix dubia var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy leiothrix, is a rare and lesser-known plant species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its softly hairy foliage, which gives it its varietal name "villosa.". The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in mountainous areas with rich, moist soil. Its leaves are usually elongated and have a distinctive texture, while its flowers are small and clustered, often in shades of green and white.
