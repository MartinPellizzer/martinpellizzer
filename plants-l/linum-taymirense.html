Linum taymirense

Linum taymirense, commonly known as the Arabian flax, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, while its seeds are small and oval, often used for various traditional purposes.

Linum taymirense plays a role in local ecosystems and has historical significance in regional agriculture and herbal practices.
