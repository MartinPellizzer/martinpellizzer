Loranthus acacietorum

Loranthus acacietorum, commonly known as the acacia mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the stems of acacia trees.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while also producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using light reflected from the host. The plant forms a dense, bushy growth with small, oval leaves and produces small, white or greenish flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that releases sticky seeds, which adhere to the bark of host trees.

This species is often found in tropical and subtropical regions where acacia trees are prevalent.
