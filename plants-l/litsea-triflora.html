Litsea triflora

Litsea triflora, commonly known as the three-flowered litsea, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and avocado trees. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, ovate leaves that are often fragrant when crushed. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and typically appear in clusters of three, giving it its common name.

The fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that ripens to a yellow or orange color.
