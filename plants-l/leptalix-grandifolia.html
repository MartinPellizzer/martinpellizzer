Leptalix grandifolia

Leptalix grandifolia, commonly known as the large-leaved waxplant, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, waxy leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, well-drained soil and requires indirect sunlight for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
