Lonicera alpigena f. nana

Lonicera alpigena f. nana, commonly known as the dwarf honeysuckle, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, typically in shades of yellow and orange. The plant has slender, wiry stems and small, opposite leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
