Lupinus diehlii var. debilis

Lupinus diehlii var. debilis, commonly known as the weak lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. The plant features spiny stems and clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Lupinus diehlii var. debilis is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a food source for native pollinators.
