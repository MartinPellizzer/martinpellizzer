Lepanthes proboscidis

Lepanthes proboscidis, commonly known as the proboscidis orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically feature a vibrant purple or blue hue. The plant produces small, elongated pseudobulbs that support its foliage and floral structures. Its flowers are often associated with pollination by specific species of bees and flies.

Botanically, it belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
