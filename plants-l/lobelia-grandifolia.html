Lobelia grandifolia

Lobelia grandifolia, commonly known as the showy lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. Lobelia grandifolia has lance-shaped leaves and produces showy inflorescences that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
