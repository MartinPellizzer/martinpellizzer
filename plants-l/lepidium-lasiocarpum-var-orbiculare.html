Lepidium lasiocarpum var. orbiculare

Lepidium lasiocarpum var. orbiculare, commonly known as the smooth wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous species. The plant is characterized by its low-growing habit, with small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or white. Its leaves are usually hairy and lobed, and the fruit is a small, round silique.

This variety is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.
