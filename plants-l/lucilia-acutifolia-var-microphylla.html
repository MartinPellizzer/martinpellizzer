Lucilia acutifolia var. microphylla

Lucilia acutifolia var. microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved sandmat, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
