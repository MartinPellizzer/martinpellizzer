Laserpitium montanum

Laserpitium montanum, commonly known as the mountain hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads and often aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils at high altitudes. Its flowering period occurs in late summer, producing clusters of yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Laserpitium montanum is known for its hardy nature and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
