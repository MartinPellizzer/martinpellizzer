Lupinus polyphyllus subsp. arcticus

Lupinus polyphyllus subsp.

arcticus, commonly known as Arctic lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers.

The plant has palmately divided leaves with multiple leaflets, typical of the legume family. It thrives in nutrient-poor, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Its root system forms symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility in its harsh environment.
