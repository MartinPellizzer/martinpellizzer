Lippia geminata

Lippia geminata, commonly known as Mexican mint or creeping mint, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, creeping growth habit. The plant features opposite, narrow leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale purple. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
