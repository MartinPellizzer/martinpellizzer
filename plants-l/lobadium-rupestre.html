Lobadium rupestre

Lobadium rupestre, commonly known as the rock lobelia, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in well-drained environments. Its leaves are often hairy and have a distinctive lobed shape, contributing to its common name.

Lobadium rupestre is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
