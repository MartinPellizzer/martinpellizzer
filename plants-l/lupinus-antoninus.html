Lupinus antoninus

Lupinus antoninus, commonly known as the Antonine lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and showy, spiny pods. The plant produces clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Lupinus antoninus is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
