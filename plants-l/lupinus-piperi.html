Lupinus piperi

Lupinus piperi, commonly known as the peppergrass, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and clusters of small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Lupinus piperi is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in ecological restoration efforts.
