Lunania

Lunania, commonly known as moonflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 feet. Its flowers emit a sweet fragrance, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

Lunania is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
