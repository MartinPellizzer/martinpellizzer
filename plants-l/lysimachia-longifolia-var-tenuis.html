Lysimachia longifolia var. tenuis

Lysimachia longifolia var. tenuis, commonly known as narrowleaf yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear small, bright yellow flowers clustered in dense inflorescences. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or stream banks.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
