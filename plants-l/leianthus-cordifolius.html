Leianthus cordifolius

Leianthus cordifolius, commonly known as the heart-leaved hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and riverine habitats.

The flowers are pollinated by insects and birds, making it an important species in local ecosystems.
