Lycopodium kangdingense

Lycopodium kangdingense, commonly known as the Kangding clubmoss, is a species of lycophyte native to the mountainous regions of Sichuan, China.

It belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae and is characterized by its small, spore-bearing cones and needle-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures are clustered at the tips of its stems, and it plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat.

Lycopodium kangdingense is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and its potential significance in studying plant evolution.
