Limonium mexicanum

Limonium mexicanum, commonly known as Mexican sea holly, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Plumbaginaceae family and is characterized by its showy, spike-like clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in a fine layer of white powder.

Limonium mexicanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its drought tolerance and long-lasting blooms.
