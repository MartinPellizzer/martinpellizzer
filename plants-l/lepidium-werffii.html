Lepidium werffii

Lepidium werffii, commonly known as Werff's cress, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in countries like Argentina and Bolivia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its small, white or yellow flowers are arranged in clusters, and it produces small, rounded seed pods.

Lepidium werffii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its resilience and beauty.
