Lipotriche scandens subsp. subsimplicifolia

Lipotriche scandens subsp.

subsimplicifolia, commonly known as the simple-leaved climbing germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It features slender, twining stems that climb by means of small, hooked hairs.

The leaves are simple, ovate to elliptic, and have a slightly hairy surface. It produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters during late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is characterized by its compact growth habit and preference for well-drained, sandy soils.
