Lycopodioides menziesii

Lycopodioides menziesii, commonly known as the Menzies' clubmoss, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and needle-like leaves. The plant produces spore-bearing cones at the tips of its stems, which are typically clustered in small, cylindrical structures. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rock crevices.

Lycopodioides menziesii is an important component of the understory vegetation in its native habitat.
