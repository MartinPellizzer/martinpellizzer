Lamprotis taxifolia

Lamprotis taxifolia, commonly known as the lace fern, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that resemble lace, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its leaves are pinnately divided, with each segment bearing small, rounded lobes.

Lamprotis taxifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
