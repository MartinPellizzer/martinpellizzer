Lepidium obtusum

Lepidium obtusum, commonly known as broad-leaved wall cress, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, ovate leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found along streambanks and in meadows. Its fruit is a short, cylindrical silique that contains several seeds.

Lepidium obtusum is a perennial herb that is native to North America and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
