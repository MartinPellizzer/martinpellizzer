Lonicera korolkowii f. monstrosa-pedicellata

Lonicera korolkowii f. monstrosa-pedicellata, commonly known as the giant honeysuckle, is a vigorous deciduous shrub characterized by its long, trailing stems and large, fragrant flowers.

It produces showy, trumpet-shaped blossoms that range in color from white to yellow, often with a hint of red or orange. The plant is native to eastern Asia and is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and as a climbing plant. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

The fruit is a small, red, fleshy berry that ripens in late summer, attracting birds and providing food for wildlife.
