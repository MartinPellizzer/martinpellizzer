Lessingianthus ramellae

Lessingianthus ramellae, commonly known as the pink fairy flower, is a strikingly beautiful flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces vibrant pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soils.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
