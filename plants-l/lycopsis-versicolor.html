Lycopsis versicolor

Lycopsis versicolor, commonly known as the variable lycopsis, is a small perennial herb native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features delicate, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color, with a fine, hairy texture. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually appearing in shades of white, pink, or purple.

Lycopsis versicolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local insect populations.
