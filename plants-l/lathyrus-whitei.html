Lathyrus whitei

Lathyrus whitei, commonly known as the White's vetch, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring. Lathyrus whitei is often found in moist, woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Fabaceae family.
