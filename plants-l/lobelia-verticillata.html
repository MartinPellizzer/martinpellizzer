Lobelia verticillata

Lobelia verticillata, commonly known as whorled lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features whorled leaves that are opposite or alternate, depending on the stem, and produces spikes of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.

Lobelia verticillata is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional uses.
