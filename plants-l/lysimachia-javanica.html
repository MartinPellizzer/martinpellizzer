Lysimachia javanica

Lysimachia javanica, commonly known as the Javan yellow myrtle, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers clustered in loose inflorescences, which bloom throughout the growing season. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a reddish or purple underside.

Lysimachia javanica is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
