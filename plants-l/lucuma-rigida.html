Lucuma rigida

Lucuma rigida, commonly known as the Peruvian sweet potato, is a tuberous plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its starchy, sweet-tasting tubers that are often used in traditional cuisine. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves and bears small, pale yellow flowers. Its tubers are rich in carbohydrates and have a nutty, caramel-like flavor.

Due to its unique taste and nutritional value, it is gaining popularity as a specialty crop in global markets.
