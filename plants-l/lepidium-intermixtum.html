Lepidium intermixtum

Lepidium intermixtum, commonly known as the mixed wallflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other well-known plants such as cabbages and mustards. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or white, arranged in clusters. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately divided, and covered in fine hairs.

Lepidium intermixtum thrives in dry, sunny habitats and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.
