Lappula albiflora

Lappula albiflora, commonly known as white-flowered lappula, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and thrives in temperate climates. The plant features slender stems and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Lappula albiflora is known for its role in ecological systems, providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for various insects.
