Lolium arvense var. speciosum

Lolium arvense var. speciosum, commonly known as the showy ryegrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and distinctive, often purple-tinged inflorescence. The plant produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are typically arranged in a panicle, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks.

Due to its hardy nature and aesthetic features, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens or used in ecological restoration projects.
