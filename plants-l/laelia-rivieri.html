Laelia rivieri

Laelia rivieri, commonly known as the Rivier's orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in rocky or mountainous habitats. The plant features a compact growth habit with a pseudobulb and elongated leaves. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellow, with a distinctive lip that is often darker in color.

Laelia rivieri is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
