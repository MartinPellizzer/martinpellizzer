Lycopodium complanatum proles chamaecyparissus

Lycopodium complanatum proles chamaecyparissus, commonly known as the clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky areas. The plant has a distinctive appearance with clustered, needle-like leaves and a creeping stem that often forms dense mats. It produces spores in cone-like structures called strobila, which are typically located at the base of the plant.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological habitats.
