Lilium japonicum f. angustifolium

Lilium japonicum f. angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved Japanese lily, is a botanical variant of the Japanese lily.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are narrower than those of the typical species. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pale pink, typically blooming in late summer. It is native to Japan and thrives in well-drained, moist soils in partial shade.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
