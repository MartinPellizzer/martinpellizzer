Lupinus lorenzensis

Lupinus lorenzensis, commonly known as the Lorentz lupine, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains in South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of vibrant blue or purple pea-shaped flowers. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Lupinus lorenzensis is often found in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
