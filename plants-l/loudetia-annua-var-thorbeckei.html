Loudetia annua var. thorbeckei

Loudetia annua var. thorbeckei, commonly known as the red-flowered lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with pink to red flowers, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
