Limonium caprariense subsp. multiflorum

Limonium caprariense subsp.

multiflorum, commonly known as the coastal flax or sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in coastal areas, sandy soils, and rocky habitats, showing a high degree of salt tolerance. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in dense spikes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes.
