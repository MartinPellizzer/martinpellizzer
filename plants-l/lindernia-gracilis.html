Lindernia gracilis

Lindernia gracilis, commonly known as slender lindernia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 6 to 12 inches. The plant features opposite, narrow, and slightly hairy leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. Its showy, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Lindernia gracilis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
