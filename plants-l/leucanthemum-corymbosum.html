Leucanthemum corymbosum

Leucanthemum corymbosum, commonly known as the Shasta daisy, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features showy white ray flowers surrounding a central cluster of yellow disc flowers, giving it a daisy-like appearance. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and has basal leaves that are deeply lobed and hairy. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
