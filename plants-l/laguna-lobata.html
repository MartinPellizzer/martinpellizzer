Laguna lobata

Laguna lobata, commonly known as the laceleaf plant, is a tropical species native to the humid regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, lobed leaves that give it a distinctive, feathery appearance. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its foliage is often a vibrant green, with some varieties displaying variegated patterns.

Laguna lobata thrives in high humidity and indirect light, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
