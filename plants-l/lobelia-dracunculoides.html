Lobelia dracunculoides

Lobelia dracunculoides, commonly known as the dragon's tongue lobe, is a striking perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features tall, slender stems that bear clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of violet or blue. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, elongated leaves that resemble the shape of a dragon's tongue. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Lobelia dracunculoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
