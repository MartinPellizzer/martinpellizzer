Licuala mirabilis

Licuala mirabilis, commonly known as the giant panda plant, is a striking member of the Arecaceae family native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, pinnately compound leaves that can reach up to 5 meters in length, giving it a dramatic and tropical appearance. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its leaves are broad and leathery, with a distinctive silvery-white midrib and green margins.

Licuala mirabilis is well-suited for indoor cultivation and can be grown in containers with proper drainage and consistent moisture.
