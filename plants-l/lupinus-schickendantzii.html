Lupinus schickendantzii

Lupinus schickendantzii, commonly known as the Schickendantz's lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Lupinus schickendantzii is also valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a food source for local wildlife.
