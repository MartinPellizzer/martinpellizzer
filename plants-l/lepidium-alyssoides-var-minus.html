Lepidium alyssoides var. minus

Lepidium alyssoides var. minus, commonly known as lesser wallflower, is a low-growing annual plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically features slender stems and narrow, linear leaves, often growing in rocky or disturbed soils. It produces yellow or white flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
