Loasa picta

Loasa picta, commonly known as the painted loasa, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a distinctive pattern of purple or red markings. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

Loasa picta is also valued for its ability to provide habitat and nectar for local pollinators.
