Lepidium grandifructum

Lepidium grandifructum, commonly known as the giant wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces distinctive, elongated pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to gardens and ecological landscapes.
