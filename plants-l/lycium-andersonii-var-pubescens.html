Lycium andersonii var. pubescens

Lycium andersonii var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent boxthorn, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, orange-red berries that mature in the fall. The plant has small, elliptical leaves covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
