Lupinaster albus

Lupinaster albus, commonly known as the white lupinaster, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in racemose inflorescences. Its seeds are encased in pods, typical of the legume family, and the plant is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Lupinaster albus is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential ecological benefits in soil enrichment.
