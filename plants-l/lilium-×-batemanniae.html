Lilium × batemanniae

Lilium × batemanniae, commonly known as the Batemann's lily, is a hybrid species within the genus Lilium.

It is characterized by its striking, often fragrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that can range in color from white to pink and even red. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with strap-like leaves that are arranged in a fan shape. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, thriving in temperate climates.

Botanically, it is noted for its complex hybrid origin, resulting from crosses between different Lilium species.
