Linaria verticillata subsp. cuartanensis

Linaria verticillata subsp.

cuartanensis, commonly known as the Mexican zigzag flower, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Mexico. It is characterized by its branched stems and clusters of bright yellow, zigzag-shaped flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are linear and opposite, often covered in fine hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
