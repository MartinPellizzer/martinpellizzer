Lupinus concinnus var. orcuttii

Lupinus concinnus var. orcuttii, commonly known as the Orcutt's lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and showy, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a food source for local wildlife.
