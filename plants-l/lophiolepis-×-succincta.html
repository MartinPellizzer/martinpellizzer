Lophiolepis × succincta

Lophiolepis × succincta, commonly known as the narrow-leafed wallaby grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences containing numerous tiny flowers, which are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is commonly found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Lophiolepis × succincta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native landscaping.
