Lindelofia lanata

Lindelofia lanata, commonly known as the hairy lindelofia, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. Its leaves are usually opposite and have a rough, textured surface.

Lindelofia lanata is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks.
