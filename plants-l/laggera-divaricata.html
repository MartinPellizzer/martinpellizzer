Laggera divaricata

Laggera divaricata, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Laggera divaricata is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a hardy and adaptable species.
