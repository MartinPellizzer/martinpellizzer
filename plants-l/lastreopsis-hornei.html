Lastreopsis hornei

Lastreopsis hornei, commonly known as the rock wallflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, showy yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically linear and covered in fine hairs, adapted to withstand arid conditions.

It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or inland fynbos habitats.
