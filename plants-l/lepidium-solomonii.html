Lepidium solomonii

Lepidium solomonii, commonly known as Solomon's cress, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately divided, and have a distinctive, peppery aroma.

Lepidium solomonii is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to thrive in sunny, well-drained environments.
