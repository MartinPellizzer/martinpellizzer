Loranthus dictyophlebus

Loranthus dictyophlebus, commonly known as the mistletoe of the genus Loranthus, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while still performing photosynthesis to some extent. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or pale green flowers, followed by small, fleshy berries that are often dispersed by birds. It is found in various regions of Asia, including China, India, and Southeast Asia.

Its parasitic nature and unique reproductive strategies make it an interesting subject in botanical studies.
