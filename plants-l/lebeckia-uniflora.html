Lebeckia uniflora

Lebeckia uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower lebeckia, is a South African native plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flower head, which is surrounded by pale green bracts. The plant typically grows in open, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation. It produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs.

Lebeckia uniflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
