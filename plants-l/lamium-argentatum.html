Lamium argentatum

Lamium argentatum, commonly known as silver nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine white hairs, giving them a silvery appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Lamium argentatum is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and early spring bloom.
