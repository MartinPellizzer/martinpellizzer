Limniris hexagona

Limniris hexagona, commonly known as the hexagon leaf cactus, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and distinctive hexagonal-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its adaptations include thick cuticles and succulent tissues that store water, enabling it to survive in dry environments.

Limniris hexagona is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
