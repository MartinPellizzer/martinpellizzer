Lycopersicon esculentum subsp. typicum

Lycopersicon esculentum subsp.

typicum, commonly known as the tomato, is a herbaceous annual plant native to South America. It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually hairy and oval in shape.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that develop into bright red, fleshy fruits. The fruit is the most economically important part of the plant, containing numerous seeds.

It is a key species in the nightshade family and is widely cultivated for its nutritional value and culinary uses.
