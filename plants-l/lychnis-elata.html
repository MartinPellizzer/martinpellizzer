Lychnis elata

Lychnis elata, commonly known as the tall catchfly, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, branching stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, covered in fine, glandular hairs. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers with five petals, arranged in a cluster at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are lance-shaped and alternate along the stem, often with a hairy texture.

Lychnis elata thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in meadows, wastelands, and disturbed soils.
