Laelia alaorii

Laelia alaorii, commonly known as the Alagoas orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Brazil.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant floral display. The plant typically produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, supporting its growth in dry environments. Its flowers are usually a mix of white and pink, with a distinctive lip that aids in pollination.

Laelia alaorii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
