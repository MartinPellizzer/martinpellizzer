Linaria bektauatensis

Linaria bektauatensis, commonly known as the Bektauatensis toadflax, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the genus Linaria in the family Plantaginaceae, characterized by its showy, often yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowers are tubular with two lips, a feature common in the genus, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.
