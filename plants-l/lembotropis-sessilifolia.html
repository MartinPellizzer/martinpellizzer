Lembotropis sessilifolia

Lembotropis sessilifolia, commonly known as the sessile-leaf lembotropis, is a rare and unique plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, clustered leaves that are sessile, meaning they are attached directly to the stem without a stalk. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom during the warmer months. Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in tropical botany.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
