Lupinus adsurgens var. lilacinus

Lupinus adsurgens var. lilacinus, commonly known as the purple lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and showy purple or lilac-colored flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
