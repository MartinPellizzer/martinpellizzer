Linum floridanum var. floridanum

Linum floridanum var. floridanum, commonly known as the Florida flax, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal dunes and open woodlands.

Botanically, it is a member of the Linaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
