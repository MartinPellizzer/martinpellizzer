Luzula abyssinica var. kilimandscharica

Luzula abyssinica var. kilimandscharica, commonly known as the Kilimanjaro sedge, is a perennial herb native to East Africa.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and mountainous regions. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are often blue-green in color, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer. It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and is often found in high-altitude habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native ecosystems.
