Lupinus potosinus var. bartlettii

Lupinus potosinus var. bartlettii, commonly known as the Bartlett's lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky habitats.

Lupinus potosinus var. bartlettii plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native environment.
