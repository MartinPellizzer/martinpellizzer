Laelia superbiens var. grandiflora-rosea

Laelia superbiens var. grandiflora-rosea, commonly known as the rose-rosea orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the high-altitude regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in clusters during the winter months. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks or branches in its natural habitat. Its foliage consists of broad, dark green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern.

This variety is prized for its vibrant coloration and is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value.
