Linosparton

Linosparton, commonly known as the golden flame orchid, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow and orange flowers that resemble flames. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are long, narrow, and covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

Linosparton thrives in warm, humid environments and requires indirect sunlight for optimal growth.
