Lotus grandiflorus var. macranthus

Lotus grandiflorus var. macranthus, commonly known as the giant lotus, is a striking aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter, typically in shades of pink or red. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds and lakes. Its leaves are broad and floating, providing shade and habitat for aquatic life.

The flowers bloom in summer, making it a popular ornamental plant in water gardens.
