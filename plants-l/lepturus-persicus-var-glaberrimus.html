Lepturus persicus var. glaberrimus

Lepturus persicus var. glaberrimus, commonly known as the Persian spurge, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the arid regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in cyathia, a unique floral structure typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in dry gardens and rockeries.
