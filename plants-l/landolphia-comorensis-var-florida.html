Landolphia comorensis var. florida

Landolphia comorensis var. florida, commonly known as the Florida star apple, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The plant bears edible fruits that resemble small apples, with a sweet and tart flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local cuisines and medicine.
