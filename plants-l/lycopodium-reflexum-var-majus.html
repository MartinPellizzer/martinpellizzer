Lycopodium reflexum var. majus

Lycopodium reflexum var. majus, commonly known as the giant clubmoss, is a perennial evergreen plant characterized by its tall, branching stems and small, scale-like leaves.

It belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae and is native to certain regions of North America. The plant produces spore-bearing cones at the tips of its branches, which are typically clustered in small, rounded structures. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
