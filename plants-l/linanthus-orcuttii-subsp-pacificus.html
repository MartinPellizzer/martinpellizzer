Linanthus orcuttii subsp. pacificus

Linanthus orcuttii subsp.

pacificus, commonly known as the Pacific flaxflower, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of California. It is a herbaceous annual with narrow, linear leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers.

The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dunes or along the coastline. It plays a role in stabilizing dune ecosystems and provides nectar for pollinators.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a species of concern in some areas.
