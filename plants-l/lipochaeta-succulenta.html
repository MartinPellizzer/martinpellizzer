Lipochaeta succulenta

Lipochaeta succulenta, commonly known as the succulent ironweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological role in its native habitat.
