Leandra hybophylla

Leandra hybophylla, commonly known as the silver leaf tree, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, silver-green leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, blooming in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Leandra hybophylla is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage and air-purifying properties.
