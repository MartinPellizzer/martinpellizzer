Lathyrus barcinonensis

Lathyrus barcinonensis, commonly known as the Barcino viper's bugloss, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the Iberian Peninsula and is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and has a bushy appearance with hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. It is a perennial species that thrives in sunny, well-drained environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
