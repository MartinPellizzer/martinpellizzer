Leptospermum flavescens var. leptophyllum

Leptospermum flavescens var. leptophyllum, commonly known as the yellow tea tree, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to coastal and sandy soils, often found in heathlands and dry forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
