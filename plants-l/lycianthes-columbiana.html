Lycianthes columbiana

Lycianthes columbiana, commonly known as the Columbian bitterleaf, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by orange or red berries containing several seeds. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its bitter taste, it is not typically cultivated for food but is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
