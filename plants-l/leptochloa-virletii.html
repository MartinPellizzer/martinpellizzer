Leptochloa virletii

Leptochloa virletii, commonly known as the African feather grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, fields, and riverbanks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive, feathery appearance. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets.

Leptochloa virletii is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.
