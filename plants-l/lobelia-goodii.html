Lobelia goodii

Lobelia goodii, commonly known as the Good's lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, spurred flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Lobelia goodii has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
