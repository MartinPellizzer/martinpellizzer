Loranthus lepidaria

Loranthus lepidaria, commonly known as the mistletoe of the genus Loranthus, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using light absorbed by its leaves. The plant has small, scale-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or greenish in color. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in Southeast Asia.

Its life cycle involves the dispersal of its seeds by birds, which consume the fleshy, sticky aril surrounding the seeds.
