Licuala ramsayi var. tuckeri

Licuala ramsayi var. tuckeri, commonly known as the Tucker's palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in Malaysia and Indonesia. This plant is characterized by its large, pinnately compound leaves that can reach several meters in length. The inflorescences are typically borne at the base of the plant, with small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
