Linociera purpurea

Linociera purpurea, commonly known as the purple-flowered linociera, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking purple or violet flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a vibrant touch to its habitat. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with woody stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

Linociera purpurea is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
