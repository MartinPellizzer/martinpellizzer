Leucopogon interstans

Leucopogon interstans, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils in open woodlands or heathlands. It produces dark red or black berries that are an important food source for birds and small mammals.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
