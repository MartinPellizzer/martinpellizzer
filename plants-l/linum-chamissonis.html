Linum chamissonis

Linum chamissonis, commonly known as coastal flax, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of North America.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along the shoreline. The plant features slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its showy blue to purple flowers bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Linum chamissonis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in harsh coastal environments.
