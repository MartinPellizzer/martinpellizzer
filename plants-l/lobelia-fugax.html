Lobelia fugax

Lobelia fugax, commonly known as the transient lily, is a herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late summer. The plant grows in moist, wooded areas and is often found in shaded environments. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Lobelia fugax is a short-lived perennial, often appearing as a seasonal plant due to its ephemeral nature.
