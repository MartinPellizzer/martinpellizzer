Lessingianthus pycnostachyus

Lessingianthus pycnostachyus, commonly known as the dense-stemmed sunflower, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, hairy stems and clustered, bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats and has small, oval-shaped leaves with a rough texture. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.

The species is also noted for its ecological role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
