Leea angulata

Leea angulata, commonly known as the angular leea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Southeast Asia, and the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Leeaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often angular in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on short pedicels. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental potential and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
