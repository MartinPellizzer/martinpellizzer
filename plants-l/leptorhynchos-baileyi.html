Leptorhynchos baileyi

Leptorhynchos baileyi, commonly known as the Baileys' rock heath, is a small, evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of South Africa.

It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple, which bloom in spring. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, adapted to conserve water in its arid environment.

Leptorhynchos baileyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and as a container plant.
