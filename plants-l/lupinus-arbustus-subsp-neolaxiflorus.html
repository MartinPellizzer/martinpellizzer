Lupinus arbustus subsp. neolaxiflorus

Lupinus arbustus subsp.

neolaxiflorus, commonly known as the narrow-flowered bush lupine, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of narrow, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to violet.

The plant has compound leaves composed of several small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rocky or gravelly habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
