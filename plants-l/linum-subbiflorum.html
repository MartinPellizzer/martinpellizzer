Linum subbiflorum

Linum subbiflorum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and showy blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Linum subbiflorum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in fiber production.
