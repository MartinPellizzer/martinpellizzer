Lafoensia densiflora var. cucullata

Lafoensia densiflora var. cucullata, commonly known as the hooded lafoensia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, fluffy inflorescences that resemble a cluster of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly velvety texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
