Luma parvifolia

Luma parvifolia, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a delicate and ornamental appearance. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and landscapes.

Luma parvifolia is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
