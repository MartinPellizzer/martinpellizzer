Liparis anemophila

Liparis anemophila, commonly known as the wind-pollinated orchid, is a species of orchid native to certain regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, often greenish or pale yellow flowers that are adapted for wind pollination. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or mountainous areas. Its leaves are usually grass-like, and the plant has a rhizomatous growth habit.

Liparis anemophila plays a role in its ecosystem by providing nectar for certain insect species and contributing to forest biodiversity.
