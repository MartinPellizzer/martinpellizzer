Languas penduliflora

Languas penduliflora, commonly known as the hanging bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branching stems that produce clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue and purple. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its delicate appearance and long blooming period add ornamental value to garden landscapes.
