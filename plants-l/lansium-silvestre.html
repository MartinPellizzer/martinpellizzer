Lansium silvestre

Lansium silvestre, commonly known as the snake fruit or kopi luwak, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its elongated, pendulous fruits that resemble small, yellowish-green bananas. The tree produces small, white flowers and has glossy, dark green leaves. The fruit is notable for its unique flavor, combining sweet and sour notes, and is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
