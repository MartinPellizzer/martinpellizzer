Lepidium virginicum var. macropetalum

Lepidium virginicum var. macropetalum, commonly known as the large-petaled peppergrass, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many economically important crops and weeds. The plant is characterized by its small, white to pinkish flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are typically alternate and pinnately divided. It thrives in disturbed soils and can often be found in fields, roadsides, and along streambanks.

Due to its adaptability, it is considered an invasive species in some regions.
