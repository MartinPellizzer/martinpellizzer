Lobelia adscendens

Lobelia adscendens, commonly known as the tall lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically reaches heights of 2 to 4 feet and has opposite, lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Lobelia adscendens is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
