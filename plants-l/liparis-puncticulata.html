Liparis puncticulata

Liparis puncticulata, commonly known as the punctuated orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers with distinctive black spots. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its unique floral structure.

Liparis puncticulata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
