Laugeria tomentosa

Laugeria tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly laugeria, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its hairy, or tomentose, leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Laugeria tomentosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
