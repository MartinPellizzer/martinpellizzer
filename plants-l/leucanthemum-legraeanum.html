Leucanthemum legraeanum

Leucanthemum legraeanum, commonly known as the everlasting daisy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to produce numerous small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant features silver-gray foliage that is covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is often cultivated as a garden plant due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

Leucanthemum legraeanum is also known for its long-lasting flowers, which remain intact even after drying, hence its common name.
