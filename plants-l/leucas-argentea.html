Leucas argentea

Leucas argentea, commonly known as silver leucas, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including India and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
