Laburnum anagyroides f. chrysophyllum

Laburnum anagyroides f. chrysophyllum, commonly known as the golden laburnum, is a hybrid form of the golden chain tree.

It is characterized by its long, drooping clusters of bright yellow flowers that appear in early spring. The plant is native to Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It has feathery, bipinnate leaves that add a delicate texture to its appearance.

The golden laburnum is a deciduous shrub that can grow up to 6 meters tall, making it a striking addition to gardens and landscapes.
