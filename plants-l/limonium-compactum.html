Limonium compactum

Limonium compactum, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and profuse display of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in saline and well-drained soils, often found in dunes and coastal meadows. Its flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Limonium compactum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand coastal conditions.
