Lycium salinicola

Lycium salinicola, commonly known as the saltbush lycium, is a shrub native to arid and saline environments.

It is characterized by its small, fleshy berries and woody stems adapted to withstand harsh conditions. The plant thrives in coastal and inland salt marshes, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion. Its leaves are typically small and covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

Lycium salinicola is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
