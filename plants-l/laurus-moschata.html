Laurus moschata

Laurus moschata, commonly known as the sweet bay tree, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant is aromatic, with a strong, spicy scent reminiscent of bay leaves. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are followed by dark purple berries.

Laurus moschata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the aromatic leaves used in cooking and traditional medicine.
