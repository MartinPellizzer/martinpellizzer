Lonicera implexa f. valentina

Lonicera implexa f. valentina, commonly known as the Valentina honeysuckle, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to China.

It features fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in shades of yellow and orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are covered in fine, pubescent hairs. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

The fruit is a small, red berry that ripens in late summer and is edible for both humans and wildlife.
