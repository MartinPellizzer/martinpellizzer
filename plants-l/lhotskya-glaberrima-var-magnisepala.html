Lhotskya glaberrima var. magnisepala

Lhotskya glaberrima var. magnisepala, commonly known as the giant Himalayan bellflower, is a striking alpine plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its large, showy blue or violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant thrives in cold, moist environments and is adapted to the harsh conditions of mountainous regions. Its leaves are typically broad and hairy, helping to retain moisture and withstand strong winds.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
