Lobivia crassicaulis

Lobivia crassicaulis, commonly known as the thick-stemmed lobivia, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It features thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the spring. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, which help minimize water loss.

Lobivia crassicaulis is well adapted to rocky and sandy soils, making it a hardy choice for xerophytic gardens.
