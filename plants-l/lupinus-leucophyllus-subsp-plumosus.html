Lupinus leucophyllus subsp. plumosus

Lupinus leucophyllus subsp.

plumosus, commonly known as the silverleaf lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and tall, upright growth habit.

The plant produces spires of blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Its ecological importance includes providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain butterfly species.
