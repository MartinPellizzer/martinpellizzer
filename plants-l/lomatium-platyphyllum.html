Lomatium platyphyllum

Lomatium platyphyllum, commonly known as broadleaf lomatium, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its fern-like leaves and umbel-shaped flowers.

It is native to the Pacific Northwest of North America, particularly in regions of Oregon and Washington. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open woodlands or meadows. Its leaves are broad and deeply lobed, while its flowers are small and white or pale green.

It is often used in herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
