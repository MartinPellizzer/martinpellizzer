Lonicera syringantha var. wolfii

Lonicera syringantha var. wolfii, commonly known as the wolfii honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are usually yellow or orange in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its berries are red or orange and contain small seeds, which are dispersed by birds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
