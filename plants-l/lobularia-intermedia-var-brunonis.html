Lobularia intermedia var. brunonis

Lobularia intermedia var. brunonis, commonly known as the purple sandwort, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to the western United States.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, depending on the variety. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a valuable addition to native plant landscapes.
