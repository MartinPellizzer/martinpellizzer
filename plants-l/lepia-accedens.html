Lepia accedens

Lepia accedens, commonly known as the slender lepia, is a species of flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Araceae, which includes many aquatic and semi-aquatic plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are often submerged in water. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in a spadix, typical of the Araceae family.

Lepia accedens is often cultivated in aquariums due to its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.
