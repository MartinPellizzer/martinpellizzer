Lycopodioides hartwegiana

Lycopodioides hartwegiana, commonly known as the Hartweg's clubmoss, is a species of lycophyte belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is a small, herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant features erect, spore-bearing cones called strobili, which are characteristic of the lycophyte group. Its leaves are spirally arranged and often have a linear or needle-like shape.

Lycopodioides hartwegiana plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
