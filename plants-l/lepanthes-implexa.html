Lepanthes implexa

Lepanthes implexa, commonly known as the tangled slipper orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its intricate, often fragrant flowers that resemble small slippers, with a distinctive coloration ranging from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as cloud forests, where it attaches itself to trees or rocks. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the air, and it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.

Lepanthes implexa is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is appreciated for its ornamental value in horticulture.
