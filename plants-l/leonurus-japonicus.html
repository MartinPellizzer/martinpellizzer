Leonurus japonicus

Leonurus japonicus, commonly known as Japanese hedgehog, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Leonurus japonicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
