Lunaria inodora

Lunaria inodora, commonly known as the moonflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its seed pods are distinctive, resembling tiny, round moons, which give the plant its common name.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits in gardens.
