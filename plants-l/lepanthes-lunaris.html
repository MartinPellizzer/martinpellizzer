Lepanthes lunaris

Lepanthes lunaris, commonly known as the lunar orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking white or pale yellow flowers that resemble the moon, hence its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical forests. Its flowers are often fragrant and bloom during the night, attracting nocturnal pollinators.

Lepanthes lunaris is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
