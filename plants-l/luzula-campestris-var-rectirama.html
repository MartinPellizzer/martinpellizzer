Luzula campestris var. rectirama

Luzula campestris var. rectirama, commonly known as the upright tussock grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, commonly known as the grass family, and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The plant forms dense tufts or tussocks, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green in color. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, which produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is often found in moist, nutrient-rich soils in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.
