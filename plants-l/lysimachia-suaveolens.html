Lysimachia suaveolens

Lysimachia suaveolens, commonly known as sweet yellow loosestrife, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, woodland areas and along stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. It is known for its fragrant blooms and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Lysimachia suaveolens is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in damp conditions.
