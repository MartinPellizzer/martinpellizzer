Lepidium hirtum var. stylatiforme

Lepidium hirtum var. stylatiforme, commonly known as the hairy cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, white or yellow flowers and its hairy stems and leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments and is often found in disturbed soils. It has a compact growth habit and produces small, rounded seed pods.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
