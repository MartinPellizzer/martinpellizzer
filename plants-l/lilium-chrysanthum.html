Lilium chrysanthum

Lilium chrysanthum, commonly known as the Easter lily, is a species of bulbous plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its tall, upright stems and showy, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces large, fragrant blossoms with six petals that often curl at the edges. Its bulbs are perennial, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

The Easter lily is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes and is a symbol of purity and renewal.
