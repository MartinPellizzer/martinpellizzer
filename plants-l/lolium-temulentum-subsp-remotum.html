Lolium temulentum subsp. remotum

Lolium temulentum subsp.

remotum, commonly known as the slender ryegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to yellowish flowers. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and agricultural fields.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a common weed in temperate regions.
