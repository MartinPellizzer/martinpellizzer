Leea pauciflora var. ferruginea

Leea pauciflora var. ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty leea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Leeaceae and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and distinctive rust-colored stems. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and usually have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.
