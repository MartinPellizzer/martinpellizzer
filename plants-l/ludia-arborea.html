Ludia arborea

Ludia arborea, commonly known as the tree hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, woody stem and broad, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to thrive in full sun and well-drained soil.

Ludia arborea is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
