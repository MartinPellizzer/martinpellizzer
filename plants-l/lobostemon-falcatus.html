Lobostemon falcatus

Lobostemon falcatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lobostemon, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in grassland or shrubland environments. It is a perennial herb that produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Lobostemon falcatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive foliage and blooms.
