Leontodon pyrenaicus subsp. cantabricus

Leontodon pyrenaicus subsp.

cantabricus, commonly known as the Cantabrian bell heather, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, yellowish flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in mountainous regions. It is adapted to harsh, high-altitude environments and is known for its resilience to cold and poor soil conditions.

This subspecies is of particular interest in botanical studies due to its limited distribution and ecological significance.
