Lycoctonum sibiricum

Lycoctonum sibiricum, commonly known as Siberian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the cold regions of Siberia and Central Asia.

It features tall, slender stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of blue or purple. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in alpine or rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens in cooler climates.
