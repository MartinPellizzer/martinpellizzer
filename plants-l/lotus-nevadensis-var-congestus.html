Lotus nevadensis var. congestus

Lotus nevadensis var. congestus, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada lotus, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking pink to purple flowers and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant thrives in moist, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a taproot system that allows it to survive in arid conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and conservation efforts.
