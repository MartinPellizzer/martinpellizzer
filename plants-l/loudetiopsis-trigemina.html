Loudetiopsis trigemina

Loudetiopsis trigemina, commonly known as the three-lobed moss, is a small, perennial moss belonging to the family Pottiaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth form and dark green, wiry stems. The leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a distinct midrib and three prominent lobes at the apex. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rock crevices.

This species is notable for its ability to tolerate low light and high humidity, making it a common component of temperate woodland ecosystems.
