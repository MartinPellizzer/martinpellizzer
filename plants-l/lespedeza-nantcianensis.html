Lespedeza nantcianensis

Lespedeza nantcianensis, commonly known as the Appalachian lespeadeza, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, clustered pink to reddish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its trifoliate leaves are alternate and have a serrated edge, while its root system is fibrous and often forms dense colonies.

Lespedeza nantcianensis is typically found in moist, acidic soils in woodlands and along streambanks.
