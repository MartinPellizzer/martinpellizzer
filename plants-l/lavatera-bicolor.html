Lavatera bicolor

Lavatera bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored mallows, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It features showy, butterfly-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, often with a contrasting central stripe. The plant has soft, fuzzy leaves that are typically gray-green in appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Lavatera bicolor is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
