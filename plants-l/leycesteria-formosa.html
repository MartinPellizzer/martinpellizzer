Leycesteria formosa

Leycesteria formosa, commonly known as the sweetshrub, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features fragrant, white, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a smooth, grayish-bark trunk. It thrives in partial to full sun and moist, well-drained soil.

Leycesteria formosa is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and pleasant aroma.
