Loxocarpus repens

Loxocarpus repens, commonly known as the creeping thimbleweed, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and small, daisy-like flowers. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowering stems produce numerous yellow flowers in the summer months. Loxocarpus repens typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
