Lobivia binghamiana

Lobivia binghamiana, commonly known as the Bingham lobivia, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains in South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and spiny stems. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is often cultivated by cactus enthusiasts.
