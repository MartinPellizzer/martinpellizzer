Lasiopogon glomerulatus

Lasiopogon glomerulatus, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture.

Lasiopogon glomerulatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
