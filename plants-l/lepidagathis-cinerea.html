Lepidagathis cinerea

Lepidagathis cinerea, commonly known as the silverleaf pimelea, is a small evergreen shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.

Lepidagathis cinerea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
