Lasiopetalum glutinosum

Lasiopetalum glutinosum, commonly known as sticky leaf honeysuckle, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape, with a sticky surface that gives the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and woodland habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
