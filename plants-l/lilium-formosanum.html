Lilium formosanum

Lilium formosanum, commonly known as the Formosan lily, is a species native to Taiwan.

It is a bulbous perennial plant belonging to the Liliaceae family, characterized by its tall, straight stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The flowers are typically pink to red in color, with six petals and a prominent central stamen. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in mountainous regions.

This plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
