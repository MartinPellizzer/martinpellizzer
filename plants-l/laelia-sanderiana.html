Laelia sanderiana

Laelia sanderiana, commonly known as the Sander's orchid, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces a single or few flower spikes with showy, violet to purple blooms that bloom in late winter to early spring. It has linear, grass-like leaves and requires well-drained soil and bright, indirect light to thrive.

Laelia sanderiana is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
