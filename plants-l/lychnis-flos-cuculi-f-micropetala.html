Lychnis flos-cuculi f. micropetala

Lychnis flos-cuculi f. micropetala, commonly known as the dwarf nightshade, is a botanical variant of the common white campion.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture. This form is distinguished by its reduced petal size compared to the typical species.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.
