Lycianthes lenta var. endopsila

Lycianthes lenta var. endopsila, commonly known as the purple eggplant, is a species native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshade plants. The plant produces distinctive purple or violet fruits that are typically egg-shaped and covered in a waxy coating. Its leaves are ovate with a slightly hairy surface, and it thrives in warm, tropical climates.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local cuisines.
