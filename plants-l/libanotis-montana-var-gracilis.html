Libanotis montana var. gracilis

Libanotis montana var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender mountain bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are lanceolate, with a slightly hairy surface, and the plant typically grows in well-drained, rocky soils. It is often found in alpine meadows and open woodlands at higher elevations.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
