Lagenaria vulgaris var. clavata

Lagenaria vulgaris var. clavata, commonly known as the bottle gourd, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its elongated, cylindrical fruit that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant is native to Africa and is widely cultivated for its edible young fruits. The leaves are large, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

The fruit develops from a single flower and matures to a pale green or yellow color, depending on the variety.
