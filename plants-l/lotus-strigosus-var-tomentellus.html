Lotus strigosus var. tomentellus

Lotus strigosus var. tomentellus, commonly known as the woolly lotus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a basal rosette of broad, hairy leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white to grayish woolly hairs. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, typically on long, slender stems. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in dry, well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
