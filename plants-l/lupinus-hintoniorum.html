Lupinus hintoniorum

Lupinus hintoniorum, commonly known as Hinton's lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of pea-like flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant has compound leaves composed of several small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Lupinus hintoniorum is a member of the legume family and has the ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it ecologically significant.
