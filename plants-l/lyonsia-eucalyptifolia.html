Lyonsia eucalyptifolia

Lyonsia eucalyptifolia, commonly known as the eucalyptus fern, is a unique plant native to the humid forests of South America.

It is characterized by its feathery, fern-like foliage that resembles the structure of eucalyptus leaves. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to the trunks of trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually dark green and have a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in the moist, shaded environments it prefers.

Lyonsia eucalyptifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
