Laurus atra

Laurus atra, commonly known as black laurel, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to the eastern United States, particularly in the southeastern region, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its berries are dark blue to black and contain seeds, which can be dispersed by birds.

Laurus atra is often found in woodlands and along stream banks, contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitat.
