Lycianthes hortulana

Lycianthes hortulana, commonly known as Mexican nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, peppers, and potatoes. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into round, orange fruits resembling tiny tomatoes. Its leaves are ovate with a slightly hairy texture, and it often grows in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

The species is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and has mild edible qualities in its young fruits.
