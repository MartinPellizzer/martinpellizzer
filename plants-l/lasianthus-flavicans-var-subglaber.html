Lasianthus flavicans var. subglaber

Lasianthus flavicans var. subglaber, commonly known as the slender yellow lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
