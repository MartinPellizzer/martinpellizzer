Laelia glauca

Laelia glauca, commonly known as the blue orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet petals and sepals, which are often adorned with white or yellow markings. The plant typically grows at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions. Laelia glauca has a pseudobulb structure that stores water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in its native environment.

It is a popular choice for orchid enthusiasts due to its beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
