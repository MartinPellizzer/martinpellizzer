Lepistemon chevalieri

Lepistemon chevalieri, commonly known as the mountain lepistemon, is a perennial shrub native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped, hairy leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its unique habitat and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
