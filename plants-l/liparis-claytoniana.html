Liparis claytoniana

Liparis claytoniana, commonly known as the Clayton's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky outcrops. The plant features a short, rhizomatous stem with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered, often with a distinctive greenish or reddish hue.

Liparis claytoniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with appropriate humidity and light conditions.
