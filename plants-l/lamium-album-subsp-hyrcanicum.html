Lamium album subsp. hyrcanicum

Lamium album subsp.

hyrcanicum, commonly known as white dead-nettle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Central Asia. It features opposite, ovate leaves with a hairy surface and produces small, white, bilaterally symmetrical flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It is characterized by its stolons, which allow it to spread vegetatively.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
