Litsea subcoriacea

Litsea subcoriacea, commonly known as the rough-leaved litsea, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Vietnam, and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are rough to the touch due to their dense pubescence. It produces fragrant white flowers followed by small, dark blue berries.

This species is often grown as an ornamental plant for its aromatic foliage and showy blooms.
