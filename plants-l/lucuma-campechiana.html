Lucuma campechiana

Lucuma campechiana, commonly known as the golden fruit or lucuma, is a species of tree native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its large, spiky fruit that contains edible, sweet, and creamy seeds. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, temperate climates and well-drained soil. Its fruit is rich in nutrients, including vitamins, minerals, and dietary fiber, making it a valuable crop for both local consumption and export.

The tree can live for several decades, producing fruit for many years once mature.
