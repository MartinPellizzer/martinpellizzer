Lupinus excubitus var. austromontanus

Lupinus excubitus var. austromontanus, commonly known as the Australian lupine, is a perennial herb native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its erect, spindly growth habit and clusters of pea-like flowers that range in color from pale blue to violet. The plant has narrow, thread-like leaves and produces legume pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along coastal areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
