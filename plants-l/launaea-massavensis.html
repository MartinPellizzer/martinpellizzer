Launaea massavensis

Launaea massavensis, commonly known as the Sahel dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the Sahel region of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant has a succulent, cylindrical stem that is often covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. It produces small yellow flowers that develop into fluffy, white seeds, facilitating wind dispersal.

Launaea massavensis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in challenging climates.
