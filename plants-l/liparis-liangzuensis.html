Liparis liangzuensis

Liparis liangzuensis, commonly known as the Liangzuo orchid, is a rare and endemic orchid species native to China.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in mountainous regions with rich soil. Its flowering period occurs during the spring, and it is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological significance.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
