Loranthus wildemanii

Loranthus wildemanii, commonly known as the wildeman's mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees.

It belongs to the Loranthaceae family and is characterized by its lack of chlorophyll, relying on its host for nutrients. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers and red berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It is typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing on a variety of tree species.

Its parasitic nature and unique reproductive strategy make it an interesting subject in botanical studies.
