Loranthus kraussianus var. transvaalensis

Loranthus kraussianus var. transvaalensis, commonly known as the Transvaal mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of host trees, primarily in southern Africa.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using the light it captures. The plant has small, elliptical leaves and is often found in forested areas where its host species thrive. It plays a role in the ecosystem by providing food for birds and insects, and its seeds are dispersed by birds that eat its berries.

This variant is notable for its adaptability and ecological significance in its native habitat.
