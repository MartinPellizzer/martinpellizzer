Lobelia seguinii f. longisepala

Lobelia seguinii f. longisepala, commonly known as the long-sepaled lobelia, is a rare and unique variant of the lobelia genus native to specific regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its elongated, showy sepals that give it its distinguishing name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories or rocky crevices. Its flowers are usually blue or purple, with five petals and prominent, elongated sepals that contribute to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
