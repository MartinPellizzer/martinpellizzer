Lonicera affinis var. pseudohypoglauca

Lonicera affinis var. pseudohypoglauca, commonly known as the white honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features slender, arching stems that often grow along the ground or climb low structures. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are fragrant, white to pale yellow, and appear in clusters, blooming in early spring.

The fruit is a small, red berry that ripens in late summer and is attractive to birds.
