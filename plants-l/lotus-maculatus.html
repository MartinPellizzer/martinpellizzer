Lotus maculatus

Lotus maculatus, commonly known as the spotted water lily, is a aquatic plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, floating leaves that are often marked with distinctive red or purple spots. The plant produces showy white or pink flowers that bloom in the early morning and close by midday. Its roots anchor it in muddy substrates, while its rhizomes help it spread across water bodies.

Lotus maculatus plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various water organisms.
