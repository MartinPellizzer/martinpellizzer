Lacinaria scariosa var. opima

Lacinaria scariosa var. opima, commonly known as the opima lace fern, is a variant of the lace fern species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are finely divided and covered in small, scale-like structures. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth or along stream banks. Its leaves are usually bright green and can reach lengths of up to 1 meter.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
