Lipochaeta molokiniensis

Lipochaeta molokiniensis, commonly known as the Molokini ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal and rocky habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its small, yellowish flowers bloom in clusters, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Lipochaeta molokiniensis plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for native pollinators.
