Linnaea borealis f. poecilosema

Linnaea borealis f. poecilosema, commonly known as the variable twinflower, is a rare and distinctive variant of the twinflower plant.

It is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the boreal and subarctic regions of North America and Eurasia. The plant features small, white, bilaterally symmetrical flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are typically oval and have a leathery texture, adapted to the cold climate.

This form is characterized by its variable leaf morphology, which contributes to its scientific name.
