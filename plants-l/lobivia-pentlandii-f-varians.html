Lobivia pentlandii f. varians

Lobivia pentlandii f. varians, commonly known as the Variegated Lobivia, is a compact, low-growing succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It features thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, white, and pink, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the warmer months. It is well adapted to dry conditions, storing water in its leaves and stems.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
