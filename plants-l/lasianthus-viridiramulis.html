Lasianthus viridiramulis

Lasianthus viridiramulis, commonly known as the green-veined lasianthus, is a species of flowering plant in the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, ovate leaves with prominent green veins. Its small, tubular flowers are typically pale green to white, arranged in spikes at the ends of the branches.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.
