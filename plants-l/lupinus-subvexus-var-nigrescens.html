Lupinus subvexus var. nigrescens

Lupinus subvexus var. nigrescens, commonly known as the black-flowered lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of dark purple to black flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has palmately compound leaves with oval to lance-shaped leaflets, and its root system includes nitrogen-fixing nodules. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
