Leichhardtia lorea

Leichhardtia lorea, commonly known as the golden bell heath, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are small, leathery, and arranged in whorls around the stems.

Leichhardtia lorea thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal regions.
