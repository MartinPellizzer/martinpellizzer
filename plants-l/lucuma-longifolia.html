Lucuma longifolia

Lucuma longifolia, commonly known as the golden fruit tree, is a tropical plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by the development of its distinctive, elongated, golden-orange fruits. The plant belongs to the family Lauraceae and is valued for its ornamental and edible qualities. Its fruit, often referred to as "the golden apple," is rich in nutrients and has a sweet, custard-like flavor.

Lucuma longifolia thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for both its aesthetic appeal and culinary uses.
