Lupinus tauris

Lupinus tauris, commonly known as the Taurian lupin, is a species of flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its erect stem and compound leaves with a feathery appearance. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in raceme inflorescences, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Lupinus tauris is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential in ecological restoration projects.
