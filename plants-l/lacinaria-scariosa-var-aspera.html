Lacinaria scariosa var. aspera

Lacinaria scariosa var. aspera, commonly known as the rough lacinaria, is a herbaceous plant characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa. The leaves are typically lance-shaped with prominent veins and a silver-gray appearance due to the dense pubescence. It produces small, yellowish composite flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators.

This plant is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
