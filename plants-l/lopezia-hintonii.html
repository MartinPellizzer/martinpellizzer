Lopezia hintonii

Lopezia hintonii, commonly known as Hinton's lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. Lopezia hintonii is adapted to grow in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments.

It plays an important role in its ecosystem by fixing nitrogen in the soil through its root nodules.
