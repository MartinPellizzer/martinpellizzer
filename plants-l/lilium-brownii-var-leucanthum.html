Lilium brownii var. leucanthum

Lilium brownii var. leucanthum, commonly known as the white lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its tall, straight stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces large, showy white or pale pink flowers with six petals, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, arranged in a basal rosette.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegance and fragrance.
