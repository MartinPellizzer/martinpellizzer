Loranthus esquirolii

Loranthus esquirolii, commonly known as the Esquiroli's mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of various host trees, primarily in the tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Loranthaceae family and is characterized by its leafless stems and clusters of small, white to yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that contain seeds covered in a sticky substance, facilitating attachment to the bark of its host. Its parasitic nature allows it to derive water and nutrients from its host, while still performing photosynthesis to some extent.

This species plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat, influencing the growth and health of its host trees.
