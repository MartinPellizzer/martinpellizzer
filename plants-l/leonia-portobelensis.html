Leonia portobelensis

Leonia portobelensis, commonly known as the Portobelense Flameleaf, is a unique flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, broad leaves that display a vibrant red and orange coloration during the flowering season. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, which are typically white or pale pink in color. Leonia portobelensis thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
