Linum usitatissimum var. angustifolium

Linum usitatissimum var. angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved flax, is a variant of the common flax plant.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and slender stems. This variety typically produces blue or white flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its fibers and seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in textile production.
