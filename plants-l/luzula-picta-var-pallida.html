Luzula picta var. pallida

Luzula picta var. pallida, commonly known as the pale sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands, where it thrives in well-drained to slightly wet soils. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with a pale green or bluish hue, giving it its characteristic appearance. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, three-lobed flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Luzula picta var. pallida is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.
