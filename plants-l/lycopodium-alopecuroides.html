Lycopodium alopecuroides

Lycopodium alopecuroides, commonly known as the Indian clubmoss, is a small evergreen shrub native to the eastern Himalayas.

It belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae and is characterized by its spore-bearing cones and needle-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species plays a role in maintaining soil stability in its native habitat.
