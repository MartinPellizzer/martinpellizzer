Lycopodioides armata

Lycopodioides armata, commonly known as the armed clubmoss, is a member of the Lycopodiaceae family and is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and spore-producing structures.

It is a terrestrial plant that thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant exhibits a sporophytic life cycle, with distinct stem and leaf structures that contribute to its unique morphology. Its spores are dispersed through wind, allowing it to colonize new areas effectively.

Lycopodioides armata plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for microorganisms and invertebrates.
