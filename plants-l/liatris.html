Liatris

Liatris, commonly known as blazing star, is a genus of flowering plants native to North America.

These plants are characterized by their tall, upright stems and showy, spikelike inflorescences composed of numerous small, tubular flowers. The flowers are typically pink, purple, or white, and they bloom in late summer to early autumn. Liatris species are often found in prairies, meadows, and open woods, thriving in well-drained soils.

They are popular in native plant gardens for their ornamental value and ecological benefits.
