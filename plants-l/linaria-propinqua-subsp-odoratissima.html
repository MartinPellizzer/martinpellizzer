Linaria propinqua subsp. odoratissima

Linaria propinqua subsp.

odoratissima, commonly known as the sweet willie or sweet scabious, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and clusters of small, tubular flowers.

This subspecies is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
