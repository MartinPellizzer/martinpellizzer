Liatris novae-angliae

Liatris novae-angliae, commonly known as New England blazing star, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, spikelike inflorescence composed of numerous purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies, woodlands, and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a bluish-gray hue, and its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn.

Liatris novae-angliae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
