Lecythis crassinoda

Lecythis crassinoda, commonly known as the broadleaf lecythis, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Lecythidaceae and is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and showy, orange-red flowers. The tree produces a heavy, oily fruit that contains a single large seed, which is often used in traditional medicine. Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in construction and furniture-making.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
