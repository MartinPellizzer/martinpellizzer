Lycopsis arvensis

Lycopsis arvensis, commonly known as the field lycopus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves that are typically lance-shaped. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually blue or purple in color, arranged in whorls along the stem. Lycopsis arvensis is often found in moist soils in meadows and along stream banks.

It is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
