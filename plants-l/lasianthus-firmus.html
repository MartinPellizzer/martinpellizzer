Lasianthus firmus

Lasianthus firmus, commonly known as the hardy lasianthus, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy inflorescences. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and its flowers are typically clustered in whorls along the stems. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.

Lasianthus firmus thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.
