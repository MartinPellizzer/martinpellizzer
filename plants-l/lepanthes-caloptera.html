Lepanthes caloptera

Lepanthes caloptera, commonly known as the golden tailed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow and green foliage and its showy, fragrant flowers with a distinctive golden hue. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters, often with a unique lip that is prominently displayed.

Lepanthes caloptera is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
