Leptoderris laurentii

Leptoderris laurentii, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a tropical plant native to parts of Africa.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, grey bark and large, palmate leaves. The plant produces showy, orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its propagation in suitable habitats.

Leptoderris laurentii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
