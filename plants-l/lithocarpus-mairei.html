Lithocarpus mairei

Lithocarpus mairei, commonly known as the Maire's stone oak, is a member of the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks, chestnuts, and beeches.

It is a medium to large evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in the mountainous regions of Vietnam, Laos, and Cambodia. The plant is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and produces hard, woody fruits that contain a single seed. Lithocarpus mairei is valued for its durable timber and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.

Its ecological importance lies in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species in its native environment.
