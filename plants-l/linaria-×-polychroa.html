Linaria × polychroa

Linaria × polychroa, commonly known as the hybrid toadflax, is a cross between Linaria vulgaris and Linaria daphnifolia.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. The plant features striking, colorful flowers that range from yellow to orange and red, often with intricate patterns. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs.

Linaria × polychroa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
