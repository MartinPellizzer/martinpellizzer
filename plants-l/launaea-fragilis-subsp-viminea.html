Launaea fragilis subsp. viminea

Launaea fragilis subsp.

viminea, commonly known as the slender launaea, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa. It is a small, annual herb with a sprawling growth habit, often forming dense mats on the ground.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to reduce water loss in dry environments.

The species is notable for its resilience to drought and its role in stabilizing sandy soils.
