Ludwigia grandiflora var. hexapetala

Ludwigia grandiflora var. hexapetala, commonly known as the six-petaled Ludwigia, is a striking aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or orange flowers, which are typically six-petaled, giving it its varietal name. The plant features elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often bright green and slightly glossy. It thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments and is often used in aquascaping for its ornamental value.

Its growth habit is rhizomatous, allowing it to spread easily in suitable conditions.
