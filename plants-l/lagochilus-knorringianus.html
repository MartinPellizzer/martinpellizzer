Lagochilus knorringianus

Lagochilus knorringianus, commonly known as Knorring's lips, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or blue in color. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
