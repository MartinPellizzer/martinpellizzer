Lycium macranthum

Lycium macranthum, commonly known as the large-flowered boxthorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Lycium macranthum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
