Licaria campechiana

Licaria campechiana, commonly known as the Mexican star-of-Bethlehem, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and Central America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its star-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves, and it produces small, round berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Licaria campechiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
