Lycopodium blepharodes

Lycopodium blepharodes, commonly known as the clubmoss, is a member of the Lycopodiaceae family and is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and spore-bearing structures called strobila.

It is a vascular plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. The plant has a sporophyte-dominated life cycle, with the gametophyte stage being short-lived and dependent on the sporophyte. Lycopodium blepharodes produces spores that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its reproductive strategy in natural habitats.

Its structure and growth patterns make it an important component of certain ecosystems.
