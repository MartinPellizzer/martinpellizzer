Loranthus dregei f. obtusifolius

Loranthus dregei f. obtusifolius, commonly known as the smooth-leaved mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant belonging to the Santalaceae family.

It typically grows on the branches of host trees, such as acacia and eucalyptus, in arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa. The plant has small, ovate, and leathery leaves that are usually smooth and rounded at the edges. Its flowers are yellowish-green and are often clustered in dense inflorescences, while its fruits are small, fleshy berries that contain seeds covered in a sticky substance.

This species plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for birds and other animals, which help in seed dispersal.
