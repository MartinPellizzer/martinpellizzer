Litsea waitziana

Litsea waitziana, commonly known as Chinese tallowtree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange to red berries that contain several seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been introduced to various parts of the world, though it can become invasive in some regions.
