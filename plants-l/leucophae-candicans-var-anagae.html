Leucophae candicans var. anagae

Leucophae candicans var. anagae, commonly known as the Anaga whiteflower, is a rare and endemic plant species native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its striking white, showy flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils on sunny slopes, showcasing its adaptation to harsh, coastal environments. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, providing a drought-resistant structure.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species requiring conservation efforts.
