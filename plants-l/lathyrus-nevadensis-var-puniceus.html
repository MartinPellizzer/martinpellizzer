Lathyrus nevadensis var. puniceus

Lathyrus nevadensis var. puniceus, commonly known as the pink burr clover, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces oblong, hairy pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and native plant gardens.
