Lewisia pygmaea var. pygmaea

Lewisia pygmaea var. pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf lewisia, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features basal rosettes of fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a fine, white powdery coating. The plant produces striking clusters of pink to magenta flowers in early summer. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and full sun, making it well-suited for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its compact size and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
