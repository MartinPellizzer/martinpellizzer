Lachnagrostis × contracta

Lachnagrostis × contracta, commonly known as the narrowleaf grass, is a hybrid species within the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows or along streambanks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are small, cylindrical flower clusters.

Lachnagrostis × contracta is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
