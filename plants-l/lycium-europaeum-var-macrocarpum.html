Lycium europaeum var. macrocarpum

Lycium europaeum var. macrocarpum, commonly known as the large-fruited boxthorn, is a shrub native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and produces small, red berries that are edible but often tart. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to arid and semi-arid environments. Its berries are used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some regions.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to prevent soil erosion.
