Linum sibiricum

Linum sibiricum, commonly known as Siberian flax, is a flowering plant native to the colder regions of Asia, including Siberia and the Russian Far East.

It is a member of the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in meadows and rocky areas.

Linum sibiricum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
