Luzula spadicea var. subcongesta

Luzula spadicea var. subcongesta, commonly known as the dense green moor grass, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the temperate regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, dark green foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils, often found in heathlands and moorlands. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are closely arranged, giving it a compact appearance.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often used in horticultural settings for its aesthetic appeal.
