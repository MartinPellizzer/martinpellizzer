Laelia pumila var. major

Laelia pumila var. major, commonly known as the miniature laelia, is a compact orchid species native to the high Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its small, clustered growth habit and vibrant, often fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white, yellow, and orange. The plant prefers cool, high-altitude environments with bright, indirect light and well-draining soil. It is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.

Its unique appearance and resilience contribute to its appeal in both horticultural and botanical studies.
