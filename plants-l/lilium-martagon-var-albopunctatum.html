Lilium martagon var. albopunctatum

Lilium martagon var. albopunctatum, commonly known as the white-spotted Turk's cap lily, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale pink flowers with distinctive white spots. The foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a loose spiral. This variety is particularly noted for its ornamental value in gardens due to its unique appearance and long blooming period.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for landscaping.
