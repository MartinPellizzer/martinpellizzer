Lobivia rossii var. boedekeriana

Lobivia rossii var. boedekeriana, commonly known as the Boedeker's lobivia, is a compact, low-growing cactus native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features a rounded stem with a smooth, gray-green surface that develops a powdery coating over time. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking contrast to its otherwise muted foliage. It is well adapted to dry conditions, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Due to its slow growth and unique appearance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
