Linaria aequitriloba var. parviflora

Linaria aequitriloba var. parviflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is a perennial herb that produces seeds in elongated pods, which are dispersed by wind.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
