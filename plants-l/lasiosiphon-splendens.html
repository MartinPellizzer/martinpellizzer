Lasiosiphon splendens

Lasiosiphon splendens, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that resemble bells, which bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has heart-shaped, opposite leaves that are often hairy, giving it a textured appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Lasiosiphon splendens is a climbing plant that can reach several meters in length, making it suitable for trellises or vertical gardens.
