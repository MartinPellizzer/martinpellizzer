Litsea dolichocarpa

Litsea dolichocarpa, commonly known as the long-fruited litsea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, elliptical leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. Its fruit is a drupe, typically containing a single seed, and it is often used in traditional medicine.

Litsea dolichocarpa thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and partial shade.
