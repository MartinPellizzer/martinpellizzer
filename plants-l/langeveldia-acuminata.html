Langeveldia acuminata

Langeveldia acuminata, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and showy, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are usually elliptical or ovate, with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy appearance.

Langeveldia acuminata is well adapted to humid environments and thrives in well-drained soil.
