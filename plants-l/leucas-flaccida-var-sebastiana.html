Leucas flaccida var. sebastiana

Leucas flaccida var. sebastiana, commonly known as the pink leucas, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or dry landscapes.
