Lonicera korolkowii var. longibracteolata

Lonicera korolkowii var. longibracteolata, commonly known as the long-bract honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy on the underside. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers in clusters, typically yellow or orange, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that ripens in late summer, attracting birds and other wildlife.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
