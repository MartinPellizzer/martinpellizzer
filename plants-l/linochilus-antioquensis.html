Linochilus antioquensis

Linochilus antioquensis, commonly known as the Antioquian lily, is a herbaceous plant native to the cloud forests of Colombia.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant features strap-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is adapted to the high-altitude conditions of its native habitat.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
