Lobelia warszewiczii

Lobelia warszewiczii, commonly known as the Florida bog lobelia, is a perennial herb native to the wetlands of Florida.

It features showy, tubular blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in marshy areas, often in association with pine flatwoods and cypress swamps. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Lobelia warszewiczii plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is considered a valuable component of wetland ecosystems.
