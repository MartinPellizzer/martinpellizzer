Lepidium salsum

Lepidium salsum, commonly known as sea rocket, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and North America.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and pungent aroma. The plant typically grows in saline or sandy soils and is often found along shorelines and coastal dunes. Its leaves are pinnately divided and have a distinctive, peppery flavor.

Lepidium salsum is also known for its ability to thrive in harsh, maritime environments.
