Lophiocarpus cordifolius

Lophiocarpus cordifolius, commonly known as the heart-leaved lophiocarpus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically large and glossy. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in moist forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
