Lagerstroemia quinquevalvis

Lagerstroemia quinquevalvis, commonly known as the five-valved crape myrtle, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its showy, fluffy panicles of pink to red flowers that appear in summer. The plant has smooth, mottled bark that becomes flaky with age, and its leaves are elliptical with a serrated margin. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental shrub or small tree.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its natural propagation.
