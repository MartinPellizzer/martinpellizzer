Lupinus chamissonis var. longifolius

Lupinus chamissonis var. longifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved lupine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and palmately compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces tall spikes of pea-like flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and inland grasslands.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in nitrogen fixation, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
