Lathyrus subrotundus

Lathyrus subrotundus, commonly known as the round-leaved pea, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems and trifoliate leaves with rounded leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, followed by cylindrical pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Lathyrus subrotundus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
