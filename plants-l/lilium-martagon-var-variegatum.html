Lilium martagon var. variegatum

Lilium martagon var. variegatum, commonly known as the variegated Turk's cap lily, is a striking perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, upward-facing, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red, with a distinctive, layered appearance. The foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings, adding to its ornamental appeal. This species thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.

Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, producing a showy display that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
