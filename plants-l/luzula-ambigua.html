Luzula ambigua

Luzula ambigua, commonly known as the slender wood rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

Luzula ambigua is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and is often found in temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.
