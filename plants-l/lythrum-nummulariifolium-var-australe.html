Lythrum nummulariifolium var. australe

Lythrum nummulariifolium var. australe, commonly known as the Australian lythrum, is a perennial herb native to Australia.

It features upright, branched stems that bear opposite, oval to rounded leaves with a hairy surface. The plant produces spikes of showy, pink to purple flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and ornamental gardening.
