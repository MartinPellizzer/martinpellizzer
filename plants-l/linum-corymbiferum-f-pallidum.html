Linum corymbiferum f. pallidum

Linum corymbiferum f. pallidum, commonly known as the pale flax or pale yellow flax, is a horticultural form of the common flax species.

It is characterized by its pale yellow to white flowers, which contrast with its dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 feet, producing clusters of flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its delicate, showy blooms.
