Lobelia rosea

Lobelia rosea, commonly known as the Indian tobacco, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. Lobelia rosea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine.

Its seeds are small and can be dispersed by wind, aiding in its natural propagation.
