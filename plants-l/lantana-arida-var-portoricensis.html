Lantana arida var. portoricensis

Lantana arida var. portoricensis, commonly known as the Puerto Rican lantana, is a shrub native to the Caribbean island of Puerto Rico.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, which attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun. It is adapted to coastal and dry inland habitats, showing resilience to salt spray and drought conditions.

This species is also notable for its role in local ecosystems and its potential use in ornamental gardening.
