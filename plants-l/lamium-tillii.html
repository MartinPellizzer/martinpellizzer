Lamium tillii

Lamium tillii, commonly known as the lesser dead-nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, heart-shaped leaves with a fuzzy texture and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Lamium tillii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
