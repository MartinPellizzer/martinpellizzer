Lonchocarpus morenoi

Lonchocarpus morenoi, commonly known as the Brazilian senna, is a leguminous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces distinctive pod-like fruits that contain seeds surrounded by a fleshy, orange aril. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for horticultural purposes.
