Ledebouria inquinata

Ledebouria inquinata, commonly known as the dirty ledebouria, is a species of bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen rosette of fleshy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers on slender stems during the summer months. It is adapted to arid and rocky environments, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and xeriscapes.
