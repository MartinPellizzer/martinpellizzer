Linaria heterophylla f. spectabilis

Linaria heterophylla f. spectabilis, commonly known as the showy toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, brightly colored flowers that range from yellow to orange and red, often with a distinctive spurred petal. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is usually narrow and grass-like, with varying leaf shapes depending on the environmental conditions.

This cultivar is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
