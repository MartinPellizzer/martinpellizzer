Lindera montanoides

Lindera montanoides, commonly known as mountain sweetshrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often reddish beneath, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring. Its fruit is a dark purple drupe that matures in the fall.

Lindera montanoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
