Lycianthes laevis subsp. subtruncata

Lycianthes laevis subsp.

subtruncata, commonly known as the rough lycianthes, is a flowering plant native to Central America. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves and showy yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its fruits are small, globose berries that turn red when mature, and it is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and gardens.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some historical use in traditional medicine.
