Lobelia gardneriana

Lobelia gardneriana, commonly known as the mountain lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, tubular blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils in woodland or mountain habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

Lobelia gardneriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
