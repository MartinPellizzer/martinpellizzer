Leymus condensatus

Leymus condensatus, commonly known as dense barleygrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, riverbanks, and disturbed sites.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental appeal, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
