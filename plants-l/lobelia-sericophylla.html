Lobelia sericophylla

Lobelia sericophylla, commonly known as the silky-leaf lobelia, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, silvery-gray leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.

Lobelia sericophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
