Linociera obtusifolia var. minoriflora

Linociera obtusifolia var. minoriflora, commonly known as the small-flower waxplant, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, blooming during the summer months. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal or lowland areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
