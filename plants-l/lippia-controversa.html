Lippia controversa

Lippia controversa, commonly known as wild lemon verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves that emit a strong lemon scent when crushed. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale yellow to white in color. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.

Lippia controversa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
