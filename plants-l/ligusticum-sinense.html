Ligusticum sinense

Ligusticum sinense, commonly known as Chinese lovage, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its aromatic, fern-like leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in umbel clusters, typical of the family. Its roots are often used in traditional Chinese medicine for their purported health benefits.

Ligusticum sinense is valued both for its culinary uses and its medicinal properties.
