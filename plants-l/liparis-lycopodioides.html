Liparis lycopodioides

Liparis lycopodioides, commonly known as the clubmoss, is a member of the Lycopodiaceae family and is characterized by its primitive, spore-bearing structure.

It is a small, evergreen plant with a rosette of narrow, linear leaves that form a dense, cushion-like appearance. The plant produces small, cone-like structures called strobila, which contain the spores for reproduction. Liparis lycopodioides thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in rocky or forested areas.

Its ancient lineage provides valuable insights into the evolution of early land plants.
