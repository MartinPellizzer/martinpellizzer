Loranthus pendulus var. taeniifolius

Loranthus pendulus var. taeniifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to host trees through its haustoria, drawing water and nutrients from the host.

It is characterized by its slender, drooping stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often glaucous in appearance. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by small, fleshy berries that are dispersed by birds. It is typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing on a variety of host trees.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the ecosystems where it is found.
