Lactuca canadensis f. steelei

Lactuca canadensis f. steelei, commonly known as the Steele's Canadian lettuce, is a variant of the common lettuce species native to North America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are typically pale green with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
