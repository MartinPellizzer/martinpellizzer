Lasianthus andamanicus var. ciliatus

Lasianthus andamanicus var. ciliatus, commonly known as the Andaman ciliates.

This variant is a flowering plant native to the Andaman Islands, characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves with ciliate margins. It produces clusters of tiny, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
