Lobelia amblyanthera

Lobelia amblyanthera, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant produces small, tubular blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Lobelia amblyanthera is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
