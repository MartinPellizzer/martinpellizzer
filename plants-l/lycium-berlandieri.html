Lycium berlandieri

Lycium berlandieri, commonly known as the Mexican wolfberry, is a species of shrub native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshade plants. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often covered in a fine layer of hairs.

The berries are edible and have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
