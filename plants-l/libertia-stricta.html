Libertia stricta

Libertia stricta, commonly known as the narrowleaf libertia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
