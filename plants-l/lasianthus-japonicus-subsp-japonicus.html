Lasianthus japonicus subsp. japonicus

Lasianthus japonicus subsp.

japonicus, commonly known as the Japanese flame flower, is a species of flowering plant native to Japan. It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers.

The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

The flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
