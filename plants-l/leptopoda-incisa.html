Leptopoda incisa

Leptopoda incisa, commonly known as the cut-leaved water plantain, is a perennial aquatic plant native to North America.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often deeply notched or cut, giving it its common name. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in dense spikes, which bloom in early summer.

Leptopoda incisa plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
