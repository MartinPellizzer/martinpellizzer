Leucaena cruziana

Leucaena cruziana, commonly known as the Cuban leucaena, is a shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable for soil enrichment. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds, and it is often used in agroforestry systems.

Its adaptability to various soil types and drought resistance make it a useful plant in ecological restoration projects.
