Loranthus hypoglaucus

Loranthus hypoglaucus, commonly known as the pale mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily in the genus Eucalyptus.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own carbohydrates through photosynthesis using the light it captures. The plant's small, white or pale green leaves are often clustered at the base of its flowers, which are typically greenish-yellow in color. Its seeds are dispersed by birds that eat the fleshy, red berries, facilitating its spread to new hosts.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, contributing to biodiversity and supporting various wildlife species.
