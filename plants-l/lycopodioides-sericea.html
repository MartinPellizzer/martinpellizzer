Lycopodioides sericea

Lycopodioides sericea, commonly known as the silky clubmoss, is a member of the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. The plant features slender, creeping stems that bear small, scale-like leaves. It produces spore-bearing structures called strobila, which are clustered at the tips of the stems.

Lycopodioides sericea plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
