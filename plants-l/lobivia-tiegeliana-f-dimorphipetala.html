Lobivia tiegeliana f. dimorphipetala

Lobivia tiegeliana f. dimorphipetala, commonly known as the Two-Petaled Lobivia, is a rare and striking succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and distinctive two-petaled flowers. The plant features thick, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in harsh, dry environments. Its flowers, which can be pink or purple, bloom in spring and are often short-lived but highly ornamental.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is a sought-after plant among succulent enthusiasts and conservationists.
