Leucanthemum crassifolium

Leucanthemum crassifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, waxy leaves and produces yellow-centered, white-rayed composite flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes. It is a perennial herb that thrives in full sun and well-drained soil.

Its flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity.
