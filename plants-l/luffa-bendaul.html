Luffa bendaul

Luffa bendaul, commonly known as the Philippine luffa or "sili," is a tropical vine belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces yellowish flowers that transition into elongated, fibrous fruits. The fruit's inner layer develops into a spongy, fibrous material used for scrubbing.

Luffa bendaul thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
