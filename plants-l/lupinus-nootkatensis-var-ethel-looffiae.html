Lupinus nootkatensis var. ethel-looffiae

Lupinus nootkatensis var. ethel-looffiae, commonly known as the Ethel Looff's lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking blue to violet pea-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features compound leaves with numerous small, lance-shaped leaflets, and its root system is adapted to thrive in well-drained, alkaline soils. It is often found in open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
