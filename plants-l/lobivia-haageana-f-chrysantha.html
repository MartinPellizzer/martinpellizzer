Lobivia haageana f. chrysantha

Lobivia haageana f. chrysantha, commonly known as the Golden Lobivia, is a rare and striking succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rounded growth habit and thick, fleshy stems. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, which contrast beautifully with its green or bluish-gray foliage. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is a prized specimen among succulent enthusiasts.
