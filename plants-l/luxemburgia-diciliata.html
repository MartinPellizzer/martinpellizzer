Luxemburgia diciliata

Luxemburgia diciliata, commonly known as the Luxemburgian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves arranged in a basal rosette, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It is often found in open woodlands and along forest edges.

Luxemburgia diciliata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
