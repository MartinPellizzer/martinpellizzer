Luzula nivea var. livida

Luzula nivea var. livida, commonly known as the silver woodrush, is a perennial plant belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of silver-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are arranged in spikelets, typical of grasses.

The variety *livida* is distinguished by its notably lighter coloration compared to the typical *nivea*.
