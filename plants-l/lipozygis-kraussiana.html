Lipozygis kraussiana

Lipozygis kraussiana, commonly known as the African sandthorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and narrow, woody leaves. The plant produces small, orange-colored fruits that are edible but have a bitter taste. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.

Due to its drought tolerance, it is sometimes used in landscaping in regions with limited water resources.
