Leiothrix glauca

Leiothrix glauca, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces clusters of small, blue-violet flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Leiothrix glauca is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
