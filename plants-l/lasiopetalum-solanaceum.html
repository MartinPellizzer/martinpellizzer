Lasiopetalum solanaceum

Lasiopetalum solanaceum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved devil's-bit, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is found in various regions of southern Australia. It has hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves, and its flowers are usually pale green or white.

Due to its restricted distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
