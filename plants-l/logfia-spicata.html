Logfia spicata

Logfia spicata, commonly known as the spiky lily, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a cool, temperate climate to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
