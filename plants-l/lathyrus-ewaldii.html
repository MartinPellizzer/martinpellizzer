Lathyrus ewaldii

Lathyrus ewaldii, commonly known as the Ewald's vetch, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Lathyrus ewaldii is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.

It also plays a role in nitrogen fixation, enriching the soil with nutrients.
