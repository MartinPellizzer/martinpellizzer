Leptothrix europaea

Leptothrix europaea, commonly known as the European filamentous bacteriocyte, is a unique aquatic bacterium that forms long, spiral-shaped colonies.

It is a type of cyanobacteria that thrives in freshwater environments, particularly in slow-moving streams and ponds. The organism is characterized by its ability to produce a gelatinous sheath that encases its filaments, providing structural support. It plays a role in nutrient cycling and can contribute to biofilm formation in aquatic ecosystems.

Due to its morphological features, it is often used as an indicator species for water quality assessments.
