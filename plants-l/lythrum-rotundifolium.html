Lythrum rotundifolium

Lythrum rotundifolium, commonly known as roundleaf verbena, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, erect stems that bear opposite, oval to round leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces spikes of deep violet to purple flowers, which bloom from June to September. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, and it prefers moist, well-drained soils in partial to full sun.

Lythrum rotundifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
