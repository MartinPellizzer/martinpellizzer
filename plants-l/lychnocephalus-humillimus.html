Lychnocephalus humillimus

Lychnocephalus humillimus, commonly known as the dwarf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of less than 30 centimeters. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually blue or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
