Lihengia gombalana

Lihengia gombalana, commonly known as the golden tamarind, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. Lihengia gombalana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its seeds, which are used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Fabaceae family.
