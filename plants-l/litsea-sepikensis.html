Litsea sepikensis

Litsea sepikensis, commonly known as the Sepik litsea, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia and Papua New Guinea.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and bay trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by round, berry-like fruits that are yellow when ripe.

The tree is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appearance and potential medicinal properties.
