Lobelia persicifolia var. angustifolia

Lobelia persicifolia var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf cardinal flower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features upright stems that bear whorled, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red and orange, typically blooming from June to September. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for wetland gardens.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract butterflies and hummingbirds, contributing to its ecological value.
