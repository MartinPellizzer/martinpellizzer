Lyonia fruticosa

Lyonia fruticosa, commonly known as the mountain beauty or redroot, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, showy pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has woody stems with a reddish hue, and its leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate with a serrated margin. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Lyonia fruticosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
