Lindera chunii

Lindera chunii, commonly known as Chinese wild laurustin, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, ovate leaves that are often reddish beneath. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, followed by edible, red berries that are a food source for various birds and small mammals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and streamside areas.

Lindera chunii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
