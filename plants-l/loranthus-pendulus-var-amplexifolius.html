Loranthus pendulus var. amplexifolius

Loranthus pendulus var. amplexifolius, commonly known as the creeping mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that attaches itself to the branches of its host trees.

It has small, leathery leaves that are often clasping, giving it the appearance of wrapping around the host. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its berries are red and fleshy, containing seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This species is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives on a variety of host trees.
