Lychnis corsica

Lychnis corsica, commonly known as the Corsican campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually oval or lance-shaped with serrated edges. Its flowers are pink to red in color, arranged in dense clusters at the top of the stems.

Lychnis corsica blooms from late spring to early summer, producing showy flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
