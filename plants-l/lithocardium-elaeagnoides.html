Lithocardium elaeagnoides

Lithocardium elaeagnoides, commonly known as the silverleaf lithocardium, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, typically blooming in the spring. Its leaves are alternate and oval-shaped, with a leathery texture.

Lithocardium elaeagnoides is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
