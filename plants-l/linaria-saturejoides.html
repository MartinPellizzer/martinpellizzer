Linaria saturejoides

Linaria saturejoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are bright yellow, with two lips and four lobes, resembling those of the genus Linaria.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.
