Lachnostoma huilaense

Lachnostoma huilaense, commonly known as the Huila ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic orchid species native to the cloud forests of Colombia.

It is characterized by its striking, ghostly white flowers that resemble translucent petals, giving it its common name. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its unique morphology, with long, slender stems and specialized roots adapted for epiphytic growth. Due to its restricted habitat and limited population, it is considered critically endangered.

Conservation efforts are ongoing to protect this rare botanical treasure from habitat loss and environmental degradation.
