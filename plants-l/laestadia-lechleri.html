Laestadia lechleri

Laestadia lechleri, commonly known as the rubber tree, is a tropical plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, lance-shaped leaves and produces a milky latex sap that is harvested for natural rubber. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil. It can grow up to 30 meters tall and has a long history of use by indigenous peoples.

Its latex contains compounds that have medicinal properties and are used in various traditional remedies.
