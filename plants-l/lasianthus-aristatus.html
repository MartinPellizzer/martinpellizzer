Lasianthus aristatus

Lasianthus aristatus, commonly known as the spike-leaved lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are opposite and often have a distinctive, linear shape with a pointed tip.

The flowers, which are usually violet or purple, bloom in clusters at the tips of the stems, making it a visually striking ornamental plant.
