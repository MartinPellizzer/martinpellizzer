Lobivia backebergii var. oxyalabastra

Lobivia backebergii var. oxyalabastra, commonly known as the "Snowy Lobivia," is a rare and striking cactus native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in dense white to pale green spines, which give it a distinctive, almost ethereal appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale pink to white, typically blooming in late winter or early spring. It is well adapted to harsh desert conditions, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems and thriving in full sun with minimal water.

Due to its rarity and unique beauty, it is highly sought after by succulent enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
