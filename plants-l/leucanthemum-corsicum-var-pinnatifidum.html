Leucanthemum corsicum var. pinnatifidum

Leucanthemum corsicum var. pinnatifidum, commonly known as the Corsican Edelweiss, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense, white to pale yellow flowers that are surrounded by a layer of fuzzy, silver-green leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is characterized by finely divided, fern-like leaves that give it a delicate appearance.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique and attractive floral display.
