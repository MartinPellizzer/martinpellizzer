Luehea ferruginea

Luehea ferruginea, commonly known as the red sandalwood tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark reddish-brown heartwood, which is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth texture. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange-red fruits that contain several seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and timber use, Luehea ferruginea is often cultivated in horticultural settings.
