Lappa minor

Lappa minor, commonly known as lesser lappa, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. The plant features slender, upright stems with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its small, greenish-white flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Lappa minor is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
