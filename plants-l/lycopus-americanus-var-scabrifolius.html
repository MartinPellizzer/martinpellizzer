Lycopus americanus var. scabrifolius

Lycopus americanus var. scabrifolius, commonly known as roughleaf blueweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, serrated leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the eastern United States.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological studies and herbal use.
