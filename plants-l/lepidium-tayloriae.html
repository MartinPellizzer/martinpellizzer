Lepidium tayloriae

Lepidium tayloriae, commonly known as Taylor's cress, is a flowering plant native to the high Andes of South America.

It is a small annual herb that typically grows in rocky, alpine environments. The plant features delicate, branched stems and small, green, lobed leaves. Its flowers are yellow and borne in clusters, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Lepidium tayloriae is known for its resilience to harsh climatic conditions and is of interest in botanical research.
