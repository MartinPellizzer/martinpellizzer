Lespedeza hirta var. typica

Lespedeza hirta var. typica, commonly known as hairy lespedeza, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in open woodlands, glades, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. The plant features slender, often hairy stems and alternate leaves that are lance-shaped with serrated edges. Its small, pink to reddish-purple flowers appear in late spring to early summer, forming dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

Lespedeza hirta var. typica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
