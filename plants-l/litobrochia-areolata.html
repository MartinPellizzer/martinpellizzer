Litobrochia areolata

Litobrochia areolata, commonly known as the spotted stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically during the summer months. Its growth habit is compact, forming low, mat-like structures that help it conserve moisture.

Litobrochia areolata is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
