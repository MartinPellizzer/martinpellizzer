Loranthus quinquangulus

Loranthus quinquangulus, commonly known as the five-angled mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own carbohydrates through photosynthesis using the light it captures. The plant has small, scale-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are often clustered in groups of five. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that contains several seeds.

This species is native to parts of Africa and is often found in forested areas where it forms a symbiotic relationship with its host trees.
