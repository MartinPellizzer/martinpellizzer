Lycopersicon peruvianum var. dentatum

Lycopersicon peruvianum var. dentatum, commonly known as the Peruvian tomato, is a botanical variant of the common tomato characterized by its unique, serrated leaf margins and smaller, often more acidic fruits.

It originates from the Andean regions of South America and is adapted to cooler, high-altitude climates. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with hairy stems and lobed leaves, exhibiting a compact growth habit. Its fruits are usually round or oval, with a thick, firm skin and a rich, tangy flavor.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary use in traditional Andean cuisine.
