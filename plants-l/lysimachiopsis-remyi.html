Lysimachiopsis remyi

Lysimachiopsis remyi, commonly known as the rock bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. The plant features basal rosettes of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to violet. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a short, flowering stem.

Lysimachiopsis remyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual interest.
