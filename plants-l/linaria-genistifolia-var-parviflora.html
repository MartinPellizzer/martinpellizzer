Linaria genistifolia var. parviflora

Linaria genistifolia var. parviflora, commonly known as the narrowleaf toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, giving the plant its species name "genistifolia," which refers to its resemblance to the leaves of the broom plant. This variety is a perennial herb that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

It is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
