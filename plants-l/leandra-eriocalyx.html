Leandra eriocalyx

Leandra eriocalyx, commonly known as the velvetleaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety green leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its root system is adapted for absorbing moisture from the air and decaying organic matter.

Leandra eriocalyx is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in specific tropical conditions.
