Lepanthopsis micheliae

Lepanthopsis micheliae, commonly known as the Michelia orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are elongated and lanceolate, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

Lepanthopsis micheliae thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
