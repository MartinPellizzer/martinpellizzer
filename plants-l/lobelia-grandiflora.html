Lobelia grandiflora

Lobelia grandiflora, commonly known as the grandiflora lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring lance-shaped leaves and showy blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for wetland gardens. Its flowers are clustered in whorls along the stems, and the plant is known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Lobelia grandiflora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant landscapes.
