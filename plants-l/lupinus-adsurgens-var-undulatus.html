Lupinus adsurgens var. undulatus

Lupinus adsurgens var. undulatus, commonly known as the undulating lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of pea-like flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets, and its root system includes nitrogen-fixing symbiotic bacteria. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
