Lindleyalis glossopogon

Lindleyalis glossopogon, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tussock grass, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and compact growth habit, often forming dense tussocks. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and open woodland habitats. Its inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spikelet that bears small, spike-like flowers.

Lindleyalis glossopogon plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
