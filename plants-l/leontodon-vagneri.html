Leontodon vagneri

Leontodon vagneri, commonly known as the Vagner's toothbrush grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous root system. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, while its flowering heads consist of small yellow disk flowers surrounded by pale green to brown bracts.

Leontodon vagneri is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
