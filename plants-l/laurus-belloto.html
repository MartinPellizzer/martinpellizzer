Laurus belloto

Laurus belloto, commonly known as the belloto laurel, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like laurel and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a small, dark berry that ripens in late summer.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
