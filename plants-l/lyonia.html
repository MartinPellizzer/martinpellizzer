Lyonia

Lyonia, commonly known as the evergreen huckleberry, is a genus of flowering plants in the heath family, Ericaceae.

It comprises several species, most of which are shrubs native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. These plants typically have small, leathery leaves and produce clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The berries, which are edible, are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

Lyonia species are valued for their ornamental appeal and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
