Lachnagrostis sodiroana

Lachnagrostis sodiroana, commonly known as the Andean hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments, often in mountainous regions. Its botanical features include rhizomatous growth and a clump-forming habit.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
