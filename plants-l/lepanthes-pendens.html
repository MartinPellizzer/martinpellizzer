Lepanthes pendens

Lepanthes pendens, commonly known as the pendant orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its pendant, drooping flowers that hang from the stems, giving it a graceful appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in the understory of tropical forests. Its flowers are usually small, with intricate patterns and a range of colors, including white, pink, and purple.

Lepanthes pendens is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
