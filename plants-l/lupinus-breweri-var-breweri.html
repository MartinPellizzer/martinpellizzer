Lupinus breweri var. breweri

Lupinus breweri var. breweri, commonly known as the Brewer's lupine, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces legume pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Lupinus breweri var. breweri is an important species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
