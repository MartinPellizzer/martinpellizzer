Lithospermum erythrorhizon

Lithospermum erythrorhizon, commonly known as Japanese burning bush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red-orange flowers and dark purple to black roots, which contain high concentrations of lithospermic acid. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Lithospermum erythrorhizon is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
