Linum usitatissimum subsp. hispanicum

Linum usitatissimum subsp.

hispanicum, commonly known as Spanish flax, is a subspecies of the flax plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is an annual herb characterized by its slender stems and lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces blue or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its seeds are smaller than those of common flax and are used in traditional culinary and medicinal applications.

The subspecies is adapted to dry, rocky soils and thrives in temperate climates.
