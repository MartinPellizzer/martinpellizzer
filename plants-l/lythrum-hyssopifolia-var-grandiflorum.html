Lythrum hyssopifolia var. grandiflorum

Lythrum hyssopifolia var. grandiflorum, commonly known as the grandiflorum lythrum, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features striking, showy spikes of deep rose-purple to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped, gray-green leaves with a fuzzy texture, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for wetland restoration and ornamental gardening.
