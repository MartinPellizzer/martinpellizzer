Libonia bicolor

Libonia bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored libonia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking bi-colored flowers, which typically feature a yellow base with a red or purple upper lip. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant and unique floral display.
