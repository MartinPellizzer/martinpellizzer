Lobelia trialata

Lobelia trialata, commonly known as the purple lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 feet. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to blue, often with a white or yellow throat. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Lobelia trialata is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and hummingbirds.
