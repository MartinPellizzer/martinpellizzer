Lippia spathulata

Lippia spathulata, commonly known as Mexican mint or winter mint, is a perennial herb native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, slightly minty scent. The plant features opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves with a rough texture and small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale purple. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental and culinary use.

Lippia spathulata is also valued for its medicinal properties, including its use in traditional remedies.
