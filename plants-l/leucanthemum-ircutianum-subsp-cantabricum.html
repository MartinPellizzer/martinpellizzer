Leucanthemum ircutianum subsp. cantabricum

Leucanthemum ircutianum subsp.

cantabricum, commonly known as the Cantabrian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the western Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its white to pale yellow flowers, which are typically arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it thrives in well-drained soils in sunny or partially shaded locations. It is a perennial herb that produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
