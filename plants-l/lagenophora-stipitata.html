Lagenophora stipitata

Lagenophora stipitata, commonly known as the hairy stalked orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure and hairy stems. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with a distinctive coloration that varies among species.

Lagenophora stipitata is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological systems of its native habitats.
