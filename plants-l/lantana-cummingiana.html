Lantana cummingiana

Lantana cummingiana, commonly known as the yellow lantana, is a flowering plant native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange and red. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Lantana cummingiana is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
