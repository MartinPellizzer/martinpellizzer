Laportea harveyi

Laportea harveyi, commonly known as Harvey's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and clusters of small, purple to pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, oblong fruits that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and along streambanks.

Laportea harveyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
