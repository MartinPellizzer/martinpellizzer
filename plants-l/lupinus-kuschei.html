Lupinus kuschei

Lupinus kuschei, commonly known as the Kusche's lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces legume pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Lupinus kuschei is also valued for its role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.
