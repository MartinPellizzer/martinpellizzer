Linnaea borealis f. pusilla

Linnaea borealis f. pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf twinflower, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to northern regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically reaches a height of only a few centimeters, making it a delicate and understated member of the family Ericaceae. Its leaves are evergreen, glossy, and elliptical, providing a dense ground cover in shaded forest environments.

This variety is notable for its compact size and its ability to thrive in cool, moist conditions.
