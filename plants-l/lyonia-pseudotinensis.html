Lyonia pseudotinensis

Lyonia pseudotinensis, commonly known as the Hawaiian lyonia, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen shrub form. The plant features small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. Its leaves are leathery and glossy, adapted to the dry, volcanic soils of its native habitat.

Lyonia pseudotinensis plays an important ecological role in the unique ecosystems of Hawaii.
