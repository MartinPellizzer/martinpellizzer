Lomatia myricoides

Lomatia myricoides, commonly known as the silky lomatia, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in heathlands and open forests, characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in spikes, which are followed by woody, cone-like fruits. Its leaves are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Lomatia myricoides is also notable for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
