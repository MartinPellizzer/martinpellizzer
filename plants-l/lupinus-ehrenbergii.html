Lupinus ehrenbergii

Lupinus ehrenbergii, commonly known as the desert lupine, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as deserts and rocky soils. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces spikes of blue to purple flowers. Lupinus ehrenbergii is adapted to withstand drought conditions and has a deep root system for water absorption.

It is also known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.
