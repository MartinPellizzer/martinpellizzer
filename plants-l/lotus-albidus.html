Lotus albidus

Lotus albidus, commonly known as the white lotus, is a species of aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, white, floating flowers that bloom in the early morning and close by midday. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its leaves are broad and floating, while its roots are anchored to the bottom of the water body.

The white lotus is also valued for its ornamental beauty and has cultural significance in various Asian traditions.
