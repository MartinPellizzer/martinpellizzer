Leptochloa dubia var. humboldtiana

Leptochloa dubia var. humboldtiana, commonly known as the Humboldt slender grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or wetland environments. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are small and tightly packed.

This variety is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
