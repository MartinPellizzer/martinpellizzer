Liparis pluridentata

Liparis pluridentata, commonly known as the many-toothed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial herb with a rhizomatous root system and is characterized by its delicate, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
