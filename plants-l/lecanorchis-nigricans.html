Lecanorchis nigricans

Lecanorchis nigricans, commonly known as the black orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features a slender, unbranched stem and produces small, greenish or brownish flowers with a distinctive, intricate structure. Its leaves are linear and often clustered at the base of the plant.

Lecanorchis nigricans is notable for its lack of showy petals, with the sepals and petals being more prominent in its floral display.
