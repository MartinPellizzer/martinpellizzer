Lobivia napina

Lobivia napina, commonly known as the Narrow-leaved Lobivia, is a succulent native to the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded shape and thick, fleshy stems that store water. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers during the cooler months. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a fine layer of white powder, which helps reduce water loss.

Lobivia napina is well adapted to arid conditions and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.
