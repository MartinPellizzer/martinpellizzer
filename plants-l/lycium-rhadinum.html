Lycium rhadinum

Lycium rhadinum, commonly known as the desert gooseberry, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, yellow-orange berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. The plant has small, oval leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It is adapted to survive in harsh environments with minimal water, making it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.

Lycium rhadinum plays an important role in supporting local wildlife and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
