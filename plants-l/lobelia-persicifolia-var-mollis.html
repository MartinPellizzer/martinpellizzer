Lobelia persicifolia var. mollis

Lobelia persicifolia var. mollis, commonly known as the soft-leaved lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its softly hairy, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in well-drained, loamy soil. It is a perennial herb that produces showy blooms from late spring to early summer.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for native gardens and wetland restoration projects.
