Leucanthemum vulgare var. legraeanum

Leucanthemum vulgare var. legraeanum, commonly known as the Shasta daisy, is a perennial plant native to western North America.

It is characterized by its white ray florets surrounding a central disk of yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 30 inches and has a bushy, compact growth habit. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its foliage consists of gray-green, lance-shaped leaves that are aromatic when crushed.
