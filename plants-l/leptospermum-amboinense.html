Leptospermum amboinense

Leptospermum amboinense, commonly known as the broad-leaved tea tree, is a flowering plant native to Australia and New Guinea.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal and subcoastal regions. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, often with a distinct aromatic scent.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
