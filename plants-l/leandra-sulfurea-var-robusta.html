Leandra sulfurea var. robusta

Leandra sulfurea var. robusta, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves and striking, pendulous inflorescences that produce clusters of yellow or golden-orange flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, warm climates with well-drained soil. Its flowers are not only ornamental but also attract pollinators such as bees and birds.

It is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and as a landscape plant in tropical regions.
