Lupinus albifrons var. albifrons

Lupinus albifrons var. albifrons, commonly known as the white-fronted lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of fluffy, white to pale pink flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
