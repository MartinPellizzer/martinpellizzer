Lobivia pugionacantha var. alberi

Lobivia pugionacantha var. alberi, commonly known as the Alberi's lobivia, is a succulent plant native to South America, specifically Argentina.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in dense, white spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it stores water in its thick, fleshy stems.

This variety is prized for its compact size and ornamental appearance, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and succulent collections.
