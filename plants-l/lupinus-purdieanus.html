Lupinus purdieanus

Lupinus purdieanus, commonly known as Purdie's lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Lupinus purdieanus plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native environment.
