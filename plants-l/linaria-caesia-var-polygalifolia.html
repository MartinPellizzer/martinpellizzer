Linaria caesia var. polygalifolia

Linaria caesia var. polygalifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf toadflax, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, delicate yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The leaves are narrow and often covered in a fine layer of white hairs, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in dry, open habitats.

This plant is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
