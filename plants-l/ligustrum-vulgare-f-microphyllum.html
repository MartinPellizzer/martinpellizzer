Ligustrum vulgare f. microphyllum

Ligustrum vulgare f. microphyllum, commonly known as the small-leaved privet, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically no more than 1 inch in length, giving it a dense, bushy appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters, which are followed by dark blue-black berries. It is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for hedges and ornamental planting.
