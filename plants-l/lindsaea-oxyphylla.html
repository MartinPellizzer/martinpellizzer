Lindsaea oxyphylla

Lindsaea oxyphylla, commonly known as the sharp-leaved spikemoss, is a member of the family Lindsaeaceae and is native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a small, evergreen fern-like plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rock crevices. The plant features erect, branching stems covered with dense, needle-like leaves that are often sharply pointed. Lindsaea oxyphylla reproduces via spores, with the sporophyte stage dominated by the leafy, photosynthetic structures.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
