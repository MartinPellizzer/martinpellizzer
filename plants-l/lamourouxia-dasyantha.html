Lamourouxia dasyantha

Lamourouxia dasyantha, commonly known as the woolly bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and specialized bladder-like structures used to trap and digest small aquatic organisms. The plant thrives in nutrient-poor water environments, relying on its prey for essential nutrients. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain its seeds.
