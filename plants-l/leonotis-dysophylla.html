Leonotis dysophylla

Leonotis dysophylla, commonly known as the blue devil, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with square stems and opposite leaves. Its flowers are clustered in whorls along the stems, creating a dense, showy display.

Leonotis dysophylla is known for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
