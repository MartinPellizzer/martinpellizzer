Lepidium lasiocarpum var. typicum

Lepidium lasiocarpum var. typicum, commonly known as the roughpod cress, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many economically important crops such as cabbage and mustard. The plant is characterized by its small, delicate leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. Its fruiting structures, called siliques, are distinctive for their rough, hairy surface.

This species is often found in rocky or disturbed soils and is adapted to arid environments.
