Leucogenes neglecta

Leucogenes neglecta, commonly known as the white-veined orchid, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, white petals with subtle green veins. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are small and fragrant, often blooming in clusters during the wet season.

Leucogenes neglecta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
