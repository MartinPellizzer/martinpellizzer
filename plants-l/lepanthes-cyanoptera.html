Lepanthes cyanoptera

Lepanthes cyanoptera, commonly known as the blue-tipped orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet petals and sepals, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical forests. Its flowers are relatively small but highly fragrant, attracting various pollinators such as bees and moths.

Lepanthes cyanoptera is valued not only for its aesthetic appeal but also for its ecological role in its native habitats.
