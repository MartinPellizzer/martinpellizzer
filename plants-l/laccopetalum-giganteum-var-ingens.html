Laccopetalum giganteum var. ingens

Laccopetalum giganteum var. ingens, commonly known as the giant laccopetalum, is a striking plant native to the humid forests of southern China.

It is characterized by its large, leathery leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the spring. Its robust growth habit and unique foliage make it a notable species in botanical gardens.

It is primarily valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
