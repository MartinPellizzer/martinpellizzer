Lobelia laurentia var. nana

Lobelia laurentia var. nana, commonly known as dwarf lobelia, is a compact, low-growing plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, tubular blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has smooth, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and as a ground cover.
