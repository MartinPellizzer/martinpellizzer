Lasiochlamys rivularis

Lasiochlamys rivularis, commonly known as the river thistle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to temperate regions of Europe.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and woodland edges. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. Its flowers are small, clustered in loose inflorescences, and are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color.

The species is characterized by its fibrous root system and its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.
