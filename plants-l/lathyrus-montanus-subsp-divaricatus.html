Lathyrus montanus subsp. divaricatus

Lathyrus montanus subsp.

divaricatus, commonly known as the Alpine Pea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats.

The plant features slender, erect stems with trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are small, pea-shaped, and usually pink or purple in color.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological systems of high-altitude environments.
