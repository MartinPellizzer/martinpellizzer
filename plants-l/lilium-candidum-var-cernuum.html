Lilium candidum var. cernuum

Lilium candidum var. cernuum, commonly known as the nodding white lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, white, bell-shaped flowers that hang downward, giving it its common name. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. It produces a tall, slender stem that bears the floral cluster at the top.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in perfumery and medicine.
