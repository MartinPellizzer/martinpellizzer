Lepturus incurvatus var. tortus

Lepturus incurvatus var. tortus, commonly known as the twisted spurge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spirally twisted stems that give it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly waxy texture.

The species is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
