Lonicera caucasica subsp. govaniana

Lonicera caucasica subsp.

govaniana, commonly known as the Caucasian honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the Caucasus region. It features opposite, simple, and usually hairy leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape.

The plant produces small, fragrant, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in temperate climates.
