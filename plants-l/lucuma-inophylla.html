Lucuma inophylla

Lucuma inophylla, commonly known as the golden apple, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its large, yellow, fleshy fruits that resemble apples. The plant typically grows in high-altitude areas, thriving in cool, humid climates with well-drained soil. Its leaves are broad and evergreen, and it produces fragrant flowers that are white or pale green.

The fruit is not only ornamental but also valued for its edible, sweet flesh, which has a unique, honey-like flavor.
