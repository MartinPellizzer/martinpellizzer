Lepidium sagittulatum var. genuinum

Lepidium sagittulatum var. genuinum, commonly known as the narrowleaf shepherd's purse, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, triangular seed pods. Native to the western United States, it thrives in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces small white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological and botanical studies.
