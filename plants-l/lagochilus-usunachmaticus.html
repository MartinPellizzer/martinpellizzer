Lagochilus usunachmaticus

Lagochilus usunachmaticus, commonly known as the Usunachmaticus hyacinth, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling those of the hyacinth. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
