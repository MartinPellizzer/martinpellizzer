Leersia sayanuka

Leersia sayanuka, commonly known as the Japanese wirestem grass, is a perennial grass species native to East Asia.

It features slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy along the edges. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the grass family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Leersia sayanuka is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
