Lotus arabicus var. verus

Lotus arabicus var. verus, commonly known as the Persian lotus, is a herbaceous perennial native to the arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It features palmately compound leaves with serrated margins and produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is adapted to thrive in hot, dry climates with minimal water requirements. Its roots are edible and have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The Persian lotus is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and landscapes.
