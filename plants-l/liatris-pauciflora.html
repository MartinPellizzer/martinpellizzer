Liatris pauciflora

Liatris pauciflora, commonly known as the few-flowered blazing star, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear clusters of purple to pinkish-purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies, woodlands, and sandy soils. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Liatris pauciflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native pollinators.
