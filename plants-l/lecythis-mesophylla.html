Lecythis mesophylla

Lecythis mesophylla, commonly known as the pink lecythium, is a tropical tree native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and produces distinctive, ornamental fruits with a pink, fleshy exterior. The tree's mesophyll tissue is thick and spongy, contributing to its unique appearance and ecological role. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The plant is valued for its ecological importance and has potential uses in traditional medicine.
