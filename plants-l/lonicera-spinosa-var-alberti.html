Lonicera spinosa var. alberti

Lonicera spinosa var. alberti, commonly known as the Albert's honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, thorny stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that ripens in the fall, attracting birds and other wildlife.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
