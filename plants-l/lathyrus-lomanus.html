Lathyrus lomanus

Lathyrus lomanus, commonly known as the Loman's vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves, which consist of three small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple, often arranged in clusters. Lathyrus lomanus is native to parts of Europe and is typically found in grassy areas or along the edges of woodlands.

Its seed pods are elongated and contain several small, kidney-shaped seeds that are dispersed by wind or animals.
