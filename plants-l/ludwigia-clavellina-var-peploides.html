Ludwigia clavellina var. peploides

Ludwigia clavellina var. peploides, commonly known as the velvet Ludwigia, is a species of flowering plant in the Onagraceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its striking, velvety foliage. The leaves are typically broad and oval, with a rich green to reddish hue, depending on light conditions. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences.

This plant is often cultivated as an ornamental in aquatic and semi-aquatic environments due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability.
