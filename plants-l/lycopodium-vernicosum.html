Lycopodium vernicosum

Lycopodium vernicosum, commonly known as the common clubmoss, is a small evergreen shrub native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae and is characterized by its spore-bearing cones and needle-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures, called strobili, are clustered at the tips of its branches.

Lycopodium vernicosum plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various small organisms.
