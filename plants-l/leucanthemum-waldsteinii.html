Leucanthemum waldsteinii

Leucanthemum waldsteinii, commonly known as the Waldstein's leucanthemum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces white to pale yellow flowers with a central disc, typically blooming in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens despite its relatively limited distribution in the wild.
