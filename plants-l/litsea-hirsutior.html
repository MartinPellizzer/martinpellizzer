Litsea hirsutior

Litsea hirsutior, commonly known as the roughleaf litsea, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Vietnam, and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are covered in fine, short hairs, giving it a rough texture. It produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by yellowish berries.

Litsea hirsutior is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
