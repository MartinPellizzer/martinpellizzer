Liparis resupinata

Liparis resupinata, commonly known as the nodding orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stem and a single, pendulous flower that droops downward. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and the flower has three sepals and three petals, with the latter being showy and often spotted.

Liparis resupinata is a perennial herb that blooms in late spring to early summer.
