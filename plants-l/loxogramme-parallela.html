Loxogramme parallela

Loxogramme parallela, commonly known as the parallel-stemmed water clover, is a perennial aquatic plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features finely divided, floating leaves that are typically arranged in a whorled pattern along its stems. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its small, greenish-white flowers are borne on short stalks and are often submerged or floating.

Loxogramme parallela plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
