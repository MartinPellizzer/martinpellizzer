Lonchocarpus guatemalensis var. mexicanus

Lonchocarpus guatemalensis var. mexicanus, commonly known as Mexican climbing bean, is a species of legume native to Central America.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets. The plant produces distinctive pods that are often hairy and contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, typically in moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both ecological and agricultural purposes.
