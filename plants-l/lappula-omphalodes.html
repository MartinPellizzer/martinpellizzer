Lappula omphalodes

Lappula omphalodes, commonly known as the bladder campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and wastelands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are usually pink or purple.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in harsh environments and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
