Lepidium crassius

Lepidium crassius, commonly known as the thick-leaved wallcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant features small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes, and its leaves are typically broad and somewhat succulent. Lepidium crassius is adapted to harsh, alpine environments and is often found growing in rocky soils.

It is a perennial herb that plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects.
