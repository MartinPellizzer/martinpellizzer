Lonchocarpus xuul

Lonchocarpus xuul, commonly known as the Mexican snakewood, is a species of leguminous plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive pod-like fruits. The plant contains various alkaloids, some of which have been studied for their potential medicinal properties. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its chemical composition, it has attracted interest in both traditional and modern pharmacological research.
