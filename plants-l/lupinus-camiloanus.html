Lupinus camiloanus

Lupinus camiloanus, commonly known as the Camiloan lupine, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of vibrant blue or purple flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal or mountainous areas.

Lupinus camiloanus plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
