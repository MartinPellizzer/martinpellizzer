Lessingianthus eitenii

Lessingianthus eitenii, commonly known as the Eiten's sunflower, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of South America, particularly in the Andes.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant yellow to orange flowers. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, providing a textured appearance. Its flowering period typically occurs during the warmer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ecological significance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts of native flora.
