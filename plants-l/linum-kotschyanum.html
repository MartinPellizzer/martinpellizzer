Linum kotschyanum

Linum kotschyanum, commonly known as Kotschyan's flax, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, delicate flowers. The plant produces capsules that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in some regions for both aesthetic and medicinal purposes.
