Lepanthes caudigera

Lepanthes caudigera, commonly known as the long-tongued orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern.

Lepanthes caudigera is often found in humid, shaded environments such as cloud forests.
