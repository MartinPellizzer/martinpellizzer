Limeum indicum

Limeum indicum, commonly known as the Indian lime or kaffir lime, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves that have a distinctive citrus scent. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that give way to round, green fruits with a segmented, aromatic rind. The fruit is widely used in cooking for its intense flavor and fragrance.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rutaceae family and is closely related to other citrus species.
