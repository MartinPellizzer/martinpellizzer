Litsea gracilipes

Litsea gracilipes, commonly known as the slender leafed-laurel, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by berries that are typically yellow or orange when mature.

Litsea gracilipes is often found in moist, shaded habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal uses.
