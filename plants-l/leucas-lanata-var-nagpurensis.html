Leucas lanata var. nagpurensis

Leucas lanata var. nagpurensis, commonly known as Nagpur leucas, is a herbaceous plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

This variant is notable for its medicinal uses in traditional systems of medicine.
