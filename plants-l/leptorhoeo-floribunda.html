Leptorhoeo floribunda

Leptorhoeo floribunda, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of blue or violet. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. It is often found in alpine meadows and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Leptorhoeo floribunda is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
