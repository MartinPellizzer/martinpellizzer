Linaria laxiflora subsp. calcarlongum

Linaria laxiflora subsp.

calcarlongum, commonly known as the mountain sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its low-growing habit and produces small, yellow-orange flowers in clusters.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
