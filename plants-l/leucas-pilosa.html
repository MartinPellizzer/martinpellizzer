Leucas pilosa

Leucas pilosa, commonly known as the hairy leucas, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated for both aesthetic and traditional uses.
