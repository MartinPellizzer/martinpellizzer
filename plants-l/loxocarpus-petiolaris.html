Loxocarpus petiolaris

Loxocarpus petiolaris, commonly known as the New Zealand tree fern, is a member of the Cyatheaceae family.

It is native to New Zealand and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can live for several decades, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its rhizome system supports the development of new shoots, contributing to its growth pattern.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
