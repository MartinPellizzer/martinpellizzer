Laserpitium latifolium var. longifolium

Laserpitium latifolium var. longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved lousewort, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom from June to August, and it is pollinated by insects.

The species is noted for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
