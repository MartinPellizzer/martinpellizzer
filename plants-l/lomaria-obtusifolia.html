Lomaria obtusifolia

Lomaria obtusifolia, commonly known as the bluntleaf lomariopsis, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads and typical foliage structure. The plant features alternate, lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in dense clusters, typical of the sunflower family.

Lomaria obtusifolia thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
