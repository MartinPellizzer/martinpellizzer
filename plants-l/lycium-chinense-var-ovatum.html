Lycium chinense var. ovatum

Lycium chinense var. ovatum, commonly known as Chinese wolfberry or goji berry, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family and is characterized by its small, orange-red berries that are rich in antioxidants. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are ovate with a slightly hairy surface, and it produces yellowish-white flowers in the spring.

The berries are harvested when fully mature and are widely used in traditional Chinese medicine and as a nutritious food supplement.
