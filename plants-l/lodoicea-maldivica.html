Lodoicea maldivica

Lodoicea maldivica, commonly known as the sea almond, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Indian Ocean.

It is characterized by its woody stem and palm-like appearance, with large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are distinctive, consisting of a large, hard seed enclosed in a fibrous, woody husk.

The plant is adapted to coastal and saline environments, making it a hardy species in tropical climates.
