Lilium kelloggii

Lilium kelloggii, commonly known as the prairie lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and woodland edges. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are usually pink to reddish in color, with six petals and a distinctive recurved shape. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, and it has a fibrous root system.

Lilium kelloggii is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
