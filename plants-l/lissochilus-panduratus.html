Lissochilus panduratus

Lissochilus panduratus, commonly known as the panduratus orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, often colorful flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically features a short, stout stem with a few leaves and produces racemes of small, fragrant flowers. It thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found in cloud forests at high altitudes.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
