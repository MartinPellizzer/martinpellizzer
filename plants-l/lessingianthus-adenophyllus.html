Lessingianthus adenophyllus

Lessingianthus adenophyllus, commonly known as the woolly sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, fluffy appearance. The plant features bright yellow flowers surrounded by dense, woolly white or gray hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in a similar fuzzy coating.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry grasslands and rocky areas.
