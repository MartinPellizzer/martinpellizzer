Litsea alveolata

Litsea alveolata, commonly known as the fragrant leaf tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Vietnam, Cambodia, and Thailand. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that have a distinctive waxy texture and emit a mild, aromatic scent. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically white or pale yellow, and it produces small, dark blue berries.

Litsea alveolata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
