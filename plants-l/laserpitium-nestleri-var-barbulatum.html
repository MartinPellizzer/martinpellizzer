Laserpitium nestleri var. barbulatum

Laserpitium nestleri var. barbulatum, commonly known as the Barbulate Meadow Grass, is a flowering plant species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and small, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are linear and slightly hairy, while its inflorescence consists of yellow disk flowers surrounded by pale green to white ray florets.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
