Limniris graminea

Limniris graminea, commonly known as the slender marsh grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh environments.

It typically grows in shallow water or damp soil, exhibiting a grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the grass family. Limniris graminea plays a vital role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for aquatic and semi-aquatic organisms.

Its adaptability to fluctuating water levels makes it an important component of riparian and marsh ecosystems.
