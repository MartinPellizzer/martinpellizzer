Lophoschoenus chamaedendron

Lophoschoenus chamaedendron, commonly known as the coastal reed, is a grass-like plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows in sandy or muddy soils along the shoreline, where it plays a role in stabilizing dunes and preventing erosion. Its flowering stems are usually short and bear small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Lophoschoenus chamaedendron is well adapted to salt spray and fluctuating water levels, making it an important component of coastal ecosystems.
