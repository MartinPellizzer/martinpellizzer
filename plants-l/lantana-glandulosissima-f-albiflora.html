Lantana glandulosissima f. albiflora

Lantana glandulosissima f. albiflora, commonly known as the whiteflower lantana, is a flowering shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, clustered white to pale yellow flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, glandular hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Its showy flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to native gardens.
