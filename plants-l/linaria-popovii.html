Linaria popovii

Linaria popovii, commonly known as the Popov's toadflax, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, spurred flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Linaria popovii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various growing conditions.
