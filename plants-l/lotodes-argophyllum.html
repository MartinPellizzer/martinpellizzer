Lotodes argophyllum

Lotodes argophyllum, commonly known as the silverleaf water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, round, silver-green leaves that float on the water's surface, providing a unique visual contrast to the surrounding environment. The plant produces white, fragrant flowers that bloom above the water, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves have a waxy coating that helps repel water and reduce evaporation, an adaptation to its aquatic habitat.

The silverleaf water lily plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by offering shelter to small aquatic organisms and contributing to water quality maintenance.
