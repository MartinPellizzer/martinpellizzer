Lipochaeta monocephala

Lipochaeta monocephala, commonly known as the single-headed bush willow, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in heads, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid regions, often found in rocky soils and along riverbanks.

Lipochaeta monocephala is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
