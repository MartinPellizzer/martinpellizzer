Liatris spicata var. macrostachya

Liatris spicata var. macrostachya, commonly known as the tall blazing star, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It features upright, spikelike clusters of showy, tubular purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is drought-tolerant and prefers well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

This variety is notable for its striking appearance and ecological value, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
