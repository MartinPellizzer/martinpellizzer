Loranthus pereskiifolius

Loranthus pereskiifolius, commonly known as the red mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily eucalyptus and other native Australian species.

It lacks true roots and absorbs water and nutrients through its leaves, which are thick and leathery. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are usually clustered in dense inflorescences. Its berries, which are bright red, contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing food for various wildlife.
