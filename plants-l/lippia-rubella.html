Lippia rubella

Lippia rubella, commonly known as red lippia, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to various garden environments. Its essential oils, derived from the leaves, are valued for their medicinal and aromatic properties.

Lippia rubella is also a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its vibrant foliage and extended blooming period.
