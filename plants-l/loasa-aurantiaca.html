Loasa aurantiaca

Loasa aurantiaca, commonly known as the orange loasa, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in high-altitude regions, often in rocky or semi-arid environments. The plant produces showy, orange-red flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Loasa aurantiaca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
