Lobelia gaudichaudii f. hirsuta

Lobelia gaudichaudii f. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to New Zealand.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, short hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Its unique appearance and ecological significance make it a notable species in New Zealand's flora.
