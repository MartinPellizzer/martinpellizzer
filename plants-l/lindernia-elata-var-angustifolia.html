Lindernia elata var. angustifolia

Lindernia elata var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf lindernia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom from summer to fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
