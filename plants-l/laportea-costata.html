Laportea costata

Laportea costata, commonly known as the roughleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and showy purple to pink flower heads. The plant features narrow, rough-textured leaves that are often hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as swamps and stream banks.

Laportea costata is valued for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local pollinators.
