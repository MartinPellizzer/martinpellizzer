Lemmatium lemmatioides

Lemmatium lemmatioides, commonly known as the yellow lichen, is a foliose lichen that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It features a complex symbiotic relationship between a fungus and green algae or cyanobacteria, which together provide the plant with nutrients. The thallus is usually bright yellow or greenish-yellow, with a soft, leathery texture. It often grows on tree bark, rocks, or soil in temperate regions.

This lichen is an important indicator species for environmental health due to its sensitivity to air pollution.
