Lepidagathis angustifolia

Lepidagathis angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed lepidagathis, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually arranged alternately along the stem. Its small, clustered flowers are usually white or pale pink, with five petals and a distinct, elongated spur.

Lepidagathis angustifolia is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks.
