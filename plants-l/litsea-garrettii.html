Litsea garrettii

Litsea garrettii, commonly known as the Chinese sandalwood or sweet-scented laurier, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including China, Vietnam, and the Philippines, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, ovate leaves and small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, woody drupe that contains a single seed.

Litsea garrettii is valued for its fragrant wood, which is used in traditional crafts and as a source of essential oils.
