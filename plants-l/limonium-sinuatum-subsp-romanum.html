Limonium sinuatum subsp. romanum

Limonium sinuatum subsp.

romanum, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spherical seed heads that are often mistaken for fruits. It thrives in coastal areas and well-drained soils, making it a hardy perennial.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its striking floral display and ability to attract pollinators.
