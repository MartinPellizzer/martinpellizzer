Lonchocarpus filipes

Lonchocarpus filipes, commonly known as the slender legume, is a tropical plant native to regions of Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a climbing legume characterized by its slender, twining stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes many nitrogen-fixing plants, contributing to soil fertility.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
