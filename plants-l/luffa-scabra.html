Luffa scabra

Luffa scabra, commonly known as the rough luffa, is a tropical vine belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, ribbed stems and large, lobed leaves that are typically green with a rough texture. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by elongated, fibrous fruits that mature to a pale yellow or beige color. The fibrous interior of the fruit is used as a natural sponge, while the outer husk is discarded.

Luffa scabra thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and ample sunlight for optimal growth.
