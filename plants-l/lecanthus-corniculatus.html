Lecanthus corniculatus

Lecanthus corniculatus, commonly known as the horned lettuce, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often tinged with purple. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Lecanthus corniculatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
