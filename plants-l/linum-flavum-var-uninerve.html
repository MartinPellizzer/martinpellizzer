Linum flavum var. uninerve

Linum flavum var. uninerve, commonly known as the narrow-veined flax, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a single prominent vein. The flowers are typically yellow or orange, with five petals that are often reflexed at the tips. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland or rocky habitats.

This variety is notable for its unique leaf structure, which distinguishes it from other flax species.
