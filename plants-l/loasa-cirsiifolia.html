Loasa cirsiifolia

Loasa cirsiifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf loasa, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Loasa cirsiifolia thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable choice for rocky or dry garden environments.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
