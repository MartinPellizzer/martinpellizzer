Loeselia involucrata

Loeselia involucrata, commonly known as the clustered sandmat, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically forms dense clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant has a fibrous root system and produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Loeselia involucrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
