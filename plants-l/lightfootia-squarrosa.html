Lightfootia squarrosa

Lightfootia squarrosa, commonly known as the woolly dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and bright yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and riverbanks. Its seed heads are equipped with a pappus, allowing for wind dispersal of its seeds.

Lightfootia squarrosa is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
