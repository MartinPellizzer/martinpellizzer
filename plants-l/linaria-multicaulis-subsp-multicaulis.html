Linaria multicaulis subsp. multicaulis

Linaria multicaulis subsp.

multicaulis, commonly known as the clustered toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and bright yellow, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are opposite, linear, and often covered in fine hairs.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
