Lobelia heyneana f. parviflora

Lobelia heyneana f. parviflora, commonly known as the dwarf lobelia, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of South America.

It features small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in dense clusters along the stems. The plant has finely divided, grass-like leaves that contribute to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for adding color and texture to shaded garden spaces.
