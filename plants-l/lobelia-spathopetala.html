Lobelia spathopetala

Lobelia spathopetala, commonly known as the broad-leaf lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features lance-shaped leaves with a bluish-green hue and produces striking, tubular flowers in shades of blue and violet. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by hummingbirds and bees, making it an important species for pollinators.

Lobelia spathopetala is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
