Lilium lophophorum

Lilium lophophorum, commonly known as the white lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant's leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. It belongs to the Liliaceae family, which includes other well-known ornamental and medicinal plants.

Lilium lophophorum is often cultivated for its aesthetic value and is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
