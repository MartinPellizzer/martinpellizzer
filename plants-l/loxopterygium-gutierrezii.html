Loxopterygium gutierrezii

Loxopterygium gutierrezii, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant orange to yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with slender, branching stems. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers consist of both ray and disk florets.

Loxopterygium gutierrezii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its bright, showy blooms.
