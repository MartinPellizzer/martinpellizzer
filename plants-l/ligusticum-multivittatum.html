Ligusticum multivittatum

Ligusticum multivittatum, commonly known as the multi-striped lovage, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescences.

It is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant features compound leaves with a distinct, multi-colored venation pattern that gives it its common name. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in umbrella-like clusters, and it produces small, dark berries as fruit.

Ligusticum multivittatum is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
