Leontodon tingitanus

Leontodon tingitanus, commonly known as the Canary Island dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, exhibiting a preference for well-drained soils. It produces yellow flowers that are followed by fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds.

Leontodon tingitanus is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in harsh, arid environments.
