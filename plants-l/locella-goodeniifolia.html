Locella goodeniifolia

Locella goodeniifolia, commonly known as the mountain morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while its stems are covered in fine hairs.

Locella goodeniifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and ecological benefits.
