Lonicera rockii

Lonicera rockii, commonly known as the Chinese honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces red berries that are edible for birds but generally considered toxic to humans. Lonicera rockii thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
