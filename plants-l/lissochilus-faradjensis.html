Lissochilus faradjensis

Lissochilus faradjensis, commonly known as the Faradjean's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or mountainous areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its inflorescence consists of a few showy, violet-colored blooms.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
