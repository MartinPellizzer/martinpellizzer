Libocedrus decurrens

Libocedrus decurrens, commonly known as the incense cedar, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to western North America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, scale-like leaves that are dark green on top and pale underneath. The tree produces small, reddish-brown cones that mature over several years. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and pleasant scent, often used in furniture and cabinetry.

Libocedrus decurrens typically grows in mountainous regions and is adapted to a variety of soil types and elevations.
