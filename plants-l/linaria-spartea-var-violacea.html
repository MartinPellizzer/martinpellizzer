Linaria spartea var. violacea

Linaria spartea var. violacea, commonly known as the violet-flowered sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of showy, violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are typically tubular with two lips, a characteristic feature of the Plantaginaceae family.

It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and dry open areas.
