Limbarda crithmifolia

Limbarda crithmifolia, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its blue or purple flowers and silver-gray foliage, which are adapted to coastal environments. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing on rocky shores or sandy beaches. Its structure consists of a basal rosette of leaves with a flowering stem that bears several flower heads.

Limbarda crithmifolia is often used in ornamental gardening due to its striking appearance and hardiness.
