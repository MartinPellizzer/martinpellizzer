Lapeirousia macrospatha

Lapeirousia macrospatha, commonly known as the Giant Cape Bell, is a striking member of the Iridaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and large, bell-shaped flowers that can reach up to 15 cm in length. The plant produces showy, often brightly colored blooms in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, forming a dense rosette at the base.

Lapeirousia macrospatha thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
