Leptecophylla pogonocalyx subsp. pogonocalyx

Leptecophylla pogonocalyx subsp.

pogonocalyx, commonly known as the coastal hakea, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its woody stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, pink or white flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom during the cooler months. It is adapted to sandy soils and coastal environments, showing resistance to salt spray and wind.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
