Linaria genistifolia var. linifolia

Linaria genistifolia var. linifolia, commonly known as the slender toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces slender, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
