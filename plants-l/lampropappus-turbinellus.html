Lampropappus turbinellus

Lampropappus turbinellus, commonly known as the turbinellus thistle, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive foliage. The plant features tall, spiny stems and narrow, silvery-green leaves. Its showy purple to pink flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in prairies and along roadsides.
