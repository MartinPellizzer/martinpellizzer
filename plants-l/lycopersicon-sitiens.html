Lycopersicon sitiens

Lycopersicon sitiens, commonly known as the desert tomato, is a species native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a small, shrub-like plant with hairy stems and small, lobed leaves adapted to withstand drought conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish-orange fruits that are edible and have a tangy, acidic flavor. Its botanical features include a woody stem, hairy foliage, and a preference for well-drained soils.

Lycopersicon sitiens is often found in rocky or sandy habitats and is considered a hardy, resilient species.
