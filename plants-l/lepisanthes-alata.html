Lepisanthes alata

Lepisanthes alata, commonly known as the veined orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive veined leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical rainforests. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with a unique structure that attracts specific pollinators.

Lepisanthes alata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
