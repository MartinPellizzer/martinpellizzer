Ligustrum oviforme

Ligustrum oviforme, commonly known as the oval privet, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy on top with a lighter underside. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by dark blue berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Ligustrum oviforme is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
