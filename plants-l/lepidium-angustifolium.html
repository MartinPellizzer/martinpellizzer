Lepidium angustifolium

Lepidium angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard family plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or arid environments. It produces small, yellow flowers and has narrow, linear leaves that are usually less than 1 inch in width.

The plant is sometimes used in horticulture for its ornamental value and can also serve as a host plant for certain insects.
