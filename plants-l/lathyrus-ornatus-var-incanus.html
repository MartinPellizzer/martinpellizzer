Lathyrus ornatus var. incanus

Lathyrus ornatus var. incanus, commonly known as the silver-haired sweet pea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking silvery-white hairs that cover the stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate foliage and long-lasting blooms.
