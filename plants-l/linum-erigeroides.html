Linum erigeroides

Linum erigeroides, commonly known as the upright flax, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, blue to purple flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Linum erigeroides is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its delicate flowers and adaptability to various soil types.
