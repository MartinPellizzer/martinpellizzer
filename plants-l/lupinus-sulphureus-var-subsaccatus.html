Lupinus sulphureus var. subsaccatus

Lupinus sulphureus var. subsaccatus, commonly known as the yellow lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it a common sight in open grasslands and disturbed areas. It has a robust root system that helps prevent soil erosion and is often used in ecological restoration projects.

The species is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.
