Lonicera porphyrantha

Lonicera porphyrantha, commonly known as the purple honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple to reddish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its berries, which are orange-red, are typically produced in late summer and are edible for birds.

Lonicera porphyrantha is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
