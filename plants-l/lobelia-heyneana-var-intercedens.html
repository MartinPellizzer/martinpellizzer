Lobelia heyneana var. intercedens

Lobelia heyneana var. intercedens, commonly known as the pink lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shady habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and wetland restoration projects.
