Linaria sessilis f. majdaculica

Linaria sessilis f. majdaculica, commonly known as the Alpine toadflax, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lanceolate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The flowers are typically yellow or orange, forming in clustered racemes, and are characterized by their distinctive bilateral symmetry. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and slopes.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
