Lepanthes quadridentata

Lepanthes quadridentata, commonly known as the four-toothed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as cloud forests, where it thrives on tree bark or in rocky crevices. Its flowers are usually small, with intricate patterns and colors ranging from white to violet.

Lepanthes quadridentata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
