Lisianthius capitatus

Lisianthius capitatus, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert or semi-desert environments. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in late summer and early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping in arid regions.
