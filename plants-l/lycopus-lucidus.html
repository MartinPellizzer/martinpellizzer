Lycopus lucidus

Lycopus lucidus, commonly known as shining blueweed, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant features slender, branching stems covered in small, opposite leaves that are lance-shaped and slightly hairy. Its flowers are blue to purple, clustered in dense spikes, and bloom in late summer.

Lycopus lucidus is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
