Lupinus humicola

Lupinus humicola, commonly known as the bog lupine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and bog habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with thread-like leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. Lupinus humicola is adapted to nutrient-poor, acidic soils and plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.

It is also a valuable plant for pollinators and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
