Luzula densiflora

Luzula densiflora, commonly known as denseflower woodrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features tufted, grass-like foliage with narrow, dark green leaves that are densely packed at the base. Its flowering stems bear clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences containing numerous tiny, white to pale green flowers.

Luzula densiflora is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded, wet conditions.
