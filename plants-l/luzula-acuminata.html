Luzula acuminata

Luzula acuminata, commonly known as slender woodrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. The plant features grass-like leaves that are narrow and linear, with a bluish-green hue. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Luzula acuminata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticultural settings for its ability to thrive in partially shaded conditions.
