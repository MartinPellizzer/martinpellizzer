Limnocharis martii

Limnocharis martii, commonly known as water chestnut, is a aquatic plant native to South America.

It has submerged and floating leaves that help it thrive in still or slow-moving freshwater environments. The plant produces small white flowers that bloom above the water surface. Its fruit is a spiny, floating structure that contains seeds.

Limnocharis martii is often cultivated for its edible young shoots and tubers, which are used in local cuisines.
