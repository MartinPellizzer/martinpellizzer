Lycopodioides intertexta

Lycopodioides intertexta, commonly known as the mossy clubmoss, is a member of the family Lycopodiaceae and is characterized by its small, creeping growth habit.

It produces spore-bearing structures called strobila, which are typically clustered at the tips of its stems. The plant has a distinct root system and possesses microphylls, which are simple, needle-like leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest floors or rocky crevices.

Lycopodioides intertexta plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms.
