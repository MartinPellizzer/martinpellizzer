Lisianthius browallioides

Lisianthius browallioides, commonly known as the coastal lisianthius, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal environments with high salt exposure and strong winds. Its flowering period typically occurs during the summer months, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in coastal gardens.
