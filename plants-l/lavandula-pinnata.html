Lavandula pinnata

Lavandula pinnata, commonly known as narrowleaf lavender, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features aromatic, silver-gray foliage with narrow, linear leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces spikes of purple or pink flowers during the summer months, which are rich in essential oils. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its botanical characteristics make it both ornamental and useful in herbal applications.
