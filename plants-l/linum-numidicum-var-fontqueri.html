Linum numidicum var. fontqueri

Linum numidicum var. fontqueri, commonly known as the Fontqueri flax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in dye production.
