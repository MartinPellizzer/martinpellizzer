Lilium brownii

Lilium brownii, commonly known as the Brown's lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces bulbous roots and has lance-shaped, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Lilium brownii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
