Lippia stachyoides var. guajajarana

Lippia stachyoides var. guajajarana, commonly known as Guajajarana, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and digestive properties.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a garden plant.
