Lycopodioides decrescens

Lycopodioides decrescens, commonly known as the creeping clubmoss, is a member of the Lycopodiaceae family and is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and spore-producing structures.

It is a terrestrial plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or rocky areas. The plant produces strobili, or cone-like structures, which contain the spores for reproduction. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor the plant in soil while absorbing nutrients.

Lycopodioides decrescens plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for small invertebrates.
