Lactuca scariola subsp. integrata

Lactuca scariola subsp.

integrata, commonly known as the European leaf lettuce, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family. It is characterized by its broad, dark green leaves that are often slightly hairy and have a slightly bitter taste.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and other open areas. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

This subspecies is often considered a weed but has historical uses in traditional medicine and cuisine.
