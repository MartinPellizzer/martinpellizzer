Linaria saxatilis var. tournefortii

Linaria saxatilis var. tournefortii, commonly known as the rock snapdragon, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and vibrant yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are spurred at the base, a distinguishing feature of the genus Linaria.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a border plant.
