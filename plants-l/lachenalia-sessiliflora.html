Lachenalia sessiliflora

Lachenalia sessiliflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lachenalia, is a bulbous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, grass-like foliage and clusters of tubular, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring or early summer, producing flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated by gardeners interested in succulent and bulbous plants.
