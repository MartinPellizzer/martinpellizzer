Lomaria rotundifolia

Lomaria rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaf lomaria, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its rounded, broad leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in the spring. Lomaria rotundifolia thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and requires a sunny to partially shaded environment.

It is often found in moist, woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
