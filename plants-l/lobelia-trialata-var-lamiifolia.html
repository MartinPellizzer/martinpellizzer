Lobelia trialata var. lamiifolia

Lobelia trialata var. lamiifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear whorled clusters of small, tubular blue to purple flowers. The foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

This variety is distinguished by its delicate appearance and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
