Lapeirousia verecunda

Lapeirousia verecunda, commonly known as the Cape bell orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant, bell-shaped flowers that come in shades of yellow, orange, and red, often with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils in the fynbos biome. It features a short, hairy stem with narrow, grass-like leaves.

Lapeirousia verecunda is known for its ephemeral blooming cycle, often flowering after rainfall in late summer.
