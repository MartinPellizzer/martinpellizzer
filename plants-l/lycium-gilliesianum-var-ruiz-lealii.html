Lycium gilliesianum var. ruiz-lealii

Lycium gilliesianum var. ruiz-lealii, commonly known as the Chilean boxthorn, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, fleshy berries. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptability to dry conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellowish-green with prominent stamens.

The berries, which are edible when ripe, have a tart flavor and are sometimes used in local cuisines.
