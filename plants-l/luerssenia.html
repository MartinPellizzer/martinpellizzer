Luerssenia

Luerssenia, commonly known as the moonflower, is a genus of flowering plants in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night and close during the day. The leaves are typically broad and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant is native to parts of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in warm, humid climates.

Luerssenia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its unique nocturnal blooming habit.
