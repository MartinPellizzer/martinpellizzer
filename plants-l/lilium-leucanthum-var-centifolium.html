Lilium leucanthum var. centifolium

Lilium leucanthum var. centifolium, commonly known as the white lily, is a striking perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers with a distinctive yellow throat, which bloom in late summer. The plant features strap-like, grassy leaves that form a basal rosette, and it produces a tall, slender stem that supports its floral display. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and historical use in floral arrangements.
