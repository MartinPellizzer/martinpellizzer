Levisticum grandiflorum

Levisticum grandiflorum, commonly known as giant parsley, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, umbrella-like compound leaves that are finely divided. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its aromatic foliage has a mild anise-like scent, making it popular in culinary and medicinal uses.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in meadows and woodland edges.
