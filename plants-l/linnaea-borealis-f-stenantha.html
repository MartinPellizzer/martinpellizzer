Linnaea borealis f. stenantha

Linnaea borealis f. stenantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaf twinflower, is a rare and distinctive variant of the twinflower plant.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring. This plant is native to the boreal and subarctic regions of North America and Eurasia. It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it a unique subject for ecological and taxonomic study.
