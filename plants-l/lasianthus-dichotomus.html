Lasianthus dichotomus

Lasianthus dichotomus, commonly known as the two-branched beauty, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions for horticultural purposes.
