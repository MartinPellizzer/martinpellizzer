Leontodon sublyratus

Leontodon sublyratus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawk's-beard, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow, with ray florets that give it a dandelion-like appearance.

Leontodon sublyratus is often found in dry grasslands and rocky soils, thriving in temperate climates.
