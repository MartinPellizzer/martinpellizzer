Lophanthus rugosus var. hypoleucus

Lophanthus rugosus var. hypoleucus, commonly known as the white-flowered lophanthus, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems, which are often covered in a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape, with a slightly serrated margin.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and delicate floral display.
