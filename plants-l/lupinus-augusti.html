Lupinus augusti

Lupinus augusti, commonly known as the August lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of showy, pea-like flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. Lupinus augusti typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in coastal and mountainous regions.

Its ecological importance includes providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for various wildlife species.
