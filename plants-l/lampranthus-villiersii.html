Lampranthus villiersii

Lampranthus villiersii, commonly known as the "Rainbow Ghost" or "Cape Rainbow," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that range from red to orange and yellow, which are often more showy than the small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant has fleshy, thick leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in arid conditions. It typically blooms during the summer months, producing clusters of tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and birds.

Due to its ornamental value, it is popular among gardeners who cultivate it in well-draining soil and full sun.
