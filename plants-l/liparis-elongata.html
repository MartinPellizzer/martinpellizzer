Liparis elongata

Liparis elongata, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and linear, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes, often blooming in late spring to early summer. Native to various regions in Asia, it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices.

Due to its delicate appearance and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
