Laurus bejolghota

Laurus bejolghota, commonly known as the Indian laurel, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a glossy, dark green appearance. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, round berries.

The botanical characteristics of Laurus bejolghota make it a notable species in both ornamental gardening and traditional medicine.
