Lycopersicon esculentum provar. pluriloculare

Lycopersicon esculentum provar. pluriloculare, commonly known as the multi-chambered tomato, is a botanical variant of the domesticated tomato.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its unique fruit structure with multiple chambers. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with hairy stems and lobed leaves. Its flowers are yellow and borne in clusters, and the fruit is a fleshy, berry-like structure that ripens to red.

This variety is valued for its genetic diversity and potential use in breeding programs for improved tomato cultivars.
