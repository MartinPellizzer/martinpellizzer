Limatodis grata

Limatodis grata, commonly known as the graceful lima vine, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or cream markings. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, bean-like pods containing edible seeds. Limatodis grata thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and fragrant blooms.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.
