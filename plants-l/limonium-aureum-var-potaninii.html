Limonium aureum var. potaninii

Limonium aureum var. potaninii, commonly known as the golden sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, showing a preference for well-drained conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery bloom.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and hardy nature.
