Loranthus basilanensis

Loranthus basilanensis, commonly known as the Basilan mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees in the Philippines.

It belongs to the Loranthaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant relies on its host for water and nutrients, while it provides some photosynthetic support through its own leaves. It plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its native habitat, contributing to biodiversity and nutrient cycling.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
