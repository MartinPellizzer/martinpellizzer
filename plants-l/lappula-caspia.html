Lappula caspia

Lappula caspia, commonly known as Caspian lappula, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in saline or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often along the stems. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Lappula caspia is part of the Camelot family (Polygonaceae) and is noted for its ecological significance in stabilizing sandy soils.
