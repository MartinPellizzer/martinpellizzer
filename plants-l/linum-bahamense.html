Linum bahamense

Linum bahamense, commonly known as Bahamian flax, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly the Bahamas.

It is a herbaceous annual that grows up to 30 centimeters tall, featuring slender stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal areas and disturbed sites.

Linum bahamense is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
