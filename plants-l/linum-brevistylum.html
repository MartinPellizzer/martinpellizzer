Linum brevistylum

Linum brevistylum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flax, is a herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Linaceae family, which includes other species of flax. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and produces small, yellow flowers. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and it has a fibrous stem that can be harvested for its fibers.

Linum brevistylum is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
