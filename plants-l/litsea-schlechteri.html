Litsea schlechteri

Litsea schlechteri, commonly known as the Schlechter's litsea, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and avocado trees. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, narrow leaves and small, clustered white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a drupe, typically containing a single seed, and it is often found in moist, shaded forest environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
