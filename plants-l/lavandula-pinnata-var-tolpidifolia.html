Lavandula pinnata var. tolpidifolia

Lavandula pinnata var. tolpidifolia, commonly known as the Mexican lavender, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It features aromatic, silver-gray foliage and produces spikes of pale purple to lavender-colored flowers. The plant is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fragrant properties.

This variety is noted for its hardiness and adaptability to dry, sunny conditions.
