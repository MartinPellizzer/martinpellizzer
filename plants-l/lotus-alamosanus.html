Lotus alamosanus

Lotus alamosanus, commonly known as the Alamos lotus, is a species of aquatic plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in shallow, freshwater habitats such as ponds and lakes. Its leaves are broad and floating, while its roots are anchored in the substrate.

Lotus alamosanus plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various organisms.
