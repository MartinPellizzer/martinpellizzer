Lindsaea decaryana

Lindsaea decaryana, commonly known as the silver lace fern, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are often covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Native to tropical regions of the Americas, this fern thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread and form dense colonies.

Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are typically found on the underside of its leaves, contributing to its propagation in natural habitats.
