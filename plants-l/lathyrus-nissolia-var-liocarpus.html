Lathyrus nissolia var. liocarpus

Lathyrus nissolia var. liocarpus, commonly known as the red-flowered grass vetch, is a perennial legume native to parts of Asia.

It features slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces vibrant red or pink pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer. Its small, elongated pods contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
