Lortetia biflora

Lortetia biflora, commonly known as the two-flowered lortetia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in a fine layer of hairs.

Lortetia biflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
