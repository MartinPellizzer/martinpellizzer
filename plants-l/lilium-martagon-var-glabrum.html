Lilium martagon var. glabrum

Lilium martagon var. glabrum, commonly known as the Turk's cap lily, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, recurved, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces a tall, slender stem with lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. It prefers well-drained, fertile soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its striking floral display.
