Lychnis laciniata

Lychnis laciniata, commonly known as the fringed campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer, arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive fringed appearance, giving it its common name.

Lychnis laciniata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
