Lobelia mildbraedii

Lobelia mildbraedii, commonly known as the mildbraed's lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces seeds in small, capsule-like structures.

Lobelia mildbraedii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
