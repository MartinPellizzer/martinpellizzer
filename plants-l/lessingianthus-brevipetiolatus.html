Lessingianthus brevipetiolatus

Lessingianthus brevipetiolatus, commonly known as the short-petioled sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in shrubland and grassland habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are generally lance-shaped with hairy surfaces, and the stems are often hairy as well.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
