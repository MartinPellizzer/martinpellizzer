Lupinus chihuahuensis

Lupinus chihuahuensis, commonly known as Chihuahua lupine, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces cylindrical seed pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Lupinus chihuahuensis plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest for its potential in ecological restoration projects.
