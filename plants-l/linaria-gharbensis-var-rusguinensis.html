Linaria gharbensis var. rusguinensis

Linaria gharbensis var. rusguinensis, commonly known as the Rusguin's toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of showy, yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, thriving in temperate and alpine climates. This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
