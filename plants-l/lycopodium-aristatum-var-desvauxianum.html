Lycopodium aristatum var. desvauxianum

Lycopodium aristatum var. desvauxianum, commonly known as the velvet clubmoss, is a low-growing evergreen fern-like plant native to the western United States.

It features dense, dark green foliage with a velvety texture, and its stems are typically clustered and short. The plant produces spore-bearing cones, which are clustered at the tips of its stems, contributing to its reproductive strategy. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
