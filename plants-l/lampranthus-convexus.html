Lampranthus convexus

Lampranthus convexus, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like birds and insects. The plant has fleshy, cylindrical stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, reducing water loss through transpiration.

Lampranthus convexus is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant appearance and low maintenance requirements.
