Lonicera saxatilis

Lonicera saxatilis, commonly known as the rock honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and its ability to thrive in rocky or gravelly soils. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Lonicera saxatilis is often found in alpine or subalpine environments, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
