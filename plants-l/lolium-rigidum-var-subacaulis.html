Lolium rigidum var. subacaulis

Lolium rigidum var. subacaulis, commonly known as rigid fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and stiff, wiry stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

This variety is often found in pastures and on roadsides, where it can form dense stands.
