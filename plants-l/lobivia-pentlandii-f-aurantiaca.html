Lobivia pentlandii f. aurantiaca

Lobivia pentlandii f. aurantiaca, commonly known as the Orange Ghost Cactus, is a rare and striking hybrid of the genus Lobivia.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and vibrant orange to red flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant features a short, thick stem with spiny, ribbed pads that help it store water in arid environments. Native to the Atacama Desert in Chile, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is highly sought after by cactus enthusiasts and collectors.
