Lessingianthus correntinus

Lessingianthus correntinus, commonly known as the Brazilian sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. It produces small, spherical fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in its native habitat.
