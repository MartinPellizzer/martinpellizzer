Laurus paniculigera

Laurus paniculigera, commonly known as the panicled laurel, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other well-known plants like laurel and avocado. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are glossy and aromatic, and its clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. It produces small, round berries that are typically green when immature and turn dark when mature.

Laurus paniculigera is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and fragrant properties.
