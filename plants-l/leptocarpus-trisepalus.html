Leptocarpus trisepalus

Leptocarpus trisepalus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sea bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Pacific Ocean.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, bean-like pods. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, where it thrives in sandy or muddy soils. Its leaves are usually trifoliate, with three small, oval-shaped leaflets.
