Lampadaria

Lampadaria, commonly known as the candleflower, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Asteraceae.

These plants are characterized by their showy, daisy-like flowers that often display vibrant colors such as yellow, orange, or red. The leaves are typically narrow and linear, arranged in a basal rosette, which helps in water conservation. Lampadaria species are native to various regions in the Americas and are often found in dry, open habitats.

Their flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to their ecological significance.
