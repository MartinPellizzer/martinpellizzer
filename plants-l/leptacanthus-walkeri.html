Leptacanthus walkeri

Leptacanthus walkeri, commonly known as the walking fern, is a unique plant characterized by its creeping rhizomes that allow it to spread along the ground.

It possesses a distinctive frond structure with a central stalk and multiple fiddlehead-like clusters of leaves. The plant is known for its ability to regenerate from its rhizomes, making it resilient to damage. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest floors or rocky crevices.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is notable for its primitive reproductive structures.
