Lupinus argenteus subsp. spathulatus

Lupinus argenteus subsp.

spathulatus, commonly known as silver lupine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to western North America. It is characterized by its silvery-blue foliage and spicate inflorescences that bear small, pea-like flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of oval to lance-shaped leaflets. Lupinus argenteus subsp.

spathulatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance.
