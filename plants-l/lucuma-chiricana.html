Lucuma chiricana

Lucuma chiricana, commonly known as the Peruvian sweet potato, is a tropical plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It is a member of the Convolvulaceae family and produces large, yellow-orange tubers that are rich in nutrients. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments with well-drained soil and moderate rainfall. Its tubers are valued for their sweet, nutty flavor and are used in various traditional dishes.
