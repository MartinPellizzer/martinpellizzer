Linum flavum var. pubescens

Linum flavum var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent flax, is a variant of the flax plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy, pubescent stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant belongs to the Linaceae family and is typically found in dry, sandy soils. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in textile production.
