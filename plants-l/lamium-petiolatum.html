Lamium petiolatum

Lamium petiolatum, commonly known as purple deadnettle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features opposite, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive purple underside, and its small, tubular flowers are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stems. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands, stream banks, and gardens. It is a member of the mint family (Lamiaceae), which is characterized by aromatic foliage and square stems.

Lamium petiolatum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to suppress weeds in garden settings.
