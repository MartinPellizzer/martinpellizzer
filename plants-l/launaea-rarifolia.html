Launaea rarifolia

Launaea rarifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, silver-green leaves and produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a fibrous root system that helps it survive in dry conditions. It is often found in rocky soils and can thrive in disturbed habitats.

Launaea rarifolia is also noted for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
