Leptospermum flavescens var. angustifolia

Leptospermum flavescens var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf tea tree, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically narrow and have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its aromatic leaves are often used in traditional medicine and for making herbal teas.

The species is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.
