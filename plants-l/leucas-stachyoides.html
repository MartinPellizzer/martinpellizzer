Leucas stachyoides

Leucas stachyoides, commonly known as the hairy leucas, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Leucas stachyoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
