Lilium ukeyuri

Lilium ukeyuri, commonly known as the Korean lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South Korea.

It is characterized by its striking white petals with a pinkish tinge and a tall, slender stem that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Lilium ukeyuri is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegance.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sunlight, thriving in temperate climates.
