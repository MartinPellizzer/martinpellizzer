Luehea candicans var. gracilis

Luehea candicans var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender yellow bell tree, is a species native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and pendant, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical with a glossy surface. The plant thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland tropical forests.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has limited use in traditional medicine.
