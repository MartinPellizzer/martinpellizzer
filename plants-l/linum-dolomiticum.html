Linum dolomiticum

Linum dolomiticum, commonly known as the dolomitic flax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, reflecting its adaptation to alkaline environments. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered with fine hairs.

Linum dolomiticum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
