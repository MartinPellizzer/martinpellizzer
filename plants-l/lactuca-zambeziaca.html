Lactuca zambeziaca

Lactuca zambeziaca, commonly known as the Zambezi lettuce, is a species of flowering plant in the daisy family, Asteraceae.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly around the Zambezi River region, and thrives in moist, sandy soils. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and has a rosette of broad, wavy leaves that are often used in local cuisines. It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.

Lactuca zambeziaca plays a role in supporting local biodiversity by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
