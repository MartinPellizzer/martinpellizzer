Lomaria confinis

Lomaria confinis, commonly known as the narrowleaf lomaria, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale blue flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Lomaria confinis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
