Limnia guadalupensis

Limnia guadalupensis, commonly known as the Guadalupe waterflea, is a small freshwater crustacean belonging to the order Cladocera.

It is characterized by its translucent exoskeleton and a distinctive, segmented body structure. This species is typically found in temporary pools and slow-moving streams, where it plays a role in the aquatic food web. Limnia guadalupensis is known for its rapid reproductive cycle, which allows it to thrive in ephemeral water habitats.

Its presence is often an indicator of water quality and ecological health in its environment.
