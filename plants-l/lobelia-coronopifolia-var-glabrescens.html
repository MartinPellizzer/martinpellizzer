Lobelia coronopifolia var. glabrescens

Lobelia coronopifolia var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth-leaf lobelia, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, erect stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are smooth and glossy. The plant produces striking spikes of tubular, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in woodland gardens.
