Lobivia sublimiflora

Lobivia sublimiflora, commonly known as the snow-flower lobivia, is a species of cactus native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded growth habit and thick, ribbed stems that store water. The plant produces small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in winter, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a sought-after species among cactus enthusiasts and botanists.
