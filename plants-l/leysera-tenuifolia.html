Leysera tenuifolia

Leysera tenuifolia, commonly known as the slender leaf lily, is a flowering plant native to the humid lowland forests of Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial herb with slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, often appearing in late spring or early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
