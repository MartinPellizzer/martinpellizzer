Leontodon taraxaci

Leontodon taraxaci, commonly known as the meadow dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in open grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while its flowers are bright yellow, followed by fluffy white seeds.

Leontodon taraxaci is known for its resilience and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
