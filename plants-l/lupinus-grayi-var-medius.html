Lupinus grayi var. medius

Lupinus grayi var. medius, commonly known as the inland lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical pea-like flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, spiny pods that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind.

The species is notable for its ecological importance in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinator populations.
