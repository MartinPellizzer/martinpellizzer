Linum alpestre

Linum alpestre, commonly known as alpine flax, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Linaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and often branched.

Linum alpestre is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
