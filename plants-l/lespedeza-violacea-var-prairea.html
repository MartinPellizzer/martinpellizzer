Lespedeza violacea var. prairea

Lespedeza violacea var. prairea, commonly known as prairie lespedeza, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, violet-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces trifoliate leaves and has a fibrous root system that helps prevent soil erosion. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a valuable species for native plant gardens and restoration projects.

Its adaptability and ornamental value make it a popular choice for ecological landscaping.
