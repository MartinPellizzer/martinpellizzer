Leucanthemum coronopifolium subsp. ceratophylloides

Leucanthemum coronopifolium subsp.

ceratophylloides, commonly known as the starry groundsel, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It produces a capsule fruit containing numerous tiny seeds.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes many flowering plants with composite flower heads.
