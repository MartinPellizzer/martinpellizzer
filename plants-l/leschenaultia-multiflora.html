Leschenaultia multiflora

Leschenaultia multiflora, commonly known as the bluebells of the mountains, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, temperate climates. The plant features slender stems and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped blue or purple flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.
