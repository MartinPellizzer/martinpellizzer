Lycopodium treitubensis

Lycopodium treitubensis, commonly known as the Treitubens clubmoss, is a species of primitive vascular plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its small, spore-bearing cones and its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments. The plant typically grows in dense mats, forming a low-growing ground cover in forest understories. Its leaves are narrow and spirally arranged, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

L. treitubensis plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
