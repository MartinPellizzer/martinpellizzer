Linum rhodopeum

Linum rhodopeum, commonly known as the Rhodope flax, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and branching.

Linum rhodopeum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in fiber production.
