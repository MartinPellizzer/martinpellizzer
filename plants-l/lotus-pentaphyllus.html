Lotus pentaphyllus

Lotus pentaphyllus, commonly known as the five-leaf lotus, is a species of aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking pink to red flowers that bloom above the water surface. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often submerged, while its flowering stems rise above the water. Lotus pentaphyllus thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

It is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its cultural and medicinal significance in traditional practices.
