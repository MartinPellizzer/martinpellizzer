Lomelosia × auriaultii

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Lomelosia alpina and Lomelosia stenophylla, showcasing a combination of traits from both parent species. The plant is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are often a vibrant green with a waxy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Due to its limited natural range and rarity in the wild, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
