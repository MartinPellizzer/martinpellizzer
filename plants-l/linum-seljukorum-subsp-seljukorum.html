Linum seljukorum subsp. seljukorum

Linum seljukorum subsp.

seljukorum, commonly known as the Seljuk flax, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces clusters of blue or violet flowers, which are typically tubular in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun exposure.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in traditional dye production.
