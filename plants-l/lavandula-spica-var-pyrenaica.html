Lavandula spica var. pyrenaica

Lavandula spica var. pyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean lavender, is a perennial shrub native to the Pyrenees region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and spikes of fragrant purple flowers. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, contributing to its aromatic properties. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild lavender scent, which is less overpowering than other lavender species.
