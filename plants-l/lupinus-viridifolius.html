Lupinus viridifolius

Lupinus viridifolius, commonly known as the green-leaved lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green, giving it its common name. The plant produces tall spikes of blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for xeriscaping.

Lupinus viridifolius is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits surrounding vegetation.
