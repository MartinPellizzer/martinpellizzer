Laelia cardimii

Laelia cardimii, commonly known as the Cardimii Cymbidium, is a species of orchid native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions. The plant produces elegant, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, often with intricate patterns on the petals. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in its native habitat's variable climate.

Laelia cardimii is valued in horticulture for its beauty and adaptability to cultivation.
