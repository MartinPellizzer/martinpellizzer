Linum austriacum var. alpinum

Linum austriacum var. alpinum, commonly known as the alpine flax, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It is characterized by its delicate blue to violet flowers and lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. It is a member of the Linaceae family and is known for its ornamental value.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
