Lahaya

Lahaya, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a tropical plant native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its bright orange to red floral bracts that surround small, yellow tubular flowers. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Lahaya is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
