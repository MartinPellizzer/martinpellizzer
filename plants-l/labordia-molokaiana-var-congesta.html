Labordia molokaiana var. congesta

Labordia molokaiana var. congesta, commonly known as the Molokaʻi hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian island of Molokaʻi.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and is often found growing in coastal areas and rocky habitats. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, and it is adapted to the unique climate of the Hawaiian Islands.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
