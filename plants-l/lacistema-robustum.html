Lacistema robustum

Lacistema robustum, commonly known as the giant morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that open in the morning and close by midday. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces long, winding stems that can reach several meters in length. Its botanical features include a robust root system and fibrous, hairy stems.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
