Lepanthes scolops

Lepanthes scolops, commonly known as the violet orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as cloud forests, where it thrives in the understory. Its flowers are usually small, with vivid colors ranging from purple to white, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Lepanthes scolops is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
