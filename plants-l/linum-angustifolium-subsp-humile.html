Linum angustifolium subsp. humile

Linum angustifolium subsp.

humile, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flax, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and produces blue or violet flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, coastal environments. It is a perennial herb that can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
