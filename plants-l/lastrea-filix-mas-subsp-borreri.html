Lastrea filix-mas subsp. borreri

Lastrea filix-mas subsp.

borreri, commonly known as the Borreri's snake plant, is a succulent perennial native to southern Africa. It features upright, sword-shaped leaves that are typically green with white or silver variegation.

The plant is characterized by its ability to store water in its fleshy leaves, making it drought-tolerant. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers on spikes, which are usually white or greenish in color.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of care in indoor gardening.
