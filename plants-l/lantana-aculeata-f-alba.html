Lantana aculeata f. alba

Lantana aculeata f. alba, commonly known as white lantana, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, branching structure and small, clustered flowers. The leaves are typically opposite, oval-shaped, and covered in fine hairs. The white flowers bloom in clusters and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
