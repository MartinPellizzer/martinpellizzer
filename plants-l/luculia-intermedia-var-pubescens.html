Luculia intermedia var. pubescens

Luculia intermedia var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy Chinese date.

This plant is a shrub native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers. It produces small, fleshy fruits that are often eaten raw or used in traditional medicine. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
