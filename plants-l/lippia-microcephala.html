Lippia microcephala

Lippia microcephala, commonly known as dwarf lemon verbena, is a small shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lemon-scented leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of essential oils. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for Mediterranean climates.

Its botanical features include hairy stems and opposite leaf arrangement, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
