Leianthus umbellatus

Leianthus umbellatus, commonly known as the white sand dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves and a cluster of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and full sun, often found in desert or semi-arid environments. Its flowers are typically arranged in an umbel, giving it the species name "umbellatus.".

The plant is adapted to survive in harsh conditions with minimal water, making it a resilient member of its ecosystem.
