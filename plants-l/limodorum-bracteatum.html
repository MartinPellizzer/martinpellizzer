Limodorum bracteatum

Limodorum bracteatum, commonly known as the bracted lily of the valley, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Liliaceae and is characterized by its clustered, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Its ornamental value is due to its fragrant, white flowers and its ability to form dense colonies in suitable habitats.
