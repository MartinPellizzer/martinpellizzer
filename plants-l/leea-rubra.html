Leea rubra

Leea rubra, commonly known as the red-leaved leeah, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the family Leeaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often reddish in color. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, round fruits. Leea rubra is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
