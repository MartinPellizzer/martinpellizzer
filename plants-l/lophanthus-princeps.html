Lophanthus princeps

Lophanthus princeps, commonly known as the royal lophanthus, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, elongated fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Lophanthus princeps thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.

Its unique floral structure and ornamental value make it a valuable addition to botanical collections.
