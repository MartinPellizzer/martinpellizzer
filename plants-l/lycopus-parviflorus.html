Lycopus parviflorus

Lycopus parviflorus, commonly known as narrow-leaved bugleweed, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, blue to purple flowers bloom in clusters during the summer months.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and is often found in wetland habitats.
