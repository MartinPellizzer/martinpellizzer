Liliastrum lusitanicum

Liliastrum lusitanicum, commonly known as the Portuguese lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its strap-shaped leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks. Its blooming period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Liliastrum lusitanicum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
