Lantana cordobensis

Lantana cordobensis, commonly known as the Cordoba lantana, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring dense foliage and showy clusters of small, colorful flowers. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are usually yellow or orange, arranged in terminal umbels. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
