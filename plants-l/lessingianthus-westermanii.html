Lessingianthus westermanii

Lessingianthus westermanii, commonly known as the Westerman's sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant features bright yellow ray flowers surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

The leaves are usually alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.
