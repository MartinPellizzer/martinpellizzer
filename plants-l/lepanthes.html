Lepanthes

Lepanthes, commonly known as the "Lady's Slipper Orchid," is a genus of orchids native to the Americas.

These plants are characterized by their showy, often colorful flowers that resemble small slippers, hence their common name. Lepanthes species typically have aerial roots and grow as epiphytes or lithophytes, attaching themselves to trees or rocks. They are found in a variety of habitats, from tropical rainforests to cloud forests.

The genus includes over 200 species, many of which are prized for their ornamental value in horticulture.
