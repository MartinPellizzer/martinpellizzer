Lobelia graniticola

Lobelia graniticola, commonly known as the granite lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils, often found in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically narrow and succulent, adapted to conserve water in its harsh habitat.

Lobelia graniticola is a hardy species that demonstrates resilience to drought and extreme temperatures.
