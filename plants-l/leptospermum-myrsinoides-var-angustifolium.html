Leptospermum myrsinoides var. angustifolium

Leptospermum myrsinoides var. angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved tea-tree, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features small, glossy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and emit a faint aromatic scent. The plant produces small, white, five-petaled flowers that bloom in spring, attracting nectar-feeding insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
