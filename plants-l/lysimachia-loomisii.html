Lysimachia loomisii

Lysimachia loomisii, commonly known as Loomis' chickweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, rounded leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of tiny, white to pinkish flowers throughout the growing season. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit.

Lysimachia loomisii is often found in areas with rich, organic soil and is relatively easy to cultivate in gardens with appropriate conditions.
