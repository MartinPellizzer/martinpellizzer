Lotus strictus

Lotus strictus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved water lily, is a aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves and showy white or pink flowers that bloom above the water surface. The plant has a complex root system that anchors it in shallow water and helps absorb nutrients. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and can grow up to 30 cm in diameter.

Lotus strictus plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
