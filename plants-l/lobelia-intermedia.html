Lobelia intermedia

Lobelia intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring slender stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking blue to purple tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Lobelia intermedia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
