Liatris squarrosa var. glabrata

Liatris squarrosa var. glabrata, commonly known as the glabrous blazing star, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright, sparsely branched stems that bear whorled clusters of purple to pinkish-purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped, gray-green leaves that are usually hairless, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
