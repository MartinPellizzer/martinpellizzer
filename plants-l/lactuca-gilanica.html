Lactuca gilanica

Lactuca gilanica, commonly known as Gilan lettuce, is a wild species native to the Caspian Sea region.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its rosette of elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate climates and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, arranged in a loose cluster at the top of the stem.

Lactuca gilanica is part of the Asteraceae family and shares some characteristics with cultivated lettuce species.
