Leptogramma gymnocarpa subsp. amabilis

Leptogramma gymnocarpa subsp.

amabilis, commonly known as the silky sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa. It features small, fleshy, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters during the winter months.

The plant has succulent leaves that help it retain moisture in dry conditions. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand periods of drought.

Its unique morphology and ecological niche make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
