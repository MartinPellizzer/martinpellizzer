Liriodendron tulipifera var. acutilobum

Liriodendron tulipifera var. acutilobum, commonly known as the narrow-lobed tulip tree, is a variant of the tulip tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tulip-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are typically narrower and more pointed compared to the standard variety, giving it a unique appearance. This plant prefers moist, well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

It is often found in riparian zones and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits.
