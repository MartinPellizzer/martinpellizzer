Lycopodium clavatum var. asiaticum

Lycopodium clavatum var. asiaticum, commonly known as the Asian clubmoss, is a small, evergreen fern-like plant native to parts of Asia.

It features delicate, spirally arranged leaves that form a dense, cushion-like structure near the ground. The plant produces spore-bearing cones, or strobili, which are clustered at the base of the foliage. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices.

Its unique morphology and reproductive structures make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
