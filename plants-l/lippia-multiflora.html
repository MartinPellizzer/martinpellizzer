Lippia multiflora

Lippia multiflora, commonly known as Mexican blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features opposite, narrow leaves and produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters. It is often found in open areas, such as roadsides and disturbed soils, and is adapted to thrive in warm, sunny conditions.

Lippia multiflora is also known for its aromatic foliage and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
