Lycopodium setaceum var. pulcherrimum

Lycopodium setaceum var. pulcherrimum, commonly known as the beautiful clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit and the presence of spirally arranged, scale-like leaves. The plant produces strobili, or cone-like structures, which contain the sporangia that release spores for reproduction. It typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or rocky crevices.

This variant is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
