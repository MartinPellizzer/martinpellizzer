Lonicera × muscaviensis

Lonicera × muscaviensis, commonly known as the musk vine, is a hybrid plant belonging to the honeysuckle family (Caprifoliaceae).

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to yellow, often emitting a sweet, musky scent. The plant is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It typically grows as a trailing or climbing vine, reaching lengths of several meters.

Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
