Lachemilla laxa

Lachemilla laxa, commonly known as the creeping sedum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and ground cover.

Lachemilla laxa is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to prevent soil erosion.
