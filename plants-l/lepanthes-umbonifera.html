Lepanthes umbonifera

Lepanthes umbonifera, commonly known as the crowned violet orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet or purple flowers with a prominent central bump, or umbon, on the lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical forests. Its leaves are usually broad and dark green, providing a lush backdrop to its vibrant blooms.

Lepanthes umbonifera is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and unique floral structure.
