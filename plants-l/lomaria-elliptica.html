Lomaria elliptica

Lomaria elliptica, commonly known as the narrowleaf lomaria, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has narrow, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, yellowish flowers appear in clusters and bloom in the summer months.

Lomaria elliptica is known for its resilience to drought and poor soil conditions, making it a hardy species in arid environments.
