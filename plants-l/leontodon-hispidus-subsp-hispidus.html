Leontodon hispidus subsp. hispidus

Leontodon hispidus subsp.

hispidus, commonly known as the hairy hawk's-beard, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It typically grows in dry meadows, grasslands, and rocky soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm.

The plant features a robust stem with hairy leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its infructescences are characterized by small, bristly achenes surrounded by persistent, hairy pappus.

The species is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
