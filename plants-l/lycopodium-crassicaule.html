Lycopodium crassicaule

Lycopodium crassicaule, commonly known as the broad-stemmed clubmoss, is a member of the Lycopodiaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage.

It is a small, herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. The plant produces spore-bearing cones called strobila, which are clustered at the tips of its upright stems. Lycopodium crassicaule has a distinctive appearance with its thick, fleshy stems and bright green, scale-like leaves.

It plays an important ecological role in providing habitat and moisture retention in its native habitats.
