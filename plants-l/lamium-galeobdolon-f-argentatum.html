Lamium galeobdolon f. argentatum

Lamium galeobdolon f. argentatum, commonly known as silver leaf dead nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its striking silver-white variegation on its foliage.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal and ornamental value. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a soft, velvety texture and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of pink or purple. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often used in garden landscapes for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.

This cultivar is prized for its unique coloration and adaptability to various soil conditions.
