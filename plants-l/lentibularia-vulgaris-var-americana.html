Lentibularia vulgaris var. americana

Lentibularia vulgaris var. americana, commonly known as the American bladderwort, is a small aquatic plant belonging to the family Lentibulariaceae.

It is characterized by its tiny, bladder-like structures called bladders, which are used to trap small aquatic organisms. The plant typically grows in shallow, still waters such as ponds and ditches. It has slender, branching stems and small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged.

Its botanical features make it an important part of aquatic ecosystems, contributing to nutrient cycling and providing habitat for microorganisms.
