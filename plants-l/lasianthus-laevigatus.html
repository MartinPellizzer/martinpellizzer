Lasianthus laevigatus

Lasianthus laevigatus, commonly known as the smooth-leaved lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its flowers are often pink or purple, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Lasianthus laevigatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
