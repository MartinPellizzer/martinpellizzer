Ligustrum rugosulum

Ligustrum rugosulum, commonly known as the roughleaf privet, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features small, dark green, ovate leaves with a rough, textured surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by black, spherical berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often used as an ornamental plant in landscaping.

Due to its adaptability, it can become invasive in certain regions if not managed properly.
