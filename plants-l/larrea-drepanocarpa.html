Larrea drepanocarpa

Larrea drepanocarpa, commonly known as the desert broom, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish-green leaves and fragrant, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant is well adapted to harsh desert conditions, with deep roots that help it survive in dry environments. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert wildlife.

Its chemical composition includes compounds with potential medicinal properties.
