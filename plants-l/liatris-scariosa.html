Liatris scariosa

Liatris scariosa, commonly known as the slender blazing star, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, spires of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Liatris scariosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
