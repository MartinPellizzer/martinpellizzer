Linum mucronatum var. syriacum

Linum mucronatum var. syriacum, commonly known as the Syrian flax, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces delicate blue to violet flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and historical significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and used in traditional medicine.
