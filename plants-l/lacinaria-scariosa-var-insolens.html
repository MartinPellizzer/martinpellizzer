Lacinaria scariosa var. insolens

Lacinaria scariosa var. insolens, commonly known as the scarious lacinaria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dry, papery layer. The plant produces delicate, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand periods of drought.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat preferences, it is of interest to botanists and ecologists studying xerophytic plant adaptations.
