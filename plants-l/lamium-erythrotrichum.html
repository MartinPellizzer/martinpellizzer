Lamium erythrotrichum

Lamium erythrotrichum, commonly known as red-haired nettle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with a distinctive reddish or purplish hue along the veins, which contribute to its common name. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorled clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Lamium erythrotrichum is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
