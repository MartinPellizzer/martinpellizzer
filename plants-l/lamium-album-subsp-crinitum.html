Lamium album subsp. crinitum

Lamium album subsp.

crinitum, commonly known as white deadnettle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features opposite, ovate leaves that are hairy and have a distinctive silver-white underside.

The plant produces clusters of small, showy white flowers with a distinctive red calyx, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in herbal medicine.
