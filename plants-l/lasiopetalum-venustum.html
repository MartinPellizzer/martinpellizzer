Lasiopetalum venustum

Lasiopetalum venustum, commonly known as the golden flame bush, is a small evergreen shrub native to eastern Australia.

It features delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
