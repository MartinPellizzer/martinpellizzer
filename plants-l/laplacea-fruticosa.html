Laplacea fruticosa

Laplacea fruticosa, commonly known as the coastal shrub, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it well-suited for coastal and sandy environments. Its leaves are typically narrow and leathery, providing a drought-resistant adaptation.

Laplacea fruticosa is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize sandy soils.
