Leptochloa dubia

Leptochloa dubia, commonly known as the slender grass or slender bluegrass, is a species of grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded or semi-shaded environments. It is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of streams.

Leptochloa dubia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
