Lobivia steinmannii var. camargoensis

Lobivia steinmannii var. camargoensis, commonly known as the Camargo's lobivia, is a rare and striking cactus native to the arid regions of northern Argentina.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded body and spiny, ribbed stems that help it retain water in its harsh environment. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring, adding a splash of color to its desert habitat. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.

Due to its limited distribution and vulnerability to habitat loss, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
