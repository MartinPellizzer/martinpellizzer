Lilium

Lilium, commonly known as the lily, is a genus of flowering plants in the amaryllid family.

It consists of about 110 species, many of which are native to the northern hemisphere. The plant is characterized by its showy, often fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, red, and yellow. Lilium plants typically have linear, grass-like leaves and are often cultivated for their ornamental value.

They are also valued in horticulture for their ability to thrive in a range of garden conditions.
