Lapsana communis f. adenoclados

Lapsana communis f. adenoclados, commonly known as the腺叶蒲公英, is a variant of the common sowthistle.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its erect, hairy stems and alternate leaves that are usually lobed or toothed. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are often glandular, giving it the specific epithet "adenoclados.".

This form is typically found in disturbed soils and can be a common weed in agricultural fields.
