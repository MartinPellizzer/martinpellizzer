Luvunga calophylla

Luvunga calophylla, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a tropical tree native to Central and Southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by winged fruits. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its valuable timber, it is also considered an endangered species in some regions.
