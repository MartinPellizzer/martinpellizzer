Lasimorpha senegalensis

Lasimorpha senegalensis, commonly known as the Senegalese fig or African wild fig, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, fig-like fruits. The tree can grow up to 15 meters tall, with a spreading canopy that provides shade in its natural habitat. Its fruit is an important food source for various wildlife species, including birds and mammals.

The plant is also valued for its wood, which is used in traditional crafts and construction.
