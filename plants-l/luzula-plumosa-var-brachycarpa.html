Luzula plumosa var. brachycarpa

Luzula plumosa var. brachycarpa, commonly known as the dwarf woodrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, commonly known as the grass family, and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowering stems produce small, clustered inflorescences of pale green to brownish flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated.

This variety is notable for its compact size and adaptability to moist, shaded environments, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.
