Lissochilus vermiculatus

Lissochilus vermiculatus, commonly known as the woolly leaf orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions. It is often found growing in rocky or forested areas, where it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
