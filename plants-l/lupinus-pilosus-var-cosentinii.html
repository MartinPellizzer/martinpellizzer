Lupinus pilosus var. cosentinii

Lupinus pilosus var. cosentinii, commonly known as the Cosentin's lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and clusters of pea-like flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It features feathery, pinnately compound leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This variety is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
