Lygistum calycosum

Lygistum calycosum, commonly known as the golden vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, making it a striking ornamental plant. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and a vigorous growth habit, often used in hanging baskets or trained on trellises. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Lygistum calycosum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
