Lunanea bichy

Lunanea bichy, commonly known as the moonflower vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night, making it a popular choice for evening gardens. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and thrives in warm, humid climates. It produces small, green fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals.

Due to its nocturnal blooming habit, it is often cultivated for its aesthetic and aromatic qualities.
