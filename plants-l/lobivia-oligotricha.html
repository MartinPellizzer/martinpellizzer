Lobivia oligotricha

Lobivia oligotricha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lobivia, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded stems that are often covered in a layer of white, waxy hair. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
