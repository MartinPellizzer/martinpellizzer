Laurus tarairi

Laurus tarairi, commonly known as the New Zealand laurel, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a member of the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and fragrant aroma. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, appearing in clusters, and it produces dark blue berries that are edible.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
