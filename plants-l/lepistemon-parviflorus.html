Lepistemon parviflorus

Lepistemon parviflorus, commonly known as the small-flowered lepistemon, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale blue or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface.

Lepistemon parviflorus is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.
