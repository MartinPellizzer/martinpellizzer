Limonium ramosissimum subsp. ramosissimum

Limonium ramosissimum subsp.

ramosissimum, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and clusters of blue to violet flowers.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, bract-like structures that surround the flower heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal areas and sandy habitats. Limonium ramosissimum subsp.

ramosissimum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate salt spray.
