Lycopodium hispidum

Lycopodium hispidum, commonly known as the hairy clubmoss, is a vascular plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky soils. The plant features erect, branching stems covered in small, scale-like leaves. Its reproductive structures are spore-bearing cones, which are clustered at the tips of the stems.

Lycopodium hispidum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and small invertebrates.
