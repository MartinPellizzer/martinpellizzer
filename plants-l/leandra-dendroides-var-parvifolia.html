Leandra dendroides var. parvifolia

Leandra dendroides var. parvifolia, commonly known as the dwarf leandra, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It features dense, evergreen foliage with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the wet season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

Due to its compact size and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
