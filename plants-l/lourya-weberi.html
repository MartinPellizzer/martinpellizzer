Lourya weberi

Lourya weberi, commonly known as the Mexican firecracker cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in spines and clusters of bright red flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soil. It is drought-tolerant and requires minimal watering once established.

The flowers are typically produced in late spring and early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
