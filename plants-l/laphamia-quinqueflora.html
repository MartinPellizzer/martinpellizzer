Laphamia quinqueflora

Laphamia quinqueflora, commonly known as the five-flowered lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Liliaceae family.
