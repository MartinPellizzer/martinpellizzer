Laurus aggregata

Laurus aggregata, commonly known as Mediterranean laurel, is a dense, evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dark green, leathery leaves that are aromatic and often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its hardy nature and ability to tolerate drought make it well-suited for Mediterranean and temperate climates.
