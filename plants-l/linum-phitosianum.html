Linum phitosianum

Linum phitosianum, commonly known as the Persian flax or wild flax, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, often appearing in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Due to its historical use in fiber production and its ornamental value, it remains a notable species in botanical studies.
