Lagopsis

Lagopsis, commonly known as the "bitterroot" or "yellow lousewort," is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

These plants are typically found in arid and semi-arid regions, often growing in sandy or rocky soils. They are characterized by their small, yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves, which are composed of three leaflets. Lagopsis species are known for their ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions, making them important components of desert ecosystems.

Some species are also used in traditional medicine due to their bioactive compounds.
