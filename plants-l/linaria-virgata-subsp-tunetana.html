Linaria virgata subsp. tunetana

Linaria virgata subsp.

tunetana, commonly known as the Tunisian toadflax, is a flowering plant native to North Africa. It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often arranged oppositely along the stems.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
