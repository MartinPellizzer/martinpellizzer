Lafoensia replicata var. lundii

Lafoensia replicata var. lundii, commonly known as the Brazilian flame tree, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets and produces small, woody pods that contain flat, winged seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
