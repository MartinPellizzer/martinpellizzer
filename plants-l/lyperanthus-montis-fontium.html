Lyperanthus montis-fontium

Lyperanthus montis-fontium, commonly known as the mountain-fonts bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, often with a white or yellow throat. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Lyperanthus montis-fontium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
