Lupinus coelestinus

Lupinus coelestinus, commonly known as the sky lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open areas such as meadows and grasslands.

Lupinus coelestinus plays an important role in ecological systems by fixing nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plant life.
