Lissochilus rendlei

Lissochilus rendlei, commonly known as the Rendle's beard orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, often hairy floral structures. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, where it thrives in cool and humid conditions. Its flowers are usually small to medium-sized, with a distinctive beard-like structure on the lip, which is a key feature in the genus Lissochilus.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
