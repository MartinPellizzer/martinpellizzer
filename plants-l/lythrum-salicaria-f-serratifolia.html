Lythrum salicaria f. serratifolia

Lythrum salicaria f. serratifolia, commonly known as the serrated redroot, is a botanical variant of the common lythrum.

It is characterized by its distinctive serrated leaf margins and vibrant pink to purple flowers. This plant typically grows in wetlands and moist soils, thriving in environments with consistent moisture. Its foliage is lance-shaped, and the flowers bloom in whorled clusters along the stem.

It is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ecological benefits and ornamental appeal.
