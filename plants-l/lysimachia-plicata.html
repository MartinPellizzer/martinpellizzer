Lysimachia plicata

Lysimachia plicata, commonly known as the wrinkled yellow-eyed grass, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, whorled leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are often folded or pleated, giving it the species name "plicata," which means "creased" or "folded.".

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
