Lonicera longipes

Lonicera longipes, commonly known as the Chinese honeysuckle, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces fragrant, tubular flowers that range from yellow to orange in color. The plant is characterized by its long, slender stems and is often used in traditional Chinese medicine. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

Lonicera longipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
