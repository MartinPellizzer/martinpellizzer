Lobelia rapunculoides

Lobelia rapunculoides, commonly known as the Indian tobacco, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features showy blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Lobelia rapunculoides is also notable for its medicinal uses in traditional herbal remedies.
