Lawsonia alba var. flavescens

Lawsonia alba var. flavescens, commonly known as the yellow henna or *Lawsonia* var. *flavescens*, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces small, yellowish flowers. The plant is known for its aromatic leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine and as a natural dye. It thrives in arid and semi-arid climates, preferring well-drained soils.

Its botanical significance lies in its role in both ecological systems and cultural practices.
