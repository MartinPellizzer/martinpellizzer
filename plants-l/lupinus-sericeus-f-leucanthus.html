Lupinus sericeus f. leucanthus

Lupinus sericeus f. leucanthus, commonly known as the white-flowered silky lupin, is a variant of the silky lupin characterized by its pale to white flowers.

This plant is native to the western United States and thrives in well-drained soils, often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is notable for its feathery, silken foliage. The flowers are typically arranged in dense, upright spikes, and the plant produces elongated, narrow pods that contain several seeds.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and ecologically valuable species for gardens and restoration projects.
