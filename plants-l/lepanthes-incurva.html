Lepanthes incurva

Lepanthes incurva, commonly known as the curled lip orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pendulous flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in cloud forests. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a distinctive curved lip.

Lepanthes incurva is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
