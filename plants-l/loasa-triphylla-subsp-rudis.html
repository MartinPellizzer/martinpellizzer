Loasa triphylla subsp. rudis

Loasa triphylla subsp.

rudis, commonly known as roughleaf loasa, is a shrub native to the western United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are often rough to the touch and have a distinct serrated edge.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in spring. Its growth habit is often upright, with branches that can become somewhat woody over time. Loasa triphylla subsp.

rudis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh conditions.
