Lablavia vulgaris

Lablavia vulgaris, commonly known as the common lablavia, is a herbaceous plant native to temperate regions.

It typically grows to a height of about 30-60 centimeters and features alternate, simple leaves that are lance-shaped. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink, blooming in the summer months. Lablavia vulgaris thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ease of care.
