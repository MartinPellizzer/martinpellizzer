Lobelia adenophora

Lobelia adenophora, commonly known as the腺花 Lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate blue to purple flowers and hairy stems, which give it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Lobelia adenophora is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and conservation efforts.
