Lobivia krahn-juckeri subsp. echinopsoides

Lobivia krahn-juckeri subsp.

echinopsoides, commonly known as the Echinops-like Lobivia, is a succulent plant native to South America, particularly Argentina. It is characterized by its compact, rounded growth habit and dense, spiny foliage that resembles the appearance of a sea urchin.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts and collectors.
