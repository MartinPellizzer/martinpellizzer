Lobivia marsoneri

Lobivia marsoneri, commonly known as the Marson's lobivia, is a small, compact cactus native to the arid regions of Argentina.

It features a short, cylindrical stem with distinctive, rounded tubercles and spines that are typically brown or reddish in color. The plant produces delicate, yellow flowers during the warmer months, which bloom from the areoles along the stem. It is well adapted to dry environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stem.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
