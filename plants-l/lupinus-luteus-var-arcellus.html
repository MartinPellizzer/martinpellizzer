Lupinus luteus var. arcellus

Lupinus luteus var. arcellus, commonly known as the yellow lupine, is a perennial legume native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or disturbed habitats. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, enriching the soil with nutrients.

However, it is also considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its aggressive growth habit.
