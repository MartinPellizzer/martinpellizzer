Lupinus albert-smithianus

Lupinus albert-smithianus, commonly known as the Albert-Smith lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and showy blue to purple flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
