Leonotis rugosa

Leonotis rugosa, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky stems and clusters of bright orange-red tubular flowers. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stem. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Leonotis rugosa is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
