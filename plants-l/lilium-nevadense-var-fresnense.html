Lilium nevadense var. fresnense

Lilium nevadense var. fresnense, commonly known as the Sierra Lily, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing pink to white petals with a yellowish throat. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a basal rosette, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

This variety is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological significance.
