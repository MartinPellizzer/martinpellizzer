Linum pallasianum subsp. pallasianum

Linum pallasianum subsp.

pallasianum, commonly known as Pallas's flax, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia. It belongs to the genus Linum in the family Linaceae, which includes other species of flax.

The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. It produces small, yellow flowers that are typically clustered in umbels.

This subspecies is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in fiber production.
