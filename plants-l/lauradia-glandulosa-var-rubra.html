Lauradia glandulosa var. rubra

Lauradia glandulosa var. rubra, commonly known as the red glandular lauradia, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and is known for its clusters of small, fragrant, reddish-pink flowers. The plant produces elliptical, dark green leaves with prominent glandular dots on their surface. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique floral display.
