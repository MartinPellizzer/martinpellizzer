Lonicera henryi var. setuligera

Lonicera henryi var. setuligera, commonly known as the hairy honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, woody stems covered in fine, hairy glands, which give it its varietal name. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a slightly serrated edge.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
