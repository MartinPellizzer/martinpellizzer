Lepidium calycinum var. macrocarpum

Lepidium calycinum var. macrocarpum, commonly known as the large-seeded wallcress, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing annual herb with small, white or yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical pods that contain numerous small, rounded seeds. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately lobed, and covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in rocky areas.
