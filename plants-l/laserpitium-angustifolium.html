Laserpitium angustifolium

Laserpitium angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved gentian, is a flowering plant belonging to the Gentianaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically found in grassy meadows and open woodlands. The plant is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers, which are usually tubular in shape. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, giving it its common name.

It blooms during the summer months and is often used in traditional herbal medicine.
