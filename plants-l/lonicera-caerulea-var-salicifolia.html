Lonicera caerulea var. salicifolia

Lonicera caerulea var. salicifolia, commonly known as the Amur honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often slightly serrated along the edges. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that ripen to a bright red color. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine.

This species is adaptable to a variety of soil types and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
