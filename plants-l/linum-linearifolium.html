Linum linearifolium

Linum linearifolium, commonly known as slender flax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and delicate blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It has a low-growing habit and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Linum linearifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
