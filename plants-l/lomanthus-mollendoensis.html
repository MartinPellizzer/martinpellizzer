Lomanthus mollendoensis

Lomanthus mollendoensis, commonly known as the Andean lomatium, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescences. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are finely divided and often emit a strong, spicy scent when crushed.
