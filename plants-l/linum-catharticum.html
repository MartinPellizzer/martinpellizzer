Linum catharticum

Linum catharticum, commonly known as the cathartic flax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of blue or purple flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Linum catharticum has been historically used in traditional medicine for its potential laxative properties.
