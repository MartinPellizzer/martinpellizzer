Lycopodium tubulosum

Lycopodium tubulosum, commonly known as the clubmoss, is a small, evergreen plant that belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its spore-bearing cones and its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its slender, branching stems and small, scale-like leaves. Lycopodium tubulosum is often found in forest understories and is adapted to nutrient-poor soils.

It plays an important role in maintaining soil stability and providing habitat for various microorganisms.
