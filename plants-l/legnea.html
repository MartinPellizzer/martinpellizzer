Legnea

Legnea, commonly known as the legume plant, is a diverse group of flowering plants belonging to the family Fabaceae.

These plants are characterized by their pod-like fruits, which contain seeds surrounded by a fibrous membrane. Many legumes have nitrogen-fixing capabilities due to symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria in their root nodules. They are often found in a variety of habitats, including grasslands, forests, and deserts.

Legumes play an important role in agriculture and ecology due to their ability to improve soil fertility.
