Lappula stylosa

Lappula stylosa, commonly known as the slender lappula, is a flowering plant native to regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and can be found in meadows and along riverbanks. Its leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and often covered in fine hairs.

The fruit is a capsule that dehisces to release small, rounded seeds.
