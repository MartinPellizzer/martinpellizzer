Lagochilus khorassanicus

Lagochilus khorassanicus, commonly known as the Khorasan hyacinth, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and showy blue to purple flowers. It typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the study of medicinal and aromatic plants.
