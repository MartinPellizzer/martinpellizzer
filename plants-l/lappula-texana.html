Lappula texana

Lappula texana, commonly known as Texas lappula, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant has small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, and its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often hairy. It typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is adapted to arid environments.

The plant is not widely cultivated but is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological role.
