Loeseneriella africana var. obtusifolia

Loeseneriella africana var. obtusifolia, commonly known as African rock fig, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers that range from white to pale yellow. The plant produces edible, fig-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is also used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
