Leucanthemum gayanum var. fallax

Leucanthemum gayanum var. fallax, commonly known as the Gaya leucanthemum, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy blooms. The plant produces white to pale yellow flowers with a central disc, typically blooming in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long-lasting flowers.
