Lupinus james-westii

Lupinus james-westii, commonly known as the James-West lupine, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Lupinus james-westii plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest for its potential in ecological restoration projects.
