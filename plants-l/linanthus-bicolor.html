Linanthus bicolor

Linanthus bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored flax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Linaceae family and is characterized by its strikingly contrasting blue and white flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and thrives in open, sunny habitats. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Linanthus bicolor plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in disturbed or arid environments.
