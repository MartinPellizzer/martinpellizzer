Laurus caustica

Laurus caustica, commonly known as the Mediterranean laurel, is a evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and aromatic fragrance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by dark blue berries. Its leaves contain essential oils that have been used historically for their medicinal and culinary properties.

However, it is toxic if ingested in large quantities, making it important to handle with care.
