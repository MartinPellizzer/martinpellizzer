Litobrochia balbisiana

Litobrochia balbisiana, commonly known as the Balbisian rock orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique morphology, featuring slender, often hairy stems and small, intricate flowers with a distinct coloration pattern. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained habitats, often growing on cliffs or in crevices, which provides it with the necessary conditions for its survival. Its pollination is primarily carried out by specific insect species, highlighting its ecological niche and the delicate balance of its ecosystem.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, Litobrochia balbisiana is considered a vulnerable species, emphasizing the need for conservation efforts.
