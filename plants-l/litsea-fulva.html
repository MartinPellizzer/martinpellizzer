Litsea fulva

Litsea fulva, commonly known as the yellow-flowered litsea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including parts of China, India, and Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, ovate leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, woody drupe that turns red when mature.

Litsea fulva is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal uses.
