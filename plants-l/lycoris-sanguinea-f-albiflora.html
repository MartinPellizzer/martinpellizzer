Lycoris sanguinea f. albiflora

Lycoris sanguinea f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered lycoris, is a hybrid variety of the lycoris genus native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its striking white flowers that bloom in the autumn, contrasting with the typical red or orange hues of its parent species. The plant features narrow, strap-like leaves and produces ornamental red berries that persist through winter. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its botanical interest lies in its hybrid origin and unique floral display.
