Lepanthes unitrinervis

Lepanthes unitrinervis, commonly known as the three-veined slipper orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that resemble small slipper-shaped pouches. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments within the understory of tropical forests. Its leaves are usually elliptical and have a distinctive three-veined structure, which is a key identifying feature.

Lepanthes unitrinervis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
