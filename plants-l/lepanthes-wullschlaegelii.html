Lepanthes wullschlaegelii

Lepanthes wullschlaegelii, commonly known as the Wullschlaegel's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of other plants without deriving nutrients from them. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Lepanthes wullschlaegelii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
