Leptoboea multiflora subsp. multiflora

Leptoboea multiflora subsp.

multiflora, commonly known as the mountain dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces numerous yellow flowers arranged in a dense inflorescence.

The plant is typically found in rocky or sandy soils in arid or semi-arid environments. It has a short flowering period, usually during the cooler months, and relies on insect pollination.

The species is relatively drought-tolerant, making it well adapted to its native habitat.
