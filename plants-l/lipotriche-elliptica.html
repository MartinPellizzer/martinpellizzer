Lipotriche elliptica

Lipotriche elliptica, commonly known as the elliptic lipotriche, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its delicate appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some areas.
