Leptocereus assurgens

Leptocereus assurgens, commonly known as the night-blooming cereus, is a member of the Cactaceae family and is native to the arid regions of South America.

This plant is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are covered in sparse, spiny areoles. It produces large, showy flowers that open at night, typically in shades of white or pale pink. The flowers have a strong, sweet fragrance that attracts nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

Due to its unique blooming pattern and striking appearance, it is a popular choice for desert gardens and cactus enthusiasts.
