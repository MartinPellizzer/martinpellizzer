Linum emirnense var. marojejyense

Linum emirnense var. marojejyense, commonly known as the Marojejy flax, is a rare flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments within the rainforests of the Marojejy National Park. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and herbaceous.

Due to its restricted habitat and threatened status, it is of significant interest to botanists and conservationists.
