Loranthus talbotiorum

Loranthus talbotiorum, commonly known as the Talbot's mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily in the genus Eucalyptus.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using the light it captures. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by small, fleshy berries that serve as a food source for birds. Its seeds are dispersed by birds that eat the berries, facilitating its spread to new host trees.

This species is native to Australia and is often found in coastal and dry forest regions.
