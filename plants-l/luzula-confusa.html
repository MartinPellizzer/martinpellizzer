Luzula confusa

Luzula confusa, commonly known as the confused woodrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers that are usually greenish or brownish in hue. It is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Luzula confusa is often found in woodland areas, stream banks, and rocky slopes, where it contributes to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
