Lisianthius racemosus

Lisianthius racemosus, commonly known as the everlasting lily, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces numerous seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to thrive in various environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are evergreen, providing year-round structure.

The plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
