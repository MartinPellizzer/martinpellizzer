Leonis

Leonis, commonly known as the lion's mouth, is a striking plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows as a shrub with woody stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its resilience to drought and mild frost.
