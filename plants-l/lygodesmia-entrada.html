Lygodesmia entrada

Lygodesmia entrada, commonly known as the Brazilian stinging nettle, is a climbing shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes other stinging plants. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, greenish flowers in clusters. Its stems are covered in fine hairs that can cause skin irritation upon contact.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
