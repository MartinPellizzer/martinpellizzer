Lilium spectabile

Lilium spectabile, commonly known as the noble lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and large, showy flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically reaches heights of 2 to 5 feet and produces striking white or pink petals with a red or orange center. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Lilium spectabile thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
