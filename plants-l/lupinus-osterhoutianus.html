Lupinus osterhoutianus

Lupinus osterhoutianus, commonly known as the Osterhout lupine, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of vibrant purple or pink pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces long, slender pods that contain several seeds. Lupinus osterhoutianus is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with soil bacteria.
