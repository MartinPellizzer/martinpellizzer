Lupinus inflatus

Lupinus inflatus, commonly known as the inflated lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive inflated seed pods. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are usually blue or purple and bloom in early summer.

Lupinus inflatus is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it ecologically significant in its native range.
