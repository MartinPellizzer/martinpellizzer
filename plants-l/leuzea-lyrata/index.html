Leuzea Lyrata

Leuzea lyrata, also known as the lyreleaf sida, is a unique flowering plant native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its distinctive lyre-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. This species thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes and swamps. Despite its modest appearance, it plays an important role in local ecosystems. Its resilience and adaptability make it a fascinating subject for botanists and nature enthusiasts alike.
