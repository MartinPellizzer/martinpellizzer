Lonicera affinis

Lonicera affinis, commonly known as the yellow honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth texture and a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces fragrant, yellow flowers in late spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Lonicera affinis is also valued for its role in supporting local wildlife and its adaptability to various garden conditions.
