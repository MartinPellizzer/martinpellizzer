Lepidagathis mitis

Lepidagathis mitis, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf sida, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. Its fibrous roots and robust stems make it adaptable to various environmental conditions.

Lepidagathis mitis is also known for its potential use in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
