Lepisorus papakensis

Lepisorus papakensis, commonly known as the Papakansis fern, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial fern characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that typically grow in shaded, moist environments. The plant exhibits a rhizomatous growth habit, with creeping rhizomes that help it spread and colonize new areas. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds, a common feature in many fern species.

Lepisorus papakensis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in humid, woodland conditions.
