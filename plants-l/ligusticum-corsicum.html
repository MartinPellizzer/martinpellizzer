Ligusticum corsicum

Ligusticum corsicum, commonly known as Corsican angelica, is a tall biennial or perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features large, compound leaves that are deeply lobed and can reach up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in umbrella-like clusters, typical of the Apiaceae family. Its roots are thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Ligusticum corsicum is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
