Lobivia campicola

Lobivia campicola, commonly known as the mountain lobivia, is a succulent plant native to South America, specifically found in the Andes region of Peru.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rounded shape and ribbed stems. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, and it stores water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Lobivia campicola thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
