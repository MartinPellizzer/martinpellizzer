Luzula kobayasii var. minor

Luzula kobayasii var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf wood rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and somewhat hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical cluster of small, pale blue to greenish-white flowers.

Luzula kobayasii var. minor is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in maintaining soil stability in woodland habitats.
