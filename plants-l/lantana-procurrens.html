Lantana procurrens

Lantana procurrens, commonly known as creeping lantana, is a low-growing shrub native to the southern United States.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, red, or purple, depending on the variety. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to spread rapidly.

However, it can become invasive in some regions, requiring careful management.
