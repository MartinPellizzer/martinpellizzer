Linum strictum var. racemosum

Linum strictum var. racemosum, commonly known as the racemose flax, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its erect, slender stems and small, blue or violet flowers arranged in racemes. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in textile production.
