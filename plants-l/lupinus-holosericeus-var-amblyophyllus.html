Lupinus holosericeus var. amblyophyllus

Lupinus holosericeus var. amblyophyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to western North America.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces spikes of blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
