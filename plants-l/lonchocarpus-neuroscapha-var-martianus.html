Lonchocarpus neuroscapha var. martianus

Lonchocarpus neuroscapha var. martianus, commonly known as the Martian climbing bean, is a tropical legume native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
