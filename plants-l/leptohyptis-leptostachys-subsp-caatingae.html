Leptohyptis leptostachys subsp. caatingae

Leptohyptis leptostachys subsp.

caatingae, commonly known as the Caatinga hyptis, is a shrub native to the northeastern regions of Brazil. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic and medicinal plants.

The plant is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, opposite leaves. It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale blue, arranged in spikes.

This species is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in the Caatinga biome, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
