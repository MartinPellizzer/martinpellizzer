Lilium martagon var. alboplenum

Lilium martagon var. alboplenum, commonly known as the white-flowered Turk's cap lily, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, recurved, bell-shaped flowers that are predominantly white with a slight pinkish tinge. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, and it produces tall, slender stems that bear its showy blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its elegant, early summer flowering display.
