Laetia crenata

Laetia crenata, commonly known as the crested fern, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its unique, feathery fronds that often display a distinctive crest or ridge.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to regions in Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in rainforests or along stream banks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread slowly, forming dense clusters over time.

Laetia crenata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
