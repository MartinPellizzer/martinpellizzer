Lepanthes pachoi

Lepanthes pachoi, commonly known as the Pachoi orchid, is a species of orchid native to the highlands of Ecuador.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, pale yellow to white flowers with intricate patterns. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its epiphytic or lithophytic growth habit.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
