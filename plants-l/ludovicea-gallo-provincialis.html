Ludovicea gallo-provincialis

Ludovicea gallo-provincialis, commonly known as the Gallo-Provincial Ludovicea, is a rare flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its delicate, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils. It is often found in maritime climates with mild winters and warm, dry summers.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is protected in several conservation areas.
