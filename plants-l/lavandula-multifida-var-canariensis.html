Lavandula multifida var. canariensis

Lavandula multifida var. canariensis, commonly known as the Canary Island lavender, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces spikes of pale purple to lavender flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its aromatic foliage and showy blooms make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
