Lasiagrostis capensis

Lasiagrostis capensis, commonly known as the Cape grass, is a perennial grass native to South Africa.

It typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, exhibiting a compact growth habit with slender, upright stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, grass-like flowers. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening for its fine texture and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
