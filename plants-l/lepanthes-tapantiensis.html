Lepanthes tapantiensis

Lepanthes tapantiensis, commonly known as the Tapanti orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments within the understory of tropical forests. The plant features delicate, greenish-white flowers with intricate patterns and a distinctive lip that aids in pollination. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
