Lonicera sempervirens var. fuchsioides

Lonicera sempervirens var. fuchsioides, commonly known as the fuchsia honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, simple, ovate to elliptic leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces showy, tubular, fuchsia-colored flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

The fruit is a red, fleshy berry that ripens in late summer and is edible for both humans and wildlife.
