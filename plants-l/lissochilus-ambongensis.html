Lissochilus ambongensis

Lissochilus ambongensis, commonly known as the Ambongensis slipper orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude cloud forests, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the rocky substrates in its natural habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
