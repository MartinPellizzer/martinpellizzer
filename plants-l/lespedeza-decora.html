Lespedeza decora

Lespedeza decora, commonly known as the pinkwood or pinkbark lespedeza, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers in dense clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture.

Lespedeza decora is often found in moist, acidic soils in woodlands and along stream banks.
