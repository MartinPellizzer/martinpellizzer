Loranthus sakarensis

Loranthus sakarensis, commonly known as the Sakarensis mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees.

It belongs to the family Loranthaceae and is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia. The plant lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using the light it captures. Its small, white or yellowish flowers are often clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, fleshy berries that are dispersed by birds.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can have significant impacts on the health of its host trees.
