Ligusticum carvi

Ligusticum carvi, commonly known as caraway, is a biennial herb native to Europe and western Asia.

It features a stout, ridged stem and feather-like, aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family. Its seeds, which are often confused with cumin, are used in culinary and medicinal applications.

The essential oil extracted from the seeds contains compounds like limonene and carvone, contributing to its distinctive aroma and therapeutic properties.
