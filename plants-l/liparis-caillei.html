Liparis caillei

Liparis caillei, commonly known as the Caille's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, linear leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments, often in humid tropical climates. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the forest floor.

Liparis caillei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
