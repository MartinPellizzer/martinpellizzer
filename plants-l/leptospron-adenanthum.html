Leptospron adenanthum

Leptospron adenanthum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rock heath, is a small shrub native to the southern regions of Australia.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal or heathland environments. The plant has small, narrow leaves that are usually covered in a fine layer of white or grey hairs. Its flowers are usually pink or white, appearing in clusters at the ends of the branches.

It is well adapted to harsh conditions, including salt spray and low nutrient soils.
