Lafoensia pacari f. angustifolia

Lafoensia pacari f. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed lafoensia, is a shrub native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces yellowish-orange fruits that contain flat, seed-like structures. It thrives in humid, tropical conditions with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
