Lactuca tamagawaensis

Lactuca tamagawaensis, commonly known as the Japanese endive, is a species of lettuce native to Japan.

It is characterized by its curly, dark green leaves that form a loose head, similar to other endive varieties. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its bitter taste. It thrives in cool climates and is often cultivated for its edible leaves.

Lactuca tamagawaensis is valued both for its culinary use and its ornamental appeal in gardens.
