Linaria persica

Linaria persica, commonly known as Persian toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, yellow-orange flowers that resemble small toads, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often branching and upright.

Linaria persica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
