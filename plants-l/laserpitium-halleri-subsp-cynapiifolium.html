Laserpitium halleri subsp. cynapiifolium

Laserpitium halleri subsp.

cynapiifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved lungwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features basal leaves that are broadly ovate to elliptic in shape, often with a bluish-green hue and a hairy surface.

The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers arranged in dense, terminal spikes. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and relatively rare species within the gentian family.
