Landolphia myrtifolia

Landolphia myrtifolia, commonly known as the African morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, containing seeds that are dispersed by animals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
