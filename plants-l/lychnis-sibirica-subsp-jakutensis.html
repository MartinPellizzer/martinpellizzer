Lychnis sibirica subsp. jakutensis

Lychnis sibirica subsp.

jakutensis, commonly known as the Siberian fireweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern regions of Russia. It belongs to the carnation family, Caryophyllaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, pink to reddish-purple flowers.

The plant typically blooms in late summer to early autumn, producing clusters of small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

This subspecies is often found in moist, rocky soils and can thrive in alpine or tundra-like habitats.
