Lolium perenne subsp. trabutii

Lolium perenne subsp.

trabutii, commonly known as Trabut's perennial ryegrass, is a grass species native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant typically produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing minute, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in forage agriculture or ecological restoration projects.
