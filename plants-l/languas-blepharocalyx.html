Languas blepharocalyx

Languas blepharocalyx, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen tree that typically grows to a height of 5 to 10 meters. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a rough, woody pod that contains several seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
