Ladenbergia macrocarpa

Ladenbergia macrocarpa, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The tree produces long, flat pods that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and fine grain, making it important in local timber industries.

The plant also plays a role in traditional medicine and ecological systems in its native habitat.
