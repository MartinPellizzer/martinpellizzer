Lolium × festucaceum

Lolium × festucaceum, commonly known as the hybrid ryegrass, is a perennial grass species that exhibits a mix of characteristics from its parent species, Lolium perenne and Festuca ovina.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted leaves and dense, compact growth habit, making it suitable for turfgrass and forage applications. The plant typically grows in temperate climates and is known for its adaptability to various soil types. It produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with both sterile and fertile florets, contributing to its reproductive strategy.

Lolium × festucaceum is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to recover quickly after grazing or mowing.
