Lobelia michauxii

Lobelia michauxii, commonly known as Michaux's lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces spikes of small, tubular blue or purple flowers. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators.

Lobelia michauxii is also noted for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a resilient addition to native gardens.
