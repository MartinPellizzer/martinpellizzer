Leea aculeata

Leea aculeata, commonly known as the spiny leea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and the plant is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.

Leea aculeata is valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
