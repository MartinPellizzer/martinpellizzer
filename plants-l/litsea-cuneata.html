Litsea cuneata

Litsea cuneata, commonly known as the Chinese evergreen or Chinese boxwood, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters. The plant features opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are glossy and dark green, with a smooth texture. Its flowers are small, white, and fragrant, appearing in clusters during the spring.

The fruit is a small, red drupe that matures in the fall, adding visual interest to the plant.
