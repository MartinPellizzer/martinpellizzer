Lirium

Lirium, commonly known as the lily of the valley, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Liliaceae.

These perennial herbs are characterized by their bell-shaped, fragrant flowers and are often found in woodland areas. The plant has strap-like leaves and produces a cluster of small, white blossoms that bloom in early spring. Lirium species are known for their medicinal properties and have been used in traditional herbal remedies.

They are also popular in horticulture for their ornamental value and symbolic meanings.
