Lobivia densispina f. eburnea

Lobivia densispina f. eburnea, commonly known as the silver-leafed lobivia, is a compact, spineless cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It features a short, cylindrical stem covered in a dense layer of white, powdery wax that gives it its distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, pale green flowers during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to dry environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
