Lycium arochae

Lycium arochae, commonly known as the Arochae boxthorn, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible and often consumed by local wildlife. Lycium arochae is adapted to withstand extreme heat and drought, making it a resilient species in harsh desert environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local flora and ecosystem.
