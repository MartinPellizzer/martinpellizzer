Lepanthes vere-aurum

Lepanthes vere-aurum, commonly known as the golden star orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, golden-yellow flowers that resemble stars, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its leaves are usually dark green and elongated, providing a lush backdrop to its vibrant blooms.

Lepanthes vere-aurum is prized in horticulture for its beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
