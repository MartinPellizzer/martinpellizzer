Lolium arvense var. hostii

Lolium arvense var. hostii, commonly known as the Host's ryegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically a bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and agricultural fields.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it is considered both a weed and a forage crop in various regions.
