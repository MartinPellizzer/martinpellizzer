Lepanthes necopina

Lepanthes necopina, commonly known as the purple-veined orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, violet-colored flowers with intricate veining. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in the understory of tropical forests. Its flowers are usually small but highly ornate, featuring a combination of white and purple hues.

Lepanthes necopina is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
