Lasiococca comberi

Lasiococca comberi, commonly known as the silverleaf spurge, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silvery-gray foliage and is often used as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, and it thrives in well-drained soils. It is drought-tolerant and prefers full sun to partial shade.

However, it can be invasive in some regions if not properly managed.
