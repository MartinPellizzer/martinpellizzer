Limnopeuce

Limnopeuce, commonly known as the water lily of the genus *Nuphar*, is a aquatic plant characterized by its broad, floating leaves and showy, fragrant flowers.

It typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and lakes. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that helps it anchor in muddy substrates. Its leaves are usually green and waxy, while its flowers can be white, yellow, or pink, depending on the species.

Limnopeuce plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
