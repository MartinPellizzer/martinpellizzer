Lotus stepposus

Lotus stepposus, commonly known as the steppe lotus, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers that bloom in summer, followed by pod fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as steppes and grasslands, and is adapted to withstand arid conditions.

Its root system helps prevent soil erosion, making it ecologically significant in its native environment.
