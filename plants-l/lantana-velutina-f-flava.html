Lantana velutina f. flava

Lantana velutina f. flava, commonly known as the yellow lantana, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The leaves are typically hairy, giving the plant a velvety texture, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

The plant is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
