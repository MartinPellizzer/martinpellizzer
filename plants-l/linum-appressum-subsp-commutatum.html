Linum appressum subsp. commutatum

Linum appressum subsp.

commutatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf flax, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant belongs to the Linaceae family and is known for its fibrous stems, which have been historically used for textile production. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and historical significance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
