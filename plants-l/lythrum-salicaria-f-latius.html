Lythrum salicaria f. latius

Lythrum salicaria f. latius, commonly known as the broad-leaved loosestrife, is a variant of the common loosestrife plant.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and tall spikes of showy pink to purple flowers. This plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been introduced to other regions, including North America. It thrives in moist soils and wetland habitats, often forming dense stands in marshes and along stream banks.

The species is known for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
