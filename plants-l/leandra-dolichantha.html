Leandra dolichantha

Leandra dolichantha, commonly known as the long-stalked orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually long and strap-like, and it produces showy inflorescences that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
