Lobelia aligera

Lobelia aligera, commonly known as the slender cardinal flower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking red-orange tubular flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Lobelia aligera is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits to pollinators.
