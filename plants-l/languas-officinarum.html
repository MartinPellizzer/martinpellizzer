Languas officinarum

Languas officinarum, commonly known as the common yarrow, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its flat, composite flower heads composed of numerous small white or pinkish flowers arranged in a circular pattern. The plant has feathery, aromatic leaves that are often used in herbal medicine for their anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Languas officinarum is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects.
