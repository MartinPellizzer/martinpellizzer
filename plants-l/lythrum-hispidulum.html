Lythrum hispidulum

Lythrum hispidulum, commonly known as rough lythrum, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lythraceae family and is characterized by its spikes of showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist soils and is often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, giving it a rough texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and also serves as a habitat for various pollinators.
