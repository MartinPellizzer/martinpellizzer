Lupinaster involucratus

Lupinaster involucratus, commonly known as the woolly lupin, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, spindly stems and dense clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep red. The plant produces distinctive, hairy, bract-like structures that surround its flower clusters, giving it a woolly appearance. Its leaves are compound, consisting of multiple oval-shaped leaflets, and it is adapted to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

Lupinaster involucratus plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing nectar for pollinators and contributing to soil stabilization in its native habitat.
