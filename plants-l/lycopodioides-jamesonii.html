Lycopodioides jamesonii

Lycopodioides jamesonii, commonly known as the Jameson's clubmoss, is a member of the Lycopodiaceae family and is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves arranged in whorls around the stem.

It is a cryptogamic plant that reproduces through spores rather than seeds, typical of its evolutionary lineage. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its structure includes a sporophyte with microphylls and specialized structures for spore dispersal.

Lycopodioides jamesonii plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for microorganisms and small invertebrates.
