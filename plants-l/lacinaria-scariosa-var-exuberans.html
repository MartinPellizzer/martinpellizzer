Lacinaria scariosa var. exuberans

Lacinaria scariosa var. exuberans, commonly known as the hairy lacinaria, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats. Its leaves are typically alternate and have a distinctive, lobed shape.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
