Lobelia roxburghiana

Lobelia roxburghiana, commonly known as the Indian tobacco, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Lobelia roxburghiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine.
