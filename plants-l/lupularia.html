Lupularia

Lupularia, commonly known as the "lupine" or "lupin," is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

These plants are characterized by their erect growth habit, compound leaves, and showy, pea-like flowers that come in a variety of colors. Lupularia species are typically found in temperate and arid regions, often thriving in well-drained soils. They are known for their ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making them valuable in soil enrichment.

Many species are cultivated as ornamental plants due to their vibrant blooms and ecological benefits.
