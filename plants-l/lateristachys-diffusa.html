Lateristachys diffusa

Lateristachys diffusa, commonly known as the hairy senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
