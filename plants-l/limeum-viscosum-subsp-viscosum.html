Limeum viscosum subsp. viscosum

Limeum viscosum subsp.

viscosum, commonly known as the sticky lime tree, is a shrub native to parts of southern Africa. It is characterized by its glandular, sticky leaves that emit a strong, unpleasant odor.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruits are small, capsule-like structures that release seeds when mature.

This species is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
