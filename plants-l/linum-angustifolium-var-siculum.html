Linum angustifolium var. siculum

Linum angustifolium var. siculum, commonly known as the Sicilian flax, is a variant of the flax plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, blue or white flowers. The plant is typically found in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It belongs to the Linaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
