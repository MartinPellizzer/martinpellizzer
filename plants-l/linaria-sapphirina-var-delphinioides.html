Linaria sapphirina var. delphinioides

Linaria sapphirina var. delphinioides, commonly known as the Sapphire Fritillary, is a strikingly beautiful flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The flowers are typically a vivid blue or purple, with a distinctive spurred shape that resembles the tail of a fritillary butterfly. This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

It blooms from late spring to early summer, adding a burst of color to the landscape.
