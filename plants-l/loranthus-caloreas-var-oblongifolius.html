Loranthus caloreas var. oblongifolius

Loranthus caloreas var. oblongifolius, commonly known as the oblong-leaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to host trees through its haustoria.

It is native to parts of Africa and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant has small, lanceolate leaves and produces white or yellowish flowers. Its fruits are typically small berries that contain seeds dispersed by birds.

This species plays a role in ecological interactions, providing food for various wildlife species.
