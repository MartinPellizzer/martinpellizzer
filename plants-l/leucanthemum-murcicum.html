Leucanthemum murcicum

Leucanthemum murcicum, commonly known as the white marguerite, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun conditions. Its white petals are surrounded by a ring of yellow to brown florets, creating a striking visual effect.

Leucanthemum murcicum is often used in ornamental gardening for its delicate and charming appearance.
