Lophopetalum oblongum

Lophopetalum oblongum, commonly known as the Chinese hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite, elliptical leaves that turn reddish in the fall. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pink flowers in clusters during the spring. Its fruits are small, red berries that ripen in the autumn.

Lophopetalum oblongum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional Chinese medicine.
