Lilium taquetii

Lilium taquetii, commonly known as the Taquet's lily, is a rare and striking species native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and red. The plant's foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern at the base. It thrives in cool, moist environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Lilium taquetii is considered a conservation concern.
