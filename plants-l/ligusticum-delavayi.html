Ligusticum delavayi

Ligusticum delavayi, commonly known as the Yunnan lovage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan Province in China.

It belongs to the family Apiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants such as parsley and carrots. The plant is characterized by its slender, branched stems and finely divided, fern-like leaves. Its flowers are small and yellow-green, arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family.

The roots of Ligusticum delavayi are often used in traditional Chinese medicine for their purported health benefits.
