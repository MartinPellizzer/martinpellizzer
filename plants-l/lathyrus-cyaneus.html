Lathyrus cyaneus

Lathyrus cyaneus, commonly known as the blue flax or blue pea, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers, which bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with slender, branching stems and trifoliate leaves. Its flowers are arranged in racemes and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Lathyrus cyaneus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
