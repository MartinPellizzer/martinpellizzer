Lobelia squarrosa

Lobelia squarrosa, commonly known as the woolly lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and small, tubular blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Lobelia squarrosa is often found in clusters, with its stems spreading along the ground.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental appeal.
