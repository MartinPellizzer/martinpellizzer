Leonurus sibiricus

Leonurus sibiricus, commonly known as Siberian hedgehog, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of Asia and Europe.

It features small, hairy, pinkish to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from June to August. The plant has a sprawling growth habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils.

Its common name refers to the hairy appearance of its stems and leaves, resembling the spines of a hedgehog.
