Limnophila siamensis

Limnophila siamensis, commonly known as the Siam water plantain, is a perennial aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and marshes. The plant has long, ribbon-like leaves that float on the water surface, while its roots are submerged. Limnophila siamensis is characterized by its delicate, small white flowers that bloom in clusters.

It plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen to aquatic life.
