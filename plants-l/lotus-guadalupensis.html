Lotus guadalupensis

Lotus guadalupensis, commonly known as the Guadalupensis lotus, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its showy, pink or red flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological role and potential for use in ecological restoration projects.
