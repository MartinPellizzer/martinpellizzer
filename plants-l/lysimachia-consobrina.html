Lysimachia consobrina

Lysimachia consobrina, commonly known as creeping Jenny, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and spreading habit. The plant typically thrives in moist, shady environments and has opposite, heart-shaped leaves. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to cover ground quickly.
