Lepanthes trichocaulis

Lepanthes trichocaulis, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed urn orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant features a slender, often hairy stem and produces delicate, urn-shaped flowers in shades of white, yellow, or green. Its leaves are usually elongated and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in moisture retention.

Lepanthes trichocaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
