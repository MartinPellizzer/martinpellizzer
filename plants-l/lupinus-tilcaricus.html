Lupinus tilcaricus

Lupinus tilcaricus, commonly known as the Andean lupine, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to thrive in nutrient-poor soils, often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Lupinus tilcaricus plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
