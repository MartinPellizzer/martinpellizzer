Ligusticum kingdon-wardii

Ligusticum kingdon-wardii, commonly known as the Yunnan lovage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southwestern China.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescences. The plant features slender, hollow stems and finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often used in traditional Chinese medicine. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in compound umbels, and it produces small, dry fruits known as schizocarps.

Ligusticum kingdon-wardii is valued for its medicinal properties and is also cultivated for its ornamental appeal.
