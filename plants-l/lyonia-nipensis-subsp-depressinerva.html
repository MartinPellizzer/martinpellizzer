Lyonia nipensis subsp. depressinerva

Lyonia nipensis subsp.

depressinerva, commonly known as the dwarf lyonia, is a low-growing shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Japan. It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers in the spring, which are followed by small, blue-black berries. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dune environments, where it plays a role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
