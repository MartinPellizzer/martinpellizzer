Liparis averyanoviana

Liparis averyanoviana, commonly known as the Averyanov's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a sympodial growth habit, characterized by its rhizomatous structure and multiple pseudobulbs. The plant produces intricate, showy flowers that typically bloom in late spring or early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern around the pseudobulbs.

Liparis averyanoviana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
