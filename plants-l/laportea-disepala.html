Laportea disepala

Laportea disepala, commonly known as the broadleaf false nettle, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are surrounded by bracts. Its stems are typically hairy and can become woody with age.

Laportea disepala is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks.
