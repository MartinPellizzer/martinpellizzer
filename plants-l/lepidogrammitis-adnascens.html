Lepidogrammitis adnascens

Lepidogrammitis adnascens, commonly known as the erect moss, is a small, delicate fern typically found in moist, shaded environments.

It has a compact, upright growth habit with slender, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white or pale green spores. The plant's rhizomes are short and creeping, helping it to spread along the forest floor. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the leaves, a characteristic feature of many ferns.

Lepidogrammitis adnascens plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for small invertebrates in its native ecosystems.
