Lophoschoenus comosus

Lophoschoenus comosus, commonly known as the tussock grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and is often found in coastal and sandy environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.

Its botanical features make it well-adapted to harsh, arid conditions.
