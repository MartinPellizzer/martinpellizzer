Lilium feddei

Lilium feddei, commonly known as the Fedde's lily, is a species of bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in late summer. The plant produces large, trumpet-shaped blooms in shades of white, pink, and red, often with a fragrant aroma. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Lilium feddei is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
