Lotus corniculatus var. alandicus

Lotus corniculatus var. alandicus, commonly known as the Aland mountain lotus, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Nordic region.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves, which are typical of the genus Lotus. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in alpine or coastal environments. It is adapted to harsh climatic conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.

Its ecological importance includes providing nectar for pollinators and stabilizing soil in marginal areas.
