Lysimachia elata

Lysimachia elata, commonly known as tall yellow loosestrife, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers during the summer months, which are arranged in terminal spikes. Its root system is fibrous, and it thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors or stream banks.

Lysimachia elata is known for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various purposes.
