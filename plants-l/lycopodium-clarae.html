Lycopodium clarae

Lycopodium clarae, commonly known as the Clara's clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant features whorled leaves that form a dense, low-growing mat, with sporophylls that bear the spores. It is characterized by its simple, unbranched stems and is often found in temperate regions.

Lycopodium clarae plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
