Lepidium abrotanifolium

Lepidium abrotanifolium, commonly known as mountain peppergrass, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other economically important plants such as mustard and cabbage. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its small, yellow flowers are arranged in clusters, and it produces small, round seeds that are dispersed by wind.

The leaves are finely divided and resemble those of the plant it is named after, Abrotanum.
