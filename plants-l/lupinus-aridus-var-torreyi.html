Lupinus aridus var. torreyi

Lupinus aridus var. torreyi, commonly known as the Torrey's lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its erect, spindly growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats.

Lupinus aridus var. torreyi is adapted to arid environments through its deep root system and drought-resistant foliage.
