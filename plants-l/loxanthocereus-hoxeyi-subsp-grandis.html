Loxanthocereus hoxeyi subsp. grandis

Loxanthocereus hoxeyi subsp.

grandis, commonly known as the giant night-blooming cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to Mexico. It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that can grow up to several meters in height.

The plant produces large, showy flowers that open at night, typically in shades of white and yellow. Its succulent stems store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

It is well adapted to full sun and requires minimal water, making it a hardy desert plant.
