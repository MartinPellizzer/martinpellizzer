Lepanthes aurita

Lepanthes aurita, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments. The plant features delicate, narrow leaves and produces showy, yellowish-green flowers with a distinctive red lip. Lepanthes aurita is known for its fragrant blooms, which attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its botanical significance lies in its role within its ecosystem and its value in horticultural collections.
