Lygodium mucronulatum

Lygodium mucronulatum, commonly known as the narrow-lobed water fern, is a member of the family Lygodiaceae.

It is a climbing fern characterized by its finely divided, pinnate fronds that resemble those of true ferns. The plant thrives in wet, tropical environments, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help it anchor to moist substrates, while its spores are dispersed by wind.

The plant plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small organisms and contributing to nutrient cycling.
