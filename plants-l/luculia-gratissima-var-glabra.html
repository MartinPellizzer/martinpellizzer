Luculia gratissima var. glabra

Luculia gratissima var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth Chinese lantern, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its distinctive, inflated, orange-red berries. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and hairy, while its flowers are small and yellow with five petals.

The fruit, which is the most notable feature, is a globose, fleshy capsule that splits open when mature to release its seeds.
