Larrea divaricata f. viscosa

Larrea divaricata f. viscosa, commonly known as sticky leafed grevillea, is a shrub native to arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, glandular hairs that give the leaves a sticky texture. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
