Larix

Larix, commonly known as tamarack, is a genus of coniferous trees belonging to the family Pinaceae.

These trees are characterized by their distinctive, deciduous needles that turn golden in the fall before shedding. They typically grow in cold climates and are often found in boreal and subarctic regions. Larix species have a shallow root system and are adapted to wet, swampy soils.

The wood of larix is lightweight and resistant to decay, making it valuable for construction and furniture.
