Linaria ruthenica

Linaria ruthenica, commonly known as the Ruthenian toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as slopes and wastelands. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces elongated seed pods that release small, dusty seeds.

Linaria ruthenica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
