Licuala chaiana

Licuala chaiana, commonly known as the giant swamp taro, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, paddle-shaped leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant thrives in wet, swampy environments and is often found in mangrove forests. Its rhizomes are sometimes used in traditional medicine.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
