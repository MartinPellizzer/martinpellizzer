Lallemantia royleana

Lallemantia royleana, commonly known as the Kashmir wallflower, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayan regions of Pakistan and India.

It features slender, hairy stems that grow up to 1 meter in height, often branching towards the top. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers with four petals, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Lallemantia royleana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
