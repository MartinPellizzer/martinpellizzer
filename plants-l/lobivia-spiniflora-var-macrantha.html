Lobivia spiniflora var. macrantha

Lobivia spiniflora var. macrantha, commonly known as the Macrantha Lobivia, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to the deserts of South America.

This succulent plant is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to survive in arid environments. The leaves are typically reduced to spines, and the plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its slow growth and unique appearance, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
