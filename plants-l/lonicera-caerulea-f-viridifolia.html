Lonicera caerulea f. viridifolia

Lonicera caerulea f. viridifolia, commonly known as the green-leaf honeysuckle, is a cultivar of the honeysuckle plant characterized by its distinctive foliage.

It is native to eastern Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible berries. The leaves of this plant are typically a vibrant green, which sets it apart from other honeysuckle varieties. It produces small, fragrant flowers in the spring, followed by berries that ripen in late summer.

This plant is well-suited for gardens and can thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a versatile addition to landscaping.
