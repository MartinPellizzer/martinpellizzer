Lobelia diversifolia

Lobelia diversifolia, commonly known as the variable lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to violet. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are erect and branching.

Lobelia diversifolia is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
