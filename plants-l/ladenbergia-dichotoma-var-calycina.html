Ladenbergia dichotoma var. calycina

Ladenbergia dichotoma var. calycina, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and often covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing flat, edible seeds. Its bark and leaves have been traditionally used in herbal medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry woodland or savanna habitats.
