Lobivia chrysochete var. markusii

Lobivia chrysochete var. markusii, commonly known as the Golden Crowned Lobivia, is a compact, low-growing cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It features a spherical or slightly elongated stem with a dense covering of golden-yellow spines that give it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the warmer months. It is well adapted to dry climates and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and succulent collections.

Its unique coloration and drought tolerance make it a striking addition to desert landscapes.
