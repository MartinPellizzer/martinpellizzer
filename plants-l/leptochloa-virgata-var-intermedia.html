Leptochloa virgata var. intermedia

Leptochloa virgata var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate slender grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its slender growth habit and is often found in lowland areas.

Botanically, it is part of the Poaceae family, which includes many economically important grasses.
