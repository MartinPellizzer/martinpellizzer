Lycium floribundum var. pruinosum

Lycium floribundum var. pruinosum, commonly known as the silverleaf lycium, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, fuzzy hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. Its berries are red and edible, often used in traditional herbal remedies.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is commonly found in rocky or coastal areas.
