Lepanthes irrasa

Lepanthes irrasa, commonly known as the red-violet orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central America.

It is a small terrestrial orchid that typically grows in humid, shaded forest environments. The plant features a slender, erect stem with a single, showy violet-blue flower that has a distinctive red throat. Its roots are often clustered and adapted to absorb nutrients from the forest floor.

Lepanthes irrasa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in specialized orchid collections.
