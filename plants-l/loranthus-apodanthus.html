Loranthus apodanthus

Loranthus apodanthus, commonly known as the mistletoe of the genus Loranthus, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches itself to host trees, primarily in the family Fabaceae.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using the light it captures. The plant has a small, inconspicuous appearance with small leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. Its seeds are dispersed by birds, which eat the fleshy, red berries and later excrete the seeds onto tree branches.

This species is found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its host trees.
