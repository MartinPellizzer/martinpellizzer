Lantana aristata var. latiuscula

Lantana aristata var. latiuscula, commonly known as the broadleaf lantana, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The leaves and flowers are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
