Lipocarpha minima

Lipocarpha minima, commonly known as the dwarf sedge, is a small herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. This species typically grows in wet or muddy habitats, such as marshes and swampy areas. It has a short stature, usually reaching only a few centimeters in height.

The plant's botanical structure reflects its adaptation to waterlogged environments, with rhizomes that help in anchoring and nutrient absorption.
