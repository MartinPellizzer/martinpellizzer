Lauro-cerasus africana

Lauro-cerasus africana, commonly known as the African cherry or African laurustin, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Prunus genus and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a glossy green upper surface. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters, followed by bright red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds and some mammals.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
