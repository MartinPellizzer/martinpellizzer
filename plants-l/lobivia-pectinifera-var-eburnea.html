Lobivia pectinifera var. eburnea

Lobivia pectinifera var. eburnea, commonly known as the White Lobivia, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It features a compact, rounded body with thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in dry environments. The plant produces clusters of small, pale green to white flowers during the warmer months. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, which help minimize water loss.

This variety is prized for its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor collections.
