Leopoldia trojana

Leopoldia trojana, commonly known as the Trojan orchid, is a rare and critically endangered orchid native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, pale green to yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has a complex flowering pattern, often requiring specific environmental conditions to trigger blooming. Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is listed as threatened by the IUCN Red List.

Conservation efforts are underway to protect and propagate this unique species.
