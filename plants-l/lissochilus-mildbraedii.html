Lissochilus mildbraedii

Lissochilus mildbraedii, commonly known as the mildbraed's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean region of South America.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pale pink. The plant grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or alpine habitats. It has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
