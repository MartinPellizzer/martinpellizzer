Litanthus

Litanthus, commonly known as the sacred lily, is a genus of flowering plants native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its showy, often fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plants typically have narrow, grass-like leaves and are often found in rocky or coastal habitats. Litanthus species are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes cultivated in gardens.

Some species are also used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
