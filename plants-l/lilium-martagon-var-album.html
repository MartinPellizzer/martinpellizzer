Lilium martagon var. album

Lilium martagon var. album, commonly known as the white Turk's cap lily, is a striking perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear clusters of large, showy white flowers with a distinctive, recurved shape. The leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem, contributing to its elegant appearance. This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its historical use in floral arrangements.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile addition to various landscapes.
