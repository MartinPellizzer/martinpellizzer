Laplacea fruticosa var. symplocoides

Laplacea fruticosa var. symplocoides, commonly known as the symplocoides variant of the laplacea shrub, is a perennial flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It features dense, compact growth with small, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in clusters. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers during the spring and early summer. It is adapted to dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or arid landscapes.

Its botanical structure includes woody stems and a compact, shrub-like form that makes it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.
