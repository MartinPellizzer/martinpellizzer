Larbrea graminea var. latifolia

Larbrea graminea var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf grass, is a perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that can grow up to several feet in length, with a distinctive bluish-green coloration. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the grass family. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in landscaping and erosion control in warm climates.
