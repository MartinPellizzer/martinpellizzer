Linum virginianum var. oppositifolium

Linum virginianum var. oppositifolium, commonly known as the opposite-leaved flax, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stem. The plant produces delicate blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

This variety is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and historical use in textile production.
