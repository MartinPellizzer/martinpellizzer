Leucaena magnifica

Leucaena magnifica, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a tropical leguminous plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow inflorescences and trifoliate leaves. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family and is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it valuable for soil enrichment. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

The seeds of Leucaena magnifica are also used in traditional medicine and as a source of protein-rich fodder.
