Leucothoe elongata

Leucothoe elongata, commonly known as the slender leucothoe, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in forest understories. The plant features small, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green, with white or pale pink flowers in the spring. Its fruits are small, red berries that mature in the fall, providing food for birds and other wildlife.

Leucothoe elongata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in woodland gardens.
