Linochilus leiocladus

Linochilus leiocladus, commonly known as the smooth-leaved linochilus, is a member of the Gesneriaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

This evergreen shrub is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically smooth and glossy. It produces small, tubular flowers that are usually brightly colored, often in shades of red, orange, or yellow. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
