Litsea caroli

Litsea caroli, commonly known as the California waxleaf-laurel, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, leathery, and dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by dark blue, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its dense foliage and winter hardiness make it suitable for use in hedges and landscape design.
