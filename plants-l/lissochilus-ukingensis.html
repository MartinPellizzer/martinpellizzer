Lissochilus ukingensis

Lissochilus ukingensis, commonly known as the Ukingensis orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, spurred flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and purple. The plant thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found growing at high altitudes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps it retain moisture in its natural habitat.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized species within the orchid family.
